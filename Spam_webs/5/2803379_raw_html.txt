     <p>
      <b>
       <a href="/wiki/Tug_of_war" title="Tug of war">
        Tug of war
       </a>
      </b>
      was contested as a team event in the
      <b>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_Olympics" title="Summer Olympics">
        Summer Olympics
       </a>
      </b>
      at every Olympiad from 1900 to 1920. Originally the competition was entered by groups called clubs. A country could enter more than one club in the competition, making it possible for one country to earn multiple medals. This happened in
      <a href="/wiki/1904_Summer_Olympics" title="1904 Summer Olympics">
       1904
      </a>
      , when the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_at_the_Olympics" title="United States at the Olympics">
       United States
      </a>
      won all three medals, and in
      <a href="/wiki/1908_Summer_Olympics" title="1908 Summer Olympics">
       1908
      </a>
      when the podium was occupied by three
      <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_at_the_Olympics" title="Great Britain at the Olympics">
       British
      </a>
      teams.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Sweden_at_the_Olympics" title="Sweden at the Olympics">
       Sweden
      </a>
      was also among the top countries with two medals, one as a member of the mixed team.
     </p>
     <p>
      During its time as an Olympic sport, it was considered to be part of the
      <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the Summer Olympics">
       Olympic athletics programme
      </a>
      , although the sports of
      <a href="/wiki/Tug_of_war" title="Tug of war">
       tug of war
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_(sport)" title="Athletics (sport)">
       athletics
      </a>
      are now considered distinct.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
