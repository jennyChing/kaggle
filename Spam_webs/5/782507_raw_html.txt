        </p>

        </p>
        <h2 class="nolinks">
         Example Use of the Bttn
        </h2>
        <p>
         The Bttn website gives some great examples of how you can use it. For instance, you can make it so that your child presses the button as soon as they get home from school which will automatically send a text message to you letting you know theyre home.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="The Bttn." class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-112615" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-out-box.jpg" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="The Bttn." width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="The Bttn." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-112615" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-out-box.jpg" title="The Bttn."/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         However, you can also make it so that if the Bttn isnt pressed within a certain amount of time of them getting home, atext can be sent to you letting you know that something may be wrong.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre an IFTTT user, the Bttn will become your new favorite channel. From sending anemail, to tweeting, to turning on or off your lights, to adding information to a Google document or spreadsheet, theres so much you can do if the Bttn is pressed.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Popular IFTTT recipes for the Bttn channel." class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-112520" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-popular-ifttt-recipes.jpg" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="Popular IFTTT recipes for the Bttn channel." width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Popular IFTTT recipes for the Bttn channel." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-112520" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-popular-ifttt-recipes.jpg" title="Popular IFTTT recipes for the Bttn channel."/>
        </noscript>
        <h2 class="nolinks">
         Setting Up The Bttn
        </h2>
        <p>
         To get started, youll need to register your Bttn on their website where youll also be able to change the name of your button, create actions and see important information (e.g. battery and signal strength).
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="You can see your Bttn's status on the website." class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-112606" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-list-view-website.png" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="You can see your Bttn's status on the website." width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="You can see your Bttn's status on the website." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-112606" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-list-view-website.png" title="You can see your Bttn's status on the website."/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         When activating your Bttn, the initial setup is involved and may take a good five to ten minutesLuckily, there is a very detailed
         <a href="https://my.bt.tn/gettingstarted" title="Get started with your Bttn.">
          Getting Started guide
         </a>
         to help you along the way.
        </p>
        <p>
         First, youll have to choose how your Bttn will connect to the Internet. You can choose to use your cellular network via a SIM card or WiFi.
        </p>
        <p>
         Next youll need to get prepared to power it up. You can use a micro-USB power adapter (not included) or 4 AA batteries. The adapter is recommended (I used my tablets charger), especially if you want to keep it on at all times. As youll see, the micro-USB slot is in a very clever location (where the batteries go), and theres even room for you to neatly wrap thecable inside the base.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="The micro-USB adapter plugged into the Bttn." class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-112521" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-back-micro-usb-plugged-in.jpg" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="The micro-USB adapter plugged into the Bttn." width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="The micro-USB adapter plugged into the Bttn." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-112521" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-back-micro-usb-plugged-in.jpg" title="The micro-USB adapter plugged into the Bttn."/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         Now its time to activate the internal WiFi access point (if you choose to use WiFi). You must press and hold the Bttn down and plug in the adapter (or add the batteries) while still holding it down. The Bttn will light up red  keep holding for about fifteen seconds. Once the Bttn shows a ring of blue lights, you can release it. This means the WiFi access point is active.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="The WiFi access point is active on the Bttn." class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-112522" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-illuminated-blue.jpg" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="The WiFi access point is active on the Bttn." width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="The WiFi access point is active on the Bttn." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-112522" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-illuminated-blue.jpg" title="The WiFi access point is active on the Bttn."/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         Finally, you have to configure the WiFi settings. Its easiest to use a mobile device to do this. Youll need to go to your WiFi connections, find the bttnconf SSID and connect to it (no password needed).
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Connect to bttnconf SSID on your mobile device." class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-112523" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-setup-wifi-mobile-device.jpg" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="Connect to bttnconf SSID on your mobile device." width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Connect to bttnconf SSID on your mobile device." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-112523" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-setup-wifi-mobile-device.jpg" title="Connect to bttnconf SSID on your mobile device."/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         Then youll be given a URL to navigate to, which is where youll need to enter your home (or work) SSID and password so that the Bttn can connect to the network. Once thats done, you can restart the Bttn by unplugging it and then plugging it back in.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Bttn WiFi setup in mobile browser." class="lazy aligncenter wp-image-112524" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-wifi-setup-browser.jpg" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="Bttn WiFi setup in mobile browser." width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Bttn WiFi setup in mobile browser." class="aligncenter wp-image-112524" height="408" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-wifi-setup-browser.jpg" title="Bttn WiFi setup in mobile browser." width="800"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         Youll see a mini light show going on once the Bttn restarts. After a few seconds, it should just start to slowly blink green. It will continue to do thiswhile its on, letting you know that the Internet connection is okay.
        </p>
        <h2 class="nolinks">
         Setting Up Actions
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Bttn actions you can configure if it's not pressed within a certain time period." class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-112605" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-configure-action-not-pressed.png" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="Bttn actions you can configure if it's not pressed within a certain time period." width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Bttn actions you can configure if it's not pressed within a certain time period." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-112605" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-configure-action-not-pressed.png" title="Bttn actions you can configure if it's not pressed within a certain time period."/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         On its own, the Bttn has a small selection of actions that you can set up  SMS, email, Twitter, Facebook, HTTP and counter. However, it gets really interesting once IFTTT or Zapier (a similar service) is connected.
        </p>
        <p>
         I wont go into details on
         <a href="http://www.maketecheasier.com/ifttt-recipes-automate-social-life/" title="5 Cool IFTTT Recipes to Automate Your Social Life">
          creating a recipe
         </a>
         , but its no different than creating any other recipe. I successfully created a recipe to send out a tweet when the Bttn was pressed, as well as send an SMS message to my husband. Both worked great and almost immediately.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="A tweet sent out via the Bttn and IFTTT." class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-112612" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-tweet-sent.png" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="A tweet sent out via the Bttn and IFTTT." width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="A tweet sent out via the Bttn and IFTTT." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-112612" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2015/02/bttn-tweet-sent.png" title="A tweet sent out via the Bttn and IFTTT."/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         One important thing youll want to do is pay attention to the lights on your Bttn. Each light indicates something different:Flashing GREEN means successful completion of the trigger, RED means error, and a circling YELLOW means wait.
        </p>
        <h2 class="nolinks">
         Final Thoughts
        </h2>
        <p>
         The Bttn is a really cool gadget that can be used in many creative ways  at home or at work. The only downside is the fact that it can only be programmed to do one action at a time. If you want to be able to do more than one thing, youll have to buy more than one Bttn. At around $78.00 a Bttn, that can get pricey.
        </p>
        <p>
         Besides that, the Bttn is the perfect accessory to any desk, table or counter.
        </p>
