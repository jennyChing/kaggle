     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Earth (The Book): A Visitor's Guide to the Human Race
       </b>
      </i>
      is a 2010
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humor" title="Humor">
       humor
      </a>
      book written by
      <a href="/wiki/Jon_Stewart" title="Jon Stewart">
       Jon Stewart
      </a>
      and other writers of
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Daily_Show_with_Jon_Stewart" title="The Daily Show with Jon Stewart">
        The Daily Show with Jon Stewart
       </a>
      </i>
      , a sequel to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/America_(The_Book)" title="America (The Book)">
        America (The Book)
       </a>
      </i>
      . It was released on September 21, 2010.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">
       Synopsis
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth_(The_Book)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Written in the past tense, the book's stated purpose is to serve as a
      <a href="/wiki/Baedeker" title="Baedeker">
       Baedeker
      </a>
      travel guide for an alien civilization that discovers Earth after humanity has died out, most likely by its own hands. As such,
      <i>
       Earth (The Book)
      </i>
      attempts to chronicle the history of the planet and the human race from the beginning to the present day, and also tries to explain human concepts and emotions such as "love" and "work" for its alien readers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew1_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ew1-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The book follows a similar format to
      <i>
       America (The Book)
      </i>
      , being written in the style of a textbook and featuring many images, including visual gags. One controversial visual gag in
      <i>
       America
      </i>
      was a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photoshop" title="Photoshop">
       photoshopped
      </a>
      image of the United States Supreme Court justices nude; a similar gag appeared in
      <i>
       Earth
      </i>
      which was an illustration of human anatomy that featured a nude man, one half of the man depicting
      <a href="/wiki/Larry_King" title="Larry King">
       Larry King
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth_(The_Book)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Keith Staskiewicz of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">
        Entertainment Weekly
       </a>
      </i>
      gave the book an A, praising it as "
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Devil%27s_Dictionary" title="The Devil's Dictionary">
        The Devil's Dictionary
       </a>
      </i>
      for a new generation" and concludes that the book's humor and writing style might lead the reader to conclude that "this would actually be a fairly comprehensive guide for extraterrestrial visitors", adding "just so long as they have a sense of humor".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew1_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ew1-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      As of December 5, 2010,
      <i>
       Earth (The Book)
      </i>
      had spent 10 weeks on the
      <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times_Best_Seller_list" title="The New York Times Best Seller list">
       <i>
        New York Times
       </i>
       Best Seller list
      </a>
      ; for four weeks it was number one on the Hardcover Nonfiction list.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On February 13, 2011,
      <i>
       Earth (The Book)
      </i>
      was awarded a Grammy from the 2011
      <a href="/wiki/53rd_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="53rd Annual Grammy Awards">
       53rd Annual Grammy Awards
      </a>
      for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Spoken_Word_Album" title="Best Spoken Word Album">
       Best Spoken Word Album
      </a>
      .
     </p>
