       <!--BEGIN .entry-content -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Restoration of existing mural on Kingston Rd.
         <br/>
         after years of stucco damage. I was commissioned to recreate the existing art after the stucco was repaired and the wall was primed, with some of my own enhancements ie: the bear, and some group of seven trees for a more canadiana look.
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://jasonmamone.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://jasonmamone.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/image6.jpg">

         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ahhhh, a fresh canvas.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://jasonmamone.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/image8.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-thumbnail wp-image-486" height="150" src="http://jasonmamone.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/image8-e1412305666760-112x150.jpg" title="image" width="112"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Projection night went smoothly, with a new tool  Liquitex acrylic spray for line work
         <br/>
         <a href="http://jasonmamone.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/image5.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-477" height="300" src="http://jasonmamone.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/image5-220x300.jpg" title="image" width="220"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Brought in a friend, Mike Echlin to assist on the painting. He executed the brush work quite nicely and we managed to complete the mural in 3.5 days
        </p>
