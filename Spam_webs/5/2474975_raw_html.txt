     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        The World's End
       </b>
      </i>
      is a 2013
      <a href="/wiki/Comic_science_fiction" title="Comic science fiction">
       science fiction comedy
      </a>
      directed by
      <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Wright" title="Edgar Wright">
       Edgar Wright
      </a>
      , written by Wright and
      <a href="/wiki/Simon_Pegg" title="Simon Pegg">
       Simon Pegg
      </a>
      , and starring Pegg,
      <a href="/wiki/Nick_Frost" title="Nick Frost">
       Nick Frost
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Paddy_Considine" title="Paddy Considine">
       Paddy Considine
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Martin_Freeman" title="Martin Freeman">
       Martin Freeman
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Rosamund_Pike" title="Rosamund Pike">
       Rosamund Pike
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Marsan" title="Eddie Marsan">
       Eddie Marsan
      </a>
      . The film follows a group of friends who discover an alien invasion during an epic
      <a href="/wiki/Pub_crawl" title="Pub crawl">
       pub crawl
      </a>
      in their home town. Wright has described the film as "social science fiction" in the tradition of
      <a href="/wiki/John_Wyndham" title="John Wyndham">
       John Wyndham
      </a>
      and Samuel Youd (
      <a href="/wiki/John_Christopher" title="John Christopher">
       John Christopher
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is the third film in the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Three_Flavours_Cornetto_trilogy" title="Three Flavours Cornetto trilogy">
        Three Flavours Cornetto trilogy
       </a>
      </i>
      , following
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Shaun_of_the_Dead" title="Shaun of the Dead">
        Shaun of the Dead
       </a>
      </i>
      (2004) and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Hot_Fuzz" title="Hot Fuzz">
        Hot Fuzz
       </a>
      </i>
      (2007). The film was produced by
      <a href="/wiki/Relativity_Media" title="Relativity Media">
       Relativity Media
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/StudioCanal" title="StudioCanal">
       StudioCanal
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Big_Talk_Productions" title="Big Talk Productions">
       Big Talk Productions
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Working_Title_Films" title="Working Title Films">
       Working Title Films
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World%27s_End_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Gary King (
      <a href="/wiki/Simon_Pegg" title="Simon Pegg">
       Simon Pegg
      </a>
      ), a middle-aged
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alcoholic" title="Alcoholic">
       alcoholic
      </a>
      , tracks down his estranged schoolfriends and persuades them to complete "the Golden Mile", a
      <a href="/wiki/Pub_crawl" title="Pub crawl">
       pub crawl
      </a>
      encompassing the 12 pubs of their hometown of Newton Haven. The group attempted the crawl as teenagers, but failed to reach the final pub, The World's End.
     </p>
     <p>
      Gary arrives in Newton Haven with friends Peter (
      <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Marsan" title="Eddie Marsan">
       Eddie Marsan
      </a>
      ), Oliver (
      <a href="/wiki/Martin_Freeman" title="Martin Freeman">
       Martin Freeman
      </a>
      ), Steven (
      <a href="/wiki/Paddy_Considine" title="Paddy Considine">
       Paddy Considine
      </a>
      ), and Andy (
      <a href="/wiki/Nick_Frost" title="Nick Frost">
       Nick Frost
      </a>
      ). The group is briefly joined for a drink by Oliver's sister Sam (
      <a href="/wiki/Rosamund_Pike" title="Rosamund Pike">
       Rosamund Pike
      </a>
      ), whose affections Gary and Steven fought over in school. In the toilets of the fourth pub, Gary picks a fight with a teenager and knocks his head off, exposing him as an
      <a href="/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life" title="Extraterrestrial life">
       alien
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Android_(robot)" title="Android (robot)">
       android
      </a>
      . Gary's friends join him and fight more androids, whom they refer to as "blanks".
     </p>
     <p>
      Several pubs later, the group runs into Sam again. After Gary saves her from twin androids impersonating her friends, she is convinced of the androids' invasion. At The Mermaid, androids impersonating attractive girls from their youth attempt to seduce the men. At the next pub, Guy Shepherd (
      <a href="/wiki/Pierce_Brosnan" title="Pierce Brosnan">
       Pierce Brosnan
      </a>
      ), their old schoolteacher, encourages them to accept their fate and be replaced by androids. Andy realises that Oliver has been replaced with an android. Another fight breaks out and the group scatters.
     </p>
     <p>
      Once reunited, the four remaining friends accuse each other of having been replaced by androids, but prove that they are human. The androids close in on the group and capture Peter, but Gary is determined to finish the pub crawl. After having a drink at the other two pubs along the way, he runs towards the final pub, the World's End. Steven is overwhelmed by the androids and Andy chases Gary through the streets.
     </p>
     <p>
      At the World's End, Andy confronts Gary. In the quarrel, Andy realises that Gary attempted
      <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">
       suicide
      </a>
      in the years since school. As he tries to stop Gary from drawing his final pint, they discover a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hidden_chamber" title="Hidden chamber">
       hidden chamber
      </a>
      and are reunited with Steven. A disembodied alien entity, the Network (
      <a href="/wiki/Bill_Nighy" title="Bill Nighy">
       Bill Nighy
      </a>
      ), reveals that the limited android invasion has been responsible for all of humanity's advances in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telecommunications" title="Telecommunications">
       telecommunications
      </a>
      in recent decades, bringing humanity closer together as a first step to joining the galaxy's community of planets. The Network offers the men the chance to contribute to Earth joining the galactic community, even offering them eternal youth by becoming androids, but they refuse. Exasperated, the Network abandons the invasion after Gary manages to prove that the Network had to replace nearly all of the citizens in the town to make it acceptable enough for its standards. Sam arrives just in time to drive the group out of Newton Haven as it self-destructs.
     </p>
     <p>
      Some time later, Andy recounts this story around a campfire in the ruins of London. The destruction of Newton Haven triggered a worldwide chain reaction that wiped out modern technology and set humanity back to the
      <a href="/wiki/Dark_Ages_(historiography)" title="Dark Ages (historiography)">
       Dark Ages
      </a>
      . The remaining androids have reactivated and are mistrusted by surviving humans. Andy's marriage has recovered, Steven is in a relationship with Sam, and android versions of Peter and Oliver have returned to a semblance of their former lives. In the ruins of Newton Haven, the now-sober Gary enters a pub with the younger android versions of his friends and orders five glasses of water. When the bartender refuses to serve androids, Gary draws a sword and leads his android friends into a brawl.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Development">
       Development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World%27s_End_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       The World's End
      </i>
      began as a screenplay titled
      <i>
       Crawl
      </i>
      about a group of teenagers on a pub crawl; it was written by Edgar Wright at the age of 21. He realised the idea could work with adult characters to capture "the bittersweet feeling of returning to your home town and feeling like a stranger".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Wright said he wanted to satirise the "strange homogeneous branding that becomes like a virus", explaining: "This doesn't just extend to pubs, it's the same with cafs and restaurants. If you live in a small town and you move to London, which I did when I was 20, then when you go back out into the other small towns in England you go 'oh my god, it's all the same!' It's like
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Body_Snatchers" title="The Body Snatchers">
        Bodysnatchers
       </a>
      </i>
      : literally our towns are being changed to death."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gq-magazine1_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-gq-magazine1-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In an interview for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">
        Entertainment Weekly
       </a>
      </i>
      , Pegg told Clark Collis, "People think we choose the genre first every time, and it's not true. We find the stories first. The notion of alienation from your hometown taken to its literal conclusion was how we got to science fiction."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After the story was complete, Wright and Pegg examined a list of real pub names and "tried to make them like tarot cards" to foreshadow the events of the story. Wright explained: "So we said, 'OK this one's the Famous Cock, because this is where Gary is trying to puff up his own importance.' ... We did go through work out in each one how the pub sign was going to relate."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gq-magazine1_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-gq-magazine1-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World%27s_End_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_World%27s_End_(2).jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/The_World%27s_End_%282%29.jpg/220px-The_World%27s_End_%282%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/The_World%27s_End_%282%29.jpg/330px-The_World%27s_End_%282%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/The_World%27s_End_%282%29.jpg/440px-The_World%27s_End_%282%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_World%27s_End_(2).jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The Gardeners Arms pub on the boundary of Letchworth was used as the shooting location for the final pub, the World's End
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Principal photography for
      <i>
       The World's End
      </i>
      began on 28 September 2012.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Filming took place in
      <a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire" title="Hertfordshire">
       Hertfordshire
      </a>
      , at
      <a href="/wiki/Elstree_Studios" title="Elstree Studios">
       Elstree Studios
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Letchworth" title="Letchworth">
       Letchworth Garden City
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Garden_City" title="Welwyn Garden City">
       Welwyn Garden City
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Part of the film was also shot at
      <a href="/wiki/High_Wycombe_railway_station" title="High Wycombe railway station">
       High Wycombe railway station
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">
       Buckinghamshire
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      All twelve pubs in the film use identical signage on menus and walls, reflecting what Wright called "that fake hand-written chalk" common to modern British pubs.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gq-magazine1_9-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-gq-magazine1-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The exteriors of the real pubs were shot at locations in Welwyn Garden City and Letchworth Garden City, with altered signage.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Letchworth_Garden_City_railway_station" title="Letchworth Garden City railway station">
       Letchworth Garden City railway station
      </a>
      got a makeover as the "Hole in the Wall".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Stunts were coordinated by Brad Allen of
      <a href="/wiki/Martial_arts_film" title="Martial arts film">
       martial arts film
      </a>
      director
      <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Chan" title="Jackie Chan">
       Jackie Chan
      </a>
      's team. Wright said: "In
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Drunken_Master" title="Drunken Master">
        Drunken Master
       </a>
      </i>
      , Jackie Chan has to get drunk to fight, but this is more the idea of
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_courage" title="Dutch courage">
       Dutch courage
      </a>
      . You know, when you're kind of drunk and you think 'ah, I can climb up that scaffolding!' Or just that you're impervious to pain. One of the things we talked about is this idea that [the characters] become better fighters the more oiled they get."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gq-magazine1_9-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-gq-magazine1-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Broadway Cinema, Letchworth, a renovated independent cinema built in a quintessentially
      <a href="/wiki/Art_Deco" title="Art Deco">
       Art Deco
      </a>
      style in the 1930s,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      was used to portray the Mermaid pub. This cinema was also the first outside London to play the film, with a special introduction by Pegg thanking the residents of Letchworth for their help during its making; over 800 viewers watched the film at the cinema on its opening night.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">
       Box office
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World%27s_End_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Box office">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <i>
       The World's End
      </i>
      earned 2,122,288 ($3,242,064 USD) during its UK opening weekend, losing the top spot to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Monsters_University" title="Monsters University">
        Monsters University
       </a>
      </i>
      . Its weekend grosses were higher than
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Shaun_of_the_Dead" title="Shaun of the Dead">
        Shaun of the Dead
       </a>
      </i>
      's 1.6 million but lower than
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Hot_Fuzz" title="Hot Fuzz">
        Hot Fuzz
       </a>
      </i>
      's 5.4 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy.co.uk_24-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-digitalspy.co.uk-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the United States, the movie was released on 23 August and earned $3.5 million on its opening day, beating out
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Mortal_Instruments:_City_of_Bones" title="The Mortal Instruments: City of Bones">
        The Mortal Instruments: City of Bones
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/You%27re_Next" title="You're Next">
        You're Next
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <i>
       The World's End
      </i>
      also had the highest per-theater average out of all films in theaters throughout the country on its opening day.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On its opening weekend, the film landed in fourth place with $8,790,237, behind
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Daniels%27_The_Butler" title="Lee Daniels' The Butler">
        Lee Daniels' The Butler
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/We%27re_the_Millers" title="We're the Millers">
        We're the Millers
       </a>
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       The Mortal Instruments: City of Bones
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This exceeded box office expectations ranging from $7 million
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      to $8.5 million,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <i>
       The World's End'
      </i>
      s opening weekend earned the most out of all films in the Cornetto Trilogy.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">
       Critical reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World%27s_End_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Critical reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <i>
       The World's End
      </i>
      received critical acclaim. The
      <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">
       review aggregator
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">
       Rotten Tomatoes
      </a>
      reported an 89% approval rating with a
      <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">
       weighted average
      </a>
      score of 7.4/10 based on 189 reviews. The website's consensus reads, "Madcap and heartfelt, Edgar Wright's apocalypse comedy
      <i>
       The World's End
      </i>
      benefits from the typically hilarious Simon Pegg and Nick Frost, with a plethora of supporting players."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The film also holds a
      <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">
       Metacritic
      </a>
      score of 81 out of 100, indicating "universal acclaim", based on 45 collected reviews.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Chris Nashawaty of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">
        Entertainment Weekly
       </a>
      </i>
      gave the film a B+, praising it as "hilarious" and the "best" collaboration of Wright, Pegg and Frost, saying that "these pint-swilling Peter Pans also know how to work the heart
      <i>
       and
      </i>
      the brain for belly laughs... The finale is a little too shaggy and silly. But what do you expect after a dozen beers?"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Mark Dinning of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Empire_(film_magazine)" title="Empire (film magazine)">
        Empire
       </a>
      </i>
      magazine gave the film four stars out of five, writing: "Bravely refusing to rigidly adhere to a formula that has been so successful, Wright, Pegg and Frost's Cornetto Trilogy closer has tonal shifts you won't expect, but the same beating heart you've been craving."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Movie blog eatpraymedia gave the film four and a half stars, calling it "...the perfect antidote to big budget movie making."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Henry Barnes of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">
        The Guardian
       </a>
      </i>
      gave the film four stars out of five, writing: "With this final film they've slowed down a bit, grown up a lot. And saved the richest bite until last."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">
       Accolades
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_World%27s_End_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Accolades">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       The World's End
      </i>
      received two
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Movie_Awards" title="Critics' Choice Movie Awards">
       Critics' Choice Movie Awards
      </a>
      nominations, for Best Actor in a Comedy (for Simon Pegg) and for Best Comedy, but lost to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Wolf_of_Wall_Street_(2013_film)" title="The Wolf of Wall Street (2013 film)">
        The Wolf of Wall Street
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Hustle_(2013_film)" title="American Hustle (2013 film)">
        American Hustle
       </a>
      </i>
      respectively.
     </p>
     <p>
      The movie won Best British Film at the
      <a href="/wiki/19th_Empire_Awards" title="19th Empire Awards">
       19th Empire Awards
      </a>
      held in London in March 2014.
     </p>
