          <span class="book-author">
           Neilesh Bose
          </span>
          <span class="book-title">
           Recasting the Region: Language, Culture, and Islam in Colonial Bengal
          </span>
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="book-meta">
         Oxford University Press, 2014
        </div>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard fn">
          Ian Cook

         <abbr class="published" title="2015-02-18">

        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <div class="author-photo wp-caption">

        </div>
        <div class="amz-book wp-caption">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/019809728X/?tag=newbooinhis-20" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/dp/019809728X/?tag=newbooinhis-20');" title="View this book on Amazon">

        <p>
         In his new book
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/019809728X/?tag=newbooinhis-20" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/dp/019809728X/?tag=newbooinhis-20');">
          <i>
           Recasting the Region
          </i>
          <i>
           : Language, Culture, and Islam in Colonial Bengal
          </i>
         </a>
         (Oxford University Press, 2014),
         <a href="http://www.stjohns.edu/academics/bio/neilesh-bose" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.stjohns.edu/academics/bio/neilesh-bose');" target="_blank">
          Neilesh Bose
         </a>
         analyses the trajectories of Muslim Bengali politics in the first half of the twentieth century.The literary and cultural history ofthe region explored in the book reveal the pointedly Bengali ideas of Pakistan that arose as an empire ended and new countries were born.
        </p>
