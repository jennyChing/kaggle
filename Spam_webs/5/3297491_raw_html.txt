             <p>
              Raising livestock takes a huge toll on the environment, including the water and feed that cows consume and the methane and waste they emit. That's why Andras Forgacs co-founded
              <a href="http://modernmeadow.com/">
               Modern Meadow
              </a>
              , a three-year-old biotech startup working to develop meat made by growing cow's cells--but not the whole animal.
             </p>
             <p>
              Forgacs argues that lab-grown meat is a more sustainable way to
              <a href="http://www.popsci.com/technology/article/2013-08/first-lab-grown-hamburger-served">
               make a hamburger
              </a>
              . But exciting technologies often have hidden costs, and not everyone thinks thats a tidy answer to a global problem.
              <em>
               Popular Science
              </em>
              ,
              <a href="http://climateconfidential.com/">
               Climate Confidential
              </a>
              , and Modern Farmer cosponsored a live event to explore the technologys upsides and downsides. Above is a short video to tell you what you need to know about the world's newest food fight. (Popular Science attended a different debate about lab-grown meat and the future of burgers in the fall;
              <a href="http://www.popsci.com/roundtable-discussion-future-meat">
               here's that video
              </a>
