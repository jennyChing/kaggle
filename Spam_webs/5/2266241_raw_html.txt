     <h1 class="post-title">
      UK is fastest growing solar energy market
     </h1>
     <div class="post-meta">
      <div class="comments">
       <a href="http://www.electronicsweekly.com/news/business/market-research/uk-is-fastest-growing-solar-energy-market-2010-08/#comments">
        no comment
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="author">
       <a href="http://www.electronicsweekly.com/news/author/richard-wilson/" rel="author" title="Posts by Richard Wilson">
        Richard Wilson
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="date">
       4th August 2010
      </div>
      <div class="newsletters">
       <a href="http://www.electronicsweekly.com/newsletters/">
        Get news by email
       </a>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      <img alt="itemid-52577-getasset.jpg" height="128" src="http://www.electronicsweekly.com/news/wp-content/uploads/sites/16/2010/08/itemid-52577-getasset.jpg" width="167"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      The UK has the fastest-growing market for photovoltaic (PV) solar energy installations this year, according to iSuppli.
     </p>
     <p>
      Installations of PV systems in the UK will total 96Megawatts (MW) this year, said iSuppli.
     </p>
     <p>
      This is a significant increase on the 6MW figure for 2009.
     </p>
     <p>
      The next fastest-growing country market is Spain, which is estimated to rise by approximately 730% in 2010.
     </p>
     <p>
      Things definitely are looking brighter for the solar market in the United Kingdom in 2010, as the country has adopted attractive Feed-in-Tariffs (FIT) to promote PV adoption, said Dr. Henning Wicht, senior director and principal analyst for
      <a href="http://www.isuppli.com/Pages/Home.aspx">
       iSuppli
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Furthermore, with leading solar country Germany cutting its FITs, the focus of the PV world is shifting to places with more favourable incentivesmaking the United Kingdom a solar hotspot this year, added Wicht.
     </p>
     <p>
      The market researcher estimates that PV installations will continue to rise in the 50% range for each year through 2014.
     </p>
     <p>
      According to the United Kingdoms Department of Energy and Climate Change, a typical household that installs a well-positioned 2.5 Kilowatt (KW) system could save 140 per year on its electricity bill.
     </p>
     <p>
      Installations conducted since July 2009 can retroactively qualify for the tariff. Returns on Investment (ROIs) for advantageous residential projects can approach 12%, iSuppli calculates.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       See: Sharp,ST, Enel, To Make PV Panels In Sicily
      </strong>
     </p>
