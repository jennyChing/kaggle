        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Dark Chocolate-Caramel Cookie Bars
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-15T20:29:03+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          March 15, 2015
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.bakersroyale.com/author/bakersroyale/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Naomi Robinson
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">

           37 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         Today Im super pumped to have a guest post from
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/162860011X/ref=as_li_ss_til?tag=slipal-20&amp;camp=0&amp;creative=0&amp;linkCode=as4&amp;creativeASIN=162860011X&amp;adid=0Q6TG8079K0DSHF5JQPC">
          cookbook
         </a>
         author,
         <a href="http://slimpalate.com/about-me/">
          Josh
         </a>
         , of
         <a href="http://slimpalate.com/">
          Slim Palate,
         </a>
         who many of you are familiar with, and for those of you who are not, make sure you head over to his awesome
         <a href="http://slimpalate.com/">
          blog
         </a>
         for good eats.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_17992" style="width: 620px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Chocolate-Caramel-Cookie-Bars-Via-Slim-Palate" class="wp-image-17992 size-full" height="915" src="http://bakersroyale.sympledesignllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Chocolate-Caramel-Cookie-Bars-Via-Slim-Palate.jpg" width="610"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.bakersroyale.com/bars-and-cookie-bars/dark-chocolate-caramel-cookie-bars/&amp;media=http://www.bakersroyale.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Chocolate-Caramel-Cookie-Bars-Via-Slim-Palate.jpg&amp;description=Dark Chocolate-Caramel Cookie Bars')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo from Josh Weissman of SlimPalate.com
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         But of course not before you take in this grain-free, gluten free Dark Chocoalte-Caramel Cookie Barthink of this as the healthy version of a Twix Bar. Sure I do a fair share of indulgent treats around, but indulgent treats that are Paleo and/or gluten-free friendly is always something I love working into my rotation of baked goods.
         <span id="more-17990">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         With that, lets do this . . .
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_17991" style="width: 620px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Spreading-Chocolate from slimpalate" class="wp-image-17991 size-full" height="915" src="http://bakersroyale.sympledesignllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spreading-Chocolate-from-slimpalate.jpg" width="610"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.bakersroyale.com/bars-and-cookie-bars/dark-chocolate-caramel-cookie-bars/&amp;media=http://www.bakersroyale.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spreading-Chocolate-from-slimpalate.jpg&amp;description=Dark Chocolate-Caramel Cookie Bars')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo from Josh Weissman of SlimPalate.com
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         So Ive always been a huge fan of Naomis blog so this is a great honor to be able to guest post here. I knew that I had to create something sweet to match the style of this blog so I whipped something up that I think everyone can thoroughly enjoy. Ill give you a hint: its Twix inspired
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_17994" style="width: 620px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Creamed-Butter from slimpalater" class="wp-image-17994 size-full" height="915" src="http://bakersroyale.sympledesignllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Creamed-Butter-from-slimpalater.jpg" width="610"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.bakersroyale.com/bars-and-cookie-bars/dark-chocolate-caramel-cookie-bars/&amp;media=http://www.bakersroyale.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Creamed-Butter-from-slimpalater.jpg&amp;description=Dark Chocolate-Caramel Cookie Bars')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo from Josh Weissman of SlimPalate.com
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         So maybe I gave it away with that onebut I dont think anyone can complain. Its a lot easier to make than you think, and its entirely gluten free, grain free, and refined sugar free. Despite lacking all of these things it still happens to have the familiar crumbly shortbread base, the sweet and salty caramel, and rich chocolate in every bite.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_17995" style="width: 640px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Shortbread Crust and Chocolate Spread via Slim Palate" class="wp-image-17995 size-large" height="472" src="http://bakersroyale.sympledesignllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Shortbread-Crust-and-Chocolate-Spread-via-Slim-Palate-630x472.jpg" width="630"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.bakersroyale.com/bars-and-cookie-bars/dark-chocolate-caramel-cookie-bars/&amp;media=http://www.bakersroyale.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Shortbread-Crust-and-Chocolate-Spread-via-Slim-Palate-630x472.jpg&amp;description=Dark Chocolate-Caramel Cookie Bars')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photos by Photo from Josh Weissman of SlimPalate.com
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         This is all possible with the power of science, and by science I mean simply subbing honey and coconut sugar in place of the regular sugar and corn syrup. You can always use sugar and corn syrup if youd rather but I think that might take away some of the magic behind this recipe.The idea that healthierfoods/ingredients dont taste good is a fallacyand Im here to eliminate that thought.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then again, its still a treat thatstill has lots of sugar in it. Which is probably why it tastes amazing. Oh it also has like a ton of butter in it so, yeah. I have no regrets.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_17993" style="width: 620px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Chocolate-Caramel-Cookie-Bars-From-Slim-Palate" class="wp-image-17993 size-full" height="915" src="http://bakersroyale.sympledesignllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Chocolate-Caramel-Cookie-Bars-From-Slim-Palate.jpg" width="610"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.bakersroyale.com/bars-and-cookie-bars/dark-chocolate-caramel-cookie-bars/&amp;media=http://www.bakersroyale.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Chocolate-Caramel-Cookie-Bars-From-Slim-Palate.jpg&amp;description=Dark Chocolate-Caramel Cookie Bars')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo from Josh Weissman of SlimPalate.com
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Sugar fiends and health nuts unite, enjoy the crumbly, buttery, ooey gooey candy that aremy Dark Chocolate-Caramel Cookie Bars. You wont regret it.
        </p>
        <div class="recipe hrecipe">
         <div class="innerrecipe">
          <img align="right" class="photo" height="125" nopin="nopin" src="http://bakersroyale.sympledesignllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Chocolate-Caramel-Cookie-Bars-Via-Slim-Palate-210x260.jpg" width="125"/>
          <p class="printbutton">
           <a href="http://www.bakersroyale.com/bars-and-cookie-bars/dark-chocolate-caramel-cookie-bars//print/" title="Print Recipe">
            <img alt="Print" src="http://bakersroyale.sympledesignllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/themes/bakersroyale2013/images/print.png"/>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Print
            </strong>

           </a>
          </p>
          <span class="item">
           <h2 class="fn">
            Dark Chocolate-Caramel Cookie Bars
           </h2>
          </span>
          <div class="time">
           <p>
            <strong>
             Yield:
            </strong>
            <span class="yield">
             Serves 6
            </span>
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="left">
           <h3 style="clear:left">
            Ingredients:
           </h3>
           <div class="ingredient">
            <p>
             Shortbread Base:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              2 cups almond flour
             </li>
             <li>
              1 cup tapioca flour
             </li>
             <li>
              1 cup softened butter plus extra for greasing
             </li>
             <li>
              2/3 cup coconut sugar
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 teaspoon salt
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             Caramel Middle:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              3/4 cup coconut sugar
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 cup honey
             </li>
             <li>
              3 tablespoon butter or ghee
             </li>
             <li>
              3/4 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 teaspoon vanilla
             </li>
             <li>
              1/4 teaspoon salt
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             Chocolate top:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              10 1/2 ounces dark chocolate, roughly chopped (anywhere around 70%)
             </li>
            </ul>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="right">
           <h3 style="clear:left">
            Directions:
           </h3>
           <div class="instructions">
            <p>
             Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Line a 9x13 baking pan with parchment in both directions, leaving some overhang. Grease the parchment with butter.
            </p>
            <p>
             In a media medium sized bowl whisk together the almond flour, tapioca flour and salt. In a stand mixer begin making the shortbread crust by adding the softened butter, and coconut sugar and creaming it on medium high until you get a frosting like consistency. While beating slowly add the almond flour and tapioca flour and continue to beat on medium high until it forms a dough. Press the dough evenly into the prepared baking pan.
            </p>
            <p>
             Place the shortbread into the oven for about 20-25 minutes or until lightly browned. Remove from the oven and allow to cool.
            </p>
            <p>
             In a medium sized heavy bottomed pot fitted with a candy thermometer add the coconut sugar, honey, 2 tablespoons of the butter or ghee, heavy cream or coconut milk, vanilla, and salt. Bring to a boil and lower the heat to about medium low-medium to hold a nice boil without boiling over. Bring it to about 265 degrees and immediately take it off the heat.
            </p>
            <p>
             Pour the caramel over the cooked and cooled shortbread crust and quickly spread it around to cover the whole top. Allow to sit for 30 minutes or until the caramel is no longer liquid or warm to the touch.
            </p>
            <p>
             Place the chocolate in a medium sized microwaveable bowl and microwave on high at 30-second intervals, stirring in between intervals, until completely melted. Alternatively you can melt it in a double boiler, stirring often, until completely melted.
            </p>
            <p>
             Pour the melted chocolate over the caramel part and spread it around with a frosting knife or spatula.
            </p>
            <p>
             Allow to cool for 30 minutes to an hour, until the chocolate is completely solid. Remove the candy from the baking tray and slice into bars with a sharp knife. Store in an airtight container or serve immediately. These will last you about 1 1/2- 2 weeks.
            </p>
            <p>
             Tips: When using your candy thermometer, make sure that it is only in the liquid and not touching the bottom of your pot.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>

             <p>
              Looks like there was a glitch on Naomis site but Im getting in touch with her. In the meantime here is the rest of the recipe:
             </p>
             <p>
              Pour the caramel over the cooked and cooled shortbread crust and quickly spread it around to cover the whole top. Allow to sit for 30 minutes or until the caramel is no longer liquid or warm to the touch.
             </p>
             <p>
              Place the chocolate in a medium sized microwaveable bowl and microwave on high at 30-second intervals, stirring in between intervals, until completely melted. Alternatively you can melt it in a double boiler, stirring often, until completely melted.
             </p>
             <p>
              Pour the melted chocolate over the caramel part and spread it around with a frosting knife or spatula.
             </p>
             <p>
              Allow to cool for 30 minutes to an hour, until the chocolate is completely solid. Remove the candy from the baking tray and slice into bars with a sharp knife. Store in an airtight container or serve immediately. These will last you about 1 1/2- 2 weeks.
             </p>
             <p>
              Tips: When using your candy thermometer, make sure that it is only in the liquid and not touching the bottom of your pot.

             <p>
              Pour the caramel over the cooked and cooled shortbread crust and quickly spread it around to cover the whole top. Allow to sit for 30 minutes or until the caramel is no longer liquid or warm to the touch.
              <br/>
              Place the chocolate in a medium sized microwaveable bowl and microwave on high at 30-second intervals, stirring in between intervals, until completely melted. Alternatively you can melt it in a double boiler, stirring often, until completely melted.

              Pour the melted chocolate over the caramel part and spread it around with a frosting knife or spatula.
              <br/>
              Allow to cool for 30 minutes to an hour, until the chocolate is completely solid. Remove the candy from the baking tray and slice into bars with a sharp knife. Store in an airtight container or serve immediately. These will last you about 1 1/2- 2 weeks.
