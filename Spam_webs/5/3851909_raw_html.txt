       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Salmon Tacos with Avocado Crema
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-07-09T07:00:24+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          July 9, 2014
         </time>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-color="gray" data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonPin" data-pin-height="28" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Frealfoodandicecream.com%2Fsalmon-tacos-avocado-crema%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Frealfoodmommd.com%2Fcontent%2Fuploads%2F2014%2F07%2Fsalmon-tacos-with-avocado-crema.jpg&amp;description=Salmon%20Tacos%20with%20Avocado%20Crema%20-%20smoky%20salmon%20meets%20creamy%20avocado%20in%20a%20tortilla.%20397%20calories." rel="nobox">
          <img data-pib-button="true" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <img alt="Salmon Tacos with Avocado Crema - smoky salmon meets creamy avocado in a tortilla. 397 calories. | realfoodmommd.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-86 size-full pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://realfoodandicecream.com/salmon-tacos-avocado-crema/" height="912" src="http://realfoodmommd.com/content/uploads/2014/07/salmon-tacos-with-avocado-crema.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Meet your next taco Tuesday.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sounds fancy, tastes delicious but easy peasy to make. Thats the way I roll in the kitchen. These are so simple that you could ACTUALLY make them on a Tuesday because they take a mere thirty minutes to get to the table. And I dont mean that in a Rachael Ray
         <span style="text-decoration: line-through;">
          two hours
         </span>
         30 Minute Meal kind of way.
        </p>
        <p>
         Please tell me Im not the only one who feels like they might lose a finger if they actually chopped fast enough to make one of her meals in thirty minutes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Salmon Tacos with Avocado Crema | realfoodmommd.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-85 size-full pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://realfoodandicecream.com/salmon-tacos-avocado-crema/" height="854" src="http://realfoodmommd.com/content/uploads/2014/07/salmon-tacos-with-avocado-crema-stack.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         All lies validating my cooking skills will be accepted in the comments below, email, Facebook, Twitter
        </p>
        <p>
         No, really, I have mad love for Rachael Ray. When I first started cooking, she was a huge role model for me. And now she also has a non-profit organization,
         <a href="http://yum-o.org/index.php" target="_blank">
          yum-o
         </a>
         , which teaches kids about healthy food and cooking. Right up my alley.
        </p>
        <p>
         But back to the tacos, made with ingredients that I also have mad love for. Were starting with salmon: most delicious fish to ever swim the sea. Topping it with avocado: awesomest fruit disguised as a vegetable (fregetable?). And dare I mention the bright, beautiful cilantro?
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Salmon Tacos with Avocado Crema | realfoodmommd.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-84 size-full pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://realfoodandicecream.com/salmon-tacos-avocado-crema/" height="872" src="http://realfoodmommd.com/content/uploads/2014/07/salmon-tacos-with-avocado-crema-lime-avocado.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I know there are cilantro haters in this world. I officially forgive you; apparently its in your
         <a href="http://www.npr.org/blogs/thesalt/2012/09/14/161057954/love-to-hate-cilantro-its-in-your-genes-and-maybe-in-your-head" target="_blank">
          genes
         </a>
         . Just leave it out of the recipe and we can all still be friends.
        </p>
        <p>
         Which is another GREAT thing about this recipe. Soooo easy to modify for the tastes of your family. I dont like to play short order cook at dinnertime, but I realize everyone has preferences. Want to add some cheese? Great. Not a sour cream fan? Add some sliced avocado instead. Youre my 4 year-old daughter who will only eat plain tortillas? Oy.
        </p>
        <p>
         I, however, will be eating these in all their smoky salmon, creamy avocado, fresh veggie deliciousness, with a lime-infused beverage, of course.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Salmon Tacos with Avocado Crema | realfoodmommd.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-83 size-full pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://realfoodandicecream.com/salmon-tacos-avocado-crema/" height="855" src="http://realfoodmommd.com/content/uploads/2014/07/salmon-tacos-with-avocado-crema-beer.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           INGREDIENTS
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 corn tortillas
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             Tbsp. olive oil
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Salmon:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             lb. salmon filet
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. smoked paprika
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. garlic powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. onion powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. chipotle chili powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. cumin
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. pepper
            <br/>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Salsa:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup corn kernels (I used frozen)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup diced tomato
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup chopped cilantro
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tbsp. lime juice
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. salt
            <br/>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Crema:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup sour cream
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 avocado
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tbsp. lime juice
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. salt
            <br/>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           INSTRUCTIONS
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Take the salmon out of the refrigerator and bring to room temperature for 10 minutes. Mix together the spices for the salmon rub.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            While the salmon is warming, begin making the salsa. If using frozen corn, measure into a microwave safe bowl and defrost for 30-60 seconds in the microwave. Dice the tomato and chop the cilantro. Mix the corn, tomato, cilantro, 1 Tbsp. lime juice and  tsp. salt. Set aside.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pour the olive oil into the pan. While the oil heats, dry the salmon with paper towels, then rub the spice mixture onto both sides of the salmon. Once the oil is hot, place the salmon in the pan and cook for 4 minutes on one side. Turn and cook for an additional 3 minutes on the other side. After cooking, set aside for 5-10 minutes.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            While the salmon is resting, make the
            <a href="http://iowagirleats.com/2014/04/25/chipotle-cheddar-turkey-burgers-with-avocado-crema/" target="_blank">
             avocado crema
            </a>
            .
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Heat the tortillas in the microwave according to the package directions. Flake the salmon and place into the tortillas, top with the salsa and crema and enjoy!
           </li>
          </ol>

           NOTES

          <div class="ERSNotes">
           This makes for a slightly spicy taco filling. If you are not a fan of spice or you are preparing for kids, swap out the chipotle chili powder for regular chili powder and omit the pepper. This will make a very mild salmon.
