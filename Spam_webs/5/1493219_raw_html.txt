             <span style="color: #666699;">
              LADAKH  BEST 14 EXPERIENCES
             </span>
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              1. Be transported to a land of isolated beauty at Zanskar valley
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Zanskar  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Visit Phuktal Monastery, Experience the unique Muslim-Budhhist culture, Go on Chadar trek on frozen Zanskar river
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             July-Oct
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11975" style="width: 632px">
             <img alt="The famous Chadar trek goes on during winters on the frozen Zanskar River" class="size-full wp-image-11975" height="473" src="http://46.51.217.6/blog/bloggermaster/uploads/2013/12/The-famous-Chadar-trek-goes-on-during-winters-on-the-frozen-Zanskar-River.-This-trek-is-considered-to-be-one-of-the-most-diffic.jpg" width="630"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              The famous Chadar trek goes on during winters on the frozen Zanskar River
             </p>
            </div>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             Zanskar valley (3500m to 7000m height) has a prestigious position in the trekking map of India. This place is very isolated and scantily habited. There are many trekking trails in this valley like the Chadar trail where you have to trek on the frozen Zanskar River in winters.Then there is the Padum Lamayuru Trek that goes through high passes and vast expanse of rocky plains dotted with monasteries precariously perched on hillocks and ridges with multi coloured prayer flags. The little, quaint villages in the middle of nowhere make you wonder about the lifestyle of the Zanskari people. This high altitude, arid and rocky landscape is a heaven for adventure seekers.
            </p>
            <h3>
            </h3>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              2. Tap to the beats of Ladakhi masked dancers during Hemis Festival
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Leh  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Culture
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Heritage walks around Leh, Mountaineering, Visit Shey and Thiksey Gompas
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             June-July
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Leh_Ladakh" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/40-new.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Photo Courtesy  MahindraHomeStays
             </p>
            </div>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             Hemis Gompa is the biggest monastery in Ladakh. Courtyard of this otherwise silent and peaceful monastery becomes an open air theatre and buzzes with the sounds of cymbals, drums and gigantic wind pipes during Hemis festival. This festival is celebrated to venerate the birth of Guru Rinpoche, the founder of Buddhism in this region. The lamas perform the mystical chaam dance wearing colourful attire and masks depicting varied emotions. This festival carries on for two days. Once in every twelve years in the Tibetan year of monkey the bejeweled, two storey thangka of Guru Rinpoche is unfurled during this festival. The experience gained during these two days is almost beautiful like a dream and is to be treasured for ever. Read more about planning your
             <a href="http://www.rltgo.com/india-travel/ladakh-trip/">
              trip to Ladak
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <h3>
            </h3>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              3. Follow the silk route on a bicycle across Khardung La
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Khardung La  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Heritage walk around Leh, Trekking
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11976" style="width: 632px">
             <img alt="Approaching Khardung La  Photo Credit - Rajarshi Dutta" class="size-full wp-image-11976" height="422" src="http://46.51.217.6/blog/bloggermaster/uploads/2013/12/Approaching-Khardung-La-Credit-Rajarshi-Dutta.jpg" width="630"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Approaching Khardung La
              <br/>
              Photo Credit  Rajarshi Dutta
             </p>
            </div>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             Many people have crossed the Khardung La on motorbikes and SUVs, but how many can claim to have done that on a cycle? Cycling in the highest motorable road in the world has its own challenges and charms and features on every adventure enthusiasts
             <strong>
              
              <a href="Top 10 Best Destinations for 2014">
               things to do in Himalayas
              </a>
              
             </strong>
             list. You may go on a cycling expedition which starts at Srinagar or Manali and cycle your way up to Leh. Alternately you can cycle downhill from Khardung La to Leh which will take only few hours. Both ways great views and surreal landscape accompany you in the journey. It is a treacherous task. But once completed gives you immense satisfaction and a precious experience that you can treasure for life.
            </p>
            <h3>
            </h3>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              4. Witness the rare dual between sand dunes and snow peaks
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Hundar &amp; Diskit  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Bactrian camel safari in Nubra valley, Visit Lamayuru
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Hundar &amp; Diskit - Ladakh" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/43-new.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Photo Courtesy  Wikipedia
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             Nubra valley in Ladakh has an average elevation of around 3000m. Because of very little rain fall the land here is barren and desert like. Hundar and Diskit are the two villages in this valley. Between these two villages there are several kilometres of sand dunes. Here you can see sand dunes and tall snowy massifs at the same time. This is one of the rarest of rare natural wonders. You can also find double humped Bactrian camels in this area as if to complete the surreal picture. While trekking in this region you will feel like being transported to another world in another time.
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              5. Admire the perak head dress in Stok palace
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Stok  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Culture
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Go on a heritage walk, Visit Stok gompa, Visit Leh
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Stok - Ladakh" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/44-new.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Photo Courtesy  ColumbusMagzine
             </p>
            </div>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             Built in the early 1800s Stok Palace is 17 km away from Leh. It is also a museum that houses a big collection of thangkas, old weapons, coins, seals, antique items used in religious rituals and royal ornaments. In this collection the main objects of attraction are the peraks or the queens head dresses studded with turquoise, emerald and other semi precious stones. The queen still uses some of these ornaments during various ceremonies. As this palace is still being used by the royal family of Leh, only a portion of it is open to the general public. But it does manage to give the onlooker a feel of the regal lifestyle in Ladakh.
            </p>
            <h3>
            </h3>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              6. Meet the Drokpas lost descendents of Alexander at Dha and Hanu
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Dha Hanu  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Culture
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Visit Lamayuru, Trekking in Nubra valley
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Dha Hanu - Ladakh" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/45.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Dha Hanu  Ladakh
              <br/>
              Photo Courtesy  UsFotoNews
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             The Drokpa community of Ladakh is considered to be of Aryan lineage. There are five villages of this community around 165km northwest of Leh, but only two villages, Dha and Hanu are open to tourists. It is said that their forefathers came to India with Alexander and settled in this valley. Their distinctive Indo-Aryan physical features, cultural and religious practices are different from those of the surrounding Ladakhi people. When you meet these people the first thing that catches your imagination is the elaborate head dress of Drokpa women made from fresh flower. Till now they have managed to uphold their cultural identity due to the remoteness of this region.
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              7. Wander in the alien landscape of Lamayuru
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Lamayuru  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Visit Dha Hanu, Trekking in Zanskar valley
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/46.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Lamayuru  Ladakh
              <br/>
              Photo Courtesy  WikiMedia
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             Lamayuru is a small village situated in western Ladakh at an altitude of 3500m. The distinguishing feature of the village is the landscape of the valley. There are sediment rock formations in different shapes which give a cratered look to the land here and hence this place is also called moon land. According to a local legend there used to be a lake in the valley. A Buddhist yogi Mahasiddha Naropa dried up the lake and these sediment rock formations became visible. Geologists may want to differ. But whatever may be the reason of formation, this strange landscape invokes the feeling of being on another planet which is a must to experience amongst other
             <strong>
              things to do in Himalayas
             </strong>

            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              8. Admire rare paintings in one of the worlds oldest monastery
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Alchi  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Culture
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Trek in Nubra valley, Visit Leh
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Alchi - Ladakh" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/47.1.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Alchi  Ladakh
              <br/>
              Photo Courtesy  IndiaTourismPortal
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             Alchi monastery was built in the 10th century. There is a very identifiable Indian touch in the frescos and murals of this monastery as artists from the Kashmir valley were employed for the work. The exquisitely painted Green Tara and Wheel of Life are surprisingly well preserved even after so many centuries. Dukhang is the oldest temple in the monastery having an elaborate wooden gateway with tiny Bodhisattvas. It is an interesting monument for archeologists as well as tourists.
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              9. Have a photo stop while traveling in the Suru valley at Panikhar
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Panikhar  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Biking in the Rangdum plains, Visit Rangdum Gompa, Trek to Parkachik glacier in the Nun Kun slopes
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Panikhar_Ladakh" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/37-new.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Panikhar Ladakh
              <br/>
              Photo Courtesy  TrekEarth
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             Suru valley is undoubtedly the most beautiful and the greenest valley in the whole of Ladakh. Watered by melting snow this valley is also the most cultivated area in Ladakh. The flowing grassy slopes of the mountains, the serpentine Suru River and the clearly visible yet distant white peaks make this place completely picture perfect. In June and July the landscape around Panikhar becomes the cradle for rainbow coloured blossoms. The bright summer sun glistening on the seemingly touchable Nun (7135 m) and Kun (7077 m) massifs enhances the charm of this place manifold. Whether you are a photographer, traveller or a nature lover a trip to this piece of heaven at least once in your life time is a must.
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              10. Ride your bike along with wild horses in the Rangdum plains
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Rangdum  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Visit Rangdum Gompa, Trek to Parkachik glacier in the Nun Kun slopes
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Rangdum_Ladakh" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/38-new.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Rangdum, Ladakh
              <br/>
              Photo Courtesy  BharatDarshan
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             The Rangdum plain at a height of 3657 m from the sea level is one of the most remote and yet most enchanting places in Ladakh. On the way from Kargil to Padum this valley is in the middle of a 245 km biking trail. The most striking feature of this place is wild horses. You can spot many of these horses grazing languidly in the vast grassland or roaming on the road itself. This is a rare sight in India. The presence of these magnificent wild beasts along with the begirding mountains in varied colours gives this place a surreal feeling that must be experienced to be believed.This is one of the most popular
             <strong>
              things to do in India.
             </strong>
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em; color: #ff9900;">
              11. Camp in an apricot orchard on the banks of Indus River at Uleytokpo
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Uleytokpo  Ladakh
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Visit Ridzong, Likir, Phyang Monasteries
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/41-new.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Uleytokpo, Ladakh
              <br/>
              Photo Courtesy  GreenCarpetClub
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details:
             </strong>
             Situated more than 3000 m above the sea level, the small Uleytokpo village is famous for its apricot orchards. During spring and summer you can see bunches of apricot swinging from the feeble branches of the trees. While the Indus River twists and turns in this cold desert, the barren landscape of the place enchants and amazes you.              </strong>
             Bumchu literally means pot of water. There is a sacred pot of water in the Tashiding Monastery which is kept locked. The level of water in this pot is considered to be an indicator of prosperity in the coming year. On the 14th and 15th day of Tibetan first month which falls around February or March after long prayers and chanting, this pot is taken out and the water level is checked. Then the holy water is distributed among devotees and again filled with fresh water and locked away for a year. This is one of the most important festivals in Sikkim. People come from far off places to participate in this festival. They camp outside the monastery and wait for hours for their turn to be blessed patiently. The unfaltering faith and undying devotion have to be seen to be believed. The surreal sacredness of the monastery increases during the festival. It is a must to celebrate Bumchu to experience the real of Sikkimese lifestyle.

             Lukla (2800m) is the starting point of the trek. On your way you will come across many quaint hamlets and witness authentic Nepali lifestyle. The lively Sherpa heartland Namche Bazaar is a days hike from Lukla along the milky blue Dudh Koshi River. The milestones that you must cross are the water driven prayer wheels at Phunki Thanga, the nunnery at Deboche and the tiny frozen lake at Gorak Shep. At the end of the trail along the Khumbu glacier awaits Kalapathar (5545m) with the rewarding view of Mt Everest flanked by other peaks like Lhotse, Nuptse and Changtse. The towering peak makes you feel little and insignificant but you can still keep on watching it for the entire eternity; such is the magnetism of Mt Everest.This is one of the most popular
             <strong>
              things to do in Himalayas
             </strong>
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              94. Trek in the least discovered Ilam district of Nepal
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Ilam  Nepal
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Visit Kathmandu, Nagarkot
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Ilam - Nepal" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/55.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Photo Courtesy:WikiMedia
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details :
             </strong>
             Ilam (3,636m), a border district between Nepal and India is famous for rolling tea estate and cardamom plantations. But not many people know about the beautiful trekking trails in this region. The views of the eastern Himalayas at different vantage points are mesmerizing. Shree Antu(2328m), Maipokhari (2121m), Siddhi Thumka (1800m) and Chhintapu (3353m) offer engrossing sunsets and sunrises. If you want you may experience Nepali hospitality in a home stay at Shree Antu. And Chhintapu is particularly well known for great sights of Mt Kanchenjunga. If you are looking for least explored destinations then this land of exotic animals like musk deer and red pandas is perfect for you.
            </p>
            <h3>
            </h3>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              95. Watch sunrise with worlds highest mountains peaks at Nagarkot
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Nagarkot  Nepal
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Visit Kathmandu, Trekking in Ilam district
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Summer
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Nagarkot - Nepal" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/56.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Photo Courtesy:WikiMedia
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details :
             </strong>
             After a journey of 32km from Kathmandu and an over night stay you can witness the best spectacle in your life. Awe inspiring, sunbathed view of Dhaulagiri in the west, Everest in the middle and Kanchenjunga in the east can be witnessed only at Nagarkot. Sun slowly rises from behind the tallest, snow blanketed massifs. It seems as if the peaks are waiting patiently for their turn to be touched by the stream of sun rays. A magnificent play of shadow and light is unfurled. You will want the moment to freeze. Trip to Nepal is never complete without a sunrise at Nagarkot.
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">
              96. Go on the Annapurna Base Camp trek
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Place:
             </strong>
             Annapurn Trek  Nepal
             <strong>
              Category:
             </strong>
             Adventure
             <strong>
              Experiences Nearby:
             </strong>
             Visit Mahendra Gupha, Boating in Phewa lake
             <strong>
              Best Time:
             </strong>
             Spring &amp; autumn
            </p>
            <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="" style="width: 623px">
             <img alt="Annapurn Trek - Nepal" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/57.jpg" width="621"/>
             <p class="wp-caption-text">
              Photo Courtesy:WikiMedia
             </p>
            </div>
            <h6>
            </h6>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Details :
             </strong>
             The Annapurna base camp trail (maximum height 4170m) is one of the best trails in the world with tea houses. Tea house trekking means evenings and nights are spent in lodges called tea houses present all along your trekking trail. The trekking starts from Pokhara and passes along side worlds deepest gorges and tallest peaks. After each days trekking you can rest in comfortable tea houses and so there is no need to camp outside. In the evenings you can explore the villages you are staying at and mingle with the locals who are mostly Gurungs and Magars. The trail ends at the Annapurna base camp which offers magnificent views of Annapurna I, Annapurna III, Annapurna south, Himchuli, tent peak, Gangapurna, fluted fishtail.
            </p>
            <p class="package">
             <a name="Tibet_Packages">
             </a>
             <span style="color: #666699;">
              TIBET  4 BEST TRAVEL IDEAS
             </span>
             0&gt;
            </p>
            <div class="activitiesCont" id="summerExperiences">
             <h3>
              <span style="color: #ff9900;">
               97. Witness Tibetan history in Potala palace at Lhasa (3612m)
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Place:
              </strong>
              Lhasa  Tibet
              <strong>
               Category:
              </strong>
              Culture
              <strong>
               Experiences Nearby:
              </strong>
              Visit Jokhand shrine, Shop at Barkor street
              <strong>
               Best Time:
              </strong>
              Summer
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5139" style="width: 623px">
              <img alt="Lhasa - Tibet" class="size-full wp-image-5139" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/58.jpg" width="621"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo Courtesy:Flickr/archer10
              </p>
             </div>
             <h6 style="text-align: right; color: orange;">
             </h6>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Details :
              </strong>
              The 13-story and 117m high Potala palace has 1000 rooms and was the economic, political and cultural nerve centre of Tibet till recently. A treasure house of ancient sculptures, murals, scripture, Buddha figures and religious jewellery, this palace gives an amazing insight into Tibetan history. The red and white building in the backdrop of blue sky creates a formidable picture. The five massive bejeweled golden stupas of the western chapel contain bodies of Dalai Lamas of yester years. Your imagination will travel back in time on visiting this magnificent palace.
             </p>
             <h3>
             </h3>
             <h3>
              <span style="color: #ff9900;">
               98. Enjoy Tibetan opera during Shoton festival at Norbu Linka, Lhasa
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Place:
              </strong>
              Norbu linka  Tibet
              <strong>
               Category:
              </strong>
              Culture
              <strong>
               Experiences Nearby:
              </strong>
              Visit Potala palace, Jokhand shrine, Shop at Barkhor street
              <strong>
               Best Time:
              </strong>
              August
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5139" style="width: 623px">
              <img alt="Norbu linka - Tibet" class="size-full wp-image-5139" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/059.jpg" width="621"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo Courtesy:WikiMedia
              </p>
             </div>
             <h6 style="text-align: right; color: orange;">
             </h6>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Details :
              </strong>
              When Lamas return from their rigorous training as a monk their families welcome them with cream, song and dance during the Shoton or yogurt banquet festival. Norbu Linka or the treasure garden palace has been the site of Tibetan opera performances during Shoton festival since the 17th century. Traditional Tibetan opera groups from across the country compete with each other. To watch these epic performances in an open air theater while gorging on Tibetan yogurt is like having a glimpse of the bygone era.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <span style="color: #ff9900;">
               99. Trek from Rongbuk Monastery to Mt. Everest base camp
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Place:
              </strong>
              Shigatse  Tibet
              <strong>
               Category:
              </strong>
              Adventure
              <strong>
               Experiences Nearby:
              </strong>
              Visit Tashilhunpo Monastery, seat of the panchen lamas
              <strong>
               Best Time:
              </strong>
              Summer
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5139" style="width: 623px">
              <img alt="Shigatse - Tibet" class="size-full wp-image-5139" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/60.jpg" width="621"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo Courtesy:WikiMedia
              </p>
             </div>
             <h6 style="text-align: right; color: orange;">
             </h6>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Details :
              </strong>
              The highest monstsery in the world Rongbuk (4980m) is just 8km away from Mt Everest base camp (5545m). From the monastery the view of the northern side of Mt Everest is like a mirage as if you can reach out and touch it. Mt Everest sits there like a white clad saint and beckons you. Even if you can not reach the peak you may try to reach the base camp while trekking through the majestic Himalayas. The snow carved peaks give you company throughout your journey. Spend a night in a tent at the base camp. The place and the experience heighten your senses so much that even a simple omelette will taste like gourmet food. And you can not ask for more if its a moonlit night. The view of Everest in the bluish moonlight is unparallel to any experience in your life. Next morning you will feel like as if you can sit and watch the yellow-orange peak forever.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <span style="color: #ff9900;">
               100. Visit the mysterious and holy Nam Co Lake
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Place:
              </strong>
              Nam Co  Tibet
              <strong>
               Category:
              </strong>
              Adventure
              <strong>
               Experiences Nearby:
              </strong>
              Visit Lhasa and Shigatse
              <strong>
               Best Time:
              </strong>
              Summer
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5139" style="width: 623px">
              <img alt="Nam Co - Tibet" class="size-full wp-image-5139" height="298" src="http://www.rltgo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/61-new.jpg" width="621"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo Courtesy:Flickr/35322527@N08
              </p>
             </div>
             <h6 style="text-align: right; color: orange;">
             </h6>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Details :
              </strong>
              The Nam Co Lake is the largest salt lake in Tibet. It is situated at a height of 4718m at the foot of Nyainqentanglha Mountain and is sprawled across 1920 square kilometer. It is one of the four holy lakes for Buddhists. It is hugely auspicious to go around the lake in clockwise direction at a particular time of the year. So you can see a path worn out in the lakeshore because of this ritual walk. There are many islands in the lake. The Zhaxiduo Island is especially enchanting with its forest of strange rocks and ancient cave hermitages. The serene, austere beauty of the lake increases manifold by the grazing yaks and the colourful Buddhist flags fluttering in the wind. If you are tempted you may camp beside the lake for overnight and be welcomed by the ethereal beauty of the lake during sunrise.
