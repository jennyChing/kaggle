           <div class="art-post-inner art-article">
            <h1 class="art-postheader">
             My TV Debut + Felt Gift Bow DIY Tutorial
            </h1>
            <div class="art-postheadericons art-metadata-icons">
             <span class="art-postdateicon">
              <span class="date">
              </span>
              <span class="entry-date" title="9:38 pm">
               December 17, 2010
              </span>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="art-postcontent">
             <!-- article-content -->
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/manualidades.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/manualidades2.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/manualidades5.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Today was my first TV debut!!! Mayis on the air baby! Martha Stewart here I come!
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              My heart is still pumping like if a cobra was chasing me through the jungle + Im loving the adrenaline rush.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              I really really have to thank Alex Medela, one of the hosts of
              <em>
               Tu Maana
              </em>
              , for inviting me on to do my thing. I had such a blast + I want to do it again + again + again. So, Im crossing my fingers for a repeat.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/regalograss.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              So what did I do? I made a super fun + crafty + eco-friendly holiday DIY tutorial  an
              <strong>
               eco-felt gift bow
              </strong>
              . They are so easy to make + so cute to wear. And as opposed to its paper ribbon counter part, this bow actually stays in place + doesnt get smashed on the way to grandmas lol.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              So let me show you how to make them
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <span style="color: #888888;">
               <strong>
                <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
                 materials
                </span>
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h2>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Felt (eco-friendly preferably)
              </li>
              <li>
               Scissors
              </li>
              <li>
               Needle
              </li>
              <li>
               Thread
              </li>
              <li>
               Ruler
              </li>
              <li>
               Silicon glue
              </li>
              <li>
               Pin (optional)
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               <strong>
                <span style="color: #888888;">
                 step by step
                </span>
                <br/>
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h2>
             <p>
              After gathering your materials
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/regalo22.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Step 1: Cut a felt page lengthwise into 9 strips. Each strip should measure 3/4 inches wide. Leave 3 strips full length, 8.5 inches long + cut 1 inch off 3 of the strips + cut 2 inches off 2 of the strips + cut the last strip down to 3 1/2 inches long.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/regalo6.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/regalo5.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/regalo3.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Step 2: Twist each strip to form a loop at both ends + sew it in the center. Shape the shortest strip into a circle + secure it with a glue dot.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Step 3: Layer the strips from longest to smallest, making sure you space each set evenly + carefully. Finally, stick the loop into the center. Once they are grouped sew all parts together.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/regalo15.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Youre done. You can use the bow as is for gift wrapping or you can add a pin to the back to wear it with your favorite blouse.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/regalo9.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <img alt="" src="http://mayicarles.com/heartmadeblog/wp-content/uploads/images/stories/regalo8.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #888888;">
               <strong>
                alternatives
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h2>
             <p>
              You can transform this bow into a headband + clip + comb or anything else youd like. You can also make them with recycled paper, like a map + magazine + newspaper or vintage paper for a nice variation.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Neat project dont you think?
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Well lovelies, its time to punch out. Work overload has been severe this week + this girls gotta rest + drink some wine + cuddlle with David. Doctors orders.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Ill probably post some weekend links tomorrow to be consequent with my editorial calendar. Till then, ta ta sweeties!
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              xo,
              <br/>
              Mayi Carles
             </p>
