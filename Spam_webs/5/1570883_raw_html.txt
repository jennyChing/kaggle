          </p>
          <p>

          <p>
           Are paranormal beings real?
           <b>
           </b>

          </p>

          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: center;">
           6 Filipino Paranormal Beings Uncovered
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            6. Santelmo
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           These are glowing orbs that float in the air, believed to be unsettled spirits of dead people. There have been instances when santelmos have been unexpectedly captured on camera. Some witnesses claim that these orbs seem to consistently appear in their photos, often causing witnesses worry and distress.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-144670" data-lazy-src="http://s2.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Santelmo.jpg" height="306" src="http://s3.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="339"/>
          </p>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-144670" height="306" src="http://s2.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Santelmo.jpg" width="339"/>
          </noscript>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           Photo from rense.com
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
          </p>
          <p>
           &gt;UNCOVERED: This is also known as St. Elmos Fire. According to science, this is a weather phenomenon caused by an electronically charged atmosphere, similar to that of lightning. It is composed of plasma or ionized air.
          </p>
          <p>
           Sources:
           <em>
            From the Profound to the Profane: A Collection of Controversial and Thought-Provoking Articles
           </em>
           by Jaime T. Licauco
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            What is St. Elmos Fire?
           </em>
           by Julia Layton. Retrieved from http://science.howstuffworks.com
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           <b>
            5. Kapre
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Kapre is a hairy gigantic demon that lives in old enormous trees. He is a chain-smoker who is said to emit a stench.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter wp-image-144668" data-lazy-src="http://s2.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Kapre.jpg" height="400" src="http://s3.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="720"/>
          </p>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter wp-image-144668" height="400" src="http://s2.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Kapre.jpg" width="720"/>
          </noscript>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           Photo fromhttp://renzzero.deviantart.com
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
          </p>
          <p>
           &gt;UNCOVERED: During the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines, Spaniards brought in numerous African slaves, who were reported to be imported from Latin America. To dissuade Filipinos from interacting with the African slaves, some historians believe that the legend of the Kapre was spread by the conquistadors. Interestingly, the word Kapre is derived from the Arabic kafir (a non-believer of Islam). Northwestern African Muslim people used it to refer to dark-skinned non-Muslim Dravidians.
          </p>
          <p>
           Source:
           <em>
            Monsters and Creatures in Philippine Myths and Folklores
           </em>
           by Philurbanlegends. Retrieved from http://philurbanlegends.blogspot.com
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            4. Tiyanak
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           It is said that the Tiyanak is a creature that pretends to be an innocent baby, crying innocently to attract its unsuspecting victim. Once the victims attention is acquired, the Tiyanak does not hesitate to devour him or her. According to folklore, Tiyanaks are babies who died without being baptized, thereby morphing into evil spirits.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-144672" data-lazy-src="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Tiyanak.png" height="282" src="http://s3.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="503"/>
          </p>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-144672" height="282" src="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Tiyanak.png" width="503"/>
          </noscript>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           Photo fromhttp://boogeymen.wikia.com
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           &gt; UNCOVERED: In the 1500s, the Spaniards strategically used religion to colonize the Philippines. According to some historians, the Tiyanak legend was spread in order to persuade people to be baptized as Christians.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           Sources:
           <em>
            I Juander: Tiyanak sa Bayan ni Juan?
           </em>
           byGMA News. Retrieved from https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c82xxVkppC8
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           <em>
            The Tikbalang Human-Horse by Philippine Mythical Creatures
           </em>
           . Retrieved from http://philippinemythicalcreatures.blogspot.com
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           <b>
            3. Tikbalang
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Tikbalang is a creature that is half-horse and half-man. It lurks in the forests and often leads travellers astray.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter wp-image-144671" data-lazy-src="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Tikbalang.jpg" height="300" src="http://s3.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="650"/>
          </p>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter wp-image-144671" height="300" src="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Tikbalang.jpg" width="650"/>
          </noscript>
          Photo from http://johnpatsakios.deviantart.com
          <p style="text-align: center;">
          </p>
          <p>
           &gt;UNCOVERED: The origin of the Tikbalang can be traced back to the Spanish colonization period. The Spaniards spread this myth in order to dissuade the natives from going out at night.
          </p>
          <p>
           Source:
           <em>
            Tikbalang Mystery in the Philippines
           </em>
           by Cotopher. Retrieved from http://www.studymode.com
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            2. Manananggal
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           A man-eating witch that is characterized by its ability to separate its upper and lower torso, the Manananggal also has big, bat-like wings. At night, its upper torso hunts for victims, while its lower torso stays stationary.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Manananggal.jpg" rel="lightbox[144666]">
            <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter wp-image-144669" data-lazy-src="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Manananggal.jpg" height="350" src="http://s3.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="640"/>
            <noscript>
             <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter wp-image-144669" height="350" src="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Manananggal.jpg" width="640"/>
            </noscript>
           </a>
           Photo fromhttps://ph.she.yahoo.com
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
          </p>
          <p>
           &gt;UNCOVERED: Before the Spaniards arrived in the Philippines, Filipino women had sexual freedom and power in their own communities. It was common for women to walk around topless. Some historians believe that the manananggals separation at the waist was an attempt of the Spanish to take away the reproductive half (The Aswang Project, 2014).  Additionally, wings were added for it to resemble a demon.
          </p>
          <p>
           Source:
           <em>
            The Aswang Project
           </em>
           by High Banks Entertainment, Ltd. Retrieved from http://www.aswangproject.com
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            1. Aswang
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           Aswangs are believed to be humans by day and monsters by night. They feed on human flesh. It is said that the province of Capiz is where a lot of aswangs reside. Cases wherein townspeople suddenly morph into aswangs have been reported.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Aswang.jpeg" rel="lightbox[144666]">
            <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter wp-image-144667" data-lazy-src="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Aswang.jpeg" height="400" src="http://s3.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="600"/>
            <noscript>
             <img alt="6 Filipino Beings Uncovered" class="aligncenter wp-image-144667" height="400" src="http://s1.wheninmanila.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Aswang.jpeg" width="600"/>
            </noscript>
           </a>
           Photo from http://www.moviepilot.de
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           &gt;UNCOVERED: According to
           <i>
            The Aswang Phenomenon
           </i>
           , what the people thought was a possession or transformation of a person into the aswang is actually an illness called X-Linked Dystonia Parkinsonism (XDP)Syndrome. The physical manifestation of this disease often happens later in life.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           Sources:
           <em>
            The Psychology of Aswangs
           </em>
           by Dr. RandyDellosa. Retrieved from https://ph.she.yahoo.com
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           <em>
            The Aswang Phenomenon
           </em>
           by High Banks Entertainment, Ltd. Retrieved from https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2ePhqoyLpXQ
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
          </p>
          <p>
           A lot of things have changed since the 16
           <sup>
            th
           </sup>
           century. The Philippines is no longer under any jurisdiction of another country. Science has progressed exponentially, allowing the explanation of a significant number of these phenomena. Regardless, tales from the past continue to survive in Filipino culture as there remains a certain degree of inability for us to understand why some things occur.
          </p>
          <p>
           As of now, there is no factual evidence that points to the existence or non-existence of these beings. Thus, we continue to ponder on the same question we began with: So, are these paranormal beings real?
          </p>
          <p>
           Just to be safe, it wouldnt hurt to stock up on
           <a href="http://www.wheninmanila.com/mad-for-garlic-the-scent-of-good-food/" target="_blank">
            garlic
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
