       </a>
      </h2>

       <p>
        The United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) has designated 129 World Heritage Sites in Africa, but these 36 are
        <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/pg.cfm?cid=31&amp;l=en&amp;&amp;search=&amp;searchSites=&amp;search_by_country=&amp;region=5&amp;search_yearinscribed=&amp;themes=&amp;criteria_restrication=&amp;type=natural&amp;media=&amp;order=country&amp;description=1&amp;&amp;mode=table">
         listed
        </a>
        as Outstanding Universal Value for natural heritage. As you might expect, there is a wide diversity of flora and fauna in each. Sadly, some of these have also been placed on UNESCOs List of World Heritage Sites in Danger, most often due to poaching of endangered wildlife. The UNESCO committee may inscribe new sites on the list, or it may delist sites if the area no longer meets the criteria, but this is UNESCOs current list for Africa in 2013 as of the time of publishing.

       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Lion at Serengeti National Park, Tanzania. It is one of many UNESCO World Heritage Sites for natural and outstanding universal value in Africa. This vast savanna has 3,648,000 acres (1,476,300 ha) and is world-renowned for its annual herd migrations in search of water and pasture. Two million wildebeests, hundreds of thousands of zebras and gazelles, and all their predators make it 
        <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/156">
         one
        </a>
        of the most impressive nature spectacles in the world. Because there is so much prey, it contains the Big Five. Serengeti is believed to hold more than 3,000 lions, the largest population of lions in Africa, about 1,000 African leopards, African Buffalo, African Elephants and Black Rhinoceros  but few rhinos are said to remain due to rampant poaching. It was designated as a natural site by UNESCO in 1981.

       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Virunga National Park has 2,000,000 acres (800,000 ha) with swamps, savannas, snowfields and the Lava Lake of the Nyiragongo Volcano, the largest in the world. It was designated to the list of African World Heritage Sites in 1979, and a Site in Danger in 1994 due to the war in Rwanda, increasing population of refugees, deforestation, poaching, departure of park staff, and depletion of forests.

       <img alt="Aerial shot of elephant herds roaming through Garamba National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11476" src="http://www.lovethesepics.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Aerial-shot-of-elephant-herds-roaming-through-Garamba-National-Park.jpg" title="Aerial shot of elephant herds roaming through Garamba National Park"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Aerial shot of elephant herds roaming through Garamba National Park, which has a total of 1,200,000 acres (500,000 ha). One of Africas oldest National parks, it was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1980.
        <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garamba_National_Park">
         Garamba
        </a>
        is (or at least was) the home to the worlds last known wild population of Northern White Rhinoceros. Due to poaching of the rhinos within the park, it was added to the list of World Heritage in Danger in 1996. The park is also well known for its African elephant domestication program started in the 1960s, which managed to train tourist-rideable animals from the naturally wild beasts.

       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        The Great Rift Valley, Kenya, has three lakes: Lake Bogoria, Lake Nakuru and Lake Elementaita that make up Kenya Lake System in the Great Rift Valley. The 399,040 acres (161,485 ha) contain a wide diversity of birds, including several threatened species, and was named a World Heritage Site in 2011. The photographer added, Situated on a volcanic hotspot on Africas Great Rift Valley, lonely Lake Bogoria is a caustic cauldron fringed by geysers and populated by over a million flamingos. When we visited Bogoria we were the only people there! (Lake Bogoria, Kenya).

        </a>
        . Caught between appearing eerie and cool, the sand dunes are moving into this house and slowly burying it in Kolmanskop ghost town. Namib Sand Sea was inscribed as a World Heritage Site in 2013.

       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Wildlife at Niokolo-Koba National Park, Senegal, Africa. UNESCO assigned the 2,260,000 acres (913,000 ha) of this park as a World Heritage Site in 1981. The
        <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niokolo-Koba_National_Park">
         national park
        </a>
        is known for its wildlife. The government of Senegal estimates the park contains 20 species of amphibian, 60 species of fish, 38 species of reptile (of which four are tortoises). There are some 80 mammal species. These included (as of 2005) an estimated 11000 buffalo, 6000 hippopotomii, 400 western giant eland, 50 elephants, 120 lions, 150 chimpanzees, 3000 waterbuck (kobus ellipsiprymnus), 2000 common duiker (Sylvicapra grimmia), an unknown number of red colobus (Colobus badius rufomitratus) and a few rare leopards and african wild dogs (Lycaon pictus), although this canid is thought to be wiped out throughout the rest of the country. Around 330 species of birds have been sighted in the park.

       <img alt="Vredefort Crater, Vredefort Dome" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11508" height="822" src="http://www.lovethesepics.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Vredefort-Crater-Vredefort-Dome.jpg" title="Vredefort Crater, Vredefort Dome" width="990"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Vredefort crater, inscribed in 2005, has 74,000 acres (30,000 ha). UNESCO
        <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/1162/">
         wrote
        </a>
        , Vredefort Dome, approximately 120 km south-west of Johannesburg, is a representative part of a larger meteorite impact structure, or astrobleme. Dating back 2,023 million years, it is the oldest astrobleme yet found on Earth. With a radius of 190 km, it is also the largest and the most deeply eroded. Vredefort Dome bears witness to the worlds greatest known single energy release event, which had devastating global effects including, according to some scientists, major evolutionary changes. It provides critical evidence of the Earths geological history and is crucial to understanding of the evolution of the planet. Despite the importance of impact sites to the planets history, geological activity on the Earths surface has led to the disappearance of evidence from most of them, and Vredefort is the only example to provide a full geological profile of an astrobleme below the crater floor.
        <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Vredefort_Dome_STS51I-33-56AA.jpg">
         <b>
          Photo #29 by NASA
         </b>
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_31" style="width: 994px;">
       <img alt="Ta National Park, in West Africa, World Heritage Site since 1982 due to flora and fauna diversity" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11470" height="743" src="http://www.lovethesepics.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Ta-National-Park-in-West-Africa-World-Heritage-Site-since-1982-due-to-flora-and-fauna-diversity.jpg" title="Ta National Park, in West Africa, World Heritage Site since 1982 due to flora and fauna diversity" width="990"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Ta National Park in West Africa has been a World Heritage Site since 1982 due to flora and fauna diversity, including 11 species of monkeys. The 820,000 acres (330,000 ha) are considered to be one of the few remaining sections of the West African tropical forest. However, the
        <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ta%C3%AF_National_Park">
         Tai Forest
        </a>
        is a natural reservoir of the Ebola virus. The World Health Organization has expressed concern over the proximity of this reservoir to the International Airport at Abidjan.
        <a href="http://encyclopedia-african-safari.blogspot.com/2011/12/safari-destination-and-travel-to-land.html">
         <b>
          Photo #30 by African Safari
         </b>
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_32" style="width: 994px;">
       <img alt="UNESCO Natural World Heritage Site in Africa, Rwenzori Mountains National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11512" height="658" src="http://www.lovethesepics.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/UNESCO-Natural-World-Heritage-Site-in-Africa-Rwenzori-Mountains-National-Par.jpg" title="UNESCO Natural World Heritage Site in Africa, Rwenzori Mountains National Park" width="990"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        In 1994, UNESCO added the 246,000 acres (99,600 ha) of Rwenzori Mountains National Park to the list of World Heritage Sites. The park contains Mount Margherita, Africas third highest peak, as well as numerous waterfalls, lakes and glaciers. UNESCO
        <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/684">
         added
        </a>
        , The Rwenzori Mountains are the highest and most permanent sources of the River Nile, and constitute a vital water catchment. The photographer wrote, Trekking the highest and most scenic Mountain range in Uganda and Africa. Climbing Africas 3rd and 4th highest mountains.
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jorneriksson/8496596299/sizes/l/">
         <b>
          Photo #31 by Jorn Eriksson
         </b>
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_33" style="width: 994px;">
       <img alt="Como National Park in Cote d'Ivoire, Africa, inscribed as World Heritage Site in danger in 2003" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11471" src="http://www.lovethesepics.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Como-National-Park-in-Cote-dIvoire-Africa-inscribed-as-World-Heritage-Site-in-danger-in-2003.jpg" title="Como National Park in Cote d'Ivoire, Africa, inscribed as World Heritage Site in danger in 2003"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        At 2,800,000 acres (1,150,000 ha), Como National Park in Cote dIvoire is the largest protected site in West Africa. UNESCO named it a World Heritage Site in 1983. The property was
        <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/227">
         inscribed
        </a>
        on the List of the World Heritage in Danger in 2003 because of the potential impact of civil unrest; decrease in the populations of large mammals due to increased and uncontrolled poaching; and the lack of efficient management mechanisms.
        <a href="http://humanandnatural.com/img-comoe-national-park-cote-d'ivoire-3070.htm">
         <b>
          Photo #32 by Human and Natural
         </b>
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_34" style="width: 994px;">
       <img alt="Hippo emerges from the grass at the edge of the Zambezi River at Mana Pools National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11516" height="660" src="http://www.lovethesepics.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Hippo-emerges-from-the-grass-at-the-edge-of-the-Zambezi-River-at-Mana-Pools-National-Park1.jpg" title="Hippo emerges from the grass at the edge of the Zambezi River at Mana Pools National Park" width="990"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Hippo emerges from the grass at the edge of the Zambezi River at Mana Pools National Park, Sapi and Chewore Safari Areas. The 1,672,000 acre (676,600 ha) area was added by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site in 1984. Every year after rainy season, the flood plain turns into a broad expanse of lakes. When it dries up, many large animals come
        <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mana_Pools_National_Park,_Sapi_and_Chewore_Safari_Areas">
         seeking
        </a>
        water, making it one of Africas most renowned game-viewing regions. It has a large  hippopotamus and Nile crocodile population as well as other wildlife like buffalo, cheetahs, elephants and leopards.
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/travfotos/1936750719/sizes/l/">
         <b>
          Photo #33 by Terry Feuerborn
         </b>
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_30" style="width: 994px;">
       <img alt="Dja Faunal Reserve in Cameroon, Africa, UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1987" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11465" height="747" src="http://www.lovethesepics.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Dja-Faunal-Reserve-in-Cameroon-Africa-UNESCO-World-Heritage-Site-since-1987.jpg" title="Dja Faunal Reserve in Cameroon, Africa, UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1987" width="990"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Dja Faunal Reserve in Cameroon, Africa, became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987 due to the diversity of species here; there are five threatened species, 107 mammals, more than 320 birds, and over 1,500 known plant species on the reserve. The Dja Faunal Reserve covers 1,300,000 acres (526,000 ha) and the boundary of the Dja River keeps the area mostly undisturbed. Seen in this photo, people are pulling the ferry over to cross the Dja River.
        <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Crossing_the_Dja_on_a_ferry.jpg">
         <b>
          Photo #34 by Amcaja
         </b>
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_32" style="width: 994px;">
       <img alt="UNESCO World Heritage Site in Africa, Victoria Falls 'the Smoke that Thunders' is the world's largest sheet of falling water" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11513" height="660" src="http://www.lovethesepics.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/UNESCO-World-Heritage-Site-in-Africa-Victoria-Falls-the-Smoke-that-Thunders-is-the-worlds-largest-sheet-of-falling-water.jpg" title="Victoria Falls 'the Smoke that Thunders' is the world's largest sheet of falling water and World Heritage Site" width="990"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Victoria Falls the Smoke that Thunders is the worlds largest sheet of falling water. Named a World Heritage Site in 1989, Mosi-oa-Tunya / Victoria Falls is about twice the height of Niagara Falls and over twice the width of Horseshoe Falls. In both height and width, it is rivaled only by Argentina and Brazils
        <a href="http://www.lovethesepics.com/2011/11/incredible-iguazu-falls-over-under-or-on-a-boat-in-devil%E2%80%99s-throat-26-pics/">
         incredible Iguazu Falls
        </a>
        . The area is 21,700 acres (8,780 ha) at the border of Zambia and Zimbabwe in southern Africa. The waterfall is about 1.2 miles wide where the Zambezi River plunges through the first of six gorges. It is possible to swim right at the very edge of the falls in a naturally formed Devils Pool. Sometimes humans, hippos and crocodiles are swept over the falls;
        <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victoria_Falls">
         animals
        </a>
        are frequently found swirling about here or washed up at the north-east end of the Second Gorge.
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pius_mahimbi/8523197247/sizes/l/">
         <b>
          Photo #35 by Pius Mahimbi
         </b>
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_33" style="width: 994px;">
       <img alt="Full moon rising over Lake Turkana National Parks" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11486" height="572" src="http://www.lovethesepics.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Full-moon-rising-over-Lake-Turkana-National-Parks.jpg" title="Full moon rising over Lake Turkana National Parks" width="990"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Full moon rising over Lake Turkana National Parks. The 399,040 acres (161,485 ha) that make up this park are actually a group of three national parks located in Kenya. The lake is the worlds largest permanent desert lake as well as the worlds largest alkaline lake. The park was inscribed as a World Heritage Site in 1997. It is considered important since it is a breeding ground for the Nile crocodile, hippopotamus, several venomous snakes and is a stopping point for numerous migratory birds.
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/rosluc6460/3319158995/sizes/l/">
         <b>
          Photo #36 by Luciano Rizzello
         </b>
        </a>
       </p>
