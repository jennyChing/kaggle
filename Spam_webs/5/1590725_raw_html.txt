        <p>
         I am seriously the biggest peanut butter fan. I simply love it. But, I am pretty picky about peanut butter. I prefer to purchase peanut butter that is all-natural without a lot of added ingredients. My kids are pretty old school, they like to snack on apples and peanut butter after school. But this fun recipe from
         <a href="http://justins.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://justins.com/', 'Justins Nut Butters']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="justins nut butter">

         , would be a very tasty snack recipe to make for the whole family.
        </p>

         <em>
          <strong>
           Are you a peanut butter lover?
          </strong>

        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.longwaitforisabella.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/peanut-butter-balls-recipe-768x1024.jpg">
          <img alt="Peanut Butter Balls Recipe" class=" wp-image-15738 aligncenter" height="667" src="http://www.longwaitforisabella.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/peanut-butter-balls-recipe-768x1024.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Peanut Butter Balls Recipe
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          INGREDIENTS
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          2 Cups Justins Classic Peanut Butter
         </li>
         <li>
          1/3 Cup Maple Syrup
         </li>
         <li>
           Cup Coconut Flour
         </li>
         <li>
           Teaspoon Salt
         </li>
         <li>
          2/3 Cup Gluten Free Granola
         </li>
         <li>
          6 Justins Dark Chocolate Peanut Butter Cups
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          COOKING INSTRUCTIONS
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          In a large bowl, combine peanut butter, syrup, coconut flour and salt. Mix well.
         </li>
         <li>
          Add granola and stir until combined.
         </li>
         <li>
          Form into bite sized balls and set aside.
         </li>
         <li>
          In a double boiler, melt peanut butter cups over low heat, stirring frequently.
         </li>
         <li>
          Using a fork, dip each peanut butter ball in the melted chocolate and place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
         </li>
         <li>
          Freeze the balls for 20 minutes or until chocolate is set.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <strong>
           Enjoy!
          </strong>
