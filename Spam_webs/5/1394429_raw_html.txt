     <p>
      The
      <b>
       SnowCastle of Kemi
      </b>
      is the biggest
      <a href="/wiki/Snow_fort" title="Snow fort">
       snow fort
      </a>
      in the world.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POTD_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-POTD-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is rebuilt every winter with a different architecture in
      <a href="/wiki/Kemi" title="Kemi">
       Kemi
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">
       Finland
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POTD_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-POTD-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1996 the first snow castle drew 300,000 visitors.
     </p>
     <p>
      The area covered by the castle has varied from 13,000 to over 20,000 square metres. The highest towers have been over 20 metres high and longest walls over 1,000 metres long, and the castle has had up to three storeys. Despite its varying configurations, the snow castle has a few recurring elements: a
      <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">
       chapel
      </a>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">
       restaurant
      </a>
      and a
      <a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">
       hotel
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       The SnowRestaurant
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POTD_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-POTD-1">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         1
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       has ice tables and seats covered with
       <a href="/wiki/Reindeer" title="Reindeer">
        reindeer
       </a>
       fur, as well as
       <a href="/wiki/Ice_sculpture" title="Ice sculpture">
        ice sculptures
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Ecumenism" title="Ecumenism">
        ecumenical
       </a>
       SnowChapel
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POTD_1-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-POTD-1">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         1
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       with 50100 seats has seen numerous weddings of couples from as far away as
       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
        Japan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">
        Hong Kong
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       The SnowHotel
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POTD_1-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-POTD-1">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         1
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       offers a choice of double rooms and a
       <a href="/wiki/Honeymoon" title="Honeymoon">
        honeymoon
       </a>
       suite, all of which are decorated by local artists using local materials.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      The SnowCastle of Kemi also hosts such things as an adventure land for children, a theatre and ice art exhibitions with lights and sound effects. Many
      <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">
       opera
      </a>
      singers and
      <a href="/wiki/Dance" title="Dance">
       dancers
      </a>
      have performed in the SnowCastle of Kemi.
     </p>
