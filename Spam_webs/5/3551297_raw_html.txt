         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Evolution of Hygiene
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="posted-on">
           Posted on
           <a href="http://www.chadwicks.ie/blog/evolution-of-hygiene/" rel="bookmark">
            <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2014-04-30T09:48:12+00:00">
             April 30, 2014
            </time>
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          The Greeks and the Romans had the right idea, long ago, with their luxurious public baths and advanced plumbing technology. Not only was keeping clean a daily routine but it was also a glorified ritual and social occasion.
         </p>
         <p>
          Obviously mankind wasnt paying close attention though as when it came to cleanliness in the Middle Ages, lets just say things became a little sticky  pardon the pun. Skip right past that blip on our monitor of social progress to the 16th Century and this is where the decline of communal bathing begins, laying the foundation for the private bathroom as we know it today.Whether youre a bath dweller, soaking yourself into a wrinkly mess, or a rapid shower-taker its all in the name of hygiene. Heres a quick rundown of our slightly chequered past.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Roman Bath
          </strong>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          The ancient Greeks and Romans were known to spend two to three hours bathing in their beautiful temple-like public baths. They created the most advanced network of aqueducts and plumbing systems. Unfortunately this was quickly forgotten.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter wp-image-1369" height="400" src="http://chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/ancient-roman-baths-england-1-100812-02.jpg" style="border: 2px solid black;" title="Roman Bath" width="534"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.livescience.com/29635-ancient-roman-baths-england.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.livescience.com/29635-ancient-roman-baths-england.html', 'Image Source');" rel="nofollow">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           Spread of Disease
          </strong>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          The uncontrollable spread of diseases in the late middle ages made public baths slightly unpopular. Understandable really. Nothing like a crowd of naked people suffering from the bubonic plague to send a person running in the opposite direction. Unfortunately for those who actually liked the occasional bath, and yes there were many who didnt, this meant bye-bye cleanliness.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter wp-image-1370 size-full" height="284" src="http://chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/300px-Smallpox01.jpg" style="border: 2px solid black;" title="Smallpox" width="379"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Smallpox01.jpg/300px-Smallpox01.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Smallpox01.jpg/300px-Smallpox01.jpg', 'Image Source');" rel="nofollow">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           Queen Elizabeth 1
          </strong>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Queen Elizabeth I (1533-1603) famously boasted that she only bathed once a month, whether she needed it or not. Eh, were guessing she probably needed it?
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter wp-image-1371" height="400" src="http://chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/654px-Elizabeth_I_Armada_Portrait_British_School.jpg" style="border: 2px solid black;" title="Queen Elizabeth 1" width="437"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Elizabeth_I_Armada_Portrait_British_School.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Elizabeth_I_Armada_Portrait_British_School.jpg', 'Image Source');" rel="nofollow">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           French Linen Shirt
          </strong>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          In 17th century France, it was believed it was more effective to simply change into a linen shirt rather than bathe. They believed in the linens cleansing ability to draw out the toxins from the skin. Whether it worked or not is besides the point, everybody looked fabulous.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter wp-image-1372 size-full" height="300" src="http://chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Linen Shirt.JPG" style="border: 2px solid black;" title="French Linen Shirt" width="400"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://isiswardrobe.blogspot.ie/2013_08_01_archive.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://isiswardrobe.blogspot.ie/2013_08_01_archive.html', 'Image Source');" rel="nofollow">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           Europe in the Middle Ages
          </strong>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          From the middle ages up until the 18th century, people adopted a laissez faire attitude to hygiene and only bothered to wash the parts of the body that were visible to the public. The reasoning being if you cant see it then you cant smell it, or something.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter wp-image-1373 size-full" height="400" src="http://www.chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Europe-in-the-Middle-Ages-2.jpg" style="border: 2px solid black;" title="Europe in the Middle Ages" width="469"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.retronaut.com/?attachment_id=7711" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.retronaut.com/?attachment_id=7711', 'Image Source');" rel="nofollow">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           Soap
          </strong>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          The late 19th Century to early 20th Century saw sciences germ theory of disease and advertising merge to promote the use of soap. This brought about massive social change, lovely scents and vigorous scrubbing of childrens ears. Around that time indoor plumbing became the norm and not just for the rich, who throughout history had the option of bathing privately.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter wp-image-1374 size-full" height="449" src="http://chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/pears soap dirty boy.jpg" style="border: 2px solid black;" title="Pears' Soap" width="273"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://vintageamericanadvertisements.blogspot.ie/2012/07/early-20th-century-pears-soap.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://vintageamericanadvertisements.blogspot.ie/2012/07/early-20th-century-pears-soap.html', 'Image Source');" rel="nofollow">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           Fancy Baths
          </strong>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Today were spoilt for choice. While showers win out over baths for daily hygiene rituals, baths are generally reserved for relaxing. A treat for the end of a long day. Whirlpool and back massage features turn many a private bathroom into a spa experience or a party.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter wp-image-1375" height="338" src="http://chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/002618-15-bath-view.jpg" style="border: 2px solid black;" title="Fancy Bath" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.kiwicollection.com/photo-gallery/bath-with-a-view" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.kiwicollection.com/photo-gallery/bath-with-a-view', 'Image Source');" rel="nofollow">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
