         <h1 class="entry-title single-title" itemprop="headline">
          Fitness: My Muscles
         </h1>
         <p class="byline vcard">
          <span class="topic-tags">
           <a href="/nutrition-fitness-index/">
            <img alt="nutrition and fitness icon" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/themes/bones/library/images/t-nutri-40.png" title="nutrition and fitness"/>
           </a>
          </span>
          Posted  under
          <a href="http://youngwomenshealth.org/category/guides/" rel="category tag">
           Health Guides
          </a>
          . Updated 22 May 2014.
         </p>
        </header>
        <!-- end article header -->
        <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          <img alt="girl holding track shoes" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-10819" height="150" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/girl-track-shoes-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
          Fitness is fun! Its a great way to experience health and wellness. Enjoying fitness is the key to keeping exercise a part of your life. Fun Fitness will teach you all about the right way to exercise. There are lots of benefits to being a fit person. You will be healthier, less stressed and more relaxed. Youll have more energy too!
         </p>
         <h2>
          Exercise Safety
         </h2>
         <p>
          Its important to follow your health care providers advice about fitness. The exercises featured here are for teens that are in good health and have no physical restrictions. Be sure to listen to your body. Exercise should feel like you are working out, but not like you are injuring your body. If a particular exercise or stretch hurts, STOP immediately. You may be performing the task incorrectly, working beyond your limits or injuring your body. If you feel dizzy, have trouble breathing, or experience any pain while you are exercising, STOP.
         </p>
         <p>
          Your body needs energy (food and fluids) to perform at its best. Our sports nutrition guide will help you understand what you should eat and drink before, during and after exercising so that you will feel good and have a safe workout.
         </p>
         <p>
          Remember, before doing this or any exercise program, be sure to consult with your own health care provider.
         </p>
         <h2 class="infotitle">
          <span class="infotitlefirstletter">
           M
          </span>
          y Muscles
         </h2>
         <p>
          Understanding the names and locations of your muscles is important when youre exercising. If you know the specific muscles youre using, it is easier to focus on each aspect of your workout. This helps you to be more efficient and to prevent injuries.
         </p>
         <p class="question">
          How to use My Muscles:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li class="bulletedlist">
           Learn the names of your muscles and where theyre located by looking at the pictures of Katy, our My Muscles trainer.
          </li>
          <li class="bulletedlist">
           Look at all 3 views: front, back, and side.
          </li>
         </ul>

          <br/>
          <img alt="muscles back view" class="alignleft wp-image-10866 size-full full" height="600" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/muscles-back.png" width="720"/>

         <h1 class="entry-title single-title" itemprop="headline">
          Fitness: Stretches
         </h1>
         <p class="byline vcard">
          <span class="topic-tags">
           <a href="/nutrition-fitness-index/">
            <img alt="nutrition and fitness icon" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/themes/bones/library/images/t-nutri-40.png" title="nutrition and fitness"/>
           </a>

          </a>

         </p>
        </header>
        <!-- end article header -->
        <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          <img alt="yoga-pose" class="alignleft wp-image-8506 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/yoga.-pose-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
          Stretching is a way to get your body ready for exercise and is also a way to improve flexibility at the end of your workout. Your muscles feel relaxed after you stretch properly and this will also help prevent muscle related injuries. Try the following stretches for your upper, middle and lower body.
         </p>
         <a class=" modal-li" data-toggle="modal" href="#custom-modal-a97cd9ca14820455cc29ceb7f61ffcae">
          Upper Body: Chest Stretch
         </a>
         <div aria-hidden="true" class="modal fade bs-modal-lg" id="custom-modal-a97cd9ca14820455cc29ceb7f61ffcae" role="dialog" tabindex="-1">
          <div class="modal-dialog modal-lg">
           <div class="modal-content">
            <div class="modal-header">
             <button aria-hidden="true" class="close" data-dismiss="modal" type="button">
              
             </button>
             <h4 class="modal-title">
              Upper Body: Chest Stretch
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="modal-body">
             <img alt="chest-stretch-1" class="alignleft wp-image-8780 size-full" height="300" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/chest-stretch-1.jpg" width="300"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Pectoralis Major (Pecs or Chest)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
              None
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Stand up straight, with knees slightly bent.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place feet hip distance apart.
              </li>
              <li>
               Make sure toes are pointing forward.
              </li>
              <li>
               Keep shoulders even as you complete this stretch.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place arms behind your back.
              </li>
              <li>
               Clasp your hands together, extending your arms behind your back and hold this position.
              </li>
              <li>
               Feel the stretch in your chest (see photo).
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) through your nose, and exhale (breathe out) through your mouth, as you complete this stretch.
              </li>
              <li>
               Hold stretch for 30 seconds.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>
              Keep your back straight (not arched). Continue to breathe.
              <br/>
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>

         <a class=" modal-li" data-toggle="modal" href="#custom-modal-275c58b8d8cf3df949dcd6639994d83d">
          Upper Body: Upper Back Stretch
         </a>
         <div aria-hidden="true" class="modal fade bs-modal-lg" id="custom-modal-275c58b8d8cf3df949dcd6639994d83d" role="dialog" tabindex="-1">

             </button>
             <h4 class="modal-title">
              Upper Body: Upper Back Stretch
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="modal-body">
             <img alt="upper-back-stretch" class="alignleft wp-image-8794 size-full" height="300" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/upper-back-stretch.jpg" width="300"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Rhomboids (Upper Back)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
              None
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Stand up straight, with knees slightly bent.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place feet hip distance apart.
              </li>
              <li>
               Make sure toes are pointing forward.
              </li>
              <li>
               Keep shoulders even as you complete this stretch.
              </li>
              <li>
               Extend both arms, forming a 90-degree angle between your arms and body.
              </li>
              <li>
               Clasp your hands together, palms facing forward, extending your arms in front of your body and hold this position.
              </li>
              <li>
               Feel the stretch in your upper back.
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) through your nose, and exhale (breathe out) through your mouth, as you complete this stretch.
              </li>
              <li>
               Hold stretch for 30 seconds.
              </li>
              <li>
               Alternate sitting version: Complete entire sequence from a sitting position. Make sure back is straight.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>
              Keep your back straight (not arched). Continue to breathe.
              <br/>
             </p>

             <img alt="hamstring-stretch-2" class="alignleft wp-image-8782 size-full" height="150" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/hamstring-stretch-2.jpg" width="300"/>
             <img alt="hamstring-stretch-3" class="alignleft wp-image-8783 size-full" height="300" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/hamstring-stretch-3.jpg" width="300"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Hamstrings (Mid Rear Thigh)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
              None
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Lie with your back and head flat against the floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Bend your knees and place both feet on the floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Straighten your right leg and point your toes. Your leg should be extended about 2 inches from the floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Your left knee remains bent, with left foot placed firmly on the floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Slowly raise your right leg toward the ceiling, keeping your leg straight. Slowly extend your leg until you feel a stretch.
              </li>
              <li>
               You will probably feel the stretch when your leg forms a 90-degree angle with your body. Some people are more flexible and can bring the leg in past a 90-degree angle, closer to the body.
              </li>
              <li>
               Feel the stretch in your right hamstring.
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) through your nose, and exhale (breathe out) through your mouth, as you complete this stretch.
              </li>
              <li>
               Hold stretch for 30 seconds.
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat steps 3-8 on the opposite side, raising left leg to stretch left hamstring.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>
              Remember to keep your head and back against the floor. Remember to lift your leg slowly when preparing to stretch the hamstring. Continue to breathe.
              <br/>
             </p>
            </div>

          Fitness: Toning Exercises
         </h1>
         <p class="byline vcard">
          <span class="topic-tags">
           <a href="/nutrition-fitness-index/">
            <img alt="nutrition and fitness icon" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/themes/bones/library/images/t-nutri-40.png" title="nutrition and fitness"/>
           </a>
          </span>
          Posted  under

        <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
         <img alt="girl lifting weights" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-1010" height="150" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/lifting-weights-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
         <p>
          Toning your body is a great way to get your muscles conditioned and strong. Toning exercises can help raise your metabolism (the way you burn food energy) by building muscle. Certain toning exercises may help prevent osteoporosis by keeping your bones strong. Toning can even help to release stress from your body. Try the following exercises for your upper, middle, and lower body.
         </p>
         <a class=" modal-li" data-toggle="modal" href="#custom-modal-dc29cfeb863480fefd0925dc149097f9">
          Upper Body: Overhead Shoulder Press
         </a>
         <div aria-hidden="true" class="modal fade bs-modal-lg" id="custom-modal-dc29cfeb863480fefd0925dc149097f9" role="dialog" tabindex="-1">
          <div class="modal-dialog modal-lg">
           <div class="modal-content">
            <div class="modal-header">
             <button aria-hidden="true" class="close" data-dismiss="modal" type="button">
              
             </button>
             <h4 class="modal-title">
              Upper Body: Overhead Shoulder Press
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="modal-body">
             <img alt="over_shldr_press_1" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8817" height="300" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/over_shldr_press_1.jpg" width="300"/>
             <img alt="over_shldr_press_2" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8818" height="300" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/over_shldr_press_2.jpg" width="300"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Trapezius (Upper Shoulder), Deltoid (Shoulder), Rhomboids (Upper Back)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
              Weights
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Light Weights (2, 3 or 5 lbs) or
              </li>
              <li>
               12 or 20 oz filled water bottle or
              </li>
              <li>
               Soup Cans (unopened)
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Stand up straight, with knees slightly bent.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place feet hip distance apart.
              </li>
              <li>
               Make sure toes are pointing forward.
              </li>
              <li>
               Keep shoulders even as you complete this exercise.
              </li>
              <li>
               Hold weights at shoulder height with palms facing forward (starting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Exhale (breathe out) as you slowly and evenly lift both arms upright past ears, above head, to a full arm extension.
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) as you evenly and slowly lower both arms down to starting position.
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat exercise for a set of 8.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>
              Do keep your back straight. Continue to breathe. Do move your arms slowly. Do move your dominant arm (your right arm if you are right handed and left arm if you are left handed) at the same pace as your non-dominant arm. Do keep your shoulders even with each other.
              <br/>
             </p>

           </div>

            <div class="modal-body">

             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Pectoralis Major (Pecs or Chest)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
              Weights
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Light Weights (2, 3 or 5 lbs) or
              </li>
              <li>
               12 or 20 oz filled water bottle or
              </li>
              <li>
               Soup Cans (unopened)
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>

              <li>
               Make sure toes are pointing forward.
              </li>
              <li>
               Hold weights at shoulder height with palms facing forward and elbows bent to form a 90-degree angle (starting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Exhale (breathe out) as you evenly and slowly move arms together. Keep elbows bent at a 90-degree angle until elbows and forearms meet in front of your face and chest. Now palms are facing each other.
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) as you evenly and slowly move both arms back to starting position.
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat exercise for a set of 8.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>

         <a class=" modal-li" data-toggle="modal" href="#custom-modal-1a45b527520420c967a51536f5cf0089">
          Upper Body: Bent Knee Push Up
         </a>
         <div aria-hidden="true" class="modal fade bs-modal-lg" id="custom-modal-1a45b527520420c967a51536f5cf0089" role="dialog" tabindex="-1">
          <div class="modal-dialog modal-lg">
           <div class="modal-content">
            <div class="modal-header">
             <button aria-hidden="true" class="close" data-dismiss="modal" type="button">
              
             </button>
             <h4 class="modal-title">
              Upper Body: Bent Knee Push Up
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="modal-body">
             <img alt="knee_pushup_1" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8811" height="150" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/knee_pushup_1.jpg" width="300"/>
             <img alt="knee_pushup_2" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8812" height="150" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/knee_pushup_2.jpg" width="300"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Pectoralis Major (Pecs or Chest), Rectus Abdominus (Abdominals or Abs) supporting muscle group
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Mat or towel to lie on (optional)
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Lie with your stomach flat against the floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Lift feet off floor, bringing heels towards your buttocks. Bend knees to form a 45-degree angle or L-shape.
              </li>
              <li>
               Extend hands next to your body at shoulder height, keeping elbows bent.
              </li>
              <li>
               Exhale (breathe out) as you push your body weight off the floor by extending arms from a bent elbow to straight elbow. Be sure to have proper alignment: a straight back, with ears, shoulders and hips in a straight line. Hold abdominal muscles in tightly (pretend you are squeezing your belly button into your spine).
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) as you bend elbows and allow your body to lower to floor level. Lower your body until your face is about 2-3 inches from the floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat exercise for a set of 8.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>
              Do keep your back straight. Continue to breathe. Do move your arms slowly. Do tighten your abdominals for proper form.
              <br/>
             </p>
            </div>

         <a class=" modal-li" data-toggle="modal" href="#custom-modal-0d4fba74a22d835c2b4e72d4ea35e0f9">
          Upper Body: Bicep Curl
         </a>
         <div aria-hidden="true" class="modal fade bs-modal-lg" id="custom-modal-0d4fba74a22d835c2b4e72d4ea35e0f9" role="dialog" tabindex="-1">
          <div class="modal-dialog modal-lg">
           <div class="modal-content">
            <div class="modal-header">
             <button aria-hidden="true" class="close" data-dismiss="modal" type="button">
              
             </button>
             <h4 class="modal-title">
              Upper Body: Bicep Curl
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="modal-body">
             <img alt="bicep-curl-1" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8798" height="300" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/bicep-curl-1.jpg" width="150"/>
             <img alt="bicep-curl-2" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8799" height="300" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/bicep-curl-2.jpg" width="150"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Biceps (Mid Upper Arm)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
              Weights
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Light Weights (2, 3 or 5 lbs) or
              </li>
              <li>
               12 or 20 oz filled water bottle or
              </li>
              <li>
               Soup Cans (unopened)
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Stand up straight, with knees slightly bent.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place feet hip distance apart.
              </li>
              <li>
               Make sure toes are pointing forward.
              </li>
              <li>
               Keep shoulders even as you complete this exercise.
              </li>
              <li>
               Hold weights in both hands, palms facing upward, with elbows positioned next to waist and rib cage. Position arms so weights are directly above thighs and elbows are slightly bent (starting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Exhale (breathe out) as you slowly and evenly lift both forearms upright to shoulders to a full bicep curl (ending position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) as you evenly and slowly lower both arms down to starting position. Remember to squeeze your bicep muscles as you lift down, instead of letting gravity do the work.
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat exercise for a set of 8.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>
              Do keep your back straight. Continue to breathe. Do keep from leaning to one side. Do move your arms slowly, especially as you lower them down to starting position.
              <br/>
             </p>

         <div aria-hidden="true" class="modal fade bs-modal-lg" id="custom-modal-57ee2107500bbbb3fa2a3608471f7d47" role="dialog" tabindex="-1">

            <div class="modal-body">

             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Triceps (Mid Posterior Arm)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
              Weights
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Light Weights (2, 3 or 5 lbs) or
              </li>
              <li>
               12 or 20 oz filled water bottle or
              </li>
              <li>
               Soup Cans (unopened)
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Stand up straight, with knees slightly bent.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place feet hip distance apart.
              </li>
              <li>
               Make sure toes are pointing forward.
              </li>
              <li>
               Keep shoulders even as you complete this exercise.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place your left arm on your left thigh for support. Hold weight in right hand, working right triceps muscle. Bend arm at elbow, lift elbow back, and hold weight next to shoulder and your underarm.
              </li>
              <li>
               Exhale (breathe out) as you slowly and evenly extend forearm behind your body, moving from the elbow joint so that arm is now straight (ending position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) as you evenly and slowly bring your forearm back to starting position. Remember to squeeze your triceps muscle, instead of letting gravity do the work.
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat exercise for a set of 8.
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat entire sequence on your left arm, steps 1-7 (working left triceps muscle).
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>
              Do keep your back straight. Continue to breathe. Do move your arms slowly without swinging them. Do keep your exercising elbow close to your side.

          Middle Body: Sit Up
         </a>
         <div aria-hidden="true" class="modal fade bs-modal-lg" id="custom-modal-361283744976a5e182c441104b5131ed" role="dialog" tabindex="-1">

             <button aria-hidden="true" class="close" data-dismiss="modal" type="button">
              
             </button>
             <h4 class="modal-title">
              Middle Body: Sit Up
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="modal-body">
             <img alt="situp-1" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8821" height="135" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/situp-1.jpg" width="300"/>
             <img alt="situp-2" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8822" height="127" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/situp-2.jpg" width="300"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Rectus Abdominus (Abdominals or Abs)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Mat or towel to lie on (optional)
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Lie with your back flat against the floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Bend your knees and place both feet on floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place hands behind ears, gently supporting your head, but not holding or lifting your head up. Do not interlace your fingers together.
              </li>
              <li>
               Push your lower spine flat against the floor. Pretend there is a zipper holding your lower spine to the floor (starting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Slowly lift your shoulders off the floor, using the strength of your abdominal muscles. As you lift, imagine you are squeezing your belly button into your spine (lifting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Exhale (breathe out) as you lift up.
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) as you slowly lower your shoulders back to floor (starting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat exercise for a set of 8.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>
              Do keep your spine flat on the floor. Continue to breathe. Do move slowly. Rushing to complete a higher number of crunches could injure your back. Do use a controlled abdominal squeeze, without rocking or swinging. Do keep your fingers loose and unlaced behind your head.

             <h4 class="modal-title">
              Middle Body: Isometric Sit Up
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="modal-body">
             <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-8809 size-full" height="135" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/iso_situp_1.jpg" width="300"/>
             <img alt="iso_situp_2" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8810" height="135" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/iso_situp_2.jpg" width="300"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Rectus Abdominus (Abdominals or Abs)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Mat or towel to lie on (optional)
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Lie with your back flat against the floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Bend your knees and place both feet on floor.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place hands behind ears, gently supporting your head, but not holding or lifting your head up. Do not interlace your fingers together.
              </li>
              <li>
               Push your lower spine flat against the floor. Pretend there is a zipper holding your lower spine to the floor (starting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Slowly lift your shoulders off the floor, using the strength of your abdominal muscles. As you lift, imagine you are squeezing your belly button into your spine (lifting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Place your hands on your upper quadriceps and hold this position for a count of 16. This is an isometric or held muscular contraction.
              </li>
              <li>
               Exhale (breathe out) as you lift and hold while you move your hands from upper quadriceps to behind your ears.
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) as you slowly lower your shoulders back to floor (starting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat exercise for a set of 8.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Remember:
              </strong>
              Do keep your spine flat on the floor. Continue to breathe. Do move slowly. Rushing to complete a higher number of isometric sit ups could injure your back. Do use a controlled abdominal squeeze, without rocking or swinging. Do keep your fingers loose and unlaced behind your head.
              <br/>
             </p>

          Middle Body: Front Arm Raise
         </a>
         <div aria-hidden="true" class="modal fade bs-modal-lg" id="custom-modal-347fed38d877b9cb730df6d87315f038" role="dialog" tabindex="-1">
          <div class="modal-dialog modal-lg">
           <div class="modal-content">
            <div class="modal-header">
             <button aria-hidden="true" class="close" data-dismiss="modal" type="button">
              
             </button>
             <h4 class="modal-title">
              Middle Body: Front Arm Raise
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="modal-body">
             <img alt="front-arm-raise" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-8806" height="300" src="http://youngwomenshealth.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/front-arm-raise.jpg" width="300"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Latissimus Dorsi (Lats Side Back), Deltoids (Shoulder)
             <p>
              <strong>
               Equipment Needed:
              </strong>
              Weights
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Light Weights (2, 3 or 5 lbs) or
              </li>
              <li>
               12 or 20 oz filled water bottle or
              </li>
              <li>
               Soup Cans (unopened)
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Instructions:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Stand up straight, with knees slightly bent.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place feet hip distance apart.
              </li>
              <li>
               Make sure toes are pointing forward.
              </li>
              <li>
               Keep shoulders even as you complete this exercise.
              </li>
              <li>
               Hold weights in each hand, with arms extended by your sides. Slightly bend your elbows.
              </li>
              <li>
               Place arms so that the held weights are in line (next to) with your hip/leg area. Grip weights so palms face behind you (starting position).
              </li>
              <li>
               Slowly and evenly lift right arm in front of body with a full arm extension, until your arm reaches shoulder height. Be sure to keep elbow slightly bent. You are working your right Latissimus Dorsi &amp; Deltoid.
              </li>
              <li>
               Exhale (breathe out) as you lift arm up.
              </li>
              <li>
               Inhale (breathe in) as you evenly and slowly lower arm down to starting position.
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat exercise for a set of 8.
              </li>
              <li>
               Repeat entire sequence on your left side, step 1-10 (working left Latissimus Dorsi &amp; Deltoid).
              </li>
             </ol>

             <br/>
             <strong>
              Muscles Used:
             </strong>
             Adductors (Inner Thigh)
             <p>

              <strong>
               Instructions:
