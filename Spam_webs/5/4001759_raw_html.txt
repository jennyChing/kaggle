At long action of sounds of the big intensity (loud music, work in noisy shops) the threshold of acoustical sensitivity raises.

         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Blood Swept Lands and Seas of Red
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="posted-by">
           By             	Slim
          </span>
          <span>
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-08-05T20:32:45+00:00">
            August 5, 2014
           </time>
          </span>
          <span class="sep">
           |
          </span>
          <span>
           <a href="http://slimpaley.com/art/blood-swept-lands-and-seas-of-red/#comments">

           </a>

         </div>

         <!-- -->

        <div class="entry-content boxed">
         <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/wp_20140803_09_31_26_pro.jpg">
           <img alt="wp_20140803_09_31_26_pro" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-24622" height="450" src="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/wp_20140803_09_31_26_pro-800x450.jpg" width="800"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            T
           </strong>
          </em>
          oday marksthe opening of Blood Swept Lands and Seas of Red a stunningly powerful installation at the Tower of London to commemoratethe centenary of the start of World War I. Artist Paul Cummins, commissioned by the British government and in collaboration with stage designer Tom Piper, chose the poppy in ceramic form to create this visually staggering tribute.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/slide_360434_4032911_free.jpg">
           <img alt="slide_360434_4032911_free" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-24620" height="691" src="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/slide_360434_4032911_free-690x691.jpg" width="690"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          At present, you see 120, 000 ceramic poppies spilling from The Tower of London and spreading across the dry moat and grass. More flowers will be planted daily by volunteers throughout the summer and continuing until Armistice Day, November 11, the last day of the war.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/wp_20140803_09_35_35_pro.jpg">
           <img alt="wp_20140803_09_35_35_pro" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-24649" height="388" src="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/wp_20140803_09_35_35_pro-690x388.jpg" width="690"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This powerful now, one can onlyimagine how moving its going to be whenthe installation eventuallyconsists of 888,246 flowers, each bloom representing the life of a British or Colonial soldier who lost his life in the war.
         </p>
         <p>
          There will be one poppy for my paternal great-grandfather, a British soldier who lost his life in Belgium.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/slide_360434_4032928_free.jpg">
           <img alt="slide_360434_4032928_free" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-24618" height="532" src="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/slide_360434_4032928_free-800x532.jpg" width="800"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The flowersare being sold (25 each) and dispersed to all buyers once the installation is unplanted. The funds will be donated to severalwell known U.K. veterans charities. Throughout the run of Blood Swept Lands and Seas of Red the names of 180 WWI soldiers will be read out in a roll of Honour.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/kate-middleton-poppy3-z.jpg">
           <img alt="kate-middleton-poppy3--z" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-24617" height="407" src="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/kate-middleton-poppy3-z.jpg" width="610"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/kate-middleton-poppy-z.jpg">
           <img alt="kate-middleton-poppy--z" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-24619" height="407" src="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/kate-middleton-poppy-z.jpg" width="610"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          A visit to the Tower installation by the younger members of the British Monarchy earlier today.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/article-2716788-204ADE6E00000578-385_964x644.jpg">
           <img alt="article-2716788-204ADE6E00000578-385_964x644" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-24655" height="460" src="http://slimpaley.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/article-2716788-204ADE6E00000578-385_964x644-690x460.jpg" width="690"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          During the evening opening ceremony, silhouettes of soldiers projected against a blood red Tower of London.
         </p>

           <div class="comment-content">
            <p>
             Did you know Canadian Lieutenant Colonel  John McCrae wrote the poem In Flanders Fields? The image of poppies growing amongst  soldiers graves was the inspiration for the red poppies for remembrance. Lt Col. McCrae died of pneumonia in 1918 in France whilst commanding No. 3 Canadian General Hospital (McGill) at Boulogne.
            </p>
            <p>
             My great grandfather joined the Australian 7th Light Horse which left Sydney in 1914. The soldiers took their own horses with them and after the war only one Australian horse came back home. The others were left in the Middle East . Many of the horses were Walers a tough Australian stock horse. Their descendants are still around today in the Middle East.
            </p>

             Ive been to the exhibit and taken photos  the ones youve put up are so much better!
             <br/>
             Still, even on my lousy digital camera, the emotions these poppies evoke are too strong to be ignored.
             <br/>
             My grandfather and his younger brother, both from Victoria BC, fought at the Battle of the Somme.  My grandfather survived that day and went on to marry my grandmother.  He lived until 1960.
             <br/>
             Upon leaving Victoria for Europe in 1914, he promised his mother hed take care of his brother, who was last seen leading his troops into action at the same battle.  Every night, until she died, my great-grandmother had the dinner table set for her lost son.
             <br/>
             My grandfather never lost his feelings of guilt, at having let down my mother, his brother and his father, upon whose birthday the Battle took place.
             <br/>
             Im grateful to have seen the poppies in person.  Ive bought one, and dedicated another to my lost great-uncle and his broken brother, my grandfather.  It seems the least I can do.
