       <span itemprop="description">
        <p>
         James Cameron says you're going to dirty your underwear whilst watching the Avatar sequels.
        </p>
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="EchoTopic">
       <span id="intelliTxt">
        <span class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/info/james_cameron">
           James Cameron
          </a>
          is really pushing these
          <em>
           Avatar
          </em>
          sequels. The Oscar-winning director has three follow-ups planned for the 2009 fantasy adventure and used a select choice of words to describe his excitement.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="James Cameron" height="370" src="http://images.contactmusic.com/images/feature-images/avatar-disney-animal-kingdom-james-cameron-building-set-636-370.jpg" style="vertical-align: top; border: 1px solid black; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="James Cameron" width="636"/>
          <span style="font-size: x-small;">
           <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/james-cameron">
            James Cameron
           </a>
           [L] is also developing a Pandora Avatar world
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          "I can tell you one thing about them, they're gonna be b**chin',"
          <a href="http://www.empireonline.com/news/story.asp?NID=42852" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           he told Empire magazine
          </a>
          . "You will s**t yourself with your mouth wide open."
         </p>
         <p>
          The director also took time to speak about the movie's frame rate, saying, "My thinking at the time [when he announced the sequels] was that 60 [FPS] might be a better segue to the video market.I'll be plugging into a system that's a little more mature, so it makes sense for me to do 48 frames at this point."
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           More:
           <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/article/james-cameron-avatar-sequels_4155793">
            James Cameron to shoot Avatar 2, 3 and 4 simultaneously
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Cameron, 60, is currently working on the scripts for Avatar 2, 3 and 4 in New Zealand having previously met with writers Josh Friedman, Amanda Silver, Shane Salerno and Rick Jaffa to "whiteboard" every scene in every movie, before assigning each writer a movie.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="Avatar World" height="370" src="http://images.contactmusic.com/images/feature-images/avatar-disney-animal-kingdom-jungle-concept-art-636-370.jpg" style="vertical-align: top; border: 1px solid black; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Avatar World" width="636"/>
          <span style="font-size: x-small;">
           Avatar World would be an alternative to Disney World
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          "I knew if I assigned them their scripts ahead of time, they'd tune out every time we were talking about the other movie," he said.
         </p>
         <p>
          Original stars
          <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/info/sam_worthington">
           Sam Worthington
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/info/zoe_saldana">
           Zoe Saldana
          </a>
          are reprising their roles as Jake Sully and Neytiri in the sequel, while Sigourney Weaver is also believed to be returning despite her character Grace seemingly dying in the first movie.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           More:
           <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/article/james-cameron-terminator-genesis_4225625">
            James Cameron is invovled in
            <em>
             Terminator: Genesis
            </em>
            . Which is good news.
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Avatar 2
          </em>
          is expected to hit cinemas on Christmas Day 2016, with the second in 2017 and third in 2018. The original movie remains the highest-grossing movie of all time, taking $2.8 billion at the global box office and pushing the long-time number-one,
          <em>
           Titanic
          </em>
          , into second place with $2.2 billion.
          <em>
           The Avengers, Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part 2
          </em>
          and Disney's
          <em>
           Frozen
          </em>
          - the biggest grossing animated movie of all time - make up the top 5.
         </p>
