            <div class="content">
             BTW, I was wondering why they don't use modern wing style parachutes that can actually fly.  Looked it up.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             ANSWER: Ram-air wing type parachutes can collapse, especially in collisions.  They also fly forwards at fairly high speeds.  When you deploy a whole bunch of soldiers, you want them to land close together, you drop them close together, and sometimes in poor visibility.  If they were all flying fast, there would be collisions, chutes would collapse, and people would plummet.  Second, even if all the above went OK, they would take much longer to descend, and that is a time when they are often vulnerable targets in the air.  Third, it takes a lot more training and skill to fly them, and they typically don't want to train all their troops that much, so your average bunch of grunts gets basic parachute drop training, and that's it.  These plain dome-style parachutes don't collapse, even in collisions, and it takes no real skill to fly them, so they are foolproof, and fast to descend.
