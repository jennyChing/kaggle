     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Jaquet-Droz automata
      </b>
      , among all the numerous
      <a href="/wiki/Automaton" title="Automaton">
       automata
      </a>
      built by the Jaquet-Droz family, refer to three doll automata built between 1768 and 1774 by
      <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Jaquet-Droz" title="Pierre Jaquet-Droz">
       Pierre Jaquet-Droz
      </a>
      , his son Henri-Louis, and Jean-Frdric Leschot: the
      <i>
       <b>
        musician
       </b>
      </i>
      , the
      <i>
       <b>
        draughtsman
       </b>
      </i>
      and the
      <i>
       <b>
        writer
       </b>
      </i>
      . The dolls are still functional, and can be seen at the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9e_d%27Art_et_d%27Histoire_(Neuch%C3%A2tel)" title="Muse d'Art et d'Histoire (Neuchtel)">
        Muse d'Art et d'Histoire
       </a>
      </i>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Neuch%C3%A2tel" title="Neuchtel">
       Neuchtel
      </a>
      , in
      <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
       Switzerland
      </a>
      . They are considered to be among the remote ancestors of modern computers.
     </p>
     <p>
      The automata were designed and built by Pierre Jaquet-Droz, Henri-Louis Jaquet-Droz and Jean-Frdric Leschot as advertisement and entertainment toys designed to improve the sales of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watches" title="Watches">
       watches
      </a>
      among the nobility of Europe in the 18th century. They were carried around, and lost at several points. The History and Archeology society of Neuchtel eventually bought them in 1906, for 75,000 gold francs, and gave them to the museum, where they have been ever since.
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_musician">
       The musician
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jaquet-Droz_automata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The musician">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030490.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1920" data-file-width="2560" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030490.jpg/220px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030490.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030490.jpg/330px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030490.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030490.jpg/440px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030490.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030490.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The Musician
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The musician is a female organ player. The music is not faked, in the sense that it is not recorded or played by a musical box: the doll is actually playing a genuine (yet custom-built) instrument by pressing the keys with her fingers. She "breathes" (the movements of the chest can be seen), follows her fingers with her head and eyes, and also makes some of the movements that a real player would dobalancing the torso for instance.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_draughtsman">
       The draughtsman
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jaquet-Droz_automata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The draughtsman">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030496.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2560" data-file-width="1727" height="252" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030496.jpg/170px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030496.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030496.jpg/255px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030496.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030496.jpg/340px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030496.jpg 2x" width="170"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">

         </a>
        </div>
        The draughtsman
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030415.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1767" data-file-width="1467" height="205" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030415.jpg/170px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030415.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030415.jpg/255px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030415.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030415.jpg/340px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030415.jpg 2x" width="170"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030415.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Two of the drawings which can be made by the draughtsman
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The draughtsman is a young child who can actually draw four different images: a portrait of
      <a href="/wiki/Louis_XV_of_France" title="Louis XV of France">
       Louis XV
      </a>
      , a royal couple (believed to be
      <a href="/wiki/Marie_Antoinette" title="Marie Antoinette">
       Marie Antoinette
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">
       Louis XVI
      </a>
      ), a dog with "
      <i>
       Mon toutou
      </i>
      " ("my doggy") written beside it, and a scene of Cupid driving a chariot pulled by a butterfly.
     </p>
     <p>
      The draughtsman works by using a system of
      <a href="/wiki/Cam" title="Cam">
       cams
      </a>
      which code the movements of the hand in two dimensions, plus one to lift the pencil. The automaton also moves on his chair, and he periodically blows on the pencil to remove dust.
     </p>
     <div style="clear:both;">
     </div>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_writer">
       The writer
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jaquet-Droz_automata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The writer">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030493.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1905" data-file-width="2055" height="204" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030493.jpg/220px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030493.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030493.jpg/330px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030493.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030493.jpg/440px-Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030493.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Automates-Jaquet-Droz-p1030493.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        the Writer
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The writer is the most complex of the three automata. Using a system similar to the one used for the draughtsman for each letter, he is able to write any custom text up to 40 letters long (the text is rarely changed; one of the latest
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
         <span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (October 2013)">
          when?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      instances was in honour of president
      <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Mitterrand" title="Franois Mitterrand">
       Franois Mitterrand
      </a>
      when he toured the city). The text is coded on a wheel where characters are selected one by one. He uses a goose feather to write, which he inks from time to time, including a shake of the wrist to prevent ink from spilling. His eyes follow the text being written, and the head moves when he takes some ink.
     </p>
