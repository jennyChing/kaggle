        <h1 class="entry-title">
         True Blood (Or, PMS  What a Bitch)
        </h1>
        <!--BEGIN .entry-meta .entry-header-->
        <div class="entry-meta entry-header">
         <a href="http://elleroywashere.com/2012/05/02/pms/">
          <span class="published">
          </span>
         </a>
         <span class="comment-count">

          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          Written by
          <strong>
           <a class="url fn" href="http://elleroywashere.com/author/lindaroy/" title="View all posts by Linda Roy">
            Linda Roy
           </a>

        <div class="entry-content article">
         <div id="triberr_endorsement">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://i1.wp.com/elleroywashere.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/pmsgpsgum.jpg">
            <img alt="pmsgpsgum" class="aligncenter wp-image-4424" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/elleroywashere.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/pmsgpsgum.jpg?resize=359%2C188"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Let me put this delicately. I havent exactly been at my best lately.
          </p>
          <p>
           Im bloated, Im bitchy, Im continually hungry, Im pissed off, Im annoyed, Im upset aboutI dont evenknow what Im upset about, Im just upset,
           <em>
            okaaayyy?
           </em>
           Im irritable, I have a headache, Im tired and Im whiney. Basically, I just want to put on a pair ofstretchy pants, get under a blanket with the kids Eastercandy, lock the door and call it a day.
          </p>
          <p>
           Dont call me. Dont anybody ask me for anything. Dont tell me you didnt like what I made you for lunch. Dont ask me where your sneakers are. Mommy needs a time out.
          </p>
          <p>
           I feel sorry for the guys. I really do. And for that matter, the kids too. At least the guys know whats up. The kids are trapped in a vortex of confusion.My oldest finally sonknows now whento duck and cover. He and my husbandhad the talk a few years back, the one that goes There will be one week every month where Mom turns into Frankenstein. Keep your head down and get your own cereal. Itll blow over soon.
          </p>
          <p>
           Husband: SoMoms been a little crabby lately
          </p>
          <p>
           Son: I know why. We talked about it in Family Life class.
          </p>
          <p>
           H: Oh. So then you know that there will be times when you need to get out of Moms way, agree with whatever she says and not give her a hard time?
          </p>
          <p>
           S: Thats all the time, though, right?
          </p>
          <p>
           H: I mean especially at these times. You know, like how we practiced it.
          </p>
          <p>
           S:I know the drill: When I come home from school, I go to my room as fast as I can, shut the door, and when she calls me for dinner, I run downstairs immediately.
          </p>
          <p>
           You guys put up with a lot of shit, butimagine how we feel? Its not like we enjoy ripping you a new one every time you leaveyour socks on the floor. That shits
           <em>
            exhausting
           </em>
           . The regret, the remorse. Its paralyzing. Thats why we sit on the couch in our sweats eating ice cream. Its an involuntary cycle of volcanic Hell being foisted upon us by an unruly Mother Nature. You understand, right?
           <b>
            <i>
             RIGHT?! ANSWER MEEEE, GODDAMMITTTTT!!!!!
            </i>
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" border="0" class="alignleft" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/2.bp.blogspot.com/-A3Lvq6rxGC4/T6GjqPQqf9I/AAAAAAAAAgU/r_UDEjixuVo/s200/pms.jpg?resize=156%2C200"/>
           My husbandhas it down to a science by now. He knows my cycle better than I do. I swear he literally has a flow chart somewhere. He well-schooled in the art of buying chocolate and ordering take- out. Also,he knows tonever, ever again tell me to relax. Dont
           <em>
            ever
           </em>
           tell a stabby woman to relax. This will only bring on more daggers.
          </p>
          <p>
           As clueless as this may seem,sometimes even I dont realize whats going on with me until Im handedajumbo-sized chocolate bar. My beloved will comehome with milk, bread and alife saving brick ofGodiva dark chocolate. And by life saving, I mean his. He slaps the bar down on the counter with a look of resignation on his face before scurrying off to the furthest reaches of our dwelling, completely unscathed as I tear open the wrapper and began feeding like a reanimated corpse on a fresh kill.
          </p>
          <p>
           He also knows not to say a word when I mention that I want to go to Burger King havinggiven him a strict mandate to save me from myself should I grab my keys andhead to the door announcing that Im going to run errands. He knows all too wellthat on certain days, there is a price to pay for intervention.
          </p>
          <p>
           I cant be held responsible for ill-advised food consumption during these trying times. Once, I caught myself standing at the cupboard shoveling chips into my visage with all the perfunctory madness of a three year old on a booger high.So completely devil-may-care was I, that I also polished off a sleeve of IKEA cookies. IKEA cookies, people.
          </p>
          <p>
           Im just one voice mail from my mother away from exploding, when my beloved walks in the door with fast food for dinner, greasy fries and all, and thats when all is right with the world. For a little while anyway. This too shall pass. And tomorrows gonna be another day. One day closer to a happy familial existence free from blood letting.
          </p>
          <p>
           Until nextmonth, suckers.
          </p>
