    <span class="photo-breakout photo-left small">
     <img alt="TR.Guestroom.JPG" src="http://media.oregonlive.com/terryrichard/photo/trguestroomjpg-e9fc9eaec6120f6a_small.jpg"/>

   A Seattle-based Web site,
   <a href="http://www.casacasa.org/">
    www.casacasa.org
   </a>
   , is soliciting members to share affordable places to stay.
   <p>
    The concept is simple.
   </p>
   <p>
    Join the club and make available a guest room in your house for other members to stay in. This allows you to ask other members if you can stay with them when you travel.
   </p>
   <p>
    Minimum age to join is 24.  The Web site is in its infancy and membership is just beginning to grow.
   </p>
   <p>
    Members have already joined from San Miguel de Allende, Mexico, and Marrakech, Morocco, so this could spur dreams of a classy vacation.
   </p>
   <p>
    There are also enough members in British Columbia so that another member could conceivably put together a one-week vacation at other members' houses.
   </p>
   <p>
    And these aren't couches or floor space. The B.C. members have guest rooms in high-rise condos in Vancouver and waterfront lots on Vancouver Island to share.
   </p>
   <p>
    Membership is $20 annually right now, though it will increase to $40 in the future. Those who join commit only to making a room in their house (or apartment) available to another member once or twice a year.
   </p>
   <p>
    Membership appeals to travelers who want more authentic experiences than traditional lodgings. The only money that changes hands during a stay is a $15 gratuity (or $20 for two people) per day. The host is obligated to provide breakfast.
   </p>
   <p>
    The membership application seeks information about member ages, interests and hobbies, to help match compatibility. Members are also asked to upload a photo of themselves, their house and their guest room.
   </p>
   <p>
    Refer to the casacasa.org Web site for full details.
   </p>
   <p>
    The site is owned by Lauren Braden, who also has
    <a href="http://www.nwcheapsleeps.org/">
     www.nwcheapsleeps.org
    </a>
    . She modeled casacasa.org after her mother's Web site,
    <a href="http://www.affordabletravelclub.net/">
     www.affordabletravelclub.net
    </a>
    , a site for retired travelers.
   </p>
   <p>

     -- Terry Richard
