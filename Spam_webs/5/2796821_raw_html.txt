           <div class="alt_panel2">
            Late PLO leader Yasser Arafat (R) prepares to leave Ramallah in October 2004 to fly to Paris for treatment for what was then an unknown illness.
           </div>
           <div class="lead">
            A report by one of the worlds leading medical journals has supported earlier findings that Yasser Arafat, the late leader of the Palestine Liberation Organization, was poisoned to death nearly a decade ago.
           </div>
           <div class="body">
            <p dir="ltr">
             According to British journal the Lancet, Arafat was poisoned with the radioactive element polonium 210.
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
             The journal published a peer review of last years research by Swiss scientists, who have been researching the suspicious circumstances surrounding Arafats death.
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
             The earlier work found high levels of the highly radioactive element in blood, urine, and saliva stains on Arafats clothes and toothbrush, according to a report on al-Jazeera.
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
             In July 2012, experts at Lausanne University, Switzerland, said they had evidence Arafat might have been poisoned with polonium.
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
             The investigation into Arafats mysterious death led to the exhumation of his body in November 2012 for further testing.
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
             The decision to exhume Arafats body was made after French prosecutors opened a murder probe into his death in August 2012 following the discovery of high levels of polonium on his personal belongings.
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
             Arafat died in 2004 at the age of 75 in a Paris military hospital.
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
             The analysis at the time was that he had a rare blood disorder.
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
