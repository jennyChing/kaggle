        <h1>
         5 Reasons We're In the Middle of a Dumb Anti-Photoshop Panic
        </h1>
       </header>
       <footer class="lineDottedBottom">
        <div class="meta">
         <span class="by">

       <section>
        <p>
         Word on the street is we're all mad about photographers and advertising agencies using Photoshop to make models look supernaturally good. They've decided, among many ludicrous things, that some people
         <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/news/ralph-lauren-strikes-again-is-photoshop-disaster-company-policy/" target="C">
          totally have waists smaller than their heads
         </a>
         and that Anne Hathaway deserves an Oscar but
         <a href="http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/us-wrap-was-anne-hathaways-armpit-photoshopped-201082" target="C">
          not an armpit
         </a>
         . Luckily, other people who would also like to sell you things have found a way to capitalize on the anger caused by literally impossible beauty standards -- denouncing the airbrush.
        </p>
        <p>
         The most prominent has been Dove and their
         <a href="http://www.takepart.com/article/2014/01/30/10-years-after-doves-real-beauty-campaign-brands-are-fighting-female-form" target="C">
          "Real Beauty" campaign
         </a>
         , but it's really picking up this year, with all the fuss over Keira Knightley's
         <a href="http://www.thetimes.co.uk/tto/life/celebrity/article4257644.ece" target="C">
          refusal to be retouched
         </a>
         for a spread in Interview magazine, fashion company ModCloth's
         <a href="http://www.today.com/style/modcloth-first-retailer-take-no-photoshop-pledge-1D80069154" target="C">
          "no Photoshop" pledge
         </a>
         , and the
         <a href="http://www.fastcocreate.com/3025443/this-is-who-we-are-now-why-aerie-stopped-retouching-the-models-in-its-ads" target="C">
          redundantly named "Real" campaign
         </a>
         by American Eagle's lingerie line. These body-image crusaders claim to be taking back the power of, um ... realness. Realhood?
        </p>
        <p>
         Unfortunately, they're completely, pornographically fucking with you. Because ...
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #5. It's Been Around Since Before Cameras Even Existed
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/9/8/380698_v1.jpg" height="203" width="610"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 610px; text-align: right;">
          Comstock Images/Stockbyte/Getty Images
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ever since the very first spoiled heiresses demanded to have their inbred faces immortalized, the brush-jockeys they hired have wisely decided to
         <a href="http://www.fastcodesign.com/3025262/a-brief-history-of-retouching" target="C">
          make a few adjustments
         </a>
         . The artist got paid, the subject got a tangible representation of their overinflated ego, everyone was happy. The artist attending to the royal Medici family in the 1500s was known to paint his subjects to "highlight [the subject's] learning and social status," which is a nice way of saying "make them look less like peasant trash," and that was pretty much the standard at the time.
        </p>
        <p>
         The truth is, we likely have no idea what anyone from that time period actually looked like. Every pre-Industrial historical figure could have had a big floppy dick growing out of their chin and we would never know. So let's just assume that they did.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/9/6/380696.jpg?v=1" height="288" width="225"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 225px; text-align: right;">
          <a href=" http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Shakespeare.jpg" target="C">
           Wikipedia
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Are you picturing it?
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         It didn't stop once photography became a thing. About two minutes after people started taking pictures, they started messing around with darkroom techniques to make shit not look like shit. If you think you need a computer to make someone look like a cartoon character, check out Joan Crawford kicking it old-school.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/7/0/0/380700_v1.jpg" height="262" width="450"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 450px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://evanbaines.blogspot.ca/2009/01/abbreviated-history-of-photo.html" target="C">
           Geoge Hurrell
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          From left to right: hideous she-beast, beloved American icon.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         If there's one upside to this centuries-old tabloid horseshit, it's that it's made us way more aware of how much they're shoveling onto us.
         <a href="http://www.cracked.com/blog/13-photos-that-shatter-your-image-famous-people-part-2/" target="C">
          What Katy Perry looks like first thing in the morning
         </a>
         might seem like the grossest kind of non-news, but knowing how much effort and flat-out voodoo goes into creating a beauty icon is really important and kind of unprecedented. If that's our goal, to spread awareness that not even Katy Perry looks like Katy Perry without several hours of makeup and Photoshop ... that knowledge itself is a pretty big step up from the entire last 500 years. It seems like that's more important than the actual Photoshopping and makeupping. Speaking of which ...
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #4. They Can Get Around It Using Makeup
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/7/1/2/380712_v1.jpg" height="203" width="610"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 610px; text-align: right;">
          Medioimages/Photodisc/Photodisc/Getty Images
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         There are all kinds of physical tricks models and photographers can use to render Photoshop unnecessary, which is arguably way more insidious. You'd be surprised how much can be accomplished with makeup. After all, models don't exist in some weird digital dimension where everything is made of 2D pixels -- they have to appear in person sometimes, at their stilt-walking parades. (
         <i>
          Edit: I have been informed that these are called "fashion shows."
         </i>
         ) At the goofy-shoe contests, they can (and do) use body makeup to create shadows and highlights that turn their butts into completely different butts. One
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/11/13/victorias-secret-models_n_4266776.html" target="C">
          Victoria's Secret model
         </a>
         said that sometimes she has 20 layers of makeup on her butt, which is like at least 16 more than most people wear for their jobs. If you're skilled enough, you can even
         <a href="http://www.whudat.de/makeup-artist-lucia-pittalis-transforms-herself-to-look-like-famous-celebrities-10-pictures/" target="C">
          paint someone else's face
         </a>
         directly on top of yours.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/7/0/8/380708_v1.jpg" height="315" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://www.whudat.de/makeup-artist-lucia-pittalis-transforms-herself-to-look-like-famous-celebrities-10-pictures/" target="C">
           Lucia Pittalis
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          This woman chose Sylvester Stallone's for some reason.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         And that's just the tip of the bullshit iceberg. Laughably simple things like lighting, posing, clothing, and hairstyle can have a startling impact. Here's personal trainer Melanie Ventura, who lost 15 pounds in 15 minutes just by opening the curtains and standing up straight.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/7/1/0/380710_v2.jpg" height="633" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/11/18/transformation-photos_n_4295391.html" target="C">
           PsychGuides.com
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Who knew your mom gave such good selfie advice?
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         That's not to say that any of that is bad on its own. We've all made an unspoken social agreement that some women paint their eyes to resemble a cat's, and that's just fine. Anyone who thinks my nails are naturally glittery has bigger problems than I can address. But you couldn't be faulted for not knowing that models get the full sci-fi special-effects treatment, which is why if we got rid of Photoshop they would just lean on these techniques more. Of course, it doesn't hurt that ...
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #3. Models Are Not "Real Women" in the First Place
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/7/2/2/380722_v1.jpg" height="203" width="610"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 610px; text-align: right;">
          Andrey Burmakin/iStock/Getty Images
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Let's all give a round of applause to Keira Knightley for telling Interview magazine, "I said, 'OK, I'm fine doing the topless shot so long as you don't make them any bigger or retouch.' Because it does feel important to say it really doesn't matter what shape you are." Yep, it sure was brave of her to display her natural body
         <a href="http://www.interviewmagazine.com/fashion/keira-knightley-by-patrick-demarchelier#slideshow_67566.1" target="C">
          in all its hideous imperfection
         </a>
         . (Warning: Bare tits all up in your grill.)
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/7/1/4/380714_v1.jpg" height="313" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://www.interviewmagazine.com/fashion/keira-knightley-by-patrick-demarchelier#" target="C">
           Interview Magazine (NSFW)
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Ugh, god, get that troll off my screen.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Right. Even without Photoshop, Keira Knightley is a breathtakingly gorgeous woman who represents a standard of beauty that is not attainable for 99 percent of women. It's worth noting that Knightley's only real problem with Photoshop is that
         <a href="http://www.eonline.com/news/362642/keira-knightley-topless-on-allure-talks-anorexia-rumors-getting-fantasy-breasts" target="C">
          she thinks it makes her look
         </a>
         <i>
          worse
         </i>
         . It's kind of weird to hold this up as a triumph for body acceptance when the very reason you made the decision was based around presenting your body in the most flattering way.
        </p>
        <p>
         It's a funny thing about models: They're all naturally beautiful, otherwise they would be accountants and not models. In order to look like Britney Spears
         <a href="http://celebrities.ninemsn.com.au/blog.aspx?blogentryid=633229&amp;showcomments=true" target="C">
          even before Photoshop
         </a>
         , I would need a very expensive surgeon and a full-time job as a treadmill tester, and I am including her paparazzi umbrella warrior phase. You know who else I totally don't look like? The
         <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/magazine-20252921" target="C">
          Dove Real Beauty
         </a>
         models.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/7/1/6/380716_v2.jpg" height="216" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Dove
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Other people I don't look like: ModCloth's "real women."
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/7/1/8/380718_v1.jpg" height="299" width="250"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 250px; text-align: right;">
          ModCloth
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          I wouldn't be caught dead with that basket.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Still more people I don't
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/01/17/aerie-unretouched-ads-photos_n_4618139.html" target="C">
          look like
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/7/2/0/380720_v1.jpg" height="218" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Aerie
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          I'm just trying to figure out what that hole's for.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Turns out you (
         <a href="http://adage.com/article/news/dove-s-real-beauty-pics-big-phonies/126914/" target="C">
          allegedly
         </a>
         ) don't need to Photoshop women who don't need Photoshop. To tout these images as a "challenge [to] supermodel standards" is like saying Freddie Mercury is a challenge to Queen singer standards. These companies are not unaware of that. Because ...
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <a href="http://society6.com/product/binary-art_t-shirt?utm_campaign=1301#11=49&amp;4=104&amp;utm_source=cracked.com&amp;utm_medium=crackedendofarticle&amp;utm_campaign=crackedhouseads#11=49&amp;4=75" target="_blank">
          <img src="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/9/2/2/377922.jpg?v=1"/>
         </a>
        </p>
