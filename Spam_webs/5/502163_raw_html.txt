   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Rack (torture)
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="hatnote">
      "Torture rack" redirects here. It is not to be confused with the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentine_backbreaker_rack" title="Argentine backbreaker rack">
       Argentine backbreaker rack
      </a>
      used in
      <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">

      </a>
      .
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:A_Torture_Rack.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1800" data-file-width="1200" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/A_Torture_Rack.jpg/200px-A_Torture_Rack.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/A_Torture_Rack.jpg/300px-A_Torture_Rack.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/A_Torture_Rack.jpg/400px-A_Torture_Rack.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:A_Torture_Rack.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        A torture rack in the
        <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_London" title="Tower of London">
         Tower of London
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       rack
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Torture" title="Torture">
       torture
      </a>
      device consisting of a rectangular, usually wooden frame, slightly raised from the ground, with a roller at one or both ends. The victim's ankles are fastened to one roller and the wrists are chained to the other. As the interrogation progresses, a handle and
      <a href="/wiki/Ratchet_(device)" title="Ratchet (device)">
       ratchet mechanism
      </a>
      attached to the top roller are used to very gradually increase the tension on the chains, inducing excruciating pain. By means of
      <a href="/wiki/Pulley" title="Pulley">
       pulleys
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Lever" title="Lever">
       levers
      </a>
      this roller could be rotated on its own axis, thus straining the ropes until the sufferer's
      <a href="/wiki/Joint" title="Joint">
       joints
      </a>
      were
      <a href="/wiki/Joint_dislocation" title="Joint dislocation">
       dislocated
      </a>
      and eventually separated. Additionally, if
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muscle_fibre" title="Muscle fibre">
       muscle fibres
      </a>
      are stretched excessively, they lose their ability to
      <a href="/wiki/Muscle_contraction" title="Muscle contraction">
       contract
      </a>
      , rendering them ineffective.
     </p>
     <p>
      One gruesome aspect of being stretched too far on the rack is the loud popping noises made by snapping
      <a href="/wiki/Cartilage" title="Cartilage">
       cartilage
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ligament" title="Ligament">
       ligaments
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Bone" title="Bone">
       bones
      </a>
      . One powerful method for putting pressure upon prisoners was to force them to watch someone else being subjected to the rack. Confining the prisoner on the rack enabled further tortures to be simultaneously applied, typically including burning the flanks with hot torches or candles or using pincers made with specially roughened grips to
      <a href="/wiki/Denailing" title="Denailing">
       tear out the nails
      </a>
      of the fingers and toes. Usually , the victim's shoulders and hips would be separated and their elbows, knees, wrists, and ankles would be dislocated.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

      <span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">

     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Theresiana-Leiter.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="706" data-file-width="460" height="307" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Theresiana-Leiter.jpg/200px-Theresiana-Leiter.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Theresiana-Leiter.jpg/300px-Theresiana-Leiter.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Theresiana-Leiter.jpg/400px-Theresiana-Leiter.jpg 2x" width="200"/>

        Constitutio Criminalis Theresiana (1768)  the approved methods of torture which could be used by the legal authorities to arrive at the truth.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_use">
       Early use
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rack_(torture)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early use">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      According to
      <a href="/wiki/Tacitus" title="Tacitus">
       Tacitus
      </a>
      , the rack was used to extract the names of the conspirators to assassinate
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Nero" title="Emperor Nero">
       Emperor Nero
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/Pisonian_conspiracy" title="Pisonian conspiracy">
       Pisonian conspiracy
      </a>
      from the freedwoman
      <a href="/wiki/Epicharis_(Pisonian_conspirator)" title="Epicharis (Pisonian conspirator)">
       Epicharis
      </a>
      in 65 A.D. The next day, after refusing to talk, she was being dragged back to the rack on a chair (all of her limbs were
      <a href="/wiki/Joint_dislocation" title="Joint dislocation">
       dislocated
      </a>
      , so she could not stand), but strangled herself on a loop of cord on the back of the chair on the way.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      It was used during
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">
       antiquity
      </a>
      , being used on
      <a href="/wiki/Vincent_of_Saragossa" title="Vincent of Saragossa">
       St. Vincent
      </a>
      (304 A.D.) and mentioned by the Church Fathers
      <a href="/wiki/Tertullian" title="Tertullian">
       Tertullian
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Jerome" title="St. Jerome">
       St. Jerome
      </a>
      (420 A.D.).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Medieval_Britain">
       Medieval Britain
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rack_(torture)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Medieval Britain">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Its first appearance in England is said to have been due to
      <a href="/wiki/John_Holland,_2nd_Duke_of_Exeter" title="John Holland, 2nd Duke of Exeter">
       John Holland, 2nd Duke of Exeter
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Constable_of_the_Tower" title="Constable of the Tower">
       constable of the Tower
      </a>
      in 1447, whence it was popularly known as the
      <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Exeter%27s_daughter" title="Duke of Exeter's daughter">
       Duke of Exeter's daughter
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1628 the question of its legality was raised by a proposal in the
      <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_England" title="Privy Council of England">
       Privy Council
      </a>
      to rack
      <a href="/wiki/John_Felton_(assassin)" title="John Felton (assassin)">
       John Felton
      </a>
      , the assassin of the
      <a href="/wiki/George_Villiers,_1st_Duke_of_Buckingham" title="George Villiers, 1st Duke of Buckingham">
       Duke of Buckingham
      </a>
      . The judges resisted this, unanimously declaring its use to be contrary to the laws of England. The previous year
      <a href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_England" title="Charles I of England">
       Charles I
      </a>
      had authorised the Irish Courts to rack a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic_priest" title="Catholic priest">
       Catholic priest
      </a>
      ; this seems to have been the last time the rack was used in
      <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">
       Ireland
      </a>
      . In 1679
      <a href="/wiki/Miles_Prance" title="Miles Prance">
       Miles Prance
      </a>
      , being questioned about the murder of Sir
      <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Berry_Godfrey" title="Edmund Berry Godfrey">
       Edmund Berry Godfrey
      </a>
      , was threatened with the rack, although it is unlikely that the threat was a serious one.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Guy_Fawkes" title="Guy Fawkes">
       Guy Fawkes
      </a>
      is also thought to have been put to the rack, since a royal warrant authorising his torture survives, ordering that the "lesser tortures" should be applied to him first, but that if he remained recalcitrant he could be racked.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="France">
       France
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rack_(torture)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: France">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The French introduced an "improvement" to the rack in the form of spiked rollers that were inserted under the spine of the victim, thus causing even more severe pain and damage.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Inquisition">
       Inquisition
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rack_(torture)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Inquisition">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Inquisition" title="Inquisition">
       Inquisition
      </a>
      adopted another method of torture. The sufferer was bound (usually at their wrists) and suspended, lifted slowly and dropped one or more times. This technique provoked fractures of arms and shoulders. It had to last less than half an hour, since keeping victims in such a position for longer periods could lead to death.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Russia">
       Russia
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rack_(torture)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Russia">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Supplice_du_Grand_Knout.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3182" data-file-width="2559" height="311" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Supplice_du_Grand_Knout.jpg/250px-Supplice_du_Grand_Knout.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Supplice_du_Grand_Knout.jpg/375px-Supplice_du_Grand_Knout.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Supplice_du_Grand_Knout.jpg/500px-Supplice_du_Grand_Knout.jpg 2x" width="250"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Supplice_du_Grand_Knout.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        "Punishment with a Great Knout"
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In Russia up to the 18th century the rack (
      <i>
       
      </i>
      , dyba) was a
      <a href="/wiki/Gallows" title="Gallows">
       gallows
      </a>
      -like device for suspending the victims (
      <a href="/wiki/Strappado" title="Strappado">
       strappado
      </a>
      ). The suspended victims were whipped with
      <a href="/wiki/Knout" title="Knout">
       knout
      </a>
      and sometimes burned with hot torches.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div style="clear:both;">
     </div>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_punitive_positioning_devices">
       Other punitive positioning devices
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rack_(torture)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Other punitive positioning devices">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The term rack is also used, occasionally, for a number of simpler constructions that merely facilitate
      <a href="/wiki/Corporal_punishment" title="Corporal punishment">
       corporal punishment
      </a>
      , after which it may be named specifically, e.g.,
      <i>
       caning rack
      </i>
      , as in a given jurisdiction it was often the custom to administer any given punishment in a specific position, for which the device (with or without fitting shackling and/or padding) would be chosen or specially made.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">

     <p>
      A similar device was the
      <i>
       intestinal crank
      </i>
      . This method of torture involved making an incision in the abdominal area, separating the
      <a href="/wiki/Duodenum" title="Duodenum">
       duodenum
      </a>
      from the
      <a href="/wiki/Pylorus" title="Pylorus">
       pylorus
      </a>
      , and attaching of the upper part of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intestine" title="Intestine">
       intestine
      </a>
      to a crank. The crank would then be rotated to extract the intestines from the gastrointestinal cavity of a conscious person.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Several devices similar in principle to the rack have been used through the ages. One of these was the
      <a href="/wiki/Wooden_horse_(device)" title="Wooden horse (device)">
       Wooden Horse
      </a>
      , a device used to torture prisoners during the Roman Empire by stretching them on top of a tall wooden frame until the shoulders were dislocated followed by a violent drop into a hanging position and beating. In another variant used primarily in ancient times, the victim's feet were affixed to the ground and his/her hands were chained to a wheel. When the wheel was turned, the person was stretched in a manner similar to the rack. The
      <i>
       Austrian Ladder
      </i>
      was basically a more vertically oriented rack. As part of the torture, victims would usually be burned under the arms with candles.
     </p>
