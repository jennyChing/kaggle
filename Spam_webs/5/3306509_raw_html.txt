    <h1 class="header">
     <span class="header-ext header-date" itemprop="datePublished">
      31.1.2014
     </span>
     Nissan installs 1000th rapid charger in EU
    </h1>
    <div class="l-grid-2 l-left article">
     <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/NewsArticle">
      <section class="l-top">
       <div class="media">
        <a class="" href="/news/6507/Nissan-installs-1000th-rapid-charger-in-EU">
         <img alt="Nissan installs 1000th rapid charger in EU" class="" src="/i/news_xlarge/nissan-ecotricity-rapid-charger.jpg" title="Nissan installs 1000th rapid charger in EU"/>
        </a>
        <div class="media-body media-overlay">
         <h3 class="media-bd-title media-article-title">
          <a href="/news/6507/Nissan-installs-1000th-rapid-charger-in-EU">
           Nissan LEAF charging at Clacket Lane rapid charger
          </a>
         </h3>
        </div>
       </div>
      </section>
      <p>
       Nissan has installed 1,000 rapid DC chargers across Europe, the 1,000th being at the Roadchef Clacket Lane Services in Surrey, UK.
      </p>
      <p>
       The rapid charging units can recharge the batteries of compatible electric vehicles  including the Nissan LEAF, Citroen C-ZERO, Mitsubishi i-MiEV and Outlander PHEV, and the Peugeot iOn  from zero to 80% charge in just 30 minutes, and at zero cost (on this network).
      </p>
      <p>
       The installation of the fastest type of chargers will continue to increases the uptake and usage of electric vehicles. In Norway, Europe's biggest EV market, the number of electric vehicles using the E18 highway increased eight fold in an 18 month period after a CHAdeMO quick charger was installed on the route.
      </p>
      <p>
       The new charger has been installed in collaboration with Ecotricity, a UK green energy specialist and pioneer in electric vehicle charging. The location south of London on the M25 motorway, one of the busiest in Europe, allows drivers west of London easy access to Kent and onwards into Europe.
      </p>
      <p>
       Director of Electric Vehicles at Nissan Europe, Jean Pierre Diernaz commented: "This is a huge landmark for zero-emission mobility, allowing a range of EVs, including the
       <a class="link" href="http://www.nextgreencar.com/model-list/NISSAN/Leaf/">
        Nissan LEAF
       </a>
       and forthcoming Nissan e-NV200 electric van, to quickly extend their journeys. The UK charging network is expanding rapidly and through our partner Ecotricity, customers are able to 'refuel' their car for free with wind and solar generated electricity."
      </p>
      <p>
       This latest charger is part of a network of 195
       <a class="link" href="http://www.zap-map.com/">
        charging points
       </a>
       in the UK, which is forming electric corridors across the country, linking major towns and cities. In the UK, Nissan has been working with partners including IKEA, Moto, Roadchef and Welcome Break to create this rapidly growing network with 124 quick chargers installed in 2013.
      </p>
      <p>
       The rate of installation of CHAdeMO rapid chargers across Europe rose sharply in 2013, hugely increasing access for Nissan LEAF customers. In 2010 there were just 16 quick charging points. This rose to 155 a year later and 540 in 2012. The UK is now home to 18% of rapid chargers installed by Nissan across Europe.
      </p>
      <p>
       The 1,000 mark in 2013 will be dwarfed by the end of 2014 with over 1,800 rapid charging points expected. Together with Nissan, investment in this Europe-wide development of infrastructure comes from a multitude of partners in the energy field, including the Swiss multinational power company ABB, French quick charger manufacturers DBT, and the Portuguese EFACEC Corporation, leaders in the electro-mechanics field.
      </p>
      <p>
       To view all the locations of the UK's rapid network, visit
       <a class="link" href="http://www.zap-map.com/">
        Next Green Car's Zap-Map
       </a>
       .
      </p>
