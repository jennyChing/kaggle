     <div class="moat-trackable pb-f-theme-normal pb-2 pb-feature pb-layout-item pb-f-article-article-body" id="fx5aa92LleSGfp" moat-id="article/article-body|local">
      <div class="article-body" id="article-body">
       <div class="pb-sig-line hasnt-headshot has-0-headshots hasnt-bio is-not-column">
        <span class="pb-byline" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
         By
         <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/people/jason-samenow">
          <span itemprop="name">
           Jason Samenow
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="pb-timestamp" content="2014-09-11T02:49-500" itemprop="datePublished">
         September 11, 2014
        </span>
        <span class="pb-tool email">
         <a href="mailto:jason.samenow@washpost.com?subject=Reader feedback for 'A cool summer, and a cool year so far for the eastern U.S.; average for U.S. overall'">
          <span class="fa fa-envelope">
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="tweet-authors">
         <span class="pb-twitter-follow unprocessed">
          <a class="twitter-follow-button" data-lang="en" data-show-count="false" href="https://twitter.com/@capitalweather">
           Follow @capitalweather
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
       </div>
       <article itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         The data are in, and its official: it was a cool summer in the eastern U.S.
        </p>
        <p>
         
         <a href="http://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/temp-and-precip/maps.php?imgs[]=statewidetavgrank&amp;year=2014&amp;month=8&amp;ts=3&amp;submitted=Submit" style="color: #0057a5;">
          Below-average summer temperatures
         </a>
         <span style="color: #444444;">
          were observed from the Rocky Mountains to much of the central U.S. and East Coast, reports the National Climatic Data Center (NCDC) in its August
          <a href="http://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/sotc/national/2014/8">
           U.S. State of the Climate
          </a>
          report.
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal">
         <a name="4a891c9dfb">
         </a>
         <img class="unprocessed is-gif" data-hi-res-src="https://images.washingtonpost.com/?url=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/files/2014/09/201406-201408.gif&amp;op=noop" data-low-res-src="https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/files/2014/09/201406-201408.gif&amp;w=480" src="https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/files/2014/09/201406-201408.gif&amp;w=480"/>
         <br/>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         For the nation as a whole, summer temperatures were near average as hotter than normal conditions in the West offset the cooler than normal conditions in the East. It was the coolest summer since 2009 for the U.S., but still a smidgen (0.3 degrees F) above the 1901-2000 average.
        </p>
        <p>
         Conditions in August were a duller version ofthe summer overall. Temperatures along the West Coast werewellabove normal, and near tobelow normal east of the Rockies. Averaged across the country, August was very close to normal, 0.1 degrees F above the 1901-2000 average.
        </p>
        <div class="inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal">
         <a name="fcdcae61c1">
         </a>
         <img class="unprocessed is-gif" data-hi-res-src="https://images.washingtonpost.com/?url=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/files/2014/09/201408-201408-1.gif&amp;op=noop" data-low-res-src="https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/files/2014/09/201408-201408-1.gif&amp;w=480" src="https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/files/2014/09/201408-201408-1.gif&amp;w=480"/>
         <br/>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Notably, Virginia had its 10th coolest August on record dating back to 1895. North Carolina, Virginia, and Maryland represented the coolest pocket of the nation with respect to normal during the month.
        </p>
        <p>
         The weather pattern so far in 2014 has been nothing if not consistent. In every season, the western U.S.has been warmer than normal and the central and eastern U.S. has been cooler than normal. A persistent dip in jet stream in the East has facilitatedthe occasional polar vortex intrusion, while the so-called ridiculously resilient ridge in the West has blockedstorms and madeit bake.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Related:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/wp/2014/09/03/u-s-weather-in-2014-is-more-bi-polar-than-ever/">
          U.S. weather in 2014 is more bi-Polar than ever
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal">
         <a name="c523af34b1">
         </a>
         <img class="unprocessed is-gif" data-hi-res-src="https://images.washingtonpost.com/?url=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/files/2014/09/201401-201408.gif&amp;op=noop" data-low-res-src="https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/files/2014/09/201401-201408.gif&amp;w=480" src="https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/files/2014/09/201401-201408.gif&amp;w=480"/>
         <br/>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Taking the hot West and the cool East together throughthe first 8 months of 2014, the temperature of the nation has balanced out near average,NCDCreports. Astemperatures have run warmer than normal in the U.S. in recent years, 2014 markedthe coldest first 8-months of a year since 1997, NCDC says.
        </p>
        <p>
         While U.S. temperatures have averaged near the long-term averagethis year, the globe has been considerably warmer. Through July, the global temperature in 2014
         <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/wp/2014/08/18/globe-sees-fourth-warmest-july-on-record-oceans-tie-for-warmest-july/">
          was tied for the third warmest on record dating back to 1880
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Related:
         <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/wp/2014/04/16/the-eastern-united-states-a-lonely-cold-pocket-on-a-feverish-planet/email/">
          The eastern United States: A lonely cold pocket on a feverish planet
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          (All graphics courtesy National Climatic Data Center)
         </em>
        </p>
