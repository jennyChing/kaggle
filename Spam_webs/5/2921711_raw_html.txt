         <p>
          It's Bastille Day!
         </p>
         <p>
          If only your 10th grade French teacher had taught you what you really need to know  impressing that special someone at the restaurant where youre maxing out your credit card. Dont let that stop you though, because what they say is true: France is a haven for food and wine enthusiasts of all levels, and French cuisine is a palate-expanding experience everyone should have.
         </p>
         <p>
          Try out some of my extensive choice vocabulary for avoiding snails, raw beef and frogs legs (although why would you want to?) or honing the basics for customizing your steak-frites and procuring the right water. Yes, you read that correctly. Bon apptit!
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Drinking
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Avec des glaons: On the rocks
          </li>
          <li>
           La bire: Beer
          </li>
          <li>
           Une bouteille: A bottle
          </li>
          <li>
           Le caf: Coffee (typically Espresso)
          </li>
          <li>
           Une crafe: A glass bottle (usually of wine)
          </li>
          <li>
           La carte de vins: The wine list
          </li>
          <li>
           Le cidre: Cider
          </li>
          <li>
           Le jus: Juice
          </li>
          <li>
           Le Kir: White wine with cassis or blackberry syrup
          </li>
          <li>
           Un pichet: A small pitcher (usually of wine)
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Things that are potentially disgusting (but are usually delicious)
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Les cuisses de grenouille: Frogs legs
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/07/12/weird-or-good-escargot">
            Les escargots: Snails served in-shell with garlic-parsley butter
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/07/07/fris%C3%A9e-salad-duck-livers-recipe">
            Le foie: Liver
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/10/03/fun-p%C3%A2t%C3%A9-sandwich-story">
            Lept: Liver mousse
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Le steak tartare: Seasoned, finely chopped raw beef
          </li>
          <li>
           Les rognons de veaux: Veal kidneys
          </li>
          <li>
           Le tte de veau: Veal head
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Know your meat
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           point: Medium rare
          </li>
          <li>
           Bien cuit: Well done
          </li>
          <li>
           Bleu, saignant: Rare (blue or bleeding)
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/04/01/our-steak-au-poivre-recipe">
            Le bifteck: Steak
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/09/29/chicken-beef-satays-peanut-sauce">
             la broche: Cooked on a skewer
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Carbonnise: Burned to a crisp
          </li>
          <li>
           La charcuterie: An assortment of cured and dried meats
          </li>
          <li>
           Le porc: Pork
          </li>
          <li>
           Une saucisse/Saucisson: Sausage
          </li>
          <li>
           Une tranche: A slice
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/05/22/roasted-veal-chops-artichokes-recipe">
            Le veau: Veal
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           La viande: Meat
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           All about chicken:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/06/27/plum-sauced-chicken-wings-recipe">
            Les ailes de poulet: Chicken wings
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/07/07/how-cook-juicy-chicken-breast">
            Le blanc de poulet: Chicken breast
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Le consommdu volaille: Chicken broth
          </li>
          <li>
           Le coquelet: Young male chicken
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/10/05/turkey-leg-roulade-recipe">
            La cuisse: Dark meat
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           La poulard: Young female chicken
          </li>
          <li>
           Le poulet/poule: Chicken
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/06/29/how-make-roast-chicken">
            Le poulet rti: Roast chicken
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           La volaille: White meat
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Great dishes
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Aoli: Garlic mayonnaise *try it on fries
          </li>
          <li>
           Aligot: Fondue-like mix of mashed potatoes and sharp cheese
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/05/20/lobster-cassoulet-recipe">
            Le cassoulet: Rich meat and bean casserole
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/05/28/how-make-sauerkraut">
            La choucroute: Sauerkraut (expect various kinds of pork)
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Le coq au vin: Chicken in red wine sauce
          </li>
          <li>
           La crpe: A very thin pancake, sweet or savory
          </li>
          <li>
           La galette: A savory crepe
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/search/apachesolr_search/oysters">
            Les huitres: Oysters
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/09/20/shepherds-pie-recipe">
            Le parmentier: Like shephards pie, ground meat covered with mashed potatoes
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/03/11/mussels-blt-recipe">
            Les moules: Mussels
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Le vol-au-vent: A small, light, savory pastry
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Menu Mainstays
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/07/12/word-day-amuse-bouche">
            Lamuse-bouche: A bite-sized hors doeuvre, compliments of the chef
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/04/11/simple-chive-butter-sauce-recipe">
            Le beurre: Butter
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/06/17/grilled-scallops-recipe">
            Les coquilles Saint-Jacques: Scallops
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/06/17/how-make-scrambled-eggs">
            Les oeufs: Eggs
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Le pain: Bread
          </li>
          <li>
           Les ptes: Pasta
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Hows it cooked?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/07/11/word-day-gratin">
            Au gratin
           </a>
           : Baked with cheese and breadcrumbs
          </li>
          <li>
            votre got: To your liking
          </li>
          <li>
           En cocotte: Cooked in a covered baking dish
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/08/14/what-duck-confit">
            Confit: Cooked in fat (either its own or the fat of something else)
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           En croute: Wrapped in pastry
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/06/09/5-awesome-stuffed-burger-recipes">
            Farci: Stuffed
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Frit(e): Fried
          </li>
          <li>
           Fum: Smoked
          </li>
          <li>
           Hach: Ground (meat)
          </li>
          <li>
           Un mli-mlo: An assortment
          </li>
          <li>
           Un morceau: A piece
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/05/11/what-makes-food-spicy">
            Piquant: Spicy
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/04/06/best-basic-pesto-recipe">
            Au pistou: With basil
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Provenal: Cooked with tomatoes, anchovies and olives
          </li>
          <li>
           Rti: Roasted
          </li>
          <li>
            la vapeur: Steamed
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Good to know
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           L'addition: The check (must be requested)
          </li>
          <li>
           Allrgique : Allergic to
          </li>
          <li>
           And for the Mrs./Miss: Et pour la madame/mademoiselle
          </li>
          <li>
           Lassiette: Plate
          </li>
          <li>
           La carte/le mnu: The menu
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.foodrepublic.com/2011/08/19/gallery-7-mystery-mushrooms">
            Les champignons: Mushrooms
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Compris/Inclus: Included
          </li>
          <li>
           Le cornichon: The tiniest pickle youve ever seen, accompanies charcuterie
          </li>
          <li>
           La cuisine: The kitchen
          </li>
          <li>
           La dgustation: Tasting menu
          </li>
          <li>
           Le djeuner: Lunch
          </li>
          <li>
           Dlicieux: Delicious
          </li>
          <li>
           Le dner: Dinner
          </li>
          <li>
           Du jour: Of the day
          </li>
          <li>
           Le fromage: Cheese
          </li>
          <li>
           Garon: What NOT to call the waiter, no matter how many times youve seen it in movies. Instead, signal the waiter with Monsieur/Madame/Mademoiselle, sil vous plait.
          </li>
          <li>
           Je voudrais: I would like
          </li>
          <li>
           Le petit djeuner: Breakfast
          </li>
          <li>
           Le poisson: Fish
          </li>
          <li>
           Les pommes de terre: Potatoes
          </li>
          <li>
           Le pourboire: Tip (basic gratuity is almost always included in French restaurants, listed as servis compris)
          </li>
          <li>
           Le serveur/la serveuse: Waiter/waitress
          </li>
          <li>
           Vgtarien: Vegetarian
          </li>
          <li>
           Vgtalien: Vegan
          </li>
         </ul>
