           <div class="content-text">
            <p>
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.redbull.com/us/en/music/events/1331676780779/30-days-in-la-red-bull-sound-select-event-series">
               Red Bull Sound Select Presents: 30 Days In LA
              </a>
             </strong>
             is only two weeks away, and we're going to start counting down to November 1 with a story, live set or interview each day, highlighting a facet of the month-long takeover of Los Angeles. Today, we're bringing you a full live set from
             <strong>
              <a href="http://redbullsoundselect.com/artists/tink" target="_blank">
               Tink
              </a>
             </strong>
             , who will open for A$AP Mob at the Hollywood Palladium on November 30 (
             <strong>
              <a href="http://redbullsoundselect.com/30days" target="_blank">
               get tickets here
              </a>
             </strong>
             ).
            </p>
           </div>
           <div class="content-embed--bordered">
            <figure class="media media--audioplayer" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/AudioObject">
             <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="166" src="https://w.soundcloud.com/player/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fapi.soundcloud.com%2Fplaylists%2F55421090" width="100%">
             </iframe>
            </figure>

           <div class="content-text">
            <p>
             Tink has been dropping tracks all over the place lately. Two days ago, she released the song 'ABC Fantasies.' A day before that, she put out a remix of Def Loaf's 'Try Me,' calling out North West. And before that, she released 'Sounds Good' (produced by DJ Wes).
            </p>
            <p>
             It's no secret that Tink is working on an album with Timbaland. She recently
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.thefader.com/2014/09/23/timbaland-preview-tink-album" target="_blank">
               told Fader
              </a>
             </strong>
             that it's about 80 percent done."When we work, it's like we're never done," she said. "We want it to get better. It's just trying to reach perfection. But we have a lot of records, we've been working every night back-to-back."
            </p>
            <p>
             Tink, who has been writing songs since she was 13 (before she began rapping), has been taking her time with her music. Regarding her very awesome mixtape, 'Winter's Diary 2,' released in January, she
             <strong>
              <a href="https://www.redbullsoundselect.com/posts/tink-4-days-in-austin" target="_blank">
               told Red Bull Sound Select
              </a>
             </strong>
             that it took an entire year to put together.
            </p>
            <p>
             "Sometimes, when I make a mixtape, I'll write it really quickly, in like a month or two," she said. "But with 'Winter's Diary 2,' it was from January to December. It was a whole year, and no song was rushed. There was a lot of thought put into each one. Every song has its own process and vibe. I would study each one and try to figure out what would make it better. And I wanted each song to have a different impact, so you might feel one way listening to one song, but the next one will make you completely different."
            </p>
