     <div class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="cT-imagePortrait">
       <img alt="'Jesus identified unambiguously with the outcast and downtrodden; He also elevated the idea of forgiveness.'" itemprop="image" src="/content/dam/images/3/6/w/m/7/image.related.articleLeadNarrow.300x0.36wpv.png/1397828901267.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        'Jesus identified unambiguously with the outcast and downtrodden; He also elevated the idea of forgiveness.'
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       Good Friday has meaning to me. Each Good Friday, I stop and contemplate the image of a plank strapped across the twisted trunk of an old tree with a man nailed to it.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       When &lt;i&gt;Piss Christ&lt;/i&gt;, a photograph by New York artist Andres Serrano of an iconic crucifix immersed in urine, appeared in Melbourne in 1997, I didnt object. I didnt object because I imagine a man in that sort of agony might well lose control of his bodily functions. (Im not saying that was the artists intention  I am saying thats what occurred to me looking at it.)
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Good Friday is for every good-hearted person who has ever been tortured or murdered or beaten down by the relentless forces of human oppression. What distinguishes these people one from the other is the magnitude of their goodness and the claim made on behalf of the 33-year-old Jewish carpenter is that He was the best of the lot. To paraphrase poet William Blake, to the greatest man or woman goes the greatest respect.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       What you cant do with Jesus is attribute an ulterior motive to Him. He was true to his word. You might argue He was a fanatic and, on my reading of the gospels, I dont find Him an easy or especially attractive character. He was too knowing, too humourless. He was a rebel who recognised no authority beyond that of an invisible entity He called His father.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       His creed was radical  far more radical, it seems to me, than is commonly acknowledged, particularly by those who combine Christianity with warmongering, extremes of personal wealth and religious pomp. Jesus identified unambiguously with the outcast and downtrodden; He also elevated the idea of forgiveness.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       My father returned from World WarII with no religion but a spirituality that revolved, in part, around some lines from Matthews gospel that he always gave as, I was hungry and you fed me, I was naked and you clothed me, I was in prison and you visited me. On the Burma Railway, he had been hungry, he had been in prison, he had been near naked. The actual last line of the quote from Matthews gospel is particularly poignant at this time in our national life: I was a stranger and you took me in.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       I know what I have written will annoy people who have a religious regard for Jesus. To them it will seem I am taking the religion out of Him. But thats how I understand Him  as a man.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       This is said to be a futile exercise by those who believe in the resurrection, not just the belief that Jesus rose from the dead on Easter Sunday but also the belief that those who live in Jesus will do likewise  that is, ascend into Heaven or exist in some munificent afterlife.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       I dont believe in an afterlife beyond that part of ourselves which is in our kids and the imprint, both good and bad, we leave upon the world.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       But whether or not you believe in an afterlife, we are still left with the problem of now, of the worlds many crises and the sense that our political leaders are no longer equal to the great challenges of our age.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       I know two things. One is that there is a force for goodness in the world that, no matter what defeats it suffers, is reborn in every generation.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Abraham Lincoln believed that, so did Gandhi; both were murdered. The second thing I know is that there are always brave men and women about.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Good Friday is about the universal heart, how it is endlessly injured, how it triumphs because it never stops.
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </p>
