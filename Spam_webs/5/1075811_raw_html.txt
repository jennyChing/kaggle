              <h2>
               <span>
                TIFF 2013 Volunteer Spot Behind The Scenes
               </span>
              </h2>
              <p>
               In July I was given the opportunity to shoot behind-the-scene photos for the
               <strong>
                TIFF 2013
               </strong>
               spot that Cineplex put together to thank the volunteers for their work during TIFF. They did two spots: one happy, happy, love one that ran before every movie at
               <strong>
                TIFF 2013
               </strong>
               and the other a dark murder, murder, kill one that ran during the Midnight Madness movies. The two spots took 3 days to shoot, one full day for Dancing in the Street and two full days for Its Alive. It was amazing to watch all the work that goes into what ultimately is 30 seconds of onscreen goodness. In between the first and second day of shooting, I had a wedding so I was pretty much on my feet with camera in hand for, four straight looong days. By the time Monday night came around I couldnt stand on my own two feet.
              </p>
              <p>
               I loved doing behind-the-scene photography and have always wanted to shoot on a set so a big Thank You to
               <strong>
                Rob Lazar
               </strong>
               for giving me the chance and
               <strong>
                Erick Sanders
               </strong>
               for pointing that chance out!
              </p>
              <p>
               As Always,
               <br/>
               Live . Love . Laugh
               <br/>
               P.S. Ill be posting the behind-the-scene photos for Its Alive later this month.
               <br/>
               P.P.S. If you do watch the trailer, look out for 0:24 to 0:26I make my big screen debut!
              </p>
