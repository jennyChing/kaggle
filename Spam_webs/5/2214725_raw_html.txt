          </li>
          <li>
           The development could be done in either way that is  Native or Cross Platform.
          </li>
          <li>
           Interestingly, building an app for say, an iPhone in Native, does not mean it will also work on an Android device as the code required is completely different. You should budget for almost twice the work.
          </li>
          <li>
           In cross platform, same code can be re used for both the platforms however, there is much issues with the performance of cross platform.
          </li>
          <li>
           To be honest, we are having developers who can work on cross platform technologies i.e. Titanium and PhoneGap as well as on Native iOS and Android. With our experience with the PhoneGap / Titanium, we have decided not to recommend the cross platforms for further projects, to our clients until and unless extremely necessary. There are lots of challenges with cross platform frameworks. We always prefer developing the application in the native platforms, which ensures the highest UI / UX experience. Cross platform is not a recommended solution when development of serious applications is involved. If you are looking for a very small APP for fun without long term planning then Cross platform is fine. A native mobile app can produce the best user experience  fast and fluid, can give you the best access to device features. Without a doubt, native apps have full access to the underlying mobile platform. Native apps are usually very fast and polished, making them great for high performance apps or games. This is more than enough; we think a reason to go native.
