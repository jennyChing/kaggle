            </div>
            <div class="thumbs-slide ">
             <a href="/image/100451717_volvo-concept-xc-coupe-2014-detroit-auto-show">
              <img src="http://images.thecarconnection.com/tmb/volvo-concept-xc-coupe-2014-detroit-auto-show_100451717_t.gif"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <div class="thumbs-slide ">
             <a href="/image/100182534_2010-volkswagen-golf-gtd-euro">
              <img src="http://images.thecarconnection.com/tmb/volkswagen_100182534_t.gif"/>
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="thumbs-nav-right">
           <div class="icon">

          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        The green car scene has never had more variety than it does today.
        <p>
         You could, if you wanted to, walk into a dealership today and buy regular gasoline and diesel cars, natural gas vehicles, hybrids, plug-in hybrids, electric vehicles and soon, hydrogen cars.
        </p>
        <p>
         And there are plenty more on the horizon. So this is our run-down of the five green vehicles due soon that we can't wait to have a go in.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1084725_2014-audi-a3-e-tron-full-details-on-audis-plug-in-hybrid" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Audi A3 e-tron
          </strong>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="ad floating-ad" id="adsense1" style="width:468px;height:80px;">
         <script type="text/javascript">
          //
		google_ad_client = "pub-4956733744046268"; 
		/* 468x80 */
		google_ad_slot = "7553041737";
		google_ad_width = 468;
		google_ad_height = 80;
    //
         </script>
         <script src="http://pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/show_ads.js" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
        </div>
        <p>
         We were introduced to the A3 e-tron at an event in Berlin last year. It's a plug-in hybrid version of the familiar A3 Sportback, but Audi has concentrated on performance as much as it has on efficiency, which is why the electric motor and 1.4-liter turbocharge gasoline setup delivers 204 horsepower.
        </p>
        <p>
         Combined EPA figures won't be available until closer to the model's launch in the fall, but its 8.8 kWh battery pack should be good for 20-or-so miles of all-electric range, and up to 80 mph all-electric. And with Audi's e-S Tronic dual-clutch transmission, it should be fun to drive as well as efficient.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1090579_2015-bmw-i8-sexy-plug-in-hybrid-sport-coupe-coming-later-this-year" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           BMW i8
          </strong>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Of all the vehicles here, we'll be waiting the shortest for the BMW i8. That's just as well, as we've been eagerly awaiting BMW's plug-in hybrid sports coupe for around two years now, since seeing the stunning concept version at the Frankfurt Auto Show in 2012.
        </p>
        <p>
         The i8 is a mid-engined sports car, feeding power from a 1.5-liter three-cylinder turbocharged engine and electric motor combo to the rear wheels, and electric power to the front wheels. Combined urge is 362 horsepower, and torque is 420 lb-ft. The frame is made from carbon-fiber reinforced plastic, to save weight. The upshot is a 0-60 time of under five seconds, 22 miles all-electric range, a low drag coefficient... and fantastic looks.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1079444_2013-mitsubishi-outlander-plug-in-hybrid-makes-world-premiere-in-paris" target="_blank">
           Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Repeated delays have seen the Outlander plug-in hybrid's release date
         <a href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1089481_mitsubishi-outlander-plug-in-hybrid-suv-now-delayed-til-2015-in-u-s" target="_blank">
          pushed further back
         </a>
         , but it's only a matter of time until Mitsubishi's first plug-in hybrid hits U.S. streets. When it does, it'll give customers a rare fuel-efficient option in the crossover market, with most brands currently offering a regular hybrid at the most.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          Slated for a 2015 debut, high demand in Japan has pushed its arrival back further than Mitsubishi intended. Its 12 kWh battery pack should return a 20-25 mile all-electric range, and a top speed of 75 mph on battery power. A 2-liter gasoline engine provides the rest of the Outlander's motive force.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          <div class="image_wrapper">
           <img alt="Volvo Concept XC Coupe, 2014 Detroit Auto Show" data-height="682" data-url="http://images.thecarconnection.com/lrg/volvo-concept-xc-coupe-2014-detroit-auto-show_100451710_l.jpg" data-width="1024" height="426" name="tccimg_100451710_m" src="http://images.thecarconnection.com/med/volvo-concept-xc-coupe-2014-detroit-auto-show_100451710_m.jpg" title="Volvo Concept XC Coupe, 2014 Detroit Auto Show" width="640"/>
           <p>
            Volvo Concept XC Coupe, 2014 Detroit Auto Show
           </p>
           <a class="enlarge" href="/image/100451710_volvo-concept-xc-coupe-2014-detroit-auto-show" name="tccwrp_100451710" target="_blank">
            Enlarge Photo
           </a>
          </div>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1089992_2015-volvo-xc90-plug-in-hybrid-could-arrive-summer-2015-execs-say" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Volvo XC90 PHEV
          </strong>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          It's all change at Volvo with the next-generation of its passenger cars. There's a new look, heralded by some stunning recent concept vehicles. There's a new platform, on which those concepts and future production vehicles are based. There's new, Apple-paired infotainment, and there's a range of fuel-efficient gasoline and diesel engines.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          And there's the XC90 plug-in hybrid, expected to debut 2015. The all-new XC90 will replace the current, and rather popular model launched over a decade ago. And a plug-in hybrid will join soon after launch, with a 2-liter gasoline direct-injected engine, eight-speed auto, and electric motor between the two. A second motor at the rear will provide all-wheel drive capabilities.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1085351_hot-vw-golf-gtd-diesel-confirmed-for-u-s-sales-debuts-2015" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           VW Golf GTD
          </strong>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Diesel vehicles are a popular part of Volkswagen's U.S. lineup but while they've always offered surprising performance, we've never really had a truly hot model. That will change in 2015 when VW debuts the Golf GTD in the States, effectively a diesel version of the iconic GTI model.
        </p>
        <p>
         Under the hood you'll find a 184-horsepower 2.0-liter TDI diesel engine, with 280 pounds-feet of torque and a 0-60 mph time of around seven seconds. It should also be as refined as any other modern diesel, and return over 40 mpg highway. If you want fun and frugality--and we do--the GTD sounds pretty much ideal.
        </p>
