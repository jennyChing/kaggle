          <div class="summary">
           <p>
            The defeat of Issue 2 leaves public employee clout  in tact at the bargaining table. Plain Dealer columnist Tom Suddes, speaking on WCPN Tuesday night, said that's likely to put a squeeze on local governments.
            <br/>
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="content">
           <div class="field field-name-field-episode-air-date field-type-datetime field-label-hidden bumper">
            <div class="field-items">
             <div class="field-item even">
              Tuesday, November 8, 2011 at 10:35 pm
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
            <div class="field-items">
             <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
              <p>
               Suddes said, "Local governments are going to be facing some interesting dilemmas about funding because ...the pot of state aid for localities isn't going to grow and may, in fact, continue to shrink.  So, then you have the question of mayors, county commissioners and school superintendents trying to muster the resources they need.  I think we'll have a better picture of this when we see how today's levy elections came out across the state."
              </p>
              <p>
               New levy votes did not go well for a lot of school systems.  Many voters supported teachers and other public workers' bargaining rights but don't want their taxes raised.
              </p>
