        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Playing Card Storage:  a Saturday Solution
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-14T10:12:03+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          March 14, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://lifeissweeterbydesign.com/author/jamie/" itemprop="url" rel="author">

           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">

           38 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Flifeissweeterbydesign.com%2Fplaying-card-storage-a-saturday-solution%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Flifeissweeterbydesign.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2015%2F03%2FPlayingCardStoragePin.jpg&amp;description=Simple%20storage%20and%20organization%20idea%20to%20keep%20your%20cards%20together%21%20Check%20out%20this%20easy%20Saturday%20Solution%21" rel="nobox">
          <img data-pib-button="true" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lifeissweeterbydesign.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/PlayingCardStoragePost.jpg">
          <img alt="Playing Card Storage" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1494" height="700" src="http://lifeissweeterbydesign.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/PlayingCardStoragePost.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
          Playing card storage may not be something you are worrying about. It may be just me.I do have a toddler that is into everything and has been scattering playing cards all over the house recently! I guess that is what made me realize I needed something besides the little lightweight box that the cards come in that she is able to rip up in seconds! So playing card storage is our next Saturday Solution!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
          As you can see by this picture.these card holders tear apart really easythis is one of ours that is in good condition! Most of our decks I have had to just throw the original package away!!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lifeissweeterbydesign.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/PlayingCardStoragePost2.jpg">
          <img alt="Playing Card Storage" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1499" height="700" src="http://lifeissweeterbydesign.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/PlayingCardStoragePost2.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
          So during my research I found a playing card storage solution that is easy and cheap! You buy a travel soap box and your cards will fit inside neatly. This is such an easy solution and it will keep your cards from getting all crumpled up by little hands!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
          I found the soap boxes at Wal-Mart in the travel toiletry section for 97 cents each! Woot, Woot! That is even cheaper than going to Dollar Tree!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
          Now I will tell you that these only hold regular sized cards, so if you have cards that are a unique size.probably wont fit. This also wont hold specialty card sets like Phase 10 or Skipbothose sets have a lot more cards in them than a regular deck of cards.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
          If you are like uswe had about 5 decks that seem to be somewhat intact and several other decks that I am sure are missing some cards! This playing card storage solution is a lot better than my previous solution.a rubber band!
          <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://lifeissweeterbydesign.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lifeissweeterbydesign.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/PlayCardStoragePost3.jpg">
          <img alt="Playing Card Storage" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1500" height="700" src="http://lifeissweeterbydesign.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/PlayCardStoragePost3.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
          I can just stick these in my game cabinet and we are ready for our next game night!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
          How do you store your playing cards? Do you have a unique playing card storage solution to share?
         </span>
        </p>
