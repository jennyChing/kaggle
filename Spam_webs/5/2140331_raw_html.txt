         <p class="publishedDate">

         <meta content="2015-02-09" itemprop="datePublished"/>

        <div id="mainBodyArea" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           Remember the days when taking a holiday meant booking a package to a beach resort? Or, if we were especially daring, flying off to explore a European capital over a long weekend?
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           Ever since the first days of jet travel in the Fifties, we have evolved into travellers. Today, we can have that sunshine holiday or city break, but we are also explorers, adventurers, yearning to absorb new cultures, wonder at ever more exotic sights and learn from the people we meet in different countries, different continents.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           <img alttext="Cathay Pacific flight destinations on a map" caption="Cathay Pacific flight destinations on a map" declared-caption="" height="287" name="Cathay Pacific flight destinations on a map" photographer="" refid="3192737" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/03192/Cathay_Pacific_fli_3192737c.jpg" version="c" width="460"/>
           <strong>
            <sup>
             Comprehensive: Cathay Pacific's routes
            </sup>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           Its the thrill, the memories and the rewards that can be gained from visiting a far-off land that has been at the heart of Cathay Pacific since the airline first started flying almost 70 years ago, having been founded by two entrepreneurs with one aircraft.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           In the UK, Cathay Pacific started flying people 35 years ago from the UK to its hub in Hong Kong  opening up a whole new world of opportunities for business and leisure travellers.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
           Today, Cathay Pacific has frequent non-stop flights from the UK to Hong Kong, the perfect hub for exploring Asia and many onward destinations. Such a frequency of flights  five times daily from Heathrow, four times weekly from Manchester  allow greater flexibility for your trip, which means you can make the most of your time away.
          </p>
          <p>
           In fact, from Hong Kong, with its sister airline Dragonair, Cathay Pacific has services to an incredible 190 destinations in Asia, Australasia and beyond.
          </p>
          <p>
           Cathay Pacific has preferred arrival, departure and transit gates at Hong Kong International Airport, so changing aircraft is made as effortless as possible.
          </p>
          <p>
           Few regions of the world present such a tapestry of sights, sounds and scents as Asia; a world that may be at once unfamiliar yet is open and welcoming to those who make the effort to embrace its many opportunities for discovery and enrichment.
          </p>
          <p>
           You can find out more about all the destinations in Asia served by Cathay Pacific and Dragonair by visiting
           <a href="http://www.cathaypacific.com/cx/en_GB/destinations.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            www.cathaypacific.co.uk
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Twenty-two of these destinations are in China, a vast and diverse country that is becoming a first choice for UK travellers seeking a more rewarding, authentic and inspiring holiday.
          </p>
          <p>
           As well as the unique sights and landmarks such as the Great Wall, the Forbidden City, the Terracotta Warriors and the giant pandas of Chengdu, visitors are discovering many other sides to China. Awesome landscapes, futuristic cities and a tangible sense of ancient culture and tradition are all being unveiled to the inquisitive traveller.
          </p>
          <p>
           One of those destinations is Kunming  see below for ideas on why this city and its surrounding region offer so many opportunities for inquisitive travellers.
          </p>
          <p>
           Cathay Pacific and Dragonair also serve a host of exciting destinations beyond China, such as Thailand, Malaysia, the Philippines, Vietnam, Japan, Indonesia, Myanmar (Burma), Korea, Singapore and Taiwan.
          </p>
          <p>
           In all of these countries theres a spectrum of cultural aspects to discover  intricate temples, cherished traditions and colourful markets, alongside breath-taking scenery or striking modern architecture. In many of these destinations, you can easily combine sightseeing and cultural encounters with a few days beside a beautiful beach.
          </p>
          <p>
           An especially alluring destination is Phuket in Thailand. This large island has everything from jungle-backed beaches to ornate temples to lively nightlife. See below for a few ideas on what to enjoy there.
          </p>
          <p>
           Hong Kong is itself one of the worlds most exciting destinations. This energised city, rising up from its iconic bay, is exotic and highly accessible, an intoxicating blend of old and new. With Cathay Pacifics flexible timetables, it is simple to combine a few days in Hong Kong before or after travelling to a different destination.
          </p>
          <p>
           As one of the leading carriers to Australasia, serving six key Australian cities and Auckland in New Zealand, Cathay Pacific offers the option of a stopover in Hong Kong, with excellent connections between the airport and the city.
          </p>
          <p>
           Wherever your destination, flying with Cathay Pacific  the airline that places the
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sponsored/travel/life-well-travelled/">
             emphasis on travelling well
            </a>
           </strong>
            will make your trip all the more unforgettable.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size:15px; font-style:bold;color:#404040;">
            KUNMING
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alttext="Bamboo Temple, Kunming" caption="The Bamboo Temple in north west Kunming was established during the Yuan Dynasty" declared-caption="" height="287" name="Bamboo Temple, Kunming" photographer="" refid="3193923" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/03193/Bamboo_Temple__Kun_3193923c.jpg" version="c" width="460"/>
           <strong>
            <sup>
             The Bamboo Temple in Kunming was established during the Yuan Dynasty
            </sup>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           As capital of the dazzlingly diverse province of Yunnan, laid-back Kunming offers plenty for visitors. Here are five of the best ways to spend time in and around The City of Eternal Spring.
          </p>
          <p>
            Also known as Qiongzhu Si, Kunmings extraordinary Bamboo Temple is notable for its hundreds of clay sculptures. The vividly coloured works were made in the mid-19th century, at the height of the Qing Dynasty.
          </p>
          <p>
            One of Kunmings great gifts is its food. Try the elaborate but delicious crossing-the-bridge noodles, Yunnans signature dish  a noodle soup with lightly cooked meat and quails eggs. Sample it at Jianxinyuan Restaurant.
          </p>
          <p>
            About 75 miles out of town youll find the stunning natural limestone towers that make up Shilin Stone Forest. The largest of them rise to heights of more than 100ft  theyve been called the first wonder of the world.
          </p>
          <p>
            Delve into the regions rich cultural heritage with a visit to Yunnan Nationalities Museum, which highlights the folklore and traditions of the various ethnic groups that live in the province.
          </p>
          <p>
            The city has some atmospheric markets, selling everything from flowers to crafts. Classic Kunming souvenirs include wax prints, wood carvings, embroidered handbags and silk brocade. Try Jingxing Street for some good options.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size:15px; font-style:bold;color:#404040;">
            PHUKET
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alttext="Sea cave in Phuket, Thailand" caption="" declared-caption="" height="287" name="Sea cave in Phuket, Thailand" photographer="" refid="3193920" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/03193/Sea_cave_in_Phuket_3193920c.jpg" version="c" width="460"/>
           <strong>
            <sup>
             Explore Phuket's sea caves by kayak
            </sup>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Thailands largest island is also one of its most absorbing. From the party atmosphere of Patong to the quiet sands of Hat Mai Khao, it throws up a whole range of travel possibilities. These are five of the best.
          </p>
          <p>
            Exploring the island by sea kayak is as fun as it sounds. You can head into sea caves, spot exotic birdlife and enjoy freshly grilled seafood when you pull up on shore. John Grays Seacanoe is the islands original operator and has some great itineraries.
          </p>
          <p>
            Head to the nearby island of Koh Yao Yai, which is easily reached by long-tail ferry. The pace of life is far removed from the bustle of Phuket and the exotically distinctive scenery gives ample reward. Its classic, unspoilt Thailand.
          </p>
          <p>
            Renowned as Phukets no-holds-barred nightlife centre, Patong lives up to its reputation  and then some. But aside from the bars and clubs, youll also find good diving and a long white-sand beach.
          </p>
          <p>
            Phuket is all about variety. Explore the rainforest on suspension bridges, rope walks and ziplines at Jungle Xtrem Adventures Park, or unwind at The Little Yoga Room in Chalong.
          </p>
          <p>
            Dont overlook Phuket Town. People often rush through en route to the beaches, but this is somewhere with a rich pan-cultural mix and a range of galleries, cafes and converted old shophouses.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alttext="Cathay Pacific green border" caption="Cathay Pacific green border" declared-caption="" height="4" name="Cathay Pacific green border" photographer="" refid="3193318" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/03193/Cathay_Pacific_gre_3193318a.jpg" width="460"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size:19px; font-style:bold;color:#404040;">
            A Life Well Travelled
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Travelling well is an important part of living well. If you travel well, your trip becomes more memorable and more rewarding. It's a philosophy that has been shared by Cathay Pacific for almost 70 years.
          </p>
          <p>
           Visit
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sponsored/travel/life-well-travelled/">
             www.telegraph.co.uk/lifewelltravelled
            </a>
           </strong>
           to find out why Cathay Pacific's service stands out above the rest.
          </p>
         </div>

        var readNextWidget = (function(){

            var settings = {
                articleImage: "",
                articleTitle: "",
                articleUrl: "#",
                articleId: "",
                currentArticleId: "",
                articleList: [],
                articleListToSkip: [],
                readNextCookieName: "tmg_read_next",
                openButton: "#readNextOpen a",
                closeButton: "#closeBtn a",
                articleContainer: "#readNextArticleContainer",
                widgetContainer: "#readNextWidget",
                displayThumbnail: false,
                panelOpen: false,
                autoOpen: true
            };

            var renderTemplate = function() {
                var template = '&lt;div id="readNextWidget" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextOpen"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div id="readNextOpenInner"&gt;&lt;a href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Next Story&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="icn"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +

                        '&lt;div id="nextArticle"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleInnerWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;p id="readNextLabel"&gt;MOST VIEWED IN NEWS&lt;/p&gt;';
                        
				if(settings.articleImage &amp;&amp; settings.articleImage!="null" &amp;&amp; settings.displayThumbnail){
					template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageThumbnail" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;img src="'+ settings.articleImage +'"/&gt;&lt;/a&gt;';
				}
                        
				template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageTitle" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;' + settings.articleTitle + '&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="closeBtn"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a href="#"&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;iframe style="border:none;position:absolute;top:0;left:0; height:100%;width:100%;z-index:-1;" id="iefix" src="about:blank"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;' + //fix for IE bug
                        '&lt;/div&gt;';
                return template;
            };


            var init = function(currentArticleId, articleList, displayThumbnail) {

                settings.articleList = articleList;
                settings.currentArticleId = currentArticleId;
                settings.displayThumbnail = displayThumbnail;

                settings.articleListToSkip = unescape($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName)).split(',');

                if ($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName) != null) {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    updateCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                } else {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    createCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                }


                if (settings.articleId != "") {

                    $(renderTemplate()).appendTo("body");

                    $("#nextArticleWrapper a, #readNextOpenInner a").click(function () {
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_headline', 'version_1']);

                        getNavPos($(this).attr("href"), "wayfarerReadNext");
                    });

                    bindUIActions();

                    var displayed = false;

                    setTimeout(function () {
                        if (!displayed) {
                            displayed == true;
                            readNextWidget.display();
                        }

                    }, 10000);

                    $(window).scroll(function () {
                        if (isScrolledIntoView("#tmg-related-links")) {
                            if (!displayed) {
                                displayed == true;
                                readNextWidget.display();
                            }
                        }

                        if(isScrolledIntoView("#shareBottom") || isScrolledIntoView("#newGigyaShareBottom")) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }

                        if($(window).scrollTop() + $(window).height() == $(document).height()) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }
                    });
                }
            };

            var display = function () {
                $(settings.widgetContainer).fadeIn(2000, function () {
                    //animation complete
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_closed', 'version_1']);
                });
            };

            var bindUIActions = function () {
                $(settings.openButton).mouseenter(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'hover_closed', 'version_1']);
                    openArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.widgetContainer).mouseleave(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    closeArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.closeButton).click(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_close', 'version_1']);
                    updateCookie(settings.articleId, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    closeArticleContainer();
                    settings.autoOpen = false;
                });
            };

            var openArticleContainer = function () {
                if (!settings.panelOpen) {
                	$("#readNextArticleContainer").show();//show/hide because height is calculated incorrectly for elements with display:none
                    $("#readNextArticleContainer").hide();
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_open', 'version_1']);
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                    settings.autoOpen = false; //set auto open to false once user has viewed this once
                }
                return false;
            };

            var closeArticleContainer = function () {
                if (settings.panelOpen) {
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                }
                return false;
            };


            var isScrolledIntoView = function(elem) {
                if($(elem).length) { //check if element exists
                    var docViewTop = $(window).scrollTop();
                    var docViewBottom = docViewTop + $(window).height();

                    var elemTop = $(elem).offset().top;
                    var elemBottom = elemTop + $(elem).height();

                    return ((elemBottom &lt;= docViewBottom) &amp;&amp; (elemTop &gt;= docViewTop));
                } else {
                    return false;
                }
            };

            var unique = function(array) {
                return array.filter(function (el, index, arr) {
                    return index == arr.indexOf(el);
                });
            }

            var createCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        articleListToSkip[0] = currentArticleId;
                        $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        updateCookie(currentArticleId, articleListToSkip);
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookie = function(articleIdToSkip, articleListToSkip) {
                articleListToSkip.push(articleIdToSkip);
                articleListToSkip = unique(articleListToSkip);
                $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
            }

            var chooseNextArticle = function (currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if ((article.id != currentArticleId) &amp;&amp; ($.inArray(article.id, articleListToSkip) === -1)) {

                        //set article
                        settings.articleTitle = article.title;
                        settings.articleUrl = article.url;
                        settings.articleId = article.id;
						settings.articleImage = article.imgUrl;
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            //public API
            return {
                settings: settings,
                init: init,
                display: display,
                openArticleContainer: openArticleContainer,
                closeArticleContainer: closeArticleContainer
            }
        })();

    };
