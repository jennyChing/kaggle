        <h1>
         <a href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/2013/07/23/dont-let-staff-holidays-give-your-business-the-blues/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Dont let staff holidays give your business the blues">
          Dont let staff holidays give your business the blues
         </a>
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="post-meta">
        <span>
         <i class="mini-ico-calendar">
         </i>
         <a class="published-time" href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/2013/07/23/dont-let-staff-holidays-give-your-business-the-blues/" rel="bookmark" title="9:31 am">
          July 23, 2013
         </a>
        </span>
        <span>
         <i class="mini-ico-user">
         </i>

         <a class="author-link" href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/author/clivelewis/">

        </span>
        <span>

        <span class="tags">

       <div class="post-description">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/www.businessadviceservice.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/rio_blogmain.png">
          <img alt="Rio de Janeiro" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-4003" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.businessadviceservice.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/rio_blogmain.png?resize=700%2C320"/>
         </a>
         At this time of year when employees will be taking their staff holidays, it is essential that firms plan ahead to ensure business is not disrupted. With an upturn in market conditions for many sectors, its important to maintain that momentum.
        </p>
        <p>
         As Head of Enterprise for ICAEW, heres my 7 top tips to help you manage while your staff are on holiday:
        </p>
        <p>
         1. Getting cash into your business is critical. During the summer you will have staff away  but so will your customers. Plan ahead to avoid payment delays
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Make sure invoices are still paid on time otherwise you could damage your credit rating and limit your access to supplies on credit
        </p>
        <p>
         3. If the person authorising payment is away make sure that someone else can authorise it in their absence. The same applies with cheque signatories
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Make sure that staff complete a proper handover and in enough time. Staff involved in raising sales invoices and chasing customers for payment must properly brief the staff standing in for them; especially on commitments made by customers regarding payment. They must also be aware of how to follow up customer queries regarding sales invoices so that disputes are resolved quickly
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Make sure that suppliers or customers are aware of any alternatives to their regular contacts to ensure a smooth handover and make sure that stand-in staff can access a list of contact details for suppliers and customers
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Systems for recording / forecasting cash flow need to be maintained in the finance directors absence
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Debrief people when they get back  this will enable them to get back up to speed more quickly after their holiday
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/?s=cashflow" target="_blank" title="Business Advice Service Blog - Cashflow">
          Cash is king
         </a>
         and businesses must ensure that cash continues to come in by examining their debt collection and bill paying procedures as well as making sure proper safeguards are in place while staff are away.
        </p>

						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
