            <div class="field-item even">
             <img height="218" src="http://www.ncsasports.org/sites/default/files/checklist_0.jpg" typeof="foaf:Image" width="549"/>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
           <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
             <p>
              One of the most effective roles you can play in the recruiting process is that of helping your athlete create a roadmap they can begin executing early in their high school career. Heres a checklist of important tasks that youll need to undertake at various points in the recruiting process, beginning freshman year of high school.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Academic:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Thoroughly review the NCAA Guide for the College-Bound Student-Athlete
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Use Division I core course worksheet inside the NCAA guide to set specific academic goals and plan a core course schedule
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Begin SAT/ACT test preparation. Sign up for a
               <a href="http://www.princetonreview.com/offer/free-practice-tests/?exid=94276252-1e0b-4c8a-a2a2-c24824f119f2">
                free practice SAT or ACT
               </a>
               to determine your prep needs
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Keep your grades up. Try a
               <a href="http://www.princetonreview.com/academic-tutoring/?exid=94276252-1e0b-4c8a-a2a2-c24824f119f2">
                free homework help session
               </a>
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Determine your Estimated Family Contribution (EFC) to familiarize yourself with the collegiate financial aid process
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Apply to all colleges you are interested in attending
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Recruiting:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Start a correspondence log to keep track of your communications with college coaches
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Begin building a scouting report to present to college coaches
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Register with the NCAA Eligibility Center and make sure your athletes high school counselor sends his or her transcript at the end of junior year
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Plan official and unofficial visits to local college campuses, always contacting the coaches beforehand to arrange a meeting
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Create a highlight or skills video using sport-specific video guidelines
              </li>
              <li style="margin-left: 40px;">
               Compile a list of target schools based on your qualifications
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p style="margin-left: 40px;">
             </p>
            </div>
