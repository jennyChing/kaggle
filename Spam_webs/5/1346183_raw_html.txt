      <article class="clearfix post-25809 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-art-history-2 category-blog" id="post-25809" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting" role="article">
       <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" class="alignnone" height="350" src="http://www.haring.com/!/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/popshop_haring.jpg" title="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Happy Birthday to
         <a href="http://blog.artsnapper.com/keith-haring-a-brief-but-meteoric-career/" target="_blank">
          Keith Haring
         </a>
         a highly influential American artist and social activist! Youd probably recognize his work if you saw it; his pop, graffiti art has become widely recognized in the 20th and 21st century.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="CELEBRATING KEITH HARING, A MASTER OF THE STREET ART" class="alignnone wp-image-26559" height="412" src="http://artsnapper.artsnapper.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/haring_55109_1-750x401.jpg" width="771"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" class="alignnone wp-image-25823" height="534" src="http://artsnapper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/keith_haring.png" style="line-height: 1.5em;" title="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" width="768"/>
         <a href="http://artsnapper.com/uniqlo-x-keith-haring-cool-or-cringe/">
          Keith Haring
         </a>
         was born in Reading, Pennsylvania on May 4, 1958. At a very young age, Haring showed an affinity for art. He studied commercial art from 1976 to 1978 at Pittsburghs Ivy School of Professional Art, but lost interest in commercial art very quickly. He said:  I went to a commercial-art school, where I quickly realized that I didnt want to be an illustrator or a graphic designer. The people I met who were doing it seemed really unhappy; they said that they were only doing it for a job while they did their own art on the side, but in reality that was never the casetheir own art was lost. I quit the school.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Very soon afterwards, Haring moved to New York City. He was greatly inspired by the graffiti art scene, and enrolled in Manhattans School of Visual Arts, where he majored in painting.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" class="alignnone" height="615" src="http://www.popandroll.com/coke-art/Keith_Haring4.jpg" title="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" width="765"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Haring achieved public attention first on the subways: these grew to later be known as his first pieces of recognizable public art. He became famous for The Radiant Baby:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" class="alignnone" height="800" src="http://www.modlivin.com/image/cache/data/Blik/BLIK-Keith-Haring-Radiant-Baby-Wall-Decals-Large-800x800.jpg" style="line-height: 1.5em;" title="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The origin of this work is believed to be Christian. He was raised in a Protestant household, but joined the 1970s Jesus Movement in his youth. Though that was a phase that ended quickly for Haring, it still left a mark on his work, and he continued to use bold lines and figures to represent his social viewpoints.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" class="alignnone" height="816" src="http://www.pcpmedia.us/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/keith-haring-heart.jpg" style="line-height: 1.5em;" title="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" width="819"/>
         Haring was public about the fact that he had AIDS; he often reflected upon his diagnosis in work. In a 1989 interview with Rolling Stone magazine, Haring stated, Thats the point that I am at now, not knowing where it stops but knowing how important it is to do it now. The whole thing is getting more articulate. In a way its really liberating. Critics comment that this is apparent in his works, but none more so than his latest works.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="CELEBRATING KEITH HARING, A MASTER OF THE STREET ART" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-26560" height="521" src="http://artsnapper.artsnapper.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/keith_haring2-1024x712.png" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" class="alignnone" height="329" src="http://www.contemporaryartdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/K_Haring_19831.jpg" style="line-height: 1.5em;" title="Celebrating Keith Haring, a Master of the Street Art" width="830"/>
         What do YOU think of social activism in art? I find it inspirational, and think that we should utilize it more than we do. Share some of your favorite activist art on our
         <a href="http://artsnapper.com/artsnapper-application-available-now/">
          new app
         </a>
         !
        </p>
