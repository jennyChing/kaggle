        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="The Goatman of Kentucky" class="aligncenter wp-image-1315" height="296" src="http://realunexplainedmysteries.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pan_goat_MAN_Napoli_Inv27709_n02-e1412873261511-1024x606.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I came across a cryptid report onThe Goatman of Kentucky yesterday and I felt it was well worth exploring a little deeper in a post here. There were claims thatit could be a relative of the
         <strong>
          New Orleans
         </strong>
         evil Cupacabra-like cryptid the
         <strong>
          Grunch
         </strong>
         . There also seems to be a mass of urban legends following this demonic creatures existence
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          <b>
           Theres Nothing Like an Urban Legend
          </b>
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Urban legends seem to drive a lot of cryptozoology articles I come across and this is
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          not always a good thing
         </span>
         . In the case ofThe Goatman of Kentucky I find the urban legends sort of add to the mystery and the atmosphere of the
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
          <a href="http://realunexplainedmysteries.com/types-of-demons-in-demonology-the-ultimate-encyclopedia" style="color: #ff0000;" title="Types of Demons in Demonology  The Ultimate Encyclopedia">
           <b>
            demon creature
           </b>
          </a>
         </span>
          they give it that extra spice!
        </p>
        <p>
         The first of these urban legends indicated that The Goatman has been known to
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          hunt down young couples
         </span>
         having alone time in parked cars. Another legend has the creature blamed for instances of missing pets in various areas.
        </p>
        <p>
         These urban legends are all pretty Hollywood in taste so what about the real truth behind the Goatman cryptid
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          <b>
           The Goatman of Kentucky
          </b>
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         The Goatman is said to resemble a
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          faun or satyr
         </span>
         from ancient mythology. I tend to picture the faun character off
         <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe" target="_blank">
          <b>
           <i>
            The Lion, The Witch And The Wardrobe
           </i>
          </b>
         </a>
         as a close resemblance.
        </p>
        <p>
         The size of the creature seems to more often than not divide opinion. Some say it is a Hobbit-like 4 ft. whilst others claim it is a towering 10 ft. tall.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Goatman of Kentucky is also known as the
         <b>
          Pope Lick Goatman
         </b>
         and he is normally sighted arounda
         <strong>
          railroad trestle in eastern Jefferson County
         </strong>
         . Nobody seems to know why he is set in this area but his hauntings have occurred there since the late 1940s.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="250" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/jAThMuRc7WY?rel=0" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </center>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          <b>
           Similarities
          </b>
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         A lot of witness reports claim that the creature has a lot of fur covering its torso and a goats head. Although it seems to be most famous for its Kentucky sightings its also been spotted in various other locations in America (primarily the southeast 
         <strong>
          Virginia and North Carolina
         </strong>
         ).
        </p>
        <p>
         The Jersey Devil seems to share the same characteristics as this strange creature but is a lot more famous in cryptozoology circles.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          <b>
           The Killer Goatman
          </b>
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         In the summer of 1962 the Goatman legend took a bit of a turn towards the dark side when the creature was accused of murdering a party of 14 hikers in
         <strong>
          Maryland
         </strong>
         . Apparently this group got a little to close to its lair causing it to emerge and kill.
        </p>
        <p>
         Twelve of the fourteen victims were said to have been children  the other two were the adult members in charge of the group. There were a handful of survivors ( apparently ) who claimed the beast just
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          tore through the group with relative ease
         </span>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         During this massacre they said the beast was
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          wailing like the devil
         </span>
         in macabre enjoyment. Its worth noting that authorities in the area have no record of this attack ever taking place  another urban legend perhaps?
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          <b>
           The Bible Belt
          </b>
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         A lot of skeptics now believe that this goat-like creature was born through rumors coming from the
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          Bible Belt
         </span>
         in the area. The reports of devil like screams seem to indicate that it may be no more than simple religious fear.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1320" style="width: 450px;">
         <img alt="The Demon Entity Known as Baphome" class="wp-image-1320" height="302" src="http://realunexplainedmysteries.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/7619853448_677d1f1f66_z-e1412873891988.jpg" width="450"/>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          The Demon Entity Known as Baphome
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The Goat creatures Pan-like visage bears a striking resemblance to the
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
          <a href="http://realunexplainedmysteries.com/types-of-demons-in-demonology-the-ultimate-encyclopedia" style="color: #ff0000;" title="Types of Demons in Demonology  The Ultimate Encyclopedia">
           <b>
            demon entity known as Baphomet
           </b>
          </a>
         </span>
         . This entity is one of the well known
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
          <a href="http://realunexplainedmysteries.com/types-of-demons-in-demonology-the-ultimate-encyclopedia" style="color: #ff0000;" title="Types of Demons in Demonology  The Ultimate Encyclopedia">
           <b>
            medieval demons
           </b>
          </a>
         </span>
         that the Bible Belt takes VERY seriously indeed.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          <b>
           The Origins of The Goatman
          </b>
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         There are many conflicting reports on the origins of this strange creature. Many members of the Bible Belt feel it was released when a satanic ritual went wrong in the area.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are also claims that the Goatman is nothing more than an escaped mental patient who evolved out in the wilds of Maryland. This patient is said to be the property of a
         <b>
          Doctor Stephen Fletcher
         </b>
         experiment that failed miserably. ApparentlyDoctor Stephen Fletcher worked nearby at the
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          U.S. Department of Agriculture laboratory in Beltsville
         </span>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Apparently this patient was at one time Fletchers medical assistant but he was driven insane by a DNA experiment. The experiment involved him being injected with goat DNA that ( apparently ) turned him into this hideous mental patient.
        </p>
