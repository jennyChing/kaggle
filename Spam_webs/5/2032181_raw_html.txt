        </a>
        ,
        <span class="pub-date">
         April 28, 2010
        </span>
       </h4>
      </div>
      <div class="widgets">
       <div class="social-deferred" id="social-httppitchforkcomfeaturesguest">

      <img alt="Gonjasufi" class="header" src="http://cdn2.pitchfork.com/features/7798/7906785a.jpg"/>
      <div class="player-target editorial">
       <p>
        Welcome to the latest edition of Pitchfork's Guest List. Each week, we ask one of our favorite artists to fill us in on what they've been up to lately: which tracks they can't stop spinning, what books they can't put down, and what new bands they've caught on tour. This week it's Gonjasufi, who talks about the inspiration he received from his cousin Ish, and his dream of being locked up in a room full of arcade games.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite New Songs from Past Year
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        I was bumping Raekwon's new album,
        <i>
         Only Built 4 Cuban Linx... Pt. II
        </i>
        , especially "Surgical Gloves" and "Cold Outside". The Portishead album that came out recently [
        <i>
         Third
        </i>
        ]-- "The Rip", all that shit.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite Older Music at the Moment
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        I have Thom Yorke's
        <i>
         Eraser
        </i>
        in my car's CD player right now. I like Beth Gibbons'
        <i>
         Out of Season
        </i>
        . [Radiohead's]
        <i>
         Amnesiac
        </i>
        has been in there for while.
        <i>
         Wu-Tang Forever
        </i>
        , the second disc. You know, old school. I've always got a little Coltrane on deck, and a little Jack White.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite New Artist
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        Other than the heads that I'm working with out of San Diego... My cousin Ish [from Digable Planets], he's got an album called
        <i>
         Shabazz Palaces
        </i>
        .I've been listening to that a lot lately.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'm just kind of listening to my own crew's stuff, my own rap, all these joints... I listen to that stuff on
        <b>
         <big>
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
        repeat forever.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          My Dream Collaboration
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img align="left" alt="" hspace="5" src="http://cdn.pitchfork.com/media/jackwhite110.jpg"/>
        I like Jack White, man. I don't know how it would work out, but if he ever gets tired of being the frontman and just wants to play guitar and let me back him or something... I like his whole shit, man. I'm like, "Damn bro, this dude is just a major bro." He's probably it for me.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite Song of All Time
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        The Gap Band: "You Dropped a Bomb on Me". That's my shit.Every time that song comes on, it takes me back--I grew up bumping that shit with my mom in the car.There's just something about that song that hits me every time.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Last Great Concert I Saw
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        Portishead at Coachella. That was pretty groovy. [Beth Gibbons] is actually better live than on the record. Especially with their style, it's kind of hard to top it, but she smashed it, man. She's hits them notes up there-- I was just floored. That's was the best concert I've been to in a minute.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Last Great Film I Saw
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        <i>
         District 9
        </i>
        [by Neill Blomkamp].I liked it 'cause it was new, as far as the plot: It was something that I had never seen before. It wasn't a recycled story. I just like the oddity
        <b>
         <big>
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
        of it, the awkwardness of the flick.
       </p>
       <p>
        I've also been watching
        <i>
         The Fly
        </i>
        [by David Cronenberg].So massive. The video store went out of business, so I went and bought as many videosas I could. I've been watching
        <i>
         The Fly
        </i>
        on repeat and just tripping on that. I make my son watch that shit. He gets scared as shit. I'm like, "Sit down and watch this shit with me, bro." It scared me as a child, so I might as well expose him to the same shit.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Last Great Book I Read
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        <i>
         <img align="left" alt="" hspace="5" src="http://cdn.pitchfork.com/media/12thplanet110.jpg"/>
         The 12th Planet
        </i>
        by Zecharia Sitchin. It's heavy, man. Zecharia Sitchin is a heavy dude. I've had his book for about a decade, and a couple of days ago I started reading it. There's a lot of heavy concepts he deals with-- the beginning of life and where we came from and human evolution.
        <b>
         <big>
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
        Basically he's talking about how the Annunaki [
        <i>
         Sumerian deities believed to be ancient astronauts --Ed.
        </i>
        ] were testing out combinations to make man, trying out different DNAs and shit, coming up with different kinds of animals: a human head with a horse body... [He also writes about] pyramids and all that shit. It's kind of trippy. Everybody has to read that book.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'm into self-improvement and books that enlighten me, and I also like to get into the stuff that people consider dark, taboo. Aleister Crowley, shit like that. It opens up doors and shit. Staying open to the infinite possibilities. Just being receptive to everything, to all information and tuning out all the lies until you get the truth.
       </p>
       <p>
        My cousin, Ish, who I told you about, he told me back in the day, when I was like 18 or 19... I said, "Hey, you got any advice
        <b>
         <big>
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
        for me?" He had already toured and done [the Digable Planets album]
        <i>
         Blowout Comb
        </i>
        and all that shit. He said, "Just read, man, as much as you can. Go to school and read. Think wide.Read, read, read." And he bought me [
        <i>
         The 12th Planet
        </i>
        ] for Christmas.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite Piece of Musical Equipment
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        My guitar. I've got a beat up piece of shit. I bought it for my daughter. It's a small guitar, but I rock that shit, man. That's the most honest piece of equipment I have. It's just me and the guitar and nothing in between it. It's a reflection. Everything else you can cheat a little bit.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite Record Shop
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        I can't tell you that.I got a spot, bro, but I'm not telling you.Then everybody else is gonna be there. That's classified information.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          First Record I Bought For Myself
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        It was a Jungle Brothers record, and I forget what song it was. I still got it, though.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite City to Play In
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        I've only done a show in L.A. and in Tokyo. Shit, I don't know, man. Ask me to be there,and I'll go.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          My Dream Merch Table
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img align="left" alt="" hspace="5" src="http://cdn.pitchfork.com/media/walkman110.jpg"/>
        Tapes, man. A Walkman that's got the tape in it with super glue so you can't open it up. It switches sides on its own. And each tape is one of a kind and comes with a fuckin' pair of headphones. "Fuckin' 500 dollars, G." And if you open it up, it disappears or some crazy shit.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Strangest Display of Affection from a Fan
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        Not much, really. Not as much as I'd like. Nothing too crazy, man. You can pretty much do anything and I'd just think it's normal. It'd take four girls to come and lock me in the backstage room and go at it in front of me or some crazy shit.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Best Thing You've Bought in Past Year
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        I finally bought another pair of 1200s. My 1200s were stolen from me about '98, and I haven't had turntables since. So my boy sold me his 1210s, and that's been the best thing I've bought in a minute.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Best Thing You've Done At All This Year
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        Dropped an album.It's the best shit I've done so far.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite Venue
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        I like Coachella a lot. I like the fact that you can camp 20-30 minutes away out by the Salton Sea. They got a lake out there. It's hot as fuck. It's outside. Got just mad different bands and so many heads. Every time I go there I run into heads I haven't seen in years. I've seen some of the best shows at Coachella. I didn't go last year, but I went every year before that for five years straight. I saw Tool, Massive Attack, Radiohead, Prince, Portishead, Kraftwerk...
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite TV Show
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        "The Lake Show"-- the fuckin' Lakers, bro. Other than that, I watch the Discovery Channel or the History Channel. Anything else is all garbage. I pay like 200 bucks for three channels.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite Video Games
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img align="left" alt="" hspace="5" src="http://cdn.pitchfork.com/media/720_110.jpg"/>
        I'm not really a video game dude. The last video game system that I bought was fuckin' original Nintendo, man. I'm hoping to get a room full of old, old arcades like
        <i>
         Tron
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         Star Wars
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         Paperboy
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         720
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         Gauntlet
        </i>
        . I need to have that shit in my life before I die. I could just lock myself in there and just fuckin' do a bunch of psychedelics and not come out for three years. That's a dream of mine to get all those old-ass arcade games and just chill out, just have a marathon.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          Favorite Radio Show
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        "Coast to Coast". George Noory. Art Bell. I've been listening to that shit for 20 years. Zecharia Sitchin was all over "Coast to Coast".
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         <big>
          My Ringtone
         </big>
        </b>
        <big>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        <i>
         Mars Attacks
        </i>
        . You know the laser guns they shoot? That's the ringtone.
       </p>
      </div>
