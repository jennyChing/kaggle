     <div class="actualcontent">
      <p>
       There are so many instances when you could almost swear you heard your
       <strong>
        food
       </strong>
       speak. If you havent had that phenomenal experience yet, here is a bunch of
       <strong>
        fun food illustrations
       </strong>
       by
       <strong>
        David Olenick
       </strong>
       that tells you what your food has been dying to say to you all this time.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Read Also:
        <a href="http://www.hashslush.com/colored-food-series-blue-turkey-side-green-noodles/" target="_blank">
         Colored Food Series  Blue turkey with a side of green noodles?
        </a>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Reflecting human frailties in a minimalistic and simple way,
       <strong>
        Creative Illustrative
       </strong>
       <strong>
        <a href="http://davidolenick.com/">
         David Olenick
        </a>
       </strong>
       knows perfectly well how you feel about your food.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <b>
        The Inner Voice
       </b>
      </h4>
      <p>
       In his drawings,
       <strong>
        David
       </strong>
       expresses your inner voice as the words of everything from
       <strong>
        ultra-healthy
       </strong>
       chows to
       <strong>
        high calorie foods
       </strong>
       and
       <strong>
        alcoholic beverages
       </strong>
       . These sassy
       <strong>
        food illustrations
       </strong>
       capture all that guilt you feel over eating more
       <strong>
        ice-cream
       </strong>
       than you should, or your perpetual obsession with morning coffee.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <b>
        Food Embarrassments
       </b>
      </h4>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Hilarious
       </strong>
       yet accurate, the art taps into food-related anxieties perfectly, striking a flawless balance between embracing and mocking your embarrassments about what you eat that you hold deep inside your heart.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <b>
        Brutally Honest
       </b>
      </h4>
      <p>
       In a parallel universe, if food could give you honest opinions, this is definitely what it would say. Including
       <strong>
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Not_safe_for_work">
         NSFW
        </a>
       </strong>
       language, the series constitute rather adorable cartoon interpretations of your food, seducing you to eat them using many tactics.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <b>
        Calm Down
       </b>
      </h4>
      <p>
       Perhaps the most apt is the cup of tea telling you to calm down, and it sort of hits home when you see it. The featured food items include chocolate, a double patty cheeseburger, beer, wine, peanut butter, lollipop, broccoli, coffee and pint of ice cream.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <b>
        Text Him
       </b>
      </h4>
      <p>
       A glass of wine recommending you to 
       <em>
        <strong>
         Text Him
        </strong>
       </em>
        is also amusingly accurate, as is a mug of beer trying to get real friendly. The cute combination of images with text makes the whole thing embarrassingly relatable in a subtle yet entertaining way.
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Prints And Mechandise
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Davids humorous illustrations
       </strong>
       are available in print on
       <strong>
        t-shirts
       </strong>
       or other merchandise and can be purchased through his shops on
       <strong>
        <a href="http://society6.com/DavidOlenick" target="_blank">
         Society6
        </a>
       </strong>
       and
       <strong>
        <a href="http://beta.threadless.com/profile/1884551/dro72/designs" target="_blank">
         Threadless
        </a>
       </strong>
       .
      </p>
