        <a href="http://www.drive.com.au/mercedes-benz" target="_blank">
         Mercedes-Benz

       </p>
       <p>
        Unveiled on the eve of the 2015

        , the futuristically-styled four-door, which goes under the name F015 Luxury In Motion, draws on a raft of new technologies being readied by the German car maker for inclusion on future production models, including a sophisticated autonomous operating system and so-called digital activity space that Mercedes-Benz predicts will allow occupants the freedom to use their time on the road more intelligently than is the case today, especially in city driving environments.
       </p>

"The car is growing beyond its role as a mere means of transport and will ultimately become a mobile living space."

       <p>
        Described as the most highly developed concept ever constructed by Mercedes-Benz, the F015 Luxury in Motion is equipped with an extensive range of state-of-the-art stereo cameras as well as a series of sensors that allow it to permanently monitor the area surrounding it and operate fully autonomously when commanded by the driver, who is then able to concentrate on other tasks.
       </p>
       <p>
        Newly developed software also provides the swoopy new concept with artificial intelligence, which Mercedes-Benz says will be crucial to building trust between humans and cars as autonomous driving is introduced in coming years.
       </p>
       <p>
        Without any traditional eye contact between the driver and pedestrians in autonomous mode, Mercedes-Benz has programmed a series of LEDs within the grille of the F015 Luxury in Motion to shine when it detects a pedestrian. Should the pedestrian wish to cross the road, the concept brakes to a halt and scans its surroundings to check whether it is safe to do so. A laser system is then used to project a virtual zebra crossing on to the road surface while prompting the pedestrian with an audible "please go ahead" message from external speakers.
       </p>
       <p>
        The self-driving ability of the F015 Luxury In Motion has played a key role in its design, which eschews the traditional three-box silhouette of today's S-Class for a distinctive mono-box profile aimed at providing maximum interior space and a lounge like interior in which the steering wheel automatically retracts into the dashboard when the driver chooses to run in autonomous mode
       </p>
       <p>
        At 5220mm in length, 2018mm in width and 1524mm in height, it is 26mm shorter, 119mm wider and 33mm taller than the existing long wheelbase S-Class. In a move that serves to provide it with significantly more interior space, the new concept also receives a wheelbase that is a considerable 445mm longer than the production Mercedes-Benz at a lengthy 3610mmmm  a move that provides it with ultra short overhangs both front and rear.
       </p>
       <p>
        Hinting at the construction techniques planned for future models, Mercedes-Benz has provided its latest concept with a lightweight body. Using a combination of carbon fibre reinforced plastic, aluminium and high strength steel, it is claimed to weigh up to 40 per cent less than the more conventional aluminium and high strength steel structures in use today.
       </p>

       <p>
        New lighting technology also plays a prominent role on the F015 Luxury in Motion, allowing it to communicate its driving mode; the LED units light up in a blue shade when the big saloon is running autonomously and in white when it is controlled manually.
       </p>
       <p>
        The F015 Luxury in Motion uses conventional front-hinged doors up front and rear-hinged door at the rear  each of which can be opened and closed independently of one another. By employing a sturdy interlocking system with mechanical elements for added rigidity, Mercedes-Benz has been able to do away with B-pillars.In the case of a side impact, elements within the doors inflate to alter the load path and absorb energy. All doors open to an angle of 90 degree to provide easy access to the interior.
       </p>
       <p>
        Inside, Mercedes-Benz's designers have provide their vision for a future luxury car for the year 2030 and beyond with a highly contemporary looking cabin boasting four rotating seats that allow a face-to-face configuration when travelling in autonomous mode and a range of natural trim materials, including walnut wood and white Nappa leather together with glass and exposed metal.
       </p>
       <p>
        Far removed from that of existing Mercedes-Benz models, the hi-tech interior is dominated by six individual monitors integrated into the dashboard, rear and side panels  each boasting gesture, touch and eye tracking functions for easy interaction in what Mercedes-Benz bills as a "digital activity space" for up to four occupants.
       </p>
       <p>
        The F015 Luxury In Motion has been conceived around a plug-in hydrogen fuel cell system that is claimed to provide a range of up to 1100km, including around 200kmof lithium ion battery propelled driving and some 900km on electricity generated by the fuel cell. Previously shown in the earlier F125 in 2009, it uses two electric motors, each delivering 100kW and 200Nm of torque, mounted at the rear and providing drive to the individual rear wheels.
       </p>
       <p>
        With a total system output of 200kW and 400Nm, the plug-in hydrogen fuel cell system provides the big monobox saloon with a theoretical 0-100km/h time of 6.7sec and a top speed limited to 200km/h. As well as storing energy produced by the fuel cell, the new concept's front mounted 29kWh lithium ion battery can be charged via inductive means without the need for cable.
       </p>
       <p>
        The hydrogen used to generate electricity on the run is stored at a pressure of 700bar in a series of tanks that are described as being "integrated into the floor to keep them protected from impacts" and posses a combined 5.4kg. Consumption is put at 0.6kg of hydrogen per 100km, or the equivalent of 2.0L/100km with a conventional diesel engine, according to Mercedes-Benz. Refuelling is claimed to take just minutes and is compatible with the hydrogen filling station standard in place today.
       </p>
