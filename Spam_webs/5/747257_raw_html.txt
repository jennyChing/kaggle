     <h1 class="postTitle">
      Game of Thrones dragon mobile
     </h1>
     <p>
      Last January when I was at the Alt Summit in Salt Lake City, I met my kindred spirit, Asia Rau Vinton, of
      <a href="http://www.sparrowsoirees.com/">
       SparrowSoires
      </a>
      . I mentioned to her that one night at the conference I just went back to my room at the hotel and snuggled up in bed to watch a few episodes of HBOs
      <a href="http://www.hbo.com/game-of-thrones#/">
       Game of Thrones
      </a>
      . She gasped as she also loves that show. Our meeting was destiny.
     </p>
     <p>
      We immediately concocted a plan to rendezvous back in Seattle to do a GOT Craft day. Was there such a place where our nerd-geek love of fantasy/sci-fi meets crafting? If so, we wanted to be there. Who wouldnt want to wear a pipe-cleaner crown and make dragon mobiles? Im sure Daenerys Targaryan would approve.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="alignnone wp-image-3055" height="585" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/got_dragon.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      This mobile was inspired by a 1964 book by the name of
      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Christmas-Magic-Margaret-PERRY/dp/B0026A09B2">
       Christmas Magic
      </a>
      written by Margaret Perry. I bought the book off Etsy and immediately fell in love with the retro mobiles featured in the book. I love them so much Im sure you will be seeing more of them in months to come. So for now instead of angels and Christmas balls, you will be getting dragons. :)
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Christmas Magic" class="alignnone wp-image-2671" height="384" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/1964_book.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       MATERIALS
      </strong>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Silhouette Cameo with Connect software
      </li>
      <li>
       Dragon template (
       <a href="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/KK_template_dragons.pdf">
        PDF
       </a>
       )
      </li>
      <li>
       Black text weight paper
      </li>
      <li>
       Glue stick
      </li>
      <li>
       X-acto knife
      </li>
      <li>
       18 gauge dark annealed wire
      </li>
      <li>
       Round needle nosed pliers
      </li>
      <li>
       Wire cutters
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      <img alt="Dragon mobile materials" class="alignnone wp-image-2667" height="384" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/materials.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       INSTRUCTIONS
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Step 1:
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Download the PDF template and open in Adobe Illustrator. Open through the Connect software and cut out 3 sets of dragons. (1 body and 2 wings each)
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Step 2:
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Glue on one wing to each side of the body, with the curved edges closest to the head.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Glue wings" class="alignnone wp-image-2668" height="384" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/glue_wings.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Step 3:
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Now measure and cut your wire in the following pieces:
      <br/>
      2  7 pieces
      <br/>
      1  5 piece
      <br/>
      3  3 pieces
     </p>
     <p>
      Use your needle nose pliers to create a small loop at each end of the pieces.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Wire" class="alignnone wp-image-2670" height="384" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/loop_wire.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Step 4:
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Now make a kink in the middle of one of the 7 pieces.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Wire kink" class="alignnone wp-image-2672" height="384" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/kink_wire.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Step 5:
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Make a small hole right in front of the wings using your x-acto knife. This is where you will thread a 3 piece of wire through. Do this step to all 3 dragons.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Thread wire" class="alignnone wp-image-2685" height="384" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/dragons_indiv.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Step 6:
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Hang on one on each end of the kinked wire.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Hang dragons" class="alignnone wp-image-2687" height="384" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/dragons_one.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Step 7:
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Take the other 7 piece and connect it onto the kinked loop. Then hang the last dragon onto the opposite end.
     </p>
     <p>
      Take your index finger and try and balance the wire on your finger. Find the sweet spot where its even and kink that wire with your pliers.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Balance" class="alignnone wp-image-2688" height="485" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/dragons_balance.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Step 8:
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Take your 5 piece of wire and connect it to that kinked wire.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Top piece" class="alignnone wp-image-2690" height="528" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/dragons_top_link.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      You can hang it directly from that top piece or thread some string or fishing line through the loop to hang.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Dragon Mobile" class="alignnone wp-image-2692" height="1110" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/staged_C.jpg" width="780"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="KK_scissors_aqua" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4033" height="50" src="http://kraftykath.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/KK_scissors_aqua.png" width="78"/>
     </p>
