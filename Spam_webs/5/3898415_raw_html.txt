     <p>
      <b>
       PG Tips
      </b>
      (pronounced
      <i>
       pee-gee tips
      </i>
      ) is a brand of
      <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">
       tea
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
       United Kingdom
      </a>
      , manufactured by
      <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">
       Unilever UK
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Brand_name">
       Brand name
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=PG_Tips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Brand name">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In the 1930s
      <a href="/wiki/Brooke_Bond" title="Brooke Bond">
       Brooke Bond
      </a>
      launched PG Tips in the UK tea market under the name
      <i>
       Pre-Gest-Tee
      </i>
      . The name implied that the tea could be drunk prior to eating food, as a digestive aid. Grocers and salesmen abbreviated it to PG.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBC-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">
       Second World War
      </a>
      , labelling regulations ruled out describing tea as aiding digestiona property that had been attributed to teaand by 1950/1 the PG name was adopted. The company added "Tips" referring to the fact that only the tips (the top two leaves and bud) of the
      <a href="/wiki/Camellia_sinensis" title="Camellia sinensis">
       tea plants
      </a>
      are used in the blend.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PGTea_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-PGTea-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Products">
       Products
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=PG_Tips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Products">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Brooke Bond name has now been dropped from all packaging, and the product is now known as PG Tips. PG Tips is available as loose tea,
      <a href="/wiki/Tea_bag" title="Tea bag">
       tea bags
      </a>
      , and in vending formats. A "Special Blend" tea, which is the same as the tea blended for the brand's 75th anniversary, is available in tea bag form only. The tea used in PG Tips is imported in bulk as single estate teas from around the world and blended in precise proportions set by the
      <a href="/wiki/Tea_tasting" title="Tea tasting">
       tea tasters
      </a>
      to make blend 777, which can contain between 12 and 35 single estate teas at any one time (depending on season, etc.) at the
      <a href="/wiki/Trafford_Park" title="Trafford Park">
       Trafford Park
      </a>
      factory in Manchester.
     </p>
     <p>
      PG Tags, tea bags with a string, were launched in 1985, and
      <a href="/wiki/Tetrahedron" title="Tetrahedron">
       tetrahedron shaped
      </a>
      tea bags in 1996 (branded as Pyramid Bags). The tetrahedral bag was designed to help the tea leaves move more freely, as loose tea moves in a teapot, and supposedly create a better infusion. One 2011 version of the product packaging makes the claim: "The PG Tips pyramid tea bag gives the tea leaves 50% more room to move around than a flat conventional tea bag. So the tea bag works more like a miniature tea pot. This allows for all the freshness to be released for the best tasting cup of PG."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      During the T-Birds era, the tetrahedral tea bags were remade with a "freeflow" material, to allow the tea to infuse better.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span id="Scottish_Blend">
      </span>
      In Scotland, Unilever sells a specially developed blend of PG called Scottish Blend. Scottish Blend is a version of PG Tips marketed in Scotland as being specially blended to optimise taste in the
      <a href="/wiki/Soft_water" title="Soft water">
       soft waters
      </a>
      of Scotland.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      PG tips, Scottish Blend and Lyons teas are exported by Unilever International, based in Leatherhead, UK and Singapore, through a worldwide network of food distributors. Unilever North America manage PG Tips in the USA since 2011. In
      <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">
       Ireland
      </a>
      , Unilever sells tea under the Lyons brand.
     </p>
     <p>
      As of 2011, a "Special Moments" range was released, initially as the "New Ones". These teas were made by pressing the leaves at different stages.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2014, Unilever have introduced a new range of fruit, herbal and green teas under the PG Tips brand.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Advertising">
       Advertising
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=PG_Tips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Advertising">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Unlike the blended teas many companies were producing, Brookes teas were pure, high-quality teas from India and China. Brooke realized the importance of advertising early on, introducing the slogan, "Good tea unites good company, exhilarates the spirits, banishes restraint from conversation and promotes the happiest purposes of social intercourse."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Tipps_family">
       The Tipps family
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=PG_Tips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Tipps family">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 1956 PG Tips began using
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropomorphic" title="Anthropomorphic">
       anthropomorphic
      </a>
      chimpanzees in their TV advertisements. These were dressed in human clothes and were known as the 'Tipps family'. Their voices were often provided by celebrities, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sellers" title="Peter Sellers">
       Peter Sellers
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Bob_Monkhouse" title="Bob Monkhouse">
       Bob Monkhouse
      </a>
      . By 1958 PG Tips had risen from fourth to first place in the British tea market.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The chimpanzees were from
      <a href="/wiki/Twycross_Zoo" title="Twycross Zoo">
       Twycross Zoo
      </a>
      in Leicestershire.
     </p>
     <p>
      These advertisements were stopped in the 1970s after complaints by animal rights organisations. However sales dropped and the chimps were bought back 18 months later. The last 'Tipps family' advert was broadcast in 2002.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The PG Tips chimps spawned a spin-off in memorabilia, including trading cards and figurines.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_T-Birds">
       The T-Birds
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=PG_Tips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The T-Birds">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The 'Tipps family' were replaced in 2002 with a house-sharing group of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claymation" title="Claymation">
       claymation
      </a>
      birds called the T-Birds, animated by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aardman" title="Aardman">
       Aardman
      </a>
      , the company behind
      <a href="/wiki/Wallace_and_Gromit" title="Wallace and Gromit">
       Wallace and Gromit
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Chicken_Run" title="Chicken Run">
       Chicken Run
      </a>
      . In Ireland these commercials were still airing in late 2006, though advertising Lyons Tea (another Unilever brand). This led to PG Tips becoming a major partner with Wallace and Gromit's first film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Wallace_%26_Gromit:_The_Curse_of_the_Were-Rabbit" title="Wallace &amp; Gromit: The Curse of the Were-Rabbit">
        The Curse of the Were-Rabbit
       </a>
      </i>
      , launched in October 2005. PG offered "Gromit" mugs on pack in the supermarket. According to
      <i>
       The Grocer
      </i>
      magazine, Unilever reported that during this "Gromit" mug promotion, PG Tips sales increased 600 percent. Wallace and Gromit also appeared in an advert with Lady Tottington (another character from the film) around the same time.
     </p>
     <p>
      PG Tips also produced a long-running series of trade cards as give-aways. These cards ceased production in 1999 after a survey of customers showed that they were not contributing to developing the business.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2005, PG Tips celebrated its 75th anniversary with special packs, including a limited edition Golden pack and a one-off
      <a href="/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">
       Diamond
      </a>
      tea bag. The Diamond tea bag cost 7,500 and was made by Boodles jewelers and used Makaibari Silver Tips (Imperial).
     </p>

     <p>
      In 2007, PG Tips reunited
      <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Vegas" title="Johnny Vegas">
       Johnny Vegas
      </a>
      as Al and the
      <a href="/wiki/ITV_Digital" title="ITV Digital">
       ITV Digital
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monkey_(advertising_character)" title="Monkey (advertising character)">
       Monkey
      </a>
      character puppeteered by
      <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Plaskitt" title="Nigel Plaskitt">
       Nigel Plaskitt
      </a>
      and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Susan_Beattie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Susan Beattie (page does not exist)">
       Susan Beattie
      </a>
      , voiced by
      <a href="/wiki/Ben_Miller" title="Ben Miller">
       Ben Miller
      </a>
      and made by Paul Jomain, following a holiday TV special about famously bad decisions (ITV Digital's launch) where they featured briefly. ITV Digital was a British digital terrestrial television broadcaster, which launched a pay-TV service on the world's first digital terrestrial television network and ceased broadcasting in May 2002.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Monkey character pointedly explains he's not a chimpanzee, he's a monkey, a nod to PG Tips' chimpanzee family.
     </p>
     <p>
      One of the adverts was a spoof of the "deli scene" from the movie
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/When_Harry_Met_Sally" title="When Harry Met Sally">
        When Harry Met Sally
       </a>
      </i>
      ; in the advert Monkey describes the taste of PG Tips by saying "Oh Yes" repeatedly like in the movie, finishing with a woman at a table nearby asking the waiter "I'll have whatever he's having". The ad ends with the tagline "How would you describe the taste?". It was first shown on 3 February 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Another advert, promoting the naturally occurring
      <a href="/wiki/Theanine" title="Theanine">
       theanine
      </a>
      in PG Tips, featured Monkey and Al playing the characters of Captain Mainwaring and Private Pike from the popular sitcom
      <a href="/wiki/Dad%27s_Army" title="Dad's Army">
       Dad's Army
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      When PG Tips released the "Special Moments" range (initially "The New Ones"), another advert was released to advertise the "Fresh" one. It featured Monkey and Al out rambling. Monkey says to Al that they need a cup of the "Fresh" one. Al takes so long to decide how the tea tastes that before he can come to a conclusion, Monkey has been swept away by an eagle. This advert was later modified showing Monkey being dropped off in the eagle's nest with the four flavours of the Special Moments range, as well as two eagle chicks next to him.

     <p>
      A
      <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">
       short film
      </a>
      entitled
      <i>
       A Tale of Two Continents
      </i>
      was released in March 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is an
      <a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">
       adventure film
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">
       parody
      </a>
      , starring Monkey "wanting to change the world one tea at a time". It was shown in
      <a href="/wiki/Movie_theater" title="Movie theater">
       cinemas
      </a>
      from 21 March 2008 until 10 April, before the showings of family films such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Spiderwick_Chronicles" title="The Spiderwick Chronicles">
        The Spiderwick Chronicles
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horton_Hears_a_Who" title="Horton Hears a Who">
        Horton Hears a Who
       </a>
      </i>
      . It was also given free in special limited edition versions of PG Tips in early 2008 as an
      <a href="/wiki/EcoDisc" title="EcoDisc">
       EcoDisc
      </a>
      , a type of DVD that is thinner and more flexible due to it being made of a single layer of
      <a href="/wiki/Polycarbonate" title="Polycarbonate">
       polycarbonate
      </a>
      , instead of two layers. The limited edition package also featured a teatowel of the EcoDisc cover, described as the "official merchandise" of the film.

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="PG_Tips_and_sustainability">
       PG Tips and sustainability
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=PG_Tips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: PG Tips and sustainability">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In May 2007, Unilever became the first company to commit to sourcing all its tea in a sustainable manner.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      To that end, the company asked the
      <a href="/wiki/Rainforest_Alliance" title="Rainforest Alliance">
       Rainforest Alliance
      </a>
      , an international environmental
      <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">
       NGO
      </a>
      to start certifying tea estates in East Africa.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Since April 2012 all of the tea used in PG Tips has been Rainforest Alliance certified.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2011 the maker of PG Tips stated it is to stop testing its teas on animals.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
