        <span class="focusParagraph">
         <p>
          <span class="articleLocation">
           DALLAS
          </span>
          Four people close to the first person diagnosed with Ebola in the United States were quarantined in a Dallas apartment where  sheets and other items used by the man were sealed in plastic bags as health officials widened their search for people who had direct or indirect contact with him.
         </p>
        </span>
        <span id="midArticle_1">
        </span>
        <p>
         Health officials said on Thursday that 12 to 18 people had direct contact with Thomas Eric Duncan, who flew to Texas from Liberia via Brussels and Washington two weeks ago, and they in turn had contact with scores of others.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_2">
        </span>
        <p>
         Up to 100 people have been contacted and a handful were being monitored, said Dr. Thomas Frieden, director of the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_3">
        </span>
        <p>
         None of those thought to have had contact with the patient were showing symptoms of Ebola, Dallas County officials said at a news conference. Duncan had been staying in an apartment in the northeastern part of the city for about a week before going to a Dallas hospital.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_4">
        </span>
        <p>
         "The sheets were placed in a sealed plastic bag and have been in the bag, as well as the belongings of Mr. Duncan, those were also in a bag," said Clay Jenkins, Dallas County's top political official.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_5">
        </span>
        <p>
         In Liberia, the head of the country's airport authority, Binyah Kesselly, said the government could prosecute Duncan for denying he had contact with someone who was eventually diagnosed with Ebola.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_6">
        </span>
        <p>
         The government said Duncan failed to declare that he helped neighbor Marthalene Williams after she fell critically ill on Sept. 15. Williams died.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_7">
        </span>
        <p>
         Kesselly said Duncan was asked in a questionnaire whether he had come in contact with any Ebola victim or was showing any symptoms. "To all of these questions, Mr. Duncan answered 'no,'" Kesselly said.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_8">
        </span>
        <p>
         Ebola can cause fever, bleeding, vomiting and diarrhea and spreads through contact with bodily fluids such as blood or saliva. Duncan's case put U.S. health authorities and the public on alert over concern for the potential of the virus to spread from Liberia and two other impoverished West African countries, Guinea and Sierra Leone. The virus has killed at least 3,338 people in the worst such outbreak since the disease was identified in 1976.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_9">
        </span>
        <span class="first-article-divide">
        </span>
        <p>
         President Barack Obama called Dallas Mayor Mike Rawlings on Thursday and "pledged federal agencies will remain in close coordination and reiterated his confidence in America's doctors and national health infrastructure to handle this case safely and effectively," White House spokesman Eric Schultz said.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_10">
        </span>
        <p>
         Officials have said the U.S. healthcare system is well prepared to contain the hemorrhagic fever's spread by careful tracking of those who have had contact with Duncan, and employing appropriate care.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_11">
        </span>
        <p>
         Dallas County officials said the problem was very localized. "When I say local, I dont mean Dallas. I mean a very specific neighborhood in the northeast part of Dallas," Dallas Mayor Rawlings told reporters.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_12">
        </span>
        <p>
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_13">
        </span>
        <p>
         HOSPITAL SENT PATIENT AWAY
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_14">
        </span>
        <span class="second-article-divide">
        </span>
        <p>
         Duncan initially sought treatment at Texas Health Presbyterian Hospital on the night of Sept. 25 but was sent back to the apartment, with antibiotics, despite telling a nurse he had just been in Liberia. By Sunday, he needed an ambulance to return to the same hospital after vomiting on the ground outside the apartment complex.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_15">
        </span>
        <p>
         He was in serious condition on Thursday, no change from Wednesday, a hospital spokeswoman said.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_0">
        </span>
        <p>
         Police and armed security guards were keeping people about 100 yards (meters) away from the apartment, with orange cones blocking the entrance and exit. Maintenance workers scrubbed the parking lot with high-pressure water and bleach.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_1">
        </span>
        <p>
         Dr. David Lakey, commissioner of the Texas Department of State Health Services, said the four people under quarantine did not have a fever and were healthy.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_2">
        </span>
        <span class="third-article-divide">
        </span>
        <p>
         Lakey said monitoring included fever checks twice a day. At the apartment, "there is a law enforcement person there in case individuals leave," Lakey told reporters on a conference call.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_3">
        </span>
        <p>
         U.S. officials initially described the number of people potentially exposed as a handful, and on Wednesday said it was up to 18. Then on Thursday, the Texas health department said there were about 100 potential contacts.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_4">
        </span>
        <p>
         CNN reported that a Dallas woman who had a child with Duncan said he had sweated profusely in the bed they shared at her apartment. The woman, whom CNN identified only as "Louisa," is quarantined in the apartment with one of her children, who is 13, and two visiting nephews in their 20s.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_5">
        </span>
        <p>
         They were all in the home when Duncan began showing signs of illness, the report said. The woman said she mentioned twice to hospital staff that he had come from Liberia.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_6">
        </span>
        <p>
         Dr. Amesh Adalja, an infectious disease physician at the University of Pittsburgh, said contact tracing is bread-and-butter public health," and something health officials do regularly to track tuberculosis, measles and sexually transmitted diseases.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_7">
        </span>
        <p>
         Adalja said the most disturbing part of the U.S. incident is  that Duncan was sent home from the hospital with antibiotics.
        </p>
        <span id="midArticle_8">
        </span>
        <p>
         This really is something that shouldnt have happened, he said. It just reinforces that taking a travel history has to be an essential part of taking care of patients."
        </p>

}

$('.slider-module').slick({
	arrows: false,
	touchMove: false,
	swipeToSlide: true
});

$('.slider-left').click(function(){
	$(".slider").slickPrev();
	checkWebTrends();
    if (webTrendsExists) {
            Webtrends.multiTrack({
                argsa: [
                    "WT.z_event", "previous-slide",
                    "WT.dl", "16"
                ]
            });
        }
});
$('.slider-right').click(function(){
	$(".slider").slickNext();
	checkWebTrends();
    if (webTrendsExists) {
            Webtrends.multiTrack({
                argsa: [
                    "WT.z_event", "next-slide",
                    "WT.dl", "16"
                ]
            });
        }
});


function weChatBox_show(tag,url){
$("#shr-qrcode").empty();  
if ( $('html').hasClass( 'canvas' ) ) {
	$("#shr-qrcode").qrcode({
		render: "canvas",
		width:178,
		height:178,
		text: url
	});
} else {
	$("#shr-qrcode").qrcode({
		render: "table",
		width:178,
		height:178,
		text: url
	});
}
    shrBoxShow(tag);
}

function shrBoxHide(tag){
	$('.shr-overlay').removeClass('active');
}

function shrBoxShow(tag){
    $('.shr-overlay').addClass('active');
}

$(document).ready(function() {
    var relatedNewsModules = $('.related-news');
    var relatedVideoModules = $('.related-video');
    var relatedNewsObj;
    var relatedVideoObj;
    var littleHeader;
    $.each(relatedNewsModules, function(i) {
        relatedNewsObj = $(relatedNewsModules[i]);
	littleHeader = relatedNewsObj.prev().prev().prev();
	if (littleHeader.text() === littleHeader.text().toUpperCase()) {
            relatedNewsObj.insertBefore(littleHeader);
        }
    });
    $.each(relatedVideoModules, function(i) {
	relatedVideoObj = $(relatedVideoModules[i]);
	littleHeader = relatedVideoObj.prev().prev().prev();
	if (littleHeader.text() === littleHeader.text().toUpperCase()) {
            relatedVideoObj.insertBefore(littleHeader);
        } 
    });
});
