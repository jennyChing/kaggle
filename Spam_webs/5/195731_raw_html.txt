     <h1>
      Which team are you cheering for at the World Cup?
     </h1>
     <div class="post-inner clearfix">
      <div class="post-meta">
       <div class="post-meta-author">
        by
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/author/matthershberger/">
         Matt Hershberger
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="post-meta-date">
        June 5, 2014
       </div>
       <div class="post-avatar">
        <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-100" height="100" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/authors/matthershberger.jpg" width="100"/>
       </div>
       <div class="post-share-wrapper post-share--vertical" data-post-id="249088" data-sticky="" data-sticky-max-width="991" data-sticky-offset-top="69" data-sticky-parent-contain="" data-sticky-parent-selector=".page-section">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="post-content">
       <div class="top-gallery-ad">
        <div class="widget matador_dfp_channel_ad_widget">

       <p>
        <span style="text-transform:uppercase;">
         For my money, the World Cup
        </span>
        is more exciting than the Olympics. Theres a lot more riding on each game, and I get to root for my home country  the United States  and be an underdog. That pretty much never happens to America in international sporting events.
       </p>
       <p>
        Theres just a few weeks left until the 2014 World Cup in Brazil starts up, and to commemorate the event,
        <a href="http://espnmediazone.com/us/photos/galleries/2014/05/02/2014-fifa-world-cup-international-posters#photo-1">
         ESPN
        </a>
        commissioned
        <a href="http://www.crisvector.com/1/">
         Brazilian artist Cristiano Siqueira
        </a>
        to create national posters for each team. The posters include one or more of the teams star players, their nickname, and a unique design. They are basically perfect for printing out and hanging up on your wall for the duration of the tournament.
       </p>

        <img alt="" src="http://cdn.matadornetwork.com.s3.amazonaws.com/assets/images/icons/mfinish.png" title=""/>

         All images via

          Business Insider
         </a>
         .
        </em>
       </p>

           <h2>
            Algeria
           </h2>
           <p>
            Algerias team nickname, Les Fennecs, translates to The Fennec Foxes. Pictured is Sofiane Feghouli, who plays for Valencia CF when hes not playing for the Algeria National Team.
           </p>

           <h2>
            Argentina
           </h2>
           <p>
            The nickname Albicelestes translates to White and Sky Blue, which, come on Argentina, you can do better than that. How about the Gauchos, or the Grass-Fed Argentine Beefs? The player, obviously, is Lionel Messi, who is a year younger than me and arguably one of the best soccer player of all time.
           </p>

            Australia
           </h2>
           <p>

Pictured here is all-time Australia National Team top-scorer and Vice Captain Tim Cahill, who, in his free time, plays for the New York Red Bulls in the MLS. He also played for Millwall and Everton in the UK.
