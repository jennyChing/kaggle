              <h1>
               Best Foods to Eat During a Crohn's Flare-Up
              </h1>
              <h2>
               Eating foods that are gentle on your stomach and lower GI tract can help you get through uncomfortable and painful Crohn's symptoms.
              </h2>
             </div>
             <div class="bottom">
              <div class="floatl">
               <div class="byline">
                By Joy Bauer, RD, creator of
                <a href="http://www.joybauer.com" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
                 JoyBauer.com

               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  1
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  In the midst of a Crohn's flare, you may not feel like eating much. By choosing foods that are easier to digest you'll get the nutrition you need even when you aren't feeling well. While some of these items are not necessarily healthy (e.g., white pasta, cereal, and rice), it's important to understand that they all serve a specific purpose: to heal your GI tract. Speak with your doctor about when it's a good time to start
                  <a href="/health-report/crohns-diet-pictures/joy-bauer-best-foods-for-crohns-disease.aspx">
                   easing up on your diet
                  </a>
                  to include more whole grains, vegetables, fruit, and other sources of dietary fiber.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  <em>
                   While these foods are generally recommended during a
flare, Crohn's disease affects everyone differently. Work with your doctor or a
registered dietitian to determine the best meal plan for you and avoid any
foods that worsen your symptoms.
                  </em>
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>

                <img alt="white pasta for crohn's" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/photogallery_foods_for_crohns_flare_ups_01_th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Regular White Pasta
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  2
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  Regular pasta is a complex carbohydrate that is low in fiber and easy on the digestive tract. It's a quick source of energy and a great fall-back meal that can help maintain your weight during times of flare-ups. Toss your pasta with olive oil or a healthy soft-tub spread to increase the flavor as well as the calories.
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                   By Joy Bauer, RD, creator of
                   <a href="http://www.joybauer.com" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
                    JoyBauer.com
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>

               <div class="galleria-stage">
                <img alt="cream of rice for crohn's" class="slideshow-image" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/photogallery_foods_for_crohns_flare_ups_02_full.jpg" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/blank.gif"/>
                <img alt="cream of rice for crohn's" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/photogallery_foods_for_crohns_flare_ups_02_th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Cream of Rice Cereal
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  3
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  This is a soothing option for people dealing with active Crohn's flare-ups; just be sure to buy a brand that is fortified with iron and zinc. To add calcium and protein, prepare the rice cereal with milk instead of water (use skim, 1 percent low-fat, soy milk, or almond milk)  then sweeten with a few teaspoons of pure maple syrup or honey and sprinkle on some cinnamon if you like.
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                   By Joy Bauer, RD, creator of
                   <a href="http://www.joybauer.com" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
                    JoyBauer.com
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>

                <img alt="ripe banana for crohn's" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/photogallery_foods_for_crohns_flare_ups_03_th.jpg " style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Ripe Banana
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  4
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  Bananas are generally well tolerated during flare-ups and provide a good source of potassium. By adding banana slices to low-fiber breakfast cereals, creamy yogurts, and cottage cheese, you can easily boost the nutrition and flavor. And definitely try my banana ice cream: Cut ripe bananas into one-inch rounds and freeze in a freezer bag. When you're ready for ice cream, place banana slices in food processer (I let them thaw for half an hour on the counter so they puree better) and puree until smooth. You'll make sweet, ultra-creamy, pure banana ice cream. You can also add one tablespoon of smooth natural peanut butter into the mix, and ta-da, you have delicious PB &amp; banana ice cream.
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                   By Joy Bauer, RD, creator of
                   <a href="http://www.joybauer.com" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
                    JoyBauer.com
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>

                <img alt="poultry and fish for crohn's" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/photogallery_foods_for_crohns_flare_ups_04_th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Poultry and Fish
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  5
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  Fish and skinless poultry tend to be very well tolerated during flare-ups and provide ample amounts of protein (and some iron), which is vital. Opt for healthy, light cooking methods such as grilling, baking, broiling, poaching, steaming, roasting, or lightly sauting  and couple your lean protein entre with white rice or pasta for a complete dinner that's gentle on your stomach. Bonus: Fatty fish like wild salmon and sardines provide the added benefit of omega-3 fats, which help ease inflammation.
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                   By Joy Bauer, RD, creator of
                   <a href="http://www.joybauer.com" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
                    JoyBauer.com
                   </a>

                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  6
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  A perfectly safe sweet treat or quick snack that travels everywhere. Natural applesauce is also a good source of vitamin C. If you'd like to slightly increase the fiber (by approximately 1 gram), as well as boost the calories and mineral content, stir in a tablespoon of wheat germ (
                  <em>
                   not
                  </em>
                  wheat bran).

                   By Joy Bauer, RD, creator of
                   <a href="http://www.joybauer.com" rel="nofollow" target="_new">

                <img alt="hard cheese for crohn's" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/photogallery_foods_for_crohns_flare_ups_06_th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>

                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Sliced Hard Cheese
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  7
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  One slice of cheese can provide 200 mg of calcium and is a concentrated source of calories. During times of flare-ups, people often experience pain that causes them to self-limit their diet. Cheese can be an easy way to keep up with your calcium requirements without the lactose, as many hard cheeses are naturally lactose-free (Swiss, cheddar, etc.). You'll definitely want to avoid greasy-cheesy foods (think pizza), though, since they can often exacerbate nausea.

During a time of flare-up, though, it's important not to eat the skin of potatoes as they might aggravate the inflamed portion of your intestine.

In fact, fortified cereals mixed with milk can provide an excellent source of calcium and vitamin D  not to mention 100 percent of your needs for most B vitamins, as well as some iron. You've got my personal green light to use these cereals as a security blanket during times of inflammation and/or malabsorption.
