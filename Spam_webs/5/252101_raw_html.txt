         <p>
          The Australian Dollar fell to its lowest levels against the US Dollar since May of 2009 in early trading on Thursday.
         </p>
         <p>
          Falling commodity prices and disappointing business confidence have increased speculation that the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) will cut rates in the coming months.
         </p>
         <p>
          The key interest rate in Australia currently stands at 2.5% while in the US the rate is 0.25%.
         </p>
         <p>
          The US Federal Reserve issued its policy decision yesterday, keeping interest rates on hold as widely expected by analysts.
         </p>

          Fed chair Janet Yellen said the economy was expanding rapidly and used language that suggested to analysts that the central bank is on track to raise interest rates in 2015, thereby underpinning the strength in the US dollar.
