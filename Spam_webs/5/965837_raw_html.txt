       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Quick and Easy Braised Tofu (Hongshao Dofu)
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-12-29T10:24:06+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          December 29, 2013
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://thewoksoflife.com/author/sarahleung838gmail-com/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Sarah
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/2013/12/quick-easy-braised-tofu-hongshao-dofu/#comments">
           6 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/2013/12/quick-easy-braised-tofu-hongshao-dofu/" title="Quick and Easy Braised Tofu (Hongshao Dofu)">
        <img alt="DSC_0213" class="post-image" height="464" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0213.jpg" width="640"/>
       </a>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fthewoksoflife.com%2F2013%2F12%2Fquick-easy-braised-tofu-hongshao-dofu%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fthewoksoflife.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2013%2F12%2FDSC_0158.jpg&amp;description=Quick%20and%20Easy%20Braised%20Tofu%20%28Hongshao%20Dofu%29" rel="nobox">
          <img data-pib-button="true" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         This is my grandpas favorite dish when we go out to eat at this little Cantonese restaurant on Division Street in Chinatown. I have to say that this is a pretty crazy authentic homemade version, courtesy of my dad, natch.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tofu has got to be the most awesomely delicious food with the worst reputation ever. Its got a huge variety of textures, and essentially takes on whatever flavor you add to it. This dish is vegetarian/vegan (if you leave out the oyster sauce or use a vegetarian brand), but you dont miss the meat. At all. I would gladly pass over a hunk of grilled meat for this. And youll only be standing at the stove for five to ten minutes, max.
        </p>
        <p>
         Try it! Youll need:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1 tablespoon cornstarch
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tablespoon water
         </li>
         <li>
          1 block of medium firm tofu
         </li>
         <li>
           cup sliced fresh black mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, or reconstituted dried mushrooms
         </li>
         <li>
          1  cups chicken or vegetable stock
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional  or use a vegetarian oyster sauce, and yes, there is a brand that makes it!)
         </li>
         <li>
           teaspoon sesame oil
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tablespoon light soy
         </li>
         <li>
          1 teaspoon dark soy
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tablespoon shaoxing wine
         </li>
         <li>
           teaspoon sugar
         </li>
         <li>
           teaspoon salt
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tablespoon oil
         </li>
         <li>
          1 clove minced garlic
         </li>
         <li>
          a handful of snow peas, washed and trimmed
         </li>
         <li>
          1 small carrot, thinly sliced
         </li>
         <li>
          1 small red bell pepper, sliced
         </li>
         <li>
           cup sliced bamboo shoots (optional)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Mix cornstarch and water into a slurry in a small bowl and set aside. Drain the tofu of excess liquid and cut into  inch thick slices. Wash and slice your mushrooms. If you are using the dried mushrooms, wash about 4 of them, soak in hot water for an hour, and trim the stems for before slicing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0158.jpg">
          <img alt="DSC_0158" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2914" height="400" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0158.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Make a stock mixture by combining the stock, oyster sauce, sesame oil, soy sauces, shaoxing wine, sugar, and salt in a bowl.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heat the wok over high heat and add the oil. Spread the tofu in 1 layer in the wok. Sear each side for about a minute. If you need more time to turn the tofu, reduce the heat to medium and take your time! Add the garlic to the wok.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0155.jpg">
          <img alt="DSC_0155" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2912" height="426" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0155.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         After another minute, add the stock mixture and the mushrooms. Return the heat to high if its not already there. Give the wok a shake to prevent the tofu from sticking. When the mixture comes to a boil, lower the heat to medium, cover and let cook for about 5 minutes. Some of the liquid should evaporate.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0163.jpg">
          <img alt="DSC_0163" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2915" height="426" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0163.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Remove the cover, turn the wok back up to high heat and add your vegetables.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0169.jpg">
          <img alt="DSC_0169" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2916" height="426" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0169.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Gently toss them with the tofu, being careful not to break the tofu pieces. Quickly add the cornstarch slurry a little at a time to thicken the remaining liquid just enough to coat tofu and vegetables. If the sauce is too thick, just add more water or stock. If its too thin, add more slurry.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0178.jpg">
          <img alt="DSC_0178" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2917" height="426" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0178.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Give the dish a final toss, and serve immediately with some steamed rice.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0192.jpg">
          <img alt="DSC_0192" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2918" height="640" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0192.jpg" width="518"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0200.jpg">
          <img alt="DSC_0200" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2919" height="426" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DSC_0200.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>

        </p>

              cup sliced fresh black mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, or reconstituted dried mushrooms
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             1  cups chicken or vegetable stock
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional - or use a vegetarian oyster sauce)
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
              teaspoon sesame oil
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon light soy
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoon dark soy
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon shaoxing wine
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
              teaspoon sugar
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
              teaspoon salt
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon oil
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-13" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 clove minced garlic
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-14" itemprop="ingredients">
             a handful of snow peas, washed and trimmed
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-15" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 small carrot, thinly sliced
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-16" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 small red bell pepper, sliced
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-17" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup sliced bamboo shoots (optional)
            </div>
           </span>
           <span class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Mix cornstarch and water into a slurry in a small bowl and set aside. Drain the tofu of excess liquid and cut into  inch thick slices. Wash and slice your mushrooms. If you are using the dried mushrooms, wash about 4 of them, soak in hot water for an hour, and trim the stems for before slicing.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Make a stock mixture by combining the stock, oyster sauce, sesame oil, soy sauces, shaoxing wine, sugar, and salt in a bowl.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Heat the wok over high heat and add the oil. Spread the tofu in 1 layer in the wok. Sear each side for about a minute. If you need more time to turn the tofu, reduce the heat to medium and take your time! Add the garlic to the wok.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             After another minute, add the stock mixture and the mushrooms. Return the heat to high if its not already there. Give the wok a shake to prevent the tofu from sticking. When the mixture comes to a boil, lower the heat to medium, cover and let cook for about 5 minutes. Some of the liquid should evaporate.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Remove the cover, turn the wok back up to high heat and add your vegetables. Gently toss them with the tofu, being careful not to break the tofu pieces. Quickly add the cornstarch slurry a little at a time to thicken the remaining liquid just enough to coat tofu and vegetables. If the sauce is too thick, just add more water or stock. If its too thin, add more slurry. Give the dish a final toss, and serve immediately with some steamed rice.
            </p>
           </span>
