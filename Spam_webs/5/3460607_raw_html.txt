       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Charles Odds and Ends (Make the Moments Count)
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta mobile-meta">
        <span class="sep">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://intersections4life.com/2014/04/charles-odds-and-ends-make-the-moments-count/" rel="bookmark" title="9:14 am">
         <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-04-04T09:14:11+00:00">
          April 4, 2014
         </time>
        </a>
        <span class="byline">
         <span class="sep">
          by
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn n" href="http://intersections4life.com/author/crutch/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Charles Crutcher">
           Charles Crutcher
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
      </header>
      <!-- .entry-header -->
      <div class="entry-content post_content">
       <p>
        I was walking along the little river in Bricktown one summer afternoon. It was a Friday, sorta like this one, and the sounds of laughter carried across the waters from the other side where people were enjoying one anothers company. The place was alive and people were everywhere.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        But then something caught my attention. A girl dressed up in a luxurious pink dress and a hat to match. It seemed odd and out of place and then I noticed when she moved her head to the side she had no hair. Her face was pale and she grimaced as she walked with a man I am sure was her father.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Cancer would not stop them from enjoying this day.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        She looked up at her dad in clear pain, but still she produced a smile for him. But the moment was short as pain shot through her. He braced with his arms while they walked together, focusing intently on her. I could see concern and heartache. They stood out against the backdrop of the mass of people caught up in their own stories.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        I dont know how far along the cancer had spread, or do I know what stage it was at. But the one thing I remember thinking to myself at that moment was that we live on borrowed time. We are each on a loaner from God and He calls us home soon. This father wasnt going to allow cancer to keep him from making a memory and neither was his daughter.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Today, take the moments as they come and give them your full attention. Make them count.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Everything in the world is about to be wrapped up, so take nothing for granted. Stay wide-awake in prayer. Most of all, love each other as if your life depended on it. Love makes up for practically anything. Be quick to give a meal to the hungry, a bed to the homelesscheerfully. Be generous with the different things God gave you, passing them around so all get in on it: if words, let it be Gods words; if help, let it be Gods hearty help.-1 Peter 4:7-10
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
