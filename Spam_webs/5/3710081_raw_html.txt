        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Heavenly Blueberry Coconut Coffee Cake
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-06-03T06:30:30+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          June 3, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/author/amandaespinoza/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Amanda Espinoza
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/2014/06/03/heavenly-blueberry-coconut-coffee-cake/#comments">
           22 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Yes, you can have a heavenly piece of coffee cake and still be walking the healthy-eating walk. Whats more, you can enjoy it, even when baked with coconut flour! I know many people have avoided
         <a href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/2014/02/11/coconut-flour/" title="A Beginners Guide to Coconut Flour">
          baking with coconut flour
         </a>
         because the results can end eggy tasting. The texture can be like cardboard and it can be a pain to work with. So let me relieve some of your worries by giving you a great recipe to work with and some tips.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Make this heavenly blueberry coconut coffee cake for dessert tonight! This recipe tastes delicious and is GAPS, candida diet, and paleo friendly. @ IntoxicatedOnLife.com #SugarFreeDesserts #HealthyRecipes" class="aligncenter wp-image-56367" height="675" src="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Untitled-design-12-682x1024.png" width="450"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         How to Work with Coconut Flour
        </h2>
        <p>
         Let me take some of the mystery out of it for you before we delve into the recipe:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          First, you should always, always mix your wet ingredients together before you do anything.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Second, you should use a whisk. Using stand mixers can leave you withover mixed batter. Besides, its so easy to work with, you dont need one.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Third, you should add the coconut flour slowly, resting between each addition. As each brand and batch of coconut flour can be different, this is a veryimportant step.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Last, if after adding your raising agent(s) and your sweetener, you find the batter too thick, add some more liquid. This always helps me to get the lift and fluff I need out of my baked coconut flour goods.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         If you follow these steps, you will be a coconut flour baker extraordinaire!
        </p>
        <h2>
         Why Use Coconut Flour?
        </h2>
        <p>
         We choose to use coconut flourover other nut flours when grain free baking, because of known thyroid issues
         <em>
          .
         </em>
         What we have found was that they were aggravating our, specifically my, Hashimotos condition. I have a very similar story to Trisha (
         <a href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/2014/05/02/doctors-gamble-health-testing-treating-hypothyroidism/" target="_blank">
          read some of her story here)
         </a>
         . For years I struggled with this. Now I have answers. We used to use almond flour and other nut flours all the time as there were more recipes online for them, and they were
         <a href="http://www.gapsdiet.com/" target="_blank" title="GAPS">
          GAPS
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.trimhealthymama.com/" target="_blank" title="Trim Healthy Mama">
          Trim Healthy Mama
         </a>
         friendly. I would end up feeling sluggish after eating such food. With coconut flour products, I do not feel the this way. After taking a break for quite some time from nuts, I can now eat them much more often. However, I quickly find out if I have over done them by the returning lethargy and over-stuffed feeling that happens. (See this
         <a href="http://www.autoimmunemom.com/diet/dr-k-autoimmune-hypothyroidism-diet.html" target="_blank" title="Food List for Hashi's and Hypothyroid">
          food list for people struggling with Hashimotosand hypothyroid.
         </a>
         )
        </p>
        <h2>
         Some Recipe Information:
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          This is a dairy-free and grain-free recipe.
         </li>
         <li>
          It also fits
          <a href="http://www.trimhealthymama.com/" target="_blank" title="Trim Healthy Mama">
           *Trim Healthy Mama
          </a>
          , paleo, gluten-free,
          <a href="http://www.gapsdiet.com/" target="_blank" title="GAPS">
           GAPS
          </a>
          , and
          <a href="http://www.thecandidadiet.com/" target="_blank" title="Candida Diet">
           Candida
          </a>
          type diets.
         </li>
         <li>
          You may tweak the recipe to suite your taste buds by changing out the berries you use.
         </li>
         <li>
          You may add
          <a href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/2012/11/27/benefits-of-honey/">
           honey
          </a>
          if you like, although it may not fit some of the above categories. Make sure to adjust the liquids.
         </li>
         <li>
          You may also sub out any milk or oil for dairy products if you like, again it may not fit the categories above if you do so.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Remember how I mentioned that you may need to add some liquids to the batter? This is what the batter looked like just after adding all the coconut flour:
         <a href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/027.jpg">
          <img alt="027" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-55906" height="300" src="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/027-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         Here is what the batter looked like after a little longer rest after the addition of the raising agents and sweetener:
         <a href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/028.jpg">
          <img alt="028" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-55907" height="300" src="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/028-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         This is what the batter looks like after adding 3/4 cup of water, placed in the pyrex dish and full of the topping. Yummy!:
         <a href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/029.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-55908 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/029-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/030.jpg">
          <img alt="030" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-55909" height="300" src="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/030-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>

         </a>
        </p>

             <img alt="Heavenly Blueberry Coconut Coffee Cake" class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/023.jpg" title="Heavenly Blueberry Coconut Coffee Cake"/>
            </p>
            <div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description">
             <p class="summary italic">
              Heavenly Blueberry Coconut Coffee Cake is a wonderful dairy free and grain free recipe for breakfast, snack or dessert. This uses coconut flour, coconut milk, coconut oil, stevia and nuts. Very adaptable to any palate and diet.  Serves 12 as is using a 9x13 pyrex baking dish, or you may half the recipe and use an 8x8 size baking dish.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <span id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <div class="ingredient-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0">
             Cake Ingredients
            </div>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             8 eggs (
             <a class="ingredient-link" href="http://www.intoxicatedonlife.com/2013/01/09/wednesday-blog-link-up-farm-fresh-eggs/" target="_blank">
              free range organic
             </a>
             or omega-3 are best)
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             3/4 cup
             <a class="ingredient-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/B001EO5Q64?tag=intoonlife-20" target="_blank">
              coconut oil
             </a>
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 - 1/2 tsp stevia extract powder (I use Trim Healthy Mama brand stevia)
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 TBS pure vanilla extract (gluten free is best)
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             13.5 oz of full fat coconut milk (homemade or typically one can)
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup
             <a class="ingredient-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/B003XB3NNE?tag=intoonlife-20" target="_blank">
              coconut flour
             </a>
             , separated into thirds (do not pack)
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tsp baking soda
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 tsp apple cider vinegar
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 tsp sea salt
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
             water
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 cups  wild blueberries (or berries of choice)
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12" itemprop="ingredients">
            </p>
            <div class="ingredient-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-13">
             Topping Ingredients
            </div>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-14" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 1/2 cups almonds (soaked is best or you may try my favorite Trail Mix Muesli as a topping)
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-15" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 cup
             <a class="ingredient-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/B000F4D5IU?tag=intoonlife-20" target="_blank">
              unsweetened coconut flakes
             </a>
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-16" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 pinches of stevia extract powder (or to taste)
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-17" itemprop="ingredients">
             3 tbs softened coconut oil
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-18" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tbs cinnamon
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-19" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 tsp nutmeg (optional)
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-20" itemprop="ingredients">
             a pinch of sea salt
            </p>
            <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-21" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cups wild blueberries
            </p>
           </span>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             With a whisk mix eggs, coconut oil, vanilla, and milk.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add coconut flour 1/3 cup at a time mixing and letting it rest a little between additions.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add stevia, salt, baking soda and apple cider vinegar.  Mix well.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             If at this point the batter is too thick after resting a little, add water.  Start with 1/4 cup at a time.  I have had to go to a full 3/4 cup of water.  You want the consistency of a nice creamy, slightly thick cake batter.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Fold in 2 cups of blueberries.  Pour into greased 9X13 inch pyrex.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Mix together nuts, stevia, salt, coconut and coconut oil for the topping with a fork in a bowl.  Sprinkle evenly over the top of the cake.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Lastly, sprinkle the blueberries evenly over the top.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Place in the oven at 350 degrees.  Bake for 45 minutes. Check at 40 minutes to be sure it is not over browning.  If it is and needs a longer cooking time, cover with aluminum foil to keep the topping from burning.  You may need to cook for another 10-15 minutes longer.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             To test cake for done-ness, make sure that the cake is not jiggling in the center when you pull it out.  You may also test using a toothpick. It is best if you have a little crumb left on the toothpick.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-9" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Let the cake cool for a few minutes before cutting or overnight. I haven't decided whether I like it better the day of or the day after.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-10" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Serve and enjoy!
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-notes">
            Notes
           </p>
           <div id="zlrecipe-notes-list">
            <p class="notes">
             This may take up to an hour to cook. So be watchful.
            </p>
