           <h3>
            Whatever Rotary may mean to us, to the world it will be known by the results it achieves.
           </h3>
           <h4>
            Paul P. Harris
           </h4>
           <p>
            Our 1.2 million-member organization started with the vision of one manPaul P. Harris. The Chicago attorney formed one of the worlds first service organizations, the Rotary Club of Chicago, on 23 February 1905 as a place where professionals with diverse backgrounds could exchange ideas and form meaningful, lifelong friendships. Rotarys name came from the groups early practice of rotating meetings among the offices of each member.
           </p>
           <h3>
            Our ongoing commitment
           </h3>
           <p>
            Rotarians have not only been present for major events in historyweve been a part of them. From the beginning, three key traits have remained strong throughout Rotary:
           </p>
           <p>
            Were truly international. Only 16 years after being founded, Rotary had clubs on six continents. Today were working together from around the globe both digitally and in-person to solve some of our worlds most challenging problems.
           </p>
           <p>
            We persevere in tough times. During WWII, Rotary clubs in Germany, Austria, Italy, Spain, and Japan were forced to disband. Despite the risks, many continued to meet informally and following the wars end, Rotary members joined together to rebuild their clubs and their countries.
           </p>
           <p>
            Our commitment to service is ongoing. We began our fight against polio in 1979 with a project to immunize 6 million children in the Philippines. By 2012, only three countries remain polio-endemicdown from 125 in 1988.
           </p>
