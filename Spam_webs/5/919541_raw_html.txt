     <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-7 col-sm-9 col-xs-12 post_content">
      <p>
       Without
       <a href="http://www.chartattack.com/tag/bb_king/" target="_blank">
        B.B. King
       </a>
       , modern music landscape would scarcely exist. The King of the Blues, born Riley B. King, died Thursday at the age of 89. His expressive style broke racial boundaries, brought bluesinto the households of millions of Americans and onto the global stage, shaped modern rock &amp; roll and revolutionized an expressive new way of playing the guitar.
      </p>
      <p>
       But rather than mourn him, we're celebrating the legacy of the blues legend. Here are six reasons B.B. King was cool.
      </p>
      <h4>
       Hebrought the blues to a world stage
      </h4>
      <div class="lyte-wrapper" style="width:640px;max-width: 100%;margin:5px;">

      <div class="lL" style="width:640px;margin:5px;">
      </div>
      <p>
       B.B. King spoke often about his goal to bring respectability to the blues, a genre that, when he was starting out, was often disparaged as "the devil's music" and "the music of the ghetto." Of course, that's all extremely racially charged, and King knew it. But his music, starting with his first
       <em>
        Billboard
       </em>
       charting single "3 O'Clock Blues," smashed through the musical segregation of the '50s.
      </p>
      <p>
       The racists couldnt legislate musical taste, King once said, as noted in
       <em>
        Race, Rock and Elvis
       </em>
       (via
       <em>
        <a href="http://www.etonline.com/news/164576_the_reign_of_bb_king_obituary/" target="_blank">
         Entertainment Weekly
        </a>
       </em>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       B.B. King's music was so popular that it, for better or for worse, made its way into the repertoire of the
       <a href="http://www.chartattack.com/tag/the_beatles/" target="_blank">
        Beatles
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="http://www.chartattack.com/tag/the_rolling_stones/" target="_blank">
        Stones
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.chartattack.com/tag/eric_clapton/" target="_blank">
        Clapton
       </a>
       , leading to the big ol' stew of appropriation that became rock &amp; roll.
      </p>

       Heplayedhis emotions
      </h4>
      <div class="lyte-wrapper" style="width:640px;max-width: 100%;margin:5px;">

      <p>
       I wanted to connect my guitar to human emotions, King said in his autobiography,
       <em>
        <a href="http://www.amazon.ca/Blues-All-Around-Me-Autobiography/dp/0062061038" target="_blank">
         Blues All Around Me
        </a>
       </em>
       (1996), written with David Ritz. His expressive, vibrato-filled guitar looked like an extension of his face - you could see thesadness, yearning, passion in his eyesas much as you could hear it wrought from his strings. He was a talented singer and lyricist, too, but his playing spoke much louder than his words, as you can see from performances of songs like his signature "The Thrill Is Gone."
      </p>
      <h4>
       He never stopped playing
      </h4>
      <p>
       <a href="http://cdn.chartattack.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/BBKing_02092010_7670.jpg">
        <img alt="BBKing_02092010_7670" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-160785" height="447" src="http://cdn.chartattack.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/BBKing_02092010_7670.jpg" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1956
       <a href="http://mobile.nytimes.com/2015/05/16/arts/music/b-b-king-blues-singer-dies-at-89.html" target="_blank">
        he played
       </a>
       342 shows and even in his later years kept a schedule that would test the endurance of musicians half his age. Until a recent diabetes-related illness BB would perform 200 to 300 shows per year. In 2014, the most shows an artist played
       <a href="http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/the_10_bands_who_played_the_most_shows_in_2014" target="_blank">
        was 206
       </a>
       . Sorry bands, you gotta try a little harder than that.
      </p>
      <h4>
       He ran into a burning building to save his guitar
      </h4>

        <meta content="2008-11-02T23:57:49.000Z" itemprop="uploadDate"/>
        <div class="pL" data-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/-Y8QxOjuYHg/hqdefault.jpg" id="lyte_-Y8QxOjuYHg">

      <p>
       <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-Y8QxOjuYHg" target="_blank">
        "Lucille"
       </a>
       is an ode to BB's Gibson ES-355 guitar, which he named Lucille. It's not just a cute nickname. It's actually pretty badass. King often
       <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EGsvAMRFivo" target="_blank">
        told the story
       </a>
       of howband used to play at a nightclub every week, and in the winter when it was too cold, they built a DIY heater. One night a fight broke out over a woman named Lucille and the heater was knocked over,igniting the wood building. Everyone rushed out, including B.B. King, only to realize his precious guitar was in the building. Andwhat do you do when a building is engulfed in flames and crumbling? You run back inside and get your guitar, of course. (He named his guitars Lucille to remind himself never to do something so stupid again.)
      </p>
