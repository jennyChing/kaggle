    <h1>
     Tips for Cooking for One or Two People
    </h1>
    <p>
     Cooking for one or two without wasting food can be challenge. You'll need to either learn to pare down recipes or use your freezer so you don't get bored eating the same thing too many times in a row.
    </p>
    <h2>
     Use your Freezer to Save Half for Another Day
    </h2>
    <p>
     Prepare a standard recipe for a casserole, like lasagna, but make it in two 8x8 pans rather than one 9x13. Line one with foil before preparing, then freeze. Once it's frozen, take it out of your pan (the foil makes it easy) and slip into a freezer bag to cook another day.
    </p>
    <p>
     Recipes like
     <a href="../Recipes/chicken-pot-pie.htm">
      chicken pot pie
     </a>
     can be treated the same way. Just freeze half the filling and make a smaller pot pie.
    </p>
    <h2>
     Use up your Leftovers with Soup
    </h2>
    <p>
     Take bits of leftover vegetables and create a soup container in your freezer. When you have 3-4 cups, depending on how much soup you want to make, make a nice vegetable soup. You can do this with more than plain vegetables. One of the best soups I ever made included some leftover scalloped potatoes. Another great one had leftover stuffing in it. There are all kinds of creative ways to
     <a href="leftovers-soup.htm">
      use up leftovers for soup
     </a>
     or
     <a href="../cooking-with-leftovers.htm">
      other dishes
     </a>
     .
    </p>
    <h2>
     Wrap up Some Burritos
    </h2>
    <p>
     Use shredded cooked chicken or beef or leftover
     <a href="../Recipes/pork-carnitas.htm">
      pork carnitas
     </a>
     or
     <a href="../Recipes/pork-chili-verde.htm">
      chili verde
     </a>
     to make burritos. Put about 1/4 cup of meat, some sauteed onions and peppers if you like, and shredded cheese in the middle of flour tortillas. Wrap up burrito style, then wrap in foil. Place all the burritos in one gallon sized freezer bag. Bake frozen at 350 for 45 minutes to an hour or thaw and bake 25-30 minutes.
    </p>
    <h2>
     Make Mini Pizzas
    </h2>
    <p>
     Instead of making a large pizza, you can easily just shape your dough into smaller personal pizzas. Freeze the leftover pizza dough for another night. Or make your pizza on English muffins or pita bread and freeze the extra sauce in 1 cup portions.
    </p>
    <h2>
     Don't Forget the Side Dishes
    </h2>
    <p>
     Make half a dozen
     <strong>
      twice baked potatoes
     </strong>
     and freeze the extras for another day.
    </p>
    <p>
     Make a loaf of
     <strong>
      garlic bread
     </strong>
     , then slice into portions that suit your needs. Wrap each portion in foil then place in freezer bags. Just thaw and broil for some quick and tasty garlic bread.
    </p>
    <h2>
     Take Advantage of Ice Cube Trays
    </h2>
    <p>
     Did you need just a few tablespoons of
     <strong>
      tomato paste
     </strong>
     ? Freeze the rest by putting spoonfuls onto waxed paper and then freezing or putting a tablespoon in the containers of an ice cube tray then freezing. Pop them out when frozen and put in a freezer bag or container.
    </p>
    <p>
     Use your ice cube trays to freeze small portions of lemon juice,
     <strong>
      chicken broth
     </strong>
     , and so forth.
    </p>
    <h2>
     Use Muffin Tins for Portions
    </h2>
    <p>
     Muffin tins are another useful asset. Mix up a
     <a href="simple-meatloaf-recipes.htm">
      meatloaf recipe
     </a>
     but package it into muffin tins rather than a loaf pan.
    </p>
    <p>
     Freeze
     <strong>
      leftover stuffing
     </strong>
     in muffin tins, then reheat as needed.
    </p>
    <h2>
     Dealing with Larger Packages
    </h2>
    <p>
     Repackage things like
     <strong>
      hamburger buns or rolls
     </strong>
     into small portions and freeze. (If you just throw the whole package in the freezer they usually stick together.)
    </p>
    <p>
     Separate
     <strong>
      bacon slices
     </strong>
     into a portion that works for you and freeze. Or cook up a whole pound and freeze the cooked bacon.
    </p>
