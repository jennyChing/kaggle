       <h1>
        Stock Position Size Calculator
       </h1>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="wsite-spacer" style="height:10px;">
     </div>
     <span class="imgPusher" style="float:right;height:0px">
     </span>
     <span style="display: table;z-index:10;width:503px;position:relative;float:right;max-width:100%;;clear:right;margin-top:0px;*margin-top:0px">
      <a>
       <img alt="Stock position size calculator" class="galleryImageBorder wsite-image" src="/uploads/2/2/6/4/22648924/8196476.png?488" style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 5px; margin-left: 0px; margin-right: 15px; border-width:0; max-width:100%"/>
      </a>
      <span class="wsite-caption" style="display: table-caption; caption-side: bottom; font-size: 90%; margin-top: -5px; margin-bottom: 5px; text-align: center;">
       Calculate the size of your stock positions based on the amount you are willing to risk
      </span>
     </span>
     <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;display:block;">
      <font size="3">
       This stock position size calculator is designed to help you manage the risks to your portfolio by calculating the number of shares you can buy based on your riskappetiteand stop-losses. Information that you can input includes:
       <br/>
      </font>
      <ul style="">
       <li style="">
        <font size="3">
         Your overall portfolio value
        </font>
       </li>
       <li style="">
        <font size="3">
         The percentage or amount of your portfolio that you are comfortable risking
        </font>
       </li>
       <li style="">
        <span style="font-size: medium; background-color: initial;">
         The stock ticker
        </span>
        <br/>
       </li>
       <li style="">
        <font size="3">
         The current stock price
        </font>
       </li>
       <li style="">
        <font size="3">
         Whether you are buying (long) or borrowing (short) the stocks
        </font>
       </li>
       <li style="">
        <span style="font-size: medium; background-color: initial;">
         Your desired stop-loss amount or percentage
         <br/>
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <br/>
      <span style="font-size: medium; background-color: initial;">
       The calculator will then work out:
      </span>
      <br/>
      <ul style="">
       <li style="">
        <font size="3">
         The number of stocks that you can purchase
        </font>
       </li>
       <li style="">
        <font size="3">
         The value of your purchased shares
        </font>
       </li>
       <li style="">
        <font size="3">
         The stop-loss amount and percent
        </font>
       </li>
       <li style="">
        <font size="3">
         The potential loss on each stock and on the position as a whole if the stop-loss is triggered
        </font>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <br/>
      <font size="3">
       You can calculate position sizes for both purchased (long) positions and borrowed (short) positions and experiment with your overall portfolio risk and the risk on individual positions - The calculator will update dynamically as you make changes.
      </font>
      <font size="3">
       Once you have worked out your risk, you can then place an order for stocks with your broker, together with a stop-loss order. You can also email the information to yourself if you want to use it later.
       <br/>
      </font>
      <font size="3">
       <br/>
       Below, you'll find the calculator itself, details on how it works, why you might want to use position sizing and further information. If you would prefer to just work out your stop-loss prices, you can find a calculator that will do exactly that
       <a href="http://www.prospersee.com/stop-loss-risk-calculator.html" title="">
        here
       </a>
       .

     <h2 class="wsite-content-title" style="text-align:left;">
      Using the calculator
     </h2>
     <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
      <ol style="">
       <li style="">
        <em style="">
         Enter the total portfolio value for the portfolio that you are going to place this stock into; the calculator is 'currency agnostic', so you can use any currency you wish
        </em>
       </li>
       <li style="">
        <em style="">
         Enter the amount or percentage of your portfolio that you can manage to lose; this will determine how many stocks you can buy, based on the stop-loss price that you will set later. Some investors say that you should never risk more than 2% of your portfolio in a single trade and more risk-averse investors may set this at 0.5% or lower; set whether you are specifying an amount or a percentage using the radio buttons
        </em>
       </li>
       <li>
        <em>
         Enter the stock ticker name or some other way to identify the position
        </em>
       </li>
       <li>

     <h2 class="wsite-content-title" style="text-align:left;">
      What is position sizing and how does it work?
     </h2>
     <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
      <font size="3">
       <a href="http://www.investopedia.com/articles/trading/09/determine-position-size.asp" target="_blank">
        Position sizing
       </a>
       is one of the most important principles that allows traders and investors to make money. It accomplishes this through minimizing the losses from a trade when the stock or share price falls. It works as follows:
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          A trader or investor decides how much of their portfolio they are prepared to risk on a specific trade; this is the amount that they stand to lose if the stock price falls
         </span>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          This loss can be decided as a specific amount, or as a percentage of the overall portfolio value (speculative traders might risk as much as 3% to 5% on a trade, while more risk averse investors might use a maximum of 0.5% or less)
         </span>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          They use the share price that they want to purchase at together with a desired stop-loss amount or percentage; the stop-loss is a type of order that is used when a share price falls to a certain amount, selling the position and limiting the amount of money that is lost
         </span>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          The combination of the overall amount at risk, the stop-loss price and the share price determines how many stocks should be purchased to keep the risk within the riskappetiteofthe trader or investor
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          When stocks are purchased (long) or borrowed (short) the investor should also place a stop-loss order at the appropriate amount to protect their investment
         </span>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <br/>
       <a href="http://www.vantharp.com/tharp-concepts/position-sizing.asp" target="_blank">
        Position sizing
       </a>
       is important because it
       <a href="http://stansberryresearch.com/investor-education/position-sizing/" target="_blank">
        limits your maximum losses
       </a>
       . Successful trading isn't just about making the most money on a profitable trade, it's also about reducing your potential losses on the unprofitable ones.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       A consistent discipline and approach to setting effective stop-loss orders and limiting the amount you risk is essential to making money when trading or investing in the stock market.
       <br/>
      </font>
