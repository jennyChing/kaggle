        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.infiniteunknown.net/2008/05/13/the-only-win-win-investment-i-know-of/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to The Only Win-Win Investment I know of ">
          The Only Win-Win Investment I know of 
         </a>
        </h2>
        <!-- Go to www.addthis.com/dashboard to customize your tools -->
        <div class="addthis_native_toolbox">
        </div>
        <script src="//s7.addthis.com/js/300/addthis_widget.js#pubid=oilreg" type="text/javascript">
        </script>

       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Golds precipitous tumble from its record-high of $1,038 set on March 17 down to the recent $850 level has lots of people asking, Is golds bull market over?
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          My answer: No! Not by a long shot!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          I know youve been getting an earful from the talking-head ninnies about how the long-running commodity bull is getting short of breath and is about to be put out to pasture. Ignore them.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Other than a pullback here and there, gold  and virtually all natural resource prices  are headed much higher in the months and years ahead.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1116" height="135" src="http://www.infiniteunknown.net/wp-content/uploads/2008/05/gold.jpg" title="gold" width="180"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; color: #0000ff;">
           My next target for gold: $1,250 an ounce. Then, its inflation-adjusted high of at least $2,270.
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Those numbers should come as no surprise. Ive mentioned them several times before. Why I am I so bullish?
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Im not going to get into all the arguments that clearly spell higher prices for gold. Youve heard them from me time-and-time again.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Instead, Im going to point out something youve probably never thought about when it comes to gold. Heck, even the smartest guys on Wall Street havent figured it out yet 
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; color: #0000ff;">
           <em>
            Gold Is In A Win-Win Situation!
           </em>
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
           Consider the following two possible macroeconomic background scenarios for gold:
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span id="more-1115">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           Scenario #1: Feds Efforts to Save the U.S. Economy and Financial System Succeed  Credit Crisis Eases.
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Under this scenario, the Feds recent actions of slashing interest rates and pumping money into the economy are successful  the U.S. economy recovers and global growth resumes.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          As a result, the credit crunch eases, and money flows through the pipeline. The big commercial and investment banks finally stop taking massive write-downs on bad mortgage securities. Foreclosures shrink, home prices stop hemorrhaging, and home sales pick up. Businesses start hiring and consumers resume spending. Life is good again.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          In this scenario, many talking heads on Wall Street say that golds bull market would be over. Theyd point out that there would be no reason for investors to flock to the safety of gold.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          But holy cow, theyd be dead wrong! Why? Because 
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           The Fed is
           <em>
            GOOD
           </em>
           for Gold!
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          If the Fed is successful at turning the U.S. economy and credit crisis around,
          <em>
           it will only be because it flooded the system with hundreds of billions of paper dollars, creating wild inflation.
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          If the economy were to pick up on top of that, between inflation and resumed economic growth, global demand for gold would soar.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Of course, the media mavens would then argue that when the economy turns back up, the Fed will jump in with both feet to head off inflation by aggressively raising interest rates, choking off the bull market in gold.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Thats also hogwash. In fact, look at the recent record. From late 2004 to mid-2006, the Fed raised interest rates 17 times, in steady quarter-point increments to 5.25% from a low of 1%. And over that period, gold surged 127%!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Look at it this way: Even with the U.S. economy in a bad funk  gold demand hit a record $79 billion in 2007.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Most of the increased demand  no surprise here  came from Asia and the Middle East. Demand from No. 1 gold customer India rose 7%.
         </span>
        </p>
        <table align="right" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="margin: 0px 0px 15px 20px;" width="275">
         <tbody>
          <tr>
           <td style="padding: 5px; background-color: #dddddd;">
            <img alt="China is home to the second-largest jewelry market in the world today." height="199" src="http://images.moneyandmarkets.com/935/china-jewelry.jpg" width="275"/>
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
           <td>
            <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; color: #990000; font-size: x-small;">
             <strong>
              <em>
               China is home to the second-largest jewelry market in the world today.
              </em>
             </strong>
            </span>
           </td>
          </tr>
         </tbody>
        </table>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Chinas gold demand jumped 26%, catapulting it to the second-largest gold jewelry market in the world  and knocking the U.S. out of the No. 2 slot.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Other Asian and Middle East countries are seeing rising gold demand as well: Hong Kong  up 15%  Saudi Arabia  up 15%  Egypt  up 12%  UAE  up 7% (a new annual record)  Russia  up 11% (a new annual record).
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          So if the Feds actions work  the U.S. economy rebounds riding a new wave of inflation  the mortgage crisis subsides: Demand for gold will fly off the charts, sending the yellow metals price skyward.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Now lets take a look at the flip side.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           Scenario #2: Fed Rescue Efforts Fail  Credit Crisis Worsens  Recession Deepens.
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Bearish for gold? No way, Jose! The Fed will just keep pumping trillions more dollars into the economy.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          The greenback will continue its long-term decline, dramatically losing even more purchasing power than it already has, and inflation will go through the roof!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Meanwhile, Asian and other emerging economies continue to chug along just as they have been doing for the past six months.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Do you think golds bull market would end in such an environment? Do you think it would be bearish for gold? Hardly!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          The plunging dollar would cause investors to flock to gold even more than they already have been. The price of the yellow metal would soar even more than in the first scenario above, and would likely even exceed my most bullish target, rising well above $2,270 an ounce!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           Plus, in Either Scenario 
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           The Supply of Gold Is Plunging!
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Many analysts are claiming that in either of the above scenarios, golds current price is high enough to bring oodles of new supply to the market, hence killing, or at least smothering the bull market for a while.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          Give me a break! These are the same analysts who have been claiming the price of oil was going to top out at $100 a barrel  and that its heading back to $50!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          In fact, when I compare the supply/demand forces in gold to the same fundamentals in oil, my analysis tells me the supply situation in gold is far worse 
         </span>
        </p>

           <td style="padding: 5px; background-color: #dddddd;">
            <img alt="Gold mine production hit an 11-year low of 2,447 metric tonnes in 2007." height="184" src="http://images.moneyandmarkets.com/935/gold-nugget-worth-20k.jpg" width="275"/>
           </td>

          <tr>
           <td>
            <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; color: #990000; font-size: x-small;">
             <strong>
              <em>
               Gold mine production hit an 11-year low of 2,447 metric tonnes in 2007.
              </em>
             </strong>
            </span>
           </td>
          </tr>
         </tbody>
        </table>
        <ul type="disc">
         <li>
          <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
           In 2007, the worlds gold supply dropped 3% to 110.7 million ounces of the yellow metal.
           <p>
           </p>
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
           Gold mine production hit an 11-year low of 2,447 metric tonnes in 2007. Africa had the most precipitous drop of 29 metric tonnes while Latin America saw a 23 metric ton decline in output.
           <p>
           </p>
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
           South Africa, once the worlds largest gold producer, is now producing its lowest amount in 86 years! And its getting worse.
           <em>
            In February alone, South Africas gold production plummeted 28.2%.
           </em>
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          So let me set the record straight on the supply situation in gold: Do not, I repeat, do NOT expect any relief from new supplies of gold.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          <strong>

           Is Alive and Well! Buy the Dips!
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>

          I have absolutely no doubt whatsoever that gold will ultimately reach at least $2,270 an ounce, and perhaps even higher.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          So any time you hear that golds bull market is over, I strongly suggest you ignore those sound bites, and instead, use virtually every price dip as an opportunity to buy.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          As I just showed you, gold is in a win-win situation  and its the only investment I know of in todays markets where its truly heads you win, tails you also win.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          With that in mind, and given golds current pullback and much needed rest, I want to make ABSOLUTELY sure youve got the basics you need to have gold in your portfolio 
         </span>
        </p>

        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
          <strong>
