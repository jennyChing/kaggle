                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     A Surfeit of Lampreys
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4516223082222463030" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
                      On this day in 1135 King Henry I died at the ripe old age of
66 (good innings for a medieval monarch). The youngest son of William the
Conqueror, Henry had succeeded his elder brother William Rufus in 1100. A
contemporary chronicler, Henry of Huntingdon, describes how the king met his
end:
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 10pt;">
                      <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
                       He had been hunting, and when he came back to Saint-Denis
in the Forest of Lyons [Lyons-la-Foret, Normandy], he ate the flesh of
lampreys, which always made him ill, though he always loved them. When a doctor
forbade him to eat the dish, the king did not take this salutary advice. As it
is said, We always strive for what is forbidden and long for what is refused.
So this meal brought on a most destructive humour, and violently stimulated
similar symptoms, producing a deadly chill in his aged body, and a sudden and
extreme convulsion. Against this, nature reacted by stirring up an acute fever
to dissolve the inflammation with very heavy sweating. But when all power of
resistance failed, the great king departed on the first day of December [1135],
when he had reigned for thirty-five years and three months.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 10pt;">
                      <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
                       Henry of Huntingdon,
                       <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                        The
History of the English People, 1000-1154
                       </i>
                       (OUP, 2002), p. 64.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-A3VwkW4ba5o/UprtL6QTAnI/AAAAAAAAA6s/qUkSui6jKww/s1600/lampreytub2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="272" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-A3VwkW4ba5o/UprtL6QTAnI/AAAAAAAAA6s/qUkSui6jKww/s400/lampreytub2.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 10pt; text-align: center;">
                      <em>
                       The trouble is they taste too good.
                      </em>
                     </div>
