  <center>
   <h1>
    20 Words That Should Exist
   </h1>
  </center>
  <p>
   1.
   <b>
    ACCORDIONATED
   </b>
   <i>
    (ah kor' de on ay tid)
   </i>
   adj.
   <br/>
   Being able to drive and re fold a road map at the same time.
  </p>
  <p>
   2.
   <b>
    AQUADEXTROUS
   </b>
   <i>
    (ak wa deks' trus)
   </i>
   adj.
   <br/>
   Possessing the ability to turn the bathtub faucet on and off with your  
toes.
  </p>
  <p>
   3.
   <b>
    AQUALIBRIUM
   </b>
   <i>
    (ak wa bib' re um)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   The point where the stream of drinking fountain water is at its 
perfect height, thus relieving the drinker from (a) having to suck 
the nozzle, or   (b)squirting himself in the eye (or ear).
  </p>
  <p>
   4.
   <b>
    BURGACIDE
   </b>
   <i>
    (burg' uh side)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   When a hamburger can't take any more torture and hurls itself through  
he grill into the coals.
  </p>
  <p>
   5.
   <b>
    BUZZACKS
   </b>
   <i>
    (buz' aks)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   People in phone marts who walk around picking up display phones and 
listening for dial tones even when they know the phones are not   
connected.
  </p>
  <p>
   6.
   <b>
    CARPERPETUATION
   </b>
   <i>
    (kar' pur pet u a shun)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   The act, when vacuuming, of running over a string or a piece of lint at 
least a dozen times, reaching over and picking it up, examining it, then
putting it back down to give the vacuum one more chance.
  </p>
  <p>
   7.
   <b>
    DIMP
   </b>
   <i>
    (dimp)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   A person who insults you in a cheap department store by asking, "Do you
work here?"
  </p>
  <p>
   8.
   <b>
    DISCONFECT
   </b>
   <i>
    (dis kon fect')
   </i>
   v.
   <br/>
   To sterilize the piece of candy you dropped on the floor by blowing on  
it, somehow assuming this will remove all the germs.
  </p>
  <p>
   9.
   <b>
    ECNALUBMA
   </b>
   <i>
    (ek na leb' ma)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   A rescue vehicle which can only be seen in the rear view mirror.
  </p>
  <p>
   10.
   <b>
    EIFFELITES
   </b>
   <i>
    (eye' ful eyetz)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   Gangly people sitting in front of you at the movies who, no matter which
direction you lean in, follow suit.
  </p>
  <p>
   11.
   <b>
    ELBONICS
   </b>
   <i>
    (el bon icks')
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   The actions of two people maneuvering for one armrest in a movie   
theater.
  </p>
  <p>
   12.
   <b>
    ELECELLERATON
   </b>
   <i>
    (el a cel er ay' shun)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   The mistaken notion that the more you press an elevator button the
faster it will arrive.
  </p>
  <p>
   13.
   <b>
    FRUST
   </b>
   <i>
    (frust)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   The small line of debris that refuses to be swept onto the dust pan and
keeps backing a person across the room until he finally decides to give up and
sweep it under the rug.
  </p>
  <p>
   14.
   <b>
    LACTOMANGULATION
   </b>
   <i>
    (lak to man gyu lay' shun)
   </i>
   v.
   <br/>
   Manhandling the "open here" spout on a milk container so badly that one 
 has to resort to the illegal side.
  </p>
  <p>
   15.
   <b>
    NEONPHANCY
   </b>
   <i>
    (ne on' fan see)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   A fluorescent light bulb struggling to come to life.
  </p>
  <p>
   16.
   <b>
    PEPPIER
   </b>
   <i>
    (pehp ee ay')
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   The waiter at a fancy restaurant whose sole purpose seems to be walking 
around asking diners if they want ground pepper.
  </p>
  <p>
   17.
   <b>
    PETROPHOBIC
   </b>
   <i>
    (pet ro fob' ik)
   </i>
   adj.
   <br/>
   One who is embarrassed to undress in front of a household pet.
  </p>
  <p>
   18.
   <b>
    PHONESIA
   </b>
   <i>
    (fo nee' zhuh)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   The affliction of dialing a phone number and forgetting whom you were 
calling just as they answer.
  </p>
  <p>
   19.
   <b>
    PUPKUS
   </b>
   <i>
    (pup kus')
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   The moist residue left on a window after a dog presses it nose to it.
  </p>
  <p>
   20.
   <b>
    TELECRASTINATION
   </b>
   <i>
    (tel e kras tin ay' shun)
   </i>
   n.
   <br/>
   The act of always letting the phone ring at least twice before you pick
it up, when you're only six inches away.
  </p>
