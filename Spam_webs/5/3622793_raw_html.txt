           Upon arrival I check in at a local guesthouse with an Albanian family. The woman is very friendly and tells me that, in the beginning, neighbours were pretty reluctant about her opening her house for strangers, but once they saw that money was coming in, they abandoned their principles pretty quickly and some of them even started to follow her example now!
           <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" data-jpibfi-description="" src="http://www.myfeetaremeanttoroam.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>

           <p>
            Gjirokastra reminds me of
            <a href="http://www.myfeetaremeanttoroam.com/helping-out-in-a-hostel-in-berat-a-unesco-heritage-site/">
             Berat
            </a>
            : the houses are built in Ottoman style and the town has been recognized by Unesco as well. The streets are pretty quiet and I enjoy a beer and a chat with two Albanian men in a strange mixture of French, Italian and Albanian. They invite me to have a meal and some raki with their family, but since communication is pretty limited, I decline politely. Instead I take the climb up to the well-preserved castle and have a quiet meal in one of Gjirokastras restaurants.
