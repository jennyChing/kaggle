       <a href="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-trends/the-10-most-innovative-uses-for-solar-cells/" rel="bookmark">
        10 Innovative Uses for Solar Power Technology
       </a>
      </h1>
     </div>
     <div class="byline">
      by Dave Llorens
     </div>
     <div class="entry-content">
      <p class="no-break">
       <span class="st_email_large" st_title="10 Innovative Uses for Solar Power Technology" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-trends/the-10-most-innovative-uses-for-solar-cells/">
       </span>
       <span class="st_facebook_large" st_title="10 Innovative Uses for Solar Power Technology" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-trends/the-10-most-innovative-uses-for-solar-cells/">
       </span>
       <span class="st_googleplus_large" st_title="10 Innovative Uses for Solar Power Technology" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-trends/the-10-most-innovative-uses-for-solar-cells/">
       </span>
       <span class="st_twitter_large" st_title="10 Innovative Uses for Solar Power Technology" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-trends/the-10-most-innovative-uses-for-solar-cells/" st_username="solarpowerrocks">
       </span>
       <span class="st_linkedin_large" st_title="10 Innovative Uses for Solar Power Technology" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-trends/the-10-most-innovative-uses-for-solar-cells/">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       I thought Id do a little sniffing around this evening and compile some of the most interesting and innovative uses for solar power technology to date. If you have more or others, please submit them!
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #10  Bolivias Solar Power Project
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="bolivia solar power project" class="alignleft" src="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/09/bolivia-solar-power-project.jpg" style="width: 400px;height: auto;"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Talk about power
       <em>
        off the grid
       </em>
       , this installation project in a remote area of Bolivia is funded by a local NGO and installed by British undergraduate students.
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #9  Solar Powered Sedan
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="solar powered sedan" class="alignleft" src="http://i48.photobucket.com/albums/f223/leafjumper/priussolar.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: auto;" title="solar powered sedan"/>
       How about a solar powered sedan? Canadian engineer Steve Lapp managed to eke 10% more fuel economy out of this Prius.
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #8  Solar Powered Carport Deck
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="solar power corport deck" class="alignleft" src="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/solar-carport-deck.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: auto;"/>
       Ahh.. solar by boat! From the project engineer, hobbyist, and boat owner: Most of the time the system works faultlessly, churning out the wattage needed to power everything I needI almost envy the ignorance of authorities and folk who dont see that renewable energy is worth the implementation costs.
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #7  Solar Power iPad Charger
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="solar power ipad charger" class="alignleft" src="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/solar-power-ipad-charger.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: auto;" title="solar power ipad charger"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       When I first saw this, I thought someone must be screwing with me. But when I considered how useful something like this vest might be, I thought this was a pretty cool idea. I mean, imagine youre on some kind of hike somewhere, got lost and needed to rely on all of your Survivorman skills to get back to humanity. Even if you turn the wrong way, get deeper into nowhere, and were on the verge of death youve got a fully functional iPod.
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #6  Google Solar Power Power Project
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="Google solar power project" class="alignleft" src="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/09/Google-solar-power-project.jpg" style="width: 400px;height: auto;"/>
       Google is in the processes of outfitting solar panels on the rooftops of the entire Googleplex. If you havent seen the scope of this project, check it out
       <a href="http://www.eispv.com">
        here
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       According to Google, the installation is projected to produce enough electricity for approximately 1,000 California homes or 30% of Googles peak electricity demand.
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #5  Solar Powered Technology used in Windows
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="solar powered windows" class="alignleft" src="http://www.worldchanging.com/Sphelar-main1.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: auto;" title="solar powered windows"/>
       Solar powered windows. What? A Japanese firm has created technology which enables the harness of solar energy from multiple directions, not merely from a flat surface. They call it Sphelar. Imagine a solar dome like the one pictured below finding its way into Green architecture. Sweet!
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="solar powered windows" class="alignleft" src="http://www.worldchanging.com/Sphelar-domemodule1.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: auto;" title="solar powered windows"/>
       Also. this thing.
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #4  Solar Fountains!
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="solar powered windows" class="alignleft" src="http://images.simplyfountains.com/mgen/master:ST039.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: auto;" title="solar powered windows"/>
       Sometimes the best ideas really are the most simple. Solar fountain! While fountains have been aesthetically pleasing all of us for quite some time, powering those pumps can take a slow but steady toll on the planet. Throw an efficient solar cell in the middle and presto! A self sustaining water-thrower. Maybe if Google ran the Bellagio, you might see a similar setup out front.
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #3  solar powered USB cell phone chargers
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="solar powered usb cell phone charger" class="alignleft" src="http://www.coolest-gadgets.com/wp-content/uploads/2006/08/scottybig.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: auto;" title="solar powered usb cell phone charger"/>
       Ok, screw the vest. If you really want to save yourself in a pinch, you might want to consider one of these solar powered USB cell phone chargers.
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #2  Solar powered LED umbrella
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="solar powered usb cell phone charger" class="alignleft" src="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/solar-powered-led-umbrella.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: auto;" title="solar powered usb cell phone charger"/>
       Remember how simple is best? Well ok. Solar powered LED umbrella. The cells charge up during the day and at night you have high-powered LED patio lighting. I wouldnt be surprised to see these popping up on ritzy homes and beachfront bistros in the near future. Nice.
      </p>
      <h2 class="clear break">
       #1  Suspended Solar Power Plants in Space
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="Suspended Solar Power Plants in Space" class="alignleft" src="http://i48.photobucket.com/albums/f223/leafjumper/space_solar.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: auto;" title="solar powered usb cell phone charger"/>
       Ok space fanatics, US government researchers have ideas for energy generation via these suspended solar power plants. Feasible? Umm. Maybe? Will this ever happen? Maybe not, but heres the justification: Sunlight is eight times less intense on the earths surface than in its geostationary orbit. So why not collect it in space and beam its energy to Earth via microwave power beam, which can penetrate the atmosphere more efficiently? They have proposed putting in orbit mega-satellites  giant, possibly inflatable structures of photovoltaic arrays and antennas  that would do just that. At receiving stations on Earth, the beam could be converted into electricity or synthetic fuels, which, in contrast to power from terrestrial solar power stations, would flow continuously to the grid independent of the season, weather or location.  US State Department Solar Energy Research
      </p>
      <p>
       What about Japan? Theyve already got concrete plans: Japan plans to launch a solar satellite by 2040, which will deliver energy to the earth via low-intensity microwaves. The satellite will absorb the suns energy 24 hours a day, unobstructed by clouds, and generate one million kilowatts per second  equal to the output of a nuclear power plant.  Worldchanging.com
      </p>
