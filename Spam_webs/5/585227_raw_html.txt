       <h3 class="slide-title">
        Ranking the Nation's Top 25 College Football Stadiums (Click Arrow to begin)
       </h3>
       <div class="slide-body" style="display: none;">
        <div>
         Fall Saturdays are special.
        </div>
        <div>
        </div>
        <div>
         Small towns, huge crowds, tailgating, bands, cheerleaders and student sections are just a few of the reasons college football is the best sport on the planet. When campuses jump to life across the nation each weekend in the fall, college stadiums become a staging ground for history.
        </div>
        <div>
        </div>
        <div>
         There is a variety of ways to evaluate the greatness of a stadium. Huge attendance numbers, home-field advantage in the win-loss column, ear-piercing decibels, rich traditions, picturesque landscapes and amenities are just a few of the aspects that must be considered to rank so many great college football cathedrals.
        </div>
        <div>
        </div>
        <div>
         With that in mind, here are the top 25 stadiums to watch a game this fall: (click arrow to begin)
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
     </li>
     <li class="slide1">
      <div class="background" data-track-event="">
      </div>
      <div class="slide-content">
       <h3 class="slide-title">
        25. Doak Campbell Stadium, Florida State
       </h3>
       <div class="slide-body" style="display: none;">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Opened:
         </strong>
         1950
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Capacity:
         </strong>
         82,300
         <br/>
         <strong>
          2012 Attendance:
         </strong>
         75,601 (19th)
         <br/>
         Named after former Florida State president Doak S. Campbell, The Doak is known for its vicious football teams and gorgeous student section. It was renamed in 2004 as Bobby Bowden Field, and a nine-foot statue and three-story stained-glass window of the legendary coach were added to the facilities. The Noles are 260-86-4 all-time in the largest stadium in the ACC and, interestingly enough, the gorgeous brick faade makes DCS the largest continuous brick building in the United States. The cowgirls go crazy when Chief Osceola rides in on his steed and plants his flaming spear into the 50-yard line before each home game.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
     </li>
     <li class="slide2">
      <div class="background" data-track-event="">
      </div>
      <div class="slide-content">
       <h3 class="slide-title">
        24. Vaught-Hemingway Stadium, Ole Miss
       </h3>
       <div class="slide-body" style="display: none;">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Opened:
         </strong>
         1915
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Capacity:
         </strong>
         60,580
         <br/>
         <strong>
          2012 Attendance:
         </strong>
         57,066 (30th)
         <br/>
         Can Ole Miss figure out a way to move The Grove inside Vaught-Hemingway? The worlds greatest pre-game tailgate takes place just a few yards away from the comparably small, but no less enjoyable, stadium. So while the third-oldest venue in the SEC hasnt been all that daunting to opposing teams over the years, it does house what may be the most beautiful fan base in the nation. Everyone should attend at least one tailgate in Oxford, Miss.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
     </li>
     <li class="slide3">
      <div class="background" data-track-event="">
      </div>
      <div class="slide-content">
       <h3 class="slide-title">
        23. Williams-Brice Stadium, South Carolina
       </h3>
       <div class="slide-body" style="display: none;">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Opened:
         </strong>
         1934
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Capacity:
         </strong>
         80,250
         <br/>
         <strong>
          2012 Attendance:
         </strong>
         80,001 (18th)
         <br/>
         Recent upgrades to the tailgating areas and stadium itself have elevated Williams-Brice into the upper echelon of SEC venues. The Cock Pit has signature lighting high above the upper deck on either side of the field as the school continues to break attendance records  85,199 in 2012 against Georgia. Each home game begins with the playing of the theme from "2001: A Space Odyssey," giving South Carolina one of the best pre-game reputations in the nation. Formerly known as Carolina Stadium, the stadium saw its name changed in 1972 to Williams-Brice after university benefactor Martha Williams-Brice. Steve Spurrier has built WBS into one of the impossible places for visitors to win, posting a 25-3 mark at home over the last four seasons.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
     </li>
     <li class="slide4">
      <div class="background" data-track-event="">
      </div>
      <div class="slide-content">
       <h3 class="slide-title">
        22. Los Angeles Coliseum, USC
       </h3>
       <div class="slide-body" style="display: none;">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Opened:
         </strong>
         1923
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Capacity:
         </strong>
         93,607
         <br/>
         <strong>
          2012 Attendance:
         </strong>
