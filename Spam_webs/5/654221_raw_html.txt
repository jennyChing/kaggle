        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         GREEN Lawn Care for CHEAP! The secret of using sugar on your lawn.
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2011-05-25T16:00:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          May 25, 2011
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://funcheaporfree.com/author/jordan/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            FunCheapOrFree
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://funcheaporfree.com/2011/05/youre-welcome-wednesday-green-lawn-care/#comments">
           19 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div style="text-align: center;">
         <i>
          Enjoy this guest post from my friend, Lauralee:
         </i>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://funcheaporfree.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/sugar-on-your-lawn.jpg">
          <img alt="How to use sugar on your lawn as an inexpensive fertilizer" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6240" height="320" src="http://funcheaporfree.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/sugar-on-your-lawn.jpg" width="246"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div>
         <a href="http://www.goodenoughmother.com/2011/04/why-mowing-the-lawn-is-mans-work/" target="_blank">
          source
         </a>
        </div>
        <div>
        </div>
        <p>
         Generally speaking, going Green means spending lots of Greens.Having a GREEN weed free lawn can can cost $300-$500 a year, just for lawn fertilizer and weed killer!
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont want to spend a fortune keeping my lawn lookingnice and I also dont like having to keep my kids off the grass for a few days after we have fertilized it. I asked my dad if he had a better solution andhe said all you need is this:
        </p>
        <p>
         All you need is this:
        </p>
        <div>
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-P8GPDrwxqfg/Tc7m1ZlWtFI/AAAAAAAAC3c/IUVFMiIdOlQ/s1600/Imperial-Pure-Cane-Sugar-4lbs.jpg">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-P8GPDrwxqfg/Tc7m1ZlWtFI/AAAAAAAAC3c/IUVFMiIdOlQ/s320/Imperial-Pure-Cane-Sugar-4lbs.jpg" width="220"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div>
        </div>
        <div>
         Thats right, SUGAR.
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           Plain white table sugar
          </b>
         </span>
         may just be the answer to your green problems!
        </div>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Sugar feeds beneficial microbes, insects, and worms
         </li>
         <li>
          Sugar wont cause allergic reactions in people or pets
         </li>
         <li>
          Sugar will fortify grass while discouraging weeds
         </li>
         <li>
          Sugar is easy to apply
         </li>
         <li>
          Sugar is cheap
         </li>
         <li>
          Sugar will not only make your lawn green  but green, that is free from chemicals that damage the soil, our water supply, and ourselves.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div>
         Chemical fertilizers and herbicides kill off the beneficial microbes and insects that enrich soil. Sugarthe simplest carbohydratefeeds them. And in turn, they will enrich your soil by 
        </div>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Increasing nutrients and enhancing the absorption of those nutrients
         </li>
         <li>
          Promoting nitrogen availability
         </li>
         <li>
          Increasing photosynthesis for a greener plant
         </li>
         <li>
          Stimulating root formation
         </li>
         <li>
          Improving soil structure
         </li>
         <li>
          Increasing humus levels
         </li>
         <li>
          Providing resistance to the climate extremes of heat, drought, and cold
         </li>
         <li>
          Controlling fungal diseases like brown spot
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div>
         St. Augustine and other grasses thrive on nutrient-rich soil. Weeds, on the other hand, love poor soil because they can take over and crowd out unhealthy sod that barely gets enough nutrients from those sporadic, artificial feedings. Nutrient-rich soil, on the other hand, nourishes your grass every day without depletion.
        </div>
        <div>
        </div>
        <div>
         If you have something like this
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000BQY7XO/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B000BQY7XO&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=fuchorfr-20&amp;linkId=IS6IRLLDSYSOKDD7">
          hand-spreader
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=fuchorfr-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B000BQY7XO" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         or this
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002YPS1KK/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002YPS1KK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=fuchorfr-20&amp;linkId=KLYKUMUMICDN6MLA">
          broadcast spreader
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=fuchorfr-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B002YPS1KK" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         to help disperse the sugarit is helpful, but not required.
        </div>
        <p>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="150" marginheight="0" marginwidth="0" scrolling="no" src="//ws-na.amazon-adsystem.com/widgets/q?ServiceVersion=20070822&amp;OneJS=1&amp;Operation=GetAdHtml&amp;MarketPlace=US&amp;source=ac&amp;ref=qf_sp_asin_til&amp;ad_type=product_link&amp;tracking_id=fuchorfr-20&amp;marketplace=amazon&amp;region=US&amp;placement=B000BQY7XO&amp;asins=B000BQY7XO&amp;linkId=LSA5YSFEMZQEVV4I&amp;show_border=true&amp;link_opens_in_new_window=true" style="width: 120px; height: 240px;" width="300">
          <br/>
         </iframe>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="150" marginheight="0" marginwidth="0" scrolling="no" src="//ws-na.amazon-adsystem.com/widgets/q?ServiceVersion=20070822&amp;OneJS=1&amp;Operation=GetAdHtml&amp;MarketPlace=US&amp;source=ac&amp;ref=qf_sp_asin_til&amp;ad_type=product_link&amp;tracking_id=fuchorfr-20&amp;marketplace=amazon&amp;region=US&amp;placement=B002YPS1KK&amp;asins=B002YPS1KK&amp;linkId=T2J3YHXU4NKQXQJX&amp;show_border=true&amp;link_opens_in_new_window=true" style="width: 120px; height: 240px;" width="300">
          <br/>
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <div>
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </div>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Its easy to apply sugar to your lawn.
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         You
         <strong>
         </strong>
         dont have to be precise and you dont have to use a spreader.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Just sprinkle it onto your lawn by hand.
          <ul>
           <li>
            I use an old juice container with a wide mouth, and just walk up and down the lawn, shaking out the sugar.
           </li>
          </ul>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Use a 5-pound bag for each 1,000 square feet of lawn.
          <ul>
           <li>
            You cannot use too much, and it wont burn your grass if more sugar spills on a concentrated area.
           </li>
          </ul>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          I would suggest applying sugar several times during the first year of use.
          <ul>
           <li>
            Since your lawn has been damaged from years of chemical applications, it needs time for those artificial fertilizers to be leached out of the ground and even more time to replenish those microbes that have been chased away.
           </li>
          </ul>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Once your lawn is normalized, you only have to spread sugar twice a year, in spring and fall.
          </strong>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          After applying, thoroughly wet down your lawn.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         If you want to make your lawn healthy and at the same time eliminate crabgrass, Bermuda grass, dandelions, clover, dollar weed and other shallow rooted weeds like chickweed, fire your lawn care company and start acquiring a sweet tooth!
        </p>
