             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               Special agent James Bond relies on high-tech gadgets, as much as his vodka martinis, to save the world. But because he's a Secret Intelligence Service agent on behalf of the Queen of England's government, he would technically be funded by UK taxpayers if a real-life person and the bill would certainly add up.
              </p>
              <p>
               Ahead of the film franchise's 23rd installment,
               <em>
                Skyfall
               </em>
                due in theaters Nov. 9  the team at
               <a href="http://www.FancyDressCostumes.co.uk" target="_blank">
                FancyDressCostumes.co.uk
               </a>
               has put together
               <a href="http://fancydresscostumes.co.uk/blog/james-bond-cost-taxpayer-infographic/" target="_blank">
                an inforgraphic
               </a>
               detailing just how much it would cost taxpayers to fund Bond. The verdict? $5.7 million.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <center>
               <strong>
                SEE ALSO:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2012/07/30/how-much-would-it-cost-to-be-batman/">
                 How Much Would It Cost to Be Batman in Real Life? [INFOGRAPHIC]
                </a>
               </strong>
              </center>
              <p>
               From weapons and hacker training to receiving a pilot license and owning a $4,000-customized smartphone, being Bond is rather pricey. Not to mention his annual salary ($160,000), pricey bar tabs and the $360,000 2012 Aston Martin DBS sports car he drives.
              </p>
              <p>
               For a full look at how much money goes into funding Bond's missions and lifestyle, check out the infographic below.
              </p>

              </p>
