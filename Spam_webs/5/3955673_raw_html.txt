         <h1 class="title">
          Fencing Design and Requirements for Your House and Garden
         </h1>
         <div class="product-types clearfix">
         </div>
         <!-- .product-types -->
         <div class="description">
          <p>
          </p>
         </div>
         <!-- .description -->
        </div>
        <!-- #product-info -->
        <div class="clear">
        </div>
        <p>
         Good
         <a href="http://www.amazingfencing.com.au/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          fencing
         </a>
         can make a world of difference to the appearance of your house. In fact, an attractive fence can enhance the value of your house if youre planning to sell in the near future. Good fencing can serve as an essential safety mechanism. While fencing companies are the best source of advice on the types of fencing most appropriate for your house and garden and the associated costs, the tips below provide an useful introduction to anyone who is contemplating an upgrade to their fencing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="fencing design Fencing Design and Requirements for Your House and Garden" height="298" src="http://www.nicehomelive.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/01/fencing-design.jpg" title="fencing design" width="447"/>
         <br/>
         <em>
          Image: amazingfencing.com.au
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Available Materials
          </em>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         The most common materials are timber, colourbond, weldmesh, steelmesh, chainwire, lattice, and treated pine. Make use of different materials throughout the different parts of your house and garden to suit the purposes. Timber fencing blend into the background and provide a casual, neighbourhood look while helping to maintain privacy. Colourbond fences are made from steel and combine security with attractiveness and durability. Weldmesh, steelmesh and chainwire fencing may be appropriate for industrial areas, or if you have small sections on your property that you want to restrict from animals or children. Lattice and treated pine are attractive and can be used for lower fences, gates, or screens.
         <span id="more-849">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Design issues should take into account the needs and location of the area to be fenced off. Whether you are intending on installing fencing for your commercial or private property, security issues will probably weigh equally to aesthetic appeal. Consider why you need the fencing and your main uses for the area to be fenced. Is security paramount or do you merely want to create a separate space in your property? Sometimes a low screen or gate may be sufficient, particularly when it comes to landscape fencing. Issues such as light and breeze should be taken into account.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Boundary Fencing
          </em>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Boundary fencing, or dividing fences, require consultation with your neighbours and may necessitate an agreement on the costs and design. If you want to build a fence that exceeds the councils height requirements, you may need a permit in addition to the consent of your neighbours. Generally, neighbours are required to splits costs of new fencing evenly between each party, though if one party wants a more expensive type of fencing, they will need to pay the difference. Check with your local council as to the legal requirements specific to your area.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Pool Fencing
          </em>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Pool fencing is an area that requires specialist advice and it is best to speak with
         <a href="http://www.amazingfencing.com.au/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          fencing contractors
         </a>
         for a definitive opinion on design requirements. Depending on site conditions, pool fencing must not be climbable or able to be scaled. Most councils require building or planning permits to be obtained before swimming pool fences can be built. There are strict pool fencing requirements in different council areas, so be sure to check the local laws for rules relating to height and materials. In most of Australia, fences must be at least 1.2 metre tall, have their own self-closing hinges and latches that work automatically after opening. They must be able to be locked, and must be completed within six months of work on the pool commencing. Latches need to be out of a childs reach. Old pools without fences are generally required to be enclosed as soon as possible.
        </p>
