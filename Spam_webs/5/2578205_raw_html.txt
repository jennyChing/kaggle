           Easter Marketing Trends 2014  The Ups And Downs
          </h2>

         <p dir="ltr">
          Holidays bring the chance of revenue generation for the marketers. People love to spend and purchase various things around a holiday. However, there are few holidays, which are not the best of occasions in terms of generating revenue for the advertisers and marketers. One such holiday is Easter. Even candy retailers, who are behind all the eggs, confectioneries and marshmallow- infused goodies, do not find this as the best holiday for business as Halloween takes the honor. But still, Easter is a holiday and Americans love shopping during holidays, even though the spending is considerably low compared to year-end holidays.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          The holidays at the end of the year bring in lot of revenues. According to the National Retail Federation (NRF), total spending last year was $600 billion dollars. But when it comes to Easter spending, it is quite low. As per a survey of NRF, this year, the total spending is expected to be under $16 billion. Majority of the spending i.e. $2.6 billion, will be for apparel, followed by gifts at $2.4 billion, then candy at $2.2 billion and finally decorations and flowers at $1.1 billion each.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          <strong>
           Read Also 
           <a href="http://www.seemoreinteractive.com/blog/2014/04/16/in-store-mobile-use">
            Study Reveals In-Store Mobile Use Reduces Shoppers by 32%
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          When compared to last year, the expected spending for this year is much lower. Last year the spending was expected to be around $17.2 billion.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          This is the low or down side of Easter
          <a href="http://www.seemoreinteractive.com/blog/category/retail-technology">
           marketing
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          Not all is bad, when it comes to Easter marketing. The expected spending of consumers might be lower than last year, but the good part of Easter is that advertisers and marketers are increasing their expenditure. A 34 percent increase in spending has been seen on TV ads which are related to Easter.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          A question arises as to why marketers are planning to spend more and in turn getting shoppers to theoretically spend more, whereas consumers are doing the opposite.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          The answer to it is, most likely
          <a href="http://www.seemoreinteractive.com/blog/2014/02/04/retail-enhances-customer-experience">
           retailers
          </a>
          are considering and depending on the change in the climate to bring about a change in the determination of the consumers in purchasing products which have an Easter theme.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          As the CEO and President of NRF, Matthew Shay said in a press release, the consumers have been left with a lot of repressed demand due to the harsh winters. So even though consumers are wary about spending in their money this year, for purchasing Easter items, retailers are expecting that warmer weather will improve the mood of the shoppers and they will want to buy holiday decorations, bright clothes and different other items.
         </p>
         <p>
          To know whether this expectation of the marketers comes true or not, we have to wait till Easter is over and the spending figures are out.
         </p>
