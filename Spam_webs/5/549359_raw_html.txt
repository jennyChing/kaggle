        <p>

        </p>

         </span>
        </p>

         </strong>
        </p>

          WordPress Email Address in Settings General

        </p>

        <div class="steps">
         Under WP Admin &gt;&gt; Users &gt;&gt; Your Profile
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3275" style="width: 310px">
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.netwebbing.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/wordpress-email-address2.gif">
          <img alt="wordpress-email-address2" class="size-medium wp-image-3275" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.netwebbing.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/wordpress-email-address2.gif?resize=300%2C52"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          WordPress Your Profile Email Address
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         This one says that for Your Profile, an email address is required ascontact info.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, these 2 email addresses do not have to be the same. In fact, mine is not.
        </p>
        <p>
         But what are they for? When do they get used?
        </p>
        <p>
         And when someone contacts you via your contact form (however you installed the contact form or via whichever of the many plugins), to which email address is that query sent to?
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I guessed  and I was wrong.
        </p>
        <p>
         Contact form queries are sent to Your Profile email address. (I would imagine that you would have the Administrator user role in WP).
        </p>
        <p>
         You may think this is trivial information. But it does assume more importance if you have problems with your contact forms and you need to troubleshoot. And knowing this, it is best to make sure that Your Profile email address is correct and up-to-date.
        </p>
        <div class="sn indent">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          FYI:
         </span>
         My current contact form is via the Jetpack plugin.
         <br/>
         My contact form problem was that it was notgoing to my email system.
         <br/>
         I finally figured out that my POP server settings were incorrect. I have just migrated to anotherhosting provider and that was one of the many unexpected changes.
        </div>
