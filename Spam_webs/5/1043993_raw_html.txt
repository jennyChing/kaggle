       Anywho. So Ive decided something recently. My attitude has sucked. About myself. I dont like not liking myself. Its just not very fun. For me or other people. Especially you guys. Who wants to read negative stuff? Not me!! So Ive made the bold decision to stop being such a downer. So bold. And to love myself a bit more, every day. Ive just decided that Im not really going to enjoy life to the fullest if Im always worrying about how I look.        And the biggest thing I worry about most of the time is my cellulite. If you are a person with cellulite, you understand. It just blows. Im much better with talking about it versus dealing with it. Its one of those haunting things. Especially since I work at a gym. I always have felt like I wasnt a great role model or looked like I worked out hard because of my cellulite. But you know what, my genetics did not swing that way so I will still always deal with that. Even if a bunch of my gym friends dont deal with that problem (b*tches), I will. And thats something I need to be ok with. Like while Im camping and swimming at the lake this week. No non-fun Juli for this trip.
      </p>
      <p>
       And you may ask, why the hell is Juli talking about cellulite? Well, I read an
       <a href="http://breakingmuscle.com/womens-fitness/the-dirty-little-secret-of-the-female-athlete-cellulite" target="_blank">
        awesome article
       </a>
       yesterday about cellulite. I love when other girls talk about that kind of stuff. Its just nice to know that Im not the only person out there who feels that way. I am in my group of friends, but not in the world. Luckily.
      </p>

       <div class="ERSIngredients">
        <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
         Ingredients
        </div>
        <ul>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 pound
          <a href="http://www.grasslandbeef.com/Detail.bok?no=524" target="_blank">
           ground beef
          </a>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           teaspoon cayenne pepper
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           teaspoon smoked paprika
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           teaspoon garlic powder
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           teaspoon onion powder
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          salt and pepper, to taste
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          8 strips of
          <a href="http://www.grasslandbeef.com/Detail.bok?no=1186" target="_blank">
           bacon
          </a>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          4 hot dogs (nitrate and filler free- I used
          <a href="http://www.colemannatural.com" target="_blank">
           Coleman Natural hot dogs
          </a>
          )
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           white onion, thinly sliced
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 avocado, sliced
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          5-6 tablespoons
          <a href="http://paleomg.com/4th-of-july-egg-and-potato-salad/" target="_blank">
           leftover homemade mayo
          </a>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          head of romaine lettuce
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div class="ERSClear">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="ERSInstructions">
        <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
         Instructions
        </div>
        <ol>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          Place bacon on a baking sheet and cook bacon for about 15 minutes until just slightly crispy.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          In a large bowl, mix together ground beef, cayenne pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and a little salt and pepper. Then form in 4 patties.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          Cut hot dog down the middle to butterfly the hot dog.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          Place beef patties on the grill and cook on both sides until you get however you like your meat cooked. I cooked mine for about 4-5 minutes per side for medium rare.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          When flip the beef patties, place butterflied hot dogs on the grill and flip after about 2 minutes.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          When all meat is done cooking, place a beef patty in a lettuce leaf, top it with mayo, then a hot dog, then bacon, then any other fixings you like. I used onion and avocado on my burger. Then top it with one more piece of lettuce.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          Enjoy summer!
         </li>
        </ol>
