     <p>
      <b>
       Intelligent falling
      </b>
      (
      <b>
       IF
      </b>
      ) is a
      <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">
       parody
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Intelligent_design" title="Intelligent design">
       intelligent design
      </a>
      (ID) movement. It is a
      <a href="/wiki/Pseudoscience" title="Pseudoscience">
       pseudoscientific
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Supernatural" title="Supernatural">
       supernatural
      </a>
      explanation of the effects of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gravitation" title="Gravitation">
       gravity
      </a>
      . The joke originated on Usenet, and has appeared in several online parodies. An article about Intelligent Falling in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Onion" title="The Onion">
        The Onion
       </a>
      </i>
      described free fall as being caused by "the
      <a href="/wiki/Invisible_hand" title="Invisible hand">
       hand
      </a>
      of God", as depicted in
      <a href="/wiki/Michelangelo" title="Michelangelo">
       Michelangelo
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/Sistine_Chapel" title="Sistine Chapel">
       Sistine Chapel
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Sistine_Chapel_ceiling" title="Sistine Chapel ceiling">
       fresco
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theonion_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-theonion-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">
       Summary
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Intelligent_falling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Intelligent Falling proposes that the
      <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">
       scientific
      </a>
      explanation of
      <a href="/wiki/Newton%27s_law_of_universal_gravitation" title="Newton's law of universal gravitation">
       gravitational force
      </a>
      cannot explain all aspects of the
      <a href="/wiki/Phenomenon" title="Phenomenon">
       phenomenon
      </a>
      , so credence should be given to the idea that things fall because a higher intelligence is moving them. Furthermore, IF asserts that theories explaining
      <a href="/wiki/Gravity" title="Gravity">
       gravity
      </a>
      are not internally consistent nor mathematically reconcilable with
      <a href="/wiki/Quantum_mechanics" title="Quantum mechanics">
       quantum mechanics
      </a>
      , making gravity a "theory in crisis". IF also makes the claim that gravity is "only a theory", parodying the claims made by creationists regarding the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evolution_as_theory_and_fact" title="Evolution as theory and fact">
       theoretical status of evolution
      </a>
      . Pretend IF apologists advocate that IF should be taught in school along with the theory of gravity so that students can make "an informed decision" on the subject in accordance with demands to "
      <a href="/wiki/Teach_the_Controversy" title="Teach the Controversy">
       teach the controversy
      </a>
      ". IF has found common ground with the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Church_of_the_Flying_Spaghetti_Monster" title="Church of the Flying Spaghetti Monster">
       Church of the Flying Spaghetti Monster
      </a>
      , a similar critique of intelligent design, and the two are often combined when mentioned in discussions.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Intelligent_falling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In June 2002 a user named Jeff Stubbs posted a draft of a letter to the editor that mentions "intelligent grappling" to the alt.atheism and talk.origins usenet groups.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He commented that "I don't like the theory of gravity, I feel personally insulted that engineers design structures only considering physical mass. What about our souls? I propose that science classes also teach the theory of "Intelligent Grappling". There's no way a weak force such as gravity can possibly hold everything onto the planet.
      <a href="/wiki/Occasionalism" title="Occasionalism">
       It must be God
      </a>
      , using our souls, to hold everything together." This was followed by
      <a href="/wiki/Elf_Sternberg" title="Elf Sternberg">
       Elf Sternberg
      </a>
      posting a "FAQ on intelligent grappling" on the sci.skeptic usenet group.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D._C._Simpson" title="D. C. Simpson">
       D. C. Simpson
      </a>
      published, in May 2005, an
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Drew_This" title="I Drew This">
        I Drew This
       </a>
      </i>
      comic strip titled "Teaching Gravity".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-idrewthis_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-idrewthis-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This was followed by Joshua Rosenau, a graduate student at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kansas" title="University of Kansas">
       University of Kansas
      </a>
      , presenting the idea on his
      <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">
       blog
      </a>
      , citing Isaac Newton.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Intelligent falling was the subject of an article in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Onion" title="The Onion">
        The Onion
       </a>
      </i>
      in August 2005.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theonion_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-theonion-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In January 2010 Indian news satire website
      <a href="/wiki/Faking_News" title="Faking News">
       Faking News
      </a>
      used the idea to write spoof news: "Apple dropped from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burj_Dubai" title="Burj Dubai">
       Burj Dubai
      </a>
      to validate Newtons laws disappears".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
