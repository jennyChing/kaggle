             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              Review: Daymoon - Fabric Of Space Divine (2013)
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div style="float:left;width:100%;padding:0;margin:10px 0 5px 0">
              <div class="fb-like" data-send="true" data-show-faces="false" data-width="450">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5318184129274502230">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-AERk6yLthIQ/UlZccmLDOkI/AAAAAAAAFWg/txqp9489RM8/s1600/fabric-of-space-divine-2013.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-AERk6yLthIQ/UlZccmLDOkI/AAAAAAAAFWg/txqp9489RM8/s400/fabric-of-space-divine-2013.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <b>
               Artist:
              </b>
              <a href="http://www.progshine.net/2013/10/daymoon.html" target="_blank">
               Daymoon
              </a>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Album:
              </b>
              Fabric Of Space Divine
              <br/>
              <b>
               Year:
              </b>
              2013
              <br/>
              <b>
               Label:
              </b>
              MALS
              <br/>
              <b>
              </b>
              <br/>
              <a name="more">
              </a>
              <b>
               Review:
              </b>
              Diego Camargo
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Rate:
              </b>
              <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-qNgnl-lWsTs/USZikb0z-UI/AAAAAAAABaY/w1qr4-YYXbo/s1600/4stars.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;">
               <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-qNgnl-lWsTs/USZikb0z-UI/AAAAAAAABaY/w1qr4-YYXbo/s1600/4stars.png"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Thoughts:
              </b>
              Last week I reviewed the first proper album by
              <b>
               <span style="font-size: large;">
                <a href="http://www.progshine.net/2013/10/daymoon.html" target="_blank">
                 Daymoon
                </a>
               </span>
              </b>
              ,
              <b>
               All Tomorrows (2011)
              </b>
              (read it
              <b>
               <span style="font-size: large;">
                <a href="http://www.progshine.net/2013/10/review-daymoon-all-tomorrows-2011.html" target="_blank">
                 HERE
                </a>
               </span>
              </b>
              ), and I found the band quite interesting, but the album was lacking some unity. So its high time to review their most recent album
              <b>
               Fabric Of Space Divine (2013)
              </b>
              .
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Fabric Of Space Divine (2013)
              </b>
              is a concept and intricate album about Evolution, the Space evolution, and consequently human evolution. The album, was released by the Russian label MALS earlier this year, but had been written and recorded since 2000.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Fabric Of Space Divine (2013)
              </b>
              is divided into 3 parts and 16 tracks. The album begins with the first part called Complexity thats divided into 3 songs: Singularity To Sol, Seed Of Complexity and Evolution.
              <br/>
              This beginning is the base of the album and explains how the Universe began and evolved in its prime days.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Once again
              <b>
               <span style="font-size: large;">
                <a href="http://www.progshine.net/2013/10/daymoon.html" target="_blank">
                 Daymoon
                </a>
               </span>
              </b>
              has a great booklet with thousands of information that enables you to follow the songs and every step of the story.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              The second part Explanations comes with the challenge of elaborating on the human being evolution and its beliefs (especially religions).
              <br/>
              This second part is divided into 5 more tracks: Beyond Nature, Beyond Trinity, Anthropocentrics, Beyond Multiplicity and Beyond Good And Evil.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Fabric Of Space Divine (2013)
              </b>
              took 12 years to be finished and what should be impossible was achieved, the album has unity! Their previous album
              <b>
               All Tomorrows (2011)
              </b>
              looked a bit as a patchwork,
              <b>
               Fabric Of Space Divine (2013)
              </b>
              is more concise and complete.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <iframe seamless="" src="http://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=1399909006/size=medium/bgcol=333333/linkcol=e99708/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; height: 120px; width: 100%;">
                <a href="http://daymoon.bandcamp.com/album/fabric-of-space-divine">
                 Fabric of Space Divine by Daymoon
                </a>
               </iframe>
              </div>
              <br/>
              The third and last part is called Expansion and it includes 8 tracks: Middle, Ice Prospector, Digital, Beyond, Grasping The Fabric, Twisting The Fabric, Beyond Zero Kelvin and One.
              <br/>
              In this last bit of the album the science fiction begins. As Fred Lessing said himself in the booklet: much of it, if not all, will prove to be humbug eventually. Or not!
              <br/>
              <br/>
              In terms of concept
              <b>
               Fabric Of Space Divine (2013)
              </b>
              is one of the most interesting albums Ive seen in years. Its complex, structured, full of good ideas and well written.
              <br/>
              When it comes to the music the album has everything you could imagine. From acoustic ballads to Gentle Giant influence, passing through Arab music and a bit of electronic too.
              <br/>
              But because the album was recorded through a spam of long 12 years you can obviously spot some older sounds in some songs, but that doesnt make the album go down, not a single bit.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Fred Lessing was able to conceive one of the most interesting and complex albums of the year. And hes also becoming one of the more interesting musicians on the Prog scene.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Sit back, headphones on, booklet in your hands and just embark on this great adventure through time and space, literally, with
              <b>
               <span style="font-size: large;">
                <a href="http://www.progshine.net/2013/10/daymoon.html" target="_blank">
                 Daymoon
                </a>
               </span>
              </b>
              !
              <br/>
              <br/>
              The band was included in our Podcast #24 and you can listen the track 'Twisting The Fabric'
              <b>
               <span style="font-size: large;">
                <a href="http://www.progshine.net/2013/08/podcast-24-130813.html" target="_blank">
                 HERE
                </a>
               </span>
              </b>
              .
              <br/>
