         <h3>
          Project Overview:
         </h3>
         <p>
          This earthtone ombre dishcloth is a fascinating crochet project. The
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/crochetterms/g/Variegated_Yarn_Definition.htm">
           variegated yarns
          </a>
          make each
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="2" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/tunisiancrochet/tp/tunisian-crochet-stitches.htm">
           stitch
          </a>
          interesting. As you crochet, youll find yourself looking forward to seeing what the colors will do next.
         </p>
         <p>
          The body of the dishcloth is crocheted using
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="3" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/tunisiancrochet/ss/crochet-afghan-stitch-instructions.htm">
           afghan stitch,
          </a>
          and the edging is worked in
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="4" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/learntocrochet/ss/Single_Crochet.htm">
           single crochet,
          </a>
          with decorative
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="5" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/crochetstitches/ss/slip-stitches-in-surface-crochet.htm">
           slip stitch details.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This dishcloth would make a fantastic homemade gift, especially if you whip up a couple of the
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="6" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/homedecorpatterns/p/variegated_earthtone_potholders.htm">
           matching potholders
          </a>
          to go along with it.
         </p>
         <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="3" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense1">
         </div>
         <div class="radlinks ads-half" data-character-limit="null" data-display-inline="true" data-display-label="false" data-num-links="5" id="radlinks3">
         </div>
         <h3>
          More Project Photos:
         </h3>
         <p>
          I've posted some
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="7" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/freecrochetpatterns/ig/Variegated-Crochet-Projects/Variegated-Earthtone-Dishcloth.htm">
           close-up photos of the dishcloth
          </a>
          and the yarns used to make it.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Similar Dishcloth Patterns:
         </h3>
         <p>
          This pattern is included in our list of free crochet
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="8" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/dishcloths/tp/dishcloth_patterns.htm">
           dishcloth patterns;
          </a>
          it's also featured in our photo gallery of
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="9" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/freecrochetpatterns/ig/Variegated-Crochet-Projects/">
           variegated crochet projects,
          </a>
          and on our list of
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="10" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/freecrochetpatterns/tp/Kitchen-Patterns.htm">
           kitchen patterns.
          </a>
          To find even more
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="11" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/homedecorpatterns/tp/Home-Decorating-Patterns.htm">
           home decorating ideas and patterns,
          </a>
          be sure to visit our
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="12" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/freecrochetpatterns/ss/free-crochet-patterns.htm">
           free crochet patterns
          </a>
          page.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Materials:
         </h3>
         <h3>
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="13" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/yarn/tp/Crochet-Yarn.htm">
           Yarn:
          </a>
         </h3>
         <p>
          This dishcloth is crocheted using Peaches &amp; Creme worsted weight
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="14" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/yarn/p/kitchen-cotton-yarn.htm">
           kitchen cotton
          </a>
          yarn. The following is a list of the colors I used to create
          <a data-component="link" data-inlink="PvftQH3ODU-7H4Yc0M-Kgw==" data-ordinal="15" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/dishcloths/a/Blue_Denim_Ombre_Dishcloth.htm">
           the sample dishcloth
          </a>
          . Please feel free to experiment with other colors or brands of yarn if you prefer.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Shaded brown (
           <a data-component="link" data-inlink="Dp_CWyDSviD8e4U5ZwfXDw==" data-ordinal="16" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/freecrochetpatterns/ig/Variegated-Crochet-Projects/Earthtone-Ombre-Potholders.htm">
            an ombre yarn
           </a>
           ) - Color A
          </li>
          <li>
           Earthtone (an ombre yarn) - Color B
          </li>
          <li>
           Camel (a solid color, used for the edging) - Color C
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="17" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/crochethooks/tp/Crochet-Hooks.htm">
           Crochet Hooks:
          </a>
         </h3>
         <p>
          For the body of the dishcloth, youll need a size J / 10 /6.0 mm afghan crochet hook measuring 10+ inches long, or your preferred size.
         </p>
         <p>
          For the edging, try using an ordinary crochet hook in whatever size you'd usually use with worsted weight yarn.
         </p>
         <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="2" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense2">
         </div>
         <p class="cb-split">
          The first time you work this pattern, it might take you a bit of experimenting before you figure out which hook will give you the best results; the goal is to find a hook that will produce consistent tension between the edging and the body of the dishcloth.
         </p>
         <p>
          For the edging in my sample dishcloth, I used a size H / 8  5.0 mm hook. I tried several sizes and found the size H hook worked best for the way I crochet. A Size G was too small, and a size I was too large.
          <a data-component="link" data-inlink="JdmBwVLpVj3bgLvvRS623Q==" data-ordinal="18" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/crochethooks/f/What-Size-Crochet-Hook-For-Rag-Rug.htm">
           The hook size
          </a>
          you need might vary, depending on the way you crochet. If you notice your edging ruffling up a bit, choose a smaller hook. If the edging seems too tight in comparison to the body of the dishcloth, choose a larger hook.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Other Materials:
         </h3>
         <p>
          You'll need a
          <a data-component="link" data-inlink="bzX7ieUmBIGxdEP_za_edA==" data-ordinal="19" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/needlework/tp/tapestry-needle.htm">
           tapestry needle
          </a>
          for weaving in ends.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="20" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/crochetabbreviations/a/Crochet_Abbreviations.htm">
           Abbreviations:
          </a>
         </h3>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="21" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/crochetabbreviations/g/Ch.htm">
            ch
           </a>
           =
           <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="22" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/learntocrochet/ss/Chain_Stitch.htm">
            chain
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="23" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/crochetabbreviations/g/sc.htm">
            sc
           </a>
           =
           <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="24" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/learntocrochet/ss/Single_Crochet.htm">
            single crochet
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           sl st(s)
           <a data-component="link" data-inlink="z6EOpNjxlP0E6rPDqASe2Q==" data-ordinal="25" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/crochetstitches/ss/join-with-a-slip-stitch-to-form-a-ring.htm">
            = slip stitch
           </a>
           (es)
          </li>
          <li>
           st(s) = stitch(es)
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Finished Size:
         </h3>
         <p>
          The dishcloth measures approx. 8.5" square.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Gauge:
         </h3>
         <p>
          When working in afghan stitch / Tunisian simple stitch, 7 sts = 2". The row gauge is not critical for this project. For that matter, the stitch gauge isn't all that critical either; it's no problem if your dishcloth turns out a little larger or smaller than the sample.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Crochet Instructions:
         </h3>
         <p>
          Using color A, ch 26.
         </p>
         <p>
          Work in
          <a data-component="link" data-inlink="Ydun1rznO87cvApcH0TW7w==" data-ordinal="26" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/tunisiancrochet/ss/crochet-afghan-stitch-instructions_11.htm">
           afghan stitch
          </a>
          (
          <a data-component="link" data-inlink="aWW76g7dx5-z-YOQwDCngg==" data-ordinal="27" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/tunisiancrochet/tp/tunisian-crochet-videos.htm">
           Tunisian simple stitch
          </a>
          ) until directed otherwise. To make the edging easier later, it is a good idea to work the
          <a data-component="link" data-inlink="M7uNnN-DChNRf8AEWURR8A==" data-ordinal="28" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/tunisiancrochet/ss/crochet-afghan-stitch-instructions_6.htm">
           first row
          </a>
          <a data-component="link" data-inlink="kwyh8mIGwXlHhc8Xl2THww==" data-ordinal="29" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/learntocrochet/ss/Chain_Stitch_4.htm">
           into the backs of the chain stitches
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           <a data-component="link" data-inlink="pJx9-9vgAJzaWNgpnWPp7A==" data-ordinal="30" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/clothingandaccessories/ss/PinkYellowFlowerNecklace_3.htm">
            Row 1
           </a>
           :
          </b>
          Work the forward pass in color A; Work the return pass in color B.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Row 2:
          </b>
          Work the forward pass in color B; Work the return pass in color A.
         </p>
         <p>
          Repeat rows 1 and 2 until the piece is square.
         </p>
         <p>
          Then work one slip stitch in each vertical bar, all the way across. End off and weave in ends.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Dishcloth Edging:
         </h3>
         <p>
          The edging is worked in rounds.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a data-component="link" data-inlink="YNoYn98QWEdlWIgRpdyi4g==" data-ordinal="31" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/motifs/ss/Abstract_Flower_3.htm">
           Switch crochet hooks
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Attach color C in a corner.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           <a data-component="link" data-inlink="wcnl5-_HPEu_lkK4OIsxCg==" data-ordinal="32" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/freecrochetpatterns/ss/Openwork_Dishcloth_Edging_2.htm">
            Round 1
           </a>
           :
          </b>
          ch 1. Work 1 sc in each stitch. When you get to a corner, (sc, ch 2, sc) and then continue working sc. Repeat this all the way around the dishcloth, and then at the end, join the round with a sl st.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Rounds 2-3:
          </b>
          Repeat round 1.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Round 4:
          </b>
          Work 1 sl st in each sc. When you get to a corner, work 1 sl st into the ch-2 space. After youve completed the round, work an extra sl st; pull the loop through the previous sl st worked at the beginning of the row to the back of the dishcloth, then cut the yarn and end off.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Optional Slip Stitch Detail:
          </b>
          Using color A, work a round of decorative sl sts in between the body of the dishcloth and the edging. If you're not sure how to do this, check out my
          <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="33" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://crochet.about.com/od/crochetstitches/ss/slip-stitches-in-surface-crochet.htm">
           surface crochet tutorial
          </a>
          for general instructions and examples of this technique.
         </p>
