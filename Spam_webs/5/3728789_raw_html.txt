        KnockKnock
       </em>
       a cheating husband dumps a loving wife for a tarty actress. Jessica will get over it. "Knock knock," says their young son."Who's that?" replies Jessica. "See? You've forgotten me already", cries the son.
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        WastedLives
       </em>
       (1993) takes a serious look at treachery in Eastern Europe and reveals a cynical Weldon. She must be hard to live with (one husband, four sons). On the other hand she writes it all down, doesn't bottle stuff up. We become her therapist and admire her for it. Life is cruel. Film exec goeshome, out of love. Local girl ispregnant, commits suicide.
      </p>
      <p>
       The travel stories plod until at Schiphol airport in
       <em>
        WhyDidSheDoThat?
       </em>
       the wife breaks down before the husband and wails, "Come back to me, love of my life". He does. "And that's why she did it," the author (yes!) tells her hubby. "He drove her mad. Thank you for asking."
      </p>
      <p>
       Then, what we've all been waiting for: Weldon's new novella
       <em>
        TheTed Dreams
       </em>
       (2014).Teddies suddenly in his sleep. A blood clot hits his brain and Phyllis, his wife, wakes to find him cold when surely he should be warm.Investigation into cyber-shenanigans are triggered by her marriage to Robbie, a sinister neuro-pharma-scientist who feeds her happy pills to excel in bed, where she dreams of Ted who returns to haunt her.
      </p>
      <p>
       Suspend disbelief, andsavour the luscious butterfly cover andshocking pink silk bookmark. Great fun.
      </p>
