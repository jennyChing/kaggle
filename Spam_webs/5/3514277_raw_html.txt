             <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
              <a href="http://www.doityourselfdivas.com/2015/06/diy-round-restoration-hardware-table.html">
               DIY Round Restoration Hardware Table and Gray Wash Stain
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4873212931510682389" itemprop="description articleBody">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>

              </div>

              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-25KAt2KLSFQ/VYHGih71fXI/AAAAAAAALTE/BrlmNmcfhcU/s1600/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-06-17%2Bat%2B1.11.26%2BPM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-25KAt2KLSFQ/VYHGih71fXI/AAAAAAAALTE/BrlmNmcfhcU/s640/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-06-17%2Bat%2B1.11.26%2BPM.png" width="404"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               I had been eying the
               <a href="https://www.restorationhardware.com/catalog/product/product.jsp?productId=prod2590010&amp;categoryId=cat160047" target="_blank">
                Restoration Hardware round farmhouse table
               </a>
               for some
 time, but not the price tag. I knew I could have my engineer hubby 
build me one just like it for a fraction of the price.
              </div>
              <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-atdo7A0inCM/VYHEbCfyYNI/AAAAAAAALS4/b52NAuTxvNc/s1600/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-06-17%2Bat%2B1.02.25%2BPM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-atdo7A0inCM/VYHEbCfyYNI/AAAAAAAALS4/b52NAuTxvNc/s640/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-06-17%2Bat%2B1.02.25%2BPM.png" width="222"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               There are many 
farmhouse table tutorials out there, so I won't go through every single 
step on how to make this, BUT I will tell you what we did to make the round shape of the table, how to make every board line up 
perfectly with the board next to it, and how to make your own Restoration Hardware gray 
washed stain.
              </div>
              <br/>
              The chairs are the
              <a href="https://www.restorationhardware.com/catalog/product/product.jsp?productId=prod6290013&amp;categoryId=cat1860003" target="_blank">
               Remy Side Chairs in Burnished Steel from Restoration Hardware.
              </a>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-vNEjASizjGI/VYHAZJwQ5JI/AAAAAAAALSg/PacKipWaA4o/s1600/IMG_8593.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-vNEjASizjGI/VYHAZJwQ5JI/AAAAAAAALSg/PacKipWaA4o/s640/IMG_8593.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              We didn't buy our wood from Home Depot or any regular home repair store like that. We drove to SLC to a place called MacBeath's where we were able to select pretty much any type of wood we wanted. We chose to use pine. The boards were 2" thick and anywhere between 7" and 9" wide. We wanted a 60" diameter table, so before we went to Macbeath's, Engineer Hubby drew the table top to scale and estimated the number/length of boards needed.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <img border="0" height="480" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-I7Cu6pmZZbg/VYGQO4HtyWI/AAAAAAAALRM/xDOgJV1U5D4/s640/IMG_1528.JPG" width="640"/>
              </div>
              <br/>
              We had Macbeath's run the pine through a planer and jointer so all sides of the boards were smooth and straight. This was crucial to getting the boards to line up perfectly while we built the table. It makes it so much easier if you don't skip this step! Here are our boards before they went through the planer.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-bgPp2iAHV7c/VYGQO3QzzmI/AAAAAAAALRQ/K_k0aQQEjzM/s1600/IMG_1530.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-bgPp2iAHV7c/VYGQO3QzzmI/AAAAAAAALRQ/K_k0aQQEjzM/s640/IMG_1530.JPG" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              We joined and glued the boards together using #20 biscuits and wood glue. This picture shows a few of the biscuits in place in the edge of a table top board. We generally spaced our biscuits between 12" and 18" on center with a smaller spacing for the outside boards that are not supported by the base.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-EBylgTkmo9M/VYGQRG8VCJI/AAAAAAAALRc/ya5TJYujVac/s1600/IMG_4660.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-EBylgTkmo9M/VYGQRG8VCJI/AAAAAAAALRc/ya5TJYujVac/s640/IMG_4660.jpg" width="480"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              This is how we cut the round shape of the table. Using a screw at the center of the table as a pivot and a router mounted to a pivot arm (a scrap piece of particle board) we made a rough cut circle in two passes.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Vw775nA7Ro4/VYGQtzBA6JI/AAAAAAAALRk/t_oiK7zTMEI/s1600/IMG_4662.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Vw775nA7Ro4/VYGQtzBA6JI/AAAAAAAALRk/t_oiK7zTMEI/s640/IMG_4662.jpg" width="480"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              After cutting through half the thickness of the wood with the first pass, we flipped the table top over and cut through the remainder with the second pass. This left us with a fairly rough edge but we were able to clean it up quickly with a belt sander.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-dZozx7CN74c/VYGQuEhCo2I/AAAAAAAALRo/LmDX8TjRNZI/s1600/IMG_1539.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="480" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-dZozx7CN74c/VYGQuEhCo2I/AAAAAAAALRo/LmDX8TjRNZI/s640/IMG_1539.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              On to the beautiful base of the table. My husband is just super handy, so there is no way I can explain this step, however there are many tutorials online for table bases. You need only search.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-OXzf7e5AO1U/VYGQ3yKANII/AAAAAAAALR0/47q4pvF9b8I/s1600/IMG_4751.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-OXzf7e5AO1U/VYGQ3yKANII/AAAAAAAALR0/47q4pvF9b8I/s640/IMG_4751.jpg" width="480"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               To get the perfect gray washed stain that you see from Restoration Hardware, simply mix 50% Minwax Provincial with 50% Minwax Weathered Oak. We only did 1 coat of stain and then sprayed on three coats of water based polyurethane. Also, before staining, we used a wood conditioner.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-LyJa7MP6-cA/VYHL2uNXT3I/AAAAAAAALTc/6hV1Dd2am1c/s1600/vscocam-photo-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="480" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-LyJa7MP6-cA/VYHL2uNXT3I/AAAAAAAALTc/6hV1Dd2am1c/s640/vscocam-photo-1.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>

               We actually stained our floor with Minwax Provincial, so in this picture it's easy to see the difference the mixture makes when Minwax Provincial is combined with Minwax Weathered Gray.

             <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
              <a href="http://www.doityourselfdivas.com/2015/04/diy-how-to-paint-kitchen-cabinets-like.html">
               DIY How to Paint Kitchen Cabinets Like a Pro
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2724191303799722411" itemprop="description articleBody">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JVz0dkd8GpE/VSwm29biTDI/AAAAAAAALN4/dAzGdSyeM1A/s1600/IMG_8250.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JVz0dkd8GpE/VSwm29biTDI/AAAAAAAALN4/dAzGdSyeM1A/s1600/IMG_8250.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              During Spring Break, instead of playing or vacationing, we worked hard and refinished our kitchen cabinets. I must say, our end results are near perfect! Going into it, you need to know this - we spent 3 full days of prepping (don't skim on this), 1 day of painting with a rented sprayer (MUST), and 1 day of putting everything back. We used this awesome waterborne paint to paint the cabinets and didn't need a polyurethane coat - more on that later. We have a large kitchen and we spent just over $300 for everything. Let's get started.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-7LyZ_9e8K9I/VSxGO60qYII/AAAAAAAALOs/HCKoMZQBeP0/s1600/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-04-13%2Bat%2B4.41.25%2BPM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-7LyZ_9e8K9I/VSxGO60qYII/AAAAAAAALOs/HCKoMZQBeP0/s1600/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-04-13%2Bat%2B4.41.25%2BPM.png" width="428"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Remove all doors and drawers. Remove hinges and number all parts so you can easily put everything back where it was.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-p_RtPDTaAbY/VSwmeZR0bsI/AAAAAAAALLs/VzA0gkjp-LA/s1600/IMG_4576.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-p_RtPDTaAbY/VSwmeZR0bsI/AAAAAAAALLs/VzA0gkjp-LA/s1600/IMG_4576.JPG" width="480"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DJGjTOjjJsw/VSwmfiG1soI/AAAAAAAALL8/wah777ulkqk/s1600/IMG_4578.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DJGjTOjjJsw/VSwmfiG1soI/AAAAAAAALL8/wah777ulkqk/s1600/IMG_4578.JPG" width="480"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               Wash everything to remove gunk and grease. I used S.O.S Wool Pads.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-tGaoVt7BYLw/VSwme-Z7beI/AAAAAAAALLw/jMKKTjCUa68/s1600/IMG_4577.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
               <img border="0" height="480" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-tGaoVt7BYLw/VSwme-Z7beI/AAAAAAAALLw/jMKKTjCUa68/s1600/IMG_4577.JPG" width="640"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               Very lightly sand
               <i>
                <span style="font-size: large;">
                 everything
                </span>
               </i>
               with about 220 grit.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-dXPNhar1rJs/VSwmfl9skkI/AAAAAAAALMA/0HCYRsfiTLo/s1600/IMG_4579.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
               <img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-dXPNhar1rJs/VSwmfl9skkI/AAAAAAAALMA/0HCYRsfiTLo/s1600/IMG_4579.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               Cover all markings/numbers with painters tape before painting.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-4GbJQNFKJDc/VSwmhVTU8DI/AAAAAAAALMU/lmpL_0tg148/s1600/IMG_4582.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
               <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-4GbJQNFKJDc/VSwmhVTU8DI/AAAAAAAALMU/lmpL_0tg148/s1600/IMG_4582.jpg" width="480"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               Tape off drawers to cover anything that you don't want painted.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gZNy6OX3fog/VSwmhCFtCuI/AAAAAAAALMM/BI9v5wsJjgI/s1600/IMG_4584.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
               <img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gZNy6OX3fog/VSwmhCFtCuI/AAAAAAAALMM/BI9v5wsJjgI/s1600/IMG_4584.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               Caulk before you paint. We even caulked the doors so there would be no cracks in our paint.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-EGHczEQiRHo/VSwmhJgufII/AAAAAAAALMQ/GkM7Veknzd0/s1600/IMG_4586.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-EGHczEQiRHo/VSwmhJgufII/AAAAAAAALMQ/GkM7Veknzd0/s1600/IMG_4586.JPG" width="480"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               Tape off boxes.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-R50sg-BfDiI/VSwmzUslaLI/AAAAAAAALM4/ZT5PpqeXRWg/s1600/IMG_8228.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-R50sg-BfDiI/VSwmzUslaLI/AAAAAAAALM4/ZT5PpqeXRWg/s1600/IMG_8228.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               Pull out appliances and cover with plastic, along with walls or open areas.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-PP7zwfaQc5Q/VSwm0PHnRzI/AAAAAAAALNI/orrWuU-J_sc/s1600/IMG_8233.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-PP7zwfaQc5Q/VSwm0PHnRzI/AAAAAAAALNI/orrWuU-J_sc/s1600/IMG_8233.jpg" width="426"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               Lay drop cloths on the floor.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-kltDTE9T8IE/VSwm0tWp1sI/AAAAAAAALNU/A5-Bue-SgKs/s1600/IMG_8236.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
               <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-kltDTE9T8IE/VSwm0tWp1sI/AAAAAAAALNU/A5-Bue-SgKs/s1600/IMG_8236.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Make a spraying center in garage and put all doors and drawers in there.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-eA7NSAZ8mbc/VSwmzAkoPDI/AAAAAAAALM8/Kezvooiv3Rw/s1600/IMG_8229.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-eA7NSAZ8mbc/VSwmzAkoPDI/AAAAAAAALM8/Kezvooiv3Rw/s1600/IMG_8229.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              We put screws in the hinge areas of each door so we could spray one side of the door with paint and flip it over so the doors rest on the screws while we spray the other side.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-_QncGdxrpPQ/VSwmzCNBoFI/AAAAAAAALM0/88xpEwjM8Kg/s1600/IMG_8230.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-_QncGdxrpPQ/VSwmzCNBoFI/AAAAAAAALM0/88xpEwjM8Kg/s1600/IMG_8230.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              As you can see, we rented a paint sprayer and we used Glidden Professional Primer along with Benjamin Moore Advance Waterborne paint in Satin. I can't rave about this paint enough! We hardly dealt with any fumes, it was fast drying, and seems to dry really hard, and it looks flawless when used with a sprayer. We did 1 coat of primer and 2 coats of paint.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-K5QsTcXxJsc/VSwm1KNU-VI/AAAAAAAALNY/5kEmcpk_Zxg/s1600/IMG_8239.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-K5QsTcXxJsc/VSwm1KNU-VI/AAAAAAAALNY/5kEmcpk_Zxg/s1600/IMG_8239.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              Begin spraying. Flip the doors over and spray the sides and fronts.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-yfqkjYahU2k/VSwm1QLmgyI/AAAAAAAALNg/ux_fYkKmkF0/s1600/IMG_8240.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-yfqkjYahU2k/VSwm1QLmgyI/AAAAAAAALNg/ux_fYkKmkF0/s1600/IMG_8240.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-CTj_5o-pXoc/VSwm1koBrmI/AAAAAAAALNs/rz5Koj6GBy0/s1600/IMG_8241.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-CTj_5o-pXoc/VSwm1koBrmI/AAAAAAAALNs/rz5Koj6GBy0/s1600/IMG_8241.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Iwa8ZPC-t7U/VSwm14KfkdI/AAAAAAAALNo/7gk7zOkSGxQ/s1600/IMG_8242.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Iwa8ZPC-t7U/VSwm14KfkdI/AAAAAAAALNo/7gk7zOkSGxQ/s1600/IMG_8242.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-_G7DxlhonLg/VSwm2TzLcaI/AAAAAAAALOQ/cZvGLZFM02o/s1600/IMG_8243.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-_G7DxlhonLg/VSwm2TzLcaI/AAAAAAAALOQ/cZvGLZFM02o/s1600/IMG_8243.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              When everything is dry, remove the tape and put everything back. Ta Da!!! A fabulous new kitchen for just about $300. It was so worth it!
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JVz0dkd8GpE/VSwm29biTDI/AAAAAAAALN4/dAzGdSyeM1A/s1600/IMG_8250.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JVz0dkd8GpE/VSwm29biTDI/AAAAAAAALN4/dAzGdSyeM1A/s1600/IMG_8250.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>

                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-pCa2ZaHhrJ8/VSwm3NwOioI/AAAAAAAALOA/TLq2-04z59I/s1600/IMG_8251.jpg" width="640"/>

             <meta content="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lqIOidmOLN0/VRAwjH_PvpI/AAAAAAAALLQ/TPJsEhkKUQ4/s1600/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-03-23%2Bat%2B9.25.31%2BAM.png" itemprop="image_url"/>

             <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
              <a href="http://www.doityourselfdivas.com/2015/03/diy-old-crib-into-toddler-bed.html">
               DIY: Old Crib Into Toddler Bed
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1839592451971575383" itemprop="description articleBody">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lqIOidmOLN0/VRAwjH_PvpI/AAAAAAAALLQ/TPJsEhkKUQ4/s1600/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-03-23%2Bat%2B9.25.31%2BAM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lqIOidmOLN0/VRAwjH_PvpI/AAAAAAAALLQ/TPJsEhkKUQ4/s1600/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-03-23%2Bat%2B9.25.31%2BAM.png" width="408"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <br/>
               That old crib was what I slept in as a baby, and so did all of my siblings. Of course crib safety has changed since then and I couldn't pass this down to my own kids without completely changing it. Converting it into a toddler bed was a great idea and my 2 year old loves his little bed. Let's get started.
               <br/>
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-MCAWOuIt1_k/VRAvw_6n9dI/AAAAAAAALLI/lpLQQqipOnQ/s1600/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-03-23%2Bat%2B9.21.44%2BAM.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
               <img border="0" height="560" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-MCAWOuIt1_k/VRAvw_6n9dI/AAAAAAAALLI/lpLQQqipOnQ/s1600/Screen%2Bshot%2B2015-03-23%2Bat%2B9.21.44%2BAM.png" width="640"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               Because cribs styles vary, this is just a general idea of what you can do to make an old crib into something new.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               The following is written by my engineer hubby.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               It is very important that you make an inventory of the parts you have available to you from the old crib before you begin to disassemble. For example, with the exception of a few fasteners, we determined no new parts (i.e. hardware, side rails) were needed for this conversion.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-8BzQS5gPSQk/U9fTyvUFyVI/AAAAAAAAKK4/Cmt--fEFINc/s1600/IMG_1161.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
               <img border="0" height="426" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-8BzQS5gPSQk/U9fTyvUFyVI/AAAAAAAAKK4/Cmt--fEFINc/s1600/IMG_1161.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Also before disassembling the crib, measure and mark your anticipated cut lines. We labeled the cut lines and measurements in between for clarity. Here are the toddler bed dimensions we followed:
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Headboard - 28" Tall
               </li>
               <li>
                Footboard - 18" Tall
               </li>
               <li>
                Length - same as original crib
               </li>
               <li>
                Width- same as original crib
               </li>
               <li>
                Distance from top of mattress support to ground - 7.5"
               </li>
               <li>
                Distance from top of safety rail to ground - 19.5"
               </li>
               <li>
                Safety rail height - 11.5"
               </li>
               <li>
                Length of safety rail - 26" or roughly half the length of the crib
               </li>
              </ul>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-5E0bxCC6QVo/U9fSzimUjfI/AAAAAAAAKJM/AM0z99WNiPY/s1600/IMG_3399.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-5E0bxCC6QVo/U9fSzimUjfI/AAAAAAAAKJM/AM0z99WNiPY/s1600/IMG_3399.jpg" width="426"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              Once you are comfortable with your plan, begin to carefully disassemble the crib. Organization of your work space is key during this step. As you can see in the photos below and depending on the style of the crib you are working on, a mountain of parts can begin to pile up.
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-VmU8o6mhJ1Y/U9fSzGCEN8I/AAAAAAAAKJQ/XeYIRMu5t_4/s1600/IMG_3401.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-VmU8o6mhJ1Y/U9fSzGCEN8I/AAAAAAAAKJQ/XeYIRMu5t_4/s1600/IMG_3401.JPG" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1t3MXiF5g9A/U9fS7GwEIsI/AAAAAAAAKJg/XgroaikvaNo/s1600/IMG_3402.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1t3MXiF5g9A/U9fS7GwEIsI/AAAAAAAAKJg/XgroaikvaNo/s1600/IMG_3402.jpg" width="426"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               We carefully removed all hardware and fasteners - utilizing plastic baggies to make sure we didn't lose anything.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-bf_vIhuxLfU/U9fT6TjTG8I/AAAAAAAAKLA/YMWmdyra49U/s1600/IMG_1165.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-bf_vIhuxLfU/U9fT6TjTG8I/AAAAAAAAKLA/YMWmdyra49U/s1600/IMG_1165.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               The challenging part about this project is to see how creative you can be in reusing old elements of the crib. Below is an example of how we used the top rail of the old side board to build the safety rail. It became tricky as we tried to align dowel holes in the bottom rail and the new location of the top rail. Once the safety rail was completely assembled, we rounded off an exterior, sharp corners.
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HMx_OU5ZGJc/U9fTCnjXZMI/AAAAAAAAKKA/Ot29dJCZgfY/s1600/IMG_3425.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HMx_OU5ZGJc/U9fTCnjXZMI/AAAAAAAAKKA/Ot29dJCZgfY/s1600/IMG_3425.JPG" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               Because this is a re-purposing project, be prepared to deal with little details like old dowel holes in the side board as shown in the following picture. In this situation, These dowels holes were no longer being used, so we had to plug each one..
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Dto7nKOumvY/U9fTCu0pDKI/AAAAAAAAKKE/zuDNWa_Tlhk/s1600/IMG_3426.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Dto7nKOumvY/U9fTCu0pDKI/AAAAAAAAKKE/zuDNWa_Tlhk/s1600/IMG_3426.JPG" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               Frequently dry fit your project together during the build process. Here is an example of the footboard and partially finished headboard. Only glue/permanently fasten pieces together when you are happy with how all dependent elements interact together.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Ct0yIBaMVU4/U9fS75FR9nI/AAAAAAAAKJs/iudBpKApTb8/s1600/IMG_3410.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Ct0yIBaMVU4/U9fS75FR9nI/AAAAAAAAKJs/iudBpKApTb8/s1600/IMG_3410.JPG" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               The most rewarding part in my opinion is applying the finish. We used an oil based stain with a urethane sealant
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-DAMP4A5ZBLI/U9fTJ5zH6eI/AAAAAAAAKKQ/w8g5kO965yE/s1600/IMG_3427.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-DAMP4A5ZBLI/U9fTJ5zH6eI/AAAAAAAAKKQ/w8g5kO965yE/s1600/IMG_3427.JPG" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               And finally, the finished product.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-I7tuaUq3mBU/VRAuHfAu82I/AAAAAAAALK8/2QS0BNvI1kA/s1600/FullSizeRender.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="351" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-I7tuaUq3mBU/VRAuHfAu82I/AAAAAAAALK8/2QS0BNvI1kA/s1600/FullSizeRender.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <span style="text-align: center;">
               Don't forget the reason for repurposing this old crib is two-fold; adding new life to an old, well-used piece of furniture and providing our kids with a safe place to rest. Make sure all exterior corners are eased, all hardware/fasteners are well protected, and that the finish is smooth and without burrs.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
