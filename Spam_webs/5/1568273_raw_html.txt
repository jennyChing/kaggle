         <h2 class="title">
          Remembering a Werewolf Classic: The Howling
         </h2>
         <div class="postmeta-primary">
          <span class="meta_date">
           April 09, 2012
          </span>
          <span class="meta_author">
           Dr. Monstrosity
          </span>
          <span class="meta_comments">
           <a href="http://worldsofweird.com/remembering-a-werewolf-classic-the-howling/#respond" title="Comment on Remembering a Werewolf Classic: The Howling">
            No comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="entry clearfix">
          <img alt="The-Howling-movie-poster" class="alignleft featured_image wp-post-image" height="165" src="http://worldsofweird.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/The-Howling-movie-poster-300x165.jpg" title="The-Howling-movie-poster" width="300"/>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Howling
           </strong>
           (1981)
           <br/>
           Movie Review
          </p>
          <p>
           Lets take a walk down memory lane back to the time of cassette tapes, rotary phones, and the birth of cable television.  The eighties, where I spent my formative years headbanging, reading sci fi, and burning stuff in my backyard.
           <em>
            And
           </em>
           going to my friends house to watch horror movies on cable television.  Yeah he had the premium channels, and thats where I first saw
           <em>
            The Howling
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            The Howling
           </em>
           is one of the premiere werewolf movies, although it was overshadowed by
           <em>
            An American Werewolf in London
           </em>
           which was released in the same year.  Clearly,
           <em>
            An American Werewolf in London
           </em>
           is a better film and more fondly remembered, but dont forget about
           <em>
            The Howling,
           </em>
           its viscously good!
          </p>
          <p>
           Early on we meet serial killer (umm, werewolf) Eddie Quist (Robert Picardo).  Hes pestering newswoman Dee Wallace (Karen White).  After a while we get to the juicy bit, the big change!  Eddies metamorphosis sequence is damn scary even today and certainly revolutionary for the time.  I love the bubbling skin and growing nails.  I also dig the creepy mid-change contorted rictus, bulging eyes, and puffed up chest.  And its all kicked off by Eddie digging out a piece of his brain in front of a horrified Dee Wallace.  Eewwwww!
          </p>
          <p>
           Other notable scenes are the chopping off of a werewolfs hand, a brutal throat chomping, and a werewolf sexual encounter with full frontal nudity.  And I got a chuckle from the Forrest Ackerman cameo complete with Forry toting a couple of copies of his mag
           <em>
            Famous Monsters of Filmland
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            The Howling
           </em>
           is director Joe Dantes crowning achievement in a career began under the tutelage of the legendary Roger Corman.  His other memorable film was the horror-comedy The Gremlins (a Steven Spielberg production).  Interestingly, the effects on
           <em>
            The Howling
           </em>
           were originally to be handled by one of the masters in the field, Rick Baker.  Baker ended up working on
           <em>
            An American Werewolf in London
           </em>
           leaving
           <em>
            The Howling
           </em>
           to be handled by Robin R. Bottin, one of Bakers employees.  Bottin certainly rose to the task.  He was obviously a major talent and went on to do most of the effects for John Carpenters
           <em>
            The Thing
           </em>
           , one of the greatest and grisliest sci-fi horror classics of them all!
          </p>
          <p>
           The Howling is a gruesome and groovy werewolf classic.  If youve never seen it, go for it.  If you saw it back in the day, its a great trip down a dark and shadowy memory lane.
          </p>

           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Smile!
           </p>

           </p>
