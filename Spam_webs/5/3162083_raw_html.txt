          <blockquote>
           <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
            <span style="color: #993366;">
             25 Things I learned from PC Games: Who says they are Waste of Time?
            </span>
           </h3>
          </blockquote>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <em>
            25 Things I learned from PC Games: I have played PC games since I was 10 years old, still remember the first games I played on PC. Prince and Break out were some of the very old games which use to run from a floppy drive. They were magical to play and it felt out of the world to press keyboard keys for the first time in life. Computers were not so common in early 90s. All these years have played many games and learned many new things from Games, played on PC as well as consoles.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="25 Things I Learned From PC Games" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-8083" height="605" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/25-Things-I-Learned-From-PC-Games.jpg" width="805"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Let me share with you 25 Things I learned from Playing Games:
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Why should youplay more PC Games? Lets See what they taught me.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            1) Total WAR
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Total War taught me a lot about the medieval history of Europe and UK, the later versions of the game taught about Alexander and Roman Empire.Also give good lessons in managing resources, supply chain, managing economy and creating army and strategy. Excellent Game Series. They also are great to learn the world map and which country is located where.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="25 Things I learned from PC Games" class="alignnone wp-image-8076 size-medium" height="260" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Total-War-600x260.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            2) Capitalism LAB
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Capitalism LAB is another great game to learn some elementary economics. I learned a lot about money management, cash flow and supply and demand from it.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="Capitalism LAB" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-8059" height="418" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Capitalism-LAB-600x418.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            3) Wing Commander: Privateer
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           An old Game, Privateer  a sci-fi space pirates and smuggling game that taught the basics of supply vs. demand and risk vs. reward.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="25 Things I learned from PC Games" class="alignnone wp-image-8077 size-medium" height="423" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Wing-Commander-Privateer-600x423.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            4)World Geography Games
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           World Geography games are flash based good games to teach (Geography), if you are weak in geography, can try your hands on it. When someone ask, where is Seychellesisland, in the class, you may be the first one to answer. A great game for kids to learn the world map in the easiest way.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="geo challenge game" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-8065" height="354" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/geo-challenge-game-600x354.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            5) Gran Turismo (Driving) &amp; Need For Speed
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Did I ever tell you I was unbeaten in the Reliance hosted national competition years back in the Need for Speed Underground 1. I learned good driving in real life as well from playing Need for Speed. Studies prove it improves your reflexes. Pilots are trained on flight simulators, most race drivers play racing games in real life to understand cornering, helps improvement of reflexes.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="25 Things I learned from PC Games" class="alignnone wp-image-8070 size-medium" height="336" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/need-for-speed-image-600x336.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            5) Formula 1
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Formula 1: Would have never figured out the rules without playing the game. A lot of things I learned, like DRS, pit stop strategy, fueling your cars at the right time, track weather impact. The most important thing was how to drive inch perfect.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="formula 1" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-8064" height="331" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/formula-1-600x331.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            6) Sports games  FIFA EA Sports
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           You wanna learn soccer, and remember all player names, teams, stadiums rules, FIFA 2015 is the game to play. All previous generation of EA sports FIFA taught me so much about the game, players, and rules.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="Fifa 2015" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-8063" height="367" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Fifa-2015-600x367.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            7) Age of Empires/Mythology (History/Mythology)
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Another classic like the Total War Series, it teaches you about History, mythology, Kings, inventions, multiplayer &amp; team cooperation.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="age of empires 3" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-8053" height="324" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/age-of-empires-3-600x324.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            8) Simutran
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           It is a transport simulator and the goal is to build an efficient transport network. I have played a lot of simulation games, but this deserves a special mention.Passenger transportation(Passenger behaves very much similar to real life, they have a destination and switch between stations and transports to reach it).
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="25 Things I learned from PC Games" class="alignnone wp-image-8073 size-medium" height="344" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Simutrans-600x344.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            9) Sid Meiers Civilization
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Sid Meiers Civilization series, in particular, really helps one visualize historical dynamics. While the civilizations and personalities are historical the game gives you an insight how the civilizations evolve over a period of time. Civilization, especially its tech tree. This was a truly great intro to world history, but it went even beyond that. the best way to learn about the Human history and the development of our technologies, social systems, and other key discoveries.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="civilization 4" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-8061" height="409" src="//candytech.in/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/civilization-4-600x409.jpg" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
