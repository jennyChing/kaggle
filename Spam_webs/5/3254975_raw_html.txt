             <p style="text-align: left;">
              This is an old standby that my family lovesI have made it for years. My friend, Mindy George, someone I knew back in college (20+ years ago!)but have since lost track of, gave me this recipe. It is easy, wonderful, hearty, and much-loved at my house!
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2 style="text-align: center;">
              <strong>
               French DipAu Jus Sandwiches
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="french dip sandwich" class="aligncenter wp-image-116" height="196" src="http://www.lifebetweenthekitchenandthecoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/french-dip-sandwich-300x168.jpg" width="350"/>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Slow cook
              </strong>
              the following ingredients in a crock pot for six hours on low:
             </p>
             <p>
              1 Chuck
              <strong>
               roast (beef)
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              1 cup of water
             </p>
             <p>
              1 packet of dry au jus mix (found at the store near gravy mixes)
             </p>
             <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
             </div>
             <p>
              1/4 cup dried minced onions
             </p>
             <p>
              Seasonwith onion powder, black pepper, and salt
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <a href="http://www.lifebetweenthekitchenandthecoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/french-dip-roast.jpg">
               <img alt="french dip roast" class="size-medium wp-image-117 aligncenter" height="207" src="http://www.lifebetweenthekitchenandthecoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/french-dip-roast-300x207.jpg" width="300"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              After slow-cooking for six hours, the roast will be very tender. You can either shred it with a fork or use a kitchen scissors to cut it into bits (just stick those scissors in the pot!). Using a slotted spoon, strain the meat and put it on sliced hard rolls. Add a slice of cheese to the sandwich if you wish. Pour a littleextra liquid into small dishes for
              <strong>
               au jus
              </strong>
              sauce to dip the sandwiches. It is amazing!
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
