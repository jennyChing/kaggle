       <h1 class="entry-title">
        <a href="http://www.runaddicts.net/tips-tricks/ice-bath-therapy-speed-up-recovery-and-enhance-performance" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Ice Bath Therapy: Speed up Recovery and Enhance Performance">
         Ice Bath Therapy: Speed up Recovery and Enhance Performance
        </a>
       </h1>
       <div class="post">
        <p id="writeforus">
         Interested in writing for RunAddicts.net?
         <a href="http://www.runaddicts.net/write-for-us" target="_blank" title="Become a writer">
          Get started now!
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you havent heard or just plain curious about Ice bath therapy, then youre in the right place. World class athletes have adopted this practice, so begs the question, why shouldnt you? Maybe youve tried it once or twice, and you didnt see any changes at all. Well, are you sure you did it right?
         <span id="more-118">
         </span>
         <strong>
          Ice bath recovery, when done properly and regularly, will speed up the recovery process of your body as well as improving your overall athletic performance
         </strong>
         . Ice bath therapy is ideally done in hydrotherapy pools for the best results.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Why should I take a bath with ice? Its freezing!
        </h2>
        <p>
         Yes, it is chilling. Although most of us dont like a cold shower,
         <strong>
          an ice bath right after an intense running session can actually do wonders for your body
         </strong>
         . Its a fact that right after an intense activity, like a long run or a set of short sprints, your muscles experience microtrauma. These are small tears in the muscle fibers, which are perfectly normal for runners.  Youre probably feeling some of it when your muscles hurt after an exhausting run, but you wont feel every tear since they are, as the name implies, micro.
        </p>
        <p>
         Because of the very low temperature,
         <strong>
          ice bath also becomes a great treatment for muscle soreness, strain, and inflammation
         </strong>
         . It also prevents the breakdown of muscle tissues. The cold water will stimulate muscle cells to start repairing the muscle tears.
        </p>
        <p>
         As you immerse yourself in the ice bath, youll get relief from your sore muscles, lets say your calves. The good thing about the ice bath is that your other leg muscles are also being treated in the process, like your hamstring, gluteus, and quadriceps. Thus, it gives your body optimal conditions for recovery.
        </p>
        <h2>
         How can I do it at home? I dont have a hydrotherapy pool
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          You dont have to be an owner of a five thousand dollar hydrotherapy pool for you to enjoy ice bath therapy
         </strong>
         . In fact, youll just need a bath tub, water, and a lot of ice packs. Here are the steps that you can take to conduct ice bath therapy at home:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Prepare a lot of ice cubes or packs
           </strong>
          </em>
          <br/>
          Remember that your target temperature for an ice bath is from
          <strong>
           12 to 15 Celsius
          </strong>
          . You can make your own if you have a decent freezer, or you can buy some at the nearest convenience store.
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Fill the tub with cold water before you run
           </strong>
          </em>
          <br/>
          Put enough water to fill up to your waists so that the whole lower body can benefit from the therapy.
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Right after your run, add ice
           </strong>
          </em>
          <br/>
          If you have a thermometer, you should measure how cold the tub is before going in.
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Slowly step in the tub and prepare for the extremely cold water
           </strong>
          </em>
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h2>
         Things to remember
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Dont stay too long in the tub
           </strong>
          </em>
          <br/>
          Ten minutes should be more than enough time to stay in the tub. Stay for more than twenty and youll risk suffering from cold-induced muscle damage.
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Your first few sessions will be the hardest
           </strong>
          </em>
          <br/>
          It would be a great idea if you would have something to keep you warm by your side, perhaps a cup of hot chocolate or tea. You might also want to find something to do while under ice bath therapy. You could bring a running book or a magazine with you.
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Take a warm bath or shower around 30 minutes to an hour later
           </strong>
          </em>
          <br/>
          Muscles, along with the tissues, have a tendency to become stiff and tense in extreme cold.
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           <strong>
            There are times when you might want to jump out of the tub because you cant handle the cold
           </strong>
          </em>
          <br/>
          I would suggest that you try your best to handle it. Keep yourself motivated by keeping in mind that this therapy will help your muscles recover, thus, possibly allowing you to have a better performance in your next run.
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Extremely cold ice baths, colder than the advised temperature, could result to fainting
           </strong>
          </em>
          <br/>
          Its always best to check the temperature from time to time. Its also better if you let someone know that youre in the tub with ice. Do this for safety reasons.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Now, youre quite ready to take an ice bath. The therapy will surely help you reach your athletic goals, whatever they may be. Famous athletes will tell you that ice bath therapy works great. They believe that ice bath therapy gives them speedy recovery, and at the same time, helps in preventing injuries.
        </p>
        <h2>
         So what are you waiting for?
        </h2>
        <p>
         If you have a competition on schedule, you might want to consider taking ice baths to make sure youre 100% ready for the big day. Quickly repair that muscle damage through regular ice baths after intense running.
        </p>

         <div class="comment-body">
          <p>
           I have been using ice bath therapy after longer runs for about a year now. I have noticed a signifcant improvement in recovery time, and a lowered incidence of injuries. I dont know what the ideal range for water temperature should be. I generally use two five pounds of ice per bath. I suggest starting with just cold water from the tap and then working up to the addition of ice. Look for results and adjust accordingly to realize desired benifits. You will know if its working or not. I would also start with no more than 10 minutes, up to 15 minutes ultimately is plenty. There is a sign when you have been in the bath long enough. Your leggs will go from feeling cold and begin feeling warm. At this point you are done. Since this will very from individual to individual, you might want to use this as a sign to end your bath.
          </p>
