        <h1 class="entry-headline">
         <span class="entry-headline-text">
          Parenting Advice  Children say the darndest things!
         </span>
        </h1>
       </div>
       <p class="post-meta">
        <span class="post-info-author">
         Author:
         <a href="http://www.parentinginmotion.com/author/shainab86/" rel="author" title="Posts by Mommy">
          Mommy
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="post-info-date">
         Saturday April 11th, 2015
        </span>
        <span class="post-info-comments">
         <a href="http://www.parentinginmotion.com/2015/04/11/kids-say-the-darndest-things/#comments">
          6 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="post-info">
        <p class="post-category">
         <span class="post-info-category">
          <a href="http://www.parentinginmotion.com/category/parenting/" rel="category tag">
           Parenting
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="post-tags">
        </p>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        I have to share an awesome story with parents out there! In the world of parenting, we know that childrensay the darndest things, I had to
        <a href="http://www.parentinginmotion.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/sassy-portrait-306178-m-150x1501.jpg">
         <img alt="sassy-portrait-306178-m-150x150" class=" size-full wp-image-843 alignright" height="150" src="http://www.parentinginmotion.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/sassy-portrait-306178-m-150x1501.jpg" width="150"/>
        </a>
        sharemy own experience!
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        I have this kiddo (for purposes of confidentiality we will name him Timmy, creative right?) that I worked with for about a year. Timmy is a 6-year-old child with Autism and recently started to enjoy pretend play with me while I was in his home for therapy. The following story is a funny example of what children are capable of picking up. My advice for this article is fairly simple  be mindful of what you say or do in front of your children. They understand more than you think.
       </p>
       <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
       </h3>
       <p>
        <span id="more-214">
        </span>
       </p>
       <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
        A bit of background
       </h3>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Children on the Autism spectrum can sometimes have difficulty with pretend play if not modeled in a systematic way. When developing his program, Timmys mommy expressedto me that she wanted him to learn how to engage in pretend play so that he could keep up with the other children on the playground atschool. Anytime we engaged in a pretend play segment at home, I always tried to gather as much information from Timmy about the scenario we were creating so that I could report back to his mommy and she could talkto him about it again.
       </p>
       <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
        Here is where it gets good!
       </h3>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        One Monday after school I was at his home for therapy. We began pretend play and I had him set up the scenario for me. Timmy where are we going? (pretending to drive a car). Timmy typically replies with answers such as the store, the zoo, the beach, and so on. Today he let me know we were going to a restaurant! Great! I thought, We have only pretended restaurant once, a local pizza place, lets see what he picks today!
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        We are driving along in our pretend car, park, and walk into our pretend restaurant. He quickly notes that I am the guest and he is the chef. He kindly asks me what would you like to drink? Last time we played this I was offered typical children-friendly beverage like orange juice, water and milk. Today when I asked what do you have to drink? he quickly replied beer or water. Trying to control my laughter I said Ill take a water thanks. He came back with my water (an empty little plastic toy cup) and said here you go, what do you want to eat? I was afraid to ask but remembered how proud I was of his progress towards learning to engage in pretend play. What do you have to eat? I asked. Without hesitation he replied chicken strips or wings. Oye! Wings would be great thanks Timmy. Timmy went to the kitchen to prepare the wings using his play food set. After about 30 seconds he came running to me and said here Ms. Shaina take these to your table. When I asked him why I needed to take them to the table he stated because the girls need to take the food and drinks to the table. Timmy where are we right now? I asked. Timmy replied Hoots. Oh man, is that what he remembers from the word Hooters?
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        I praised Timmy for a job well done and told him that he could have a 5-minute break. I went to mom who was in the other room with Timmys younger brother. I asked mom if they went out to eat this weekend and she noted that dad took Timmy out for lunch the day before. When I inquired about where this was mom didnt know and began to grow concerned. I let her know that I am pretty sure dad took him to Hooters for lunch. Mom told me I was crazy and called dad who was at work. BUSTED! Sure enough dad admitted he took the boys to Hooters for lunch because he was craving wings and never in a million years thought either boy would understand anything that was going on. Timmy sure gave them a parenting lesson they will never forget!
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Stay tuned for more tips and parenting advice! Next time we are talking all about choices!
       </p>

              <p>
               Hi Nicole! Thanks so much for stopping by and sharing that with me! Pretend play is such a great outlet! I have always pushed therapists to incorporate it into session as much as possible, especially for kiddos who have long, overly structured days at school :/ The pretend play that I come up with for kiddos is nothing fancy. Kids have the greatest imaginations! Some ideas depending on your kiddos age: going on a treasure hunt (you can place real coins or random little toys around the house or backyard or even a park), going on an adventure like a jungle cruise or safari ride (I have placed stuffed animals all along the house while the kiddo isnt looking and done a lot of back and forth story telling this way), pretend restaurant (you can use plastic toy foods or even real foods and teach them to pretend to cook and eat it), pretend school, pretend zoo, using action figures to act out their day at school, playing superheros with props such as capes (one of my kiddos had few resources but he had these bead necklaces so we put different colors together, mine was green so I was the hulk and his was red so naturally he picked spider man!). The list is endless! I am sort of a kid at heart so I have been able to come up with a lot of ideas over the years. Feel free to let me know how old your little one is and his current interests I can help you come up with even more ideas!! Thanks so much Nicole!
