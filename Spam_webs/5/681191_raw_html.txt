             <h2 class="post-title">
              <a href="#">
               Machine learning magic open sourced by Facebook
              </a>
             </h2>
             <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="Machine learning magic open sourced by Facebook" property="dc:title">
             </span>
             <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="0" datatype="xsd:integer" property="sioc:num_replies">
             </span>
             <div class="metas">
              <div class="default-date">
               <div class="date">
                <p>
                 Thu, Apr 30, 2015 - 10:28
                </p>
               </div>
               <div class="comments">
                <p>
                 <i class="fa fa-comments-o">
                 </i>
                 0 Comments
                </p>
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="clear">
             </div>
             <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
              <div class="field-items">
               <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                <div class="highlight_news">
                 Facebook has open sourced its tools that were used with the Torch framework as a part of its strive to become a cardinal and leading force in AI.
                </div>
                <p>
                 The
                 <a href="http://www.attuneww.com/services/consulting.html">
                  <strong>
                   open source
                  </strong>
                 </a>
                 engineering efforts by Facebook are broad enough to give a slight pause to even its seasoned and veteran haters including Chef Cookbooks, Hadoop HBase improvements, and the Hack programming language &amp; the virtual machine.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Recently, the AI Research division of Facebook has added to the list of
                 <a href="http://www.attuneww.com/services/consulting.html">
                  <strong>
                   open source
                  </strong>
                 </a>
                 projects by open sourcing its series of deep learning or machine learning modules that were created by leveraging the Torch scientific computing framework.
                </p>
                <p>
                 As per a blog post provided by the Facebook, these modules subsume GPU-optimized modules for the large ConvNets (Convolutional Nets) along with the networks with the sparse activations which are generally used in the NLP (Natural Language Processing) applications.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The Convolutional Nets are primarily used in the video and image recognition technologies while the latter networks are majorly used for creating the algorithms which can train themselves on the newer data without requiring an inordinate amount of guidance from people.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Facebook now is banking on its investments which it made in machine learning, and which is now paying off in various categories. Apart from leveraging the machine learning for some obvious moneymaking strategies such as intelligent ad placement, the company aims at making a distinct name for it as an overall machine learning/ AI pioneer and has already hired superior and major-league talent. It brought two renowned professors hailing from New York University, Rob Fergus and YamnLeCuin on board around the end of 2013 for leading the AI research initiative and efforts of Facebook in the facilities of the New York City, London and California.
                </p>
                <p>
                 However, what is not completely clear is the long term picture of the plans of Facebook in order to push the envelope in ML and AI. In the scenario most likely, the innovations of the company in those fields shall be rolled back to its social network, where the applications of the company for the work will stay relatively closely guarded, even when the tools and algorithms are realised as the
                 <a href="http://www.attuneww.com/services/consulting.html">
                  <strong>
                   open source
                  </strong>
                 </a>
                 projects.
                </p>
