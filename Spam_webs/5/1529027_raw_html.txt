        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         How to Dry Flowers
        </h2>
        <p>
         I love flowers, when theyre nice and freshand pretty..and alive. When they start slumping over, it makes me want to slump over too. Its soooooooooo depressing when they start dying. Most of the time I just dump them straight in the trash to avoid a complete depression but sometimes Ill just dry them out to make potpourri.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Did I just spell that right? Potporriepotpourrie? I know it has a T in it.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The first time I ever dried a flower, I was as sappy then as I am now. Imagine a 12 year old, hormonal, cry at the drop of a hat  pre-teen girl. That was me. My grandma had passed and I wanted to keep a rose from the service. My mom showed me how to dry flowers, and now that same dried flower from Grandmas service is in a box with some other keepsakes upstairs.20 some odd years later.
        </p>
        <p>
         Okayyyyy.25 years later. Sheesh. Excuse me while I lather some wrinkle cream all over my face.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its so easy to do. Ive got a dozen roses that my sweet feller bought me for Valentines Day. The day that they slumped
         <em>
          (they were as dead as a door nail)
         </em>
         , I instantly yanked them out of their pretty vase and hung them to dry. Im planning to make potpourri with them and theyre now
         <a href="http://sweetpenniesfromheaven.com/tips-closet-organization-including-closet-storage-ideas-solutions/" target="_blank" title="Tips on Closet Organization Including Closet Storage Ideas and Solutions">
          hanging in my closet
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh yeah  I googled potpourri and I did spell it right! Booyah!
        </p>
        <p>
         Other people like to craft with dried flowers, some like to decorate with them. But me, I like potpourri. Now that I know I spelled it right, I cant quit saying it. Po-purr-eeeeee!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://sweetpenniesfromheaven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/how-to-dry-flowers-2.jpg">
          <img alt="how to dry flowers" class="aligncenter" height="428" src="http://sweetpenniesfromheaven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/how-to-dry-flowers-2-1024x680.jpg" width="645"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Heres what youll need:
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Your dead-as-a-door-nail-flowers
         </li>
         <li>
          Paper Towels
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;field-keywords=scissors&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;rh=i%3Aaps%2Ck%3Ascissors&amp;sprefix=scisso%2Caps&amp;tag=sweepennfromh-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Daps" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;field-keywords=scissors&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;rh=i%3Aaps%2Ck%3Ascissors&amp;sprefix=scisso%2Caps&amp;tag=sweepennfromh-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Daps', 'Scissors');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Scissors
          </a>
          (or knife)
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;field-keywords=string&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;rh=i%3Aaps%2Ck%3Astring&amp;tag=sweepennfromh-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Daps" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;field-keywords=string&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;rh=i%3Aaps%2Ck%3Astring&amp;tag=sweepennfromh-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Daps', 'String');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           String
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;field-keywords=plastic%20clothes%20hangers&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;rh=i%3Aaps%2Ck%3Aplastic%20clothes%20hangers&amp;tag=sweepennfromh-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Daps" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;field-keywords=plastic%20clothes%20hangers&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;rh=i%3Aaps%2Ck%3Aplastic%20clothes%20hangers&amp;tag=sweepennfromh-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Daps', 'Plastic Clothes Hangers');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Plastic Clothes Hangers
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Heres How to Dry Flowers:
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Take flowers out of the vase and dry stems really well with paper towels.
         </li>
         <li>
          Cut off any thorns with scissors or knife. {Im acting like your mother here.PLEASE dont cut yourself!}
         </li>
         <li>
          Remove dead leaves from the stems.
         </li>
         <li>
          Cut one string for each flower. Cut the strings to about 7-10 inches in length.
         </li>
         <li>
          Tie string to stem (upside down), then tie the flower to the bottom of a clothes hanger.You can hang several flowers from one hanger, you just dont want them touching. Make sure theyre hanging upside down.
         </li>
         <li>
          Place hanger(s) in a dim and dry spot. Closets are great. Somewhere with no humidity.
         </li>
         <li>
          It may take 2-6 weeks before theyre completely dry.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <img alt="how to dry flowers" class="aligncenter wp-image-60021" height="430" src="http://sweetpenniesfromheaven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/drying-flowers-674x1024.jpg" width="283"/>
         The lighting is bad, but I just snapped this photo of these roses hanging from a hanger in my closet. I pulled it out of the closet and hung it on the closet door so thered be enough light just for the photo, then I put them right back in the dark.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          TIP: If you hang them in a closet, just make sure theyre not touching clothes or anything else. There are a couple of reasons for that. First  you dont want the color of the flowers bleeding onto any clothes. Secondly, you may want to retain the shape of the flower. If its bumped up against something, it can flatten or mishape the flower.
         </p>
         <p>
          TIP: Whatever you use them for, make sure that theyre placed away from humid spaces (even after theyre dry) because they can hold moisture and mold. You dont want thatyuck!
         </p>
         <p>
          TIP: Keep away from sunlight (even around windows) because the sun can and will fade the color of the flowers.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
