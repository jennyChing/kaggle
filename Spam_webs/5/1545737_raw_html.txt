        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Episode 1: An Overview  Battle Lines
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-date">
          <a href="http://enmitydocumentary.com/en/episode-1-overview/" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-05-19T21:24:15+00:00">
            May 19, 2014
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <span class="author vcard">

            Amazing Discoveries
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="comments-link">

          </a>
         </span>

        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Episode One of our new documentary, the Enmity Series is beginning to take shape. In the first episode,we introduce the concept of the war between good and evil  a foundational concept that will run through each episode as a unifying thread  and we examine this enmity right at its point of origin. Without enmity, we, and this world, would have already been extinguished. But with enmity, a natural tension results between good and evil and this enmity has expanded further into a declaration of war.
        </p>
        <p>
         The idea of enmity is first introduced in Genesis in the setting of the Garden of Eden shortly after the creation of the world. However, the Bible tells us the enmity didnt have its start there but began even before planet Earth was created. In the first episode, we peek into heaven to examine what really happened therewhen Lucifer rebelled against God and was cast out of heaven.
        </p>
        <p>
         We know that when the world was made, Lucifer was already at enmity withGod and came to Eve, Gods created child, with a concoction of lies and deception. Because Adam and Eve ate the forbidden fruit, our world became a very different place than it might have been. In reality, Eves simple act was nothing short of revolutionary, moving the conflict from heaven to new territory. In Episode one, we consider how God dealt with the crisis.
        </p>
        <p>
         As we examine this war between God and Lucifer, we are faced with someintriguing questions:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <em>
           What if Adam and Eve had never sinned? How would Gods plan have worked out?
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           What is heaven like? Why did God create angels and how do they relate to us?
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           How great a threat was Lucifers rebellion to Gods government? How did he manage to steal the hearts of fully one third of the angels?
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           What was Lucifers original argument against God anyway?
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           Why didnt God just kill him for his rebellion? Why did He even let him continue to exist and infect earth with his evil insinuations?
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           What is our part in this great drama? Do we have any contribution to make at all or are we just pawns caught in the middle?
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           Why were we even created in the first place? Didnt God know we might sin? Hes God. He must have understood the risk, right?
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           How much of the mess we are in is actually Gods fault?
          </em>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Stay tuned for progress on this exciting new project from Amazing Discoveries.
        </p>
       </div>
