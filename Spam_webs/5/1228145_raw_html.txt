                </p>
                <p>
                 Locate the current driver for your touchpad from Dell Support by entering your service tag number here:
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.dell.com/support/drivers/us/en/19/DriversHome/ShowProductSelector?fkey=Drivers_PS">
                  http://www.dell.com/support/drivers/us/en/19/DriversHome/ShowProductSelector?fkey=Drivers_PS
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 SAVE the download to somewhere convenient like your desktop (you can put it somewhere else later)
                </p>
                <p>
                 1) From the control panel under Programs / Uninstall a Program locate the Synaptics Pointing Device Driver and completely uninstall the software. but DO NOT RESTART if prompted. Instead select the restart later option.
                </p>
                <p>
                 2) From the control panel under Hardware and Sound locate the Device Manager in the Devices and Printers category and click on it (device manager may require admin permission.)
                </p>
                <p>
                 3) Locate Mice and other Pointing Devices and click to expand. You may see the Synaptics PS/2 port touch pad still there. If so right click and select Uninstall. okay the warnings. when prompted to restart use the TAB key on your keyboard to highlight the restart option and then press the Enter Key on keyboard.
                </p>
                <p>
                 **After you have restarted, the touchpad will operate with limited functionality, without gestures and preference settings. THATS PERFECT for now.
                </p>
                <p>
                 4) Repeat steps 1,2 &amp;3 BUT THIS TIME, Select the mouse that's listed there...probably HID compliant mouse. Restart computer as in step 3
                </p>
                <p>
                 5) Windows will automatically "discover" new hardware and install it for HID Compliant Mouse on PS/2 port so the touchpad is back to it's limited state. THATS what we want!
                </p>
                <p>
                 6) Double click on your downloaded Synaptics Dell Touchpad driver and install.
                </p>
                <p>
                 IMPORTANT NOTE: if your driver does not launch immediately after expanding locate the "setup.exe" This will be located in C:\Dell\Drivers\(5 digit alpha numeric identifier of your software)\ setup.exe. Restart normally if prompted. That's it!
                 <img alt="Big Smile" src="/emoticons/emotion-2.gif"/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 HERE"S WHY:
                </p>
                <p>
                 Whenever a SP or major upgrade to Windows is conducted the original operating system is fundamentally changed. One would hope driver support for the upgrade or SP would be automatic and in most cases it IS either automatic or available as an update. You should always use your My Dell program utility as your FIRST resource for updates to Dell products.Microsoft Windows Update only offers security and system-critical driver updates and usually only when the original manufacturer has stopped supporting a product.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The device driver (Dell device driver in this case) Installs to it's operating system and system configuration...which is why one driver works on multiple configurations. This procedure allows the driver install package a "fresh look" at the OS and system so in most cases resolves driver problems when the drivers aren't rendered completely obsolete by an update.  If this solution resolves your touchpad issue please drop a quick note. I'd really appreciate it. Thanks and Good Luck!
                </p>

               <div class="post-content user-defined-markup">
                <p>
                 I have bought a used laptop Dell 3137 about a month ago and everything worked fine. I used it for a month and after that it started problem of touchpad. On the same day before I have noticed the problem, I bought LCD screen cleaning liquid and cleaned my laptop screen as well as keyboard and touch-pad and palm support area. After noticing that the touch-pad is not working properly, just like its seized or like uncontrolled movement, I opened my laptop to figure out the problem. I opened the screws of the laptop from back side and opened it and When I removed the battery, i found that liquid on the back of the battery. I cleaned that liquid from battery and blew air in to the touch-pad with my mouth to remove entrapped liquid from it (do not do like this because its ineffective method but you can use hair drier). I assembled the laptop again and turned on the laptop but still the touch-pad did not work. I changed drivers, and did anything I could do but not effective. One thing more I want to mention that the touch-pad icon in the system tray was showing that something is still touched to the touch-pad which I guess was that liquid inside.
                </p>
                <p>
                 For a few days I used external mouse but after that when I used the touch-pad, its now working perfect. I guess the liquid inside got dried and now its working perfect.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The point for the whole story here is that the touch-pad has opened spaces around it and most probably everyone who's experiencing such problem has dropped some liquid into the touch-pad in very little drops form (may be hand sweat, wet fingers, water or any liquid) and due to which the touch-pad responses like your finger is touched with it.
                </p>
                <p>
                 I have also experienced such thing with a touch screen smart phone. When I touch wet fingers with it, it does not respond correctly.
                </p>
                <p>
                 To solve the problem, open your laptop from back side, remove the battery, and dry your touch-pad with hair drier. Hope your touch-pad starts responding correctly after the liquid inside fully gets dried. Also cover the opened space around touch-pad with a transparent plastic mobile screen protector to stop liquids entering the touch-pad.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Do not look for touch-pad drivers, its only hardware problem. Believe me.
                </p>

               <div class="post-content user-defined-markup">
                <p>
                 Hi,
                </p>
                <p>
                 SO, You're having problems with 8.1 and the Synaptics Dell Touchpad!? I wish I could say I've never seen this before. I won't open up with "Have you tried this?" Instead, I'm confident this will "fix what ails ya" There have been a wealth of problems with touchpads (Alps included) and not just on Dell systems, during the transition from windows 8 to 8.1 (I still can't figure out why they didn't just call it SP1) IN ANY EVENT&gt; give this a go and please get back to me after you've done it so I can share with others who are experiencing the same problem. I'll explain WHY these steps at the end...Go ahead peek ...I won't tell anyone!
                </p>
                <p>
                 Locate the current driver for your touchpad from Dell Support by entering your service tag number here:
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.dell.com/support/drivers/us/en/19/DriversHome/ShowProductSelector?fkey=Drivers_PS">
                  http://www.dell.com/support/drivers/us/en/19/DriversHome/ShowProductSelector?fkey=Drivers_PS
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 SAVE the download to somewhere convenient like your desktop (you can put it somewhere else later)
                </p>
                <p>
                 1) From the control panel under Programs / Uninstall a Program locate the Synaptics Pointing Device Driver and completely uninstall the software. but DO NOT RESTART if prompted. Instead select the restart later option.
                </p>
                <p>
                 2) From the control panel under Hardware and Sound locate the Device Manager in the Devices and Printers category and click on it (device manager may require admin permission.)
                </p>
                <p>
                 3) Locate Mice and other Pointing Devices and click to expand. You may see the Synaptics PS/2 port touch pad still there. If so right click and select Uninstall. okay the warnings. when prompted to restart use the TAB key on your keyboard to highlight the restart option and then press the Enter Key on keyboard.
                </p>
                <p>
                 **After you have restarted, the touchpad will operate with limited functionality, without gestures and preference settings. THATS PERFECT for now.
                </p>
                <p>
                 4) Repeat steps 1,2 &amp;3 BUT THIS TIME, Select the mouse that's listed there...probably HID compliant mouse. Restart computer as in step 3
                </p>
                <p>
                 5) Windows will automatically "discover" new hardware and install it for HID Compliant Mouse on PS/2 port so the touchpad is back to it's limited state. THATS what we want!
                </p>
                <p>
                 6) Double click on your downloaded Synaptics Dell Touchpad driver and install.
                </p>
                <p>
                 IMPORTANT NOTE: if your driver does not launch immediately after expanding locate the "setup.exe" This will be located in C:\Dell\Drivers\(5 digit alpha numeric identifier of your software)\ setup.exe. Restart normally if prompted. That's it!
                 <img alt="Big Smile" src="/emoticons/emotion-2.gif"/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 HERE"S WHY:
                </p>
                <p>
                 Whenever a SP or major upgrade to Windows is conducted the original operating system is fundamentally changed. One would hope driver support for the upgrade or SP would be automatic and in most cases it IS either automatic or available as an update. You should always use your My Dell program utility as your FIRST resource for updates to Dell products.Microsoft Windows Update only offers security and system-critical driver updates and usually only when the original manufacturer has stopped supporting a product.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The device driver (Dell device driver in this case) Installs to it's operating system and system configuration...which is why one driver works on multiple configurations. This procedure allows the driver install package a "fresh look" at the OS and system so in most cases resolves driver problems when the drivers aren't rendered completely obsolete by an update.  If this solution resolves your touchpad issue please drop a quick note. I'd really appreciate it. Thanks and Good Luck!
                </p>
