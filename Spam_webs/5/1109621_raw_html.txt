Hearing and understanding are basic human needs. Hearing problems and the resulting social consequences represent a challenge to society. We believe in a world, in which people with restricted hearing can communicate again without limitations thanks to advanced technology.&lt;br class="none" /&gt;&lt;br class="none" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Our Mission&lt;/strong&gt; &lt;br class="none" /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;

&lt;p&gt;Our every product pools highest standards of Swiss Engineering; ever since in 1946 Hans Gfeller Junior developed a hearing instrument  Switzerland&#8217;s first  for his father, the founder of Bernafon.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h1&gt;&lt;br class="none" /&gt;Swiss Engineering&lt;/h1&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;img style="margin: 10px;" alt="" src="http://www.bernafon.com/Consumers/AboutBernafon/NavigationAbstraction/Cons_Company/%7E/media/D0B3D0F0926B47C3A9D4B5098F07034A.ashx?w=140&amp;h=105&amp;as=1" width="140" height="105" align="left" /&gt;Bernafon introduced the worlds first digitally programmable hearing instrument in 1988. The introduction of such a revolutionary hearing system marked a quantum leap, and with it, Bernafon made a lasting impression in the hearing instrument industry. In 2002, Bernafon struck again, introducing the first Channel Free hearing system, a unique approach to signal processing.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Today, digital signal processing, improved miniaturization, as well as creative and innovative programming have resulted in increasingly complex systems with expanded customer benefits.&lt;/p&gt;
