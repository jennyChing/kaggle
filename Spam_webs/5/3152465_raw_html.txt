					&lt;h2&gt;Red White &#038; Blue Veggie Kabobs&lt;/h2&gt;
					&lt;span class="meta"&gt;By &lt;a href="http://foodandfam.com/author/admin/" title="Posts by Christi" rel="author"&gt;Christi&lt;/a&gt; &lt;span class="bullet"&gt;&bull;&lt;/span&gt; June 25, 2014 &lt;span class="bullet"&gt;&bull;&lt;/span&gt; &lt;a href="http://foodandfam.com/category/grilling/" title="View all posts in Grilling" rel="category tag"&gt;Grilling&lt;/a&gt;, &lt;a href="http://foodandfam.com/category/holiday/" title="View all posts in Holiday" rel="category tag"&gt;Holiday&lt;/a&gt;, &lt;a href="http://foodandfam.com/category/veggies/" title="View all posts in Veggies" rel="category tag"&gt;Veggies&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;

				&lt;div class="post-entry"&gt;
				
					&lt;div class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style addthis_32x32_style" addthis:url='http://foodandfam.com/red-white-blue-veggie-kabobs/' addthis:title='Red White &amp; Blue Veggie Kabobs ' &gt;&lt;a href="http://foodandfam.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Red-White-Blue-Veggie-Kabobs-Brightened.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article-int', 'http://foodandfam.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Red-White-Blue-Veggie-Kabobs-Brightened.jpg', '']);" class="addthis_button_facebook"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;a class="addthis_button_twitter"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;a class="addthis_button_email"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;a class="addthis_button_pinterest_share"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;a class="addthis_button_compact"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;a class="addthis_counter addthis_bubble_style"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;a&gt;&lt;img class="size-full wp-image-682 aligncenter" alt="Red-White-Blue-Veggie-Kabobs-Brightened" src="http://foodandfam.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Red-White-Blue-Veggie-Kabobs-Brightened.jpg" width="620" height="411"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The Fourth of July is right around the corner, and if you&#8217;re like us you&#8217;ll probably spend part of Independence Day grilling out. We usually have burgers and hot dogs for the Fourth, but like I mentioned in yesterday&#8217;s &lt;a href="http://foodandfam.com/ten-tasty-grilling-recipes/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article-int', 'http://foodandfam.com/ten-tasty-grilling-recipes/', 'Tasty Ten Grilling Recipes']);"   target="_blank"&gt;Tasty Ten Grilling Recipes&lt;/a&gt; post, I&#8217;m looking to shake up our grilling menu. I think these red white &amp; blue veggie kabobs will be the perfect addition, don&#8217;t you?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I was inspired by Gimme Some Oven&#8217;s beautiful &lt;a href="http://www.gimmesomeoven.com/rainbow-veggie-skewers-recipe/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.gimmesomeoven.com/rainbow-veggie-skewers-recipe/', 'rainbow veggie skewers']);"   target="_blank"&gt;rainbow veggie skewers&lt;/a&gt; to start thinking about taking advantage of colors in ingredients &#8211; I noticed that the purple potatoes in her skewers had a blue tone to them, and I decided to make those the blue contribution alongside the red peppers and white onions.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;span id="more-678"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;These kabobs are incredibly easy. You&#8217;ll need to boil the potatoes for just a bit to soften them, since potatoes will take longer than the onions and peppers to cook on the grill, and you&#8217;ll want to soak the skewers in water. Other than that, there are just a few quick steps and you&#8217;ll be ready to enjoy these delicious &#8211; and gorgeous &#8211; veggies.&lt;/p&gt;

   <ul>
    <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
     12-16 purple potatoes
    </li>
    <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
     2 white onions
    </li>
    <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
     4 red peppers
    </li>
    <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
     Olive oil
    </li>

  <div class="ERSInstructions">
   <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
    Instructions
   </div>
   <ol>
    <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
     Soak bamboo skewers in water for at least 30 minutes prior to assembling your kabobs.
    </li>
    <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
     Boil purple potatoes in salted water for about 10 minutes, or until slightly tender; drain and let cool.
    </li>
    <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
     Cut each onion into 12 wedges, and cut the red peppers into rectangular pieces similar in size to the onion wedges.
    </li>
    <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
     Cut the rounded portions off of the potatoes so you end up with a cubed shape; depending on the size of the potato you may be able to get 2-3 cubes per potato. Try to keep the cubes close to the same size as the onions and red peppers.
    </li>
    <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
     Slide three red pepper slices up to about 2 inches from the top of the skewer; follow with an onion wedge and a potato piece, then repeat the process one more time.
    </li>
    <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
     Fill all your skewers with the veggies, then brush with olive oil, sprinkle with salt &amp; grind black pepper over the tops of each one.
    </li>
    <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
     Place on a hot, oiled grill for about 10 minutes, making sure to flip the kabob over to the other side about halfway through.
    </li>
    <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
     Serve immediately or place on a plate covered with foil to retain heat for a bit until the other elements of your meal are ready to serve.
    </li>
   </ol>
