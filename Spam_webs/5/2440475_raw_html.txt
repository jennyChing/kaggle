              <br/>
              <br/>
              Kids Business Cards
             </strong>
             <br/>
             This next project is incredibly easy, fun, and even a bit inspiring. Have your kids create their own business cards using blank business cards (or regular paper cut into the appropriate size), and some colourful pencils, markers, stickers, and stencils. Whether its a princess, pilot or astronaut, these cards allow kids to get creative and excited about their dreams. This project will keep kids busy for hours and requires very minimal supervision.               Candy Necklace
             </strong>
             <br/>
             Your kids will really love this next craft, as it features the one thing they cant get enough of: candy. This is the easiest way to get your kids to join you in a crafting project, even if it is technically a bit of a bribe. Besides being a tasty project, it is also easy to make and very customizable. For a healthier twist, try including items like dried apple rings or multigrain cheerios.
             <a href="http://www.bulkbarn.ca/en-ca/index.html" target="_blank">
              Bulk Barn
             </a>
             is located less than an 8-minute drive away from our
             <a href="http://apartments.timbercreekcommunities.com/Ottawa/apartment_rentals/222-Maclaren-Street/b54c9d/en">

             </a>
             apartments, and has all the ingredients you will need for this tasty craft. For this necklace (or bracelet) you will need flavoured candy rings (such as peach or apple), circle cereal (i.e. Apple Jacks or Cheerios), and multi-colored candy string or licorice. Simply lie out your string, candy rings and cereal and have your kids customize their necklace. The full tutorial is available
             <a href="http://www.sheknows.com/living/articles/958107/diy-crafts-for-kids" target="_blank">
              here
             </a>
             , from She Knows Living.

             <strong>
              Paper Plate Aquarium
             </strong>
             <br/>
             Let your kids imagination run wild as they create a miniature underwater world out of a paper plate and colourful paper. For this craft you will need two light blue paper plates (or just plain ones painted light blue), coloured paper in your kids favourite colours, googley eyes, a handful of colourful beads, colourful markets, and glue. With an adults assistance, cut fish and plant shapes from the colourful paper and glue to the plate. Add the googley eyes to the fish, and glue the beads to the plate for some added sparkle and texture. Grab the colourful markers and decorate the fish with stripes, dots, or whatever else you can think of! The full tutorial is available from Kix Cereal
