      </h2>

       <p>
        This old world influenced wedding at the College of William and Mary in Virginia is one after my own heart. Starting with the old parchment look of the invitation suite combined with a monogrammed seal, this wedding is full of wonderful details. The ceremony was accompanied by a string quartet playing traditional melodies. Portraits were taken in the Governors Palace gardens in Colonial Williamsburg adding the perfect backdrop to these images taken by
        <a href="http://onthegobride.com/hmk9">
         Caitlin Gerres Photography
        </a>
        . Fall colors add a bit of rustic charm to the navy color palette and this couple also incorporated personal touches throughout their day. One of my favorites are the wine bottles marked with different anniversary dates so they could enjoy reading well wishes from their guests for years to come!
       </p>

       <p>
        <strong>
         What makes you an On the Go Bride?
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        I was in pharmacy school full time and working part time in a pharmacy-as well as planning a wedding.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         What did you find most challenging about planning your wedding as an On the Go Bride?
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        I often found I had to start planning projects and putting ideas together just to have to stop to do something else. I am fairly good at multi-tasking, but it became difficult to always pick back up where I had started. I became overwhelmed with ideas, and it was hard to decide what I wanted and didnt want. I also had an incredibly tight budget coming out of school with fairly large student loans. I had to find a way to be creative enough to save money, which everyone knows requires time. That was something I did not have a lot of.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         What made the wedding planning process easier or helped save you time?
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        I used pinterest a lot to find ideas on how to make things. I asked friends that had just gotten married to buy things from their weddings. Since some of that was made, it saved me time. I also took advantage of my days off from work when I did not have school or exams to study for. I would make a check-list of things to do and commit myself to doing one or two projects at a time. I also started making my burlap banners one year in advance. The things I knew I wanted, I went ahead and made. This allowed me to take my time, and continue to keep planning.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         What advice would you give to brides trying to plan their weddings while working?
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Take advantage of the time you have early during your engagement. Enjoy being engaged, but start planning early and dont over plan. In other words, dont get wrapped up in what everyone else has done and extravagant pictures. Think reasonably about what you want and your budget. If you can afford a wedding coordinator, I would suggest it. I couldnt afford one, but I did seek advice from others that had recently planned weddings. Your day will be exactly what YOU want it to be, and dont worry what anyone else says about anything. Its your day, so make it as creative or cheesy as you want.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         What special things or moments between you and your husband did you include in your wedding?
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        We did several small things to make our wedding special for us. We used a yellow umbrella for some photos that said Right Place, Right Time that we also used for our engagement photos. We fell in love watching How I Met Your Mother, so the yellow umbrella played a huge part for us (In case you dont know, the show is all about a guy trying to find the love of his life. The yellow umbrella always appears, because that is the woman he will marry, and she is revealed at the end of the show.) We also loved the Im Gonna Be (5000 Miles Song) from the show, and we stumbled upon a slow version that we played for our first dance song. It was beautiful. We had little details hidden, that no one else would know anything else about. We got married in a church on my old college campus that I love (William &amp; Mary), and he also proposed on the campus there. To tie into the colonial theme, we also had fifers-which we love to listen to in Colonial Williamsburg together. We did our wedding photos in Colonial Williamsburg, and we had little moments here and there that was just for us.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         What was your favorite memory from your wedding day?
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Walking down the aisle! My husband is not an emotional guy, but I caught him wiping a tear from his face as I made it to the front of the stage. This reconfirmed how much this man loved me and was just as happy as I was to be getting married on this day! It was the best memory I have ever had, and I will never forget the look in his eyes.
       </p>
