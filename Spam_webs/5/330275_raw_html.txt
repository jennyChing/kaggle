          Air, Trees, Water, and Murderous Animals
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p class="byline large">
         <a href="/contributor/KathyShaidle/250">
          by Kathy Shaidle
         </a>
         <a href="/contributors/contributor_feed/KathyShaidle/">
          <img class="leftX" height="12" src="/images/global/feed-32x32.png" width="12"/>

        </p>
        <p class="date">
         November 26, 2013
        </p>

        <div class="img_article" style="width:270px;background-color:#f9f9f9;">
         <img alt="Air, Trees, Water, and Murderous Animals" src="http://takimag.com/images/uploads/charles-manson-720x340.jpg" width="270"/>
         <br/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Charles Mansons 25-year-old girlfriend Star says theyre getting married, but the aging criminal ringleader scoffs at the idea.
        </p>
        <p>
         Isnt that just like a man?
        </p>
        <p>
         In the
         <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/culture/news/charles-manson-today-the-final-confessions-of-a-psychopath-20131121">
          <em>
           Rolling Stone
          </em>
          interview
         </a>
         thats got everyone talking, the 79-year-old cult leader calls Stars marriage claims a bunch of garbage.Were just playing that for public consumption.
        </p>
        <p>
         The crimes that made him infamous occurred in 1969, but well into a new century, Charles Manson still sells papers and apparently needs little to no introduction. When
         <a href="http://newsfeed.time.com/2013/11/22/charles-manson-has-a-girlfriend-and-you-dont/">
          <em>
           TIME
          </em>
         </a>
         picked up
         <em>
          Rolling Stone
         </em>
         s semi-scoop, it didnt even feel obligated to explain why he was in prison.
        </p>
        <p>
         Charles Manson is practically a brand, like Coke or Nike. Everyone knows what heor, more accurately, his Family of flaky young followers
         <a href="http://www.vancouversun.com/entertainment/Video+Infamous+Crimes+Charles+Manson+Family+Tate+LaBianca+Murders/9200925/story.html">
          did
         </a>
         , right?
        </p>
        <p>
         Everyone, it seems, except his young love.
        </p>
        <div class="pullquote">
         Manson simply takes leftist environmentalism and social justice to their logical conclusionmass genocide.
        </div>
        <p>
         At the of age 19, a friend turned Star on to Mansons jailhouse writings on radical environmentalism. She claims that at the time, shed never heard of the Tate-LaBianca Helter Skelter slayings that had shaken the world long before she was born. Even after Star found out that this Charlie fellow put the gore in Al Gore, she remained besotted.
        </p>
        <p>
         She now lives in Corcoran, California, all the better to visit her beloved, who resides in the state prison there. With her long, straight, center-parted brown hair and that x carved between her eyebrows, Star looks like shes just stepped out of a Spahn Ranch Family snapshot.
        </p>
        <p>
         No one even passingly familiar with the lefts twisted history can possibly be surprised that Manson presents himself as a typically misanthropic friend of the Earth. His ATWA philosophy contends that air, trees, water, and animals are the most sacred things on the planet, and all are being irresponsibly consumed by us greedy humans.
        </p>
        <p>
         Yes, even air. In his
         <em>
          Rolling Stone
         </em>
         interview, Manson justifies murder in the cause of saving the air from being inhaled by undeserving people. At one interval, he describes the (we hope) imaginary torture and killing of an infant to make his point, in terms so graphic the
         <em>
          Stone
         </em>
         s scribe cant bring himself to repeat them in print.
        </p>
        <p>
         To his credit (or something like it), at least Charles Manson has been preaching this anti-human ecology guff for decades. Back in the 1980s when he was still allowed to do television interviews, he railed on
         <a href="http://www.prisonplanet.com/charles-manson-global-warming-prophet.html">
          <em>
           The Today Show
          </em>
         </a>
         about the melting polar ice caps.
        </p>
        <p>
         Maybe I should have killed four or five hundred people, he continued, then I would have felt better, I would have felt like Id really offered society something. The truth is the planet Earth is dying.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you factor out all the murder chatter, Manson sounds like any number of far more wealthy, powerful, and respected progressives. Being crazier, hes also more candid: Manson simply takes leftist environmentalism and social justice to their logical conclusionmass genocide.
        </p>
        <p>
         (Although not quite: Note that neither Manson nor any of these other population bomb types weve been listening to since the 1970s ever has enough courage of their convictions to kill themselves, unless you count Jim Joness revolutionary suicide.)
        </p>
