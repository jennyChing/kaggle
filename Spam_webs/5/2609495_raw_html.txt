       <h2>
        <a href="http://afterbreakingup.com/have-you-said-thank-you-to-her-lately/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent link to Have You Said Thank You To Her Lately?">
         Have You Said Thank You To Her Lately?
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         Words of thanks for the unrelenting love of mothers and wives everywhere who do things we dont thank them for nearly often enough.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           If youre a husband, son or daughter, this is one for you!
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         I was driving home the other day. I had been out on errands all day and I couldnt wait to get home and have a shower.  It had been one of those busy old days, hot and sticky (summer comes home early in South Florida) and all I could think about was to be done with it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-297">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I wanted to be home and when I finally got there, I rushed upstairs for my long awaited shower. As I poured the last drop of shampoo onto the last remaining hair still standing on my head I could already feel the effect of the shower running over me, each drop of water acting as my own personal energy reservoir and it is a brand new man that came out of that shower, even if I say so myself! The heat of the day was gone, all was well in paradise again and I couldnt even remember that I had just run out of shampoo!
        </p>
        <p>
         The rest of the day passed like most do, bits by bits, pushed forward by the momentum of a family in motion until eventually it was time to say good night.  Soon, quiet settled down into our household, slowly reaching each and every one in an awe inspiring hush that always comes from the noise that was, the usual bubble of chatter and laughter (and sometimes tears) eventually exploding into gradual silence.
        </p>
        <p>
         And with the left over noises bouncing back and forth from bedroom to bedroom, it wasnt too long until each and every one of the sleepy souls of our gang began to give in to their nightly dreams, not without the gentle probing of mom of course, our very own
         <strong>
          Mama Ninja
         </strong>
         as we like to call her. Shortly after that, everyone was asleep apart from Mama Ninja who was still doing some motherly or wifely thing that all mothers and wives do.
        </p>
        <p>
         The next morning, as usual mom was first to rise and by the time I had ventured out of my bed, our youngest daughter (9) was already dressed, had already had breakfast, had brushed her teeth and brushed her hair.
         <strong>
          Now any mom will tell you that these 4 simple tasks are not that big of a deal but if you are a Dad, try to do that every morning
         </strong>
         knowing that school starts at 8am and tardiness isnt regarded as a cool thing nowadays!
        </p>
        <p>
         Soon after, they were both gone, leaving my other daughter (13) and I to our own devices!  It was time to make a move so at 7:30 am, a full hour and a half after my wife had woken up, I, being the man that I am, finally walked to the shower.  It was time to get up and start the day! It was time to be THE man of the house!
        </p>
        <p>
         And as I turned the water on, and waited for it to get to the right temperature, I remembered that I had used the last drop of shampoo the day before and how could I have forgotten to tell my wife (who else!) that I had run out.  Man!  I said in a loud silence (men will understand) I am not going to be able to wash my hair!  But before I could give in to any further lamentation, which I never admit to being good at, lo and behold there it was  A brand new bottle of shampoo!
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          How did she know? I marveled, inspired as I always am by this act of love, extraordinary in so many ways, not least because like most women who are mothers and wives, Mama Ninja cared so much, loved so much to the extend that she did these things without ever being asked, and rarely thanked for.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         I finished my shower got dressed and undertook my 30 seconds daily commute to my office. (Youve got to love working from home!)
        </p>
        <p>
         As the kitchen stands neatly on the way to my office, I made a quick stop to get coffee going, and of course there was coffee in the jar.  I opened the fridge, and there was milk as there has always been.  In fact everything that I like for breakfast was in the fridge, as if some miracle worker knew exactly when to replenish things.
        </p>
        <p>
         When my other daughter came down (she is 13 and starts school at 9:30) neatly dressed in fresh clothes that she had found in her closet, just as I had found my own, she too found everything she needed for her own breakfast.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once again, I was amazed and humbled, knowing that Mama Ninja did all this, for all of us, day after day, just like most mothers and wives also do!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Think about it if you are a husband, a daughter or a son! When was the last time we told our own Mama Ninja how thankful we really are?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thankful for all the things they do for us.
        </p>
        <p>
         For the love.
        </p>
        <p>
         And the little things we dont even notice
        </p>
        <p>
         Ill give you five examples but there are really hundreds
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          We go to the bathroom, reach out for toilet paper, and its there
         </li>
         <li>
          We want to brush our teeth, look for the tooth paste, and the tube is there
         </li>
         <li>
          We want milk for our coffee, and its there just where it should be
         </li>
         <li>
          We run out of shampoo, dont tell anyone and its there
         </li>
         <li>
          We reach for our favorite shirt, remember the lost button and find that its been replaced
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         And on and on.
        </p>
        <p>
         So for all those miraculous moments where everything a family needs is just there
        </p>
        <p>
         From each and everyone of us, your husbands and your kids
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          THANK YOU MAMA NINJA!
         </strong>
        </p>
