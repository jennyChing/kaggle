              I headed to
              <a href="http://microapp.laweekly.com/tacolandia" target="_blank">
               Tacolandia
              </a>
              , an event held at the historic El Pueblo de Los Angeles. There were unlimited tacos from 43 of L.A. and Mexico's taco vendors, plus desserts, tequila, and mariachi music. It was a hot day with lots of lines to stand in, so next year I'll be sure to bring a few more friends and lots of sunblock! But of course, the food was delicious and gorgeous, so it was absolutely worth it.

              <br/>
              Overall, it was a great event! The good news is that the food was absolutely spectacular, and the ticket price was amazing. I just wish it was longer! The not-so-good news is that the line to get in took about 45 minutes, even though I showed up a bit early to account for the difficult parking. The lines for the taco stands and drink stations were pretty long, and I even had one food blog colleague turn around when he saw everyone. I showed up alone to cover the event, so even though I had a good time socializing with new friends in the lines, I could only hit a few stands before the event was over.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              So, note to self for next year (because I absolutely still recommend it):
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <ol>
               <li>
                Show up REALLY early
               </li>
               <li>
                Bring as many friends as possible
               </li>
               <li>
                Possibly buy premium tickets
               </li>
               <li>
                Expect the long lines
               </li>
               <li>
                Plan for the must-eat stations beforehand
               </li>
              </ol>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                So, who's with me next year? :)
