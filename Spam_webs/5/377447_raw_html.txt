        <h1 class="entry-title">
         19th c. Reclaimed French Tapis Tiles | Item of the week
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
          Posted on
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/2014/11/19th-c-reclaimed-french-tapis-tiles-item-of-the-week/" rel="bookmark" title="2:05 pm">
          <span class="entry-date">
           November 25, 2014
          </span>
         </a>
         <span class="meta-sep">
          by
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/author/admin/" title="View all posts by Chateau Domingue">
           Chateau Domingue
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          This 19th c.
          <em>
           Tapis
          </em>
          boasts rows of bright and intricate stoneware tilesthat originally graced the entrance floors of a Maison de Maitre in Bordeaux, France.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/unnamed-11.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-486" height="800" src="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/unnamed-11.jpg" title="This 19th c. Tapis boasts rows of bright and intricate stoneware tiles that originally graced the entrance of a Maison de Maitre in Bordeaux, France. Chateau Domingue" width="600"/>
          </a>
          Maison de Maitre translates to masters house in French. It refers to a particular style of bourgeois home with high ceilings and four rooms on each floor. This
          <em>
           Tapis
          </em>
          would have run the length of the homes central corridor, dividing the homes parlor and social rooms from those dedicated to dining and food preparation with bright and intricate tile flooring.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Tiles1.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-487" height="640" src="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Tiles1.jpg" title="Reclaimed French stoneware floor tiles from Maison de Maitre in Bordeaux, France. Chateau Domingue" width="600"/>
          </a>
          At first glance the
          <em>
           Tapis
          </em>
          is a
          <em>
           trompe loeil,
          </em>
          appearing to be an intricately woven carpet. In fact,
          <em>
           Tapis
          </em>
          translates directly tocarpet, refers to the sets design, a tile
          <em>
           tapis
          </em>
          has a center pattern surrounded by lively borders. The larger border and center tiles measure 6.75 by 6.75. The smaller border tiles each measure 6.75 by 3.5 wide.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Tiles2.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-488" height="640" src="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Tiles2.jpg" title="Tiles2" width="600"/>
          </a>
          Stoneware tiles result from a unique process. Rich pigments are pressed into a wet mixture of finely ground stone  creatingbold colors that permeate beyond the tiles surface.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ContainerBridge-32.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-489" height="600" src="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ContainerBridge-32.jpg" title="Antique, reclaimed French stoneware floor tiles from Maison de Maitre in Bordeaux, France. Chateau Domingue Architectural Antiques" width="600"/>
          </a>
          153 square feet of these beautiful, rare antique stoneware tiles were reclaimed by the Chateau Domingue team during a recent buying excursion in France, and are
          <a href="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/product.php?id=57114&amp;cat=Colored%20Cement">
           available for purchase
          </a>
          .
          <a href="http://www.chateaudomingue.com/about.php">
           Contact us
          </a>
          to learn more about how you can bring these tiles home.
         </p>
