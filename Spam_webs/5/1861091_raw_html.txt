           <p>
            A voice-activated GPS system for the blind will direct guide dogs and their owners to new destinations using a system of vibrations, according to the inventor of the device, a British student.
           </p>
           <p>
            Jason Perkins created
            <a href="http://www.jamesdysonaward.org/Projects/Project.aspx?ID=116">
             Peepo
            </a>
            after speaking to members of the
            <a href="http://www.cardiffinstitutefortheblind.org">
             Cardiff Institute for the Blind
            </a>
            . They told him that only 6 per cent of guide dog owners will venture to new destinations "due to anxiety levels", so they have to rely on friends to help them when walking a route for the first time.
           </p>
           <div class="incontentAd" data-ad-placeholder-state="not-rendered" id="incontentAd_first">
           </div>
           <p>
            Perkins then set about creating a device, built around existing sat-navs, that would let blind people travel safely and confidently to a new destination with only their dog for company.
           </p>
           <p>
            Peepo comes in two parts. The first is a handheld, voice-activated GPS device system, the second a device that attaches to the handle on the guide dog's harness, using vibrations to tell the owner which way he or she needs to turn. The centre of the handle vibrates when the person is on the right track, and all zones vibrate simultaneously when the destination has been reached.
           </p>

           <p>
            Perkins says that he opted for vibrations after the people he spoke to explained that GPS devices that give verbal directions are hard to hear in noisy situations, such as city streets.
           </p>
           <p>
            Perkins has already patented the device in the US, which he says is a bigger market than the UK, and is now hoping to secure further funding from
            <a href="http://wales.gov.uk/?lang=en">
             the Welsh Assembly Government
            </a>
            to perfect the device before taking it to market.
           </p>
           <p>
            Peepo is one of the entries to this years
            <a href="http://www.jamesdysonaward.org/">
             James Dyson Design Award
            </a>
            , which has a top prize of 10,000. Entries close on the June 15.
