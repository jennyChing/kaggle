          Cupcakes may be trendy and oh-so-cute when decorated with frosting, candies and colored sugar. But they aren't real snacks I'd give my kids. I save those for birthdays and special occasions. But I still love an adorable and fun
          <a href="/category/lunch/" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            lunch food
           </strong>
          </a>
          activity!

Today, instead of lunch the kids and I made
          <strong>
           circus clowns from fruit
          </strong>
          !
          <p style="text-align: center">
           <a href="/circus-clown-lunch/clown-food-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-29245">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="463" src="http://media.tipjunkie.com/2011/10/clown-food-2.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Circus Clown Fruit
          </h2>
          It all started because of a trip to the apple orchard. We brought home 3 bushels of apples to make applesauce and pies.
          <strong>
           We also bought a peeler, slicer, corer machine-thingie.
          </strong>
          If you do not have one of these, you are missing out on some real fun! All you do is poke the apple on, turn the crank and voila--your apple is cored, peeled and spiral sliced. SUPER cool!

As we were preparing our three bushels of apples for applesauce,
          <strong>
           I got inspired by the long curly strands of peels!
          </strong>
          They looked like big curls of red and green! Kinda exactly like clown hair!
          <h2>
           How To Make
          </h2>
          So, we pulled out all the rest of the healthy food in our kitchen--
          <strong>
           grapes, raisins, cheese, crackers, bananas, popcorn
          </strong>
          and some not-so-healthy stuff like
          <strong>
           maraschino cherries and chocolate chips
          </strong>
          .
          <p style="text-align: center">
           <a href="/circus-clown-lunch/clown-food-4/" rel="attachment wp-att-29247">
            <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-29247 aligncenter" height="335" src="http://tj-prod-media.s3.amazonaws.com/2011/10/clown-food-4.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <strong>
           I put them all in a muffin tin because it's portion control and makes for less mess
          </strong>
          as the kids aren't reaching their sticky hands into bags and bowls!
          <p style="text-align: center">
           <a href="/circus-clown-lunch/clown-food-3/" rel="attachment wp-att-29246">
            <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-29246 aligncenter" height="335" src="http://tj-prod-media.s3.amazonaws.com/2011/10/clown-food-3.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <strong>
           I thinly sliced the apples to be our clown faces.
          </strong>
          We added the curly apple peel hair. Then we had at it with the rest of our food! The kids were waay more creative than I was. But I'll let you see my three creations anyway (yeah, that one on the bottom is a bear...*shrugs*...bears are in the circus, right?):
          <p style="text-align: center">
           <a href="/circus-clown-lunch/clown-food-1/" rel="attachment wp-att-29244">
            <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-29244 aligncenter" height="335" src="http://tj-prod-media.s3.amazonaws.com/2011/10/clown-food-1.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          There was not one empty plate and not one picky-eater at the table! One of the most exciting lunches we've ever had!
          <a href="/circus-clown-lunch/clown-food-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-29245">
