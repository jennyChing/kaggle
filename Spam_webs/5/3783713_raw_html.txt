			&lt;h3 class="entry-header"&gt;7 Reasons You Need a Facebook Fan Page&lt;/h3&gt;
	



	&lt;div class="entry-content"&gt;
		&lt;div class="entry-body"&gt;
			&lt;span style="font-size: 14px; font-family: Arial;"&gt; 
&lt;a href="http://bookmarketingmaven.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551344c0a88330134869b5e6e970c-pi" style="float: left;"&gt;&lt;img alt="Facebook" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551344c0a88330134869b5e6e970c " src="http://bookmarketingmaven.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551344c0a88330134869b5e6e970c-120wi" style="margin: 0px 15px 0px 0px;" title="Facebook" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt; Facebook Pages (also known as Fan Pages) look similar to personal Profiles, but they are designed for business use and they are a terrific way to promote yourself and your business. You can create a Page for your business, book, or even a character in a book. People join your Page by clicking the &quot;like&quot; button at the top of the page. (That button used to say &quot;become a fan.&quot;)&lt;br /&gt;&lt;p&gt;Facebook Pages have several advantages over personal Profiles:&lt;/p&gt;1.&#0160; You&#39;re limited to 5,000 friends on your Profile, but there&#39;s no limit on the number of people who can &quot;like&quot; your Page. &lt;br /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;2.&#0160; Pages are designed for business use, so it&#39;s more acceptable to be promotional on a Page than on a Profile. &lt;br /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;3.&#0160; You can create multiple Pages to promote different products or businesses.&lt;br /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;4.&#0160; You can send a message to everyone who has joined your page. Your message will show up in the Update section of each fan&#39;s Inbox.&lt;br /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;5.&#0160; You can create customized &quot;tabs&quot; or screens on your Page. For example, you can create a tab to promote your book and include your cover image, book video trailer and other content on that tab.&lt;/span&gt;

		&lt;/div&gt;
					&lt;a id="more"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;
			&lt;div class="entry-more"&gt;
				
&lt;span style="font-size: 14px; font-family: Arial;"&gt;&lt;br /&gt;6.&#0160; After people join your Page, they will be directed to the Wall tab each time they visit the Page. But you can create a customized welcome screen for new visitors to land on. And each of the &quot;tabs&quot; or screens on your Page has its own URL that you can link to directly from outside of Facebook.&lt;br /&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;span style="font-size: 14px; font-family: Arial;"&gt;7.&#0160; You can use your Page to increase your email subscriber list, by adding an opt-in form and free bonus to one of the tabs. In the screenshot below you can see the Free Ebook tab that I added to my own Page. &lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;span style="font-size: 14px; font-family: Arial;"&gt; 


&lt;p&gt;

Youll also get a free copy of my ebook, &lt;em&gt;Top Book Marketing Tips&lt;/em&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;span style="font-size: 14px; font-family: Arial;"&gt;To learn more about how to use Facebook to promote yourself and your book, read &lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://bookmarketingmaven.typepad.com/savvy_book_marketer/facebook.html" target="_blank"&gt;Facebook Guide for Authors&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt; or &lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://bookmarketingmaven.typepad.com/savvy_book_marketer/successful-social-marketing.html" target="_blank"&gt;The Savvy Book Marketers Guide to Successful Social Marketing&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;. &lt;br /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;Have you already created a Facebook Page?
