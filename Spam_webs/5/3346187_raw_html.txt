       <h1 class="omc-post-heading-standard">
        Turn Your House into a Water Conservation Station
       </h1>
       <p>
        September 24th, 2008 by
        <a href="http://planetsave.com/author/joemohr/" rel="author">
         Joe Mohr
        </a>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
       </p>
       <div class="icx-toolbar-align-right">
        <!-- iCopyright OneButton Article Toolbar -->
        <br/>

       <h3>
        On average, humans need approximately 13 gallons of water a day. In the U.S. the average family uses 245 gallons per day!
       </h3>
       <p>
        <a href="http://planetsave.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/800px-drops_imapct1.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2994" height="485" src="http://planetsave.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/800px-drops_imapct1.jpg" width="653"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <em>
         If you did your homework and purchased the
         <a href="http://blueplanetrun.org/the_book">
          Blue Planet Run book
         </a>
         as I suggested
         <a href="http://planetsave.com/blog/2008/09/18/book-review-blue-planet-run-the-race-to-provide-safe-drinking-water-to-the-world/">
          last week
         </a>
         , then you will find most of what Im about to share in the beautiful charts and graphs on page 174 and 175.
        </em>
        <span id="more-2992">
         <center>
          <!-- /4800635/PS_pl_middle -->
         </center>
        </span>
       </p>
       <div id="div-gpt-ad-1429211637622-0" style="height:60px; width:468px;">
        <script type="text/javascript">
         googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1429211637622-0'); });
        </script>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Water use habits: The Average Home vs. The Conservation Station
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center">
        <strong>
         The Average Home: Indoors
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bath
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         36 gallons
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         5-minute Shower
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         25-35 gallons
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Brushing Teeth
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         2 gallons (faucet running)
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Shaving
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         20 gallons! (faucet running) More than the average person needs ALL day!
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Toilet
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         5-7 gallons
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Dishwasher
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         15 gallons
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Washing Machine
        </strong>
        (top loading)
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         40 gallons
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Washing Dishes by Hand
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         20-30 gallons (faucet running)
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <!--more-->
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center">
        <strong>
         The Conservation Station: Indoors
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bath
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         15 gallons (21 less)Take showers instead of baths.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         5-minute Shower
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         5 gallons (20 to 30 less)Use the water to get wet and lather up, scrub with it off, and turn it on again to rinse off. Replace your shower head with an ultra low-flow version (I use onethere is no noticeable difference in shower quality at all!). Put a bucket in the shower to catch some of the water and use that water to water thirsty plants. Consider going
         <a href="http://www.lowimpactliving.com/blog/2007/11/13/graywater-recycling-systems/">
          gray
         </a>
         .
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Brushing Teeth
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         .5 gallons (1.5 less)Turn the water off while you brush.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Shaving
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         1 gallon (19 less)Use only a bowl. I use a cupthen again, Im not the hairiest man in the world.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Toilet
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         2 gallons (3-5 less)Buy a low-flow model. Put a jug of water in the cistern (I use a large glass pasta sauce jar, filled with water). This reduces the amount of water lost with each flush (amount depends on the size of the container).Dont flush every time. If its yellow let it mellowif its brown flush it down. Finally, dontuse the toilet simply to disposeof tissues; put them in the trash.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Dishwasher
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         7 gallons (8 less)Use the short cycle.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Washing Machine
        </strong>
        (front loading)
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         24 gallons (16 less)Only run machines when they are full.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Washing Dishes by Hand
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         5 gallons (15-25 less)Use a wash sink and a rinse sink. Also, replace the washers on dripping faucets. One drop per second wastes 27,000 gallons per year!
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p style="text-align: center">
        <strong>
         Conserving WaterOutside
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Plant native and drought tolerant grasses, flowers, ground covers, shrubs, and trees. They require less water and will survive dry spells.
        </li>
        <li>
         Apply mulch around flowers and plants to retain moisture in the soil and reduce evaporation. This will also promote growth and control weeds (which compete with plants for water).
        </li>
        <li>
         Use soaker hoses. They are more efficient than regular hoses (which use 10 gallons per minute) because the water goes straight to the roots.
        </li>
        <li>
         Do your watering early in the morning to minimize evaporation.
        </li>
        <li>
         Avoid sprinklers that use a fine mist. Most of the mist evaporates before it hits the ground.
        </li>
        <li>
         Dont overfertilize. Fertilizer increases the need for water. Dont use chemical fertilizers. They pollute the groundwater supply!
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Dont overwater!
         </strong>
         A heavy rain means you dont have to water for up to two weeks. Most of the year lawns only need one inch of water per week.
        </li>
        <li>
         Collect rainwater by installing a
         <a href="http://jeffmcintirestrasburg.greenoptions.com/2007/11/04/celsias-green-home-improvements-rain-barrels/">
          rainbarrel
         </a>
         . I used mine all summer to water the garden, flowers, and bushes, and washed the car few times as well. I never used the house-connected hose for any of these tasks.
        </li>
        <li>
         Make sure sprinklers do not spray water on the driveway (or any other pavement).
        </li>
        <li>
         Dont hose down the driveway or patio to clean it. Use a broom!
        </li>
        <li>
         Dont let the hose run when washing the car. Use a nozzle that has a shut off valve and can be adjusted to a fine spray. Park the car on the grass when you wash it so you are watering the water at the same time.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        In a time of increasing demand for water and adecreasing fresh water supply, these conservation steps are important for EVERYONE! Be a role model.
       </p>

        Share this article with friends and family too!
