					&lt;span style="font-family: 'Verdana'"&gt;&lt;span style="color: DarkOrange"&gt;&lt;div style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;span style="font-size: 12px"&gt;&lt;b&gt;Round the World - Do you have beer we are coming to visit?&lt;/b&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;span style="color: white"&gt;The initial &lt;b&gt;&quot;Hello Africa tell me how you doing?&quot;&lt;/b&gt; trip was only intended to cover South Africa to Europe. There were no plans after that. Since we started in 2010 this trip has evolved into an odyssey that&#039;s now going around the world on a continuous basis. Let us introduce ourselves, we are &lt;span style="color: SandyBrown"&gt;Michnus and Elsebie&lt;/span&gt;, our home is in East London, South Africa. Our passions are motorcycle overland travel, amateurish photography, drinking beer while watching the sunset over a new horizon, and hooking up with cool people. &lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;span style="color: DarkOrange"&gt;Why?&lt;/span&gt; The aim is not to set records or to rush just to get the stamps. We are not the highway asphalt type, we love the backroads, the dirt roads and roads less travelled, the out of way places where tourist never go. Eat local, really dig into a place. We want to rub shoulders with the world, see the amazing places around the globe and get some first hand experiences of other people&#039;s cultures. &lt;br /&gt;
Our trip is completely unsupported and without sponsors.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;span style="color: Red"&gt;&lt;span style="font-size: 15px"&gt; Our website is: &lt;a href="http://www.pikipiki.co.za" target="_blank" class="externalLink" rel="nofollow"&gt;www.pikipiki.co.za&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;.&lt;/span&gt; &lt;/span&gt; &lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;span style="font-size: 22px"&gt;But most of all to have fun!!&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br /&gt;

Our first night in Namibia we stayed over in Ai-Ais, a hot spring in the lower part of Namibia. It&amp;#8217;s a great place to relax for a few days and enjoy the moon-like surroundings. It forms part of the Fish river canyon which is the second biggest canyon in the world, the Grand canyon is bigger. &lt;br /&gt;
Namibia is a arid desert type of country, only 2 million people in a area that is just bigger than Texas. It&#039;s a wonderful laid back country with different tribes of people making a living in this dry hot corner of Africa.&lt;br /&gt;

&lt;b&gt;Fish river canyon, what a place, the beauty and size leaves you breathless. Second biggest canyon in the world after the Grand Canyon&lt;/b&gt;&lt;br /&gt;

On the road to Aus a small one horseman town on the way to Sossusvlei, we found a place called the Canon Roadhouse. Old rusted cars, engines, stickers and everything from way past when. It&amp;#8217;s unbelievable that they were able to put this together in the middle of nowhere. Any real man deserve a garage like this, even the toilets are a work of art. &lt;b&gt;Talking shit with some local bikers that also visited the place at the same time&lt;/b&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
Just outside the Fish River canyon we came across this French couple cycling all the way from France to Cape town. How dehell people decide to do this is beyond me, this is serious hardcore stuff. If you do cycling touring in Europe it&#039;s one thing. Doing it in Africa add quite a good measure of risk and danger to this adventure. The spirit and friendliness of travellers always amazes me. Maybe they are just a different breed of person, as they always seem in good spirit. This is extreme travelling and way past my abilities, respect, that&amp;#8217;s all I can say. &lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
He came down the West coast of Africa which is the most difficult route you can do in Africa and had zero troubles, she joined him in Lusaka and they pedal around 100km per day, eating 5 times a day mostly rice. The draw back is they did not go to the Canyon or Ai-Ai&#039;s as it&#039;s 12km and 9km they must pedal in and out each time, and so with many other places. &lt;br /&gt;
I wonder if doing a trip like this is really that a good idea if you choose to miss all the best places and just stick to the best roads as pedalling there and back are just too much work. &lt;br /&gt;

&lt;div style="text-align: center"&gt;At this stage we hoped we could get some excitement along the way to take away the boredom of some of the never ending dirt roads. We read up in some magazine the best way to Sossusvlei is best riding the D707 a sandy road running along the edge of the Namib park. It&amp;#8217;s also on this road that I had my wish come true for a bit of excitement.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
At Ai-Ais we were asked whether we were part of the gaggle of GS&amp;#8217;s, all French people and around 30 of them,  that were doing a loop of SA, Zim, Bots, Zambia and Namibia. Aus&amp;#8217;s garage owner told us not to take the D707 &amp;#8230; sand, sand, sand, apparently some of the French fell them into hell, on that road and one pillion broke a collar bone. Well, that&amp;#8217;s the sign right there brothers and sisters!! We had to take this road! &lt;br /&gt;

&lt;br /&gt;
It&#039;s one of those roads where you want to stop every 10 minutes and take pictures. Breath taking jaw drop views, I stopped taking pictures knowing that my amateurish attempts will just not do justice to this place. I just took it all in and enjoyed the moment.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
It&#039;s easy to understand why so many people get injured in Namibia on bikes and why the locals keep telling us stories of people hitting the dirt. The wide long stretched out roads lures you to go faster and faster and then you hit a sand patch and everything goes belly up. For new riders and cat-piss drenched testosterone cowboys it&amp;#8217;s the biggest danger. They normally fall them selves into a different blood group.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
As we rode into Betta, another small town, well it can&#039;t be called a town, there&#039;s only 3 houses. This young dirty Jenson Button look-a-like named Neil with an oooold XT500 6Volt bike was sitting sipping on hot water. He started riding bike a few months ago after drooling over Nardus&amp;#8217; ride report on his Africa travels &lt;a href="http://www.wilddog.za.net/forum/index.php?topic=21558.0" target="_blank" class="externalLink" rel="nofollow"&gt;MAN WITHOUT SHOES RIDE AFRICA&lt;/a&gt; Nardus is his all-time biggest hero, he nearly gave up his religion for Nardus and travelling. It inspired him so much he just had to do something similar, except he does not have the funds or time for the entire Africa he will do a loop of Namibia, Zambia, Malawi and back SA. Nardus is the same man that did &quot;Angola is not what they said&quot; with us&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
This guy is a true adventure rider, and I am super impress with him and his exuberance. Only got his learners licence a few months ago and the previous owner had to show him how to ride a bike, he even asked if the bike got a reverse gear! He kitted his bike with empty 2L plastic Coca Cola bottles and set off to Springbok a small town close to the border of Nambia, he phoned ahead to make arrangements for his bike license and still had to stay a week longer in Springbok to get the booking and pass his license before he was allowed to enter into Namibia.&lt;br /&gt;
This picture is dedicated to Nardus, he could not get a Heineken can so a Cream Soda had to do.&lt;br /&gt;

In Solitaire I gave him a crash course in dirt road riding, the poor guy was taking strain on these roads. It&amp;#8217;s humbling and makes one think about our perceptions of what we need in life. This guy does the same as us with a hellova lot less and still enjoys it. &#8203;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;br /&gt;

At Sesriem and Sossuslvei the border to the oldest desert,  there&amp;#8217;s only one place to stay and it&amp;#8217;s the Oasis camp site. It&amp;#8217;s cheaper and miles better than the National Park camp site. Each camp site has its own private facilities.&lt;br /&gt;

&lt;div style="text-align: center"&gt;One sad thing about returning to Namibia is to see the influence that tourism has had on the local tribes. It&#039;s not nice to see the Himba (apparently the oldest Herero tribe) setting up &amp;#8216;come see us&amp;#8217; shacks along the main routes &amp;#8211; dancing in the road to attract attention, just like street beggars next to traffic lights, child on the hip. At least the crafts on sale are all actually made by themselves. Numerous curios shacks also line the once deserted roads. One even said &amp;#8216;do not take a picture, come and see us inside&amp;#8217;. Guess there&amp;#8217;s positives and negatives for this. The influence of main stream tourism on this region has changed them forever.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;img src="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-B6Bdn5x/0/XL/i-B6Bdn5x-XL.jpg" class="bbCodeImage LbImage" alt="[&#x200B;IMG]" data-url="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-B6Bdn5x/0/XL/i-B6Bdn5x-XL.jpg" /&gt;&#8203;&lt;/div&gt; &lt;div style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;b&gt;You will not get me down!&lt;/b&gt;&lt;br /&gt;

&lt;img src="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-MSKhPSx/0/XL/i-MSKhPSx-XL.jpg" class="bbCodeImage LbImage" alt="[&#x200B;IMG]" data-url="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-MSKhPSx/0/XL/i-MSKhPSx-XL.jpg" /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;b&gt;Ongongo near Sesriem&lt;/b&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
Ongongo is a place where water flow over rocks baking in the sun and then into this pool. It&#039;s the perfect place to take a relaxing rest for a few days. There&#039;s no people and the locals can get you beers in 2min flat. &lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;img src="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-4BDZzCH/0/XL/i-4BDZzCH-XL.jpg" class="bbCodeImage LbImage" alt="[&#x200B;IMG]" data-url="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-4BDZzCH/0/XL/i-4BDZzCH-XL.jpg" /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;img src="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-pws5xM8/0/XL/i-pws5xM8-XL.jpg" class="bbCodeImage LbImage" alt="[&#x200B;IMG]" data-url="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-pws5xM8/0/XL/i-pws5xM8-XL.jpg" /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;b&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
First of many puncture to come, at least I taught her well to do it while I rest in the shade.&lt;/b&gt;&#8203;&lt;/div&gt; &lt;br /&gt;
&lt;div style="text-align: center"&gt;I do not have the vocabulary to describe the astonishing beauty of the landscape and scenery to its fullest, if only I had paid more attention in school maybe it would have helped. Especially this part of Namibia with its zebra striped mountains and red coloured sand flowing from broken mountain sides. Why did we had to sit in school with rubbish subjects rather than being taught photography or something worthwhile to enable me to take some decent true too life pictures and to portray this incredible country.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;img src="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-zbPKwz7/0/XL/i-zbPKwz7-XL.jpg" class="bbCodeImage LbImage" alt="[&#x200B;IMG]" data-url="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-zbPKwz7/0/XL/i-zbPKwz7-XL.jpg" /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
Last time I camped here was on our Angola trip 3 years ago with Metaljockey and the gang. It&#039;s great being back.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
It&amp;#8217;s unthinkable how people can live in this arid place called Damaraland. God burned this place black and dark red in anger over Eve&amp;#8217;s infidelity, maybe not, but I can&amp;#8217;t think of any other reason riding through this beautiful land. If this place does not touch your soul you either do not have one or you are an alien.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
The harshness of living in Damaraland is visible on people&amp;#8217;s faces, but one thing that you do not expect is the friendliness and welcome smiles every single time you talk to one of the locals.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;img src="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-SQK26Rt/0/XL/i-SQK26Rt-XL.jpg" class="bbCodeImage LbImage" alt="[&#x200B;IMG]" data-url="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-SQK26Rt/0/XL/i-SQK26Rt-XL.jpg" /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;img src="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-RzkLJ3n/0/XL/i-RzkLJ3n-XL.jpg" class="bbCodeImage LbImage" alt="[&#x200B;IMG]" data-url="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-RzkLJ3n/0/XL/i-RzkLJ3n-XL.jpg" /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;b&gt;Bought Elsebie this necklace in Opuwo from a very persistent seller. I thought I lost it, as I took the seat off I nearly fainted from  the horror, thought this fucking snake was under my ass the entire time while riding!! Well at least it&#039;s not gone. &lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
In the end we travelled Namibia from bottom to top on gravel and it is one of the most beautiful inviting countries and an absolute on the bucked list.&lt;/b&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;img src="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-ScSKRR9/0/XL/i-ScSKRR9-XL.jpg" class="bbCodeImage LbImage" alt="[&#x200B;IMG]" data-url="http://www.smugmug.com/photos/i-ScSKRR9/0/XL/i-ScSKRR9-XL.jpg" /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;b&gt;That&#039;s Angola in the back ground, yes baby your next!&lt;/b&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;b&gt;Angola will be a short visit and then back to Caprivi and onto Zambia.&lt;/b&gt;&#8203;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
