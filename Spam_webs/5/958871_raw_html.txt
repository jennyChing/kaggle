         <h1 class="page-title" itemprop="name" style="border:0;">
          An Open Letter to Group Projects
         </h1>
         <p style="font-size: 76%;">
          <strong>
           Tara Goutermout
          </strong>
          in
          <strong>
           Humor

        <div class="entry-content clearfix" style="margin-top: 20px;">
         <div itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           Dear Group Project,
          </p>
          I know its not your fault that you were assigned.
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           I know
that some middle-aged professor who spent his entire 20s sinking himself in graduate degrees anddebt assigned you. A
professor who is furious that his peak earning potential is $60,000 a year. A professor who often fantasizes of life as a UPS Driver, a
stress-free, six-figure existence of dropping off boxes. A professor that is so pissed about how overqualified he is in his knowledge of Russian Literature that he spends his days planning ways to torment hisstudents
all while decreasing his grading workload. Alas, he concluded to assign the
group project.
          </p>
          <aside class="side-article-ad">
           <div class="ad ad-300x250">
            <div class="dfp-tag-wrapper" id="dfp-ad-article-1">
             <script>

             </script>
            </div>

          <p>
           So group project, I dont blame you. And yet, you exist.
          </p>
          <p>
           Your very design breeds mediocrity. But how could you not? You are like a social experiment that sees what happens when you
combine the anal overachiever, the try-hard with a low IQ, the student whose life
motto is "Cs get degrees" and the too-high-to-function kid. This is not
           <em>
            The
Breakfast Club
           </em>
           . We are not going to realize we actually alllike each other and then all go to theprom.
          </p>
          <p>
           Instead, the overachiever is
going to pretty much do everything, while the try-hard chirps in her never-ending wrongadvice just to feel like she's contributing. The stoned kid
will constantly apologize for not knowing whats going on, all while the C-lover browses Facebook and thinks
about his dog.
          </p>
          <aside class="side-article-ad">
           <div class="ad ad-300x250">
            <div class="dfp-tag-wrapper" id="dfp-ad-article-2">
             <script>

          <p>
           When its time to present, the overachiever will clench up
in sweaty fear that she might not get an A, the try-hard will read straight off her notecard in monotone, the C-lover will basically present a
Broadway-worthy performance and leave the teacher with the lasting impression
that he was the brains and butter of the operation, all while the stoner blacks
out during the entire presentation and giggles, What just happened? to his bros
afterwards.
          </p>
          <p>
           When it comes time to turn in peer reviews, everyone will
end up saying the group "worked together nicely" and "contributed fairly" and was "fine" because who wants to be that narc?
          </p>
          <aside class="side-article-ad">
           <div class="ad ad-300x250">

          <p>
           So group project, I know you like to defend yourself by
saying you prepare students for "real working environments." But last time I
checkedfellow employees are hired for a skill-set, not for taking a
course because they heard the professor only skims the essays.
          </p>
          <p>
           Basically I'm telling you, group project, thatnot even your mom likes you.
          </p>
          <p>
           Sincerely,
          </p>
          <p>
           The College Population
          </p>
          <p>
           P.S. Everyone knows you cheated on your girlfriend, the Essay,last summer at leadership camp.
          </p>
