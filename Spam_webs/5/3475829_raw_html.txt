      Transparent bridges are devices which connects more than one network segments with other
      <a href="/TERM/B/bridge.html">
       bridges
      </a>
      to make all
      <a href="/TERM/R/router.html">
       routing
      </a>
      decisions. A transparent bridge is essentially used to learn the
      <a href="/TERM/M/MAC_address.html">
       MAC addresses
      </a>
      of all
      <a href="/TERM/N/node.html">
       nodes
      </a>
      and their associated
      <a href="/TERM/P/port.html">
       port
      </a>
      , to
      <a href="/TERM/F/filter.html">
       filter
      </a>
      incoming frames whose destination MAC addresses are located on the same incoming port, and to forward incoming frames to the destination MAC through their associated port@.
      <p>
       There are two types of Transparent Bridge Modes:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Store-and-Forward
         </strong>
        </em>
        : Stores the entire frame and verifies the
        <a href="/TERM/C/CRC.html">
         CRC
        </a>
        before forwarding the frame. If a CRC error is detected, the frame is discarded.
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         <em>
          Cut-Through
         </em>
        </strong>
        : Forwards the frame just after it reads the destination MAC address without performing a CRC check.
        <em>
         <br/>
         Adapted from [
         <a href="http://www.ciscopress.com">
          CCIE Routing and Switching Exam Certification Guide
         </a>
         ]
        </em>
       </li>
      </ul>
