       <h1 class="title title-large">
        Easy Rider: father-daughter duo make first coast-to-coast trip in a Tesla Model S
       </h1>
       <p class="postmetadata">
        By
        <a href="http://www.siliconbeat.com/author/danahull/" rel="author" title="Posts by Dana Hull">
         Dana Hull
        </a>
        /
        <time datetime="2014-01-26" pubdate="">
         January 26, 2014 at 10:57 PM
        </time>
       </p>
       <div class="post-content">
        <img src="http://www.siliconbeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/chargingmaps.jpg" width="100%"/>
        <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Easy Rider: father-daughter duo make first coast-to-coast trip in a Tesla Model S" data-url="http://www.siliconbeat.com/2014/01/26/easy-rider-the-first-coast-to-coast-trip-in-a-tesla-model-s/">
        </div>
        <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
        </div>
        <p>
         Its happened: a driver of a Tesla Model S just traveled from New York to Los Angeles using only the companys free Supercharging stations for fuel.
        </p>
        <p>
         John Glenney, 62, drove with his daughter Jill Glenney, 26, as his co-pilot. The duo drove in a green Model S named Ivy and kept track of their trip using a spreadsheet that they filled out by hand. They left New York January 20 and arrived in Los Angeles Saturday, using 28 Supercharging stations along the way.
        </p>
        <p>
         I love talking about the trip, but Im not that conversant in social media, said Glenney, who lives in Lexington, Kentucky and spoke by phone Monday morning from Paso Robles. Im planning to drive to Fremont today and would love to take a tour of the Tesla factory, but I havent heard back from Tesla.
        </p>
        <p>
         The groundbreaking trip was unscripted and has largely gone unnoticed outside of the EV community. Other Model S owners have been following the
         <a href="http://www.teslamotors.com/forum/forums/heading-cross-country?page=16">
          news of the journey
         </a>
         at the Tesla Forums page with interest. It sounds like the duo plan to drive to Teslas Fremont factory sometime Monday in the hopes of getting a factory tour.
        </p>
        <p>
         Meanwhile, Tesla CEO Elon Musk h
         <a href="https://twitter.com/elonmusk">
          as announced via Twitter
         </a>
         that two teams from Tesla aim to set a cross-country EV speed record this week. They will depart from Los Angeles on Friday and plan to arrive in New York Sunday. Cant wait to find out who from the company is on the teams. Personally, Im rooting for
         <a href="http://www.teslamotors.com/executives#straubel">
          JB Straubel
         </a>
         in one car and
         <a href="http://www.teslamotors.com/executives#guillen">
          Jerome Guillen
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.teslamotors.com/executives#passin">
          Gilbert Passin
         </a>
         in the other.
        </p>
        <p>
         Musk himself plans to do his Los Angeles -to-New York road trip with his five sons over their Spring Break.
        </p>
