           <a href="http://gather.com/hurricane-irene-expected-to-cause-billions-in-damage-video/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Hurricane Irene Expected to Cause Billions in Damage (Video)">
            Hurricane Irene Expected to Cause Billions in Damage (Video)
           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="meta">
           <span class="meta-cats">
            Filed in
            <a href="http://gather.com/category/news/" rel="category tag">
             Gather News Channel
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="meta-author">
            by
            <a href="http://gather.com/author/lauriebeth/" rel="author" title="Posts by Lauren Moccio">
             Lauren Moccio
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="meta-date">
            on August 26, 2011
           </span>
           <span class="meta-comments">
            
            <a href="http://gather.com/hurricane-irene-expected-to-cause-billions-in-damage-video/#comments" rel="bookmark" title="Comments for Hurricane Irene Expected to Cause Billions in Damage (Video)">
             0 Comments
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
          <p>
           Hurricane Irene is expected to cause billions of dollars in damages along the East Coast of the United States. Over 70 million people are in her path, and are either evacuating or battening down the hatches and preparing to weather the storm. Irene
           <a href="http://abcnews.go.com/US/hurricane-irene-billions-damage-expected-east-coast/story?id=14385269" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            is currently downgraded to a Category 2
           </a>
           , with winds of 110 miles per hour. Its a slow-moving storm, which worries specialists because it may linger over the cities in its path. A former chief of the National Hurricane Center, Max Mayfield, told media: One of my greatest nightmares was having a major hurricane go up the whole Northeast Coast. He confirms that it will lead to billions of dollars in damages, This is going to have an impact on the United States economy. This could be the worst hurricane to affect the East Coast since the horrendous Hurricane Gloria in 1985which East Coasters still mention in hushed tonesso everyone is understandably nervous, and some are terrified.
          </p>
          <p>
           FEMA Director Craig Fugate also pointed out, This is one of the largest populations that will be impacted by one storm at one time. A little bit of damage over big areas with large populations can add up fast. Scientists believe the eye of Hurricane Irene will fall right on New York City, which could have devastating effects on the boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn. Homeland Security Secretary Janet Napolitano, after conversing with East Coast mayors and governors in Irenes path, issued a press release stating: Given the unpredictability of these storms, we are currently planning for several scenarios, including potential impacts to major metro areas and critical infrastructure. The governors of New York, New Jersey, Connecticut, Maryland, Virginia and
           <a href="http://governor.delaware.gov/news/2011/1108august/20110825-Irene-Serious-Threat.shtml" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Delaware
           </a>
           have declared a state of emergency.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/gsfc/6076866493/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <img src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6183/6076866493_626cd0e5f0.jpg" style="border-style: initial;border-color: initial"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Mayor Michael R. Bloomberg of New York City says that police are deploying more than 80 boats around the city as well as several helicopters in preparation for emergency rescue. The citys airports, JFK and LaGuardia, both in Queens county, are stockpiling diapers, cots, blankets, pillow and bottles of water. Experts say that the skyscrapers will have no problem withstanding the wind; its the flooding that will be devastating. Amtrak is cancelling all train service Friday through Sunday, and the MTA says it
           <a href="http://gothamist.com/2011/08/26/mta_may_shut_down_transit_service_d.php" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            might suspend service
           </a>
           as well. Evacuation orders were issued along the coast of North Carolina today in Dare, Currituck and Cateret counties.
          </p>
          <p>
           As the storm travels over land it is expected to weaken, but winds will still be at 50 to 70 miles per hour when it reaches New York City and Boston. The more terrifying aspect is that Hurricane Irene is expected to dump six to twelve inches of rain on New Jersey, Massachusetts and Connecticut, and North Jersey is
           <a href="http://blog.nj.com/njv_mark_diionno/2011/03/di_ionno_for_some_waterlogged.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            extremely vulnerable to flooding
           </a>
           .
          </p>
