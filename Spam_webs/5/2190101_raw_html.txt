           <span class="slide-copy">
            <p>
             For some people, thre stress of traveling can be compounded by the tempting snacks surrounding you in the airport terminal. Candy, bagels, cinnamon buns, cookies  when youre waiting to catch a flight and feeling hungry or anxious, these goodies seem to call out to you from every store. But having
             <strong>
              <a href="/diabetes/type2/understanding.aspx">
               type 2 diabetes
              </a>
             </strong>
             means you need to choose healthy snacks  theres no free pass for the weary air traveler.
            </p>
            <p>
             The best option is to pack your own food, of course. But what if you're unwilling or unable to bring your own? The next best option is to choose snacks that are lower in fat (5 grams or less per serving) and know the carbohydrate content so you can fit them into your meal plan or carbohydrate choices for that time of the day, advises Toby Smithson, RD, LDN, CDE, a dietitian for the Lake County Health Department in Waukegan, Ill. Here are some healthy ideas to guide your snack-stand shopping.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

          <span class="slide-wrapper ">
           <span class="slide-img-container">

            <noscript>

           </span>
           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Go (Moderately) Nuts!
            </h2>
            <p>
             On
the plane or in the airport, you can hardly avoid packets of nuts. In fact,
nuts are one of the best
             <strong>
              <a href="/type-2-diabetes/healthy-snacking-with-type-2-diabetes.aspx">
               snack
              </a>
             </strong>
             ideas, as long as you can keep the amount you eat in check. The calories, fat,
and carbohydrates can add up quickly, warns Smithson, who has managed her own
diabetes for 42 years and is a spokesperson for the American Dietetic
Association. Aim for a healthy snack of one ounce, about 28 peanuts. People who
are watching their salt should select unsalted varieties.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>
         <li data-ind="2" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <span class="slide-wrapper ">
           <span class="slide-img-container">
            <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="3" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/pg-diabetic-snacks-air-travel-03-full.jpg" data-title="Find+the+Fruit" id="slide-img-3" src=""/>
            <noscript>
             <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/pg-diabetic-snacks-air-travel-03-full.jpg"/>
            </noscript>
            <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">

            <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
           </span>
           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Find the Fruit
            </h2>
            <p>
             A
serving of fresh
             <strong>
              <a href="/type-2-diabetes/including-fruit-in-a-diabetes-diet.aspx">
               fruit
              </a>
             </strong>
             is a healthy snack and very easy to eyeball. It should be the equivalent in
size of a baseball, says Smithson, which counts as one carbohydrate for the
day. Searching for this snack idea in an airport terminal can also help you get
in a little exercise, an additional benefit. I usually walk around all of the food stands
at the airport to see all the food choices and then piece my snack or meal
together from the different stands, advises Smithson.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>
         <li data-ind="3" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <span class="slide-wrapper ">
           <span class="slide-img-container">
            <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="4" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/pg-diabetic-snacks-air-travel-04-full.jpg" data-title="Go+for+the+Jerky" id="slide-img-4" src=""/>
            <noscript>
             <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/pg-diabetic-snacks-air-travel-04-full.jpg"/>
            </noscript>
            <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">

            <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
           </span>
           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Go for the Jerky
            </h2>
            <p>
             Youre
more likely to find this in a convenience store at the terminal than food
stalls, but if you like jerky (dried meat), pick some up as a snack idea for
later on. [Jerky] is low in carbohydrate and is a source of protein, so it shouldn't
affect
             <strong>
              <a href="/type-2-diabetes/monitoring-blood-sugar.aspx">
               blood sugar
              </a>
             </strong>
             and would be a good snack item to have in your carry-on bag, says Smithson.
However, if youre watching your salt as well as your type 2 diabetes, this
might not be the best snack idea  check the sodium content on the
label before you buy.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Pick a Smart Sandwich
            </h2>
            <p>
             If you want a snack idea thats more substantial, go for a small sandwich. This may be part of a boxed lunch if offered on the plane (check with your carrier to see if they offer special
             <strong>
              <a href="/type-2-diabetes/healthy-meals-for-type-2-diabetes.aspx">
               diabetes meals
              </a>
             </strong>
             ). A junior burger or a sandwich with meat or cheese provides a combination of protein and carbohydrate. Make these truly healthy snacks by adding as many vegetables as you can for flavor and fiber. However, says Smithson, skip the mayo, which just adds fat.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>
         <li data-ind="5" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <span class="slide-wrapper ">
           <span class="slide-img-container">
            <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="6" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/pg-diabetic-snacks-air-travel-06-full.jpg" data-title="Savor+a+Slice+of+Pizza" id="slide-img-6" src=""/>
            <noscript>
             <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/pg-diabetic-snacks-air-travel-06-full.jpg"/>
            </noscript>
            <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
            </div>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Savor a Slice of Pizza
            </h2>
            <p>
             Pizza
can be a healthy snack for the person with diabetes if you can load it up with
veggies and avoid eating too much of a good thing (some of those slices are
more like a quarter of a pie). A slice provides
             <strong>
              <a href="/diabetes/monitoring-carbohydrates.aspx">
               carbohydrates
              </a>
             </strong>
             ,
proteins, and fat. However, says Smithson, avoid pizza as a snack late in the
day, when the fat in the pizza slows down the absorption of the carbohydrate.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Try a Snack With a Straw
            </h2>
            <p>
             Liquid
nutrition can be a good snack idea. Just choose wisely.
             <strong>
              <a href="/weight/the-importance-of-water-in-your-diet-plan.aspx">
               Water
              </a>
             </strong>
             and diet drinks are good choices if you just want refreshment. For a healthy
snack thats more filling, go with low-fat milk. Milk has 12 grams of
carbohydrate in an 8-ounce glass and also provides protein, says Smithson. If
you have diabetes, steer clear of fruit juices, but consider tomato juice
(which has about one-third as many carbs as orange juice) for a treat.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>
         <li data-ind="7" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <span class="slide-wrapper ">
           <span class="slide-img-container">

            <noscript>
             <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/pg-diabetic-snacks-air-travel-08-full.jpg"/>
            </noscript>
            <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
            </div>
            <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">

            <span class="slide-pinterest">

            <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
           </span>
           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Salads Make Sense
            </h2>
            <p>
             Small, fresh salads are smart and delicious snack ideas. Just pay attention to the nutrition labeling on any salad dressing you choose  these can have a lot of sugar. Hard-boiled eggs or meat canbe added to turn your healthy snack into more of a meal. Another salad option good for a diabetes diet is a fruit and cheese plate, which offers both carbohydrates and proteins. Again, watch your portions  cheese, like nuts, can add up to too many
             <strong>
              <a href="/diabetes/targeting-fats.aspx">
               fat
              </a>
             </strong>
             grams very quickly.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Snacks to Avoid
            </h2>
            <p>
             There
are many airport snack ideas you should avoid, no matter how tempting. Steer
clear of smoothies (generally very high in carbohydrates), candy bars (not even
nuts redeem them), and French fries  never a first choice for a
snack or a meal if you have diabetes, says Smithson. None of these are
             <strong>
              <a href="/diet-nutrition/meal-planning/snacking/healthy-snacking.aspx">
               healthy
snacks
              </a>
             </strong>
             , and all will spike your blood sugar.
