       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Holding Her BackJust a Little
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.lifeinpleasantville.com/author/candace/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Candace Derickx
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.lifeinpleasantville.com/holding-her-back-just-a-little/#comments">
           12 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         By Candace Derickx
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.lifeinpleasantville.com/holding-her-back-just-a-little/my-baby/" rel="attachment wp-att-5500">
          <img alt="my baby" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5500" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/my-baby-e1360766966878.jpeg?fit=560%2C362"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ahhh, my oldest daughter. She is me. I am her. It is for this reason of course that we butt heads frequently. After all, we are both always right.
        </p>
        <p>
         So you understand of course that No is not a word that my daughter has ever accepted without question  ever. Which is really just karma giving me a good old kick in the head for being exactly this way with my mother. Bitch. (Karma, not my mother)
        </p>
        <p>
         I tell you all this because last Friday I handed down a no. A no so unjust, so monumentally unfair, that I was given the
         <a href="http://www.yummymummyclub.ca/blogs/candace-derickx-see-mummy-juggle/i-am-the-worst-mother-ever" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.yummymummyclub.ca/blogs/candace-derickx-see-mummy-juggle/i-am-the-worst-mother-ever', 'worst mother ever']);" target="_blank">
          worst mother ever
         </a>
         designation, yet again.
        </p>
        <p>
         She asked to go to our local community dance.
        </p>
        <p>
         Shes asked before and the answer has always been no, but because it conflicts with cheerleading practice I wasnt getting much kick back on it. This Friday however, hell froze over and cheerleading was canceled due to bad weather and the dance was not. It was the perfect storm.
        </p>
        <p>
         It started with a friend of hers telling her to come and that the dance was perfectly safe and prodding Why wont your mom let you go anyway?
        </p>
        <p>
         But Moooom, so-and-so is going, and she says its safe.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cue the standard retort.
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont care what other moms are letting their kids do.
        </p>
        <p>
         And I dont. If other parents want to send their kids to this dance, thats great. Im not here to question why. The bottom line is that my instinct tells me its not right for my daughter yet. And very often all we have is our instinct to guide us.
        </p>
        <p>
         But then the tears started. The big ugly cry with the gasps for air and the runny nose. The cry that makes a mother start to question her instinct. So I sat and I thought hard about why I didnt want her at this dance.
        </p>
        <p>
         Shes 10.
         <br/>
         I think attending dances are milestones in life.
         <br/>
         That everything in life doesnt have to happen before youre 12.
         <br/>
         That Ive heard negative comments about this dance from others.
         <br/>
         That I didnt think 10 year olds and 14 year olds should be at the same dance together.
         <br/>
         That I didnt want her at a dance with 300 kids.
        </p>
        <p>
         And I gave her all these reasons and in the end my resolve was strong. And she hated me.
        </p>
        <p>
         But heres the one reason I kept to myself. I dont want to push her over the edge. And by edge, I mean the edge of childhood.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enter her room and its a strange mix of dolls, boy band posters, stuffed animals and make-up. She is dabbling on either side now.
        </p>

        <p>
         And I know I cant keep her on this side of childhood forever. But this time is so short, it is so fleeting, that Im not going to help rush it along either. For its only a matter of time before the dolls get packed away and boy posters get replaced by boys, that stuffies become childish and makeup gets worn out of the houseto a dance.
        </p>
        <p>
         And when that happens Ill be along for the ride to help her maneuver through the most challenging times of a girls life. But not just yet. Not while we still have a little bit of childhood to cling on to.
        </p>
