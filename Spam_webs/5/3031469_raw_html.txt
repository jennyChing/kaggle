           <p class="title" style="margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 10px">
            <font size="4">
             Renee's Kitchen Garden Designs
            </font>
            <align>
             <p align="left" style="margin-right: 15px; margin-left:10px">
              <b>
               <font color="#800000" size="4">
                <img align="right" alt="garden design plan" border="0" height="190" src="http://www.reneesgarden.com/Email/images/gardenplan.jpg" width="288"/>
               </font>
              </b>
              We've created two Kitchen Garden 
Design Plans designed to maximize 
space for a succession of tasty vegetables and herbs throughout the spring, 
summer and fall. Each shows you what to plant, when to plant, and what crop 
follows another as the seasons change.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              The
              <b>
               <a class="textlink" href="long-season-garden.pdf">
                Long Summer, Mild Winter Garden
               </a>
              </b>
              is for gardeners in parts of the country where summers are long and winters are 
mild without deep frost:
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.usna.usda.gov/Hardzone/ushzmap.html">
               <font color="#004000">
                USDA
               </font>
              </a>
              zones 
8-10, as well as warmer areas of zone 7.
              <font size="2">
               <br/>
              </font>
              <i>
               <font size="2">
                California Trial Garden Manager, Lindsay Del Carlo, laid out this plan.
               </font>
              </i>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              The
              <b>
               <a class="textlink" href="short-season-garden.pdf">
                Short Summer, 
Cold Winter Garden
               </a>
              </b>
              is for gardeners in parts of the country where winter
              <br/>
              starts early and brings hard freezes:
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.usna.usda.gov/Hardzone/ushzmap.html">
                <font color="#004000">
                 USDA
                </font>
               </a>
               <font color="#004000">
               </font>
              </b>
              zones 
3-6 and colder areas of zone 7.
              <i>
               <font size="2">
                <br/>
                Vermont Trial Garden Manager, Jay Leshinsky, laid out this 
plan.
               </font>
              </i>
              <b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Each kitchen garden design plan has a Quick Order link so you can select and order 
seeds of my favorite varieties for your garden.
               <br/>
               <br/>
              </b>
              <i>
               <font size="2">
                Note: These plans do not cover areas with extremely hot and 
intense summers such as large parts of Texas and Arizona. Gardeners there should 
contact their local
                <font color="#004000">
                 <a class="textlink" href="mastergard.html">
                  Master Gardeners
                 </a>
                </font>
                <a href="mastergard.html">
                </a>
                or a good local garden center for advice on timing and crops.
                <br/>
               </font>
              </i>
             </p>
