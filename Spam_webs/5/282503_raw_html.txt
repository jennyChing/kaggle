              <p>
               Do you want to don a beard and eye-patch and scour for treasures? Short of raping, pillaging and commandeering boats, attractions around the U.S. offer opportunities to be a pirate for a day, or at least be surrounded by buccaneers. Not since Johnny Depp assumed the role of Jack Sparrow in the Pirates of the Caribbean film series has pirate tourism been such a hot commodity. Whether youre more into a laid-pack pirate cruise, or yearn for a rowdy pirate invasion, there is an option to satisfy your inner buccaneer, with some attractions designed to stave off a hot summer day with water-filled fun. Ahoy, mate!
              </p>

               <li class="dv-item dv-item-first">
                <h2>
                 1.&amp;nbspPirates of the Coeur d'Alene cruise, Lake Coeur dAlene, Idaho
                </h2>
                <div class="m">
                 <img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/U.S./876/493/pirate_idaho.jpg?ve=1&amp;tl=1"/>
                 <div class="caption alt">
                  <p>
                   Joel Riner
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <p>
                 Packing a lot of fun into a 90-minute sail, which departs twice daily, the family-friendly
                 <a href="http://www.cdacruises.com ">
                  Pirates of the Coeur dAlene
                 </a>
                 cruise is escorted by pirates. Kids engage in sing-a-longs, swordfights, face painting and treasure hunts. As the cruise is sponsored by
                 <a href="http://www.cdaresort.com">
                  The Coeur dAlene Resort
                 </a>
                 , stay at the posh 338-room resort and extend the buccaneer fun until dawn.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <i>
                  Available through Aug. 24; $32.75 adults, $22.75 kids
                 </i>
                </p>
               </li>
               <li class="dv-item dv-item-first">
                <h2>
                 2.&amp;nbspPirate Beach at LEGOLAND Discovery Center, Grapevine, Texas
                </h2>
                <div class="m">
                 <img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/U.S./876/493/legoland_pirate.jpg?ve=1&amp;tl=1"/>
                 <div class="caption alt">
                  <p>
                   Legoland
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <p>
                 Set to open Aug. 16 at
                 <a href="http://www.legolanddiscoverycenter.com/dallasfw">
                  LEGOLAND Discovery Center
                 </a>
                 in Dallas/Fort Worth, Pirate Beach puts a cool spin on visiting Texas third-largest city. Designed for kids (between the ages of three and 10), the 10,000-square-foot water-parks beach adopts a pirate theme. Thirty gadgets  from cannons to fountains, and a 50-gallon bucket dumping water  spray water on guests all day long. Tucked into a play area are treasure chests, pirates and cannons.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <i>
                  Admission to LEGOLAND Discovery Center, $14.40 (children two and under free).
                 </i>
                </p>
               </li>
               <li class="dv-item dv-item-first">
                <h2>
                 3.&amp;nbspPirates of the Caribbean, Anaheim, Calif.
                </h2>
                <div class="m">
                 <img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/U.S./876/493/PiratesoftheCaribbean14-No_Escape-533x800.jpg?ve=1&amp;tl=1"/>
                 <div class="caption alt">
                  <p>
                   Disneyland
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <p>
                 Since 1967, Disneylands
                 <a href="https://disneyland.disney.go.com/attractions/disneyland/pirates-of-the-caribbean/">
                  Pirates of the Caribbean
                 </a>
                 attraction has transported guests to the 17
                 <sup>
                  th
                 </sup>
                 century with spectacular sets and special effects, including dozens of life like audio-animatronics figures. Pirates Lair  which debuted in 2007  takes it to the next level. Hop on a raft to Tom Sawyer Island, where Captain Jack is overtaking that island. Enjoy special effects, a sunken ship, a blacksmith shop, a suspended bone cage and  of course  pirates. Pirates League (Sept. 12-Oct. 31) offers a full-on pirate makeover, including makeup, beards, props and eye patches.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <i>
                  Admission to Disneyland, $90-$96 (one-day ticket); Pirates League, $29.95-$34.95
                 </i>
                </p>
               </li>
               <li class="dv-item dv-item-first">
                <h2>
                 4.&amp;nbspPerdido Key Pirate Fest, Perdido Key, Florida
                </h2>
                <div class="m">
                 <img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/U.S./876/493/PerdidoKeyPirate Fest.jpg?ve=1&amp;tl=1"/>
                 <div class="caption alt">
                  <p>
                   Perdido Key Pirate Fest
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <p>
                 For this annual family-friendly fete  hosted in Perdido Key, Floridas Big Lagoon Park on Oct. 25-26 expect a multi-faceted look at piracy.
                 <a href=" http://www.visitperdido.com/pirate-fest">
                  Perdido Key Pirate Fest
                 </a>
                 is chock full of pirate games, a treasure hunt (including a grand prize: gift certificates to local restaurants and condominium stays), live music, face painting, arts &amp; crafts activities, and Polynesian-style dancing.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <i>
                  Admission free with $6 park entrance.
                 </i>
                </p>
               </li>
               <li class="dv-item dv-item-first">
                <h2>
                 5.&amp;nbspBeaufort Pirate Invasion, Beaufort, North Carolina
                </h2>
                <div class="m">
                 <img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/U.S./876/493/Beaufort_Pirate_Invasion_Ship_1.jpg?ve=1&amp;tl=1"/>
                 <div class="caption alt">
                  <p>
                   The Crystal Coast Tourism Authority
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <p>
                 Modern-day pirates invade Beauforts waterfront in August each year (this year its Aug. 8-9), a fitting site for the
                 <a href="http://www.beaufortpirateinvasion.com ">
                  Beaufort Pirate Invasion
                 </a>
                 as troops fought off Spanish pirates from invading Beaufort several times during 1747. Historical demonstrations cover a hanging, mock trial, sword fighting, cannon firing and a blackpowder-weapons display, and pirates attack by land on Friday and by sea on Saturday. Pirate activities are also at the North Carolina Maritime Museum, with Blackbeard hosting a pirate lunch and fashion show on Saturday. A BBQ dinner and pirate play is on Friday night.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <i>
                  Museum and fest activities, free; pirate lunch, $15; pirate dinner, $17 adults and $8 kids (12 and under).
                 </i>
                </p>
               </li>
               <li class="dv-item dv-item-first">
                <h2>
                 6.&amp;nbspAvon Luau, Cape Hatteras, North Carolina
                </h2>
                <div class="m">
                 <img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/U.S./876/493/AvonLuau983757_790095141034649_7149936172008721926_n.jpg?ve=1&amp;tl=1"/>
                 <div class="caption alt">
                  <p>
                   Avon Luau
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <p>
                 Merging mermaids and pirates, Avon Luau happens on Tuesday nights at Koru Village Spa &amp; Beach Klub Resort, located within the Cape Hatteras National Seashore area. A buccaneer-outfitted band called The Freebooters jams while guests mingle among live mermaids, fire performers and drumming circles, with island-themed food and drinks served from an oceanfront bar. Events take place poolside and swimming is encouraged.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <i>
                  Admission, $5 adults; free kids (12 and under).
                 </i>
                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
               </li>
              </ul>
