      <div style="padding-left:5px;padding-right:5px;">
       <h1>
        Postcard from Paris  Icarus en Ville at spring art fair Art Paris
       </h1>
       <p>
        <b>
         By
         <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/author/matthewrose/" rel="author" title="Posts by matthew rose">
          matthew rose
         </a>
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         April 6, 2014
					
					
						
							  

						
						2 Comments
        </b>
       </p>
       <img alt="Space Odyssey: Chinese artist Li Wei flies above the crowd at the Grand Palais in Paris." class="img-main wp-post-image" height="800" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/Li-Wei.jpg" width="600"/>
       <div style="clear: both;">
       </div>
       <p>
        <em>
         [Matthew visits the spring art fair, Art Paris, and talks about the infusion of Chinese art there.  the artblog editors]
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Even the casual Paris observer will note that the Elysian Fields (Les Champs Elyses) have slowly become a bit more Chinese over the last two decades. Sushi restaurants are run by Chinese immigrants; old French tabacs and cafs are being taken over by Chinese and Vietnamese families. Chinese is almost a second language in the Louvre and my friends children all learn Mandarin at school. So its no wonder
        <a href="http://artparis.fr/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://artparis.fr/', 'Art Paris']);" target="_blank">
         Art Paris
        </a>
        , the spring salon that takes place each spring, made China the guest of honor at its wall-to-wall mall of 140 galleries at the always-gorgeous Grand Palais.
       </p>
       <h2>
        An impressive showing of Chinese artists
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_52966" style="width: 598px">
        <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/Li-Wei.jpg">
         <img alt="Space Odyssey: Chinese artist Li Wei flies above the crowd at the Grand Palais in Paris. " class="size-large wp-image-52966" height="784" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/Li-Wei-588x784.jpg" width="588"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Space odyssey: Chinese artist Li Wei flies above the crowd at the Grand Palais in Paris.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_52967" style="width: 598px">
        <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/WANG-DU.jpg">
         <img alt="Wang Du's Women were not overtly even Asia, but served to illustrate how the Chinese have made the art world their own, especially in Paris. " class="size-large wp-image-52967" height="784" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/WANG-DU-588x784.jpg" width="588"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Wang Dus Women were not Asian, but served to illustrate how the Chinese have made the art world their own, especially in Paris.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Chinese artists cropped up like dandelions, but a viewing space was set up to celebrate some of the better-known namesalthough there was no sign of broken vases by celebrated outcast/insider
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ai_Weiwei" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ai_Weiwei', 'Ai Weiwei']);" target="_blank">
         Ai Weiwei
        </a>
        . The group exhibition featured comic resin sculptures of what appeared to be three muses: Femme, femme, Femm, Fem, fe(2006), each with her own specially-sized head by star Wang Du. They were curious, because the women were not overtly Asian, but extremely well-made. Whatever you might feel about the Chinese wavetoo cartoony, politically shy or courageous, or even too colorful or clever its quality is felt through its great attention to detail and a clarity one doesnt find in Europe. And typically, the works involve figures. Perhaps its part of the DNA from the days of Mao and socialist realism? Or the mindblowing and innate ability to copy from the world? (Download the Art Paris PR information on the Chinese artists here:
        <a href="https://admin.mailpro.com/imgClients/12/mailpro10851/DP/cp_LA_CHINE_ArtParis_ArtFair_us.pdf" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','download','https://admin.mailpro.com/imgClients/12/mailpro10851/DP/cp_LA_CHINE_ArtParis_ArtFair_us.pdf']);" target="_blank">
         Art Paris China
        </a>
        ;or view the all the artworks here:
        <a href="http://artparis.fr/fr/artworks" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://artparis.fr/fr/artworks', 'Art Paris 2014']);" target="_blank">
         Art Paris 2014
        </a>
        .)
       </p>
       <h2>
        From tongue-in-cheek to just plain cheeky
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_52968" style="width: 598px">
        <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/MAO.jpg">
         <img alt="Mao has long been a hit in the West ever since Warhol bathed him in art world light. Above: Jean-Pierre Raynaud's " class="size-large wp-image-52968" height="441" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/MAO-588x441.jpg" width="588"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Mao has long been a hit in the West, ever since Warhol bathed him in art world light. Above: Jean-Pierre Raynauds Flag on a Frame.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Big-name French galleries like
        <a href="http://www.danieltemplon.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.danieltemplon.com', 'Daniel Templon']);" target="_blank">
         Daniel Templon
        </a>
        chimed in and showed the terribly gleeful canvases of Yue Minjun (Free Sky n1, oil on canvas, 100 x 90 cm, 2012), an artist who recently enjoyed a retrospective here at the
        <a href="http://fondation.cartier.com/#/en/art-contemporain/26/exhibitions/294/all-the-exhibitions/758/yue-minjun-l-ombre-du-fou-rire/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://fondation.cartier.com/#/en/art-contemporain/26/exhibitions/294/all-the-exhibitions/758/yue-minjun-l-ombre-du-fou-rire/', 'Fondation Cartier']);" target="_blank">
         Fondation Cartier
        </a>
        . French star Jean-Pierre Raynaud weighed in with a 2001 work at
        <a href="http://www.ilovemyjob.eu" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ilovemyjob.eu', 'Galerie Caroline Smulders']);" target="_blank">
         Galerie Caroline Smulders
        </a>
        Flag on a Frame, featuring a Communist-era portrait of Mao bracketed by a pair of Chinese flags. I wonder what Chinese President Xi Jinping said when he saw this piece while visiting the fair on opening night. Im sure Im not the only one who pondered the notion.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_52970" style="width: 598px">
        <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/PEPPERMINT.jpg">
         <img alt="Manuel Merida's Usuyki/Chantier II takes kinetic art and gives it a peppermint flavor." class="size-large wp-image-52970" height="441" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/PEPPERMINT-588x441.jpg" width="588"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Manuel Meridas Usuyki/Chantier II takes kinetic art and gives it a peppermint flavor.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Art Paris offered a full art-world buffet, with many works standing out due to their large-scale installations, like
        <a href="http://espace-zafra.com/ " onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://espace-zafra.com/ ', 'Manuel Merida']);" target="_blank">
         Manuel Merida
        </a>
        s Usuyuki/Chantier II wall-sized, 11-meter installation. It lookedand movedlike a perpetual peppermint candy-making machine.
        <a href="http://www.ritschfisch.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ritschfisch.com/', 'Galerie J.P. Ritsch-Fisch']);" target="_blank">
         Galerie J.P. Ritsch-Fisch
        </a>
        showed the 1989 six-meter drawing by British outsider artist
        <a href="https://www.google.fr/search?q=chris+hipkiss&amp;client=firefox-a&amp;hs=s8e&amp;rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&amp;channel=sb&amp;source=lnms&amp;tbm=isch&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=qrU6U5PJG8qc0AWo9YDQDQ&amp;ved=0CAgQ_AUoAQ&amp;biw=1846&amp;bih=943" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.google.fr/search?q=chris+hipkiss&amp;client=firefox-a&amp;hs=s8e&amp;rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&amp;channel=sb&amp;source=lnms&amp;tbm=isch&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=qrU6U5PJG8qc0AWo9YDQDQ&amp;ved=0CAgQ_AUoAQ&amp;biw=1846&amp;bih=943', 'Chris Hipkiss']);" target="_blank">
         Chris Hipkiss
        </a>
        . The work, a haunting and obsessed apocalyptic vision in pencil, is destined for a French museum, according to the Strasbourg dealer.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_52971" style="width: 496px">
        <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/VASERELY.jpg">
         <img alt="Grandpop of Op Art: Victor Vasarely's 1959 canvas Geminorium.  " class="size-full wp-image-52971" height="717" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/VASERELY.jpg" width="486"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Grandpop of Op Art: Victor Vasarelys 1959 canvas, Geminorium.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        I had to stop by one booth showing classic late 1950s geometric canvases and drawings. The Hungarian-born Victor Vaserely (1906-1997) was the granddad of Op Art, and his 1959 canvas Geminorium is a perfect example of that slice of history, on view at the
        <a href="http://www.gimpel-muller.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.gimpel-muller.com/', 'Paris-based Gimpel Muller']);" target="_blank">
         Paris-based Gimpel Muller
        </a>
        . And if you mistook Vera Molnars 1955 gouache lent mouvement sur fond blue, orange, rouge et noir (slow movement in blue, orange, red and black), for a Damien Hirst, well, thats good news. The dot grid was not cooked up in London in the 21st century.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_52972" style="width: 550px">
        <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/Yue-Minjun.jpg">
         <img alt="Chinese art star Yue Minjun has been laughing all the way to the bank for years. His canvases are self-portraits of the artist, usually in his underwear." class="size-full wp-image-52972" height="600" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/Yue-Minjun.jpg" width="540"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Chinese art star Yue Minjun has been laughing all the way to the bank for years. His canvases are self-portraits of the artist, usually in his underwear.
        </p>
       </div>
       <h2>
        What it is to be human
       </h2>
       <p>
        As with most art fairs, there were dozens of works that wanted to shock, and they didsome with a good sense of humor regarding the human condition, like Bernardi Roigs Practices to suck the light (hanging), 2012, from
        <a href="http://www.galerie-mam.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.galerie-mam.com/', 'Mario Mauroner Contemporary Art, Salzburg/Vienna']);" target="_blank">
         Mario Mauroner Contemporary Art, Salzburg/Vienna
        </a>
        . We all try so hard, dont we, to lift ourselves up beyond our own condition? And the results are often depressingfunny but depressing.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_52975" style="width: 598px">
        <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/MOTH.jpg">
         <img alt="Bernardi Roig's reinvention of Icarus  another view of the human condition: perverse and humorous." class="size-large wp-image-52975" height="784" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/MOTH-588x784.jpg" width="588"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Bernardi Roigs reinvention of Icarus. Another view of the human condition: perverse and humorous.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        And in the same spirit, there was Chinese artist Li Weis own attempt to fly. Sporting a space suit, the artist was lifted aloft via a handsome crane so that he could hover above a crowd of about 200. The
        <a href="https://www.google.fr/search?q=Li+Wei&amp;client=firefox-a&amp;hs=hEJ&amp;rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&amp;channel=sb&amp;tbm=isch&amp;tbo=u&amp;source=univ&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=DKM6U7aoEqTu0gWL5IDgCg&amp;ved=0CDoQsAQ&amp;biw=1846&amp;bih=943" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.google.fr/search?q=Li+Wei&amp;client=firefox-a&amp;hs=hEJ&amp;rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&amp;channel=sb&amp;tbm=isch&amp;tbo=u&amp;source=univ&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=DKM6U7aoEqTu0gWL5IDgCg&amp;ved=0CDoQsAQ&amp;biw=1846&amp;bih=943', 'artist obsesses about flying, and his photographs of feet and people in the air have long been a huge hit']);" target="_blank">
         artist obsesses about flying, and his photographs of feet and people in the air have long been a huge hit
        </a>
        . As he was wavering about aloft, I wondered if this were his first time in the in the air himselfif not his first time in Paris. One observer said, Well, sure, its not the moon, but the Grand Palais is not so bad.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_52973" style="width: 491px">
        <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/BRICK.jpg">
         <img alt="Free Wang Kai Cheng for 10 euros a brick.  His performance was communism as capitalist enterprise." class="size-full wp-image-52973" height="775" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/BRICK.jpg" width="481"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Free Wang Kai Cheng for 10 euros a brick. His performance was communism as capitalist enterprise.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        On my way out, I ran across this young Beijing-based artist in a Kafkaesque castle of 1,000 bricks. Wang Kai Cheng used a Chinese design for 1000 red bricks, had them made in France and shipped to the Grand Palais, and then quickly walled himself in. One of us can be free offered visitors the chance to free him for 10 euros a brick, each of which the artist signed and numberedand bagged! I liked the idea of paying cash for Communist art and the idea of freeing himin Paris!so I bought one (number 98) and dutifully lugged it home to put next to my half-dozen plants. Let freedom grow!
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_52976" style="width: 569px">
        <a href="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/SIMON-EVANS.jpg">
         <img alt="Canson's Prix 2014 brought together five artist-finalists under the roof of the Grand Palais.  Above : Englishman Simon Evan's &quot;Companion.&quot;  No, it's not the Year of the Rat.  " class="size-full wp-image-52976" height="746" src="http://www.theartblog.org/wp-content/uploaded/2014/04/SIMON-EVANS.jpg" width="559"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Cansons Prix 2014 brought together five artist-finalists under the roof of the Grand Palais. Above: Englishman Simon Evans Companion. No, its not the Year of the Rat.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        A final note: As I sat down to write up my notes, I came across this headline on the computer interwebsChinas Secret Tiger Meat Trade. Why am I not surprised that there is currently a scandal in China, and now the world, centering around an illegal tiger-eating dinner club for wealthy businessmen in Guangdong province? The report said that, while forbidden, the practice is popular among Chinas ruling class, who enjoy watching the animals die before dining on them. Art world metaphor?
       </p>
