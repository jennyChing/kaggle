   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    <i>
     Dancemania
    </i>
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
     Jump to:
     <a href="#mw-head">
      navigation
     </a>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="hatnote">
      This article is about a compilation series.  For the label, see
      <a href="/wiki/Dance_Mania_(record_label)" title="Dance Mania (record label)">
       Dance Mania (record label)
      </a>
      .  For the album, see
      <a href="/wiki/Dance_Mania_(album)" title="Dance Mania (album)">
       Dance Mania (album)
      </a>
      .
     </div>
     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Dancemania
       </b>
      </i>
      is a series of remix
      <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">
       compilation albums
      </a>
      by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toshiba_EMI" title="Toshiba EMI">
       i-DANCE
      </a>
      . The series deals primarily with
      <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">
       dance music
      </a>
      , especially
      <a href="/wiki/Eurodance" title="Eurodance">
       Eurodance
      </a>
      . Despite many of its tracks being made by various musicians from all over the world and mainly from the European continent, the albums have been released exclusively in
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japan
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The first issue,
      <i>
       Dancemania 1
      </i>
      , was released on April 10, 1996 with artists including
      <a href="/wiki/E-Rotic" title="E-Rotic">
       E-Rotic
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Cut_%27N%27_Move" title="Cut 'N' Move">
       Cut 'N' Move
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Me_%26_My" title="Me &amp; My">
       Me &amp; My
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Maxx_(band)" title="Maxx (band)">
       Maxx
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Basic_Element_(music_group)" title="Basic Element (music group)">
       Basic Element
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Magic_Affair" title="Magic Affair">
       Magic Affair
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Odyssey_(band)" title="Odyssey (band)">
       Odyssey
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Captain_Jack_(band)" title="Captain Jack (band)">
       Captain Jack
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Interactive_(band)" title="Interactive (band)">
       Interactive
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_Pilots" title="Space Pilots">
       Space Pilots
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quicksilver" title="DJ Quicksilver">
       DJ Quicksilver
      </a>
      as the leading mixer.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amazon_1_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-amazon_1-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       1
      </i>
      debuted at number 14 on the
      <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">
       Oricon
      </a>
      weekly album chart in April 1996
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and reached number 4 in the same chart in May 1996
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and ranked number 56 in the yearly Top 100 best-selling album chart in 1996 with 480,980 copies sold.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Dancemania
      </i>
      has spawned many sub-series projects, such as
      <i>
       Summers &amp; Winters
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Bass
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Euro Mix
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Super Techno
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Classics
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Delux
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Game
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Club
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Zip Mania
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Trance
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Trance Paradise
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dancemania_Covers" title="Dancemania Covers">
        Covers
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dancemania_Speed" title="Dancemania Speed">
        Speed
       </a>
      </i>
      . Many of these sub-series have further each spawned their own sub-series projects.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emi_index_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-emi_index-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The series released approximately 50 volumes in its first four years and the label claims that over 10 million copies were sold by the end of the 20th century.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Many songs from the series have been licensed for use in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bemani" title="Bemani">
       Bemani
      </a>
      games by
      <a href="/wiki/Konami" title="Konami">
       Konami
      </a>
      , such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dance_Dance_Revolution" title="Dance Dance Revolution">
        Dance Dance Revolution
       </a>
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dance_Maniax" title="Dance Maniax">
        Dance Maniax
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
