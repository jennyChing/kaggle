              <a href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/homeandgarden/2012/04/vintage-silverware-garden-markers/" title="Vintage Silverware Garden Markers">
               Vintage Silverware Garden Markers
              </a>
             </h1>
             <div class="meta-tasks">
              <span class="author">
               Posted by Ree
              </span>
              <a class="comment-number-wrap" href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/homeandgarden/2012/04/vintage-silverware-garden-markers/#comments" title="comments">
               <div class="comment-number">
                74
               </div>
               <span>
                Comments
               </span>
              </a>
             </div>
            </header>
            <div class="post-padding">
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/7058655621/" title="TPW_3844 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_3844" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7092/7058655621_38627b5561_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              I received this beautiful vintage flatware as a gift recently (thank you, Emm!) which was plenty exciting for me considering vintage flatware makes my soul soar.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6912574046/" title="TPW_3846 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_3846" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm6.staticflickr.com/5195/6912574046_dc72403876_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              But then I looked a little closerand I realized what they were.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/7058656157/" title="TPW_3849 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_3849" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm6.staticflickr.com/5324/7058656157_b2774188e5_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Garden markers! Dear goodness. Im in Heaven.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6912575370/" title="TPW_3855 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_3855" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm6.staticflickr.com/5452/6912575370_a5b905a184_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Basically, the old flatware is hammered so that its somewhat flatthen its stamped with names of different plants.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6912574844/" title="TPW_3852 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_3852" class="width_427 frame-img" height="640" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7065/6912574844_e8a8846c85_z.jpg" width="427"/>
              </a>
              The verdict: I am in love with these. Theyre whimsical yet practical, and (I speak from firsthand experience here) make such a sweet gift for anyone who loves to garden.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/7058657513/" title="TPW_3856 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_3856" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7117/7058657513_6b1ee711c8_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Im not sure where these were made, but I found several Etsy shops that offer silverware garden markers. Heres a handful:
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop/monkeysalwayslook?ref=seller_info" target="_blank">
               Monkeys Always Look
              </a>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/66733281/custom-silverplate-silverware-garden" target="_blank">
               Heavy Metal Milkman
              </a>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/69739169/you-pick-6-spoons-vintage-flatware?utm_source=googleproduct&amp;utm_medium=syndication&amp;utm_campaign=GPS" target="_blank">
               Pretty Paris
              </a>
             </p>

              </h4>
              <p>
               These are actually really easy to makeall you need is a heavy hammer/sledgehammer, extra fine-grade steel wool, a permenant marker, and some letter stamps. You can google letter stamp set and should easily find some. Some Michaels locations (craft store) sell them in 2 sizes, too. You can google spoon garden markers how to and can find blog tutorials or how-to videos on You Tube. It works best if you flatten the spoons and stamp on a hard surface, such as an anvil or cement. First you hammer them flat with sledgehammer (open, food-side down toward the surface, hammer the rounded back), then you stamp the letters on the front of the spoon (the part where the food would go!)I use the sledgehammer for this step, too. A couple of good whacks does the trick if you have a hard surface (dont use a wood surface) behind the spoon. Then you color in the letters with permenant marker and buff away the excess marker with extra fine-grade steel wool. What youre left with are indented/stamped letters that are darkened for contrast. Sometimes (if youre fussy) you need to flip the spoon back over and smooth out (with the sledgehammer) where the impact of the letter stamps dented the back. Super easy and great results!
              </p>
