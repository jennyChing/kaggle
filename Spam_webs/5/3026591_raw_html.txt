&lt;p&gt;You are simply not going to believe how simple this caramel sauce recipe is! Granted it's not as simple as opening a bottle of store bought caramel sauce, but I took a look at the ingredients on one of those bottles recently and found no less than 20 ingredients, including more high fructose corn syrup and sugar than milk and a myriad of dyes and preservatives. Blech!&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Funsophisticook.com%2Fcaramel-sauce-recipe%2F&#038;media=http%3A%2F%2Funsophisticook.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2013%2F10%2FCaramel-Sauce-Recipe.jpg&#038;description=Homemade%20Caramel%20Sauce%20in%20Your%20Slow%20Cooker%20--%20you%20won't%20believe%20how%20simple%20this%20caramel%20sauce%20recipe%20is%2C%20just%20one%20ingredient!!!" data-pin-do="buttonPin" data-pin-config="beside"&gt;&lt;img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In contrast, this homemade caramel sauce requires only sweetened condensed milk! I was happy to see that the brand that I chose lists only milk and sugar as the ingredients, so that's a far cry from the store bought version.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://unsophisticook.com/caramel-sauce-recipe/" title="Homemade Caramel Sauce Recipe"&gt;&lt;img src="http://unsophisticook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Homemade-Caramel-Sauce-Recipe.jpg" alt="Homemade Caramel Sauce Recipe" width="529" height="353" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4587" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;But the very best part about this caramel recipe is that it doesn't require you to stand over the stove for long periods of time stirring and watching or repeatedly microwaving until your desired level of caramelization occurs. I make it right in my slow cooker which allows it to cook at a low temperature for a long period of time, so there's virtually no chance of burning it.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;While we're on the topic of slow cookers, have you seen these adorable &lt;a href="http://linksynergy.walmart.com/fs-bin/click?id=ZHMrjVFhx/k&#038;offerid=100143&#038;type=10&#038;tmpid=1082&#038;RD_PARM1=http%253A%252F%252Fwww.walmart.com%252Fip%252FCrock-Pot-6-Quart-Cook-N-Carry-Slow-Cooker%252F24183277" title="Patterned Crock-Pot Slow Cookers at Walmart" target="_blank" rel="nofollow"&gt;patterned Crock-Pot Slow Cookers at Walmart&lt;/a&gt;? They caught my eye when I was picking up another box of jelly jars for this recipe. As much as I love my basic stainless steel slow cooker, I've been seriously considering picking one of these cuties up to brighten up my kitchen.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://unsophisticook.com/caramel-sauce-recipe/" title="Homemade Caramel Recipe"&gt;&lt;img src="http://unsophisticook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Homemade-Caramel-Recipe.jpg" alt="Homemade Caramel Recipe" width="529" height="353" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4586" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Some of you may have made homemade caramel sauce in the past using a similar method where you boiled an unopened can of sweetened condensed milk on the stovetop. Please don't do this again! Not only is it very difficult to tell when the caramel is finished cooking, but it's also &lt;em&gt;extremely&lt;/em&gt; dangerous because the cans can explode. Not to mention there are surely numerous chemicals leaching from the can into your cooked caramel...&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Instead, transfer the sweetened condensed milk into a clean jelly jar, place a lid on it and tighten the band down well. I filled my jars up to the 6-oz. line to allow for expansion during cooking. As these jars are made to withstand the home canning process, they're much safer, and they allow you to visually check the caramel for doneness.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Place the jars in your slow cooker and then fill the slow cooker up with water to about one inch above the top of the lids. Place the lid on your slow cooker and cook on low for 7.5-8 hours, making sure not to let the water drop below the lid level. I didn't need to add water at any point, but I'd still recommend checking for safety's sake.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;After the caramel sauce has cooked for about 7 hours, you'll want to check it periodically to see if its reached your desired doneness. You should be able to do this just by looking through the lid so that you don't let the heat escape. When it's done, remove the jars from the water and allow them to cool for about 20 minutes.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Note that you may hear the lids sealing themselves as the caramel cools, but they are not sufficiently processed for long term shelf storage.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://unsophisticook.com/caramel-sauce-recipe/" title="Homemade Caramel Sauce"&gt;&lt;img src="http://unsophisticook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Homemade-Caramel-Sauce.jpg" alt="Homemade Caramel Sauce" width="353" height="529" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4588" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;You may notice that the caramel seems a little chunky at this point. No worries -- it's absolutely delicious and I have no problem scarfing it down with some of the beautiful seasonal apples available right now. But if you want a silky smooth homemade caramel sauce, simply whisk it briskly until any lumps are gone.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://unsophisticook.com/caramel-sauce-recipe/" title="Caramel Sauce Recipe"&gt;&lt;img src="http://unsophisticook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Caramel-Sauce-Recipe.jpg" alt="Caramel Sauce Recipe" width="353" height="529" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4589" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Store leftover caramel in the refrigerator -- that is if there is any! Bring it to room temperature before serving or heat until warm, about 30 seconds at a time in the microwave, whisking between each heating.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div id="zlrecipe-container-139" class="zlrecipe-container-border" style="border: 1px dotted;"&gt;

&lt;div id="zl-recipe-link-139" class="zl-recipe-link fl-r zl-rmvd"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div id="zlrecipe-title" itemprop="name" class="b-b h-1 strong" &gt;Homemade Caramel Sauce&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class="zlmeta zlclear"&gt;
&lt;div class="fl-l width-50"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class="fl-l width-50"&gt;
&lt;p id="zlrecipe-yield"&gt;Yield: &lt;span itemprop="recipeYield"&gt;5 (6-oz.) jars&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class="zlclear"&gt;
      &lt;/div&gt;
&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class="img-desc-wrap"&gt;
&lt;p class="t-a-c hide-card hide-print"&gt;
			  &lt;img class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://unsophisticook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Caramel-Recipe.jpg" title="Homemade Caramel Sauce" alt="Homemade Caramel Sauce" style="width: 490px;" /&gt;
			&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p id="zlrecipe-ingredients" class="h-4 strong"&gt;Ingredients:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list"&gt;
&lt;li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"&gt;3 (14-oz.) cans sweetened condensed milk
&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"&gt;5 (8-oz.) jelly jars
&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"&gt;water&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p id="zlrecipe-instructions" class="h-4 strong"&gt;Instructions:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ol id="zlrecipe-instructions-list" class="instructions"&gt;
&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;Fill each jelly jar up to the 6-oz. line with the sweetened condensed milk. Three cans of sweetened condensed milk should fill about 5 jars. Place a lid on each jar and tighten the band down well.
&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;Place the jars inside the slow cooker and pour water in to about 1" above the top of the jars.
&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;Cover and cook on low for 7.5-8 hours, checking occasionally to see if more water needs to be added. Do not let the water drop below the lid level.
&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;Carefully remove the jars from the slow cooker and enjoy! The caramel may be a tad chunky and is perfectly delicious as is, but if you prefer a creamier caramel, simple whisk it until it's silky smooth.
&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;Store in the refrigerator. Bring to room temperature or heat until warm before serving.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ol&gt;
