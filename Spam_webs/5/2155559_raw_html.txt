     <a href="http://thediscoveryblog.com/6-living-room-diy-projects-for-your-new-home/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to 6 Living Room DIY Projects For Your New Home">
      6 Living Room DIY Projects For Your New Home
     </a>
    </h1>
    <div id="content">
     <div class="col_main">
      <div class="post-meta">
       Posted by
       <a href="http://thediscoveryblog.com/author/vserpas/" rel="author" title="Posts by Vserpas">
        Vserpas
       </a>
       <span class="separator">
        
       </span>
       December 3, 2013 at 8:23 pm
      </div>
      <!-- /.post-meta -->
      <div class="post-9854 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-home-improvement tag-diy tag-home tag-home-improvement-2 tag-house tag-living-room tag-projects clearfix" id="post-9854">
       <div class="entry">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Once you finally sign the last piece of paperwork and grab the keys for your new place, its inevitable that youre going to find something that you want to change right away. For that reason, weve created a list of DIY projects for your new living room. Some of these can be done over the weekend, or some may take a bit longer, but youll be proud to call these projects your own.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Update Your Window Treatments
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The window treatment is often the first thing that people see when they look in a room, so why not give it a facelift? Switch out the drapes and allow more light into the room. Throw out that old curtain rod and get a brand new one. Updating your window treatments doesnt cost much, and its very easy to do.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Fix Those Annoying Little Things
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="6 Living Room DIY Projects For Your New Home" class="aligncenter" height="333" src="http://farm6.staticflickr.com/5090/5200709876_96aa73fa39.jpg" title="6 Living Room DIY Projects For Your New Home" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Image via
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/aloha75/5200709876/" target="_blank">
          Flickr
         </a>
         by Sam Howzit
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Nows the time to fix all the little things that have bothered you around the room. Has the knob on your entertainment center been loose? Either tighten it up or buy a new knob. Do you have a chair thats unbalanced? Drill a
         <a href="http://www.marthastewart.com/266298/how-to-fix-a-wobbly-chair" target="_blank">
          tiny hole into the leg of the chair
         </a>
         and fill it with glue. Quit putting off that tiny project youve meant to do and take care of it!
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Make a Landing Pad
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         After a long day at work, you come home and immediately shed everything, including your wallet and your keys, leaving you retracing your steps later while you try to figure out where you left them. Stop doing that and make a landing pad! This could be as simple as buying a small bowl and leaving it near the door to put your wallet and keys in. Make a pattern of dumping everything in that bowl when you come in from work, right before you prop your feet up and watch TV. Youll never lose your keys again.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Add a Dimmer Switch
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Have you wanted your living room to have that perfect theater feel, but never got around to making that happen? Nows the perfect chance to install some dimmer switches in the living room. When its time to watch a movie, lower the lights to create the perfect theatrical atmosphere. Dimmer switches are fairly inexpensive and can be installed in just a few hours. Since California is all about saving energy, most of the
         <a href="http://ranchomissionviejo.com/soc/" target="_blank">
          Orange County new homes
         </a>
         already have dimmer switches, but if yours doesnt theyre really easy to add!
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Create a Mail Holder
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Do you constantly have mail scattered everywhere in your house? Eliminate that with a mail holder! These are very easy to make  in fact, all youll likely need is a shutter! You can drill the
         <a href="http://www.apartmenttherapy.com/roundup-diy-mail-holders-83099" target="_blank">
          shutter
         </a>
         into the wall either vertically or horizontally, depending on how you want to display the mail. You could appoint one side for yourself and one for another person, or split it up into multiple sections, depending on how many people are in your household. There wont be a need for mail on the counter ever again.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Paint the Room
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Though its not the easiest of tasks, youll be surprised at how great a newly painted living room can make you feel. It can change the vibe the entire room gives. Go with a bright color if you have stylish art, or choose a more muted color to give off a relaxed feel. It all depends on what you want to do and how you want the room to feel. Make sure the
         <a href="http://www.diynetwork.com/topics/painting/index.html" target="_blank">
          paint
         </a>
         that you choose doesnt clash too much with the items you have in your room, or youll end up with a room that no ones a fan of.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         There are hundreds of things that you can do to your living room once you move in, and these are just a few. When you moved in to your new place, what was one of the first things you did? Was there something specific that you hated about the room? How did you work to improve it? Leave a comment in the section below.
        </p>
