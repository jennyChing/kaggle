     <header class="article_header">
      <h1 class="article_title">
       The Best-Kept Secret to Targeting a Vertical Market
      </h1>
      <div class="contributor article_contributor">
       <figure class="contributor_avatar">
        <a href="http://www.sitepoint.com/author/john-tabita/">
         <img alt="John Tabita" class="avatar avatar-96 wp-user-avatar wp-user-avatar-96 alignnone photo" height="96" src="http://dab1nmslvvntp.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/jtabita-96x96.png" width="96"/>
        </a>
       </figure>
       <div class="contributor_details">
        <div class="contributor_name">
         <a href="http://www.sitepoint.com/author/john-tabita/">
          John Tabita
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="article_meta-data">
       <p class="article_pub-date">
        Published
        <time datetime="2011-12-04" pubdate="">
         December 4, 2011
        </time>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="article_header__buttons">
       <!-- make sure to update trackTweetsAndSubscriptions in scripts.js if the Tweet button DOM path changes in the future -->
       <a class="button small transparent radius sp-twitter-share-button" href="https://twitter.com/share?text=The+Best-Kept+Secret+to+Targeting+a+Vertical+Market&amp;via=sitepointdotcom" target="_blank">
        <i class="icon-twitter">
        </i>
        Tweet
       </a>
       <!-- make sure to update trackTweetsAndSubscriptions in scripts.js if the Subscribe button DOM path changes in the future -->
       <a class="button small transparent radius subscribe-button" href="https://confirmsubscription.com/h/y/1FD5B523FA48AA2B" target="_blank">
        <!--&lt;i class="icon-envelope-alt"&gt;&lt;/i&gt;-->
        Subscribe
       </a>
      </div>
     </header>
     <section class="article_body">
      <p>
       Ive been writing about how to use targeted marketing to
       <a href="http://www.sitepoint.com/target-marketing-the-secret-to-finding-better-clients/" target="_blank" title="Target Marketing: The Secret to Finding Better Clients">
        attract better clients
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.sitepoint.com/clone-your-best-client/" target="_blank" title="Clone Your Best Client!">
        clone your best ones
       </a>
       . One way to do this is by focusing on a vertical market.
      </p>
      <p>
       To recap, a vertical is simply a specific industry, like photographers. Yet there are different specialties in photography, from wedding photography, to food photography, and more. You could narrow the field and focus exclusively on wedding photographers. But heres another way to look at a vertical market:
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       A set of customers having the same product needs
      </p>
      <p>
       This means that bridal shops, florists, disc jockeys, caterers, and banquet facilities also fall into the same vertical as wedding photographers. This is important to consider when targeting a vertical, because you can focus on marketing to
       <em>
        all
       </em>
       the companies serving a common customer base.
      </p>
      <p>
       Focusing on a vertical is a double-edged sword, however, because your marketing message must be
       <em>
        very
       </em>
       specific in order to address their needs. If youre going to provide marketing and advertising services like web design and search marketing, you must know: [1] If your target vertical advertises and [2] if so, why.
      </p>
      <p>
       What problem are they trying to solve by advertising? If you decided to target hospitals, what are some reasons a hospital would advertise? Figure that out then weave it into your marketing message. We help hospitals attract more patients is weak. Okay, its downright pathetic. I found more than a dozen specific reasons why hospitals advertise. Here are a few that stand out:
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li>
        To drive elective private-pay patients to a particular provider
       </li>
       <li>
        To encourage consumers to seek elective treatment for a condition they havent considered treating, or to consider their hospital for the treatment they
        <em>
         have
        </em>
        chosen
       </li>
       <li>
        Because differentiating themselves and attracting consumers as they move from one health plan or community to another is absolutely essential to survival
       </li>
       <li>
        Because consumers are empowered and have more flexibility in their plans to choose providers
       </li>
       <li>
        Because consumers facing more out-of-pocket expenses are financially motivated to make the most of their health care dollars and are shopping around for the best value
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       Those are their words, not minegleaned from various hospital marketing blogs and websites around the Internet. Lets see if we can turn those words into a something we can use to market back to them:
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        We help hospitals attract patients who are facing more out-of-pocket expenses and are searching for a hospital or clinic to get the most out of their health care dollars. More and more hospitals are turning to online advertising because they know that consumers have more flexibility in their plans to choose providers, and that marketing to them is essential to their survival.
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Add an introduction and strong call-to-action at the end, and you have a sales letter, telemarketing script, or some website copy.
      </p>
      <p>
       The secret to successfully penetrating a vertical market is to
       <em>
        speak their language
       </em>
       . Immerse yourself in their industry and youllbecome the expert they look to for proven solutions from someone who understands their industry.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">

         Image credit
        </a>
       </em>
      </p>
