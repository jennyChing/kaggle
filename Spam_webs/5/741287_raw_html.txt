       <h1 class="xxlarge">
        The International Space Station as Seen From Earth
       </h1>
       <p class="postmetadata">
        <span class="author">
         By
         <a href="http://weinterrupt.com/author/van/" rel="author" title="Posts by Van">
          Van
         </a>
        </span>
        /
        <a href="http://weinterrupt.com/2009/03/the-international-space-station-as-seen-from-earth/#comments">
         <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="557 http://weinterrupt.com/?p=557">
          4 comments
         </span>
        </a>
       </p>
       <div class="post-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="iss_sunrise" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-558" height="224" src="http://2l5j2xfyavy1vtsq1smudbr95.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/iss_sunrise-300x224.jpg" width="300"/>
         Many people are not aware that the International Space Station (ISS) is visible from Earth. It is, in fact it can be seen without a telescope under the right conditions: it has to be in light and you have to be in darkness; which makes just before sunrise and just after sunset prime ISS viewing time.
         <span id="more-557">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         A telescope, even a cheap one, makes for some very impressive viewing as the pictures below show. The following picture, taken by  Ralf Vandebergh of Maastricht, Netherlands shows the space shuttle docked to the ISS. For this incredible picture, he used a simple 10 manually tracked Newtonian telescope.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="iss1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-559" height="346" src="http://2l5j2xfyavy1vtsq1smudbr95.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/iss1.jpg" width="420"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The next picture, captured by Australian Vincent Miu, shows the shuttle more clearly. Mius setup consisted of a 30-centimeter telescope with a $70 webcam in place of the eyepiece.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="iss2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-560" height="313" src="http://2l5j2xfyavy1vtsq1smudbr95.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/iss2.jpg" width="420"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The final picture, is a little harder to make out. It was taken by Etienne Simian of Saint Martin de Crau, France. The picture was shot during the day.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="iss3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-561" height="338" src="http://2l5j2xfyavy1vtsq1smudbr95.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/iss3.jpg" width="450"/>

          Gear Log
         </a>

        </p>
