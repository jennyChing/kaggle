                  <h2 class="title">
                   Clinique
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    Clinique came out with a revamped version of its flagship app this week that includes a fresh new design and extra personalization features.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     The new stuff:
                    </strong>
                    The app now uses your location to provide personalized skin forecasts that recommend skincare routines and products based on your location and skin type. Clinique also stepped up its consultant feature, giving you the ability to video chat with one of its many makeup consultants. Available Monday through Friday from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. ET, the consultants provide expert advice, product recommendations and tips on how to change your daily routine to fit your needs.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Keep an eye on:
                    </strong>
                    The apps many tips and tricks, articles and quizzes, which provide a daily refresher on the dos and donts of how to get the most out of your products.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Pro tip:
                    </strong>
                    Take the apps skin diagnostic test to get even more targeted recommendations for your specific skin type.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Free,
                    <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/clinique-skin-care-makeup/id894749892?mt=8">
                     iOS
                    </a>
                   </p>
                  </div>

                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    Nat Geo View, the latest app from
                    <em>
                     National Geographic,
                    </em>
                    offers a daily digest of some of the publications best photos and video, as well as the story behind those images.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Why we like it:
                    </strong>
                    One of the best parts of
                    <em>
                     National Geographic
                    </em>
                    is its stunning visuals, and this app provides some of the best it has to offer in an easy-to-view format. The app also includes just the right amount of context without being overwhelming.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Keep an eye on:
                    </strong>
                    Archival photos that go back more than 100 years and a dedicated Instagram section, which showcases some of the best pics from
                    <em>
                     National Geographic
                    </em>
                    's feed, taken by its photographers around the world.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Pro tip:
                    </strong>
                    Miss a day? The menu in the app's right-hand corner enables you to go back and view posts from the previous week.

                    Free,
                    <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/nat-geo-view/id958610015?mt=8">
                     iOS
                    </a>

                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   SubscriptMe
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>

                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     How it works:
                    </strong>
                    SubscriptMe automatically detects all the apps on your phone that use a subscription model. Select the ones you pay for, and the app will set up a tracker that keeps tabs on how much you pay, when your bill is due and how long youve subscribed to the service.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Keep an eye on:
                    </strong>
                    The apps insights, which provide a detailed report breaking down exactly how much you spend on apps each month and year.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Pro tip:
                    </strong>
                    SubscriptMe also lets users rate and review services, so if youre debating whether or not it's worth shelling out extra money for a premium tier, check out the featured section, which shows the most popular apps and services and how others have rated them.
                   </p>

                     iOS
                    </a>

                     Sheepop
                    </em>
                    's premise is deceptively simple: "just keep throwing the sheeps, according to its App Store description.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     We like it for:
                    </strong>
                    Jumping sheep plus large bodies of water equals whimsical sweet spot. Really simple, yet addictive gameplay makes for the perfect subway companion.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Watch out for:
                    </strong>
                    It might just be the most maddeningly addictive game since
                    <em>
                     Flappy Bird

                    . You could very well go crazy trying to beat your high score of 3."

                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   OneMinute
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    OneMinute aims to crowdsource the answer to one question: What is everyone doing right now?
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     How it works:
                    </strong>
                    Each day, all the apps users receive just one notification. It occurs at a different time every day, all users get the notification simultaneously. Once you receive it, you have just one minute to start snapping photos of whatever is around you. Once that minute is up, you can view what everyone else captured in the apps feed.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Keep an eye on:
                    </strong>
                    The lower-right corner of the photos in the feed, where you can see the city in which the image was taken, is your only hint of the photographer's identity.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Pro tip:
                    </strong>
                    OneMinute crops the photos into narrow rectangles, so make sure you position the camera wisely during the one-minute window.
                   </p>

                     Android
