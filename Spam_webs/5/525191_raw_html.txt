        <p>
         Melbourne has fallen in love with the BBQ mastery of America's southern states, particularly the slow-cooked cool of Texas and the smoky promise of New Orleans. Our sticky fingers couldn't be any happier, so here's our round up of ten of the best spots for
         <strong>
          American BBQ in Melbourne
         </strong>
         . Sink your teeth in!
        </p>
        <h2>
         Le Bon Ton
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          Collingwood
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         New Mexican brothers, Will and Mick Balleau, have transformed the raggedy bones of the former Glasshouse Hotel into the rather sexy, candlelit surrounds of
         <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/restaurants-cafes/le-bon-ton" target="_blank">
          Le Bon Ton's
         </a>
         New Orleans-style BBQ smokehouse that's been permanently buzzing since they threw open the doors early this year. The 12-hour mesquite-smoked pork shoulder comes in half-pound serve and we love the jalapeno and cheddar bratwurst done over cherry wood coals. Late-night revellers rejoice  the kitchen pushes through to the wee hours on Friday and Saturday nights, with the pit-smoked beef brisket with BBQ sauce, coleslaw and house made pickles a saviour.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Bluebonnet Barbecue
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          Collingwood
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/restaurants-cafes/bluebonnet-bbq" target="_blank">
          Bluebonnet
         </a>
         chef, Chris Terlikar, was all fine dining fancy at NYC's PUBLIC when he stumbled into Williamsburg's Fette Sau in Brooklyn and a new passion for American BBQ was born, seeing him hit the road, travelling Texas-wide for big BBQ tips. Thankfully he's returned to Melbourne to share his expertise and whip up a storm with a smoker retrofitted from a club smoke machine. Tuck into the likes of smoked Berkshire pulled pork and a  pound serve of Angus beef ribs with a side of apple and kohlrabi slaw. (Editor's note: check out our round up of
         <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/a-list/where-find-best-ribs-melbourne" target="_blank">
          Melbourne's best ribs
         </a>
         . Bib not included).
        </p>
        <h2>
         Ike's Rack Shack
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          Carlton
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Dubbed
         <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/restaurants-cafes/ikes-rack-shack">
          Ike's Rack Shack,
         </a>
         the dining area out the back of The Beaufort on Rathdowne Street is another awesome BBQ outfit that serves up a choice of pork, lamb or a salt and pepper-rubbed beef ribs on wooden chopping boards and brown paper. Or go all out and get a triple back rack stack combining all three. They also do a mean smoky burger, loaded with pulled pork. They even manage to make Brussels sprouts palatable, frying them with sea salt and miso lemon dressing.
        </p>
        <h2>
         The Lumber Yard
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          Fitzroy
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          While we're on the subject of smokehouses sharing pads with pubs, the
         </span>
         <strong style="line-height: 1.6;">
          Iron Bark BBQ
         </strong>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          boys have set up shop inside
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/restaurants-cafes/workers-club" style="line-height: 1.6;" target="_blank">
          The Worker's Club
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          on the corner of Brunswick and Gertrude Streets. Dubbed The Lumber Yard, the idea's technically British comfort food, but the Iron Bark crew have fused US and UK sensibilities together, with pit-smoked beef short ribs dished up with cauliflower piccalilli and smoked hot links with a (sneakily not British) Guinness mustard. Order some of the killer mac n cheese on the side.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          Big Boy BBQ
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
           Melbourne CBD, Richmond and Caulfield South
          </span>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          The
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.bigboybbq.com.au/" style="line-height: 1.6;" target="_blank">
          Big Boy BBQ
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          guys have not one but three venues spread out across Melbourne, covering the city, Richmond and Caulfield South. Pioneers in bringing the American BBQ craze down under, they proudly proclaim themselves to be Melbourne's 'first "low and slow" smoked meat experience.' Founding father, Lance Rosen, was smitten by Kansas City BBQ while OS and felt compelled to bring the smoky goodness back with him. We love the value for money of the wings 'n' ribs platter with half a rack of lamb with a serve of chicken wings and two sides for $35 that will happily feed two.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          Fancy Hank's BBQ
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
           Melbourne CBD
          </span>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          After a stint as an ever-popular pop-up at the Mercat Cross Hotel on Queen Street, the
         </span>
         <a href="http://fancyhanks.com/" style="line-height: 1.6;" target="_blank">
          Fancy Hank's
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          boys have claimed a permanent spot upstairs at the same venue and dubbed it Fancy Hank's BBQ Joint, laid out under a huge barn-like roof lined with windows to the stars with American paraphernalia clustered on the walls and a big neon sign. Slow-smoked meat like Angus beef brisket and Kansas City-style pork ribs are served up on red plastic trays by the 100g measure for the most part, or you can opt for 200g of beef or Andouille sausages, or the intriguingly titled quarter beer can chook. The corn bread and the burnt end beans are magic, too.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          Nieuw Amsterdam
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
           Melbourne CBD
          </span>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          Cocktail aficionados may head direct to
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/restaurants-cafes/nieuw-amsterdam" style="line-height: 1.6;" target="_blank">
          Nieuw Amsterdam's
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          basement bar for their mixology, but what they might not know is that the funky spot on Hardware Street is also a fantastic place for American BBQ food in Melbourne CBD. Diners have a choice of three spangly metal meat trays (love the retro pub joke) for $24  two-day beef brisket, spiced chicken or pork belly chops  each accompanied by potato salad, slaw, pickles, bread and sauce. Even those cocktails sneak a bit of meat in, with the duck fat-washed Tuxedo a corker.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          Meatmaiden
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
           Melbourne CBD
          </span>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          The younger, sexier sibling of Neil Hamblen and Nick Johnston's American smokehouse
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/restaurants-cafes/meatmother" style="line-height: 1.6;" target="_blank">
          Meatmother
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          (more on that in a mo),
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/a-list/meatmaiden-verdict" style="line-height: 1.6;" target="_blank">
          Meatmaiden
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          has breathed new life into the Little Collins basement space formerly occupied by Little Hunter. Exposed brick walls and dark wood floors create a moody ambiance that's perfect for date nights, so long as your date doesn't mind you going all Henry VII and tearing great hunks of meat off the bone with your fingers. Justin Wise shifted over from Albert Park's The Point to set up shop before handing the reins to head chef, Riki Day. Their shiny smoker delivers the goods with dishes including 20-hour Rangers Valley wagyu brisket with a Tassie pepper berry rub and Gippsland beef short ribs.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
          Meatmother
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          <span style="line-height: 1.6;">
           Richmond
          </span>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Before Meatmaiden, there was Swan Street's insanely popular sister venue,
         <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/restaurants-cafes/meatmother" target="_blank">
          Meatmother
         </a>
          home to some of the best ribs in Melbourne. A little more rough and ready, with cleavers mounted on a wooden boar donning the wall, they sure pack in the flavour, with meat trays combining your choice of the likes of pulled pork or pasture-fed beef short rib served up with sides like chipotle slaw, BBQ beans or a killer mac and cheese. In true southern style, there's an outstanding range of bourbon to be had, as well as craft beers.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Smokey's All American BBQ
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          Port Melbourne
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         A rare southside serve of smokehouse action,
         <a href="http://www.smokeysbbq.com.au/" target="_blank">
          Smokey's All American BBQ
         </a>
         on Rouse Street is a straight-talking, sharp-shooting kinda joint that serves up big portions of hickory-smoked goodness. The New Orleans-style BBQ shrimp are finger licking good, while the BBQ pork belly is glazed with bourbon-infused maple syrup. Beef brisket does ten hours in the smoker, with a trio of house made sauces to choose from.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           American BBQ is NOTHING without a side of mac'n'cheese! Read our mac'n'cheese hit-list
           <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/a-list/melbournes-best-macncheese" target="_blank">
            here
           </a>
           . Or peruse our new list of the best
           <a href="http://www.theurbanlist.com/melbourne/a-list/best-american-food-melbourne-barbeque" target="_blank">
            American food in Melbourne
           </a>
           !
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
