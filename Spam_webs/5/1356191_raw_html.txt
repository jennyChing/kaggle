        <p>
         If you visit Pinterest on a regular basis, you know theres no shortage of ideas for
         <strong>
          painting on jars
         </strong>
         . I had experimented with glass stain paint before when I made this
         <a href="http://craftsbyamanda.com/2013/07/beach-flag-suncatcher.html">
          Beach Flag Sun Catcher
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Oh these are beautiful! Painting on Jars with Glass Stain by Amanda Formaro of Crafts by Amanda" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-8304" height="1092" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/glass-stain-jars-pin-me-600x1092.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         These paints give a similar result to the much more expensive and tricky process of doing stained glass. But this time I wanted to try something a little different and see how these paints performed when used in a way that was other than instructed.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Painting on Jars with Glass Stain by Amanda Formaro of Crafts by Amanda" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8302" height="490" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/glass-stain-jars-7.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         One of my favorite aspects of these glass stains is that they dry transparent but dont lose their gorgeous colors.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Painting on Jars with Glass Stain by Amanda Formaro of Crafts by Amanda" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8303" height="600" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/glass-stain-jars-600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Doing a stained glass type design on a jar would take forever due to the drying time needed for the glass stains. Because a jar is rounded, you would have to do one small piece at a time over a series of quite a few days. So instead, I experimented with some rough brushing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Painting on Jars with Glass Stain by Amanda Formaro of Crafts by Amanda" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8300" height="400" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/glass-stain-jars-3.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I really love how pretty they turned out. They are truly lovely in a window with the light shining through them! You could use them in various ways, such as:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          vases for pretty flowers youve picked
         </li>
         <li>
          fill them with plastic utensils for a summer barbecue
         </li>
         <li>
          lovely luminaries in your garden
         </li>
         <li>
          use them in your craft room to hold buttons, beads and more
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Painting on Jars with Glass Stain
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          Note: some links below are affiliate links. That means if you happen to buy something, a small percentage of that sale goes back into this site, which enables me to continue to bring you free tutorials such as this one. So, thanks!
         </em>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          As always, a full printable supply list and instructions are at the end of this post.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           For this project you will need the following supplies:
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003ULND78/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B003ULND78&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thefamilycorne02&amp;linkId=ZIXBBTRZK4J46RJ2">
           DecoArt Glass Stain
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=thefamilycorne02&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B003ULND78" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          in colors of your choice
         </li>
         <li>
          Recycled jars (jelly, mayonnaise, etc)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="Painting on Jars with Glass Stain by Amanda Formaro of Crafts by Amanda" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8305" height="450" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/glss-stain-jars-product.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The glass stains come in quite a few colors, so let your imagination and creativity run wild!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Painting on Jars with Glass Stain by Amanda Formaro of Crafts by Amanda" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8306" height="450" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/glss-stain-jars-step-1.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its not technical.I just kind of slapped the stain on the jar with a paintbrush, not truly smoothing it out or taking any care with the design. I wiped excess back and forth with the brush until it took on a slightly tacky feel.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Painting on Jars with Glass Stain by Amanda Formaro of Crafts by Amanda" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8307" height="450" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/glss-stain-jars-step-2.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then I used another color and (again, roughly) added a few patches of alternating color.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Painting on Jars with Glass Stain by Amanda Formaro of Crafts by Amanda" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8301" height="811" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/glass-stain-jars-4.jpg" width="550"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         After a 24-hour drying period I am happy to report that I think they came out beautiful!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Painting on Jars with Glass Stain by Amanda Formaro of Crafts by Amanda" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8299" height="401" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/glass-stain-jars-2.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you give this project a go, Id love to hear about it! Hope you can glean some inspiration from this post.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://craftsbyamanda.com/2013/08/faux-stained-glass-mosaic-luminary.html">
          <img alt="Faux Stained Glass Mosaic Luminary" class="aligncenter" height="486" src="http://craftsbyamanda.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/faux-stain-glass-luminary-h.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         One of my favorite projects using these glass stains was this
         <a href="http://craftsbyamanda.com/2013/08/faux-stained-glass-mosaic-luminary.html">
          Faux Stained Glass Mosaic Luminary.
         </a>
         It lives in my living room and is still as beautiful as it was a year ago when I originally made it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/U01Rjs1GFJU" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you would like a quick demonstration on how to use these fun glass stain paints, check out this video from DecoArt!
        </p>
