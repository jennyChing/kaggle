       <p>
        Okay, so for those of you that have the straight from the gutter minds that I mentioned in the earlier post, Ive changed the langage on the invitations to read Blow It Up instead of Blow Me Up for obvious reasons. Before you begin, you need to order your custom party balloon from
        <a href="http://www.fastballoons.com/" target="_blank">
         Fast Balloons
        </a>
        , which takes about 2-3 days for delivery.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.stylemepretty.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Audreys-Blow-It-Up-Invitation.pdf" target="_blank">
         Click here to download the pink template
        </a>
        /
        <a href="http://www.stylemepretty.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Audreys-Blow-It-Up-Invitation-Blue.pdf" target="_blank">
         Click here to download the blue template
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         <strong>
          Materials
         </strong>
        </span>
        <br/>
        8.5 x 11 Cardstock
        <a href="http://www.paper-source.com/cgi-bin/paper/color/8-1-2-x-11-cover-weight-paper/2505.131/833.html" target="_blank">
         like this one
        </a>
        from Paper Source
        <br/>
        Paper Cutter (or scissors and a steady hand)
        <br/>
        Hole Punch
        <br/>
        Gold Ribbon
        <br/>
        Pre-printed Balloons from
        <a href="http://www.fastballoons.com/" target="_blank">
         fastballoons.com
        </a>
        <br/>
        Color Printer
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         <strong>
          Instructions
         </strong>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        1. Download and print
        <a href="http://www.stylemepretty.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Audreys-Blow-It-Up-Invitation.pdf" target="_blank">
         this template
        </a>
        (or
        <a href="http://www.stylemepretty.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Audreys-Blow-It-Up-Invitation-Blue.pdf" target="_blank">
         this one for blue
        </a>
        ) on your 8.5 x 11 card stock. Cut each card out.
        <br/>
        2. Punch a hole in each of the designated spots.
        <br/>
        3. Coming up from the back, thread one side of the ribbon through hole, then the other side through the opposite hole again coming up through the back.
        <br/>
        4. Place your balloon on the front so that the ribbon is hugging the top of the balloon on either side. Tie a snug knot or a pretty bow so that the balloon is held in place securely.
        <br/>
        5. Added style tipwe lined our envelopes with pink paper, placed each card inside then sprinkled them with gold sequins.
       </p>
