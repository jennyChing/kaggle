        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Healthy eating leads to healthy living. A straightforward set of reasoning, however, no less true or important. What might not seem so evident is the inner workings of your body that rely on sustained nourishment and care. A body undergoing wear and tear from day-to-day activities relies on healthy cells that are able to repair themselves, and these are supported by a healthy immune system. The body needs fuel for energy, vitamins and minerals to support the bodys systems and protect the body against toxins, while maintaining nerve signals and strong bones. Children with poor eating habits can suffer from issues with growth and development. With a healthy body comes a healthy mind: to be focused and productive, to sleep soundly, to get the most out of life with a positive outlook all relies on inner healthy, a result of healthy eating. Balance and proportion is key  remember the food pyramid, with starchy grains comprising the largest portion of what you eat, alongside fruits and vegetables, and sugars and fats topping the lot with the smallest allowance comprising a nutritionally balanced diet.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 600px">
         <a href="http://photobucket.com/images/healthy%20eating" target="_blank">

         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Image Credit: Photobucket.com-smarthealthyandgreen
         </p>

        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px; line-height: 1.5em;">
          Healthy eating is not solely achieved by what you eat, but also how much. The key to maintaining your body weight is to burn an equal amount of calories you ingest over a prolonged amount of time. Should you wish to lose weight, this is best achieved by consuming fewer calories than those you use for energy, as the body converts unused calories into fat tissue. The risk of accumulating fat tissue is weight gain, which can result in a variety of health problems, such as respiratory, blood pressure or heart irregularities, through to diabetes and cancer in more extreme circumstances. Nutritional consideration is one factor of a healthy lifestyle, and for those who do not live a regularly active lifestyle, finding a way to burn calories may pose a challenge.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Of course, what is conducive to a healthy eating plan for one person might not necessarily be beneficial for another. Individual considerations must be made when formulating a healthy eating plan. For example, an expectant mother must avoid certain foods for the risks they pose to her un-born child. Elderly people might not be able to digest certain foods with the ease they used to, which can cause gastric issues. Athletes are known to ingest foods that might seem excessive to someone who does not live an overly active lifestyle. The key to a healthy lifestyle is balance  you should consult your health care professional in the event that you react badly to your current eating plans, or should you wish to drastically change them.
        </p>
