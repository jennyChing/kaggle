            <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
             <a href="http://healthservicesprograms.blogspot.com/2013/07/peritoneal-mesothelioma-symptoms.html" target="_blank">
              Peritoneal mesothelioma
             </a>
             is not an easy cancer to diagnose. It often takes several months from the initial presentation of symptoms to confirm a diagnosis. This delay, combined with the speedy progression of mesothelioma tumors, usually results in a late-stage diagnosis for most patients.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             A peritoneal mesothelioma diagnosis cannot be made on the basis of symptoms alone. The process begins with a thorough examination of a patients medical history and physical condition, followed by CT scans and other X-rays. Any incidence of asbestos exposure in a patients history should alert doctors to the possibility of mesothelioma cancer.
             <br/>
             <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              <a href="http://healthservicesprograms.blogspot.com/2013/07/peritoneal-mesothelioma-diagnosis.html" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
               <img alt="Peritoneal Mesothelioma Diagnosis" border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-dNYGlhZUzvs/UesH2L8-fDI/AAAAAAAAAWU/Xli0XvYDQQY/s1600/peritoneal_mesothelioma.jpg" title="Peritoneal Mesothelioma Diagnosis"/>
              </a>
             </div>
             <br/>
             Imaging scans are useful for detecting abnormalities and may even help with staging, but only a biopsy can confirm a mesothelioma diagnosis. Doctors often use the imaging scans as a guide for where the biopsy should be performed. Biopsies are minor surgical procedures that involve the removal of fluid or tissue samples from areas of the peritoneum.

             <br/>
             ref: asbestos.com
