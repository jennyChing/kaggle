     <p>
      <b>
       Gerry Lester
      </b>
      "
      <b>
       Bubba
      </b>
      "
      <b>
       Watson, Jr.
      </b>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-augusta_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-augusta-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-publicrecords_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-publicrecords-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      (born November 5, 1978) is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Professional_golfer" title="Professional golfer">
       professional golfer
      </a>
      who plays on the
      <a href="/wiki/PGA_Tour" title="PGA Tour">
       PGA Tour
      </a>
      . One of the few
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-handed" title="Left-handed">
       left-handed
      </a>
      golfers on tour, he is a multiple
      <a href="/wiki/Men%27s_major_golf_championships" title="Men's major golf championships">
       major champion
      </a>
      , having won the
      <a href="/wiki/Masters_Tournament" title="Masters Tournament">
       Masters Tournament
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/2012_Masters_Tournament" title="2012 Masters Tournament">
       2012
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/2014_Masters_Tournament" title="2014 Masters Tournament">
       2014
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In February 2015, Watson reached a career-high 2nd place in the
      <a href="/wiki/Official_World_Golf_Ranking" title="Official World Golf Ranking">
       Official World Golf Ranking
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Watson is among the longest drivers on the PGA Tour; in 2007 he had an average drive of 315.2 yards (288.2m) and can hit a ball over 350 yards (320m), capable of generating a ball speed up to 194mph (312km/h).

      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Amateur_career">
       Amateur career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bubba_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amateur career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Watson was born and raised in
      <a href="/wiki/Bagdad,_Florida" title="Bagdad, Florida">
       Bagdad, Florida
      </a>
      , near
      <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">
       Pensacola
      </a>
      . He played on the golf team at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milton_High_School_(Milton,_Florida)" title="Milton High School (Milton, Florida)">
       Milton High School
      </a>
      , which had featured future PGA Tour members
      <a href="/wiki/Heath_Slocum" title="Heath Slocum">
       Heath Slocum
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Boo_Weekley" title="Boo Weekley">
       Boo Weekley
      </a>
      just before he attended.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redneck_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-redneck-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Watson played golf for
      <a href="/wiki/Faulkner_State_Community_College" title="Faulkner State Community College">
       Faulkner State Community College
      </a>
      in nearby
      <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_County,_Alabama" title="Baldwin County, Alabama">
       Baldwin County
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">
       Alabama
      </a>
      , where he was a junior college
      <a href="/wiki/All-America" title="All-America">
       All-American
      </a>
      . He transferred to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgia_Bulldogs#Golf" title="Georgia Bulldogs">
       University of Georgia
      </a>
      , the defending
      <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I_Men%27s_Golf_Championships" title="NCAA Division I Men's Golf Championships">
       NCAA champions
      </a>
      , and played for the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgia_Bulldogs#Golf" title="Georgia Bulldogs">
       Bulldogs
      </a>
      in 2000 and 2001. As a junior, Watson helped lead the Bulldogs to the
      <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Conference" title="Southeastern Conference">
       SEC
      </a>
      title in 2000.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">
       Professional career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bubba_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Watson turned professional in
      <a href="/wiki/2003_Nationwide_Tour" title="2003 Nationwide Tour">
       2003
      </a>
      and joined the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nationwide_Tour" title="Nationwide Tour">
       Nationwide Tour
      </a>
      where he played until 2005. He finished 21st on the Nationwide Tour's money list in
      <a href="/wiki/2005_Nationwide_Tour" title="2005 Nationwide Tour">
       2005
      </a>
      , making him the last player to qualify for the following year's
      <a href="/wiki/PGA_Tour" title="PGA Tour">
       PGA Tour
      </a>
      . As a rookie in
      <a href="/wiki/2006_PGA_Tour" title="2006 PGA Tour">
       2006
      </a>
      , he earned $1,019,264 (90th overall) and led the PGA Tour in driving distance at 319.6 yards (292.2m). His longest drive in professional competition was 442 yards (404m) on the PGA Tour at the
      <a href="/wiki/WGC-Bridgestone_Invitational" title="WGC-Bridgestone Invitational">
       WGC-Bridgestone Invitational
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Watson played well at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_U.S._Open_Golf_Championship" title="2007 U.S. Open Golf Championship">
       2007 U.S. Open
      </a>
      . He was in the final group on Saturday after shooting rounds of 70-71 (+1) at
      <a href="/wiki/Oakmont_Country_Club" title="Oakmont Country Club">
       Oakmont Country Club
      </a>
      near
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">
       Pittsburgh
      </a>
      . Watson was one stroke off the lead after 36 holes but then slipped, shooting 74 (+4) in both the third and fourth rounds; he finished in a tie for fifth.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2010">
       2010
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bubba_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Watson claimed his first
      <a href="/wiki/PGA_Tour" title="PGA Tour">
       PGA Tour
      </a>
      win on June 27, 2010, in
      <a href="/wiki/Cromwell,_Connecticut" title="Cromwell, Connecticut">
       Cromwell, Connecticut
      </a>
      , at the
      <a href="/wiki/Travelers_Championship" title="Travelers Championship">
       Travelers Championship
      </a>
      on the second hole of a sudden-death playoff with
      <a href="/wiki/Corey_Pavin" title="Corey Pavin">
       Corey Pavin
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Scott_Verplank" title="Scott Verplank">
       Scott Verplank
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Watson tearfully dedicated the win to his parents, specifically his father who was battling
      <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">
       cancer
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Watson was runner-up to
      <a href="/wiki/Martin_Kaymer" title="Martin Kaymer">
       Martin Kaymer
      </a>
      at the
      <a href="/wiki/2010_PGA_Championship" title="2010 PGA Championship">
       PGA Championship
      </a>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/Whistling_Straits" title="Whistling Straits">
       Whistling Straits
      </a>
      , falling in the three-hole aggregate playoff that initially included
      <a href="/wiki/Dustin_Johnson" title="Dustin Johnson">
       Dustin Johnson
      </a>
      , before he incurred a two-stroke penalty on the 72nd hole. Watson led the playoff after a birdie on the first hole, but Kaymer birdied the par-3 second hole to tie, effectively turning the playoff into sudden-death. Watson's second shot found the water hazard and Kaymer bested him by a stroke to win the major championship.
     </p>
     <p>
      Watson had his own clothing line called "Bubba Golf" at the former
      <a href="/wiki/Steve_%26_Barry%27s" title="Steve &amp; Barry's">
       Steve &amp; Barry's
      </a>
      . He was invited on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">
        The Ellen DeGeneres Show
       </a>
      </i>
      after he sent her a video of a golf trick shot he completed for her birthday.
     </p>

     <h4>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Masters_win">
       Masters win
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bubba_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Masters win">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h4>
     <p>
      Watson's first
      <a href="/wiki/Men%27s_major_golf_championships" title="Men's major golf championships">
       major championship
      </a>
      win came at the
      <a href="/wiki/2012_Masters_Tournament" title="2012 Masters Tournament">
       2012 Masters Tournament
      </a>
      . He began the final round at six-under-par, three strokes off the lead, held by
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hanson" title="Peter Hanson">
       Peter Hanson
      </a>
      . On the back nine, Watson bogeyed the par-3 12th hole to return to even par for the round. He then recorded four consecutive birdies for a round of 68 (-4) and tied for the 72-hole lead with fourth-round playing partner
      <a href="/wiki/Louis_Oosthuizen" title="Louis Oosthuizen">
       Louis Oosthuizen
      </a>
      at ten-under-par. In the
      <a href="/wiki/Sudden_death_(sport)" title="Sudden death (sport)">
       sudden-death
      </a>
      playoff, Oosthuizen and Watson both made par on the uphill 18th hole. On the next hole, the downhill 10th, both drove their tee shots towards the woods to the right of the hole. Oosthuizen's landed in the rough 220 yards (200m) away, while Watson's ball landed deep in the woods on pine straw, 164 yards (150m) from the pin without a clear shot to the green. Watson executed a miraculous recovery shot with 40 yards of hook on his 52-degree gap wedge and stopped the ball within fifteen feet of the hole. Oosthuizen's approach shot landed short of the green, but he chipped past the hole and narrowly missed his lengthy putt for par. Watson trickled his birdie putt a foot past the hole, took his time on the very short par putt, then made it for the emotional victory.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wetzel_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Wetzel-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The win took him to a world ranking of four, a career-high at the time.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>

        </span>

      <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Tournament_of_Champions" title="Hyundai Tournament of Champions">

      </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WGC-Accenture_Match_Play_Championship" title="WGC-Accenture Match Play Championship">

      <a href="/wiki/WGC-Cadillac_Championship" title="WGC-Cadillac Championship">

     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Palmer_Invitational" title="Arnold Palmer Invitational">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Masters_Tournament" title="Masters Tournament">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/The_Players_Championship" title="The Players Championship">

      </a>
      . On the second major of the year, the
      <a href="/wiki/U.S._Open_(golf)" title="U.S. Open (golf)">
       U.S. Open
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Travelers_Championship" title="Travelers Championship">
       Travelers Championship
      </a>
      he took the lead after a second round of 67, but in the final round, leading by one with three to play, he triple-bogeyed the par-3 16th and finished two shots back in 4th place.
     </p>
     <p>
      Tying for 30th at the
      <a href="/wiki/Greenbrier_Classic" title="Greenbrier Classic">
       Greenbrier Classic
      </a>
      , he then played in the third major of the year,
      <a href="/wiki/The_Open_Championship" title="The Open Championship">
       The Open Championship
      </a>
      . After two solids rounds of 70 and 73, he shot 77 in the third round, and tied for 32nd.

     <h4>

      <a href="/wiki/2014_Masters_Tournament" title="2014 Masters Tournament">
       2014 Masters Tournament
      </a>
      by three shots, with a score of 8.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He entered the final round tied for the lead with 20-year-old Masters rookie
      <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Spieth" title="Jordan Spieth">
       Jordan Spieth
      </a>
      . Playing together in the final pairing, Spieth birdied the seventh hole for a two-stroke lead over Watson. However, the momentum turned on the par-5 eighth hole. Spieth had a birdie putt, but ended up three-putting for bogey while Watson birdied to pull into a first-place tie.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Then, on the ninth hole, Watson birdied again while Spieth bogeyed, and the four-shot swing over two holes gave Watson a lead that he never relinquished in a win over Spieth and
      <a href="/wiki/Jonas_Blixt" title="Jonas Blixt">
       Jonas Blixt
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>

      </sup>
      With the win, Watson became the 17th player to win the Masters two or more times.

       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-owgr_18-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-owgr-18">
        <span>
