        <div class="tweetmeme_button" style="float: right; margin-left: 10px;">
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="61" scrolling="no" src="http://api.tweetmeme.com/button.js?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.bytejacker.com%2Fblog%2Fadventure-time-creator-pendleton-ward-tells-us-about-the-game-he-wants-to-make&amp;source=bytejacker&amp;style=normal&amp;service=bit.ly&amp;service_api=bytejacker%3AR_c1f7b599a21774e98021b54c042cc631&amp;b=2" width="50">

        <p>
         <a href="http://twitpic.com/31ik0c/full">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-5214" height="130" src="http://www.bytejacker.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/adventure-time-final.jpg" width="180"/>
         </a>
         If you havent been watching
         <em>
          Adventure Time
         </em>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LErwHmjBuYQ">
          heres a great example of what youre missing
         </a>
         . If you have, youre already familiar withthe Land of Ooo, and its probablyoccurredto you at least once that it would be a great setting for a video game.
        </p>
        <p>
         Apparently we arent alone in thinking this, because a couple weeks ago, show creator Pendleton Ward tweeted out some concept sketches for an Adventure Time game that
         <a href="http://tinycartridge.com/post/1528199893/sketches-for-an-adventure-time-ds-game-by">
          got
         </a>
         <a href="http://gonintendo.com/viewstory.php?id=141917">
          some
         </a>
         <a href="http://kotaku.com/5686122/how-to-turn-a-great-cartoon-into-a-video-game">
          attention
         </a>
         . Theyre dope as hell, and if you havent seen them yet,
         <a href="http://twitpic.com/31ik0c/full">
          you should definitely check them out before reading on
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Awesome, right? Well, we got in touch with Pendleton to ask a couple questions about these sketches and to find out whether he actually wants to make this whole
         <em>
          Adventure Time
         </em>
         game thing happen. Here are his responses!
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: medium;">
           This whole idea you sketched out of a physics-based Adventure Time video game is incredibly cool! How serious are you about actually trying to get a real Adventure Time game made?
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Damn Hell Serious.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <span style="font-size: medium;">
          <strong>
           <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-5321" height="300" src="http://www.bytejacker.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/12/penward-225x300.jpg" width="225"/>
           What are some of your favorite games? Are there any in particular that inspired this idea?
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         my favorite games.. ehmm.. A Boy and His Blob.. The Tron FPS LIMBO (the xbox downloadable that came out recently).. ehmm.. I really liked
         <a href="http://www.autofish.net/clysm/art/video_games/seiklus/">
          Seiklus
         </a>
         and all the other games that Clysm made using Game Maker.. hmm.. I like Myst a lot.. I like adventure games that are full of mystery and story without dialog.. I like discovering secrets.. like checking behind every waterfall in every game that has waterfalls.. like Oblivion.. or.. I was just playing Dark Messiah of Might and Magic.. which had a secret behind a waterfall.. ehmm.. i love secret rooms.. and finding places that feel like secret areas.. like that little cat village in the middle of the Monster Hunter Wii maps.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: medium;">
           Do you see this as being 2D or 3D game?
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         3D game. Im thinking about katamari inspired graphics.. simple and stylized..
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <span style="font-size: medium;">
          <strong>
           Theres a lot of stuff about Adventure Time that feels a bit game-y. Just about everything, from your use of chiptunes in the soundtrack to little details in the Land of Ooo, seems to incorporate bits (no pun intended) of retro aesthetic. What is it about that era and style that appeals to you?
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         I grew up in it. It makes me feel warm and happy.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: medium;">
           Anything else?
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         I love games.. I want the Adventure Time game to be so good.. I will work every waking extra hour that I have.. to make it as excellent as i can.. mmm.. mmhmm..
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: medium;">
           Thanks so much for talking to us, Pendleton!
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont think I could be more psyched about this. If any talented indie devs are reading this:
         <a href="http://www.buenothebear.com/">
          GET IN TOUCH WITH PEN AND MAKE THIS GAME HAPPEN
         </a>
         . For the good of America. Okay? Okay.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: small;">
          <strong>
           Alright, readers! Those of you who watch
           <em>
            Adventure Time
           </em>
           : what would you want out of an
           <em>
            Adventure Time
           </em>
           game? Those of you who dont: would you play the game that exists in Pendletons head? Let us know in the comments!
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
