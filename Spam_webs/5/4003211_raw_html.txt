       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Youve heard Mariah Careys All I Want For Christmas Is You abouttwenty-five times already. The stores are in the thick of their Christmas sales but you just cant figure out what to buy for your cousin who has everything. Your uncle still has (and still doesnt wear) all the ties youve been giving him every year for Christmas and your grandparents havefifteen framed photos of you and your siblings scattered throughout their house. Coming up with unique Christmas gifts for your family members not to mention paying for all of them could be overwhelming during an already stressful time of year. And dont forget the hostess gifts for all the holiday parties throughout the month of December; dont arrive to the party empty handed! So, this year, give the gift that everyone always loves  food! You can never go wrong with homemade treats and you can be creative by packaging and decorating them however you want. DIY is always fun and meaningful!
        </p>
        <p>
         My favorite food gift around the holidays is to make these
         <a href="http://www.tastebook.com/recipes/3094855-Sugar-and-Spice-Candied-Nuts" target="_blank" title="Sugar-and-Spice Candied Nuts">
          Sugar-and-Spice Candied Nuts
         </a>
         from Smitten Kitchen and put them in a large, wide mouthed mason jar. You can use whatever kind of nuts you want (as long as they are raw and unsalted). Buy a decorative bowl (Anthropologie or West Elm often have cute ones), put the mason jar in the bowl, and wrap them together with clear cellophane and tons of pretty ribbon. Its the perfect hostess gift. Make lots of extra nuts because youll want to eat them as you package them up.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont want all the sugar? Make homemade
         <a href="http://www.tastebook.com/recipes/3739934-Coconut-Almond-Granola" target="_blank" title="Granola">
          granola
         </a>
         and put it into a glass jar with a clamp lid. You can buy a mini scoop and attach it to the jar with some plaid ribbon.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.tastebook.com/recipes/3887841-Five-Flavored-Salts" target="_blank" title="Flavored Salts">
          Flavored Salts
         </a>
         are also a great gift. Make 5-10 different kinds, put them in large test tubes and wrap them all together with some raffia ribbon. Dont forget to label each tube with the appropriate flavor.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are so many more great homemade food gifts and Ive rounded up a few of my favorites on Pinterest.
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/lillykinnally/food-gifts/" target="_blank">
          See them here.
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Have fun with it and be proud of your creative homemade gift!
        </p>
