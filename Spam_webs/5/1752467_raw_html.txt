         <em>

          <strong>
           broccoli cheddar hashbrown casserole with ham
          </strong>
          . Its not just delicious, though, its easy to make, too! You can prep it ahead of time and refrigerate or freeze it until youre ready to bake it up. Saving time, for the big win!

        <p>
         The holiday rush is over, but that doesnt mean my life has slowed down. Truthfully, the week between Christmas and New Years is more busy for me than the pre-holiday craziness.Thatsmainly because Brian and I dont exchange gifts or have company to serve a meal to at our home, so were able to sit back and enjoy the holidays a bit more.
        </p>
        <p>
         After the holiday is over, though, I start my end of the year book keeping and goal planning for the new year. Right about now is when things get a bit crazy for me, because I realize that I have less than 2 weeks to get my house in order. I simply dont have time to mess around in the kitchen, so I rely on casseroles like this one.
        </p>

         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          This recipe comes together quickly with help from frozen hashbrown potatoes and broccoli, plus cooked ham. All you're really doing is heating everything through and melting the cheese. It's SO creamy and delicious!
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           WHAT'S NEEDED
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 cups frozen hashbrown potatoes
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup frozen chopped broccoli
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 ounces (1/2 pound) cooked ham, diced small
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            14 ounces (1 can) condensed cream of potato soup (low sodium if you can find it)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 ounces (1 cup) plain Greek yogurt (or sour cream)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 ounces shredded sharp cheddar cheese
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/4 teaspoon onion powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 teaspoon black pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/4 teaspoon salt (optional, as condensed soups are usually very salty)
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           HOW TO MAKE IT
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Heat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with olive oil or cooking spray.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a large bowl, mix potatoes, ham, broccoli, cream of potato soup, yogurt, and cheddar cheese. Spread evenly into prepared dish. Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake 40-45 minutes, or until bubbly and lightly brown.
           </li>
          </ol>
