       <p>
        <a href="http://www.stuff.co.nz/life-style/fashion/66751750/What-colour-is-this-dress-white-and-gold-or-blue-and-black" target="_blank">
         Forget #dressgate,
        </a>
        it's now all about #catgate.
       </p>
       <p>
        Everyone's so over whether that dress is black or blue, and the latest issue vexing the web community is whether this cat's going up the stairs or down the stairs.
       </p>
       <p>
        Cats dominating the internet is nothing new - the world wide web just loves their general apathy towards humans, but this time it's all about one cat's ambiguous sense of direction.
       </p>
       <p>
        Posted on user-generated content site 9gag, this mind-bending picture has had nearly 4,000 comments posted below it, with people debating the direction issue.
       </p>
       <p>
        "Down for two reasons, one architectural and the other biological," one user debated, adding that the tiles used "are traditionally facing the person coming up the stairs which they are here. And second, look at the cats tail - its facing upwards, or the opposite of the direction its moving in. If the cat were to go up the stairs, its tail would point downwards [sic]."
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="http://static.stuff.co.nz/1428703954/774/11527774.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        A user named Lily0205 thinks the cat is moving on up: "Given the cat and its body language, it's going up, look at its front paw. It wants to tap on the next stair... PLUS: If the Lamp in this room is on the ceiling, the shadow fits perfectly. Also, if it would have been walking down, it wouldn't stand so stiff. It would be flatter on the ground, in order to hold balance. UP [sic]."
       </p>
       <p>
        But another user responded to Lily to debunk her claims: "Wrong. Look at the steps. They have an extruding end. Those are never on the horizontal part of the step because you would trip on them and fall. Also, the cat cannot possibly go up because its left paw would be touching the vertical portion of the step. Just look at it."
       </p>
       <p>
        Then a user called Nasirio stepped in and confused us even further: "It can't possibly be going down, looking at its body position. And looking at the stairs the cat isn't up either. Just IMAGINE the cat going down, but look at the side of the stair instead. The "stairs" are placed/made to move upwards... The cat's walking straight(forward) onto a painted floor, made to look like a pair of stairs [sic]"
       </p>
       <p>
        It's official, our minds are blown.
        <img src="http://static.stuff.co.nz/1428703801/773/11527773.gif"/>
