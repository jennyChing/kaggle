          <h1>
           FlorenceTravel Guide
          </h1>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The birthplace of opera, and the Italian Renaissance where pioneers like Dante, Petrach and Boccaccio, the use of language other than Latin, or Filippo Brunelleschi and Leon Batist'Alberti who invented Renaissance and neoclassical architecture, forever changing the face of cities all over Europe. With more than 80 museums, art galleries, churches with even more art and great places to walk and explore the city, it is no wonder that Florence is a favourite city of so many people all over the world.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Best Time To Visit Florence
          </h3>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Florence has a Mediterranean climate, so expect summer to have high temperatures and sunny days while winter is colder and damper. July and August are the hottest months (around 30 C). The best time to visit Florence is from May to October when the warm weather means alfresco dining, art festivals and the type of Italian that inspired so many painters. Sadly, the warm weather also means sweltering heat, swarms of tourists and increased hotel rates. If you decide to visit in autumn or winter, you will enjoy lower rates and fewer people, but the weather will not be as pleasant.
          </p>
          <h3>
           How To Reach Florence
          </h3>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Fly into Florences Amerigo Vespucci International Airport  there are taxis and buses that can get you into the city easily. Florence is well connected by train with the rest of Italy and express trains from around Europe also arrive in Florence regularly. You can drive to Florence easily but since driving in the historic centre is banned to everyone except residents with permits it isnt advised to bring your car in. Once you are in Florence, the easiest way to get around is to walk  most of the main tourist sites are within walking distance of each other, it is also the best way to see the city. There is a bike rental service arranged by the city  just pay attention to traffic. There are taxis available, but expect to pay up in Florence. Florence has a great public bus service  ideal if you are a family or group staying for a long weekend.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Tourist Attractions In Florence
          </h3>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Uffizi is the most famous museum in Florence and other sites that are worth noting are the Duomo (Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore)  the citys gorgeous cathedral and the Boboli Gardens behind Pitti Palace which has gorgeous sculptures and stunning views of the city.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Nightlife:
           </strong>
           There is a thriving nightlife in Florence. Try La Cite, Enoteca Le volpi e l'Uva, Bebop, Irish Pub, Ambrosia and Rivoire if you like the more relaxed atmosphere of a bar. Central Park Club is a seasonal club open in late spring and summer, but worth trying. Cavalli Club and Tenax are upscale. Otherwise, Twice Club is recommended.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Hotels &amp; Restaurants In Florence
          </h3>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you are on a budget try Hotel Florence Masaccio. Otherwise, head to Hotel Mia Cara which has friendly, English speaking staff. For those wanting to splurge, contact Grand Hotel Cavour or NH Porta Rossa, a hotel in a building from the 12th Century.
          </p>
          <p>
           More than 130 restaurants that you find in this beautiful city. Some of them are;
          </p>
          <p>
           11 Santo Bevitore is a place where you can go even if you are on a budget. They serve savoury pastas, salumi (cold cuts) and steak tartare. They also have a good wine list.
          </p>
          <p>
           Amon is a family run restaurant that serves unique and delicious food.
          </p>
          <p>
           For reasonably priced food and something classy why not try Osteria del Gatto e la Volpe, or you can dine at 11 LAtini, this restaurant attracts a lot of tourists. They serve a wide range of Italian meals and also offer a good wine list.
          </p>
          <p>
           11 Chicco di Caffe only opens for lunch, you will not believe the prices because they are quite reasonable. Water and wine are buffet style in this restaurant, so you drink all you want until you drop.
          </p>
          <p>
           Trattoria 1 Fratellini is a combination of a wine bar and grocery shop, the prices are also very stable. You can buy nice food from this shop and some of their meals have the lowest calories, like the homemade tiramisu.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Festivals &amp; Events In Florence
          </h3>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           There are a number of festivals in Florence. Festa del Grillo is a cricket festival held in late April or early May in honour of crickets, as their song is believed to bring luck. Rificolona is celebrated in the first week of September  expect children in their Sunday best, carrying paper mache lanterns along the streets. Some believe this festival celebrates Florences entrance into Siena. Scoppio del Carro celebrates the Holy Fire that was kindled using stone chippings from the Holy Sepulchre and then the whole town. The festival occurs on Easter Sunday.
          </p>
