        <a href="/rugby-league/league-news/how-many-70s-rugby-league-stars-can-you-name-masterclass-edition-20150225-13ovxn.html">
         Quiz: How many 70s footy stars can you name: Masterclass edition
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Just as they'd view a koala or kangaroo at a zoo, Americans have responded to Jarryd Hayne's decision to join the San Francisco 49ers with a mix of wonder and curiosity.
      </p>
      <p>
       Hayne's decision to sign with the five-time Super Bowl-winning 49ers received widespread media coverage in the US, with segments aired on TV sports network ESPN and stories in newspaper sports sections across the nation.
      </p>
      <p>
       On major US websites, such as Yahoo.com, Hayne stories have links to YouTube highlight reels from his days as a Parramatta, NSW and Australia rugby league star.
      </p>
      <div class="ad adAlignRight" id="adspot-300x250-pos3" style="display:none;">
       <small>

       </small>

      <p>
       "He won't look out of place physically on an NFL field," Yahoo's Frank Schwab wrote.
      </p>
      <p>
       A Hayne story was the second most read on the San Francisco Chronicle website on Tuesday morning.
      </p>
      <p>
       Like most US media reports, Hayne is referred to as a "rugby" player, with most Americans unable to distinguish between union, league and AFL.
      </p>
      <p>
       "Australian rugby star Jarryd Hayne will attempt to master a second sport with the 49ers," the Chronicle piece began.
      </p>
      <p>
       While the 49ers beat rival NFL teams the Detroit Lions and Seattle Seahawks for the Australian, the team made no mention of Hayne on their 49ers.com website.
      </p>
      <p>
       Hayne is yet to sign a contract, so the 49ers are expected to keep quiet until the 27-year-old puts pen to paper.
      </p>
      <p>
       Hayne said he would soon fly from Sydney to California and would have about five months to prove to the 49ers he could be an NFL player.
      </p>
