       <h1>
        5 Simple Steps For Writing An Amazing Query Letter
       </h1>
       <span>
        by
    Nikki Woods    on Friday, August 8, 2014
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="mk-blog-meta">
       <div class="post-text" style="width:100%;">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.nikkiwoodsmedia.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/5-Simple-Steps-For-Writing-An-Amazing-Query-Letter.jpg">
          <img alt="5 Simple Steps For Writing An Amazing Query Letter" class="size-full wp-image-2196 alignleft" height="251" src="http://www.nikkiwoodsmedia.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/5-Simple-Steps-For-Writing-An-Amazing-Query-Letter.jpg" width="177"/>
         </a>
         So, youve come up with a great idea and youve invested the time into writing a good book that you believe will sell. Dont close your laptop yet. You have one more crucial writing assignmenta Query Letter.
        </p>
        <p>
         A query letter introduces you and your book to potential agents. If they like what they see, theyll invite you to send a sample chapter or the whole manuscript
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are 5 simple steps for writing an amazing query letter:
        </p>
        <p>
         1.
         <strong>
          Keep it Short
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         It should be single page letter with three concise paragraphs: the hook, a mini-synopsis, and your bio.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.nikkiwoodsmedia.com/12-day-plan-simple-writing/">
          <strong>
           RELATED CONTENT: 12 Day Plan of Simple Writing
          </strong>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Hook em!
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The hook is a one-sentence tagline intended to pull the reader in and make her want to read more. It should include where the book takes place and the main characters goal or dilemma.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. Write a Mini Synopsis
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is a summary that gives you a chance to go into more detail about your book. Its the perfect place for you to talk a little more about characters and their motives. Reading the back of your favorite book will give you a good idea on how to write a good synopsis.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Impress with your Bio
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         What have you done as a writer? Include being published in newspapers and literary magazines, writing awards, anything that highlights your skills.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.nikkiwoodsmedia.com/5-reasons-write-day/">
          <strong>
           RELATED CONTENT:
          </strong>
          <strong>
           5 Reasons You Should Write Every Day
          </strong>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5. Have a Big Close
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The formal closing of your query letter should thank the agent for considering you. Also let them know if youve included sample chapters or that your full manuscript is available upon request. Make sure your novel is finished
         <i>
          before
         </i>
         writing a query letter.
        </p>
        <p>
         After sending a well-done query letter you should expect a response from each agent, some good and some bad. Use any criticism you receive to make improvements in your letter and keep reaching out to agents. It only takes one yes to send you on your way to becoming a published author and closer to Global Visibility.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
