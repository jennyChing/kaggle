       <p class="author">
        Posted by
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/blog/guest-bloggers/carol-ward/">
         Carol Ward
        </a>
        , guest blogger of
        <a href="http://www.mvol.com/">

       <p>
        By the look of the events taking place on Marthas Vineyard in the month of October, one might well think that all we do around here in the almost-off-season is eat, drink and make merry.
       </p>
       <p>
        From the onset of October until Halloween ghosts and goblins roam, the Island will host six culinary events that pretty much leave no weekend free of some event that is just about guaranteed to expand your food knowledge and waistband.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sheep-by-Celine-Segel.jpg">
         <img alt="Sheep by Celine Segel" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6561" height="2448" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sheep-by-Celine-Segel.jpg" width="3264"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <i>
         Photo: Harvest time on Marthas Vineyard by Celine Segel
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        The months festivities begin on Saturday, October 4
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        , with the
        <a href="http://livinglocalmv.org" target="_blank" title="Living Local Harvest Fest, 2014">
         Living Local Harvest Fest, 2014
        </a>
        , which takes place annually at the Marthas Vineyard Agricultural Hall in West Tisbury. The day-long event features all sorts of green, farm-focused activities for all ages, including pumpkin carving, hay rides, beer tastings and music. A series of workshops exploring agricultural and locavore lore will also be available. As the night moves on, the music starts and a Community Pig Roast fires up. There is a $15/per person charge for dinner, and this is a BYOB and BYO place-setting event.
       </p>
       <p>
        The very next week brings the
        <a href="http://mvol.com/events/11th-annual-art-chocolate-festival/" target="_blank" title=" 11th Annual Art of Chocolate Festival">
         11th Annual Art of Chocolate Festival
        </a>
        at Featherstone Center for the Arts on both Saturday and Sunday, October 11 and 12, from noon to 4 p.m. Inside, two tastes of the tempting chocolates, candies, cakes, and cookies are $5, and five tastes run $10.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Grapes-by-M-Rosenthal.jpg">
         <img alt="Grapes by M Rosenthal" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6562" height="750" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Grapes-by-M-Rosenthal.jpg" width="1000"/>
        </a>
        <i>
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <i>
         Photo: Fresh grapes by M Rosenthal
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        Recover from your chocolate-induced daze the next day, on October 13, at the
        <a href="http://www.localwildfoodchallenge.com/" target="_blank" title="Local Wild Food Challenge">
         Local Wild Food Challenge
        </a>
        . The event is part of an international series that encourages the culinary use of local, wild foods. In addition to the MV Challenge, Maui, Hawaii; Punkaharju, Finland; Savoie, France; and Eastbourne and Glenorchy in New Zealand hold challenges of their own. Stop by to see what this years entrants have created in their quest to create the best dish with at least one wild ingredient.
       </p>
       <p>
        With your interest in things culinary piqued, the next weekend (October 16-19) brings about Marthas Vineyards
        <a href="http://www.mvfoodandwine.com/" target="_blank">
         7th Annual MV Food &amp; Wine Festival
        </a>
        . As in years past, this event offers a combination of culinary experiences, dinners and tastings. Some of the most popular events of the Festival are the Grand Tasting and the wine dinners, and you can view a complete list and description of the event on their
        <a href="http://www.mvfoodandwine.com/" target="_blank">
         website
        </a>
        . A number of Island hotels and inns, including Edgartowns
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/business/1822/" target="_blank">
         Harbor View Hotel
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/business/3992/" target="_blank">
         Hob Knob
        </a>
        , are offering special packages around this event.
       </p>
       <p>
        Next on the menu is
        <a href="http://www.ffsmv.com/" target="_blank">
         Farm.Field.Sea
        </a>
        , billed as An Island Culinary Adventure, and for good reason! This multifaceted event includes full- and half-day options, during which participants explore the farms, fields and surrounding seas, learning how local farmers, fishermen and chefs combine and complement each others talents to create Marthas Vineyards vibrant, farm-and-sea-centric food scene. There is also a locally sourced dinner with wine pairings created by
        <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/09/22/travel/on-marthas-vineyard-celebrating-the-local-bounty.html?_r=0" target="_blank">
         chef Chris Fischer
        </a>
        on offer at
        <a href="http://www.beachplumrestaurant.com/" target="_blank">
         The Beach Plum Restaurant
        </a>
        in Chilmark.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Harbor.jpg">
         <img alt="Harbor" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6563" height="2448" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Harbor.jpg" width="3264"/>
        </a>
        <i>
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <i>
         Photo: Looking out over the harbor by Celine Segel
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        The feast of food-related events concludes on Friday, October 31, with
        <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/09/22/travel/on-marthas-vineyard-celebrating-the-local-bounty.html?_r=0" target="_blank">
         Swine &amp; Dine
        </a>
        at the Harbor View Hotel. There, youll find live country music, local beer, and all-you-can-eat Pulled Pork Sandwiches, slow-smoked for an entire day from locally raised pigs and then served with a selection of BBQ sauces created by Island chefs.
       </p>
       <p>
        If youve got an appetite for some fun and food this fall  Marthas Vineyards table is set and ready.
       </p>
