         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe border="0" frameborder="0" height="90" marginwidth="0" scrolling="no" src="http://rcm-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/cm?t=tomswebclass-20&amp;o=1&amp;p=48&amp;l=ur1&amp;category=computers_accesories&amp;banner=0N0WAHVZD5GCTQQ3MP82&amp;f=ifr&amp;linkID=FCM5GGLRMIHCJQ6C" style="border:none;" width="728">

         <p>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          When installing a computer, especially notebooks there is a strong possibility to face with unknown device issues. After you have installed your PC or notebook, you have to install all drivers from a CD/DVD, which came with your computer. In modern times you dont get the drivers on a CD or DVD, you have to download all drivers from the internet. The problem appears when you dont know your hardware configuration. For example, you have an Toshiba notebook and you openToshiba support website and there are 3 different drivers for your wi-ficard. A possible way is to download all three drivers and try to install. If it is not, try the other. But this is a waste of time. In this post I will explain how to determine your configuration and how to reveal the unknown device.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          After a fresh Windows 7 installation go to your device manager, there are two steps:
         </p>
         <ol style="text-align: justify;">
          <li>
           Start &gt;Computer (right click &gt; Properties), in left upper corner click on device manager and run as administrator
          </li>
          <li>
           Click on start and write down
           <span style="color: #ff0000;">
            devmgmt.msc
           </span>
           . When it appears in the search, right click on it and select the Run as administrator option.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          You now should see a picture like this. Now focus on the Other devices.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pic1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-471" height="568" src="http://www.tomswebclass.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/pic1.png" width="787"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          First thing you can try is right click on a device you want to install, right click on it and select Update driver software. It the windows update doesnt find any drivers than you need to do it by hand.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          In a few steps I will explain how to do it.
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
          Solution
         </h2>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Right click on 
          <strong>
           Unknown device
          </strong>
           and select
          <strong>
           properties
          </strong>
          . A new window will appear, then go to
          <strong>
           Details
          </strong>
          tab, and select
          <strong>
           Hardware Ids.
          </strong>
          You should see a window like this below.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pic2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-474" height="575" src="http://www.tomswebclass.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/pic2.png" width="785"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Now, we know more about the unknown device. Right click on the first value (in this case ACPI\HPQ0006) and select Copy.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Open internet browser, go to your search engine and paste the copied text. Just look the first 2-3 answers and you will find your device name and driver download location. In my case it was like the picture shows.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <img alt="quick launch buttons" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-476" height="116" src="http://www.tomswebclass.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/quick-launch-buttons.jpg" width="345"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The guy that wrote this post in 2009 shared the name of the device and a direct link to the HP support to download the drivers. I solved my problem in 2 minutes.I could download all the hp drivers and install it for 2-3 days, but it is a huge waste of time. Dont panic if your search engine writes computer models that are not related to yours, because Toshiba, HP, Asus, Acer and others have the same hardware(chip) that controls quick launch buttons.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          I hope this will help you, if you have any questions, suggestions or different problems, contact me.
         </p>
