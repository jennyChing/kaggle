            <a href="http://gnr8.typepad.com/gnr8_news/2006/12/interactive_cou.html">
             Interactive Couch
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/59612088@N00/321591336" title="remoteImage.jpg">
              </a>
              <a href="http://gnr8.typepad.com/photos/uncategorized/couch.jpg">
               <img alt="Couch" border="0" class="image-full" src="http://gnr8.typepad.com/photos/uncategorized/couch.jpg" title="Couch"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Forget couples counseling to tell you you're drifting apart from your lover. A UK designer has come up with a concept thats illuminatingrelationships from their living rooms.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              This sofa is embedded with patterned lights which react to seating arrangements. As a couplesit apart on the sofa, they are surprised when a cold blue patternbegins to grow around them signifying their distance apart from eachother. With the pattern changing in relation to weight and proximity,as they move towards each other warm pink petals begin to formrepresenting their closeness.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Using light emitting(Electroluminescent) technology in conjunction with trigger sensitivematerials, the pattern is displayed in relation to where a person issitting. The petals will randomly illuminate around your body anddisappear when you move to another spot, meaning an ever evolving andchanging pattern display occurs. With the thoughtful and playfuldevelopment of the pattern, the wonderment of where the pattern willlead adds to the experience of using the sofa.
             </p>

           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://gnr8.typepad.com/gnr8_news/2005/04/solar_wallpaper.html">
             solar wallpaper
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a href="http://gnr8.typepad.com/photos/uncategorized/solar_wallpaper_copy.jpg">
               <img alt="Solar_wallpaper_copy" border="0" class="image-full" src="http://gnr8.typepad.com/photos/uncategorized/solar_wallpaper_copy.jpg" title="Solar_wallpaper_copy"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Designers: Marta Lwin, Ty Whitfield, Teresita Cochran, and Ramakrishnan Subramanian
             </p>
             <p>
              In a similar vein to
              <a href="http://www.frontdesign.se/">
               Front Design
              </a>
              's
              <a href="http://gnr8.typepad.com/gnr8_news/2004/06/light_sensitive.html">
               Light Sensitive Wallpaper
              </a>
              , "
              <a href="http://stage.itp.nyu.edu/~mjl359/sustain/wall.html">
               solar wallpaper
              </a>
              " (designed at NYU's Interactive Telecommunications Program) uses light (or lack thereof) as it's primary catalyst.
             </p>
             <p>
              By embedding electroluminescent materials into the design pattern of the wall paper and incorporating a built-in light sensor, the wallpaper can respond to the lighting requirement of a room, acting as a decorative element when a room is naturally bright, and as a flat wallpaper light when the room requires more light. With power supplied from a solar charged battery, it can also be manually controlled to increase or decrease luminosity. Sustainable, efficient, functional, and technologically sexy.
             </p>

             hugms
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a href="http://gnr8.typepad.com/photos/uncategorized/hugms3_1.jpg">
               <img alt="Hugms3_1" border="0" height="200" src="http://gnr8.typepad.com/gnr8_news/images/hugms3_1.jpg" title="Hugms3_1" width="223"/>
              </a>
              <a href="http://gnr8.typepad.com/photos/uncategorized/hugms_2_1.jpg">

              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Designer:
              <a href="http://www.argobot.com/">
               Mark Argo
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Have you ever wanted to send a hug to someone, or tell them you love them, without getting bogged down in some humdrum chitchat about dinner or the kids or whatever...if the answer is yes, you need a
              <a href="http://www.mobjects.net/hugms/">
               hugms
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <h4>
              The latest in a growing number of "
              <a href="http://gnr8.typepad.com/gnr8_news/2004/05/dal_lamp.html">
               emotional technology
              </a>
              " products, hugms is a device designed for sending someone you care about a hug using your mobile phone.
             </h4>
             <p>
              once
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.mobjects.net/hugms/">
                hugms
               </a>
              </strong>
              is connected to your mobile phone all you have to do is send it the phone number of the person you'd like to hug and then squeeze. sensors inside the device read how long and how hard you have squeezed and will format a text message based on your hug.
             </p>
             <h4>
              for example, a long squeeze would look like
              <ul>
               <p>
                'hhhhhhuuuuuuuuuuggggggg'
               </p>
              </ul>
              while a short and hard squeeze would look like
              <ul>
               <p>
                'hhHHUUUUUUugg'
               </p>
              </ul>
             </h4>
             <p>
              <strong>
               hugms
              </strong>
              also communicates with light and color. the strength of the squeeze is mapped to a change in color. if the person you're hugging also has a hugms, then your message will also trigger the same color change. finally, your loved one can return the hug to you, either by squeezing their hugms or just replying to the text message. once received your hugms will glow a warm color, giving you the knowledge that you're being hugged in return.
             </p>

         <h2 class="date-header">
          22.02.2005
         </h2>
         <div class="entry-category-prototypes entry-author-gnr8 entry-type-post entry" id="entry-3659655">
          <div class="entry-inner">
           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://gnr8.typepad.com/gnr8_news/2005/02/lovejackets.html">
             loveJackets
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <img alt="Lovejacket" border="0" src="http://gnr8.typepad.com/photos/uncategorized/lovejacket.jpg" title="Lovejacket"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Concept &amp; Design:
              <a href="http://www.5050ltd.com/peppy.html">
               Despina Papadopoulos
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              As part of an ongoing series of proposed products, from
              <a href="http://www.5050ltd.com/">
               Studio 5050
              </a>
              , that function at the interface of emotion, technology &amp; design, the love jackets takes the concept of "finding each other" to a whole new level.
             </p>
             <p>
              From the website:
             </p>
             <blockquote dir="ltr" style="MARGIN-RIGHT: 0px">
              <p dir="ltr" style="MARGIN-RIGHT: 0px">
               "A pair of jackets emits, and polls for a particular signal. Once the pair finds each other, in at least 10 feet distance, facing each other, the two beep  emitting a sound akin to crickets mating, and a pattern of LEDs blinks (light emitting diodes; small, bright, energy efficient lights). Each jacket responds only to its unique pair.
              </p>
              <p>
               The technology used is basic: an infra-red receiver and transmitter, a PIC chip (programmable interrupt controller) that controls the LEDs and speaker output and sends out the bits of code that allows the pairs to find each other. The components are all surface mount which means that the technology is as transparent as possible. Instead of wires, the components are attached to the circuit board via conductive fabric conduits.
              </p>
              <p>
               While the project aims to explore social interactional patterns and institute new ones, it also elaborates ways in which technology can seamlessly be integrated in garments. The aim of the project is not to create cyber garments, but use technology in surprising and innovative ways and place emphasis not on the technology but on its uses."
              </p>
