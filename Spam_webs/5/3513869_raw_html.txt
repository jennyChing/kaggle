           2D Animation Effects: Volcanoes, Hails, And Rain
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p class="posted-by-text">
          <span>
           Posted by:
          </span>
          <span class="vcard author">
           <span class="fn">
            <a href="http://www.cubiclite.com/author/thecubiclite/" rel="author" title="Posts by TheCubiclite">
             TheCubiclite
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>

        <div class="post_content">
         <div class="shadow_img_frame tt-blog-featured">
          <div class="img-preload">
           <img alt="2D Animation Effects: Volcanoes, Hails, And Rain" height="218" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.cubiclite.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Thumbnail-Blog-Post.jpg?resize=804%2C327" width="538"/>
          </div>
          <!-- END img-preload -->
         </div>
         <!-- END post_thumb -->
         <p>
          <span id="more-647">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Sometimes your animation scenes may be set up in special environments like in the rain or snow. Or your story may take your characters to interesting or dangerous places like near a volcano or into the deep oceans withunderwater steam vents.
          <strong>
           Toon Boom Studio
          </strong>
          lets you easily create these scenes through the programs
          <strong>
           Forces Of Nature Effects
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          In this post, Ill show you how to usethe
          <strong>
           Forces Of Nature Effects
          </strong>
          such as the
          <strong>
           Hail Effect
          </strong>
          ,
          <strong>
           Rain Effect
          </strong>
          , and the
          <strong>
           Volcano Effect
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <h3>
          The Hail, Snow, And Rain Effects
         </h3>
         <p>
          The
          <strong>
           Hail, Snow,
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           Rain Effects
          </strong>
          work very similarly, and can be used for creating weather conditions like snow, rain, and hail. Watch this demo video to see how its done:
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/M_WVGdYYLNg" width="560">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Be sure to view the usage example for the
          <strong>
           Hail and Rain Effects
          </strong>
          that I put together near the end of the video (spoiler alert: it rains money!). You can see in that example that the coins fall on the ground as well as on the characters arm. That is accomplished by creating two separate Hail Effects with different parameters. If you have the software, you can try recreating something similar for practice.
         </p>
         <h3>
          The Volcano Effect
         </h3>
         <p>
          The next demo shows you how to use the
          <strong>
           Volcano Effect
          </strong>
          . As the name implies, this effect lets yousimulate lava spewing out ofan erupting volcano. However, in the video example, I use the effect to create underwater steam vents. Heres the demo video (watch till the end for examples):
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/l6sacn4v-XA" width="560">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Tips For Using The Forces Of Nature Effects
         </h3>
         <p>
          From my experience, using the
          <strong>
           Forces Of Nature Effects
          </strong>
          can be a little confusing at first. One important concept is the difference between the effects
          <strong>
           Static Parameters
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           Dynamic Parameters
          </strong>
          (in the
          <strong>
           Properties Tab
          </strong>
          ). Generally speaking, the
          <strong>
           Static Parameters
          </strong>
          allow you to program an effects common settings that apply to
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           all frames
          </span>
          . The
          <strong>
           Dynamic Parameters
          </strong>
          , however, can change from frame to frame. You can create variations in the
          <strong>
           Dynamic Parameters
          </strong>
          by creating key-frames (CTRL-I) and programming the desired values in the parameter entry fields. Remember to go to the correct frame where you created the key-frame for setting the
          <strong>
           Dynamic Parameters
          </strong>
          . The following figures should help to clarify these key points.
         </p>
         <p>
          First, the Nature Effect element will appear in brown in the Timeline View by default. Add key-frames to create changes in the effects behavior as time progresses in your scene.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Toon Boom Studio Timeline View - Nature Effect 1" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-664" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.cubiclite.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Toon-Boom-Studio-Timeline-View-Nature-Effect-1.jpg?resize=500%2C251"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The figure below shows the Static Parameters pane for the Hail Effect (one of the Forces Nature Effects).
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Toon Boom Studio Hail Effect STATIC Settings" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-667" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.cubiclite.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Toon-Boom-Studio-Hail-Effect-Settings.jpg?resize=440%2C500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The figure below shows the Dynamic Parameters pane for the Hail Effect. In the figure, the values are disabled (greyed out) because the selected frame is NOT a key-frame. You need to go to the desired key-frame to make changes to the values in the Dynamic Parameters pane.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <img alt="Toon Boom Studio Hail Effect Dynamic Settings" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-666" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.cubiclite.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Toon-Boom-Studio-Hail-Effect-Dynamic-Settings.jpg?resize=468%2C500"/>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Happy creating!
         </p>
