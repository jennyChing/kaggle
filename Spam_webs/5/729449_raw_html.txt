            Disney's "Frozen" Movie Review
           </h1>

            <p>
             <span class="font-size-4">
              <a href="http://cs412225.vk.me/v412225863/c5f3/p2mMiEjqPyg.jpg" target="_blank">
               <img class="align-center" src="http://cs412225.vk.me/v412225863/c5f3/p2mMiEjqPyg.jpg"/>

            <p>
             <span class="font-size-4">
              Disneys Frozen Movie Review
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Frozen, Disneys latest musical film, is the most anticipated production from the company in years. When movie critics learned that Idina Menzel would be one of the singing voices in the film, the news traveled fast, and many people who are fans of Broadway immediately became interested in the show. Some critics have attested that Frozen is Disneys greatest production since The Lion King, which is an incredible comparison. The Lion King is regarded as Disneys most successful production for its productivity at the box office, longevity and its ability to be adapted for the Broadway stage. The success of Frozen will likely reflect an identical pattern, including high sales, popularity and a future Broadway production.
             <iframe _origwidth="560" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/TbQm5doF_Uc?wmode=opaque" width="560">
             </iframe>
            </p>
            <p>
             Frozen is loosely based on Hans Christian Andersens fairy tale, The Snow Queen. The movie features the voices of Kristen Bell, Idina Menzel, Jonathan Goff, Josh Gad, Santino Fontana and others. Frozen also has some plot and music similarities with the Broadway production of Stephen Schwartzs Wicked.
             <a href="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/image002-600x364.jpg" target="_blank">
              <img class="align-center" src="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/image002-600x364.jpg"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Frozen sounds like an animated version of a live theater production. This seems to be one of the major goals set by the production staff at Disney. It is easy to see that the company will translate the film for the stage. While many Disney films are musicals, there really is no guarantee that they will attempt to turn a movie into a play. While some Disney productions intentionally give off the impression that they are movies that just so happen to have music in them, Frozen is the companys most overt musical production since High School Musical. Frozen is a musical in the traditional sense, but it also captures all of the adventure and character development that makes Disney movies great.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.lamercerissima.it/grafica/puntocroce_disney.jpg" target="_blank">
              <img class="align-center" src="http://www.lamercerissima.it/grafica/puntocroce_disney.jpg"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Memorable characters are what make Disney movies have such a lasting effect on observers. Traditionally, Disney has created the most popular animated characters in the history of American cinema. Many of these characters are princesses that capture the imagination of audiences with their personalities and motivations to change the world. Frozen presents a new princess and a queen to add to its list of most memorable characters.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.tienphong.vn/Cache/478/412478_630.jpg" target="_blank">
              <img class="align-center" src="http://www.tienphong.vn/Cache/478/412478_630.jpg"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Anna and Elsa were sisters who were inseparable playmates when they were young. One day the girls were playing when Elsa used her special powers to turn everything into ice and snow and accidentally hurts Anna. A troll king heals Anna, and her parents decide to have him erase the incident from Annas memory. To further protect this from happening again, the parents decided to shelter Elsa from others, especially her sister. They shut her away inside the castle, and gave her as little contact with others as possible.
            </p>
            <p>
             When the girls grow a little older, Anna does not understand why Elsa shuts her out. Anna has no memory of the accident and believes that her sister dislikes her. Elsa loves Anna, but keeps her distance because she wants her to be safe. Elsa becomes Queen of the castle, and Anna becomes Princess. Elsa leaves the castle when she nearly turns everyone in the castle into ice. Frozen is about Anna's pursuit to get Elsa to accept herself, and to bring back the closeness they had when they were younger.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://cdn.images.express.co.uk/img/dynamic/36/590x/frozen-446781.jpg" target="_blank">
              <img class="align-center" src="http://cdn.images.express.co.uk/img/dynamic/36/590x/frozen-446781.jpg"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Frozen has a great plot and strong characters. Its another solid film to add to the collection of Disney classics. The production raises the bar for animated films because it does everything well. It tells a good story, features captivating music and has some of the best computer-animation in the history of film.
            </p>
