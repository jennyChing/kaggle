        <div class="entry-content">
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          This day is the celebration dedicated to the Lord Shiva which falls on the Trayodashi of the month Fagun (February/March).
         </p>
         <p>
          <script async="async" src="//s7.addthis.com/js/300/addthis_widget.js#pubid=ra-53fec806754e8d85" type="text/javascript">
          </script>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://www.samratnepal.com/Nepal/tours/mutinath_pashupatinath_tour.htm" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Maha-Shivaratri-pashupatinath" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-937" height="1067" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Maha-Shivaratri-pashupatinath.jpg" width="1600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.samratnepal.com/kailash-yatra.html" target="_blank">
            Nepal
           </a>
          </strong>
          is the only Hindu kingdom in the world and thus the land of Lord Shiva, Lord of all Lords, for here you can feel his presence everywhere. Even in the sacred texts of the Hindus it has been stated that
          <strong>
           <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Tibet/kailash_mansarovar_tour.htm" target="_blank">
            Mt. Kailash
           </a>
          </strong>
          in the Himalayas is the abode of
          <strong>
           <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Tibet/kailash_tourby_helicoptor_tour.htm" target="_blank">
            Lord Shiva
           </a>
          </strong>
          or Mahadeva as he is also known. Shiva the Destroyer of Evil is among the most praised and worshipped of all the gods in the Hindu religion. Hindus all over the world know him through different names and forms. The country has thousands of idols and monuments, which glorify his name, the most common one being the Shiva Linga or the phallus of Shiva that represents him. For it is the Shiva linga that Hindus regard as the symbol of creation, the beginning of everything. Shiva Ratri is the night of Lord Shiva when He himself was created by His own Divine Grace and Hindus all over the world celebrate this day with a lot of zeal and enthusiasm. Shiva Ratri literally means  the night consecrated to Shiva. This auspicious festival falls on the fourteenth day of the waning moon in the month of Falgun, (February  March in the Gregorian calendar ). The temple of
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.samratnepal.com/Nepal/tours/mutinath_pashupatinath_tour.htm" target="_blank">
            Pashupatinath
           </a>
          </strong>
          in
          <strong>
           <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/tours/kathmandu_chitwan_pokhar_8D_tour.htm" target="_blank">
            Kathmandu
           </a>
          </strong>
          which is considered as one of the holiest shrines of the Hindus, glorifying Lord Shiva, thus receives more than 100,000 worshippers during the festival of Shiva Ratri. These worshippers come from far and wide to pay their respects and homage to Mahadev on his sacred day.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://www.samratnepal.com/kailash-yatra.html" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Maha-Shivaratri" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-936" height="360" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Maha-Shivaratri.jpg" width="540"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://www.samratnepal.com/kailash-yatra.html" target="_blank">
           <strong>
             : 
           </strong>
          </a>
          We worship Lord Shiva with the maha mantra Om Namaha Shivaya. The Mahamantra  Om Namaha Shivaya also known as shadakshari mantra ( ). Each letter in this mantra has spiritual meaning. This mantra is also known as maha mantra.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Om (
          </strong>
          <strong>
           )
          </strong>
          Om alone is the sound of life. It is the endless vibration that flows through the universe  and provides life in every living being. Chanting Om revitalizes the mind and body and settles the mind. Om is the beginning and it is the end. In Sanskrit, the meaning of Om is avati, or rakuati. Rakuati means one who protects or sustains. Om is the most powerful mantra.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          (Om as made up of three parts. These are a, o, and m. Our sound starts with a, when we speak go to O and end with M, so Om is the totality. It is a mantra which provides complete rest to our body and energy to our mind. )
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Meaning of each letters of the mantra
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          N (): Main god of all the regions (loks)
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          M (): One who gifts supreme and spiritual knowledge (dnyan) and destroys greatest sins
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Shi (): generous, calm and responsible for the beginning by Lord Shiva
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          va () : Symbol of vehicle (Nandi) the bull and the Vasuki and Vamangi Energies (Shakti)
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Y (): Positive residence of highest heaven and Lord Shiva.
         </p>

          </a>
         </p>
