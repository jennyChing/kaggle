          <p>
           After years of success in Europe, beautiful Spanish actress
           <b>
            Penlope Cruz
           </b>
           the longtime muse of
           <b>
            Pedro Almdovar
           </b>
           made a splash stateside by jumping borders and language barriers with her turn as Alejandra Villarreal in
           <b>
            Billy Bob Thornton
           </b>
           s
           <i>
            All the Pretty Horses
           </i>
           in 2000. A year later, the actress starred in
           <i>
            Blow
           </i>
           and
           <i>
            Vanilla Sky;
           </i>
           American audiences fell in love with herand so did one of the worlds biggest movie stars, her
           <i>
            Vanilla Sky
           </i>
           costar,
           <b>
            Tom Cruise.
           </b>
           The same year
           <i>
            Vogue
           </i>
           featured the actress for the first time on the cover of the March Power issue. On the occasion of
           <a href="http://www.vogue.com/magazine/article/the-dream-life-of-penelope-cruz/" target="_blank">
            her June 2011 cover
           </a>
           (her fourth for the magazine), we look back at Penlope Cruz in
           <i>
            Vogue.
           </i>
          </p>
          <p>
           <i>
            Featuring covers, advertisements, articles, photographs, and illustrations in their original context, the
           </i>
           Vogue
           <i>
            Archive offers a glimpse of
           </i>
           Vogue
           <i>
            s unparalleled record of fashion, social, and cultural ideas.
           </i>
          </p>
          <p>
           Vogue.com registered users have access to a selection of editor-chosen issues from the Vogue
           <i>
            Archive.
           </i>
           Vogue
           <i>
            magazine  subscribers have access to a selection of 36 issues, including the very  first issue of the magazine from 1892. To access the
           </i>
           Vogue
           <i>
            Archive, go to
            <a href="http://www.vogue.com/archive/" target="_blank">
             voguearchive.com
            </a>
            and use the Archive Login in the upper-right-hand corner.
           </i>
          </p>
