         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          The Generosity Of Twitter At Its Best
         </h1>
         <div class="post-info">
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-60 photo" height="60" src="http://2.gravatar.com/avatar/28eeb739594ac70194cad4fd26b82b8c?s=60&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g" srcset="http://2.gravatar.com/avatar/28eeb739594ac70194cad4fd26b82b8c?s=120&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g 2x" width="60"/>
           <a class="author-name" href="http://www.careerealism.com/author/sparktalk/">
            CAREEREALISM
           </a>
           <br/>
           April 27, 2009
          </p>
          <p class="categories">
           <a href="http://www.careerealism.com/category/job-search/">
            Job Search
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.careerealism.com/category/uncategorized/">

          </p>
         </div>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <div class="inpost">
          <section class="widget widget_text" id="text-272629749">
           <div class="widget-wrap">
            <div class="textwidget">
             <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
             </script>
             <!-- In Post Ad - Top-fold Location -->
             <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7827670739696453" data-ad-slot="3864162526" style="display:inline-block;width:300px;height:250px">
             </ins>
             <script>
              (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
             </script>
            </div>
           </div>
          </section>
         </div>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            By CAREEREALISM Founder, J.T. ODonnell
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          For those of you that dont tweet  let me tell you the
          <strong>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            #1 thing
           </span>
          </strong>
          you are missing out on: generosity. Twitter is the most giving community Ive ever experienced. Yes, there are spammers and unsavory folks who are trying to make a quick buck, but get past that, do your homework on
          <a href="http://www.careerealism.com/career-rx/" target="_blank">
           the right way to use Twitter
          </a>
          and you can enter a world that, until now, has only been dreamed of. Let me explain.
          <span id="more-3266">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Twitter is the great equalizer.
          </strong>
          You dont need extensive credentials, lots of money or a secret password to become a player in the Twittersphere. Anyone can sign up and tweet. Aside from internet access, theres no barrier to entry. Your success on Twitter will be determined by your ability to convey your message in a mere 140 characters. All it takes is some thoughtful consideration of what to say and how to say it. In other words, those using Twitter effectively have mastered how to say more with less. (A trait we can all work on, dont you think?)
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Youll connect with the greatest folks youll
           <em>
            NEVER
           </em>
           meet!
          </strong>
          In the last 7 months Ive used Twitter, Ive made more professional contacts, and even have a whole new set of folks I call friends, that started via simple Twitter exchanges. Like minds show no discrimination on Twitter. You can connect and befriend people on Twitter that I GUARANTEE you may not have otherwise. Where else can you get access to someone clear across the country (the world, for that matter) who lives in a small town that you most likely never intend to visit in your lifetime?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           The Twitter community is rooted in kindness and good professional etiquette.
          </strong>
          If you want people to follow you, listen to you, and most importantly, respect you on Twitter  you need to create interesting, non self-serving tweets that make people say, That was worth reading. Honestly, Twitters power to do good makes me giddy. Why? Because it helped me make a dream come true
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Twitter Makes FREE Worth Something.
          </strong>
          Years ago, when I became a career strategist, I took a course on how to promote my services. The advice given was, Charge as much as you can. The higher the fees, the more satisfied the customer will be because they wont want to feel they made a bad buying decision, and thus, will embrace your ideas and rave about your services. The old, you-get-what-you-pay-for theory. As you can imagine, that didnt sit well with me. I already knew thousands of people were unhappy in their careers and couldnt afford working with a professional strategist (and that was in a good economy!). Fast forward to today, and Twitters enabling me help those who need it most!
         </p>
         <p>
          We started the
          <a href="http://twitter.com/careerealism" target="_blank">
           Twitter Advice Project
          </a>
          (a.k.a. T.A.P. into CAREEREALISM) less than 2 months ago. The idea was to have professional experts (I was lucky enough to convince a few charitable colleagues using Twitter to help me) donate their time and expertise to those in need by tweeting answers to career questions sent in by followers of the CAREEREALISM Twitter feed. As of today, we have
          <a href="http://www.careerealism.com/1300-new-followers-6-more-experts-1-career-advice-tool-on-twitter-wellwe-think-so/" target="_blank">
           19 AMAZING experts tweeting to OVER 5,000 followers
          </a>
          in need of advice. The program has exceeded every expectation I had for it. I get tweets daily from job seekers, thanking us for donating our time to help them get the advice they need. Ive received dozens of requests to join the program from fellow experts as well. The best part everyone can participate. Experts who want to share their advice can tweet their own answers that will show up in the search results on Twitter, or they can post their advice directly on the original blog post of the question. And job seekers can easily go back and look at all the questions weve already answeredto immediately see the advice shared by the experts. Or, if they have a new question, they can submit it to us at
          <a href="mailto:twitter@careerealism.com" target="_blank">
           twitter@careerealism.com
          </a>
          and get it answered directly.
         </p>
         <p>
          So, if you are one of those folks whos been saying, Twitter?! What do I need to do that for?  I hope I just convinced you to get on board. There is a lot of wonderful giving and receiving going on via Twitter. The kind of stuff that makes you feel really happy. Dont miss out on your chance to experience the generosity of Twitter. Whether you choose to give or receive  its all good!
         </p>
        </div>
