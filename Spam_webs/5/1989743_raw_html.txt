        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Hard-Cook Eggs in Your Oven. For Real!
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-03-02T18:16:59+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          March 2, 2013
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://homemakinghacks.com/author/admin" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Dawn Lopez
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Am I the only person that cant boil eggs properly? I always have some issue and end up cracking eggs or they kind of explode in the pot. So frustrating! Ive been meaning to try out hard cooked eggs in the slow cooker too, that seemed more up my alley. Well, I just learned about another way to cook em too and that is in the oven! I wish that Id heard of this years ago, I could have spared myself a lot of frustration and more importantly self esteem 
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://homemakinghacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/how-to-make-hard-cooked-eggs.jpg" rel="nofollow">
          <img alt="How To Make Hard Cooked Eggs in the Oven #Recipes #Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-28933" height="759" src="http://homemakinghacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/how-to-make-hard-cooked-eggs.jpg" width="520"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How To Make Hard Cook Eggs in the Oven
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What youll need:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Eggs
         </li>
         <li>
          Muffin Tin
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://homemakinghacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/eggs-in-the-oven.jpg" rel="nofollow">
          <img alt="How to make hard cooked eggs in the oven #recipes #tutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-28936" height="313" src="http://homemakinghacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/eggs-in-the-oven.jpg" width="520"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What to do:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Preheat your oven to 325
          <b>
           .
          </b>
         </li>
         <li>
          Place your eggs into your muffin tin.
         </li>
         <li>
          Cook for 25 minutes.
         </li>
         <li>
          Remove from oven.
         </li>
         <li>
          Using tongs, transfer eggs to a large bowl of cold water for 10 minutes.
         </li>
         <li>
          Voila, you now have perfect hard cooked eggs!
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://homemakinghacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Hard-Cooked-Eggs.jpg" rel="nofollow">
          <img alt="Hard Cooked Eggs in the Oven #Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-28937" height="347" src="http://homemakinghacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Hard-Cooked-Eggs.jpg" width="520"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I am definitely going to be baking my eggs from now on! It worked flawlessly for me. No more eggs exploding in my pan!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://homemakinghacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/hard-cooked-egg.jpg" rel="nofollow">
          <img alt="Hard Cooked Eggs in the Oven" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-28938" height="347" src="http://homemakinghacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/hard-cooked-egg.jpg" width="520"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
