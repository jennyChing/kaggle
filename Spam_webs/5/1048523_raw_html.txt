         <h1 class="mainheadline">
          Paramus Tesla service center, showroom gets green light
         </h1>
        </div>
        <time>

          June 28, 2014
Last updated: Saturday, June 28, 2014, 1:21 AM
         </div>
        </time>
        <div class="storyauthorv2" style="text-transform:uppercase;font-size:12px;font-weight:bold;padding-top:9px;">
         By JOAN VERDON
        </div>
        <div class="storyauthorv2" style="float:left;text-transform:uppercase;font-size:10px;line-height:14px;">

        <div id="storybody" style="margin-top:20px; font-size:16px; line-height:24px; color: #2c2c2c;">
         <p>
          * Electric-car maker gets zoning OK for showroom, service center in Paramus
         </p>
         <p>
          Tesla Motors has a green light to open a 27,000-square-foot service center, showroom and charging site for its electric cars on Route 17 in Paramus, its first full-service location in North Jersey.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Paramus Zoning Board of Adjustment on Thursday evening unanimously approved several variances Tesla needed to move forward with its plan to convert the former Sixth Avenue Electronics store.
         </p>
         <p>
          The company said 807 Tesla owners currently live within a 25-mile radius of the site, and it is hoping the showroom, education center and area where buyers can take delivery of their new vehicles will spur more Tesla ownership in the region.
         </p>
         <p>
          Christy Ortins, a design manager for Tesla, said the company picked Paramus because response to the Tesla retail store at Westfield Garden State Plaza has demonstrated that there are many potential buyers in North Jersey. "There seems to be quite a market here where there are people who are forward-looking" and interested in switching to an electric vehicle.
         </p>
         <p>
          Tesla stopped selling cars in New Jersey because of rules the Motor Vehicle Commission began enforcing in April. The rules effectively barred Tesla from selling cars because it does not use franchises as dealerships. That is expected to change soon, however. The state Assembly on June 16 voted unanimously to allow Tesla to again sell its cars directly to consumers. The Senate is considering similar legislation.
         </p>
         <p>
          The company now operates the Garden State Plaza location and another store in Short Hills as so-called galleries, where consumers can look at cars and learn about them  but not purchase. Consumers in New Jersey can purchase the vehicles only online, through a process that allows each new Tesla to be customized by the buyer.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Route 17 facility will have three super-charger stations that can charge a Tesla battery to 50 percent of capacity within 20 minutes. It will also have seven standard charging stations for longer-term charging of cars that are being serviced or picked up by new owners. The charging stations will be open 24 hours, seven days a week. The service center and showroom will be closed Sundays.
         </p>
         <p>
          Attorney Stuart Liebman, in presenting Tesla's request for the variances to the Paramus board, called the proposal a great opportunity to replace a long-vacant and neglected retail space with a brand "Paramus can be very proud to have within its borders." Tesla officials did not say when the new location would open.
         </p>
         <p>
          Route 17 is becoming a magnet for luxury car brands, drawn by household incomes in northern New Jersey that are among the highest in the country. Tesla prices start at close to $70,000, and a vehicle can cost more than $100,000 depending on the features.
         </p>
         <p>
          Email: verdon@northjersey.com
         </p>
