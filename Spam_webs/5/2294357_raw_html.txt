&lt;p&gt;&nbsp;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;There is a large need to educate both consumers and investors alike when it comes to the future of energy. I mean most people can tell you what they pay for a gallon of gasoline but how many can really tell you what they pay for a kilowatt? Did you know software and cybersecurity are critical to energy infrastructure and assets? Can we develop sustainable new energy resources without depending on water which is finite? &lt;span id="more-4508"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;There are just so many questions when it comes to renewable energy and how to prepare oneself for a career that may help us keep our lights on. This suggests that education needs to play a vital role in shaping the minds of our youth in order to better prepare them to become the capable energy leaders of tomorrow.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I recently gave presentations at the World Nuclears Fall Symposium in London and during New York Climate Week. On both occasions I was asked, how can we get more of todays youth interested in the cleantech space? This question keeps coming up, so outside of increased gamification, public awareness campaigns and more unbiased research written in laymans terms, I thought it may make sense to list for you some of the many U.S. academic programs specifically devoted to those interested in energy, sustainability, water and cybersecurity.&lt;/p&gt;
