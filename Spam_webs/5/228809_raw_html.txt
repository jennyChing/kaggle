             <h4 class="nunito">
              <img alt="Orange Icon" src="/Img/public/Home/OrangeIcon.png"/>
              <b>
               Strawberry Shortcake
              </b>
             </h4>
             <p>
              <span class="bdBlue">
               1 Protein
              </span>
              <span class="bdOrange">
               2 Fats
              </span>
              <span class="bdGreen">
               1 Carb
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Prep Time:
              </b>
              20 minutes
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Cook Time:
              </b>
              18 minutes
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Servings:
              </b>
              10
             </p>
             <p>
              Great idea if you're craving something sweet but don't want to ruin your eating habits.
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="large-12 medium-12 small-12 columns padding-top05">
             <p>
              <b>
               Ingredients
              </b>
              <br/>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               2 cups almond flour
              </li>
              <li>
               1/2 cup tapioca flour
              </li>
              <li>
               1/2 tsp sea salt
              </li>
              <li>
               1/2 tsp baking soda
              </li>
              <li>
               1/2 cup butter, at room temperature
              </li>
              <li>
               2 cage-free, organic eggs
              </li>
              <li>
               3/4 tsp lemon juice
              </li>
              <li>
               2 Tbsp raw honey
              </li>
              <li>
               2 cans full-fat coconut milk
              </li>
              <li>
               1 tsp vanilla extract
              </li>
              <li>
               1-2 Tbsp maple syrup
              </li>
              <li>
               2 cups fresh strawberries
              </li>
             </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="large-12 medium-12 small-12 columns padding-top05">
             <p>
              <b>
               Directions
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              Preheat oven to 350F. Combine almond flour, tapioca flour, sea salt, and baking soda in a bowl. Add the butter to the bowl and massage it into the flour mixture.
                In a separate bowl, combine eggs, lemon juice, and honey. Use a whisk to mix well. Add wet mixture to dry mixture a little at a time, mixing gently with a spatula or your hands after each addition. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Spoon batter onto the baking sheet  should make about 20 medium-sized biscuits. Bake for about 18 minutes.
              <br/>
              Cream - Refrigerate cans of coconut milk overnight.
                Open can and use a spoon to carefully scoop out all of the fat at the top. Put the fat in a bowl. (Save the liquid to use in smoothies!)
                Add vanilla and maple syrup to the bowl and beat with a hand mixer until cream reaches desired consistency. Store in refrigerator until ready to use.
                Top a biscuit (or two) with cream and sliced strawberries. Enjoy!
             </p>
            </div>

             <h4 class="nunito">
              <img alt="" src=""/>
              <b>
               Garlic &amp; Red Pepper Flake Shrimp
              </b>
             </h4>
             <p>
              <span class="bdBlue">
               4 Proteins
              </span>
              <span class="bdOrange">
               1 Fats
              </span>
              <span class="bdGreen">
               0 Carbs
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Prep Time:
              </b>
              5 minutes
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Cook Time:
              </b>
              7-10 minutes
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Servings:
              </b>
              4
             </p>
             <p>
              This goes fast...... Have all your ingredients measured out and ready to go! Ready, Set, AMAZING!
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="large-12 medium-12 small-12 columns padding-top05">
             <p>
              <b>
               Ingredients
              </b>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
                Tbsp coconut oil
              </li>
              <li>
               1 lb. shrimp (16-20 count)  peeled, deveined and patted dry
              </li>
              <li>
               Pinch of sea salt to taste
              </li>
              <li>
               6 cloves garlic, minced finely
              </li>
              <li>
               3 Tbsp lemon juice
              </li>
              <li>
               1/3 cup chopped parsley, divided
              </li>
              <li>
               2 Tbsp cold butter, cut in 4 pieces
              </li>
              <li>
               Water as needed
              </li>
              <li>
                tsp red pepper flakes
              </li>
              <li>
               6 large Romaine lettuce leaves torn (optional)
              </li>
              <li>
               Sprinkle of parmesan cheese (optional)
              </li>
             </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="large-12 medium-12 small-12 columns padding-top05">
             <p>
              <b>
               Directions
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              Have your ingredients measured and ready, theres no time to do this between steps. Heat coconut oil in a skillet on high. Once the oil starts smoking, add the shrimp and salt while stirring continuously. When the shrimp have started changing color, add the garlic and continue to stir. Add lemon juice, half of the parsley and butter and turn heat down to simmer. As the sauce thickens, pull the shrimp out and serve over a bed of Romaine lettuce, or by itself. Add water as needed to thin the sauce and pour over the shrimp. Top it with the remaining parsley, red pepper flakes and parmesan cheese.
             </p>
