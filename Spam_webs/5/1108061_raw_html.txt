        <div class="enlarge_html">
         <div class="image_data">
          <p class="caption">
           MGMT's sophomore release is adventurous and bold, featuring nine psychedelic, space-inspired tracks.
          </p>
          <span class="credit">
           Courtesy of the artist
          </span>
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=88212719">
         MGMT
        </a>
        band members Andrew VanWyngarden and Ben Goldwasser have dubbed their second album,
        <em>
         Congratulations
        </em>
        , a "no singles" album.
       </p>
       <p>
        They will not release or promote any one song from the record. Instead, they encourage both listeners and radio stations to play the songs that jump out at them. It's a bold idea and a counterintuitive one, considering the band rose to fame on the popularity of "Kids" and "Time To Pretend" from their debut,
        <em>
         Oracular Spectacular
        </em>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Congratulations
        </em>
        is musically adventurous, featuring nine psychedelic, space-inspired tracks. The space-rock aesthetic was no doubt influenced by producer and experimental musician Peter Kember, formerly of Spacemen 3. VanWyngarden also says the album was influenced by surf rock.
       </p>
       <p>
        Though
        <em>
         Congratulations
        </em>
        has no singles, several songs stand out. "I Found A Whistle" boasts a chorus that begs for a sing-along. It's an anthem  though for what, I'm not sure. It's often difficult to make out what Van Wyngarden and Goldwasser are saying, but it works on a record where the vocals are primarily a vehicle for melody.
       </p>
       <p>
        Track six, "Siberian Breaks," is perhaps the strangest and most interesting song on the album. Clocking in at over 12 minutes, the band schizophrenically jumps from one mood to another: One moment, it's '70s AM Gold and the next, it's an instrumental soundtrack to time travel.
       </p>
       <p>
        There's a chance that fans looking for more fun, electro-rock singles from MGMT won't find what they're looking for in the band's sophomore release. But
        <em>
         Congratulations
        </em>
         available here for streaming in its entirety until its release on April 13  turns out to be a more rewarding album to digest.
       </p>
       <p>
        Please leave your thoughts on
        <em>
         Congratulations
        </em>
        in the comments section below.
       </p>
