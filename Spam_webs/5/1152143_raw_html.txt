        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          The Tasmanian Museum &amp; Art Gallery is the second oldest museum in Australia and its collection mandate is the most diverse of any in the country. Its campus at Constitution Dock on Hobarts waterfront includes a rich collection of heritage buildings including the Commissariat Store (1808-10), the Private Secretarys Cottage (1815) and Custom House (1902).
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-03.jpg">
           <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79905" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-03.jpg?resize=474%2C711"/>
          </a>
          This masterplan project will expand the museum through new additions and adaptive reuse as well as extend the public open space and museum forecourt. The redevelopment will be the single largest and most significant cultural development ever undertaken in Tasmania.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-02.jpg">
           <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79904" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-02.jpg?resize=474%2C316"/>
          </a>
          The scheme interweaves heritage buildings, new contemporary elements and archaeology to create a rich and unique experience for visitors. A series of new elements have been integrated into the existing heritage fabric to create a coherent museum complex. A new louvred and glass roof is suspended on fine timber columns to enclose the central courtyard.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-04.jpg">
           <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79906" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-04.jpg?resize=474%2C316"/>
          </a>
          The intentional randomness of the columns within the courtyard allude to the trees of the original waterfront landscape while the multi-layered timber louvre, steel and glazed roof reinterprets the tree canopies and provide environmental control of the courtyard space underneath.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-05.jpg">
           <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79907" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-05.jpg?resize=474%2C318"/>
          </a>
          The operable curved timber louvres on the underside of the roof enable natural light to be manipulated at different times of the day and throughout the year, transforming the character of light and shade in the courtyard.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-06.jpg">
           <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79894" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-06.jpg?resize=474%2C316"/>
          </a>
          Two new box-like timber volumes are carefully placed in relation to the heritage elements. The first frames the new entrance into the courtyard with a caf underneath the suspended volume and a gallery/lecture space above.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-07.jpg">
           <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79895" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-07.jpg?resize=474%2C316"/>
          </a>
          The second box is locked into position between the Private Secretarys Cottage courtyard and street-front opening out to Sullivans Cove and connects the extensive new gallery spaces with the public waterfront.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-08.jpg">
           <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79896" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-08.jpg?resize=474%2C316"/>
          </a>
          These suspended timber forms are akin to fine jewellery boxes protecting their contents, and along one side a series of spiral wood louvres behind glass rotate to reveal Hobarts waterfront.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-09.jpg">
           <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79897" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-09.jpg?resize=474%2C711"/>
          </a>
          Two new courtyards are formed by this juxtaposition of new and existing elements: the Central Gallery with its glazed lantern roof and the Private Secretarys Cottage courtyard. Both atrium spaces improve orientation and circulation for both the new galleries and heritage galleries.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-10.jpg">
           <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79898" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-10.jpg?resize=474%2C316"/>
          </a>
          Finally, a new public open space is created that also forms a forecourt to the new Watergate museum entrance. The ground plane of this new public square has been delineated by a series of folded strips that have been generated from the existing built fabric geometry. The topography of the site both artificial and natural is transformed to provide a dynamic civic square that offers a range of opportunities and scales for intimate and large gatherings.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="JUSTIFY" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <a href="http://i0.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-12.jpg">
            <img alt="Tasmanian Museum" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-79900" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/aasarchitecture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Tasmanian-Museum-and-Art-Gallery-by-FJMT-12.jpg?resize=474%2C711"/>
           </a>
           Location:
          </strong>
          Hobart,
          <a href="http://aasarchitecture.com/category/australia" target="_blank">
           Australia
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
