        <div class="body " data-context="1472747" data-friendly-name="Article Inline Link Tracking" data-max-times-to-track-links="5" data-widget="tm.omniture.tracking" itemprop="articleBody">
         <figure class="inline-image clearfix portrait-right">
          <img alt="Lee Kildare" class="captify" height="300" src="http://i3.chroniclelive.co.uk/incoming/article1388953.ece/BINARY/59C3EF01-A118-F164-26EDD8FF039D53F8.jpg" title="" width="200"/>
         </figure>
         <p>
          HE STARTED a mini-crimewave by going where ordinary crooks cant go.
         </p>
         <p>
          But dwarf Lee Kildare was caught short when he raided a Tyneside hospital to steal hundreds of pounds worth of sweets.
         </p>
         <p>
          We told how the 3ft 11in crook, of Southern Road, Walker, has become a hit with Tyneside burglary gangs, who recruit him to slip through tiny nooks and crannies and plunder swag.
         </p>
         <p>
          Kildare, 22, was convicted of burgling a derelict council house in Newcastles East End, which he had entered through a tiny hole in the front door.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now we can reveal just days after he was put on curfew for that offence, he was in the dock again at Newcastle Magistrates Court. This time, the bench heard how he broke into the Conservatory Cafe at the citys General Hospital by climbing through a smashed window.
         </p>
         <p>
          He was then spotted at a bus stop with a bin liner crammed with sweets.
         </p>
         <p>
          Kildare has now had his curfew extended.
         </p>
         <p>
          Kildare has bragged how he has raided houses for years. He said: People do come to me to and ask me to help them on burglaries and that is because I am small and can get through holes but it doesnt mean they are taking advantage  everything I do is my own choice.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is something I have been doing for years but I always burgle derelict houses for metal that I can sell on as scrap. It is something I have to do because I cant live on the money I get and cant get a normal job like other people.
         </p>
         <p>
          I do get incapacity benefit but I have never worked so I burgle for extra money. I can normally get away with it and I have even shoplifted in the past because no-one would suspect me.
         </p>
         <p>
          Kildares latest court appearance came just days after he was convicted of non-dwelling house burglary for a raid on a former council house on Bernard Street, Walker.
         </p>
         <p>
          That offence had come just hours after he had been convicted of a similar burglary, for which he got a 12-month conditional discharge.
         </p>
         <p>
          In court for burgling the hospital, the pint-sized prowler blamed his offending on booze, saying his condition means he is unable to handle his drink. Michael Crowe, defending, said: He says at the time he was very drunk. He can quite quickly get drunk and not always does he remember what he has done. He thinks he went to the hospital for treatment then committed this offence.
         </p>
         <p>
          Kildare had been given a 12-month community order and was placed on curfew for three months after the house raid. But when details of the hospital burglary, which dated back to July last year, came to light, magistrates extended his curfew to four months, between 8pm and 7am.
         </p>
