      <h1 class="title" itemprop="name">
       Bullish options on fire in Bank of America
      </h1>
      <div class="source" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
       <a href="/pete-najarian/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
        Pete Najarian

       Friday, 19 Jul 2013 |  5:38  AM ET
      </time>
      <span class="sourcelogo" id="sourcelogo-cnbccom" itemprop="sourceOrganization">

           <meta content="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2013/06/24/100838585-100838585-20130622-0730-171rr.jpg?v=1374010491" itemprop="image"/>
           <meta content="width='530'" itemprop="width"/>
           <meta content="height='298'" itemprop="height"/>

          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            Upside option activity in
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="10623" data-inline-quote-symbol="BAC" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/BAC" target="_self">
             Bank of America
            </a>
            was on fire again yesterday.
           </p>
           <p>
            The October 15
            <a href="/id/42901969" target="_self">
             calls
            </a>
            really stood out, with most of the large prints purchased for $0.50 to $0.65 as almost  12,000 contracts changed hands, according to OptionMonster's real-time tracking systems. There was also heavy buying in the Weekly 15 calls that expire next Friday, which saw more than 20,000 contracts go for $0.04 to $0.14.
           </p>
           <p>
            These calls lock in the price where shares can be purchased no matter how far the stock might rise, potentially profiting from a move higher for much less cost than buying shares outright. The leverage can be extreme if traders time the rally correctly, but the contracts can end up worthless if the shares fail to gain enough by their expiration dates.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container last">

            <p>
             Gabelli's  Favorite Bank Stocks

            <p>
             Mario Gabelli, Gabelli Funds CEO, provides his play on the banking sector, including  trades Legg Mason, PNC and M&amp;T Bank.
            </p>
           </iframe>

          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            Bank of America rose 3.14 percent to $14.76 yesterday, closing near the peaks of the session. Boosted by a strong earnings report earlier in the week, the stock is now back to its highest levels since early 2011.
           </p>
           <p>
            Total option volume in the name reached 885,400 yesterday, four times its daily average, with 711,000 calls versus 174,000
            <a href="/id/43253227" target="_self">
             puts
            </a>
            . More than 1 million contracts turned over in the previous session.
           </p>
