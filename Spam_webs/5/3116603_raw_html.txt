         <a href="http://tastesbetterfromscratch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fresh_Squeezed_Lemonade11.jpg">

         Were approaching mythird summer living inSaint Louis but Im still getting used to the humidity. I get all ready in the morningshowered and hair doneand the second I step outside of the house Im sweating! Its not even super hot here yet, but the humidity is killer.

        <p>
         <a href="http://tastesbetterfromscratch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fresh_Squeezed_Lemonade2.jpg">
          <img alt="Fresh Squeezed Lemonade on TastesBetterFromScratch.com" class="alignnone wp-image-4520 size-full" height="1350" src="http://tastesbetterfromscratch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fresh_Squeezed_Lemonade2.jpg" width="900"/>
         </a>
         Fresh cool drinks save my this time of year. Theres seriously nothing more refreshing than a big glass of cold, fresh squeezed lemonade. This easy recipes has a little bit of fresh mint infused in it as well, and its absolutely delicious! Perfect for serving at summer parties too.
        </p>

         <div class="blog-yumprint-recipe-contents">
          <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="0">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Ingredients
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             1 3/4 cups sugar
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             1 cup fresh lemon juice (about 5-6 large lemons)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             1/4 cup fresh lime juice (1-2 limes)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
             1/2 cup loosely packed fresh mint leaves
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
             8 -10 cups water, divided
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
             Ice cubes
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="1">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Instructions
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
             Combine 2 cups water and sugar in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Stir until sugar has dissolved and simmer for 5 minutes. Remove from heat and cool to room temperature. Add the sugar syrup and fresh lemon and lime juices to a large pitcher and stir well. Add the mint leaves, stir, and allow to stand for 30 minutes. Remove the mint leaves. Refrigerate until cold. When ready to serve, add 6 cups of cold water and stir. Taste, and add more water if it's still too sweet, until you get it to your perfect sweetness.
            </li>
           </ol>
