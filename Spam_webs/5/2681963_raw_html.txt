     <div class="td-template4-header ">
      <div class="td-header-wrap">
       <div class="td-header-grid">
        <header>
         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
          Parts Unknown: Behind the Scenes of the Roman Colosseum
         </h1>
         <div class="meta-info">
          <ul class="td-category">
           <li class="entry-category">
            <a href="http://www.culturalxplorer.com/destinations/europe/italy/">
             Italy
            </a>
           </li>
           <li class="entry-category">
            <a href="http://www.culturalxplorer.com/destinations/europe/italy/rome/">
             Rome
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <time class="entry-date updated" datetime="2014-12-24T09:00:56+00:00" itemprop="dateCreated">
           Dec 24, 2014
          </time>
          <meta content="UserComments:6" itemprop="interactionCount"/>
          <div class="entry-comments-views">
          </div>
         </div>
        </header>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="container td-page-wrap">
      <div class="row">
       <div class="span12">
        <div class="td-grid-wrap">
         <div class="container-fluid">
          <div class="row-fluid ">
           <div class="span8 column_container td-post-content" itemprop="mainContentOfPage" role="main">
            <div class="td-post-text-content">
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="1176" src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-rqvFC9JKADs/VGFW6J8LaJI/AAAAAAAADBo/-tNHis01Frc/w1764-h1176-no/Roman%2BColosseum%2B%7C%2BCultural%2BXplorer.JPG" width="1764"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               Did you know that the Colosseum is the most famous attraction in all of Rome, with nearly 5 million people visitingevery year?
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              During my trip to Rome
              <a href="http://www.culturalxplorer.com/2014-euro-trip-itinerary/" title="My Euro-Trip Itinerary: Summer 2014">
               on my 2014 Euro-Trip
              </a>
              , I got a chance to go behind the scenes of the Roman Colosseum and had the chance to areas that were off-limits to the average visitor with the company
              <a href="http://www.walksofitaly.com/" target="_blank">
               Walks of Italy
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h4>
              <span style="color: #000000;">
               <strong>
                Dressed to Kill: A Brief History of the Colosseum
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h4>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="1176" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-2jcUNYTlEfI/VGFXwyRXcRI/AAAAAAAADDE/LJGivw-Uu4g/w1764-h1176-no/Walks%2Bof%2BItaly%2BTour%2BGuide%2BRoman%2BColosseum.JPG" width="1764"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Built in the outskirts of Rome in A.D.70-72 by Emperor Vespasian of the Flavian dynasty, the Colosseum (formerly known as the Flavian Amphitheater) was originally constructed as a gift to the Roman people.
             </p>
             <p>
              The amphitheater was built for citizens of Rome to gather and watch brutal and fiercegladiator games. In the four centuries that the amphitheater was actively used, thousands of mens and animals foughtfor their lives, while eager onlookers cheered.
             </p>
             <p>
              Today, the Colosseum is a symbol of the former powerof the Roman empire as well as a place that is remembered for being the site where many lives were lost.
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h4>
              <span style="color: #000000;">
               <strong>
                A Behind the Scenes Look at the Colosseum
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h4>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="1176" src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/-gTYg5-vYqFc/VGFWycouxPI/AAAAAAAADBg/Zs74BpAdUcc/w1764-h1176-no/Walks%2Bof%2BItaly%2BRoman%2BColosseum%2B2.JPG" width="1764"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Once my group collected our tickets outside of the Colosseum gate, we breezed past the line of people waiting to enter the building. As I walked past the line ofpeople thatseemed to stretch on forever, I was very thankful that I had chosen to takea guided tour.
             </p>
             <p>
              Once we had our tickets scanned, my group was led away from the general visitors and into a roped off area mannedby a security guard standing in front of a large gate. Our group was then led through the gate before it was closed and locked behind us.
             </p>
             <p>
              We were told a little more about the history of the Colosseum before we were led through an open doorway and onto the stage of the Colosseum overlooking the arena. It was an amazing experience standing on the stage with a small handful of people looking out at an ancient masterpiece.
             </p>
             <p>
              After departing from the stage, we went downstairs to the basement of the Colosseum, a place that is strictly off limits to those who are not on a behind the scenes tour.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="1176" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-7ac8nxMmRFM/VGFXkcxb_nI/AAAAAAAADCs/4QUZf5PMfPY/w1764-h1176-no/Walks%2Bof%2BItaly%2BRoman%2BColosseum%2B4.JPG" width="1764"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              The chambers and tunnels in the basement of the Colosseum were created to hold the wild beasts and gladiators before they fought to the death. When it was time for the show to begin, the victims wereled outinto the mazes of the Colosseum floor where the battle for their lives begun.
             </p>
             <p>
              While thinking about what happened down in those mazes made me shutter a little bit, it was still quite interesting to see in person.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="1176" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/zosIPk50Z1ksLsChARHZC-D-UMYou-4OmMe6KrPHsT4=w1764-h1176-no" width="1764"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="1176" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-D_yxfcZoeVo/VGFW61g79zI/AAAAAAAADBw/jCekqyA9Wgs/w1568-h1176-no/Walks%2Bof%2BItaly%2BRoman%2BColosseum%2B1.JPG" width="1568"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              After leaving the basementof the Colosseum, our group was led up to the very top part of the building, overlooking the entire Colosseum and the surrounding city.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <center>
              <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="377" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/n7FkKYuYZiI" width="670">
              </iframe>
             </center>
             <hr/>
             <h4>
              <span style="color: #000000;">
               <strong>
                An Added Bonus: Palatine Hill &amp; The Roman Forum
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h4>
             <p>
              As part of the admission price of the Colosseum, visitors also have access to the nearby Palatine Hill and the Roman Forum.
             </p>
             <p>
              After leaving the Colosseum, my group headed up to the famed
              <a href="http://www.turismoroma.it/cosa-fare/palatino?lang=en" target="_blank">
               Palatine Hill
              </a>
              , which is known for being both where Rome began and the birthplace of the mythical twin brothers Romulus and Remus, the founders of the cityof Rome.
             </p>
             <p>
              Making our wayaround the hill, we came upon the beautiful
              <a href="http://www.turismoroma.it/cosa-fare/fori?lang=en" target="_blank">
               Roman Forum
              </a>
              . The Forum once served as the citys administrative and monumental centerbefore itbecame the epicenter of public life within Rome.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="1176" src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-r2gZEgiq9lc/VGFXUbA_yuI/AAAAAAAADCU/JtnHV0NIx8E/w1568-h1176-no/Walks%2Bof%2BItaly%2BRoman%2BForum.JPG" width="1568"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="1176" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-ELyr5R8-T7I/VGFXxJc0hGI/AAAAAAAADDA/Tw2Im4PuhcU/w1568-h1176-no/Walks%2Bof%2BItaly%2BTour%2BGuide%2BRoman%2BForum.JPG" width="1568"/>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h4>
              <strong>
               Tour Review
              </strong>
             </h4>
             <p>
              I thought the tour was very informative and myguide was extremely knowledgable. I am very glad that I chose to take a guided tour through the Colosseum and in the Roman Forum, as there was not much signage telling me what I was looking at.
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h4>
              Know Before You Go  Helpful Tips for Visiting the Colosseum
             </h4>
             <ul>
              <li>
               On the first Sunday of each month, access to the Colosseum is free
              </li>
              <li>
               Certain areas (such as under the Colosseum and the stage area) are only open to people on specific booked tours
              </li>
              <li>
               Wear comfortable shoes and be prepared to do a lot of climbing and walking: up the steep stairs of the Colosseum, up Palatine Hill, and around the Roman Forum
              </li>
             </ul>

              <strong>
               Booking thisTour with Walks of Italy

             <p>
              You too can go behind the scenes of the Roman Colosseum by booking the

               VIP Access: Colosseum Underground &amp; Ancient Rome
              </a>
               tour with Walks of Italy! Tickets start at89 for a 3.5 hour tour that includesthe Colosseum,the Roman Forum, and Palatine Hill.
             </p>
