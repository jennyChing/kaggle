          <p>
           <img alt="Jakub Rozalski 2" class="size-full wp-image-6451 alignleft" height="179" src="//inktank.fi/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Jakub-Rozalski-2.png" width="175"/>
           Enjoy history? Get a kick out of science fiction?If so, then youll love the digital artwork by
           <a href="http://www.artstation.com/artist/jakubrozalski">
            Jakub Rozalski
           </a>
           , a concept artist and illustrator based in Hamburg, Germany. He incorporates fantasy and sci-fi elements such as mechs, werewolves, and even Predator into historic and picturesque landscapes, resulting in artwork thats both awe-inspiring and entertaining.
          </p>
          <p>
           Jakubs latest project 1920+ features giant, menacing mechs (and occasionally
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wojtek_(bear)">
            Wojtek
           </a>
           , Polish bear/soldier of WWII) lurking around early 20th century Polish villages  a concept that may sound odd to some. But the series has just the right balance of weirdness, anachronism, and talent to really pull it off. And what good is concept art if its not a little weird? Check out a few of Jakubs stunning artworks from the 1920+ project below and see for yourself!
