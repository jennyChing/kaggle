     <p class="subtitle">
      Bullfight Images, 1901
      <br/>
      <span class="body">
       To better understand the composition that is beneath 
        the visible one, an
       <a href="xradio.htm" onclick="NewWindow(this.href,'new','320','280','no');return false;">
        x-radiograph
       </a>
       was taken of the painting. When viewed horizontally, the x-radiograph 
        shows an elongated, tortured horse at the lower right-hand corner. In 
        addition to the horse, one can identify the outline of the
       <a href="animation/animation1.htm" onclick="NewWindow(this.href,'new','320','230','no');return false;">
        bullring 
        and the arches
       </a>
       of the stadium.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="body">
      The image revealed by the x-radiograph is clearly related 
        to Picassos 1901 bullfight scenes, such as those shown here. Along 
        with the emergence of a bright color palette beneath the surface blue 
        of
      <i>
       The Tragedy,
      </i>
      there is ample evidence to suggest that he painted 
        a large bullfight composition on this wooden support.
     </p>
