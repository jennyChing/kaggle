      <h2 class="title">
       Milford Haven
      </h2>
      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        <a href="http://miss-chips.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_0052b.jpg">
         <img alt="Milford Haven anchorage" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-1675" height="150" src="http://miss-chips.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_0052b-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         051 42.0338 N 005 09.2131 W
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Castle Beach Bay is tucked just inside the entrance of Milford Haven and offers excellent shelter from northerly winds. We came around St. Davids head from the North with winds building behind us. Having tried the bigger anchorage around the corner at Dale Yacht club  we found this way too exposed  the tiny Castle Beach Bay provided almost perfect shelter. Once tucked inside, there was hardly any wind at all.
       </p>
       <p>
        We thought we were going to be lucky and have the bay to ourselves but a small yacht that we had past on the way down anchored up close to us a couple of hours later. They were gone in the morning by first light and we saw them a couple of days later in Padstow. The chart shows a mooring bouy  this is used by Pilot and Police boats at all times of the day and night, so expect some wash occasionally as they come in and out.
       </p>
       <p>
        From here we did an overnight sail across the Bristol Channel to get to Padstow.
       </p>
