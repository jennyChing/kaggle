       <h2>
        Deadline for submissions: 31st July 2015
       </h2>
      </div>
      <div class="col text_col half">
       <p>
        Designed to have meaningful impact on the career of one emerging artist, the
        <strong>
         Griffin Art Prize
        </strong>
        is an annual residency and exhibition award for UK based artists in painting and drawing. It provides the time, space, materials and support to develop work for a one-person exhibition.
       </p>
       <p>
        The
        <strong>
         Griffin Art Prize
        </strong>
        winner has unique access to a large studio in theStudio Building, 21, Evesham Street, London W11. Materials of choice are provided by the worlds leading fine art brands,
        <strong>
         Winsor &amp; Newton
        </strong>
        ,
        <strong>
         Liquitex
        </strong>
        and
        <strong>
         Cont  Paris
        </strong>
        . The six month residency culminates in an exhibition with catalogue in
        <strong>
         Griffin Gallery,
        </strong>
        located on the ground floor of the
        <strong>
         Studio Building
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        20 artists selected for the long list of the
        <strong>
         Griffin Art Prize2015
        </strong>
        will have their work showcased onthis website.
       </p>
       <p>
        A showcase exhibition of 10 short-listed artists will be held at
        <strong>
         Griffin Gallery;
        </strong>
        from this the winner of
        <strong>
         Griffin Art Prize2015
        </strong>
        will be selected by the judges and announced at the private view on
        <strong>
         18
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         November 2015.
        </strong>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="col text_col half">
       <p>
        The judges look for innovation in painting and drawing, commitment to practise and for the potential to benefit from the residency.
       </p>
       <p>
        Each stage of the
        <strong>
         Griffin Art Prize
        </strong>
        provides a platform for professional exposure. To make the long list is a considerable achievement; the short-list exhibition is a popular and well attended event that attracts collectors, critics, art world professionals and the general public. The winning artists solo exhibition at
        <strong>
         Griffin Gallery
        </strong>
        is a high-profile showcase accompanied by a catalogue.
       </p>
      </div>
