       </a>
      </h1>
      <p class="byline">
       by
       <span class="author vcard">
        <a class="url fn n" href="http://whippedstyle.com/author/casie/" title="Casie Terry">

      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        Ive been throwing such a fit lately because Ive really wanted to dive into more DIYs using fun fabrics but.. I dont own a sewing machine. I also dont know the first thing about selecting a sewing machinewhich is an even bigger problem. I keep meaning to research the matter so that I can head out and buy a machine with confidence but, who am I kidding? I have no time!!! Researching sewing machines somehow always lands last on my ginormous list of things to do. But it occurred to me this morning that I have a forum on which I can ask my super DIY savvy audience and Im sure
        <em>
         someone
        </em>
        would have some advice!! Duh. So lay it on me!!!! Any must-have machine features? Any tools or tips to share? My objectives for said machine would be rather elementary: tailoring clothes, making pillows, possibly skirts, etc.
        <br/>
        HELP!!!!
       </p>

          <p>
           I sew every day and would be lost without a sewing machine. Alas, I have just busted the clutch on mine.again! My neighbor has a quilting business and is a machine tech and didnt understand how someone could so abuse a machine. I guess this time it means I need a better more industrial machine.
           <br/>
           As a person whos now been sewing for many years (almost 50) I would advise you to get a second-hand machine for your fist one. And if you can find one that is all metal (it will be heavy) go for it. They are sturdy machines and will do all of what you will need until you get comfortable with a machine enough to want more. THEN, you can shop with confidence because you will know what you want and what you want it for. I think you could be wasting your money starting off with a new machine. And who knows, you might just fall in love with the old first one and decide that its plenty enough for your needs.
           <br/>
           Personally, I use the straight stitch, the stretch stitch, the zig-zag, the hem stitch and the buttonholer. My ideal machine would probably be a Husqvarna because it has a variable speed motor (for heavy duty sewing) and is programmable. But thats like the person driving the VW  but wants to one day drive a Bentley! One can dream.
           <br/>
           Good luck with whatever decision you make. I hope you enjoy sewing as much as I do!
           <br/>
           Sincerely,
           <br/>
           Suzanne from NB, Canada
