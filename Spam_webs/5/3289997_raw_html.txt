         <p>
          <img alt="DoSplash-Top-Publisher-Interview" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-310" height="600" src="http://www.dosplash.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/DoSplash-Top-Publisher-Interview.jpg" width="590"/>

         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Ithought it would be great to introduce some of the top players of DoSplash to the community and the whole internet. So I have started this interview series which will feature the top publishers and top submitters of DoSplash.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          This week I have Erik who is one of the first few members who joined DoSplash when it was started and when the site still had some nastyissues. I must say Eriks support has been great so far.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          He not only joined the community in its early days, but stuck with us so far. He did not just join to see whats new here and then leave. He has been an active member and also has helped us very patiently in fixing some of the bugs.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Without further delay, I will let Erik speak his words
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.blog.dosplash.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <span lang="IT">
             Hey Erik, please introduce yourself to the DoSplash Community
            </span>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           Hello DoSplash Community!
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           I am Erik and I live in a fantastic town close to Garda Lake, in North of Italy.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           I worked as Export Manager during 8 years for three distinct companies, I traveled a lot, visiting many countries.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           Since more than 4 years, I started blogging for passion, which turned now into a steady income online.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <span lang="IT">
             You have bought nopassiveincome.com instead of starting it from scratch. Can you please tell us about it?
            </span>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="EN-US">
           The founder and previous owner of
           <span class="apple-converted-space">
           </span>
           <a href="http://nopassiveincome.com/" target="_blank">
            NoPassiveIncome blog
           </a>
           <span class="apple-converted-space">
           </span>
           was Chris, a friend of mine.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="EN-US">
           We met online and shared our experiences during his online adventure.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="EN-US">
           Ending 2012, he mentioned about a great chance of working offline, and the impossibility of continuing to run the blog.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="EN-US">
           So he offered me his site.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="EN-US">
           It was great, because he did an amazing work of promotion and content writing during his blogging career.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <span lang="IT">
             What other blogs/sites/projects do you run apart from nopassiveincome.com. In general let us know about your online presence.
            </span>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          My first blog was
          <a href="http://www.freemakemoneyadvice.com/" target="_blank">
           Free MAKE MONEY Advice
          </a>
           : my first joys and sorrows. Imade some huge errors at the beginning of my blogging journey. Ive tried to recover it, but Big G updates hit very hard.
         </p>
         <p>
          Then I own
          <a href="http://www.tokyoezine.com/" target="_blank">
           TOKYO ezine
          </a>
          , aboutTokyo and Japan, with guide, trends and travel information. It hasa steady niche and targeted traffic.
         </p>
         <p>
          Another blog is
          <a href="http://www.fillmymoneybox.com/" target="_blank">
           Fill My Money Box
          </a>
          . Its about Finance, Business and Making Money Online. I monetized it in many different ways and I still receive decent monthly income.
         </p>
         <p>
          I run also 
          <a href="http://www.howtoquitwow.com/" target="_blank">
           How to Quit WoW
          </a>
          , a website built in order to sell my eBook How to Quit World of Warcraft.
         </p>
         <div class="content-box-greenbox">
          <p>
           My latest online startups:
          </p>
          <p>
            
           <a href="http://wikibabylon.com/" target="_blank">
            Wiki Babylon
           </a>
           , an history site;
          </p>
          <p>
            
           <a href="http://bizsmallbiz.com/" target="_blank">
            Biz Small Biz
           </a>
           ,a blog about small business trends, tips, ideas;
          </p>
          <p>
            
           <a href="http://smartmarketerz.com/" target="_blank">
            Smart Marketerz
           </a>
           , a site for everything about marketing;
          </p>
          <p>
            
           <a href="http://travelntips.com/" target="_blank">
            Travel n Tips
           </a>
           , my travel niche blog.
          </p>
          <p>
           I also own a blogging community, called 
           <a href="http://www.klinkk.com/" target="_blank">
            Klinkk
           </a>
           .
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <span lang="IT">
             Can you tell us a little bit about your personal life? (just so readers can get to know more about you!)
            </span>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           I am 35, engaged, with many interests.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           First, I love to travel. I had the luck to visit many countries with my previous job, but Ive gladly spent money for travel for pleasure to many destinations all around the world.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           Ihave fixed in my mind the skyscrapers of Dubai, the never ending fun in Las Vegas, the fantastic sunset of Santorini, the colored market in Barcelona or the amazing beaches in Venezuela.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           I could go on and on again : this is what I love of traveling, I do not need any picture or souvenir, the best memories are recorded forever in my mind.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           And another big part of my life : Ive always been fascinated by extreme sports.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           I did b
          </span>
          <span lang="IT">
           ungee jumping, canyoning, rafting, free-climbing, diving, snowboarding, kick Boxing. And I am now learning how to skydive (actually just ended the official training last weekend).
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <span lang="IT">
             How were the initial few months of your online startup?
            </span>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           Especiallyat the beginning, you need to build your name or brand. I mean an online reputation.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           People are less likely to visit your blog or trust you, if they do not know you.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           So I strongly suggest new bloggers to start their online adventures while they still have a full time job, or else another steady income.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           Because you will need several months, or even years (depending on the time you invest in your online startups), before building a consistent source of income online.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <span lang="IT">
             What are your major income sources?
            </span>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           I mainly make money online from selling ads on my blogs.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           But I have different income sources, as sponsored posts and reviews, affiliate products and some sales from my eBooks.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <span lang="IT">
             How did you hear about DoSplash?
            </span>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           I remember the first time I heard about DoSplash.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           It was a share from Ileane Smith on her Google+ profile(
           <em>
            Janes note: Thank you Ileane!)
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           I was happy to cheer Jane, wishing her a great success. (
           <em>
            Janes note: Thank you Erik, that means a lot to me
            <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.blog.dosplash.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
           </em>
           )
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <span lang="IT">
             What are your views on blogging communities?
            </span>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           I prefer to point up the benefits in terms of relationships you can receive from being active in blogging communities, instead to mention any SEO benefits of link building.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           Ive met several new friends just for using the most popular blogging communities. And friendships are forever!
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span lang="IT">
           Also, you can get targeted traffic for sharing your content, as you share the same interests of the other users.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <span lang="IT">
             Briefly tell the DoSplash community 5 blogging tips that you think are most important.
            </span>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           You should pick a topic for your blog, you are always inspiredto write about. People will see the passion you devote into it.
          </li>
          <li>
           Its important to create relationships online with bloggers who have the same interests of you. You can learn so much from them!
          </li>
          <li>
           You need passion in what you do, or you will lose interest and persistance in future.
          </li>
          <li>
           Remember constancy. As for anything else, your online success wont be overnight.
          </li>
          <li>
           And one big tip for every of your business : DO NOT PUT ALL YOUR EGGS IN ONE BASKET. In other words, you should create more than one blog, with different sources of income and diversify your online activities. You never know if the near Google update willhitone of your blogs or if someday you will lose your main source of income.
          </li>
         </ol>

          <em>

             What do you think about DoSplash? Any suggestions for improvement?
            </span>
           </strong>
