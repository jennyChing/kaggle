       <p>
        <strong>
         Its hard to believe that it has been just about two months since the tragic death of
         <a href="//blog.dashburst.com/pic/robin-williams-web-comic/" title="Robin Williams: A Spark of Madness, A Web Comic Depicting Williams Life as a Video Game">
          Robin Williams
         </a>
         .
        </strong>
        Still, people all over the world continue to pay tribute to the great actor and comedian. Twenty-six-year-old animator, pixel artist, illustrator and game maker
        <a href="http://pug-of-war.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
         Ivan Dixon
        </a>
        has created a fabulous looping GIF in a wonderful tribute to Williams.
       </p>
       <p>
        The GIF shows Williams transforming into many of his
        <a href="//blog.dashburst.com/pic/robin-williams-life-lessons/" title="Life Lessons from Robin Williams and His Movie Characters">
         iconic movie roles
        </a>
        from the 1980s and 90s. The loop starts with Williams character from
        <em>
         Jumanji
        </em>
        (1995), then turns into The Genie in Disneys
        <em>
         <a href="//blog.dashburst.com/video/broadway-aladdin-tribute-robin/" title="Broadways Aladdin Pays Tribute to Genie Robin Williams with Audience Sing-Along [VIDEO]">
          Aladdin
         </a>
        </em>
        (1992), Peter Pan in
        <em>
         Hook
        </em>
        (1991),
        <em>
         Mrs. Doubtfire
        </em>
        (1993), Popeye (1980) and back to Jumanji.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="https://cdn.burst.zone/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/tumblr_nbodskC5rQ1rtbl5vo1_400.gif">
         <img alt="tumblr_nbodskC5rQ1rtbl5vo1_400" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-86727" height="256" src="https://b2.burst.zone/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/tumblr_nbodskC5rQ1rtbl5vo1_400.gif" title="tumblr_nbodskC5rQ1rtbl5vo1_400" width="256"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        On his Tumblr, Dixon writes about the looping GIF:
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         A tribute to the man who pretty much represents childhood, Robin Williams.
