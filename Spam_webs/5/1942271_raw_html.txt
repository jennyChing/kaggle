     <p>
      <b>
       Living Colour
      </b>
      is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">
       rock
      </a>
      band from
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York City
      </a>
      , formed in 1984. Stylistically, the band's music is a creative fusion influenced by
      <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">
       heavy metal
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">
       funk
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">
       jazz
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">
       hip hop
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">
       alternative rock
      </a>
      . Their lyrics range from personal to political.
     </p>
     <p>
      Living Colour rose to fame with their debut album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Vivid_(Living_Colour_album)" title="Vivid (Living Colour album)">
        Vivid
       </a>
      </i>
      in 1988.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Great_Rock_Discography_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Great_Rock_Discography-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Although the band scored a number of hits, they are best remembered for their
      <a href="/wiki/Signature_song" title="Signature song">
       signature song
      </a>
      "
      <a href="/wiki/Cult_of_Personality_(song)" title="Cult of Personality (song)">
       Cult of Personality
      </a>
      ", which won a
      <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">
       Grammy Award
      </a>
      for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Hard_Rock_Performance" title="Best Hard Rock Performance">
       Best Hard Rock Performance
      </a>
      in 1990. They were also named Best New Artist at the 1989
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="MTV Video Music Awards">
       MTV Video Music Awards
      </a>
      . After disbanding in 1995,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Great_Rock_Discography_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Great_Rock_Discography-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Living Colour reunited in late 2000.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Living_Colour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_.281984.E2.80.931986.29">
       Early years (19841986)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Living_Colour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years (19841986)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      English-born guitarist
      <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Reid" title="Vernon Reid">
       Vernon Reid
      </a>
      had formed a number of bands, and after a few years, he formed Living Colour in New York in 1984. Reid assembled a number of bands under the name Living Colour from 1984 to 1986. They grew out of the
      <a href="/wiki/Black_Rock_Coalition" title="Black Rock Coalition">
       Black Rock Coalition
      </a>
      , a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-profit_organization" title="Non-profit organization">
       non-profit organization
      </a>
      founded by (among others) Reid for black musicians interested in playing rock music. Reid was well known on the downtown New York jazz scenes because of his tenure in
      <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Shannon_Jackson" title="Ronald Shannon Jackson">
       Ronald Shannon Jackson
      </a>
      's Decoding Society.
     </p>
     <p>
      Early band members included bassists Alex Mosely,
      <a href="/wiki/Jerome_Harris" title="Jerome Harris">
       Jerome Harris
      </a>
      and Carl James, drummers Greg Carter,
      <a href="/wiki/Pheeroan_akLaff" title="Pheeroan akLaff">
       Pheeroan akLaff
      </a>
      and J.T. Lewis, keyboardist
      <a href="/wiki/Geri_Allen" title="Geri Allen">
       Geri Allen
      </a>
      , and vocalists D.K. Dyson and
      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Ledford" title="Mark Ledford">
       Mark Ledford
      </a>
      , with Reid occasionally singing lead vocals himself. The band's sound was vastly different from the songs that showed up later on their major label recordings. Material from this period included instrumental jazz/funk workouts, politically pointed
      <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">
       punk rock
      </a>
      burners, experimental excursions via Reid's guitar synth, and an early version of the song "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funny_Vibe" title="Funny Vibe">
       Funny Vibe
      </a>
      ", which was reworked for their debut album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Vivid_(Living_Colour_album)" title="Vivid (Living Colour album)">
        Vivid
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mainstream_success_.281986.E2.80.931991.29">
       Mainstream success (19861991)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Living_Colour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mainstream success (19861991)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 1986 a stable lineup was formed, consisting of vocalist
      <a href="/wiki/Corey_Glover" title="Corey Glover">
       Corey Glover
      </a>
      , guitarist
      <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Reid" title="Vernon Reid">
       Vernon Reid
      </a>
      , bassist
      <a href="/wiki/Muzz_Skillings" title="Muzz Skillings">
       Muzz Skillings
      </a>
      , and drummer
      <a href="/wiki/Will_Calhoun" title="Will Calhoun">
       Will Calhoun
      </a>
      (who had graduated with honors from
      <a href="/wiki/Berklee_College_of_Music" title="Berklee College of Music">
       Berklee College of Music
      </a>
      ),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Great_Rock_Discography_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Great_Rock_Discography-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the band hired managers Jim Grant and Roger Cramer. Soon after, the band became experienced at touring, including performing regular gigs at the club
      <a href="/wiki/CBGB" title="CBGB">
       CBGB
      </a>
      .

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Second_line-up_and_breakup_.281992.E2.80.931995.29">
       Second line-up and breakup (19921995)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Living_Colour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Second line-up and breakup (19921995)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 1992, Skillings left the band due to creative differences and was replaced by
      <a href="/wiki/Doug_Wimbish" title="Doug Wimbish">
       Doug Wimbish
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Great_Rock_Discography_2-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Great_Rock_Discography-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This new line up released their third full-length album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Stain_(album)" title="Stain (album)">
        Stain
       </a>
      </i>
      , in February 1993. The album reached No.26 in the U.S., a further drop since their debut.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Great_Rock_Discography_2-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Great_Rock_Discography-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Despite retaining their strong fan base, Living Colour disbanded in January 1995, after failing to settle on a common musical goal during sessions for their fourth studio album.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Great_Rock_Discography_2-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Great_Rock_Discography-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Four of these tracks were included on the compilation
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pride_(Living_Colour_album)" title="Pride (Living Colour album)">
        Pride
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Following the breakup, individual band members released a variety of solo efforts.
     </p>

      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Collide%C3%B8scope" title="Collidescope">
        Collidescope
       </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">

        Rolling Stone
       </a>
      </i>
      have called the band "
      <a href="/wiki/Funk_metal" title="Funk metal">
       funk metal
      </a>
      pioneers."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In early 2009,
      <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">
       WWE
      </a>
      used "
      <a href="/wiki/Cult_of_Personality_(song)" title="Cult of Personality (song)">
       Cult of Personality
      </a>
      " in the video promoting the induction of
      <a href="/wiki/Stone_Cold_Steve_Austin" title="Stone Cold Steve Austin">
       Stone Cold Steve Austin
      </a>
      into its Hall of Fame. WWE performer
      <a href="/wiki/CM_Punk" title="CM Punk">
       CM Punk
      </a>
      used the song as his entrance theme in
      <a href="/wiki/Ring_of_Honor" title="Ring of Honor">
       Ring of Honor
      </a>
      for a period of time. CM Punk used other licensed music while working with WWE, but, after feigning a departure from the company in July 2011, he returned with "Cult of Personality" as his entrance theme.
     </p>
