        <h4 itemprop="name">
         London riots on CCTV: now the hunt begins
        </h4>
        <p itemprop="description">
         UK police release images of riot suspects taken with sophisticated, high-definition CCTV cameras.
        </p>
        <span class="gone" itemprop="duration">
         PT0M0S
        </span>
        <span class="gone" itemprop="embedUrl">
        </span>
        <span class="gone" itemprop="width">
         620
        </span>
        <span class="gone" itemprop="height">
         349
        </span>
        <ul>
         <li class="autoplay">
          Autoplay
          <strong>
           On
          </strong>
          <a data-autoplayoverlay="true" href="javascript:;">
           Off
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a data-kampylefeedback="true" href="javascript:;">
           Video feedback
          </a>
         </li>
         <li class="last">
          <a data-settingsoverlay="true" href="javascript:;">

      <!-- id:video-player-content -->
      <p>
       Just as quickly as it was blamed for fuelling the London riots, social media and other technologies are being harnessed to rally people for the clean-up effort and to identify looters.
      </p>
      <p>
       Despite police advice to remain in their homes, London residents began sweeping the city even as rioters continued to burn down buildings and loot shops.
      </p>
      <p>
       An
       <strong>
        <a href="http://twitter.com/#!/Riotcleanup" target="_blank">
         @Riotcleanup
        </a>
       </strong>
       Twitter page created by musician Sam Duckworth has more than 85,000 followers, and broadcasts clean-up locations, times and other information. Many are using the #riotcleanup hashtag, which was devised by artist and activist Dan Thompson, and the
       <strong>
        <a href="http://twitter.com/#!/search?q=%23broomsquad" target="_blank">
         #broomsquad
        </a>
       </strong>
       hashtag.
      </p>
      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Looters rampage through a convenience store in Hackney." src="/content/dam/images/1/i/j/n/f/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.1ilif.png/1312834617483.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Looters rampage through a convenience store in Hackney.
        <cite>
         <i>
          Photo: Reuters
         </i>
        </cite>
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       A similar Facebook page - "
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.facebook.com/londoncleanup" target="_blank">
         Post riot clean-up: let's help London
        </a>
       </strong>
       " - has more than 17,000 likes and there is also a dedicated website, riotcleanup.co.uk, and a
       <strong>
        <a href="http://wiki.crisiscommons.org/wiki/London_Riots#London_Riots_Cleanup" target="_blank">
         Wiki page
        </a>
       </strong>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="ad adAlignRight" id="adspot-300x250-pos3" style="display:none;">
       <small>
        Advertisement
       </small>
       <script>
        titan.requestAd.push("adspot-300x250-pos3");
       </script>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Metropolitan Police of London has published a
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/metropolitanpolice/sets/72157627267892973/" target="_blank">
         set of photos on Flickr
        </a>
       </strong>
       showing people police believe to be riot participants. The police are asking the public to identify anyone they recognise in the pictures, which were captured by some of London's 1.5 million CCTV cameras.
      </p>
      <p>
       "Operation Withern's priority is to bring to justice those who have committed violent and criminal acts," police said on the Flickr page.
      </p>
      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Clapham residents wait to be allowed through a police cordon to help council workers with the clear up." src="/content/dam/images/1/i/l/i/j/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.1ilif.png" title=""/>
       <p>
        Clapham residents wait to be allowed through a police cordon to help council workers with the clear up.
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       "As the detailed and thorough investigation progresses we will be issuing photographs of suspects, like those of alleged looters we are releasing today."
      </p>
      <p>
       An unofficial effort using Tumblr,
       <strong>
        <a href="http://catchalooter.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
         Catch A Looter
        </a>
       </strong>
       , is also publishing images of looters carrying handfuls of stolen goods. Another Tumblr blog,
       <strong>
        <a href="http://photoshoplooter.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
         Photoshoplooter
        </a>
       </strong>
       , seeks to add humour to what is a very grim situation with a series of doctored images.
      </p>
      <p>
       A Facebook group dubbed "
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.facebook.com/londoncleanup#!/pages/Lets-catch-the-London-2011-rioters-and-looters/107213176046031" target="_blank">
         Lets catch the London 2011 rioters and looters
        </a>
       </strong>
       " is also growing rapidly, while another, "
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.facebook.com/pages/Supporting-the-Met-Police-against-the-London-rioters/152937041453243?sk=wall" target="_blank">
         Supporting the Met Police against the London rioters
        </a>
       </strong>
       ", has more than 850,000 likes.
      </p>
      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Volunteers make their views on looters known." src="/content/dam/images/1/i/l/i/l/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.1ilif.png" title=""/>
       <p>
        Volunteers make their views on looters known.
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       A Google Group -
       <strong>
        <a href="http://groups.google.com/group/london-riots-facial-recognition" target="_blank">
         London Riots Facial Recognition
        </a>
       </strong>
       - is taking things a step further by using facial recognition technologies to identify looters automatically depicted in photographs. One of the tools it says it will use is the Face API from Facebook that helps identify people in uploaded photos.
      </p>
      <p>
       "Whilst those intimately involved in the riots may be thankful for the way in which social media has allowed them to avoid immediate detection by authorities, in the long term social media will only make it easier for the police to isolate and identity those involved," said James Griffin, partner with the social media monitoring firm SR7.
      </p>

      <p>
       Following revelations that the encrypted BlackBerry Messenger service was one of the key tools used to spread the word about riot locations, BlackBerry-maker RIM has agreed to co-operate with police in tracing the key rioters.
