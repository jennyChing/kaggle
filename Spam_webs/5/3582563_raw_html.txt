         <h1>
          Ghost Owl // Band of the Week | Athens Music Blog
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta">
          by
          <a href="http://consume-media.com/author/madison/" rel="author" title="Posts by Madison Snelling">
           Madison Snelling
          </a>
          | Jun 20, 2013 |
          <a href="http://consume-media.com/category/blog/athfest/" rel="category tag">
           Athfest
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://consume-media.com/category/blog/band-of-the-week/" rel="category tag">
           Band of the Week
          </a>
          |
          <span class="comments-number">
           <a href="http://consume-media.com/ghost-owl-band-of-the-week/#respond">
            0 comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           By Madison Snelling, June 20, 2013
           <br/>
           This coming weekend marks the 16th anniversary of AthFest and the first AthFest debut of Ghost Owl, a band thats sure to make some waves at this years festival. Ghost Owl got its start in January of this year and have since played live only a handful of times, most notably opening for Toubab Krewe at the Brooklyn Bowl in New York City this past month. Although Ghost Owl was conceived mere months ago, fans shouldnt write them off as a group of inexperienced musicians performing live for the first time. The band consists of three veteran musicians from the widely known jam band, Perpetual Groove, who took an indefinite hiatus last year. Original band members Matt McDonald, Adam Perry, and Albert Suttle have come together since the dissolution of P Groove and now make up the creative collaboration that is Ghost Owl.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <center>
           [vimeo id=68642099 width=600 height=350]
          </center>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           After playing in a jam band for over a decade, Adam, Matt and Albert decided they wanted to try something different and new, so as the door to their P Groove chapter closed, another door opened. The guys wanted to expand upon their knowledge of electronic music and experiment with different genres and sounds in order to give their fans something fresh and exciting to listen to. If youre a die hard P Groove fan, dont expect to hear Ghost Owl playing any covers or break into a fifteen minute guitar solo thats distinctive of jam bands as a genre. Thats not to say that if you loved P Groove, you wont love Ghost Owl. On the contrary, the guys feel that fans of their P Groove tunes will enjoy and appreciate this new sound that they are continuously developing with the Ghost Owl collaboration.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           With a unique light rig, projection mapping and many new tools and instruments to work with the guys have developed an all encompassing experience that allows fans to delve into the realm of intricate electronic music. With Matt on guitar and two small keyboards, Albert on the drums and Adam playing three separate keyboards, the band is set on providing a new sound for fans, Adam describes the collaboration as a blank canvas ready to create anything new and anything different. Matt agrees that with Ghost Owl, in regards to the music theyve previously made, the guys are enabled to bring to the table new ideas that wouldnt have fit in the mold of P Groove, but seem to form organically with Ghost Owl. The guys are thrilled to expose their new sound to the open ears of AthFest audiences and to shake off the cobwebs and start a new, as Albert puts it. So dont miss out on being one of the first to experience this ever evolving, collaborative group that is set to perform at AthFest on Saturday, June 22 at 8pm.
          </p>
