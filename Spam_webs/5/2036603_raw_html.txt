        <header class="post-header">
         <h1 class="post-title">
          6 Important Things You Should Know About Bacon
         </h1>
         <p class="brand-color post-subtitle">
          Tips From The Kitchn
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="post-body branded-links">
         <div class="image-wrapper mt-image-center" style="max-width: 540.0px;">
          <span class="image lazy-image" data-alt="">
           <div class="pad" style="padding-bottom: 128.65%;">
            <span data-src="http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/5504f2f9dbfa3f4dab008739/_w.540_s.fit_/Bacon.jpg">
            </span>
            <noscript>
             <img alt="" src="http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/5504f2f9dbfa3f4dab008739/_w.540_s.fit_/Bacon.jpg"/>
            </noscript>
           </div>
          </span>
          <div class="image-controls">
           <a class="js-custom-pinterest-button custom-pinterest-button--big" data-social-action="lazy image pin" data-social-network="pinterest" data-social-target="http://www.thekitchn.com/6-important-things-about-bacon-tips-from-the-kitchn-217172" data-track-social="true" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.thekitchn.com%2F6-important-things-about-bacon-tips-from-the-kitchn-217172&amp;description=6%20Important%20Things%20You%20Should%20Know%20About%20Bacon%20%E2%80%94%20Tips%20From%20The%20Kitchn&amp;media=http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/5504f2f9dbfa3f4dab008739/_w.540_s.fit_/Bacon.jpg">
            Pin it
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          It's Sunday morning, which means it's time for bacon. If you're craving a salty, crispy slice this morning take a spin through our six essentials of bacon, from the weird tip for cooking it on the stovetop, to the best way to freeze it for later.
         </p>
         <h3>
          The best way to cook bacon? The oven!
         </h3>
         <p>
          Why? You can make a lot of it at once, and it's hands-off  no flipping, no turning. And again: you can make
          <em>
           a lot
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <div class="content-highlight">
          <p>
           <strong>
            Learn how 
            <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/how-to-cook-perfect-bacon-in-the-oven-cooking-lessons-from-the-kitchn-107970">
             How To Make Perfect Bacon in the Oven
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="304" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/AYVWWKwfSnM" width="540">
         </iframe>
         <h3>
          If you do make bacon on the stovetop, use this trick.
         </h3>
         <p>
          If you just want a slice or two, cook it quickly in a skillet but make sure you add a little water first. If that sounds weird, just read our tip  it works!
         </p>
         <div class="content-highlight">
          <p>
           <strong>
            See the tip 
           </strong>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/tip-for-perfect-bacon-add-a-little-water-to-the-pan-191595">
             Weird Tip That Really Works: For Perfect Bacon, Add a Little Water to the Pan
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <h3>
          In a rush? It's OK  you can cook it in the microwave.
         </h3>
         <p>
          But if the oven and the stovetop aren't quite quick enough for your morning, why yes, the microwave is actually a very good way to cook bacon  especially if you like it ultra-crisp.
         </p>
         <div class="content-highlight">
          <p>
           <strong>
            The best way to cook bacon in the microwave 
            <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/how-to-cook-bacon-in-the-microwave-cooking-lessons-from-the-kitchn-200719">
             How To Cook Bacon in the Microwave
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/how-to-cook-bacon-in-the-microwave-cooking-lessons-from-the-kitchn-200719">
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <h3>
          Cook more bacon  it's good for your pans.
         </h3>
         <p>
          But if you do have time to cook it in a pan, keep doing that  it's really good for your pans, from cast iron skillets to woks.
         </p>
         <div class="content-highlight">
          <p>
           <strong>
            Keep your pans in good shape 
           </strong>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/bacon-in-wok-tips-from-the-kitchn-214641">
             For Healthy Wok Cooking, Start with Bacon
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <h3>
          Save your bacon fat... and put it on popcorn.
         </h3>
         <p>
          Don't throw out that bacon fat.
          <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/tip-save-your-bacon-fat-83937">
           Save it
          </a>
          and add a little smoky goodness to your popcorn.
         </p>
         <div class="content-highlight">
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/tip-save-your-bacon-fat-83937">
            </a>
            Get a recipe 
            <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/how-to-make-bacon-fat-popcorn-114252">
             How To Make Bacon Fat Popcorn
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="image-wrapper mt-image-center" style="max-width: 540.0px;">
          <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/how-to-freeze-individual-slices-of-bacon-cooking-lessons-from-the-kitchn-184687">
           <span class="image lazy-image" data-alt="">
            <div class="pad" style="padding-bottom: 100.0%;">
             <span data-src="http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/51bd45bbdbd0cb1eb4001f03._w.540_s.fit_.jpg">
             </span>
             <noscript>
              <img alt="" src="http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/51bd45bbdbd0cb1eb4001f03._w.540_s.fit_.jpg"/>
             </noscript>
            </div>
           </span>
          </a>
          <div class="image-caption">
           <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/how-to-freeze-individual-slices-of-bacon-cooking-lessons-from-the-kitchn-184687">
            How To Freeze Bacon for Easy Cooking Later
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="image-controls">
           <a class="js-custom-pinterest-button custom-pinterest-button--big" data-social-action="lazy image pin" data-social-network="pinterest" data-social-target="http://www.thekitchn.com/6-important-things-about-bacon-tips-from-the-kitchn-217172" data-track-social="true" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.thekitchn.com%2F6-important-things-about-bacon-tips-from-the-kitchn-217172&amp;description=6%20Important%20Things%20You%20Should%20Know%20About%20Bacon%20%E2%80%94%20Tips%20From%20The%20Kitchn&amp;media=http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/51bd45bbdbd0cb1eb4001f03._w.540_s.fit_.jpg">
            Pin it
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
         <h3>
          Won't use it all? Freeze it.
         </h3>
         <p>
          Bacon should be used after just a few days  unless it's frozen. Freeze bacon in individual slices for easy thawing and quick cooking.
         </p>
         <div class="content-highlight">
          <p>
           <strong>
            Tips for freezing bacon 
            <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/how-to-freeze-individual-slices-of-bacon-cooking-lessons-from-the-kitchn-184687">
             How To Freeze Bacon for Easy Cooking Later
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          What are your best tips and advice for the very, very, very best bite of bacon?
