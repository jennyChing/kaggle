       <div class="description group">
        <p>
         <img alt="Botox Injections" height="166" src="/images/blog_images/Botox_Injections.jpg" style="margin: 5px; float: left;" width="250"/>
         Its true! Gastric bands that you can swallow; have you heard of Obalon? Its been making headlines throughout the UK this week. The new treatment is similar to that of gastric band surgery in that the patient has a balloon put in to their stomach to reduce appetitebut its not invasive. The balloon is taken like a tablet and simply swallowed by the patient. Take a look at this video report from the
         <a href="http://youtu.be/s__tCmqrH_E" target="_blank">
          Huffington Post website on YouTube
         </a>
         which demonstrates how it works. You can also view it at the bottom of this page.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The Process
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/health-and-families/health-news/obalon-gastric-weight-loss-pill-which-releases-a-balloon-in-patients-launches-in-the-uk-9075353.html" target="_blank">
          The Independent
         </a>
         also reported on the story and the way in which this variation on invasive gastric band surgery works. The process is as follows:
        </p>
        <p>
         - The balloon is swallowed in pill form (it is the size of a large vitamin tablet) attached to a thin tube
         <br/>
         - When the capsule containing the balloon reaches the stomach, the balloon is inflated by the attached micro-catheter (tube that the patient swallows)
         <br/>
         - The capsule containing the balloon dissolves
         <br/>
         - The small tube is removed
         <br/>
         - The balloon sits on top of the patients stomach so in theory they feel fuller, eat less and lose weight
         <br/>
         - After 30 days depending on progress patients consume a second balloon, then a third, another 30 days after the second
         <br/>
         - After treatment, the balloon is deflated and removed using a tube inserted into the patients throat
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Side Effects and Cost
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Side effects of this in trials included vomiting, cramps and reflux which generally subsided after 24 hours. The cost for patients is around 3,000.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Non-Invasive Surgery
         </strong>
         <br/>
         In addition to Obalon, the Huffington Post has reported on
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/2014/01/02/cosmetic-surgery-popular-procedures-2014_n_4530181.html#es_share_ended" target="_blank">
          cosmetic and facial surgery trends for 2014
         </a>
         . In addition to the usual surgical suspects, non-surgical procedures are expected to set the trend in 2014. Facial fillers such as Sculptra, Juvaderm,Restylane and Botox injections are now more popular than ever and the predictions are that they will stay this way.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Other Facial Surgery

         <br/>

        </p>
