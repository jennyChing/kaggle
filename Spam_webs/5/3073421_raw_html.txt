             <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
              <p>
               In order to succeed in business, you need to carefully manage your legal and financial affairs. But it is just as crucial that you manage your business risks, too.
              </p>
              <p>
               Businesses face a number of risks in their day-to-day activities, including:
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Damage or loss of buildings.
               </li>
               <li>
                Damage or loss of plant, equipment, vehicles and stock.
               </li>
               <li>
                Lawsuits and claims by third parties (liability insurance, including product liability and public liability).
               </li>
               <li>
                Providing advice and services (professional indemnity insurance).
               </li>
               <li>
                Mismanagement (management liability insurance).
               </li>
               <li>
                Data breaches (cyber liability insurance).
               </li>
               <li>
                Interruption to normal trading (business interruption insurance).
               </li>
              </ul>
              <p>
               Sadly, 50 per cent of all businesses that suffer major losses fail within two years
               <span style="font-size:11px">
                <sup>
                 1
                </sup>
               </span>
               .Business insurance is designed to get your business back to where it was before an event. Those businesses without good cover, or with no insurance at all, are less likely to overcome the financial impact of a major event. Even a small event, like a burglary, can significantly reduce both cashflow and revenue if key equipment is stolen or damaged. This could result in financial distress for you, your staff, your creditors and suppliers and for your customers.
              </p>
              <h2>
               Why should I use an insurance broker for my business insurance?
              </h2>
              <p>
               It is certainly possible to buy a standard business insurance pack for many trades and businesses. The critical issue, however, is whether those insurance packs actually cover the risks in your own business. You may be an expert in your business, but it is unlikely you have the time or desire to be an expert in business insurance as well.
              </p>
              <p>
               A good insurance broker can help by guiding you through the key things that you need to consider. In 2014, the Cameron Research Group found that 89 per cent of broker clients were either very satisfied or fairly satisfied with the service they received.
              </p>
              <h2>
               What will a business insurance broker do for me?
              </h2>
              <p>
               A business insurance broker will help you understand and assess your risks. They will find the insurance cover that best meets your needs and they will get the most competitive price the market offers for that cover. They will also have experience making claims with different insurers. In short, an insurance broker will help make the whole process, from buying and comparing policies to making a claim, much easier for you.
              </p>
              <p>
               An insurance broker will:
              </p>
              <ol>
               <li>
                Assess and understand your risks, see what can be done to manage those risks and reduce or eliminate the risks where possible. There will always be some risks you cannot eliminate, and you protect yourself from the financial impact of those risks by buying insurance.
               </li>
               <li>
                Determine what insurance coverage is most appropriate for you. The main insurance policy wording is important, but all major insurers also offer special terms and conditions for the wide variety of business circumstances that exist across Australia. Importantly, all insurance policies are not the same, and great care is needed to make sure your policy gives you the protection you need.
               </li>
               <li>
                Ensure you get value for money. They will get quotes specifically for your business from several insurers and will be able to highlight the benefits of each. Be very wary of taking the cheapest cover, though, because it is likely to give you the least protection when something goes wrong.
               </li>
               <li>
                Assist with claims. If something does go wrong and you need to make a claim, your insurance broker becomes your advocate and will help you get all you are entitled to under the policy you have taken out.
               </li>
              </ol>
              <h2>
               How much does it cost to use a business insurance broker?
              </h2>
              <p>
               Insurance brokers fees will vary depending on the services they provide. Insurance brokers may receive commissions from insurers. You may be charged a direct fee for their time and expertise, just as you would for a doctor or lawyers services. Remember that brokers may actually be able to negotiate either a better price and/or cover that is more suitable for your businesss specific needs, providing better value in the long-run. You should feel free to discuss fees and charges with your insurance broker when you first meet with them  they will be happy to answer any questions you may have.
              </p>
              <h2>
               How can I choose a business insurance broker thats right for me?
              </h2>
              <p>
               When considering using an insurance broker, there are two main things you should look for.
              </p>
              <p>
               Firstly, the insurance broking firm should be a member of the National Insurance Brokers Association of Australia (NIBA). All members of NIBA are insurance professionals who are committed to high standards of customer service. These standards are set out in the Insurance Brokers Code of Practice.
              </p>
              <p>
               Secondly, the brokers of your insurance broking firm should carry the status of Qualified Practising Insurance Broker (QPIB) on their business cards. This signifies that they have the accepted industry training and experience, and are regarded by their peers as competent and committed professionals.
              </p>
              <p>
               You may also wish to choose an insurance broker that specialises in your industry.
              </p>

                Dallas Booth is the Chief Executive Officer of the National Insurance Brokers Association of Australia.
               </em>
              </p>

              <h2>
