       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         The Weekly Playlist: 11.11.13
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="posted-on">
          <a href="http://onesmallwindow.com/music/the-weekly-playlist-11-11-13/" rel="bookmark" title="10:18 am">
           <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2013-11-11T10:18:47+00:00">
            November 11, 2013
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="The Weekly Playlist - Back To The Future" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7878" height="395" src="http://onesmallwindow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/h_music_btf.jpg" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im not a big on EDM so I missed this song by Swedish DJ/producer Avicii when it became a monster hit, and only discovered it recently. The lead vocal immediately grabbed my attention, because it was just so good, and also uncredited in the original release. A little digging revealed that it was the gorgeous voice of American singer Aloe Blacc. I also found an alternate acoustic version of the song. Here are both for you to check out.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Aloe Blacc 
          <em>
           Wake Me Up
          </em>
          (Acoustic)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="349" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/M_o6axAseak?rel=0" width="620">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Avicii 
          <em>
           Wake Me Up
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="349" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/IcrbM1l_BoI?rel=0" width="620">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          U137 
          <em>
           Pearl Lakes
          </em>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Beautiful cinematic soundscapes from Sweden. Ive selected one song, but I suggest you stream the entire album, which can be found
         <a href="http://deepelmdigital.com/album/dreamer-on-the-run" target="_blank" title="Dreamer On The Run album">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe seamless="" src="http://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=3734408593/size=medium/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/t=3/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; width: 620px; height: 120px;">
          <a href="http://deepelmdigital.com/album/dreamer-on-the-run">
           Dreamer On The Run by U137
          </a>
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Fozzey and VanC 
          <em>
           Perfect Couple 1 &amp; 2
          </em>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         I really like the arrangement on this track  spare guitar, piano and percussion.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="349" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//player.vimeo.com/video/66057479?title=0&amp;byline=0&amp;portrait=0&amp;color=ffffff" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="620">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          The Cranberries 
          <em>
           No Need To Argue
          </em>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         The music is so spare, this almost counts as acapella. It is the title track of their 1994 release but I dont think it was ever released as a single. My absolute favourite by the band.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="465" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/nXLwPUr5F1k?rel=0" width="620">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Huey Lewis and the News 
          <em>
           The Power of Love
          </em>
          (
          <em>
           Back To The Future
          </em>
          OST)
         </strong>
         <br/>
         I rediscovered this song after listening to Huey Lewiss conversation with comedian Marc Maron on his podcast
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.wtfpod.com/" target="_blank" title="WTF with Marc Maron">
           WTF
          </a>
         </em>
         . You can check out the interview
         <a href="http://www.wtfpod.com/podcast/episodes/episode_384_-_huey_lewis" target="_blank" title="Huey Lewis - WTF Pod">
          here
         </a>
         after you watch this video, cut to clips from
         <em>
          Back To The Future
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="349" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/N-0-9JUMBSw?rel=0" width="620">
         </iframe>
        </p>
