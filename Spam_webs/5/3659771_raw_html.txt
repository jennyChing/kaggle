         <h1>

         </h1>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:14px;">
           Those who drive large semi trucks for a living will tell you that one of the biggest nightmares keeping them up at night is the thought of their expensive truck breaking down, and losing money while being stuck on the side of the road. After a breakdown, the ability to get a replacement Freightliner Radiator ordered and installed quickly can be the difference between making or losing money.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:14px;">
           A well functioning Freightliner Radiator helps to keep your engines cool, especially during long trips where a truck is required to travel a high amount of consecutive miles. These long miles can put a lot of wear and strain on your trucks engine. When a Freightliner Radiator isnt working properly it can allow your engine to get too hot, which can lead to engine oil breaking down and in the most extreme cases, cause engine failure.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:14px;">
           In the unfortunate occurrence that a Freightliner Radiator breaks down, it is crucial that drivers are able to get a new one as quickly as possible, while also having the confidence that the Freightliner Radiator they are ordering will not only fit their truck, but also be of high enough quality that drivers can trust that they will protect them over the long haul. S&amp;F Radiator Service has built a reputation for not only being Freightliner Radiator experts, but also for carrying the highest quality parts in the industry.
          </span>

          Breakdown of S&amp;Fs Freightliner Radiator Plastic/Aluminum Construction
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:14px;">
           Finding a Freightliner Radiator or header that matches the original is extremely important when looking for replacement parts for your truck. Our Freightliner headers are not only equivalent to the original Freightliner Radiator in your truck, they are superior. Provided are a few reasons why we believe that purchasing a Freightliner radiator from us can give you the confidence of knowing you are purchasing a product that is superior to the OEM
          </span>

         <h2>
          Reinforced Headers
         </h2>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:12px;">
           Ev
           <span style="font-size:14px;">
            ery Freightliner Radiator purchased from S&amp;F contains fully reinforced headers. In the late 1990s a wider version of a Freightliner Century Radiator was created for the Sterling A9500 and L9500. This solved the problem of additional cooling capacity, but because it was wider, it also caused additional stress on the radiators and consequently the failure rates were extremely high. The original manufacturer, Behr, decided to put a full header reinforcement on these Sterling and Freightliner Radiators. While this fixed the problem they only used this method on one model which is found on the Sterling AT9500 and LT9500 as well as on certain FLD120 and Classic models.
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>

         </p>

         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:14px;">
           S&amp;F understands the benefit of full header reinforcement and decided that if it was a good solution for a few models, it is also a good solution for all the models that we are able to install it on. We include this full header support on all models able to support it, including the following along with many others.

         <h2>
          Tube Supports
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:14px;">
           Another benefit of a Freightliner Radiator purchased from S&amp;F is that all units include Tube supports. The weak point on any Freightliner Radiator is the corner tubes. In many copper brass radiators of old, the end tubes were simply soldered over and canceled. Modern aluminum brazing does not require this process, but the end tube still receives the most stress from thermal expansion. To be able to provide our customers with the highest quality Freightliner Radiator available, we had our supplier install tube supports on the four end tubes in order to distribute the stress evenly and avoid failure. This process has contributed to our near zero failure rate from corner leaks.
