         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          2 Ways To Prove Your Value On A Resume (Or LinkedIn Profile)
         </h1>
         <div class="post-info">
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-60 photo" height="60" src="http://2.gravatar.com/avatar/e273c575dd49edf0d5c9cd3bb756b014?s=60&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g" srcset="http://2.gravatar.com/avatar/e273c575dd49edf0d5c9cd3bb756b014?s=120&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g 2x" width="60"/>

            Margaret Buj
           </a>
           <br/>
           January 22, 2015
          </p>
          <p class="categories">
           <a href="http://www.careerealism.com/category/job-search/">

        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <div class="inpost">
          <section class="widget widget_text" id="text-272629749">
           <div class="widget-wrap">
            <div class="textwidget">
             <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">

         <p>
          I still remember my shock at seeing clichs similar to I can work on my own initiative as well as part of a team in 19 out of 20 applications I had received for the first role I was recruiting for when I started my recruitment career 10 years ago.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Related:
          </strong>
          <a href="http://www.careerealism.com/quantify-experience-numbers/">
           3 Ways To Quantify Your Experience With Numbers
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Even now, I often receive resumes with phrases such as Self-motivated team player, responsible, or innovative. If your resume if full of these mundane and boring phrases (with no examples to back them up with!) it is going to be difficult for you to stand out or for recruiters to really see your value proposition.
         </p>
         <p>
          It is not that certain power words cant be used on your resume. But you want to use them effectively so that you dont sound like everyone else.
         </p>
         <p>
          The problem is these words are very subjective. Anyone can say they are efficient or well organized or that they have great leadership skills. NOTHING about that makes you stand out from the competition unless you show some examples.
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are some tips on how to prove your value on a resume/LinkedIn profile:
         </p>
         <h3>
          Use evidence and concrete examples to back up your statements.
         </h3>
         <p>
          Lets suppose youre an internal auditor whos implemented new payroll and tax accounting systems that will saveyour company $200k in staff costs over the next three years. It is a result driven example that demonstrates you being innovative or good at problem solving.
         </p>
         <p>
          Perhaps youre an admin assistant and youve managed switchboard with eight incoming lines, routing an average of 300 calls per day. You immediately sound more impressive than a candidate who simply says they have great organizational skills.
         </p>
         <p>
          I see lots of resumes of people who are results-driven. Id suggest adding some information that actually PROVES your drive for results. In what ways has your performance outpaced that of your peers? Perhaps youve earned three promotions in 18 months. If so, put that information on your resume.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Use numbers.
         </h3>
         <p>
          Numbers are great for demonstrating your skills and expertise. Did you increase revenue, saved time, or money? How many events did you organize? How many clients did you deal with every month?
         </p>
         <p>
          Another clichd quote I see on resumes is proven success or proven track record. Again, unless you prove it, I wouldnt bother with such phrases. If youve exceeded quota every year for the past three years or finished all projects under budget, then mention that.
         </p>
         <p>
          These tangible achievements will help a hiring person understand the scope of your work and the reasons behind your career progression.
         </p>
         <p>
          To summarize, remove all redundant phrases from your resume and LinkedIn profile. Provide specific details of your achievements and watch your interview invites go up!
         </p>
