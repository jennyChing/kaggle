          <div class="post-inner group">
           <h1 class="post-title">
            Flashback Friday: The Top 5 Most Exciting Regular Season Games Ever
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://sportsrants.com/nfl/author/mattandruscavage/" rel="author" title="Posts by Matt Andruscavage">
             Matt Andruscavage
            </a>
             May 22, 2015
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <img alt="fakespike" class="attachment-thumb-large wp-post-image" height="340" src="http://sportsrants.com/nfl/files/2015/05/fakespike-720x340.jpg" width="720"/>
             <p style="text-align: center">
              <strong>
               (Photo from: www.sun-sentinel.com )
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              There have been so many exciting games in the last 40 years that it is definitely hard to narrow it down to five games, but Im going to give it my best shot!
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              The criteria used to determine the five are the following:
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              1) The game should either be a back-and-forth type game where both teams look like theyre going to have a chance to win the game, or,
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              2) The game shouldhavean amazing comeback, preferably in the 4th quarter.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              That said, here are the top five regular season games in NFL history:
             </p>
             <h3 style="text-align: left">
              #5  1994 Week 13  Miami at NY Jets (The Fake Spike Game)
             </h3>
             <p>
              Going into the game, the Jets were 6-5 and only a game behind the 7-4 Miami Dolphins for first place in the AFC East.
             </p>
             <p>
              The Jets had a very productive game leading 17-0 in the 3rd quarter, when Miami finally scored on a ten yard pass from Dan Marino to Mark Ingram. The problem for Miami was that they went for two and the pass attempt failed. The Jets promptly added another touchdown with a 14 yard touchdown pass from Boomer Esiason to tight end Johnny Mitchell to extend the lead to 24-6.
             </p>
             <p>
              That was when Marino got the Dolphins started on the one of the best comebacks in the history of the league.
             </p>
             <p>
              Ingram struck again first with a ball that was popped up in the air and was able to come down with it to extend the chains for a first down at the Jets 18. Marino found him open again for a touchdown to cut the lead to 24-14 after a successful two-point conversion attempt.
             </p>
             <p>
              Esiason was driving the Jets, but threw a costly interception to Troy Vincent, which set up a 28 yard touchdown to yes, you guessed it, Mark Ingram.
             </p>
             <p>
              After the Dolphins muffed a punt, the Jets recovered and it seemed like they were going to put the game away, but Esiason made another poor throw that was intercepted by J.B. Brown.
             </p>
             <p>
              Down 28-24 with just over two minutes to play, Marino began driving the Dolphins from their own 15. Pass after pass, Marino got his team down to the Jets 7 yard line with 37 seconds to go. Everyone in the building thought Marino was going to spike the ball, as he even gave the spike signal, but instead, as the Jets froze, Marino dropped back throw and quickly found Ingram for the game-winning touchdown with 20 seconds left. It was not only amazing to watch, but it was very unexpected.
             </p>
             <div class="video-container">
              <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
               <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="435" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/9S_biZV-lOk?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="720">
               </iframe>
              </span>
             </div>
             <h3 style="text-align: left">
             </h3>
             <h3 style="text-align: left">
              #4  1978 Week 12  Dolphins at Oilers  The Luv Ya Blue Game
             </h3>
             <p>
              This game, on
              <a href="http://sportsrants.com/nfl/2013/12/17/monday-night-football/">
               Monday Night Football
              </a>
              , was a classic matchup featuring Earl Campbell for the Oilers and Bob Greise on the Dolphins. It went back and forth through three quarters and was tied at 21 heading into the 4th quarter.
             </p>
             <p>
              The Dolphins started the 4th quarter scoring by sacking quarterback DanPastorini in the endzone for a safety, putting them up 23-21.
             </p>
             <p>
              Houston responded by driving down the field and letting powerful running back Earl Campbell carry the rock for a 12 yard touchdown, his thirdof the day.
             </p>
             <p>
              Leading 28-23 and the crowd going completely crazy, Houston got the ball back on their own 19 and once again leaned on Campbell and he got to the outside and outran everyone for an amazing 81 yard touchdown to put the Oilers up 35-23.
             </p>
             <p>
              Miami was able to cut the lead to 35-30, but the Oilers held on, winning one of the most thrilling games in Monday Night Football history.
             </p>
             <div class="video-container">
              <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
               <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="435" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/wXMH_LEju-M?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="720">
               </iframe>
              </span>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h3>
              #3  2000 Week 8  Dolphins at NY Jets
             </h3>
             <p>
              Yes, I realize the Dolphins have been in all 3 games thus far, and the Jets in two of them. These were all very exciting games featuring very different versions of those franchises.
             </p>
             <p>
              This Monday night game saw Miami take a 30-7 lead into the 4th quarter and a large portion of the TV audience decided to go to bed. (Thankfully, I wasnt tired and had nothing else to do)
             </p>
             <p>
              The Jets scored a couple quick touchdowns and the defense held the Dolphins. After a field goal and another quick score, suddenly the Jets had TIED the game at 30!
             </p>
             <p>
              The Dolphins responded with a deep ball to Leslie Shepherd for a 46 yard touchdown. 37-30 Jets and it seemed like it was over. But the Jets responded with a good drive and a touchdown pass to offensive lineman Jumbo Elliot. Tie game. Overtime.
             </p>
             <p>
              Jets cornerback Marcus Coleman came up with an interception, and then a long gain by Wayne Chrebet set up a game-winning 40 yard field goal for John Hall.
             </p>
             <p>
              It was an amazing comeback and was rightfully called The Monday Night Miracle.
             </p>
             <div class="video-container">
              <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
               <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="435" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/vCKu5rbL2_Q?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="720">
               </iframe>
              </span>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h3>
              #2  2003 Week 5  Colts at Buccaneers
             </h3>
             <p>
              The Bucs were the defending champions, at home, and on Monday Night Football. The Colts, while good, werent expected to be able to move the ball at will, especially against the Tampa-2 defense.
             </p>
             <p>
              After Ronde Barber intercepted Peyton Manning and took it back for a touchdown to put Tampa up 35-14 with 5 minutes to go, it seemed hopeless for Indy.
             </p>
             <p>
              Brad Pyatt returned the ensuing kick 90 yards to set up a quick touchdown run by James Mungro. Next, came the onside kick and the Colts recovered!
             </p>
             <p>
              Manning was able to find Marvin Harrison for a 28 yard touchdown and suddenly it was 35-28 with a couple minutes left. The defense was able to hold Tampa and force them to punt with under two minutes to go and it would be Manning finding Harrison again for a deep ball all the way to the Tampa 5. Ricky Williams (not that Ricky Williams) scored from a yard out and the game was tied at 35 and headed for overtime!
             </p>
             <p>
              It was amazing. No team had ever overcome a three-touchdown deficit with only 4 minutes to go in the 4th quarter. But it wasnt over yet. The Colts had to get another score in overtime. Tampa had a chance to win after the Colts ran into the punter, but ultimately Tampa had to give the ball back to Manning.
             </p>
             <p>
              Manning led the Colts down for a long field goal attempt, which sailed wide right, however, the Bucs were called for leaping (Simeon Rice landed on one of his own players attempting to block the kick, which is against the rules). The Colts were able to get a shorter field goal and finished one of the most improbable comebacks in league history.
             </p>
             <div class="video-container">
              <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
               <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="435" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/ovNqPGEp7I4?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="720">
               </iframe>
              </span>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h3>
              #1  1994 Week 7  Chiefs at Broncos
             </h3>
             <p>
              John Elway vs Joe Montana. The Chiefs hadnt won in Mile High Stadium in 18 seasons. Both teams had legendary quarterbacks, but if you were watching, you had to believe that it would be Elway once again leading his Broncos past the Chiefs in Denver.
             </p>
             <p>
              Heading into the 4th quarter, both teams had played evenly, tied at 21. The Chiefs drove down the field and took a 24-21 lead on a Lin Elliot field goal.
             </p>
             <p>
              Shannon Sharpefumbled the ball and it seemed to be over until Marcus Allen fumbled it right back and Denver had a shot to take the lead with 1:33 left in the game.
             </p>
             <p>
              Elway had noticed that the Broncos only had 10 players in the game and wisely did a quarterback draw and found himself in the endzone with little time left for Montana, leading 28-24.
             </p>
             <p>
              But it would be Montana who would get the last laugh, driving the Chiefs all the way down to the 5 yard line with 13 seconds left. After a timeout, Montana would find Willie Davis in the endzone with almost no time on the clock, leading Kansas City to a thrilling 31-28 win on Monday Night Football.
             </p>
             <div class="video-container">
              <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
               <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="435" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/_SEHrFgS8s8?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="720">
               </iframe>
              </span>
             </div>
             <p style="text-align: left">
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              There were many great regular season games, but it was these five that stood out the most.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              Honorable mentions:
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              1979 Week 16 Redskins at Cowboys (Staubachs last regular season game)
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              1983 Week 7 Redskins at Packers (the 48-47 MNF game)
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              1980 Week 14 Saints at 49ers (the 35-7 comeback by Montana which is still the greatest regular season comeback in history)
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left">
              1986 Week 3 Dolphins at Jets (the 51-45 Marino/OBrien shootout)
             </p>
