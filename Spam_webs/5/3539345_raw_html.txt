         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.electronics-lab.com/blog/?p=13795" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Laser sparks revolution in internal combustion engines">
           Laser sparks revolution in internal combustion engines
          </a>
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="entrybody">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.electronics-lab.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/three-beam-laser.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13796" height="171" src="http://www.electronics-lab.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/three-beam-laser.jpg" title="three-beam-laser" width="304"/>
          </a>
          <span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          For more than 150 years, spark plugs have powered internal combustion engines. Automakers are now one step closer to being able to replace this long-standing technology with laser igniters, which will enable cleaner, more efficient, and more economical vehicles.
         </p>
         <p>
          In the past, lasers strong enough to ignite an engines air-fuel mixtures were too large to fit under an automobiles hood. At this years Conference on Lasers and Electro Optics (CLEO: 2011), held in Baltimore May 1  6, researchers from Japan described the first multibeam laser system small enough to screw into an engines cylinder head.
         </p>
         <p>
          Equally significant, the new laser system is made from ceramics, and could be produced inexpensively in large volumes, according to one of the presentations authors, Takunori Taira of Japans National Institutes of Natural Sciences. [
          <a href="http://www.elektor.com/news/laser-sparks-revolution-in-internal-combustion.1912070.lynkx" target="_blank">
           via
          </a>
          ]
         </p>
         <p>

           Laser sparks revolution in internal combustion engines
