      <h1 class="title">
       Gluten-Free Lemon Bars with an Almond Crust
      </h1>
      <div class="phn-featured">
       <img alt="gluten free lemon bars big girls small kitchen" class="attachment-post-large wp-post-image" height="750" src="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/gluten-free-lemon-bars-big-girls-small-kitchen.jpg" title="" width="600"/>
      </div>
      <div class="phn-cats">
       <a href="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/category/blog/baking-for-others" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Baking For Others">
        Baking For Others
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="phn-date">
       Posted by
       <a href="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/author/cara_bgsk" rel="author" title="Posts by Cara">
        Cara
       </a>
       on Monday Apr 28th, 2014 |
       <a href="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/2014/04/gluten-free-lemon-bars-with-an-almond-crust.html/print/" rel="nofollow" title="Print">
        Print
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="clear">
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="content">
      <p>
       <em>
        Natalie of
        <a href="http://bit.ly/1cgmkvy" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://bit.ly']);">
         Good Girl Style
        </a>
        is back today to share a really lovely sweet for Mothers Dayeternal classic, Lemon Bars, gone gluten-free.
       </em>
       <em>
        Natalie joins us each month to share incredible desserts with Big Girls, Small Kitchen readersdesserts that are entirely
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/category/blog/gluten-free-3">
          gluten-free
         </a>
         ,
        </strong>
        but not like obviously gluten-free. That means no specialty flours or hard-to-find ingredients, just good old-fashioned butter, sugar, chocolate, cream, and rich, creamy peanut butter. Dont miss her most recent posts, about
        <a href="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/2014/03/strawberry-mousse.html">
         Strawberry Mousse
        </a>
       </em>
       <em>
        and
        <a href="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/2014/04/homemade-reeses-style-peanut-butter-eggs.html">
         Peanut Butter Eggs
        </a>
        .
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       Oh, lemon. Its one of my favorite dessert flavors, which puts lemon bars at the top of the list, too.
      </p>
      <p>
       This version is even more delicious because the almond crust holds its own against the filling and offers a wonderful texture switch from gooey to crunchy. Using an already cooked lemon curd instead of a liquid filling that bakes fully in the oven allows the crust to remain crisp and the filling to remain firm. Re-baking the lemon curd firms it up, though, creating a smooth and cheerful bright-yellow filling.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-7057" height="459" src="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/lemonbars4-690x459.jpg" title="gluten free lemon bars crust | big girls small kitchen" width="690"/>
       <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-7058" height="459" src="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/lemonbars3-690x459.jpg" title="gluten free lemon bars | big girls small kitchen" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Though this recipe takes a bit of effort, the resulting treats are perfect to bring to a baby shower or Mothers Day brunch.
       <span id="more-7054">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <em>
        **Recipe**
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7056" height="750" src="http://www.biggirlssmallkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/gluten-free-lemon-bars2-big-girls-small-kitchen.jpg" title="gluten free lemon bars | big girls small kitchen" width="600"/>
        Lemon Bars with Almond Crust
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <em>
        Adapted from
       </em>
       <a href="http://thenourishinghome.com/2012/05/luscious-lemon-bars-gf/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://thenourishinghome.com']);">
        <em>
         The Nourishing Home
        </em>
       </a>
       <em>
        <br/>
        Makes 16 small squares
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       Ingredients
      </p>
      <p>
       For the almond crust:
       <br/>
       1 cup almonds (blanched or unblanched), ground to a fine powder in a blender or food processor (be careful not to over-process or youll end up with almond butter!)
       <br/>
       2 tablespoons butter, melted
       <br/>
       2 tablespoons sugar
      </p>
      <p>
       For the lemon curd filling:
       <br/>
       6 egg yolks
       <br/>
        cup sugar
       <br/>
       1/3 cup fresh lemon juice (plan on about 2-3 lemons)
       <br/>
       Zest from one lemon
       <br/>
       1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, cut into pieces
       <br/>
       Confectioners sugar, for serving
      </p>
      <p>
       Make the crust: Mix the ground almonds, melted butter, and sugar together in a small bowl. Pour into an 8-by-8-inch square pan (or 8 1/2-inch round) lined with parchment paper. Use the back of the spoon to spread and press the mixture together to cover the bottom of the pan. Bake at 350F for 8 to 10 minutes, until just beginning to look golden and dry. Let cool. Turn oven down to 300F.
      </p>
      <p>
       Make the filling: In a large heatproof metal or glass bowl, whisk the egg yolks, sugar, lemon juice, and lemon zest together. Place on top of a pot of simmering water and add the butter a few pieces at a time until melted and combined, stirring frequently. Continue to cook over the simmering water for an additional 10 to 12 minutes, stirring frequently, until thick and coating the back of the spoon. Remove from heat and strain into a shallow bowl to remove any curdled egg bits and keep the filling smooth. Allow to cool to room temperature. Spread the lemon curd filling over the crust and bake at 300F for 18 to 20 minutes, until the filling is set. Let cool to room temperature, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before cutting. Dust with powdered sugar if desired.
      </p>
