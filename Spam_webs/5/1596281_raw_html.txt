        <h2>
         Shadows Over Grimora
        </h2>
        <div style="position: relative">
         <div class="story-page-text">
          Cyreina sat waiting
in the dark and felt out the contents of her coin purse for the
seventh time. The boiling smell of powerful incense made her
light-headed and added to the silent scolding she was giving herself.
          <i>
           You barely earn enough as is and here you are, wasting even more
seis on another card reading.
          </i>
          <p>
           Part of her
reasoned that an occasional treat was all that kept her sane; she
could continue eating a broth of wilted cabbage and tomatoes past
their prime for another assignment.
          </p>
          <p>
           <i>
            And just how are
you going to buy out your contract spending seis like this?
           </i>
           Her
inner thoughts demanded.
          </p>
          <p>
           <i>
            Master Domiris is
never going to let me go free. His cut of my assignment earnings has
gone up again and he keeps assuring that hell find me clients
willing to pay more  but its been a year, now. Better
assignments arent coming.
           </i>
           She answered back in silence.
          </p>
          <p>
           The curtain
that served as a door billowed open and she jumped, startled from her
argument with herself. On instinct, she curled inward and clutched
her satchel tightly to her bust. Once the door had opened and the
rusting chime sang, she eased.
           <i>
            I do wish Madame Safani would fix
the rust. Its smell is awful, even with the incense.
            <br/>
           </i>
          </p>
          <p>
           <i>
            Come
in, dear, come in. Her voice was airy as ever but Cy knew that was
a front. Shed heard the Madames real voice once when shed
begun coughing from the smoke and remembered her crusted lungs
hurling out a curse or two.
           </i>
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Cyreina stood and
shuffled across the waiting room, groping for the curtain door
 a lovely, rustic crimson, dear, as Safani had breathed
once.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Oh, it is good
to see you, my dear! Her chair creaked on the plush carpet and she
took Cys hands. In spite of the smell of blush flower and oil bird
feathers, her wrinkled hands always had a strong scent of perfume. Cy
never could figure out what that scent was.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Safani led her to
the table and helped her sit. Oh, youre cut across that cute
little nose, dear! That crude Dominic has worked you ragged!
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           He just sells me
to people temporarily, Cy murmured, uncomfortable, so, really,
they run me ragged.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Her bangs shivered,
meaning the Madame had done one of her dreamy hand waves. Poor
dear. Were you put to work in the sun again? Youre so pale, I
worry next time I see you, youll look like one of those lobster
Nuburans!
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Nuburans were
typically bipedal people baring the animal characteristics of their
clan. Cy tried to imagine a lobster and quickly gave up on the idea.
Last time shed met one, hed been a shorter rat gentleman with a
charming brogue and hed sweet-talked her into buying a moon charm.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Cy realized shed
stayed quiet too long and laughed politely. Im not really that
pale, am I?
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Madame Sefani
sucked in a bit of air as though offended someone disagreed with her
beautifully crafted observation. My dear, you are whiter than a
snow fox!
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           The younger woman
paused before asking, Is there...anything else unusual about my
appearance?
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Sefani paused as
well, a rare sound in her home. OhIm sorry, child. I still
cannot tell your origin or, she almost mumbled, race. If I
knew, Id gladly tell you. All I know is that it is unusual to see
such pale skin with black hair. I had thought perhaps you were an
albino Meijin; rare, but not unheard of. You always keep your ears
bandaged up. Beyond that, I simply cannot say.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           There was another
moment of silence between them though this one was decidedly
melancholy.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Lets take a
look at your cards, shall we? the proclaimed seer offered.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Oh. Yes, thank
you. Cy fished out the majority of her coin and laid it on the
table.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           And thank you,
dear. Once the coin clinked into a rather full jar, Sefani
extended a worn deck of Serentian cards. From what she had told Cy
before, the deck was very rare and harkened back to the infamous
witch queen of ancient lore. Shuffle like normal.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Cy took the deck in
her left hand and cut it three times before shuffling. She tried to
take her time, not wanting to repeat her first read mistake of
dropping them near Sefanis small army of candles; itd been the
only other time the Madame had cursed.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Once she felt
content with the order, she cut it once more into three piles and sat
back.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           The seer made her
typical show of slowly laying the cards out, giving each one a quiet
sound of wonder and awe. On the last one, Cy noticed Sefani had
fallen silent.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Its the death
card again, isnt it? Cy asked quietly.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Three times in a
row now youve drawn that card in your near future spot, Sefani
admitted, genuinely awed, Ive never seen anyone draw such a
hand. She quickly finished with, Of course, it doesnt mean a
literal death, my dear!
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           What does it
mean in this context?
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Wellit seems
to indicate being around a good bit of death in the near future.
Death, strife but, oddly enough, jubilant times as well.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Cy wrinkled her
nose, I see death all the time. Especially with new slavesmost
cannot handle the more extreme assignments or people who buy a slave
to have a whipping boy.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Her tone had taken
the seer aback. Yes, of course, dearI am just telling you what
the cards say.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           The other girl
exhaled and bowed her head lower. Forgive my outburst. It was
rude.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           All is
forgiven. The seer glanced at the cards once more and stifled a
small giggle, Oheven odder still, the cards see you meeting a
very handsome gentleman soon.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Cy held in a scoff.
I see. More likely a new master?
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           They do not
specify, my dear. All they say is that hell be very mysterious and
quite taken with you.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Despite herself, Cy
felt a bit intrigued. Thank you, Madame Sefani. Its always fun
to speak with you. Fun felt like such an odd word on her tongue.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Here, the
seer pressed a card into her hand as she stood. I feel as though
this is best kept with you. It is the Death card.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Resigned, the young
slave thanked her and tucked it into a book resting in her pack.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Oh, and do be
careful, dear. She added as they walked to the door together, The
guards have been warning everyone of a group slinking about Grimora
thats been kidnapping young women.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Im heading
right to my night job, Madame. Grave-digging isnt exactly
thrilling work but Ill have the gruff old undertaker watching to
make sure I dont slack. Ill be careful, though.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>

           <img class="profile-picture-l img-circle" src="http://www.gravatar.com/avatar/9e1a26b29b86ab4147ecffeaaae60f93?s=50&amp;d=mm"/>
           <a class="button create-review-button-in-text" ng-click="clickCreateReview($event)" ng-mouseenter="clearReviewHint()" url-for="CreateReviewCtrl({id: 12501})">
            Write a Review
           </a>
           Are you enjoying my story? Please let me know what you think by leaving a review! Thanks, AmberE
          </div>
          <p>
           Please do.
Her mystic tone dropped. The seer bid her farewell and vanished
inside her home.
          </p>
          <i>
          </i>
          <p>
           Cy felt the card in
between the frayed pages of her guilty pleasure A Guide to
Mystical Beasts &amp; Spirits. She pondered its presence,
weighing coincidence against probability before she started towards
the large graveyard tucked away from the discerning eyes of the
nobility.
          </p>
