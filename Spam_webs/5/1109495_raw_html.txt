       <h2 class="posttitle">
        Strawberry Shortcake Bars
       </h2>
       <div class="postmeta">
        <em>
         posted by
        </em>
        Jenn
        <em>
         on
        </em>
        June 13, 2014
       </div>
      </div>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       <img alt="Strawberry Shortcake Bars" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3705" height="900" src="http://deliciouslysprinkled.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Strawberry-Shortcake-Bars-21.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       When I was a little girl, I wouldget so excited when I heard the ice cream truck coming down our street in the summer! My brother and I would always run into the house to get my mom so she could pay for our $0.25 cent ice cream treat. One of my favorite ice cream bars to get were the Good Humor Strawberry Shortcake. They were so delicious and the crumble topping on the outside of the ice cream bar was the best part.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       And now that we arefull swing intosummer, I thought I would make these Strawberry Shortcake Bars topped with a ton of the best part of those Good Humor bars, the strawberry crumble!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       I could just eat a handful or two of this delicious crumble, its amazing and I think I need to put it on everything like cookies, cakes, cupcakes, ice cream, yogurt whatever I am eating needs to have some strawberry vanilla crumble on it. Trust me its that good!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       <img alt="Strawberry Shortcake Bars" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3706" height="900" src="http://deliciouslysprinkled.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Strawberry-Shortcake-Bars-24.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <h4>
       <span style="color: #ffa7b4;">
        JUST A FEW BAKING TIPS:
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       <span id="more-1752">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <span style="color: #87d1d5;">
         
        </span>
       </strong>
       Keep an eye on the crumble when baking it, to make sure it doesnt burn! I checked and stirred mine at 5 minutes to make sure the crumble was not burning. After baking the crumble for 10 minutes, I put the whole pan in the freezer for another 10minutes to cool it down quickly so that I could SPRINKLEthe crumble on top of thefrosted bars.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong style="color: #000000; line-height: 1.5;">
        <span style="color: #87d1d5;">
         
        </span>
       </strong>
       To make it super easy for you to cut these bars into squares, line your 99 inch baking pan with aluminum foil, leaving enough overhang on the sides and spray the foil with non-stick cooking spray. Once your ready to serve, just lift the barsout of the pan, folding down the aluminumfoil sides. Then use a sharp knife to cut these bars into squares. SUPER EASY!
      </p>

         <h3 id="ingredients">
          Ingredients:
         </h3>
         <div class="ingredients">
          <h5>
           VANILLA BARS:
          </h5>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 box vanilla cake mix
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 large eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/3 cup vegetable oil
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h5>
           STRAWBERRY VANILLA CRUMBLE:
          </h5>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1
            <em>
             (3 oz.)
            </em>
            package strawberry gelatin mix, dry
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1
            <em>
             (3 oz.)
            </em>
            package vanilla INSTANT pudding mix, dry
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup all purpose flour, divided
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h5>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
            COOL WHIP FROSTING:
           </span>
          </h5>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 ounces cream cheese, softened
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup powdered sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon vanilla extract
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tub
            <em>
             (8 oz.)
            </em>
            Cool Whip, thawed
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
          Directions:
         </h3>
         <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ffa7b4;">
             PRE-STEP:
            </span>
           </strong>
           Preheat oven to 350F degrees. Line 9x9 cake pan with aluminum foil and spray with non-stick cooking spray. Set aside.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ffa7b4;">
             STEP 1:
            </span>
           </strong>
           In a large bowl, using an electric or stand mixer, mix together the cake mix, eggs, and vegetable oil until combined and dough forms.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ffa7b4;">
             STEP 2:
            </span>
           </strong>
           Press dough evenly into prepared pan using a spatula. Bake for 20 minutes or until edges are lightly brown. Let cool completely before frosting.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #87d1d5;">
            MAKE CRUMBLE:
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ffa7b4;">
             STEP 3:
            </span>
           </strong>
           In a small bowl combine the strawberry gelatin mix, 4 Tablespoons butter, and 1/2 cup flour. Using a fork to mix ingredients together until crumble forms. In another small bowl, combine the vanilla pudding mix, 4 Tablespoons butter, and 1/2 cup flour. Using a fork to mix ingredients together until crumble forms.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ffa7b4;">
             STEP 4:
            </span>
           </strong>
           Spread both the strawberry and vanilla crumble on to a lined cookie sheet with parchment paper or a silicon baking mat. Bake at 350F degrees for 10 minutes.*Check crumble at 5 minutes, stirring so that the crumble doesn't burn.
           <span style="color: #ff99cc;">
            <b>
            </b>
           </span>
           Place pan in freezer for 10 minutes to cool down the crumble quickly to sprinkle on top of bars.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #87d1d5;">
            MAKE FROSTING:
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ffa7b4;">
             STEP 5:
            </span>
           </strong>
           In a large bowl, using an electric or stand mixer, mix together the cream cheese, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and Cool Whip blending on high until frosting is light and fluffy.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #ffa7b4;">
            <strong>
             STEP 5:
            </strong>
           </span>
           Frost top of cooled bars and sprinkle a generous amount crumble on top. Cut into squares and enjoy!
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="notes">
          <p>
           <em>
            *Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
           </em>
          </p>
         </div>
