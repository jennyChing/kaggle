      <p>
       Somewhere between a law degree and struggling to decipher legal language with just a bachelors, there now lies another possibility: The master of legal studies.
      </p>
      <p>
       Not nearly a juris doctor but considerably beyond the coursework in undergraduate business law and government classes, this and similar degrees are an option for a range of professionals, from legislative staff and patent examiners to corrections officers and public advocates.
      </p>
      <p>
       They need to know the law, but they dont want to practice the law, said Megan M. LaBelle, an associate professor at Catholic University of Americas Columbus School of Law.
      </p>
      <p>
       MLS graduates will able to read, find, understand and apply the law in myriad situations, whether theyre small-business owners or court administrators, LaBelle said. Theres a market nationwide, but in Washington theres a substantial market.
      </p>
      <p>
       Catholics program welcomed its first class this fall. The University of Maryland Francis King Carey School of Law will inaugurate its master of science in law program in fall 2015. Both are geared at working professionals. The Catholic program is part-time with evening classes, allowing students to complete the program in as little as one year or to take as long as four. Marylands will be a two-year evening program.
      </p>
      <p>
       Law schools are changing, and people are changing, said Susan Krinsky, associate dean for students and student services at Carey. Law schools are beginning to recognize that were uniquely positioned to meet this need.
      </p>
      <p>
       The need, officials at both schools agree, is for professionals fluent in the knowledge and analytical skills needed to understand legalese, whether its a complex report or a conversation with attorneys.
      </p>
      <p>
       Both programs offer a chance to specialize. At Catholic, students can concentrate on compliance, human resources or intellectual property. Maryland will offer specialties in crisis management or environmental or health care law.
      </p>
      <p>
       Maryland selected its specialty tracks based on demand in law schools for that training. They also are fields where people would benefit from having some legal training, said Barbara Gotrum, the schools associate dean for academic affairs.
      </p>
      <p>
       Graduates will not be eligible to take bar exams to become licensed to practice, nor will they be able to transfer credits from the MLS program to the juris doctor program. For that reason, LaBelle encourages students to think carefully before enrolling.
      </p>
