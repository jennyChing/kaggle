      <header class="entry-header wrapper">
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="entry-category">
         <a href="http://www.flamingotoes.com/category/jewelry/">

       <h1 class="entry-title single-title">
        <span>
         Anthro Knockoff  Akela Necklace
        </span>
       </h1>
      </header>
      <div class="entry-media">
      </div>
      <div class="wrapper">
       <div class="grids">
        <div class="grid-8">
         <div class="single-box clearfix entry-content">
          <p>
           Anthropologie is such a great source
           <span style="text-decoration: line-through;">
            to steal ideas from
           </span>
           for inspiration. One of my favorite things to do late at night is browse through the Anthro site with a cup of hot tea. Gets my brain going.
          </p>
          <p>
           Much to my delight Anthro just added a darling little fabric and bead necklace that I just had to knockoff!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1018005.jpg" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Heres the Anthro version:
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="435" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Akela.jpeg" title="Akela" width="290"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I love the style of this necklace but the color, not so much. I dont want anything named Guacamole for the color hanging around my neck. I dont have the style to pull that off.
          </p>
          <p>
           Second, according to the description, this little baby is 72 long. That would go down to my knees. No thanks. Never fear. Anthro Knockoff Girl is here!
          </p>
          <p>
           Ok, that was weird. But the necklace that resulted is pretty darn cute, and a much better fit for me in size and color. The beauty of creating things ourselves  we get it the way
           <em>
            WE
           </em>
           want it.
          </p>
          <p>
           One of the other things I like about this necklace is that you dont need any jewelry hardware or tools. Just a sewing machine. Ready to get started? This one wont take long, I promise!
          </p>
          <p>
           Heres what youll need:
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2317 aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017981.jpg" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Fabric for necklace and ruffles  I used a sort of sheer fabric I had on hand
           </li>
           <li>
            Beads  3 sizes. Buy the cheapies  they dont show. The largest bead I used was 1/2 diameter, then two smaller sizes.
           </li>
           <li>
            Thread to match
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Cut your fabric into long pieces  measuring total of around 90 long by 2. Mine were 2 pieces that I sewed together in the middle. Thats no big deal, well just hide the seam in a knot.
          </p>
          <p>
           Cut out your circles  2 1/2 diameter. I used 22 but you would want to cut out a few more in case you want it a little rufflier.
          </p>
          <p>
           Do any piecing of your long strip first. Fold the strip in half, longways, and sew together using a 1/4 seam. You might need to adjust this if you are using different sized beads. You want your casing to be just a little wider than your largest bead.
          </p>
          <p>
           Get ready to turn your casing inside out. You can either sew a ribbon in one end, then as you sew your casing, keep the ribbon running through the center so that you can just pull the ribbon to turn it inside out.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2318 aligncenter" height="480" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017983.jpg" width="640"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Or you can pin a safety pin in one end and feed that through the casing. That is what I did.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2319 aligncenter" height="480" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017985.jpg" width="640"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Place a large bead inside the casing. Fold your tube in half and move the bead to the bottom center. Make a tight knot on either side of the bead. If you have a seam in the middle, try and put your seam inside the knot so it doesnt show. You might need to twist the fabric a bit as you knot it to keep the knot as small as possible.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2320 aligncenter" height="480" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017986.jpg" width="640"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Add a couplelarge beads on either side of this bead, knotting after them.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2321 aligncenter" height="480" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017987.jpg" width="640"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Continue adding beads on each side of your center bead, keeping the number of beads on each side even. Here is the pattern I used  feel free to do your own thing. ;)
          </p>
          <p>
           Center Bead:3 Large Beads on either side of this: 6 Medium Beads on either side: 3 Small Beads on either side. So 7 Large Beads, 12 Medium Beads &amp; 6 Small Beads for a total of 25.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-2323 aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017991.jpg" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Turn the ends of your fabric tube to the inside of the tube  sew the ends closed.
          </p>
          <p>
           Time to sew on your circles! Start by folding a circle in half, then in half again.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2324 aligncenter" height="480" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017994.jpg" width="640"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            (In a weird optical illusion I actually look tan here. Must be the flash.)
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           Sew the corner of the fold to one of the knots on the right side of the necklace. Mine is started on the knot below the 5th bead on the right side.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2325 aligncenter" height="480" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017995.jpg" width="626"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Keep sewing folded circles to the fabric over the beads and the knots, keeping them to the front of the necklace so it lays flat. Sew about 12 circles over a space of three beads. Change directions that the ends of the circle point to fill in the area.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2326 aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017996.jpg" width="503"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Repeat this process on the lower left side of the necklace  start sewing below the 8th bead down on the left side, covering 3 beads of space again.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2327 aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1017997.jpg" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           To finish off the necklace and give it a little more of store-bought look, I pressed the ends of the necklace that didnt have beads into folds, like the
           <a href="http://www.anthropologie.com/anthro/catalog/productdetail.jsp?subCategoryId=&amp;id=18994939&amp;catId=HOME-AUTUMN&amp;pushId=HOME-AUTUMN&amp;popId=HOME&amp;sortProperties=&amp;navCount=0&amp;navAction=jump&amp;fromCategoryPage=true&amp;selectedProductSize=&amp;selectedProductSize1=&amp;color=098&amp;colorName=GUACAMOLE&amp;isSubcategory=&amp;isProduct=true&amp;isBigImage=true&amp;templateType=templateA&amp;tabStyle=Info" target="_blank">
            Anthro version
           </a>
           . I also kind of smooshed up the ruffles, so they werent so puffy.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2328 aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1018000.jpg" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Try the necklace on and tie it to the length youd like. Show off to everyone else so they can seehow cool you are.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2333 aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1018010.jpg" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1018005.jpg" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2330 aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1018006.jpg" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="" class=" wp-image-2331 aligncenter" height="480" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1018007.jpg" width="640"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-2332 aligncenter" height="600" src="http://www.flamingotoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/P1018008.jpg" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I made the necklace with what I had on hand  but the bead package I used was originally about 3 dollars and if you bought the fabric youd need less than a 1/4 of a yard.
          </p>
          <p>
           A little better than $38 huh??? ;)
          </p>
