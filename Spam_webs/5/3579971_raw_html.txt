       <font color="#FFFFFF" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
        <h1>
         The Universe as mapped by SDSS and WMAP
        </h1>
        <p>
         <a href="sdss3snap1.jpg">
          <img align="right" src="sdss3snap1.jpg" width="100"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="sdss3snap3.jpg">
          <img align="right" src="sdss3snap3.jpg" width="100"/>
         </a>
         The
         <a href="http://www.sdss.org">
          Sloan Digital Sky Survey
         </a>
         is a
huge and important project to map, in a quarter of the sky, as much of universe as is visible
from earth. The SDSS releases data periodically, and will hopefully be
complete in a few more years.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <p>
          <b>
           Download:
          </b>
          <a href="sdss4wmap.zip">
           sdss4wmap.zip
          </a>
          (for Windows and Linux)
          <a href="sdss4wmap_mac.tar.gz">
           sdss4wmap_mac.tar.gz
          </a>
          (for Macs) : 440 000 galaxies (375 000 main sequence, 65 000 large red galaxies) and 70 000 quasars from
          <b>
           SDSS Data Release 4
          </b>
          , plus the cosmic microwave background as measured by the NASA/WMAP science team. Size: 55 Mb. You can find the movie made with this on the
          <a href="../sloanmovie">
           Cosmus movie
          </a>
          page or on
          <a href="http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=-8252705102362324792&amp;q=sdss">
           Google Video
          </a>
          . Made in October 2005.
         </p>
         <p>
          [Caution: not many machines can handle this data set, so try the DR3 version - below - first.]
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="sdss3snap2.jpg">
           <img align="right" src="sdss3snap2.jpg" width="100"/>
          </a>
          <b>
           Download:
          </b>
          <a href="sdss3wmap.zip">
           sdss3wmap.zip
          </a>
          300 000 galaxies
and 49 000 quasars from SDSS Release 3, plus the cosmic microwave background.  Includes Milky Way and Local Group. Size: 48 Mb. (Note: updated 2 Nov 05 with some galaxy texture corrections).
         </p>
        </ul>
        <div align="centeR">
         <p>
          <img align="center" src="sloanbutterfly.jpg" width="700"/>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Mark Subbarao,
         <a href="http://people.cs.uchicago.edu/~dinoj">
          Dinoj Surendran
         </a>
         , and Randy Landsberg have made a 3d model of the galaxies and quasars found by the
SDSS. Currently there are 250 000 galaxies and 40 000 quasars. Also
included is the cosmic microwave background as mapped by the Wilkinson
Microwave Anisotropy Probe.
        </p>
        <p>
         This model is interactive, which means you can fly around in it
whereever you want, seeing both galaxies close up and the large scale
structure of the universe.
        </p>
