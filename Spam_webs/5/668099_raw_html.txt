       <h1 class="xlHeader">
        "Batman v Superman" Revealed: 5 Things Time Warner, Inc. Doesn't Want You to Know
       </h1>
       <p class="articleMeta">
        <span class="vcard byline">
         By
         <a class="qsAdd qs-source-iapsitlnk0000002" href="http://my.fool.com/profile/tmfmilehigh/info.aspx">
          <span content="Tim Beyers" itemprop="author">
           Tim Beyers
          </span>
         </a>
         <meta content="1286" itemprop="contributor"/>
         |
         <a class="qsAdd qs-source-iapsitlnk0000003" href="/author/1286/index.aspx">
          More Articles
         </a>
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="dateline">
         September 24, 2014
        </span>
        |
        <span class="comments">
         <a href="#commentsBoxAnchor">
          Comments (16)
         </a>
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="image small">
         <img alt="" src="http://g.foolcdn.com/editorial/images/145741/batman-vs-superman-dawn-of-justice_large.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p class="caption">
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman
         </em>
         may not be the movie some think it is. Source: Warner Bros.
        </p>
        <p>
         Make no mistake. Executives are betting that
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman: Dawn of Justice
         </em>
         can be for
         <strong>
          Time Warner, Inc
         </strong>
         .  (
         <span class="ticker">
          NYSE:
          <a class="qsAdd qs-source-isssitthv0000001" data-id="205832" href="http://caps.fool.com/Ticker/TWX.aspx">
           TWX
          </a>
         </span>
         <a class="addToWatchListIcon qsAdd qs-source-iwlsitbut0000010" href="http://my.fool.com/watchlist/add?ticker=TWX" title="Add TWX to My Watchlist">
         </a>
         )  what Marvel's
         <em>
          The Avengers
         </em>
         was for
         <strong>
          Disney
         </strong>
         (
         <span class="ticker">
          NYSE:
          <a class="qsAdd qs-source-isssitthv0000001" data-id="203310" href="http://caps.fool.com/Ticker/DIS.aspx">
           DIS
          </a>
         </span>
         <a class="addToWatchListIcon qsAdd qs-source-iwlsitbut0000010" href="http://my.fool.com/watchlist/add?ticker=DIS" title="Add DIS to My Watchlist">
         </a>
         ) .
        </p>
        <p>
         And why not? Thanks to
         <em>
          The Avengers
         </em>
         , Marvel movies possess a magnetic brand audiences are finding tough to resist. All four of the films to follow it have earned at least $600 million at the worldwide box office. Mixing in tens of millions in DVD, Blu-ray, and download sales has to make this one of the most profitable film franchises in Disney history. Of course Warner wants that sort of action.
        </p>
        <p>
         But can
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman
         </em>
         deliver as
         <em>
          The Avengers
         </em>
         did? Everything depends on how well the final product resonates with audiences, and given history and Warner's strategy, an
         <em>
          Avengers
         </em>
         -sized win is anything but assured. Here are five things you might not know about
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman: Dawn of Justice
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1.
         </strong>
         <strong>
          This movie will make money whether you show up or not.
         </strong>
         Remember the concerns over Warner's soaring
         <a href="http://www.fool.com/investing/general/2013/06/15/good-news-for-time-warner-stock-investors-man-of-s.aspx">
          budget for
          <em>
           Man of Steel
          </em>
         </a>
         ? Turns out they were unfounded, but not because of the box office gross. According to reporting by U.K. newspaper
         <em>
          The Independent
         </em>
         , Warner covered $170 million of the film's reported $225 million production costs with product placement. It's a fair bet that
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman: Dawn of Justice
         </em>
         will employ a similar strategy, ensuring a low hurdle to earning profits.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. The Justice League will come to TV first.
         </strong>
         The CW hit
         <em>
          Arrow
         </em>
         has introduced
         <a href="http://www.fool.com/investing/general/2014/02/14/forget-batman-vs-superman-this-is-now-dcs-signatur.aspx">
          more DC Comics characters
         </a>
         in two seasons than Warner Bros. Studios has introduced in over three decades of making comic book films. Green Arrow, Black Canary, Deadshot, Merlyn, Deathstroke... the list goes on. Now that
         <em>
          The Flash
         </em>
         is coming to The CW this fall -- and with him, DC superheroes The Atom and Firestorm -- it seems likely that
         <a href="http://www.fool.com/investing/general/2014/05/11/the-key-question-for-time-warner-inc-now-that-the.aspx">
          some form of the Justice League
         </a>
         will show up on TV before director Zack Snyder has a chance to bring his own interpretation to the big screen.
        </p>
        <div class="image small">
         <img alt="" src="http://g.foolcdn.com/editorial/images/145741/arrow-and-the-flash-v2_large.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p class="caption">
         <em>
          Arrow
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          The Flash
         </em>
         will air on back-to-back nights this fall on The CW. Source: The CW.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3.
         </strong>
         <strong>
          Warner and DC don't want this to feel like a comic book movie.
         </strong>
         When Snyder first spoke of his plan for
         <em>
          Man of Steel
         </em>
         , he made clear that his intention was to make a more serious film. Critics didn't like that and pummeled
         <em>
          Man of Steel
         </em>
         with poor reviews. Don't expect that to influence Snyder's decisions with
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman: Dawn of Justice
         </em>
         . Reports are that Warner wants to continue with a somber tone in order to differentiate DC movies from their more fun-loving Marvel counterparts. That the studio hasn't said much in response suggests that executives aim to preserve hope that
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman
         </em>
         will be more light-hearted than its predecessor, even if it isn't.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Both Batman and Superman have failed at the box office in years past.
         </strong>
         We often forget that Christopher Nolan helped resurrect the Batman film franchise eight years after it had been all but buried by the 1997 flop,
         <em>
          Batman and Robin
         </em>
         . Before that,
         <em>
          Superman III
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          Superman IV: The Quest for Peace
         </em>
         tarred Christopher Reeve's otherwise acclaimed run as the Man of the Steel. Both films suffered terrible reviews and lackluster box office results. Putting
         <a href="http://www.fool.com/investing/general/2013/07/27/time-warner-just-became-the-worlds-finest-stock.aspx">
          the "World's Finest" heroes
         </a>
         together in a film doesn't guarantee success.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5.
          <em>
           Batman vs. Superman
          </em>
          isn't really about Batman or Superman.
         </strong>
         Rather, Snyder is using
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman: Dawn of Justice
         </em>
         as a staging ground for new characters. Gal Gadot will appear as Wonder Woman.
         <a href="http://www.fool.com/investing/general/2014/04/26/heres-how-big-a-risk-the-batman-vs-superman-movie.aspx">
          Ray Fisher will appear as Cyborg
         </a>
         . Jason Momoa is rumored to appear as Aquaman. Warner is betting that those who aren't in-the-know fans will show up to see Batman and Superman, and then become interested in other characters. A risky but appropriate strategy given that Warner badly needs new cinematic franchises, and studio boss Kevin Tsujihara has made clear that DC characters are some of its main go-tos.
        </p>
        <p>
         "We have
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman
         </em>
         coming out in [2016], but there's going to be, in the coming months, a lot of announcements regarding ... the future movies, television, games, and consumer products that are going to be coming from DC," Tsujihara said at a Sept. 2013 investor conference. He also noted that
         <a href="http://www.fool.com/investing/general/2013/09/22/is-the-dc-movie-machine-revving-up.aspx">
          DC Comics properties would form the "foundation"
         </a>
         of the studio's proposed slate of 12-14 new films a year.
         <em>
          Batman vs. Superman: Dawn of Justice
         </em>
         begins that process.
