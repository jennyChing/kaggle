     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn">
      2010 Burbank Ranch Grenache Blanc Summer Time
     </h1>
     <div class="grub-byline">
      Wine Review by
      <a href="https://plus.google.com/108922357065455665505" rel="author">
       Matt Browne
      </a>
       January 31, 2014
     </div>
     <div class="yui3-g grub-statPairs">
      <div class="yui3-u">
       <span class="label">

      <div class="yui3-u">

      </div>
      <div class="yui3-u">
       <span class="label">

       </span>

       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="yui3-u">

     <div class="grub-mainPostContent">
      <p>
       Let me just say that the
       <a href="http://www.burbankranch.com/wines/2010-summer-time-grenache-blanc.php">
        2010 Burbank Ranch Grenache Blanc
       </a>
       is off the hook. You heard me. Its so tasty that its $27.00 price point doesnt even phase me; Ill take a whole case, please!
      </p>
      <p>
       This wine is quite aromatic and just another reason to enjoy it at room temperature. Seriously, its as if my glass is swirling with flower petals. Somewhat typical of Grenache Blanc, there are hints of apricot on the palate. But this wine is anything but fruity. Honey and jasmine round out the flavor quite nicely.
      </p>
      <p>
       I dig me some G-blanc and I think its crushing on me too. #justsaying
      </p>
      <p>
       Like most whites, its perfect for a warm sunny day but this is definitely a
       <em>
        winter white.
       </em>
       So give it a try, like right now at room temperature. Especially if your New Years Resolution is to eat healthy. This wine will toss your salad er, go with your salad. You know what I mean. Seasonal fruits from the farmers market (or your garden), some crushed pine nuts and peppery greens are all you need.
      </p>
      <p>
       Oh, and to invite me over. That is a must!
      </p>
      <p>
       Burbank Ranch is a relatively new winery to the ever-booming Central Coast wine scene. But these noobs know what they are doing. They are currently located in downtown Paso Robles but
       <a href="http://www.burbankranch.com/winegrowing/winery-construction.php">
        in the midst of building
       </a>
       a pretty bad ass looking winery and tasting room over at their Templeton property. I have only visited them a few times but everyone there is super fun and very friendly, so I see myself going back. A lot.
      </p>
      <p>
       I also love their use of
       <a href="http://instagram.com/burbankranch">
        Instagram
       </a>
       as you can tell by the photos in this post.
      </p>
      <p>
       The vineyard is
       <a href="http://www.sipcertified.org">
        SIP Certified,
       </a>
       and as you know by following Mr. Stone and myself, taking care of this round ball of magicEarthis a big priority of ours. It is for Burbank Ranch too!
      </p>
      <p>
       We thought we were doing a conscientious job of managing our land and practicing sustainability, but we wanted to test ourselves, so were applied for SIP. We love our beautiful property and we are meticulous in our vineyard management. The SIP certification verified that we met the high standards of sustainability we are aiming for, says Melody Burbank.
      </p>
      <p>
       I look forward to visiting their new winery and tasting room so I can see their sustainable efforts first hand. If youre going to sip, sip sustainable.
      </p>
      <p>
       Cheers!
      </p>
