       <p>
        <span>
         <tt>
          Hunger  its the one thing that can throw off almost any effort at healthier eating, and especially make it difficult to stick to a weight loss plan.
         </tt>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        Luckily, an exceptional technique that utilizes your mind, coupled with tapping of the acupressure points on the body, provide a very handy way to quell hunger when it hits.
       </p>
       <p>
        <tt>
         Its called Emotional Freedom technique (EFT) and is used widely by a wide variety of health professionals and laypeople alike.
        </tt>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         EFT is a powerfulnew method based on the discovery that emotional trauma contributes greatly to disease.
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Scientific studies have shown that EFT is able to rapidly reduce the emotional impact of memories and incidents that trigger emotional distress. Once the distress is reduced or removed, the body can often rebalance itself, and accelerate healing.
       </p>
       <p>
        <tt>
        </tt>
        <tt>
         EFT falls under the heading of energy psychology.
        </tt>
        Energy Psychology provides simple methods for shifting brain patterns that lead to unwanted thoughts, feelings, and actions. Drawing from ancient healing traditions, it has been called psychological acupuncture without the needles. It
        <tt>
         combines consciously speaking key phrases with tapping of specific endpoints of what are known as theacupuncture meridian energy channelsmapped on the body by oriental medicine.
        </tt>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://watchfit.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Cutting-Hunger-with-Emotional-Freedom-Technique.jpg">
         <img alt="Cutting Hunger with Emotional Freedom Technique" class="size-full wp-image-7579 aligncenter" height="471" src="http://watchfit.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Cutting-Hunger-with-Emotional-Freedom-Technique.jpg" width="700"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span>
         Using EFT correctly, signals are sent to the brain that changedysfunctional responses
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <span>
         In other words, if youre hungry orcraving something that isnt on your healthy eating plan, usingEFT can
         <a href="http://watchfit.com/diet/reduce-food-cravings-reducing-stress/" target="_blank">
          take the hunger and cravings away
         </a>
          or at the very least,will much decrease them. Or, if you are stressed and your usualanswer is to use food to calm your stress, you may not actuallybe hungry in the physical sense, but we all know that stress eatingis a common problem.EFT will create a release of stress that you can physically feelafter just one round. When the stress is relieved then it is fareasier to make a better choice than to go and overeat.
        </span>
       </p>
       <h3>
        Energy Psychology has been establishing itself as one of the most promising clinical innovations on the horizon. Variations of EFT include TFT (Thought Field Therapy), and TAT (Tapas Acupuncture Technique), among numerous other formats.
       </h3>
       <p>
        <tt>
         To go into a little more depth: in Oriental medicine doctors use acupuncture needles to create a healing effect on the body by placing the needles on acupuncture points in such a way as to either increase energy flow or decrease energy flow. This energy is called Qi (pronounced chee) and probably the best translation would be the life force.
        </tt>
       </p>
       <p>
        <tt>
        </tt>
        <tt>
         Doctors of Oriental medicine have used acupuncture needles instead of anesthesia when performing surgery very successfully. Its amazing that with just the acupuncture needles placed in the correct points that no anesthetic drug is needed! Now if thats possible, hopefully it will help you to trust that tapping on other specific acupuncture points can stop hunger and cravings.
        </tt>
       </p>
       <p>
        <tt>
        </tt>
        <tt>
         EFT sounds too simple to be true, but there is a tremendously high percentage of success with (to name a few) addiction problems, traumatic memory relief, abuse issues, phobias, psychosomatic illnesses, pain, and other physical and emotional symptoms.
        </tt>
       </p>
