       <h1 class="entry-title">
        How To Take On A New Sport
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://clippedoutdoors.com/how-to-take-on-a-new-sport/" rel="bookmark" title="6:00 am">
         <span class="entry-date">
          August 22, 2013
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="comments-link">
         <span class="meta-sep">
          |
         </span>
         <a href="http://clippedoutdoors.com/how-to-take-on-a-new-sport/#comments">
          2 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         If you spent your childhood with your nose rooted firmly into the spine of a book, we probably should be best friends. Although my mother would chase my siblings and I outside when the weather was nice, I was basically the most unathletic child in my grade. I was a pale and skinny child with big glasses and a fondness for eating twinkies while my more badass girl friends were out playing baseball at recess with the boys. If you were to ask me to run more than a kilometer, I would have gravely blinked at you from my golden-rimmed glasses.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-685">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         There was a huge moment in grade eight when everything changed; I was forced to run a long distance race in our yearly track and field events and by some luck of the draw, I won. I remember being stunned that I could be good at anything remotely athletic, so I decided to keep going.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_686" style="width: 312px">
         <a href="http://clippedoutdoors.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/xc.jpg">
          <img alt="xc" class="size-full wp-image-686" height="604" src="http://clippedoutdoors.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/xc.jpg" width="302"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          First cross country meet in high school.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Fast forward to today and I am proud to say that I have not stopped! I just completed a 100 Mile trek in North Dakota this spring and I spend every spare second I have biking, swimming, climbing and running.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Maybe I lost you at the idea of running or you are thinking of trying out a new sport but are unsure of how to start. Here are a few things that I have found helpful as I began my journey into new sports in the past:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Start slowly and manage your expectations
         </strong>
          When I started running, I didnt expect to be able to run a marathon in the first week. I knew that I needed to take my time, walk during the runs and acquire a base knowledge about the sport.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Find a coach or mentor
         </strong>
          One of the first things I did when I began running was to find a running group and, much later, a team. Not only will you have a support network to keep you consistent and energized about your sport, but also will have guidance and advice as you progress. CLIPPED Outdoors is focused on mentorship and encouragement. If you want to get into an outdoor sport, drop us an email and we will be happy to help you get started!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://clippedoutdoors.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/947307_10152806194205704_613591884_n.jpg">
          <img alt="947307_10152806194205704_613591884_n" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-689" height="465" src="http://clippedoutdoors.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/947307_10152806194205704_613591884_n.jpg" width="414"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. Invest in gear
         </strong>
          One of the biggest mistakes I made early on was running in the wrong shoes. For me, the difference was incredible once I began investing in quality gear. It may seem tempting to use whatever you already have, but it is important to have a safe experience when it comes to sports. If buying is not an option, consider renting gear or borrowing from a friend.
        </p>
        <p>
         Just remember to build a support network and to be kind to yourself. Nothing good comes easily, but like all things you need to work for, it is a rewarding experience.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What are some of the steps you take when learning a new sport?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Written by Jessica Kuepfer
        </p>
