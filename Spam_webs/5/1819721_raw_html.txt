      <h1 class="omc-post-heading-standard">
       Are You Making These 5 Common Content Marketing Mistakes?
      </h1>
      <div addthis:title="Are You Making These 5 Common Content Marketing Mistakes? " addthis:url="http://www.verticalresponse.com/blog/making-5-common-content-marketing-mistakes/" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style addthis_">
       <a class="addthis_button_facebook">
       </a>
       <a class="addthis_button_twitter">
       </a>
       <a class="addthis_button_email">
       </a>
       <a class="addthis_button_pinterest_share">
       </a>
       <a class="addthis_button_compact">
       </a>
       <a class="addthis_counter addthis_bubble_style">
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       There are over 27,000,000 pieces of content being shared every day, but did you know that 60-70 percent of content produced by B2B marketing goes unused? Why? Most often its due to common content marketing mistakes like not knowing who youre producing content for, trying too hard to sell with your content, and other content mishaps.
      </p>
      <p>
       If youre creating any type of content for your business, find out if youre making any of these 5 common content marketing mistakes and what you can do to clean up your content act.
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Dont Know Your Audience
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       One of our biggest challenges is to create and deliver content that people actually want to consume. With so much competing for their attention, if your content cant break through the noise, itll never be seen.
      </p>
      <p>
       To create content thats valuable, you need to know what your prospects and customers care about. You can get started by creating a list of the traits your very best customers and ideal buyers have. Identify what their biggest pain points are, what their concerns are in relation to your product/services and what they hope to accomplish. Too often, we fall into the routine of creating content thats all about the benefits of our product and services instead of helping a potential buyer. Once you identify the types (or buyer personas) youre interested in
       <a class="glossaryLink" href="http://www.verticalresponse.com/blog/glossary/targeting/">
        targeting
       </a>
       , you can better create content with the intention of helping these folks.
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Trying too Hard to Sell, Sell, Sell
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       Around this time last year, we saw Jay Baer speak at
       <a href="http://nmxlive.com/2014-lv/" target="_blank">
        New Media Expo
       </a>
       . Baer blew the roof off by preaching a revolutionary thought to bloggers and marketers in attendance:
       <a href="http://www.verticalresponse.com/blog/start-helping-stop-selling-content-marketing/" target="_blank">
        Stop selling and start helping
       </a>
       . Wait, what? You want us to stop selling? Hows that going to work?
      </p>
      <p>
       Baer outlined it in his book,
       <i>
        Youtility
       </i>
       . He explained that Youtility is the concept of providing valuable content for your readers and customers, to the point where your company becomes valued, trusted, and synonymous with being useful. So when the time comes to make a purchase, your company is the obvious choice.
      </p>
      <p>
       As an example, Baer shared a story about Marcus Sheridan from River Pools and Spas. Back when the economy began to go south,
       <b>
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         <a href="http://www.riverpoolsandspas.com/" target="_blank">
          River Pools and Spas
         </a>
        </span>
       </b>
       was hit hard, as not many people install pools during tough economic times. However, the folks at River Pools and Spas didnt give up. They got smart and started answering questions
       <a href="http://www.riverpoolsandspas.com/blog/">
        <b>
         via their blog
        </b>
       </a>
       that customers and potential buyers had. Then they turned those blog posts into a popular
       <a href="http://www.riverpoolsandspas.com/fiberglass-pool-buying-guide-free/" target="_blank">
        <b>
         eBook
        </b>
       </a>
       . In just four years, River Pools and Spas grew their company from nearly going out of business to the largest pool seller in the US. Based on all the information they provide,75% of their customers complete a purchase without ever talking to a real person!
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Dont Create Enough, or Create Too Much
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       Many of us struggle to have enough content to share on our social networks, in our emails and in our newsletters. Then there are those of us who blog 6 times or more a week and have content pouring out of every corner of our website, social networks and emails. So whats the magic content frequency number? There isnt one thats right for everyone, but keep in mind that
       <a href="http://www.verticalresponse.com/blog/quality-vs-quantity-content-marketing-pros-pick-sides/" target="_blank">
        quality trumps quantity
       </a>
       . You can and should create different types of content from short tweets, digestible blog posts, longer case studies/whitepapers/guides, infographics and videos and even eBooks if youve got the resources. By producing a variety of content types, you can also repurpose content into different formats, say by taking a data rich blog post and turning it into an infographic. Or take a few guides or whitepapers with a common theme and turn them into an eBook.
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Trying to Do it All Alone
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       While you may be a sole proprietor, it doesnt mean you have to create everything yourself. You can share content that others have created, as long as you
       <a href="http://www.verticalresponse.com/blog/are-you-a-content-thief-and-dont-know-it/" target="_blank">
        provide proper source credit and attribution
       </a>
       . By sharing other points of view, opinions and expertise, you can grow the quality and quantity of content you have to help your audience. You can also recruit guest writers or bloggers to help contribute to your efforts. Just ensure they create unique, high quality posts that add value for your customers and potential buyers. Another way to create content, while not having to be the expert, is by interviewing someone. You can interview a supplier, a new vendor, a customer or an employee. Weve got 17 more ideas for creating content in
       <a href="http://www.verticalresponse.com/blog/17-questions-content-marketing-program/">
        this post
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Not Measuring Results
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       If youre not measuring your content marketing results, dont stick your head in the sand and hide, youre not alone. Stats indicate that only 15 percent measure results. Its important to know whats working (so you can do more of it), and whats not so you can either ditch it, or tweak it to perform better the next time around. To measure your results, there are lots of performance metrics, but to start, you can focus on the big four:
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li>
        Consumption  Page views, video views, document views, downloads and social chatter
       </li>
       <li>
        Sharing  Like, shares, tweets, +1s and pins, forwards, number of inbound
        <a class="glossaryLink" href="http://www.verticalresponse.com/blog/glossary/links/">
         links
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        Lead Generation  Form completions and downloads, email and blog subscriptions, blog comments
       </li>
       <li>
        Sales  Look at both online and offline sales that are influenced by content
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       Many of these results are available in free tools like
       <a href="http://www.google.com/analytics/" target="_blank">
        Google Analytics
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.inc.com/profile/facebook?lc=int_mb_1001" target="_blank">
        Facebook
       </a>
       , Twitter, YouTube, your email service provider, content management systems (like WordPress), as well as your own internal data from sources like your CRM system (if youre using one).
      </p>
      <p>
       If youve been making any of these common content marketing mistakes, youre now armed with the knowledge to fix whats broken.
      </p>
