       <article class="full-post post">
        <header class="post-header">
         <h1 class="post-title">
          Recipe:
          <em>
           Sweet Potato Hash with Sausage &amp; Eggs
          </em>
         </h1>
         <p class="brand-color post-subtitle">
          Breakfast Recipes from The Kitchn
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="post-body branded-links">
         <span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
          <div class="image-wrapper mt-image-center" style="max-width: 540.0px;">
           <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/thekitchn/recipe/brunch-recipe-sweet-potato-hash-with-sausage-eggs-162997">
            <span class="image lazy-image" data-alt="15 Best Make-Ahead Recipes for Brunch">
             <div class="pad" style="padding-bottom: 124.25925925925927%;">
              <span data-src="http://p-fst1.pixstatic.com/506b336cd9127e30de00196b._w.540_s.fit_.jpg">
              </span>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="15 Best Make-Ahead Recipes for Brunch" itemprop="image" src="http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/506b336cd9127e30de00196b._w.540_s.fit_.jpg"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
            </span>
           </a>
           <div class="image-controls">
            <a class="js-custom-pinterest-button custom-pinterest-button--big" data-social-action="lazy image pin" data-social-network="pinterest" data-social-target="http://www.thekitchn.com/recipe-sweet-potato-hash-with-sausage-and-eggs-breakfast-recipes-from-the-kitchn-162997" data-track-social="true" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.thekitchn.com%2Frecipe-sweet-potato-hash-with-sausage-and-eggs-breakfast-recipes-from-the-kitchn-162997&amp;description=Recipe%3A%20Sweet%20Potato%20Hash%20with%20Sausage%20%26amp%3B%20Eggs%20%E2%80%94%20Breakfast%20Recipes%20from%20The%20Kitchn&amp;media=http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/506b336cd9127e30de00196b._w.540_s.fit_.jpg">
             Pin it
            </a>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           I have told you that brunch is my
           <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/thekitchn/recipe-roundup/breakfast-casseroles-beyond-15-recipes-for-brunch-recipe-roundup-160783">
            favorite meal of the day
           </a>
           , and winter is perhaps the high point for brunch. But while I am looking forward to these sleepy morning meals, I have to admit that many brunch recipes are too sweet for me. If this
           <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/thekitchn/-recipe-monkey-bread-with-bourbon-creme-anglaise-162150">
            sweet monkey bread with a gooey glaze
           </a>
           showed up it certainly wouldn't go to waste, but I would need something else too, something to nourish and satisfy. I need something savory to start the day off right.
          </p>
          <p>
           The other requirement for a good brunch recipe, of course, is that it be easy to make ahead. Because who can roll out dough and fry potatoes
           <em>
            before
           </em>
           breakfast? Not me. So I must tell you about my make-ahead favorite recipe for a big family brunch: savory, garlicky sweet potato hash, roasted with sausage and rosemary, and baked the next day with eggs on top.
          </p>
          <div class="image-wrapper mt-image-center" style="max-width: 540.0px;">
           <span class="image lazy-image" data-alt="">
            <div class="pad" style="padding-bottom: 116.11111111111111%;">
             <span data-src="http://p-fst1.pixstatic.com/51bb8f46dbd0cb1ee00000da._w.540_s.fit_.jpg">
             </span>
             <noscript>
              <img alt="" src="http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/51bb8f46dbd0cb1ee00000da._w.540_s.fit_.jpg"/>
             </noscript>
            </div>
           </span>
           <div class="image-controls">
            <a class="js-custom-pinterest-button custom-pinterest-button--big" data-social-action="lazy image pin" data-social-network="pinterest" data-social-target="http://www.thekitchn.com/recipe-sweet-potato-hash-with-sausage-and-eggs-breakfast-recipes-from-the-kitchn-162997" data-track-social="true" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.thekitchn.com%2Frecipe-sweet-potato-hash-with-sausage-and-eggs-breakfast-recipes-from-the-kitchn-162997&amp;description=Recipe%3A%20Sweet%20Potato%20Hash%20with%20Sausage%20%26amp%3B%20Eggs%20%E2%80%94%20Breakfast%20Recipes%20from%20The%20Kitchn&amp;media=http://p-fst2.pixstatic.com/51bb8f46dbd0cb1ee00000da._w.540_s.fit_.jpg">
             Pin it
            </a>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           This entire thing is made ahead. (No chopping sweet potatoes at 9am.) In the morning you spread the refrigerated hash in a big pan, and crack a few eggs on top. Slide it into a hot oven, and go make coffee. When you come back the potatoes will be hot and crisped, and the eggs just baked. Easy, nourishing, delicious  your kitchen will smell amazing, and you'll be ready to serve a crowd.
           <br/>
           And don't worry  you'll still have plenty of room for something sweet, too.
          </p>
          <div class="image-wrapper mt-image-center" style="max-width: 540.0px;">
           <span class="image lazy-image" data-alt="">
            <div class="pad" style="padding-bottom: 86.29629629629629%;">
             <span data-src="http://p-fst1.pixstatic.com/51bb8f44d9127e24f200013f._w.540_s.fit_.jpg">
             </span>
             <noscript>
              <img alt="" src="http://p-fst1.pixstatic.com/51bb8f44d9127e24f200013f._w.540_s.fit_.jpg"/>
             </noscript>
            </div>
           </span>
           <div class="image-controls">
            <a class="js-custom-pinterest-button custom-pinterest-button--big" data-social-action="lazy image pin" data-social-network="pinterest" data-social-target="http://www.thekitchn.com/recipe-sweet-potato-hash-with-sausage-and-eggs-breakfast-recipes-from-the-kitchn-162997" data-track-social="true" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.thekitchn.com%2Frecipe-sweet-potato-hash-with-sausage-and-eggs-breakfast-recipes-from-the-kitchn-162997&amp;description=Recipe%3A%20Sweet%20Potato%20Hash%20with%20Sausage%20%26amp%3B%20Eggs%20%E2%80%94%20Breakfast%20Recipes%20from%20The%20Kitchn&amp;media=http://p-fst1.pixstatic.com/51bb8f44d9127e24f200013f._w.540_s.fit_.jpg">
             Pin it
            </a>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           This recipe is also very vegetarian-friendly. You can leave the sausage out entirely, or substitute Soy-Rizo or another vegetarian sausage. And it's flexible; the proportions are all quite forgiving.
          </p>
          <p>
           A couple other small notes: I leave the potato skins on, for convenience and for flavor, so I really do prefer organic potatoes given that their skins are staying on. Also, if you can find Italian sausage with fennel seed, scoop it up for this dish; it's delicious with the sweet potatoes.
          </p>
          <p>
           This feeds at least eight, and I think it really could be stretched to more, depending on how many eggs you bake, and what else is served. I'm thinking of this with
           <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/thekitchn/recipe/sweet-breakfast-recipe-cream-cheese-braids-162823">
            cream cheese braids
           </a>
           , some fruit, and coffee. Brunch heaven!
          </p>
          <div class="image-wrapper mt-image-center" style="max-width: 540.0px;">
           <span class="image lazy-image" data-alt="">
            <div class="pad" style="padding-bottom: 77.77777777777779%;">
             <span data-src="http://p-fst1.pixstatic.com/51bb8f47d9127e24f2000140._w.540_s.fit_.jpg">
             </span>
             <noscript>
              <img alt="" src="http://p-fst1.pixstatic.com/51bb8f47d9127e24f2000140._w.540_s.fit_.jpg"/>
             </noscript>
            </div>

          <div id="recipe">
           <h2>
            Sweet Potato Hash with Caramelized Onions, Sausage &amp; Eggs
           </h2>
           <p>
            <em>
             <div itemprop="recipeYield">
              Serves 8
             </div>
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span itemprop="ingredients">
             2 pounds yellow onions (about 2 large)
            </span>
            <br/>
            <span itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon unsalted butter
            </span>
            <br/>
            <span itemprop="ingredients">
             1 pound fresh Italian sausage or chorizo
            </span>
            <br/>
            <span itemprop="ingredients">
             3 pounds sweet potatoes (about 3 large potatoes, ideally organic)
            </span>
            <br/>
            <span itemprop="ingredients">
             6 large garlic cloves
            </span>
            <br/>
            2 long stems fresh rosemary
            <br/>
            <span itemprop="ingredients">
             3 tablespoons olive oil
            </span>
            <br/>
            <span itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon kosher salt, plus more to taste if necessary
            </span>
            <br/>
            Freshly ground black pepper
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             To serve:
            </em>
            <br/>
            <span itemprop="ingredients">
             8 or more large eggs
            </span>
            <br/>
            Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
            <br/>
            Parmesan cheese, to serve
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             To make the hash:
            </strong>
            Heat the oven to 450F. Peel the onions and cut them in half lengthwise, then cut them into thin half-moons. Cut the half-moons in half. Melt the butter in a skillet over medium-high heat. When it foams up add the onions and sprinkle lightly with salt. (Don't worry if they are crammed into the pan; they will rapidly cook down.) Lower the heat slightly and cook the onions for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, and lowering the heat further if they seem to be burning. Cook them until they are very dark brown and caramelized.
           </p>
           <p>
            Meanwhile, put the sausage in another skillet and brown over medium-high heat, chopping it up into fine crumbles with a spatula. Cook the sausage for about 10 minutes, or until browned and beginning to crisp. Drain away any excess fat.
           </p>
           <p>
            While the onions and sausage are cooking, chop the unpeeled sweet potatoes into cubes that are about 1/2-inch to a side. Finely mince the garlic and rosemary leaves, and toss them in a large bowl with the sweet potatoes. Toss with the olive oil, kosher salt, and a generous helping of black pepper.
           </p>
           <p>
            When the onions are dark brown and the sausage is beginning to get crispy, stir these into the sweet potatoes as well. Line a large baking sheet with foil or parchment paper, and spread out the sweet potato mixture evenly. Roast the sweet potatoes for 30 to 45 minutes (roasting time depends on the size and uniformity of the sweet potato chunks, as well as the variety of sweet potato you buy) or until they are soft and browned.
           </p>
           <p>
            If not serving immediately, cool the hash and refrigerate for up to 3 days.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             To serve:
            </strong>
            Heat the oven to 425F. Spread a relatively thin layer of the (already cooked) sweet potato hash in a baking dish, such as a cast iron skillet or a 9x13-inch baking dish. You can also bake in individual ramekins. Make small wells in the sweet potatoes and crack in large eggs. Sprinkle lightly with salt and pepper.
           </p>
           <p>
            Bake for 10 to 20 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are hot and the eggs are baked through. (Test the eggs by prodding them with a fork to check the firmness of the white and the yolk; baked eggs are deceptive in that the white often looks much less cooked than it really is.)
           </p>
           <p>
            Serve immediately, with shavings or sprinkles of Parmesan cheese, if desired.
           </p>
           <h3 itemprop="name">
            Recipe Notes
           </h3>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Alternate Method:
             </strong>
             Some of the commenters felt that roasting the sweet potatoes with the cooked sausage made the sausage too crispy and even a bit burnt. Personally, I like the sausage very crispy and dark, and I also like the flavor of the sweet potatoes when they're cooked with the sausage. But this is up to you  you can roast the sweet potatoes separately while cooking the sausage and onions, which also speeds up the cooking process.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Make-Ahead Notes:
             </strong>
             You can cook the sausage and onions a day ahead of time and refrigerate or even freeze them until you are ready to cook the sweet potatoes.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            <em>
             This recipe was originally published December 2011.
            </em>
           </p>
