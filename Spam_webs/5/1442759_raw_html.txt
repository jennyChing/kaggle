        <h1 class="entry-title">
         How to:  Root Android 2.2 for the Motorola DROID
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="entry-thumbnail">
        </div>
        <div class="ad-block centered">
         <aside class="widget widget_text" id="text-71">
          <div class="textwidget">
           <!-- TopPost -->
           <div id="div-gpt-ad-1387222638033-3">
            <script type="text/javascript">
             googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1387222638033-3'); });
            </script>
           </div>
          </div>
         </aside>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/motorola-droid-root.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-12664 aligncenter" height="359" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/motorola-droid-root.jpg" title="motorola droid root" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now that the
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/2010/08/03/manual-android-2-2-update-for-motorola-droid/">
          Motorola Droid has received Android 2.2
         </a>
         , we get asked quite often when well update our guide to rooting especially from those that were waiting on
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/tag/froyo">
          Froyo
         </a>
         before taking the dive. Fortunately for everyone, the process takes a simple click rather than that long drawn out RSD Lite process we had to run through during the 2.1 days.
        </p>
        <p>
         This post should act as your one-stop guide to rooting your Droid running Android 2.2, flashing a custom recovery image, and creating your first nandroid backup to get you on your way to custom ROMs and so much more.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you are looking for reasons as to why someone would root their phone,
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/2010/04/14/wednesday-poll-why-root-your-motorola-droid/">
          check out this poll
         </a>
         we ran a while back. The comments serve as one of the best compilations of reasoning youll find anywhere.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Update
          </span>
          <span style="font-weight: normal;">
           : This rooting method works on Android 2.2 build FRG01B. It does not currently work on FRG22D. If you would like root access, I suggest rooting instead of
           <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/2010/08/26/manually-update-to-frg22d-froyo-for-droid-now/">
            installing the new FRG22D update
           </a>
           .
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         _________________________________________
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span id="more-12645">
          </span>
          Instructions for Rooting Android 2.2:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           *Warning*
          </strong>
           Rooting your Droid will void its warranty. There is also a chance that you could do permanent damage to your device and possibly brick it. You accept all risk should you decide to follow this process. Droid Life accepts no responsibility.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         1. Download and install
         <a href="http://www.megaupload.com/?d=MMRJGZWE">
          Universal Androot
         </a>
         .
         <br/>
         2. Open Universal Androot and tap the Root :-) button.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/root-droid1.png">
          <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-12647 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/root-droid1-168x300.png" title="root droid1" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. It will run through a series of steps.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/root-droid2.png">
          <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-12648 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/root-droid2-168x300.png" title="root droid2" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. If you the process completes successfully, it will look like this.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/root-droid3.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-12649" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/root-droid3-168x300.png" title="root droid3" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Congrats! You are rooted!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/root-droid4.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-12650" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/root-droid4-168x300.png" title="root droid4" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Now reboot your phone to finish off the process.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         _________________________________________
        </p>
        <h3>
         *You can stop here and enjoy being rooted or you can hit up the next set of instructions to get a custom recovery image on your phone which will allow you to make full backups and flash custom ROMs.
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         _________________________________________
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Installing a Custom Recovery:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         1. Open the Market and search for ROM Manager. Download and install it.
         <br/>
         2. Open ROM Manager and select Flash Clockwork Recovery at the top.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork1.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-12651" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork1-168x300.png" title="clockwork1" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. If asks you for permission, check the box and tap Allow.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork2.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-12652" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork2-168x300.png" title="clockwork2" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. ROM Manager will now flash Clockwork Recovery onto your phone. When it finishes, it will tell you.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork3.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-12653" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork3-168x300.png" title="clockwork3" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         5. It is now time to create a full nandroid backup and prepare for custom ROMs!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         _________________________________________
        </p>
        <h3>
         *Time to create a nandroid backup which will save you should you have amisstep somewhere in the future while flashing a custom ROM, new recovery, etc. Nandroid backups should be created constantly to ensure you have a current backup of your phone.
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         _________________________________________
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Creating a nandroid backup:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         1. In ROM Manager, tap the option to Reboot into Recovery.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork4.png">
          <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-12657 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork4-168x300.png" title="clockwork4" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Using the volume up/down keys, scroll down to nandroid and press the camera button.
         <br/>
         3. Choose Backup.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork6.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-12659" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork6-168x300.png" title="clockwork6" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Clockwork Recovery will now create a full nandroid backup of your current phone settings.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork5.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-12658" height="300" src="http://www.droid-life.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/clockwork5-168x300.png" title="clockwork5" width="168"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         5.  When it finishes, choose reboot phone.
         <br/>
         6. You now have a rooted phone, custom recovery and a full nandroid backup.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can now participate in our Custom Theme Fridays, flash future ROMs, Android leaks and so much more with your Motorola Droid. Hope this helped and feel free to drop your comments or questions below!
        </p>
       </div>
