          <h3>
           <strong>
            <em>
              Gators Team of the Century
            </em>
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            <em>
              Pro Bowl &amp; Super Bowl
            </em>
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            BORN, RAISED IN TITUSVILLE
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Wilber Marshalls performance on the high school football fields of Central Florida was a harbinger of things to come in the Hall of Fame career of the man who is clearly one of, if not the Space Coasts greatest football player ever.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://spacecoastdaily.com/2012/05/harris-lead-scorps-to-consecutive-soccer-titles/parade-all-american/" rel="attachment wp-att-26002">
            <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-26002" height="169" src="http://spacecoastdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/parade-all-american.jpg" title="parade-all-american" width="180"/>
           </a>
           Born in Titusville in 1962 to Wilbur Sr. and Lillie Marshall, he attended Astronaut High School, where he played linebacker, running back, tight end, quarterback, returned punts and even kicked field goals and extra points for Coach Jay Donnelly.
          </p>
          <p>
           His War Eagle heroics earned him national recognition as a
           <em>
            Parade
           </em>
           magazine High School All-American, and in 2007 he was named to the states All-Century Team by the Florida High School Athletic Association, which places him in an elite group of the 33 greatest Florida high school players of the last 100 years.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            PLAYER OF THE YEAR
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Heavily recruited, Marshall committed to the then lowly Gators coached by Charley Pell.
          </p>
          <p>
           Although he started his career at tight end, as a sophomore, Pell and his staff quickly recognized that Marshalls speed, strength and intuitive nose for the ball was exactly what was needed to anchor their line-backing corp.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_29032" style="width: 360px">
           <a href="http://spacecoastdaily.com/2012/05/inaugural-space-coast-sports-hall-of-fame-dazzling-for-inductees/marshall-350-1/" rel="attachment wp-att-29032">
            <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-29032" height="454" src="http://spacecoastdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/MARSHALL-350-1.jpg" title="MARSHALL-350-1" width="350"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Wilber Marshall during his induction in the Space Coast Sports Hall of Fame. (SpaceCoastDaily.com image)
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           He became the core of a ferocious Florida defense in 1981, setting school records for sacks with 11 and tackles for loss with 27, and was named first team All-SEC and honorable mention All-American.
          </p>
          <p>
           The first team All-SEC fixture was a consensus first team All-American and finalist for the Lombardi Award as a Junior in 82 and a senior in 83, when he was again a Lombardi Award finalist and also named National Defensive Player of the Year by ABC Television.
          </p>
          <p>
           Statistically he was off the chart, finishing his college career with 343 tackles-58 for a loss, and 23 sacks.
          </p>
          <p>
           The
           <em>
            Gainesville Sun
           </em>
           named him a first-team selection to the Gators Team of the Century in 1999, as well as the Defensive Player of the Century.
          </p>
          <p>
           Marshall was inducted into the University of Florida Athletic Hall of Fame as a Gator Great, was named to the University of Floridas Ring of Honor in 2007, and elected to the College Football Hall of Famein 2008.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            TWO-TIME SUPER BOWL CHAMP
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           As good as he was at the University of Florida, he got even better in the NFL.
          </p>
          <p>
           The Chicago Bears picked Marshall with the 11th overall selection of the 1984 NFL Draft, and his fearless, aggressive style of play was in perfect character for the famous Monsters of the Midway Bear defense.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://spacecoastdaily.com/2012/05/marshall-leader-key-performer-for-bears-skins/marshall-topps-298-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-26425">
            <img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-26425" height="420" src="http://spacecoastdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/MARSHALL-TOPPS-2981.jpg" title="MARSHALL-TOPPS-298" width="300"/>
           </a>
           In 1985, his second season with the Bears, he was a pillar of the famous line-backing corps of one of the most celebrated defenses in league history, which finished the regular season 151, and beating the New England Patriotsin Super Bowl XX.
          </p>
          <p>
           In his third NFL season, Marshall had five interceptions and 5.5 sacks to earn first team All-Pro honors.
          </p>
          <p>
           He angered the Bears and the NFL during the spring of 1988 when he became the first NFL free agent in 11 years to sign with another team  the Washington Redskins  who had eliminated the Bears in the playoffs in the previous two seasons.
          </p>
          <p>
           A key leader and star performer on the NFC Champion Redskins, in the 1991 Super Bowl Marshall and the Redskins defeated the Buffalo Bills. During the last two years that he anchored the Skins defense, Marshall was named All-Pro.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            MEMBER OF 20-20 CLUB
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           In 1993 Marshall signed with the Houston Oilers, reuniting with Buddy Ryan, who had been the Bears defensive coordinatorduring Marshalls first two seasons.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_26430" style="width: 410px">
           <a href="http://spacecoastdaily.com/2012/05/marshall-leader-key-performer-for-bears-skins/marshall-card-400-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-26430">
            <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-26430" height="291" src="http://spacecoastdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/MARSHALL-CARD-4001.jpg" title="MARSHALL-CARD-400" width="400"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            A key leader and star performer on the NFC Champion Redskins, in the 1991 Super Bowl Marshall and the Redskins defeated the Buffalo Bills. During the last two years that he anchored the Skins defense, Marshall was named All-Pro.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           When Ryan took the head-coaching job with the Arizona Cardinals in 1994, Marshall joined him there for one season, and then finished his NFL career in 1995 as a member of the New York Jets.
          </p>
          <p>
           In 12 NFL seasons, the 6-foot-1, 231-pound Marshall had 45 sacks, 23 interceptions, which he returned for 304 yards and three touchdowns, and 16 fumble recoveries, which he returned for 70 yards and 2 touchdowns.
          </p>
          <p>
           He is one of only a few players who have recorded 20 sacks and 20 interceptions in their career.
          </p>
          <p>
           His 12 year NFL career took a physical toll on Marshall, leaving him permanently disabled.
          </p>
          <p>
           After litigation that went on for 11 years, Marshall won a dispute for total disability benefits from the Bert Bell/Pete Rozelle Player Retirement Plan in 2008.
          </p>
          <p>
           Now living in Virginia, he struggles to walk without severe pain.
          </p>

          <div class="author-profile-bio">
           For more than a century, athletes from Brevard have been considered among the finest in America and now their accomplishments will be honored and memorialized with the creation of the Space Coast Sports Hall of Fame.

Maverick Multimedia, the parent company of SpaceCoastDaily.com, as well as Space Coast Sports magazine, Space Coast Medicine &amp; Healthy Living and Central Florida Medicine magazines, has created the Space Coast Sports Hall of Fame, with support from Space Coast Sports Promotions and Bright House Networks.

This 2014 class of inductees will be enshrined on Friday, May 9 at the 2014 Induction Banquet and Annual Sports Awards.

Space Coast Sports Hall of Fame inductees are selected from nominees in four categories including professional sports, college sports, high school sports and amateur sports.

Special honorary recognition is also bestowed upon individuals and groups that have made significant contributions to sports on the Space Coast.

The Space Coast Sports Hall of Fame Banquet and Induction Ceremony will take place at on Friday, May 9, 2014.
