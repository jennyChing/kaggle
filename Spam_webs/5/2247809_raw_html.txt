         <h1 class="post-title">
          Is That Hot Dog in Your Sushi or are You Just Happy to Sashimi Me?
         </h1>
        </div>
        <div class="clear">
        </div>
        <div class="post-content-content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.endlesssimmer.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/11/frushi-433-x-284.jpg" title="frushi-433-x-284.jpg">
           <img alt="frushi-433-x-284.jpg" src="http://www.endlesssimmer.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/11/frushi-433-x-284.jpg"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Mango, persimmon and raspberry sushi  with coconut shavings
           </em>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Scene
          </em>
          :
          <em>
           6pm. Thursday night.  Pinch of Minch and BS lounge on the couch
           <br/>
           during hour two of Curb Your Enthusiasm marathon.  A crumbled bag of
           <br/>
           pretzels lay on the table.  Somewhere, a stomach growls.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Pinch of Minch: Dude, Brendan, Im hungry.
          <br/>
          BS: Yah.
          <br/>
          P o M: I mean, really hungry.
          <br/>
          BS: Uh huh.
          <br/>
          P o M: I want sushi. Real bad.  Like, if I dont have it, I may die.
          <br/>
          Do you know what I mean?
          <br/>
          BS: No I dont, but sushi sounds good.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           They go back to watching TV, until 30 minutes later.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          P o M: Dude, Ive got a crazy idea.  We should make sushi.
          <br/>
          BS: Eureka.
          <br/>
          P o M: Out of whats in the fridge.  Like what-ev-er is in there,
          <br/>
          mayonnaise and mac and cheese sushi.  Pickle and coffee ice cream
          <br/>
          sushi.*
          <br/>
          BS: Hmmm.  What about fruit?   Fruit plus sushi is fruishi.
          <br/>
          P o M: I have some frozen hot dogs.  Im making hot dog sushi.  I am a genius.
          <br/>
          BS: Lets make some crazy sushi.
          <br/>
          P o M: This could be really gross.
         </p>
         <p>
          They give each other high fives and race to the kitchen.  What follows
          <br/>
          is a Lewis and Clark expedition of culinary craziness.  And a short
          <br/>
          time later, some of the oddest- and damn fine- sushi emerges from the
          <br/>
          kitchen and into the bellies of our two protagonists.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-793">
          </span>
          The sushi rice was surprisingly easy to replicate. Using
          <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/food/recipes/recipe/0,1977,FOOD_9936_31390,00.html" target="_blank">
           the basic directions
          </a>
          , we whipped up the vinegar-sugar concoction that gets folded into the sushi rice, making it sweet and glistening, a la restaurant sushi. Not having all the appropriate equip., we just rolled with saran wrap on a cutting board  highly unethical, surely, but effective:
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.endlesssimmer.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/11/hotdogsushiroll-562-x-380.jpg" title="hotdogsushiroll-562-x-380.jpg">
           <img alt="hotdogsushiroll-562-x-380.jpg" src="http://www.endlesssimmer.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/11/hotdogsushiroll-562-x-380.jpg"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Hot dog, pimento, cucumber and coconut sushi in progress
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.endlesssimmer.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/11/hotdogsushi-391-x-201.jpg" title="hotdogsushi-391-x-201.jpg">
           <img alt="hotdogsushi-391-x-201.jpg" src="http://www.endlesssimmer.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/11/hotdogsushi-391-x-201.jpg"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Hot dog and pimento sushi
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          This dessert sushi was my favorite. First, we spread out some leftover
          <a href="http://www.endlesssimmer.com/2007/11/15/for-liza/" target="_blank">
           coconut-rum icing from Lizas b-day cake
          </a>
          .  Then added persimmons, mango and raspberries, rolled it up, topped with pomegranate seeds and coconut shavings:
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.endlesssimmer.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/11/pomegranatesushi-400-x-450.jpg" title="pomegranatesushi-400-x-450.jpg">
           <img alt="pomegranatesushi-400-x-450.jpg" src="http://www.endlesssimmer.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/11/pomegranatesushi-400-x-450.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Some of this may sound a little far out, but seriously, sushi is way more versatile than it gets credit for. Anything rolled in sweet rice and seaweed tastes like perfection. Sushi is the new bacon!
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           *Editors Note: No mayo and mac n cheese or pickle and coffee sushi was actually attempted. That would be disgusting.
          </em>
         </p>
