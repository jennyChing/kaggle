        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-01-26T22:23:42+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 26, 2013
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/author/cheneycat/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Heather Cheney
           </span>
          </a>

         <span class="entry-comments-link">

        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders" data-frizzlyposturl="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/beef-and-blue-sliders/" type="hidden" value=""/>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="Beef and Blue Sliders | heatherlikesfood.com" class=" size-full wp-image-3927 aligncenter" height="900" src="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/DSC02806_1.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Confession time: Up until recently, Ive had a fear of blue cheese.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I dont really know why. Well, okay I do but Im over it now so I dont really want to talk about it. What if talking about why I didnt like it so much talks me back into not liking it?That wouldnt be cool.
         <span id="more-3919">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I LIKE liking it. Just like I like being the girl that orders cobb salads without hesitation or the shopper that happily takes a sample from theeccentriccheese lady at the super hoity-toity gourmet food market that charges $5.50 for a string cheese.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I also like eating these sliders. Why? Because they are the bomb dot com.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Imagine, if you will, a soft roll topped with slow-cooked beef, mozzarella, tangy blue cheese,horseradishmayo, and baked in a savory butter sauce to crispy perfection. Would you want to sacrifice all this because of some silly aversion to cheese? I didnt think so.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         You can definitely use left-over pot roast for these sliders, but if you arent so lucky to have some in the fridge its just about as easy to throw a chuck roast into the slow-cooker with a little bit of thyme, celery seed, granulated garlic, kosher salt, brown sugar, and a bay leaf. Sprinkle a few tablespoons of soy sauce over the top and cook until its tender and easily shreds. (I did spread the seasonings around before starting to cook, btw. And the same seasonings make for a great pot roast if you throw in some potatoes, carrots, and onion)
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/beef-and-blue-sliders/sony-dsc-776/" rel="attachment wp-att-3923">
          <img alt="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3928" height="896" src="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/DSC02668.jpg" title="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders | Heather Likes Food" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Once your roast is cooked and shredded, cut some plain white dinner rolls in half and top each with a good pile of meat.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3920" height="896" src="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/DSC02748_1.jpg" title="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders | Heather Likes Food" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Next, sprinkle a few tablespoons of shredded mozzarella cheese over each roll
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders" class="aligncenter" height="896" src="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/DSC02750_1.jpg" title="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders | Heather Likes Food" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         followed by some blue cheese crumbles.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders" class="aligncenter" height="896" src="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/DSC02751_1.jpg" title="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders | Heather Likes Food" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         For the sauce youll combine mayonnaise with a couple teaspoons of prepared horseradish and some black pepper.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3923" height="895" src="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/DSC02752_1.jpg" title="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders | Heather Likes Food" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Spread the tops of each roll with a schmear of sauce and place on top of the meat and cheese. Youll then make your butter sauce by combining a stick of melted butter with a bit ofWorcestershire sauce, poppy seeds, sugar and dehydrated onion and pour evenly over the rolls.
         <a href="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/beef-and-blue-sliders/sony-dsc-781/" rel="attachment wp-att-3928">
          <br/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3925" height="402" src="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/DSC02763_1.jpg" style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;" title="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders | Heather Likes Food" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Cover the pan with foil and bake for 10 minutes, uncover and bake for an additional 10 minutes or until the cheese is melted and tops are golden.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3926" height="896" src="http://www.heatherlikesfood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/DSC02784_1.jpg" title="Beef and Blue Cheese Sliders | Heather Likes Food" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Oh blue cheese, why did I ever doubt you?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">

          Beef and Blue Sliders

         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Meat
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 2lb beef chuck roast, trimmed of excess fat
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tbsp brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp dried thyme
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp celery seed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp granulated garlic
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 bay leaf
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             C soy sauce
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Sliders
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            12 white dinner rolls
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 C mozzarella cheese, shredded
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3-5 oz blue cheese crumbles
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             C mayonnaise
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tsp prepared horseradish
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp ground black pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             C butter
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp poppy seeds
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tsp dehydrated onion
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp sugar
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Meat:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Combine thyme, sugar, celery seed, garlic, and bay leaf and sprinkle over roast in slow-cooker. Pour soy sauce over roast.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cook for 4-5 hours on high, or 7-8 hours on low setting. Meat is done when tender and shreds easily.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Remove meat from slow-cooker and shred.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Sliders:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cut dinner rolls in half and lay bottom half of each one on a baking sheet close together. You want the sides of each roll to be touching.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place a portion of shredded beef on top of each roll followed by the mozzarella and blue cheeses.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Combine the mayonnaise with horseradish and pepper, stir to combine and then spread on the bottom side of the top half of each roll.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place the roll tops on the cheese and beef to create a sandwich.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Melt the butter and combine Worcestershire sauce, poppy seeds, onion, and sugar. Pour butter mixture evenly over the rolls.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cover baking sheet with foil and bake in a preheated oven at 375 for 10 minutes. Uncover and continue to cook for an additional 5-10 minutes or until the cheese is melted and tops are browned.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
