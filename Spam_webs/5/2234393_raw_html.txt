           <p>
            <strong>
             A sanitation worker in an Atlanta suburb has been sentenced to 30 days in jail for working too early.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Kevin McGill had been working for only a few months for a company contracted to do sanitation work in Sandy Springs when he was cited for picking up trash just after 5 a.m. one morning, according to WSB-TV.
           </p>
           <p>
            Kevin McGill, 48, who works for Waste Management Inc, took a guilty plea deal after violating an ordinance in Sandy Springs, north of Atlanta, that bans collections before 7am.
           </p>
           <p>
            He turned 48 on Friday, but reported to a local jail at 6pm after being allowed to serve his time on the weekends so he can still work collecting wealthy residents trash so he can support his wife and two children.
           </p>
           <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1542" style="width: 634px;">
            <a href="http://i2.wp.com/itsenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/jailed.jpg">
             <img alt="Kevin McGill " class="lazy lazy-hidden size-full wp-image-1542" data-lazy-src="http://i2.wp.com/itsenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/jailed.jpg?resize=634%2C346" data-lazy-type="image" data-recalc-dims="1" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
             <noscript>
              <img alt="Kevin McGill " class="size-full wp-image-1542" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/itsenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/jailed.jpg?resize=634%2C346"/>
             </noscript>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
             Kevin McGill
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            The bin collector didnt have a lawyer when he was sentenced and has filed a motion to withdraw his plea, McGills new attorney Kimberly Bandoh told Daily Mail Online.
           </p>
           <p>
            However, the Court Chief Solicitor Bill Riley asked for a 30 day sentence for the offense of picking up trash around 5am in the suburb and the judge granted the stiff penalty.
           </p>
           <p>
            He was sentenced on February 20th at the local courthouse, the running of which has been handed over to private contractors by the city.
           </p>
           <p>
            There were no real negotiations between her client and Mr Riley when he was sentenced, Ms Bandoh told Daily Mail Online, adding that the solicitor had claimed the offense meant automatic jail time.
           </p>
           <p>
            Mr Riley has stood by the request for jail and says that fines dont seem to work and The only thing that seems to stop the activity is actually going to jail.
           </p>
           <p>
            The solicitor said that residents of Sandy Springs, which include professional athletes and executives for Delta Air Lines, begin calling 9-1-1 when they hear early garbage men, according to WSPA.
           </p>
           <p>
            He said it was the right decision to punish McGill, who had only been on the route for three months, rather than his company, which the city has contracted to pick up its residents trash.
           </p>
