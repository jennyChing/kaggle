              <header>
               <span>
                Dr. Sanjay Gupta
               </span>
               <h1>
                A Doctor's 35-year "Grudge Match" Against Hepatitis C
               </h1>
              </header>
              <aside class="disclaimer">
              </aside>
              <h2>
               Dr. Douglas Dieterich got hepatitis C from a patient when he was in medical school. When he recovered, he switched specialties to fight the disease.
              </h2>
              <div class="bottom">
               <div class="byline">
                Video featuring Dr. Sanjay Gupta
               </div>
               <div class="reviewedby">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="clear">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div id="video-player-top">
             </div>
             <aside class="aside">
              <img alt="A Doctor's 35-year &quot;Grudge Match&quot; Against Hepatitis C" class="left" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/cs-gupta-resizes-dietrich-story1-article.jpg"/>
             </aside>
             <p>
              TRANSCRIPT:
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Sanjay
Gupta, MD, Everyday Health:
              </strong>
              Dr. Douglas Dieterich is a leading specialist in
hepatitis C. When he was in medical school he was planning on becoming an
ophthalmologist, an eye doctor. But one mistake with a needle changed his life,
and his career, forever.
             </p>
             <p>
              How did
you get interested in hepatitis?
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Douglas
Dieterich, MD, The Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai:
              </strong>
              I got interested in
              <a href="/health-report/hepatitis-c-living-well.aspx">
               hepatitis C
              </a>
              the hard way. That is, I stuck myself with a needle and I got it when I was a
medical student.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Gupta:
              </strong>
              Did you know right away when you stuck yourself? I mean how worried were
you at that time?
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Dieterich:
              </strong>
              I knew I was in trouble.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr. Gupta:
              </strong>
              What did you experience?
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Dieterich:
              </strong>
              Oh, I got really sick, I got
jaundiced, you know, yellow. I was bedridden for a couple of months.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Gupta:
              </strong>
              But that changed your life in many ways.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Dieterich:
              </strong>
              It did, it did actually. It
did a lot of things, actually. It changed my perspective on life. I decided
that I needed to fight back actually. This was a grudge match now.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Gupta:
              </strong>
              A grudge match against hepatitis C. But back then, very little was known
about this disease. How does one start that process of fighting back?
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Dieterich:
              </strong>
              I did a lot of HIV research and
              <a href="/health-report/hepatitis-c-living-well/treating-hepatitis-c-need-to-know.aspx">
               treatment
              </a>
              and learned a lot about
viruses and the immune system, which has actually helped a lot once we started
to figure out what was going on with hepatitis C.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Gupta:
              </strong>
              Do you still have hepatitis C?
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Dieterich:
              </strong>
              I dont. I have been cured for
like 14 years now, actually.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Gupta:
              </strong>
              You took some of these earlier
drugs including Interferon, what was that like?
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Dieterich:
              </strong>
              Interferon has lots of
              <a href="/health-report/hepatitis-c-living-well/hepatitis-c-treatment-side-effects.aspx">
               side effects
              </a>
              , flu-like side effects. You know
that first day when you are just coming down with a viral infection or the flu?
You feel terrible and irritable and then you start getting chills and fever.
Its like you get an injection like that every week.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Dr.
Gupta:
              </strong>
              Today doctors have much better treatments for fighting hepatitis C,
drugs that can attack the virus directly. They are far more effective and they
also dont have the terrible side effects that Dr. Dieterich experienced. When
Dr. Dieterich began his grudge match against hepatitis C, he had few tools.
Today, he can finally say hes winning. With Everyday Health, Im Dr. Sanjay
Gupta. Be well.
             </p>
            </article>
