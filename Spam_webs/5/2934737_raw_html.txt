        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Should There be an Aptitude Test for Social Media Professionals?
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-01-23T08:05:55+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 23, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://maximizesocialbusiness.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Neal Schaffer
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://maximizesocialbusiness.com/aptitude-test-social-media-professionals-12545/#comments">
           14 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div id="triberr_endorsement">
        </div>
        <div class="pf-content">
         <div class="printfriendly pf-alignright">
          <a class="noslimstat" href="http://www.printfriendly.com/print?url=http%3A%2F%2Fmaximizesocialbusiness.com%2Faptitude-test-social-media-professionals-12545%2F" onclick="window.print(); return false;" rel="nofollow">
           <img alt="Print Friendly" src="//cdn.printfriendly.com/pf-button-both.gif" style="border:none;-webkit-box-shadow:none; box-shadow:none;"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://cdn.maximizesocialbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Should-There-be-an-Aptitude-Test-for-Social-Media-Professionals-V2.jpg">
           <img alt="Should-There-be-an-Aptitude-Test-for-Social-Media-Professionals-V2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-18477" height="600" src="http://cdn.maximizesocialbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Should-There-be-an-Aptitude-Test-for-Social-Media-Professionals-V2.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
           As someone who teaches as part of the
           <a href="http://cmd.rutgers.edu/programs/mini-mba-social-media-marketing#tab-Overview" target="_blank">
            Rutgers University Social Media Marketing Mini-MBA Program
           </a>
           , I find myself being called an educator more and more. Whether it is through teaching, consulting, coaching, blogging, writing, or speaking, my end goal really is to educate professionals and businesses on social media and help them cross the chasm to become more efficient and strategic in how their companies utilize social media.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           The background for a social media aptitude test
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          However, if youre a social media professional looking for a job in social media, or if youre a company looking to hire a professional with social media expertise, how do you know the aptitude that person might or might not have for social media above and beyond their taking a certification course?
         </p>
         <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
          <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
          </script>
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7284850702328431" data-ad-slot="1904859849" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
         </div>
         <p>
          After publishing
          <a href="http://wind.mn/maxyoursocial" target="_blank">
           Maximize Your Social
          </a>
          , I was looking for a way to create some sort of program to make this concept a reality. That is when I was approached to be an early adopter of an exciting new platform I will be introducing below.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           First, is there need for a social media education?
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Before I introduce this new platform, though, there is still some debate amongst some about the need for a corporate social media education and why a
          <a href="http://maximizesocialbusiness.com/themes/social-media-mba/" target="_blank">
           Social Media MBA
          </a>
          or taking additional education provided at universities is a valuable investment to make. To me, this is a no brainer. I think anybody who has gone through one of these programs will tell you the same thing. If 93% of U.S. businesses are utilizing social media for marketing, and now many are starting to use social media beyond marketing and throughout the enterprise, shouldnt there be
          <em>
           some
          </em>
          education about social media included in business courses?
         </p>
         <p>
          The first thing that people tell me when I tell them I am teaching a Social Media MBA program is that everything that you learn is irrelevant a few months from now, so how can you charge people money for something that is a fleeting, soon to be irrelevant type of subject matter?
         </p>
         <p>
          I think the problem is that what some people provide in social media training, whether it is corporate training or consulting or if you read books or blog posts, they basically go over the technical specifications for the various social media sites and provide precious little insight that you couldnt find yourself going through the help screens and tutorials of these sites on your own.
         </p>
         <p>
          So, yes, to some degree you can figure out some of this stuff yourself. True, taking these classes can give you a quicker time to market and you may learn a few tips and tricks. But consider this analogy: It does not come down to the specifications of your smart phone. It comes down to what you can do with the applications on and functionality of your smartphone, right?
         </p>
         <h3>
          Social media education is about applicable concepts, not technical specifications
         </h3>
         <p>
          In social media, similarly, it comes down to concepts that you can use and apply to the various social networks. When I teach about social media platforms, I bring up various concepts that show people that although the platform may change, a lot of the core concepts whether how they use metrics to help determine social media ROI, the need of constant experimentation, creating a social media strategy, the existence of news feed algorithms, different ways to micro-target your social media advertisements, et. al. dont change.
         </p>
         <p>
          These are all core concepts that are platform independent. Meaning, these are the concepts that can be applied across all the platforms. I like to show a slide at the end of my presentations that says social media is here to stay. It has a bunch of boxes, like someone just moved into a new apartment. There are names of different social platforms on each box. The names on the boxes might change, as many have (the image I use includes Digg, StumbleUpon and Reddit and doesnt have Pinterest, Instagram, or Google Plus!), but the key message is that social media truly is here to stay. It is about understanding how to apply the concepts to any given social media platform that help you leverage the opportunity at hand to Maximize Your Social.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
           So, if there is a need for social media education and programs like the one I teach at Rutgers, I argue there is a similar need to have some sort of system to measure aptitude for both those that do take these courses as well as those that dont. And if no one else was going to create one, I would.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Introducing CredSpark
         </h3>
         <p>
          Enter
          <a href="http://credspark.com" target="_blank">
           CredSpark
          </a>
          ,
          <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
           a new way for people to showcase the specialized knowledge and skills theyve acquired. CredSpark
          </span>
          believes that diplomas, resumes, and online profiles dont tell the whole story. Employers want to better understand job candidates, and individuals want recognition for very specific things theyve learned along their journey including in social media!
         </p>
         <p>
          On the flip side,
          <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
           CredSpark believes that Experts (Im one of the first ones they invited to participate in the platform) are in a great position to validate a persons specialized knowledge. Their platform allows Experts to easily develop and publish Challenges based upon specific topics in their area of expertise. By taking Experts Challenges, individuals gain validation and recognition for what they know, as well as guidance from the Expert about how they can learn more and up their game in their field.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre curious, about CredSparks credentials, theNew York City-based startup was launched in late 2013 by edtech veteran and Harvard MBA Lev Kaye, a former executive at both Kaplan and The Princeton Review. Excited by the tectonic shifts in the education landscape, Lev wanted to create a better way for people to show what they know outside of formal diplomas and certificates. In addition, Levs glad that people have a deeper way to engage with and learn from Experts, so as to foster their career growth.
         </p>
         <p>
          After I got to know Lev, I realized that he had created the platform that would allow me to realize my own dream. Our vision was one and the same.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Introducing my first social media challenge (because Test is a four-letter-word!)
         </h3>
         <p>
          With that, I introduce to you the very first challenge for social media professionals that I have created:
          <a href="http://bit.ly/JnoW0n" target="_blank">
           A Google Plus challenge
          </a>
          . It is actually Lev who chose this particular platform of G+ first after reading Maximize Your Social. Now, some of you are undoubtedly going to be skeptical about the concept, but I did get positive feedback from many of my fellow social media professionals, and I look forward to continuing to receive YOUR feedback as I create and implement more of these educational challenges in the near future.
         </p>
         <p>
          More importantly, I see these challenges as another way of engaging with social media professionals and educating them on things they may have overlooked or not have experience on when managing different social media platforms. In other words, I think youll find that the challenge in itself will provide you an educational opportunity!
         </p>
         <p>
          What are you waiting for?
          <a href="http://bit.ly/JnoW0n" target="_blank">
           Take my Google Plus challenge by clicking this link
          </a>
          , and let me know what you think in the comments below. Thanks!
         </p>
