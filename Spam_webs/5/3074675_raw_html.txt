         <h1>
          Why Failure is Just a Stepping Stone to Success {Guest Post}
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta">
          by
          <a href="http://www.inspirationwithval.com/author/valeriegroth/" rel="author" title="Posts by valeriegroth">
           valeriegroth
          </a>
          | Jul 10, 2014 |
          <a href="http://www.inspirationwithval.com/category/get-inspired/" rel="category tag">
           Get Inspired
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.inspirationwithval.com/category/get-un-stuck/" rel="category tag">
           Get Un-stuck
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.inspirationwithval.com/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag">
           Uncategorized
          </a>
          |
          <span class="comments-number">
           <a href="http://www.inspirationwithval.com/failure-just-stepping-stone-success-guest-post/#respond">
            0 comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p class="p1">
           <a href="http://www.valeriegroth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/62.jpg">
            <img alt="http://www.dreamstime.com/royalty-free-stock-photo-car-woman-showing-car-keys-image23808415" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4814" height="3464" src="http://www.valeriegroth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/62.jpg" width="2309"/>
           </a>
           Hello, friends!
          </p>
          <p class="p1">
           As Im gallavanting through the fabulous city of Portland, Mary-Elizabeth Schurrer of
           <a href="http://healthbeahippie.wordpress.com/">
            Health Be a Hippie
           </a>
           so kindly offered to write a guest post. Enjoy!
          </p>
          <p class="p2">
           ***
          </p>
          <h2 class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            Human beings are largely products of the social conditions in which they were raised. And for many of us, that means a deeply-ingrained fear of failing. We aim to please people. Our parents. Peers. College professors. Employers. Significant others. Hell, even ourselves.
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            Whether its a grievous mistake or a marginal oversight, the act of screwing-up often seems unforgiveable. But consider this: to fear failure is not instinctual; it is a learned behavior that has paradoxically set us up for a lifetime of mediocrity.
           </span>
           <span id="more-4813">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            Remember when you brought home your first elementary school report card with straight As and a shiny gold star? Mom wasted no time in displaying it on the fridge, as a not-so-subtle reminder that youd better start bringing home
            <i>
             more
            </i>
            shiny gold stars. Then, what happened when you  gasp!  received a B+ instead of an A?
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            You blew it. Period. You fell short of your own potential and everyone elses expectations. And so, the cycle began. Failure became perceived as fatal, a serious misconception that has followed you into adulthood.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            But youre not alone, of course. Most people torture themselves wondering what might happen if they miss the mark and never achieve their goals. They fixate on the worst case scenario, which cripples them from pursuing those goals in the first place. Its a self-fulfilling prophecy. Allow the fear of failure to fester and, consequently, pass up any chance for success.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            So, whats the counterpoint to this gloomy prospect? Well, how about a change of thinking? In a society that demands perfection, what if we allowed ourselves to fall down a few times instead? What if we learned, grew, toughened, and matured through trial-and-error?
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            Maybe  just maybe  wed come to realize that failure
            <i>
             is
            </i>
            an option. Not a monster to avoid, but a teachable moment to embrace. And, irony of ironies: maybe if we persevered in spite of failing, wed ultimately find success.
           </span>
          </p>
          <h2 class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            Still not convinced, huh? Alright, then. Here are 5 reasons failure may, in fact, be the
            <i>
             best
            </i>
            thing thats ever happened to you.
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            1. Embracing failure promotes
            <b>
             risk taking
            </b>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            <i>
             A person, who never made a mistake, never tried anything new.
            </i>
             Albert Einstein
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            There is no greater feeling than overcoming an obstacle, no matter how many tries it took, and this taste of triumph in the face of opposition often spurs people to jump even more hurdles and push more limits.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            2. Embracing failure promotes
            <b>
             resilience
            </b>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            <i>
             Every strike brings me closer to the next home run.
            </i>
             Babe Ruth
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            The cycle of stumbling then getting back up again gradually builds a thick skin, which enables people to keep forging ahead through lifes trials rather than throwing their hands up in lazy surrender.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            3. Embracing failure promotes
            <b>
             responsibility
            </b>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            <i>
             Dont blame anyone for your situation or problems. When you do that, youre saying that you are powerless over your own life.
            </i>
             Jillian Michaels
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            Instead of passing the buck onto someone else when plans go awry, people who take ownership for their errors, can subsequently apply what went wrong toward a lesson in personal development.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            4. Embracing failure promotes
            <b>
             creative thinking
            </b>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            <i>
             I like the challenge of trying different things and wondering if its going to work, or if Im going to fall flat on my face.
            </i>
             Johnny Depp
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            Whenever a strategy or course of action backfires, it becomes an opportunity for people to brainstorm outside the box, rather than rigidly trying the same unsuccessful approach over and over again.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p6">
           <span class="s1">
            5. Embracing failure promotes
            <b>
             humbleness
            </b>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            <i>
             We learn humility by accepting humiliations cheerfully.
            </i>
             Mother Teresa
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p8">
           <span class="s1">
            In the midst of an emerging generation that expects hand-outs and entitlements, people who
            <i>
             dont
            </i>
            get what they want every time they turn around, are less likely to take their accomplishments for granted.
           </span>
          </p>
         </div>
