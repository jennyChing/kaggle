               <p>
                Are you a potential client? A new graphic designer? Or someone who just enjoys annoying people? If youve never worked with graphic designers before or you simply want to tick us off, here are20 ways to drive graphic designers mad.
               </p>
               <h2>
                FORMATS
               </h2>
               <p>
                <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.ucreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/psd-vs-jpg-vs-word.jpg" rel="lightbox[58587]">
                 <img alt="psd-vs-jpg-vs-word" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-58827" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.ucreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/psd-vs-jpg-vs-word.jpg?resize=660%2C288"/>
                </a>
                <strong>
                 1) Send your images inMS Word format
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Seeing MS Word file attachments in oure-mails makes us want to pull our hair out, more so when you treatMS Word as a photo album.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 2) Send low-res images
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Graphic designers are always asked to scale images and its hard to stretch a low-res file without sacrificing its sharpness. Itll be hard for us to make your logo bigger, but well get to that part later.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 3) Ask us to edit flattened files
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Although it is possible on some levels, there is a limit to how much we can edit your flat files. Please make sure that you send us files with editable layers. Usually its the file with the blue Photoshopicon.
               </p>
               <h2>
                BUSINESS
               </h2>
               <p>
                <img alt="chart" class="size-full wp-image-58920 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.ucreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/chart.jpg?resize=660%2C490"/>
                <strong>
                 <br/>
                 4) Ask for mock-ups before hiring us
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Nope, thats as good as working for free. Mock-ups are submitted afterwe sign a contract and agree on the project details. We have portfolios that host our past works from which you can judge our art style before hiring us.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 5) Discussbusiness terms and statisticswith us
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Were not dumb but there are certain things that you should leave to your colleagues, the same way that we leave you out of hex codes. Unless the information is crucial, limit yourwords to 3 syllables and the numbers to 2 decimal places.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 6) Ask us to work for free
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                We want to give you your moneys worth but we have to consider our cost of living.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 7) Tell us its good forour portfolio
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Eyes of graphic designers sparkle when they find work that would be good for their portfolios, but thats not for anyone else to decide but the artist himself.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 8) Haggle endlessly
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Haggling is only minimally considered before the project starts.We are in a creative market, not a fish market where you can get the stale fish at a discounted price.
               </p>
               <h2>
                STYLE
               </h2>
               <p>
                <a href="http://i1.wp.com/www.ucreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/NOT-SAME.jpg" rel="lightbox[58587]">
                 <img alt="NOT-SAME" class="size-full wp-image-58958 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.ucreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/NOT-SAME.jpg?resize=660%2C419"/>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 9) Insist that we use fontsinappropriate for the project
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Are we making a childrens book? A teenage comic book? If not, lets leave Comic Sans on the shelf. You want your brand to look cool by using Helvetica? Hmm. We dont think Helvetica would go well with infant needs.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 10) Compare us with other designers
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Please dont tell us about the graphic designers you found on the internet who charge $5 for a logo. We all speak the same language but we dont all have the same accent.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 11) Tell us you dont like white space
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                We will not accept the argument that you paid for the white space, hence we should fill it up with something, anything just to get rid of it. White space is not a waste of space. Its part of the design.
               </p>
               <h2>
                PRODUCTION
               </h2>
               <h2>
                <img alt="big-logo" class="size-full wp-image-58956 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.ucreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/big-logo.jpg?resize=660%2C448"/>
               </h2>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 12) Tell us to make the logo bigger
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                The number one client feedback that we hate hearing is to make the logo bigger. Big isnt always better. Graphic designerssometimes forget that the logo should be prominent, but the clienthas to understand that the logo is not the artwork, its only a small part of it unless the logo is the hero image.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 13) Expect us to know what you like
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                We are graphic designers, not fortune tellers. What we can tell you now though, is that you will not get what you want unless you tell us exactly what it is.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 14) Expect unlimited revisions
                 <br/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                There is a limit to how much we can revise our work based on the brief you have given us. If our work bounces back over a couple of times because you dont like it, it mighttell us that you are indecisive and we might not work for you again.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 15) Use vague instructions
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Jazz it up a bit. Can you make it more web-ish? It lacks sparkle. Theres no oomph. Can you make it pop? Please try to be as specific as possible because vague comments lead to vague revisions.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 16) Watch us work
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Go ahead and hover at our back and crawl above our shoulders when were so in the zone. We just cant promise not to punch you in the face.
               </p>
               <h2>
                COURTESY
               </h2>
               <p>
                <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.ucreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/kidcansee.jpg" rel="lightbox[58587]">
                 <img alt="kidcansee" class="size-full wp-image-58954 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.ucreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/kidcansee.jpg?resize=660%2C478"/>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 17) Ask feedback from non-authorities
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Go ahead and show our work to your friend/child/spouse, but dont rely solely on their feedback unless theyre adeptwith graphic design, business, ormarketing. Discuss our work with your business partner, your director, an advertising major, or anyone with authority and enough knowledge within your company.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 18) Take too much time sending revisions
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Before we started our project, we laid out a timetable to gauge the production time. That timetable can only be followed if we get your feedback for revisionspromptly.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 19) Tell us were wrong
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Yes, were humans and we commit mistakes, but dont tell us outright that were wrong when you dont like our designs. It is likely that we did not meet your expectations due to different reasons, but that doesnt mean were wrong.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 20) Refer to us as just a designer
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                This is probably the worst thing you can say about a graphic designer, that he is JUST a designer. We dont dress in suits everyday orcarry briefcases but we take pride inwhat we do.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <span style="color: #676767;">
                 As a graphic designer, what else drives you mad that isnt in this list? Share them
                </span>
                <span style="color: #676767;">
                 through the comments below!
                </span>
               </p>
