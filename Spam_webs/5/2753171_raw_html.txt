           <h1 class="post-title">
            The Voyager 1 is the only spacecraft to reach interstellar space
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://factsloader.com/author/pijush" rel="author" title="Posts by Pijush Mandal">
             Pijush Mandal
            </a>
             February 14, 2015
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <div class="mailmunch-forms-before-post" style="display: none !important;">
             </div>
             <p>
              The Voyager 1 is a space probe which was official approved in May 1972 and was launched by NASA on 5 September 1977, aim to study the outer Solar system. It weighs at 722 kilograms and comprises of 65,000 individual parts, the circuit complexity of the Voyager is equivalent to some 2000 color Television sets.
             </p>
             <p>
              The total cost of the mission from 1972 through the Neptune encounter is 865 million dollars. As a part of the Voyager program, its sister spacecraft Voyager 2 is in an extended mission to locate and study the regions and boundaries of the outer heliosphere, and finally to begin exploring the interstellar medium. A total of five trillion bits of scientific data had been returned to Earth by both the spacecrafts which is equivalent to seven thousand music CDs.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_307" style="width: 730px">
              <a href="http://factsloader.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/voyager.jpg">
               <img alt="The Voyager 1 is the only spacecraft to reach interstellar space" class="wp-image-307 size-large" height="405" src="http://factsloader.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/voyager-1024x576.jpg" width="720"/>
              </a>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Voyager 1 in interstellar space [Image credit:
               <a href="http://nasa.gov" target="_blank" title="NASA">
                nasa.gov
               </a>
               ]
              </p>
             </div>
             At the traveling velocity of 61,000 km/h, it is the farthest spacecraft from Earth and the Voyager 1 is the only spacecraft to reach interstellar space. At the time of this post the Voyager 1s distance from Earth is19,566,783,192 Kilometers and distance from Sun is19,522,997,341 Kilometers. The primary mission ended on November20, 1980, after encounters with the Jovian system in 1979 and the Saturnian system in 1980. It was the first probe to provide detailed images of the two planets and their moons. It also studied the weather, magnetic fields, and rings of those worlds.
             <p>
              To receive routine commands and return data, the spacecraft communicates with the Deep Space Network and the operation of the cameras for visible light is not autonomous, but rather it is controlled by an imaging parameter table contained in one of the on-board digital computers, the Flight Data Subsystem (FDS). The resolution of the Voyager narrow-angle television cameras is sharp enough to read a newspaper headline at a distance of 1 km.
             </p>
             <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
             </div>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               <strong>
                Voyager 1 views the Solar System from over 6 billion km away.
               </strong>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
