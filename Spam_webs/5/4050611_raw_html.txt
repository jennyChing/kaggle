          <div class="article-body__content">
           <p>
            Nov. 3 (Bloomberg) -- Stryker Corp. agreed to pay about $1
billion to resolve lawsuits over thousands of hip implants it
recalled in 2012 after patients complained of pain, swelling and
metal debris from the devices, a federal judge said.
           </p>
           <p>
            Stryker, based in Kalamazoo, Michigan, withdrew its
Rejuvenate and ABG II devices in July 2012 after warning
surgeons that year that the devices could harm tissue around the
hip and cause other health problems. The company faces 1,800
cases consolidated before U.S. District Judge Donovan Frank in
St. Paul, Minnesota. A New Jersey state judge is overseeing
about 2,100 lawsuits over the devices.
           </p>
           <p>
            In those cases, hip recipients blamed the Stryker implants
for high levels of metal debris found in their blood.
           </p>
           <div data-view-uid="1|0_5_1_5">
            <div class="ad-v2 on-mobile" data-position="box1" id="1|0_5_1_5">
             <script type="text/javascript">
              window.__bloomberg__.ads.createAd({ adCode: "business/news/science-energy/article", dimensions: {"mobile":[[300,250],[1,1]]}, id: "1|0_5_1_5", strategy: "", targeting: {"tagr":[],"ni":["001511NODE","2500","500","?15165605","?1703765","?1707812","?3046760","?3723802","ALLTOP","AMERICAME","BIZNEWS","BNALL","BNCOPY","BNMINNY","BNNEWARK","BNSTAFF","BNTEAMS","BUSINESS","CLASS","COS","DUPLICATE","FINNEWS","FIRST","HEA","HEATOP","HEATOPSTG","HEATOPZ3","HLDUPLICAT","HLNOVELTY0","LAW","LAWSUITS","LAWTOP","LAWTOPSTG","LAWTOPZ1","LITIGATE","MDS","MI","MN","MSCINAMER","MSCIWORLD","MTC","NJ","NORTHAM","NOVELTY0","ONWEB","ONWEBSTG","READ250","READ500","SENT","SF","TOP","TOPBIZMKT","UPDATE","US","USBNX","USHEALTH","USLEGAL","USMA","USMW","USSTORY","USTOP","USTOPZ3","WEBELIG","WWTOP","WWTOPBIZ","WWTOPSTG","WWTOPZ3","XWD"],"kwl":[],"suid":"NEHET56JIJVC01","currentResource":"Story|NEHET56JIJVC01","page":"article","position":"box1","url":"/news/articles/2014-11-03/stryker-agrees-to-settle-litigation-over-recalled-hip-devices"} });
             </script>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            Last year, Johnson &amp; Johnson, the worlds biggest seller of
health-care products, agreed to pay at least $2.5 billion to
settle litigation over its ASR metal-on-metal hip device.
           </p>
           <p>
            The consolidated New Jersey case is In Re Stryker
Rejuvenate and AGB II Modular Hip Implant Litigation, Case No.
296 BER-L-936-13, Bergen County Superior Court (Hackensack). The
consolidated federal case is In Re Stryker Rejuvenate and AGB II
Hip Implant Products Liability Litigation, 13-MDL-2441, U.S.
District Court for the District of Minnesota (St. Paul).
           </p>
