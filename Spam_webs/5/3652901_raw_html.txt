      <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">
       Richmond
      </a>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_ride">
       The ride
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebel_Yell_(roller_coaster)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The ride">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Rebel Yell is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racing_roller_coaster" title="Racing roller coaster">
       racing roller coaster
      </a>
      with two individual tracks that are parallel mirror images of each other. Its design was inspired by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racer_(Kings_Island)" title="Racer (Kings Island)">
       Racer
      </a>
      at sister park
      <a href="/wiki/Kings_Island" title="Kings Island">
       Kings Island
      </a>
      near
      <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">
       Cincinnati
      </a>
      , which opened three years earlier in 1972. In turn, Rebel Yell's design was the basis for
      <a href="/wiki/Thunder_Road_(roller_coaster)" title="Thunder Road (roller coaster)">
       Thunder Road
      </a>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/Carowinds" title="Carowinds">
       Carowinds
      </a>
      , near
      <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">
       Charlotte
      </a>
      , which has an identical layout.
     </p>
     <p>
      There is one red and one blue train on each side for a total of two trains on each side. When only running one train on each side, an effort is made to run a train of each color. There are often fun spirited arguments on the lift hill about which train is better.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Rebel Yell received the
      <a href="/wiki/American_Coaster_Enthusiasts" title="American Coaster Enthusiasts">
       ACE
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coaster_Landmark" title="Coaster Landmark">
       Coaster Landmark
      </a>
      award on June 20, 2003.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebel_Yell_(roller_coaster)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <ul>
      <li>
       When opened in 1975, the tracks ran parallel to Lake Charles, a large man-made lake on the northwest corner of Kings Dominion. Two-thirds of the lake was drained in the early 1990s, when the space was needed for construction of the
       <a href="/wiki/Water_park" title="Water park">
        water park
       </a>
       . (
       <a href="/wiki/Anaconda_(roller_coaster)" title="Anaconda (roller coaster)">
        Anaconda
       </a>
       still runs over and under the remainder of the lake) Rebel Yell now divides the water park in half with a walkway running under it.
      </li>
      <li>
       The trains on one side of Rebel Yell were turned to face backward from 1992 until 2007. Beginning in 2008, both trains were faced forward again so that the ride operates under the train manufacturer's specifications.
      </li>
      <li>
       The ride was named for the historic
       <a href="/wiki/Rebel_yell" title="Rebel yell">
        Rebel Yell
       </a>
       , a
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_cry" title="Battle cry">
        battle cry
       </a>
       used by
       <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_Army" title="Confederate States Army">
        Confederate soldiers
       </a>
       during the
       <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">
        Civil War
       </a>
       . At the end of the ride, the trains pass through a tunnel that reverberates with a sound that resembles screaming.
      </li>
      <li>
       Rebel Yell was featured in the
       <a href="/wiki/1977_in_film" title="1977 in film">
        1977 movie
       </a>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Rollercoaster_(1977_film)" title="Rollercoaster (1977 film)">
         Rollercoaster
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvmk_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-mvmk-4">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         4
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upandd_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-upandd-5">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         5
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       Portions of the ride were re-tracked for the 2010 season.
      </li>
      <li>
       Seat dividers and individual lap bars were added in the early 1990s.
      </li>
      <li>
       For the park's 40th anniversary in 2014, the coaster was repainted the original color scheme (red, white, &amp; blue) and the similarly colored "chaser" lights were restored to the north side of the coaster. Additionally, many of the trains front cars were restored to the original, script-style logo.
      </li>
     </ul>
