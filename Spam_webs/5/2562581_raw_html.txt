        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Double Chocolate Protein Cheesecake
        </h1>
        <div class="unique-name">
         <p>

         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

        <p>
         <img alt="double chocolate protein cheesecake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1016" height="3400" src="http://www.lavenderandcream.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/double-chocolate-protein-cheesecake.png" width="1172"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive been working on this recipe for about two weeks. Dozens of trial cakes have been eaten during this journey*, but Ive finally developed a recipe that I think you all will love.
        </p>
        <p>
         *Not complaining.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-1015">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         It took me a while to really perfect this recipe. I was testing the protein cheesecake (notoriously known as #procheesecake on the instasphere) and while it tasted perfect, there was just somethingoffabout it. I kept adjusting the ingredients and remaking it, trying to pin point what exactly was going wrong when it finally it hit me.
         <em>
          It just wasnt creamy enough.
         </em>
         It was coming out more like ricotta and less like cream. I need my cheesecake creamy, smooth, velvety, silky even. I finally realized that the cooking time played a huge role in the end result of my cheesecake. Long story shortI was over cooking it.
        </p>
        <p>
         So heres a tip: your cheesecake is perfect is when the outer rim is sturdy but the middle seems almost like jell-o. Itll jiggle as your take it out of the oven and make you think that it isnt quite done. Youll be tempted to leave it in the oven for jussst a minute longer but dont give in! As it cools it will continue to cook, and youll end up with a cheesecake that is picture perfect.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="double chocolate protein cheesecake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1025" height="3264" src="http://www.lavenderandcream.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_32991.jpg" width="2448"/>
         <a href="http://www.lavenderandcream.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_3298.jpg">
          <img alt="double chocolate protein cheesecake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1020" height="3264" src="http://www.lavenderandcream.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_3298.jpg" width="2448"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.lavenderandcream.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_3294.jpg">
          <img alt="double chocolate protein cheesecake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1017" height="2448" src="http://www.lavenderandcream.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_3294.jpg" width="3264"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Presenting the double chocolate protein cheesecake!
        </p>
        <p>
         Coming in at almost 14g protein per serving and only 184 calories, this dessert is far from sinfulbut it sure doesnt taste guilt-free! Of course I chose to make my procheesecake double chocolate flavored (duh) but you could leave out the cocoa powder and chocolate chips for a classic cheesecake (just as yum), or add other flavors and play around with the recipepumpkin, perhaps?
        </p>
        <p>
         This recipe makes eight servings, but heres the nutritional breakdown for the ENTIRE cheesecake:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="double chocolate protein cheesecake nutrition" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1026" height="1982" src="http://www.lavenderandcream.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/cheesecake-nutrition.jpg" width="2248"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Those are some gorgeous stats (remember to divide by 8 for serving stats).
        </p>

          <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="0">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Ingredients
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             2 scoops whey protein (I used chocolate but you could use any to change up the flavor)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             1/2 cup Truvia baking blend (or 1 cup sugar, or sweetener of choice)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
             2 room temp. eggs
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
             1.5 cups room temp. unsweetened Greek yogurt
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
             16oz. room temp. Fat free cream cheese (or low fat)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="6">
             2 tsp vanilla extract
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="7">
             1 tbs Enjoy Life mini chocolate chips
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="1">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Instructions
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
             Preheat oven to 250 degrees
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">
             Mix whey, Truvia, and cocoa powder in a small bowl
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">
             Mix eggs, Greek yogurt, cream cheese and vanilla extract until combined (do not overbeat)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">
             Add chocolate chips and give it one last stir before transferring into a round, greased 9" springform pan
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="4">
             Bake at 250 degrees for 30 minutes, then bake for another 30-40 minutes at 300 degrees
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="5">
             Let cool for at least one hour, then transfer to the fridge
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-note-section" yumprintsection="2">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Notes
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-notes">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="0">
             If wet ingredients are not room temp, they will be harder to mix-causing you to overbeat, causing air bubbles in the cake.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="1">
             Cheesecake is done when the outer rim is sturdy and the middle is still "wet" looking and jiggly.
            </li>
           </ol>
