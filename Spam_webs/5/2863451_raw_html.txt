       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Acorn OREO Cookie Balls
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-categories">
          <a href="http://www.homemadeinterest.com/category/recipes/desserts/" rel="category tag">
           Desserts
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="5496950" data-link="http://www.homemadeinterest.com/acorn-oreo-balls/" data-summary="" data-title="Acorn OREO Cookie Balls">
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          This #OREOCookieBalls shop has been compensated by #CollectiveBias, Inc. and its advertiser. All opinions are ours alone.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Acorn OREO Cookie Balls
         </strong>
         are not only easy to make they are delicious. Just a few simple ingredients and a little creativity and youve got the perfect treat for any occasion. #OREOCookieBalls #CollectiveBias
         <img alt="Acorn OREO balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO cookie ball." class="aligncenter wp-image-2984 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.homemadeinterest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Acorn-Oreo-Balls_pinterest.jpg" title="Acorn OREO cookie balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO truffles." width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im sure youve all had, or at least heard of, a cake ball by now, but have you ever had an
         <a href="http://www.deliciousmomentsofjoy.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="OREO Delicious Moments of Joy">
          OREO cookie ball
         </a>
         ? Seriously guys, they are amazing. I made them as favors for a friends wedding a few years ago and they were a hit. I actually prefer them to cake balls because OREO cookies are my favorite cookie ever, and because they are mixed with cream cheese rather than frosting so they arent quite as sweet as a cake ball.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-3023">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Acorn OREO balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO cookie ball." class="aligncenter wp-image-2989 size-full" height="758" src="http://www.homemadeinterest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Acorn-Oreo-Balls_instore.jpg" title="Acorn OREO cookie balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO truffles." width="400"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         For Thanksgiving Melinda and I decided to try our hand at making some acorn OREO cookie balls for Sawyers class. Walmart has so many OREO flavors to choose from, we decided to try something new and use Peanut Butter OREOS. Yes, they are as good as they sound. If you decide to try making some of your own use this
         <a href="http://coupons.walmart.com/?cid=18519004" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Walmart Coupon for OREO cookies">
          Walmart coupon for $0.50 off
         </a>
         !
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Acorn OREO balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO cookie ball." class="aligncenter wp-image-2987 size-full" height="600" src="http://www.homemadeinterest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Acorn-Oreo-Balls_tutorial.jpg" title="Acorn OREO cookie balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO truffles." width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The recipe is so easy. Its just three ingredients, OREO cookies, cream cheese and chocolate. You mix the crushed OREO cookies and the cream cheese together then form the mixture into balls. We made ours slightly oblong with a flat bottom so we could put a cap on them. Once they were shaped we stuck them in the freezer for about 10 minutes to firm up. That makes them easier to dip, and helps the chocolate harder faster.
        </p>
        <p>
         After 10 minutes take your OREO cookie balls out of the freezer and push a lollipop stick into the flat end. We just used the stick to make it easier to dip and then dry smoothly. Chop up the chocolate that youll be using as a coating and melt it in the microwave. I like to use a coffee mug, or something tall and narrow so that the melted chocolate is deeper and I can dip the ball straight into it, covering it completely, and then pull it back out. After you pull it out slowly rotate it and tap the stick against the edge of the mug letting the excess chocolate fall back into the mug. When you feel like it wont drip anymore put the ball aside to dry. We use a piece of styrofoam to push the stick down into so that it can dry upright.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Acorn OREO balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO cookie ball." class="aligncenter wp-image-3027 size-full" height="600" src="http://www.homemadeinterest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Acorn-Oreo-Balls_tutorial1.jpg" title="Acorn OREO cookie balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO truffles." width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The chocolate will harden fairly quickly because the balls are so cold, once the chocolate is firm move the balls to the refrigerator and chill them. This just makes them easier to handle for the next step.
        </p>
        <p>
         The last step is to add the cap. Take your cookie balls out of the refrigerator and pull the sticks out of them. Place a little chocolate on a Nilla Wafer and place it on the flat end of the OREO cookie ball. Then put a small dab of chocolate on a peanut butter chip and place that on top of the wafer. Now you have a delicious acorn!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Acorn OREO balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO cookie ball." class="aligncenter wp-image-2986 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.homemadeinterest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Acorn-Oreo-Balls_tag.jpg" title="Acorn OREO cookie balls are simple to make all you need is OREO cookies, cream cheese, and melted chocolate to make a delicious OREO truffles." width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         These would be great as a classroom treat, a Thanksgiving dessert, or even a hostess gift to say thank you to whoever is cooking the turkey this year. Weve even made a printable tag for you to save you a little time.
        </p>

            36 OREO Cookies, finely crushed (about 3 cups)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 pkg. (4 oz. each) semi-sweet baking chocolate, melted
           </li>

            MIX cream cheese and cookie crumbs until blended.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            SHAPE into 48 (1-inch) balls. Freeze 10 min. Dip balls in melted chocolate; place in single layer in shallow waxed paper-lined pan.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            CUSTOMIZE it with your own ingredient additions or decorative skills.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            REFRIGERATE 1 hour or until firm.
           </li>
