        <h1 class="title">
         George Soros Warns "No Exaggeration" That China-US On "Threshold Of World War 3"
        </h1>
        <div class="tabs">
        </div>
        <div class="node">
         <div class="picture">
          <a href="/users/tyler-durden" title="View user profile.">

         <div class="content">
          <p>
           While admitting that reaching agreement between the two countries will be difficult to achieve, George Soros -
           <em>
            <a href="http://www.marketwatch.com/story/soros-sees-risk-of-another-world-war-2015-05-19">
             speaking at The World Bank's Bretton Woods conference this week
            </a>
           </em>
           - warned that
           <strong>
            <em>
             unless the U.S. makes 'major concessions' and allows China's currency to join the IMF's basket of currencies, "there is a real danger China will align itself with Russia politically and militarily, and then the threat of world war becomes real."
            </em>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Much in global geopolitics depends on the health and trajectory of the Chinese economy,
           </strong>
           was the undertone of George Soros' comments as he spoke this week,
           <a href="http://www.marketwatch.com/story/soros-sees-risk-of-another-world-war-2015-05-19">
            but as MarketWatch reports,
           </a>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            Billionaire investor
            <strong>
             George Soros said flatly that hes concerned about the possibility of another world war.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            ...
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             If Chinas efforts to transition to a domestic-demand led economy from an export engine falter,
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              there is a likelihood that Chinas rulers would foster an external conflict to keep the country together and hold on to power
             </span>
             .
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            ...
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             To avoid this scenario, Soros called on the U.S. to make a major concession and allow Chinas currency to join the International Monetary Funds basket of currencies.
            </strong>
            This would make the yuan a potential rival to the dollar as a global reserve currency.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            In return, China would have to make similar major concessions to reform its economy, such as accepting the rule of law, Soros said.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Allowing Chinas yuan to be a market currency would create a binding connection between the two systems.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            An agreement along these lines will be difficult to achieve, Soros said, but the alternative is so unpleasant.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <strong>
              Without it, there is a real danger that China will align itself with Russia politically and militarily, and then the threat of third world war becomes real, so it is worth trying.
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           And while on the topic, Soros also spoke recently,
           <a href="http://www.valuewalk.com/2015/05/soros-china-is-risk-for-world-war-3/">
            as ValueWalk notes,
           </a>
           on
           <strong>
            the situation in Europe...
           </strong>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The European Union was a very inspiring idea to people like me,
            </strong>
            he commented, reflecting back to when EU economies were more balanced. It was the embodiment of the idea of an open society, like minded countries getting together and sacrificing part of their sovereignty for the common good. It was meant to be a voluntary association of equals.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Soros continued to say:
            <strong>
             Because of the Euro crisis, [the E.U.] has been transformed into something radically different.
            </strong>
            He also emphasized that over time two different classes of countries have evolved: creditors and debtors. The debtors had difficulty meeting their obligations and this put the creditors in charge. They (the creditors) set the rules and made it very difficult for the debtors to exit their inferior status. A voluntary association of equals turned into an involuntary association of un-equals.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            While avoiding making predictions, on Greece Soros noted:
            <strong>
             Greece is a poisonous situation. All sides have made a lot of mistakes, and there is a lot of hostility, a lot of negative sentimentsBoth sides are willing to hurt the other side even if it hurts them.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           * * *
          </p>
          <p>
           The billionaire investor concluded by pointing out that military spending is currently on the rise in both Russia and China, warning ominously...
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <strong>
              "If there is conflict between China and a military ally of the United States, like Japan, then it is not an exaggeration to say that we are on the threshold of a third world war."
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
