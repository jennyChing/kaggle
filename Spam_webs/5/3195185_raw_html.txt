       <div class="the_content">
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38214" height="336" src="http://media.parentsociety.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/summer-swimsuits-family.jpg" width="506"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As summer comes to an end, I have a hard time dealing with the fact that we have to put our soon to be 8-year-old son back on medication for ADHD. Summers are the best. It is exhausting having to entertain three kids at home  but it is so fun! And there is never a dull moment. Our family always has an even louder summer than most, because we take our oldest off ADHD medication for the season.
        </p>
        <p>
         During the school year, the meds help our son slow down and pay attention, but they have many negative side effects.The meds act as an appetite suppressant; they make him moody and irritable.The meds give him dry mouth and random tics.The meds give him insomnia and lower his immune system.The meds make him emotionally detached; he never hugs us.
        </p>
        <p>
         We obviously have not found the perfect dosage for him.So for all these reasons, we have taken him off his ADHD medication for the last three summers. He is loud, quick, and has way too much to say, but with these annoying characteristics comes some amazing things. He eats whatever I put in front of him; he even asks for broccoli.He can think logically about the health benefits of food, because hes not starving and binge eating out of pure hunger.He laughs, smiles, and is always hugging us. The hugs are hard and painful because he puts all his energy into it, but it is so great to see him love. He has ADHD, so when he is up, he is very up, and when he is down, he is very down  but only for a few minutes, because the ADHD makes him get over things quickly.
        </p>
        <p>
         He has been sleeping so well and actually sleeping in, compared to a midnight bedtime and 4 a.m. wake time when he is on meds. These are some great things about our son that we can enjoy over the short summer. No meds has side effects as well.He is so quick and rough that he has lots of accidents, running into walls, hitting his brothers, breaking more things than I can count on accident. He is an amazing reader, but without the meds he cant sit for more than a minute in between shouting and doing flips on the couch to read anything.
        </p>
        <p>
         So you can see my internal dilemma. This sweet, loving, very spirited boy is about to be put back into a quiet box.What can I do about it? Home school, private school, new or different meds? Yes and no to all these options.I have exhausted every aspect. I just dont know what to do.
        </p>
        <p>
         I wish summer were a little longer. I wish I could enjoy my loud ball of joy a little bit longer.Heres to an amazing summer and to a hopeful school year.
        </p>
