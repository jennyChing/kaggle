          <div class="post-inner group">
           <h1 class="post-title">
            Why I Carry a GLOCK 19 9mm
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://thefirearmsshow.com/author/kerryg/" rel="author" title="Posts by KerryG">
             KerryG
            </a>
             March 9, 2014
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <p>
              <a href="http://i0.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_5826.jpg" rel="lightbox[703]">
               <img alt="IMG_5826" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-208" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_5826.jpg?resize=300%2C200" title="GLOCK 19"/>
              </a>
              Since I get asked about my daily carry
              <b>
               gun
              </b>
              probably more often than anything else, I finally decided to write it all out so I can just point people to an article and not have to explain it every time. When I decided that I wanted to pick my EDC
              <i>
               gun
              </i>
              my selection process took right at three months. This was not a decision I was going to make without extensive research and testing. The decision was based on a set of criteria:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Reliability
              </li>
              <li>
               Size (carry consideration)
              </li>
              <li>
               My ability to shoot it accurately
              </li>
              <li>
               Caliber
              </li>
              <li>
               Round capacity
              </li>
              <li>
               Safety considerations
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              This article is not intended to sell you specifically on the
              <b>
               GLOCK 19
              </b>
              , this is about the process of selecting something that is best
              <strong>
               <em>
                for you
               </em>
              </strong>
              .
             </p>
             <h2>
              Caliber and Round Capacity
             </h2>
             <p>
              <a href="http://i2.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_5801.jpg" rel="lightbox[703]">
               <img alt="IMG_5801" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-205" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_5801.jpg?resize=300%2C200" title="GLOCK 19"/>
              </a>
              Lets start off with the most controversial topic of caliber. If you are of the mindset that the .45 is the best man-stopper round in existence and that anyone carrying less than a hand canon is as dumb as a wet brick, then you should probably just skip to the next section. For some people, a .45
              <strong>
               <em>
                is
               </em>
              </strong>
              the best choice for them and thats totally fine. For others its a .22 and thats fine as well. Whatever makes
              <strong>
               <em>
                you
               </em>
              </strong>
              comfortable and allows
              <em>
               <strong>
                you
               </strong>
              </em>
              to hit the target is the best choice for
              <strong>
               <em>
                you
               </em>
              </strong>
              . I chose 9mm because its a balance between firepower, recoil, cost, and capacity. A good defensive 9mm round is damned near as effective as a .45 and given that in only a very small percentage of defensive
              <u>
               gun
              </u>
              uses is this even going the relevant.
             </p>
             <p>
              The argument here is that the stopping power of a .45 far outweighs that of a 9mm, after all, the military used the .45 for over a century. While there is a nugget of truth in that argument, the flip side is that the military issues full metal jacket rounds and not hollow point rounds as do most police departments. The larger, heavier hitting .45 would certainly make a difference when using only FMJ rounds. A hollow point 9mm will be more destructive than a .45 FMJ.
             </p>
             <p>
              Yes, I know the facts about how many rounds are typically fired in a defensive gun use (2.5) but while criminals may not be the sharpest tools in the shed, they do tend to have a little bit of survival instinct and more and more burglaries and attacks are happening with multiple attackers. Generally you only need to give one a small dose of lead poisoning to make the others turn tail and run, but the 15 round capacity of my
              <i>
               GLOCK 19
              </i>
              is double that of my 1911 .45 and is a nice bit of insurance in the case of multiple attackers.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Frame Size and Weight
             </h2>
             <p>
              <a href="http://i1.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/iver_johnson_1911A1-4.jpg" rel="lightbox[703]">
               <img alt="iver_johnson_1911A1-4" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-188" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/iver_johnson_1911A1-4.jpg?resize=300%2C244" title="GLOCK 19"/>
              </a>
              Carrying a concealed handgun is not comfortable and anyone that says theirs is so good that they forget its there is either delusional or has developed an enormous callous that prevents them from feeling anything. I wanted to find a gun that was lightweight and a little smaller than a full frame gun. I wasnt looking at pocket pistols as they caliber and capacity werent there. The
              <u>
               GLOCK 19
              </u>
              is almost identical proportions to a 1911 Commander which I had already thought was pretty much the ideal size for me. While I did like the
              <b>
               GLOCK 17
              </b>
              , the GLOCK 19 was just a hair smaller which fit my criteria better.
             </p>
             <p>
              The GLOCK 19, being a polymer frame, is lighter than a steel frame 1911 which means it is a little less noticeable for you when in a holster.
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_189" style="width: 610px">
              <a href="http://i0.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/iver_johnson_1911A1-5.jpg" rel="lightbox[703]">
               <img alt="Glock 19 on left / 1911A1 Commander on right" class="size-full wp-image-189" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/iver_johnson_1911A1-5.jpg?resize=600%2C900" title="GLOCK 19"/>
              </a>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Glock 19 on left / 1911A1 Commander on right
              </p>
             </div>
             <h2>
              SafetyConsiderations
             </h2>
             <p>
              There are three basic arguments when it comes to safety features on handguns:
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               External safeties like on the 1911 style guns are necessary and are simply to disengage
              </li>
              <li>
               A self-defense gun should have minimal safeties to ensure that there is no mechanical failure
              </li>
              <li>
               A double-action/single-action is the best because the first trigger pull is much heavier the rest
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              There are extremely valid arguments to any of these and some explain why I have a GLOCK 19 as my EDC, a 1911 in my bedroom, and my wife prefers her Berreta PX4 Storm SC. I am not a fan of the safety of the 1911 (yes, I love 1911s and own one as mentioned) but in the heat of a situation, I dont want to forget it, have it fail, or snag when unholstering. What I wanted was a gun with reasonable safety against an accidental discharge and one that would do its job if you had to pull the attitude adjustment switch.
             </p>
             <p>
              Where there are some advantages of a double-action/single-action, I just didnt find one that appealed to me.
             </p>
             <p>
              The GLOCK 19s safe-action trigger works to prevent a discharge if the trigger is snagged or not used properly.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Reliability
             </h2>
             <p>
              <a href="http://i2.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_5820.jpg" rel="lightbox[703]">
               <img alt="IMG_5820" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-207" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/thefirearmsshow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_5820.jpg?resize=300%2C208" title="GLOCK 19"/>
              </a>
              If your gun doesnt go boom when your booger hook pulls the bang switch, then what good is the gun to you? Yes, I realize that your 1911 has never misfired and is the most reliable gun you have ever used, but facts speak for themselves. The
              <i>
               GLOCK 17
              </i>
              /19 have proven themselves to be the most reliable and consistent guns ever made. No 1911 has come close in the reliability tests by any military group or law enforcement department. When you need a mute button that will work every time, the GLOCK is the best remote control for me.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Shooting Accuracy
             </h2>
             <p>
              While
              <em>
               <strong>
                you
               </strong>
              </em>
              may shoot more accurately with your 1911, your Sig 226, your 92F or your Kimber Custom, but I shoot most accurately with the
              <u>
               GLOCK 17
              </u>
              /19 platform. I dont know why but I do. Maybe its the weight, the grip angle, the trigger, or something else but when shooting comparisons came into play, I nailed it with the GLOCK 19.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Summary
             </h2>
             <p>
              As I said before, this is not a GLOCK 19 commercial, it is about the thought process that
              <strong>
               <em>
                I
               </em>
              </strong>
              went through when choosing a personal defense handgun that I would carry on a daily basis. You may want a .45 or a .380 or a 10mm, I am not going to debate your choice of caliber, that is a decision that you have to make. Maybe you are OK with 7, 8, 10 or however many rounds a particular gun can hold, again, those are choices you make about your comfort level. Probably most importantly, how does a gun feel to you? Does it fit your hand, is it comfortable to shoot, and do you shoot it well? Those are extremely important considerations. Whatever you decide, make sure it is your choice and not some interweb gun weenie like myself trying to force their opinions down your throat.
             </p>
