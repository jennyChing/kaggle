           <p>
            Netflix Inc. (
            <a href="/company/nflx/" target="_blank">
             NFLX
            </a>
            ) is a major internet TV provider based out of the US. It is a market leader with approximately 38 million streaming subscribers in 40 countries around the world. It not only provides online video streaming, it also delivers DVDs and Blu-ray disks to subscribers doorsteps.
           </p>
           <p>
            Netflixs success can be credited to its early adoption of a secular trend from traditional to digital media. It has surprised the world by continuously growing its subscriber base and consistently outperforming other TV providers like Hulu, Comcast (
            <a href="/company/cmcsa/" target="_blank">
             CMCSA
            </a>
            ), Time Warner Cable (
            <a href="/company/twc/" target="_blank">
             TWC
            </a>
            ), Dish Network (
            <a href="/company/dish/" target="_blank">
             DISH
            </a>
            ) and Direct TV (
            <a href="/company/dtv/" target="_blank">
             DTV
            </a>
            ).  Its stock price is up approximately 222% year to date, following continuous innovation and new additions to its content library.
           </p>
           <p>
            Netflix has been able to maintain higher growth in revenues over the last few quarters as compared to other TV providers because it has had a high success rate in pulling subscribers from conventional cable and satellite TV providers. Netflix added a net 1.71 million subscribers (of streaming content and DVDs both) in the second quarter of fiscal year 2013 (2QFY13), despite a loss of 0.48 million DVD subscribers. In comparison, other TV providers lost 80,000 to 160,000 subscribers over the same period.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/Netflix-increase-total-subscribers-1024x577.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/Netflix-video-revenue-growth-1024x577.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Read our article on
            </em>
            <a href="http://bidnessetc.com/2013/10/02/small-screen-next-big-thing/">
             <em>
              digital TV
             </em>
            </a>
            <em>
             for a detailed analysis of the shift from traditional to digital media.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            Two of Netflixs most successful differentiation strategies include a focus on original content and expansion into international markets.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Focus on Original Content
           </h2>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Netflixs major focus is on providing original and exclusive content to customers by licensing content and signing deals with various content producers. The strategy has allowed it to stand out against other TV providers, most of which do not have original content. Netflixs most popular series include House of Cards, Hemlock Grove, Lilyhammer, Arrested Development, and Orange is the New Black.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The success of Netflixs original content is evident from the fact that three of its shows earned 14 Emmy nominations this year. House of Cards earned nine nominations, Arrested Development three and Hemlock Grove two nominations. House of Cards walked away with the first award for Netflix.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Netflixs cash spend on producing content increased from approximately $0.41 billion in 2010 to $2.5 billion in 2012, and it plans to continue investing in original content to grow its subscriber base. Although investments in original content impact cash flows negatively, Netflixs margins expand through the resultant increase in subscribers.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Netflixs US streaming subscribers grew 24.5% YoY in 2QFY13, while its contribution margins expanded 610 basis points  210 basis points higher than Netflixs target of 400 basis points. Netflix continues to target an expansion of 400 basis points in its contribution margins every year.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/netflix-03-1024x656.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Netflix launched a series named Mako Mermaids in July 2013 to target teen audiences, followed by the series Derek by Ricky Gervais in September 2013. It plans to launch season two of Lilyhammer and Turbo by the year end. Netflixs plans for 2014 include the launch of the second seasons of House of Cards, Hemlock Grove, and Orange is the New Black series, along with multiple DreamWorks Animations programs for kids.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Netflixs management believes that its subscriber base has the potential to increase to 60-90 million, which means a growth of approximately 60-137% in the current subscriber base of 38 million. Following the success of its TV series, Netflix might even start diversifying into talk shows, news programs and even movies.
           </p>
           <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
            International Expansion:
           </h2>
           <p>
            Netflix has witnessed higher growth in international streaming compared to domestic streaming. As the US market matures, international markets will allow Netflix to sustain its growth. Netflixs YoY revenue growth from international streaming was 155% in 2QFY13, compared to 21% growth in US streaming over 2QFY13.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/netflix-04-1024x601.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Although Netflixs international streaming is currently running losses, its management is redirecting revenues from the US due to its high growth potential. Growth in the number of Netflixs international subscribers is helping it restrict contribution losses as well. International subscribers increased by approximately 114% YoY in 2QFY13, which led to a 25.8% decrease in contribution losses over the same period.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Continuous innovation and additions to the content library and international expansion remain the key drivers for Netflixs growth. Although the number of DVD subscribers, who currently contribute approximately 25% of Netflixs total revenues, is falling, growth in Netflixs streaming revenues is large enough to offset the loss. This is evident from the fact that Netflixs total subscribers (streaming + DVDs) increased by approximately 22.5% YoY in 2QFY13 despite an 18.7% fall in DVD subscribers over the same period.
           </p>
