        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Bahian Fried Halloumi Cheese
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-07-03T03:00:44+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          July 3, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.garnishwithlemon.com/author/admin2/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Lisa
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.garnishwithlemon.com/bahian-fried-halloumi-cheese/#comments">
           28 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <a data-pin-color="white" data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" data-pin-height="20" data-pin-lang="en" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/">
         <img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pinit_fg_en_rect_white_20.png"/>
        </a>
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
           Celebrate World Cup soccer and taste the flavors of Brazil with Bahian Fried Halloumi Cheese.
          </span>
         </em>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.garnishwithlemon.com/bahian-fried-halloumi-cheese/">
          <img alt="Bahian Fried Halloumi Cheese" class="aligncenter wp-image-4758 size-full" data-pin-description="Bahian Fried Halloumi Cheese ~ http://www.garnishwithlemon.com" height="874" src="http://www.garnishwithlemon.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Bahian-Fried-Halloumi-Cheese-1-of-2.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          Has the World Cup Soccer craze hit your house like it has mine?
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          Even Greta, who prefers HGTV to pretty much anything else on television, is into the World Cup frenzy this summer.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          So I decided to make the most of my familys enthusiasmand tackle a Brazilian-inspired dish for us to enjoy while watching the action. Fortunately I knew just where to go for my flavor inspiration. When we were in Miami for BlogHer Food in May, we learned about the 2014
          <a href="http://www.mccormick.com/Flavor-Forecast-2014" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mccormick.com/Flavor-Forecast-2014', 'McCormick Flavor Forecast');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           McCormick Flavor Forecast
          </a>
          . Every year, McCormick gathers information from top foodie sources from around the globe. This latest and greatest intel reflects emerging trends and key cultural influences that are shaping the tastes of tomorrow, including flavors from the home of this years World Cup: Brazil. Using an easy-to-make, all-purpose seasoning blend from the Brazilian state of Bahia, I made sure my soccer-loving family never went hungry while watching the latest big match with this
          <strong>
           Bahian Fried Halloumi Cheese
          </strong>
          .
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          I mean, cmonCan you really go wrong with fried cheese?
         </span>
         <span id="more-4724">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          Since we cant be in Brazil to watch the World Cup live, its fun to bring a little bit of that country here and experiment with Brazils melting pot of flavors from European, African, Asian and Native Amazonian influences.Fried cheese is a traditional snack in Brazil made with queijo coalho (a firm rennet cheese that keeps its shape when heated), perfect for pan frying or grilling and eating on a stick. I used halloumi because it has the same characteristics and is more readily available here in the US. And the salty halloumi cheese is the perfect companion to the lively Bahian spice blend filled with smoky cumin, dried oregano, ground white pepper and finished with a touch of heat from ground cayenne pepper. Add a little olive oil, sear it in a pan and youre in business.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          Perfect party food.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.garnishwithlemon.com/bahian-fried-halloumi-cheese/">
          <img alt="Bahian Fried Halloumi Cheese " class="aligncenter wp-image-4759 size-full" data-pin-description="Bahian Fried Halloumi Cheese ~ http://www.garnishwithlemon.com" height="603" src="http://www.garnishwithlemon.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Bahian-Fried-Halloumi-Cheese-2-of-2.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
