               <p>
                I made a post a coupleweeks ago about the sale at Target. In the post I had mentioned I make my own food for Ezra and I had several people inquire about what I make for her. So Im going to start a small series on different types of food I cook for her.
               </p>
               <p>
                Today, I decided to make some applesauce for her. I buy a bag of apples for $2.50. With one bag of apples, I can make about 22oz of baby applesauce. A lot of people ask why I bother, with applesauce alone, Im saving about $.25 per jar! Thats a savings of almost $100 a year in just applesauce! Plus, I like applesauce so its a win win!
               </p>
               <p>
                Now to the good part!
               </p>
               <p>
                Step 1: Peal and core the apples. I started out doing this by hand. Id peel the apple then cut it as close to the core as I could to get as much of the apple as possible. Then my mother turned me onto a cool kitchen toy,
                <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001C6E0Q6/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001C6E0Q6&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=porontheche-20&amp;linkId=IIGLWIWCKSSYKRYW" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001C6E0Q6/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001C6E0Q6&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=porontheche-20&amp;linkId=IIGLWIWCKSSYKRYW', 'The Pampered Chef Apple Peeler/Corer/Slicer']);">
                 The Pampered Chef Apple Peeler/Corer/Slicer
                </a>
                <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=porontheche-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B001C6E0Q6" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                . This thing does exactly what it says and in a matter of seconds!
                <img alt="2014-09-06 13.33.50" class="aligncenter wp-image-2326 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.missezra.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/2014-09-06-13.33.50-225x300.jpg" width="225"/>
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p>
                Step 2: Wash the apples  Most people wash the apples before they peal. I like to do it after I peal the apple.
                <img alt="2014-09-06 13.34.06" class="aligncenter wp-image-2339 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.missezra.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/2014-09-06-13.34.06-225x300.jpg" width="225"/>
               </p>
               <p>
                Step 3: Cook the apples  To cook the apples I use a
                <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000WIQJ9Q/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000WIQJ9Q&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=porontheche-20&amp;linkId=WVNEAZ3NRQSAPENS" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000WIQJ9Q/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000WIQJ9Q&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=porontheche-20&amp;linkId=WVNEAZ3NRQSAPENS', 'microwave steamer']);">
                 microwave steamer
                </a>
                <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=porontheche-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B000WIQJ9Q" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                because I like the convenience
                <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=porontheche-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B000WIQJ9Q" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                . I add about 1/4 cup of water and microwave it for 10 min. After the 10 min, I check to see how soft they are. I usually put back in for another 5-8 min depending on how many apples were in the bag.
                <img alt="2014-09-06 13.58.24" class="aligncenter wp-image-2340 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.missezra.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/2014-09-06-13.58.24-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p>
                Step 4: Now its time to puree  Again, I like simple and fast so I use my
                <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003XU3C7M/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B003XU3C7M&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=porontheche-20&amp;linkId=F5TTDQNJO56ROLFK" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003XU3C7M/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B003XU3C7M&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=porontheche-20&amp;linkId=F5TTDQNJO56ROLFK', 'blender']);">
                 blender
                </a>
                <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=porontheche-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B003XU3C7M" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                for this step. I just dump the apples in with the liquid in the bowl and blend.
               </p>
               <p>
                <img alt="2014-09-06 14.00.22" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2332" height="300" src="http://www.missezra.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/2014-09-06-14.00.22-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p>
                Step 5: Into the freezer  After the apples are all pureed I put them into ice cube trays. This is the easiest way I have found to make it easy to handle and keep fresh for Ezra. After frozen, Ill remove from the trays and put them into gallon sized bags and leave in the freezer. When Its time to eat, Ill grab the number of 1 oz cubes I want to feed her, thaw and feed! If its a little too watery Ill mix in a little cereal to thicken up if needed .
               </p>
               <p>
                <img alt="2014-09-06 14.08.58" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2338" height="300" src="http://www.missezra.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/2014-09-06-14.08.58-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
               </p>
               <p>
                Have you ever made your own applesauce? Id love to hear how you do it!
               </p>
