                <h2 class="blog-title">
                 <a class="blog-title-link blog-link" href="http://www.collegefootballuniverseblog.com/blog/east-carolinas-first-10-win-season-since-1991">
                  East Carolina's first 10-win season since 1991
                 </a>
                </h2>
                <p class="blog-date">
                 <span class="date-text">
                  12/25/2013
                 </span>
                </p>
                <!-- 333083055908420746 -->
                <p class="blog-comments">
                 <a class="blog-link" href="http://www.collegefootballuniverseblog.com/blog/east-carolinas-first-10-win-season-since-1991#comments">
                  0 Comments
                 </a>
                </p>

               <div class="blog-separator">
               </div>
               <div class="blog-content">
                <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
                 <strong style="">
                  <a href="http://www.collegefootballuniverseblog.com/justin-burnette.html" style="" target="_blank" title="">
                   By Justin Burnette
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:left">
                  <a>
                   <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/7/2/4/4/7244881/8887064.jpg?499" style="width:auto;max-width:100%"/>
                  </a>
                  <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
                   East Carolina OL Ike Harris post game being knighted by ECU mascot or maybe it's pirated?
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
                 On Monday, East Carolina beat Ohio 37-20 in the Beef 'O' Brady's Bowl for their first postseason win since 2007 and their first 10-win season since 1991 when they won 11 games including a victory over North Carolina State in the Peach Bowl. There have only been two double-digit wins in the program's history.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 The Pirates did it in record breaking fashion against Ohio. ECU gained 281 rushing yards, 6.9 yards per carry, three rushing touchdowns, and 568 total yards - all Beef 'O' Brady's Bowl records.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 "In the first half, the offensive line played way beyond what was expected,"said ECU running back Vintavious Cooper. "But for us, that's the standard that we hold for our team. We hold ourselves to a high standard."
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Cooper, the bowl's MVP, set the bowl's single-game record for rushing yards with 198. The senior running back has great vision on the field and good cutting ability.Quarterback Shane Carden added to the accolades by setting the bowl's mark for completions (29) and attempts (44).
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 On the season, Cooper rushed for 1,193 yards and 13 touchdowns while Carden passed for 4,139 yards, rushed for 103 yards, and scored 43 total touchdowns with 10 interceptions."We wanted it so bad. We keptfighting," said Carden when asked about the fourth quarter surge todefeat Ohio.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Carden, who was named the C-USA Most Valuable Player for 2013, should continue to help put points on the board in 2014 when he returns for his senior year.In yet another achievement, East Carolina scored the most points in school history this season with 522 and the next closest was in 2010 when the Pirates tallied 478.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 ECU support may have dwindled some after the 59-28 blowout loss to Marshall for the C-USA East Division, but the fans have plenty to be proud of for the accomplishments this season. The previous two losses were only by a combined eight points against Virginia Tech and Tulane. ECU had a weak schedule overall this year, but they were able to beat two ACC schools and in-state rivals, North Carolina and NC State, to go along with the recent Beef 'O' Brady's Bowl victory.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 "I was asked what I want for Christmas and I replied, I got what I want for Christmas," said head coach Ruffin McNeill (29-22 at ECU). "I have 125 of my sons here in St. Petersburg where I can watch all of them."
