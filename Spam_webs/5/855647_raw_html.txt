       <div>
        <p>
         Although the name Marthas Vineyard may be a misnomer as far as wine production is concerned, the Island more than makes up for it in the culinary category, with great local seafood, as well as farm-raised meats and produce.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        These attributes make MV an excellent place to experience great food, fine wines, and the work of chefs who combine these ingredients. Even more fortuitously for visitors, the events during upcoming months present the perfect opportunity to make the most of this scene!
       </p>
       <p>
        Starting in May, Marthas Vineyard embarks on a season of celebrating food and drink. As youll discover, events focused on wine, local producers, and restaurants, along with just eating and drinking with good friends in beautiful surroundings, begin in the spring and continue throughout the summer and into the fall.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you are thinking of visiting the Island and love to eat, drink and celebrate dining at its best, keep these dates in mind:
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         May 8-11
        </b>
        <br/>
        First out of the gate, and coinciding with Mothers Day weekend, is the
        <a href="http://www.winefestmv.com/" target="_blank">
         Marthas Vineyard Wine Festival
        </a>
        . In its second year, the Festival focuses on wine tastings and pairing dinners held in some of the Islands loveliest restaurants and private homes. Its most unique component, however, is the
        <a href="http://www.winefestmv.com/events-and-tickets.html" target="_blank">
         Man vs. Wild Food Challenge
        </a>
        , an island-wide competition in which participants vie to create the most extraordinary meal using foraged, local ingredients. Results from past year have been impressive and the contest welcomes professionals, amateurs and kids. To learn more about the Man vs. Wild Food Challenge and everything else happening at the Marthas Vineyard Wine Festival, click
        <a href="http://www.winefestmv.com" target="_blank">
         here
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <b>
         June 12
        </b>
        <br/>
        The long-established
        <a href="http://www.mvpreservation.org/p.php/preservation/happening" target="_blank">
         <i>
          Taste of the Vineyard Stroll
         </i>
        </a>
        marks the official start of the Vineyards summer social season. Island restaurateurs, wine merchants and foodies gather under the gleaming white tents set up on the grounds of the historic
        <a href="http://www.mvpreservation.org/p.php/wedding/venues/daniel-fisher-house" target="_blank">
         Dr. Daniel Fisher House
        </a>
        on Main Street in Edgartown. Restaurants participating in this annual MV Preservation Trust fundraiser strive to put their best plates forward, laden with tempting bites offered up for the pleasure of Strollers, nibblers and grazers. Later in the evening, live music lures sated guests onto the dance floor. As the Stroll is always a sell-out, you are well advised to book early if you are interested in attending. Click
        <a href="http://www.mvpreservation.org/p.php/preservation/happening" target="_blank">
         here
        </a>
        for details.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Taste_DavidWelch.jpg">
         <img alt="Taste_DavidWelch" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5471" height="1001" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Taste_DavidWelch.jpg" width="1500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        Every June, beautifully presented tastes await guests at the MV Preservation Trusts Taste of the Vineyard. Photo by David Welch.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         June 14, Aug. 2, Oct. 25 &amp; 26
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
        </b>
        One of the more recent additions to the events calendar on Marthas Vineyard,
        <a href="http://www.ffsmv.com/info.html" target="_blank">
         <i>
          Farm. Field. Sea.
         </i>
        </a>
        bills itself as a culinary adventure. Hosted on three different occasions in three different locations, the dinners and the adventures are dictated by the sources of foods at each location. Participants experience how foods are obtained at the source, take part in learning how to coax the most out of their ingredients, cook their foods, and enjoy the conviviality of meals together. The June 14
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        event takes place at
        <a href="http://marthasvineyard.patch.com/listings/north-tabor-farm" target="_blank">
         North Tabor Farm
        </a>
        and the
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Menemsha-Fish-House/409555492428836" target="_blank">
         Menemsha Fish House
        </a>
        and is led by Chef Chris Fischer. On August 2, the venues are
        <a href="http://www.morninggloryfarm.com/" target="_blank">
         Morning Glory Farm
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.honeysuckleoyster.com/" target="_blank">
         Honeysuckle Oyster Farm
        </a>
        ; on October 25 &amp; 26, Chef Fischer hosts again at
        <a href="http://marthasvineyard.patch.com/listings/north-tabor-farm" target="_blank">
         North Tabor Farm
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.beetlebungfarm.com/home.html" target="_blank">
         Beetlebung Farm
        </a>
        . More information is available about
        <i>
         Farm. Field. Sea.
        </i>
        <a href="http://www.ffsmv.com/info.html" target="_blank">
         here
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Morning-Glory.jpg">
         <img alt="Morning Glory" height="1536" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Morning-Glory.jpg" width="2048"/>
        </a>
        <i>
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <i>
         Morning Glory Farm, one of the venues to be visited in this years Farm.Field.Sea experience.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        <i>
        </i>
        <b>
         June 15-19
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.mvrestaurantweek.com/" target="_blank">
         Restaurant Week
        </a>
        on Marthas Vineyard is the perfect opportunity to check out Island restaurants, pre-season and at special prices. From haute to homey, graze your way through a week of lunch and dinner menus that feature every aspect of Marthas Vineyard cuisine. The 4-day event offers dinner at the
        <a href="http://www.beachpluminn.com/" target="_blank">
         Beach Plum
        </a>
        restaurant up in Chilmark, where beach roses cover the path to the nearby Atlantic; a prix fixe dinner at the iconic
        <a href="https://www.theblackdog.com/" target="_blank">
         Black Dog
        </a>
        , harbor-side in Vineyard Haven; a lobster bake at Oak Bluffs
        <a href="http://www.coopdevillemv.com/" target="_blank">
         Coop de Ville
        </a>
        ; or a 3-course prix fixe dinner on the lawn of
        <a href="http://www.letoile.net">
         letoile
        </a>
        among Edgartown whaling captains homes and many, many more. Look for details upcoming on
        <a href="http://www.mvrestaurantweek.com/" target="_blank">
         http://www.mvrestaurantweek.com/
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chesca.jpg">
         <img alt="Chesca" height="2448" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chesca.jpg" width="3264"/>
        </a>
        <i>
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <i>
         Chescas in Edgartown has been a yearly participant in Marthas Vineyards Restaurant Week. Photo by David Welch.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <b>
         October 16-19
        </b>
        <br/>
        The 7
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        Annual
        <a href="http://www.mvfoodandwine.com/" target="_blank">
         Marthas Vineyard Food and Wine Festival
        </a>
        brings the season full circle, as travelers and residents honor the Islands harvest and plenty. Typically featuring presentations, tastings and dinners by outstanding chefs from Boston, the Island and beyond, the highlights of
        <a href="http://www.mvfoodandwine.com/mvfw/photos.html" target="_blank">
         last years event
        </a>
        included a Chocolate and Wine Tasting, Sommelier Throw-down, several Continental food tours, and the always-popular Grand Tasting. Visiting chefs included Andy Husbands of Tremont 647, Tim Cushman of o ya restaurant in Boston, and Hungry Mothers Barry Maiden. Though plans for the 2014 Festival have not yet been finalized, you can check in with the Marthas Vineyard Food and Wine Festival
        <a href="http://www.mvfoodandwine.com" target="_blank">
         website
        </a>
        , which should have all the details shortly.
       </p>
