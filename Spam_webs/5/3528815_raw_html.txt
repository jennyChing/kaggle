       <blockquote>
        <p>
         <span>
          Anna Boch was a Belgian painter,
         </span>
         <span>
          born in Saint-Vaast, Hainaut,
         </span>
         <span>
          in the 5th generation of the Boch family
         </span>
         <span>
          who made Ceramics since 1748.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          15 months later The Red Vineyard was exhibited in the
         </span>
         <strong>
          1890
         </strong>
         <span>
          The XX Art Expo in Brussels.
         </span>
         <span>
          Anna Boch bought it for 400 Francs (about 1600 $ in todays values). was the only painting that Van Gogh had sold in his life!!
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span>
          Different reasons might have been at the origine of the purchase:
         </span>
         <span>
          She must have liked the beauty of the painting.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span>
          She wanted to show public support to Van Gogh, as his arrival at the XX Expo was very criticized.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span>
          She wanted to financially support him as she knew the artist was very poor
         </span>
         <span>
          and last not least she might have wanted to please her
         </span>
         <span>
          younger brother
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.eugeneboch.com/" target="_blank">
          Eugene
         </a>
         <span>
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span>
          who was a friend of Vincent. read more
         </span>
