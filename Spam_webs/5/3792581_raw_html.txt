       <h1>
        The top 10 neighbourhoods for shopping in Toronto
       </h1>
       <p class="author">
        Posted by
        <a href="/author/alexbrown">
         Alex Brown
        </a>
        <span class="date">
         / January  5, 2015
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="stuff">
        <span class="comments">
         <a href="#comments">
          20 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
        <div class="clear">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
       <div class="content">
        <p>
         <img alt="neighbourhoods shopping toronto" height="393" src="http://www.blogto.com/upload/2014/12/20141224-neighbourhoods-shopping-toronto.jpg" width="590"/>
         The top neighbourhoods for shopping in Toronto allow for hours and hours of perusing at your leisure, with each additional store within reasonable walking distance from the last. For these trips, park your car or bike nearby and get your recyclable bags ready for some stuffing, since you surely won't leave empty-handed.
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are my picks for the top neighbourhoods for shopping in Toronto.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/westqueenwest">
           West Queen West
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         While
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/queenwest">
          Queen West
         </a>
         has given way to mainstream stores, West Queen West has a bit of everything: carefully curated vintage stores like
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/cabaretvintage">
          Cabaret
         </a>
         , boutiques dedicated to hard-to-find and cult lines, like
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/sauvage-toronto">
          Sauvage
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/gravitypope-toronto">
          Gravitypope
         </a>
         , and a dose of slick menswear from
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/nomad-queen-toronto">
          Nomad
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/sydneys">
          Sydney's
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/frank-and-oak-toronto">
          Frank &amp; Oak
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/dundaswest">
           Dundas West
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         What was formerly just Little Portugal - a hood littered with churrasqueira restaurants and European bakeries - has given way to fashion havens like the
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/blue-button-shop-toronto">
          Blue Button Shop
         </a>
         and perfectly minimalist spots like
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/bodega-thirteen-toronto">
          Bodega Thirteen
         </a>
         .
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/vsp-consignment-toronto">
          VSP Consignment
         </a>
         is a must-visit, along with vintage spots like
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/bridge-and-bardot-toronto">
          Bridge + Bardot
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/penny-arcade-vintage">
          Penny Arcade
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/n/81/ossington">
           Ossington
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Sandwiched between Dundas West and West Queen West, Lower Ossington's shopping haunts are scattered among its bars and dining destinations. At boutiques like
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/annie-aime-toronto">
          Annie Aime
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/crywolf-toronto">
          Crywolf
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/victoire-boutique-toronto">
          Victoire
         </a>
         , the vibe is fun and relaxed; step up your menswear game at
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/lost-found-ossington-toronto">
          Lost &amp; Found
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/philip-sparks-toronto">
          Philip Sparks
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/parkdale">
           Parkdale
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Between vintage mainstays like
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/publicbutter">
          Public Butter
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/common-sort-parkdale-toronto">
          Common Sort
         </a>
         , and newer shops like
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/sylvie-shimmy-toronto">
          Sylvie &amp; Shimmy
         </a>
         , Parkdale mixes old-fashioned surprises and modern variety.
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/three-fates-toronto">
          Three Fates
         </a>
         offers fun vintage and new finds on the cheap, while lifestyle shops
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/future-of-frances-watson-toronto">
          Future of Frances Watson
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/community-54-toronto">
          Community 54
         </a>
         are perfect for window shopping and/or dropping serious cash.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/kensington">
           Kensington Market
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Besides the jewellery, toques, and sunglass stands, Kensington boasts numerous shops dedicated to crafty jewellery and vintage pieces:
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/couragemylove">
          Courage My Love
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/exile-kensington-toronto">
          Exile
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/flashback">
          Flashback
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/bungalow">
          Bungalow
         </a>
         . Expect nothing new in this hood - with notable exceptions being Bungalow's front end,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/modelcitizen">
          Model Citizen
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/kid-icarus-augusta-ave-toronto">
          Kid Icarus
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/good-egg">
          Good Egg
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/roncesvalles ">
           Roncesvalles
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         If you're looking for those catch-all boutiques that carry great gift items, cards, soaps, accessories, and anything else under the sun, Roncesvalles has a sweet selection. At
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/likely-general-toronto">
          Likely General
         </a>
         you'll find everything from honey to tote bags, while
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/scout-toronto ">
          Scout
         </a>
         is stocked with super neat things you'd scour for on Etsy for hours. Also of note: Unisex Toronto-based brand
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/muttonhead-toronto">
          Muttonhead
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/kingeast">
           King East
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         King East is one of the best spots in the city for high end furniture and other home dcor necessities.
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/klaus">
          Klaus by Nienkamper
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/kiosk-toronto">
          Kiosk
         </a>
         provide all the high-design window-shopping you could ever want, while
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/radform-toronto">
          RADform
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/suite-22-interiors-richmond-toronto">
          Suite 22 Interiors
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/calligaris-toronto">
          Calligaris
         </a>
         will satisfy your need for contemporary fixings, i.e. expensive clear plastic furniture that appears to be from IKEA but is definitely much better quality.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/yonge-and-eglinton-toronto ">
           Yonge &amp; Eglinton
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Midtown's notable shopping destinations are often overlooked by their mainstream neighbours--yes, there's a huge RioCan shopping centre and one outstanding Shoppers Drug Mart - but there's also more than a few spots worth taking a gander at. There's
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/canon-blanc-yonge-toronto">
          Canon Blanc
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/poor-little-rich-girl ">
          Poor Little Rich Girl
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/heidi-ho-toronto">
          Heidi Ho
         </a>
         for women's fashions and jewellery;
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/squint-eyewear-toronto">
          Squint
         </a>
         for eyewear and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/sporting-life">
          Sporting Life
         </a>
         for the whole family.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/yorkville">
           Yorkville
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Yorkville has maintained its rep as the home of luxury brands and designer storefronts. Chanel and Prada aside, the area is also home to Toronto's own
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/seventy-seven-toronto">
          Pink Tartan
         </a>
         , while men's fashion stores cater to the high-end, tailored types - think
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/suitsupply-toronto ">
          Suitsupply
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/loding-toronto">
          Loding
         </a>
         . On the streetwear side, there's
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/contraband-toronto">
          Contraband
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/uncle-otis-toronto">
          Uncle Otis
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/working-title-toronto">
          Working Title
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion/untitled-and-co-yorkville-toronto">
          Untitled &amp; Co.
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.blogto.com/fashion_style/2009/11/the_castlefield_design_district/ ">
           Castlefield Design District
          </a>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         This north-end neighbourhood (near Eglinton and Caledonia) has the space to house the kind of massive home-design stores you can't always find downtown. Check out
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/elte">
          ELTE
         </a>
         's brand new
         <a href="http://www.eltemkt.com/">
          ELTE Market
         </a>
         space and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/shelter-furniture-toronto">
          Shelter
         </a>
         for modern furnishings, as well as vintage furniture stores like
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/door-store-toronto">
          The Door Store
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/kantelberg-co-toronto">
          Kantelberg + Co.
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/design/sharon-o-dowd">
          Sharon O'Dowd
         </a>
         (where you can customize your furniture, too).
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Did I miss any? Leave your favourite shopping neighbourhoods in Toronto in the comments.
         </em>
        </p>
