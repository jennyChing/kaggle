         <h2 id="id_deck" itemprop="alternativeHeadline">
          IGN kicks off its latest list, now with absolutely zero controversy.
         </h2>
        </h2>
       </hgroup>
       <span class="byline">
        By
        <span id="id_author" itemprop="author">
         IGN Staff
        </span>
       </span>
       <div class="articleBody" id="id_text" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         We all like to pretend, to make believe, to daydream. When we do, it's usually not about spreadsheets, laundry or homework. No, we dream of becoming heroes, becoming kings, becoming saviors, and doing whatever we like - right or wrong - while we conquer, save and survive harrowing adventures in faraway lands.
        </p>
        <p>
         No single genre in video games lets us do exactly this - in exactly the way we want - like role-playing games. The Top 100 RPGs of All Time list celebrates the games that we believe most accurately exemplify the tenants of the role-playing game genre: building characters based on experience earned in the game, scaling combat based on the statistics of weapons, armor and abilities, making decisions - good or bad - that impact the game world. These core fundamentals make up the heart and soul of the RPG, and it is with these values as our guide that we present to you IGN's Top 100 RPGs of All Time.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We will updating the list everyday with ten new entries, so bookmark this page and keep checking back! To make it easier to follow the updates, each page will have ten games. So far, it looks like this:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          - Page 1 - #100 to #90
          <br/>
          - Page 2 - #89 to #81
          <br/>
          - Page 3 - #80 to #70
          <br/>
          - Page 4 - #69 to #61
          <br/>
          - Page 5 - #60 to #50
          <br/>
          - Page 6 - #49 to #41
          <br/>
          - Page 7 - #40 to #30
          <br/>
          - Page 8 - #29 tp #20
          <br/>
          - Page 9 - #19 to #11
          <br/>
          - Page 10 - #10 to 01
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68284" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_gms6.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         For those who remember the TurboGrafx-16's pioneering CD-ROM add-on, Ys Book I &amp; II wasn't just the peripheral's killer app, it was a technical revelation. You knew right from the start when you heard the then-astounding voiceover narration (remember, every game up until then had been text dialogue only) that this would be something special. Heck, that narrator even told you how to properly pronounce the name of the game (it's like "fleece" without the 'fl', by the way).
        </p>
        <p>
         The astounding soundtrack lent Ys an air of authority, and the hack-and-slash gameplay proved to be both epic and challenging. To this day, there still isn't quite an RPG with the same wrinkle of gameplay that Ys offers. It's downloadable in 100% pure form today from the Wii Virtual Console, too.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68285" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_bq7w.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Lunar: Eternal Blue is one of those RPGs that fans love to wax nostalgic about, and little wonder. Who could forget the game's charming cast of characters, engaging cutscenes or whimsical soundtrack? Not many, which is why Eternal Blue still looms large in the minds of RPG connoisseurs nearly twenty years after its original release. The second entry into the Lunar series was never quite as acclaimed as its predecessor, but it still managed to push both visual and audio boundaries by successfully utilizing the Sega CD's advanced capabilities and including an hour's worth of dialogue-- a remarkable achievement in its day. Though the game's opening song laments a legend untold, a story forgotten, fans who have had the privilege of experiencing Lunar: Eternal Blue will never allow the memories of this game to be stolen from time.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68286" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_j1f9.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yuri Hyuga, the "Godslayer," returned to another quest steeped in dark magic and darker demons. In Shadow Hearts: Covenant (also known as Shadow Hearts II), players once again walked through a morose fiction, this time framed by the global tragedy of World War I. A German lieutenant, Karin, joined the cast to freshen up the grim and familiar faces. The well-known Judgment Ring system returned to Covenant as well, along with soul-wrenching transformations that gave the player's party devastating power. With a unique, shadowy setting and memorable combat, Shadow Hearts: Covenant remains a well-loved (albeit niche) favorite in the PlayStation 2's vast collection of RPGs.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68287" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_1p1f.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The story of Squall and Rinoa marks one of the greatest romances in the Final Fantasy series. From a flirtatious glance across a dance floor to a battle through time -- bubbling to a desperate embrace in a field of flowers. These images remain burned into our collective memory, just as brightly as the glorious Balamb Garden drifting across the ocean. Many things divided the players that dove into Final Fantasy VIII, including the controversial Draw system and film-worthy summon sequences, but few can deny Final Fantasy VIII's grand scale, and its unique narrative that waltzed between Squall and the shy man that came before him.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68288" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_rr3d.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This may come as a surprise to some, but back in the day RPGs weren't known for their captivating graphics and deep storylines. Phantasy Star changed all that with its unique 3D-like dungeons, fully transversable overworld and story-driven gameplay. It even featured the genre's first female protagonist, Alis Landale. Younger gamers may laugh at its simplicity now, but Phantasy Star was a true pioneer in its day, and set the standard for all the RPGs that followed
         <span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; ">
          .
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; ">
          <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68289" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_bhc4.jpg"/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Few could have predicted the incredible impact that The Legend of Dragoon had on the RPG community. It arrived like so many other Japanese RPGs at the time but lingered on, like the lilting melody of its heart-breaking theme. It endured, thanks to its stunning cinematics and an action-heavy battle system that put the power of devastating combos in the hands -- and keen eyes -- of players. And of course we mustn't forget the titular Dragoon Spirits. They gave the heroes alternate forms and extraordinary abilities that could turn the tide of battle, as well as wow us with effects that were always spectacular and, more often than not, unforgettable.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68290" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_56sx.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Back in the day, game developers weren't afraid to take chances and do things a little differently. Vagrant Story is a perfect example of that intrepid spirit with its labyrinth-like story, conflicted characters, mature themes and one-of-a-kind gameplay that combined role-playing with puzzle and platforming elements. It was both innovative and engaging, and raised the bar in terms of graphics and sound on the PSX. To this day Vagrant Story remains a favorite of RPG lovers around the world, and with good reason.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68291" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_3z9t.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Tales series is a huge phenomenon, but it all started rather modestly nearly two decades ago in Japan in the form of Tales of Phantasia. While it didn't come out in the west until over ten years after it arrived on Super Famicom, Tales of Phantasia held a special place in gamers' hearts around the world for beginning a franchise that gave us the likes of Tales of Destiny, Tales of Eternia, Tales of Symphonia and more. Phantasia may not be the best game in the series, but it began one of the best-selling and most beloved modern RPG franchises, and that's really saying something.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68292" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_vedg.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The anticipation leading into the launch of Diablo III was palpable. With years separating Lord of Destruction from a proper follow-up, fans of the classic, loot-based RPG trembled at the thought of Diablo's return. Diablo III introduced new character classes, abilities, and showers of gold and loot. Nothing satisfies quite like the click of an open treasure chest and the following rain of trinkets upon the floor -- all waiting for your eager cursor. And even though many gamers have already trounced Diablo's forces time and time again, the developers at Blizzard continue to support Diablo III with sweeping patches and refinements. All the more reason to return to the quest spanning the High Heavens and Burning Hells.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68293" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_gz35.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Peter Molyneux's sequel is nothing if not charming. Fable II took the unfulfilled potential of the mega-hyped original game and made the most of it. Relationships ruled the day, whether it was a man or woman you were wooing or the bond you formed with your canine companion. The basic gameplay was largely similar to Zelda: an open overworld you're free to roam, with plenty of dungeons to battle your way through. Gorgeous art direction gave the fantasy setting a likeable look, while NPCs were chock full of humorous dialogue. A plethora of useful sidequests added meat to the main quest's meal, and the ability to be saintly good or diabolically evil added both variety and replayability.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="embed image" data-model="image" data-pk="68294" src="http://im.ziffdavisinternational.com/ign_me/screenshot/t/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time/top-100-rpgs-of-all-time_kz9z.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Persona 4 isn't just an RPG. It's also a dating simulator, a time management game and a detective story. Throw in a great soundtrack, a story that leaves you on the edge of your seat, characters you care about and a unique point system that makes levelling up fun through a series of clever gameplay mechanics, and you easily have one of the most incredible RPG experiences there is.
        </p>
        <div class="accentDivider" style="border-top-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 100px; border-top-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-left-style: solid; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; font-size: 0px; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: baseline; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-color: rgb(211, 32, 40); height: 10px; line-height: 0; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); ">
        </div>
