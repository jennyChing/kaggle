        <h1 class="entry-title">
         <a href="http://lingeringresonations.com/2015/03/05/why-you-should-allow-yourself-to-be-vurnerable/" rel="bookmark">
          Why vulnerability is a good thing
         </a>
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <ul class="meta-list">
         <li class="meta-cat">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         According to
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigmund_Freud" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigmund_Freud', 'Freud');" target="_blank">
          Freud
         </a>
         , we live our lives continuouslyadrift. At one end of the spectrum we have
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eros_(concept)" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eros_(concept)', 'eros');" target="_blank">
          eros
         </a>
         , our life instinct, driving us tobuild a life full of meaning,new experiences, adventure and excitement. On the other endtheres
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_drive" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_drive', 'tanathos');" target="_blank">
          tanathos
         </a>
         , our death drive. Tanathosmakes us look for safety,stability, predictability and ultimately total relaxation.
        </p>
        <p>
         In other words, its natural to wanttoeliminatepain byavoiding that which is unknown and uncertain.When nothing new happens for a long time however,boredom sets in and happiness is out of range as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         It seems both are inevitable, so how can we go ahead and find a good balance?
        </p>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stoicism" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stoicism', 'stoicist ');" target="_blank">
          stoicist
         </a>
         schooltells us that therewillalways be things outside of our control, whichwe cannot influence no matter what we want. What we can do however,is choose the way the events affect us. The idea is that we feel how we think, so by changing the way we think about things, we can also change our reaction.
        </p>
        <p>
         It can help you all the way from keeping your calm in a traffic jamand not getting depressed when your holidays drown in bad weather (cant help it anyways),up tocoping withlife threatening situations.In
         <a href="http://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Man%27s_Search_for_Meaning" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Man%27s_Search_for_Meaning', 'Mans search for meaning');" target="_blank">
          Mans search for meaning
         </a>
         for example, Victor Franklshows ushow strong our mind can be. In a Nazi death camp, he manages to maintain his self-worth and keep a positive lookout even when surrounded bytorture and death every single day. Whatever happens, he remains free in his mind.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lingeringresonations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/stoic1.jpg">
          <img alt="stoic1" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-414" height="214" src="http://lingeringresonations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/stoic1-300x214.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         The philosophers of the
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophy_in_the_Tragic_Age_of_the_Greeks" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophy_in_the_Tragic_Age_of_the_Greeks', 'tragic ');" target="_blank">
          tragic
         </a>
         agetake on another approach.Instead of trying to block out pain and setbacks, you are encouraged to welcome them. Its just part of taking on life in all its facets, adding beauty and meaning to it along the way.
        </p>
        <p>
         A good example is when we are in a relationship. We expose ourselves while wecannot control what the other person is thinking or doing.This makes us vulnerable. But does this mean we shouldnt risk it? Orthat we should haveour reservations towards everyone we meet?And how about the tragic example of being in love with someone who ends up gettingkilled in an accident. Excluding all chances of setbacks would lead to a life that is lonely,cold and thin. It feels inhuman.
        </p>
        <p>
         We should also keep in mind the old saying that what doesnt kill us makes us stronger. Setbacks can turn out to be good for us. They force us to re-evaluate our choices, make changes for the better. Personal growth is hard to achieve without pain.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lingeringresonations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/tragedy.jpg">
          <img alt="tragedy" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-447" height="234" src="http://lingeringresonations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/tragedy-300x234.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism', 'Buddhism ');">
          Buddhism
         </a>
         proposes the middle road between the schools described above and adds an interesting insight.
        </p>
        <p>
         When things happen to us, our ego makes it allvery personal. We start weaving theories andconspiraciesand get lost in a vortex of thoughts that make us feel bad. Even when the root cause is something we cannot do anything about.
        </p>
        <p>
         Instead of identifying ourselves with our emotions (we are not our ego), we can try to become an observer. Instead of saying I have been treated unfair you can say I feel treated unfair.
        </p>
        <p>
         Giving space to your emotions and evaluating them as an observer helps you give them a place and overcome them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lingeringresonations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/buddhism-4x3-160sc6k.jpg">
          <img alt="buddhism-4x3-160sc6k" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-469" height="225" src="http://lingeringresonations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/buddhism-4x3-160sc6k-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Personally I really believe that you should allow yourself to be vulnerable. Dare to love someone, dare to take on new experiences. Allow your life to become more than just a flat line, a bathtubwith tepid water where you find yourself dosing off continuously. Dont let it stop at the level where you have achieved the comforts of modern life, sitting in front of your tv, surrounded by the stuff that you have acquired. Expand your view, educate yourself, get out there!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Time to reach out: where do you find yourself? How do you cope with setbacks? Thank you for sharing your thoughts
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://lingeringresonations.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         This post wasinspiredby a
         <a href="http://www.bol.com/nl/p/het-voordeel-van-de-twijfel/9200000038638007/?Referrer=ADVNLGOO002008J-G-15194443695-S-72020810655-9200000038638007" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bol.com/nl/p/het-voordeel-van-de-twijfel/9200000038638007/?Referrer=ADVNLGOO002008J-G-15194443695-S-72020810655-9200000038638007', 'The benefit of the doubt');">
          The benefit of the doubt
         </a>
         , a bookfrom the author and philosopher
         <a href="http://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefaan_Van_Brabandt" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefaan_Van_Brabandt', 'StefaanVan Brabandt ');">
