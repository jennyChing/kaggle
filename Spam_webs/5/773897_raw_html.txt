         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name headline">
          Crochet Beanie Pattern
         </h1>
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <aside class="entry-meta">

        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">
         <script>
          &lt;!--
dynamicgoogletags.update();
//--&gt;
         </script>
         <p>
          One of the first things that I knew that I wanted to learn how to crochet while I was learning was how to crochet beanies. I found a lot of beanie patterns for children, but didnt find many that made a symmetrical looking hat. I put together a formula and created this extremely basic (and extremely symmetrical) crochet beaniepattern.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Display Image Crochet Beanie Pattern - Make and Bake Blog www.makeandbakeblog.com" class=" size-large wp-image-268 alignnone" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Crochet-Beanie-Pattern-9.jpg?resize=648%2C645"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-265">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Crochet-Rosette-Bouquet-1.jpg">
           <img alt="Crochet Rosette Bouquet Supplies - Make and Bake Blog http://www.makeandbakeblog.com" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-386" height="297" src="http://i1.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Crochet-Rosette-Bouquet-1.jpg?resize=648%2C349" width="552"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Supplies
         </h2>
         <p>
          I love this Yarn! Multicolor Yarn
          <br/>
          4.0mm Crochet Hook
          <br/>
          Yarn Needle
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Difficulty
          </strong>
          : Advanced Easy
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           Stitch Abbreviations and Terms
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://makeandbakeblog.com/2015/01/how-to-crochet/" target="_blank">
           Ch  Chain stitch
          </a>
          <br/>
          HDC  Half Double Crochet
          <br/>
          HDC Inc  Half Double Crochet Increase (TWO HDC sts in one st)
          <br/>
          Hk  Crochet Hook
          <br/>
          <a href="http://makeandbakeblog.com/2015/01/how-to-crochet/" target="_blank">
           SC  Single Crochet Stitch
          </a>
          <br/>
          SC Inc  Single Crochet Increase(TWO single crochet sts in one st)
          <br/>
          Sl St  Slip stitch
          <br/>
          St  stitch
          <br/>
          Sts  Stitches
          <br/>
          BPDC  Back post, double crochet
          <br/>
          FPDC  Front post, double crochet
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h1>
          Crochet Beanie Pattern
         </h1>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Crochet-Beanie-Pattern-1.jpg">
           <img alt="Magic Ring Crochet Beanie Pattern - Make and Bake Blog www.makeandbakeblog.com" class=" size-large wp-image-269 alignnone" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Crochet-Beanie-Pattern-1.jpg?resize=648%2C431"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          1. Begin with a magic loop. SC 10 stitches around the magic loop and tighten the magic loop. Use a sl st to complete the circle.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="r2 Crochet Beanie Pattern - Make and Bake Blog www.makeandbakeblog.com" class=" size-large wp-image-270 alignnone" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Crochet-Beanie-Pattern-2.jpg?resize=648%2C401"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          2. Chain 2. HDC 2 stitches in every SC from the first round. (20 stitches total)
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="R3 Crochet Beanie Pattern - Make and Bake Blog www.makeandbakeblog.com" class=" size-large wp-image-272 alignnone" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Crochet-Beanie-Pattern-3.jpg?resize=648%2C476"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          3. Chain 2 stitches. *HDC 1 st. In the next stitch, HDC two stitches in the same stitch.* This is called an increase (inc). From this point further, two stitches in the same stitch will be called a HDC inc. Repeat from * to * 9 more times. Slip stitch to complete the circle. (30 stitches total)
         </p>
         <p>
          As you can see in the picture above, each round is expanding the circumference of the beanie.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="R4 Crochet Beanie Pattern - Make and Bake Blog www.makeandbakeblog.com" class=" size-large wp-image-273 alignnone" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Crochet-Beanie-Pattern-4.jpg?resize=648%2C454"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          4. Chain two stitches. *HDC, HDC, HDC inc* Repeat * to * nine more times.  (40 Stitches total)
         </p>
         <p>
          By round four you can tell this pattern does not take long to work up. I had the entire project done in about an hour. To finish this pattern if you have been following along so far, pick up at round 5 (R5) in the crochet beanie pattern below.
         </p>
         <h1>
          Crochet Beanie Pattern
         </h1>
         <p>
          Begin with a magic loop.
          <br/>
          R1: SC 10 in magic loop. Sl st to complete circle. (10 st)
          <br/>
          R2: Ch 2. HDC inc. 10 st. Sl st to complete circle.(20 st)
          <br/>
          R3: Ch 2. *HDC, HDC inc* Repeat * to * nine more times. Sl st to complete circle. (30 st)
          <br/>
          R4: Ch 2. *HDC, HDC, HDC inc* Repeat * to * nine more times. Sl st to complete circle.(40 st)
          <br/>
          R5: Ch 2 *HDC 3, HDC Inc* Repeat * to * nine more times. Sl st to complete circle.(50 st)
          <br/>
          R6: Ch 2 *HDC 4, HDC Inc.* Repeat * to * nine more times. Sl st to complete circle.(60 st)
          <br/>
          R7-19: Ch 2, HDC 60. Sl st to complete circle. (60 st)
          <br/>
          R20-22: *BPDC, FPDC* Repeat * to * 29 more times. Sl St to complete circle. Tie off end.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Crochet-Beanie-Pattern-8.jpg">
           <img alt="Completed Crochet Beanie Pattern - Make and Bake Blog www.makeandbakeblog.com" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-274" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/makeandbakeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Crochet-Beanie-Pattern-8.jpg?resize=648%2C651"/>
          </a>
         </p>
