          <p>
           In the spirit of a hot, hot summer we bring you over 40 cocktails from around the world. From the popular to the suave to the downright weird and wonderful, these are the colourful drinks that will turn your taste buds upside down this festive season. Enjoy responsibly and remember this good advice for cocktail parties: If you cant say something nice about someone, just hold your drink and listen to others who cant either.
          </p>
          <h2>
           The Cucumber Collins
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Scott Beattie, the Living Room, Florida.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Cucumber-Collins.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31267" height="625" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Cucumber-Collins.jpg" title="Cucumber Collins" width="940"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Is this a meal, a cocktail or a work of art? Its hard to tell! This is probably the best looking cocktail you will see this year, and plenty of preparation goes into getting it perfect. The drink is based on Square One cucumber vodka, to which yuzu juice, fresh lemon and blueberry-stained cucumbers (which have been pickled in mirin, sake and rice wine vinegar!) are added. It looks too good to drink, and tastes better than it looks. Just the thing you need to cope with the Florida heat.
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://friendswithfridges.com/cucumber-tom-collins/" target="_blank">
            friendswithfridges.com
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Caipirinha with Candy Floss
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           OnlyOne, Dresden, Germany.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Candy-Floss-Caipirinha.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31268" height="335" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Candy-Floss-Caipirinha.jpg" title="Candy Floss Caipirinha" width="598"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           The genius mixer at OnlyOne in Dresden has come up with a wonderfully exciting take on the traditional Brazilian Caipirinha, which you eat rather than sip. The cocktail is solidified, probably by freezing with liquid nitrogen, and then served decorated with candy floss. Your favourite cocktail presented as a dessertwho can resist?
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.dresden-onlyone.de/" target="_blank">
            dresden-onlyone.de
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           The Bull Shooter
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           OnlyOne, Dresden, Germany.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Bull-Shooter.jpg">

           Sidecar
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           The Ritz, Paris, or Bucks Club, London.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Sidecar1.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31300" height="666" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Sidecar1.jpg" title="Sidecar" width="1200"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           This is a great classic cocktail that has controversial origins. The Ritz Hotel in Paris has claimed that it was invented by one of their regular customers, just after World War I, a certain American Army captain who was rather fond of visiting The Ritz for a night cap of Cognac, Cointreau and lemon juice. The drink came to be known as a Sidecar, since the officer always arrived at the Ritz in a sidecar. However, London claims the drink was made (or perhaps introduced?) by Pat MacGarry at Bucks Club. Its a nice story and adds a touch of intrigue to a really good cocktail.

           Tijuana, Mexico.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Margarita.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31280" height="683" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Margarita.jpg" title="Margarita" width="1024"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           The classic Margarita is one of the worlds most enduring cocktails, and it is still just as popular today as it was decades ago; it also has a rather endearing history  the story goes that the Margarita was formerly known as a Tequila Daisy, and was created by accident when a busy barman in Tijuana, Mexico grabbed a bottle of Tequila in error, while making a Daisy cocktail. The resulting mistake went down a treat and was soon the most popular drink in Tijuana. Margarita is the Spanish word for a daisy, and the rest, as they say, is history.
          </p>
          <h2>
           The Black Russian
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Gustav Tops, Hotel Metropole, Brussels.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Black-Russian.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31281" height="447" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Black-Russian.jpg" title="Black Russian" width="670"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Black Russian was invented as a signature drink for the American ambassador to Luxembourg in the 1940s, just as the Cold War was starting; a dark and mysterious cocktail that just smacks of espionage and intrigue! The name is quite deceptive as the only thing Russian is the Vodka. Expect 2 parts Vodka, 1 part Kahlua stirred over ice and poured into an old fashioned glass full of ice. Later, milk or cream were added to make a White Russian, and then someone added Baileys Irish Cream instead of milk and the Mudslide was born!
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.wikihow.com/Make-a-Black-Russian-Cocktail" target="_blank">
            wikihow.com
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           The Mohawk
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Kenneth Gray, Lafitte, San Francisco.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Mohawk-Cocktail-Lafitte.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31282" height="1200" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Mohawk-Cocktail-Lafitte.jpg" title="The Mohawk" width="1600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Since you are not allowed to smoke in bars these days, the Mohawk is a sneaky way to get a quick shot of nicotine without having to brave the elements. This most unusual cocktail is a heady blend of tobacco syrup (real whole leaf tobacco boiled with water and sugar to form a syrup), Rye Whiskey and a dash of Bonal Gentiane-Quina (a herb-infused aperitif wine), bitters and a lemon twist. Try it if you dare!
          </p>
          <h2>
           Double Truffle Martini
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Duo in New York.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DUO-Double-Truffle-Martini.jpg">
            <img alt="DUO Restaurant and Lounge in New York City - home of the Double Truffle Martini." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31283" height="803" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DUO-Double-Truffle-Martini.jpg" title="DUO Double Truffle Martini" width="1200"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Heaven for truffle lovers  now you can drink your truffle as well as eat it! The basis for this rather different concoction is basically a Double Cross Vodka martini, to which the mixologist has cleverly added a good glug of truffle juice, before garnishing the drink with a truffle-stuffed olive. (This one is definitely shaken, not stirred!) I imagine it would pair beautifully with your favourite truffle pasta.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Meatequita
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Shaka Zulu Bar, London.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Meatequita-Cocktail.jpg">
            <img alt="The Meatequita cocktail served at Shaka Zulu Bar in London photographed by Simon-Wheeler for The London Magazine." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31284" height="522" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Meatequita-Cocktail.jpg" title="Meatequita Cocktail" width="414"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Daring and unusual, this cocktail is strictly for all the carnivores out there! It is an inspired blend of chorizo sausage-infused Tequila, vegetable juice, balsamic vinegar, smoked sea salt, pepper and Port, garnished with strips of chorizo and biltong. Its a bit like having Tapas in a glass! Try one at Shaka Zulu Bar, London.
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.thelondonmagazine.co.uk/Going-Out/London-Bars/Meatequita-at-Shaka-Zulu.html" target="_blank">
            thelondonmagazine.co.uk
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Vanilla Chocolate Malt
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Oxo Tower Bar, alongside the Thames in London.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Vanilla-Chocolate-Malt-Oxo-Tower-Bar.jpg">
            <img alt="The Oxo Tower Bar. Great cocktails with an awesome view of London from the 8th floor." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31285" height="388" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Vanilla-Chocolate-Malt-Oxo-Tower-Bar.jpg" title="Vanilla Chocolate Malt Oxo Tower Bar" width="620"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           This fabulous and most unusual cocktail will take you straight back to the 50s style American soda fountains, if you are old enough to remember them! If not, dont worry, there really were some great things in the Good Old Days, and this is a new take on one of them, the classic chocolate malted milkshake. This one is strictly for adults thoughit is a blend of Vanilla Vodka, almond milk, Mozart dry chocolate spirit and vanilla sugar and malted milk. It is craftily served in a mini-milk bottle with a jaunty straw!
          </p>
          <h2>
           Dropje Daiquiri
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           The Tippling Club, Singapore.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Dropje-Daiquiri.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31286" height="528" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Dropje-Daiquiri.jpg" title="Dropje Daiquiri" width="736"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Tippling Club in Singapore believes in pushing the envelope when it come to boozy innovation, which is why it is probably the coolest and most on-trend venue in that city. This cocktail is a dramatic remake of the classic daiquiri, but adds salted licorice from Holland to the mix of Venezuelan rum, orange curacao and citrus. For added drama (and taste) a thick line of licorice is painted inside the glass. Some may hate it, but the Dutch love it!
          </p>
          <h2>
           Royal Toast
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           The Langham, London, 2013.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Royal-Toast.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31287" height="419" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Royal-Toast.jpg" title="Royal Toast" width="630"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Langham Hotel in London were really quick to capitalize on the hype surrounding the birth of the new heir to the British throne, Prince George. The newly invented Royal Toast features both unusual ingredients and a most novel presentation, all put together by the International Bartender of the Year, Alex Kratena. The aromatic cocktail combines tequila, cedar wood syrup, lemon juice and bitters with a beautiful blanc de blanc; served in a coupe glass ensconced in scented blue crepe paper.
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.insignia-lb.com/2013/07/24/the-langham-celebrates-royal-birth-with-cocktail/" target="_blank">
            insignia-lb.com
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Sourtoe Cocktail
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Downtown Hotel, Dawson City, Yukon.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Sourtoe-Cocktail.jpg">
            <img alt=" The Dawson City Downtown Hotel, where the Sourdough Saloon hosts the Sourtoe Cocktail!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31288" height="720" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Sourtoe-Cocktail.jpg" title="Home of the Sourtoe Cocktail" width="960"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you are squeamish about what you put in your mouth you should skip this one. This is probably the weirdest cocktail of all time and interestingly, any of your favourite tipples can be converted into the Sour Toe Cocktail by the simple addition of a real, preserved, amputated human toe! To join the Sour Toe Club you need to down the drink and make sure that the toe touches your lipsYou are not intended to swallow the toe, which will go back into salt-storage, but recently one man didand lived to tell the tale. Now they are looking for a new toe to pickle
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.facebook.com/SourtoeCocktailClub" target="_blank">
            facebook.com/sourtoecocktailclub
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Doug Lamings Margarita
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           The Rabbit Hole, Sydney, Australia.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Doug-Lamings-Margarita.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31289" height="1060" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Doug-Lamings-Margarita.jpg" title="Doug Lamings Margarita" width="1600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           This Aussie take on the classic Margarita is a cocktail to be taken seriously! Firstly, you will not recognize it when it arrives and secondly, it tastes like no other Margarita in the world. The cocktail is made by using molecular mixology and a very special Spherification Kit to transform Cointreau into caviar like pearls that literally pop on your tongue. The drink is uniquely served on a half lemon, and is truly one of the most unusual cocktails I have come across; add it to your bucket-list.
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://economic.bg/news/22727/1/1/12-ot-naj-neobichajnite-koktejli-v-sveta.html" target="_blank">
            economic.bg
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           The Gunpowder Plot
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Zeta Bar, George St, Sydney.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Gunpowder-Plot-Cocktail.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31290" height="402" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Gunpowder-Plot-Cocktail.jpg" title="Gunpowder Plot Cocktail" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           This one is also from Down Under where molecular mixology is the flavour of the day. It was invented by Grant Collins, who loves using liquid nitrogen, dry ice and jelly to WOW his customers. The cocktail is inspired by Guy Fawkes and is an ingenious mix of gin (infused with gunpowder flavours), Fernet Branca and egg white, shaken to produce a cocktail with a thick frothy head, which is then infused with smoke and served under a clear glass cloche amid smouldering twigs producing an oak-scented fog; totally unforgettable!
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.zetabar.com.au/gallery/photos/cocktails-gallery.html" target="_blank">
            zetabar.com.au
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           The Crapper
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Rock &amp; Ritas, Circus Circus Hotel, Las Vegas.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/The-Crapper-Cocktail.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31291" height="360" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/The-Crapper-Cocktail.jpg" title="The Crapper Cocktail" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           This is a rather low-brow cocktail hailing from Las Vegas where it gets many a stag party off to a roaring start. It is a calorie laden (good for lining the stomach guys!) mix based on chocolate Pina Colada, laced with a Snickers Bar and served in, yes, you may have guessed  a miniature toilet bowl! Its not for everyone, but perfectly acceptable in Las Vegas!
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/08/19/travel/unusual-cocktails/" target="_blank">
            cnn.com
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           White Lady
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origins:
           </strong>
           The Savoy, London.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/White-Lady-Savoy-Cocktail.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31292" height="541" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/White-Lady-Savoy-Cocktail.jpg" title="White Lady Savoy Cocktail" width="800"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           A White Lady started life as a rather sugary concoction made with far too much Cointreau, Crme de Menthe and lemon juice, back in the 1920s, when it was called a Delilah. It was later refined by Harry Craddock of the Savoy, who exchanged the Crme de Menthe for gin, added some egg white for body and changed the name to White Lady: it was an instant hit. It has also been called a Chelsea Sidecar and a Lillian Forever.
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.gintime.com/features/harry-craddocks-favourite-gin-cocktails/" target="_blank">
            gintime.com
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           The Mojito
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origins:
           </strong>
           Havana, Cuba.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Mojito.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31293" height="768" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Mojito.jpg" title="Mojito" width="1024"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Its fair to bet that most people who enjoy quaffing down their summer Mojitos have no idea that this very popular cocktail has been around for centuries, and it is believed that its origins can be traced to a 16th century drink originally called El Draque which was invented in Havana in honour of Sir Francis Drake. The original Mojito was made with cane sugar juice and aguardiente, a fiery pre-cursor to rum which had to be modified with mint, lime and sugar to make it palatable. Mojo is also an African word for magic, and the modern name could have come from African slaves in Cuba.
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.hotelalpin.ro/blog/en/summer-cocktails/" target="_blank">
            hotelalpin.ro
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           The Coquetier
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Pret a Diner, Berlin.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Coquetier.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31294" height="360" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Coquetier.jpg" title="Coquetier" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Antoine Amde Peychaud was an 18th century Creole apothecary in New Orleans who became famous for toddies made of Cognac and bitters, which he dispensed in little egg cups. This cocktail eventually morphed into the Sazerac, the official cocktail of New Orleans. Today there are several versions of the Coquetier, but the most interesting one can be found in Berlin, where a blend of rum, chocolate and cinnamon are served in an eggshell.
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/08/19/travel/unusual-cocktails/" target="_blank">
            cnn.com
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Diamonds Are Forever
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Skyview Bar, Burj Al Arab Hotel, Dubai.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Diamond-is-Forever-Martini.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31295" height="400" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Diamond-is-Forever-Martini.jpg" title="Diamond is Forever Martini" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Probably the worlds most expensive cocktail, the Diamonds are Forever flight of fantasy is only for the seriously wealthy. It contains only the best and most exclusive alcohol, including LHeraud Grand Champagne Cognac, Luxor 24k Gold Flake Champagne and bitters, and is served in a Swarovsky Crystal glass with diamonds in the stem. Thank goodness, you do get to keep the glass as a souvenir! A bargain at $1,347.
          </p>

           image:
           <a href="http://two57.com/most-expensive-drink-cocktail.html" target="_blank">
            two57.com
           </a>

          <h2>
           Chocolate Bunny
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Fairmount Pacific Rim, Vancouver, Canada.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Chocolate-Bunny.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31306" height="1192" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Chocolate-Bunny.jpg" title="Chocolate Bunny" width="1550"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           This is another great cocktail cum dessert cum coffee, for chocoholics who just cannot resist the chocolate/alcohol combination. This one is a heady blend of Van Gogh Espresso Vodka, Creme de Cacao, Galliano and a good shot of freshly brewed espresso, all shaken together with an egg white which produces a great foamy head. It looks good and tastes even better, and its easy for you to try at home  a great finale to that come dine with me dinner party!
          </p>

           <a href="http://barmagazine.co.uk/easter-inspires-chocolate-cocktails-in-bars/">
            barmagazine.co.uk
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Winston
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Club 23, Melbourne, Australia.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Winston-Cocktail.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31296" height="536" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Winston-Cocktail.jpg" title="Winston Cocktail" width="759"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           On 07/02/2013 Joel Hefferman set a new Guinness World Record for the most expensive cocktail ever, with this one dedicated to Winston Churchill. The ambrosial drink is made with cognac dating from 1858, as well as other rare and exceptional alcohols. It is then served with chocolate and nutmeg soil and garnished with pulled sugar made using the same precious alcohols. It is then misted with dry ice essence infused with citrus peel. If you fancy one, do phone aheadit takes about 2 days to make. The price? A mere $12,000.
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://imgur.com/gallery/SaRkBzB" target="_blank">
            imgur.com
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Salvatores Legacy
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           Salvatore at Playboy, London.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Salvatores-Legacy-Cocktail.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31297" height="408" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Salvatores-Legacy-Cocktail.jpg" title="Salvatores Legacy Cocktail" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Before the Aussies notched up a world record for the most expensive cocktail this honour belonged to famous mixologist Salvatore Calabrese in London, and his Legacy cocktail still uses the oldest ingredients in the world, including Cognac from 1778, Kummel from 1770 and 2 other ingredients, none of which are younger than 100 years. Yes, it is completely over the top, but those millionaires have to spend their money on something! It costs about $8316.
          </p>
          <p>
           image:
           <a href="http://www.luxury-insider.com/luxury-news/2012/05/creative-spirit-the-revolution-glass" target="_blank">
            luxury-insider.com
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           More Tea Vicar?
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Origin:
           </strong>
           The Orphanage Cocktail Emporium, Bree St, Cape Town.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/More-Tea-Vicar.jpg">
            <img alt='The "More Tea Vicar" cocktail from Orphanage in Cape Town.' class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31241" height="480" src="http://www.travelstart.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/More-Tea-Vicar.jpg" title="More Tea Vicar" width="570"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           From Cape Town comes this interesting cocktail featuring Vanilla Finlandia, rooibos syrup, cranberry juice and lemon, all served up in a lovely vintage teacup. The Orphanage cocktail bar takes its cheeky name from a real orphanage that was once housed in the church around the corner. If you order the More Tea Vicar cocktail, you will be pleased to know that a percentage of the price is donated to a real-life childrens home in Cape Town. Have a few moreits all for a good cause!
          </p>
