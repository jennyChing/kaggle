      <h1>
       A guide to including MLA Citations in Research Papers
      </h1>
      <div id="item_info">
       by
       <a class="green" href="http://www.wamda.com/aoneessays">
        Aitana Cardoso
       </a>
       ,
       <span class="date">
        December 11, 2014
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="content left" id="item_body">
       <div class="disclaimer top">
       </div>
       <div class="item_body_text">
        <p>
         When writing a research paper, it is necessary to use information from other sources that asks for proper referencing, since it is essential for the integrity of a researchers work that they avoid plagiarism. The Modern language Association has laid down a set of rules to standardize citation, i.e. it has put forward some guidelines for referencing which are briefly explained in the following paragraphs.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         MLA has given an in-text referencing style, so instead of using footnotes, the reference is included inside the text of the research paper. There are a few basic rules to include the citations in the body of the paper. Cite information that has come from an outside source in parenthesis after that information is quoted (directly or indirectly). If the author is mentioned in your sentence, just insert the page number between the parentheses, i.e. in such a case, only the reference of the page number needs to be given. For example: Franklin stated that the convolution of discrete time signals is much easier to comprehend than that of continuous time signals (65).
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         If the author is not mentioned, first write the authors last name and then the page number. For example: In an authors words winter froze life itself (Alex 83).
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         If there are multiple authors, their last names will be mentioned in alphabetical order before the page number. For example: Studies show that social networking is decreasing the attention span of children (Alan, Blanc, Chris 73).
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         If the author of the text is not known, write the title of the text followed by the page number. For example, if the title of the text is The Monologue of a Librarian and the page number is 34, the citation will be written as (The Monologue of a Librarian 34).
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Finally, a page will be added at the end of the research paper with the same margin setting and text format as the rest of the paper. This page will be titled Works Cited and will list all the references that have been included in the research paper. Each reference will include the title of the text, the date on which the text was published, the publishers name and the page number on which the extract or quotation is found.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Mentioned above are just the basic rules of MLA referencing in
         <strong>
          research writing
         </strong>
         . Various printed and online sources are available providing detailed information.
        </p>
        <p>
