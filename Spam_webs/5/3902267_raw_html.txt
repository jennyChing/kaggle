      <header class="article-header">
       <h1>
        FAQs About Psoriatic Arthritis Treatment With DMARDs and Biologics
       </h1>
       <ul class="inline">
        <li class="byline" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
         By
         <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/authors/chris-iliades/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
          <span itemprop="name">
           Chris  Iliades, MD
          </span>
         </a>
        </li>
        <li class="reviewed-by " itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
         Reviewed by
         <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/authors/farrokh-sohrabi/" itemprop="url" rel="reviewer">
          <span itemprop="name">
           Farrokh  Sohrabi, MD
          </span>
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </header>
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="row-fluid">
     <h2 class="eh-deck">
      Psoriatic arthritis treatment often includes disease-modifying anti-rheumatic drugs, or DMARDs. Find answers to common questions about traditional medications and newer biologic therapies.
     </h2>
     <span class="ehshare share-inline clearfix" data-count="true" data-description="Psoriatic%2barthritis%2btreatment%2bmay%2binclude%2btraditional%2bdisease-modifying%2banti-rheumatic%2bdrugs%2b(DMARDs)%2band%2bnewer%2bbiologics.%2bGet%2banswers%2bto%2byour%2bquestions%2babout%2bthese%2bdrug%2btherapies%2bfor%2bpsoriatic%2barthritis." data-migratedbyline="" data-networks='["facebook", "twitter", "pinterest", "email"]' data-title="FAQs%2bAbout%2bPsoriatic%2bArthritis%2bTreatment%2bWith%2bDMARDs%2band%2bBiologics" data-track-self="false" data-url="http%3a%2f%2fwww.everydayhealth.com%2fhs%2fpsoriatic-arthritis-management-treatment%2fpsoriatic-arthritis-treatment-faqs%2f">
     </span>
     <br clear="all"/>
    </div>

        <header>
         <h2>
          <span>
           Featured
          </span>
         </h2>
        </header>
        <div class="content">
         <div class="media first">
          <a class="media-object pull-left" href="/health-report/psoriatic-arthritis-treatment/psoriatic-arthritis-medical-animation-video.aspx">
           <img alt="" class="lazy img-responsive" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/cs-psoriatic-arthritis-medical-animation-400.jpg?width=75"/>
          </a>
          <div class="media-body">

            Video: Understanding Psoriatic Arthritis

         <div class="media ">
          <a class="media-object pull-left" href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/solutions/tools/psoriatic-arthritis-treatment/course-of-psoriatic-arthritis.html">
           <img alt="" class="lazy img-responsive" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/cs-course-of-psoriatic-arthritis-400.jpg?width=75"/>

          <div class="media-body">

            The Course of Psoriatic Arthritis

         </div>
         <div class="media ">
          <a class="media-object pull-left" href="/custom/psoriatic-arthritis-management-treatment/psoriatic-arthritis-treatment/">
           <img alt="" class="lazy img-responsive" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/cs-psoriatic-arthritis-treatment-400.jpg?width=75"/>
          </a>
          <div class="media-body">
           <a class="title" href="/custom/psoriatic-arthritis-management-treatment/psoriatic-arthritis-treatment/">
            Matching Psoriatic Arthritis Treatment With Symptoms
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
       </section>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="article-body" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article">
      <p>
       If youve been diagnosed with
       <a href="/solutions/landing/psoriatic-arthritis-management-treatment/">
        psoriatic arthritis
       </a>
       , its good to know there are effective treatments. For people with mild psoriatic arthritis, taking a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID), such as ibuprofen or naproxen, may be enough. These drugs can relieve pain and inflammation, but they dont change the course of your disease.
      </p>
      <p>
       The next step in
       <a href="/custom/psoriatic-arthritis-management-treatment/psoriatic-arthritis-how-treated/">
        psoriatic arthritis treatment
       </a>
       is a family of drugs called disease-modifying anti-rheumatic drugs, or DMARDs. Examples of these drugs include cyclosporine, leflunomide, methotrexate, and sulfasalazine. Your psoriatic arthritis doctor may prescribe these drugs alone or in combination to slow down or stop your condition from progressing.
      </p>
      <p>
       The newest medications for psoriatic arthritis are a type of DMARDs called biologics, which are made from living organisms and proteins to target a specific part of the immune system that causes inflammation in some
       <a href="/custom/psoriatic-arthritis-management-treatment/psoriatic-arthritis-types/">
        types of arthritis
       </a>
       . For example, a type of biologic called TNF inhibitors target a protein called tumor necrosis factor alpha (TNF-alpha) thats associated with psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis. Examples of biologics include adalimumab, etanercept, golimumab, infliximab, and ustekinumab. These drugs are usually reserved for treating very aggressive disease or for people whove already tried at least two DMARDs with little or no success.
      </p>
      <p>
       Choosing the best treatment for psoriatic arthritis can be challenging because there are no strict rules, says Rochella Ostrowski, MD, assistant professor of rheumatology at Loyola University Health System in Maywood, Ill. The best treatment for you may be different than for someone else with the condition.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Why would I need to start taking a DMARD or biologic for psoriatic arthritis?
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       If you have a more aggressive form of psoriatic arthritis with multiple joint involvement or involvement of your spine, the first DMARD to try is usually methotrexate, Dr. Ostrowski says. If you dont respond well to this medication or to one of the other traditional DMARDs, your doctor may suggest a biologic therapy.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        What do I need to know about methotrexate for psoriatic arthritis?
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Methotrexate is a DMARD because it reduces pain and swelling and may
       <a href="/custom/psoriatic-arthritis-management-treatment/psoriatic-arthritis-protect-joints/">
        prevent joint damage
       </a>
       and disability. It works by blocking inflammation caused by your immune system. It's often started at a weekly dose, and it may take up to six weeks to provide results.
      </p>
      <p>
       Side effects of methotrexate can include hair loss and mouth ulcers. It can also cause inflammation of the liver, so liver function tests need to be done. Also, you shouldnt drink alcohol while taking methotrexate. Taking the B vitamin folic acid may help prevent some of these side effects, Ostrowski says.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        What if methotrexate isnt working for my psoriatic arthritis?
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       If methotrexate doesnt work for you, or if youre having side effects, your doctor may try prescribing another DMARD or a biologic therapy.
      </p>
      <p>
       The dose of methotrexate may be increased over two to three months if its tolerated but not effective at a lower dose, says Ostrowski. If that doesnt work, other options would be to try another traditional DMARD or switch to a newer biologic DMARD, such as a TNF inhibitor.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        What other DMARDs are used for psoriatic arthritis?
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Two other DMARDs that might be used before trying a biologic DMARD are leflunomide and sulfasalazine, Ostrowski says. These are both oral drugs and may take several months to show results.
      </p>
      <p>
       The most common side effect of sulfasalazine is upset stomach. This drug can also increase sensitivity to sunlight, so youll need to use a strong sunscreen while taking it. If youre allergic to any other sulfa drugs, you may not be able to take this drug.
      </p>
      <p>
       Like methotrexate, leflunomide can cause liver injury, and the most common side effect is diarrhea.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        When would I need a biologic DMARD for psoriatic arthritis?
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Your doctor may suggest a
       <a href="/custom/psoriatic-arthritis-management-treatment/psoriatic-arthritis-tnf-inhibitors/">
        biologic drug
       </a>
       if you have severe disease or you havent responded to traditional DMARDs, Ostrowski says.
      </p>
      <p>
       According to current guidelines for the treatment of psoriatic arthritis, developed by the British Society of Rheumatology, a biologic may be considered if you have three or more sore joints despite having tried two or more non-biologic DMARDs. Another reason would be that youve tried one non-biologic DMARD, yet you have five or more sore joints and are developing joint damage.
      </p>
      <p>
       There are several choices for biologics, including adalimumab, etanercept, golimumab, infliximab, and ustekinumab. Dosing of these drugs can vary from a self-administered injection under the skin once per week to once every few months. Another option is an intravenous infusion administered at a medical facility about once every two months.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        What else do I need to know about biologics for psoriatic arthritis?
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Biologics are effective, but they increase your risk of infection, Ostrowski says.
      </p>
      <p>
       If you develop an active infection while taking one of these drugs, you may need to stop treatment until you recover. Similarly, if you have active tuberculosis, hepatitis, or the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV), you will need to get the condition under control before starting biologic treatment.
      </p>
      <p>
       If youve recently (within the last 10 years) had cancer, biologic treatment may not be a good option for you.
      </p>
      <p>
       Stay away from unpasteurized milk, raw meat, and raw eggs while taking these drugs to avoid catching a food-borne illness.
      </p>
      <p>
       A final question about biologic therapy for women with psoriatic arthritis involves pregnancy and breastfeeding. Many DMARDs, including biologics, cannot be used if youre pregnant or breastfeeding, Ostrowski says. If you may become pregnant, you need to discuss taking a biologic drug with your doctor first.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Is there anything else I should discuss with my doctor about DMARDs and biologics for psoriatic arthritis?
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       There many options when it comes to psoriatic arthritis treatment. No matter what drug you may be prescribed, ask your doctor or pharmacist about side effects, long-term complications, safety precautions, and drug interactions.
      </p>
      <p>
       Which psoriatic treatment you and your doctor choose also depends on how aggressive you want to be, Ostrowski says. You need to ask your doctor if youre getting a more aggressive or conservative treatment. There may not be a right or wrong treatment, but you and your doctor should be on the same page.
      </p>
