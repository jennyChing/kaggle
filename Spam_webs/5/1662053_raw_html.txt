        <h1 class="entry-title">
         The Best Cheap Flight Tool Youve Never Heard Of
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2013-07-21T04:36:24+00:00">
          2013-07-21
         </span>
         By
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/author/admin/" rel="author">
            Mike
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/the-best-cheap-flight-tool-youve-never-heard-of/#comments">
           5 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Have you always wondered if your favorite cheap airfare website is actually getting you the cheapest flights? Weve uncovered a 2 step approach that will
          <strong>
           show you exactly how to quickly finding the cheapest airfares
          </strong>
          . You start by easily finding the best airfare using a travel agent grade search tool. Once you know your best flight, just hop over to an airfare website to buy the ticket.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Blog-Post-1-Screen-Shot-1.jpg">
           <img alt="Matrix Airfare Search" class=" alignright wp-image-65 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Blog-Post-1-Screen-Shot-1-297x300.jpg" title="Default Advanced Screen on Matrix Airfare Search" width="297"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Your Definitive 1st Stop  Travel Agent Grade Airfare Search
         </h2>
         <p>
          If youre not starting out with
          <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/matrix" target="_blank">
           Matrix Airfare Search
          </a>
          then youre wasting your precious time. This little-known airfare search tools power puts it in a league of its own. Its simple-to-use yet advanced search capabilities allow you to see many more airfare combinations (in the billions actually) with a single search than the next best option (which is
          <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/kayak" target="_blank">
           Kayak
          </a>
          , in case you were wondering). Even though you cant actually buy tickets through the tool directly,
          <strong>
           youll save so much time in your airfare search that itd be crazy not to start with the
           <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/matrix" target="_blank">
            Matrix Airfare Search
           </a>
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           3 Features to Take Advantage Of
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          There are 3 unique features offered by Matrix Airfare Search that make it the place to start your cheap airfare search.
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          <strong>
           1. Unrivaled Date Flexibility:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Its ridiculously easy to search different departure/return dates and trip lengths. Matrix will even automatically highlight the cheapest ticket price for you in a 30 day period. Many cheap airfare search engines limit your search to +/- 2 days of a selected selected date. Matrix quickly returns whole month worth of fares in a visually pleasing way.
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Here, Matrix quickly finds us the cheapest day to fly from New York to Manchester, England during January (based on the date of the search).
          <strong>
           It could have easily taken you 8 to 10 searches to uncover this airfare with the more common airfare search tools.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/matrix_airfare_calendar.jpg">
           <img alt="matrix airfare calendar" class="alignright wp-image-67 size-medium" height="263" src="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/matrix_airfare_calendar-300x263.jpg" title="Matrix Calendar View" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          <strong>
           2. Search Airports in Bulk:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Extending your search to nearby airports you can save $100s of dollars. You might be thinking Orbitz offers this feature too and youd be right. The difference is that ITAs software lets you individually
          <strong>
           include airports in a 2,000 mile radius
          </strong>
          through a perfectly designed menu (that even has a map!).
          <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/orbitz" target="_blank">
           Orbitz
          </a>
          limits your search to a generic 80-mile radius where you cant pick and choose specific airports  forcing you to sift through flights youd never want.
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Even better,
          <strong>
           you can enter as many of the 3 letter airport codes as youd like
          </strong>
          into the Departing From and Destination fields. This is especially useful when you want to go on a trip, but are considering a few destination options. You can search them all at once with this tool! Here we choose to search for flights that leave from any of three different nearby airports by entering: MDW, ORD, MKE into the Departing From field.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_68" style="width: 394px">
          <img alt="Searching for Multiple Destinations" class="wp-image-68 size-full" height="198" src="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/MDW-MKE.jpg" title="Searching for Multiple Destinations" width="384"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Searching for Multiple Destinations
          </p>
         </div>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Expanding your search to add in airports within 300 or more miles can sometimes return great results. You might be pleasantly surprised to find some of the super cheap airfares that can be had at small airports around the country. Budget carriers, like Spirit Airlines and Ryan Air in Europe, often make use of small airports in rural areas, where their costs can be much lower. If you can get yourself out to one of these far-flung airports, you might be able to save hundreds on your next get-away.
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          <strong>
           3. Filter Search Results:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Once you move past the Calendar step, youll see the Flights page, which offers great ways to sort and filter the search results.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/flight_search.jpg">
           <img alt="flight search" class="alignright wp-image-69 size-medium" height="34" src="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/flight_search-300x34.jpg" title='The "Flights" Page' width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          You have more than
          <strong>
           8 filter options to make sure the flights fit your schedule, budget, and patience level
          </strong>
          !. The most useful filters are Departure Time, Arrival Time, Flight Duration, and Advisories. For starters, you filter the results to show convenient flight departure and arrival times and flights that dont leave you stuck with really long layovers.
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          With the advisory filter you can eliminate really inconvenient flights with super tight connections and over night flights  an especially important consideration when travelling with kids. For example, weve found some cheap tickets that require you to haul yourself across New York City to get from JFK to LaGuardia airports  yuck! This is huge pain even when you dont consider the $75 taxi ride.)
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          In the example below, when shopping for cheap airfare to Evansville, Indiana, we can see that the cheapest flights all include long layovers (for $428 Round Trip). When we
          <strong>
           filter out flights with lengthy layovers
          </strong>
          we are left with more convenient schedules that are just a little more expensive (about $25 extra).
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_70" style="width: 380px">
          <img alt="Filtering Annoying Connections" class="wp-image-70 size-full" height="363" src="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/long_layovers.jpg" title="Filtering Annoying Connections" width="370"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Filtering Annoying Connections
          </p>
         </div>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Keep in mind that the Matrix Airfare Search is so useful, partly because it shows you a huge number of airfares. Sometimes even really awkward schedules are offered at an extra discount. If you make use of the filtering features, be aware that you might be filtering out some great cheap airfare deals.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Buy Your Cheap Airfare Ticket
         </h2>
         <p>
          Once youve found the cheapest fare that fits your needs, its time to locate that same fare on a website that DOES sell plane tickets.
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.orbitz.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.orbitz.com/', 'Orbitz.com']);" target="_blank">
            Orbitz.com
           </a>
           is our top choice for buying cheap airfares
          </strong>
          that we found using Matrix Airfare Search. Take note of dates, origin/destination information, and flight numbers from the Matrix Airfare Search and head on over to
          <a href="http://www.orbitz.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.orbitz.com/', 'Orbitz.com']);" target="_blank">
           Orbitz.com
          </a>
          and see if they offer the same fare. Usually they do, but from time to time they dont. In that case you may need to try your search on a few smaller Online Travel Agent websites. (See below)
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/airfare.png">
           <img alt="" class="alignright wp-image-103 size-medium" height="282" src="http://www.trulytraveled.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/airfare-300x282.png" title="Finding Your Best Flight through Matrix" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are our top choices for buying cheap flights that weve found using Matrix Airfare Search:
         </p>
         <p>
          1.
          <a href="http://www.orbitz.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.orbitz.com/', 'Orbitz.com']);" target="_blank">
           Orbitz.com
          </a>
           Our top pick for purchasing tickets. They almost always offer the same low fares that you find on the Matrix Airfare Search. (Sadly they just dont have the great search flexibility offered by Matrix Airfare Search.)
         </p>
         <p>
          2.
          <a href="http://www.trulytraveled.com/kayak" target="_blank">
           Kayak.com
          </a>
           Another good choice. They offer some additional flexibility when it comes to multi-city trips.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Get in Touch
         </h2>
         <p>
          Got questions about our chap flight tool(s)? Id love to know
          <strong>
           how you have been using Matrix Airfare Search
          </strong>
          to find an awesome deal by using these travel hacks!
         </p>
