                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Top 5 Loyalty Cards in the Philippines
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8765428862269714334" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Do you remember your first loyalty card? I do! It was a
GenTxt (Are You One of Us?) loyalty card from Globe that offers all these
amazing freebies from my favorite clothing brands back when I was still a
teenager. I feel so thrilled whenever I use this, particularly when I think
about all the savings I get from all my purchases.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Having a loyalty card is a way for companies to thank their regular customers
through the form rewards. In the
Philippines, there are a lot of loyalty cards currently available. Drug stores,
supermarkets, gas stations, shopping malls, and clothing brands, are among
those that offer loyalty cards. Of all the existing loyalty cards, only a few
stand out. Heres the five of them:
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       SM Advantage Card
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                      With 48 malls all over the country, there is no way this card will be left
unused. SM Advantage Card can be used in purchasing not only in SM stores,
(Department Stores, Appliances, Supermarket) but also in SMs external partners
such as Petron and Shell gasoline stations. The card allows a holder to
accumulate points. and every point earned has a value of one peso which can be
used to avail discounts and freebies from SM or from its partner
establishments.
                     </div>

                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Z7VuICfnsxA/UudKLVcZlxI/AAAAAAAAEyY/6GeACoAizOY/s1600/Loyalty+%25281%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Z7VuICfnsxA/UudKLVcZlxI/AAAAAAAAEyY/6GeACoAizOY/s1600/Loyalty+%25281%2529.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>

                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       Suki Card
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      With more than 900 Mercury Drug establishments in the country, having a Suki
Card is a must. I bet almost every family has one. This card is absolutely
free, having no yearly fees; to get one, present receipts worth P1000.00. Much
like the other cards, the Suki Card allows a holder to earn points (1 point for
every P200.00 purchase) which can be used to avail of perks and special
discounts for subsequent purchases. Customers can even use the points as cash
credits. Who doesnt want to take care of their
                      <a href="http://www.imoney.ph/health-insurance">
                       health
                      </a>
                      and get discounts at
the same time?
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       Laking National Card
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      Established by the most
                      <a href="https://www.blogger.com/blogger.g?blogID=4111108754148185091" name="_GoBack">
                      </a>
                      widely known bookstore in the
country, National Book Store, the Laking National card is another great addition
in the list of must-have loyalty cards. With this card, a holder is entitled to
discounts on books and non-book items on special occasions, email alerts for
events and promotions, free wrapping on selected days, a premium item for every
Php 1000 purchase, pre-sale &amp; event privileges, and a lot more. You can get
all these for purchasing a card worth only Php 100 (Php 60 for students with
school ID) or you can get for free if you present receipts worth Php 5000 (accumulated
within 12 months).
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       <a href="http://www.jammyjamspread.com/2013/07/bench-lifestyle-card.html">
                        Bench Lifestyle Card
                       </a>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      Brought by the popular Philippine clothing brand that has become
internationally-known, Bench. This Lifestyle card is also accepted in Human,
Kashieca, Fix, Dimensione, Charles &amp; Keith, Celio, PCX, and The Face Shop. The
great thing about this loyalty card is that it has no minimum purchase to earn
points. So, whether your purchase is big or small, you will always be
rewarded.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       Petron Value Card
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      With free 24-hour towing and roadside assistance, personal accident insurance
of Php 10,000 plus 1000 medical reimbursement, and discounts on selected
products, whats not to like about Petron Value Card? This card allows you to
earn 1 point (equal to 1 peso) for every Php 250 fuel payment. The points
earned can then be used to pay for products and services in participating
Petron stations.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Having a loyalty card is one of great way to maximize the
products and services of a certain company. This is why owning such cards wont
make you feel regretful and a wise method to save because
most of the time, earned points are good as cash. Remember, we should spend our money prudently and even some people prefer to have financial advisor. However, most of the time we just do a research and becomes internet savvy to seek advice for particular services or products we want to have. With that, I learned that there is a site like
                      <a href="http://www.imoney.ph/">
                       iMoney
                      </a>
                      that provides helpful information for people that has concern for their outgoings.
