     <p>
      <b>
       Link
      </b>
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">
        Japanese
       </a>
       :
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
        
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">
        Hepburn
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        Rinku
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      refers to several different incarnations of the same
      <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">
       protagonist
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">
       Nintendo
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda" title="The Legend of Zelda">
       <i>
        The Legend of Zelda
       </i>
       series
      </a>
      . Link has been also featured in other media from Nintendo, including its merchandising, comic books, and a cartoon series, becoming one of Nintendo icons as well as one of the most well-known and popular characters in video game history.
     </p>
     <p>
      Through
      <i>
       The Legend of Zelda
      </i>
      series, Link is depicted as a child, teenager, or adult of the
      <a href="/wiki/Universe_of_The_Legend_of_Zelda#Hylian" title="Universe of The Legend of Zelda">
       Hylian
      </a>
      race, originating from the fictional land of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyrule" title="Hyrule">
       Hyrule
      </a>
      . Link often travels through Hyrule, defeating creatures, evil forces, and the series' primary antagonist,
      <a href="/wiki/Ganon" title="Ganon">
       Ganon
      </a>
      , while attempting to save
      <a href="/wiki/Princess_Zelda" title="Princess Zelda">
       Princess Zelda
      </a>
      and Hyrule. To defeat him, Link usually requires the mystic
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_Sword" title="Master Sword">
       Master Sword
      </a>
      and Light Arrows, or a similar legendary weapon, obtained after many trials and battles, and magical objects or using other items such as musical instruments and weaponry.
     </p>

        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="1280" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Linkamoto_E3_2004.jpg/220px-Linkamoto_E3_2004.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Linkamoto_E3_2004.jpg/330px-Linkamoto_E3_2004.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Linkamoto_E3_2004.jpg/440px-Linkamoto_E3_2004.jpg 2x" width="220"/>

       <div>
        <p>
         I am not Link, but I do know him! Even after 18 years, the
         <i>
          Legend of Zelda
         </i>
         never stops changing and this game is no different. We are now taking you to a world where Link has grown upa world where he will act different and look different. In order to grow, Link must not stand still and neither will I.
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_2-0">

           <span>
            [
           </span>
           2
           <span>
            ]
           </span>
          </a>
         </sup>
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div style="text-align: left;">
       Shigeru Miyamoto announcing
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda:_Twilight_Princess" title="The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess">
         The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess
        </a>
       </i>
       on stage at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E3_2004" title="E3 2004">
        E3 2004
       </a>

     <p>
      Link has almost no spoken dialogue in the game series, except for his grunts (not including the
      <a href="/wiki/CD-i_games_from_The_Legend_of_Zelda_series" title="CD-i games from The Legend of Zelda series">
       CD-i games
      </a>
      ). Prominent facial expression has only been visible since the series appeared on the
      <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_64" title="Nintendo 64">
       Nintendo 64
      </a>
      . Link's creator,
      <a href="/wiki/Shigeru_Miyamoto" title="Shigeru Miyamoto">
       Shigeru Miyamoto
      </a>
      has said in interviews that his conceptualization of
      <i>
       The Legend of Zelda
      </i>
      , and of Link, was based on his childhood memories of books, movies, stories and personal experiences. He tried to make people identify with Link and have the opportunity to be heroes like the character.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Although at the end of some games, Link becomes vastly talented in physical and magical arts, he usually starts off the game as a regular boy. On the origin of the name "Link", Miyamoto had this to say: "Links name comes from the fact that originally, the fragments of the Triforce were supposed to be electronic chips. The game was to be set in both the past and the future and as the main character would travel between both and be the link between them, they called him Link."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      There have been several iterations of Link in the history of Hyrule. The existence of multiple Links is made obvious on many occasions in the games; for example, the introduction sequence of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda:_The_Wind_Waker" title="The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker">
        The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker
       </a>
      </i>
      refers to an ancient, legendary champion who is identical in appearance to Link, and directly mentions the "Hero of Time" (a title given to Link in
      <i>
       Ocarina of Time
      </i>
      ) as a historical entity. Miyamoto has stated, "For every
      <i>
       Zelda
      </i>
      game we tell a new story, but we actually have an enormous document that explains how the game relates to the others, and bind them together. But to be honest, they are not that important to us. We care more about developing the game system ... give the player new challenges for every chapter that is born."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, the exact
      <a href="/wiki/Chronology" title="Chronology">
       chronology
      </a>
      of the
      <i>
       Zelda
      </i>
      series and the lineage of the various Links, though written down by Miyamoto and his team, has long been kept secret.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-creation_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-creation-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/Anthology" title="Anthology">
       anthology
      </a>
      titled
      <i>
       Hyrule Historia
      </i>
      in celebration of
      <i>
       Zelda's
      </i>
      25th anniversary. Released in Japan in December 2011 and in North America in early 2013, the anthology details the long-sought-after official
      <i>
       Zelda
      </i>
      timeline.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-historia_9-0">

        </span>

     </p>
     <p>
      Link's teenage appearance in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda:_Ocarina_of_Time" title="The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time">
        The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time
       </a>
      </i>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">

      <i>

      </i>

      <i>
       The Wind Waker
      </i>
      was the best option for expressing that."

       </a>

     <p>
      Link debuted on February 21, 1986, with the Japanese release of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda_(video_game)" title="The Legend of Zelda (video game)">
        The Legend of Zelda
       </a>
      </i>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recurring_characters_in_The_Legend_of_Zelda_series#Impa" title="Recurring characters in The Legend of Zelda series">

      <a href="/wiki/Triforce#Triforce_of_Power" title="Triforce">
       Triforce of Power
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Zelda_II:_The_Adventure_of_Link" title="Zelda II: The Adventure of Link">
        Zelda II: The Adventure of Link
       </a>

        The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past
       </a>
      </i>
      (1991), a new Link must intercept the wizard
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agahnim" title="Agahnim">
       Agahnim
      </a>
      before he breaks the seal on the Dark World and unleash Ganon's fury upon Hyrule. Along the way, Link must collect three magical Pendants of Virtue and claim the legendary Master Sword before facing Agahnim. Then he must venture into the Dark World to rescue the Seven Sages and defeat Agahnim and Ganon for good.
     </p>

Link uses the Ocarina of Time to play the Song of Time, which he can use to travel back in time when he plays it, to relive these three days repeatedly so he can prevent the disaster. Link does all these things and receives little more of a reward than the occasional 'thank you.' He is the truest of all video game heroes.
