          <div class="entry-content hentry" id="ec-251170">
           <article>
            <span content="http://calltothepen.com/2014/09/17/nori-aoki-sets-royals-record-hits-three-game-series/" itemprop="permalink">
            </span>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/a/aokino01.shtml?utm_campaign=Linker&amp;utm_source=direct&amp;utm_medium=linker-calltothepen.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Nori Aoki
              </a>
             </strong>
             has had 11 hits in last 12 at-bats. Thats pretty unbelievable but it happened. The 32-year-old right fielder set the Kansas City Royals record for most hits in a three-game series.
            </p>
            <p>
             He surpassed
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/w/wilsowi02.shtml?utm_campaign=Linker&amp;utm_source=direct&amp;utm_medium=linker-calltothepen.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Willie Wilson
              </a>
             </strong>
             (1980) and Royals legendary third baseman
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/b/brettge01.shtml?utm_campaign=Linker&amp;utm_source=direct&amp;utm_medium=linker-calltothepen.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               George Brett
              </a>
             </strong>
             (1982) when he got his 11th hit of the Royals series against the Chicago White Sox.
            </p>
            <p>
             Not only that, Aoki hit it off of White Sox ace and American League
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/y/youngcy01.shtml?utm_campaign=Linker&amp;utm_source=direct&amp;utm_medium=linker-calltothepen.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Cy Young
              </a>
             </strong>
             candidate
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/s/salech01.shtml?utm_campaign=Linker&amp;utm_source=direct&amp;utm_medium=linker-calltothepen.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Chris Sale
              </a>
             </strong>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Aoki had back to back four-hit games Monday and Tuesday. He added three more Wednesday against the lefty Sale.
            </p>
            <p>
             His first hit in Wednesdays game was a double off of Sale, it was one of just two extra-base hits that Sale has allowed to left-handed batters during the entire season.
            </p>
            <p>
             Thats an incredible feat for both players. Its a testament to what Aoki accomplished this week and certainly one of the many reasons Sale should finish in the top five in the voting for the A.L. Cy Young Award.
            </p>
            <p>
             In these last three games between the White Sox and the Royals Aoki improved his slash line from .265/.333/.333 to .282/.349/.352, according to
             <a href="http://m.royals.mlb.com/news/article/95186880/nori-aoki-sets-kc-mark-for-hits-in-a-three-game-set">
              MLB.coms Jackson Alexander
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Aoki who bats second in the Royals lineup, should have a chance to add to his record as the Royals are currently leading the White Sox by a score of 5-1 in the top of the seventh. He is perfect on the day having gone 3-3 with a walk.
            </p>
            <p>
             Stay with
             <a href="http://Calltothepen.com">
              Call to the Pen
             </a>
             for updates on Aoki and his record setting day.
            </p>
