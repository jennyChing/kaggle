      <span itemprop="articleBody">
       <p>
        <em>
         by Anna Mazur
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </em>
        In the June/July 2009 issue of
        <a href="/subscribe" target="_blank">
         <em>
          Threads
         </em>
        </a>
        (Issue #143) author Anna Mazur creates 4 fabulous
        <a href="http://bit.ly/edges-list-page">
         edge enhancements
        </a>
        inspired by designer Elie Tahari. On one of her styles, the yellow, silk charmeuse blouse with pearls over the
        <a href="/item/34536/video-how-to-sew-and-press-tucks">
         tucks
        </a>
        , she added a shell hem edge on the sleeve cuff. Below, Anna walks you through the steps of creating this shell treatment to use on an edge of any garment.
       </p>
       <p>
        1. Fold over the raw edge 1/4". then fold over again another 1/4".
        <br/>
        <br/>
        2. Anchor the thread along the folded edge and take a tiny stitch directly below on the garment.
       </p>
       <table border="0">
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td valign="top">
           <img alt="" height="323" src="/assets/uploads/posts/6935/shell-hem-008_lg.jpg" width="430"/>
          </td>
          <td style="width: 10px;" valign="top">
          </td>
          <td style="width: 143px;" valign="top">
           <span class="caption">
           </span>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
        <br/>
        3. Travel the needle along the folded edge for 1/8".
       </p>
       <table border="0">
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td valign="top">
           <img alt="" height="323" src="/assets/uploads/posts/6935/shell-hem-010_lg.jpg" width="430"/>
          </td>
          <td style="width: 10px;" valign="top">
          </td>
          <td style="width: 143px;" valign="top">
           <span class="caption">
           </span>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        4. Take a tiny stitch on the garment directly below where the thread exits the fold.
       </p>
       <table border="0">
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td valign="top">
           <img alt="" height="323" src="/assets/uploads/posts/6935/shell-hem-012_lg.jpg" width="430"/>
          </td>
          <td style="width: 10px;" valign="top">
          </td>
          <td style="width: 143px;" valign="top">
           <span class="caption">
           </span>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
        5. Travel the needle along the fold for 1/8" and repeat step #4 above.
       </p>
       <table border="0">
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td valign="top">
           <img alt="" height="323" src="/assets/uploads/posts/6935/shell-hem-013_lg.jpg" width="430"/>
          </td>
          <td style="width: 10px;" valign="top">
          </td>
          <td style="width: 143px;" valign="top">
           <span class="caption">
           </span>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
        6. Wrap the thread around the outer edge, bringing the needle up from the front side through the fabric and through the edge on the fold.
       </p>
       <table border="0">
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td valign="top">
           <img alt="" height="323" src="/assets/uploads/posts/6935/shell-hem-014_lg.jpg" width="430"/>
          </td>
          <td style="width: 10px;" valign="top">
          </td>
          <td style="width: 143px;" valign="top">
           <span class="caption">
           </span>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        7. Pull the thread to tighten the stitch and loop the thread a second time over the first stitch, tightening again.
       </p>
       <table border="0">
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td valign="top">
           <img alt="" height="323" src="/assets/uploads/posts/6935/shell-hem-017_lg.jpg" width="430"/>
          </td>
          <td style="width: 10px;" valign="top">
          </td>
          <td style="width: 143px;" valign="top">
           <span class="caption">
           </span>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
        8. Secure the stitch.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        9.Repeat steps 3-8.
       </p>
       <table border="0">
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td valign="top">
           <img alt="" height="323" src="/assets/uploads/posts/6935/shell-hem-028_lg.jpg" width="430"/>

        10. This is the finished hem as seen from thefront side.
       </p>
