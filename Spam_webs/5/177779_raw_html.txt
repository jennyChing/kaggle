                 <h2 class="date-header">
                  <span>
                   27 July 2014
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry">

                    </a>
                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://www.mywalkabout.net/2014/07/oneonta-trail.html">
                      Oneonta trail
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                       <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                        Whilst many first time travelers to the Columbia River Gorge make their way to the infamous
                        <a href="http://www.oregon.com/recreation/hike-multnomah-falls">
                         Multnomah Falls,
                        </a>
                        I find myself just a few clicks down the road to the much less crowded
                        <a href="http://www.portlandhikersfieldguide.org/wiki/Oneonta_Gorge_Hike">
                         Oneonta trail
                        </a>
                        . Today I search for cooling waterfalls and crystal clear rivers to splash around in under the summer sun.
                        <br/>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-g2o4JDZfZZM/U9T6AlBHryI/AAAAAAAADbA/M-sfPtyxBic/s1600/007.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="426" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-g2o4JDZfZZM/U9T6AlBHryI/AAAAAAAADbA/M-sfPtyxBic/s1600/007.JPG" width="640"/>
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        You have two clear choices of hikes here ; follow the creek bed upriver, or hiking above the gorge. With the water rising to above five feet in some areas and me with no waterproof gear, I choose to go above the gorge.
                        <br/>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6YnGWzSOvgk/U9T7K1XJXXI/AAAAAAAADbM/UwHzRKC_TYg/s1600/G0101200.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="480" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6YnGWzSOvgk/U9T7K1XJXXI/AAAAAAAADbM/UwHzRKC_TYg/s1600/G0101200.JPG" width="640"/>
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        Forest greens liven my mood the higher I ascend. The water becoming clearer, eventually a transparent looking glass to the life below. I dip my feet into the cool transcendence.
                        <br/>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-M_QEFhGo8ss/U9T8A3cAOjI/AAAAAAAADbU/PvPaTZRzdlM/s1600/G0081176.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="480" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-M_QEFhGo8ss/U9T8A3cAOjI/AAAAAAAADbU/PvPaTZRzdlM/s1600/G0081176.JPG" width="640"/>
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        Moss covered boulders beg to be climbed. I slowly make my way up river, cragging and pulling this body ever forward, sometimes slipping and falling backwards into the river. A baptism that clears the cobwebs of a cluttered mind.
                        <br/>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-6b9jADHs-7I/U9T8vdYnbaI/AAAAAAAADbc/ym5o5EZ12VM/s1600/009.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="512" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-6b9jADHs-7I/U9T8vdYnbaI/AAAAAAAADbc/ym5o5EZ12VM/s1600/009.JPG" width="640"/>
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        A rocky precipice calls me to its edge. I feel death as I venture out, see a vision of an old man clumsily losing his foothold and tumbling over the watery side, crushed upon the rocks below. A deep breath pulls me back.
                        <br/>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-tMHQwqtzD4g/U9T9snQmoSI/AAAAAAAADbk/Ofk7XlwVqDY/s1600/G0071156.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="480" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-tMHQwqtzD4g/U9T9snQmoSI/AAAAAAAADbk/Ofk7XlwVqDY/s1600/G0071156.JPG" width="640"/>
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        A few more breaths and I slowly make my way down, wondering about my experience along the Oneonta trail....
                        <br/>
                        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="445" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//player.vimeo.com/video/101847745" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="640">
                        </iframe>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
