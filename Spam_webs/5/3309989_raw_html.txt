          <p>
           Despite the colossal Internet market place of the 21st century, NRF Stores has round up the 100 most successful retailers, and according to the statistics, with the exception of one or two names, they all come in three-dimensional and concrete form (literally). In fact, five of the Top 100 large-format retailers 
           <strong>
            Wal-Mart
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=WMT" target="_blank">
            NYSE:WMT
           </a>
           ),
           <strong>
            Target
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=TGT" target="_blank">
            NYSE:TGT
           </a>
           ),
           <strong>
            Costco
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=COST" target="_blank">
            NASDAQ:COST
           </a>
           ),
           <strong>
            BJs Wholesale
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=BJ" target="_blank">
            NYSE:BJ
           </a>
           ), and Meijer  generate almost half a trillion dollars in sales;
           <strong>
            Amazon.com
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=AMZN" target="_blank">
            NASDAQ:AMZN
           </a>
           ) and
           <strong>
            Dell Direct
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=DELL" target="_blank">
            NASDAQ:DELL
           </a>
           )  the two e-based only retailers on the Top 100 list  combined generate $38.8 billion in U.S. sales.
          </p>
          <p>
           In ascending order, here are the Top 30 most successful retailers of the United States. All data is from NRF/Retailers.
          </p>

          <h2>
           30. BJs Wholesale Club
          </h2>
          <p>
           BJs, a membership-only warehouse club, ranks 30th on our list. It competes mainly with two other major U.S. club-like warehouses, Costco and Sams Club (owned by Wal-Mart), and holds an employee team of approximately 23,500.
          </p>

           </a>
          </p>

          <h2>
           29. J.C. Penney
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            J.C. Penney
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=JCP" target="_blank">
            NYSE:JCP
           </a>
           ) is a department store chain located in suburban shopping mall centers across the U.S. Like most retail department stores, it is usually located amongst a group of other competing clothing retailers.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_475743" style="width: 650px;">
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/view-image?src=2013/07/29_JCP.jpg&amp;1e63fc">

           28. Wakefern/ShopRite
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            ShopRite
           </strong>
           is the biggest chain of co-operative supermarkets, which are spread out through New Jersey, New York, Connecticut, Delaware, Maryland, and Pennsylvania. It is managed by its corporate distribution name
           <strong>
            Wakefern Food Corporation
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/view-image?src=2013/07/28_wakefernshoprite.jpg&amp;1e63fc">

          <h2>
           27. Meijer
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Meijer
           </strong>
           is a privately held U.S. hypermarket  a superstore that combines groceries and department store products in the same location. Meijer is also known for combining its superstore locations with a personal line of gas stations and convenience stores.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/view-image?src=2013/07/27_meijer.jpg&amp;1e63fc">
            <img alt="27_meijer" class="size-full wp-image-475739" height="800" src="http://www.cheatsheet.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/27_meijer.jpg?1e63fc" width="640"/>
           </a>
           <!--nextpage-->
          </p>
          <h2>
           26. Dollar General
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Dollar General
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=DG" target="_blank">
            NYSE:DG
           </a>
           ) is a chain of discount and variety stores, catered to smaller community locations that dont host superstore such as Wal-Mart. Its main competitors are other dollar store formatted chains, such as
           <strong>
            Dollar Tree
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=DLTR" target="_blank">
            NASDAQ:DLTR
           </a>
           ),
           <strong>
            Family Dollar
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=FDO" target="_blank">
            NYSE:FDO
           </a>
           ), and
           <strong>
            Freds
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/stock-research/company/?qs=FRED" target="_blank">
            NASDAQ:FRED
           </a>
           ).

           <a href="http://www.cheatsheet.com/view-image?src=2013/07/21_Kohls.jpg&amp;1e63fc">

          <h2>
           20. TJX

           <strong>
