     <h2>
      <a href="http://andrewtheprophet.com/blog/2014/06/17/the-mahdi-army-marches-against-isis-revelation-1316/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: The Mahdi Army Marches Against ISIS (Revelation 13:16)">
       The Mahdi Army Marches Against ISIS (Revelation 13:16)
      </a>
     </h2>
     <div class="entrytext">
      <div class="imageSmall" style="width: 569px;">
       <div style="text-align: right; position: relative; z-index: 95;">
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1256" style="width: 550px">
         <img alt="Mahdi Marches Against ISIS" class="size-medium wp-image-1256" data-wp-pid="1256" height="387" src="http://andrewtheprophet.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Mahdi-Army-540x387.jpg" width="540"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Mahdi Marches Against ISIS
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span class="Source">
          Reuters
         </span>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <span class="Caption">
         Volunteers march with Shiite cleric
         <a href="http://www.andrewtheprophet.com">
          Moqtada al-Sadrs Mehdi Army
         </a>
         march in Najaf, Iraq, vowing to defend the city from the Sunni ISIS insurgents.
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <p class="leadin" id="">
       Iraqi government forces led by a U.S.-trained commander lost control of another key northern Iraqi city to Sunni extremists on Monday, triggering warnings from neighboring Turkey that the
       <a href="http://www.andrewtheprophet.com">
        escalating violence risked opening another front in sectarian fighting.
       </a>
      </p>
      <p id="">
       <a href="http://www.andrewtheprophet.com">
        The loss of Tal Afar and the defeat of one of Iraqs top generals underlined the fragility of Prime Minister Nouri al-Malikis government
       </a>
       and raised fresh questions about its ability to counter advances by fighters of the Islamic State of Iraq and al-Sham, or ISIS, as the extremist Sunni militants are called.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       As worries continued to mount over the Baghdad governments ability to counter the ISIS threat, a U.S. official said the U.S. and Iran may hold talks as early as Monday on the spiraling violence there. White House spokesman Josh Earnest said talks were possible on the sidelines of this weeks nuclear negotiations in Vienna.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       A senior Iranian official told The Wall Street Journal on Monday evening in Vienna that there had been no bilateral discussions with U.S. officials so far on the Iraq crisis at the talks. The official said Irans only meeting with the U.S. was in the publicly announced three-way meeting that included the European Unions negotiating team.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       They only discussed the nuclear issue, the official said of the meeting.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       A U.S.
       <span class="mandelbrot_refrag">
        <a class="mandelbrot_refrag" data-ls-seen="1" href="http://www.marketwatch.com/organizations/State_Department?lc=int_mb_1001">
         State Department
        </a>
       </span>
       spokeswoman declined to comment.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       U.S. Secretary of State John Kerry said on Monday that Washington was open to discussions with Tehran about the military offensive by extremist Sunnis in Iraq. Mr. Kerry said he would not rule out
       <a href="http://www.andrewtheprophet.com">
        possible military cooperation with Iran.
       </a>
      </p>
      <p id="">
       But the White House on Monday ruled out coordinated military action. Any conversation with the Iranian regime will not include military coordination, Mr. Earnest told reporters traveling with President Barack Obama on Air Force One. Were not interested in any effort to coordinate military activity with Iran.
      </p>
      <div class="pvideo">
       <p>
        As the U.S. and Iran prepare for talks on the declining situation in Iraq, some experts say that Washington should not engage with terrorist organizations. Jonathan Schanzer, vice president for research at the Foundation for Defense of Democracies, discusses this view.
       </p>
      </div>
      <p id="">
       Kurdish fighters and forces loyal to Mr. Maliki, including soldiers from an elite unit of Irans Revolutionary Guard Corps, have been frantically trying to defend northern Iraq since ISIS took control of Mosul last week, with unconfirmed reports of fighting west of Mosul and near Iraqs border with Syria.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       An Iranian soldier from Quds Force, the IRGCs elite overseas branch, was killed in Iraq fighting Sunni extremists, news agencies affiliated with Irans government reported on Monday.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       International officials, alarmed at reports of mass killings committed by ISIS troops over the weekend, and Iraqi officials continued to investigate the incidents around Mosul, but conceded that facts were difficult to compile amid the fighting.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       The United Nations top human-rights official, Navi Pillay, said the number of people killed hasnt been verified, but that her agency is paying particular scrutiny to ISIS, given what she called their well-documented record of committing grave international crimes in Syria.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       In a small piece of positive news for Mr. Maliki, the leader of the Kurdish Peshmerga forces, the autonomous Kurdish governments independent fighting force, reported that his men had taken over the Rabiaa border crossing with Syria, northwest of Mosul.
      </p>
      <p id="">
       If the claim is true, ISIS could lose a key avenue for receiving reinforcements from Syria.
      </p>
      <p class="editorcomment">
       Margaret Coker in London, Laurence Norman in Vienna and Jay Solomon and Jeffrey Sparshott in Washington contributed to this article.
      </p>
