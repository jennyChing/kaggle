       <p>
        <b>
         Lowbrow Art
        </b>
        a.k.a.
        <b>
         Pop Surrealism
        </b>
        is an underground pop-art movement which originated in Los Angeles, California in the 70s. The term was coined by
        <b>
         Robert Williams
        </b>
        in reference to his own paintings.
       </p>
       <p>
        This style is recognizable by its distinctive characteristics, and features a mixture of images  including the grotesque, cartoon and comic-book characters, monsters, childish elements... with
        <b>
         a sense of humor that is at times sarcastic and even grotesqu
        </b>
        e.
       </p>
       <p>
        Undoubtedly influenced by the Underground Comix, Lowbrow Art is often associated with hot-rod street culture and punk music. The first expressions of this movement were from the hand of underground comic strip artists.
       </p>
       <p>
        Among the featured artists are
        <b>
         Robert Williams, Gary Baseman, Camile Rose Garcia, Takashi Murakami,Mark Ryden orTim Biskup.
        </b>
       </p>
