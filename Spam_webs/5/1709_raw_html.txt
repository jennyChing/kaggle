        <div class="article-content js-sticky-ad">
         <p>
          <strong>
           UPDATE
          </strong>
          : Kanye West will perform at the Grammys in what is being billed as a "never-before-seen solo performance." The solo performance will complement West's appearance with Paul McCartney and Rihanna for "FourFiveSeconds."
         </p>
         <p>
          The 57th annual Grammy Awards have announced the first wave of artists that will perform live on the Staples Center stage February 8th:
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/artists/ac-dc">
           AC/DC
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/artists/madonna">
           Madonna
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/artists/ariana-grande">
           Ariana Grande
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/artists/ed-sheeran">
           Ed Sheeran
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/artists/eric-church">
           Eric Church
          </a>
          . The award show's official Twitter
          <a href="https://twitter.com/TheGRAMMYs" target="_blank">
           made the announcement
          </a>
          this morning following rumors late Monday night that Madonna had been recruited to take the stage.
         </p>
         <aside class="related-article">
          <h4>
           Sidebar
          </h4>
          <a data-track="" data-track-action="Tap Sidebar" data-track-label="/music/features/rebel-heart-madonna-reveals-the-story-behind-six-surprise-songs-20141221" href="/music/features/rebel-heart-madonna-reveals-the-story-behind-six-surprise-songs-20141221">
           <div class="image-holder">
            <img alt="Madonna" class="" src="http://assets.rollingstone.com/assets/2014/article/rebel-heart-madonna-reveals-the-story-behind-six-surprise-songs-20141221/178575/small_rect/1419192905/300x169-20141221_madonnanew.jpg"/>
           </div>
           Exclusive: Madonna's First 'Rebel Heart' Interview 
          </a>
         </aside>
         <p>
          Madonnafirst teased her Grammy involvement Monday night when she
          <a href="https://twitter.com/Madonna/status/554853287821066240" target="_blank">
           tweeted
          </a>
          a picture of her "Rebel Heart" artwork meshed with a Grammy Award. "Rebel Hearts see you Feb 8th @TheGRAMMYs," shewrote. Her manager Guy Oseary
          <a href="http://www.billboard.com/articles/events/grammys-2015/6436598/madonna-will-perform-at-2015-grammy-awards" target="_blank">
           confirmed to
           <em>
            Billboard
           </em>
          </a>
          that she would perform at the 57th annual Grammys. This will be Madonna'sfifth time performing at the event; in 2014,
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/madonna-to-perform-same-love-with-macklemore-at-the-grammys-20140125">
           she joined Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis
          </a>
          for "Same Love" and "Open Your Heart."
         </p>
         <p>
          The Grammys will also be our first look at
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/artists/ac-dc">
           AC/DC
          </a>
          since the group released their new LP
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/ac-dc-rock-or-bust-20141202">
            Rock or Bust
           </a>
          </em>
          and should provide some clarity as to whether the rockers plan to move forward with or without embattled drummer Phil Rudd. The drummer, who
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/acdcs-phil-rudd-threatened-kill-man-daughter-phone-calls-20141205">
           faces drug possession and threatening to kill charges
          </a>
          in New Zealand, is due back in court on February 10th, two days after the 57th annual Grammys. Rudd has
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/acdc-phil-rudd-i-want-my-job-back-20141222">
           publicly stated
          </a>
          "I want my job back, I want my reputation back and I'm gonna get it back."
         </p>
         <p>
          Sheeran is up for three awards at this year's Grammys, including Album of the Year for
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/x-20140623">
            X
           </a>
          </em>
          . Grande was nominated for two awards: Best Pop Duo/Group Performance alongside Jessie J and Nicki Minaj for
          <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/videos/jessie-j-nicki-minaj-ariana-grande-bang-bang-video-20140826">
           "Bang Bang"
          </a>
          and Best Pop Vocal Album for
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/ariana-grande-my-everything-20140826">
            My Everything
           </a>
          </em>
          . Church scored four nominations, most notably Best Country Album for his
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/the-outsiders-20140211">
            The Outsiders
           </a>
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Expect the Grammys to announce many more performers before the awards show arrives February 8th.
         </p>
