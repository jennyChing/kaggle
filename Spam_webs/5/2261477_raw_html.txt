              You may have noticed that I have been highlighting notable artists this week. My goal is to blog about African American artist for black history month and do a little learning myself. While selecting artist to blog each day I am learning each day about the artist who have influenced my life. Today I would like to highlight Whitfield Lovell (born in 1959 Bronx, New York), a contemporary African-American artist who is known primarily his installations that incorporate masterful Conte crayon portraits of anonymous African Americans from between the Emancipation Proclamation and the Civil Rights Movement.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I have always been so surprised at how just a little bit of graphite, oil, charcoal and wood can tell so much of a story about our heritage. I guess you can say that Lovell has traveled many countries and seen many styles based on his style of art.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Most importantly to me is that he managed to captivate my interest in the story with a portrait and found objects.  Lovell creates these drawings in pencil, oil stick, or charcoal on paper, wood, ordirectly on walls.
             </div>

             </div>
             <div style="text-align: center;">
              Lovell used vintage photography with found objects. The combination of photos and found objects evokes personal memories, ancestral connections, and the collective American past.
              <br/>
              <br/>
