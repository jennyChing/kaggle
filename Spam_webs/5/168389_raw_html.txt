        <p>
         <img alt="security guard officer" class="caption" src="/images/stories/users/803/3407131008_1294d33019_t.jpg" title="...from rheault 1121"/>
         There are millions of Security Agents/Officers throughout the United States but sometimes they get a bad reputation. They're not frustrated want-to-be-cops, they are retirees, college students, people trying to raise a family and all that is in between. Most of all, they are licensed in their State with clean records. The duties run the gamut from Homeland Security to the public and private sectors and the jobs are not always easy, even though some offer far more stimulation than others.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         They take a course that teaches far more about the law than what was previously known and for those who take this career choice seriously, can make it a good one but you simply have to want to be good at it.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Things You'll Need:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         * A willingness to take on duties that are not always pleasant
         <br/>
         * Keen surveillance
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Step 1
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Decide if you are physically and mentally fit to take on the job of securing someone's property, valuables or their life. Security has a wide range of aspects. Ask yourself if you want to know the structure for which you may work, inside and out, and the type of people that will occupy or frequent it.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Step 2
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Determine if you are the type of person who is always alert to your surroundings and can be on constant surveillance.. Are you willing to acclimate yourself to questions about all the conveniences that the public would inquire about, i.e. nearest exit, water fountains, restrooms, dining, parking, etc. and still observe your surroundings? Do you want to be an armed Security Officer?
        </p>
        <p>
         Step 3
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Are you willing and able to keep your uniform always in the best possible condition and available in case you are called in on a day off for someone who can't come in. Then ask yourself if you can wait the usual required two hours for a worker that calls in late.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Step 4
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Are you willing to adhere to the concept of 'abandoning your post' as a reason for reprimand that could result in discharge. To abandon a post can cause a severe security breach.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Step 5
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Do you take punctuality and reliability seriously? The client that will employ the Company that hires you as a Security Agent is not interested in how long a person's day is. He is interested in coverage. Be willing to take into consideration how your tardiness will affect the Officer whom you are to relieve.
         <br/>
         .
         <br/>
         Step 6
         <br/>
         <br/>
         After you have thought about these challenges, ask yourself if you want to take this job as a career or as a means to an end. Many of the unarmed security jobs pay less than $12 an hour in Illinois. Can you support yourself or a family on this?
        </p>
