              <p>
               <strong>
                Today is
                <a href="http://blogactionday.change.org/">
                 Blog Action Day 2010
                </a>
                . The theme is water, that innocuous liquid that also happens to be an absolute requirement for any human civilization to survive.
               </strong>
               Its a very relevant theme, and one that is sadly underreported in the media.
              </p>
              <p>
               To commemorate water for Blog Action Day, weve listed 10 things you need to know about water (this list also appeared for springs World Water Day):
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                1. 1%
               </strong>
               of the worlds fresh water is currently accessible to humans.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                2. 346,000 million:
               </strong>
               Number of gallons of fresh water the US consumes per day.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                3. 80%
               </strong>
               of that water is used for irrigation and thermoelectric generation.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                4. 80-100 gallons:
               </strong>
               Amount of water the average person in the US uses
               <em>
                per day
               </em>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                5. 13.2 gallons:
               </strong>
               Estimated amount of water required for any single person for cooking, sanitation, bathing, and survival needs.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                6. 3.575 million
               </strong>
               : Number of people who die every year from water-related disease.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                7. 84%
               </strong>
               of them are children.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                8. 50%
               </strong>
               of global hospital beds are occupied by someone with a waterborne illness.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                9. 25%
               </strong>
               of bottled water is basically tap water in a bottletreated or untreated.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                10. 1.5 million
               </strong>
               tons of plastic are used to bottle 89 billion liters of water every year.
              </p>
              <p>
               Help sustain your own local water supply by installing water-saving devices into your home and using filtered rather than bottled water.
              </p>
              <p>
               Sources:
               <a href="http://www.allaboutwater.org/">
                All About Water
               </a>
               ,
               <a href="http://water.org/learn-about-the-water-crisis/facts/">
                Water.org
               </a>
              </p>
