                      <h1 class="entry-title cb-entry-title cb-single-title" itemprop="headline">
                       Detox Kitchens Top 10 Tips on a Healthy September
                      </h1>
                      <div class="cb-byline">
                       <div class="cb-author">
                        <i class="fa fa-user">
                        </i>
                        <a href="http://mybaba.com/author/my-baba/">
                         My Baba
                        </a>
                       </div>

                        <i class="fa fa-clock-o">

                        <time class="updated" datetime="2014-09-01">
                         1 September, 2014
                        </time>
                       </div>

                   </header>
                   <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Keep your summer glow
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     As the day gets shorter and the faint smell of sun cream dissipates, its important that we remember to keep those levels of vitamin D up and boost our diet with some omega 3 and vitamin E for beautifully glowing skin. Stock up on the following ingredients, Salmon, Mackerel, Eggs, Nuts and seeds, and Avocados, and youll be glowing in no time!
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Aim for 7-a-day
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Start your day with a juice and a handful of nuts, then try and have a piece of fruit with breakfast. Pack two good helpings of veg into your lunch and dinner and snack on berries or vegetables batons throughout the day. Youll find it surprising how easy it is to squeeze in the veg and fruit, especially if you are organized and take healthy snacks with you to work.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Say goodbye to refined sugar
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Just cut it out, it has no nutritional benefit to us and you can find sweetness is so many other natural foods. Try making your own batch of muesli without adding and sugar and instead serve it with some naturally sweet rice milk and some juicy fresh peaches. Or when those chocolate cravings come into play, reach for a raw cacao alternative, there are so many options in our supermarkets now. You can also try making raw cacao brownies at home.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Cut down on the caffeine
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Its all about keeping those blood sugar levels balanced. Caffenine makes our bodies stressed, when are bodies are stressed we produce insulin which makes our blood sugar levels rise, and then crash. If you find it difficult to cut out caffeine completely try to cut down to one weak coffee a day and drink green tea where possible.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Swap white for brown
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Its a simple and popular solution to getting more fiber and more nutrients into your diet. It is one of the easiest changes to implement into your diet and you will be surprised by how much it affects your energy levels. Brown rice and wholegrain bread release energy into our blood stream more slowly that their white counterparts, thus there is less of a spike in our blood sugar and therefore less of an energy crash. A simple but effective change.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Go Green one day a week
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Sticking to all that is green for one day a week will boost your nutrient intake and leave you feeling cleansed and energised. Going green doesnt mean scrimping on calories, you can still fit in your 1,800-2,000 calories by eating green vegetables, lean protein, nuts and seeds. Try having an egg and avocado breakfast, followed by a green juice and some nuts at 11, then for lunch go for a salad of green vegetables with lots of fresh herbs, nuts and seeds. For your mid afternoon snack have a green smoothie with fruit and vegetables, a good 500ml smoothie will fill you up until dinner. Stir-fry some vegetables with lots of fresh ginger and garlic to pack some flavour and serve with a big piece of grilled salmon. For pudding why not try knocking up some avocado ice-cream. The next day you will feel like a green goddess!
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <a href="http://www.mybaba.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Salmon-burgers-and-1145366.jpg">
                      <img alt="Salmon burgers and #1145366" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-57797" height="499" src="http://www.mybaba.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Salmon-burgers-and-1145366-1024x682.jpg" width="750"/>
                     </a>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Get juicy
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Why not set yourself a goal for September to have a juice every day. The extra nutrient boost will undoubtedly have an effect on your energy levels, skin and hair, and mood. Always have a handful of nuts with your juice to ensure you are getting the fiber needed to make the most of the nutrients from the juice.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Plan your work out
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Being organised when it comes to working out is the most important part. Plan your month and stick to it. Whether its just one yoga class a week or four bums and tums classes a week, work out what you can reasonably fit into your schedule and just do it. Remember, it only takes two weeks to get into a routine so if you can stick to your plan throughout September your more likely to keep on track for the rest of the year.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Reduce the amount of dairy in your diet
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     We dont recommend that people completely cut out dairy but reducing it or eliminating it for a few days wont do you any harm and you may notice a difference in your energy levels. If you are cutting out dairy make sure you are still getting a good amount of calcium in your diet. Almonds and leafy green vegetables contain good amounts.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Remember, healthy living shouldnt feel like a drag
                     </strong>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     The most important part about leading a healthy lifestyle is that you enjoy it. There is absolutely no point on embarking on a new diet or new fitness program if every day you wake up and dread it. Think of the foods you love and try to incorporate them into your diet as much as possible. Use herbs and spices to make your food as tasty and enjoyable as possible. When you want to have a little treat, then have a little treat, never ever feel guilty about the food you are eating. The more goodness you put in, the less likely you are to want to eat unhealthy food. And be realistic with exercise, if you are just not the type of person to want to work out, dont beat yourself up about it, start small and find activities that you really enjoy.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <a href="http://www.mybaba.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/MG_6683.jpg">

                     </a>
                    </p>

                     </strong>
                    </p>
