     <div class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Education Minister Christopher Pyne is writing a book, A Letter to My Children." src="/content/dam/images/z/r/4/h/g/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.1ms6ol.png/1429877048864.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Education Minister Christopher Pyne is writing a book, A Letter to My Children.
        <cite>
         <i>
          Photo: Andrew Meares
         </i>
        </cite>
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       He's been called the
       <a href="http://www.smh.com.au/national/abc-apologises-and-pulls-pynehate-article-20101001-160ev.html">
        most hated man in Australian politics
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/tv-and-radio/qa-panel-member-likens-christopher-pyne-to-an-alien-from-another-galaxy-20150331-1mbgm0.html">
        an alien from another planet
       </a>
       , and describes himself as a "
       <a href="http://www.smh.com.au/federal-politics/political-news/i-want-it-to-be-a-surprise-for-you-christopher-pyne-smiles-through-grilling-from-david-speers-20150317-1m0p91.html">
        fixer
       </a>
       ".
      </p>
      <p>
       But now Christopher Pyne is preparing to show a different side of himself.
      </p>
      <p>
       While publicly battlinga hostile Senate crossbench to try force through the Abbott government'suniversity reforms,the Education Minister has found time to turn his hand to writing a book.
      </p>
      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Carolyn and Christopher Pyne with their four children (from left) Eleanor, Barnaby, Aurelia and Felix, in 2012." src="/content/dam/images/1/m/s/h/e/d/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.1ms6ol.png/1429877048864.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Carolyn and Christopher Pyne with their four children (from left) Eleanor, Barnaby, Aurelia and Felix, in 2012.
        <cite>
         <i>
          Photo: SUPPLIED
         </i>
        </cite>
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       And it's not what you'd expect from the political brawler.
      </p>
      <div class="ad adAlignRight" id="adspot-300x250-pos3" style="display:none;">
       <small>
        Advertisement
       </small>
       <script>
        titan.requestAd.push("adspot-300x250-pos3");
       </script>
      </div>
      <p>
       Rather than a policy-heavypolitical treatise, Mr Pyne is penning a personal memoir entitled
       <em>
        A Letter to My Children
       </em>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The prospective author told Fairfax Media the book will be "an explanation to my children of why people choose a life of public service, despite the obvious sacrifices".
      </p>
      <div class="cT-imagePortrait">
       <img alt="Mr Pyne with sons in January in this year." itemprop="image" src="/content/dam/images/1/m/s/8/b/6/image.related.articleLeadNarrow.300x0.1ms6ol.png/1429877048864.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Mr Pyne with sons in January in this year.
        <cite>
         <i>
          Photo: James Brickwood
         </i>
        </cite>
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       It will be "told through the story of my father's life and my own political career so far".
      </p>
      <p>
       The publicity blurb fromMelbourneUniversity Press promises Mr Pyne will explore why "seemingly intelligent men and woman leave their families to spend more than half the year travelling to Canberra, and night after night out at electorate and campaign events? Surely there are easier ways to earn a living?"
      </p>
      <p>
       Mr Pyne and his wifeCarolyn have four children -Eleanor, Barnaby, Felix and Aurelia. His father, Remington Pyne, was a distinguished ophthalmologist who was named South Australian father of the year in 1976.
      </p>
      <p>
       Though only 47 years old,Mr Pyneentered politics as a fresh-faced 25-year-old way back in 1993.
      </p>
      <p>
       The book, which is set downto be published by MUP in September, is a rareforay into the world ofliterature by a conservative politician.
      </p>
      <p>
       Prime Minister Tony Abbottpolitical manifesto
       <em>
        Battlelines
       </em>
       , pennedafter the Howard government lost office, is a recent exception, whileSouth Australian Senator Cory Bernardi is another recent exception to the rule.
      </p>
      <p>
       In contrast, Labor politicians frequently write books. Threeformer Labor frontbenchers - ex-prime minister Julia Gillard as well asWayne Swan andGreg Combethave all written books since the change of government.
      </p>
      <p>
       Current shadow ministers Chris Bowen and Kim Carr also publishedbooks in 2013, and there are more on the way from the Labor side of politics.
      </p>
      <p>
       Mr Bowen nowhas another policy-focused book in the works, as do shadow ministerial colleaguesJenny Macklin andMark Butler.
      </p>
