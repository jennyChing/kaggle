           <a href="http://gather.com/ubaldo-jimenez-closing-in-on-deal-with-orioles/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Ubaldo Jimenez Closing In on Deal with Orioles">
            Ubaldo Jimenez Closing In on Deal with Orioles
           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="meta">
           <span class="meta-cats">
            Filed in
            <a href="http://gather.com/category/sports/" rel="category tag">
             Gather Sports News Channel
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="meta-author">
            by
            <a href="http://gather.com/author/dpare71/" rel="author" title="Posts by Darren Pare">
             Darren Pare
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="meta-date">
            on February 18, 2014
           </span>
           <span class="meta-comments">
            
            <a href="http://gather.com/ubaldo-jimenez-closing-in-on-deal-with-orioles/#comments" rel="bookmark" title="Comments for Ubaldo Jimenez Closing In on Deal with Orioles">
             0 Comments
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
          <p>
           Ubaldo Jimenez has been waiting for months for the perfect deal and it appears that deal is close to fruition.
           <a href="http://www.masnsports.com/school_of_roch/2014/02/sources-orioles-working-to-finalize-deal-with-jimenez.html" target="_blank">
            MASN Sports is reporting
           </a>
           that the Baltimore Orioles and Jimenez are close to finalizing a deal for four years and $48 million.
          </p>
          <p>
           The Orioles have been looking for a starter to add to their addition since the off-season began. They were in talks with both A.J. Burnett and Bronson Arroyo, but were unable to land either of those two.
          </p>
          <p>
           Jimenez had a bounce back campaign in 2013 with the Cleveland Indians after two lackluster seasons in 2011 and 2012. He posted a 13-9 record with an earned run average of 3.30, down over two runs from the previous year. He also became more of a strikeout pitcher with 194 punch outs in 182 2/3 innings or work.
          </p>
          <p>
           The Orioles have had a disappointing off-season so far with the biggest blunder being the Grant Balfour situation in which the parties agreed to terms and then the Orioles pulled out of the deal, supposedly because they saw something they didnt like in the medical reports. So Orioles fans may want to wait until the deal is official before the start getting excited.
          </p>
          <p>
           Another concern is that Jimenez might not be able to recreate the magic of last season without Indians pitching coach Mickey Callaway. He could easily backslide to the guy that led the American League in losses and wild pitches in 2012 and if that happens he would become a big free agent bust. Orioles pitching coach Dave Wallace may have his hands full with Jimenez.
          </p>
          <p>
           Do you think Ubaldo Jimenez is a good fit in Baltimore?
          </p>
