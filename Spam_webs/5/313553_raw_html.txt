       <h2>
        <strong>
         Wilderness at Tokyos Doorstep
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Situated within the Chichibu Tama Kai National Park in the western part of Tokyo, the
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Okuchichibu_Mountains" target="_blank">
         Okuchichibu Mountains
        </a>
        are a hikers paradise allowing us city dwelling folk to escape the confines of the nearby metropolis. The mountains comprise five peaks included in the
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/100_Famous_Japanese_Mountains" target="_blank">
         100 Famous Japanese Mountains
        </a>
        including the striking Mount Kinpu the second highest of the Okuchichibu Mountains. The following ten peaks and mountain passes hope to provide a quick snapshot of this remarkable wilderness area.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em>
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         1.
         <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/hiking/mt-kinpu/">
          Mount Kinpu
         </a>
         2,599 m (Yamanashi / Nagano Prefecture)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i2.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Okuchichibu-Mountains-Mt.-Kinpu.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Okuchichibu Mountains Mt. Kinpu" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-8056" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Okuchichibu-Mountains-Mt.-Kinpu.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          #
         </em>
         2.
         <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/hiking/mt-kumotori/">
          Mount Kumotori
         </a>
         2,017 m (Tokyo Prefecture)
        </strong>
       </h2>
