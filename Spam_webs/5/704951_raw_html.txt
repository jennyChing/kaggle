        <br/>
        <center>
         <img class="lazy" data-original="http://www.gamerevolution.com/images/misc/heath-hindman-most-interesting-gamer.jpg" src="http://media.gamerevolution.com/style/structure/spacer.gif"/>
        </center>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <em>
         (For the first column in this series, check
         <u>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.gamerevolution.com/features/he-is-the-most-interesting-gamer-in-the-world" target="_blank">
            here
           </a>
          </strong>
         </u>
         .)
        </em>
        <br/>
        I just watched The 84th Annual Academy Awards and was inspired to write a movie screenplay. I also don't actually know anything about writing a screenplay, as I'm sure will become obvious. My movie is called "
        <strong>
         Square Enix: The Restaurant
        </strong>
        ." Here's my first draft.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
        <br/>
        <em>
         [Our scene begins inside a restaurant called "Square Enix. Customers are sitting down and placing orders.]
        </em>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Bob
        </strong>
        : You know I've had the
        <em>
         *glances at menu*
        </em>
        "
        <strong>
         Console Kingdom Hearts Pie
        </strong>
        " two times and liked it, so I believe I'll have that again.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Waitress
        </strong>
        : Ooo, sorry, yeah, a lot of people -- whole lot of people -- have been asking for it but the restaurant doesn't make it anymore.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Bob
        </strong>
        :
        <em>
         *Eyebrows Raised*
        </em>
        I...see.
        <br/>
        Waitress: We do have a smaller version of it that uses very few of the original ingredients. Should I have the cook make you up some of that?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Bob
        </strong>
        : What? No, why would you even suggest that?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Jane
        </strong>
        : Let's just get something else honey, it's nothing to get worked up over. This is such a big and famous restaurant, I'll bet there are dozens of great things you've never even tried.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Waitress
        </strong>
        :
        <em>
         *Smiles warmly*
        </em>
        We sure do. Would you like to try some
        <strong>
         Third Birthday
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Cake
        </strong>
        ?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Bob
        </strong>
        : Gah, ugh,
        <em>
         *motions as if he is about to gag*
        </em>
        , uh, about as much as I'd like to try failed eye surgery.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <em>
         [In the background, a man in top hat and monacle walks by, a woman in a fancy evening gown on his arm.]
        </em>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Man in top hat
        </strong>
        : I tell you daahling, they make the finest original creations heeah.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Hostess
        </strong>
        : Where can I seat you today, smoking or non?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Man in top hat
        </strong>
        : Oh I dare say non, please.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Hostess
        </strong>
        : And current generation or last generation?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Man in top hat
        </strong>
        : Current, of coahse!
        <br/>
        <em>
         [The trio moves to their table in the non-smoking, current-generation section.]
        </em>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Waitor
        </strong>
        : Evening. What can I get you?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Man in top hat
        </strong>
        : Mmmyes I'll have your finest top-of-the-line MMORPG, please.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Waitor
        </strong>
        : Oh, I'm sorry, we are fresh out of those.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Man in top hat
        </strong>
        : I say, what eva do you mean?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Waitor
        </strong>
        : Well you see, you're in the current generation section. If you move to last generation, we have plenty of
        <strong>
         Final Fantasy XI
        </strong>
        we could bring out. We had something else in mind for our Current Generation customers but...well, it's not quite cooked yet.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Well-dressed Woman
        </strong>
        : Honey I do think I recall reading about that in the papers! Some awful things happened to people who ate the
        <strong>
         Final Fantasy XIV Parfait
        </strong>
        before it was ready.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Waitor
        </strong>
        : We're getting closer to fixing it though. Now that it's been on the menu for a year and a half, we feel like it's almost to the point it'd be worth buying.
        <br/>
        <em>
         [The man and woman look at each other, not quite sure of what to say]
        </em>
        <br/>
        <div style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" class="lazy" data-original="/images/misc/dragon-quest-drink.jpg" src="http://media.gamerevolution.com/style/structure/spacer.gif" style="width: 500px; height: 375px;"/>
        </div>
        <br/>
        <em>
         [Cut to inside the kitchen]
        </em>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Cook
        </strong>
        : Cripes! I'm getting so many orders for something called "
        <strong>
         FFVII Remake Somethingsomething
        </strong>
        !" It's distracting me from the menu items that we actually have!
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Dish Washing Guy
        </strong>
        : If so many custimers want it, mayb eit'd be profitable to--
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Owner
        </strong>
        : Stop talking and get working you two, we're busy! I'll make the business decisions, thanks.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <em>
         [A voice is heard]
        </em>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Angry customer
        </strong>
        : Hey! I ordered my
        <strong>
         Versus XIII
        </strong>
        Steak like forever ago! Feels like I've been waitin' 6 years over here! What's a guy gotta do?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Manager
        </strong>
        : Whoa whoa, sir, we're terribly sorry, it's just taking longer than expected. Hey, would you like to sample a medium
        <strong>
         XIII-2
        </strong>
        ?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Angry customer
        </strong>
        : What? No, I didn't order that.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Manager
        </strong>
        : I know. No one did, actually, I was just hoping you might buy it. Hmmm...I wonder what I can do to make someone want this.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Angry customer
        </strong>
        : ...
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Manager
        </strong>
        : I mean because some people complained about the cuts of
        <strong>
         XIII
        </strong>
        so I thought I'd spice it up a bit.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Angry customer
        </strong>
        : What's that got to do with me? Your boy with the hair told me about
        <strong>
         Versus XIII
        </strong>
        when I walked in the door. He showed me pictures. He made me want it. I said yeah, hey, that's where my money will go. I've been waiting. And now you tell me you rehashed something I didn't like, and you think I'm going to be happy that you spent your time doing that instead of making what I ordered?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Manager
        </strong>
        : You mean you're not?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Angry customer
        </strong>
        : I'm sitting here watching people who came in after me sit down, order, and get something and leave, and I still haven't gotten what I want most.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Manager
        </strong>
        : Well this kind of thing happens with all establishments, sir. It's just that one item and--
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Angry customer
        </strong>
        : I only ordered because you talked it up so much! You made it look irresistible!
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Manager
        </strong>
        : And I am sorry for the mistake, honestly. We shouldn't have done that. Is there anything else I can get you? Anything at all?
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Angry customer
        </strong>
        : *Begins cooling down, lets out a heavy sigh and looks at his menu*
        <br/>
        Well. I heard your waitress walking by and talking about a lot of people ordering a, what was it called,
        <strong>
         FFVII Remake
        </strong>
        ? Must be good if it's so hot. I guess I'll try that.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Manager
        </strong>
        : Oh, that's out of the question. If we made that, you'd be waiting twice as long as you already have.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Angry customer
        </strong>
        : Son of a--
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <u>
         <em>
          <strong>
           SQUARE ENIX: THE RESTAURANT
          </strong>
         </em>
        </u>
