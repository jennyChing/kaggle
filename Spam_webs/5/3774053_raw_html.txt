      <h2 class="post-title">
       <a href="http://blog.simply-docs.co.uk/2014/05/15/some-of-the-most-ridiculous-ts-and-cs-youll-ever-see/">
        Some of the most ridiculous Ts and Cs youll ever see
       </a>
      </h2>
      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        End User Licensing Agreements are some of the most pointless things on the face of the earth; theyre up there with fridge magnets and Justin Bieber. So lets celebrate them in all of their ridiculous glory by writing an entire blog post on them. Some of them prevent us from carrying out lifelong ambitions like taking over the war with nuclear weapons (iTunes) whereas others deal in trivial matters like Googles complete and utter ownership of your digital soul. Lets look at some of the best in the world of EULAs and wonder at our own stupidity for handing over our souls to the digital ether.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         iTunes
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://blog.simply-docs.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/1.png">
         <img alt="1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1395" height="396" src="http://blog.simply-docs.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/1.png" width="514"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://main.makeuseoflimited.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/safari_windows.png">
         Image Source
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        iTunes take the proverbial biscuit with this one. There you are wanting to take over the world like that over ambitious mouse from Pinky and the Brain and then you realise you cant. That last Nickelback album you downloaded has stopped you in your tracks. Time to put the iPod down and put the missiles away then.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         PC Pitstop
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://blog.simply-docs.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/2.png">
         <img alt="2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1396" height="238" src="http://blog.simply-docs.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/2.png" width="434"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://main.makeuseoflimited.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/pcpitstop_eula.png">
         Image Source
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres one you wish you had read. Whoever read this first and claimed won $1,000. It makes you want to go back through all of the agreements youve ever read to recover the fortune you may have had. This EULA is also kind of smart-assed. Its literally saying You didnt read me, so Im going to punish you Not cool PC Pitstop. Not cool. It also took someone four months to win the prize. So theres a lot of other ignorant users out there too, how comforting.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Google
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://blog.simply-docs.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/3.png">
         <img alt="3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1397" height="183" src="http://blog.simply-docs.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/3.png" width="658"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Mephistopheles has been trading as Google for years and we didnt even know it. Were handing over our souls to Google? Thats a clause we really should have paid attention to. Its the use of the words perpetual and irrevocable it sounds so Shakespearean. I irrevocably disagree, in a state of perpetuum.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Far Cry 2
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://blog.simply-docs.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/4.png">
         <img alt="4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1398" height="167" src="http://blog.simply-docs.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/4.png" width="436"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Video games have slowly been taking over our lives since they invented Sonic the Hedgehog. Now it seems these games are trying to direct our collective moral compass. Whilst this may not necessarily be a bad thing, it may be hard to draw a line in Far Cry 2. What counts as an immoral act? Playing a violent video game might be, best to avoid Far Cry 2 then.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         The Fallout
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        So whats the backlash of all of this ridiculous demanding from anonymous companies? A documentary, thats what. Some geniuses made a documentary that highlights the power of our personal information and how it is used to make huge amounts of money. And points out that we agreed to it all. Watch the trailer to get yourself thinking.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yzyafieRcWE">
         https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yzyafieRcWE
        </a>
       </p>
