      <h1 id="post-title">
       7 tips for a stress free day
      </h1>
      <div class="post_info">
       Posted  by
       <a href="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by bodhi">
        bodhi
       </a>
       on Nov 14, 2011 in
       <a href="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/category/articles/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Articles">
        Articles
       </a>

       <img alt="Comments" height="18" src="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/wp-content/themes/Polished/images/comments.png" width="20"/>
       <a href="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/2011/11/14/7-tips-for-a-stress-free-day/#respond" title="Comment on 7 tips for a stress free day">

      <div class="postcontent">
       <img alt="7 tips for a stress free day" class="post_img wp-post-image" height="185" src="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/000040030fv5-185x185.jpg" title="7 tips for a stress free day" width="185"/>
       <div style="float:left;margin-right:10px;">
        <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="69" scrolling="no" src="http://widgets.fbshare.me/files/fbshare.php?url=http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/2011/11/14/7-tips-for-a-stress-free-day/&amp;title=7 tips for a stress free day&amp;size=large" width="53">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <p>
        Stress has become a major concern in almost everybodys life. Even though you think you are not stressed, you may be more than you think. Although I could get into very specific details of the how and why you may be stressed and how it affects your body and mind, I wont because the whole point of this post is to lead you toward a stress free day. However, you have to understand the fact that some of you may go through a great deal of stress and not even show it and others will go through a little problem and see it as the end of the world. Some stressors are clear as day but others sneaks in from the back door and just makes matters worst. Understanding your stressors may help you go through the day easier and hopefully, eliminate them definitely.
       </p>
       <p>
        Why is it so important to try and take out major stressors in our lives? Besides the obvious reasons, it can also wreck havoc on the inside, viscerally. More and more people have some kind of digestion dysfunction issues, if not most of the people you know. Irritable bowel syndrome, Crohns disease, Acid reflux, duodenal ulcers, bloating and so on are some of the most common conditions and are related to 2 things, stress and habits. Study after study reveals that most of these problems occur in people who suffer from anxiety or that are under a great deal of stress. Maybe because people under stress have more of a tendency to over eat, smoke or indulge in bad habits, which leads to these uncomfortable symptoms of conditions, but no. Even if you look at the bacterial load a person may have and fix it, the other factors (lifestyle and stress) still comes out as the major culprits.
       </p>
       <p>
        What I failed to understand was why is that people would get sick after a stressful event? Why some people would get some cancer or major conditions all of a sudden but looked amazing and without any signs of stress for years on end? After seeing tons of people and working with executives and stress management lifestyle strategies, I came to realize that most of the digestive problems were caused by a rebound of I dont what. It turns out that this theory goes by the name of Acid Rebound
        <a href="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/2011/11/14/7-tips-for-a-stress-free-day/#_edn1" title="">
         [i]
        </a>
        . Heres a little crash course.
       </p>
       <p>
        Your stomach has its own defence mechanism which is his built in acid production. It breaks down everything into useable nutrients and energy. That hydrochloric acid is very powerful and could even digest another stomach. But why is it that our stomach does not digest itself? Turns out that our stomach builds many layers of stomach walls and coats them with layers of mucus to sooth and protect. There is also bicarbonate that is secreted to neutralize the acid. After a stressful situation that lasted longer than it should, your stomach cuts down on his own acid production. So many meals werent digested properly. Due to a lack of acidity, your stomach starts cutting corners and put less energy in building wall layers, less mucus and less bicarbonate. Now comes the time where stress is at a minimum and everything is back to normal. You decide to reward yourself with a great binge and eat everything you see. Since stress is not an issue, hydrochloric acid kicks in like it should, but your built in defense mechanism are still down. Bicarbonate cant keep up, stomach walls have thinned out and there isnt as much mucus to protect it so bacterial infections rear its ugly head and BOOM! Stomach ulcers. If its not stomach ulcers, some type of digestive discomfort is near. If these periods of stress goes on and off for a while, Irritable bowel syndrome or even crohns disease is a sure possibility. Now that you understand the mechanism and the impact of stress on digestion, let me show you what you can do about it.
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         1.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Your surroundings
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        One of the first things to do is to identify your major sources of stress and this is why the following saying is very truthful. You are the reflection of the 5 person who you hang out with the most. This ones says a lot. If you have a group of friends, Im sure that one of them, as mean as it sounds, may be somewhat of Debbie Downer type of friend as portrayed in the hilarious
        <a href="http://panasonicyouth.buzznet.com/user/video/228337/snl-debbie-downer-featuring-lindsay/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://panasonicyouth.buzznet.com']);">
         Saturday Night live sketch
        </a>
        . Never let someone drain your energy out. I know it is easier said than done, but it is what it is. Instead of having some friends always come to you to complain or to speak their mind about subjects that you couldnt care less. I know it sounds harsh but again, thats life, your life. Hang around people you may want to learn from, people you relate too, positive people. I learned it the hard way. Years ago I was involved in a business partnership with my best friend. After he tried to pull a fast one on me, I decided that it was time to go on my own. Since then, my business grew exponentially and I am thankful for that.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        <strong>
         <em>
          The Ass and His Purchaser
         </em>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        (You might prefer to call this The Donkey and His Purchaser.)
       </p>
       <p>
        A man wished to purchase an Ass (a Donkey), and decided to give the animal a test before buying him. He took the Ass home and put him in the field with his other Asses.
       </p>
       <p>
        The new Ass strayed from the others to join the one that was the laziest and the biggest eater of them all.
       </p>
       <p>
        Seeing this, the man led him back to his owner. When the owner asked how he could have tested the Ass in such a short time, the man answered, I didnt even need to see how he worked. I knew he would be just like the one he chose to be his friend.
       </p>
       <p>
        Here something else I learned along the way. I had some clients who although they were amazing people, they were draining the living hell out of me. I dreaded every session I had to do with them. It was not their fault; it was just a matter of bad energy exchange. Every time I trained them, I was drained for about an hour or two and the clients before and after didnt get my full attention. So I learned to refer those clients to my trainers or just refer them to another trainer, never with a bad intention, only because I knew they wouldnt get my full attention and professionalism.
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         2.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Compose your own Mantra.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Sometimes it is inevitable. Stress comes and hits us straight in the face. So what is left to do is cope with it. What I would suggest is to compose your own Mantra, a short and powerful phrase that will help you go back to a calmer self. This affirmation will help calm that self-critical voice that always speaks louder in those situations just to make matters worse. Just retire, go somewhere you wont be bothered, close your office door and sit back and try to chillax. Concentrate on your breathing, deep and long exhalations while you recite your Mantra phrase in your head a dozen of times. It always works.
        <a href="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/320058_206284669444675_127583850648091_495001_1770129620_n.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1842" height="198" src="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/320058_206284669444675_127583850648091_495001_1770129620_n-300x198.jpg" title="320058_206284669444675_127583850648091_495001_1770129620_n" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         3.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Eat like a warrior, not like a champion.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        How you start your day may be the single most important factor of how you will cope with stressful situation. Starting your day with a bowl of Wheaties the breakfast of champions, toast and a coffee is not what I call the breakfast of champion in my honest opinion. What it actually does is mess up your insulin levels throughout the day and will be one of the reasons your cortisol is going to be out of whack all day. You see, cortisol, which is the stress hormone is most of the time blunted by a spike of insulin. This is why most of you will eat junk or crave certain foods during periods of stress, to magically lower your cortisol levels. However, cortisol does not go down, you only put a band aid on it. Once you have given into your craving, then starts a great ride on the roller coaster to fatness.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/wheaties1.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1843" height="300" src="http://www.bodhifitonline.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/wheaties1-274x300.jpg" title="wheaties1" width="274"/>
        </a>
        What you need to do is try to control your insulin by avoiding high glycemic index foods, even during those stressful periods. Keep your protein and veggies intake a priority and cut down on processed and refined sugar. Start the day with a
        <a href="http://www.charlespoliquin.com/ArticlesMultimedia/Articles/Article/676/Meat_and_Nuts_Breakfast_A_Scientific_Update.aspx" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.charlespoliquin.com']);">
         meat and nuts breakfast
        </a>
        . Eating protein first thing in the morning will boost your metabolism and brain function by affecting positively neurotransmitters dopamine and acetylcholine. Why is it so important for stress management? The better your brain and your insulin, the better you will be in those situations. It is known as the fight or flight situation. You will be mentally ready to fight and mentally ready to attack (figure of speech). We are not fighting for our life like the caveman did against the Saber tooth tigers, which was a physical feat more than anything, even if it lasted only a few minutes. Now days, stress lasts weeks on end, nonstop, mentally and in some cases physically, a lot different than what it used to. Today, the corporate world is a matter of who gets the best ideas and who is the best talker, so being mentally and physically (health wise) ready is in my opinion the true weapon, an advantage in all situations. Did I mention that you will lose fat and gain muscle in the process?
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         4.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Depressed and/or stressed
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        I know some people will disagree but depression is self induced. It does not happen overnight, its a matter of years of bad habits, stress and lack of proper nutrients. Most people will suffer a great depression following a big shot or prolonged period of stress. Again, like our IBS and Crohns subjects, an accumulation of stress or very harsh life situations will beat up someone but will never lead to the same health concerns. If you manage your stress, you wont end up going on the depression road which once you are deep into it, it looks somewhat impossible to return to normal. Remember, what you do today will have an impact on how you will feel in the coming years.
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         5.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Cut down on Coffee
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        I know some will disagree, and even want to cut my head off, but coffee has an ugly head. First thing you need to ask yourself, do you really, absolutely, unconditionally need that huge mochachino with 4 sugars and a cream in the morning? Can you function without it? The problem is that if you absolutely need that shot of java in the morning is maybe hiding something else. You may have low morning cortisol and that shot of coffee is what you need to bring up your cortisol, temporarily. Unfortunately, you are only feeding the problem, without repairing it. It may be also a source of energy you may not need, which could be why you may be over reacting in stressful situations and make you look like a crazy S.O.B. at meetings. I love to see those people when they didnt have their morning dose of coffee; they are so out of it, completely vulnerable. People, who are observant, see this coming and use it to their advantage. Thats what I do, my way of teaching them a lesson.
       </p>
       <p>
        After I had a good laugh, on to fix the possible problem. Low morning cortisol is a matter of habits gone badly. Eating prior bed time, the use of energy drinks, hectic work hours or working on computers before bed time are always the prime suspects. I always try to change those bad habits first and see what happens. To go faster, I may use supplements such as licorice, Acetyl-L-carnitine, alpha GPC, or just simple water and seas salt in the morning. Everything that can help raise low morning cortisol naturally. In a matter if days, we always feel and SEE a difference. Body composition improves as well as energy levels.
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         6.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Learn to say no
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Instead of saying yes to every task that is given to you, do whatever you can to be the very best at a few things. Deadlines are a killer for one project; imagine a few, more so if you are a procrastinator. Trying to get everything done all at once is always a stress wreck. Focus on just a few things, say no to the obvious time wasters and be excellent at the tasks at hand.
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         7.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Stress management
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Stress management is very much different from one person to another. Some may need to unwind and go out with friends; others will go and kick their own ass with an intense workout while some will just need some time alone. Find you truly want and go do it. Anything that can help reset your brain and recharges your batteries. I personally suggest weight lifting, and precisely building lean muscle mass. Stress is the sworn enemy of muscle mass. Both dont work well together. However, the best way to counter the effects of stress is to train like a bodybuilder. More rest between sets, lower volume, and low recruitment of the nervous system which helps the adrenal regenerate, especially following extended periods of physical and mental stress.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres a little example of a video that I do for clients (this one is mine as an example, did it in about 5 minutes) which can help boosts someones confidence and is a great reminder of why we get up every morning and love what we do. This is only seven of many ways of staying on track even though you are going thru hard times. You can do something about it, everyone can.
       </p>
