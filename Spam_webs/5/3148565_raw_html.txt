      <div class="title" id="post-17204">
       <h1>
        <a href="http://naturemoms.com/blog/2015/02/23/health-wellness-enhancing-things-to-buy-with-your-tax-refund/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Health + Wellness Enhancing Things To Buy With Your Tax Refund">
         Health + Wellness Enhancing Things To Buy With Your Tax Refund
        </a>
       </h1>
      </div>
      <div class="details">
       
       <span>
        by
       </span>
       Tiffany
       <span>
        in
       </span>
       <a href="http://naturemoms.com/blog/category/frugal-green/" rel="category tag">
        Frugal Green
       </a>
      </div>
      <div id="triberr_endorsement">
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="http://naturemoms.com/blog/2015/02/23/health-wellness-enhancing-things-to-buy-with-your-tax-refund/healthy-tax-refund-ideas/" rel="attachment wp-att-17206">
        <img alt="healthy tax refund ideas" class="aligncenter wp-image-17206 size-large" height="369" src="http://naturemoms.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/healthy-tax-refund-ideas-520x369.png" style="border: 2px solid #808000;" width="520"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Okay, it is that time of year. Tax time! You may be thinking (or agonizing) about how you have to do your taxesor maybe you already have them done. Either way, if you are expecting a refund from the governmentthen you have already started dreaming about what you will do with it. Same here.
      </p>
      <p>
       Sowhat to do with themoola? Well, big or small there are probably a few purchases that you can make that will be beneficialin the long runhelping you a newlevel of wellness. I understand the tendency to want to blow the money on a trampoline for the kids or a down payment on a newer (but really unneeded car). Part of our personal refund is in fact allotted to purely fun stuff. Hello, DisneyWorld! But now is also the time to make smart purchases that will pay all year long and even for years to come. Investing in your future is always good. Here are a few ideas:
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Start Using Essential Oils
       </strong>
        I am a HUGE fan of essential oils and have been for about 20 years. Last year I started using
       <a href="http://naturemoms.com/blog/essential-oils/" target="_blank">
        Young Living essential oils
       </a>
       and fell in love all over again. I discovered quality that I never dreamed of and my family has been benefiting greatly. I use them every single day and I have not been sick in ages. Plus my three kids havent seen the inside of a doctors office in years. That is an amazing savings.
      </p>
      <p>
       Save about $40-$150 of that tax money, sign up as a
       <a href="http://naturemoms.com/blog/essential-oils/" target="_blank">
        wholesale member
       </a>
       (distributor) and get astarter kit. Plus as a wholesale member you get 24% off any future oil purchases and you can even make money when you help others get started with oils too. Thissmall investment can have a big impact on your life in multiple ways.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Join a Herdshare or CSA
       </strong>
        Health begins with healthy food but it can be hard to manage on a modest income. Use the extra cash from tax time to invest in healthy food for a big portion of the year. A CSA share requires a larger one time investment and then you get a bag/box of fresh fruits and veggies all throughout the growing season. A herdshare is a great way to buy grassfed or pastured meats in bulk and save money. Fill your freezer with a quarter cow and half pig and you will be set with healthy meats for a long time.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Grow Your Own Garden
       </strong>
        Take charge and start growing your own healthy, organic foods. Look into raised beds, a rain barrel, a compost bin, garden tools, heirloom seeds, and even some fruit bushes and trees. Once you get the garden going you will benefit for years to come.
      </p>
      <p>
       Alternatively you could also buy a plot at a community garden.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Take Cooking Classes
       </strong>
        If healthy eating and cooking is a struggle for you then take some classes! Learn how to cook from scratch, bake your own bread, can and preserve food from the garden and farmers market, and generally be more self sufficient in the kitchen.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Join a Gym or Hire a Personal Trainer
       </strong>
        With a little money in your pocket you might want to prepay for some CrossFit classes or sessions with a personal trainer. Investing in fitness is a win win.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        BuyA Bike 
       </strong>
       Bicycle riding is great exercise and it is great or the planet. Get a good bike and promise to pedal more instead of using your car.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Invest in Healthy Sleep
       </strong>
        A great green mattress is wonderful way to reduce toxins in your sleep environment and give you a health boost. If that is too much money then try a good wool mattress cover, some eco friendly pillows, and organic sheets.
      </p>
      <p>
       How will YOU use tax money to enhance your health?
      </p>
