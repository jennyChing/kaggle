        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Last year I wrote a
          <a href="http://www.addictivetips.com/windows-tips/create-a-quick-ad-hoc-wireless-network-connection-between-two-computers-in-windows-vista/" target="_blank">
           detailed guide
          </a>
          for creating a quick ad-hoc wireless connection between two computers in Windows Vista. The problem is that not everybody has wireless card installed in their computers. So this guide will focus on
          <strong>
           creating a Local Area Network(LAN) connection between two computers in both Windows Vista and XP
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          First go and buy the straight patch Ethernet LAN cable(make sure that patch or crossover is written on the cord) and connect them to both computers.
         </p>
         <div id="single-paragraph-add">
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           In Windows XP:
          </strong>
          Go to Network Connections Control Panel, right-click Local Area Network connection and select Properties. Now select Internet Protocol(TCP/IP) and click Properties.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           In Windows Vista:
          </strong>
          Go to Network and Sharing Center, select Manage Network Connections, right-click Local Area Network connection and select Properties. Now select Internet Protocol Version 4(TCP/IPv4) and click Properties.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-5000">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Now once you are inside the TCP/IP properties of your computer, click Use the following IP and enter 192.168.0.1 as IP address and 255.255.255.0 as subnet mask. Leave the other details blank and click OK.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Internet Protocol IP Properties" border="0" height="265" src="http://cloud.addictivetips.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/04/internetprotocolipproperties.jpg" style="border-right: 0px; border-top: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-left: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-bottom: 0px" title="Internet Protocol IP Properties" width="400"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Now go to TCP/IP properties of the second computer and enter the same details except the IP address, which should be 192.168.0.2 and click OK.
         </p>
         <!--Ad Injection:random-->
         <div style="float:left;">

         <br clear="all"/>
         <p>
          <img alt="Internet Protocol IP Properties 2" border="0" height="261" src="http://cloud.addictivetips.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/04/internetprotocolipproperties2.jpg" style="border-right: 0px; border-top: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-left: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-bottom: 0px" title="Internet Protocol IP Properties 2" width="408"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Now the networks are ready, you might need to restart both computers in some cases. If you are looking to share the Internet Connection between two computers, go to Local Area Network properties again and click on Sharing tab. Here you can select the enable internet connection sharing.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="local area connection internet sharing" border="0" height="226" src="http://cloud.addictivetips.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/04/localareaconnectioninternetsharing.jpg" style="border-right: 0px; border-top: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-left: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-bottom: 0px" title="local area connection internet sharing" width="367"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          To share files and folders between the two computers, go to the properties of that file/folder and select Sharing tab. Click Share to being sharing it on the network.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="sharing properties" border="0" height="309" src="http://cloud.addictivetips.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/04/sharingproperties.jpg" style="border-right: 0px; border-top: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-left: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-bottom: 0px" title="sharing properties" width="373"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Let me know if this works for you. Enjoy!
         </p>
