   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    High roller
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
     Jump to:
     <a href="#mw-head">
      navigation
     </a>
     ,
     <a href="#p-search">
      search
     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="hatnote">
      This article is about the gambling term.  For other uses, see
      <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/High_roller_(disambiguation)" title="High roller (disambiguation)">
       High roller (disambiguation)
      </a>
      .
     </div>
     <p>
      A
      <b>
       high roller
      </b>
      is a gambler who
      <a href="/wiki/Gambling" title="Gambling">
       wagers
      </a>
      large amounts of money. High rollers often receive lavish "
      <a href="/wiki/Comps_(casino)" title="Comps (casino)">
       comps
      </a>
      " from casinos to lure them onto the gambling floors, such as free
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Private_jet" title="Private jet">
       private jet
      </a>
      transfers,
      <a href="/wiki/Limousine" title="Limousine">
       limousine
      </a>
      use and use of the casinos' best
      <a href="/wiki/Suite_(hotel)" title="Suite (hotel)">
       suites
      </a>
      . Casinos may also extend credit to a player to continue betting,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-latimes-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      offer rebates on betting turnover or losses,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entitle_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-entitle-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and salaries of employees may also contain incentive arrangements to bring in high rollers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crown_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-crown-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The definition of a high roller varies. At
      <a href="/wiki/Crown_Casino_and_Entertainment_Complex" title="Crown Casino and Entertainment Complex">
       Crown Casino
      </a>
      in Australia, for example, it involves bringing between
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A$" title="A$">
       A$
      </a>
      50,000 and $75,000 to the table.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      High roller players often have very high
      <a href="/wiki/Table_limit" title="Table limit">
       table limits
      </a>
      allowing the high roller exclusive use. Casinos compete on bet limits. In Australia limits of A$300,000 are common, in
      <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Strip" title="Las Vegas Strip">
       Las Vegas
      </a>
      they are between US$150,000 and $300,000, and in
      <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">
       Macau
      </a>
      they are up to US$500,000. Only casinos with "substantial financial firepower" can accommodate high-stakes gambling due to the "volatility" of results.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entitle_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-entitle-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      High rollers may also be subject to exceptions from various rules and regulations; for example the high roller rooms at
      <a href="/wiki/Crown_Casino_and_Entertainment_Complex" title="Crown Casino and Entertainment Complex">
       Crown Casino
      </a>
      in Melbourne, Australia are the only licensed venue in the state not subject to a
      <a href="/wiki/Smoking_ban" title="Smoking ban">
       ban on smoking
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smoke_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-smoke-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      High rollers are said to provide only a small fraction of casino "action."
      <a href="/wiki/John_Eidsmoe" title="John Eidsmoe">
       John Eidsmoe
      </a>
      , in his book
      <i>
       Legalized Gambling: America's Bad Bet
      </i>
      , claims that it is actually gamblers from the lower and lower-middle classes in the United States that provide much of the gambling money. "The occasional wealthy 'high roller' does indeed exist, but he is the exception, not the standard. The fact that more than 50% of Nevada's gambling income comes from
      <a href="/wiki/Slot_machine" title="Slot machine">
       slot machines
      </a>
      as opposed to the card tables should be an indication high rollers are not the main source of revenue."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      One example of a high roller is an Australian man who turned over more than A$1.5 billion in a 14-month period from 2005, becoming "one of Crown's largest Australian players but not in the same league as [its] top international players".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crown_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-crown-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      There have been many cases around the world where high rollers have committed fraud to provide funds for gambling beyond their means, after becoming seduced by the lifestyle.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-latimes-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      While high rollers may not provide a significant portion of the revenues in the casino industry as a whole, they can have a major effect on the net income of casinos that cater to them. There are significant costs associated with attracting the highest stakes gamblers, so if a casino takes this chance and the patron wins, its expenses can be extraordinarily large. But if the casino's investment pays off and the high roller loses, the casino's gain can far exceed its expenses for the high roller's visit.
     </p>
