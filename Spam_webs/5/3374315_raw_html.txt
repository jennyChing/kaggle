    <h1>
     <i>
      Harpagophagus
     </i>
    </h1>
    <p align="left">
     <b>
      Name:
     </b>
     <i>
      Harpagophagus
     </i>
     .
     <br/>
     <b>
      Phonetic:
     </b>
     Har-poe-fag-us.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Named By:
     </b>
     L. de Bonis - 1971.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Classification:
     </b>
     Chordata, Mammalia, Carnivora,
Caniformia, Amphicyonidae.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Species:
     </b>
     <i>
      H. sanguinensis (type).
     </i>
     <br/>
     <b>
      Diet:
     </b>
     Carnivore.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Size:
     </b>
     Unavailable.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Known locations:
     </b>
     France.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Time period:
     </b>
     Rupelian to Chattian of the Oligocene.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Fossil representation:
     </b>
     Partial remains.
    </p>
    <p align="left">
     <i>
      Harpagophagus
     </i>
     is but one of many 
     <a href="http://www.prehistoric-wildlife.com/articles/bear-dogs-of-the-amphicyonidae.html">
      bear
dog
     </a>
      genera that lived in Europe during
the Oligocene.
