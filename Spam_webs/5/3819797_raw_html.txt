          <h3 class="entry-header">
           Eggnog Pancakes with Hot Eggnog Syrup
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://mixingbowlkids.typepad.com/.a/6a010536fe5b48970c017ee5bc6589970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Holiday pancakes" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a010536fe5b48970c017ee5bc6589970d" src="http://mixingbowlkids.typepad.com/.a/6a010536fe5b48970c017ee5bc6589970d-500wi" title="Holiday pancakes"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Advent starts this weekend, which, to me, marks
the beginning of the holidays. I've busted out the
             <a href="http://www.sheandhim.com/#/splash" target="_self">
              seasonal music
             </a>
             , baked
gingerbread cookies, andam planning a full day of gift-wrapping,
Christmas decorating and movie watching with Jackson this coming Sunday (the rest of
the family is away for hockey).
            </p>
            <p>
             I kicked off the holiday spirit by
making these eggnog pancakes earlier this week. I was working on an assignment
that required me to make cocktails with holiday beverages (eggnog, pomegranate
juice, etc.) and the leftover dairy was used in place of milk in our favourite breakfast recipe. While the pancake is tasty, its really the syrup that brings
out the flavour of the eggnog. Its hot and thick, not to mention sweet and
seasonal, and I have a feeling well be eating it on waffles and French toast,
too.
            </p>
            <p>
             PS - I should also mention that while Ben
             <em>
              loathes
             </em>
             eggnog, he happened to love these.
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              <strong>
               Eggnog Pancakes with Hot Eggnog Syrup
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              makes 8-12 pancakes
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              For the pancakes:
             </span>
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              1 cup all-purpose
             </li>
             <li>
              1 tablespoon sugar
             </li>
             <li>
              2 teaspoons baking powder
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 teaspoon fine salt
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 teaspoon grated nutmeg
             </li>
             <li>
              2 tablespoons melted butter + extra for the pan
             </li>
             <li>
              1 cup eggnog
             </li>
             <li>
              1 egg
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              For the syrup:
             </span>
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              4 tablespoons eggnog
             </li>
             <li>
              3 tablespoons pure maple syrup
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 teaspoon fresh grated nutmeg
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, cinnamon and nutmeg.
            </p>
            <p>
             In a separate bowl, beat together the melted butter, egg and eggnog. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry, and stir just until combined.
            </p>
            <p>
             Warm a skillet over medium heat and add a pat of butter. When melted, pour 1/4 cup batter for each pancake into the pan. Cook until the top forms bubbles that have burst, then flip them over. Repeat with remaining batter.
            </p>
            <p>
             While the pancakes are cooking, combine the syrup ingredients in a saucepan and bring to a boil. Lower the heat to a simmer, and cook until slightly thickened. Pour the hot syrup over the cooked pancakes and serve.
            </p>

             1/2 teaspoon fresh grated nutmeg
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, cinnamon and nutmeg.
           </p>
           <p>
            In a separate bowl, beat together the melted butter, egg and eggnog. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry, and stir just until combined.
           </p>
           <p>
            Warm a skillet over medium heat and add a pat of butter. When melted, pour 1/4 cup batter for each pancake into the pan. Cook until the top forms bubbles that have burst, then flip them over. Repeat with remaining batter.
           </p>
           <p>
            While the pancakes are cooking, combine the syrup ingredients in a saucepan and bring to a boil. Lower the heat to a simmer, and cook until slightly thickened. Pour the hot syrup over the cooked pancakes and serve.
           </p>
