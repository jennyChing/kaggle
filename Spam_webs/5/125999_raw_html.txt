     <p>
      <b>
       Pizzle
      </b>
      is an old English word for
      <a href="/wiki/Penis" title="Penis">
       penis
      </a>
      , derived from
      <a href="/wiki/Low_German" title="Low German">
       Low German
      </a>
      <i>
       pesel
      </i>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">
       Flemish Dutch
      </a>
      <i>
       pezel
      </i>
      , diminutive of
      <i>
       pees
      </i>
      , meaning 'sinew'.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The word is used today to signify the penis of an animal,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympics_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-olympics-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      chiefly in Australia and New Zealand.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <p>
      It is also known, at least since 1523, especially in the combination "bull pizzle", to denote a flogging instrument made from a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bull%27s_penis" title="Bull's penis">
       bull's penis
      </a>
       compare
      <a href="/wiki/Bullwhip" title="Bullwhip">

      .

     <p>
      Pizzles are represented in
      <a href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">
       heraldry
      </a>
      , where the adjective
      <b>
       pizzled
      </b>
      (or
      <b>
       vilen
      </b>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rietstap-1884_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-rietstap-1884-4">
        <span>

        </span>

        <span>

        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ) indicates that part of an animate
      <a href="/wiki/Charge_(heraldry)" title="Charge (heraldry)">
       charge
      </a>
      's anatomy, especially if coloured differently.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Animal_consumption">
       Animal consumption
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Animal consumption">

     </h3>
     <p>
      Pizzles, or Bully Sticks, are almost exclusively used/produced today as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chewing_treats" title="Chewing treats">
       chewing treats
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">
       dogs
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympics_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-olympics-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Glue">
       Glue
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Glue">
        edit
       </a>

       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The pizzle of a bull was commonly
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rendering_(animals)" title="Rendering (animals)">
       rendered
      </a>
      for use as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glue" title="Glue">
       glue
      </a>
      .

        <span>

        </span>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Human_consumption">
       Human consumption
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Human consumption">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote">
      See also:
      <a href="/wiki/Deer_penis" title="Deer penis">
       Deer penis
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tiger_penis" title="Tiger penis">
       tiger penis
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Tiger_penis_soup" title="Tiger penis soup">
       tiger penis soup
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      In addition to being used as a dog treat, pizzles are also eaten by humans for their health benefits such as being low in
      <a href="/wiki/Cholesterol" title="Cholesterol">
       cholesterol
      </a>
      and high in
      <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">
       protein
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hormone" title="Hormone">
       hormones
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Vitamin" title="Vitamin">
       vitamins
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dietary_mineral" title="Dietary mineral">
       minerals
      </a>
      such as
      <a href="/wiki/Calcium" title="Calcium">
       calcium
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Magnesium" title="Magnesium">
       magnesium
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympics_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-olympics-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Pizzles for human consumption are prepared either by freezing or by drying.
      <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">
       Scottish
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Deer_penis" title="Deer penis">
       deer pizzles
      </a>
      are thought to boost
      <a href="/wiki/Endurance" title="Endurance">
       stamina
      </a>
      and were used by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">
       Chinese
      </a>
      athletes at the
      <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">
       2008 Summer Olympics
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympics_2-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-olympics-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scot_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-scot-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Pizzles can be served in
      <a href="/wiki/Soup" title="Soup">
       soup
      </a>
      , and if they have been dried they can be turned into a paste. Pizzles may also be mixed with alcoholic beverages or simply thawed (if frozen) and eaten.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olympics_2-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-olympics-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In Jamaica, bull pizzles are referred to as "cow cods" and are eaten as
      <a href="/wiki/Cow_cod_soup" title="Cow cod soup">
       cow cod soup
      </a>
      . Like many pizzle-based foods, cow cod soup is claimed to be a male
      <a href="/wiki/Aphrodisiac" title="Aphrodisiac">
       aphrodisiac
      </a>
      .
     </p>
