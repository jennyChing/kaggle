      <h1 class="span12">
       How To: (Finally!) Stop Picking at Your Face
      </h1>
      <p>
       You know better than to pop that enormous pimpleitll likely exacerbate the problem and lead to scarring. But you cant help yourself. Again. And again. While skin picking varies in severity from the occasional fusser to a full-blown disorder, we can all benefit from a few professional tips. We sat down with psychologist Dr. Laura Curtis Feder of New Yorks
       <a class="track-event" href="http://www.clearclinic.com/">
        Clear Clinic
       </a>
       , a dedicated acne treatment center, to learn the techniques she uses to help her patients stop obsessing over their faces.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="row-fluid article-content">
      <h2>
       Identify Triggers
      </h2>
      <p>
       Often a specific placea particular mirror or a bathroom with bright lighting, for exampletriggers the urge to pick. Identify those places says Dr. Feder, and raise a mental flag or red alert when youre going into those spots to guard against engaging in the behavior. If completely avoiding that space isnt realistic, Dr. Feder suggests setting a time limit for how long youre in there and visualizing something in the mirror that will make you walk away, like a giant red stop sign.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Check In
      </h2>
      <p>
       When youre feeling the urge to pick, check in with yourself to examine the thoughts and feelings that are driving that urge. Is it anxiety? Stress? Boredom? Try to identify the underlying reason behind your picking habit, then consider how to address the root problem.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Occupy Your Hands
      </h2>
      <p>
       If your hands are otherwise occupied, they cant be picking at blemishes. Some easy recommendations: playing a game on your phone, typing out emails, or giving yourself a manicure.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Take A Photo
      </h2>
      <p>
       If you cant stop obsessing, try taking a picture of the pimple and saying to yourself,
       <em>
        If it still bothers me in 24 hours, then I can touch it
       </em>
       . Often what we see is that picking is an instant gratification, impulse thing, say Dr. Feder. The person is unable to wait, even though they know that the damage theyre doing to themselves is so much worse than if they were to just wait for a day for the pimple to resolve itself or for medicine to kick in.
      </p>
