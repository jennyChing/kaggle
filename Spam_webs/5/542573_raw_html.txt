      <h1>
       Earth Day Recycle Craft: Tin Can Wind Chimes
      </h1>
      <p class="catlist">
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/category/crafts">
        crafts
       </a>
       
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/category/crafts/kids-crafts-crafts">
        kids crafts
       </a>
      </p>
      <p class="byline">
       by Rachel Lewis on April 22nd, 2013 |
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/earth-day-recycle-craft-tin-can-wind-chimes#respond">
        No Comments 
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Want a fun
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/earth-day-gummy-worm-dirt-pudding-cups">
        Earth Day craft
       </a>
       to do with your kids? What a better way to teach them about Earth Day than how to recycle? Making tin can wind chimes can be fun and
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/party-ideas-how-to-throw-an-eco-friendly-party">
        good for theenvironment
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       First, be sure to choose three or four cans that vary in size. You will want the cans to be able tosequentiallyfit inside of each other. Then rinse them out and peel off the outside label. This may require hot water and a little scrubbing.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1586.jpg">
        <img alt="tin can wind chimes 1" class=" wp-image-72023 aligncenter" height="432" src="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1586-1024x768.jpg" width="577"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Next, take a hammer and a nail and punch holes in the top of the cans. Make sure the hole is as centered as possible and to use a nail wide enough for a piece of yarn to fit through.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1590.jpg">
        <img alt="tin can wind chimes 2" class=" wp-image-72024 aligncenter" height="438" src="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1590-1024x768.jpg" width="583"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Now, get out the paint brushes andacrylicpaint!Your wind chimes will be outside, so acrylicpaint will is perfect for this project because it is weather resistant and permanent.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1592.jpg">
        <img alt="tin can wind chimes 4" class=" wp-image-72026 aligncenter" height="442" src="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1592-1024x768.jpg" width="590"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Then let your kids go to work! Feel free to make a tin can wind chime of your own! Once everyone is done decorating it takes at least 30 minutes for the paint to dry.Make sure all cans are dry before proceeding!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1594.jpg">
        <img alt="tin can wind chimes 5" class=" wp-image-72027 aligncenter" height="442" src="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1594-1024x768.jpg" width="590"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Take a durable piece of yarn and thread it through the smallest can. If it is difficult to thread through, pull the yarn with tweezers through the hole so you can get a more secure grasp.
      </p>
      <p>
       Before you thread the second can tie a large knot in the yarn. Make sure to measure it out so that when you thread the next can through there is the amount of space you wantin betweenthe cans (I overlapped around an inch).
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1597.jpg">
        <img alt="tin can wind chimes 6" class=" wp-image-72028 aligncenter" height="430" src="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1597-1024x768.jpg" width="574"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Continue to do this for as many cans as you prepared, and then hang your tin can wind chime from your porch or a tree!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1603.jpg">
        <img alt="tin can wind chimes 7" class=" wp-image-72029 aligncenter" height="430" src="http://momitforward.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/SAM_1603-1024x768.jpg" width="574"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

       </p>
