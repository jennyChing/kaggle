        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Gluten-Free Kimchi Quesadillas
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-05-11T10:00:20+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          May 11, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://momstart.com/author/catherine/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Catherine
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://momstart.com/2015/05/gluten-free-kimchi-quesadillas/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img alt="kimchiquesadillas5" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61451" height="757" src="http://momstart.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/kimchiquesadillas5.jpg" width="485"/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sometimes, you just need a snack. These kimchi quesadillas are gluten-free, probiotic, and can be made vegetarian.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ingredients
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          4 oz sharp cheddar
         </p>
         <p>
          4 large corn tortillas (8 if using small tortillas)
         </p>
         <p>
          1 cup kimchi
         </p>
         <p>
          2 oz protein (eg ground beef, shredded chicken, breakfast sausage)
         </p>
         <p>
          Sriracha
         </p>
         <p>
          Light mayonnaise
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img alt="kimchiquesadillas" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61448" height="476" src="http://momstart.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/kimchiquesadillas.jpg" width="679"/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Prepare the meat. I used a breakfast sausage, which I needed to cook 4 minutes per side; while doing so, drain kimchi and arrange on one side of your tortillas.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img alt="kimchiquesadillas2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61449" height="487" src="http://momstart.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/kimchiquesadillas2.jpg" width="864"/>
        </center>
        <p>
         2. Once the meat is cooked, layer it on the kimchi with your cheese slices.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img alt="kimchiquesadillas3" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61450" height="487" src="http://momstart.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/kimchiquesadillas3.jpg" width="864"/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. On your other tortillas, spreada thin layer of Sriracha and mayo.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img alt="kimchiquesadillas4" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61452" height="487" src="http://momstart.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/kimchiquesadillas4.jpg" width="864"/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Cook 5 minutes per side, until cheese is melted and bubbling. Top with salsa, avocado, or hot sauce to taste. Serves 2 for an appetizer, or 1 if a meal.
        </p>
