          <h1 class="title single-title">
           Sleeping Heals Mind and Health
          </h1>
          <span class="single-postmeta">
           Posted by
           <a href="http://www.renewpsyche.com/author/admin/" rel="nofollow" title="Posts by Admin">
            Admin
           </a>
           in
           <a href="http://www.renewpsyche.com/category/health/" rel="nofollow">
            Health
           </a>
           On February 22, 2015
          </span>
         </header>
         <!--.headline_area-->
         <div class="post-single-content box mark-links">
          <h3>
           <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.renewpsyche.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/sleepingwoman-e1424566629495.jpeg">
            <img alt="sleepingwomanrenewpsyche" class=" wp-image-60 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.renewpsyche.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/sleepingwoman-e1424566629495.jpeg?resize=631%2C287"/>
           </a>
          </h3>
          <h3>
           Early studies have linked lack of sleep to both colorectal and aggressive breast cancers.
          </h3>
          <p>
           Multiple studies have suggested a relationship between sleep deprivation and increased obesity risk.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Stroke risk quadruples
           </li>
           <li>
            Obesity risk jumps
           </li>
           <li>
            Risk of some cancers may increase
           </li>
           <li>
            Diabetes risk goes up
           </li>
           <li>
            Heart disease risk increases
           </li>
           <li>
            Sperm count decreases
           </li>
           <li>
            Risk of death goes up
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           A sleep study evaluating 1,741 men and women over the course of 10 to 14 years found that men who slept fewer than six hours had a significant increase in mortality risk, even after adjusting for diabetes.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h4>
           Research has linked short term sleep deprivation with a propensity to load up bigger portions. A preference for high-calorie, high-carb foods and a greater likelihood of choosing unhealthy foods while grocery shopping.
          </h4>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Hungrier and apt to eat more.
           </li>
           <li>
            More likely to have an accident.
           </li>
           <li>
            Not looking your best  or your most approachable
           </li>
           <li>
            More likely to catch a cold
           </li>
           <li>
            Losing brain tissue
           </li>
           <li>
            More likely to get emotional
           </li>
           <li>
            Less focused and having memory problems
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           A small, recent study of 15 men, published in the journal SLEEP, found that just one night of sleep deprivation was linked with signs of brain tissue loss.
          </p>
