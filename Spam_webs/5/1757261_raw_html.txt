             <h3 class="post-title">
              <a href="http://www.thethinkingblog.com/2007/06/weirdest-named-places-on-earth.html">
               Weirdest Named Places on Earth
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body" id="post-1082347264359868772">
              <style>
               #fullpost{display:inline;}
              </style>
              <p>
              </p>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5078285194937937730" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_Di1HzTLtBlM/Rnmxephmr0I/AAAAAAAABQQ/VndNiovn-XQ/s400/fucking.jpg" style="margin: 0pt 10px 10px 0pt; float: left;"/>
               <b>
                Welcome to Fucking, Austria. Please be safe and first go to Wedding, Germany!
               </b>
               If you thought that was awkward, then wait till you read what's coming up. All places have historical origins, but there are places with names that are weird, funny and not appealing to its English meaning. Regardless the words have no etymological connection to the meaning in English. There are so many of them, it's strange that a "Worst Named Place" contest doesn't exist.
               <br/>
              </div>
              <span id="fullpost">
               <div style="text-align: justify;">
                <br/>
                Among the places that top the list of places with weird English words, there are
                <i>
                 kind
                </i>
                words like:
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <b>
                </b>
                <blockquote>
                 <b>
                  Nice
                 </b>
                 in Provence-Alpes-Cote-d'Azure,
                 <b>
                  Kissing
                 </b>
                 in Germany,
                 <b>
                  Cool
                 </b>
                 in California,
                 <b>
                  Beclean
                 </b>
                 in Romania,
                 <b>
                  Good
                 </b>
                 in West Virginia-USA,
                 <b>
                  Smile
                 </b>
                 in Kentucky and one of the most common word for a town,
                 <b>
                  Paradise
                 </b>
                 in United States (California, Michigan, Kansas, Kentucky, Montana, Texas, Utah, Nevada, Pennsylvania and Washington) and also in England, Canada, Australia and New Zealand. Since earth has a balance of good and evil; if a place is named Paradise the opposite can also be found. Yes,
                 <b>
                  Hell
                 </b>
                 is also on Earth, with three places named as Hell. One in Grand Cayman, one in Michigan and another in Norway. Looks like good wins this time!
                </blockquote>
                <br/>
                Few places are named with
                <i>
                 nasty
                </i>
                words such that you feel dirty after reading them, let alone living there:
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <b>
                </b>
                <blockquote>
                 <b>
                  Bitche
                 </b>
                 ,
                 <b>
                  Pussy
                 </b>
                 , and
                 <b>
                  Die
                 </b>
                 in France (also
                 <b>
                  Condom
                 </b>
                 in Gers-France),
                 <b>
                  Fake
                 </b>
                 in Nigeria,
                 <b>
                  Horneytown
                 </b>
                 in North Carolina,
                 <b>
                  Fucking
                 </b>
                 and
                 <b>
                  Kicking
                 </b>
                 in Austria,
                 <b>
                  Intercourse
                 </b>
                 in Pennsylvania,
                 <b>
                  Cockburn
                 </b>
                 in Australia,
                 <b>
                  Cockup
                 </b>
                 in England (also
                 <b>
                  Cockermouth
                 </b>
                 in England),
                 <b>
                  Bangkok
                 </b>
                 in Thailand,
                 <b>
                  Dick
                 </b>
                 in Mozambique,
                 <b>
                  Toad Suck
                 </b>
                 in Arkansas,
                 <b>
                  Dildo
                 </b>
                 in Newfoundland,
                 <b>
                  Muff
                 </b>
                 in Ireland,
                 <b>
                  Titty Hill
                 </b>
                 in England,
                 <b>
                  Kinki
                 </b>
                 in Japan,
                 <b>
                  Thong
                 </b>
                 in England,
                 <b>
                  Hookersville
                 </b>
                 in West Virginia,
                 <b>
                  Sexmoan
                 </b>
                 in the Philippines,
                 <b>
                  Tittmoning
                 </b>
                 in Germany,
                 <b>
                  Assawoman
                 </b>
                 in Virginia,
                 <b>
                  Middelfart
                 </b>
                 in Denmark,
                 <b>
                  Rectum
                 </b>
                 in Netherlands,
                 <b>
                  Shitterton
                 </b>
                 in England,
                 <b>
                  Shit
                 </b>
                 in Iran,
                 <b>
                  Rottenegg
                 </b>
                 and
                 <b>
                  Worm
                 </b>
                 in Germany. Then there are four places called
                 <b>
                  Moron
                 </b>
                 in Sweden, Mongolia, Argentina, and Cuba. How about we finish with
                 <b>
                  Climax
                 </b>
                 in Saskatchewan province, Canada? or:
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 -
                 <b>
                  Climax
                 </b>
                 , North Carolina
                 <br/>
                 -
                 <b>
                  Climax
                 </b>
                 , Colorado
                 <br/>
                 -
                 <b>
                  Climax
                 </b>
                 , Georgia
                 <br/>
                 -
                 <b>
                  Climax
                 </b>
                 , Kansas
                 <br/>
                 -
                 <b>
                  Climax
                 </b>
                 , Michigan
                 <br/>
                 -
                 <b>
                  Climax
                 </b>
                 , Minnesota
                 <br/>
                 -
                 <b>
                  Climax Township
                 </b>
                 , Michigan
                 <br/>
                 -
                 <b>
                  Climax Springs
                 </b>
                 , Missouri
                </blockquote>
                <br/>
                Other names might simply mislead unfamiliar people in places called:
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <b>
                </b>
                <blockquote>
                 <b>
                  America
                 </b>
                 in Netherlands (spelled Amerika in Dutch),
                 <b>
                  La France
                 </b>
                 in South Carolina,
                 <b>
                  Canada
                 </b>
                 in Kentucky and
                 <b>
                  California
                 </b>
                 in Pennsylvania. Not forgotten are heavenly body names like
                 <b>
                  Mars
                 </b>
                 and
                 <b>
                  Moon Township
                 </b>
                 in Pennsylvania and Mercury in Nevada.
                </blockquote>
                <br/>
                <b>
                 Here is the best part of it all
                </b>
                . How lucky is it to be named after a famous super hero;
                <b>
                 Batman
                </b>
                ! He who we know is in Gotham city, is also named for a real city in Turkey. There is even a
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batman_River">
                 Batman River
                </a>
                !
