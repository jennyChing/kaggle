       <p>
        What do you do if you get lost in the woodlands in Iceland?
       </p>
       <p>
        If youve been to Iceland, youve heard this joke multiple times. Its a favourite among tour guides, meaning it must be a pretty big hit with tourists, despite its overuse.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you havent heard it, and you cant hazard a guess, you can always stick around til the end of the post.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-6220">
        </span>
       </p>
       <h3>
        10 Quick Facts About Iceland
       </h3>
       <p>
        <strong>
         1. Iceland is themost
         <a href="http://reneweconomy.com.au/2012/iceland-a-100-renewables-example-in-the-modern-era-56428">
          environmentally friendly
         </a>
         countries in Europe.
        </strong>
        Its also one of the happiest andhas the lowest population density.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         2. The most famous restaurant in the country is a
         <a href="http://www.tripadvisor.ca/Restaurant_Review-g189970-d1852122-Reviews-Baejarins_Beztu_Pylsur-Reykjavik_Capital_Region.html">
          hot dog stand
         </a>
         .
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         3. Dior imports Icelandic water to use in its creams.
        </strong>
        The tap water is pure and filtered naturally, with nothing added.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="water" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6309" height="450" src="http://marieaway.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/water.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         4.
         <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/magazine-24399599">
          One in ten Icelanders is a published author
         </a>
         .
        </strong>
        Reykjavik has won an award for the most cultural city in Europe due to have the most writers and books published per capita.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         5. Icelands first day of summer is the first Thursday after April 18.
        </strong>
        It is a public holiday and a day to be nice to children. If you fail to treat children well on this day, you have a bad summer ahead of you.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         6. Icelanders use their lands geothermal properties to their advantage.
        </strong>
        Aside from geothermal pools, they use the hot water to heat homes. In downtown Reykjavik, hot water pipes below the streets heat up the road and
        <a href="http://www.nea.is/geothermal/direct-utilization/snow-melting/">
         melt the snow
        </a>
        in the winter. Who needs snow clearing?
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="DT" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6303" height="470" src="http://marieaway.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DT.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         7. The houses in Iceland are built to withstand earthquakes
        </strong>
        , especially in areas that straddle both tectonic plates. Built from cement and steel, the US Army considers the houses to be bomb shelters.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="10881959_1622333731335636_1020281273_n" class=" size-full wp-image-6221 aligncenter" height="640" src="http://marieaway.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/10881959_1622333731335636_1020281273_n.jpg" width="640"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        8
        <strong>
         .Icelanders are proud of their horses, which have been bred in the country for 1000 years and are incredibly pure.
        </strong>
        They are the only horses that can run through lava fields. If a horse leaves the country, its not allowed back in. Seriously.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="horses" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6308" height="449" src="http://marieaway.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/horses.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         9. Icelanders actually have Celtic ancestry in addition to their Nordic genetics.
        </strong>
        Why? Vikings would stop over at the British Isles on their way to Iceland and pick up the best-looking women they could find to bring with them.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         10. On average there is one murder in the country per year.
        </strong>
        Crime rate is low, but there are still criminals. Each year, around 139 men and six women go to prison long term.
       </p>
       <h4>
        <em>
         So, what do you do if you get lost in the woodlands in Iceland?
        </em>
       </h4>
       <h4>
        <strong>
         Stand up.
        </strong>
       </h4>
