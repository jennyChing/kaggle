         <div class="entry">
          <p>
           A Texas judge ruled Tuesday that Microsoft cannot sell one of its flagship products, Word, in the United States because of patent infringement.
          </p>
          <div class="widget ad_position widget-ad-position-A300 widget-fake">
           <div class="hdn-ad-position ad-A300 hdn-ap-visible-mobile">
            <div class="juice-ad-position juice-ad-position-A300">
             <script id="A300-0-91789800-1436913923" type="text/javascript">
              window.ad_position_widget.place_named_ad('A300', 'A300-0-91789800-1436913923');
             </script>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           <img src="http://blog.seattlepi.com/microsoft/files/library/word_logo.jpg" style="float: right; margin-left: 10px;"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           You read that right: Microsoft cannot sell Word, the judge ruled.
          </p>
          <p>
           Judge Leonard Davis, of the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Texas, ordered a permanent injunction that prohibits Microsoft from selling or importing to the United States any Microsoft Word products that have the capability of opening .XML, .DOCX or DOCM files (XML files) containing custom XML, according to
           <a href="http://media.prnewswire.com/en/jsp/latest.jsp?resourceid=4041905&amp;access=EH">
            an announcement
           </a>
           by the plaintiff, Toronto-based
           <a href="http://www.i4i.com/">
            i4i
           </a>
           Inc.
          </p>
          <p>
           XML is a markup language that allows users to customize the underlying format of their word-processing documents, for example, and makes them readable across different word-processing programs. The ability to read and write XML documents is an integral feature of Microsoft Word.
          </p>
          <div class="caption" style="width: 150px; margin-bottom: 18px; border-bottom: 1px solid #999; padding-bottom: 6px; float: left; margin-right: 10px;">
           <a href="/microsoft/files/library/20090811i4iinjunction.pdf" target="_blank">
            <img alt="Photo" border="0" src="http://blog.seattlepi.com/microsoft/files/library/20090811i4iinjunction.jpg" width="150"/>
           </a>
           <div class="credit" style="margin-top: 2px; text-align: right;">
           </div>
           <p>
            Download the injunction (
            <a href="/microsoft/files/library/20090811i4iinjunction.pdf" target="_blank">
             PDF
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           In its complaint (
           <a href="/microsoft/files/library/20090811i4icomplaint.pdf" target="_blank">
            PDF
           </a>
           ), i4i alleges Microsoft willingly violated its 1998 patent (No. 5,787,449) on a method for reading XML. The company, whose Web site
           <a href="http://www.i4i.com/solutions/Edit_XML.htm">
            advertises
           </a>
           that users can Create and edit XML content in Microsoft Word, helps clients work with XML.
          </p>
          <p>
           i4i filed the lawsuit in March 2007, seeking an injunction and damages. The Eastern District of Texas is known for being a haven for patent litigation.
          </p>
          <p>
           The injunction (
           <a href="/microsoft/files/library/20090811i4iinjunction.pdf" target="_blank">
            PDF
           </a>
           ), which becomes effective in 60 days, prohibits Microsoft from selling future Word products that allegedly use the patented technology. It also enjoins Microsoft from testing, demonstrating, marketing or offering support for those future products.
          </p>
          <p>
           Davis also ordered Microsoft to pay i4i more than $290 million in damages.
          </p>
          <p>
           We are disappointed by the courts ruling, Microsoft spokesman Kevin Kutz said in a statement. We believe the evidence clearly demonstrated that we do not infringe and that the i4i patent is invalid. We will appeal the verdict.
          </p>
          <p>
           Indeed, Microsoft will likely do everything in its power to overturn Tuesdays ruling. And indeed, Microsoft will likely prevail.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Updated 3:10 p.m.:

           Clarified XML as a markup language.
          </p>
