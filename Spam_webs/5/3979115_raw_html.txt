         <img alt="Mother of three Gemma Moss died of a heart attack after smoking cannabis to help her sleep" src="http://cdn.images.express.co.uk/img/dynamic/1/590x/gemma-moss-457104.jpg" title="Mother-of-three Gemma Moss died of a heart attack after smoking cannabis to help her sleep"/>

       <section class="text-description">
        <p class="storycopy">
         Churchgoer Gemma Moss, 31, had a heart attack soon after a late-night smoke.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Tests showed the cardiac arrest was triggered by cannabis toxicity  and a coroner ruled that her death was down to cannabis abuse.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         The inquest was told that she was found dead in her bedroom in Boscombe, Bournemouth, last October by her teenage sons girlfriend.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         There was a partially smoked joint and a bag of cannabis nearby.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Her mother, Kim Furness, told the hearing Miss Moss had stopped smoking cannabis for two years before her death.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         But she started taking the drug again to help her to sleep and fight depression after a split with her boyfriend.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         It was one half of a joint to get to sleep, said Ms Furness. She never smoked in the day.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Where we come from its normal to smoke cannabis. She wasnt smoking excessively. 
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Friends told the Bournemouth inquest that Miss Moss  a member of the local Citygate Church  spent up to 60 a week on the drug.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Tests revealed no problems with any organs, including her heart. But pathologist Dr Kudair Hussein found moderate to heavy levels of cannabinoids in her blood.
        </p>
       </section>
       <section class="pull-quote on-right">
        <blockquote class="nointellitxt ctx_blocked" id="nointellitxt">
         <p class="quote">
          There are reports which say cannabis can be considered as a cause of death because it can induce a cardiac arrest
         </p>
         <p class="author">
          Sheriff Payne
         </p>
        </blockquote>
       </section>
       <section class="text-description">
        <p class="storycopy">
         He said: There are reports which say cannabis can be considered as a cause of death because it can induce a cardiac arrest.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Recording a verdict that Miss Moss died from drug abuse, coroner Sheriff Payne said: With the balance of probability it is more likely than not that she died from the effects of cannabis.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Miss Moss is thought to be the first woman in Britain to die as a direct result of poisoning from the illegal Class B drug. The only other known case was in 2004 when Lee Maisey, 36, from Pembrokeshire died after smoking an estimated 25,000 joints over 11 years.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         David Raynes, of the National Drug Prevention Alliance, said: It is extremely unusual for a coroner to rule death from cannabis abuse.In 40 years I have never come across deaths from cannabis alone. This case serves as a warning that cannabis can cause immense harm.
