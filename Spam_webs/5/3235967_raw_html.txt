     <h1 class="post-title mob-marg-b-25 med-marg-b-50 big-marg-b-50" data-js="postTitle" itemprop="name" tabindex="0">
      Tech Time Warp of the Week: Watch Steve Jobs Pitch Apples Spaceship Headquarters
     </h1>
    </header>
    <section class="post-container clearfix relative" data-js="post" id="start-of-content" tabindex="0">
     <article class="content link-underline relative body-copy" data-js="content" itemprop="articleBody">
      <p>
       <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="495" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/meVQqYNGzYA" width="660">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <p>
       Steve Jobs knew how to work a crowd. He knew how to convince people that Apples vision for
       <a href="http://www.wired.com/2014/01/tech-time-warp-jobs-1980/" target="_blank">
        the future
       </a>
       was exactly what they needed. He did it first with the Mac, and later with
       <a href="http://www.wired.com/2011/10/steve-jobs-greatest-achievements/?viewall=true" target="_blank">
        the iPod, the iPhone, and the iPad
       </a>
       . It was a skill he used to the very end.
      </p>
      <p>
       One of his last wins for Apple wasnt launching a blockbuster product, but helping to cement the companys future in Cupertino, California, the city where Apple was born and whose name became synonymous with the company. It was a project he nursed for at least five years (see videos below).
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2006the year Apple turned 30he attended a town meeting to tell the city council about Apples expansion plan. In typical Steve fashion, he laid on the charm. He told them he wanted to stay in Cupertino, even if it was much more costly than moving. The pitch certainly wooed the council, but for a while, it didnt look like his mission would succeed.  Then Apple found a way: It bought up nine properties totaling 50 acres just down the street from One Infinite Loop.
      </p>
      <p>
       Five years after his first presentation, Jobs took the stage again. Now looking rather frail, hestepped up to the podium, wearing what looks like same outfit, to pitch the city governmenton
       <a href="http://www.wired.com/2013/11/a-glimpse-into-apples-crazy-new-spaceship-headquarters/?viewall=true" target="_blank">
        Apples spaceship campus
       </a>
       ,a 175-acre development the company wanted to build on Hewlett-Packards old stomping grounds. It would be his
       <a href="http://www.businessweek.com/articles/2013-04-04/apples-campus-2-shapes-up-as-an-investor-relations-nightmare" target="_blank">
        last public appearance
       </a>
       . He died of a
       <a href="http://www.wired.com/2011/10/steve-jobs-1955-2011/all/" target="_blank">
        rare cancer in October 2011, seven years after he was diagnosed
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       He looks visibly tired at points, but the 20-minute presentationwhich you can also see belowis still classic Steve. He pokes fun at the poorly designed buildings Apple has had to rent out for its ever expanding workforce, a joke hed made five years earlier. And he speaks in hyperbole, calling the all-glass centerpiece of the project the best office building in the world.
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="495" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Nf6C5Uc3ix4" width="660">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <p>
       To keep up with its growing operations, Jobs says, Apple
       <a href="http://www.wired.com/2011/06/cupertino-jobs-ufo-building/" target="_blank">
        wants to build a one-of-a-kind facility
       </a>
       that includes a2.8 million square foot circular office space,a 1,000-seat auditorium, a fitness center, cafeterias, parking and transit structures, and research facilities. All this will be nestled in a well-manicured, manmade forest sprinkled with apple, plum, cherry and apricot trees.
      </p>
      <p>
       He sees it as an embodiment of the companys culture of design-driven innovation, and a nod to the way the Santa Clara Valley looked when he was a kid. Even the choice of location was symbolic,as it was at HP that Jobs got his start in the tech industry at 13. Weve seen these office parks with lots of buildings, and they get pretty boring, pretty fast, Jobs says. So wed like to do something better than that.
      </p>
      <p>
       Two years after the presentation, were still waiting for this new-age campus. But it will happen. Its just a shame that Steve Jobs wont see it.
      </p>
