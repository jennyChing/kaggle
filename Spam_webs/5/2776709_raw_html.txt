      <article class="entry clearfix">
       <h3>
        Think Mobile Content First
       </h3>
       <p>
        Customer expectations have undeniably changed in todays mobile era. Offering readers content that is easy to read and fun to explore is crucial when it comes to meeting customers needs. Mobile phones and tablets are rapidly becoming the platform of preference for content discovery and consumption.
       </p>
       <p>
        According to a
        <a href="http://pewinternet.org/Commentary/2012/February/Pew-Internet-Mobile.aspx" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://pewinternet.org/Commentary/2012/February/Pew-Internet-Mobile.aspx', 'Pew Research study');" target="_blank">
         Pew Research study
        </a>
        , more than 50% of American adults own either a tablet or a smartphone. And
        <a href="http://money.msn.com/top-stocks/post.aspx?post=b6a81d77-f122-4548-9d47-5dd739cf4780" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://money.msn.com/top-stocks/post.aspx?post=b6a81d77-f122-4548-9d47-5dd739cf4780', 'according to IDC');" target="_blank">
         according to IDC
        </a>
        , smart device sales will grow 174% between 2012 and 2017.
       </p>
       <p>
        Additionally, the
        <a href="http://www.online-publishers.org/index.php/opa_news/press_release/opa_study_defines_todays_smartphone_user" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.online-publishers.org/index.php/opa_news/press_release/opa_study_defines_todays_smartphone_user', 'Online Publishers Association');" target="_blank">
         Online Publishers Association
        </a>
        reports smartphone owners arent just playing Angry Birds: Theyre some of the most avid consumers of digital content  like news, magazines and email. To this last point, SEO expert Michael Truby thinks that mobile marketing is
        <a href="http://sparksheet.com/is-mobile-all-about-email/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://sparksheet.com/is-mobile-all-about-email/', 'still all about email');" target="_blank">
         still all about email
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        What does this mean for you?
       </p>
       <blockquote class="tr_bq">
        <p>
         <i>
          Simply put, your customers  or prospective customers  are probably accessing your content, particularly your magazines and newsletters, on mobile devices as well. Its time to make sure your customers have a great experience on them too.
         </i>
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        The good news is The Telegraph (who offer an adapted tablet experience), have found readers are
        <a href="http://tabtimes.com/feature/deployment-strategy/2012/01/06/telegraph-how-revered-newspaper-ventured-world-tablet" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://tabtimes.com/feature/deployment-strategy/2012/01/06/telegraph-how-revered-newspaper-ventured-world-tablet', 'eight times more deeply engaged');" target="_blank">
         eight times more deeply engaged
        </a>
        with their mobile content than they are on a desktop. Additionally, Pew Research found that about twice as many people (about 21%) read in-depth articles on their tablet than they do other devices.
       </p>
       <p>
        The question is: How can you increase the reach of your content with mobile customers?
       </p>
       <p>
        Here are some tips for your magazines and newsletters:
       </p>
       <h3>
        Tip 1: Adapt the Reading Experience for Mobile and Tablets
       </h3>
       <p>
        Make sure your content is easy to navigate and read. Mobile devices are different than desktops, so its important that your content is designed with those differences in mind: Larger font sizes, easy-to-navigate layouts and simplicity are all important factors to consider if you want to make your content more readable.
       </p>
       <p>
        Its impossible to overstate how important this is. You might think that making sure everything looks beautiful on a smartphone or a tablet isnt the most important thing you could be spending time on, but The Economist has claimed tablets have created a 
        <a href="http://tabtimes.com/news/media/2011/12/16/economist-says-tablets-are-responsible-rebirth-reading" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://tabtimes.com/news/media/2011/12/16/economist-says-tablets-are-responsible-rebirth-reading', 'rebirth of reading');" target="_blank">
         rebirth of reading
        </a>
        . If your content isnt readable on a tablet or a phone, its worth asking where you expect people to read it.
       </p>

       <h3>
        Tip 2: Remove the barriers
       </h3>
       <p>
        If you have been involved with User Interface design, youve definitely come to appreciate the effort required to get your audience involved. Professionals in the arena are obsessively trying to remove the unnecessary design elements that keep your customers from reaching their intended goals.
       </p>
       <p>
        You simply want to make it easy to do business with you, and that starts with access to your content.
       </p>
       <p>
        This is why native apps are likely not the best platform to use for newsletters, or any form of marketing or corporate communications content. Native apps require downloads, installation, and updates. Nobody has researched the drop-off during this process, but its likely considerable.
       </p>
       <p>
        Another reason why native apps are less suitable for content marketing is the convoluted discovery process. By definition, apps limited to specific stores. As those marketplaces become increasingly crowded, it gets harder and harder to get noticed by even the savviest of consumers.
       </p>
       <p>
        Beyond that, most content apps offer whats basically a glorified print experience. Theyre difficult to use, and because theyre so bloated, their large downloads often fill up users hard drives and consume most of their mobile data.
       </p>
       <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">

       <p>
        Getting noticed is not going to get easier.
       </p>
       <p>
        A good content strategy includes an adapted content for a strong mobile presence. Content marketing  and thus content production  is
        <a href="http://contentmarketinginstitute.com/2013/04/enterprise-2013-content-marketing-research/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://contentmarketinginstitute.com/2013/04/enterprise-2013-content-marketing-research/', 'headed for a 46% increase');" target="_blank">
         headed for a 46% increase
        </a>
        so it is going to get more and more crowded. With all the effort, investment and energy going into content gathering and creation, it would be a waste not to make the cut and be ignored because of a lack of investment in a proper publishing platform.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Tip 3: Create a Habit
       </h3>
       <p>
        You might want to consider consistently publishing content at the same time. Make updates predictable, and notify your audience when new content is available.
       </p>
       <p>
        As Andrew M. Davis explains in his book
        <a href="https://www.brandscapingbook.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.brandscapingbook.com/', 'Brandscaping');" target="_blank">
         Brandscaping
        </a>
        , the concept of appointment consumption is the notion that your audience expects the content theyve grown to love on a predictable schedule. It was pioneered by television, but marketeers and publishers can leverage this concept to build successful platforms of their own.
       </p>
       <p>
        For example, social media guru Chris Brogan publishes his website on a predictable schedule, every Sunday. The result of this is the creation of anticipation with his readers: something I look forward to every Sunday morning.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Tip 4: Adapt the Reading Experience to Your Email Updates
       </h3>
       <p>
        You made everything accessible, but dont forget that your email update has to adapt to smaller screens too. Your phone is the most personal device you own. Its always with you. In fact, 40-50% of all emails are read on mobile devices, so the importance of this market cant be overstated.
       </p>
       <p>
        The key to mobile email marketing is readability. Some email layouts are unresponsive. On a phone or a small tablet, that can mean that fonts are unreadably small, or images are unwieldy and large.
       </p>

        Its important that your content is readable, so make sure its aesthetically pleasing and conforms to the dimensions of every possible screen size with a clean layout. Heres an example of a non-responsive email on a phone vs a responsive one:
