        <h2>
         What you can do with a cardboard box
        </h2>
        <small>
         July 05, 2012
         <i class="fa fa-circle">
         </i>
         Kerala Taylor
        </small>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="row">
       <div class="col-md-11 col-sm-11 col-xs-12 blog-index-body">
        <p>
         Each summer, parents dole out hefty amounts of cash to keep their children entertained. Yet the wildly popular recent video about
         <a href="/blog/what_if_kid_had_gone_soccer_camp_instead">
          Caine's Arcade
         </a>
         -- which chronicled the creation of a cardboard arcade built by a bored boy who had to tag along with his dad to work last summer -- proved that not only can a bit of boredom fuel ingenuity and creativity, but also that kids don't need a whole lot to occupy themselves.
        </p>
        <p>
         This summer, try giving your wallet a break and your kids a cardboard box. Even better, give them a dozen boxes so they can build their very own
         <a href="http://www.popupadventureplay.org/" target="_blank">
          pop-up playground
         </a>
         ! Get inspired by some of our favorite photos of cardboard box play from around the web:
