       <h1 class="post-title">
        Dog-fighting Arena in Queens: 20 Malnourished Dogs Saved
       </h1>
       <div class="post-meta">
        <span class="date">
         Published
         <a href="http://nextgendog.com/2014/08/">
          August 3, 2014
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="post-container">
        <p>
         <em>
          QUEENS, NEW YORK (August 2, 2014)
         </em>
         
         <strong>
          Addison Holder, 44, and Keisha Hall, 33, will face charges after cops raided their Jamaican house and found 20 malnourished dogs kept in cages with no food or water supply.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Five of the dogs were found with huge weighted collars tied around their necks, as reported by
         <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/nyc-crime/2-arrested-20-emaciated-dogs-found-queens-raid-article-1.1889347" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          NY Daily News
         </a>
         . Instead of the necessities for keeping the animals healthy and alive,there was a power supply which connected to the electricalwires and treadmill whichwere used in dog-fighting world.
        </p>
        <p>
         The home was usedas a training ground as well as dog fighting arena for at least twenty emaciated Pit Bulls which were found during the search of their blooded garage. In addition, the police have also found stashes ofecstasy and methamphetamine.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          You heard the dogs, but you never saw them.Twenty dogs  I had no idea, said one of the neighbors.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         All dogs have been taken and will be taken care of by the
         <span style="color: #111111;">
          American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals. Holder and Hall will face charges for animal fighting, animal cruelty and drug possession. They are currently being held on a $100,000 bail.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         President and CEO of the ASPCA
         <strong>
          Matthew Bershadker
         </strong>
         had this to say after the incident:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Organized dog fighting is a brutal form of animal abuse where dogs are exploited and forced to fight as their owners profit from their torture.Through our partnership, the ASPCA and the NYPD are determined to protect New York Citys animals from this form of cruelty and bring their abusers to justice.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <em>

           NYDailyNews.com
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
