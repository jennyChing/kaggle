       <section class="raw" id="content-body">
        <div class="panel-pane pane-orion-blocks pane-blocks-post-body-html" id="current_article_body-0">
         <div class="pane-content">
          <div itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            When J and J (who wish to remain anonymous) left the advertising world in New York, they headed to Portland, Oregon, with the aim of creating something like the Tribeca loft they had left behind. I met themat our
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/posts/shop-portland-48-hours" style="line-height: 1.538em;" target="_blank">
             Remodelista Market in
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/posts/shop-portland-48-hours" style="line-height: 1.538em;" target="_blank">
             Portland
            </a>
            duringtheir first week in the city. That was three years ago, and they had just bought a one-story, nondescriptindustrial building on top of which they planned build a warehouse-style living space.Fresh from Manhattan, they were enthusiastic about their new hometown, and the feeling has since only grown: "We are crazy about this city. At its core is a young, creative spirit driving an amazing food, fashion, and retail scene. Here, you can be local and buy local; everything we sourced for the loft came from Portland retailers and artisans.
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            Local history is what informed the look of the loft, too. It's located in Southeast, Portland, nearLadd's Addition, the city's oldest planned residential development, now a historic district. (The couple themselves owned homes for over two decades in Florida's groundbreaking planned community, Seaside, where they were exposed to New Urbanism and leading American architects.) Another inspiration is the nearby 1914Ford building, originally an assembly plant for the Model T, sensitively adapted for use as creative offices and retail space by
            <a href="http://emerick-architects.com/" style="line-height: 1.538em;" target="_blank">
             Emerick Architects
            </a>
            of Portland, the firm that J and J hired for their own place. The architects were given a mandate: to create a net zero-energy loftmeaning it generates the energy it consumesthat looks very much at home in its setting.
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            Photography by
            <a href="http://www.lincolnbarbour.com/" target="_blank">
             Lincoln Barbour
            </a>
            except where noted.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland loft with stainless steel kitchen | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-stainless-kitchen-counters-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland loft with stainless steel kitchen | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above:The loft was built atop a one-story structure with
            <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
             an 8,000-square-foot footprint
            </span>
            <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
             . An 11 foot setback was required for the addition, and this area became a wraparound terrace and garden.Factory windows were used to create a sense of history. In the kitchen area, the owners sourced astainless kitchen counter and units with integrated draining board from a restaurant supply company. They note, "The best counter is stainless; it gets a great old patina and you can put anything on it, from blueberries and lemon juice to hot and cold plates. The architects designed the industrial custom hood in keeping with the look. For more on stainless counters, see
             <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/posts/remodeling-101-stainless-steel-countertops" target="_self">
              Remodeling 101
             </a>
             .
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland loft kitchen | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-kitchen-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland loft kitchen | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above: The stainless steel kitchen island is topped with Carrara marble, chosen because, as one of the Js explains, "I wanted it to look like a 1924 Paris baking table." To give it a worn patina, the marble is honed rather than polished.The ceiling lighting is based on the idea of an old sprinkler system; Emerick Architects enlisted the help of local company
            <a href="http://www.schoolhouseelectric.com/" target="_blank">
             Schoolhouse Electric
            </a>
            to help in the execution.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland loft with dining table | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-dining-room-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland loft with dining table | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above: The loft's floor is Douglas fir painted a very warm vanilla creamthe painters had to be persuaded not to fill the natural knots and dings in the floor and to leave it with a weathered look. The wood on the ceiling was stained to replicate the look and feel of the original. Tar paper tacks from the roof were added for some visual wear.The harvest table was sourced from
            <a href="http://www.uniquestorepdx.com/" style="line-height: 1.538em;" target="_blank">
             Seek the Unique
            </a>
            and originally sat in Oregon's Aurora Historical Society. The built-in open metal shelving houses
            <a href="http://www.fishseddy.com/" target="_blank">
             Fishs Eddy
            </a>
            dinnerwaremixed with old ironstone and clear glassware.
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Terrace of Portland warehouse loft | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/Indsutrial-loft-portland-terrace-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Terrace of Portland warehouse loft | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above: A 13-foot-wide-opening with accordion doors connects the dining area to the terrace, and allows for indoor-outdoor living; the
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/posts/new-t14-chair-series-from-tolix" target="_self">
             Tolix chairs
            </a>
            are often brought out for pre-dinner drinks. J and J explain, "The inspiration for the penthouse came from living in a loft in Tribeca. We always loved loft living, but in New York if we wanted to enjoy the outdoors, we had to ride down the elevator and go to a public space. We wanted to incorporate outdoor space and Portland's lush greenery into our living space, and essentially bring the outside in."
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland terrace garden desk | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-terrace-garden-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland terrace garden desk | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above: The couple tell us that their wraparound rooftop garden "tends to be a combination of lavender, tall grasses, boxwood, and kitchen herbs with jasmine across the west and south wall, a four-in-one espaliered apple tree on another wall, and a grape leaf arbor very much in progress with sedum as ground cover." Solar panels on the new construction make it a net zero-energy building.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland loft | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-living-room-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland loft | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above: The loft has two concrete masses that anchor the space, one that houses the fireplace, the other the elevator. Two shelving brackets above the fireplace serve as an architectural detail and are used to hang seasonal floral displays by Portland florist
            <a href="http://annamaraflowers.com/" target="_blank">
             Anna Mara
            </a>
            . The owners enlisted local metal worker
            <a href="http://adbusch.com/index.php?/bench/miscellaneous/" target="_blank">
             Aaron Busch
            </a>
            ,who made thebrackets, to also fabricate some fire tools that hang from the wall by magnets.The nine-foot-long wingback sofa is from a hotel lobby; the armchairs are by
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/shop?retailer=Cisco+Home" style="line-height: 1.538em;" target="_blank">
             Cisco Brothers
            </a>
            , an LA company on the vanguard of using green materials and methods in its furniture.The 18-inch-squarecoffee tables were made
            <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
             from an Oregon farmhouse beam
            </span>
            <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
             by
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.salvageworkspdx.com/" style="line-height: 1.538em;" target="_blank">
             Salvage Works
            </a>
            <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
             in Portland.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland loft vintage desk | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-vintage-desk-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland loft vintage desk | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above: The library-office area is anchored by a custom 16-foot-long counter with a walnut top sourced from nearby Tillamookand drawers that hide everything, including a printer.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland loft metal sliding barn door| Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-metal-sliding-barn-door-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland loft metal sliding barn door| Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above: In keeping with the warehouse vernacular, the living area is separated from the bedroom and bath by a sliding fire door. In lieu of the traditional kickstand to prop open the door, a pulley-and-weight system was created.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland loft bedroom | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-bedroom-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland loft bedroom | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above: In the master bedroom, the backdrop wall ispainted
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/products/behr-premium-plus-ultra-8-oz-ul260-1-cracked-pepper-interior-exterior-paint-sample" target="_blank">
             Cracked Pepper
            </a>
            from Behr, the only color in the loft. In the corner, a
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/posts/storage-steele-canvas-basket-co-" target="_blank">
             Steele Canvas Basket
            </a>
            is used for stowing the bed's extra pillows at night. The bed linens are from
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/shop?featured_categories=Fabrics+%26+Linens&amp;brand=Matteo" target="_self">
             Matteo
            </a>
            , and the couple created their dust ruffle from a king-sized sheet: "We prefer it to a store-bought dust ruffle because you can mush it however you want." The white filmy curtains used throughout the loft are hung in panels allowing them to be moved as needed to block the sun.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland loft bathroom | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-bathroom-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland loft bathroom | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above: In the bathroom, J and J opted for a salvaged Frenchurinal over a Kohler double sink because it's thinner and longer in form, though it required the drilling of holes for plumbing. They opted to keep the patina on the bottom of the sink.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Door handle and light switch details in Portland industrial loft | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Door-handle-light-switches-Portland-loft-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Door handle and light switch details in Portland industrial loft | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above:The
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/products/black-porcelain-door-knob" target="_self">
             Black Porcelain Doorknobs
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/products/lewis-triple-push-button-switchplate" target="_blank">
             Push-Button Light Switch Plates
            </a>
            were sourced from Rejuvenation.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="embedded-Media-image img-caption-c">
             <img alt="Industrial Portland hallway | Remodelista" class="imagecache-article_full_width image-insert img-caption-c" itemprop="image" src="http://www.remodelista.com/files/styles/733_0s/public/fields/Industrial-Portland-loft-tiled-hall-Remodelista.jpg " style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Industrial Portland hallway | Remodelista"/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Above:
            <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
             The ground floor of the building houses guest quarters and a laundry room, and leads to the loft.
            </span>
            <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
            </span>
            <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
             The building's entry has a floor of classic hexagonal tiles that incorporate a mat.The wooden ceiling was taken from the original roof that had been covered in tar paper, but had faced the elements for over 75 years. The couple insisted on using the weathered side because they loved the patina.For the doors, they wanted old reeded glass, butbuilding codes required tempered glass; instead, they were able to source something that looks like historic reeded glass. They hired local sign painter,
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.signworkspdx.com/" style="line-height: 1.538em;" target="_blank">
             Pete Mckearnan
            </a>
            <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
             who specializes in historic fonts, to come upwith the 1924 lettering that he hand-painted on the doors. Photograph via Emerick Architects.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Heading to Portland? Allow us to point you to our
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/cityguides?country=United+States&amp;region=Portland%2C+Oregon" target="_self">
             Favorite Places
            </a>
            in the city, including the original
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/posts/hotels-lodging-ace-hotel-in-portland" target="_self">
             Ace Hotel
            </a>
            , a
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/posts/a-shaker-inspired-cafe-in-portlandia" target="_self">
             Shaker-Inspired Portlandia Cafe
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.gardenista.com/posts/shoppers-diary-pistils-nursery-in-portland-or" target="_self">
             Pistils Nursery
            </a>
            , and much more. For inspirational lofts the world over, have a look at our
            <a href="http://www.remodelista.com/gallery?keyword=lofts" target="_self">
             Photo Gallery
            </a>
            .
           </p>
          </div>
