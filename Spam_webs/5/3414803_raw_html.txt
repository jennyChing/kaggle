       <h2 class="title">
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/mozzarella-potato-pie">
         Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie

       </h2>

       <div class="article">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6299171763" title="View 'Potato &amp; Mozzarella Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Potato &amp; Mozzarella Pie" border="0" height="751" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6053/6299171763_1ce34181ac_b.jpg" title="Potato &amp; Mozzarella Pie" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I already expected a lot of laughs when I got a package from my girl
         <a href="http://makingnoapologies.blogspot.com/" style="text-decorations: none;" target="_blank">
          Teri
         </a>
         . Why? Oh, I dont know. Call it gut-feeling, call it intuition, call it a sixth-sense.
        </p>
        <p>
         Or maybe it were the S.L.U.T napkins (stressed-out ladies unwinding together) she mailed me some time ago. Or the Tired Old Ass Soak bath salt, the Heavy-Duty Bitch tissues and pillbox that said: 
         <em>
          Its such a beautiful day I think Ill surprise everyone and skip my medication
         </em>
         , that she felt was so me.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now thats Teri for ya.
        </p>
        <p>
         What I didnt expect was this beautifully old 
         <em>
          The Southern Junior League Cookbook
         </em>
          filled to the brim with mouth-watering recipes that she put into the package as well. I dont think she could have made me any happier, though I must admit the tired old ass soak worked wonders on me!
        </p>
        <p>
         I flipped through recipes all afternoon and stumbled on a few (well, a lot, actually) of them that were right up my alley. So simple and tasty but so unDutch that I felt compelled to turn them into a posting and spread the love.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now this ones amazing: a fluffy mashed potato pie topped with creamy mozzarella, tangy tomatoes and Italian herbs. If you want to put some flavor into your mashed taters next time, try this one!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ingredients:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         2 pound potatoes
         <br/>
         1/2 pound fresh mozzarella balls
         <br/>
         1/2 cup grated Parmesan
         <br/>
         3 tomatoes
         <br/>
         1 oz butter
         <br/>
         1/4 cup milk
         <br/>
         1 tsp oregano
         <br/>
         1 tsp basil
         <br/>
         pepper &amp; salt to taste
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Optionally:
         </strong>
         nutmeg
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Directions:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Isnt it gorgeous? I dont need all the fancy cooking, give me a cup of coffee and an old cookbook and Im happy.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6305591038" title="View 'recipe' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="recipe" border="0" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6050/6305591038_3f284a31cb_b.jpg" title="recipe"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Peel and boil the potatoes in salted water for 25 minutes, until completely tender and mash them.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6299115474" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6229/6299115474_d0977ae4c6_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         The original recipe calls for 1/4 cup of melted butter poured on top + 1/4 cup mashed into the potatoes. Nuh uh. Not afraid of butter, but Im so not gonna put a stick of butter into a side dish, especially not since it already has tons of cheese in it. So I replaced the butter with milk.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive added 1 oz of butter and 1/4 cup warm milk and kept mashing. The mashed potatoes will stay thick and firm, but thats okay.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6299115334" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6044/6299115334_89e16bab40_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Season with pepper and salt to taste. I always add a pinch of nutmeg to my mashed potatoes; its a Dutch thing.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6299115218" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6036/6299115218_d7efe56efb_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont have a 9 1/2 pie plate (should buy one, theyre pretty), so I simply used an oven dish and spread the mashed potatoes in there.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6299115124" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6100/6299115124_bea020dfee_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thinly slice the mozzarella and the (peeled) tomatoes.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6298582941" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6042/6298582941_951478a8ce_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Place half the mozzarella on top of the mashed potatoes.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6299114898" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" height="751" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6049/6299114898_f36f9766bf_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Put the tomato slices on top of the mozzarella.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6298582723" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6229/6298582723_0c83eec723_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sprinkle the dried basil and oregano all over the tomatoes and cover the tomatoes with the remaining mozzarella.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6298582575" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6114/6298582575_f119043e6a_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         And grate the Parmesan all over the casserole.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6299114482" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6231/6299114482_392ccc42c5_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The reason I kept my mashed potatoes firm is that during the cooking process the tomatoes as well as the mozzarella will release liquid that seeps into the mashed potatoes and adds flavor. If your potatoes are real fluffy to begin with, it might end up as something you can drink through a straw.
        </p>
        <p>
         Place the casserole or pie into a preheated oven and bake at 400F (200C) for 25 to 30 minutes.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6299114352" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" height="751" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6234/6299114352_7dc169a4fb_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Until the cheese has turned golden brown on top.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6299114248" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" height="751" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6235/6299114248_4769179f64_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         And has melted underneath creating this gooey, cheesy, and incredibly tasty puree with the slight tang of the tomatoes and Italian seasonings. So good.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/6298582049" title="View 'Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie' on Flickr.com">
          <img alt="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" border="0" height="752" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6039/6298582049_5823700ef4_b.jpg" title="Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The only thing I might change next time, aside from cutting out a gratuitous amount of butter, is adding a little Parmesan to the mashed potatoes as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         And Teri? Oh yeah, Im plotting my revenge. Shell come undone.
        </p>

           <td class="fnname" colspan="2">
            Mozzarella &amp; Potato Pie
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr class="ingredients">
           <td class="hrlabel">
            Ingredients
           </td>
           <td class="hritem">
            <ul class="ingredients">
             2 pound potatoes
             <br/>
             1/2 pound fresh mozzarella balls
             <br/>
             1/2 cup Parmesan (grated)
             <br/>
             3 tomatoes
             <br/>
             1 oz butter
             <br/>
             1/4 cup milk
             <br/>
             1 tsp oregano
             <br/>
             1 tsp basil
             <br/>
             pepper &amp; salt to taste
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Optionally:
             </strong>
             nutmeg
             <tr class="instructions">
              <td class="hrlabel">
               Directions
              </td>
              <td class="hritem">
               <ol class="instructions">
                Peel and boil the potatoes in salted water for 25 minutes, until completely tender.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Add the butter and warm milk and mash them. Season them with salt and pepper to taste and optionally a hint of nutmeg. They will still be firm. Spread them in a 9 1/2 pie pan or casserole.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Thinly slice the mozzarella and tomatoes. Place half the mozzarella on top of the mashed potatoes, place te tomato slices on top of that, sprinkle the basil and oregano over the tomatoes and top it off with the remaining mozzarella.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Finish the casserole with the grated Parmesan and bake it in a preheated oven at 400F (200C) for 25 to 30 minutes.
