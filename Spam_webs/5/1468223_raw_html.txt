         THE HEART OF THE EARTH (2009)  Review
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="posted-on">

            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             <img alt="The Heart of The Earth" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-17970" height="252" src="http://aidyreviewsnet.c.presscdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/The-Heart-of-The-Earth-590x252.png" title="The Heart of The Earth" width="590"/>
            </p>
            <h3 style="text-align: center;">
             Let me touch your heart.
            </h3>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             In
             <strong>
              Jean-Michel Roux
             </strong>
             <strong>
              THE HEART OF THE EARTH
             </strong>
             (2009)
             <em>
              Le Cur De La Terre,
             </em>
             aman (
             <strong>
              Hilmar Oddsson
             </strong>
             ) from the city finds himself on an expedition to find the heart of the Earth in this captivating short written and directed Frenchman
             <strong>
              Jean-Michel Roux
             </strong>
             . Surrounded by the beautiful landscape of the Icelandic terrain, topped off with a mystical soundtrack lead by
             <strong>
              Bjork
             </strong>
             and
             <strong>
              Sir John Taverner,
             </strong>
             the viewer is taken on a journey of disconnection from nature and inner reflection.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             The film opens with cars rushing about, their horns honkinga sight not uncommon of the larger towns and cities. Humanity has become a hurried race; hardly taking the timeto stop and admire what is around us or live in the moment. Oddsson has decided to venture out into the countryside as if he is suddenly being called there. In some scenes, a heartbeat can be heardthe heart of the Earth calling this wanderer to be reunited. Oddsson also hears a soothing female voice instructing him to allow the light of the planet to embrace him without resistance. The voice serves as his guide, even during moments where he believes hes losing his mind.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             Among the natural landmarks lies a labyrinth, which may also serve as the logo for the film. A labyrinth is a type of maze that is commonly used in meditative rituals to center ones self or to mimic the path to enlightenment. Oddsson stayshe refers to the labyrinth and walk within thecrevicesof the mountainside,hinderingto the spirit of the mountain, or the voice of the Earth. He was guided there it seems to initiate himself, to prepare to meet the spirit. He wanted to find the source of the heartbeat. Heading up the glacier, the wanderer spoke his last words:
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: right;">
             <em>
              Earth becomes one with the sky. Has he risen to meet his guide?
             </em>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             <em>
              The Heart of the Earth
             </em>
             is a stunning visual short that relies on simple, yet powerful dialogue to tell a story of one mans reconnection to nature and his ascent into higher realms of consciousness via tuning into the voice of his guidethe earth itself.
            </p>
           </div>
