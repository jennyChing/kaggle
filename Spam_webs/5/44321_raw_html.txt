      <h1 class="entry-title">
       Sitting By My Telephone
      </h1>
      <div class="post-info post-details">
       <p>
        <span class="detail-item date published updated time" title="2009-06-10T00:02:44+00:00">
         June 10th, 2009

        <span class="vcard author detail-item">

      <p>
       Maddie had long, skinny fingers like her Daddy. Mike hoped that she would use them to make music. He could picture her fingers playing the guitar or dancing across a keyboard. Id had those hopes, too, but I also knew those fingers would also help her with something very important  pushing buttons. Not figurative buttons, though. Literal buttons.
      </p>
      <p>
       Maddie was obsessed with pushing buttons. She loved to push on our keyboards, the stereo, and my point and shoot camera.
      </p>
      <p>
       But her first love? PHONES. I told Mike her phone &amp; texting bills were going to be HUGE.
      </p>
      <p>
       Back in March at a play date, Maddie encountered her first toy cell phone. It belonged to another little boy, but Maddie NEVER PUT IT DOWN. I thought for sure shed throw a tantrum when I finally gave it back to the little boy, but of course she didnt. She was awesome like that.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/plasticcandy/3322378208/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.flickr.com/photos/plasticcandy/3322378208/', '']);" title="the phone Maddie stole by The Spohrs Are Multiplying..., on Flickr">
        <img alt="the phone Maddie stole" height="333" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3575/3322378208_1b73563a55.jpg" width="500"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       A few weeks after that, Mike and I took her on her first plane flight. We walked by a kids store in LAX and I saw a toy phone just like the one the little boy had, but in pink. So of course we HAD to get it for her. It was immediately her favorite toy.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/plasticcandy/3364456478/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.flickr.com/photos/plasticcandy/3364456478/', '']);" title="_MG_9277 by The Spohrs Are Multiplying..., on Flickr">
        <img alt="_MG_9277" height="500" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3563/3364456478_57692f2b2a.jpg" width="333"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       But nothing appealed to her like a REAL phone. More specifically, MY phone. She LOVED my blackberry. It was the perfect thing to quiet her. I always let her have it at Dr. Loooves office when she needed a shot. Shed cry for a second, but as soon as she saw my blackberry shed light up brighter than the tiny screen on the phone.
       <br/>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/plasticcandy/3400478609/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.flickr.com/photos/plasticcandy/3400478609/', '']);" title="IDK, My BFF Jill by The Spohrs Are Multiplying..., on Flickr">
        <img alt="IDK, My BFF Jill" height="500" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3623/3400478609_fd897463bd.jpg" width="500"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Even though I locked the phone so she couldnt accidentally call China, it would still light up if she hit a button. My screensaver was a picture of Maddie &amp; Rigby, and she loved to look at it.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/plasticcandy/3608651184/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.flickr.com/photos/plasticcandy/3608651184/', '']);" title="checking her email by The Spohrs Are Multiplying..., on Flickr">
