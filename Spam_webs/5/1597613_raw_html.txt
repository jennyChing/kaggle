            <h1 class="entry-title">
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2012/03/23/mirrored-people-in-the-forest/" rel="bookmark">
              Mirrored People in the Forest
             </a>
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta">
             on March 23, 2012 by Shawn Saleme
            </div>
            <!-- .entry-meta -->
           </header>
           <!-- .entry-header -->
           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/?p=40502">
              <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-40503" height="557" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/vestige-by-rob-mulholland_1.jpeg" title="vestige-by-rob-mulholland_1" width="600"/>
             </a>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Next time you find yourself walking in the forests of
              <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/">
               Scotland
              </a>
              , look for mirrored people.
             </strong>
             Located on the woodland walk at the
             <a href="http://www.forestry.gov.uk/website/recreation.nsf/LUWebDocsByKey/ScotlandStirlingQueenElizabethForestParkAchrayForestDavidMarshallLodgeVisitorCentre">
              David Marshall Lodge
             </a>
             , Vestige is an art installation created by
             <a href="http://www.robmulholland.co.uk/">
              Rob Mulholland
             </a>
             . A contemporary sculptor and installation artist based in
             <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/">
              Scotland
             </a>
             , he had originally intended for the six mirrored people (three women and three men) to be temporary. Hikers passing through the forest enjoyed the displays so much however, that it has now become a permanent art installation for all to enjoy.
             <span id="more-40502">
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             The vision behind the art is twofold: 1) to create a vestige of the people who once occupied the land until World War I, when they were re-located while forests were planted to generate timber and; 2) to make people reflect upon mans impact on nature.
            </p>
