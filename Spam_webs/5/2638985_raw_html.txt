        <p>
         The participants for this year's Sprite Slam Dunk were unveiled earlier this week; for the most part an impressive field of high-flying basket assailants. Human springboard Victor Oladipo will join lanky midair contortionist Giannis Antetokounmpo, odds-on favorite Zach LaVine...and Mason Plumlee.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Sure, Plumlee is technically capable of forcing the ball into the cylinder with his hands, but there is scant evidence of him being the kind of athlete or artist that should be squaring off for the league's dunking crown.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Instead, here's ten guys who could (or should) be in Plumlee's place, helping to keep the Dunk Contest off life support.
        </p>

         <strong>
          LeBron James
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Any conversation about guys who should be in the Dunk Contest always begins with LeBron - and with good reason. For over a decade, he's regularly teased us with his rim-rattling acrobatics, once openly declaring that he'd participate.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Of course, we're still waiting for his debut, and it's more or less definitely not happening at this point, but LeBron's ignorance of the Dunk Contest will be the only stage on which he didn't rise to the occasion...Well OK, that and the 2011 Finals.
         <br/>

         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7UBFvcB7Hjo" target="_blank">
          solid body of work
         </a>
         suggesting that he isn't even the best dunker in his own family.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Miles has been buried on the Phoenix bench behind Alex Len, and is probably chomping at the bit to unleash some of the vicious dunks he threw down far more regularly last year.

         <br/>
         <br/>
         But the way he attacks the rim like a reckless cruise missile suggests he'd be huge in All-Star Saturday's marquee, so even in vain (and in a hypothetical world) he should be here over Mason Plumlee.

Their discouraged fanbase could use a little excitement this season, and the rest of the league's fans could use a reminder that the Sixers haven't in fact been relegated to the NBDL.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Enter KJ McDaniels in the Dunk Contest: a high-flyer who fits the NBA's preferred age window (the whole 'Rising Stars' thing), isn't Mason Plumlee, and as one of four contestants, stands the best mathematical odds of the Sixers actually winning something this year.

       <a class="text-word" href="/tags/plumlee" target="_blank">
        Plumlee
       </a>
       .
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Sure,
       <a class="text-word" href="/tags/plumlee" target="_blank">
        Plumlee
       </a>
       is technically capable of forcing the ball into the cylinder with his hands, but there is scant evidence of him being the kind of athlete or artist that should be squaring off for the league's dunking crown.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Instead, here's ten guys who could (or should) be in
       <a class="text-word" href="/tags/plumlee" target="_blank">
        Plumlee
       </a>
       's place, helping to keep the
       <a class="text-word" href="/tags/dunk-contest" target="_blank">
        Dunk Contest
       </a>
       off life support.
      </p>
