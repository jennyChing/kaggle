       Sweet &amp; Sticky Baked Chicken Drumsticks
      </a>
      <span class="asterisk">
       *
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="postmeta">
      <em>
       By
      </em>
      Jessica /
      <strong>
       <a href="http://www.thenovicechefblog.com/2012/02/sweet-sticky-baked-chicken-drumsticks/#comments">
        160 comments

      </strong>
     </div>
     <p>
      <img alt="chicken, drumstick, wing, honey, balsamic vinegar, appetizer, snack, entree, recipe" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7572" height="468" src="http://www.thenovicechefblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Sweet-and-Sticky-Drumsticks-1-sm.jpg" title="Sweet and Sticky Drumsticks" width="600"/>
      <br/>
      <a href="javascript:void((function(){var%20e=document.createElement('script');e.setAttribute('type','text/javascript');e.setAttribute('charset','UTF-8');e.setAttribute('src','http://assets.pinterest.com/js/pinmarklet.js?r='+Math.random()*99999999);document.body.appendChild(e)})());">

     <p>
      Sometimes we do things for our significant others that we dont like. At least, thats what I told myself as I was standing over a bowl of drumsticks. I dont know why, but I cant eat meat off a bone. Ribs, Drumsticks, Wings and anything else that you eat off a bone ::shudder:: I just cant. I dont know what it is, but I just can
      <strong>
       not
      </strong>
      do it.
     </p>
     <p>
      But
      <a href="http://www.thenovicechefblog.com/2012/01/red-velvet-cheesecake-milkshake/" target="_blank">
       Jorge
      </a>
      , well that man can clean a wing in .2 seconds flat. Seriously, I have seen him do it. The wing goes in whole and comes out cleanits like hes the official wing cleaner of the southeast. He proudly tells people about the time he beat the nuclear wing (read: hot as a muthertrucker wings) challenge at Beef O Bradys. And as a winner, he got to write his name on the wall and was given a free t-shirt! And obviously those totally awesome prizes made it soooo worth having fire come out of both ends for the next 2 days. TMI? Long story short, the man loves him some saucy dark meat.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="chicken, drumstick, wing, honey, balsamic vinegar, appetizer, snack, entree, recipe" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7573" height="747" src="http://www.thenovicechefblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Sweet-and-Sticky-Drumsticks-2-sm.jpg" title="Sweet and Sticky Drumsticks 2 sm" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      So being the good wife I am, I made him Sweet &amp; Sticky Baked Chicken Drumsticks. And after witnessing his happy dance, I have concluded that the way to a mans heart is through the following combination:
     </p>
     <p>
      Delicious Crispy Skin + Juicy Dark Meat + Coated in a Wonderfully Sticky Sauce = Happy Man
      <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.thenovicechefblog.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
     </p>

      <h2 itemprop="name">
       Sweet &amp; Sticky Baked Chicken Drumsticks
      </h2>
      <div class="time">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Yield:
        </strong>
        <span itemprop="recipeYield">
         6 Drumsticks
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="summary" itemprop="description" style="clear:left">
       <p>
        <em>
         Recipe Note: I used a chicken breast instead of drumsticks for my dinner and loved this sauce! So feel free to change it up to fit your tastes!
        </em>
       </p>
      </div>
      <h3 id="ingredients">
       Ingredients:
      </h3>
      <div class="ingredients">
       <ul>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/4 cup honey
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/4 cup light brown sugar, packed
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         3 garlic cloves, minced
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/2 teaspoon fresh cracked black pepper
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         6 chicken drumsticks, about 1.25 pounds
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 tablespoon sesame seeds, lightly toasted
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         a few pinches chopped fresh parsley
        </li>
       </ul>
      </div>
      <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
       Directions:
      </h3>
      <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
       <p>
        In a medium bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, honey, brown sugar, soy sauce, garlic, ginger and cracked black pepper. Pour into a large ziplock bag and add chicken drumsticks. Shake to coat chicken and refrigerate for 8 hours, or overnight, giving the bag a shake whenever you think about it.
       </p>
       <p>
        Preheat oven to 400F. Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil and place a
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00030CGKY/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=thnochbl-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00030CGKY" target="_blank">
         Cooling Rack
        </a>
        on top.
       </p>
       <p>
        Remove the chicken from the bag, reserving the marinade, and place the drumsticks on the rack. Bake the drumsticks, on the middle oven rack, for 25 minutes.
       </p>
       <p>
        While the chicken is baking, place the leftover marinade in a small saucepan. Bring the marinade to a boil, then reduce the heat to a slow simmer. Cook over low heat, stirring as needed, for about 15 minutes or until thickened.
       </p>
       <p>
        Remove drumsticks from oven (after first 25 minutes of cooking) and heavily coat with the thickened marinade, using a silicone pastry brush. Return to the oven and cook for 10 more minutes. Coat the drumsticks again and return to oven to cook for 5 more minutes, or until an internal temperate of 160F is reached.
       </p>
       <p>
        Coat the drumsticks one last time, with any remaining sauce, and sprinkle with sesame seeds and parsley. Serve immediately.
       </p>
      </div>
