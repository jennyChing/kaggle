        <h2>
         Overview
        </h2>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-before-post" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <h3>
         Kanha  Rudyard Kiplings muse
        </h3>
        <p>
         KANHA NATIONAL PARK is one of Asias premier wildlife reserves. The area has been protected since 1933. In 1973, it was included among the nine original Tiger Reserves under Project Tiger. The Parks spectacular sal and bamboo forests, interspersed with grasslands, ravines and hills, offer unparallel opportunities to photograph Indian wildlife. Kanha is also considered to be probably the best place in the world to see wild tigers.
        </p>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <div class="summary-display">
         <ul>
          <li>
           Area:
          </li>
          <li>
           Kanha Tiger Reserve  1,945 sq km(including National Park Core Area of 940 sq km)
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Altitude:
          </li>
          <li>
           600 to 900 metres above sea level
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Temperatures:
          </li>
          <li>
           Winter:Max. 24o CMin. 1o C
           <br/>
           Summer:Max. 44o CMin. 24o C
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Location:
          </li>
          <li>
           Mandla District, Madhya Pradesh, in central India
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Nearest Towns
          </li>
          <li>
           Mandla:45 km
           <br/>
           Jabalpur:160 km
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Tiger Estimation:
          </li>
          <li>
           These include the tiger, leopard, jungle cat, common langur (hanuman langur), wild boar, gaur (Indian bison), sambar, chital, muntjac (barking deer), hyena, wolf, jackal, dhole (wild dog), Indian fox, ruddy mongoose, sloth bear, and the extremely rare barasingha (swamp deer).
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Birds:
          </li>
          <li>
           Over 260 species of birds have been recorded, including the crested hawk eagle, greyheaded fishing eagle, honey buzzard, king vulture, black ibis, whitenecked stork, cotton teal, green pigeon, grey hornbill, pygmy woodpecker, blackheaded oriole, greater racket-tailed drongo, scarlet minivet, and Tickells blue flycatcher.
          </li>
         </ul>
        </div>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-after-post" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="panel entry-content" id="tab-detailed-itinerary">
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-before-post" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <h2>
         Kanha Adventure Package 2 nights and 3 days
        </h2>
        <p>
         The sun is just up, and, Kanha Meadow shimmers. At the start of a new year, Kanha is once again a meeting place, an intersection of wildlife and humankind. The rains have renewed Kanha, and Kanha will now nourish and renew her guests. A gentle, silvery mist still wraps the great vista of the Meadow in its folds. A small party of chital saunters daintily across the road and ambles toward Partak Nallah. Last to cross is a majestic stag. Emerging from the long grass, he stands stock still for a moment, carving a silhouette against grasslands and trees, low hills and horizon and ghostlike sun. He offers an invitation.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kanha National Park  Kiplings own country, is the setting for the famous Jungle Book. Just a short distance from the main entrance gate of the Kanha National Park, Jungle Resort provides visitors with a truly exciting and rewarding experience. The lodge is spread in 6 acres of thick forest and provide an ideal setting for bird watching for a natural history oriented traveler.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="main-heading">
          Day 01 Delhi-Kanha National park
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span class="subheading">
          Meals:Lunch &amp; Dinner
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Arrival at Jabalpur by train or flight in the morning. Drive to Kanha NP (3.5 hours). After lunch, you will be taken for a safari for tiger tracking and wildlife viewing in customized 4WD safari vehicles. The duration of safari is about four hours and is conducted early morning and in the afternoon till sunset. Each safari vehicle can seat up to four persons comfortably. Besides the visitors, the vehicle has an expert driver and a talented naturalist.
         <br/>
         Overnight stay in comfortable cottage at Tuli Tiger Resort.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="main-heading">
          Day 02 Kanha National Park
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span class="subheading">
          Meals: Breakfast, Lunch &amp; Dinner
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <p>
         Today you will get an early wake up call with Tea/Coffee &amp; cookies, get ready for another Jeep safari through the stately dense Sal forest (Shorea robusta) and the wildlife adventure of Kanha begins. Jeep Safari by the lower jungle track along the Kanha Meadows accompanied by an expert guide.
         <br/>
         You will be given an elephant ride, (subject to the tiger sightings) you return to lodge by 11 am. Take a short nap or a dip in our crystal clear swimming pool.
         <br/>
         Get ready for the evening game drive. You return to lodge as the sun sets. Camp fire followed by dinner.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="main-heading">
          Day 03 Kanha National Park
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span class="subheading">
          Meals: Breakfast
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         After breakfast drive to Jabalpur (165 km). Connect direct train to Delhi OR drive to Nagpur and fly to Delhi or Mumbai in the evening.
        </p>
