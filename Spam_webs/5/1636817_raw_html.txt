        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Catcalls Are Not Compliments: Challenging Street Harassment Worldwide
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2012-03-15T12:42:27+00:00">
          March 15, 2012
         </span>
         by
         <a href="http://msmagazine.com/blog/author/hollykearl/" rel="author" title="Posts by Holly Kearl">
          Holly Kearl
         </a>
         |
         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://msmagazine.com/blog/2012/03/15/catcalls-are-not-compliments-challenging-street-harassment-worldwide/#comments">
           9 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <div addthis:title="Catcalls Are Not Compliments: Challenging Street Harassment Worldwide " addthis:url="http://msmagazine.com/blog/2012/03/15/catcalls-are-not-compliments-challenging-street-harassment-worldwide/" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style addthis_">
          <a class="addthis_button_preferred_1">
          </a>
          <a class="addthis_button_preferred_2">
          </a>
          <a class="addthis_button_preferred_3">
          </a>
          <a class="addthis_button_preferred_4">
          </a>
          <a class="addthis_button_compact">
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://testdb.msmagazine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Teacher-with-her-HS-students-at-52nd-St.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-62484" height="450" src="http://testdb.msmagazine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Teacher-with-her-HS-students-at-52nd-St.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           When I was in sixth grade, I had to walk a long way home from the bus stop. A few times, men followed me. I ran to a store so they wouldnt know where I lived.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          A high school junior in Virginia told me this when I visited her class last week to talk about street harassment. As soon as she shared her story, the room flooded with tales from other girls: the sexual remarks shouted at them from cars; the man who made humping motions against a girl on a public bus; the creepy men following them in stores; the men masturbating in front of them at public swimming pools; the man who told a girl on her bike to get in the car.
         </p>
         <p>
          For many of the girls, this was the first time talking about these experiences.
          <strong>
          </strong>
          For all, it was the first time doing so in front of male classmates. The girls spoke about it in a matter-of-fact way, as part of their lives. The boys were stunned by what they heard; several commented on how much their awareness was raised.
         </p>
         <p>
          Sharing stories to raise awareness is what the upcoming
          <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/">
           International Anti-Street Harassment Week
          </a>
          is all about. Street harassment can only begin to be curbed after more people are aware, first, that it happens; second, that it happens a lot; third, that it happens to most women and girls and many men in the LGBQT community; and fourth, that when it happens, it has a negative impact on the harassed peoples lives.
         </p>
         <p>
          From March 18 to 24, more than
          <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/cosponsors/">
           100 groups in 18 countries
          </a>
          will join thousands of individuals worldwide to
          <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/action/">
           collectively bring attention
          </a>
          to this global problem. Tactics range from hashtags to radio campaigns to in-your-face street theater. Participants can choose
          <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/participate/">
           whatever approach
          </a>
          they think will be most effective in their community.
         </p>
         <p>
          In Sanaa, Yemen,
          <a href="http://www.thesafestreets.org/">
           the Safe Streets Campaign
          </a>
          will distribute copies of a book containing womens street harassment stories to womens groups and human rights groups, members of Parliament and journalists. They will also post it online as a PDF. This isnt their first innovative approach to awareness raising: In 2011, they hosted an art exhibit (some of their work will be on display in a Washington, D.C.
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/events/129484250512461/">
           art show
          </a>
          on March 18.)
         </p>
         <p>
          Yemen Safe Streets Campaign founder
          <a href="http://www.stopstreetharassment.org/2012/02/ghaidaaal-absi/">
           Ghaidaa Alabsi
          </a>
          says:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           This problem is damaging [our] community cohesion, but it is getting very little attention.  We want to unify our voices with the other campaign[s] to tell the world in one voice Stop Sexual Harassment.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Since football (soccer) is so popular in Germany, the activist group
          <a href="http://prochange.tumblr.com/">
           Pro Change
          </a>
          will visit buses and subways to hand out a red card against sexism and street harassment. They will also go to clubs and pubs, distributing the cards along with special beer mats to spotlight the harassment that happens there.
         </p>
         <p>
          In Delhi, India, Kuber Sharma and
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iFwth3PsjT8&amp;feature=player_embedded">
           Dhruv Arora
          </a>
          , two male activists with the anti-violence campaign
          <a href="http://www.mustbol.in/">
           Must Bol
          </a>
          , launched a website called
          <a href="http://gotstared.at/">
           GotStared.At
          </a>
          . The website allows people from anywhere to upload photos of what they were wearing when they were harassed, in order to dispel the myth that clothes cause harassment. The
          <a href="http://www.facebook.com/gotstared">
           GotStaredAt Facebook page
          </a>
          has anti-victim-blaming posters for download. Sharma and Arora say theyre involved because,
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Street sexual harassment is probably the most rampant form of violence in Delhi  We all know that it happens, but few people want to do anything about it. Gotstared.at is an answer to people who hold women responsible for street harassment. Its time the good mendo something about it!
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          In Montreal, Canada,
          <a href="http://www.femmesetvilles.org/">
           Women in Cities International
          </a>
          will conduct a
          <a href="http://www.unhabitat.org/pmss/listItemDetails.aspx?publicationID=2847">
           Womens Safety
          </a>
          <a href="http://testdb.msmagazine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Debbie-and-Chris-with-their-signs-outside-City-Hall-Saskatoon-Saskatchewan.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-62485" height="300" src="http://testdb.msmagazine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Debbie-and-Chris-with-their-signs-outside-City-Hall-Saskatoon-Saskatchewan.jpg" width="282"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.unhabitat.org/pmss/listItemDetails.aspx?publicationID=2847">
           Audit Walk
          </a>
          in partnership with CKUT-FM radio station and Concordia Universitys Gender Advocacy Centre to evaluate the safety of the citys streets. They have also released the initial findings of their
          <a href="http://www.femmesetvilles.org/english/index_en.htm">
           three-year study on gender inclusive cities
          </a>
          , which measures the safety of public space for women in four cities, based in large part on street harassment.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://meetusonthestreet12.crowdmap.com/">
           Dozens of events
          </a>
          are occurring in the United States.
          <a href="https://meetusonthestreet12.crowdmap.com/">
           See if one
          </a>
          is happening in your area. There are many other ways anyone can become involved.
          <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/participate/">
           Ideas on the website
          </a>
          include:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Sharing your stories on or offline,or reading/listening to other peoples stories
          </li>
          <li>
           Tweeting stories or messages with
           <a href="https://twitter.com/#!/search/%23EndSHWeek">
            #EndSHWeek
           </a>
           .
          </li>
          <li>
           Changing your
           <a href="https://www.facebook.com/MeetUsOnTheStreet">
            Facebook
           </a>
           profile picture for the week. Here is an
           <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/FBgraphic.jpg">
            image in English
           </a>
           and you can visit the tools page to access logos in 13 languages.
          </li>
          <li>
           Gathering a few friends together to create fliers to post or hand out in the community or write sidewalk messages. Here are
           <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Ideas-for-messages-on-flierssignschalk.pdf">
            ideas for messages
           </a>
           and a
           <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Anti-StreetHarassmentWeek2012FactSheet.pdf">
            fact sheet
           </a>
           with
           <a href="http://www.stopstreetharassment.org/resources/statistics/">
            statistics
           </a>
           to pull from. Here are fliers you can print and pass out:
           <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/NotHeyBabyFliers.pdf">
            1
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.meetusonthestreet.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/mutscard_41.pdf">
            2
           </a>
           . Or visit the tools page and download an 8.511 size flier in 5 languages.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          However you can, in whatever way feels comfortable to you, you can participate in the week by simply starting a dialogue around the issue. Raising awareness is the important first step toward stopping street harassment.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Top: Anti-street harassment activists in Philadelphia, 2011. Right: Anti-street harassment demonstrators in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan.
           <br/>
          </em>
