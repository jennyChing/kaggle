                    &lt;h2&gt;5 international networking tips from Vodafone's Ute Marke &lt;/h2&gt;

                    &lt;div class="likeBox pull-left"&gt;
                        &lt;span id="totalLikes"&gt;10&lt;/span&gt;
                        
                        &lt;p id="liked" style="display: none;"&gt; LIKED&lt;/p&gt;

                        &lt;p id="loaderLike" style="display: none;"&gt;

                    &lt;/div&gt;

            &lt;div class="textCnt"&gt;
                &lt;p&gt;&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;Ute Marke is Product Delivery Manager (Governance) and Employee Champion for Germany at Vodafone and is based in Duesseldorf. As part of her role, Ute liaises with colleagues, clients and customers across the global Vodafone network on a daily basis  and as such, is an international networking whizz. &lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;We sat down with Ute to glean her top tips on meeting, networking and nurturing global contacts. &lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;strong&gt;&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;&lt;span style="color:#512654;"&gt;1. BE FRIENDLY, OPEN &amp; COLLABORATIVE&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;iframe align="right" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="210" mozallowfullscreen="" scrolling="no" src="//player.vimeo.com/video/97883441" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="375"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;One moment I'm on the phone with stakeholders from South Africa, the next organising a workshop with customers in Europe and next holding a meeting with my German team. Simplicity is the key to dealing with global contacts. Be likable, be genuine, be humble and talk, make calls, send emails  don't hold back. But also remember to be clear and straightforward in your requests and be aware of your contact's time  they probably take lots and lots of calls each day.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;Focus on what you want from your international network and enjoy the journey. See the challenges of finding the right people to talk to as an adventure. You'll meet many people along the way. I believe in working in an open and collaborative way and aim to build a family of key contacts that I can rely on to keep me involved in the everything related to the business  but remember, it's important to nurture your local contacts first.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;strong&gt;&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;&lt;span style="color:#512654;"&gt;2. STAY ON TOP OF YOUR DIARY&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;I talk to people across the globe from Spain to India on a daily basis, so managing my diary is essential. It's also quite challenging, as my day-to-day life consists of raising my three children, business, and the charity work I do for the Vodafone Foundation. I have no choice but to be super-organised so I get up early and run through my diary before anything else. I make sure to mix my work with tonnes of fun, however  otherwise the pressure would become too much. Mixing work with fun helps you become focused and positive.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;strong&gt;&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;&lt;span style="color:#512654;"&gt;3. LEARN ABOUT YOUR CONTACTS' COUNTRIES &amp; CULTURE&lt;/span&gt;S&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;When it comes to making initial contacts I try to learn as much as I can in advance of the geo-political and economic state of the countries I'm dealing with. For example, I make sure to find out where each of my Indian stakeholders are based as there are huge differences between regions  so there'll be very different conversation points to consider.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;Another piece of advice I would give is to learn how to greet your international contacts in their own language before you begin to converse in English. Knowing a bit of the local dialect or remembering that your client is in the middle of a very important religious festival, for example, shows respect and will give you great business leverage. It also makes your next call so much easier. &lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;There are challenges of course and there will always be differences with international contacts but part of the fun and adventure is learning as you go along. There are even differences in the ways Germans and Brits network. Whereas Brits can be quite indirect with their answers, Germans can be very direct with theirs! However, whatever you do, don't form opinions based on stereotypes as this can take you down the wrong networking path altogether! Ultimately, every client loves an honest approach. The key is to be open and genuine. You cant go wrong.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;strong&gt;&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;&lt;span style="color:#512654;"&gt;4. SEND YOUR GLOBAL CONTACT AN E-CARD OR TWO&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;My diary is my best friend when it comes to nurturing my network. I try to record regional festivals, bank holidays and note down bits of information about my international clients. It's good to show an interest in what they're doing inside and outside of work. I send e-cards but I prefer not to send them when it's more typically expected, such as on birthdays or at Christmas; I prefer to send them as a surprise  for instance, a congratulations or a "Hey isn't your newborn six months old now?" It really opens up the conversation and delivers a personal touch.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;strong&gt;&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;&lt;span style="color:#512654;"&gt;5. COMBINE YOUR BUSINESS LIFE WITH CHARITY WORK&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;Getting involved and being enthusiastic about your business's charity work (or an external charity) could see your network widen exponentially.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;Im taking part in the Vodafone Foundation Moyo Big Bike Challenge Across Britain in September. It's a 1,000-mile bike ride from Land's End to John O'Groats to raise money for children living with HIV in Lesotho. It's something I'm genuinely passionate about and from the moment I got involved my international network almost exploded!&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;Being involved with the Foundation has put me in contact with many different people from the business around the globe. Now I speak to part of the senior leadership of Vodafone South Africa like a friend because he's a charity rider, too. And I can write to the German CEO and say "Hello, you're the head of the company and I'm one of the only people in Germany taking part in our global fundraiser".&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;If you're proud of the business you're working in and the fundraising initiative at stake, then it's the perfect recipe to get involved and to not only contact your friends but your business circle. Be open and keen, get in touch and say "You might not know me yet but I'm proud to be in your business and to be doing this. Now, maybe you might want to coach and mentor me".&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;
	&lt;span style="font-family:trebuchet ms,helvetica,sans-serif;"&gt;Being involved in the Foundation has opened doors to such high levels in the company it's unbelievable  I couldn't have contacted and become friends with some of these people, as part of my current role. And without the cycling I would never have been in contact with the everywomanNetwork either. It's opened doors I never thought possible.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
