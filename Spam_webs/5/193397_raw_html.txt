        <p>
         At least 3 of these phone addiction symptoms will be completely new to you. You might be shocked to discover which ones
        </p>
        <p>
         A staggering amount of people, especially teens, are
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addict/">
          addicted to smartphones
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         This increased usage of smartphones has led to a lot of
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/brain-phone-addiction/">
          physical and psychological effects
         </a>
         that are harmful for the well-being of these individuals.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addiction-information/">
          This issue is so serious
         </a>
         that a word has been invented just for it: Nomophobia. This term is given to phone addicts that completely lose their mind when they are away from their phones.
        </p>
        <p>
         The smartphone was invented to keep us connected to the world,
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addiction-friends/">
          our friends, and family
         </a>
         . However, its over-use has become a major reason of keeping us disconnected from our real relations.
        </p>
        <p>
         This misuse of technology has severe consequences, among which the most prominent symptoms are given below.
        </p>
        <h2>
         1. Increased Levels of Stress
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="increased stress" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-362" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/media/1-increased-stress-300x250.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <div class="twitter-button single-twitter-button">
         <a href="#" onclick="ga('send', 'event', 'Share', 'Twitter-sharethis', '/phone-addiction/phone-addiction-symptoms/'); socialPopup(event, 'http://twitter.com/home?status=12+Unexpected+Symptoms+of+Phone+Addiction+http%3A%2F%2Fwww.addictiontips.net%2Fphone-addiction%2Fphone-addiction-symptoms%2F');return false;">

        <p>
         People that
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addiction-facts/">
          compulsively check their phones every minute
         </a>
         are extremely addicted to it. Their mind and body constantly remains under stress due to their habit of checking their phones after short intervals.
        </p>
        <p>
         When you are on a vacation, where you are supposed to feel stress-free, you check your phone first thing in the morning. You chat with your colleagues and get anxious about your work.
        </p>
        <p>
         The fact that you can check your phone and get updates from work, you are putting a lot of work-stress on your mind.
        </p>
        <h3>
         1a. Stress on Fingers
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addiction-study/">
          A research conducted in the University of Zurich
         </a>
         recorded the brain activity of smartphone addicts with an EEG.
        </p>
        <p>
         The scientists found that the participants that constantly use their smartphones have a high level of brain activity every time they touch their fingers on their phones screen.
        </p>
        <p>
         This increased electrical activity in the brain results in severe stress on the fingers and the mind.
        </p>
        <h3>
         1b. Stress on the Mind
        </h3>
        <p>
         After a long and hectic day at work, many people excessively use their phones.
        </p>
        <p>
         Working hours are enough stressful to drain you off energy. However, phone addicts still feel the need of putting their mind under massive stress.
        </p>
        <p>
         Not only their eye muscles get tired, their energy levels deteriorate.
        </p>
        <h3>
         1c. Stress on the Body
        </h3>
        <p>
         When you use your phone, you look down at it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tilting your head forward puts a strain on your neck and back and results in a permanently disturbed posture.
        </p>
        <p>
         This tilting of head puts a lot of stress on your neck, shoulder, and cervical spine, which can turn into chronic back pain in the long run.
        </p>
        <h2>
         2. Insomnia
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="insomnia" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-363" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/media/2-insomnia-300x250.jpg"/>
        </p>

        <p>
         People who are addicted to phones find it hard to unplug from work and social networks.
        </p>
        <p>
         At night, lying on the bed, one should focus on giving the mind and body some rest and this is exactly what sleep is for.
