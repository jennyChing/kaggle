       <div class="post-thumbnail-wrapper">
        <a class="swipebox" href="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/20-must-have-psych-rock-albums.jpg" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to image of 10 Must Have Psych Rock Albums (According to Reddit Users)">

       <div class="post-content clearfix">
        <div class="at-above-post addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="10 Must Have Psych Rock Albums (According to Reddit Users)" data-url="http://psychrock.com/10-must-have-psych-rock-albums-according-to-reddit-users/">
        </div>
        <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
        </div>
        <p>
         We here at
         <strong>
          PsychRock.com
         </strong>
         have a collection of people with musical experiences that add up to many years. But . . . being we are open minded and always craving something new , we still seek out music weve never heard before. New &amp; old. So, we took to
         <a href="http://Reddit.com" target="_blank" title="Reddit">
          Reddit
         </a>
         to see what users from the Interwebs believed were the Go To Psych Rock Albums. Comment for any suggestions!
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          The Psychedelic Sounds of the 13th Floor Elevators
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <img alt="The Psychedelic Sounds of the 13th Floor Elevators" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-686" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/01-The-Psychedelic-Sounds-of-the-13th-Floor-Elevators.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Psychedelic Sounds of the 13th Floor Elevators is a 1966 album by The 13th Floor Elevators. The albums sound, featuring elements of folk, garage rock, blues and psychedelia, is notable for its use of the electric jug, as featured on the bands only hit, Youre Gonna Miss Me, which reached number 55 on the Billboard Charts with Tried to Hide as a B-side. Another single from the album, Reverberation (Doubt), reached number 129 on the Billboards Bubbling Under Chart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/QEQBfJwYlLY?rel=0" width="420">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          The 13th Floor Elevators  Easter Everywhere
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="The 13th Floor Elevators" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-688" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/02-easter-everywhere-13th-floor-elevators.jpg" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Easter Everywhere is the second studio album by American psychedelic rock band The 13th Floor Elevators. It was released in November, 1967.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/bYsyArYerMg?rel=0" width="420">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Spiritualized  Ladies And Gentleman We Are Floating In Space
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Spiritualized - Ladies And Gentleman We Are Floating In Space" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-689" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/03-spiritualized-Ladies-and-Gentlemen-We-Are-Floating-in-Space.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ladies and Gentlemen We Are Floating in Space is the third studio album by English space rock band Spiritualized, released by Dedicated Records on 16 June 1997. The album features guest appearances from the Balanescu Quartet, The London Community Gospel Choir and Dr. John.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/j9G7n8DBpO8?list=ALBTKoXRg38BAKXomg7XXzmVJOppXDjcHE" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Can  Tago Mago
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Can - Tago Mago" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-690" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/04-can-tago-mago.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Tago Mago is the third album by the German krautrock band Can, and was originally released as a double LP in 1971 by United Artists. It was the bands second studio album and the first to feature Kenji Damo Suzuki after their previous vocalist, Malcolm Mooney, quit the band in 1970 to return to the United States.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/IxGfuOmzfQo?rel=0" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Flower Travellin Band  Satori
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Flower Travellin' Band - Satori" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-691" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/05-Flower-Travellin-Band-Satori.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Satori is the second album by Japanese rock band Flower Travellin Band, but their first of original material.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/WAoI-ANlgpU?list=PLm6bsTDy9efIsprmPC1nqhxf97CXmmYkE" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          The Psychic Paramount  Gamelan-Into-the Mink Supernatural
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="The Psychic Paramount - Gamelan-Into-the Mink Supernatural" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-692" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/06-The-Psychic-Paramount-Gamelan-Into-the-Mink-Supernatural.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Not everyone will like this because its non-linear and avant garde, but its good noise-rock with a muddied sounds that can take time to hit crescendos, like in post-rock.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/q3Hpb9u5CFg" width="420">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Ghost  Lama Rabi Rabi
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Ghost - Lama Rabi Rabi" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-694" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/07-Ghost-Lama-Rabi-Rabi.jpg" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Ghost is an experimental rock group formed in Tokyo, Japan, in 1984. Core-member Masaki Batoh grew up in Kyoto, Japan where he attended a private school. During this time, he became interested in American and British rock music ranging from Bob Dylan and Pink Floyd to the Velvet Underground and Japanese rock bands such as the Taj Mahal Travellers and the Flower Travellin Band.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/9SugMYKGp2Q" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Parson Sound
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Parson Sound" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-703" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/08-parson-sound.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/8qPHqGydVVQ" width="420">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          International Harvester  Sovv-Gott Rose-Marie
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="International Harvester - Sovv-Gott Rose-Marie" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-704" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/09-Trad-Gras-and-Stenar-sov-gott-rose-marie.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/M6OxuAYPYB0" width="420">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          No-Neck Blues Band  Qvaris
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="No-Neck Blues Band - Qvaris" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-705" height="500" src="http://psychrock.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/10-Qvaris-No-Neck-Blues-Band.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The No-Neck Blues Band, also known as NNCK, is a seven-member free-form improvisational musical collective from New York City. Formed in 1992, the original band was of eight members (until John Fell Ryan left to join noise group, Excepter), and has practiced weekly in a space in Harlem since. Membership includes Dave Nuss, Keith Connolly, Dave Shuford, Jason Meagher, Pat Murano, Matt Heyner, and Mico.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/D6YVra3CbE4" width="420">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Honorable Mentions:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
          The Beat of the Earth (Self Titled)
         <br/>
          Flying Saucer Attack  Rural Psychedelia
         <br/>
          Spacemen 3: Playing With Fire
         <br/>
          Loop  Fade Out
         <br/>
          Comets on Fire  Avatar
         <br/>
          High Rise  Live
         <br/>
          Scenic  Acquatica
         <br/>
          Roy Montgomery  Allegory of Hearing
         <br/>
          Dungen  Ta Det Lugnt
         <br/>
          Gandalf  Gandalf
        </p>
