        <h1 class="headline">
         Raise the Roof: How to Plant a Rooftop Garden
        </h1>
        <div class="post-author">
         <p class="align-left">
          <i class="icon-time">
          </i>
          Posted on May 26, 2015 by
          <a href="http://commongroundgarden.org/?author=4" rel="author" title="Posts by Mia">

         <p class="align-right">

        <p>
         Gardening is one of the fastest-growing, and most-loved hobbies of our time. Urban dwellers are taking advantage of every space available to get their hands dirty growing their own food and flowers  even their own roofs. Custom Made, a site that connects
         <span class="s1">
          peoplewho want one-of-a-kind goodswith professional and passionateartisans, has created
         </span>
         an
         <a href="http://www.custommade.com/blog/rooftop-garden/" target="_blank">
          infographic entitled Raise the Roof: How to Plant a Rooftop Garden
         </a>
         , which details just how you can get a rooftop garden of your own.
         <span id="more-740">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         From structure concerns and weight limitations, to wind-proofing and crop selection, this
         <a href="http://www.custommade.com/blog/rooftop-garden/" target="_blank">
          infographic contains everything you need to know before starting your own rooftop garden
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre one of the many people who live in an urban area and only have a rooftop available for gardening,
         <a href="http://www.custommade.com/blog/rooftop-garden/" target="_blank">
          peruse the article, share the infographic, andand learn about how to expand your garden and raise your roof to be a bit greener.
         </a>
        </p>
