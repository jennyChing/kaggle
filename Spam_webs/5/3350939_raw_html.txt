        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         How Dangerous Is Your Deodorant?
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-10-14T10:15:59+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 14, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/author/healthperch/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Health Perch
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dangers-deodorant-header.jpg">
          <img alt="How Dangerous Is Your Deodorant?" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1422" height="400" src="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dangers-deodorant-header.jpg" width="1068"/>
         </a>
        </h2>
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          Plenty of speed sticks dont stack up. Heres what you need to know before you de-stink.
         </em>
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         When you think about natural beauty products, what do you envision? Mineral-free makeup? Soaps and shampoos without sketchy ingredients? Maybe an organic moisturizer? While these items can definitely fall under the umbrella of natural living, theres one go-to toiletry most of us dont consider: deodorant.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Swiping on scented, odor-blocking sweat stoppers seems innocent enough, especially when its a saving grace after a particularly sweaty subway ride or workout. Unfortunately, years of research suggest the ingredients used in many deodorants can actually be harmful to our health.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: center;">
         Blacklist: The Dangerous Ingredients Lurking in Deodorant
        </h3>
        <h5 style="text-align: left;">
         Parabens
        </h5>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.rodalenews.com/natural-deodorants" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.rodalenews.com/natural-deodorants', 'Parabens']);">
          Parabens
         </a>
         , preservative chemicals added to personal care products (as well as some processed foods), help prevent bacteria from growing. Parabens mimic estrogen, resulting in hormonal disruption previously inked to breast cancer and reproductive issues. According to a
         <a href="http://www.wiley.com/WileyCDA/PressRelease/pressReleaseId-102079.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.wiley.com/WileyCDA/PressRelease/pressReleaseId-102079.html', 'study']);">
          study
         </a>
         published in the
         <em>
          Journal of Applied Toxicology
         </em>
         , at least one form of paraben was present in samplings of breast tissue from 160 women who underwent mastectomies in the United Kingdom.
        </p>
        <h5 style="text-align: left;">
         Triclosan
        </h5>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Triclosan is another hormonally disruptive chemical found in deodorants that also appears in dish detergents and toothpaste. According to the National Health and Nutrition Examination
         <a href="http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/all.12058/abstract;jsessionid=63478618D1D394CD452F8BEE89FE2759.f03t04" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/all.12058/abstract;jsessionid=63478618D1D394CD452F8BEE89FE2759.f03t04', 'Survey']);">
          Survey
         </a>
         , overexposure can increase a childs risk for allergies. And to boot, a
         <a href="http://news.ucdavis.edu/search/news_detail.lasso?id=10301" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://news.ucdavis.edu/search/news_detail.lasso?id=10301', 'study']);">
          study
         </a>
         from the University of California-Davis finds the chemical can also impair muscle function. Following consumer protest, this toxin made headlines earlier this year when the
         <a href="http://www.fda.gov/forconsumers/consumerupdates/ucm205999.htm" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.fda.gov/forconsumers/consumerupdates/ucm205999.htm', 'FDA announced']);">
          FDA announced
         </a>
         plans to review the safety and efficacy of the ingredient in antibacterial soaps.
        </p>
        <h5 style="text-align: left;">
         Phthalates
        </h5>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Trailing behind triclosan are
         <a href="http://www.cdc.gov/biomonitoring/phthalates_factsheet.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.cdc.gov/biomonitoring/phthalates_factsheet.html', 'phthalates']);">
          phthalates
         </a>
         . While these chemicals are mostly used as plasticizers, or to make plastic more flexible, they appear in personal care products too. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), phthalates have been linked to learning disabilities, behavior problems and even pre-term birth.
        </p>
        <h5 style="text-align: left;">
         Aluminum
        </h5>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Aluminum isnt just used in foil to wrap leftoversits also another scary deodorant ingredient to look out for. A
         <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23916117" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23916117', 'study']);">
          study
         </a>
         published in the
         <em>
          Journal of Inorganic Biochemistry
         </em>
         compared the breast tissue of 19 breast cancer patients to that of healthy women. The researchers found the women with breast cancer had increased levels of aluminum, which the Centers for Disease Control consider a toxic substance. Per
         <a href="http://www.atsdr.cdc.gov/toxfaqs/tf.asp?id=190&amp;tid=34" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.atsdr.cdc.gov/toxfaqs/tf.asp?id=190&amp;tid=34', 'the CDCs website']);">
          the CDCs website
         </a>
         , exposure to high levels of aluminum may result in respiratory and neurological problems.
        </p>
        <h5 style="text-align: left;">
         Fragrances
        </h5>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Powder fresh and lavender fields may smell lovely, but these artificial fragrances can be problematic. A
         <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21480912" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21480912', 'study']);">
          study
         </a>
         published in the journal
         <em>
          Contact Dermatitis
         </em>
         found the (often unlabeled) ingredients manufacturers use to achieve a certain fragrance can cause contact allergies. And these fragrance mixes, according to the
         <a href="http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2006/03/060303204044.htm" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2006/03/060303204044.htm', 'Mayo Clinic']);">
          Mayo Clinic
         </a>
         , are some of the leading causes of dermatitis, which is inflammation resulting in swollen, red, and itchy skinnot quite the fresh feeling consumers hope for.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: center;">
         Dont Sweat The Small Stuff
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Taking a step back from commercial deodorant doesnt mean you have to commit to a lifetime of smelliness. Instead, there are natural, cleaner brands to choose from in an effort to minimize toxic exposure. For example, the gals who wrote
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/More-Dirty-Looks-Products-Cosmetics/dp/0738213969" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/More-Dirty-Looks-Products-Cosmetics/dp/0738213969', 'No More Dirty Looks']);">
          <em>
           No More Dirty Looks
          </em>
         </a>
         recommend certain brands, such as
         <a href="http://www.weleda.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.weleda.com/', 'Weleda']);">
          Weleda
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://soapwallakitchen.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://soapwallakitchen.com/', 'Soapwalla']);">
          Soapwalla
         </a>
         (
         <a href="http://nomoredirtylooks.com/2011/06/your-questions-answered-whats-the-best-natural-deodorant/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://nomoredirtylooks.com/2011/06/your-questions-answered-whats-the-best-natural-deodorant/', 'among others']);">
          among others
         </a>
         ), while
         <a href="http://wellandgood.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wellandgood.com/', 'others']);">
          others
         </a>
         highly approve of the brand
         <a href="http://wellandgood.com/2011/07/06/a-new-natural-deodorant-for-sweaty-summer-workouts/#axzz1RQRySmOb" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wellandgood.com/2011/07/06/a-new-natural-deodorant-for-sweaty-summer-workouts/#axzz1RQRySmOb', 'Pure Pitz']);">
          Pure Pitz
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         These options swap out toxic chemicals for essential oils, coconut oil, shea butter, and hops. Yep, you read that right: The ingredient you recognize from your happy-hour beer works naturally to
         <a href="http://www.tomsofmaine.com/ingredients/overlay/hops-extract-(co2)-and-caprylic-capric-triglyceride" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.tomsofmaine.com/ingredients/overlay/hops-extract-(co2)-and-caprylic-capric-triglyceride', 'stop the bacteria']);">
          stop the bacteria
         </a>
         that produces odor. In fact, Toms of Maine is one cleaner brand that uses it in just about all products. While it may take a little time and research to find a good fit for a new deodorant, the good news is that there are budget-friendly solutions.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: center;">
         To the Kitchen! DIY Deodorant
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Common household ingredientsthink baking soda, cornstarch and again, essential oilscan be whipped together to concoct a DIY (dare we say delicious-smelling) deodorant. Many recipes (like
         <a href="http://www.mindbodygreen.com/0-12842/diy-all-natural-coconut-deodorant.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mindbodygreen.com/0-12842/diy-all-natural-coconut-deodorant.html', 'this one from MindBodyGreen']);">
          this one from MindBodyGreen
         </a>
         ) also call for coconut oil, known for its
         <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24328700" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24328700', 'antibacterial properties']);">
          antibacterial properties
         </a>
         .
         <a href="http://wellnessmama.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wellnessmama.com', 'Wellness Mama']);">
          Wellness Mama
         </a>
         has similar coconut-inspired recipes
         <a href="http://wellnessmama.com/1523/natural-deodorant/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wellnessmama.com/1523/natural-deodorant/', 'here']);">
          here
         </a>
         . Bottom line: Deodorant can, and should be, included in the clean beauty conversation. Going
         <em>
          au natural
         </em>
         doesnt have to be, ahem  stinky.
