     <p>
      <b>
       Hortense Eugnie Ccile Bonaparte
      </b>
      (
      <small>
       French pronunciation:
      </small>
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">
        [ts eni sesil bnapat]
       </a>
      </span>
      ) (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N%C3%A9e" title="Ne">
       ne
      </a>
      <b>
       de Beauharnais
      </b>
      ,
      <small>
       pronounced:
      </small>
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">
        [d boan]
       </a>
      </span>
      ) (10 April 1783  5 October 1837),
      <b>
       Queen consort of Holland
      </b>
      , was the stepdaughter of Emperor
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I" title="Napoleon I">
       Napoleon I
      </a>
      , being the daughter of his first wife,
      <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9phine_de_Beauharnais" title="Josphine de Beauharnais">
       Josphine de Beauharnais
      </a>
      . She later became the wife of the former's brother,
      <a href="/wiki/Louis_Bonaparte" title="Louis Bonaparte">
       Louis Bonaparte, King of Holland
      </a>
      , and the mother of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_III_of_France" title="Napoleon III of France">
       Napoleon III, Emperor of the French
      </a>
      . She had also an illegitimate son,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_Auguste_Louis_Joseph,_duc_de_Morny" title="Charles Auguste Louis Joseph, duc de Morny">
       Charles Auguste Louis Joseph, duc de Morny
      </a>
      , by her lover
      <a href="/wiki/Charles_Joseph,_comte_de_Flahaut" title="Charles Joseph, comte de Flahaut">
       Charles Joseph, comte de Flahaut
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hortense_de_Beauharnais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Hortense was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">
       Paris
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
       France
      </a>
      on 10 April 1783, the daughter of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexandre,_Vicomte_de_Beauharnais" title="Alexandre, Vicomte de Beauharnais">
       Alexandre, Vicomte de Beauharnais
      </a>
      and Josphine Tascher de la Pagerie. Her parents separated shortly after her birth. Her father was executed on 23 July 1794, at the time of the
      <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">
       French Revolution
      </a>
      , a few days before the end of the
      <a href="/wiki/Reign_of_Terror" title="Reign of Terror">
       Reign of Terror
      </a>
      . Her mother was imprisoned in the
      <i>
       Carmelites
      </i>
      prison, from which she was released on 6 August 1794, thanks to the intervention of her best friend
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Th%C3%A9r%C3%A8se_Tallien" title="Thrse Tallien">
       Thrse Tallien
      </a>
      . Two years later, her mother married
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" title="Napoleon I of France">
       Napoleon Bonaparte
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Hortense was described as having been an amusing and pretty child with long, pale golden-blonde hair and blue eyes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-epton51_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-epton51-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She received her education at the school of Madame
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeanne_Campan" title="Jeanne Campan">
       Jeanne Campan
      </a>
      in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St-Germain-en-Laye" title="St-Germain-en-Laye">
       St-Germain-en-Laye
      </a>
      together with Napoleon's youngest sister
      <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Bonaparte" title="Caroline Bonaparte">
       Caroline Bonaparte
      </a>
      , who later married
      <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Murat" title="Joachim Murat">
       Joachim Murat
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-epton51_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-epton51-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She had an elder brother,
      <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_de_Beauharnais" title="Eugne de Beauharnais">
       Eugne de Beauharnais
      </a>
      . Hortense was an accomplished amateur musical composer and supplied the army of her stepfather with rousing marches, including
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Partant_pour_la_Syrie" title="Partant pour la Syrie">
        Partant pour la Syrie
       </a>
      </i>
      . She also enjoyed playing games and particularly excelled at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billiards" title="Billiards">
       billiards
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1802, at Napoleon's request, Hortense married his brother
      <a href="/wiki/Louis_Bonaparte" title="Louis Bonaparte">
       Louis Bonaparte
      </a>
      . The couple had three sons:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Charles_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Charles Bonaparte">
        Napolon Louis Charles Bonaparte
       </a>
       (10 October 1802 - 5 May 1807)
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Louis_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Louis Bonaparte">
        Napoleon Louis Bonaparte
       </a>
       (11 October 1804 - 17 March 1831)
      </li>
      <li>
       Charles Louis Napolon Bonaparte, later
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_III_of_France" title="Napoleon III of France">
        Napoleon III
       </a>
       , Emperor of the French (20 April 1808- 9 January 1873)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Queen_of_Holland_.281806.E2.80.931810.29">
       Queen of Holland (18061810)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hortense_de_Beauharnais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Queen of Holland (18061810)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote">
      See also:
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Dutch_consorts" title="List of Dutch consorts">
       List of Dutch consorts
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      In 1806, Napoleon appointed his brother Louis to be the King of Holland, and Hortense accompanied her husband to
      <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">
       The Hague
      </a>
      . Hortense's negativity towards being appointed Queen of Holland was twofold: First, it was necessary for her to move there with Louis, with whom she did not get along, and second, she had to leave her life as a celebrated member of Parisian society. She had hoped to be
      <i>
       "a Queen of Holland in Paris"
      </i>
      , but Napoleon did not agree. She was therefore forced to depart with Louis to the Netherlands, where she arrived on 18 June 1806.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:147px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Royal_Monogram_of_Hortense_de_Beauharnais,_Queen_of_Holland.svg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="314" data-file-width="245" height="186" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Royal_Monogram_of_Hortense_de_Beauharnais%2C_Queen_of_Holland.svg/145px-Royal_Monogram_of_Hortense_de_Beauharnais%2C_Queen_of_Holland.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Royal_Monogram_of_Hortense_de_Beauharnais%2C_Queen_of_Holland.svg/218px-Royal_Monogram_of_Hortense_de_Beauharnais%2C_Queen_of_Holland.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Royal_Monogram_of_Hortense_de_Beauharnais%2C_Queen_of_Holland.svg/290px-Royal_Monogram_of_Hortense_de_Beauharnais%2C_Queen_of_Holland.svg.png 2x" width="145"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Royal_Monogram_of_Hortense_de_Beauharnais,_Queen_of_Holland.svg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Royal Monogram
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Queen Hortense was pleasantly surprised
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inghist.nl_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-inghist.nl-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      by the warm welcome from the public. She quickly became accustomed to life in the Netherlands and came to like the country. She was present at official celebrations and ceremonies, visited the market places where she made large purchases, and was much liked by the public, which annoyed her husband. She learned water colour painting and made trips around the countryside. Nevertheless, she hated her stay there because of her bad relationship with Louis: The couple lived in different parts of the palace and avoided each other at every opportunity, with Hortense describing herself as a prisoner.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inghist.nl_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-inghist.nl-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1807, her son died; she was subsequently allowed to visit France as the climate there was considered better for her other son Louis-Napolon. She remained in France, again pleased by her status as queen at the French court, until 1810, when Napoleon forced her to return to the Netherlands at his new weddinghe did not consider it suitable to have the daughter of his former spouse at court. Hortense returned temporarily to the Netherlands, but on 1 June 1810, she was allowed to leave again on the pretext of her health.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1810, after his Dutch kingdom was taken away from him, Louis remained in Holland for nearly three years, turning to writing and poetry. Louis wrote to Napoleon after the latter's defeat in Russia to request that the Dutch throne be restored to him. However, Napoleon refused. Louis finally returned to France in 1813. He spent much of his later life in Italy.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Illegitimate_son">
       Illegitimate son
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hortense_de_Beauharnais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Illegitimate son">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hortense_de_Beauharnais.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4702" data-file-width="3826" height="246" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Hortense_de_Beauharnais.jpg/200px-Hortense_de_Beauharnais.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Hortense_de_Beauharnais.jpg/300px-Hortense_de_Beauharnais.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Hortense_de_Beauharnais.jpg/400px-Hortense_de_Beauharnais.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hortense_de_Beauharnais.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Portrait of Hortense painted by
        <a href="/wiki/Anne-Louis_Girodet_de_Roussy-Trioson" title="Anne-Louis Girodet de Roussy-Trioson">
         Anne-Louis Girodet de Roussy-Trioson
        </a>
        , 1808
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Hortense was now free to respond to the romantic overtures of the man whom she had long admired, Colonel
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_Joseph,_Comte_de_Flahaut" title="Charles Joseph, Comte de Flahaut">
       Charles Joseph, Comte de Flahaut
      </a>
      , a sophisticated, handsome man rumoured to be the illegitimate son of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Talleyrand" title="Talleyrand">
       Talleyrand
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      They soon became lovers. In 1811, at an unspecified inn in
      <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
       Switzerland
      </a>
      , close to
      <a href="/wiki/Lake_Geneva" title="Lake Geneva">
       Lake Geneva
      </a>
      , Hortense secretly gave birth to a son by de Flahaut,
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_Auguste_Louis_Joseph,_duc_de_Morny" title="Charles Auguste Louis Joseph, duc de Morny">
        Charles Auguste Louis Joseph
       </a>
       ( 21 October 1811 - 10 March 1865), created
       <b>
        Duke of Morny
       </b>
       by his half-brother, Napoleon III, in 1862.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Only her brother Eugne, Adlade Filleul de Souza, de Flahaut's mother, and her closest companions were aware of her pregnancy and the subsequent birth. She had used poor health to explain her prolonged visit to Switzerland, the journey having been arranged by Adlade. Hortense cleverly disguised her pregnancy (she was by then, in her sixth month), during the baptism of Napoleon's son,
      <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_II" title="Napoleon II">
       Napoleon II
      </a>
      when she was chosen to be one of the child's godmothers, an honour she shared with
      <a href="/wiki/Letizia_Ramolino" title="Letizia Ramolino">
       Madame Mre
      </a>
      , mother of the Emperor.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years">
       Later years
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hortense_de_Beauharnais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Later years">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Labhardt2.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="339" data-file-width="495" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Labhardt2.JPG/220px-Labhardt2.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Labhardt2.JPG/330px-Labhardt2.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Labhardt2.JPG/440px-Labhardt2.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Labhardt2.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Arenenberg
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      At the
      <a href="/wiki/Bourbon_Restoration" title="Bourbon Restoration">
       Bourbon Restoration
      </a>
      in 1814, Hortense received the protection of
      <a href="/wiki/Alexander_I_of_Russia" title="Alexander I of Russia">
       Alexander I of Russia
      </a>
      . At his instigation, she was granted the title of Duchess of Saint-Leu (
      <i>
       duchesse de Saint-Leu
      </i>
      ) by King
      <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVIII_of_France" title="Louis XVIII of France">
       Louis XVIII
      </a>
      on 30 May 1814.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      During the
      <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Days" title="Hundred Days">
       Hundred Days
      </a>
      , however, Hortense supported her stepfather and brother-in-law Napoleon. This led to her banishment from France after his final defeat. She traveled in
      <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
       Germany
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
       Italy
      </a>
      before purchasing the Chteau of
      <a href="/wiki/Arenenberg" title="Arenenberg">
       Arenenberg
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
       Swiss
      </a>
      canton of
      <a href="/wiki/Thurgau" title="Thurgau">
       Thurgau
      </a>
      in 1817. She lived there until her death on 5 October 1837, at the age of fifty-four. She is buried next to her mother Josphine in the
      <i>
       Saint-Pierre-Saint-Paul
      </i>
      church in
      <a href="/wiki/Rueil-Malmaison" title="Rueil-Malmaison">
       Rueil-Malmaison
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      A portrait of Hortense hangs at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ash_Lawn-Highland" title="Ash Lawn-Highland">
       Ash Lawn-Highland
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">
       Virginia
      </a>
      plantation home of
      <a href="/wiki/James_Monroe" title="James Monroe">
       James Monroe
      </a>
      , fifth
      <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">
       President of the United States
      </a>
      . It was one of three portraits given by Hortense to Monroe's daughter Eliza, who went to school with Hortense in France. (The other two portraits are of Hortense's brother Eugne de Beauharnais and of Madame Campan, the headmistress of the school attended by Hortense and Eliza.) Eliza's daughter, Hortensia Monroe Hay was named in honour of Hortense.
     </p>
