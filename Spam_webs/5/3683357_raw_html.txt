          <p>
           There is one thing that I have in common with this incredibly talented street artist from Ghent, Belgium and it is
           <b>
            the love, admiration and respect for animals
           </b>
           we both share.
           <a href="http://roaweb.tumblr.com/" target="_blank" title="ROA's Work on Tumblr">
            <b>
             ROA
            </b>
           </a>
           s street art is mainly representing animals, both wild and not, in the urban environment and sometimes how they adapted to it. His huge animal murals and the way he represents them is what has brought
           <b>
            ROA
           </b>
           to my attention.
          </p>
          <p>
           Once again Berlin has played an important role for me to discover
           <b>
            ROAs
           </b>
           street art, for starting to like his murals and wanting to know more about him. A lot of people define his street art morbid, not appropriate and in some cases they asked for it to be removed which I totally disagree with, as you might have guessed.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="ROAs hedgehog in London - Animal Realistic Street Art by ROA in Europe" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13564" height="769" src="http://www.angloitalianfollowus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/animal-realistic-street-art-by-roa-in-europe-01-1024x769.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           After having done a little bit of research I came to realize that the animals that
           <b>
            ROA
           </b>
           immortalizes on giant murals are all from the local environment. In fact he gets his inspiration by observing the area he has chosen to leave his street art signature, at times by getting to know its history helps too and, based on what he finds, he tries to pass on a message. I would define
           <b>
            ROA
           </b>
           both as a watcher and a reporter of what he discovers and sees around him.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Street art by ROA in East London - Animal Realistic Street Art by ROA in Europe" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13565" height="769" src="http://www.angloitalianfollowus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/animal-realistic-street-art-by-roa-in-europe-02-1024x769.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Creative But Still Close to Reality
          </h2>
          <p>
           The animals that
           <b>
            ROA
           </b>
           paints are incredibly well detailed and that is impressive if you ask me considering he is not painting on normal canvases but in a very large scale on a walls of public buildings or abandoned ones. I absolute adore how
           <b>
            ROAs
           </b>
           murals are incredibly precise, the animals are so extremely close to how they look in reality that its impossible to walk by and ignore them.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Street Art by ROA in Berlin - Animal Realistic Street Art by ROA in Europe" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13566" height="769" src="http://www.angloitalianfollowus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/animal-realistic-street-art-by-roa-in-europe-03-1024x769.jpg" width="1024"/>
           <br/>
           <img alt="Detail of a mural by ROA in Berli - Animal Realistic Street Art by ROA in Europe" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13567" height="769" src="http://www.angloitalianfollowus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/animal-realistic-street-art-by-roa-in-europe-04-1024x769.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </p>
          <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
          </div>
          <p>
           These animals are posing dead or alive, sometimes they are even in a decomposing state and what I love about the way they are represented is how
           <b>
            peaceful and calm
           </b>
           they look even when they are involved into a fight or an escape. To me the expression of these animals eyes is tranquil and inoffensive almost like if they have accepted their fate which in most cases is decided by humans and not themselves.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Rabbit by ROA in Zaragoza - Animal Realistic Street Art by ROA in Europe" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13568" height="769" src="http://www.angloitalianfollowus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/animal-realistic-street-art-by-roa-in-europe-05-1024x769.jpg" width="1024"/>
           <br/>
           <img alt="Fish by ROA in Zaragoza - Animal Realistic Street Art by ROA in Europe" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13569" height="1024" src="http://www.angloitalianfollowus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/animal-realistic-street-art-by-roa-in-europe-06-769x1024.jpg" width="769"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Another thing that I love about
           <b>
            ROAs
           </b>
           street art is that he uses
           <b>
            mainly black and white
           </b>
           paint, sometimes red or other vivid colors if he wants to represent the internal animals organs rotting away for instance. I know he isnt the only street art artist to paint black and white murals, but his style his very recognizable and it cannot be confused or misjudged. When I look at
           <b>
            ROAs
           </b>
           murals I think of death as an inevitable part of everyones life, Id like to think that is the reason why the animals have all such a calm expression. I dont personally see his street art as morbid and morose, not at all, but has a perfect recreation of the reality.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Mices mural by ROA in Berlin - Animal Realistic Street Art by ROA in Europe" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13570" height="769" src="http://www.angloitalianfollowus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/animal-realistic-street-art-by-roa-in-europe-07-1024x769.jpg" width="1024"/>
           <br/>
           <img alt="Detail of mices mural by ROA in Berlin - Animal Realistic Street Art by ROA in Europe" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13571" height="769" src="http://www.angloitalianfollowus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/animal-realistic-street-art-by-roa-in-europe-08-1024x769.jpg" width="1024"/>
           <br/>
           <img alt="Mural by ROA on a flat in Berlin - Animal Realistic Street Art by ROA in Europe" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13563" height="1024" src="http://www.angloitalianfollowus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/animal-realistic-street-art-by-roa-in-europe-09-769x1024.jpg" width="769"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            ROA
           </b>
           has traveled and left his signature in many countries in Europe but also in other places like New York, Africa, Mexico, Cambodia and more. Im so very fascinated by his works and by street art in general, it must be such a challenge every time to turn up somewhere and create incredible art pieces of the sort. I cannot imagine how extremely satisfying it must be for the artists to stand in front of their finished artwork, it must be an incredible feeling, dont you think so too?
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
