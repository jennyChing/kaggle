             <p>
              <a href="http://www.mymodernmet.com/profiles/blogs/romulo-celdran-macro-sculptures">
               <img class="align-full" src="http://api.ning.com:80/files/Ja6gu*i0lR7jGv-2a65qFEI7YMpyWEeYDXEVirxvMv5b5muagqUCyWBxvBc84*3UzXCPJjNsUs2FJcs9T4eDB3XNQKukYhoT/romuloceldran2.jpg" width="721"/>

             </p>
             <p>
              Artist
              <a href="http://www.romuloceldran.com/" target="_blank">
               Rmulo Celdrn
              </a>
              reimagines everyday objects as larger-than-life sculptures for his playful series called
              <em>
               Macro
              </em>
              . Things like bottle caps, ice cube trays, and sponges are scaled hundreds of times bigger for the sculpture series. Sometimes, this makes them well over seven feet tall.
             </p>
             <p>
              The gigantic sizing acts like a magnifying glass for these objects. By blowing up the small details, we see characteristics of the item that wed normally miss because theyre so small. At this oversized scale, the zig-zag surface pattern on a hot water bottle becomes much more pronounced, as does the joints of clothes pins.              </p>

             <p>

             <p>
              Artist
              <a href="http://dinabrodsky.com" target="_blank">

              </a>

Despite the fact that these canvases can fit into the palm of your hand, Brodsky's work is both detailed and lifelike. Each intricate leaf, distinct building, and vivid light source stands out amidst the landscapes that she expertly reproduces.
             </p>
             <p>
              Typically, it will take the painter 15-20 hours to meticulously design her small-scale worlds. During this time, she sources inspiration fromItalian Renaissance fresco paintings, 15th-century Flemish portraits, and Islamic miniature art. When combined, all three of these techniques culminate in paintings that appear to be from another time period.

              <h3 class="entry-title">
               <a href="http://www.mymodernmet.com/profiles/blogs/gretchen-roehrs-fashion-illustrations?context=tag-art">
                Fashion Illustrations Utilize Colorful Food Items for a Finishing Touch
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="entry-byline">
               Posted by
               <a href="/members/AnnaGragert">
                Anna Gragert
               </a>
               on July 3, 2015 at 9:30am
              </div>
             </div>
            </header>
            <section class="entry-content cf">
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.mymodernmet.com/profiles/blogs/gretchen-roehrs-fashion-illustrations">
               <img class="align-full" src="http://api.ning.com:80/files/YTFvh9JoYPFUmZDrVjT4iD9xdDGPrjkq5rNF1y5IM4hL4tN6hqPHJW6xKAIg9VdK-dQDFBh7mvKu34eMp8wHm2R1i-dYTygc/Fashion10.jpeg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Artist
              <a href="https://instagram.com/groehrs/">
               Gretchen Rehrs
              </a>
              completes her playful fashion illustrations by utilizing colorful food items, as a finishing touch. From a rustic slice of bread to leafy greens, Rehrs has the ability to transform these edibles into pieces that are reminiscent of structured, flowing, and shapely articles of clothing.
             </p>
             <p>
              In each illustration, the foods are manipulated so that they mirror the human body's many curves and angles. The twisted banana, deep red cherries, layered artichoke, and other provisions add a naturalistic texture to the sketches. This allows them to come to life, especially when Rehrs's characters appear to be out and about in a chic metropolis. The creative drawings give a whole new perspective of food that is all at once playful and artistic.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img class="align-full" src="http://api.ning.com:80/files/YTFvh9JoYPFbqQAfBKrItcUIHj96R1TgY6t0qBoEh*vvKMlxb2EZVzYPHSNmZFfCys1-2cMJmhhG0cp4j3xUSL8yOMHsAO1p/Fashion1.jpeg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img class="align-full" src="http://api.ning.com:80/files/YTFvh9JoYPHSh5N2Mijkp1TQjW8r8aZIBBTEwisCBNM5pT6E3Z*4cW8KTtCzLH69YpyJh6acbzlW3UPh1hN61HgoQE9IpL64/Fashion2.jpeg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>

             </p>
