         <h1 class="entry-title">
          These Were Michael Vicks Fighting Dogs. Where They Are Now is Beautiful.
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-header-social clear row">

        <div class="entry-author clear">
         <a href="http://barkpost.com/author/stacie/" title="Stacie Grissom">
          <img alt="Stacie Grissom" class="avatar avatar-60 wp-user-avatar wp-user-avatar-60 photo avatar-default" height="60" src="http://0.gravatar.com/avatar/6537568dad099b45fd59123814a77017?s=60&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g" width="60"/>

         <h2>
          <a href="http://barkpost.com/author/stacie/" title="Stacie Grissom">
           Stacie Grissom
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p class="post-time">
          1 year ago
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          *Update April 2015: A few more pups have left this world, but still one of the most beautiful stories of hope, survival, love, and triumph.*
         </p>
         <p>
          In honor of national
          <a href="http://www.aspca.org/dogfighting" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
           Dog Fighting Awareness Day through the ASPCA
          </a>
          , we wanted to feature a few pups who overcame all the odds and proved that these pups are not vicious killers theyre just regular dogs whove been put through extreme circumstances.
         </p>
         <p>
          The pups weve featured below are a few of the Vicktory Dogs, the dogs who were rescued from Atlanta Falcon Michael Vicks dog fighting ring back in 2007. When the dogs were discovered in Vicks home, many people said even though the situation was horrible, these dogs should be put down because of their obvious violent natures and killer instincts.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12281" style="width: 510px">
          <img alt="Aerial shot of Michael Vick's home with the dogs out back." class="size-full wp-image-12281" height="350" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/MVick5.jpg" width="500"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Aerial shot of Michael Vicks home with the dogs out back. Photo from
           <a href="http://pilotonline.com/" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
            Pilot Online
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Organizations like the Best Friends Animal Sanctuary stepped in and tried to argue that there was still hope for these lost creatures.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="michael_vick_sports_illustrated" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12279" height="420" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/michael_vick_sports_illustrated.gif" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Almost 8 years later, the Vicktory dogs have some incredible stories of perseverance and hope. Best Friends Animal Sanctuary took in 22 dogs and set out to prove that these dogs could be turned around they could live normal lives in cozy homes. Below are snippets about 9 of those pups, snippets that show the flexible, fiercely-loving natures that live inside every dog.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           1. Cherry
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Cherry went from a dog who slept with one eye open to a cuddly pup who goes to events and gets the word out that abused animals arent inherently evil. People watched him grow as one of the tough pups on the show Dogtown and
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/Vicktory4cherry" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
           now hes a bit of a celebrity
          </a>
          . Even though he was bred and taught to fight, today thats the last thing he wants to do. He prefers constant snuggles with his family including his canine sister and his cats.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12265" style="width: 785px">
          <img alt="Photo source: Best Friends" class="size-full wp-image-12265" height="500" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SICherry.jpg" width="775"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/Cherry-the-Vicktory-dog/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2. Georgia
          </strong>
          <br/>
          One thing that surprised Georgias adopter, Amy, when she came to her new home was how quickly Georgia adapted to a loving family life. Georgias favorite thing to do was go on walks and she taught Amy powerful lessons on forgiveness, love, and learning to trust again. (Georgia passed away in 2013 but her legacy will always live on.)
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12266" style="width: 785px">
          <img alt="Photo source: Best Friends" class="size-full wp-image-12266" height="500" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SIGeorgia0097.jpg" width="775"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/Georgia-the-Vicktory-dog/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3. Halle
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Halles story continues to reiterate that no one should give up on a dog because of their past. Today Halle lives with two dog brothers and constantly plays with them. One of her brothers climbs on Halle, sits on her, bites her back legs to play and she hasnt gotten angry at him once. Whenever she sees another dog, her tail starts wagging and she does a happy dance. The first Vicktory dog to be adopted, Halle and her human started a dog enrichment program for shelter dogs called
          <a href="http://jasmineshouse.org/dogfighting-rescue/vicktory-dog-halle/" rel="external nofollow">
           Halles Enrichment Club
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12268" style="width: 785px">
          <img alt="Photo source: Best Friends" class="size-full wp-image-12268" height="500" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SIJuly2009024.jpg" width="775"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/Halle-the-Vicktory-dog/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           4. Handsome Dan
          </strong>
          <br/>
          When Handsome Dan first came out of Michael Vicks home, he was very skittish and fearful. As hes grown older, Dan learned how to be more trusting. In 2010 his family welcomed a baby (human) sister to the family and Handsome Dan, like many pitties, was the pawfect nanny dog.
         </p>
         <p>
          Today Handsome Dan has a
          <a href="http://www.handsomedansrescue.org/" rel="external nofollow">
           dog rescue named after him
          </a>
          and he has
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/handsomedan10" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
           his own Facebook page
          </a>
          with almost 600k likes. Handsome Dan also received the 2014 Barkie Award for the
          <a href="http://barkpost.com/barkies/" target="_blank">
           Best Dog on the Interne
          </a>
          t!
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12274" style="width: 785px">
          <img alt="Photo source: Best Friends" class="size-full wp-image-12274" height="500" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SIStubbyDogPhotoShoot.jpg" width="775"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/Handsome-Dan-the-Vicktory-dog/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5. Little Red
          </strong>
          <br/>
          The five years Little Red spent in Michael Vicks dog fighting ring, she was used as a bait dog. Bait animals are animals used to test a dogs fighting skills dogs like Little Red are often mauled or killed in the process. Little Reds owner, Susan, never thought that Little Red needed to be rehabilitated, instead the pup needed time to recover. After being rescued from the dog fighting ring, Little Red lives a life full of treats and zooming around 6 acres of pasture.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12267" style="width: 910px">
          <img alt="little red vicktory" class="aligncenter" height="598" src="http://i.huffpost.com/gen/1730267/thumbs/o-MACKENZIE-900.jpg" width="900"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/Little-Red-the-Vicktory-dog/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           6. Oliver
          </strong>
          <br/>
          The thing that Olivers adoptive parents were most surprised about after they brought him home was how willing the Pit pup allowed his new family to love him to pieces. Oliver had no fear in his new home or surroundings, despite the fact that his original evaluation stated that he was extremely fearful of people. Oliver would jump on his mom Erikas lap and shower her with kisses.
         </p>
         <p>
          Oliver passed away from cancer in 2009. He died in Erikas arms.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12270" style="width: 785px">
          <img alt="Photo source: Best Friends" class="size-full wp-image-12270" height="500" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SIOliverFamilyPhoto2009.jpg" width="775"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/Oliver-the-Vicktory-dog/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           7. Squeaker
          </strong>
          <br/>
          When new people meet Squeaker they continuously say, Wow I cant believe a former fighting dog is so sweet. Even though the beginning of her life included violent interactions with dogs, today Squeaker lives with her adoptive human family and their pack of 6 additional pups. Her adopter says that Squeaker also stares at you like she is in love with you. Her eyes are filled with love and tell a story.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12273" style="width: 785px">
          <img alt="Photo source: Best Friends" class="size-full wp-image-12273" height="500" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SISqueaker2.jpg" width="775"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/Squeaker-the-Vicktory-dog/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           8. Mel
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Richard Hunter, Mels adoptive pop, was a
          <a href="http://blogs.dallasobserver.com/sportatorium/2010/06/one_of_michael_vicks_dogs_find.php" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
           radio show host in Dallas back in 2007
          </a>
          . Richard reported on the gruesome details of the Michael Vick dog fighting story and became emotionally invested in the well-being of the pups. When the opportunity came to adopt one of the Vicktory dogs, Richard jumped at the chance and drove to pick up a very scared Mel at the Best Friends sanctuary in Utah. Today Mel continues to recover and goes on car rides and sleeps on the pillow with his pop.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12269" style="width: 785px">
          <img alt="Photo source: Best Friends" class="size-full wp-image-12269" height="500" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SIMel.jpg" width="775"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/Mel-the-Vicktory-dog/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           9. Oscar
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Oscars mom, Rachel, never set out to adopt a Vicktory dog. She just wanted a Pit Bull. But after Oscar came into her life, Rachel realized she didnt just adopt a dog. She herself was adopted into the Vicktory dog family where everyone stays in close contact swapping stories, advice, and support to spread the word that unconditional love, forgiveness, and a bit of understanding goes a long, long way.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12271" style="width: 785px">
          <img alt="Photo source: Best Friends" class="size-full wp-image-12271" height="500" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SIPostParty201206.jpg" width="775"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/Oscar-the-Vicktory-dog/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           10. Curly
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Curly still lives with his Best Friends family as the head dog over at the Dogtown Management office. Although he still struggles a bit when in unfamiliar territory, he is gaining confidence with the help of his best dog pal, fellow Vicktory dog Mya. In the summertime, Curly enjoys splashing about in the pool.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_40632" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/curly1.jpg">
           <img alt="Best Friends" class="size-full wp-image-40632" height="718" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/curly1.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo source:
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/Get-involved/Donate/Sponsor-an-Animal/Curly/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           11. Layla
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Sweet Layla passed her Canine Good Citizen test in February 2013, which is a huge deal for the fearful pup who had trouble relaxing due to her past. When she first arrived at Best Friends, Layla would shy away from people, but today she has
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/VicktoryForLayla/" rel="external nofollow">
           her own Facebook page
          </a>
          , where fans can follow along on her journey through recovery.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_40650" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SITamaraLaylaJacque67374-e1428509445661.jpg">
           <img alt="layla vicktory dog" class="size-full wp-image-40650" height="413" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/SITamaraLaylaJacque67374-e1428509445661.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           <a href="http://bestfriends.org/News-And-Features/News/A-Vicktory-for-Layla/" rel="external nofollow">
            Best Friends
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>

            visit the ASPCA
