   <b>
    Explanation:
   </b>
   One of the largest sunspot groups in recent years is now crossing the Sun.

Labelled
   <a href="http://www.spaceweatherlive.com/en/news/view/35/20141020-active-aurora-sunspot-region-2192">
    Active Region 2192
   </a>
   , it has already thrown a
   <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KQMS7-0Sp-A">
    powerful solar flare
   </a>
   and has the potential to produce more.

The
   <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J19vi8UPKck">
    featured video
   </a>
   shows a time lapse sequence of the Sun in
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_Dynamics_Observatory#Atmospheric_Imaging_Assembly_.28AIA.29">
    visible and ultraviolet light
   </a>
   taken yesterday and incorporating the previous 48 hours.

AR 2192, rotating in from the left, rivals
   <a href="ap090106.html">
    Jupiter
   </a>
   in size and is literally crackling with magnetic energy. 

The
   <a href="http://sdo.gsfc.nasa.gov/data/">
    active Sun
   </a>
   has caused some
   <a href="ap140714.html">
    spectacular auroras
   </a>
   in recent days, 
and energetic particles originating from
   <a href="http://www.universetoday.com/115473/awesome-photo-shows-monster-sunspot-aiming-our-way/">
    AR 2192
   </a>
   may help continue them over the next week.

Tomorrow, the Sun will appear unusual for even another reason: a
   <a href="ap140430.html">
    partial solar eclipse
   </a>
   will be
   <a href=" http://www.nasa.gov/content/goddard/how-to-safely-watch-the-october-23-partial-solar-eclipse/index.html">
    visible before sunset
   </a>
   from much of North America.
  </p>
