               Allergic Asthma in Adults
              </div>
              <h1>
               9 Everyday Asthma Symptom Soothers
              </h1>
              <h2>
               Lifestyle changes for asthma are a given, but they don't have to be complicated or time-consuming. Try these fast and effective asthma soothing ideas today.
              </h2>
             </div>
             <div class="bottom">

               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  1
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   Making small, simple changes can help limit your asthma symptoms and minimize episode triggers in a big way. Around your house, for example, there are a number of things you can do  from getting rid of dust mites and mold to
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/asthma/living-with-pets-and-asthma.aspx">
                     keeping pets out of your bedroom
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   , according to allergy and asthma specialist Richard J. Lee, MD, of Advanced Specialty Care in Danbury, Conn. Read on for more asthma-soothing tips that can leave you breathing easier.
                  </p>
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="printslide galleria-container">
               <div class="galleria-stage">
                <img alt="asthma 2" class="slideshow-image" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-02-pg-full.jpg" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/blank.gif"/>
                <img alt="asthma 2" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-02-pg-th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Have a Cup of Coffee
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  2
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   Start your day with a steamy cup of coffee and you could savor up to four hours of better airway function. That's what a recent analysis of seven small studies, published in the
                   <em>
                    Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews
                   </em>
                   , part of the Cochrane Library, concluded. What's the secret to
                   <a href="/asthma-pictures/allergic-asthma-management-tips.aspx">
                    asthma relief
                   </a>
                   ? The chemical structure of caffeine is a lot like theophylline, an asthma drug used to relax airway muscles. It reduces respiratory muscle fatigue, which can help with wheezing and shortness of breath. One caution about coffee: Avoid caffeine before lung function tests, as it could skew the results.
                  </p>
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="printslide galleria-container">
               <div class="galleria-stage">
                <img alt="asthma 3" class="slideshow-image" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-03-pg-full.jpg" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/blank.gif"/>
                <img alt="asthma 3" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-03-pg-th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Get a Magnesium Boost
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  3
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   Not getting enough magnesium may worsen asthma symptoms. According to a study from Bastyr University in Kenmore, Wash., magnesium supplements can work as a natural asthma soother. Study participants included 55 men and women, ages 21 to 55, with mild to moderate asthma. For six and a half months, one group took a 170 milligram magnesium supplement twice a day, while the other group did not. The group who took magnesium reported better breathing,
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/asthma/allergic-asthma.aspx">
                     better control of their asthma
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   , and better quality of life overall.
                  </p>
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="printslide galleria-container">
               <div class="galleria-stage">
                <img alt="asthma 4" class="slideshow-image" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-04-pg-full.jpg" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/blank.gif"/>
                <img alt="asthma 4" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-04-pg-th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Change Your Toothpaste
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  4
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   It seems strange (and evidence is anecdotal), but certain toothpaste ingredients can
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/sanjay-gupta/when-allergies-trigger-asthma.aspx">
                     trigger asthma
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   for some people. Switching formulas or brands might help. An often cited report from the
                   <em>
                    New England Journal of Medicine
                   </em>
                   describes a young woman who found her asthma reactions were due to the wintergreen flavoring in her paste-based toothpaste. The symptoms went away when she switched to a spicy gel formula.
                  </p>
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="printslide galleria-container">
               <div class="galleria-stage">
                <img alt="asthma 5" class="slideshow-image" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-05-pg-full.jpg" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/blank.gif"/>
                <img alt="asthma 5" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-05-pg-th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Stay Awake from Smoke
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  5
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   You probably know you shouldn't smoke or let people light up inside your house, but smoke from a wood stove or fireplace could cause you just as much trouble. Hot, dry air tends to
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/allergic-asthma-in-adults/allergic-asthma-prepare-for-unexpected-triggers.aspx">
                     trigger asthma
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   . On top of that, wood stoves emit volatile organic acids that cause even more drying. Pellet stoves, according to Dr. Lee, are slightly better for some people, in part because they're well sealed. Keep in mind that smoke from cooking can also trigger asthma, so run the exhaust fan to clear out kitchen fumes.
                  </p>
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="printslide galleria-container">
               <div class="galleria-stage">
                <img alt="asthma 6" class="slideshow-image" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-06-pg-full.jpg" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/blank.gif"/>
                <img alt="asthma 6" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-06-pg-th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Give Dust Mites the Boot
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  6
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/sanjay-gupta/allergic-asthma-in-adults.aspx">
                     Allergic asthma
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   is often triggered by microscopic dust mites in your home. One of the easiest and fastest steps you can take to keep these tiny insects under control is to cover bedding with mite-proof barriers, zippered covers in which you encase your mattress and pillows. Then wash all your linens at least once a week in hot water  150 degrees; anything less won't kill the critters. You can't eliminate dust mites altogether, but you can make your bed into a mite-free zone for asthma relief.
                  </p>
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="printslide galleria-container">
               <div class="galleria-stage">
                <img alt="asthma 7" class="slideshow-image" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-07-pg-full.jpg" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/blank.gif"/>
                <img alt="asthma 7" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-07-pg-th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Control Your Climate
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  7
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   Keeping the humidity in the air in your home between 30 and 50 percent can help asthma symptoms. Any higher, and dust mites thrive. Mold also grows in high humidity. If your home is damp and humid, clean with fungicides and use a dehumidifier, an air filtration system, or central air conditioning. On the other hand, if the air is too dry, your respiratory tract reacts. You may actually need to add back some humidity into the air during
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/sanjay-gupta/allergic-asthma-and-seasonal-allergies.aspx">
                     dry, winter months
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   .
                  </p>
                  <div class="galleria-credits">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="printslide galleria-container">
               <div class="galleria-stage">
                <img alt="asthma 8" class="slideshow-image" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-08-pg-full.jpg" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/blank.gif"/>
                <img alt="asthma 8" class="gallery-thumbnail" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/everyday-asthma-symptom-soothers-08-pg-th.jpg" style="display:none;"/>
               </div>
               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Clear Out Cockroaches and Ladybugs
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  8
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   Cockroaches are one of the worst
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/sanjay-gupta/when-allergies-trigger-asthma.aspx">
                     allergy and asthma triggers
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   , says Lee, and getting an exterminator to eliminate them is worth your while. But another insect trigger you may not know of is the ladybug. "And ladybugs cross-react with cockroaches," Lee adds. Whenever ladybugs arrive in droves, they settle around the house and then break down into a dust-like powder that's a powerful allergen. It might sound mean, especially to seemingly innocent ladybugs, but Lee recommends that you vacuum them.
                  </p>

                  </div>

               <div class="galleria-info">
                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Get Reacquainted With a Dust Cloth
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  9
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   A review of vacuum cleaners, including ones with HEPA (high-efficiency particulate air) filters, published in the journal
                   <em>
                    Environmental Science &amp; Technology
                   </em>
                   , found that although vacuums can suck up dust bunnies and
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/sanjay-gupta/allergic-asthma-and-seasonal-allergies.aspx">
                     other allergens
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   that make you sneeze and wheeze, some work better than others. Both the machines and the act of vacuuming itself stir up allergens, worsening your exposure and your reactions. Furthermore, bacteria can live in the vacuum cleaner bag for up to two months. Those same bacteria get reintroduced into the air every time you turn on the machine. To help your asthma, try using a dust cloth with furniture spray or a cloth sweeper that attracts and holds fine particles  it may be more effective and less expensive. If you rely on a vacuum cleaner, even in part, consider upgrading to one with the latest technology, make sure it works correctly, and change the filter and bag regularly.
                  </p>

                <div class="galleria-info-text">
                 <h3 class="galleria-info-title">
                  Keep Your Distance from Kitty Litter
                 </h3>
                 <div class="galleria-info-id">
                  10
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-type">
                  image
                 </div>
                 <div class="galleria-info-description">
                  <p>
                   Pets are a common source of allergens  cats more than dogs. Although the best asthma relief advice might be to find your furry friend another loving home, few people want to make that change. So, to
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/asthma/living-with-pets-and-asthma.aspx">
                     live with a cat
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   and be as wheeze-free as possible, consider making some key changes. Many people are allergic to cat urine, says Lee, so keep the litter box in a remote area of your home and put someone else in charge of emptying it. Cat dander and cat saliva also often provoke asthma reactions. When a cat licks and cleans itself, particles of saliva float on air currents for six to eight hours. To limit dander, use a dry cat shampoo weekly to help remove excess dander from the cat. That way, it's less likely to stir up around the house.
                  </p>
