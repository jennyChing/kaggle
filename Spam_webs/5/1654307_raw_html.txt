       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Hawaii Restaurant Review:  Cafe 100
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2014-09-16T07:00:09+00:00">
         September 16, 2014
        </span>
        By
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/author/Mrs.Diner" rel="author">
           Mrs. Diner
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/2014/09/hawaii-restaurant-review-cafe-100.html#comments">
          4 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="pf-content">
         <p>
          Aloha! Im back with another review from paradise. Local Hawaiian food isnt known for being health-conscious, and this well-liked eatery in Hilo is artery-clogging, but well-known for their local food.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100.jpg">
           <img alt="Cafe 100" class="aligncenter size-Default wp-image-4025" height="450" src="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-600x450.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.cafe100.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Cafe 100
          </a>
          has been around for over 60 years, serving up cheap plate lunches and their specialty, Loco Moco. Whats Loco Moco, you ask? The basic Loco Moco is white rice topped with a hamburger patty, fried egg, and gravy. There are quite a few variations and Cafe 100 serves up at least a dozen different kinds.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its a super casual walk-up cafe with just a couple order windows and some covered outdoor seating. Food is served on paper plates and in plastic to-go boxes. The line wasnt long when we were there, but I hear it can be sometimes. When we were there, there were several friendly regulars, all ordering the Loco Moco.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-Portuguese-Sausage-Loco.jpg">
           <img alt="Cafe 100 Portuguese Sausage Loco" class="aligncenter size-Default wp-image-4023" height="550" src="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-Portuguese-Sausage-Loco-498x550.jpg" width="498"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            Portuguese Sausage Loco Moco
           </strong>
           ($3.55)  Rice topped with brown gravy, fried egg, &amp; sliced of Portuguese sausage.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-Spam-Moco-No-Egg.jpg">
           <img alt="Cafe 100 Spam Moco No Egg" class="aligncenter size-Default wp-image-4024" height="450" src="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-Spam-Moco-No-Egg-600x450.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Spam Loco, No Egg
           </strong>
           ($3.55)  Rice, brown gravy, &amp; Spam. Yep, you can order it however you want it, but I think if youre going for Loco Moco, get the egg!
           <br/>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Cafe 100 Beef Teriyaki" class="aligncenter wp-image-4027 size-Default" height="550" src="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-Beef-Teriyaki-445x550.jpg" width="445"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Cafe 100 Beef Teriyaki" class="aligncenter wp-image-4026 size-Default" height="450" src="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-Beef-Teriyaki-3-600x450.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-Beef-Teriyaki.jpg">
           <br/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            Beef Teriyaki with Rice &amp; Tossed Salad or Potato Mac Salad
           </strong>
           ($7.45)  I love Teri Beef, but this was a little on the dry side. The portions were huge, though. I wouldnt recommend the tossed salad at allas you can see, its shredded similarly to a cole slaw, but with a different dressing. The Potato Mac Salad is a hybrid of potato &amp; macaroni salad.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-Keiki-Bento.jpg">
           <img alt="Cafe 100 Keiki Bento" class="aligncenter size-Default wp-image-4022" height="550" src="http://www.ratetheplateutah.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Cafe-100-Keiki-Bento-582x550.jpg" width="582"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Keiki Bento
           </strong>
           ($4.75)  Nice combination of Beef Teriyaki, Fried Chicken Drumettes, Smoked Sausages or Spam, Rice, &amp; a sampling of fish cake, ume fruit, &amp; takuwan.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          If youre in the Hilo area and want to give Cafe 100 a try, Id stick to the Loco Moco. You could go for the classic with a hamburger patty or choose from several different meats. Its definitely a Hawaiian comfort food. For plate lunches, go somewhere else. I thought Zippys and L &amp; L Hawaiian BBQ had better plate lunches than Cafe 100. And actually, we werent really impressed with anything at Cafe 100. Other Hilo eateries that serve Loco Moco include Blanes Drive Inn and Kens House of Pancakes. Ive heard great things about both of these, but havent been. Have you?
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           Cafe 100
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <span class="street-address">
           969 Kilauea Ave
          </span>
          <span class="locality">
           <br/>
           Hilo, HI
           <span class="hidden-xs">
            96720
            <br/>
            (808) 935-8683
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
