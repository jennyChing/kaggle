        <h2>
         12 Days of DIY Gifts in a Jar: Reeses Pieces Cookies in a Jar
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/pin/295971006736233047/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Reese's Pieces Cookies in a Jar 1" class="aligncenter wp-image-26907 size-full" height="480" src="http://www.freebiefindingmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Reesescookesinajar.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          MATERIALS
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         1 box of chocolate cake mix (any brand)
         <br/>
         1 bag of Reeses Pieces Mini Baking Bits
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000VDRAVI/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000VDRAVI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=monandsavhel-20&amp;linkId=5AQ7YGLEOPHPPICMrs%2Caps%2C167&amp;tag=monandsavhel-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Daps&amp;linkId=PTKWNTNPGXXL7UTS" target="_blank">
          1 large wide mouth Mason jar
         </a>
         <br/>
         Double-sided tape
         <br/>
         Gift tag (available to print
         <a href="http://www.freebiefindingmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Reeses_Cookies_in_a_Jar_Printable_Gift_Tag1.pdf" target="_blank">
          here)
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.freebiefindingmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Reesescookiesinajar4.jpg">
          <br/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/pin/295971006736233047/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Reese's Pieces Cookies in a Jar 2" class="aligncenter wp-image-26916 size-full" height="426" src="http://www.freebiefindingmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Reesescookiesinajar41.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="Standard">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          DIRECTIONS
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="Standard">
         1. Add the box of chocolate cake mix to the wide mouth Mason jar. Tap the jar a few times on the counter to get the cake mix to settle.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Slowly pour in the Reeses Pieces Mini Baking Bits. You may need to tap the jar again a few times to get them all to fit.
        </p>
        <p class="Standard">
         <a href="http://www.freebiefindingmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Reeses_Cookies_in_a_Jar_Printable_Gift_Tag1.pdf" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Reesescookiesinajar2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26909" height="176" src="http://www.freebiefindingmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Reesescookiesinajar2.jpg" width="267"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="Standard">
         3. Print
         <a href="http://www.freebiefindingmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Reeses_Cookies_in_a_Jar_Printable_Gift_Tag.pdf" target="_blank">
          this gift tag
         </a>
         and secure it to the side of the jar with double-sided tape. I added a small piece of brown paper (old grocery bag) to the back of my label and taped both onto the jar. You could also use a single hole punch and secure the label with twine.
        </p>
        <p class="Standard">
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/pin/295971006736233047/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Reese's Pieces Cookies in a Jar 3" class="aligncenter wp-image-26918 size-full" height="404" src="http://www.freebiefindingmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Reesescookiesinajarfinal.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This DIY gift in a jar Mason jar recipe only requires the addition of two other ingredients (eggs and vegetable oil) to make mouth watering chocolate peanut butter cookies. Maybe if youre really nice the person you gave it to will invite you over to sample the finished product! Yum!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <strong>
           If you give this DIY gift in a jar to someone let me know how they like it in the comments. Also, please share your favorite Mason jar recipes in the comments, Im always looking for new ideas. Happy holidays!
          </strong>
         </em>
