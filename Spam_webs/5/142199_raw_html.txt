       <h1 class="hLargeTitle">
        Mann On Men: Stop Clucking Like Hens
       </h1>
       <h2 class="hSubTitle">
       </h2>
       <div class="author clearfix">
        <div class="authorInfo">
         <img alt="" src="http://images.askmen.com/authors/responsive/AskMen-Contributor-2.png" width="32"/>

       <i>
        <b>
         In 1959, men lived by certain codes and protocols. That's why Dick Mann is shocked when he sees how carelessly men comport themselves in 2010. Every week Dick comments on the men in today's society  from his 1959 perspective  taking note and giving pointers on everything from etiquette, broads and style to the way a real man should walk through life.
        </b>
       </i>
       <p>
        During Korea, I was stationed with this guy nicknamed "Speak Easy." The name had nothing to do with an affinity for gin joints and
        <a href="http://www.askmen.com/top_10/entertainment/top-10-jazz-standards.html" omni_link="jazz" title="jazz">
         jazz
        </a>
        . He just had this easy manner in which he blabbed his mouth. This cat spilled the beans about everything going on in his life in and out of the uni: his wife, his daddys love of ladies' underwear and even the size of the bombs he dropped in the latrine.
       </p>
       <p>
        The rest of the platoon joked that if the enemy ever got a hold of Speak Easy the country would lose the war that day because he'd spit up every military secret, including where the rest of us 1st Cavalry Division were hiding and even what MacArthur had for breakfast. Loose lips sink ships. Thankfully Speak Easy wasnt in the Navy. The guy just had a hard time dummying up, but we were kids at war, and so he was one of our only sources of entertainment. Speak Easy was a favorite among the guys.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h3>
        Spreading the word
       </h3>
       I was reminded of old Speak Easy when my barber told me his son was
       <a href="http://www.askmen.com/money/investing_300/318_lessons-from-dennis-hoppers-divorce.html" omni_link="getting a divorce" title="getting a divorce">
        getting a divorce
       </a>
       . I expected him to paint a picture of a heart-to-heart over a beer or maybe a confession on a father and son fishing trip. Nope. Poor guy got the skinny last week from something called a status update. He started to explain the way the kids share personal information with each another these days. I was lost by the time he got around to mentioning something about poking. The kid thought it smart to tell hundreds of random people about his divorce, but didnt have the decency to have a sit down with his old man.
       <br/>
       <h3>
        Diminished value
       </h3>
       Honestly, it floors me how guys cluck like hens about their lives, gossiping like they are broads killing time in a beauty parlor. It used to be a person had to pull teeth to get a guy just to say a word, and now men are blabbing to random strangers about their jobs, sexual conquests and the corned beef they had for lunch. Men dont realize all this yapping just diminishes the value of the important things they occasionally say because people tuned them out long ago. Men also fail to realize, more often than not, that no one really cares.
       <p>
        There is no upside to being an open book, especially when it comes to the dames. In their men, women dont want a girlfriend to talk about feelings with and to dish the latest rag-mag gossip with over cocktails. Women claim to want a man who expresses himself, but what they really crave is the strong, silent type. Think of some of the classic men from this or any generation:
        <a href="http://www.askmen.com/fashion/style_icon/3_style_icon.html" omni_link="McQueen" title="McQueen">
         McQueen
        </a>
        , The Duke,
        <a href="http://www.askmen.com/fashion/style_icon/23_style_icon.html" omni_link="Brando" title="Brando">
         Brando
        </a>
        . These were men that were harder to read than Braille by a fingerless man. Women want a mystery. Its hard to pull off mysterious when a woman knows more about you than she does her best friend.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        clam up if you want to be heard
       </h2>
       By the end of the war, guys were yeah, yeah, yeahing Speak Easy to death because they lost interest in anything he had to say unless it was INCOMING! I dont remember any of his stories, his anecdotes and frankly I cant even remember his real name; proves how little of an impact all that talking made.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Speak with your actions and not with your flapping gums. Keep the lips sealed, fellas.
