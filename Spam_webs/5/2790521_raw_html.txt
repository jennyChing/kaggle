        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Food Blogger Hands : Trying Out Polysporin Eczema Essentials
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.thekitchenmagpie.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Karlynn Johnston
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.thekitchenmagpie.com/food-blogger-hands-trying-out-polysporin-eczema-essentials/#comments">
           <span class="livefyre-commentcount" data-lf-article-id="24408" data-lf-site-id="306369">
            2 Comments
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-before-post" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.thekitchenmagpie.com/wp-content/uploads/images/2014/01/polysporin.jpg">
          <br/>
          <img alt="polysporin" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-24409" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.thekitchenmagpie.com/wp-content/uploads/images/2014/01/polysporin.jpg?resize=650%2C433"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Food blogger hands. Oh, if you are a food writer/blogger for living you know exactly what I am talking about.
        </p>
        <p>
         Alligator hands. Crocodile digits. Horrible, horrible dry skin in the winter time.
        </p>
        <p>
         While not only people who have their hands in sinks/under taps all day are affected, it surely is a sign of Canadian winter when your hands crack up.
        </p>
        <p>
         When the opportunity came to try the new line of Polysporin Eczema Essentials I jumped on board. I get offers to write about all sorts of things all year-long and rarely do I have any interest in anything like this. I mean, who wants to write about creams and soap?
        </p>
        <p>
         This time was different. I almost begged them to send me the products, seriously.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have the most horrible hands in the winter time.
        </p>
        <p>
         As much I as like to claim that I dont wash dishes, my hands are in the sink all day long.
        </p>
        <p>
         Not only that, because I prep food and am a hygiene freakazoid, I wash my hands all day long. If I counted the times I washed my hands, it might number in the high twenties. Thirties even, depending how many recipes I make that day and if I am out in public.
        </p>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <p>
         I can barely keep my hands presentable, never mind fully healed up. My hands get so bad that I have to use hydrocortisone cream on them every once in a while to heal them up. We arent talking dry skin here folks, we are talking cracks. I dont want to expand LOL but I am sure there are many of you out there who have the same problem. Its so bad that you have to turn to using cortisone cream as a last resort.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.thekitchenmagpie.com/wp-content/uploads/images/2014/01/polysporin1.jpg">
          <img alt="polysporin1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-24411" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.thekitchenmagpie.com/wp-content/uploads/images/2014/01/polysporin1.jpg?resize=650%2C440"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Polypsorin worked like a charm and healed up my hands quite nicely over the course of a few days. Mike has been using the wash on his beard which has worked like a charm, so for men with sensitive beards, that is a seriously fabulous product. I never thought about it before, but I guess men have to wash their beards somehow LOL! And shampoo is so hard on your face, according to Mike. (Um, Im really glad Im a girl.)
        </p>
        <p>
         So anyone else out there get dish-pan hands beyond measure? These Canadian winters take a huge toll on your skin?
        </p>
        <p>
         Love,
        </p>
        <p>
         Karlynn
        </p>
