   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    King Clone
   </h1>

     <p>
      <b>
       King Clone
      </b>
      is thought to be the oldest
      <a href="/wiki/Larrea_tridentata" title="Larrea tridentata">
       Creosote bush
      </a>
      ring in the
      <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Desert" title="Mojave Desert">
       Mojave Desert
      </a>
      . The ring is estimated to be 11,700 years old, making it one of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_long-living_organisms" title="List of long-living organisms">
       oldest living organisms on Earth
      </a>
      . This single
      <a href="/wiki/Clonal_colony" title="Clonal colony">
       clonal colony
      </a>
      plant of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Larrea_tridentata" title="Larrea tridentata">
        Larrea tridentata
       </a>
      </i>
      reaches up to 67 feet (20m) in diameter, with an average diameter of 45 feet (14m).

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
       Geography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=King_Clone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The King Clone ring is on restricted-access public land in the central Mojave Desert, approximately 0.6 miles north of California Route 247 on Bessemer Mine Road near the towns of
      <a href="/wiki/Lucerne_Valley,_California" title="Lucerne Valley, California">
       Lucerne Valley
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Landers,_California" title="Landers, California">
       Landers
      </a>
      . It is within the
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.lucernevalley.net/creosote/index.htm" rel="nofollow">
       Creosote Rings Preserve
      </a>
      of the Lucerne Valley and Johnson Valley.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-creosote_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-creosote-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Dating_methodology">
       Dating methodology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=King_Clone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Dating methodology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      King Clone was identified and the 11,700 years old age documented by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frank_Vasek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frank Vasek (page does not exist)">
       Frank Vasek
      </a>
      , a professor at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Riverside" title="University of California, Riverside">
       University of California, Riverside
      </a>
      . After Vasek hypothesized that the creosote ring was in fact one organism,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Leonel_Sternberg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Leonel Sternberg (page does not exist)">
       Leonel Sternberg
      </a>
      (then a graduate student working in Vasek's lab), was able to show that plants in a ring had some identical characteristics, but those shared characteristics differed from other plant clusters. Vasek then used two methods to determine the age of the ring. One method counted
      <a href="/wiki/Dendrochronology" title="Dendrochronology">
       rings
      </a>
      and measured the distance of annual growth, and the other used
      <a href="/wiki/Radiocarbon_dating" title="Radiocarbon dating">
       radiocarbon dating
      </a>
      on chunks of wood found in the center of the ring, and measuring their distance from each other and the living bushes. Both dating methods yielded the same result.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-creosote_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-creosote-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
