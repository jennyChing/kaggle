 *  - ga-event-type - {optional} the action that should trigger the event (default is 'click')

    // Tracks each ga-event handler, one per event type
    var _handlers = {};

    // The comma separated value of the ga-event attribute follows the params for the _trackEvent() 
    // call in google analytics (in same order):
    //
    //  category (required) - name of group wanting to track
    //  action (required) - type of interaction
    //  label (optional) - optional string for identification

            len = args.length,

      <div class="section intro slideshow_flagged no_image">
       <div class="section_body">
        <p>
         When looking at the possibility of replacing handlebars whether it's to get rid of hand numbness for long touring rides or simply provide better posture while commuting for better peripheral vision look for a handlebars with a significant degree of sweep.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>

     <div class="module subject_section ">
      <div class="section overview slideshow_flagged ">
       <span class="section_image" data-index="2">
        <img alt="Handlebars that have sweep are gentle on the hands." class="image" src="//file3.answcdn.com/answ-cld/image/upload/f_jpg,w_672,c_fill,g_faces:center,q_70/v1/tk/view/cew/f8397a90/5189aa291d2b8e647c3ea7cedbfbfb2d98f9d3c4.jpeg"/>
       </span>
       <h2 class="section_title">
        <span>
         Handlebars that have sweep are gentle on the hands.
        </span>
       </h2>
       <div class="section_body">
        <p>
         Decreased body weight on the handlebar not only helps to prevent or eliminate hand numbness but also provides what has been termed a less aggressive riding position.  Riding in a more relaxed position by bringing the handlebars closer to the rider is very common on most bicycles specifically designed for bike commuters.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="section analysis slideshow_flagged ">
       <span class="section_image" data-index="3">
        <img alt="Replacing handlebars is easy and cost effective." class="image" src="//file3.answcdn.com/answ-cld/image/upload/f_jpg,w_672,c_fill,g_faces:center,q_70/v1/tk/view/cew/0b575ee6/a350358a5f7d90a3b8f53af5ea8428ce3b7c2653.jpeg"/>
       </span>
       <h2 class="section_title">
        <span>
         Replacing handlebars is easy and cost effective.
        </span>
       </h2>
       <div class="section_body">
        <p>
         Many handlebars are available and for as little as ten to twenty dollars for a quality set of handlebars discussed here it is the easiest approach to a more comfortable ride.  For over sized bars of 31.8mm or what is referred to as "North Road" style handlebars prices can be the forty to sixty dollar range depending on personal preferences when shopping from popular name brand companies.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="section pro slideshow_flagged ">
       <span class="section_image" data-index="4">
        <img alt="Custom Sized Handlebars Provide Personal Options" class="image" src="//file.answcdn.com/answ-cld/image/upload/f_jpg,w_672,c_fill,g_faces:center,q_70/v1/tk/view/cew/12ef8a57/bfe04221e8fd51035d8d2dccd65bee833f984f01.jpeg"/>
       </span>
       <h2 class="section_title">
        <span>
         Custom Sized Handlebars Provide Personal Options
        </span>
       </h2>
       <div class="section_body">
        <p>
         Many of the handlebars available today are marked to cut for individual sizing.  Another handlebar that has gained in popularity is like the mountain riser comfort bar in photo which is accurately marked on each side of the handlebar providing an added option and accurate sizing guide. A very comfortable style of handlebar with thirty degrees of sweep.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="section con slideshow_flagged ">
       <span class="section_image" data-index="5">
        <img alt="Potential for loss of traction on front wheel." class="image" src="//file2.answcdn.com/answ-cld/image/upload/f_jpg,w_672,c_fill,g_faces:center,q_70/v1/tk/view/cew/a8847d01/a80fc255e12f94cc8cc9fb7173cf841524e43703.jpeg"/>
       </span>
       <h2 class="section_title">
        <span>
         Potential for loss of traction on front wheel.
        </span>
       </h2>
       <div class="section_body">
        <p>
         When discussing converting a mountain bike for commuting keep in mind that while less weight is applied to the front wheel for comfort, not only does the feel of handling change but traction on the front wheel may feel a bit lighter. That's because it is.  Mountain bikes are designed for maximizing front tire traction so the aggressive riding position is appropriate for loose and rough terrain.  Something to consider while selecting a handlebar if you're converting a mountain bike for commuting and want to continue hitting the trails for recreation.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="section conclusion slideshow_flagged no_image">
       <div class="section_body">
        <p>
         This general idea in a handlebar is finding popularity with fat bikes, commuter bikes and even finding its' way on to some touring bikes.  There are many options providing varying degrees of sweep and posture positions.  Visit a local bike shop for more information and suggestions.
        </p>

           <div class="content_text" ga-event="Feed_Click,index/article/15514,f_g_325769">

These types of bikes are usually used by beginners or people who just love to go riding. The bikes usually have flat handlebars and create an upright posture for riders. There are different classifications of this type of bike including the cross bike, the city bike, and the commuter bike.A road bike is a bike that can travel at high speeds and is used mostly on paved roads. Utility and touring are a few of the different categories of road bikes. These bikes have lightweight frames and have no suspension. The handlebars are curled and dropped. Those who are into fitness or are competitive riders prefer this style of bike.These bikes come in a range of prices. You can find a hybrid bike in the price range of $100 to $2,000. How the bike will be used and which category it falls into will also determine how much you will need to spend.The price range for a road bike is between $500 and $6,000. About $500 will provide you with a good bike, but it will not be as ergonomic as those that cost more. Prices change based on what you will be doing with the bike. Bikes that are purchased in the lower range will require more maintenance also.When deciding on which type of bike to buy, you should consider the types of surfaces on which you will be riding. Will you be riding short commutes or entering races that span many miles? Will the bike be used for competition or recreation? How fast do you want the bike to travel? Answering these questions will help determine which of these two types of bikes will best suit your needs.Whether you enjoy riding a bike for sport or just for leisure, the hybrid and road bikes are sure to please. Keep in mind all of the things you want to do with your bike when you go to acquire a bicycle. Know how much you can afford. Also, think about accessories that will be needed, like a helmet. Know the differences between these two bikes.Hybrid and road bikes are gender specific. Bikes for males and females are different in the way that they are made. Be aware of this when purchasing a hybrid or road bike.

           <h1 class="title " ga-event="Feed_Click,index/article/15514,f_g_300794">
            <a class="title_link black" href="http://biking.answers.com/buying/what-should-you-know-when-obtaining-a-quote-for-a-bicycle">
             What Should You Know when Obtaining a Quote for a Bicycle?
            </a>
           </h1>
           <div class="content_text" ga-event="Feed_Click,index/article/15514,f_g_300794">
            Buying a bike is a bit more involved than just collecting a lot of quotes then picking the cheapest one. Whether you are a casual rider or a beginner, there are many factors t
            <span class="hellip">
             
            </span>
            <span class="hidden">
             hat you should consider when getting a quote. Based on these factors, you can make a sound judgment call when selecting a bike. If you fail to carefully consider your options, you could wind up with a bike that proves to be more trouble than it is worth.Bikes are complex. Structural and design differences in each type of bike make them specialized to certain types of riding. A bike that is ideal for a recreational rider may not be a good fit for a professional biker. Before you can buy a bike, you need to know what type of rider you are. Although you are free to ride any bike you want, you may not feel as comfortable on a bike made for a professional rider if you are a casual rider.Certain bikes perform better on certain surfaces. You need to consider your riding surface when gathering quotes for your new bike. For instance, a road bike performs better on a pavement. Mountain bike and commuting bikes are a bit more versatile and can be used on the pavement and gravel roads. If you select the wrong bike for the surface you typically ride, the bike may not perform as well as it normally would on the right surface.Ensure that you can remove the seat on a bike before purchasing it. If the seat on the bike is too thin or long, you could be in for an uncomfortable ride. If you are an occasional rider, a seat that has lots of padding is ideal. The padding can help to reduce any soreness you may experience. If you ride daily, look for a seat that fits your bone structure. While collecting each bike quote, find out if you can make a simple swap of the seat without paying an additional fee.The handlebars are equally important as the seat and size of the bike. A bike that has handlebars that are lower than the seat is designed to go faster. However, you may experience some discomfort due to the position you must be in to operate the bike. On the other hand, a bike with handlebars that are higher is more comfortable to ride but is meant to go slower. This is considered more a recreational bike that is ideal for the occasional rider.There are many options available for children's bikes. When you purchase a bike for a child, the most important thing you should consider is the size. Children's bikes are measured by the wheel size instead of the frame like adult-size bikes. The right size bike for a child allows for his feet to touch the ground while standing over the bike. Do not buy a larger bike than the child needs. The bike may be more than he or she can handle and can lead to an accident.Research is important when shopping for a bike. Outside of gathering quotes, you need to know specifically how you want to use the bike. By making a hasty decision based on the quotes you receive, you could miss out on getting a bike that is more compatible with your riding style and comfort levels. Although the price is important, your safety and comfort are even more so.Before purchasing any bike, you should take a test ride with it. The test ride can help you get a feel for how the bike rides. It also gives you a chance to test for comfort. After you have narrowed your choices down, you can consider the quotes to help you make your final decision.

           <h1 class="title " ga-event="Feed_Click,index/article/15514,f_g_302324">
            <a class="title_link black" href="http://biking.answers.com/tires-wheels/wheels-for-biking-what-features-are-necessary-in-commuter-bike-tires">
             Wheels for Biking: What Features Are Necessary in Commuter Bike Tires?
            </a>
           </h1>
           <div class="content_text" ga-event="Feed_Click,index/article/15514,f_g_302324">
            The commuting biker needs to be sure they have reliable tires for their bike. Everyday commuters are going to experience more debris in the roadways and rougher patches in the
            <span class="hellip">
             
            </span>
            <span class="hidden">
             road than that of a pleasure ride through the park. For this very reason, the wheels on your bike must be both durable and safe. Take the time to know the roads you will be riding each day and do your proper tire research from there.One must consider the difference in the riding they are doing when looking at the differences between tires found on a commuter bike, a mountain bike, or pleasure bike. When commuting everyday on urban roadways, you will experience more debris on the roads, such as glass, rocks, and trash; not to mention the potholes or different road surfaces. With that said, commuter tires must be more durable to withstand all of the various conditions on a daily ride. Another consideration is the weight that the tires will need to hold. Daily riders will often be carrying an extra 10-30 lbs. on their ride; they might be carrying clothes to change into at work, a laptop, books, a purse, or all of the above. The tires need to be able to handle the extra weight.The main feature that you want to keep in mind is durability. Some tires feature a Kevlar lining, which is more resistant to punctures. There should be a good tread on the tire for more traction. You will need better traction on everyday commutes for different road conditions, such as wet roads when it is raining. As a safety feature, you should consider tires with reflective sidewalls. If you are biking during the early morning or at dusk, this reflective sidewall will make you more noticeable on the road.With daily riding, you will want to be sure to check the air pressure in your tires frequently. Another daily habit should be to check the treads of the tires for any debris that might be lodged in the treads. Being proactive and removing debris from the treads can prevent punctures; for example, if a sharp rock is lodged in the tread and you continue to ride on it, it can eventually puncture the tire and the inner tube of your wheel. You should always be prepared in the event that you would get a flat tire. Carry a patching kit, an adjustable wrench, a tire lever, a spare inner tube, and either a mounted air pump or a CO2 system to inflate the tire.The average price range for commuter tires is $20 to $70. It will depend on what you are looking for in a tire, whether it be tread, puncture resistance, or performance. The suggestion for commuter biking is that you would choose the most durable and have some added protection against punctures. You should be able to get a decent, reliable tire within the price range mentioned. When researching cost of the tire, don't always base your decision on price; look at the rating of the tire and the reviews. The key with commuter tires is that you are riding on them every day; look at the terrain you ride on and start with trying to prevent punctures by getting a resistant tire.In general, you will want to pay attention to the tread on your tires and replace them when they are getting worn. Some key things to watch for are thread beginning to show through the rubber of the tire, or bulging in the tire. These are definitely signs that your tire needs to be replaced. Of course, there is always going to be the inevitable and unavoidable incidents, such as a puncture. This will require you to either patch or replace the tire and inner tube if heavily damaged. Taking good care of your tires can add to their life. To review, you should be checking your air pressure often and checking for debris in the tread of the tires after each daily commute.It is important for daily commuters to take great care and pay attention to their bike tires. Putting durable tires on your bike can help in preventing all punctures, however, you will always run into a situation where you may need to replace your tires. Remember to pay close attention to the roadways you are riding on and try to avoid debris and potholes; your tires will thank you and you will have a much more pleasurable commute.With steadily increasing gas prices, more and more people are starting to bike their daily commutes versus driving a car. Bike shop owners are finding people taking those old mountain bikes or 10-speeds that have been collecting dust in the garage and converting them into commuter bikes. This, oftentimes, can be done by just changing the tires out to a commuter-friendly style.

Some of the best performing touring bike tires are included in this list.Think of the Continental Travel Contact as the convertible of touring bicycles. Bald tread down the center is perfect for roads. At the moment the tire hits dirt or gravel, the tread on the outside of the tire wall provides the necessary grip. These tires consistently endure high mileage and are a popular choice for extended touring. Continentals are easier to mount but not as long lasting as Schwalbes, with which they are frequently compared.These tires are oriented toward the commuter bicyclist. Because of that, they command a higher price and generally last longer than their competition.The Maxxis Overdrive Kevlar features a durable all-season construction, complete with a central groove to channel water away from the tread. These tires frequently last for over 5,000 miles, which is astonishing for any tire but especially good for bike touring tires. While the rubber does not expel debris like some other tires, the cost and overall performance during the life of the tire make it an excellent investment.A good pair of touring bike tires is a fundamental ingredient in a successful bike tour. Before you begin yours, research the best tire for your tour plans, and mount them well in advance of your trip. Taking time to break in and get to know your tires increases your comfort and familiarity with them when you are on the road.Far and away, the Schwalbe Marathon line of tires is the leading seller among recreational cyclists in Europe. Its market share is also growing in the United States as its reputation spreads.

            </span>
            <span class="hidden">
             ing, where you want to go and how long your journey is. There are several types of road bikes, all with different qualities to suit different riding.A touring bike is a common choice for long-distance trips. It is stronger than other types of road bikes because its design carries a rider and loads of gear. Racks over the tires accommodate packs and baggage, freeing the rider of having to carry anything on his or her back. These bikes also provide better stability with their long wheelbases and thicker frame. If you plan to carry a lot of things with you, a touring bike is your best option.All other road bike designs simulate the traditional model. The skinny tires and curved handlebars offer comfort and control to the rider. Traditional bikes are for paved surfaces and adapt to all sorts of road riding. Racing versions weigh less than 20 pounds, but for a longer ride, you want a model that is a little heavier and moves at a slow, steady pace.Endurance is key if you are going to ride for miles and miles. These bikes earn their name for stamina. On endurance bikes, the handlebars are at almost seat level versus a few inches below on traditional bikes. This saves the rider's arms and back from extended strain. The frames and forks absorb the road better as well, which makes for a smoother ride. Finally, endurance bikes also have a longer wheelbase for greater stability and ease of control.Being in an upright position is more comfortable than leaning over. What you lose in aerodynamics, you gain in comfort and endurance - things that are invaluable to long-distance trips. The flat bar style of road bike replaces the usual curved handlebar with a single, straight bar similar to those on mountain bikes. This puts the rider in a more upright condition and reduces backache. This change in handlebar is the only difference between a flat bar and an endurance bike.While any road bike is suitable for extended rides, each has their specific advantages. The small differences between bikes make huge differences in the last hours of an all-day ride. How fast you want to go, how comfortable you want to be and how much gear you bring with you, are all factors that affect your bike-buying decision.Road bikes typically have a break-in period, where the brake and gear cables stretch during the first few rides. Ask your bike shop to realign the bike and check the bearings after 30 to 60 days of riding.
