  <article class="post-3778 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-birthday category-entertaining category-occasions category-spring">
   <div class="hero">
    <img alt="" class="hero-img" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/WashiTapeGif-Wrap_hero2.png"/>

     <h1 class="entry-title">
      Spring DIY: Washi Tape Gift Wrap
     </h1>
     <div class="meta right">
      <time class="published" datetime="2015-03-02T10:47:35+00:00">
       March 2, 2015
      </time>
      <p class="byline author vcard">
       By 
			Shari's Berries
      </p>
     </div>
    </header>
   </div>
   <!-- /.hero -->
   <div class="wrap">
    <div class="container" role="document">
     <div class="content row">
      <main class="main " role="main">
       <p class="lead">
        Washi tape. If you don't know about it (and you love to get crafty with gift wrap),  get ready to fall in love. Sometimes called wasabi tape by mistake, it does have one thing in common with the spicy green condiment: it originated in Japan. Washi tape is made from natural fibers, like bamboo, hemp and the bark of native Japanese treesthe mulberry, the mitsumata shrub and the gampi tree.
       </p>
       <div class="entry-content col-md-offset-1 col-md-10 col-lg-offset-2 col-lg-8">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         As the story goes, it was simply a Japanese version of masking tape until 2006, when a group of artists approached masking tape manufacturer Kamoi Kakoshi. They showed them a book of art they had made using the companys tapes and asked them to create colorful masking tapes for artists. They were a huge hit, and the rest is washi tape history.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, of course, when we discovered washi tapes in bright seasonal patterns and shades, we just had to get crafty and use it to wrap our spring gifts. Its so easy, we couldnt resist. Here are a few of our favorite ideas to inspire you.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Sweet Thoughts
        </h4>
        <h4>
         <img alt="Spring Washi Tape Gift Wrapping: Say Hello" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3780" height="1000" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/WashiTapeGif-Wrap_2.jpg" width="750"/>
        </h4>
        <p>
         Skip the gift tag and simply washi tape your message on the gift. Keep it short and cheerful like hello, smile, love, congrats or cheers.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Fresh flair
        </h4>
        <h4>
         <img alt="Spring Washi Tape Gift Wrapping: Add fresh flowers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3783" height="1000" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/WashiTapeGif-Wrap_5.jpg" width="800"/>
        </h4>
        <p>
         Keep it fresh with a few flowers, leaves, pine tree clippings or herbs from your yard. A little goes a long way. Fresh sprigs of lavender and rosemary will make your gift smell as lovely as it looks.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you dont have a garden, add festive flair by washi taping deflated balloons, feathers, birthday candles, sparklers and more. We got our inspiration from this
         <a href="http://www.youaremyfave.com/2014/07/15/thirty-in-a-box-is-my-fave/" target="_blank">
          youaremyfave blog
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h4>
         Fun flags
        </h4>
        <p>
         <img alt="Spring Washi Tape Gift Wrapping: Fun Flags" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3782" height="1000" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/WashiTapeGif-Wrap_4.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Wrap evenly spaced pieces of washi tape around twine. Then secure and cut into flags. String them across a gift bag, box or card for a festive touch. Or use them as party decor. Attach a string of flags to two straws and youve got a stylish cake topper.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Artful gift tags
        </h4>
        <p>
         <img alt="Spring Washi Tape Gift Wrapping: Custom Gift Tags" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3781" height="1000" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/WashiTapeGif-Wrap_3.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Start with pre-made tags or cut a regular piece of cardstock into a gift tag shape. Then simply wrap your favorite washi tape around the tag, punch a hole in it and tie it to your gift with twine.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Geometric glam
        </h4>
        <h4>
         <img alt="Spring Washi Tape Gift Wrapping: Jazz up plain paper" height="1000" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/WashiTapeGif-Wrap_1.jpg" width="800"/>
        </h4>
        <p>
         Choose washi tape in complementary colors andlet your creativity loose. Cut the tape into triangles or squares and arrange in a trendy geometric pattern. Or layer pieces across each other in a washi tape bow. Cut v-shapes into the ends of the tape strips at various lengths and youve got a stylish flag pattern.
        </p>
        <p>
         No time to washi a gift? Send them our
         <a href="http://www.berries.com/cake-truffles-STF?" target="_blank">
          cake truffles
         </a>
         . Theyre packed in a stylish, extra-special box with fun, heartfelt hidden messages that will make them feel amazing.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Your Washi Wonders
        </h4>
        <p>
         What did you make? Tell us all about. Or better yet, show us. Post a pic to Instagram or Facebook with #SharetheBerries.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.428571429;">
         </span>
        </p>
