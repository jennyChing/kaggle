        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         7 Ways to Fit Pricey Meals into a Small Grocery Budget
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-06-20T07:00:19+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          June 20, 2014
         </time>
         By:
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
           Jazmin Rode
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://frugalitygal.com/2014/06/fit-pricey-meals-into-small-grocery-budget.html#comments">
           11 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="11213739" data-link="http://frugalitygal.com/2014/06/fit-pricey-meals-into-small-grocery-budget.html" data-summary="" data-title="7 Ways to Fit Pricey Meals into a Small Grocery Budget">
        </div>
        <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
        <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
         This post may contain affiliate links. Read my
         <a href="http://frugalitygal.com/disclosure">
          disclosure policy
         </a>
         to learn more.
        </div>
        <p>
         Meal planning is an incredibly effective way to save money on groceries. But, what do you do when a birthday, anniversary, or other special occasion comes up that requires a more intricate meal than your typical budget allows?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/frugalitygal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/save-money-on-groceries-meal-planning.png">
          <img alt="7 Ways to make room for pricey meals in a small grocery budget." class="aligncenter wp-image-988" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/frugalitygal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/save-money-on-groceries-meal-planning.png?resize=450%2C659"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         One of the obvious answers is simply to go over budget. These things happen and every once in a while isnt going to drastically impact your spending. But if youre determined to stay within your grocery budget for the week, here are 7 tips to help you accommodate more expensive meals in a small grocery budget.
        </p>
        <h2>
         1. Make it earn its keep.
        </h2>
        <p>
         One of my favorite ways to work pricier ingredients into my budget is to use the same ingredient in more than one meal. Because I
         <a href="http://frugalitygal.com/category/meal-planning" target="_blank">
          meal plan
         </a>
         , I will ensure that the specific ingredient is used in at least two meals even if it isnt the star of the dish in order to justify the cost. Of course, this isnt possible for all ingredients (e.g. it might be hard to stretch salmon or shrimp), but whenever reasonably possible its my go-to money saving trick.
        </p>
        <h2>
         2. Use smaller quantities.
        </h2>
        <p>
         In many dishes, you can get away with using less of a specific ingredient without anyone taking notice. I do this religiously with meat. The
         <a href="http://frugalitygal.com/2013/10/how-to-save-money-on-meat.html">
          cost of meat is steadily increasing
         </a>
         , but it isnt something we want to give up entirely or have sparingly. So, when a recipe calls for 2 lbs. of chicken, Ill use one. Or if it calls for 1lb. of ground beef, Ill use  lb. I almost always beef up my ground beef with beans in order to make them stretch further, in recipes that allow the addition to go unnoticed. Keep in mind, I have a household of two adults. These quantities will be different for every family.
        </p>
        <h2>
         3. Substitute ingredients.
        </h2>
        <p>
         Some not all ingredients allow for easy substitution of lower cost alternatives. For example, if the recipe calls for whole vanilla beans, opt for vanilla extract. If possible, exclude the ingredient thats the culprit of weighing on your grocery budget altogether. Recently, I tried a recipe that was perfect except for the addition an obscure $5+ ingredient that I had a hard time finding (and pronouncing). I excluded it completely and the recipe turned out just fine.
        </p>
        <h2>
         4. Watch for garnishes.
        </h2>
        <p>
         Many times, recipes will include fresh herbs, but upon closer inspection youll realize that the herb is just for garnish. Give your recipe a close look before crafting your meal plan and exclude garnishes that will eat into your budget. Sometimes doing this alone can save you $2 to $3 per herb.
        </p>
        <h2>
         5. Shop with a discerning eye.
        </h2>
        <p>
         If your recipe calls for a dried herb that you dont generally keep at home, dont spend $3 to $4 for a small bottle. Instead, head into the ethnic food aisle and look for the small herb packets. I usually pay $0.75 for these packets as opposed to the $3+ for the small bottles Ill never fully use.
        </p>
        <h2>
         6. Account for leftovers.
        </h2>
        <p>
         If there is nothing you can do to lessen the cost of the recipe, turn the
         <a href="http://www.frugalitygal.com/2014/04/how-to-reuse-leftovers.html">
          leftovers into a second meal
         </a>
         . Cook dinner as usual and properly package and freeze the leftover portion for later consumption, if the recipe will store well in the freezer.
         <a href="http://frugalitygal.com/category/freezer-cooking">
          Freezer cooking
         </a>
         is one of my favorite ways to save money in the kitchen, and freezing leftovers is a simple way to stock your freezer with very minimal effort.
        </p>
        <h2>
         7. Work the cost into other meals.
        </h2>
        <p>
         If all else fails, offset the cost of the meal by saving on other meals throughout the week. Plan simpler meals and make as much as you can yourself. For example,
         <a href="http://frugalitygal.com/2014/06/easy-homemade-breadsticks-recipe.html">
          breadsticks
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://frugalitygal.com/2014/02/homemade-tortilla-chips.html">
          tortilla chips
         </a>
         and refried beans are easy to make at home. Saving a little here and a little there really does add up and will create extra room in your grocery budget to allow for the pricier meal.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, what do you do when youre trying to fit a pricier meal into your grocery budget? If you have any tips to share, please include them in the comments below.
        </p>
