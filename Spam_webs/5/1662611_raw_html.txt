        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Its history. Its poetry.
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2009-05-05T12:00:10+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          May 5, 2009
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.contrariwise.org/author/admin-2/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Jen
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.contrariwise.org/2009/05/05/its-history-its-poetry/#comments">
           11 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <img alt="Literary Tattoos: The Catcher in the Rye J. D. Salinger " class="alignnone size-full wp-image-621" height="335" src="http://contrariwise.contrariwiseorg.netdna-cdn.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2009/04/historypoetry2.jpg" title="Its history. Its poetry.   Contrariwise: Literary Tattoos" width="500"/>
         <img alt="Literary Tattoos: The Catcher in the Rye J. D. Salinger " class="alignnone size-full wp-image-620" height="333" src="http://contrariwise.contrariwiseorg.netdna-cdn.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2009/04/historypoetry.jpg" title="Its history. Its poetry.   Contrariwise: Literary Tattoos" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This tattoo is on Becca:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          My tattoo is a quote from
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0316769487/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0316769487&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=contrariwiseo-20">
           The Catcher in the Rye
          </a>
          , spoken by Mr. Antolini. Im not really a huge fan of the book itself, but this quote really sums up how I feel about learning.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <em>
          Among other things, youll find that youre not the first person who was ever confused and frightened and even sickened by human behavior. Youre by no means alone on that score, youll be excited and stimulated to know. Many, many men have been just as troubled morally and spiritually as you are right now. Happily, some of them kept record of their troubles. Youll learn from them  if you
          <span class="il">
           want
          </span>
          to. Just as someday, if you have something to offer, someone will learn something from you. Its a beautiful reciprocal arrangement. And it isnt education.
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           Its history. Its poetry.
          </span>
          
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         - Excerpt from
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0316769487/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0316769487&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=contrariwiseo-20">
          The Catcher in the Rye
         </a>
         by J. D. Salinger
         <em>
          <br/>
         </em>
        </p>
