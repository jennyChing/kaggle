       <h2>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="About_National_Ice_Cream_Day">
         About National Ice Cream Day
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        National Ice Cream Day was officially designated as a holiday in 1984 by U.S. President Ronald Reagan. The president also specified July as National Ice Cream Month and set aside the third Sunday of the month as the official holiday. President Reagan acknowledged that 90 percent of American citizens enjoyed the treat. Today, the industry has grown, and generates billions of dollars annually.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-idfa_1-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-idfa-1">
          [1]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </p>
       <p>
        Ice cream has a history dating back to the second century B.C., though no exact origins have been traced. Although historians recognize today's ice cream as having first appeared in the 16th century, it was not made available to the public until 1660.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
         <a href="#cite_note-2">
          [2]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </p>
