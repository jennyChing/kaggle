          <h3>
           <img alt="US Air Customer Service LInes" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-4053 alignright" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/heelsfirsttravel.boardingarea.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/US-Air-Customer-Service-LInes-150x150.jpg?resize=150%2C150"/>
           1. Call customer service immediately
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            And, if youre in the airport, RUN to the customer service desk
           </strong>
           .
           <em>
            Time is of the essence.
           </em>
           Theyre trying to rebook people as quickly as possible, and the longer you wait the fewer available seats. You dont know whether youll get an agent on the phone
           <br/>
           or move through line first, which is why you want to do both.
          </p>
          <p>
           Note: If you have lounge access, head there instead of the customer service desk, the lines are often much shorter.
          </p>
          <h3>
           2. Decide Ahead of Time What You Want
           <img alt="http://routemap.staralliance.com/" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-6618" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/heelsfirsttravel.boardingarea.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/routemap-150x150.png?resize=150%2C150"/>
          </h3>
          <p>
           Would you rather get on the first flight out even if means extra connections?
           <br/>
           Are you willing to wait several days?
           <br/>
           Can you fly into a nearby airport?
           <br/>
           Do you want to declare it a
           <a href="http://heelsfirsttravel.boardingarea.com/2013/01/29/trip-in-vain-is-an-actual-policy/" target="_blank" title="Trip in Vain is an Actual Policy">
            Trip in Vain
           </a>
           and reschedule for different dates?
           <br/>
           Figure out in advance what youd be willing to accept so theres no delay on your end when the agent starts giving you options. If youre traveling with others, decide if youre willing to split up to get better flights.
          </p>
          <h3>
           3. Do Your Homework
           <img alt="delayed flights" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-1387" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/heelsfirsttravel.boardingarea.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/delayed-flights-150x150.jpg?resize=150%2C150"/>
          </h3>
          <p>
           If you have internet access or can contact someone who does, start investigating all your flight options on any carrier. The airlines will try first to find you space on their own flights, or those of their partners, but in a pinch, theyll sometimes put you on any open flight even if its out of network. Having a list of flight numbers that work best for you can often help focus their search. I see theres a 10PM flight to Phoenix that connects to Flight 178
          </p>
          <h3>
           4. Be Niceto the Agents!
          </h3>
          <p>
           They didnt create the weather, sabotage the plane, or call for the ground stop. If it was up to them, theyd probably put you on a non-stop flight in first class to get you on your way. No matter how stressed you are, dont raise your voice and dont get angry.
           <a href="http://heelsfirsttravel.boardingarea.com/2013/05/18/the-power-of-a-half-smile/" target="_blank" title="The Power of a Half Smile">
            Perhaps even try to smile
           </a>
           ! Theres only so much they can do, but taking out your frustration on them is not going to inspire them to try harder on your behalf.
          </p>
          <h3>
           5. Check the Cancellation Policy on Any Trip Reservations
          </h3>
          <p>
           The grace period for for cancelling a car rental, hotel, or other reservation varies, and sometimes 15 minutes can be the difference between getting your money back and having to eat the cost. For instance, if your flight is cancelled at 5:45PM, you may still have time to hit the 6PM deadlinefor cancelling your hotel that night.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://heelsfirsttravel.boardingarea.com/2012/05/19/my-plane-was-hit-by-a-truck-or-what-do-you-do-when-your-flight-is-cancelled/" target="_blank" title="My Plane Was Hit By a Truck! (Or What Do You Do When Your Flight is Cancelled?)">

                <a href="https://www.refund.me/en/flight-delay/" rel="nofollow">
                 https://www.refund.me/en/flight-delay/
                </a>
                ). Up to $800 or something like that  its worth remembering. It can be a bit of a hassle getting airlines to pay but once they do it really does make up for the inconvenience.
               </p>
