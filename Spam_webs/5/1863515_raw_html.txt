         <h2>
          20 Animals With Goofy-Looking Faces You Can't Help But Laugh At
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="content-header-meta">
         <div style="display: inline; float: left;">
          <span class="pubtime">

       <div>
        <p>
         All animals are beautiful in their own special way, but if you can't stop giggling at these goofy-looking faces, no one would blame you.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div id="google-adsense-bottom">
        <!-- kaixinfun-detail-bottom -->

       <div id="raw-content">
        <h3>
         1. Exotic Shorthair Cats
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="1. Exotic Shorthair Cats" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face2-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face1-620x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         While there are a few breeds of cats that looked like they ran into a wall and never recovered properly, the exotic shorthair is probably the goofiest-looking of them all. Their massive eyes, round faces, and long, droopy mouths give them a sad and slightly confused expression.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. Tapir
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="2. Tapir" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face3-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face4-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Tapirs are odd-looking mammals that are most closely related to the rhinoceros and horse. There are four species, but they all display a long, goofy-looking snout that enables them to reach food that they otherwise might not be able to grab.
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Proboscis Monkey
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="3. Proboscis Monkey" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face5-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face6-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Both male and female proboscis monkeys have unusually large noses, but for the males, it's even more pronounced--- their noses are usually so huge, they cover up their mouths!
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Elephant Seal
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="4. Elephant Seal" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face7-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>

        <div>

        </div>
        <p>
         The male elephant seal's large proboscis helps it make loud roaring sounds during mating season. It also has a bunch of cavities that are used to help reabsorb moisture when it breathes.
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Blobfish
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="5. Blobfish" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face9-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face10-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         The flesh of the blobfish has a density less than that of water, which allows it to float along the ocean floor without expending a lot of energy on swimming.
        </p>
        <h3>
         6. Aye-Aye
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="6. Aye-Aye" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face11-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face12-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         The aye-aye is a primate with unusually large middle fingers that it uses to tap on trees and root around inside them for insects to eat. It also happens to look like someone who just realized they slept through their alarm and is an hour late for work.
        </p>
        <h3>
         7. Shoebill Stork
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="7. Shoebill Stork" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face13-620x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face14-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         This endangered bird is most closely related to pelicans, and its most noteworthy feature is its broad, proportionally-awkward bill for which it was named.
        </p>
        <h3>
         8. Red-Lipped Batfish
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="8. Red-Lipped Batfish" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face15-284x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
         <script type="text/javascript">
          ( function() {    if (window.CHITIKA === undefined) { window.CHITIKA = { 'units' : [] }; };    var unit = {"calltype":"async[2]","publisher":"ZhongLinQu","width":550,"height":250,"sid":"Chitika Default"};    var placement_id = window.CHITIKA.units.length;    window.CHITIKA.units.push(unit);    document.write('&lt;div id="chitikaAdBlock-' + placement_id + '"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;');}());

         <script async="" src="//cdn.chitika.net/getads.js" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face16-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Despite the fact that it's a fish, these guys aren't very good swimmers and instead use their pectoral fins to walk along the floor of the ocean. They also happen to look like great aunts with names like Mildred and Agnes.
        </p>
        <h3>
         9. Saiga
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="9. Saiga" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face17-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face18-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         The saiga's proboscis is not something you'd expect to see on an antelope, but it does far more than just make the animal look sexy. It filters out dust when the herds move in the summer months, and in the winter, it helps warm up the air before it reaches the lungs.
        </p>
        <h3>
         10. Parrot Fish
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="10. Parrot Fish" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face19-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face20-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         There might be plenty of fish in the sea, but the parrot fish looks like the jolliest of all. Their teeth are packed closely together, giving them a beak-like appearance that earned the fish its name. Of course, it also happens to make them look like they're just giving a big, goofy grin.
        </p>
        <h3>
         11. Purple Frog
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="11. Purple Frog" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face21-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face23-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Adult male purple frogs are about a third of the length of their female counterparts, but both sexes have an equally perplexed expression on their odd faces.
        </p>
        <h3>
         12. Tarsier
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="12. Tarsier" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face24-620x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face25-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         The tarsier's large eyes can reach up to 18 cm in diameter, making each one as large as the primate's entire brain.
        </p>
        <h3>
         13. Sun Bear
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="13. Sun Bear" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face26-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face27-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         The sun bear's ridiculously long tongue helps it gain access to honey and honeycombs, for which it has an insatiable appetite. Its flat, wrinkly head gives it the appearance of a grumpy old man.
        </p>
        <h3>
         14. Star-Nosed Mole
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="14. Star-Nosed Mole" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face28-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face29-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         This tiny rodent's crazy, yet highly sensitive nose is used for finding prey, since it is technically blind. But even cooler is that the mole's nose can smell underwater. It blows bubbles onto its surroundings and then inhales the bubbles back up through the nose to find scent trails or other objects.
        </p>
        <h3>
         15. Shar Pei
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="15. Shar Pei" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face30-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face31-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Shar Pei puppies are basically all wrinkles from head to toe, but they grow into their skin as they get older. Thankfully, even adult Shar Peis have faces that look like folded towels, giving them a funny (and completely adorable) appearance.
        </p>
        <h3>
         16. Axolotl Salamander
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="16. Axolotl Salamander" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face32-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face33-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Like all salamanders, these guys are amphibians. But unlike other amphibians, they never lose their gills, enabling them to stay underwater for as much time as necessary. They also look like they have a crazy hairdo.
        </p>
        <h3>
         17. Emperor Tamarin Monkey
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="17. Emperor Tamarin Monkey" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face34-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face35-620x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         This whiskered primate was named for its resemblance to the German emperor Wilhelm II. But more importantly, all of these animals grow up to look like wise old men. All of them.
        </p>
        <h3>
         18. Pig-Nosed Turtle
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="18. Pig-Nosed Turtle" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face36-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face37-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         They might look cute, but these unique turtles are some of the most aggressive in the world and will do just about anything to defend their territory.
        </p>
        <h3>
         19. Irrawaddy Dolphin
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="19. Irrawaddy Dolphin" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face38-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face39-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         While they look more like beluga whales, these goofy-looking porpoises are actually most closely related to orcas. They're also incredibly smart; fishermen in Burma have trained these wild dolphins to herd fish into their nets in exchange for some of the bycatch.
        </p>
        <h3>
         20. Pygmy Marmoset
        </h3>
        <div>
         <img alt="20. Pygmy Marmoset" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face40j-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.kaixinfun.com/Images/content/201409/8/face41-934x.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         It's the smallest monkey in the world, but these little critters can leap up to five meters between branches. They also look like gremlins, so you probably shouldn't feed them after midnight.
        </p>
