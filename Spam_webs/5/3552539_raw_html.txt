      <h1>
       Wood vs. Concrete Retaining Walls
      </h1>
      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        <img alt="retaining wall" class="alignright wp-image-780" height="134" src="http://www.cinderblockonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/2011_p60_MiniCreta_Athena_PA_A00288_4411-300x200.jpg" width="201"/>
        Both wood and concrete retaining walls are used frequently in landscaping. They provide an attractive aesthetic element, but also help to support the soil and the vertical slant of a garden so that it doesnt collapse. When looking into retaining walls, you may not know which type to choose and ask yourself
        <em>
         wood or concrete?
        </em>
        Both wood and concrete retaining walls have many benefits, but in the end, there can only be one. And were pretty sure that one is concrete. Heres why:
       </p>
       <p>
        For one, concrete retaining walls are more durable. Although wood retaining walls may be the cheaper choice, they will not hold up in the long run. Wood is easily affected by weathering, and can decay due to fungus or other moisture-related growths. Wood may also have to constantly be retreated over time, unlike concrete walls that will hold up without much maintenance.
       </p>
       <p>
        Installation is another affecting factor. Because wood retaining walls are put together using nails, bolts, and other fasteners, there is a possibility for deterioration of the wood due to the constant strain of the fastenings. If the metal pieces begin to rust or corrode, they can harm the wood structure. A concrete retaining wall will be installed with cement, making it stronger and less likely to collapse. Also, wood retaining walls are not installed at a great base depth, making it easier for them to collapse if harmed. Concrete walls are installed with a greater base depth, making them much more stable.
       </p>
       <p>
        Although concrete retaining walls are more expensive than wood, the benefits and low maintenance of concrete makes up for the cost. This season, choose concrete masonry for your landscaping needs instead of wood. For help with getting started, contact Cinder &amp; Concrete Block.
       </p>
