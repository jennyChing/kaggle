         <h1 class="posttitle">
          <a class="entry-title" href="http://streetstance.co.uk/2014/02/03/nimbys-in-sussex-block-drilling-under-their-land/" rel="bookmark" title="NIMBYs in Sussex Block Drilling Under Their Land">
           NIMBYs in Sussex Block Drilling Under Their Land
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="postdata fix">
          <span class="author">
           <span class="icon">
           </span>

        <div class="entry fix">
         <p>
          <img alt="NIMBY Not in My BackYard" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-5924" height="187" src="http://streetstance.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/NIMBY-300x187.jpg" width="300"/>
         </p>
         <p style="margin-bottom:0px;">
          I have written on this website that I am concerned that the UK urgently needs a
          <a href="http://streetstance.co.uk/2013/10/21/nuclear-power-required-as-part-of-mixed-energy-supply-strategy/">
           mixed energy supply strategy
          </a>
          to prevent the lights going out.
          <a href="http://streetstance.co.uk/2014/01/11/save-the-planet-but-do-not-affect-me/">
           NIMBYs paralyse the UK
          </a>
          and prevent critical progress because politicians will not make difficult decisions because they are scared of losing votes.
         </p>
         <p>
          A few weeks ago I was pleased to read that David Cameron was taking brave decisions by
         </p>
         <ul style="margin-top:0px;">
          <li>
           offering
           <a href="http://streetstance.co.uk/2014/01/13/fracking-tax-boosts-for-councils/">
            tax incentives for councils
           </a>
           that support fracking.
          </li>
          <li>
           giving the go ahead for the construction of a nuclear power plant at Hinckley
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          This is probably because the government has realised that there are more votes to lose with the lights going out than from local NIMBYs protesting. Whatever the reason I am pleased that there is positive progress with developing a mixed energy supply strategy.
         </p>
         <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
         <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
          <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
          </script>
          <!-- StreetStance Content Responsive -->
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-2935149147432497" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="9231763831" style="display:block">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
         </div>
         <p>
          With all this good news I was very disappointed to read that residents in West Sussex have mounted a legal objection to drills that go under their property. Frustrating that once again NIMBYs are preventing forward progress.
         </p>
         <p>
          I am not a fracking or drilling expert but from what I have read, drills dont just just go straight down vertically. The drills go down vertically but then change to drill horizontally. So as the drill head moves horizontally it will move outside the ground level boundaries of the land owned by the fracking company. The legal argument is therefore that the fracking company needs permission of the landowner at ground level before they can drill horizontally.
         </p>
         <p>
          I was surprised that ground level land owners can control what goes on below their house because there is a long history of coal mining in the UK and this must have been a problem for coal mines which are also horizontal. Also a land owner does not own the sky above the land because planes would then need permission to fly over each individuals land! However, it does appear that a land owners does own the ground under their land but not the sky above the land. So, land owners do have the legal power to prevent what goes on under their land and slow progress towards a mixed energy supply for the UK.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is disappointing so I was pleased to read that the government is investigating changing the law of trespass so that drilling under land owners is easier.
         </p>
         <p class="note" style="width:85%;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;">
          NIMBYs must not be allowed to prevent drilling which will help to ensure that the UK has a secure energy supply through the development of a mixed energy supply strategy.
         </p>
         <p style="margin-bottom:0px;">
          Control and restrictions on drilling should be light to:
         </p>
         <ul style="margin-top:0px;">
          <li>
           prevent multiple companies drilling the same geographical area
          </li>
          <li>
           ensure that there is geological information that justifies investigative drilling which may or may not be followed by the implementation of a commercial drilling
          </li>
         </ul>
