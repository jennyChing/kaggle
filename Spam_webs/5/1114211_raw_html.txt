            <h1>
             <strong>
              IT
             </strong>
             <span style="font-weight: normal; font-size: 15.6px;">
              is the biggest factor for the most prestigious position in professional sports. Coaches and general managers lose jobs searching for the right one. If you dont know that were talking about quarterbacks here, then you can go back into the hole you just crawled out of. In Super Bowl XLV, well be treated to two of the elite quarterbacks in the league. They dont get it done in the same way, but there are many different paths on the way to success.
             </span>
            </h1>
            <p>
             We begin with The Chico Kid. Its been said that you never want to be the one who follows a legend; UCLA basketball coaches can attest to that. So can every Steelers QB between Bradshaw and Ben. Aaron Rodgers has filled the void left by Brett Favruh in seamless fashion. Do you even remember the shots of Rodgers sitting idly by while such household names like Alex Smith, Troy Williamson, and Erasmus James were drafted ahead of him? Well, even if you dont, you can believe that
             <em>
              Rodgers
             </em>
             does.
            </p>
            <p>
             His play on the field this postseason has been classic. Hes as accurate as a sniper whether in the pocket or on the move, and hes blessed with one of the best receiving corps in the league. Impressively, most of his damage is done without the benefit of a strong running game or, this year, his most dynamic target, tight end Jermichael Finley. Some would pick him first to start a franchise with. I would take AP, but Rodgers isnt far down on my list.
            </p>
            <p>
             His counterpart in this matchup is the complete opposite of Rodgers. Big Ben isnt as accurate. Ben poo-poos the clean cut image as he dons an offensive lineman beard. To top it off, hes built like an ox, and to paraphrase Trevor Pryce, wears more pads than anyone in the league, and is the hardest to bring down. Another Jets lineman, Shaun Ellis, said this, Hes not scared to take a hit. You see him have three, four guys on him and still get the ball downfield. In other words, hes the perfect leader for the Steelers.
            </p>
            <p>
             He just gets it done. Youll hear this all week long. Dont look at the passing statistics to measure Roethlisbergers worth. Look at his postseason record, 11-2, including 2 Super Bowl victories. Hes done it at home, and on the road. The Steelers dont ask him to be a world beater, but he does have the ability as evidenced in the 09 meeting against Green Bay when he threw for 503 yards in a 38-37 win.
            </p>
            <p>
             What both these quarterbacks
             <em>
              do
             </em>
             have is the IT factor. Its easy to tell who has IT when you see them play the game, and its easy to tell which ones dont. Rivers, Manning, and Brees are some signal callers that come to mind with IT; McNabb, Cutler, and Palmer are some that come to mind that dont and never will. IT makes players follow you to the end. IT slows games down in the clutch while others are lost. IT gets you rings. Well see which one of these leaders can get the job done on February 6
             <sup>
              th
             </sup>
             .
            </p>
