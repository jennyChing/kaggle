      <h2>
       Building Scissor Horses
       <a href="http://wonderfulwoodworking.com/building-scissor-horses/ndugyt5/" rel="attachment wp-att-2742">
        <img alt="saw horse scissor" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2742" height="225" src="http://wonderfulwoodworking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/ndugyt5-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
       </a>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Building scissor horses is becoming more and more popular all the time in the woodworking shop. Being very inexpensive to build, they are very compact and can be used for many applications around the shop.
      </p>
      <p>
       The name scissor horses means just that. When in use, the horses legs are spread open allowing a 24 to be inserted into the upper jaws of the horse and then with a ratchet strap attached to the lower leg bracing, it is then tightened pinching the 24 securely in position.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Constructing Scissor Horses
      </h2>
      <p>
       The constructing of scissor horses is relatively easy to do. Most of the material can be gathered from scrap wood in the shop.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://wonderfulwoodworking.com/building-scissor-horses/ndugz5t/" rel="attachment wp-att-2743">
        <img alt="ndugz5t" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2743" height="225" src="http://wonderfulwoodworking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/ndugz5t-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
       </a>
       By looking at the photos and understanding the concept, the project can be shop built in very little time using 24s.
      </p>
      <p>
       To start out, it is a good idea to draw out a full size plan on a large piece of cardboard to help you determine the angle of the upper scissors. Once this has been determined, the build can proceed using ready rod or threaded rod with flat washers and shelf locking nuts as the pivot point of the scissor horses. This rod also serves as a carrying handle when the horse is folded up.
      </p>
      <div class="wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle" style="width:620px; margin-bottom:10px;">
       <div style="float:left; display:block; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1.5em;">

       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       By attaching 24 cross braces at the bottom of the legs, a ratchet strap can be used to pull the legs together securing the material in the jaws.
      </p>
      <p>
       These particular scissor horses were built for use with a miter saw. By building a deck on the 24 cross piece, the miter saw can sit on there. An adjustable material support the height of the miter saw bed canbe doweled to be placed in different locations along the 24.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Different Uses Of The Scissor Horses
      </h2>
      <p>
       The scissor horses you build can be customized to the job you want to use them for.
       <a href="http://wonderfulwoodworking.com/building-scissor-horses/ndugzhf/" rel="attachment wp-att-2744">
        <img alt="scissor horse" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2744" height="225" src="http://wonderfulwoodworking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/ndugzhf-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       By building two scissor horses perhaps a little higher than the miter saw horse, with the use of two, you could place a deck on them and have an instant work wench.
      </p>
