     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Single_Action_Army">
       Single Action Army
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      True to his real life counterpart, General George S. Patton (
      <a href="/wiki/George_C._Scott" title="George C. Scott">
       George C. Scott
      </a>
      ) in the film carries an ivory-gripped
      <a href="/wiki/Single_Action_Army" title="Single Action Army">
       Single Action Army
      </a>
      in his holster with his initials "GSP" engraved on the grip. When a reporter asks him about his "pearl-handled revolvers", Patton quickly retorts
      <i>
       "They're ivory. Only a pimp from a cheap New Orleans whorehouse would carry a pearl-handled pistol."
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      -Fact: George C. Scott's Patton (and his real life counterpart) only carries one ivory handle Colt 1873 Single Action Army. The second pistol he carried was an ivory-handled Smith and Wesson .357 'Registered Magnum', later given the designation Model 27. The Single Action Army was engraved and had an Eagle carved into the left grip panel. Both the Registered Magnum and Single Action Army also had Patton's monogram on the right grip panel.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Smith_.26_Wesson_.357_Registered_Magnum">
       Smith &amp; Wesson .357 Registered Magnum
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Patton's signature ivory-handled SAA is replaced by a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smith_%26_Wesson_Model_27" title="Smith &amp; Wesson Model 27">
       Smith &amp; Wesson Registered Magnum
      </a>
      with his "GSP" initials engraved on it for the shot during his opening speech. It is otherwise seen at points in the film being carried in combination with his Colt SAA revolver. General Patton purchased the Smith &amp; Wesson 'Registered Magnum' (known later as the Model 27) in 1935 directly from Smith &amp; Wesson. He had the nickname "Two Guns" as he wore matching Colt SAA that he had until giving one away as a gift. He then wore that Smith &amp; Wesson in place of the one given away. He also wore Colt 1908 Hammerless Pistol and a
      <a href="/wiki/Remington_Model_51" title="Remington Model 51">
       Remington Model 51
      </a>
      pistol.
     </p>

     </h1>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="M48_.22Patton.22_Tanks">
       M48 "Patton" Tanks
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The tanks used in the film to represent German armor are in reality anachronistic US made M48's, also known as the "Patton". These were likely on loan from the Spanish Army, who provided a variety of military equipment for the film. These tanks were painted in desert camouflage with German markings for the production.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tnone">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:602px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Patton_411.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" height="272" src="/images/thumb/b/bc/Patton_411.jpg/600px-Patton_411.jpg" width="600"/>

        The tanks used in the film to represent German armor are in reality anachronistic US made M48's, also known as the "Patton".
