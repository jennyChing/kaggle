               <p>
                The Australian heatwave that has forced many from their homes and left
                <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/gallery/2013/jan/08/australian-heatwave-sparks-fires-in-pictures" target="_blank">
                 100 people missing
                </a>
                has now made it impossible to pump gas in one town in the southern Outback. Oodnadattayes, that is the town's real namehas hit a scorching 119 degrees Fahrenheit, only a four degrees off the national record, which the town also holds.
               </p>
               <blockquote>
                "The ground, the building, everything is so hot, you walk outside and you feel it's going to burn you," Pink Roadhouse owner Lynnie Plate said. Mrs. Plate said the Roadhouse couldn't serve unleaded fuel after midday because it was vapourising and wouldn't pump in the extreme heat.
               </blockquote>
               <p>
                Earlier this week, the countrys weather bureau

                 added new colors
                </a>
                to its heat index due to soaring temperatures across the center of the continent. God speed to all of the kangaroos out there trying to keep cool.
               </p>

                                          &lt;p&gt;A community in the far northern region of Quebec is asking for government assistance to help &lt;a href="http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/north/story/2013/01/09/north-inukjuak-orcas-trapped.html?cmp=rss" target="_blank"&gt;free roughly a dozen killer whales&lt;/a&gt; that have become trapped under the sea ice. Inukjuak mayor Peter Inukpuk asked the government on Wednesday to send an icebreaker to help free the whales, which have been videotaped gathered around the same hole in the ice, gasping for air.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;
&lt;object width="640" height="373" data="http://www.youtube.com/v/VUs7tsgUlj0" type="application/x-shockwave-flash"&gt;
&lt;param name="data" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/VUs7tsgUlj0" /&gt;
&lt;param name="src" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/VUs7tsgUlj0" /&gt;
&lt;/object&gt;
&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Time is running out for the whales, as the hole is rapidly shrinking. A Department of Fisheries and Oceans spokeman said in a statement that they are sending representatives to assess the situation, but indicated that they might not be able to help.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;According to cetacean researcher Christian Ramp, who spoke to &lt;a href="http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/north/story/2013/01/09/north-inukjuak-orcas-trapped.html?cmp=rss" target="_blank"&gt;&lt;em&gt;CBC News&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/a&gt;, the trapped whales may be indicative of disturbing fallout from climate change.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;blockquote&gt;He said what makes this case unique is that it could be the first sighting of killer whales in the Canadian Arctic in January. Unlike narwhals, belugas, and bowheads, orcas are not an ice-loving species, Ramp said, following their prey north during the summer months but retreating before the ice moves in. He said with climate change, it appears the animals are straying further and further north&mdash;and perhaps, staying too long.&lt;/blockquote&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Hopefully this plays out like Operation Breakthrough in 1988, in which icebreakers were able to free two of three gray whales trapped in the Beaufort Sea&mdash;and not like the 2005 incident in the Japanese Arctic where the whales&rsquo; hole froze over and they drowned.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;UPDATE&lt;/strong&gt;: A small boy with a big heart has helped the whales jump to safety. Kidding: According to ABC News, &lt;a href="http://abcnews.go.com/International/killer-whales-trapped-canadian-sea-ice-free/story?id=18180286" target="_blank"&gt;the floes shifted and the orcas were able to swim to out&lt;/a&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;

                                          &lt;p&gt;At a loose end? Possess no real skill set, but eager to prove your mettle? Why not apply for Earth's first interplanetary settlement!&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The Dutch company Mars One, which &lt;a href="http://www.outsideonline.com/news-from-the-field/Dutch-Startup-To-Colonize-Mars-By-2023-20120606.html" target="_blank"&gt;seven months ago announced its intention to establish a series of permanent colonies on Mars&lt;/a&gt;, has released their selection criteria for their 2023 one-way mission. And, surprisingly, they're not asking much.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;blockquote&gt;Applicants need to be at least 18 years of age, have a deep sense of purpose, willingness to build and maintain healthy relationships, the capacity for self-reflection, and ability to trust. They must be resilient, adaptable, curious, creative, and resourceful.&lt;br /&gt;&lt;/blockquote&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Other requirements: "You have a 'Can do!' attitude."; "You have a good sense of play and spirit of playfulness[!]"&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Not exactly NASA, is it? The private company has previously promised that the mission will be the greatest "media  spectacle" our solar system has ever seen. "The whole world will be  watching. Big Brother will pale in comparison,"  said team member Dr.  Gerard T. Hooft, a Nobel Prize winner in theoretical physics, without a grain of irony.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Mars One goes on to say they're not seeking specific skill sets as "all   skills  required on Mars will be learned while in training." (Like how  to &lt;a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Planet_%28film%29" target="_blank"&gt; fight off a swarm of Martian insects with a debonair flourish&lt;/a&gt;.)&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;If you're interested in joining an experimental space community with a group of high-spirited, sensitive folks who, y'know, aren't doctors or geologists or anything fancy, &lt;a href="http://mars-one.com/en/faq-en/21-faq-selection/251-do-i-qualify-to-apply/www" target="_blank"&gt;apply here&lt;/a&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;

                  &lt;div class="content post-entry post-entry--spacious has-dropcap"&gt;
                                          &lt;p&gt;Boulder residents outraged by the &lt;a href="http://www.dailycamera.com/news/boulder/ci_22304687?source=pkg" target="_blank"&gt;New Year's Day killing of a neighborhood elk&lt;/a&gt; by a police officer voiced their concerns at a meeting with the city's chief of police on Monday. Mark Beckner told attendees that his department was conducting an internal investigation, and assured them that the officer implicated, who is on paid leave, would be fired if necessary. "If we didn't think there were issues, we would not be investigating," he said. "It appears policies and procedure weren't followed. Now we need to either prove or disprove that."&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;According to the &lt;em&gt;Daily Camera&lt;/em&gt;, that did not satisfy some Boulder residents.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;blockquote&gt;But some members of the crowd did not take the same approach, with one comparing the paid leave to putting "murderers on vacation." Beckner also said he has received emails from people going as far as asking that the officers be executed.&lt;/blockquote&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The controversy start on January 1, when Boulder police officer Sam Carter shot the elk in Boulder's Mapleton Hill neighborhood. Afterward, he called off-duty police officer and part-time taxidermist Brent Curnow, who, with the assistance of a sheriff's deputy, loaded the elk into a pickup truck and brought it home to process it.  While the officers claimed the elk was wounded, a Boulder police spokesperson said the next day that the pair had not reported the incident to the department as required.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Residents of Mapleton Hill have widely criticized the killing of the friendly elk, whom they called "our guardian." (Though &lt;a href="http://www.dailycamera.com/news/boulder/ci_22298787?source=pkg"&gt;at least one person says the elk had behaved aggressively toward people in the past&lt;/a&gt;.) In a uniquely Boulder response, a local group &lt;a href="http://www.dailycamera.com/news/boulder/ci_22304687?source=pkg" target="_blank"&gt;organized a vigil last Thursday&lt;/a&gt;&mdash;complete with candles and pictures of the elk&mdash;to "celebrate the life" of the animal.&lt;/p&gt;
