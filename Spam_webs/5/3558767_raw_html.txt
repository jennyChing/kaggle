        Doomsday Preppers on National Geographic
       </h1>

        <p>
         Prepare for the worst and come out on top. Thats the motto of Doomsday Preppers, the new documentary series on the National Geographic Channel. The show profiles families who believe cataclysmic disaster is inevitable. They prepare for D-day with stockpiles, evacuation plans, emergency kits and secure bunkers. Many of the profiled families describe themselves as survivalist and regularly require their children to practice disaster drills by helping transport survival food and shooting potential enemy targets. Although some Doomsday Preppers live on secure compounds in the mountains, others live on densely-populated suburban streets, or even downtown metropolitan areas. They come from a range of religious, political and socio-economic backgrounds, but their one unifying factor is a commitment to preparing for the worst.
        </p>
        <h2>
         The goal of Doomsday Preppers is surviving disaster through preparation and self-sufficiency.
        </h2>
        <p>
         One family the show profiled detailed an intricate, three-point evacuation plan that would transport themselves, their animals, survival food and supplies to an undisclosed, underground destination. Another family prepared for catastrophic food shortages and power outages by converting their swimming pool into a self-sustaining green house for produce, fish and chickens.
        </p>
        <p>
         While the methods of many Doomsday Preppers might seem extreme, they employ several important survival tips that all families should keep in mind.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Rehearse Your Plan
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Whether youre preparing for Armageddon or a major house fire, rehearsing the roles and responsibilities of each family member is important when instituting an evacuation or survival plan. Decide where youll go, what youll bring and how youll get there before disaster strikes. Practice evacuating your home with your children and designate a safe meeting place where everyone will meet.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Build Your Stockpile
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         A stockpile of dead batteries and expired canned goods is useless. A well-built stockpile of food, medical supplies, batteries and gasoline can make the difference between life and death during a disaster. Take inventory of your stockpile every six months and check battery-powered devices and replace expired food. Choose items specific to your familys medical needs or food preferences. Most of the Doomsday Preppers are either entirely self-sufficient or have large stockpiles capable of sustaining their family for decades, if necessary. Ideally, you should have at least one weeks worth of supplies, including food, non-electrical power sources and medical equipment.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Streamline Your Supplies for All Types of Disasters
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         In the event of an instantaneous disaster, you wont necessarily have time to transport years worth of stockpiled supplies. Streamline the absolute basic necessities into multiple bug out bags. The term, bug out refers to the immediate employment of a disaster plan. A bug out bag typically holds 72-hours worth of necessities for you and your family. Items such as freeze-dried food, canned goods, crank-powered flashlights, medication and a can opener. It can take several days before roads reopen or help arrives, and a bug out bag prepares you while you wait for help or during your in-route transition to a more secure facility. Assign each member of your family his own bug out bag to make the evacuation process even more expedient. The families profiled on Doomsday Preppers have multiple bug out bags that include solar-powered equipment, vacuum-packaged food, maps, and occasionally, artillery.
