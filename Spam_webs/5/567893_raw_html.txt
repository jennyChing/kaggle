    <h1>
     Solar Architecture
    </h1>
    <br/>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     The future is here. Gone are the old days a building a house without any thought to its environmental impact. Today, architecture design seamlessly integrates solar and other sustainable materials and technologies. We call it passive solar home design, and its making waves around the world.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      What is solar architecture?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Solar architecture  or passive solar home design  weaves solar technology with contemporary construction techniques.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Its a combination of orientation (that is, how the house is positioned on the block), renewable materials and creating open, light spaces that allow air to flow. And of course, it features solar panels to generate energy and reduce the households reliance on the electricity grid.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     In essence, a solar home takes the load of heating and cooling, and makes up for the shortfall with solar. Its smart, its sustainable and it could save you bucket loads in the long run.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      How does passive solar home design work?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     A smart solar home is designed around the principles of thermal mass. This is simply a process whereby heat is collected as the sun penetrates the north-facing windows. This heat is retained by special building materials. So the trick is to choose building materials and features with favourable thermal mass or light dispersing features.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Here are the elements your architect will consider when designing your solar home:
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <strong>
       Thermal mass:
      </strong>
      Excellent thermal mass materials include brick, stone and tiles.
     </li>
     <li>
      <strong>
       Windows:
      </strong>
      Properly positioned windows mean maximum sunlight and heat retention.
     </li>
     <li>
      <strong>
       Heat distribution:
      </strong>
      Fans, blowers and other features help distribute solar heat throughout the home, while colour choice can also keep the interiors climate controlled.
     </li>
     <li>
      <strong>
       Heat control:
      </strong>
      There are endless innovations that architects integrate into solar homes, such as efficient shutters and blinds, shading structures and savvy thermostats.
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Your building designer will also consider insulation, heating and cooling options, ventilation and cutting-edge techniques to seamlessly blend solar into a sophisticated building design.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      <img alt="Modern architects seamlessly integrates solar and other sustainable materials and technologies in their designs." src="http://www.3quotes.com.au/content/images/solar-architecture.jpg" style="margin: 10px; float: right; width: 500px; height: 334px;"/>
      Choosing a site for your solar home
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     If you want to create an eco-aware abode, you should enlist an architect who has experience designing sustainable properties. They will be aware of the orientation, styles and other considerations crucial to passive solar home design.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     In Australia, the sun shines down on us from the North. So in a smart solar home, the north side of the property will enjoy unobstructed access to sunlight. You also need to choose a specific block of land that will provide the
     <a href="http://www.3quotes.com.au/solar-power/should-solar-panels-face-north.html">
      best solar orientation
     </a>
     . This all depends on where you live.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      A roof thats ready for solar
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Savvy architects will also know how to design the
     <a href="http://www.3quotes.com.au/solar-power/what-is-the-best-roof-type-for-solar-panels.html">
      right roof for solar panels
     </a>
     . Its must easier to design your roof with solar in mind, rather than try to retrofit panels to an existing structure.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     In Australia, a tilt of 18 or 22.5 degrees is standard, and suitable for solar. Again, the tilt depends on where you live, due to the position of the sun. In Sydney, solar panels should be tilted at 34 degrees; in Perth a 31 degree tilt is best.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Building your solar home
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     When all these elements combine, the result is a cutting-edge, solar-powered home that reduces environmental impact and produces significant savings over time. Your architect or solar expert can consult with you to design your very own solar home.
    </p>
