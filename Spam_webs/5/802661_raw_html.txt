      <p>
       I harbor romantic notions of myself as a farm girl. Id gather fresh eggs every morning, milk my goats and make cheese in the afternoon, then cook cozy dinners like Butternut Squash Casserole with Sausage, Kale, and Sage Breadcrumbs, using ingredients picked from my land of course.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Butternut Squash Casserole with Sausage" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8654" height="884" src="http://www.wellplated.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Butternut-Squash-Casserole-with-Sausage.jpg" width="575"/>
       In farm girl reality, Id whine about ruining my shoes in the chicken coop and provoke a goat bite. I still, however, hold fast to the beautiful notion of cooking from what the earth provides right where I am, right noweven if I pick from the grocery store bin, instead of my backyard.
      </p>
      <p>
       This concept of seasonal eating became real to me when I first started cooking for myself. Directly out of college and on a budget, I quickly realized that seasonal ingredients both cost lest than out-of-season ones and taste much better too. What began as a logical budgetary decision has turned into a passion, and I now look forward to the way the colors in my grocery cart change as the year progresses.
       <br/>
       <span id="more-8268">
       </span>
      </p>
      <div style="display: none;">
       <img alt="Healthy" src='http://www.wellplated.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Healthy-Butternut-Squash-Sausage-Casserole-with-Sage-Breadcrumbs.jpg"'/>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Butternut Squash Casserole with Crumb Topping" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8619" height="819" src="http://www.wellplated.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Butternut-Squash-Casserole-with-Crumb-Topping.jpg" width="575"/>
       My farm girl alter ego came into being after reading
       <em>
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0060852569/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0060852569&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thlastswi-20&amp;linkId=T3JGGQNE6MBMUKYA" target="_blank">
         Animal Vegetable Miracle
        </a>
       </em>
       by Barbara Kingsolver. Though Kingsolver has written some of my favorite fictional works,
       <em>
        Animal Vegetable Miracle
       </em>
       is the true story of how she and her family moved to an inherited farm in Appalachia and ate only what they could grow themselves or buy within 100 miles for a full year. Its an enthralling read and inspired this self-proclaimed city girl to dream about country life. Two days ago, I asked Ben if he ever thought it would be neat to live on a farm. He responded with an emphatic
       <em>
        NO
       </em>
       . My suede booties were relieved, but in my defense, Ben hasnt read the book.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Healthy Butternut Squash Casserole with Chicken Sausage" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8621" height="863" src="http://www.wellplated.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Healthy-Butternut-Squash-Casserole-with-Chicken-Sausage.jpg" width="575"/>
       Though Im certainly not a seasonal-eating purist (last February,I bought a $6 pint of raspberries just to remember what summer tastes like), dishes such as this Butternut Squash Casserole with Sausage and Kale remind me that even chilly winter offers ingredients to celebrate. The sweet butternut squash and hearty kale, together with spicy-sweet sausage and rosemary create a vibrant casserole filling so tasty, I snacked on itby the light of myrefrigerator door.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Healthy Butternut Squash Casserole with Sausage and Kale" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8622" height="922" src="http://www.wellplated.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Healthy-Butternut-Squash-Casserole-with-Sausage-and-Kale.jpg" width="575"/>
       Though the butternut squash casserole filling is lovely on its own, it is the breadcrumb topping that makes this recipe something special. I was inspired by a butternut squash crumble recipe in David Lebovtiz latest cookbook
       <em>
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1607742675/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=1607742675&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thlastswi-20&amp;linkId=BPPF7EW3ZDBUKPDG" target="_blank">
         My Paris Kitchen
        </a>
       </em>
       . Davids version is intended to be a side dish and is rather decadent, but I was so captivated by the idea of a savory (vs. dessert) crumble, I had to try my own version.
      </p>
      <p>
       I transformed the original sideinto an all-in-one main dish by adding chicken sausage and kale, shortened the ingredient list to make it more accessible on a weeknight, and adapted the herbs to suit what I had on hand. Davids ingenious cornmeal, breadcrumb, Parmesan, and sage topping, however, had to stay.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Butternut Squash Casserole with Cornmeal Topping" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8618" height="863" src="http://www.wellplated.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Butternut-Squash-Casserole-with-Cornmeal-Topping.jpg" width="575"/>
       I lightened up the topping by swapping some of the butter for
       <a href="http://www.pompeian.com/products/olive-oils/extra-virgin.aspx" rel="no follow" target="_blank">
        Pompeian Extra Virgin Olive Oil
       </a>
       and used whole wheat breadcrumbs. It still turned out perfectly golden and richno one will ever notice the healthy substitution.
      </p>
      <p>
       I cant say enough wonderful things about this butternut squash casserole. The filling is hearty and flavorful, and the topping is a delightful combination of sweet, salty, cheesy, crunchy. It can be prepped ahead and then baked in the evening, and it tastes fantastic leftover too. This butternut squash casserole is also loaded with veggies and lean protein. Youll feel like a super star for eating comforting food thats good for you too.
      </p>

If the neighbors ask questions, Ill just take them over a big pan of Butternut Squash Casserole with Sausage and Kale (and maybe some cheese). Im sure theres no misunderstanding we cant resolve over plates of comfort food.

       <h2 itemprop="name">
        Butternut Squash Casserole with Sausage, Kale, and Sage Breadcrumbs
       </h2>
       <div class="summary" itemprop="description" style="clear:left">
        <p>
         A healthy casserole with butternut squash, chicken sausage, and kale, topped with a golden sage breadcrumb topping. This flavorful recipe is sure to be a family favorite!
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="time">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Yield:
         </strong>
         <span itemprop="recipeYield">
          Serves 6
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Prep Time:
         </strong>
         <meta content="PT20M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
         20 minutes
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Total Time:
         </strong>
         <meta content="PT1H45M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
         1 hour 45 minutes
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3 id="ingredients">
        Ingredients:
       </h3>
       <div class="ingredients">
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
          For the Butternut Squash and Sausage Filling
         </span>
        </h4>
        <ul>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           3 tablespoons Pompeian Extra Virgin Olive Oil, divided
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1 pound chicken or turkey sausage, casings removed
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           3-pound butternut squash, peeled, seeded and diced into 3/4-inch cubes
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1 tablespoon mined fresh rosemary
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1 teaspoon minced garlic (about 2 cloves)
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1/4 teaspoon black pepper
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           12 ounces kale, Swiss chard, or other hearty leafy green, tough stems removed and torn into bite-sized pieces
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1/2 cup thinly sliced yellow onion (about 1/2 large)
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1 cup low sodium chicken stock
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
          For the Crumble Topping
         </span>
        </h4>
        <ul>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           3 slices whole wheat bread (or 3/4 cup breadcrumbs)
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1/2 cup stone ground cornmeal or polenta
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1 tablespoon minced fresh sage
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1 teaspoon granulated sugar
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           2 tablespoons Pompeian Extra Virgin Olive Oil
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           2 tablespoons cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           1 large egg
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
       <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
        Directions:
       </h3>
       <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
        <ol>
         <li>
          Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F. Generously grease a shallow 3-quart baking dish with softened butter or cooking spray.
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           Prepare the squash and sausage filling: Heat 1 tablespoon of the olive oil in a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Brown and crumble the sausage until fully cooked, about 5 minutes. Remove from the pan and set aside. Being careful not to burn yourself, wipe out the pan with a paper towel.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           Cook the butternut squash filling in two batches (this will ensure the ingredients brown properly): Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in the skillet, then add half of each of the following: the squash, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Saut until the squash is beginning to brown, then add half of: the kale, the onions, and the garlic. Cook until the kale and onions begin to soften, about 4 minutes. Add the 1/2 cup of the chicken stock, stir, and let cook 1 minute so that the stock reduces slightly. Stir in half of the reserved sausage. Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish and press into a relatively even layer.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           Heat the remaining tablespoon olive oil in the skillet, then repeat the above step with the remaining half of each of the ingredients. Transfer the rest of the filling to the casserole dish. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes or until the squash is soft when pierced with a sharp knife.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           While the squash bakes, prepare the topping: Place the slices of bread in the bowl of a food processor and pulse into crumbs (if using ready-made breadcrumbs, simply add them to the food processor without pulsing). Add the cornmeal, Parmesan, sage, sugar, and salt. Plus a few times to combine. Drizzle in the olive oil, add the butter cubes, and pulse until crumbly and the butter is fully incorporated. Add the egg and pulse a few additional times, until the mixture just begins to clump together. (Alternatively, you can make in a mixing bowl, cutting in the butter by hand. In this case, you will need prepared breadcrumbs).
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="color: #5b5b5b; font-family: inherit; font-size: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; line-height: inherit; background-color: transparent;">
           Remove the casserole from the oven, take off the foil, then sprinkle the top evenly with the breadcrumb mixture. Reduce the oven temperature to 350 degrees F, return the dish to the oven, then bake uncovered, until the bread crumbs are golden, about 20 minutes. Serve warm.
          </span>
         </li>
        </ol>
       </div>
