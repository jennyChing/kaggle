         Inception
        </span>
       </h2>
       <div class="section_body">
        <p>
         With its multiple layers of time and space, Inception weaves a complex tale centering on the idea of invading someone's dreams to plant an idea in their head.  It's similar in some regards to Ocean's 11, except far more complicated and conceptual. The major misconception centers around the top in the movie's final sequence--will it fall, or will it remain spinning? The director wanted that final scene to represent the film's pervasive sense of ambiguity as well as the fact that Leonardo DiCaprio's character has left that behind and is focusing on his children.

         The Dark Knight Rises
        </span>
       </h2>
       <div class="section_body">
        <p>
         The most controversial element of The Dark Knight Rises is the scene where Batman carries the bomb out to sea seconds before it explodes making it nearly impossible for him to have survived the incident. Moments later, viewers see the closing scene of Alfred, Bruce's Butler sitting down in a cafe in Florence where he spies Bruce Wayne, alive and well, seated next to Selina Kyle. While many fans believe it's unclear whether the scene was supposed to represent Alfred's wishful thinking or reality, it was not intended to be ambiguous. The movie specifically points out the autopilot feature of The Bat and emphasizes that Bruce fixed it. The fact that Selina Kyle is with Bruce in Florence further shows that this is not a dream or hallucination because Alfred wouldn't have known about Selina.

At the end of the film, people think that Donnie has travelled back in time to kill himself when the jet engine hits his house. In fact, Donnie never travels through time in the movie.  Donnie constructs a time portal from water and uses telekinesis to rip the jet engine off the plane and send it through the portal where it kills him to destroy the second universe created at the beginning of the film.

         Atonement
        </span>

Viewers mistakenly believe that the ending is an attack on Briony for accusing Robert of sexual abuse and causing the entire incident.  In reality, Briony represents how human nature is to fictionalize things and hide from the truth, and the resulting impact when that fiction is taken away.

         Blue Valentine
        </span>
       </h2>

The film has an ambiguous ending, but it hints that the marriage is over.  The misinterpretation is that people think that the movie is sad because of the ending of their relationship.  What is really sad though, is that they were together at all, Dean had nothing else in his life but Cindy.  Dean wasn't the bad guy, they were just in a bad situation.

Thus, his older self is not there to kill Sara and Cid never becomes 'The Rainmaker.'Teddy Daniels is actually the missing asylum patient he is searching for, and his entire persona is nothing more than a delusion.
