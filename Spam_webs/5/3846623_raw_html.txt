         Seven ways you know you're an African, according to Jacob Zuma
        </h1>

       <section class="content_blurb">
        <p>
         From knowing corruption is a "Western paradigm" to abhorring dogs and gay people: welcome to Jacob Zuma's idea of what it means to be an African.
        </p>
       </section>
       <section class="content_body group">
        <figure class="" group="" id="content_photo">
         <a href="http://mg.co.za/article/2014-10-14-seven-ways-you-know-youre-an-african-according-to-jacob-zuma" title="">

          <figcaption data-animate="bottom" data-overlay="127" data-overlay-reset="253" data-overlay-reset-h="">
           <p class="hidden">
            <em>
             The rest of us have been wrangling with the concept of what it means to be an African when this whole time Zuma has had it down pat. (Gallo)
            </em>
           </p>
          </figcaption>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <div id="body_content">
         <p>
          Thabo Mbeki may have written the definitive speech on being an African, but his successor Jacob Zuma is determined to rewrite the script.
         </p>
         <p>
          Zuma apparently believes that the dubious financial relationship he enjoyed with his previous adviser Schabir Shaik, involving illicit loans to Zuma, was not corrupt  if you thought about it as an African.
         </p>
         <p>
          Western paradigm brands this criminal, read his written submission to the National Prosecuting Authority in 2009 to have charges against him dropped,
          <a href="http://www.citypress.co.za/politics/corruption-western-thing/">
           according to a City Press report
          </a>
          published on Sunday.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our president has a bad rep. The rest of us have been wrangling with the concept of what it means to be an African when this whole time Zuma has had it down pat. 
He didnt need to make an
          <a href="http://www.anc.org.za/show.php?id=4322">
           epic speech on the matter like Mbeki did
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Instead, hes been dropping hints throughout his two terms in power so far. Youre only truly African if:
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1)	Youre not too clever
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Clever blacks are a scourge upon the continent, according to Zumas speech to the National House of Traditional Leaders in Parliament in November 2012. Even some Africans, who become too clever, take a position, they become the most eloquent in criticizing themselves about their own traditions and everything.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2)	Youll never need a lawyer
          </strong>
          <br/>
          His
          <a href="http://www.citypress.co.za/news/zuma-scolds-clever-blacks-20121103/">
           off the cuff remarks
          </a>
          on the above occasion included a rant about prisons, lawyers, and other white man ways, over the African way. Whats that about the Constitution? Yeah, Im not sure either.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3)	You have crappy roads
          </strong>
          <br/>
          We may not need lawyers or prisons in Africa but we DO need e-tolls. Because otherwise well be too African. Apparently one needs just the right amount of African-ness in Zumas books. We cant think like Africans in Africa. Its not some national road in Malawi, he told an audience at the University of the Witwatersrand in October 2013 when questioned about the logic of the deeply unpopular e-tolling system.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           4)	You know that a little corruption between friends is totally fine
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Can there be anything more insulting than conflating the West with accountability and clean governance? In labelling the idea of corruption a Western paradigm, Zumas lawyers have said that Zuma as an African subscribes to a different culture where corrupt dealings, such as the one that existed between him and Shaik, are ok. Because Africans have a lower standard when it comes to ethics?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5)	If you see a gay person, you would knock them down
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Zuma may have apologised for this one, but its hard to forget the time he told people in September 2006 that he would knock down an
          <em>
           ungqingili
          </em>
          , or homosexual in Zulu. Same sex marriage is a disgrace to the nation and to God. When I was growing up,
          <em>
           ungqingili
          </em>
          could not stand in front of me, I would knock him out.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           6)	You know that when a woman wears a kanga, its business time
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Some of Zumas more
          <a href="http://mg.co.za/article/2006-04-03-zuma-describes-sex-session-to-court">
           bizarre comments
          </a>
          during his rape trial in 2006, in which he was later acquitted, included the idea that a woman wearing a wrap or a kanga, construed a sexual signal, and that in Zulu culture, you dont just leave a woman [when she is aroused] ... she will have you arrested and say you are a rapist.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           7)	You wouldnt dream of having a dog
          </strong>
          <br/>
          In a speech during the festive season in KwaZulu-Natal in 2012,
          <a href="http://www.iol.co.za/news/politics/pet-dogs-not-for-blacks-zuma-1.1445123#.VDxNkCmSzbw">
           Zuma made dog lovers everywhere doubt their African-ness
          </a>
          when he described those who loved dogs more than people as having a lack of humanity. He also weighed in on the great hair debate (move over Chris Rock) and told black people to stop trying out other cultures habits. Even if you apply any kind of lotion and straighten your hair you will never be white, Zuma said.
         </p>
         <p>
          With a list like that, youd be forgiven for doubting your African-ness  at least as it is currently defined by our president.
         </p>
