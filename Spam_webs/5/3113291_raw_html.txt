        <p>
         <a href="http://deflipside.com/wp-content/DS156.mp3">
          DeFlip Side #156: Best (and Worst) Reads of 2014
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Welcome everyone. Im Christopher DeFilippis and this is DeFlip Side.
        </p>
        <p>
         Break out your bifocals, because its time once again for DeFlip Sides annual best and worst reads show, where I count down the best and worst genre books Ive read in the past year. And Im happy to reintroduce a long-abandoned category this year: Best Graphic Novel.
        </p>
        <p>
         As always, the books featured werent necessarily published in the last year; hence the title Best Reads instead of Best Books.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, on with the list for 2014!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           Book Five:
          </b>
          <b>
           <em>
            City of Truth
           </em>
           by James Morrow
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div style="float:left; margin-right:20px;">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00E9501D8/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00E9501D8&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=desi0bb-20&amp;linkId=7PPBSK4ULIN6D44P" target="_blank">
          <br/>
          <img alt="City of Truth James Morrow" border="0" height="196" src="http://deflipside.com/wp-content/uploads/WiRCoTMorrow.jpg" style="border:none;" width="130"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Satirist James Morrow ushers in my picks for 2014 with this Hugo Award-winning novella.
        </p>
        <p>
         Veritas is the city of truth, where citizens lose their ability to lie, and where lying can mean death. And art critic Jack Sperry likes it that way, happily eradicating falsehoodsfrom sculptures to Shakespearefor the greater good. But when his son gets a terminal disease, Jack joins an underground faction of dissemblers in an attempt to learn to lie, hoping it will help save his sons life.
        </p>
        <p>
         Jacks personal journey underscores Morrows wickedly witty exploration into the nature of truth, making
         <em>
          City of Truth
         </em>
         a powerful, thought-provoking read.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           Book Four:
          </b>
          <b>
           <em>
            The Last Unicorn
           </em>
           by Peter S. Beagle
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div style="float:left; margin-right:20px;">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0451450523/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0451450523&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=desi0bb-20&amp;linkId=ONV2BJ75ZY3IAPUE" target="_blank">
          <br/>
          <img alt="Last Unicorn Peter Beagle" border="0" height="195" src="http://deflipside.com/wp-content/uploads/WiRUniBeagle.jpg" style="border:none;" width="130"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Beagle has landed on this list a lot, so I figured that it was past time for me to read his most popular book. And it was certainly worth the ribbing I took from friends for reading what they called, 13-year-old girl fiction.
        </p>
        <p>
         As the title indicates, the story is about a unicorn who fears shes the last of her kind, so she goes looking for her kin, befriending a floundering wizard named Schmendrick and an indomitable washer woman named Molly Grue during her misadventures.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          The Last Unicorn
         </em>
         is both a fairytale and a self-aware parody of fairytales. But its a terrific story, with wonderful characters and none of the snark that plagues most modern genre deconstructions. Beagles signature humor and sweet melancholy drive a picaresque tale of love, loss and adventure.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           Books Three and Two (TIE):
          </b>
          <b>
           <em>
            Parable of the Sower
           </em>
           and
           <em>
            Parable of the Talents
           </em>
           by Octavia E. Butler
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div style="float:left; margin-right:20px;">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0446675504/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0446675504&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=desi0bb-20" target="_blank">
          <br/>
          <img alt="Parable of the Sower Octavia Butler" border="0" height="202" src="http://deflipside.com/wp-content/uploads/WiRButlerPoS.jpg" style="border:none;" width="130"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Butler sucked me right in with this pair of dystopian Science Fiction novels, set in 2021 America where ecological and economic collapse have driven society into mayhem.
        </p>
        <p>
         The story centers on Lauren Olamina, an astute young woman with unique empathic abilities that allow her to feel others joys and pains. Burned out of her walled community, Lauren wanders bereft and alone, seeking a better life. Her wry first-person narrative brings her harrowing world to vivid life. And the crux of her journey is the formation of a philosophy she dubs Earthseed, a pragmatic belief system that helps adherents cope with their savage reality.
        </p>
        <div style="float:right; margin-left:20px;">
         <a href=" http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0446675784/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0446675784&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=desi0bb-20&amp;linkId=ASYKZ76FGTONUXRF" target="_blank">
          <br/>
          <img alt="Parable of the Talents Octavia Butler" border="0" height="194" src="http://deflipside.com/wp-content/uploads/WiRPoTButler.jpeg" style="border:none;" width="130"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         But Laurens struggle to survive with her fellow travelers in
         <em>
          Parable of the Sower
         </em>
         is just prelude.
         <em>
          Parable of the Talents
         </em>
         is where her true story begins. And boy is it a dark one. Butler pulls no punches with the brutal extremes of her future dystopia. And Laurens stubborn devotion to the ideals of Earthseed begin to conflict with the few good things she has managed to build in her life.
        </p>
        <p>
         But this unflinching honesty is what makes the novel so engaging, and what makes the ultimate resolution of Laurens story so affecting. Butlers
         <em>
          Parable
         </em>
         novels arent for the faint of heart. But readers who stick with them will be glad that they did.
        </p>
        <p>
         And speaking of glad, its time for my best read of the year.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           Book One:
           <em>
            Mighty Mighty
           </em>
           by William Freedman
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div style="float:left; margin-right:20px;">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0615985068/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0615985068&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=desi0bb-20&amp;linkId=GMCAE3PFAEKHOW2K" target="_blank">
          <br/>
          <img alt="Mighty Mighty Freedman" border="0" height="207" src="http://deflipside.com/wp-content/uploads/MightyMighty.jpg" style="border:none;" width="130"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         To call
         <em>
          Mighty Mighty
         </em>
         the most pleasant surprise of 2014 would be an understatement. This superhero satire goes from delightful to enthralling to shocking and its the perfect vehicle for Freedmans manic inventiveness and unbounded comedic style.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Mighty Mighty
         </em>
         is set in a world where super-powered people are so common that theyre mall cops. But when minions of the American Malevolence Institute descend on the Chesterfield Mall in St. Louis, the malls super contingent is forced to take up the fight: Orville Ortleya.k.a. Mucus-Man; mild-mannered Mindy Maguire, who must reluctantly transform into her distractingly beautiful alter-ego Supermodel; superslacker Bobby Botler, also known as The Blur; and their seemingly un-heroic backoffice manager Shel Shapiro.
        </p>
        <p>
         And thats just the beginning of a story that becomes so sprawling and intertwined that it gives DCs Silver and Bronze Ages a run for their money. Mighty Mighty is quick paced, funny and breezily embraces every convoluted comic book trapping. Freedmans scathing eye is all-encompassing, but every satirical tackle is grounded in genuine love and respect.
        </p>
        <p>
         Chock full of terrific characters, furious action and jaw-dropping plot twists,
         <em>
          Mighty Mighty
         </em>
         is my pick for the mightiest book of 2014.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           Best Graphic Novel:
          </b>
          <b>
           <em>
            Sex Criminals
           </em>
           (Vol. 1) by Matt Fraction and Chip Zdarsky
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div style="float:left; margin-right:20px;">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1607069466/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1607069466&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=desi0bb-20&amp;linkId=4ZJZH4JLVKJO65XB" target="_blank">
          <br/>
          <img alt="Sex Criminals Matt Fraction" border="0" height="195" src="http://deflipside.com/wp-content/uploads/WiRSCVol1.jpg" style="border:none;" width="130"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          Sex Criminals
         </em>
         is largely responsible for bringing me back into the comic-reading fold last year, and what a wonderful homecoming it has been.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Sex Criminals
         </em>
         is about Suzy, a girl who stops time whenever she has an orgasm. When she meets Jon, who has the same ability, they do what any loving couple in a similar situation would do: rob a bank.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Sex Criminals
         </em>
         is unique and intriguing, and despite its overtly sexual premise, it isnt juvenile or prurient. It deals frankly with sex, but within the context of a smart, character-driven story. And its maybe the funniest comic Ive ever read.
        </p>
        <p>
         Which puts it into stark contrast with my worst read of the year:
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           Worst Read:
           <em>
            The Ammonite Violin and Others
           </em>
           by Caitlin R. Kiernan
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div style="float:left; margin-right:20px;">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/159606305X/ref=as_li_tf_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=159606305X&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=desi0bb-20" target="_blank">
          <br/>
          <img alt="Ammonite Violin Kiernan" border="0" height="202" src="http://deflipside.com/wp-content/uploads/AmViolinKiernan.jpg" style="border:none;" width="130"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         This story collection had such promise, with its darkly fantastic, mythological bent. And Kiernan can really write.
        </p>
        <p>
         But this book is just a fraught downer. Every story is about sexual violence, and I can only tolerate so many variations on the theme of rape before punching out.
        </p>
        <p>
         So if you want stories with seeds of hope or transcendence, look elsewhere. But if you identify primarily as a victim and demand that the world bear witness to every scrap of your pain, this collection is right up your alley. You should feel at home wallowing in these pools of ice-cold emotional vomit.
        </p>
        <p>
         And there you have my list for 2014, the standouts in a year of mostly solid reads. You can purchase any of these books by clicking on their cover images. If you read any of them based on my recommendation, leave a comment and let me know what you thought. And if you have any titles of your own to recommend, Im all ears. Theres always room on the shelf for one more!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         -30-
        </p>
