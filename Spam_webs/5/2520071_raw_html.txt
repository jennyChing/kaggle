        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         The Art of Contouring: Advice for Every Face Shape
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-04-03T10:34:30+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          April 3, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            admin
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/the-art-of-contouring/#comments">
           1 Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p align="center" style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-header.jpg">
          <img alt="The Art of Makeup Contouring" class="size-full wp-image-16 aligncenter" height="521" src="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-header.jpg" width="960"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p align="center" style="text-align: left;">
         Picture this: Youre waiting in a check-out line, glancing from one glossy magazine to the next, when you notice your favorite celebrity sporting some serious cheekbones. Then for a split second you think,
         <i>
          hey I could do that
         </i>
         and head to your local Sephora. Only the overwhelming amount of powders, bronzers and blushes is enough to send you running straight from the store.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sound familiar? Youre not alone, says makeup artist Linda Stephenson. Contouring is the easiest way to achieve the celebrity look but with so much conflicting information on the topic, many women dont know where to start, she says. Some articles are written as though the typical woman has her own glam team to do this for her and spend hours making her look gorgeous when, for most women, the opposite is true.
         <i>
          Le sigh.
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         But since it
         <i>
          is
         </i>
         such a great way to add dimension to your face, dont let yourself get discourage. Instead, focus on the make-up and application tips meant specifically for your face shape. Using the correct contouring techniques for your face can be transformative, says beauty coach Sonia Baghdady.
        </p>
        <p>
         To narrow down the (too) many techniques, it helps to ask some questions. For example, do you want better cheekbones? Do you want a slimmer nose? A softer jaw line? A shorter chin? Allor noneof the above? Determining the look youre after is going to make the entire process considerably less complicated.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once you do, youre ready to go back to Sephora, or even the drug store, for products. When contouring the face, it is important to remember that applying a dark product is going to create a shadow, making your features appear more chiseled, says makeup artist and artistic director for Bodyography Professional Cosmetics Lori Leib, where as highlighting will makes your features pop.
        </p>
        <p>
         Leib recommends a matte cream or powder one to four shades darkerno shimmer!than your skin tone to accomplish a more sculpted look. Though if youre a beginner, stick to something one to two shades darker and gradually work your way up as you get more comfortable. When its all said and done, dont forget to blend as contour lines are a big beauty dont, adds Baghdady.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ready to give that cover model a run for her money? We thought so. Here are the simple steps you need to follow for every face shape.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-round.jpg">
          <img alt="Makeup Contouring for Round Face Shapes" class="size-full wp-image-20 aligncenter" height="471" src="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-round.jpg" width="643"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Round
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Celebrity with Round Shaped Face: Drew Barrymore
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Contour your cheekbones to create angles, says Los Angeles-based make-up artist Lindsey Rivera. To determine where to apply your product, suck in your cheeks and feel where the hollow of your cheek is and apply along that line, she says. Rivera also suggests lightly sweeping product along your jaw line to give the illusion of a longer, slimmer jaw.
        </p>
        <p>
         Baghdady agrees, and recommends you highlight the bridge of your nose, the apples of your cheeks and the top of your forehead at your hairline, too. This will help to elongate and thin out a persons face, she says.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-square.jpg">
          <img alt="Makeup Contouring for Square Face Shapes" class="size-full wp-image-22 aligncenter" height="472" src="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-square.jpg" width="643"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Square
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <i>
           Celebrity with Square Shaped Face: Olivia Wilde
          </i>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Instead of creating angles, I use contouring to soften them on a clients face, says Rivera. I focus on applying product near the outer corners of the hairline. Since square faces tend to have prominent jaws, I apply contour along the jaw to pull it in and soften it.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-heart.jpg">
          <img alt="Makeup Contouring for Heart Face Shapes" class="size-full wp-image-23 aligncenter" height="472" src="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-heart.jpg" width="643"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Heart
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <i>
           Celebrity with Heart Shaped Face: Reese Witherspoon
          </i>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Heart shapes have a smaller chin and very high cheekbones, so you want to balance this by contouring depth under the cheekbone applebut not too low as to make the chin area appear even smaller. Baghdady suggests sweeping your hairline and both sides of your forehead, blending along the outer part of your cheekbones. This will give a heart-shape more symmetry, she says.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-diamond.jpg">
          <img alt="Makeup Contouring for Diamond Face Shapes" class="size-full wp-image-24 aligncenter" height="472" src="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-diamond.jpg" width="643"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Diamond
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <i>
           Celebrity with DiamondShapedFace:
          </i>
          <i>
           Madonna
          </i>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Diamond faces tend to have great cheekbones, so I dont often need to contour them, says Rivera. But to soften, or shorten, the length of the face, Rivera will apply contour along the hairline and lightly across the chin. Just be sure to highlight on more of a slant. Otherwise it will widen up the center of your face.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-oblong.jpg">
          <img alt="Makeup Contouring for Oblong Face Shapes" class="size-full wp-image-25 aligncenter" height="472" src="http://www.bluegala.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/makeup-oblong.jpg" width="643"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Oblong
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <i>
           Celebrity with Oblong Shaped Face: Sarah Jessica Parker
          </i>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Oblong faces are very similar to diamonds in that you want to keep highlighting to a minimum. And since you may want to add some angles, youll want to contour cheekbones, says Rivera.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Practice makes perfect
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         Does the idea of a face shape elude you? Section your face into thirds from the eyes up, below your nose and then your chin. With the divided focus, youll be better able point out the balance and symmetry of your face, which is what, ultimately, defines the five shapes listed above.
        </p>
        <p>
         Better yet, a woman who is serious about contouring, says Constance Dunn, author of
         <i>
          Practical Glamour: Presenting Your Most Beautiful &amp; Polished Self to the World,
         </i>
         will spend time experimenting with shades, intensity and blending.
        </p>
        <p>
         This might mean you take before and after pictures, says Dunn, so that when you discover a method that does the trick, you can practice it again and again until you can achieve the look you loveno guidelines necessary.
        </p>
        <p>
         Of course, if youre really struggling, theres no shame in consulting a pro. A specialty store can provide a more comfortable setting for women looking to experiment with makeup.
        </p>
        <p>
         Bottom line: anyone can contour. Whether you work at it yourself, or enlist someone elses help, you can do it. And your cheekbones will never have looked more fabulous.
         <b>
         </b>
        </p>

         </a>
