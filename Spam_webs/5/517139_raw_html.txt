             <h1 class="post-title">
              <a href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/cooking/2011/06/perfect-iced-coffee/" title="Perfect Iced Coffee">
               Perfect Iced Coffee
              </a>
             </h1>
             <div class="meta-tasks">
              <span class="author">
               Posted by Ree
              </span>
              <a class="comment-number-wrap" href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/cooking/2011/06/perfect-iced-coffee/#comments">
               <div class="comment-number">
                1252
               </div>
               <span>
                Comments
               </span>
              </a>
              <a class="print-icon" href="#recipe-form-144285" id="print-recipe-link-144285">
               Print Recipe
              </a>
             </div>
            </header>
            <div class="post-padding">
             <p>
              <a href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/cooking/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2011/06/iced.jpg">
               <img alt="iced" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13879 frame-img width_426" height="640" src="http://thepioneerwoman.com/cooking/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2011/06/iced.jpg" width="426"/>
              </a>
              Iced coffee is my life. When I wake up, often around the time party animals on the west coast are just heading home, I start each day not with a cup of freshly brewed hot java, but with a tall, blessed glass of creamy iced coffee in a glass. Ive been an iced coffee freakazoid for years and years. To say I couldnt live without it is an understatement. It gives me the tools I need to cope.
             </p>
             <p>
              Iced coffee is a complicated thing, and there are many different approaches. One would think that one could merely pour brewed coffee into a glass full of ice and call it a daybut I find that method extremely flawed. First, no matter how packed with ice the glass is, once the hot coffee hits, some of the ice is bound to melt. This has two disastrous results:
             </p>
             <p>
              1. The overall strength of the coffee flavor is diluted.
              <br/>
              2. The iced coffee isnt as cold as it could (or should) be. The finished glass of iced coffee should be frigid, not sorta cold with half-melted ice cubes floating around.
             </p>
             <p>
              Given the previous set of facts, one would assume that the logical solution would be to brew hot coffee, then transfer the brew to the fridge, allow it to cool, and use it to make iced coffee from there. Its an okay solution, one I subscribed to for quite awhileuntil I picked up an issue of Imbibe Magazine three summers ago. It contained a
              <a href="http://www.imbibemagazine.com/Iced-Coffee-How-To" target="_blank">
               huge spread
              </a>
              on the subject of iced coffee, and suggested the following cold-brew method for creating a sort of iced coffee concentrate. I tried it immediately, have made it this way ever since, and can tell you that there is no better (or simpler) method for having the most delicious iced coffee at your fingertips.
             </p>
             <p>
              There are reasons this method results in a smoother, richer, more delicious concentrate than simply brewing strong coffee and refrigerating it. I would take the time to explain them to you if I knew what they were. But since I dont, Im just going to show you instead.
             </p>
             <p>
              (Note: Ive totally adapted/tweaked coffee/water amounts to suit my own tastes. Experiment to find your own perfect ratio.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825898241/" title="REE_4983 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_4983" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5280/5825898241_789098be9c_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              I start with a big ol container. I love these food storage containers, by the way. I got these at restaurant supply, but Sams Club had them last time I was there.
             </p>
             <p>
              You can use a big bowl, a large pitchereven a really clean bucket will work if youre going for a huge quantity. (Or you can halve the original quantity and use a pitcher.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826453502/" title="REE_4984 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_4984" class="width_426 frame-img" height="640" src="http://farm2.static.flickr.com/1423/5826453502_b2b2d0c22c_z.jpg" width="426"/>
              </a>
              Rip open a pound of ground coffee. Any kind will do; the stronger and richer the better.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825898923/" title="REE_4986 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_4986" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3192/5825898923_84529146b3_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Pour in the coffee.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826454162/" title="REE_4991 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_4991" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5200/5826454162_b8b439098f_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Mount Bliss. Who invented coffee, anyway? They should be awarded the keys to the city.
             </p>
             <p>
              Or, at the very least, my heart.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826454394/" title="REE_4995 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_4995" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2347/5826454394_a19ae5941f_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Pour in 8 quarts (2 gallons) cold water.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825899927/" title="REE_4998 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_4998" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5061/5825899927_a68178d1a8_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Give it a stir to make sure all the grounds make contact with the water
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825900163/" title="REE_5000 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5000" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5109/5825900163_47e0554a9d_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Then cover the container and go live your life as the coffee steeps for at least eight hours. (And you can go much longer if youd like.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826455142/" title="REE_5001 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5001" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3344/5826455142_6671df733f_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              When the time has passed, grab a separate container and place a fine mesh strainer over the top.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825900725/" title="REE_5008 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5008" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3170/5825900725_bcdbcc6ff0_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Place a couple of layers of cheesecloth inside the strainer
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825901015/" title="REE_5010 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5010" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3198/5825901015_413bdda8d8_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              And slowly pour the steeped coffee through the strainer.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826456300/" title="REE_5015 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5015" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3195/5826456300_dd6645eda9_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Itll take awhile for all the liquid to pass through. (Doesnt this look like one of the acid pools at Yellowstone?)
             </p>
             <p>
              My friend
              <a href="http://www.anotherchanceranch.com/" target="_blank">
               Julie
              </a>
              is there right now.
             </p>
             <p>
              Hi, Julie!
             </p>
             <p>
              Oh. Guess she didnt hear me.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825901943/" title="REE_5019 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5019" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2785/5825901943_e615390064_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Use a spoon to gently press/force the last of the liquid through. And note: Ive tried the straining method without the cheesecloth, and stray grounds did make it through the mesh strainer. Definitely try to use cheesecloth (or even paper towels) to filter out the finer pieces.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826457014/" title="REE_5022 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5022" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5152/5826457014_235a0aab5d_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              And there we have it. The dregs (left)and the gold (right.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825902703/" title="REE_5028 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5028" class="width_426 frame-img" height="640" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5232/5825902703_3b2662f4a5_z.jpg" width="426"/>
              </a>
              You can store the liquid in the same container, or you can transfer it to a pitcher or other dispenser. Though its difficult to wait, I refrigerate this gorgeous concoction before consuming it. Its meant to be cold!
             </p>
             <p>
              Note: this amount of coffee concentrate lasts me a good three weeks to a month if kept tightly covered in the fridge.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825903123/" title="REE_5033 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5033" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3113/5825903123_400c5e46a5_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Now, when youre ready to make yourself an iced coffee, you can do two things. Start by filling a glass with ice.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826458140/" title="REE_5037 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5037" class="width_426 frame-img" height="640" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5238/5826458140_48b1f23aef_z.jpg" width="426"/>
              </a>
              Reach into the fridge and dispense enough of the coffee liquid to fill the glass half full (or maybe a little more).
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826458338/" title="REE_5039 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5039" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2716/5826458338_583de58da9_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Splash in skim, 2%, or whole milkor, if youre a naughty, naughty bad girl like me: half-and-half.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826458530/" title="REE_5040 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5040" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5223/5826458530_6e69ea4533_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Make that naughty, naughty, naughty, bad, bad girl.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825903919/" title="REE_5042 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5042" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2133/5825903919_62d279981d_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Add enough sugar to achieve the level of sweetness you like, or you can drizzle in vanilla or hazelnut syrup if you have those kinds of things lying around.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825904305/" title="REE_5047 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5047" class="width_426 frame-img" height="640" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3494/5825904305_46ee440570_z.jpg" width="426"/>
              </a>
              Stir it all up
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825904587/" title="REE_5050 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5050" class="width_426 frame-img" height="640" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2563/5825904587_e14136ab78_z.jpg" width="426"/>
              </a>
              Stick in a straw
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825905359/" title="REE_5055 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5055" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3046/5825905359_60345cf09f_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              And go fer it. Look at that miracle. The ice is all there. The flavors all there.
             </p>
             <p>
              Im all there.
             </p>
             <h6>
              VARIATION: VIETNAMESE ICED COFFEE
             </h6>
             <p>
              And now for something entirely different. Samebut different.
             </p>
             <p>
              This is a variation on classic Vietnamese Iced Coffee (also recommended by Imbibe), which actually does begin with a hot brewbut Im using the cold stuff.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826461224/" title="REE_5062 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5062" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3040/5826461224_580d890ee7_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Fill the glass with ice and coffee concentrate as we did before, then crack open a can of sweetened condensed milk.
             </p>
             <p>
              A glorious substance. One you should get to know better if you dont already.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825906637/" title="REE_5066 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5066" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2422/5825906637_55bb77f42b_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Drizzle in at least 2 tablespoons for a big glass (I wound up adding three.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5825906889/" title="REE_5069 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5069" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5277/5825906889_053e95496b_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              CometoMAMA.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826461970/" title="REE_5071 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5071" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3342/5826461970_d0eb8178f0_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              On top of this, add a small splash of milk or half-and-half.
             </p>
             <p>
              (I recommend the latter, of course.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826462162/" title="REE_5073 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5073" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2702/5826462162_595528b1f6_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Glorious.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5826462676/" title="REE_5075 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="REE_5075" class="width_630 frame-img" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3358/5826462676_2bb2c74280_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Stir, take a small sip, and add a little more sweetened condensed milk if needed.
             </p>
             <p>
              Ive tried all combinations of milk, half-and-half, sugar, artificial sweeteners, flavored syrups, and sweetened condensed milk, and I will tell you that there is no more winning combo than the half-and-half/sweetened condensed milk mixture. It is out-of-this-world creamy and wonderful, and is worth at least a spot in your weekend rotation if youre not willing to go there daily.
             </p>
             <p>
              Either way, try this cold-brewed method of iced coffee sometime soon. The flavor, convenience (and cost savings) can not be underestimated.
             </p>
             <p>
              Love,
              <br/>
              Pioneer Woman

				&lt;div itemprop="instructions"&gt;
				&lt;p&gt;(Adapted from Imbibe Magazine)&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In a large container, mix ground coffee with water. Cover and allow to sit at room temperature eight hours or overnight. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Line a fine mesh strainer with cheesecloth and set over a pitcher or other container. Pour coffee/water mixture through the strainer, allowing all liquid to run through. Discard grounds. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Place coffee liquid in the fridge and allow to cool. Use as needed. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;To make iced coffee, pack a glass full of ice cubes. Fill glass 2/3 full with coffee liquid. Add healthy splash of half-and-half. Add 2-3 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk (can use plain sugar instead) and stir to combine. Taste and adjust half-and-half and/or sweetened condensed milk as needed.&lt;/p&gt;
