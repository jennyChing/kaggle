        <h1 class="postTitle" id="a2296865">
         61 years ago today the Red Wings played against a team of convicts at Marquette State prison
        </h1>
        <h4 class="postedBy">
         Posted
        
          
          By
         <a href="http://www.metrotimes.com/detroit/ArticleArchives?author=2135249" rel="author">
          Lee DeVito
         </a>
         on
         <span class="postTime">
          Mon, Feb  2, 2015
         </span>
         at
         <span class="postTime">
          4:44 PM
         </span>
        </h4>
        <div class="postBody">
         <!-- myParagraphType: &lt;br&gt;, myParagraphCount: 4 -->
         <div class="contentImageCenter" style="overflow:hidden;">
          <span class="clicktozoom">
           click to enlarge
          </span>
          <a class="zoomable" href="http://media1.fdncms.com/metrotimes/imager/61-years-ago-today-the-red-wings-played-ag/u/original/2296868/prison3_slide.jpg" rel="contentImg_gal-2296865" title="">
           <img alt="prison3_slide.jpg" height="420" src="http://media1.fdncms.com/metrotimes/imager/61-years-ago-today-the-red-wings-played-ag/u/blog/2296868/prison3_slide.jpg?cb=1432851317" width="580"/>
          </a>
          <ul style="width:580px;overflow:hidden;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;">
          </ul>
         </div>
         <br/>
         The Red Wings game on Feb. 2, 1954 was notable for two major distinctions: It was the Wing's first outdoor game, and it was against a team of inmates at Marquette State prison.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         The unlikely match happened when the Wings' general manager Jack Adams and team captain Ted Lindsay visited the maximum security prison as part of a sponsored trip. That's when former Purple Gang ringleadersHarry Keywell and Ray Bernstein approached Adams and asked if they would bring the Wings up for a scrimmage, to which Adams surprisingly agreed.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         An outdoor rink was made just for the occasion, and despite the fact that they were playing against a team of some of Michigan's most dangerous criminals, the Wings went ahead and clobbered the "Marquette Prison Pirates" (they stopped keeping score after the first period's 18-0). To switch things up, the Wings swapped players with the Pirates, and Gordie Howe donned a Pirates jersey.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Read more about it at the Detroit Athletic Co.
         <a href="http://blog.detroitathletic.com/2009/10/13/the-jailhouse-rocked-when-the-wings-played-in-prison/" target="_blank">
          blog
         </a>
         and the Red Wing's
         <a href="http://redwings.nhl.com/club/news.htm?id=615868" target="_blank">
          official site
         </a>
         .
