        <p>
         You know the usual suspects when it comes to Sheepskin, right? Everyone knows about luxurious rugs, fluffy pillows, comfy UGG boots, and of course, special made Sheepskin seat covers! Their warmth and comfort is to die for! Distasteful joke alert 
        </p>
        <p>
         In all seriousness, Sheepskin is a real luxury item that provides us with the ultimate comfort. It is even used for medical purposes.But there are other interesting yet quite unexpected uses for Sheepskin as well:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Sheepskin can make you Rich?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_569" style="width: 507px">
         <a href="http://www.shearcomfort.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/golden-fleece.jpg">
          <img alt="sheep skin gold" class=" wp-image-569 " height="688" src="http://www.shearcomfort.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/golden-fleece.jpg" width="497"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Credit:
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Fleece" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Wikipedia
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Just when you thought your day couldnt get any better, your Sheepskin rug or seat cover could potentially make you rich? Thats right folks! Sheepskin can be used in gold mining.
        </p>
        <p>
         This isnt new either; its ancient methodology. Villagers in the Svaneti region of Georgia (the country, not the state) still line their stream beds with Sheepskin hoping that gold will embed itself into the fleece.
        </p>
        <p>
         This historical use of Sheepskin is linked to the Greek myth of the Golden Fleece. In fact, it could even be the basis of the Greek mythic tale of
         <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sciencetech/article-2851531/Legend-Golden-Fleece-real-hunt-sheepskin-used-filter-flakes-gold-mountain-streams.html" target="_blank">
          Jason and the Golden Fleece
         </a>
         . That would be one expensive jacket thought. Oh man!
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, I am not saying we should all run out and start gold mining with our Sheepskin seat covers
        </p>
        <p>
         There would be none left for me.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Sheepskin Rafts for your Daily Commute
         </strong>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_570" style="width: 720px">
         <a href="http://www.shearcomfort.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/sheepskin-raft.jpg">
          <img alt="sheepskin raft china" class="size-full wp-image-570" height="476" src="http://www.shearcomfort.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/sheepskin-raft.jpg" width="710"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Credit:
          <a href="http://www.yellowriver.gov.cn/trsweb/fund/hhwm/fsmq/201006/t20100604_76301.htm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Government of China
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Quick history lesson: Sheepskin was used by troops in China during the Han Dynasty to build rafts to travel down the Yellow River. These rafts were also used in 20th Century China to transport part of the railway that is now known as the Baotou-Lanzhou. Sheepskin has been around forever! Kind of cool right?
        </p>
        <p>
         There is a surprising amount of labor involved in making a Sheepskin raft. So if you think you can just throw your sheepskin rug onto a few logs and tie it all together, youre sadly mistaken. Youll not only ruin your nice rug, but youll end up swimming back to shore.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are actually four stages to complete the process. Read more about how you can have your
         <a href="http://traditions.cultural-china.com/en/16Traditions2720.html" target="_blank">
          very own Sheepskin raft!
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. Sheepskin Scaring Phantoms?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_571" style="width: 578px">
         <a href="http://www.shearcomfort.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/bell-ringers.jpg">
          <img alt="sheepskin bell ringers" class=" wp-image-571 " height="423" src="http://www.shearcomfort.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/bell-ringers.jpg" width="568"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Credit:
          <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Rijecki_karneval_140210_Halubajski_zvoncari_12.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Wikipedia
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Booooo! No wait I mean Bahhhhh!
        </p>
        <p>
         Just when you thought you knew everything In Croatia, some people dress up in Sheepskin and horned masks and parade around their villages. This is not your typical parade though . These individuals do it to terrify the winter ghosts, ghouls, and phantoms.
        </p>
        <p>
         This warding march derives from pagan cleansing rituals. The people are called bell-ringers  also known as Zvonari  who travel through villages ringing bells and cleansing towns of their demons. These bell ringers would gather all the villagers in one place and commence a cleansing ceremony. Once finished, the bell ringers would proceed to gather one item from every villager and complete the cleansing by burning them (the items, not the villagers). The quasi-exorcists would then continue on to the next village. Pretty cool, right? These horned bell-ringers are legit! Theres more about the ritual on
         <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2571529/Enough-scare-away-phantom-Sheepskin-wearing-bell-ringers-ward-evil-spirits-terrifying-village-parade.html" target="_blank">
          MailOnline.com
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         So remember the horned sheepskin-wearing Croatians next time you see a ghost, because after all, ghosts exist. American Horror Story and Supernatural prove this.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Keeping Brave Soldiers Warm
         </strong>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_572" style="width: 720px">
         <a href="http://www.shearcomfort.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/digger-vest.jpg">
          <img alt="digger vests australia" class="size-full wp-image-572" height="537" src="http://www.shearcomfort.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/digger-vest.jpg" width="710"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Credit:
          <a href="http://www.mortels.com.au/shop/home.php?cat=37" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Mortels Sheepskin Factory
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         During World War I (WWI), European soldiers had to deal with extremely cold winters. How did they keep warm you ask? Sheepskin vests! In fact, an article in The Maitland Mercury says that over 75,000 Sheepskin vests were sent as gifts to Australian soldiers on the front lines during WWI to help keep them warm. How nice!
         <br/>
         Whats even cooler is that these Digger vests have returned as a fashion statement  one that commemorates and remembers the soldiers  but looks pretty badass too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5. Keeping A Brave Monkey Warm?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/pZ80Zpi7N-E" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         Okay, so this is not as profound as commemorative vests for soldiers, but come on! A monkey wearing a fancy Sheepskin vest? Now, my post is complete. This little guy was roaming IKEA all by himself. Luckily he had his fashionable Sheepskin on to keep himself warm. Poor guy
        </p>
        <p>
         Well there you have it  5 unexpected uses of Sheepskin. I am sure there are many others out there, but these are definitely on the more interesting side. Because, who really wants to read about how to make parchment paper? Just in case you do though,
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parchment" target="_blank">
          Wikipedia
         </a>
         has a full page dedicated to it.
        </p>
