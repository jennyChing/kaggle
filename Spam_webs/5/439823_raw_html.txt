          <li>
           Point the cursor to any search engine from the Google or Yandex network. Press the
           <strong>
            green
			cross-shaped button
           </strong>
           that
			appears.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p class="center">
          <a class="lightbox" href="../rank-tracker/images/help/rankings/custom-geo-location-search.png">
           <img alt="" height="385" src="../rank-tracker/images/help/rankings/custom-geo-location-search-sm.png" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <br/>
         <ul class="bird-red-big">
          <li class="site-audit-text-15 bold">
           Monitor how well your website performs in Vertical search  Videos, Images,
            Places, etc.:
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Go to Rank Tracker's
           <em>
            Preferences &gt; Rank Checking Mode
           </em>
           .
          </li>
          <li>
           Mark the
           <em>
            Use Universal Search Results
           </em>
           checkbox. You can also
           <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/video.html?vid=universal-search-tracking-direct" target="_blank">
            watch help video here&gt;&gt;
           </a>
           .
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p class="center">
          <a class="lightbox" href="../rank-tracker/images/help/rankings/universal-search-mixed-results.png">
           <img alt="" height="154" src="../rank-tracker/images/help/rankings/universal-search-mixed-results-sm.png" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <br/>
         <ul class="bird-red-big">
          <li class="site-audit-text-15 bold">
           Make sure that
           <strong>
            the right pages rank
           </strong>
           for your keywords and take
		measures when a wrong page shows up in search instead of
		your intended landing page:
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Go to
           <em>
            Preferences &gt; Match Keywords to URLs &gt; Add
           </em>
          </li>
          <li>
           Specify landing pages that should rank for particular keywords
          </li>
          <li>
           Get notified, when a wrong page appears in search results. You can also
           <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/video.html?vid=keyword-url-match-in-rank-tracker-direct" target="_blank">
            watch help video here&gt;&gt;
           </a>
           .
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p class="center">
          <a class="lightbox" href="images/notify.png">
           <img alt="" height="372" src="images/notify-sm.png" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <br/>
        </div>
        <div class="title-bg-sm title-bg-green" style="padding-left: 20px;">
         2. Organic traffic
        </div>
        <div class="white-block-seo-guide " style="padding: 25px 60px;">
         <p>
          Monitor the number of unique visitors who get to your site from search engines. The
		traffic data from Google Analytics
		is also available on your
          <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/rank-tracker/" target="_blank">
           Rank tracker
          </a>
          graph, and if you've created events in Rank Tracker, you'll spot how a
		certain
		SEO activity has changed your organic traffic.
         </p>
         <p class="center">
          <a class="lightbox" href="images/site-audit-04.png">
           <img alt="" height="177" src="images/site-audit-04-sm.png" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          By tracking the traffic volume on a keyword level, you will see which keywords are
		your strongest traffic sources. As
		traffic keyword data is now hidden under the "Not provided" label in Google Analytics, you'll find
		Rank Tracker's
		calculated keyword traffic immensely useful for your SEO analytics:
         </p>
         <p class="center">
          <a class="lightbox" href="images/not-provided-3.png">
           <img alt="" height="372" src="images/not-provided-3-sm.png" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          Read more about the
          <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/news/not-provided-solution.html" target="_blank">
           "Not
		provided" feature
          </a>
          here.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="title-bg-sm title-bg-green" style="padding-left: 20px;">
         3. Incoming links
        </div>
        <div class="white-block-seo-guide " style="padding: 25px 60px;">
         <p>
          If your site's growing, it's natural to have an increasing number of backlinks.
		Remember that search engines consider
		backlinks as users' "votes" for the good and useful content. You can monitor your backlink profile growth using
          <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/seospyglass/" target="_blank">
           SEO SpyGlass
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Tip:
          </strong>
          Not all backlink a born equal. Some of them can out your
		rankings at risk of Google penalty. So make sure to
		monitor not only the quantity, but the quality of each link (and its
          <strong>
           Penalty Risk
          </strong>
          ).
         </p>
         <p class="center">
          <a class="lightbox" href="images/link-risk-audit/link-risk-01.png">
           <img alt="" height="372" src="images/link-risk-audit/link-risk-01-sm.png" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          Read more about the
          <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/news/link-risk-audit.html" target="_blank">
           Anti-Penalty
		Link Audit
          </a>
          feature here.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="title-bg-sm title-bg-green" style="padding-left: 20px;">
         4. Indexed pages
        </div>
        <div class="white-block-seo-guide " style="padding: 25px 60px;">
         <p>
          Is the number of pages from your website increasing or decreasing in Google's index?
		The more pages indexed in Google,
		the more opportunity you have to be found.
         </p>
         <p>
          You can also see the "Cache date" column in
          <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/website-auditor/" target="_blank">
           WebSite Auditor
          </a>
          to check how often Google comes to crawl your
		pages:
         </p>
         <p class="center">
          <img alt="" height="93" src="http://www.link-assistant.com/images/landings/checklist-04-sm.png" width="512"/>
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          The more frequent is the crawl, the better for your website.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="title-bg-sm title-bg-green" style="padding-left: 20px;">
         5. Social signals for landing pages
        </div>
        <div class="white-block-seo-guide " style="padding: 25px 60px;">
         <p>
          Social media is one of the safest ways of risk-free, natural link building. Facebook
		likes and shares, tweets and
		retweets, Google +1's and bookmarks  all include a backlink to a web page. That is why monitoring the
		growth of
		social
		signals for your domain and separate landing pages is part of tracking your link building success.
         </p>
         <p>
          You can use a special dashboard in
          <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/seo-spyglass/" target="_blank">
           SEO
		SpyGlass
          </a>
          for a quick social overview. You'll find all the stats under
          <em>
           Preferences
		&gt; Domain ranking factors &gt; Factors overview
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p class="center">
          <a class="lightbox" href="images/social-link-building/1.png">
           <img alt="" height="207" src="images/social-link-building/1-sm.png" width="450"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          Or
          <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/website-auditor/" target="_blank">
           WebSite Auditor
          </a>
          's Social media workspace to track the number of social signals to
		separate site pages:
         </p>
         <p class="center">
          <a class="lightbox" href="images/site-audit-06.png">
           <img alt="" height="159" src="images/site-audit-06-sm.png" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="title-bg-sm title-bg-green" style="padding-left: 20px;">
         6. Conversion rate from search traffic
        </div>
        <div class="white-block-seo-guide " style="padding: 25px 60px;">
         <p>
          <strong>
           Number of conversions
          </strong>
          from organic traffic will let you know if the
		traffic you are receiving from higher rankings
		is
		turning into actions. If you are not seeing more conversions with the additional traffic, it could be an
		indicator
		that
		either you are not ranking for relevant keywords or there are conversion problem areas on your site.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Conversion rate from organic traffic
          </strong>
          gives you a simple metric that
		can be used in comparison to any other online
		marketing efforts you may be running.
         </p>
         <p>
          You can find ample conversion data in your
          <a href="http://www.google.com/analytics/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Google Analytics
          </a>
          account.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="title-bg-sm title-bg-green" style="padding-left: 20px;">
         7. Profit
        </div>
        <div class="white-block-seo-guide " style="padding: 25px 60px;">
         <p>
          The final and the most important metric to measure your results is, surely, return on
		investments. As no rankings or
		conversions growth are beneficial to your business if your SEO revenue falls short go behind the actual SEO
		spends.
         </p>
         <p>
          The key reasons leading to over-spending in SEO include:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Poor keyword research
           </strong>
           . Targeting wrong keywords and competing in the niches that are too
			hard to squeeze into can
			inflate your SEO spending without bringing expected results. Read our
           <a href="http://www.link-assistant.com/news/keyword-research-2014.html" target="_blank">
            Keyword Research in 2014
           </a>
           guide to refine
			the
			key
			phrases you target.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Manual SEO
           </strong>
           . The more tasks you or your SEO team runs manually, the bigger your manhour
			spends and budget. Make sure
			you
			automate anything possible to automate.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Inaccessible/poorworking website
           </strong>
           . Bad hosting, pages taking too long to load, code
			errors, pages closed from
			indexing 
			there are lots of issues that can put your SEO efforts down the drain. The rule number one for any SEO
			should is
			fixing
			site's structure  and codingrelated issues before investing into other SEO activities. Running
			regular site audits
			can
			help avoid the frustration of wasted resources.
          </li>
         </ul>
        </div>
