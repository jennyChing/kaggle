                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2293009606058645383">
                     Ingrid Bck's
                     <a href="http://www.jovis.de/index.php?idcatside=4246&amp;lang=2">
                      <i>
                       <b>
                        Six Canonical Projects by Rem Koolhaas: Essays on the History of Ideas
                       </b>
                      </i>
                     </a>
                     will be released by Jovis on July 28.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-TYK0n7B-daY/VXnH9jDcYOI/AAAAAAAAR0A/NU_L65nIy0o/s1600/81BO0L-kJBL.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-TYK0n7B-daY/VXnH9jDcYOI/AAAAAAAAR0A/NU_L65nIy0o/s1600/81BO0L-kJBL.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Per the
                     <a href="http://www.jovis.de/index.php?idcatside=4246&amp;lang=2">
                      publisher's website
                     </a>
                     :
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      <i>
                       Rem Koolhaas has been part of the international avant-garde since the nineteen-seventies and has been named the Pritzker Architecture Prize for the year 2000. This book, which builds on six canonical Koolhaas projects [Exodus, or the Voluntary Prisoners of Architecture; Ville Nouvelle Melun-Snart; Maison  Bordeaux; Dutch Embassy; Seattle Public Library; and CCTV], traces the discursive practice behind the design methods used by Koolhaas and his office OMA. It uncovers recurring key themessuch as wall, void, montage, trajectory, infrastructure, and shapethat have structured this design discourse over the span of Koolhaas's oeuvre. The book moves beyond the six core pieces, as well: It explores how these identified thematic design principles manifest in other works by Koolhaas as both practical re-applications and further elaborations.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       In addition to Koolhaas's individual genius, these textual and material layers are accounted for shaping the very context of his work's relevance. By comparing the design principles with relevant concepts from the architectural Zeitgeist in which Koolhaas has operated, the study moves beyond its specific subjectRem Koolhaasand provides novel insight into the broader history of architectural ideas.
                      </i>
                     </blockquote>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2015/03/book-of-moment-herman-hertzberger.html">
                      Book of the Moment: Herman Hertzberger
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-906025642946269476">
                     If any architect deserves a monumental survey of their work it is the Dutch architect
                     <a href="http://www.ahh.nl/">
                      Herman Hertzberger
                     </a>
                     . Heck, the 82-year-old architect deserves the Pritzker Architecture Prize, too. Although nothing can be done about the latter, at least for another year, April sees the release of Robert McCarter's
                     <a href="http://www.nai010.com/en/hertzberger">
                      <i>
                       Herman Hertzberger
                      </i>
                     </a>
                     from nai010 publishers, a 524-page monograph with 600 full-color illustrations. For those in The Netherlands, a book launch is taking place in Amsterdam on Saturday, April 11; details are below.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-XB3TCOBt0yQ/VRq3rSLcNsI/AAAAAAAARRw/-2b_ryGCmo8/s1600/hertzberger-cover.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-XB3TCOBt0yQ/VRq3rSLcNsI/AAAAAAAARRw/-2b_ryGCmo8/s1600/hertzberger-cover.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     [Cover and spreads courtesy of nai010 publishers]
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Description
                     <a href="http://www.nai010.com/en/hertzberger">
                      from the publisher
                     </a>
                     :
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      <i>
                       Herman Hertzberger (b.1932) is one of the most important and critically influential figures in international architecture of the late twentieth and early twenty-first centuries. A constant champion of fundamentally humanist modern architecture, Hertzberger is rightly regarded as the worlds foremost designer of schools, a building type he has almost single-handedly redefined.
                      </i>
                      <br/>
                      <i>
                       <br/>
                      </i>
                      <i>
                       Hertzberger believes that architecture is above all else a shared, collective discipline. His world famous lessons in architecture involve ethics and edification, engagement of the built legacy of those who lived before us, and the fundamentally optimistic and constructive intention to make the world a better place for the people who live in it. With a foreword by Kenneth Frampton, this monograph by acclaimed international author Robert McCarter examines Hertzbergers most important architectural works through analysis of the design process and guiding ideas, particularly as these reflect Hertzbergers engagement with the Modernist tradition, architectural history, urban space and the way we experience it.
                      </i>
                     </blockquote>
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-y1TWJiMToZY/VRq4APuMxoI/AAAAAAAARR4/RAbZoIH2Eew/s1600/foto0401.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-y1TWJiMToZY/VRq4APuMxoI/AAAAAAAARR4/RAbZoIH2Eew/s1600/foto0401.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-NV2jPDCemdM/VRq4AKQEi_I/AAAAAAAARSA/_Ee7_kBW-N4/s1600/foto0402.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-NV2jPDCemdM/VRq4AKQEi_I/AAAAAAAARSA/_Ee7_kBW-N4/s1600/foto0402.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-a119HHudmtA/VRq4ACcCW3I/AAAAAAAARR8/Ggx5ehu_5Jg/s1600/foto0403.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-a119HHudmtA/VRq4ACcCW3I/AAAAAAAARR8/Ggx5ehu_5Jg/s1600/foto0403.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Book Launch:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     Saturday, 11 April 2015, 15:00-17:00
                     <br/>
                     De Amsterdamse Montessori School
                     <br/>
                     Willem Witsenstraat 14, Amsterdam
                     <br/>
                     Please register via
                     <a href="mailto:rsvp@nai010.com">
                      rsvp@nai010.com
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Program:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                      Welcome by Eelco van Welie , director nai010 publishers
                     <br/>
                      Architect and researcher Hans Teerds interviews author Robert McCarter about his research into the oeuvre of Herman Hertzberger
                     <br/>
                      Nanne de Ru, director of The Berlage and co-founder of Powerhouse Company, will discuss the influence of Hertzberger on architectural education and the new generation of architects
                     <br/>
                      Presentation of the first copy to Herman Hertzberger
                     <br/>
                     Book sales and book signing afterwards
                     <br/>
                     Special discount price  49.50 (regular price  69.50)
                     <br/>
                     The language of this afternoon is English

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2014/09/long-awaited-dvd-of-moment.html">
                      Long-Awaited DVD of the Moment
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8923147094278525782">
                     Finally! Thom Andersen's brilliant, nearly three-hour documentary
                     <i>
                      Los Angeles Plays Itself
                     </i>
                     is being released on
                     <a href="http://cinemaguild.com/mm5/merchant.mvc?Screen=PROD&amp;Store_Code=HE&amp;Product_Code=5207&amp;Category_Code=BLU">
                      DVD
                     </a>
                     and
                     <a href="http://cinemaguild.com/mm5/merchant.mvc?Screen=PROD&amp;Store_Code=HE&amp;Product_Code=5208">
                      Blu-Ray
                     </a>
                     on October 14, eleven years after it was completed.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://cinemaguild.com/mm5/merchant.mvc?Screen=PROD&amp;Store_Code=HE&amp;Product_Code=5208" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-39GpgQKR6Kk/VCWGrYDY2EI/AAAAAAAACy4/YlG_nFhF4oU/s1600/81Kz%2BRzvZFL._SL1500_.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     At its most basic, the video essay (as it's been called) is an analysis of Los Angeles through movies. On a deeper level, film critic
                     <a href="http://www.jonathanrosenbaum.net/2004/10/la-existential/">
                      Jonathan Rosenbaum calls it
                     </a>
                     , in addition to "a masterpiece," "an essay that qualifies as social history, as film theory, as personal  reverie,
                     <b>
                      as architectural history and criticism
                     </b>
                     , as a bittersweet  meditation on automotive transport, as a critical history of mass  transit in southern California, as a wisecracking compilation of local  folklore, as a city symphony in reverse, and as a song of nostalgia  for lost neighborhoods such as Bunker Hill and unchronicled lifestyles  such as locals who walk or take buses." (my emphasis) Rosenbaum's description of the film as part architectural history and  criticism is spot on, just one aspect that makes it a stimulating and  enjoyable experience for every one of its 169 minutes.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-bd5ftV_hb5E/VCWIlWNuz1I/AAAAAAAACzE/gme60v8RjC4/s1600/losangelesplaysitself_toppage.jpg"/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     So, you may be asking, why has it taken eleven years for a DVD release? The main reason is that the film was made without studio backing and distribution, and since it's completely made up of clips from other films (with Andersen's highly opinionated narration on top), the rights to use the
                     <a href="http://www.twotreatises.org/409">
                      hundreds of clips
                     </a>
                     was too much for the filmmaker or any distributor wishing to take on the task.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-AP5X2Sgbcog/VCWMQLkF--I/AAAAAAAACzQ/DOd4_VilJQs/s1600/los-angeles-plays-itself.jpg"/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Enter
                     <a href="http://www.cinemaguild.com/">
                      The Cinema Guild
                     </a>
                     , which announced in July that it would be releasing
                     <i>
                      Los Angeles Plays Itself
                     </i>
                     and three more Andersen titles:
                     <i>
                      Red Hollywood
                     </i>
                     ,
                     <i>
                      Eadweard Muybridge, Zoopraxographer
                     </i>
                     , and
                     <i>
                      Reconverso
                     </i>
                     . As other documentaries have invoked "fair use" protections in recent years, such as
                     <i>
                      This Film Is Not Yet Rated
                     </i>
                     , so is Andersen. As
                     <a href="http://variety.com/2013/film/news/los-angeles-plays-itself-a-decade-later-1200661848/">
                      he said last year
                     </a>
                     last year on the film's 10th anniversary: "I was, am, and will be able to use [the clips] under fair use. No copyright owners were harmed in the making of this film."
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     For a taste of the joys of Andersen's film, here is a 6:40 clip on the use of modern houses in films:
                     <br/>
                     <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="480" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/4hYg01uqz9U?rel=0" width="640">
                     </iframe>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00LOFYBP0/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00LOFYBP0&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=aweeklydoseof-20&amp;linkId=OXMSVKKBQBICNHZ3" target="_blank">
                      Available at
                      <img alt="Buy from Amazon.com" border="0" src="http://www.archidose.org/books/buy-from-tan.gif"/>
                     </a>
                     (but cheaper to buy direct from Cinema Guild on
                     <a href="http://cinemaguild.com/mm5/merchant.mvc?Screen=PROD&amp;Store_Code=HE&amp;Product_Code=5207&amp;Category_Code=BLU">
                      DVD
                     </a>
                     and
                     <a href="http://cinemaguild.com/mm5/merchant.mvc?Screen=PROD&amp;Store_Code=HE&amp;Product_Code=5208">
                      Blu-Ray
                     </a>
                     .)

                      Book of the Moment: War of Streets and Houses
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4350853014276347498">
                     Not many graphic novels treat buildings and cities as an integral part of their stories, so I'm intrigued by
                     <a href="http://www.uncivilizedbooks.com/comics/war_of_streets_and_houses.html">
                      <i>
                       War of Streets and Houses
                      </i>
                     </a>
                     , a new graphic memoir from cartoonist and author Sophie Yanow.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://uncivilizedbooks.com/comics/full/war_streets_houses_cover_temp.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-bjG7twsC4Hs/UzRU0IN28JI/AAAAAAAABe0/9Qzz8TmUaUE/s1600/war-streets-houses.png"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     [All images
                     <a href="http://www.uncivilizedbooks.com/comics/war_of_streets_and_houses.html">
                      via Uncivilized Books
                     </a>
                     ]
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Text
                     <a href="http://www.uncivilizedbooks.com/comics/war_of_streets_and_houses.html">
                      from the publisher
                     </a>
                     :
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      <i>
                       The War of Streets and Houses
                      </i>
                      is named after General Thomas Bugeaud's 19th century essay; the first manual for the preparation and conduct of urban warfare. The text greatly influenced Baron Haussmanns famous re-development of Paris, and the planning of modern cities. In 2012 the author participated in the massive Montreal student strikes. In the midst of protesting crowds and police kettles, the military origins of urban planning suddenly became an undeniable reality. Sophie Yanows most ambitious work to date deftly melds the history of urban planning, theories of control with personal experiences of political activism.
                     </blockquote>
                     <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-mcXuIcW0Gnw/UzRVTsTS58I/AAAAAAAABe8/Kf84mR8WosA/s1600/war_0002_2.jpg"/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-MQot1L6qy20/UzRVT7gQuuI/AAAAAAAABfI/3dic1zJL6AQ/s1600/war_0003_3.jpg"/>

                     <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-E5VxvN2Nfzk/UzRVTwieRLI/AAAAAAAABfA/tpSmX_Zyna8/s1600/war_0004_4.jpg"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2014/03/book-of-moment-collage-and-architecture.html">
                      Book of the Moment: Collage and Architecture
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3663046085191885992">
                     Over spring break I gave my first-year students an assignment to make a collage as part of a precedent study. If I had known about Jennifer A.E. Shields's
                     <a href="http://www.routledge.com/books/details/9780415533270/">
                      <b>
                       <i>
                        Collage and Architecture
                       </i>
                      </b>
                     </a>
                     , published in paperback last month by Routledge, I certainly would have used it to give them a little bit more guidance. Half the book, after all, is devoted to "collage methodologies in architectural analysis and design." The second half, in case you're wondering, includes six case studies, from Le Corbusier to Weiss/Manfredi, of "architecture as collage."
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.routledge.com/books/details/9780415533270/" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-cgEwgKQQeYE/Uy93ou_RNHI/AAAAAAAABdc/9pDow7fqaFY/s1600/collage-architecture1.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Text
                     <a href="http://www.routledge.com/books/details/9780415533270/">
                      from the publisher
                     </a>
                     :
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      <i>
                       Collage and Architecture
                      </i>
                      is the first book to cover collage as a tool for design in architecture, making it a valuable resource for students and practitioners. Author Jennifer Shields uses the artworks and built projects of leading artists and architects, such as Le Corbusier, Daniel Libeskind, and Teddy Cruz to illustrate the diversity of collage techniques. The six case study projects from Mexico, Argentina, Sweden, Norway, the United States, and Spain give you a global perspective of architecture as collage. Collage is an important instrument for analysis and design, and Shieldss presentation of this versatile medium draws on decades of relevance in art and architecture, to be adapted and transformed in your own work.
                     </blockquote>
                     Or maybe I'm just a sucker for a timeline chart (from the introduction,
                     <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-g14BkyGgis8/Uy-AOgPwEPI/AAAAAAAABd0/VKiBHUY1c9I/s1600/collage-architecture.png">
                      best viewed large
                     </a>
                     ):
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-g14BkyGgis8/Uy-AOgPwEPI/AAAAAAAABd0/VKiBHUY1c9I/s1600/collage-architecture.png" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-g14BkyGgis8/Uy-AOgPwEPI/AAAAAAAABd0/VKiBHUY1c9I/s640/collage-architecture.png"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0415533279/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0415533279&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=aweeklydoseof-20" target="_blank">
                      Available at
                      <img alt="Buy from Amazon.com" border="0" src="http://www.archidose.org/books/buy-from-tan.gif"/>
                     </a>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2014/02/book-of-moment-working-drawing.html">
                      Book of the Moment: The Working Drawing
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8739814520847053777">
                     <a href="http://www.park-books.com/index.php?lang=en&amp;page=books&amp;view=co&amp;booktype=order_1_releasedate&amp;subject=1&amp;artist=all&amp;author=all&amp;pd=pb&amp;book=519">
                      <b>
                       <i>
                        The Working Drawing: The Architect's Tool
                       </i>
                      </b>
                     </a>
                     , edited by
                     <span class="html-element html-span headline-text" id="ID_52f91cf463be5">
                      Annette Spiro and David Ganzoni
                     </span>
                     and published by Park Books, is a must-have book for architects who love drawings...which should pretty much be all of them. A selection of drawings from the book can be found in
                     <a href="http://www.world-architects.com/en/pages/page_item/the-working-drawing/">
                      this week's World-Architects eMagazine
                     </a>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-g_9kEAISwyQ/UvkeyxvswMI/AAAAAAAABQ8/BfJxnlx5_g8/s1600/9783906027319.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-g_9kEAISwyQ/UvkeyxvswMI/AAAAAAAABQ8/BfJxnlx5_g8/s1600/9783906027319.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Text
                     <a href="http://www.park-books.com/index.php?lang=en&amp;page=books&amp;view=co&amp;booktype=order_1_releasedate&amp;subject=1&amp;artist=all&amp;author=all&amp;pd=pb&amp;book=519">
                      from the publisher
                     </a>
                     :
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      <i>
                       Large-size working drawings are an elementary means in the architectural process and the actual construction of a building. Yet very little has been written and published about the architects quintessential tool. This new book aims to close this gap. It draws on a vast collection of working drawings from many centuries held by the Department of Architecture at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Zurich (ETH Zurich). The collection, put together and categorized under the direction of Annette Spiro, comprises plans for a wide range of architectural tasks and features manifold representational techniques.
                      </i>
                      <br/>
                      <i>
                       <br/>
                      </i>
                      <i>
                       The book presents around 100 of the collections highlights from five centuries, arranged by category for direct comparison. All plans are depicted entirely in color on large-size spreads and fold-outs. Full catalog details and a detail in true size are provided for each drawing.
                      </i>
                      <br/>
                      <i>
                       <br/>
                      </i>
                      <i>
                       Mario Carpo, Hermann Czech, Tom Emerson, Philipp Esch, David Ganzoni, Uta Hassler and Daniel Stockhammer, kos Moravnszky, Urs Primas, Kornel Ringli, Stephan Rutishauser, Jonathan Sergison, and Philip Ursprung contribute essays on various aspects of the topic.
                      </i>
                     </blockquote>
                     <br/>
                     The book is being released in the United States on February 15, 2014.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/3906027317/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=3906027317&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=aweeklydoseof-20" target="_blank">

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2014/01/exhibition-and-catalog-of-moment.html">
                      Exhibition and Catalog of the Moment
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7919604581366561953">
                     <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-jLuE-jz-xuc/UtVzXLSkj3I/AAAAAAAABFI/yGkNrgsR0Bo/s1600/musem-marta-herford-gehry-1.jpg"/>
                     <br/>
                     [Marta Herford (2005) designed by Frank Gehry]
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://marta-herford.de/index.php/iwan-baan-2/?lang=en">
                      <i>
                       52 Weeks, 52 Cities: Photographies by Iwan Baan
                      </i>
                     </a>
                     is on display at Marta Herford in Germany until February 16. Per Marta Herford, "
                     <a href="http://www.iwan.com/">
                      Iwan Baan
                     </a>
                     takes the viewer on a one-year photographic journey around the  world  always on the lookout for ingenious homes in unexpected places  and outstanding construction projects." Accompanying the exhibition is a catalog
                     <a href="http://www.artbooksheidelberg.com/html/detail/en/iwan-baan-978-3-86828-477-5.html">
                      published by Kehrer
                     </a>
                     :
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-au84Aa_UM3Y/UtV0EzXkTmI/AAAAAAAABFQ/eKFOhuGHVRA/s1600/katalog_iwanbaan.jpg"/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     As the cover indicates, the book has some recent buildings by big names (SANAA's Louvre Lens graces the cover), but not necessarily photos with blue skies, perfect framing, and the other perfectionist traits people associate with architectural photography. It also shows places that do not exhibit the hand of the architect, if you will, but are just as fascinating, if not more so:
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-CqqopYQbvhY/UtV0Njp93aI/AAAAAAAABFY/yPVvxzihTZs/s1600/randbild_iwanbaan40KumbhMela.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-CqqopYQbvhY/UtV0Njp93aI/AAAAAAAABFY/yPVvxzihTZs/s1600/randbild_iwanbaan40KumbhMela.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     [Allahabad, India: Kumbh Mela]
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     In a sense, Baan is like a ping pong ball, veering back and forth between bespoke luxury starchitecture:
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-1fTRio1F27g/UtV0RKp4qLI/AAAAAAAABFg/9yyD3024wi4/s1600/randbild_iwanbaan52BroadArt.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-1fTRio1F27g/UtV0RKp4qLI/AAAAAAAABFg/9yyD3024wi4/s1600/randbild_iwanbaan52BroadArt.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     [East Lansing, Michigan, USA: Eli and Edythe Broad Art Museum by Zaha Hadid]
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     and vernacular, and often informal, conditions of living:
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-BbR9-9lPGEg/UtV0UARcEMI/AAAAAAAABFo/XaupsIFVVwA/s1600/randbild_iwanbaan41ZabaleenCairo.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-BbR9-9lPGEg/UtV0UARcEMI/AAAAAAAABFo/XaupsIFVVwA/s1600/randbild_iwanbaan41ZabaleenCairo.jpg"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     [Cairo, Egypt: Zabaleen community]
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     As Marta Herford describes the exhibition (and therefore the catalog, to a degree): "The exhibition features 52 photographs from last year [2012] accompanied by personally spoken commentaries by the photographer. These are sensitive encounters with everyday and extraordinary places all over the globe which Baan has put together to form an engaged commentary on human living and survival strategies. His documentation, for example, of a 45-story, uncompleted skyscraper in Caracas occupied by around 750 families who are living 'extra-legally' in a 'vertical slum' has become one of his best known projects. Here and elsewhere the boundaries between architecture documentation and the interpretation of social living spaces become blurred."
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-vUNbH8RLXp0/UtV0XJEU4II/AAAAAAAABFw/ek4ngjox2w0/s1600/randbild_iwanbaan54NewYork.jpg" imageanchor="1">
                      <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-vUNbH8RLXp0/UtV0XJEU4II/AAAAAAAABFw/ek4ngjox2w0/s1600/randbild_iwanbaan54NewYork.jpg"/>
                     </a>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2013/10/book-of-moment-sage-handbook-of.html">
                      Book of the Moment: The SAGE Handbook of Architectural Theory
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6902070396322392509">
                     <a href="http://www.sagepub.com/books/Book231735?siteId=sage-us&amp;prodTypes=any&amp;q=architectural+theory&amp;fs=1#tabview=title">
                      <i>
                       The SAGE Handbook of Architectural Theory
                      </i>
                     </a>
                     is a 776-page tome that "documents and builds upon some of the most innovative developments in architectural theory over the last two decades." To mark the release of the book, Parsons SCE is
                     <a href="http://events.newschool.edu/event/architectural_theory_in_an_expanded_field_book_launch">
                      hosting a panel discussion
                     </a>
                     (free, no RSVP required) this evening; details are below.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-2JpRLJJ49SI/Um5TjUhGVKI/AAAAAAAAA2I/XlcF3Ew64bo/s1600/sage-28.jpg"/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      <a href="http://events.newschool.edu/event/architectural_theory_in_an_expanded_field_book_launch">
                       <b>
                        Architectural Theory in an Expanded Field
                       </b>
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Monday, October 28, 2013 at 6:00 pm to 9:00 pm
                      <br/>
                      Wollman Hall (B500), Eugene Lang College
                      <br/>
                      65 West 11th Street, New York, NY
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Join Parsons SCE for a panel discussion with the general editors and US-based contributors of the
                      <i>
                       Sage Handbook of Architectural Theory
                      </i>
                      , celebrating the launch of the paperback edition convened by
                      <b>
                       Brian McGrath
                      </b>
                      , Dean of the School of Constructed Environments at Parsonsand moderated by
                      <b>
                       Joanna Merwood
                      </b>
                      , associate professor of Architectural History at Parsons.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      The panel discussion will explore the handbooks agenda and consider  its significance for architectural research, education and practice. A  questions and answer session will follow remarks from the panelists.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       General Editors:
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         C. Greig Crysler
                        </b>
                        , Associate Professor of Architecture; Arcus Chair, College of Environmental Design, UC Berkeley
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         Hilde Heynen
                        </b>
                        , Professor of Architecture, University of Leuven
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         Stephen Cairns
                        </b>
                        , Professor of Architecture and Urbanism, University of Edinburgh; Future Cities Lab Coordinator, ETH Singapore
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       Speakers:
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         Stefan Al
                        </b>
                        , Associate Professor of Urban Design, University of Pennsylvania
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         M. Christine Boyer
                        </b>
                        , William R. Kenan, Jr. Professor of Architecture and Urbanism, Princeton University
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         Brian McGrath
                        </b>
                        , Dean, School of Constructed Environments, Parsons The New School for Design
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         Deborah Natsios
                        </b>
                        , Cryptome.org
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         Vyjayanthi Rao
                        </b>
                        , Assistant Professor of Anthropology, The New School
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         David Solomon
                        </b>
                        , Assistant Professor, Architectural Studies, Ithaca College
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         Gwendolyn Wright
                        </b>
                        , Professor of Architecture, Columbia University
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <br/>
                      The
                      <i>
                       Sage Handbook of Architectural Theory
                      </i>
                      documents and builds upon the most innovative developments in architectural theory over the last two decades.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      With over 40 chapters of original material from a roster of  contributors from around the world, the handbook connects together  issues, institutions, authors and readers in ways that mark a  significant departure from other collections of its kind. Eight major  sections explore issues in architectural theory today, from new  formations of power, difference and embodiment, and questions around  science and technology, to the changing conditions in cities and  metropolitan territories in the global present.
                     </blockquote>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2013/05/the-images-of-architects.html">
                      The Images of Architects
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6601146855688012028">
                     For the
                     <a href="http://www.world-architects.com/en/pages/biennale-architettura-2012">
                      2012 Venice Biennale
                     </a>
                     , Swiss architect
                     <a href="http://www.olgiati.net/">
                      Valerio Olgiati
                     </a>
                     curated a selection of "PictographsStatement of contemporary architects." The inspirational images from well-known architects were displayed on a large table in the Arsenale, and soon they'll be collected in a book published by
                     <a href="http://www.quart.ch/">
                      Quart
                     </a>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/WfCZvRYYyfA?rel=0" width="640">
                     </iframe>
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      <i>
                       <br/>
                       I asked architects to send me important images that show the basis of their work. Images that are in their head when they think. Images that show the origin of their architecture.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       In this book we find 44 individual "muses imaginaires". The most unique architects living today each present up to 10 images to explain the autobiographical roots of their oeuvre. The images are explanations, metaphors, foundations, memories and intentions. They are poetic and philosophical avowals. They reveal a personal perspective on thoughts. They show the roots of architecture and expectations concerning projects. Conscious and unconscious.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       This book has the format of a reader. As little as possible is said. The images are small, legible and interpretable as icons. As individual collections, they present a personal view of an individual world, while as a whole they provide a universal view of the perceptible origin of contemporary architecture.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       Valerio Olgiati
                      </i>
                     </blockquote>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2013/04/peter-zumthor-1986-2013.html">
                      Peter Zumthor: 1986-2013
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-963314736373419668">
                     The most recent monographthe only one, actuallyon Swiss architect Peter Zumthor was released in 1998. It's so desirable that
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0817660992/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0817660992&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=aweeklydoseof-20">
                      used copies are being sold on Amazon
                     </a>
                     for around $1,500.
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/3858817236/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=3858817236&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=aweeklydoseof-20">
                      A forthcoming monograph
                     </a>
                     from
                     <a href="http://www.scheidegger-spiess.ch/">
                      Scheidegger and Spiess
                     </a>
                     may temper the desirability of that book, since it will cover Zumthor's work from 1986-2013 (the earlier one covers 1979-1997) and will be more than twice as big (800 pages versus 318). Sure, it comes with a $250 cover price and won't be released until September, but if anybody can find me a more anticipated architecture book I'd like to know about it.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="zumthor1986-2013.jpg" src="http://www.archidose.org/Blog/zumthor1986-2013.jpg" title="Where Are the Utopian Visionaries?"/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Book description
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/3858817236/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=3858817236&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=aweeklydoseof-20">
                      via Amazon
                     </a>
                     :
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      Unquestionably one of the most influential and revered contemporary  architects, Peter Zumthor has approached his work with a singular  clarity of vision and a strong sense of his own philosophy, both of  which have earned him the admiration of his peers and the world at  large. Choosing to only take on a few projects at a time and keep his  studio small, Zumthor has produced a comparatively few number of  realized buildings, but they rank among the worlds most stunning: St.  Benedicts Chapel in Sumvitg, Switzerland; Therme Vals in Vals,  Switzerland; Kunsthaus Bregenz in Bregenz, Austria; and the Kolumba Art  Museum in Cologne, Germany number among his most famous buildings. This  collection, however, explores his entire body of award-winning work from  1986 to 2012 in five volumes, including his lesser-known but  nonetheless critically acclaimed works such as the Field Chapel for  Brother Klaus near Mechernich, Germany, and the
                      <i>
                       Steilneset. Memorial for the Victims of the Witch Trials in Vard
                      </i>
                      <i>
                       
                      </i>
                      <i>
                       ,
                      </i>
                      Norway.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <i>
                       Peter Zumthor
                      </i>
                      presents  around forty of his projects, both realized and unrealized, through  Zumthors own writing, and with photographs, sketches, drawings, and  plans. A complete catalog of his works starting in 1979 rounds out the  book. Richly illustrated and beautifully designed, this book serves as  both an introduction to Zumthors work and philosophy for the layperson  and a required addition to any architects library.
                     </blockquote>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2013/01/book-of-moment-building-seagram.html">
                      Book of the Moment: Building Seagram
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7543248867288517241">
                     The importance of Mies van der Rohe's Seagram Building (1958) to Manhattan, modern architecture and the 20th century city is undeniable. So the upcoming release of Phyllis Lambert's
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0300167679">
                      <i>
                       Building Seagram
                      </i>
                     </a>
                     from
                     <a href="http://yalebooks.co.uk/display.asp?K=9780300167672">
                      Yale University Press
                     </a>
                     (Spring 2013) is a highly anticipated one.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="lambert-seagram.jpg" src="http://www.archidose.org/Blog/lambert-seagram.jpg" title="Building Seagram"/>
                     <br/>
                     [Photo on right:
                     <i>
                      Philip Johnson, Ludwig Mies van der Rohe, and Phyllis Lambert in front of an image of the model for the Seagram building, New York, 1955. Photographer unknown. Fonds Phyllis Lambert, Canadian Centre for Architecture, Montreal.  United Press International.
                     </i>
                     | photo
                     <a href="http://www.grahamfoundation.org/grantees/4906-building-seagram">
                      source
                     </a>
                     ]
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     From the
                     <a href="http://www.cca.qc.ca/">
                      CCA
                     </a>
                     :
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      Lambert was a 27-year-old artist living in Paris when her father Samuel Bronfman, founder of the Seagram distillery, asked her to take over the search for an architect to design his companys headquarters in New York. Lambert in turn made the cogent decision to commission the pioneering modern master Mies van der Rohe (1886-1969), in collaboration with Philip Johnson (1906-2005). The resulting Seagram Building, completed in 1958, is an indisputable icon of twentieth-century architecture, 38 stories of elegant bronze and glass set back from New York Citys Park Avenue. In her role as patron, and Director of Planning for the project, Lambert had singlehandedly changed the face of American urban architecture. "
                      <i>
                       Building Seagram
                      </i>
                      represents what has always been the focus of my practice: architecture as a major socio-economic manifestation in society," explained Lambert.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      The culmination of more than a decade of research by Lambert,
                      <i>
                       Building Seagram
                      </i>
                      tells the story not just of this important building, but of the culture of post-WWII design, including the significant part corporate patronage played in the eras real estate development, and of the projects substantial role in shaping landmark legislation and zoning laws in New York City. Lambert provides an unprecedented personal history of her experience managing the project, as well as of the working relationship between van der Rohe and Johnson and Johnsons under- recognized contributions to lighting design, and offers a detailed scholarly assessment of the design and construction process and the buildings cultural legacy.
                     </blockquote>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://archidose.blogspot.com/2012/07/book-of-moment-five-borough-farm.html">
                      Book of the Moment: Five Borough Farm
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1778287011983603628">
                     Last night the
                     <a href="http://www.designtrust.org/">
                      Design Trust for Public Space
                     </a>
                     and project partner
                     <a href="http://added-value.org/">
                      Added Value
                     </a>
                     celebrated the launch of
                     <i>
                      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B008JEA9X2?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B008JEA9X2&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=aweeklydoseof-20">
                       Five Borough Farm: Seeding the Future of Urban Agriculture in New York City
                      </a>
                     </i>
                     . The book, which is accompanied by
                     <a href="http://www.fiveboroughfarm.org/">
                      a website
                     </a>
                     , surveys the more than 700 citywide farms and gardens that grow food; metrics are outlined toward gauging the success of urban agriculture; and the project is aimed at affecting policy, so that farms and gardens will be formally recognized and supported in the city. Much more will follow from the Design Trust and Added Value in the next year, but the book is a watershed in examining the benefits of urban agriculture. A review of the book on this blog is forthcoming.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="5borough-farm.gif" src="http://www.archidose.org/Blog/5borough-farm.gif" title="Five Borough Farm"/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.fiveboroughfarm.org/about/">
                      About the Project
                     </a>
                     :
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote>
                      <b>
                       Need for
                       <i>
                        Five Borough Farm
                       </i>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Urban agriculture is booming in New York City, with more than 700 food-producing urban farms and gardens citywide. In all five boroughs, New Yorkers have turned vacant lots and rooftops, schoolyards and NYCHA gardens into places to grow food.  But urban agriculture also encompasses a wide range of other activities: participants earn income at farmers markets, capture stormwater, compost food waste, gain leadership and job skills, learn about nutrition and the environment, and create safe, attractive public spaces. These activities contribute to many citywide health, social, economic, and ecological benefits, as well as to the goals of municipal agencies and elected officials.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Yet while many government agencies are engaged in urban agriculture, there is no citywide policy or plan to coordinate actions across agencies, and few systematic efforts to track the full range of urban agricultural activities that take place at the citys farms and gardens.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       Project Origins
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      In 2009, the Design Trust for Public Space issued an open call for projects to improve public space in New York City, receiving twenty submissions from organizations citywide. The nonprofit organization Added Value (with the assistance of the design firm thread collective) submitted a proposal to address urban agriculture. This proposal was one of two selected by an independent jury of architects, policy experts, open space advocates, and Design Trust board members for the Design Trust to take on as a project.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       Project Goals
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <ol>
                       <li>
                        Survey and document New York City's existing urban agricultural activity using photographs, maps, and infographics.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        Establish a shared framework and tools to enable farmers and gardeners to track urban agricultural activity and evaluate their social, health, economic, and ecological benefits.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        Develop policy recommendations that will help make urban agriculture a more permanent part of the city's landscape and governance.
                       </li>
                      </ol>
                     </blockquote>
