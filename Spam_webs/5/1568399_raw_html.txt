        <p>
         By Rajyasree Sen
        </p>
        <div class="mceTemp" style="text-align: left">
         <dl class="wp-caption alignleft caption-alignleft " style="width: 262px">
          <dt class="wp-caption-dt">
           <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-5" height="174" src="http://s.wsj.net/public/resources/images/OB-XG562_imango_D_20130429074659.jpg" width="262"/>
          </dt>
          <dd class="wp-caption-dd wp-cite-dd" style="text-align: right">
           Agence France-Presse/Getty Images
          </dd>
          <dd class="wp-caption-dd" style="text-align: left">
           There are hundreds of varieties of mangoes in India, all with a distinctive taste.
          </dd>

        </div>
        <p>
         This is the best season of the year: mango season. You cant walk into a market in India without being enveloped by the sweet smell of ripe mangoes.
        </p>
        <div class="insetCol3wide">
         <div class="insetContent">
          <h3 class="first">
           <a href="#">
            More In Mangoes
           </a>
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/indiarealtime/2014/06/15/top-five-mango-must-haves/">
             Top Five Mango Must-Haves
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/indiarealtime/2014/06/11/mango-farmers-struggling-as-e-u-ban-leads-to-glut/">
             Mango Farmers Struggling as E.U. Ban Leads to Glut
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/indiarealtime/2014/05/02/britains-house-of-commons-to-debate-indian-mango-ban/">
             Britains House of Commons to Debate Indian Mango Ban
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/indiarealtime/2014/04/29/european-union-bans-import-of-indian-mangoes/">
             European Union Bans Import of Indian Mangoes
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/indiarealtime/2012/05/08/dunkin-donuts-indian-menu/">
             Dunkin' Donuts' 'Indian' Menu
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         I remember the tiny yellow mangoes that fit in my palm when I was a kid. Youd squeeze them and make a gash at the point where the mango had been connected to the tree, then suck out the deliciously sweet flesh.
        </p>
        <div class="adtext">
         <span>
          Advertisement
         </span>
        </div>
        <div data-cb-ad-id="RealtorAd" id="ad_placeholder">
        </div>
        <p>
         There are hundreds of varieties of mangoes in India, all with a distinctive taste. Many people consider themselves connoisseurs, turning their noses up at one type or another.
        </p>
        <p>
         In India, people dont just eat mangoes as a fruit. Theyre used in desserts like aam kheer, also known as payasam,and shrikhand, as well as smoothies and lassis. Ive also had delicious mango kulfi, which is our version of home-made creamy ice lollies.
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are some of the mangoes you should look out for in the market. You may get a favorite of your own soon, as it is difficult not to develop an affinity for one.
        </p>
        <p>
         To me, the king of mangoes is the langra. Its greener and rounder than many others; smaller and far sweeter than the alphonso.Ive seen lots in Delhi.
        </p>
        <p>
         Langra mangoes are originally from near Varanasi. This variety of mango, so the story goes, is named after the lame farmer who harvested it for the first time. Langra is the Hindi and Bengaliword for lame.
        </p>
        <p>
         Another king of mangoes is the alphonso. Ive seen people carry crates of these to the U.K. Alphonoso mangoesare harvested in Ratnagiri, Maharashtra. They have an unforgettable and distinctive fragrance.Alphonsos are also referred to as hapoos, which is worth remembering when shopping for them.
        </p>
        <p>
         The delightful kesar is a highly unappreciated variant. This mango originates from Gujarat and Saurashtra. It is called kesar, which means saffron, because of its color and fragrance. If youre making desserts, milkshakes and other goodies, this is a good mango to use because the pulp is non-fibrous.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres also the dassehri mango, which was harvested from a village by the same name in Uttar Pradesh. Its a slightly longer shape than other mangoes and is green outside, never yellow. Dassehris taste divine.
        </p>
        <p>
         The totapuri is green and sort of elliptical in shape. Its great for salads and salsas, but not so good on its own as its not so sweet.
        </p>
        <p>
         Speaking of sweet, I tried badami mangoes when I was visiting a friend in Bangalore. If you want to bounce off the walls with a sugar high like no other, this is the one for you.
        </p>
        <p>
         Make the most of the mango season and eat as many varieties as you can.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres one of my favorite recipes for cooking with mangoes. Its the perfect accompaniment to a main course, or even as a starter with nachos.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Mango salsa
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1 large mango (chopped into small pieces)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 green capsicum (diced)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 red capsicum (diced)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 small onion (diced)
         </li>
         <li>
           cup chopped coriander
         </li>
         <li>
          3 chilis chopped finely
         </li>
         <li>
          Juice of 1 lime
         </li>
         <li>
          Salt to taste
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Mix all the ingredients together.
        </p>
        <p>
         Be careful not to mash the mango pieces; they must retain their shape.
        </p>
        <p>
         Keep in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before serving.
        </p>

// performs callback when element becomes visible, then removes listeners
function whenElementVisibleDo(el, cb) {
  var handler = function() {
    if (isElementInViewportAtAll(el)) {
      cb();
      removeListeners(handler);
    }
  }
  attachListeners(handler);
}

// similar to http://stackoverflow.com/a/7557433/458614 but returns true if any part of the element is in the viewport, not just the whole thing
// should work on: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, IE8+, iOS Safari, Android2+, Blackberry, Opera Mobile, IE Mobile
// doesn't handle z-index, being outside of viewable area of parent element, and possibly other edge cases
// we check if either the top OR the bottom of the element is within the viewport, while either the left right or the element is also in the viewport
function isElementInViewportAtAll (el) {
    var rect = el.getBoundingClientRect();
    var height = window.innerHeight || document.documentElement.clientHeight;
    var width = window.innerWidth || document.documentElement.clientWidth;
    return (
      (
        (rect.top &lt;= height &amp;&amp; rect.top &gt;= 0) ||
        (rect.bottom &lt;= height &amp;&amp; rect.bottom &gt;= 0)
      ) &amp;&amp;
      (
        (rect.left &lt;= width &amp;&amp; rect.left &gt;= 0) ||
        (rect.right &lt;= width &amp;&amp; rect.right &gt;= 0)
      ) 
    );
}

function attachListeners(fn) {
  if (window.addEventListener) {
    window.addEventListener('DOMContentLoaded', fn, false);
    window.addEventListener('load', fn, false);
    window.addEventListener('scroll', fn, false);
    window.addEventListener('resize', fn, false);
  } else if (window.attachEvent) { // IE fallback
    window.attachEvent('DOMContentLoaded', fn);
    window.attachEvent('load', fn);
    window.attachEvent('onscroll', fn);
    window.attachEvent('onresize', fn);
  }
}
function removeListeners(fn) {
  if (window.removeEventListener) {
    window.removeEventListener('DOMContentLoaded', fn, false);
    window.removeEventListener('load', fn, false);
    window.removeEventListener('scroll', fn, false);
    window.removeEventListener('resize', fn, false);
  } else if (window.attachEvent) { // IE fallback
    window.detachEvent('DOMContentLoaded', fn);
    window.detachEvent('load', fn);
    window.detachEvent('onscroll', fn);
    window.detachEvent('onresize', fn);
  }
}

// adds a class when top of element is off screen, removes class again when scrolling back up to that point
function addClassWhenOffTop(el, classname) {
  var scrollTopWhenAdded;
  attachListeners(function() {
    if (scrollTopWhenAdded === undefined) {
      // class has not been added , only add for ads with size less than 400
      if ( (el.getBoundingClientRect().top) &lt; 0 &amp;&amp; ($(el).children()[0].height &lt; 400 ) ) {
        // it's off the top of the screen
        el.className += ' ' + classname;
        scrollTopWhenAdded = document.documentElement.scrollTop || document.body.scrollTop;
      }
    }
    else {
      if ((document.documentElement.scrollTop || document.body.scrollTop) &lt;= scrollTopWhenAdded) {
        // we've gone back up past it
        el.className = el.className.replace(new RegExp('\\b'+classname+'\\b'), ''); // remove class. in PHP double-quoted string have to do 4 backslashes to get 2
        scrollTopWhenAdded = undefined;
      }
    }
  });
}
