           <div class="n j-text " id="cc-m-8207925084">
            <p>
             In 1952, the first remote seat for the state government was established somewhere south of the Danish border. It was one of four similar facilites that would have been used to evacuate key
    personnel of the regional government in case of war or nuclear emergency.
             <br/>
            </p>
            <p>
             The buildings above ground level belonged to the facility as well as an extensive subterranean part.
             <br data-mce-bogus="1"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             The bunker provided basic protection against fire, gas, cemical and biological weapons as well as radiation but would not have withstood a direct attack with high explosive or even nuclear
    weapons. There are no real documents about these underground bunkers - their existence has only been made public at the beginning of the 2000s.
             <br data-mce-bogus="1"/>
            </p>

         <p data-mce-style="text-align: left;" style="text-align: left;">
          As the size of the Navy in the city of Kiel grew, so did the need for a military hospital: In 1901/02, an area of about 6 hectares was selected as a construction site. Until 1907, a military
    hospital was built in the typical style of the Kaiser's time and embedded in a spacious park for the patients' recreation.
         </p>
         <p data-mce-style="text-align: left;" style="text-align: left;">
          Edged with a fence of brick columns and cast iron, the 16 buildings included a villa for the director, residences for the doctors, officials and servants, an operating house and bed houses as
    well as a boiler house for district heating.
         </p>
         <p data-mce-style="text-align: left;" style="text-align: left;">
          One of the six so-called pavilion buildings was used for the seclusion of patients with infectious diseases.
         </p>
         <p data-mce-style="text-align: left;" style="text-align: left;">
          At the time, penicillin was not known yet, so the contagious patients had to be seperated from the others.
         </p>
         <p data-mce-style="text-align: left;" style="text-align: left;">
          A little futher, you can find the former mortuary in which autopsies were conducted and the dead were laid out.
         </p>
         <p data-mce-style="text-align: left;" style="text-align: left;">
          After World War II, the nurses from a sisterhood took over most of the medical operations in the hospital and eventually leased it in 1950. Later, different parts of the university's hospitals
    found a new home in the old buildings in exchange for the ones destroyed in the war.
         </p>
