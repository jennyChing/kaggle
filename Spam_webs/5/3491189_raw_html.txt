          </b>

          <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.endomondo.android">
           Endomondo
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.mapmyrun.android2">
           MapMyRun
          </a>
          , and
          <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.runtastic.android.pro2">
           Runtastic
          </a>

          <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.shotzoom.golfshot2">
           Golfshot
          </a>
          gives you accurate distances from tee to pin so you can always grab the right club from your bag.

          </a>

          <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.keep">

          </a>

          You say you want a mobile revolution...
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="post-header">
         <div class="published">
          <span class="caption">
           Posted:
          </span>
          <span class="publishdate" itemprop="datePublished">
           Thursday, May 28, 2015

          </span>
          <span class="twitter-custom social-wrapper" data-href="http://twitter.com/share?text=Official Android Blog:You say you want a mobile revolution...&amp;url=http://officialandroid.blogspot.fr/2015/05/io-2015-mobile-revolution.html&amp;via=android">

        <div class="post-body">
         <div class="post-content" itemprop="articleBody">
          <div>
           <div>
            <i>
             (Cross posted on the
             <a href="http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2015/05/io-2015-mobile-revolution.html">
              Official Google Blog
             </a>
             )
            </i>
           </div>
           <div>
            <br/>
           </div>
           This morning, more than 6,000 developers descended on San Franciscos Moscone Center to burn through 1,500 gallons of coffee and join millions of others via live stream for our 8th annual Google I/Oa time to fill people in on what weve been building recently, and how were tackling the future.
          </div>
          <br/>
          <b>
           Android growth and momentum
          </b>
          <br/>
          In just a short number of years, mobile technology has completely changed the way we find information and entertainment, communicate with friends and family, and get things done. Having a supercomputer in our pocket is now second nature; today more searches on Google come from mobile than from desktop computers, and by
          <a href="http://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/gadgets-and-tech/news/there-are-officially-more-mobile-devices-than-people-in-the-world-9780518.html">
           some estimates
          </a>
          there are more mobile devices than there are people on the planet. For evidence of the mobile revolution, look no further than the growth of Android. There are now more than one billion Android users worldwidea long way from when we launched the first Android phone back in 2008. And there are 4,000 unique Android devices on the market, from more than 400 manufacturers and over 500 carriers.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          The devices themselves have changed a lot, too. In todays multi-screen world, you can now use Android on your phone, your tablet, your wrist, in your car and in your living room, and move seamlessly between each. Many of these new form factors have arrived just in the last year. You can now choose from seven different Android Wear watches, not to mention bands, styles, and more than 1,500 watch faces built by developers.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          By the end of this year, 35 car models will offer Android Auto, helping you access Search, Maps, music and other information through your cars controls. And the first sets running Android TV have now arrived.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          With all of these new places and devices for people to use Android, developers have even more opportunities to build the apps that people use for education and engagement and entertainment. So today we talked about the new tools and features were giving them to build more powerful experiences on the Android platform.
          <br/>
          <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2qMA7DyAaLo/VWdHrtEyuhI/AAAAAAAAAfI/s2upBwfPe5I/s1600/device-lineup-2015.001.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
            <img border="0" height="360" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2qMA7DyAaLo/VWdHrtEyuhI/AAAAAAAAAfI/s2upBwfPe5I/s640/device-lineup-2015.001.png" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <div>
           <div>
            <b>
             M is for more performance and an improved user experience
            </b>
            <br/>
            <a href="http://android-developers.blogspot.com/2015/05/android-m-developer-preview-tools.html">
             Android M
            </a>
            is the most powerful Android release yet, with
            <a href="http://android.com/">
             hundreds
            </a>
            of improvements made to the platform. Among the highlights, weve improved battery life and streamlined permissions for apps to make it easier for you to decide what information the apps on your phone can use. We previewed
            <a href="http://officialandroid.blogspot.com/2015/05/pay-your-way-with-android.html">
             Android Pay
            </a>
            , which lets you pay for things with your phone, without even opening an app. And were making it much easier to find information in apps, as well as making some important updates to Google Now (more on that below!).
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <b>
             Organizing the worlds information, better
            </b>
            <br/>
            Your mobile phone packs a lot of information, but its not always easy to find that nugget of information when you need itas you know if youve ever tried to navigate your email, organize hundreds of photos across devices, or search for restaurant reviews when youre chatting about dinner plans with friends. Luckily, finding and organizing information is something Google is good at (some might even call it our
            <a href="https://www.google.com/webhp?sourceid=chrome-instant&amp;ion=1&amp;espv=2&amp;ie=UTF-8#q=google%20mission">
             mission
            </a>
            ).
            <br/>
            <br/>
            So as part of M release, were expanding
            <a href="http://insidesearch.blogspot.com/2015/05/now-on-tap.html">
             Google Now
            </a>
            to give people on-demand assistance in the moment they need itlike seeing if theres an open table at a new restaurant or when and where Pitch Perfect 2 is playingno matter where you are on your phone. Were also making it much easier to
            <a href="http://insidesearch.blogspot.com/2014/06/more-apps-in-google-searchnow-even.html">
             find new apps and in-app content
            </a>
            which is good news for both users and developers.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Weve also put our years of research into machine learning to work in other ways, making Search more useful and your
            <a href="http://gmailblog.blogspot.com/2015/05/thanks-to-you-inbox-by-gmail-is-now.html">
             inbox more insightful
            </a>
            . And now its also helping you make sense of all your photos. Today we launched a
            <a href="http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2015/05/picture-this-fresh-approach-to-photos.html">
             new Photos app
            </a>
            that gives you a single place for all your photos and videos, and helps you sort through them more quickly, bring them to life in cool new ways, and share them however you choose.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <b>
             A new platform for the Internet of Things
            </b>
            <br/>
            Were surrounded by devices, but they often exist independently of each other. Our day-to-day lives will be much simpler when these technologies can talk to each otherif our recipe app, for example, could communicate with our smart oven to turn the temperature to exactly the right setting. Or outside the homefrom transportation systems that notify commuters of schedule changes, to farms where harvesters and irrigation systems are controlled from phones. 
            <br/>
            <br/>
            But many roadblocks remainthe user experience is inconsistent and confusing, manufacturers often redo their work for every device, devices dont interoperate, and developers often have no way to create great experiences across devices.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Enter
            <a href="http://developers.google.com/brillo">
             Project Brillo
            </a>
            , a new platform derived from Android that lets developers and manufacturers build connected devices. As part of Brillo, were introducing a communications protocol (Weave) developed in partnership with Nest, a set of developer APIs, a core set of schemas and a certification program to ensure device and app interoperability.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Although it will launch later this year, we previewed Brillo today because were committed to fostering a vibrant ecosystem in which we all work together to move the industry forward.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <b>
             New mobile experiences
            </b>
            <br/>
            Mobile has evolved so much in the past few years, with connected screens for different experiences depending on your needs. But we are just at the start of what will prove to be a much more immersive mobile experience. At last years I/O we introduced
            <a href="http://g.co/cardboard">
             Cardboard
            </a>
            , which lets you turn your phone into a virtual reality experience. Now there are more than 500
            <a href="http://g.co/cardboardapps">
             Cardboard apps
            </a>
            for film, games, tours and learning, and more than 1 million Cardboard viewers have been shipped. Today we announced iOS support for developers and debuted
            <a href="http://g.co/expeditions">
             Google Expeditions
            </a>
            , which lets students take virtual trips with Cardboard to places like the moon and underwater. We also shared a preview of
            <a href="http://g.co/jump">
             Jump
            </a>
            , which lets you capture the world in video that you can step inside of.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <b>
             The next billion users
            </b>
            <br/>
            The first billion users of the Internet came online through desktops. The next billion are taking a different path to computingcoming online through mobile and smartphonesand present a unique set of opportunities and challenges. Were working hard on ensure these people have a great experience across our products.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            In addition to making devices more affordable with
            <a href="https://www.google.com/chromebook/">
             Chromebooks
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://www.android.com/intl/en_us/one/">
             Android One
            </a>
            (now in seven countries), were making changes to ensure that our software works even where there arent great Internet connections. Weve launched a
            <a href="https://plus.google.com/+google/posts/XZo6qgrNDE9">
             streamlined version of our Search results page
            </a>
            in 13 countries, and 73 million people now use
            <a href="https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/2392284?hl=en">
             data saver mode
            </a>
            in Chrome to browse the web more efficiently. Finally, we previewed the new offline mapsthats right, and its as simple as it soundsmaps that you can take offline, even with turn-by-turn directions.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <b>
             Solving complex problems for a mobile world
            </b>
            <br/>
            From our earliest days in Search, our aim has always been to build products for everyone, applying unique technical insight to tackle big problems. Thats just as relevant in todays mobile-centric worldfrom finding the information scattered across apps, to helping someone organize and share the photos of their kids; from taking people on a virtual trip to the Pyramids to helping the next billion people come online.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            And by providing a platform on top of which any developer can innovate, we can reach people around the world and put the power of the Internet in their handsno matter what device they use, where they live or who they are.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            So heres to the mobile revolution. We cant wait to see what comes next.
           </div>

        <h2 class="title" itemprop="name">
         <a href="http://officialandroid.blogspot.fr/2015/05/pay-your-way-with-android.html" itemprop="url" title="Pay your way with Android">
          Pay your way with Android
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="post-header">
         <div class="published">
          <span class="caption">
           Posted:
          </span>
          <span class="publishdate" itemprop="datePublished">
           Thursday, May 28, 2015
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="share">
          <span class="gplus-wrapper">
           <div class="g-plusone" data-href="http://officialandroid.blogspot.com/2015/05/pay-your-way-with-android.html" data-size="medium">
           </div>
          </span>
          <span class="twitter-custom social-wrapper" data-href="http://twitter.com/share?text=Official Android Blog:Pay your way with Android&amp;url=http://officialandroid.blogspot.fr/2015/05/pay-your-way-with-android.html&amp;via=android">
           <img alt="Share on Twitter" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/-R6KvqFZpBr4/VSJ05uiVt3I/AAAAAAAAHdA/8B440EEZaFc/s1600/twitter.png"/>
          </span>
          <span class="fb-custom social-wrapper" data-href="https://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?u=http://officialandroid.blogspot.fr/2015/05/pay-your-way-with-android.html">
           <img alt="Share on Facebook" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-s-JsfkxGJWs/VSJ0we73JWI/AAAAAAAAHc0/_i33oEmer1w/s1600/facebook%2B%25281%2529.png"/>
          </span>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-body">
         <div class="post-content" itemprop="articleBody">
          <span id="docs-internal-guid-72615307-9b46-9905-17a2-5b0294ad5cbf">
           Credit cards, debit cards, loyalty cards, cash, coins. Forget fumbling through your wallet next time youre in a store
          </span>
          what if you could pay with just a tap?
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Introducing Android Pay, the simple and secure way to pay with your Android phone.
          <br/>
          <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/OueObu2aA_M" width="560">
           </iframe>
          </div>
          <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           <span id="docs-internal-guid-72615307-9b46-9905-17a2-5b0294ad5cbf">
            <span style="-webkit-transform: rotate(0rad); border-color: initial; border-image-outset: initial; border-image-repeat: initial; border-image-slice: initial; border-image-source: initial; border-image-width: initial; border-width: initial; font-size: 13.3333333333333px; line-height: 1.38; margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; transform: rotate(0rad); white-space: pre-wrap;">
            </span>
           </span>
          </div>
          <div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
           <span style="vertical-align: baseline;">
            Things are more interesting when we create them together. So weve approached Android Pay the same way we approach every other part of the Android family -- partnering with the ecosystem -- bringing together mobile carriers, payment networks, banks and retailers to deliver you choice and flexibility. And offering developers an open platform to collectively push mobile payments forward.
           </span>
          </div>
          <div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <b>
            Tap to pay in stores
           </b>
           <br/>
           With Android Pay, you can simply unlock your phone like you normally do,  place it near a merchants contactless terminal, and youre good to go. Android Pay does all the heavy lifting. You dont even need to open an appjust tap and go. Youll also see a payment confirmation and get transaction details right on your phone.
          </div>
          <div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
           <span style="clear: right; float: right; font-family: 'Open Sans'; font-size: 13.3333333333333px; line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">
           </span>
           <span style="vertical-align: baseline;">
            <br/>
            Also, with select retailers, your loyalty programs and special offers will be automatically applied at checkout. For example, when you tap to buy a Coke at a vending machine, your MyCokeRewards will be automatically applied. With Android Pay the contactless terminal not only receives your payment info, but also your loyalty programs and offers.
           </span>
           <br/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           </div>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           </div>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-AfnIin3eFYk/VWdU19NpY7I/AAAAAAAAAfc/7VQ7JD0tq8I/s1600/N6%2BAndroid%2BPay.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
             <img border="0" height="452" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-AfnIin3eFYk/VWdU19NpY7I/AAAAAAAAAfc/7VQ7JD0tq8I/s640/N6%2BAndroid%2BPay.png" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <b>
            Tap to pay in apps
           </b>
          </div>
          <div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
           Making an online purchase? The days of entering your credit card and shipping address each time you make a purchase online will be a thing of the past. Just select  Buy with Android Pay and leave the rest to us; checkout is as fast as a tap.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           To make it easier for developers to add Android Pay to your favorite apps, weve designed our platform to work with any payment processor. And were partnering with top payments processors including Braintree, CyberSource, First Data,  Stripe and Vantiv to make integration even easier.
          </div>
          <div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
           <br/>
           <b>
            The choice is yours
           </b>
          </div>
          <div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
           With Android Pay you will be able to pay with your credit or debit card, across multiple Android devices, and at thousands of stores and apps that you already know and love.And by enabling bank apps to integrate with our platform, youll be able to add your credit and debit cards directly from bank apps for use with Android Pay.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           It's still early days, but we're very excited and think that this type of open platform will help drive adoption in mobile payments.
          </div>
          <div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
           <br/>
           <b>
            Security is at the center of Android Pay
           </b>
           <br/>
           We know how important it is for you to keep yourpersonal and financial information secure. Therefore, were working with leading payment networks and financial institutions in the US to deliver industry standard security tokenization, to support Android Pays launch later this year.
           <br/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-y1M27MHyFK8/VWi0AwDx7-I/AAAAAAAAAf0/3r5jZ5OCqto/s1600/%255BUpdate%255D-Logo-grid.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
             <img border="0" height="281" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-y1M27MHyFK8/VWi0AwDx7-I/AAAAAAAAAf0/3r5jZ5OCqto/s400/%255BUpdate%255D-Logo-grid.png" width="400"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <br/>
           </div>
           <span id="docs-internal-guid-134e3d72-9ce2-4087-5469-2817469b1bb7">
           </span>
          </div>
          So when you shop at a store, Android Pay wont send your actual credit or debit card number with your payment. Instead well use a virtual account number to represent your account information -- providing you with an extra layer of security. And if your phone is ever lost or stolen, simply use
          <a href="https://accounts.google.com/ServiceLogin?service=androidconsole&amp;passive=3600&amp;continue=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.google.com%2Fandroid%2Fdevicemanager%3Fu%3D0&amp;followup=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.google.com%2Fandroid%2Fdevicemanager%3Fu%3D0&amp;authuser=0">
           Android Device Manager
          </a>
          to instantly lock your device from anywhere, secure it with a new password or even wipe it clean of your personal information.
          <br/>
          <div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
           <div style="text-align: left;">
            <br/>
           </div>
           <div style="text-align: left;">
            <b>
             Shop at your favorites
            </b>
           </div>
           <div style="text-align: left;">
            Android Pay will soon be accepted at over 700,000 store locations from your favorite brands across the US, and in over 1000 Android apps. And well be adding more every day.
           </div>
           <br/>
           <b>
            Android Pay in stores
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
           </b>
           <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans';">
            <span style="background-color: white; font-size: 13.3333333333333px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">
             <img height="282" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/oAxdO6e6a4pnxmTtx_zptj3gUb1yEtCKtw-4fccU2teVknscBehJR9d-vvb4S-n0ZPBXjXiIg1_4KlMU7bYwT-631WuoyDCu-Orz53TIoUylGVOZgBG8ci8UnFJtdIWQ43KaGRM" style="-webkit-transform: rotate(0.00rad); border: none; transform: rotate(0.00rad);" width="400"/>
            </span>
           </span>
          </div>
          <div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
           <b>
            Android Pay in apps
           </b>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans';">
            <span style="font-size: 13.3333333333333px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">
             <img height="280" src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/F_5xqh2804R2xdFPxLY4b9jWvdvJab8Ta5fUi16BTmNjb7K6YmPX9BIgEd0K6hhTRrQ1RvpV7vTizKXsp_u9f9Yv2yeBddz0T8K8UBcIPGrS65qk3E4XqtTywXoieOtjMhCNiJw" style="-webkit-transform: rotate(0.00rad); border: none; transform: rotate(0.00rad);" width="400"/>
            </span>
           </span>
          </div>
          <b>
           Stay Tuned
          </b>
          <br/>
          Android Pay will be available on Google Play for download soon. And when your bank integrates, you could also activate Android Pay directly from your bank app. We are also working with major US mobile carriers (AT&amp;T, T-Mobile, Verizon) to help ensure that when you buy a new Android phone, you can walk out the door ready to use Android Pay.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Look forward to sharing more in the coming months.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <i>
           Posted by Pali Bhat, Director, Product Management
          </i>
