      <article class="post-1265 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-flying-is-beautiful tag-flight-training tag-flight-training-school tag-flying-school tag-private-pilot-license tag-sightseeing-flight" id="post-1265">
       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         10 Views You Cant Beat From the Cockpit
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="fontsize13">
         Posted on
         <a href="http://www.torontoairways.com/blog/10-views-you-cant-beat-from-the-cockpit/" rel="bookmark" title="5:41 pm">
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2013-12-06T17:41:12+00:00">
           December 6, 2013
          </time>
         </a>
         <span class="by-author">
          by
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.torontoairways.com/blog/author/admin/" rel="author" title="View all posts by admin">
            admin
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style=" float: left; margin-right: 10px;  width: auto;">
        </div>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          The airplane has unveiled for us the true face of the earth.
          <br/>
          - Antoine de St-Exupery
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         Flying gives you a whole different view of the world. While youre up in the sky looking down at the landscape below, youre treated to a magnificent array of sights. Here are ten of the most surprising and strangely beautiful things youll see flying around Ontario.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         1. Messages in Farmers Fields
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Blue Jays Flying Farm" class="size-medium wp-image-1272 aligncenter" height="168" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Blue-jays-Farm-Logo-300x168.jpg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         In the spring, people will sometimes set up their farm fields and draw messages or designs on their fields that can only be seen from the air, like this Lets go Blue Jays show of support.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: left;">
        </h3>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         2. Mirrored Reflection
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Flying Mirrored Reflection" class="size-medium wp-image-1273 aligncenter" height="167" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-08-05-at-9.55.58-PM-300x167.png" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         When youre flying in winter, as the temperature starts to drop and the water begins to freeze, the thin glossy ice that forms looks like a mirror. Pilots flying at a decent altitude can see their reflections mirrored in the ice.
         <span id="more-1265">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         3. Ice Formations
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Flying Lake Scugog Ice" class="size-medium wp-image-1274 aligncenter" height="168" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/lake-scugog-300x168.jpg" style="font-size: 1rem; line-height: 1;" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         In the winter, youll also notice the way ice forms from shore, from the shallow to deep areas and the patches of water that didnt freeze. When flying over many of Ontarios frozen lakes, such as Cooks Bay on the southern part of Lake Simcoe, youll spot small ice fishing huts with hopeful fishermen inside.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         4. Fireworks Displays
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Flight Fireworks" class="size-medium wp-image-1275 aligncenter" height="202" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Flight-fireworks-300x202.jpg" style="font-size: 1rem; line-height: 1;" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         Happy Canada Day! As the sky lights up with brilliant fireworks, why just enjoy it from the ground when you can get a much better view of the dramatic explosions from up in the air.
        </p>
        <h2>
         5. The Fall Colours
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/sightseeing-flight-autumn.jpg" style="color: #0f3647; line-height: 24px;">
          <img alt="Sightseeing Flight Autumn" class="size-medium wp-image-1277 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/sightseeing-flight-autumn-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         While always magnificent, the annual fall display of colours is even more splendid when seen from above.
        </p>
        <h2>
         6. The Northern Lights
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/northen-lights-flight.jpg" style="color: #0f3647; line-height: 24px;">
          <img alt="Northern Lights Flying" class="size-medium wp-image-1289 aligncenter" height="201" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/northen-lights-flight-300x201.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         If youre flying in northern areas of Canada, away from the bright lights of the city, one of the most spectacular sights is the shimmering spectacle of the Northern Lights.
        </p>
        <h2>
         7. City Skyline
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/toronto-skyline.jpg" style="color: #0f3647; line-height: 24px;">
          <img alt="Toronto Skyline Flight" class="size-medium wp-image-1280 aligncenter" height="168" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/toronto-skyline-300x168.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         Flying out of Buttonville Airport, youll see one built-up skyline after another  Scarborough, North York, downtown Toronto, Mississauga, Thornhill, Vaughan and York Region.
        </p>
        <h2>
         8. The City Lights at Night
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/toronto-night.jpg" style="color: #0f3647; line-height: 24px;">
          <img alt="Toronto Night Flight" class="size-medium wp-image-1281 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/toronto-night-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         At night time, you can see an entire city from a birds eye view and the boundaries where the city stops and starts are clearly delineated.
        </p>
        <h2>
         9. Niagara Falls
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Niagara-Falls-from-the-air.jpg" style="color: #0f3647; line-height: 24px;">
          <img alt="Niagara Falls Sightseeing Flight" class="size-medium wp-image-1282 aligncenter" height="225" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Niagara-Falls-from-the-air-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         One of the seven wonders of the world, Niagara Falls, is spectacular when viewed from above.
        </p>
        <h2>
         10. The Sunset
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/sunset-airplane.png" style="color: #0f3647; line-height: 24px;">
          <img alt="Sunset From an Airplane" class="size-medium wp-image-1283 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.torontoairways.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/sunset-airplane-300x300.png" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left; line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1em; padding-left: 10px;">
         What do most pilots never get tired of seeing? The ever-changing panorama and beauty of the sunset  and you cant beat the view from the cockpit.
        </p>
        <p>
         These and many more wonderful sights are what you can see with your Private Pilot Licence. Visit us now at
         <a href="http://www.torontoairways.com/">
          www.torontoairways.com
         </a>
         to find out how you can earn your wings!Not interested in flight training but still want to see some incredible sights? Take a
         <a href="http://www.torontoairways.com/ft-flight-training/flying-school-toronto/fam-flight-toronto/">
          sightseeing tour with us
         </a>
         . Sightseeing tours make great Christmas gifts. For more information call
         <strong>
          905-477-8110
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         What sights do you enjoy seeing from the air? Share your photos and your thoughts on our
         <a href="https://www.facebook.com/TorontoAirways" target="_blank">
          Facebook page
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">
          or
         </span>
         <a href="https://twitter.com/TorontoAirways" target="_blank">
          Twitter stream
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">
          .
         </span>
        </p>
