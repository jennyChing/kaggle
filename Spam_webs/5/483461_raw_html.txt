            <p>
             <img alt="Money Saving tips" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-75174" height="237" src="http://www.savethestudent.org/uploads/Money-Saving-tips.jpg" width="608"/>
             NatWest have just released the 8
             <sup>
              th
             </sup>
             Year of their
             <a href="http://personal.natwest.com/personal/current-accounts/compare-current-accounts/student-account/student-living-index.html">
              Student Living Index
             </a>
             and the results this year are interesting to say the least.
            </p>
            <p>
             Competition between universities is always something that students are interested in (theres nothing like a little friendly rivalry) and NatWest pitted the major student cities in the UK against each other to find out who were the most savvy spenders (and earners).
            </p>
            <p>
             The survey was filled out by over 2,000 students and then the major cities were ranked by their scores calculated by the average local weekly student expenditure on living and accommodation costs divided by average local weekly income for working students.
            </p>

            <p>
             br&gt;
             <br/>
             br&gt;
            </p>
            <p>
             Glasgow were the winners this year with their students spending on average 2.5x more than they earn.
            </p>
            <p>
             The real worry is for those in Brighton, Edinburgh, Birmingham, Portsmouth &amp; London who are spending more than 4x what they are earning.
            </p>
            <p>
             With the gap in spending vs earning so large where is the money coming from? Extra results from the survey shed some light on this.
            </p>
            <p>
             Although there has been a drop in parental contributions it still averages out as 114.10 per student per week (not a value to be scoffed at). Most surprising is that males claim they are given, on average, 5 more a week than their female counterparts. Girl power!
            </p>
            <p>
             The remainder of the gap between spending and work earnings comes from the maintenance loans as well as grants and other funding on offer to students. There has also been a rise in students looking to maximise the value of their
             <a href="http://www.savethestudent.org/money/student-banking/student-bank-accounts.html">
              0% student account overdraft
             </a>
             too.
            </p>
