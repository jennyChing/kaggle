    <p>
     Locals claim that before becoming a tourist attraction, the sea glass at Glass Beach was inches deep in some places, and smooth enough that one could comfortably walk barefoot atop the glass without fear of leaving with a bloody heel. These days, sea glass numbers are dwindling. Though park officials prohibit visitors from removing the treasures, they have been unable to stop the growing number of sea glass collectors who disobey the rules, often taking the sea glass by the bagful.
    </p>
    <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_13846" style="width: 710px">
     <img alt="Glass Beach Waste To Sea Glass" castles="" class="size-full wp-image-13846" dreaming="" height="465" of="" source:="" src="http://all-that-is-interesting.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Glass-Beach-Waste-to-Sea-Glass.jpg" width="700"/>

    <p>
     Years ago, it wasnt uncommon to come across sea glass in bright, brilliant red, deep pinks and purples and other rare colors usually originating from perfume bottles or other specialized knick knacks. Now, though there is still an abundance of glass, most pieces are clear, green, or brownish in color, coming from ordinary objects like bottles.
    </p>
    <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_13841" style="width: 710px">
     <img alt="Glass Beach Rare Sea Glass Colors" anjou="" class="size-full wp-image-13841" clothing="" height="564" source:="" src="http://all-that-is-interesting.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Glass-Beach-Rare-Sea-Glass-Colors.jpg" width="700"/>
     <p class="wp-caption-text">
      Source:
      <a href="http://www.anjouclothing.com">
       Anjou Clothing
      </a>
     </p>
    </div>
    <p>
     While Glass Beach boasts troves of sea glass, many beaches worldwide contain smaller amounts of sea glass for those willing to search for it. The best time to look for sea glass is in spring during low tide, especially after storms. When picking up the glass, be careful. Glass with sharp edges is fairly common to come acrossas it means that the glass hasnt been in the ocean long enough to undergo that smooth, shiny sea glass transformation.
    </p>
