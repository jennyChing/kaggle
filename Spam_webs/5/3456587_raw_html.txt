      <h2 class="h2-date">
       <a href="http://www.goodlifeeats.com/2010/09/growcookeat-garden-tour-guest-post-bellalimento.html" rel="bookmark" title="GrowCookEat  Garden Tour {Guest Post: bellalimento}">
        GrowCookEat  Garden Tour {Guest Post: bellalimento}
       </a>
      </h2>
      <p class="tags">
       <a href="http://www.goodlifeeats.com//category/guest-post">
        Guest Posts
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.goodlifeeats.com//category/uncategorized">
        Uncategorized
       </a>
       | 5 comments
      </p>
      <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
       <table class="pib-count-table pib-count-table-horizontal">

           <a always-show-count="true" class="pin-it-button-no-iframe pin-it-button-user-selects-image" count-layout="horizontal" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.goodlifeeats.com%2F2010%2F09%2Fgrowcookeat-garden-tour-guest-post-bellalimento.html&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.goodlifeeats.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2010%2F09%2Fgarden_squash_collage.jpg&amp;description=GrowCookEat%20%26%238211%3B%20Garden%20Tour%20%7BGuest%20Post%3A%20bell%26%238217%3Balimento%7D" rel="nobox">

      <p>
       My name is Paula and I have a little food blog called
       <a href="http://www.bellalimento.com/">
        bellalimento
       </a>
       . I think Katies
       <a href="http://www.goodlifeeats.com/2010/04/announcing-growcookeat.html">
        Grow Cook Eat
       </a>
       series is fantastic; I am so happy to share my little garden that could with you! Ive always grown my own herbs because I use them in just about every meal I make but have never really grown much else {until recently that is}. Last year we added some Roma Tomatoes and Bell Peppers to our garden with good success so this year weve added even more!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://www.goodlifeeats.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/garden_squash_collage.jpg">
        <img alt="squash" class="size-full wp-image-2459 aligncenter" height="432" src="http://www.goodlifeeats.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/garden_squash_collage.jpg" title="garden_squash_collage" width="504"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       We grow the majority of our plants in containers. Yes, in containers on our deck. Im proof that you dont need a large space to grow a garden. We live on the east coast {NC to be exact} and our summers are H O T. Our deck is in full sun and is the perfect situation for growing in containers. Not to mention my deck is directly off of my kitchen &amp; I can go and pick what I need while Im cooking. Farm {or ehem deck to table ; ) }
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.goodlifeeats.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/garden_collage.jpg">
        <img alt="garden collage pictures" class="size-full wp-image-2461 aligncenter" height="480" src="http://www.goodlifeeats.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/garden_collage.jpg" title="garden_collage" width="560"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       We have also expanded to a small area just off of our deck that housed our ginormous rosemary bush. We *gasp* cut it back and tilled the ground around it to allow for more plantings. We added the larger tomatoes {celebrity and big boy varieties} along with the squash, eggplant, and cantaloupe.
      </p>
      <p>
       Here is exactly what we have planted this year
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Herbs:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Flat Leaf Italian Parsley
       </li>
       <li>
        Sweet Basil
       </li>
       <li>
        Lemon Thyme
       </li>
       <li>
        Rosemary
       </li>
       <li>
        Dill
       </li>
       <li>
        Chives
       </li>
       <li>
        Sage
       </li>
       <li>
        Oregano
       </li>
       <li>
        Mint
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Fruits:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Tomato {Varieties: Cherry, Roma, Big Boy, and Celebrity }
       </li>
       <li>
        Cantaloupe
       </li>
       <li>
        Blueberry
       </li>
       <li>
        Strawberries
       </li>
       <li>
        Bell Pepper {Varieties: green, yellow and red}
       </li>
       <li>
        Jalapeno
       </li>
       <li>
        Japanese Eggplant
       </li>
       <li>
        Crooked Neck Squash
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Some plantings that didnt make it {boo hoo} were shallots, cilantro and spinach. Well give those a go again next year. Speaking of next year, we hope to add a few raised beds {just have to figure out how to keep those pesky deer out} to grow even more homegrown goodies.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.goodlifeeats.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/garden_tomato_collage.jpg">
        <img alt="garden tomatoes" class="size-full wp-image-2460 aligncenter" height="480" src="http://www.goodlifeeats.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/garden_tomato_collage.jpg" title="garden_tomato_collage" width="560"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       There is truly nothing like fresh fruits, herbs and vegetables that youve grown yourself. Theyre more vibrant, theyre more flavorful and YOU KNOW exactly what has gone into growing them! I love seeing what new ripe and ready goodies are waiting for me each day. I plan my daily meals around my little harvest and when theyre coming in fast and furious weve also begun canning {which will allow us to enjoy our goodies during the winter months}
      </p>
      <p>
       A garden {big or small} is such a great experience! Even our little ones enjoy seeing where their food comes fromand picking the fruits of their labors when theyre ripe {or in some cases not so ripe}. Thanks so much for letting me share my little green corner with you! Happy gardening!
      </p>
      <p>
       Whats going on in your garden this week? Have you made any great recipes with your fresh produce or planted something new? Id love to hear about it!
      </p>
