         <p>
          New Line Cinema has partnered with YA author Gayle Foreman to adapt her latest novel
          <em>
           I Was Here
          </em>
          into a feature film, according to
          <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/new-line-picks-up-gayle-777459" target="_blank">
           The Hollywood Reporter
          </a>
          . The dramatic mystery follows Cody, an 18-year-old who goes on a long and dark journey to discover the truth behind her best friend Megs suicide.
          <span id="more-29221">
          </span>
          <img alt="i_was_here_cover" class=" wp-image-29224 alignright" height="273" src="http://movies.mxdwn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/i_was_here_cover.jpg" width="182"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          With YA adaptations being at the forefront of teen films inrecent years (
          <a href="http://movies.mxdwn.com/feature/lets-talk-about-the-fault-in-our-stars/" target="_blank">
           <em>
            The Fault in Our Stars
           </em>
          </a>
          ,
          <em>
           <a href="http://movies.mxdwn.com/news/final-trailer-for-the-hunger-games-mockingjay-part-1/" target="_blank">
            The Hunger Games
           </a>
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           <a href="http://movies.mxdwn.com/news/shailene-woodley-fights-back-in-new-insurgent-trailer/" target="_blank">
            Divergent
           </a>
          </em>
          series, and this months
          <a href="http://movies.mxdwn.com/reviews/movie-review-the-duff/" target="_blank">
           <em>
            The DUFF
           </em>
          </a>
          ), Foreman finds herself as one of thetrends front-running authors. The fact that
          <em>
           I Was Here
          </em>
          was just published last month furtherproves the industrys hunger to option these stories as soon as they hit the shelves. In addition to this recent deal, Foreman has already had her novel
          <a href="http://movies.mxdwn.com/reviews/movie-review-if-i-stay/" target="_blank">
           <em>
            If I Stay
           </em>
          </a>
          turn into a hit drama among teens, and her novel
          <em>
           Just One Day,
          </em>
          along with its follow-up
          <em>
           Just One Year,
          </em>
          were recently picked up by Universal to be combined into a single film produced by
          <em>
           The OCs
          </em>
          Josh Schwartz. It seems that Foreman is beginning to surpass John Green (author of
          <em>
           TFIOS
          </em>
          , and
          <em>
           Paper Towns
          </em>
          - to be released later this year) in the monopoly of all the feels, as the authors website advertises.
         </p>
