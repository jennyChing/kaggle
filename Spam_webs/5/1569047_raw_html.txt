             Bananas Foster Recipe
            </a>
           </h1>
           <div class="meta single">
            <span class="meta-author">

            <img alt="Bananas Foster Recipe" class="caption aligncenter" height="360" src="http://www.reluctantgourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/00e7056ec788d5b42162a6c13dbba43c.jpg" title="Bananas Foster Recipe" width="500"/>

           <h3>
            New Orleans Style Bananas Foster
           </h3>
           <p>
            I wanted to share with you my adaptation of a Bananas Foster recipe because it was much easier to make than I thought it would be and maybe youre looking for something to serve at one of your holiday dinners. The ingredients are simple to prep and the cooking technique is easy (except you have to be extra careful when you flamb at the end!).
           </p>
           <p>
            The
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0848731034/thereluctantgour" target="_blank">
             Williams-Sonoma cookbook
            </a>
            gives the story of how Bananas Foster was created at the famous Brennans restaurant back in 1951 for a magazine article and describes the procedure for preparing and flaming but they dont provide ingredient amounts. Here is how my wife and I prepared it.
           </p>
           <div align="center" style="margin-top: 30px; margin-bottom: 30px">
            <div class="sam-container sam-place" data-sam="1" id="c8142_7_4">
             <img height="60" src="http://www.reluctantgourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/little_rg.png" style="opacity:0.4; margin-right: 20px" width="60"/>

              <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
               Ingredients
              </p>
              <span id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
               <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
                 cup ( stick) butter
               </p>
               <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
                1-cup brown sugar
               </p>
               <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
                 teaspoon cinnamon
               </p>
               <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
                 cup banana liqueur
               </p>
               <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
                4 bananas cut in half lengthwise, then halved
               </p>
               <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
                 cup dark rum
               </p>
               <p class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
                4 scoops vanilla ice cream
               </p>
              </span>
              <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
               How To Prepare At Home
              </p>
              <span class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
               <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                In a good-sized saut or fry pan, combine the butter, brown sugar and cinnamon and heat over low heat until the butter melts and the sugar dissolves. Add the banana liqueur and stir. Add the bananas and cook until they start to soften and begin to brown. Be careful not to burn them.
               </p>
               <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                Remove the pan from the flame and add the rum. I always advise to remove a hot pan from the flame when adding any kind of alcohol just to be careful. It takes a bit for the rum to get hot enough to flame but you never know. Return the pan to the stove; cook until the rum is hot, about two minutes.
               </p>
               <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                The official method to flame the bananas is to tip the pan slightly and ignite the rum. When I tried this nothing happened. It may be I didnt let the rum get hot enough or I was just being a little timid. So I ended up using one of those long nosed fire starters and had no problem igniting the rum to flame the bananas.
               </p>
               <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                Please be careful whenever you are cooking with alcohol and although I dont think you will need one for this recipe, it makes sense to always have a small fire extinguisher in the kitchen just in case you ignite more than the bananas.
               </p>
               <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                The flames burn off the alcohol in the rum and leave a wonderful flavor.
               </p>
               <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                Spoon the ice cream into individual bowls, add the bananas, and top with the warm sauce. Youll want to serve this immediately since it doesnt take long for the sauce to melt the ice cream.
               </p>
              </span>
