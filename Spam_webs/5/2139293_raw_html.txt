       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         No Holes, Renter Friendly Window Treatments!
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-10-02T06:00:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 2, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/author/mriker/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Melissa
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/2014/10/no-holes/#comments">
           11 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="how to hang curtains with command hooks" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8238" height="755" src="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/how-to-hang-curtains-with-command-hooks.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/2014/10/no-holes/&amp;media=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/how-to-hang-curtains-with-command-hooks.jpg&amp;description=No Holes, Renter Friendly Window Treatments!')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </center>
        <p>
         The transition from living in a house we built and owned to living in a rental these past few months has been a bit of a struggle to be honest. As a DIY blogger especially its difficult to not be able to paint or make major changes to the house. It hasnt all been bad-the current townhouse has some great features like these huge windows in the living room-heres a picture from when we first moved in:
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="living room" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6711" height="450" src="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/living-room.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/2014/10/no-holes/&amp;media=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/living-room.jpg&amp;description=No Holes, Renter Friendly Window Treatments!')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </center>
        <p>
         The ceilings are ten feet high on this level of the house and I knew I wanted my typical white puddling curtains on these windows to accentuate their height. There were two problems though-first, I wanted the curtains to hang from the molding above the windows and I didnt think my landlord would appreciate me drilling into the molding. Second-a neighbor shared a story with us just after we got here of the horrors they encountered putting up curtains on their front windows-apparently the brick facade of the house is just behind the drywall! So I started thinking about other options.
        </p>
        <p>
         At Haven I talked with the folks from Command about my situation and they offered to send me some command hooks to try out-we werent sure if theyd work but I was willing to give it a try. They sent me a bunch of these awesome
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00006IBLN/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00006IBLN&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thehapphome06-20&amp;linkId=47DMXVV5ZEON2R2G" target="_blank">
          Jumbo Command Hooks
         </a>
         and my husband and I got to work!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="cutting command hooks" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8236" height="542" src="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/cutting-command-hooks.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/2014/10/no-holes/&amp;media=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/cutting-command-hooks.jpg&amp;description=No Holes, Renter Friendly Window Treatments!')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </center>
        <p>
         The first thing we realized was that the hooks were a little too tall for the space we had on the molding. My husband used some bolt cutters to cut about a half inch off the top of the hook and a metal file to smooth the edge. You can see how much he cut off here:
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="command hooks before after cutting" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8235" height="538" src="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/command-hooks-before-after-cutting.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/2014/10/no-holes/&amp;media=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/command-hooks-before-after-cutting.jpg&amp;description=No Holes, Renter Friendly Window Treatments!')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </center>
        <p>
         Because I wanted the hooks to blend into the molding I decided to paint the back surface of the hook white. I taped off the front section (that part I wanted to blend with the wrought iron curtain rod) and primed it with
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B008F4N5PA/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B008F4N5PA&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thehapphome06-20&amp;linkId=LFRWYUEZ6X67XBFO" target="_blank">
          Valspar plastic primer
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="taping command hooks" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8234" height="405" src="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/taping-command-hooks.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/2014/10/no-holes/&amp;media=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/taping-command-hooks.jpg&amp;description=No Holes, Renter Friendly Window Treatments!')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </center>
        <p>
         Then I used a touch up can of the molding paint that our landlord had left us to paint them the same color/finish as the molding.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="painting command hooks" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8233" height="450" src="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/painting-command-hooks.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/2014/10/no-holes/&amp;media=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/painting-command-hooks.jpg&amp;description=No Holes, Renter Friendly Window Treatments!')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </center>
        <p>
         Once they were dry we attached them to the wall, you can see how they blend in nicely on the back. (incidentally I could not have done this with my husband-even with my ladder I wasnt tall enough to put them up!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="putting up command hooks window curtains" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8232" height="737" src="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/putting-up-command-hooks-window-curtains.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/2014/10/no-holes/&amp;media=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/putting-up-command-hooks-window-curtains.jpg&amp;description=No Holes, Renter Friendly Window Treatments!')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </center>
        <p>
         The only drawback to the process was that using the Command hooks with our curtain rod didnt allow us to use the finials that came with the rod-they were much to big to allow the rod to sit as close to the wall as required by the hooks. Its no problem for me though-Im on the hunt for new finials now!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="floor length curtains" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8237" height="900" src="http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/floor-length-curtains-.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/2014/10/no-holes/&amp;media=http://www.thehappierhomemaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/floor-length-curtains-.jpg&amp;description=No Holes, Renter Friendly Window Treatments!')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </center>
        <p>
         I used a coupon and gift card from Pottery Barn to help offset the cost of the curtains-it is SO hard to find affordable 120 curtains and yall know Im a huge fan of puddling curtains so shorter curtains werent going to cut it.
        </p>
        <p>
         I love the look of the curtains and the height they lend to the room! I also love that when we move I can simply remove the command hooks with no issues or repairs!
        </p>
        <p>
         Are you a fan of puddling curtains too? My mom thinks Im crazy for wanting them so long, I just love the look!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
