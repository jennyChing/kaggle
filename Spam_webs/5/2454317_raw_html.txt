     <p>
      <b>
       Angel Maturino Resndiz
      </b>
      (August 1, 1959
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
       June 27, 2006), aka
      <b>
       The Railroad Killer/The Railway Killer/The Railcar Killer
      </b>
      , was an itinerant Mexican
      <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">
       serial killer
      </a>
      responsible for as many as 15 murders across the United States and Mexico during the 1990s. Some also involved sexual assault. He became known as "The Railroad (or Railway) Killer" as most of his crimes were committed near railroads where he had jumped off the trains he was using to travel about the country. On June 21, 1999, he briefly became
      <a href="/wiki/FBI_Ten_Most_Wanted_Fugitives,_1990s" title="FBI Ten Most Wanted Fugitives, 1990s">
       the 457th fugitive listed by the FBI
      </a>
      on its
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ten_Most_Wanted_Fugitives" title="Ten Most Wanted Fugitives">
       Ten Most Wanted Fugitives
      </a>
      list before surrendering to the
      <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
       Texas
      </a>
      authorities on July 13. He was 39 years old.
     </p>
     <p>
      Resndiz had many aliases but was chiefly known and sought after as
      <b>
       Rafael Resendez-Ramirez
      </b>
      . One of his aliases,
      <b>
       ngel Reyes Resndiz
      </b>
      , was very close to the name
      <b>
       ngel Leoncio Reyes Recendis
      </b>
      given on his birth certificate from
      <a href="/wiki/Iz%C3%BAcar_de_Matamoros" title="Izcar de Matamoros">
       Izcar de Matamoros
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puebla" title="Puebla">
       Puebla
      </a>
      , Mexico.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Resndez was in the United States illegally.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>

        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Murders_and_methodology">
       Murders and methodology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81ngel_Maturino_Res%C3%A9ndiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Murders and methodology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      By illegally jumping on and off trains both within and across Mexico, Canada and the United States, generally crossing borders illegally, Resndiz was able to evade authorities for a considerable time. He also had no fixed addresses. US government records show that he has been deported to Mexico at least four times since first entering the US in 1973.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OIG_report_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-OIG_report-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Resndiz killed as many as 15 people
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      with rocks, a pick axe and other blunt objects, mainly in their homes. After each murder he would linger in the homes for a while, mainly to eat; he took sentimental things and laid out the victims' driver's licenses to learn a bit about the lives he had taken. He stole jewelry and other items and gave them to his wife in Mexico. Much of the jewelry was sold or melted down. Some of the items that were removed from the homes were returned by his wife after his surrender/capture. Money, however, was sometimes left at the scene. He
      <a href="/wiki/Rape" title="Rape">
       raped
      </a>
      some of his female victims; rape served as a secondary intent. Most of his victims were found covered with a blanket, or otherwise obscured from immediate view.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Victims">
       Victims
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81ngel_Maturino_Res%C3%A9ndiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Victims">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      1. In 1986,
      <b>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bexar_County_Jane_Doe" title="Bexar County Jane Doe">
        an unidentified woman
       </a>
      </b>
      was shot four times with a .38-caliber weapon. Her body was dumped in an abandoned farmhouse. Resndiz stated that he met the woman at a homeless shelter. They took a motorcycle trip together, bringing a gun along to fire for target practice. Resndiz said that he shot and killed the woman for disrespecting him.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      2. Resendiz stated that soon after killing the homeless woman, he shot and killed her boyfriend and dumped his body in a creek somewhere between San Antonio and Uvalde. Resndiz said that he killed the man because he was involved in black magic. This man's body has never been found, and nothing is known about him except what Resndiz told authorities. Resndiz confessed to these first two murders in September 2001, in hopes that doing so would speed up his execution.
     </p>
     <p>
      3. On July 19, 1991, the body of
      <b>
       Michael White
      </b>
      , 33, was found in the front yard of an abandoned downtown house. Resndiz also confessed to this murder in September 2001. He drew a map of the crime scene and said that he killed White because he was homosexual. Police concluded in April 2006 that Resndiz did in fact kill White. He was bludgeoned to death with a brick.
     </p>
     <p>
      4 and 5. March 23, 1997,
      <a href="/wiki/Ocala,_Florida" title="Ocala, Florida">
       Ocala, Florida
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       Jesse Howell
      </b>
      , 19 years old. He was bludgeoned to death with an air hose coupling and left beside the tracks. His fiancee
      <b>
       Wendy Von Huben
      </b>
      , 16 years old, was raped, strangled, suffocated with his hands and duct tape and buried in a shallow grave in
      <a href="/wiki/Sumter_County,_Florida" title="Sumter County, Florida">
       Sumter County, Florida
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      6. In July 1997, an
      <b>
       unidentified transient
      </b>
      was beaten to death with a piece of plywood in a rail yard located in the City of Colton, California. Resndiz is considered the prime suspect in this case.
     </p>
     <p>
      7. August 29, 1997,
      <a href="/wiki/Lexington,_Kentucky" title="Lexington, Kentucky">
       Lexington, Kentucky
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       Christopher Maier
      </b>
      , 21 years old. He was a
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kentucky" title="University of Kentucky">
       University of Kentucky
      </a>
      student walking along nearby railroad tracks with his girlfriend, Holly, when the two were attacked by Resndiz, who bludgeoned Maier to death with a 52 pound rock. Resndiz raped and severely beat Maier's girlfriend, who nearly died as a result. Holly Dunn Pendleton, the only known survivor, went on to appear on the Biography channel television program "I Survived", and "48 Hours: Live To Tell"; the ID channel series "Dates From Hell" (episode 8, "A Killer Night"); and her story was told in the UK newspaper, The Guardian.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Currently she helps other victims of rape, sexual assault, and crime. She also founded "Holly's House" in her native Evansville, Indiana to benefit those victims of rape, sexual assault, and crime as well as working closely with
      <a href="/wiki/Rape,_Abuse_%26_Incest_National_Network" title="Rape, Abuse &amp; Incest National Network">
       RAINN
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      8. October 4, 1998,
      <a href="/wiki/Hughes_Springs,_Texas" title="Hughes Springs, Texas">
       Hughes Springs, Texas
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       Leafie Mason
      </b>
      , 81 years old. She was hammered to death with an antique flat iron by Resndiz, who entered through a window. Fifty yards outside her door was the Kansas City-Southern Rail line.
     </p>
     <p>
      9. December 17, 1998,
      <a href="/wiki/West_University_Place,_Texas" title="West University Place, Texas">
       West University Place, Texas
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       Claudia Benton
      </b>
      , 39 years old. Benton, a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pediatric" title="Pediatric">
       pediatric
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neurologist" title="Neurologist">
       neurologist
      </a>
      at the
      <a href="/wiki/Baylor_College_of_Medicine" title="Baylor College of Medicine">
       Baylor College of Medicine
      </a>
      , was raped, stabbed, and bludgeoned repeatedly after he entered her home, which is near the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_Pacific" title="Union Pacific">
       Union Pacific
      </a>
      railroad tracks. Police found her Jeep Cherokee in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Antonio,_Texas" title="San Antonio, Texas">
       San Antonio
      </a>
      and found Resndiz's fingerprints on the steering column. After the murder, Resndiz had a warrant for his arrest for burglary, but not yet for murder.
     </p>
     <p>
      10 and 11. May 2, 1999,
      <a href="/wiki/Weimar,_Texas" title="Weimar, Texas">
       Weimar, Texas
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       Norman J. Sirnic
      </b>
      , 46 years old, and
      <b>
       Karen Sirnic
      </b>
      , 47 years old. The Sirnics were bludgeoned to death by a sledgehammer in a parsonage of the United Church of Christ, where Norman Sirnic was a pastor. The building was located adjacent to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_Pacific" title="Union Pacific">
       Union Pacific
      </a>
      railroad. The Sirnics' red
      <a href="/wiki/Mazda" title="Mazda">
       Mazda
      </a>
      was also found in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Antonio,_Texas" title="San Antonio, Texas">
       San Antonio
      </a>
      three weeks later, and fingerprints link their case with the Claudia Benton case.
     </p>
     <p>
      12. June 4, 1999,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">
       Houston, Texas
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       Noemi Dominguez
      </b>
      , 26 years old. Dominguez, a schoolteacher at
      <a href="/wiki/Houston_Independent_School_District" title="Houston Independent School District">
       Houston Independent School District
      </a>
      's Benjamin Franklin Elementary School, was bludgeoned to death with a pickaxe in her apartment near the rail tracks. Seven days later, her white
      <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">
       Honda
      </a>
      Civic was discovered by state troopers on the International Bridge in
      <a href="/wiki/Del_Rio,_Texas" title="Del Rio, Texas">
       Del Rio, Texas
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      13. June 4, 1999,
      <a href="/wiki/Schulenburg,_Texas" title="Schulenburg, Texas">
       Fayette County
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
       Texas
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       Josephine Konvicka
      </b>
      , 73 years old. Konvicka was killed by a blow of the same pickaxe used to kill Noemi Dominguez on the head while she lay sleeping. Her farmhouse is not far from Weimar. Resndiz attempted to steal the car but was unable to take it away since he could not find the car keys.
     </p>
     <p>
      14 and 15. June 15, 1999,
      <a href="/wiki/Gorham,_Illinois" title="Gorham, Illinois">
       Gorham, Illinois
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       George Morber, Sr.
      </b>
      , 80 years old, and
      <b>
       Carolyn Frederick
      </b>
      , 52 years old. Resndiz shot George Morber in the head with a shotgun and then clubbed Carolyn Frederick to death with a tire iron. Their house was located only 100 yards (90 m) away from a railroad track. Later, someone spotted a man matching Resndiz's description driving Carolyn Frederick's red pickup truck in
      <a href="/wiki/Cairo,_Illinois" title="Cairo, Illinois">
       Cairo, Illinois
      </a>
      , which is located 40 miles south of Gorham. In addition, the Jackson County Sheriff's Office found fingerprints in the Morbers' ransacked home, positively identifying Resndiz as the killer.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      16. Resndiz is suspected in the death of
      <b>
       Fannie Whitney Byers
      </b>
      , 81, who was found Dec. 10, 1998, bludgeoned to death in her
      <a href="/wiki/Carl,_Georgia" title="Carl, Georgia">
       Carl, Georgia
      </a>
      home located near CSX Transportation railroad tracks with a tire rim. A Lexington couple was charged in this
      <a href="/wiki/Barrow_County,_Georgia" title="Barrow County, Georgia">
       Barrow County
      </a>
      murder, but Resndiz admitted to an FBI agent that he killed Byers, according to authorities.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      He confessed to seven other killings as well, which he said took place in Mexico.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2011)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Arrest_and_trial">
       Arrest and trial
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81ngel_Maturino_Res%C3%A9ndiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Arrest and trial">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PolunskyUnitWestLivingstonTX.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="540" data-file-width="720" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/PolunskyUnitWestLivingstonTX.jpg/220px-PolunskyUnitWestLivingstonTX.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/PolunskyUnitWestLivingstonTX.jpg/330px-PolunskyUnitWestLivingstonTX.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/PolunskyUnitWestLivingstonTX.jpg/440px-PolunskyUnitWestLivingstonTX.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PolunskyUnitWestLivingstonTX.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Allan_B._Polunsky_Unit" title="Allan B. Polunsky Unit">
         Allan B. Polunsky Unit
        </a>
        houses the State of Texas death row for men.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The police tracked down Resndiz's sister, Manuela. She feared that her brother might kill someone else or be killed by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI">
       FBI
      </a>
      , so she agreed to help the police. A
      <a href="/wiki/Texas_Ranger_Division" title="Texas Ranger Division">
       Texas Ranger
      </a>
      , Drew Carter, accompanied by Manuela and a spiritual guide met up with Resndiz on a bridge connecting
      <a href="/wiki/El_Paso,_Texas" title="El Paso, Texas">
       El Paso, Texas
      </a>
      , with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ciudad_Ju%C3%A1rez,_Chihuahua" title="Ciudad Jurez, Chihuahua">
       Ciudad Jurez, Chihuahua
      </a>
      . Resndiz surrendered to Carter.
     </p>
     <p>
      During a court appearance, Resndiz accused Carter of lying under oath because his (Resndiz's) family was under the impression that he would be spared the death penalty; however, Resndiz's ultimate fate would be decided by the jury, not Carter.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1999, former Texas Attorney General
      <a href="/wiki/Jim_Mattox" title="Jim Mattox">
       Jim Mattox
      </a>
       wary of the controversy miring the many confessions and recantations by
      <a href="/wiki/Henry_Lee_Lucas" title="Henry Lee Lucas">
       Henry Lee Lucas
      </a>
       remarked of Resndiz that "I hope they don't start pinning on him every crime that happens near a railroad track."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Resndiz would be tried and sentenced to death for Benton's murder.
     </p>
     <p>
      He received the
      <a href="/wiki/Texas_Department_of_Criminal_Justice" title="Texas Department of Criminal Justice">
       Texas Department of Criminal Justice
      </a>
      ID#999356.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mental_health">
       Mental health
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81ngel_Maturino_Res%C3%A9ndiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mental health">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      On June 21, 2006, a Houston judge ruled that Resndiz was mentally competent to be executed. Upon hearing the judge's ruling, Resndiz said, "I don't believe in death. I know the body is going to go to waste. But me, as a person, I'm eternal. I'm going to be alive forever." He also described himself as half-man and half-
      <a href="/wiki/Angel" title="Angel">
       angel
      </a>
      and told
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychiatrists" title="Psychiatrists">
       psychiatrists
      </a>
      he couldn't be executed because he didn't believe he could die.
     </p>
     <p>
      Statements like the above have led specialists to conclude that Resndiz was not competent to be executed. In the words of a bilingual
      <a href="/wiki/Psychiatrist" title="Psychiatrist">
       psychiatrist
      </a>
      who evaluated Resndiz on two occasions in 2006, delusions had completely taken over [Resndizs] thought processes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Death">
       Death
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81ngel_Maturino_Res%C3%A9ndiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HuntsvilleUnitHuntsvilleTX.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="508" data-file-width="704" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/HuntsvilleUnitHuntsvilleTX.jpg/220px-HuntsvilleUnitHuntsvilleTX.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/HuntsvilleUnitHuntsvilleTX.jpg/330px-HuntsvilleUnitHuntsvilleTX.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/HuntsvilleUnitHuntsvilleTX.jpg/440px-HuntsvilleUnitHuntsvilleTX.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HuntsvilleUnitHuntsvilleTX.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Huntsville_Unit" title="Huntsville Unit">
         Huntsville Unit
        </a>
        , where Resndiz died.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Despite an appeal pending with the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/5th_U.S._Circuit_Court_of_Appeals" title="5th U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals">
       5th U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals
      </a>
      , Resndiz had the death warrant signed for the murder of Claudia Benton. He was housed in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polunsky_Unit" title="Polunsky Unit">
       Polunsky Unit
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/West_Livingston,_Texas" title="West Livingston, Texas">
       West Livingston, Texas
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      He was executed in the
      <a href="/wiki/Huntsville_Unit" title="Huntsville Unit">
       Huntsville Unit
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Huntsville,_Texas" title="Huntsville, Texas">
       Huntsville, Texas
      </a>
      , on June 27, 2006, by
      <a href="/wiki/Lethal_injection" title="Lethal injection">
       lethal injection
      </a>
      . In his final statement, Resndiz said "I want to ask if it is in your heart to forgive me. You don't have to. I know I allowed the Devil to rule my life. I just ask you to forgive me and ask the Lord to forgive me for allowing the devil to deceive me. I thank God for having patience in me. I don't deserve to cause you pain. You do not deserve this. I deserve what I am getting." Resndiz was pronounced dead at 8:05p.m.
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Time_zone" title="Central Time zone">
       CDT
      </a>
      (01:05
      <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">
       UTC
      </a>
      on June 28, 2006).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Claudia Benton's husband George was present at the execution and said Resndiz was "evil contained in human form, a creature without a soul, no conscience, no sense of remorse, no regard for the sanctity of human life."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Media">
       Media
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81ngel_Maturino_Res%C3%A9ndiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Media">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Resndiz case was featured in four criminal documentaries:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <i>
        Crime Stories
       </i>
       on the
       <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">
        Discovery Channel
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Infamous Murders
       </i>
       , "Death in the Country", on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="History (U.S. TV channel)">
        The History Channel
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Murder She Solved: True Crime
       </i>
       , "Episode 13: Railway Killer", on
       <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey_Network_(Canadian_TV_channel)" title="Oprah Winfrey Network (Canadian TV channel)">
        Oprah Winfrey Network (Canadian TV channel)
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        The FBI Files
       </i>
       , "Tracks of a Killer", on the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biography_Channel" title="Biography Channel">
        Biography Channel
       </a>
       (2003)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Resndiz was the focus of the December 11, 2010, episode of
      <i>
       48 Hours Mystery
      </i>
      (CBS), "Live to Tell: The Railroad Killer", in which Holly Dunn shared the story of her attack and the murder of Christopher Maier. That incident was also shown on the television show "Dates from Hell".
     </p>
     <p>
      One episode of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds" title="Criminal Minds">
        Criminal Minds
       </a>
      </i>
      , "Catching Out", featured a serial killer named Armando Salinas, who appears to have been based on Resndiz. Like Resndiz, he was a Hispanic drifter who traveled by railroad and killed most of his victims by bludgeoning them.
     </p>
