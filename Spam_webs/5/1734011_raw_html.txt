         <div class="tituloslide2">
          <h2>
           Top 10 Trends of Spring/Summer 2015
          </h2>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="catedate">
         <div class="categ">
          <ul class="post-categories">
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/category/fashion-trends/" rel="category tag">
             Fashion Trends
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="spantime2">
          October 14, 2014
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
       <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="7867170" data-link="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/fashion-trends/top-10-trends-of-spring-summer-2015/" data-summary="" data-title="Top 10 Trends of Spring/Summer 2015">
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        With hundreds of shows across four cities, showing amazing looks of new fashion ideas from spring and summer 2015 collections, the Fashion Weeks its over.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Fashion Design Weeks show to you the biggest impact on the runways in New York, London, Paris, and Milan.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Whether youre looking for pointers for your working wardrobe, or desperately seeking ways to up your festival-girl game next spring, heres everything youll need to know about the look of the upcoming season.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Bohemian Evening
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Heading out into the night just got a lot more romantic thanks to the likes of Valentino, Dries Van Noten, and Vera Wang. Even designer Joseph Altuzarra loosened up his signature curvilinear silhouette for a softer, diaphanous look.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Bohemian-Evening.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Bohemian-Evening" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2360" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Bohemian-Evening.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Trouser Suit
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Forget everything you know about traditional power dressing, the new trouser suit comes with whimsical touches, like the twine and cowbell belts at Cline or shimmering pinstripes at Chanel. You can leave the shoulder pads at home, but this season a cropped trouser is essential.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Trouser-Suit.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Trouser-Suit" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2369" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Trouser-Suit.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Gingham
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Referenced on both sides of the pond, the gridlock pattern has a history that includes American barn dance and French bombshell Brigitte Bardot, who famously wore the tablecloth fabric on her wedding day. This season gingham covered everything from baja minidresses to mink fur coats.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Gingham.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Gingham" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2364" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Gingham.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Suede
        </strong>
        <br/>
        With a vaguely seventies mood in the air, the look of suede was bound to make an appearance on the runways. Trench coats, A-line skirts, and classic boot-cut pants came with a soft touch.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Trouser-Suede.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Trouser-Suede" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2368" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Trouser-Suede.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Bride Wore
        </strong>
        <br/>
        For anyone in the midst of wedding planning, the spring 2015 collections were brimming with fresh and new bridal ideas. The look of white lace was reimagined in beautiful ways, from thigh-grazing minidresses at Louis Vuitton and Chlo to a modern spin on Victoriana at Erdem.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Bride-Wore.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Bride-Wore" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2361" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Bride-Wore.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Denim
        </strong>
        <br/>
        The most American of textiles in more iterations (Dresses! Coats! Pants! Jumpsuits!) than you can shake a leg at; from dark wash to lace-up, bedazzled and moreits jean genius.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Denim.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Denim" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2362" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Denim.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         No Wallflowers
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Never has the concept of florals for spring felt fresher: Big and beautiful or minute and abstract, this season, flower power is boldly feminine and here to stay.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-No-Wallflowers.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-No-Wallflowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2366" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-No-Wallflowers.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         The New Festival Girl
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Give your cutoff denim shorts a rest. From Saint Laurent to Gucci, Pucci, and Dior, the festival-bound next summer will have something new to celebrate: Stretches of printed chiffon and stolen from my boyfriends band jackets, lacy baby-doll dresses, relaxed-fit print trousers, and long flowing vests that practically guarantee your backstage access.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-The-New-Festival-Girl.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-The-New-Festival-Girl" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2367" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-The-New-Festival-Girl.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Midriff Baring Evening
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Who needs a gown? A crop top and ladylike skirt will do the trickand never has a stretch of skin felt so revolutionary. Waist-whittling proportions and only the subtlest flash of flesh turn a tried-and-true evening option into something decidedly modern.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Midriff-Baring-Evening.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Midriff-Baring-Evening" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2365" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Midriff-Baring-Evening.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Fringe
        </strong>
        <br/>
        A little shimmy, a little shake: This spring is all about movementstart with the runways ubiquitous flapping, swinging fringe. Go get your Josephine Baker on.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <a href="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Fringe.jpg">
         <img alt="Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Fringe" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2363" height="345" src="http://www.fashiondesignweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Top-10-Trends-of-Spring-2015-Fringe.jpg" width="690"/>
        </a>
       </p>
