     <div class="no-border" id="the_content">
      <div class="fold_content " style="display:block;">
       <p>
        If youve used Blender for any length of time, youve probably heard of the Blender Addons: plugins that allow you to do very specific things.
       </p>
       <p>
        And since the advent of Blender 2.5, there have been an avalanche of addons that cater to all different needs and requirements  some
        <a href="http://youtu.be/3WGQ6_T6HRA" target="_blank">
         pointlessly hilarious
        </a>
        and some that are immensely useful. In this article well be exploring the latter.
       </p>
       <p>
        Here are my most used addons:
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         #1: Import Images as Planes
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Authors:
         </strong>
         Florian Mayer (tstscr), mont29, matali
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Documentation:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Extensions:2.6/Py/Scripts/Add_Mesh/Planes_from_Images" target="_blank">
          http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Extensions:2.6/Py/Scripts/Add_Mesh/Planes_from_Images
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h3>
        What It Does:
       </h3>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Eliminates stretching when applyingimagesto planes
        </li>
        <li>
         Creates the material node setups for you
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        This is probably the most useful of all addons in my arsenal. Prior to this addon, adding planes with textures wasfrustrating, because unless the image was exactly 1:1 in ratio, the image wouldnt fit the plane perfectly. So you had to scale the plane manually. But this addon cuts out all the hard work for you.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_1.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_1" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16717" height="336" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_1.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Once the Addon is enabled, goto the Add Menu, choose the
        <em>
         Image as Planes
        </em>
        entry:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_2.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16718" height="553" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_2.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Select the image you wish to import along with the type of material (left bar):
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_3.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_3" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16719" height="798" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_3.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        And voila! The material has been added for you, and the plane matched to the exact dimensions of the image:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_4.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_4" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16720" height="795" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_4.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h2>
       </h2>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         #2: Amaranth Toolset
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Authors:
         </strong>
         Pablo Vasquez, Bassam Kurdali, Sergey Sharybin
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Documentation:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://pablovazquez.org/amaranth/" target="_blank">
          http://pablovazquez.org/amaranth/
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h3>
        What it Does:
       </h3>
       <p>
        Provides a host of powerful functions that make using Blender less painful
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_5.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_5" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16721" height="410" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_5.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Amaranth has too many functions to name here(
        <a href="http://pablovazquez.org/amaranth/" target="_blank">
         see the full list
        </a>
        ), but theseare my favourites:
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         1. Display Only Render Hotkey (ALT-SHIFT-Z)
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Hides all those mess grids, empties and parent lines at the press of a button!
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_6.gif">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_6" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16722" height="617" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_6.gif" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h3>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <strong>
         2. Camera as Render Border (Object Mode &gt; W)
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        In renderview mode looking through the camera, you can tell Blender to only render whats within the borders of the camera. That way your computer doesnt slow to a holt trying to render things that arent even in view!
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_7.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_7" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16723" height="619" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_7.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         3. Render Size Preview
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        When rendering at a percentage of the normal size (or using borders)Amaranth will tell you exact dimensions of the resulting image:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_8.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_8" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16724" height="798" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_8.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h2>
       </h2>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         #3: Edit Linked Library
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Authors:
         </strong>
         Jason Van Gumster (fweeb), Bassam Kurdali, Pablo Vasquez
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Documentation:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Extensions:2.6/Py/Scripts/Object/Edit_Linked_Library" target="_blank">
          http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Extensions:2.6/Py/Scripts/Object/Edit_Linked_Library
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <strong>
         What it Does:
        </strong>
       </h3>
       <p>
        Allows you to select a linked object and edit the original blend file at the click of a button.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_9.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_9" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16725" height="226" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_9.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Example,heres a linked chair that we want to edit. Instead of searching your harddrive for the .blend, this addon puts a button in the toolbarthat will open the original .blend:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_10.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_10" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16726" height="571" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_10.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Then once you finished editing, you can simply hit
        <em>
         Return to Original File
        </em>
        to go back. A huge timesaver!
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_11.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_11" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16727" height="572" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_11.png" width="580"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         #4: Node Wrangler
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Authors:
         </strong>
         Bartek Skorupa, Greg Zaal
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Documentation:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Extensions:2.6/Py/Scripts/Nodes/Nodes_Efficiency_Tools" target="_blank">
          http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Extensions:2.6/Py/Scripts/Nodes/Nodes_Efficiency_Tools
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <em>
         <strong>
          What it Does:
         </strong>
        </em>
       </h3>
       <p>
        Adds a bunchof timesaving functions for the node editor.
       </p>
       <p>
        Similarto the Amaranth Toolset, but focused on the node editor. It allows you to quickly; combinenodes, preview material setups, copy settings, swap inputs and much much more.
        <a href="http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Extensions:2.6/Py/Scripts/Nodes/Nodes_Efficiency_Tools" target="_blank">
         See the full list of features
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_12.png">
         <img alt="must_have_blender_addons_12" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16728" height="363" onerror="javascript: wp_broken_images = window.wp_broken_images || function(){}; wp_broken_images(this);" src="http://qgv6y6drlr2sy3es4blouyvd9.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/08/must_have_blender_addons_12.png" width="580"/>
        </a>

         #5: Auto Render Tile Size
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>

         Greg Zaal
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Documentation:
          <a href="http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Extensions:2.6/Py/Scripts/Render/Auto_Tile_Size" target="_blank">
           wiki.blender.org/index.php/Extensions:2.6/Py/Scripts/Render/Auto_Tile_Size
          </a>
         </strong>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          What it Does:
         </em>

         which is important for render times

        ), depending on whether youve selected GPU, CPU or Border Rendering.
