                     <img alt="This photo shows the backside of the granny squares connected with a single crochet stitch." class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/51117916e4b0a36262d8e0d0/t/5484b6c8e4b0e74f06543baf/1417983689147/Mini+granny+square+tech+case+tutorial.+SugarBeans.org" data-image-dimensions="600x659" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="5484b6c8e4b0e74f06543baf" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/51117916e4b0a36262d8e0d0/t/5484b6c8e4b0e74f06543baf/1417983689147/Mini+granny+square+tech+case+tutorial.+SugarBeans.org" data-type="image"/>

                    <div class="image-caption-wrapper">
                     <div class="image-caption">
                      <p>
                       This photo shows the backside of the granny squares connected with a single crochet stitch.
                      </p>
                     </div>
                    </div>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1417983013276_35473">
                 <div class="sqs-block-content">
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    Tutorial:
                   </strong>
                  </p>
                  <ol dir="ltr">
                   <li>
                    Make a two round mini granny square.
                   </li>
                   <li>
                    Connect the mini squares in a three row pattern using a single crochet stitch.
                   </li>
                   <li>
                    Continue connecting rows of three until 24 granny squares are in rows.
                   </li>
                   <li>
                    Match up 2 rows of granny squares and connect to begin making a longer column of grannys.
                   </li>
                  </ol>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1417983013276_42877">
                 <div class="sqs-block-content">
                  <div class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-below ">
                   <div class="intrinsic" style="max-width:600.0px;">
                    <div class="image-block-wrapper has-aspect-ratio" data-description="" style="padding-bottom:104.0%;">
                     <noscript>
                      <img alt="Mini granny square tech case; backs. SugarBeans.org" src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/51117916e4b0a36262d8e0d0/t/5484b850e4b096d419caca85/1417984081465/Mini+granny+square+tech+case%3B+backs.+SugarBeans.org"/>
                     </noscript>
                     <img alt="Mini granny square tech case; backs. SugarBeans.org" class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/51117916e4b0a36262d8e0d0/t/5484b850e4b096d419caca85/1417984081465/Mini+granny+square+tech+case%3B+backs.+SugarBeans.org" data-image-dimensions="600x624" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="5484b850e4b096d419caca85" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/51117916e4b0a36262d8e0d0/t/5484b850e4b096d419caca85/1417984081465/Mini+granny+square+tech+case%3B+backs.+SugarBeans.org" data-type="image"/>
                    </div>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1417983013276_46170">
                 <div class="sqs-block-content">
                  <p>
                   5. Continue in this manner until all 24 granny squares are connected in a long piece of cotton goodness.
                   <br/>
                   6. Weave in the ends.
                   <br/>
                   7. Using a single crochet stitch, close up the case around perimeter with a chosen color of cotton yarn making a flap and one row of squares overlapping at the top. {Photos above with iPad or Kindle in case show where the fold and flap should be crocheted shut}.
                   <br/>
                   8. Add scallop edging around two sides of case. ( 1 sc, *skip 2 sp, 5 dc in third stitch, 1 sc, repeat from *).
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_3_1417983013276_23164">

                  <div class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-below ">
                   <div class="intrinsic" style="max-width:600.0px;">

                     </noscript>

                    </div>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>

                 <div class="sqs-block-content">
                  <p>
                   When I got to the top of the flap, I single crocheted 12 stitches to make the loop for the button and reconnected with a slip stitch and continued the single crochet around the edge.I finally got to use my SugarBeans wood button I purchased from
                   <a href="http://www.pickyourplum.com" target="_blank">
                    Pick Your Plum
                   </a>
                   a while ago.
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>

                   <div class="intrinsic" style="max-width:600.0px;">

                     </noscript>
                     <img alt="Mini Granny square tech case with scallop edging.                  <div class="sqs-block-content">
                  <p>
                   I'm sure there will be more mini granny square projects in my future; these cute squaresgive off a vintage vibe and the color combinations just make me giddy. This tech case could also work as a sweet pouch for small gifts this Christmas season; maybe even a stationary case for your writing friends. Whatever the purpose, it sure is a little piece of 'pretty'.
                  </p>
