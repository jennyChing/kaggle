       <h1>
        So you think you know SaaS? 19 must-see resources for getting to grips with Software as a Service
       </h1>
       <div style="text-align: justify;">
        <p>
         63% of organisations now adopt Software as a Service (SaaS) in their daily operations as migration to the cloud continues to dominate IT trends. In fact, with Gartner predicting that worldwide SaaS application revenue will reach $22.1bn by 2015, its clear that SaaS-based solutions wont be going away anytime soon.
        </p>
        <p>
         Weve collated some of the best portals of information from all over the web to create this comprehensive list of SaaS sources.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          SaaS Books
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="https://www.facebook.com/thesaasedge" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          The SaaS Edge Book
         </a>
         , by Sahil Parikh
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         In the first non-technical book about the SaaS phenomenon, Parikh writes first and foremost for the business owner looking to employ a SaaS model in the workplace.
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         The book is a practical guide to how businesses can harness the power of SaaS, Web 2.0 and on-demand computing, and use this to lower operational costs whilst increasing efficiency and productivity.
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="http://www.softletter.com/Resources/SaaSEntrepreneurTheBook.aspx" rel="nofollow">
          SaaS Entrepreneur: The Denitive Guide to Succeeding in Your Cloud Application Business
         </a>
         , by Merril R. Chapman
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         This is a comprehensive resource for budding tycoons looking to take advantage of the evolving computing industry. Anybody looking for a practical and modular guide to making a success of their SaaS start-up will really see a lot of benefit from this volume.
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="http://www.rockynook.com/book/312/testing-cloud-services.html" rel="nofollow">
          Testing Cloud Services: How to test SaaS, PaaS &amp; IaaS
         </a>
         , by Kees Blokland, Jeroen Mangerink and Martin Pol
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         This one is for anybody who finds themselves with a need to implement or maintain cloud services. It is an incredibly effective and valuable guide detailing the potential risks involved in deploying cloud solutions, including methods of testing.
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Architecture-Monetization-Transition-Operation-Improvement/dp/192152314X" rel="nofollow">
          SaaS Architecture, Adoption and Monetization of SaaS Projects
         </a>
         , by Ralph Hatch
        </p>
        <p>
         Hatchs book gives a great overall management-level guide of best practice IT service management in relation to SaaS.
        </p>
        <p>
         As the industry continues to grow and advance, many organisations will struggle to develop their framework to support the integration of SaaS solutions. This book is designed to support IT business leaders through this transition for successful deployment of cloud services.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsonormal">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          SaaS Blog Posts
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="http://tomtunguz.com/magic-numbers" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Sales Efficiency Benchmarks for SaaS Startups
         </a>
         by Tomasz Tunguz
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         Venture capitalist Tomasz Tunguz talks us through how investors gauge the value of SaaS start-ups.
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="http://www.networkworld.com/news/2012/072512-cloud-sla-261153.html" rel="nofollow">
          5 Tips for Making Your Cloud SLA Watertight
         </a>
         by Brandon Butler
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         This is an everything-you-need-to-know guide of what to consider before starting a cloud deployment with a public vendor.
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="http://www.adexchanger.com/agencies/why-one-creative-agency-is-banking-on-saas-and-big-data/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Why One Creative Agency is Banking On SaaS (And Big Data)
         </a>
         by Kelly Liyakasa
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         An interesting account of how Bowery-based creative agency, Gin Lane Media, has embraced SaaS to develop a retail business tool that will allow commerce data to be connected with mobile apps and other loyalty data.
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="http://efounders.co/infographic-saas-is-beautiful-10-key-facts-about-the-growing-saas-market/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          [Infographic] SaaS is Beautiful: 10 Key Facts About the Growing SaaS Market
         </a>
         by Clemen Vouillon
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         This insightful infographic highlights the recent and potential rise of SaaS in business IT, from humble beginnings in 2010, to the market outlook for 2015.
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="http://sixteenventures.com/sell-itself" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          SaaS Marketing Plan: 5 Ways to Get your App to Sell Itself
         </a>
         by Lincoln Murphy
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         One for developers, this post talks you through developing a SaaS marketing plan to get your product to sell itself.
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2011/11/14/cloud-iaas-paas-saas/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Cloud 101: What the heck do IaaS, PaaS and SaaS companies do?
         </a>
         by Sean Ludwig
        </p>
        <p class="ecxmsolistparagraph">
         This is a comprehensive laymans guide for anyone getting to grips with all aspects of the as-a-Service arena.
        </p>
