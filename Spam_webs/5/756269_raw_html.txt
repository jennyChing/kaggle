       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Effects of Working Night Shifts
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <a class="author" href="http://myhotelrevelation.com/author/electra5150/" rel="author">
          pete williams
         </a>
         /
         <a class="entry-date" href="http://myhotelrevelation.com/effects-of-shift-work-on-the-body/">
          August 29, 2014
         </a>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <h2 style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         <em>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <strong style="font-weight: bold;">
            The effects of working shifts on the body.
            <img alt="The Effects of Shift Work on the Body" class="alignright wp-image-1268 size-medium" height="225" src="http://myhotelrevelation.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/By-U.S.-Navy-photo-by-Lt.-Richard-Stone-Public-domain-via-Wikimedia-Commons-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
           </strong>
          </span>
         </em>
        </h2>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Working in the shift work industry can be an incredibly rewarding career, however,the one area that seems to be the hardest to adapt to for newcomers are the effects of night shift work. Among Truck Drivers, Air Traffic Controllers,Factories and Hospitals and hundreds of many other professional services there lies the world of shift work, 24 hour operations that always needs staff members working around the clock 7 days a week.
        </p>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         If youve never experienced shift work and the effects it has on the body, its quite a difficult phenomenon to explain. If youve gone through jet lag, its a bit like that, except that it lasts longer and sadly youre not on holiday. There can be something quite disconcerting about finishing your working day at 7 or 8 a.m. and eating dinner while everybody else is having breakfast. Also, it will seriously dampen your social life, not many people want to socialise and go out for a few drinks at dawn. and if they do, its probably best to steer well clear of them! Turning night into day takes a certain mindset that not everyone can adapt to.
        </p>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Aside from the social oddities, shift work brings with it the potential for health risks, while most are only temporary, in some cases these can be permanent. The breaking of our natural Circadian rhythm has been shown to have a detrimental effect on heath.
        </p>
        <ul style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         <li>
          <h3>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              Suppression of Melatonin production.
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </h3>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Melatonin is a hormone which is produced in the body during the natural night hours but is suppressed in workers who spend too long under artificial light and limit their exposure to daylight. It regulates our sleep-wake cycle. Melatonin is a powerful antioxidant in our system that also interacts with our immune system and has been shown to be a powerful aid in our medical wellbeing, when we limit its production we are putting ourselves at risk.
        </p>
        <ul style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         <li>
          <h3>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              Increased weight gain and obesity.
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </h3>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Numerous studies have shown that shift workers are at risk from type 2 diabetes and obesity. It is thought that this is due to the disruption of the circadian rhythm(Natural Heart rate patterns) altering insulin production in the body.
        </p>
        <ul style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         <li>
          <h3>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              Breast cancer
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </h3>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         A 2007 study showed that women whose working pattern involved night shifts had a 50% greater risk of breast cancer than those who did not. The most likely explanation is thought to be the disruption of melatonin production due to broken Circadian rhythm. The same year, the World Health Organisation listed shiftwork that involves circadian disruption as a probable carcinogen.
        </p>
        <ul style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         <li>
          <h3>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              Shift work sleep disorder (SWSD)
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </h3>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Shift work sleep disorder is characterised by insomnia, excessive fatigue and sleepiness. Even after shift work has ended, symptoms can remain until treatment has been sought.
        </p>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Shift work really can have a huge impact on your health, however there are some steps you can take to help control these and make those
         <a href="http://myhotelrevelation.com/night-porter-blues/" target="_blank">
          night shifts
         </a>
         easier to manage.
        </p>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Due to the huge amount of professions where shift work is essential, many tried and tested solutions have been invented to help workers cope with sleep disruption from keeping unsociable hours.
        </p>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333; text-align: center;">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            Here are a few handy tips to consider.
           </strong>
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         <li>
          <h3>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              Caffeine is not really your friend!
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </h3>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Most normal peoples first thought at having to work through the night is to swig back oceans of coffee. Seasoned shift workers on the other hand will tell you that you are just delaying the inevitable and
         <strong>
          setting yourself up for a big fall.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         <li>
          <h3>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              Ease yourself into your new schedule.
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </h3>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         If you have the chance,
         <em>
          <strong>
           ease yourself into your new schedule
          </strong>
         </em>
         gradually over the preceding few days before your night shift begins. It will make that first night all the more easier and set you up for an easier week
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <h3>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              Get your 3 Meals
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </h3>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Even though your breakfast is at lets say for example 6p.m and your dinner at 6.am make sure you
         <em>
          <strong>
           eat 3 meals a day
          </strong>
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          <strong>
           never skip
          </strong>
         </em>
         your
         <em>
          <strong>
            lunch
          </strong>
         </em>
         in between as this will help maintain blood sugar levels throughout your day 
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <h3>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              Make your sleeping environment healthy
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </h3>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         Keep your
         <em>
          <strong>
           bedroom darkened
          </strong>
         </em>
         and as
         <em>
          <strong>
           quiet
          </strong>
         </em>
         as possible, consider using an eye mask or putting in some ear plugs,softening out any unwanted distractions will ensure you get some
         <em>
          <strong>
           good quality
          </strong>
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          <strong>
           useful
          </strong>
         </em>
         slumber time. Drink plenty of water to hydrate yourself and take a glass of it to your bedside.
        </p>
        <p style="font-weight: normal; color: #333333;">
         If you put in a little effort and can manage to be strict and stick to your schedule then working in shift work can become more healthier and that little more easier to cope with.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.wealthyaffiliate.com?a_aid=49da8576">
