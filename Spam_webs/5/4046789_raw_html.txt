      <h4>
       <a href="/category/superhead-tags/shiva-shuffled-his-feet">
        Shiva Shuffled His Feet
       </a>
      </h4>
      <h2>
       Crash Test Yogi
      </h2>
      <dl class="image medium_left">
       <dt>
        <img alt="" class="imagecache imagecache-200w" src="http://blog.socialworkout.com/sites/default/files/imagecache/200w//sites/default/files/images/crash%20test%20dummy.jpg" title=""/>
       </dt>
       <dd>
        Crash Test Dummy
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <span class="print-link">
      </span>
      <p>
       There is a moment I look forward to every Monday night...it's the moment when yoga class starts and everyone gets all chanty together and the sound of deep connection fills the void in my life...err, space in the room.
      </p>
      <p>
       Last week the instructor,
       <a href="http://www.susannaharwoodrubin.com/" target="_blank">
        Susanna Harwood Rubin
       </a>
       , briefly mentioned that there would be live musicalaccompaniment, which I didn't think about again until I walked into class at this week and was greeted by Brad, a shaggy haired, vaguely familiar looking guy holding a guitar.
      </p>
      <p>
       Well, when the chanting started I knew instantly why the familiarity. THAT voice. Unmistakeable: Brad is Brad Roberts, the lead singer of the
       <a href="http://crashtestdummies.com/">
        Crash Test Dummies
       </a>
       . He's the guy who had me singing "
       <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9q91_kcx3no">
        Mmm Mmm Mmm Mmm
       </a>
       " for most of sixth grade. Holy shit!
      </p>
      <p>
       Now, weirdly enough, chanting with Brad proved problematic, (and I tell you this because you too can chant with Brad). While I should have been thinking "I'm one with my inner somethingorother," I was first thinking "THIS IS SO COOL." And then I got annoyed. Brad's voice is way lower than anyone else'sin the history of my life, and while I knew that my meagersopranocould not rival his deep bass, the fact that I couldn't match his pitch really started to piss me off. I decided to try and harmonize, which was really just awkward, and then I started to feel like I had been placed in a yogic spin-off of
       <em>
        Glee
       </em>
       . Not only was Brad making me feel like a sub par vocalist, he was chanting at a faster speed than I was used to. Frankly, Brad was ruining my happy place.
      </p>
      <p>
       Once chanting subsided and class began, however, Brad quieted his god-like vocal cords and picked politely at his guitar, providing pretty music to back-bend to  and then all was well with my feelings about Brad.
      </p>
      <p>
       A final 'Om' ended the class, and I was able to look past the harmonic complexities, and focus on how friggin' awesome it was to be chanted to by a Crash Test Dummy. It helped that I had concluded that no one could hear my awkward attempts and chant-harmonizing over Brad's super human volume.
      </p>
      <p>
       Cool, right? If you want in on some Brad Roberts' chanting action, and are slightly less competitive with your Om-ing pitch than I, he's teaching a Chant Mantra course at
       <a href="http://virayoga.com/workshopsevents-annex.htm">
        Virayoga
       </a>
       on April 13th from 8-9:30pm. For $20 it's probably way cheaper than seeing him in concert.
      </p>
