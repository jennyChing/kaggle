     <p>
      <b>
       JWH-210
      </b>
      is an
      <a href="/wiki/Analgesic" title="Analgesic">
       analgesic
      </a>
      chemical from the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naphthoylindole" title="Naphthoylindole">
       naphthoylindole
      </a>
      family, which acts as a potent
      <a href="/wiki/Cannabinoid" title="Cannabinoid">
       cannabinoid
      </a>
      agonist at both the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cannabinoid_receptor_1" title="Cannabinoid receptor 1">
       CB
       <sub>
        1
       </sub>
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cannabinoid_receptor_2_(macrophage)" title="Cannabinoid receptor 2 (macrophage)">
       CB
       <sub>
        2
       </sub>
      </a>
      receptors, with K
      <sub>
       i
      </sub>
      values of 0.46nM at CB
      <sub>
       1
      </sub>
      and 0.69nM at CB
      <sub>
       2
      </sub>
      . It is one of the most potent 4-substituted naphthoyl derivatives in the naphthoylindole series, having a higher
      <a href="/wiki/Dissociation_constant#Protein-ligand_binding" title="Dissociation constant">
       binding affinity
      </a>
      (i.e. lower K
      <sub>
       i
      </sub>
      ) at CB
      <sub>
       1
      </sub>
      than both its 4-methyl and 4-
      <i>
       n
      </i>
      -propyl
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homolog_(chemistry)" title="Homolog (chemistry)">
       homologues
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/JWH-122" title="JWH-122">
       JWH-122
      </a>
      (CB
      <sub>
       1
      </sub>
      K
      <sub>
       i
      </sub>
      0.69nM) and
      <b>
       JWH-182
      </b>
      (CB
      <sub>
       1
      </sub>
      K
      <sub>
       i
      </sub>
      0.65nM) respectively, and than the 4-methoxy compound
      <a href="/wiki/JWH-081" title="JWH-081">
       JWH-081
      </a>
      (CB
      <sub>
       1
      </sub>
      K
      <sub>
       i
      </sub>
      1.2nM).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was discovered by and named after Dr.
      <a href="/wiki/John_W._Huffman" title="John W. Huffman">
       John W. Huffman
      </a>
      . JWH-210 and JWH-122 were banned in Sweden on 1 October 2010 as hazardous goods harmful to health, after being identified as ingredients in "herbal"
      <a href="/wiki/Synthetic_cannabis" title="Synthetic cannabis">
       synthetic cannabis
      </a>
      products.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The substances JWH-210, JWH-122 and
      <a href="/wiki/JWH-203" title="JWH-203">
       JWH-203
      </a>
      were classified as illegal drugs by the Swedish government as of 1 September 2011.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
