        <a href="http://honestlywtf.com/diy/diy-moleskine-scrapbook/" rel="bookmark">
         <h2>
          DIY Moleskine Scrapbook
         </h2>
        </a>
        <div class="post-author">
         by
         <a href="http://honestlywtf.com/author/lauren/" rel="author" title="Posts by Lauren">
          Lauren
         </a>
         in
         <a href="http://honestlywtf.com/category/diy/" rel="category tag">
          D.I.Y.
         </a>
        </div>
       </header>
       <div class="post-content clearfix">
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Erica and I never leave home without a
         <a href="http://www.moleskineus.com/moleskine-books.html">
          Moleskine
         </a>
         notebook(we still like to take notes the old fashioned way with a notebook and pen)! So when our friend Karen (of
         <em>
          Lulu Loves Caleb)
         </em>
         showed us her fabric embellished Moleskine Cahier Notebook, we jumped at the opportunity to have her show us how she did it. Youll need a sewing machine, a kraft brown
         <a href="http://www.moleskineus.com/moleskine-cahier-notebooks.html">
          Moleskin Cahier Notebook
         </a>
         of any size, a piece of fabric, a glue stick and a pair of scissors.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://cdn.honestlywtf.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/mokeskin11.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4818" height="390" src="http://cdn.honestlywtf.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/mokeskin11.jpg" title="mokeskin1" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <span id="more-4812">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Start by cutting down your fabric to a size that is small enough to fit on your notebook. We cut a 2.5 x 5 rectangle for the pocket sized book and 3 x 8 rectangle for the larger book. Glue your fabric to the front cover of your notebook. Start stitching alongthe edge of thefabric, leaving a small seam.Leaving your needle in the down position, turn your notebook 90 degrees and continue these steps until all four sides are stitched. Knot the ends and trim any excess threads.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.honestlywtf.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/moleskin21.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4819" height="247" src="http://cdn.honestlywtf.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/moleskin21-1024x383.jpg" title="moleskin2" width="662"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Its possibleyou wont be able to stop yourself from sewing fabric to paperits
         <em>
          that
         </em>
         addicting. The possibilities are endless and you really cant go wrong with this project. We love the raw edges but of course, you canfold your edges under before sewing. You can also try experimenting with patchwork, using more than one fabric.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://cdn.honestlywtf.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/moleskin31.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4820" height="378" src="http://cdn.honestlywtf.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/moleskin31.jpg" title="moleskin3" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">

         </em>
        </p>
