       <h2>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Wings_of_Liberty">
         Wings of Liberty
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/starcraft2/index.php?title=INnoVation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Wings of Liberty">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        INnoVation played his first official StarCraft II match during the hybrid
        <a href="/starcraft2/2011-2012_Proleague_Season_2" title="2011-2012 Proleague Season 2">
         2011-2012 Proleague Season 2
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h3>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="OSL">
         OSL
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/starcraft2/index.php?title=INnoVation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: OSL">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        On July 19, 2012, INnoVation participated in the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2012_OnGameNet_Starleague_Season_1/Preliminaries/KeSPA" title="2012 OnGameNet Starleague Season 1/Preliminaries/KeSPA">
         2012 OSL KeSPA Preliminaries
        </a>
        . INnoVation received a BYE in round one and then took down
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Sang" title="Sang">
         Sang
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/HerO(jOin)" title="HerO(jOin)">
         herO
        </a>
        , and faced
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Crazy-Hydra" title="Crazy-Hydra">
         Crazy-Hydra
        </a>
        in the finals. After taking down
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Crazy-Hydra" title="Crazy-Hydra">
         Crazy-Hydra
        </a>
        by a score of 2:0, INnoVation advanced in to the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2012_OnGameNet_Starleague_Season_1/Dual_Tournament/KeSPA" title="2012 OnGameNet Starleague Season 1/Dual Tournament/KeSPA">
         2012 OSL KeSPA Dual Tournament
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h3>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="GSL">
         GSL
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/starcraft2/index.php?title=INnoVation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: GSL">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        In the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2012_Global_StarCraft_II_League_Season_4" title="2012 Global StarCraft II League Season 4">
         2012 GSL Season 4
        </a>
        , INnoVation qualified for Code A after going through the preliminaries. There, he beat the king of Code A,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/YuGiOh" title="YuGiOh">
         YuGiOh
        </a>
        , in the first round with a score of 2-1, and then followed it up with a 2-0 win over
        <a href="/starcraft2/Bomber" title="Bomber">
         Bomber
        </a>
        . After beating
        <a href="/starcraft2/Happy_(Korean_player)" title="Happy (Korean player)">
         Happy
        </a>
        in the round of 24 with a score of 2-1, INnoVation made it into Code S for
        <a href="/starcraft2/2012_Global_StarCraft_II_League_Season_5" title="2012 Global StarCraft II League Season 5">
         the next season
        </a>
        . There, his round of 32 group consisted of
        <a href="/starcraft2/Leenock" title="Leenock">
         Leenock
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/KeeN" title="KeeN">
         KeeN
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/starcraft2/Squirtle" title="Squirtle">
         Squirtle
        </a>
        . INnoVation first played
        <a href="/starcraft2/Leenock" title="Leenock">
         Leenock
        </a>
        , losing 2-0, then he beat both
        <a href="/starcraft2/Squirtle" title="Squirtle">
         Squirtle
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/starcraft2/KeeN" title="KeeN">
         KeeN
        </a>
        2-0, which placed him 2nd and gave him a spot in the round of 16. In his Ro16 group, he was placed with an even harder group, that had
        <a href="/starcraft2/Polt" title="Polt">
         Polt
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/Sniper" title="Sniper">
         Sniper
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/starcraft2/PartinG" title="PartinG">
         PartinG
        </a>
        . He placed top in his group this time, going 2-0 on matches, and dropping only one map to
        <a href="/starcraft2/Sniper" title="Sniper">
         Sniper
        </a>
        . This secured him a spot in next seasons Code S, and advanced him to the next round. In the round of 8 he was against fellow KeSPA player,
        <a href="/starcraft2/Soulkey" title="Soulkey">
         Soulkey
        </a>
        , who he beat with a very solid 3-0. In the semifinals he played against
        <a href="/starcraft2/HyuN" title="HyuN">
         HyuN
        </a>
        , in which he lost 1-3 and was knocked out of the tournament.
       </p>
       <p>
        With his semi-finals finish in the last GSL season of 2012, INnoVation headed into the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_Global_StarCraft_II_League_Season_1" title="2013 Global StarCraft II League Season 1">
         last Wings of Liberty GSL
        </a>
        in Code S. In his round of 32 group, INnoVation took out the foreign Zerg
        <a href="/starcraft2/Stephano" title="Stephano">
         Stephano
        </a>
        , but lost to the GSL Champion
        <a href="/starcraft2/DongRaeGu" title="DongRaeGu">
         DongRaeGu
        </a>
        . After beating Stephano again, INnoVation advanced to the next round in second place. In his round of 16 group, he took out the GSL veteran
        <a href="/starcraft2/TaeJa" title="TaeJa">
         TaeJa
        </a>
        before facing DongRaeGu again. This time however, INnoVation defeated DongRaeGu 2-0 to secure first place in his group. Facing another Zerg in
        <a href="/starcraft2/Symbol" title="Symbol">
         Symbol
        </a>
        in the round of 8, INnoVation narrowly lost with a score of 2-3 to end his final Wings of Liberty GSL run.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Heart_of_the_Swarm">
         Heart of the Swarm
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/starcraft2/index.php?title=INnoVation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Heart of the Swarm">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h2>
       <h3>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Close_Finishes">
         Close Finishes
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/starcraft2/index.php?title=INnoVation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Close Finishes">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        INnoVation played in the first ever
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_MLG_Winter_Championship" title="2013 MLG Winter Championship">
         Heart of the Swarm MLG event
        </a>
        shortly after the release of the expansion. He dominated his first three matches, taking out the Canadian player
        <a href="/starcraft2/HuK" title="HuK">
         HuK
        </a>
        , the multiple MLG champion
        <a href="/starcraft2/Leenock" title="Leenock">
         Leenock
        </a>
        and his fellow KeSPA player
        <a href="/starcraft2/Rain_(Korean_Protoss)" title="Rain (Korean Protoss)">
         Rain
        </a>
        all by 3-0 scores. He met the legendary Brood War Terran
        <a href="/starcraft2/Flash" title="Flash">
         Flash
        </a>
        in the semi-finals where Flash showcased his superior TvT to take the series 3-1. Dropping down into the third place match, INnoVation played another Protoss player in
        <a href="/starcraft2/MC" title="MC">
         MC
        </a>
        , another decorated StarCraft II player. He defeated the Protoss player 2-1 which secured him a third place finish in the first official premier Heart of the Swarm tournament.
       </p>
       <p>
        On returning to Korea, INnoVation dominated the Korean scene, acting as the ace player on his team in
        <a href="/starcraft2/2012-2013_Proleague" title="2012-2013 Proleague">
         Proleague
        </a>
        and going 15-5 in his first 20 games. He also achieved success in the individual leagues as his round of 8 finish in the last Wings of Liberty GSL allowed him to participate in the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Season_1_Korea_GSL/Code_S" title="2013 WCS Season 1 Korea GSL/Code S">
         WCS Korea 2013 Season 1 Premier League
        </a>
        . Despite losing his very first series in the Round of 32 to the reigning OSL champion
        <a href="/starcraft2/Rain_(Korean_Protoss)" title="Rain (Korean Protoss)">
         Rain
        </a>
        , INnoVation easily defeated
        <a href="/starcraft2/HyuN" title="HyuN">
         HyuN
        </a>
        2-0 to face Rain again. This time he would take the victory over the Protoss player, knocking him out and securing second place in the group. In the second group stage, he was placed in what was considered the group of death with
        <a href="/starcraft2/PartinG" title="PartinG">
         PartinG
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/Life" title="Life">
         Life
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/starcraft2/Flash" title="Flash">
         Flash
        </a>
        . INnoVation took out Flash 2-1 and Life 2-0 to comfortably secure first place in his group and move onto the playoff bracket. There, he showcased impressive TvZ, taking down the defending GSL champion
        <a href="/starcraft2/RorO" title="RorO">
         RorO
        </a>
        3-0 before facing the defending GSL runner up
        <a href="/starcraft2/Symbol" title="Symbol">
         Symbol
        </a>
        . Symbol managed to take two games in the series using Roach-based all-ins, but his efforts were not enough as ultimately INnoVation managed to take the win in the series to advance to the finals against
        <a href="/starcraft2/Soulkey" title="Soulkey">
         Soulkey
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        The finals was hyped up by both fans and casters as the supposedly best Terran player and best Zerg player faced off. Going into the finals INnoVation had a dominating 21-3 record in Heart of the Swarm TvZ in both
        <a href="/starcraft2/2012-2013_Proleague" title="2012-2013 Proleague">
         Proleague
        </a>
        and WCS Korea where the only macro game he lost had been against his finals opponent
        <a href="/starcraft2/Soulkey" title="Soulkey">
         Soulkey
        </a>
        . INnoVation opened up the series with a quick win after proxying two Barracks and dealing critical economic damage to the Zerg player. He would then secure his lead and pull far ahead in the following two games after deflecting Soulkey's
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Roach" title="Roach">
         Roach
        </a>
        heavy compositions to lead the series 3-0. Soulkey fought back in the next two games however and through the use of Roach/Baneling all-ins and timings, he managed to take the momentum away from INnoVation and start making his comeback. In the sixth game, INnoVation opted to go for another proxy two Barracks push, but this time his attack was scouted by Soulkey, who defended effectively and crushed INnoVation's push. After losing his army, Soulkey busted up the Terran player's ramp, and managed to take the series to the seventh and final game. Up until this point, there were only two other GSL finals where a Terran player was up three games in the series before his opponent clawed his way back into the game to take it to the final map. In both situations, the Terran player managed to close out the series with a win. However despite having history in his favor, INnoVation was overwhelmed by Soulkey's Mutaling style, and after suffering critical economic damage could not keep up with the Zerg macro. INnoVation had been up three games in the series but he could not finish off the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Woongjin" title="Woongjin">
         Woongjin
        </a>
        Zerg, and thus lost the series and took second place in the first Heart of the Swarm GSL.
       </p>
       <h3>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Finally_a_Champion">
         Finally a Champion
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/starcraft2/index.php?title=INnoVation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Finally a Champion">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        After losing the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Season_1_Korea_GSL/Code_S" title="2013 WCS Season 1 Korea GSL/Code S">
         WCS Season 1 Korea finals
        </a>
        3-4 against Soulkey despite leading 3-0, INnoVation sought revenge at the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Season_1" title="2013 WCS Season 1">
         2013 WCS Season 1 Global Finals
        </a>
        . He was able to advance in first place from his initial group stage, still showing great form in TvZ, defeating
        <a href="/starcraft2/RorO" title="RorO">
         RorO
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/starcraft2/Revival" title="Revival">
         Revival
        </a>
        , and secured himself a spot in the quarterfinals where he would face
        <a href="/starcraft2/ALive" title="ALive">
         aLive
        </a>
        . Showing that he was capable in more than just TvZ, INnoVation swept aLive 3-0. He then went on to defeat
        <a href="/starcraft2/Mvp_(player)" title="Mvp (player)">
         Mvp
        </a>
        in a very close series 3-2 to advance to his second consecutive premier finals, where he faced off against
        <a href="/starcraft2/SOs" title="SOs">
         sOs
        </a>
        , a player who had played two very close series against INnoVation's rival Soulkey in both the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Season_1_Korea_GSL/Code_S" title="2013 WCS Season 1 Korea GSL/Code S">
         WCS 2013 Season 1 Korea
        </a>
        semifinals and
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Season_1" title="2013 WCS Season 1">
         WCS 2013 Season 1
        </a>
        semifinals. INnoVation once again started the series 3-0 without showing signs of weakness. The fourth game, however, was much more difficult, as sOs used an unusual Tempest/Templar composition to test INnoVation's ability to adapt. He was not shaken, and he closed the series 4-0 against sOs who looked visibly nervous, and finally found himself a champion on one of the biggest stages in StarCraft 2 history. His accomplishment earned him the #1 rank on TeamLiquid.net's Power Rank for July 2013.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-July_0-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-July-0">
          [1]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="2013_WCS_Season_2_.26_Beyond">
         2013 WCS Season 2 &amp; Beyond
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/starcraft2/index.php?title=INnoVation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013 WCS Season 2 &amp; Beyond">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        After his first major individual championship, INnoVation entered the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Season_2_Korea_OSL" title="2013 WCS Season 2 Korea OSL">
         2013 WCS Season 2 Korea OSL
        </a>
        looking to further prove himself. He looked strong in the round of 32, advancing 2-0 after defeating
        <a href="/starcraft2/Bbyong" title="Bbyong">
         Bbyong
        </a>
        and
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Savage" title="Savage">
         Savage
        </a>
        . He was placed in a four-Terran group in the Round of 16 along with
        <a href="/starcraft2/Flash" title="Flash">
         Flash
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/Bbyong" title="Bbyong">
         Bbyong
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/starcraft2/Bomber" title="Bomber">
         Bomber
        </a>
        , where he eventually advanced 2-1, losing to Bomber 0-2 but defeating Flash and Bbyong 2-0. He would end up facing off against
        <a href="/starcraft2/Soulkey" title="Soulkey">
         Soulkey
        </a>
        for the first time in a WCS match since
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Season_1_Korea_GSL/Code_S" title="2013 WCS Season 1 Korea GSL/Code S">
         WCS Korea 2013 Season 1
        </a>
        finals. INnoVation took out Soulkey convincingly 3-0, finally redeeming himself against his
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Woongjin" title="Woongjin">
         Woongjin
        </a>
        rival. At this point he was expected to reach the finals once again, as he was set to face off against
        <a href="/starcraft2/Maru" title="Maru">
         Maru
        </a>
        , a player that was considered to be the underdog due to his lack of notable results despite his substantial GSL experience. However, INnoVation was taken off-guard by Maru's aggressive one-base builds, which revealed his nerves and lack of composure under stress. After losing to Cloaked Banshee play in game one, INnoVation overreacted in game two by building a Raven, which was ineffective against Maru's Marine/Hellion drop. The third game saw INnoVation fall to a double proxy Barrack build when he attempted to take an expansion before building his own Barracks. Down 0-3, he exhibited poor control in the final game, which led to his being swept 0-4 by his young opponent.
       </p>
       <p>
        On August 23, 2013, INnoVation went to Gamescom in Cologne, Germany to play in the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Season_2" title="2013 WCS Season 2">
         2013 WCS Season 2 Finals
        </a>
        , where he was placed in a Round of 16 group with
        <a href="/starcraft2/TaeJa" title="TaeJa">
         TaeJa
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/Duckdeok" title="Duckdeok">
         duckdeok
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/starcraft2/NaNiwa" title="NaNiwa">
         NaNiwa
        </a>
        . After barely losing a very intense TvT against TaeJa in the initial match, he was bested by
        <a href="/starcraft2/NaNiwa" title="NaNiwa">
         NaNiwa
        </a>
        in the losers' match 0-2, which knocked him out of the tournament in the group stage on the first day of the tournament. Despite not making the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Season_3" title="2013 WCS Season 3">
         2013 WCS Season 3 Finals
        </a>
        , he would earn a ticket to
        <a href="/starcraft2/BlizzCon" title="BlizzCon">
         BlizzCon
        </a>
        for the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_WCS_Global_Finals" title="2013 WCS Global Finals">
         2013 WCS Global Finals
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h3>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="GSTL_2013_Season_2">
         GSTL 2013 Season 2
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/starcraft2/index.php?title=INnoVation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: GSTL 2013 Season 2">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        After joining Team Acer, INnoVation would also shortly after participate in the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2013_Global_StarCraft_II_Team_League_Season_2" title="2013 Global StarCraft II Team League Season 2">
         2013 Global StarCraft II Team League Season 2
        </a>
        for
        <a href="/starcraft2/Axiom-Acer" title="Axiom-Acer">
         Axiom-Acer
        </a>
        - his GSTL debut. INnoVation played in the team's first match versus
        <a href="/starcraft2/FXOpen_e-Sports" title="FXOpen e-Sports">
         FXOpen e-Sports
        </a>
        on September 3, 2013, as the starting player. His first opponent was
        <a href="/starcraft2/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">
         Hurricane
        </a>
        , whom he dispatched without difficulty. He then played against the Terran player
        <a href="/starcraft2/SC" title="SC">
         sC
        </a>
        , against whom INnoVation fell behind due to early aggression. However sC lost his early lead, and from there INnoVation proved too strong during the mid-game and took the victory. INnoVation defeated his next opponent, the former GSL champion
        <a href="/starcraft2/Seed" title="Seed">
         Seed
        </a>
        , which forced FXOpen to put out their final player.
        <a href="/starcraft2/Leenock" title="Leenock">
         Leenock
        </a>
        was chosen as the ace player, but INnoVation was able to close out the match, giving him his fourth
        <a href="/starcraft2/All-Kill_Format" title="All-Kill Format">
         All-Kill
        </a>
        in Korean team leagues and his team their first win of the season. His form continued throughout the season as he won two clinching games in the finals against
        <a href="/starcraft2/AZUBU" title="AZUBU">
         AZUBU
        </a>
        , including a deciding ace match against
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/SuperNoVa" title="SuperNoVa">
         SuperNoVa
        </a>
        , to not only win Axiom-Acer its first GSTL title but also win the season MVP award for himself.
       </p>
       <h3>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="INnoVation_in_2014">
         INnoVation in 2014
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/starcraft2/index.php?title=INnoVation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: INnoVation in 2014">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        In 2014 INnoVation has shown strong play. After previously dropping out of Code A in January 2014 from a group with 3 Protoss players at a time when TvP was widely regarded as imbalanced, in the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2014_Global_StarCraft_II_League_Season_2" title="2014 Global StarCraft II League Season 2">
         2014 Global StarCraft II League Season 2
        </a>
        he advanced through the qualifier before achieving first place in his Code A group, securing himself a place in Code S for the season. Changes to game balance, a new map pool discouraging blink stalker play, and INnoVation's apparent return to form bode well for his success this season. He showed excellent control at the
        <a href="/starcraft2/2014_DreamHack_Open/Bucharest" title="2014 DreamHack Open/Bucharest">
         2014 DreamHack Open/Bucharest
        </a>
        , especially in his series against Finnish player
        <a href="/starcraft2/Welmu" title="Welmu">
         Welmu
        </a>
        . In a nail-biting deciding game, INnoVation managed to secure the win with his precise bio micro, dodging Psi Storms and stutter stepping, after being at a 20 worker disadvantage.
In the end, he would fall in the round of 4 to eventual champion
        <a href="/starcraft2/Life" title="Life">
         Life
        </a>
        , once more showing his weakness to roach busts in TvZ.
       </p>
       <p>
        In April, INnoVation started his team out with a win in the
        <a href="/starcraft2/Acer_TeamStory_Cup_Season_3" title="Acer TeamStory Cup Season 3">
         Acer TeamStory Cup Season 3
        </a>
        with an all-kill against
        <a href="/starcraft2/Evil_Geniuses" title="Evil Geniuses">
         Evil Geniuses
        </a>
        , this would be the first of three all-kills (Against
        <a href="/starcraft2/Evil_Geniuses" title="Evil Geniuses">
         Evil Geniuses
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/MYinsanity" title="MYinsanity">
         mYinsanity
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/starcraft2/Invictus_Gaming" title="Invictus Gaming">
         Invictus Gaming
        </a>
        ) as well as achieving 3 wins against
        <a href="/starcraft2/Axiom" title="Axiom">
         Axiom
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/Millenium" title="Millenium">
         Millenium
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/starcraft2/Yoe_Flash_Wolves" title="Yoe Flash Wolves">
         yoe Flash Wolves
        </a>
        , then finally winning 4 games against
        <a href="/starcraft2/Team_Liquid" title="Team Liquid">
         Team Liquid
        </a>
        to lead his team to first place in the group stage, while scoring himself an outstanding 28-4 record.
       </p>
       <p>
        In May, INnoVation defeated
        <a href="/starcraft2/Center" title="Center">
         Center
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/Impact" title="Impact">
         Impact
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/starcraft2/HyuN" title="HyuN">
         HyuN
        </a>
        in the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Dragon_Invitational_Tournament/Tournament_1" title="Dragon Invitational Tournament/Tournament 1">
         Dragon Invitational Tournament #1
        </a>
        , showing strong TvZ in the latter two series. He went on to face
        <a href="/starcraft2/RagnaroK" title="RagnaroK">
         RagnaroK
        </a>
        in the finals, winning 3-2 and earning the $1,200 first-place prize. The next month, he won against
        <a href="/starcraft2/TaeJa" title="TaeJa">
         TaeJa
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/JYP" title="JYP">
         JYP
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/starcraft2/MMA" title="MMA">
         MMA
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/starcraft2/Life" title="Life">
         Life
        </a>
        to take first place in the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Dragon_Invitational_Tournament/Tournament_2" title="Dragon Invitational Tournament/Tournament 2">
         Dragon Invitational Tournament #2
        </a>
        and defend his championship title.
       </p>
       <p>
        After one year with
        <a href="/starcraft2/Team_Acer" title="Team Acer">
         Team Acer
        </a>
        , he decided to leave the team on September 3rd, 2014. Even though his results in team leagues were great, he was not satisfied with his individual results and he wanted to return to the KeSPA environment and its training method.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-1">
          [2]
         </a>
        </sup>
        On September 25 it was announced that he had joined
        <a href="/starcraft2/SK_Telecom_T1" title="SK Telecom T1">
         SK Telecom T1
        </a>
        .
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
         <a href="#cite_note-2">
          [3]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </p>
       <p>
        In July, INnoVation defeated
        <a href="/starcraft2/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">
         Hurricane
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/starcraft2/Ruin" title="Ruin">
         Ruin
        </a>
        with SCV pull strategies to requalify for Code S. In Code S he lost his initial match to
        <a href="/starcraft2/MyuNgSiK" title="MyuNgSiK">
         MyuNgSiK
        </a>
        but showed a better performance in TvP by defeating
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Paralyze" title="Paralyze">
         Paralyze
        </a>
        and then MyuNgSiK in the rematch, earning him his spot in the Ro16. In the Ro16 group selection he ended up in the group of death with
        <a href="/starcraft2/Maru" title="Maru">
         Maru
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/starcraft2/Parting" title="Parting">
         Parting
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/starcraft2/Stats" title="Stats">
         Stats
        </a>
        . Showing excellent TvP and resilience INnoVation topped his group and advanced to the Ro8 for the first time in over a year. In the Ro8 he faced his first non-Protoss opponent in
        <a href="/starcraft2/DongRaeGu" title="DongRaeGu">
         DongRaeGu
        </a>
        and defeated him in a one sided series going 3-0. Then he faced the rising Terran
        <a href="/starcraft2/Cure" title="Cure">
         Cure
        </a>
        and after a close fought series featuring mech play, INnoVation ultimately triumphed 4-3. Facing his now teammate
        <a href="/starcraft2/SoO" title="SoO">
         soO
        </a>
        in his second GSL finals many fans were expecting an amazing series. While the series was tense, with all-ins and cheese on both sides, the games ultimately didn't live up to the hype. After going down to 2 Roach-Bane all-ins, INnoVation managed to pull out the win in a base race. He then employed two 2 Barracks rushes to take the lead. On match point for INnoVation both players opened up macro, after a relatively calm opening soO started to throw attacks at the man known as the Machine. Nonetheless, INnoVation was able to hold off the attacks and managed to do eco damage with Widow Mine drops at the same time. Soon after securing his 4th and getting 3-3 the victory was his and INnoVation secured his first GSL title and his first Premier tournament victory since last year.
       </p>
