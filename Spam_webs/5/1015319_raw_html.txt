             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://louboosandshoes.blogspot.com/2010/10/so-many-things-of-beauty.html">
               So many things of beauty...
              </a>
             </h3>
             <script>
              var ultimaFecha = 'Friday, 22 October 2010';
             </script>
             <h2 class="date-header">
              posted on: 
                        Friday, 22 October 2010
             </h2>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              A week of unexpected things. A week that I thought would be tough was
              <i>
               easy
              </i>
              . Life is taking on an autumnal rhythm now. My son and I went to the beach for a beach hut picnic. So deserted now that the tourists have left and the beach is restored to a place for dog-walkers and people looking for the freshest of air. We wondered through the sand dunes whilst he chattered away, as only five year old boys do, about nothing, about something; whatever happened to enter his little head. Just so lovely; one of those moments that I much more aware of now as daily he grows older and wiser. One time in the future when he is a surly teenager I will remember times like that walk on the beach.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Meanwhile, I have been thinking a lot about clothes (no change) and hair (off to hairdresser tomorrow), the blocked drains and empty oil tank associated with our old,
              <i>
               old
              </i>
              house. In between the constant half term commentary of the Boos, this is what floats in and out of my mind.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              And finally to blogging. I still question why I blog and
              <i>
               what it all means (so typical me; just let it go Lou)
              </i>
              . I look back on posts and see parts of myself that I have shared and the lovely comments I get and it's all completely and utterly validating. This can only be a good thing so I continue...always on the look out for the perfect image or mulling over thoughts in my head that might just expand into a blog post. Who'd have thought?!
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I have some many lovely pictures to share today - sometimes I have to hem myself in as there's just too much beauty!

             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              I feel remarkably chilled, despite the current status of things. Husband's new job = away from home. It's a two week half term (did I mention?) and I have very little planned for my charges. In response, I made a cassoulet...just off the cuff (OK, Jamie Oliver helped
              <i>
               abit
              </i>
              ) and I shucked some broad beans. Shucked is such an underused word don't you think?
              <br/>
              <br/>

              Boo has dental issues; seriously the amount of time we spend at the dentist, he must think I have some sort of dental Mnchausens. I don't by the way, but my poor children are subjected to daily brushing rituals in my attempt to undo where I went wrong. Never,
              <i>
               ever
              </i>
              enough flossing.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              My house this time of year takes on it's right and proper personality. In the mornings when I get up before everyone else, I go downstairs and have come to habitually skip the fourth step as it has a creak that will surely wake every other member of the household. And as the nights draw in now at 6.30pm, it triggers me to start cooking in a way I never do in the summer months. The wind whistles down the chimney funnel to the ancient Rayburn cooker in a way that reminds me; winter is coming.
              <br/>
              <br/>

This constitutes holiday activity for my kids...and pocket money for spending. We are off to IKEA for a Swedish meatball lunch and maybe a bit of retail.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Each day, dressing decisions for me are whittled down to jeans, boots, leggings (why did I never discover leggings til now, at the ripe age of 36? They have rocked my world).
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I am liking these home times...

              <br/>
              <br/>
              Even though I try
              <i>
               really
              </i>
              hard (honest I do), I get seduced by the contents of the
              <a href="http://www.toast.co.uk/">

              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

              <i>

              <i>
               yep, she really does know me
              </i>
              inside out. Those moments are the best...

              <br/>
              Details matter to me; I notice things like ribbons, buttons and zips; if they are not quality I can't buy the item.

              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_EKSe1GNwvp8/TLyPb8CDgSI/AAAAAAAADDU/93dYIbDgPYY/s1600/opulence_05.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_EKSe1GNwvp8/TLyPb8CDgSI/AAAAAAAADDU/93dYIbDgPYY/s400/opulence_05.jpg" width="395"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              My wardrobe veers dramatically between classic preppy and quirky bohemian.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              I have a sneaking suspicion I generally manage to get any conversation back to something that is relevant to something that has happened to
              <i>
               me
              </i>
              . Does that secretly mean that I think my life experience is the most validated kind and look for it in everyone else?
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Given the choice I would always choose vegetables over meat, yet am not vegetarian - is this a missed opportunity?
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Words like
              <i>
               compote, gingham, mellowed, hessian
              </i>
              please me. Does this mean I aspire to live in cabin in the woods making jam?
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_EKSe1GNwvp8/TLxohqtoLbI/AAAAAAAADDM/kvM6DH47pv0/s1600/autumn_gold_14.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_EKSe1GNwvp8/TLxohqtoLbI/AAAAAAAADDM/kvM6DH47pv0/s400/autumn_gold_14.jpg" width="273"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              I conduct
              <i>
               elaborate
              </i>
              arguments in my mind for and against things - constantly weighing up each side of everything, be it whether I should buy that dress (usually yes), to whether I make my kids eat enough healthy food (usually no).
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Does it make me a cliche that I walk in to the newly opened
              <a href="http://www.cathkidston.co.uk/">
               Cath Kidston
              </a>
              shop in my hometown and genuinely think
              <i>
               I could find a use for a union jack pin cushion
              </i>
              - even though I can't sew!
              <br/>
              <br/>

              <br/>
              <br/>
              I often wonder...am I the only one like me?

             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://louboosandshoes.blogspot.com/2010/10/fine-line.html">
               The fine line...
              </a>
             </h3>
             <script>
              var ultimaFecha = 'Tuesday, 12 October 2010';
             </script>
             <h2 class="date-header">
              posted on: 
                        Tuesday, 12 October 2010
             </h2>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              One of my most-used phrases is this: 'it's a fine line between...whatever and whatever' [fill in the blanks;
              <i>
               whatever
              </i>
              can be anything]. This premise seems to apply to almost all life situations that I face. Some examples:
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div style="color: #76a5af;">
               It's a fine line between pushy parenting and instilling competitiveness in one's children.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               It's a fine line between raising a girl to be a woman and making her grow up too soon.
              </div>
              <div style="color: #76a5af;">
               <br/>
               <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                <tbody>
                 <tr>
                  <td style="text-align: center;">
                   <img border="0" height="397" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_EKSe1GNwvp8/TLNUwPzt3ZI/AAAAAAAADB4/VdpwJJf9PYk/s400/tumblr_l8bq60sKkx1qzwhhno1_500.png" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="400"/>
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                 <tr>
                  <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                   <span style="color: #ea9999; font-size: xx-small;">
                    via
                    <a href="http://bippityboppityboo.tumblr.com/">
                     bippitybobbityboo
                    </a>
                   </span>
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                </tbody>
               </table>
              </div>
              <div style="color: #76a5af;">
               <br/>
               It's a fine line between keeping up with what everyone else needs and doing what
               <i>
                you
               </i>
               need.
              </div>
              <div style="color: #76a5af;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="color: #76a5af;">
               It's a fine line between staying trim and looking gaunt/old/ill. Case in point: Victoria Beckham.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               It's a fine line between wanting those shoes and needing them.
              </div>
              <div style="color: #76a5af;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="color: #76a5af;">
               It's a fine line between spending all your time keeping house and living in squalor if housework is left to its own devices.
              </div>
              <div style="color: #76a5af;">
               <br/>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_EKSe1GNwvp8/TLNThQ1DisI/AAAAAAAADBw/2Y8smlL5XNY/s1600/DSC_4181_main.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="306" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_EKSe1GNwvp8/TLNThQ1DisI/AAAAAAAADBw/2Y8smlL5XNY/s400/DSC_4181_main.jpg" width="400"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <br/>
              </div>
              <span style="color: #76a5af;">
               It's a fine line between blogging cos I love it and spending all my time on the computer. 'Are you on the computer
              </span>
              <i style="color: #76a5af;">
               again
              </i>
              <span style="color: #76a5af;">
               Mummy?'
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              When did this 'fine line' start featuring in every thought I have?! I'm always one to try to find the balance in any circumstance. On the one hand
              <i>
               this
              </i>
              and on the other hand
              <i>
               that
              </i>
              ...
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Achieving the balance of this fine line is like walking a tight rope. If you get your balance it's a wondrous thing, a great view, a thing of art and beauty. If you wobble it can make onlookers wince, you waver there for tantalising moments and then sometimes you regain composure or...you fall. Most of the time there's a safety net so it's OK. I don't recall ever weighing these things up when I was younger, even though I was always a wise soul. But now, with advancing years I see that
              <i>
               it's all about
              </i>
              this fine line; finding it, walking it, straying from it every now and then...
              <br/>
