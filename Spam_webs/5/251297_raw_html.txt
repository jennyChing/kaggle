          <h1>
           10 things we didn't know last week
          </h1>
          <div class="meta">
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/magazinemonitor/10_things/" rel="tag" title="">

           <p>
            <strong>
             Snippets from the week's news, sliced, diced and processed for your convenience.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            1.
            <strong>
             Dr Seuss wrote
            </strong>
            an adult book with nude illustrations.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/entertainment/archive/2012/03/dr-seusss-little-known-book-of-nudes/253891/">
             More details (The Atlantic)
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            2.
            <strong>
             Bees have different
            </strong>
            personalities and some are thrill-seekers.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.sciencenews.org/view/generic/id/339036/title/Bee_genes_may_drive_them_to_adventure">
             More details (Science News)
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            3.
            <strong>
             Wearing a white
            </strong>
            lab coat helps you perform better in tests.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sciencetech/article-2113798/Wearing-lab-uniform-helps-pay-attention-make-fewer-errors.html">
             More details (Daily Mail)
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            4.
            <strong>
             The Masons have
            </strong>
            six million members around the world.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/magazine-17272611">
             More details
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            5.
            <strong>
             Typing with your
            </strong>
            left hand can make you sad.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/9131379/Why-typing-sad-makes-us-miserable-but-jolly-cheers-us-up.html">
             More details (Daily Telegraph)
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            6.
            <strong>
             Oreos
            </strong>
            are the best-selling cookies in the world.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-us-canada-17281342">
             More details
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            7.
            <strong>
             A chicken nugget
            </strong>
            resembling George Washington is worth 5,000 on eBay.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.metro.co.uk/weird/892300-chicken-nugget-resembling-george-washington-sold-on-ebay-for-5k">
             More details (Metro)
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            8.
            <strong>
             Women who have
            </strong>
            just finished ovulating are better at detecting snakes than at other times of their menstrual cycle.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/newstopics/howaboutthat/9132222/Women-who-have-just-finished-ovulating-are-best-at-spotting-snakes.html">
             More details (Daily Telegraph)
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            9.
            <strong>
             LSD could help
            </strong>
            alcoholics give up drinking.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/health-17297714">
             More details
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            10.
            <strong>
             The moon may
            </strong>
            be to blame for the sinking of the Titanic.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.independent.co.uk/news/science/moons-close-proximity-to-earth-may-have-pushed-icebergs-into-path-of-titanic-7544513.html">
             More details (Independent)
            </a>
           </p>
