       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         Sometimes an on-screen snooze can be totally trumped by a killer soundtrack. It's often the most difficult decision a director faces  how do you make the visuals leap off the screen and force the viewer into an unflinching chokehold? There are instances, however, when the music is something more than a backing track: its singular voice is louder than the problematic plot it underscores. A beautifully crafted film score can catapult a so-so plot to fresh, starry heights and far outlive a plain slice of cinematography or a visual lesson in genre conformity. Here are just a few of our favourite examples, including the soundtrack that put the purple in
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0087957/">
           Purple Rain
          </a>
         </em>
         and the party in
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0320244/">
           Party Monster
          </a>
         </em>
         . And whilst these are all good films in their own ways, we think the tunes are better.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0320244/">
           <em>
            PARTY MONSTER
           </em>
          </a>
          (2003)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Money, Success, Fame, Glamour we are living in the age of the thing,
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0000346/">
          Macaulay Culkin
         </a>
         drools through a megaphone over thumping, industrial beats in this low budget disco oddity that details the rise and fall of king of the club kids-turned-murderer
         <a href="/artsandculture/article/19809/1/michael-alig-is-finally-free-and-tweets-a-selfie">
          Michael Alig
         </a>
         . Love it or loathe it, the cult classic oozes a brilliantly camp, trashy spirit that emanates from its dance floor-ready soundtrack via
         <a href="https://soundcloud.com/nettwerkmusicgroup/sets/ladytron-gravity-the-seducer-selections">
          Ladytron
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="https://soundcloud.com/scissorsisters">
          Scissor Sisters
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="/tag/marilynmanson">
          Marilyn Manson
         </a>
         and original club kid
         <a href="https://twitter.com/keokikeokikeoki">
          DJ Keoki
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="67b5b507-c1b5-447c-82ac-a42c00d66014">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/z5gxjvYDPJQ" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1602613/">
            ONLY GOD FORGIVES
           </a>
          </em>
          (2013)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The lush, distorted synthesizers and bubbling piano of
         <a href="http://cliff-martinez.com/">
          Cliff Martinez
         </a>
          original film score conjured up the witching hour ambience of
         <a href="http://www.dazeddigital.com/tag/nicolas-winding-refn">
          Nicolas Winding Refn
         </a>
         'snocturnal Bangkok thriller (take that categorisation very lightly), and propelled
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0809040/">
          Larry Smith
         </a>
         s neon-sheened cinematography to life. The darkly sublime creation also acted as a much-needed antidote against the films pornographic levels of violence. As did
         <a href="/tag/ryan-gosling">
          Ryan Gosling
         </a>
         s face, of course.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="b17ce073-4a52-4fe9-9f6e-a42c00d66014">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/JCiEWALcrFw" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1017451/">
            THE RUNAWAYS
           </a>
          </em>
          (2010)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         While the rebellious, glitter-soaked biopic of all-girl rock band
         <em>
          <a href="http://therunaways.com/">
           The Runaways
          </a>
         </em>
         was a stylish feat in itself, it was the soundtrack that really brought this film to life. From
         <a href="http://www.nickgilder.com/">
          Nick Gilder
         </a>
         to
         <a href="/tag/david-bowie">
          David Bowie
         </a>
         to
         <a href="http://www.iggyandthestoogesmusic.com/us/news">
          Iggy and the Stooges
         </a>
         , the films musical dialogue is a strictly glam rock affair. Punk hadnt even begun when The Runaways started, at least not in the US,
         <a href="/artsandculture/article/8124/1/the-runaways-cherie-currie-on-dakota-fanning">
          Cherie Curry told Dazed
         </a>
         when the film came out. We had our own sound  straight up glam rock.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="b76259a7-b35a-4bc6-a199-a42c00d66014">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/uHpEJ749TRM" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0116922/">
            LOST HIGHWAY
           </a>
          </em>
          (1997)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="/tag/david-lynch">
          David Lynch
         </a>
         s neo-noir horror was knotted together with feverish, alienating complexities, making it one of his harder-to-follow cinematic head-scratchers. The real star of this film was its soundtrack  a darkly brooding, night-time creation produced by
         <a href="/music/article/16951/1/trent-reznor">
          Trent Reznor
         </a>
         of
         <a href="/tag/nine-inch-nails">
          Nine Inch Nails
         </a>
         . Featuring the plunging, industrial depths of Marilyn Manson and
         <a href="http://www.rammstein.de/en">
          Rammstein
         </a>
         , the soundtrack gave the film a tangibly ominous, razor-sharp edge.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="9ffd0b90-4f81-4ae3-b422-a42c00d66014">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/hvGvjnqSKF8" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0087957/">
            PURPLE RAIN
           </a>
          </em>
          (1984)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         In the defining pop culture moment of 1984,
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_%28musician%29">
          Prince
         </a>
         unleashed rock opera
         <em>
          Purple Rain
         </em>
         in a glittering shower of ruffles, shoulder pads, dry ice and a buffed-up Honda. However, rather than the ego-feeding 'extended music video' plot, it was the omnipresent soundtrack  which stayed at number one for a consecutive 24 weeks  that elevated the film to inimitable, electro-funk heights.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="f8afc901-ed4a-4a2d-b205-a42c00d66014">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/AuXK8ZbTmLk" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1421051/">
            SOMEWHERE
           </a>
          </em>
          (2010)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="/tag/sofia-coppola">
          Sofia Coppola
         </a>
         s filmic back catalogue is as musically stylish as it is visual and her 2010 drama
         <em>
          Somewhere
         </em>
         is no different. Whereas the somnambulant tracks added some sense of propulsion to the story, it inched alongwithits languid pace. Film critic Roger Moore said it bluntly: "Even so-called 'slow cinema' shouldn't be this dull."Set in the matte-gold sunlight of the Hollywood Hills, the rich, new wave film score was composed by French quartet
         <a href="http://www.wearephoenix.com/">
          Phoenix
         </a>
         , fronted by Coppola'shusband. The diegetic soundtrack is equally as stylish, featuring
         <a href="http://juliancasablancas.com/">
          Julian Casablancas
         </a>
          woozy ballad 
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ee06fbggZ0g">
          Ill try anything once
         </a>
          and
         <a href="http://www.gwenstefani.com/">
          GwenStefani
         </a>
         s sugary, girl-pop anthem 
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TGwZ7MNtBFU">
          Cool
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="2d66c147-15d4-4862-b9ec-a42c00d66014">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/_PGcaxxd8Yo" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0120238/">
            SUBURBIA
           </a>
          </em>
          (1996)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         They dont make slacker films like they did in the 90s, and they certainly dont make soundtracks like this one. The plot-thin
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0000500/?ref_=tt_ov_dr">
          Richard Linklater
         </a>
         classic might have outstayed its welcome if it wasnt for the no wave noise collage courtesy of
         <a href="/tag/sonic-youth">
          Sonic Youth
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elastica">
          Elastica
         </a>
         &amp;
         <a href="http://stephenmalkmus.com/">
          Stephen Malkmus
         </a>
         and
         <a href="/tag/butthole-surfers">
          Butthole Surfers
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="8fd3aff0-8df3-49bc-928b-a42c00d66014">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/UyWUOgcJXxg" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0168629/">
           <em>
            DANCER IN THE DARK
           </em>
          </a>
          (2000)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The story goes that halfway through production of this Danish drama,
         <a href="/tag/bjork">
          Bjrk
         </a>
         became so enraged with director
         <a href="/tag/lars-von-trier">
          Lars Von Trier
         </a>
         following a series of rows that she ripped up a blouse and proceeded to eat it. Sensationalist tales aside, it was the Oscar-nominated soundtrack that really caught the world's attention. Solely composed by Bjrk herself, the textural, luscious sweeps of 
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ovf6vRgmg8o">
          Overture
         </a>
         , the twisted rhythms of 
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=09BkUQFWQgs">
          Cvalda
         </a>
          and the slow building, militaristic beats of 
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N8FJyhnC2Eo">
          Ive Seen It All
         </a>
          elevated the polarizing film into a majestic, affecting piece of cinema.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="b34fb4eb-a7d0-42af-b198-a42c00d66014">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/VJQkMaZ4fBA" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2345737/">
            THE ROVER
           </a>
          </em>
          (2014)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         This years bleakest, dystopian vision of the future hosted a beautifully warped, dissonant film score to match, as well as the unlikely addition of
         <a href="http://www.kerihilson.com/">
          Keri Hilson
         </a>
         s glossy pop track 
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HtXOVKNazYU">
          Pretty Girl Rock
         </a>
         , which
         <a href="/tag/robert-pattinson">
          Robert Pattinson
         </a>
         sings to himself in the car. I can't remember how and when Keri Hilson found herself in the mix, said director
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm2391575/">
          David Michod
         </a>
         . I wanted that moment in the movie to function as a potent reminder of the fact that Rob's character is a kid who, in different circumstances, would just be doing the kinds of things kids do everywhere. Instead, he has found himself in the middle of nowhere, tethered to a monstrously damaged drifter.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="8626e8cf-815c-415b-aae9-a42c00e5cffa">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/ChM2icbWo9w" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2980592/?ref_=fn_al_tt_1">
            THE GUEST
           </a>
          </em>
          (2014)
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The 80s synth-soaked soundtrack to
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1417392/?ref_=tt_ov_dr">
          Adam Wingard
         </a>
         s slick, blood-splattered thriller ensures that the genre-conforming film is oozing with icy-hearted cool. It's a hell of a tale  houseguest with hidden agenda goes on murderous rampage  but at times it feels a bit budget. Thank God for the 80s injection, Initially, I was thinking more 80s goth rock stuff like
         <a href="http://www.deathinjune.net/">
          Death In June
         </a>
         ,but as we made the picture, I realised the movie was going in a more poppy direction, said Wingard. The electronic sound just worked with the look of the movie more.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="embed-content video-container" id="82f805eb-c108-44f3-b5c5-a42c00e5cffa">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="442" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/y0E2Qh6wLS4" width="786">
        </iframe>
       </div>
