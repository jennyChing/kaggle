					&lt;h1 class="post-title"&gt;All Blacks Set Down Marker in The Rugby Championship&lt;/h1&gt;
					&lt;p class="post-byline"&gt;by &lt;a href="http://sportsrants.com/uk/author/davidroberts/" title="Posts by davidroberts" rel="author"&gt;davidroberts&lt;/a&gt; &middot; Aug 26, 2014&lt;/p&gt;

					
					&lt;div class="clear"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;

					&lt;div class="entry"&gt;
						&lt;div class="entry-inner"&gt;

												&lt;img width="720" height="340" src="http://sportsrants.com/uk/files/2014/08/All-Blacks-Bledisloe-720x340.jpg" class="attachment-thumb-large wp-post-image" alt="All Blacks Bledisloe" /&gt;				
							&lt;p style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;em&gt;(Photo by dailymail.co.uk)&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Talk of the All Blacks decline prior to the second round of matches in the Rugby Championship was truly blown out of the water as New Zealand produced a breathtaking display in a record 6-try hammering over Australia.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The previous week in Sydney, a below-bar All Blacks laboured to a 12-12 draw against the Wallabies, following on from a rather disjointed Test Series win over England. Although they whitewashed the English 3-0, it wasnt for lack of criticism as inconsistency and errors plagued them throughout the series.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;A number of observers therefore, mainly in the Australian press, saw the game as a great opportunity to win their first Test at Eden Park since 1986 and securing the Bledisloe Cup for the first time since 2002.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;!--END MERCHANT:merchant name Bodybuilding Warehouse from affiliatewindow.com--&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&nbsp;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;These assumptions couldnt have been more misplaced as the All Blacks dazzled the Eden Park crowd and the whole Rugby World with a performance of pure class, attitude and commitment as they racked up more than 50 points against the Wallabies for the very first time on New Zealand soil.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The match was competitive early on as Aaron Cruden and Kurtley Beale traded penalties with New Zealand leading 9-6. The sin-binning of Rob Simmons was the turning point in the match as the All Blacks, as they often do when their opposition are down to 14-men, taking full advantage, racing out to a 23-6 lead and never looking back.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;a href="http://sportsrants.com/uk/files/2014/08/julian_savea_try_N2.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-851" src="http://sportsrants.com/uk/files/2014/08/julian_savea_try_N2.jpg" alt="julian_savea_try_N2" width="300" height="225" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;Raining tries in Auckland. Julian Savea celebrates&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;em&gt;(Photo by tvnz.co.nz)&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;A devastating period of pace, power and accuracy in the early stages of the second half produced 3 more tries, extending the lead to 44-6 as the game became increasingly embarrassing for the shell-shocked Wallabies .&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Two quick responses made it more respectable at 44-20 but New Zealand had the last word with Steven Luatua going over late on to bring up the final margin of 51-20 and winning the Bledisloe Cup for the 12th straight year&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.awin1.com/awclick.php?mid=2676&amp;id=214927" target="_blank"&gt;Canterbury.com&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The second game of the round switched focus to Salta, Argentina, as the Springboks were given an almighty fright, edging the Pumas 33-31. With the contest at the breakdown being ferociously fought throughout and with a hugely impressive Argentina scrum, the Pumas found themselves 28-16 in front with 20 minutes remaining, and a historic win seemed in their grasps&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;But as South Africa showed against Wales in June, coming back from 30-17 down to win 31-30 late on, they are a team with a firm belief in themselves, and they showed this once again as they fought back late on with two tries and a penalty to break Argentinian hearts.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;a href="http://sportsrants.com/uk/files/2014/08/Boks-edge-it-in-Salta.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-850" src="http://sportsrants.com/uk/files/2014/08/Boks-edge-it-in-Salta-300x171.jpg" alt="Boks edge it in Salta" width="300" height="171" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;Springboks edge it in Salta&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;em&gt;(Photo by rugby.sportrightnow.com)&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;It was another below-par performance from the Springboks however, missing out on the all-important bonus point for the second straight game, which could prove costly towards the end of The Rugby Championship.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;As the tournament takes a break for a week we are left to digest the impact this weekends action will have on the rest of the tournament. The Wallabies will certainly have a lot of soul-searching to do after crashing back down to earth, with Coach Ewen Mckenzie maybe thinking of bringing Waratahs half-backs Nick Phipps and Bernard Foley into his starting line-up against the Springboks.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.awin1.com/awclick.php?mid=3831&amp;id=214927" target="_blank"&gt;Bodybuilding Warehouse&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;It will allow Kurtley Beale to move to inside centre where he has flourished for the Waratahs all season, and take away the responsibility of game management, which Bernard Foley does brilliantly, allowing him to focus on his attacking game.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;South Africa, receiving much criticism back &lt;a title="home" href="http://sportsrants.com/uk/home/"&gt;home&lt;/a&gt; for their opening two games, will look for major improvements as they travel to Perth for a crunch match against Australia. Its fair to say that the loser of this match will have it all to do as the All Blacks, facing Argentina in back-to back contests, will fancy themselves to pick up the maximum 10 points.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;If South Africa can get through the two tests against Australia relatively unscathed, then a two test showdown with the All Blacks for the Championship seems the likely situation.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;What an exciting prospect.&lt;/p&gt;
