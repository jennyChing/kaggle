  <p>
   <b>
    Explanation:
   </b>
   What are those strange rock structures?

They are towers and walls of sedimentary rock that are particularly plentiful in
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bryce_Canyon">
    Bryce Canyon
   </a>
   in
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utah">
    Utah
   </a>
   ,
   <a href="https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/us.html">
    USA
   </a>
   .

The rock columns may rise higher than 50 meters and are called
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hoodoo_(geology)">
    hoodoos
   </a>
   .

On the far left is
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thor">
    Thor
   </a>
   's Hammer,
perhaps the most famous
   <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CBmeOzSzKoY">
    hoodoo
   </a>
   .

The tall rock columns were carved, most typically, when a unusually dense cap of rock provided a layer of protection to rock underneath from rain-based
   <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zE4j-vScBwo">
    erosion
   </a>
   .  

In the
   <a href="http://www.launchphotography.com/Milky_Way_Bryce_panorama.jpg">
    above panoramic picture
   </a>
   taken earlier this month and compressed horizontally, the foreground rocks were momentarily illuminated by a roving spotlight.

Visible in the background are a few
   <a href="ap090203.html">
    water clouds
   </a>
   a few kilometers away hovering over the nearby Earth.

Visible well beyond that are thousands of
   <a href="ap990113.html">
    individually discernible stars
   </a>
   averaging a few hundred
   <a href="http://starchild.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/StarChild/questions/question19.html">
    light years
   </a>
   away in the nearby
   <a href="http://cass.ucsd.edu/public/tutorial/MW.html">
    Milky Way Galaxy
   </a>
   .

Far in the distance lie billions of stars that are thousands of light years away and compose the faintly glowing arch that is the visible
   <a href="ap090729.html">
    central band
   </a>
   of the flat disk of our Milky Way.  

Over many years, wind and rain will eventually cause the tops of the
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Cappadocia_Turkey_2.jpg">
    hoodoos
   </a>
   to topple, whereafter the
underlying column will likely completely erode away.
  </p>
