       <p class="meta">
        September 12, 2014 
        <a href="http://www.mouthfrog.com/category/dumbass-of-the-week/" rel="category tag">

       </p>
       <p>
        Leadwood, MO  Peter Billingsly and his wife, Laurie, decided to spent a Saturday night at home for once. They go out with friends quite a bit andfelt as thoughthey needed a quietdate night at home.
       </p>
       <p>
        Peter made dinner and Laurie set up the scrabble board. They ate spaghetti and meatballs, did the dishes, and moved over to play their favorite word game. Peter even took a Viagra pill after their meal, thinking things were going so well he may get lucky later that evening. That certainly would have been more desirable than what actually happened.
       </p>
       <p>
        Laurie started off big with the word banquets. Peter followed nicely by building off of the all important Q words by placing down plaque. Game on. Peter and Laurie looked up at each other knowing that this may be one of thosegame nightsto remember. The winner of this game would hold decision power in their marriage for at least the next week. Laurie placed zygote and Peter countered with Zinc. Laurie was thenable to pull off the wordcircumnavigate. She was clearly taking a nice lead now and Peter was getting desperate. Peter thought for some time and then placed the word self-evident down on the game board. Thats when things got ugly.
       </p>
       <p>
        Laurie stated that Peter could not use this word because it was hyphenated. What? yelled Peter. Where in the rules does it say that? The couple pulled out the rules sheet and shouted at each other over how they interpreted the rules differently. Peter finally settled down and agreed that he was out of line. Its just a game after all, Peter said. He didnt mean it.
       </p>
       <p>
        The two sat back down to finish the game. Peter glared at his letters, knowing he had nothing left to defeat his wife. With all of his fury, Peter flipped the glass coffee table they were playing on up in the air, smashing the glass top into the face of his wife. Laurie was left sprawled on the floor in a prone position. She was bleeding profusely. Peter grabbed a few of the blood spotted letters and spelled out ex-wife and placed it on her forehead. Peter muttered, hyphen that, bitch, and stormed out of the house.
       </p>
       <p>
        Laurie was not reached by paramedics in time to save her life. Peter Billingslyhas a warrant out for his arrest but he is still at large. Leadwood Police caution that if a strange man approaches you and challenges you to a game of scrabble, exercise extreme caution. He may be armed and is definitely dangerous.
