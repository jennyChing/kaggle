           <header class="entry-header page-header">
            <h1 class="entry-title ">
             Anatomy of a 60 second Shark Tank pitch
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta">
             <span class="posted-on">
              <i class="fa fa-calendar">

              <a href="http://treplifedad.com/grinding/anatomy-of-a-60-second-shark-tank-pitch/" rel="bookmark">

             <span class="byline">
              <i class="fa fa-user">

           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: normal; -webkit-text-size-adjust: auto; background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0);">
              Yesterday I returned from Minneapolis, MN where I started my second attempt to get on the TV show,
              <i>
               Shark Tank
              </i>
              .
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: normal; -webkit-text-size-adjust: auto; background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0);">
              I had 60 seconds to pitch the producers for a chance to move to the next steps in the audition process, which are to produce a 10-minute video, then go to LA to pitch the Sharks.
             </span>
             <span id="more-3370">
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             Some may think 60-seconds is not a lot of time to convey a compelling message about your business; it is plenty of time.
            </p>
            <p>
             If you cant quickly and succinctly describe what your business does, and exactly what youre asking for from a potential investor, you either dont understand your business well, or simply dont have a compelling enough idea.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #333333;">
              What should your 60-second Shark Tank pitch contain?
             </span>
            </h3>
            <ol>
             <li>
              Problem/solution statement
             </li>
             <li>
              Description of the product or service
             </li>
             <li>
              Business/revenue model
             </li>
             <li>
              Market opportunity
             </li>
             <li>
              Sales traction
             </li>
             <li>
              The money askand what youre offering in terms of equity
             </li>
             <li>
              Use of funds
             </li>
            </ol>
            <p>
             And be super energetic!
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #333333;">
              My practice pitch video
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             Heres me monkeying around getting ready for my pitch. I actually modified the problem statement to say:
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              Do you hate putting nails in your walls, AND expensive picture frames. But have lots of great photos you want to display?
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             The rest of my pitch to the producers was basicallythe same as in this video.
            </p>

             Based on feedback from the producer I pitched, there is a
             <em>
              very good
             </em>
             chance that, like last year, Ill make it to the next round. Fingers crossed.
