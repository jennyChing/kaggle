      <h1>
       Around The World in 80 Photos  D.I.Y Photo World Map
      </h1>
      <div class="clear">
      </div>
     </header>
     <section>
      <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
      <p>
       <a href="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Screen-Shot-2014-09-26-at-11.48.13.jpg">
        <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-09-26 at 11.48.13" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7224" src="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Screen-Shot-2014-09-26-at-11.48.13.jpg" width="100%"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       With Summer practically over, weve been reminiscing on the fantastic photos that we took on our travels in 2014.
      </p>
      <p>
       We wanted a way to display our photos that was a little out of the ordinary  something special that reminded us of the fantastic times that we had and the wonderful places we visited.
      </p>
      <p>
       We wanted to go BIG with displaying our photos and make it a real centre piece for everyone to admire (and admire they did!).
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        For our World map photo project, youll need:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/doublelength.jpg">
        <img alt="Items needed" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7227" src="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/doublelength.jpg" width="100%"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        A world map
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        Travel photos
       </strong>
        whether these are your own or images from your bucket list of places to travel to. These can be printed in a variety of sizes  its completely up to you!
       <br/>
       <strong>
        Coloured pins
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        String or twine
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        Blu-tac
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       All in all, this project should take around 2-3 hours  but the beauty is you can keep adding and adding the more you travel. Lets begin!
      </p>
      <h2 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        Step 1:
       </strong>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Pin or blu-tac your map to a wall  depending on the size of the map &amp; amount of pictures you have, youll want to leave around 30cm of space (if possible) around each edge.
      </p>
      <h2 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        Step 2:
       </strong>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Arrange your photos at random on a table, a select one to begin with. We found that a close your eyes and point method worked best for us.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/worldmapprints.jpg">
        <img alt="worldmapprints" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7219" src="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/worldmapprints.jpg" width="100%"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Now comes the tricky partfinding the country on the map! Just kidding  were sure youre smart enough &amp; didnt have to use Google Maps like us.
      </p>
      <p>
       Position your photo around the edge of the map and secure with blu-tac. Where you place it is completely up to you, weve gone for quite a random variation, with some photos near the country they were taken in, and some on the opposite side of the map.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmapsideview.jpg">
        <img alt="Worldmapsideview" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7215" src="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmapsideview.jpg" width="100%"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h2 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        Step 3:
       </strong>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Using twine or string, measure the distance between your chosen photo and the country it was taken in, cut to length.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmaphands.jpg">
        <img alt="Worldmaphands" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7216" src="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmaphands.jpg" width="100%"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Using a coloured pin, secure one end of the string to the map, placing it in the correct country. Secure the other end of the string with blu-tac behind the photo  this works best if the string is taut against the map.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmapstring.jpg">
        <img alt="Worldmapstring" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7217" src="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmapstring.jpg" width="100%"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmappins.jpg">
        <img alt="Worldmappins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7218" src="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmappins.jpg" width="100%"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Repeat for all photos until you have an even spread around the edge of the map.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Top tip
       </strong>
       : If you have currency left over from your trip, secure small amounts behind the relevant photos or on the map for extra impact. This could also be done with ticket stubs or any mementos you have leftover from your trip.
      </p>
      <h2 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        Step 4
       </strong>
       :
      </h2>
      <p>
       Stand back and admire!
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmapstraighton.jpg">
        <img alt="Worldmapstraighton" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7220" src="http://blog.photobox.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Worldmapstraighton.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       There are so many different variations that can be creating using this idea  we mostly used our standard 6x4 prints, with some cropped to fit, but when we add to the map well be trying some different sizes.
      </p>
      <p>
       To get you going weve got an exclusive offer code that will get you 20% off all of our standard and enlargement prints. Just use the code:
       <strong>
        GETMAPPY
       </strong>
       (or
       <strong>
        GETMAPPIE
       </strong>
       for customers in Ireland) at checkout
      </p>
      <p>
       We also had the opportunity to use an A1 sized map, but this project could be done on a smaller scale using an A3 map as well. Size &amp; space shouldnt hold you back!
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        Is this a project youd try? Let us know which countries youd want to include, or even better, give the project a try and share your versions with us!
       </em>
      </p>
