           <h1>
            How a Lack of Understanding Causes Relationship Problems during Menopause?
           </h1>
           <ul class="navigation byline">
            <li>
             <a class="nolink">
              Posted by
             </a>
             <a href="/profile/saddison94">
              sophie addison
             </a>
             <a class="nolink">
              on August 21, 2014 at 8:34am
             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             <a class="xg_sprite xg_sprite-view" href="http://community.healthywomen.org/profiles/blog/list?user=07jg6ropwu0jm">
              View Blog
             </a>
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="xg_module_body">
          <div class="postbody">
           <div class="xg_user_generated">
            <p>
             <a href="http://api.ning.com:80/files/hpMD5afDfdbTdPG6gYkQV*aV*2tXb*cufPTJ0ZSDacYbn2Dx6C06lRSy4HOJbhz4j0TpKDZ72EyuZlhPdNzF-YfAe2N93PFw/relationshipproblemsduringmenopause.jpg" target="_self">
              <img class="align-center" height="536" src="http://api.ning.com:80/files/hpMD5afDfdbTdPG6gYkQV*aV*2tXb*cufPTJ0ZSDacYbn2Dx6C06lRSy4HOJbhz4j0TpKDZ72EyuZlhPdNzF-YfAe2N93PFw/relationshipproblemsduringmenopause.jpg?width=750" width="689"/>
             </a>
             Menopause can bring about symptoms in some women that have life altering effects. While many seem to breeze through this transitional period of life with few problems, some others encounter a wide array of difficulties. Every woman experiences menopause differently and as an individual. For those who have a rough go of it, the impacts of menopause can take a serious toll on romantic relationships. Many marriages have been damaged or destroyed during this stage. Can menopause destroy a marriage? Severe symptoms and a lack of understanding on the behalf of both partners can certainly contribute to this scenario
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              How menopause creates physical problems in women
             </b>
             ?
             <br/>
             Hormonal imbalances in the body are the largest culprit of the symptoms that can be devastating. The ovaries cease production of estrogen as the body ends its fertile stage of life. Estrogen helps to regulate many of the functions of the body. A deficiency of this hormone is what can cause hot flashes, mood swings, night sweats, insomnia, irritability, depression and many other less common symptoms.
            </p>
            <p>
             Not all women experience all of the symptoms. They are also presented in varying degrees of frequency and intensity. When you combine a few of these symptoms with high levels of intensity and frequency, they can wreak havoc on a woman's physical and emotional well-being.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              Sexual problems due to menopause
              <br/>
             </b>
             The lack of estrogen can cause a condition known as vaginal atrophy. This happens because there is not enough estrogen to help the vaginal tissues maintain their normal elasticity, and the result can be thinning of the vaginal walls and more fragile tissue. Additionally, secretions of natural lubricating fluids during sex may be decreased resulting in
             <a href="http://blog.menoquil.com/menopause-vaginal-dryness.html" rel="nofollow">
              menopause vaginal dryness
             </a>
             . These two factors can cause a condition known as dyspareunia, or painful sex. If both partners are not aware of the reasons for this, it can result in misunderstandings, hurt feelings and damage to the relationship.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              Emotional impacts of menopause
              <br/>
             </b>
             Some women experience a period of grief and mourning over the loss of their fertility. Others go through periods of intense mood swings, anxiety, panic attacks and for some, depression. Any single occurrence or combination of these symptoms can be hard for a couple to deal with. The odds for a marriage surviving are much worse if there is limited communication.
            </p>
            <p>
             Communication is the key factor is helping both parties to understand that there is a problem, but it may not have been caused intentionally. Women need to understand what is happening to their bodies and their emotional health. By understanding what is happening, they can relate the honest truth of the situation to their partners. When partners understand the biological aspects of the situation, they are more apt to be understanding and supportive during this potentially difficult time.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              What can be done to preserve relationships during menopause?
              <br/>
             </b>
             Keeping clear lines of communication open are vital when the waters get rough. Women who experience symptoms that create issues in day to day functioning should seek ways to address the problem. There are several different treatment methods available to help manage and control the
             <a href="http://www.empowher.com/community/share/common-symptoms-menopause" rel="nofollow">
              symptoms of menopause
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              Treatment options
              <br/>
             </b>
             Natural supplements that contain estrogen like compounds known as isoflavones and phytoestrogens may help to restore hormonal balance and relieve many of the symptoms. This option offers few if any known risks of side effects or long term health complications and has been effective for many women are achieving a measure of relief. If this option does not take care of the problems, consulting a health care professional for other
             <a href="http://www.consumerhealthdigest.com/menopause-center/menopause-treatments.html" rel="nofollow">
              menopausal treatments
             </a>
             , such as hormone replacement therapy or counseling is an option.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              Conclusion
              <br/>
             </b>
             Menopause does not generally present a problem for well-grounded couples, as the majority of women go through the process with few complications. For those who experience severe or debilitating symptoms, the risk of damage to a partnership or marriage can be high. When neither partner understands what is happening, the odds for failure go even higher. By keeping communications open and seeking relief for the symptoms that are partly responsible for causing the issues, couples can help to keep their relationships on track and avoid the pitfall of falling victim to failure due to menopause.
            </p>
