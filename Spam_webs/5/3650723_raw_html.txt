     <p>
      <b>
       San Pedro
      </b>
      (
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">
        [sanpido]
       </a>
      </span>
      ) is a community within the City of
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
       Los Angeles
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">
       California
      </a>
      . Formerly a separate city, it consolidated with Los Angeles in 1909. The
      <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Los_Angeles" title="Port of Los Angeles">
       Port of Los Angeles
      </a>
      , a major international seaport, is partially located within San Pedro. The district has grown from being dominated by the
      <a href="/wiki/Fishing_industry" title="Fishing industry">
       fishing industry
      </a>
      to become primarily a working class community within the City of Los Angeles.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pedro,_Los_Angeles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The site, at the southern end of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palos_Verdes_Peninsula" title="Palos Verdes Peninsula">
       Palos Verdes Peninsula
      </a>
      , on the west side of
      <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Bay_(California)" title="San Pedro Bay (California)">
       San Pedro Bay
      </a>
      , was used by Spanish ships starting in the 1540s. The peninsula, including all of San Pedro, was the homeland of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tongva" title="Tongva">
       Tongva-Gabrieleo
      </a>
      Native American people for thousands of years. In other areas of the Los Angeles Basin archeological sites date back 8,00015,000 years. The Tongva believe they have been here since the beginning of time. Once called the "lords of the ocean," due to their mastery of oceangoing canoes (Ti'ats), many Tongva villages covered the coastline. The Tongva lived in a virtual paradise, until colonization. Their first contact with Europeans in 1542 with Joo Cabrilho (Juan Cabrillo), the Portuguese explorer who also was the first to write of them. Chowigna and Suangna were two Tongva settlements of many in the peninsula area, which was also a departure point for their rancherias on the Channel Islands. Legend has it that the Native Americans blessed the land of Palos Verdes, making it the most beautiful place on Earth. The Tongva called the San Pedro area "Chaaw".

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_name">
       Origin of name
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pedro,_Los_Angeles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origin of name">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      San Pedro was named for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_of_Alexandria" title="Peter of Alexandria">
       St. Peter of Alexandria
      </a>
      , a fourth-century bishop in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexandria,_Egypt" title="Alexandria, Egypt">
       Alexandria, Egypt
      </a>
      . His feast day is November 24 on the local ecclesiastical calendar of Spain, the day on which
      <a href="/wiki/Juan_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Cabrillo" title="Juan Rodrguez Cabrillo">
       Juan Rodrguez Cabrillo
      </a>
      discovered the bay in 1542 which would be known as San Pedro.
      <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catalina_Island,_California" title="Santa Catalina Island, California">
       Santa Catalina Island
      </a>
      , named after
      <a href="/wiki/Catherine_of_Alexandria" title="Catherine of Alexandria">
       Catherine of Alexandria
      </a>
      , was claimed for the
      <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">
       Spanish Empire
      </a>
      the next day, on her feast day, November 25. In 16021603,
      <a href="/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A1n_Vizca%C3%ADno" title="Sebastin Vizcano">
       Sebastin Vizcano
      </a>
      (15481624) officially surveyed and mapped the California coastline, including San Pedro Bay, for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">
       New Spain
      </a>
      . The anglicized pronunciation, popularized by the English-speaking people of Midwestern America, is "san-PEE-dro".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Settlement">
       Settlement
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pedro,_Los_Angeles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Settlement">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Map_of_the_Rancho_San_Pedro_finally_confirmed_to_Manuel_Dominguez_et_al.,_December_1859_(sc_hwmc000790001).jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3220" data-file-width="5648" height="125" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Map_of_the_Rancho_San_Pedro_finally_confirmed_to_Manuel_Dominguez_et_al.%2C_December_1859_%28sc_hwmc000790001%29.jpg/220px-Map_of_the_Rancho_San_Pedro_finally_confirmed_to_Manuel_Dominguez_et_al.%2C_December_1859_%28sc_hwmc000790001%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Map_of_the_Rancho_San_Pedro_finally_confirmed_to_Manuel_Dominguez_et_al.%2C_December_1859_%28sc_hwmc000790001%29.jpg/330px-Map_of_the_Rancho_San_Pedro_finally_confirmed_to_Manuel_Dominguez_et_al.%2C_December_1859_%28sc_hwmc000790001%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Map_of_the_Rancho_San_Pedro_finally_confirmed_to_Manuel_Dominguez_et_al.%2C_December_1859_%28sc_hwmc000790001%29.jpg/440px-Map_of_the_Rancho_San_Pedro_finally_confirmed_to_Manuel_Dominguez_et_al.%2C_December_1859_%28sc_hwmc000790001%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Map_of_the_Rancho_San_Pedro_finally_confirmed_to_Manuel_Dominguez_et_al.,_December_1859_(sc_hwmc000790001).jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        1859 survey map of the Rancho San Pedro
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      European settlement began in 1769 as part of an effort to populate California, although trade restrictions encouraged more smuggling than regular business.
      <a href="/wiki/Rancho_San_Pedro" title="Rancho San Pedro">
       Rancho San Pedro
      </a>
      is the site of the first
      <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">
       Spanish
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Land_grant" title="Land grant">
       land grant
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Alta_California" title="Alta California">
       Alta California
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">
       New Spain
      </a>
      . The land was granted in 1784 by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Carlos_III" title="King Carlos III">
       King Carlos III
      </a>
      to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juan_Jose_Dominguez" title="Juan Jose Dominguez">
       Juan Jose Dominguez
      </a>
      , a retired Spanish soldier who came to California with the
      <a href="/wiki/Gaspar_de_Portol%C3%A0" title="Gaspar de Portol">
       Gaspar de Portol
      </a>
      expedition.
     </p>
     <p>
      When
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">
       New Spain
      </a>
      won its independence from the
      <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">
       Spanish Empire
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Alta_California" title="Alta California">
       Alta California
      </a>
      became part of
      <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
       Mexico
      </a>
      , the trade restrictions were lifted, and the town flourished.
     </p>
     <p>
      Under United States control after 1848, when the United States defeated Mexico in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican-American_war" title="Mexican-American war">
       Mexican-American war
      </a>
      , the harbor was greatly improved and expanded under the guidance of
      <a href="/wiki/Phineas_Banning" title="Phineas Banning">
       Phineas Banning
      </a>
      and John Gately Downey, the seventh governor of California after the
      <a href="/wiki/Free_Harbor_Fight" title="Free Harbor Fight">
       Free Harbor Fight
      </a>
      . In 1868 Banning created the
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_%26_San_Pedro_Railroad" title="Los Angeles &amp; San Pedro Railroad">
       Los Angeles &amp; San Pedro Railroad
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">
       Southern California
      </a>
      's first railroad and used it to transport goods from
      <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Bay_(California)" title="San Pedro Bay (California)">
       San Pedro Bay
      </a>
      to
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
       Los Angeles
      </a>
      which soon became a major city in Southern California.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kcet_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-kcet-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1906, the City of Los Angeles annexed the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harbor_Gateway" title="Harbor Gateway">
       Harbor Gateway
      </a>
      , a long, narrow strip of land connecting the city to the northern border of
      <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_Los_Angeles" title="Wilmington, Los Angeles">
       Wilmington
      </a>
      , and in 1909, the larger city consolidated with Wilmington and with San Pedro.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_Navy_Battle_Fleet_home_port_1919.E2.80.931940">
       United States Navy Battle Fleet home port 19191940
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pedro,_Los_Angeles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United States Navy Battle Fleet home port 19191940">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 1888, the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">
       War Department
      </a>
      took control of a tract of land next to the bay and added to it in 1897 and 1910. This became
      <a href="/wiki/Fort_MacArthur" title="Fort MacArthur">
       Fort MacArthur
      </a>
      in 1914 and was a coastal defense site for many years.
      <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson" title="Woodrow Wilson">
       Woodrow Wilson
      </a>
      transferred 200
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">
       United States Navy
      </a>
      ships from the Atlantic to the Pacific in 1919 when tension arose between the United States and Japan over the fate of China.
      <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Bay" title="San Diego Bay">
       San Diego Bay
      </a>
      was considered too shallow for the largest ships, so the
      <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">
       battleships
      </a>
      anchored in San Pedro Bay on August 9, 1919. Local availability of fuel oil minimized transportation costs, and consistently good weather allowed frequent gunnery exercises off the nearby
      <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands_of_California" title="Channel Islands of California">
       Channel Islands of California
      </a>
      . The heavy
      <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">
       cruisers
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Scouting_Fleet" title="Scouting Fleet">
       Scouting Force
      </a>
      were transferred from the Atlantic to San Pedro in response to the 1931
      <a href="/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_Manchuria" title="Japanese invasion of Manchuria">
       Japanese invasion of Manchuria
      </a>
      . By 1934, 14 battleships, two
      <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">
       aircraft carriers
      </a>
      , 14 cruisers, and 16 support ships were based at San Pedro. On April 1, 1940, the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Pacific_Fleet" title="United States Pacific Fleet">
       Pacific Fleet
      </a>
      battleships sailed to Hawaii for annual fleet exercises. The battleships remained in the
      <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_Islands" title="Hawaiian Islands">
       Hawaiian Islands
      </a>
      to deter Japanese aggression until the
      <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">
       attack on Pearl Harbor
      </a>
      . The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fleet_post_office" title="Fleet post office">
       fleet post office
      </a>
      , supply depot, fuel depot,
      <a href="/wiki/Degaussing" title="Degaussing">
       degaussing
      </a>
      range,
      <a href="/wiki/Electronic_countermeasure" title="Electronic countermeasure">
       ECM
      </a>
      repair facility, and naval training schools for small craft, fire fighters, merchant ship communications, and anti-submarine attack remained at San Pedro through World War II;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but the battle fleet never returned.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-proceedings_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-proceedings-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Appropriately, San Pedro was selected as the final home port of the battleship
      <a href="/wiki/USS_Iowa_(BB-61)" title="USS Iowa (BB-61)">
       USS
       <i>
        Iowa
       </i>
       (BB-61)
      </a>
      . The
      <i>
       Iowa
      </i>
      now serves as a museum ship and memorial recognizing "the positive contributions of this battleship and its crew at critical moments in American history".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Locations_of_interest">
       Locations of interest
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

     </h2>
     <p>
      One San Pedro landmark is the
      <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Thomas_Bridge" title="Vincent Thomas Bridge">
       Vincent Thomas Bridge
      </a>
      , a 1,500-foot (457m)-long
      <a href="/wiki/Suspension_bridge" title="Suspension bridge">
       suspension bridge
      </a>
      linking San Pedro with
      <a href="/wiki/Terminal_Island" title="Terminal Island">
       Terminal Island
      </a>
      and named after
      <a href="/wiki/California_State_Assembly" title="California State Assembly">
       California Assemblyman
      </a>
      Vincent Thomas. It is the third longest suspension bridge in California. Nearby is the Battleship
      <a href="/wiki/USS_Iowa_(BB-61)" title="USS Iowa (BB-61)">
       USS
       <i>
        Iowa
       </i>
      </a>
      educational museum &amp; attraction, the
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Maritime_Museum" title="Los Angeles Maritime Museum">
       Los Angeles Maritime Museum
      </a>
      , the largest
      <a href="/wiki/Maritime_museum" title="Maritime museum">
       maritime museum
      </a>
      in California, as is the
      <a href="/wiki/Museum_ship" title="Museum ship">
       museum ship
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/SS_Lane_Victory" title="SS Lane Victory">
       SS
       <i>
        Lane Victory
       </i>
      </a>
      , a fully operational
      <a href="/wiki/Victory_ship" title="Victory ship">
       victory ship
      </a>
      of World War II and
      <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Landmark" title="National Historic Landmark">
       National Historic Landmark
      </a>
      . There is also the "Ports O' Call" tourist destination built in 1963 which provides shopping venues, waterfront eateries and Crafted at the Port of Los Angeles, a permanent craft marketplace housed inside the historic World War II Naval Warehouse #10.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Frank_Gehry" title="Frank Gehry">
       Frank Gehry
      </a>
      designed
      <a href="/wiki/Cabrillo_Marine_Aquarium" title="Cabrillo Marine Aquarium">
       Cabrillo Marine Aquarium
      </a>
      had its origins in the old Cabrillo Beach Marine Museum which was located in the historic Bath House at Cabrillo Beach. The
      <a href="/wiki/Point_Fermin_Light" title="Point Fermin Light">
       Point Fermin Lighthouse
      </a>
      , a Victorian-era structure built in the late 19th century, still exists as a museum and park on a bluff overlooking the ocean. The
      <a href="/wiki/Korean_Bell_of_Friendship" title="Korean Bell of Friendship">
       Korean Bell of Friendship
      </a>
      is a massive bronze memorial
      <a href="/wiki/Bell_(instrument)" title="Bell (instrument)">
       bell
      </a>
      donated by
      <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">
       South Korea
      </a>
      in 1976 to the people of Los Angeles. The church of
      <a href="/wiki/Mary_Star_of_the_Sea_Catholic_Church,_San_Pedro,_California" title="Mary Star of the Sea Catholic Church, San Pedro, California">
       Mary Star of the Sea
      </a>
      is a prominent landmark with a steeple-top statue overlooking the harbour.
     </p>
     <p>
      On July 19, 2003, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_of_LA_Waterfront_Red_Car" title="Port of LA Waterfront Red Car">
       San Pedro Waterfront Red Car Line
      </a>
      was opened, along the waterfront between downtown San Pedro and the Cruise Ship Terminal. This line includes two newly constructed trolleys built to resemble the wood-bodied 500 class cars introduced in 1905 for the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Electric_Railway" title="Pacific Electric Railway">
       Pacific Electric Railway
      </a>
      , which once operated more than 1,000 miles (1,600km) of track running streetcars and interurbans in Southern California. The 1.5-mile (2.4km) line operates along former Pacific Electric right-of-way. The line, rebuilt and maintained by the
      <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Los_Angeles" title="Port of Los Angeles">
       Port of Los Angeles
      </a>
      , also has one original restored Pacific Electric interurban, which is used only for special charter excursions and special events. The original car is in fact Pacific Electric 963 (former Los Angeles Pacific 713 as built in 1907) rebuilt by Richard Fellows and renumbered 1058. Discussions have been held to extend the line to the
      <a href="/wiki/Cabrillo_Marine_Aquarium" title="Cabrillo Marine Aquarium">
       Cabrillo Marine Aquarium
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Twenty-Eighth Street in San Pedro, between Gaffey Street and Peck Avenue, is the steepest section of public roadway in Los Angeles. For about 50 feet (15m), the street climbs at a 33.3% angle, although the rest of the street is less steep.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The location known as Liberty Hill was the birthplace of the Southern California ACLU. ON May 15, 1923, social activist Upton Sinclair together with Hunter S. Kimbrough, his brother-in-law, Prince Hopkins and Hugh Hardyman were arrested for reading from the Bill of Rights of the US Constitution at Liberty Hill, which is believed to be the intersection of 4th and Beacon Streets. This exercise of free speech took place during a strike of longshoreman. The waterfront was tense when the strike was called. The employers' fear of unions and eagerness to cut back on union gains made during the war was set against keen union interest not to lose benefits gained during the war.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Population_history">
       Population history
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pedro,_Los_Angeles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Population history">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Spectators_watching_a_bicyclist_on_Beacon_Street,_San_Pedro,_ca.1907_(CHS-4783).jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3395" data-file-width="4527" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Spectators_watching_a_bicyclist_on_Beacon_Street%2C_San_Pedro%2C_ca.1907_%28CHS-4783%29.jpg/220px-Spectators_watching_a_bicyclist_on_Beacon_Street%2C_San_Pedro%2C_ca.1907_%28CHS-4783%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Spectators_watching_a_bicyclist_on_Beacon_Street%2C_San_Pedro%2C_ca.1907_%28CHS-4783%29.jpg/330px-Spectators_watching_a_bicyclist_on_Beacon_Street%2C_San_Pedro%2C_ca.1907_%28CHS-4783%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Spectators_watching_a_bicyclist_on_Beacon_Street%2C_San_Pedro%2C_ca.1907_%28CHS-4783%29.jpg/440px-Spectators_watching_a_bicyclist_on_Beacon_Street%2C_San_Pedro%2C_ca.1907_%28CHS-4783%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Spectators_watching_a_bicyclist_on_Beacon_Street,_San_Pedro,_ca.1907_(CHS-4783).jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>

       </div>

     <p>
      Ethnically diverse San Pedro was a magnet for European immigrants from various countries for years, reflected in the number of restaurants representing diverse cuisines, especially
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuisine_of_Croatia" title="Cuisine of Croatia">
       Croatian
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuisine_of_Portugal" title="Cuisine of Portugal">
       Portuguese
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mexican_cuisine" title="Mexican cuisine">
       Mexican
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuisine_of_Italy" title="Cuisine of Italy">
       Italian
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_Cuisine" title="Irish Cuisine">
       Irish
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuisine_of_Greece" title="Cuisine of Greece">
       Greek
      </a>
      . San Pedro is home to the largest Italian-American community in
      <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">
       Southern California
      </a>
      , centered on the "Via Italia" (South Cabrillo Avenue). Estimates state that the community numbers about 45,000 Italian-Americans. San Pedro is also considered the heart of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Croatian_people" title="Croatian people">
       Croatian
      </a>
      and Norwegian communities in Los Angeles. The Croatian community, originally composed of seafarers and fishermen from the
      <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">
       Dalmatia
      </a>
      (especially the islands of
      <a href="/wiki/Bra%C4%8D" title="Bra">
       Bra
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hvar" title="Hvar">
       Hvar
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Vis_(island)" title="Vis (island)">
       Vis
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Kor%C4%8Dula" title="Korula">
       Korula
      </a>
      ) region, has been present in San Pedro since the settlement began more than 200 years ago. The City of Los Angeles even named a stretch of 9th Street "Croatian Place" in honor of the city's old Croatian community. The Norwegian presence can be felt at the
      <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Seamen%27s_Church,_San_Pedro" title="Norwegian Seamen's Church, San Pedro">
       Norwegian Seamen's Church
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      A large portion of San Pedro is also composed of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican-Americans" title="Mexican-Americans">
       Mexican-Americans
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hispanic" title="Hispanic">
       Hispanic
      </a>
      immigrants and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African-Americans" title="African-Americans">
       African-Americans
      </a>
      with long-time roots in the community. Much of their populations are based in the older, east side of the community surrounding the downtown area and bordering the Port of Los Angeles.
     </p>
     <p>
      Until February 1942, San Pedro was home to a vibrant
      <a href="/wiki/Japanese_diaspora" title="Japanese diaspora">
       Japanese
      </a>
      immigrant community of about 3,000 people who lived in what had been described as a
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.cr.nps.gov/history/online_books/5views/5views4h87.htm" rel="nofollow">
       " typical Japanese Fishing Village"
      </a>
      on
      <a href="/wiki/Terminal_Island" title="Terminal Island">
       Terminal Island
      </a>
      (East San Pedro).These Japanese immigrants pioneered
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.cr.nps.gov/history/online_books/5views/5views4h87.htm" rel="nofollow">
       albacore fishing out of San Pedro Bay and harvesting abalone off of White Point
      </a>
      , thus leading the way in establishing a viable fishing industry in San Pedro. The 48-hour forced expulsion of these San Pedro residents and the razing of their homes and shops, as part of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese-American_internment" title="Japanese-American internment">
       Japanese-American internment
      </a>
      during World War II, is described in
      <a href="/wiki/Jeanne_Wakatsuki_Houston" title="Jeanne Wakatsuki Houston">
       Jeanne Wakatsuki Houston
      </a>
      's memoir
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Farewell_to_Manzanar" title="Farewell to Manzanar">
        Farewell to Manzanar
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">
       2000 census
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pedro,_Los_Angeles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2000 census">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A total of 80,065 people lived in San Pedro's 12.06 square miles, according to the 2000 U.S. censusaveraging 6,640 people per square mile, among the lowest
      <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">
       population densities
      </a>
      in the city as a whole. Population was estimated at 86,012 in 2008. The median age was 34, considered average in Los Angeles.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MappingLASanPedro_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-MappingLASanPedro-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      San Pedro is considered highly diverse ethnically, with a
      <a href="/wiki/Diversity_index" title="Diversity index">
       diversity index
      </a>
      of 63.0.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2000
      <a href="/wiki/White_people#United_States" title="White people">
       whites
      </a>
      made up 44.2% of the population,
      <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans" title="Hispanic and Latino Americans">
       Latinos
      </a>
      were at 40.8%,
      <a href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">
       blacks
      </a>
      at 6.1%,
      <a href="/wiki/Asian_American" title="Asian American">
       Asians
      </a>
      at 4.8% and others at 4.1%. Mexico and Italy were the most common places of birth for the 24.5% of the residents who were born abroad, considered a low percentage of foreign-born when compared with the city as a whole.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MappingLASanPedro_14-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-MappingLASanPedro-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The $57,508 median household income in 2008 dollars was average for the city and county. Renters occupied 56.1%% of the housing units, and homeowners occupied the rest. In 2000 there were 3,394 families headed by single parents, or 17.5%, a rate that was average for the county and the city. In the same year there were 6,559 military veterans, or 11% of the population, considered high when compared to the city and county as a whole.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MappingLASanPedro_14-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-MappingLASanPedro-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Education">

      <span class="mw-editsection">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Primary_and_secondary_schools">
       Primary and secondary schools
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

     </h3>
     <p>
      San Pedro is served by the
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Unified_School_District" title="Los Angeles Unified School District">
       Los Angeles Unified School District
      </a>
      . The area is within Board District 7.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As of 2012, Dr. Richard Vladovic represents the district.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>

     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_High_School" title="San Pedro High School">
       San Pedro High School
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mary_Star_of_the_Sea_High_School" title="Mary Star of the Sea High School">
       Mary Star of the Sea High School
      </a>
      , and the
      <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Los_Angeles_High_School" title="Port of Los Angeles High School">
       Port of Los Angeles High School
      </a>
      are the primary high schools within the region. San Pedro High School is home to the protected landmarks in the form of The English Language Arts and Administration Buildings (c. 1939, 1936, resp.). The school recently celebrated its 100 year anniversary in 2003. It is home to both the Marine Science and Police Academy Magnet programs. Port of Los Angeles High School is a public charter high school, fusing a college preparatory program with elective coursework in International Business and Maritime Studies. Such studies reinforce the significant impact of California's ports on the global economy and international trade.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Press">
       Press
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Pedro,_Los_Angeles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Press">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Random Lengths News started publishing in San Pedro in 1979 and now continues to be the last newspaper published there. It covers politics, local news, arts and culture for the seven communities that surround the San Pedro Bay. The paper has been continuously published by James Preston Allen since its inception.Random Lengths News has published continuously for nearly 35 years. The current bi-monthly format was introduced in 1985. Random Lengths News has taken on the role of local watchdog over Port expansion, city government, land use and civil rights. Random Lengths News founders intentionally mirrored San Pedro's local ties to the Socialist movement of the 1930s.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53">
       <a href="#cite_note-53">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        53
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <i>
       San Pedro News-Pilot
      </i>
      , long the area's daily newspaper, ceased publishing in 1998. The
      <i>
       News-Pilot
      </i>
      traced its history back to 1906; it was created from the merge of the
      <i>
       San Pedro Daily News
      </i>
      and the
      <i>
       San Pedro Pilot
      </i>
      . Some of the staff of the
      <i>
       N-P
      </i>
      were hired by the
      <i>
       South Bay Daily Breeze
      </i>
      ; still covering San Pedro is former
      <i>
       News-Pilot
      </i>
      reporter Donna Littlejohn. An online community news and social network, called SanPedroNewsPilot.com, is not connected to the original newspaper of a similar name. In 2002, the
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Beach_Press-Telegram" title="Long Beach Press-Telegram">
        Long Beach Press-Telegram
       </a>
      </i>
      launched the monthly publication
      <i>
       San Pedro Magazine
      </i>
      serving the San Pedro and Rancho Palos Verdes areas.
      <i>
       San Pedro Magazine
      </i>
      was cancelled in December 2008 after the
      <i>
       Press-Telegram
      </i>
      eliminated their magazine department. In January 2009, a new independently-owned monthly magazine called
      <i>
       San Pedro Today
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54">
       <a href="#cite_note-54">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        54
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      debuted. Other papers available for subscription or purchase include The South Bay newspaper
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Daily_Breeze" title="Daily Breeze">
        Daily Breeze
       </a>
      </i>
      although they mainly cover news from Torrance and the South Bay cities. In 2003, it created a weekly,
      <i>
       More San Pedro
      </i>
      , in the San Pedro Harbor Area.
      <i>
       More San Pedro
      </i>
      was canceled in 2008 after the
      <i>
       Breeze
      </i>
      was purchased and subsequently gutted by
      <a href="/wiki/MediaNews_Group" title="MediaNews Group">
       MediaNews Group
      </a>
      .the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">
        Los Angeles Times
       </a>
      </i>
      and the
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Beach_Press-Telegram" title="Long Beach Press-Telegram">
        Long Beach Press-Telegram
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
