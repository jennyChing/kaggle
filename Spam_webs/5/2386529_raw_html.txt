     <p>
      <b>
       Mu Arae d
      </b>
      (also known as
      <b>
       HD 160691 d
      </b>
      ) is an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planet" title="Extrasolar planet">
       extrasolar planet
      </a>
      orbiting the star
      <a href="/wiki/Mu_Arae" title="Mu Arae">
       Mu Arae
      </a>
      . The planet has a mass about half that of
      <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">
       Jupiter
      </a>
      and orbits at a
      <a href="/wiki/Distance" title="Distance">
       distance
      </a>
      of 0.921
      <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">
       AU
      </a>
      from the star with a period of 310.55 days. The planet may be located at a distance close enough to the star to receive a comparable amount of
      <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet" title="Ultraviolet">
       ultraviolet
      </a>
      radiation as the
      <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
       Earth
      </a>
      does from the
      <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
       Sun
      </a>
      , however it is too close to the star to be able to support
      <a href="/wiki/Liquid" title="Liquid">
       liquid
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">
       water
      </a>
      at its surface.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buccino_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-buccino-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Furthermore, given its mass, the planet is likely to be a
      <a href="/wiki/Gas_giant" title="Gas giant">
       gas giant
      </a>
      with no
      <a href="/wiki/Solid" title="Solid">
       solid
      </a>
      surface.
     </p>
