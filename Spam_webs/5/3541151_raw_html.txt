               <div class="section post-header">
                <h2>
                 <a class="link hubspot-editable" data-hubspot-form-id="name" data-hubspot-name="Blog Title" href="http://news.portalfinancial.co.uk/blog/how-to-keep-your-money-safe-part-1-avoiding-pension-scams" id="hubspot-name">
                  <span class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_text" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="text" id="hs_cos_wrapper_name" style="">
                   How to keep your money safe, Part 1: Avoiding pension scams
                  </span>
                 </a>
                </h2>
                <p class="hubspot-editable" data-hubspot-form-id="author_data" data-hubspot-name="Blog Author" id="hubspot-author_data">
                 <span class="hs-author-label">
                  Author:
                 </span>
                 <a class="author-link" href="http://news.portalfinancial.co.uk/blog/author/jamie-smith-thompson">
                  Jamie Smith-Thompson
                 </a>
                 <span class="custom-date">
                  Tue 28 Apr, 2015 10:00AM
                 </span>
                </p>
               </div>
               <div class="section post-body">
                <span class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text" id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" style="">
                 <p>
                  <img alt="pension_scams" src="http://news.portalfinancial.co.uk/hubfs/Blogs/pension_scams.png?t=1436885060295"/>
                  <br/>
                  <!--more-->
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   The threat of
                   <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                    <a href="/blog/authorities-tackle-the-growing-problem-of-pension-liberation-scams" target="_blank">
                     pension scams
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   is on the rise due to the recent changes. The lure of huge or guaranteed returns can turn the head of even cautious people, but falling prey to a bad opportunity can wipe out a big chunk of your pension fund  perhaps all of it. To avoid a pension scam, its key to look for the warning signs.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   <strong>
                    Are you old enough?
                   </strong>
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   One of the most important things to remember is that you are only able to remove money from your pension fund from the age of 55, and the new pension rules have not changed this. Removing money earlier is known as pension liberation, and you
                   <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                    <a href="/blog/authorities-tackle-the-growing-problem-of-pension-liberation-scams" target="_blank">
                     could lose a lot of your money in taxes and fees
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   .
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   <strong>
                    What are you planning with the money?
                   </strong>
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   Although its now possible to remove as much money from your pension as you want, it may not be the best decision. In particular, if you are hoping to get a better return on your investments then you need to think carefully and talk to an adviser, as pensions can be hard to beat, which we explain in our blog What we found,
                   <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                    <a href="/blog/what-we-found-part-2-pensions-are-misunderstood-but-hard-to-beat" target="_blank">
                     Part 2: Pensions are misunderstood but hard to beat
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   . Only 25% of your pension fund is tax free, and anything above that is taxed at your marginal rate. If you plan to withdraw a large amount of money you could pay a considerable amount of tax, and a subsequent
                   <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                    <a href="http://www.portalfinancial.co.uk/investment.php" target="_blank">
                     investment
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   will need to perform excellently to recoup that. Money inside a pension is also tax free, whereas income generated from other investments may have income tax applied to it.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   <strong>
                    Is it regulated?
                   </strong>
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   If you are approached by a firm offering an investment opportunity, you should check to see if they are regulated by the FCA. With regulated advice, you have access to the Financial Ombudsman Service and your money is protected by the Financial Services Compensation Scheme if things go wrong. With unregulated firms, however, there is a lot of risk and you may not be able to recoup any of your money if the investment does not go as planned.
                  </span>
                 </p>

                 <h2>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   <strong>
                    Is there diversification?
                   </strong>
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   A standard rule of financial advice is that having money in a single place puts it at higher risk, while spreading it out into different investments is safer because it isnt so vulnerable. Many scams or risky investments ask you to put your money into a single place, often promising unrealistic returns.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   <strong>
                    Are you being rushed?
                   </strong>
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   A frequent tactic by
                   <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                    <a href="/blog/authorities-tackle-the-growing-problem-of-pension-liberation-scams" target="_blank">
                     pension fraudsters
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   is encouraging you to sign up immediately because the opportunity is only available for a limited time. Some are so pushy that a courier turns up and asks for a signature on the spot. Never be rushed into a decision  your future financial situation depends on it, so find out the details and take your time to learn the facts.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   <strong>
                    Do you know the company?
                   </strong>
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   Text messages and cold calls are particularly popular methods used by scammers, so be very wary if you are contacted this way. If you know the name of the company, you can check the
                   <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                    <a href="http://www.fsa.gov.uk/register/indivSearchForm.do" target="_blank">
                     FCA register
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   to see if they are regulated.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   If in doubt, either walk away or seek
                   <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                    <a href="http://www.portalfinancial.co.uk" target="_blank">
                     regulated advice
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   . You can also check the FCAs
                   <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                    <a href="http://scamsmart.fca.org.uk/" target="_blank">
                     ScamSmart
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   website, which can tell you if a company or investment is on its warning list.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   Have you been targeted by a pension scam? Let us know with a comment below.
