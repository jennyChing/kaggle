                 <h2 class="date-header">
                  <span>
                   Friday, April 1, 2011
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-L_yqaVutBHU/TZfTe6k0YkI/AAAAAAAAABc/yu6VfT10sbo/s200/gf_risotto_01.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Intervention with GF Risotto
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4213655833586426178" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       Once upon a time I met a fabulous friend who suffered from Celiac disease. I use "suffer" in the past tense because once she discovered this risotto recipe, I'm fairly certain that most of her life's complications were mitigated. The first time she introduced me to this magical dish I think I recreated it three or four times in the same week. And a standard recipe makes a TON of servings.
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       Anyway. CK has been suggesting this dinner for months and for some reason I've been really unmotivated to make it. Probably because my cravings for warm, soothing risotto are much more intense during sub-freezing winters, as opposed to the 50-degree-excuse-for-a-season that we have in LA. I know, life is so rough.
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       Going back to the magicality of this recipe, this risotto is adequately deserving of such a classification for several reasons: first, and in my dear friend's case, this recipe is gluten free; secondly, it's super duper healthy: aside from 2T butter (for the ENTIRE POT) and Parmesan cheese (to your taste), there's really nothing else fattening in it; third(ly?), the taste resembles a grownup mac&amp;cheese and in my mind, resemblance to mac&amp;cheese is always a good thing; finally, it is de-li-CIOUS. Kind of time consuming, maybe not for a week night, but a good call regardless.
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       Tonight was a particularly good occasion because AD joined us for dinner. AD recently admitted (and with all due respect) that Sizzler, Domino's and, yes, candy were three of his primary food groups. Naturally, I decided to take him
                       <u>
                        way
                       </u>
                       outside his comfort zone and serve up something a. vegetarian (well, for you fact-checkers out there, swap out the stock), b. homemade, and c. healthy. And, bonus: I am A-OK staying in on Friday nights. Here goes:
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        <b>
                         GF Risotto
                        </b>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        1 c Arborio rice
                        <b>
                        </b>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        32 oz low sodium chicken stock
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        1 c water
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        1/2 large onion, diced
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        1 medium zucchini, diced
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        1 medium squash, diced
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        6-8 Roma tomatoes, peeled &amp; roughly chopped*
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        2 T unsalted butter
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        2 cans white beans (Great Northern, Navy, etc.), rinsed
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        1/2 c grated Parmesan cheese (+ more to season)
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        Black pepper / salt (to season)
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        <b>
                        </b>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Melt butter in a large pot over low heat and add onions; cook for approx. 5 minutes then add zucchini &amp; squash, cook for another 5 minutes, stirring regularly
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Add tomatoes, cook for approx 10 minutes, stirring occasionally
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Once the tomatoes are going, heat chicken stock and water in a small sauce pan over low heat - keep at simmer, not a boil
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Add Arborio rice and stir for a minute or so, until well incorporated
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Start adding liquid to rice/veggie mixture, 1 cup at a time delaying each addition until the previous amount of liquid is absorbed (it takes 5-10 mins to incorporate a cup of liquid)
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Keep a close eye on risotto mixture, and stir regularly so that rice doesn't stick to the bottom of the pot
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Upon final addition of liquid, also pour in beans, cheese and black pepper
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Keep rice over heat and continue stirring until liquid is absorbed, rice is soft but has a little bite and cheese is melted
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Remove from heat and serve immediately. Add more Parmesan, black pepper and salt to accommodate tastes
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        Enjoy; particularly on a cold night, by a warm fire and with a delicious beverage in hand
                       </span>
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                      *Instead of using fresh tomatoes I prefer to use whole, peeled ones in can (go for 28 oz) as a good, time saving option. Just crush the tomatoes, remove the top/stem part and then roughly chop.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                      For you concerned parties in the audience, AD made it through dinner just fine: he was skeptical at first, I can't say he's fully converted, but I'll call it a mitzvah. Happy weekend!
                     </span>
