     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Marquesas Islands
      </b>
      (
      <span class="nowrap">
       <span class="IPA nopopups">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">
         /
         <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
          <span title="'m' in 'my'">
           m
          </span>
          <span title="// 'a' in 'father'">
           
          </span>
          <span title="// primary stress follows">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'k' in 'kind'">
           k
          </span>
          <span title="/e/ long 'a' in 'base'">
           e
          </span>
          <span title="'s' in 'sigh'">
           s
          </span>
          <span title="// short 'a' in 'bad'">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'s' in 'sigh'">
           s
          </span>
         </span>
         /
        </a>
       </span>
      </span>
      ;
      <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
       French
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">
       <i>
        les Marquises
       </i>
      </span>
      or
      <i>
       Archipel des Marquises
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       Marquises
      </i>
      ;
      <a href="/wiki/Marquesan_language" title="Marquesan language">
       Marquesan
      </a>
      :
      <i>
       Te Henua (K)enana
      </i>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Marquesan_language" title="North Marquesan language">
       North Marquesan
      </a>
      ) and
      <i>
       Te Fenua `Enata
      </i>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Marquesan_language" title="South Marquesan language">
       South Marquesan
      </a>
      ), both meaning "The Land of Men") are a group of
      <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">
       volcanic
      </a>
      islands in
      <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">
       French Polynesia
      </a>
      , an
      <a href="/wiki/Overseas_collectivity" title="Overseas collectivity">
       overseas collectivity
      </a>
      of France in the southern Pacific Ocean. The Marquesas are located at 9 00S, 139 30W. The highest point is the peak of Mount
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oave" title="Oave">
       Oave
      </a>
      (French:
      <i>
       Mont Oave
      </i>
      ) on
      <a href="/wiki/Ua_Pu" title="Ua Pu">
       Ua Pu
      </a>
      island at 1,230 m (4,035ft) above sea level.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Marquesas Islands form one of the
      <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_French_Polynesia" title="Administrative divisions of French Polynesia">
       five administrative divisions
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       subdivisions administratives
      </i>
      ) of French Polynesia. The capital of the Marquesas Islands administrative subdivision is the settlement of
      <a href="/wiki/Taiohae" title="Taiohae">
       Taiohae
      </a>
      on the island of
      <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">
       Nuku Hiva
      </a>
      . The population of the Marquesas Islands was 9,264 inhabitants at the August 2012 census.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-census-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_(Peue_%27Ei),_19th_century..jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1202" data-file-width="1536" height="172" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_%28Peue_%27Ei%29%2C_19th_century..jpg/220px-Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_%28Peue_%27Ei%29%2C_19th_century..jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_%28Peue_%27Ei%29%2C_19th_century..jpg/330px-Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_%28Peue_%27Ei%29%2C_19th_century..jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_%28Peue_%27Ei%29%2C_19th_century..jpg/440px-Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_%28Peue_%27Ei%29%2C_19th_century..jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kaimoko_Family._Headdress_(Peue_%27Ei),_19th_century..jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Kaimoko family. Headdress (Peue 'Ei), 19th century. Porpoise teeth, beads, coir. It is likely that this woman's headdress was made on the island of Ua Pou, where a great number of porpoises were available.
        <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Museum" title="Brooklyn Museum">
         Brooklyn Museum
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Marquesas" title="History of the Marquesas">
       History of the Marquesas
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The first recorded settlers of the Marquesas were
      <a href="/wiki/Polynesians" title="Polynesians">
       Polynesians
      </a>
      , who, from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archeology" title="Archeology">
       archeological
      </a>
      evidence, are believed to have arrived before 100AD.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      However, a study using revised, high-precision radiocarbon dating pushes forward the date of Polynesian settlement to between 1200 and 1277AD.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Ethnology" title="Ethnology">
       Ethnological
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Historical_linguistics" title="Historical linguistics">
       linguistic
      </a>
      evidence suggests that they likely arrived from the region of
      <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">
       Tonga
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">
       Samoa
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The first Europeans to reach the Marquesas may have been the crew of
      <i>
       San Lesmes
      </i>
      , a Spanish vessel which disappeared in a storm while passing through the region as part of an expedition headed by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garc%C3%ADa_Jofre_de_Loa%C3%ADsa" title="Garca Jofre de Loasa">
       Garca Jofre de Loasa
      </a>
      in June 1526.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The islands were given their name by the Spanish explorer
      <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_de_Menda%C3%B1a_de_Neira" title="lvaro de Mendaa de Neira">
       lvaro de Mendaa
      </a>
      who reached them seventy years later on 21 July 1595. He named them after his patron,
      <a href="/wiki/Garc%C3%ADa_Hurtado_de_Mendoza,_5th_Marquis_of_Ca%C3%B1ete" title="Garca Hurtado de Mendoza, 5th Marquis of Caete">
       Garca Hurtado de Mendoza, 5th Marquis of Caete
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       Spanish
      </i>
      : Marqus de Caete), who was
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viceroy_of_Peru" title="Viceroy of Peru">
       Viceroy of Peru
      </a>
      at the time. Mendaa visited first
      <a href="/wiki/Fatu_Hiva" title="Fatu Hiva">
       Fatu Hiva
      </a>
      and then
      <a href="/wiki/Tahuata" title="Tahuata">
       Tahuata
      </a>
      before continuing on to the
      <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">
       Solomon Islands
      </a>
      . They charted the four southernmost Marquesas as
      <i>
       Magdalena
      </i>
      (Fatu Hiva),
      <i>
       Dominica
      </i>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Hiva_Oa" title="Hiva Oa">
       Hiva Oa
      </a>
      ),
      <i>
       San Pedro
      </i>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moho_Tani" title="Moho Tani">
       Moho Tani
      </a>
      ), and
      <i>
       Santa Cristina
      </i>
      (Tahuata).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The American
      <a href="/wiki/Maritime_fur_trade" title="Maritime fur trade">
       maritime fur trader
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Ingraham" title="Joseph Ingraham">
       Joseph Ingraham
      </a>
      first visited the northern Marquesas while commanding the
      <a href="/wiki/Brig" title="Brig">
       brig
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Hope_(ship)" title="Hope (ship)">
        Hope
       </a>
      </i>
      in 1791, giving them the name
      <i>
       Washington Islands
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WDL_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-WDL-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1813, Commodore
      <a href="/wiki/David_Porter_(naval_officer)" title="David Porter (naval officer)">
       David Porter
      </a>
      claimed
      <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">
       Nuku Hiva
      </a>
      for the United States, but the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">
       United States Congress
      </a>
      never ratified that claim. In 1842, Francefollowing a successful military operation on behalf of a native chief (named
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Iotete&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Iotete (page does not exist)">
       Iotete
      </a>
      ) who claimed he was king of the whole island of Tahuatatook possession of the whole group and established a settlement (abandoned in 1859) on Nuku Hiva. France reestablished control over the group in 1870, and later incorporated it into
      <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">
       French Polynesia
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Of all major island groups in the Pacific, the Marquesas suffered the greatest population decline from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infectious_disease" title="Infectious disease">
       diseases
      </a>
      brought by Western explorers. Imported diseases reduced the eighteenth-century population of over 78,000 inhabitants to about 20,000 by the middle of the nineteenth century, and to just over 4,000 by the beginning of the 1900s.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      During the course of the twentieth century, the population increased to 8,548 at the November 2002 census,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RP71_02_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-RP71_02-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      not including the Marquesan community residing on
      <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">
       Tahiti
      </a>
      , and it has continued to increase since then, reaching 9,264 inhabitants at the August 2012 census.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-census-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The sparsely populated Marquesas Islands, located 1,371km (852mi) from Tahiti, the most populous island and dominant political center of French Polynesia, often feel neglected by politicians in Tahiti,
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      and some
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
         <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (July 2015)">
          who?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      favor a direct link with Paris instead of depending on
      <a href="/wiki/Papeete" title="Papeete">
       Papeete
      </a>
      . Several prominent Marquesan political leaders have repeatedly declared themselves in favor of separating from French Polynesia but remaining within the French Republic, in case French Polynesian political leaders in Tahiti proclaim independence from France.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-secession_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-secession-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This has generated controversies in Tahiti, where pro-independence Tahitian leaders have accused the French central government of encouraging the separation of the Marquesas Islands from French Polynesia.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-secession_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-secession-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
       Geography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marquesas_geography" title="Marquesas geography">
       Marquesas geography
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="812" data-file-width="1020" height="239" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg/300px-Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg/450px-Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg/600px-Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg 2x" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rainforest_Fatu_Hiva.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Breadfruit" title="Breadfruit">
         Breadfruit
        </a>
        tree on
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatu-Hiva" title="Fatu-Hiva">
         Fatu-Hiva
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The Marquesas Islands group is one of the most remote in the world, lying about 1,371km (852mi) northeast of
      <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">
       Tahiti
      </a>
      and about 4,800 kilometres (3,000mi) away from the west coast of Mexico, the nearest continental land mass. They fall naturally into two geographical divisions: the northern group, consisting of
      <a href="/wiki/Eiao" title="Eiao">
       Eiao
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hatutu" title="Hatutu">
       Hatutu
      </a>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hatutaa" title="Hatutaa">
       Hatutaa
      </a>
      ),
      <a href="/wiki/Motu_One_(Marquesas_Islands)" title="Motu One (Marquesas Islands)">
       Motu One
      </a>
      , and the islands centered around the large island of
      <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">
       Nuku Hiva
      </a>
      :
      <a href="/wiki/Motu_Iti_(Marquesas_Islands)" title="Motu Iti (Marquesas Islands)">
       Motu Iti
      </a>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hatu_Iti" title="Hatu Iti">
       Hatu Iti
      </a>
      ),
      <a href="/wiki/Ua_Pu" title="Ua Pu">
       Ua Pu
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Motu_Oa" title="Motu Oa">
       Motu Oa
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Ua_Huka" title="Ua Huka">
       Ua Huka
      </a>
      , and the southern group of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatu_Uku" title="Fatu Uku">
       Fatu Uku
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tahuata" title="Tahuata">
       Tahuata
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moho_Tani" title="Moho Tani">
       Moho Tani
      </a>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motane" title="Motane">
       Motane
      </a>
      ),
      <a href="/wiki/Terihi" title="Terihi">
       Terihi
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Fatu_Hiva" title="Fatu Hiva">
       Fatu Hiva
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Motu_Nao" title="Motu Nao">
       Motu Nao
      </a>
      (Thomasset Rock), clustered around the main island of
      <a href="/wiki/Hiva_Oa" title="Hiva Oa">
       Hiva `Oa
      </a>
      . With a combined land area of 1,049 square kilometres (405sqmi), the Marquesas are among the largest island groups of French Polynesia originally discovered by Spanish galleons fleets en route to Manila, Nuku Hiva being the second largest island in the entire territory, after
      <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">
       Tahiti
      </a>
      . With the exception of Motu One, all the islands of the Marquesas are of volcanic origin.
     </p>
     <p>
      In contrast to the tendency to associate Polynesia with lush tropical vegetation, the Marquesas are remarkably dry islands. Though the islands lie within the tropics, they are the first major break in the prevailing easterly winds that spawn from the extraordinarily dry (from an atmospheric perspective)
      <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_Current" title="Humboldt Current">
       Humboldt Current
      </a>
      . Because of this, the islands are subject to frequent drought conditions, and only those that reach highest into the clouds (generally, above about 750 m/2,500ft above sea level) have reliable precipitation. This has led to historical fluctuations in water supply, which have played a crucial role in the sustainability of human populations in certain sections of the various islands throughout the archipelago. This is especially evident in the low historical population of Ua Huka (maximum elevation 857 m/2,812ft.) and the intermittent inhabitability of Eiao (maximum elevation 576 m/1,890ft.). The Marquesas Islands are thought to have formed by a center of upwelling
      <a href="/wiki/Magma" title="Magma">
       magma
      </a>
      called the
      <a href="/wiki/Marquesas_hotspot" title="Marquesas hotspot">
       Marquesas hotspot
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">
       Climate
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Climate">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Temperatures in the Marquesas are stable year around, but precipitation is highly variable. Precipitation is much greater on the north and east (windward) parts of the islands than on the western (leeward) parts. Average annual precipitation can vary from more than 100 inches (2,500mm) on windward shores and mountains to a low as 20 inches (510mm) in the "desert" region of Nuku Hiva. Droughts, sometimes lasting several years, are frequent and seem to be associated with the
      <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o" title="El Nio">
       El Nio
      </a>
      phenomena.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The statistics from the weather station at
      <a href="/wiki/Atuona" title="Atuona">
       Atuona
      </a>
      on Hiva Oa is representative of the average sea-level climate of the Marquesas. Illustrating the variability of precipitation, the highest annual rainfall recorded in Atuona is 148.2 inches (3,760mm); the lowest is 22 inches (560mm).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">
       Geology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Geology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FatuIva_BasalticRocksAboveHanavave_20061111.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="3888" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/FatuIva_BasalticRocksAboveHanavave_20061111.jpg/300px-FatuIva_BasalticRocksAboveHanavave_20061111.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/FatuIva_BasalticRocksAboveHanavave_20061111.jpg/450px-FatuIva_BasalticRocksAboveHanavave_20061111.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/FatuIva_BasalticRocksAboveHanavave_20061111.jpg/600px-FatuIva_BasalticRocksAboveHanavave_20061111.jpg 2x" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FatuIva_BasalticRocksAboveHanavave_20061111.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Basalt" title="Basalt">
         Basaltic
        </a>
        rock
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The bulk of the Marquesas Islands are of volcanic origin, created by the
      <a href="/wiki/Marquesas_hotspot" title="Marquesas hotspot">
       Marquesas hotspot
      </a>
      that underlies the
      <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Plate" title="Pacific Plate">
       Pacific Plate
      </a>
      . The Marquesas Islands lie above a submarine volcanic
      <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">
       plateau
      </a>
      of the same name. The plateau, like the islands, is generally believed to be less than 5 million years old, though one hypothesis has the plateau (not the islands) as significantly older and having a mirror image, the
      <a href="/wiki/Andean_Volcanic_Belt#Volcanic_gaps" title="Andean Volcanic Belt">
       Inca Plateau
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">
       subducting
      </a>
      under northern
      <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">
       Peru
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Except for
      <a href="/wiki/Motu_One_(Marquesas_Islands)" title="Motu One (Marquesas Islands)">
       Motu One
      </a>
      , all the Marquesas are
      <a href="/wiki/High_island" title="High island">
       high islands
      </a>
      . Motu One is a
      <a href="/wiki/Low_island" title="Low island">
       low island
      </a>
      , comprising two small
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sand_bank" title="Sand bank">
       sand banks
      </a>
      awash on a
      <a href="/wiki/Coral" title="Coral">
       coral
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">
       reef
      </a>
      . Unlike the majority of French Polynesian islands, the Marquesas are not surrounded by protective fringing reefs.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tp_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-tp-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Except for Motu One, and in bays and other protected areas, the only other coral in the Marquesas is found in a rather strange place: on the top of the island of
      <a href="/wiki/Fatu_Huku" title="Fatu Huku">
       Fatu Huku
      </a>
      . The
      <a href="/wiki/South_Equatorial_Current" title="South Equatorial Current">
       South Equatorial Current
      </a>
      lashes the islands mercilessly, which has led to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea-cave" title="Sea-cave">
       sea-caves
      </a>
      dotting the islands' shores. Except for where the valleys empty into the small bays, the islands are remarkable for their mountain ridges, which end abruptly as cliffs where they meet the sea. The islands are estimated to range in age from the youngest, Fatu Hiva (1.3 my) to the oldest, Eiao (6 my).
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">
       Administration
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Administration">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote">
      See also:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flags_of_the_Marquesas_Islands" title="Flags of the Marquesas Islands">
       Flags of the Marquesas Islands
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The Marquesas Islands do not have a provincial or regional assembly. Administratively, they form a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decentralisation" title="Decentralisation">
       deconcentrated
      </a>
      subdivision of both the French central State and the government of French Polynesia. As a deconcentrated subdivision of the French central State, the Marquesas Islands form the administrative subdivision of the Marquesas (French:
      <i>
       subdivision administrative des Marquises
      </i>
      ), one of
      <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">
       French Polynesia
      </a>
      's five
      <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_French_Polynesia" title="Administrative divisions of French Polynesia">
       administrative subdivisions
      </a>
      . The head of the administrative subdivision of the Marquesas is the
      <i>
       administrateur d'Etat
      </i>
      ("State administrator"), generally simply known as
      <i>
       administrateur
      </i>
      , also sometimes called
      <i>
       chef de la subdivision administrative
      </i>
      ("head of the administrative subdivision"). The
      <i>
       administrateur
      </i>
      is a civil servant under the authority of the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=High_Commissioner_of_the_French_Republic_in_French_Polynesia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="High Commissioner of the French Republic in French Polynesia (page does not exist)">
       High Commissioner of the French Republic in French Polynesia
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Papeete" title="Papeete">
       Papeete
      </a>
      . The
      <i>
       administrateur
      </i>
      and his staff sit in
      <a href="/wiki/Taiohae" title="Taiohae">
       Taiohae
      </a>
      , on the island of
      <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">
       Nuku Hiva
      </a>
      , which has become the administrative capital of the Marquesas Islands, having replaced
      <a href="/wiki/Atuona" title="Atuona">
       Atuona
      </a>
      on the island of
      <a href="/wiki/Hiva_Oa" title="Hiva Oa">
       Hiva Oa
      </a>
      , which was previously the capital.
     </p>

       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">

         Hanavave
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Acting as the representative of the French central State and delegate of Papeete's High Commissioner, the
      <i>
       administrateur
      </i>
      of the Marquesas is in charge of:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Offering legal advice to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commune_in_France" title="Commune in France">
        communes
       </a>
       (municipalities) of the Marquesas and verifying the legality of decisions made by the communes
      </li>
      <li>
       Issuing official documents (ID cards, driving licences, etc.), applying immigration rules, organising elections
      </li>
      <li>
       Managing security (coordination of
       <a href="/wiki/National_Gendarmerie" title="National Gendarmerie">
        gendarmerie
       </a>
       forces, handling of major crises such as natural disasters, etc.)
      </li>
      <li>
       Overseeing public services of the French central State in the Marquesas Islands (such as the correctional facility on Nuku Hiva)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      As a deconcentrated subdivision of the government of French Polynesia, the Marquesas Islands form the
      <i>
       circonscription des Marquises
      </i>
      ("district of the Marquesas"), one of French Polynesia's four
      <i>
       circonscriptions
      </i>
      ("districts") created in 2000 by the
      <a href="/wiki/Assembly_of_French_Polynesia" title="Assembly of French Polynesia">
       Assembly of French Polynesia
      </a>
      to serve as deconcentrated subdivisions of the government of French Polynesia in the islands away from
      <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">
       Tahiti
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moorea" title="Moorea">
       Moorea
      </a>
      . The head of the
      <i>
       circonscription des Marquises
      </i>
      is the
      <i>
       tavana hau
      </i>
      , known as
      <i>
       administrateur territorial
      </i>
      in French (English: "territorial administrator"), but the
      <a href="/wiki/Tahitian_language" title="Tahitian language">
       Tahitian
      </a>
      title
      <i>
       tavana hau
      </i>
      is most often used. The
      <i>
       tavana hau
      </i>
      is the direct representative of the
      <a href="/wiki/President_of_French_Polynesia" title="President of French Polynesia">
       president of French Polynesia's government
      </a>
      who appoints him. The
      <i>
       tavana hau
      </i>
      and his staff sit in Taiohae on Nuku Hiva, same as the State administrator.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       tavana hau
      </i>
      is in charge of:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Coordinating the work of French Polynesian administrations in the Marquesas Islands (such as the French Polynesian administrations in charge of roads, fisheries, etc.)
      </li>
      <li>
       Ensuring the enforcement of acts passed by the Assembly of French Polynesia and decisions taken by the government of French Polynesia
      </li>
      <li>
       Evaluating the performance of French Polynesian civil servants and sending the evaluations to the responsible ministries in Papeete
      </li>
      <li>
       Acting as a liaison between the local population and the government of French Polynesia in Papeete
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      The Marquesas Islands also form the electoral district of the Marquesas Islands, one of
      <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">
       French Polynesia
      </a>
      's six electoral districts for the
      <a href="/wiki/Assembly_of_French_Polynesia" title="Assembly of French Polynesia">
       Assembly of French Polynesia
      </a>
      (see also
      <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_French_Polynesia" title="Politics of French Polynesia">
       Politics of French Polynesia
      </a>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      The Marquesas Islands are subdivided in six
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commune_in_France" title="Commune in France">
       communes
      </a>
      (municipalities). In each of the six communes the local residents elect a municipal council and a mayor in charge of managing local affairs within the commune. Three communes (
      <a href="/wiki/Nuku-Hiva" title="Nuku-Hiva">
       Nuku-Hiva
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ua_Pu" title="Ua Pu">
       Ua-Pou
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Hiva-Oa" title="Hiva-Oa">
       Hiva-Oa
      </a>
      ) are further subdivided into
      <a href="/wiki/Associated_communes_of_France" title="Associated communes of France">
       associated communes
      </a>
      due to their larger population. The communes and associated communes are the only elected councils in the Marquesas since there does not exist a provincial or regional assembly for the entire archipelago. Municipal elections are held every six years on the same day as municipal elections in the rest of France (see
      <a href="/wiki/French_municipal_elections,_2008" title="French municipal elections, 2008">
       French municipal elections, 2008
      </a>
      for the last municipal elections).
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Language">
       Language
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Language">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Marquesan_language" title="Marquesan language">
       Marquesan language
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Although French and
      <a href="/wiki/Tahitian_language" title="Tahitian language">
       Tahitian
      </a>
      are the only official languages of French Polynesia, and therefore of the Marquesas Islands as well, the
      <a href="/wiki/Marquesan_language" title="Marquesan language">
       Marquesan languages
      </a>
      , in their various forms, remain the primary means of communication within the archipelago.
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <a href="/wiki/Marquesan_language" title="Marquesan language">
        Marquesan
       </a>
      </b>
      is a collection of
      <a href="/wiki/Polynesian_languages" title="Polynesian languages">
       East-Central Polynesian
      </a>
      dialects, of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marquesic_languages" title="Marquesic languages">
       Marquesic
      </a>
      group, spoken in the Marquesas Islands of
      <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">
       French Polynesia
      </a>
      . They are usually classified into two groups,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Marquesan_language" title="North Marquesan language">
       North Marquesan
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Marquesan_language" title="South Marquesan language">
       South Marquesan
      </a>
      , roughly along geographic lines.
     </p>
     <p>
      The North Marquesan dialects are spoken on the islands of
      <a href="/wiki/Ua_Pu" title="Ua Pu">
       Ua Pu
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">
       Nuku Hiva
      </a>
      , and South Marquesan dialects on the islands of
      <a href="/wiki/Hiva_Oa" title="Hiva Oa">
       Hiva `Oa
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tahuata" title="Tahuata">
       Tahuata
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Fatu_Hiva" title="Fatu Hiva">
       Fatu Hiva
      </a>
      . The dialects of
      <a href="/wiki/Ua_Huka" title="Ua Huka">
       Ua Huka
      </a>
      are often incorrectly classified as North Marquesan; they are instead transitional. While the island is in the northern Marquesas group, the dialects show more morphological and phonological affinities with South Marquesan. The North Marquesan dialects are sometimes considered two separate languages: North Marquesan and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tai_Pi_Marquesan_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tai Pi Marquesan language (page does not exist)">
       Tai Pi Marquesan
      </a>
      , the latter being spoken in the valleys of the eastern third of the island of Nuku Hiva, in the ancient province of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tai_Pi_(province)" title="Tai Pi (province)">
       Tai Pi
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The most striking feature of the Marquesan languages is their almost universal replacement of the
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       /r/
      </span>
      or
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       /l/
      </span>
      of other Polynesian languages by a
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       //
      </span>
      (glottal stop).
     </p>
     <p>
      Like other Polynesian languages, the phonology of Marquesan languages is characterised by a paucity of consonants and a comparative abundance of vowels.
     </p>

     <p>

       Marquesan
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Tahitian_language" title="Tahitian language">

      </a>

      7.2% of the population whose age was 15 and older reported that they had no knowledge of any Polynesian language at the 2007 census, whereas 92.8% reported that they had some form of knowledge of at least one Polynesian language.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Airports">
       Airports
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Airports">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      There are four airports in the Marquesas, one each on the islands of Nuku Hiva, Ua Pu, Ua Huka, and Hiva Oa. The terrain of Tahuata is too irregular to allow for the construction of a landing strip without significant investment, and while the upland plateau of central Fatu Hiva is large enough to permit the construction of an airstrip, the island's minuscule population makes such an exercise of dubious benefit.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Telecommunications">
       Telecommunications
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

       </span>
      </span>

     <p>
      The Marquesas are served by telephone as well as by radio and television, mainly from
      <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">
       Tahiti
      </a>
      . Recent additions include the "
      <a href="/wiki/Vini" title="Vini">
       Vini
      </a>
      " a mobile phone service that, in about 6 years, has expanded to cover most of the populated islands. There also is "
      <a href="/wiki/Mana" title="Mana">
       Mana
      </a>
      ", an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_server" title="Internet server">
       internet server
      </a>
      with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DSL" title="DSL">
       DSL
      </a>
      broadband that is expanding with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wifi" title="Wifi">
       Wifi
      </a>
      stations too.
     </p>

     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Marquesan_chiefess_in_tapa_garments_with_tapa_parasol.jpg">

     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marquesan_culture" title="Marquesan culture">
       Marquesan culture
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The Marquesas Islands were once a major center of eastern
      <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">
       Polynesian
      </a>
      civilization.
      <a href="/wiki/Wood_carving_in_the_Marquesas_Islands" title="Wood carving in the Marquesas Islands">
       Wooden
      </a>
      and stone crafts and tattooing are common practices among the locals.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biology">
       Biology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Biology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">
       ecosystem
      </a>
      of the Marquesas has been devastated in some areas by the activities of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feral" title="Feral">
       feral
      </a>
      livestock. As a first step in preserving what remains, the
      <a href="/wiki/Marquesan_Nature_Reserves" title="Marquesan Nature Reserves">
       Marquesan Nature Reserves
      </a>
      were created in 1992.
     </p>

       Fauna of the Marquesas Islands

      and
      <a href="/wiki/Flora_of_the_Marquesas_Islands" title="Flora of the Marquesas Islands">
       Flora of the Marquesas Islands
      </a>
     </div>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_Western_culture">
       In Western culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquesas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: In Western culture">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <ul>
      <li>
       In the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Gilligan%27s_Island#Island" title="Gilligan's Island">
         Gilligan's Island
        </a>
       </i>
       episode "X Marks the Spot", the Professor gives coordinates for the castaways' imaginary island that would put it in the outer fringes of the Marquesas group.
      </li>
      <li>
       Famous French painter
       <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gauguin" title="Paul Gauguin">
        Paul Gauguin
       </a>
       and Belgian singer
       <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Brel" title="Jacques Brel">
        Jacques Brel
       </a>
       spent the last years of their lives in the Marquesas, and are buried there. Brel composed a song, "Les Marquises", about the Marquesas Islands, his last home.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       The Marquesas inspired American novelist
       <a href="/wiki/Herman_Melville" title="Herman Melville">
        Herman Melville
       </a>
       , whose experiences in the Marquesas formed the basis for his novel
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Typee" title="Typee">
         Typee
        </a>
       </i>
       . (Despite some sources,
       <a href="/wiki/Omoo" title="Omoo">
        Omoo
       </a>
       is based in the
       <a href="/wiki/Society_Islands" title="Society Islands">
        Society Islands
       </a>
       , not in the Marquesas.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       )
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Robert_Louis_Stevenson" title="Robert Louis Stevenson">
        Robert Louis Stevenson
       </a>
       visited the Marquesas in 1888, and wrote about his experiences and impressions there in 1900, in a book called
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Robert_Louis_Stevenson#Journey_to_the_Pacific" title="Robert Louis Stevenson">
         In the South Seas
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         20
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frederick_O%27Brien&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frederick O'Brien (page does not exist)">
        Frederick O'Brien
       </a>
       wrote the 1919 book
       <i>
        White Shadows in the South Seas
       </i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         21
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       based on his experiences in the Marquesas. This book was also adapted into an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MGM" title="MGM">
        MGM
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/White_Shadows_in_the_South_Seas" title="White Shadows in the South Seas">
        film
       </a>
       of 1928.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Thor_Heyerdahl" title="Thor Heyerdahl">
        Thor Heyerdahl
       </a>
       wrote his book
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Fatu_Hiva_(book)" title="Fatu Hiva (book)">
         Fatu Hiva
        </a>
       </i>
       during a year-long stay on the island.
      </li>
      <li>
       The island group is mentioned in passing in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crosby,_Stills_%26_Nash_(and_Young)" title="Crosby, Stills &amp; Nash (and Young)">
        Crosby, Stills &amp; Nash
       </a>
       song "
       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Cross_(Crosby,_Stills_and_Nash_song)" title="Southern Cross (Crosby, Stills and Nash song)">
        Southern Cross
       </a>
       ": "off the wind on this heading lie the Marquesas".
      </li>
      <li>
       The Marquesas Islands temporarily received an international spotlight in the United States when the reality TV show
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Marquesas" title="Survivor: Marquesas">
         Survivor: Marquesas
        </a>
       </i>
       was filmed on
       <a href="/wiki/Nuku_Hiva" title="Nuku Hiva">
        Nuku Hiva
       </a>
       . It was the fourth installment of the TV series
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Survivor_(US_TV_series)" title="Survivor (US TV series)">
         Survivor
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       In the book
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/In_the_Heart_of_the_Sea" title="In the Heart of the Sea">
         In the Heart of the Sea
        </a>
       </i>
       , it is detailed that the Marquesas were the closest land to where the whaleship
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Essex_(whaleship)" title="Essex (whaleship)">
         Essex
        </a>
       </i>
       had been sunk by a whale. However the crew reportedly feared rumours of
       <a href="/wiki/Cannibalism" title="Cannibalism">
        cannibalism
       </a>
       , war, and ritualized homosexuality on the islands, and instead chose to make the much longer voyage to South America, which resulted in the deaths of most of them.
      </li>
      <li>
       The Marquesas Islands feature extensively as a major setting in the book series
       <i>
        The Virtual War
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         22
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       The books call the islands
       <i>
        The Isles of Hiva
       </i>
       , which are supposedly the only uncontaminated lands left after a nuclear apocalypse. Most of the second novel takes place on Nuku Hiva, and part of the last novel takes place on Hiva Oa. Cannibalism is also a major element, and a character feels that he is in tune with Nuku Hiva's "spirits".
      </li>
      <li>
       The Wisconsin city
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Markesan" title="Markesan">
        Markesan
       </a>
       indirectly took its name from the Marquesas Islands. The city was named
       <i>
        Granville
       </i>
       from 1849 to 1854. The city had to change its name due to complications with the U.S. Postal Service. The name
       <i>
        Markesan
       </i>
       was submitted by a citizen who said the newly discovered Marquesas Islands influenced the name.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       In Aldous Huxley's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Brave_New_World" title="Brave New World">
         Brave New World
        </a>
       </i>
       (1932), the Marquesas Islands are a place of exile for those who think for themselves and are considered dangerous to the World State. Mustapha Mond presents this location to Helmholtz Watson as an option for banishment.
      </li>
      <li>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Jean-Luc_Godard" title="Jean-Luc Godard">
        Jean-Luc Godard
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Goodbye_to_Language" title="Goodbye to Language">
         Goodbye to Language
        </a>
       </i>
       (2014), a voice-over describes the dog Roxy as "dreaming of the Marquesas".
      </li>
     </ul>
