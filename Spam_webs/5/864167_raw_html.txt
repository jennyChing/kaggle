         <p>
          <a href="http://practicalfrugality.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/World_Nutella_Day_Final_m-300x207.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1614 aligncenter" height="207" src="http://practicalfrugality.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/World_Nutella_Day_Final_m-300x207.jpg" title="World Nutella Day" width="300"/>
          </a>
          Today is World
          <a href="http://www.nutellausa.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.nutellausa.com/', 'Nutella']);">
           Nutella
          </a>
          Day, so I thought Id try a recipe for Nutella Banana Chocolate Chip Muffins for Breakfast. The original recipe I have had for years, uses Peanut Butter, but as I mentioned in my
          <a href="http://whenisdinner.com/no-bake-chocolate-oatmeal-cookies/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://whenisdinner.com/no-bake-chocolate-oatmeal-cookies/', 'No Bake Chocolate Oatmeal Cookies Recipe']);">
           No Bake Chocolate Oatmeal Cookies Recipe
          </a>
          , you can pretty muchsubstituteNutella in many recipes that call for Peanut Butter.
          <span id="more-1613">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://practicalfrugality.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Nutella-Banana-Choc-Chip-Muffin.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1615" height="413" src="http://practicalfrugality.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Nutella-Banana-Choc-Chip-Muffin.jpg" title="Nutella Banana Choc Chip Muffin" width="550"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Joshua is home sick today, and I was just throwing this together, and forgot that I needed to be taking pictures of all the steps, my preferred way to share recipes. Instead, I only have one picture of the final product, as seen above. But hey, I did remember to slice one open so you could see the insides
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://practicalfrugality.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are the ingredients I use:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           1/4 cup Butter (salted)
          </li>
          <li>
           1/2 cup sugar
          </li>
          <li>
           2 eggs
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tsp Vanilla Extract
          </li>
          <li>
           1/2 cup milk
          </li>
          <li>
           3 bananas  you can mash them up, youll be blending them into the batter
          </li>
          <li>
           2 cups self rising flour
          </li>
          <li>
           1 cup chocolate chips
          </li>
          <li>
           2 tablespoons nutella (Original recipe called for Peanut Butter. Can also use Sunbutter, etc to meet your dietary needs).
          </li>
         </ul>
         <div>
          This is how I put it all together:
         </div>
         <div>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Preheat your oven to 375
           </li>
           <li>
            Place your muffin liners in the cupcake/muffin tin
           </li>
           <li>
            Melt your butter  I prefer to use a saucepan on the stovetop
           </li>
           <li>
            Mix your Sugar and eggs, then add in the butter, vanilla, milk, nutella, and bananas one at a time until its smooth and creamy. *Note: Some people LIKE banana chunks in their muffins. That is NOT me, so I blend mine in. If that IS you, you can wait and add the chunks post blending. Yall know I am picky
            <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://practicalfrugality.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
           </li>
           <li>
            Slowly add in the Flour, then the wait and add the chocolate chips at the very end. DO NOT use the blender on those, just a quick spin or two with a spoon will add them in.
           </li>
           <li>
            I use an ice cream scoop to transfer my batter from the bowl to the muffin liners.
           </li>
           <li>
            Bake for about 20 mins at 375. If they arent quite done, bake a few more mins until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div>
           <strong>
            On a side note
           </strong>
           : For this recipe (and many others) you can add a drop of food coloring if youre making this around a holiday. A couple drops of red for Valentines, Green for St Patricks Day, etc. The kids get excited to find that, and it doesnt affect the recipe!
          </div>
         </div>
