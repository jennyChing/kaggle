                 The nebula glows with the light of the small white-dwarf star left of center, all that remains of a star once the size of our own sun.
                </p>
               </span>
              </div>
             </figcaption>
            </div>

                </div>

              <div class="media__caption--text">
               <span class="media__caption--title" itemprop="headline">
                Mercury Bathed in Sunlight
               </span>
               <span itemprop="caption">
                <p dir="ltr">
                 The cratered surface of
                 <a href="https://solarsystem.nasa.gov/planets/profile.cfm?Object=Mercury">
                  Mercury
                 </a>
                 bathes in sunlight in this recently released view from NASA's
                 <a href="http://messenger.jhuapl.edu/">
                  Messenger spacecraft
                 </a>
                 . The spacecraft has taken more than 170,000 pictures of the planet since 2011.
                </p>
                <p dir="ltr">
                 The innermost planet of the solar system sports
                 <a href="http://www.nasa.gov/content/sunlit-side-of-the-planet-mercury/#.UnO3gJTSMVo">
                  a pock-marked surface
                 </a>
                 , mementos of earlier impacts that have left behind craters such as the one 75 miles (120 kilometers) wide, near the center of the picture.
                </p>
               </span>
              </div>
             </figcaption>

                  Chandra X-Ray Observatory
                 </a>

                </p>

                  Auroras

                 </a>
                 , taken October 26.
                </p>
                <p dir="ltr">
                 Northern lights are particularly common during the high point of the sun's 11-year solar cycle, when solar outbursts more often collide with Earth's magnetic field and upper atmosphere. (See
                 <a href="http://www.nasa.gov/multimedia/imagegallery/iotd.html#lowerAccordion-set1-slide4">
                  more pictures of the northern lights
