     <p>
      <b>
       Gerald Brom
      </b>
      (born March 9, 1965 in
      <a href="/wiki/Albany,_Georgia" title="Albany, Georgia">
       Albany, Georgia
      </a>
      ), known professionally as
      <b>
       Brom
      </b>
      , is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Gothic_art" title="Gothic art">
       gothic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">
       fantasy
      </a>
      artist and illustrator, known for his work in
      <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_game" title="Role-playing game">
       role-playing games
      </a>
      , novels, and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_books" title="Comic books">
       comics
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Brom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Brom was born March 9, 1965, in
      <a href="/wiki/Albany,_Georgia" title="Albany, Georgia">
       Albany, Georgia
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As the son of a
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">
       U.S. Army
      </a>
      pilot he spent much of his early years on the move, living in many countries such as
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japan
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
       Germany
      </a>
      (he graduated from
      <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_American_High_School" title="Frankfurt American High School">
       Frankfurt American High School
      </a>
      ), and U.S. states including
      <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">
       Alabama
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">
       Hawaii
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Brought up as a military dependent he was known by his last name only, and now signs his name as simply
      <b>
       Brom
      </b>
      : "I get that asked more than just about any other question. It's my real name, my last name. I got called Brom all the time as a kid, and it just stuck."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Brom has been drawing and painting since childhood, although he had never taken any formal art classes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      "I wouldn't exactly call myself self-taught, because I've always looked at the work of other artists and emulated what I liked about it. So you can say they taught me."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Brom cites the work of
      <a href="/wiki/Frank_Frazetta" title="Frank Frazetta">
       Frank Frazetta
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N.C._Wyeth" title="N.C. Wyeth">
       N.C. Wyeth
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Norman_Rockwell" title="Norman Rockwell">
       Norman Rockwell
      </a>
      as influences on his style: "Okay... Rockwell isn't the kind of inspiration most people expect from me, but he just painted things so well. To me it's not so much the genre but the way it's done, and you have to admire his technique."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Career">
       Career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Brom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      At the age of 20, Brom started working full-time as a commercial illustrator.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wizardsbio_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-wizardsbio-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      By age twenty-one, he had two national art representatives, and was doing work for such clients as
      <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">
       Coca-Cola
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">
       IBM
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">
       CNN
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Pictures" title="Columbia Pictures">
       Columbia Pictures
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wizardsbio_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-wizardsbio-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TSR,_Inc." title="TSR, Inc.">
       TSR, Inc.
      </a>
      hired Brom on full-time in 1989 at the age of 24.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Brom contributed to all of TSR's game and book lines, particularly the
      <a href="/wiki/Dark_Sun" title="Dark Sun">
       Dark Sun
      </a>
      setting: "I pretty much designed the look and feel of the Dark Sun campaign. I was doing paintings before they were even writing about the setting. I'd do a painting or a sketch, and the designers wrote those characters and ideas into the story. I was very involved in the development process. I've been fortunate to be involved in the development end of a lot of projects I've worked on, from role-playing games to computer games."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      According to Shannon Appelcline, Brom "contributed the unique illustrations for
      <i>
       Dark Sun
      </i>
      that helped to set it apart from the other TSR games with their more typical fantasy drawings".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-designers_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-designers-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       :26
      </sup>
      His paintings have been published in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collectible_card_games" title="Collectible card games">
       collectible card games
      </a>
      such as
      <a href="/wiki/Wizards_of_the_Coast" title="Wizards of the Coast">
       Wizards of the Coast
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Magic:_The_Gathering" title="Magic: The Gathering">
        Magic: The Gathering
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Last_Unicorn_Games" title="Last Unicorn Games">
       Last Unicorn Games
      </a>
      '
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Heresy:_Kingdom_Come" title="Heresy: Kingdom Come">
        Heresy: Kingdom Come
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SendaiBubble-Artists_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-SendaiBubble-Artists-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Brom's paintings, along with
      <a href="/wiki/Frank_Frazetta" title="Frank Frazetta">
       Frank Frazetta
      </a>
      's, were used in the development of the visual look of the game series
      <a href="/wiki/Warlords_(game_series)" title="Warlords (game series)">
       Warlords
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1993, after four years at TSR, Brom returned to the freelance market, still specializing in the darker side of the roleplaying game, card game, and comic book genres.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Shane_Lacy_Hensley" title="Shane Lacy Hensley">
       Shane Lacy Hensley
      </a>
      came up with the idea for the game
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Deadlands" title="Deadlands">
        Deadlands
       </a>
      </i>
      after he saw Brom's cover to
      <i>
       Necropolis: Atlanta
      </i>
      from
      <a href="/wiki/White_Wolf_Publishing" title="White Wolf Publishing">
       White Wolf
      </a>
      , and got Brom to do the cover for the initial release.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-designers_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-designers-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       :325
      </sup>
      His artwork also appeared on book covers from authors such as
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Moorcock" title="Michael Moorcock">
       Michael Moorcock
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Anne_McCaffrey" title="Anne McCaffrey">
       Anne McCaffrey
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Terry_Brooks" title="Terry Brooks">
       Terry Brooks
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Brom contributed conceptual work to computer games such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Heretic_II" title="Heretic II">
        Heretic II
       </a>
      </i>
      , and several top creature houses for films such as
      <a href="/wiki/Stan_Winston" title="Stan Winston">
       Stan Winston
      </a>
      Studios; he also co-created, art directed, and illustrated the
      <i>
       Dark Age
      </i>
      collectible card game.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dragon_.23264_2-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dragon_.23264-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He has since worked as a
      <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
       movie
      </a>
      concept artist, and created illustrations for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic" title="Comic">
       comics
      </a>
      (by
      <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">
       DC
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaos_Comics" title="Chaos Comics">
       Chaos
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics" title="Dark Horse Comics">
       Dark Horse
      </a>
      ) and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_game" title="Computer game">
       computer games
      </a>
      (for
      <a href="/wiki/Id_Software" title="Id Software">
       id Software
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Blizzard_Entertainment" title="Blizzard Entertainment">
       Blizzard
      </a>
