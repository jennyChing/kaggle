          <h3 style="text-align: center;">
           <strong>
            Five of the Best
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           At Cruise1st, we believe these are five of the best ships for babies and toddlers with exceptional facilities and services to keep the youngest members of the family happy.
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            Allure of the Seas
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           The largest ship on the seas (alongside sister ship, Oasis of the Seas) Royal Caribbeans
           <a href="http://www.cruise1st.co.uk/allure_of_the_seas.phtml" title="Allure of the Seas">
            Allure of the Seas
           </a>
           is comprehensively packed with features and amenities for all members of the family. For babies and toddlers, the Allure of the Seas pioneered the Royal Babies and Tots programme offering childcare services for 6 to 18 month old babies and 18 to 36 month old tots. The programme was a central reason for
           <a href="http://www.parents.com/american-baby-magazine/" target="_blank">
            American Baby magazine
           </a>
           naming Royal Caribbean the Best Baby Getaway of 2012.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://news.cruise1st.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Allure-11.jpg">
            <img alt="Allure-11" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4287" height="400" src="http://cdn4.cruise1stblog.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Allure-11.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            Norwegian Breakaway
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           When launching the Breakaway Class of ships, Norwegian Cruise Line overhauled their kids clubs and childcare programmes. Launching a new childcare programme for babies and toddlers aged 6 months to 3 years, called Guppies, with dedicated play areas and learning facilities. The complimentary service includes visits from Nickelodeon characters, child-friendly workout sessions and arty and crafty games. Kids ages 3+ can also attend late-night babysitting groups for an additional fee.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://news.cruise1st.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Norwegian_Breakaway_01.jpg">
            <img alt="Norwegian_Breakaway_01" class="alignnone wp-image-4127 size-large" height="527" src="http://cdn4.cruise1stblog.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Norwegian_Breakaway_01-1024x768.jpg" width="702"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            Disney Dream
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           Unsurprisingly, Disney Cruises focus heavily on keeping youngsters happy, comfortable and entertained at all times. The
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/It%27s_a_Small_World" target="_blank">
            Its a Small World
           </a>
           nursery (nowhere near as irritating as the ride of the same name) provides care and entertainment for babies and toddlers from 12 weeks to 3 years old. Babysitting services continue throughout port days as well as days at sea, so parents can enjoy a little alone time during the excursions. The Disney Dream also includes a dedicated pool area for babies and toddlers to enjoy the water in water-proof nappies.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://news.cruise1st.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/fur494353LARGE1.jpg">
            <img alt="Disney Cruise - Mickey" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5982" height="467" src="http://cdn4.cruise1stblog.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/fur494353LARGE1.jpg" width="702"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            P&amp;O Ventura
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           Whilst some members of the P&amp;O fleet (Arcadia, Adonia and Oriana) are reserved for adults, the rest of the ships like to indulge babies and toddlers  in particular the P&amp;O Ventura. There are a number of dedicated spaces for parents to play with their babies and toddlers throughout the day. At night, the ship provides Night Nursery  a free service where parents can leave their child between 6pm and 2am, giving you the chance to enjoy peaceful dinners and romantic strolls on the top deck.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://news.cruise1st.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/PO-Ventura-Paul-1024x681.jpg">
            <img alt="PO-Ventura-Paul-1024x681" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4315" height="466" src="http://cdn2.cruise1stblog.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/PO-Ventura-Paul-1024x681.jpg" width="702"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            Queen Mary 2
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           The daily Childrens Tea served in the Chefs Galley is just one reason why your toddler will love cruising aboard Cunards
           <a href="http://www.cruise1st.co.uk/ship/cunard-queen-mary-2" title="Cunard's Queen Mary 2">
            Queen Mary 2
           </a>
           . With a menu packed full of kids favourites including pizza and baked beans as well as baby food upon request  getting the kids to finish their dinner wont be a problem. The night time nursery is stocked with cribs and small beds for babies and toddlers to sleep in as their parents go for their dinner in one of the ships fantastic restaurants.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://news.cruise1st.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/far-flung-cruise-destinations.jpg">
            <img alt="Queen Mary 2 cruise ship in Sydney. Sourced from Flickr creative commons, credit: Sacha Fernandez" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6886" height="468" src="http://cdn3.cruise1stblog.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/far-flung-cruise-destinations.jpg" width="702"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h3 style="text-align: center;">
           <strong>
            Tips
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Book dinner seatings well in advance so you dont get stuck with the last sitting and cranky children.
           </li>
           <li>
            Book a balcony cabin so you can enjoy the sea air whilst keeping an eye on a sleepy baby in the cabin.
           </li>
           <li>
            Consider a ship with 24 hour buffet service  especially if you have fussy eater for a child (or a spouse!)
           </li>
           <li>
            Look for a ship with free laundry service, for obvious reasons.
           </li>
           <li>
            Have a look on TripAdvisor for fun kid-friendly activities in the port cities youre planning on visiting.
           </li>
           <li>
            Bring the smallest pushchair which supports your children  this could save valuable cabin space.
           </li>
           <li>
            Pack a baby emergency kit  the ships shops may not sell the medicines your young ones require.
           </li>
           <li>
            Explore the ship for empty or quiet lounges so toddlers can stretch their legs and have a little explore and adventure.
           </li>
           <li>
            Try to avoid booking excursions which include long bus journeys  fellow travellers will thank you.
           </li>
          </ul>
