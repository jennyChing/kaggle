       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Dave Silverman Goes on FOX News to Discuss Obamas Attack on Christianity
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2012-06-25T21:51:23+00:00">
         June 25, 2012
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://www.patheos.com/blogs/friendlyatheist/author/hemant-mehta/" rel="author" title="Hemant Mehta">
           Hemant Mehta
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://www.patheos.com/blogs/friendlyatheist/2012/06/25/dave-silverman-goes-on-fox-news-to-discuss-obamas-attack-on-christianity/#comments">
          <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="62270 http://www.patheos.com/blogs/friendlyatheist/?p=62270">
           75 Comments
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Earlier tonight, American Atheists
         <strong>
          Dave Silverman
         </strong>
         appeared on
         <em>
          The OReilly Factor
         </em>
         (with guest host
         <strong>
          Laura Ingraham
         </strong>
         ) to discuss how religion has influenced Obamas policies as well as the
         <a href="http://www.patheos.com/blogs/friendlyatheist/2012/06/21/atheists-are-still-the-most-unelectable-minority-group-in-america/">
          latest Gallup poll
         </a>
         showing how only about half of Americans would vote for a qualified atheist for President.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mediaite
         <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.mediaite.com/tv/on-oreilly-pastor-jeffress-clashes-with-atheist-over-obama-admins-attack-on-religion/" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
          has the clip
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="421" marginheight="0" marginwidth="0" scrolling="no" src="http://videos.mediaite.com/embed/player/?content=9BN1Y1241YQ75ZBF&amp;content_type=content_item&amp;layout=&amp;playlist_cid=&amp;media_type=video&amp;widget_type_cid=svp&amp;read_more=1" width="420">
         </iframe>
        </center>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Silverman called the trend a progression towards tolerance, or the de-bigoting of America. It doesnt mean were going to become an anti-religious nation, he said. But you would prefer that, Ingraham responded, which Silverman refuted, saying we need to get to a point where people can be who they are and not be judged simply on that fact.
         </p>
         <p>
          
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Because hes an atheist, Ingraham said of Silverman, he doesnt understand. He doesnt understand that people of faith feel like second-class citizens under this administration.
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Apparently the fact that shows like
         <em>
          Modern Family
         </em>
         are popular is evidence that our country is anti-Christian.  (Gay families are
         <em>
          happy
         </em>
         ?! YOURE MAKING BABY JESUS CRY!)  Also anti-Christian?  Obama wanting employers to provide employees with healthcare that includes contraception services.  Doesnt Obama realize that giving other people access to birth control prevents Christians from controlling them?!
        </p>
        <p>
         Dave Silverman didnt make his rageface, but it was called for during the entire segment.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was an amazing demonstration of Christian privilege in action.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </p>
