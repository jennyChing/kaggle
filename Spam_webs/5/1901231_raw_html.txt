                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Cat pumpkin
                 </strong>
                 <br/>
                 1. Cut out the pumpkin's mask from white stiffened felt.
                </p>
                <p>
                 2. Smear it with glue and sprinkle it with white glitter. Let dry and shake off excess. Before carving, position and tape eyesto the pumpkin so they fit under the mask's eyes.
                </p>
                <p>
                 3. Parents: Carve the face of a carvable artificial white pumpkin ($13; Michaels stores) with a box cutter.
                </p>
                <p>
                 4. Paint the pumpkin bright white and the stem silver. Let dry.
                </p>
                <p>
                 5. Hot-glue the mask on the pumpkin over the eye holes.
                </p>

                 6. Hot-glue six 3-1/2-inch iridescent chenille pipe cleaners, three on each side of the nose.
                </p>
                <p>
                 7. With a sharpened pencil, punch a hole 1 inch from either side of the stem. Then punch another hole 1-1/2 inches from the first holes.
                </p>
                <p>
                 8. Cut two 4-inch pieces from a giant chenille pipe cleaner. Bend in half and stick each end in the holes to make the ears.
                </p>
                <p>
                 9. Punch a hole in the back of the pumpkin, about 1-1/2 inches from the bottom centerand stick the rest of the giant white pipe cleaner in it for the tail.
                </p>

                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Wicked-Witch Pudding Cups
                 </strong>
                 <br/>
                 <em>
                  Serves: 6
                 </em>
                </p>
                <p>
                 What your need:
                 <br/>
                 2 packages black licorice laces (about fourteen 36-inch laces)
                 <br/>
                 Black permanent marker
                 <br/>
                 1 package 6 vanilla-flavored pudding snacks
                 <br/>
                 Neon-green food coloring
                 <br/>
                 Six 3-inch round chocolate cookies
                 <br/>
                 6 chocolate sugar cones
                 <br/>
                 Brown decorating icing
                </p>

                  </p>

               <div class="field-content">
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Witch Lanterns
                 </strong>
                 <br/>
                 What you need:
                 <br/>
                 Half-gallon plastic milk or juice jug (empty and clean)
                 <br/>
                 Mod Podge gloss finish (available at crafts stores; may be substituted by mixing a few teaspoons water with 1/3 cup craft glue)
                 <br/>
                 4 sheets lime-green tissue paper, cut into 2- and 3-inch squares and rectangles
                 <br/>
                 Black permanent marker
                 <br/>
                 7-inch black plastic dessert plate
                 <br/>
                 Black raffia ribbon
                 <br/>
                 Cellophane tape
                 <br/>
                 Black poster board
                 <br/>
                 Stapler
                 <br/>
                 Hot-glue gun and glue sticks
                 <br/>
                 Black ribbon
                 <br/>
                 Small flashlight or LED battery-powered tap light
                </p>
                <h5>
                 For the witch head:
                </h5>
                <ol>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   Using a kitchen knife, cut a 3-inch square opening below the jug handle, just above the bottom.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   With the Mod Podge and tissue paper, decoupage the entire outside of the jug and let dry. No need for perfection! A little sloppy is good.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   use the permanent marker to draw on the face.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                </ol>
                <h5>
                 <strong>
                  For the witch hat and hair
                 </strong>
                 :
                </h5>
                <ol>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   Poke a hole in the center of the black plate and, using scissors, continue to cut an opening the size of the jug spout. This will be the brim.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   To make hair, loop the raffia into 12-inch sections, approximately 12 to 15 times. Turn the plate over, and tape the raffia bunch to the brim. Repeat until you have covered a little more than half of the plate.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   Place the brim over the spout and push until secure. Cut the loops and trim to even out.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   On the black poster board, draw a circle 14 inches in diameter. (You can trace around a large plate.) Cut out with scissors, and then cut in half.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   Fold one half to make into hat, and staple.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   Secure the top of the hat to the brim with hot glue.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   Cut a 14-inch piece of ribbon and glue around the base of the hat.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <p>
                   Place light inside the jug, through the cutout.
                  </p>
                 </li>
                </ol>
