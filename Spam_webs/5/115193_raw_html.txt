       <h2>
        <a href="http://foodlava.com/blog/19/cooking-for-two/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: Cooking for Two">
         Cooking for Two
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="post_byline">
        by
        <span class="byline_author">
         michelle
        </span>
        on
        <span class="byline_date">
         Mar 24, 2007 (Sat)
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         Since I got married nearly 3 years ago, I now cook almost everyday. In the first months, I discovered its hard work cooking for two! Fresh vegetables I bought perished before I get to use them and our fridge was filled with leftovers. It seems that I am not alone. I found books, articles and websites to help those who cook for two. In this post, I hope to share the numerous online resources on the topic.
        </p>
        <p>
         First up are books. These are the top three picks from Amazon on the subject:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Healthy-Cooking-Two-Just-You/dp/0875964486">
           Healthy Cooking for Two (or Just You): Low-Fat Recipes with Half the Fuss and Double the Taste
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Cooking-Two-Recipes-Special-Nights/dp/0060522593/ref=pd_bxgy_b_img_b/103-3748393-3135814">
           Cooking for Two: 120 Recipes for Every Day and Those Special Nights
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Quickies-Couples-Fast-Fresh-Recipes/dp/1581823479/ref=pd_sim_b_3/102-1698468-5160932">
           Quickies for Couples: Fast, Fresh Recipes for Two (Quickies, 1)
          </a>
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         I cant help but notice: Why do books on cooking for two have such long titles? Anyway, next up are recipe sites that have sections devoted to the topic:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.recipegoldmine.com/cooktwo/cooktwo.html">
           Recipe Goldmine
          </a>
          - The recipes here look easy to do. They are mostly soups, salads and side dishes.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://allrecipes.com/Recipes/Everyday-Cooking/Cooking-for-Two/ViewAll.aspx">
           All Recipes
          </a>
          - Over 500 recipes for two. Read up on the reviews and ratings for each recipe before trying them out yourself.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.recipezaar.com/recipes/number-of-servings,for-1-or-2">
           Recipezaar
          </a>
          - Scroll to the bottom of each recipe page, and find other recipes compatible to the one youve chosen.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         Finally, we go to some useful websites. The ones I like the most are
         <a href="http://lifehacker.com/">
          Lifehacker
         </a>
         and the
         <a href="http://www.once-a-month-cookingworld.com/">
          OAMC site
         </a>
         .
         <a href="http://lifehacker.com/software/getting-to-done/getting-to-done-cooking-for-two-158032.php">
          Lifehacker
         </a>
         has collated a lot of great tips from its readers on the topic of cooking for two. I hope you will find them as interesting as I did.
        </p>
        <p>
         On the OAMC site you will learn about Once A Month Cooking. Basically, you cook big batches and freeze it in small portions. Because of this, OAMC is also called Freezer Cooking. It makes a lot of sense since preparation and clean up does take up a lot of time. With OAMC, most of time you just need to take the portion needed - say for two, reheat it and youre done with dinner. I have yet to try OAMC but I found their step by step guide quite useful. It will help us save time and money for groceries. Ordinarily, Ill go to the grocery every week. But following their tips, I can reduce the frequency to once a month.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres also a
         <a href="http://www.recipezaar.com/cookbook.php?bookid=31279">
          vegetarian OAMC plan
         </a>
         in Recipezaar. I think Ill find this one really useful, since I find myself preparing less meat dishes and cooking more vegetables. I love that clean up after preparing vegetarian dishes is less of a hassle. I dont have to go crazy sterilizing everything I used, for fear of salmonella and other malodors left on my kitchenware.
        </p>
        <p>
         I hope you like the resources I posted here. If you have more you can leave a link to them in the comments below.
        </p>
