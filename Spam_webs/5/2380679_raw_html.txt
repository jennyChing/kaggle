     <p>
      <b>
       Pixley Falls State Park
      </b>
      is a 375-acre (1.52km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      )
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_New_York_state_parks" title="List of New York state parks">
       New York state park
      </a>
      located in the town of
      <a href="/wiki/Boonville,_New_York" title="Boonville, New York">
       Boonville
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Oneida_County,_New_York" title="Oneida County, New York">
       Oneida County
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">
       New York
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USA" title="USA">
       USA
      </a>
      . The park is located on
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Route_46" title="New York State Route 46">
       New York State Route 46
      </a>
      and is 18 miles (29km) north of
      <a href="/wiki/Rome,_New_York" title="Rome, New York">
       Rome
      </a>
      and six miles (9.7km) miles southwest of
      <a href="/wiki/Boonville_(village),_New_York" title="Boonville (village), New York">
       Boonville
      </a>
      , near the community of Hurlbutville.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">
       Facilities
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pixley_Falls_State_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Facilities">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Open all year, the park gets its name from a 50-foot (15m) waterfall, accessible via a short hiking trail. The park offers picnicking, a nature trail,
      <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">
       hiking
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">
       fishing
      </a>
      , seasonal
      <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">
       hunting
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cross_country_skiing" title="Cross country skiing">
       cross country skiing
      </a>
      . The park has steep wooded hills, a mountain
      <a href="/wiki/Trout" title="Trout">
       trout
      </a>
      stream, and a trail that runs along the ruins of the 19th-century
      <a href="/wiki/Black_River_Canal" title="Black River Canal">
       Black River Canal
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PFSP_main_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-PFSP_main-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The park became a day-use park in 2010, at which time camping was no longer permitted.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
