       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Bic Runga Is Your Spirit Animal and That Time Neil Finn Took Requests
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="posted-on">
          <a href="http://fromtheoutlands.com/2014/09/bic-runga-is-your-spirit-animal-and-that-time-neil-finn-took-requests/" rel="bookmark" title="5:23 am">
           <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2014-09-21T05:23:31+00:00">
            September 21, 2014
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="comments-link">
          <a href="http://fromtheoutlands.com/2014/09/bic-runga-is-your-spirit-animal-and-that-time-neil-finn-took-requests/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
       </header>
       <div class="clearfix entry-content">
        <p>
         Bic Runga, Neil Finn and the surprise Finn (Tim!) took to the stage last night at Mount Edens Powerstation for the third night of the five date Dizzy Heights New Zealand Tour.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kicking off in a subtle way, the ever-stunning Bic Runga took the stage, being joined later in her set by Neil Finn himself (eek!) who accompanied her on the piano. Having written many of the piano parts for the songs on Bics third studio album, Birds, the pairing was effortless and natural.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Neil and Bic" class="size-medium wp-image-33 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://fromtheoutlands.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/10599605_10152444369737017_7073033036277016186_n-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its easy to see how they found each other  although both artists have different sounds, I think they are lyrically similar. Theyre both incredibly masterful at writing approachable and relatable lyrics and their imagery is fabulous.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was just lovely to see and while Bic seemed nervous (who wouldnt be Neil Finn!), she was beautifully pitched and had a faultless performance. She played the classics that everybody in the room knew and adored, but part of me did wish she had something new for me to relate to Its not a terrible problem to have, lets face it. Rumour has it though that Bic is working on her fifth studio album, which, as an angst-y 22 year old who prefers lifes intricacies explained to her in dream like verse, I for one am relieved to hear.
        </p>
        <p>
         After Bics rather lovely set and a slightly lengthy interval, Neil took the stage for a second time, remaining there for around two and a half hours(!).
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="10713000_10152445074847017_8938687424597898866_n" class="size-medium wp-image-35 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://fromtheoutlands.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/10713000_10152445074847017_8938687424597898866_n-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Kicking off with Distant Sun and going on to play a mix of songsfrom his new album Dizzy Heights, released in February of this year, and old favourites from his Crowded House/Split Enz/other solo endeavour days, the show was balanced incredibly well. Finn was obviously excited to be there, welcoming us all to 
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          the alternative election special. No Mike Hosking!
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         a statement met with a rather large cheer from a crowd obviously suffering from Hosking-itus (UGH can that man please learn how to be neutral during a debate??!).
        </p>
        <p>
         I still cant believe how excited Finn is about what he does. Bouncing around the stage like a man in his 20s, his passion for performing was evident and, as a musician myself, SO inspiring. That man. Sheesh.
        </p>
        <p>
         And then, the surprise Finn! Invited on stage in such a modest and endearing fashion  heres my brother Tim, I was in bits. Tim and Neil! One stage? DREAM.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="10676200_10152444366217017_1822730108170042121_n" class="size-medium wp-image-34 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://fromtheoutlands.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/10676200_10152444366217017_1822730108170042121_n-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Speaking of the people on stage, a special mention should be made for the rest of the band. It was great to see a band of young up and coming talent  along with Neils wife Sharon on base guitar (who killed me with her coolness and rad 60s-esque style), Neil gathered together Alistair Deverick (Ruby Suns), Andrew Everding (an import from the States), Lisa Tomlins, or as Neil called her She of the Beautiful Voice (Rhombus), and Jesse Sheehan (remember his song By Your Side released in 2012? Guy has got GAME). Such a talented and lovely bunch of people that were simply a joy to watch play.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="10444598_10152445075297017_4988047975413869339_n" class="size-medium wp-image-32 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://fromtheoutlands.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/10444598_10152445075297017_4988047975413869339_n-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I think the best way to describe the show was as a jam session between 300 people  I mean, NEIL FINN opened up to requests. And there were plenty The most infecting thing about Neils music is that theres a sense of ownership thats shared with the crowd. These are our songs and for many at the show last night, part of the description of their identity. Hell, theyre definitely part of mine.
        </p>
        <p>
         Although I would have loved to see my personal favourite Pour le Monde being given a whirl, I was happy with a rousing rendition of Message to My Girl and an everlasting sense of man I want to do that.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ecstatic as always,
        </p>
        <p>
         S
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
