          <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
           <h2 class="graf--h2 graf--first" id="title" name="title">
            College is a ranking mechanism
           </h2>
           <h4 class="graf--h4 graf--last" id="subtitle" name="subtitle">
            A tool to place people in limited roles
           </h4>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="section-captionContent u-ignoreBlock">
        </div>
       </section>
       <section class=" section--body" name="1a03">
        <div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn">
         <hr class="section-divider"/>
        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <p class="graf--p graf--first" id="d047" name="d047">
           The industrial age. Factories full of assembly lines. Assembly lines full of average people, doing average work, creating average products for the average consumer. If the industrial age taught us anything its that good jobs are in limited quantities.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="02b9" name="02b9">
           Big companies, the ones youre trained to want to work for, have limited budgets. Theres only one CEO. Theres only one manager. Theres only one head of department.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p graf--last" id="8de0" name="8de0">
           Of course, the problem with this thinking starts far earlier.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </section>
       <section class=" section--body" name="b5ba">
        <div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn">
         <hr class="section-divider"/>
        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <p class="graf--p graf--first" id="7918" name="7918">
           As weve seen, ranking starts unfairly early. Standardized testing in elementary school. If you dont get into the 4th grade advanced reading class, you wont get into it in 5th grade. If you dont get in in 5th grade, you certainly wont in middle school. Come high school youll be so far behind you shouldnt even bother. Then the fear starts.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p graf--last" id="bcf5" name="bcf5">
           The industrialized system of education doesnt allow for the slow-starter to grow and find their passion. Instead, its easier to tell them they arent good at something. Its easier to raise kids to meet a lowered average than it is to raise the average. In this system its far too easy for natural gifts to go unnoticed by those who arent focused on finding and amplifying them.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </section>
       <section class=" section--body" name="d3c0">
        <div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn">
         <hr class="section-divider"/>
        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <p class="graf--p graf--first" id="acf4" name="acf4">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            In my eyes, teachers are approaching their craft like this:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol class="postList">
           <li class="graf--li" id="f4f8" name="f4f8">
            Get kids to behave
           </li>
           <li class="graf--li" id="1609" name="1609">
            Fill them full of techniques and information
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p class="graf--p" id="d556" name="d556">
           Its almost as if a teacher can get them to behave, be obedient, walk in single file lines, raise their hand before speaking
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            then fill them full of technique
           </em>
           suddenly passion for a topic will come bursting through the soil!
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="b563" name="b563">
           Unfortunately, that just isnt how it works. Rarely does a studenta young one at thatsay,
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            I really love this topic! Is there a
           </em>
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
             text book
            </em>
           </strong>
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            I can read?
           </em>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p graf--last" id="6a8d" name="6a8d">
           I believe passion comes from success. Solve a difficult problem, get praise, and maybe youll want to do it again. Do something better than your friends and you might get hooked on that feeling.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </section>
       <section class=" section--body" name="aab5">
        <div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn">
         <hr class="section-divider"/>
        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <p class="graf--p graf--first" id="ad00" name="ad00">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            Things I want my sons to know while they fight through the industrial system of education:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol class="postList">
           <li class="graf--li" id="11d2" name="11d2">
            Grades are an illusion
           </li>
           <li class="graf--li" id="e6ca" name="e6ca">
            Your passions are reality
           </li>
           <li class="graf--li" id="7d81" name="7d81">
            Your work is worth more than trying to match an answer key
           </li>
           <li class="graf--li" id="953a" name="953a">
            Persistence in the face of opposition is powerful
           </li>
           <li class="graf--li" id="c3f8" name="c3f8">
            Fitting in is a short-term strategy
           </li>
           <li class="graf--li" id="3293" name="3293">
            Standing out pays off in the long run
           </li>
           <li class="graf--li graf--last" id="2557" name="2557">
            If you care deeply enough about something to be criticized for it, your work is done for the day. Go home.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </div>
        </div>
       </section>
       <section class=" section--body" name="94a6">
        <div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn">
         <hr class="section-divider"/>
        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <p class="graf--p graf--first" id="8de9" name="8de9">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            What I want the teachers teaching my sons to know:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol class="postList">
           <li class="graf--li" id="bbb1" name="bbb1">
            Teach my sons how to lead
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p class="graf--p" id="58f8" name="58f8">
           Leadership is the most important skill in the modern revolution. In this revolution, nothing happens until you step up and simply begin.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="5e83" name="5e83">
           2. Help them learn how to solve interesting problems
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p graf--last" id="9852" name="9852">
           As the world changes faster than ever before, people who solve problems with yesterdays instructions will be rewarded far less. The value is in the individual who can draw a new map, who can solve a problem that didnt exist yesterday.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </section>
       <section class=" section--body section--last" name="3ce7">
        <div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn">
         <hr class="section-divider"/>
        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <p class="graf--p graf--first" id="0c58" name="0c58">
           Last but not least, if you ever send my sons home with a report card that reads,
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
             Lacks determination and interest.
            </em>
           </strong>
           Im not going to talk to my sons about it. Im going to talk to you. Because whos fault is that? Whos fault is it that theyre not interested in your teaching? It doesnt matter if the student is 6 or 16.
