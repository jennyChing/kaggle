         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Cases-Crafts-for-Kids-Image-2.jpg">
           <img alt="Monster Pencil Cases Crafts for Kids" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18276" height="480" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Cases-Crafts-for-Kids-Image-2.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The new school year is just around the corner and personalizing your childs back to school gear is a great way to make the first day of school more special for them. My brain has been working overtime coming up with fun back to school crafts for kids and teachers gifts, after Michaels invited me to participate in their First Day of School Blogger Challenge. Michaels provided me with a gift card to gather supplies. Then I quickly got busy looking for inspiration online, and in craft books and magazines. Laura and I will be sharing quite a few back to school inspired crafts over the next couple of weeks, in fact we already got started with these
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/2013/07/back-to-school-crafts-pipe-cleaner-pencil-toppers.html" target="_blank">
           Pipe Cleaner Pencil Toppers
          </a>
          and this fun
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/2013/07/back-to-school-teacher-gifts-pencil-flowers-craft.html" target="_blank">
           Pencil Flower Bouquet
          </a>
          teacher gift. For the Michaels First Day of School Blogger Challenge we are sharing these adorable Monster Pencil Cases.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case.jpg">
           <img alt="Monster Pencil Case Craft for Kids" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18262" height="480" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Kids can practice their sewing skills making these pencil cases, and theyre not only cute but also very practical. Pencils, crayons and other school supplies will fit perfectly into this personalized pencil case.
         </p>
         <p>
          All of the supplies for these pencil cases came from Michaels craft store. Its a very inexpensive project that the kids are sure to have a lot of fun making.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Supplies Youll Need:
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          large 12 in x 18 in piece of Creatology Craft Foam
          <br/>
          yarn in a contrasting color (youll want yarn thats not overly fluffy)
          <br/>
          child safe plastic needle (found in the embroidery section at Michaels)
          <br/>
          large googly eyes
          <br/>
          sticky back double sided Velcro (we used 3 1/2 inch strips but a couple of dots would be fine)
          <br/>
          craft glue
          <br/>
          scissors
          <br/>
          ruler
         </p>
         <p>
          1. Begin by cutting the foam sheet in half so that you end up with two pieces that are 9 in by 6 in.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Step-One.jpg">
           <img alt="Monster Pencil Case Step One" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18263" height="480" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Step-One.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          2. Measure about 4 1/2 inches from the bottom of the foam sheet, lengthwise. Fold that section up, forming the bottom of the pencil case.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Step-Two.jpg">
           <img alt="Monster Pencil Case Step 2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18266" height="480" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Step-Two.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          3. Thread a long piece of yarn onto the needle and sew both 6 inch sides together using an overcast stitch. This will create the pencil case pouch. The top flap will be folded down and secured later. To make the overcast stitch, start on one side of the edge you want to finish. Make a series of equal-spaced and diagonal stitches that loop around the edge of the foam, tying off to secure at each end. Youll want to double knot the ends to make sure it is secure.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Step-3.jpg">
           <img alt="Monster Pencil Case Step 3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18267" height="480" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Step-3.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          4. If you want to give your monster some teeth, simply do a large zig zag stitch along the bottom of the foam flap that folds down over the top of the case.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Crafts-for-Kids.jpg">
           <img alt="Monster Pencil Case Crafts for Kids" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18260" height="480" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Crafts-for-Kids.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          5. Attach the sticky back Velcro to the underside of the top flap and the bottom of the pencil case, as shown in the picture below. This will ensure that all those pencils and crayons dont fall out.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Step-4.jpg">
           <img alt="Monster Pencil Case Step 4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18268" height="480" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Monster-Pencil-Case-Step-4.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          6. Glue on googly eyes and fill with pencils!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/image001-1.jpg">
           <img alt="Michaels Back to School Adventure" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18271" height="336" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/image001-1.jpg" width="624"/>
          </a>
         </p>
