       <p>
        This Ferrari 458 Italia signals the arrival of a brand new generation of V8-engined Ferrari berlinettas. Every Ferrari is the result of an uncompromising design approach that integrates styling and aerodynamic requirements. The Pininfarina design features compact, aerodynamic lines, underscoring the concepts of performance-oriented efficiency that inspired the project. The nose features a single opening for the front grille and side air intakes, with aerodynamic sections and profiles designed to direct air to the coolant radiators and the new flat underbody. The nose also sports small aeroelastic winglets which generate downforce and, as speed rises, deform to reduce the section of the radiator intake and cut drag. The result is a highly innovative design, characterized by cutting-edge technologies perfected for use on a road-going car. Two of these innovations for production cars: the electronic differential (E-Diff) initially developed by Ferrari for its F1 single-seaters and designed to make the most of the engines torque to optimize traction, and the handily placed steering wheel-mounted rotary switch (better known to the Formula 1 drivers as the manettino) which directly controls the integrated systems governing vehicle dynamics. On the track, the E-Diff guarantees maximum grip out of bends, eliminating wheel spin. On the road it is a formidable technological refinement that improves roadholding. This 2010 Ferrari 458 Italia is equipped with Ferraris all-new 274.5 cubic inch V8 motor which produces 570hp and goes 0-60Mph in less than 4 seconds! It is also capable of speeds over 196Mph. The Ferrari 458s engines improvement in terms of performance, weight and reduction of overall dimensions is the result of applying Ferraris wealth of F1 experience to this fine sportscar. The F1 transmission in this beautiful exotic is state of the art, introducing a number of important modifications. Thanks to inputs from the engineers on the Gestione Sportiva racing side, the F1 Dual-Clutch gearbox management incorporates a new control strategy which further perfects gearchange speed and smoothness under hard use.
