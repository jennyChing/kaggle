     <p>
      <b>
       Thlopthlocco Tribal Town
      </b>
      is both a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federally_recognized_tribes" title="Federally recognized tribes">
       federally recognized Native American tribe
      </a>
      and a traditional township of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muscogee_(Creek)" title="Muscogee (Creek)">
       Muscogee Creek Indians
      </a>
      , based in
      <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">
       Oklahoma
      </a>
      . The tribe's native language is
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Creek_language" title="Creek language">
       Mvskoke
      </a>
      , also called Creek.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Pronunciation">
       Pronunciation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thlopthlocco_Tribal_Town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pronunciation">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      According to the Oklahoma Historical Society, the name of the town can be roughly pronounced by English speakers as "Rop-ro-co."
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      An item in the
      <i>
       Tulsa World
      </i>
      explained: "The sound of the "thl" is usually spelled with an "r" in the Muscogee language and is pronounced in English by placing the tongue halfway between the "th" position and the "l" position."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Government">
       Government
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thlopthlocco_Tribal_Town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Government">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Thlopthlocco Tribal Town is now headquartered in
      <a href="/wiki/Okemah,_Oklahoma" title="Okemah, Oklahoma">
       Okemah
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Clearview,_Oklahoma" title="Clearview, Oklahoma">
       Clearview, Oklahoma
      </a>
      . Tribal enrollment is 845, with 728 members living within the state of Oklahoma, and is based on lineal descent. George Scott is the elected Mekko, or Town King.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oia_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-oia-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He succeeded Vernon Yarholar.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tribal_jurisdictional_area" title="Tribal jurisdictional area">
       tribal jurisdictional area
      </a>
      of Thlopthlocco is within
      <a href="/wiki/Creek_County,_Oklahoma" title="Creek County, Oklahoma">
       Creek
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hughes_County,_Oklahoma" title="Hughes County, Oklahoma">
       Hughes
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mayes_County,_Oklahoma" title="Mayes County, Oklahoma">
       Mayes
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/McIntosh_County,_Oklahoma" title="McIntosh County, Oklahoma">
       McIntosh
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Muskogee_County,_Oklahoma" title="Muskogee County, Oklahoma">
       Muskogee
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Okfuskee_County,_Oklahoma" title="Okfuskee County, Oklahoma">
       Okfuskee
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Okmulgee_County,_Oklahoma" title="Okmulgee County, Oklahoma">
       Okmulgee
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Rogers_County,_Oklahoma" title="Rogers County, Oklahoma">
       Rogers
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Seminole_County,_Oklahoma" title="Seminole County, Oklahoma">
       Seminole
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tulsa_County,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa County, Oklahoma">
       Tulsa
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Wagoner_County,_Oklahoma" title="Wagoner County, Oklahoma">

      </a>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oia_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-oia-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The tribe maintains a close relationship with the
      <a href="/wiki/Muscogee_(Creek)_Nation" title="Muscogee (Creek) Nation">
       Muscogee (Creek) Nation
      </a>
      and falls under the jurisdiction of their tribal courts.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_development">
       Economic development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thlopthlocco_Tribal_Town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economic development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Thlopthlocco operates its own tribal housing program, smoke shop, and the Golden Pony Casino, located in Okemah. The tribe's economic impact for 2011 was $12,500,000.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oia_1-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-oia-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In August 2012, the National Indian Gaming Commission notified the Thlopthlocco Tribal Town that it was in violation of the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act for allowing two Atlanta, Georgia companies to operate the Golden Pony Casino for several years without a contract. The companies named were Titan Network LLC and Mercury Gaming Group LLC. The violations occurred from September 2005 through December 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thlopthlocco_Tribal_Town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Thlopthlocco_sign.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="450" data-file-width="432" height="208" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Thlopthlocco_sign.jpg/200px-Thlopthlocco_sign.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Thlopthlocco_sign.jpg/300px-Thlopthlocco_sign.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Thlopthlocco_sign.jpg/400px-Thlopthlocco_sign.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Thlopthlocco_sign.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Sign to Tribal Town
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The Muscogee Creek confederacy was composed of autonomous tribal towns, governed by their own elected leadership. The Creek originated in the Southeastern United States, in what is now
      <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">
       Alabama
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">
       Georgia
      </a>
      . They were collectively removed from the southeast to
      <a href="/wiki/Indian_Territory" title="Indian Territory">
       Indian Territory
      </a>
      under the United States'
      <a href="/wiki/Indian_Removal_Act" title="Indian Removal Act">
       Indian Removal Policy
      </a>
      of the 1830s.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ohs_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ohs-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Before 1832, the Thlopthlocco Tribal Town split from a larger town. It was removed to
      <a href="/wiki/Indian_Territory" title="Indian Territory">
       Indian Territory
      </a>
      in 1835.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ohs_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ohs-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The members of the town settled in an area south of
      <a href="/wiki/Okemah,_Oklahoma" title="Okemah, Oklahoma">
       Okemah, Oklahoma
      </a>
      , in what would become
      <a href="/wiki/Okfuskee_County,_Oklahoma" title="Okfuskee County, Oklahoma">
       Okfuskee County
      </a>
      , on lands that were originally occupied by the
      <a href="/wiki/Osage_Nation" title="Osage Nation">
       Osage
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Quapaw" title="Quapaw">
       Quapaw
      </a>
      . Those tribes ceded their lands to the US by 1825.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oc_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-oc-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      During the
      <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">
       American Civil War
      </a>
      , Thlopthlocco Tribal Town was briefly the headquarters of Confederate Col.
      <a href="/wiki/Douglas_H._Cooper" title="Douglas H. Cooper">
       Douglas H. Cooper
      </a>
      . Greenleaf Town, located five miles northwest of Thlopthlocco, was the headquarters of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Opothleyahola" title="Opothleyahola">
        Opothleyahola
       </a>
      </i>
      , a Muscogee leader who worked to resolve conflicts between the Creek factions during the war. He and 5000 others moved north to
      <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">
       Kansas
      </a>
      to avoid the Civil War. After the war, the Muscogee Creek collectively signed the 1866 Treaty with the United States and freed their slaves. The treaty also called for the
      <a href="/wiki/Creek_Freedmen" title="Creek Freedmen">
       Creek Freedmen
      </a>
      to have membership in the Muscogee Nation. The freedmen settled new townships in Okfuskee County, including
      <a href="/wiki/Boley,_Oklahoma" title="Boley, Oklahoma">
       Boley
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bookertee,_Oklahoma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bookertee, Oklahoma (page does not exist)">
       Bookertee
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Clearview,_Oklahoma" title="Clearview, Oklahoma">
       Clearview
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chilesville,_Oklahoma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chilesville, Oklahoma (page does not exist)">
       Chilesville
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rusk,_Oklahoma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rusk, Oklahoma (page does not exist)">
       Rusk
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oc_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-oc-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Thlopthlocco Tribal Town retained its tribal identity despite
      <a href="/wiki/Dawes_Act" title="Dawes Act">
       allotments
      </a>
      of land to individual households under the
      <a href="/wiki/Dawes_Commission" title="Dawes Commission">
       Dawes Commission
      </a>
      of 1896. From 1898-1906, members among the
      <a href="/wiki/Five_Civilized_Tribes" title="Five Civilized Tribes">
       Five Civilized Tribes
      </a>
      were registered on what have come to be known as the Dawes Rolls. After making allotments to households registered with the tribes, the US government declared other formerly tribal land as surplus and sold it to European-American settlers after 1906. This further broke up tribal communal territory.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Thlopthlocco Tribal members organized as a distinct tribe under the
      <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_Indian_Welfare_Act" title="Oklahoma Indian Welfare Act">
       Oklahoma Indian Welfare Act
      </a>
      of 1936, which followed the national
      <a href="/wiki/Indian_Reorganization_Act" title="Indian Reorganization Act">
       Indian Reorganization Act
      </a>
      of 1934.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ohs_5-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-ohs-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The original headquarters for the tribe was the Thlopthlocco Methodist Episcopal Church, located between
      <a href="/wiki/Wetumka,_Oklahoma" title="Wetumka, Oklahoma">
       Wetumka
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okemah" title="Okemah">
       Okemah
      </a>
      .
     </p>
