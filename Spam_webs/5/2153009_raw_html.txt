         <p>
          Yoga is a system of physical and mental practices that originated in India more than three thousand years ago. Yoga's stated purpose is to help each one of us achieve our highest potential and to experience enduring health and happiness. With yoga, it is said that we can extend our healthy, productive years far beyond the accepted norm and, at the same time, improve the quality of our lives. (specialyoga.com )
         </p>
         <p>
          Does Yoga really offer all that or is it just the newest fad?
         </p>
         <p>
          Whatever it is, yoga is a popular form of exercise and advertising for it appears everywhere these days.
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are 8 of the most creative ways for marketing the practice of yoga:
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           1. Yoga and the Bendable Straw
          </b>
         </h2>
         <p>
          You have probably seen this bendable straw around emphasizing how bendable you get from doing yoga.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-img_assist_custom" height="400" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/yoga straw.jpg" title="" width="318"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Source:
          <a href="http://www.trendhunter.com/trends/yoga-straw/" rel="nofollow">
           Trendhunter
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           2. Yoga and the Folding Posters
          </b>
         </h2>
         <p>
          One center used interactive wall posters to help promote the flexibility you get from yoga.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-img_assist_custom" height="445" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/yoga wall bendy posters.jpg" title="" width="312"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Source:
          <a href="/www.adverblog.com/.../img/leo_burnett_china.jpg" rel="nofollow">
           Adverblog
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           3. Yoga and the Foldable Sidewalk Sign
           <br/>
          </b>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Another used outside sidewalk advertising to show the flexibility you can have with yoga.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-img_assist_custom" height="249" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/yoga outdoor sign.jpg" title="" width="250"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           4. Yoga and the Wall Clock
           <br/>
          </b>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Once you're in the studios, the marketing for yoga does not stop. Here is a wall clock used for that very purpose.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-left">
           <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="339" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/CLOCK.jpg" title="" width="460"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Source:
          <a href="http://adsoftheworld.com/media/ambient/y_yoga_center_clock" rel="nofollow">
           Ads of the World
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           5. Yoga and the Laptop
          </b>
          <br/>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Yoga is for everyone!  This one reads : Yoga for working professionals. For a stress-free 9 to 9.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="298" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/yoga.jpg" title="" width="460"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Source:
          <a href="http://adsoftheworld.com/media/print/sharmilas_yoga_laptops" rel="nofollow">
           Ads of the World
           <br/>
           <br/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           6. Yoga and the Energy Booster
          </b>
          <br/>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Here is a print ad for an energy yoga class.  Yoga provides you with energy. Interesting way the ad emphasizes that, no?
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-img_assist_custom" height="487" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/blow dryer butt.jpg" title="" width="326"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Source: Coloribus
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           7. Yoga and Balance
          </b>
          <br/>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Another print ad for a studio called Body Balance.  The caption reads, "Restore balance to your world. The supremely balancing group workout that blends tai chi, yoga and Pilate's."
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="460" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/body.jpg" title="" width="325"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="460" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/LES0650-US-A4-RPM_HR_1.preview.jpg" title="" width="325"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Source:
          <a href="http://adsoftheworld.com/media/print/les_mills_balance" rel="nofollow">
           Ads of the World
          </a>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           8. Yoga and the Rubber Band
          </b>
         </h2>
         <p>
          And finally, a series of print ads for a yoga studio inspiring you to think of yoga as making you as flexible as a rubber band.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="318" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/yoga print tan.jpg" title="" width="450"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="318" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/yoga print pink.jpg" title="" width="450"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="inline inline-middle">
           <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="318" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/yoga print gray.jpg" title="" width="450"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Source:
          <a href="/www.ibelieveinadv.com/commons/position2.jpg" rel="nofollow">
           I believe in advertising
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          What do you think of these ad programs for yoga?
         </p>
         <p>
          Do youthink they effectively promote the benefits of yoga or do they miss the mark?  What would you have used to promote the benefits of yoga?
         </p>
