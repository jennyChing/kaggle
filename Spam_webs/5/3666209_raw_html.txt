         MP3 Blog aggregators scan the most popular music blogs and filter them into a searchable MP3 database. Aggregators allow you to search for music by artist or song, as well as discover some of the most popular MP3 blogs online. Here is how to use a few of the most popular:

         <h2 class="title">
          How to Find Music Using The Hype Machine
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="notify">
        </div>
        <div class="body" data-button-class="all">
         <ol>
          <li>
           <p>
            Go to the music blog aggregator The Hype Machine.
            <a class="citation" href="http://hypem.com/" title="http://hypem.com/">
             c
            </a>
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            Type the name of an artist or song into the Hype Machine search box. This will pull up a list of blog posts that match your query.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            Click on the name of the song in order to view the original blog post, where you will typically find the link to the song in question.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            MP3 blogs will usually only host specific MP3s for a week or two. Since The Hype Machine lists blog posts in chronological order, you will have a better chance of finding available songs at the top of the list.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            If you just want to listen to the tracks on the results page, click on the button that says "Pop-up Flash Player." This will launch a stand-alone music player that will stream the available songs on the list.
           </p>
          </li>
         </ol>

          <li>
           <p>

            <a class="citation" href="http://elbo.ws/" title="http://elbo.ws/">
             c
            </a>
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            Type the name of an artist or song into the "Keyword" search box.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            Look through the list of artist matches and click on the one that best matches your search. This will bring up a list of blog posts that mention your search term.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            At the top of your search results, click on the heading "Tracks". This will bring up a list of only those tracks that match your search term.
           </p>

          <li>

         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="notify">
        </div>
        <div class="body" data-button-class="all">
         <ol>
          <li>
           <p>
            Go to the Social Bookmarking site
            <a href="/del-icio-us">
             del.icio.us
            </a>
            .
            <a class="citation" href="http://del.icio.us/" title="http://del.icio.us/">
             c
            </a>
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            Create a custom search by typing "http://del.icio.us/tag/system:filetype:mp3" into the URL box. This will bring up a list of all of the MP3s on del.icio.us.
           </p>
          </li>

           <p>

           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            Search through the results and click on a link in order to play the track.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            Right-click on the link and choose "Save Link As" in order to download the file.
           </p>

         <h2 class="title">
          Finding Music With Bit-Torrent
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="notify">
        </div>
        <div class="body" data-button-class="all">
         Bit-Torrent file sharing protocol allows multiple Internet users to download and distribute large amounts of data at the same time. Using
         <a href="/bittorrent">
          BitTorrent
         </a>
         may be a little more involved than just saving a file from a website, but the fruits of your labor are often of higher quality. To get started:
         <div>
          <br/>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Choose a
            <a href="/bit-torrent-client">
             Bit-Torrent client
            </a>
            . There are plenty of choices to get yourself started, but some of the more popular clients include:
            <ul>
             <li>
              The Official Bit-Torrent client from the protocol's designers
              <a class="citation" href="http://www.bittorrent.com/download" title="http://www.bittorrent.com/download">
               c
              </a>
             </li>
             <li>
              uTorrent
              <a class="citation" href="http://www.utorrent.com/" title="http://www.utorrent.com/">
               c
              </a>
             </li>
             <li>
              <a href="/vuze">
               Vuze
              </a>
              (formerly known as Azureus)
              <a class="citation" href="http://www.vuze.com/" title="http://www.vuze.com/">
               c
              </a>
             </li>
             <li>
              Torrent Swapper
              <a class="citation" href="http://bit-torrent.sourceforge.net/" title="http://bit-torrent.sourceforge.net/">
               c
              </a>
             </li>
            </ul>
           </li>
           <li>
            Although each client may work slightly different than the other, the general usage is the same for this kind of software:
            <ol>
             <li>
              Download and install your chosen client
             </li>
             <li>
              Locate
              <b>
               .torrent
              </b>
              files that will allow you to download the music you're searching for.
             </li>
             <li>
              Leave the program running until the download completes.
             </li>
            </ol>
           </li>
          </ol>
          Now that your computer can process torrents, it's time to start looking for them on the Web.
         </div>
         <div>
          <br/>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Visit
            <a href="/the-pirate-bay">
             The Pirate Bay
            </a>
            , one of the largest and most trusted torrent "trackers."
            <a class="citation" href="http://thepiratebay.org/" title="http://thepiratebay.org/">
             c
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            Click on "Browse Torrents."
           </li>
           <li>
            Click on "Audio."
           </li>
           <li>
            You will now see a list of the most recently updated torrents in Pirate Bay.
           </li>
           <li>
            The column marked "Size" indicates the size of the file.
           </li>
           <li>
            The column marked "SE" indicates the number of users currently "seeding" this file (see Step 12).
           </li>
           <li>
            To download, click the name of the file you wish to access.
           </li>
           <li>
            You will see an information page giving a quick rundown about the file.
           </li>
           <li>
            Click "Download This Torrent."
           </li>
           <li>
            Your Bit-Torrent client will pop up on screen and begin the download.
           </li>
           <li>
            Most clients include a "percent downloaded" so you can watch your file's progress.
           </li>
           <li>
            Once your download is complete, it is considered polite to continue to "seed" the file for other users.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <b>
           Seeding
          </b>
          : Continuing to upload the file to other users even after you have finished with your download.
          <br/>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Generally, the more users are seeding a file, the faster it will download to your computer.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <b>
           Beware of "honeypots":
          </b>
          <br/>
          <ul>
          </ul>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Some sites have been set up to trap users looking for torrents to illegally download copyrighted material.
            <br/>
           </li>
           <li>
            For example, the large tracker site TorrentSpy has been ordered to track and log all of its visitor activity.
            <a class="citation" href="http://www.torrentspy.com/http://news.com.com/TorrentSpy+ordered+to+start+tracking+visitors/2100-1030_3-6189866.html" title="http://www.torrentspy.com/http://news.com.com/TorrentSpy+ordered+to+start+tracking+visitors/2100-1030_3-6189866.html">
             c
            </a>
            <br/>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>

         <h2 class="title">
          Getting Past a Firewall
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="notify">
        </div>
        <div class="body" data-button-class="all">
         If you're using a Wireless Router to connect to the Internet, chances are it comes with a
         <a href="/firewall">
          Firewall
         </a>
         installed.
         <br/>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Firewalls will sometimes block your computer's access to peer-to-peer file sharing networks.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Go to the "Options" or "Preferences" menu in your P2P client and look for the "Port" or "Listening Port" setting.
          </li>
          <li>
           You'll need to know which port the client will be using in order to set up your router. (Ports are usually three-to-five digit numbers.)
          </li>
          <li>
           Log on to the router from your computer.
          </li>
          <li>
           Instructions for most routers can be found online.
           <a class="citation" href="http://portforward.com/routers.htm" title="http://portforward.com/routers.htm">
            c
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Look for the headings "Application" or "Configuration" in your router's menu and click on it.
          </li>
          <li>
           Enter in the same port number from your P2P client and, if possible, enter in the name of your P2P client so you'll remember why you left open this port later.
          </li>
          <li>
           Click "Save."
          </li>
          <li>
           Your router should now be set to allow for P2P downloads.
          </li>
         </ol>
        </div>

          Downloading Over a Peer-to-Peer Network
         </h2>

        <div class="notify">

        <div class="body" data-button-class="all">
         <p>
          Search for new music. When you see a song you want to download, just double click, and it will usually begin downloading or queuing automatically. If many users are attempting to access one set of files at the same time, an online queue automatically forms, and downloads will start on a first-come first-served basis. (Tip: Monitor your queued or pending downloads as disconnections or hiccups are common.)
         </p>

          Most clients allow for multiple simultaneous downloads. Check the files you have downloaded before burning to a CD, as occasionally they will be incorrectly labeled or of low quality.

         </div>
         <h2 class="title">
          Getting the Most Out of P2P Downloading
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="notify">
        </div>
        <div class="body" data-button-class="all">
         <p>
          Be specific; overly general or vague searches can produce too many results. For example: Instead of "Prince," try "Prince Purple Rain."
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Once you're downloading a song from another user, most P2P clients will give you the option to view all of their shared content. Try right-clicking on their username. This will enable you to find whole albums to download rather than individual songs. Likewise, if you find another user with similar musical tastes to your own, it can be a great way to discover new music.
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Most P2P clients have a chat option. Network chat rooms are a good way to find other users with similar tastes, and to see who is online and what music they can share.
         </p>
