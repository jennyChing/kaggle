             <i>
              Psoriasis is cyclic in nature.
             </i>
            </b>
            Psoriasis is a cyclic condition, meaning that flares/patches of Psoriasis occur sporadically with triggers. A patient may go weeks or months without a flare, and then experience the onset of symptoms once more after encountering a trigger. Common triggers include alcohol, smoking, certain medications, cold weather, sunburn, stress, cuts or injury to the skin, and stress. No one trigger is universal, and certain triggers may affect a Psoriasis patient more than another.
