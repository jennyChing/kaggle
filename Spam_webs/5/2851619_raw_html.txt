            <p>
             The greatest singles of the '80s - as in any era - possess the power to change the world in three minutes, or failing that, they at least share the ability to leave their stamp on a musical landscape already filled nearly to capacity. And while chart performance, radio airplay and longevity are all important criteria to help build a list of the best of the best, these essential '80s songs announce themselves as vital through sheer force of gravity. Each of these tunes, presented in no particular order, is simply impossible to ignore in the context of '80s music.
            </p>

J. Geils Band - "Centerfold"

             <p>
              Even though I sometimes get the "na-na-na-na-na-na" part mixed up with
              <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1echfiwXycA">
               the Smurfs theme
              </a>
              , this song is a highlight for me personally and for the entire decade's musical catalogue. The band's frontman,
              <a data-inlink="Qc66rX8YuhNxm-PfHBt_xA==" href="http://classicrock.about.com/od/artistsnz/fr/souvenirs_rvw.htm">
               Peter Wolf
              </a>
              , was tailor-made for the '80s with his
              <a href="http://classicrock.about.com/od/bandsandartists/p/mick_jagger.htm">
               Mick Jagger
              </a>
              swagger and exuberance, even though he had more than paid his dues before the band went pop. It may be undeniable that the group reached its highest level of success during the '80s, but this tune's memorable playfulness is a big reason why.
