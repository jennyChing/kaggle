           <h3 class="post-name">
            <a href="http://communities.bentley.com/other/old_site_member_blogs/peer_blogs/b/kurtss_blog/archive/2009/05/24/luxology-render-new-features-new-tests">
             Luxology Render: new features - new tests! (same render engine as in Modo 401)
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="content">
            <p>
             The Luxology Render Engine is developing incredibly fast thanks to the Luxology team, and thanks to the Bentley team for the equal fast implementation into Microstation V8i!
            </p>
            <p>
             There are a lot of enhancements and new features since my previous blog post. Here is a new collection of my Test Renderings (as usual no post processing):
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" height="645" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38318/original.aspx" width="768"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38298/original.aspx"/>
             displacement test. Each ammonite is made of two plane rectangles. The geometry is created by the displacement...!
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38299/original.aspx"/>
             displacement test: only 1 material is used.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" height="1298" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38300/original.aspx" width="768"/>
             skl!
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38302/original.aspx"/>
             testing caustics
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38303/original.aspx"/>
             <img alt="" border="0" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38303/original.aspx"/>
             <img alt="" border="0" height="992" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38304/original.aspx" width="768"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             simple displacement used to avoid complicated modeling
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" src="http://communities.bentley.com/cfs-file.ashx/__key/CommunityServer-Blogs-Components-WeblogFiles/00-00-00-25-63/8877.motherboard.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Blurred reflections: only one (1) material is used in this scene! This entire testcase is more or less "stolen"  from a thread at the Maxwell Render Forum where the user Polynurb presented a simple and interesting test using only one single material for a detailed motherboard model.  I've downloaded the same model and photo (top view) that Polynurb used, and recreated the test in Luxology. 30 minute preparation and a little less than 30 minute rendering
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" height="556" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38306/original.aspx" width="768"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             metal test
            </p>
            <p>
             more blurred reflections testing
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" height="609" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38308/original.aspx" width="768"/>
             testing one of the Lighting Challenge scenes from cgtalk.com
            </p>

             dino head
            </p>

             testing the new camera distortion features
            </p>

            </p>

             materials testing....
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" height="550" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38316/original.aspx" width="768"/>
             clip maps (opacity maps), materials...
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" border="0" height="453" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38317/original.aspx" width="768"/>
             fabrics! (thanks to
             <a href="http://www.luxology.com/gallery/image.aspx?id=6449">

            </p>
            <p>

            </p>
            <p>
             I love DOF! (Depth of Field) This image was rendered at 3200px wide to avoid postprocessing.
            </p>

             <img alt="" border="0" height="2343" src="http://communities.bentley.com/photos/kurtss_images/images/38319/original.aspx" width="768"/>
