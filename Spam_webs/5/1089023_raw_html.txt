           <div class="news-article-content">
            <p itemprop="description">
             <strong>
              Japans Naoya Kihara, Belgiums Pierre Neuville and Italy Luca Moschitta were selected by a panel of PokerListings experts this week to win the first-ever PokerListings.com Spirit of Poker awards.
             </strong>
            </p>
            <div itemprop="articleBody">
             <p>
              Reaching the top three in their respective categories via fan voting and then getting the most nods on the PokerListings ballots, the three esteemed poker pros will receive their awards at the 2013 Battle of Malta live tournament on September 26.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <b>
               Kihara Outduels Negreanu for Most Inspiring Player
              </b>
             </h3>
             <div class="image-container image-portrait-size-medium image-position">
              <a class="lightbox" href="http://edge1.pokerlistings.com/assets/photos/IMG9024.JPG">
               <img alt="Naoya Kihara" class="image-position image-portrait-size-medium" height="320" src="http://edge1.pokerlistings.com/assets/photos/_resampled/CroppedImage180320-IMG9024.JPG" width="180"/>
              </a>
              <div class="image-osd image-osd-compact">
               <div class="image-osd-content">
                Naoya Kihara
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              Kihara, perhaps the biggest surprise of this years awards, beat out big-name sponsored pros including
              <b>
               Marvin Rettenmaier
              </b>
              ,
              <b>
               Max Lykov
              </b>
              ,
              <b>
               Isaac Haxton
              </b>
              and
              <b>
               Daniel Negreanu
              </b>
              to scoop the Most Inspiring Player Award.
             </p>
             <p>
              The
              <a href="http://www.pokerlistings.com/naoya-kihara-wins-first-ever-wsop-bracelet-for-japan-16320" target="_blank">
               first Japanese WSOP bracelet winner ever
              </a>
              , Kihara has $722,218 in live tournament earnings in just two years on the international circuit but his impact goes far beyond just cashing.
             </p>
             <p>
              With live poker illegal in Japan and online poker only accessible since 2008, Kiharas accomplishments are doubly impressive. And since his win in 2012 hes also kickstarted a wider interest in the game in Japan single-handedly.
             </p>
             <p>
              Right now people in Japan think poker is just gambling, Kihara told us at the 2013 PCA, so when I'm on TV I want to explain it better.
             </p>
             <p>
              Poker is a game and you can use it to gamble but the way I play is as an investment business. Poker is a skill game and I'm trying to tell people in Japan.
             </p>
             <p>
              Playing on PokerStars under the nickname nkeyno," Kihara has racked up millions of hands and a growing rail of Japanese players hoping to follow in his footsteps.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <b>
               Neuville, Moschitta Rep Old, Young
               <br/>
              </b>
             </h3>
             <div class="image-container image-landscape-size-medium image-position-alt">
              <a class="lightbox" href="http://edge1.pokerlistings.com/assets/photos/lucamoschitta1.jpg">
               <img alt="lucamoschitta1" class="image-position-alt image-landscape-size-medium" height="180" src="http://edge1.pokerlistings.com/assets/photos/_resampled/CroppedImage320180-lucamoschitta1.jpg" width="320"/>
              </a>
              <div class="image-osd image-osd-compact">
               <div class="image-osd-content">
                Luca Moschitta
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              Young gun Moschitta won the hotly-contested
              <b>
               Here Comes the Future
              </b>
              award for rising superstars, narrowly beating two Scandinavians in Swedens
              <b>
               Sofia Welllbet Lvgren
              </b>
              and Finn
              <b>
               Lauri Pesonen
              </b>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              One of the youngest players ever to reach SuperNova Elite on PokerStars, Moschitta is most famous for collecting two Porsches using only FPPs thanks to the amazing volume hes put in online.
             </p>
             <p>
              He's only recently turned his focus to the live tournament scene but he's immediately found success, taking down IPT Saint Vincent in March for 180,000 and finishing second at IPT Nova Gorica. Hes also spent countless hours over the past few years sharing his love for the game at the tables and through social media.
             </p>
             <div class="image-container image-landscape-size-medium image-position">
              <a class="lightbox" href="http://edge1.pokerlistings.com/assets/photos/NeuvilleCopenhagen.JPG">
               <img alt="NeuvilleCopenhagen" class="image-position image-landscape-size-medium" height="180" src="http://edge1.pokerlistings.com/assets/photos/_resampled/CroppedImage320180-NeuvilleCopenhagen.JPG" width="320"/>
              </a>
              <div class="image-osd image-osd-compact">
               <div class="image-osd-content">
                Pierre Neuville
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              Finally, Neuville won the veterans
              <b>
               This is 50+
              </b>
