           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000FKK01O?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=thebeabun-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B000FKK01O">
            Redken All Soft Heavy Cream
           </a>
          </strong>
          : For dry, brittle hair.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001AQT68K?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=thebeabun-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001AQT68K">
            Redkens Smooth Down Butter Treat
           </a>
          </strong>
          : Good for dry and unruly hair, and just the name sounds delicious!
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         These are also a good idea after any
         <strong>
          chemical process
         </strong>
         on the hair, like color or a perm (although I dont think anyones gotten a perm since 1987).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Each deep conditioner has its own recommended time to leave it on, usually somewhere between 5 and 20 minutes. I know I constantly preach the virtues of following the directions on the product, but sometimes I throw caution to the wind and improvise, leaving a shorter term deep conditioner on overnight.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         You can do this too with a hair cap so your pillows dont get all gross. Just make sure that the deep conditioner doesnt have alcohol in it if youre going to leave it on for a long time. I personally dont like any hair products that have alcohol in them.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         If youd rather not spend the money on a chemical filled commercial product, you can deep condition with an oil instead from your kitchen. (Just make sure you plan on a wash the next morning, or your hair could be pretty disgustingly stringy the next day.)
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         My favorites are
         <strong>
          coconut oil or olive oil for softness and strength, and castor oil for growth.
         </strong>
         Youve REALLY gotta wash castor oil out, though. Its thick and sticky and will not produce a nice hair texture.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         What are your favorite ways to deep condition?
