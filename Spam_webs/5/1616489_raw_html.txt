         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <img alt="ectomorph muscle gain" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-223" height="565" src="http://www.ectohub.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/less.jpg" width="849"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          We ectomorphs are a different breed sometimes. Being stuck in skinny bodies all our lives, it almostmakes uswant fast muscle gain even more than the averagenon-ectomorph.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Just after writing about the
          <a href="http://www.ectohub.com/the-ectomorph-diet/" title="The Ectomorph Diet">
           ectomorph diet
          </a>
          , I happened to speak to a friend who was, as far as I could see, getting it right but still only managed to put on 2kg in 4 months. A rather paltry figure. Whatcould bewrong this time? It seems a neverending battle isnt it? That we ectomorphs are deficient at just about everything. That every little thing needs to be done just right or itll be doom. All the while seeing our more naturally blessed comrades coast by, getting away with things we cant possibly dream of. How could that not kill your motivation?
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Well the good news is, it doesnt have to bethat hard. As an ectomorph, youre not as physically deficient as you think. Its more or less that we believe its too difficult because its the reality weve known all our lives. Call it pre-conditioning if you will but the fact is you absolutely do have the capacity to grow. You can get as big as you want to, as long as you remember this.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left; font-size: 18px;">
          <strong>
           LESS IS MORE.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          I found out that despite getting his diet right, my friend was hitting the gym 6 days a week and working every muscle each time. I admired his dedication and couldnt help but feel a sense of serenity about it for I too had been guilty of doing exactly the same thing.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Often, we hear the no pain no gain mantra in bodybuilding and equate that with working harder and longer. We think that if were not getting results then were simply not working hard enough. We think that being ectomorphs means were somewhat handicappedso we need to strive harder in order to catch up to everyone else, that somehow our gains waste away faster than others so we need to be getting back in the gym to ensure that every single muscle gets another solid workout to build upon those gains before its too late. Sound familiar?
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          It is true that you shouldnt be leaving anything behind on the gym floor and theres something deeply satisfying about putting your blood, sweat, and tears on the line, but its also important that you do it right. Doing more of the wrong thing wont get you far. Doing more itself,called overtraining,is also counterproductive. In this way, the idea of no pain no gain is often misdirected as the process isnt quite so linear.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Heres how you can really gain more by doing less.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           1. Do less reps
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          When I first started out, I was doing 12 reps per set. I found myself stuck on the same weight for months. I was so frustrated that one day I decided to double the weight and halve the rep count on the basis that my overall workload would still be the same. I didnt really understand it at the time but I would soon find that I could go higher and higher at an astronomical rate. So much so that within weeks, the original weight I had been stuck on for 12 reps became an easy 12-rep warmup.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The science behind this is simple. Your body comprises of2 types of muscles  TypeI and Type II. TypeI muscles carry more oxygen and are much slower at converting glycogen to energy but give you endurance. TypeII muscles are those that give you explosive power but only in short bursts.TypeII muscles have a greater capacity to grow than TypeI muscles. It makes sense then to be targeting Type II muscles, which is exactly what lowering the rep count is designed to do, resulting in significantly increased strength and bigger bulk. Comparing like for like, think about the respective physiques of sprinters (Type II) and marathon runners (Type I). That should give you a really good idea of howto approach gym.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          From a psychological point of view, its also much easier to do 6 reps than 12, especially when youre talking about trying to go up to a higher weight. You can steel yourself to blast out 6 reps, take a rest, and then go again whereas with 12 reps, you can often get too fatigued before the end and end up not doing the last few reps properly. Imagine this. In a 12-rep set, if you get too fatigued by the 8th rep that would mean youd be doing 4 reps with poor technique. Thats one third of the whole set!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Recommendation: 6-8 reps is ideal. Work at high intensity. This means the weight should still be tough, complete each rep with proper technique, and rest around 30-60 seconds between sets. Move up to the next highest weight once you can complete all sets at the same weight.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           2. Do less muscles
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          This is what is called a split routine, where instead of doing a full body workout, you spend an entire session focused on say just the chest and biceps, then thenext session on just back and shoulders, and the next on just legs, and so on.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Full body workouts can be a good start when youre trying to get active initially but its not an approach suitable for increasing bulk. Imagine youve been spending your time working your entire body and its time to finish off the session with some leg exercises. How would you expect to put in maximum effort when youre already 90% depleted? Remember, to promote growth you need explosive power and trigger those TypeII muscles. You need to be working a certain part of your body at the absolute highest intensity and that simply wont be possible if youre already huffing and puffing with 10% left in the tank before you even begin.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Using a split routine allows you to do that and give the muscle ample time to recover and grow. As an ectomorph, I know that it can be hard to let go of full body workouts. The thought might be that if you go as far as to leave the muscle unattended for more than a few days, your evil ectomorph body will devour the new muscles before youve had a chance to take advantage and it wouldve all but undone all the great work you put in the previous day.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          From my experience, I can tell you with great certainty that when the time comes to do the same muscle again in a split routine cycle, you will find that you will be able to do the same weight easily. And if you can do the same weight, it means youre not getting any smaller. Bearing in mind you were stuck on the same weight doing a lengthy full body workout anyway, you really have nothing to lose. At the very least, youre saving time doing shorter, more intense workouts.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The more likely scenario is you will be able to push harder next time. The equation is simple. As long as youre doing more than the last time, whether its a little or a lot, youre getting bigger. As the weeks go by, work your way up incrementally and before long youll be surprising yourself how much progress youve made.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Recommendation: Split the routine and commit to it for the next 10 weeks. Setup a system where youre doing approximately 1-2 muscles at high intensity per session covering the entire body over the course of a week.Accelerate your progress by seeing if you can go heavier than the last time.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           3. Do less sessions a week
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Doing 6 sessions a week like my friend and I did is a mistake. Just as with full body workouts, we think that our ectomorph bodies would waste away with the absence of constant attention. However, this logic is completely unfounded.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          To be honest, I did enjoy doing 6 times a week and being around the gym environment almost everyday but it simply wasnt sustainable. Work responsibilities, social commitments, andother obligations were sometimes unavoidable and its hard to expect that things will always fall into place on the one rest day you have for the week. Our bodies cant sustain that much punishment either, not if your goal is growth anyway. Believe me, I would if I could but recovery time is paramount. The time out of the gym is when muscle growth occurs so going back for a workoutwhile this is happening will actually hinder your progress.Say you fell over and cutyourself. Wouldit be better to letthe wound heal by itself or picking at it constantly? Muscle repair is really no different and if you are working at a high enough intensity, you
          <em>
           will
          </em>
          need the full week to recover beforedoing the same muscle again.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          If youre not as keen as me, then this is great news as its a significant time saver. Doing less sessions can as much as halve the amount of time spent exercising and still yield better results!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Recommendation: 3-4 sessions a week in a split routine cycle is ideal. Try to give the same muscle groups rest between workouts on consecutive days e.g. avoid doing legs one day and then schedule a session of running or cycling the next.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           4. Do shorter sessions
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          There are 2 aspects to this, with the outcome of even more time savings and better results.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          As you may have guessed, the first aspect is related to explosive power and hitting your TypeII muscles. The second, is about recovery. Strenuous exercise weakens the bodys immune system. Marathon weightsessions may also starve your body too much for too long. In all, you want to prevent your body reaching emergency state, as covered in the ectomorph diet. Lengthy sessions can lead to this, with your body releasing stress hormones that are not conducive to reaching anabolism. Henceit is recommended you limit each muscle to around 3-4different compound movements per session,making the session short and intense but still reasonably thorough.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Recommendation: In addition to 3-4 exercises per muscle, spending 1-hour to 1-hour 15-minutesper session is ideal. Ive known some people that can successfully do 45 minute sessions, so if you can then by all means do. No matter what, ensure you get home and fill up with nutrients to get the recovery process going as soon as possible.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Summary
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          So there you have it. Less reps, less muscles, less sessions, shorter sessions. I hope this article was as informative as it was encouraging. I hope you do see now that ectomorphic freedom isnt as hard asit needs to be.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          To all the hardworking and determined ectomorphs out there that still arent sure, I challenge you today to let go of your perceptions and commit to doing less. Results dont have to come so slowly just because youre an ectomorph. Give it 10 weeks and youll see. Ectomorphic freedom awaits!
         </p>
