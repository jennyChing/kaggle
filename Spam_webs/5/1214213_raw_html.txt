       <header>
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Exploring The Denton County Courthouse
        </h1>
        <h2 class="entry-meta">
        </h2>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p class="first-child ">
         <a href="http://thiseclecticlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Courthouse-Denton-County.jpg">
          <img alt="Courthouse Denton County" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-19244" height="522" src="http://thiseclecticlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Courthouse-Denton-County.jpg" width="698"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <span class="cap" title="L">
          <span>
           L
          </span>
         </span>
         ooking at our beautiful courthouse on the square, its almost
         <em>
          impossible
         </em>
         not to want to investigate every nook and cranny of it.  How exciting it would be to climb all the way up to that clock tower!  I want to have a picnic on that balcony at the top.  Unfortunately, that activity is
         <em>
          not
         </em>
         allowed.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are no tours that explore the upper floors of the courthouse and take tourists to the tower.  Let me repeat that, just in case you didnt get it the first go-round:
         <em>
          the county
          <strong>
           does not
          </strong>
          give tours of the upper floors of the building
         </em>
         .  Visitors may wander somewhat freely in the basement, on the first floor (where the Courthouse-on-the-Square Museum is located), on the second floor, and even on the third floor.  Some of it is restricted because its office space for Heavens sake.  We cant just be barging in on county commissioners while they work.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thiseclecticlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Authorized-Personnel-only.jpg">
          <img alt="Authorized Personnel only" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-19237" height="573" src="http://thiseclecticlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Authorized-Personnel-only.jpg" width="325"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         However, thats the extent of it.  A chain blocks the spiral staircase that leads to the upper floors, with a sign that says Authorized Personnel Only.
        </p>
        <p>
         That sign tells the public
         <em>
          not
         </em>
         to go futher (sorry, when faced with deciphering the proper English usage between farther and further I opt for the Texan futher) and it means
         <em>
          exactly
         </em>
         what it says!  Ive heard some folks say, One day Im just going to step over that chain and climb up there.  I urge you not to do it.
        </p>
        <p>
         First, you could get arrested.  Yes, you really
         <em>
          could
         </em>
         .  And second, there is more than one darned good reason that the public isnt allowed up there.  Its
         <em>
          dangerous
         </em>
         .  Folks could damage the courthouse or themselves!  Some unscrupulous scalawags have even defaced that lovely edifice with graffiti.  And, its
         <em>
          dangerous
         </em>
         (didnt I already tell you that?).
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Nope.  The public doesnt get to see the mysteries of the upper floors of the courthouse.  That said,
         <strong>
          <em>
           I
          </em>
         </strong>
         got to take a tour of it! Yes,
         <em>
          moi
         </em>
         ! Its not that Im particularly special, although I
         <em>
          will
         </em>
         admit that Im a legend in
         <em>
          my
         </em>
         own mind.  I just happened to be in the right place at the right time.  I got an invite to climb those stairs, and I jumped at the chance.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you arent familiar with our courthouse (the third courthouse built in the town), the brochure for the
         <a href="http://www.dentonedp.com/downtown_development/pdf/walking_tour_brochure.pdf" target="_blank" title="walking tour of Denton brochure">
          walking tour of Denton
         </a>
         describes it this way: Denton County Courthouse On the Square was built between 1895 and 1897, using locally quarried limestone, gray sandstone from Mineral Wells, and Burnet County red granite for the columns. That doesnt quite do it justice, but it will have to do for the moment.  Suffice to say it is
         <em>
          magnificent
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         You want to see what I did, dont you?  Yes, I knew you would.  As a friend said, its like getting to see the Popes bathroom or Antarctica. Its something that you probably wont ever get to do, but you can at least experience it vicariously.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive put together a few pictures for you, although I wont bore you with all of them.  I just want you to get a chance to see what is behind those walls.  Click on the first picture below and you can view a slide show.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

        <p>
         No, that isnt all of the pictures I took, but it should give you an idea of the mysteries beyond that chained gate.
        </p>
        <p>
         While I am
         <em>
          immensely
         </em>
         grateful to have gotten the opportunity to see it, Im not sure I would climb up there again. At least, I wouldnt go all the way to the clock tower. I
         <em>
          really
         </em>
         balked at the stairs for the last two flights  after all, Im a little old lady and Im
         <em>
          not
         </em>
         an athlete. However, I had
         <em>
          bragged
         </em>
         about the fact that I was going up there!  If I backed away from the challenge, it would be like a ten-year-old walking away from a triple-dog-dare.
        </p>
        <p>
         I would have loved to show you the bell, but I was too afraid to take my hands from the ladder rungs.  And, I hoped to video the clock moving when it struck the hour  but, duh, I forgot to press the button to make it record.  Still, I hope you enjoyed the virtual tour, since you probably wont ever get to take one.  Remember,
         <em>
          the county does not offer tours of the upper floor of the courthouse
         </em>
         (sheesh, I hope that satisfies the powers that be!).
        </p>
