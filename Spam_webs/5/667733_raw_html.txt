      <h1 class="article-title">
       This 8-Year-Old's Dancing May Bring You To Tears
      </h1>
      <ul class="social">
       <li class="fb">
        <div class="fb-share-button" data-href="http://espn.go.com/espnw/athletes-life/the-buzz/article/12654916/" data-type="button_count">

      <div class="article-byline">
       By D'Arcy Maine | Apr 9, 2015
       <br/>
       espnW.com
      </div>
      <p>
       If you're looking for some inspiration today, please allow me to introduce you to 8-year-old Alissa Sizemore. After losing her right leg below the knee in an accident with a truck last May, the Vernal, Utah, native made her triumphant return to the dance floor recently.
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <a href="http://www.people.com/article/utah-amputee-8-still-dancing-after-losing-leg" target="_blank">
        an interview with People magazine
       </a>
       , Sizemore said dancing has been her favorite thing since she was 4 and that she refused to let her amputation stop her: "I don't have part of my leg, but I can't let that stop me. I still want to dance."
      </p>
      <p>
       Thanks to donations from her community -- including $10,000 raised by 11-year-old Lily Wilson, who auctioned off her prize pig -- Sizemore was able to get a prosthetic leg in September. She was determined to return to dancing and immediately started practicing for a recital.
      </p>
      <p>
       In late February, she participated in the dance recital in the Vernal High School's gymnasium and brought tears to almost everyone in attendance with her incredible performance to Colbie Caillat's "Try." She dances with her prosthetic leg, and then removes it halfway through. It's courageous and beautiful -- and you really need to press play right now.
      </p>
      <p>
       Warning: You might want to grab an entire box of tissues for this one.
      </p>
