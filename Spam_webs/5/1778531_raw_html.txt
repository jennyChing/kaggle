        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Sweet Barbecue Pulled Pork
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2014-08-09T09:00:43+00:00">
          9 August, 2014
         </span>

         <span class="author vcard">

         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/whoneedsacape.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Sweet-Barbecue-Pulled-Pork.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barbecue Pulled Pork" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14661" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/whoneedsacape.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Sweet-Barbecue-Pulled-Pork.jpg?resize=1900%2C1260"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Pulled pork in the slow cooker has been done  I know  Weve even done it a few ways. I think why I love it is pulling that pork gives the meat a chance to soak up all that extra flavor  it always comes out SO GOOD. So anytime Im making a sauce thats delicious I love having it on pulled meat.
         </p>
         <p>
          Sweet Barbecue Pulled Pork is my first attempt at using root beer in a recipe. I knew it could be done  and heard it was delicious. I cant lie  they were right  SO GOOD. Honestly even if you dont like root beer you should like this  by the time its done cooking it just adds the right amount of sweet! Give it a try!
         </p>
         <p>
          I served this the first night as sandwiches, the left overs were also used on a salad (another reason pulled meat is so good).
         </p>

         </p>

             </p>
            </div>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
             Ingredients
            </p>
            <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
              3-5 pounds of boneless pork loin
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 C root beer
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 (12 ounces) bottle of chili sauce
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/4 C vinegar
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
              4 cloves of garlic - minced
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 red onion - chopped
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
             Instructions
            </p>
            <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Spray slow cooker with non-stick cooking spray
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Place meat in slow cooker
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Mix remaining ingredients together and pour over meat
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Cook on low for 7-8 hours
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Pull pork and place back in slow cooker for 1/2 - 1 hour
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              If using as a freezer meal put pork into gallon size bag (may have to cut in 2 - and that's ok) mix remaining ingredients and pour into bag.  Freeze.
             </li>
            </ol>
