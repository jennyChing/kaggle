             <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
              Greek Orthodox Church Shelter for Palestinians
             </h1>
             <div class="meta-info">
              <ul class="td-category">
               <li class="entry-category">
                <a href="http://world.greekreporter.com/category/middle-east-2/">
                 Middle East
                </a>
               </li>
               <li class="entry-category">
                <a href="http://world.greekreporter.com/category/middle-east-2/israel/">
                 Israel

               </li>

              </ul>
              <div class="td-clear-author">
              </div>
              <span class="td-block-author">

                Daphne Tsagari
               </a>

            <div class="td-post-text-content">
             <p>
              <a href="http://world.greekreporter.com/files/st-porphyrius.jpg">
               <img alt="st porphyrius" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6413" height="467" src="http://world.greekreporter.com/files/st-porphyrius.jpg" width="700"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <div class="td-a-rec td-a-rec-id-content_inlineleft td-rec-hide-on-p">
              <div class="social">
               <span class="st_facebook_vcount" displaytext="Facebook">
               </span>
               <span class="st_twitter_vcount" displaytext="Tweet">
               </span>
               <span class="st_email_vcount" displaytext="Email">
               </span>
               <span class="st_sharethis_vcount" displaytext="ShareThis">
               </span>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              About 1,000 Palestinian Muslims fleeing Israeli shells devastating their Gaza neighbourhood have found shelter in a building they otherwise would rarely if ever enter, the citys
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               12th-century
              </span>
              Greek Orthodox Church.
             </p>
             <p>
              Despite its thick walls dating back to the Crusades, the Church of Saint
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               Porphyrius
              </span>
              was still not a very safe haven. Shortly after they arrived, Israeli aircrafts bombed a nearby field, spraying shrapnel on the church and damaging graves.
             </p>
             <p>
              Still though, children from the
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               Shejaia
              </span>
              district, where some 72 Palestinians  many of them women and children  were killed during fighting between Israeli forces and Hamas militants on Sunday, were busy playing football in the yard on Tuesday.
             </p>
             <p>
              Their mothers watched on mattresses and plastic chairs provided by the church, along with food, blankets and toys.
              <br/>
              We have opened the church in order to help people. This is the duty of the church and we are doing all we can to help them, Archbishop
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               Alexios
              </span>
              told Reuters as the sounds of small children echoed outside his office at the church.
              <br/>
              At the beginning there were 600 people and today they became a thousand  mostly children and women. Some of those children are a week old, said the head of Gazas Greek Orthodox minority, the largest of the Christian communities here.
             </p>
             <p>
              Only about 1,400 Christians  Orthodox, Catholics and Protestants  live among the 1.8 million Muslims, meaning they make up 0.08 percent of the population in the crowded Gaza Strip dominated by Hamas, an Islamist group.
              <br/>
              Their life as a religious minority has not always been easy.When the then Pope Benedict quoted a medieval scholar describing Islam as violent and irrational in 2006, unknown militants attacked five churches in Palestinian areas, including Saint
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               Porphyrius
              </span>
               even though it is Orthodox, not Catholic.
             </p>
             <p>
              Palestinian officials, including Hamas members, denounced the violent acts and they were not repeated. It all seems far away now as Gazans reached out to help neighbours who have lost their homes in the two-week Israeli offensive.
              <br/>
              The mosque nearby and the neighbours of the church are all helping. We are still in need of mattresses, blankets, food and most importantly petrol, as we suffer blackouts. If there is no electricity, we cannot have water also, said the archbishop.
             </p>
             <p>
              Since the start of the offensive, more than 100,000 Palestinians  about 6 percent of Gazas entire population  have sought shelter with the United Nations Relief and Works Agency (UNRWA), crowding into 70 schools, the organisation said.
             </p>
             <p>
              Refugees streaming from
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               Shejaia
              </span>
              in the eastern part of Gaza City to the church in the southwestern area of
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               al-Zaytun
              </span>
              said they did not know where else they could go now.We have escaped from our houses and came here and they bombed the church. Where should we go? Let them tell us where we should go, insisted
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               Jawaher
              </span>
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               Sukkar
              </span>
              , who fled with her children.
             </p>
             <p>
              Accusing Israel of targeting civilians, she said: The shells chased us as we ran  can you imagine, a boy falling on the ground and his mother cannot stop to pick him up?
             </p>
             <p>
              One possible reason for the shelling close to the church is that Israels military believe militants have used cemeteries to launch rockets fired at its cities and to hide weaponry.
             </p>
             <p>
              Despite the overcrowding and the serious danger,
              <span class="hiddenSpellError">
               Alexios
              </span>
              said there has been some joy in the church in the midst of tragedy.
             </p>
