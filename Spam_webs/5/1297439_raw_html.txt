         Space Story #1  Rusteye
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="date">
          <a href="http://mynamedotcom.net/2014/12/space-story-1/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Space Story #1  Rusteye">

        <hr/>
        <p>
         FADE IN:
        </p>
        <p>
         We enter ona genericestablishing wide shot of OUTER SPACE. Theres absolutely nothing out of the ordinary going on here. Its just space looking the way wed allexpect space to look: an empty black void peppered with unimaginably distant nuclear furnaces.
        </p>
        <p>
         The camera pans to reveal the RIM COLONY OF HOSPICE, a desert planet with only a single city visible from space.
        </p>
        <p>
         As the camera drifts towards one of the planets main settlements
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: right;">
         CUT TO:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         INT. BAR  NIGHT
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-84">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         We enter the interior of a SEEDYGENERIC SPACE BAR full of wacky looking aliens and asuitably space-y cantina band playingin the corner.
        </p>
        <p>
         Our focus, however, ison a heated match of SPACE POKER between a cyborg, RUSTEYE, and a SCOUNDRELin a cowboy hat and duster. About half of RUSTEYES face has peeled away over the years, revealing a glowing red eye and a rusting, terminator-like robotic endoskeleton beneath whats left of his skin. A small crowd is gathered around the table, murmuring as the game plays out.
        </p>
        <p>
         Three SPACE COMMUNITY CARDS are face up on the table.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The SPACE TURN is flipped over. The MAN IN THE HAT smiles confidently and raises.RUSTEYE matches the bet.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         THE MAN IN THE HAT motions to check.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Finally, the SPACE RIVER is flipped over. THE MAN IN THE HAT raises one last time. RUSTEYES cybernetic eye narrows as he calls. The two play their hands. THE MAN IN THE HAT has very obviously won.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         RUSTEYE
         <br/>
         Well, well, well. Looks like I won again, dont it boys?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The goons gathered around the table murmur in agreement, and some intimidatingly tighten the grips on their guns. THE MAN IN THE HAT smiles smugly.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         RUSTEYE
         <br/>
         Why you lookin so smug over there?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         MAN IN THE HAT
         <br/>
         Because I know how this storys going to end.
        </p>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         RUSTEYE laughs as he scoops the pot toward his end of the table.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         RUSTEYE
         <br/>
         Do you now? Cause life aint no story, princess.
         <br/>
         No frou-frou happy endings here.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         MAN IN THE HAT
         <br/>
         Oh, but thats where youre
         <br/>
         wrong.Everythings a story.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The MAN IN THE HAT smiles as confusion washes over RUSTEYES face.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         MAN IN THE HAT
         <br/>
         See, Ive learned to game the system. Ive gone and
         <br/>
         made myself toointeresting to kill off without a plot,
         <br/>
         butIm alsonot evil enoughto workasa villain of the week,
         <br/>
         and
         <em>
          just
         </em>
         enigmatic enoughto not quite fit into whatever big,
         <br/>
         ambitious storyarcsomeone might wantto stick me
         <br/>
         in. In other words,Im safe in the writers workshop.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         (Beat)
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         You, on the other hand? Youre just a small time
         <br/>
         slaver with a nasty little habit of beating women
         <br/>
         and robbing gamblers. Youre not interesting or
         <br/>
         threatening enough to warrant anything more
         <br/>
         than a guest spot. Hell, youve even been dumb
         <br/>
         enough to let me finish this little speech
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         RUSTEYE doesnt appreciate being called dumb, and tightens his grip on his holster.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         RUSTEYE
         <br/>
         You plan on gittin to the point fore I kill ya?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         MAN IN THE HAT
         <br/>
         The point, Rusteye, is that Im a regular Han Solo
         <br/>
         to your Greedo Well, except for the part where Han
         <br/>
         joins the Rebellion and helps save the galaxy, which
         <br/>
         is pretty much the exact opposite of what Ive been
         <br/>
         trying to tell you about myself. But thats not the point.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         RUSTEYEs eye narrows.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         MAN IN THE HAT
         <br/>
         The point, Rusteye, is thatHan shot first.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: right;">
         RUSTEYE moves to signal his men, but its too late: the MAN IN THE HAT already has a PLASMA PISTOL drawn under the table and fires it several times into RUSTEYEs body, launching off his chair.
        </p>
        <p>
         TO BE CONTINUED (i.e. hit the next button below)
        </p>

        <hr/>
        <p>
         And here we go!
         <em>
          Space Story
         </em>
         (originally
         <em>
          Most Sci-fi Shows are Named After the Ship
         </em>
         ) is a feature length screenplay I wrote as my senior thesis back when I was still in college (its been a whole year! Almost.) Its currently going through its third or fourth major rewrite, except this time, instead of doing itall in Celtx, I decided tochronicle itpiece by piece, from start to finish here on my blog. Updates come every Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday!
        </p>
