          <h2>
           Hypoallergenic Dog Breeds and How to Protect Yourself From Your Best Friend
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/person-sneezing-with-dog.jpeg">
            <img alt="person with dog allergy " class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3196" height="285" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/person-sneezing-with-dog-420x285.jpeg" title="person sneezing with dog" width="420"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The term hypoallergenic is often used in reference to dogs that are less likely to cause
           <a href="http://www.acaai.org/allergist/allergies/Types/pet-allergies/Pages/dog-allergy.aspx" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.acaai.org/allergist/allergies/Types/pet-allergies/Pages/dog-allergy.aspx', 'allergic reactions']);" target="_blank" title="Dog Allergies">
            allergic reactions
           </a>
           . In the dog world, the general consensus is that these are some ways in which you can determine whether a dog would be hypoallergenic or not. While this list is not exhaustive, it is of a lot of help while determining the perfect breed for your health.
          </p>
          <div class="middleCenter">
           <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
           </script>
           <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-1217667265547239" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="6784074211" style="display:block">
           </ins>
           <script>
            (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
           </script>
          </div>
          <h2>
           How do I know whether or not a dog is hypoallergenic?
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Dog breeds that shed less are more likely to be hypoallergenic, since the dogs dander and saliva stick to the hair and are not released into the environment. However, protein expression levels play a major role and amount of shedding alone does not determine degree of allergic reaction. Even if you get a hairless dog, its still going to produce the allergen, says Dr. Wanda Phipatanakul, chair of the Indoor Allergen Committee for the
           <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.aaaai.org/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.aaaai.org/', 'American Academy of Allergy, Asthma, and Immunology']);" rel="homepage" target="_blank" title="American Academy of Allergy, Asthma, and Immunology">
            American Academy of Allergy, Asthma, and Immunology
           </a>
           in the newsmagazine U.S. News &amp; World Report. How hypoallergenic a particular dog is for a particular person may vary with the individual dog and the individual person. If a person is allergic, they may be best able to tolerate a specific dog, possibly of one of the hypoallergenic breeds.
           <a href="http://www.medicine.virginia.edu/clinical/departments/medicine/divisions/allergy/home-page" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.medicine.virginia.edu/clinical/departments/medicine/divisions/allergy/home-page', 'Dr. Thomas A. Platts-Mills']);" target="_blank" title="Dr Thomas A Platts-Mills">
            Dr. Thomas A. Platts-Mills
           </a>
           , head of the Asthma and Allergic Disease Center at the
           <a class="zem_slink" href="http://maps.google.com/maps?ll=38.035,-78.505&amp;spn=0.01,0.01&amp;q=38.035,-78.505 (University%20of%20Virginia)&amp;t=h" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://maps.google.com/maps?ll=38.035,-78.505&amp;spn=0.01,0.01&amp;q=38.035,-78.505 (University%20of%20Virginia)&amp;t=h', 'University of Virginia']);" rel="geolocation" target="_blank" title="University of Virginia">
            University of Virginia
           </a>
           , explained that there are cases in which a specific dog (not breed) might be better tolerated by a specific person, for unknown reasons. We think there really are differences in protein production between dogs that may help one patient and not another, Dr. Platts-Mills said.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           All dogs shed, and all dogs produce dander and saliva in some degree. As noted above, the amount of the allergenic protein present on the dander and in saliva varies by breed. Also, the amount of the allergen can be reduced or eliminated in individual dogs by treatments such as bathing. But for most breeds, when not regularly bathed, even a dog that sheds very little or has little dander can trigger a reaction in a sensitive person. Also, the length of a dogs hair is
           <a href="http://www.completeherbalguide.com/2012/12/5-ways-to-cut-pet-allergy.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.completeherbalguide.com/2012/12/5-ways-to-cut-pet-allergy.html', 'really insignificant']);" target="_blank" title="Hypoallergenic dogs">
            really insignificant
           </a>
           when you consider that Portuguese Water Dogs and Poodles are both hypoallergenic and both have long hair!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Size may be a factor in determining the most suitable hypoallergenic dog breeds. It is possible that the total body surface area of the dog is more indicative of reduced production of allergens than its breed. Smaller dogs will also leave fewer environmental pollutants containing dog dander and dog allergens (reduced fecal matter, urine and saliva). Small hairless dogs may be less likely to cause allergic reactions because its so easy to bathe them and the dander falls off them. Dogs may leave behind urine, saliva and fecal matter as allergen sources. Dogs with access to the outdoors may introduce outdoor allergens such as mold and pollen with larger animals tracking in more of these allergens. It is well established that most individuals with
           <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allergies_in_dogs" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allergies_in_dogs', 'dog allergy']);" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Allergies in dogs">
            dog allergy
           </a>
           also suffer with additional environmental allergies. Individuals with dog allergy may also be at increased risk for human protein hypersensitivity with cross-reactivity of dog dander allergen and human seminal fluid.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Which are the best hypoallergenic dog breeds?
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           While no dog has officially won the hypoallergenic dog breed award of the year (yet), here is a list of some dogs that tend to be better fighters of allergens and related health problems. Again, no dog is really
           <a href="http://www.thedogdaily.com/p/health/dogs_allergies/index.html#.U6XYDY1dVy9" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.thedogdaily.com/p/health/dogs_allergies/index.html#.U6XYDY1dVy9', 'hypoallergenic']);" target="_blank" title="Hypoallergenic dog myth">
            hypoallergenic
           </a>
           . In reality, some are just less likely to cause allergic reactions. Here are the top ten hypoallergenic dog breeds:
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            9. CHINESE CRESTED
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Chinese-Crested.jpeg">
            <img alt="Chinese Crested hypoallergenic" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3187" height="335" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Chinese-Crested-420x335.jpeg" title="Chinese Crested" width="420"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The most efficient way to avoid dog-induced allergies is to get a dog without any, or with very little, hair. You cant go wrong with the Chinese crested; they barely shed at all. They do produce dander and can be a little clingy, but theyre very good with children.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            8. BASENJI
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/basenji.jpeg">
            <img alt="Basenji hypoallergenic" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3184" height="350" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/basenji-420x350.jpeg" title="Basenji" width="420"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           These pups dont shed or bark, they have very little dander and theyre darling, so theyre a great choice. They do sometimes make an odd yodelling noise, and they can be hard to train, like most hounds). But this stubborn demeanor comes packed with a lot of lovable personality.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            7. ITALIAN GREYHOUND
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Italian-Greyhound.jpeg">
            <img alt="Italian-Greyhound Hypoallergenic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3188" height="288" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Italian-Greyhound.jpeg" title="Italian-Greyhound" width="344"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Perfecto! This little
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/dog-breed/greyhound" title="Greyhound">
            Italian
           </a>
           has a thin coat, so he barely sheds, and its easy to keep him clean of allergens. The breed is very playful and loyal, and does not need a big yard. Theyre extremely sensitive to cold, though, so this isnt a good breed for a family living in a chilly climate.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            6. POODLE
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Poodle.jpeg">
            <img alt="Poodle hypoallergenic" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3189" height="329" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Poodle-420x329.jpeg" title="Poodle" width="420"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Like the schnauzer, the
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/dog-breed/poodle" title="Poodle">
            poodle
           </a>
           comes in three sizes: Standard, miniature and toy. They do not shed, but they do require a lot of grooming and care. Theyre easy to train, but can get a little yappy.Poodles too prissy for your liking? Try a labradoodle. They have the hypoallergenic coat of a poodle but on a Labradors body.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            5. SHIH TZU
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Shih-Tzy.jpeg">
            <img alt="Shih Tzu hypoallergenic" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3192" height="420" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Shih-Tzy-343x420.jpeg" title="Shih Tzu" width="343"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Despite those long, silky locks, this breed sheds very lightly. Although they need a lot of grooming, they are affectionate, kind and easygoing. But be wary:
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/dog-breed/shih-tzu" title="Shih Tzu">
            Shih tzus
           </a>
           tend to become jealous of babies and toddlers, so protect your other little loved ones from their insecurity.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            4. BEDLINGTON TERRIER
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Bedlington-Terrier.jpeg">
            <img alt="Bedlington Terrier Hypoallergenic" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3185" height="311" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Bedlington-Terrier-420x311.jpeg" title="Bedlington Terrier" width="420"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Although at first glance, he looks more like a lamb than a dog, the Bedlington terrier could be the perfect canine addition to a laid-back family. These do not shed and are very mild-mannered.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            3. YORKSHIRE TERRIER
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/yorkshire-terrier.jpeg">
            <img alt="Yorkshire-terrier hypoallergenic" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3193" height="420" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/yorkshire-terrier-280x420.jpeg" title="Yorkshire-terrier" width="280"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Dont have a big yard? Then a
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/dog-breed/yorkshire-terrier" title="Yorkshire Terrier">
            Yorkie
           </a>
           is perfect for you! They are content playing and cuddling with you indoors. This pup doesnt shed and or have a lot of dander. She does require heavy grooming, however, so keep the brush in hand when she paws at your lap.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            2. SCHNAUZER
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Schnauzer.jpeg">
            <img alt="Schnauzer hypoallergenic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3191" height="250" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Schnauzer.jpeg" title="Schnauzer" width="300"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           These shed-less pups are great for the owner who loves to be the center of attention  or the kid who loves playing Follow the Leader. The schnauzer loves his owners so much that he wont let them out of his sight, so expect to have a buddy by your side at all times. He does need a firm hand, though, to balance his stubborn, energetic and protective temper. Schnauzers come in three sizes: Standard, giant and miniature.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            1. BICHON FRIS
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Bichon.jpeg">
            <img alt="Bichon hypoallergenic" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3186" height="420" src="http://bunkblog.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Bichon-336x420.jpeg" title="Bichon" width="336"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Dont let the puffy coat fool you. These little
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/dog-breed/bichon-frise" title="Bichon Frise">
            marshmallows
           </a>
           are jolly, easy to train and dont shed. As long as you brush their curly tresses, they wont shower dander and dust. This breed is great for a family wanting a small, cheerful dog as an easygoing companion.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Are pugs allergenic?
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/pugs/do-pugs-shed" title="Do Pugs Shed?">
            Pugs DO shed
           </a>
           and they are NOT considered a hypoallergenic dog. That being said, I have researched and found a lot of interesting thoughts on this matter.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Some people believe that pugs are hypoallergenic because they have wrinkles and those trap in the little allergens that are trying to escape into the air surrounding the pug dog. I personally do not think there are much more to those
           <a href="http://bunkblog.net/home/724-2" title="Pug Wrinkles">
            wrinkles
           </a>
           that good looks and a simple storage place for extra food.
          </p>
          <h2>
           How to manage your dog allergy
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Each persons
           <a href="http://www.humanesociety.org/animals/resources/tips/allergies_pets.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.humanesociety.org/animals/resources/tips/allergies_pets.html', 'allergies are different']);" target="_blank" title="Pet allergies">
            allergies are different
           </a>
           . No matter which dog you choose, you should spend some time with it before agreeing to take it home for good, to test your reaction to that individual. I know some people with allergies who do fine with Pugs, and others who can not even be in the same house with one. And some people do okay with one individual of a breed, but cant be near others of the same breed.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           You also need to decide for yourself how far you are willing to go for your dog. Are you willing to take allergy medicine? Get allergy shots? Vacuum daily? Do you groom/bathe your dog regularly? All of those things will also determine which types/breeds of dogs are possible for you.
          </p>

              <p>
               I am severely allergic to dogs and cats. If I am around them (ex. German shepherds, Chihuahuas, Huskies, and even the Bichon frises) for more than 5-10 minutes I begin wheezing, coughing and experience trouble breathing and hives. With that said, in my school we have a PT program which uses dogs to help patients regain mobility. The dog of choice is the Samoyed. The Samoyed owner was in my class and sat near me with this dog and I was scared to death but did not develop a reaction. I sat near him for 3 hours and even petted him. It was amazing since Ive only been able to do this with Schnauzers. I think the article makes it clear that some may develop a reaction while others may not and in this case the Samoyed was great for me. Ive now added the Samoyed to my list of potential pets.
              </p>
