       <h2>
        The Seductive Myth Of The Perfect Online Dating Profile
       </h2>
       <div class="meta">
        <span class="date">
         August 13, 2014
        </span>
        <span class="comments">
         <a href="http://winkwisely.com/the-seductive-myth-of-the-perfect-online-dating-profile/#respond" title="Comment on The Seductive Myth Of The Perfect Online Dating Profile">
          0 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="content">
        <p>
         <img alt="perfect okcupid profile" class="aligncenter wp-image-1751" height="476" src="http://winkwisely.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/perfect-okcupid-profile.png" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You might have noticed that OkCupids CEO recently revealed the online dating site
         <a href="http://blog.okcupid.com/index.php/we-experiment-on-human-beings/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://blog.okcupid.com/index.php/we-experiment-on-human-beings/', 'runs blind experiments on their users']);" target="_blank" title="We Experiment On Human Beings!">
          runs blind experiments on their users
         </a>
         . While Christian Rudders blog postwas met with controversy, OkCupid has built their reputation on providing an information-centric approach to online dating: they use data to match users together, improve their site, and even publish the insights they find in the trends.
        </p>
        <p>
         Early this March, OkCupid and Match partnered with Wired Magazine to publish an article:
         <a href="http://www.wired.com/2014/02/how-to-create-good-online-dating-profile/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.wired.com/2014/02/how-to-create-good-online-dating-profile/', 'How to Create the Perfect Online Dating Profile, in 25 Infographics']);" target="_blank" title="How to Create the Perfect Online Dating Profile, in 25 Infographics">
          How to Create the Perfect Online Dating Profile, in 25 Infographics
         </a>
         . The factoidsincluded a ranked chart of the top 1000 words to include in an OkCupid profile, pastimes sortedby highest ranked on male or female profiles, and illustrated examples of descriptions to include or discard. The article claimed that self-describingwith keywords like
         <strong>
          Surfing
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          Yoga
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          The Ocean
         </strong>
         , or
         <strong>
          Radiohead
         </strong>
         will optimize anonline dating profile to be more successful.
        </p>
        <p>
         The problem is this guide was built under three assumptions.
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Some profiles are more desirable than others.
         </li>
         <li>
          A profiles desirability canbe measured by the overall number of hits and responses.
         </li>
         <li>
          These desirable profiles are better for all users.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1754" style="width: 340px">
         <img alt="2202FF_lovebynumbers05_inline" class="wp-image-1754 size-full" height="554" src="http://winkwisely.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/2202FF_lovebynumbers05_inline.jpg" width="330"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          An illustration from Wireds article. Is it better to have more dates with people who dont want to date you, or fewer dates with people who share your interests?
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The third assumption breaks down quickly. What happens to someone who wants to follow Wireds advice but hates Radiohead, is afraid of the ocean, and finds yoga incredibly boring? They probably will not want to go on a date with someone who expects yoga classes by the ocean, especially if it involves sun salutationsto Hail To The Thief. While the article points out that 48 percent of the profile pics of the most popular gay men on OkCupid were snapped outside, a gay man who hates the outdoors is not likely to have a long-lasting relationship with someone who was led to expect the man to enjoy long hikes.
        </p>
        <p>
         If Wireds advice encourages readers to describe themselves with desirable qualities that they do not want or have, then they are setting their readers up for failure. Worse, if readers hide undesirable intereststhat they want or need to share with partners,they will be lesslikely to attract potential dates who have the sameundesirable interests.
        </p>
        <p>
         In an article on Mandatory,
         <a href="http://www.mandatory.com/2012/11/13/ok-cupid-an-exploration-into-just-how-low-some-guys-will-go/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mandatory.com/2012/11/13/ok-cupid-an-exploration-into-just-how-low-some-guys-will-go/', 'Ok Cupid: An Exploration Into Just How Low Some Guys Will Go']);" target="_blank" title="A link, not an endorsement.">
          Ok Cupid: An Exploration Into Just How Low Some Guys Will Go
         </a>
         , writer Rob Fee wanted to make a profile that was
         <em>
          so undesirable
         </em>
         that men could only respond if they either did not bother to read the text or only cared about the fictitious womans picture. This profile had it all: Racism! Poor spelling! Obsession with a non-existent movie in the Happy Feet franchise!Krumping! In the description Feetossed in another keyword meant to be undesirable: I am very vegan by the way.
        </p>
        <p>
         Vegan is a probably not a word that Wired would rate as highly desirable. Alot of people see itas a deal-breaker. It is also a word I described myself with on OkCupid. I happen to be a friendly vegan. (Dont worry, I told you.)
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://matecheck.ca/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://matecheck.ca/', '']);">
          <img alt="Online , dating, online dating, relationaships" class="alignright wp-image-1814" height="225" src="http://winkwisely.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/WinkWisely-Share-Repost-150x150.jpg" width="225"/>
         </a>
         Including the v-word in my online profile did not turn out to be a mistake. If it was a deal-breaker for anyone, they found out right away and either did not message me or rejected me quickly and painlessly. In the meantime, I received messages from women who were actively seeking out other vegans. By describing myself with a polarizing term that was desirable for some women, but not the majority of women, I was able to filter for peoplewho are more likely to be interested in me.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want to make the most of online dating, my advice is to forget about attracting
         <em>
          everybody
         </em>
         . Developyour niche by building your profile for people who would want to date you; hopefully people who have qualities that you can appreciate too. Include terms that will appeal to them, whilegiving whoever does not want to date you the opportunity to opt-out fast.
        </p>
        <hr/>

         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Green Beard
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <em>
          Green Beardis Canadian pedant and non-fiction writer in his mid-20s. While not gorging onkale or scrutinizingbad advice on the internet, he tends to hislong-term relationship with theothervegetarian he met on OkCupid, and their wild beast of a smallrescue dog.
         </em>
        </p>
