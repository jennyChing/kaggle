        <h1>
         Hotter Than Fish Grease: Mahina Garcia
        </h1>
        <!-- carousel, header image and video -->
        <div class="social-buttons">
         <span class="time">
          29 Jan 2015
         </span>
         <span class="tweet-button">
          <a class="twitter-share-button" data-count="none" data-text="Hotter Than Fish Grease: Mahina Garcia" data-url="http://stabmag.com/hotter-than-fish-grease-mahina-garcia/" href="http://twitter.com/share" target="_blank">
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="plusone-button">
          <g:plusone annotation="none" size="medium">
          </g:plusone>
         </span>
         <span class="fblike-button">
          <fb:like action="like" href="http://stabmag.com/hotter-than-fish-grease-mahina-garcia/" layout="button_count" send="false" show_faces="false" width="90">
          </fb:like>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="article-content">
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           Let Stab introduce you toMahina Garcia, 20 years old and from the North Shore of Oahu
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <strong>
           Stab: Please, describe yourself.
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Mahina: I was born on Oahu and live here now, but Kauai is my second home where we have lots of farm land. Its a beautiful place with fruit trees, goats, chickens, cows and a few dogs and theres a lot of labor involved to keep it maintained. I live a simple life and Im not materialistic. As long as I have food on the table, my family, my love, God and the beach Im happy.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_75574" style="width: 410px">
          <img alt="surf_067_069_mahina-2" class="wp-image-75574 size-full" height="569" src="http://stabmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/surf_067_069_mahina-2.jpg" width="400"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo: Ana Monique
          </p>
         </div>
         <p class="p1">
          <strong>
           Loves and loathes of the North Shore?
          </strong>
          Well, I wouldnt have it any other way! The life we live is what many can only dream of. Who wouldnt want to walk around barefoot eating poke &amp; acai bowls in a bikini? The only thing I hate about the North Shore is the crazy, bumper-to-bumper traffic every contest season.
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <strong>
           Your father, Kaiborg, is a charming and lovely gent, but hes certainly an imposing figure.
          </strong>
          Kaiborg who? Just kidding! Im actually more afraid of my mother, except Dad yelled at me the other day and my heart fell out of my chest! But Mom is the only woman who can lay down the rules to Dad. Hes bad-ass, very good at Jujitsu, and Cross Fit is his newfound joy. I couldnt have asked for a better Dad, hes a gentle giant  if you dont do anything stupid, that is.
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <strong>
           I imagine boys werent in a rush to meet your parents, then.
          </strong>
          Being the daughter of Kaiborg is actually a blessing because its saved me from a lot of typical teenage drama. The most common line I use to get was: Youre really pretty and I want to talk to you but your Dad is too scary. It was the lamest excuse Id ever heard and Id tell them.
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <strong>
           But one gent didnt fold, now youre dating a Florence brother!
          </strong>
          Yeah, I never went on a real date and I never had a serious boyfriend until Mr. Nathan Florence came around. Nathan and I actually dated in third grade, where I made the first move of course, and weve been best friends since. He is the coolest cat and he brings out the best in people. They were well raised.
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <strong>
           For a beautiful woman like you, modelling must come easy.
          </strong>
          I was scouted at a young age by Niche Models and moved to LA and Miami. There, I modelled full-time for four years. I scored jobs with amazing clients, and the money was unbeatable! But, all of a sudden my hips grew and I started to fill into my womanly body.
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <strong>
           Its a rose-coloured glass industry. Did you learn hard lessons?
          </strong>
          The industry treats hips, thighs, butts and boobs like theyre a sin and I struggled alongside younger models. But I couldnt keep living that lifestyle and I was sick of being what other people wanted me to be. So many girls and boys are told they are too fat. Ive watched friends struggle with their image. But I released I had to embrace my curves because the healthiest, strongest me is better than a weak, unhealthy me. People seem to forget we come in all shapes and sizes, skinny is beautiful, if its natural. Curvy is beautiful, if its natural and healthy. I am trying to be the change I want to see!
         </p>
