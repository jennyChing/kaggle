           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-c0e3ad86-2095-4114-99aa-5e3be05c5196">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              "
              <strong>
               6:43
              </strong>
              Alarm goes off. No snooze. Pull on John Eshaya sweatpants and clogs."
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-1c783e7c-e037-476f-a12e-abe98ecfda15">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              "
              <strong>
               6:50
              </strong>
              Make coffee (fair trade); microwave Zen Bakery muffin (vegan) First breakfast."
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-8b1bc1fe-18f1-4814-a172-4078c901e355">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              "
              <strong>
               6:45
              </strong>
              Head down to the home gym for a workout."
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-f70b8aa1-51d9-4c93-b8b8-c69a4ed813b8">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              "
              <strong>
               6:45
              </strong>
              I pour my coffee and make breakfast and then sit by myself in my living room. Sometimes with the Post or WSJ, sometimes the news, sometimes my iPhone. Breakfast could be a smoothie or ezekial toast with cashew butter, or oatmeal with chia seeds and almond butter."
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-55005a2f-e26c-4d1a-a2c8-814b1dc23f6a">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Almost all of the routines of these high-achieving women include luxuries like drinking warm coffee, doing yoga
              <em>
               (without a toddler rolling around under her)
              </em>
              and eating quinoa smoothies for breakfast.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_ad_c" data-view="droplets/ad">
            <div class="droplet droplet_ad" id="ad-365feccf-a6da-4b5a-8b43-24659a79bf67">
             <div data-height="250" data-slot="/32950521/SM_300x250_ATF_Article" data-view="partials/ad" data-width="300">
              <div class="ad" id="3b56616f-c49a-4f84-f3d5-8ce0c4dcc2a3" style="width:300px; height:250px;">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-68a69264-4005-4fb6-9f85-e9a2ad0c198c">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The article is meant to show how people thrive from the consistency of routines, but for most moms, the reality of the morning routine is, well, a little bit different. Most of us don't get time to ourselves to read the news or workout. I rounded up a couple of friends to share their morning routines.  I think it's safe to say that many of our mornings as moms look less like those in
              <em>
               Forbes
              </em>
              and more like this:
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-0dcee531-7bda-4591-ae40-9e629de28758">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               1. Kate Parlin, Writer at
              </strong>
              <em>
               <strong>
                <a href="http://shakespearesmom.com/">
                 Shakespeare's Mom,
                </a>
               </strong>
              </em>
              <strong>
               Non-Morning-Person, and Mom of Three
              </strong>
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-e166a617-1e42-4518-9738-0daafb30c710">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               6:00
              </strong>
              Wake up to iPhone alarm. Choose an attitude of peace and productivity for the day. JUST KIDDING. Curse iPhone alarm for being so freaking hard to snooze. And for possibly waking up the children in my bed.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-bb66d1bb-546e-4e25-a7ba-7c1f7ba28b5c">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               6:30
              </strong>
              Shuffle to the shower.
              <em>
               (On a good day. Some days, there is no showering.)
              </em>
              Get dressed in my "WhateverTheHellBrandTargetSells" sweatpants."
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-993d72a6-69af-4fc8-9024-0710437cb23b">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               7:00
              </strong>
              Go downstairs to let the dogs out. Step in dog pee on the way. Curse dogs. Clean up pee. Assemble the children's organic kale smoothie  oh who am I kidding? Assemble regular old cereal and milk. Throw in a banana.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-3fbcea00-efeb-4f4f-b79f-30f6a6c24cae">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               7:15
              </strong>
              Check to make sure the twins have extra underwear in their backpacks. They don't. They do have a plastic eggplant and an acorn. Run upstairs to frantically search for underwear. This is considered "exercise." Run back downstairs to make coffee.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-e68f7063-23d3-49ee-879a-22cc79c84009">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               7:30
              </strong>
              Go back upstairs to get three-year-old twins up and dressed for preschool. Do everything twice. Shepherd the twins downstairs to eat breakfast. Say, "Please finish eating!" Say it again. Try not to yell, "YOU HAVE ONE JOB RIGHT NOW AND IT IS TO EAT CEREAL. HOW IS THAT A HARD JOB?!?" Supervise teeth-brushing, putting on of shoes, and zipping up of coats as husband brings two-year-old downstairs.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-cca02b7a-c224-4f39-878e-8fe8db5f51d7">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               8:15
              </strong>
              Maneuver three small bodies out the door and into car seats. Break up a thousand whiny fights. Feel sweaty and anxious. Chug more coffee on the way to preschool and think,
              <em>
               Oh, hey. I didn't get to eat breakfast. Again. And look at that. I'm wearing slippers. Looks like today is not going to be the day I become a "leader."
              </em>
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_ad_c" data-view="droplets/ad">
            <div class="droplet droplet_ad" id="ad-15d1884d-afc7-4154-b8cc-8f571ce8c3ed">
             <div data-height="250" data-slot="/32950521/SM_300x250_ATF_Article" data-view="partials/ad" data-width="300">
              <div class="ad" id="2ba6ca2b-9376-d214-4eb1-22a4b215569d" style="width:300px; height:250px;">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-f7664c44-3eae-4737-8391-c25305c1de99">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               2. Rachel Cashen, First-Time-Mom With a Newborn Baby
              </strong>
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-224d78f6-d42f-4ed6-b709-5690caec5bc6">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               4:30
              </strong>
              Baby cries. Reach over and put pacifier in mouth hoping she'll go back to sleep. Pacifier falls out four more times. Wonder if scotch tape can hold the pacifier in.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-30c4b98d-42fd-4739-bd6e-8b78624aac49">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               5:00
              </strong>
              Finally feed baby while constructing a business plan for a service that delivers coffee and donuts to new moms. Plan to definitely get a donut today. Try to sleep and hope to total the nights sleep at a minimum of four hours.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-aae0c8bf-afef-4237-823d-8be987e1171a">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               5:30
              </strong>
              Baby cries again. Baby eats an organic breakfast made from last night's kale and spinach salad with prosciutto. Just kidding, I had a breakfast sandwich from Dunkin Donuts.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-1193d584-3161-4b6e-8fc0-24cffa6c9597">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               6:00
              </strong>
              Baby still awake. Stares at me while I change her diaper. I detect she's spied daylight out the window and senses it's time to greet the day. But not with coffee and donuts.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-e1be36fc-c217-49b4-bf4a-6b0f3ef1efb6">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               6:30
              </strong>
              Baby showing no signs of sleeping. She's still staring at me. Maybe she wants a donut. Hand baby to her well-rested, chipper daddy while he enjoys a leisurely breakfast and browses all his iPhone notifications he's missed while sleeping the entire night.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-4ffcdb9d-7c74-4cbf-b015-7fce66f2a6ac">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               7:00
              </strong>
              Shower at warp speed, thinking I hear baby screaming the whole time. Turns out it was auditory hallucinations due to lack of sleep. Throw on husband's sweatshirt and spit-up spotted sweatpants.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="droplet_p_c" data-view="droplets/p">
            <div class="droplet droplet_p" id="d-c9d779ae-2d1a-4cda-af86-210e0b8890be">
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               7:15
