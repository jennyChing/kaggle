                <h1 class="entry-title">
                 Stage Fright
                </h1>
               </div>
               <div class="entry_header_bottom">
                <div class="hidden">
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="entry_content entry-content">
               <div class="pad">
                <div class="wdgpo wdgpo_medium_nocount">
                 <g:plusone count="false" href="http://speakfearlessly.net/stage-fright/" size="medium">
                 </g:plusone>
                </div>
                <p>
                 That well-known dramatic fear of public speaking (40% of American adults suffer from it) is also called stage fright.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <img align="left" alt="" height="175" src="http://i261.photobucket.com/albums/ii76/THG_tribe/fear20factor202.jpg" width="203"/>
                 Your hands shake, your mouth dries up, your heart pounds loud enough (youre sure) for the audience to hear. Youd like to escape but your knees are shaking or too weak for you to run. Youve heard of panic attacks and now youre having one. Join a very large segment of humanity who have this phobia, also called performance anxiety. You can add your name to a list which includes Barbra Streisand, Rod Stewart, Carly Simon and more.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Here are the four As of stage fright:
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
                 1. Anticipation: Imagining the disaster youll be on stage.
                </p>
                <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
                 2. Avoidance: Not going on stage and thus losing the chance to cope and grow an inner strength.
                </p>
                <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
                 3. Anxiety: Those panicky physical symptoms which youre sure will cause you to pass out.
                </p>
                <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
                 4. Appraisal: The Whew! I did it! conclusions after the performance or presentation.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Should you try to overcome your stage fright, here are some helpful hints:
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
                 1. Breathe from the diaphragm. Practice this type of breathing before going on stage.
                </p>
                <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
                 2. No one can tell youre a wreck. Its just your inside self thats panicking.
                </p>
                <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
                 3. Realize you will be anxious and use the shot of adrenaline which comes from our fight or flight response. Turn it into power. You never know; dealing with anxiety just might improve your presentation.
                </p>
                <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
                 4. Focus. Know your lines or your material.
                </p>
