    <h1>
     <i>
      Dongyangosaurus
     </i>
    </h1>
    <img border="" src="../../images/species/d/dongyangosaurus-size.jpg"/>
    <p align="left">
     <b>
      Name:
     </b>
     <i>
      Dongyangosaurus
     </i>
     (Dongyang lizard).
     <br/>
     <b>
      Phonetic:
     </b>
     Dong-yang-o-sore-us.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Named By:
     </b>
     Lu Junchang Yoichi Azuma Chen Rongjun
Zheng Wenjie &amp; Jin Xingsheng - 2008.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Classification:
     </b>
     Chordata, Reptilia, Dinosauria,
Saurischia, Sauropoda, Titanosauria, Saltasauridae.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Species:
     </b>
     <i>
      D. sinensis
     </i>
     (type).
     <br/>
     <b>
      Diet:
     </b>
     Herbivore.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Size:
     </b>
     Uncertain but estimated to be somewhere about
15 meters long.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Known locations:
     </b>
     China, Zhejiang Province -
Fangyan Formation.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Time period:
     </b>
     Estimated at some point during the
earlier stages of the Late Cretaceous.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Fossil representation:
     </b>
     Partial but articulated
postcranial skeleton comprising 10 dorsal (back), and two
caudal (tail) vertebrae as well as a complete pelvis.
    </p>
    <p align="left">
     <i>
      Dongyangosaurus
     </i>
     is a genus of
     <a href="http://www.prehistoric-wildlife.com/articles/sauropod-dinosaurs.html">
      titanosaurid
     </a>
     dinosaur that is known to have lived in
China at some point during the earlier stages of the late Cretaceous,
Though only known from partial remains,
     <i>
      Dongyangosaurus
     </i>
     has been
confidently identified as a saltasaurid titanosaur, and similar in
form to
     <a href="http://www.prehistoric-wildlife.com/species/s/saltasaurus.html">
      <i>
       Saltasaurus
      </i>
     </a>
     which is known from South America. The genus that
     <i>
      Dongyangosaurus
     </i>
     seems to be closest too however is
     <a href="http://www.prehistoric-wildlife.com/species/o/opisthocoelicaudia.html">
      <i>
       Opisthocoelicaudia
      </i>
     </a>
     from Mongolia. Overall it is unknown if
     <i>
      Dongyangosaurus
     </i>
     had any
bizarre features, or even if it had similar bony armour plates in the
skin that
     <i>
      Saltasaurus
     </i>
     is known to have had. The
pubic bone of
     <i>
      Dongyangosaurus
     </i>
     is notably shorter than the ischium
however,
something that in dinosaurs as a whole is usually the other way around.
    </p>
    <p align="left">
     <b>
      Further reading
     </b>
     <br/>
     - A new titanosauriform sauropod from the early Late Cretaceous of
Dongyang, Zhejiang Province. - Acta Geologica Sinica (English
Edition) 82 (2): 225235. - Lu Junchang Yoichi Azuma Chen
Rongjun Zheng Wenjie &amp; Jin Xingsheng - 2008.
    </p>
