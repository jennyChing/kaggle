          <div class="content">
           <p>
            While solar arrays and other renewable energy technology can help homeowners go off the grid, such technology is still too expensive and impractical for many of us to consider as a sole source of electricity, given the high costs of the equipment and installation.
           </p>
           <p>
            But these days, its becoming easier than ever to supplement your electrical grid power with alternative energy sources, thanks to simple plug-in devices.
           </p>
           <p>
            Consider a plug-in wind turbine called the
            <a href="http://www.clarianpower.com/index.html" rel="nofollow">
             Jellyfish.
            </a>
            At only 36 inches high, the micro-turbine isnt much bigger than a typical household fan. The small turbine can be mounted on your roof or on an existing street light pole, and plugged directly into a household electrical socket. But instead of drawing power from your electrical grid, the turbine harvests the winds force to feed electricity back to the grid: The appliance can generate around 40 kilowatt hours of electricity each month, enough to light an average home with high-efficiency bulbs for the whole month and offset lighting costs in your electric bill.
           </p>
           <p>
            The Jellyfish is not yet available in stores, but the manufacturer, Clarian Power, expects it to retail for under $800; it should provide payback in 5 to 8 years, factoring in eligible tax credits and rebates.
           </p>
           <p>
            Simple plug-in renewable energy generators are also available in solar form: Clarian plans to offer the
            <a href="http://www.clarianpower.com/solar.html" rel="nofollow">
             SmartBox Solar panel
            </a>
            at a similar price point, and another company,
            <a href="http://www.spinrayenergy.com/" rel="nofollow">
             SpinRay Energy
            </a>
            , is already offering hanging solar panels which can be easily installed on a home deck or lawn and plug into your existing circuitry. Both systems are modular, so that you can easily combine multiple units to boost your energy-generating capacities.
           </p>
           <p>
            Hybrid home power is ideal for boosting energy-efficiency when youre not prepared to make the commitment to go completely off the grid. New technologies like these make saving energy as simple as plugging in.
           </p>
