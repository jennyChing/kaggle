         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <p class="graf--p" id="f518" name="f518">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            Five Things a Nanny Should Know the First Day on the Job
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="fa90" name="fa90">
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            As a nanny, you probably spend more time prepping for the interview with the family and less time thinking about what will happen if you actually get the job. Check out this article for a few tips on how to properly prepare for your first day.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="31b4" name="31b4">
           Youve nailed the interview and shown your new employers that youre trustworthy enough to look after their kid. Great! The tough part isnt over though  you still need to dazzle them with your excellent nanny skills from the very beginning.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="adb2" name="adb2">
           The truth is that the first day working as a nanny for a new family is both exciting and challenging. You need to learn the kids routine, familiarize yourself with the home and the parents schedule, adapt to a new family structure. Theres a lot of stress that comes with the job, so its easy to overlook something. Weve put together this list to help get you ready.
          </p>
          <figure class="graf--figure" id="72b6" name="72b6">
           <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 640px; max-height: 424px;">
            <div class="aspect-ratio-fill" style="padding-bottom: 66.3%;">
            </div>
            <img class="graf-image" data-height="424" data-image-id="1*IdURRgZ87rMf6UdVhxrLYg.jpeg" data-width="640" src="https://d262ilb51hltx0.cloudfront.net/max/800/1*IdURRgZ87rMf6UdVhxrLYg.jpeg"/>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p class="graf--p" id="41e1" name="41e1">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            Practical stuff
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="2bac" name="2bac">
           First off, you need a set of keys. You should also know where the spare key is hidden. Some families, however, prefer to give out the garage door code. If thats the case, make sure you have it memorized. Telephone numbers are also important. You should have the parents personal and business numbers programed in your cell phone before your first day. Additionally, a good idea would be to ask for
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://www.parttimenanny.org/blog/what-to-include-in-an-emergency-plan/" href="http://www.parttimenanny.org/blog/what-to-include-in-an-emergency-plan/" rel="nofollow">
            emergency contact numbers
           </a>
            from close relatives, friends, or neighbors. You never know when they might need them.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="7886" name="7886">
           Then, you should double check that your work agreement is complete and signed before the big day arrives. Both you and the parents should have a copy. They might also need to submit a form granting you permission to pick up the kid from school, if thats the case. Make sure its taken care of before you actually start working. As a side benefit, asking about the form will show your new employers how cautious and professional you are.
          </p>
          <figure class="graf--figure" id="de14" name="de14">
           <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 700px; max-height: 466px;">
            <div class="aspect-ratio-fill" style="padding-bottom: 66.5%;">
            </div>
            <img class="graf-image" data-action="zoom" data-action-value="1*L8usEmlBd7zO5QjSoTWs6g.jpeg" data-height="1996" data-image-id="1*L8usEmlBd7zO5QjSoTWs6g.jpeg" data-width="3000" src="https://d262ilb51hltx0.cloudfront.net/max/800/1*L8usEmlBd7zO5QjSoTWs6g.jpeg"/>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p class="graf--p" id="1254" name="1254">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            The kids routine
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="61c0" name="61c0">
           Sit down with the parents and go through the childs routine together. You should know when the little one needs to be dropped off at school and picked up in the afternoon, as well as when they have any activities planned that you have to drive them to. Figure out the amount of time it will take you to drive to the school, so you wont be late on your very first day. Also, familiarize yourself with the kids nap and bedtime routine and ask about any special requirements when it comes to nutrition.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="0203" name="0203">
           If youre expected to take the kid out for some recreational activities, ask about what places the parents approve of. Maybe theyre okay with you taking their child to the park, but the shopping mall is strictly off limits. You should know these things from the very beginning. Additionally, understand what the kid
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://www.supernanny.co.uk/Advice/-/Parenting-Skills/-/Discipline-and-Reward/House-Rules.aspx" href="http://www.supernanny.co.uk/Advice/-/Parenting-Skills/-/Discipline-and-Reward/House-Rules.aspx" rel="nofollow">
            is and isnt allowed to do
           </a>
           . This includes watching TV, playing video games, surfing the web, and so on. Never try to win over a child by allowing them to do something their parents disapprove of  its a surefire way to lose your new employers trust and respect.
          </p>
          <figure class="graf--figure" id="8060" name="8060">
           <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 640px; max-height: 427px;">
            <div class="aspect-ratio-fill" style="padding-bottom: 66.7%;">
            </div>
            <img class="graf-image" data-height="427" data-image-id="1*-2qSpEU_AWfFCMMeYMOXGw.jpeg" data-width="640" src="https://d262ilb51hltx0.cloudfront.net/max/800/1*-2qSpEU_AWfFCMMeYMOXGw.jpeg"/>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p class="graf--p" id="743a" name="743a">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            Safety issues
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="d1e0" name="d1e0">
           Where is the first aid kit located? Is the child on any medication? If so, what are the dosage requirements? Do they suffer from any medical conditions you should know of? Just in case, ask for the number of the kids pediatrician and get a letter of permission from the parents allowing you to take the children to the doctor.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="319d" name="319d">
           Having all your bases covered before your first day ensures that it will run smoothly and youll have less to worry about. Also, ask about any allergies or particular food issues the child may have.
          </p>
          <figure class="graf--figure" id="0928" name="0928">
           <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 427px; max-height: 481px;">
            <div class="aspect-ratio-fill" style="padding-bottom: 112.6%;">
            </div>
            <img class="graf-image" data-height="481" data-image-id="1*kX1A0L_SAwzgK0rqxlyZ2w.jpeg" data-width="427" src="https://d262ilb51hltx0.cloudfront.net/max/800/1*kX1A0L_SAwzgK0rqxlyZ2w.jpeg"/>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p class="graf--p" id="b053" name="b053">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            Additional tasks
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="fa5d" name="fa5d">
           If youll also handle cooking and cleaning for the family, make sure you know how all the appliances work and where the cleaning supplies are stored. If the family has a gardener, maid, dog walker or any other kind of help coming in, familiarize yourself with their schedules. If they have pets, inquire about whether theyre expecting you to feed them or take them out for a walk.
          </p>
          <figure class="graf--figure" id="f730" name="f730">
           <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 640px; max-height: 426px;">
            <div class="aspect-ratio-fill" style="padding-bottom: 66.60000000000001%;">
            </div>
            <img class="graf-image" data-height="426" data-image-id="1*LpqbhrqPLQkVFt9sM6wAhA.jpeg" data-width="640" src="https://d262ilb51hltx0.cloudfront.net/max/800/1*LpqbhrqPLQkVFt9sM6wAhA.jpeg"/>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p class="graf--p" id="fb46" name="fb46">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            Expectations regarding communication
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="a2d5" name="a2d5">
           Keeping in touch with the parents during the day may be required, so ask about their expectations before you start work. Do they want an hourly update on how the little one is doing? Should you text or call? Is it a good idea to call them, or would it be best to wait for them to reach out, to avoid disturbing them at work? Make sure all these questions are answered before the first day.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="8dc2" name="8dc2">
           Additionally, if you were hired through a nanny agency, they might expect you to keep them posted about your progress as well. For instance,
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://bestinclassnanny.com/" href="http://bestinclassnanny.com/" rel="nofollow">
            Best in Class Nanny
           </a>
           , a nanny agency from Atlanta, calls to both the nanny and the family to make sure everything is going smoothly. Other agencies may demand you to call in regularly, to give them an update about how things are going. Those calls are important, so dont skip them  especially not after your first day.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p graf--last" id="a078" name="a078">
           Being a nanny takes a lot of responsibility, but its highly rewarding as well. It may take some time to get used to working for a new family; if you act like a caring professional, theyll soon start treating you as one of their own. Make it a point to be properly prepared for the job from day one and youll be on the right track to winning them over.
          </p>
