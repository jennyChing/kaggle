           <h5>
            This post is Sponsored By
            <a href="http://www.piazzadorolorespresso.com.au">
             LOr Espresso
            </a>
           </h5>
           <p>
            <a href="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/DSCF3963.jpg">
             <img alt="DSCF3963" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11588 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/DSCF3963.jpg" data-pin-url="http://www.smaggle.com/2013/11/13/5-easy-peasy-ways-improve-morning-routine/" height="281" src="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/DSCF3963.jpg" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            My morning routine changes periodically. Sometimes I eat breakfast and then have a shower and sometimes I shower first.
            <strong>
             Im just a loose canon like that.
            </strong>
            The one thing that never changes is that my routine always includes coffee.
            <strong>
             In fact it generally
             <em>
              starts
             </em>
             with coffee.
            </strong>
            We had to buy a Nepresso recently because
            <a href="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/2013/10/30/make-coffee-home/">
             my body doesnt communicate with my limbs for at least an hour after Ive woken up.
            </a>
            This makes it difficult to make coffee with complicated apparatus like plungers and syphons because everything just ends up on the floor. With my
            <strong>
             little morning joy maker
            </strong>
            I literally press a button and delicious caffeine comes pouring out. So good.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Ive been trying out all the different capsules from Lor Espresso
            </strong>
            and I was having a chat to their marketing rep about my morning routine and then everyone started talking about their own morning routines.
            <strong>
             Dragging their husbands out of bed,
            </strong>
            banging around blindly in the dark looking for socks and generally just being completely
            <strong>
             sleepy and useless
            </strong>
            . I thought it was high time I hatched together a little article on
            <strong>
             how to improve your morning routine
            </strong>
            because some people seem to enjoy the wee small hours a bit more than others. There are those
            <strong>
             bright and eyed bushy tailed little possums
            </strong>
            who jump out of bed like theres a pot of gold waiting for them in the kitchen and those of us who need to take mornings very slowly and with much caution.
            <strong>
             Here are a few tips to help make your morning a little easier and give you the best start to the day.
            </strong>
            (Keep reading for your chance to win a snazzy $100 voucher)
           </p>
           <h1>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             5 Easy Peasy Ways To Improve Your Morning Routine
            </span>
           </h1>
           <h1>
            Prepare The Night Before
           </h1>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Make sure the kitchen is neat and tidy to make breakfast preparation easier.
            </strong>
            If you pack your lunch for school or work prepare as much as you can the night before  chop up your carrot sticks, premix any dressings and put things like nuts and crackers in containers.
            <strong>
             Its really easy to waste time in the morning scrambling for sandwich wraps and drink bottles so make sure youre as organised as you can be before you go to bed.
            </strong>
            Have your wallet, train ticket, keys, sunglasses and other essentials ready to grab as you walk out the door in morning. A little preparation makes such a difference to your
            <strong>
             stress levels in the morning.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <h1>
            Develop a Set of Morning Rituals
           </h1>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Make your bed.
            </strong>
            Eat breakfast with your housemate or partner.
            <strong>
             Watch 10 minutes of your favourite morning show.
            </strong>
            Make a beautiful cup of delicious coffee and enjoy it slowly. Having little regular landmarks through out the morning helps to create habits and gives a lovely feeling
            <strong>
             of comfort and consistency.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/DSCF3954.jpg">
             <img alt="DSCF3954" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11591 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/DSCF3954.jpg" data-pin-url="http://www.smaggle.com/2013/11/13/5-easy-peasy-ways-improve-morning-routine/" height="281" src="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/DSCF3954.jpg" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <h1>
            Have an Estimated Time of Departure
           </h1>
           <p>
            If you have no idea what time youre going to leave,
            <strong>
             how do you know how much time you have?
            </strong>
            No matter where youre going in the morning, its important to know what time youre going to leave your house.
            <strong>
             This ensures that you can budget your minutes effectively and get out the door in time to get to where youre going.
            </strong>
            It sounds like a really obvious suggestion but sometimes we all need reminding of the simple things.
           </p>
           <h1>
            Only Do Essential Tasks In The Morning
           </h1>
           <p>
            Food  essential. Clothing  essential.
            <strong>
             Scanning Facebook for early morning rant posts from people who you hate follow  not essential.
            </strong>
            Not many people have time for unnecessary tasks in the morning so save unpacking the clean dishwasher, scrubbing the toilet, sorting through your mail or trolling on the internet for later in the day
            <strong>
             when you arent running late for the bus.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <h1>
            Take 5 Minutes For Yourself
           </h1>
           <p>
            Even if its just a few minutes to stand still in the shower and perhaps do a three minute treatment on your hair or to
            <strong>
             sit outside and enjoy your breakfast in peace.
            </strong>
            We can really only start the day well if we allow ourselves.
            <strong>
             Rushing around like a maniac and getting grumpy when things go wrong is a sure fire way to start the day on a bad note!
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/DSCF3961.jpg">
             <img alt="DSCF3961" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11589 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/DSCF3961.jpg" data-pin-url="http://www.smaggle.com/2013/11/13/5-easy-peasy-ways-improve-morning-routine/" height="281" src="http://smaggle.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/DSCF3961.jpg" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             So we thought it would be a cool idea to get all of you Smaggle readers to share
             <i>
              your
             </i>
             morning routines.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Ive got a $100 Westfield voucher for the person who gives me the biggest giggle in the comments
            </strong>
            with tales of their morning trauma or triumphs because mornings are a struggle for a lot of people.
           </p>
           <h1>
            Tell me whats your morning routine?
           </h1>

                 <p>
                  Well, Im single, I work nights and am a freelancer from home, so morning and routine are fluid concepts for me. Oh, my alarm goes off at 9am yeah, Im gonna get up at nine every morning no matter what time I crashed the night before and Ill do yoga and then run and then read five novels and then write two and then have f*&amp;ling asparagus and organic sourdough bananas for breakfast! I stare at the alarm, then at my dumb fat dog, hoping my dumb fat dog hasnt been roused, then at my alarm. I mentally chastise myself for what Im about to do. I turn it off, and start daydreaming about being in an episode of Deadwood before falling asleep again.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  My REAL alarm comings at about 11.30am when my dumb fat dog becomes tired of staring unblinking at my face and initiates a tactical maneuver best described as the Bed Bomb and Spin. In which hewell, bombs the bed then spins for a while.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  I roll out from under my dumb fat dog, stumble toward the bathroom, cry to the heavens for direction, purpose, mercy! I remember the two bottles of wine I drank the night before, shifty-eyed and bitter, I put a berocca in a glass of water, to be ready after I pee. I pee. I down the lifeless red liquid that sureless they have the technology to make it taste better, and get in the damn shower before I have an existential breakdown.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  After the shower I feel fine, walk my dog and enjoy the rest of the day. Maybe skip a little as I do.
