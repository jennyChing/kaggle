        <div class="author">
         <h3 class="cat-badge cat-features">
          Features
         </h3>
         By:
         <span class="byline-override">
          Terrence Watson
         </span>
         <span class="date">
          / October 28, 2014
         </span>
         <div class="article-social-icons">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="article-content clearfix">
         <p>
          Its early October and MMG rapper Stalley has
          <a href="http://www.vibe.com/photo-gallery/6-things-expect-stalleys-debut-album-ohio">
           invited us
          </a>
          to hear his debut album
          <em>
           OHIO
          </em>
          once again. This time, instead of sitting in the back of a tricked out Cadillac, were smack in the middle of an industry celebration for Double Ms Man of the Hour. Stalleys album listening party speaks volumes to who he is and what his core fan base is about. The eclectic, retro 90s around the way girls dripping in camo, Air Jordans and gold bamboo earrings are a great contrast to the notable media hip hop heads that cant stop checking emails, Twitter or the Gram.
         </p>
         <p>
          Bobby Shmurda fills the air and rings off before the mood music changes at Coopers Classic Car shop on the West Side of Lower Manhattan. Once the garage door opens, Stalley arrives in, what else, but a candy painted ol school. Massillons favorite son is all daps and hugs, addressing the crowd with a warm welcome. Tracks from his album play, but whats most notable is the energy. Stalleys Blue Collar Gang homies hit the center of the floor and surround him with lyrics and love. Tonight is about both the music and Stalleys emergence. Sure, hes a rapper, but for a brief moment hes heralded, gawked at and mobbed like he just won MVP of the NBA Finals.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is his moment.
         </p>
