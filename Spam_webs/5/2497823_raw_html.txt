          <h1 class="entry-title">
           Facebook  Word Games by zynga
          </h1>
          <div class="meta-below-title entry-meta clearfix clearfix-title">
           <div class="left">
            Written on
            <span class="entry-date published">
             May 26, 2008
            </span>
            by

           <p>
            Facebook holds few attractions for some. But for others, its a replacement for Excite and Yahoo games. The bonus being your opponents are taken from your contacts.  That aside, there are hundreds of games now available on Facebook.
           </p>
           <p>
            Few games though are in the category of both challenging and of high replay value. zynga has taken a stab at bringing us some word games that meet both those criteria.  Those games are
            <a href="http://apps.facebook.com/wordtwist/index.php?navsrc=50" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://apps.facebook.com/wordtwist/index.php?navsrc=50']);">
             Word Twist
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://apps.facebook.com/pathwords/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://apps.facebook.com/pathwords/']);">
             Pathwords
            </a>
            , and
            <a href="http://apps.facebook.com/scramblegame/index.php?navsrc=12" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://apps.facebook.com/scramblegame/index.php?navsrc=12']);">
             Scramble
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            All three games present time based challenges where word construction is the key.  In each case, minimum word length is always three characters.  The tiles in each case are randomly arranged and your task is to discover all of the words.
           </p>
           <p>
            Word Twist presents the player with six letters.  The player types or clicks the letters to form words. Score is based on the number of words found, with a bonus for bingo words.  Thats words of six characters.  Heres a screenshot.
           </p>
           <div style="text-align:center;">
            <img alt="Facebook | Word Twist.jpg" border="0" height="451" src="http://www.toddrjordan.com/thebroadbrush/wp-content/uploads/2008/05/facebook-word-twist.jpg" width="434"/>
           </div>
           <p>
            Word Twist allows the players to complete all of their turns without waiting.  There is no direct competition between the players.  The number of terms and time is set at game creation.
           </p>
           <p>
            Pathwords presents an entirely different challenge.  Where Word Twist gives the player six characters to work with, Pathwords gives a whole field.  A picture will help explain.
           </p>
           <div style="text-align:center;">
            <img alt="Facebook | PathWords-1.jpg" border="0" height="471" src="http://www.toddrjordan.com/thebroadbrush/wp-content/uploads/2008/05/facebook-pathwords-1.jpg" width="427"/>
           </div>
           <p>
            The players selects the words by clicking on the initial letter of the word and then clicking on the rest of the letters.  To enter a word, a double click is required on the last letter.  An alternate method is clicking on the first letter and holding the mouse button.  The player can then drag over the rest of the letters in the word.  When the mouse button is released the word is entered.  Wrong words do not score, and clear the letter selection.
           </p>
           <p>
            Once a word is accepted, the letters in the word are removed from the board. Each columns letters then slide down and new letters are added at the top.  An added feature allows the player to scramble the letters on the board at a penalty of points.
           </p>
           <p>
            Scramble is my favorite of the three games. Scramble plays like the home game, Boggle(tm).  The players take turns finding words in the 4 by 4 array of letters.  With the classic tile look, it feels a lot like Boggle.
           </p>
           <div style="text-align:center;">
            <img alt="Facebook | Scramble.jpg" border="0" height="331" src="http://www.toddrjordan.com/thebroadbrush/wp-content/uploads/2008/05/facebook-scramble.jpg" width="438"/>
           </div>
           <p>
            Players alternate turns finding words.  Score is based on word length.  There are no bingos or such.  Typical games are 5 rounds of 3 minutes each. A word hit feature exits that requires 2 credits each use.  Credits are earned on the first visit to Scramble during a day.
           </p>
