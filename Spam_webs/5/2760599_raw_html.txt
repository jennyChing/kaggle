          <p class="mol-para-with-font">
           <span style="font-size: 14px;">
            <a class="" href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sport/football/article-2946628/Manchester-United-continue-stutter-stats-shame-Louis-van-Gaal-s-big-spending-side.html" style="font-weight: bold;" target="_blank">

        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           1 Why are Manchester United so dull to watch?
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           JAMIE REDKNAPP:
          </span>
          When you played against United at their peak under Sir Alex Ferguson, there was so much freedom in their movement. Now they look rigid, with too much structure. Football is sometimes about taking a risk. They have so much talent in their team and it is a waste. People speak very highly of Louis van Gaal and his attention to detail but the players look confused.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           MARTIN KEOWN:
          </span>
          They dont move the ball quickly enough. When they get possession in midfield theyre not confident going forward and lack pace up front. They dont dominate games or look convincing. Manchester United used to have an aura about them but the halo is slipping.
         </font>
        </p>

        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           2 Is Wayne Rooney being used properly?
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           KEOWN:
          </span>
          In the short term, yes, but not in the long term. Van Gaal is trying to find a long-term plan. Hes trying to build a team and deploy players where he sees them playing in the future. Rooneys future lies in midfield, certainly, but at the moment he needs to be playing up front. Robin van Persie and Radamel Falcao are not working up top together.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           REDKNAPP:
          </span>
          How would I feel if I was lining up against Rooney in midfield? Delighted. Its where he can hurt the opposition least. As a defender you want him as far from the goal as possible. So playing him in midfield makes no sense. Most players can ping 30- or 40-yard passes but most cant do what Rooney does in the opposing box. Thats what he is about.
         </font>
        </p>

         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           3 Can Falcaos United career be saved?
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           REDKNAPP:
          </span>
          I dont think so and its a shame. I still believe he is a great player and you dont want to see him struggle. It reminds me of Fernando Torres and Andriy Shevchenkos stints at Chelsea.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          He doesnt look like hes enjoying his football and he doesnt look like hes a fan of the manager. Van Gaal might be better off not playing him. Falcao made a terrible footballing decision to go to Monaco, then was unlucky with his cruciate injury.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          It is so hard to come back to the top level from an injury like that but there is a fine line between losing your pace and losing your confidence  look how well Torres is now doing at Atletico Madrid.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           KEOWN:
          </span>
          Falcao could argue he is not getting enough balls into the box. He likes to come off the shoulder of the defender, get in front of people and nick goals at the near post.
         </font>
        </p>
        <div class="artSplitter mol-img-group">
         <div class="mol-img">
          <img alt="Questions have been asked about Robin van Persie's ability since former manager Sir Alex Ferguson left" class="blkBorder img-share" height="447" id="i-d032f709ea9c458c" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2015/02/09/2578E9A200000578-0-image-a-28_1423518800314.jpg" width="634"/>

         </div>

          Questions have been asked about Robin van Persie's ability since former manager Sir Alex Ferguson left
         </p>

        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           4 How can Louis revive Di Maria and Van Persie?
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           KEOWN:
          </span>
          Angel di Maria needs to get back to what he was doing when he first joined Manchester United  running with the ball for fun.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          At the start he made very good decisions on the ball. But the more he plays the more desperate he looks: he is always shooting from distance. As for Van Persie, is it time for him to play in a deeper role? Its almost as if his battery has run flat at the moment.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           REDKNAPP:
          </span>
          I thought Di Maria would rip the league apart but hes faded. Hes been really poor, especially compared to Alexis Sanchez at Arsenal. Hes been played in too many different positions though and it hasnt paid off. Van Persie has not been the same since Fergie left.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          The only real solution for Van Gaal is to forget about egos and price tags and leave a big name or two on the bench.
         </font>
        </p>

        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           5 What has been Van Gaals biggest error?
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           REDKNAPP:
          </span>
          Not addressing their defensive weaknesses. David de Gea has kept them in so many matches. United look fearful that they will be punished at the back if they commit too many men forward so they play with no freedom.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          In the past they knew they had Nemanja Vidic and Rio Ferdinand behind them. But how will we ever find out if Van Gaal is any good? Hell probably just get another 200million to spend in the summer.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          <span class="mol-style-bold">
           KEOWN:
          </span>
          Trying to play Falcao and Van Persie up front together. They dont work. He needed to pick one or the other and play Rooney alongside them.
         </font>
        </p>
