       <p class="nd_single">
        <a href="http://beforeitsnews.com/self-sufficiency/2013/08/how-to-build-an-underground-greenhouse-2461422.html">
         (Before It's News)
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         This is amazing! Can you imagine growing food year round in a hostile climate underground? This technique was developed in the mountainous regions of South America for cold weather farming and has been revisited and updated for today. This how-to project can be done for under $300. Take a look.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         This article first appeared at
         <a href="/r2/?url=http://www.treehugger.com/green-architecture/build-underground-greenhouse-garden-year-round.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Treehugger.com
         </a>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="http://media.treehugger.com/assets/images/2013/02/walipini-2.jpg.492x0_q85_crop-smart.jpg"/>
        <br/>
        <em>
         
         <a href="/r2/?url=http://neo-farms.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/walipini-2.png" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Neo-farms
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Growers in colder climates often utilize various approaches to extend the growing season or to give their crops a boost, whether its
        <a href="/r2/?url=http://www.treehugger.com/green-architecture/jenny-sabin-futuristic-greenhouse-uses-110-laser-cut-coldframes.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         coldframes
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/r2/?url=http://www.treehugger.com/lawn-garden/build-your-own-mini-hoop-house-in-hours-video.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         hoop houses
        </a>
        or greenhouses.
       </p>
       <p>
        Greenhouses are usually glazed structures, but are typically expensive to construct and heat throughout the winter. A much more affordable and effective alternative to glass greenhouses is the
        <em>
         walipini
        </em>
        (an Aymara Indian word for a place of warmth), also known as an underground or pit greenhouse. First developed over 20 years ago for the cold mountainous regions of South America, this method allows growers to maintain a productive garden year-round, even in the coldest of climates.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres a video tour of a
        <em>
         walipini
        </em>
        that even incorporates a bit of interior space for goats:
