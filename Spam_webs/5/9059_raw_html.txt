   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Primate city
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      A
      <b>
       primate city
      </b>
      is the leading city in its country or region, disproportionately larger than any others in the
      <a href="/wiki/Urban_hierarchy" title="Urban hierarchy">
       urban hierarchy
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dictionary_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dictionary-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A 'primate city distribution' has one very large city with many much smaller cities and towns, and no intermediate-sized urban centres, in contrast to the linear '
      <a href="/wiki/Rank-size_distribution" title="Rank-size distribution">
       rank-size distribution
      </a>
      '.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The 'law of the primate city' was first proposed by the
      <a href="/wiki/Geographer" title="Geographer">
       geographer
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Jefferson_(geographer)" title="Mark Jefferson (geographer)">
       Mark Jefferson
      </a>
      in 1939.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about.com_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-about.com-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He defines a primate city as being "at least twice as large as the next largest city and more than twice as significant."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jefferson_1939_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Jefferson_1939-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A primate city is number one in its country in most aspects, like politics, economy, media, culture and universities.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Significance">
       Significance
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Primate_city&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Significance">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Not all countries have primate cities, but in those that do, the rest of the country depends on it for cultural, economic, political, and major transportation needs.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      On the other hand, the primate city depends on the rest of the country as paying consumers of the cultural, economic, political and other services produced in the area.
     </p>
     <p>
      The presence of a primate city in a country may indicate an imbalance in development usually a progressive core, and a lagging periphery, on which the city depends for labor and other resources.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, the urban structure is not directly dependent on a country's level of
      <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">
       economic development
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dictionary_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Dictionary-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Many of the primate cities are increasing their percentage of their country's population. This can be because the number of traditional workers have been reduced because of mechanization in the manufacturing industry, agriculture, and other
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue-collar_workers" title="Blue-collar workers">
       blue-collar industries
      </a>
      , which are generally located throughout all of the country. At the same time, the number of educated employees in service business such as politics, economy, culture, media, and higher education has been rising, and those sectors are often located in the capital where the power and money is concentrated.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">
       Examples
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Primate_city&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Examples">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_primate_cities" title="List of primate cities">
       List of primate cities
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Among the best known examples of primate cities are
      <a href="/wiki/Global_city" title="Global city">
       alpha world cities
      </a>
      such as
      <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">
       Paris
      </a>
      and by most measures
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jefferson_1939_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Jefferson_1939-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">
       Budapest
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">
       Dublin
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">
       Vienna
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      have also been described as primate cities.
      <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">
       Bangkok
      </a>
      has been called "the most primate city on earth", being forty times larger than Thailand's second-largest city.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
