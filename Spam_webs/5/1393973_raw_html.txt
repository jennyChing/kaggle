        <h2>
         TICKET TO RIDE
        </h2>
        <h3>
         THE HIGH COST OF UNINSURED MOTORISTS IN THE UK
        </h3>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        More than 1 million drivers in the UK are uninsured, though that number is significantly lower compared to just five years ago. Still, the bevy of uninsured motorists cause a huge strain on insured drivers and the economy as a whole.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <h3>
         UNINSURED DRIVERS ARE EVERYWHERE
        </h3>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <b>
         Uninsured Behind the Wheel
        </b>
        <br/>
        Though the number of uninsured drivers in the UK has been falling over the last few years, there are still millions on the road.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        &gt;&gt; There are currently 1.2 million uninsured motorists on the road in the UK.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <b>
         Driving Uninsured Is Socially Unacceptable
        </b>
        <br/>
        Nearly all adults agree that driving uninsured is wrong. Surprisingly, more adults are in agreement about uninsured drivers than are about other various issues.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Per Cent of Respondents Who Agree With Each Statement
        <br/>
        96% Its socially unacceptable to drive an uninsured vehicle.
        <br/>
        83% Its socially unacceptable not to pay for council tax.
        <br/>
        81% Its socially unacceptable not to tax your vehicle.
        <br/>
        51% Its socially unacceptable to download illegal music.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <b>
         Younger People More Likely to Drive Uninsured
        </b>
        <br/>
        One in 10 people under 25 years old admit to driving their own car without insurance. The number jumps to nearly 1 in 4 people under 25 whove driven someone elses vehicle without insurance.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <b>
         Per Cent of Each Group Who Have Driven Own Vehicle Without Insurance
        </b>
        <br/>
        18-24 10%
        <br/>
        25-34 6%
        <br/>
        35-44 7%
        <br/>
        45-54 3%
        <br/>
        55+ 3%
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <h3>
         UNINSURED DRIVERS ARE COSTLY
        </h3>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <b>
         Colliding With Insured Drivers
        </b>
        <br/>
        Not only are uninsured drivers prevalent, theyre often in accidents with insured drivers.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        &gt;&gt; 1 in 10 drivers have been involved in a collision with a driver who was uninsured.
        <br/>
        &gt;&gt; 25% of drivers in a crash with an uninsured driver said they or their passengers had been hurt.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <b>
         The Cost of the Uninsured
        </b>
        <br/>
        All uninsured drivers are burdening the economy and injuring  sometimes fatally  people every year.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        &gt;&gt; Uninsured motorists kill 160 people and injure 23,000 every year.
        <br/>
        &gt;&gt; Uninsured motorists cost the economy over 500m every year.
        <br/>
        &gt;&gt; Uninsured motorists add 30 to the average motorists annual insurance premium.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <b>
         The Cost of Getting Caught
        </b>
        <br/>
        In addition to the inconvenience of having a vehicle held, those who are caught driving without insurance face other steep penalties.
        <br/>
        <br/>
         200 fixed penalty
        <br/>
         150 collection charge
        <br/>
         20 per day for vehicle storage
        <br/>
         6 points on the license
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <h3>
         CRACKDOWN ON THE UNINSURED SEES RESULTS
        </h3>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <b>
         Younger Uninsured Drivers Down Dramatically
        </b>
        <br/>
        Young people are more likely to drive without insurance, but in just a few years, the number has decreased by half.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        17-20 Year Olds Who Are Uninsured
        <br/>
        2008 About 250,000
        <br/>
        2012 About 125,000
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <b>
         Number of Uninsured Falls Over Five Years
        </b>
        <br/>
        Across all age groups, since 2005 the number of uninsured drivers has fallen sharply, thanks in part to continuous enforcement.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        2005 1.9 million uninsured
        <br/>
        2011 1.4 million uninsured
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <h3>
         UNINSURED HOT SPOTS
        </h3>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        London and Merseyside areas  where 1 in 10 drivers is uninsured  top the list of police force areas with the highest proportion of uninsured vehicles on the road.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        UKs Uninsured Driving Hot Spots And Per Cent of Vehicles Uninsured
        <br/>
        <br/>
        South Wales - 4.22%
        <br/>
        Cleveland - 4.53%
        <br/>
        South Yorkshire - 4.83%
        <br/>
        Bedfordshire - 4.99%
        <br/>
        Lancashire - 5.48%
        <br/>
        West Midlands - 5.87%
        <br/>
        West Yorkshire - 6.41%
        <br/>
        Greater Manchester - 8.26%
        <br/>
        Merseyside - 9.51%
        <br/>
        Metropolitan London - 10.10%
        <br/>
        <br/>
        SOURCES: MOTOR INSURERS BUREAU, TELEGRAPH.CO.UK, GUARDIAN.CO.UK, DIRECT LINE, BRAKE
