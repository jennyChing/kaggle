         <p>
          Sometimes I eff around in the grocery store for hours. Yes,
          <i>
           hours.
          </i>
          My typical grocery shopping routine is quick andconcise thanks to my finely tuned cart-stuffing system [
          <i>
           one that involves Iggy the Ipad and my trusty ZipList app
          </i>
          ] but sometimes I just want to wander! I recently spent a little extra time playing in the ethnic food aisle [
          <i>
           my
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            favorite
           </span>
           !
          </i>
          ] and found myself gawking at all the colorful Indiansauces and fancy packaged meals. When was the last time I made an Indian dish!? Its been forever right? I glanced at a random pouch of Madras Lentils and decided to play a little game of copycat.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: justify;">
         <p>
          Though this will be my most inventive copycat creation yet, simply because Ive [a.] never had madras lentils and [b.] glanced at the pouch for a total of 2.5 seconds before getting distracted by something shiny. Im writing this part of the post before the dish has even been completed but
          <i>
           oh. my. gosh.
          </i>
          does my kitchen smell amazing! Ill be back in a little bit with the outcome!
         </p>
         <p>
          <i>
           Im back! Ahhh! This dish is
          </i>
          <span style="font-style: italic; text-decoration: underline;">
           insane
          </span>
          !!!
          <i>
          </i>
         </p>
         <p>
          <i>
           It was love at first bite. Make it.
          </i>

           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            You're going to laugh at how easy this is:
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Dump EVERYTHING into your
            <a href="http://amzn.to/1FBpXvx" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             slow cooker
            </a>
            .
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Set to HIGH for 3.5-4 hours and go about your day/evening without so much as glancing at the pot. Once time is up, taste and add any additional seasonings your taste buds prefer.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            I added an extra pinch of red pepper flakes and was good to go!
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Garnish with a sprinkle of parsley and serve over a bed of fluffy rice, quinoa, or alongside a big green salad!
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <em>
             Hungry NOW? This dish can also be whipped up on the stove-top in under 30 minutes!
            </em>
           </li>
          </ol>

          </div>
         </div>

          3.3.3070
         </div>

        <b>
         <span style="color: #e06666; font-size: large;">
          t-rex chefs
         </span>
        </b>
        add chicken, serve this up as a side dish, or go veggie!

         <b>
          <span style="color: #93c47d; font-size: large;">
           vegansaurus chefs
          </span>

          crockpot black beans
         </a>
         <span style="text-align: start;">
          , I cook up a big batch of lentils, portion them into 1-2 cup servings and then pop them in the freezer to enjoy all month long! Theyre ridiculously simple to cook. The instructions on the bag should tell you sort and rinse the lentils while you bring 6 cups of water to a boil in a pot. Once the water is boiling, add the lentils and reduce to simmer, in about 15-25 minutes youll have a pot of lentils to play with! Drain the water from the pot, portion them out and make this recipe!
