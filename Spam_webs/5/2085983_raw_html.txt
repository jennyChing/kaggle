   <h1 class="title tencol">
    <a href="http://the-talks.com/interviews/christopher-walken/">
     Christopher Walken: Thats it, its over
    </a>
    <a class="audio-present" title="audio sample available">
    </a>
   </h1>
   <span class="date">
    January 9, 2013
   </span>
  </div>
  <div class="container">
   <div class="article-content">
    <div class="copy eightcol">
     <p>
      <strong>
       Mr. Walken, has anybody told you lately that you have great hair?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      You know, its funny you say that because the lady who sold me a movie ticket in New York recently said, Youve got great hair! Its funny to have a stranger say to you, Heres your ticket. Wow, youve got really good hair.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       But youve always been into your hair, havent you?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Yeah and Ive always had good hair.
     </p>
     <p>
      <em>
      </em>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       How do you feel now that its gray?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <em>
       
      </em>
      its true, I wish I was young.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Is it that bad?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      What happens now is I remember I was in Toronto and I went to the movies. And there was a young girl at the counter and she automatically gave me the senior discount. That hadnt really happened before. You know, theyll ask you sometimes, but she just gave it to me automatically. And I thought, Thats it, its over.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       At least you still seem to have the energy of a young man.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Yeah, but Id still rather be young again.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       What do you do to keep fit?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      I exercise everyday. I have a treadmill and I do that 7 days a week. I do 2 miles and I watch Charlie Rose.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Whats the secret to maintaining a youthful attitude?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Well, just trying. It comes and goes. Green tea. I mean, my wife, I have a wife of almost 50 years, and I have a feeling that she looks after me a little bit like I was 7 years old. I dont have kids. Maybe that has kept me young.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       What about pets?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      I have a cat.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Whats he called?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bowtie.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Bowtie?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      I had another cat who died, Flapjack. Thats the thing about cats, they die. Have you seen Henri, the French cat?
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       No, what is that?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Oh you have to see it. Its hilarious. Its on YouTube and its a cat who speaks French. And hes very depressed. He says things like, Im surrounded by morons.
      <em>
       (Laughs)
      </em>
      Theres a white cat that he lives with who he loathes. And he says, Look at this idiot. All he thinks about are cheeseburgers. I just saw it the other day and I tell everybody to watch it.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Who do you look up to, beside Henri the French cat?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Well, in my generation its obvious. When I was a kid it was Brando and Olivier.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Now you are an icon yourself.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      No.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Maybe not quite like Brando, but youre more respected and admired than most people out there.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Im just the oldest.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Well you have also been in some incredible films  the scene between you and Dennis Hopper in
       <em>
        True Romance
       </em>
       is one of my favorites of all time. Are you ever fully satisfied with your performances?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Oh, I dont know. For me, in movies, its always a mixed bag. Ive never made a movie where I thought, You were really good in that movie; you were good all the time. No. Its always, You didnt get it, you didnt do it in that scene, but the other scene is pretty good. So I just hope that in balance theres more good scenes than not.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Are you a perfectionist?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      I dont think its perfection. I think its just for real.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Thankfully the editor can take out most of your mistakes.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      A lot of it has to do with the director, actually. There are certain directors where you know youre going to be good or youre not going to be there. There are people where you kind of know that if you miss the mark then itll probably not be in the movie and thats very reassuring.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       There are also people where thats unfortunately not the case
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      There are movies that Ive made where I thought I was going to be good, but when it was cut it together it wasnt. And there are a lot of movies that, for one reason or another, just dont become popular. So to me its always been a little bit of a roll of the dice. Thats the way it goes. Ive made quite a number of movies that Ive never even seen and Ive made some movies that I thought were good that nobody saw Sometimes they end up on television, you know.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Whats it like when you come across an old film of yours on TV?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Ive done a lot of things I cringe when I watch and some things Im proud of Movies are strange. You have to be a little bit lucky with them. Ive never made a movie I wasnt surprised to see.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Surprised in a good way?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Not necessarily, no. But Im always surprised.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Do you have any regrets?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Well, yeah. You mean parts that I shouldnt have taken?
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Yeah.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Oh yes, absolutely.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       For example?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      No, no, I wont go there
     </p>
