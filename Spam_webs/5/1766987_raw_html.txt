        <div class="article-content clearfix">
         <p>
          <strong>
           Republican presidential candidate Ron Paul said today that President Obama could face impeachment for the killing of American-born al-Qaeda terrorist Anwar al-Awlaki.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/147754/ron-paul-suggests-anwar-al-awlaki-killing-impeachable-offense-for-obama/ronpaulimpeach/" rel="attachment wp-att-147756">
           <img alt="Ron Paul Suggests Anwar al-Awlaki Killing 'Impeachable' Offense For Obama" class="alignright size-full wp-image-147756" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/ronpaulimpeach.png" title="ronpaulimpeach" width="585"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Almost all of the Republican presidential candidates have offered President Obamas administration praise for the killing last week of American-born al-Qaeda operate Anwar al-Awlaki, leader of external operations for al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/147497/hunting-camps-racist-name-will-doom-rick-perry-and-possibly-gop-as-a-whole/">
           Embattled
          </a>
          Texas Gov.
          <a href="http://thehill.com/video/campaign/184881-perry-al-awlaki-death-a-serious-setback-for-al-qaeda-">
           Rick Perry
          </a>
          said the killing, ordered by Obama, was a serious setback for the terrorist organization, while
          <a href="http://www.michelebachmann.com/2011/09/bachmann-responds-to-death-of-al-qaeda-leader-anwar-al-awlaki/">
           Michele Bachmann
          </a>
          said of the fatal drone strike, The United States military and our intelligence community have demonstrated again why they are the best in the world.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://mittromneycentral.com/2011/09/30/mitt-romney-death-of-al-awlaki-a-major-victory/">
           Mitt Romney
          </a>
          went a bit further, specifically congratulating the president himself, I commend the President, the members of the intelligence community, our service members, and our allies for their continued efforts to keep Americans safe.
         </p>
         <p>
          But not all of the GOPs White House hopefuls are so keen to celebrate al-Awlakis death: libertarian-leaning Rep.
          <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-503544_162-20113962-503544.html">
           Ron Paul
          </a>
          joined the ACLU and other liberal organizations in blasting the attack.
         </p>
         <p>
          We cannot allow the War on Terror to diminish our steadfast adherence to the notion of due process for American citizens, he said last week. The protections under the Constitution for those accused of crimes do not just apply to people we like  they apply to everyone, including a terrorist like al-Awlaki. It is a question of due process for American citizens.
         </p>
         <p>
          And Paul kept up this argument during a campaign stop in
          <a href="http://politicalticker.blogs.cnn.com/2011/10/03/paul-impeachment-a-possibility-for-obama/">
           New Hampshire
          </a>
          , where an audience member asked whether the assassination warranted impeachment proceedings. I think the impeachment process would be possible, Paul replied. I put responsibility on the president because this is obviously a step in the wrong direction. We have just totally disrespected the Constitution.
         </p>
         <p>
          While Pauls answer may please his core ideological base,
          <a href="http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1011/65035.html">
           Dan Hirschhorn
          </a>
          points out that the congressmans remarks once again put Paul at odds with his Republican rivals over foreign policy and the war on terror
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats especially true because the Republican base has proven itself to be absolutely, positively
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/141932/rick-perry-also-taken-aback-by-debate-crowd-cheering-for-uninsured-sick-people/">
           bloodthirsty
          </a>
          .
         </p>
