       <h3 style="text-align: center;">
        Favorite ideas for picnicking in the summer
       </h3>
       <div class="dotted">
       </div>
       <h4>
        Batch Drinks
       </h4>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Buy a few glass pitchers or carafes with lids. These come in so handy for mixing up batch drinks {like our Thyme Lemonade or sangria} that guests can serve up themselves.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h4>
        Goodie Bags
       </h4>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Head to the grocery store or bakery for favorite desserts and treats. Package them with sweet tags and ribbon and send guests home with a goody bag post-hang out.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h4>
        String Up Lights and Garland
       </h4>
       <ul>
        <li>
         String up a few garlands and lights across your patio. If youre wanting to kick up the magic factor, Italian string lights can do the trick immediately. Add beautiful and lush greenery garlands {you can pre-order from your local florist about a week in advance} and your patio will be the place to be!
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h4>
        Stock that Picnic Basket
       </h4>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Keep your picnic basket stocked with cloth napkins, melamine glasses and dishes, and non-perishable goodies like Italian olives, olive oil dips, artichoke hearts, wine, and rosemary crackers. Then all you need to do is grab a few items like cheeses, a baguette and dessert on your way to the park!
        </li>
       </ul>

       <p>
        Once you find out how easy it is to make crumble, theres no going back. Like a rustic pie without all the hard work, its what we turn to when we want to impress without working up a sweat. Were telling you now to stock up on peak-fresh strawberries at the market, because this crumble from Laura of
        <a href="http://www.forkknifeswoon.com/">
         Fork Knife Swoon
        </a>
        will have you hooked. Served in ramekins, its perfect to serve and eat at a summer shindig!
       </p>
       <img class="smp-iboard" data-board="true" data-json='{"width":"300","height":"419","margin":4,"images":[{"height":"419","img_pid":"3088359","width":"300","img":"http:\/\/isa2.stylemepretty.com\/submissions\/uploads\/121002\/5589d644c2ce2.jpg","img_height":"440","img_width":"315","href":"\/gallery\/picture\/3088359","credits":[{"type":"Photography","by":"Fork Knife Swoon","href":"www.forkknifeswoon.com\/"}],"description":"Photography: Fork Knife Swoon - www.forkknifeswoon.com\/","img_offset":{"top":"-13","left":"0"},"offset":{"top":0,"left":0},"margin":{"left":0,"top":0}}]}' data-src="http://o.aolcdn.com/smp/is/wp-content/gallery/ibb/erineverafter/ibb-1435337308.4515.11955$!600x.jpg" id="smp-board-325038" src="http://o.aolcdn.com/smp/is/wp-content/gallery/ibb/erineverafter/ibb-1435337308.4515.11955$!600x.jpg"/>
       <p>
        A dusting of confectioners sugar or a scoop of vanilla ice cream is nearly mandatory, and if the weather is nice where you are, these taste
        <em>
         even
        </em>
        better
        <em>
         al fresco,
        </em>
        says Laura. Well gladly follow suit.For the full post and more mouthwatering recipes, check out
        <a href="http://www.forkknifeswoon.com/food-drink/2015/06/individual-strawberry-crumbles/">
         Fork Knife Swoon
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>

       <div class="recipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
        <meta content="2015-07-12" itemprop="datePublished"/>
        <img itemprop="image" src="" style="display:none!important;"/>
        <span class="hide" itemprop="author">
         Christina Lee
        </span>
        <span class="arvo large" itemprop="name">
         Individual Strawberry Oat Crumbles
        </span>
        <span class="hide" itemprop="description">
        </span>
        <meta content="http://www.stylemepretty.com/living/2015/07/12/individual-strawberry-crumbles/" itemprop="url"/>
        <table style="width:100%">
         <tr>
          <td style="width:33%">
           <span class="blue caps">
            prep time
           </span>
           <br/>
           <meta content="PT15M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
           15 Minute/s
          </td>
          <td style="width:34%">
           <span class="blue caps">
            cook time
           </span>
           <br/>
           <meta content="PT30M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
           30 Minute/s
          </td>
          <td style="width:33%">
           <span class="blue caps">
            total time
           </span>
           <br/>
           <meta content="PT45M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
           45 Minute/s
          </td>
         </tr>
        </table>
        <span class="hide" itemprop="recipeYield">
         Serves  0
        </span>
        <span class="blue large quicksand">
         Ingredients
        </span>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           Strawberry Filling
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           4 cups sliced strawberries
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           2 Tbsp pure cane sugar
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           1 Tbsp fresh lemon juice
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           optional but recommended: 2 tsp arrowroot starch
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           Oat Crumble Topping
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           1/2 cup rolled oats
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           1/4 cup all-purpose flour*
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           1/4 cup (packed) light brown sugar
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           pinch fine sea salt
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span itemprop="ingredients">
           3 Tbsp unsalted butter,* softened
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <span class="blue large quicksand">
         Instructions
        </span>
        <div itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         <ol>
          <li>
           Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F. In a medium mixing bowl, toss together the strawberries, sugar, arrowroot starch and lemon zest, if using. Set aside.
          </li>
          <li>
           Make the crumble topping: In a small mixing bowl, stir together the oats, flour, brown sugar and salt until combined. Add the butter, and use a pastry blender or your fingers to cut the butter into the dry ingredients. Continue until the butter is completely incorporated, and the mixture comes together into a chunky crumb.
          </li>
          <li>
           Divide the strawberry mixture between 4 oven-safe ramekins. Top with the crumble topping and bake for 25-30 minutes, until the crumble topping is golden brown and the berry juices begin to bubble. Remove from the oven and let cool for 10 minutes. Serve warm with fresh whipped cream, a scoop of vanilla ice cream and/or a dusting of powdered sugar.
          </li>
         </ol>
        </div>
