     <h2 class="posttitle">
      <a href="http://www.nutmegnanny.com/2015/02/13/orange-hot-chocolate/" rel="bookmark">
       Orange Hot Chocolate
      </a>
     </h2>
     <div class="postmeta">
      <em>
       Posted By
      </em>
      nutmegnanny
      <em>
       on
      </em>
      February 13, 2015
     </div>
     <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="16924710" data-link="http://www.nutmegnanny.com/2015/02/13/orange-hot-chocolate/" data-summary="" data-title="Orange Hot Chocolate">
     </div>
     <p>
      <em>
       This orange hot chocolate combines classic winter flavors and is easily made on the stove top or in your Vitamix. Plus its ready in under 10 minutes.
      </em>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="This orange hot chocolate is ready in less than 10 minutes and is perfect for cold winter days. " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8799" height="819" src="http://nutmegnanny.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Orange-Hot-Chocolate-pic-1.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      I usedtohave a serious distaste for all things chocolate and orange. I thought the flavor combination was weird and didnt understand why everyone went gaga over it. Then I had a really {like really really really} good truffle from a local chocolate shop. I swear to you I about lost it after I realized how delicious it could taste.
     </p>
     <p>
      I also blame my hate for chocolate and orange on those crappy chocolate oranges they sell at Christmas. I know I have talked about my distaste for them before and I just cant get over their weirdness.
     </p>
     <p>
      {Looking for more chocolate orange deliciousness? Check out these recipes 
      <a href="http://www.nutmegnanny.com/2014/11/18/chocolate-orange-pudding-pie/">
       chocolate orange pudding pie
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://www.nutmegnanny.com/2014/12/15/chocolate-orange-gingerbread-cake/">
       chocolate orange gingerbread cake
      </a>
      and
      <a href="http://www.nutmegnanny.com/2014/01/15/chocolate-orange-baked-doughnuts/">
       chocolate orange baked doughnuts
      </a>
      .
      <span id="more-8793">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="This orange hot chocolate is perfectly flavored and can be made in a Vitamix or on the stove top." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8795" height="318" src="http://nutmegnanny.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/drinking-hot-chocolate-image-1.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      I dont know why they gross me out so much considering some ofthe weird stuff I ate in my childhood {spam, vienna sausages, wild game meat} but I just cant take chocolate seriously when it comes in the shape of an orange and you have to slam it on a table in order for it to break into bite size segments.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="This orange hot chocolate is perfect for cold winter nights. Easily make a batch in under 10 minutes. " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8798" height="900" src="http://nutmegnanny.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Orange-Hot-Chocolate-photo-1.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Soooooo back to the hot chocolate. I made mine in the
      <a href="http://amzn.to/1uMiJSg" rel="nofollow">
       Vitamix
      </a>
      BUT you can easily make it on the stove top too. Either way its delicious and ready in under 10 minutes. Plus its perfect topped with giant marshmallows or whipped cream. I would have probably use whipped cream if I wasnt traipsing around outside while taking pictures. Putting a marshmallow on top is a lot easier than trying to whip cream in the frozen tundra.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="This orange hot chocolate is the perfect addition to your outdoor winter activities. Deliciously flavored and ready in under 10 minutes. " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8800" height="480" src="http://nutmegnanny.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Thermos-out-in-the-snow-photo.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Im sure youre also wondering why there is 5603950945 pictures in this post. Well, I had this idea to this outdoor hot chocolate photo shoot in the middle of snow storm. Luckily my husband was working from home and indulged me in my crazy idea.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="This warm orange hot chocolate is perfect for snowy winter days. Plus it's ready in under 10 minutes!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8796" height="900" src="http://nutmegnanny.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/drinking-hot-chocolate-photo-2.jpg" width="449"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://truvia.com/" rel="nofollow">
       Truvia
      </a>
      asked me to spread the word about their #SweetWarmUp winter contest. Share your sledding or sledding inspired photos on the
      <a href="https://www.facebook.com/Truvia" rel="nofollow">
       Truvia Facebook page
      </a>
      or by using #SweetWarmUp on Instagram or Twitter. The contest runs from February 9th  24th and fans have the chance to some fun winter inspired prizes!
     </p>
     <p>
      Since Im not one to sled (and it was freeeeeeeezing!) I opted to share a few pictures of myself in the snow with a giant cup of my orange hot chocolate. Which by the way would be the perfect beverage to share outside in the snow after an intense round of sledding.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="This orange hot chocolate is ready perfect for winter. Easily made on the stove top or in a Vitamix!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8797" height="900" src="http://nutmegnanny.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Orange-Hot-Chocolate-image-1.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>

        </a>
       </div>
       <h2 itemprop="name">
        Orange Hot Chocolate
       </h2>
       <div class="summary" itemprop="description" style="clear:left">
        <p>
         This orange hot chocolate combines classic winter flavors and is easily made on the stove top or in your Vitamix. Plus it's ready in under 10 minutes.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="time">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Yield:
         </strong>
         <span itemprop="recipeYield">
          2 servings
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Prep Time:
         </strong>
         <meta content="PT5M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
         5 minutes
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Cook Time:
         </strong>
         <meta content="PT5M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
         5 minutes
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Total Time:
         </strong>
         <meta content="PT10M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
         10 minutes
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="left">
        <h3 id="ingredients">
         Ingredients:
        </h3>
        <div class="ingredients">
         <ul>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1-1/2 cups whole milk
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1/2 cup dark chocolate chips
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           2 teaspoons Truvia sweetener (more if you want it sweeter)
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 teaspoon orange zest
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (orange extract can be used for even more flavor)
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           Whipped cream or marshmallows, for topping
          </li>
         </ul>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="right">
        <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
         Directions:
        </h3>
        <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         <p>
          Add all ingredients except the whipped cream into your Vitamix blender. If you have a Vitamix Blender that has a hot soup preset option set it and forget it! (Well, don't forget it...drink it after it's done) After your hot chocolate is finished pour it into a mug, top with whipped cream or marshmallows.
         </p>
         <p>
          If your Vitamix does not have a preset option simple blend on high until hot. This will take about 4 minutes.
         </p>
         <p>
          * If you don't have a Vitamix (or Blendtec) you could simply add all ingredients (except marshmallows and whipped cream) to a small saucepan and heat over medium heat until fully melted and combined.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
