                           <p style="text-align: center;">
                            When your baby is in the womb, all he or she hears is white noise. Some resources even say that the sound in the womb is 3 times louder than avacuumcleaner! Thats why it is no surprise when you hear many moms mention that their secret for calming their baby includes white noise. White noise is not really noise per say, but rather a steady sound frequency or signal. White noise can often be found in nature. Some examples of white noise include the sound of wind blowing, a steady fan, a rain shower, waves crashing the shore, thunder, a waterfall, the sound inside the airplane, and much more.
                           </p>

                              </span>
                             </span>
                            </a>
                           </p>
                           <p style="text-align: center;">
                            White noise is a great way to calm your baby, and help them sleep for longer periods of time. Some doctors recommend that you should impliment white noise in a babys sleep regimen, because of how soothing it can be for them.Whenever I put Squishy to sleep, I make sure to play some white noise for him using an app I have on my phone. Ive tried different kinds of white noise, and he seems to enjoy rainfall the most. As soon as I put it on, he becomes more relaxed and falls asleep quicker than when I dont have it playing for him. Often times, when I go to sleep next to him, Ill have it playing and it will even help me fall asleep faster, too.
                           </p>
                           <p style="text-align: center;">
                            Ive heard some parents run to grab a hair dryer or even start the vacuum cleaner when their babies are being fussy, and even when their babies have colic. The purpose of this is to simulate white noise so that their babies can calm down. They tell me that it works all of the time! But can you image running for the hair dryer or vacuum every single time your baby is crying hysterically? That is a lot of work! What you can do instead is download the white noise app on your iPhone or Android phone. You can even make your own white noise by shhhh!-ing. The steady and frequent it is, the more it will work. Before the app, I used to shhh! Squishy to sleep, and it would calm him down just as well.
                           </p>
                           <p style="text-align: center;">
                            <strong>
                             What are your white noise methods to calming your baby?
                            </strong>
