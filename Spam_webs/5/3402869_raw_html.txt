    <h1>
     <i>
      Bogolubovia
     </i>
    </h1>
    <p align="left">
     <b>
      Name:
     </b>
     <i>
      Bogolubovia
     </i>
     (Named after Nikolai Nikolaevich Bogolubov).
     <br/>
     <b>
      Phonetic:
     </b>
     Bo-go-lu-bo-vee-a.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Named By:
     </b>
     Lev Nesov &amp; Alexander Yarkov -
1989.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Classification:
     </b>
     Chordata, Reptilia, Pterosauria,
Pterodactyloidea, Azhdarchidae.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Species:
     </b>
     <i>
      B. orientalis (type).
     </i>
     <br/>
     <b>
      Diet:
     </b>
     Carnivore/Picivore.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Size:
     </b>
     4.3 meter wingspan.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Known locations:
     </b>
     Russia, Saratov Oblast - Rybushka
Formation.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Time period:
     </b>
     Campanian of the Cretaceous.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Fossil representation:
     </b>
     Initially described from a
single partial cervical vertebra. Further material has since been
referred to it including a radius.
    </p>
    <p align="left">
     <i>
      Bogolubovia
     </i>
     did not come into existence until seventy-five years after the holotype
was discovered in 1914 by Nikolai Nikolaevich Bogolubov. Initially the
remains were attributed to
     <i>
      Ornithostoma
     </i>
     as
     <i>
      O.
orientalis
     </i>
     , and later
reclassified as a species of
     <a href="http://www.prehistoric-wildlife.com/species/p/pteranodon.html">
      <i>
       Pteranodon
      </i>
     </a>
     .
Now the original description
can be compared with greater understanding of pterosaurs in general,
     <i>
      Bogolubovia
     </i>
     is thought to represent an azhdarchid
pterosaur.
    </p>
