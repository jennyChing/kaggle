       <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
        <font color="#ffffff" size="4">
         Many times when shooting under, over or around concealment a shooter will have to cant the rifle.
         <font>
          W
         </font>
         hat this article will cover is the point of aim holds for a center of mass impacts on target out to 200yds. We are not going to "can" these pictures, we only show the results!!
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         For the true measurement please use the following formula found in page 126 in the
         <u>
          <em>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.appliedballisticsllc.com/index_files/Book.htm" target="_blank" title="">
             Applied Ballistics For Long Range Shooting
            </a>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </u>
         2nd edition book by Bryan Litz.
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         <br/>
         <font>
          <span style="">
          </span>
          1. Determine the elevation required to engage the target at a given range in MOA
         </font>
         or MIL
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         2. Add 3 MOA or 1 MIL to the elevation correction you would use to engage the target
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         3. Multiply the sum by the sin of the cant angle. The result is the horizontal displacement of the shot
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         4. Multiply the sum by the cosine of the cant angle to approximate your effective elevation
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         <strong>
          EXAMPLE:
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         Target at 600yds with 5 degree rifle cant to a side. Lets assume it takes 13 MOA to be zeroed at 600yds. Add 3 MOA, now multiply that by sin of the cant angle 16xsin(5)=1.4MOA, or 8.8 at 600yds. Thats how far to the side your bullet will impact at 600yds with a 5 degree cant. Your effective vertical adjustment would be 16xcos(5)=15.94 MOA. This means your shot will be 0.06 MOA lower than intended, or .04 lower.
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         As we know, running in a dynamic situation a D.M. or Sniper can make a chart and based off of his / her DOPE can have this built in for SLOPE DOPE. But for the fast paced carbine shooter that is engaging targets from under a truck, or equipment doing this math or looking at a chart will not be fast enough.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span>
         </span>
         <font>
          This speed and efficiency
         </font>
         <font>
          is the heart of this article, just like all aspects of Trace Armory Group we focus on what works and is proven.
         </font>
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="">
         </span>
         Lets look at the three targets engaged with a 200yd ZERO with an AimPoint H1 4 MOA reticle.
        </font>
