             <h1>
              Zombie Toddlers Own the Streets in HEALTH's New Short Film
             </h1>
             <p class="author">
              By
              <a href="/author/benjamin-martin-shapiro">
               Benjamin Shapiro
              </a>
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="inline_socials" id="yw0">

            <div class="article_content">
             <p>
              <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" scrolling="no" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/IBdISuGnxpQ" width="640">
              </iframe>
             </p>
             <p>
              The early-to-mid aughts was a weird time for noise rock. Within a five year period, basically everybody who'd ever taken a RISD college tour started up a noise band, with varying degrees of success. Some were good, and some were shit terrible. One of the most lasting bands of that crop was
              <a href="http://noisey.vice.com/article/gsearch?q=HEALTH">
               HEALTH
              </a>
              , who emerged from LA at the tail end of the cycle, writing eerily synthetic, thumping noise, topped with spectral vocals that came straight from
              <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jitg-3xbmKU">
               the theme to
               <i>
                Are You Afraid Of The Dark?
               </i>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p class="p1">
              Part of HEALTH's success was their willingness to collaborate with artists outside of the insulated noise milieu. They immediately aligned themselves with offbeat dance, hip hop, and electro producers like
              <a href="http://noisey.vice.com/article/gsearch?q=Pictureplane">
               Pictureplane
              </a>
              ,
              <a href="http://noisey.vice.com/article/gsearch?q=Crystal+Castles">
               Crystal Castles
              </a>
              , and
              <a href="http://noisey.vice.com/article/gsearch?q=Salem">
               SALEM
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p class="p1">
              This past year, HEALTH composed the
              <a href="http://noisey.vice.com/blog/health-max-payne-3-soundtrack">
               fantastic in-game score for
              </a>
              <a href="http://www.rockstargames.com/maxpayne3/restricted_content/restricted_content_agegated/ref?redirect=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.rockstargames.com%2Fmaxpayne3%2F&amp;hash=294f6c318189ab99b4c902e37089401a">
               Max Payne 3
              </a>
              , a third-person shooter developed by
              <a href="http://www.rockstargames.com/">
               Rockstar Games
              </a>
              . The soundtrack was spare, minimal, and creepily atmospheric, and it's easily the best video game soundtrack I've ever heard.
             </p>
             <p class="p1">
              Now, they've teamed with directors David Altobelli and Jeff Desom to produce a short film structured around "Tears," a cut off that same soundtrack. Just in time for Halloween (and Hurricane Sandy, for that matter), the video is set in a post-apocalyptic urban landscape where blood runs openly in the gutters and an army of zombie toddlers own the streets.
             </p>
             <p class="p2">
              <img alt="" src="http://assets.noisey.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/b773290561381dde5b9fb11f45df1893.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 276px; "/>
             </p>
             <p class="p1">
             </p>
             <p class="p1">
              HEALTH - "Tears"
              <br/>
              Directors: David Altobelli, Jeff Desom
              <br/>
              Producer: Sarah Park, Richard Kim
              <br/>
              Production Co.: The Directors Bureau
             </p>
             <p class="p1">
              Editor: Luke Lynch
              <br/>
              DP: Larkin Seiple
              <br/>
              PD: Jason Kisvarday
              <br/>
              Costume Designer: Laura Francis
              <br/>
              Makeup Artist: Cici Anderson
             </p>
             <p class="p1">
             </p>
             <p class="p1">
              <em>
               Want to find out how this creepshow was made? The Creators Project, our arty sister site, interviewed the directors about gore effects and zombie toddlers. You can check that out
               <a href="http://www.thecreatorsproject.com/blog/zombie-babies-star-in-the-short-film-for-healths-tears">
                right here
               </a>
               .
              </em>
             </p>
