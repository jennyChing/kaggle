       <div class="e-content BlockContent">
        <p>
         There are many different useful accessories to use
to assist and challenge you in yoga, but the two most important props to help
you increase balance and flexibility are Yoga balls and Yoga straps .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           YOGA BALLS
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.theyogamatstore.com/premium-anti-burst-yoga-ball-75cm/">
          <img alt="Anti-Burst Yoga Ball" src="http://cdn6.bigcommerce.com/s-1qgtbka/product_images/uploaded_images/yoga-ball.jpg?t=1416350509" style="width: 186px; float: left; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px;"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.theyogamatstore.com/premium-anti-burst-yoga-ball-75cm">
          <u>
           Yoga balls
          </u>
         </a>
         are by far the most widely known yoga accessory, because they can be used in so many different settings. Yoga balls still maintain their original use in yoga practice, but we also see them being utilized in strength building exercises, and even in the office. That being said, a yoga ball is an essential prop to incorporate in and out of your yoga practice.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Yoga Ball Benefits :
          </em>
         </strong>
         Utilizing a ball in your yoga routine can provide
you with additional support and stabilization for difficult positions. It can
be used in positions like, downward dog, to help place your feet firmly on the
ground. It can be used to give more complexity to yoga positions youve
mastered to build your balance and extend your flexibility.Yoga balls can add another dimension to well-known strength
training exercises like squats, abdominal crunches, and much more. Depending on
what exercise you do, you may want to use a smaller or larger ball to maneuver
more easily.  For example, for exercises
like sit-ups you need a ball with a larger surface area to rest half of your
body on, but for sideway leg lifts using too large of a ball may be challenging.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://cdn6.bigcommerce.com/s-1qgtbka/product_images/uploaded_images/yoga-office.png?t=1416350119" style="width: 431px; float: right; margin: 0px 0px 10px 10px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Yoga Balls in the Office:
          </em>
         </strong>
         Another popular use for yoga balls is swapping them
out for chairs in office settings. Sitting on a yoga ball rather than a chair
helps align the spine to promote better posture, as well as strengthens the
core, since the spherical surface forces you to actively work to maintain your
balance. Since you will be sitting on the ball for hours, it can build your
lower abdominal muscles substantially. Using a yoga ball instead of a chair
also allows you to do a quick workout as you are working on your tasks or on
your breaks, relieving stress and increasing blood flow.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Selecting a Yoga Ball:
          </em>
         </strong>
         To select a yoga ball that is right for you, it is
important to select the ball according to your height.  Most yoga balls come in three different sized
diameters. If you are below 54 it would be suitable to purchase a
         <u style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana, Tahoma, sans-serif; font-size: 15px;">
          <a href="http://www.theyogamatstore.com/premium-anti-burst-yoga-ball-55cm/" style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana, Tahoma, sans-serif; font-size: 15px;" target="_blank">
           55 cm ball
          </a>
         </u>
         , andfor heights in between 55 and 511 buy a
         <u style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana, Tahoma, sans-serif; font-size: 15px;">
          <a href="http://www.theyogamatstore.com/premium-anti-burst-yoga-ball-65cm/" target="_blank">
           65 cm ball
          </a>
         </u>
         <a href="http://www.theyogamatstore.com/premium-anti-burst-yoga-ball-65cm/" style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, Verdana, Tahoma, sans-serif; font-size: 15px;" target="_blank">
         </a>
         If you are above
6 feet, purchase a ball that measures
         <a href="http://www.theyogamatstore.com/premium-anti-burst-yoga-ball-75cm/" target="_blank">
          <u>
           75 cm ball
          </u>
         </a>
         in diameter.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.theyogamatstore.com/studio-cotton-d-ring-strap" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Cotton Yoga Strap D-Ring" src="http://cdn6.bigcommerce.com/s-1qgtbka/product_images/uploaded_images/yoga-d-ring.jpg?t=1416350920" style="float: left; width: 228px; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px;"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           YOGA STRAPS
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yoga straps are another great prop to use in your
yoga workouts and general stretches. Like yoga balls, these can be used to
assist you during your asanas, as well as help you increase the complexity of
the workout.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Choosing a Yoga Strap:
          </em>
         </strong>
         These straps are usually adjustable, composed of cotton and are
often several feet long. Yoga straps also differ according to ring or closure
type.  Our
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <a href="http://www.theyogamatstore.com/studio-cotton-d-ring-strap" target="_blank">
          <u>
           Studio Cotton D-ring Straps
          </u>
         </a>
         ring shape, for example, makes it easier for users to change direction, and
therefore enables a wider range of motion.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Yoga Strap Benefits:
          </strong>
         </em>
         Yoga straps can be utilized by yogis of all levels
to elongate limbs to deepen stretches or to aid you in grabbing out of reach
limbs. For example, if you are having difficulty bringing your hands together
behind your back or if you are trying to touch your toes, you can use the strap
to bridge the space between the two limbs.  With continued use of yoga straps, there
should be an increase in your overall flexibility and mastery of tough yoga poses.
        </p>
        <p>
         Remember, it is always important to consult a doctor
before deciding to incorporate a new accessory in your workout.Find all of the items mentioned in this post on our website.
        </p>
        <p>
         Namaste.
