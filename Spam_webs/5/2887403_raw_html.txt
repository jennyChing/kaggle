                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry">

                    </a>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>

                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-002.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-138" height="300" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-002.jpg?w=225" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 002" width="225"/>
                      </a>
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-050.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-185" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-050.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 050" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Start with a thrift store sweater. This one was 50 cents on Wednesday at my friendly neighborhood thrift store. It's mostly acrylic and a little bit of wool. Kind of itchy and way too small for me.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-003.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-139" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-003.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 003" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-0041.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-188" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-0041.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 004" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Cut up one side of the sweater.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-005.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-141" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-005.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 005" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Cut off the sleeves.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-006.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-142" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-006.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 006" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Cut a rectangle 1 inch larger than the circumference of your head (this allows for a half inch seam on each side)by 4 or 5 inches tall. (Depending on how tall you want your hat. I cut mine 23in x 4in. I might go a little taller next time by perhaps a half inch).
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-009.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-145" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-009.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 009" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-012.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-148" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-012.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 012" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Next I used a lid to trace the top of the hat. This one measured 7 inches across.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-014.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-150" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-014.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 014" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Then cut out the circle.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-015.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-151" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-015.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 015" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Next choose some cotton fabric for the lining. I used the black gingham from an old tablecloth I bought at the Salvation Army, years ago.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-034.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-169" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-034.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 034" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Cut out a circle and rectangle with the same dimensions as your sweater pieces. Mine were 23x4 and a 7 in diameter circle.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-016.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-152" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-016.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 016" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Make a paper pattern for a brim. I copied a brim from another hat. I made the brims too big on my first hats and had to rip out the seams and make them smaller. It was so much work but I liked the smaller brims much better.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-021.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-157" height="300" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-021.jpg?w=225" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 021" width="225"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Cut 2 with about a half an inch around each side. Then cut two pieces of heavy fusible interfacing the same size as your pattern and iron on the wrong side of your sweater.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-022.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-158" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-022.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 022" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-023.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-159" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-023.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 023" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Place right sides together and pin in place.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-024.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-160" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-024.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 024" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Just sew the convex side with a half inch seam. (Now I'll see who is really reading this and see if I used the correct word there. It's been so long since I've had an opportunity to use that word:)) Leave the concave side open.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-026.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-162" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-026.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 026" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Turn it right side out.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-028.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-164" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-028.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 028" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Topstitch around the convex side. I put 2 rows of topstitching next to each other. You could do as many as you like.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-031.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-166" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-031.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 031" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Fold your sweater rectangle in half right sides together.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-032.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-167" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-032.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 032" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Stitch together starting with a bigger seam at the top and angling the seam down so the top with be smaller than the bottom.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-033.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-168" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-033.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 033" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Do the same with you lining fabric.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-036.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-171" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-036.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 036" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Right sides together, pin your circle piece onto the smaller side of the sewn rectangle piece.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-035.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-170" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-035.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 035" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Sew around the circle.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-039.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-174" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-039.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 039" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Next pin and sew the brim onto the larger side of the sewn rectangle. Right sides together.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-040.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-175" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-040.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 040" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Now it's finally starting to look like a hat.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-041.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-176" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-041.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 041" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Pin and sew the circle piece onto the other side. Right sides together.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-042.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-177" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-042.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 042" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Now turn your sweater part right side out and the lining wrong side out.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-043.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-178" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-043.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 043" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Place the sweater part inside the lining part. The right sides should be together and the brim should be between the lining and the sweater part.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-044.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-179" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-044.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 044" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-045.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-180" height="300" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-045.jpg?w=225" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 045" width="225"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Sew all around the edge leaving a 4 inch opening so you can pull it through and turn it right side out.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-046.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-181" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-046.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 046" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Pull it through the opening so it's right side out.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-047.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-182" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-047.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 047" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      Fold the raw edges under and pin all around the hat. Topstitch around the entire hat.
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      <a href="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-050.jpg" style="color: rgb(116, 51, 153); line-height: 1.5;">
                       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-185" height="225" src="http://chicenvelopements.files.wordpress.com/2011/12/red-hat-tutorial-050.jpg?w=300" style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); line-height: 1.5; border-width: 0px; margin: 0px; max-width: 100%; height: auto;" title="red hat tutorial 050" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p style="color: rgb(68, 68, 68); font-family: Georgia,'Bitstream Charter',serif; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 24px;">
                      And "Wah Lah"! You've got a sweater hat!!!!
                     </p>
