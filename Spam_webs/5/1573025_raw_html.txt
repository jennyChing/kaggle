       High Frequency Trading Tips  Can Foot Locker Break Out of a 20 Year Slump?
      </h2>

       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.highfrequencytradingprofit.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/high-frequency-trading-foot-locker.jpg">
         <img alt="high frequency trading tips - foot locker" class=" wp-image-236 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.highfrequencytradingprofit.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/high-frequency-trading-foot-locker.jpg?resize=375%2C284" title="high frequency trading foot locker"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Being in a fairly strong sector, Foot Locker is riding the up wave of retail spending with the likes of Dicks sporting goods (Nyse: DKS) and Hibbet Sports (Nasdaq: HIBB). But is it just a case of Foot Locker playing follow the leader? Or is there more to the run than meets the eye?
       </p>
       <p>
        Looking back at the highs in the mid $33 range from back in the late 80s and early 1990s, we see that not only is Foot Locker moving toward those all time highs, but its doing so on almost 5 times the volume.
       </p>
       <p>
        As well, FL is looking to make a 500 percent gain from the sturdy lows of the $7 range it posted back in 2008.
       </p>
       <p>
        With a stop loss set at just under $29.00 and a potential price target of just under $50, FL could be entered here with a $3 down $15 up target at 5-1.
       </p>
       <p>
        If its being carried by NKE and the sporting goods sector, a strong summer could break FL to all time highs. If not, it may still make a go of it on its own since it is still lagging behind others in its sector and could attract value seeking investors who take profits from positions in competitive stocks like those mentioned earlier.
       </p>
       <p>
        Eitherway, FL is certainly worth a second look if you think it will fit in with one of your
        <a href="http://www.highfrequencytradingprofit.com/trading-strategies/" target="_blank" title="Trading Strategies">
         trading strategies
        </a>
       </p>
