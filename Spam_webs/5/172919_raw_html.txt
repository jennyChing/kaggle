                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                         </span>
                        </div>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       As always, we also welcome any and all suggestions for going invisible online in the comments below. Here, in no particular order, are a few suggestions:
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <strong>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        Use Tor to mask your IP address
                       </span>
                      </strong>
                      <br/>
                      <strong>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       </span>
                      </strong>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       Your internet protocol (IP) address is a numerical representation assigned to any device (computers, printers, phones, etc.) that connects to the internet. Outsiders can trace your IP to track who youve been communicating with, what websites youve been visiting, and especially if youve been up to anything illegal, like downloading copyrighted stuff from Piratebay.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       Thats where
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://www.torproject.org/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        Tor
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       comes in. Originally developed by the U.S. Navy, Tor is widely used by activists, hackers, journalists, law enforcement, and many others to evade detection. Basically, its a system designed to mask your IP address by making its path to the website you want to visit as confusing as possible.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-7tywl5HgDE4/U1U0Ihq_UxI/AAAAAAAAAjs/aJjviZTvIUg/s1600/z_matrix000.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       </a>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       It works by funneling your connection through a series of networked Tor computers at random  like a relay race through a dense crowd of people. Each relay stop can only see the IP address of the computer directly before it; by the time you finally connect to your destination, your data path should be split among multiple computers around the world. The more users there are on the Tor network, the harder you are to find.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       The easiest way to use it is to download the
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://www.torproject.org/projects/torbrowser.html.en" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        Tor browser
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       . MIT
                       <em>
                        Technology Review
                       </em>
                       also has a great explainer of how the technology works
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://www.technologyreview.com/video/413186/how-tor-works/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        here
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       .
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       If youd rather stick with Chrome or Firefox as your primary browser, you can download the
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://www.eff.org/https-everywhere" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        HTTPS Everywhere extension
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       , which encrypts your connection with major websites to make browsing more secure. While the extension project is a collaboration between The Tor Project and the Electronic Frontier Foundation, its not quite as sophisticated as Tor itself. One big downside: Many sites offer limited support for HTTPS, so browsing can be a bit unpredictable.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-MDo-LxKXAfU/U1Uz-CrQt-I/AAAAAAAAAjk/ntJ6eZv3Ln4/s1600/z_NSA_warrant000.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                         <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-MDo-LxKXAfU/U1Uz-CrQt-I/AAAAAAAAAjk/ntJ6eZv3Ln4/s1600/z_NSA_warrant000.jpg"/>
                        </span>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       <strong>
                        Consider setting up a VPN
                       </strong>
                       Virtual Private Networks more-or-less hide your data from the public domain. VPNs were originally set up to allow remote employees access the companys secure servers through encrypted tunnels. But average users can set-up their own VPNs to keep their traffic secure, especially when using something like Starbucks free WiFi (which crooks can use to glean your password).
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       You can find plenty of free VPN services out there. One we like is called
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://secure.logmein.com/products/hamachi/download.aspx" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        Hamachi
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       , which is a free tool that allows users to route traffic back through their home internet connection when theyre out on the road. Its easy to set up, too.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       Another VPN service worth checking out is a one-flick app called
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://www.tunnelbear.com/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        TunnelBear
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       , which
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://thenextweb.com/apps/2011/07/21/tunnelbear-the-most-beautiful-vpn-app-weve-ever-seen/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        <em>
                         The
                        </em>
                        <em>
                         Next Web
                        </em>
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       recommends. Its also free though youll have to pay a $5 fee if you surpass 1GB in monthly data.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       Not only is it neat-looking (its mascot is a bear!), but it also has apps for both iOS and Android.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       <strong>
                        Encrypt your sensitive emails
                       </strong>
                       Why should you, a non-whistleblowing, non-spy, encrypt your email messages? Well, if youre sending sensitive information  like social security numbers or bank account information for a wireless transfer  you obviously dont want that kind of stuff to get intercepted by the wrong eyes.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       You can encrypt your important emails in a few ways.
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://www.pcworld.com/article/254338/how_to_encrypt_your_email.html" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        <em>
                         PC World
                        </em>
                        has a great walkthrough
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       that helps you manually encrypt three different things to ensure maximum security: (1) The connection to your email provider, (2) the actual emails themselves, and (3) the archived and cached versions of your emails.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       One service we really like is called
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://lockbin.com/Messaging" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        Lockbin
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       , which promises to protect messages using FIPS 140-2 encryption libraries. Better yet: Its free, and you dont have to register to use it. You just fill out a form with your message and seal it shut with a secret password.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       If youre a Mac user,
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://gpgtools.org/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        GPG Mail is a free service
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       that generates a PGP (Pretty Good Privacy) key to encrypt your messages. Heres how it looks in action, but you can try it out for yourself
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://gpgtools.org/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        here
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       . (Also, if youre interested in learning more about PGP communication,
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://cryptography.org/getpgp.htm" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        this
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       is a good place to start.)
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       <strong>
                        Dont Google
                       </strong>
                       If youve been following the NSA tracking story, youll know that Google, Yahoo, and Microsoft (which owns Bing) were all reportedly sending data to the government. (An accusation they have all denied, of course.) If you dont want your search history recorded, try
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://duckduckgo.com/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        DuckDuckGo
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       , which promises not to track or store your searches. (Although it
                       <em>
                        does
                       </em>
                       use your searches to improve its algorithms.) The services traffic has increased
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://searchengineland.com/duck-duck-go-prism-private-search-164333" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        50 percent
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       since news of the PRISM program leaked.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <strong>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       </span>
                      </strong>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <strong>
                        <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ZB4LkN1Hhjk/U1U03MIvv-I/AAAAAAAAAj4/QM97pxbsG3k/s1600/z_prisons000.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <img border="0" height="239" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ZB4LkN1Hhjk/U1U03MIvv-I/AAAAAAAAAj4/QM97pxbsG3k/s1600/z_prisons000.jpg" width="320"/>
                         </span>
                        </a>
                       </strong>
                      </div>
                      <strong>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        Chat in private
                       </span>
                      </strong>
                      <br/>
                      <strong>
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       </span>
                      </strong>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       Gchat, iMessage, and their ilk are eminently snoopable. If you want to have a
                       <em>
                        real
                       </em>
                       private conversation online,
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://www.propublica.org/article/a-buyers-guide-to-safer-communication" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        Quinn Norton at
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <em>
                       <a href="http://www.propublica.org/article/a-buyers-guide-to-safer-communication" target="_blank">
                        <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                         ProPublica
                        </span>
                       </a>
                      </em>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       recommends an encrypted chat service like Cryptocat. To use it, simply
                      </span>
                      <a href="https://crypto.cat/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        download the browser plugin
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       , create a name and a chatroom, and invite whoever youd like to talk to. As Norton
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://www.propublica.org/article/a-buyers-guide-to-safer-communication" target="_blank">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                        notes
                       </span>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       , Cryptocat is hands-down the easiest way to get started with end-to-end encryption, where only you and the person youre talking to can see the message.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
