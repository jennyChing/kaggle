              <div style="margin-left: 5px">
               That's what so many people say about this movie (and also "Requiem for a Dream"), but when I read the book/saw the movie (I was probably something like 11 or 12) for some reason it just seemed really glamorous and sexy and exciting to me, and I decided that when I got older, I was going to try heroin, haha. I guess I was probably just twisted from the start. Fast forward nearly 10 years, and I am, in fact, a heroin addict. But I've got no regrets. It sort of
               <i>
                was
               </i>
               glamorous and sexy and exciting, in my perhaps dubious opinion. I've had my fun and made it out alive.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               I wonder how many other kids had the same experience as me? Am I just a total red herring? Nothing in any of the dope movies of the 90s deterred me, it just sort of led me to romanticize the whole thing. I guess it didn't help that Lou Reed and Iggy Pop were sort of my idols later on.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Does anyone remember the speech president Clinton gave in the mid90s about "heroin chic" in the fashion industry, and "Trainspotting", etc? He scolded the fashion and film industries for portraying drug addiction in an appealing way and claimed that it was negatively shaping children's impressions of drug use. Up until a couple years ago I would have laughed at that notion, but I guess ultimately it was true, in my case at least. I'm really curious to find out how many others had the same experience as me.
