          <h1>
           Miles Davis 59 Quintet Skateboards
          </h1>
          <p>
           Posted by
           <a href="http://designcrave.com/author/jeffrey-hyatt/" rel="author" title="Posts by Jeffrey Hyatt">
            Jeffrey Hyatt
           </a>
          </p>
         </header>
         <div class="post-content">
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7491" height="415" src="http://designcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/Miles-Davis-59-Quintet-Skateboards.jpg" title="Miles Davis 59 Quintet Skateboards" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Growing up I was more into surfing than skateboarding, but one look at
           <a href="http://westernedition.wordpress.com/2009/03/06/miles-59-quintet-series-in-stock/">
            <strong>
             Western Editions Miles Davis 59 Quintet Boards
            </strong>
           </a>
           , and Im ready to hit the local skate park immediately. If there were any doubt about Miles Davis everlasting appeal to creative types look no further than these awesome,
           <a href="http://www.ianmjohnson.com/" target="_self">
            Ian Johnson
           </a>
           -designed skateboard decks; a celebration of the musicians who played on the legendary
           <em>
            Kind of Blue
           </em>
           50 years ago.
          </p>
          <p>
           Maybe youd prefer to catch mad air with John Coltrane under your feet. Or grind with bassist Paul Chambers. I will take all five please! The series includes two tee shirts featuring the Miles Davis graphic and a quintet of postcards included with each board. Cannonball Adderley and Wynton Kelly, also part of the
           <em>
            Blue
           </em>
           sessions, were not included. [via
           <a href="http://www.radcollector.com" target="_self">
            rad collector
           </a>
           ]
          </p>
