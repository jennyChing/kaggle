         <p>
          We continue our coverage of gay marriage and equal rights with a new film that explores the divisions among African-Americans on the issue of marriage equality. Despite longstanding opposition from church leadership, the documentary looks at how the black church played a critical role in the passage of the Maryland vote upholding marriage equality in 2012. Set to air on
          <span class="caps">
           PBS
          </span>
          next year, "The New Black" has just won the audience award at
          <span class="caps">
           AFI
          </span>
          Docs and this weekend plays at Frameline, the nations largest
          <span class="caps">
           LGBT
          </span>
          film festival, in San Francisco. Were joined by the films director, Yoruba Richen. "The black church is a big factor in Maryland," Richen says. "[After] they really engaged, I think thats how come you saw the outcome in Maryland, which for the first time the public voted to uphold marriage equality."
