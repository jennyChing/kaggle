     <p>
      <b>
       WASP-1b
      </b>
      is an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planet" title="Extrasolar planet">
       extrasolar planet
      </a>
      orbiting the
      <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">
       star
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/WASP-1" title="WASP-1">
       WASP-1
      </a>
      located 1,239
      <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">
       light-years
      </a>
      away in the
      <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">
       constellation
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Andromeda_(constellation)" title="Andromeda (constellation)">
       Andromeda
      </a>
      . In recognition of the regional support given to the project on
      <a href="/wiki/La_Palma" title="La Palma">
       La Palma
      </a>
      , the discoverers gave the planet the alternative designation
      <b>
       Garafia-1
      </b>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Orbit_and_mass">
       Orbit and mass
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=WASP-1b&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Orbit and mass">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:277px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:WASP-1_b_rv.pdf">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1125" data-file-width="1583" height="195" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/WASP-1_b_rv.pdf/page1-275px-WASP-1_b_rv.pdf.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/WASP-1_b_rv.pdf/page1-413px-WASP-1_b_rv.pdf.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/WASP-1_b_rv.pdf/page1-550px-WASP-1_b_rv.pdf.jpg 2x" width="275"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:WASP-1_b_rv.pdf" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The radial velocity trend of WASP-1, caused by the presence of WASP-1 b.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The planet's mass and radius indicate that it is a
      <a href="/wiki/Gas_giant" title="Gas giant">
       gas giant
      </a>
      with a similar bulk composition to
      <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">
       Jupiter
      </a>
      . Unlike Jupiter, but similar to many other planets detected around other stars, WASP-1b is located very close to its star, and belongs to the class of planets known as
      <a href="/wiki/Hot_Jupiter" title="Hot Jupiter">
       hot Jupiters
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      WASP-1 b was discovered via the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methods_of_detecting_extrasolar_planets" title="Methods of detecting extrasolar planets">
       transit method
      </a>
      by
      <a href="/wiki/SuperWASP" title="SuperWASP">
       SuperWASP
      </a>
      , for which the star and planet are named. Follow-up radial velocity measurements confirmed the presence of an unseen companion, and allowed for the mass of WASP-1 b to be determined.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cameron2007_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cameron2007-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
