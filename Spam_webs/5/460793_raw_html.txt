                  <h2 class="short-teaser">
                   Deadpool's solo series will no longer have him flying solo! This April, our antihero ties the knot!
                  </h2>
                  <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                   <div class="field-items">
                    <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       The Deadpool news keeps on coming. Not only is he getting his own miniseries where he
                       <a href="http://www.denofgeek.us/books-comics/deadpool/231855/deadpool-vs-carnage-1-coming-in-april">
                        hunts down Carnage
                       </a>
                       , but
                       <a href="https://www.nerdist.com/2014/01/exclusive-guess-which-marvel-superhero-is-getting-married-this-april/">
                        the Nerdist broke the big story
                       </a>
                       that this April, Deadpool's getting hitched! Details of the bride are scarce, but the event will take place in
                       <em>
                        <strong>
                         Deadpool #27
                        </strong>
                       </em>
                       by the writing team of Brian Posehn and Gerry Duggan. If it was anyone else, I'd have my reservations, but these two have been killing it on the title, so I can't wait to see where it goes.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       While the big superhero audience is cover image fluff, Deadpool getting married is far from it. According to Duggan in the Nerdist interview, this is a bit of an emotional, yet impulsive, reaction to the events from
                       <em>
                        <strong>
                         the Good, the Bad and the Ugly
                        </strong>
                       </em>
                       .
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <blockquote>
                      <p>
                       <span style="font-size: medium;">
                        The honest answer is that it was written into a Deadpool story, but more or less as a gag, and Jordan D. White, the editor, was like a dog with a bone. He was like, No, no, no  this is going to be a big deal. You know, we wrote it, so its not like we werent behind it. The fun thing has been  after his misadventures, I guess youd call them, in North Korea, in the story arc
                        <em>
                         <strong>
                          the Good, the Bad, and the Ugly
                         </strong>
                        </em>
                        , he learned a lot about himself and why he is the way he is. He doesnt really have too much of a life. Yes, this is a knee-jerk reaction from a wounded guy to go out there and sort of do something crazy to sort of jump start a life, but there is a real emotional reason for doing it, so I think that will be the part that will resonate. Then the actual gags of the wedding will be fun too, but there is a real grounding to it, and thats what will make it fun to explore.
                       </span>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <span style="font-size: medium;">
                        As for how long shell be around, Im not 100% sure, because when you get close to Deadpool, thats maybe when I start to think about adding you in my deadpool.
                       </span>
                      </p>
                     </blockquote>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       For those who haven't been keeping up on
                       <em>
                        <strong>
                         Deadpool
                        </strong>
                       </em>
                       , said North Korea storyline dealt with Deadpool finding out about an illegitimate daughter he had. She and her mother were kept hostage for the sake of blackmail and despite Deadpool's best efforts, the mother was killed and the fate of the girl is unknown. Deadpool also took care of Butler, a man who had experimented on Deadpool relentlessly over the years and injected him with so many false memories that Deadpool's own continuity comes into question. Now Deadpool is able to move on and live his own life.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       Well, not quite yet. He still has the soul of SHIELD's Agent Preston stuck in his head. In the current storyline, he's trying to find her a new body to inhabit and if he succeeds, I imagine that would only add to the need for another. A head that's suddenly lonely while Preston gets to live with her loving family? It's only natural that he'd thirst for commitment to fill the void.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       As part of the announcement, Scott Koblish drew up this wonderful wrap-around cover.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <img alt="" class="media-image attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__232259 img__view_mode__content_full_width attr__format__content_full_width" height="479" src="http://cdn.denofgeek.us/sites/denofgeekus/files/styles/insert_main_wide_image/public/deadpool_27_cover.jpg?itok=Dh3YnfnN" width="620"/>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       Some fun things to note:
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       - Human Torch burning Thing while making it look like it's Lockheed.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       - Black Panther staring longingly at Storm.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       - Other than Red Hulk, Venom and Ghost Rider, none of Deadpool's fellow Thunderbolts are there for him. Unless Elektra is... Nah. Couldn't be.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       - The Agents of Atlas are there. I only mention this because I miss reading comics with the Agents of Atlas in them.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       - Danielle Cage looks an awfully lot like a boy.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       - Old flame Siryn is one of the bridesmaids, which is their way of taking one of the biggest red herrings off the board.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       This isn't Deadpool's first foray with marriage by a longshot. He insisted to be married to a woman named Mercedez, but it turned out that he killed her and her husband before taking the husband's identity and calling himself "Wade Wilson." Whether that's canon or not is up to which writer you enjoy better, I suppose, since different scribes disagree on this origin.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       In
                       <em>
                        <strong>
                         Identity Disc
                        </strong>
                       </em>
                       , Deadpool mentioned having an ex-wife named Gretchen, who left him and stayed gone. He joined the villain team of Juggernaut, Sabretooth, Vulture, Bullseye and Sandman because their mysterious benefactor had information on where he could find her. He never did get his payoff.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       More recently, Deadpool's been married to an overweight alien hippo lady and even sultry demon spawn Satana. If Marvel feels that marriage and divorce age a character, then Wade Wilson is Larry King in more ways than one!
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       So who is the lucky bride? Duggan's keeping that a secret. Her bouquet resembles a skull, causing speculation that it's Death herself, but I can't see that fitting in with the story Posehn and Duggan are trying to tell.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       I wouldn't mind it being Outlaw. She hasn't shown up in a while and there's a history between the two. Hell, she appears to be the go-to love interest for him whenever they do an out-of-continuity story like
                       <em>
                        <strong>
                         Deadpool Pulp
                        </strong>
                       </em>
                       and
                       <em>
                        <strong>
                         Deadpool MAX
                        </strong>
                       </em>
                       .
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       We'll see in three months, I suppose. In the meantime, Marvel sent us an invitation...erm...we mean...a press release!
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <img alt="" class="media-image attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__232260 img__view_mode__content_full_width attr__format__content_full_width" height="938" src="http://cdn.denofgeek.us/sites/denofgeekus/files/styles/insert_main_wide_image/public/deadpool_wedding_invitation.jpg?itok=SGjhTEep" width="620"/>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       Save the date! Marvel Comics would like to formally request the honor of your presence at the wedding of Deadpool this April in the pages of the super-sizedDEADPOOL #27! Presiding over the ceremony will be Messers Gerry Duggan and Brian Posehn with the artistic aesthetics of Mike Hawthorne and Scott Koblish.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       A day you never dreamed would come has finally arrived  as Deadpool and his mysterious bride to be (wouldnt you like to know?) tie the knot in front of friends and family alike. And in a way only the Merc With a Mouth can! Believe us, this one is going to beinteresting.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       Were complicating his life and well be pulling him in different directions, said writer Gerry Duggan in an interview with
                       <a href="http://marvel.com/" target="_blank">
                        Marvel.com
                       </a>
                       . I think thats something everyone can relate to, and will lead to some more fun comic books.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       The ceremony will be followed by a reception unlike any other  as pool writers of yesteryear return for new stories featuring the garish groom-to-be. Including Fabian Nicieza, Mark Waid, Joe Kelly, Christopher Priest, Jimmy Palmiotti, Frank Tieri, Gail Simone, Daniel Way &amp; Victor Gischler.
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       Though gifts are not required, the happy couple has requested you bring your own weapons to the reception. Please RSVP at your local comic shops whenDEADPOOL #27walks down the aisle this April!
                      </span>
                     </p>
