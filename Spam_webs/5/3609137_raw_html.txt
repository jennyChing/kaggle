                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     The width of a circle --- Venice (1)
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2285496263254713365" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     You've made it, you're wealthy now, and preferably American, because Americans are more likely to do it than other mortals. You've already donated a bundle to many causes (causes, let's face it, is now a standard entry on any celebrity's resum), but soup kitchens and AIDS and blood diamonds get you only so far, and you're among the 53% that love art (as opposed to the 47%), you totally love it, and you totally admire artists, who need all the help they can get since
                     <i>
                      Puccini
                     </i>
                     told us about
                     <i>
                      Mimi
                     </i>
                     and
                     <i>
                      van Gogh
                     </i>
                     , so a foundation it is, a new foundation in support of the
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      ARTS,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      because there are simply not enough of these foundations.
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      Like.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      Now, your foundation needs to be visible since this is not about you, but about the
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      ARTS
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      . And you always totally loved Venice. Venice,
                      <i>
                       la serenissima,
                      </i>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      the only city in the world that is in itself a
                      <i>
                       Gesamtkunstwerk
                      </i>
                      , the only city worth your efforts except your hometown that's already stuffed,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      stuffed
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      ,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                      stuffed
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      with a
                      <i>
                       Lisa Hooksey
                      </i>
                      museum (that's your name), and a Lisa Hooksey wing of the local hospital, and a Lisa Hooksey conference room at the local college, and so on, and so it's Venice.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-YrXc_fhp7KM/UnKRgbhXj-I/AAAAAAAAKE4/sf0CEL6UbR0/s1600/20131029_085008.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="300" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-YrXc_fhp7KM/UnKRgbhXj-I/AAAAAAAAKE4/sf0CEL6UbR0/s400/20131029_085008.jpg" width="400"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         Venice, Grande Canale, home to the grandest art foundations
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <br/>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      How to move from here? A foundation has a board, and you might preside over it, or you're too busy, swamped, obligations, causes everywhere, so there's professional help being hired. People who really understand (about art).
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      Your's truly had been superficially in touch with some of these people and he can tell you they won't come cheap, because you need the best,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      the best
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      ,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                      the best
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      ,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      and in our market society bestness has
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      its price (15 years ago, the beginning salary for a beginning charity foundation person was around 100k annually in Geneva,
                      <i>
                       Suisse
                      </i>
                      , but perhaps they charge less in Venice). Also from memory: an international foundation is lucky if it has an efficiency quota of 20%, meaning that 80% of your money will go to the people who deserve it, ie. the best, the best, the best.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      The board will meet and wonder what to do with the remaining 20%. You may or may not preside over the meeting but it doesn't matter. A part of it will got to the
                      <i>
                       Lisa Hooksey Award
                      </i>
                      , which is like the
                      <i>
                       Nobel Price
                      </i>
                      , except that it's dedicated to a
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      new
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      and extremely talented artist who excels, really, and has shown vision, and doesn't smoke, and is so new,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      new
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      ,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                      new
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      , and has surprised the art world a great deal, recently, totally, everybody, and doesn't beat up her wife, because that (the beating) was the reason that
                      <i>
                       Norman Mailer
                      </i>
                      never got the Nobel Price. And her work means a paradigm shift, except that paradigm shifts are so yesterday and revolutions are so yesterday and subversion is so yesterday, so somebody has to fill in the blanks, and that will be the artist herself, the recipient of the Lisa Hooksey Award, as noted, which will go this year to N. N. (
                      <i>
                       nomen nominaturem
                      </i>
                      ), who happens to be a niece of the vice president of your board.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      Then there's the the
                      <i>
                       Lisa Hooksey Stipend
                      </i>
                      . Idem.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ayg_XkocCfc/UnKQBhdHTPI/AAAAAAAAKEs/Ss3mrQRr4Xc/s1600/20131029_165540.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="300" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ayg_XkocCfc/UnKQBhdHTPI/AAAAAAAAKEs/Ss3mrQRr4Xc/s400/20131029_165540.jpg" width="400"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         The universe of unreality (click for enlargement)
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      And then there's the show in Venice. The
                      <i>
                       Lisa Hooksey Foundation Exhibition
                      </i>
                      that will present to the world the work,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      work
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      ,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                      work
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      of an absolutely renowned artist who's totally incredible and terrific and lateral and whom every tourist needs to see. Like
                      <i>
                       Damien Hirst
                      </i>
                      , say, who declines politely through his publicist. Or
                      <i>
                       Natascha Ungeheuer,
                      </i>
                      about whose ontological status we're somewhat iffy. Or, wait,
                      <i>
                       Zhong Biao
                      </i>
                      , who has done this totally terrific work about visions, and art is about visions, too. Or it's another N.N. with a German name, if memory serves, who has this sense-challenging exhibition about "Solving the Problem of Measuring the Width of a Circle." You have to trust us on that one, we're not extemporizing, we just failed to take a picture and totally lost our way the next day when trying to return to the scene of the crime.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      Or another German name which is on the placard for the
                      <i>
                       Monument to a Monument
                      </i>
                      , because, you know,
                      <i>
                       reflexivity
                      </i>
                      , the little sister of revolutions and paradigm shifts and subversions is back,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      back
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      ,
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                      back
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      since Damien Hirst gave an interview on his own web site where he mentions reflexivity exactly 3.141592... times.
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-k8QpvNBz4zY/UnKRnMw4ntI/AAAAAAAAKFA/FwzSDWedCGM/s1600/Untitled-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-k8QpvNBz4zY/UnKRnMw4ntI/AAAAAAAAKFA/FwzSDWedCGM/s400/Untitled-1.jpg" width="280"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         The Monument to a Monument
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: small;">
                      That's it, folks, that was our first post about Venice. Apologies. Yes, you've guessed right, we visited Venice this week.
                     </span>

                   <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-w6YNJNoWSo8/VFj2ifwxTHI/AAAAAAAANrc/1Kmy5g0MyRA/s1600/John-Lee-200-200.jpg"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                  John Lee
                 </span>
                 , 29. Narrator. Assistant professor of French at
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Southern Georgia College
                 </span>
                 , an institution he refers to as hippocampus. Bipolar. Addicted to Alex Iglesias. Accumulating baggage on the side, starting with
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Ben Fletcher
                 </span>
                 . Founder and CEO of the
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Georgia Beach A-level escort service
                 </span>
                 . Member of the jury of the Georgia Beach Festival 2014.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                  <div style="text-align: left;">
                   <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-IH6rn7HSKco/VFj2gLwoWhI/AAAAAAAANrE/Zh9sN3K0v2A/s1600/Alex-200-200.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                    <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-IH6rn7HSKco/VFj2gLwoWhI/AAAAAAAANrE/Zh9sN3K0v2A/s1600/Alex-200-200.jpg"/>
                   </a>
                  </div>
                  <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                   Alexander Iglesias
                  </span>
                  , 29. Main object of desire. Paramedic and computer science dropout. Clinically depressed in his former life, committed suicide or a least tried---he's stubbornly maintaining he succeeded and is "in heaven" now. Suffers from amnesia, lost his personal episodic memory, including the memory of his sexual preferences. Professes love to
                  <span style="font-style:italic;">
                   John Lee
                  </span>
                  though, because "wishes are fulfilled in heaven and John is the only angel who wants his love."(Part I, Ch. 54) Gradually morphing into a neo-neo Sherlock Holmes.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                   <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rG9Q_J9paKs/VFj2kC8TKDI/AAAAAAAANrw/W9J_w41SiXw/s1600/Maurice-200-200.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                    <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rG9Q_J9paKs/VFj2kC8TKDI/AAAAAAAANrw/W9J_w41SiXw/s1600/Maurice-200-200.jpg"/>
                   </a>
                  </div>
                  <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                   Maurice Dymond
                  </span>
                  , 29. Tourist agent and budding amateur author of a script about the aftermath of the George W. Bush presidency ("Freedom Fries"). Arrived in Georgia Beach on an exchange program with Torquay, his home town in England. Still bedridden as a consequence of a vicious assault by psychopathic cop
                  <span style="font-style:italic;">
                   Dick Benson
                  </span>
                  ; staying with John at the moment.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                   <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-LIYIbAf67h4/VFj2hoxPNiI/AAAAAAAANrU/2LBZLpvI-NI/s1600/Godehart.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                    <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-LIYIbAf67h4/VFj2hoxPNiI/AAAAAAAANrU/2LBZLpvI-NI/s1600/Godehart.jpg"/>
                   </a>
                  </div>
                  <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                   Godehart Wagner
                  </span>
                  , 45. Fifth-generation member of the Richard Wagner clan, widower of
                  <span style="font-style:italic;">
                   Eleanor Wagner-Beasley
                  </span>
                  , a spouse of convenience. Makes and sells Wagner-memorabilia, including the famous Bavarian leather shorts embroidered with a crotch-centered likeness of the composer. Oversexed. Tricked by Mayor H. Hamblin into underwriting the Dracula Award of the Georgia Beach Festival. Entered the contest to win his money back.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                   <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--hM7Wp8Hqng/VFj2jIrmPXI/AAAAAAAANrg/I4d3YGYYcUw/s1600/Marion-Zimmer-Bradley.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                    <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--hM7Wp8Hqng/VFj2jIrmPXI/AAAAAAAANrg/I4d3YGYYcUw/s1600/Marion-Zimmer-Bradley.jpg"/>
                   </a>
                  </div>
                  <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                   Alice Sandeman
                  </span>
                  , MD ("Dr. Dyke"), 45. Head of the much-needed ER of the local hospital, confidante of Alex. Former lover of
                  <span style="font-style:italic;">
                   Eleanor Wagner-Beasley
                  </span>
                  .
                 </div>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-n0irFrraLz4/VFj2g0a0J2I/AAAAAAAANrI/pKKd8A7HJVA/s1600/Ben-200-200.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-n0irFrraLz4/VFj2g0a0J2I/AAAAAAAANrI/pKKd8A7HJVA/s1600/Ben-200-200.jpg"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                  John ("Ben") Fletcher
                 </span>
                 , 20, son of
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Dr. and Mrs. Martin Luther
                  <span style="font-style:italic;">
                   Fletcher.
                  </span>
                 </span>
                 Studies engineering at
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Georgia Tech
                 </span>
                 . Briefly in the employ of Luke's convenience store, now freelancing for John's A-level escort service.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                  <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-_iFIoAuOWPE/VFkIYBo1KyI/AAAAAAAANsM/-NNbUipn-9U/s1600/Barbette-Bienpensant.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-_iFIoAuOWPE/VFkIYBo1KyI/AAAAAAAANsM/-NNbUipn-9U/s1600/Barbette-Bienpensant.jpg"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <div style="text-align: left;">
                 </div>
                 <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                  Barbette Bienpensant
                 </span>
                 , professor of quantitative metaphysics, 38. Renowned forecaster of doom. Half-sister of
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Juliette B
                 </span>
                 . Smoker. Nymphomaniac. Member of the festival jury.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                  <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-oiE3a65tUWg/VFkIYg_pvdI/AAAAAAAANsQ/xlSMddZDwsg/s1600/Juliette-Bienpensant.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-oiE3a65tUWg/VFkIYg_pvdI/AAAAAAAANsQ/xlSMddZDwsg/s1600/Juliette-Bienpensant.jpg"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <div style="text-align: left;">
                 </div>
                 <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                  Juliette Bienpensant
                 </span>
                 , 16, attendant of
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Exeter Academy
                 </span>
                 . Dreams a lot when not reading the English canon or playing with her i-thing (or playing the vamp). Orphaned two years ago. Half-sister of Barbette B. Falls in Love with
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Romeo
                 </span>
                 .
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                  <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-489ElWsY0b0/VFj2j3p-RvI/AAAAAAAANrs/KIfXYSFqR8A/s1600/Romeo-200-200.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-489ElWsY0b0/VFj2j3p-RvI/AAAAAAAANrs/KIfXYSFqR8A/s1600/Romeo-200-200.jpg"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                  Romeo
                 </span>
                 (pseudonym), 16. Background unclear, living a trailer existence with sugar daddy
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Richard Roper
                 </span>
                 . Possesses eidetic memory.  Falls in love with
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Juliette Bienpensant
                 </span>
                 .
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                  <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-dyCNTHRMk-Y/VFkYxPq4taI/AAAAAAAANsk/B7cTXlIsVfg/s1600/Brigitta-Haagen-Dasz-200-200.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-dyCNTHRMk-Y/VFkYxPq4taI/AAAAAAAANsk/B7cTXlIsVfg/s1600/Brigitta-Haagen-Dasz-200-200.jpg"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                  Brigitt Dazs
                 </span>
                 , 45, author of flame-hot romance and spouse of
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Hgen D.
                 </span>
                 Joins
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Jane Trumpleton
                 </span>
                 for an A-level encounter with
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Ben Fletcher
                 </span>
                 .
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                  <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-8qj3wKTUckQ/VFkYypVymzI/AAAAAAAANss/2Ii8r8bHND8/s1600/Jane-Trumpleton.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-8qj3wKTUckQ/VFkYypVymzI/AAAAAAAANss/2Ii8r8bHND8/s1600/Jane-Trumpleton.jpg"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                  Jane Trumpleton
                 </span>
                 , 40, socialite/desperate housewife. Intimate friend of
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Muffy Doolittle
                 </span>
                 , another desperate housewife. Met
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  John Lee
                 </span>
                 in a chance encounter and co-inspired his A-level service. Joins
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Brigitta D
                 </span>
                 . for an A-level encounter with
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Ben Fletcher
                 </span>
                 . Also well-acquainted with
                 <span style="font-style:italic;">
                  Nick
                 </span>
                 , the owner of Nick's restaurant.

                  <span style="font-weight:bold;">
                   Nick.
                  </span>
                  Owner of Nick's restaurant, the largest eatery in the world. Acquaintance of John Lee, and, in a different sense, of Jane Trumpleton, Muffy Doolittle, and many other fine ladies. Very straight.
