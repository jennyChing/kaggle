          <h1>
           5 Minute Brown Butter Honey Granola
          </h1>
         </div>
         <div class="post-content clearfix">
          <p>
           <img alt="5 Minute Brown Butter Honey Granola // The Sugar Hit" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4514" height="533" src="http://www.thesugarhit.com/wp-content/uploads/5-Minute-Brown-Butter-Honey-Granola-1.jpg" width="800"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Its Monday. Maybe we spent the whole weekend doing other things. Maybe we were laughing about this
           <a href="http://www.newyorker.com/humor/daily-shouts/a-day-in-the-life-of-pinterest" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.newyorker.com/humor/daily-shouts/a-day-in-the-life-of-pinterest', 'Pinterest']);" target="_blank">
            Pinterest
           </a>
           article, I dont know. One way or another we didnt get around to thinking about the week ahead. Specifically, how we would feed ourselves first thing in the morning during that week.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           So we know have a serious breakfast deficiency. Well guess what? In 5 minutes time that problem will be SOLVED my disorganised amigos!
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="5 Minute Brown Butter Honey Granola // The Sugar Hit" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4518" height="1200" src="http://www.thesugarhit.com/wp-content/uploads/5-Minute-Brown-Butter-Honey-Granola-5.jpg" width="800"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           This here is a small-batch (&lt; buzzword) of granola, and its crazy easy to make. When I say five minutes, I mean it. And its not a crappy, thisll do version of granola  this isnt just a quick way to liven up some oats.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Its actually really tasty, flavoursome, crunchy, delicious granola. In 5 minutes! WIN.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="5 Minute Brown Butter Honey Granola // The Sugar Hit" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4517" height="1200" src="http://www.thesugarhit.com/wp-content/uploads/5-Minute-Brown-Butter-Honey-Granola-4.jpg" width="800"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           This recipe is the things I always have in my cupboard version. But you hardly need me to tell you that granola is open to variation. Just think of your additions as things that need toasting and things that dont.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           So feel free to add other nuts, seeds and things like that at the start, and then stir through dried fruits or delicate things (like coconut  I prefer my coconut untoasted) at the end.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="5 Minute Brown Butter Honey Granola // The Sugar Hit" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4516" height="1200" src="http://www.thesugarhit.com/wp-content/uploads/5-Minute-Brown-Butter-Honey-Granola-3.jpg" width="800"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           If youre anything like me during the week, finding 5 minutes is hard enough, so you can take my word for it that this is a solid spend of time.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           And once youve spent that five minutes on this, you get to dig in to the spoils for the rest of the week. I love that feeling of accomplishment on a Monday. Enjoy it!
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>

           <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
            A super quick weekday granola.
           </div>
           <div class="divERSHeadItems">
            <div class="ERSAuthor">
             Author:
             <span itemprop="author">
              Sarah Coates
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="ERSServes">
             Serves:
             <span itemprop="recipeYield">
              1 cups
             </span>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSIngredients">
            <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
             Ingredients
            </div>
            <ul>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 tbsp butter
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              3 tbsp honey
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 cup (110g) whole rolled oats
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               cup (50g) flaked almonds
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 tbsp sesame seeds
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              pinch of salt
             </li>
            </ul>
            <div class="ERSClear">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSInstructions">
            <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
             Instructions
            </div>
            <ol>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Place the butter into a large non-stick frying pan, and cook until it turns light golden brown.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Add in the honey and stir to combine, before adding the rest of the ingredients.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Cook, stirring, over a low-medium heat, until the nuts and oats become toasted and golden (about 2-3 minutes).
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Once it looks ready, spread the granola out onto a lined baking sheet and leave to cool completely.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Stored in a sealed jar, it will keep well for at least a 7-10 days.
             </li>
            </ol>
