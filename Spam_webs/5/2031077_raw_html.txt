                Turkey Pesto Meatballs
               </a>

              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-dCJTpq3y5gs/VZUeY-YDW1I/AAAAAAAAB6c/Hrv2CTEkZ00/s1600/Turkey-Pesto-Meatballs-%25284%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="Turkey Pesto Meatballs" border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-dCJTpq3y5gs/VZUeY-YDW1I/AAAAAAAAB6c/Hrv2CTEkZ00/s1600/Turkey-Pesto-Meatballs-%25284%2529.jpg" title="Turkey Pesto Meatballs"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
              </div>
              I worked at a pizza place in Ocean City (MD) during the summer right before I started college.
              <br/>
              It was called "Circus Pizza" and it was owned by an Italian guy and his Russian wife.
              <span class="st">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span class="st">
               They ran the place like it was their own home. They
              </span>
              <span class="st">
               offered honest Italian dishes and a really good thin-crust pizza. They were both  adorable and arguably the best employers I've worked for.
              </span>
              <br/>
              One night after service was
              <span class="st">
               over and everyone had gone home
              </span>
              , I started making tomato sauce for the next day in
 one of those huge commercial mixers. Brilliant me decides that if 
the sauce takes 10 minutes to mix on speed 1, it should only take 1 
minute to mix on speed 10.
              <br/>
              I patted myself on the back for coming up 
with such a smart idea and happily turned the dial to 10.  Only for a 
second.
              <br/>

              <br id="docs-internal-guid-a5d2ef6f-3ef6-c31c-f9e6-d939f5f84231"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              The heatwaves are coming and we all know it.
              <br/>
              When it gets hot outside the last thing I want to do is turn on the oven.
              <br/>
              My apartment is always hot during summer months and turning on the oven would make it the hottest place on the planet.
              <br/>
              Not really, obviously, but thats what it feels like when its 100 degrees outside and the oven is cranking at 400 degrees for half an hour.
              <br/>
              I have A/C (not central, just a window units) so I can cool the apartment down, but I feel its a total waste of money  I dont know about yours, but my electric bill is bad enough already.
              <br/>
              That being said, I dont completely avoid the oven during the summer, but I try not to use it as much.

                Zoodles Aglio Olio e Peperoncino
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="date-outer">
             <h2 class="date-header">
              <span>
               June 24, 2015

             <div class="date-posts">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-821665905209739430">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hAP2NbqVvpM/VYqiZ5Cr79I/AAAAAAAAB4U/20tqM2P1joA/s1600/Zoodles-Aglio-Olio-e-Peperoncino.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="Zoodles Aglio Olio e Peperoncino" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hAP2NbqVvpM/VYqiZ5Cr79I/AAAAAAAAB4U/20tqM2P1joA/s1600/Zoodles-Aglio-Olio-e-Peperoncino.jpg" title="Zoodles Aglio Olio e Peperoncino"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br id="docs-internal-guid-f8fe9826-258a-19b5-b312-a923206d7091"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Spaghetti aglio, olio, e peperoncino is an awesome Italian dish  spaghetti made with garlic, olive oil, and hot chili pepper. Served with chopped parsley and a sprinkling of Parmesan cheese.
              <br/>
              Its well loved because its so easy and inexpensive to make, and its a perfect solution when youre in a crunch.
              <br/>
              For instance, when youre starving after a night out and youve got nothing in your fridge or you dont feel like getting greasy food from the nearby Deli, making this spaghetti is a cinch.
              <br/>
              Even when its 3am and youve had a few too many.
              <br/>
              You know, just to throw a random example out there.
              <br/>
              And just for the record, making spaghetti aglio, olio e peperoncino late at night is pretty common in in Italy  according to my friend Francesco.

                Eggplant Rollatini
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="date-outer">
             <h2 class="date-header">
              <span>
               June 22, 2015
              </span>
             </h2>
             <div class="date-posts">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4626212442789882921">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-GPYN64nQ3eI/VYe-6rQMk0I/AAAAAAAAB4A/mu-SFA2I4NI/s1600/Eggplant-Rollatini.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="Eggplant Rollatini" border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-GPYN64nQ3eI/VYe-6rQMk0I/AAAAAAAAB4A/mu-SFA2I4NI/s1600/Eggplant-Rollatini.jpg" title="Eggplant Rollatini"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br id="docs-internal-guid-63c8b1af-1a41-455f-7fc7-1fb5f49354e7"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I have the distinct feeling my Macbook Pro is going to die soon.
              <br/>
              I purchased it in September 2008. So I know it is old, but everything works perfectly fine  except for the battery which died after two years and that was about it.
              <br/>
              However, two months ago I started getting tons of freezing.
              <br/>
              It happens sporadically and it doesnt seem to be a direct result of me doing anything in particular. The screen will just suddenly spaz out and then the whole Macbook will freeze up.
              <br/>
              Every time that happens, I freak out.
              <br/>
              I religiously back-up to an external drive with Time Machine every week, so I felt pretty good about not losing any data. Still, I freak out as it feels like the laptop is going to explode or something.
              <br/>
              Other than the freezing, the Macbook works perfectly. It is not slow, but the fan starts to run continually, about 1 minute after start-up, and very loudly too.
              <br/>
              It feels like the laptop is about to take-off or that its hyperventilating.
              <br/>

              </div>
