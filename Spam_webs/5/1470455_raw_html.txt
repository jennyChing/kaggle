        <p>
         Are you a Disney addict and ever wondered if you could ever magically teleport into their world? Disneyland isnt what were going to talk about. Its going to be all about the places that made it into the sketchbooks of the creatives behind every Disney animation. These illustrations later became the backdrops that completed every classic Disney animation  from 2D to 3D  loved by all ages. Some settings were made by drawing inspiration fromdifferent real-life locations and combining them into a whole new fictional town; while some were entirely based onspecific locations. Find out the real-life places that inspired your favorite Disney animated movies:

        <p>
         <a href="http://www.exploring-castles.com/chillon_castle.html">
          Chteau de Chillon

         Located about six kilometers north of Siem Reap, south of Angkor Thorn, Angkor Wat is an architectural masterpiece that was built in the first half of the 12th century. This is known as the largest monument of the Angkor group occupying a rectangular area of about 208 hectares. It has a 200-meter wide moat where you can find the long sandstone causeway thatll lead you the way to the monument. Angkor Wat was a funerary temple for King Suryavarman II and symbolically, it is a miniature
         <a href="http://www.tourismcambodia.com/attractions/angkor/angkor-wat.htm">
          replica of the universe in stone
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h3>
         22. The Emperors New Groove
        </h3>
        <h4>
         Kuzcos Empire // Machu Picchu, Peru
        </h4>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_17542" style="width: 700px">
         <img alt="Photo credit: Pinterest" class="wp-image-17542" height="690" src="http://whenonearth.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/7b27fc4dfc85571f533950eeb81ccd10.jpg" width="690"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo credit:
          <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/pin/398990848208913298/">
           Pinterest
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_17541" style="width: 700px">
         <img alt="Photo credit: wbirt1" class="wp-image-17541" height="368" src="http://whenonearth.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/9093323982_13f9f1df9c_z.jpg" width="690"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo credit:
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/12496504@N06/9093323982">
           wbirt1
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The amazing creation of the Inca Empire
         <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/274">
          in the middle of a tropical mountain forest
         </a>
         , the Machu Picchu, is an ancient site made up of giant walls, hundreds of stone structures, and agricultural terraces. It is set on the eastern slopes of the Andes,
         <a href="http://sacredsites.com/americas/peru/machu_picchu.html">
          2000 feet above the rumbling Urubamba river
         </a>
         . The small city contained palaces, baths, temples, storage room, and some 150 houses  all carved from gray granite.
        </p>
        <p>
         An all-Disney-inspired trip anyone?
        </p>
