           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-fbaea9c82e6996c317f5">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <p>
              As an avid trail runner, hiker and skier I though picking up mountain biking would come somewhat naturally. Especially considering learning to bike is something most people do as kids. Since I am on the trails often I see loads of mountain bikers who make it look like a piece of cake. I live in a cute little mountain town called Squamish. The area is considered to have world class mountain bike trails so I thought it was about time I tookup the sport. Well let me tell you that learning to mountain bike has been one of the most humbling and rewarding experiences I have had to date. Living in the Sea to Sky region over the last five years has taught me a lot about believing in my own abilities, living outside my comfort zone and the importance of always being open to new experiences. A few lessons that come to mind after my first day out on a mountain bike:
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               <strong>
                Letting go of control -
               </strong>
               Typically I am a girl that likes to try new things in a slow, steady and calculated manner. I have learned from skiing and trail running that it's important to focus on where I want to go and to trust my peripheral vision to handle what isjust in front of me. On a bike however I found this challenging. The tires hit rocks and roots and I was taken off course. I was obsessing over each obstacle on the trail. It took sometime to relax and let go of the need to control every bump in the road and trust that I could handle the shifts and not fly into the bushes.
              </li>
              <li>
               <strong>
                Building confidence
               </strong>
               - If you are new to mountain biking, start off small. Especially if you are anything like me and haven't been on a bike for years. It had been so long since I was on a bike that I had to learn how to shift gears and just get used to the feeling of having my center of gravity be higher than what I'm usedto in other sports I love. So if it takes riding a few gravel trails with next to no incline/decline, do it! I found it helpful to pick a trail that was relatively wide too. I started on a single track trail and quickly realized I didn't have enough sense of control over the bike to feel comfortable that I wouldn't flyoff into the bushes with every bump, root or rock I passed.
              </li>
              <li>
               <strong>
                Take a leap of faith
               </strong>
               - When you are at the top of a trail the only thingyou can do is take a deep breath and start pedaling. The best way to learn is by doing. We can read all we want about technique but until we put it into practice we don't know what weare capable of. Of course it's important to not over estimate your capabilities but it's equally important to believe in yourself and to try new things.
              </li>
              <li>
               <strong>
                Be willing to look a little silly -
               </strong>
               Part of learning is being willing to look a little silly when you bail. Then dusting yourself off and trying again. No one is graceful in their first attempt. Even the best of the best fall when they are pushing their limits and are trying to improve.
              </li>
              <li>
               <strong>
                It's all about attitude
               </strong>
               <strong>
                -
               </strong>
               Attitude to me doesn't mean I am always little Ms. Sunshine. It means I can communicate what is challenging me in a constructive and respectful manner to those I am with and being open to their feedback. When we surround ourselves with others who are more experienced it can help improve our abilities and push our limits. It is essential to channel our frustrations into open communication so people want to help. Otherwise we risk being left in the dust because nobody wants to play with a Debbie Downer.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              There is something special about connecting with people who are willing to take risks and learn new skills. Learning anything new is a humbling experience. I still have a long way to go before I am one of the mountain bikers making the sport look easy. But I am dedicated to improving and surrounding myself with people who inspire me to keep growing.
             </p>
             <p>
              If you have ever wanted to take avacation with a group of awesome women who share a passion for trail running, hiking, skiing and yoga then check out Kindred Escapes Adventure Retreats
              <a href="http://www.kindredescapes.com/">
               Click here
              </a>
              . All ability levels are welcome. We are focused on getting women into the outdoors and creating a community of adventurous women open to trying new things.
             </p>
            </div>
