         <h2 class="hdg hdg_0">
          Ros Hibiscus Spritzer
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="feature-author">
         <div class="user-content">
          Posted 06/02/14 by Kate Arends
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="feature-bd">
         <div class="user-content">
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Tis the season to enjoy ros! And for that, we are all very thankful. Lets get one thing out of the way before we get to the good stuff: Theres no shame in drinking pink wine. End of story.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="W&amp;D for Apothic Wine_09" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16012" src="http://witanddelight.com/content/uploads//2014/06/WD-for-Apothic-Wine_09.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="W&amp;D for Apothic Wine_35" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16011" src="http://witanddelight.com/content/uploads//2014/06/WD-for-Apothic-Wine_35.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Lets just go ahead anddeclared ros as the unofficial beverage of the summer. Ros tends to be an affordable (yay!) and food-friendly, but the delicious pink wine is ideal for mixing cocktails and low-alcohol spritzers. Now, many people think wine spritzers are synonymous with wine coolers (which I find kind of charming for some reason), but they dont have to be.
          </p>
          <p>
           To dispel those myths, we put together a little
           <a href="http://blog.apothic.com/meet-the-blend-apothic-rose/" target="_blank">
            Apothic Rose
           </a>
           Hibiscus Spritzer to wet your whistle. We accompanied the low-alcohol spritzer with a super simple spread I normally put together for friends. My get-togethers most often happen last minute, especially in the summer months. Weve learned to just wing it and rely on the basics: cocktails, meats, cheeses, and breads. Yum. For you ambitious readers looking for a challenge, take the extra time youd normal spend making an elaborate meal to kick back and enjoy some ros. Well get to those recipes this fall, and in the meantime, youre welcome.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="W&amp;D-for-Apothic-Wine_60" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16014" src="http://witanddelight.com/content/uploads//2014/06/WD-for-Apothic-Wine_60.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Apothic Rose Hibiscus Spritzer
           </strong>
           (
           <a href="http://www.vosgeschocolate.com/product/Hibiscus-Rose-Spritzer-Summer-Drink-Recipe" target="_blank">
            via
           </a>
           )
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://blog.apothic.com/meet-the-blend-apothic-rose/" target="_blank">
            Apothic Rose
           </a>
           Hibiscus Spritzer
           <strong>
            <br/>
           </strong>
           2 cups lemon juice
           <br/>
           2 cups water
           <br/>
           2 cups ice
           <br/>
           1 tbs Hibiscus Simple Syrup
           <br/>
           To taste
           <a href="http://blog.apothic.com/meet-the-blend-apothic-rose/">
            Apothic Rose
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Hibiscus Simple Syrup
           <strong>
            <br/>
           </strong>
           1/2 cup sugar
           <br/>
           1/2 cup water
           <br/>
           1 tbs Organic Hibiscus powder
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="W&amp;D for Apothic Wine_33" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16013" src="http://witanddelight.com/content/uploads//2014/06/WD-for-Apothic-Wine_33.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           What to serve alongside
           <a href="http://blog.apothic.com/meet-the-blend-apothic-rose/" target="_blank">
            Apothic Rose
           </a>
           Hibiscus Spritzer:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Cold meats (especially cold cuts) tend to have saltiness and spice, which enhances the flavor of ros wines.
           </li>
           <li>
            We had a Morbier from France on hand, which held up just find with the spritzer. If youre going to enjoy a ros on its own, try a tangy and earthy Zamorano or Majorero sheeps cheese. You could also try one of the beautiful Cabrales blue cheeses.
           </li>
           <li>
            Crusty Breads.I prefer the sourdough variety.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           If you want to pair this spritzer with an herb-crusted roast chicken and a light salad, no one would be mad.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="W&amp;D for Apothic Wine_30" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16015" src="http://witanddelight.com/content/uploads//2014/06/WD-for-Apothic-Wine_30.jpg"/>
          </p>
