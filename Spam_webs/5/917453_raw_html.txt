          <h3>
           <div class="altblock">
           </div>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Delta_Kite">
            Method 2 of 4: Delta Kite
           </span>
          </h3>
          <div class="section_text" id="steps_2">
           <ol class="steps_list_2">
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              1
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Get a hold of a large plastic bag.
              </b>
              A big garbage bag or a garden bag is ideal. A plastic grocery bag won't do your kite any favors and won't be big enough to reap any real high-flying reward. Go for the bigger guns.
              <ul>
               <li>
                Lay it on a flat surface. You'll need a clean, flat surface to work on to make sure your kite is built as stream-lined as possible.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              2
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Work from the bottom seam.
              </b>
              You'll be drawing
              <i>
               half
              </i>
              of your kite, so when you open the bag, it doubles in size. Starting near the top of the bottom seam, draw two sides of a triangle (the third side is the edge). When you have all the dots in place, flip it over and trace on the other side.
              <ul>
               <li>
                The seam should be 11 1/2" (29 cm) long.
               </li>
               <li>
                The bottom line should be 10 3/4" (27 cm) long and should end 1 1/2" (3.75 cm)
                <i>
                 higher
                </i>
                than where it started.
               </li>
               <li>
                The top line should connect from the top of the seam to the end of the bottom line. Draw a line -- this will be referred to as the "fold line."
                <b>
                 Then
                </b>
                add a 2" wide (5 cm) flap that begins 2 1/2" past the starting point of the top line and extends all the way to the bottom. Two of your edges will now be small squares instead of points.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              3
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Draw your keel separately.
              </b>
              This is a small triangle that will control the flight of your kite. It should be 5 3/4" (14.5 cm) tall and 4 1/2" (11.6 cm) wide. Draw those two lines starting at a common point and connect the two with a third line. Cut it out with scissors.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              4
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Adhere tape to the outline of the kite except for the length of the 2" flaps.
              </b>
              Clear as mud? Place tape on the edges of the kite (only 1/2 of it or so, to minimize weight) but
              <i>
               not
              </i>
              the flaps that start 2 1/2" from the top and extend to the bottom.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              5
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Place your skewers.
              </b>
              Long, wooden, BBQ grill skewers should do the job. The straightest one you can find should be placed down the middle. Cut it to length and snip off the pointy end. It should go from the highest point to the bottom.
              <ul>
               <li>
                You'll also need skewers on the edges. Place one on each side on the fold line that stops with the edge of the flap (2 1/2" from the top). Ideally, these are just as straight as the center skewer.
               </li>
               <li>
                4 3/4" (12.2 cm) down from the top of the center skewer, place another skewer perpendicular, forming a cross. Cut it to length, with the ends resting on the skewers on the left and right. This piece is known as the spread and gives your kite structure. Apply glue to both ends where it meets the side skewers.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              6
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Move onto your keel.
              </b>
              Grab some flying line and tape down 2 lengths onto it on
              <b>
               both
              </b>
              sides (sides mirroring each other). Wrap a bit of tape around the corners to make them strong.
              <ul>
               <li>
                Where the four strings meet, tie a knot. Then, towards the end of the string, tie another knot. Tie the loose ends of the strings at their respective corners into knots, too, flush against the plastic.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              7
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Go back to your kite.
              </b>
              The glue is dry? Great. Fold over those pesky sail tabs over the left and right skewers and adhere with tape. Then fold over the tape you previously applied onto the ends of your skewer cross.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              8
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Attach your keel.
              </b>
              Poke a hole through the plastic right where the two center skewers intersect. The keel should match the angle the center skewers form; thread the upper keel lines through the hole and secure with a knot.
              <ul>
               <li>
                Do the same for the edge of the keel. Poke another hole for where the keel ends and thread the line through, again securing with a knot. Now that the keel is lined up properly, attach tape to secure it to the plastic.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              9
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Attach a line and a tail.
              </b>
              You know that extra string you have hanging from your keel, the one with two knots? That's what you'll be attaching your line to -- that's how the keel controls the flight of your kite. Because of this, make sure your keel isn't coming to come off or adjust!
              <ul>
               <li>
                As for a tail, simply slide a length of plastic (or ribbon) between the center skewer and the plastic and loop around. The tail is more for looks than anything. Don't wrap it too tightly and you can always remove it later if you don't like it.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>

          <h3>
           <div class="altblock">
           </div>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Bumble_Bee_Paper_Kite_.28Schaefer.29">
            Method 3 of 4: Bumble Bee Paper Kite (Schaefer)
           </span>
          </h3>
          <div class="section_text" id="steps_3">
           <ol class="steps_list_2">
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              1
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Use a regular sheet of printer paper.
              </b>
              It should be 8 1/2 x 11 inches. Decorate it with pencils or markers.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              2
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Fold it in half so the short ends meet up with each other.
              </b>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              3
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Measure 2 1/2" (6.
              </b>
              <b>
               25 cm) from one edge.
              </b>
              Place a mark for later reference. Then, place a second mark 5" (12.5 cm) from the edge, also along the bottom of the paper.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              4
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Fold down one edge to your first mark, but don't crease it.
              </b>
              You'll need the curve to maintain flight. Grab the other side of the paper and fold it down as well (still without creasing). Then, staple the edges to the center of the paper.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              5
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Punch a hole through your second mark.

          <h3>
           <div class="altblock">
           </div>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Eddy_Kite">
            Method 4 of 4: Eddy Kite
           </span>
          </h3>
          <div class="section_text" id="steps_4">
           <ol class="steps_list_2">
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              1
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Cut the sail from a thin plastic garbage bag.
              </b>
              Fold the bag in half then cut. That will ensure that both sides are equal.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              2
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Attach a 1/8 dowel (the vertical spine) with strapping tape by folding the tape over equally on front and back.
              </b>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              3
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Attach a 1/8 dowel (the horizontal spreader) with tape in the same manner.
              </b>
              The sticks should cross at the hole in the sail.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              4
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Put four pieces of tape on the sticks near the center hole as reinforcements.
              </b>
              That's the back of the kite.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              5
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Bow the spreader by putting a tension line on the back of the kite.
              </b>
              Tape it to one side of the spreader, pull it into a 2" bow, then tape it to the other side of the spreader.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="step_num">
              6
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Tie a tail to the bottom of the spine.
              </b>
              (6 foot long and 1 inch wide strip from a garbage bag)
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li class="final_li">
             <div class="step_num">
              7
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Attach the flying line through the hole in the front where the sticks cross.
              </b>
              The sticks should be on the back. Tie a knot.
             </div>

         <div class="section tips sticky ">
          <h2>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Tips">
            Tips
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="section_text" id="tips">
           <ul>
            <li>
             The longer the string, the better. But remember to wind it up properly!
            </li>
            <li>
             Size A4 paper is a bit too small to use for the first method. If you have larger, use it.
            </li>
            <li>
             Write some inspirational words on it. When people see it in the air, you'll brighten their day!
            </li>
            <li>
             You can color your kite with pencil or markers. Write your name on it in case it blows away!
            </li>
            <li>
             It is best to fly a kite at the park or on the beach where there is lots of space.
            </li>
            <li>
             Make the kite the right size according to the person you make it for. Children will need child-sized kites.
            </li>
            <li>
             A good way to decorate a kite is to use different style tapes. Try zebra and cheetah print.
            </li>
           </ul>

          <h2>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Warnings">
            Warnings
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="section_text" id="warnings">
           <ul>
            <li>
             Paper kites tear easily so make sure the wind is gentle and light; do not attempt to fly it in medium or high winds.
            </li>
            <li>
             Keep kites away from storms, roads and power lines. And listen to everything that Louie the Lightning Bug has to say when it comes to electricity.
            </li>
            <li>
             Do not color with pen or marker unless you are sure that these will not create holes or bleed through to the other side and weaken the paper.
            </li>
