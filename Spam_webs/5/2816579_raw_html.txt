       <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
        Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial
       </h1>
       <!-- Go to www.addthis.com/dashboard to customize your tools -->
       <div class="addthis_sharing_toolbox">
       </div>
       <p class="entry-meta">
        <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-06-04T06:00:44+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
         June 4, 2013
        </time>
        by
        <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
         <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://mellysews.com/author/mellysews" itemprop="url" rel="author">
          <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
           Melissa Mora
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="entry-comments-link">

      <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial by Melly Sews with free pattern" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2507" height="700" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-17.jpg" width="467"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Hello lovely readers  today Im sharing a sundress tutorial  the first of 4 I wrote for you this month.
       </p>
       <p>
        I made this dress from jersey bedsheets from Target. That was also my fabric source for
        <a href="http://mellysews.com/2012/09/ruched-ombre-dress-tutorial.html">
         this dress
        </a>
        , but I wasnt thrilled with that jersey. Its just not that soft (not like the fab stuff I get from sponsor
        <a href="http://bit.ly/VNO4yz" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Near Sea Naturals
        </a>
        , which youll get to see more of next week). But the striped jersey sheets are very soft, and so far this dress is wearing very well  its now been to Salt Lake City for SNAP and Los Angeles for Fabric Weekend 2013. Bonus: I only used the fitted sheet, with the elastic cut out, of my full-size sheet set to make this dress, which means I have a whole flat sheet and pillowcase left to use!
       </p>
       <p>
        So, want to make your own? Youll need
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         3 yards of knit fabric, or a full size jersey bedsheet
        </li>
        <li>
         1/2 elastic  6 inches less than your under bust measurement
        </li>
        <li>
         1/2 ribbon  1 yard
        </li>
        <li>
         To download the pattern, subscribe to the
         <a href="http://mad.ly/signups/86876/join" target="_blank">
          Melly Sews Weekly Newsletter.
         </a>
         AFTER you sign up, youll get a confirmation email  and you have to confirm or my email service cant send you the free pattern. But once you do confirm, youll get access to not just this but several other free patterns. Woot!
         <a href="http://mellysews.com/2014/09/make-sewing-pattern-bigger-smaller.html" style="line-height: 1.5;" target="_blank">
          If you need to make the pattern bigger or smaller, check out this post
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          .
         </span>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        (
        <i>
         PS  if you have trouble finding either your confirmation email or the email with the pattern link, try checking your spam, trash, junk, bulk, social and/or promotional email tabs or folders. Free pattern links are also included in every weekly newsletter; these go out on Friday afternoons. Due to the number of subscribers I cant email pattern links to individuals.)
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        If you need some tips and guidance about sewing with knits,
        <a href="http://mellysews.com/2012/10/tips-for-sewing-with-knits.html" target="_blank">
         read this post
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        To start, you need to cut out the bodice pieces as instructed by the pattern. For the skirt, you need to cut out two pieces on the fold with these measurements (keep in mind, Im only 53, so you may want to add length to this skirt).
       </p>
       <p>
        1/2 (12mm) seams are allowed on this dress.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial skirt" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2510" height="290" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/maxiskirtdiagram.jpg" width="434"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Place your bodice fronts on your bodice back, right sides together. Sew across shoulders. Repeat with other bodice pieces.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial - step 1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2509" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Lay your two bodices right sides together and pin along arm holes and neckline. Stitch. Turn this right side out.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial neck seam" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2494" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-2.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Match the front side seams to the back side seams on each piece. On top in the picture is the outside of the dress, below is the lining. Stitch on both sides (red line) and turn right side out again. Side seams should be enclosed and center front still open.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial side seams" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2495" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-3.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Now match the fronts to each other along the center front seam, matching at the neckline, in the same way you did the side seams. Stitch.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial center front" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2496" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-4.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Now the bodice is fully lined with all raw edges inside.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial bodice" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2497" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-5.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Stitch the skirt side seams.
       </p>
       <p>
        Place the bodice right sides together over the skirt, matching the side seams. Stitch around the waistline USING A 3/4 SEAM.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial waist" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2498" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-6.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Press the waist seam open. Trim the seam allowance from the bodice down to 1/4.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial seam allowances" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2499" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-7.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        With the seam still pressed open, make 2 1/2 buttonholes on the right side of the dress, on each side of the center front seam and above the waist seam.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial buttonholes" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2500" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-8.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Flip the seam allowance for the skirt up, and stitch along the raw edge to form the casing for the elastic.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial casing" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2501" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-9.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Cut your ribbon in half. Sew one piece to each end of your elastic to make a drawstring.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial drawstring" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2502" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-10.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Using a safety pin, thread your elastic through the casing. Burn, tie, or use fray check on your ribbon ends to seal them.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial - elastic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2503" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-11.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        If desired, sew buttons along center front seam for decoration. Hem dress. Youre done!
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial - made from sheets" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2505" height="700" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-14.jpg" width="467"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial - close up" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2504" height="467" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-13.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Striped Maxi Sundress Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2508" height="700" src="//mellysews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/striped-maxi-dress-18.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
