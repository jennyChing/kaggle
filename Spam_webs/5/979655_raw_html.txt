                     <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-U_jI9H9Kaw0/VSwKdHlekRI/AAAAAAAAGBc/BuXg5OMve0U/s1600/Police%2Bline%2Bdo%2Bnot%2Bcross.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="240" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-U_jI9H9Kaw0/VSwKdHlekRI/AAAAAAAAGBc/BuXg5OMve0U/s1600/Police%2Bline%2Bdo%2Bnot%2Bcross.jpg" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        Chicago's police superintendent, Garry McCarthy was recently quoted during a radio interview saying that he will instruct his troops to shoot concealed carry holders ...
                        <a href="http://bearingarms.com/chicago-top-cop-warns-that-his-officers-will-shoot-concealed-carriers/" target="_blank">
                         You put more guns on the street expect more shootings  I dont care if theyre licensed legal firearms, people who are not highly trained  putting guns in their hands is a recipe for disaster. Ill train our officers that there is a concealed carry law, but when somebody turns with a firearm in their hand the officer does not have an obligation to wait to get shot to return fire and were going to have tragedies as a result of that Im telling you right up front.
                        </a>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       I guess I'm fortunate I do not live in Chicago, because I have
                       <i>
                        so
                       </i>
                       many issues with this statement that are just chilling to the bone. First, how do you know concealed carriers are not as responsible as your police officers? What puts them above everybody else, especially lately with all the negative news media surrounding officers shooting unarmed, or 'innocent', individuals all over this country. I haven't heard one story about a concealed carrier shooting someone unwarranted. It's also known that in at least
                       <a href="http://uhaweb.hartford.edu/kdowst/competen.html" target="_blank">
                        one study from Hartford
                       </a>
                       showing that police officers are five times more likely to shoot the wrong person than concealed carriers. Huh, how interesting? But we're not as 'responsible' as your police officers?
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        Secondly, who are you to say that I am not as well
trained as your officers, Mr. Superintendent? What do you know about me or any
other concealed carry holder? You dont know anything about someones training,
background, history, etc. Most of the concealed carriers Ive met spend hours and hours on the range every month because they respect their craft, or they are former law enforcement, correctional officers or veterans, our nation's hero's, who have at minimum equal or in some cases better training than your officers who only requalify.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        I know from first-hand experience (having gone through the
Sacramento County Sheriffs Academy) that officer trainees spend 90% of their
time studying laws and regulations, city ordinances, arrest procedures, radio codes,
and policies and procedures while spending only about 10% of
their time on the range. And once they hit the streets, most police officers only go to the range
twice a year for their re-qualification which takes them about one hour to
complete. So, how does that two hours a year add up to more training then the
8-10 hours I spend on the range a month, not counting all my past experience and
training? It doesn't.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        Furthermore, it is incredibly frustrating that McCarthy doesn't believe his officers have a responsibility to assess a situation
before shooting; that just because someone has a gun in their hand it
automatically makes them a threat or a 'bad guy'?
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        I guess his officers plan to execute a lot of off-duty Police, Corrections and armed Security Guards who took their firearms out in an attempt to stop a threat but didn't drop the weapon quick enough before a Chicago's finest got on scene.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        <span style="font-size: small;">
                         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Dd7_xYjl7nE/VS0-6TBnVNI/AAAAAAAAAPs/8cVxWGybIeU/s1600/CCW3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="180" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Dd7_xYjl7nE/VS0-6TBnVNI/AAAAAAAAAPs/8cVxWGybIeU/s1600/CCW3.jpg" width="320"/>
                         </a>
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        Heck, can you imagine if a concealed carrier is holding a robber at gun point and the police responded using common sense, instead of outright fear for every single citizen? It's a remarkable thought but one Salt Lake City's finest understand, see the video
                        <a href="http://fox13now.com/2015/03/23/citizen-holds-man-at-gunpoint-after-seeing-him-swing-knife-in-fight/" target="_blank">
                         here
                        </a>
                        .
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        Sadly we as citizens apparently can't get common sense or even respect from those like McCarthy. Instead we're just peons in his way. As a mother, a veteran, and someone who refuses to be victimized, I carry a gun, every single day. I also follow the law and I thank GOD I live in an area like
                        <a href="http://www.azcentral.com/story/news/12-news/2015/04/08/12-news-cottonwood-citizens-honored/25487427/" target="_blank">
                         Arizona where Law Enforcement works with the people
                        </a>
                        because if I lived in Chicago, it doesn't appear to matter whether I follow the law, whether I'm attempting to protect my life or my children's, it doesn't matter that Chicago PD is completely incapable of doing their job, all that matters is the potential fear a cop might feel. If he feels fearful enough its then deemed "ok" for him to gun me down in front of my children.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-MtQD-Ih6_ew/VSwNL5r7UFI/AAAAAAAAGBo/9PaDBGF-0E8/s1600/cute%2Bpic%2Bof%2Bme%2Band%2Bthe%2Bgirls.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="240" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-MtQD-Ih6_ew/VSwNL5r7UFI/AAAAAAAAGBo/9PaDBGF-0E8/s1600/cute%2Bpic%2Bof%2Bme%2Band%2Bthe%2Bgirls.jpg" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        In closing, I want to ask, is having more law-abiding citizens with proper
training and background checks really going to add to more deaths, more victims
or more disasters? Because the more than 250 murders already this year in
your so called gun-free city is really working for you, huh? I think McCarthy
should be cracking down on all those illegal guns murdering his citizens instead of picking on concealed carriers, who follow the law. I tend to
believe that if half of those victims had a legal means to defend themselves then
maybe their families would be attending their graduations or weddings instead of their funerals.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        I just cant believe that a police superintendent could be
so naive and so ignorant to believe that only his officers are trained enough
to handle a firearm. Do you not have any faith in your federal government for
training soldiers with their firearms? What about other police departments or
law enforcement entities; are you really that ignorant Mr. Superintendent to
believe that nobody except for
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         your
                        </i>
                        department has the capability of effectively training individuals with a
firearm? Come on, thats not even logical.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        <span style="font-size: small;">
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        In my opinion, this is just another attack on concealed
carriers who are simply exercising their right to NOT be a victim, as well as
their constitutional right to bear arms. Its a scary thought that I could be
shot on sight just because Im carrying a firearm; that my children will be
without a mother just because I chose to defend them with the same deadly force
that some terrorist would not hesitate to use against me. I will take any means
necessary to protect my children, as any mother should, and I shouldnt have to
live in fear of being shot by the police because I chose to do so.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        Don't get me wrong, I'm not attacking our men and women in blue, I have utmost respect for what they do and it's why I hope that they have enough common sense to not take their top cop's words to heart and value, and not fear concealed carriers. At the end of the day, I guess all I can say is Im glad I dont live in Chicago and I pray for those officers to make smart, educated decisions. Oddly enough most concealed carriers stand with law enforcement, we want them to make it home to their families and for the bad guys to lose. Now only if their leaders would extend that mutual respect.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
