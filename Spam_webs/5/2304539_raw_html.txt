     <p>
      <b>
       Skull and crossbones
      </b>
      is a symbol of a human skull with two long bones crossed below it. It is used in several contexts:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Skull_and_crossbones_(symbol)" title="Skull and crossbones (symbol)">
        Skull and crossbones (symbol)
       </a>
       , as a symbol of death and especially as a memento mori on tombstones.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skull_and_crossbones_(poison)" title="Skull and crossbones (poison)">
        Skull and crossbones (poison)
       </a>
       , a warning symbol of poisonous substances and danger
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Skull_and_crossbones_(military)" title="Skull and crossbones (military)">
        Skull and crossbones (military)
       </a>
       , in variations used by several military forces
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Skull_and_crossbones_(fraternities_and_sports)" title="Skull and crossbones (fraternities and sports)">
        Skull and crossbones (fraternities and sports)
       </a>
       , used also by sororities and secret societies
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Skull_and_crossbones_(Spanish_cemetery)" title="Skull and crossbones (Spanish cemetery)">
        Skull and crossbones (Spanish cemetery)
       </a>
       "
       <i>
        campo santo
       </i>
       ", used to mark the entrances to cemeteries
      </li>
      <li>
       Unicode represents the symbol "" in its
       <a href="/wiki/Miscellaneous_Symbols" title="Miscellaneous Symbols">
        Miscellaneous Symbols
       </a>
       block.
      </li>
      <li>
       Skull and crossbones are sometimes shown on a
       <a href="/wiki/Crucifix" title="Crucifix">
        crucifix
       </a>
       , referring to Golgotha or Adam's skull.
      </li>
     </ul>
