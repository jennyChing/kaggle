       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Simply everything you need to know about Japanese whisky. In 11 peg-size points.
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-12-06T10:30:27+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          December 6, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://mannersandmischief.com/author/anubha76/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            anubha76
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://mannersandmischief.com/japanese-whisky-guide/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Whisky connoisseurs are still recovering from the shock of having their beloved Scotch edged out of the top rung by a relative stranger  the Japanese single malt. The
         <a href="http://time.com/3555773/worlds-best-whisky-yamazaki-single-malt-sherry-cask-2013-suntory-jim-murrays-whisky-bible-2015/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://time.com/3555773/worlds-best-whisky-yamazaki-single-malt-sherry-cask-2013-suntory-jim-murrays-whisky-bible-2015/', 'Yamazaki Single Malt Sherry Cask 2013']);" target="_blank">
          Yamazaki Single Malt Sherry Cask 2013
         </a>
         was given the
         <em>
          Worlds Best
         </em>
         titleby the iconic
         <a href="http://www.whiskybible.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.whiskybible.com', 'Jim MurraysWhisky Bible 2015']);" target="_blank">
          Jim Murrays
          <em>
           Whisky Bible 2015
          </em>
         </a>
         . Murray praised the Japanese malt as near indescribable genius, with a nose of exquisite boldness and a finish of light, teasing spice, giving it an astounding97.5 out of 100.
        </p>
        <p>
         To add further insult to injury, not a single Scotch managed to make the final five shortlist.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, what is it that the rest of us have been missing about Japanese whisky? Lets find out, in 11 peg-size points.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_197" style="width: 690px">
         <a href="http://mannersandmischief.com/japanese-whisky-guide/yamazaki-single-malt-sherry-cask-2013/" rel="attachment wp-att-197">
          <img alt="Collection of Suntory single malts, including the award winning Yamazaki Single Malt Sherry Cask 2013" class="size-full wp-image-197" height="292" src="http://mannersandmischief.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Yamazaki-Single-Malt-Sherry-Cask-2013.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Collection of Suntory single malts, including the award winning Yamazaki Single Malt Sherry Cask 2013
         </p>
        </div>
        <h3>
         1. Japanese whisky isnot exactly the new kid on the block
        </h3>
        <p>
         Whisky has been commercially produced in Japansincethe early 1920s, afterMasataka Taketsuru, a young chemist, studied the art inScotland. On returning, he set upthe
         <a href="http://theyamazaki.jp/en/index.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://theyamazaki.jp/en/index.html', 'Yamazaki distillery']);" target="_blank">
          Yamazaki distillery
         </a>
         , in partnership with Torii Shinjiro, now the owner of
         <a href="http://www.suntory.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.suntory.com', 'Suntory']);" target="_blank">
          Suntory
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Bonus fact: RecentlySuntory announced a $16 billion takeover of
         <a href="http://www.beamsuntory.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.beamsuntory.com', 'Beam Inc.,']);" target="_blank">
          Beam Inc.,
         </a>
         the maker of Laphroaig and Ardmore Scotches among others. This makes Suntory theworlds third largest spirits-maker afterDiageo and Pernod.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2.  and its the worlds third largest producer now
        </h3>
        <p>
         Japan is the worlds third largest producer of whisky behind the Scots and the Americans  yes, they beat the Irish!
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. TheMissing E
        </h3>
        <p>
         SinceJapanese whisky has morein common with Scottish ones,rather than Irish or American, its name follows the Scotch tradition and is spelled without an e.
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Getting the grain
        </h3>
        <p>
         Barley is notindigenous to Japan, so the grainstill tends to be imported from Scotland.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_198" style="width: 690px">
         <a href="http://mannersandmischief.com/japanese-whisky-guide/japanese-whisky-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-198">
          <img alt="Photo: Norio NAKAYAMA/Flickr" class="size-full wp-image-198" height="453" src="http://mannersandmischief.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/japanese-whisky-2.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo: Norio NAKAYAMA/
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/norio-nakayama/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/norio-nakayama/', 'Flickr']);" target="_blank">
           Flickr
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <h3>
         5. The age factor
        </h3>
        <p>
         Though the Japanese whisky makers use manytypes of barrel forageing  includingFrench oak andbourbon several distilleries employ the nativeMizunara oak, which imparts almost ecclesiasticalaromas of temple incense and sandalwood. Plus,Mizunara-matured whiskies losean exceptional amount of water through evaporation, making for a much more condensed end product.
        </p>
        <h3>
         6. Nature steps in
        </h3>
        <p>
         Japans extreme climate speeds up the maturation, making the whisky seemolder than its actual age. Plus, the
         <a href="http://www.whiskypapa.com/what-is-whisky-terroir-made-of" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.whiskypapa.com/what-is-whisky-terroir-made-of', 'mineral water used for making these whiskies']);" target="_blank">
          mineral water used for making these whiskies
         </a>
         is treasured enough to be bottled and sold on its own. All thishas a distinctive effect on thewhisky.
        </p>
        <h3>
         7.The flavour profile
        </h3>
        <p>
         Like with any and every facet of their lives, the Japanese prizeharmony and balance in their whiskies as well, paying more attention to thebody and texturethan other countries. The top rung ones are usually floral,more rounded and have asofter, silkier texture than those from Scotland, while being equally complex. Suavely bold rather than jaw-grabbingly aggressive is how one would sum them up.
        </p>
        <h3>
         8. Breaking the rules
        </h3>
        <p>
         Unlike Scotch,Japanese whisky is not regulated in the USA, so producers have morefreedom to experiment with flavors and techniques.For example, theScots cannot use acombination of different types of stills and casks but its very usual in Japan. Even the ageing process can run through different iterations: theYoichi distillery uses coal fires (instead of steam) to heat the pot stills, creating a richer, peatier flavour.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_196" style="width: 690px">
         <a href="http://mannersandmischief.com/japanese-whisky-guide/japanese-whisky/" rel="attachment wp-att-196">
          <img alt="Photo: Norio NAKAYAMA/Flickr" class="size-full wp-image-196" height="453" src="http://mannersandmischief.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/japanese-whisky.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo: Norio NAKAYAMA/
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/norio-nakayama/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/norio-nakayama/', 'Flickr']);" target="_blank">
           Flickr
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <h3>
         9. Mix-n-Match, the Japanese way
        </h3>
        <p>
         In Japan, there is no trading of unfinished whiskies between distillers.Instead, each distillery createsevery conceivable style of whisky by itself, providing themaster blender with a wide range of minutely calibrated in-house variations. This makesJapanese blended whiskies extremely specific to each house: individualized, idiosyncratic, sometimes even offbeat.
        </p>
        <h3>
         10. Drinking it, the Japanese way
        </h3>
        <p>
         Though technically speaking, you can drink a Japanese whisky any which way  neat, on the rocks, with a splash or in a cocktail  the most authentic is
         <a href="http://www.japanese-whisky.com/about-whisky/discovery/how-would-you-like-your-whisky" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.japanese-whisky.com/about-whisky/discovery/how-would-you-like-your-whisky', 'mizuwari']);" target="_blank">
          <em>
           mizuwari
          </em>
         </a>
         , which translates asmixed with water. To make it right, fill your glass with ice and add one part of whisky to twopartsof mineral water. Younger generations of Japanese are now also leaning towards thehighball  aversion of
         <em>
          mizuwari
         </em>
         where whisky is mixed with sparkling water.
        </p>
        <h3>
         11. Taking the world stage
        </h3>
        <p>
         TheYamazaki Single Malt Sherry Cask 2013isnt the first whisky to shine on the world stage:Nikkas Yoichi whisky was named
         <em>
          Best of the Best
         </em>
         by
         <em>
          Whisky Magazine
         </em>
         in 2001
         <em>
          .
         </em>
         Suntorys 30-year-old Hibiki
         <em>
         </em>
         won the top award at the International Spirits Challengein 2003, after whichSuntory went on to earn awards at the competition for the next 11 years.
        </p>
