            <img alt="how to root your android phone" class="align-right" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/superuser2.jpg?9e15ff"/>
            Since I first bought my Motorola Droid over a year ago, Ive done everything with it. Ive gone on GPS-based
            <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/summer-fun-geocaching/">
             geo-caching adventures
            </a>
            , Ive transformed my phone into a
            <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/android-media-center-remote-control/">
             media center remote control
            </a>
            , and Ive installed some of the coolest surveillance apps available. However, the one single thing that I could not let myself do is root my Android phone.
           </firstimage>
          </p>
          <p>
           The fear of bricking my expensive mobile device was too much, even for a guy that prides himself on his moderate hacking abilities. What convinced me to take the plunge and learn how to root my Android phone? Well, the first thing was seeing how easily my iPhone-toting MUO colleagues could
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/jailbreak-iphone-slide/">
            jailbreak their phones
           </a>
           . The second thing that convinced me was my need to take screenshots on the go, not only when Im attached to my PC via a USB cable. The final thing was seeing how many cool apps on the Android Market require root.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-67307">
           </span>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Root Options Available To Android Users
          </h2>
          <p>
           In the early days of Android ownership, the process of rooting your brand-spanking-new and expensive Android device involved a fairly complicated 10 to 15 step process that involved the very real and present danger of bricking your Android phone and making it a worthless block of metal and glass.
          </p>
          <p>
           If youre in the same boat and youve been fearfully avoiding the complicated rooting process like the plague, Ive got some good news for you. There are now a fair number of one-click applications out there that will root your phone for you. These apps work on the large majority of Android devices. Dont get me wrong  the danger of bricking is always there, but if you check to see that the app has been tested with your phone and version of Android, you should be safe enough.
          </p>
          <p>
           Just to give you some options to look at, some of the top one-click root apps for Android include
           <a href="http://androidspin.com/2010/08/10/universal-1-click-root-app-for-android-devices/">
            Universal Androot
           </a>
           , the
           <a href="http://www.ryebrye.com/blog/2009/08/16/android-rooting-in-1-click-in-progress/">
            Recovery Flasher
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.androidtapp.com/one-click-android-root-with-easy-root/">
            Easy Root
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://phandroid.com/2010/09/07/unrevoked-3-2-provides-1-click-root-for-2-2-sense-devices/">
            Unrevoked
           </a>
           , and last but certainly not least 
           <a href="http://forum.xda-developers.com/showthread.php?t=803682">
            Super One-Click
           </a>
           from XDA Developers. SuperOneClick is the app that I went with, and I believe its the app that will work for the majority of people reading this. But before you get started, dont forget to enable USB debugging on your phone by going to
           <em>
            Settings-&gt;Applications-&gt;Development
           </em>
           and enabling 
           <em>
            USB Debugging
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="how to root your android phone" class="aligncenter" height="450" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/super42.jpg?9e15ff" width="253"/>
          </p>

          <p>
           Also, SDK is required. Just follow my instructions in the
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/how-to-capture-screenshots-with-your-android-mobile-phone/">
            taking Screenshots article
           </a>
           that I wrote a while back to install the SDK developers kit. Nothing else below will work until this is done. Take the time to do it, because itll also open up a lot of cool features like
           <em>
            high-resolution
           </em>
           screenshots, file exploring and easy file transfers. Once youve set it up, youre ready to roll.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="android root access" class="aligncenter" height="398" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/super12.jpg?9e15ff" width="576"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Download Super One Click, make sure you have the Android debugger (SDK developer kit) running, expand the Super One Click zip file and click the SuperOneClick icon to run it. When SuperOneClick launches, youll see several large buttons at the top of the screen. Make sure your phone is connected to the computer via USB and that the debugger can see it (activity will start logging). Make sure your SD card is
           <em>
            not
           </em>
           mounted, and go ahead and click Root in SuperOneClick.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="android root access" class="aligncenter" height="372" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/super21.jpg?9e15ff" width="565"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Youll see the scrolling activity as SuperOneClick does its job, with OK appearing after each task. You may see warnings, but so long as it doesnt hang on waiting for device, things should keep scrolling until it finally returns the Success! message.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="android root access" class="aligncenter" height="477" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/super31.jpg?9e15ff" width="425"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           At this point, check out the installed apps on your phone and you should see a Superuser icon that looks like a Droid skull and crossbones. If you see it, youre rooted. If you dont see it, reboot the Droid. In fact, Id suggest rebooting even if you do see it, as I had some issues getting rooted apps to work until I actually rebooted my own device.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="how to root android" class="aligncenter" height="450" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/super51.jpg?9e15ff" width="253"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           When you open up SuperUser, youll see that you can configure notifications so that no app can actually run with root access unless you give it permission. Also, according to the folks at Nexus One Forum, SuperOneClick doesnt actually unlock the Bootloader, so you still retain your warranty. I havent confirmed if thats true or not, but if true its a pretty good plus to using SuperOneClick with the SDK approach.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="how to root android" class="aligncenter" height="450" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/super61.jpg?9e15ff" width="253"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Whenever you run an app that requires root (superuser), youll see a notification requesting permission. I like this because it also means that if an app gets installed and tries to utilize superuser access without your knowledge, it cant.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="how to root android" class="aligncenter" height="450" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/super71.jpg?9e15ff" width="253"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you check off Remember, the program gets added to the list of approved apps with superuser access. You can see the ones on your list when you click on the Apps tab.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="how to root your android phone" class="aligncenter" height="450" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/super81.jpg?9e15ff" width="253"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           As you can see, once you have SDK installed and connected to your phone, the process is as simple as installing and running SuperOneClick. The program takes care of rooting your phone from the PC. Finally, you can enjoy a rooted Android phone, and all of the fun and excitement that offers!
          </p>
          <p>
           Did you give any of the one-click Android root apps a try? How was your experience? Share your rooting adventures in the comments section below.
          </p>
