       <h2 class="hSubTitle">
        Discovered: This Padlock Has One Technology That No Other Padlock Has... But It'll Surprise You
       </h2>
       <div class="author clearfix">
        <div class="authorInfo">
         <img alt="" src="http://images.askmen.com/authors/responsive/AmosKwon.png" width="32"/>

       Certain basic necessities in life haven't advanced much over the decades. Toilet paper, clothing irons and mailboxes come to mind. It seems no one's put much thought into how to make them better  not that there's a ton of room for improvement. Then there's the padlock. Securing your toolshed,
       <a href="http://www.askmen.com/money/how_to_200/245_how_to.html">
        bike
       </a>
       or locker with a key or combination padlock has always been a bit of an exercise in frustration. We can't count the number of times we've had to toss out a perfectly good padlock because we lost the combination or the key. Let's not even talk about the occasions where we had to employ the use of bolt cutters on a lock we couldn't get open (calm down  only used on locks we owned). Well, all that nightmarish fussing and waste might just come to an end with the Noke ("no key") Bluetooth padlock.
       <p>
        <a href="https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/fuzdesigns/noke-the-worlds-first-bluetooth-padlock?ref=category_popular">
         FUZ Designs
        </a>
        has taken the basic padlock and brought it well into the 21st century by making it a smart electronic locking device rather than just a singular lock that depends on how well your memory functions (or doesn't). The Noke is operated simply by depressing the shackle, thereby waking the Noke and automatically searching for your phone that uses the iOS- and Android-compatible app via Bluetooth 4.0. If your phone is within a 10-foot radius of the Noke, it will automatically unlock for you when the shackle is depressed. That's it. No muss, no retrying the combination 20 times before ripping your hair out and cursing the gods of antiquation.
       </p>
       <p>
        If the thought of automatic unlocking frightens the crap out of you, opt to disable that feature in the app. You can still open the Noke by using the Noke's Quick-Click code that you choose or by pressing the unlock button in the app. The app, thankfully, also does much more than that. You can give access to those privileged few (or many) who need to borrow your bike, pick up an item on your behalf, etc. You can choose different levels of access, too  one time, permanent or more custom settings. You can also track history of user access, as well as get notifications when the Noke is being unlocked. Is your buddy borrowing your titanium road bike way too often? Lock him out in an instant or just decide to start renting to him on an hourly basis. Noke tracks his usage so you know who's using your stuff and when.
       </p>
       <p>
        Of course, the Noke requires a power source, but fear not. It's a long-lasting battery that will hold up for about a year. When it needs replacing, your phone app will send you a notification (yes, you'll need to make sure your phone is charged to get that heads-up). But even if your Noke's juice is depleted, there's a nifty "jump start" feature you can employ by using an external battery. You'll never find yourself without options.
       </p>
       <p>
        It's also a device that gives you peace of mind via a hardened steel and boron construction, anti-shim technology that prevents prying and four silicone gaskets to keep the wet stuff out. No word on whether or not it'll hold up to a really big dude with a sledgehammer. What the Noke provides is a new level of convenience for padlock users, as well as a world of less frustration and the ability to share your stuff with those you trust.
        <a href="https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/fuzdesigns/noke-the-worlds-first-bluetooth-padlock?ref=category_popular" target="_blank">
         The Kickstarter campaign
        </a>
        is currently under way, and it's more than surpassed its goal of $100,000. The introductory price is $59, but will go up to $89 once the campaign is over.
       </p>
