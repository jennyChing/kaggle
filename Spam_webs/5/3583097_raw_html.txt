      <div class="story-content p402_premium clearfix">
       <section class="photo main_photo">
        <p>
         <img alt="Project X Megadrive games" class="photo kalooga_12952" src="http://images.dailystar.co.uk/dynamic/23/photos/136000/620x/Project-X-Megadrive-games-430748.jpg"/>
         <meta content="http://images.dailystar.co.uk/dynamic/23/photos/136000/620x/Project-X-Megadrive-games-430748.jpg" itemprop="image"/>
         <span class="caption">
          NEW: The 90's console is getting a new game [SEGA]
         </span>
        </p>
       </section>
       <section class="text-description">
        <p>
         The old console's glory days were in the 1990s, pumping out classics like Sonic the Hedgehog and Streets of Rage.
        </p>
        <p>
         But despite the fact Sega no longer make the machines, a new beat-em-up game is coming to cartridge.
        </p>
        <p>
         Project Y is a work-in-progress fight title and it even has a new gameplay trailer to prove it.
        </p>
        <p>
         The video shows off colourful characters, familiar combat and retro looks that fit perfectly with the Megadrive.
        </p>
        <p>
         The game has been in development for several years.
        </p>
        <p>
         But this is the first time we've seen it in action.
        </p>
       </section>
       <section class="photo">
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="photo kalooga_12952" src="http://images.dailystar.co.uk/dynamic/23/photos/137000/208137.jpg"/>
         <span class="caption">
          BACK: Project Y is a work-in-progress fight title [PH]
         </span>
        </p>
       </section>
       <section class="text-description pullquote-left">
        <blockquote>
         <p class="quote">
          The game borrows heavily from Streets of Rage and other old-school side-scrolling beat 'em ups
         </p>
         <cite>
          Choli Rad, of IGN.com
         </cite>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Choli Rad, of IGN.com, said: "The game borrows heavily from Streets of Rage and other old-school side-scrolling beat 'em ups, but is not, according to developer WaterMelon Games, a rip or a mod of the classics."
        </p>
        <p>
         Project Y isn't the only new game made specifically for the decades-old console.
        </p>
        <p>
         The studio also released Sacred Line Genesis, a "16-bit visual novel" horror about a detective.
        </p>
        <p>
         What's classified as "retro" isn't always the same everywhere in the world, though.
        </p>
        <p>
         In Brazil, the Megadrive never ceased production. A company called Tectoy is still manufacturing its own models of the Sega console today.
        </p>
        <p>
         WaterMelon says on its website: "The original intended release date has been missed but the game is still in steady development and will soon enter beta-testing phase.
        </p>
        <p>
         "A new release date will be communicated soon, along with opening pre-orders."
        </p>
       </section>
       <section class="photo">
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="photo kalooga_12952" src="http://images.dailystar.co.uk/dynamic/23/photos/143000/208143.jpg"/>
         <span class="caption">
          IT'S COMING:  new release date will be communicated soon, along with opening pre-orders [PH]
         </span>
        </p>
       </section>
