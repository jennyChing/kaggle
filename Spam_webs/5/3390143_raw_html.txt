      <h2>
       Why Boko Haram and ISIS Target Women
      </h2>
     </div>
     <div class="wrapper expandContent">
      <hr/>
      <div class="dateDiv">
       <span class="date">
        May20
       </span>
       2015
      </div>
      <div class="leftPanel alignLeft">
       <div class="deck">
        The oldest way to spread a religion is not to evangelize people; its to create new ones.
       </div>
       <div class="byline">
        <a href="/women/writers/katelyn-beaty.html">
         Katelyn Beaty
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="singlePage">
        <a href="?paging=off">
         Read as Single Page
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="singlePage languageToggle">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="rightPanel alignLeft" id="articlesWrapper">
       <div class="body" id="body">
        <p class="text">
         In all the battles waged by men, one of the most effective ways to destroy the enemy is to destroy its women. Rape and sexual abuse are wartime strategies as old as war itself.
        </p>
        <p class="text">
         Yesterday
         <em>
          The New York Times
         </em>
         ran a
         <a class="" href="http://www.nytimes.com/2015/05/19/world/africa/boko-haram-militants-raped-hundreds-of-female-captives-in-nigeria.html">
          devastating report
         </a>
         that hundreds of girls as young as 11 have been raped and impregnated by members of Boko Haram. Based in Nigeria, the radical Islamist sect has long proven its cowardice by targeting girls, including the 300 mostly Christian schoolgirls they kidnapped last year, inciting the
         <a class="" href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/BringBackOurGirls?src=hash">
          #BringBackOurGirls
         </a>
         campaign. For the young victims now carrying their abusers unborn children, returning to anything like normal life is impossible to imagine.
        </p>
        <p class="text">
         The same goes for those targeted by another radical Islamic group, this one spreading terror throughout the Middle East. Popular Christian writer Ann Voskamp recently
         <a class="" href="http://www.aholyexperience.com/2015/05/into-iraq-2-what-the-news-isnt-telling-you-why-we-cant-afford-to-pretend-its-not-happening-sozans-impossible-choice-and-our-very-possible-one/">
          visited
         </a>
         refugees in Northern Iraq who had fled ISIS, the self-declared Islamic State. Voskamp noticed 5- and 7-year old girls among the families, but no 9-year-olds. The reason, writes Voskamp: ISIS sells 9-year-old girls in slave bazaars They are categorized. Stripped. And shipped naked. Examined and distributed. Sold and passed around like meat. Livestock.
        </p>
        <p class="text">
         ISIS has institutionalized sexual violence and the brutalization of women as a central aspect of their ideology and operations, a UN representative confirmed last week. On the ground, females are
         <a class="" href="http://www.iraqinews.com/features/exclusive-isis-document-sets-prices-christian-yazidi-slaves/">
          sold
         </a>
         as sexual slaves for $43with the price going up to $172 for the youngest (ages 19). The UN reports that one girl was 
         <a class="" href="http://www.jpost.com/Middle-East/UN-official-on-life-under-ISIS-Girls-are-being-stripped-naked-examined-in-slave-bazaars-402611">
          married
         </a>
          more than 20 times, each time forced to undergo surgery to restore her virginity.
        </p>
        <p class="text">
         Why does war and terror target women in particular? The reasons are as numerous as they are horrid. Sometimes a group intends to infect the enemys women with disease in order to destabilize a community. One of the countless women raped during the Rwandan genocide recounts her abusers, Hutu men with HIV/AIDS,
         <a class="" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=3PLqetBxiOEC&amp;pg=PA28&amp;lpg=PA28&amp;dq=We+are+not+killing+you.+We+are+giving+you+something+worse.+You+will+die+a+slow+death%E2%80%9D.&amp;source=bl&amp;ots=PRS0xWbj1y&amp;sig=adP3tyfCkva0iq67EPpUBh0yf2o&amp;hl=en&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=upNbVaXLMovXsAWH6YHQBg#v=onepage&amp;q=We%20are%20not%20killing%20you.%20We%20are%20giving%20you%20something%20worse.%20You%20will%20die%20a%20slow%20death%E2%80%9D.&amp;f=false">
          saying
         </a>
         , We are not killing you. We are giving you something worse. You will die a slow death. Some women get sold as brides by their families to placate an insurgent group; others as sex slaves to fund a groups own military campaign. And throughout history, systematic rape has been used to wipe out an enemys numbers and increase ones own.
        </p>
        <p class="text">
         Such is the case with Boko Haram. As one Nigerian governor told
         <em>
          The New York Times
         </em>
         ,
        </p>
        <blockquote class="text">
         <p class="text">
          The sect leaders make a very conscious effort to impregnate the women. Some of them, I was told, even pray before mating, offering supplications for God to make the products of what they are doing become children that will inherit their ideology.
         </p>
        </blockquote>

				authDelegate = new fyre.conv.RemoteAuthDelegate();
			
			authDelegate.login = function(handlers) {
				// This is a fail-safe in case the user tries to log in and closes
				// the modal. Otherwise, the login modal would not be triggered
				// the second time the login buttons are clicked. Since the page
				// has to refresh for a user to be logged in, this shouldn't have
				// any negative consequences.
				handlers.failure();
				
				
					try {
						cti.janrain.login.package.modal.load($('&lt;a data-loginhash="comments"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;'));
					} catch(e) {
						try {
							cti.loginOptions = {
								displayOnLoad: true,
								redirectUrl: {
									hash: 'comments'
								}
							};
							cti.showLoginOnLoad = true;
							window.janrainWidgetOnload();
						} catch(e) {}
					}
				
			};
			
			authDelegate.logout = function(handlers) {
				var query = location.search;
				
				if (query.indexOf('logout=true') === -1) {
					query += (query ? '&amp;' : '?') + 'logout=true';
				}
				
				handlers.success();
				
				location.href = location.protocol + '//' + location.host +
						location.pathname + query + location.hash;
			};
			
			authDelegate.viewProfile = function() {
				return false;
			};
			
			authDelegate.editProfile = function() {
				var url = 'https://secure2.christianitytoday.com/myaccount/';
				
				location.href = url + '?page=profile';
			};
			
			fyre.conv.load(
				{
					
						network: "christianitytoday-prod-0.fyre.co",
					
					authDelegate: authDelegate,
					"strings": {
						"signIn" : "Log in",
						"signOut": "Log out",
						"flagEmailPlaceholder": "Your email address",
						"editProfile": "Edit my profile",
						"flagNotesPlaceholder": "Your thoughts..."
					}
				},
				streamConfig,
				function(widget) {
					if ($) {
						widget.on('initialRenderComplete', function() {
							setTimeout(function() {
								setViewAllComments();
								trimComments();
							}, 500);
						});
					}
					
					
				}
			);
		})(window.jQuery);
