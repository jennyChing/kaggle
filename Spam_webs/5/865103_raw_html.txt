        <p>
         <img alt="How to Pack up Cookies for Mailing! Find out how to PROS do it!" class="aligncenter wp-image-46483 size-large" data-pin-description="How To Package and Ship Cookies ~ http://iambaker.net" height="650" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/howtopackcookies-613x650.jpg" width="613"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         One question I get asked
         <em>
          all the time
         </em>
         is, How do you ship cookies?
        </p>
        <p>
         Most of the time I answer, Very carefully!
        </p>
        <p>
         Oddly, people fail to see the humor in that.
        </p>
        <p>
         But I think I am ready to share with you how I ship cookies,
         <em>
          most of the time
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         This works for me because I often do round or square cookies. If you are baking cookies with small apendages or delicate arms and legs, I highly recommend using
         <a href="http://bakeat350.blogspot.com/2010/07/how-to-package-cookies-for-shipping.html" target="_blank">
          Bridgets method
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         The reason I use this particular format is this;
        </p>
        <p>
         Once upon a time Brides Magazine emailed and asked if I would ship them some
         <a href="/facebooktwitter-cookies" target="_blank">
          facebook cookies
         </a>
         for their magazine.
        </p>
        <p>
         Um, yes. Yes I will.
        </p>
        <p>
         When I walked into UPS carrying the precious cookie cargo, I explained in great (and annoying) detail how each and every cookie
         <em>
          must
         </em>
         arrive in mint condition and there was no room for error.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is what they (the professionals) did. (interpreted by me)
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="IMG_0073.supplies" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551040fb78834014e5f753913970c" data-pin-description="How To Package and Ship Cookies ~ http://iambaker.net" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/6a00e551040fb78834014e5f753913970c-500wi.jpg" title="IMG_0073.supplies"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Supplies I use. (Not pictured: packing popcorn. My sweet post office guy always gives me some.)
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="IMG_0081.cookeis" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551040fb78834014e5f753bd7970c" data-pin-description="How To Package and Ship Cookies ~ http://iambaker.net" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/6a00e551040fb78834014e5f753bd7970c-500wi.jpg" title="IMG_0081.cookeis"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         First I started out by wrapping each cookie in parchment paper. I them placed it in their own bag. I often double up the cookies to save room and matericals.
        </p>
        <p>
         To double up just place cookies back to back and wrap parchment around.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="IMG_0092.cookie" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551040fb78834014e864f8a13970d" data-pin-description="How To Package and Ship Cookies ~ http://iambaker.net" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/6a00e551040fb78834014e864f8a13970d-500wi.jpg" title="IMG_0092.cookie"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         In the image above the front cookie is not packaged correctly.
        </p>
        <p>
         The cookie in the package behind IS packed correctly, as it is wrapped in parchment.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you are sending a cookie that does not have glaze on it you may be able to skip this step.
        </p>
        <p>
         (I have found that the
         <a href="/sugar-cookie-frosting-recipe" target="_blank">
          glaze icing
         </a>
         I use can end up sticking to the bags if I do not use the parchment around them.)
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="IMG_0101.collage" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551040fb788340147e2d01b31970b" data-pin-description="How To Package and Ship Cookies ~ http://iambaker.net" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/6a00e551040fb788340147e2d01b31970b-500wi.jpg" title="IMG_0101.collage"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         They (the professionals) wrapped each cookie package in bubble wrap. So I wrap each cookie in bubble wrap.
        </p>
        <p>
         They (the professionals) filled up a
         <em>
          bigger then you would think you would need
         </em>
         box with packing popcorn.
        </p>
        <p>
         They (the professionals) placed the cookies in the box sporadically, filling in between with more popcorn.
        </p>
        <p>
         They (the professionals) closed the box and sent me on my way trying to not laugh in my face for being so freaked out about cookies.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="IMG_0072.stamp" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551040fb788340147e2d01d55970b" data-pin-description="How To Package and Ship Cookies ~ http://iambaker.net" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/6a00e551040fb788340147e2d01d55970b-500wi.jpg" title="IMG_0072.stamp"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         This cookies was for a science themed party for sweet Nicholas who is turning two. The invite said, Our Little Monster is turning two. Ha! Love that.
        </p>
        <p>
         I think that about covers it! If you have any questions, please feel free to leave them on this post, and I will go ahead and answer them right here.
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" data-pin-description="How To Package and Ship Cookies ~ http://iambaker.net" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I use this
         <a href="http://iambaker.net/christmas-cookies-cookie-recipe/" target="_blank">
          BEST SUGAR COOKIE
         </a>
         recipe. Once you try it you will be hooked!
        </p>
