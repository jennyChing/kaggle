     <h2 class="page_title">
      Homemade pickled ginger for sushi
     </h2>
     <small>
      <span class="post-author">
       by Vohn
      </span>
      on March 23, 2014
     </small>
     <div class="entry">
      <p>
       Hello dear reader,
      </p>
      <p>
       It is officially spring here in the Northern Hemisphere and,when I see the cherry blossoms appear, my thoughts often turn to Japan. Cherry trees are known as Sakura in Japan and their pretty blossoms are celebrated with many parties and feasts.
      </p>
      <p>
       Never having been to Japan, my sole knowledge of its cuisine are the commonly-known teriyaki and sushi. I especially adore sushibut when I checked out the ingredients I found that the pickled ginger accompaniment often has lots of colourings and E-numbers added. In my quest to eliminate all unnecessary additives from my diet, I decided to make my own pickled ginger.
       <br/>
       It is really easy and a great way to use up any excess root ginger, if you only needed a small amount for a recipe.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/ginger1.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-3399">
        <img alt="Ginger" class="aligncenter wp-image-3399 size-full" height="450" src="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/ginger1.jpg" width="600"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       You will need 150g of root ginger to make a 450g jar of pickled ginger. First of all peel the ginger. You only want to remove the outermost papery skin and the easiest way to do this is to simply scrape it off with a spoon. I was delighted to recently discover this method, as it is really quick and easy plus you lose hardly any of the tasty ginger.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/peeling-ginger1.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-3400">
        <img alt="Peeling ginger using a spoon" class="aligncenter wp-image-3400 size-full" height="450" src="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/peeling-ginger1.jpg" width="600"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/peeling-ginger2.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-3401">
        <img alt="Peeling ginger using a spoon" class="aligncenter wp-image-3401 size-full" height="450" src="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/peeling-ginger2.jpg" width="600"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Next cut off any dried ends and slice the remaining ginger really thinly. I just used the vegetable slicer on my food processor, as I was in a rush, but the ginger should ideally be thinner than this. It is best to use a mandolin or vegetable peeler. For the tenderest pickled ginger, slice across the grain. Toss your sliced ginger in tsp sea salt and leave for a few hours or overnight.
      </p>
      <p>
       Rinse off the salt &amp; then pop the sliced ginger into a sterilised jar. My simple method to sterilise my jars is to wash them in hot soapy water, rinse and then leave to dry in a warm oven.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/sliced-ginger1.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-3403">
        <img alt="sliced ginger" class="aligncenter wp-image-3403 size-full" height="600" src="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/sliced-ginger1.jpg" width="405"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Next heat 1 cup of rice wine vinegar with 1 tbsp honey over a medium heat until the honey has dissolved. Pour into the jar, making sure all the ginger is submerged in the pickling liquid.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/ginger-pickling1.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-3398">
        <img alt="Ginger pickling" class="aligncenter wp-image-3398 size-full" height="600" src="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/ginger-pickling1.jpg" width="374"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Leave to cool, pop on a lid and then store in the fridge for at least a week before using. This allows the ginger to pickle, giving a lovely rounded flavour with no harshness  perfect for delicate sushi! Sometimes the ginger will turn a pretty pale pink colour  this is fine  it depends on the age of the ginger and type of rice wine vinegar.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/pickled-ginger1.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-3402">
        <img alt="Pickled ginger" class="aligncenter wp-image-3402 size-full" height="450" src="http://VohnsVittles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/pickled-ginger1.jpg" width="600"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Homemade Pickled Ginger  recipe
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
         150g root ginger
       </span>
       <br/>
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
          tsp sea salt
       </span>
       <br/>
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
         1 cup rice wine vinegar
       </span>
       <br/>
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
         1 tbsp honey
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong style="line-height: 1.5em;">
        Method
        <br/>
       </strong>
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
        1) Peel the ginger and slice as thinly as possible.
       </span>
       <br/>
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
        2) Place in a bowl and mix in the salt. Leave for a few hours or overnight.
       </span>
       <br/>
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
        3) Rinse to remove excess salt, then place in a sterilised jar.
       </span>
       <br/>
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
        4) Heat rice wine vinegar and honey together over a
       </span>
       gentle
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
        heat until the honey has dissolved.
        <br/>
        5) Pour the honey-vinegar mixture over the ginger, leave to cool and then store in the fridge for at least a week before using.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       Watch this space for step-by-step instructions to make your own sushi to serve with your home-pickled ginger!
      </p>
