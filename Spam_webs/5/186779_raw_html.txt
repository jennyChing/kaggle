can devise potent new medicines.

preservation, and many other disciplines.

                 </a>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="background-color: #f9f9f9; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 22.390625px;">
                  (Fermenting tanks with
                 </span>
                 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yeast" style="background-color: #f9f9f9; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 22.390625px;" title="Yeast">
                  <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none;">
                   yeast
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 <span style="background-color: #f9f9f9; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 22.390625px;">
                  being used to
                 </span>
                 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brewing" style="background-color: #f9f9f9; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 22.390625px;" title="Brewing">
                  <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none;">
                   brew
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 <span style="background-color: #f9f9f9; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 22.390625px;">
                 </span>
                 <span style="background-color: #f9f9f9; color: black; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 22.390625px;">
                  beer)
                 </span>
                </div>
                <o:p>
                </o:p>
                <br/>
                <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                 <br/>
                </span>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="background-color: white; background-position: initial initial; background-repeat: initial initial; line-height: 14.4pt; margin: 4.8pt 0in 6pt;">
                 <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
                  Bacteria can be used for the industrial
production of
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acid" title="Amino acid">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    amino acids
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  .Corynebacterium glutamicumis one of
the most important bacterial species with an annual production of more than two
million tons of amino acids, mainly L-glutamate and L-lysine.
                  <o:p>
                  </o:p>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="background-color: white; background-position: initial initial; background-repeat: initial initial; line-height: 14.4pt; margin: 4.8pt 0in 6pt;">
                 <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
                  A variety of
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biopolymer" title="Biopolymer">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    biopolymers
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
such as
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polysaccharide" title="Polysaccharide">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    polysaccharides
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyester" title="Polyester">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    polyesters
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
and
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyamide" title="Polyamide">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    polyamides
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
are produced by microorganisms. Microorganisms are used for the
biotechnological production of biopolymers with tailored properties suitable
for high-value medical application such as
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tissue_engineering" title="Tissue engineering">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    tissue engineering
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  and drug delivery.
Microorganisms are used for the biosynthesis of
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xanthan" title="Xanthan">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    xanthan
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alginate" title="Alginate">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    alginate
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellulose" title="Cellulose">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    cellulose
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanophycin" title="Cyanophycin">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    cyanophycin
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
poly(gamma-glutamic acid),
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levan_polysaccharide" title="Levan polysaccharide">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    levan
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyaluronic_acid" title="Hyaluronic acid">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    hyaluronic acid
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
organic acids,
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oligosaccharide" title="Oligosaccharide">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    oligosaccharides
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  and
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polysaccharide" title="Polysaccharide">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    polysaccharide
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  ,
and polyhydroxyalkanoates.
                  <o:p>
                  </o:p>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="background-color: white; background-position: initial initial; background-repeat: initial initial; line-height: 14.4pt; margin: 4.8pt 0in 6pt;">
                 <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
                  Microorganisms are beneficial for
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microbial_biodegradation" title="Microbial biodegradation">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    microbial biodegradation
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  or
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioremediation" title="Bioremediation">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    bioremediation
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  of
domestic, agricultural and industrial wastes and subsurface
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    pollution
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  in
soils, sediments and marine environments. The ability of each microorganism to
degrade
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toxic_waste" title="Toxic waste">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    toxic waste
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  depends on the nature of each
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contaminant" title="Contaminant">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    contaminant
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  .
Since sites typically have multiple pollutant types, the most effective
approach to
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microbial_biodegradation" title="Microbial biodegradation">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    microbial biodegradation
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  is to use a
mixture of bacterial and fungal species and strains, each specific to the
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodegradation" title="Biodegradation">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    biodegradation
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  of
one or more types of contaminants.
                  <o:p>
                  </o:p>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="background-color: white; background-position: initial initial; background-repeat: initial initial; line-height: 14.4pt; margin: 4.8pt 0in 6pt;">
                 <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
                  Symbiotic microbial communities are known to
confer various benefits to their human and animal host's health including
aiding digestion, production of beneficial vitamins and amino acids, and
suppression of pathogenic microbes. Some benefit may be conferred by consuming
fermented foods,
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probiotics" title="Probiotics">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    probiotics
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  (bacteria potentially beneficial to the
digestive system) and/or
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prebiotics" title="Prebiotics">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    prebiotics
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  (substances consumed to promote the growth of
probiotic microorganisms).The ways the microbiome influences human and
animal health, as well as methods to influence the microbiome are active areas
of research.
                  <o:p>
                  </o:p>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <br/>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="background-color: white; background-position: initial initial; background-repeat: initial initial; line-height: 14.4pt; margin: 4.8pt 0in 6pt;">
                 <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
                  Research has suggested that microorganisms could
be useful in the treatment of
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    cancer
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  .
Various strains of non-pathogenic
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clostridium" title="Clostridium">
                   <span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">
                    clostridia
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  can
infiltrate and replicate within solid tumors. Clostridial vectors can be safely
administered and their potential to deliver therapeutic proteins has been
demonstrated in a variety of preclinical models.
                  <o:p>
                  </o:p>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="background-color: white; background-position: initial initial; background-repeat: initial initial; line-height: 14.4pt; margin: 4.8pt 0in 6pt;">
                 <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
                  <br/>
                 </span>
                </div>

        </h2>

           12 Ways to Prepare for Hepatitis C Treatment
          </span>
          <span>
           As many as one-fourth of infected people will clear the virus from their systems without it. For those whose immune systems need a helping hand, doctors typically recommend a treatment regimen lasting several months with two or three medications. This therapy can have serious side effects, so head into it with the knowledge and resources you'll need to cope.

          <span>
           1. Learn All You Can
          </span>
          <span>
           Ask your doctor about all your options. Take time to read up on the medications you'll be taking. Typically, you'll take a drug called a peginterferon (brand names Pegasys, PegIntron) with ribavirin (Copegus, Rebetol, Ribasphere). With triple therapy, you'll also take a new drug, either boceprevir (Victrelis) or telaprevir (Incivek).

           2. Make Healthy Changes
          </span>
          <span>
           To support your body during treatment, eat a nutritious diet, including whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and lean protein. Avoid fatty, salty, and sugary foods. Ask your doctor about your iron levels; if they're high, avoid iron-rich foods such as red meat and fortified cereal. Get enough sleep, exercise regularly, and drink plenty of water. Avoid alcohol during your treatment

           3. Assess Your Other Health Problems
          </span>
          <span>
           Your overall health should be as strong as possible before beginning hepatitis therapy. If you have other conditionssuch as diabetes, heart disease, substance abuse, or goutyou may need other tests or treatments first. Also, ask if you should get a vaccine against hepatitis A and B.

           4. Think Through Your Contraception Methods
          </span>
          <span>
           Ribavirin, one of the medications used in hepatitis therapy, causes serious birth defects. And birth control pills may be less effective during treatment. You and your partner should use two other forms of birth control while you're undergoing hepatitis treatment and for six months afterward. For instance, use condoms plus an intrauterine device or a diaphragm.

           5. Ask for Help
          </span>
          <span>
           As your treatment progresses, you'll likely feel tired and run down. You may not be able to handle all your regular responsibilities at home or work, especially on the day or two following a peginterferon shot. Make arrangements with your employer and your friends and family beforehand. Explain that you'll need extra time to rest and could use assistance with certain tasks.

           6. Talk With Your Doctor About Alternatives
          </span>
          <span>
           Some people use alternative therapies alongside traditional hepatitis treatments. Massage, yoga, and meditation can help you cope with the stress and side effects of your hepatitis medication. But alternative medicinessuch as mega-vitamins or herbscan harm your liver or interact with your prescription medication. Check with your doctor before taking them.

           7. Sort Out Your Schedule
          </span>
          <span>
           Most hepatitis regimens require you to take medications at particular intervals. For instance, on triple therapy, you'll take boceprevir every eight hours with food and ribavirin twice daily, and you'll get a weekly peginterferon injection. Adjust your daily routine as needed. Keep side effects in mindribavirin can disrupt sleep, so you'll want to finish your daily dose before 6 p.m.

           8. Book Your Appointments
          </span>
          <span>
           Talk with your doctor about how often you'll need to visit him or her during your treatment process. You may need monthly check-ins and blood draws. Your doctor will check to see if your treatment is working and also monitor for side effects, such as low blood cell counts.

           9. Have a Backup Plan
          </span>
          <span>
           Missing a dose can happen to anyone. It's important to know what to do when it occurs. In most cases, taking pills within an hour of your scheduled time won't make a difference. But if too much time passes, your doctor may have different instructions depending on when your next dose is scheduled. Ask ahead of time how to handle missed doses, and call your doctor with questions along the way.

           10. Explore Your History
          </span>
          <span>
           If a certain hepatitis treatment doesn't work, you may undergo different types or rounds of therapy. It's important for you and your doctor to understand what happened during past treatments before you begin a new plan. Ask specifically whether your viral loads decreased and whether you have cirrhosis, or scarring of the liver. This can change your doctor's advice about new medications.

           11. Seek Out Support
          </span>
          <span>
           Discuss your feelings about illness and treatment with those closest to you, including family, friends, and spiritual leaders. Consider joining a hepatitis C support group to connect with others in a similar situation. Look to this network for help in difficult times, but also to remind you to engage in activities that bring you enjoyment

           12. Stay Organized
          </span>
          <span>
           Gathering all your medical and insurance information in one place can make treatment go more smoothly. Include such documents as recent test results, a list of your medications and immunizations, phone numbers for your health care providers, your insurance card, and a list of emergency contacts. Consider using a phone or other alarm to remind you to take medications.
