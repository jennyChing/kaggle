        <h2 class="sectiontitle">
         Chicago Cubs left handed pitcher Jon Lester named Opening Day starter
        </h2>
        <p>
         First year Chicago Cubs manager Joe Maddon on Monday has named former Boston Red Sox and Oakland Athletics left handed pitcher Jon Lester as his Opening Day starting pitcher.
         <br/>
         The Chicago Cubs Opening Day game will be played at Wrigley Field on April 5, 2015 versus the defending National League Central Division champions and bitter rivals the St Louis Cardinals.
         <br/>
         Lester signed a six year, $155 million contract in December 2014 after his current contract with the Oakland Athletics expired at the end of the 2014 baseball season.
         <br/>
         Lester will be pitching in his fourth Opening Day game. Lester was the Opening Day pitcher for the Boston Red Sox from 2011-2013.
         <br/>
         Lester will be making his Spring Training debut on Friday in Mesa Arizona versus the Cincinnati Reds.
         <br/>
         Lester will be facing the challenge of leading the Cubs to win their first World Series since the 1908 baseball season. Lester signed on with the Cubs since he believes he can be part of a team that can win a World Series. Lester has won two World Series rings with the 2007 and 2013 Boston Red Sox teams.
         <br/>
         What Jon will find out is that in the National League there is no designated hitter rule. Lester will be required to make some plate appearances in the National League.
         <br/>
         St Louis Cardinals manager Mike Matheny has not mentioned who will be his starting pitcher on April 5, 2015.
         <br/>
         The irony is that the Cardinals will be facing the same pitcher that beat in Game Five of the 2013 World Series that gave the Red Sox a 3-2 series lead before Red Sox pitcher John Lackey was the winning pitcher in the sixth game that wrapped the series in favor the Red Sox.
        </p>
