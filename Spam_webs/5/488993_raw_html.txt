         DAWN for Laundry Pre-treating
        </h1>

         </div>
         <p>
          This is a compensated post. All opinions expressed are my own. #DawnBeyondtheSink
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/whoneedsacape.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Dawn-for-laundry-pretreating.jpg">
           <img alt="DAWN for Laundry Pre-treating | Who Needs A Cape?" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17241" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/whoneedsacape.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Dawn-for-laundry-pretreating.jpg?resize=750%2C1000"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://bit.ly/dawndish" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://bit.ly/dawndish', 'Dawn']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="#DawnBeyondtheSink">
            Dawn
           </a>
          </strong>
          is a product that you probably have in your kitchen, I know I do. Did you know though that you can use Dawn for more than just cleaning dirty dishes? You sure can! Sharing with you today one of those life-hacks that will help you in your everyday life. Who needs multiple products when one amazing household product like Dawn can multi-task? Using Dawn as a laundry pre-treater is an awesome life-hack that will save you from: 1) tossing clothing that is too stained, 2) buying additional products and 3) convenience of grabbing the bottle and pre-treating immediately. Amazing what this product can do!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/whoneedsacape.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dawn-logo.png">
           <img alt="dawn logo" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-17244" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/whoneedsacape.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dawn-logo.png?resize=300%2C264"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I found my bottle of Dawn to be useful for pre-treating stained laundry just last week. Have you ever had one of those days? You know the onewhere everything, and I MEAN EVERYTHING just went wrong. From coffee-overflowing in my cup first thing in the morning to stepping on a spiky toy on the way to get a shower, yeah weve all been there. My day got a little messier as I was transferring that nights dinner leftovers to a plastic container to place into the refrigerator. I had made a gigantic pot of CHILI, yup a tomato filled dish that was saucy and oh so good! The problem occurred when the spoon I was using slipped out of the pan and
          <strong>
           KERSPLOOSH
          </strong>
          it landed in the plastic bowl
          <em>
           splattering chili all over my shirt,
          </em>
          it was a mess!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/whoneedsacape.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Dawn-collage.jpg">
           <img alt="DAWN for Laundry Pre-treating | Who Needs A Cape? " class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-17246" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/whoneedsacape.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Dawn-collage.jpg?resize=1024%2C1024"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Luckily, I knew what to do, and thats what Im sharing with you today.
         </p>
         <div class="gmail_default">
          <ul>
           <li dir="ltr">
            <p dir="ltr">
             <strong>
              Apply Dawn directly to any cotton/polycotton fabric*, covering the entire stained area, and rub it in using your fingers.
             </strong>
            </p>
           </li>
           <li dir="ltr">
            <p dir="ltr">
             <strong>
              Then wash it normally with Tide in your washing machine**.
             </strong>
            </p>
           </li>
           <li dir="ltr">
            <p dir="ltr">
             <strong>
              Do not reapply Dawn to the same spot and wash again.
             </strong>
            </p>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h6 dir="ltr">
           <em>
            * For best results, use a little less than two teaspoons (9.5 mL) of Dawn in a top-loading washing machine  be sure not to use too much, and dont use on delicate fabrics like silk. Spot test with a small drop in a less visible area to make sure the material will not be damaged.
           </em>
          </h6>
          <h6 dir="ltr">
           <em>
            ** HE washing machines can be sensitive and cycle times may vary.
           </em>
          </h6>
          <p dir="ltr">
           As you can clearly see, my shirt was saved! I quickly grabbed my bottle of Dawn, pre-treated as directed and laundered. Such a great product!
          </p>
          <p dir="ltr">
