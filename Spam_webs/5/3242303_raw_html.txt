      <h1 class="title">
       <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/2013/09/20/how-to-get-your-run-on-after-a-rut/" rel="bookmark" title="How To Get Your Run On After a Rut">
        How To Get Your Run On After a Rut
       </a>
      </h1>
      <p class="date">
       <span class="day">
        20
       </span>
       <span class="month">
        Sep
       </span>
      </p>
      <div class="entry">
       <a id="dd_start">
       </a>
       <p>
        Yesterday, like most days, I told myself throughout the work day that when I got home I was going for a run.  It has been a while since my last run, the weather in NYC is perfect &amp; I knew I would be able to get home before 6pm. I had high hopesbut those hopes were there Monday and Tuesday, too. &amp; guess what? I did not go for a run Monday or Tuesday. Instead, I got home, sat on my couch and said to myself, Tomorrow. Tomorrow I will go for a run. I worked all day. I'm too tired.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        But that cycle can continue for way too long, if you let it. &amp; I have let it continue for quite some time. In fact, as I was walking to my apartment from the train yesterday I actually started thinking of ways to talk myself out of a run.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Well, I barely ate today. I probably shouldn't run on an empty stomach.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         My feet kinda hurt. Don't wanna make it worse.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Maybe I will just take the dog for a walk and go for a run Friday
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         BLC should be home soon. I should probably be here when he gets home.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         I do have Tamra's OC Wedding that has been burning a hole in my DV-R. I should probably watch it nowto save my poor DV-R.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        You get the idea. It's so much easier to talk yourself out of exercise than to just put on your sneakers and go. But I can tell you from experience  you feel much better about yourself (&amp; for me, life in general) after you force yourself to get that workout in. Plus, it definitely gets to the point where it is second naturebut for me, it takes quite a few forced workouts after a rut to truly get back into it.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        It certainly doesn't help that I read primarily healthy living blogs. Seeing Betty Blogger get in her umpteenth workout of the day while I am drinking a beer on my couch sometimes makes me resentful rather than motivated. Not to say that there aren't moments where I read about a blogger's omigosh-best-most-amazing-PR-DPR-transformative-run-EVER &amp; I wanna jump out of my office chair, throw on my running shoes and make a mad dash to the East River Path.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        But most of the time, motivation hits when it just isn't plausible to get a workout in. Instead, laziness rears its sinister head during those moments when I actually have free time. Laziness can be overpowering sometimes&amp; a big jerk.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        So with that in mind, I want to give you some tips that help me when I need that push to get out of my laziness rut.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <strong>
         <u>
          Tips to get your run on when motivation is lacking
         </u>
        </strong>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <em>
         (&amp; when you work full-time and like to sleep-in in the morning)
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         Fight those excuses!
        </u>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        When all of those ridiculous excuses started popping up in my head, I just had to refute them with some cold, hard facts. BLC, Tamra's OC Wedding and my dinner will all be there when I get back. My feet don't hurt anymore than they normally do after walking around in heels all day.  &amp; if your reasoning fails to convince your subconscious  just lie to it and tell yourself that you will just go for a ten minute run. Because we all know, once you get out there, you will push yourself a little longer, for a little further. I mean, you didn't leave your couch for nothing, did you?
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         Don't turn on the TV! (Or take the dog out, or grab a snack)
        </u>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Stop with the procrastination. When I got home yesterday I immediately got changed into my running clothes, put on my sneakers and ran out the door. Had I turned on the TV while I changed, I may have never left the apartment. Don't give yourself the opportunity to even consider bailing.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         Put on some fun workout gear!
        </u>
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: none;">
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM1.jpg" style="" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="887" id="blogsy-1379680123301.1296" src="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM1.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        I was excited to put on my green long sleeved running shirt (FYI  it is still a bit too warm to wear long sleeves during a run in NYC. I was in sweat city.) since I had not worn it since last spring!
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: none;">
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM.jpg" style="" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="612" id="blogsy-1379680123279.8745" src="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        I was also stoked to wear my
        <a href="http://malyarosa.com" target="_blank" title="Malya Rosa">
         Malya
        </a>
        Rosa headband  ain't it cute? It was made by one of my Reach the Beach: NJ teammates and the little birdies just make me smile.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         Find something that motivates you!
        </u>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        I had read about this music app that was supposed to be great for running on
        <a href="http://www.makingmine.com/" target="_blank" title="Making Mine">
         Making Mine
        </a>
        blog the other day that I had been dying to try out. I decided that if I was gonna run,  I was gonna try out this app.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The name of the app is
        <a href="http://www.fitradio.com/" target="_blank" title="Fit Radio">
         FitRadio
        </a>
        &amp; I loved it!
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: none;">
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM2.jpg" style="" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="889" id="blogsy-1379680123208.365" src="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM2.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The download took two minutes and I was out the door. If you know me at all, you know what channel I chose. If you don't know me, I'll just tell you  I immediately turned on the Electronic Dance Music channel and started running.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The app is a little different than Pandora because (at least for the channel I chose) it was 47 minutes of a DJ spinning, with new songs seamlessly starting after the last song ended. &amp; they were all at what seemed to be the perfect tempo. It was like being at a concert. (Maybe not, but close).
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: none;">
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM3.jpg" style="" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="889" id="blogsy-1379680123256.1648" src="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM3.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        DJ Ryu definitely helped my run out.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         Get out the door!
        </u>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        This, my friends, is always the hardest part of any run (or workout). But once you get out the door, it is smooth sailing and I highly doubt you will regret it.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         Go to a happy place (or path, or track, or park, or trail)!
        </u>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: none;">
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM4.jpg" style="" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="887" id="blogsy-1379680123243.9497" src="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM4.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        It's important to have a destination in mind that gets you excited. If you plan on going on a route that is difficult, boring or just not your favorite when your motivation is already lacking  recipe for disaster. I think those silly excuses just may win.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        For me, my happy place is the track.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        I can keep track of my distance without a Garmin, I get to race other people (without them knowing, of course), I don't have to stop at street lightsyou get the idea. For more of the reasons I love the track, check out an entire post I wrote dedicated to the &lt;3
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/2012/08/22/10-reasons-why-i-love-the-track/" target="_blank" title="Why i love the track">
         track
        </a>
        &lt;3.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: none;">
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM5.jpg" style="" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="887" id="blogsy-1379680123276.446" src="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM5.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        If you don't have a happy place  do your best to find one! Or substitute a happy place with a happy companion  your favorite run buddy! Whether its your spouse, dog, friend, siblingwhatever (or whoever) makes you happy.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         Relish in the fact that when you get done your run, you get to:
        </u>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: none;">
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM6.jpg" style="" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="887" id="blogsy-1379680123280.9717" src="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM6.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        Play with your dog (this is what I get to come home to every day!),
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: none;">
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM7.jpg" style="" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="887" id="blogsy-1379680123271.8125" src="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM7.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Eat your favorite snack (for me, this is sliced cucumber with hummus and Frank's Red Hot),
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Watch that reality show you've been dying to see,
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: none;">
        <a href="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM8.jpg" style="" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="757" id="blogsy-1379680123248.3948" src="http://healthylifehappywife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wpid-Photo-Sep-19-2013-1124-AM8.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Take a sweaty selfie,
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        &amp;
        <u>
         know
        </u>
        that you already got your run on.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Sowhat are you waiting for?! Get out there and run (or swim, or bike, etc.)!
        </strong>
       </p>
