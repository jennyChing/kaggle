        <p>
         Over the years, your body loses the ability to prevent diseases. The aging process is a key factor for this condition. However, is it always so?
        </p>
        <p>
         A team of researchers from UCLA, led by David Walker, has identified a gene that can bypass the aging process. They found a way to affect one part of the body, which will cause a reaction on the gene on a different part of the body. The results of this research were published in
         <a href="http://www.cell.com/cell-reports/fulltext/S2211-1247(14)00669-X">
          Cell Reports
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         AMP  activated protein kinase (
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AMP-activated_protein_kinase">
          AMPK
         </a>
         ) is an enzyme in eukaryotic organisms thathelp in regulatingcellular energy levels. Low energy activates AMPK, which synthesizes
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adenosine_triphosphate">
          ATP
         </a>
         and by fatty acidsincreases the level of glucose. Fruit flies that own this gene live 30% longer. This gene extends their lifespan by an average of 6 to 8 weeks. Itimproves the quality of their life also. The Walkers team discovered that fruit flies that live longer,were healthier.
        </p>
        <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
        <div style="float:left;margin:0px 0px 0px 0;">

        <p>
         If similar effect occurred in humans, it would mean slowing down the aging process and extending the lifespan for 24 years.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Delay of the Aging Process.
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         This finding is the basis for further intervention on the
         <a href="http://newsroom.ucla.edu/releases/ucla-biologists-delay-the-aging-process-by-remote-control">
          delay
         </a>
         of the aging process and beginning of many human diseases, such as Parkinsons disease,
         <a href="http://healthandlovepage.com/vitamin-d-alzheimers-disease/" target="_blank" title="What Is the Link between Vitamin D and Alzheimers disease?">
          Alzheimers disease
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://healthandlovepage.com/high-dose-vitamin-c-cancer/" target="_blank" title="What Impact Has the High-Dose of Vitamin C on Cancer?">
          cancer
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://healthandlovepage.com/8-stroke-warning-signs/" target="_blank" title="8 Stroke Warning Signs Recognized By One in Five Women">
          stroke
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://healthandlovepage.com/reverse-diabetes-in-30-days/" target="_blank" title="6 Test Subjects Reverse Diabetes in 30 Days with This One Change">
          diabetes
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://healthandlovepage.com/6-heart-disease-signs/" target="_blank" title="6 Unusual Heart Disease Signs  What Should You Do?">
          cardiovascular diseases
         </a>
         and others. More researches are needed to prove this, but the goal is realistic 
         <strong>
          to promote healthy aging in humans
         </strong>
         .
        </p>

        <p>
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microautophagy">
          Microautophagy
         </a>
         is the key to this process. For example, if cells in the brain have damaged or old pieces and those cells stay there, they can cause Alzheimers or Parkinsons disease. Clearing this mess avoids the negative effects of the aging process in other parts of the body.
        </p>
        <p>
         Previous research has shown that AMPK can activate
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autophagy">
          autophagy
         </a>
         and this latest research shows thatitcan be strengthened. In fact, they found that when this gene is activated in the nervous system of fruit flies, there is an increase in autophagy in the stomach. Furthermore, activation of AMPK in the intestines causes autophagy in the brain.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://healthandlovepage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Aging-Process-Autophagy.jpg">
          <img alt="Aging Process - Autophagy" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4784" height="256" src="http://healthandlovepage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Aging-Process-Autophagy-600x256.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         Fruit flies are the first great step for genetic experiments, because they own 75% of the genes that cause human diseases. Although researchers during the survey studied more than 100,000 fruit flies, the real result will come when they experiment on animals whose physiology is closer to human.
        </p>
        <p>
         Indeed, the connection between AMPK and the early signs of Alzheimers disease were described in
         <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23583109">
          the study
         </a>
         published on April 10, 2013. Therefore, we should wait for a while to see how this approach will work in complex organisms.
