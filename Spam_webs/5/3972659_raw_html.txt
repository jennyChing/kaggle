         <h1>
          Appreciating a Stay-At-Home Mom
         </h1>
         <p class="post-category">

         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.asylum.com/2010/11/17/psa-talking-to-your-kids-about-star-wars/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.asylum.com/2010/11/17/psa-talking-to-your-kids-about-star-wars/', '']);">
             <img align="right" alt="palpatin" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.scientologyparent.info/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/palpatin1.jpg?resize=151%2C103" style="margin: 0px 0px 0px 15px; display: inline; border: 0px;" title="palpatin"/>
            </a>
            Young fool. Only now, at the end, do you understand.
           </strong>
           <br/>
           
           <em>
            <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
             Palaptine, Sith Emperor
            </span>
           </em>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          After 10 months of living the luxurious existence of having my wife as a stay-at-home mom, my wife finally returned to work yesterday  accepting a great job with some good friends of hers.
         </p>
         <p>
          I thought that, after all this time of constant introspection about parenting, that I would have a proper appreciation of all of the
          <em>
           million
          </em>
          things that my wife has been doing to keep the house going while I worked. Those times she straightened up the living room during the day, made some food I could re-use for the next days lunch, where she did the laundry, folded the diapers, prepared the clothes for the kids, gave the baby a haircut, etc, I now find some additional appreciation for.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tadnkat/6112062201/sizes/l/in/photostream/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.flickr.com/photos/tadnkat/6112062201/sizes/l/in/photostream/', '']);">
           <img alt="My awesome wife &amp; daughter" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.scientologyparent.info/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/6112062201_8a6a8fb40c_b.jpg?resize=604%2C404" style="display: inline; border-width: 0px;" title="My awesome wife &amp; daughter"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Perhaps it has something to do with the fact that, after my wife went to bed early  having to get up at 5:30am to catch an early train into the city, and I find myself here at 2:30am  authoring this blog post while I wait for the next load of diapers to come out of the wash.
         </p>
         <p>
          For our family, having my wife home as a stay-at-home mom was not a luxury but was the right thing to do, what with our young daughter being in a limbo between her former daycare and her now-started preschool (which is
          <em>
           awesome
          </em>
          by the way), and with my newborn son. It was the right way to play our cards. But now, after having gotten used to the many husband-perks of having a wife able to do so many things around the house during the day, I do indeed now appreciate so much more the work that she was doing.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.lronhubbard.org/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lronhubbard.org/', 'L. Ron Hubbard']);">
           L. Ron Hubbard
          </a>
          s precept in
          <em>
           The Way to Happiness
          </em>
          of 
          <a href="http://www.thewaytohappiness.org/thewaytohappiness/precepts/honor-and-help-your-parents.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.thewaytohappiness.org/thewaytohappiness/precepts/honor-and-help-your-parents.html', 'Honor and Help Your Parents']);">
           Honor and Help Your Parents
          </a>
           was primarily authored to make sure children respect the work their parents did in raising them.
         </p>
         <p>
          Well, I would like to submit that we as husbands should
          <strong>
           also
          </strong>
          make sure to honor, help, and respect the work our wives do in making the world go round. Because its a lot. Trust me.
         </p>
         <p>
          And, I figured I might as well close with a pair of tidbits of from the interwebs regarding SAHM awesomeness:
         </p>
         <p>
          First, courtesy of MSNBC and
          <a href="http://thelaughingstork.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://thelaughingstork.com/', 'TheLaughingStork']);">
           TheLaughingStork
          </a>
          , 10 things to never say to a stay-at-home mommy:
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1.
          </strong>
          When the kids are older, do you think youll get a real job?
          <br/>
          <strong>
           2.
          </strong>
          How June Cleaver of you!
          <br/>
          <strong>
           3.
          </strong>
          Oh, so you dont work?
          <br/>
          <strong>
           4.
          </strong>
          Since you have extra time on your hands, could you whip up a few dozen brownies for the bake sale tomorrow?
          <br/>
          <strong>
           5.
          </strong>
          All day with your kids? I cant even imagine.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           6.
          </strong>
          Im jealous. I wish my husband were rich so I wouldnt have to work either.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           7.
          </strong>
          What do you do all day, anyway?
          <br/>
          <strong>
           8.
          </strong>
          Im sure youre not the only one whos ever wasted money on a college degree.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           9.
          </strong>
          That explains why your son is so clingy!
          <br/>
          <strong>
           10.
          </strong>
          Weird. I assumed your house would be superclean.
         </p>
         <p>
          Folks have said 7 out of 10 of those to my wife on more than one occasion, which is why we relate to this list.
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.scientologyparent.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          And lastly, this amazing infographic on what a stay-at-home mommys work is actually worth:
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://degreesearch.org/blog/2011/05/what-is-a-moms-work-worth-infographic/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://degreesearch.org/blog/2011/05/what-is-a-moms-work-worth-infographic/', '']);">
           <img alt="What is a Moms Work Worth? [infographic]" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/degreesearch.org/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/moms_salary.gif?resize=600%2C1374"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          Via:
          <a href="http://www.degreesearch.org/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.degreesearch.org/', 'DegreeSearch.org']);">
           DegreeSearch.org

         </p>
