            <div class="blog-header">
             <h2 class="blog-title">
              <a class="blog-title-link blog-link" href="http://www.homeecathome.com/the-home-economist/country-fried-steak">
               Country Fried Steak
              </a>
             </h2>
             <p class="blog-date">
              <span class="date-text">
               2/10/2013
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="blog-comments">
              <a class="blog-link" href="http://www.homeecathome.com/the-home-economist/country-fried-steak#comments">
               2 Comments
              </a>
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="blog-separator">
            </div>
            <div class="blog-content">
             <div>
              <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/2/1/6/4216519/4678557_orig.jpg" style="width:100%;max-width:666px"/>
               </a>
               <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
              <u>
               Steak Ingredients:
              </u>
              <br/>
              1 lb. cube steak (4 pieces)
              <br/>
              3/4 cup milk
              <br/>
              1 egg
              <br/>
              1 1/2 cups flour
              <br/>
              <span style="">
              </span>
              <span style="">
              </span>
              1/2 tsp. seasoned salt
              <br/>
              <span style="">
              </span>
              <span style="">
              </span>
              1/2 tsp. creole (cajun) seasoning
              <br/>
              salt
              <br/>
              pepper
              <br/>
              oil
              <br/>
              <span style="">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <u>
               Gravy Ingredients:
              </u>
              <br/>
              3 Tbsp. pan grease (add butter if you don't have enough grease left in the pan)
              <br/>
              3 Tbsp. flour
              <br/>
              1 to 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
              <br/>
              1 to 1 1/2 cups milk
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Directions:
              <br/>
              <br/>
              1. Mix the egg with 3/4 cup milk.
             </div>
             <div>
              <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/2/1/6/4216519/3328399_orig.jpg" style="width:100%;max-width:1066px"/>
               </a>
               <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
              2. Mix 1 1/2 cups flour with the creole seasoning and seasoned salt.
             </div>
             <div>
              <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/2/1/6/4216519/8038015_orig.jpg" style="width:100%;max-width:1066px"/>
               </a>
               <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
              3. Salt and pepper each side of the cube steaks. Then dredge each steak in the milk mixture, then the flour mixture. Dredge in the milk then flour again.
             </div>
             <div>
              <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/2/1/6/4216519/174254_orig.jpg" style="width:100%;max-width:1066px"/>
               </a>
               <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
              4. Heat skillet at medium heat and coat with about 1/4 inch oil. When oil is hot, place the steaks in the skillet and cook until browned on each side. (4-5 minutes a side)
             </div>
             <div>
              <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/2/1/6/4216519/6976709_orig.jpg" style="width:100%;max-width:1066px"/>
               </a>
               <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
              Feel free to add more creole seasoning while the steaks are cooking.
             </div>
             <div>
              <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/2/1/6/4216519/4766283_orig.jpg" style="width:100%;max-width:1066px"/>
               </a>
               <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
              5. When the steaks are done, place them on a cooling rack over a pan. This will prevent then from sitting in grease and keep them a little crispier while you make the gravy. If you are concerned about them getting too cool, feel free to place them in a warm oven.
             </div>
             <div>
              <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/2/1/6/4216519/3551488_orig.jpg" style="width:100%;max-width:1066px"/>
               </a>
               <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
              6. Leave about 3 Tbsp. grease in your skillet. If you don't have enough grease, add a couple Tbsp. butter. Stir 3 Tbsp. flour into the fat to make a roux. Cook and stir until golden brown.
             </div>
             <div>
              <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/2/1/6/4216519/6343526_orig.jpg" style="width:100%;max-width:1066px"/>
               </a>
               <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
              7.
              <span style="font-family: Helvetica; line-height: normal; ">
               Add chicken broth to deglaze the pan, then add milk. Use more liquid to make a thinner gravy and less to make a thicker gravy. Add more salt and pepper as needed. Cook at a low heat until desired thickness is reached.
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-thin " style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/2/1/6/4216519/7455768_orig.jpg" style="width:100%;max-width:1066px"/>
               </a>
               <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
              Serve over country fried steak.
             </div>
            </div>
