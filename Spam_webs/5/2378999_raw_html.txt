     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Z Nation
       </b>
      </i>
      is an American horror-drama/
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Post-apocalyptic" title="Post-apocalyptic">
       post-apocalyptic
      </a>
      television series that airs on
      <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">
       Syfy
      </a>
      , created by
      <a href="/wiki/Karl_Schaefer" title="Karl Schaefer">
       Karl Schaefer
      </a>
      and Craig Engler, and produced by
      <a href="/wiki/The_Asylum" title="The Asylum">
       The Asylum
      </a>
      . The first season of 13 episodes premiered on September 12, 2014.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       Z Nation
      </i>
      is filmed in the
      <a href="/wiki/Spokane,_Washington" title="Spokane, Washington">
       Spokane, Washington
      </a>
      area and was renewed for a second season to air in fall 2015, which will consist of 15 episodes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swift_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Swift-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">

      </sup>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Premise">
       Premise
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Z_Nation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Premise">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Z Nation
      </i>
      begins three years into a virus-caused zombie apocalypse, which has already killed most humans. A group must transport Murphy, the only known survivor of a zombie bite who did not himself turn into a zombie, from New York to the world's last known functioning research lab in California. His blood contains
      <a href="/wiki/Antibody" title="Antibody">
       antibodies
      </a>
      that are mankind's last and best hope for a vaccine. However, Murphy harbors a dark secret that threatens them all.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Murphy had been the unwilling subject of a government test vaccine in the days just before society fell apart.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Z_Nation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The first season of
      <i>
       Z Nation
      </i>
      has received mixed reviews from professional television critics. Its aggregate score on
      <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">
       Metacritic
      </a>
      is 48
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      on scale of 0 to 100.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">
        Rotten Tomatoes
       </a>
      </i>
      Critics Consensus gave the show a rating of 53%, admitting that "although it's hampered by an overcrowded narrative,
      <i>
       Z Nation
      </i>
      manages to muster up some fun scares without taking itself too seriously."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Chris Carabott of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">
        IGN
       </a>
      </i>
      noted that the show "continues to set itself apart in campy and inventive ways."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In January 2015, Graeme Virtue of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">
        The Guardian
       </a>
      </i>
      called
      <i>
       Z Nation
      </i>
      a "brazen Walking Dead rip-off" and included it on a list of five great US TV shows unavailable in Britain.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-virtue20150113_28-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-virtue20150113-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">
       Forbes
      </a>
      magazine found the show had a high entertainment value.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In its first season,
      <i>
       Z Nation
      </i>
      averaged 1.42 million viewers per episode, including 480,000 in the 18- to 49-year-old demographic.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The series' premiere episode was the subject of a large number of unlicensed downloads shortly after it first aired.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_31-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Variety-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
