        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Sync Your Windows Mobile Contacts and Calendar using Funambol for FREE
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="date">

        <p>
         <img align="right" alt="moto-q.png" src="http://www.tipsfor.us/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/moto-q.png"/>
         I have a love/hate relationship with my MOTO Q smartphone. I
         <strong>
          love
         </strong>
         it because it helps me stay connected on-the-run, because it has a big keyboard, and because I got a great deal on it.
        </p>
        <p>
         I
         <strong>
          hate
         </strong>
         it because it runs Windows Mobile 5, which has crashed a few times so badly that Ive had to reset my phone, wiping out all my information.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres a big problem with Windows Mobile  Microsoft wants you to use their Activesync software to backup your contacts, calendar, and other files. Thats all fine and dandy, but surprise, surprise! Activesync only interfaces nicely with Outlook. Linux/Mac users, and Windows users who dislike (or cant afford) Outlook are locked out in the cold. Sorry.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is a HUGE problem, especially for potential GNU/Linux adopters. Heres a typical scenario: Joe User hears about this Linux thing and decides to give it a shot. He tries it, likes what he sees, and is considering switching full-time, but then Joe decides to plug in his Windows Mobile-based smartphone or PocketPC. Uh oh. Nothing happens. Joe User is savvy enough to do a little searching, but quickly realizes that syncing his Windows Mobile device to Linux is going to be about as easy as convincing Microsoft to switch to a UNIX-based kernel!
        </p>
        <p>
         Ever since I bought my phone, Ive been looking for an easy, free way to sync my contacts and calendar without using Activesync and Outlook. Ive spent many hours researching and testing a handful of methods, with varying amounts of success. Over the next few weeks, Im going to post tutorials on a few of my findings.
        </p>
        <p>
         I only have two requirements:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          The method must
          <strong>
           not
          </strong>
          require Outlook or Activesync (or Windows Mobile Device Center, or whatever Vista calls it).
         </li>
         <li>
          As the self-proclaimed ruler of the free world, I require that the entire process be FREE. No subscription services, one-time fees, or anything like that.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         Lets get started!
        </p>
        <h3>
         Method One  Funambol
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img align="right" alt="funambol-logo.png" src="http://www.tipsfor.us/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/funambol-logo.png"/>
         One possible solution that Ive found is by using Funambol (
         <a href="http://www.funambol.com/" title="get Funambol!">
          http://www.funambol.com
         </a>
         ). According to their site, they are the worlds leading mobile open-source project. I cant verify if theyre truly the worlds leading project, but I DO know that they offer the
         <strong>
          easiest
         </strong>
         syncing solution that Ive tried so far.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step One  myFUNAMBOL Portal
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you are so inclined, you may download their server software (for Windows or Linux) and run your own sync server. For the rest of us, they offer the free
         <a href="http://www.funambol.com/solutions/portal.php" title="myFUNAMBOL Portal">
          myFUNAMBOL Portal
         </a>
         . The portal is currently in beta, and they claim that sign-up is by invitation only. However, I had no problem registering directly for an account.
        </p>
        <p>
         Create and activate your myFUNAMBOL account. Once activated, you should receive a message like this:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center">
         <img alt="funambol-success.png" src="http://www.tipsfor.us/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/funambol-success.png"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You may also choose to synchronize e-mail with Funambol by giving it your e-mail address and e-mail password. Be sure to register your correct phone number with Funambol, since this is how you are going to receive the phone plug-in.
        </p>
        <p>
         Your new myFUNAMBOL portal should look something like this (click for larger view):
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.tipsfor.us/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/funambol-portal-default.png" title="funambol-portal-default.png">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center">
         <a href="http://www.tipsfor.us/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/funambol-portal-default.png" title="funambol-portal-default.png">
          <img alt="funambol-portal-default.png" src="http://www.tipsfor.us/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/funambol-portal-default.thumbnail.png"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step Two  Install the Phone Software
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now that the portal is working, its time to install the Funambol client onto your phone. From inside the myFUNAMBOL portal, choose
         <em>
          Profile
         </em>
         , and then drill down to the
         <em>
          Phone
         </em>
         option. Verify that the information is correct, and then from the drop-down list at the bottom, select
         <em>
          Download Funambol Windows Mobile Plug-in to my phone
         </em>
         . See screenshot below.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center">
         <img alt="funambol-dl-wm-plugin.png" src="http://www.tipsfor.us/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/funambol-dl-wm-plugin.png"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You should receive a text message with a link to the plug-in, or you can choose to download the CAB file directly by browsing to this link on your phone 
         <a href="http://my.funambol.com/me/funambol-sm-plugin.cab">
          http://my.funambol.com/me/funambol-sm-plugin.cab
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center">
         <img alt="funambol-wm-plugin.png" src="http://www.tipsfor.us/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/funambol-wm-plugin.png"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Once the plug-in is successfully installed, you just have to configure it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step Three  Configuration and Initial Sync
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="right" alt="wm-funambol-login.png" src="http://www.tipsfor.us/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/wm-funambol-login.png"/>
         Youre almost done. The final step is to tell the Funambol client on your phone how to connect to the myFUNAMBOL portal. Enter the credentials for the account you created. The server location should be filled in for you automatically.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once the Windows Mobile plug-in is connected to the web portal, all thats left to do is start syncing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Chances are high that you can just press the
         <strong>
          Sync All
         </strong>
         button to have your information sent to the portal. If you want to specify which elements to sync, choose
         <strong>
          Menu
         </strong>
         
         <strong>
          Settings
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         See the slideshow below for a visual overview of the Funambol plug-in on my MOTO Q.
        </p>

        <p>
         There you have it. Once the initial sync is finished, log back in to your myFUNAMBOL portal and verify that your Contacts and Calendar settings have been updated.
        </p>
        <p>
         Try it out. Add or delete a contact via the web portal and press
         <strong>
          Sync All
         </strong>
         again from the Funambol phone plug-in. The changes should update on your phone. Also, any contacts or calendar information that you add on your phone should update in the web portal. Nice.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Other Thoughts
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Naturally, to make your phone sync with
         <a href="http://www.funambol.com/">
          Funambol
         </a>
         , you need Internet access on your smartphone or PocketPC. My mobile plan has unlimited data access, so this works well for me. If you pay for all data access, consider setting the
         <strong>
          Sync Method
         </strong>
         to
         <em>
          Manual
         </em>
         so as to not incur costly data charges.
        </p>
        <p>
         For me, the main purpose of Funambol is to backup my Contacts and Calendar without Activesync or Outlook, and it does the job with aplomb. If Windows Mobile crashes and forces me to do (another) hard reset, within minutes I can reinstall the Funambol plug-in and reload my critical information. It gives me peace of mind. The best part is that it doesnt matter what operating system your computer is running. You can still keep your information in sync.
        </p>
        <p>
         For you Windows/Outlook users out there, youll be pleased to know that Funambol also offers an Outlook plug-in to synchronize your Contacts and Calendar. If you throw
         <a href="http://www.google.com/support/calendar/bin/answer.py?answer=89955" target="_blank">
          Google Calendar Sync
         </a>
         (for Outlook) into the equation, you can create one lean, mean, syncing machine!
        </p>
        <p>
         As I mentioned, Ive spent a lot of time researching this lately, and in the coming weeks I plan to post a few more tutorials on syncing Windows Mobile information without Activesync or Outlook, including information on making it work with Thunderbird and Google Calendar. Stay tuned for my findings.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the meantime, if all you need is a simple, elegant, and FREE method to backup your Windows Mobile Contacts and Calendar over the Internet,
         <a href="http://www.funambol.com/">
          Funambol
         </a>
         has you covered.
        </p>
