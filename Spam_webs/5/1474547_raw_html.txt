            <h1 class="ArticleHeader">
             Ex NBA Star Gilbert Arenas Arrested for Illegal Fireworks in Los Angeles, California
            </h1>
            <span>
             By Randy Collins
            </span>
            <span class="Pubdate">
             Jun. 27, 2013 8:32a
            </span>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <div id="ArticleImage">
             <img src="/images/cmssys/thumbnails//profiles/246220/articles/fireworks-350275-20-ffe7e7e7White-0.20.3-0.90.850.85-1.png" style="border-width:0px;width:350px;height:275px;"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             Ex-NBA star
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gilbert_Arenas">
              Gilbert Arenas
             </a>
             was arrested on the morning of June 27
             <sup>
              th
             </sup>
             , 2013 in Los Angeles, California for possession of illegal fireworks and speeding. According to sources, Arenas was in possession of numerous boxes of fireworks that resulted in an investigation by the Los Angeles Police Department bomb squad.
            </p>
            <p>
             This is not Arenas' first run-in with the law, having plead guilty to a felony charge of carrying an unlicensed pistol in 2010. At the time, Arenas was a member of the Washington Wizards NBA team and was released from the Wizards shortly after the incident.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Penalties for Illegal Fireworks in California
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             In California, it is illegal for a person to possess dangerous fireworks if they do not have a valid permit. Permits are usually given to experienced pyrotechnic operators operating as part of large-scale firework displays during national holidays or celebrations. "Safe and Sane" fireworks are legal within California, but they must be used responsibly to avoid any firework violations.
            </p>
            <p>
             In Arenas' case, sources report him to have been in possession of a large quantity of illegal fireworks, but these initial reports have not been validated. If Gilbert Arenas is found guilty of possessing illegal fireworks, he could face misdemeanor or felony charges. If
             <a href="http://www.cmcdefense.com/">
              convicted of felony illegal fireworks possession
             </a>
             , he could be ordered to pay up-to $10,000 in fines or serve up-to three years in a California state prison.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Gilbert Arenas Criminal History Could Be a Factor
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             Due to Arenas' history of brandishing firearms, prosecutors may try to convict him of felony possession. When a person has a history of committing crimes that put the public in danger, prosecutors are more likely to try and prosecute violators to the fullest extent of the law. Arenas' most recent arrest may also be the final straw for his involvement in the National Basketball Association. Those convicted of misdemeanor or felony firework charges may have trouble obtaining future employment as a result.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Possible Defenses for California Firework Possession
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             Defense strategies for those facing felony or misdemeanor firework charges differ greatly depending on the circumstance of a person's case. As there are very few facts currently available regarding Gilbert Arenas' case, developing a specific course of action to defend his charges is not realistic.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Misdemeanor and Felony Firework Possession Legal Assistance
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             If you or your loved one are facing charges for firework-related charges, taking advantage of a free legal case evaluation could help you avoid making costly mistakes. Call 949-250-6097 to speak with one of our experienced criminal defense lawyers and find out what your options are.
            </p>
