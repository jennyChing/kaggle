         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-02-09T00:51:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 9, 2015
         </time>
        </p>
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         10 Km Training Week 5
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <script>
         &lt;!--
dynamicgoogletags.update();
//--&gt;
        </script>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-KjTemDwZGD4/VNZkLdViQzI/AAAAAAAABN0/n2d14XeShZw/s1600/TO%2B%C2%A0RUNNING%2Bweek%2B5.png" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-KjTemDwZGD4/VNZkLdViQzI/AAAAAAAABN0/n2d14XeShZw/s1600/TO%2B%C2%A0RUNNING%2Bweek%2B5.png', '');" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Title image for 10 km training Week 5" border="0" height="400" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-KjTemDwZGD4/VNZkLdViQzI/AAAAAAAABN0/n2d14XeShZw/s1600/TO%2B%C2%A0RUNNING%2Bweek%2B5.png" title="Title image for 10 km training Week 5" width="266"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Im only 1 week away from reaching my first goals of 2015. On February 16 I will running in my very first 10 km race ever! This will also be my very first race in winter as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         Signing up for a race in February was quite strategic on my part. Signing up for a race gets me motivated to train very much like a deadline at work gets you motivated to finish the project. Also, winter is usually when Im in hibernation mode so its also helping me to be active during a season when usually enjoying the comforts of the indoors.
        </p>
        <p>
         My training has been going very well until this week when I came down with a terrible sinus infection. That has derailed my training somewhat this week! There is also a blizzard on the forecast to add icing to my cake, however, I havent seen the evidence of this yet, thankfully.
        </p>
        <p>
         On a slightly more positive note, my training is starting to taper this week to let my body rest a bit before the big day next week.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, despite the set back, here was my training for this week:
        </p>
        <h2>
         Tuesdays Runch
        </h2>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-v6YI0dVLHyQ/VNZv1FeQ1mI/AAAAAAAABOE/FH_zwZdZ83U/s1600/week%2B5%2Brun%2B1.png" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-v6YI0dVLHyQ/VNZv1FeQ1mI/AAAAAAAABOE/FH_zwZdZ83U/s1600/week%2B5%2Brun%2B1.png', '');" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="diagram showing speeds for 30 min interval run" border="0" height="132" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-v6YI0dVLHyQ/VNZv1FeQ1mI/AAAAAAAABOE/FH_zwZdZ83U/s1600/week%2B5%2Brun%2B1.png" title="30 min interval run" width="320"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         I did some interval training on Tuesday. Luckily, the scheduled training on miCoach was a 30 min run, so I was able to keep with training plan.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Just to recap the zones:
         </b>
         <br/>
         <b>
          <span style="color: blue;">
           Blue zone
          </span>
         </b>
         : Easy pace, good for warm-ups &amp; cool downs
         <br/>
         <b>
          <span style="color: #38761d;">
           Green Zone
          </span>
         </b>
         : Medium Pace, my regular running pace
         <br/>
         <b>
          <span style="color: #f1c232;">
           Yellow Zone
          </span>
         </b>
         : Difficult Pace, used to improve your cardio strength
         <br/>
         <b>
          <span style="color: #cc0000;">
           Red Zone:
          </span>
         </b>
         Maximum Effort , about 90% of your sprint pace
        </p>
        <h2>
         Thursdays Runch
        </h2>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LQ4gr-8b9zg/VNZv53iVwhI/AAAAAAAABOM/pmLM8kNztrI/s1600/week%2B5%2Brun%2B2.png" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LQ4gr-8b9zg/VNZv53iVwhI/AAAAAAAABOM/pmLM8kNztrI/s1600/week%2B5%2Brun%2B2.png', '');">
          <img alt="image depicting 25 min easy run" border="0" height="119" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LQ4gr-8b9zg/VNZv53iVwhI/AAAAAAAABOM/pmLM8kNztrI/s1600/week%2B5%2Brun%2B2.png" title="25 min easy run" width="320"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         I had am easy 25 min run scheduled on Thursday, but since I started my sinus infection on that day, I skipped the workout at lunch. I instead headed over to the new amazing (aka dangerous on the wallet) Outlet Collection at Niagara and shopped for a few items that I think I will need for my up coming race. I picked up a pair of Nike running gloves, some sunglasses (to prevent snow blindness since Im desperately hoping for a sunny day) and new pair of running tight. This last one is just because Im vain and I want to look fashionable for my up coming race. A review of my new compression leggings is coming.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Weekend run
        </h2>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-dEpeFfABsn8/VNZw37ro26I/AAAAAAAABOc/lyoo1PYCCRA/s1600/week%2B5%2Brun%2B3.png" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-dEpeFfABsn8/VNZw37ro26I/AAAAAAAABOc/lyoo1PYCCRA/s1600/week%2B5%2Brun%2B3.png', '');" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="image showing a mid intensity 45 min run" border="0" height="150" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-dEpeFfABsn8/VNZw37ro26I/AAAAAAAABOc/lyoo1PYCCRA/s1600/week%2B5%2Brun%2B3.png" title="image showing a mid intensity 45 min run" width="320"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Today, I had a 45 minute run (as seen above) but since Im still suffering for a sinus infection, I decided to do a simple 25 minute run where I covered 3.25 kilometers. The temperature was great but the sidewalks are still snow covered, which made running difficult. I used a new to me running app,
         <b>
          Runtastic Pro
         </b>
         , which I will review shortly.
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Motivate Me Monday
        </h2>
        <p>
         Also, I will be participating in the #MotivateMe Monday Linkup on the
         <a href="http://fitnesscheerleader.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://fitnesscheerleader.com/', 'Fitness Cheerleader.com');" target="_blank">
          Fitness Cheerleader.com
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.runningrachel.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.runningrachel.com/', 'Running Rachel.com');" target="_blank">
          Running Rachel.com
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here is whats on the training agenda for week 6, my final week of training for my 10 Km race:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <b>
           Tuesday
          </b>
          <b>
           
          </b>
          31 minute interval run (blue/red zone)
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Thursday
          </b>
          <b>
           
          </b>
          25 minute easy run
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Weekend 
          </b>
          20 minute easy run
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Monday
          </b>
          Race Day. Monday is Family Day, a very welcomed Canadian Holiday!
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div>
         Alright you seasoned racers out there! Do you have any tips to offer this first time 10 km racer? Id love to hear any imput!
