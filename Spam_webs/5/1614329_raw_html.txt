              <h2>
               <a href="http://www.mi40k.com/?p=395" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Space Marine Command Tank Rules">
                Space Marine Command Tank Rules
               </a>
              </h2>
              <span class="author">
               posted by TheRascalKing13
              </span>
             </div>
             <div class="time">
              <span class="month">
               Jun
              </span>
              <span class="date">
               4
              </span>
             </div>

            <br clear="all"/>
            <div class="main_txt">
             <div class="entry">
              <div class="main">
               <p>
                <a href="http://www.mi40k.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/land_raider_excelsior.png">
                 <img alt="land_raider_excelsior" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-408" height="196" src="http://www.mi40k.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/land_raider_excelsior-300x196.png" width="300"/>
                </a>
                Im back from vacation! Sorry for the dearth of articles recently.
               </p>
               <p>
                The Space Marines Command Tanks rules have been leaked. Check out the two images below to see the rules. These are something Space Marine players have been asking for for a very long time, and its finally going to happen. Forge World released the Damocles-pattern Rhino, which is similar to the Rhino Primaris in concept, almost five years ago.
               </p>
               <p>
                The tanks are interesting in that they come as a unit, and the Excelsior is a Character. Obviously, this is so it can engage in close combat challenges with its tank adversaries. The whole unit clocks in at 400 points, which is 130 more than they would be without the bonus rules. Lets see if the new rules justify the price.

               </p>

               <br/>
               First lets look at the Excelsior. My first problem with it is that I will always see Al Gore from the South Park episode ManBearPig. He always yells Excelsior! right before going off on some misadventure.
               <p>
                On to the rules. Right off the top of the bat it gets an Aquila Aegis Field, which grants it a 6++ invulnerable and ignores Shaken and Stunned. This is crazy good for a tank with AV 14 all the way around. The stayability of this guy is monstrous.

              <h2>
               <a href="http://www.mi40k.com/?p=392" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Warlord Titan Head Picture Released">
                Warlord Titan Head Picture Released
               </a>
              </h2>
              <span class="author">
               posted by TheRascalKing13
              </span>
             </div>
             <div class="time">
              <span class="month">
               May
              </span>
              <span class="date">
               11
              </span>
             </div>
            </div>
            <br clear="all"/>
            <div class="main_txt">
             <div class="entry">
              <div class="main">
               <p>
                <a href="http://www.mi40k.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/warlord-head.jpg">
                 <img alt="warlord-head" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-393" height="209" src="http://www.mi40k.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/warlord-head-300x209.jpg" width="300"/>
                </a>
                While there may have been some doubt that
                <a href="http://www.forgeworld.co.uk/">
                 Forge Worlds
                </a>
                new model is a Warlord Titan, those doubts have been removed today. Forge Worlds
                <a href="http://www.forgeworld.co.uk/News/FORGE_WORLD_BULLETIN_59.html">
                 bulletin #59

                Forge World Now Standard For All 40K Games
               </a>
              </h2>
              <span class="author">
               posted by TheRascalKing13
              </span>
             </div>
             <div class="time">
              <span class="month">
               Apr
              </span>
              <span class="date">
               29
              </span>
             </div>
            </div>
            <br clear="all"/>
            <div class="main_txt">
             <div class="entry">
              <div class="main">
               <p>
                <a href="http://www.mi40k.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/forge_world.jpg">
                 <img alt="forge_world" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-351" height="229" src="http://www.mi40k.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/forge_world-300x229.jpg" width="300"/>
                </a>
                Ever since the
                <a href="http://www.forgeworld.co.uk/New_Stuff/IMPERIAL_ARMOUR_THE_SIEGE_OF_VRAKS.html">
                 Siege of Vraks Imperial Armour
                </a>
                release,
                <a href="http://www.forgeworld.co.uk">
                 Forge World
                </a>
                has stated that all of the models and rules contained therein would be official and standard. A reader over at
                <a href="http://www.spikeybitsblog.com/2015/04/is-everything-40k-approved-now-2.html">
                 Spikey Bits
                </a>
                wrote in to
                <a href="http://www.games-workshop.com/">
                 Games Workshop
                </a>
                and seems to have confirmed that yes, everything is valid in this brave new world.
               </p>
               <p>
                It really shows how much things have changed in the last five years. Before MI40K was hacked and went down from 2012-2014, there was 15 or 16 codexes, and it was possible to know every single rule for every army. Now there are dozens of hardcover books, and even the huge Forge World catalog is a legal thing to bring to the table. Personally, I think that this is a good thing for hobbyists and a bad thing for tournaments. We are discussing running another MI40K tournament sometime soon, but figuring out the rules for it might be tough. On the other hand, having all these silky sweet models to build and gawk over is awesome. Its a tough call on whether this is a positive change or not.
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="http://www.mi40k.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/FW-stanmp.jpg">
                 <img alt="FW-stanmp" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-352" height="300" src="http://www.mi40k.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/FW-stanmp-216x300.jpg" width="216"/>

Bringing this to the table is like surfboarding down an avalanche of skulls while shredding a sweet guitar solo and banging a supermodel. Its that awesome.
               <br/>

               <a href="http://www.mi40k.com/?p=161">
                we reported on it earlier
               </a>

However, you can now fire twice if you sit still, and you can fire three times if you stand still and take what amounts to a Gets Hot roll. Thats a lot better, making this better than a standard all-Lascannon Predator!
               <br/>
               I will never accept that this has four barrels and fires one, two, or three shots. I know the fluff is that its shooting them offset with a miniscule difference to drill into enemy armor, but it just feels like it should be a four-shot tank.
