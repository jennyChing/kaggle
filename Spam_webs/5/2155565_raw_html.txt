         <b>
          What Is A Total Hip Replacement?
         </b>
         <br/>
         A total hip replacement is a surgical procedure, where a damaged hip joint is removed and is replaced with an artificial hip joint. A few medical conditions that can damage the hip joint include osteoarthritis, which means that the cartilage covering the ends of the bones that come together to form a joint, has broken down. This causes the joint bones to rub together and produce spurs that lead to extreme pain and stiffness to the point that sitting, standing, and climbing is an excruciating activity. Other conditions that can damage the hip joint are fractures, injuries, tumors, arthritis, and osteonecrosis. Arthritis conditions like rheumatoid arthritis is when the body's natural immune system begins to attack the membranes that also line the hip joints. Osteonecrosis is a condition where the blood supply to the bone is cut off, causing the bone to die.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <div style="text-align: center">
          <img border="0" class="imagen" src="http://i1.huffpost.com/gen/1045341/thumbs/r-HIP-KNEE-REPLACEMENT-SEX-LIFE-large570.jpg"/>
         </div>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          Total Hip Replacement Recovery Tips
         </b>
         <br/>
         When an individual has undergone a total hip replacement, there are precautions that must be taken to ensure that the hip does not become dislocated. These precautions include: (a) do not bend at the hip more than 90 degrees; (b) avoid moving the operative leg across the mid line (belly button) of the body; and (c) avoid rotating the toes of the operative hip area, in an inward movement. Movement restrictions must be strictly followed for four to six weeks after a hip joint surgery. Sex can begin in about six weeks, but movement must still be kept to a minimum. Waiting a full six weeks to resume sexual intimacy allows the hip muscles to be fully recovered and better able to control the movements and positions of your leg.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          Sexual Positions after a Hip Replacement
         </b>
         <br/>
         When you are ready to resume sexual relations, both men and women must find initial missionary positions to enjoy a full-on sexual experience. Sexual positions that minimize motion of the hip, limits pain, and saves energy are the conditions in which to have healthy, pain-free sex. Some of these positions can include the following:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          1. Missionary Position
         </b>
          men and women will enjoy this position after hip surgery. For women, they can bend their knees slightly with feet on the bed. Pillows are used to support the legs. For men, he can stretch his legs out behind him and place a pillow between his knees to keep his hip leg from crossing the mid-line, while supporting his weight with his arms.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          2. Face-To-Face Position 
         </b>
         men and women can use this position. The person with the hip surgery is on the bottom, reclined with the help of a pillow. A woman can bend her knees slightly and a man can put a pillow between his knees to keep the operative leg supported.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          3. Sitting Position -
         </b>
         operative men are to sit on a chair with his knees spread slightly apart and his feet on the floor. The operative woman sits on his lap, but she should not lean in too far forward to prevent her hip from bending more than at a 90 degree angle.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          4. Lying and Kneeling
         </b>
          the woman will lie on the bed on her back and the man stands behind her. This position is best for women with a new hip. Other comfortable and safe sexual positions to perform after a total hip joint surgical procedure are the kneeling position, the side lying positions, and the standing position.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          Improved Sex after Hip Replacement
         </b>
         <br/>
         Patients all report that sexual relations are satisfying and very fulfilling following a total hip replacement. Men and women report an improved sex life, as well as an overall improvement in the quality of the daily lives. Researchers have always touted the improved mobility following a total hip replacement procedure, but recent research studies also prove that this surgery offers a bonus in sexual intimacy. The American Academy of Orthopedic Surgeons noted that after a total hip replacement procedure, 81% of their patients who had said that their sex lives had begun to suffer as a result of their painful and debilitating bad hips, reported that the frequency of their sexual activity increased by 50%, meaning that instead of sex monthly, they began having sex again on a weekly basis. Also, many male and female patients reported increased libido emotions,

          <b>
           improved stamina
          </b>
         </a>
         , and a more enjoyable climax.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          Conclusion
         </b>
         <br/>
         To enjoy sex after a total hip replacement procedure, remember to plan your sexual intimacy, rather than spontaneously needing to jump each other's bones. It is important that both partners are educated in what their hip replacement partner can and cannot do. Work together to control your movements and pace. As the National Institute of Health and orthopedics suggest, remember the 3 P's: precautions, positions, and plans.
