     <h1>
      <a class="taggedlink" href="/post/2013/10/08/How-Worldwide-Shipping-Methods-Have-Changed.aspx">
       How Worldwide Shipping Methods Have Changed
      </a>
     </h1>
     <span class="author">
      by
      <a href="/author/Admin.aspx">
       Administrator
      </a>
     </span>
     <span class="pubDate">
      8. October 2013 11:47
     </span>
     <div class="text">
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       <img alt="" height="367" src="/image.axd?picture=%2f2015%2f04%2fiStock_000023137840_Small.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        Looking at century old newspaper clippings, you may find an occasional article about a shipment from overseas. Perhaps you would even see an aged photo of a ship being unloaded by deck hands, toting heavy boxes on their shoulders. At the edge of the photo, you may be able to see a dray, or a low, heavy cart pulled by a horse or two. It would seem, when looking at antique newspapers, that ways of shipping, unloading, and delivering have changed in your world today. Today's photo would contain a massive cargo ship, piled high with container boxes, and the horse and carriage would be replaced by a
       </span>
       <a href="http://www.godependable.com/harbor/Aboutus/Overview.aspx" style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;" title="Container Drayage">
        container drayage
       </a>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        company, ready to transport those containers to various locations.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        Today, overseas shipping has become much more prevalent, due to the fact that these container ships that can carry many more products overseas in a much more cost effective manner. Container shipping has, indeed, changed delivery methods across the world. The world's largest container ship is now capable of carrying 16,000 shipping containers. Even if you look back into history 25 years, you will find that ships then were only able to carry 1,600 containers.
       </span>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        With the ability to carry so many containers, comes the necessity for methods of delivery of said containers once they reach dock. Some will be transported across the United States, while others will be transported only a few miles. Many different methods of delivery are needed to accommodate such large transcontinental deliveries.
       </span>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
       </span>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        Some of these containers will be loaded onto train cars that wait at the dock.
       </span>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
       </span>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        This is done by a long swinging crane arm that is able to lift the containers off the ship and directly onto the flat train cars. The containers traveling this way generally travel the longest distance.
       </span>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
       </span>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        Container drayage is necessary for boxes that will be traveling shorter distances. They may be transported to warehouses for holding, to flatbed semi-trucks, even to airports to be flown to the needed locations.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        Shipping methods have certainly changed over the last 100 years, but even looking at the last 20 years may make you realize that there is much more to shipping and distribution than you had expected.
       </span>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
       </span>
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        Container drayage is a critical part of this ever expanding industry. Projections for new ocean liners say that in the next few years, the number of containers will reach 18,000 per ship. As these numbers continue to grow, delivery companies will continue to grow and change as well.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       <span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt;">
        <br/>
       </span>
      </p>
