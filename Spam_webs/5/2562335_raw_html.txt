        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         saffron doughnuts with rosewater icing
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-02-08T05:08:38+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 8, 2012
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.spabettie.com/author/kristina/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Kristina Sloggett
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/2012/02/08/saffron-donuts-with-rosewater-icing-2/#comments">
           39 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_3255.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9243" height="500" src="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_3255.jpg" title="saffron rosewater doughnuts" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here I am with
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/2012/02/06/meyer-lemon-mousse-in-chocolate-cups/" target="_blank">
           another pink and yellow dessert
          </a>
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         I have had the flavors of this doughnut in my head for a while. I couldnt wait to taste it, and I have to say, they are as good as I imagined they would be.
        </p>
        <p>
         These will be a unique and decadent Valentines breakfast or dessert option. Well, one of them. Its Valentines Week around here, I cannot limit myself to one pink dessert.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_3262.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9244" height="500" src="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_3262.jpg" title="saffron doughnut rosewater icing" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I love the complex flavor saffron brings, and pairing it with a subtle rosewater is simply meant to be. I love these flavors, I love these doughnuts.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_3240.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9242" height="500" src="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_3240.jpg" title="vegan doughnuts" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          saffron doughnuts
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          dairy, gluten and soy free, vegan
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          makes 6 doughnuts
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1 cup flour (gluten free ap)
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
         </strong>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          pinch sea salt
          <br/>
          1/4 cup coconut milk (carton)
          <br/>
          3 large pinches saffron strands (or 1 teaspoon powdered)
          <br/>
          1/3 cup maple syrup
         </strong>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
          <br/>
          1/4 cup canola oil
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Pour coconut milk into mixing bowl, pinch saffron strands between fingers to break strands, stir into milk. Add maple syrup, vinegar and oil. In separate bowl*, combine flour, baking powder and salt, add to liquid mixture. Fill doughnut pan between 1/2  3/4 full (or use muffin tin). Bake at 375 for 12 minutes or until inserted toothpick comes clean. Allow to cool before icing.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          *the way my mom taught me when I was 5: mix all wet ingredients; add flour on top of wet but
          <em>
           do not mix
          </em>
           add remaining dry ingredients to the flour, mix into just the flour,
          <em>
           then
          </em>
          mix into wet ingredients. Save a bowl!
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          rosewater icing
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2 cups powdered sugar, sifted
          <br/>
          1 1/2 tablespoons* coconut milk
          <br/>
          1 drop
          <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_3283.jpg" target="_blank">
           rosewater essence
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           coloring, optional (I used Seelect Tea  vegan + organic)
          </strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Sift powdered sugar into mixing bowl. Whisk in coconut milk, *adjusting amount for desired consistency. Mix in a drop of rosewater essence and color if using.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_3265.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9245" height="500" src="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_3265.jpg" title="gluten free doughnuts" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>

         Do you cook with saffron? What kinds of dishes do you make with it?
