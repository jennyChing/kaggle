               </span>

            </figure>
           </div>
           <div class="by-line-top hide-desktop">
           </div>
           <p>
            Friends can often be closer than family for us, and carry us through times of trouble. Theyre often our biggest supporters and help us celebrate our most important life events, also. But when a friend dies, it can be difficult to work through the grieving process.
           </p>
           <p>
            While friendships are important to us, their deaths dont receive the same level of acknowledgment and sympathy that some of our other relationships do.
           </p>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="3" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense1">
           </div>
           <div class="radlinks ads-half" data-character-limit="null" data-display-inline="true" data-display-label="false" data-num-links="5" id="radlinks3">
           </div>
           <div data-defer="onPageLoad" id="videoPlaceholderLarge">
           </div>
           <p class="cb-split">
            For instance, we cant necessary get paid time off for the death of a friend like we can for a relative.
           </p>
           <p>
            Heres how to handle the death of your friend so you can honor their influence on your life.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Allow Yourself Time to Grieve, But Dont Isolate
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Some people try to minimize the grief they feel when a friend dies, but this just delays the grieving process. Grief takes time and everyone goes through it a little bit differently, so allow yourself the time to cry and mourn the loss of your friendship. Dont shrug it off or try to talk yourself out of feeling bad.
           </p>
           <p>
            Even if an
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Types_of_Friendships/g/Acquaintance.htm">
             acquaintance
            </a>
            has passed, you might be feeling sad, which is natural. Youll feel a loss even if you werent that close with the person. Any death can make us think about our own lives and mortality, and an acquaintance, or someone you saw frequently and thought of as a casual friend, can still bring on feelings of grief.
           </p>
           <p>
            As you continue to move through the grief process, you might experience feelings of isolation. Not everyone will understand your grief.
           </p>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="2" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense2">
           </div>
           <p class="cb-split">
            Not everyone has a
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="2" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Friendship_Definitions/fl/Difference-Between-a-Friend-and-a-Close-Friend.htm">
             close friend
            </a>
            or even an acquaintance that has touched them in a positive way. While your instinct in this situation might be to pull back and spend more time by yourself, be mindful that youll need to balance your need for solitude and reflection with friend time. This can totally feel wrong when youre grieving the loss of a friend, but you need to reach out at a time like this so you can move forward.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             When Youre Ready, Make an Effort to Meet New People
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Your friend will remain a cherished part of your life always, but when theyre gone you will have to get out and
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="3" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Meeting-New-Friends/tp/How-Can-I-Make-Friends.htm">
             meet new people
            </a>
            . This can be really hard at first, especially if your friend was your
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="4" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Types_of_Friendships/f/Is-It-Normal-Not-To-Have-A-Best-Friend.htm">
             BFF
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Dont feel like you have betrayed your friendship because youre getting out and doing things with people. Even if you find someone else that you immediately click with, it doesnt diminish your deceased friends importance in your life.
           </p>
           <p>
            Force yourself out of your comfort zone little by little with activities and people until you either make a few new friends or reconnect with others. This will help you move on from your friends death in a healthy way.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Do an Event or Get Involved With a Charity That Honors Your Friends Memory
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            A good way to keep your friends memory alive is by doing something to honor them. This can be something like continuing to visit their favorite coffee shop and mentally acknowledging them each time, making a digital picture frame with pictures of the two of you, or donating your time to a charity your friend was involved in. If your friend passed away from a bout with a disease, you could also do a run/walk in their memory.
           </p>
           <p>
            Perhaps the best way to honor your friend is just by remembering the lessons their friendship taught you. People come into our lives for different reasons but we usually learn something from them, even if that lesson is being more open or forgiving. You can be a better friend to someone else, all because you were lucky enough to have a good friend in your life even for a short period of time.
           </p>
