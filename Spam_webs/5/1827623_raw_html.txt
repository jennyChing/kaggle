     <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
      One-Pot Unstuffed Pepper Pasta #OnePotPasta
     </h1>
     <p class="entry-meta">
      <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-27T09:00:46+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
       March 27, 2015
      </time>
      by
      <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
       <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://m.simplystacie.net/author/simplystacienet/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
        <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
         Stacie
        </span>
       </a>
      </span>
      <span class="entry-comments-link">
       <a href="http://m.simplystacie.net/2015/03/one-pot-unstuffed-pepper-pasta/#comments">
        29 Comments
       </a>
      </span>
     </p>
    </header>
    <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
     <p>
      I love taking a familiar recipe and adding my own unique twist to it. I find when Im stuck in a dinner rut, it helps me to come up with new and interesting meals to serve to my family. However, I dont do complicated. I like recipes that have simple ingredients that are easy to find and dont require any special skills or equipment. I guess thats why I make one-pot pastas as often as I do! My pantry always has an ample supply of the base ingredients.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ive teamed up with
      <a href="http://www.ragu.com/recipes/Easy-One-Pot-Pasta" target="_blank">
       Rag
      </a>
      to create some easy and delicious one-pot pasta recipes that you can whip up for your family in under 30 minutes. Earlier this week, I shared my recipe for
      <a href="http://m.simplystacie.net/2015/03/one-pot-chili-rigatoni/" target="_blank">
       One-Pot Chili Rigatoni
      </a>
      and today Ive got another special recipe to share. This one is a twist on a classic recipe  stuffed peppers. Meet my One-Pot Unstuffed Pepper Pasta!
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.simplystacie.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/unstuffed-pepper-pasta-text-2.jpg">
       <img alt="One-Pot Unstuffed Pepper Pasta" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-109424" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://images.mobile.glam.com/images/?/600/100/480/i2.wp.com%2Fwww.simplystacie.net%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2015%2F03%2Funstuffed-pepper-pasta-text-2.jpg%3Fresize%3D736%252C1040"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      The best part about this recipe is that itincorporates all the amazing flavours of thetraditional stuffed peppers recipe. The big difference is, of course, that it doesnt require hardly any effort to prepare it. And there is no stuffing involved whatsoever. Unless you count stuffing your face because its
      <em>
       that
      </em>
      good! I love stuffed peppers, but they are a pain to make. I never make them when Im in a rush. This Unstuffed Pepper Pasta recipe? Perfect for busy nights.
     </p>
     <p>
      The key ingredients in many of my
      <a href="http://www.ragu.com/recipes/Easy-One-Pot-Pasta" target="_blank">
       one-pot pasta recipes
      </a>
      areveggies, pasta, meat and a jar ofRag Sauce. Mix and match these ingredients and throw in a some spices to create a variety of unique dishes.
     </p>
     <p>
      I want to show you just how easy my recipe for Unstuffed Pepper Pasta is. The first step is to brown your ground beef, onions and green peppers in a Dutch oven. Youll be using this same pot to make the entire recipe. I used two green peppers since it is the feature ingredient in the dish. I also add in the spices and Worcestershire sauce after its been cooking for about three minutes.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://i1.wp.com/www.simplystacie.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/unstuffed-pepper-pasta-collage-2-1.jpg">
       <img alt="One-Pot Unstuffed Pepper Pasta" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-109425" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://images.mobile.glam.com/images/?/600/100/480/i1.wp.com%2Fwww.simplystacie.net%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2015%2F03%2Funstuffed-pepper-pasta-collage-2-1.jpg%3Fresize%3D736%252C504"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Once your meat is cooked, then its what I like to call dumping time. Literally just dump your ingredients into your pot and stir.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://i1.wp.com/www.simplystacie.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/unstuffed-pepper-pasta-collage-3-1.jpg">
       <img alt="One-Pot Unstuffed Pepper Pasta" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-109426" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://images.mobile.glam.com/images/?/600/100/480/i1.wp.com%2Fwww.simplystacie.net%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2015%2F03%2Funstuffed-pepper-pasta-collage-3-1.jpg%3Fresize%3D736%252C501"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      It looks like you have a soup here, but dont forget that you need that extra liquid to cook the noodles. I used Ditalini noodles, but you could use any small pasta. Normally rice is used in stuffed peppers, but since this is a one-pot
      <em>
       pasta
      </em>
      version, I wanted to find a small noodle that was somewhat rice-like. Orzo would also work.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.simplystacie.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/unstuffed-pepper-pasta-collage-4-1.jpg">
       <img alt="One-Pot Unstuffed Pepper Pasta" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-109427" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://images.mobile.glam.com/images/?/600/100/480/i0.wp.com%2Fwww.simplystacie.net%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2015%2F03%2Funstuffed-pepper-pasta-collage-4-1.jpg%3Fresize%3D736%252C502"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      See it thickened up! It will thicken up even more if you ended up adding some shredded cheese. I like mine with cheese because Im crazy like that.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.simplystacie.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/unstuffed-pepper-pasta-1.jpg">
       <img alt="One-Pot Unstuffed Pepper Pasta" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-109428" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://images.mobile.glam.com/images/?/600/100/480/i0.wp.com%2Fwww.simplystacie.net%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2015%2F03%2Funstuffed-pepper-pasta-1.jpg%3Fresize%3D736%252C1026"/>
      </a>
      <br/>
     </p>

        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 cups Ditalini pasta (or other small pasta like orzo)
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 green peppers, chopped
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 onion, chopped
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 jar Rag Old World Style Traditional Sauce
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          tsp garlic powder
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 tsp Italian seasoning
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 cups beef broth
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 cups cheddar cheese, shredded (optional)
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 green onions, chopped, for garnish (optional)
        </li>
       </ul>
       <div class="ERSClear">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="ERSInstructions">
       <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
        Instructions
       </div>
       <ol>
        <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         Add ground beef, onions and green pepper to a Dutch oven over medium high heat. Stir to break up the beef.
        </li>
        <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         Cook for about 3 minutes and then add in the spices and Worcestershire sauce. Continue cooking until beef is browned, about 5 minutes.
        </li>
        <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         Stir in Ragu Old World Style Traditional Sauce and beef broth. Bring to a boil.
        </li>
        <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         Mix in Ditalini pasta and reduce heat to medium. Cover, cook for about 12 to 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until pasta is tender.
        </li>
        <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         Remove from heat and add in shredded cheese, if using. Serve immediately. Garnish with green onions, if desired.
        </li>
       </ol>
       <div class="ERSClear">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="endeasyrecipe" style="" title="style002">
       3.2.2925
      </div>
     </div>
     <br/>

     </a>
     <p>
      Dress up the bowls with a little bit of chopped green onion and shredded cheese, if you like. I think it tastes even better than a stuffed pepper and its 100 times easier to make. Plus the clean-up afterwards? Easy-peasy. Like I said one-pot. This is the best kind of dinner to make when time
      <em>
       isnt
      </em>
      on your side.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.simplystacie.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/unstuffed-pepper-pasta-text-3.jpg">

      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      If you have a garden in your yard, here is a recipe to try to use up your fresh green peppers.
     </p>

       <img alt="One-Pot Unstuffed Pepper Pasta" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-109431" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://images.mobile.glam.com/images/?/600/100/480/i2.wp.com%2Fwww.simplystacie.net%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2015%2F03%2Funstuffed-pepper-pasta-11.jpg%3Fresize%3D736%252C1055"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      I think this recipe comes pretty close to capturing the mouthwatering flavours of a stuffed pepper. Its one that I will be making againespecially after seeing how much my family enjoyed eating it!
