            <div class="entry-content cf">
             <p>
              <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/mSuObDM5i3E" width="560">
              </iframe>
             </p>
             <p class="x_MsoNormal">
              <img alt="CtD-Logo2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-148038" height="292" src="http://www.pbs.org/newshour/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/CtD-Logo21.jpg" width="1000"/>
             </p>
             <p class="x_MsoNormal">
              According to the Urban Institute in Washington, D.C., the number of people living in high-poverty areas in the U.S. has risen since the year 2000 by almost 80 percent.
              <br/>
              This trend has lead to an increase inimpoverishedclusters, neighborhoods where poverty is endemic and upward mobility and economic opportunity is increasingly out of reach for many community residents.
             </p>
             <div class="nhlinkbox related-content alignright">
              <div class="nhlinkbox-head">
               RELATED CONTENT
              </div>
              <div class="nhlinkbox-links">
               <ul>
                <li>
                 <a href="http://www.pbs.org/newshour/updates/buffett-people-living-poverty-suffer-american-nightmare/">
                  Buffett: People living in poverty suffer from the 'American Nightmare'
                 </a>
                </li>
                <li>
                 <a href="http://www.pbs.org/newshour/bb/can-u-s-break-clusters-poverty-roiling-south/">
                  Can 'neighborhoods of last resort' be lifted out of poverty?
                 </a>
                </li>
               </ul>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p class="x_MsoNormal">
              One such area is the Lakewood Heights community in Atlanta, Georgia, where crime rates are high and many homes sit dilapidated and abandoned. Here, the poverty rate hovers around 30 percent.
             </p>
             <p class="x_MsoNormal">
              NewsHour Weekend toured the neighborhood with community organizers Tina Arnold, the executive director of
              <a href="http://www.sustainablelakewood.org/">
               Sustainable Lakewood
              </a>
              , and Rev. Houston Wheeler, who has worked in urban development in Atlanta since the 1970s.
             </p>
             <p>
              You have some $300,000 homes, but you also have some homes for $10,000, Arnold said. If youre making eight dollars an hour and only getting 24 hours a week, I mean, where can you live?
             </p>
