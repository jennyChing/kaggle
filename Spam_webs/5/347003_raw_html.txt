            <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="4">
              The praise that The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim has garnered is well earned. The whole Elder Scroll series, since Morrowind, hasset such a high standard that nothing can really compare, and most other open world games cant stand up in comparison, interms of immersion and atmosphere. Grand Theft Auto V is a great open world game, but it is an empty land of nothing comparedto Skyrim.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              This also means that there is so much to see and do that no single person can find it all on their own. I've looked atnumerous lists like this one, as well as putting in over 500 hours in the game, and I still found new things when researchingthis article. Maybe the most interesting thing about Skyrim is that, even after putting so much time into it, spending a fewmonths away from it makes the game fresh and interesting once again.Enjoy.
             </font>

            <h2 class="wsite-content-title" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="5">
              1.The Wild and Domestic Mammoth
             </font>
            </h2>
            <div>
             <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-hairline wsite-image-border-black" style="padding-top:0;padding-bottom:15px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
              <a>
               <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/3/4/2/43422831/5445519.jpg?751" style="width:751;max-width:100%"/>
              </a>
              <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="4">
              The frost giants of Skyrim will gladly pound your skull to jelly to protect their precious mammoths, and until you are around20th level or so, thats what will happen if you try to go toe to toe with one of these brutes. One good way, to avoid one of these fights, is to look at the tusks of the mammoths. The Giants will carve designs in the mammoths ivory, marking it astheirs, but wild mammoths won't have these markings and are fair game. Its one of those wonderful little details that alsohas a practical use.
             </font>
             <br/>
             <br/>
            </div>
            <h2 class="wsite-content-title" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="5">
              2. Ants!
             </font>
            </h2>
            <div>
             <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-hairline wsite-image-border-black" style="padding-top:0;padding-bottom:15px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
              <a>
               <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/3/4/2/43422831/1425144.jpg?751" style="width:751;max-width:100%"/>
              </a>
              <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="4">
              Nothing special other than being able to marvel at the insane level of detail that Bethesda has put in this game. If you lookat certain parts in the southern part of Skyrim, most notably tree stumps, you will see ants hard at work. Its just neat,but a few giant ants trying to eat me would have been neater.
             </font>
             <br/>
             <br/>
            </div>
            <h2 class="wsite-content-title" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="5">
              3. Screw the Dark Brotherhood
             </font>
            </h2>
            <div>
             <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-hairline wsite-image-border-black" style="padding-top:0;padding-bottom:15px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
              <a>
               <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/3/4/2/43422831/9091425.jpg?751" style="width:751;max-width:100%"/>
              </a>
              <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="4">
              Part of what makes Skyrim fun is the little murder fest it becomes at times. And if you kill enough people, the DarkBrotherhood will eventually take notice of your bloody efforts, and invite you to join in an offer you can't refuse, sortof way. Maybe your character is a bloodthirsty warrior but feels that outright assassination just isn't honorable.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Or maybeyou just don't like being kidnapped and waking up in a strange cabin. As with so many things in this game, you have a choice.When you first wake up in the cabin with the speaker, instead of killing the three prisoners, you can kill her and theneradicate the last of the Dark Brotherhood. You wont get nearly as much awesome Dark Brotherhood loot and will miss out onShadowmere, but you will have the honor and satisfaction of eradicating one of the vilest organizations to ever plagueTamriel.
             </font>
             <br/>
             <br/>
            </div>
            <h2 class="wsite-content-title" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="5">
              4.Character Creation
             </font>
            </h2>
            <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="4">
              While literally impossible to miss, the flexibility of just what you can do beyond an in-game avatar is interesting. Trymaking celebrities, the weirdest looking being you can imagine, or your favorite pet lizard or horrid evil creature (cat).You can even use it to show the rest of your tabletop group what your D&amp;D character looks like. And the mods on the PC makeit even crazier.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Bonus: You can save right at the point right before character creation so you dont have to go through the wagon ride everytime.
             </font>
             <br/>
             <br/>

            <h2 class="wsite-content-title" style="text-align:left;">
             5.Life After Death -
             <font size="4">
              or Sovngarde You Rather, for Ulfric Stormcloak
             </font>
            </h2>
            <div>
             <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-hairline wsite-image-border-black" style="padding-top:0;padding-bottom:15px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
              <a>
               <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/4/3/4/2/43422831/5529128.jpg?751" style="width:751;max-width:100%"/>
              </a>
              <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
             <font size="4">
              If the player chooses to side with the Imperials in the civil war, as long as they can forgive and forget the whole businessof trying to cut off your head, they will eventually face Ulfric Stormcloak in battle. That may not be the last you see ofhim, however. If you finish the civil war quest, and then go on to finish the main quest, you will see Ulfrics lost spirit inSovngarde. He is a little humbled by what has happenedand has gained some insight into the war he brought to the land and thedragons.
             </font>
