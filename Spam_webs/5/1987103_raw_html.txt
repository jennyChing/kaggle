         <h1>
          Google Plus and its SEO effects
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta">
          Jul 14, 2011 |
          <a href="http://www.zagoumenov.com/blog/category/seo/" rel="category tag">
           SEO
          </a>
          |
          <span class="comments-number">
           <a href="http://www.zagoumenov.com/blog/google-plus-seo-effects/#respond">
            <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="5465 http://www.zagoumenov.com/?p=5465">
             2 comments
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <img alt="Google Plus and its SEO effects" height="675" src="http://www.zagoumenov.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/gplus-zagoumenov-seo.jpg" width="1080"/>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p>
           My take is that
           <a href="https://plus.google.com/114809804180092218128/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="My Google Plus">
            Google+
           </a>
           is not a new social network. We have an existing network already, G+ and Facebook are just tools we choose to use to connect, share with and expand our existing networks.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-5465">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           IMHO, Google just took the most important components of social interaction on the web (removing all theunnecessarythings) and created this easy to use, simple tool such as G+. And for this I have immense respect for the team at Google.
          </p>
          <p>
           Having G+ account helps from SEO standpoint in the following simple ways:
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            As a rule of thumb, if you want to have a close relationship with a search engine, use its products. G+ is Googles product, so as Reader, Alterts, Gmail, etc. Having a G+ account ensures that Google has the most complete info about you, your brand, business. This helps Google understand who you are and rank higher for relevant searches.
           </li>
           <li>
            With G+ you get a new search property that ranks high in Google because its Gs product. Therefore having a
            <a href="https://plus.google.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Google Plus Profile">
             Google Plus profile
            </a>
            helps your branded search too. Please note that currently your Google Profile redirects to your G+ Profile if you have one.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           Currently my G+ profile does not rank #1 in Google for my branded search. But my assumpion is that it will. So, the recommendation is to ensure your Google+ profile is clean, informative and points people to all the right places (other things you control, i.e. your website or blog).
          </p>
          <p>
           Heres a few important things to get your Google Plus profile right for
           <a href="http://www.zagoumenov.com/services/" rel="nofollow" title="SEO services">
            SEO
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Description of your business (which helps Google understand you and you to rank higher for relevant terms). therefore ensure your description includes your keywords too.
           </li>
           <li>
            Link to your site / blog, etc. Every G+ profile has a list of related links to your brand / business. Each of those links lead to your other properties transfering a certain amount of Google Juice!
           </li>
           <li>
            Keeping your posts up to date and public ensures that your G+ profile produces content on regular basis. Google loves content.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Valuable update:
           </strong>
           Just noticed a good presentation on the subject of Google+ and its effects on SEO and wanted to share it with you. Its a rather lengthy one but fun and provides good details.
          </p>
