               <h2 class="title" itemprop="name">
                diy mini pallet magnets
               </h2>
               <h4 class="date-header">
                Tuesday, February 18, 2014 |
                <span class="post-author vcard">
                 <span class="fn" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
                  <span itemprop="name">
                   Marisa
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </span>
               </h4>
               <div class="entry" id="post-body-5918017564994520378" itemprop="articleBody">
                <p>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </p>
                <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-wR4zxeKbdQ8/Uv6LLUkAYyI/AAAAAAAABQc/WYKTuMJbgV4/s1600/diy+mini+pallet+magnets.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                  <img alt="" border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-wR4zxeKbdQ8/Uv6LLUkAYyI/AAAAAAAABQc/WYKTuMJbgV4/s1600/diy+mini+pallet+magnets.jpg" title="DIY mini pallet magnets made from popsicle sticks-click for easy directions!" width="528"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                So
                <a href="http://www.cookinglikelou.com/2014/01/diy-mini-pallet-coasters.html">
                 these
                </a>
                mini pallet coasters were just so popular, I decided to try another project with them! That and I still had a bunch of popsicle sticks and wood stain left over and this project takes no time at all.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-QyXoJam8NFE/Uv6LahDKdxI/AAAAAAAABRI/7I4QGNS49MQ/s1600/DSC00784.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                  <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-QyXoJam8NFE/Uv6LahDKdxI/AAAAAAAABRI/7I4QGNS49MQ/s1600/DSC00784.jpg" width="265"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-tDm6X9xgDbI/UvzouTt6iBI/AAAAAAAABQE/dB0Nb9CM7ts/s1600/pencil+sharpener+bathroom.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="200" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-tDm6X9xgDbI/UvzouTt6iBI/AAAAAAAABQE/dB0Nb9CM7ts/s1600/pencil+sharpener+bathroom.jpg" width="187"/>
                </a>
                I love them on my little retro fridge, which is in my room and has a great "Mad Men" vibe about it, but it also slowly freezes everything I try to put in there. So basically it's good for beer for no more than 12 hours (not much worse than frozen beer), freezing Snickers bars (yum), and as a magnetic board. It's probably more functional than the quirks of my last apartment---I had a pencil sharpener in my bathroom...
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h2>
                </h2>
                <h2>
                 <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                  DIY Mini Pallet Magnets
                 </span>
                </h2>
                <div>
                </div>
                <h3>
                 what you need:
                </h3>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  popsicle sticks
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  sharp scissors
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  sponge brush
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  wood stain
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  cloth (I just used a paper towel)
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  gorilla/crazy glue
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  adhesive magnet strip or old flat magnets (think like an old sports team schedule)
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-O3MEdsGfjm8/Uv6LLa9jR1I/AAAAAAAABQY/Lq8JtjuBU7E/s1600/diy+magnet+pallets.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                  <img border="0" height="400" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-O3MEdsGfjm8/Uv6LLa9jR1I/AAAAAAAABQY/Lq8JtjuBU7E/s1600/diy+magnet+pallets.jpg" width="102"/>
                 </a>
                 <br/>
                 <h3>
                  the how-to:
                 </h3>
                 <div>
                  <br/>
                 </div>
                 <div>
                  1. Discarding the ends of the popsicle sticks, cut sticks into 2-inch pieces. Cut the ends off of the popsicle sticks using very sharp scissors. This is definitely the hardest and least fun part of the project.
                 </div>
                 <div>
                  <br/>
                 </div>
                 <div>
                  2. Coat the sticks with wood stain, wipe with cloth per the directions for the stain and let dry. I used 1 coat of Martha Stewart wood stain.
                 </div>
                 <div>
                  <br/>
                 </div>
                 <div>
                  3. Once the sticks are dry, arrange like the second photo in the strip above. Use gorilla/crazy glue to attach the sides and middle "beams?" I don't know the terminology.
                 </div>
                 <div>
                  <br/>
                 </div>
                 <div>
                  4. Make sure you are holding the sticks down to allow the glue to dry, probably about a minute for each stick.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                 </div>
                 <div>
                  5. Add the top row of sticks and make sure the glue is dry. (See third pic)
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                 </div>
                 <div>
                 </div>
                 <div>
                  <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-SWI9WKnIF_o/UwUL7VYywuI/AAAAAAAABSg/ctqOjKojxYU/s1600/mini+pallet+magnets+2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" height="204" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-SWI9WKnIF_o/UwUL7VYywuI/AAAAAAAABSg/ctqOjKojxYU/s1600/mini+pallet+magnets+2.jpg" width="320"/>
                  </a>
                  6. Attach magnet strips with self-adhesive or glue. Feel free to trim some of the sides if your sticks aren't very even. Perfection is overrated. :) Now you're done!
                 </div>
                 <div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <br/>

                 <img alt="" border="0" height="522" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-pomcGz9z-Qk/UwPo7ItCLLI/AAAAAAAABSQ/VCV9TRIy0Lk/s1600/diy+mini+pallet+magnets.jpg" title="DIY mini pallet magnets made from popsicle sticks-click for easy directions!" width="640"/>
