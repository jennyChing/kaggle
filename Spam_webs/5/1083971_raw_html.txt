                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Los Angeles street sign hacked to say read a F&amp;*$#@ book
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3531044712332753211" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       I wasnt planning on reading a book today, but then I saw
this sign.
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-0BH6rq08-Bw/VLCI4gcWj8I/AAAAAAAADJE/QzufmCKKObU/s1600/read-a-fucking-book.PNG" imageanchor="1">
                        <img alt="los angeles road sign hacked read a fucking book" border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-0BH6rq08-Bw/VLCI4gcWj8I/AAAAAAAADJE/QzufmCKKObU/s1600/read-a-fucking-book.PNG"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       May I just say, I love any initiative that encourages people
to read books and the fact that this was orchestrated by hackers only makes me
love this one even more. I dont know how they Hacked this electronic road
sign system, all I know is that this is great.
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       Just think of all the motorists that are now rekindling
their love affair with the ancient art of storytelling, mysteries and wonder.
