        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          6 Popular Types of Yoga
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Yoga is far more than a sport. Yoga is an ancient physical and mental discipline. Yoga encompasses meditative and physical practices rooted in ancient Hindu, Buddhist and Jain teachings.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          The term Yoga is an ancient Sanskrit word whose ultimate definition is still a topic of debate amongst linguists, philosophers, religious leaders and practitioners. This is mainly due to the fact that the Sanskrit word Yoga has many meanings including to control, to unite and  to contemplate.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Recently, in the western world, all types of Yoga are becoming more and more popular amongst men and women of all age groups. The number of Yoga studios offering a variety of different types of Yoga classes is on the rise. Since many of these studios offer such Yoga classes as Hatha Yoga, Ashtanga Yoga, Power Yoga, Lyengar Yoga, Bikram Yoga, Vinyasa Yoga and many more it is hard to decide which types of Yoga are right for you.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          This article will help you understand the differences between the most popular types of Yoga so that you can choose which types of Yoga are right for you.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.theundergroundbootcamp.com/fitness/yoga-exercises-for-a-post-pregnancy-flat-stomach-part-ii/" target="_blank">
          Yoga: For a Post-Pregnancy Flat Stomach
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Hatha Yoga
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Hatha Yoga is one of the most popular types of Yoga. In fact, in the West, the term Yoga is typically associated with Hatha Yoga. In Sanskrit, Ha means sun and tha means moon. So, as youve most likely guessed Hatha Yoga aims to unite the mind, body and spirit. This type of Yoga is relatively slow paced and gentle and is a good place to start if you are completely new to Yoga and dont know any of the asanas (poses).
         </span>
        </p>

        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Ashtanga Yoga
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          The term Ashtanga means eight limbs in Sanskrit. Ashtanga Yoga is a fast moving, intense type of Yoga which contains a progressive set of asanas, synchronized with the breath. Ashtanga Yoga can be physically demanding as you constantly move from one asana in the sequence to the next. Therefore, this type of Yoga is highly effective at helping you improve your stamina as well as your flexibility and strength.
         </span>

        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Power Yoga
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Power Yoga is a western interpretation of Yoga and is based on Ashtanga Yoga. A Power Yoga class may not necessarily stick to an exact sequence of poses like Ashtanga Yoga does, but it does involve practicing a series of poses without stopping and starting.
         </span>

        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Lyengar Yoga
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Lyengar Yoga is based on the teachings of B.K.S Lyengar. Lyengar Yoga focuses on the development of strength, stamina, flexibility and balance as well as concentration and meditation. The asanas of this type of Hatha Yoga focus on the proper alignment of the body. Unlike Ashtanga Yoga, there is an emphasis on holding each pose for a long period of time rather than moving constantly from one pose to the next. Lyengar Yoga uses props such as blocks and straps to help align the body into the different poses.
         </span>
        </p>

        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Vinyasa Yoga
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Vinyasa means breath synchronized movement. Vinyasa Yoga is another fast paced type of Yoga, which places an emphasis on proper breathing technique. A practice typically starts with sun salutations and moves on to more intense stretching. Throughout the practice each pose is balanced with a counter pose.

        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Bikram Yoga
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Bikram Yoga, also known as Hot Yoga, is practiced in a room heated to 105 degrees, with a humidity of around 40%. Generally a sequence of 26 different poses is practiced during any given 90 minute Bikram Yoga class session. The hot temperature helps to loosen muscles, reduce tension and help prevent injuries. Due to the high temperature participants sweat a lot during the class. This sweating is believed to help cleanse the body of toxins and facilitate weight loss.
         </span>
        </p>
