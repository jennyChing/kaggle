     <p>
      <b>
       The Wailin' Jennys
      </b>
      are a Canadian music group. They have released several albums and received a
      <a href="/wiki/Juno_Award" title="Juno Award">
       Juno Award
      </a>
      . The group has been featured on the
      <a href="/wiki/American_Public_Media" title="American Public Media">
       American Public Media
      </a>
      program
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/A_Prairie_Home_Companion" title="A Prairie Home Companion">
        A Prairie Home Companion
       </a>
      </i>
      and their album
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Firecracker_(The_Wailin%27_Jennys)" title="Firecracker (The Wailin' Jennys)">
        Firecracker
       </a>
      </i>
      peaked at number two on the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">
        Billboard
       </a>
      </i>
      <a href="/wiki/Bluegrass_music" title="Bluegrass music">
       Bluegrass
      </a>
      charts, in 2006.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wailin%27_Jennys&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The group was founded in 2002, when a
      <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg" title="Winnipeg">
       Winnipeg
      </a>
      guitar shop called Sled Dog Music brought Ruth Moody, Nicky Mehta and
      <a href="/wiki/Cara_Luft" title="Cara Luft">
       Cara Luft
      </a>
      together for a joint performance. The show was well received and the owner scheduled a follow-up performance
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sled_Dog_Music_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Sled_Dog_Music-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and suggested they "go on tour and call themselves the Wailin' Jennys."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Prairie_Home_Companion_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-A_Prairie_Home_Companion-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The group's name is a
      <a href="/wiki/Pun" title="Pun">
       pun
      </a>
      on the country singer
      <a href="/wiki/Waylon_Jennings" title="Waylon Jennings">
       Waylon Jennings
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The group now consists of soprano
      <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Moody" title="Ruth Moody">
       Ruth Moody
      </a>
      , mezzo
      <a href="/wiki/Nicky_Mehta" title="Nicky Mehta">
       Nicky Mehta
      </a>
      and alto
      <a href="/wiki/Heather_Masse" title="Heather Masse">
       Heather Masse
      </a>
      . In previous years, the Jennys have also toured with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiddler" title="Fiddler">
       fiddler
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mandolinist" title="Mandolinist">
       mandolinist
      </a>
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremy_Penner&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jeremy Penner (page does not exist)">
       Jeremy Penner
      </a>
      , who is from Ruth's former band,
      <a href="/wiki/Scruj_MacDuhk" title="Scruj MacDuhk">
       Scruj MacDuhk
      </a>
      . Other band members have included
      <a href="/wiki/Annabelle_Chvostek" title="Annabelle Chvostek">
       Annabelle Chvostek
      </a>
      , a singer/songwriter from
      <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">
       Montreal
      </a>
      and Ruth Moody's brother Richard on viola and mandolin. Both Penner and Moody have appeared on The Wailin' Jennys' albums. In 2007, Chvostek left the group and was replaced by
      <a href="/wiki/Heather_Masse" title="Heather Masse">
       Heather Masse
      </a>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">
       Maine
      </a>
      -born singer and member of the band
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Heather_%26_the_Barbarians&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Heather &amp; the Barbarians (page does not exist)">
       Heather &amp; the Barbarians
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The Wailin' Jennys have won two
      <a href="/wiki/Juno_Award" title="Juno Award">
       Juno Awards
      </a>
      for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juno_Award_for_Roots_%26_Traditional_Album_of_the_Year_-_Group" title="Juno Award for Roots &amp; Traditional Album of the Year - Group">
       Roots and Traditional Album of the Year (Group)
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Juno_Awards_of_2005" title="Juno Awards of 2005">
       2005
      </a>
      for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/40_Days" title="40 Days">
        40 Days
       </a>
      </i>
      and in
      <a href="/wiki/Juno_Awards_of_2012" title="Juno Awards of 2012">
       2012
      </a>
      for
      <i>
       Bright Morning Stars
      </i>
      . They were also nominated for the same award for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Firecracker_(The_Wailin%27_Jennys_album)" title="Firecracker (The Wailin' Jennys album)">
        Firecracker
       </a>
      </i>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Juno_Awards_of_2007" title="Juno Awards of 2007">
       2007
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
