                  <span>
                   Thursday, July 15, 2010
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry">
                    <a name="2609170509620002510">
                    </a>
                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://www.mommyskitchen.net/2010/07/homemade-dulce-de-leche-ice-cream.html">
                      Dulce De Leche Ice Cream + DIY Crock Pot Dulce de Leche
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     <div align="center">
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TD0Yge2XAgI/AAAAAAAAKiA/0J7xfyH0VkQ/s1600/002mk.jpg">
                        <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5493574066777817602" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TD0Yge2XAgI/AAAAAAAAKiA/0J7xfyH0VkQ/s400/002mk.jpg" style="display: block; height: 267px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                       </a>
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        Last weekend I wanted to try my hand at
                        <i style="color: #cc0000;">
                         <b>
                          <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                           Homemade Dulce de leche
                          </span>
                         </b>
                        </i>
                        . I was a little bit intimidated by the stove top method. Making Dulce de leche on the stove consisted of cooking the cans in a pan of hot water for several hours.
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        This made me a bit nervous because I kept thinking the cans would get to hot and burst. And then I would have sweetened condensed milk all over my kitchen. I
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;">
                        'm sure this process works, but it just wasn't for me. Instead I chose a slow cooker method which I found on
                       </span>
                       <a href="http://crockpot365.blogspot.com/2008/03/dulce-de-leche-crockpot-recipe.html" style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;" target="_blank">
                        <i>
                         A Year of Slow cooking
                        </i>
                       </a>
                       <span style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;">
                        .
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       </span>
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TEDz01kC0wI/AAAAAAAAKj4/5hWmaDnyqY8/s1600/005.JPG">
                        <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5494659634449011458" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TEDz01kC0wI/AAAAAAAAKj4/5hWmaDnyqY8/s400/005.JPG" style="cursor: hand; display: block; height: 267px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span id="fullpost">
                       </span>
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        Making homemade Dulce de leche out of sweetened condensed milk is genius!
                       </span>
                       <span id="fullpost">
                        <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                         I'm so glad that I finally got around to trying the slow cooker method, because it really was easy. I know you can purchase canned Dulce de Leche in the grocery store, but wheres the fun in that?
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span id="fullpost">
                        <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        </span>
                       </span>
                       <span id="fullpost">
                        <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                         The whole process is pretty straight forward and the hardest  part of the whole thing is waiting for those cans to cool down so you  can open them. Of course when I did open up a can of the Dulce de Leche  and got my first taste It was pure heaven. All that creamy yummy caramel  goodness I was hooked.
                         <br/>
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span id="fullpost">
                        <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                         After tasting the DIY version the next thought that popped into my mind  was I gotta make something spectacular with this stuff! The kids wanted to use it for apple dipping, b
                        </span>
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;">
                        ut I wanted to use it to make
                        <b>
                         <i>
                          <span style="color: #990000;">
                           Homemade Dulce de Leche Ice Cream
                          </span>
                         </i>
                        </b>
                        .
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;">
                        I have been wanting for the perfect opportunity to make this recipe, so I thought today would be only fitting.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span id="fullpost">
                        <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TD0XhAiG00I/AAAAAAAAKhw/DyRrW2-RH1Y/s1600/005mk.jpg">
                         <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5493572976308048706" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TD0XhAiG00I/AAAAAAAAKhw/DyRrW2-RH1Y/s400/005mk.jpg" style="display: block; height: 267px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                        </a>
                        <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                         I found a recipe on
                         <a href="http://smittenkitchen.com/2008/05/dulce-de-leche-ice-cream/" target="_blank">
                          <i>
                           Smitten Kitchen
                          </i>
                         </a>
                         and adapted the recipe to accommodate my 4 quart ice cream maker. I increased the whole milk and the whipping cream but I also added half and half to the recipe.
                         <br/>
                         <br/>
                         If you want the original recipe which make 1 1/2 quarts click on the link at the end of the post. Now keep in mind this recipe is not diet friendly with all the cream added and topit off by adding dulce de leche and that's a deadly combination. I toasted some pecans and added it to the ice cream and it was so good.
                        </span>
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        Well, actually out of this world great. I cant wait to make this ice cream again.
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;">
                        I have included the directions on how you can make your own homemade Dulce de leche along with the recipe at the end of this post. I hope you'll give both of these recipes a try,because they're definitely keepers!
                       </span>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center">
                      <br/>
                      <div align="center">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TD0XgLLCr9I/AAAAAAAAKhY/85HRY5JvNYc/s1600/Picnik+collage.jpg">
                        <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5493572961984229330" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TD0XgLLCr9I/AAAAAAAAKhY/85HRY5JvNYc/s400/Picnik+collage.jpg" style="cursor: hand; display: block; height: 293px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style="font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        Step by step photos of how to make dulce de leche in your crock pot
                       </span>
                       .
                       <span style="font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        (recipe below)
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: start;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        <span style="color: #cc0000;">
                         <b>
                          Crock Pot Dulce De Leche
                         </b>
                        </span>
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        2 - 14 oz cans sweetened condensed milk
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        plenty of water
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        crock pot
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        Remove the label from the cans. Put cans in the bottom of your crock pot. I placed dish cloth at the bottom of my crock pot to prevent rusting before placing the cans on top of the cloth. Add enough water to completely cover the cans about 2 inches.
                        <br/>
                        <br/>
                        Cover and cook on low for 8 hours. When done using tongs carefully remove the cans and place on a covered counter and let cool until the cans come to room temperature. When cooled you can open the cans.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div align="center">
                       <div style="text-align: left;">
                        <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                         <span style="color: #cc0000;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </span>
                       </div>
                       <div style="text-align: left;">
                        <!-- Begin ZipList Button Code -->
                       </div>
                      </div>
                     </div>

                      <span style="color: #993399;">
                       <b>
                        <a class="small-butn-link" href="javascript:getZRecipe(document.getElementById('zlr-elem'),%20'mommyskitchen',%20'');" id="zlr-elem" title="Add this recipe to your ZipList, where you can store all of your favorite web recipes in one place and easily add ingredients to your shopping list.">
                        </a>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <!-- End ZipList Button Code -->
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TD0XgjSHyRI/AAAAAAAAKho/MidbXSK7dj0/s1600/Picnik+collage.jpg">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5493572968456374546" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gVe56iUjJEg/TD0XgjSHyRI/AAAAAAAAKho/MidbXSK7dj0/s400/Picnik+collage.jpg" style="cursor: hand; display: block; height: 289px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                      To make the ice cream bring the milk and cream just to a boil in a 3-quart heavy saucepan over moderate heat, then remove from heat and whisk in Dulce de leche until dissolved. Whisk in vanilla and transfer to a metal bowl. I left mine in the same pot and did the quick chill.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                      Quick-chill by putting bowl or pot in a larger bowl of ice and cold water and stirring occasionally until cold, 15 to 20 minutes. Add the mixture to your ice cream canister, then add enough milk until the mixture hits the fill line on the canister. Proceed with you manufacturer directions for making ice cream.
                     </span>

                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       3 - cups milk
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       2 - cups half &amp; half or fat free half &amp; half
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       1 - cup heavy cream
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       2 - 14 oz cans dulce de leche (found in the latin isle or homemade)
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       1/2- teaspoon pure vanilla extract
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       3/4 - 1 - cup chopped toasted pecans
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       additional milk - enough to hit the
                       <b>
                        'fill Line'
                       </b>
                       of your ice cream maker
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       crushed ice &amp; rock salt, for the ice cream pail (if needed)
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Bring milk and cream just to a boil in a 3-quart heavy saucepan over moderate heat, then remove from heat and whisk in dulce de leche until dissolved.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Whisk in vanilla and transfer to a metal bowl. I left mine in the same pot and did the quick chill. Quick-chill by putting bowl or pot in a larger bowl of ice and cold water and stirring occasionally until cold, 15 to 20 minutes.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Add the mixture to your ice cream canister, (add enough milk until it reaches the fill line in your ice cream maker).
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Add the paddle and place the lid on the canister. Place the whole canister into the freezer pail making sure to center the canister in the pail.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Add some ice to hold the canister in place and then attach the motor. Start your ice cream maker and begin packing the space around the canister with ice and rock salt.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Continue adding ice and salt until it reaches the top, but do not cover the top of the ice cream canister.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Freezing can take anywhere from 20-30 minutes, so add more ice and salt as needed. You will know when the ice cream is done because the ice cream maker will slow down and then stop when finished.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Open canister and remove the paddle. Fold in pecans if using then transfer ice cream to an airtight container and put in freezer to harden, at least 1 hour.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-size: small;">
                       <i>
                        <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                         <b>
                          Yields
                         </b>
                         - 4 quarts ice cream
                        </span>
                       </i>
                      </span>
                     </div>
