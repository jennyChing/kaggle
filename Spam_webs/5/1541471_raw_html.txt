          <div class="mk-text-block " id="mk-text-block-546" style="text-align: left;">
           <h1>
            <strong>
             Would you like to know where to drink the best beer in Barcelona? Want to learn about beer culture in Spain? We bring you a true brewmaster to tell you about these secrets and more. And dont miss the end of this article where we give you basic Spanish to order your beer in Spain. Go on Reading
            </strong>
           </h1>
           <p>
            There are several people who love their work and love beer, but for Alex Clemente his work is BEER! This chemical engineer has the maximum responsibility of beer production in the old industrial brewery of Cerveses Moritz, in the center of Barcelona. He is one of the most knowledgeable people about the art of brewery in Spain.
           </p>
           <p>
            You can contact him at
            <a href="mailto:alex.clemente.b@gmail.com">
             alex.clemente.b@gmail.com
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Spaniards are heavy consumers of beer and in the past years we have observed a revolution in the beer sector with an increase interest in international beers, looking for more quality and new trends for producing and drinking handcrafted beer.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_641" style="width: 802px">
            <img alt="Alex Clemente brewing" class="wp-image-641 size-full" height="1056" src="http://www.spainforreal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/IMG_19700320_084346-e1406804082844.jpg" width="792"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Alex Clemente brewing
            </p>
           </div>
           <ol>
            <li>
             <strong>
              What are the best places to drink a beer in Barcelona?
             </strong>
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            The
            <strong>
             Biercab
            </strong>
            <a href="http://biercab.com/en/">
             http://biercab.com/en/
            </a>
            because they have a wide selection of draught beers in Barcelona.
           </p>
           <p>
            La
            <strong>
             cerveteca
            </strong>
            <a href="http://www.lacerveteca.com/">
             http://www.lacerveteca.com/
            </a>
            , because they have a good selection of imported beers
           </p>
           <p>
            And for sure the
            <strong>
             handcrafted and non-pasteurized beer made at the microbrewery in Moritz
            </strong>
            is delicious
            <a href="http://moritz.com/en/section/fbrica-moritz-barcelona">
             http://moritz.com/en/section/fbrica-moritz-barcelona
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Sa Cava in Sants district also has a great selection of imported beers although none of them are handcrafted. And it is also a great place for eating.
            <a href="https://plus.google.com/106236224431984538717/about?gl=es&amp;hl=es">
             https://plus.google.com/106236224431984538717/about?gl=es&amp;hl=es
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            And finally the Homo Sibaris in the same square as Sa Cava is a great place for drinking beer:
            <a href="http://www.homosibaris.com/">
             http://www.homosibaris.com/
            </a>
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_642" style="width: 802px">
            <img alt="the brewmaster and the beer student in Sa Cava, Barcelona" class="wp-image-642 size-full" height="594" src="http://www.spainforreal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/20140701_215244-e1406804091955.jpg" width="792"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             the brewmaster and the beer student in Sa Cava, Barcelona
            </p>
           </div>
           <ol start="2">
            <li>
             <strong>
              Any other great places to drink beer in Spain?
             </strong>
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            I love Naturebeer in Madrid:
            <a href="http://www.naturbier.com/">
             http://www.naturbier.com/
            </a>
           </p>
           <ol start="3">
            <li>
             <strong>
              In past years, Spain handcrafted beers have become increasingly popular, but how do you define what a handcrafted beer is?
             </strong>
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            There is a lot of confusion about this matter. People think that an ecological beer is handcrafted or that it is the absence of technology that makes it handcrafted, but thats not correct.
           </p>
           <p>
            The essential ingredients of a beer are water, cereals (usually malted), hop and yeast.
            <strong>
             A handcrafted beer is by definition one that doesnt have any additives.
            </strong>
            The additives enable to accelerate the process taking down the cooking time from 8 hours to only 3 hours.
           </p>
           <p>
            Besides, a handcrafted beer is usually not pasteurized, however, a beer can be non-pasteurized and yet not be handcrafted.
           </p>
           <p>
            In these days there are many people claiming to produce handcrafted beer but this isnt always true because
            <strong>
             there is no standard ruling regarding what is handcrafted and what is not.
            </strong>
            There should probably be something similar to the certificate of origin that already exists for wine. And an important thing to keep in mind is that a handcrafted beer isnt good just because it is handcrafted.
           </p>
           <ol start="4">
            <li>
             <strong>
              Then, what makes a beer a great one?
             </strong>
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            Whereas when it comes to wine the difference is in the quality of the grape, in the case of beer
            <strong>
             the most important thing is the technique
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Then the ingredients are also important: water, cereals (malted or not), hop and yeast. Water is essential for getting a great quality beer. And the cereals, the hop and the yeast have more or less importance depending on the type of beer.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_643" style="width: 410px">
            <img alt="Spanish beers" class="wp-image-643 size-full" height="298" src="http://www.spainforreal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/cervezas-espanya.jpg" width="400"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Spanish beers
            </p>
           </div>
           <ol start="5">
            <li>
             <strong>
              With regards to the taste, what is the difference between an industrial beer and a handcrafted one?
             </strong>
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            The difference is huge. In a handcrafted beer the liquid is more turbid, the bitterness changes, more flavors and aromas are present. It is a more sophisticated drink with more nuances.
           </p>
           <ol start="6">
            <li>
             <strong>
              Tell me about beer culture in Spain
             </strong>
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            Beer was initially brought to Spain by Carlos V (Charles 5
            <sup>
             th
            </sup>
            of Germany and 1
            <sup>
             st
            </sup>
            of Spain) in the 16
            <sup>
             th
            </sup>
            century. He brought a Belgian monk to Spain who started making beer in our country.
           </p>
           <p>
            Spain doesnt quite measure up to the strong tradition of Belgium or Germany in regards to beer. However, Spaniards are heavy consumers of beer and in the past years people are more and more interested in trying international beers and getting better quality.
           </p>
           <p>
            Now there are blogs and magazines specialized in beer, for example,
            <a href="http://eljardindellupulo.blogspot.com.es/">
             http://eljardindellupulo.blogspot.com.es/
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.bar-beer.com/">
             http://www.bar-beer.com/
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            And there are more people who practice homebrewing. This is very common in the USA and there are more Spanish people doing it. For example, in Catalonia there are many engineers and chefs doing it as a hobby.
           </p>
           <ol start="7">
            <li>
             <strong>
              According to you, what would be the world podium of beer?
             </strong>
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            The number one is Belgium. The second one is the USA because they are very innovative. And the third one would be Germany or maybe the United Kingdom. I would also make a special mention to the Czechs.
           </p>
           <ol start="8">
            <li>
             <strong>
              Pairing with beer
             </strong>
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            We have heard a lot about pairing with wine, but I know you are a big fan of pairing with beer. What would be a good beer pairing?
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             I believe that beer matches very well with popular food: tapa, cold meats, fish, seafood, sandwiches, cheese.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Cheese is actually great for pairing. I like having sweet beers with salted and cured cheese, hoppy pale beers accompanied by cream cheese and having black beer paired with chocolate.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_644" style="width: 802px">
            <img alt="Brewmaster tasting beer" class="wp-image-644 size-full" height="594" src="http://www.spainforreal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/20140701_215448-e1406804101119.jpg" width="792"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Brewmaster tasting beer
            </p>
           </div>
           <ol start="9">
            <li>
             <strong>
              In Spain for Real we believe that traveling is about the experience, tell us some great experiences related to beer in Spain
             </strong>
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            The
            <strong>
             Barcelona Beer Festival
            </strong>
            is a young festival dedicated to beer. It is a wonderful occasion to taste different kinds of beers, meet brewers and learn about the new trends related to beer.
            <a href="http://www.barcelonabeerfestival.com/index_en.html">
             http://www.barcelonabeerfestival.com/index_en.html
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            There are some handcrafted beer festivals in different towns of Spain such as the one in Mediona.
           </p>
           <p>
            Visit the
            <strong>
             Moritz brewery in the center of Barcelona
            </strong>
            . The old Moritz plant has been turned into an innovative center that connects la haute gastronomie with beer. In this place you can eat great food, drink an excellent handcrafted beer and learn about the brewing process.
           </p>
           <p>
            And a visit to
            <strong>
             Ambar brewery and malting plant in Zaragoza
            </strong>
            is a very authentic and inspirational visit because the installations have remained the same as they were in 1900.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_649" style="width: 802px">
            <img alt="Moritz Brewery in Barcelona" class="wp-image-649 size-full" height="532" src="http://www.spainforreal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/fabrica_moritz-e1406804137262.jpg" width="792"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Moritz Brewery in Barcelona
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             In case you are interested in any of these experiences, contact me! And if you would like to take part of a beer tasting with Alex, dont miss our Facebook page and Newsletter where we regularly inform about our activities and a coming soon beer pairing with this master!
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            And now some extra bonus!
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Some basic Spanish beer vocabulary
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Ordering a beer has its own rules in each country. Here we give you some Spanish vocabulary to make sure you order the size and type of beer you want in Spain.
           </p>
           <p>
            Una
            <strong>
             caa
            </strong>
            , por favor = A caa is a beer served from the tap, its standard size is 0,20 l, but you can get larger sizes of beer served from the tap, such as la copa (0,33 l)
           </p>
           <p>
            Un
            <strong>
             quinto
            </strong>
            , por favor = a quinto is a beer bottle containing 0,20 l (most commonly used in Catalonia)
           </p>
           <p>
            Un
            <strong>
             botelln
            </strong>
            = a botelln is a beer bottle containing 0,20 l (most commonly used in different places of Spain such as Madrid and Andalusia)
           </p>
           <p>
            Un
            <strong>
             tercio
            </strong>
            , por favor = a tercio is a beer bottle containing 0,33 l
           </p>
           <p>
            Una
            <strong>
             jarra
            </strong>
            , por favor = jarra means jug in Spanish, when it is referred to beer we usually mean a 0,5 l served from the tap
           </p>
           <p>
            Una
            <strong>
             litrona
            </strong>
            , por favor = this is a bottle of 1 l beer and you dont order this in a bar! However, you might want to buy a litrona in a supermarket or at a convenient store
           </p>
           <p>
            Un
            <strong>
             cachi de cerveza
            </strong>
            , por favor = this is a big glass of 1 l beer that is usually served at bars, concerts and traditional festivals. The word cachi is commonly used in
            <strong>
             Euskadi
            </strong>
            and in other regions of the North of Spain. A cachi can also contain other drinks. In the
            <strong>
             South of Spain
            </strong>
            people use the word
            <strong>
             maceta
            </strong>
            and in
            <strong>
             Madrid
            </strong>
            they have the word
            <strong>
             mini
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_645" style="width: 360px">
            <img alt="Copa, medium glass of beer" class="wp-image-645 size-full" height="263" src="http://www.spainforreal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/20140708_205640-e1406804114273.jpg" width="350"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Copa, medium glass of beer
            </p>
           </div>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_647" style="width: 306px">
            <img alt="Jarra, big glass of beer" class="wp-image-647 size-full" height="170" src="http://www.spainforreal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/jarra.jpg" width="296"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Jarra, big glass of beer
            </p>
           </div>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_646" style="width: 269px">
            <img alt="Caa, small glass of beer" class="wp-image-646 size-full" height="194" src="http://www.spainforreal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Canya.jpg" width="259"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Caa, small glass of beer
            </p>
