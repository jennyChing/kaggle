      <a href="http://wp.me/p3FiEC-6gf" rel="attachment wp-att-24121">

        <img alt="Logo for dairy hub Blanca designed by Lo Siento" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-24121" height="300" src="http://bpando-site.flywheelsites.com/wp-content/uploads//2012/12/blanca-001.jpg" width="600"/>
        <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://bpando.org/2012/12/17/logo-blanca/&amp;media=http://bpando-site.flywheelsites.com/wp-content/uploads//2012/12/blanca-001.jpg&amp;description=Blanca designed by Lo Siento')">

     </p>
     <p>
      Blanca is a dairy hub created to mediate between two extreme poles of the industry; [milk] production and [consumer] knowledge andto produce honestly, think critically and consume responsibly. Based around a simple stencil cut logo-type solution executed with a monochromatic simplicity and subtle craft cues, Blancasvisual identity, developed byBarcelona-based brand and graphic design agency
      <a href="http://www.losiento.net/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.losiento.net']);" target="_blank">
       Lo Siento
      </a>
      , manages to infuse and visually convey the disparate perceptionsof commodity and care.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span id="more-24071">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Constructed from a single, consistent line weight, rounded terminals and a monochromatic colour palette, the logo-type manages to resolve, albeit in an expected (but clearly communicative) manner, the themes of agricultural industry, commodity, simplicity and functionality.The additional cuts and sentence-case rather than uppercase characters give it a slightly more proprietary and accessible quality that in conjunction with the black and white colour palette perhaps references the marks of Holstein-Friesian cows.
     </p>
     <p>
      These contemporary utilitarian qualities have been appropriately tempered by the crafted, tactile finishes of die cut and uncoated material textures, hand stamps, a blind emboss, screen print, badges and stickers across the collaterals. Choices that introduce a sense of personal care and responsibility to the commoditised perceptions of the dairy industry.
     </p>
