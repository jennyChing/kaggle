       <h1 class="entry-title single-title">
        <span>
         Civil War Battlefield Gets Solar Power
        </span>
       </h1>
      </header>
      <div class="wrapper">
       <div class="grids">
        <div class="grid-8">
         <div class="single-box clearfix entry-content">
          <span class="wpsr_floatbts_anchor" data-offset="25">
          </span>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.nps.gov/kemo/index.htm">
            Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park
           </a>
           preserves historic earthworks, cannon emplacements andmonuments that help people learn about and interpret the great Civil War battle that took place there in June 1864  a
           <a href="http://www.civilwaralbum.com/atlanta/kennesaw1.htm">
            rare full-frontal assault
           </a>
           by Gen. William T. Shermans on Confederate troops in his bid to reach nearby Atlanta. Now, a decidedly 21st century element has been grafted onto the park, with theinstallation of a
           <a href="http://www.earthtechling.com/tag/solar-power/">
            solar power
           </a>
           system, LED lamps and a new roof with improved insulating material at the visitor center.
          </p>
          <p>
           In a
           <a href="http://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20101214006338/en/AEG-Power-Solutions-Kennesaw-National-Battlefield-Park">
            press release
           </a>
           , AEG Power Solutions described how it designed, engineered and did the installation of the various improvements. The aspect of the project visitors will most readily notice: the rooftop PV system. AEG said it consists of 288 modules, made by Georgia-based panel company Suniva, that are expected to generate 67,000 kilowatt hours of energy every year.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18092" style="width: 460px">
           <img alt="Kennesaw Mountain Battlefield, solar system" class="lazy lazy-hidden size-full wp-image-18092" data-lazy-src="http://d2r42o2f7hk334.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/12/battle.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="302" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="450"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="Kennesaw Mountain Battlefield, solar system" class="size-full wp-image-18092" height="302" src="http://d2r42o2f7hk334.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/12/battle.jpg" width="450"/>
           </noscript>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            image via National Park Service
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           The work at Kennesaw was featured in a presentation for National Park Service facility managers in November, and according to AEG will serve as a demonstration for future renewable energy implementations in other national parks across the United States.
          </p>
          <p>
           By the way, though Sherman was stymied at Kennesaw, it was just about two months later that he would be able to send word to President Lincoln stating, Atlanta is ours, and fairly won.
          </p>
