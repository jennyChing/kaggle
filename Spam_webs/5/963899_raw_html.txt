        <p>
         If you have recently purchased a home theater system, the next essential step is getting yourself a few DVD titles to show off your new toys, and impress the sexy chicks / stud who come over for a Friday-night movie.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres a list of 5 killer titles sorted by genre.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-2423">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Note: Since upconverting DVD players are doing a very respectable job, Ive included links to both DVD and Blu-Ray Amazon pages.
        </p>

         Documentary:
         <strong>
          Planet Earth
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="left" alt="Planet Earth" height="115" src="http://gaspull.geeksaresexytech.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/planet-earth.jpg" width="115"/>
         If you would ask me to choose the ultimate Blu-Ray and DVD title to own for showing off your HD set, Id recommend Planet Earth hands-down. This is the best nature and wildlife documentary series ever produced. Filled with oohs, ahhs and wows, BBCs Planet Earth contains hours of breathtaking shots and scientific facts to blow you away.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2Fgp%2Fproduct%2FB000MRAAJM&amp;tag=geeksaresexyt-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325">

         Computer-Animated:
         <strong>
          Cars
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="left" alt="Cars" height="115" src="http://gaspull.geeksaresexytech.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/cars.jpg" style="margin-top:20px" width="115"/>

         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FRatatouille-Ian-Holm%2Fdp%2FB000VBJEEG%3Fie%3DUTF8%26s%3Ddvd%26qid%3D1209042250%26sr%3D1-1&amp;tag=geeksaresexyt-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325">
          Ratatouille
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=geeksaresexyt-20&amp;l=ur2&amp;o=1" style="border:none !important; margin:0px !important;" width="1"/>

         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FMeet-Robinsons-Daniel-Hansen%2Fdp%2FB000ROAK2W%3Fie%3DUTF8%26s%3Ddvd%26qid%3D1209042287%26sr%3D1-1&amp;tag=geeksaresexyt-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325">

          Happy Feet

         are all spectacular. But when geeky dads want to demo the family home theater, Lightning McQueen and his friends are their best bet, with racing scenes that feel as if you were in the middle of a Nascar race.

         Horror:
         <strong>
          Aliens vs Predator  Requiem
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="left" alt="Aliens vs Predator - Requiem" height="115" src="http://gaspull.geeksaresexytech.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/aliens-vs-predator.jpg" style="margin-top:20px" width="115"/>
         While this one would also fit in the Science Fiction category, I think its better suited under Horror, because its truly horrifying. Alien vs Predator will not demo spectacular images, but thats ok considering that its the DTS-HD sound that will make your drywall wish it were sold to a library. If youve invested big bucks in a top notch sound system, wake up the neighbors to the sound of Aliens vs Predator  Requiem, a real treat for subwoofers.

        <h3>
         Science Fiction:
         <strong>
          The Fifth Element
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="left" alt="The Fifth Element" height="115" src="http://gaspull.geeksaresexytech.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/the-fifth-element.jpg" width="115"/>
         Released in 1997, Luc Bessons Fifth Element is a cult sci-fi movie. Used by Sony as a demo disk for a long time, the Blu-Ray version will produce amazing colors on your HDTV display. This is truly a win-win as you will have both a great movie to show off, and an excellent addition to your film collection.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FFifth-Element-Remastered-Blu-ray%2Fdp%2FB000QTD368%2F&amp;tag=geeksaresexyt-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325">

         Action:
         <strong>
          Casion Royale
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="left" alt="Casion Royale" height="115" src="http://gaspull.geeksaresexytech.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/casino-royale.jpg" style="margin-top:10px" width="115"/>
         The latest James Bond, Casino Royale, feels like it was made for HD. The lighting and photography combined with post-production picture processing create astonishing results on high-definition televisions. If you need a little assurance that your home-theater purchase was a good one after the credit card statement comes in, pop in Casino Royale and let Daniel Craig and his crew do their job.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FCasino-Royale-Blu-ray-Daniel-Craig%2Fdp%2FB000MRA5NS%2F&amp;tag=geeksaresexyt-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325">
