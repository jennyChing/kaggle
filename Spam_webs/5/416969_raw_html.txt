     <p>
      <b>
       La Clandestine Absinthe
      </b>
      is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swiss" title="Swiss">
       Swiss
      </a>
      <i>
       La Bleue
      </i>
      , or clear,
      <a href="/wiki/Absinthe" title="Absinthe">
       absinthe
      </a>
      brand produced by Artemisia-Bugnon distilleries. It is an
      <a href="/wiki/Anise" title="Anise">
       anise
      </a>
      -flavored,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Distilled" title="Distilled">
       distilled
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liquor" title="Liquor">
       liquor
      </a>
      containing the herb
      <a href="/wiki/Artemisia_absinthium" title="Artemisia absinthium">
       wormwood
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Artemisia_absinthium" title="Artemisia absinthium">
        Artemisia absinthium
       </a>
      </i>
      ), and when prepared with cold water will
      <a href="/wiki/Ouzo_effect" title="Ouzo effect">
       louche
      </a>
      . La Clandestine Absinthe comes in four main styles, as detailed below.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="La_Clandestine_Absinthe">
       La Clandestine Absinthe
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Clandestine_Absinthe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: La Clandestine Absinthe">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      According to the manufacturer, La Clandestine is based on a 1935 recipe by Swiss distiller Charlotte Vaucher
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      which Bugnon had been circulating unofficially for several years prior to the Swiss lifting a near one-hundred-year ban on absinthe March 1, 2005.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Following the lift, Bugnon applied for an official license and became one of the first distillers in the Val-de-Travers region to be granted one. It is now commercially produced and sold by Artemisia-Bugnon. In June 2008, the US
      <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_and_Tobacco_Tax_and_Trade_Bureau" title="Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau">
       Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau
      </a>
      approved a version of La Clandestine for sale in the United States,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and USA launch followed in October 2008.
     </p>
     <p>
      La Clandestine, which is produced at 53% abv (alcohol by volume) is served in a manner similar to the traditional 19th-century French method, differing only in the use of sugar. The manufacturer recommends that La Clandestine be served without sugar.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="La_Capricieuse">
       La Capricieuse
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Clandestine_Absinthe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: La Capricieuse">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      La Capricieuse, at 72% abv, was added in summer 2005.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="La_Recette_Marianne">
       La Recette Marianne
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Clandestine_Absinthe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: La Recette Marianne">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      La Recette Marianne, at 55% abv, was launched in autumn 2005, in response to
      <a href="/wiki/Fenchone" title="Fenchone">
       fenchone
      </a>
      regulations in France.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Ang.C3.A9lique">
       Anglique
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Clandestine_Absinthe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Anglique">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Anglique, at 72% abv, is the first Verte (or green) Suisse absinthe from the La Clandestine distillery, and was launched at the 10th Annual Absinthe Festival at
      <a href="/wiki/Boveresse" title="Boveresse">
       Boveresse
      </a>
      , Switzerland, in June 2007.
     </p>
