     <h3>
      <font face="arial,helvetica,sans serif">
       <a name="over">
        Overview
       </a>
      </font>
     </h3>
     <p>
      This Web feature offers a glimpse behind the scenes during the planning and construction of an exhibition at the National Gallery of Art.
      <i>
       Art Nouveau,1890-1914,
      </i>
      the largest and most comprehensive exhibition on the subject ever organized, 
      presents one of the most innovative and exuberant of all modern art styles and the places where it flourished. The exhibition 
      is on view at the Gallery October 8, 2000, through January 28, 2001.
     </p>
     <p>
      An
      <a href="exhibit_intro.shtm">
       introduction to the exhibition
      </a>
      traces the historical roots of the the Art 
      Nouveau style.
     </p>
     <p>
      Conversations with the
      <a href="concept_paul.shtm">
       V&amp;A Curator
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="concept_dodge.shtm">
       Chief of 
      Exhibitions
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="design_mark.shtm">
       Chief of Design
      </a>
      help define the intellectual 
      framework that informed the selection of objects and the design of the galleries that house them.
     </p>
     <p>
      Two photo essays highlight the technical skill of the craftspeople who
      <a href="design_maq.shtm">
       design
      </a>
      and
      <a href="construct_silk.shtm">
       build
      </a>
      the exhibition spaces.
     </p>
     <p>
      Two timelines provide
      <a href="exhibit_time.shtm">
       historical context
      </a>
      for the Art Nouveau period and 
      illustrate the
      <a href="design_time.shtm">
       exhibition production process
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      A
      <a href="construct_movie.shtm">
       time-lapse movie
      </a>
      compresses three months of exhibition construction 
      into a ten-second slide show.
     </p>
     <p>
      A
      <a href="construct_fact.shtm">
       production trivia
      </a>
      section reveals many little-known facts about the
      <i>
       Art Nouveau
      </i>
      installation.
     </p>
     <p>
      A streaming
      <a href="exhibit_audio.shtm">
       audio tour
      </a>
      focuses on fifteen works in 
      the exhibition.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
