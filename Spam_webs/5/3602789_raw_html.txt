      <h1>
       Finnriver Cider: Family, Farm and Flavor on the Olympic Peninsula
      </h1>
      <div class="meta">
       <em>
        Posted on:
       </em>
       September 5th, 2013
       <em>
        by
       </em>
       <a href="/about" rel="author">
        Jameson
       </a>
       <!--&lt;a href="http://jamesonfink.com/finnriver-cider-family-farm-and-flavor-on-the-olympic-peninsula/#comments" class="comments-link"  title="Comment on Finnriver Cider: Family, Farm and Flavor on the Olympic Peninsula"&gt;4 Comments&lt;/a&gt;-->
      </div>
      <div class="entry">
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jamesonfink/8857677738/" title="Dry Hopped Cider and Dry Roasted Peanuts by jamesonf, on Flickr">
         <img alt="Dry Hopped Cider and Dry Roasted Peanuts" class="alignleft" height="450" src="http://farm4.staticflickr.com/3741/8857677738_60cac4d0e2.jpg" width="327"/>
        </a>
        This city boy spent the month of January 2012 living and volunteering at
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.finnriver.com/" target="_blank">
          Finnriver Farm and Cidery
         </a>
        </strong>
        on Washington States Olympic Peninsula. So many wonderful memories of my time there,
        <strong>
         you can catch up on all my adventures during that month in
         <a href="http://jamesonfink.com/read-about-my-month-at-finnriver-farm-in-edible-seattle/" title="Read About My Month at Finnriver Farm in Edible Seattle">
          this
         </a>
         blog post
        </strong>
        . (Including a link to my
        <strong>
         <em>
          <a href="http://edibleseattle.com/" target="_blank">
           Edible Seattle
          </a>
         </em>
        </strong>
        article recalling my winter tenure.)
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         [Sidebar: Its
         <strong>
          Washington Cider Week
         </strong>
         from today to September 15th. Yeah, thats right: 7 days are not enough to contain this week full of super cool
         <a href="http://www.nwcider.com/washington-cider-week-events-calendar/" target="_blank">
          events
         </a>
         . Go forth and celebrate cider!]
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        But the most wonderful part is that I still feel like a member of the farm family. Its a relationship that I treasure, and enjoy being a tireless advocate for this
        <strong>
         organic family farm
        </strong>
        . I also happen to love their cider. Which is why I was overjoyed to be hired by Finnriver to add tasting notes on their website for each cider (and fruit wine).
       </p>
       <p>
        I spent an afternoon at the farm sampling everything they made and putting my (very non-technical) thoughts into words. You can head to Finnrivers product page, click on anything you wish to learn more about, and scroll to the bottom for my thoughts. I dont want to give away too much, but lets talk about the pictured
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.finnriver.com/cidery/products/dry-hopped-cider" target="_blank">
          Dry Hopped Cider
         </a>
        </strong>
        . While the adults of the Finnriver family enjoy it with
        <strong>
         popcorn tossed with nutritional yeast
        </strong>
        , I trumpet pairing the Dry Hopped Cider with
        <strong>
         dry roasted peanuts
        </strong>
        from
        <a href="http://cbsnuts.com/" target="_blank">
         CBs Nuts
        </a>
        in nearby Kingston. (Note: While this cider is kind of like apples meeting an IPA, it doesnt have any of that hoppy bitterness. More of the floral, herbaceous side of the hop.)
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://jamesonfink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wa-cider-week-logo-2013.jpg">
         <img alt="washington cider week" class=" wp-image-6691 alignleft" height="180" src="http://jamesonfink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/wa-cider-week-logo-2013-198x300.jpg" width="119"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        The Dry Hopped Cider hints at the spirit of experimentation at Finnriver.
        <strong>
         Habenero? Fir tips? Rosehips?
        </strong>
        Check, check, check. (Though not all in the same cider.) And Id like to give special mention to the
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.finnriver.com/cidery/products/artisan-sparkling-cider" target="_blank">
          Artisan Sparkling Cider
         </a>
        </strong>
        . Its made using the same method as
        <strong>
         Champagne
        </strong>
        and hand-riddled in riddling racks hand-made by Finnrivers boat-building neighbor Pete. (Damn, wish my neighbors were that crafty.) The Artisan Sparkling Cider is extremely elegant, light in alcohol, and perfect for adjusting the mood of any day into one with a significant amount of celebratory charm.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 330px">
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jamesonfink/6705030773/" title="Me in the snow by jamesonf, on Flickr">
         <img alt="finnriver farm snow" class=" " height="240" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7162/6705030773_af4e366b8d_n.jpg" width="320"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Hat last seen at The Neighbor Lady. If you see anyone in Seattle with a possum fur hat from New Zealand, please show them this blog post and confiscate.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        This afternoon at the farm inspired a trip down memory lane. I have a whole set of photos on
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jamesonfink/sets/72157628767309979/" target="_blank">
         Flickr
        </a>
        documenting my month at Finnriver that you can peruse at your convenience. Theyre full of
        <strong>
         people, chickens, ducks, blueberry bushes, eggs, cats, vegetables, and dogs
        </strong>
        . Oh, and
        <strong>
         SNOW
        </strong>
        ! We had quite a bit of a storm (for these parts) that really transformed the farm. And when the sun came out, the contrast between the blue sky and white snow was remarkable. Here are some of my favorite images:
       </p>
       <p>
        My cabin at dusk:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jamesonfink/6802259209/" title="Cabin at dusk in the snow by jamesonf, on Flickr">
         <img alt="Cabin at dusk in the snow" height="500" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7021/6802259209_642a50005e.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        In the morning:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jamesonfink/6721147191/" title="My cabin with snow by jamesonf, on Flickr">
         <img alt="My cabin with snow" height="500" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7155/6721147191_d870b94d1c.jpg" width="375"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Blueberry bushes:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jamesonfink/6721147983/" title="Blueberry Bushes, Snow by jamesonf, on Flickr">
         <img alt="Blueberry Bushes, Snow" height="500" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7032/6721147983_4a4569c112.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Sun emerging after the storm:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jamesonfink/6717352695/" title="Snow and Sun by jamesonf, on Flickr">
         <img alt="Snow and Sun" height="375" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7162/6717352695_b5a83139b6.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        And Clarkie, staying warm inside:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jamesonfink/6655931349/" title="Farm Cat by jamesonf, on Flickr">
         <img alt="Farm Cat" height="500" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7161/6655931349_f7c53178bf.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        If youve had any of Finnrivers ciders, share your impressions in the comments. Just dont wake Clarkie.
       </p>
       <p>
