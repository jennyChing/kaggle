    YSTEMS
   </b>
  </h1>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   The main purpose of this document is to
provide tables based on statistical data, to convert characters
and adventures from one RPG system to an other. The conversion
values where determined by the analysis of success probabilities
for different tasks in the given systems.
  </p>
  <h3>
   Statistic Conversion Tables
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   These tables assign CORSY stats to the stats
of the character. Each character can be converted to CORSY
standard stats and the CORSY standard stats can be converted in
an other system.
  </p>
  <h3>
   Rating Conversion Tables
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   These tables allow to convert stat ratings of
a character from one system into stat ratings of other
systems.
  </p>
  <h3>
   Probability Analysis Tables
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   These tables are provided as "proof" for the
correctness of this system. They show the probabilities of
success for a character with a given skill rating at a task with
a given difficulty rating.
  </p>
  <h2>
   Definitons
  </h2>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   First, it is necessary to explain a few basic
terms that are frequently used in CORSY. These terms are used to
classify the capabilities of characters and items and the
difficulty of tasks performed by them and with them.
  </p>
  <ul style="page-break-after:always" type="square">
   <li>
    <b>
     Character:
    </b>
    A figure in the game. The Character is
described on a sheet of paper and exists only in the imagination
of the Players and the Referee
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Item:
    </b>
    Any Item in the game. This includes equipment,
vehicles, star ships, countries, nations and planets.
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Capability:
    </b>
    Every Character is defined by
Capabilities. A Capability describes how well a Character is
trained or gifted in a certain area.
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Attribute:
    </b>
    A general Capability that effects the
ability of the Character to succeed at a lot of different
tasks.
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Skill:
    </b>
    A narrowly defined Capability that does not
effect as many different tasks as an Attribute.
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Feature:
    </b>
    A Capability that does more depend on
circumstances at the Characters birth or on his social position,
rather than training.
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Difficulty:
    </b>
    The Difficulty of a task describes how
good or bad a Characters chance to succeed at that task is,
compared to the Characters Capability.
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Steps:
    </b>
    A rating system for Capabilities, Difficulties
and more. A step usually has a verbouse name and a two letter
abbreviation.
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Rating:
    </b>
    A Step Rating that defines how well a
Character is trained or gifted in a Capability, or how great the
Difficulty of a task is.
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Source System:
    </b>
    The RPG System you want to convert
from. You have stats of the Character/Item/Task in that system
already.
   </li>
   <li>
    <b>
     Target System:
    </b>
    The RPG System you want to convert
into. You want to know the stats of the Character/Item/Task in
that system.
   </li>
  </ul>

  <h3>
   Average(AV) Difficulty
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   The Difficulty Rating, at which a character
with AV Skill succeeds 50% of the time.
  </p>

   The current conversion of attributes is based

  </p>
  <h3>
   Skill Ratings
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   Skill Ratings are rated as attributes. Two
step increases should increase the probability of success by a
factor of 2, therefore one step increases probability by a factor
of square root of 2.
  </p>
  <h3>
   Difficulty Ratings
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   Difficulty Ratings are rated in the same
manner as skills, but each two step increase decreases
probability of success by a factor of 2.
  </p>
  <h2>
   Rating Conversion Tables
  </h2>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   The Rating Conversion Tables allow the
conversion of a Rating of any RPG System covered in CORSY to any
other. Simply find out the row the Rating (or a better Rating) is
listed in the column of the Source System and look up the Rating
in the column of the Target System.
  </p>
  <h3>
   Attribute Ratings
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   To convert the Attribute of a Character of one
system to an other, select the column headed with the name of the
Source System. Then look up the Attribute Rating of the Character
in that column. Now stay on the same row you found the Attribute
Rating and look up the entry in the column of the Target System.
This entry is the Attribute Rating of the Character in the new
system.
  </p>

   This table was calculated based on the skill

  <h2>
   Character conversion process
  </h2>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   To finaly convert a character is a lot more
complicated than just looking up ratings. The man problem is the
extreme difference in basic attributes the different systems use.
The recommended method is:
  </p>
  <ol>
   <li>
    Convert the skills
   </li>
   <li>
    Determine the attributes/level
   </li>
   <li>
    Determine advantages/disadvantages/feats
   </li>
   <li>
    Determine derived values
   </li>
  </ol>
  <h3>
   Convert the skills
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   Try to figure out the corrosponding skills in
the different systems. Take each skill rating of the character in
the source system, convert it to the target system and assign it
to a similar skill in the target system.
  </p>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   If there are several skills in the source
system matching one skill in the target system, the following
methods are recommended:
  </p>
  <ul>
   <li>
    Assign the lowest rating in the source system to the skill in
the target system, and emulate the higher ratings using some kind
of specialisation mechanic in the target system.
   </li>
   <li>
    Average the rating of all skill ratings and assign the result
to the skill in the target system.
   </li>
   <li>
    Assign the highest rating in the source system to the skill
in the target system.
   </li>
  </ul>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   The method used is of course determined by the
gamemaster, not the player.
  </p>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   If one skill in the source system matches
several skills in the target system, the usual method is to
assign the skill rating to all matching skills in the target
system.
  </p>
  <h3>
   Determine the attributes/level
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   Most attributes are best determined not by
trying to convert them from the source system, but by looking at
the skills. The only exception is the attribute governing hand
weapon damage(Strength in most cases). This could be converted
using the Attribute ratings table, but I will try to create a
special table for this purpose in the future.
  </p>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   The d20 System level and class is difficult to
find. A guideline for the class may be the most important skill
of the character. Choose the class that would get the best bonus
for that skill. If there are several very high skills the
character may have to be multiclassed. Then figure out the worst
skill rating in a skill important for that class. The bonus in
that skill is a good indicator for the characters level.
  </p>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   For all other attributes, figure out the
lowest important skill in the target system, that is governed or
modified by that attribute in the target system. Then give the
character the highest possible attribute rating that would allow
a skill rating that low. If you can easily figure out an
attribute in the source system that is clearly similar to the
attribute in the target system, you may use the Attribute Rating
table and find out which result fits better to the character
concept and choose any of the two values or a value in
between.
  </p>
  <h3>
   Determine advantages/disadvantages/feats
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   Try to figure out which
advantages/disadvantages/feats fit into the character concept or
may be converted from the source system. Some odd results in the
skill values may be explained by an advantage, disadvantage or
feat.
  </p>
  <h3>
   Determine derived values
  </h3>
  <p align="JUSTIFY">
   Determine derived values as described in the
rules of the target system.
  </p>
