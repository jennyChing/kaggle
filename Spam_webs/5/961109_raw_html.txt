      <font face="Arial">
       <b>
        Julia Cameron
       </b>
      </font>
      <p>
       Julia Cameron is an active artist who teaches internationally. A poet,
      playwright, fiction writer, and essayist, she has extensive credits in
      film, television, and theater, and is an award-winning journalist. She is
      author of the best selling book on creative practice
       <i>
        The Artist's Way
       </i>
       ,
      and has taught and refined her methods for nearly two decades.
      </p>
      <p>
       <font face="Arial">
        <b>
         <i>
          <a href="part1.shtml">
           <img align="left" border="0" height="140" src="book1.gif" vspace="4" width="114"/>
          </a>
          <img align="left" border="0" height="148" src="http://www.SpiritSite.com/space.gif" width="5"/>
          The Artist's Way
         </i>
        </b>
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        The Artist's Way (
        <a href="buy.shtml">
         review or buy
        </a>
        )
       </i>
       is an
      empowering book for aspiring and working artists. Working with the
      basic principle that creative expression is the natural direction of life,
      Ms. Julia Cameron leads the reader through a comprehensive three-month program
      to recover creativity.
       <i>
        The Artist's Way
       </i>
       links creativity to
      spirituality by showing how to tap into the higher power that connects
      human creativity and the creative energies of the universe.
      </p>
      <p>
       <font face="Arial">
        <b>
         <i>
          <a href="part5.shtml">
           <img align="right" border="0" height="140" hspace="8" src="book2.gif" vspace="4" width="111"/>
          </a>
          The Vein of Gold
         </i>
        </b>
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <i>
        The Vein of Gold (
        <a href="buy2.shtml">
         review or buy
        </a>
        )
       </i>
       ,
      Julia Cameron again draws from her extensive artistic and teaching
      experience to steer readers toward ever-widening creative horizons. This
      inspiring book of learning-through-doing features teachings on the
      creative process and more than one hundred imaginative, involving, and
      energizing tasks.
      </p>
      <p>
       <font face="Arial">
        <b>
         <i>
          <a href="part9.shtml">
           <img align="left" border="0" height="140" src="book3.jpg" vspace="4" width="90"/>
          </a>
          <img align="left" border="0" height="148" src="http://www.SpiritSite.com/space.gif" width="5"/>
          The
      Right to Write
         </i>
        </b>
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       Creativity teacher Julia Cameron continues her enthusiastic
      cheerleading in
       <i>
        The Right to Write (
        <a href="buy3.shtml">
         review or buy
        </a>
        ).
       </i>
       Readers are shown how to make writing a natural, intensely personal part
      of life. Cameron's instruction and examples include the details of her
      very own writing processes while creating her own bestselling books.
      Cameron's tools make writing a playful and practical as well as profound
      experience.
      </p>
      <p>
       <font face="Arial">
        <b>
         <i>
          <a href="part14.shtml">
           <img align="right" border="0" height="140" hspace="8" src="book5.jpg" vspace="4" width="114"/>
          </a>
          Walking
      in This World
         </i>
        </b>
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <i>
        Walking in This World (
        <a href="buy5.shtml">
         review or buy
        </a>
        )
       </i>
       ,
      Julia Cameron shows readers how to inhabit the world with a sense of wonder, a childlike inquisitiveness that
      each of us was born with. "Great artists are actually great amateurs," she writes
      in her introduction. "They have learned to wriggle out of the seriousness of
      rigid categorization and allow themselves to pursue the Pied Piper of delight." Full of valuable new
      strategies and techniques for breaking through difficult creative ground, this is the "intermediate
      level" of the Artist's Way program.
      </p>
