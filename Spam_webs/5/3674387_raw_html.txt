     <h1 class="posttitle">
      Cheesecake Stuffed Chocolate Cupcakes
     </h1>
     <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="Cheesecake Stuffed Chocolate Cupcakes" data-frizzlyposturl="http://www.handletheheat.com/cheesecake-stuffed-chocolate-cupcakes/" type="hidden" value=""/>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <em>
       Chocolate Stuffed Cheesecake Cupcakes are ridiculously amazing and pretty easy, too. Your family will love them!
      </em>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <img alt="Cheesecake Stuffed Chocolate Cupcakes" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4199" height="800" src="http://src2.handletheheat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Cheesecake-Stuffed-Chocolate-Cupcakes.jpg" width="533"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      If you follow me on
      <a href="http://instagram.com/handletheheat#" target="_blank">
       Instagram
      </a>
      or saw
      <a href="http://www.handletheheat.com/2014/03/diy-spray-paint-door-knob-makeover.html" target="_blank">
       last Sundays post
      </a>
      , you know Ive been slightly obsessed with home decor and DIY house projects lately. I completely and utterly blame
      <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/handleheat/" target="_blank">
       Pinterest
      </a>
      . Im really not sure exactly
      <em>
       what
      </em>
      my house or my wardrobe would look like if we didnt have Pinterest. Im not really too mad about it, though. Beyond the danger of self-comparison and making sure to realize the stuff people post on Pinterest is not usually everyday life achievable by most people, its so fun to get inspired to start new projects. Luckily we have been pretty successful and Im addicted to the high of making our house look pretty! Have you done any Pinterest-inspired or other house projects lately? Do you have any favorite home decor blogs or magazines?
     </p>
     <p>
      If Im being honest here, Pinterest also inspired this recipe. I saw on there and knew I had to make. Once I tried a bite I knew I would have to share it with you guys. So rich, so crave-worthy, and best of all easy. You gotta make these cupcakes!
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recipe Rundown
      </strong>
      <br/>
      Taste: I love the combination of chocolate with the tangy sweetness of the cheesecake filling, its a great balance!
      <br/>
      Texture: The cupcake is moist and soft, while the filling is luscious, gooey, and loaded with chocolate chips.
      <br/>
      Ease: Even though you have to make a filling and dirty up a few more dishes, its all offset by the fact that the cupcakes are sprinkled with chocolate chips out of the oven to make a melted chocolate frosting. SO it ends up being the same amount of effort as normal cupcakes.
      <br/>
      Appearance: They look pretty ordinary until you take a bite!
      <br/>
      Pros: Fantastic cupcake recipe.
      <br/>
      Cons: None.
      <br/>
      Would I make this again? Of course!
      <br/>
      <span id="more-4198">
      </span>
     </p>
     <div class="recipe hrecipe">
      <img align="right" class="photo nopin" height="125" nopin="nopin" src="http://src2.handletheheat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Cheesecake-Stuffed-Chocolate-Cupcakes-180x180.jpg" width="125"/>
      <p class="printbutton">
       <a href="http://www.handletheheat.com/cheesecake-stuffed-chocolate-cupcakes//print/" title="Print Recipe">
        <img alt="Print" class="nopin" height="30" src="http://src2.handletheheat.com/wp-content/themes/handletheheat/images/print.png" width="30"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <span class="item">
       <h2 class="fn">
        Cheesecake Stuffed Chocolate Cupcakes
       </h2>
      </span>
      <div class="time">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Yield:
        </strong>
        <span class="yield">
         16 cupcakes
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <h3 id="ingredients">
       Ingredients:
      </h3>
      <div class="ingredients">
       <p>
        For the cupcakes:
        <br/>
        2 cups (1 12-ounce package) semisweet chocolate chips, divided
        <br/>
        1 1/2 cups (6.61 ounces or 187 grams) all-purpose flour
        <br/>
        1 teaspoon baking soda
        <br/>
        1/2 teaspoon salt
        <br/>
        1/2 cup granulated sugar
        <br/>
        1/3 cup vegetable oil
        <br/>
        1 large egg
        <br/>
        1 teaspoon vanilla
        <br/>
        1 cup water
       </p>
       <p>
        For the cheesecake filling:
        <br/>
        6 ounces cream cheese, at room temperature
        <br/>
        1/4 cup granulated sugar
        <br/>
        1 large egg
        <br/>
        1/8 teaspoon salt
       </p>
      </div>
      <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
       Directions:
      </h3>
      <div class="instructions">
       <p>
        For the cupcakes:
        <br/>
        Preheat the oven to 350F. Line two standard muffin tins with 16 paper liners.
       </p>
       <p>
        Place 1/2 cup of the chocolate chips in a small microwave-safe bowl and microwave on high for 45 seconds. Stir until smooth. If not completely melted, return to the microwave for 10 second bursts until smooth. Let cool.
       </p>
       <p>
        In a medium bowl combine the flour, baking soda, and salt. In a large bowl vigorously whisk the sugar, oil, egg, and vanilla until well combined. Beat in the melted chocolate chips. Gradually add the flour mixture alternatively with the water. The batter will be thin.
       </p>
       <p>
        For the filling:
        <br/>
        In the bowl of an electric mixer beat the cream cheese, sugar, egg, and salt until creamy. Stir in 1 cup of the chocolate chips.
       </p>
       <p>
        Fill each muffin cup half full. Spoon a tablespoon of the cream cheese filling over the batter. Spoon the remaining batter over the cream cheese filling. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, or until a cake tester inserted in the center comes out clean. While still hot, sprinkle the remaining 1/2 cup chocolate chips onto the cupcakes. Let cool for 5 minutes, or until the chocolate chips are shiny, then spread to frost. Remove to wire racks to cool completely.
       </p>
      </div>
