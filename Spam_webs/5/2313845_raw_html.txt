          <p>
           <!-- BEGIN THEIA POST SLIDER -->
          </p>
          <p>
           Fresh Strawberry Ice Pops. Since its strawberry season, my family and I ventured out to the farm to pick some fresh in season strawberries. We wanted the pick of the litter so we left the house at the crack of dawn hoping to be the first ones there. Sadly we werent the only ones, who wanted to get an early start, the farm was packed.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Fresh Strawberry Ice Pops" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5439" height="694" src="http://www.theafropolitanmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Fresh-Strawberry-Ice-Pops.jpg" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           It rained the night before, so the grounds were fairly wet which according to the farmer makes it a perfect time to harvest. We were encouraged to walk around, explore the different types of strawberries they had, while doing a taste test.
          </p>
          <p>
           Two hours later, we had more strawberries than we could ever had asked for. We went home, and I spent the rest of the weekend, canning, baking, and juicing. By Sunday night, I had made strawberry cream cheese hand pies, strawberry jam, strawberry pie, strawberry lemonade, strawberry ice cream and these yummyFresh Strawberry Ice Pops. The kids couldnt have asked for a better weekend.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Fresh Strawberry Ice Pops
          </h3>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Fresh Strawberry Ice Pop" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5450" height="773" src="http://www.theafropolitanmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Fresh-Strawberry-Ice-Pop.jpg" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Ingredients
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            1 quart fresh strawberries  washed and hulled
           </li>
           <li>
            1/2 cup sugar
           </li>
           <li>
            1 fresh lemon  squeezed  2 tablespoons lemon juice
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Directions
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            In a small bowl, add strawberries and sugar, toss till well coated
           </li>
           <li>
            Cover and store in refrigerator for about 20  30 minutes
           </li>
           <li>
            Add marinated strawberries plus lemon juice in a blender and blend until smooth
           </li>
           <li>
            *Pour strawberry puree into mold
           </li>
           <li>
            Freeze until firm, roughly 6  24 hours.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           We chose to leave the strawberry seeds in, but if you dont like the seeds, before step 4,strain toget the seeds out.
          </p>
