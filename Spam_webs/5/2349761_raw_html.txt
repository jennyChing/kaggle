       <div class="txt step-body">
        In this Instructable you'll learn a very quick and easy way to make your own concrete fossil (leaf impression). Leaf impressions can be nice details in larger-scale concrete work, or you can follow these instructions as-is to make stepping stones for the backyard or garden. If you have a big project to pour, this is something easy for the kids to play around with and it keeps any leftover concrete from going to waste.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Materials:
        </strong>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Fresh Leaf
         </li>
         <li>
          Modeling Clay
         </li>
         <li>
          Smooth, Flat, Waterproof surface (a scrap piece of 3/4" melamine was used, but thick plastic will work too)
         </li>
         <li>
          Sacked Concrete
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.concreteexchange.com/new-cheng-outdoor-pro-formula.html" rel="nofollow">
           CHENG Outdoor Pro-Formula Admixture
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <strong>
         Tools / Supplies:
        </strong>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Spray Adhesive
         </li>
         <li>
          Diamond Sanding Pads / Sandpaper
         </li>
         <li>
          Plastic Bag to cover while curing
         </li>
        </ul>
        <strong>
         Concrete Mixing:
        </strong>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Bucket
         </li>
         <li>
          Water
         </li>
         <li>
          Particle Mask
         </li>
         <li>
          Rubber Gloves
         </li>
        </ul>

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 1: Step 1: Make Clay Wall
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SB2UGJ0H2G6040U" data-entry-url="/id/DIY-CONCRETE-Stepping-Stone-Fossil/step1/Step-1-Make-Clay-Wall/" id="photoset-SB2UGJ0H2G6040U">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SB2UGJ0H2G6040U" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/F0X/5TKB/H1YOHE0R/F0X5TKBH1YOHE0R.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/F0X5TKBH1YOHE0R" data-notes="" href="/file/F0X5TKBH1YOHE0R" id="photoset-link-F7U4RN9HCAX4F12-0" style="">

          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SB2UGJ0H2G6040U" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FTU/E7TQ/H1YOHE0S/FTUE7TQH1YOHE0S.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FTUE7TQH1YOHE0S" data-notes="" href="/file/FTUE7TQH1YOHE0S" id="photoset-link-FQFSVHVHCAX4F13-1" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:600px;">
           <img alt="DSC_0326.jpg" class="photoset-photo id_FTUE7TQH1YOHE0S lazyphoto" data-notes="" data-original="http://cdn.instructables.com/FTU/E7TQ/H1YOHE0S/FTUE7TQH1YOHE0S.MEDIUM.jpg" src="/static/img/pixel.png" style="width:600px;"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="DSC_0326.jpg" class="photoset-photo" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FTU/E7TQ/H1YOHE0S/FTUE7TQH1YOHE0S.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>
           </noscript>
          </div>

       <div class="txt step-body">
        Clean up the surface you'll be casting on with denatured alcohol. Any dirt, scratches, imperfections and texture will be visible in the finished piece of concrete.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        For a very smooth finish, you might try casting on a piece of glass or acrylic. Any surface that won't absorb water will work. If you want to use something like wood, it has to be sealed first or the wood wick the moisture away from the concrete and the surface will be clouded and rough.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Roll out a few 'snakes' and form up the walls by pinching the clay between your fingers and pressing down. It's important that the clay has a good bond to the casting surface. This technique will only work with relatively short walls - anything too tall and the pressure of the concrete will cause the walls to blow out.
