         <p style="color: #617984;">
          <strong>
           By
           <a href="http://www.jasondemakis.com/" target="_blank">
            Jason Demakis
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             You are 100% responsible for yourself, your actions, and the consequences of those actions  no matter if:
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             1. your behavior is positive, productive, and progressive toward your goals
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             or
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             2. your behavior is lazy, limiting, self-sabotaging, and stagnation-laden.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             You and you alone must decide when, where, and how to act.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Understand that nobody is coming to rescue you.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             No one will (or can) force you into action.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             You are responsible for your victories as well as your failures.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Nobody can orchestrate said victories or failures in the first place other than you.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
            How Does The Above Make You Feel?
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
           Does it demotivate you to realize that you and you alone are responsible for how things in your life unfold? Does it make you not want to even try, since youre already convinced the outcome isnt worth pushing yourself for? Have you ever considered that this very limiting belief is the reason why you hesitate to push yourself?
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Does this statement empower you? Do you feel a surge of positive energy with the recognition that nobody else can create your life for you; youre the one behind the wheel? Does taking 100% responsibility for your life equate to more freedom than fear for you?
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             The goal of this article is to get you to stop identifying with the first group, and start identifying with the second.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Lets explore both of the above paths a bit, and see where our true power lies.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
            Self-Discipline v.s. Laziness
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
           Take note of how you feel when you decide to be lazy about something. It could be a particular goal youve set, a routine youve started, or even just your everyday daily actions. When you allow yourself to skimp and slide, how do you usually feel afterwards?
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             What about those times when you actually discipline yourself to take action? Youll most likely notice that even though there may be a bit of resistance getting started, once you get moving, you enter a flow state. Acting becomes effortless, and your attention shifts to what youre doing rather than Should I do this or not?.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             The end results of our actions and efforts are nice, but in truth theyre secondary to taking action from a consciously-chosen state of being. That state of being, and the natural, intrinsic state of satisfaction, happiness, and fulfillment you get from acting  is the true reward here. Many rob themselves of this pleasure because of limiting beliefs, assumptions, and semi-conscious habits that keep them at arms length from such rewards.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
            Passion-Driven Goals v.s. Passive-Driven Goals
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
           Further more,
          </span>
          <em style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
           whos
          </em>
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
           deciding what goals youre setting for yourself in the first place?
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Are you setting goals anchored in social conditioning and expectation, more often than not at the expense of what you really desire for yourself? Do you put your true, passion-driven goals on the back burner? Are you carrying the belief that If I just follow the status quo for x more years, then Ill have the time and money to do what I
             <em>
              really
             </em>
             want with my life!? If so, congratulations! Youre delusional!
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             I dont say that to be rude, elitist, or any other number of things people will probably insist upon in the comments.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             I say it because living a life structured around other peoples goals, while depriving yourself of a life fueled by genuine passion and excitement is to deny courage and embrace cowardice.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             When you allow your fear of the unknown to capitalize on your ability to take action, you lose your power.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Instead of putting your energy to use via inspired action, you use that same energy to choose fear, shine a spotlight on it, and feed it until it incapacitates you.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             This most often happens when contemplating a big goal that requires serious change on your part.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             All good goals do this.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             A good rule of thumb is that if it doesnt require changing something for the better, it probably isnt a good goal/goal worth investing time and energy into.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
            Laziness v.s. Ease
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
           Dont confuse easy with lazy. Another rule of thumb that will keep you in good standing is the conscious recognition that you want neither of those things.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             You dont want easy or lazy.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             You want challenge and growth.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             The first is a beginners trap, much like a lounge or a restaurant where patrons eat and chat. The latter is like the VIP lounge in the back, where all the high-rollers gather because they are on a different wavelength than traditional customers.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Anyone can become a VIP, but very few will make the effort to act outside of their comfort zone.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Talking the talk and walking the walk are two very different things.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Most will choose easy and lazy, and thats precisely why you shouldnt.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             The sheer fact that most choose easy and lazy should empower you to choose challenge and growth, because there are less people hanging out at the finish line. The more conscious you become, the less competition there actually is. Its completely backwards from what most would assume and expect, but its absolutely true. The room with all the easy, lazy-choosing people is packed to the walls. The challenge and growth room has a few people hanging out wondering where everyone else is.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             It would actually be more accurate to say
             <em>
              the more conscious you become, the less competition, and more cooperation there is.
             </em>
             <br/>
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Laziness and ease-seeking leads to stagnation and suffering. You already know this. You know thats not what you want. Use this as a guide stone for empowering yourself toward going beyond the comfort zone of fear-level thinking. Youve already tried that method hundreds of times, and its not for you.Embrace this truth, and use it to catalyze yourself into inspired action whenever worry, fear, and doubt begin rearing their head.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
            Following Your Path with a Heart
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: #222222;">
           It should be obvious that the truth in nobody is coming to save you should empower and excite you to push yourself beyond your comfort zone. If it doesnt, take some time to write out your goals. Analyze them, and think deeply about why youre choosing to embrace them. Be honest with yourself here. Now is the time to begin consciously shedding excuses and embracing the truth.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             The fact that nobody is coming to save you also means that nobody can take your results away from you. Nobody can experience your highs and accomplishments, and nobody else has to withstand and endure your lows and aggravation. Its all yours for the taking. This is an incredible gift, and the more you discipline yourself to act in alignment with your passion path, the more obvious this is and the more powerful you become.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #222222;">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
            <span style="font-size: medium;">
             Admitting that youre sick of what youre getting, wishing to strive for more, and aligning your daily actions and behaviors with those new, conscious values is the first step to unlocking your true potential.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
