      <h3 class="title">
       Perspectiva Literaria
      </h3>
      <p>
       In 1567, Nuremburg goldsmith Hans Lencker published his illustrated treatise
       <a href="http://www.mathe.tu-freiberg.de/~hebisch/cafe/lencker/perslit1.html" title="Title-page of Lencker's 'Perspective' at Prof. Dr. Udo Hebisch's Mathematischen Caf site.">
        Perspectiva Literaria
       </a>
       , one of three remarkable studies in perspective published in Bavaria at around the same time (I have already made mention here of
       <a href="http://www.spamula.net/blog/archives/000212.html" title="Giornale entry about Lorenz Stoer's 'Geometria...'">
        Stoer
       </a>
       s
       <i>
        Geometria et Perspectiva
       </i>
       (Augsburg, 1567) and
       <a href="http://www.spamula.net/blog/archives/000153.html" title="Giornale entry about Wentzel Jamnitzer's 'Perspectiva...'">
        Jamnitzer
       </a>
       s
       <i>
        Perspectiva Corporum Regularium
       </i>
       (Nuremburg, 1568).
      </p>
      <center>
       <a href="http://www.spamula.net/blog/i14/lencker11.html" onclick="window.open('http://www.spamula.net/blog/i14/lencker11.html','popup','width=350,height=459,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0'); return false" title="Geometrical object in perspective, from Lencker's 'Perspectiva...'">
        <img alt="Geometrical object in perspective, from Lencker's 'Perspectiva...'" border="0" height="393" src="http://www.spamula.net/blog/i14/lencker1-thumb.jpg" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        *
       </p>
       <img alt="Geometrical object in perspective, from Lencker's 'Perspectiva...'" border="0" height="473" src="http://www.spamula.net/blog/i14/lencker2.jpg" title="Geometrical object in perspective, from Lencker's 'Perspectiva...'" width="298"/>
      </center>
      <blockquote>
       <i>
        Lenckers Literary Perspective was first published in 1567, with a second edition in 1595. This splendidly illustrated work [] with elaborate perspectival designs, beautifully etched by his Nuremberg colleague Matthias Zndt, became the model for a whole generation of artists on perspective, including Jamnitzer and Brunn. Hans Lencker the Elder was one of the most outstanding members of the famous Nuremberg dynasty of goldsmiths and engravers. His works are found in various fields, from enamel drinking vessels to book-bindings
       </i>
       (source
       <a href="http://www.forum-hes.nl/forum/main_stocklist.phtml/view/5404?view=yes" title="Info about Lencker and his book, from a page offering a copy for sale: it could be yours for only 22,690 Euros!">
        here
       </a>
       .)
      </blockquote>
      <center>
       <a href="http://www.spamula.net/blog/i14/lencker3.html" onclick="window.open('http://www.spamula.net/blog/i14/lencker3.html','popup','width=658,height=1015,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0'); return false" title="Geometrical object in perspective, from Lencker's 'Perspectiva...'">

       </a>
       <p>
        *
       </p>
       <img alt="lencker4.jpg" border="0" height="472" src="http://www.spamula.net/blog/i14/lencker4.jpg" width="301"/>
      </center>
      <p>
       The plates etched by Zndt after Lenckers designs include nine geometrical figures in perspective, six of which I have excerpted for display here, and twelve plates depicting letters of the alphabet in various perspectival arrangements, the first and last of which follow below.
      </p>
