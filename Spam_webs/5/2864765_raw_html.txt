      <div class="post-content-body entry-content">
       <p>
        According to reports the software company isbuilding a lightweight browser, codenamed
        <b>
         Spartan,
        </b>
        bears out.
       </p>
       <p>
        Microsoft intends
        <b>
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows_10" target="_blank">
          Windows 10
         </a>
        </b>
        to be working well on different platforms and wants developers to be able to develop once and deploy far and wide, then creating a fresh browsing experience that is built to hold all sorts of inputs without hurdles of traditional desktop browsing experience.
       </p>
       <p>
        The report cites unnamed sources as saying Microsofts browser project, code-named Spartan, will substitute
        <b>
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_Explorer" target="_blank">
          Internet Explorer
         </a>
        </b>
        and accompany Windows 10. Microsofts new web browser is expected to set up a whole new set of compatibility issues for every website, platform, and app running online.
       </p>
       <p>
        The new browser may be publicized duringMicrosofts January 21 event, with a lot more information about Windows 10. But thats not a given, since its uncertain whether Spartan will be serviceable enough by then to be included in the January Technical Preview build of Windows for computers, or for the mobile preview builds that should also surface early next year.
       </p>
       <p>
        Internet Explorer has had a coarse history, growing from zero market share, to market-dominating heights, to slow decline in the face of Firefox, to quicker turn down in the face of Chrome, to a new re-acceleration under a latest, standards-based approach. Its life has been bitcoins late 2013 to date, but stretched out over several decades.
       </p>
       <p>
        Spartan isnt IE12, but something entirely new, as it turns out. It will be available for both the desktop version of Windows as well as the mobile one (for phones and tablets). As for the name  it seems that is just a codename. That said, it hasnt yet been disclosed what its marketable name will be.
       </p>
       <p>
        When contacted about the rumor, Microsoft said that it had
        <b>
         nothing to share
        </b>
        regarding any rumors of a new browser.
       </p>
