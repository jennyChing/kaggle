        <p>
         WOW!
         <a href="http://digg.com/tech_news/Google_sends_blogger_aspirin_after_complaining_about_Google_headaches">
          Digg.com
         </a>
         is going crazy!!! For those unbelievers, here it is, the actual scanned letter. I am sure new comments will appear that I wrote it, but I am not sure how I can prove I did not. I guess you are going to have to take my word for it
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.marketingpilgrim.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         Al
        </p>
        <p>
         UPDATE 2 (from Andy): Google has emailed me to confirm the letter was from them. The sender is
         <a href="http://www.bladam.com/">
          Adam Lasnik
         </a>
         , one of Matt Cutts
         <a href="http://www.bladam.com/main/entry/now-a-googler">
          search evangalists
         </a>
         . Google certainly knows how to keep good relations with search marketers, and it just proves that Google thrives on word of mouth.
        </p>
        <p>
         UPDATE 3: We told you!  Googles
         <a href="http://www.bladam.com/main/entry/under-the-microscope/">
          Adam Lasnik confirms
         </a>
         he sent Al the stuff he needed to ease his headache.
        </p>
        <p>
         Todays article is below.
        </p>
        <p>
         ________________________________________________________________
        </p>
        <p>
         Several days ago, I wrote an article titled, 
         <a href="http://www.marketingpilgrim.com/2006/07/dear-google-youre-giving-me-headache.html">
          Dear Google, Youre Giving Me a Headache
         </a>
         . To my surprise, they sent me the pain relief I requested.
        </p>
        <p>
         After a meeting at Fortune Interactive, where I was actually discussing Google reporting, I went to my desk and found an envelope from Google. I opened the envelope and a small pack of acetaminophen fell out. The hand written letter said:
        </p>
        <p>
         Dear Al,
        </p>
        <p>
         I hope this helps you keep up with the many Adwords changes.
        </p>
        <p>
         PS  You are reading the Inside Adwords Blog, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         Google continues to amaze me. Even though they are a huge company, they obviously found my article on
         <a href="http://www.marketingpilgrim.com/">
          Marketing Pilgrim
         </a>
         , got some acetaminophen, wrote a letter, and mailed it to me. This may not have taken much of their time, but I am sure it is the small things, like this, that make their employees love working there.
        </p>
        <p>
         I took the acetaminophen, read their 
         <a href="http://adwords.blogspot.com/">
          Inside Adwords
         </a>
          Blog, and I feel a lot better. As a matter of fact I feel so good I am preparing my next article, Dear Google, I Need a Vacation.
        </p>
