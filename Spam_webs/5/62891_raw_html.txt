     <p>
      A
      <b>
       hug
      </b>
      is an international form of
      <a href="/wiki/Physical_intimacy" title="Physical intimacy">
       physical intimacy
      </a>
      in which two people put their
      <a href="/wiki/Arm" title="Arm">
       arms
      </a>
      around the neck, back, or waist of one another and hold each other closely. If more than two persons are involved, this is informally referred to as a
      <b>
       group hug
      </b>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
       Description
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A hug, sometimes in association with a
      <a href="/wiki/Kiss" title="Kiss">
       kiss
      </a>
      , is a form of
      <a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_communication" title="Nonverbal communication">
       nonverbal communication
      </a>
      . Depending on culture, context and
      <a href="/wiki/Interpersonal_relationship" title="Interpersonal relationship">
       relationship
      </a>
      , a hug can indicate familiarity,
      <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">
       love
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Affection" title="Affection">
       affection
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Friendship" title="Friendship">
       friendship
      </a>
      , brotherhood or
      <a href="/wiki/Sympathy" title="Sympathy">
       sympathy
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A hug can indicate support, comfort, and consolation, particularly where words are insufficient. A hug usually demonstrates affection and emotional warmth, sometimes arising from joy or happiness when reunited with someone or seeing someone absent after a long time. A non-reciprocal hug may demonstrate a relational problem. A hug can range from a brief one second squeeze, with the arms not fully around the partner, to an extended holding. The length of a hug in any situation is socially and culturally determined. In the case of lovers, and occasionally others, the
      <a href="/wiki/Hip" title="Hip">
       hips
      </a>
      may also be pressed together.
     </p>
     <p>
      Unlike some other types of physical contact, a hug can be practiced publicly and privately without
      <a href="/wiki/Social_stigma" title="Social stigma">
       stigma
      </a>
      in many countries, religions and cultures, within families, and also across age and gender lines,
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      but is generally an indication that people are familiar with each other. Moving from a handshake (or touch-free) relationship to a hug relationship is a sign of a closer friendship such as best friends
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      An unexpected hug can be regarded as an invasion of a person's
      <a href="/wiki/Personal_space" title="Personal space">
       personal space
      </a>
      , but if it is reciprocated it is an indication that it is welcome. Some Western culture commentators advise avoiding hugs at work to prevent uncomfortable moments, especially with people who dislike hugging.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Also, a person, especially a
      <a href="/wiki/Child" title="Child">
       child
      </a>
      , may caress and hug a
      <a href="/wiki/Doll" title="Doll">
       doll
      </a>
      or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stuffed_animal" title="Stuffed animal">
       stuffed animal
      </a>
      . Young children will also hug their parents when they feel threatened by an unfamiliar person, although this may be regarded as clinging onto rather than hugging because it demonstrates a need for protection rather than affection.
     </p>
     <p>
      While less common, hugging may be undertaken as part of a ritual or social act in certain social groups. It is a custom in Latin cultures such as
      <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
       France
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
       Spain
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">
       Latin America
      </a>
      for male friends to hug (as well as slap each other on the back) in a joyous greeting. A similar hug, usually accompanied by a kiss on the cheek, is also becoming a custom among
      <a href="/wiki/West" title="West">
       Western
      </a>
      women at meeting or parting. In
      <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">
       Portugal
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
       Brazil
      </a>
      , it is common, mostly among males, to finish letters and emails with
      <i>
       Um abrao
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       Abrao
      </i>
      followed by the sender's signature. Similar formulas may be used in oral communication.
     </p>
     <p>
      In May 2009,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">
        The New York Times
       </a>
      </i>
      reported that "the hug has become the favorite social greeting when
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teenager" title="Teenager">
       teenagers
      </a>
      meet or part these days" in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A number of schools in the United States have issued bans on hugs, which in some cases have resulted in student-led protests against these bans.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">
       Roman Catholic
      </a>
      rite of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Mass" title="Holy Mass">
       Holy Mass
      </a>
      a hug may be substituted for a kiss or handshake during the
      <a href="/wiki/Kiss_of_peace" title="Kiss of peace">
       kiss of peace
      </a>
      ritual. Some cultures
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      do not use hugging as a sign of affection or love, such as the
      <a href="/wiki/Himba_people" title="Himba people">
       Himba
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">
       Namibia
      </a>
      . During the Islamic fasting month of
      <a href="/wiki/Ramadan" title="Ramadan">
       Ramadan
      </a>
      , it is
      <a href="/wiki/Halal" title="Halal">
       halal
      </a>
      (permissible) for someone to hug one's significant other during daylight hours if one has self-control. However if accompanied by libidinous urges, it is
      <a href="/wiki/Haram" title="Haram">
       haram
      </a>
      (sinful).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Hugging has been proven to have health benefits. One study has shown that hugs increase levels of
      <a href="/wiki/Oxytocin" title="Oxytocin">
       oxytocin
      </a>
      and reduce
      <a href="/wiki/Blood_pressure" title="Blood pressure">
       blood pressure
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A group hug has been found to be a useful tool in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Group_therapy" title="Group therapy">
       group therapy
      </a>
      to cement a sense of cohesion among the participants after a session,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pesso_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pesso-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      although it may cause discomfort for group members who shy away from physical contact.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pesso_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pesso-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      </a>
      do, since canines interpret putting a limb over another animal as a

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cuddling">
       Cuddling
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cuddling">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote">
      See also:
      <a href="/wiki/Cuddle_party" title="Cuddle party">
       Cuddle party
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Cuddling is a related form of
      <a href="/wiki/Physical_intimacy" title="Physical intimacy">
       physical intimacy
      </a>
      in which two people hold one another with each person's arms wrapped around the other's body. Cuddling is an act usually associated with lovers and family members though cuddling between friends is not unheard of. Similar to hugging, cuddling is a more affectionate and intimate embrace, normally done for a longer period of time (usually lasting from a few minutes to several hours). In contrast to hugging, which can often be a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nonverbal" title="Nonverbal">
       nonverbal
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Greeting" title="Greeting">
       greeting
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Parting_tradition" title="Parting tradition">
       parting tradition
      </a>
      , cuddling is usually shared between two people who are lying down together or sitting somewhere in an intimate manner. Like hugging, cuddling makes the body release oxytocin, which has
      <a href="/wiki/Oxytocin#Actions" title="Oxytocin">
       a variety of effects
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cuddling_Business">
       Cuddling Business
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cuddling Business">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Cuddling has become a profitable business worldwide for the many professional cuddlers who offer their cuddling services for a fee. Professional cuddlers promote their business as non-sexual and offer it those who are missing the touch and connection of another person.
     </p>
     <p>
      Some professional cuddlers prefer to open retail shops while booking is available via official websites. One of the first ones, Cuddle Up To Me, was launched in Portland, Oregon by
      <a href="/wiki/Samantha_Hess" title="Samantha Hess">
       Samantha Hess
      </a>
      and gained a lot of media attention worldwide. Following the success of Cuddle Up To Me - A number of online startups emerged. While some suggest choosing from a great variety of cuddlers (both male and female), others let anyone run their own business in any country. Cuddlr was the first mobile app (launched in 2014) and is mainly focused on offering free hugs.
      <a class="external text" href="http://cuddleup.com" rel="nofollow">
       CuddleUp.com
      </a>
      launched in early 2015 and is a network which connects professional cuddlers with clients and helps the latter hire a spooning partner. CuddleUp.com removes the barrier to entry and allows virtually anyone with access to the internet become a professional cuddler.
     </p>
     <p>
      Another way of running a cuddling business is
      <a href="/wiki/Cuddle_party" title="Cuddle party">
       cuddle parties
      </a>
      (or cuddle workshops, usually paid
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      Despite not everybody approving this kind of relationship and business model, it has proven to be successful. For example, Cuddling Con crowdfunding campaign has raised $169,000 and the Cuddlr app has hundreds of thousand downloads.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
