            <object height="355" width="425">

             This movie has everything you could possibly want: a Martian invasion, an S&amp;M queen dressed in skin-tight black leather, a cardboard robot with a police light on top of his head, and a disintegrator ray.

          <h3 class="entry-header">
           <a href="http://tonova.typepad.com/thesuddencurve/2007/07/admv-space-babe.html">
            Armed and Dangerous, Music Video, Space Babe :: Joi Lansing
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             Wow, gorgeous Joi Lansing is a TRIPLE threat
             <em>
              TSC
             </em>
             babe!
            </p>
            <p>
             You know, I used to watch the amazing Joi Lansing on
             <em>
              Love That Bob!
             </em>
             which was the rerun version of
             <em>
              The Bob Cummings Show
             </em>
             ... although that was when it was on Nik at Nite in the 1980's.
             <br/>
            </p>
            <p>
             But I do remember her as a regular on
             <em>
              The Beverly Hillbillies
             </em>
             as Lester Scruggs' wife... or was she Earl Flatt's wife? Oh well, I guess it doesn't really matter. All she ever did was look slinky and go shopping - once I seem to remember that Granny tried to teach her to cook, but I don't think it worked.
            </p>
            <p>
             Joi, a former Miss Hollywood and a Goldwyn Girl and a rival of Marlyn Monroe really was a pretty good actress and all around entertainer who was a very popular and successful blonde bombshell right at the time blonde bombshells dropped out of favor in movies. Suddenly she had to make a living modeling polka-dot bikinis ~ something she did exceptionally well,
             <em>
              woof!
             </em>
             She was part of the swanky world of Frank Sinatra in the 50's and worked steadily throughout the 60's as a television and stage performer and she became one of the most popular pin-ups of American soldiers in Viet Nam.
            </p>
            <br/>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Here she is vamping it up and singing the Elmer Bernstein title song from
             <em>
              The Silencers
             </em>
             - which, coincidentally, happens to be one of our favorite movies starring the great Dean Martin (who costarred with Joi in at least two movies) as super spy Matt Helm. Although in the movie the song is sung by Cyd Charisse.
            </p>
            <p>
             <object height="350" width="425">
              <param name="movie" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/cpbF9H0Taa0">
              </param>
              <param name="wmode" value="transparent">
              </param>
              <embed height="350" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/cpbF9H0Taa0" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="425" wmode="transparent">
              </embed>
             </object>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              posted by Tom Novak, who says Joi is a space babe in Queen of Outer Space, a movie so bad it's good.
             </strong>
            </p>

             <a href="http://www.tammybruce.com/">

It's a portrait, not an instructional photograph."
