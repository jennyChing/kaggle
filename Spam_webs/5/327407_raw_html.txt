            <h2 class="title">
             <a class="journal-entry-navigation-current" href="/blog/2010/1/16/the-three-wheeled-dale-1975.html">
              The Three-Wheeled Dale(1975)
             </a>
            </h2>
            <div class="journal-entry-tag journal-entry-tag-post-title">
             <span class="posted-on">
              <img alt="Date" class="inline-icon date-icon" rel="smalllight" src="/universal/images/transparent.png" title="Date"/>
              Saturday, January 16, 2010 at 2:57PM
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="body">
             <p>
              <span class="thumbnail-image-float-right ssNonEditable">
               <span>
                <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/7156208@N02/3500670703/">
                 <img alt="" src="/storage/thumbnails/3374620-5406661-thumbnail.jpg?__SQUARESPACE_CACHEVERSION=1263706167217"/>
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
              </strong>

             <p>
              <strong>

              <strong>

             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <p>
                <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                 My friend
                 <a href="http://twitter.com/misterberndt">
                  Andy
                 </a>
                 , an
                 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unsolved_Mysteries">
                  <em>
                   Unsolved Mysteries
                  </em>
                 </a>
                 nerd of the highest order, introduced me to
                </span>
                <a href="http://www.dailymotion.com/video/x8he8c_dale-car_tech">
                 <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                  an episode
                 </span>
                </a>
                <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                 of the series which looked at the Dale; an economical three-wheeled car of the future. The Dale was to be produced in the mid-1970s by the not-quite-futuristically-named
                </span>
                <span>
                 <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twentieth_Century_Motor_Car_Corporation">
                   Twentieth Century Motor Car Corporation
                  </a>
                  ,
                 </span>
                 <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                  and bore some resemblance to the General Motors
                 </span>
                 <a href="http://www.paleofuture.com/blog/2008/1/7/gms-shopping-cart-car-1964.html">
                  <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                   three-wheeled concept car
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                  (in reverse) from 1964
                 </span>
                </span>
                <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                 .
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                 Funny thing about the supposed 70 mile-per-gallon Dale? It was a fraud. There was no Dale car, and no intention to manufacture it. Twentieth Century Motor Car Corp was started by entrepreneur-poseur
                </span>
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liz_Carmichael">
                 <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                  Liz Carmichael
                 </span>
                </a>
                <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                 who, according to this
                </span>
                <a href="http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20065184,00.html">
                 <em>
                  <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                   People
                  </span>
                 </em>
                 <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                  magazine
                 </span>
                </a>
                <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                 article from 1975, was born Jerry Dean Michael.I love that Liz added the word "car" to her given last name.
                </span>
               </p>
               <strong>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <strong>
                   <strong>
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       <strong>
                        <strong>
                         <strong>
                          <strong>
                           <strong>
                            <strong>
                             <strong>
                              <strong>
                               <strong>
                                <strong>
                                 <strong>
                                  <strong>
                                   <strong>
                                    <strong>
                                     <strong>
                                      <strong>
                                       <strong>
                                        <strong>
                                         <strong>
                                          <strong>
                                           <strong>
                                            <strong>
                                             <strong>
                                              <strong>
                                               <strong>
                                                <p style="display: inline !important;">
                                                 <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                                                  Carmichael made off with thousands of dollars gleaned from excited inventors, until being arrested and charged with grand theft,fraudandsecuritiesviolations. She jumped bail but was again caught after the airing of this episode.
                                                 </span>
                                                </p>
                                                <strong>
                                                 <strong>
                                                  <strong>
                                                   <p style="display: inline !important;">
                                                    <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                                                     Remember kids, the Future can be used for both good and evil.
                                                    </span>
                                                    <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                                                    </span>
                                                   </p>
                                                  </strong>
                                                 </strong>
                                                </strong>
                                               </strong>

                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </strong>
                  </strong>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </strong>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               <span style="font-weight: 900;">
                <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                 "We went to the research and development lab and observed what appeared to be people
                </span>
                <em>
                 <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                  appearing
                 </span>
                </em>
                <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                 to be busy but in wandering through the lab I saw no evidence that they were designing a vehicle or were in the process of making a vehicle."
