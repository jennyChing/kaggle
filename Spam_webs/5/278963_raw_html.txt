         <p>
          <span>
           The Nativity and the 1914 truce are two remarkable stories of peace at Christmas. Order copies of our
           <a href="http://www.sgmlifewords.com/uk/resources/details/silent-night" target="_blank">
            Silent Night comic booklet
           </a>
           to tell them side by side.
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.sgmlifewords.com/uk/resources/details/silent-night-downloads" target="_blank">
           Download our free online resource pack
          </a>
          <span>
           to help you plan an unusual and thought-provoking Christmas service, carol event or school assembly.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           Follow these steps to plan your Christmas Truce service:
          </span>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           SHOW a short film to set the scene.
           <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ishSpxfrWNw" target="_blank">
            Stream
           </a>
           or
           <a href="http://vimeo.com/106932713" target="_blank">
            download
           </a>
           the Christmas Truce film
          </li>
          <li>
           TELL the story of the truce and the first Christmas story.
           <span>
            Use the illustrated Silent Night PowerPoint slides in the
            <a href="http://www.sgmlifewords.com/uk/resources/details/silent-night-downloads" target="_blank">
             Silent Night download pack
            </a>
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           REFLECT on what both stories mean today.
           <span>
            Use our sermon notes to plan your talk and download a sketch with no lines to learn and suitable for children to take part - all available in our
            <a href="http://www.sgmlifewords.com/uk/resources/details/silent-night-downloads" target="_blank">
             downloadableresource pack
            </a>
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           PRAY for peace in your community, and further afield.
           <span>
            <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Igff12t3DBk" target="_blank">
             Stream
            </a>
            or
            <a href="http://vimeo.com/108594781" target="_blank">
             download
            </a>
            the Silent Night reflection film
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           GIVE each person at the service a copy of the Silent Night booklet
           <span>
            , so that people can continue to reflect over the holiday period.
            <a href="http://www.sgmlifewords.com/uk/resources/details/silent-night" target="_blank">
             Order Silent Night here
            </a>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          There are even more Silent Night resources available from our partner
          <a href="http://www.hopetogether.org.uk/Groups/194465/HOPE/Mission_Moments/Christmas/Christmas.aspx#.VE5SHPmsVQ0" target="_blank">
           HOPE
          </a>
          . All around the country, churches will be re-telling the story of the 1914 Christmas Truce. A new version of Silent Night (with a new verse and chorus) has been written and many events will be using
          <a href="http://www.sgmlifewords.com/uk/resources/details/ww1-johns-gospel" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            SGM Lifewords
           </strong>
           WW1 Anniversary Gospels
          </a>
          , as well as Silent Night booklets.
         </p>
         <p>
          For more resources and to host your own HOPE Silent Night carol event, visit
          <a href="http://silentnightcarols.org/" target="_blank">
           silentnightcarols.org
          </a>
          . Youll find song sheets, more drama ideas, ways to pray, and the words of the new version of Silent Night.
         </p>
