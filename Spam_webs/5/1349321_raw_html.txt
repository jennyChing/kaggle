     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Sarah Palin Interviews with Katie Couric
      </b>
      were a series of interviews of the 2008
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       U.S.
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">
       Republican
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice_President" title="Vice President">
       vice presidential
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nominee" title="Nominee">
       nominee
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Palin" title="Sarah Palin">
       Sarah Palin
      </a>
      conducted by
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/CBS_Evening_News" title="CBS Evening News">
        CBS Evening News
       </a>
      </i>
      anchor
      <a href="/wiki/Katie_Couric" title="Katie Couric">
       Katie Couric
      </a>
      . They were recorded and broadcast on television in several programs before the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2008" title="United States presidential election, 2008">
       2008 U.S. presidential election
      </a>
      . Couric received the
      <a href="/wiki/Alfred_I._duPont%E2%80%93Columbia_University_Award" title="Alfred I. duPontColumbia University Award">
       Alfred I. duPontColumbia University Award
      </a>
      and the Walter Cronkite Award for Journalism Excellence for the interview.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cronkite-huff_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-cronkite-huff-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cronkite-rr_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-cronkite-rr-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cronkite-cbs_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-cronkite-cbs-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The interviews were widely seen as a disaster for Palin's image and for the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_McCain_Presidential_Campaign,_2008" title="John McCain Presidential Campaign, 2008">
       McCain Campaign
      </a>
      , and were cited by many as the cause of a turning of the tide of public opinion against her.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">
       Origins
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarah_Palin_interviews_with_Katie_Couric&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Couric interview was preceded by heavy media scrutiny over the McCain campaign's alleged unwillingness to allow press access to Palin.
     </p>
     <p>
      Palin explains in her
      <a href="/wiki/Going_Rogue:_An_American_Life" title="Going Rogue: An American Life">
       memoirs
      </a>
      that from the beginning
      <a href="/wiki/Nicolle_Wallace" title="Nicolle Wallace">
       Nicolle Wallace
      </a>
      , a senior McCain staffer, pushed for Couric and the CBS Evening News. "The campaign's general strategy involved coming out with a network anchor, someone they felt had treated John well on the trail thus far. My suggestion was that we be consistent with that strategy and start talking to outlets like FOX and the Wall Street Journal. I really didn't have a say in which press I was going to talk to, but for some reason Nicolle seemed compelled to get me on the Katie bandwagon," wrote Palin.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goingrogue_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-goingrogue-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      "Katie really likes you," Wallace said according to Palin. "She's a working mom and admires you as a working mom. She has teenage daughters like you. She just relates to you...believe me, I know her very well. I've worked with her...She just has such low self-esteem...She just feels she can't trust anybody. She wants you to like her. You know what? We'll schedule a segment with her, If it doesn't go well, if there's no chemistry, we won't do any others."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goingrogue_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-goingrogue-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Wallace disputed Palin's account. "The whole notion there was a conversation where I tried to cajole her into a conversation with Katie [Couric] is fiction".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Wallace earlier had praised the channel, We had no input on usage...we had no ground rules on the interview. I think thats pretty unprecedented. A lot of people negotiate platforms. We didnt negotiate platforms or air dates.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYO-Fantastic_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYO-Fantastic-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      "We were initially supposed to interview hersit down with her in Philadelphia on Sunday and travel with Senator McCain and Governor Palin on that Monday," Couric recalled. "And then the campaign felt they didn't want a week to go by without hearing anything from Governor Palin because they were doling out the interviews very selectively. So they decided when she was visiting some world leaders at the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">
       UN
      </a>
      , that that would be an opportunity for her to sit down that morning and talk to me and it was very serendipitous for us, because we couldthat opened the door to a lot of interesting foreign policy questions. And, also, in addition to that, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Global_financial_crisis_of_2008%E2%80%932009" title="Global financial crisis of 20082009">
       financial crisis
      </a>
      was sort of really heating up during that week, so that was another opportunity. Then, we had scheduled an interview the following Monday, during which we were going to talk about a lot of domestic and social issues, so they gave us tremendous access."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Couric-Letterman_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Couric-Letterman-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Newsweek reported that at the time of the Couric interview, Palin felt that she had been overmanaged for her first one-on-one debut with a network anchor,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlie_Gibson" title="Charlie Gibson">
       Charlie Gibson
      </a>
      of ABC and "rebuffed Wallace's help with her Couric interview."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newsweek-GreatDebates_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Newsweek-GreatDebates-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      McCain advisers said that Palin "did not have the time or focus to prepare for the interview."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-internalbattles_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYT-internalbattles-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      "She did not say, 'I will not prepare,'" a McCain adviser said. "She just didnt have a bandwidth to do a mock interview session the way we had prepared before. She was just overloaded."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-internalbattles_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYT-internalbattles-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Format">
       Format
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h2>
     <p>
      CBS News producers segmented key moments from the interviews over the course of several days and on multiple formats, including
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Early_Show" title="The Early Show">
        The Early Show
       </a>
      </i>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/CBS_Evening_News" title="CBS Evening News">
       Evening News
      </a>
      , and the Internet.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYO-Fantastic_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYO-Fantastic-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The New York Observer noted that this "prolonged the interviews saliency in the news cycle," and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bill_Kristol" title="Bill Kristol">
       Bill Kristol
      </a>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/Fox_News_Channel" title="Fox News Channel">
       Fox News Channel
      </a>
      commentator who was a prominent supporter of Palin, referred to the network's seemingly never-ending installments as a nine-thousand-part interview.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYO-Fantastic_6-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYO-Fantastic-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Fallout">
       Fallout
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarah_Palin_interviews_with_Katie_Couric&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fallout">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The interviews aired on
      <i>
       CBS Evening News
      </i>
      showed ratings increases on both nights and clips posted on YouTube garnered more than 10 million views.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newsweek-GreatDebates_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Newsweek-GreatDebates-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/National_Review" title="National Review">
        National Review
       </a>
      </i>
      editor Rich Lowry called Palin's performance in the interview "dreadful."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">
        The New York Times
       </a>
      </i>
      television critic
      <a href="/wiki/Alessandra_Stanley" title="Alessandra Stanley">
       Alessandra Stanley
      </a>
      described the interview as "in some ways [...] the worst" interview Palin had done. The exchange on Russia was "startling," her answer "surprisingly wobbly." While it's "perhaps understandable" that Palin "felt nervous," it still "wasnt a reassuring performance."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Beliefnet" title="Beliefnet">
       Beliefnet
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/Rod_Dreher" title="Rod Dreher">
       Rod Dreher
      </a>
      wrote that he was "well and truly embarrassed" for Palin. She's "a good woman who might well be a great governor of Alaska," but this was a "train wreck."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The interviews were later
      <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live_parodies_of_Sarah_Palin" title="Saturday Night Live parodies of Sarah Palin">
       parodied
      </a>
      on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">
        Saturday Night Live
       </a>
      </i>
      , with
      <a href="/wiki/Tina_Fey" title="Tina Fey">
       Tina Fey
      </a>
      as Palin.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      While helping
      <a href="/wiki/Seth_Meyers" title="Seth Meyers">
       Seth Meyers
      </a>
      write the sketch, Fey decided to use Palin's answer regarding her opinion on the bank bailout nearly verbatim. Fey later said on the
      <a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">
       Late Show with David Letterman
      </a>
      that, in answering that question, Palin "got lost in a
      <a href="/wiki/Corn_maze" title="Corn maze">
       corn maze
      </a>
      ," noticeably struggling to find an answer and meandering between several seemingly unrelated topics such as health care, job creation, lowering government spending, international trade, and lowering taxes, ultimately not stating a clear position for or against it.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-palinfey_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-palinfey-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      CNN commentator
      <a href="/wiki/Jack_Cafferty" title="Jack Cafferty">
       Jack Cafferty
      </a>
      was particularly critical of Palin's answer to the bailout question, saying that if Palin being "one 72-year-old's heartbeat away from being President of the United States... doesn't scare the hell out of you, it should" and that in all his years of covering politics, "that was one of the most pathetic pieces of tape I have ever seen from someone aspiring to one of the highest offices in this country."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The airing of the Couric interviews coincided with a collapse in her approval ratings and a loss of McCain's gains among white women.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the immediate aftermath of the interview, Palin voiced irritation she had with Couric's interview:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-palinannoyed_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-palinannoyed-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <blockquote class="templatequote">
      <p>
       The Sarah Palin in those interviews was a little bit annoyed. It's like, man, no matter what you say, you are going to get clobbered. If you choose to answer a question, you're going to get clobbered on the answer. If you choose to try to pivot and go on to another subject that you believe that Americans want to hear about, you'll get clobbered for that too. [...] In those Katie Couric interviews, I did feel that there were a lot of things that she was missing, in terms of an opportunity to ask what a VP candidate stands for, what the values are represented in our ticket.
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      Palin also criticized Couric's question on what newspapers and magazines she read:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-palinannoyed_18-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-palinannoyed-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <blockquote class="templatequote">
      <p>
       My response to her, I guess it was kind of filtered. But, I was sort of taken aback, like, the suggestion was, you're way up there in a far away place in Alaska. You know, that there are publications in the rest of the world that are read by many. And I was taken aback by that because I don't know, the suggestion that this was a little bit of perhaps we're not in tune with the rest of the world.
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      According to campaign manager
      <a href="/wiki/Rick_Davis_(politics)" title="Rick Davis (politics)">
       Rick Davis
      </a>
      , Palin thought the questions would be softer than they were: "She was under the impression the Couric thing was going to be easier than it was. Everyones guard was down for the Couric interview."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NR-13Nov08_19-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NR-13Nov08-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      "I knew it didnt go well the first day, and then we gave her a couple of other segments after that," Palin said in a retrospective with conservative filmmaker
      <a href="/wiki/John_Ziegler_(talk_show_host)" title="John Ziegler (talk show host)">
       John Ziegler
      </a>
      on the Couric interview. "My question to the campaign was, after it didnt go well the first day, why were we going to go back for more? Because of however it works in that upper echelon of power brokering, in the media and with spokespersons, it was told [to] me, yeah, were going to go back for more. And going back for more was not a wise decision, either."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ziegler-Palin_20-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ziegler-Palin-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After the election, Couric appeared on the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">
        Late Show with David Letterman
       </a>
      </i>
      and discussed her interviews with Palin, especially in regards to her question on what newspapers or magazines she read to "stay informed and to understand the world":
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Couric-Letterman_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Couric-Letterman-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       COURIC: Im not sure whether she was afraid to offend certain people, by, she would offend conservatives by saying she read the
       <i>
        New York Times
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <br/>
       LETTERMAN: Or people who dont read. She was afraid of offending people who dont read. Maybe that was it.
      </p>
      <br/>
      COURIC: Even in the post-election interviews, Dave, that she's done, nobody has really asked her, "Why didn't you answer that question?"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Couric-Letterman_7-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Couric-Letterman-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      Palin directly responded to that question in Ziegler's interview, "Because, Katie, you're not the center of everybody's universe, maybe that's why they didn't think to ask that question among so many other things to be asked. To me the question was more along the lines of: Do you read? What do you guys do up there? What is it that you read? And perhaps I was just too flippant in my answer back to her..."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ziegler-Palin_20-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ziegler-Palin-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Ziegler has called the awarding of the Walter Cronkite award to Couric an "outrage." "What really happened here is that Katie Couric showed Governor Palin that she had an agenda on the abortion issue," Ziegler said. "She kept coming back to it time and time again, obviously trying to trap Governor Palin into saying something stupid or extreme. Everything after that has to be seen in the context of that episode, because Governor Palin never trusted Katie Couric after that. It is very, very obvious that Katie Couric had an agenda and that she is being rewarded for having pursued that agenda."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ziegler-outraged_21-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ziegler-outraged-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On the other hand, former Republican presidential candidate
      <a href="/wiki/Mike_Huckabee" title="Mike Huckabee">
       Mike Huckabee
      </a>
      defended Couric, "Now I must say I did not think that ... the Katie Couric interviews were unfair. In fact, if anything, Katie Couric was extraordinarily gentle, even helpful. [Palin] just ... I don't know what happened. I can't explain it. It was not a good interview. I'm being charitable."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Steve_Schmidt" title="Steve Schmidt">
       Steve Schmidt
      </a>
      , McCain's senior campaign strategist and advisor, later reflected on the interview, first by defending Couric by saying that there were no "gotcha questions" or "unfair questions," and then added, "I think it was the most consequential interview from a negative perspective that a candidate for national office has gone through, not since
      <a href="/wiki/Roger_Mudd" title="Roger Mudd">
       Roger Mudd
      </a>
      interviewed
      <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">
       Ted Kennedy
      </a>
      in the late 1970s."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schmidt_23-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Schmidt-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This interview was notable for Kennedy's vague and incoherent response to the question of "[w]hy do you want to be President?" which some say derailed Kennedy's presidential ambitions.
     </p>
     <p>
      Accepting the Walter Cronkite Award for Special Achievement for National Impact on the 2008 Campaign, Couric said, "I believe one of the reasons that the interview I conducted with Sarah Palin was so impactful is because it wasnt done through any particular ideological prism. I was so mindful of my personal affect, knowing every head tilt, expression, and follow-up question would be carefully dissected for any evidence of bias. My goal was simply to be a conduit to allow her to express her views and give those watching a chance to come to their own conclusions."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In November 2010, Palin ruled out ever granting Couric another interview, accusing Couric of bias and intentionally trying to create a controversy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In April 2012, Palin temporarily assumed Couric's old job as
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Today_(NBC_program)" title="Today (NBC program)">
        Today Show
       </a>
      </i>
      co-host for the April 3rd show, competing with Couric's week-long run as host of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">
        Good Morning America
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
