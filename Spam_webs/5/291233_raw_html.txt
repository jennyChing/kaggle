                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Chocolate Truffles
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-9118548938673841402" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_wjO9ZSr8ZTA/S-iAelqqsSI/AAAAAAAAIqU/5TwAGFaVUes/s1600/SSWF-ChocTruffles.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5469763010436968738" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_wjO9ZSr8ZTA/S-iAelqqsSI/AAAAAAAAIqU/5TwAGFaVUes/s800/SSWF-ChocTruffles.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 500px; height: 350px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     This is a super easy Chocolate Truffles recipe that my daughter made for me on Mothers Day. She said she learnt how to make them from a Japanese cooking website. There is no cooking involved here, apart from melting the chocolate. Make sure you use the best or your favorite chocolate for this recipe. Have fun making them.
                     <br/>
                     <span id="fullpost">
                      <br/>
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_wjO9ZSr8ZTA/S-iAePVVWLI/AAAAAAAAIqM/IIWCrOCcPsQ/s1600/SSWF-ChocTruffles1.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5469763004441909426" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_wjO9ZSr8ZTA/S-iAePVVWLI/AAAAAAAAIqM/IIWCrOCcPsQ/s800/SSWF-ChocTruffles1.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 500px; height: 350px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-weight: bold;">
                       Chocolate Truffles
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <a href="http://sites.google.com/site/seasaltwithfoodrecipes/chocolate-truffles" style="font-style: italic;">
                       (Printable Recipe)
                       <br/>
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-weight: bold;">
                       Ingredients
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      200 g Dark Chocolate, any flavor
                      <br/>
                      6 Tbsp Whipped Cream
                      <br/>
                      2 tsp Vegetable Oil
                      <br/>
                      Dutch-Process Cocoa Powder, for coating
                      <br/>
                      Boiling Water
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-weight: bold;">
                       Method
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Pour the boiling water in a large mixing bowl. Then place a medium-mixing bowl on top of the boiling water. Add the cream and warm thoroughly. Mix in the chocolate, and stir until melted. Then add the oil and stir until smooth. Cover and freeze just until firm enough to mound in a spoon, about 40 minutes.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Line the bottom of a baking sheet with a plastic wrap. Scoop out the filling by tablespoonfuls and drop onto the sheet, spacing them evenly. Cover and freeze until almost firm but still pliable, about 30 minutes.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Spread the cocoa on a flat plate. Place each chocolate mound into a piece of plastic wrap and roll in between your palms into a smooth ball, then roll in cocoa powder to coat evenly.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Makes 20 Chocolate Truffles
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
