       <header>
        <h1 class="postlisttitle entry-title">
         <a href="http://www.thetruthaboutguns.com/2015/05/daniel-zimmerman/three-foreign-battle-rifles-you-should-know-about/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Three Foreign Battle Rifles You Should Know About">
          Three Foreign Battle Rifles You Should Know About
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="author vcard">
          By
          <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.thetruthaboutguns.com/author/daniel-zimmerman/" title="View all posts by Dan Zimmerman">
           Dan Zimmerman
          </a>
         </span>
         on May 6, 2015
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://truthaboutguns-zippykid.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ftd-foreignguns-620x330.jpg">
          <img alt="ftd-foreignguns-620x330" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-358269" height="330" src="http://truthaboutguns-zippykid.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ftd-foreignguns-620x330.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         By Jake Moore via
         <a href="http://www.wideopenspaces.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          wideopenspaces.com
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Want to know some history on three badass foreign battle rifles? You got it.In 2003, United States Special Operations Command sanctioned the approval for a battle rifle that was as versatile and effective as the U.S. Special Forces operators that wielded them. Manufactured in Herstal, Belgium, the
         <a href="http://www.fnhusa.com/l/products/scar-family/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          FN SCAR
         </a>
         or Special Forces Combat Assault Rifle is exactly what the name implies and then some . . .
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <span id="more-358268">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_358274" style="width: 660px">
         <a href="http://truthaboutguns-zippykid.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/FN_SCAR-H_Standard.jpg">
          <img alt="FN_SCAR-H_(Standard)" class="wp-image-358274" height="189" src="http://truthaboutguns-zippykid.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/FN_SCAR-H_Standard.jpg" width="650"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          FN SCAR-H
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         This GLOCK of the assault rifle world was tailored to fit the specific requirements proposed by U.S. Special Forces. The SCAR is rugged, accurate, and reliable; capable of one MOAwith a maximum effective range of 800 meters. Its available in 7.6251 NATO with a 20-round magazine, or 30 rounds of 5.56X45 NATO, as well as assorted barrel lengths ranging from seven inches on out to 20 inches.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_358273" style="width: 660px">
         <a href="http://truthaboutguns-zippykid.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-05-at-12.49.41-PM.png">
          <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-05-05 at 12.49.41 PM" class="wp-image-358273" height="276" src="http://truthaboutguns-zippykid.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-05-at-12.49.41-PM.png" width="650"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          HK 417
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Precision manufactured in the heart of Germany, the
         <a href="http://www.heckler-koch.com/en/products/military/assault-rifles/hk417/hk417-a2-13/overview.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          HK 417
         </a>
         is a semi-auto .308 (7.62x51NATO) that boasts excellent marks for accuracy and reliability, capable of 1.3 MOA from a 12-inch barrel and sub-MOAgroups through a 20-inch barrel. It has a magazine capacity of 20 rounds and can effectively engage targets well beyond 800 meters.
        </p>
        <p>
         This rifle is being fielded by U.S. Special Forces operators and is in very close competition with the SCAR for the title of best modern universal battle rifle. The ergonomics and features are similar to the classic M4 or M16 making this weapon feel very familiar to the U.S. soldier. It is also highly customizable but, unlike its M4 counterpart, the 417 utilizes a piston system rather than direct gas impingement which allows the 417 to operate cooler and much cleaner. You can find the HK 417 under its civilian alias
         <a href="http://hk-usa.com/hk-models/mr762a1lrp/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          MR762
         </a>
         for somewhere close to $4,500 dollars.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_358272" style="width: 660px">
         <a href="http://truthaboutguns-zippykid.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Israel_Weapon_Industries_IWI_ACE_52_7.62x51mm_NATO_Battle_Rifle_2.jpg">
          <img alt="Israel_Weapon_Industries_IWI_ACE_52_7.62x51mm_NATO_Battle_Rifle_2" class="wp-image-358272 size-full" height="211" src="http://truthaboutguns-zippykid.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Israel_Weapon_Industries_IWI_ACE_52_7.62x51mm_NATO_Battle_Rifle_2.jpg" width="650"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          IWI ACE 52
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="http://www.israel-weapon.com/default.asp?catid=%7B5A1E08D3-BCC0-4EC2-991E-BE8413B9A89A%7D" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          ACE 52
         </a>
         is a 7.6251 battle rifle developed and produced by Israel Weapon Industries in Ramat HaSharon, Israel. The ACE 52s predecessor, the
         <a href="http://www.israel-weapon.com/?catid=%7B813E02F8-D6E3-48B1-A0D4-171BBDD46E28%7D" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          GALIL
         </a>
         , was modeled after the fabled Kalashnikov AK-47 design known for its legendary reliability and has since been upgraded to fit the growing needs of the Israeli military.
        </p>
        <p>
         The rifle is lightweight with manageable recoil for a .308, and is highly customizable with the addition of a MIL-STD-1913 rail system. It is available in a host of different barrel lengths including the 20-inch sniper variant capable of sub MOAaccuracy. The ACE 52 has an effective range of 800 meters and can hold 25 rounds in the magazine surpassing the normal 20-round capacity of the HK 417 and SCAR-H which is much appreciated by the soldier in combat. The rifle comes in black and is priced around $2,800 dollars.
        </p>
        <p>
         These three weapons systems are at the forefront of modern firearms innovation and are currently being fielded by militaries around the globe.
        </p>

             <div class="comment-body">
              <p>
               Ive found that Springfield Armory (the commercial gun maker, not the real Springfield Armory) rifles, rack grade, hold 1.5 to 2 groups at 100 with M80 ball ammo, and better with Federal match 168gr loads and really nicely with Lake City match ammo from the late 60s, which admittedly is like shooting unicorns today. At least, thats my experience from the early 90s vintage of commercial Springfield Armory M1As. Ive not shot much with any rifles made more recently by SA.
              </p>
              <p>
               The Garand (the parent design of the M14) was good for about 2MOA, and usually did better. Most battle rifles did 2MOA. People today forget that battle rifles of 100 to 60 years ago were made by manual machines and gaged for acceptance. There were none of the tools we have today for mass precision measurement of finished parts  no CMMs, no laser scanning, no CNC machines with Renishaw probes, never mind CNC machines that would compensate for tool wear, machine heating, etc. If you wanted to measure something down to 0.001, you either had a set of gages made to over/under the acceptance, or you hauled out the manual measurement tools and slowly started taking down numbers. If you wanted to machine sizes down to 0.001 so you hit them by design, rather than chance, you had to have a ready supply of sharp tooling, a tool guy ready to re-grind and size tools all day long, and then you had to tolerate a bunch of older men with really short tempers and no tolerance for stupidity in your shop. Most industrial management didnt tolerate such men well, if at all.
              </p>
              <p>
               In that manufacturing environment, 2MOA is pretty good.
              </p>
              <p>
               Today, you kids who are making a mess of my lawn sniff and pout when you buy a brand-new rifle and it just holds a minute of angle. 50 years ago, thats what shooters called a keeper of a rifle, one you didnt sell when your buddies started to covet it and got to waving money, pickup trucks, hunting dogs and other such items under your nose to get you to part with it.
              </p>
