         <h1 class="title entry-title">
          Magic mushrooms expand your mind and amplify your brains dreaming areas  heres how
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="small">
          By
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://disinfo.com/author/majestic/" rel="author" title="Posts by majestic">
            majestic
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="small">
          on
         </span>
         <abbr class="date time published updated" title="2014-07-07T12:30:29-0400">
          July 7, 2014
         </abbr>
         <span class="small">
          in
         </span>
         <span class="categories">
          <a href="http://disinfo.com/category/news/" title="View all items in News">
           News
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments comments">
          <a href="http://disinfo.com/2014/07/magic-mushrooms-expand-mind-amplify-brains-dreaming-areas-heres/#comments">
           <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="120166 http://disinfo.com/?p=120166">
            <i class="icon-comment">
            </i>
            17
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <script class="lockerdome-js-lite" data-box="box-off" data-follow_up="share" data-href="http://disinfo.com/2014/07/magic-mushrooms-expand-mind-amplify-brains-dreaming-areas-heres/" data-owner="7565172005012737" data-size="custom-medium" data-size_specifics="24" data-style="text" data-width="fixed-width" src="//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/widget_interest_1_0.js">

        <section class="entry">
         <p>
          <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File%3ADried_Cubensis.jpg" title="By Erik Fenderson (Own work) [Public domain], via Wikimedia Commons">
           <img alt="Dried Cubensis" class="alignright" height="228" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Dried_Cubensis.jpg/512px-Dried_Cubensis.jpg" width="320"/>
          </a>
          Definitely safer than
          <a href="http://disinfo.com/2014/07/psychonauts-explore-unknown-world-legal-highs/">
           synthetic drugs
          </a>
          , but still not for the faint of heart, heres how magic mushrooms expand your mind, expounded by Robin Carhart-Harris, Post-doctoral Researcher, Centre for Neuropsychopharmacology at Imperial College London, at
          <a href="https://theconversation.com/magic-mushrooms-expand-your-mind-and-amplify-your-brains-dreaming-areas-heres-how-28754" target="_blank">
           The Conversation
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p style="color: #383838;">
           Psychedelic drugs alter consciousness in a profound and novel way that increases the breadth and fluency of cognition. However, until recently, we were unable to offer an explanation for how the brain was altered to account for these effects.
          </p>
          <p style="color: #383838;">
           In
           <a href="http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/hbm.22562/abstract" style="font-weight: bold; color: #557585;">
            a new study
           </a>
           , published in Human Brain Mapping, we scanned the brains of volunteers who had been injected with psilocybin  the chemical found in magic mushrooms which gives a psychedelic experience  and a control group who hadnt, and discovered two key things: that psilocybin increased the amplitude (or volume) of activity in regions of the brain
           <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9236630" style="font-weight: bold; color: #557585;">
            that are reliably activated
           </a>
           during dream sleep and form part of the brains ancient emotion system; and that psychedelics facilitate a state of expanded consciousness  meaning that the breath of associations made by the brain and the ease by which they are visited is enhanced under the drugs.
          </p>
          <h2 style="color: #383838;">
           Ego and emotion
          </h2>
          <p style="color: #383838;">
           This finding of a similar pattern to dream activity is intriguing. While the psychedelic state
           <a href="http://karnacbooks.metapress.com/content/n5225u3264047605/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #557585;">
            has been previously compared
           </a>
           with dreaming,
           <a href="http://www.jneurosci.org/content/33/38/15171.short" style="font-weight: bold; color: #557585;">
            the opposite effect
           </a>
           has been observed in the brain network from which we get our sense of self (called the default-mode network or ego-system). Put simply, while activity became louder in the emotion system, it became more disjointed and so quieter in the ego system.
          </p>
          <p style="color: #383838;">
           Evidence from this study, and also preliminary data from an ongoing brain imaging study with LSD, appear to support the principle that the psychedelic state rests on disorganised activity in the ego system permitting disinhibited activity in the emotion system. And such an effect may explain why psychedelics have been considered useful facilitators of
           <a href="http://www.popsci.com/science/article/2013-04/new-science-lsd-therapy" style="font-weight: bold; color: #557585;">
            certain forms of psychotherapy
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p style="color: #383838;">
           We also looked at the range of connectivity configurations  or motifs  in the emotion system and found that a broader range of motifs emerged under psilocybin, and this effect began with the onset of the drugs psychological effects.
          </p>
          <p style="color: #383838;">
           This is an entirely novel analysis and its validity needs to be further tested  but it may offer an initial insight into the biological basis of the often described consciousness-expansion that is one of the hallmarks of a psychedelic experience
          </p>
         </blockquote>
