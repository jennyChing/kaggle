        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Whats In My Bag: Andy Hendriksen Edition
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn" href="http://www.thephoblographer.com/author/andy-hendriksen/">
           Andy Hendriksen
          </a>
         </span>

          02/15/2012
         </abbr>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">

        <p>
         <a href="http://3zgehi1uaxi23dphbrgqa50r6z.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_6018.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-19544" height="450" src="http://3zgehi1uaxi23dphbrgqa50r6z.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_6018-680x450.jpg" title="ahendriksen_020612_02" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im the new guy here at The Phoblographer, so its time I inaugurate myself by explaining exactly what I carry with me when I go out shooting.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive been taking pictures since I was a kid, but only recently have I taken the time to evaluate my gear and shape my camera bag around exactly the type of photographs Im taking. Having moved to Los Angeles recently, street photography seemed like an obvious transition for me. I fell in love with it, and it is now my primary style of shooting. Ive now molded my gear around my needs, and while very simple, it handles just about anything I can throw at it. Lets take a look!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-19540">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         The Bag
        </h2>
        <p>
         I use a few different bags, but when I want to bring
         <em>
          most
         </em>
         of my gear with me, theres only one I use: the
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0045NYLT4/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0045NYLT4" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0045NYLT4/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0045NYLT4', 'Tamrac Rally 5');">
          Tamrac Rally 5
         </a>
         . Theres a lot to love about this bag. First, it doesnt look like a camera bag. When seen from afar, it looks like nothing more than a standard messenger bag. Its also comfortable to wear, not too big, and very well built. Lastly, it seems to be just about the perfect size for my gear, which includes two cameras, two lenses, and an iPad. More on that later.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Gear
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.thephoblographer.com/2012/02/15/whats-in-my-bag-andy-hendriksen-edition/dsc_6030/" rel="attachment wp-att-19545">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-19545" height="466" src="http://3zgehi1uaxi23dphbrgqa50r6z.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_6030-680x466.jpg" title="ahendriksen_020612_03" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0043RS864/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0043RS864" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0043RS864/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0043RS864', 'Fuji X100');">
          <strong>
           Fuji X100
          </strong>
         </a>
          This is my main camera these days, as Im primarily doing street photography. Its small, light, and spits out really fantastic looking files. It also performs very well in low light, and is an absolute blast to shoot with. For more on my thoughts about the X100, see
         <a href="http://www.thephoblographer.com/2012/02/07/why-the-fuji-x100-will-eliminate-your-fear-of-street-photography">
          my post about shooting on the street with it
         </a>
         . Ive pimped out mine with my favorite strap from Artisan and Artist, along with a soft shutter release from Tom Abrahamsson. Both can be had over at
         <a href="http://www.popflash.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.popflash.com/', 'PopFlash.Photo');" target="_blank">
          PopFlash.Photo
         </a>
         . These little things make a big difference.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.thephoblographer.com/2012/02/15/whats-in-my-bag-andy-hendriksen-edition/dscf1025/" rel="attachment wp-att-19551">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-19551" height="451" src="http://3zgehi1uaxi23dphbrgqa50r6z.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSCF1025-680x451.jpg" title="ahendriksen_020612_07" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0042X9LC4/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0042X9LC4" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0042X9LC4/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0042X9LC4', 'Nikon D7000');">
          <strong>
           Nikon D7000
          </strong>
         </a>
          This camera is my workhorse. I cant say enough about the D7000. Beautiful files, great in low light, wonderful feeling shutter button, dual SD card slots, and so much more. It has the tendency to scare people when shooting on the street with it, but when Im shooting landscapes, portraits, or events, its just perfect. Ill go full-frame someday, but for now, the D7000 is an absolute gem, and I have no complaints.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.thephoblographer.com/2012/02/15/whats-in-my-bag-andy-hendriksen-edition/dscf1024/" rel="attachment wp-att-19546">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-19546" height="492" src="http://3zgehi1uaxi23dphbrgqa50r6z.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSCF1024-680x492.jpg" title="ahendriksen_020612_04" width="680"/>
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001S2PPT0/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001S2PPT0" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001S2PPT0/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001S2PPT0', 'Nikon 35mm f/1.8G');">
          <strong>
           Nikon 35mm f/1.8G
          </strong>
         </a>
          You could weld this lens to my D7000 and I wouldnt complain. Its one of Nikons least expensive lenses, and one of my absolute favorites. Its inexpensive, so I dont mind carrying it everywhere, its a wonderfully versatile focal length, and its plenty fast enough for great low light shooting. Its been a long time since Ive done a shoot without using this lens at some point, and Ive gotten some really wonderful photos from it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.thephoblographer.com/2012/02/15/whats-in-my-bag-andy-hendriksen-edition/dsc_6033/" rel="attachment wp-att-19547">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-19547" height="450" src="http://3zgehi1uaxi23dphbrgqa50r6z.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_6033-680x450.jpg" title="ahendriksen_020612_05" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000ZMCILW/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000ZMCILW" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000ZMCILW/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000ZMCILW', 'Nikon 18-55mm f3.5-5.6 VR');">
           Nikon 18-55mm f3.5-5.6 VR
          </a>
         </strong>
          This
         <strong>
          <em>
           is
          </em>
         </strong>
         Nikons cheapest zoom lens, at just over $100. But I gotta say, I think its also Nikons most underrated lens. Yes, it feels cheap and plasticky in the hand, but its fantastically sharp at all focal lengths, and having VR in a lens this cheap is pretty great. And again, no big loss if its damaged or stolen. Ive done side-by-side comparisons with the vastly more expensive Nikon 16-85mm f/3.5-5.6 VR, and theyre nearly identical in every way other than build quality.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.thephoblographer.com/2012/02/15/whats-in-my-bag-andy-hendriksen-edition/dsc_6045/" rel="attachment wp-att-19548">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-19548" height="469" src="http://3zgehi1uaxi23dphbrgqa50r6z.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/DSC_6045-680x469.jpg" title="ahendriksen_020612_06" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Apple iPad 2 w/ Camera Connection Kit
         </strong>
          Ive made a place for the iPad in just about everything I do, and photography is no exception. I use it (along with the camera connection kit) quite a bit during the day to backup my photos after I fill up an SD card. Its also a lot of fun to use apps like
         <a href="http://www.snapseed.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.snapseed.com/', 'Snapseed');" target="_blank">
          Snapseed
         </a>
         to very quickly manipulate photos and share them socially. Its also a great way of showing off your portfolio to potential clients, not to mention the 500px app is fantastic!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Apple iPhone 4s
         </strong>
          Obviously the iPhone 4s goes with me everywhere, not just when shooting. But its an important piece for me when shooting because it actually has a fantastic camera, and like the iPad 2, has a plethora of apps that make taking pictures with it quite fun. ProHDR, Instagram, and Snapseed have made iPhone photography special, and sharing very easy.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0046EBTQ0/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0046EBTQ0" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0046EBTQ0/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0046EBTQ0', 'Mophie Juice Pack Air');" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Mophie Juice Pack Air
          </strong>
         </a>
          If youre taking pictures with your iPhone, or even just listening to podcasts, youre very likely to run out of power at some point during a shoot. Because of this, I always keep a fully charged Mophie Juice Pack Air with me. I dont keep it on my iPhone at all times, but if I need it, its there and ready to charge me back up. I own two of these, and one of them lives permanently in my camera bag.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Other Bits
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Business Cards
         </strong>
          You never know who youll run into while shooting, so I never leave the house without a handful of these. I get mine printed at
         <a href="http://www.moo.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.moo.com', 'Moo');" target="_blank">
          Moo
         </a>
         , and they do a fantastic job. The ink and paper they use is top quality, and their customer service is unbelievable. I made a design mistake on my first run of cards, but they offered to reprint them again at no charge. Just fantastic!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Cards and Batteries
         </strong>
          I can get through a day on one D7000 battery, but the Fuji X100 only gets about 300 shots to a charge, so I generally carry 3 spare batteries with me. Theyre cheaptheres no reason not to.
        </p>
        <p>
         I also carry a handful of
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004JJU6I4/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004JJU6I4" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004JJU6I4/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004JJU6I4', 'Sandisk Extreme Pro SDHC');" target="_blank">
          Sandisk Extreme Pro SDHC
         </a>
         cards. Theyre lightning quick, and one of the few SD cards that the X100 behaves well with. Plus, the D7000 takes two at once, so I need a bunch. I generally use 8 GB cards, because if a card fails, Ive only lost 8 GB of photos. Losing 32 GB of shots frightens me, and Id much rather carry a few more 8 GB cards.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B006CUSJS2/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B006CUSJS2" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B006CUSJS2/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=andyhendr-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B006CUSJS2', 'Incase Capsule Headphones');" target="_blank">
           Incase Capsule Headphones
          </a>
         </strong>
          This is a weird one, but for some reason I like to listen to podcasts while shooting on the street. It makes me a little bit more comfortable in the environment, and I feel that Im slightly less likely to be confronted if Im seen wearing headphones. The Incase Capsule headphones are a recent purchase, but Im really enjoying them. They fit well, are comfortable, sound great, and they also have an integrated microphone and remote in-line so I can record voice notes or take calls without pulling out my phone.
        </p>
