     <p>
      <b>
       Semper Gumby
      </b>
      is an unofficial motto for the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">
       United States Marine Corps
      </a>
      , Navy fast attack submariners, Navy Seabees,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USCG" title="USCG">
       USCG
      </a>
      personnel, and the Civil Air Patrol. It is a play on the official motto
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semper_Fidelis" title="Semper Fidelis">
        Semper Fidelis
       </a>
      </i>
      , usually abbreviated
      <i>
       Semper Fi
      </i>
      , which means "
      <i>
       Always Faithful
      </i>
      ", and that of the US Coast Guard,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semper_Paratus" title="Semper Paratus">
        Semper Paratus
       </a>
      </i>
      , meaning "
      <i>
       Always Ready.
      </i>
      "
      <i>
       Semper Gumby
      </i>
      , referring to the
      <a href="/wiki/Cartoon" title="Cartoon">
       cartoon
      </a>
      character
      <a href="/wiki/Gumby" title="Gumby">
       Gumby
      </a>
      , means "
      <i>
       Always Flexible
      </i>
      ." The real
      <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
       Latin
      </a>
      phrase meaning "
      <i>
       always flexible
      </i>
      " is
      <i>
       semper flexibilis
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      While the first use of Semper Gumby is often assigned to Captain Jay Farmer of HMM-264 in 1984 who actually flew with a Gumby character toy mounted on the standby Compass on the instrument panel of his CH-46E nicknamed "Airwolf", the term was in use in 1977/78 in 1st Battalion, 5th Marines at Camp Pendleton.
     </p>
     <p>
      While popular belief has it that the term was first referenced by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_Sergeant" title="First Sergeant">
       1st Sgt
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/BGM-71_TOW" title="BGM-71 TOW">
       TOW
      </a>
      Co. 3rd Tank Battalion
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/7th_Marines" title="7th Marines">
       7th
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Marine_Expeditionary_Brigade" title="Marine Expeditionary Brigade">
       Marine Expeditionary Brigade
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       Task Force Ripper
      </i>
      ) prior to deployment of
      <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War#Operation_Desert_Shield" title="Gulf War">
       Operation Desert Shield
      </a>
      from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MCAGCC_29_Palms" title="MCAGCC 29 Palms">
       MCAGCC 29 Palms
      </a>
      , Ca. on August 15, 1990. "Marines, My platoon commander in Nam used to tell us 'Semper Gumby, Always Flexible'." The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3rd_Battalion,_11th_Marines" title="3rd Battalion, 11th Marines">
       3rd Battalion, 11th Marines
      </a>
      has "
      <i>
       always flexible
      </i>
      " as part of their official motto.
     </p>
     <p>
      The phrase was also said by Corporal Gabriel 'Gabe' Garza played by
      <a href="/wiki/Rey_Valentin" title="Rey Valentin">
       Rey Valentin
      </a>
      on the
      <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">
       HBO
      </a>
      series
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Generation_Kill_(TV_series)" title="Generation Kill (TV series)">
        Generation Kill
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
