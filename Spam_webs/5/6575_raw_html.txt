                <div class="field field-name-field-intro-text field-type-text-long field-label-hidden">
                 <div class="field-items">
                  <div class="field-item even">
                   Poach an egg in the microwave in less than one minute!
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="field field-name-field-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden">
                 <div class="field-items">
                  <div class="field-item even">
                   <p>
                    Whenever I have the luxury of ordering eggs at a restaurant or hotel, I always ask to have them poached. Ive never been game enough to do it at home because every time Ive tried, Ive failed miserably. I hadnt considered using the microwave but as it turns out, its a super easy way to get a perfectly
                    <a href="http://homeheaven.com.au/food-recipes/how-tos/article/how-poach-egg">
                     poached egg
                    </a>
                     without the hassle.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Ingredients:
                    </strong>
                   </p>
                   <ul>
                    <li>
                     1 egg
                    </li>
                    <li>
                     1/2 cup water
                    </li>
                    <li>
                     1/2 tsp vinegar (optional)
                    </li>
                   </ul>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Method:
                    </strong>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    1) Place  cup water in small microwave-safe bowl. Add vinegar to water.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    2) Gently crack egg into water, ensuring the egg white and egg yolk are floating in the water and not touching the bottom of the bowl.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    3) Microwave on high for 45 seconds.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    hh tip: If you like your yolk to be a little harder, add 10 seconds to the time.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    4) Lift egg from water with spoon and allow excess water to drain off.
                   </p>
                   <p>
