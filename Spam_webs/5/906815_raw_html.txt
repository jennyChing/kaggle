     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        The Marvel Super Heroes
       </b>
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is an
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       American
      </a>
      /
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian">
       Canadian
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animated_television_series" title="Animated television series">
       animated television series
      </a>
      starring five
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic-book" title="Comic-book">
       comic-book
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Superheroes" title="Superheroes">
       superheroes
      </a>
      from
      <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">
       Marvel Comics
      </a>
      . The first TV series based on Marvel characters, it debuted in
      <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_syndication" title="Broadcast syndication">
       syndication
      </a>
      on
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       U.S.
      </a>
      television in 1966.
     </p>
     <p>
      Produced by
      <a href="/wiki/Grantray-Lawrence_Animation" title="Grantray-Lawrence Animation">
       Grantray-Lawrence Animation
      </a>
      , headed by Grant Simmons, Ray Patterson and Robert Lawrence,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      it was an umbrella series of five segments, each approximately seven minutes long, broadcast on local
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_stations" title="Television stations">
       television stations
      </a>
      that aired the show at different times. The series ran initially as a half-hour program made up of three seven-minute segments of a single superhero, separated by a short description of one of the other four heroes. It has also been broadcast as a mixture of various heroes in a half-hour timeslot, and as individual segments as filler or within a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Children%27s_TV_program" title="Children's TV program">
       children's TV program
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The segments were: "
      <a href="/wiki/Captain_America" title="Captain America">
       Captain America
      </a>
      ", "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk" title="The Incredible Hulk">
       The Incredible Hulk
      </a>
      ", "
      <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man" title="Iron Man">
       Iron Man
      </a>
      ", "
      <a href="/wiki/Thor_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Thor (Marvel Comics)">
       The Mighty Thor
      </a>
      ", and "
      <a href="/wiki/Namor" title="Namor">
       The Sub-Mariner
      </a>
      ".
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Marvel_Super_Heroes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Sixty-five half-hour episodes of three seven-minute chapters were produced, for a total of 195 segments that ran initially in broadcast syndication from September 1, 1966 to December 1, 1966.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The series, produced in color, had extremely
      <a href="/wiki/Limited_animation" title="Limited animation">
       limited animation
      </a>
      produced by
      <a href="/wiki/Xerography" title="Xerography">
       xerography
      </a>
      , consisting of photocopied images taken directly from the comics and manipulated to minimize the need for animation production. The cartoons were presented as a series of static comic-strip panel images; generally the only movement involved the lips, when a character spoke, the occasional arm or leg, or a fully animated black silhouette. The series used the original stories largely in their entirety, showcasing
      <a href="/wiki/Jack_Kirby" title="Jack Kirby">
       Jack Kirby
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Steve_Ditko" title="Steve Ditko">
       Steve Ditko
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Don_Heck" title="Don Heck">
       Don Heck
      </a>
      art, among others, from the period fans and historians call the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silver_Age_of_comic_books" title="Silver Age of comic books">
       Silver Age of comic books
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Stan_Lee" title="Stan Lee">
       Stan Lee
      </a>
      , Marvel's editor and art director at the time, said in 2004 that he believed publisher
      <a href="/wiki/Martin_Goodman_(publisher)" title="Martin Goodman (publisher)">
       Martin Goodman
      </a>
      negotiated the deal with Grantray-Lawrence and that Lawrence chose the characters to be used. Lawrence rented Lee and his wife a
      <a href="/wiki/Penthouse_apartment" title="Penthouse apartment">
       penthouse apartment
      </a>
      at 30 East 60th Street, near
      <a href="/wiki/Madison_Avenue" title="Madison Avenue">
       Madison Avenue
      </a>
      , for Lee's use while he worked on the series. (Lee lived in
      <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Harbor,_New_York" title="Hewlett Harbor, New York">
       Hewlett Harbor
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">
       New York
      </a>
      , on
      <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">
       Long Island
      </a>
      , at the time.) Lee recalled, "I really don't remember any reaction from the Marvel artists involved. I wish I could claim to have written the [theme song] lyrics, because I think they're brilliant, but alas, I didn't".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the meantime, Grantray-Lawrence subcontracted production of
      <i>
       The Mighty Thor
      </i>
      segments to
      <a href="/wiki/Famous_Studios" title="Famous Studios">
       Paramount Cartoon Studios
      </a>
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      (the animation division of
      <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">
       Paramount Pictures
      </a>
      , formerly known as Famous Studios), headed at that time by
      <a href="/wiki/Fleischer_Studios" title="Fleischer Studios">
       Fleischer Studios
      </a>
      veteran
      <a href="/wiki/Shamus_Culhane" title="Shamus Culhane">
       Shamus Culhane
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Marvel announced the series in the "Marvel Bullpen Bulletins" of the November 1966 issues, stating in that monthly fan page's hyperbolic style that, "It won't be long before our swingin' super-heroes [
      <a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">
       sic
      </a>
      ] make their star-studded debut on TV, appearing five nights a week  that's right, five  count 'em  five nights a week, for a half-hour each night. So you've just got time to make sure your set's in good working order  check your local paper for time and station  and prepare to have a ball!"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <p>
      Appearing in guest roles were:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/X-Men" title="X-Men">
        The X-Men
       </a>
        The original lineup of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warren_Worthington" title="Warren Worthington">
        Angel
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Beast_(comics)" title="Beast (comics)">
        Beast
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cyclops_(comics)" title="Cyclops (comics)">
        Cyclops
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Iceman_(comics)" title="Iceman (comics)">
        Iceman
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Jean_Grey" title="Jean Grey">
        Marvel Girl
       </a>
       appeared in a Sub-Mariner episode, "Dr. Doom's Day / The Doomed Allegiance / Tug of Death". The story was an adaption of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four" title="Fantastic Four">
         Fantastic Four
        </a>
       </i>
       #6 (Sept. 1962), but since Grantray-Lawrence Animation did not own rights to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four_(1967_animated_series)" title="Fantastic Four (1967 animated series)">
        Fantastic Four
       </a>
       , the producers substituted the X-Men  although referring to them instead as "Allies for Peace". However, the characters retained their original designs and individual names from the comics.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">
        The Avengers
       </a>
        Episode 8 of The Incredible Hulk was an adaptation of
       <i>
        Avengers
       </i>
       #2 (November 1963), and co-starred Thor, Iron Man (with his all-golden armor (as published in the original comic book) recolored to match the red and gold design featured in the Iron Man episodes),
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giant-Man" title="Giant-Man">
        Giant-Man
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Wasp_(comics)" title="Wasp (comics)">
        Wasp
       </a>
       . The lineup beginning in
       <i>
        Avengers
       </i>
       #4 (March 1964), with Thor, Iron Man, Giant-Man, the Wasp and the newly installed Captain America, appears in several
       <i>
        Captain America
       </i>
       episodes, as does the later line-up from Avengers #16 with
       <a href="/wiki/Hawkeye_(comics)" title="Hawkeye (comics)">
        Hawkeye
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Quicksilver_(comics)" title="Quicksilver (comics)">
        Quicksilver
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Scarlet_Witch" title="Scarlet Witch">
        Scarlet Witch
       </a>
       .
      </li>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">
       Home media
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [

        edit

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Segments of the series appear on at least two
      <a href="/wiki/VHS" title="VHS">
       VHS
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Home_video" title="Home video">
       home video
      </a>
      releases, containing three
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Videocassettes" title="Videocassettes">
       videocassettes
      </a>
      each:
      <i>
       Marvel Superheroes: Triple Pack #1
      </i>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Universal_Product_Code" title="Universal Product Code">
       UPC
      </a>
      #024543004127) and
      <i>
       Marvel's Mightiest Heroes: Triple Pack #2
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_Video" title="Fox Video">
       Fox Video
      </a>
      released a version titled
      <i>
       Marvel's Mightiest Super Heroes Gift Set
      </i>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Article_Number" title="European Article Number">
       EAN
      </a>
      #0024543004134).
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2003, Hulk segments giving his
      <a href="/wiki/Origin_story" title="Origin story">
       origin story
      </a>
      appeared as an extra on the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buena_Vista_Home_Entertainment" title="Buena Vista Home Entertainment">
       Buena Vista Home Entertainment
      </a>
      DVD release of the 1996
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animated_television_series" title="Animated television series">
       animated television series
      </a>
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk_(1996_animated_series)" title="The Incredible Hulk (1996 animated series)">
        The Incredible Hulk
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In September 2004,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buena_Vista_Pictures" title="Buena Vista Pictures">
       Buena Vista Home Video
      </a>
      announced plans to release the series on June 28, 2005, as a five-DVD set titled
      <i>
       The 60's Superheroes
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      By February 2005, however, the release was off the schedule.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On May 21, 2007, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">
       UK
      </a>
      company
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maximum_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maximum Entertainment (page does not exist)">
       Maximum Entertainment
      </a>
      released four two-disc sets, for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Region_2" title="Region 2">
       Region 2
      </a>
      , each set containing 13 episodes of the Captain America, Iron Man, Sub-Mariner and Thor segments respectively, with each episode re-edited into continuous, half-hour segments.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On August 25, 2008, the UK company
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Liberation_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Liberation Entertainment (page does not exist)">
       Liberation Entertainment
      </a>
      released a two-disc set of the Hulk segments, re-edited into 13 20-minute episodes.
