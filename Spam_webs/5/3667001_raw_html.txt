             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               There are a ton of great docks available for iOS devices. But what about a dock that can charge your iPhone, your
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/apple-watch/">
                Apple Watch
               </a>
               , an external USB battery
               <em>
                and
               </em>
               another USB device?
              </p>
              <p>
               Oh yeah, and it's also an LED desk lamp.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/04/05/apple-watch-blocked-in-switzerland/">
                 Apple Watch reportedly blocked in Switzerland over patent, for now
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               The
               <a href="https://www.indiegogo.com/projects/apple-watch-iphone-docking-station--2" target="_blank">
                NuDock Power Station Lamp
               </a>
               from
               <a href="http://mitagg.com/gallery/" target="_blank">
                MiTagg
               </a>
               is the latest crowdfunding success story. Already the lamp has beat its Indiegogo goal of $30,000, which means it will be headed for production soon.
              </p>
              <p>
               <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" mozallowfullscreen="" src="https://player.vimeo.com/video/122483170?title=0&amp;byline=0&amp;portrait=0" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="640">
               </iframe>
              </p>
              <p>
               The NuDock is an LED lamp that has a built-in iPhone dock, as well as a dock for the Apple Watch. Put the Apple Watch's magnetic charger inside the NuStand, and you can leave your watch beside your bed or desk to charge.
              </p>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="20150402155050-install-gif-revised" class="" data-caption="" data-credit-link="" data-credit-name="" data-credit-provider="" data-fragment="m!bdc2" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzA0LzA2LzIzLzIwMTUwNDAyMTU1LjEzZTk3LmdpZgpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/e1450acf/f65/20150402155050-install-gif-revised.gif" data-micro="1" data-width="620" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzA0LzA2LzIzLzIwMTUwNDAyMTU1LjEzZTk3LmdpZgpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/e1450acf/f65/20150402155050-install-gif-revised.gif" title="20150402155050-install-gif-revised"/>
               </p>
              </figure>
              <p>
               The prototype also includes a small portable external USB battery for charging your iPhone on the go. MiTagg also has a Bluetooth-based app for locating the battery, and for helping the battery work with your phone or watch.
              </p>
              <p>
               To top it off, the lamp has a 2.4amp USB port built into the side for charging up other devices such as tablets.
              </p>
              <p>
               MiTagg says the lamp will retail for $249, but you can get it for $129 for a limited time via Indiegogo. The next pricing tier is $149.
              </p>
              <p>
               The product is expected to ship in July, and will be available in silver or space gray. MiTagg is also offering a stand-alone dock for the Apple Watch for $19.
              </p>
             </section>
