     <p>
      Howdy, folks, and greetings from scenic Redmond, Washington, where Microsoft is about to give us a
      <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2015/01/20/what-to-expect-from-microsofts-next-windows-10-event/">
       <em>
        lot
       </em>
       more information
      </a>
      about its next-gen OS,
      <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2014/09/30/microsoft-windows-10/">
       Windows 10
      </a>
      . Sure, we may have already gotten a peak at the new and improved
      <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2014/04/02/microsoft-teases-a-classic-start-menu-for-windows-8-1-with-built/">
       Start Menu
      </a>
      , but there are still so many unanswered questions. What will it be like to use Cortana
      <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2015/01/08/windows-10-browser-and-cortana/">
       from the desktop
      </a>
      ? How will those "
      <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2014/04/02/microsoft-pushes-universal-apps-that-run-on-everything-windows/">
       universal
      </a>
      " apps look across different devices? And where does Xbox fit in, exactly? Follow our liveblog starting at 9AM PT / 12PM ET, where we'll be reporting all the news, as it breaks. And who knows, maybe you'll even get to download a preview for yourself once it's all over. Only one way to find out, though: stay tuned!
