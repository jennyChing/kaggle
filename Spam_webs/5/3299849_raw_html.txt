      </li>
      <li>
       <span data-original="1kg (2 1/4 lb) venison stew meat" itemprop="ingredients">

      <li>
       <span itemprop="ingredients">
        1 tablespoon salt
       </span>
      </li>
      <li>
       <span itemprop="ingredients">

       </span>

      <li>
       <span itemprop="ingredients">
        7 small potatoes, peeled and quartered

      <li>
       <span itemprop="ingredients">

       </span>
      </li>
      <li>
       <span itemprop="ingredients">

&lt;section class="recipeDirections"&gt;
    &lt;h2&gt;
        Method&lt;br /&gt;
        &lt;small&gt;
                &lt;span&gt;Prep:&lt;span class="accent"&gt;15&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;min&lt;/span&gt; &nbsp;&#8250;&nbsp;&lt;meta content="PT15M" itemprop="prepTime" /&gt;&lt;/span&gt;
                            &lt;span&gt;Cook:&lt;span class="accent"&gt;2&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;hr&lt;/span&gt; &nbsp;&#8250;&nbsp;&lt;meta content="PT2H" itemprop="cookTime" /&gt;&lt;/span&gt;
                                        &lt;span&gt;Ready in:&lt;span class="accent"&gt;2&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;hr&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="accent"&gt;15&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;min&lt;/span&gt;&nbsp;&lt;meta content="PT2H15M" itemprop="totalTime" /&gt;&lt;/span&gt;
        &lt;/small&gt;
    &lt;/h2&gt;

    &lt;ol itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;
                    &lt;li&gt;&lt;span&gt;Heat oil in a large casserole or stockpot and brown the meat. Add onions, garlic, Worcestershire sauce, bay leaf, thyme, salt and 750ml water.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;li&gt;&lt;span&gt;Simmer, covered, for 1 1/2 to 2 hours, or until meat is tender.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;li&gt;&lt;span&gt;Stir in potatoes and parsnips; cook until tender. Combine flour and remaining water. Stir into the stew to thicken slightly. Remove bay leaf before serving.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;

        &lt;p&gt;
                &lt;span itemprop="reviewBody"&gt;Something else. 
An excellent venison stew recipe for folks who do not like a "wild game taste".  A slow cooker is essential.  I recommend slow cooking for 5 hours, chilling overnight, and then again slow cooking for another 5 hours. This maximises flavour without making the veggies mushy.  Also, additional flour should not be needed if the meat is first dredged in flour before browning.&lt;/span&gt;
