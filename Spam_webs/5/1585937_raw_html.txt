        <p>
         Do you think using a cell phone will make an addict more calm or more excited? (Hint: its probably not what you think.)
        </p>
        <p>
         More about that in a minute, but first, lets take a look at the way we use technology these days.
        </p>
        <p>
         It should come as no surprise to anyone: with all of the people using smartphones today, there are some people that you can say truly have
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addict/">
          some type of smartphone addiction
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         They are
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addiction-signs/">
          never without their phone by their side
         </a>
         , on their desk, on the table or have
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addiction-symptoms/">
          their face buried into the screen
         </a>
         as the look to see what is going on in the world while they miss what is going on in the world around them.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are some quirks and peculiarities that may go along with having a smartphone addiction, and here are 20 odd things a smartphone addict does so you can see if you or someone you know falls into this category.
        </p>
        <h2>
         1. You panic when your smartphone is out of your sight
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="panic phone sight" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-433" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/media/1-panic-phone-sight-300x250.jpg"/>
        </p>

        <p>
         You could be sitting casually at your desk, going about your business, when all of a sudden you realize that you cannot see your smartphone.
        </p>
        <p>
         It may not even be turned on, but you need to know that it is safely within your sight at all times so that you can have peace of mind throughout the day.
        </p>
        <h2>
         2. You panic when you leave the phone at home
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="panic phone home" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-434" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/media/2-panic-phone-home-300x250.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <div class="twitter-button single-twitter-button">

        <p>
         This is even worse than not having the phone by your side and in your sight the whole time.
        </p>
        <p>
         It happens to everyone at some point, where you may accidentally leave your phone on your nightstand or in the kitchen as you were grabbing that cup of coffee before you go.
        </p>
        <p>
         By the time you reach work, school or are out on whatever errand you had to run that day, you panic when you cannot find your phone, racing back to your car, tearing through your purse or calling your spouse to see if someone knows where the phone is and you will not rest easily until you get home to check on its safety.
        </p>
        <h2>
         3. You sleep with your phone in bed with you
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="sleep with phone" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-435" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/media/3-sleep-with-phone-300x250.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <div class="twitter-button single-twitter-button">

          </span>
          <span class="button-title">
           Tweet this
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Someone needs directions to get to that fancy new restaurant? You need a picture of that cute little dog on the street? You want to know who the ruler of Romania was in 1842?
        </p>
        <p>
         Perhaps you find yourself as being the constant source of information for everything and everyone, even for people you do not know and just happen to overhear their conversation just so you can take the opportunity to make use of your phone for a few minutes.
        </p>
        <h2>
         19. You take pictures and video everywhere that you go
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="take pictures everywhere" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-451" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/media/19-take-pictures-everywhere-300x250.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <div class="twitter-button single-twitter-button">
         <a href="#" onclick="ga('send', 'event', 'Share', 'Twitter-sharethis', '/phone-addiction/smartphone-addict/'); socialPopup(event, 'http://twitter.com/home?status=20+Odd+Things+A+Smartphone+Addict+Does+http%3A%2F%2Fwww.addictiontips.net%2Fphone-addiction%2Fsmartphone-addict%2F');return false;">
          <span class="button-icon">
           <img alt="twitter icon" data-lazy-src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/wp-content/themes/addictiontips/img/tw.png" height="20" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="20"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="twitter icon" height="20" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/wp-content/themes/addictiontips/img/tw.png" width="20"/>
           </noscript>
          </span>
          <span class="button-title">
           Tweet this
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         There is nothing wrong with wanting to take pictures or video when you are going on vacation somewhere special or you are out at a club with your friends and something crazy is happening.
        </p>
        <p>
         It is even okay to break it out at the concert you are at when they start playing your favorite song. However, if you find that you are constantly seeing everything that is going on through the screen on your smartphone rather than seeing it all for yourself, you are likely using your phone too often and may need to take a break from it all.
        </p>
        <h2>
         20. You try to open applications that you already have open
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="opening phone app" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-452" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/media/20-opening-phone-app-300x250.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <div class="twitter-button single-twitter-button">
         <a href="#" onclick="ga('send', 'event', 'Share', 'Twitter-sharethis', '/phone-addiction/smartphone-addict/'); socialPopup(event, 'http://twitter.com/home?status=20+Odd+Things+A+Smartphone+Addict+Does+http%3A%2F%2Fwww.addictiontips.net%2Fphone-addiction%2Fsmartphone-addict%2F');return false;">
          <span class="button-icon">
           <img alt="twitter icon" data-lazy-src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/wp-content/themes/addictiontips/img/tw.png" height="20" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="20"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="twitter icon" height="20" src="http://cdn.addictiontips.net/wp-content/themes/addictiontips/img/tw.png" width="20"/>
           </noscript>
          </span>
          <span class="button-title">
           Tweet this
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addiction-facts/">
          You are so used to opening applications
         </a>
         all day long for everything that you keep tapping on icons on your screen even though that screen has already been open for you for the last hour while you were using your phone.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you find yourself fitting into more than one of these 20 odd things a smartphone addict does, it may be time for you to take a closer look at how you are spending your time so you can
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/brain-phone-addiction/">
          get away from your phone
         </a>
         for a little bit and
         <a href="http://www.addictiontips.net/phone-addiction-situations/">
          see the world as it really is
         </a>
         without having to worry about all that is going on in your smartphone.
        </p>
        <p>
         Smartphones can be a very addicting tool in your life despite what they can do.
        </p>
