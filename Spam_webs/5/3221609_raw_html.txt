                 <h2 class="date-header">
                  <span>
                   Thursday, May 21, 2015
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <a name="4623728406860564040">
                    </a>
                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2015/05/digital-inclusion-fellowship.html">
                      Now accepting applications for the first-ever Digital Inclusion Fellowship
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4623728406860564040" itemprop="articleBody">
                     When people have access to the web, opportunities are just a click awayfrom learning how to sign up for health care, to finding affordable housing, to keeping in touch with family and friends. But today, more than 60 million Americans still arent using the Internet at home. While there are organizations across the U.S. that want to help bring these people online, many of them dont have the in-house expertise or resources to launch new programs.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Thats why, in partnership with the
                     <a href="http://www.nten.org/" target="_blank">
                      Nonprofit Technology Network (NTEN)
                     </a>
                     , were launching the Digital Inclusion Fellowship. The fellowship will pair 16 people with local community organizations in our eight Google Fiber metro areasAtlanta, Austin, Charlotte, Kansas City, Nashville, Provo, Raleigh-Durham and Salt Lake Citywhere theyll spend a year building a digital inclusion program from the ground up.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-HIiy2fPGEp0/VV17zYPNE_I/AAAAAAAAAWA/Yu7gStghqtw/s1600/Digital%2BInclusion%2BFellowship%2BGraphic.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="148" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-HIiy2fPGEp0/VV17zYPNE_I/AAAAAAAAAWA/Yu7gStghqtw/s400/Digital%2BInclusion%2BFellowship%2BGraphic.png" width="450"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     This fellowship will help community organizations get more people connected to the web. For example, the
                     <a href="http://www.saltlakeeducationfoundation.org/" target="_blank">
                      Salt Lake Education Foundation
                     </a>
                     s fellows will teach parents how to communicate with their childrens teachers and access grades and attendance records online. Meanwhile
                     <a href="http://www.triangleliteracy.org/" target="_blank">
                      Triangle Literacy Council
                     </a>
                     s fellow will create a mobile computing lab, which will travel to libraries, community centers, jails, and schools to teach people basic online tasks, like sending emails or finding health clinics.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     NTEN will choose fellows from their local community, since theyll know what kinds of programs will work best in their city. Once selected, theyll travel to Googles offices in Mountain View, Calif., to receive specialized training from NTEN on how to build effective digital inclusion programs. And throughout the fellowship, Google Fiber will provide approximately $1 million to support salaries and benefits for fellows, and stipends to organizations so they have the resources to launch their new programs.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     So if youre passionate about access to the Internet and want to unlock new opportunities for residents in your community, apply at
                     <a href="http://nten.org/community/dif">
                      nten.org/community/dif
                     </a>
                     now through June 10, 2015.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span class="post-author">
                      Posted by Andrew Bentley, Digital Inclusion Program Manager, Google Fiber

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2015/03/introducing-dial-up-mode.html">
                      Introducing dial-up mode
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5041560918280628078" itemprop="articleBody">
                     At Google Fiber, we are passionate about superfast Internet. We believe that people shouldnt wait to connect to the things they care aboutlike that awesome YouTube video, cant-miss TV series or those photos from the last family vacation.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     But as we've rolled out Google Fiber in Kansas City, Provo and Austin, weve learned that Fiber has been impacting our subscribers in ways that we didnt expect. Loading bars used to give people an opportunity to pause and take care of the little thingslike making a cup of coffee, taking a bathroom break or playing with the dog. Weve been told that Fibers seemingly instant connections have taken away that precious time.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     This got us thinkingwhat if we could help people get their time back even while they used Fiber? We worked with dial-up engineers, 56k researchers and T1 enthusiasts across the world to build our newest feature: dial-up mode.
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="225" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/XTTtkisylQw" width="400">
                      </iframe>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     It wasnt easy, but we got to the root of the problem. By incorporating
                     <a href="https://youtu.be/XTTtkisylQw" target="_blank">
                      dial-up technology
                     </a>
                     , we were able to reduce Fiber speeds up to 376 times by withholding photons from the fiber strands. In doing so, the light-based fiber optic technology dims to a flicker of its previous capacity, giving our users those precious moments to load the dishwasher, hug the kids or walk the dog.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     To activate this new feature, simply click the menu at the top right-hand corner of your browser and select 'Dial-up Mode'. Then sit back, relax and enjoy the power of slowness.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-kyLRGkZ5f5o/VRt0GMmUVoI/AAAAAAAAAUE/e9EBjnR1Xw8/s1600/Dial-up%2Bmode.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="225" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-kyLRGkZ5f5o/VRt0GMmUVoI/AAAAAAAAAUE/e9EBjnR1Xw8/s1600/Dial-up%2Bmode.png" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Over the coming weeks, we'll be rolling out dial-up mode to gigabit customers in Kansas City, Provo, and Austin, helping meet the need for slow. We hope to bring this feature to all Google Fiber subscribers soon.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span class="post-author">
                      Maurice Clarke, Fiber Technologist

                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2015/03/google-fiber-is-coming-to-salt-lake-city.html">
                      Google Fiber is coming to Salt Lake City
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4153465598339942729" itemprop="articleBody">
                     During my time working in Provo, Ive seen the impact of Google Fiber firsthand. Hackers from across the country have gathered at
                     <a href="https://devmounta.in/" target="_blank">
                      DevMountain
                     </a>
                     to
                     <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fR1AfJu-Ck4" target="_blank">
                      develop
                     </a>
                     new web and mobile applications; the
                     <a href="https://www.unitedwayuc.org/" target="_blank">
                      United Way of Utah County
                     </a>
                     has
                     <a href="http://youtu.be/a3YUtncGniE" target="_blank">
                      promoted
                     </a>
                     new digital literacy programs throughout the community; and one organization, called
                     <a href="http://www.nowican.org/" target="_blank">
                      Now I Can
                     </a>
                     , has used Google Fiber to remotely
                     <a href="http://youtu.be/Gj9upyBRzk0" target="_blank">
                      connect
                     </a>
                     parents with their children undergoing intensive physical therapy in Provo.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Now,
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2013/04/silicon-slopes.html" target="_blank">
                      another city
                     </a>
                     in the
                     <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_slopes" target="_blank">
                      Silicon Slopes
                     </a>
                     is poised to show the world whats possible with gigabit Internet. Today, were ready to bring Google Fiber to one more metro areaSalt Lake City.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Salt Lake has more than breathtaking mountain vistas and fantastic ski slopes. It also hosts a booming technology sector, world-renowned universities and a vibrant local culture. Were looking forward to seeing Salt Lake residents use gigabit Internet to spark creative ideas, jumpstart businesses and collaborate in ways they couldn't before.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-qmqLrAMunRg/VRFyC9TskaI/AAAAAAAAATQ/1SxBV2Vzlp0/s1600/SLC_van.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="210" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-qmqLrAMunRg/VRFyC9TskaI/AAAAAAAAATQ/1SxBV2Vzlp0/s1600/SLC_van.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Salt Lake City will
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2015/01/google-fiber-is-coming-to-atlanta.html" target="_blank">
                      join
                     </a>
                     the Atlanta, Charlotte, Nashville and Raleigh-Durham metro areas in the design phase of building our fiber network. Over the coming months, well work closely with these cities to map out just where to lay our fiber-optic cables. Theres a lot of work ahead; as the new Associate City Manager for Google Fiber in Salt Lake City, I cant wait to see what the city does with superfast Internet.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Qgc9P-eTa9E/VRGEJ0QlpBI/AAAAAAAAATo/5P7N3DiIrcg/s1600/blog_graphic.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="283" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Qgc9P-eTa9E/VRGEJ0QlpBI/AAAAAAAAATo/5P7N3DiIrcg/s1600/blog_graphic.png" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     For information about Google Fibers progress in your area, visit our
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/saltlakecity" target="_blank">
                      website
                     </a>
                     and sign up for updates.
                     <br/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2015/03/google-fiber-for-small-business-arrives.html">
                      Google Fiber for Small Business arrives in Provo (plus more of Kansas City)
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3330903130893667402" itemprop="articleBody">
                     Since we
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2014/11/a-next-step-for-google-fiber-and-small.html" target="_blank">
                      launched
                     </a>
                     our Early Access program in Kansas City, small businesses have
                     <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gmZNZ3QMFMA&amp;feature=youtu.be" target="_blank">
                      experienced the magic
                     </a>
                     that comes with gigabit speeds. Take, for example, the documentary film studio that no longer has to ship hard drives to clients to avoid long upload times; or the software developer who can reliably access cloud systems that are vital to his business. Without the stress and lost time of a slow Internet connection, small businesses can focus on what matters most.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Today, were bringing Google Fiber for Small Business to Provo and its vibrant small business community, as well as expanding to additional areas of Kansas City (Kansas City North and South). Small businesses in these areas can
                     <a href="http://google.com/fiber/smallbusiness" target="_blank">
                      sign up
                     </a>
                     for our Early Access program, and get gigabit Internet for just $100 per month. In the
                     <a href="http://provomayor.com/2015/02/10/google-fiber-small-business-epic-big-deal/" target="_blank">
                      words
                     </a>
                     of Provo Mayor John Curtis, small businesses can kiss loading bars goodbye.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="253" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/gmZNZ3QMFMA" width="450">
                      </iframe>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Provo has already shown us whats possible when an emerging technology hub has access to gigabit Internet  from the
                     <a href="https://devmounta.in/" target="_blank">
                      DevMountain
                     </a>
                     coding school, to a growing community at the
                     <a href="http://www.meetup.com/The-Provo-Startup-Dojo/" target="_blank">
                      Startup Dojo
                     </a>
                     , or a
                     <a href="https://medium.com/@reidrobison/how-google-fiber-is-changing-the-game-for-genomics-20a6e67af092" target="_blank">
                      geneticist
                     </a>
                     who is using gigabit Internet to download an entire human genome in under an hour. We cant wait to see what the citys small businesses do with Google Fiber.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     If youre a small business in Provo or in Kansas City,
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/smallbusiness/" target="_blank">
                      visit our website
                     </a>
                     to get the latest and check if your business is eligible for Google Fiber.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>

                  <span>

                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2015/01/google-fiber-is-coming-to-atlanta.html">
                      Google Fiber is coming to Atlanta, Charlotte, Nashville and Raleigh-Durham
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8263486255395305553" itemprop="articleBody">
                     Its been nearly five years since we
                     <a href="http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2010/02/think-big-with-gig-our-experimental.html" target="_blank">
                      offered to build a fiber-optic network in one U.S. city as an experiment
                     </a>
                      and were met with
                     <a href="http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2011/03/ultra-high-speed-broadband-is-coming-to.html" target="_blank">
                      overwhelming enthusiasm
                     </a>
                     . Now, Google Fiber is live in Kansas City, Provo and Austin, and we've started to see how gigabit Internet, with speeds up to 100 times faster than todays basic broadband, can transform cities. It can give them new platforms for economic development and new ways of using technology to improve life for their citizens. And, around the country, it seems to be catching on.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Check out the
                     <a href="http://www.kcstartupvillage.org/" target="_blank">
                      Kansas City Startup Village
                     </a>
                     and Provo learn-how-to-code hub
                     <a href="https://devmounta.in/faq" target="_blank">
                      DevMountain
                     </a>
                     . Take a look at the
                     <a href="https://medium.com/@reidrobison/how-google-fiber-is-changing-the-game-for-genomics-20a6e67af092" target="_blank">
                      work of a geneticist
                     </a>
                     whose speedy connection could one day help newborns in intensive care, or how one city's network is connecting a high school classroom to an underwater microscope so students can study oceanic life in the Pacific... from Chattanooga, Tenn. There are many more stories like thisstories about how people are using gigabit internet to spark innovation, inspire creativity, and collaborate in ways they simply couldn't before. And we want to see even more.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     So, today, were happy to announce that Google Fiber is coming to 18 cities across four
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/newcities/" target="_blank">
                      new metro areas
                     </a>
                     :
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/atlanta/home/" target="_blank">
                      Atlanta
                     </a>
                     ,
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/charlotte/home/" target="_blank">
                      Charlotte
                     </a>
                     ,
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/nashville/home/" target="_blank">
                      Nashville
                     </a>
                     , and
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/raleighdurham/home/" target="_blank">
                      Raleigh-Durham
                     </a>
                     . We cant wait to see what people and businesses across the Southeast U.S. do with gigabit speeds.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aUfpSQsAtaE/VMdMxqj98HI/AAAAAAAAASc/0GoAo0Uqjwk/s1600/Expansion_plans_web_v2_large.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="283" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aUfpSQsAtaE/VMdMxqj98HI/AAAAAAAAASc/0GoAo0Uqjwk/s1600/Expansion_plans_web_v2_large.png" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Bringing Google Fiber to these cities is a long-term investment. Weve been working closely with city leaders over the past year on a
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2014/05/checking-in-on-checklist.html" target="_blank">
                      joint planning process
                     </a>
                     to get their communities ready for Google Fiberand now the really hard work begins. Our next step is to work with cities to create a detailed map of where we can put our thousands of miles of fiber, using existing infrastructure such as utility poles and underground conduit, and making sure to avoid things like gas and water lines. Then a team of surveyors and engineers will hit the streets to fill in missing details. Once were done designing the network (which we expect to wrap up in a few months), well start construction.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-GEgGRU6pGag/VMdNf0ZQssI/AAAAAAAAASs/6dfL2ViHYjk/s1600/Screen%2BShot%2B2015-01-14%2Bat%2B3.15.54%2BPM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="106" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-GEgGRU6pGag/VMdNf0ZQssI/AAAAAAAAASs/6dfL2ViHYjk/s1600/Screen%2BShot%2B2015-01-14%2Bat%2B3.15.54%2BPM.png" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     Were also continuing to explore
                     <a href="http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2014/02/exploring-new-cities-for-google-fiber.html" target="_blank">
                      bringing fiber to five additional metro areas
                     </a>
                     Phoenix, Portland, Salt Lake City, San Antonio and San Jose, and will have updates on these potential Fiber cities later this year.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Today, we aren't the only ones talking about gigabit broadband. From the White House to main street, a chorus of new voices is standing up for speed. Just last week during the
                     <a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2015/01/20/remarks-president-state-union-address-january-20-2015" target="_blank">
                      State of the Union
                     </a>
                     , the President called for faster networks so that innovators and entrepreneurs can build the next big idea. New research from the Fiber to the Home Council
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2014/09/gigabit-enthusiasm-economic-reality.html" target="_blank">
                      shows
                     </a>
                     gigabit networks are contributing billions of dollars in economic growth. Communities across America are demanding more speed for their own homes and businesses, and were going to keep doing our part to help.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span class="post-author">
                      Posted by Dennis Kish, Vice President, Google Fiber

                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2014/12/its-fiber-monday-in-austin.html">
                      Its Fiber Monday in Austin!
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3444337970980541353" itemprop="articleBody">
                     Access to abundant, super fast Internet can open up new opportunity for communities  and from the day we
                     <a href="http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2013/04/google-fibers-next-stop-austin-texas.html" target="_blank">
                      announced
                     </a>
                     our plans to build in Austin, the city has embraced the possibilities that will come from Internet that's up to 100 times faster than today's basic speeds. Today were kicking off Google Fiber signups for Austinites, their neighbors, and local small businesses in the
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/austin/fiberhoods" target="_blank">
                      South and Southeast areas
                     </a>
                     of the city. This is just the beginning. Well be opening new areas of the city for signups on an ongoing basis, and we hope to bring Fiber to every neighborhood in Austin that wants it.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">

                          <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-js34m-yM8jc/VHyrsHsDKRI/AAAAAAAAASM/Q9z-tD0sHnQ/s1600/austin_fiber_van.jpg" width="380"/>

                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2013/11/just-for-austin-how-youll-be-able-to.html" target="_blank">
                      early look at how this will work
                     </a>
                     ; now youll be able to see this process live so that you know how many more people need to sign up in order to bring Fiber to your neighborhood.
                     <br/>
                     For Austinites in neighborhoods (or fiberhoods as we call them) that are open for signups, you can choose
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/austin/plans" target="_blank">
                      one of three
                     </a>
                     Google Fiber plans.

                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/smallbusiness/" target="_blank">
                      Google Fiber for Small Business
                     </a>
                     . Austin is already booming with ambitious and creative entrepreneurs, and we cant wait to see what small businesses do with Fiber.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Keep in mind: youll have a limited window of time to sign up. We do this so that we can bring you Google Fiber as quickly as possible. We focus our efforts on one neighborhood at a time, doing an all-out installation blitz if your fiberhood meets its signup goal. After were finished in your fiberhood, well move on to the next. Be sure to check your
                     <a href="http://fiber.google.com/cities/austin/fiberhoods" target="_blank">
                      sign up deadline
                     </a>
                     so you dont miss us when were in your part of town.

                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/austin/events/" target="_blank">

                 <div class="date-posts">

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2014/11/unlocking-connection-for-austin.html">
                      Unlocking the Connection for Austins public housing residents
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4227837395465617164" itemprop="articleBody">
                     For Austin's public housing residents, access to the Internet can mean the difference between keeping up or falling behind. It can mean more resident children using computers in after-school programs and robotics clubs, more students using a home computer to finish their homework as their peers do, more working adults completing online GED training modules at home, and more seniors participating in basic computer skills workshops.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Thats why today, the
                     <a href="http://www.hacanet.org/">
                      Housing Authority of the City of Austin
                     </a>
                     (HACA) is
                     <a href="http://www.hacanet.org/press/NR_Digital%20Inclusion%20(final).pdf" target="_blank">
                      launching
                     </a>
                     Unlocking the Connection, a homegrown Austin initiative to help close the digital divide for the 4,300 people who live in public housing. Unlocking the Connection addresses the full set of resources needed to make the Web more available and relevant to Austinites who arent online today  an affordable Internet connection, access to devices, basic computer skills training, and opportunities to better understand how they can use the Web in their daily lives. HACA has brought together more than 20 national and local partners, including Google Fiber, to play a role in the program.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     We started talking with HACA after the City of Austin selected its Booker T. Washington training center as a site to receive Google Fiber as part of our
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2013/12/fast-free-internet-for-100-austin.html">
                      Community Connections
                     </a>
                     program. That conversation, and the many that followed, demonstrated that HACA had an ambitious vision for a digital inclusion program  one that was deeply rooted in the often complex reasons that people werent yet online  and that there was a natural way that Google Fiber could help.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Google Fiber will bring state of the art infrastructure to Austin's public housing; we will provide a free fiber connection to any existing HACA property in a neighborhood that meets its signup goal to get Google Fiber. If a family in one of these properties signs up for our Basic Service, they get an in-home Internet connection at todays basic broadband speeds, free for ten years after construction, They can also upgrade and pay for gigabit speeds anytime. In addition, were providing computers to HACA education and training centers, as well as funding
                     <a href="http://www.austinfree.net/">
                      Austin Free-Net
                     </a>
                     , a local nonprofit, to provide digital literacy training in HACA properties. Along with the contributions of
                     <a href="http://austinpathways.org/unlocking-the-connection/">
                      dozens of other partners
                     </a>
                      foundations, corporations, educational institutions  this support can advance HACAs vision to bring more of its residents online.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Google Fiber is making a
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2014/10/digital-inclusion-long-term-investment.html">
                      long-term investment
                     </a>
                     in each city where we build  including Austin, where digital inclusion is a
                     <a href="http://www.austintexas.gov/digitalinclusion">
                      citywide priority
                     </a>
                     . Here and in any city, our approach is shaped by potential partners, the cues of local leaders, and the unique needs of residents. Unlocking the Connection is the result of the collaboration and commitment of the Austin community  its driven by HACAs vision, supported by local leaders, and engages the talents of community organizations. Were proud to work hand-in-hand with all of these partners to help create a more connected Austin.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Posted by Parisa Fatehi-Weeks, Community Impact Manager, Google Fiber Austin

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2014/11/a-next-step-for-google-fiber-and-small.html">
                      A next step for Google Fiber and small business
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5913284289470322096" itemprop="articleBody">
                     A super-fast Internet connection can have a big impact on unlocking new possibilities for small businesses. From the start, small business owners have
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2013/10/some-clarification-for-small-businesses.html">
                      told us
                     </a>
                     that they want Google Fiber to help them move faster, work better together, reach new parts of the world without boarding a plane, and save time for the important thingslike growing their business.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Now were taking another step to speed up the Internet for small businessestoday, were kicking off an Early Access Program for Google Fiber for Small Business in select areas of central Kansas City. And whether a business uses their connection to move to the cloud, get closer to customers on Hangouts, or bring more transactions online, were excited to see what happens when Kansas City businesses say farewell to slow speeds and hello to all the tools and technologies that they need to grow.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The Early Access Program is available today in a
                     <a href="https://fiber.google.com/smallbusiness/#lightbox=smb-service-area">
                      handful of fiberhoods
                     </a>
                     in Kansas City, KS and Kansas City, MO. Small businesses in these areas can sign up for a gigabit Internet connection and be among the very first businesses to get Google Fiber. Though not all areas of central Kansas City are open for signups just yet, well keep you posted as we expand. Small businesses can visit
                     <a href="http://google.com/fiber/smallbusiness">
                      our website
                     </a>
                     to get more details, register for email updates, and be the first to know when they can sign up.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>

                      <tbody>

                         </a>
                        </td>

                     <br/>
                     Earlier this year, we started a pilot in Kansas City to learn more about what business owners need. We heard from documentary filmmakers, flower shops, web development agencies, and more. All of these small businesses have a lot of ideas on how a faster Internet connection could speed up their entire business  we cant wait to see what they do with Google Fiber.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     We know that small businesses play a big part in Provo and Austin, too. And while we dont have specific plans for small businesses in other cities right now, well be sure to share updates when we can.

                 <h2 class="date-header">
                  <span>
                   Monday, November 03, 2014
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2014/11/kansas-city-now-home-to-fastest.html">
                      Kansas City: Now Home to the Fastest Starbucks Wi-Fi in the U.S.
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4185209090062386021" itemprop="articleBody">
                     At the corner of 41st and Main Street, a Kansas City
                     <a href="http://www.starbucks.com/store/10264/us/41st-main/4101-main-street-kansas-city-mo-641112314">
                      Starbucks
                     </a>
                     is teeming with people writing emails, streaming music, sharing videos, and more. Now that same Starbucksone of the busiest in Kansas Cityis the first to be connected directly to Google Fiber, so anyone visiting the store can get super-fast Internet with their Pumpkin Spice Latte.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-vho1j6PmRmU/VFf029SW41I/AAAAAAAAAPs/8rFNLz2rhI0/s1600/_X2A6378.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="225" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-vho1j6PmRmU/VFf029SW41I/AAAAAAAAAPs/8rFNLz2rhI0/s1600/_X2A6378.jpg" width="400"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         Kansas City now has the fastest Wi-Fi at any Starbucks in the U.S.
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     At this Starbucks, visitors can connect to supercharged Wi-Fi through their wireless devices, so they can skim the morning headlines while they order or enjoy a cup of joe. There are also new community tables, which have Chromebooks wired directly to Google Fiber, as well as built-in
                     <a href="http://news.starbucks.com/news/national-rollout-of-wireless-charging-by-duracell-powermat-begins-in-starbu">
                      wireless charging technology
                     </a>
                     to power up devices. With these new connections, theres plenty of bandwidth for everyone to search, stream, and sharewithout the loading bars or buffering.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-BVO9z911o-0/VFf1CYb2WZI/AAAAAAAAAP0/1_rlG7OVlAE/s1600/_X2A6360.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="191" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-BVO9z911o-0/VFf1CYb2WZI/AAAAAAAAAP0/1_rlG7OVlAE/s1600/_X2A6360.jpg" width="400"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         Community tables come equipped with Chromebooks connected directly to Google Fiber
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     Along with fast Internet for the whole community, this store features a digital take on the Starbucks community board, so anyone can find and share local news, upcoming events, special offers and more. If you live nearby or are passing through town, be sure to swing by
                     <a href="https://www.google.com/maps/place/4101+Main+St,+Kansas+City,+MO+64111/@39.0512563,-94.5859722,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x87c0efc3a9f1c92d:0x91cdc5b681231639?sa=X&amp;ei=LI1WVMjOKLbIsATFkoLYDA&amp;ved=0CB0Q8gEwAA">
                      41st and Main Street
                     </a>
                     and check it out.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-P3X0Wl9DL2o/VFf1IHHFOII/AAAAAAAAAP8/neY-3POV8Is/s1600/_X2A6490%2B(1).jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="266" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-P3X0Wl9DL2o/VFf1IHHFOII/AAAAAAAAAP8/neY-3POV8Is/s1600/_X2A6490%2B(1).jpg" width="400"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         The digital community board makes it easy to find and share local news and events
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     This isnt the first time Google and Starbucks have teamed up to bring high-quality Wi-Fi to Starbucks patrons. Last year, Google and Starbucks
                     <a href="http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2013/07/starbucks-wifi-goes-google.html">
                      announced
                     </a>
                     a new effort to bring speedy Wi-Fi access to 7,000 Starbucks stores across the U.Swere well on our way, with more than half of locations connected. Connecting this Kansas City Starbucks to Google Fiber is a next step to bring more people an extra shot of Internet speed.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span class="post-author">
                      Posted by Rachel Merlo, Community Manager, Google Fiber
