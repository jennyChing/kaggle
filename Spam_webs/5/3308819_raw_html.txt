      <div class="page-title post-title">
       <h1>
        Water  The Answer To Fatigue In The Workplace
       </h1>
      </div>
      <div class="post-view">
       <p class="post-date when">
        This entry was posted on November 7, 2014
        <span class='\"by-author\"'>
         by admin
        </span>
        .
       </p>
       <div class="post-entry entry std">
        <p>
         The clocks have just gone back and the evenings are drawing in. There is a definite wintry chill to the morning air and heaving yourself out of bed requires a monumental effort. You drive to work in the gloom, pour a
         <a href="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/catalogsearch/result/?q=coffee" title="Coffee Search">
          coffee
         </a>
         and sit bleary-eyed at the computer, still half asleep. Sound familiar?
        </p>
        <div class="fullwidth">
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-384 alignright" height="200" src="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/wp/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/pou_spec_image1.jpg" title="pou_spec_image" width="133"/>
          When it comes to this time of year, it can be hard to maintain that all important Can Do attitude in the workplace and this can be very damaging for productivity. So what can you do to bring back that enthusiasm and drive that everybody had during the summer? It would be easy to think that turning up the
          <a href="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/catalogsearch/result/?q=heat" title="Heating Search">
           heating
          </a>
          and maintaining a healthy flow of tea for comfort would be the answer.
         </p>
         <p>
          The truth is such an approach is, more often than not, counter productive. So whats the answer?
          <a href="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/refrigeration-products/water-coolers.html" title="Water Coolers">
           Water
          </a>
          . Yes, plain and simple water. For many, drinking water is very much a summer thing. However, maintaining a steady intake all year round is key to beating the winter blues out of your business and keeping that competitive edge.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="fullwidth">
         <h2>
          Concentration
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div>
          <a href="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/wp/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/shutterstock_168661826.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-366 alignright" height="150" src="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/wp/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/shutterstock_168661826.jpg" title="shutterstock_168661826" width="150"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          The brain is a muscle and like all muscles it requires the right balance of electrolytes and fluids to function properly. Maintaining sufficient hydration is important to boost concentration, which in turn leads to improved performance and better productivity. Studies have shown that if you are only 1 percent dehydrated, you will likely have a 5 percent decrease in cognitive function.
          <strong>
          </strong>
          Whilst it is true that
          <a href="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/catalogsearch/result/?q=coffee" title="Coffee Search">
           coffee
          </a>
          or tea contain a substantial amount of fluid, they also contain other ingredients that can reduce their effectiveness as a form of hydration in comparison to pure water and therefore do not aid concentration in the same way.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="fullwidth">
         <h2>
          Energy
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div>
          <a href="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/wp/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/shutterstock_172267868.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-371 alignright" height="150" src="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/wp/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/shutterstock_172267868.jpg" title="shutterstock_172267868" width="150"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          What is the most common cause of fatigue in the workplace? Work would be the obvious answer. However, the truth is that the most common cause of daytime fatigue and headaches is mild dehydration. In the course of a working day, the body loses a tremendous amount of fluid. To maintain a healthy fluid level, this balance must be restored. As the weather becomes colder, it is easy to stop drinking water and dehydration becomes a real risk. Providing a
          <a href="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/refrigeration-products/water-coolers.html" title="Water Coolers">
           water cooler
          </a>
          can encourage staff to drink a sufficient amount of water, improving energy levels and boosting both motivation and productivity.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="fullwidth">
         <h2>
          Health
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div>
          <a href="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/wp/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/shutterstock_2228405471.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-368 alignright" height="150" src="http://www.carltonsales.co.uk/wp/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/shutterstock_2228405471.jpg" title="shutterstock_222840547" width="150"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          With the onset of flu season, sickness absence is no doubt already a worry for your office. Ensuring that staff have access to a water cooler all year round is a great way to help keep staff hydrated and healthy through the winter months. Whilst tea and coffee are often more satisfying than a glass of water, they also contain ingredients such as caffeine and sugar along with other impurities. Pure water helps the body to flush out waste and bacteria, playing a key role in maintaining a healthy organism. This couldnt be more important than in autumn, as the cold weather sets in and our immune systems are more vulnerable.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </div>

          leasing
         </a>
         and credit solutions to help you spread the cost.
        </p>
