      <p>
       Packer Fastener added a new nut to its inventory Thursday morning.
      </p>
      <p>
       Just before 9 a.m., a crew from De Pere's Orde Sign and Graphics Inc. bolted the world's biggest hex nut into place on a concrete slab near the northeast corner of South Ashland Avenue and Lombardi Avenue.
      </p>
      <p>
       Packer Fastener President Terry Albrecht commissioned De Pere's Robinson Metals to fabricate the 10-foot hex nut after seeing the company's gigantic replica of the Lombardi Trophy almost two years ago.
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        RELATED:
       </b>
       <a href="/story/news/local/2015/05/13/worlds-largest-hex-nut/27265659/" title="http://www.greenbaypressgazette.com/story/news/local/2015/05/13/worlds-largest-hex-nut/27265659/">
        World's largest nut coming to Green Bay
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Albrecht said he wanted to celebrate the company's new headquarters at 728 Lombardi Ave. as well as to add something unique to the Greater Green Bay landscape.
      </p>
