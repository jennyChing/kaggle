         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span lang="EN-US">
           Whats your favorite word in the English language? If you asked me, I might say something like sleep, pizza, football or vacation. But then again, I have to say that my very favorite word in English is the word free.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span lang="EN-US">
           In the particular context that I think of the word free it means without cost. Theres not very many things in life worth having that are free. But thanks to the Internet, a very useful tool/ toy is available to anyone absolutely free! What is it that Im talking about when I say this? Obviously Im talking about free emulators!
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span lang="EN-US">
           Emulators are one of the greatest things ever invented, in my opinion. They take old consoles that are no longer sold or used in the mainstream and make their games available for free to anyone on the Internet. Essentially, they recreate the entire gaming experience for that particular platform and store it all on your hard drive. Its honestly nothing short of a miracle.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span lang="EN-US">
           I know that personally, I would have thought of an idea like this by myself, but it would have stayed as just an idea. Never would I ever be able to create this beautiful piece of computer software that has brought so much joy to fans of retro gaming.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span lang="EN-US">
           Developers that have created these emulators have spent countless hours writing the code and maintaining the sites that store these files on the Internet for anyone that would like to download them to do so for free. Its seriously a beautiful thing.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span lang="EN-US">
           Due to free emulators being readily available on the Internet, other devoted gamers have found a way to get games up on the Internet as well to go with the emulators. Because really, what good would an emulator be without the games?
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span lang="EN-US">
           Many of these games are stored on the same sites as the emulators, so the site can be a one stop shop for gamers looking to enjoy these old games and systems on their computer. As nice as it is for these programs to host the software on their sites for free, some of the software that is available is not particularly good. Considering that the process of emulation is not an easy one, it shouldnt surprise us that mistakes are made and bugs exist in this free software. If youre new to emulation, it might take you a little bit of time to find an emulator and game library that contains software you find to work consistently for you.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span lang="EN-US">
           If the situation I just described sounds like you, theres no need to worry. Let me point you toward a site where you can find the software that you need to take advantage of the great free emulators available on the Internet! The site is called Gameex.com. Its over ten years old now and its supported by regular updates and improvements to make sure that you have a top notch emulation experience! Check it out now and enjoy their hard work for free! Happy gaming!
          </span>
         </p>
