      <p>
       When is an autobiography not an autobiography?
      </p>
      <p>
       When it involves Billy Joel.
      </p>
      <p>
       Six years ago, the Piano Man signed with a publisher to tell his life story, with ghost help from veteran music writer Fred Schruers. Deep into the process, Joel pulled out, due to worries over how personal and probing the book might become. Even so, he allowed Schruers to finish the tome on his own, drawing on approximately 100 hours of interviews he already granted him.
      </p>
      <p>
       Whether any back deals exist between Joel, 65, and Schruers to block specific information isnt known. But there wouldnt seem to be much, judging by the candor of Billy Joel: The Definitive Biography (Crown Archetype, $29), out Tuesday.
      </p>

      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       The book offers a funny and revealing account of one of the most popular songwriters of our time  not to mention of our area. Here are some of the books biggest revelations:
      </p>
      <p>
        Joel's parents, German Jews, barely survived the Holocaust, having escaped at the last minute through Cuba. Many of Joel's relatives weren't so fortunate. The family's whole harrowing story is chronicled in a little-known, 2001 documentary titled "The Joel Files."
      </p>
      <p>
        Always self-deprecating, Joel assessed his star appeal early on, with the line Look at me. Do I look like a rock star? I look like a guy who makes pizza.
      </p>
      <p>
        Despondent over his failing career in his 20s, Joel tried to commit suicide by swallowing fistfuls of Nembutal, washed down with furniture polish. When he awoke, he thought, Oh great, I couldnt even do this right.
      </p>
      <p>
        Atlantic Records tried to sign Billy right before Columbia did. They lost out because label honcho Jerry Wexler told the artist his song "Piano Man" sounded too much like Jerry Jeff Walker's 1968 hit "Mr. Bojangles." He also told Joel, "You play too much. Stop."
      </p>
      <p>
        Joel didnt want to marry his first wife, Elizabeth, back in the early 70s because he was only 24. Looking at their wedding pics he remarked, this man looks like hes getting booked for a crime.
      </p>
      <div class="a-module" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.1987141!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/face-face-tour.jpg" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
       <figure class="a-image h">
        <img alt="Exported.;" src="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.1987141.1414284021!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/face-face-tour.jpg"/>
        <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">
         DELORES JOHNSON/AP
        </span>
        <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">
         Billy Joel's rivalry with Elton John (left) dates back to 1976, he says in the book.
        </h2>
        <a class="a-enlarge" href="#">
        </a>
       </figure>
      </div>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
        Even with "Piano Man" on the radio, the like-named album netted Joel less than $8000 in its breakthrough year.
      </p>
      <p>
        Joels first rivalry with frenemy Elton John dates back to 1976. Im sick of being called The American Elton John, Joel fumed. Despite that, his producer insisted on hiring Elton's band to back him. Rather than do that, Billy fired the producer and tried to hire in his place the Beatles' guy George Martin. When Martin also insisted Joel fire his band, he balked. Joel says losing a chance to work with Martin "still hurts."
      </p>
      <p>
        The famed 1975 Daily News headline Ford to City: Drop Dead inspired Billys song Miami 2017 which envisions a post-apocalyptic New York.
      </p>
      <p>
        Billy wrote New York State of Mind not about the city but about the whole state. It was inspired by a bus trip to West Point.
      </p>
      <div class="a-module" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.1987145!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/joel.jpg" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
       <figure class="a-image h">
        <img alt="Exported.;" src="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.1987145.1414284025!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/joel.jpg"/>
        <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">
         Corkery, Richard
        </span>
        <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">
         Billy Joel is seen with Christie Brinkley. He once had a date with her and Elle Macpherson in the same day, according to the book.
        </h2>
        <a class="a-enlarge" href="#">
        </a>
       </figure>
      </div>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
        Joels record company honcho Walter Yetnikoff advised Billy to fire his wife Elizabeth as his manager back in 77, calling her a sadist.
      </p>
      <p>
        Joel wrote Just The Way You Are for Liz. But when he played it for her, the first thing she said was Do I get half the publishing? Right then, Joel realized he wrote a song about loving someone who doesnt need to change for someone who in fact had changed.
      </p>
      <div class="a-module">
       <blockquote class="quotations">
        I couldnt help thinking that Frank Sinatra would have made a threesome out of that night.
       </blockquote>
      </div>
      <p>
        Joel didn't want to sing "Just The Way You Are" at first, fearing he'd get tagged "as a lounge singer." Linda Ronstadt said "you have to - it's a hit!"
      </p>
      <p>
        Billy isn't enamored of his own piano skills. "There's no subtlety in my playing, no real nuance at all," he says.
      </p>
      <p>
        When Joel realized, in 1982, he was ripped off of millions by his wife and her brother Frank, he concluded that I have no concept how this family works. It was poison from day one. I hooked up with the Borgias.
      </p>
      <p>
        Billy was meeting Christie Brinkley for the first time in St. Barts when an untested singer named Whitney asked if he would listen to her. There was an edge of go away kid, youre bothering me, Joel remembers as he dismissed her. Ive kicked myself a million times since. I couldve discovered Whitney Houston.
      </p>
      <p>
        When Joel dated model Elle Macpherson, he thought she was so tall, I look like Bubbles the Chimp next to her. I realized this was just not going to work. Also, she was 19 and he was in his 30s.Through a mix-up, both Macpherson and the woman he dated next  Brinkley  ended up in Billys Central Park West apartment at the same time. Brinkley bowed out and he spent the night with Macpherson. But, Billy thought, I couldnt help thinking that Frank Sinatra would have made a threesome out of that night.
      </p>
      <p>
        Billy hated his 1986 album "The Bridge" and only put it out due to record company pressure. It sold 2 mllion copies.
      </p>
      <p>
        Billy so despised the recording of his Russian concert "Koheup," he called it "Kaput."
      </p>
      <p>
        Jann Wenner, co-founder and publisher of Rolling Stone magazine, told Billy to name his song We Didnt Start The Fire and encouraged him to put it out. The melody made Billy squirm. It was like a droning mosquito, he says.
      </p>
      <div class="a-module" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.1987144!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/entertainment-grammy.jpg" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
       <figure class="a-image h">
        <img alt="Exported.;" src="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.1987144.1414284024!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/entertainment-grammy.jpg"/>
        <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">
         MIKE BLAKE/REUTERS
        </span>
        <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">
         Billy Joel poses (right) with Elton John at the Grammys in 2000.
        </h2>
        <a class="a-enlarge" href="#">
        </a>
       </figure>
      </div>
      <p>
        In 1988, then Columbia Records chief Walter Yetnikoff told a money-strapped Billy that something must be wrong with his finances. "Do you know how many records you've sold," he asked. "You should have $50 million in cash."
      </p>
      <p>
        Joel dropped acid all of three times and used heroin just once on a plane from Amsterdam to Stockholm. Billy says the feeling of incredible pleasure heroin gave him scared the hell out of him. He worried he might always chase that experience. Instead, alcohol became his drug of choice. The truth is, I dont know much about sobriety, I know a hell of a lot about drinking, he told Schruers.
      </p>
      <p>
        The spats between Billy and Elton escalated when Joel was sick and had to cancel a show in Manchester. Elton would not forgive him. "I don't give a f--k," he said, blaming Joel's drinking. "He's not taking care of himself."
      </p>
      <p>
        When Billy didn't want to extend some later "Face to Face" live dates with Elton, the British pianist told the press he respected Billy's decision. Schruers says "privately, he was fuming."
      </p>
      <p>
        In 2011, Elton told Rolling Stone that Billy "was coasting., We've had so many cancelled tours because of illness and...alcholism. Every time he goes to rehab, it's rehab light." Billy shot back a note to Elton saying "what gives you the omnipotent moral certainty and authority to justify the public humiliation of anyone? We are done." It took several years for them to reconcile.
      </p>
      <p>
        Joel earned so much money, despite the rip-offs, that, in 2010, Forbes reported his net worth at $160 million. "It's north of that," billy told Schruers.
      </p>
      <p>
        When Jerry Seinfield wanted to buy Billys house in the Hamptons, he asked How much? Billy answered out of thin air: thirty two point five million. Jerry said okay. And so the deal was done. No house had ever sold for that much on Long Island, or in all of New York state, at that time.
      </p>
