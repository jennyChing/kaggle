                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Christmas Mantel: The Stars Are Brightly Shining
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2586423736399138041" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <a href="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-D3Q7klqxIac/VH6WTuGu_BI/AAAAAAABLEs/kx3IBHxtsgo/christmas-mantle-with-stars.jpg">
                      <img alt="Christmas mantel with stars in gold silver and red. BEAUTIFUL!" border="0" height="600" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-D3Q7klqxIac/VH6WTuGu_BI/AAAAAAABLEs/kx3IBHxtsgo/christmas-mantle-with-stars.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="christmas-mantle-with-stars.jpg" width="476"/>
                     </a>
                     My first Christmas Mantel of the season is finally complete! This year I wanted to do a design that was a little more modern and stream lined vs my typically more
                     <a href="http://www.thekimsixfix.com/2013/11/o-come-let-us-adore-him-rustic.html">
                      rustic
                     </a>
                     or
                     <a href="http://www.thekimsixfix.com/2013/12/the-nutcracker-land-of-sweets-christmas.html">
                      kid friendly
                     </a>
                     versions. I decided on highly graphic stars and I love how they stand out.
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="Star christmas mantel" border="0" height="600" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-nV6WOGeCZtA/VH6a2ZaDcyI/AAAAAAABLF0/r7XOOqqnteE/star-christmas-mantel.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="star-christmas-mantel.jpg" width="374"/>
                     I countered the non-traditional star grouping with the line The Stars Are Brightly Shining from the Christmas Hymn O Holy Night."
                     <br/>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="Stars are brightly shining" border="0" height="600" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-zYdA10BEugs/VH6assd75rI/AAAAAAABLFM/jOebm8mdw5I/Stars-are-brightly-shining.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Stars-are-brightly-shining.jpg" width="394"/>
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="Stars are brightly shining mantel" border="0" height="436" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-LCZQ7pgJgjs/VH6auZF0T-I/AAAAAAABLFU/vjB6EsuJ2Tc/Stars-are-brightly-shining-mantel.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Stars-are-brightly-shining-mantel.jpg" width="600"/>
                     I found the two large stars at Ikea on clearance last year, and the gold star (which is metal) was also on sale at a local boutique. I have been saving them all year to do something like this. The three small stars are from the dollar store and I bought them this year to complete the collection.
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="The Stars Are Brightly Shining Banner" border="0" height="362" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-koDPNOkyDo0/VH6aqSkiRKI/AAAAAAABLFE/o7_vO1zmTGc/The-Stars-Are-Brightly-Shining-Banner.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="The-Stars-Are-Brightly-Shining-Banner.jpg" width="600"/>
                     The banner is made from red glittered card stock that I cut on my
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Silhouette-Electronic-Cutting-Starter-Bundle/dp/B00NAX7H78/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1417583092&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=silhouette+cameo">
                      Silhouette
                     </a>
                     . The letters are glue gunned onto two pieces of quilting thread (to keep them from rotating. This was by far the trickiest part of the mantel. I could have just taped them up onto the fireplace directly, but in person they look like they are magically floating, and I love it.
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="The Stars Are Brightly Shining" border="0" height="409" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-IX0LPOcd-eo/VH6ayHnESaI/AAAAAAABLFk/d0ozk7VTCd0/The-Stars-Are-Brightly-Shining.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="The-Stars-Are-Brightly-Shining.jpg" width="600"/>
                     The greenery is traditional, and a lot like all my
                     <a href="http://www.thekimsixfix.com/p/christmas.html">
                      other Christmas mantels
                     </a>
                     . I know what I like and this is it!
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="Reindeer poinsettias" border="0" height="600" src="http://lh5.ggpht.com/-s25jQEE0nmk/VH6aweMh8TI/AAAAAAABLFc/q8fM9ZffV0Y/reindeer-poinsettias.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="reindeer-poinsettias.jpg" width="380"/>
                     Once again I picked up the 99 cent Home Depot black Friday poinsettias so I had to use them here along with a few other silver accessories.
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="Red gold silver star mantel" border="0" height="600" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-8nJ5jveUATc/VH6aoIzw1kI/AAAAAAABLE8/sedH0FSSM1k/Red-gold-silver-star-mantel.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Red-gold-silver-star-mantel.jpg" width="428"/>
                     <br/>
                     <img alt="Stars mantle" border="0" height="600" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-EmbPrJ-VyrI/VH6a0StUGhI/AAAAAAABLFs/A0SDr98YvgI/stars-mantle.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="stars-mantle.jpg" width="483"/>
                     <br/>
                     My plans for the other
                     <a href="http://www.thekimsixfix.com/2014/11/airstone-stacked-stone-fireplace-update.html">
                      newly remodeled fireplace
                     </a>
                     are much more rustic and less colorful, but I am not sure I can top this. It came out better than I ever imagined!
