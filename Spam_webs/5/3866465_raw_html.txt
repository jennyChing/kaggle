          <p>
           <b>
            NEW YORK
           </b>
           - When a
           <b>
            Philippine Airline flight to Manila was cancelled
           </b>
           last Sunday, passengers complained about the way PAL handled the situation.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Dozens of passengers said they were left in the dark and missed a few days stay in the Philippines.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Philippine
 Airlines flight PR 127 to Manila via Vancouver was already on JFK 
Airports runway when the flight was stalled due to some mechanical 
problem.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Passenger Shawnee Motos-Denovesi was booked on that flight Sunday.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Theres something wrong and they fixed and fixed it for an hour and a half so sabi well try again,"Denovesi said.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           But she said an hour and a half later the flight was eventually cancelled.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           *How
 could a freaking 300 passenger Airbus not be cleared to take off? Na 
they wait for until the engine is turned on and they found something 
major wrong. Its unbelievable*, she said.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           New York resident Harlan Ragays mother was one of those inconvenienced by the cancelled PAL flight.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           In
 an email to ABS-CBN news, Ragay said that flight vouchers for another 
airline were given but were not accepted because the vouchers were 
allegedly not properly filled, or lacked a validation stamp from PAL 
officials.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           "It was so disorganized, unprofessional, theyre not even sympathetic and the fact that theyre leading us on, Denovesi said.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           PAL
 official Leah Trinidad, who declined an on cam interview, said that 
Sundays flight cancellation was due to a minor engine problem but the 
flight captain reportedly refused to fly the plane due to safety issues.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           She also said that all passengers who missed their flight Sunday have been now all accommodated in later flights.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           It
 is understandable that things break down. We get this problem. But, 
like to be handled the way we were, not even apologetic, not even nice, 
it was so disorganized, Denovesi said.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           In an emailed response to
 Balitang America, PAL officials wrote the Sunday flight to Manila was 
grounded for a maintenance check.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           They are verifying if vouchers issued to affected passengers were indeed not honored by other airlines.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           PAL said they empathize with passengers over the inconvenience.
