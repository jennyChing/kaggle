         <h1 class="title single-title">
          Kill for success? Maybe you should think about it.
         </h1>
         <!-- End Title -->
         <!-- Start Post Meta -->
         <div class="post-info">
          <span class="theauthor">
           <a href="http://www.survivingchange.co.za/author/willem-gous/" rel="nofollow" title="Posts by Willem Gous">

          <span class="thetime">
           October 2, 2014
          </span>
          |
          <span class="thecategory">
           <a href="http://www.survivingchange.co.za/category/surviving-change-journey/" rel="nofollow">
            Surviving Change Journey
           </a>
          </span>
          |
          <span class="thecomment">
           <a href="http://www.survivingchange.co.za/kill-for-success-maybe-you-should-think-about-it/#comments">

        <div class="post-single-content box mark-links">
         <p>
          I have not always followed this advice and thus have had major failures and lost money because of it. The times I did follow my advice, it saved me and made me lots of money and helped me to focus on the things that will bring success.
         </p>
         <p>
          Opportunity and success are interesting things. The problem we face is that we focus on the size of the opportunity and chase that into the sunset and many times until our demise and even the demise of our relationships with our spouses and our children. The size of this opportunity is not always based on fact or hard data. It is susceptible to biases, big talk, ego and our wish and yearning to be successful. Inflating the size of the opportunity makes our wishes grow even more, resulting in bigger dreams and expectations on the return potential of the opportunity.
         </p>
         <p>
          I like the analogy of a plane hurtling down a runway in order to get airborne to describe us chasing opportunity. While that plane hurtles down the runway it burns fuel and other resources and costs money, the plane is not airborne but it is costing money. This is where we lose all the money from our savings accounts; retirement annuities and loans from friends and family. I have done this myself. Where I tell myself, If I only push a little harder, work a little more, dedicate more of myself to this, I will make it. I have done this not for a short period of time, but for years on end on one project in my life. I have lost the equivalent of an Aston Martin DB9 sports car.
         </p>
         <p>
          A time to kill
         </p>
         <p>
          The quicker we kill these non-performing projects, the quicker we can focus on those projects that work. We become blind to reality the longer we go on and the longer we wait for our expected returns. You have a limited amount of time upon this earth, use that time for many projects and not just one single one that might wipe you out completely. Kill those non-performing ideas and projects. Remove them from your life completely. Authors refer to this as killing your darlings when they have to kill a much loved character that they created for a book or story.
         </p>
         <p>
          Where to kill and where to nurture
         </p>
         <p>
          The kill switch is a balance between long term and short term and the time available to you. If all your projects and ideas are long term, then I sure as hell wish you A LOT of money and a faith and surety that those ideas will pan out.
         </p>
         <p>
          A good idea is to start all short term. Just start. Just do something and see how it pans out. Nothing special about this advice. See what opportunities you have right now and sell it and keep selling it and see where it leads. If it does not lead somewhere significant in a short space of time, then kill it. I have found that most of my long term projects started with an idea that I simply started executing. A magazine did an interview with me a while back about people who have multiple careers and I discovered that all my careers were by chance, because I simply started doing something without much idea of the return or what it might become in the future. I just did it because it looked like an opportunity. I focused on what I can do and not on what I will get in future. That is the important advice I am giving you.
         </p>
         <p>
          So do yourself and the world a favour, and go and do something today. Start something now!
         </p>
