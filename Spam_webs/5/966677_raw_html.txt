       <h4 class="title">
        <a href="/articles/buenos-aires-street-art">
         A Tour of Buenos Aires Street Art
        </a>
       </h4>
       <div class="attribution">
        by
        <strong>
         <a href="/users/allison?view=articles">

         </a>
        </strong>

      <div class="body-wrap">
       <div class="body">
        <!DOCTYPE html PUBLIC "-//W3C//DTD HTML 4.0 Transitional//EN" "http://www.w3.org/TR/REC-html40/loose.dtd">
        <p>
         In a city where people were once imprisoned just for saying the wrong things, a celebration of free dialogue has sprung up on the building walls. I found out about the Buenos Aires street art tour put on by
         <a href="http://www.graffitimundo.com/" target="_blank">
          Graffiti Mundo
         </a>
         from the excellent
         <a href="http://buenosaires.for91days.com/2011/04/18/a-tour-of-buenos-aires-best-graffiti/" target="_blank">
          91 Days
         </a>
         bloggers and was excited to make it a part of my recent visit to Argentina.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5148/5659504967_d6832b5b04_z.jpg" title="Gualicho" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The tour met on the edge of Palermo Hollywood, where we started with some examples of large scale murals featuring colorful creatures painted with roller brushes over entire buildings. The vibrant, playful works were put up in the early 2000's, when at one point Argentina had five different presidents in two weeks.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img align="middle" alt="" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5263/5659485669_00cb10cda7_z.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Protests and angry political declarations were scrawled on buildings throughout Buenos Aires, and some porteo artists decided to collaborate on some positive work to brighten up the streets. This idea of creating art for the city continued as more and more people offered their building walls, and now the city is adorned everywhere with paintings and stencils.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5267/5660145994_8942ac1f20_z.jpg" title="Octopus" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The tour was led by expat Jonny and local Ana, both connected with the street art scene in Buenos Aires and incredibly well-informed on the history behind the movement. The reason that huge pieces can be done in broad daylight, and even tags that look like vandalism are rarely criminalized, is a result of Argentina's tumultuous 20th century.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5307/5660081846_dbb7c9c323_z.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         From 1976 to 1983, thousands of people went missing in state-sponsored detentions. Members of the guerrilla group the Montoneros and leftist-activists, as well as their sympathizers, were tortured and murdered, their bodies carefully disposed of so that to this day it is impossible to get an exact count of how many were killed and how many fled the country. Most of the perpetrators in the military dictatorship were pardoned, and justice is still actively sought, most visibly by the Mothers of the Plaza de Mayo who continue to gather in the most visited public area of Buenos Aires.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" height="640" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5304/5660222050_384fe23e0d_z.jpg" title="Eternauta" width="480"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         A constant reminder of the injustices of the "Dirty War" is El Enternauta, whose stencil stalks the streets of the city. El Enternauta (loosely translated as "the eternal voyager") is a comic book character created by Hctor Germn Oesterheld, who himself went missing in 1976, apparently for no other reason than he wrote a "beautiful" biography on Ch Guevara. The science fiction
         <em>
          El Eternauta
         </em>
         comic books follow a deadly snowfall in Buenos Aires that kills everyone it touches, followed by an alien invasion. A stencil that has the face of the late Argentine president and humanitarian Nstor Kirchner as El Eternauta is everywhere in Buenos Aires, a constant sentinel to the city, where disappearances still go unexplained, even if some people want to forget.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5305/5660202120_9925e6a61d_z.jpg" title="Turtle" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         After so many years of repression, the streets of Buenos Aires are a site of free speech. Above is an example of one of the more political murals, painted by the artist
         <a href="http://graffitimundo.com/en/artists/jazz/" target="_blank">
          Jaz
         </a>
         , remembering the 1994 bombing of the Argentine Israelite Mutual Association that killed 85 people. Other walls will have scrawled slogans for and against the current president Cristina Fernandez de Kirchner side-by-side.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5030/5659641595_7f4ce9b083_z.jpg" title="Tour" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Graffiti Mundo tour covered much of Palermo, and included a visit to a studio that's used by artists Jaz and Ever. Below are some more of my photos and you can see a
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/astrozombie/sets/72157626500870459/" target="_blank">
          complete set here
         </a>
         . The tour is 90 pesos (about $22), and is a ridiculous bargain for the amount of art you get to see (they rent a minibus to get around to more obscure sites) and the insight into Argentina's history.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5141/5660177822_a87a2ae0e1_z.jpg" title="Wolves" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Fighting wolves by Jaz.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5065/5659636745_45c5bfbd6b_z.jpg" title="Jaz" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Boxing tigers by Jaz.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5223/5659616209_2221e3c652_z.jpg" title="Studio Cat" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Cat in a studio shared by Jaz and Ever.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5261/5660183556_27e6784678_z.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Studio space shared by artists Jaz and Ever.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5062/5660065578_cbae791ceb_z.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Street art on the edges of Palermo Hollywood.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5181/5660173546_991545d656_z.jpg" title="Plane Eyes" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Mural by
         <a href="http://graffitimundo.com/en/artists/ever/" target="_blank">
          Ever
         </a>
         . The clouds coming out of the eyes are meant to represent that person's dreams.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5149/5659628159_9dcd6a3b73_z.jpg" title="Mao" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Mao by Ever.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5228/5659539189_749b9537f7_z.jpg" title="Rhinos" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p id="firstHeading">
         Drer's rhinos by
         <a href="http://graffitimundo.com/en/artists/cabaio-stencil/" target="_blank">
          Cabaio Stencil
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5021/5659543975_9b892c2618_z.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Tiger in stencil mural by Cabaio Stencil.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" height="435" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5064/5659548983_a1d5d68bcf_z.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Detail of Cabaio Stencil mural.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5104/5659534231_2b30b0ff5a_z.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Tattooed octopus man in theCabaio Stencil mural.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
