                     <h2>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        15 Fun for Teen Girls Activities: A Sleepover, Slumber Birthday Party
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </h2>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       A teenage girl sleepover party with fun games, activities, and swag bags is
                       <a href="http://www.kidscreativechaos.com/2015/02/bestsleepovergamesandactivitiesforteenslumberparty.html" target="_blank">
                        the best slumber party ever
                       </a>
                       ! Don't just read these titles and think, been there done that. We've added a little twist to each
                       <a href="http://www.kidscreativechaos.com/2013/10/birthday-party-ideas-and-activities-for.html" target="_blank">
                        birthday party activity
                       </a>
                       .
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <img alt="15 Slumber Party Games for Teen Girls: Best Sleepover Ever" border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-7jcni0MF1mw/U31cA_WdSRI/AAAAAAAAJio/tFnmLaUIpFI/s1600/Slumber+party+sleepover+activities.png" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="15 Slumber Party Games for Teen Girls: Best Sleepover Ever" width="640"/>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         Slumber Party Sleepover Games and Activities for Teen Girls.
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <br/>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       1.
                       <b>
                        Balloon Charades
                       </b>
                       . Grabs some stationary and write down ideas for Charades. What are charades? How do you play charades? You know, when you act something out, pull on your ear for "sounds like", put two fingers in the air to signify two words, but you never speak. Yeah, that game. So, fold the paper up into skinny rectangles, slide it inside a balloon, blow it up, and use it as a party decoration. When you are ready to play charades give each guest a balloon. Have her pop it - sitting on it is the most fun, read it, and act it out for the other guests to guess. Super Fun!
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       Yeah, you can do this with "Truth or Dare" too, but oh how I loathe Truth or Dare. Shouldn't it be called "Humiliation or Bullying"?
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       Hate it!
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       2.
                       <b>
                        Sleeping Bag Switch.
                       </b>
                       For this game, each girl brings a sleeping bag to the party. To start the game, the girls unroll their bags and lay them out on the floor. Then, one girl goes out of the room and the remaining girls pick someone else's bag to hide in. The girl then returns and tries to guess who is who or who is where. You can also have only two girls switch, and see if she can tell who switched. Fun times!
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       3.
                       <b>
                        Crazy Makeovers
                       </b>
                       . Time to pull out the make up, and let the girls give each other makeovers. No peeking until it is done. The beautician can choose to make a crazy makeover or a beautiful, glamorous one. The girl getting the makeup has to guess which type of makeover she got before the big reveal.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       4.
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <b>
                        Spin the Nail Polish Bottle.
                       </b>
                       Each girl takes a turn picking out a color and spinning the bottle. This works best on a table. Who ever the bottle points to when it finishes spinning paints
                       <i>
                        a nail
                       </i>
                       with that color. Then, she chooses the next color to spin. When everyone has 10 nails painted, take a picture and share them with your friends on Instagram. Make sure you have lots of colors to choose!
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                       <tbody>
                        <tr>
                         <td>
                          <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ZfI8G4YQFX4/UjXqN9KV0MI/AAAAAAAABDo/rR4u_zC91P4/s1600/2013-09-15+12.59.36.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                           <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                            <img alt="Nail polish for nail polish bottle spin game" border="0" height="480" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ZfI8G4YQFX4/UjXqN9KV0MI/AAAAAAAABDo/rR4u_zC91P4/s320/2013-09-15+12.59.36.jpg" title="Nail polish for nail polish bottle spin game" width="640"/>
                           </span>
                          </a>
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                         <td class="tr-caption">
                          <span style="font-size: x-small;">
                           Choose a variety of colors for Nail Polish Spin the Bottle game.
                          </span>
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                       </tbody>
                      </table>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       5.
                      </span>
                      <b style="font-family: inherit;">
                       Make your Own Glitter Body Scrub.
                      </b>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       Find small jars that seal tightly, fine glitter (white is nice), coconut oil (a wonderful moisturizer for the scrub), scented extract, and sugar or coarse salt. Sugar is best. Salt is great for exfoliating, but bad if it gets in your eyes. Choose an extract: Peppermint, Almond (my favorite), Coconut, or Vanilla. Add a few drops of extract to the sugar and glitter to make a paste. Now you have your own moisturizer and
                      </span>
                      exfoliator
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       . When purchasing coconut oil you want this kind,
                       <a href="http://www.heartynaturals.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                        extra virgin coconut oil
                       </a>
                       .
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                       <tbody>
                        <tr>
                         <td style="text-align: center;">
                          <a href="http://heartynaturals.com/image/cache/data/3-226x387.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                           <img alt="Coconut Oil for Glitter Scrub." border="0" height="320" src="http://heartynaturals.com/image/cache/data/3-226x387.png" title="Coconut Oil for Glitter Scrub." width="188"/>
                          </a>
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                         <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                          Coconut Oil for Glitter Scrub.
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                       </tbody>
                      </table>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       6.
                       <b>
                        Dress Up Divas.
                       </b>
                       Go to a thrift shop and find evening gowns or formal wear and fancy heels the girls can use to dress up.
                      </span>
                      Place the gowns in a large bag.
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       Before you let the girls see the gowns, have them make
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://www.kidscreativechaos.com/2014/03/activities-10-fun-things-to-make-at.html" style="font-family: inherit;" target="_blank">
                       blindfolds that double as beauty masks
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       . Once everyone is blindfolded, have them choose a dress by touch alone. After they model the dresses, they can trade and have a fashion show or make up a reality show. Don't forget a full-length mirror.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                       <tbody>
                        <tr>
                         <td style="text-align: center;">
                          <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-RDcWoJv5-bo/UwFrvhNstuI/AAAAAAAAJC0/C7_kh-ok_IQ/s1600/thingsforteenstomakesleepingmask.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                           <img alt="Make a sleeping mask or blindfold." border="0" height="258" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-RDcWoJv5-bo/UwFrvhNstuI/AAAAAAAAJC0/C7_kh-ok_IQ/s1600/thingsforteenstomakesleepingmask.jpg" title="Make a sleeping mask or blindfold." width="400"/>
                          </a>
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                         <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                          For a blindfold or sleeping mask tutorial visit
                          <a href="http://www.henryhappened.com/diy-sleep-mask-2.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                           Henry Happened
                          </a>
                          blog.
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                       </tbody>
                      </table>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      7.
                      <b>
                       Pillowcase Decorating Activity.
                      </b>
                      Purchase pillowcases to decorate. Grab fabric makers, tacky glue, and ribbon. You can also find fun,
                      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00004TS2H/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00004TS2H&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=crekidhavfun-20&amp;linkId=JDALTNSFK5SJYLHY" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                       iron on transfers
                      </a>
                      <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=crekidhavfun-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00004TS2H" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                      to embellish. If you plan in advance, you can decorate a large gift bag for each guest. Load it up with all that Birthday Party Swag!
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      8.
                      <b>
                       Pillow Fight.
                      </b>
                      A classic game. As part of the swag bag items, create mini-pillows. You can often find little pillows at the dollar store or use travel pillows. Decorate them as mentioned above. When it is time for bed, be a cool party host. Tell the girls to grab their mini-pillows. On your mark, set, pillow fight! Just watch out for the lamps :)
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      9.
                      <b>
                       Flip Flop Decorating Activity.
                      </b>
                      Find out the Flip Flop shoe sizes of each guest before the party. Get some yarn, baubles, Sharpie Markers, and cloth scraps. Decorate to your heart's desire. Toss them in that swag bag to take home.
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       10.
                       <b>
                        Cup Stacking.
                       </b>
                       When you are buying party supplies, get some extra cups for the party guests to stack. Take turns to see who can get the most cups stacked in a minute or who has the most creative tower. Even better, everyone can start at the same time. This makes it more nerve wracking and chaotic! Hey, don't knock my tower down!
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <img alt="Minute to Win it Games for a Birthday Party." border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-_Wl6gWalYJ0/UjX8EOJnG-I/AAAAAAAABEM/NU8ynfW7e2s/s320/2013-09-15+14.24.55.jpg" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Minute to Win it Games for a Birthday Party." width="640"/>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         Minute to Win it Games for a Birthday Party.
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       11.
                       <b>
                        Minute to Win it Games.
                       </b>
                       Imagine the possibilities! Just like cup stacking, come up with several activities that can be completed in one minute. Everyone grab one of those cups. Set it in the middle of a table, grab a Ping Pong ball, and with only one bounce allowed, bounce the ball into the cup. Hurry! The clock is ticking. Tick Tock. You can also use the a cup to fill a bucket with water (use a colorful sand pail).
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                      12.
                      <b>
                       Dance Off.
                      </b>
                      Time for the girls to get their dance moves on and have fun. This is great to play after the Dress up Diva game. She who dances the longest wins. Ha! You thought it was a game of skill. Nope. It is a game of "get them tired so they don't put your panties in the freezer"! Sweet Dreams. What's that? You're dreaming of popsicles?
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                      13.
                     </span>
                     <b style="font-family: inherit;">
                      Board Games.
                     </b>
                     <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                      Don't think bored games. These board games are fun because each girl makes her own game to play or
                     </span>
                     collaborate
                     <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                      on onesuper, cool game. Cereal boxes, paper plates, and the white side of the wrapping paper are great ways to make a board. Paper plates make game spinners. Old, recycled business cards make excellent playing cards or game cards. Stickers are fun too. Fold paper and cut it into a cube pattern to make your own dice. Still boring? Take those balloons and fill them with game topics. For example, "Make a game about Rock Stars" or "Make a Mall Shopping Card Game" or "Make a game of questions". Let your imaginations run wild. This can be especially fun for preteens and tweens.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       14.
                       <b>
                        Classic Board Games.
                       </b>
                       Find out who your moguls are when you pull out the
                       <i>
                        Monopoly
                       </i>
                       game or start up the giggles with
                       <i>
                        Trouble
                       </i>
                       . But that's boring, right? Pass around a basket of accessories. Think grandma glasses, nerd spectacles, giant clip-on earrings, men's ties,
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      satin gloves, and cowboy hats. Now, play those games in character with silly accents.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       15.
                       <b>
                        Slurpin' for Worms.
                       </b>
                       <a href="http://www.kidscreativechaos.com/2012/05/slurpin-for-worms-birthday-activity.html" target="_blank">
                        Gummy Worm Game
                       </a>
                       Grab a bag of gummi worms or gummi bears and a can of whipped cream. Place the worms on a paper plate and cover them with the cream. It should look like a fake pie, you know, the kind you get in the face? Yeah, you might want to wear a raincoat for this one, and Mom might want to lay down a painting drop cloth. Players must keep their hands behind their backs at all times. Dive in. This is a great game for "Minute to Win It" too. Who can slurp up the most worms? Who can eat the most?
                      </span>
                     </div>
