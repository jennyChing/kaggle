       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Repeat of the screen grab, except with linkage in working order : Rubaiyat For Rocky is from the book
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Cottonmouth-Kisses-Clint-Catalyst/dp/0916397653/">
         Cottonmouth Kisses
        </a>
        . Thanks to
        <a href="http://beautynursedondarkness.tumblr.com/post/26590436403/rubaiyat-for-rocky">
         Beauty Nursed On Darkness
        </a>
        for the spiffy lay-out / introduction to the Tumblrsphere.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Apologies for the didactic, but simply For Those Whom . . . [blah blah blah]
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <span id="more-11545">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <big>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          R E F E R E N T I A L
         </span>
        </big>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <u>
          DEFINITION OF RUBAIYAT
         </u>
         , via
         <a href="http://www.poetrysoup.com/definition/rubaiyat">
          Poetry Soup
         </a>
         :
        </small>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        The Rubaiyat is a Persian form of several quatrains. Its name derives from the Arabic plural of the word for quatrain. This, in turn, comes from the Arabic Rub, meaning four.
        <br/>
        This Persian form of poetry is a series of rhymed quatrains. In each quatrain, all lines rhyme except the third, leading to this pattern:
       </p>
       <p>
        a
        <br/>
        a  2nd line rhymes with the first.
        <br/>
        b
        <br/>
        a  4th line rhymes with the first and second
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         EXPLANATION OF THE RUBAIYAT STANZA
        </u>
        , via
        <a href="http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/rubaiyat%20stanza">
         Merriam-Webster Dictionary
        </a>
        :
       </p>
       <p>
        An iambic pentameter quatrain with a rhyme scheme aaba
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         ORIGIN OF RUBAIYAT STANZA
        </u>
        :
       </p>
       <p>
        The Rubiyt of Omar Khayym, quatrains translated by Edward FitzGerald [1859]
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        And now, all formalities aside . . . Rocky, In My Heart and In My Art : You Remain
       </p>
