       <h1 class="entry-title">
        On-Page SEO Guide &amp; Optimization Checklist
       </h1>
       <div class="post-content">
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 1.5em;">
          <em>
           A Step-by-Step Guide Showing You EXACTLY How To Optimize Your Website Using Beginner to Advanced On-Page SEO Best Practices
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         On-Page SEO is the cornerstone of every well-rounded SEO campaign. Being on the first page of Google can be a game changer when you rank for keywords that are profitable for your business.
        </p>
        <p>
         The impact of #1 rankings cannot be understated.
        </p>
        <p>
         Search engines like Google work to deliver the best results every time a search is entered. Higher search engine placement means more traffic.
        </p>
        <p>
         When you increase the amount of people discovering your business, the number of people
         <em>
          contacting
         </em>
         your business goes up.More contact = more customers.
        </p>
        <p>
         The internet
         <em>
          is
         </em>
         word of mouth.
        </p>
        <p>
         Local SEO, or local search engine optimization, is the process of optimizing your website to rank higher in local search results. There are a number of factors that play into how sites are ranked.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want to get more mileage out of your SEO campaign, you need to focus on these 3 key areas:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          The quality of your content and site structure,
         </li>
         <li>
          The number of backlinks pointing to your domain;
         </li>
         <li>
          The number of legitimate social signals mentioning your website.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         Here is what you need to understand about SEO: the company that hones in and executes first is usually the one that dominates their market.
        </p>
        <p>
         When someone types a phrase like locksmith into their search bar, Google will also show local results, even though the specific city is not mentioned.
        </p>
        <p>
         With local SEO, youre dealing with a much smaller geographic area. Youre not targeting a national audience, so the competition pool is much smaller. Youre dealing with less businesses vying for the same customers.
        </p>
        <p>
         This gives you an advantage that is twofold:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Easier to rank for geo-targeted keywords.
         </li>
         <li>
          Easier to attract local customers that are ready to buy.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         By optimizing for your city or service area the majority of your website traffic comes from people who are local to you, interested in your services, and looking for a solution  right now.
        </p>
        <p>
         Do yourself a favor: optimize your website from the get-go.
        </p>
        <p>
         SEO takes time.
        </p>
        <p>
         The longer you put off optimizing your site, the longer it will be until you see results. Bottomline.
        </p>
        <h2>
         What is On-Page SEO?
        </h2>
        <p>
         On-page SEO is how well your site is optimized, from code to content.
        </p>
        <p>
         Is your site
         <a href="http://blog.rankpay.com/optimizing-for-mobile/" title="Optimizing for Mobile">
          responsive
         </a>
         ?
        </p>
        <p>
         Has your website been built to w3c standards (i.e. clean, readable code)?
        </p>
        <p>
         Have you used your
         <a href="http://blog.rankpay.com/how-to-choose-great-keywords/" title="How to Choose Great Keywords">
          keywords
         </a>
         throughout your site?
        </p>
        <p>
         Are you using
         <em>
          the right keywords?
         </em>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          On-page SEO is critical. Forget all the talk about links for a second. Yes,
          <a href="http://blog.rankpay.com/link-building-best-practices/" title="Link Building Best Practices">
           backlinks
          </a>
          are important, but your first priority is optimizing your website from thebeginning.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <h2>
         On-Page SEO Factors
        </h2>
        <p>
         Google looks at a number of different factors to determine if a site or web page has been optimized correctly. In the next few sections you will learn everything you need to know to optimize a web page.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1338" style="width: 780px">
         <img alt="On-Page SEO Tutorial" class="wp-image-1338 size-full" height="1040" src="http://blog.rankpay.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/onpage-seo-tutorial.gif" width="770"/>

         Our On-Page SEO Checklist is broken into two sections:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Basic On-Page SEO Optimization:
          </strong>
          Learn the exact steps you should take to optimize any webpage.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Advanced On-Page SEO Techniques:
          </strong>
          Dig into advanced on-page optimization strategies that you can implement right away.
         </li>

        <h2>
         On-Page SEO Checklist (Or How To Do On-Page SEO)
        </h2>
        <p>
         Backlinks are still the #1 Google ranking signal, but good SEO starts with on-page optimization. Heres how to optimize your site, page by page.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #1 Title tag
        </h3>
        <p>
         Place your keyword in the beginning of your title. The closer your keyword is to the beginning of your title, the easier it is for Google to determine what your page is about.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="On-Page SEO Tactics" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1335" height="272" src="http://blog.rankpay.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/on-page-seo-tactics.jpg" width="539"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Make your title more than 40 characters, but no longer than 70 characters. Any longer and your message will be cut off in search results.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #2 Meta description
        </h3>
        <p>
         Be sure to include your keyword in your meta description. While meta descriptions do not hold major weight in terms of SEO, a well written
         <a href="http://blog.rankpay.com/optimizing-the-keyword-meta-tag/" title="Optimizing the Keyword Meta Tag">
          meta description
         </a>
         helps increase CTR (click through rate), which is something Google looks at.
        </p>
        <p>
         Keep your meta descriptions under 160 characters and be as compelling a possible.Scope the competition and check out Google Ads to see what others are doing and use these examples to optimize your own.
        </p>

        <h3>
         #4 H1 tag
        </h3>
        <p>
         The H1 tag is the most important tag on the page. Be sure to make it your first heading tag and include your keyword (with a modifier).
        </p>
        <h3>
         #5 Optimize images
        </h3>
        <p>
         Use your keyword to name your image. Be sure to include your keyword in the title and alt descriptions as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         For example, using the example from Marys chocolate donuts, you would add an image to the page with a url structure like this:
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         &lt;img src =chocolate-donuts.jpg alt=Delicious Chocolate Donuts title=Delicious Chocolate Donuts /&gt;
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Notice how the image is named with the keyword? Youd also include image and alt tags that further described the image.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #6 The beginning of your post
        </h3>
        <p>
         Make sure you use your keyword in the first paragraph of your post. This is one of a variety of ranking factors looked at by Google and other search engines.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tip: If you use WordPress, install
         <a href="https://yoast.com/wordpress/plugins/" target="_blank" title="WordPress SEO by Yoast">
          WordPress SEO by Yoast
         </a>
         . Youll get clear instructions on how to optimize each page of your site in a very easy to understand way.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #7 H2 and H3 tags
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="On-Page SEO Tips" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1359" height="142" src="http://blog.rankpay.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/on-page-seo-tips.jpg" width="667"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Adding H2 and H3 heading tags helps structure your content for readers and search engines. They provide a hierarchy for the page that tells search engines which heading should have more weight.
        </p>
        <p>
         You should include variations of your keyword in your headings to give your on-page SEO a boost.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #8 Use variations throughout
        </h3>
        <p>
         Your keyword should appear throughout your post. Include your keyword (and variations) in your body text, headings and images. Synonyms of the word or alternative phrases are also important to add.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Advanced On-Page SEO Techniques
        </h2>
        <h3>
         #9 Use outbound links
        </h3>
        <p>
         Link to 1-2 authoritative sites. Do not use a nofollow tag. Linking to reputable sources adds trust to your site in Googles eyes. Plus, its a great way to offer your readers additional value by pointing them to new resources they can discover.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #10Make your content easily consumable
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.slate.com/articles/technology/technology/2013/06/how_people_read_online_why_you_won_t_finish_this_article.html" target="_blank">
          People read differently online
         </a>
         . We
         <em>
          scan
         </em>
         pages. In fact, very few of you will read every word of this post. Part of the reason is the sheer volume of content that is published online. The other half is the fact that our new digital lives offer far too many distractions.
        </p>
        <p>
         Brevity is key. Breaking up the text in your posts makes your content more consumable and easier to read. Break up the text on your page and be sure to ditch publishing big blocks of text.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #11 Publish long form content
        </h3>
        <p>
         Content over 1,000 words performs better in search and gives you ample room to naturally addkeywords throughout the page.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Ideal length of a blog post" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1346" height="368" src="http://blog.rankpay.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/ideal-length-of-a-blog-post.jpg" width="601"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         SerpIQ analyzed
         <a href="http://blog.serpiq.com/how-important-is-content-length-why-data-driven-seo-trumps-guru-opinions/" target="_blank">
          the top 10 positions
         </a>
         on search results pages and counted the words in each article. Their data included all text on the page (including sidebars and comments), so thats something to keep in mind. As you can see, the higher the word count, the higher the rankings.
        </p>
        <p>
         What it really comes down to is effort. Good content gets links. Great content gets shared.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #12 Link to other posts and pages
        </h3>
        <p>
         Link internally within your site to important posts and pages. This keeps Googlebot on your site longer, which means more pages get crawled on your site. It also helps improve visitor time on site  meaning more opportunities to convert traffic into conversions.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #13 Improve pagespeed
        </h3>
        <p>
         A fast website is one of the quickest (no pun intended) paths towards better conversions. Google measures bounce rate and
         <a href="http://www.wordstream.com/blog/ws/2014/06/10/dwell-time" target="_blank">
          dwell time
         </a>
         . A slow website impacts the user experience, and therefore can ding you in the SEO department. The speed of your server is just one more factor that plays into good on-page SEO. A fast website is a must-have.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          There are a number of things you can do to speed up your website.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Minimize HTTP Requests
         </li>
         <li>
          Reduce server response time
         </li>
         <li>
          Enable compression
         </li>
         <li>
          Enable browser caching
         </li>
         <li>
          Minify resources
         </li>
         <li>
          Optimize images
         </li>
         <li>
          Optimize CSS Deliver
         </li>
         <li>
          Prioritize above-the-fold content
         </li>
         <li>
          Reduce the number of plugins you use on your site
         </li>
         <li>
          Reduce redirects
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Get more info on speeding up your website
         <a href="http://blog.crazyegg.com/2013/12/11/speed-up-your-website/" target="_blank">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h3>
         #14 Add social sharing buttons
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.rankpay.com/social-seo" target="_blank" title="Social SEO">
          Social SEO
         </a>
         is here to stay. Give your visitors the opportunity to amplify your message by adding social sharing buttons throughout your site. Looking for a solution? Check out
         <a href="http://sumome.com/" target="_blank">
          Sumo.me
         </a>
         . You can grab a free social sharing widget, just like the one were using right here on the site.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #15 Maximize CTR
        </h3>
        <p>
         If your search results listing isnt getting clicked on it sends a strong signal to Google that visitors are not finding your content useful.
        </p>
        <p>
         The opposite is also true: more clicks, more relevancy. Google will usually give you a bump in search results. You can maximize your CTR by writing extremely compelling copy that is geared towards making people click.
        </p>
        <p>
         Hint: Check out Adwords listings under the keyword you are targeting. You should be able to come up with some ideas from the ads. Ads that are higher up on the page have typically proven to have good click-through.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #16Use rich snippets, schema, and microdata
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="Using snippets for on-page SEO" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1378" height="237" src="http://blog.rankpay.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/snippets-on-page-seo.png" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The short version is that adding markup to your web pages gives you more visibility in search and helps improve your on-page SEO.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Heres what Google has to say:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          Including structured data markup in web content helps Google algorithms better index and understand the content. Some data can also be used to create and display Rich Snippets within the search results. For example, the Rich Snippet at the right shows search results for a movie, including review stars, an aggregate rating value, and vote count  very useful to anyone searching for information about this movie.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Check out
         <a href="http://www.vervesearch.com/blog/a-guide-to-rich-snippets-schema-and-microdata/" target="_blank">
          this resource
         </a>
         for a more complete breakdown of rich snippets, schema, and microdata.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #17 Make fonts easy to read
        </h3>
        <p>
         Hard to read fonts make it hard on visitors. If people have trouble reading your content, its almost a sure bet they will click the back button. Use easy to read fonts and make sure that your font type is not too small. Avoid typefaces that are too complicated or dont display well on the web.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #18 Optimize clicks to content
        </h3>
        <p>
         The closer a page is to your home page the better it will rank. You have to ask yourself this one important question:
        </p>
        <p>
         What page do you
         <strong>
          need
         </strong>
         visitors to reach?
        </p>
        <p>
         Make it no more than 3 clicks from the home page. Youll get better rankings and conversion.
         <a href="http://blog.rankpay.com/what-is-pagerank-and-why-does-it-matter/" title="What is PageRank and Why Does It Matter?">
          PageRank
         </a>
         will flow easier to these pages as well.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #19 Reduce bounce rate
        </h3>
        <p>
         All you have is 3 seconds.
         <strong>
          Make it count.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         What you want your visitors to do should be immediately obvious within 3 seconds of landing on your site. Check Google Analytics and see what your bounce rate is, from there work to reduce the number by testing different value propositions and calls-to-action.
        </p>
        <h3>
         #20 Make your site mobile-friendly
        </h3>
        <p>
         Googles mobile update means sites that are not
         <a href="http://blog.rankpay.com/google-mobile-update/" title="Google Mobile Update: You Need A Mobile Website (Like Right Now)">
          mobile-friendly
         </a>
         will have a harder time showing up in mobile search. This doesnt affect desktop search results, but if you get heavy mobile traffic your site will definitely see a dip in traffic. Regardless, moving to a responsive website should be top priority.
