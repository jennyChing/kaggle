       <div class="post_content clearfix">
        <h1 class="title">
         Sketchy Saturday Top 10- No.001
        </h1>
        <div class="wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertRight" style="float: right;  padding: 5px; margin:5px; float:left;
">
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- In article larger -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle middlead" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7126272587241902" data-ad-slot="3258684833" style="display:inline-block;width: 100%;height:100%; ">
         </ins>
         <script>
          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
        </div>
        <div id="media-pass-tease" style="display:none;">
         This Top Ten showcases the most beautiful, intriguing and inventive sketches by our some of 
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          This Top Ten showcases the most beautiful, intriguing and inventive sketches by our some of our talented LAN (Landscape Architects Network) readers.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Our
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/drawing-dead/">
          previous article
         </a>
         discussed the pros and cons of digital and hand drawings. Since then, our readers have definitely been busy with their hands for our
         <em>
          <strong>
           Sketchy Saturdays
          </strong>
         </em>
         series. Here is the big top ten:
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           No. 10
          </b>
          by Ira-Anna Antonopoulou and Viki Mpania
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         This sketch is part of a proposal project about Lake Koronia. Since 1996, the area has been under severe environmental stress with the gradual drainage of the lake and destruction of the surrounding ecosystem. Since 2004, the projects have been based on studies assigned to experts for improving the implementation of the agro-environmental program.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_13048" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/10.jpg">
          <img alt="From land_arch_studio in Larissa, Greece, by Anna Antonopoulou and Viki Mpania " class="size-full wp-image-13048 " height="483" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/10.jpg" title="https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151656626796983&amp;set=a.10150709498021983.451817.401951976982&amp;type=3&amp;permPage=1" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          From land_arch_studio in Larissa, Greece, by Anna Antonopoulou and Viki Mpania
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           No. 9
          </b>
          by
          <b>
          </b>
          Cobi van Kollenburg from the Netherlands
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         This drawing is about an existing square in an old city in The Netherlands (Haarlem). The square is full with cars and all kinds of obstructions. Through the drawing I have given the space its identity and intimacy back. In my drawing its a place for the people who live around the square. A place for life: for sitting, relaxing, talking, playing, having a picnic
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_13047" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/9.jpg">
          <img alt="Drawing from Cobi van Kollenburg, The Netherlands" class="size-full wp-image-13047 " height="422" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/9.jpg" title="https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151650572041983&amp;set=a.10150709498021983.451817.401951976982&amp;type=3&amp;permPage=1" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Drawing from Cobi van Kollenburg, The Netherlands
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           No. 8
          </b>
          by Lydia Bradshaw from Australia
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sketching with ink is not only a hobby Ive practiced for the past fifteen years, but above all, a release from my 9-5 job. I love the challenge of drawing outdoors and capturing a moment in time using as much detail as possible. Even though digital art is becoming more and more prominent, I will never escape the satisfaction of bringing a drawing to life on paper.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_13046" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/8.jpg">
          <img alt="Drawing of a park in Mainz, Germany by Lydia Bradshaw from Australia. " class="size-full wp-image-13046 " height="434" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/8.jpg" title="Source: https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151650567791983&amp;set=a.10150709498021983.451817.401951976982&amp;type=3&amp;permPage=1" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Drawing of a park in Mainz, Germany by Lydia Bradshaw from Australia.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           No. 7
          </b>
          by Anne Mnstermann, landscape architect, Germany
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         This sketch is a classic two-dimensional view, getting a three-dimensional effect by overlapping and shading. Views are a great addition to floor plans, if the height situation is not too complex. People, furniture and vehicles in a drawing illustrate dimensions and drawings can appear lively. The view was drawn with fine-liner Staedler and Pentel Sign Pen and then colored with copic markers and crayons.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_13045" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/7.jpg">
          <img alt="Drawing from Anne Mnstermann a landscape architect from Germany who studied in Hxter, Germany." class="size-full wp-image-13045 " height="361" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/7.jpg" title="Source: https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151650562911983&amp;set=a.10150709498021983.451817.401951976982&amp;type=3&amp;permPage=1" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Drawing from Anne Mnstermann a landscape architect from Germany who studied in Hxter, Germany.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           No. 6
          </b>
          by Predrag Kovacevic from Belgrade, Serbia
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its a fictitious project that represents a path of 2 meters and a plateau, area approximately 100m, containing facilities for children and benches. Theyre connected by stairs and paved with quarry stone plates. Surfaces are surrounded with a combination of coniferous, deciduous and evergreen trees and shrubs. This task is supposed to coordinate construction skills with horticultural knowledge.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_13044" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/6.jpg">
          <img alt="Drawing from Predrag Kovacevic,from Belgrade, Serbia, studying landscape architecture at Faculty of Forestry, Belgrade University." class="size-full wp-image-13044 " height="569" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/6.jpg" title="Source: https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151650565466983&amp;set=a.10150709498021983.451817.401951976982&amp;type=3&amp;permPage=1" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Drawing from Predrag Kovacevic,from Belgrade, Serbia, studying landscape architecture at Faculty of Forestry, Belgrade University.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           No. 5
          </b>
          by Preston Montaguea, landscape designer at Stimmel Assoc. in Winston-Salem, NC.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Geyser Garden is a landscape design created for a residence in downtown Raleigh, NC. The client, a geologist who recently moved from Wyoming, wished to celebrate the grandeur of Yellowstone National Park in her tiny urban yard. Heated by a wood-fired stove and employing a system of pumps and ultrasonic foggers on timers, the Geyser Garden suspends reality through a connection to some of natures most colossal forces.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_13043" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/5.jpg">
          <img alt="Drawing from Preston Montaguea a recent graduate of the MLA program at NCSU, who works as a landscape designer at Stimmel Assoc. in Winston-Salem, NC." class="size-full wp-image-13043 " height="776" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/5.jpg" title="Source: https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151650579221983&amp;set=a.10150709498021983.451817.401951976982&amp;type=3&amp;permPage=1" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Drawing from Preston Montaguea a recent graduate of the MLA program at NCSU, who works as a landscape designer at Stimmel Assoc. in Winston-Salem, NC.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           No. 4
          </b>
          by MOX landscape architects from St.Petersburg, Russia
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle" style="margin: 5px; padding: 0px;">

         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <b>
           No. 2
          </b>
          by Bernardro Lee
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         It is a sketch of a HDB (Housing and Development Board) at Ang Mo Kio, Singapore. One afternoon, I was sitting next to my window, looking at the beautiful shadow casted on the building and ground and those swaying trees. I just couldnt stop myself to capture it through drawing. I was trying to capture the relationship between lighting, shadow, architecture, and landscape in this sketch. In my opinion, landscape and architecture are not two separate studies.
        </p>
