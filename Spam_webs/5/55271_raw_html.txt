				&lt;div id="cb-featured-image" class="cb-fis cb-fis-block-standard"&gt;&lt;div class="cb-mask"&gt;&lt;img width="759" height="500" src="http://www.pirancafe.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Sneznik-Castle-759x500.jpg" class="attachment-cb-759-500 wp-post-image" alt="Sneznik Castle, Slovenia" /&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div class="cb-entry-header cb-meta clearfix"&gt;&lt;h1 class="entry-title cb-entry-title cb-title" itemprop="headline"&gt;Snenik Castle, Slovenia (Pic du Jour)&lt;/h1&gt;&lt;div class="cb-byline"&gt;&lt;span class="cb-author"&gt; &lt;a href="http://www.pirancafe.com/author/bob-r/"&gt;Bob R&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="cb-separator"&gt;&lt;i class="fa fa-times"&gt;&lt;/i&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="cb-date"&gt;&lt;time class="updated" datetime="2014-02-22"&gt;February 22, 2014&lt;/time&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div class="cb-post-meta"&gt;&lt;span class="cb-category cb-element"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.pirancafe.com/category/pic-du-jour/" title="View all posts in Pic du Jour"&gt;Pic du Jour&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="cb-category cb-element"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.pirancafe.com/category/europe/slovenia/" title="View all posts in Slovenia"&gt;Slovenia&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="cb-comments cb-element"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.pirancafe.com/2014/02/22/sneznik-castle-slovenia-pic-du-jour/#comments"&gt;2 Comments&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
				&lt;section class="cb-entry-content clearfix" &gt;

					&lt;p&gt;This is Grad Snenik (Snenik Castle) near the village of Kozarie, about 28 km from &lt;a href="http://www.pirancafe.com/2013/10/27/cerknica-lake-horizons/"&gt;Lake Cerknica&lt;/a&gt;,Europes largest intermittent lake, in south central Slovenia. Its tucked nicely into the southwest corner of the Lo Valley, on the edge of hundreds of square kilometers of beautiful forests and rough forest service roads where one can and where I have, on several occasions! get pleasantly lost.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Its said to date back to the 13th century and was likely built on a site where a Roman stronghold once stood. It was fully renovated in 2008 and is the only castle in Slovenia with surviving authentic interiors.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Its far enough away from main highways and train stations truly off the beaten path that its not very heavily visited. Ive been here many times, in all seasons, weekdays and weekends, and besides one wedding that I nearly crashed, I never shared the castle with more than half a dozen people.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;A little more is &lt;a href="http://www.nms.si/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=325&amp;Itemid=56&amp;lang=en"&gt;here on the national museum website&lt;/a&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;
