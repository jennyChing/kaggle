     <p>
      <b>
       Heij-ky
      </b>
      (, also
      <i>
       Heizei-ky
      </i>
      , sometimes
      <i>
       Nara no miyako
      </i>
      ), was the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">
       capital
      </a>
      city of
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japan
      </a>
      during most of the
      <a href="/wiki/Nara_period" title="Nara period">
       Nara period
      </a>
      , from 71040 and again from 74584. The
      <a href="/wiki/Heij%C5%8D_Palace" title="Heij Palace">
       imperial palace
      </a>
      is a listed
      <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">
       UNESCO World Heritage
      </a>
      together with other places in the city of
      <a href="/wiki/Nara,_Nara" title="Nara, Nara">
       Nara
      </a>
      (cf.
      <a href="/wiki/Historic_Monuments_of_Ancient_Nara" title="Historic Monuments of Ancient Nara">
       Historic Monuments of Ancient Nara
      </a>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empress_Gemmei" title="Empress Gemmei">
       Empress Gemmei
      </a>
      ordered the Imperial capital moved from Fujiwara-ky to Heij-ky in 708, and the move to Heij-ky was complete in 710. Heij-ky was modeled after
      <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">
       Chang'an
      </a>
      , the capital of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">
       Tang Dynasty
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
       China
      </a>
      , although Heij-ky lacked walls. In the city, merchants and traders from China, Korea and India introduced various foreign cultures to Heij-ky through the Silk Road. As a result, Heij-ky flourished as Japan's first international and political capital, with a peak population of approximately 100,000. The overall form of the city was an irregular rectangle, and the area of city is more than 25km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1300th_anniversary">
       1300th anniversary
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heij%C5%8D-ky%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1300th anniversary">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The year 2010 marked the passage of 1300 years since the establishment of Nara Heij-ky. Commemorative events of the 1300th anniversary of Nara Heij-ky Capital (Japanese:1300) were held in and around Nara Prefecture from April 24 to November 7, 2010. These events included special displays of
      <a href="/wiki/National_treasure" title="National treasure">
       national treasures
      </a>
      and other cultural properties, walking events that explore famous places in Nara, and traditional events in various places throughout Nara.
     </p>
