                </sup>

               <li>
                Battles between Mexican and American forces ceased in 1919 after the American/Carrancista victory in the
                <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ciudad_Ju%C3%A1rez_(1919)" title="Battle of Ciudad Jurez (1919)">
                 Battle of Ciudad Jurez
                </a>
                <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
                 <a href="#cite_note-2">
                  <span>
                   [
                  </span>
                  2

               </li>

                  3
                  <span>

                 </a>
                </sup>
               </li>

     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Border War
      </b>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      or the
      <b>
       Border Campaign
      </b>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      refers to the military engagements which took place in the
      <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
       Mexico
      </a>
      -
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican-American_border" title="Mexican-American border">
       border
      </a>
      region of
      <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
       North America
      </a>
      during the
      <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Revolution" title="Mexican Revolution">
       Mexican Revolution
      </a>
      . The
      <a href="/wiki/Bandit_War" title="Bandit War">
       Bandit War
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in Texas was part of the Border War. From the beginning of the Mexican Revolution in 1910, the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">
       United States Army
      </a>
      was stationed in force along the border and on several occasions fought with Mexican rebels or federals. The height of the conflict came in 1916 when revolutionary
      <a href="/wiki/Pancho_Villa" title="Pancho Villa">
       Pancho Villa
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Columbus_(1916)" title="Battle of Columbus (1916)">
       attacked
      </a>
      the American border town of
      <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_New_Mexico" title="Columbus, New Mexico">
       Columbus, New Mexico
      </a>
      . In response, the United States Army, under the direction of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General" title="General">
       General
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/John_J._Pershing" title="John J. Pershing">
       John J. Pershing
      </a>
      , launched an
      <a href="/wiki/Pancho_Villa_Expedition" title="Pancho Villa Expedition">
       expedition
      </a>
      into northern Mexico, to find and capture Villa. Though the operation was successful in finding and engaging the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Villistas" title="Villistas">
       Villista
      </a>
      rebels, the revolutionary himself escaped and the American army returned to the United States in January 1917. Conflict was not only subject to Villistas and Americans;
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maderistas" title="Maderistas">
        Maderistas
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carrancistas" title="Carrancistas">
        Carrancistas
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constitutionalistas" title="Constitutionalistas">
        Constitutionalistas
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">
       Germans
      </a>
      also engaged in battle with American forces during this period.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1910">
       1910
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Border_War_(1910%E2%80%9319)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1910">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Revolutionary activity breaks out in Mexico.
      </li>
      <li>
       United States Army deploys to several
       <a href="/wiki/Border_town" title="Border town">
        border towns
       </a>
       to protect American lives and property and to ensure that fighting between rebel and federal forces remains on the Mexican side of the border.
      </li>
      <li>
       In late 1910,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francisco_Madero" title="Francisco Madero">
        Francisco Madero
       </a>
       issues the
       <a href="/wiki/Plan_of_San_Luis_Potos%C3%AD" title="Plan of San Luis Potos">
        Plan of San Luis Potos
       </a>
       , a proclamation which called for Mexican citizens to rise up against the federal government of Porfirio Daz, in
       <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">
        San Antonio
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
        Texas
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       On 20 November, Madero planned to attack the border town of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ciudad_Porfirio_Diaz" title="Ciudad Porfirio Diaz">
        Ciudad Porfirio Diaz
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Coahuila" title="Coahuila">
        Coahuila
       </a>
       , across the border from
       <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Pass,_Texas" title="Eagle Pass, Texas">
        Eagle Pass, Texas
       </a>
       . Due to the lack of reinforcements, Madero canceled the operation and left to
       <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">
        New Orleans
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">
        Louisiana
       </a>
       , to prepare another plan.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1911">
       1911
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Border_War_(1910%E2%80%9319)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1911">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Pofirio Daz pressured the United States government into issuing orders for Madero's arrest. Madero escapes across the border back into Mexico on 14 February.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magonistas" title="Magonistas">
        Magonistas
       </a>
       began campaigning in northern
       <a href="/wiki/Baja_California" title="Baja California">
        Baja California
       </a>
       in February. They
       <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Mexicali" title="Capture of Mexicali">
        captured
       </a>
       the Mexican border town of
       <a href="/wiki/Mexicali" title="Mexicali">
        Mexicali
       </a>
       on 11 February and then marched to
       <a href="/wiki/Tijuana" title="Tijuana">
        Tijuana
       </a>
       where they
       <a href="/wiki/First_Battle_of_Tijuana" title="First Battle of Tijuana">
        defeated
       </a>
       the federal
       <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">
        garrison
       </a>
       . The Mexican government retaliated and attacked Tijuana in June, forcing the rebels to cross the border and surrender to the United States Army at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Ysidro,_California" title="San Ysidro, California">
        San Ysidro, California
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       In March, Francisco Madero led 130 men at the
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Casas_Grandes" title="Battle of Casas Grandes">
        Battle of Casas Grandes
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Chihuahua_(state)" title="Chihuahua (state)">
        Chihuahua
       </a>
       . The rebels lost the battle, but later the federals retreated which left Madero's army in control. Madero then began smuggling arms and ammunition on a large scale from across the border.
      </li>
      <li>
       On 16 March, rebel saboteurs in Ciudad Jurez bombed the barracks and homes of the Mexican Army garrison. A large nitroglycerin explosion was seen on the American side of the border. Two days later, a large cannon which sat in the town square of El Paso, Texas, disappeared and was presumably taken to Ciudad Jurez.
      </li>
      <li>
       Maderista rebels fought federal troops loyal to Porfirio Daz at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agua_Prieta,_Sonora" title="Agua Prieta, Sonora">
        Agua Prieta, Sonora
       </a>
       , in April. United States troops across the border in
       <a href="/wiki/Douglas,_Arizona" title="Douglas, Arizona">
        Douglas, Arizona
       </a>
       , were attacked by Mexican forces and in response the Americans intervened which left the rebels in control of the town.
      </li>
      <li>
       Madero's rebels under Pancho Villa and
       <a href="/wiki/Pascual_Orozco" title="Pascual Orozco">
        Pascual Orozco
       </a>
       attacked federal forces at the major Second
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ciudad_Juarez" title="Battle of Ciudad Juarez">
        Battle of Ciudad Juarez
       </a>
       from 7 April-10 May. The American garrison of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Paso" title="El Paso">
        El Paso
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
        Texas
       </a>
       , exchanged fire with rebels resulting in minor casualties on both sides.
      </li>
      <li>
       Porfirio Daz exiled. Francisco Madero becomes President of Mexico and calls for an end to warfare in the country. He offered to pay rebels of different factions but only if they would lay down their arms or join his new federal Army.
      </li>
      <li>
       Fighting breaks out between rebel factions.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>

     <h3>

       1913

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [

       </a>

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Nogales,_Sonora" title="Nogales, Sonora">

       </a>
       , was
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nogales_(1913)" title="Battle of Nogales (1913)">
        attacked
       </a>
       by General Obregn's army of over 2,000 Constitutionalistas in 1913. Defending federal forces under General
       <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Kosterlitzky" title="Emilio Kosterlitzky">
        Emilio Kosterlitzky
       </a>
       collapsed and surrendered to the United States Army garrison of
       <a href="/wiki/Nogales,_Arizona" title="Nogales, Arizona">
        Nogales, Arizona
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Naco" title="Battle of Naco">

       is fought. lvaro Obregn's rebel army defeated the federal Mexican border town garrison of
       <a href="/wiki/Naco,_Sonora" title="Naco, Sonora">
        Naco, Sonora
       </a>

      </li>
      <li>

       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Naco" title="Fort Naco">
        Fort Naco
       </a>
       , one of 12
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort" title="Fort">
        forts
       </a>

      </li>
      <li>
       General John Pershing and Pancho Villa meet at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Bliss,_Texas" title="Fort Bliss, Texas">

       , and would meet again later in 1914 at
       <a href="/wiki/Ojinaga" title="Ojinaga">
        Ojinaga
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Chihuahua_(state)" title="Chihuahua (state)">
        Chihuahua
       </a>
       .
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1914">
       1914
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Border_War_(1910%E2%80%9319)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1914">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       On 9 April, the
       <a href="/wiki/Tampico_Affair" title="Tampico Affair">
        Tampico Affair
       </a>
       , an incident in
       <a href="/wiki/Tampico" title="Tampico">
        Tampico
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">
        Tamaulipas
       </a>
       , between United States Navy sailors and Mexican troops, occurred. It resulted in the severing of diplomatic relations between Mexico and the United States.
      </li>
      <li>
       In response to the Tampico Affair,
       <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">
        President
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson" title="Woodrow Wilson">
        Woodrow Wilson
       </a>
       asked Congress to approve an armed invasion of Mexico.
      </li>
      <li>
       Congress approves the invasion. The United States Navy's Atlantic fleet under
       <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">
        Admiral
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Frank_Friday_Fletcher" title="Frank Friday Fletcher">
        Frank Fletcher
       </a>
       was sent to the port of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veracruz,_Veracruz" title="Veracruz, Veracruz">
        Veracruz
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_occupation_of_Veracruz" title="United States occupation of Veracruz">
        occupied
       </a>
       the city after an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphibious_assault" title="Amphibious assault">
        amphibious assault
       </a>
       and a street battle with Mexican defenders.
      </li>
      <li>
       The longest battle of the Mexican Revolution was fought at
       <a href="/wiki/Naco,_Sonora" title="Naco, Sonora">
        Naco, Sonora
       </a>
       , across the border from Fort Naco and Naco, Arizona. Pancho Villa's men attacked General Obregn's garrison on 17 October. During the 119 following days of siege warfare Villa was defeated. Also during the battle several United States Army
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buffalo_Soldiers" title="Buffalo Soldiers">
        Buffalo Soldiers
       </a>
       stationed in Naco, Arizona, were wounded by rebels shooting into their camp. Eight men were wounded but they did not return fire and were later recognized for their good discipline.
      </li>
      <li>
       Pancho Villa and
       <a href="/wiki/Emiliano_Zapata" title="Emiliano Zapata">
        Emiliano Zapata
       </a>
       capture Mexico City but soon after are forced to retreat by lvaro Obregn's army.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1915">
       1915
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Border_War_(1910%E2%80%9319)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1915">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <i>
        Carrancistas
       </i>
       draft the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plan_de_San_Diego" title="Plan de San Diego">
        Plan de San Diego
       </a>
       , an operation to overthrow the state governments of Texas, New Mexico, Arizona, and California by starting a
       <a href="/wiki/Race_war" title="Race war">
        race war
       </a>
       . The plan was discovered by the Americans after a
       <i>
        Carrancista
       </i>
       leader was arrested in Texas though some fighting did occur in the form of raids, launched by rebels into Texas territory.
      </li>
      <li>
       Pancho Villa attacks General Obregn's Constitutionalist garrison at Nogales, Sonora. Villa initiates a siege but over time is defeated due to the lack of
       <a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">
        artillery
       </a>
       and insufficient supplies. During the siege the United States
       <a href="/wiki/12th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="12th Infantry Regiment (United States)">
        12th Infantry
       </a>
       garrison of Nogales, Arizona was attacked by
       <i>
        Villistas
       </i>
       and in turn skirmished for a half hour. Only one American was killed along with several of Villa's rebels.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Villistas
       </i>
       and Constitutionalists
       <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Agua_Prieta" title="Second Battle of Agua Prieta">
        fought
       </a>
       again at Agua Prieta in November. Later, Villa attributed his defeat to large searchlights used during the battle by the United States Army garrison of Douglas, Arizona. The battle ended in defeat for Villa and led to the more disastrous
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Hermosillo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Battle of Hermosillo (page does not exist)">
        Battle of Hermosillo
       </a>
       on 15 November. At this time, Villa's forces pillaged the city instead of fighting the garrison, resulting in a repulse. Also at this time Constitutionalist forces were allowed access to American railways for troop movement.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1916">
       1916
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Border_War_(1910%E2%80%9319)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 1916">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       The January 1916
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=San_Isabel_Massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="San Isabel Massacre (page does not exist)">
        San Isabel Massacre
       </a>
       occurred. Villistas stopped a train near
       <a href="/wiki/Santa_Isabel,_Chihuahua" title="Santa Isabel, Chihuahua">
        Santa Isabel, Chihuahua
       </a>
       , and killed eighteen American passengers from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ASARCO" title="ASARCO">
        ASARCO
       </a>
       company of
       <a href="/wiki/Tucson,_Arizona" title="Tucson, Arizona">
        Tucson, Arizona
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       Now losing the war, Pancho Villa decided to
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Columbus_(1916)" title="Battle of Columbus (1916)">
        raid
       </a>
       Columbus, New Mexico, for supplies on 9 March 1916. The raid did not go as planned and Villa's 500 cavalrymen were defeated by over 300 United States infantry and cavalry who were stationed in a border fort outside of town. Columbus was heavily damaged by the
       <i>
        Villistas
       </i>
       who burned several of the town's buildings. Sixty to eighty
       <i>
        Villistas
       </i>
       were killed along with over a dozen American troops and civilians.
      </li>
      <li>
       In response to the attack on Columbus, President Wilson ordered General John J. Pershing to proceed into Mexico with over 5,000 men to capture or kill Pancho Villa, thus beginning the
       <a href="/wiki/Pancho_Villa_Expedition" title="Pancho Villa Expedition">
        Pancho Villa Expedition
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       On 5 May, Villa's rebels attacked two more American border towns,
       <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Springs,_Texas" title="Glenn Springs, Texas">
        Glenn Springs, Texas
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Boquillas,_Texas" title="Boquillas, Texas">
        Boquillas, Texas
       </a>
       . Over 200 men under
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rodriguez_Ramirez&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rodriguez Ramirez (page does not exist)">
        Rodriguez Ramirez
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Natividad_%C3%81lvarez&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Natividad lvarez (page does not exist)">
        Natividad lvarez
       </a>
       crossed the Texas border with the intention of capturing supplies. At Glenn Springs, a United States Army squad of nine men resisted the
       <i>
        Villista
       </i>
       attack for several hours but eventually the raiders set fire to the adobe building the Americans were held up in and forced the
       <a href="/wiki/14th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="14th Infantry Regiment (United States)">
        14th Infantry
       </a>
       men to retreat. Three of the United States troops were killed and four others were wounded. One young American boy was also killed by the Mexicans. At Boquillas, 12mi (19km) from Glenn Springs, the Americans there captured Alvarez and discovered he was a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Colonel" title="Lieutenant Colonel">
        Lieutenant Colonel
       </a>
       in Pancho Villa's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Divisi%C3%B3n_del_Norte" title="Divisin del Norte">
         Divisin del Norte
        </a>
       </i>
       and was a veteran of the
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Celaya" title="Battle of Celaya">
        Battle of Celaya
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       The raiders of Glenn Springs and Boquillas took two captives with them when they withdrew across the border.
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jesse_Deemer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jesse Deemer (page does not exist)">
        Jesse Deemer
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Monroe_Payne&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Monroe Payne (page does not exist)">
        Monroe Payne
       </a>
       who were later rescued by American Army forces during a small cavalry expedition into Mexico. The expedition of eighty men, two wagons and a car began on 8 May from
       <a href="/wiki/Marathon,_Texas" title="Marathon, Texas">
        Marathon, Texas
       </a>
       , and was under the command of
       <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">
        Colonel
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Frederick_W._Sibley" title="Frederick W. Sibley">
        Frederick W. Sibley
       </a>
       and Colonel
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=George_T._Langhorne&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="George T. Langhorne (page does not exist)">
        George T. Langhorne
       </a>
       . The rebels were held up at
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=El_Pino,_Chihuahua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="El Pino, Chihuahua (page does not exist)">
        El Pino, Chihuahua
       </a>
       , and at first Colonel Langhorne negotiated for the release of the two Americans and when this failed he ordered his men to embark his personal car and head for El Pino. Upon their arrival the Villistas fled and Deemer and Payne were freed. During the operation which ended on 21 May, five Mexicans were killed in skirmishes with no American losses.
      </li>
      <li>
       President Wilson orders the
       <a href="/wiki/National_Guard_of_the_United_States" title="National Guard of the United States">
        National Guard
       </a>
       to reinforce the United States Army garrisons at the border line. By August, an estimated 117,000 guardsmen were stationed along the border in Texas, New Mexico, Arizona and California.
      </li>
      <li>
       On 12 April, American forces and Carrancistas fight the
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Parral" title="Battle of Parral">
        Battle of Parral
       </a>
       in Chihuahua. When United States troops under
       <a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">
        Major
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Frank_Tompkins" title="Frank Tompkins">
        Frank Tompkins
       </a>
       attempted to leave the city of
       <a href="/wiki/Parral,_Chihuahua" title="Parral, Chihuahua">
        Parral
       </a>
       , they were attacked by
       <i>
        Carrancista
       </i>
       riflemen. The Americans returned fire, and over the course of several hours 45 Mexicans lay dead along with two Americans. The engagement marked the furthest penetration into northern Mexico by American forces, Parral is over 500miles from the border.
      </li>
      <li>
       In June, Buffalo Soldiers of the
       <a href="/wiki/10th_Cavalry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="10th Cavalry Regiment (United States)">
        10th Cavalry
       </a>
       suffered a defeat at the
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Carrizal" title="Battle of Carrizal">
        Battle of Carrizal
       </a>
       . Federal Mexican troops attacked 100 cavalrymen when they attempted to enter the town of
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carrizal,_Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carrizal, Mexico (page does not exist)">
        Carrizal
       </a>
       . The most famous battle of the Border war was fought and ended with the deaths of 45 Mexicans and 16 Americans. Forty-four other Mexicans and Americans were wounded.
      </li>
      <li>
       Raids on American border towns continued during and for years after the Pancho Villa Expedition. On 15 June, raiders killed four American soldiers at
       <a href="/wiki/San_Ygnacio,_Texas" title="San Ygnacio, Texas">
        San Ygnacio, Texas
       </a>
       . On 31 July, another soldier and a United States customs inspector were killed in a second raid. During both engagements, Mexicans were killed or wounded but their casualties are not known.
      </li>
      <li>
       Future General
       <a href="/wiki/George_S._Patton" title="George S. Patton">
        George S. Patton
       </a>
       of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/8th_Cavalry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="8th Cavalry Regiment (United States)">
        8th Cavalry
       </a>
       conducted America's first assault with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armored_vehicle" title="Armored vehicle">
        armored vehicles
       </a>
       at a ranch near
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=San_Miguelito,_Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="San Miguelito, Mexico (page does not exist)">
        San Miguelito
       </a>
       . Three Mexicans were killed including the
       <i>
        Villista
       </i>
       General
       <a href="/wiki/Julio_C%C3%A1rdenas" title="Julio Crdenas">
        Julio Crdenas
       </a>
       . Patton is said to have carved notches into the pistols he carried, representing the men he killed with them.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1917">
       1917
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Border_War_(1910%E2%80%9319)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 1917">

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Due to the defeat at Carrizal, President Wilson ordered General Pershing to withdraw from Mexico and end the expedition. By January, most of the American expeditionary troops had left Mexican territory and were back at garrison duty along the border.
      </li>
      <li>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Zimmermann_Telegram" title="Zimmermann Telegram">
        Zimmermann Telegram
       </a>
       was intercepted by the
       <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
        British
       </a>
       in 1917. In the telegram the German government formally requested that Mexico join
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">
        World War I
       </a>
       on the side of the
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Powers" title="Central Powers">
        Central Powers
       </a>
       . The Germans asked the Mexicans to attack the
       <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">
        southwestern United States
       </a>
       and promised to return land to Mexico that was lost to the United States during the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican-American_War" title="Mexican-American War">
        Mexican-American War
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Gadsden_Purchase" title="Gadsden Purchase">
        Gadsden Purchase
       </a>
       . Mexico ignored the request, but the United States did not and used the affair as a reason to declare war on Germany. So began the
       <a href="/wiki/American_entry_into_World_War_I" title="American entry into World War I">
        American entry into World War I
       </a>
       .
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1918">
       1918
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Border_War_(1910%E2%80%9319)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 1918">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       United States Army Intelligence stationed at
       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Huachuca" title="Fort Huachuca">
        Fort Huachuca
       </a>
       , Arizona, detected a German military presence in Sonora and ordered troops to begin surveillance operations to prepare for war with Mexico. Mexican railways, train stations, and other related enterprises were inspected as possible routes for a large-scale American invasion.
      </li>
      <li>
       Revolutionary
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yaqui_people" title="Yaqui people">
        Yaqui
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">
        native Americans
       </a>
       established a base in
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bear_Valley,_Arizona&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bear Valley, Arizona (page does not exist)">
        Bear Valley, Arizona
       </a>
       , to store weapons intended to be smuggled into Mexico. When the base was discovered by the United States Army,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Blondy_Ryder&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Blondy Ryder (page does not exist)">
        Blondy Ryder
       </a>
       of the 10th Cavalry was ordered to evict the rebel Yaquis. On 9 January, Ryder's patrol
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bear_Valley" title="Battle of Bear Valley">
        attacked
       </a>
       the Yaquis in a small half-hour engagement. The camp was destroyed, one Yaqui was killed, and nine others were captured. The United States Cavalry suffered no casualties.
      </li>
      <li>
       In mid-August, Lieutenant Colonel
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frederick_J._Herman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frederick J. Herman (page does not exist)">
        Frederick J. Herman
       </a>
       received an anonymous message from a Mexican revolutionary about a possible attack on
       <a href="/wiki/Nogales,_Arizona" title="Nogales, Arizona">
        Nogales, Arizona
       </a>
       , by Mexican federal soldiers and a group of German military advisers. On 27 August, a Mexican suspected of gun smuggling crossed the border into Nogales, Sonora, followed by a US Customs agent and two US Army troops. A Mexican soldier watched the incident and fired on the American agent. The shot was a miss but hit one of the soldiers, and the other two Americans returned fire and killed the Mexican soldier.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      From there, the incident escalated from a small dispute into the
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ambos_Nogales" title="Battle of Ambos Nogales">
       Battle of Ambos Nogales
      </a>
      . Reinforcements from both sides rushed to the border to fight; men of the
      <a href="/wiki/35th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="35th Infantry Regiment (United States)">
       35th Infantry Regiment
      </a>
      called for aid, and a squadron of 10th Cavalry under Herman responded. When they arrived, they attacked the Mexican positions on top of hills along the other side of the border. The assault was successful and the Mexican troops with their German advisers were defeated. In all, 30-129 Mexicans, two Germans, and seven Americans died in the fighting. After the battle, German military activity in Sonora ceased. The
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ambos_Nogales" title="Battle of Ambos Nogales">
       Battle of Ambos Nogales
      </a>
      was the last major engagement of the Border War.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1919">
       1919
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Border_War_(1910%E2%80%9319)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 1919">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       American and Mexican forces skirmished near
       <a href="/wiki/El_Paso,_Texas" title="El Paso, Texas">
        El Paso, Texas
       </a>
       , on the border on 16 June in what was known as the
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ciudad_Ju%C3%A1rez_(1919)" title="Battle of Ciudad Jurez (1919)">
        Battle of Ciudad Jurez
       </a>
       . This conflict is singular for the fact that the Mexican army and the American army joined forces to fight the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Villistas" title="Villistas">
        Villistas
       </a>
       led by
       <a href="/wiki/Pancho_Villa" title="Pancho Villa">
        Pancho Villa
       </a>
       . It was the second-largest battle of the Mexican Revolution involving the United States, and is considered the last battle of the Border War.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         7
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
