          <h1 class="publication__title" itemprop="headline">
           8 things you can do on campus to boost your CV
          </h1>
          <p class="publication__standout" itemprop="description">
           Apply now and get ahead of the game next term -  boosting your CV whilst on campus makes perfect sense.
          </p>
          <p class="support">
           <span itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
            <a data-spevent="editorial|click|/uk/author/robin-edds" href="/uk/author/robin-edds" itemprop="url" rel="author">

         <div itemprop="text">
          <div class="content-block text-block">
           <p>
            What better place to get valuable work experience than on campus?
           </p>
           <p>
            The
            <a href="http://www.studentbeans.com/national/jobs-for-students/internship-windows-uk-student-ambassador.html" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Windows UK Brand Ambassador programme
             </strong>
            </a>
            enables you to do just that  - focus on your degree PLUS get experience that will set you apart and help you get a fantastic job when you graduate.
           </p>
           <p>
            Theyre looking for students to become the go-to people on Campus for help and information about Windows 8. Its a flexible role, that you can work round your lectures and you DONT need to be technical - its more about being pro-active, personable and having a passion for the brand.
           </p>
           <p>
            There are a huge variety of jobs at a big company like Microsoft, and this is a great way of getting yourself noticed by them - lots of previous ambassadors have gone on to internships there.
           </p>
           <p>
            Boosting your CV on campus is a great idea because youre there so much anyway. Read on for some other options.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.studentbeans.com/national/jobs-for-students/internship-windows-uk-student-ambassador.html" target="_blank">
              Apply to become a Windows UK Student Ambassador for next term, now!
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            (Closes July 12th 2013)
           </p>
           <h2>
            1. Student politics
           </h2>
           <p>
            For a few weeks every year campuses up and down the country are invaded with thousands of posters, detailing exactly why you should vote for various members of the student body.
           </p>
           <p>
            Some want to be president of their SU, some want to be social sec, others were dared to run thanks to some sort of drunken wager.
           </p>
           <p>
            But there is more to student politics than seeing your face plastered onto a toilet door. The student union is the perfect place to prove to future employers that you can handle pressure 
and responsibility.
           </p>
           <p>
            The fact that you can make all your friends wear T-shirts with your face on is just a bonus.
           </p>
           <h2>
            2. Windows UK Student Ambassador
           </h2>
           <p>
            This is the perfect opportunity for students who want to get their feet on the first rung of the employment ladder - Microsoft are looking for
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.studentbeans.com/national/jobs-for-students/internship-windows-uk-student-ambassador.html" target="_blank">
              150 Student Ambassadors
             </a>
            </strong>
            who will be responsible for spreading the word of Windows 8.
           </p>
           <p>
            You'll set up partnerships between Windows and local student bodies, as well as helping out with wider marketing campaigns.
           </p>
           <p>
            The role will only demand  around 6-10 hours of your time per month, and seeing as there are two positions per university you can even apply with a friend.
           </p>
           <p>
            If you're proactive, chatty, personable, and want to make your CV shine, click the following link and apply today...
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.studentbeans.com/national/jobs-for-students/internship-windows-uk-student-ambassador.html" target="_blank">
              Apply to become a Windows UK Student Ambassador for next term, now!
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            (Closes July 12th 2013)
           </p>
           <h2>
            3. Course rep
           </h2>
           <p>
            Most courses - particularly the larger ones - require at least one student to sit on a student/teacher committee. You pass on your peers issues to the lecturers and vice versa.
           </p>
           <p>
            This is perfect for demonstrating that you have good communication skills - and trust us when we say that becoming chummy with the staff is not the worst thing you can do!
           </p>
           <h2>
            4. Start a society
           </h2>
           <p>
            Have you got an interest that you dont feel is catered for? Perhaps you think your uni lacks a Cat GIF society? Well rather than moaning, do something about it.
           </p>
           <p>
            Speak to your student union. Drum up popular support. Bribe your friends. Do everything you have to do to make it happen.
           </p>
           <p>
            This will prove that you are willing to put in the hard work and take the initiative - these are qualities that every employer looks for when hiring a graduate.
           </p>
           <h2>
            5. Campus tour guide
           </h2>
           <p>
            Universities hold campus tours almost all year round, and the prospective students are going to need someone to show them the sights.
           </p>
           <p>
            Which bars to drink in. Which corner of the library is best if you want to nap without getting disturbed. Which bench you slept on in Freshers Week. That sort of thing.
           </p>
           <p>
            But the fact that youre willing to take time out of your uni life to help others wont go unnoticed when it comes to looking for jobs.
           </p>
           <h2>
            6. Student media
           </h2>
           <p>
            For anyone with the intention of going into the media world after uni, campus radio and newspapers are the ideal proving ground.
           </p>
           <p>
            You get practical experience and learn the techniques and skills that are invaluable when trying to get the attention of employers.
           </p>
           <p>
            There are significantly more journalism graduates than there are entry-level jobs, but by working for a student newspaper or radio station - particularly if its in a position of power - you can ensure that your CV will be placed on the top of any pile.
           </p>
           <h2>
            7. Nightline volunteer
           </h2>
           <p>
            Nightline provides emotional support to students in distress with phones manned throughout the night during term time when other specialist university welfare services are closed. This much you may have known already.
           </p>
           <p>
            What you probably didnt know is that anyone can become a Nightline volunteer, and they are always looking for students to help out.
           </p>
           <p>
            You will receive all necessary training, and again it is perfect for displaying your communication skills, as well as showing the you are a genuinely kind and helpful person.
           </p>
           <h2>
            8. Club rep
           </h2>
           <p>
            Unless you suffer from papyrophobia (the fear of paper) anyone can give out flyers, but if you  work hard and go the extra mile you can really reap the rewards.
           </p>
           <p>
            Anyone who wants to work in events, PR, marketing or sales should seriously consider becoming a club rep.
           </p>
           <p>
            It gives you practical experience of persuading people to do what you want them to, which is something we at studentbeans.com look for in all our employees!
           </p>
           <h2>
            9. Student mentor
           </h2>
           <p>
            If you are a returning undergrad or a postgrad student then you will usually have the option of applying to be a student mentor.
           </p>
           <p>
            If you take up this role, you will be assigned a group of first year students, and its your job to try to help them during their often daunting transition from school to uni.
           </p>
           <p>
            Perhaps theyve moved 50 miles from home and dont know how to cook, maybe theyve travelled 10,000 miles and are suffering from a bit of a culture shock - its up to you to make university a happy place for them to be.
           </p>

              Apply to become a Windows UK Student Ambassador for next term, now!
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
