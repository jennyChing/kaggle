         <a href="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-5aW.jpg">
          <img alt="Honey Sriracha Pulled Chicken and Waffles with Buttermilk Ranch Dressing" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10378" height="1024" src="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-5aW-683x1024.jpg" width="683"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here is my total twist on the classic chicken and waffles. Tender chicken slow cooked with honey and Sriracha, then shredded and served on buttery buttermilk waffles with a Buttermilk Ranch dressing. A dish foodie dreams are made of.
        </p>
        <p>
         I got a new toy to make this dish!Picked up the cheapest little waffle iron I could find, specifically to make this recipe idea become Saturday dinner reality. My daughter immediatelyasked to have another batch of these waffles for breakfast the next day OK Peanut, twist my arm and I shared with you the glorious
         <a href="http://ericasrecipes.com/2015/02/sunday-morning-buttermilk-waffles/" title="Sunday Morning Buttermilk Waffles">
          Sunday Morning Buttermilk Waffles
         </a>
         that resulted.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-1aW.jpg">
          <img alt="Honey Sriracha Pulled Chicken and Waffles with Buttermilk Ranch Dressing" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10374" height="1024" src="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-1aW-683x1024.jpg" width="683"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         But that was just the beginning. This recipe here was my goal.
        </p>
        <p>
         So there is a hella-lot going on here, but it is easy. Just trust. The chicken is super simple  toss in the slow cooker, then shred it. The dressing is easy  just whisk the ingredients together. And the waffles, as I learned, are very easy too  just make a quick batter and make them happen.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-8aW.jpg">
          <img alt="Honey Sriracha Pulled Chicken and Waffles with Buttermilk Ranch Dressing" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10380" height="1024" src="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-8aW-767x1024.jpg" width="767"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         So the recipes here for the Buttermilk Ranch dressing and the Buttermilk Waffles could both be used erywhere else. On their own, they are amazing. The dressing would be great on a salad (no way, right? just thought I should still point that out). Or today Im having some with chicken and arugula in my lunch wrap. All kinds of possibilities. And the waffles are soooodelicious too. When I made them for breakfast, I added a little sugar and vanilla to the batter for sweet vice savory, so that
         <a href="http://ericasrecipes.com/2015/02/sunday-morning-buttermilk-waffles/" title="Sunday Morning Buttermilk Waffles">
          recipe
         </a>
         is just a little different. Both are amazing  I think adding butter rather than vegetable oil makes all the difference.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-2aW.jpg">
          <img alt="Honey Sriracha Pulled Chicken and Waffles with Buttermilk Ranch Dressing" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10375" height="1024" src="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-2aW-861x1024.jpg" width="861"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The chicken is crazy easy and crazy tasty. The idea there came from my grocery store. Before the Superbowl, my store had samples of wings grilled with a honey-Sriracha sauce. That planted the seed. How I went from grilled wings to pulled chicken and waffles I cant tell you. But honestly, I am giving myself some pretty major props that I did.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-3aW.jpg">
          <img alt="Honey Sriracha Pulled Chicken and Waffles with Buttermilk Ranch Dressing" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10376" height="1024" src="http://ericasrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Honey-Sriracha-Pulled-Chicken-and-Waffles-with-Buttermilk-Ranch-Dressing-3aW-683x1024.jpg" width="683"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now eating all this awesomeness? I ate the dish in the photos. With a knife and fork. My husband folded his up all sammie-like. Both methods are perfectly acceptable to get your chickn n waffles on with this delicious recipeeee!
        </p>

          Honey Sriracha Pulled Chicken and Waffles with Buttermilk Ranch Dressing
         </div>
         <div class="blog-yumprint-recipe-published" itemprop="datePublished">
          2015-02-07 19:51:06
         </div>
         <div class="blog-yumprint-header">
          <div class="blog-yumprint-save blog-yumprint-action">

            Save Recipe
           </a>

          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="blog-yumprint-spacer">

         <div class="blog-yumprint-recipe-contents">
          <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="0">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            For the Honey Sriracha Pulled Chicken
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             2 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             2 T Sriracha hot sauce
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             2 T honey
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
             1 t light soy sauce
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
             1 clove garlic, minced
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
             kosher salt
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="6">
             fresh cracked pepper
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="1">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            For the Buttermilk Waffles
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             2 eggs
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             1-3/4 c buttermilk
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             1-3/4 c all-purpose flour
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
             1 t baking soda
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
             1-1/2 t baking powder
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
             1/2 t kosher salt
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="6">
             4 T unsalted butter, melted
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="2">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            For the Buttermilk Ranch
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             1/2 c plain Greek yogurt
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             1/2 c light buttermilk (plus more to adjust consistency)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             1/4 c light mayonnaise (I use olive oil mayo)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
             1 clove garlic, minced
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
             pinch kosher salt
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
             pinch fresh cracked pepper
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="6">
             1 t dried dill
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="7">
             2 T fresh flat-leaf parsley, minced (plus additional for sprinkling as garnish)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="8">
             2 t fresh lemon juice
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="3">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Instructions
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
             To prepare the chicken, season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper add to the crockpot. Whisk together the honey, Sriracha, and soy sauce and pour over the chicken. Cook on low 8-10 hours. Shred the meatusing two forks. Set aside.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">
             To make the Buttermilk Ranch, simply combine the listed ingredients. Store in the refrigerator until use.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">
             To make the Buttermilk Waffles, whisk together the ingredients listed in the order given. Cook to golden brown in the waffle iron. Waffles can be put on a rack on a cookie sheet and kept warm in a 200F oven while the rest of the batch is cooked. In my waffle iron (which is smallish), I get 8 waffles using 1/2 c batter for each.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">
             Serve the waffles topped with pulled chicken and Buttermilk Ranch. Sprinkle fresh parsley over top. Yield: 4 servings.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-note-section" yumprintsection="4">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Notes
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-notes">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="0">
             The Buttermilk Ranch dressing can be made a day ahead. Also, my ranch recipe here is on the thick side - you can always add a bit more buttermilk to thin it if desired.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="1">
             If you transfer the shredded chicken from the crockpot to a bowl or something, be sure to add plenty of sauce from the crockpot to keep the meat moist.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
