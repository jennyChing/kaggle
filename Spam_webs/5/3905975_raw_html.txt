       <h1>
        Vaccines Do Not Cause Autism
       </h1>
       <p>
        <a href="/ncbddd/autism/facts.html">
         <em>
          Autism spectrum disorder  (ASD)
         </em>
        </a>
        <em>
         is a  developmental disability that is caused by differences in how the brain  functions. People with ASD may communicate, interact, behave, and learn in  different ways. Recent estimates  from
         <a href="/ncbddd/autism/addm.html">
          CDC's Autism and Developmental  Disabilities Monitoring Network
         </a>
         found that about 1 in  68 children have been identified with ASD in communities across the United  States. CDC  is committed to providing essential data on ASD, searching for causes of and  factors that increase the risk for ASD, and developing resources that help  identify children with ASD as early as possible.
        </em>
       </p>
       <h3>
        There is no link between vaccines and autism.
       </h3>
       <img alt="Group of kids" border="1 px" class="righty" height="152" src="/vaccinesafety/images/kids.jpg" width="200"/>
       <p>
        Some people have had concerns  that ASD might be linked to the vaccines children receive, but studies have  shown that there is no link between receiving vaccines and developing ASD. In  2011, an Institute of Medicine (IOM)
        <a href="http://www.iom.edu/Reports/2011/Adverse-Effects-of-Vaccines-Evidence-and-Causality.aspx">
         report
        </a>
        on eight vaccines given to children and adults found  that with rare exceptions, these vaccines are very safe.
       </p>
       <p>
        A
        <a href="http://www.jpeds.com/article/S0022-3476(13)00144-3/pdf">
         2013 CDC study
        </a>
        added to the research showing that vaccines do not cause ASD. The  study looked at the number of antigens (substances in vaccines that cause the  bodys immune system to produce disease-fighting antibodies) from vaccines  during the first two years of life. The results showed that the total amount of  antigen from vaccines received was the same between children with ASD and those  that did not have ASD.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Vaccine ingredients do not cause autism.
       </h3>
       <p>
        One vaccine ingredient  that has been studied specifically is
        <a href="/vaccinesafety/Concerns/thimerosal/thimerosal_faqs.html">
         thimerosal
        </a>
        , a mercury-based preservative used to prevent contamination of multidose vials of vaccines.  Research shows that thimerosal does not cause ASD. In fact, a 2004
        <a href="http://www.iom.edu/Reports/2004/Immunization-Safety-Review-Vaccines-and-Autism.aspx">
         scientific review
        </a>
        by the IOM concluded that "the  evidence favors rejection of a causal relationship between  thimerosalcontaining vaccines and autism." Since 2003, there have been
        <a href="/vaccinesafety/00_pdf/CDCStudiesonVaccinesandAutism.pdf">
         nine CDC-funded or conducted studies [PDF - 316 KB]
        </a>
        that have found no link between  thimerosal-containing vaccines and ASD, as well as no link between the measles,  mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine and ASD in children.
       </p>
       <p>
        Between 1999 and 2001, thimerosal was  removed or reduced to trace amounts in all childhood vaccines except for some  flu vaccines. This was done as part of a broader national effort to reduce all  types of mercury exposure in children before studies were conducted that determined  that thimerosal was not harmful. It was  done as a precaution. Currently, the only childhood vaccines that contain  thimerosal are flu vaccines packaged in multidose vials. Thimerosal-free  alternatives are also available for flu vaccine. For more information, see the
        <a href="/vaccinesafety/Concerns/thimerosal/thimerosal_timeline.html">
         Timeline for Thimerosal in Vaccines.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Besides thimerosal, some people have had  concerns about other
        <a href="/vaccines/vac-gen/additives.htm">
         vaccine ingredients
        </a>
        in relation to ASD as well. However, no  links have been found between any vaccine ingredients and ASD.
       </p>
