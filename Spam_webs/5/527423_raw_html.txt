        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Responsive Web Design
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         With the evolution of smartphones and mobile internet websites have gone beyond the traditional boundaries of computers and laptops to explore a whole new world of mobile devices.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          According to an Accenture Report 
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Mobile Internet continues to be on a sharp upward trajectory with a massive 69% of users connected to the Internet through a mobile device in the past 12 month.
          <br/>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         This necessitates making browsing a hassle free experience for users who are constantly searching for your goods and services on their mobile devices. To make this possible technology gave birth to what today is known as a Responsive Website. At Netgains America we are pros at this.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What is a Responsive Website?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="middle" alt="Responsive Website" border="0" height="250" src="http://www.netgainsamerica.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Responsive-Website6.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         A Responsive Website is site which has a flexible layout that detects the visitors screen size and orientation and changes the layout accordingly. Its a site which is contextual to the size of the screen so that a user doesnt have to scour the length of the website to find what he is looking for.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What are the benefits of a Responsive Website?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="left" alt="Responsive Website" border="0" height="150" src="http://www.netgainsamerica.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Responsive-Website1.jpg" style="padding-right: 20px;" width="190"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Optimal Browsing
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         A responsive website would give your users an optimal experience while browsing through your products and services through different mobile devices without any glitches.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="left" alt="Responsive Website" border="0" height="150" src="http://www.netgainsamerica.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Responsive-Website3.jpg" style="padding-right: 20px;" width="190"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          One website to Make, Maintain and Market
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         It can prove quite troublesome and expensive to maintain and market different websites for different platforms. A Responsive website removes you from the trouble of doing this by working on every smartphone and tablet platform.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="left" alt="Responsive Website" border="0" height="150" src="http://www.netgainsamerica.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Responsive-Website2.jpg" style="padding-right: 20px;" width="190"/>
         <strong>
          Tap New Audience
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         With more and more users connected to a mobile device your chances to increase your global clientele are bright. Tap a whole new world of audience by this novel concept of a Responsive Website. Dont build for the present, build for the future NOW! Your business is in the hands of the millions of users with mobile devices.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         To know more about the concept of Responsive Website, take a
         <a href="http://www.netgainsamerica.com/blog/what-are-responsive-websites-a-visual-walkthrough/">
          Responsive Website Visual Walkthrough.
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
