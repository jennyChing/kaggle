             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               Most casual
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/tumblr/">
                Tumblr
               </a>
               users view the site as a veritable goldmine of funny GIFs, memes and pictures of the
               <em>
                Supernatural
               </em>
               cast. What many don't realize is that Tumblr is also home to an extremely talented community of comic artists.
              </p>
              <p>
               Tumblr's hassle-free sharing buttons and customizable templates make it a perfect place to start building a website for creative content like comics, and tons of cartoonists and illustrators have taken note.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2013/11/13/tumblr-subcultures/">
                 The Complete Guide to Tumblr Subcultures
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               "At first, I decided to post my comics on Tumblr simply because it was free,"
               <em>
                <a href="http://tumblr.safelyendangered.com/" target="_blank">
                 Safely Endangered
                </a>
               </em>
               artist Chris McCoy told
               <em>
                Mashable
               </em>
               . "But the kind messages and words of encouragement from users, and even other artists, are why I still post there."
              </p>
              <p>
               If you're looking to browse through some great comics, or start one of your own, look no further than our list of great Tumblrs to follow. From the hilariously absurd to the thoughtfully resonating, these comics are some of the best the web has to offer. The images you see below are just a sampling of each comic  we strongly encourage that you view the blogs individually for more laughs.
              </p>
              <h2>
               1.
               <a href="http://threewordphrase.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                Three Word Phrase
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p>
               <img alt="Three Word Phrase" class="" data-fragment="m!31bb" data-image="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyL2Q3L1RocmVlV29yZFBoLmI0ODNhLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/aa76bcf0/4f2/Three-Word-Phrase.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyL2Q3L1RocmVlV29yZFBoLmI0ODNhLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/aa76bcf0/4f2/Three-Word-Phrase.jpg"/>
               <br/>
               <small>
                Image:
                <a href="http://threewordphrase.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                 Ryan Pequin
                </a>
               </small>
              </p>
              <p>
               Ryan Pequin, a storyboard artist for Cartoon Network's
               <em>
                <a href="http://www.cartoonnetwork.com/tv_shows/regularshow/" target="_blank">
                 Regular Show
                </a>
               </em>
               , created and maintains this hilarious, offbeat Tumblr comic. His site features Tumblr celebrities like President Bird, and will lead new users to other great artists.
              </p>
              <h2>
               2.
               <a href="http://twitterthecomic.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                Twitter: The Comic
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p>
               <img alt="TwitterTheComic" class="" data-fragment="m!deb6" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyL2ZkL1R3aXR0ZXJUaGVDLjYwZGVlLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/924ad90d/a06/TwitterTheComic.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyL2ZkL1R3aXR0ZXJUaGVDLjYwZGVlLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/924ad90d/a06/TwitterTheComic.jpg"/>
               <br/>
               <small>
                Image:
                <a href="http://twitterthecomic.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                 Mike Rosenthal
                </a>
               </small>
              </p>
              <p>
               Twitter: The Comic is the artistic brainchild of comedian Mike Rosenthal. Rosenthal takes his content from the weird side of the Twitter tracks, creating hilarious, panelled illustrations from the tweets.
              </p>
              <h2>
               3.
               <a href="http://forlackofabettercomic.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                For Lack of a Better Comic
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p>
               <img alt="For Lack of a Better Comic" class="" data-fragment="m!87dc" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyLzJjL0ZvckxhY2tvZmFCLmZkZDFhLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/f282ce63/d7b/ForLackofaBetterComic.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyLzJjL0ZvckxhY2tvZmFCLmZkZDFhLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/f282ce63/d7b/ForLackofaBetterComic.jpg"/>
               <br/>
               <small>
                Image:
                <a href="http://forlackofabettercomic.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                 Jacob Andrews
                </a>
               </small>
              </p>
              <p>
               In describing his creative process, creator Jacob Andrews says that he "haphazardly combines words and pictures until something remotely funny happens." Follow his comic and see that he's doing something right.
              </p>
              <h2>
               4.
               <a href="http://zenpencils.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                Zen Pencils
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p>
               <img alt="Zen Pencils" class="" data-fragment="m!e037" data-image="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyLzAyL1plblBlbmNpbHMuZWFhNTEuanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/9359349c/bbe/ZenPencils.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyLzAyL1plblBlbmNpbHMuZWFhNTEuanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/9359349c/bbe/ZenPencils.jpg"/>
               <br/>
               <small>
                Image:
                <a href="http://zenpencils.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                 Gavin Aung Than
                </a>
               </small>
              </p>
              <p>
               Taking famous, inspirational and thought provoking quotes from both history and present day,
               <em>
                Zen Pencils
               </em>
               creator Gavin Aung Than brings the words to life with extraordinary illustrations. The above image is only a small section of one of his comics, click the link to view Than's work in full.
              </p>
              <h2>
               5.
               <a href="http://pdlcomics.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                Poorly Drawn Lines
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p>
               <img alt="Poorly Drawn Lines" class="" data-fragment="m!92c9" data-image="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyLzZjL1Bvb3JseURyYXduLjFjYzdlLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/7b362233/543/Poorly-Drawn-Lines.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyLzZjL1Bvb3JseURyYXduLjFjYzdlLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/7b362233/543/Poorly-Drawn-Lines.jpg"/>
               <br/>
               <small>
                Image:
                <a href="http://pdlcomics.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                 Reza Farazmand
                </a>
               </small>
              </p>
              <p>
               Often featuring the antics of cute animals, naive robots and French conservationist Jacques Cousteau, this comic by Reza Farazmand will hold you captive in front of your laptop for hours.
              </p>
              <h2>
               6.
               <a href="http://tumblr.safelyendangered.com/" target="_blank">
                Safely Endangered
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p>
               <img alt="Safely Endangered" class="" data-fragment="m!a0ef" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyL2Y0L1NhZmVseUVuZGFuLjQ2MWZjLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/d137e36c/a48/Safely-Endangered.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyL2Y0L1NhZmVseUVuZGFuLjQ2MWZjLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/d137e36c/a48/Safely-Endangered.jpg"/>
               <br/>
               <small>
                Image:
                <a href="http://tumblr.safelyendangered.com/" target="_blank">
                 Chris McCoy
                </a>
               </small>
              </p>
              <p>
               Pun-filled, simply illustrated and geniously written, Chris McCoy's
               <em>
                Safely Endangered
               </em>
               is a must follow for all Tumblr users with a sense of humor.
              </p>
              <h2>
               7.
               <a href="http://timetrabble.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                Time Trabble
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p>
               <img alt="Time Trabble" class="" data-fragment="m!c482" data-image="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyLzUxL3RpbWV0cmFiYmxlLjAwZTIzLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/9c31a5d2/a6a/time-trabble.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAyLzUxL3RpbWV0cmFiYmxlLjAwZTIzLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/9c31a5d2/a6a/time-trabble.jpg"/>
               <br/>
               <small>
                Image:
                <a href="http://timetrabble.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                 Michael Heller
                </a>
               </small>
              </p>
              <p>
               Parodying the outer edges of pop culture  think the Cheese Nips/ Cheez-Its war  you'll always be able to count on Michael Heller's comic,
               <em>
                Time Trabble
               </em>
               to deliver darkly hysterical comics to your dashboard.
              </p>
              <h2>
               8.
               <a href="http://eatmorebikes.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                Eat More Bikes
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p>
               <img alt="Eat More Bikes" class="" data-fragment="m!2266" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAzLzgyL2RyYXduZHJhZ29uLjdiNTBiLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/1aab0d52/d52/drawn-dragon.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAzLzgyL2RyYXduZHJhZ29uLjdiNTBiLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/1aab0d52/d52/drawn-dragon.jpg"/>
               <br/>
               <small>
                Image:
                <a href="http://eatmorebikes.tumblr.com/post/60142402883" target="_blank">
                 Nathan Bulmer
                </a>
               </small>
              </p>
              <p>
               This art blog by Nathan Bulmer, who works on Nickelodeon's cartoon
               <em>
                Sanjay and Craig
               </em>
               ,  updates three times a week with irreverent, inventful comics.
              </p>
              <h2>
               9.
               <a href="http://jeremykaye.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                UP and OUT
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p>
               <img alt="Up and Out" class="" data-fragment="m!3692" data-image="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAzLzcyL2plcmVteWtheWUuNzc4MjAuanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/1927f49c/664/jeremy-kaye.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzEyLzAzLzcyL2plcmVteWtheWUuNzc4MjAuanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/1927f49c/664/jeremy-kaye.jpg"/>
               <br/>
               <small>
                Image:
                <a href="http://jeremykaye.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
                 Jeremy Kaye
                </a>
               </small>
              </p>
              <p>
               Jeremy Kaye, creator of
               <em>
                UP and OUT
               </em>
               , describes his comic as being "about how weird everything is." His mixture of cute illustrations with dry, dark humor is perfect for the Tumblr community.
              </p>
