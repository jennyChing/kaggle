      <h2>
       Crochet Pattern: Dmod Summer Cowl
      </h2>
      By Amy 
      <a href="http://www.crochetspot.com/crochet-pattern-demode-summer-cowl/#comments">
       31 Comments
      </a>
      <div class="entry">
       <table>
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td>
           <strong>
            Pattern has been corrected!
           </strong>
           <br/>
           When the weather begins to grow warm you might hesitate to wear some of your crocheted accessories. The answer? Crochet lacier projects and you wont have to sacrifice your style. This cool summer cowl has an antique lace look, hence the French name for old-fashioned. But contrary to some belief, lacy doesnt have to mean more difficult. The stitch pattern is fairly simple, and with a lovely variegated yarn, will present just the right amount of character. Wear it with any summer outfit for the perfect look!
          </td>
          <td width="300">
           <img alt="Dmod Summer Cowl" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-25390" height="300" src="http://www.crochetspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Dmod-Summer-Cowl-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
        <span id="more-25389">
        </span>
       </p>
       <table>
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td>
           <strong>
            Skill Level:
           </strong>
          </td>
          <td width="130">
           <img alt="" src="http://www.crochetspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/02-easysmall.jpg"/>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Finished Size:
        </strong>
        32 (81cm) in circumference, 4 (10cm) wide
       </p>
       <table>
        <tbody>
         <tr>
          <td>
           <strong>
            Materials
           </strong>
           :
           <br/>
           Medium Weight Yarn (approximately 120 yards)
           <br/>
           Crochet hook J (6.00 mm)
          </td>
          <td width="50">
           <img alt="crochet yarn size 4" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-84" height="45" src="http://www.crochetspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/4-mediumsmall.jpg" title="crochet yarn size 4" width="50"/>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Gauge:
        </strong>
        <br/>
        7 dc = 2
        <br/>
        3 rows = 2
        <br/>
        Gauge Swatch: 2w x 2h (5 cm x 5 cm) ch 9.
        <br/>
        Row 1: dc in fourth ch from hook and in each ch across: 7 dc
        <br/>
        Row 2  3: ch 3, turn, dc in each dc across: 7 dc
        <br/>
        Finish off.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Need help understanding the abbreviations and symbols?
        </strong>
        Check out the
        <a href="http://www.crochetspot.com/crochet-abbreviations/">
         crochet abbreviation
        </a>
        chart.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Pattern has been corrected!
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Crochet Pattern: Dmod Summer Cowl
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Round 1: ch 112, sl st in first ch to form ring, ch 1, sc in first ch, *skip 2 ch, (dc, ch 1, dc, ch 1, dc) in next ch, skip 2 ch, sc in next ch, ch 3, sc in next ch, repeat from * until 7 ch remain, skip 2 ch, (dc, ch 1, dc, ch 1, dc) in next ch, skip 2 ch, sc in next ch, ch 3, sl st in first sc: 48 dc
       </p>
       <p>
        Round 2: ch 6 (counts as dc and 3 ch), *skip 1 dc, (sc, ch 3, sc) in next dc, ch 3, dc in next ch-3 space, ch 3, repeat from * until 3 dc remain, skip 1 dc, (sc, ch 3, sc) in next dc, ch 3, sl st in third ch of beginning ch-6: 32 sc
       </p>
       <p>
        Round 3: ch 1, sc in first ch (where last sl st was worked), skip next ch-3 space, *(dc, ch 1, dc, ch 1, dc) in next ch-3 space, skip next ch-3 space, (sc, ch 3, sc) in next dc, repeat from * until 1 ch-3 space remains, sc in same ch as first sc, ch 3, sl st in first sc: 48 dc
       </p>
       <p>
        Round 4  6: repeat Rounds 2 and 3
        <br/>
        Finish off.
       </p>
       <p>
        I really enjoyed the way this cowl pattern turned out. Let me know what you think and if you run into any snags in the comments below!
       </p>
