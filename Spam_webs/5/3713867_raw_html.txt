         <figure class="article-image article-image--landscape">
          <img alt="So Lus, Maranho, Brazil 2007" height="350" rel="image_src" src="http://cdn2-b.examiner.com/sites/default/files/styles/image_content_width/hash/32/bd/32bd50c158cb0453d2634eaffa7fe224.jpg?itok=IrMHo-eq" title="So Lus, Maranho, Brazil 2007" typeof="foaf:Image" width="467"/>
          <figcaption class="caption media__body">
           <div class="caption__title">
            So Lus, Maranho, Brazil 2007
           </div>
           <div class="caption__credit">
            Daniel9d www.9d.com.br, Creative Commons
           </div>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden clearfix">
          <div class="field-items">
           <div class="field-item even" property="schema:articleBody content:encoded">
            <p>
             In the horrifying aftermath of
             <a class="inline_link" href="/topic/events">
              events
             </a>
             where a mob decapitated a referee, a video has hit the Internet showing the mutilated and dismembered corpse of said referee. The gruesome video, which was
             <a href="http://www.liveleak.com/view?i=ab6_1372968263" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              posted to LiveLeak.com
             </a>
             (warning: not safe for work or for the faint of heart) on July 4, is quite graphic as members of a medical team attempt to reassemble the various pieces of the body.
            </p>
            <p>
             As with most things macabre these days, it would seem, the
             <a class="inline_link" href="/topic/decapitated-referee-video">
              decapitated referee video
             </a>
             has gone viral.
            </p>
            <p>
             Most unnerving is the footage that comes just seconds into the video when a white-robed doctor takes the head of the
             <a class="inline_link" href="/topic/decapitated-referee">
              decapitated referee
             </a>
             and places it atop the bodies headless shoulders. A female in a white t-shirt stands at the foot of the gurney with what appears to be two missing pieces of the body's legs.
            </p>
            <p>
             In short, the team are piecing the dismembered corpse back together.
            </p>
            <p>
             The body is reportedly that of Octavio da Silva Catanhede Jordan, a football (soccer) referee from killed during a football game in Sao Luis, the capital of the Brazilian state of Maranho. The unfortunate referee's death was the result of an angry mob that restrained him, lynched him, decapitated him, and quartered him after the 20-year-old allegedly stabbed a player, 31-year-old Josenir dos Santos Abreu, multiple times with a machete-like knife for refusing to leave the football field after the ref red-carded him.
            </p>
            <p>
             Abreu would die shortly thereafter of his stab wounds while en route to hospital.
            </p>
            <p>
             After Abreu was loaded up to be taken away for treatment friends, family, and fans of Abreu descended upon the referee and restrained him.
             <a href="http://au.sports.yahoo.com/news/article/-/17899996/football-match-ends-in-referee-being-decapitated/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              According to Yahoo 7 Sport
             </a>
             , they not only tied him up but tortured him. When the crowd heard that Abreu was dead on arrival at the hospital, they stoned the referee to death.
            </p>
            <p>
             He was then lynched, quartered, and decapitated there on the football field. The referee's head was then placed on a stake and erected in the center of the playing field.
            </p>
            <p>
             The lynching was captured on various cell phones and uploaded to the Internet. Police would later use the videos to make arrests in the killing.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/americas/brazil-referee-decapitated-after-stabbing-player-in-amateur-football-match-8692484.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              The Independent reported
             </a>
             Saturday that one suspect, a 27-year-old man, had been arrested. Two other suspects were still being sought in connection with the slaying.
            </p>
            <p>
             Sao Luis Police chief Valter Costa said: "One crime never justifies another crime. Actions likes this do not collaborate with the legality of state law."
            </p>
           </div>
