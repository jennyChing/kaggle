          <h1>
           Gym Etiquette for Beginners
          </h1>
          <p>
           Despite reports to the contrary, the gym is a welcoming place. A friendly environment full of people who are all striving for the same goal  an improvement in their health and fitness. The days of gyms being full of angry, steroid-enhanced hulks are gone for good, and the modern day commercial gym is inclusive and caters for anyone, regardless of fitness level. There is no need to be intimidated when using the gym as a beginner. Just follow a few simple gym etiquette guidelines and you will fit right in!
          </p>
          <p>
           They are basic courtesy really, but following them will make your gym experience an altogether more pleasurable one!
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            1. This is a power cage/squat rack. Dont stand in it unless you are using it for a suitable purpose.
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           Suitable purposes are limited to exercise that require its use. Bicep curls for example, do not require the use of the squat rack, so stand somewhere else to do your curls!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.hoylesfitness.com/general/gym-etiquette-for-beginners/attachment/img_1736/" rel="attachment wp-att-2133">
            <img alt="www.hoylesfitness.com, Squat rack, Personal Training stockport, Gym etiquette" class="aligncenter wp-image-2133" height="371" src="http://www.hoylesfitness.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/IMG_1736.jpg" width="317"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           One of the most common complaints amongst gym users is people performing bicep curls in the squat rack. Dont be the person who does that!
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Remember: If you are performing an exercise that doesnt require the use of the rack, dont do it in there.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            2. If a fellow member is using a piece of kit that you want/need, ask them to give you a shout when they are done with it.
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           Dont hang around them like a bad smell, asking them every 30 seconds how many more sets they have left. There seems to be one of these idiots in every gym in the world. Dont be one of them.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Remember: If someone is using kit you want, be patient or be creative and use an alternative exercise.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            3. Put your kit away with you when you are finished.
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           This is my personal biggest hate in the gym. I cant stand it when people use kit and dont put it away after them. The staff at the gym arent there to do it for you  they arent waiters. Their job is to help members achieve their goals, not put away kit that you have used.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.hoylesfitness.com/general/gym-etiquette-for-beginners/attachment/img_1734/" rel="attachment wp-att-2132">
            <img alt="www.hoylesfitness.com, personal training, fitness, fat loss, strength, exercise, Gym etiquette" class="aligncenter wp-image-2132" height="303" src="http://www.hoylesfitness.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/IMG_1734.jpg" width="303"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Apart from the mess that having kit everywhere makes, it is also a major trip hazard. Respect your fellow gym members  keep the place tidy, put kit where it should be and do your bit to make the gym a positive place.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.hoylesfitness.com/general/gym-etiquette-for-beginners/attachment/img_1755/" rel="attachment wp-att-2137">
            <img alt="Gym, Fitness, Gym etiquette" class="aligncenter wp-image-2137" height="259" src="http://www.hoylesfitness.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/IMG_1755.jpg" width="345"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Remember: When you finish using kit, put it away.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <h2>
           4. Remember you are in the gym to train, not socialise.
          </h2>
          <p>
           The worst offenders of this tends to be the younger guys, who use the gym as a social place. In the gym where I train, it is not uncommon for them to turn up in groups of 5 or 6, standing around the benches and squat racks and using them for up to 30 minutes at a time.
          </p>
          <p>
           The other kind of member is the one who wants to chat for ages between sets, slowing down your workout if you are unlucky enough to be engaged by him! In the gym where I train there are a few people like this, who think its appropriate to keep you talking for ages between sets.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Remember: If its anything more than small-talk, save it for another time.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <h2>
           5. If cardio is your thing, keep usage of machines down to a realistic period of time.
          </h2>
          <p>
           Despite cardio being a pretty poor choice of exercise, thanks to poor advice from many experts, it is still popular in most gyms. The problem with cardio is that it takes a long time, so each piece of cardio kit is out of action for a significant period. With space and cost being factored in, most gyms have a shortage of cardio kit at peak times.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.hoylesfitness.com/general/gym-etiquette-for-beginners/attachment/img_1753-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-2135">
            <img alt="Life Leisure Hazel Grove, Stockport Gym, Personal Trainer Stockport, Gym etiquette" class="aligncenter wp-image-2135" height="190" src="http://www.hoylesfitness.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/IMG_1753.jpg" width="254"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Long, drawn-out cardio sessions are beneficial to nobody other than endurance athletes. If you want a good quality cardio workout, go hard and go fast. Not only will you need the kit for less time, making your usage fairer for others, but you will achieve more results in less time  everyones a winner!
          </p>
          <p>
           Some gyms try to enforce a 20 minute maximum time limit at peak times, which I think is fair enough. Some members complain about this, saying that they pay for use so why should it be capped, but the people waiting are also paying for its use, so its a least-worst option.Train sensibly and 20 minutes will be all you need anyway!
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Remember: At peak times, keep your cardio kit usage down to a reasonable period of time.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           So there you go, new gym user  five gym etiquetteguidelines to keep you on the good side of the more experienced gym users! They take no effort, but a bit of courtesy and care for the common training environment will make everybody happy!
          </p>
          <p>
           Enjoy your training!
          </p>
          <p>

           </a>
          </p>
