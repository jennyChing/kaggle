         <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/112852/how-to-backup-your-text-messages-to-your-gmail-account/" rel="bookmark" title="How To Backup Your Text Messages to Your Gmail Account">
          How To Backup Your Text Messages to Your Gmail Account
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="thecontent">
         <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-01_143013" border="0" height="300" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-01_143013.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="2012-05-01_143013" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Backing up your text messages from your Android phone to your Gmail account is so simple theres no reason to not back them up and make them search-friendly in the process. Read on to see how you can turn your Gmail account into an SMS vault.
         </p>
         <h3>
          What Do I Need?
         </h3>
         <p>
          Its easy to lose your text messages. Everything from switching phones to fumble fingers can drop your messages in front of the digital reaperjust last night I managed to accidently delete a massive SMS thread when I really only intended to delete a single message that refused to send.
         </p>
         <p>
          Backing up your SMS messages to your Gmail account is so simple, however, theres no good reason not to do it. For this tutorial youll need three things:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Your Android phone
          </li>
          <li>
           A free copy of
           <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.zegoggles.smssync&amp;hl=en">
            SMS Backup+
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           A
           <a href="https://gmail.com">
            Gmail account
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Got all that? Lets get started!
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Note:
          </em>
          Technically, you can dig around the advanced settings of SMS Backup+ to reconfigure it to work with any IMAP-enabled email server. However, since it was designed to work with Gmail and works so well with Gmails search, threading, and starring functionality, were not going to mess with a good thing.
         </p>

         </p>

           Connect
          </strong>
          . The default browser on your Android phone will launch and youll be prompted to login to the Gmail account you want to use for backing up your messages. After you login youll need to authorize SMS Backup+ to utilize your Google account like so:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-01_150735" border="0" height="210" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-01_150735.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="2012-05-01_150735" width="320"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Click
          <strong>
           Grant Access
          </strong>
          . Youll be prompted to begin a backup immediately or skip the initial backup:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-01_151006" border="0" height="258" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-01_151006.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="2012-05-01_151006" width="321"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Click
          <strong>
           Backup
          </strong>
          ; we didnt come all this way not to back things up! If you hit Skip then all the messages on your phone will be flagged as already backed up and will be ignored.
         </p>
         <p>
          The backup process will start, and depending on how many messages you have, will take anywhere from a minute to a half hour to complete. It clips along at about a message per second:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-01_151359" border="0" height="239" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-01_151359.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="2012-05-01_151359" width="321"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          You dont even have to wait until the process is finished to jump over to the Gmail account and check the progress. Login to your Gmail account from a web browser. Youll see a new label in the sidebar,
          <strong>
           SMS
          </strong>
          . Click on it:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-01_152045" border="0" height="250" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-01_152045.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="2012-05-01_152045" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Success! SMS Backup+ automatically backs up your SMS messages as well as your MMS messages. Not only are all of our text messages there but the pictures weve sent back and forth are backup up to Gmail along with the messages. Now that weve got everything humming along, lets look at some advanced options.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Advanced SMS Backup+ Configuration
         </h3>
         <h3>
          <img alt="2012-05-01_152639" border="0" height="167" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-01_152639.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="2012-05-01_152639" width="321"/>
         </h3>
         <p>
          If you do nothing else before leaving this tutorial, you need to turn on the automatic backup feature. Leaving things up to manual backup in a sure fire way to forget. From the main screen tap
          <strong>
           Auto backup
          </strong>
          to turn it on and then tape on
          <strong>
           Auto backup settings
          </strong>
          to configure the frequency. The default configuration is a bit aggressive. You may wish to, as we did, decrease the frequency of backups and even set it to only backup one Wi-Fi if youre backing up a lot of MMS and dont want to burn through your mobile data quota.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-01_153615" border="0" height="226" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-01_153615.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="2012-05-01_153615" width="321"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          After youve set up the automatic backup, return to the main screen and head into
          <strong>
           Advanced settings
          </strong>
          . There you can change the settings for backing up, restoring, and notifications. Under
          <strong>
           Backup
          </strong>
          there are some useful settings you may wish to toggle include turning off MMS backup (again, to save on data consumption, and creating a whitelist of contacts you want backed up (instead of the default where every single message is backed up).
         </p>
         <p>
          There isnt much to look at under the
          <strong>
           Restore
          </strong>
          settings, but you can take advantage of a handy Gmail-centric trick. When SMS Backup+ stores your messages in Gmail it creates a thread for each contact. You can tell SMS Backup+ to only restore the contacts with starred threads which allows you to quickly select which conversations are important enough to restore via the star system in Gmail.
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <p>
          So there you have it! All your text messages (including multimedia attachments) are backed up within Gmail where you can easily search them and restore them to your handset should the need arise.
         </p>
