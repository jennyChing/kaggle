         <div class="body">
          <div class="media-text">
           <p>
            Cancer is to blame for nearly one out of every four deaths, according to the
            <a href="/group/acs" target="_blank">
             <b>
              American Cancer Society
             </b>
            </a>
            (ACS). If caught late, the prognosis for many types of cancer is less than ideal. But if caught early, through preventive screenings, the chances of beating the Big C can be much higher. Screening tests can help detect cancer before it spreads, which can mean less invasive treatments and a greater likelihood of success. Click through to find out which screening tests may be right for you, according to ACS guidelines.

          <h2 class="media-title">
           Breast Cancer Screening
          </h2>
          <div class="media-text">
           <p>
            Our best data indicates that screening on a regular basis decreases a womans risk of death by 15% in her 40s to about 30% as she gets older, says
            <a href="/user/otis-webb-brawley" target="_blank">
             <b>
              Otis Brawley, MD
             </b>
            </a>
            , chief medical officer for ACS. But mammography has its limitations. It will find some lesions that turn out not to be cancer. It will find a few cancers that dont need to be treated. It will also miss some cancers.
           </p>
           <div>
           </div>
           <div>
            ACS Screening Guidelines:
           </div>
           <div>
            <a href="/health/breast-cancer/slideshow/what-you-need-to-know-about-mammograms" target="_blank">
             <b>
              Mammogram
             </b>
            </a>
            : yearly for women 40 and older
           </div>
           <div>
            <a href="/health/womens-health/what-is-clinical-breast-exam" target="_blank">
             <b>
              Clinical Breast Exam
             </b>
            </a>
            <b>
             :
            </b>
            every three years for women in their 20s and 30s, every year for women in their 40s
           </div>
           <div>
            Women at
            <strong>
             <a href="/health/breast-cancer/risk-factors-for-breast-cancer" target="_blank">
              high risk
             </a>
            </strong>
            should speak with their doctor about earlier screenings.

          <h2 class="media-title">
           Cervical Cancer Screening
          </h2>
          <div class="media-text">
           <div>
            <b>
             
            </b>
            Cervical cancer screening saves a lot of lives, says Brawley. In fact, its one of the only screening tests where the ACS doesnt recommend people weigh the risks and benefits. This particular type of cancer is highly curable when treated early, and screening is very accurate at detecting cell irregularities.
           </div>
           <div>
            <b>
            </b>
           </div>
           <div>
            ACS Screening Guidelines:
           </div>
           <div>
            <b>
             Who should be screened:
            </b>
            Women between the ages of 21 and 65
           </div>
           <div>
            <b>
             Women 21-29:
            </b>
            <a href="/health/gynecology/what-pap-test" target="_blank">
             <b>
              Pap test
             </b>
            </a>
            every three years
           </div>
           <div>
            <b>
             Women 30-65:
            </b>
            <a href="/health/cervical-cancer/what-human-papillomavirus-cervical-cancer" target="_blank">
             <b>
              HPV test
             </b>
            </a>
            and Pap test (combined) every five years, or Pap test (by itself) every three years
           </div>
           <div>
            <b>
             Women 65+
            </b>
            : Talk to your doctor; you may not need to be screened.
           </div>
          </div>

          <div class="media-text">
           <div>
            We have really good data to show that screening for colon cancer can decrease the number of deaths by 20-35%, says Brawley. That's because screening can find precancerous polyps before they turn into cancer. They can also detect cancer early, when treatment is most effective.
           </div>
           <div>
           </div>
           <div>
            ACS Screening Guidelines:
           </div>
           <div>
            <b>
             Who should be screened:
            </b>
            Men and women 50 years and older, although high-risk individuals may need to be screened earlier and more often
           </div>
           <div>
            <a href="/health/lab-tests/what-is-fecal-occult-blood-test" target="_blank">
             <b>
              FOBT
             </b>

             /

             </b>

            <b>

            </b>

            <b>

            </b>

              Flexible sigmoidoscopy
             </b>
            </a>
            <b>
             :
            </b>
            every five years (or)

          <h2 class="media-title">
           Prostate Cancer Screening
          </h2>
          <div class="media-text">
           <div>
            Weve got data to show that screening for prostate cancer is beneficial for certain people, says Brawley. But the ACS recommends that men know the
            <a href="/health/prostate-cancer/how-effective-psatest-prostate-cancer" target="_blank">
             <b>
              potential benefits
             </b>
            </a>
            and
            <a href="/health/prostate-cancer/what-pros-cons-psa-test" target="_blank">
             <b>
              harms
             </b>
            </a>
            associated with prostate cancer screening before making the decision to get screened. Screening involves a
            <a href="/health/prostate-cancer-treatment/what-is-psa-test" target="_blank">
             <b>
              prostate-specific antigen (PSA) blood test
             </b>
            </a>
            and/or a
            <a href="/health/diagnostic-procedures/how-digital-rectal-examination-performed" target="_blank">
             <b>
              digital rectal exam (DRE).
             </b>
            </a>
           </div>
           <div>
           </div>
           <div>
            <div>
             The ACS suggests a man discuss screening at:
            </div>
            <div>
             <b>
              Age 50:
             </b>
             If he's at average risk and is expected to live 10+ years
            </div>
            <div>
             <b>
              Age 45:
             </b>
             If he's at high risk (African American, men with a first-degree relative diagnosed with cancer before 65)
            </div>
            <div>
             <b>
              Age 40:
             </b>
             If he's had more than one first-degree relative diagnosed with cancer early in life
            </div>

              skin exam
             </b>
            </a>
            , says Brawley. According to the Skin Cancer Foundation, you should get a skin exam every year as part of your annual checkup, or with a dermatologist. The ACS also recommends that you do a skin self-exam once a month, preferably after a bath or shower. Know all your moles, blemishes and birthmarks. If any grow or change color, or any new ones appear, be sure to show them to your doctor.

         <div class="body">
          <h2 class="media-title">
           Reduce Your Cancer Risk
          </h2>
          <div class="media-text">
           <div>
            We dont have data to show that changing from a bad lifestyle to a good lifestyle prevents cancer, says Brawley. But we do have data that suggests people who have a lifestyle low in meat and high in exercise have a lower lifetime risk of cancer. Some other possible ways to lower your risk of cancer are limiting alcohol consumption, not smoking, staying at a healthy weight and having regular check-ups, which may include exams for cancers of the thyroid, lymph nodes, testes and ovaries. Always keep your doctor up to date if you experience new or unusual symptoms that could signify cancer. Early diagnosis of cancer can make a big difference in treatment success.
