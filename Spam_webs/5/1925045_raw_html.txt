       <div>
        <div>
         <h2>
          read more:
          <a href="http://malwaredoc.com/fud">
           fud
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Ive taught myself everything I need to know to do decent static analysis. Im still a beginner, but learn more everyday. Im a networking student i.e. no programming other than high level scripting, no low level understanding of operating systems, no understanding of encoding, and no understanding of advanced maths.My starting level was literally 0.Ill tell you exactly what my path of learning was.1)buy the practical malware analysis book.
         </p>
         <p>
          2)read the first few chapters(basic static and dynamic analysis)
         </p>
         <p>
          3)get to the A crash course in assembly chapter. In my case I couldnt even understand a single x86 instruction. This chapter is a crash course, so this was not clear enough for a beginner like me. I spent a few weeks reading many websites for x86 guides. The amazing thing I discovered was that assembly is terribly easy to understand. The main problem is that all sources I found were boring or over complicating things(maybe because of my lack of low level understanding..).
         </p>
         <p>
          4) At this point I was able to read assembly. I found an amazing video series:
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wqGepeYntFo">
           http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wqGepeYntFo
          </a>
          . This video series taught me how to recognize high level programming structures in assembly(this obviously makes malware analysis a lot easier). After you fully understand the bomb.exe exercises(Now I think about it, I may have only done day 1 of the series) from the video series, you are at a competent level for reverse engineering.
         </p>
         <p>
          5) Continue reading the book. You will learn about more about disassembler(IDA pro) and debuggers (Ollydbg and Windbg).
         </p>
         <p>
          6)To practice debugging, I spent a few weeks doing crackmes. These are little cracking challenges. Try not to use IDA pro at all for these.
         </p>
         <p>
          7) At the moment Im creating programs in C++ using the windows API. And at the same time learning more about cryptography(by creating decoders/decryptors in C++ and assembly).
         </p>
         <p>
          Of course your path will be different, but I hope this step-by-step guide will give you a general idea.
         </p>
