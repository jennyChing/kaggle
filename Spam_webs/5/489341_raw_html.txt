      <h1 class="page-header">
       RGIII's T-Shirt: 'No Jesus, No Peace;' NFL: No Wear
      </h1>
      <div class="region region-content">
       <section class="block block-system clearfix" id="block-system-main">
        <div about="/news/article/terence-p-jeffrey/rgiiis-t-shirt-no-jesus-no-peace-nfl-no-wear" class="node node-article clearfix" id="node-69313" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">
         <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="RGIII's T-Shirt: 'No Jesus, No Peace;' NFL: No Wear" property="dc:title">
         </span>
         <div class="authors">
          By
          <a class="username" href="/author/terence-p-jeffrey" title="View user profile.">
           Terence P. Jeffrey
          </a>
          | September 15, 2014 | 10:45 PM EDT
         </div>
         <div class="addthis_jumbo_share">
         </div>
         <div class="content">
          <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
           <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
             <p>
              (CNSNews.com) - Washington Redskins' quarterback Robert Griffin III, who dislocated his ankle in Sunday's gameagainst the Jacksonville Jaguars, entered the room for a post-game press conference wearing a T-shirt that said "[K]No[w] Jesus, [K]No[w] Peace" but turned it inside-out before speaking to reporters.
             </p>
             <p>
              Video and photographic images show Griffin entering the room wearing the shirt--showing its emphatically Christianinscription--and then making remarks at the podium while wearing the shirt turned inside out so that the inscription did not show.
             </p>
             <p>
              <iframe class="video-filter video-mrctv video-center vf-129333" frameborder="0" height="360" src="http://www.mrctv.org/embed/129333" width="640">
              </iframe>
             </p>
             <p>
              The "K"s and the "w"s in the "Know Jesus, Know Peace" inscription on RGIII's shirt were in black while the other letters were in white--so that thedeclaration "No Jesus No Peace" stood out.
             </p>
             <p>
              Michael Phillips, a sportswriter for the Richmond Times Dispatch,
              <a href="https://twitter.com/michaelpRTD">
               who was in the room tweeted
              </a>
              : "RGIII was wearing a t-shirt that said 'Know Jesus, Know Peace.' NFL Uniform Inspector Tony McGee (former Skins DE) approached Griffin...McGee told Griffin he couldn't wear the shirt because it wasn't a Nike product. Made him turn it inside out for the press conference."
             </p>
             <div class="insert-box">
              <img class="insert-image" src="http://cns7prod.s3.amazonaws.com/styles/ap_image/s3/images/ROBERT GRIFFIN-AP PHOTO-EVAN VUCCI_0.jpg" typeof="foaf:Image" width="200"/>
              <p class="insert-caption">
               Redskins quarterback Robert Griffin III before and after his T-shirt was turned inside out after Sunday's game. (AP Photo/Evan Vucci)
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
              Phillips later
              <a href="http://www.timesdispatch.com/redskins-xtra/redskins-win-home-opener-but-lose-rgiii-deseanjackson/article_99e05a96-3c4a-11e4-81c9-001a4bcf6878.html">
               published a story that said
              </a>
              : "He entered the news conference room on crutches, wearing a neon-blue T-shirt that said: 'Know Jesus, Know Peace,' though he was asked to turn it inside-out by an NFL uniform inspector, who informed him only officially-licensed Nike products could be worn on camera."
             </p>
             <p>
              Los Angeles Times reporter Chuck Schilken reported that RGIII had been fined $10,000 last year for wearing a T-shirt with a message.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.latimes.com/sports/sportsnow/la-sp-sn-robert-griffin-iii-rg3-shirt-20140915-story.html">
               Schilken wrote
              </a>
              :
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000; text-transform: none; line-height: normal; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; font-family: Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 10px; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
              <span style="font-size: small;">
               <i>
                "The NFL has pretty strict rulesabout what players can and can't wear at the stadium on game days at any time they might be visible to the public. Players' outfit can't feature 'personal messages either in writing or illustration" and cannot "honor or commemorate individuals,' at least not without league approval.
               </i>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000; text-transform: none; line-height: normal; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; font-family: Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 10px; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
              <span style="font-size: small;">
               <i>
                "Furthermore, the league won't grant permission for any items 'which relate to political activities or causes, other non-football events, causes or campaigns, or charitable causes or campaigns.' Anything that is approved is supposed to be, among other things, "non-controversial.
               </i>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000; text-transform: none; line-height: normal; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; font-family: Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 10px; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
              <span style="font-size: small;">
               <i>
                "So while there doesn't seem to be any specific rules pertaining to religious-themed shirts like the one Griffin was wearing, there looks to be plenty of gray area and language that could have caused some grief for RG3.
               </i>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000; text-transform: none; line-height: normal; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; font-family: Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 10px; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
              <span style="font-size: small;">
               <i>
                "After all, this is a guy who was fined$10,000 last year for wearing a shirt that said 'Operation Patience'--a reference to the Redskins' slow pace in returning him to action after an ACL injury--before fans even arrived at the stadium."
               </i>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
              <span style="font-size: small;">
               <i>
                "No wonder he decided to play it safe."
               </i>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
              <span style="font-size: small;">
               <a href="http://www.csnwashington.com/redskinsblog/investigating-what-happened-rg3s-t-shirt-after-jags-win">
                J.P. Finlay of Comcast Sportsnet
               </a>
               also looked into the issue. He posted video of Griffin entering the press room wearing the "No Jesus, No Peace" message on his T-shirt and then answering questions at the podium with the T-shirt inside-out so people would not see the message.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
              <span style="font-size: small;">
               Finlay also posted photographs of Redskins star defensive end Ryan Kerrigan--at the same press conference--wearing a T-shirt not made by Nike, but sporting writing on the front.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
              <span style="font-size: small;">
               Kerrigan's T-shirt however made no mention of "Jesus" or "Peace"--or any relationship between the two
              </span>
             </p>
             <span style="font-size: small;">
              <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                "It's worth noting that other players came to the podium not wearing Nike t-shirts. Like Ryan Kerrigan," wrote Finlay.
               </span>
              </p>
              <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                Finlay cited--
                <a href="http://static.nfl.com/static/content/public/image/rulebook/pdfs/8_Rule5_Players_Subs_Equip_GeneralRules.pdf">
                 and linked to
                </a>
                --an NFL rule that prohibits players from "wearing, displaying, or otherwise conveying personal messages" on game day when they are visible to the public.
               </span>
               <span style="font-family: times new roman,times;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                </span>
               </span>
              </p>
              <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
               <span style="font-family: times new roman,times;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 Throughout  the period on game-day that a player is visible to the stadium and  television audience (including in pregame warm-ups, in the bench area,  and during postgame interviews in the locker room or on the field),  players are prohibited from wearing, displaying, or otherwise conveying  personal messages either in writing or illustration, unless such message  has been approved in advance by the League office," says the rule. "Items to celebrate  anniversaries or memorable events, or to honor or commemorate  individuals, such as helmet decals, and arm bands and jersey patches on  players uniforms, are prohibited unless approved in advance by the  League office. All such items must relate to team or League events or  personages.
                </span>
               </span>
              </p>
              <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                Finlay reported that the Redskins said that RGIII turned his T-shirt inside-out by his own choice--rather than face a possible fine from the NFL.
               </span>
              </p>
              <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                "When reached by phone, a Redskins spokesperson indicatedRobert Griffin III flipped his T-shirt inside out by choice, and was not asked to do so by an NFL representative," wrote Finlay. "Due to the personal message bylaw cited above, Griffin could have faced a fine by the NFL, and chose to avoid the situation, the Redskins spokesperson indicated."
               </span>
              </p>
              <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                In his remarks to reporters, Griffin discussed his injury.
                <br/>
               </span>
              </p>
              <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                "In moments like these you just have to keep the faith," he said. "Stay with it, and just believe that everything happens for a reason, and God has a plan."
                <br/>
               </span>
              </p>
             </span>
             <span style="font-size: small;">
              <span style="font: small/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; float: none; display: inline !important; white-space: normal; background-color: #ffffff; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
               <span style="font: small/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; float: none; display: inline !important; white-space: normal; background-color: #ffffff; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
                <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  Michael Phillips of the Richmond Times Dispatch, who initially drew attention to RGIII's T-shirt and the fact that it had been turned inside out at a press conference, has a line
                  <a href="https://twitter.com/michaelpRTD">
                   at the top of his Twitter page
                  </a>
                  that simply says: Romans 10 14-15.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p style="font: 10px/normal Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000; text-transform: none; text-indent: 0px; letter-spacing: normal; word-spacing: 0px; white-space: normal; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px;">
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  That passage of Scripture says:How, then, can they call on the one they have not believed in? And how can they believe in the one of whom they have not heard? And how can they hear without someone preaching to them?And how can anyone preach unless they are sent? As it is written: 'How beautiful are the feet of those who bring good news!"
                 </span>
                </p>
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </div>
