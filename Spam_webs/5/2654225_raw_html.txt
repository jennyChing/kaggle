       How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather
      </a>
     </h1>
     <div class="date-comments-post">
      <p class="fl">
       Mon, Jul 29, 2013
      </p>
      <p class="fl">
       . Posted by: Sophie
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="title_categories">
      Filed under:
      <a href="http://blog.hostelbookers.com/category/travel/" rel="category tag">
       travel inspiration
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="entry">
      <div addthis:title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather " addthis:url="http://blog.hostelbookers.com/travel/great-photos-bad-weather/" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style addthis_32x32_style">

      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <a href="http://blog.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Fotografieren-bei-miesem-Wetter-banner.jpg">
        <img alt="Fotografieren bei miesem Wetter banner How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="size-full wp-image-43860 aligncenter" height="192" src="http://blog.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Fotografieren-bei-miesem-Wetter-banner.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="540"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <em>
        By Alexander Koval,
        <a href="http://www.out-n-about.de" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Out-n-About.de
        </a>
        . Translated from our
        <a href="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/reisetipps/reise-helferlein-staedtetrip-fotografieren-und-mieses-wetter/" target="_blank">
         German blog
        </a>
        <br/>
       </em>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       You wake up in a new city, ready to seize the day, but alas! Theres no warm sun hitting your face and as you glance out the window, your fears are confirmed  its a miserable day outside. The city that you wanted to capture with your camera is wrapped in gray.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       But dont let that discourage you! After you have studied the weather forecast in advance and read the tips below, you have the opportunity to create spectacular images  despite the miserable weather!
      </p>
      <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
       Warm-up exercises for your creativity
      </h2>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <img alt="Brucke How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12223" height="357" src="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Brucke.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="540"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Anyone who believes that they can deliver good images with the quick click of a button is mistaken. Its not just your muscles that need warm-up  but your creativity too. I carry my camera from breakfast time onwards, or take pictures for an hour of everything in front of my lens on my way to see the sights.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       For me, its all about getting default images out of my head. I also take images using familiar and ingrained perspectives of objects and people. I essentially try to tick off all the usual suspect type photos in my head.
       <br/>
       <img alt="Bar How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12224" height="357" src="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Bar.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="540"/>
       Only then am I able to get engage with new and daring perspectives and compositions. You should really try it for an hour and take pictures of the things that seem ordinary. The aim is to photograph everything exactly as you would otherwise do it.
      </p>
      <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
       Identifying and exploiting advantages
      </h2>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       An overcast sky is usually annoying for travellers, but not necessarily for photographers. A gray sky holds the advantages, if you know how to exploit it. Here are 5 examples.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       1.
       <b>
       </b>
       <strong>
        Portraits
       </strong>
       look better, because you have no strong shadows on the face. So you can snap your friend and photograph them with great textures and alignment.
       <img alt="Akim How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12219" height="358" src="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Akim.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="540"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       2. Detailed shots of buildings are now more evenly illuminated and you get
       <strong>
        clearer photos
       </strong>
       .
       <img alt="Hauser Details How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12225" height="357" src="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Hauser-Details.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="540"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       3. You can use the wetness of the road to create
       <strong>
        dramatic images.
       </strong>
       <img alt="Tauben How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12226" height="357" src="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Tauben.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="540"/>
       <strong>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       4.
       <strong>
        Puddles
       </strong>
       can be used as a great
       <strong>
        reflective
       </strong>
       surface.
       <img alt="Pfutze How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="aligncenter" height="400" src="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Pfutze.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="265"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       5. You can
       <strong>
        shoot through the whole day
       </strong>
       and not have to pay attention to the position of the sun
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       6.
       <strong>
        Blue hour
       </strong>
       , which lasts approximately one hour after sunset, produces a beautifully stronger blue in the sky. For blue hour shots, its best to have already selected a sight that you want to photograph.
       <br/>
       <img alt="FISCHERBASTEI 3 How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12222" height="357" src="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/FISCHERBASTEI-3.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="540"/>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
       <img alt="FISCHERBASTEI 2 How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12227" height="357" src="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/FISCHERBASTEI-2.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="540"/>
      </h3>
      <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
       Challenging yourself
      </h2>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       If youre not sure exactly how to challenge yourself, then I recommend you try to give yourself a theme for the day. It can be a colour, an object or a phrase.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       In Budapest for example, I set myself the task of incorporating the colour blue in my pictures. So then I searched all day for scenes that had held something blue in them. It stimulated my creativity and prompted me in a completely different way.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <img alt="Blau 4 How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12228" height="357" src="http://blog-de.hostelbookers.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Blau-4.jpg" title="How to Take Great Photos in Terrible Weather" width="540"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Hopefully with these tips, you should  despite seemingly bad weather  bring back some great images of your city break!
      </p>
