       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Coffee Shop {Pretend Play} Activity
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         This
         <strong>
          coffee shop pretend play activity
         </strong>
         with sand, soap and water provides hours of open-ended, imaginative play for kids of all ages. Its become one of
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/25-water-play-ideas-backyard/">
          my favourite backyard water play activities
         </a>
         here in my home daycare.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youve been following us for any length of time, you know that I love setting up easy and inexpensive activities in our
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/backyard-play-space-ideas/" title="18 Free {or almost free} Cool Features to Add to Your Backyard Play Space">
          backyard play space
         </a>
         . Its a wonderful way to allow children toexplore, experiment and learn. I especially love an activity that is captivating enough to hold their attention for a long period of time. Our coffee shop met all the criteria and then some.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/coffee-shop-activity-with-sand-soap-and-water-happyhooligans.jpg">
          <br/>
          <img alt="coffee shop activity with sand, soap and water (happyhooligans)" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9737" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/coffee-shop-activity-with-sand-soap-and-water-happyhooligans.jpg" title="coffee shop activity with sand, soap and water - hours of pretend play (happy hooligans)" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Last Friday was a P.A. day so a few of our graduates joined us for the day. Because of the larger group and the wider age-range (1 to 7), I wanted to offer an engaging outdoor activity that would appeal to everyone. I came up with the idea for this coffee shop activity just a few short hours before they arrived Friday morning, (cuz some of my best ideas come to me in the 11th hour) and I have to say, it was without a doubt, one of the best activities weve ever done here.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre wondering where the inspiration came from, my hubby and I had picked up a couple of Timmies coffees the night before, and when I saw empty coffee cups in the recycling bin, a light-bulb went on.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9749.jpg">
          <img alt="kids working at the pretend play coffee shop" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9750" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9749.jpg" title="coffee shop pretend play - great outdoor play activity (happy hooligans)" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         In the past, other super-popular activities that have held the hooligans attention for the better part of an entire morning have been our
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/2012/05/26/muddy-car-wash/" title="muddy car wash">
          Muddy Car Wash,
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/2012/05/25/baby-bath-station/" title="baby bath station">
          Baby Bath Station
         </a>
         , and our
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/2012/06/25/ice-age-bin/" title="ice age bin">
          Ice Age Bin
         </a>
         . Please click on the links to see what theyre all about. Each one is easy to prepare, and requires basic items that you likely already have around your home.
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9722.jpg">
          <img alt='scooping sand "sugar"' class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9752" height="560" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9722.jpg" title="pretend play coffee shop" width="373"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This one was no different. I simply gathered some odds and ends from the toy room and craft room, and the only ingredients we used were sand, water and dish soap.
        </p>
        <h2>
         What I used to create our coffee shop:
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          metal or plastic tea pots, coffee pots or pitchers
         </li>
         <li>
          milk foamer/frother (you could probably use a whisk or a hand-held egg-beater or even a blender)
         </li>
         <li>
          clean milk/cream carton
         </li>
         <li>
          plastic mugs, disposable coffee cups
         </li>
         <li>
          stir sticks
         </li>
         <li>
          spoons
         </li>
         <li>
          spice shaker with large holes (ours still had popcorn flavouring in it)
         </li>
         <li>
          small sugar bowls
         </li>
         <li>
          sand
         </li>
         <li>
          water
         </li>
         <li>
          a few squirts of dish soap
         </li>
         <li>
          plastic serving tray or cardboard take-out drink tray
         </li>
         <li>
          our camping water jug so the children had access to and control of running water in the yard
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         This is what the coffee shop looked like before the hooligans descended on it. The photo isnt great, but the kids pounced on everything so quickly I wasnt able to get a better one shot of the set-up.
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9714.jpg">
          <img alt="coffee shop activity set up" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9738" height="560" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9714.jpg" title="coffee shop activity" width="373"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Inexpensive work table:
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The table that we use for a lot of our activities is a really light-weight coffee table that I picked up at a second-hand shop. Because its so light, its easy to move around the yard or in and out of the house to where-ever we need it. I paid about 6 bucks for it, and I dont know how we ever did without it.
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9723.jpg">
          <img alt="pretend play coffee shop" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9749" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9723.jpg" title="pretend play coffee shop" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Easy prep:
        </h2>
        <h2>
         <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: 13px; font-weight: normal;">
          I simply filled the teapots and coffee pots with water, and I added some sand to the sugar bowls. I added water and a few squirts of dish soap to the milk foamer, and I pumped the handle a few times to produce the beautiful foam that youll see in the following photos.
         </span>
        </h2>
        <h2>
         Coffee time!
        </h2>
        <p>
         It was an instant hit! They all crowded around, and there was a frenzy of activity that continued for most of the morning.
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9753.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_9753" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9751" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9753.jpg" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         The milk frother was a big hit. When the kids pumped the plunger up and down, the soap and water produced loads of gorgeous foam that was perfect for scooping or pouring onto their lattes.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9717.jpg">
          <img alt="soap and water in the milk frother" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9739" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9717.jpg" title="soap and water in the milk frother" width="560"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9748.jpg">
          <img alt='soap "milk" for the pretend coffee shop' class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9743" height="560" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9748.jpg" width="373"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Our camping water-jug is always a popular, warm-weather addition to our outdoor play space. The kids enjoy having access to running water, and they love to crank the spigot to refill their containers or to rinse their hands.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em id="__mceDel">
          <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9854.jpg">
           <img alt="a camping water jug in the outdoor play space" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9746" height="560" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9854.jpg" width="373"/>
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         They poured cream,
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9719.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_9719" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9740" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9719.jpg" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         scooped sugar,
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9742.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_9742" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9747" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9742.jpg" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         stirred and shook,
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9725.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_9725" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9741" height="560" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9725.jpg" width="373"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9755.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_9755" class="aligncenter" height="560" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9755.jpg" width="373"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         and served up some of the best looking play-coffee Id ever seen.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9737.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_9737" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9742" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9737.jpg" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         And shortly before lunch, they realized that snowball bush was in full bloom,
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9833.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_9833" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9744" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9833.jpg" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         so they spent the rest of the morning running back and forth, gathering blossoms to garnish their drinks with.
         <a href="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9844.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_9844" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9748" height="373" src="http://happyhooligans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/IMG_9844.jpg" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Mmmmm Mmmm! Muddy, sandy play never looked so good!
        </p>
        <p>
         So good, in fact that this activity has given me an idea for another one that I think will be just as fun! Stay tuned because well be doing that one next week!
         <br/>

        </p>
