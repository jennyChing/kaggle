        <h1 class="entry-title">
         B-2 Spirit stealth bomber burnt down in 2010 brought back to operative status
        </h1>
        <div class="info_area">
         <span class="single-time">
          Jan 10 2014
         </span>
         -
         <a href="http://theaviationist.com/2014/01/10/spirit-of-washington-back/#comments">
          <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="23055 http://theaviationist.com/?p=23055">
           7 Comments
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
        <img class="single-thumb-img" src="http://theaviationist.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Spirit-of-Washington.jpg"/>
        <div class="single-author">
         By Jacek Siminski
        </div>
        <div class="single-content">
         <p>
          The U.S. Air Force has recently announced that the 4 year repair process of the
          <a href="http://theaviationist.com/2013/09/18/b-2-atlantic-mission/" target="_blank" title="All you need to know about last weeks B-2 stealth bombers round trip mission across the Atlantic">
           B-2
          </a>
          <em>
           Spirit of Washington
          </em>
          has been completed.
         </p>
         <p>
          The plane burnt down back in February 2010 during a take-off from
          <a href="http://theaviationist.com/2013/04/23/calcm-guam/" target="_blank" title="Meanwhile, in Guam: B-52 bomber is loaded with (dummy) Air-Launched Cruise Missiles">
           Andersen Air Force Base on Guam
          </a>
          . A minor engine fire turned out to be disastrous for the airframe and all four powerplants of the
          <a href="http://theaviationist.com/2013/04/04/video-b-2-refueling/" target="_blank" title="[Video] B-2 stealth bomber out of Guam refuels over the Pacific">
           stealth bomber
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          The process took so long mainly due to the complicated structure of the bomber that is based on a composite airframe.
         </p>
         <p>
          USAF did not publicize the estimated costs of the repair.
         </p>
         <p>
          Both USAF engineers and Northrop Grumman professionals were involved in the process. The repair made the B-2 reachieve its full operational capability with the entire USAF force of stalth batwing planes back to 20 Spirit bombers out of 21 delivered (one was lost in an accident
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_ZCp5h1gK2Q" target="_blank">
           at Andersen AFB on Feb. 23, 2008
          </a>
          ).
         </p>
         <p>
          It was on Dec. 17. last year that Northrop Grumman celebrated the 20th anniversary of the first
          <a href="http://theaviationist.com/2013/10/28/b-2-mop/" target="_blank" title="Rare image of a B-2 stealth bomber and its Massive Ordnance Penetrator bunker buster bomb">
           B-2

          delivery.
