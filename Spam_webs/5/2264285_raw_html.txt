     <p>
      <b>
       Jud Strunk
      </b>
      (June 11, 1936October 5, 1981) was an
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       American
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">
       singer
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">
       songwriter
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">
       comedian
      </a>
      .

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jud_Strunk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Born Justin Strunk, Jr. in
      <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_New_York" title="Jamestown, New York">
       Jamestown
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">
       New York
      </a>
      , he was raised in
      <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">
       Buffalo
      </a>
      , New York, where as a small boy his showmanship became evident. After he learned to play the
      <a href="/wiki/Banjo" title="Banjo">
       banjo
      </a>
      , Strunk began entertaining locals and went on to wide recognition after appearances on national
      <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">
       television
      </a>
      network shows such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Rowan_%26_Martin%27s_Laugh-In" title="Rowan &amp; Martin's Laugh-In">
        Rowan &amp; Martin's Laugh-In
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Johnny_Carson" title="The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson">
        The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      He graduated from the
      <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Military_Institute" title="Virginia Military Institute">
       Virginia Military Institute
      </a>
      in 1959 with a B.A in History.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Although much of Strunk's material was humorous, his most popular song was not. "Daisy A Day", which Strunk wrote and recorded in 1973, is a gentle, sentimental ballad in 3/4 time, describing the relationship between a boy and girl that ultimately grow old together. For every day of their lives, he gives her a daisy as a sign of their love. In the last verse, she has died, but her widower husband continues to make daily visits to her grave. The song made the
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" title="Billboard magazine">
        Billboard
       </a>
      </i>
      Top 20 on both the
      <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">
       country
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">
       pop music
      </a>
      charts. A cover version
      <i>
       Een Roosje, M'n Roosje
      </i>
      (A Rose, My Rose) by
      <a href="/wiki/Conny_Vandenbos" title="Conny Vandenbos">
       Conny Vandenbos
      </a>
      reached No. 7 on the Dutch Top 40 hit list in 1974.
     </p>
     <p>
      When Strunk performed this song on
      <i>
       Rowan &amp; Martin's Laugh-In
      </i>
      (seated on a stool and accompanying himself on banjo), a bizarre decision was made to film his performance on a set resembling a lunar landscape, apparently to remind the audience that this song had been played on the moon. Jud Strunk was a regular member of the
      <i>
       Laugh-In
      </i>
      cast during its last season; he often reported fictitious sporting events direct "from Farmington, Maine."
     </p>
     <p>
      Strunk also wrote three humorous songs that made it into the country music charts, and he toured with the
      <a href="/wiki/Andy_Williams" title="Andy Williams">
       Andy Williams Road Show
      </a>
      . One of these songs, "The Biggest Parakeets in Town," was a tongue-in-cheek story of a woman who is a bird fancier. Its central joke is the unspoken pun of "parakeets/pair o'
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breasts" title="Breasts">
       tits
      </a>
      " used in the title. Other singles, such as "Next Door Neighbor's Kid" and the patriotic "My Country," appeared on various Billboard surveys. Strunk also scored a songwriting hit with Bill Jones' General Store  [
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">
        sic
       </a>
      </i>
      ], the title track of his 1971 album of a similar name; Canadian musician
      <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hunter" title="Tommy Hunter">
       Tommy Hunter
      </a>
      charted in the top 20 on the country and adult contemporary charts in Canada with the song.
     </p>
     <p>
      Strunk became a folk hero in
      <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">
       Maine
      </a>
      , and in 1970 narrowly lost the election for Senate seat in the
      <a href="/wiki/State_legislature_(United_States)" title="State legislature (United States)">
       state legislature
      </a>
      . He was also a private pilot and purchased a 1941
      <a href="/wiki/Fairchild_PT-19" title="Fairchild PT-19">
       Fairchild M62-A
      </a>
      . On 5 October 1981,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      he suffered a
      <a href="/wiki/Myocardial_infarction" title="Myocardial infarction">
       heart attack
      </a>
      while taking off in the aircraft at Carrabassett Valley Airport in Maine and was killed instantly along with passenger, local businessman Dick Ayotte. He was 45 years old.
     </p>
     <p>
      Jud's sons contribute to the Sugarloaf Community, with Jud's grandson performing onstage at The Rack on Sunday afternoons.
     </p>
