     <h1>
      Messi top of Barca hotshots after hat-trick
     </h1>
     <div class="share-buttons clearfix">
      <div>
       <div class="addthis_sharing_toolbox">
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="byline">
      Wednesday, March 21, 2012
     </div>
     <div class="generic-text">
      <!-- C-ON-TEXT_CONTENT_START -->
      <div class="ctx_content">
       <p>
        Lionel Messi last night broke Barcelonas all-time goalscoring record by netting a hat-trick in the clubs 5-3 Primera Division win over with Granada at the Nou Camp.
       </p>
       <div class="story-image">
        <img height="160" itemprop="image" src="http://www.irishexaminer.com/media/images/l/LionelMessicelebrates.jpg" width="300"/>
       </div>
       <p>
        Messi equalled the mark of 232 official goals set by Cesar Rodriguez during the 1940s and 50s when he slotted home Isaac Cuencas cross in the 17th minute. The Argentina international secured the record outright in the 68th minute when he lobbed the ball over Granada goalkeeper Julio Cesar.
       </p>
       <p>
        The 24-year-old then completed his hat-trick  his eighth of the season for Barca  in the 86th minute after rounding Julio Cesar.
       </p>
       <p>
        The three-time FIFA World Player of the Year has now scored an incredible 48 goals in 40 games for the Catalan giants this season.
       </p>
       <p>
        It was initially thought Rodriguez had scored 235 official goals for the Blaugrana, but Barca announced yesterday morning that the figure had been amended to 232 following a joint study by the club and La Vanguardia newspaper.
       </p>
       <p>
