         <div style="clear: both">
         </div>
         <p>
          They say experiences and memories are the only things that could never be taken away from you. Well, I beg to differ 
          <span id="more-7613">
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_7614" style="width: 560px">
          <a href="http://voiceboks.com/the-inevitable-reality-of-age">
           <img alt="grandmother-puzzles" class="wp-image-7614 size-full" height="567" src="http://voiceboks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/grandmother-puzzles.jpg" width="550"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Doctors order  puzzles help exercise the brain
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          For the past few days, Ive had the chance to spend time with my dear old grandmother. Im sure most of you have wonderful stories to tell about your grandparents. As do I.
         </p>
         <p>
          My grandparents used to take care of my brothers and I while my parents went off to work. Just as my parents do the same for my nieces now. I see many similar patterns, which has gotten me thinking a lot about life and age recently.
         </p>
         <p>
          My grandmother is now in the early stages of Alzheimers. She struggles to remember many things, which makes it difficult to give her the independence she wants.
         </p>
         <p>
          I often see her sitting alone, staring into space, and then crying for reasons she doesnt understand. She seems to struggle with depression because she wants so badly to remember and sometimes even know where shes at, but cant. Its heartbreaking to watch this lady, who I love so dearly, forgetting about her life, bit by bit.
         </p>
         <p>
          I never used to worry about old age as I used to tell myself that Id just worry about it when it gets here. Now, I notice many of the complaints my parents used to have when they were my age and have started to creep up on me quite slowly. I am only 35 and still have a lot of life to live and things I want to do, but I cant help thinking (and actually scary myself with the thought) of the fact that no matter how much money we make or how much power anyone has, we live and age.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive been having many discussions with my husband about all the different things that could happen to us after we leave planet earth. All the things weve done and struggled to do to live for us, for our future generations, for the people we love, for what exactly?
         </p>
         <p>
          Would you mind sharing some of your thoughts about life, age, or the after life? No religious preaching please. Just soft thoughts
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://voiceboks.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>

            <p>
             Wow this is a deep question!
            </p>
            <p>
             I think a lot of mental health in old age (actually all ages) is related to our outlook on life.  As an example, my father-in-law has early stages of autismnot that it is noticeable or there are any outward signs.  Hes got no regrets about his life and how its turned out, and hes a very happy chap!
             <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://voiceboks.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             On the other hand, my mother-in-law is always looking back at her life.  She feels she had a tough childhood, that she lost freedom when she got married, lost freedom when she had children, and loads of terrible other things besides.  She feels her useful life is over and is so hell-bent on hanging onto the past that she cant enjoy the presentor think about the future.  Tragically, her outlook is full of resentment and bitterness and it really holds her back.
            </p>
            <p>
             On medical grounds, she has more marbles remaining and better physical health than her husband, but her mental outlook is bringing her down and shes older than her years.
            </p>
            <p>
             Whilst sticking heads in sand isnt a solution, there is a lot to be said about our outlook on life!
            </p>
            <p>
             In the end it seems to be a bit of the half full / half empty glass of wateralthough Im sure I read somewhere (really cant remember where) that it is usually the pessimists who have a more realistic view on lifeeven if it is less desirable.
            </p>
            <p>
             It must be really difficult for your grandmother, and I really wish her, and you all the very best.
            </p>
