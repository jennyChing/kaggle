      </a>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=LSU_Lady_Tigers_soccer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The LSU Tigers soccer team's first season was in 1995. The Tigers' first coach was
      <a href="/wiki/Miriam_Hickey" title="Miriam Hickey">
       Miriam Hickey
      </a>
      (1995-1996) who compiled a record of 22-17-1 (.563) at LSU. The second coach in LSU soccer history was
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gregg_Boggs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gregg Boggs (page does not exist)">
       Gregg Boggs
      </a>
      (1997-1999). He coached the Tigers to a record of 12-44-3 (.229). In 2000,
      <a href="/wiki/George_Fotopoulos" title="George Fotopoulos">
       George Fotopoulos
      </a>
      (2000-2004) was hired as head coach of the LSU soccer team and amassed a record of 52-39-8-49 (.566) during his 5 years at LSU. During George Fotopoulos' final season in 2004, his wife Danielle Fotopoulos was hired as co-head coach of the LSU soccer team. During her only season at LSU, she along with her husband compiled a record of 8-11-1 (.425) in 2004. The Tigers brought in a new head coach for the 1981 season.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2005,
      <a href="/wiki/Brian_Lee_(soccer)" title="Brian Lee (soccer)">
       Brian Lee
      </a>
      (2005-Present) was named head soccer coach at LSU. During his tenure the Tigers have compiled a record of 102-69-33 (.581) and have won 4 SEC West Division titles in 2007, 2008, 2009 and 2011.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="LSU_Soccer_Stadium">
       LSU Soccer Stadium
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=LSU_Lady_Tigers_soccer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: LSU Soccer Stadium">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/LSU_Soccer_Stadium" title="LSU Soccer Stadium">
       LSU Soccer Stadium
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       LSU Soccer Stadium
      </b>
      is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">
       soccer
      </a>
      facility located on the campus of
      <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_State_University" title="Louisiana State University">
       Louisiana State University
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge,_Louisiana" title="Baton Rouge, Louisiana">
       Baton Rouge
      </a>
      . The facility, built in 1996, serves as the home of the LSU Tigers soccer team. The two-level
      <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">
       stadium
      </a>
      has a seating capacity of 2,197.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_Training_facilities">
       Practice and Training facilities
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=LSU_Lady_Tigers_soccer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Practice and Training facilities">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="LSU_Indoor_Practice_facility">
       LSU Indoor Practice facility
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=LSU_Lady_Tigers_soccer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: LSU Indoor Practice facility">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/LSU_Indoor_Practice_Facility" title="LSU Indoor Practice Facility">
       LSU Indoor Practice Facility
      </a>
      , built in 1991, is a climate-controlled 8,250 square feet facility. It is used when inclement weather prevents the soccer team from practicing at the
      <a href="/wiki/LSU_Soccer_Stadium" title="LSU Soccer Stadium">
       LSU Soccer Stadium
      </a>
      . It holds the 100-yd Anderson-Feazel LSU Indoor field. The playing surface is
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sportexe_Momentum_Turf" title="Sportexe Momentum Turf">
       Momentum Field Turf
      </a>
      by SportExe.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Strength_and_Conditioning_facility">
       Strength and Conditioning facility
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=LSU_Lady_Tigers_soccer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Strength and Conditioning facility">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The LSU Tigers soccer team
      <a href="/wiki/LSU_Football_Operations_Center" title="LSU Football Operations Center">
       weight room
      </a>
      is over 10,000 square feet
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and includes multi-purpose flat surface platform, bench, incline, squat and Olympic lifting stations along with dumbbell bench stations.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LSU_Weight_Room_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-LSU_Weight_Room-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is also equipped with medicine balls, hurdles, plyometric boxes, assorted speed and agility equipment, treadmills, stationary bikes and elliptical cross trainers. The weight room features multiple high-definition TV's for multimedia presentations. It is located in the
      <a href="/wiki/LSU_Football_Operations_Center" title="LSU Football Operations Center">
       LSU Football Operations Center
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Training_room">
       Training room
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
