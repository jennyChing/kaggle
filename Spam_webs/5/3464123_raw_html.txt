      (spelled as Gannon in the original Legend of Zelda), is a
      <a href="/wiki/Character_(arts)" title="Character (arts)">
       fictional character
      </a>
      and a commonly reoccurring antagonist of
      <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">
       Nintendo
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda" title="The Legend of Zelda">
       <i>
        The Legend of Zelda
       </i>
       series
      </a>
      . He is often the final
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boss_(video_games)" title="Boss (video games)">
       boss
      </a>
      of most games in the series. He was first given a back-story in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda:_A_Link_to_the_Past" title="The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past">
        A Link to the Past
       </a>
      </i>
      . The English version of the
      <i>
       A Link to the Past
      </i>
      instruction manual adds the surname
      <b>
       Dragmire
      </b>
      and the alias "Mandrag Ganon," which means "Ganon of the Enchanted Thieves".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ganon.27s_Name_1-0">

        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

     <p>
      In the games, the character alternates between two forms: a massive
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boar" title="Boar">
       boar
      </a>
      -like creature and a tall,
      <a href="/wiki/Red_hair" title="Red hair">
       redheaded
      </a>
      wizard. Ganondorf is the archenemy of
      <a href="/wiki/Link_(The_Legend_of_Zelda)" title="Link (The Legend of Zelda)">
       Link
      </a>
      and the leader of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gerudo" title="Gerudo">
       Gerudo
      </a>
      , a race of desert brigands, until he embarks on an evil quest to conquer the kingdom of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyrule" title="Hyrule">
       Hyrule
      </a>
      , and seeks godlike power to facilitate this.

      His specific motives vary from game to game, but most often they include capturing
      <a href="/wiki/Princess_Zelda" title="Princess Zelda">
       Princess Zelda
      </a>
      and planning to achieve the domination of Hyrule (and presumably the world beyond it). To this end, he seeks the full
      <a href="/wiki/Triforce" title="Triforce">
       Triforce
      </a>
      , a powerful magical relic, which will grant whatever wish the bearer desires, and usually manipulates several other villains to accomplish his plans and ambitions.
     </p>
     <p>
      In most games he already possesses the Triforce of Power, which gives him godlike strength and boundless mystical power and makes him invulnerable to all but the most powerful holy weapons such as the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_Sword" title="Master Sword">
       Master Sword
      </a>
      . However, Ganondorf's lust for power can never be satisfied, and he seeks the complete relic to grant his wish of
      <a href="/wiki/World_government" title="World government">
       world domination
      </a>
      . The character has been very well received by critics and fans alike, becoming one of the most popular and recognizable villains in gaming.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Character_design">
       Character design
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ganon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Character design">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Ganon was originally known as "Hakkai" during development of the
      <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Nintendo Entertainment System">
       Nintendo Entertainment System
      </a>
      video game
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda_(video_game)" title="The Legend of Zelda (video game)">
        The Legend of Zelda
       </a>
      </i>
      , in reference to a humanoid pig character known as
      <a href="/wiki/Zhu_Bajie" title="Zhu Bajie">
       Zhu Bajie
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       Cho Hakkai
      </i>
      in Japanese) from the famed 16th-century Chinese novel
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Journey_to_the_West" title="Journey to the West">
        Journey to the West
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      For the development of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda:_Ocarina_of_Time" title="The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time">
        Ocarina of Time
       </a>
      </i>
      , Ganon was conceived by character designer Satoru Takizawa. Takizawa had imagined Ganon as a "crooked and complex thief, who was basically an all around abominable human being".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, script director Toru Osawa claimed that this image was "not the case". He began to speak of how Ganondorf was meant to have "parts where he is rather good", comparing him to the character of
      <a href="/wiki/Raoh" title="Raoh">
       Raoh
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Fist_of_the_North_Star" title="Fist of the North Star">
        Fist of the North Star
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ocarina_Interview_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ocarina_Interview-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      With this idea in mind, Takizawa created a tentative model of Ganondorf based on actor
      <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Lambert" title="Christopher Lambert">
       Christopher Lambert
      </a>
      . The end result of Ganondorf was very different from this model, with three forms of the character being created: one of Ganondorf in the beginning; one of Ganondorf seven years later, with long hair; and one of Ganon in the end.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ocarina_Interview_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ocarina_Interview-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Since Ganon was a pig-like creature, Takizawa decided that Ganondorf should transform into such at the end of the game, despite the opinions of other staff members. He decided on making Ganon a beast "with the feeling of a pig" to reference

     <p>
      Ganon is depicted as the ultimate embodiment of pure evil and hatred. His roles range from savage beast to scheming tyrant to godlike entity, sometimes in the course of a single game. The book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda:_Hyrule_Historia" title="The Legend of Zelda: Hyrule Historia">
        Hyrule Historia
       </a>
      </i>
      reveals that Ganon's hatred is so intense that he can survive even the total obliteration of his body and remain conscious.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Despite these negative traits, he does show some knowledge of the fine arts, since he can play his own
      <a href="/wiki/Leitmotif" title="Leitmotif">
       leitmotif
      </a>
      on an organ in
      <i>
       Ocarina of Time
      </i>
      . Ganondorf is incredibly arrogant, believing himself to be completely invulnerable. Because of this he never considers anything to be a threat and appears to put minimal effort into fighting. Unsurprisingly, Ganondorf is often in total disbelief when he is defeated, once even bursting into laughter from having been outsmarted at the last second. He is not completely beyond sympathy, though. In
      <i>
       The Wind Waker
      </i>
      , Ganon admits his motives were once good, claiming to have sought the Triforce to allow the Gerudo Valley relief from its scorching desert wind. Zelda also expresses pity for him in
      <i>
       Ocarina of Time
      </i>
      , for trying to steal the Triforce when its power was too great for him to control.
     </p>
     <p>
      He is portrayed in
      <i>
       Ocarina of Time
      </i>
      as an ambitious, cunning manipulator, gaining the king's trust before betraying him and tricking Link into opening the Door of Time, which allows him to access the Triforce. In
      <i>
       Twilight Princess
      </i>
      , Ganondorf is stated to have been known as a demon thief and an evil magic wielder renowned for his ruthlessness. When he obtains the Triforce of Power, then he develops a conscienceless
      <a href="/wiki/God_complex" title="God complex">
       god-complex
      </a>
      , believing himself to have been chosen by the goddesses who created Hyrule to be its ruler. In
      <i>
       Wind Waker
      </i>
      , Ganon is much older and prepares for the circumstances that have defeated him before, ordering the capture of girls thought to be Zelda and depowering the Master Sword so that it cannot be used against him.
     </p>

      <a href="/wiki/Magician_(fantasy)" title="Magician (fantasy)">

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transvection_(flying)" title="Transvection (flying)">

     <p>
      Ganon has appeared in most games in the series, some of which hide his existence until late in the game. Ganon made his first appearance in
      <i>
       The Legend of Zelda
      </i>
      . Ganon invades Hyrule with his minions, stealing the Triforce of Power. To protect the world from Ganon,
      <a href="/wiki/Princess_Zelda" title="Princess Zelda">
       Princess Zelda
      </a>
      breaks the
      <a href="/wiki/Triforce#Triforce_of_Wisdom" title="Triforce">
       Triforce of Wisdom
      </a>
      into eight pieces and scatters them across the land, but is then captured. She sends her nursemaid, Impa, to find someone to defeat Ganon. Impa discovers
      <a href="/wiki/Link_(The_Legend_of_Zelda)" title="Link (The Legend of Zelda)">
       Link
      </a>
      . He gathers the Triforce pieces and defeats Ganon, reducing him to a pile of ashes and recovering the Triforce of Power. Afterwards, he brings both the Triforces of Power and Wisdom to Zelda. Ganon makes no major appearance in the sequel
      <i>
       Zelda II: The Adventure of Link
      </i>
      , as it focuses on his minions' attempt to revive him by killing Link and spilling Link's blood over Ganon's ashes. If they succeed, Ganon's silhouette appears on the Game Over screen accompanied by his laugh.
     </p>
     <p>
      He later appeared in the
      <a href="/wiki/Super_Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Super Nintendo Entertainment System">
       Super Nintendo Entertainment System
      </a>
      game
      <i>
       A Link to the Past
      </i>
      , trapped in a place called the Dark Worldformerly called the Sacred Land, before Ganon corrupted it. The game focuses on Ganon's attempt to escape from the Dark World and conquer the Light World, utilizing minions such as the wizard Agahnim to sacrifice the seven descendants of the sages who sealed him away, in order to break the seal. Zelda summons Link to rescue her, but she is captured and sent to the Dark World. Once Link defeats Agahnim, he is sent to the Dark World, where he rescues the seven maidens, defeats Agahnim yet again, and pursues Ganon into his lair, where Ganon is eventually defeated. Link finds the completed Triforce, and uses it to undo everything Ganon has done. In
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda:_Link%27s_Awakening" title="The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening">
        Link's Awakening
       </a>
      </i>
      , Link battles Dethl, a physical manifestation of the Wind Fish's nightmares. During the final battle, Dethl assumes several forms, all of which are based on creatures from Link's past. One of Dethl's forms is "Ganon's Shadow" and is constructed from Link's memories of his battle with Ganon in
      <i>
       A Link to the Past
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      He makes a major appearance in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda:_Ocarina_of_Time" title="The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time">

However, the Zelda of the game asks Link to take the Master Sword, a mystical weapon used to defeat evil. After Link finds all the items necessary to take the sword, Zelda and her nursemaid Impa are chased from Hyrule Castle. Once Link opens the door protecting the Master Sword and takes it, he falls into a deep sleep, allowing Ganondorf to steal the Triforce of Power from the room. Seven years later, Link awakens as an adult, and finds Hyrule corrupted by Ganondorf. Link collects the power of the seven sagesincluding Zelda, who is kidnapped after she reveals herself. He encounters Ganondorf, defeating him and escaping from the castle with Zelda. However, Ganondorf uses the Triforce of Power to turn into his bestial form. Link defeats him with the help of Zelda and the other sages, and Ganon is banished to the Sacred Realm, swearing vengeance against Zelda, the Sages and Link. Ganon does not make an appearance in
      <i>

Becoming Yuga-Ganon, Yuga reveals plans to complete the Triforce once again and remake both Lorule and Hyrule in his own image, but is defeated in this by Link. Ganon is also mentioned by several characters and old legends, which describe his actions during the events of
      <i>
       A Link to the Past
      </i>
      , and his prior invasion between the events of
      <i>
       Link's Awakening
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       A Link Between Worlds
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Ganondorf appears as a playable character in the
      <i>
       Legend of Zelda
      </i>
      spin-off title
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Hyrule_Warriors" title="Hyrule Warriors">
        Hyrule Warriors
       </a>
      </i>
      , where he is the one who corrupted the sorceress Cia in order to have her revive him. Though sealed by the witch, Ganondorf is released upon her death and leads a campaign composing of himself and the summoned Ghirahim and Zant to gain the Triforce.
     </p>

      <a href="/wiki/Underworld" title="Underworld">
       Underworld
      </a>
