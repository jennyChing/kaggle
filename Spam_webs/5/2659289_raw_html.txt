     <p>
      <b>
       Lopburi
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">
       Thai
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
       
      </span>
      ) (
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.forvo.com/word/lopburi" rel="nofollow">
       Pronunciation
      </a>
      ) is the capital city of
      <a href="/wiki/Lopburi_Province" title="Lopburi Province">
       Lopburi Province
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">
       Thailand
      </a>
      . It is about 150 kilometres (93mi) northeast of
      <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">
       Bangkok
      </a>
      . As of 2006 it had a population of 26,500. The town (
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thesaban_mueang" title="Thesaban mueang">
        thesaban mueang
       </a>
      </i>
      ) covers the whole
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tambon" title="Tambon">
        tambon
       </a>
      </i>
      Tha Hin and parts of Thale Chup Son of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphoe_Mueang_Lopburi" title="Amphoe Mueang Lopburi">
       Mueang Lopburi district
      </a>
      , a total area of 6.85km. Lopburi lies 138 km north of Bangkok.
     </p>

      <a href="/wiki/Lavo_Kingdom" title="Lavo Kingdom">
       Lavo
      </a>
      or Lavapura, meaning "city of
      <a href="/wiki/Lava_(Ramayana)" title="Lava (Ramayana)">
       Lava
      </a>
      " in reference to the ancient South Asian city of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lavapuri" title="Lavapuri">
       Lavapuri
      </a>
      (present-day
      <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">
       Lahore
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lopburi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The city has a history dating back to the
      <a href="/wiki/Dvaravati" title="Dvaravati">
       Dvaravati
      </a>
      period more than 1,000 years ago.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Higham_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Higham-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       :302,308
      </sup>
      According to the
      <i>
       Northern Chronicles,
      </i>
      it was founded by King Kalavarnadish (Thai, "Phraya Kalavarnadit"), who came from
      <a href="/wiki/Taxila" title="Taxila">
       Taxila
      </a>
      (Takkasila) in northwest India (now Pakistan) in 648 CE.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Lopburi, or Lavapura as it then was, was under the rule of the rising Angkor regime and became one of the most important centers in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chao_Phraya" title="Chao Phraya">
       Chao Phraya
      </a>
      Basin from then on.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Epigraphy" title="Epigraphy">
       Epigraphic evidence
      </a>
      indicates that the dominant population of the city was
      <a href="/wiki/Mon_people" title="Mon people">
       Mon
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The earliest confirmed occurrence of the name Lavapura is on silver coins inscribed "lava" on the obverse and "pura" on the reverse in a Pallava-derived script of the seventh or eighth century; several such coins were recovered in 1966 from a hoard found in an ancient jar in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U_Thong" title="U Thong">
       U Thong
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Lopburi (Lavo) is described in Book III of Marco Polos
      <i>
       Travels
      </i>
      , where it is called
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Locach" title="Locach">
        Locach
       </a>
      </i>
      . This came from the Chinese (Cantonese) pronunciation of Lavo, "Lo-huk".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The city is referred to as "Lo-ho" in chapter 20 of the
      <a href="/wiki/History_of_Yuan" title="History of Yuan">
       History of Yuan
      </a>
      (:
      <i>
       Yun Sh
      </i>
      ), the official history of the Mongol, or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" title="Yuan Dynasty">
       Yuan Dynasty
      </a>
      of China. Due to a scribal error in
      <a href="/wiki/The_Travels_of_Marco_Polo" title="The Travels of Marco Polo">
       Book III of Marco Polos travels
      </a>
      treating of the route southward from
      <a href="/wiki/Champa" title="Champa">
       Champa
      </a>
      , where the name Java was substituted for Champa as the point of departure, Java Minor was 1,300 miles to the south of Java Major, instead of from Champa, on or near an extension of the
      <a href="/wiki/Terra_Australis" title="Terra Australis">
       Terra Australis
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As explained by Sir Henry Yule, the editor of an English edition of Marco Polo's
      <i>
       Travels
      </i>
      : "Some geographers of the 16th century, following the old editions which carried the travellers south-east of Java to the land of
      <i>
       Boeach
      </i>
      (or Locac), introduced in their maps a continent in that situation".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After the foundation of the
      <a href="/wiki/Ayutthaya_Kingdom" title="Ayutthaya Kingdom">
       Ayutthaya Kingdom
      </a>
      in the 14th century, Lopburi was a stronghold of Ayutthaya's rulers. It became the capital of the kingdom during the reign of King
      <a href="/wiki/Narai" title="Narai">
       Narai
      </a>
      the Great in the mid-17th century and the king resided there about eight months a year.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
       Geography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lopburi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Lopburi lies on the
      <a href="/wiki/Lopburi_River" title="Lopburi River">
       Lopburi River
      </a>
      at an elevation of 20 metres (66ft) mostly surrounded by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alluvial_plains" title="Alluvial plains">
       alluvial plains
      </a>
      , although some hills rise to between 300 metres (980ft) and 600 metres (2,000ft) to the northeast.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">
       Climate
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lopburi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Lopburi has a
      <a href="/wiki/Tropical_savanna_climate" title="Tropical savanna climate">
       tropical savanna climate
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Kppen climate classification">
       Kppen climate classification
      </a>
      <i>
       Aw
      </i>
      ). Winters are dry and very warm. Temperatures rise until April, which is very hot with the average daily maximum at 36.8C (98.2F). The
      <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">
       monsoon
      </a>
      season runs from late April through October, with heavy rain and somewhat cooler temperatures during the day, although nights remain warm.
     </p>

        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The main road through Lobpuri is Route 1 (
      <a href="/wiki/Phahonyothin_Road" title="Phahonyothin Road">
       Phahonyothin Road
      </a>
      ), which starts in
      <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">
       Bangkok
      </a>
      , and continues through Lopburi,
      <a href="/wiki/Chai_Nat" title="Chai Nat">
       Chai Nat
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nakhon_Sawan" title="Nakhon Sawan">
       Nakhon Sawan
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Kamphaeng_Phet" title="Kamphaeng Phet">
       Kamphaeng Phet
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tak_(town)" title="Tak (town)">
       Tak
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Lampang" title="Lampang">
       Lampang
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiang_Rai" title="Chiang Rai">
       Chiang Rai
      </a>
      , and the border with
      <a href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">
       Burma
      </a>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/Mae_Sai_District" title="Mae Sai District">
       Mae Sai
      </a>
      . Route 311 leads west to
      <a href="/wiki/Sing_Buri" title="Sing Buri">
       Sing Buri
      </a>
      , and Route 3196 leads southwest to
      <a href="/wiki/Ang_Thong" title="Ang Thong">
       Ang Thong
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Lopburi is a station of the
      <a href="/wiki/State_Railway_of_Thailand" title="State Railway of Thailand">
       State Railway of Thailand
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/State_Railway_of_Thailand#Northern_Line" title="State Railway of Thailand">
       Northern Line
      </a>
      , forming the end of
      <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">
       Bangkok
      </a>
      's suburban service.
     </p>
     <p>
      Lopburi is served by the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Khok_Kathiam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Khok Kathiam (page does not exist)">
       Khok Kathiam
      </a>
      airport, 9 kilometres (5.6mi) north of the town.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">
       Culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lopburi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Today the city is best known for the hundreds of
      <a href="/wiki/Crab-eating_macaque" title="Crab-eating macaque">
       crab-eating macaques
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       Macaca fascicularis
      </i>
      ) that live in the middle of the city, especially around the
      <a href="/wiki/Khmer_Empire" title="Khmer Empire">
       Khmer
      </a>
      temple,
      <i>
       Prang Sam Yot
      </i>
      and a Khmer shrine,
      <i>
       Sarn Phra Karn
      </i>
      . They are fed by the local people, especially during the
      <a href="/wiki/Monkey_Buffet_Festival" title="Monkey Buffet Festival">
       Monkey Festival
      </a>
      in November. Because they are not afraid of humans, they steal whatever items or food they can find from unwary visitors.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the city signs are posted reading:
      <i>
       To prevent monkeys attacking people, the officer will feed monkeys in 3 designated areas outside San Phrakan twice a day, at 10am and 4pm. Those who want to feed monkeys other than these times, please contact the officer or caretaker
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">
       Religion
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lopburi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Religion">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Prang Sam Yot
      </i>
      , originally a
      <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">
       Hindu
      </a>
      shrine, has three
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Prang_(architecture)" title="Prang (architecture)">
        prangs
       </a>
      </i>
      that represent
      <a href="/wiki/Brahma" title="Brahma">
       Brahma
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Vishnu" title="Vishnu">
       Vishnu
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Shiva" title="Shiva">
       Shiva
      </a>
      (the Hindu trinity). It was later converted to a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddhist" title="Buddhist">
       Buddhist
      </a>
      shrine.
     </p>
