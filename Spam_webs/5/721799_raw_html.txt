      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       A Brief Look At Upcoming Indie Co-Op RPG 'Legends Of Aethereus'
      </h1>
      <!-- Make individual group author appear -->
      <div class="mninst">
      </div>
      <div class="comment_links">
       <a class="scroll_to post_your_comment" href="#comment_reply">
        Comment Now
       </a>
       <div class="comments_follow followable_block">
        <div class="followable_block initializing" data-object-json="{&quot;title&quot;:&quot;A Brief Look At Upcoming Indie Co-Op RPG 'Legends Of Aethereus'&quot;,&quot;avatar_src&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;follow_count&quot;:&quot;&quot;}" data-target-id="blogAndPostId/blog/post/1360-10628" data-target-name="" data-target-type="article" data-tracking-link-name="articleLink_ArticleCommentsFollow" data-type-advoice="false">
         <div class="follow_unireg">

     <div class="article_body article" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         <a href="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/erikkain/files/2012/08/legend-of-aethereus.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10630" height="360" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/erikkain/files/2012/08/legend-of-aethereus.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.threegates.se">
          Three Gates,
         </a>
         the Swedish studio behind upcoming co-op RPG
         <em>
          Legends of Aethereus,
         </em>
         has had a successful Kickstarter drive, topping their $25,000 goal by just over $10,000 this past July.
        </p>
        <p>
         Before that, the team worked without pay, volunteering their time for a year until the game was close enough to justify a Kickstarter drive. The crowd-funding will allow them to polish the final product and get it launched, but the vast majority of game development occurred prior to the Kickstarter drive.
        </p>
        <p>
         I think thats a good sign, both for the game and for those who backed the project.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im also intrigued by the concept of the game itself.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can play solo or in co-op mode. Each player has their own Nexus City State with a PvP/PvE arena they can populate with their own monsters (that players capture themselves) where you can engage in combat with other players. You can also go on procedurally generated quests on your own or with friends.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Aethereus
         </em>
         isdescribed as an in-depth, low-fantasy, high action-RPG, with lots of customization and crafting elements.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-10628">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Over the last few years the trend in the gaming industry has been to remove complexity and intricacies from their products. In short, a lot of triple decker banana splits with all the trimmings have turned into bland vanilla syntheto-mush, the developer
         <a href="http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/460738485/legends-of-aethereus-co-op-hack-n-slash-action-gam">
          writes on their Kickstarter page
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         We realize game companies do this because they want their products to be accessible to the broadest mainstream audience possible. This however has come at a cost. Weve seen many disappointed gamers as we watch beloved titles get watered down or console-adapted to the point where the gaming experience is no longer appealing to the original fans.
        </p>
        <p>
         We believe that the core Gaming audience is really hungry for a game that can deliver a deep experience, interesting choices, and more control over how you look as an individual, as well as how you play the game. Were committed to delivering quality games that include these elements.
        </p>
        <p>
         All of which sounds great. Three Gates is obviously tapping into the discontent thats brewing in the gaming community. Hopefully the game itself will live up to the promise.
        </p>
        <p>
         And, of course, Im always happy to hear of a new low-fantasy RPG.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres the Kickstarter trailer:
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="365" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/tTrg_k6jjgQ" type="text/html" width="485">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         <!--donotpaginate-->
         <br/>
         I like the fact that alongside more Medieval choices you have some blackpowder guns and can lob bombs and javelins at enemies (though the boulder tossing is a bit much.)
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres a barrel roll option for defense, which is nice. While Ive seen some shields I cant tell whether theyre particularly necessary or not.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Legends of Aethereus
         </em>
         looks promising, all told, and Im glad to see that Three Gates didnt jump headfirst onto the MMORPG bandwagon. Hopefully the gameplay and questing reflects that choice and isnt just an endless series of fetch-quests and grinding.
        </p>
        <p>
         Three Gates will offer a DRM Free version with single player, LAN, and direct ip connect. So youll be able to play and enjoy Legends of Aethereus in a stand alone version free from DRM. So thats a good sign, too.
        </p>
