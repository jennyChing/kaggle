         <p>
          WOMEN are having 4,500 quick-fix breast implant operations during their lunch breaks.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          They have been started by a clinic in Cheshire. The hour-long surgery involves the patient being given a local anaesthetic so they are awake throughout the procedure.
         </p>
         <p>
          Cheshire student Jessica Kirkham was one of the first to undergo the operation - a 21st birthday gift from her mum which was also part-funded by a student loan.
         </p>
         <p>
          She said: "You have to spend two weeks recovering from the traditional general anaesthetic operation and I couldn't afford time off, so I decided on this instead.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "I was awake but you are on such a high you don't feel pain or anything.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "Then I put my clothes on and went home. Although it did start to hurt when the anaesthetic wore off, I was out shopping a couple of days later."
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Jessica said she had wanted the operation since the age of 18. She said: "Having small breasts doesn't bother some people but it bothered me.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "We live in a visual culture and my confidence was really affected. You could really tell the difference in a bikini. There are lots of types I couldn't wear.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Saggy
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "My mum said they were saggy and I could have the operation for my birthday if I wanted. The surgeon told me it looked as though I had breast-fed two children.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "The gel silicone implants I chose are really natural and I have gone up to a size 34D.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "You only live once so you might as well. I would like to try being a TV presenter if I got the chance."
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Conventional surgery involves an overnight stay in hospital. The high-speed ops have been launched by the Medispa Clinic in Adlington, near Poynton, Cheshire. Clinic bosses maintain they are responding to a huge demand for cosmetic surgery in the region.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Owner Carl Lewis said: "This has totally revolutionised breast enlargement surgery. The recovery time is amazing. You walk into hospital and within hours you are walking out.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "Professional women haven't got time to spend lying in hospital and are turning away from general anaesthetic.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "In years to come the lunch time boob job will be happening across the industry.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "The total amount of time spent at our clinic from walking in to walking out is four hours."
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Demand
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Operations' director Victoria Clark said. "We have seen a huge leap in the demand for this kind of same day surgery as there is no general anaesthetic, the recovery time is shorter and there is less bruising and bleeding."
          <br/>
          <br/>
          But David Whitby, consultant plastic surgeon at Wythenshawe Hospital and Bupa in Whalley Range, who is a member of the British Association of Aesthetic Plastic Surgeons, expressed concerns about the speedy operations.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          He said: "The question really is what are the benefits for the patient?
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "Local anaesthetic is not completely risk-free because you are still using drugs. It certainly works for some kind of procedures but even if we did patients under local anaesthetic I am not sure I would discharge them straight away.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "If you get up and go home straight away your blood pressure goes up and you have more risk of bleeding. I would have hesitation in thinking that is the ideal scenario.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Quicker
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "It's not advisable to be back at work the same day, for instance. I would say there's no benefit to having surgery quicker.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "Having breast implants is not like picking clothes and changing your mind when you get home."
         </p>
         <p>
          Manchester has been dubbed the "boob job capital" by the country's largest private cosmetic surgery provider, Transform Medical Group, which has clinics across Greater Manchester and Cheshire.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          A spokesman said: "Breast augmentation procedures are still the most popular procedure, accounting for the largest percentage of all operations, currently standing at about 57 per cent.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          "Manchester is the breast surgery capital of the country with over 60 per cent of all operations carried out in the region being breast augmentations."
