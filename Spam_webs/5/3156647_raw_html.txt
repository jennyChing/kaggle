      <h1 id="headline">
       EXCLUSIVE: Documents show Calgary police considered anti-Israel riot a public relations problem, not a crime
      </h1>
      <h6 id="author">
       <a href="/ezralevant">
        Ezra Levant
       </a>
       <span>
        Rebel Commander
       </span>
      </h6>
     </div>
     <div id="content">
      <p>
       I filed an Access to Information request to investigate police indifference to last July's anti-Israel riot on the steps of Calgary City Hall. Here's what I discovered.
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="embedly-embed" frameborder="0" height="338" scrolling="no" src="//cdn.embedly.com/widgets/media.html?src=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.youtube.com%2Fembed%2FUuVjaiBkF28%3Fwmode%3Dtransparent%26feature%3Doembed&amp;wmode=transparent&amp;url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.youtube.com%2Fwatch%3Fv%3DUuVjaiBkF28&amp;image=http%3A%2F%2Fi.ytimg.com%2Fvi%2FUuVjaiBkF28%2Fhqdefault.jpg&amp;key=e1208cbfb854483e8443b1ed081912ee&amp;type=text%2Fhtml&amp;schema=youtube" width="600">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <p>
       These documents show that Calgary police were preoccupied with the public relations nightmare my Sun News Network investigation into this crime was creating for them.
      </p>
      <p>
       However, they didn't seem quite as concerned that a Jewish family had been assaulted in broad daylight in their jurisdiction.
      </p>
      <p>
       In fact, in one of these documents, that family is referred to as "victims" -- complete with quotation marks.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.therebel.media/calling_all_anti_israel_thugs_calgary_just_pay_fine_and_write_essay">
        <strong>
         As we reported yesterday
        </strong>
       </a>
       , three of the pro-Hamas rioters were finally sentenced to pay a small fine -- and write an essay.
      </p>
      <p>
       Today's revelations compound the injustice that occurred last July -- not in Gaza, but right here in Canada.
      </p>
