           <h2>
            Playing with filters: How to give your photos a touch of magic
           </h2>
           <p>
            Filters can make even the most amateur photography look fabulous. FatMumSlim's Chantelle Ellem shares her favourite photography filters and tips to make them really work well for you.
           </p>
           <div class="no-print" id="social">
            <div class="row collapse">
             <div class="mobile-twelve twelve columns">
              <div class="share-tools-bar-small">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div id="meta">
            <div class="row">
             <div class="twelve columns mobile-twelve">
              <figure>
               <img alt="Playing with filters: How to give your photos a touch of magic" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/olympus-600x420.jpg"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </header>
          <section class="text">
           <p>
            If you own a smart phone, you probably know about filters already. Let me explain them to you quickly anyway. In my terms, a filter makes things look better. It makes colours pop, hides a multitude of sins (wrinkles!) if youre taking a selfie and just makes photos more beautiful.
           </p>
           <p>
            Depending on which phone you have and which apps you have access to, you can play forever with filters. Seriously, you could. Its such a pretty time waster. Im going to take you through my favourite filters for your phone and on your computer, too. And some dont even cost a cent. THAT we love!
           </p>
           <div class="advertisement" id="mrec-mobile">
           </div>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/sheye-littlemoments.jpg">
             <img alt="sheye-littlemoments" height="673" src="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/sheye-littlemoments.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
            <br/>
            <em>
             Left: Straight out of camera / Right: Little Moments app (Sheye filter)
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            OK, Im predictable and I know it. A month and a bit ago I released an app called
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/au/app/little-moments-by-fat-mum-slim/id869271458?mt=8" target="_blank">
             Little Moments
            </a>
            , and inside are some filters that I helped create and picked names for (it was like naming all the babies at once, fun!). I also had some talented photographers create some filters, too. The one on the right is the Sheye filter, by
            <a href="http://www.sheyerosemeyerphotography.com/blog/" target="_blank">
             Sheye Rosemeyer
            </a>
            . Its a bit dreamy and lovely. Notice how the colours of the water change? It also adds a little definition to the branches. Filters do magic stuff, I tell you.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/PicMonkey.jpg">
             <img alt="PicMonkey" height="668" src="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/PicMonkey.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
            <br/>
            <em>
             Left: Straight out of camera / Right: Picmonkey.com (Orton filter)
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            Now here comes that free part I talked about, because we love FREE.
            <a href="http://www.picmonkey.com/" target="_blank">
             Picmonkey.com
            </a>
            is an online program where you can add text to photos, edit them and make collages, too. With this photo I ran the Orton filter over it. You can adjust the brightness and fade the filter as well.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/olympus.jpg">
             <img alt="olympus" height="660" src="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/olympus.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <em>
             Left: Straight out of camera / Right: Olympus edit feature (bumping up the saturation a bit)
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            If youre lucky enough to have the Olympus O-MD E-M10 you can edit in the camera. So you can take all your photos and then play around with them afterwards. With the photo above Ive bumped up the saturation but you can change it to black and white or sepia and adjust the shadows and size of the photo, too. You can also play around with their art setting, which I talked about in
            <a href="http://www.kidspot.com.au/photography-fun-three-projects-to-try-this-weekend/">
             this post
            </a>
            previously.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/mextures.jpg">
             <img alt="mextures" height="614" src="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/mextures.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <em>
             Left: Straight out of camera / Right: Mextures (various filters)
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.mextures.com/" target="_blank">
             Mextures
            </a>
            might seem slightly more complicated than other apps but it definitely gives you more control over your images. You can create your own filter in the app so all your photos have the same continuity and your own style. You just add filters one by one, adjust the strength and create your own personalised filter.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Paint-The-Moon.jpg">
             <img alt="Paint The Moon" height="697" src="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Paint-The-Moon.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <em>
             Left: Straight out of camera / Right: Paint The Moon action (A Little Zest &amp; Miracles)
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            If you have Photoshop, you can play around with actions (like filters). Photoshop can be expensive to buy, especially if youre not going to use it. I use it every day, but
            <a href="http://www.adobe.com/au/creativecloud.html" target="_blank">
             I just pay by the month
            </a>
            . You can
            <a href="https://www.adobe.com/creativecloud/catalog/desktop.html" target="_blank">
             try a 30-day trial
            </a>
            and see how that goes for you. Actions do just as filters do, but you can control them a little more. If you know how to use Photoshop you can really use them to your advantage. I dont know how to use Photoshop very well, so I play around and see what I like. Above Ive used the
            <a href="http://paintthemoon.net/blog/photoshop-actions-2/" target="_blank">
             Paint The Moon
            </a>
            set of actions and ran the A Little Zest and Miracles actions over the ice cream. You can see its given it a bit of a pop and more clarity.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/florabella.jpg">
             <img alt="florabella" height="697" src="http://fatmumslim.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/florabella.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <em>
             Left: Straight out of camera / Right: Florabella action (milk)
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            Using Photoshop and actions again, this time Ive used the
            <a href="http://www.florabellacollection.com/photoshop-actions.html" target="_blank">
             Florabella actions
            </a>
            andmilk. It adds a little light and dreaminess to the picture. You can tone it down if you think its too much.
           </p>
           <p>
            So there you go, this is just a handful of ways filters can change up your photos.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Do you have a favourite filter or action that you like to use on your photos?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <h4>
            More photography tips on Kidspot:

             <a href="http://www.kidspot.com.au/read-this-post-become-an-ace-photographer/">

             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             <a href="http://www.kidspot.com.au/photography-how-to-tell-a-story-through-your-photos/">
              Photography how-to: How to tell a story through photos
             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             <a href="http://www.kidspot.com.au/goodbye-smart-phone-hello-fancy-camera/">
              Goodbye smart phone. Hello fancy camera.
