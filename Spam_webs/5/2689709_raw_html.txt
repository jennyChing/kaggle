        <div class="entry-content clearfix">
         <p>
          I am a long time Civ player, and fan, but I had reached a point where I was getting a little tired of the Civ formula and had pretty well decided that Civ 5 would be my last purchase in the franchise.
          <span id="more-815">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          That was until I saw that
          <a class="easyazon-link" data-identifier="B00JLK6ULS" data-locale="US" data-tag="wine0634-20" href="http://www.amazon.com/Sid-Meiers-Civilization-Beyond-Online/dp/B00JLK6ULS?tag=wine0634-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           CIVILIZATION: BEYOND EARTH
          </a>
          was taking us into a Civ world that many players had been dreaming of since Civilization Alpha Centauri, myself included.
         </p>
         <p>
          Of course there has been the ridiculous backlash as expected, mainly centered around the price and the fact Firaxis has said that they are using the same engine as with Civ 5. This has lead to the inevitable whining about how people arent going to spend $50 on a reskinned Civ game.
         </p>
         <p>
          You know what  I dont give a shit. Seriously, if you think that other game companies dont reuse their engines with new graphics then you are an idiot. It happens all the time and there is absolutely nothing wrong with it.
         </p>
         <p>
          Personally I will be waiting with baited breath for the game to go live on Steam on October 24th, and I wont be whining one little bit about some stupid theyre ripping us off argument.
         </p>
