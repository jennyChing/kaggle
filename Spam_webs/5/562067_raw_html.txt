     <p>
      A
      <b>
       stampede
      </b>
      is an act of mass impulse among
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herd_animal" title="Herd animal">
       herd animals
      </a>
      or a crowd of people in which the herd (or crowd) collectively begins running with no clear direction or purpose.
     </p>
     <p>
      Species associated with stampede behavior include
      <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">
       cattle
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Elephant" title="Elephant">
       elephants
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Blue_wildebeest" title="Blue wildebeest">
       blue wildebeests
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walruses" title="Walruses">
       walruses
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Wild_horse" title="Wild horse">
       wild horses
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Rhinoceros" title="Rhinoceros">
       rhinoceros
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">
       humans
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cattle_stampedes">
       Cattle stampedes
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stampede&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cattle stampedes">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Anything unusual may start a stampede among cattle. Especially at night, things such as lighting a match, someone jumping off a horse, a horse shaking itself, a lightning strike, a tumbleweed blown into the herd, or "a horse running through a herd kicking at a saddle which has turned under its belly" have been known to cause stampedes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ward_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ward-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A large stampede typically eliminates everything in its path. With livestock,
      <a href="/wiki/Cowboy" title="Cowboy">
       cowboys
      </a>
      attempt to turn the moving herd into itself, so that it runs in circles rather than running off a cliff or into a river and avoids damaging human life or property. Tactics used to make the herd turn into itself include firing a pistol, which creates noise to make the leaders of the stampede turn.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ward_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ward-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Animals that stampede, especially cattle, are less likely to do so after having eaten and spread out in smaller groups to digest.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ward_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ward-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      To further reduce the risk of stampedes, cowboys sometimes sing or whistle to calm the herds disquieted by nightfall. Those on watch at night avoid doing things which could startle the herd and even distance themselves before dismounting a horse or lighting a match.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Sometimes people purposefully induce cattle to stampede as a component of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warfare" title="Warfare">
       warfare
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">
       hunting
      </a>
      , such as some
      <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">
       Native Americans
      </a>
      , who were known to cause
      <a href="/wiki/American_bison" title="American bison">
       American bison
      </a>
      to kill themselves at a
      <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_jump" title="Buffalo jump">
       buffalo jump
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Human_stampedes">
       Human stampedes
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stampede&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Human stampedes">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Human stampedes most often occur during religious pilgrimages
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Illiyas_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Illiyas-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and professional sporting and music events, as these events tend to involve a large number of people. They also occur in times of panic (e.g. as a result of a fire or explosion) as people try to get away. However, more common causes are when the crowd are trying to get
      <i>
       toward
      </i>
      something as in the former examples, because the crowd is so big that those in the back continue pushing forward not knowing that those in the front are being crushed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seabrook_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-seabrook-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">
       Causes
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stampede&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Causes">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Deaths from human stampedes occur primarily from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compressive_asphyxia" title="Compressive asphyxia">
       compressive asphyxiation
      </a>
      and not trampling.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fruin_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-fruin-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This is referred to as
      <b>
       crowd crush
      </b>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ripley_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ripley-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The compressive force occurs from both horizontal pushing and vertical stacking.
     </p>

     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main articles:
      <a href="/wiki/Crowd_control" title="Crowd control">
       Crowd control
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Riot_control" title="Riot control">
       Riot control
      </a>

     <p>
      It is believed that most major crowd disasters can be prevented by simple crowd management strategies.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fruin_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-fruin-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Human stampedes can be prevented by organization and traffic control, such as barriers. On the other hand, barriers in some cases may funnel the crowd towards an already-packed area, such as in the
      <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_disaster" title="Hillsborough disaster">
       Hillsborough disaster
      </a>
      . Hence barriers can be a solution in preventing or a key factor in causing a stampede. One problem is lack of feedback from people being crushed to the crowd pressing behind  feedback can instead be provided by police, organizers, or other observers, particularly raised observers, such as on platforms or horseback, who can survey the crowd and use loudspeakers to communicate and direct a crowd.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ripley_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ripley-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      At the individual level, warning signs of a crowd crush include density of more than four people per square meter, at which each person is being touched on four sides. To avoid or escape from a crowd crush, one is advised to move sideways, particularly between swells.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ripley_6-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-ripley-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After the stampede in the
      <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Hall_disaster" title="Victoria Hall disaster">
       Victoria Hall disaster
      </a>
      in 1883, a law was passed in England which required all public entertainment venues to be equipped with doors that open outwards, for example using
      <a href="/wiki/Crash_bar" title="Crash bar">
       crash bar
      </a>
      latches that open when pushed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vicdisaster_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-vicdisaster-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Crash bars are required by various
      <a href="/wiki/Building_code" title="Building code">
       building codes
      </a>
      .
     </p>
