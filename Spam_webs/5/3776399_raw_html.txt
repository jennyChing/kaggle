        <em>
         The following is a creative non-fiction piece that I presentedat the
         <a href="http://www.clarke.edu/media/files/Academics/Departments/Language_and_Literature/Program%20Streamlines%20UD%202014P.pdf" target="_blank">
          Streamlines
         </a>
         Literary Conference last weekend. The piece is broken down into an intro followed by three short stories.
        </em>
        <h1 style="text-align: center;">
         Getting Old
        </h1>
        <p>
         Getting old is a weird process. The transition from adolescence to adulthood doesnt happen slowly over time. The aging process isnt a nice, gradually sloping continuum. Its more like a cliff.
        </p>
        <p>
         One day, youre worried about whether or not you have enough time to jerk off before your roommates get home, and the next, you get dropped out of a proverbial time machine with nothing but the clothes on your back and a stick that you found and whittled into a point to fend off coyotes.
        </p>
        <p>
         No one tells you how to be a functioning adult in todays world. There are no classes on choosing insurance policies. No professors to tell you how many dependents to claim on your tax return. No one tells you how to
         <em>
          not
         </em>
         royally fuck up your credit score. Instead, they just throw you out there like a gladiator to the lions and say, Whelp, youre on your own!
        </p>
        <p>
         I didnt think I would have to worry about any of that shit for a while. I thought I had at least another seven months to be an irresponsible jackass before Id need to face reality. But lately, its getting harder and harder to deny the truthIm old now.
         <span id="more-2042">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Fat
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         My stomach was fucking killing meit had been for weeks. It hurt on both sides, almost like my rib cage was stabbing me in the kidneys. I couldnt sleep at night. Laying down just made it worse. Did I have pancreatitis? Was I dying? Did I accidentally rib-cage myself on a park bench the last time I got blackout drunk? It was probably the latter.
        </p>
        <p>
         Okay, maybe Im being overdramatic. The pain actually wasnt that bad. It was more of a nuisance than anything. But still, I couldnt figure it out. What the hell was going on with me?
        </p>
        <p>
         Then one day, the pain disappeared.
        </p>
        <p>
         I remember it vividly. There I was, standing naked in front of the bathroom mirror, performing my daily pre-shower ritual.
        </p>
        <p>
         No, Im not talking about
         <em>
          that
         </em>
         . Get your mind out of the gutter, perverts.
        </p>
        <p>
         My ritual is pretty simple. I suck in my stomach, flex a couple times, snap my fingers, point at mirror, give myself a little Fonzie Ayyy, and then go about my business. Its my little way of convincing myself that I still got it, ya know?
        </p>
        <p>
         Only this time, I didnt still have it. I couldnt believe my eyes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Holy shit Im fat now.
        </p>
        <p>
         It all made senseit wasnt pancreatitis, it wasnt drunk Ryans fault. No, the pain Id been experiencing was the skin on my stomach stretching to meet the demands of the fat piece of shit that Im slowly becoming.
        </p>
        <p>
         Up until that moment, I had never been fat in my life. Ive always had a high metabolism. It never mattered how much I ate or how little I exercisedI never gained weight. Ive been a solid 160 pounds since my sophomore year of high school. Now all of a sudden Im blowing up like Tim Allen in
         <em>
          The Santa Clause
         </em>
         . The other day, I jumped down a small flight of stairs and I swear to God I felt my tits bounce. Since when do I have tits?!
        </p>
        <p>
         What the hell am I supposed to do now? Change my diet? I dont know the first thing about healthy eating. My diet for the past eight years has consisted almost entirely of DiGiorno pizza and Busch Light. To this day, I have no idea how to shop for healthy food. I hate grocery shopping. To me, HyVee is like that weird tunnel in
         <em>
          Willy Wonka and the Chocolate Factory
         </em>
         . You know, the one they float through on the boat, with all the weird subliminal messages and that terrifying music.
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyway, since eating healthy was out of the question, I thought about exercising. I actually did it, once. I went to the pool to swim laps for half an hour. Bad idea. I spent the next week feeling like Id been run over by a rhinoceros.
        </p>
        <p>
         So Im fat. I guess thats just going to be a thing from now on.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Little Caesars
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I think one of the signs Im getting old is that when I see high schoolersI HATE them. Like automatic, completely unwarranted hatred pumps through my veins. They dont even have to be doing anything wrong, either. They could be planting trees to save the planet or some shit and Id still want to bash them over the head with a folding table.
        </p>
        <p>
         I think my hatred stems from a sense of self-entitlement, to be honest. I feel like these kids
         <em>
          need
         </em>
         to respect me, because, well, Im older. Which is fucking stupid, but still. I thought high school kids would look up to me once I got to college. I thought theyd come up to me and be like, Dude, you live in your own house!? THATS AWESOME. You get drunk at college parties!? WHY CANT I BE YOU??? But no, high schoolers have no respect for anyone. They really are awful people. Ill give you an example.
        </p>
        <p>
         About a year ago, I walked into Little Caesars to buy a $5 pizza for me and my girlfriend at the time. On this day, for some reason, the entire staff was made up of high schoolers. Im talking no adult supervision here, just a God damn bonanza of high schoolers. The entire restaurant smelled like Axe Body Spray and weed.
        </p>
        <p>
         So I saunter over to the counter where Im (well call it) greeted by a sixteen year old girl in a blue uniform and visor. Greeted probably isnt the right word here. She mostly just stared at me with that
         <em>
          look
         </em>
         . You know, that Dont fucking talk to me right now look that every sixteen-year-old girls permanently dons.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ill have one hot-and-ready pizza, I said.
        </p>
        <p>
         She shot me a look like I just swallowed a goldfish whole.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ummm, we dont have any right now, she said. A long, awkward silence followed.
        </p>
        <p>
         So what the fuck? Like, are we done here? What happens next? Was she going to pull a lever and drop me down a trap door into a snake pit? What the hell was she waiting for?
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, can you make one? I said, finally.
        </p>
        <p>
         She let out the biggest sigh I had ever heard. Was this bitch serious right now?
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, I guess. But itll take like twenty minutes, she said, hoping Id be in too big of a hurry to wait.
        </p>
        <p>
         She wasnt going to get off that easy. I was going to make this disrespectful little shit do her job.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sure, Ill wait, I said. I mean, I was under the impression that it was supposed to be hot-and-ready pizza, but I was too emotionally invested in torturing this girl to leave now.
        </p>
        <p>
         Let me just say right off the bat, there was absolutely zero professionalism in this establishment. I could hear them back thereswearing, dancing to music, talking about some girl named Ashley who was apparently a huge slut. You know what Ashley; Im on your side. Fuck these kids. You do you.
        </p>
        <p>
         It wouldve been more tolerable if they were playing decent music. They were listening to 2 Chainz, which, if you couldnt tell by his stage name, is a rapper. If youre not familiar with his musicbasically, imagine rap music and then stab yourself in the fucking throat. Thats pretty much 2 Chainz in a nutshell. If youre going to listen to rap, listen to
         <em>
          real
         </em>
         rap musiclike stuff from the 90s or Kendrick Lamar or anything by Earl Sweatshirt or really anything that isnt just an assortment of guttural noises recorded over a bass-heavy beat.
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyway, so as if their music wasnt bad enough, these fine customer service representatives decided to start telling dirty jokes. Being the inquisitive mind I am, I edged closer to listen as the aforementioned cashier began to tell her joke. She must have sensed a disturbance in the force, because she stopped her train of thought mid-joke. She then poked her head around the corner, looked directly at me, turned back around to her coworkers and said:
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont worry guys
         <em>
          Hes
         </em>
         not Mexican.
        </p>
        <p>
         ARE YOU FUCKING KIDDING ME!? Who does that!? Absolutely no respect, these kids. What if I was Mexican? What then, huh? Just to fuck with them, I shouldve gone up to the counter and said:
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          De qu hastas hablando? Tiens un problema?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         I look back on this now and wonder, When the hell did I become
         <em>
          that
         </em>
         guy? That crotchety, old man who complains about restaurant service, is that me now? When does the change occur in adults when they start expecting a certain standard of conduct from people they come across? Does it all stem from an attitude of entitlement? (i.e. Im an important adult now whos very important so you have to show me respect because Im very important.) Maybe we just hate people more as we get older. Or maybe its a matter of jealousy. Old people hate young people because young people dont realize how much the world sucks yet. They have absolutely no fear of the shit-storm headed their way and that pisses old people off.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its probably a combination of all those things. Still, coming to this rational understanding doesnt change the fact that I hate high schoolers.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Pinches nios!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Jennifer Lawrence
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I had just submitted my article
         <em>
         </em>
         on the game I covered that night. It was 11:30 on a Friday, and I was stone-cold sober. I wasnt planning on going out, that is, until I got a text from this girl Id been hooking up with for a few weeks now. To protect her identity, well call her Jennifer Lawrence. She wanted me to come meet her at a party. A freshman party (yuck). But I agreed, with the mindset of Hey, itd be nice to relive the old glory days, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         I arrived at the party, met Jennifer Lawrence at the door and followed her inside. The interior of the house was a god damn zoo. My arrival was met with confusion from most and lustful glares from a few drunken freshman girls. To be honest, I had no interest in sleeping with these girls. Back when I was 18? Sure. But now, I just wanted to give these girls a glass of water and a ride home. I felt bad for them, you know? They dont realize how fucked up the world is yethow many scumbags there are out there looking to take advantage of them. There were plenty of said scumbags at this particular party, too. I swear to God, the rape in the air was tangible. Speaking of the air, it was like 9,000 degrees in that fucking house. How the hell were these kids comfortable in this environment? I had to take a step outside.
        </p>
        <p>
         After zig-zagging through the wall of drunk, eighteen-year-old strangers, I made my way onto the porch. Luckily, I wasnt the only senior who couldnt hang. I saw my friend, who, to protect his identity, well call Ashiq Al Muhammad.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im too old for this shit, he said as he cracked open a beer.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know exactly what you mean, I said. Its just so crowded in there.
        </p>
        <p>
         And hot, too, he said.
        </p>
        <p>
         I nodded.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im glad I ran into you, he said. I have no idea who any of those people are.
        </p>
        <p>
         Me either, I said. Its weird. I used to live for this shit.
        </p>
        <p>
         You know what I did today? I continued. I went to the bank and set up a god damn 401k. And now Im supposed to go play beer pong like Im some freshman?
        </p>
        <p>
         Ashiq Al Muhammad laughed. You know, its actually smart that youre putting money away for retirement at such an early age.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know, I said. The guy at the bank broke it down for me. He told me that if I put away two grand every year from now until Im 26, Ill be looking at six-figures by the time I retire.
        </p>
        <p>
         Yeah, its crazy, he said. You gotta get that compounding interest going. I started putting money away when I was nineteen.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats smart, I said. Youre gonna be set by the time youre 65.
        </p>
        <p>
         A group of about twenty drunken freshmen walked out of the house, each kid louder and more obnoxious than the last.
        </p>
        <p>
         What do these fucking kids know about 401ks, he said, pointing to the group.
        </p>
        <p>
         Nothing, I said, laughing. These dumb mother fuckers need to start planning for the future.
        </p>
        <p>
         Eventually, Jennifer Lawrence came outside looking for me.
        </p>
        <p>
         Are you going to come inside? she asked.
        </p>
        <p>
         I looked at Jennifer Lawrence. I looked at Ashiq Al Muhammad. I reached into my backpack, cracked another beer and said:
        </p>
        <p>
         You know what? Im actually good right here.
        </p>
        <p>
         Okay, can we take a step back and analyze what just happened? I was at a party. I had plenty of beer. There was music, tons of people dancing the whole shebang. Yet, instead of taking part in the festivities, I decided to stand outside in the cold and talk about 401ks.
        </p>
        <p>
         And you know what? I couldnt have been happier.
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont know when it happened. I cant pinpoint the moment when I stopped being the party guy and became an out of shape old man who complains about restaurant service and has conversations about retirement planning. But here I am. I used to look forward to things like going to Iowa City or getting pussy. Now, Im looking forward to things like complimentary life insurance and retirement. I
         <em>
          could
         </em>
         force myself to go to parties. I
         <em>
          could
         </em>
         get drunk on week days and shirk my responsibilities. I
         <em>
          could
         </em>
         pretend that Im still in my prime. Or I can just admit the truth.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fuck it. Im old.
        </p>
