              <div class="field-items">
               <div class="field-item even">
                Text messages, emails, love oh my! Advice about technology and relationships.
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="field-value body no-label">
             <p>
              Ever sent a
              <a href="http://www.yourtango.com/2014222560/how-send-sexy-selfies-worth-bragging-about">
               sexy
              </a>
              text to your boyfriend... and then realized it was actually to your boss? Do you know your partner's email password? Are you constantly checking your Blackberryeven when you're on a date? Even if you've never experienced these tech troubles, it's likely that you've encountered the intersection of technology and
              <a href="http://www.yourtango.com/relationships">
               relationships
              </a>
              and maybe you've wondered how to set some rules for yourself and your partner. Below, 20 dos and don'ts when mixing
              <a href="http://www.yourtango.com/love">
               love
              </a>
              with the latest technology. What are your rules?Let us know in the comments.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               1. DO
              </strong>
              plug in your significant other's iPhone, Blackberry or computer in when you see the juice is low.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               2. DO
              </strong>
              share the joke with your
              <a href="http://www.yourtango.com/2013191628/ways-support-your-spouse-through-mental-illness">
               spouse
              </a>
              if you laugh out loud at something on your Blackberry or phone.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               3. DO
              </strong>
              text and email your spouse with the same care you did when you first started dating. Grocery lists and forwards don't count as correspondence. Make time to send sweet-nothings to each other over Facebook, IM, and email.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               4. DO
              </strong>
              upload new music to your honey's iPod and new books to his/her Kindle. New tunes and words will be a sweet surprise and will show that you've been thinking of them while they were away.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               5. DON'T
              </strong>
              exchange email passwords with your partner no matter how good of an idea it seems.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               6. DON'T
              </strong>
              hack into your partner's email or phone, read the messages, assume they are cheating on you, freak out and post your suspicions on a social network or a web site.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               7. DO
              </strong>
              save cute texts, email and voicemails.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               8. DON'T
              </strong>
              send flirty text messages or emails on your company Blackberry unless you want to explain "gr8 morning sex!" to the Director of IT.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               9. DO
              </strong>
              set a time limit for internet surfing while you're hanging out together.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               10. DO
              </strong>
              give technology as a gift, but not for a romantic occasion. Note! If you need to charge it up, plug it in or program it, it probably doesn't make for a good (read: romantic) anniversary present.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               11. DON'T
              </strong>
              email or browse the net while talking to or on the phone with the other person. You may think you're a multi-tasking queen (or king), but it's distracting for both of you and it makes it seem like you only half-care about what he's talking about.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               12. DON'T
              </strong>
              bring up important topics in a medium that your partner is uncomfortable with. (Don't text him that you're going to miss dinner if he's a bad texter. Don't IM him that you're mad at him if he prefers to hear your voice.)
             </p>
             <div>
              <strong>
               13. DO
              </strong>
              have a discussion with your partner about what technologies are OK for big discussions. (IM can be a great way to talk about emotional topics, for example, since you have time to formulate your thoughts before typing and you're less likely to blurt something out in anger.)
              <p>
               <strong>
                14. DO
               </strong>
               refrain from using emoticons and/or tech lingo (eg. LOL, ROTFL), if it annoys your partner.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                15. DO
               </strong>
