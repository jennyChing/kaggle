    <p>
     This is the home page for Memphis Cyclist. If you are interested in, or love cycling, you  have come to the right place. This home  page is the launch pad to a comprehensive website for almost any issue that would be of interest to cyclists.  There is also a
     <a href="http://memphiscyclist.blogspot.com/">
      Memphis Cyclist blog
     </a>
     you can  check out as well. This Memphis cycling website is primarily focused on serving cyclists in the Memphis area community, but it offers news and information that could be extemely useful for both the novice or advanced cyclist anywhere.  (

    <h3 align="justify">
     <strong>
      Cycling Has Few Boundaries
     </strong>
    </h3>
    <p align="justify">
     <img alt="home6a" border="1" class="fltrt" height="263" src="images/Home6a.jpg" width="350"/>
     Cycling is one of those rare activities that has almost no boundaries. It can be done alone or with others, can be  enjoyed by the young and
     <a href="http://www.vtt.fi/inf/pdf/workingpapers/2008/W92.pdf">
      old
     </a>
     , men and women, and those who are physically fit  and those who are not. Even a physical  disability does not necessarily prohibit people from riding a bike since many  bikes can be made or can be modified to accommodate the rider. Here is a link to see
     <a href="http://www.allabilitycycling.com/">
      bikes for those with handicaps
     </a>
     and the many different
     <a href="http://www.caree.org/bike101biketypes.htm">
      types of bikes
     </a>
     available on the market.
     <br/>
     From the time that the first bike came on to the scene in the early 19th  Century, bicycles have changed dramatically and have grown in popularity over  the years. For many adults today, they  likely have fond memories of getting their first bike as a present and, perhaps  riding around with training wheels, and then later, spending countless hours  riding throughout their neighborhood afterwards.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <img alt="home2a" border="1" class="fltlft" height="265" src="images/Home2a.jpg" width="400"/>
     <br/>
     Although society has continued to change with significant developments in  technology communications and transportation, bicycles have remained and have benefitted from  some of the advancements that have occurred.  Many bicycles can be found today that have extremely efficient
     <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bicycle_gearing">
      gear  mechanisms
     </a>
     , are often made of durable and extremely lightweight
     <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bicycle_frame">
      materials
     </a>
     , and are capable of speeds that  generations before could have only dreamed of. For more on the science on bicycles, go to:
     <a href="http://www.exploratorium.edu/cycling/">
      http://www.exploratorium.edu/cycling/
     </a>
     <br/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="home7a" border="1" class="fltrt" height="286" src="images/Home7a.jpg" width="300"/>
     Even with all of the improvements that have been made to bicycles, they still  remain one of the few devices around that are
     <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human-powered_transport">
      powered exclusively by a  person
     </a>
     . They are also one of the least  expensive and one of the most
     <a href="http://www.conserve-energy-future.com/modes-and-benefits-of-green-transportation.php">
      environmentally-friendly means of transportation
     </a>
     that can  be found and the health benefits of cycling cannot be overstated.
