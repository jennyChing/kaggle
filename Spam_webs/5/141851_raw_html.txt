         <h1>
          The Cake Mamas' main mama wants to bring business education to baking industry
         </h1>
        </div>
        <div class="post-view">
         <!-- AddThis Button BEGIN -->
         <div class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style post-shat">

         <p class="post-date when">

          <span class="by-author">
           by Beto the Baker
          </span>

         </p>
         <div class="post-entry entry std">
          <p>
           Most people who get into the baking business do so because of their passion for flour and fondant, but many of them lack the necessary business acumen to truly thrive.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            The Cake Mama
           </em>
           s Janelle Copeland wants to change that.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_975" style="width:354px;">
           <a href="http://www.bakersbodega.com/c/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Janelle-Copeland.jpg">
            <img alt="Janelle Copeland of The Cake Mamas" class=" wp-image-975 " height="446" src="http://www.bakersbodega.com/c/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Janelle-Copeland.jpg" width="344"/>
           </a>
           Janelle Copeland of The Cake Mamas
          </div>
          <p>
           Thats my new passion, just to share with people in the industry what it really entails to go into business for yourself, Copeland said during a recent phone interview.
          </p>
          <p>
           Copeland has a business class coming up in March and plans on continuing to offer more like it in the future.
          </p>
          <p>
           Having a background in retail management, Copeland understands what it takes to run a business and she wants to share that knowledge with others in the baking industry, who start a business because of their passion for baking, but dont always consider how much work the administrative side of the business involves.
          </p>
          <p>
           Almost every single class related to the baking industry involves hands-on baking or decorating, Copeland pointed out. And while those skills are obviously important, of equal importance is being able to manage an advertising budget, a payroll, equipment costs and the numerous other issues a small business has to consider.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <a href="http://www.thecakemamas.com/" target="_blank" title="The Cake Mama's main mama wants to bring business education to baking industry">
             <em>
              The Cake Mamas
             </em>
            </a>
           </span>
           president sees a niche here that currently isnt being filled and she aims to be the one to fill it.
          </p>
          <p>
           I just want to help grow our industry around knowledge and professionalism, she said.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The dream of a beginning
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Copeland actually got into the baking game in the opposite way of how it usually happens. Typically, people will have a passion for baking and decorating and decide to start a business without realizing all the administrative work that goes into it.
          </p>
          <p>
           Copeland, though, already had a career in business, which was cut short when the corporation she was working for went under in 2009, leaving her without a job.
          </p>
          <p>
           This was around the same time that Buddy Valastros
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <a href="http://www.tlc.com/tv-shows/cake-boss" target="_blank" title="The Cake Mama's main mama wants to bring business education to baking industry">
             <em>
              Cake Boss
             </em>
            </a>
           </span>
           TV show was hitting the airwaves, which drew Copelands attention.
          </p>
          <p>
           The mother of three had always been passionate about anything to do with crafts and creating things. Making Halloween costumes for her children and scrapbooking are favorite activities. And baking had already been a hobby of hers that she did with her grandmother and other members of her family.
          </p>
          <p>
           So, when she saw the the artistry and the baking being put together with such great success on
           <em>
            Cake Boss
           </em>
           , Copeland thought she could do that. So, she bought herself a couple of buckets of fondant and some cake ingredients and made a cake out of a cake book just to test it out. The results were great, with friends and family immediately taking note of her newly-discovered talent.
          </p>
          <p>
           And then things got downright serendipitous.
          </p>
          <p>
           She had a dream that her and the mother of her two step-children, Fabiola, had opened a baking business together that the children would help out in after school.
          </p>
          <p>
           Copeland told her children about the dream and they loved the idea and urged her to tell Fabiola about the dream, too. When she did, Fabiola revealed that not only had she been a baker for years, but it had always been a goal of hers to open a business.
          </p>
          <p>
           So, with Fabiolas baking background and Copelands crafts and business background, and
           <em>
            The Cake Mama
           </em>
           s moniker given to them by their children, they were set.
          </p>
          <p>
           And things took off quickly.
          </p>
          <p>
           Within a week of making my first cake, I had a company name, a website and social media sites and business cards and I had taken three orders within a week, Copeland recalled.
          </p>
          <p>
           Fabiola eventually left the business, staying on from 2009 until 2012.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            From watcher to participant
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Seeing as how Copeland was partially inspired by a TV program, its fitting that she wound up on TV, winning the Wicked episode of Season 4 of
           <em>
            Cupcake Wars
           </em>
           , which aired in January of 2012.
          </p>
          <p>
           While the TV appearance didnt propel her to A-list celebrity status, it did give her a bit of minor celebrity in her neighborhood, with people even now posting on
           <em>
            The Cake Mamas
           </em>
           
           <em>
            Facebook
           </em>
           page that theyve seen her at
           <em>
            Michael
           </em>
           s or out celebrating her anniversary at the
           <em>
            Coco Palms Restaurant
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Although Copeland appreciates the recognition the TV appearance brought her business, she admitted that it took some time to get use to having people shes never met talking about how theyd stood behind her at the store or sat beside her in a restaurant.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Gratification
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           For Copeland, the best part of the business is connecting with the people she gets to meet and knowing that she and her staff are playing an integral role in meaningful events in these peoples lives. While cakes and cupcakes are usually associate with birthday celebrations, she said, many customers order them for other purposes like funerals or to cheer up a loved one who is battling cancer.
          </p>
          <p>
           She and her staff  15 to 18 members at any given time  know theyve done their job well from the reactions they get from customers, many of whom shed tears of joy when they see their finished cakes and cupcakes.
          </p>
          <p>
           Sweets just seem to touch people in a way that flowers cant, Copeland noted.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Challenges
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Thus far, the most complicated cake she and her staff have had to conquer has been one with a three-dimensional sculpture of a
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandrail" target="_blank">
             sandrail
            </a>
           </span>
           on it. (A sand rail is similar to a dune buggy.)
          </p>
          <p>
           That cake was really challenging because here we were trying to make something that weve never even physically seen, she said.
          </p>
          <p>
           The staff is often asked to create sculptures of things theyve never seen for real, which makes it difficult to get the dimensions down, Copeland said, as its not easy to work solely from pictures.
          </p>
          <p>
           But while these 3D sculptures are the most challenging item to create, theyre not the most challenging aspect of running
           <em>
            The Cake Mamas
           </em>
           . That would be the actual administrative work involved in keeping the business thriving.
          </p>
          <p>
           In a large corporation, Copeland said, there are teams for marketing, human resources, finances and all the other activities that go into running a successful business. But a small business doesnt have those luxuries.
          </p>
          <p>
           Although she got into the baking industry because of her passion for the artistry of the decorating, she estimates that she only spends about 10-15% of her working time per week actually doing the hands-on creation while the rest of her time is tied up in administrative work.
          </p>
          <p>
           Anyone who thinks that running a bakery or specialty cake shop is all fondant and flour is in for an unpleasant surprise, she said.
          </p>
          <p>
           And this is where her newest passion  teaching the business side of the industry  comes into play. She aims to fill that business education void in the baking industry.
          </p>
          <p>
           And, as for expansion plans for
           <em>
            The Cake Mamas
           </em>
           , she wants to eventually expand the bakery to make it bigger and better while continuing to offer baking, decorating and business classes, doing party planning and giving demonstrations at
           <em>
            The Cake Mamas
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <a href="http://www.thecakemamasparties.com/sweet-celebration-studio#!__sweet-celebration-studio" target="_blank" title="The Cake Mama's main mama wants to bring business education to baking industry">
              Sweet Celebration Studio
             </a>
            </span>
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Considering that Copeland never intended to start her own business, its amazing to see how much its grown in the time its been around. Not bad for something that came from a dream.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          </p>
