      <h1 class="post-title">
       Love, Betrayal, and Colored Pencils
      </h1>
      <div class="post-meta">
       Posted on February 9, 2014 in:
       <a href="http://jefita.com/blog/category/story-time/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Story Time">
        Story Time
       </a>
       <span class="sep">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="#comments">
        Jump To Comments
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="post-content">
       <!-- AddThis Button Begin -->
       <script type="text/javascript">
        var addthis_product = 'wpp-261';
var addthis_config = {"data_track_clickback":true};
       </script>
       <script src="//s7.addthis.com/js/250/addthis_widget.js#pubid=jefita" type="text/javascript">
       </script>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <a href="http://blog-images.jefita.com.s3.amazonaws.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/pencilsall_sm.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-804" height="397" src="http://blog-images.jefita.com.s3.amazonaws.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/pencilsall_sm.jpg" width="600"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        In light of approaching Valentines Day, here is a short story of love
        <em>
         and betrayal!
        </em>
        Because thats more exciting.
        <span id="more-802">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        The other day I unearthed my box of colored pencils, and was struck by a poignant sense of guilt. For more than a year they had sat alone in the dark, unused and unloved. I had abandoned them. How could I be so cold?
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://blog-images.jefita.com.s3.amazonaws.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/SadRobot_sq.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-808" height="300" src="http://blog-images.jefita.com.s3.amazonaws.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/SadRobot_sq-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         But we were so good together!
        </em>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        Once upon a time, I deeply loved my colored pencils. This love affair started when I was quite young. My grandmother had a set of Prismacolor colored pencils that she would let me play with. Having already been bitterly betrayed by watercolor, their soft and rich consistency instantly won me over.Over several years, I carefully built up a beautiful collection of my own, pencil by pencil. (At $2-3 a pop, the box above represents a lot of money invested in colorful little wooden sticks.) I lavished them with attention and adoration. I made wonderful pictures with them. They were my
        <em>
         favorite
        </em>
        . They were the
        <em>
         best
        </em>
        until fate crushed our love.
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <a href="http://blog-images.jefita.com.s3.amazonaws.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/loverivals.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-805" height="397" src="http://blog-images.jefita.com.s3.amazonaws.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/loverivals.jpg" width="600"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        One day a set of fancy Copic markers came along and swayed my heart. These markers fulfilled my unrequited desire for watercolor that my pencils could not touch. A new love blossomed and I cruelly left my old love by the wayside with nary a look back. Oh, how my heart is fickle!
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <a href="http://blog-images.jefita.com.s3.amazonaws.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/pencilghosts_sm.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-806" height="397" src="http://blog-images.jefita.com.s3.amazonaws.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/pencilghosts_sm.jpg" width="600"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         All those broken leads are rough on a girls heart.
         <br/>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        Its okay, my pencils, you will always have a special place in my heart. Youre still just as lovely and vibrant and I miss your warm cedar smell. Lets just say I was the one who changed. Someday I will bring you out and we will do beautiful things again. Maybe you and the markers can learn to be friends. Sure, Im trying to assuage my guilt a bit, but I really do feel bad about neglecting you.
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        Other people empathize with their art supplies, right? Right?
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
