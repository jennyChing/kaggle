         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/111866146X/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=111866146X&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=books4ict-20&amp;linkId=FUQ6US7XYUSFDMUQ" title="Data Smart on Amazon">
           <em>
            Data Smart
           </em>
          </a>
          makes modern statistic methods and algorithms understandable and easy to implement. Slogging through textbooks and academic papers is no longer required. Ill give you also the definition of
          <em>
           data science
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Data science
          </strong>
          is the transformation of data using mathematics and statistics into valuableinsights, decisions, and products.
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
          A word about Data Smart book
         </h2>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The truth is most people are going about data science all wrong. Theyre starting withbuying the tools and hiring the consultants. Theyre spending all their money before theyeven know what they want, because a purchase order seems to pass for actual progressin many companies these days.By reading this book, youre gonna have a leg up on those jokers, because youre goingto learn exactly what these techniques in data science are and how theyre used. When itcomes time to do the planning, and the hiring, and the buying, youll already know howto identify the data science opportunities within your own organization.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          This is not a book that will be covering database technologies, like MongoDB and HBase.This is not a book that will be covering data science coding packages like Mahout, NumPy,various R libraries, and so on. There are other books out there for that stuff .But thats a good thing. Data Smartignores the tools, the storage, and the code. Instead,it focuses as much as possible on the techniques.
         </p>
         <h3>
          The famous Big Data term
         </h3>
         <p>
          You have heard the term
          <strong>
           big data
          </strong>
          even more than
          <strong>
           data science
          </strong>
          most likely. Is this a book onbig data?
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          That depends on how you define big data. If you define big data as computing simplesummary statistics on unstructured garbage stored in massive, horizontally scalable,NoSQL databases, then no, this is not a book on big data.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          If you define big data as turning transactional business data into decisions and insightusing cutting-edge analytics (regardless of where that data is stored), then yes, this is abook about big data.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Who is Data Smart written for ?
         </h3>
         <ul>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           The vice president of marketing who wants to use her transactional business datamore strategically to price products and segment customers. But she does not understandthe approaches her software developers and overpriced consultants are recommendingshe try.
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           The demand forecasting analyst who knows his organizations historical purchasedata holds more insight about his customers than just the next quarters projections.But he does not know how to extract that insight.
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           The CEO of an online retail start-up who wants to predict when a customer is likelyto be interested in buying an item based on their past purchases.
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           The business intelligence analyst who sees money going down the tubes from theinfrastructure and supply chain costs her organization is accruing, but doesnt knowhow to systematically make cost-saving decisions.
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           The online marketer who wants to do more with his companys free text customerinteractions taking place in e-mail, Facebook, and Twitter, but right now theyrejust being read and saved.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          The purpose of Data Smart book ?
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Thepurpose of
          <em>
           Data Smart
          </em>
          book is to strip away all the distractions around data science (the code,the tools, and the hype) and teach the techniques using practical use cases that someonewith a semester of linear algebra or calculus in college can understand. Assuming youdid not fail that semester. If you did, just read slower and use Wikipedia liberally.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Data smart will introduce you to the practice of data science in a comfortableand conversational way. When youre done, I hope that much of that data scienceanxiety youre feeling is replaced with excitement and with ideas about how you can usedata to take your business to the next level.
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
          Contents and Chapters
         </h2>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <div class="omsc-toggle">
          <div class="omsc-toggle-title">
           Data Smart chapters preview
          </div>
          <div class="omsc-toggle-inner">
           <p>
            <strong>
             Data Smart : Using Data Science to Transform Information into Insight by Wiley consists of the following 10 chapters:
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <ol>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              Everything You Ever Needed to Know about Spreadsheets but Were Too Afraid to Ask
             </strong>
             This book relies on you having a working knowledge of spreadsheets, and Im going toassume that you already understand the basics. If youve never used a formula beforein your life, then youve got a slight uphill battle here. Id recommend going through a ForDummies book or some other intro-level tutorial for Excel before diving into this.
            </li>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              Cluster Analysis Part I: Using K-Means to Segment Your Customer Base 
             </strong>
             How do you take a bunch of transactional data from your customers (or audience, users,subscribers, citizens, and so on) and use it to understand them?
            </li>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              Nave Bayes and the Incredible Lightness of Being an Idiot 
             </strong>
             In this chapter youre going to move from unsupervised into supervised artificial intelligencemodels by training up a nave Bayes model, which is, for lack of a better metaphor,also a chicken nugget, albeit a supervised one.
            </li>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              Optimization Modeling: Because That Fresh Squeezed Orange Juice Aint Gonna Blend Itself 
             </strong>
             An artificial intelligence model predicts the result of a process by analyzing its inputs.
            </li>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              Cluster Analysis Part II: Network Graphs and Community Detection 
             </strong>
             This chapter continues the discussion on cluster identification and analysis using thewholesale wine dataset from Chapter 2.
            </li>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              The Granddaddy of Supervised Artificial Intelligence  Regression 
             </strong>
             New parents blow alot of money on the accouterments of child rearing, and what better time to turn them intoloyal customers than before the baby even shows up? Theyll be buying the store branddiapers for years!
            </li>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              Ensemble Models: A Whole Lot of Bad Pizza 
             </strong>
             Youll be going over a type of ensemble model called bagged decision stumps, which isvery close to an approach used constantly in industry called the random forest model.
            </li>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              Forecasting: Breathe Easy; You Cant Win 
             </strong>
             The only guarantee with forecasting is that your forecast is wrong. You hear that a lot inthe world of forecasting. But that does not mean you dont try. When it comes to planningyour business, you often need some projection.
            </li>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              Outlier Detection: Just Because Theyre Odd Doesnt Mean Theyre Unimportant 
             </strong>
             A good example of outlier removal is in gymnastics, where the highest and lowest judgesscores are always trimmed from the data before taking the average score.Outliers have a knack for messing up machine learning models.
            </li>
            <li style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              Moving from Spreadsheets into R 
             </strong>
             After spending the previous nine chapters injecting Excel directly into your veins, Imnow going to tell you to drop it. Well, not for everything, but lets be honest, Excelis not ideal for all analytics tasks.
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
           </p>
