       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Since late last year Ive cut everything I can from my
         <a href="http://www.rooshv.com/2007/how-to-get-your-finances-in-order">
          monthly budget
         </a>
         . Ive reduced all recurring expenses to only three items:
        </p>
        <p>
         Student Loans: $177
         <br/>
         Cell Phone: $38
         <br/>
         Blog Hosting: $15
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Total: $230
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you dont count the cell phone, which Im getting rid of when I leave, that means I only need $200 to cover my ass every month. Im not counting car insurance because I just sold my car two days ago.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now lets include optional spending on things like
         <a href="http://www.dcbachelor.com/2006/my-future-coffeeshop">
          coffee
         </a>
         , transportation,
         <a href="http://www.rooshv.com/2007/top-10-most-important-books">
          books
         </a>
         , and entertainment. The reason Im calling all these expenditures optional is because technically I can stay in my Dads house all day and eat his food and surf his internet without spending a dollar. These figures are from May, which Id say was a typical month.
         <!-- medium200x200 DISABLED-->
        </p>
        <p>
         Coffeeshop: $72
         <br/>
         Dating: $24
         <br/>
         Nightlife:
         <u>
          $186
         </u>
         <br/>
         Outside food &amp; 7-11: $43
         <br/>
         Used books: $35
         <br/>
         Public transportation and gas: $103
         <br/>
         Gifts &amp; favors: $4
         <br/>
         Netflix: $16
         <br/>
         Grocery store: $48
         <br/>
         Target: $36
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Total: $567
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Therefore I need about $800 in income a month to maintain the status quo of drinking, reading, writing, and banging. But since living with my Dad is only a short-term arrangement, Ill really need about $1500 a month in income a month to account for shelter and electricity. Thats $375 a week, which I can get by bartending again for 3-4 nights a week. If I lived in a developing country Id need about half that.
        </p>
        <p>
         A lot of people search for higher paying jobs (or even a second job) to pay for an expensive lifestyle, but my strategy is to go the other way. If you need less things then you can work less as well, giving you more leisure time to do the things you really want to do. While money issues will surface eventually, the only thing I worry about right now in life is missing the last Metro train home.
         <!--adsense#underpost-->
        </p>
