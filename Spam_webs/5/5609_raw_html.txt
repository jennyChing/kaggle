         <section>
          <h2 class="hide">
           6 Delicious Ways to Use Up Leftover Champagne
          </h2>
          <p>
           Unlike wine, you can't re-cork or stopper leftover
           <a href="http://food-hacks.wonderhowto.com/how-to/keep-champagne-bubbly-hint-spoon-doesnt-work-0155028/">
            bubbly
           </a>
           after you've opened it, but all is not lost even if you haven't managed to finish every last drop. You can use your
           <a href="http://food-hacks.wonderhowto.com/how-to/save-your-money-sparkling-wine-can-be-just-as-good-as-champagne-0158565/">
            leftover champagne
           </a>
           to make light-as-air crpes or pancakes, to create a detox face mask, to cook seafood and rice, or to make dips and salad dressings.
          </p>
         </section>
         <section>
          <h2 class="sectionHeadline">
           #1. Make Light-as-Air Champagne Crpes
          </h2>
          <p>
           Leftover champagne lends these crpes a sweet taste and helps them cook up extra light and with exceptionally crisp, lacy edges.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Ingredients:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            3 eggs, lightly beaten
           </li>
           <li>
            1 cup milk
           </li>
           <li>
             cup champagne
           </li>
           <li>
            1 cups all-purpose flour
           </li>
           <li>
            1 pinch salt
           </li>
           <li>
            2 tablespoons vegetable oil and melted butter, combined
           </li>
          </ul>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="53083386docPartGal880005" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
              <img alt="6 Delicious Ways to Use Up Leftover Champagne" height="532" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/17/22/63557803482459/0/6-delicious-ways-use-up-leftover-champagne.w654.jpg" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;" width="532"/>
             </figure>
            </div>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Instructions:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            In a large bowl, whisk together
            <a href="http://food-hacks.wonderhowto.com/how-to/breakfast-badass-makes-eggs-scrambled-and-hard-boiled-without-cracking-shell-0144525/">
             eggs
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://food-hacks.wonderhowto.com/inspiration/why-skim-low-reduced-fat-milk-are-actually-worse-for-you-than-whole-milk-0151182/">
             milk
            </a>
            , and champagne.
           </li>
           <li>
            Whisk in flour.
           </li>
           <li>
            Add salt and butter/oil mixture and then whisk quickly for a few minutes.
           </li>
           <li>
            Let batter sit for one hour in the refrigerator.
           </li>
           <li>
            Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Brush it with rest of butter/oil mixture.
           </li>
           <li>
            When skillet is hot but not smoking, pour a small amount of batter in to make a thin crepe.
           </li>
           <li>
            Cook until golden, about one minute for first side and 30 seconds for the other side.
           </li>
           <li>
            Continue with the rest of batter, covering cooked crpes to keep warm.
           </li>
           <li>
            Fill or top with sweet or savory toppings.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           If you want thick and fluffy champagne pancakes instead, follow
           <a href="http://veggieandthebeastfeast.com/2013/12/28/fluffy-strawberry-and-champagne-pancakes/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            this recipe
           </a>
           from Veggie and the Beast.
          </p>
         </section>
         <section>
          <h2 class="sectionHeadline">
           #2. Make Champagne-Soaked Mussels
          </h2>
          <p>
           I love mussels cooked in beer or wine, and champagne might be even better. It's simple to make this and the flavor is incredible.
          </p>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="5854163docPartGal880009" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
              <img alt="6 Delicious Ways to Use Up Leftover Champagne" height="532" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/35/28/63557803548818/0/6-delicious-ways-use-up-leftover-champagne.w654.jpg" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;" width="532"/>
             </figure>
            </div>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Ingredients:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            2 lbs fresh mussels
           </li>
           <li>
            2 tbsp garlic, onions, or
            <a href="http://food-hacks.wonderhowto.com/how-to/secret-trick-peeling-shallots-more-easily-0156687/">
             shallots
            </a>
            , minced (alternatively, you can use/combine other diced aromatics like celery or carrots)
           </li>
           <li>
            2 cups leftover champagne, more or less
           </li>
           <li>
            Herbs, salt, and pepper to taste
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Instructions:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Clean your mussels well. (You can get a good guide
            <a href="http://www.seriouseats.com/2014/11/how-to-clean-debeard-mussels.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             here
            </a>
            .)
           </li>
           <li>
            In a large pot over medium-low heat, saut some garlic and onions (or shallots) in olive oil.
           </li>
           <li>
            Add some herbs and salt and pepper and cook for a couple minutes.
           </li>
           <li>
            Turn up the heat and add the mussels and the champagne.
           </li>
           <li>
            Bring to a boil and cover for about five minutes, or until the mussels open.
           </li>
           <li>
            Serve with some good, crusty bread.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </section>
         <section>
          <h2 class="sectionHeadline">
           #3. Make a Lovely Champagne Vinaigrette
          </h2>
          <p>
           This vinaigrette works well on any green or tossed salad, but works especially well on salads with a lot of fruit and nuts. It's light and brings out the natural sweetness in these ingredients. The champagne adds a vibrant, piquant taste.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Ingredients:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            3 tbsp olive oil
           </li>
           <li>
            1 tbsp champagne or balsamic vinegar
           </li>
           <li>
            1 tbsp champagne
           </li>
           <li>
             tbsp sugar, or more and less to taste
           </li>
           <li>
            sea salt and pepper to taste
           </li>
          </ul>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="65251884docPartGal880013" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">

             </figure>
            </div>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p>
           Combine ingredients in shallow bowl and
           <a href="http://food-hacks.wonderhowto.com/how-to/whisking-were-all-doing-wrong-0155915/">
            whisk
           </a>
           together. Alternatively, you can put all the ingredients in a sealed jar and shake it.
