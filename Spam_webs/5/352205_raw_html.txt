        <h1 class="page-title">
         Using Visual Content On Social Media For Your Business.
        </h1>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <i class="icon-time">
         </i>
         <a href="http://www.squaresfordays.com/blog-post/" rel="bookmark" title="9:16 am">
          February 25, 2015
         </a>
         <i class="icon-user">

         <span class="author vcard">

           future

         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- end of .post-meta -->
        <div class="post-entry">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.squaresfordays.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/IMG_0091.jpg" rel="colorbox">
           <img alt="Got Visual Content?" class=" size-medium wp-image-267 alignleft" height="300" src="http://www.squaresfordays.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/IMG_0091-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          Ever since social media began to flourish with the founding of MySpace and of Facebook in 2003 of 2004 respectively, the use of social media on the Internet has skyrocketed to include almost two billion users of Facebook alone! Social networking through the Internet has become the norm in the twenty-first century. Now, businesses and companies across the globe have utilized the usefulness of social media to reach prospective costumers around the world and have become successful because of one term: visual content. Business and companies have discovered that the use of popular memes, hashtags, wall posts, photos, etc. to promote their business can generate a huge amount of interest into their products. Here is a list of three popular social media sites, and how you can utilize these sites and use the visual content to promote your product.
         </p>
         <p>
          Facebook is the current reigning king of social media. Although its debated among social media experts on the longevity of Facebooks relevance to the Millennial Generation, it boasts on having 1.17 billion users on its site, thus making it the most popular social-networking site on the web. If you want to utilize the power of Facebook for your business, you can create a page for your business on Facebook and create status-updates, polls, and memes. These different types of visual content will attract attention from those who like the page. Engagement is what youre looking for. Memes are definitely popular on the virtual sphere nowadays, and a clever and hilarious meme can be shared all over the web. If you want to cover your basics in utilizing social media, Facebook should be your first site to utilize.
         </p>
         <p>
          Another popular social-networking site that most companies use now is Twitter. With Twitter, you can create a profile and posts tweets that engage with your followers. The tweets that a business can post via Twitter are especially potent if a business uses hashtags in their tweets so that it can increase the chance of Twitter users finding that particular post. Its a great tool to directly engage with Twitter users (and its especially cool that every Tweet is guaranteed to be seen in each followers feed, as opposed to Facebook).
         </p>
         <p>
          Finally, theres Instagram. Photography is now the pastime for most Millennials, so companies that integrate a social-networking site specifically used to post pictures (Instagram) can now get the upper hand in public relations. Most companies will integrate their Instagram and their Facebook accounts together and will post pictures of anything company-related, such as their employees, their products, etc. Some businesses have even started photo competitions via Instagram and interact with their followers through shared photos. If you are looking for a medium of communication that has pure visual content, then Instagram is the social-media site to utilize.
         </p>
         <p>
          No matter which sites you use, just always remember to stay informed on which social-networking sites are becoming more popular or obsolete because technology in the Internet-age doesnt stay relevant for long (Myspace, for example). If you start off your PR campaign with these three sites, you should be set in establishing your companys online PR campaign!
         </p>
        </div>
