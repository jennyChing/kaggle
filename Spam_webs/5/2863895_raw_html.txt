                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Day 637 - Who Am I in Conflict?  Part two
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3187107980333436855" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-PtxPhabOb0s/U7pWoKSpGlI/AAAAAAAADcQ/-qCfJpPdgl8/s1600/images.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-PtxPhabOb0s/U7pWoKSpGlI/AAAAAAAADcQ/-qCfJpPdgl8/s1600/images.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Here I am continuing on with the previous post:
                     <br/>
                     http://7yearjourneytolifekims.blogspot.co.uk/2014/07/day-636-who-am-i-in-conflict.html -
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I am going to look at how I see myself within and as conflict -
                     <i>
                      Conflict refers to some form of friction, disagreement, or discord arising within a group when the beliefs or actions of one or more members of the group are either resisted by or unacceptable to one or more members of another group. Conflict can arise between members of the same group, known as intragroup conflict, or it can occur between members of two or more groups, and involve violence, interpersonal discord, and psychological tension, known as intergroup conflict. Conflict in groups often follows a specific course. Routine group interaction is first disrupted by an initial conflict, often caused by differences of opinion, disagreements between members, or scarcity of resources. At this point, the group is no longer united, and may split into coalitions. This period of conflict escalation in some cases gives way to a conflict resolution stage, after which the group can eventually return to routine group interaction once again. - Wikipedia
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The above description from wikipedia typifies what I have recently been involved in and witnessed, within my working environment and in a group situation outside of work.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Generally, when I look to see how I experience myself in this kind of conflict, I see that I am stable and calm and rarely these days lose my temper or react to, and towards another that is looking to create conflict or even how I react to others that have a strong mind/ idea about how things should be. However, where I do see that there is a slight movement still, is in the point of being 'glad' that it isn't me in the firing line so to speak, and within this I can sit back and hide in my secret mind about what is happening It is a kind of relief of " Whaoo nothing to do with me this time, cool"  I have realised that this point of relief comes from my upbringing of often believing when ever something went wrong in my environment I would be at fault somewhere. The point of self blame or believing that it was something I did wrong was pretty ingrained within and as me. There are times that, I still check myself and go over in my mind, my foot steps to make sure I really didn't put one foot wrong, so to speak, somewhere along the line and within this create or contribute to the conflict between others.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Now, with the situation of the man stepping out in front of me the other day and arrogantly walking across the road - didn't faze me immediately, it was only after I thought about it I started to become annoyed, then I thought about the two situations where there was conflict in the group and I was kind of glad that for a change it wasn't me. There are two different points that need looking at here and the first being glad for not being the one in the 'firing line' and on the flip side of that there was me firing abuse at another within and as my mind, so within this what I realised is that I will put another down, and create a conflict within and as myself and suppress, and back chat instead of speaking out and reacting in the moment, I allow it to build within and as me as back chat and play the superiority card to some extent that because I wasn't seen to react I am better than them, and instead I am hiding in my secret mind.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself in a moment be glad and relieved that another was the one being reacted to, because in a moment I created a belief within and as me that I am left alone or that I wasn't being singled out. And within this I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to create a belief within and as me that I am a 'good' girl, and that others will see me as such, as someone that isn't to be reacted to, or isn't reacting, all of this based on childhood memories and programming, of being punished in someway for messing up or being told I am not good/useless at certain things.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to have back chat about how I am a good person and that it is fortunate for me that another is the subject of reaction from others, or even being singled out as someone that creates friction in the group. And within this I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to be relieved that I am not alone as someone that is singled out
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to become paranoid within and as me that I will be singled out or the one at fault or 'wrong doing' as I have seen it in my minds eye.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to create a belief within and as me that I often fuck up or get 'things' wrong.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to create a belief within and as me that if I have back chat or slander another in my own mind or under my breath it doesn't matter.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to take anothers moods 'off day' or reaction personally and within this react in an offended stance of calling them names and feeling anger to and towards them.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to feel sorry for others that are in the firing line, so to speak, because within this I see how I would 'feel' if it were me.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to be secretly relieved that the onus in on another for a moment and not me.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to become paranoid and fearful of saying or doing the 'wrong' thing as I have defined it.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to create a belief within and as me that I am bound to be picked up or singled out for something I have said or done, and within this I have programmed myself to expect it. And I will even define myself by it by saying things like " I bet it was me, something is bound to go wrong"
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to create a belief within and as me that if something goes wrong, which causes others to be in conflict as I see it; that it is my fault in someway, that it was perhaps something I did, and within this I forgive myself that I have accepted and allowed myself to hold on to childhood memories of being told, " I bet you did that" or " Look what you have done now" and within this program myself as someone that is responsible for others being upset/unhappy or in conflict.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I will continue looking at this point of relief within and as me and what feelings it brings up - and that the opposite of this feeling is anxiety.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     To be continued....
