      ABOUT THE TALK
      <span class="mobArrow">
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="extraInfoBody">
      <p>
       Novelist Dara Horn uses the most unlikely of foundations to tackle the emotionally charged issue of anti-Semitism - a childrens book about a mouse dentist treating a carnivorous and ill-intentioned fox client. This deceptively simple story serves as an illustration of what Horn refers to as the Eicha problem, a uniquely Jewish approach to suffering and destruction. Text and tradition, psychology and education, history and communal responsibility all take center stage in this nuanced, theologically challenging talk.
      </p>

      ABOUT THE SPEAKER
      <span class="mobArrow">
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="speakerMob">
      <p>
       Dara Horn is the award-winning author of the novels In the Image (Norton 2002), The World to Come (Norton 2006), All Other Nights (Norton 2009), and A Guide for the Perplexed (Norton 2013), as well as the nonfiction book The Rescuer (Tablet 2012). She is one of Granta's "Best Young American Novelists" and the recipient of two National Jewish Book Awards among other national honors. She received her Ph.D. in Hebrew and Yiddish literature from Harvard University in 2006 and has taught Hebrew and Yiddish literature at Harvard, Sarah Lawrence College, and City University of New York, and has lectured at over 200 universities and cultural institutions throughout North America and in Israel. She lives in New Jersey with her husband and four children.
