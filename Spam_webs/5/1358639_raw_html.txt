          ALB Best of the Worst 2014: Kids and Parents
         </h1>
         <div class="iwd-postheadericons iwd-metadata-icons">

         <div class="iwd-postcontent iwd-postcontent-0 clearfix">
          <p>
           To kick off our Worst of 2014 posts, we are focusing on those books that would be likely to interest kids and parents. Our friend Doris had a banner year on this site with quite a few selections. I think she will always be a permanent fixture in our hall of fame. For your convenience, Holly and I started a category called
           <a href="http://awfullibrarybooks.net/category/the-doris-hall-of-infamy/">
            The Doris Hall of Infamy
           </a>
           . I urge you to use caution with this category as too much Doris can warp your sense of reality.We also included
           <a href="http://awfullibrarybooks.net/my-eyes-my-eyes/">
            Jennifer Jean, the Cross Eyed Queen
           </a>
           in this line up. (I think this authormust be friends with Doris.)
          </p>
          <p>
           My favorite is the
           <a href="http://awfullibrarybooks.net/parents-tv-mind-control/">
            Saturday Morning Mind Control
           </a>
           book with the premise that those cartoons like
           <em>
            Care Bears
           </em>
           ,
           <em>
            Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles
           </em>
           and
           <em>
            Casper the friendly ghost
           </em>
           are responsible for brainwashing children into a life of evil.
          </p>
          <p>
           Finally, I had to include the
           <a href="http://awfullibrarybooks.net/dolls-from-hell/">
            Dolls From Hell
           </a>
           book because they just creep me out.
          </p>
          <p>
           Tomorrow: We will be featuring some of the worst advice found @yourlibrary.
