    <div addthis:title="Ben Affleck As Batman: Should We Wait To Pass Judgment? " addthis:url="http://www.pocketfullofliberty.com/ben-affleck-as-batman-should-we-wait-to-pass-judgment/" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style addthis_32x32_style">

    <p>
     <img alt="affleck1" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-5660" height="426" src="http://www.pocketfullofliberty.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/affleck1-580x426.jpg" width="580"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Graeme McMillan at Wired thinks he should be given a chance
     <a href="http://www.wired.com/underwire/2013/08/ben-affleck-batman-2/" target="_blank">
      given his recent work
     </a>
     :
    </p>
    <blockquote>
     <p>
      Last night, Warner Bros announced that Ben Affleck would follow Christian Bale as the next Caped Crusader, taking on the role of Batman in 2015s still-unnamed follow-up to Zack Snyders
      <em>
       Man of Steel
      </em>
      . Within minutes, social media imploded.
     </p>
     <p>
      Really looking forward to seeing Affleck bring the depth and gravitas to Batman that he brought to Daredevil and Gigli,
      <a href="https://twitter.com/wilw/statuses/370728839376166912">
       snarked Wil Wheaton
      </a>
      . I hope he fights the Joke-ah! #wickedpissah
      <a href="https://twitter.com/pattonoswalt/statuses/370726641267245056">
       added Patton Oswalt
      </a>
      . Damon as Robin! Damon as Robin!
      <a href="https://twitter.com/JoshMalina/statuses/370717433218093056">
       demanded Joshua Malina
      </a>
      . Some were more philosophical about things  I can think of worse things plaguing this planet than Ben Affleck as Batman,
      <a href="https://twitter.com/jtorrescomics/statuses/370722894365855744">
       tweeted comic creator J. Torres
      </a>
      . Jennifer Garner as Wonder Woman, for example. Others just couldnt believe the news, like comic creator Frank Tieri, who
      <a href="https://twitter.com/FrankTieri/statuses/370718854617378816">
       asked
      </a>
      These Ben Affleck as Batman reports cant possibly be real, right? RIGHT?
     </p>
     <p>
      When fans finally accepted that it
      <em>
       was
      </em>
      real, some just couldnt quite deal with the news. Like John Roden, of Ludlow, KY, who
      <a href="http://www.change.org/petitions/warner-brothers-remove-ben-affleck-as-batman-bruce-wayne-in-the-superman-batman-movie">
       created a petition on Change.org to have the actor removed from the project
      </a>
      . His acting skill is not even close to being believable as Bruce Wayne and he wont do the role justice, Rodens introduction to the petition explains. Hes not built, nor is he intimidating enough for the role of Batman. His portrayal of Daredevil was atrocious and hes not remotely close to an action star. Please find someone else.
     </p>
     <p>
      Internet, I love you, but youre bringing me down.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ill admit it: I dont get the outright disdain and fear for the idea of Affleck playing Batman. Maybe its because I havent seen
      <em>
       Daredevil
      </em>
      in years  or perhaps its because I
      <em>
       have
      </em>
      seen movies like
      <em>
       Hollywoodland
      </em>
      ,
      <em>
       The Town
      </em>
      and
      <em>
       Argo
      </em>
      more recently, and think that Afflecks a pretty good actor when hes got decent material to work with. Coincidentally, I was watching
      <em>
       Argo
      </em>
      last night when I got the news about Affleck; When a friend texted to complain, I thought, But hes good?
     </p>
    </blockquote>
    <p>
     As a director, Ben Affleck is top notch. Hes directed three films  Gone Baby, Gone, The Town and his Oscar winning effort, Argo.
    </p>
    <p>
     But as an actor, even in his better efforts, Affleck is only..decent. Yes, he was good in The Town and Argo but in the former he was paired with Jeremy Renner who was excellent and in the latter, he shared screen time with Alan Arkin who stole every scene he was in.
    </p>
    <p>
     In addition, many actors are given the chance to play comic book heroes or villains and they get one shot. Sometimes it works and sometimes it does not.
    </p>
    <p>
     Michael Keaton (Batman), Tobey Maguire (Spiderman) and Robert Downey Jr. (Iron Man) might have seemed liked odd choices when they were chosen, but they pulled it off.
    </p>
    <p>
     Ryan Reynolds (The Green Lantern), George Clooney (Batman) and..Ben Affleck (Daredevil) could not.
    </p>
    <p>
     In the realm of super heroes, its one strike and youre out.
    </p>
    <p>
     Batman first had Michael Keaton. The franchise then suffered with the awful Val Kilmer (Would Batman ever say, Chicks dig the car.?) and the even worse George Clooney only to have the character rescued by Christian Bale (with an assist from Chris Nolan).
    </p>
    <p>
     Ben Affleck is just too much of a risk. He should stay behind the camera and give himself the supporting roles he has taken as of late. Hes been doing his best work there.
    </p>
