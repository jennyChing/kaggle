                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Cloud Security Features: Google Drive Stepping Up
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2331777463957756064" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AVQUrx-4ZzQ/VRxaEJj6CWI/AAAAAAAAAKk/-HSU9M7FtT0/s1600/edtech-cloud%2Bsecurity%2Bfeatures.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="Cloud Security Features" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AVQUrx-4ZzQ/VRxaEJj6CWI/AAAAAAAAAKk/-HSU9M7FtT0/s1600/edtech-cloud%2Bsecurity%2Bfeatures.jpg" title="Google Drive Stepping Up"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Apps are all the rage in education right now! It makes
sense, right? With technology use in education on the rise, the use of
applications is the next logical step.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      All
of the different options, however, can make it hard to keep track of everything
thats out there! Of course, some apps are bound to be better than others, with
their own risks and rewards.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      One of the
most promising application suites on the rise, however, belongs to none other
than tech giant Google.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <a name="more">
                      </a>
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <h4>
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        Taking the Cloud for
a Test Drive
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </b>
                      </h4>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="https://www.google.com/drive/">
                       Google Drive
                      </a>
                      is
a cloud-based suite for storing, sharing, and accessing any files you might
create or view in a typical office suite.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      With its Drive suite, Google even provides 15gb of storage space! Thats
more than enough space for your typical individual to store office files!
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      For the classroom, Google adds even more features.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      Google Drive for Education is another aspect
of the Drive suite, tailored specifically to educational institutions.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      Through
                      <a href="https://www.google.com/edu/products/productivity-tools/">
                       Drive for
Education
                      </a>
                      , Google provides easy access to collaboration tools, classroom
sharing, and even grading, all through their suite.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      The Education suite even provides deals and
access to devices for schools that are tailored to work efficiently with the
suite, with access to support as needed.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      From the tech side of things, thats a really great option!
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <h4>
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        Cloud Security
                        <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                         Features
                         <o:p>
                         </o:p>
                        </b>
                       </b>
                      </h4>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Unfortunately, though, most IT Heads and administrators look
at Cloud storage and sharing and see only the associated security risks.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      With so many data breaches making headlines,
and thousands of dollars of loss recorded every week, cloud security features are
definitely a valid concern. However, as we have all come to expect from Google,
the head honchos over there have taken steps to increase security and provide a
sense of relief and ease for those who would like access to their product, but
may be reluctant to do so.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      According to
                      <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2015/03/31/google-drive-for-work-and-education-get-new-data-security-features/?ncid=rss&amp;utm_source=feedburner&amp;utm_medium=feed&amp;utm_campaign=Feed%3A+Techcrunch+%28TechCrunch%29">
                       Techcrunch.com
                      </a>
                      ,
Google has just rolled out a brand new update for its Drive for Work and Drive
for Education suites with a huge array of new security and audit options
integrated directly within.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Interestingly enough, these tools are tailored almost
specifically to administrators within the environment.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      The new update allows admins access to the
following features: ability to restrict access to documents on a user or
department basis, regulate who may share specific documents (within or even
outside the institution if needed), and even receive alerts when certain
documents are shared or otherwise accessed when they shouldnt be.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      For the latter option, Techcrunch gives the
example of a user sharing a file with confidential in the headline: admins
would receive an alert and may investigate the situation further, without risking
data.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
