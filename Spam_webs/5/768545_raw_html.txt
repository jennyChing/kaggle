   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Alien abduction insurance
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      <b>
       Alien abduction insurance
      </b>
      is an insurance policy issued against
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abduction_phenomenon" title="Abduction phenomenon">
       alien abduction
      </a>
      . The insurance policy is redeemed if the insured person can prove they were abducted by aliens and returned to Earth.
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geico" title="Geico">
       Geico
      </a>
      insurance and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">
        The Daily Telegraph
       </a>
      </i>
      report that one English company has sold over 30,000 policies.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Some companies offer policies for alien pregnancy, alien examinations and death caused by aliens.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alien_abduction_insurance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The very first company to offer UFO abduction insurance was the St. Lawrence Agency in
      <a href="/wiki/Altamonte_Springs,_Florida" title="Altamonte Springs, Florida">
       Altamonte Springs, Florida
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfgate_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-sfgate-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The company pays the claimant $1 per year until their death or for 1 million years, whichever comes first.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfgate_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-sfgate-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Over 20,000
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      people have purchased the insurance. The insurance is normally purchased by the "
      <a href="/wiki/Feeble-minded" title="Feeble-minded">
       feeble-minded
      </a>
      ", according to Simon Burgess, former Managing Director and co-founder of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Insurance" title="British Insurance">
       British Insurance
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Heaven%27s_Gate_(religious_group)" title="Heaven's Gate (religious group)">
       Heaven's Gate religious group
      </a>
      had purchased alien abduction insurance before their mass suicide.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Their insurance company (London brokerage
      <i>
       Goodfellow Rebecca Ingrams Pearson (GRIP)
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      suspended sales of alien abduction insurance after the suicide but later resumed sales.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfgate_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-sfgate-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The policy has been sold to about four thousand people (mostly in England and the United States). At a cost of roughly $155 a year the GRIP policy would pay about $160,000 to someone who could show that they had been abducted by a being who was not from Earth. The payment would double if the insured person was impregnated during the event. Men were also able to purchase the impregnation insurance for protection against the unknown capabilities of alien technology.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
