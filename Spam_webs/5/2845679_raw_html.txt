     <p>
      <img alt="" class="aligncenter photo" height="500" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3287/5762147809_2fb9ed45a2.jpg" title="Vegan Taco Salad" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      For a long time after I started really focusing on eating healthy, I refused to eat iceberg lettuce because everyone always says how its nutritionally useless compared to other leafy greens. Which is definitely true  leafy greens are amazingly nutritious and have a ton of different vitamins and minerals in them, and iceberg really doesnt bring it in the same way. But then I started to feel kind of bad for itits not like its hurting anyone. I dont think theres any evidence that eating iceberg lettuce is
      <em>
       bad
      </em>
      for you unless you eat it instead of all the other delicious greens (and admittedly, a lot of people do only eat iceberg lettuce, which is probably not great).
     </p>
     <p>
      But if youre like me and get plenty of spinach, kale, and chard in your diet, then I dont see why you cant share the love with iceberg once in a while. Especially if its in a salad with lots of other vegetables and beans that have plenty of nutrition.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="419" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3284/5762690028_5d214dcd5d.jpg" title="Gluten Free Taco Salad" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Also, I needed it to make this shell thing for my taco salad. I supposed I could have used cabbage but it didnt seem like quite what I wanted for this particular meal.
     </p>
     <p>
      This is pretty easy to throw together  not super fast but not especially time consuming. Maybe half an hour  I roasted the vegetables under the broiler and everything else was ready by the time they were done. You just chop up some zucchini, squash, onion, mushrooms, or whatever good roasting vegetables you like and spread them out on a cookie sheet. You can either stick them under the broiler for maybe 10 minutes or else roast them at abour 450 for maybe 20 minutes, or until they are nice and browned.
     </p>
     <p>
      While your veggies are roasting, chop up some tomato, onion and cilantro and toss it together in a bowl with a pinch of salt:
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="332" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2646/5762144197_2cf3c5edde.jpg" title="Easy Salsa" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Then throw an avocado in the food processor along with maybe a quarter cup of your tomato mixture (you could also just mash it by hand if you dont want to get out the food processor). Youll end up with delicious guacamole:
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="332" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3233/5762687664_4e0d8bdac2.jpg" title="Guacamole Recipe" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Then open up a can of black beans (pinto would work great too) and rinse them, then mix them together with some cumin, chili powder, salt and pepper.
     </p>
     <p>
      The only thing left to do is chop or tear up some lettuce. If you want to make a shell just reserve a couple of the outer leaves and set them sideways on the plate before you add the rest of the lettuce. Add your beans, then your roasted veggies, then your tomatoes and guacamole. So delicious!
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="500" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3341/5762145893_eec07e094b.jpg" title="Taco Salad Recipe" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Then just make sure you have some nice spinach or kale for your next meal so the nutrition police dont come after you, and you should be good to go.
     </p>

          <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
           Ingredients:
          </p>
          <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0">
            1 small zucchini, sliced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1">
            6 mushrooms, sliced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2">
            1/2 an onion, sliced plus an additional 2 Tbsp diced onion
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3">
            1 small summer squash, sliced or chopped into 1/2 inch pieces
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4">
            1 can of black or pinto beans, rinsed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5">
            1 tsp cumin
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6">
            1 Tbsp chili powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7">
            1/4 tsp salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8">
            pinch pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9">
            1 avocado, peeled and pit removed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10">
            2 tomatoes, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11">
            1/4 cup cilantro, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12">
            1 head iceberg lettuce, a couple of the outer leaves removed to make a "shell," the rest chopped
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
           Directions:
          </p>
          <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
           <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0">
            Preheat oven to 450 or preheat broiler. Spread zucchini, mushrooms, onion, and squash out in a single layer and either broil for 10 minutes or roast for 20 minutes (either way keep an eye on them, you just want them slightly softened and browned).
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1">
            While vegetables cook, toss your beans with the cumin, chili powder, salt and pepper and set aside.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2">
            Mix tomatoes, 2 Tbsp diced onion, cilantro, and salt in a small bowl.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3">
            In a food processor, combine avocado and 1/4 cup of tomato mixture and blend until smooth. You could also just mash it with a fork if you prefer.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4">
            Once vegetables are done, assemble the salad on a plate by adding lettuce, then beans, then roasted vegetables, then tomato mixture, then guacamole. Serve immediately.
           </li>
          </ol>
