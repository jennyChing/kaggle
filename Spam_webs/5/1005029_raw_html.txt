        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Commander Keen Review
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-09-17T10:00:01+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          September 17, 2012
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://arcticsunburn.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            AS Andrew
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://arcticsunburn.com/commander-keen-review/#respond">
           <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="56 http://arcticsunburn.com/?p=56">
            Leave a Comment
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div style="margin: 10px 0;">
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- AS Movies &amp; Games Below Post -->

        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_57" style="width: 310px">
         <a href="http://arcticsunburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Commander-Keen.jpg">
          <img alt="Commander Keen" class="size-medium wp-image-57" height="187" src="http://arcticsunburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Commander-Keen-300x187.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          It may be old school, but its still a great game.
         </p>
        </div>
        A truly entertaining, original and challenging game,
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00005JY65/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00005JY65&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=arsuvigaremor-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Commander Keen
        </a>
        is a platformer in the vein of Duke Nukem (
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000034DMO/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B000034DMO&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=arsuvigaremor-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         1
        </a>
        or
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0009YWP1E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B0009YWP1E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=arsuvigaremor-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         2
        </a>
        ) or the former installations of Super Mario Bros. (
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000TUZCQI/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B000TUZCQI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=arsuvigaremor-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         1
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00004SVV8/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00004SVV8&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=arsuvigaremor-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         2
        </a>
        , or
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00004SVV9/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00004SVV9&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=arsuvigaremor-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         3
        </a>
        ). The game, in fact, plays a lot like the original Duke Nukem with less shooting.
        <p>
         Early game, incidentally, there is little reason to shoot. They dont provide you with much ammo throughout the game. Yet, shooting is a skill you have to master, because its necessary in order to beat the game. I use the term skill because you have to hit the ctrl key and alt key at the same time to produce a blast. This is my one disappointment. They really should have made it easier to fire your weapon, especially since your weapon gets used more frequently in the two sequels that follow. Ill have more on that later.
        </p>
        <div class="insert-post-ads" style="clear:both;float:left;width:100%;margin:0 0 20px 0;">
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- AS Movies &amp; Games Below Post -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-6283352745525853" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="4243340195" style="display:block">
         </ins>
         <script>
          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
        </div>
        <p>
         One of the fun aspects of the game is the exploration. In each level, you need to proceed to the exit to finish it, but sometimes getting to the exit is a difficult task. In some cases, the challenge lies in finding the exit, and other times
         <em>
          getting to
         </em>
         the exit. Each level is filled with various items (for points), charges for your blaster, and menacing foes. If you get too caught up in trying to get all the items in a level, youll find yourself dead before you can reach the finish.
        </p>
        <p>
         Overall, however, the challenge added to the game. It wouldnt be quite as fun if it were too easy to beat.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Commander Keen The Earth Explodes
        </h2>
        <p>
         This is everything a game sequel should be. New collectibles, new enemies, and a new weapon (well, its function is the same but it looks different). This time the setting for the game is the Vorticon ship. One bad move results in the detonation of Earth. Same fun game-play, more challenge.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Commander Keen Must Die
        </h2>
        <p>
         Another Commander Keen sequel? Great! Only this one isnt quite as good as its predecessors. They decided to change up the enemies and items again, but this time they didnt really receive a full makeover. Each level feels a bit too much like the last, and only gets progressively harder. More often than not, you find yourself looking for the quickest way out instead of exploring or dawdling. The charges you can obtain for your weapon are superfluous at times, and insufficient at other. The extra Keens you can get are unbalanced as well; too many early game, not enough late game. The game-play, of course, is basically the same as the last but theres a lot more shooting in this one.
        </p>
        <p>
         In all honesty, though, theres only so much bad I can say about the Commander Keen trilogy. After all, its Commander Keen; its a classic DOS game. It isnt just some sort of kids game, its a game thats challenging and fun, even for a seasoned gamer like me.
        </p>
        <p>
         Check out
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00005JY65/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00005JY65&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=arsuvigaremor-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Commander Keen on the Game Boy Color
         </a>
         .
        </p>
