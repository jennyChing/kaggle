        <h4 class="entry-title" id="post_2306">
         <a href="/users/chris/posts/2306-the-best-affordable-whiskey-6-top-shelf-bottles-under-40" rel="bookmark">
          The Best Affordable Whiskey: 6 Top Shelf Bottles Under $40
         </a>
        </h4>
        <div class="hentry">
         <div class="post-body">
          <p>
           <img height="346" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/manmadediy-uploads-production/photos/8624/tastingwhiskey_large.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="495"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           These cold winter months, with their insane wind chills and 5:00 p.m. sunsets, are the season of curling up with a tumbler of, as Don Draper says, "something big and brown." No mixers, shakers, or bar tools required - just a heavy glass, and a quality whiskey that can stand up on its own for some serious sipping. And if you know what you're looking for, there's no reason you have to spend more than $40 a bottle. Single malts included.
           <span class="pagebreak">
           </span>
           Here are six excellent whiskeys that are both widely available for $39.99 or less...and many, much less. Organized by style, they'll keep you warm all winter long.
          </p>
          <div class="centered" id="postContent" style="clear:both; margin: 2em 1em 4em 1em;">
           <div class="advertisement">
            <div id="div-gpt-ad-1385750840403-5">
             <script type="text/javascript">
              googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1385750840403-5'); });
             </script>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p id="jump">
           <img height="300.0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/manmadediy-uploads-production/photos/20778/41CLbjU5ftL._SL500_AA300__large.jpg?1358884216" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="300.0"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            1. Kentucky Bourbon: Buffalo Trace Straight Kentucky Bourbon - $21.99
            <br/>
           </strong>
           If you're looking for an every day straight bourbon, this one is hard to beat. Available for under $25, it's a serious best buy. It has strong vanilla flavors mixed with citrus and the oiliness of corn, making it great for sipping or mixing.
           <br/>
           <em>
            Other ideas: Bulleit Bourbon ($22.50), Eagle Rare Single Barrel 10 Year ($29.99), Russell's Reserve 10 Year ($34.99), Charter 101 Kentucky Straight ($17.99)
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <img height="275.0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/manmadediy-uploads-production/photos/20779/94299_large.jpg?1358884218" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="115.0"/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            2. Rye Whiskey: Bulleit 95 Small Batch Rye Whiskey - $23.99
            <br/>
           </strong>
           I love bourbon in classic whiskey cocktails like the Old Fashioned and Manhattan in the spring and summer, but opt for spicier rye in the cold weather months. Bulleit's relatively new rye has become a go-to - aged in the distillery's bourbon casks, it has plenty of toasty oak and smoothness, but at 95% rye content, allows that grain to dominate. Good.
           <br/>
           <em>
            Other ideas: Rittenhouse Rye 100 Proof ($25.99), Sazerac Straight Rye ($26.99)
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <img height="300." src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/manmadediy-uploads-production/photos/20780/chivas12_large.jpg?1358884221" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            3. Blended Scotch Whiskey: Chivas Regal 12 Years Old Blended Scotch - $28.99
           </strong>
           <br/>
           The Chivas 12 is great at what blended Scotches should be: easy to drink and easy to share. I like to keep a bottle of this (or Dewar's 12 [blue label]) for having friends over and enjoying while chatting after dinner or playing a board game. This is also a great choice for those that like to put ice in their whiskey, or mix with water or soda. It doesn't knock your socks off, but it's an excellent choice for first-timers or when it doesn't make sense to bust out the single malt.
           <br/>
           <em>
            Other ideas: Dewar's 12 Year ($27.99), The Black Grouse Blended Scotch ($28.99), Cutty Sark ($24.99)
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            4. Single Malt Scotch Whiskey:
           </strong>
           This category is the toughest, since there are so many styles, and lots to appreciate about each. So, I'll recommend two quite different bottles, each with lots to love.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img height="300." src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/manmadediy-uploads-production/photos/20781/LIQ-SMS-002-600X600-Glenmorangie_10Yr_1L_large.jpg?1358884223" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Glenmorangie 10 Years Old (Scottish Highlands) - $39.99
            </strong>
            Known by many as the best value in scotch, or "the perfect ten," Glenmorangie's standard is light golden, sweet, and fruity - tasting just like it smells. It's not big, nor very complex, but it's excellent, and a great first single malt to try for drinkers used to blended scotch. Sometimes, clean, easy, and straightforward is exactly what you're looking for. This is the one.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <img height="300." src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/manmadediy-uploads-production/photos/20782/LAPHROAIG_10_YEAR_OLD__55618.1276348912.600.900_large.jpg?1358884226" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Laphroaig 10 Year (Islay) - $39.99
            </strong>
            <em>
             Note: I've seen this as high as $45-50 in some stores, but I can regularly find it at $40.00. It's still a great bang-for-your-buck
            </em>
            <em>
             value at $50.
            </em>
            Islay-region whiskeys are, literally, off the coast from the much larger Highland region, and their malts are quite different. Laphroaig is among the most intense whiskeys you can find, which huge peaty, smoky, and salt flavors. It tastes of the sea, of the beach, and it's as distinctive as a $40 bottle can get. No, it's not the ultimate Islay scotch, but it's the one that costs less than $70, and you'll be willing to share. A personal favorite. If you're looking where to step up from here, try Laphroaig's Quarter Cask or Lagavulin 16.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <em>
            Other ideas: Aberfeldy 12 ($39.99), Glenlivet 12 ($32.99), Singleton of Glendullen 12 ($36.00)
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <img height="300." src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/manmadediy-uploads-production/photos/20783/michael-collins-10-year-old-lightly-peated-single-malt-irish-whiskey-ireland-10306263_large.jpg?1358884228" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            5. Blended Whiskey: Michael Collins Blended Irish Whiskey - $28.99
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Plenty of countries other than Scotland make great whiskey - the United States, Japan, Canada, Wales. Great for mixing and barroom favorites, these whiskeys aren't inferior, just of a different tradition. My favorite in this category is Michael Collins from Ireland. It tastes of almonds and ripe fruit, moves around your palette well, and is quite complex for a sub-$30 bottle. It may take some getting used to, but it's a great value.
           <br/>
           <em>
            Other ideas: Tullamore Dew (Ireland, $28.00), Canadian Mist Black Diamond (Canada, $14.00), Barton (Ireland, $19.99)
           </em>
          </p>
