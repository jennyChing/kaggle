         <div class="text_article_extra">
          The aftermath of star Paul Walker's death will naturally cause a production delay.
         </div>
         <div class="article_body">
          <div class="article_body_text ">
           Hollywood and the car world is still in shock over the sudden and tragic death of actor and devoted gear head Paul Walker. At the time of the lethal accident Walker was in the final weeks of production for "Fast and Furious 7" but still had a few key scenes left to shoot, which were scheduled to happen this week in Atlanta, Georgia. Executives from Universal Studios and the films director, James Wan, are currently figuring out how to handle things from here, and how exactly theyll deal with Walkers Brian OConnor character.

          </div>
         </div>

            </a>

           Walker had been filming the movie since September and was on a Thanksgiving weekend hiatus when the horrific car crash occurred. Its expected that Brian OConnor will be written out of the franchise in some way, but the big question is how. For a blockbuster movie thats all about high-speed car chases, stunts and explosions, its going to be a challenge to find an appropriate sendoff. Killing Brian OConnor off would simply be seen as a lack of sensitivity to Walkers many fans, family and friends, so something else is going to have to be thought up, and quickly.
