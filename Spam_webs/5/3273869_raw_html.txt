       </a>
       <h1 itemprop="headline">
        Marcos Chin
       </h1>
      </div>
      <a class="swipebox" href="http://www.design42day.com/uploads/Marcos-Chin-2.png" title="Marcos-Chin-2">
       <img alt="Marcos-Chin-2 - Cover" itemprop="image" src="http://www.design42day.com/uploads/Marcos-Chin-2.png"/>
      </a>
      <div id="testo" itemprop="articleBody">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Marcos Chin
        </strong>
        is a multi-talented and award-winning graphical illustrator and artist based in Brooklyn in New York. He works with a limited palette of colour, carefully chosen for each project. His graphics are clear and distinct illustrations with echoes of Asian art traditions in the way he
        <strong>
         treats form
        </strong>
        and structure. This may be explained by the fact that he is ethnically Chinese, born and in Mozambique. The family later moved to Toronto in Canada. He is a graduate Ontario College of Art and Design (OCAD) in Toronto.
       </p>
       <p>
        He has been working as a graphic illustrator since 2002 with clients such as Google, MTA Arts for Transit, Neiman Marcus, Fiat, Budweiser, Time, Rolling Stone, The New Yorker, GQ, Sports Illustrated, The Wallstreet Journal, Flare, Elle Canada, Financial Times and The New York Times. In 2014, he produced a version of the
        <strong>
         Kama Sutra
        </strong>
        , using plant and nature metaphors to express the erotic nature of the book, published by Penguin India. Recently, he collaborated with the writer Mallory Kasdan on a childrens book for 5 to 8 year olds, Ella, published by Viking Books for Young Readers in January 2015. He is also running his own T-shirt brand
        <strong>
         YEE YEE
        </strong>
        , with a range of designs mainly expressed as patterned textiles, making the T-shirts look both stylish and casual.
       </p>
       <p>
        In addition to his work in the highly competitive business world of
        <strong>
         graphical illustrations
        </strong>
        , he lectures both in the US and abroad. He is teaching Illustration at the School of Visual Arts in New York.
       </p>
