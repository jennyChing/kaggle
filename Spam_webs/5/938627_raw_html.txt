                 <div class="rbi-art-detail" xmlns:custommethod="urn:custExtension" xmlns:msxml="urn:schemas-microsoft-com:xslt" xmlns:msxsl="urn:schemas-microsoft-com:xslt" xmlns:rbi="urn:rbi-couk:xslt">
                  <div class="rbi-art-header">
                   <h1>
                    Israel in talks with USA over F-22 orders
                   </h1>
                   <h2>
                   </h2>
                   <div class="ArticleSubHeader">
                    <div class="articleOwner">
                     By:
                     <a href="http://www.flightglobal.com/landingpage/Arie Egozi.html ">
                      ArieEgozi
                     </a>
                    </div>
                    <div class="articleSite">
                     Tel Aviv
                    </div>
                    <div class="articleSource">
                     Source: Flightglobal.com
                     <div class="tooltip purple">
                      This story is sourced from Flightglobal.com
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="publishedDate" id="publishedDateDiv121">
                    </div>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                  <div class="rbi-art-body">
                   <p>
                    Israel has approached the
                    <st1:place w:st="on">
                     <st1:country-region w:st="on">
                      USA
                     </st1:country-region>
                    </st1:place>
                    about acquiring
                    <a href="http://www.lockheedmartin.com/f22/">
                     LockheedMartin F-22s
                    </a>
                    as concern mounts about new threats to the countrys regional air superiority.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Sources say that the issue was raised during a one-day trip by
                    <st1:country-region w:st="on">
                     US
                    </st1:country-region>
                    defence secretary Robert Gates to
                    <st1:place w:st="on">
                     <st1:country-region w:st="on">
                      Israel
                     </st1:country-region>
                    </st1:place>
                    last week.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <st1:state w:st="on">
                     Washington
                    </st1:state>
                    has so far not authorised the export of the
                    <a href="http://www.af.mil/factsheets/factsheet.asp?fsID=199" target="_blank" title="Factsheet">
                     Raptorstealth fighter
                    </a>
                    , but sources in
                    <st1:country-region w:st="on">
                     <st1:place w:st="on">
                      Israel
                     </st1:place>
                    </st1:country-region>
                    say this is the first time a reasonable request has been made.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Among the subjects discussed with the countrys prime minister and defence minister were
                    <st1:country-region w:st="on">
                     Israel
                    </st1:country-region>
                    s concerns about the
                    <st1:country-region w:st="on">
                     USA
                    </st1:country-region>
                    s possible sale of advanced weaponry to
                    <st1:country-region w:st="on">
                     <st1:place w:st="on">
                      Saudi Arabia
                     </st1:place>
                    </st1:country-region>
                    and the Israeli intelligence assessment of the Iranian nuclear programme.
                   </p>
                   <p align="center">
                    <img alt="f-22" border="0" src="/assets/getasset.aspx?itemid=17327" style="border-right: 1px solid; border-top: 1px solid; margin: 2px; border-left: 1px solid; width: 445px; border-bottom: 1px solid; height: 297px"/>
                   </p>
                   <h3 align="left">
                    F-22 Specifications:
                   </h3>
                   <p align="left">
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Primary function:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     Air dominance fighter
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Official nickname:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     Raptor
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Airframe builders:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     <a href="/landingpage/Lockheed%20Martin.html">
                      Lockheed Martin
                     </a>
                     (
                     <st1:city w:st="on">
                      Marietta
                     </st1:city>
                     ,
                     <st1:country-region w:st="on">
                      Georgia
                     </st1:country-region>
                     , and
                     <st1:city w:st="on">
                      Fort Worth
                     </st1:city>
                     ,
                     <st1:state w:st="on">
                      Texas
                     </st1:state>
                     ) and
                     <a href="/landingpage/Boeing.html">
                      Boeing
                     </a>
                     (
                     <st1:place w:st="on">
                      <st1:city w:st="on">
                       <a href="/landingpage/Seattle%20Tacoma%20International.html">
                        Seattle
                       </a>
                      </st1:city>
                      ,
                      <st1:state w:st="on">
                       Washington
                      </st1:state>
                     </st1:place>
                     )
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Engine builder:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     Pratt &amp; Whitney (
                     <st1:place w:st="on">
                      <st1:city w:st="on">
                       East Hartford
                      </st1:city>
                      ,
                      <st1:state w:st="on">
                       Connecticut
                      </st1:state>
                     </st1:place>
                     )
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Power plant:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     Two Pratt &amp; Whitney F119-PW-100 turbofan engines with afterburners and two-dimensional thrust-vectoring nozzles. Each engine is in the 35,000-lb-thrust class.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Length:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    62 ft. 1 in.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Wingspan:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    44 ft. 6 in.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Height:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    16 ft. 5 in.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Top speed:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    Mach 2 class
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Supercruise speed:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    Mach 1.5+
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Ceiling and weight:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    Classified
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Armament:
                    </strong>
                    <span>
                     Internally, six radar-guided AIM-120C advanced medium-range air-to-air missiles (or two 1,000-pound class GBU-32 joint direct attack munitions in place of four of the AIM-120Cs) in main weapons bay; two heat-seeking AIM-9 Sidewinder short-range air-to-air missiles in side weapons bays (one in each bay).
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     One M61A2 20 mm multi-barrel cannon.
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     Four external stations can carry additional stores (weapons or fuel tanks)
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Crew:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    Pilot only
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      First flight:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     September 7, 1997
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Flight test aircraft:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     Nine
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Initial operational capability:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     December 15, 2005
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <span>
                     <strong>
                      Planned production:
                     </strong>
                    </span>
                    <span>
                     Current
                     <a href="/landingpage/USAF.html">
                      USAF
                     </a>
                     requirements:381 aircraft
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p align="left">
                   </p>
