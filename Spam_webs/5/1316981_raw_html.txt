        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         miso &amp; sesame kale chips
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2011-10-21T04:46:06+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 21, 2011
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.spabettie.com/author/kristina/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Kristina Sloggett
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/2011/10/21/miso-sesame-kale-chip-recipe/#comments">
           68 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/DSC_9034.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8636" height="499" src="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/DSC_9034.jpg" title="kale chips" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.veganmofo.com/" style="text-decoration: none;">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8635" height="84" src="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/vegan_mofo8.jpg" title="vegan_mofo" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Have you ever eaten two whole trays of kale chips in one afternoon? Yeah, me either.
        </p>
        <p>
         One of my birthday gifts last month was a dehydrator. Ive been wanting one for some time, so this is definitely not a Homer Gift.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have a long list of dehydrator ideas, but I had to break it in with a classic.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/DSC_8969.jpg" style="text-decoration: none;">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8632" height="499" src="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/DSC_8969.jpg" title="kale chips" width="499"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         oops, theres the photographic evidence of just how many kale chips I
         <em>
          scarfed
         </em>
         in a matter of hours.
        </p>
        <p>
         I couldnt stop. I tried to save some for Jason, I really did. Good thing they are so easy to make.
        </p>
        <h2>
         miso sesame kale chips
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          vegan, gluten free, dairy free
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          kale
          <br/>
          miso dressing
          <br/>
          sesame seeds
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Wash and dry kale. Tear / cut leaves into small pieces, removing ribs and stems. Coat kale pieces with miso dressing (toss in bowl or massage with hands) and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Arrange in single layer on baking pan or dehydrator sheet.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Oven: bake at 250-275 for 45  60 minutes*.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Dehydrator: 145 for 2-3 hours*.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          *Depending on thickness of dressing application, these times may vary. I made these a second time with a thicker application of miso dressing, dehydrated for four hours. Check and flip.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h2>
         miso dressing
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          vegan, gluten free, dairy free
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1/4 cup smooth miso paste
          <br/>
          1-2 tablespoons olive oil
          <br/>
          juice of two lemons
          <br/>
          1 green onion, minced fine
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Whisk all ingredients  adjusting liquids for desired consistency.
          <del>
           Insert straw and drink
          </del>
          Storein airtight container in fridge.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I make this miso dressing thinner for salads, thicker to use as a dip or spread (delicious on sandwiches and wraps!). Thick or thin consistency, this works well as a kale chip coating!
        </p>
        <p>
         These have incredible flavor-
         <em>
          very umami
         </em>
         . I make these in small batches now, especially if I am alone. I cannot be trusted around them!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/DSC_9027.jpg" style="text-decoration: none;">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8633" height="500" src="http://www.spabettie.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/DSC_9027.jpg" title="kale chips" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Are you a miso fan? What about kale chips?
         </strong>
        </p>
