         <h1>
          Chicago Startups Looking Out for the Environment
         </h1>
         <div class="user-info clearfix">
          <div class="user-detail">
           <img alt="found but no image" src="http://chicago.hubdin.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Andrea-Racine-150x150.jpg"/>
           By: Andrea
          </div>
          <div class="real-estate-btn">
           <a href="http://chicago.hubdin.com/tag/giving-back/">
            Giving Back
           </a>
           <!--&lt;a href="#" class="active"&gt;Real Estate&lt;/a&gt;-->
           <a href="http://chicago.hubdin.com/tag/tech/">
            Tech
           </a>
           <!--&lt;a href="#" class="active"&gt;Real Estate&lt;/a&gt;-->
           <a href="http://chicago.hubdin.com/tag/where-to-work/">
            Where to Work
           </a>
           <!--&lt;a href="#" class="active"&gt;Real Estate&lt;/a&gt;-->
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="blog-content-text padding-top-lg">
          <div class="blog-content-text">
           <div class="blog-img">
            <img alt="Chicago Startups Looking Out for the Environment" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="407" src="http://chicago.hubdin.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/4/2014/11/Chicago-Startups-Looking-Out-for-the-Environment.jpg" width="750"/>
            <figcaption>
            </figcaption>
           </div>
           <p>
            It seems like in Chicago right now its impossible to turn a corner without running into a startup company. But some of these startups should stand out above the rest because of their commitment to going green and changing the shape of the environment for today, and for our futures. Here are a few we think everyone should check out, as they are not only green, but can help benefit your everyday life as well.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.getampy.com/" target="_blank" title="AMPY">
             <strong>
              AMPY
             </strong>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Developed by engineering grads of Northwestern University, AMPY is a wearable device that takes energy produced by the movement of your body and allows you to charge your smart phone, or anything else that uses a USB battery charger. AMPY is made so that you can place it anywhere on your body, so that whenever you move, even without realizing it, youre working up energy. Just walking throughout the day can build up to three hours of charge for a smartphone. AMPY is first step in green, renewable energy for charging the things most people use constantly throughout the day. But it also means that if youre out, and your phone is getting ready to die, you can just plug it into AMPY and youre good to go.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.nobi.co/" target="_blank" title="Nobi">
             <strong>
              Nobi
             </strong>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Founded in January of this year, Nobi is an electronic buyback company that works to reduce electronic waste in landfills by recycling old cellphones and computers. Focusing on iPhones, iPads, Blackberrys, and Androids, Nobi purchases old electronics from corporations and schools with the goal of putting zero percent of the waste back into landfills. By working in ISO certified properties, anything that can be recycled or reused is, reducing the huge impact that technological waste is having on our environment today.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.subscriptionlighting.com/" target="_blank" title="Verde">
             <strong>
              Verde
             </strong>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Verde is looking out for the environment in perhaps one of the simplest ways out there: by changing light bulbs. Companies sign up for Verde by paying a monthly fee, while Verde changes all of their lighting to LED, low energy-use lights. VERDEs guarantee is that by switching to LED lights, companies are bound to save move money than their fee of $29 per month. Not only is Verde working to reduce energy bills and therefore saving companies money, but it is working to reduce energy consumption and make something as ubiquitous as lighting sustainable.
           </p>
           <p>
            These are just a few of the companies looking out for the future of the environment by creatingsimple, forward-thinking changes. Supporting one or more of them can lead to a better environment and a healthierworld to come.
           </p>

      function loadScroll()
      {
          var elem = $('#sidebar-wrapper');
          if (!elem.attr('data-top')) {
              if (elem.hasClass('sub-navbar-fixed-top'))
                  return;
              var offset = elem.offset()
              elem.attr('data-top', offset.top);
          }
          if (elem.attr('data-top') &lt;= $(this).scrollTop()+40 ){
              elem.addClass('sub-navbar-fixed-top');
              jQuery("#top-content").addClass("fix-from-header");


          } else {
              elem.removeClass('sub-navbar-fixed-top');
              jQuery("#top-content").removeClass("fix-from-header");
          }
      }
