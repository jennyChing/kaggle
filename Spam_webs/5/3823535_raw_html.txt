     <h2>
      Architectural Renderings by Hugh Ferriss
     </h2>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6454" height="359" src="http://www.dailyicon.net/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/ferriss01dailyicon.jpg" title="ferriss01dailyicon" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6461" height="433" src="http://www.dailyicon.net/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/ferriss02dailyicon.jpg" title="ferriss02dailyicon" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6459" height="309" src="http://www.dailyicon.net/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/ferriss06dailyicon.jpg" title="ferriss06dailyicon" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6460" height="329" src="http://www.dailyicon.net/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/ferriss03dailyicon.jpg" title="ferriss03dailyicon" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6455" height="345" src="http://www.dailyicon.net/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/ferriss07dailyicon.jpg" title="ferriss07dailyicon" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6453" height="402" src="http://www.dailyicon.net/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/ferriss04dailyicon.jpg" title="ferriss04dailyicon" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6452" height="493" src="http://www.dailyicon.net/magazine/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/ferriss05dailyicon.jpg" title="ferriss05dailyicon" width="500"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Hugh Ferriss
      </strong>
      (1889  1962) was an American delineator (one who creates perspective drawings of buildings) and architect. According to
      <strong>
       Daniel Okrent, Ferriss
      </strong>
      never designed a single noteworthy building, but after his death a colleague said he influenced my generation of architects more than any other man.
      <strong>
       Ferriss
      </strong>
      also influenced popular culture, for example Gotham City (the setting for Batman) and
      <strong>
       Kerry Conrans
      </strong>
      Sky Captain and the World of Tomorrow. Just Imagine (movie from 1930), strongly influenced by
      <strong>
       Hugh Ferrisss
      </strong>
      book,
      <strong>
       Metropolis of Tomorrow
      </strong>
      (1929), takes the archetype vision of the future city as defined by a Manhattan-like skyline, and portrays it in all its beauty and majesty.
     </p>
     <p>
      Drawings by
      <a href="http://www.columbia.edu/cu/lweb/indiv/avery/da/ferriss.html" target="_blank">
       Hugh Ferriss
      </a>
      , view the
