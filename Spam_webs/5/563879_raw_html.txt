        <h1 class="entry-title">
         The Poem Patrol, Volume One: The Empty Inbox and Perfection
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-date">
          <a href="http://www.tonyheld.hoboandbowser.net/the-poem-patrol-volume-one-the-empty-inbox-and-perfection/" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-03-12T18:11:38+00:00">
            March 12, 2015
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.tonyheld.hoboandbowser.net/author/tony/" rel="author">
            Tony
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         I used to post a lot of poetry to Bubblews.com. The massive changes recently made to the site, as well as a (thankfully resolved) pay dispute with them has sapped my enthusiasm for posting to the site.
        </p>
        <p>
         I will continue to make occasional posts there, but they will not include short stories or poetry. With that in mind, I am starting to bring aboard The Write Life my Bubblews poetry in posts I hereby dub The Poem Patrol.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kicking off volume one, here are my poems The Empty Inbox and Beach Memory.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2950" style="width: 300px;">
         <a href="http://www.tonyheld.hoboandbowser.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/The-Empty-Inbox-sign.png">
          <img alt="The Empty Inbox sign" class="wp-image-2950 size-medium" height="232" src="http://www.tonyheld.hoboandbowser.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/The-Empty-Inbox-sign-300x232.png" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Image copyright (C) 2015 by Tony Held, all rights reserved.
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Every day I awake
         <br/>
         And go log in to my email
         <br/>
         Hoping that today I make
         <br/>
         Some money doing the work I love
         <br/>
         But no messages from new clients
         <br/>
         Ever greet my eyes.
         <br/>
         How I loathe and despise
         <br/>
         The sight of that empty inbox!
        </p>
        <p>
         What a screw!
         <br/>
         Oh how I rue
         <br/>
         The day I assumed
         <br/>
         All would be fine
         <br/>
         Once I decided to go into business
         <br/>
         As a freelancer who edited written lines
         <br/>
         Marching down a printed page.
        </p>
        <p>
         My bank account threatens to drop into the red
         <br/>
         More often than it is in the black
         <br/>
         No matter what I have done or said
         <br/>
         To promote my talents to potential clients
         <br/>
         Oh alas and alack,
         <br/>
         Why am I such a stumbling fool
         <br/>
         Who cannot tap into a pool
         <br/>
         Of steady work?!
        </p>
        <p>
         Why do I keep having these damn dry spells
         <br/>
         That make me sit in penurys jail cells?
         <br/>
         Why do I keep running into jerks
         <br/>
         Who refuse to hire me
         <br/>
         After wasting my time
         <br/>
         Claiming they wanted to spend their dime
         <br/>
         On my talents?
        </p>
        <p>
         The empty inbox is a place worse than hell!
         <br/>
         Why
         <br/>
         Why
         <br/>
         Why
         <br/>
         Cannot my business start to fly
         <br/>
         And forever lift the curse
         <br/>
         Of the empty inbox from me?
        </p>
        <p>
         (Poem originally published onJune 30th, 2014.)
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3136" style="width: 300px;">
         <a href="http://www.tonyheld.hoboandbowser.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/perfection-sign.png">
          <img alt="perfection sign" class="wp-image-3136 size-medium" height="162" src="http://www.tonyheld.hoboandbowser.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/perfection-sign-300x162.png" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Image copyright (C) 2015 by Tony Held, all rights reserved.
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         When I see people doing things I wish I could achieve
         <br/>
         But know they are things beyond my ability
         <br/>
         No matter how small a challenge they might seem
         <br/>
         I want to break down and start crying bitter tears.
        </p>
        <p>
         Why cannot I be perfect?
        </p>
        <p>
         Why cannot I do each and every thing in the universe?
        </p>
        <p>
         If I could achieve perfection
         <br/>
         Goes my line of reasoning,
         <br/>
         I could then become eternally at peace
         <br/>
         With myself.
        </p>
        <p>
         But every human being is imperfect,
         <br/>
         So why should I hurl angry derision at myself because I am not its
         <br/>
         Opposite?
        </p>
        <p>
         Why cannot I find happiness with myself, flaws and all?
        </p>
        <p>
         The lust for perfection burns in every persons soul,
         <br/>
         Be they healthy, crippled, young or old 
         <br/>
         But it is something we are not meant to be.
        </p>
        <p>
         (Originally published on Bubblews.com December 10th, 2013.)
        </p>
