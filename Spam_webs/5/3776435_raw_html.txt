     <div class="Title">
      visiondivision: hollow
     </div>
     <div class="Info">
      <div class="Social addthis_toolbox">
       <div class="Square">

     <p>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font size="1">
       interior
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      hollow is an organic pavilion that changes the conditions for social interaction and behavior. simply built,
      <br/>
      using compression to hold the structure together, the concept is designed by
      <a href="http://thegoldbrain.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">
       visiondivision
      </a>
      <br/>
      (ulf mejergren and anders berensson) as their competition entry for
      <a href="http://www.sukkahcity.com/" target="_blank">
       sukkah city
      </a>
      , where
      <a href="/weblog/cat/8/view/10163/sukkah-city-competition.html" target="_blank">
       designers / architects were asked
       <br/>
       to design a modern sukkah
      </a>
       a temporary hut created for an annual jewish harvest festival  in union square, new york city.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="614" src="/cms/images/andrea10/hollow01.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <font size="1">
       hollow
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      some of the constraints implied in the competition, for example, was that the sukkah had to have a roof made
      <br/>
      out of branches, but that they could not be in bundles. this in turn influenced visiondivisions decision to use the
      <br/>
      construction technique of compression to build hollow.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="600" src="/cms/images/andrea10/hollow03.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <font size="1">
       the sukkah was designed for one of the most populated places on earth  union square, new york city
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      the competition asked teams to design a sukkah to be erected in one of the most populated and dense places on earth,
      <br/>
      filled with built structures and a stressful ambience, changing its function from its initial protection purpose
      <br/>
      from sandy winds and a blazing sun to a shelter from building mass, infrastructure, scale, city pace and constant movement.
     </p>
     <p>
      the swedish duos intention is to build something which contrasts the scale and pace of the rest of the city.
      <br/>
      when entering the sukkah, you automatically slow your pace and behaviour, switching to more restful position as a result
      <br/>
      of the physical restrictions which make up the small space.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="628" src="/cms/images/andrea10/hollow04.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <font size="1">
       birds-eye view
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="606" src="/cms/images/andrea10/hollow05.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <font size="1">
       model of hollow constructed from straws
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="369" src="/cms/images/andrea10/hollow06.gif" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <font size="1">
       drawing of profile
      </font>
      <br/>
      <img alt="" height="448" src="/cms/images/andrea10/hollow16.gif" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <font size="1">
       drawing of the floor plan
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      hollow consists of two interlinked rooms; a social area for dining and interactions and a more closed space for
      <br/>
      sleeping and star gazing. the undulating ceiling  not higher than a mans average height  changes the conditions
      <br/>
      for social interaction and behavior. the different heights and thickness of the roof sets the atmosphere and the
      <br/>
      hollow reeds change its light and visible permeability by how thick it is. because of the the size of the sukkah,
      <br/>
      one is no longer able to perform as they would under normal circumstances. speed and strength is reduced and
      <br/>
      you are protected from the potential physical threat of your surroundings.
      <br/>
      <img alt="" height="475" src="/cms/images/andrea10/hollow08.gif" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <font size="1">
       topography of the undulating roof
      </font>
     </p>

      <img alt="" height="507" src="/cms/images/andrea10/hollow09.gif" title="" width="818"/>
