         <h2>
          A live parrot, a glass eye and a bag of sand are just a few of the many bizarre items left behind on planes each year, according to our survey of 700 international cabin crew.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Cabin crew members revealed an extensive list of items they have found on flights after passengers have disembarked. Animals and birds account for several of the more unusual items, including a falcon, frog, tortoise, and a (live) parrot.
         </p>
         <p>
          Dentures, underwear, a glass eye and a prosthetic leg were also on the long list of weird and wonderful items abandoned at the end of a flight. Other random curiosities discovered include a single egg (without packaging), a bag of sand, and a written marriage proposal. Whether the latter was intended for a cabin crew member or a passenger is unclear. And lets hope the wedding dress left in the overhead locker found its owner before the big day.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           More
          </strong>
          :
          <a href="http://www.skyscanner.net/news/cabin-crew-reveal-how-get-upgrade">
           Cabin crew reveal how to get an upgrade
          </a>
         </h3>
         <p>
          When it comes to the more regular travel essentials, it seems that passengers are no less forgetful. Almost a quarter (24%) of crew have found a lost passport on board, which is even higher than the number of phones (23%) and books (21%) left behind.
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are just some of the most random items that cabin crew have found on a plane:
         </p>
         <p>
           Live parrot
          <br/>
           Glass eye
          <br/>
           Prosthetic leg
          <br/>
           A bag of sand
          <br/>
           A clog
          <br/>
           Box of dried fish
          <br/>
           One shoe
          <br/>
           Wedding dress
          <br/>
           Bag of diamonds
          <br/>
           Wig
          <br/>
           Bag of onions
          <br/>
           Toupee
          <br/>
           Handcuffs
          <br/>
           Double bass
          <br/>
           One egg (without packaging)
          <br/>
           Frog
          <br/>
           Underwear
          <br/>
           Written marriage proposal
         </p>
         <p>
          Skyscanner conducted a survey of 700 cabin crew from 83 countries through
          <a href="http://www.cabincrew.com/">
           <em>
            Cabincrew.com
           </em>
          </a>
