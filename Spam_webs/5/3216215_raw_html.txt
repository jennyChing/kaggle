         <h1 class="entry-title single-post-title" itemprop="name">
          Easy Morning Yoga Stretches
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta meta-main-img">
          <span class="post-date updated">
           <i class="fa fa-clock-o">
           </i>
           Apr 17, 2015
          </span>
          <span class="meta-cat">
           <i class="fa fa-book">
           </i>
           <a href="http://foodhomelifestyle.com/category/health/" rel="category tag">
            Health
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://foodhomelifestyle.com/category/life/" rel="category tag">
            Life
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="meta-comment">
           <a href="http://foodhomelifestyle.com/morning-yoga-stretches/#respond">

           </a>
          </span>
         </p>

        <div class="clearfix">
        </div>
        <div class="post_content">
         <p>
          Yoga is experiencing a surge in popularity right now, but its not just for Lululemons Who Lunch. Regular people of any age and ability can do yoga anywhere. You dont have to take a $15 class to get all the benefits.
         </p>
         <p>
          In fact, if youre just starting out, doing yoga at home may be the best way to begin. Its easy to get caught up in where you rank in the room when you attend a class, and yoga isnt a sport. I was struck by an article I read once by a yoga teacher who said the best student she ever had was a man who was blind. She said that he couldnt do most of the poses fully, even things like a simple forward bend. But she said that since he was unable to see the others in his class, he practiced each pose to his own full extent, getting the most out of each pose without being concerned about anyone around him.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="morning yoga stretch - yoga mat" class="aligncenter wp-image-706 size-large" height="960" src="http://foodhomelifestyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/IMG_4603-1024x1024.jpg" width="960"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Dont get me wrong, yoga classes are great for learning proper technique and how to go deeper into poses that come easy to you. But the temptation to compare yourself or your practice to others is greater for some people, especially those with a naturally competitive nature.
         </p>
         <p>
          Check out some of the many free yoga podcasts available for professional instruction, and practice in front of a full-length mirror to check your alignment and to be sure you are doing poses correctly. Push yourself to the point that you feel challenged in the pose, but not to the point that you are in pain.
         </p>
         <p>
          This morning sequence is a great way to waken your body to the day and get yourself in the right frame of mind. Focus on what you need for the day as you go through the poses, whether thats patience, wisdom, peace, or something else. Then practice giving that to yourself as you feel challenged in each pose.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Easy Morning Yoga Stretch Sequence:
         </h2>
         <ol>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.yogajournal.com/article/beginners/learn-malasana/" target="_blank" title="link to yoga journal article about squats">
            Squat
           </a>
            This pose will build heat in your body and help wake up your muscles. Stand with feet about E3 feet apart and pointed forward. Bring your arms to prayer position in the middle of your chest and sink down into your hips, bringing elbows to meet knees. Keep your knees outside your toes to avoid injury. Sit here for a few breaths. Return to standing on an exhale.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.womenshealthmag.com/fitness/how-to-do-a-plank" target="_blank">
            Plank
           </a>
            Lay on your belly with forearms under you, and rise into a push-up position, resting on the forearms. Focus on keeping shoulders down and relaxed, butt down and in line with your body and face relaxed. Breathe.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.artofliving.org/yoga/yoga-poses/triangle-pose-trikonasana" target="_blank" title="link to art of living - triangle pose">
            Triangle
           </a>
            Place feet about three feet apart, keeping one foot facing forward and turning one foot at a 90-degree angle. Sweep the arm on the turned-out side down as far as you can comfortably go, whether its resting on your knee, ankle, or the floor, and look up. Create space in your ribs on the bottom side and dont allow it to collapse. Bring your body in line with your leg, like you are a sail on a ship. Raise your arm on the forward-facing foot side straight up so that a triangle could be drawn to connect it and your feet. Breathe. Repeat on the other side.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.gaiamtv.com/pose/lunge-pose-anjaneyasana" target="_blank" title="link to low lunge - Gaiam TV">
            Low Lunge
           </a>
            Turn from triangle and face the forward-facing foot. Drop your back knee down to the floor and lean over the front leg until your knee makes a 90-degree angle. Rest your hands on your thigh and breathe. Repeat on the other side.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rbJaj0Aqw5k" target="_blank" title="link to YouTube downward dog video">
            Downward dog
           </a>
            Bring both hands in front of you and place them on the ground shoulder-width apart. Bring feet hip-width apart and move your hips back and up, stretching your back gently and flattening it out.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.yogabasics.com/asana/mountain/">
            Mountain pose
           </a>
            Stand with feel shoulder-width apart and arms in prayer position at the middle of your chest. Take a deep breath and life your arms straight up, keeping your shoulders grounded in their sockets and lifting with your torso. Focus on creating space between each of your vertebrae. Bring your arms back to prayer with your exhale. Repeat for a few breath cycles.
          </li>
          <li>
           Squat  Repeat your squat, observing your body and see if its a little easier now. Can you stay longer, go lower, or simply maintain at the same level without feeling as challenged as before? Observe the difference without judgment for a few breaths. Return to standing and go about your day.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          I like to be able to focus on the postures I am doing when I start a new sequence, so often I will practice the poses in order and make a cheat sheet to use when I begin practicing the sequence. The cheat sheet could be stick figure drawings or pose names, or even DIY
          <a href="http://www.yogailluminations.com/media/Asana_flash_cards.pdf" target="_blank" title="link to yoga flash cards">
           yoga flash cards
          </a>
          lined upwhichever helps you the most. This way you dont have to worry about remembering everything or taking yourself out of the moment to look something up. You can use music or not, its up to you.
         </p>
         <p>
          The most important thing is that you find poses that flow, challenge you and help you go about your day with purpose and power. Enjoy!
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
