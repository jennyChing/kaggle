             <h1>
              Manchester United agree to loan for Monaco star Radamel Falcao
             </h1>
             <div class="body full">
              <div class="above-fold">
               <p>
                Manchester United have agreed to a loan deal for Monaco striker Radamel Falcao, subject to a medical and terms.
               </p>
               <p>
                The Colombia international, who cost Monaco 60 million euros last summer, is set to join Louis van Gaal's squad, sources told ESPN FC.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 - Brewin:
                 <a href="/club/manchester-united/360/blog/post/2015091/is-radamel-falcao-the-answer-to-manchester-united-problems" target="_blank">
                  Is Falcao right for United?
                 </a>
                </strong>
                <br/>
                <strong>
                 - Darke:
                 <a href="/club/manchester-united/360/blog/post/2013945/manchester-united-and-louis-van-gaal-face-mounting-pressure-in-premier-league" target="_blank">
                  United must improve fast
                 </a>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Falcao had also been a target of Real Madrid and had been linked with Arsenal and Manchester City.
               </p>
               <p>
                His arrival will be one of two transfers United are expected to complete on deadline day, along with finalising Daley Blind's move from Ajax.
               </p>
               <p>
                Van Gaal had already brought in Southampton left-back Luke Shaw, Athletic Bilbao midfielder Ander Herrera, Sporting Lisbon defender Marcos Rojo and Real Madrid winger Angel Di Maria.
               </p>
               <div class="article-module competition-module">
                <div class="competition-logo">
                 <img height="128" src="http://a.espncdn.com/combiner/i/?img=/photo/2014/0902/sog_g_danwts_1296x729.jpg&amp;h=128&amp;site=espnfc"/>
                </div>
                <div class="content-holder">
                 <div class="about-content">
                  <h4>
                   Summer transfer window roundup
                  </h4>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    - Premier League:
                    <a href="/blog/transfer-talk/79/post/1952708/premier-league-whos-inwhos-out" target="new">
                     Team-by-team ins and outs
                    </a>
                   </b>
                   <br/>
                   <strong>
                    - Transfer Centre:
                    <a href="http://www.espnfc.com/transfers" target="_blank">
                     All the done deals
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   <strong>
                    - Marcotti:
                    <a href="/video/latest-videos/600/video/2019256" target="_blank">
                     Mind-boggling transfers
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   <strong>
                    - Delaney:
                    <a href="/barclays-premier-league/23/blog/post/2016378/miguel-delaney-what-did-we-learn-from-transfer-deadline-day" target="_blank">
                     What did we learn on deadline day?
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   <strong>
                    - Horncastle:
                    <a href="/blog/transfer-talk/79/post/2016453/transfer-grades-for-the-elite-clubs-across-europe" target="_blank">
                     European transfer grades
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   <strong>
                    - Smith:
                    <a href="/barclays-premier-league/23/blog/post/2016490/deadline-day-in-the-premier-league-has-become-more-important-than-the-football-itself">
                     Transfers more important than the game?
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   <strong>
                    - Macintosh:
                    <a href="/barclays-premier-league/23/blog/post/2016781/deadline-day-in-the-premier-league-lead-to-many-strange-incidents">
                     We worship goals, not balance sheets
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
               <p>
                European champion Madrid had been the favourite to sign Falcao, whose departure from Monaco seemed to be signaled when he was omitted from their squad to face Lille on Saturday.
               </p>
               <p>
                The 28-year-old, who missed the World Cup because of a cruciate ligament injury, scored 70 goals in 91 games for Atletico Madrid and 72 goals in 87 matches for Porto before joining Monaco.
               </p>
               <p>
                Falcao's signing has paved the way for Javier Hernandez to move to Real Madrid, with
                <a href="/story/2015179/javier-chicharito-hernandez-joins-real-madrid-on-loan-from-manchester-united" target="_blank">
                 the Mexican's loan move to the Bernabeu confirmed
                </a>
                later on Monday.
               </p>
