       <h1>
        8 Unexpected Times You Could Get Sick
       </h1>
      </header>
     </div>
    </div>
    <span class="ehshare share-inline clearfix" data-count="true" data-description="We%2bknow%2bdoctors%2527%2boffices%2band%2bdaycare%2bare%2blikely%2bplaces%2bto%2bpick%2bup%2bcold%2band%2bflu%2bgerms.%2bLearn%2babout%2beight%2bunexpected%2bways%2byou%2bcan%2bget%2bsick.%2b" data-migratedbyline="" data-networks='["facebook", "twitter", "pinterest", "email"]' data-title="8%2bUnexpected%2bTimes%2bYou%2bCould%2bGet%2bSick" data-track-self="false" data-url="http%3a%2f%2fwww.everydayhealth.com%2fcold-flu-pictures%2funexpected-times-you-could-get-sick.aspx">

         <p>
          What started with a few sniffles and a sneeze from
someone who crossed your path could unleash mayhem,
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/cold-and-flu/signs-youre-too-sick-for-work.aspx">
           leading
to days or weeks of missed work
          </a>
          , doctor visits, and canceled social
events. It goes without saying that no one likes to get sick, and there are
some germ-ridden situations you simply can't avoid, such as having a child in
daycare or sitting on a plane next to a traveler who's hacking the whole time.
But you can avoid certain situations that make you more vulnerable to catching
the common cold, influenza, and other illnesses.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="2" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-02-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Using+an+ATM" id="slide-img-2" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-02-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-02-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Using an ATM
         </h2>
         <p>
          "ATMs are
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/cold-and-flu-pictures/10-hot-spots-for-germs.aspx#/slide-8">
           hotbeds
for germs and viruses
          </a>
          ," says Jock Lawrason, MD, pulmonary medicine
physician at Nantucket Cottage Hospital in Massachusetts. Watch a popular ATM
for 15 minutes, and you'll see the number of people who touch the buttons. If
any of those people were recently ill, they can pass on their germs for cold
and flu for weeks afterward. "The influenza virus, for example, can stay on an
inanimate object for one to two days, and strep pneumonia can last anywhere
from two to three and a half weeks," Dr. Lawrason says. "The longer objects are
touched without being washed, the higher the risk of someone getting sick," he
said.  But you can take some precautions.
"You should be conscious to wash your hands after using them," said Dr.
Lawrason, and "keeping hand sanitizer around would be the best way to ensure
the germs are killed immediately after exposure."
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="3" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-03-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Touching+Your+Own+Stuff+" id="slide-img-3" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-03-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>

          <span class="slide-pinterest">

          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Touching Your Own Stuff
         </h2>
         <p>
          You may
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/cold-and-flu-pictures/10-hot-spots-for-germs.aspx#/slide-2">
           be
cautious about touching a grocery cart at the supermarket to avoid a case of
the sniffles
          </a>
          , but you may be more likely to get ill from your own
things. "People are susceptible to germs that can make them ill everywhere and
from everything," says Alexander Tomich, RN, MSN, manager of infection control
at Loyola University Health System, in Maywood, Ill. "Your cell phone, car
keys, purse or briefcase, gym bag, kids' backpacks, money, and your pets are
all germ-infested," he says. Not to mention the germs on your own skin  the
more than 200 different cold viruses can live on you for a few hours.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="4" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-04-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Being+in+Crowds" id="slide-img-4" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-04-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-04-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Being in Crowds
         </h2>
         <p>
          Want to avoid influenza or that stomach bug going
around? Avoid crowded places. "Large gatherings of people will always enhance a
person's chances of getting sick," Lawrason says. "The exposure to influenza
and other viruses is greater, and shaking a lot of hands or being near someone
who is sneezing or coughing will raise your odds of becoming ill." Viruses like
cold and flu are particularly likely to run through offices and daycares
because many people are gathered there, interacting with one another, but
they're not the only germy congregating spots. Grocery stores, hotels, and
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/fitness-pictures/how-to-fend-off-germs-at-the-gym.aspx">
           the
gym are also particularly germ-infested
          </a>
          , Lawrason says.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="5" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-05-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Staying+Indoors+in+the+Winter" id="slide-img-5" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-05-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-05-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Staying Indoors in the Winter
         </h2>
         <p>
          On the flip side, you can't hole up at home all the
time either to dodge the sniffles or influenza. Contrary to popular belief,
it's not being outside in cold weather without a coat that makes you more
likely to catch cold and flu  it's being bundled up inside. "Illness tends to be seasonal, and
people are more likely to get sick when the seasons change," Lawrason says.
"For example,
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/cold-and-flu/1206/why-is-flu-more-common-in-winter-2498.aspx">
           influenza
tends to strike during the winter months
          </a>
          ." The reason people are more
vulnerable to viruses such as cold and flu in the winter? "People are generally
cooped up together indoors in an enclosed space trying to keep warm, which
increases their chances of spreading germs and getting ill," he explains.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="6" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-06-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Swimming+or+Using+a+Hot+Tub" id="slide-img-6" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-06-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-06-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Swimming or Using a Hot Tub
         </h2>
         <p>
          If you think the chlorine and chemicals
in pools and hot tubs will kill everything, think again.
          <a href="/healthy-living/0516/many-public-pools-contaminated-with-human-waste-cdc.aspx">
           Fecal
matter can make its way into pools
          </a>
          and hot tubs, making bathers ill.
"In general, bacteria and germs can easily be transmitted around water, so
avoid hot tubs and pools if you don't want to get sick," Lawrason says.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="7" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-07-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Right+After+Being+Sick" id="slide-img-7" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-07-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-07-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Right After Being Sick
         </h2>
         <p>
          Talk about a double whammy! You're more likely to get
an illness after just being sick. "We have cilia lining in our respiratory
systems, which are tissues that can control what goes in and out of our
bodies," Lawrason says. When you're ill, the cilia can be temporarily damaged
or weakened, which allows new bacteria or viruses like cold and flu to enter.
"Furthermore,
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/colds/boost-immunity.aspx">
           illness
in general can temporarily lower immunity
          </a>
          , creating a better chance
of getting sick after the body has already been sick," he says.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="8" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-08-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Neglecting+Your+Health+When+You%27re+Well" id="slide-img-8" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-08-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-08-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Neglecting Your Health When You're Well
         </h2>
         <p>
          Don't take your immunity to cold and
flu for granted. "
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/cold-and-flu/foods-that-fight-colds.aspx">
           Eating
healthfully to maintain a good immune system
          </a>
          , drinking lots of water,
not drinking too much alcohol, and sleeping appropriately are all common sense
ways to avoid getting sick," Tomich says. "It's also very important to stay up
on your immunizations and get an influenza shot during flu season. Vaccinations
save lives."
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="9" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-09-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Not+Being+Mindful+of+the+Germs" id="slide-img-9" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-09-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Unexpected-Times-You-Could-Get-Sick-09-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Not Being Mindful of the Germs
         </h2>
         <p>
          It may sound like common sense, but in
the hustle and bustle of busy life, it's easy to forget the most effective ways
to avoid getting ill. "Washing your hands is extremely important because the
hands are the most common vehicle for transmitting illnesses," Lawrason says.
"Hand wipes and bacteria hand sanitizers can also keep germs and bacteria that
can make you sick off your hands." But how you use it is important: If your hand
sanitizer dries on your hands in less than 20 seconds, you're not using enough of
it to keep from getting sick. "And finally,
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/cold-flu-pictures/people-giving-you-a-cold.aspx">
           if
you yourself are sick, try to avoid public places
          </a>
          to prevent others
from becoming ill," he says.
         </p>
        </li>
       </ul>
