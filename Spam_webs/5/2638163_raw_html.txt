        <h2>
         <a class="book-episode-title" href="http://newbooksinsciencefiction.com/2013/07/17/hugh-c-howey-wool-simon-schuster-2012/" title="Permalink for Wool">
          <span class="book-author">
           Hugh C. Howey
          </span>
          <span class="book-title">
           Wool
          </span>
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="book-meta">
         Jet City Comics, 2012
        </div>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard fn">
          Michael Zummo

         on
         <abbr class="published" title="2013-07-17">
          July 17, 2013
         </abbr>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <div class="author-photo wp-caption">

         <p>
          Hugh C. Howey
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="amz-book wp-caption">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1476733953/?tag=newbooinhis-20" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/dp/1476733953/?tag=newbooinhis-20');" title="View this book on Amazon">

         <p>

        <p>
         <a href="http://www.hughhowey.com/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.hughhowey.com/');" target="_blank">
          Hugh C. Howey
         </a>
         , author of the award-winning
         <em>
          Molly Fyde Saga
         </em>
         , is best known for his self-published and bestselling series
         <em>
          Wool
         </em>
         . This post apocalyptic tale of human survival within the infamous silos has taken the world by storm.
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1476733953/?tag=newbooinhis-20" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/dp/1476733953/?tag=newbooinhis-20');" target="_blank">
          <em>
           The Wool OmnibusEdition
          </em>
         </a>
         (Simon and Schuster, 2012) won the Kindle Book Review's 2012 Indie Book of the Year award, in addition to making the bestseller lists in both
         <em>
          The New York Times
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          USA Today
         </em>
         . In the two years since releasing a series he originally believed "no one would care about," it's been picked up by Simon and Schuster for Canadian and US distribution, and film rights sold to 20th Century Fox. If you have yet to experience WOOL, it's a recommended must read!
        </p>
        <p>
         In this interview with Michael Zummo, Hugh shares his approach to writing, his endeavors in self-publishing, the origins of the
         <em>
          Wool
         </em>
         series, along with what's coming up.
        </p>
