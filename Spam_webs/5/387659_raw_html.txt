        <h1>
         <a href="http://www.neatorama.com/2014/11/12/15-Fun-Facts-You-Might-Not-Know-about-The-Twilight-Zone/">
          15 Fun Facts You Might Not Know about
          <i>
           The Twilight Zone
          </i>
         </a>
        </h1>
       </header>
       <footer class="byline">
        <a class="userlink author-name" href="http://www.neatorama.com/whois/john/" itemprop="author" rel="author" title="Profile for John Farrier - Member Since Aug 4th, 2012">

       <section class="postbody">
        <div class="postbody-text">
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="449" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415581150-0.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           There is a fifth dimension beyond that which is known to man. It is a dimension as vast as space and as timeless as infinity. It is the middle ground between light and shadow, between science and superstition, and it lies between the pit of man's fears and the summit of his knowledge. This is the dimension of imagination. It is an area which we call the Twilight Zone.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Rod Serling first read these words on television in 1959. In 6 years, he changed the American television landscape and captured generations of fans. Here are a few fun facts you might not know about
          <em>
           The Twilight Zone
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" height="304" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415570237-0.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="600"/>
          <br/>
          (Image of Welles from
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0053735/">
            Crack in the Mirror
           </a>
          </em>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="docs-internal-guid-63a3a285-968e-e023-d747-314469c2ca2c">
           <span>
            <strong>
             1. Serling narrated for the show, first through just his voice and then on camera during the second season. Serling's first choice for that role was not himself, but Orson Welles.
            </strong>
            It never came to pass as the producers could not meet Welless salary demands.
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           <span>
            <img alt="" height="192" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415571854-0.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="600"/>
            <br/>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2.
          </strong>
          <em>
           Star Trek
          </em>
          fans may remember William Shatner's famous role in the
          <em>
           Twilight Zone
          </em>
          episode "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440824">
           Nightmare at 20,000 Feet
          </a>
          ." In that episode, Shatner played an airline passenger who alone
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=827OGkGn_Kk">
           could see a monster on the wing
          </a>
          , tearing the plane apart. But he also appeared in another episode. In "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440803">
           Nick of Time
          </a>
          ," Statner played a man who discovered a frighteningly accurate fortune telling machine.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="300" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415571854-1.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3.
          </strong>
          Leonard Nimoy, who played Spock on
          <em>
           Star Trek
          </em>
          , appeared in the episode "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440762">
           A Quality of Mercy
          </a>
          ." He played a weary American infantryman in the Philippines in 1945.
         </p>
         <a name="more">
         </a>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="235" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415573062-0.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           4.
          </strong>
          George Takei, who played Sulu on
          <em>
           Star Trek
          </em>
          , appeared in the episode "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440852">
           The Encounter
          </a>
          ." He played a Japanese-American whose father was a spy for the Japanese. He meets an American veteran who killed an unarmed Japanese soldier.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5.
          </strong>
          For conceiving of
          <em>
           The Twilight Zone
          </em>
          , Rod Serling is known as a great writer. But those who worked with him on the show also thought of him as a great executive producer. He had a knack for knowing when to take control and when to delegate responsibility.
          <strong>
           Serling ran a remarkably well-organized show.
          </strong>
          Director Buzz Kulik wrote:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <span id="docs-internal-guid-63a3a285-96b4-6f28-e247-5cd5897ecc99">
            <span>
             Rod would never come onto the set because he felt that it would be intrusive. Every once in a while I would hear from him, if he had seen some dailies or something. It was an amazing organization. Working the way those people worked was just a sheer joy . . . And they would get nine scripts and put them together, and get them in as good shape as they could, and then youd shoot them. Most shows you would be getting pages the day you start shooting, or they would still be writing while you were shooting. Never on The Twilight Zone. That work was done and it was ready to go. It was such a well-oiled, organized operation. (Presnell and McGee 15)
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <span>
           <span>
            <img alt="" height="613" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415573062-1.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="400"/>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           <strong>
            6.
           </strong>
           In 1997, the DC
           <em>
            Looney Tunes
           </em>
           comic book series published a parody of
           <em>
            The Twilight Zone
           </em>
           . The
           <a href="http://www.comicvine.com/looney-tunes-30-the-looney-zone/4000-117744/">
            Comic Vine wiki
           </a>
           describes
           <em>
            The Looney Zone
           </em>
           as funny takes on classic episodes:
          </span>
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           The Looney Tunes take on that classic TV anthology of weird stories and unexpected plot twists known as The Twilight Zone, with Daffy Duck being mistaken for an alien in "Will the Real Martian Please Stand," Penelope the Cat seeing Pepe Le Pew everywhere she drives in "Le Hitchhiker," Daffy's ventriloquism act coming to life in "The Dummy," Sylvester seeing a gremlin Tweety tear apart the plane he's on in "Nightmare of 20,000 Tweets," and Bugs accepting a challenge from Daffy in "Wanna See Something Really Scary?"
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="444" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415573490-0.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           7. In "
           <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440839">
            A World of His Own
           </a>
           ," a character interrupts Rod Serling's closing narration.
          </strong>
          It's quite funny. Serling routinely watched over the damnation of his characters, but this character reverses that role.
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2pfL8mdNtw8#t=07m20s">
           You can watch the scene here.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" height="450" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415573956-0.jpg" style="vertical-align: top; float: left; margin:0 1em 1em 0;" width="600"/>
          <br/>
          (Image: CBS)
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           8.
          </strong>
          Bob Crane, who played Colonel Robert Hogan on
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0058812/">
            Hogan's Heroes
           </a>
          </em>
          , was a radio announcer in the episode "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440736">
           Static
          </a>
          ." You can listen to his voice
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Heipqh_DcuE#t=00m41s">
           here
          </a>
          . Since Crane got his start as a disc jockey, the role fit.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="179" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415578305-0.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           9.
          </strong>
          Ivan Dixon, who played Sergeant Kinchloe on
          <em>
           Hogan's Heroes
          </em>
          , starred in two episodes of
          <em>
           The Twilight Zone
          </em>
          . In "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440868">
           I Am the Night--Color Me Black
          </a>
          ," Dixon played an insightful minister in a town about to execute a criminal. In "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440872">
           The Big Tall Wish
          </a>
          ," he played a boxer who experienced an inexplicable victory in the ring.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="364" mozallowfullscreen="" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/NIufLRpJYnI?version=3&amp;controls=1&amp;fs=1&amp;border=0&amp;autoplay=0&amp;cc_load_policy=0&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;loop=0&amp;egm=0&amp;rel=0&amp;showsearch=0&amp;wmode=transparent&amp;enablejsapi=0&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;showinfo=1&amp;themelight&amp;playerapiid=1222222462&amp;wmode=transparent" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="600">
          </iframe>
          <br/>
          (
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NIufLRpJYnI">
           Video Link
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           10.
          </strong>
          The episode "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440883">
           To Serve Man
          </a>
          " shows aliens visiting Earth and offering to take them to their home planet. They say that they have nothing but benevolent purposes and possess a book titled
          <em>
           To Serve Man
          </em>
          . After linguists translate the text, they discover that it's a cook book.
         </p>
         <p>
          That episode starred Lloyd Bochner as one of the translators.
          <strong>
           Bochner reprised his role and the episode's most famous line in the movie
           <em>
            <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0102510/">
             The Naked Gun 2 1/2
            </a>
           </em>
           .
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="425" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415579005-0.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           11.
          </strong>
          Jack Klugman was most famous for playing in
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0065329/">
            The Odd Couple
           </a>
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0074042/">
            Quincy, M.E
           </a>
          </em>
          . He starred in 4 different episodes of
          <em>
           The Twilight Zone
          </em>
          . In "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440829">
           In Praise of Pip
          </a>
          ," he played opposite Bill Mumy as a father who gets a last chance to see his son. "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440805">
           Death Ship
          </a>
          " shows Klugman as a spaceship captain who searches for a habitable planet. In "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440810">
           A Game of Pool
          </a>
          ," he plays a dangerous game of pool against a ghostly Jonathan Winters. "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440849">
           A Passage for Trumpet
          </a>
          " imagines Klugman as a washed up, alcoholic trumpet player in need of a second chance.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="364" mozallowfullscreen="" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/tEUHl8RGrpk?version=3&amp;controls=1&amp;fs=1&amp;border=0&amp;autoplay=0&amp;cc_load_policy=0&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;loop=0&amp;egm=0&amp;rel=0&amp;showsearch=0&amp;wmode=transparent&amp;enablejsapi=0&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;showinfo=1&amp;themelight&amp;playerapiid=202213548&amp;wmode=transparent" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="600">
          </iframe>
          <br/>
          (
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tEUHl8RGrpk">
           Video Link
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           12.
          </strong>
          <span id="docs-internal-guid-63a3a285-9703-0ab4-e855-cb8efdc39541">
           <span>
            Sterling wrote TV scripts throughout the late 40s and early 50s. His breakthrough script was for 
           </span>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0622424/">
            <span>
             Patterns
            </span>
           </a>
           <span>
            , a 1955 episode of
            <em>
             <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0039123/">
              Kraft Television Theater
             </a>
            </em>
            . This episode featured a very young Elizabeth Montgomery (later famous for
           </span>
           <em>
            <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0057733/">
             Bewitched
            </a>
           </em>
           <span>
            ) in a tale of conflict and intrigue within the leadership of a corporation. It was enormously popular and earned Serling an Emmy.
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           <span>
            <img alt="" height="454" src="http://uploads.neatorama.com/images/posts/368/77/77368/1415580428-0.jpg" style="vertical-align: top;" width="600"/>
            <br/>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           13.
          </strong>
          Burgess Meredith, the actor who played
          <em>
           The Penguin
          </em>
          in the
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0059968/">
           original
           <em>
            Batman
           </em>
           series
          </a>
          , also played 4 roles in
          <em>
           The Twilight Zone
          </em>
          . In "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440791">
           Printer's Devil
          </a>
          ," he was a reporter who had a dark power for getting great scoops. "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440858">
           The Obsolete Man
          </a>
          " shows Meredith as a heroic librarian in a tyrannical dystopia. "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440742">
           Mr. Dingle, The Strong
          </a>
          " shows aliens experimenting on a hapless vacuum cleaner salesman. In "
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/440802">
           Time Enough At Last
          </a>
          ," Meredith is a bookish bank clerk who yearns for time to read.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="364" mozallowfullscreen="" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/ctHltBauGc8?version=3&amp;controls=1&amp;fs=1&amp;border=0&amp;autoplay=0&amp;cc_load_policy=0&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;loop=0&amp;egm=0&amp;rel=0&amp;showsearch=0&amp;wmode=transparent&amp;enablejsapi=0&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;showinfo=1&amp;themelight&amp;playerapiid=1701750618&amp;wmode=transparent" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="600">
          </iframe>
          <br/>
          (
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ctHltBauGc8">
           Video Link
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           14.
          </strong>
          Several episodes were remade for the 1983 movie
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0086491/">
            Twilight Zone: The Movie
           </a>
          </em>
          . The clip embedded above shows John Lithgow in William Shatner's old role from "Nightmare at 20,000 Feet."
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="364" mozallowfullscreen="" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/kLjK8uBK8-s?version=3&amp;controls=1&amp;fs=1&amp;border=0&amp;autoplay=0&amp;cc_load_policy=0&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;loop=0&amp;egm=0&amp;rel=0&amp;showsearch=0&amp;wmode=transparent&amp;enablejsapi=0&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;showinfo=1&amp;themelight&amp;playerapiid=466644798&amp;wmode=transparent" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="600">
          </iframe>
          <br/>
          (
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kLjK8uBK8-s">
           Video Link
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           15.
          </strong>
          The series was
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0088634/">
           revived from 1985-1989
          </a>
          for a total of 110 episodes. Embedded above is one of my favorites: "Button, Button."
         </p>

            <div class="comm-body">
             And if you're going to talk about an actor who played multiple roles on Twilight Zone then went on to other things, you should've had an item just for Bill(y) Mumy, who talked to his dead grandmother on a toy phone in "Long Distance Call", was a younger version of Jack Klugman's son (as mentioned above) in "In Praise of Pip" and most iconically, was the selfish little boy with psychic powers in "It's a Good Life". He was one of the hardest working child actors at the time. Among other roles, he was in three "Alfred Hitchcock" episodes (one of them in the unsuccessful hour-long format), and was the little-remembered "third actor to play Darrin" in a Bewitched episode where Endora turned Samantha's husband into a child (best TV trivia factoid EVER). All that before he became Will ("Danger!") Robinson on "Lost in Space", and almost 30 years later returned to TV Sci-Fi as an Alien Ambassador's Assistant in "Babylon 5". (And among other things inbetween, became beloved on Dr. Demento's radio show as half of the goofball duet 'Barnes &amp; Barnes') Is there a "Twilight Zone Alumni" story better? I don't think so.
