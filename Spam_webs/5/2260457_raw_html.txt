                 <h2 class="date-header">
                  <span>
                   Thursday, July 3, 2014
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="https://d.gr-assets.com/books/1348308477l/16039808.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Book Review- Pocket Full of Posies by Angela Roquet
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7063322407736128241" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="https://d.gr-assets.com/books/1348308477l/16039808.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="https://d.gr-assets.com/books/1348308477l/16039808.jpg" width="206"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <b>
                      Goodreads Summary:
                     </b>
                     Not Your Grandma's AfterlifeThe promise of peace in Limbo City is threatened once again, but this time the terrorists have a more specific target in mind: Lana Harvey. The up and coming reaper thought passing her classes at the Reaper Academy was going to be her biggest challenge, but when a rebel demon sends her apartment up in flames, she realizes that her victories from the previous year havent gone so unnoticed after all
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     To make matters worse, the Afterlife Council has taken notice of Lana too. The Egyptian god Horus is blackmailing her into joining the Posy Unit so she can do an illegal side job for him, and Cindy Morningstar, Lucifers daughter, insists that she take a two week training course with the devilishly tempting Beelzebub, much to her angelic boyfriends chagrin.
                     <div>
                      <b>
                       Goodreads Rating:
                      </b>
                      4.35 with 75 ratings
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <b>
                       Genre Listing:
                      </b>
                      Paranormal, Demons, Fantasy, Angels, Grim Reapers, Mythology,
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <b>
                       Get the Book:
                       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B009RT7XF0?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B009RT7XF0&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=lintheboo-20">
                        Amazon
                       </a>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <b>
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      I sort of apologize in advanced for having a post a day. Actually... No I don't. I enjoy reading, and I enjoy making these posts, so you're stuck with them. :p Seriously though, I have 400 books on my to-be-read list and nothing but time this summer. Also, I'm a big nerd and turned my to-be-read list into a fancy spreadsheet. It's about 10 times more helpful then the half-assed list I had going on. I like spreadsheets. Sorry, rambling, on to the book review!
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      I like this series a lot. As soon as I finished with Graveyard Shift I instantly bought Pocket Full of Posies, and before I started this review I bought For the Birds. I may or may not have a book addiction.... Anyways. I will say that I liked Graveyard Shift better, but this one was still good. It didn't have as much humor in it as the first book, but I assume that's because there was so much chaos that it had to be a little bit darker. Lana was still sarcastic, which I absolutely love.
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      This book was all about Lana bettering herself through school and really kind of taking control of her life. She gets a lot of things thrown her way that FORCES her to have some ambition, if nothing else for her safety. I'm really excited to see where these advancements, and big changes in her life take her.
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      So this is a spoiler, but I have to talk about it. The angelic boyfriend gets SUPER controlling and intervening. It's obvious that he does it to protect her, but it gets to the point where she almost can't even make a decision for her self. I've been complaining a lot about controlling boyfriends in books. So let me tell you how happy I am to read that she kicked him to the damn curb because of it. I swear if I read one more book where the main character falls more IN LUV with their boyfriend the more he controls them, I'm going to scream. I think that's kind of why I like Lana so much. She does whatever the hell she pleases, and not afraid to stand up for her self. Go Lana!
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      So here are some great quotes (I'm getting obsessed with highlights) from the book.
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      This book has the quotes at the beginning of the chapter just like the first. This is one I really liked. It's from George Eliot, but I don't remember what chapter. The mark says page 54. "It's never too late to be what you might have been."
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      "I was beginning to wonder if there was a vaccine for annoyance." This was early on in the book when Lana is getting annoyed with her friends. If this vaccine exists, sign me up.
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      "But that wasn't what grated my cheese." This made me chuckle, because I say that something grates my cheese when I get annoyed.
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      "It was time to stop worrying so much and start enjoying some of the crazy life was throwing my way." This is at the end of the book, and I just like it because it reminds me that I need to do this from time to time.
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      So over all, I really enjoyed the second installment. I'm not prepared to give it five moons, but it's a definite four-mooner.
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
