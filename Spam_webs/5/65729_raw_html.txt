       <p class="author">
        Posted by the Mass Office of Travel and Tourism
       </p>
       <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
       <p>
        Even among Massachusetts many immersive museums, the
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/business/2745/" target="_blank">
         Museum of Russian Icons
        </a>
        in Clinton stands out.
       </p>
       <p>
        This central MA institution houses the largest collection of Russian icons in North America, an assembly that is unmatched outside of the artifacts native Russia.
       </p>
       <p>
        Recognized by The Boston Globe
        <a href="http://museumofrussianicons.org/en/about/the-museum/news-articles/" target="_blank">
         multiple times
        </a>
        for its excellent exhibits, the Museum of Russian Icons is a superb stop for art aficionados, history fans and students of culture.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres a peek inside.
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Museum-of-Russian-Icons-staircase.jpg">
          <img alt="Museum of Russian Icons staircase" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7149" height="628" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Museum-of-Russian-Icons-staircase.jpg" width="628"/>
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        While the museums icons date back hundreds of years, the Museum of Russian icons itself is a far more recent endeavor. It was founded in 2006 by Massachusetts art collector Gordon B. Lankton, and contains more than 1,000 unique pieces. Once upon a time, the building was actually an old mill, but its much more welcoming and spacious nowadays.
       </p>
       <p>
        The artifacts inside span a variety of media and material; the physical
        <a href="http://museumofrussianicons.org/en/about/iconography/history-of-icons/" target="_blank">
         icons
        </a>
        , a distinct, symbolic part of Russian society since the large-scale conversion to Orthodox Christianity around 1,000 years ago, make up the most significant part of the collection, but there are a number of paintings, prints and other works as well.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Icons-at-the-museum.jpg">
         <img alt="Icons at the museum" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7150" height="720" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Icons-at-the-museum.jpg" width="960"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         Icons at the museum via
        </em>
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/themuseumofrussianicons/">
         <em>
          Facebook
         </em>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Along with permanent attractions, the Museum of Russian Icons also hosts a series of
        <a href="http://museumofrussianicons.org/en/special-exhibits/on-view/" target="_blank">
         special exhibits
        </a>
        , which rotate every few months.
        <em>
         Siberia Imagined and Reimagined
        </em>
        , one such display that was shown at the museum from September 2014 through early January 2015, brought the work of several Siberian photographers to the United States for the very first time.
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Secret Symbolism: Decoding Color in Russian Icons
        </em>
        , which the museum showcased during early 2014, helped visitors decipher the meaning behind colors and hues in sacred paintings. This year, there are already a couple of
        <a href="http://museumofrussianicons.org/en/special-exhibits/upcoming/" target="_blank">
         special expositions
        </a>
        on the way, with the first one due to arrive shortly.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Ethiopian-exhibit-Museum-of-Russian-Icons.jpg">
         <img alt="Ethiopian exhibit Museum of Russian Icons" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7151" height="720" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Ethiopian-exhibit-Museum-of-Russian-Icons.jpg" width="960"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         Ethiopian icons at the Museum of Russian Icons via
        </em>
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/themuseumofrussianicons/photos_stream">
         <em>
          Facebook
         </em>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        After browsing icons, the museums
        <a href="http://museumofrussianicons.org/en/visit/plan/russian-tea-room/" target="_blank">
         Russian Tea Room
        </a>
        is the place to go. This self-described most beautiful tea room in the region lives up to its billing, offering charming scenery, as well as a number of tea and coffee choices, of course. To munch on, there are traditional Russian snacks, plus Moscow chocolates for any visitors who have a sweet tooth.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Museum-of-Russian-Icons-Tea-Room.jpg">
         <img alt="Museum of Russian Icons Tea Room" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7152" height="401" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Museum-of-Russian-Icons-Tea-Room.jpg" width="600"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         Traditional Russian porcelain teapots via
         <a href="https://www.facebook.com/themuseumofrussianicons" target="_blank">
          Facebook
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        When it comes to planning a visit, the museums website has
        <a href="http://museumofrussianicons.org/en/visit/plan/hours-and-admission/" target="_blank">
         all the details
        </a>
        covered. The hours are from 11:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. Tuesday through Friday and from 9:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. on Saturday; both Sunday and Monday are off-days. Overnights in the area are made easy as well, as the museum website has
        <a href="http://museumofrussianicons.org/en/visit/plan/restaurants-and-hotels/" target="_blank">
         a list of suggestions
        </a>
        for nearby lodgings.
       </p>
       <p>
        Keen to experience more history in Massachusetts? You can learn about many more museums, heritage trails and significant sites
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/explore/history/">
         here
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/imagine/idea-board/14/">
         here
        </a>
        .
       </p>
