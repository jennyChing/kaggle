                Lunch breaks that involve scouting the perfect locations for a quick outfit shot. Yes, that doesn't even sound remotely close to a working class lunch break. Unfortunately, it is happening in my lunch circle. Nouveau working class maybe?
               </div>

                <br/>

               <div style="text-align: justify;">
                My colleague/friend is wearing a pair of pin-striped palazzo pants that I convinced her to get because I believe every girl needs a pair in their wardrobe. Essentially, it's a classic piece that you can dress up and down, with heels or non. This ZARA version is so versatile (maybe lose the belt), even I got myself a pair. For myself, I am in my black minimalist self as usual; a simple tank top with a netted pencil skirt. Can't go wrong with classic staples like a simple tank, can you?

               THE ARTISAN FLORIST
              </a>
             </h3>
             <h2 class="date-header">
              <span>
               Tuesday, May 12, 2015
              </span>
             </h2>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">

               <br/>
               <b>
                Happy Bunch Flowers
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <div style="text-align: justify;">
                I find plant symbolism interesting whereby its origins are extremely subjective and we, humans, have a tendency to interpret its meanings. According to the general understanding, blue roses symbolizes mystery and attaining the impossible. Coincidently, this is my view of blue roses. They possess a sense of mystique that differs vastly compared to its commercial counterparts; red roses.
               </div>
               <div style="text-align: justify;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="text-align: justify;">
                Here, this bouquet of blue roses is from the local florist subscription called Happy Bunch. Brilliant idea and brilliant execution. I really wouldn't mind subscribing to its monthly service because who wouldn't mind waking up to fresh flowers right? It's very frustrating how flowers wilt so easily from their perfect form and as such, an expensive form of decoration to maintain.

               <br/>
               <b>
                Villa Samadhi, Kuala Lumpur
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <div style="text-align: justify;">
                It is sometimes refreshing to know that there is a hidden sanctuary hidden within the hustle and bustle of the city. I planned a staycation at this private estate one labour day morning. Just me, the beau, the pool, the books and the verendah. It was just pure relaxation without much 21st century technology. Of course, we were well taken care of as well. Complimentary baskets of local snacks, in-house made butter biscuits, fresh tropical fruits, dehydrated fruits and evening drinks. It's moment like this where I find myself again; in the form of reading. Sometimes I feel our surroundings caused us to be so self absorbed with social media and the reality of life, it's good to have a break from it.
               </div>
               <div style="text-align: justify;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="text-align: justify;">
                Irony isn't it. Here I am talking about how social media is the reason for our gradual self absorbing ways, but at the same time I am writing this down in a social media platform.

               BOWERHAUS
              </a>
             </h3>

              <span>
               Thursday, April 30, 2015

               <b>
                Bowerhaus Silver Hematite
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <div style="text-align: justify;">
                Homegrown and celebrated in Asia Pacific. Bowerhaus specializes in handmade jewelry that are made of semi precious beads and pearls. The whole catalogue might easily seemed as a curated collection of beautiful trinkets and well-cared vintage jewelry that are sourced from weekend markets in Europe. It definitely exudes an eccentric old world charm and understated confidence that is befitting of how I feel as of late.
