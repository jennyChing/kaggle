        <h2>
         <a href="http://writerswrite.co.za/the-locked-room-a-simple-way-to-test-your-plot">
          The Locked Room  A simple way to test your plot
         </a>
        </h2>
       </div>
      </header>
      <div class="post-body" id="post_body_677357">
       <p>
        A trapped character comes alive on the page or screen because he has to fight his way out a corner. The character has to push back against the predicament placed there by the plotgiving us conflict, intensity, and barriers we can define. The locked room is a way to interrogate your plot.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <div class="posthaven-gallery" id="posthaven_gallery[657664]">
        <div class="posthaven-gallery-car">
         <p class="posthaven-file posthaven-file-image posthaven-file-state-processed">
          <img class="posthaven-gallery-image" data-large-height="333" data-large-src="https://phaven-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/files/image_part/asset/1136309/xrPeEQ58RNHDszWu7l45L1G6_gA/large_The_Locked_Door_Plotting.jpg" data-large-width="491" data-medium-height="333" data-medium-src="https://phaven-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/files/image_part/asset/1136309/xrPeEQ58RNHDszWu7l45L1G6_gA/medium_The_Locked_Door_Plotting.jpg" data-medium-width="491" data-orig-height="333" data-orig-src="https://phaven-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/files/image_part/asset/1136309/xrPeEQ58RNHDszWu7l45L1G6_gA/The_Locked_Door_Plotting.jpg" data-orig-width="491" data-posthaven-id="1136309" data-posthaven-state="processed" data-thumb-height="200" data-thumb-src="https://phaven-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/files/image_part/asset/1136309/xrPeEQ58RNHDszWu7l45L1G6_gA/thumb_The_Locked_Door_Plotting.jpg" data-thumb-width="200" data-xlarge-height="333" data-xlarge-src="https://phaven-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/files/image_part/asset/1136309/xrPeEQ58RNHDszWu7l45L1G6_gA/xlarge_The_Locked_Door_Plotting.jpg" data-xlarge-width="491" src="https://phaven-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/files/image_part/asset/1136309/xrPeEQ58RNHDszWu7l45L1G6_gA/medium_The_Locked_Door_Plotting.jpg"/>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="posthaven-gallery-cdr">
        </div>
       </div>
       <a href="http://hushsupperclub.wordpress.com/category/underground-restaurant/" target="_blank">
        Source
       </a>
       for Image
       <div>
        <p>
         The character can be someone accused of murder who is trapped in a witness box by a dogged prosecutor. It can be a woman trapped in a loveless marriage and career, with no window or door to help her escape. It can be someone stuck in a deep depression or illnessthe locked room is an inner demon or addiction.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         It doesnt matter. The idea is to put your character in a room. Lock the door, bar the windows, take away food and comfort and see what happens. As a writer, you have to get him out the locked room.
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Who locked him in the room?
         </li>
         <li>
          Why did they lock him in the room?
         </li>
         <li>
          Why does he need to get out the room? What will happen if he doesnt get out the room?
         </li>
         <li>
          Is there anyone else or anything else in the room with him?
         </li>
         <li>
          How is he going to get out of the locked room? With force? Words?
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         This is what the locked room could look like on the page:
         <br/>
         A nave young heiress is persuaded into an engagement with a wealthier older man. This is the 1920sso the locked room is the morals, pressures and expectations of the period. She needs to get out of the relationship because she fears she will end up a miserable uptight snob like her mother.
         <br/>
         The only person who can understand, and help her, is a free-spirited and rebellious female journalistwho encourages her to break from her rich family and find her own identity.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         When were writing or planning our stories, we sometimes wander off course. We have interesting characters doing things in great settings with some lovely descriptionbut theres no conflict or consequence. The locked room test is a great way to get back inside the plot.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As the writer Luke Short once said: First I write myself onto a corner. Then I write myself out.
         <br/>
        </p>
