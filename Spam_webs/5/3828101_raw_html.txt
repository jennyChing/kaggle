         <h2 class="entry-header font-entryheader">
          <span class="entry-title">
           <a href="http://www.neebeep.com/2014/05/unappreciated.html">
            Unappreciated
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <div class="entry-body font-entrybody">
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            <a href="http://www.neebeep.com/itsownsweetwillneebeepc/2014/03/holy-crap-look-what-im-doing-in-april.html" target="_blank">
             {Dubious}LifeStyleBlogger
            </a>
            ingredients:
            <em>
             <a href="http://www.neebeep.com/itsownsweetwillneebeepc/family-life/" target="_blank">
              family, teens, kids
             </a>
             , #RantingBecauseICan, #ComplainingMakesYouFeelBetter
            </em>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Unappreciated
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            I, like many parents and people in general, feel unappreciated some of the time.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            A lot of the time.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Ok  most of the time.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            I just dont get it.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            I gave up my (more or less) stunning figure to give birth to these children. (Really I was a slide on easy size three at one time. Probably zero in designer clothes  had I ever bought any designer clothes).
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            And what size am I now? Suffice to say not a three. Or a five.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            And yes I know there are simply reams of women out there with five or seven or fifteen children who look absolutely glorious in a string bikini. But at this point I aint one of em.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            {Note how I had to add at this point?
            <a href="http://www.neebeep.com/itsownsweetwillneebeepc/2013/07/how-to-be-a-dorkoptimist.html" target="_blank">
             Such is the curse/blessing of the Eternal Optimist aka Dorkoptimist.}
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            But I am working on it, as evidenced by this fascinating series:
            <a href="http://www.neebeep.com/itsownsweetwillneebeepc/lisawalksaroundplanetearth/" target="_blank">
             #LisaWalksAroundPlanetEarth
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Though I have been neglectful of my exercise regimen of late due to my infamous deformed and painful vertebrae, a nasty chest cold, and a serious bout of allergic reaction over the last few days.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            {And yeal those are excuses and Im stickin to em.}
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            But back to Unappreciated.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            It isnt as though I want flowers every day, or chocolates, or tickets to the opera or theatre or a cleaning crew to arrive and clear this joint out once every couple of weeks or so.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Though of course all those things would be nice. (Especially the last).
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            I want respect and general tidiness.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            I want to know I didnt raise a bunch of slovenly sloths.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            I want to be able to hear the doorbell ring unexpectedly without going apeshit over what a mess the house is.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            I want to be able to open the front door without shoving the dog bed outta the way, wishing the library had a door so I could close it, and too terrified to glance in the mirror to see
            <em>
             How Awful I Look
            </em>
            cause Ive been too busy to think about any of that till now.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Appreciation would help in each of these matters.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Appreciative people (even teenage boys) clean up after themselves.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            They respect and care for the house, doing those little things like picking up garbage or dirty socks even when they arent theirs rather than stepping on or over them while bemoaning
            <em>
             Theres Nothing To Eat
            </em>
            though our pantry looks as though it were stocked to feed the entire town of De Smet during Laura Ingalls Wilders
            <em>
             The Long Winter.
            </em>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Have they been trained to do all this? Have they always had chores, been taught to clean properly, to cook, and expected to do their part in helping the family?
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Yes.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            But something, Ive no idea what and really feel medical science should get on this one, happens to a boys brain at around fourteen years of age.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            They suddenly forget everything damn thing you have ever told them and begin to think/talk almost exclusively about four subjects:
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Cars
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Their Hair
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Guitars/Music
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Their Social Life
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            And let me tell you the above four topics are four of the most boring things to spend more than eight minutes talking about on the entire planet.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            I wanna talk about other things once in a while.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            Such as history, how theyre doing in math, how one successfully washes a load of delicates, current events, why they havent washed the pans when it clearly states on the chore chart its their week, do they have a suggestion for dinner, and remember
            <em>
             How We All Decided Thursday Is Your Night To Think Of And Prepare Dinner?
            </em>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            If they accomplished these tasks on a consistent basis, or even once a month or so I really dont think Id feel so Unappreciated.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            But they dont and I do.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            So there.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">

          </p>

           Unappreciated
          </div>
          <div id="tpc_post_message" style="display:none;">
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
            </span>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             <a href="http://www.neebeep.com/itsownsweetwillneebeepc/2014/03/holy-crap-look-what-im-doing-in-april.html" target="_blank">
              {Dubious}LifeStyleBlogger
             </a>
             ingredients:
             <em>
              <a href="http://www.neebeep.com/itsownsweetwillneebeepc/family-life/" target="_blank">
               family, teens, kids
              </a>
              , #RantingBecauseICan, #ComplainingMakesYouFeelBetter
             </em>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Unappreciated
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             I, like many parents and people in general, feel unappreciated some of the time.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             A lot of the time.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Ok  most of the time.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             I just dont get it.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             I gave up my (more or less) stunning figure to give birth to these children. (Really I was a slide on easy size three at one time. Probably zero in designer clothes  had I ever bought any designer clothes).
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             And what size am I now? Suffice to say not a three. Or a five.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             And yes I know there are simply reams of women out there with five or seven or fifteen children who look absolutely glorious in a string bikini. But at this point I aint one of em.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             {Note how I had to add at this point?
             <a href="http://www.neebeep.com/itsownsweetwillneebeepc/2013/07/how-to-be-a-dorkoptimist.html" target="_blank">
              Such is the curse/blessing of the Eternal Optimist aka Dorkoptimist.}
             </a>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             But I am working on it, as evidenced by this fascinating series:
             <a href="http://www.neebeep.com/itsownsweetwillneebeepc/lisawalksaroundplanetearth/" target="_blank">
              #LisaWalksAroundPlanetEarth
             </a>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Though I have been neglectful of my exercise regimen of late due to my infamous deformed and painful vertebrae, a nasty chest cold, and a serious bout of allergic reaction over the last few days.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             {And yeal those are excuses and Im stickin to em.}
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             But back to Unappreciated.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             It isnt as though I want flowers every day, or chocolates, or tickets to the opera or theatre or a cleaning crew to arrive and clear this joint out once every couple of weeks or so.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Though of course all those things would be nice. (Especially the last).
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             I want respect and general tidiness.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             I want to know I didnt raise a bunch of slovenly sloths.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             I want to be able to hear the doorbell ring unexpectedly without going apeshit over what a mess the house is.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             I want to be able to open the front door without shoving the dog bed outta the way, wishing the library had a door so I could close it, and too terrified to glance in the mirror to see
             <em>
              How Awful I Look
             </em>
             cause Ive been too busy to think about any of that till now.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Appreciation would help in each of these matters.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Appreciative people (even teenage boys) clean up after themselves.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             They respect and care for the house, doing those little things like picking up garbage or dirty socks even when they arent theirs rather than stepping on or over them while bemoaning
             <em>
              Theres Nothing To Eat
             </em>
             though our pantry looks as though it were stocked to feed the entire town of De Smet during Laura Ingalls Wilders
             <em>
              The Long Winter.
             </em>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Have they been trained to do all this? Have they always had chores, been taught to clean properly, to cook, and expected to do their part in helping the family?
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Yes.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             But something, Ive no idea what and really feel medical science should get on this one, happens to a boys brain at around fourteen years of age.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             They suddenly forget everything damn thing you have ever told them and begin to think/talk almost exclusively about four subjects:
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Cars
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Their Hair
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Guitars/Music
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Their Social Life
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             And let me tell you the above four topics are four of the most boring things to spend more than eight minutes talking about on the entire planet.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             I wanna talk about other things once in a while.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             Such as history, how theyre doing in math, how one successfully washes a load of delicates, current events, why they havent washed the pans when it clearly states on the chore chart its their week, do they have a suggestion for dinner, and remember
             <em>
              How We All Decided Thursday Is Your Night To Think Of And Prepare Dinner?
             </em>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             If they accomplished these tasks on a consistent basis, or even once a month or so I really dont think Id feel so Unappreciated.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             But they dont and I do.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13pt;">
             So there.
            </span>
           </p>
