     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Headless Heroes of the Apocalypse
       </b>
      </i>
      is an album of American
      <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">
       soul music
      </a>
      by artist
      <a href="/wiki/Gene_McDaniels" title="Gene McDaniels">
       Eugene McDaniels
      </a>
      , released in 1971.
     </p>
     <p>
      As with McDaniel's previous album, this is not a typical Soul album, which can even be seen by the cover image (a picture of McDaniels screaming between two warring
      <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">
       samurai
      </a>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      This album dabbles in form between
      <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">
       soul
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">
       Funk
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">
       jazz
      </a>
      and even
      <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">
       folk
      </a>
      . In addition, it has been a collector's item among rap music and
      <a href="/wiki/Rare_groove" title="Rare groove">
       rare groove
      </a>
      enthusiasts since the early 90s when several of the songs were sampled by many hip hop producers including
      <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">
       Pete Rock
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(rapper)" title="Q-Tip (rapper)">
       Q-Tip
      </a>
      .
     </p>
