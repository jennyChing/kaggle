       <h2 class="entry-title">
        Environmental Award Won by Mother for Beating Monsanto
       </h2>
       <div class="title-wrapper">
       </div>
      </header>

       <div class="author-image">

        <p>

        <p>
         After experiencing the traumatizing death of her daughter to kidney failure just three days after her daughter was born,Sofia Gatica from Argentina became determined to find out what killed her daughter. Her conclusion? Monsantos genetically modified soy fields that surrounded her neighborhood, laced with damaging insecticides negatively affecting nearby neighborhood children and adults alike. Gatica began to detail how her small town was plagued with astronomically high birth defect rates, respiratory disease, and even infant mortality. What she didnt know was that she would later receive an
         <strong>
          environmental award
         </strong>
         for her efforts to make change.
        </p>

        <h2>
         <span style="color: #959138;">
          Top Environmental Award Given to a Mother for Beating Monsanto
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         From this point, the courageous
         <a href="http://naturalsociety.com/woman-receives-anonymous-threats-opposing-monsanto/">
          mother decided
         </a>
         to take on Monsanto. Amazingly, she is not alone in her struggle against the biotechnology colossus when it comes to causing birth problems, as a large group of farmers  also from Argentina  have launched a lawsuit against Monsanto forcausing devastating birth defects
         <a href="http://naturalsociety.com/monsanto-knowingly-poisoned-workers-devastating-birth-defects/">
          in children
         </a>
         . Gatica was initially alone, however, when she first began her uphill battle. Forming a group of concerned mothers in her local area of Ituzaingafter hosting an event at her home to discuss her experiences, the mother would be one of the very few who has actually beat Monsanto.
        </p>
        <p>
         After sharing her story with local mothers who were also concerned for the safety of their children and families as a whole, Gatica co-founded theMothers of Ituzaing  an action group of 16 mothers collaborating to end Monsantos rampant chemical usage. The team took to the streets, going door to door to create what was the firstepidemiological study of the area, only to discover that the effects of Monsantos concoctions were dramatically affecting many families in the town ofItuzaing. With
         <strong>
          cancer rates 41 times the national average
         </strong>
         , something had to be done.
        </p>
        <p>
         As a result of the serious campaign to eradicate Monsanto, the mothers were rewarded. Argentinas Supreme Court not only banned chemical spraying near populated areas, but demanded that the government as well as soy manufacturers now prove that these chemicals are safe. Sofia Gatica is now being honored for her great environmental work with the
         <a href="http://www.goldmanprize.org/recipient/sofia-gatica" rel="nofollow">
          Goldman Environmental prize
         </a>
         , a major environmental award given to activists. Although not everyone will receive an environmental award for creating change, this story shows just how serious activism can take down most any threat  even Monsanto.
         <br/>
        </p>
