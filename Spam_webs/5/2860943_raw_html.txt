         <h1 class="title">
          Attention, Californians: Your Water-Wasting Days Are Over
         </h1>
         <div class="headline">
          The drought-stricken state is on the verge of imposing mandatory water restrictions that carry $500 a day fines.
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="section">
         <div class="row row-remove-xs">
          <div class="main-image">
           <img alt="" class="main-media" src="http://www.takepart.com/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/506516131.jpg?itok=7mSOReJX"/>
           <div class="caption">
            <p>
             (Photo: Ed Clark/Getty Images)
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>

         <div class="main-content">
          <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
           <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item even">
             <p>
              Californians reaction to their states
              <a href="http://www.takepart.com/article/2014/06/20/californias-catastrophic-drought-just-got-worse-lot-worse" target="_self">
               record-breaking drought
              </a>
              so far has been to shrug and hose down their cars.
             </p>
             <p>
              Gov. Jerry Browns call for a voluntary 20 percent reduction in water consumption has gone largely unheeded, and now the hammer is coming down. Sort of. On Tuesday, state regulators will consider imposing mandatory water restrictions for 270 days. While some California cities and counties have imposed water rationing, this is the first move to do so statewide.
             </p>
             <p>
              Want to wash your car in the driveway? No go. Unless your hose is fitted with a shut-off nozzle. And just be sure water doesnt drip on the pavement. The application of water to any hard surface, including, but not limited to, driveways, sidewalks, and asphalt is strictly prohibited, according to the
              <a href="http://www.waterboards.ca.gov/board_info/agendas/2014/jul/071514_10.pdf" target="_blank">
               draft regulations
              </a>
              to be voted on by the California State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB).
             </p>
             <p>
              Also banned: Excessive lawn watering and using nonrecycled water in fountains. Urban water suppliers will be required to limit their customers outdoor water use to two days a week.
             </p>
             <p>
              And if you get caught violating the restrictions, it'll cost you $500 a day.
             </p>
             <div class="takepart-take-action-widget align-left" data-action-id="9034989" data-article-id="45801" data-widget-type="actions_widget" data-widget-width="300">
             </div>
             <p>
              The emergency regulations set a minimum standard requiring only modest lifestyle changes across the state, wrote the SWRCB staff, recommending the board adopt the proposed measures. Many communities are already doing more and have been for years. They should be commended, but can and should do more. Others are not yet doing so and should at least do this, but should do much more given the severity of the drought.
             </p>
             <p>
              Only 30 percent of urban water suppliers have enacted mandatory cuts in water consumption, according to the SWRCB. While
              <a href="http://qz.com/161935/california-faces-a-catastrophic-drought-next-year/" target="_blank">
               Californias farmers have seen their irrigation water cut off
              </a>
              , the states big cities have kept the taps running.
             </p>
             <aside class="inline-content col-sm-5 pull-right">
              <a class="inline-content-link" href="http://www.takepart.com/article/2014/06/20/californias-catastrophic-drought-just-got-worse-lot-worse">
               <div class="line line-style-3 top">
               </div>
               <div class="inline-label text-center">
                Related
               </div>
               <div class="inline-content-inner">
                <div class="field field-name-field-thumbnail field-type-media field-label-hidden">
                 <div class="field-items">
                  <div class="field-item even">
                   <img alt="" src="http://www.takepart.com/sites/default/files/styles/inline_thumbnail/public/465639979_0.jpg?itok=ZtsIBwK7"/>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <h3 class="inline-title text-center">
                 California's Catastrophic Drought Just Got Worsea Lot Worse.
                </h3>
               </div>
               <div class="line line-style-3 bottom">
               </div>
              </a>
             </aside>
             <p>
              Large cities in various regions of the state supplied by wholesale water agencies have relied upon stored reserves, and therefore may not have instituted significant conservation efforts so far this year, the SWRCB staff noted. Preliminary survey data suggests that urban water suppliers can do more to support water conservation by residents and businesses.
             </p>
             <p>
              Even so, state regulators for now are avoiding more draconian actions, such as imposing limits on residents' daily water consumption.
             </p>
             <p>
              Getting individual Californians to take the drought seriously isnt the only problem. When the SWRCB asked Californias 440 water suppliers to fill out a survey detailing their efforts to conserve water, 61 percent didn't bother to reply.
             </p>
