          Easy Garden: Fall Leaves Make Great Mulch for Your Lawn
         </h1>
         <h2 class="subtitle">
          Fall leaves make a great mulch for the lawn and adds nutrients too! All you need for this easy garden tip is a lawn mower to break up the leaves and nature takes care of the rest!
         </h2>

        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          <img alt="Autumn Leaves" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5288331" height="416" src="http://www.birdsandblooms.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Autumn-Leaves.jpg" width="628"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Fall has officially arrived and leaves will soon be turning beautiful shades of yellow, orange and red, providing a fall display that we look forward to each year.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, its what comes after the show that does not make most people happy  fallen leaves! BUT, did you know that there are some people who look forward to seeing fallen leaves on their lawns?
         </p>
         <p>
          Now before you start thinking that people like this must be a little crazy  let me tell you an easy garden secret that they have already discovered 
          <strong>
           Fallen leaves make a great mulch and add nutrients to your lawn.
          </strong>
          Whats even better is that you dont have to haul out your rake and trash bags  all you need is your lawn mower.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="fall leaves as organic matter" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5288330" height="416" src="http://www.birdsandblooms.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fall-leaves-as-organic-matter.jpg" width="628"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          When leaves are broken up into smaller pieces, they gradually break down and make their way down into the grass where they provide
          <strong>
           mulch
          </strong>
          . The mulching action of the leaves keeps a majority of weeds from germinating in spring. Now wait, it gets even better  as the leaves break down, they add
          <strong>
           nutrients
          </strong>
          to your lawn as well, which is vital to rapid recovery and regrowth in spring while needing less supplemental fertilizer.
         </p>
         <p>
          So, are you ready to mow your leaves instead of raking them this fall?
         </p>
         <p>
          To create leaf mulch from your autumn leaves simply set your lawn mower on its highest setting (without the bag) and run over the leaves twice, which will break them down into small pieces.
          <em>
           (I dont know about you, but Id much rather mow leaves than rake them!)
          </em>
          Thereafter, every couple of weeks, continue to mow your leaves until they are finished falling. Youll be surprised at how quickly they disappear from the lawn surface as they break down.
         </p>
         <p>
          Creating leaf mulch for your lawn is a great example of sustainable, organic and easy gardening!
         </p>
         <p>
          So, as the temperatures begin to cool and the first brightly colored leaves begin to fall, leave the rake in the shed and reach for your lawn mower  youll end up with a healthier lawn with much less effort.
