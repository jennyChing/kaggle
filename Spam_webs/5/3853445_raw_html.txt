     <h2 class="title">
      <a href="http://blogpond.com.au/2007/09/27/emotional-abuse-against-children/" rel="bookmark">
       Emotional Abuse Against Children
      </a>
     </h2>
     <script charset="utf-8" src="http://feeds.feedburner.com/~s/DippingIntoTheBlogpond?i=http://blogpond.com.au/2007/09/27/emotional-abuse-against-children/" type="text/javascript">
     </script>
     <div class="meta">
      <p>
       Published by
       <a href="http://blogpond.com.au/author/admin/" title="Posts by Meg">
        Meg
       </a>
       at 4:43 pm under
       <a href="http://blogpond.com.au/category/random/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in random">
        random
       </a>
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="entry">
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.blogcatalog.com/group/bloggers-unite/discuss/entry/badges-for-blogging-against-abuse-place-them-on-your-blog" target="_blank" title="Emotional Abuse">
       </a>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       <a href="http://www.blogcatalog.com/group/bloggers-unite/discuss/entry/badges-for-blogging-against-abuse-place-them-on-your-blog" target="_blank" title="Emotional Abuse">
        <img alt="Emotional Abuse" src="http://blogpond.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2007/09/bl_unite_badge_childabuse.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Children are a wonderful gift, so it upsets me when I hear parents say truly awful things to their children. While most parents gasp in disbelief at despicable acts of physical and sexual abuse,
       <strong>
        emotional
       </strong>
       abuse can often go unnoticed. The outward
       <a href="http://www.helpguide.org/mental/child_abuse_physical_emotional_sexual_neglect.htm#signs">
        signs
       </a>
       are more difficult to pick. The behaviour is hard  to describe (especially for a child) and they would probably not be aware this is
       <strong>
        not
       </strong>
       normal. The results, however, can be devastating to a developing psyche.
      </p>
      <p>
       I am not an expert in such abuse. I was (and am) blessed with wonderful, supportive parents. Nor am I a perfect parent. But I do try to be very conscious of what I say, and how I say it, to my children.
      </p>
      <p align="center">
       
       <em>
        Youll never amount to anything
       </em>
       
       <br/>
       
       <em>
        Why cant you be more like your sister
       </em>
       
       <br/>
       
       <em>
        Look at what youve done to me
       </em>
       
       <br/>
       
       <em>
        Your friends are no good
       </em>
       
      </p>
      <p>
       While it is certainly our place to guide our children, and instruct them about appropriate behaviour, it is
       <strong>
        NOT
       </strong>
       our place to:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        overly criticise
       </li>
       <li>
        humiliate, belittle or ridicule
       </li>
       <li>
        terrorise (e.g. ranting tirade, breaking things, threats of violence or un-named consequences)
       </li>
       <li>
        withdraw love and affection
       </li>
       <li>
        manipulate or brainwash
       </li>
       <li>
        invalidate (dismiss) their feelings
       </li>
       <li>
        use them as a substitute for whats missing in
        <em>
         our
        </em>
        life
       </li>
       <li>
        blame them for whats not right with our life
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Usually verbal abuse is a large part of emotional (or psychological) abuse. However actions (recoiling), or no action (ignoring) and non-verbal communication (the stare,  glare) are also a large part.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Please treat children with respect. Nurture, encourage, support and love them unconditionally so that they can grow up to be happy, confident individuals.
       </strong>
       <em>
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.whitney-houston.com/musician/lyrics/wh85/09.htm">
        Greatest Love of All
       </a>
       performed by Whitney Houston
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        I believe that children are our future
        <br/>
        Teach them well and let them lead the way
        <br/>
        Show them all the beauty they possess inside
        <br/>
        Give them a sense of pride to make it easier
        <br/>
        Let the childrens laughter remind us how we used to be
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        I decided long ago
        <br/>
        Never to walk in anyones shadows
        <br/>
        If I fail, if I succeed
        <br/>
        At least I will live as I believe
        <br/>
        No matter what they take from me
        <br/>
        They cant take away my dignity
       </p>
      </blockquote>
