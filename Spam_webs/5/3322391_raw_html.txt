                <div class="js-h3-container-upper">
                 <p class="ui-block description--lead">
                  Why you might feel faint and dizzy in pregnancy, what it could be a sign of and how to avoid it.
                 </p>
                </div>
                <div class="js-advert-marker">
                </div>
                <div class="advert-container js-advert-wrapper-1">
                </div>
               </div>
               <div class="main-article ui-block" data-pagecount="1">
                <div class="js-h3-container-lower">

                </div>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 If youre experiencing fainting, faintness or dizziness during your pregnancy it can because of an iron deficiency and the lack of red blood cells being created.
                </p>
                <p>
                 When you're pregnant, you have more blood flowing around your system. This increase in blood means you need higher
                 <a href="http://www.madeformums.com/pregnancy-diet-and-nutrition/nutrients-for-pregnancy/391.html">
                  iron
                 </a>
                 levels.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Although youre tested early in your pregnancy for anaemia it can develop later on and many midwives or doctors will ask you to have another iron test if theyre worried your iron levels are low.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Anaemia can be easily treated with a course of iron tablets, but dont start taking iron tablets just in case without talking to your doctor first.
                </p>
                <p>
                 You should increase your intake of iron-rich foods. Vitamin C helps you absorb iron, so try to have a source of vitamin (orange juice is one example) when youre eating iron-rich foods.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Another reason you feel faint or dizzy can be because your blood sugar level is low.
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <h3>
                 How to avoid fainting and dizziness
                </h3>
                <p>
                 Always carry some snacks with you, as going without food will affect your more now than when you werent pregnant.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Try to avoid getting too hot. Be aware when youre going in and out of shops, for example, and undo your coat when inside so you dont inadvertently overheat. Even in winter youll notice youre much hotter than you usually are. Wear layers you can peel off easily.
                </p>
                <p>
                 If you feel faint or dizzy, lying down will help blood circulation to your brain.
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>

                </p>
                <p>
                 [#1.2]If you do get frequent dizzy spells, mention it to your midwife. If you faint, call your midwife or doctor. Its probably nothing major, but its worth double-checking sooner rather than later.
                </p>

                <h3>
                 <strong>
                  Mums story
                 </strong>
                </h3>
                <h4>
                 <strong>
                  I felt spaced out and wobbly
                 </strong>
                </h4>
                <p>
                 I used to have a big breakfast everyday but then usually work through until lunch. A couple of months ago I had a meeting that lasted the whole morning so I didnt get lunch until 1.30pm. By the time I went out of the office to the Sainburys across the road I felt really spaced out and wobbly. At that point I learned that I really needed to keep some nuts or fruit with me, even if it meant discreetly snacking during long meetings!
                </p>
