        <p>
         <span style="font-size: medium;">
          At the beginning of every
          <a href="http://www.seotraininglondon.org/" title="SEO Training London">
           SEO training course
          </a>
          I conduct a quick straw poll to gauge how much people understand about search and how they feel about the factors that might be affecting their search results.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: medium;">
          In an effort to test these responses more scientifically, I commissioned a survey in conjunction with CINTto aska census-representative sample of 1000 U.S. citizens how they felt about organic search, PPC, personalization, social search and
          <a href="http://www.seotraininglondon.org/seo-services/">
           SEO
          </a>
          .
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          I hope that this research will be helpful to any
          <a href="http://www.seotraininglondon.org/seo-services-london/">
           SEO Consultants
          </a>
          who need to convince their clients or employer of the merits of organic search over other, less-trusted forms of online marketing.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.seotraininglondon.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Google_Survey-v04_HRcomp.png" rel="http://www.seotraininglondon.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Google_Survey-v04_HRcomp.png" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Business_SEO_Comp" class="aligncenter wp-image-992" height="2826" src="http://www.seotraininglondon.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Google_Survey-v04_HRcomp.png" style="border: 1px solid black;" width="650"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          The results were, for the most part, consistent with my own findings and indicate a revealing (and often surprising) range of attitudes, misconceptions and behaviours. Of particular interest to those working in SEO is likely to be the overall positive attitude towards sites that rank prominently in organic SERPs compared to those that appear as sponsored listings, even where there exists no real understanding of what makes a page rank prominently on Google.
         </span>
        </p>
