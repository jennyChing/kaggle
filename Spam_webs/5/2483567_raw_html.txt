         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
          BOOM: All Federal Gun Laws are Unconstitutional
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="article-byline">
         Posted on February 7, 2014
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody" style="color: #363636">
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" height="281" src="//player.vimeo.com/video/60944105" width="500">
          </iframe>
          <br/>
          Freedom Outposts Constitutional scholar Publius Huldah recently explained why Federal gun laws are unlawful. She noted that the first gun control measures put in place in the United States did not take place until 1927, when Congress banned the mailing of certain weapons. We went from 1776 to 1927, 150 years after our founding, when Congress decided, We better start disarming the American people.
         </p>
         <p>
          Huldah goes through the history of the Federal governments unlawful actions to regulate firearms in America and she points out that when it started, the Progressives had already begun a takeover. Ill also note the Federal Reserve had been established in 1913 as well.
         </p>
         <p>
          In 1938 Congress legislated that gun dealers had to obtain a Federal Firearms Licenses and maintain names and addresses of those they sold their firearms to.
         </p>
         <p>
          She was sarcastic when she asked if we would ask the current President and Congress for crumbs, begging them to let us keep some of our firearms.
         </p>
         <p>
          She called the letter from the Utah Sheriffs Association shameful because it begged Obama not to impose restrictions on firearms by executive order, but rather let Congress determine those things.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, Huldah said that We must make a principled resistance. To do that, we must learn the applicable principle. She then pointed her listeners to the Constitution to see whether or not the Federal government can impose such legislation.
         </p>
         <p>
          Huldah then pointed out that there is a little known fact about the Constitution:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           It is one of enumerated powers only. When We the people ordained and established the Constitution, we created the Federal government. It is our creature. We are the creator. It is the creature. It is not our master.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          The Constitution is so short, she continued, because all of the powers enumerated to the Federal government are listed in it. Depending on how you count, we delegated only 21 powers to the Federal government. Article 1, Section 8, Clauses 1-16 are those powers.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Huldah then courageously pointed out that all laws made by Congress, any restrictions imposed by the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms, any restrictions made by executive order, and all Supreme Court decisions that restrict firearms are unconstitutional. They are unconstitutional because there is no authority to do so.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          God is the giver of mens rights, according to the Declaration of Independence, and the right to defend ones self and ones family is not only a right, but it is a duty and responsibility before God, according to the Bible which is a demonstration of our love for others. Therefore, she rightly pointed out that the Second Amendment is not the source of our right. It merely recognizes that the right is to be free from any interference whatsoever to defend ourselves, our families and our communities from attack. This understanding is as old as human history, she says.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://freedomoutpost.com/2013/03/all-federal-gun-laws-are-unconstitutional/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://freedomoutpost.com/2013/03/all-federal-gun-laws-are-unconstitutional/', 'This article continues at freedomoutpost.com');">
            This article continues at freedomoutpost.com
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
