       <h2 class="single">
        Pasta with Creamy Mushroom Sauce
       </h2>
       <div class="postDate">
        October 22nd, 2013
        <!-- by MushroomChannel -->
       </div>
       <p>
        <em>
         Rather than tossing mushrooms into your next pasta, infuse them directly into your sauce with this versatile recipe from
         <a href="http://www.bellalimento.com/">
          Bellalimento
         </a>
         .
        </em>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <img alt="Pasta with Creamy Mushroom Sauce by Bellalimento" class="aligncenter wp-image-4127" height="800" src="http://mushroominfo.com/mushroomchannel/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Pasta-with-Creamy-Mushroom-Sauce-www.bellalimento.com-019-533x800.jpg" title="Pasta with Creamy Mushroom Sauce by Bellalimento" width="533"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        This creamy mushroom sauce is a mushroom lovers dream sauce, but its also a cooks dream since it comes together in about 10 minutes time and is incredibly versatile. Similar to the texture of alfredo, its best served over any long pasta you have on hand such as pappardelle or egg noodles. Its simply a perfect sauce to have in your repertoire.
       </p>
       <p>
        The sauce is not only delicious tossed with perfectly cooked al dente pasta; its fantastic as a topping for pork chops or chicken. Dont forget the bread because youll want to sop up every drop of this creamy concoction.
       </p>
       <p>
        Baby bella mushrooms add an earthy undertone to the sauce but feel free to substitute white buttons or your favorite mushroom variety.
       </p>
       <p>
        Versatile and delicious? Yes please.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Pasta with Creamy Mushroom Sauce
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Serves 4
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Ingredients:
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         <br/>
        </span>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 13px;">
          1 pound pappardelle pasta
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 13px;">
          salt
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 13px;">
          3 tablespoons olive oil
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 13px;">
          2 tablespoons unsalted butter
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           onion  minced
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 13px;">
          1 clove garlic  smashed
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 13px;">
          8 ounces whole baby bella mushrooms  stems removed and minced, mushroom caps quartered
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 13px;">
          1 cup heavy whipping cream
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 13px;">
          Parmigiano Reggiano cheese  grated
         </span>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        Directions:
       </p>
       <p>
        1. Place a large pot of generously salted water onto boil. Cook pasta until al dente. Reserve  cup pasta water. Drain pasta. Set aside.
       </p>
       <p>
        2. In a large skillet heat oil and butter over medium heat. Add onion, garlic and minced mushroom stems. Cook for approximately 5-6 until softened. Stir as necessary.
       </p>
       <p>
        3. Add mushroom caps, cream and season with salt and pepper. Continue cooking additional 4-5 minutes.
       </p>
       <p>
        4. Add cheese and drained pasta. Stir to combine. If sauce is too thick add some of the reserved pasta water.
       </p>
       <p>
        5. Garnish with additional cheese if desired.
       </p>
