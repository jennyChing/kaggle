       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Website management Introduction
       </h1>
       <div class="subheading-top">
       </div>
       <h2 class="subheading">
        Introducing our newest series: Website management
       </h2>
       <p class="meta post-meta">
        Posted on
        <span class="updated">
         October 8, 2014
        </span>
        by
        <span class="vcard author">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://webninjablog.com/author/missyg/" rel="author" title="Posts by Melissa G">
           Melissa G
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        in
        <a href="http://webninjablog.com/category/series/" rel="category tag">
         Series
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://webninjablog.com/category/series/website-management/" rel="category tag">
         Website Management
        </a>
        //  0 Comments
       </p>
      </header>
      <div class="entry clearfix">
       <p>
        Whether you are a small startup company with a home-grown website, or the largest corporation with a budget in the millions, youll need a web management plan. Websites dont take care of themselves  as evidenced by the countless abandoned sites, like so much litter on the side of the highway.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        There is always a temptation to take a great idea and push it forward, regardless of whether the framework to support that idea is really in place. All too often this rush to market is the eventual doom of even the brightest ideas.
        <br/>
        Beware the trap: 
        <em>
         Once people see my brilliant idea they wont be able to resist what Im offering, and the cash will roll in
        </em>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The idea that you have may, in fact, be genius; but if you havent figured out how you are going to get it in front of your potential customers, then Im afraid cash will pour out rather than in.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Marketing is only one aspect of website management; content updates, website upgrades and security are just a few of the necessary considerations of a successful online venture.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Limited budget doesnt mean limited research or limited home work. In fact, it means quite the opposite. Large companies have the benefit of being able to hire the best, brightest minds to ensure that their message is both distributed and understood. A new website with a small budget is far more likely to fail. Notice we didnt say will fail. We dont fall into the Need money to make money camp; if we were, then we wouldnt be in business either.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Do your homework.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Are you reading this article wondering what we are talking about? If so, you should probably do some more research.
        <br/>
        Wait, isnt that why were here? Well, yes it is, and we want you to be successful. This is why we will present helpful checklists and articles throughout this website.
        <br/>
        This series, Website management, will cover all the bases; from starting a successful online business and creating your website, to website promotion, maintenance, even monetization.
       </p>
