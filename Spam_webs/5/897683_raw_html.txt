     <h2>
      <a href="http://www.eprintfast.com/onlineprintingblog/entrepreneurs-business-card-designs/" rel="bookmark">
       Business Card Design Tips for Entrepreneurs
      </a>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As an aspiring entrepreneur, your business card remains one of your primary marketing tools. Attractive and compelling presentations deliver results. Pay attention to your business card design. Make that first impression count!
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://www.eprintfast.com/business-cards.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       <img alt="Business Cards for Entrepreneurs" class="aligncenter wp-image-950 size-medium" height="195" src="http://www.eprintfast.com/onlineprintingblog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/stock-SPA-bcard-pile-01-300x195.gif" width="300"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      There are many design options to choose from, but business cards typically fall into five main categories. Understanding how each one works will help you make the right choice for your needs.
     </p>
     <h2>
      How business card designs work:
     </h2>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <b>
        Basic business cards
       </b>
       : Typically black and white (or cream stock), these get straight to the point and are ideal for clients and prospects who just want the facts. If your target audience fits into this category, choose a simple design and ensure the text is minimal, clear and concise.
      </li>
      <li>
       <b>
        Picture business cards:
       </b>
       Having your face (a photo or a drawing or caricature) on the business card promotes recall value the next time you meet a prospect or client.
       <b>
       </b>
       Certain images or pictures may also better represent your product or service. Use a splash of colour to help your card stand out from the rest.
      </li>
      <li>
       <b>
        Tactile business cards:
       </b>
       How your business card feels can also make a distinct impression on your prospects. You can choose from a variety of non-standard materials like metal or wood, or have unusual shapes, edges, folds or embossing instead. Tactile cards are more expensive, but the response they can generate, may be worth the price.
      </li>
      <li>
       <b>
        Multi-purpose business cards
       </b>
       . A business card has many purposes other than simply promoting your name and business. It can also serve as a discount coupon, an appointment reminder or provide valuable information connected with your industry e.g. a local map for hotel guests. Use it creatively or ask the creative team at
       <b>
        ePrintFast
       </b>
       for ideas.
      </li>
      <li>
       <b>
        Creative business cards:
       </b>
       A little bit of imagination can go a long way in making sure you are remembered and will get a quick or immediate response. Whether you choose to be widely original, flamboyant or extravagant, it will cost you those extra dollars. Should you decide to go out on a limb, make sure the end justifies the means. Thats the main purpose isnt itresults?
      </li>
     </ul>
