        Why you may want to work with small to mid size bloggers rather than large bloggers

        <p>
         The website that I own was founded back in 2005. I stepped onto the scene in 2007 and things really took off in 2009. During these last few years Ive watched the Online culture move and shift through a great deal of change  namely  the world of mom bloggers (with a few dads tossed into the mix, yes).
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Im going to share some of my marketing research with you now and a little secret to boot:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Working with small to mid size bloggers is often-times more effective than working with larger bloggers.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Heres why:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Small to mid size bloggers are generally still interested in building relationships and not just stats, recognition and media attention
         </li>
         <li>
          Small to mid size bloggers will usually reply to your contact forms and emails, rather than ignoring you
         </li>
         <li>
          Small to mid size bloggers may post about your business for free orfor a reasonable rate
         </li>
         <li>
          Small to mid size bloggers are often-times more gracious, humble and kind
         </li>
         <li>
          Small to mid size bloggers will generally write a more productive post about you (or your product), spend more time tweeting, more time facebooking, more time referring you to others and more time engaging with you
         </li>
         <li>
          Small to mid size bloggers, often, will be grateful to you for the content, the opportunity to review something and the chance to share your product with their readers
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Heres what happened to me
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         During the last year my business model has modified quite a bit. More and more small business owners want me to help them with Online PR, social media campaigns and online recognition. This has been a wonderful shift for me, getting more and more business every month and working with small businesses that I love. I have a heart for helping small businesses succeed, especially parent-owned and educational businesses.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some of my clients have wanted me to reach out to large bloggers on their behalf, hoping to get a review done: top pick sites, large mom review sites, top ten mommy blogger sites, power mom sites, etc. I generally reply by saying, I would rather reach out to the bloggers that I know and love first. Ive built relationships with them and they know me. They always reply and engage. I can attempt to reach some of these other people but Im telling you now  many of them have jumped aboard the Ego Train, its taken off, and its not returning any time soon.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some of my clients have insisted, Please contact them and see what happens. This is fine  they are paying me to pitch them so of course, I try.
        </p>
        <p>
         I currently have a spread sheet made up of the bloggers Ive worked with and made contact with, hoping for a reply. Of all the bloggers on the lists of Top ________ bloggers with the highest reach lists, can you guess how many of them replied to me?
        </p>
        <p>
         One.
        </p>
        <p>
         I didnt send them rude emails. I was very polite, gracious, thanked them for their time and asked that they get back to me to chat. I even said, If you charge for your services, please send me your price list. Many of the large bloggers demand payment and wont even chat with you unless you offer cash. Ive read enough of their Im entitled to this posts to know. So I never approach them empty handed. Even soonly ONE of the larger bloggers in my spread sheet replied (with the price list).
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Shift over to the smaller and mid size bloggers.
         </strong>
         On my spread sheet I have listed out more than 65 bloggers that Ive been working with recently. Most of those are small to mid size bloggers. Do you know how many of them replied to me, agreed to work with us, and followed through?
        </p>
        <p>
         About 70% of them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Of the other 30%, I had to remind them a few times that they had agreed to work with me and I kindly nudged them along. They nicely replied, Oh, I have been busy. I am so sorry! Working on this now! I never felt as though I was pestering them by checking back or angering them simply for asking about a review/feature that they agreed to post.
        </p>
        <p>
         So far, only two of the smaller bloggers never replied at all. I will be removing them from my blogger contact list.
        </p>
        <p>
         On a positive note, some of these smaller and mid size bloggers have tweeted numerous times for my clients, posted their links on facebook multiple times, sent more twitter followers their way and personally referred them to family members and loved ones.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ill tell you right now
         </strong>
         finding someone like this among the LARGE blogger scene is about as easy as skiing while doing your taxes. Large bloggers, many times, have attended conferences and events and they have been given fancy gifts and gadgets. They have built their online community as a means to an end  to get free stuff and earn more money, fame and attention. Many of them have long forgotten the meaning of
         <strong>
          <em>
           relationship marketing
          </em>
         </strong>
         and have settled into the mindset that they are better than others and have the right to treat their peers with an attitude of belittlement and arrogance.
        </p>
        <p>
         Remember the cool table in high school? This follows the very same pattern of thinking. We are too cool for you. Why are you even bothering us with your trivial questions? Run away now and only return when you can help US more than we can help YOU. (In other wordscome back when you can offer us TV spots, radio interviews and endorsement contracts.)
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, really. As a small business owner, is that someone you think will represent your Brand with love and attention? Im here to tell you that they wont. Even if you pay them big bucks to promote you, they will likely post your feature and forget about you before the publish button has been hit. You may get one tweet and one facebook post if youre fortunate. Youll never hear from them again (other than to update you on their own accomplishments) and that will be that. Youll hope for traffic to come and sales to soar in. You will probably be disappointed when that doesnt happen. In the end youll feel used and unappreciated and wonder where you went wrong. They had such a huge twitter following! Maybe they had a high Klout score! They had a huge facebook following and their Google Friend box was flooded with faces!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The disconnect between their promotion and your lack of sales is simple:
         </strong>
         They dont care about the people they post about, on a personal level. You are a number to them and not a beating heart. They get countless inquiries daily and if you were lucky (said with a chuckle) enough to get a response at all, it was probably because you offered a price or product worth enough dough to gain their attention  something to match their usual exception of product endorsement fees.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont blame yourself. Its not you. Its the Online culture thats been created over the last few years that gave a number of people the chance to ego-out. The selfish side of human nature has reared its ugly head with the Rise of the Power Bloggers.
        </p>
        <p>
         Several of my clients went out seeking these large bloggers on their own, before hiring marketing help. They have expressed to me their feelings of anger and disgust over the way they were treated by some of the more snooty bloggers and websites. Some of the sites/blogs even claim to be working for the little guy but when you get down to it, the attention they have gotten from media sources and readers has mushed the gratitude portion of their brains.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          As they rose to the top they forgot that in order to climb that ladder, others were holding them up.
         </strong>
         They forgot to look down and extend a hand, pulling others up with them. They quickly forget that fame is fleeting and popularity doesnt last. They are enjoying, a little too much, the glory of it all. If you look around youll notice that many large companies have ceased offering bloggers contracts now. They have ceased forking out the large incentives because it simply wasnt worth the return. They were using bloggers all along (cheap advertising) and many of the bloggers were using them  to gain popularity and attention. Such relationships are not made to last and were built on sinking sand to begin with.
        </p>
        <p>
         Emotional Intelligence and Social Emotional Development matter. Some folks say that these traits matter more than anything else, when it comes to long term success. This includes being responsive to others, replying to inquiries with kindness and humility and understanding that a large following doesnt make you a Super human. The large bloggers seem to have forgotten much of this  likely because their vision was clouded by the potential to endorse a Brand and the chance to show others that they matter.
        </p>
        <p>
         Many small and mid size bloggers will never be huge, off-the-chart, out of this world, loved by the masses, worshiped by many, big-time ta-das. And quite frankly  thank God. I like them, just where they are. These bloggers would not be willing to sacrifice their character in order to make the journey to the top.
        </p>
        <p>
         When humble bloggers do make that journey upwards, I have noticed something intriguing over the monthsmany of them back off after they see whats on the other side. They look down, dont like the view, and back up a bit.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is perhaps a very good thing. If they stepped over, there may be no returning.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          My advice to small business owners seeking bloggers to work with:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Value the little guys. Respect those who respect others. In the end, their humility and friendship will matter more to you that a cold interface with a stat-focused fame seeker. You may even end up with a new friend to join you on the journey through social media land. Loyalty matters and you are more likely to find that inside the hearts of those who havent given up their kindness  in trade for fleeting fame.
        </p>
        <p>
         
        </p>
        <p>
         Disclaimer: Im sure there are large bloggers out there who are kind and thoughtful and great to work with. I simply havent found them yet nor has anyone referred them to me. Thats not to say that they dont exist. Somewhere
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

             Well, this is certainly relative. A new blogger would consider a large blogger to be anyone with more than 100 readers, perhaps. For this article, though, the author is focusing more on the bloggers who have thousands of twitter followers, hundreds  thousands of blog subscribers, a large facebook following (most of them have fan pages but not all) and the bloggers who make it clear that they work for Big Brands and not the little guy. The bloggers who attend the conferences in hopes of landing accounts with big brands, free cars to drive around, free trips, free swag bags full of nice things, etc. Its not that GETTING these things is so awfulits that the expectation of these bloggers is that they deserve it and no longer have time to even speak to, or reply to, the smaller folks. Their eyes are set on bigger prizes and the small business owners are worthless to them, as they cant afford to buy their time. Even if they cant work with smaller guys, they could at least reply, right? That takes just a moment to do and they could be kind about it, too: Hi there! Im sorry I cant review ______, however, there are so many great bloggers out there who review products such as yours. I would reach out to them if I were you. Anything, right? A nice, polite response goes a long, long wayso many business owners are used to being ignored that when they DO get a reply from someone, that reply makes the blogger stand out as one of the kind onesthis is a good thing!
