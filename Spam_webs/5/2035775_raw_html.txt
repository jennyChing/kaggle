       <h1 itemprop="headline name">
        A tour of Champagne with Victoria Moore: Telegraph Tours
       </h1>
       <h2 itemprop="alternativeHeadline description">
        Join the Telegraphs wine correspondent for an exclusive weekend festival 
  celebrating some of the greatest champagnes  including Taittinger, 
  Bollinger and Dom Prignon  in some stunning venues
       </h2>
       <style media="print">

        <div class="byline">
         <div>
          <p class="bylineBody">
           By
           <span rel="author">
            Victoria Moore
           </span>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p class="publishedDate">
          3:06PM BST 10 Oct 2014
         </p>
         <div class="cl">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div id="mainBodyArea" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           Ill always remember a Damascene moment of clarity from my first visit to Champagne. I was standing on the driveway of one of the regions 15,000 growers, a glass of his brut NV in my hand. Wed just tasted in his house and, in the winery  it looked more like a garage with tanks, to be honest  across the road, a borrowed bottling line was chinking and popping and clanking as his most recent batch of wine was disgorged. Encountering wine like this, I connected with it in a different way. Its no longer an advertising campaign, a brand, a toast to someone elses good luck; its a gorgeous glass of fizz with its own personality and a unique taste.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           Different champagnes, of course, do taste different. Like any other wine, that taste depends on where precisely on this chalky subsoil the grapes have grown, how theyve been blended (chardonnay brings a creamy gleam, pinot meunier is like a puffball of a spice, and pinot noir gives structure and a sappy energy and focus) and who has made it.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           There are different types of producers, too: the glamorous big houses with the famous names, which buy in grapes and strive to achieve a consistency of style that can be recognised the world over; grower-producers, who make wine from grapes they have grown themselves; and co-operatives, some of which make extremely good wine.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           I wanted this Telegraph Tour to Champagne
           <strong>
           </strong>
           to contain all these elements, to be a fillet of the best bits of my own research trips there  or as much as we could cram into a weekend.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           So in partnership with the wine and travel specialist Arblaster &amp; Clarke we have organised precisely that. On the Friday evening, there will be a dinner in the vaulted crypt of the medieval Palais du Tau in Reims with a handful of Champagnes most prestigious names (and their wines, of course): Bollinger, Gosset, Dom Prignon and Charles Heidsieck (the great and much undervalued secret among champagne houses, adored by all the wine trade). On the Saturday morning well have a Champagne Festival at which youll have the chance to try wines from a selection of my top-rated growers, some of whom are discoveries I unearthed when I spent a week in Champagne last autumn.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
           Weve also arranged a grand gourmet dinner with Taittinger for the Saturday evening, and on the Sunday those taking up the Grand and Premier Cru options of the trip will have chance to visit a vineyard and winery to see where the grapes are grown and learn how the wine is made  and, of course, if you want to buy some bottles to squeeze into your suitcase, that will be possible, too.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ill be on hand on the Friday and Saturday to talk you through the wines and to answer questions. The only questions I will not answer are: is there too much champagne on this weekend away? And: is there, in fact, any such thing as too much champagne? You will have to work that out for yourselves.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <u>
             How to book
            </u>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="https://www.winetours.co.uk/tours/telegraph-tours-champagne-festival-victoria-moore" target="_blank">
             Follow this link
            </a>
           </strong>
           or call 020 7462 8414
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <u>
             Highlights
            </u>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Victoria Moore
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Telegraphs wine correspondent will be on hand to introduce the wines and discuss the subtleties and pleasures of tasting champagnes.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Palace Dinner
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Our Champagne Dinner will be held in the spectacular vaulted crypt of the Palais du Tau and will be served with a magnificent line-up of vintage 2004 champagnes from Pol Roger, Gosset, Charles Heidsieck, Bollinger and Dom Prignon.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Champagne Festival
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Held in the Taittingers Demeure des Comtes de Champagne, the medieval meeting hall of the Counts of Champagne. Here you will have the rare opportunity to taste around 40 different champagnes in a convivial atmosphere with a canap lunch.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Taittinger Dinner
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Over the course of our four-course dinner we will taste the Taittinger range which includes Prelude Grand Cru, the single-vineyard Folies de la Marquetterie and the Comtes de Champagne blanc de blancs. A family member will join us to host this superb evening.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Chance to buy
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           There will be absolutely no pressure, but should you wish to buy direct from the producers at the festival you will be able to. Travelling by coach, you will be able to bring up to 36 bottles of champagne back each with you  more if you are travelling independently.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Visit to Reims
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Reims was badly damaged in the First World War, but, thankfully, some of the most beautiful buildings  the cathedral, the Basilica of St Remi, the Palais de Vergeur, Demeure des Comtes and Palais du Tau, and the Roman Triumphal Arch  remain.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Supplement free
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Ten rooms are available for singles at no supplement.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alttext="The ultimate champagne tour: Telegraph Tours" caption="" declared-caption="" height="307" name="champagne2" photographer="" refid="3068953" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/03068/champagne2_3068953a.jpg" width="460"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <u>
             Full itinerary
            </u>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Day One, Friday March 27
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Morning
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Departure from London for the easy 175-mile journey to the Champagne region (travelling by coach and crossing to Calais or making your own travel arrangements). Here, youll be based for the weekend in a four-star hotel in the centre of the vibrant city of Reims.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Evening
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           This evening we dine in the stunning setting of the vaulted crypt of the medieval Palais du Tau next to Reimss famous cathedral. Here the leading prestige champagne producers will present their selection from their superb cellars for you to savour over dinner hosted by Victoria Moore. We will be joined by one of the Dom Prignon team, Hubert de Billy from Pol Roger, Stephen Leroux from Charles Heidsieck and the incoming director of Gosset, which is the oldest of all champagne producers.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Day Two, Saturday March 28
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Morning
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           This morning after your buffet breakfast you have time to explore Reims.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Lunch
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           A short stroll to the Demeure des Comtes de Champagne, the medieval meeting hall of the Comtes de Champagne, owned by Champagne Taittinger and the setting for the Telegraph Champagne Festival.
          </p>
          <p>
           This private venue has been put aside exclusively for Telegraph readers, who have the opportunity to taste champagnes in a setting simply not available to the general public. We have invited Victoria Moores favourite wine growers from around the Champagne region. These are all small family-run producers making top-quality, terroir-driven champagnes from their own vineyards.
          </p>
          <p>
           Among many others, these will include E Barnaut, which makes powerful pinot noir-based champagnes in the Grand Cru village of Bouzy; the Le Mesnil co-operative of growers who make minerally pure chardonnay champagnes; Ren Geoffroy from Cumires in the Valle de la Marne; bio-dynamic specialists Fleury, up from Champagnes most southerly vineyards; Champagne Moutard from Buxeuil in the Aube which produces aromatic styles; and Champagne Collard-Picard of pernay, which uses oak and modern technology but more importantly has top vineyards and great wines.
          </p>
          <p>
           These producers will present their wines to you and to Victoria, who will be tasting alongside you. If you wish, youll be able to order the champagne that you enjoy on the spot at great value cellar-direct prices. Well have these delivered to the hotel for you before departure.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Dinner
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           We return to the Demeure des Comtes for a spectacular evening hosted by a member of the Taittinger family. Youll enjoy a generous and comprehensive selection of their elegant champagnes over a splendid four-course gourmet dinner.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Day Three, Sunday March 29
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Morning
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           This morning there will be specially arranged visits to champagne producers for those on the Grand &amp; Premier Cru options, so you can see the cellars where thousands of bottles at a time are ageing, hear about the mthode champenoise and enjoy a further glass or two. Youll return to Reims by lunchtime, so you can choose a brasserie or gourmet restaurant for lunch.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Afternoon
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           We head back to the UK by coach, arriving in Dover or Folkestone by around 8pm and London by 10.30pm.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <u>
             Price
            </u>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Premier Cru: 699 per person
           </strong>
           (or 649 if you prefer to make your own travel arrangements) including return executive coach from London or Dover with Eurotunnel Le Shuttle crossing and a visit to champagne producers on Sunday morning.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Grand Cru: 899 per person
           </strong>
           in twin/double includes return executive coach from London or Dover, Club Class P&amp;O ferry crossings (includes a glass of champagne and access to the Club Class lounge), superior rooms at a four-star hotel and a visit to champagne producers on Sunday.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ten single rooms are available for no supplement on a first-come-first-served basis. When these are sold doubles for sole occupancy are available at a supplement of 100 or 140 per person, depending on the standard of the room.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            All options include:
           </strong>
           two nights accommodation in a four-star hotel with buffet breakfasts, two gourmet dinners with champagnes and the Champagne Festival with a canap lunch, Arblaster &amp; Clarke Wine Tours managers and wine experts.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <u>
             Date
            </u>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           March 27-29, 2015
          </p>

           or call 020 7462 8414

                articleId: "",
                currentArticleId: "",
                articleList: [],
                articleListToSkip: [],
                readNextCookieName: "tmg_read_next",
                openButton: "#readNextOpen a",
                closeButton: "#closeBtn a",
                articleContainer: "#readNextArticleContainer",
                widgetContainer: "#readNextWidget",
                displayThumbnail: false,
                panelOpen: false,
                autoOpen: true
            };

            var renderTemplate = function() {
                var template = '&lt;div id="readNextWidget" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextOpen"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div id="readNextOpenInner"&gt;&lt;a href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Next Story&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="icn"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextArticleContainer" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticle"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleInnerWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;p id="readNextLabel"&gt;MOST VIEWED IN NEWS&lt;/p&gt;';
                        
				if(settings.articleImage &amp;&amp; settings.articleImage!="null" &amp;&amp; settings.displayThumbnail){
					template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageThumbnail" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;img src="'+ settings.articleImage +'"/&gt;&lt;/a&gt;';
				}
                        
				template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageTitle" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;' + settings.articleTitle + '&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="closeBtn"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a href="#"&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;iframe style="border:none;position:absolute;top:0;left:0; height:100%;width:100%;z-index:-1;" id="iefix" src="about:blank"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;' + //fix for IE bug
                        '&lt;/div&gt;';
                return template;
            };


            var init = function(currentArticleId, articleList, displayThumbnail) {

                settings.articleList = articleList;
                settings.currentArticleId = currentArticleId;
                settings.displayThumbnail = displayThumbnail;

                settings.articleListToSkip = unescape($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName)).split(',');

                if ($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName) != null) {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    updateCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                } else {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    createCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                }


                if (settings.articleId != "") {

                    $(renderTemplate()).appendTo("body");

                    $("#nextArticleWrapper a, #readNextOpenInner a").click(function () {
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_headline', 'version_1']);

                        getNavPos($(this).attr("href"), "wayfarerReadNext");
                    });

                    bindUIActions();

                    var displayed = false;

                    setTimeout(function () {
                        if (!displayed) {
                            displayed == true;
                            readNextWidget.display();
                        }

                    }, 10000);

                    $(window).scroll(function () {
                        if (isScrolledIntoView("#tmg-related-links")) {
                            if (!displayed) {
                                displayed == true;
                                readNextWidget.display();
                            }
                        }

                        if(isScrolledIntoView("#shareBottom") || isScrolledIntoView("#newGigyaShareBottom")) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }

                        if($(window).scrollTop() + $(window).height() == $(document).height()) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }
                    });
                }
            };

            var display = function () {
                $(settings.widgetContainer).fadeIn(2000, function () {
                    //animation complete
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_closed', 'version_1']);
                });
            };

            var bindUIActions = function () {
                $(settings.openButton).mouseenter(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'hover_closed', 'version_1']);
                    openArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.widgetContainer).mouseleave(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    closeArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.closeButton).click(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_close', 'version_1']);
                    updateCookie(settings.articleId, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    closeArticleContainer();
                    settings.autoOpen = false;
                });
            };

            var openArticleContainer = function () {
                if (!settings.panelOpen) {
                	$("#readNextArticleContainer").show();//show/hide because height is calculated incorrectly for elements with display:none
                    $("#readNextArticleContainer").hide();
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_open', 'version_1']);
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                    settings.autoOpen = false; //set auto open to false once user has viewed this once
                }
                return false;
            };

            var closeArticleContainer = function () {
                if (settings.panelOpen) {
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                }
                return false;
            };


            var isScrolledIntoView = function(elem) {
                if($(elem).length) { //check if element exists
                    var docViewTop = $(window).scrollTop();
                    var docViewBottom = docViewTop + $(window).height();

                    var elemTop = $(elem).offset().top;
                    var elemBottom = elemTop + $(elem).height();

                    return ((elemBottom &lt;= docViewBottom) &amp;&amp; (elemTop &gt;= docViewTop));
                } else {
                    return false;
                }
            };

            var unique = function(array) {
                return array.filter(function (el, index, arr) {
                    return index == arr.indexOf(el);
                });
            }

            var createCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        articleListToSkip[0] = currentArticleId;
                        $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        updateCookie(currentArticleId, articleListToSkip);
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookie = function(articleIdToSkip, articleListToSkip) {
                articleListToSkip.push(articleIdToSkip);
                articleListToSkip = unique(articleListToSkip);
                $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
            }

            var chooseNextArticle = function (currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if ((article.id != currentArticleId) &amp;&amp; ($.inArray(article.id, articleListToSkip) === -1)) {

                        //set article
                        settings.articleTitle = article.title;
                        settings.articleUrl = article.url;
                        settings.articleId = article.id;
						settings.articleImage = article.imgUrl;
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            //public API
            return {
                settings: settings,
                init: init,
                display: display,
                openArticleContainer: openArticleContainer,
                closeArticleContainer: closeArticleContainer
            }
        })();

    };
     </script>
