              <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
               UPDATE:
               <br/>
               On the same date that this image was posted (Feb. 19), Gap Inc.
               <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/02/20/business/gap-to-raise-minimum-hourly-pay.html">
                announced that it would be raising minimum wage
               </a>
               for its employees to $9/hour this year, and to $10/hour in 2015.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               While this is certainly good news, the article also points out even more injustice, though. The corporation itself admits that
               <strong>
                over 70 percent of its employees - 65,000 from a total of 90,000 - have been making that paltry amount below $9/hour
               </strong>
               . And all while the company CEO has been making 1,480 times more than that wide majority of his employees.
