        <p>
         <a href="http://ecosnobberysucks.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/beer-glasses.jpg" rel="lightbox[1482]">
          <img align="right" alt="Mmmm, beer!" height="276" src="http://ecosnobberysucks.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/beer-glasses.jpg" title="Good beer" width="400"/>
         </a>
         If you need one more excuse to
         <a href="http://ecosnobberysucks.com/tag/alcohol/" target="_blank" title="Awesome drinking ideas and reviews">
          get your drink on
         </a>
         as the week ends, then you should know that its
         <a href="http://www.punchbowl.com/holidays/2011/8/5/international-beer-day" target="_blank" title="International Beer Day">
          International Beer Day
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         By the way, I think Michelob Ultra or Bud Light can barely be classified beer. Beer should be flavorful, colorful, and hoppy (but not overly-so). So why not graduate from your the cheap domestic pitches you may normally slam down with your buddies at the bar and branch out to a better beer.
        </p>
        <p>
         What makes a better beer? In my experience, beer that has been made locally (micro-brew) or is sustainably brewed or
         <a href="http://www.greenopia.com/SF/explore_blogs/15559/8-28-2009/This-Beer-is-Green--But-How-Does-it-Taste--Organic-Beer-Review" target="_blank" title="Organic beer taste test">
          certified organic
         </a>
         has the best flavor and drinking experience.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Some of my favorite organic, or sustainable, or microbrew beers
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://ecosnobberysucks.com/2011/07/new-belgium-tour-de-fat-the-best-party-on-2-wheels/" target="_blank" title="New Belgium Tour de Fat">
           New Belgium
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          Yazoo
         </li>
         <li>
          Samuel Smith Organic Lager
         </li>
         <li>
          Bison Brewing
         </li>
         <li>
          Sierra Nevada Brewing Co
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         If youre really adventurous, you could even dive into the
         <a href="http://click.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/click?id=nl181FfWiGY&amp;offerid=206468.30328&amp;type=2&amp;subid=0" target="new">
          home brew beer
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ad.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/show?id=nl181FfWiGY&amp;bids=206468.30328&amp;type=2&amp;subid=0" width="1"/>
         trend.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whats your favorite local, organic, or sustainable beer and will do you have any (or will now be making plans to) get in on some International Beer Day action?
        </p>
