         <div class="entry-content">
          <p>
           DETROIT, MI - For the second day in a row, Detroits small business climate has been ragged on by a report from a national, credit-oriented firm.
          </p>
          <p>
           Yesterday
           <a href="http://www.mlive.com/business/detroit/index.ssf/2013/06/detroit_is_worst_city_to_work.html">
            it was credit card promotional site CardHub
           </a>
           , today it is Biz2Credit, a lending network for small- and medium-sized businesses.
          </p>
          <p>
           Biz2Credit said it has ranked America's fastest growing small business cities based on average annual revenue, average number of employees, average age of business (in months), highest average credit score, and local sales tax rates.
          </p>
          <p>
           Detroit ranks 24th of 25 cities included.
          </p>
          <p>
           To reach its conclusion, Biz2Credit says it analyzed 15,000 businesses with fewer than 250 employees and less than $10 million in annual revenues. The firms had to have been in business for at least a year.
          </p>
          <p>
           Although its initial report does not offer commentary specific to Detroits ranking, it does say that cities that fared well are seeing an influx of entrepreneurial immigrants, while high costs of living and local tax burdens hurt other cities rankings.
          </p>
          <p>
           According to a breakdown of the
           <a href="https://www.biz2credit.com/viewSharedFile.php?action=viewFile&amp;downloadfile=ed9fa4c701355ae700c6fbce9db1ee09">
            numbers in the analysis
           </a>
           , Detroits relatively low sales tax rate of 6 percent was not enough to offset a low average credit score.
          </p>
          <p>
           Here are the complete rankings:
           <br/>
           1.     Houston
           <br/>
           2.     Washington
           <br/>
           3.     Los Angeles
           <br/>
           4.     New York City metro
           <br/>
           5.     Miami-Fort Lauderdale
           <br/>
           6.     Chicago
           <br/>
           7.     Seattle
           <br/>
           8.     Philadelphia
           <br/>
           9.     San Francisco-Oakland
           <br/>
           10.  Riverside-San Bernardino, CA
           <br/>
           11.  Phoenix
           <br/>
           12.  Orlando
           <br/>
           13.  Boston
           <br/>
           14.  Dallas-Fort Worth
           <br/>
           15.  Baltimore
           <br/>
           16.  Cincinnati
           <br/>
           17.  Charlotte
           <br/>
           18.  Denver
           <br/>
           19.  Atlanta
           <br/>
           20.  Tampa-St. Pete
           <br/>
           21.  Jacksonville, FL
           <br/>
           22.  Cleveland
           <br/>
           23.  St. Louis
           <br/>
           24.  Detroit
           <br/>
           25.  San Diego
          </p>
          <p>
           On Monday, CardHub named Detroit the worst place to work for a small business in a report called "
           <a href="http://www.cardhub.com/edu/best-worst-cities-for-small-business-employees/#methodology">
            The Best &amp; Worst Cities to Work for a Small Business.
           </a>
           "
          </p>
          <p>
           Using 10 different metrics form several different sources of data, CardHub concluded, The Motor City was the epicenter for much of the economic strife during the Great Recession, and while bailouts breathed new life into the citys automotive industry, Detroits small business community continues to be marred by low rankings in terms of small businesses per capita (22/30), small business vitality (28/30), net small business job growth (27/30), unemployment rate (29/30), new hire earnings (21/30), hours worked (24/30), and industry variety (26/30).
          </p>
