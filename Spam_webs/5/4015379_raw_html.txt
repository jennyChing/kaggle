     <p>
      <b>
       William Frederick "Billy" Gibbons
      </b>
      (born December 16, 1949)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-imdb-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is an American musician, producer and actor, best known as the guitarist of the American rock band
      <a href="/wiki/ZZ_Top" title="ZZ Top">
       ZZ Top
      </a>
      . He began his career in the
      <a href="/wiki/Moving_Sidewalks" title="Moving Sidewalks">
       Moving Sidewalks
      </a>
      , who recorded
      <i>
       Flash
      </i>
      (1968) and opened four dates for the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jimi_Hendrix_Experience" title="Jimi Hendrix Experience">
       Jimi Hendrix Experience
      </a>
      . Gibbons formed ZZ Top in late 1969 and released
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/ZZ_Top%27s_First_Album" title="ZZ Top's First Album">
        ZZ Top's First Album
       </a>
      </i>
      in 1971. The albums that followed,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_Mud" title="Rio Grande Mud">
        Rio Grande Mud
       </a>
      </i>
      (1972) and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tres_Hombres" title="Tres Hombres">
        Tres Hombres
       </a>
      </i>
      (1973), along with extensive touring, solidified the group's reputation as a hard-rocking power trio. In the 1980s, ZZ Top released their three biggest-selling albums:
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Eliminator_(album)" title="Eliminator (album)">
        Eliminator
       </a>
      </i>
      (1983),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Afterburner_(album)" title="Afterburner (album)">
        Afterburner
       </a>
      </i>
      (1985) and
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recycler_(ZZ_Top_album)" title="Recycler (ZZ Top album)">
        Recycler
       </a>
      </i>
      (1990). A wave of music videos for the hit singles "
      <a href="/wiki/Legs_(song)" title="Legs (song)">
       Legs
      </a>
      ", "
      <a href="/wiki/Gimme_All_Your_Lovin%27" title="Gimme All Your Lovin'">
       Gimme All Your Lovin'
      </a>
      ", and "
      <a href="/wiki/Sharp_Dressed_Man" title="Sharp Dressed Man">
       Sharp Dressed Man
      </a>
      ", among others, became mainstays on
      <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">
       MTV
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Despite ZZ Top's loss of their early fans with radio-friendly sound and blunders such as the remixed compilation
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Six_Pack_(ZZ_Top_album)" title="Six Pack (ZZ Top album)">
        Six Pack
       </a>
      </i>
      (1987), the band's unique blend of boogie and humorous, sometimes raunchy, lyrics, supported by Gibbons' blues-based prowess, continues to attract fans. In recent years, Gibbons has made appearances with other artists and acted on television shows, most notably
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Bones_(TV_series)" title="Bones (TV series)">
        Bones
       </a>
      </i>
      . He was ranked at number 32 on the 2011
      <i>
       Rolling Stone
      </i>
      list of the 100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ZZ Top's album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/La_Futura" title="La Futura">
        La Futura
       </a>
      </i>
      was released in September 2012.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Gibbons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Gibbons was born to Frederick Royal (Freddie) and Lorraine Gibbons in the
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Houston_neighborhoods#T" title="List of Houston neighborhoods">
       Tanglewood
      </a>
      suburb of
      <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">
       Houston
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
       Texas
      </a>
      . His father was an entertainer,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orchestra_conductor" title="Orchestra conductor">
       orchestra conductor
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concert_pianist" title="Concert pianist">
       concert pianist
      </a>
      who worked alongside second cousin, art director
      <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Gibbons" title="Cedric Gibbons">
       Cedric Gibbons
      </a>
      , for
      <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Goldwyn" title="Samuel Goldwyn">
       Samuel Goldwyn
      </a>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer" title="Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer">
       MGM Studios
      </a>
      . In 1963, Gibbons received his first electric guitar following his thirteenth birthday, a
      <a href="/wiki/Sunburst_(finish)" title="Sunburst (finish)">
       sunburst
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Gibson_Melody_Maker" title="Gibson Melody Maker">
       Gibson Melody Maker
      </a>
      , accompanied by a
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Large_Cat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Large Cat (page does not exist)">
       Large Cat
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Amplifier" title="Amplifier">
       amplifier
      </a>
      and was influenced by guitarists such as
      <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Reed" title="Jimmy Reed">
       Jimmy Reed
      </a>
      . While attending Warner Brothers' art school in Hollywood, California, Gibbons engaged with his first bands including The Saints, Billy G &amp; the Blueflames, and The Coachmen. By 18, Gibbons went forward forming an artfully designed band, conceptually inspired by friend and fellow musician,
      <a href="/wiki/Roky_Erickson" title="Roky Erickson">
       Roky Erickson
      </a>
      and The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/13th_Floor_Elevators" title="13th Floor Elevators">
       13th Floor Elevators
      </a>
      , naming the group the "Moving Sidewalks", penning the hit single, "99th Floor" and engaging a friendship with
      <a href="/wiki/Jimi_Hendrix" title="Jimi Hendrix">
       Jimi Hendrix
      </a>
      . Hendrix went on to say on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show" title="The Tonight Show">
        The Tonight Show
       </a>
      </i>
      , and "The Dick Cavett Show" that Gibbons would be the next hottest guitarist.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">
       Musical career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Moving_Sidewalks">
       Moving Sidewalks
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Gibbons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Moving Sidewalks">

     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Moving_Sidewalks" title="Moving Sidewalks">
       Moving Sidewalks
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Gibbons founded the Texas psychedelic group The Moving Sidewalks,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      which recorded several singles and one full-length album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Flash_(Moving_Sidewalks_album)" title="Flash (Moving Sidewalks album)">
        Flash
       </a>
      </i>
      . Gibbons and The Moving Sidewalks came to prominence opening for
      <a href="/wiki/The_Jimi_Hendrix_Experience" title="The Jimi Hendrix Experience">
       The Jimi Hendrix Experience
      </a>
      during Hendrix's first American tour as a headliner. Also notable was the Gibbons-penned song, "99th Floor," its title a nod to the influence on Gibbons of fellow Texans and pioneering psychedelic band
      <a href="/wiki/The_13th_Floor_Elevators" title="The 13th Floor Elevators">
       The 13th Floor Elevators
      </a>
      . He has also commented during live performances while playing the
      <a href="/wiki/String_bending" title="String bending">
       string-bending
      </a>
      intro to "
      <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Lady" title="Foxy Lady">
       Foxy Lady
      </a>
      " that Hendrix taught him how to play the song when Gibbons was "about 17" in Dallas.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Longstanding rumors have it that at the end of the tour Hendrix gave Gibbons the pink Stratocaster he had been playing as token of his appreciation for Gibbons' level of talent, and that Hendrix subsequently stated that Gibbons was one of the best guitarists in the US.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>

        </span>

      </sup>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="ZZ_Top">
       ZZ Top
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Gibbons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: ZZ Top">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/ZZ_Top" title="ZZ Top">
       ZZ Top
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Gibbons formed ZZ Top in late 1969, which then quickly settled on bassist/vocalist
      <a href="/wiki/Dusty_Hill" title="Dusty Hill">
       Dusty Hill
      </a>
      and drummer
      <a href="/wiki/Frank_Beard_(musician)" title="Frank Beard (musician)">
       Frank "Rube" Beard
      </a>
      , both being members of the band
      <a href="/wiki/American_Blues" title="American Blues">
       American Blues
      </a>
      . After honing their trademark
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blues-rock" title="Blues-rock">
       blues-rock
      </a>
      style, they released the aptly titled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/ZZ_Top%27s_First_Album" title="ZZ Top's First Album">
        ZZ Top's First Album
       </a>
      </i>
      on London Records in 1971.
     </p>
     <p>
      The band rolled on, intensively touring and recording/releasing albums until 1977, when they took an extended hiatus. Their long-time manager took this time to negotiate a deal that allowed the band to keep control of their previous recordings, to be distributed by their new label,
      <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" title="Warner Bros. Records">
       Warner Bros. Records
      </a>
      . They reunited two-and-a-half years later in order to start recording under a new Warner Bros. contract. Unbeknownst to each other at the time, both Dusty Hill and Billy Gibbons had grown the chest-length beards that quickly became a part of their image.
     </p>
     <p>
      The band hit international prominence and their commercial peak with the release of 1983's diamond-selling disc
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Eliminator_(album)" title="Eliminator (album)">
        Eliminator
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <i>
       Eliminator
      </i>
      was named after Gibbons' customized
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ford_Model_B_(1932)#1933_Ford" title="Ford Model B (1932)">
       1933 Ford Coupe
      </a>
      , which was featured in three of the band's music videos. This vehicle is on exhibition at the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in Cleveland, Ohio. The album featured the hits "
      <a href="/wiki/Gimme_All_Your_Lovin%27" title="Gimme All Your Lovin'">
       Gimme All Your Lovin'
      </a>
      ", "
      <a href="/wiki/Sharp_Dressed_Man" title="Sharp Dressed Man">
       Sharp Dressed Man
      </a>
      ", and "
      <a href="/wiki/Legs_(song)" title="Legs (song)">
       Legs
      </a>
      ".
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1994, the band signed a multi-million dollar, five-disc deal with
      <a href="/wiki/RCA" title="RCA">
       RCA Records
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2003, a comprehensive collection of recordings from the London and Warner Bros. years entitled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Chrome,_Smoke_%26_BBQ" title="Chrome, Smoke &amp; BBQ">
        Chrome, Smoke &amp; BBQ
       </a>
      </i>
      was released. In 2004, ZZ Top was inducted into the
      <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">
       Rock and Roll Hall of Fame
      </a>
      . They have the distinction of being among a very small group of bands with a 40-year-plus history that still has all of its original members.
     </p>

       Personal life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Gibbons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In December 2005, Gibbons married long-time girlfriend Gilligan Stillwater.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-imdb-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dt>
       Automobile enthusiast
      </dt>
     </dl>
     <p>
      Gibbons is an avid car collector and custom car enthusiast with an extensive collection that includes 1948 Cadillac Series 62 (known as CadZZilla), 1962 Chevrolet Impala (known as Slampala), 1950 Ford Business Coupe, and a 1958 Ford Thunderbird.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ComplexCars_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ComplexCars-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      One of his earliest custom cars, a 1933 Ford coupe (known as Eliminator), was featured in three of his band's videos. Gibbons also published a book in 2011 about his love of cars and guitars titled
      <i>
       Billy F Gibbons: Rock + Rock Gearhead
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gearhead_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Gearhead-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The November 2014 issue of
      <a href="/wiki/Guitar_World" title="Guitar World">
       Guitar World
      </a>
      magazine features an interview of Gibbons and fellow guitarist
      <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Beck" title="Jeff Beck">
       Jeff Beck
      </a>
      about their mutual appreciation of "cars, guitars, and everything in between".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
