          Homeless Folks Find Lost Boy
         </h1>

        <div class="post-meta">

         <div addthis:title="Homeless Folks Find Lost Boy " addthis:url="http://www.freerangekids.com/homeless-folks-find-lost-boy/" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style addthis_">

         <p>
          Readers  A 6-year-old boy with autism had disappeared from his San Jose, CA home at 9 .a.m. Turns out, he had wandered off and
          <a href="http://www.mercurynews.com/bay-area-news/ci_26487152/homeless-people-who-found-missing-boy-declare-were">
           was found in the middle of the night by a group of homeless men and women
          </a>
          who live in an encampment nearby.
         </p>
         <p>
          Does this remind anyone else of the
          <a href="http://www.freerangekids.com/guinevere-and-the-fire-and-us/">
           Guinevere and the Fire
          </a>
          song posted here last week? The one in which a girl, heeding her moms advice, avoids seeking help from the Aboriginal encampment near her home and runs to a white family much further away  and in the meantime, her mom dies?
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.mercurynews.com/bay-area-news/ci_26487152/homeless-people-who-found-missing-boy-declare-were">
           What a much happier story
          </a>
          this one was! While my guess is that if San Jose residents knew they were living near a camp of homeless folks, many might not have been thrilled about its proximity to their homes and children, and yet 
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           SAN JOSE  Jose Salmeron woke early in the homeless encampment he often stays at and intended to take a makeshift shower when he heard a strange rustling noise.
          </p>
          <p>
           I popped out of my tent and I saw a naked person. At first I thought it was an alien, like ET, said Salmeron, who said he did a double-take and grabbed his flashlight. I shined my flashlight at him and when I looked closer it was a little boy. He hollered to his fellow camp mates, Come out, there is a naked boy out here.'
          </p>
          <p>
           The boy was 6-year-old Sergio Zepeda, who went missing Saturday morning and was the subject of a frantic overnight search until he wandered into the encampment, startling the residents. Soon after, around 4:15 a.m. Sunday morning, another resident of the encampment called 911 and gave what turned out to be good news to the dispatcher.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          The other good news is just a reminder that were all in this together, whether we have homes or not, whether we have served time or not.  L.
         </p>
