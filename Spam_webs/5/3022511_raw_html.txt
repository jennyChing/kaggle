        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         The Importance of Pornography
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-01-20T09:00:11+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 20, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://findingjustice.org/author/ericdye/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Eric Dye
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://findingjustice.org/importance-of-pornography/#comments">
           3 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <img alt="sports stadium" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-13716" height="384" src="http://findingjustice.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/sports-stadium-580x384.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The NFL Playoffs wrapped-up this weekend and social media was busy with football messages. The grocery stores around the United States were full of NFL football junk food displays for football parties, and with the Super Bowl just around the corner, there will be lots of memorabilia and clothing sales, too.
        </p>
        <p>
         Professional sports are a big, but do you know what is even bigger?
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-13715">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Pornography.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats right. According to
         <a href="http://www.everymanministries.com/" target="_blank">
          Every Man Ministries
         </a>
         , it is estimated that $14 billion is spent on pornography per year  not to mention the money that is exchanged for prostitution, sex clubs and novelty items. That $14 billion  with a B  is more than what the United States spends on professional baseball, basketball and football combined.
        </p>
        <p>
         Considering
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/jesselawrence/2014/01/19/seahawks-broncos-are-most-expensive-super-bowl-tickets-in-last-six-years/" target="_blank">
          how much a Super Bowl ticket is
         </a>
          an average of $4,080 ($2,110 for cheap seats) for Super Bowl XLVIII  that is a chunk of change.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, consider how much you see and hear about professional baseball, basketball and football  on television, clothing, the Internet and in stores, day-to-day life and even in church. This is an outward appearance and manifestation of what our society values. It is how we spend our money. It is the unequivocal vote of importance.
        </p>
        <h3>
         The Future of Pornography &amp; Sex Trafficking
        </h3>
        <p>
         What should concern us the most about this $14 billion spent on pornography is that the majority of males consuming online pornography are between the ages of 11-15. These boys are not paying for pornography, today, but they will pay for it tomorrow. These are future customers. If the majority of online porn users are not contributing to that $14 billion dollar figure, this number will only increase in the future.
        </p>
        <p>
         Pornography is a part of the sex trade. Unlike professional baseball, basketball and football, porn is a business that the public does not see. You are not going to find an isle display at your local grocery store promoting it, but it is a business  a $14 billion business. And like prostitution, pornography is a part of the sex trafficking problem.
        </p>
        <p>
         Often times women in pornography are manipulated and coerced into the pornography business, and in some cases, are trafficking victims. No young women, full of confidence and freewill, would intelligently choose the pornography business. It is both unhealthy and degrading. Just ask
         <a href="http://churchm.ag/truth-pornography-video/" target="_blank">
          Dr. Shelley Lubben
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         As the pornography business continues to inflate and the demand for women rises, the battle against sex trafficking is only going to become more difficult. This business, this machine, is gaining power and the only way we are going to stop it, is by cutting it off at the source. We need to take a strong stand against pornography. Stand against counselors who recommend pornography to couples, Internet without pornography filters in the home and against indulging in it ourselves.
        </p>
