           <p>
            Recently, I have been struggling. My fight against
            <a href="http://www.stayathomemum.com.au/my-lifestyle/health/mums-on-drugs/" title="Mums on Drugs">
             depression
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.stayathomemum.com.au/my-lifestyle/health/anxiety-in-adults/" title="Anxiety in Adults">
             anxiety
            </a>
            have been well-documented, I am open about the dark side I occasionally live on, but this is different. Lately I have been battling against a different kind of uncontrollable feeling, one that grips me down in my stomach and squeezes until the twisted rage makes it way out of my mouth, directed head on at those who have unwillingly been the source of my red-hot anger; my family.
           </p>
           <div class="yarpp-related">
            <div>
             <strong>
              SEE ALSO:
             </strong>
             <a href="https://www.stayathomemum.com.au/my-kids/my-birth-story/doula-from-hell/" rel="bookmark">
              Doula From Hell
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            I knew it was time to go and see the doctor about a possible change in meds when I called my son an idiot last week. To his face. He had left his dirty washing on the laundry floor RIGHT NEXT TO THE LAUNDRY HAMPER for the 400th time and when I asked him to put it where it should go, he actually told me he didnt know where that was! WTF!?! I have been regularly catching myself muttering the phrase dumbarse under my breath more than a few times in the last few months, but when I actually told my 10 year old that he must be some kind of (expletive) idiot, I knew the straw had broken the camels back, BAD! I know its not PND, Ive been through that before and this is definitely different. Im not depressed, Im not anxious, Im not sad, I am just REALLY REALLY pissed off. all the freakin time!
           </p>
           <div class="hidden-lg hidden-md sahm-contains-ad" style="padding-top: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;">
            <div class="ad_category_atf sahm-ad">
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            I hate the way I feel, so clenched and stressed and angry for the majority of my day. The morning rush hour is peppered with yelling and snide comments said under my breath as my children argue and physically fight, whinge, ask stupid questions, do stupid stuff and basicallyannoy the crap out of me as they prepare to leave the house. I feel as though they are doing it on purpose, that they plot their behaviour with intent to make Mummy go completely batshit cray-cray before the morning is through. That they are old enough to know better, to do better. I hate it. I hate that I yell so much before they leave me for the day. I hate that all my intentions of being a better Mum today were spoilt the moment they barged into the toilet first thing and whacked me in the head with the door and I just want to yell at them to F*%k the f&amp;$k off!. I hate that they cant decide who is taking which drink bottle, who wants what for lunch, who is having the last LCM/oreo packet/banana. I hate that I have 5 people to get out that front door every morning and no-one listens to a word I say. But most of all, I hate that I love it when they are gone.
            <img alt="Not Quite Completely Cuckoo" class="aligncenter wp-image-67345 size-medium" height="300" src="//www.stayathomemum.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Not-Quite-Completely-Cuckoo-NF-201x300.jpg" width="201"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            My husband knew there was something wrong, he had been asking me for a while if everything was OK and I always said, Yep, Im fine, because I just couldnt put my finger on what is exactly wrong. Weve just moved from the city to a smaller town, Ive got four kids and I work basically full time from home. Chuck in all the other usual life stuff and things can get pretty hectic, but its not the organised chaos that knots me up inside. The kids shit me, there are a lot of them and I am significantly outnumbered on any given day. But its not normal to be so angry that you use the hot water of your morning shower to hide your tears when you have already broken your promise to yourself to be a better Mum today, to be more patient, to cuddle more and yell less. Its not normal to be furious at your husband when he unexpectedly has to work late and I miss out on my evening run, (my equivalent of me-time and meditation) or to throw the clothes/shoes/wet towels/hair accessory/ipod at your child, no matter how many times you have asked them to pick it up. Its not normal to start to hate yourself for being so unreasonably irate, which just compounds the anger and things start to spiral out of control.
           </p>
           <p>
            I was scared to tell my husband I wasnt coping, because I didnt want to be a less than perfect parent. I have 4 kids, I should have this down pat. Im not textbook depressed or psychotic or having a nervous breakdown, I feel like Im not even getting crazy right! When he asked what he could do to help, I told him to leave me alone and keep the kids away from me to give me some time to just calm down, but when he did that, I accused him of ignoring me and keeping the kids away from me as a measure for their safety. Poor bugger cant catch a break! I opened up to a friend about my unexplained anger and feelings of tension, she told me she had been through it too. Apparently Im supposed to recognise that a situation is pissing me off and verbalise it. Literally say I am feeling really pissed off right now. I tried it, in the middle of Coles when one child had pushed the other ones head into a freezer, one was screaming in the trolley and another one had accidentally opened a packet of skittles all over the floor. It didnt work, and I must have looked really insane as the lady working in the aisle gently suggested I pop into the bottle shop on my way through the checkout.
           </p>
           <div class="yarpp-related">
            <div>
             <strong>
              SEE ALSO:
             </strong>
             <a href="https://www.stayathomemum.com.au/guest-blogs/guests/cope-losing-best-friend/" rel="bookmark">
              How To Cope With Losing Your Best Friend
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            So as my long-suffering doctor has switched me from little round white pills to large square pink pills, I cant help thinking this must be a somewhat common problem amongst mothers/women/librans/runners/writers/chocolate lovers/rockmelon haters out there. He did not seem too surprised by my revelation, or the fact that I didnt seem to be able to fit into any of the boxes of medical diagnosis for mental health. He recognised I was upset that Im not being the best mother I can be, that I love my kids and that I need a little help.
           </p>
           <p>
            So maybe Im not alone in this battle against uncontrollable irritability? Maybe Im not the only one who feels the tightness in her shoulders, the stretch of a vexed forehead or the pinch of fingernails digging into clenched fists? Maybe, just maybe, I might not be the only one who doesnt quite meet the criteria for a specific problem, who hasnt quite met the mark for a certain label? Who is almost, but not quite, completely cuckoo?
           </p>
