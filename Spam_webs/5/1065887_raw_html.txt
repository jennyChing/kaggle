       <h1>
        Big city life: street art gives Rome a new face
       </h1>
       <div class="content">
        <p>
         <img alt="big city life, tor marancia, ltvs, lancia trendvisions" src="/images//articoli/big-city-life-tor-marancia/ltvs-bigcitylife-01.jpg" style="width: 650px; height: 441px;"/>
         <br/>
         <small>
          Diamond (IT)  Hic Sunt Adamantes, 2015
         </small>
        </p>
        <p>
         Who says that art and the public can't collaborate? "Big City Life" is a prime example. A participatory street art project that transformed 11 buildings of the Marancia district in Rome into monumental murals. How? By connecting its 500 plus inhabitants with local schools, associations and  obviously  artists, coming from 10 different countries.
        </p>
        <p>
         The works created by each artist are the result of this exchange between the public and the authors: twenty monumental wall paintings that occupy the facades of eleven buildings of the neighborhood of via di Tor Marancia 63.
        </p>
        <p>
         Conceived by
         <a href="http://www.999gallery.com" target='\"_blank\"'>
          999Contemporary
         </a>
         , organized by Francesca Mezzano and curated by Stefano S. Antonelli and Gianluca Marziani, "Big City Life" had the support of Roma Capitale Division of Culture, Creativity, artistic Promotion and Tourism, and by Foundation Roma-Arte-Musei.
        </p>
        <p>
         As has occurred in many other European cities, "Big City Life" aims at encouraging tourism to lesser known areas so as to offer visitors testimonies of contemporary art and not just Rome's archeological and architectural heritage.
        </p>
        <p>
         Urban art is here not just a decisive agent for redevelopment, but also an element that strengthens the social and cultural fabric, combining all the energies of the city.
        </p>
