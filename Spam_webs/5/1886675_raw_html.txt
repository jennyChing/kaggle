    <h1>
     How Long Does It Take To Install Solar Panels?
    </h1>
    <br/>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Youll be happy to know that the answer to how long does it take to install solar panels? isnt how long is a piece of string? It is possible to estimate the time it takes to get your solar system up and running.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Simple solar installations: short and sweet
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="Once youve &lt;a href=" http:=""/>
     chosen your solar system and hired an installer, getting the panels on your roof is relatively fast and seamless." height="304" src="http://www.3quotes.com.au/content/images/solar-installations.jpg" style="margin: 10px; float: right;" width="456" /&gt;
    </p>
    <p>
     Once youve chosen your solar system and hired an installer, getting the panels on your roof is relatively fast and seamless.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Most installers will take anywhere from a few hours to two days to get the system installed and connected. Then you can start producing power using sunlight almost immediately.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     That being said, a solar installation isnt so simple that you can do it yourself. Placing the panels properly and connecting the system is complex and can be dangerous work. So stay off the roof and hire an expert.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Start with a quote
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     But before the installation, you need to shop around for the right solar panels for your needs. This depends on where you live, your roof orientation, budget, household size and future needs. Your
     <a href="http://www.3quotes.com.au/solar-power/how-to-choose-the-best-solar-installer.html">
      solar installer will help you
     </a>
     determine this during a consultation.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Getting a solar quote is quick. Simply fill in the form and send it off. Solar installers will then get back to you with their recommendations, rates and how long it will typically take to install your system.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Investigate solar rebates
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     In most areas of Australia, households qualify for rebates, incentives or credits towards the cost of a solar system. It can take time to apply for and receive these incentives, as well as getting a net metering system set up. Your installer can help speed up this process.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Solar planning and consultation
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Many solar installers will come and assess your property. That way, they can measure the roof space and determine the type and size of solar panels best for you. This consultation takes no longer than a few hours for most urban installations; though a large-scale system may require more time.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      More complicated installations
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     While most installations are done in a day or two, how long it takes does depend on the system size. Your roof may also be shown to be unsuitable for solar. In this instance, installation can be delayed while you get the repairs or replacement taken care of. Trees may also need to be cut back to allow for maximum sunlight penetration.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Depending on where you live, you may also need council approval or a building permit. Relying on local government for these can take time and hold up the installation process. Your installer should be up to speed on the rules and regulations in your neck of the woods.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Bear in mind, too, that if your roof is hard to access or has little available space, the installation may take up to a week to complete.
    </p>
