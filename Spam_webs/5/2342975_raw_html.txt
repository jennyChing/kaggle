          <p>
           Injury Lawsuits Explained
          </p>
          <p>
           Most modern countries have two different types of law, criminal and civil. Criminal law punishes those who have intentionally fractured a statute, while civil law compensates those who have suffered a loss at the hands of another. Criminal and civil law can even intersect in some instances, depending upon the crime that was committed. For example, anyone who has been involved in a DUI accident will face criminal charges for the act and they may be required to pay the hospital bills of the victims. The victims may have to seek damages for the medical bills in an injury lawsuit. On the job injuries in Australia result in claims that are handled by the likes of a
           <a href="http://www.richardsonandlyons.com.au/">
            brisbane workers compensation lawyer
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/gO40OmnL9w0" width="560">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
           What are Injury Lawsuits
          </p>
          <p>
           Injury lawsuits are suits that are filed by someone who has been injured by the negligent actions of others. This doesnt have to be the result of an auto injury, but car collisions are a common cause. Injury lawsuits dont even have to be filed against another person. Sometimes a company or corporation is liable for damages to the employees. Anyone who is injured on the job is entitled to certain compensation to cover the medical expenses. A company can also be named in an injury suit if a consumer is injured by a faulty product. This is the reason for all the bright warning stickers on lawn mowers and other products so the company can assume no liability in the event of an injury.
          </p>
          <p>
           Class-Action Injury Suits
          </p>
          <p>
           Sometimes multiple beneficiaries can be named in a single suit if they were all injured by a single plaintiff. These are known as class action lawsuits and they can name thousands of different beneficiaries in some cases. Some of the better known class action injury suits involve former workers of the construction trade. There was a time when the Carcinogenic element asbestos was regularly used in the construction trade. Many of the former construction workers were stricken with a rare form of lung cancer known as mesothelioma. Some of these suits have resulted in more than $500,000 for the victims and families of those who worked in the construction trade.
           <a href="http://www.dundaslawyers.com.au/">
            Brisbane lawyers
           </a>
           can help with situations like these.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.qls.com.au/Knowledge_centre/Areas_of_law/Personal_injuries_law" rel="nofollow">
            <img alt="injury law" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-90" height="480" src="http://www.greatmanagement.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/injury-law-640x480.jpg" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Filing an Injury Lawsuit
          </p>
          <p>
           Theres no requirement to file a lawsuit, but its best to leave it to a skilled lawyer. There are a lot of motions and papers which must be filed, and only someone who has passed the bar can ensure everything is filed properly. There is also the act of examining and cross examining witnesses which only a good lawyer will know how to do. Since there are potentially millions of dollars at stake, its best not to leave things to chance. A skilled lawyer will charge more money but he or she typically gets better results. There are also a lot of politics involved in these types of suits and its best to choose an attorney who knows all of the local judges.
          </p>
          <p>
           The only time you dont need a lawyer is when the suit is being settled in small claims court. The settlement are too small to involve attorneys, and the judgements never go to juries. However, its still necessary to gather evidence and build a strong case.
          </p>
