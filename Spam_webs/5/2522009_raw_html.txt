     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        The Greatest Event in Television History
       </b>
      </i>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Mockumentary" title="Mockumentary">
       mockumentary
      </a>
      television series co-created by
      <a href="/wiki/Adam_Scott_(actor)" title="Adam Scott (actor)">
       Adam Scott
      </a>
      and his wife Naomi Sablan. The series premiered on
      <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">
       Cartoon Network
      </a>
      's late night programming block
      <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">
       Adult Swim
      </a>
      on October 12, 2012 and ended on January 23, 2014 with a total of four episodes. Each episode originally aired independently as a
      <a href="/wiki/Television_special" title="Television special">
       special presentation
      </a>
      , with several months between the broadcast of each episode.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Format">
       Format
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Greatest_Event_in_Television_History&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Format">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The format of each special is a brief
      <a href="/wiki/Mockumentary" title="Mockumentary">
       mockumentary
      </a>
      about the shot-for-shot remake of a 1980s TV series
      <a href="/wiki/Title_sequence#Television" title="Title sequence">
       opening credits sequence
      </a>
      , followed by the remake itself. Series cocreator Adam Scott says the specials are at least in part motivated by his own memories of watching '80s TV as a kid.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-splitsider1_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-splitsider1-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The overall program is hosted by
      <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Probst" title="Jeff Probst">
       Jeff Probst
      </a>
      with other actors portraying fictional versions of themselves. After each episode, the original opening credit scene is shown.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Greatest_Event_in_Television_History&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Four episodes had were produced during the show's run; the
      <a href="/wiki/Series_finale" title="Series finale">
       series finale
      </a>
      aired on January 23, 2014. In an interview with
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Awl" title="The Awl">
        Splitsider
       </a>
      </i>
      , Scott explained the conclusion of the series: "It's a lot of work for such a short, stupid thing... they're really fun and they're fun to make, but we're ready to move on."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-splitsider2_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-splitsider2-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
