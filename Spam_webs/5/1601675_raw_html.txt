      <article class="post-457 post type-post status-publish format-standard category-blog-party category-decorating category-holidays category-way-back-when entry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Christmas topiary
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2009-12-08T02:55:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          December 8, 2009
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.fingerprintsonthefridge.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Rachelle
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         When I saw this photo over at
         <a href="http://www.bhg.com/holidays/christmas/crafts/glass-ornament-topiary/">
          BHG
         </a>
         I knew I had to have it. No doubt, it had to be mine. I do love the colors but they wouldnt go with much in my house at the moment, so I had to improvise.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5412678270418054674" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_D75xk-TDsc8/Sx2yNZe-bhI/AAAAAAAABLA/lpT-rFFoBY4/s400/207.JPG" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; width: 400px; cursor: pointer; height: 300px; text-align: center;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I made a ton of these Santa Jars last year to give Oreo truffles in so I just made another big ol one.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_D75xk-TDsc8/Sx2yL86Ro1I/AAAAAAAABKo/TRHC7GtmpbI/s1600-h/196.JPG">
          <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5412678245568062290" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_D75xk-TDsc8/Sx2yL86Ro1I/AAAAAAAABKo/TRHC7GtmpbI/s400/196.JPG" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; width: 400px; cursor: pointer; height: 300px; text-align: center;"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         You cant really mess this up, red body, white paint for fur, black belt and a silver buckle, last year the buckles were gold. This year, gold was not in my stash, which actually worked out because I used red and silver ornaments.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5412678253564887506" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_D75xk-TDsc8/Sx2yMas3gdI/AAAAAAAABKw/0O_7JYjpXY4/s400/199.JPG" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; width: 400px; cursor: pointer; height: 300px; text-align: center;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         Fate I tell you. ;)
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now take your foam ball and heat up your glue gun. Put a rim of glue around each neck of the ornaments then stick them in place. I used mini ornaments to fill in the gaps.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5412683552356747026" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_D75xk-TDsc8/Sx23A2PSxxI/AAAAAAAABLI/KoTqXXqX7uo/s400/408.JPG" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; width: 400px; height: 300px; text-align: center;"/>
         <br/>
         <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5412683563321995090" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_D75xk-TDsc8/Sx23BfFnL1I/AAAAAAAABLQ/8lfkkohqF3Q/s400/410.JPG" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; width: 300px; height: 400px; text-align: center;"/>
        </p>
        <div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div align="center">
         <span style="font-size:85%;">
          (Yes, I do my best work on the kitchen floor.)
         </span>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Looking back on this project I would most definitely spray paint the ball first, you can still kind of see green, oh well live and learn.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5412683571020077282" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_D75xk-TDsc8/Sx23B7w-mOI/AAAAAAAABLY/aMMgogyU0PE/s400/201.JPG" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; width: 300px; height: 400px; text-align: center;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Glue the top and bottom of the dowel and stick it in the foam ball and the foam blocks and stick it in your pot. Throw some left over fake snow from your
         <a href="http://lovinmylovebugs.blogspot.com/2009/12/how-to-decorate-faux-mantel.html">
          mantel project
         </a>
         in the pot to hide the the dusty devil green floral foam. Done and done! See how easy that was?
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5412678265265511250" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_D75xk-TDsc8/Sx2yNGSg21I/AAAAAAAABK4/DTGxw-wc4c8/s400/205.JPG" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; width: 226px; cursor: pointer; height: 400px; text-align: center;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The most tedious part was filling in the gaps with the minis, and painting a straight line for the belt when all I had was a large foam brush. For now, Ill live with a little smudginess. Ohhh, the things you do to insure you finish a project before DIY day. ;)
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
