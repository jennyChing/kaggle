        <div class="artist-blurb">
         <h2>
          Biography
         </h2>
         <div class="blurb">
          <p>
           Known for her incisively political collage and photomontage works,
           <a href="/gene/dada">
            Dada
           </a>
           artist Hannah Hch appropriated and rearranged images and text from the mass media to critique the failings of the Weimar German Government. Hch drew inspiration from the collage work of
           <a href="/artist/pablo-picasso">
            Pablo Picasso
           </a>
           and fellow Dada exponent
           <a href="/artist/kurt-schwitters">
            Kurt Schwitters
           </a>
           , and her own compositions share with those artists a similarly dynamic and layered style. Hch preferred metaphoric imagery to the more direct, text-based confrontational approach of her contemporary
           <a href="/artist/john-heartfield">
            John Heartfield
           </a>
           , whose work she found tendentious. She rejected the German government, but often focused her criticism more narrowly on gender issues, and is recognized as a pioneering feminist artist for works such as
           <a href="/artwork/hannah-hoch-das-schone-madchen-the-beautiful-girl">
            <em>
             Das schne Mdchen (The Beautiful Girl)
            </em>
           </a>
           , (1920), an evocative visual reaction to the birth of industrial advertising and ideals of beauty it furthered. Hch was, for a period of time, the partner of Dada artist Raoul Haussman.
          </p>
         </div>
