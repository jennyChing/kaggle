         <p>
          <img align="none" alt="Reasons to Get in Shape #2" border="0" height="231" hspace="10" src="http://cdn.builtlean.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/reasons-to-get-in-shape-1.jpg" title="Reasons to Get in Shape #1" width="515"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          I compiled a list of what I believe are the 31 most important reasons why you should get in shape, or stay in shape.  What getting in shape means to me is you have a baseline of (1) strength, (2) muscular endurance,  (3) cardiovascular capacity, and (4) flexibility to lead a fuller, healthier life.  If you are weak in one of these 4 areas, then you have a gap to fill my friend!
         </p>
         <p>
          Next time youre thinking of skipping exercise, or youre contemplating getting in shape (see
          <a href=" http://www.builtlean.com/2010/06/01/5-stages-of-change-model-which-stage-are-you-in/">
           5 Stages of Change Model
          </a>
          ), you can use this list as a motivational reference.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you have any friends who are not exercising, definitely shoot them this post!
         </p>
         <p>
          So here are 31 reasons to get, or stay in great shape:
         </p>
         <h3>
          1. Reduces Cholesterol
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-1" id="fnref-1718-1" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            1
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Regular exercise has been proven to reduce bad cholesterol levels (LDL) and increase good cholesterol (HDL)
         </p>
         <h3>
          2. Sleep Better
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-2" id="fnref-1718-2" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            2
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Exercise can help you sleep better.  People who exercise tend to fall asleep quicker and stay asleep longer.
         </p>
         <h3>
          3. Improves Self Esteem
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-3" id="fnref-1718-3" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            3
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Regardless of your body appearance, regular fitness helps improve your self-esteem.
         </p>
         <h3>
          4. Reduces Blood Pressure
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-4" id="fnref-1718-4" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            4
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Exercise not only reduces high blood pressure, it helps prevent it.
         </p>
         <h3>
          5. Decreases Back Pain
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-5" id="fnref-1718-5" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            5
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          By increasing muscle strength, endurance, and improving flexibility and posture, regular exercise helps to prevent back pain.  Studies show that exercise is an effective treatment for recurrent low back pain.
         </p>
         <h3>
          6. Reduces Risk of Injury
         </h3>
         <p>
          If you have a strong, fit body, the chances of injury significantly decrease.
         </p>
         <h3>
          7. Prevents Certain Cancers
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-6" id="fnref-1718-6" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            6
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Some studies show that exercising regularly can help decrease colon cancer risk by 40% and may help reduce the risk of breast cancer as well.
         </p>
         <h3>
          8. Increases Metabolic Rate
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-7" id="fnref-1718-7" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            7
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Exercise not only will increase the total number of calories that you burn, but also can increase your resting metabolic rate, so you burn more calories while at rest.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img align="right" alt="Reasons to Get in Shape #2" border="0" height="244" hspace="10" src="http://cdn.builtlean.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/reasons-to-get-in-shape-2.jpg" title="Reasons to Get in Shape #2" width="325"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          9. Increases Range of Motion
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-8" id="fnref-1718-8" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            8
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          An increase in flexibility can decreases stiffness in joints, and can decrease pain and inflammation associated with arthritis.
         </p>
         <h3>
          10. Increases Functional Strength
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-9" id="fnref-1718-9" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            9
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          From getting out of a chair, to lifting an object off the ground, our bodies are able to perform daily activities better.
         </p>
         <h3>
          11. Increases Insulin Sensitivity
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-10" id="fnref-1718-10" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            10
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Not only does getting in shape help increase insulin sensitivity (ability of muscles to uptake glucose), but helps prevent type 2 diabetes.
         </p>
         <h3>
          12. Improves Sex Drive
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-11" id="fnref-1718-11" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            11
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Exercise increases circulation, which helps prevent erectile dysfunction and impotence.  Exercise can increase sex hormones like testosterone.
         </p>
         <h3>
          13. Reduces Risk of Heart Disease
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-12" id="fnref-1718-12" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            12
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Regular exercise strengthens the heart and improves contractile function
         </p>
         <h3>
          14. Exercise is Fun and Enjoyable
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-13" id="fnref-1718-13" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            13
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Yep, its really true.  There are so many ways to move that there is bound to be one you enjoy.  Try making exercise more competitive, or social if you need extra motivation.  The hard part of course is getting started.
         </p>
         <h3>
          15. Exercise Prolongs Life
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-14" id="fnref-1718-14" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            14
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          People who exercise regularly tend to live longer.
         </p>
         <h3>
          16. Improves Balance and Coordination
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-15" id="fnref-1718-15" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            15
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Exercise can improve  your stability and also what is called your kinesthetic awareness.  You have a better feel of where you are spatially, so you dont knock into things at the grocery store by mistake!
         </p>
         <h3>
          17. Reduces Anxiety and Depression
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-16" id="fnref-1718-16" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            16
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Exercise is a great mood elevator to help you manage stress and reduce anxiety.  The antidepressant effect of regular physical exercise is comparable to the potent antidepressants like Sertraline.
         </p>
         <h3>
          18. Gastrointestinal Tract Benefits
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-17" id="fnref-1718-17" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            17
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Exercise is beneficial for persons suffering from cholelithiasis and constipation. Physical activity may reduce the risk of diverticulosis, gastrointestinal haemorrhage, and inflammatory bowel disease.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img align="right" alt="Reasons to Get in Shape #3" border="0" height="375" hspace="10" src="http://cdn.builtlean.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/reasons-to-get-in-shape-3.jpg" title="Reasons to Get in Shape #3" width="250"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          19. Weight Control
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-18" id="fnref-1718-18" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            18
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          While some research shows exercise increases appetite while others shows exercises suppresses appetite, one thing is for sure: regular exercise helps control weight.
         </p>
         <h3>
          20. Improves Fat Utilization
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-19" id="fnref-1718-19" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            19
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Improves your bodys ability to use fat for energy during physical activity.  Our bodies use glycogen and fat as stored energy to help sustain us.
         </p>
         <h3>
          21. Strengthens Immune System
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-20" id="fnref-1718-20" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            20
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Just about any doctor will tell you no pill or nutritional supplement has the power of near daily moderate activity in lowering the number of sick days people take.
         </p>
         <h3>
          22. Strengthens Your Bones
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-21" id="fnref-1718-21" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            21
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Lifting weights helps prevent osteoporosis, which is so important because a shocking 1 out of 2 women will be diagnosed with osteoporosis.
         </p>
         <h3>
          23. Improves Focus
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-22" id="fnref-1718-22" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            22
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Exercise helps improve your brains ability to concentrate, remember, visualize, plan ahead, and solve problems because a fitter body will supply a more rich amount of oxygen to your brain.
         </p>
         <h3>
          24. Reduces Aches &amp; Pains
         </h3>
         <p>
          Improves your bodys ability to sit still and be comfortable for long periods of time. Keeping your muscles toned and strong and your joints flexible will prevent aches and pains from distracting you and keeping you from concentrating.  If you have an office job, exercise is a necessity!
         </p>
         <h3>
          25. Lose the Hidden Fat
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-23" id="fnref-1718-23" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            23
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          But just like the fat that leads to belly rolls and love handles, internal fat  called visceral fat  also can be prevented and eradicated with regular exercise.
         </p>
         <h3>
          26. Improves Energy Levels
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-24" id="fnref-1718-24" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            24
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Research suggests regular exercise can increase energy levels, even among people suffering from chronic medical conditions associated with fatigue, like cancer and heart disease.  One study in 2006 showed the average effect of regular exercise was greater than the improvement from using stimulant medications, such as those that treat ADHD and narcolepsy.  Forget the cup of coffee, or energy drink, and go for a brisk walk, or workout!
         </p>
         <h3>
          27. Prevents Alzheimers
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-25" id="fnref-1718-25" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            25
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          The latest research indicates exercise can keep the brain sharp into old age and might help prevent Alzheimers disease along with other mental disorders that accompany aging.
         </p>
         <h3>
          28. Improves Confidence
          <sup class="footnote">
           <a href="#fn-1718-26" id="fnref-1718-26" onclick="return fdfootnote_show(1718)">
            26
           </a>
          </sup>
         </h3>
         <p>
          While appearance certainly isnt everything, getting in better shape can help you look a lot better, and improve your confidence.  From the appearance of your skin, to your overall body shape, just about every physical attribute can improve when youre in shape.
         </p>
         <h3>
          29. Reach Your Potential
         </h3>
         <p>
          Youre not at your best if youre flabby and out of shape.  Simply put, when youre in great shape, youre a better version of YOU, and this physical transformation can also help improve your mental, emotional, and spiritual state.
         </p>
         <h3>
          30. Because you can commit a couple hours out of your 168 hour week to get all these benefits!
         </h3>
         <h3>
          31. Because I just listed 30 compelling reasons to exercise and get in shape.
         </h3>
         <p>
          So there you have it,  a solid 31 reasons to get in shape and stay motivated to exercise.
         </p>
         <p>
          Did I not include any benefits of staying in shape that you think are important?
         </p>
         <p>
          Are there any reasons that are particularly motivational for you?
         </p>
         <p>
          Please leave a comment below and share your opinion.
         </p>

               <p>
                @Wendy  The easiest way to get started is simply to commit to something that you can reasonably achieve.  Lets say you start walking for 20 minutes 2, or 3 days per week.  Over time, you can increase the pace of your walk to a jog, or find more activities like a workout class, or running group.
               </p>
               <p>
                It is VERY easy to get overwhelmed by the prospect of getting in shape, because its a process that takes time and effort.  When I first started this website for example, I was overwhelmed with everything I needed to do; write articles, create and edit videos, get a logo, create a web design, organize the information, create a product, find systems to sell the product, choose an email software provider etc. just to name a few.
               </p>
               <p>
                Instead of getting overwhelmed, I realized that accomplishing my goal of creating this website would take time.  I ended up writing 1-2 articles per week, and created a list of things I would need to do over the next several months.  Over time, I crossed off everything on my list.  The point Im trying to make is dont think about the totality of changing your body, but break it down into small steps.  Then over time, you will make positive changes that you can build on.  Just committing to walking a few times per week to clear your head and get all the benefits in this article is well worth it.  From there, as you get in better shape, you can join a workout class, or other activity.
               </p>
