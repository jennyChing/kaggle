          When I was in the fourth grade I moved to a small town in the upper peninsula of Michigan. Yoopers! My family was low on cash, so the six of us stayed in someones living room. I wore the same outfit everyday to school.
         </p>
         <p>
          By January my shirt had wear marks in it on the elbows, and my jeans were heavily patched. It was hard for me to make friends. The other kids did not understand that this wasn't a fashion choice. I didn't have any other clothes. During recess I was picked on a lot. Instead of dealing with the taunts I hide in the library. I wasn't supposed to but the teachers let it slide. They understood my position.
         </p>
         <p>
          During one of my hide away recesses I was reading Harry Potter under a desk. This girl came up to me and asked me why I was hiding. I told her and we talked for the rest of the period.
         </p>
         <p>
          It became a regular thing. We would hide under the desk together and talk for 45 minutes. She wouldn't let me sit with her at lunch but I understood. I was the dirty poor kid, hanging out with me was a reputation killer. A week before school let out she invited over to her house for a sleep over. I thought that it was weird because she was a girl, but I said yes. I didn't have pajamas. I remember really freaking out about that.
         </p>
         <p>
          Her mother picked me up in the nicest car I had ever ridden in. She put a towel down for me to sit on before I got in. That hurt.
         </p>

          Growing up
         </h5>
         <h5 class="divider-line">
          and
         </h5>
         <h5>
          moving on
         </h5>
         <div class="clearfix">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="clearfix">
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="row">
       <div class="col-md-10 col-md-offset-1 post-content-page-wrapper">
        <div class="post-content-page" id="">
         hotel, there was only one in town. He said that I should have called him. I agreed to stay at his house. Emily had crossed my mind over, and over again through the years, but I didn't think of her being there.
         <p>
          She was. I took my boots off, and sat there for a moment remembering when her mom tossed my socks out. War had taken something from me; it felt good to be in the place of a positive memory. I went up the stairs two at a time with my pack.
         </p>
         <p>
          Emily was standing in the hallway brushing her teeth. She had this pink highlight in her hair. She asked me if it looked stupid, I told her that it reminded me of Willy Wonka. It felt good to share a laugh.
         </p>
         <p>
          We sat in her room listening to music, and talking for almost six hours. She was on the deans list. I was doing push ups for the G I Bill. She missed me. I missed her. She was single. I was too.
         </p>
         <p>
          For the rest of my time on leave it was like freshman year in high school. Is it weird that I remember exactly what her breath smelled like? I do. It seemed important at the time that I did not forget it.
         </p>
         <p>
          I had to go, she had to go. We stayed in touch, but focused on making something of our lives. I flew out to her as often as I could. She did the same. God, we had some good, good times.
         </p>
         <p>
          She died last summer. A drunk driver hit her. I have never missed anyone more.
         </p>

   Handlebars.registerHelper('ifEqual', function(v1, v2, options) {
		if(v1 === v2) {
			return options.fn(this);
		}
		return options.inverse(this);
	});
	Handlebars.registerHelper('contains', function(v1, v2, options) {
		if ( v1 instanceof Array &amp;&amp; v1.indexOf(v2) &gt; -1 ) {
			return options.fn(this);
		}
	});
	Handlebars.registerHelper('isEditable', function(created_date, options) {
		var created = new Date(created_date);
		var now = new Date();
		if ( now - created &lt; 259200000 ) {
			return options.fn(this);
		}
	});
	Handlebars.registerHelper('formatDate', function(date) {
		return moment(date).fromNow();
	});

	//Note, we should refactor below once the messaging system is out.
	//Below is from https://gist.github.com/arbales/1654670
	var ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION, LINK_DETECTION_REGEX, MARKSAFE_FUNCTION, emojify, formatTextForHTML, linkify;

	//below regex is from: http://stackoverflow.com/questions/6927719/url-regex-does-not-work-in-javascript
	LINK_DETECTION_REGEX = /(([a-z]+:\/\/)?(([a-z0-9\-]+\.)+([a-z]{2}|aero|arpa|biz|com|coop|edu|gov|info|int|jobs|mil|museum|name|nato|net|org|pro|travel|local|internal))(:[0-9]{1,5})?(\/[a-z0-9_\-\.~]+)*(\/([a-z0-9_\-\.]*)(\?[a-z0-9+_\-\.%=&amp;]*)?)?(#[a-zA-Z0-9!$&amp;'()*+.=-_~:@/?]*)?)(\s+|$)/gi;

	ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION = Handlebars.Utils.escapeExpression;

	MARKSAFE_FUNCTION = function(str) {
	  return new Handlebars.SafeString(str);
	};
	linkify = function(safeContent) {
	  return safeContent.replace(LINK_DETECTION_REGEX, function(url) {
	  	brEnd = false;
	  	if(url.indexOf("\n")) {
	  		brEnd = true;
	  	}
	  	url = url.trim();
	    url = url.replace(/(?:\r\n|\r|\n)/g, '');
	    var address;
	    address = /[a-z]+:\/\//.test(url) ? url : "http://" + url;    
	    url = url.replace(/^https?:\/\//, '');
	    link = "&lt;a href='" + address + "' target='_blank' rel='nofollow'&gt;" + url + "&lt;/a&gt;";
	    if(brEnd) {
	    	return link + '&lt;br/&gt;';
	    } else {
	    	return link;
	    }
	  });
	};

	formatTextForHTML = function(content) {
	  var safeContent;
	  safeContent = ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION(content);
	  safeContent = linkify(safeContent);
	  safeContent = safeContent.replace(/(?:\r\n|\r|\n)/g, '&lt;br /&gt;');
	  //safeContent = emojify(safeContent);
	  return MARKSAFE_FUNCTION(safeContent);
	};

	Handlebars.registerHelper('formatTextForHTML', formatTextForHTML);
