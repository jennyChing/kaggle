        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Three recipes to convince you to enjoy a summer BBQ the Mexican way
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-08-12T11:55:38+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          12 August, 2014
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/author/emily-leary/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Emily Leary
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/2014/08/mexican-bbq/#comments">
           7 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="7568938" data-link="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/2014/08/mexican-bbq/" data-summary="When you think Mexican food, what situation do you picture?         </div>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso alternative lamb being cut" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22308" height="945" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-alternative-lamb-being-cut.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Oh mummy! Can we EAT that?  JD, 6
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         When you think Mexican food, what situation do you picture? Sure its a great winter dish  a tray of enchiladas is incredible on a cold day, but Mexican food also works GREAT in summer.
        </p>
        <p>
         Today, Im going to show you how to inject someMexican vibrancy into the typically British BBQ and show just how delicious  and social  Mexican food can be.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Mexico meets the gardens of Britain
        </h2>
        <p>
         Its no secret thatus Brits love charring a bit of meat on the barbecue, but you may not knowthat a favourite Mexican dish is Carne Asada, which literally means grilled meat and involves preparing delicious strips of meat, served in tacos or wraps  perfect for summer dining.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, thanks to Old El Pasos extensive range of spices, wraps, salsas, dips and sauces, trying out your own Mexican barbecue is super easy.
        </p>
        <p>
         Old El Paso are truly Mexican food lovers, born out of the town of El Paso  the Mexican food capital of the world, theyvebeen blending the freshest Mexican ingredients, chillies and spices since 1938, to bring an authentic, flavour-filled Mexican food experience to dinner tables the world over.
        </p>
        <p>
         Is your mouth watering yet? Here are three recipe ideas to inspire your imagination, tempt your taste buds and getyou out and cooking in the garden.
        </p>
        <p>
         Presenting:Beef Carne Asada Mexican Salad,Lamb Carne Asada Burrito, andChicken Carne Asada Tacos.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Beef Carne Asada Mexican Salad
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="old-el-paso-salad-lead" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22315" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-salad-lead.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This salad is a perfect sharing dish for an outdoor party. It looks incredibly colourful once its all laid out. The beef is spiced with Old El Paso Smoky BBQ Fajita Seasoning and barbecued until its juicy and delicious, then sliced in classic carne asada style.
        </p>
        <p>
         Beneath the beef its all crunchy salad and zingy lime, while the grilled tortilla wedges are great for scooping up delicious bites. Meanwhile, a homemade chipotle dressing gives everything a fiery extra kick.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Ingredients
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          2 tbsp runny honey
         </li>
         <li>
          2 tbspred wine vinegar
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tbsp chipotle paste
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tbsp tomato puree
         </li>
         <li>
          3 tbsp olive oil
         </li>
         <li>
          salt and pepper to taste
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 bottle of Old El Paso Guacamole
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 bottle of Old El Paso Sour cream
         </li>
         <li>
          Approx 500g long strip of beef  skirt steak is ideal
         </li>
         <li>
          3 tbsp oil
         </li>
         <li>
          1 packet of Old El PasoSmoky BBQ Fajita Seasoning
         </li>
         <li>
          200g iceberg lettuce
         </li>
         <li>
          1 red pepper
         </li>
         <li>
          1 green pepper
         </li>
         <li>
          1 yellow pepper
         </li>
         <li>
          1 corn on the cob
         </li>
         <li>
          1 large ripe avocado
         </li>
         <li>
          3 salad tomatoes
         </li>
         <li>
          175g pinto beans (300g tin drained and rinsed)
         </li>
         <li>
          2 small limes
         </li>
         <li>
          1 jar Old El Paso Sliced Green Jalapeos
         </li>
         <li>
          1 pack OldElPaso Regular Super Soft Flour Tortillas
         </li>
         <li>
          Sprinkle of paprika
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         Instructions
        </h3>
        <p>
         1.To make a chipotle dressing for your salad, place the honey, red wine vinegar, chipotle paste, tomato puree, olive oil into a blender and whiz until smooth, season to taste, transfer to a jar and keep in the fridge until youre ready for it
        </p>
        <p>
         2.Mix the Old El Paso Smoky BBQ Fajita Seasoning with the oil in a shallow bowl, add the meat and rub all over
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Cover the bowl with cling film and place in the fridge to marinade for at least a couple of hours
        </p>
        <p>
         4. When your barbecue has been lit and the coals are covered with ash, shake off the meat to remove the excess oil and place on the grill
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso beef on the bbq" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22306" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-beef-on-the-bbq.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Turn regularly until cooked through. It should take up to 25 minutesbut it really depends on the temperature youve achieved on your barbecue so go by eye and test with a meat thermometer, if you wish, aiming for 65C/150F for medium well done
        </p>
        <p>
         6. When cooked, transfer the meat to a plate and leave to rest while you prepare the rest of the dish
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Place the corn on the cob on the barbecue and cook for up to 15 minutes, turning regularly until juicy and just beginning to char in small areas all over
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso sweetcorn" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22291" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-sweetcorn.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Set aside to cool, then slice the corn off the cob with a sharp knife
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso sweetcorn being cut" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22293" height="945" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-sweetcorn-being-cut.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         9. In the centre of a large plate/sharing platter, place two dip bowls and fill with the guacamole and sour cream
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso guacamole" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22300" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-guacamole.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         10. Shred the lettuce and use to cover the rest of the plate
        </p>
        <p>
         11. De-seed anddice peppers, then add to the plate, followed by diced avocado, diced salad tomatoes, the pinto beans and the freshly cooked sweetcorn
        </p>
        <p>
         12. Slice the rested beef into thin long strips,and arrange around the salad
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso beef being cut" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22307" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-beef-being-cut.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         13. Top thatwithlime cut into wedges and a few spoonfuls ofOldElPasoSlice Green Jalapeos
        </p>
        <p>
         14. Drizzle all over with the homemade chipotle dressing
        </p>
        <p>
         15. To make the taco chips, cut the OldElPasoTortillas into triangles and grill on the barbecue for a couple of minutes until crisp
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso tacos on the grill" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22289" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-tacos-on-the-grill.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         16. Arrangearound the rim of the platter.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso mexican salad" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22296" height="945" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-mexican-salad.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Doesnt that look inviting? It didnt last long at our house!
        </p>
        <h2>
         Lamb Carne Asada Burrito
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="old-el-paso-burrito-lead" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22318" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-burrito-lead.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Burritos are ideal fodder for when youre outside because theyre wrapped in foil. This recipe takes succulent lamb, marinades it in Old El Paso Fajita Spice and then grills it to perfection before slicing into strips and wrapping in soft flour tortillas with refried beans, spinach, avocado and salsa. Its juicy, zingy and satisfying  perfect for a BBQ the Mexican way.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Ingredients
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Approx 400g boned lamb
         </li>
         <li>
          1 pack Old El Paso Regular Super Soft Flour Tortillas
         </li>
         <li>
          1 packet of Old El Paso Spice Mix for Roasted Tomato &amp; Pepper Fajitas
         </li>
         <li>
          200gbaby spinach
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 can ofOldElPasoRefried Beans
         </li>
         <li>
          1 large ripe avocado
         </li>
         <li>
          OldElPaso Chunky Salsa, Jalapeo Relish or Corn Relish as preferred
         </li>
         <li>
          Foil
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         Instructions
        </h3>
        <p>
         1. Mix the Old El Paso Spice Mix for Roasted Tomato &amp; Pepper Fajitas with the oil in a shallow bowl, add the meat and rub all over
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Cover the bowl with cling film and place in the fridge to marinade for at least a couple of hours
        </p>
        <p>
         3. When your barbecue has been lit and the coals are covered with ash, shake off the meat to remove the excess oil and place on the grill
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso lamb on the bbq" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22297" height="945" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-lamb-on-the-bbq.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Turn regularly until cooked through  it should take up to 25 minutes depending on your barbecue  its as much an art as a science, just be sure its cooked through safely. If you have a meat thermometer, aim for 65C/150F for medium well done.
        </p>
        <p>
         5. When cooked, transfer the meat to a plate and leave to rest while you prepare the rest of the dish
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Warm thetortillas briefly on the barbecueto soften
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso tortillas" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22288" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-tortillas.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Lay out in slightly overlapping pairs to create a longer tortilla. Use a little of the refried beans to bond the two together
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso refried beans" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22295" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-refried-beans.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Spread a spoonful of refried beans across each tortilla pair, coming close to the edges all round
        </p>
        <p>
         9. Put a line of shredded spinachin the middle along the length of each one
        </p>
        <p>
         10. Slice the rested lamb into thin strips and put on top of the spinach
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso lamb being cut" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22298" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-lamb-being-cut.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         11. Top with avocado and your preferred salsa or relish
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso salsa" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22294" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-salsa.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso jalapeno relish" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22299" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-jalapeno-relish.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso corn relish" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22301" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-corn-relish.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         12. Fold the top and bottom of the tortillas over then roll to create a closed burrito
        </p>
        <p>
         13. Wrap each firmly in foil and briefly place back on the barbecue, seam side down to seal
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso burritos on bbq" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22305" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-burritos-on-bbq.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         14. Slice at an angle through the centre to serve
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso burritos-V1" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22310" height="945" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-burritos-V1.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The beauty of popping them back on the grill is that everything is sealed in, so even when you peel back the foil, your burrito stays together, meaning maximum enjoyment, minimum mess.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Chicken Carne Asada Tacos
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="old-el-paso-taco-lead" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22316" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-taco-lead.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         When I think of Mexican food, I think tacos. These soft tacos are filled with salad, guacamole, cheese, chunky salsa and slow barbecued chicken that has been marinated in Old El Paso Spice Mix for Roasted Tomato &amp; Pepper Fajitas. They can be put together all at once, or you can serve the ingredients to the table and let guests build their own. This is a perfect recipe for an informal gathering in the garden with friends.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Ingredients
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Approx 500g boneless chicken fillets
         </li>
         <li>
          1 pack Old El Paso Regular Super Soft Flour Tortillas warmed briefly on the BBQ
         </li>
         <li>
          1 packet of Old El Paso Spice Mix for Roasted Tomato &amp; Pepper Fajitas
         </li>
         <li>
          3 salad tomatoes
         </li>
         <li>
          OldElPasoGuacamole
         </li>
         <li>
          Old El PasoChunky Salsa
         </li>
         <li>
          100giceberg lettuce
         </li>
         <li>
          100g grated mature cheese
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         Instructions
        </h3>
        <p>
         1. Mix the Old El Paso Spice Mix for Roasted Tomato &amp; Pepper Fajitas with the oil in a shallow bowl, add the meat and rub all over
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Cover the bowl with cling film and place in the fridge to marinade for at least a couple of hours
        </p>
        <p>
         3. When your barbecue has been lit and the coals are covered with ash, push some of the coals to one side. This is what allows the chicken to cook more slowly, ensuring its safely cooked through and still succulent and juicy.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso chicken on bbq" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22302" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-chicken-on-bbq.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Shake off the meat to remove the excess oil and place on the grill, to the side of the area you cleared of coals. Turn regularly until cooked through  it should take up to 25 minutes depending on your barbecue but as this is chicken, you need to be absolutely sure its safely cooked through before serving  yourelookingfor clear juices andabsolutely no pink bits. If you have a meat thermometer, aim for a core temp of 75C/165F.
        </p>
        <p>
         5. When cooked, transfer the meat to a plate and leave to rest while you prepare the remainder of the dish
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Warm thetortillas briefly on thebarbecueto soften
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Fold each tortilla in your hand to create a soft C shape. If you want to keep them together before your guests arrive, you can line them up in a roasting tin as you work
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Fill with shredded lettuce and diced tomatoes
        </p>
        <p>
         9. Thinly slice the rested chicken and line on top of thesalad
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso chicken being cut" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22303" height="945" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-chicken-being-cut.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         10. Top withsalsa and guacamole
        </p>
        <p>
         11. Finish with a pinch ofgrated cheese
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="old el paso taco" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-22290" height="420" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/old-el-paso-taco.png" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Super tempting, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         If youd never have thought of serving up Mexican food in the garden on a hot day, I hope thesewill be the dishesto tempt you. With a little love and a little spice, you could experiment with Carne Asada recipesthis weekend to create something truly delicious.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Therecipes were commissioned by Old El Paso
         </em>
        </p>
