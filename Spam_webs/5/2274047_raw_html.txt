        <h2>
         Ill admit  I do it as much as anyone else: see the cool things that others are doing, and wish I were doing something cool like that, too.
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
          <img alt="Creating a contentment habit" class="aligncenter wp-image-20803 size-full" height="594" src="http://4fkjxm1bitfb1nn5cs27qrgn.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/Creating-a-contentment-habit.jpg" width="830"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://myria.com/is-better-always-best-how-to-create-a-contentment-habit&amp;media=http://myria.com/wp-content/uploads/Creating-a-contentment-habit.jpg&amp;description=Is better always best? How to create a contentment habit')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          By
          <a href="http://leobabauta.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Leo Babauta
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <h5>
         Should you be living a better life?
        </h5>
        <p>
         You see great
         <a href="http://myria.com/parenting/everyday-life/fun-activities/travel-fun-activities/">
          travel
         </a>
         photos on Instagram and other social media  people living amazing lives, creating cool things, going on adventures. And instantly, theres the thought that
         <em>
          you
         </em>
         should be living a better life.
        </p>
        <p>
         But this is the wrong habit. It leads to a feeling that your life isnt good enough, that you arent good enough.
        </p>
        <p>
         And the habit doesnt end: if you pursue a better life, you will always feel that you should be doing more, partying more, creating more, learning more, reading more, traveling more. You cant possibly do it all, but youll always wish you were.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          So whats a better habit? The contentment habit.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         What Ive been learning is that happiness and contentment and greatness isnt out there. Its not where everyone else is, even if it seems like it. You can spend your whole life chasing this happiness, contentment,
         <a href="http://myria.com/tag/dreams/">
          dreams
         </a>
         , greatness, coolness, and never reach it. Thats because its right where you are now.
        </p>
        <p>
         Before we talk about that, lets look at the
         <a href="http://myria.com/tag/habits/">
          habits
         </a>
         that most of us fall into.
        </p>
        <h5>
         The discontentment habits
        </h5>
        <h6>
         Tell me if these habits sound familiar:
        </h6>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           You see people doing great things, traveling, having fun,
          </strong>
          and wish you were doing something like that too. This never ends, because no matter how much you do, there will always be other people doing still more things that sound really cool. So youll never reach the pinnacle of fun and cool and achievement.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           You look at yourself and think that you can improve
          </strong>
           get fitter, leaner, more learned, calmer, happier, more productive. This too never ends, because even if you do amazingly at improving yourself, you will never be perfect, and there will always be more to improve. So youll never be content, and then you die.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           You feel you could be doing more.
          </strong>
          Youre rushing around, doing a lot, but theres always a feeling that you could be doing more. This never ends, because there is
          <em>
           always
          </em>
          more you could be doing. You can never do everything, so theres always more that youre not doing than there is that you are doing.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           You criticize others for what theyre not doing.
          </strong>
          Your kids, your spouse, your
          <a href="http://myria.com/tag/family/">
           family
          </a>
          , your friends  theyre all doing something you think they shouldnt, or maybe not doing something you think they should. There isnt happiness in criticizing others, because youre dissatisfied with life when youre dissatisfied with other people.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         If you find yourself doing any of these things  and Id bet $1,000 that you do them more than you realize  then its a good time to think about whether that habit will ever end, and if it will bring you happiness.
        </p>
        <p>
         The answer to both questions is no  those habits dont have an end, and they dont bring happiness
         <em>
          or
         </em>
         contentment.
        </p>
        <h5>
         The contentment habit
        </h5>
        <h6>
         When you catch yourself doing those discontentment habits, by being mindful of your feelings and thoughts during the day  try this instead:
        </h6>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Stop looking elsewhere for happiness
          </strong>
           in what others are doing, in what you should be doing, in what others should be doing but arent, in the things you should improve. Happiness and contentment arent out there.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Instead, turn to where you are right at this moment.
          </strong>
          Pause and feel your body, your breath, and all the sensations surrounding you right now. See how this moment contains so much that you dont normally notice, if you start paying attention.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Realize that this moment is enough.
          </strong>
          All around you, right now, is a moment that is as high quality as any of the possibilities that often enter your mind of what you should be doing or where you should be. Those ideal experiences arent of any higher quality than the experience youre having right now.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           See the wonder in this moment.
          </strong>
          Right where you are, right now. Notice the amazing things around you, and in you, as if youre seeing it
          <a href="http://zenhabits.tumblr.com/post/105096985755/kaydens-first-time-experiencing-rain-x" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           for the first time ever
          </a>
          . Notice the miracle of your body, your mind, your surroundings. How did all this come to be? The building youre in, or the
          <a href="http://myria.com/tag/nature/">
           nature
          </a>
          youre in, didnt just magically appear  its almost as if the world conspired to make this moment happen, and you get to be here to witness it. Awesome!
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Thats the contentment habit, and you can do it anytime, anyplace, no matter what youre doing or who youre with. It is free, always available, always miraculous. And it never ends.
        </p>
