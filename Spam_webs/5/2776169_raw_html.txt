         <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/2008/04/22/happy-earth-day-in-pictures/" rel="bookmark">
          Happy Earth Day in Pictures!
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="byline vcard author">
         <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-58 photo" height="58" src="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2013/10/SWAB_logo-58x58.jpg" srcset="http://0.gravatar.com/avatar/6e260b0d51ec7a86c48b3360c8682a32?s=116&amp;d=mm&amp;r=pg 2x" width="58"/>

       <div class="main">
        <div class="content entry-content">
         <p>
          As you may have noticed from
          <a href="http://startswithabang.com/?p=347">
           yesterdays unusual post
          </a>
          , today is
          <a href="http://ww2.earthday.net/">
           Earth Day
          </a>
          ! I thought Id share with you some of my favorite pictures from space of it, including the
          <a href="http://www.universetoday.com/2008/04/22/apollo-8-astronaut-bill-anders-reflects-on-earthrise-picture/">
           famous photograph from Apollo 8 known as Earthrise
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
         </center>
         <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/earthrise_apollo8.gif">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25493" height="292" src="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/earthrise_apollo8.gif" title="earthrise_apollo8" width="499"/>
         </a>
         <p>
          This combination shot made from NASAs Terra satellite and NOAAs Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellite:
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
         </center>
         <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/600px-earth_from_space.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25494" height="600" src="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/600px-earth_from_space.jpg" title="600px-earth_from_space" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p>
          The known satellites at least 0.1 meters in size
          <a href="http://www.theglobaleducationproject.org/earth/global-ecology.php">
           in orbit around Earth
          </a>
          (there are ~11,000 of them as of April 2005, and another 100,000 between 1 cm and 10 cm in size):
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
         </center>
         <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/g-space-debris.gif">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25495" height="440" src="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/g-space-debris.gif" title="g-space-debris" width="550"/>
         </a>
         <p>
          Looking at the Earth and the docked Space Shuttle from the International Space Station:
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
         </center>
         <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/shuttle_view_of_earth_space_freecomputerdesktopwallpaper_p.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25496" height="300" src="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/shuttle_view_of_earth_space_freecomputerdesktopwallpaper_p.jpg" title="shuttle_view_of_earth_space_freecomputerdesktopwallpaper_p" width="400"/>
         </a>
         <p>
          And the emergence of night over Europe and Africa (yes, the night lights are enhanced):
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
         </center>
         <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/earth_from_space.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-25497" height="600" src="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2008/04/earth_from_space-600x600.jpg" title="earth_from_space" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p>
          This is our home. Ill be back with more of the standard stuff you love about the Universe tomorrow, but for today, remember that in the vastness of space, in the depths of our galaxy, this little wet rock is where we came to be. And its our responsibility to take care of it, just as it takes care of us. Happy Earth Day.
         </p>
