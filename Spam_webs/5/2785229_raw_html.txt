     <h1>
      How to Tune a Guitar
     </h1>
     <p class="lead">
      Tuning the guitar is the first thing you need to master on your way to rock stardom. Here are some simple instructions that explain the most commonly used guitar tuning in the world.

     </h1>

     <h2>
      Step 1: The 6th String  E (thickest)
     </h2>
     <p>
      Tune the bottom E String, as accurately as you can. If you dont have a tuner you can use our
      <a href="/">
       handy guitar tuning app
      </a>
      .You can also use other reference tones such as a Piano or any other musical instrument that is handy. Whats important is that you have a reference tone.
     </p>
     <div class="panel panel-info">
      <div class="panel-heading">
       Tip: Fake It Till You Make It
      </div>
      <div class="panel-body">
       Did you get lost alone in the forest with just your guitar and cant find a reference tone anywhere? Assuming you dont have any other musicians playing with you, you can always just tune that Fat E String as best you can and tune the rest of your guitar to it. As long as the guitar is in tune with itself you can still play.
      </div>
     </div>

     <h2>
      Step 2: The 5th String  A
     </h2>
     <p>
      Place your index finger on the fifth fret of the bottom E string. Thats an A note. Keep your finger on that fret. Now pick the open fifth string and fretted six string in turn, gently adjusting the fifth string tuning peg until the two notes are in harmony. Tightening the peg will make the pitch higher, loosening the peg will make the pitch lower.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="string-a" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-573 img-responsive" height="179" sizes="(min-width: 750px) 585px, (max-width: 749px) 90vw" src="http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-a-585x179.jpg" srcset="http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-a-280x86.jpg 280w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-a-585x179.jpg 585w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-a-864x264.jpg 864w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-a-432x132.jpg 432w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-a-675x206.jpg 675w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-a-1170x358.jpg 1170w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-a.jpg 1200w" width="585"/>
     </p>
     <h2>
      Step 3: The 4th String  D
     </h2>
     <p>
      Place your index finger on the the fifth fret of the A string. Thats a D note. Pluck the fretted 5th string and the open 4th string together or alternating between them and adjust the 4th string tuning peg until the pitch of the 4th string matches the pitch of the 5th fret of the 5th string.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="string-d" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-574 img-responsive" height="179" sizes="(min-width: 750px) 585px, (max-width: 749px) 90vw" src="http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-d-585x179.jpg" srcset="http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-d-280x86.jpg 280w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-d-585x179.jpg 585w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-d-864x264.jpg 864w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-d-432x132.jpg 432w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-d-675x206.jpg 675w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-d-1170x358.jpg 1170w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-d.jpg 1200w" width="585"/>
     </p>
     <h2>
      Step 4: The 3rd String  G
     </h2>
     <p>
      Place your index finger on the fifth fret of the D string. Thats a G note. Pluck or alternate pick the fretted 4th and open 3rd strings, adjusting the 3rd string tuning peg until the 3rd string is in harmony with the 5th fret of the 4th string.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="string-g" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-575 img-responsive" height="179" sizes="(min-width: 750px) 585px, (max-width: 749px) 90vw" src="http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-g-585x179.jpg" srcset="http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-g-280x86.jpg 280w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-g-585x179.jpg 585w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-g-864x264.jpg 864w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-g-432x132.jpg 432w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-g-675x206.jpg 675w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-g-1170x358.jpg 1170w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-g.jpg 1200w" width="585"/>
     </p>
     <h2>
      Step 5: The 2nd String  B
     </h2>
     <p>
      Place your index finger on the fourth fret of the G string. Thats a B. Tune your 2nd string to that note.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="string-b" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-576 img-responsive" height="179" sizes="(min-width: 750px) 585px, (max-width: 749px) 90vw" src="http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-b-585x179.png" srcset="http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-b-280x86.png 280w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-b-585x179.png 585w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-b-864x264.png 864w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-b-432x132.png 432w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-b-675x206.png 675w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-b-1170x358.png 1170w, http://cdn1.tommymedia.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/string-b.png 1200w" width="585"/>
     </p>
     <div class="panel panel-info">
      <div class="panel-heading">
       Tip: How to Remember Standard Tuning Pattern
      </div>
      <div class="panel-body">
       The B string is the only string that gets its reference tone from the 4th fret of the string above it. All the rest of the open strings utilize the 5th fret of the string above as a reference tone. If you can remember this tuning pattern you will always be able to tune your guitar.
      </div>
     </div>
     <p class="ad-mobile ad-default">
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
       Step 6: Tuning the E String
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Place your index finger just behind the fifth fret on the B string. Thats a E note. Tune the thinnest and last string to that, again by adjusting the 1st string tuning peg until the pitch of the E string matches the 5th fret of the 2nd String.
     </p>
     <p>
