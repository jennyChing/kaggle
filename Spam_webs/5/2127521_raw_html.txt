         <p>
          Compared to the previous episode,
          <em>
           Scandal
          </em>
          , Inside the Bubble is almost low-key. Its another one of those setup episodes, like the get set call before the gun fires and the runners take off down the track. Not that theres a mellow vibe or anything going on. Right before the race starts is the most tension-filled times of a race. Inside the Bubble is filled with a similar tension. In this
          <em>
           Scandal
          </em>
          43 review Ill be looking at why everyones so on edge!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://gossipandgab.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Scandal-4x3-Olivia-and-Her-Dad.jpg">
           <img alt="Scandal 4x3 Olivia and Her Dad" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-55862" height="277" src="http://gossipandgab.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Scandal-4x3-Olivia-and-Her-Dad-460x277.jpg" width="460"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I called this review Illusions &amp; Delusions because thats what Inside the Bubble is looking at  the illusions being cast and the delusions being held by various characters. There is so much going on that I decided to approach the review via the characters rather the stories. Thats because of how I heard the phrase used in the show. When that occurred is a good place to start off with.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <h5>
            Attorney General David Rosen
           </h5>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          The
          <em>
           Scandal
          </em>
          title, Inside the Bubble refers to what Cyrus Beene (
          <b>
           Jeff Perry
          </b>
          )says to the newly minted Attorney General David Rosen (
          <b>
           Joshua Malina
          </b>
          ). David presents to Cyrus and President Fitzgerald Fitz Grant (
          <b>
           Tony Goldwyn
          </b>
          ) where they are in terms getting the presidents gun control law to make through the legality challenge by the gun lobby. After the presentation the president stands up, clasps him on the shoulder and says hes counting on him. Cyrus sees him beaming and says.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           I know that look. Youre inside the bubblefeels good doesnt it
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          David may take it as Cyrus congratulating him for becoming an insider with the President and finally being respected, but I think Cyrus had a double meaning in mind. Yes, it is about being an insider. However, being inside the Bubble also refers to the good times being illusionary. Theres the phrase, I dont want to burst your bubble  ie shatter your illusion of what you think you know. You think youve made it, you think youre safe. but its a bubble, it will pop. He knows those moments never last.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
          When it looks like David is going to lose the gun control law case (thanks to some work done on behalf of the gun lobby) he has to face letting the president down.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="259" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/eUvd2CUx3Hc?showinfo=0" width="460">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          After getting that despairing talk from the president, David goes back to the B613 files and finds some dirt on the swing vote judge. Its not his best, but its certainly some of his dirtiest. Reluctantly the Judge agrees to vote for the law. The next day, to everyones surprise (especially to the Republican National Committee Chair) the court rules in favor of the president. After being toasted to by Fitz and other major White House staffers, David hears the news that the judge he blackmailed has committed suicide  by blowing his brains out. Whether the man actually killed himself (I doubt it) or if his defying the Republican gun lobby got him B613d David feels the blood on his hands. Being a Washington insider, playing the hardball politics suddenly doesnt feel so great. Bubble burst.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <h5>
            White House Chief of Staff Cyrus Beene
           </h5>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Given Cyruss subtle smugness with David about being in the bubble, you would think hed recognize that if something seems too good to be true thats because it is. Instead, Cyrus is floating along inside his own bubble. He has no idea hes being set up by Republican National Committee (RNC) Chair Lizzie Bear (
          <strong>
           Portia de Rossi
          </strong>
          ) via the hot male hustler, Michael (
          <strong>
           Matthew Del Negro
          </strong>
          ). Michael, dressed in a business suit is at an upscale breakfast restaurant that Cyrus frequents. Its planned of course. Michael tells Cyrus he goes to GW business school and is just 6 credits shy of his MBA. He admonishes Cyrus Oh, you assumed that because Im a whore, I dont have a brain. He then pretends he has friends coming to meet him and asks Cyrus to go since his friends dont know what he does to pay tuition. Cyruss assertions that he doesnt pay for sex fall apart with Michaels rationalizing it.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Youre not paying for sex, youre paying for privacy and discretion.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          That night Cyrus spends the night with Michael  for twenty-five hundred dollars!
         </p>
         <p>
          With that act Cyrus fulfills his role as Lizzy Bears contingency plan (she thinks). Just that morning she was telling the head of the gun lobby that she has back up plans for her backup plans but that she had a meeting with Judge Sparks that morning to make sure he voted the bill down. As we know, because of what David did, the next day the Judge flip-flopped and didnt vote the bill down.
         </p>
         <p>
          My question is, who had him killed? Lizzy Bear seems to be just as ruthless and amoral as Cyrus, but would she have the Judge killed just because she didnt get her way? Im not sure. What I am sure of is that if herstrategy is to blackmail Cyrus into doing what she needs Lizzy Bear is going to end up being a dead bear. She may have set up Cyrus, but I dont know if she realizes Cyrus will have no qualms having her taken out. One way or another, Lizzy Bears days are numbered!
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <h5>
            White House Press Secretary Abby Whelan
           </h5>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Abby Whelan (
          <strong>
           Darby Stanchfield
          </strong>
          ) bubble is constantly being popped  not that shes unaware of things. Her perception that no one really takes her seriously around the White House is pretty accurate, and shes definitely not an insider. I suppose her illusion is that at some point something will change and she wont be a pale substitute for Olivia Pope.
         </p>
         <p>
          This week Abby has to deal with the new obsession of First Lady Mellie Grant (
          <strong>
           Bellamy Young
          </strong>
          )- proving a woman innocent thats being crucified in the media for seemingly pushing her husband off a cliff in Yellowstone Park. Theyre calling her the killer cliff bride.
         </p>
         <p>
          Mellies obsession starts when she walks into the press room and sees Abby and some White House aides watching the story on TV. Mellie becomes intrigued with in and decides that she can prove the woman didnt do it. The problem arises when Mellie demands Abby set up an official meeting with the heads of the investigation at Yellowstone, and FBI heads in D.C.
         </p>
         <p>
          The first person she goes to is Cyrus, who doesnt let her explain. He just informs Abby shes not in the bubble and to stay out of the First Ladys business. She then tries Fitz. Fitz has been happy to see Mellie involved with anything, and Abbys timing is bad. Hes just learned theyre going to lose the gun control bill. He doesnt listen to what the actual issue is either. Instead he yells at Abby to just give Mellie what she wants.
         </p>
         <p>
          If that isnt bad enough. When Abby hears that daughter of a mutual friend of hers and Olivias has been murdered she rushes over to Olivias out of concern  and gets completely frozen out.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="259" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/2nNAsZTX4og?showinfo=0" width="460">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Yes, this is as bad as it seems between Abby and Olivia. Ill get to the rest of that when I get to Olivias bubble.
         </p>
         <p>
          The next day is Mellies meeting. She is hyper excited to have proven the wife didnt do it  with the help of people like the head of NASA and the Surgeon General. Aside from how inappropriate it is for her to be using these people for this kind of project, it turns out to be completely unnecessary.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="259" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/caBIwG9EKw8?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" width="460">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          This scene is about another minute or so longer than the above clip. It is one uncomfortable scene. Everyone feels sorry for Mellie basically making a fool of herself. The discomfort in the room is palpable! Thank goodness Abby is able to keep it together and get Mellie out of there!
         </p>
         <p>
          Later on the President summons Abby to his office. He offers her a glass of scotch, and Abby gets to impress him with her knowledge. She even offers to get him a bottle of the best Scotch in the world, From her Majesty, the Queen. Fitz apologizes for underestimating her and thanks her for her handling of Mellie. Abby uses the moment to impress upon him her name. Things look like they are finally getting better. Thats when the President sits her down and asks her how Olivia is doing.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55840" style="width: 446px">
          <a href="http://gossipandgab.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Scandal-4x3-Fitz-asks-Abby-about-Olivia.jpg">
           <img alt="Scandal 4x3 Fitz asks Abby about Olivia" class="wp-image-55840 " height="378" src="http://gossipandgab.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Scandal-4x3-Fitz-asks-Abby-about-Olivia-460x419.jpg" width="436"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           <strong>
            Poor Abby, she just cant get out of Olivias shadow!
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <h5>
            Crisis Manager Olivia Pope
           </h5>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          While Abby spends this trying to assert herself as worthy in her own life without Olivia,  Olivia is reconnecting with her father.  Talk about a huge bubble waiting to be pricked!  Olivia seems to have come to the belief that her Dad is less of a monster than he is. As such she finds herself over at her fathers house bringing him coffee!  For his part, Rowan Pope (
          <strong>
           Joe Morton
          </strong>
          ) plays the part of the understanding dad beautifully. He talks about needing time to adjust to being back, and even suggests that she invite Jake over to dinner. Seriously, he does!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="259" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/8luvIojJKAg?showinfo=0" width="460">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Look up the word compartmentalization and there will be a photo of Rowan Pope. The manI dont even know if theres a word for it. Like, we know Charlie (
          <strong>
           George Newburn
          </strong>
          ) is your basic sociopath, but Rowan with this divided self, the wanting to be a good dad vs the evil, murderous control freak with a god-complex. What ever he is, hes certainly got Olivia fooled. This bubble of hers is gonna make a huge mess when it pops!
         </p>
         <p>
          In the meantime though, Abby calls Olivia with some news. Katherine Winslow (
          <strong>
           Sonya Walger
          </strong>
          ), a good friend of Abbys and Olivias from when they went to law school together, is looking for Olivia because her daughter didnt come home the night before. Abby starts to suggest that perhaps this isnt a major deal, but Olivia cuts her off.
         </p>
         <p>
          You said Katherine was looking for me, I said Ill take care of it. So, why dont we stay out of each others jobs.
         </p>
         <p>
          Olivia then hangs up on her. On thing about cell phonestheres no more slamming the phone down for emphasis anymore. If there were, Id say Olivia would have done it. Shes still mad at Abby about Abby being territorial about working at the White House and suggesting Olivia was responsible for what happened to Harrison. I feel bad for them both. Being at odds with a girlfriend is the worst!
         </p>
         <p>
          Katherine went to law school with Olivia and Abby  but she never took the bar exam. Instead she married another lawyer, Jeremy, who has made major money  they are super rich. She tells Olivia that she and her 17-year-old daughter Caitlin had a fight over college and that Caitlin took off. According to Katherine its not the first time Caitlin has run off, but never for this long. With her husband out of the country on business, she just wants to locate the girl and make sure shes safe. Calling the police would be a media circus.
         </p>
         <p>
          For Olivia this looks like an easy job, but a job none-the-less. She calls to cancel her dinner with Jake, and then invites him to dinner. Jake informs her that if she was his girlfriend hed go  despite all the horrible things her dad has done to him. Has no idea hes researching how to bring him down. (Ill be getting to Jakes issues after were done with Olivia!)
         </p>
         <p>
          Olivia, Huck (
          <strong>
           Guillermo Daz
          </strong>
          )and Quinn Perkins (
          <strong>
           Katie Lowes
          </strong>
          ) manage to track down the daughterthrough one of her girlfriends credit cards. The girl has checked herself into a hotel. Olivia offers to have Quinn bring her home, but Katherine says she wants to go over there and talk to her  mother to daughter. It all seems benign  until Huck discovers a sex video on Caitlins computer. Its of Caitlins boyfriend and Katherine! If thats not awful enough Huck has also found a phone message from Caitlin to her mother. She calls her a pedophile  and says nothing her mother can say will keep her from telling her father. Katherine will lose everything!
         </p>
         <p>
          Olivia thinks the worst  theyve sent Katherine to her daughter and very well may kill her! They call the hotel, but its too late. Katherine is at the morgue identifying Caitlins body!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="259" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/vj_xH2RsBJs?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" width="460">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          This is why Olivia cant begin to tell Abby whats going on. Kathrines behavior  even if she didnt kill her daughter  is horrific. This was one of her closest friends in college  as was Abby. Abby who recently blamed Olivia for Harrison death. Who the hell can Olivia trust? Everything she thought she knew about people is proving to be completely off. (This actually could be a good thing for her to realize  that sometimes she can be wrong!)
         </p>
         <p>
          Katherine comes to her the next day and swears she didnt kill her daughter. She gets brutally honest.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           The truth is I was bored, out of my mind and I slept with a 17-year-old. I screwed a kid, and it was wrong, and dirty, and you know what? It felt fricken great. And then Caitlin, found out, and I ruined everything. My marriage, my family. I feel sick. I am disgusting. But I am not, a murderer.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Sick and disgusting is right Mrs. Robinson! (Id feel the same way if it were a man with a 17-year-girl. Theres no gender bias. Both areawful!) What bugged me about it is she feels bad because Caitlin
          <em>
           found out 
          </em>
          not about her actions. I mean, not only is she a pedophile, but its her daughters boyfriend! Like, of the 17-year-boys in the world.
         </p>
         <p>
          Olivia apparently believes Katherine may not have killed her daughter, because she comes into the office
          <i>
          </i>
          and has Huck start looking into other possible subjects. Obviously, the show is coming back to this case, since nothing about it is resolved!
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <h5>
            Former B613 Agent &amp; Former Command Jake Ballard
           </h5>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Jake is inside the bubble because he really thinks if he can get this information on Rowan he can get Rowan to leave town and that, in time, Olivia Pope will agree to be his girlfriend. As such, he backtracks on his absolutely not decision and has dinner with Olivia and her father. Unfortunately theres a moment where Olivia leaves the two men alone at the table
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="259" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/6t0XOXR1EEw?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" width="460">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Jake has seriously lost his mind  and really blown it for himself. First of all, Rowan Pope isnt someone you get to leave town. Rowan Pope is someone who is going to have to be stone cold murdered if you want him to stop doing anything he doesnt want to do. Secondly, why tell your enemy your plans, especially when hes trying to play nice? Its like a declaration of war! Jake could have played along with the charade, had Rowans approval to be with Olivia, all the while plotting his demise, but no, he had to go all macho man and tell Rowan what was up.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its moments like this that reminds me that Jake has never been the most competent B613agent  Charlie and Huck have always been more efficient. Remember how he was tailing Charlie back in season two (Any Questions). Charlie was able make and shake Jake like it was nothing, and its Huck who talks Jake through a computer hack in the season three episode More Cattle, Less Bull. Some of it is Jake is similar to Olivia. He wants to be a white hat. Hes way too B613 warped of course, but the reason he killed James Novak and two other reporters himself is that he didnt want any other B613 person to have to carry that the burden of killing three innocent people. Unfortunately, I think Jakes desire to somehow make things right also makes him more naive. Even Huck knew that the only way to get rid of Rowan Pope would be to kill him. Trying to blackmail Command into leaving the country, Jake? Get real!
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <h5>
            Pope Associate Quinn Perkins
           </h5>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          When Charlie tells Jake the only way hell talk is to get Quinn and him locked in a room for a night, Jake goes for it. The results arent exactly what Charlie had in mind.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="259" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/01AZZPlMjhE?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" width="460">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          I really am loving Quinn Perkins this season! She really has become a kick-ass, take-no-prisoners kind of woman. She still has a bubble though. Its that shes convinced herself she doesnt need anyone  especially Charlie.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="259" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/pQ1vdKCXHLg?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" width="460">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          The reason Charlie was able to get to her the first time is basic, he listened to her and expressed admiration for who she was, and supported her in her goals. Those goals werewarped and unhealthy ones, but hey, the woman likes to drill. Meanwhile, everyone at Pope and Associates was ignoring her.
         </p>
         <p>
          When Charlie and Quinn were together it felt to him that hed found his soul-mate because she was so into being able to pull off jobs and torturing guys for information. The problem is that while Quinn is a tough bad-ass, shes really not like Charlie. Quinn got into Huck and Charlies line of work because she felt powerless. She wanted to feel capable, strong, and to never again be put in the position that got her name changed to Quinn Perkins. Charlie does what he does because hes a sociopath whod be a serial killer if it werent for B613. Yes, he does have moments of empathy  letting Huck live and leave B613 was one of those moments  but Charlies a guy who likes to kill. Quinn doesnt enjoy killing. She just likes feeling in control of things.
         </p>
         <p>
          I do feel somewhat sorry for Charlie. His feelings for Quinn are as close to love as hes capable of, but hes just too much of a bad guy to ever really have Quinns heart. However, if Huck doesnt come to his senses soon, Quinn could end up drifting back to Charlie. Ironically, Charlie having Quinn kidnapped for the night may have helped speed that along. When returns to Pope and Associates and realizes no one has even noticed shed been missing she really let them have it!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="259" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/FqGWtBFn2Sc?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" width="460">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Lowes really has blended the Quinns strength and sense of competence and the characters underlying vulnerability and Im finding the layers in Quinns scenes riveting.  Im glad Quinns words got through to Huck enough for him to later come over and say Ill look for you if she goes missing again. Whatever else is storming around in his head, the truth Quinn states, that she never abandoned them (the way they did her) clearly hit home. Week after week she came and checked on him  despite his refusal to talk to her. If she goes missing, theres got to be something wrong, because Quinn, is loyal. What this will mean for HuckleberryQuinn remains to be seen. I hope Huck comes to his senses soon, Quinn is a passionate woman and she and Charlie do have chemistry.
         </p>
         <p>
          I almost included this clip in my Olivia Pope section, because Olivia is truly flabbergasted by Quinns outburst. Given the two issues she has going on with her closest friends from college, this point Quinn is making about loyalty and how they were all culpable in Harrisons death has to seem like its out of nowhere. Although Olivia looks at Quinn as if shes nuts, Im hoping Quinns blunt assessment will sink in the way it did with Huck. She needs to think about exactly when Harrison went missing if shes every going to figure out the truth regarding his death.
         </p>
