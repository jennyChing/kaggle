       <h1>
        Setting Fitness  Goals
       </h1>
       <div class="row margin-top-10">
        <div class="twelve columns">

         <div class="margin-top-5 margin-bottom-15">
          <a href="http://bfdev.wsiefusion.net/ask-the-trainer.html">
           <img alt="Meet with a Fitness Professional today to set your SMART goals!" src="/images/cta-meet-with-a-fitness-professional.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          Whether you want to lose weight or increase your endurance,  its important to structure your workout program to fit your goals. Sounds  simple, right? Actually, only about 5% of people who begin a fitness routine on  their own succeed, largely because they do not have a real goal in mind. It  only works if you set SMART goals.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           SMART
          </strong>
          goals are Specific, Measurable, Action-Oriented,  Realistic and Time-Sensitive.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           SPECIFIC
          </strong>
          means setting goals to get to a target. I want to  lose weight or I want to tone up are popular reasons for people to start a  workout routine, but these goals are not specific and the likelihood of them  yielding positive results are low. Ask yourself a question like How much  weight do you want to lose? Thats a specific question that can really help  set attainable goals.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           MEASURABLE
          </strong>
          goals help you track your progress throughout  your fitness journey. If you are currently a size 18 and you strive to be a  size 8, you can now measure your progress. Each time you drop a size you inch a  percentage closer to reaching your goal. You could also consider overall health  goals that are specific like I want to reduce my blood pressure because you  can easily and successfully measure your progress with that.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           ACTION-ORIENTED
          </strong>
          approach allows you to create specific  actions to achieve your desired results. For example, if you want to lose 50  pounds, you may reduce your daily calorie intake by 20%. Additionally, you  could break that down specific actions (like switching from whole milk to skim  milk) that can help you achieve your goal.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           REALISTIC
          </strong>
          goal setting is incredibly important for your  commitment and your psyche throughout the process. If your goal is to lose 50  pounds, thats great! If your goal is to lose 50 pounds in a month, thats not  very realistic, and you will almost assuredly end up failing. By breaking up  your weight loss goal into smaller components, like losing 50 pounds in a year,  or a pound per week, that is a far more realistic aspiration.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           TIME-SENSITIVE
          </strong>
          nature of goals helps you stay engaged and  accountable to achieving your goals. If you are getting married in 3 months,  you have a final date to achieve some of your goals. However, please make sure  that you keep those goals realistic as well.
         </p>
         <p>
          Setting goals may seem like a trivial thing, but it really  helps you stay on track and have a plan to get where you want to get.
         </p>
         <p>
          At Best Fitness, we take pride in helping you achieve your  goals. Our trained, dedicated Fitness Professionals are here to help you set  SMART goals and encourage you until you reach them!
         </p>
