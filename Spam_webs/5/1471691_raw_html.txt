           <h1>
            His Royal Highness, The Prince of Cambridge is One!
           </h1>
           <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="His Royal Highness, The Prince of Cambridge is One!" property="dc:title">
           </span>

             <div class="field-items">
              <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
               <p>
                Years seem to fly by for other people, and especially those in the public eye compared to one's own world.
               </p>
               <p>
                Since Prince George, the adorable little son of the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge shares a birth date with one of my own grandchildren, I was quite aware of it. Of course, the first year of any little tyke's life is full of milestones, and will bring out more physical changes from new born babe to a toddler taking first steps than any other year of life.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Happy Birthday, Prince George!
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                As it was with both his birth and baptism, Prince George's first birthday is being commemorated with a new UK coin of sterling silver. There is a limited mintage amount of 7,500 and the 5 pound coin will cost collectors 80 pounds.
               </p>
               <p>
                What is quite memorable this year is the Buckingham Palace's Children's Exhibit which opened today. The theme of "Life as a Royal Child" is very in keeping with Prince George's birthday but is featuring not only articles belonging to his cuteness, but also eight generations of royal children before him.
               </p>
               <p>
                Visitors to the exhibit will see outlandish toys such as a miniature Aston Martin DB5 belonging to a six year old Prince Andrew, as well as typical parent-sentimental keepsakes as animal drawings done by a young Prince William. Many dolls will be displayed belonging to the Princesses Elizabeth and Margaret, which touches a soft spot in my heart as I coveted my very own Princess Margaret doll in the 1940s.
               </p>
               <p>
                There will also be gifts displayed to Prince George that had been received from other country leaders, so one may be able to see the silver breakfast set sent by Prime Minister of Malta or President Obama's three gifts: a blue alpaca wool blanket, a hand-made rocking horse and a specially designed polo mallet made out of oak wood from a tree on the White House's south lawn.
               </p>
               <p>
                Also in tandem to little Prince George's first birthday anniversary, will be displays of his baptismal robe, the 17 inch silver-gilt lily font and the famous gold easel used for royal birth announcements. The "Royal Children" display is open to the public at Buckingham Palace from July 26 to September 28.
               </p>
