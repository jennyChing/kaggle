     <p>
      <b>
       Philippe Croizon
      </b>
      (born 1968) is a
      <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">
       French
      </a>
      athlete and the first quadruple
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amputee" title="Amputee">
       amputee
      </a>
      to swim across the
      <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">
       English Channel
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pa-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Accident">
       Accident
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippe_Croizon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Accident">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      His amputations were required due to a severe
      <a href="/wiki/Electric_shock" title="Electric shock">
       electric shock
      </a>
      accident which occurred in March 1994.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fr1_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-fr1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At the time, Croizon was employed as a steelworker at the fondry of Poitou,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lad_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-lad-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      26 years old and married, with one son; his wife was expecting a second child.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lad_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-lad-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At his home in
      <a href="/wiki/Saint-R%C3%A9my-sur-Creuse" title="Saint-Rmy-sur-Creuse">
       Saint-Rmy-sur-Creuse
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Vienne" title="Vienne">
       Vienne
      </a>
      , while standing on a metal ladder on the roof to work on a television antenna, Croizon received a severe electric shock from a high-voltage power line which earthed (grounded) through the ladder,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fr1_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-fr1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lad_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-lad-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      to which he became
      <a href="/wiki/Adhesion" title="Adhesion">
       adhered
      </a>
      . Twenty minutes passed before a neighbour could raise the alarm. He was hospitalized in
      <a href="/wiki/Tours" title="Tours">
       Tours
      </a>
      , where doctors removed his left arm above the elbow, his right arm below the elbow, then his right leg above the knee. Surgeons had thought that the left leg could be saved, but when that also required removal, Croizon reported feeling "despair".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lad_3-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-lad-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="English_Channel_Challenge">
       English Channel Challenge
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippe_Croizon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: English Channel Challenge">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">
       Preparations
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippe_Croizon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Preparations">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      During his recuperation in the hospital, he saw a television programme about a female channel-swimmer, who Croizon said inspired him.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fr1_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-fr1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-voix_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-voix-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He began a regimen of swimming, training for over five hours per day with the
      <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Gendarmerie" title="Maritime Gendarmerie">
       Maritime Gendarmerie
      </a>
      , the French
      <a href="/wiki/Water_police" title="Water police">
       marine police
      </a>
      , in the sea near
      <a href="/wiki/La_Rochelle" title="La Rochelle">
       La Rochelle
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lad_3-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-lad-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He experimented with different
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prosthetic" title="Prosthetic">
       prosthetic
      </a>
      limbs designed for swimming, with fins attached to the stumps of his legs.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lad_3-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-lad-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      One set of specially designed prosthetics cost
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_(currency)" title="Euro (currency)">
       
      </a>
      12,000, and are made from
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon" title="Carbon">
       carbon
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Titanium" title="Titanium">
       titanium
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-voix_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-voix-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beranger_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Beranger-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      He wrote a book entitled
      <i>
       J'ai dcid de vivre
      </i>
      (
      <i>
       I Decided to Live
      </i>
      ), using a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Speech-to-text" title="Speech-to-text">
       speech-to-text
      </a>
      computer system.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fr1_2-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-fr1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He also made a parachute jump.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fr1_2-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-fr1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Croizon's preparations for the channel-swim attempt took 35 hours per week for two years; throughout the training period, he received letters of support from national politicians, including then-President of France
      <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Sarkozy" title="Nicolas Sarkozy">
       Nicolas Sarkozy
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tel_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-tel-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was during this period that he completed a swim from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noirmoutier" title="Noirmoutier">
       Noirmoutier
      </a>
      to
      <a href="/wiki/Pornic" title="Pornic">
       Pornic
      </a>
      in less than five hours.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tel_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-tel-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Event">
       Event
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippe_Croizon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Event">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      On Saturday, 18 September 2010, at the age of 42, he swam across the
      <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">
       English Channel
      </a>
      in less than 14 hours.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvnz_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-tvnz-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He set off from
      <a href="/wiki/Folkestone" title="Folkestone">
       Folkestone
      </a>
      at 06:45, arriving at
      <a href="/wiki/Cap_Gris_Nez" title="Cap Gris Nez">
       Cap Gris Nez
      </a>
      at 20:13, a distance of 21 miles (34km).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBC-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indep2010_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Indep2010-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After the crossing, he reported that he had felt pain, but was confident that he would finish.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-pa-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Intercontinental_Straits_Swimming_Challenge">
       Intercontinental Straits Swimming Challenge
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippe_Croizon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Intercontinental Straits Swimming Challenge">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In April 2012, Croizon announced a new project, to swim four straits separating five continents, in which he would be accompanied by long-distance swimmer Arnaud Chassery.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telfer-2012_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Telfer-2012-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-les-nageurs_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-les-nageurs-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The planned trips included Australia to Asia, across the
      <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">
       Red Sea
      </a>
      (linking Asia and Africa), the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straits_of_Gibraltar" title="Straits of Gibraltar">
       Straits of Gibraltar
      </a>
      (linking Africa and Europe), and the
      <a href="/wiki/Bering_Strait" title="Bering Strait">
       Bering Strait
      </a>
      (linking Asia and America).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telfer-2012_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Telfer-2012-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Croizon completed the first swim, linking Australia and Asia, in May 2012, when he swam 20km (12mi) from
      <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">
       Papua New Guinea
      </a>
      to
      <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">
       Indonesia
      </a>
      in seven-and-a-half hours, accompanied by Chassery and a local man, Papua New Guinean Zet Tampa, who swam with them in support. Their course was along New Guinea Island, shared by both countries.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GPost2012_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-GPost2012-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC-PNG-2012_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBC-PNG-2012-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In June, he crossed the Red Sea from Egypt to Jordan, over a distance of 19km (12mi) in about 5 hours.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indep2012_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Indep2012-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Croizon completed his Straits of Gibraltar crossing in July, from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tarifa,_Spain" title="Tarifa, Spain">
       Tarifa, Spain
      </a>
      , over 14km (9mi), to land near the city of Tangier, Morocco. He completed the journey with a friend in "just over five hours."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian0712_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian0712-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      His planned August Bering Strait swim from
      <a href="/wiki/Little_Diomede_Island" title="Little Diomede Island">
       Little Diomede Island
      </a>
      , across the international date line, to Russian
      <a href="/wiki/Big_Diomede" title="Big Diomede">
       Big Diomede
      </a>
      , was to occur a week after the 25th anniversary of
      <a href="/wiki/Lynne_Cox" title="Lynne Cox">
       Lynne Cox
      </a>
      's historic crossing on 7 August 1987. Croizon's training regimens included temperature conditioning for the below 40-degree (F) water by swimming in ice-melt mountain lake water, and ice water baths.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AlaDisp_16-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-AlaDisp-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The August 13 dawn attempt was delayed, then canceled, due a storm arriving early.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      When a lull in the bad weather arrived on 17 August, the team's start was delayed by high waves until the late afternoon (3 pm local time).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BeringVic_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BeringVic-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Croizon and Arnaud completed the 4.3km (2.7 miles) swim across the Bering Strait in one hour and 20 minutes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BeringVic_18-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-BeringVic-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Dense fog and strong currents added time and distance to the trip in 4 degree C (39 F) water. After crossing the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_date_line" title="International date line">
       International date line
      </a>
      , they continued for a few hundred yards to Great Diomede island in Russian waters without official permission.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
