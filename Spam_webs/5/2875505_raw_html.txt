        <h1 class="title">
         Fujitsu Flexbook Teases with a Bendy Screen Future
        </h1>
        <span class="author">
         <span>
          By Wesley Fenlon
         </span>
         <time>
          on May 13, 2011 at 9:29 a.m.
         </time>
        </span>
        <p class="description">
         Will Fujitsu ever actually make the Flexbook? Probably not, but that doesn't make the wait for real flexible laptops and tablets any easier.
        </p>
       </header>
       <section class="social social-article">
        <ul>
         <li class="facebook">
          <div class="fb-like" data-font="arial" data-layout="button_count" data-send="true" data-show-faces="false">

         <li class="google-plus">
          <div class="g-plusone" data-count="true" data-size="medium">

         </li>
        </ul>
       </section>
       <section class="article-content parchment-content">
        <p>
         <span id="internal-source-marker_0.5507683913887765">
          The slow crawl of flexible OLEDs towards commercial viability isnt getting any easier to bear when concept devices like the
         </span>
         <a href="../../../../news/flip-concept-phone-makes-the-case-for-3-screens/1677/" rel="nofollow">
          triple-screened Flip phone
         </a>
         pop up every couple months. The latest comes from Fujitsu in the form of the bright and bubbly
         <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2011/05/12/flexbook-concept-serves-up-candy-coated-convertible/" rel="nofollow">
          Flexbook
         </a>
         , which is as dexterous as its name implies: the Flexbook can transform from a miniature laptop with a 21:9 touchscreen to a slate form with its keyboard hidden.
        </p>
        <div class="embed-cage embed-type-image embed-position-center" data-id="24412" data-large-url="http://files.tested.com/photos/2012/03/17/55-24412-flexbook_teaser.jpg" data-small-url="http://files.tested.com/photos/2012/03/17/56-24412-flexbook_teaser.jpg">
         <a href="http://files.tested.com/uploads/0/5/24412-flexbook_teaser.jpg">
          <img data-sizes='{"320": "http://files.tested.com/photos/2013/08/28/61-24412-flexbook_teaser-1377707752.jpg", "575": "http://files.tested.com/photos/2013/08/28/70-24412-flexbook_teaser-1377707755.jpg", "480": "http://files.tested.com/photos/2013/08/28/62-24412-flexbook_teaser-1377707753.jpg"}' src="http://files.tested.com/photos/2012/03/17/55-24412-flexbook_teaser.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Thanks to that bendable screen, the Flipbook can fold over on itself to take on the shape of a book or protectively clam up for travel. Fujitsus singled out the Flexbook as one of the finalists for its
         <a href="http://www.designboom.com/weblog/cat/8/view/14584/flexbook-by-hao-chun-huang-fujitsu-design-award-2011-shortlisted-entry.html" rel="nofollow">
          A Life with Future Computing
         </a>
         award. Check out more renders below--would you buy a Flexbook if Fujitsu could get it on the market for the same price as, say, an
         <a href="../../../../news/asus-eee-pad-transformer-transforms-runs-honeycomb/2070/" rel="nofollow">
          Eee Pad Transformer
         </a>
         ?
        </p>
