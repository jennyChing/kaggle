             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://carmen.buzzbuzzhome.com/2010/11/old-condo-renovation-or-new-condo.html">
               OLD CONDO RENOVATION OR NEW CONDO?
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <a class="twitter-share-button" data-count="vertical" href="http://twitter.com/share">
               Tweet
              </a>
              <script src="http://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" type="text/javascript">
              </script>
              <div class="separator" style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none; clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhpvvya5jI/AAAAAAAAAkE/5e9E99L5_e8/s1600/old+condo.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; cssfloat: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="149" px="true" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhpvvya5jI/AAAAAAAAAkE/5e9E99L5_e8/s200/old+condo.jpg" width="200"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                M
               </span>
               ost
               <strong>
                old condos
               </strong>
               show their age and when purchasing an old condo there are few advantages- one would be the
               <strong>
                size of the rooms
               </strong>
               which in some cases are larger than in newer condos and the location- some of the older condos are in great locations in the city.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                However, there are many
                <strong>
                 factors to consider
                </strong>
                when buying or selling an old condo, and one I came across very often lately is the renovation aspect. There are mostly two categories:
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               1. People want to
               <strong>
                move in and renovate their condo
               </strong>
               so it looks like new or so it sells better in few years, however when they get down to making the list and getting quotes from contractors they end up with five or six digit numbers!
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                2. People want to
                <strong>
                 move out and renovate it
                </strong>
                for a
                <strong>
                 better selling price
                </strong>
                .
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                Before you buy an old condo make sure you obtain all available documentation from the Condo Board (your real estate agent will know exactly what documents are required). You need to know the history of that condo suite. Many times condo owners take on their own renovation projects without letting the condo board know and this affects you if you move into a unit thats doesnt look like on the floor plan. You should also provide the condo board with all paperwork required before you start a renovation so the next owner will know what changes have been made.
               </span>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               Lets start with the 2nd category: of
               <strong>
                condo owners renovating for a better selling price
               </strong>
               . Depending on the timing you may actually consider to drop the price and let the future owner renovate according to their own taste.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                At the end of the day it may cost you about the same amount unless your condo is in a really bad shape.
                <strong>
                 Renovations dont only cost money
                </strong>
                ;
                <strong>
                 it takes time and patience
                </strong>
                of living in an under construction space and it will definitely affect your lifestyle during the renovation.
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                <strong>
                 Bathroom renovations
                </strong>
                would be the first area to tackle where new plumbing fixtures alone may help. Next in line would be tiles upgrade- you can read more about bathroom renovations in two previous blog posts
                <a href="http://carmen.buzzbuzzhome.com/2010/07/condo-bathroom-to-upgrade-or-not-to.html" target="_blank">
                 here
                </a>
                and
                <a href="http://carmen.buzzbuzzhome.com/2010/10/finally-time-to-renovate-your-condo.html" target="_blank">
                 here
                </a>
                .
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
                <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhrmAxBylI/AAAAAAAAAkU/fpNX_O6bQGY/s1600/old+bedroom.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; cssfloat: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="148" px="true" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhrmAxBylI/AAAAAAAAAkU/fpNX_O6bQGY/s200/old+bedroom.jpg" width="200"/>
                </a>
                <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhqfwrJ6CI/AAAAAAAAAkI/ErwN8raJu3M/s1600/old+parquet.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; cssfloat: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="199" px="true" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhqfwrJ6CI/AAAAAAAAAkI/ErwN8raJu3M/s200/old+parquet.jpg" width="200"/>
                </a>
                <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                 <strong>
                  Flooringthroughout
                 </strong>
                 is the next item I would consider. It is a larger investment but done properly it could create the look of a brand new space! If your current floors are carpet- definitely upgrade to engineered orlaminate flooring.If your floors are the old type of 6x6 parquet, you can just consider re-staining the entire floor in a darker color which will give a
                 <strong>
                  more up to date look
                 </strong>
                 .
                </span>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
                <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                 One item that is often forgotten is the
                 <strong>
                  baseboard
                 </strong>
                 . Most old condos come with a 3 baseboard or even a 2 . Many retail stores carry inexpensive paint grade mdf or wood baseboards and depending on your ceiling height you can consider at least a 4 if not a 5 baseboard.
                </span>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhq4xzN_NI/AAAAAAAAAkM/WRjKlXG-8zQ/s1600/old+kitchen.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; cssfloat: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="159" px="true" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhq4xzN_NI/AAAAAAAAAkM/WRjKlXG-8zQ/s200/old+kitchen.jpg" width="200"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                Next- I would
                <strong>
                 consider renovating the kitchen
                </strong>
                . Upgrading the
                <strong>
                 appliances
                </strong>
                would be a must and with all great discounts offered by most retails stores, it may be an easy upgrade to consider.
               </span>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                Most old condos kitchens do not have an open space layout. Is this a reason to knock down walls and reconfigure the space in order to create an open concept? Maybemaybe not!
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               If the living/dining area is large enough you dont necessarily need to change the layout. Having a
               <strong>
                closed in kitchen
               </strong>
               offers a different type of lifestyle- it may not be large enough for entertaining guests while you prepare or just organize meals during a party.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhrVIimc_I/AAAAAAAAAkQ/gV9ZswmG6tM/s1600/arclinea-kitchens.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; cssfloat: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="142" px="true" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNhrVIimc_I/AAAAAAAAAkQ/gV9ZswmG6tM/s320/arclinea-kitchens.jpg" width="320"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                The
                <strong>
                 new open concept kitchens
                </strong>
                are designed for a different
                <strong>
                 lifestyle
                </strong>
                , one that incorporates meals preparation and spending time with family and friends. However, if you absolutely detest having the smell of cooked food going into the living area (I have met people who never wanted the smell of cooked food in any room!), you may consider keeping kitchen as a separate room.
               </span>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                For the 1st category my advice is:
                <strong>
                 renovate for yourself first
                </strong>
                and then think of resale. If you just moved in, change the items that you absolutely must! If you havent moved in, perhaps talk to a contractor and get an idea of how much you would need to invest to create the condo of your dreams, you may end up knowing exactly how much you will end up spending and you may choose a new condo.
               </span>
              </div>
              <div style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
               <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNiPe2vapfI/AAAAAAAAAkc/UKF_NZNItGs/s1600/on+the+fence.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; cssfloat: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="150" px="true" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_7HXNFDj5EYs/TNiPe2vapfI/AAAAAAAAAkc/UKF_NZNItGs/s200/on+the+fence.jpg" width="200"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <span style='font-family: "Trebuchet MS", sans-serif;'>
               So
               <strong>
                is it worth to move in an old condo and renovate
               </strong>
               ? It may be! If you like the location, the neighbours, the common areas look, the monthly condo fees, the size, design and functionality of the amenity spaces, the size of your condo, the ceiling height, the size of the windows (sometimes smaller in older condos vs floor to ceiling, wall to wall in new condos) the view and the price (after you considered how much you would need to invest in renovating it) then it looks like a great find! If not, there are plenty of new condos to choose from,
               <strong>
                the number of New Developments are constantly increasing
               </strong>
               , click
               <a href="http://www.buzzbuzzhome.com/Cities.aspx" target="_blank">
