          <h1>
           A Gamer's Valentine: Video Games for Couples to Enjoy Together
          </h1>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <time datetime="2014-02-11T10:54:57-06:00">
             February 11, 2014
            </time>
           </li>
           <li>
            Written by
            <a href="/contributors/jordan-maison.html" rel="author">
             Jordan Maison
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span>
             Published in
            </span>
            <a href="/games-culture.html">
             Gamer Culture
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </header>
         <div class="itemImageBlock">
          <a class="itemImage modal" href="/media/k2/items/cache/46fbede812ae82a3cc8923acb58f8923_XL.jpg" rel="{handler: 'image'}" title="Click to preview image">
           <img alt="A Gamer&#039;s Valentine: Video Games for Couples to Enjoy Together" src="/media/k2/items/cache/46fbede812ae82a3cc8923acb58f8923_L.jpg" style="width:670px; height:auto;"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="itemBody">
          <div class="itemIntroText">
           <p>
            Despite the growth of video games and it's general inclusion into the mainstream culture, when asked, most people still envision gamers as being lonely introverts sitting and staring at the screen. That's simply not that case, and many gamers (both male and female) live happy lives with their signicant others. In the spirit of the romantic holiday I've decided to take a look at the best games for couples to play together on Valentine's Day (or any day really)!
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="itemFullText">
           <p>
            <em>
             Each month Cinelinx will be gearing up for a new topic and cover a variety of movies that fall into that topic. You may have seen it already for the movie side of the site, and now its time for the video game side of the site. With that being said, this month is all about
             <a href="/games-culture/item/5232-a-gamers-valentine-you-d-make-a-great-couple-said-no-one.html" target="_blank">
              Valentine's Day
             </a>
             ! If you are like us, you are probably a nerd or geek and just went ugh Valentine's Day. Dont worry we have you covered here. We are not covering mushy gushy love stories, we are celebrating as nerds! As nerds we will make fun of the love stories and have fun with it.
            </em>
           </p>
           <hr/>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Games are always better when you play with someone else. It makes for a more enjoyable experience and something you can share down the road (just look at how prominent multiplayer gaming has become). What's better than that? Playing with the person you care about most! It's always important to spend quality time with your loved ones, so why not do so while also playing some great games?
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="portal-2-coop-hug" height="281" src="/images/jmaison/Editorials/Games_for_Couples/portal-2-coop-hug.jpg" width="500"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <strong>
              Portal 2
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            While everyone enjoyed the solo adventures Valve offered up in the first couple of
            <strong>
             Portal
            </strong>
            games, with it's mind-bending puzzles and quirky story, there's lots more fun to be had in when you play
            <strong>
             Portal 2
            </strong>
            's multiplayer experience. The game challenges players to work together in unique ways in order to solve harder puzzles, but the satisfaction in successfully completing a level is nearly unmatched.
           </p>
           <p>
            It's a great game to play as a couple, as it's level designs will have you working together and thinking in ways you might not normally consider. It's an excellent teamwork exercise, getting you and your partner in sync with one another. It'll allow you to reap the rewards of a job well done in the end, that is, if you don't kill one another in the process of getting there!
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="scottpilgrimvstheworldthegame screenshot world4 fightinthegarden" height="281" src="/images/jmaison/Editorials/Games_for_Couples/scottpilgrimvstheworldthegame_screenshot_world4_fightinthegarden.jpg" width="500"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <strong>
              Scott Pilgrim vs. The World
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            I already talked about the movie over on
            <a href="/movie-stuff/item/5261-rom-com-refelction-scott-pilgrim-vs-the-world.html">
             Movielinx earlier this week
            </a>
            , but I feel like the video game adaptation deserves a mention here as well. Done in the style of old school 16-bit sidescrolling beat 'em ups, the
            <strong>
             Scott Pilgrim
            </strong>
            game is just as much fun as the books and movie it's based off of. With fun action, skill trees and the ability to level up, it goes far beyond a basic action game. But just like the retro titles it's designed like, it's way more fun to play with a someone else.
           </p>
           <p>
            You and your loved one can face down the hordes of enemies together, and ultimately prove that love can seriously conquer all. If that's not romantic, I don't know what is! It's just a damn fun game and doesn't require a great deal of skill to enjoy (though its system does have nuances to master if you so choose) meaning if your partner isn't a gamer they can still button mash their way through without falling behind or getting frustrated.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="LEGO-Marvel-Super-Heroes" height="250" src="/images/jmaison/Editorials/Games_for_Couples/LEGO-Marvel-Super-Heroes.jpg" width="500"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <strong>
              The LEGO Game Franchise
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            The LEGO games (from
            <strong>
             Star Wars
            </strong>
            to
            <strong>
             Marvel Super Heroes
            </strong>
            ) have done a lot of things right, and top among them is how they integrate drop in and out co-op play into every title. Every level is riddled with hidden objects for gamers to find, and many of them are tough to reach unless you have a second person play with you. More than just that, they're fun worlds to explore and simply run around in, with a sense of humor that everyone can enjoy.
           </p>
           <p>
            What makes it so perfect for couples is it's ease of entry. The
            <strong>
             LEGO
            </strong>
            games aren't exactly hard. Characters can't actually die and the levels aren't so confusing that someone could get lost in them. Thus it's fun to just roam around and interact with the world without worrying about messing up the other player. So even if your partner isn't into gaming, it's an easy game for them to get into and not feel like they're slowing you down.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="130910 feature rockband rockband1" height="278" src="/images/jmaison/Editorials/Games_for_Couples/130910_feature_rockband_rockband1.jpg" width="500"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <strong>
              Rock Band
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Teamwork is the cornerstone of any great relationship, and few games encourage that in the same way as
            <strong>
             Rock Band
            </strong>
            . The game does a great job of rewarding you when you're in sync with one another; pumping up the crowd and rating your overall performance. No matter how many times you've played through a song, you feel the compulsion to try again, doing better and better every time.
           </p>
           <p>
            It's easy to lose hours and hours of play time in this game with your significant other, because even when you fail it's still a lot of fun. It's easy enough to laugh (at one another) when you fumble up a certain segment, and be silly with the instrument peripherals the game comes with. While it's an older title now and no longer supported with extra tracks that doesn't make it any less enjoyable with plenty of songs that both you and your partner will love.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="mario and peach married 3" height="375" src="/images/jmaison/Editorials/Games_for_Couples/mario_and_peach_married_3.jpg" width="500"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <strong>
              The Mario Franchises
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Super Mario
            </strong>
            has gone well beyond it's platforming roots and spawned a ridiculous amount of spin-offs; all of which are incredibly fun to play. From Karting, Parties, Brawls, and all manner of sports, just about every title is designed to bring players together and be enjoyed with more than one person. While many have decried the various titles for sticking with older formulas, there's no denying how fun these games are.
           </p>
           <p>
            The sports and karting games are excellent for pick-up-and-play options if you want to do something quick and fun with your significant other. The latest platformers (
            <strong>
             New Super Mario Bros.
            </strong>
            for Wii and Wii U, and the recent
            <strong>
             Super Mario 3D World
            </strong>
            ) are excellent long play options that can provide tons of fun for all players, while also giving you the chance to play with another couple for even more fun! Pretty much, if Mario is on the cover you're in for a time that may be simple, but ridiculously fun.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="Retro Games" height="313" src="/images/jmaison/Editorials/Games_for_Couples/Retro_Games.jpg" width="500"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <strong>
              The Classics
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            There's a reason retro games continue to resonate with people today: they're so damn fun! Back then, games were designed with the idea to be played with another person in the same room, only feet apart from one another. Who doesn't remember sitting down and playing with friends, cheering one another on, passing the controller around, or trying desperately to save one another's lives from a particularly tough boss?
           </p>
           <p>
            That sense of camaraderie and cooperation is one of the most endearing aspects of older titles every time you play one, that feeling returns. Why not enjoy that with your closest companion? Modern consoles now offer a wealth of retro titles to download and replay once again, with the multiplayer still intact. I could spend an entire day listing out the various great two-player games of eras gone bye, but if there's a classic game you love, chances are it'll be just as fun to enjoy with your loved one!
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <strong>
              Runner Ups:
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Just Dance
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Little Big Planet
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Wii Sports
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Halo
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             StarCraft
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Injustice: Gods Among Us
            </strong>
            (Get some of that aggression out!)
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             DC Universe Online
            </strong>
           </p>
           <hr/>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Valentine's Day is all about being together and enjoying the company of someone special. While some people feel this means a fancy dinner and what not, what's important is doing what you enjoy with one another. If sitting on the couch and playing games will make this Valentine's Day special to you and yours, then there's no reason not to enjoy some fun virtual times together!
           </p>
           <p>
            These are just some of my personal suggestions on what to play together, but we'd love to hear yours added to this list. Be sure to let us know what games you play with your loved ones in the comments below!
           </p>
           <p>
            -Jordan
           </p>
