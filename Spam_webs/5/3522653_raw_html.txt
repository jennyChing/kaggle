     <p>
      <b>
       Travis Brandon Richter
      </b>
      (born November 3, 1981 in
      <a href="/wiki/Albany,_Georgia" title="Albany, Georgia">
       Albany, Georgia
      </a>
      ) is an
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       American
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Musician" title="Musician">
       musician
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_producer" title="Music producer">
       music producer
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Dubstep" title="Dubstep">
       dubstep
      </a>
      producer and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vocalist" title="Vocalist">
       vocalist
      </a>
      . He is known for being the original vocalist and
      <a href="/wiki/Guitarist" title="Guitarist">
       guitarist
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/From_First_to_Last" title="From First to Last">
       From First to Last
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Lead_vocalist" title="Lead vocalist">
       lead vocalist
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/The_Color_of_Violence" title="The Color of Violence">
       The Color of Violence
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/The_Human_Abstract_(band)" title="The Human Abstract (band)">
       The Human Abstract
      </a>
      . He is also a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_producer" title="Music producer">
       music producer
      </a>
      and produces electronic music with the group Modified Drugs.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">
       Musical career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="From_First_to_Last_.282002.E2.80.932010.2C_2013-present.29">
       From First to Last (20022010, 2013-present)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Travis_Richter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: From First to Last (20022010, 2013-present)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Richter was the original guitarist/screamer of
      <a href="/wiki/Post-hardcore" title="Post-hardcore">
       post-hardcore
      </a>
      band
      <a href="/wiki/From_First_to_Last" title="From First to Last">
       From First to Last
      </a>
      , and was featured on every release by the band, both albums and music videos. After being with the band from the beginning,
      <a href="/wiki/From_First_to_Last" title="From First to Last">
       From First to Last
      </a>
      announced that Richter and the band had parted ways due to artistic differences; Not long after his departure, the band announced their indefinite hiatus after eight years of touring and four full-length albums. Upon the bands reformation, Richter rejoined the band as guitarist and scream vocals.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Color_of_Violence_.282002.E2.80.932003.2C_2006.E2.80.93present.29">
       The Color of Violence (20022003, 2006present)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Travis_Richter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Color of Violence (20022003, 2006present)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Richter formed
      <a href="/wiki/Grindcore" title="Grindcore">
       grindcore
      </a>
      band
      <a href="/wiki/The_Color_of_Violence" title="The Color of Violence">
       The Color of Violence
      </a>
      with future members of
      <a href="/wiki/From_First_to_Last" title="From First to Last">
       From First to Last
      </a>
      , including current
      <a href="/wiki/Guitarist" title="Guitarist">
       guitarist
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vocalist" title="Vocalist">
       vocalist
      </a>
      Matt Good and drummer Derek Bloom, as well as former bassist Joe Antillion, and his friend Chad Crews on guitar. The band started as a full-time band from 2002 to 2003, but the all of the members, except for Crews, decided to focus on
      <a href="/wiki/From_First_to_Last" title="From First to Last">
       From First to Last
      </a>
      . However, in 2006, Richter and Bloom decided to restart the band as a two-man duo, with Richter under the alias of Guy Nucleosity and Bloom went under the alias of Glitch Killgasm. The two released their debut full-length album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Youthanize" title="Youthanize">
        Youthanize
       </a>
      </i>
      , on April 7, 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The band said that "[Youthanize] may not be groundbreaking, and it may not sell any copies, but we tried to be ourselves and experiment, and we're all really happy with the way it turned out..."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2008, Richter helped produced
      <a href="/wiki/Post-hardcore" title="Post-hardcore">
       post-hardcore
      </a>
      group
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Set_My_Friends_On_Fire" title="I Set My Friends On Fire">
       I Set My Friends On Fire
      </a>
      's debut album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/You_Can%27t_Spell_Slaughter_Without_Laughter" title="You Can't Spell Slaughter Without Laughter">
        You Can't Spell Slaughter Without Laughter
       </a>
      </i>
      , along with main producer Lee Dyess of Earthsound Recordings and additional production with former band member Nabil Moo. The band recorded their second album with Dyess in early to mid-2010, but several factors, including Moo leaving the band, pushed production to Los Angeles, California where Richter ultimately produced the album in late 2010 to early 2011. This album, entitled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Astral_Rejection" title="Astral Rejection">
        Astral Rejection
       </a>
      </i>
      was released on June 21, 2011.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Human_Abstract_.282010.E2.80.932011.2C_2012.E2.80.93present.29">
       The Human Abstract (20102011, 2012present)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Travis_Richter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Human Abstract (20102011, 2012present)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Richter became the third lead vocalist of the
      <a href="/wiki/Progressive_metal" title="Progressive metal">
       progressive metal
      </a>
      band
      <a href="/wiki/The_Human_Abstract_(band)" title="The Human Abstract (band)">
       The Human Abstract
      </a>
      , replacing Nathan Ells. He joined recently after his departure of
      <a href="/wiki/From_First_to_Last" title="From First to Last">
       From First to Last
      </a>
      . His membership was confirmed in an interview with
      <a href="/wiki/AbsolutePunk" title="AbsolutePunk">
       AbsolutePunk
      </a>
      , where he stated that he was contacted by guitarist Andrew Tapley while he was departing from First to Last.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
