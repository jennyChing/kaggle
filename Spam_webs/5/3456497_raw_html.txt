       <h1>
        The best Android feature youre not using: voice commands
       </h1>
      </div>
      <div id="primary">
       <div class="article clearfix">
        <div class="post-141491 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-android-101 category-android-how-to category-featured category-tips-tricks category-video tag-ok-google apps-google-now" id="post-141491">

          <p>
           <img alt="ok-google" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-141592 aligncenter colorbox-141491" height="147" src="http://phandroid.s3.amazonaws.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/ok-google-640x147.png" width="640"/>
          </p>

          <p>
           Google Now has become a central part of the Android operating system, introducing new functions and capabilities with every candy-flavored iteration. While voice commands have been core to the service since its launch, Google has done their part to continuously add new options regularly. Did we mention its now easier than ever to control your phone just by speaking to it? As of the latest Android KitKat update,users can jump right into Google Now simply by saying OK, Google at any time.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <div class="vid">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="360" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/0d02ZbLko30" width="640">
           </iframe>
          </div>
          <p>
           If you havent checked out voice commands in a while or are not acquainted with some of the newer (and very useful functions), here is a sampling of what you can do. Simply replace the text inbrackets with whatever you are searching for or inquiring about andwait for Google to spit out the pertinent information.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Essential commands
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Go to [Phandroid.com].
           </li>
           <li>
            Search for [
            <!--SHR -->
            <a href="http://phandroid.com/samsung-galaxy-s5/" target="_blank">
             Samsung Galaxy S5
            </a>
            <!-- EHR -->
            ].
           </li>
           <li>
            Open [Gmail].
           </li>
           <li>
            Take a picture.
           </li>
           <li>
            Record a video.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           Productivity
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Remind me to [call John at 6PM].
           </li>
           <li>
            Remind me to [buy toilet paper at Target].
           </li>
           <li>
            Set an alarm for [6PM].
           </li>
           <li>
            Create a calendar event: [Happy Hour atUnion onFriday at 5:00PM].
           </li>
           <li>
            Wheres my package?
           </li>
           <li>
            Note to self: [remember to buy milk].
           </li>
           <li>
            Whats the tip for [$42]?
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2 style="color: #444444;">
           Communications
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Call [George Smith].
           </li>
           <li>
            Call [Mom mobile].
           </li>
           <li>
            Text [Leethat Im running 5 minutes late].
           </li>
           <li>
            Send email to [Rob Jackson, subject, Google Now, message, Check out these Voice Commands].
           </li>
           <li>
            Listen to voicemail.
           </li>
           <li>
            Find [Bruces]number.
           </li>
           <li>
            When is [Marys] birthday?
           </li>
           <li>
            Post to [Google+]: [Google Now is awesome].
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           Media
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Listen to: [TheDirty Projectors].
           </li>
           <li>
            Play: [The Beatles].
           </li>
           <li>
            Whats this song?
           </li>
           <li>
            Play some music.
           </li>
           <li>
            Watch [Toy Story 3].
           </li>
           <li>
            What movies are playing tonight?
           </li>
           <li>
            Where is [X-Men] playing?
           </li>
           <li>
            Show me pictures of the [Chesapeake BayBridge].
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           Navigation and Travel
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Navigate to [Ocean City, MD].
           </li>
           <li>
            [Biking] directions to [The Brewers Art].
           </li>
           <li>
            Find [the Eiffel Tower].
           </li>
           <li>
            Where is [the Empire State Building]?
           </li>
           <li>
            Wheres the nearest [ice cream shop]?
           </li>
           <li>
            Show me the menu for [Holy Frijoles].
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="color: #444444;">
             Call [Ledos
            </span>
            Pizza].
           </li>
           <li>
            Show me my flights.
           </li>
           <li>
            Where is my hotel?
           </li>
           <li>
            What are some attractions in [Baltimore]?
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2 style="color: #444444;">
           Information
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            What time is it in [London]?
           </li>
           <li>
            Do I need an umbrella today?
           </li>
           <li>
            Whats the weather in [Cape Cod] for [the weekend]?
           </li>
           <li>
            Where was [Albert Einstein] born?
           </li>
           <li>
            How old is [Brad Pitt]?
           </li>
           <li>
            How do you say [hello] in [Spanish]?
           </li>
           <li>
            What does [onomatopoeia] mean?
           </li>
           <li>
            Whats [Googles] stock price?
           </li>
           <li>
            What is [Twitter] trading at?
           </li>
           <li>
            Whats [16 ounces] in [pounds]?
           </li>
           <li>
            What is the square root of 2209?
           </li>
           <li>
            When is sunset?
           </li>
           <li>
            Did the [Orioles] win today?
           </li>
           <li>
            How did [the San Francisco Giants]do?
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Then, of course, Google has also included some Easter Eggs. Try telling Google Now to Do a barrel roll, or asking What does the fox say? for a giggle or two. And dont forget, many if not all of these commands should work just as well with
           <!--SHR -->
           <a href="http://phandroid.com/google-glass/" target="_blank">
            Google Glass
           </a>
           <!-- EHR -->
           and Googles web voice search, as well.
          </p>
          <p>
           What else can you do with Google Now? Let us know your favorite voice commands, Easter Eggs, tips, and tricks in the comments below!

                   <div class="dsq-comment-body" id="dsq-comment-body-823956">
                    <div class="dsq-comment-message" id="dsq-comment-message-823956">
                     <p>
                      the key bit of my comment was the hardware required to do this without burning the battery quickly
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      and yes, specialized hardware is REQUIRED for this task as the alternative is to use the CPU/GPU to process the audio, and those are several orders of magnitude more power-hungry than even the first gen voice processing asics.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      the moto X includes such an asic, a custom-designed Natural Language Processor core  (source:
                      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moto_X#Specifications" rel="nofollow">
                       http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moto_X#Specifications
                      </a>
                      )
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      the note 3 does as well, as i understand it, but the core is simply left unused.
                      <br/>
                      such a core provides the ability to put the apps processor to sleep (along with the display, sram, flash, etc) and STILL be listening. at that moment the listening feature would be 100% hardware (absolutely no kernel module required for this bit) and with a specialized core purpose-built for this the current draw can be very low.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      when audio is received by the low-power digital mic it is sent to this core; when this core receives the audio it samples it, performs some transforms, then runs an algorithm to determine if the audio sample matches any of a predefined set of signatures. (such as, ok google now)
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      if the audio does NOT match, nothing happens and the mics just keep sending the next snippet of audio to the core over and over again. if it DOES match, on the other hand, the core continues to pre-process the audio while waking the apps processor and launching a speech recognition app (which MIGHT rely on a kernel module, but as a fan of microkernels id personally rather see it elsewhere). pre-processed audio is sent to that app which in turn performs a deep analysis on it (potentially sending it to googles speech recognition engine online).
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      to do this WITHOUT specialized hardware would require that the apps processor be left running at all times the phone should be listening, which means it would be a horrible design that drains the battery rather quickly even when youre not saying the launch phrase. that alone is a deal breaker for me, but adding insult to injury is the fact that youd have to implement the full pre-processing algorithm in software. you might be able to offload to the GPU (allowing the apps processor to at least remain in its lowest power active state), but if youve got a high-end phone the GPU is likely very power hungry too, meaning that to process always listening data youd have to burn power on both the cpu and the gpu, neither of which are perfectly ideal for this task (consume orders of magnitude more power than required for the task if only you had some specialized hardware available)
                     </p>
                    </div>
                   </div>
