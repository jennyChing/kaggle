        <h1 class="entry-title post-title" itemprop="name">
         Natural Pest Control In An Organic Garden
        </h1>
        <span content="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/03/organic-pest-control-150x150.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl">
        </span>
        <span content="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/03/organic-pest-control-1024x801.jpg" itemprop="image">
        </span>
        <span content="Kat Yares" itemprop="author">
        </span>
        <span content="March 11, 2007" itemprop="dateCreated">
        </span>
        <span content="Gardening Know How" itemprop="publisher">
        </span>
        <span content="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/special/organic/natural-pest-control-in-an-organic-garden.htm" itemprop="url">
        </span>
        <span content="Organic Gardens" itemprop="about">
        </span>
        <span content="Walk into any garden store and you will find tons of chemicals to control pests in the garden. You can spend hundreds of dollars for these products. What works and what doesnt? Read here to learn more." itemprop="description">
        </span>
        <div class="post-meta">
        </div>
        <!-- end of .post-meta -->
        <div class="post-entry entry-content">
         <div class="singmig">
          <img alt="organic-pest-control" class="attachment-medium wp-post-image" height="313" src="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/03/organic-pest-control-400x313.jpg" width="400"/>
          <div class="imageby">
           Image by
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/wonderferret/2186620508/" rel="nofollow">
            wonderferret
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div id="main-art" itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           <span id="authorname">
            By Kat Yares
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Walk into any garden store and you will find shelf after shelf of chemicals to help control the pests in your garden. You can spend hundreds of dollars for these products every season. Not this year. Youve decided to go organic instead. You know this means that you wont be using those chemicals with unpronounceable names.
          </p>
          <p>
           Youre going to be using natural ingredients and nature itself to keep your garden pest free. So, the question is: what works and what doesnt? Keep reading to learn more about
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/special/organic/natural-home-pesticides-organic-garden-pest-control.htm">
            natural pest control
           </a>
           in an organic garden.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Tips for Natural Pest Control
          </h2>
          <p>
           The best defense against garden pests is good soil and healthy plants. Following that, simple garden protection includes items you can safely use to deter pests as well as the addition of certain plants that repel insect pests or attract the predators which feed on them.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Healthy Soil and Plants
          </h3>
          <p>
           Always
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/vegetables/vgen/rotating-vegetables.htm">
            rotate crops
           </a>
           so that nothing is growing in the same spot that it did last year. Begin your organic garden by working in
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/special/organic/compost-for-organic-gardens.htm">
            compost
           </a>
           to fertilize the soil. You cannot add too much compost to your garden.
          </p>
          <div class="adhar">
           <span class="adtext">
            Advertisement
           </span>
           <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
           </script>
           <!-- GKH 336 In article ad -->
           <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-2380209421431791" data-ad-slot="3651568248" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
           </ins>
           <script>
            (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
           </script>
          </div>
          <p>
           If you plan to use
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/garden-how-to/seeds/f1-hybrid-seeds.htm">
            hybrid seeds
           </a>
           , instead of
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/vegetables/vgen/heirloom-vegetables.htm">
            heirloom
           </a>
           , choose seeds and plants that are bred to resist pests. Each year, more and more varieties of vegetables are being developed that are pest and disease resistant.
          </p>
          <p>
           Cull out any plant that looks unhealthy, as a sick plant only invites unwanted guests to your garden. A sick or diseased plant will not produce as well as a healthy plant, so you are not losing anything by pulling it from the ground.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Natural Garden Deterrents
          </h3>
          <p>
           Fine mesh netting, available from your garden center, is your next line of defense. By placing the netting over the plants, you protect the plant from flying insects,
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/plant-problems/pests/animals/garden-mouse-control.htm">
            mice
           </a>
           and other varmints. Netting is the preferred preventative for vegetables such as
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/vegetables/cabbage/growing-cabbage.htm">
            cabbage
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/vegetables/lettuce/growing-lettuce.htm">
            lettuce
           </a>
           and other leafy produce.
          </p>
          <p>
           Protecting young vegetable plants from worms and
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/plant-problems/pests/insects/kill-garden-slugs.htm">
            slugs
           </a>
           can be accomplished by using old soda pop bottles. These can be either the single serve or the two liter type. Simply cut out the top and bottom of the bottle and place around the plant.
          </p>
          <p>
           Another method of organic pest control is
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/vegetables/vgen/companion-vegetable-garden.htm">
            companion planting
           </a>
           . By planting annuals, such as
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/ornamental/flowers/marigold/growing-marigold-flowers.htm">
            marigolds
           </a>
           and California poppies, in and among your vegetable crop, youwill help attract
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/plant-problems/pests/insects/beneficial-insects.htm">
            beneficial insects
           </a>
           to your garden. These beneficial insects, like the
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/garden-how-to/beneficial/attract-ladybugs.htm">
            ladybug
           </a>
           , eat not the plant, but other insects. Some plants, such as
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/herbs/wormwood/wormwood-plant-growing-sweet-annie.htm">
            wormwood
           </a>
           , give off an odor that many pests do not like and will cause them to go to someone elses garden.
          </p>
          <p>
           Many organic gardeners plant hot peppers, like
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/vegetables/pepper/growing-chili-pepper-plants.htm">
            chili peppers
           </a>
           ,throughout their garden. The capsaicin in the pepper plants deters many insects from biting on the plants near them. Using hot pepper sprays on the vegetable plants themselves will also send many bugs elsewhere for their dinner. Hot peppers should not be planted nearcrops such as
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/fruits/melons/growing-melons.htm">
            melons
           </a>
           , however, because they may pick up the flavor of the pepper.
          </p>
          <p>
           Another trick to try, especially for
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/plant-problems/pests/insects/homemade-aphid-control.htm">
            aphids
           </a>
           , is a mixture of water and bleach-free dish soap or other detergent. Spray the leaves of the plants lightly and it should destroy the little annoying insects.
          </p>
          <p>
           It may be easier to just grab a bottle of pesticide from the store shelf, but for the healthiest, purest, freshest tasting vegetables, organic is the way to go. You may have to put forth a tad bit more effort, but when you know you can safely grab that
           <a href="http://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/vegetables/tomato/tips-for-growing-tomatoes.htm">
            tomato
           </a>
           off the vine and eat it right there, then youll know why organic is the best way to go.
          </p>
