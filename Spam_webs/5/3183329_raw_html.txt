      <h2>
       Crochet Pattern:  Faux Drop-Stitch Scarf
      </h2>
      By Candace 
      <a href="http://www.crochetspot.com/crochet-pattern-faux-drop-stitch-scarf/#comments">
       7 Comments
      </a>
      <div class="entry">
       <table>
        <tr>
         <td>
          While thinking about the problem of replicating the knitted drop-stitch effect in crochet, I hit upon an idea.  Solomons Knot might work if it was stacked instead of worked as a net.  Fortunately for those of us who like the look of strategically dropped knit stitches, it does work, and once you get past the foundation row, its easy for an experienced crocheter to work up.  If you get stuck, remember to check out the links in the Special Instructions section to get an idea of how the stitches should be made.
         </td>
         <td width="300">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-13013" height="225" src="http://www.crochetspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/crochet_faux_drop_stitch_scarf-300x225.jpg" title="crochet_faux_drop_stitch_scarf" width="300"/>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
       <p>
        <span id="more-13001">
        </span>
       </p>
       <table>
        <tr>
         <td>
          <strong>
           Skill Level:
          </strong>
         </td>
         <td width="130">
          <img src="http://crochetspot.com/wp-content/uploads/03-intermediate%5bsmall%5d.jpg"/>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Finished Size:
        </strong>
        6.5 (16.5 cm) wide, 54 (137 cm) long
       </p>
       <table>
        <tr>
         <td>
          <strong>
           Materials
          </strong>
          :
          <br/>
          Light Weight Yarn (125 yards)
          <br/>
          Crochet Hook E (3.50 mm)
         </td>
         <td valgin="bottom" width="50">
          <img src="http://www.crochetspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/04/2-finesmall.jpg"/>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Gauge:
        </strong>
        <br/>
        9 rows in pattern = 6 1/2 (16.5 cm) wide by 3 (7.5 cm) long
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Special Instructions:
        </strong>
        <br/>
        fsc=
        <a href="http://www.crochetspot.com/how-to-crochet-foundation-single-crochet-fsc/">
         Foundation single crochet
        </a>
        <br/>
        SK=
        <a href="http://www.crochetspot.com/how-to-crochet-knot-stitch/">
         Solomons Knot
        </a>
        (counts as 2 stitches, chain should be 1 inch long)
        <br/>
        Ech= Extended crochet chain (made as long as the ch in your Solomons Knot)
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Need help understanding the crochet abbreviations and symbols?
        </strong>
        Check out the
        <a href="http://www.crochetspot.com/crochet-abbreviations/">
         crochet abbreviation
        </a>
        chart.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Crochet Pattern: Faux Drop-Stitch Scarf
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Row 1: 4 fsc, (SK, 3 fsc) 3 times, fsc, turn: 19 sts
        <br/>
        Row 2: Ch 2, hdc in first 4 Fsc, (Ech, hdc in next 4 fsc) 3 times, turn: 19 sts
        <br/>
        Row 3: Ch 2, hdc in first 4 hdc, (Ech, hdc in next 4 hdc) 3 times, turn: 19 sts
       </p>
       <p>
        Repeat Row 3, 163 times or until desired length is reached.
       </p>
       <p>
        Finish off.
       </p>
       <p>
        Need help while crocheting? Feel free to leave a comment below and Ill help you out!
       </p>
