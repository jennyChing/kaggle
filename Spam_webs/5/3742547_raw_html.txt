         <h2>
          Urban Street Art Festival
         </h2>
         <h3>
          April 18, 10 a.m.-5 p.m.
          <br/>
          The Banana Factory
         </h3>
         <p>
          Break dancing, colorful body painting and the click-clack of rollerblades on concrete are some of the sights and sounds that attendees will discover at the brand-new Urban Street Art Festival at the Banana Factory community arts center this spring!
         </p>
         <p>
          At the Urban Street Art Festival, you can check out a variety of demonstrating artists, as well as create masterpieces of your own while working alongside some of the artists. The festival, which coincides with SouthSide Bethlehems annual Spring on 4th celebration, is an opportunity for the public to explore and experience a variety of unique art forms, with appearances by metal sculptor Steven Condra, graffiti artist Max Meano, body art painters from Funtastic Faces and Body Art, and more.
         </p>
         <p>
          Highlighting the event is the appearance of Jennifer Chaparro, an award-winning chalk illusion artist and director of the International Street Painting Society.  A resident of Florida, Chaparro creates amazing chalk scenes at festivals and art shows throughout the nation and has garnered major recognition and awards for her unique style of chalking. During the street art festival, she will join forces with Allentown artist Holly Fields-Scott of Bella Pintura, a local art and design firm, to transform the Banana Factory parking lot into a captivating chalk scene, as well as offer a free, family-friendly workshop on 3-D chalk art.
         </p>
