      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       To Undermine Paris Climate Talks, Stop EPA Clean Power Plan
      </h1>
      <!-- Make individual group author appear -->
      <div class="mninst">
      </div>
      <div class="comment_links">

      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         <!--donotpaginate-->
        </p>
        <p>
         Republicansmay be ableprevent an international climate deal at the UN Climate Change Conference in Parisif they canscuttle the Environmental Protection Agencys Clean Power Plan before the talks convene in December,an expert on environmental law said in ChicagoWednesday.
        </p>
        <p>
         Republicans in Congress will use riders on appropriations bills to try to choke off funding for the EPA Clean Power Plan and other rulesthat curb greenhouse-gas emissions, Michael Gerrard, director of the
         <a href="http://web.law.columbia.edu/climate-change" target="_blank">
          Sabin Center for Climate Change Law
         </a>
         atColumbia Law School, told about 50 people at the Universityof Chicago. The rules will also face an onslaught of lawsuits that will try to prevent EPA from regulating power plant emissions.
        </p>
        <p>
         If that happens before Paris, that would have disastrous implications for the international negotiations, he said, because its really U.S. participation that is helping to drive it, and if that is shut down, thats very bad news.
        </p>
        <p>
         Gerrard predictedPresident Obama will defend theClean Power Plan, which wouldregulate greenhouse gas emissions from power plants, but less knownregulations are not likely to survive the Republican majority.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Clean Power Plan is one of his signature issues, and hes said he would go to the mat on that, Gerard said.There are lots of other rules relating to greenhouse gases that are less prominent but are also important: rules on methane, on aircraft emissions, on a number of other things that he may not stand pat on.
        </p>
        <p>
         The new Republican majority in the Senate still lacks the 60 votes neededto repeal legislation, and itlacks the 67 votes they need to override a veto, so nomajor environmental legislation will pass or be repealed in the next two years, Gerrard predicted.
        </p>
        <p>
         But Republicanscan easily attach riders to spending bills that prohibit agencies from spending money on greenhouse-gas regulation. And ifappropriations for many agencies are consolidated and presented to the president for a single signature, the president has to either accept the riders, Gerrard said, or shut down the government.
        </p>
        <p>
         The greatest danger to U.S. climate action comes in the form of appropriations riders, hesaid. Theres already a lot of discussion about inserting riders that would stop EPA from regulating greenhouse gases, that would stop many other EPA regulations.
        </p>
        <p>
         Even if the Clean Power Plan survives a spending duel, it could face years of litigation before it begins to curbgreenhouse-gas emissions from power plants, he said.
        </p>
        <p>
         Three lawsuits have already been filed challenging the EPA proposal. They are almost certainly premature, because you canusually only challenge final agency action in court, and we dont have that, but once the rules do go final in June there will be, almost certainly, more than a hundred lawsuits filed.
        </p>
        <p>
         The question then is whether the courts will staythe rule while litigation proceeds or allow the rule to take effect. It took four years for the Supreme Court to rule on litigation over the EPAs greenhouse-gas
         <a href="http://www.epa.gov/climatechange/endangerment/" target="_blank">
          endangerment finding
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         During that period, the courts declined to stay the endangerment finding, which allowedEPA to proceed with its draft ofthe Clean Power Plan.
        </p>
        <p>
         If the courts dont stay the rule, states will have to submit plans to the EPA to reduce greenhouse gas emissions from the power sector, and the EPA will begin to review their implementation.
        </p>
        <p>
         By the time that happens, there will be a new president in the White House.
        </p>
        <p>
         The consequences of the 2016 election are just enormous, Gerrard said.
        </p>
        <p>
         Gerrards appearance was hosted by the
         <a href="http://epic.uchicago.edu" target="_blank">
          Energy Policy Institute of Chicago
         </a>
         , a groupof climate scientists, physicists, economists, and public policy experts at the University of Chicago.
        </p>
        <p>
         Gerrard predicted the Republican majority in Congress will alsotry to limit the Obama Administrations ability to negotiate at COP-21, the UN Climate Change Conference in Paris this December, and they do have enough votes, right now, to prevent a treaty from being ratified.
        </p>
