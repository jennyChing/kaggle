                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Teaching Babies to Talk - How We Talk to Babies Is Key
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      <span class="post-author vcard">
                       Posted by
                       <span class="fn" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
                        <meta content="https://plus.google.com/103363672701317435825" itemprop="url"/>
                        <a class="g-profile" href="https://plus.google.com/103363672701317435825" rel="author" title="author profile">

                         </span>

                       </span>

                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8997917552273405873" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      Recent Article on Babble.com confirms that how parents react to the noises and speech attempts a baby makes is key to baby speech progress. When learning to talk babies need to learn what words mean and how mothers and fathers help them to do that is an important skill to learn.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <blockquote class="tr_bq">
                       <i>
                        <a href="http://www.babble.com/baby/the-science-of-talking-to-your-baby/?cmp=SMC|bbl|soc|FB|BabbleMain|InHouse|060813|BabyTalk|Link|famM|Social&amp;utm_source=facebook.com&amp;utm_medium=referral&amp;utm_campaign=SMC|bbl|soc|FB|BabbleMain|InHouse|060813|BabyTalk|Link|famM|Social" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                         The Science Of Talking To Your Baby
                        </a>
                        <br/>
                        <b>
                         A moms responsiveness to her babys talk and movements
                        </b>
                        predicted the
 age at which the babies reached milestones like saying their first 
words, having 50 words in their vocabularies, putting two words together, and speaking in the past tense. Moms response predicted 
these milestones over and above the babys own vocalizations and 
gestures at those ages.
                       </i>
                      </blockquote>
                      <br/>
                      I followed this principle and other research findings closely when I wrote my ebook : " How to Teach Baby to Talk" and you can see the idea in use by a father here in this Youtube Video.
                      <br/>
                      He begins trying to get the baby to copy him, then ends up copying the sounds the baby makes. But the important thing is they are having a conversation or interaction at the baby's level of development. The father is tuning in his baby talk to what makes the child happy and entertained.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
