                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXWyrZo5I/AAAAAAAAAGc/VVma5IsPvQ0/s1600/blooog+013.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXWyrZo5I/AAAAAAAAAGc/VVma5IsPvQ0/s400/blooog+013.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Disposable Cup and Mixing spoon, Measuring Spoon, Unflavored Gelatine, and Milk. I believe in whole milk. Hell, with all the calories I'm saving with this 5 calorie gelatine, my face deserves whole milk.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXX9tD0sI/AAAAAAAAAGg/tD7CS1DFnec/s1600/blooog+014.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXX9tD0sI/AAAAAAAAAGg/tD7CS1DFnec/s400/blooog+014.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     Alright! Wash your face before all of this, don't want any oils getting all up in your way. You're gonna need one tablespoon of gelatine. (Am I the only one that thought gelatine didn't have any e? Hm. French.) Basically, one packet will do. Looks like sugaah, omnomnom.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXY5zWx8I/AAAAAAAAAGk/SZgvPEkzveY/s1600/blooog+015.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXY5zWx8I/AAAAAAAAAGk/SZgvPEkzveY/s400/blooog+015.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Add one to two tablespoons of milk. I ended up using two in the batch I used. Probably would have been more cohesive if I had added a but more.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXaKraneI/AAAAAAAAAGo/FNDwlnQA5dU/s1600/blooog+016.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXaKraneI/AAAAAAAAAGo/FNDwlnQA5dU/s400/blooog+016.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Look at that focus. I love you, camera. This is what happens when you wait too long to start smushing it all over your face. It gels. BE SWIFT, YOUNG ONE! I made another batch.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXb4AvDdI/AAAAAAAAAGs/B1cDzwx02vM/s400/blooog+017.JPG" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="300"/>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         Feelin' glamorous.
                        </td>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                        </td>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     Unflavored gelatine smells pretty yuck. It's not unbearable, but it's about as lovely as you'd expect ground up animal hooves to smell like. I waited about twenty minutes to peel it off. It gets really, really stiff. I imagine this is what botox feels like. No emotion face!
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXdsFMwTI/AAAAAAAAAGw/MIJTLhEF4qU/s1600/blooog+021.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXdsFMwTI/AAAAAAAAAGw/MIJTLhEF4qU/s400/blooog+021.JPG" width="300"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         Argggh, Am zombieee. Want to eat your brains because my skin is peeling offf, Arghhh.
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      Peeling it off was a bit of a pain, but fun nonetheless. I rinsed the little bits that were left off with warm water. Additionally, try not to get this stuff in your hair. Makes it all stiff and ew.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXe5ds8zI/AAAAAAAAAG0/gatA0XT-kd4/s1600/blooog+023.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUOXe5ds8zI/AAAAAAAAAG0/gatA0XT-kd4/s320/blooog+023.JPG" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I look so enthusiastic! But yay! Clean face! My skin definitely felt smoother and tighter, but also a bit dry. Slap some moisturizer on and enjoy your pretty self.

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://lovelybabystego.blogspot.com/2011/01/spaghetti-la-me.html">
                      Spaghetti a la Me
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      <span class="post-author vcard">
                       Posted by
                       <span class="fn" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
                        <meta content="https://www.blogger.com/profile/00071572888947322497" itemprop="url"/>

                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7233192878281729894" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     To say that I'm a gourmet chef is a far stretch. I actually prefer baking, the exact measurements are much more comfortable for me. I get nervous when I have to improvise. With nothing in the fridge but sandwich meat and turkey broth, I figured I should head to the grocery store. Pasta (pretty much any version of pasta), hamburger steaks and mashed potatoes (I'm a simpleton) and potato soup are the things I know I'm pretty good at. Lots of practice/easy stuff. Tonight? Tonight was a spaghetti night.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI92wlRrjI/AAAAAAAAAFo/J_YGLssTqMU/s1600/wee+006.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI92wlRrjI/AAAAAAAAAFo/J_YGLssTqMU/s400/wee+006.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Oh Haaaaaay, ingredients. How ya been? I actually didn't use the Red Gold tomatoes, but no one likes to leave out the obsolete one.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI93_OP1_I/AAAAAAAAAFs/OPkibKMIsRg/s1600/wee+007.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI93_OP1_I/AAAAAAAAAFs/OPkibKMIsRg/s400/wee+007.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     The meat looks like its in animal shapes? Can I just say I hate browning meat? It's the most tedious process ever. And don't even get me started on boiling water. You could season your meat and the water if you want, I didn't. Well, other than salting the water. Rebel spaghetti.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI95qT2-OI/AAAAAAAAAFw/KG0_tGupJmk/s1600/wee+008.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI95qT2-OI/AAAAAAAAAFw/KG0_tGupJmk/s400/wee+008.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Y'all know what browned cow looks like, so I figured I'd skip ahead. Yay, sauce! It looks really juicy right now but it cooks down. I love stewed tomatoes in spaghetti. Mmm.. chunks.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI96o6-6pI/AAAAAAAAAF0/_3caoze4alw/s1600/wee+009.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI96o6-6pI/AAAAAAAAAF0/_3caoze4alw/s400/wee+009.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     And the water still isn't boiling. Ahhh.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI97hn3J9I/AAAAAAAAAF4/UoKSJYij0-Y/s1600/wee+010.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI97hn3J9I/AAAAAAAAAF4/UoKSJYij0-Y/s400/wee+010.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     I'd like to note that I started at 8:00. 22 minutes to boil? Sigh. By the way, if your noodles look like this, they aren't done. Sad, stiff noodles.

                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI98nCaZqI/AAAAAAAAAF8/Oqt1jBmLNsM/s1600/wee+011.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUI98nCaZqI/AAAAAAAAAF8/Oqt1jBmLNsM/s400/wee+011.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     FINALLY! WE ARE READY TO ASSEMBLE! Steamy saucy ;)

                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5052614901874201636" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     I've played with this idea several times and I've seen it other places, done other ways. This is just how I've perfected it fo' me!
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUHGGA47d0I/AAAAAAAAAFc/PZlBz5w2JEk/s1600/cl+049.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_0KunxgTF2d8/TUHGGA47d0I/AAAAAAAAAFc/PZlBz5w2JEk/s320/cl+049.JPG" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     Did this to the girlfriend recently because she wanted both Color Club Pure Energy and Volt of Light on her nails. Maybe one day I'll do a more in depth explanation but for right now, this is how it went down.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     -Base coat all your nails to prevent staining. Yellows are known for that, as are reds.
                     <br/>
                     -Take your base color (Volt of Light) and paint a rather thick coat. It's gonna feel really rebellious and messy, you crazy kid, you. I'mma take a moment to mention that you should stay away from nail polish that is known for it's crazy quick dry time. It only hinders the design process.
                     <br/>
                     -Next take the other color and dot on blobs of color. So technical, this is. I discovered I like the effect most when you concentrate on the tip/base/side of the nail. Kinda looks like flames.
                     <br/>
                     -Lightly draw a toothpick through both colors, making swirls, patterns, whatevaaa. Toothpicks are perfect for this because they're so tiny. Work fast though! If you make a line and it drags/you see nail through the design, it means the polish is drying up!
                     <br/>
                     -Wait a bit, and then throw on some top coat. I used Seche Vite, because it's awesome and it dries all that polish up pretty quickly.
                     <br/>
                     -Marvel at your loveliness.
