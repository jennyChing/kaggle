     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn" itemprop="name" style="font-size: 27px;">
      Thai Iced Tea
     </h1>
     <img height="553" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Thai-Iced-Tea-790x553.jpg" width="790"/>
     <div class="yui3-g">
      <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-well">
       <div class="grub-byline" style="margin-top: 1em;">
        Recipe by
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/113274240511284571445" rel="author">
         Jerry James Stone
        </a>
         June 21, 2014
       </div>
       <div class="grub-mainPostContent" itemprop="recipeInstructions" not-style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 23px;">
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="700" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/thai-iced-tea/thai-iced-tea/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/thai-iced-tea" rel="attachment wp-att-5899">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I love Thai food. In fact, Im a bit embarrassed to admit that the whole reason I went to Thailand was for the cuisine; it fascinates me. The month I was there, I pretty much lived off Thai Ice Teas which costs 20 cents each at the time. Making it at home isnt quite that inexpensive but its less than the $4 you pay in a restaurant.
        </p>
        <div class="yui3-g" id="grub-recipeSummary">
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-left">
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul id="grub-ingredients">
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cups
            <a href="http://amzn.to/1lZ0MVc">
             Thai Tea Mix
            </a>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            6 Water
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup Sweetened Condensed Milk
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup Sugar
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup Cream
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            crushed ice
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-right">
          <h2>
           <a class="grub-button" href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/thai-iced-tea/printable/" target="_blank">
            <span class="grub-printIcon">
            </span>
            Printable View
           </a>
          </h2>
          <h2>
           Yield:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            4 thai iced teas
           </span>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT1H10M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          <h2>
           Total Time
           <div style="float: right;">
            1 hour, 10 minutes
           </div>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT5M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Prep time
           <div style="float: right">
            5 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Inactive time
           <div style="float: right">
            1 hour
           </div>
          </div>
          <meta content="PT5M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Cook time
           <div style="float: right">
            5 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Recipe Type:
           </h2>
           Beverage
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         1. First thing first, lets get to know
         <a href="http://amzn.to/1lZ0MVc">
          Thai Iced Tea
         </a>
         a bit better. This beverage, for all intents and purposes, is Kool-Aid. Im serious. Despite being made with a rather healthy tea, its dyed orange, flavored with natural flavors and who knows what else.
         <a href="http://amzn.to/1lZ0MVc">
          Its imported from Thailand
         </a>
         and the packaging isnt very forthcoming. That said, I effing love this stuff! Its like crack and for all I know, that might be one of the ingredients. And I am okay with that.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="405" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/thai-iced-tea/thai-tea-mix/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/thai-tea-mix" rel="attachment wp-att-5900">
          <img alt="Thai Tea Mix" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5900" height="243" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Thai-Tea-Mix-600x243.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. To create the tea, simple warm 6 cups of water in a small saucepan (dont boil), remove from heat, and then add 1 1/2 cups of
         <a href="http://amzn.to/1lZ0MVc">
          Thai Tea Mix
         </a>
         to it. Cover and let steep for about 5 minutes. Then carefully strain out the tea. I do this by straining it through a cheesecloth-lined sieve, then twisting the cloth to remove as much liquid as possible. When it is all done, you should have about 4 cups of brewed tea remaining.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Add in 1/2 cup of sugar and 1/2 cup of sweetened condensed milk, and mix it together. Now transfer it to the fridge to chill, for about an hour or so.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Divide the sweetened tea between four glasses filled with crushed ice and float a bit of cream on the top.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         Let me just add that my trip to Thailand was epic. I rode elephants, hung out with tigers, took cooking classes, visited breathtaking waterfalls and that just some of it.
         <a href="https://www.facebook.com/jerryjamesstone/media_set?set=a.69450282316.104849.561767316&amp;type=3">
          You can see the photos from my trip
         </a>
         over on Facebook. But the best part of Thailand is the people. They are warm, gracious, and so welcoming. I never felt out of place or mistreated, regardless of how badly I fumbled through their language.
        </p>

         </a>
        </p>
