         <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
          <a href="http://www.handsoccupied.com/2013/11/how-to-diy-a-wide-belt/">
           How-to: DIY a Wide Belt
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="byline">
          By
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.handsoccupied.com/author/hjg/" rel="author" title="Heidi">
            Heidi
           </a>
          </span>
          <time class="published" datetime="2013-11-25T00:00:57-05:00" title="Monday, November 25th, 2013, 12:00 am">
           November 25, 2013
          </time>
          |
          <a class="comments-link" href="http://www.handsoccupied.com/2013/11/how-to-diy-a-wide-belt/#comments" title="Comment on How-to: DIY a Wide Belt">
           4 Responses
          </a>
         </div>
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="How-to: DIY a Wide Belt at Hands Occupied" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6731" height="400" src="http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/widebelt5.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.handsoccupied.com/2013/11/how-to-diy-a-wide-belt/&amp;media=http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/widebelt5.jpg&amp;description=How-to: DIY a Wide Belt')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Im a lady with an hourglass figure. Literally, my bust and hips have the same measurement and my waist is exactly 10 inches smaller. I like my figure, but a lot of modern clothes arent necessarily designed to flatter my silhouette. Belts, dresses and higher-waisted pieces are staples in my wardrobe. Sometimes I find a cute dress that doesnt have a tailored waist, or a top that hits my boobs and hangs straight down, making me look a lot bigger than I am. Thats when a wide elastic belt comes in
          <em>
           very
          </em>
          handy. Plus wide belts elongate your waist, are super cute, and luckily for me, theyre incredibly easy to make.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Supplies
         </h3>
         <p>
          1 yd. wide elastic
          <br/>
          belt buckle, same height as your elastics width
          <br/>
          scissors
          <br/>
          straight pins
          <br/>
          measuring tape
          <br/>
          needle
          <br/>
          thread
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="How-to: DIY a Wide Belt at Hands Occupied" class="aligncenter wp-image-6727" src="http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/widebelt1.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Directions
         </h3>
         <p>
          Measure your natural waist with a measuring tape. Cut your elastic to the size of your natural waist plus 1.5 to 2 inches, depending on how wide your belt buckle is. Your belt should fit quite snugly, with just a little stretch to the elastic when youre wearing the finished belt. I recommend pinning your cut elastic to your belt buckle as shown below and trying it on, pin side out, for size before sewing it together. If its too loose, you can increase the seam allowance or trim excess elastic before sewing. Easy!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="How-to: DIY a Wide Belt at Hands Occupied" class="aligncenter wp-image-6728" src="http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/widebelt2.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          When youre satisfied with the fit, fold the raw end of the elastic under twice and pin in place.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="How-to: DIY a Wide Belt at Hands Occupied" class="aligncenter wp-image-6730" src="http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/widebelt4.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Finally, you can either hand or machine sew the elastic parallel to the belt buckle, removing straight pins as you go.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="How-to: DIY a Wide Belt at Hands Occupied" class="aligncenter wp-image-6729" src="http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/widebelt3.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats it! A crazy simple DIY belt thats super slimming for people needing to accentuate an hourglass waist, or for people wanting to emphasize a subtle waist (my mom and I have very different body types  she tells me creating a waist illusion is important). :)
         </p>
