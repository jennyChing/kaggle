              <h1 class="entry-title">
               Grab Some Bread and Devour Gambas Al Ajillo
              </h1>
              <div class="entry-meta">
               <a href="http://www.pbs.org/food/author/matsumotom/" rel="author" title="Posts by Marc Matsumoto">
                Marc Matsumoto
               </a>
               | November 6, 2012
              </div>

           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             <img alt="Gambas Al Ajillo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14481" height="400" src="http://www.pbs.org/food/files/2012/11/garlic-shrimp-1.jpg" width="600"/>
            </p>
            <div style="float:right; width=204px; padding:2px; border: solid 1px; border-color: #f6d4d5;">
             <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/pbsfood">
              <img alt="Follow PBS Food on Pinterest" height="26" src="http://www.pbs.org/food/wp-content/blogs.dir/2/files/2012/02/pinterest-follow.png" title="Follow PBS Food on Pinterest" width="200"/>
             </a>
             <p align="center">
              <a data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonPin" href="//pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.pbs.org%2Ffood%2Ffresh-tastes%2Fgambas-al-ajillo-garlic-shrimp%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.pbs.org%2Ffood%2Ffiles%2F2012%2F11%2Fgarlic-shrimp-1.jpg&amp;description=Garlic%20Shrimp%20recipe%20from%20PBS%20Food">
               <img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png"/>
              </a>
             </p>
            </div>
            <p>
             Gambas al ajillo, or shrimp with garlic is perhaps one of the most popular tapas outside of Spain. I love shrimp, and you cant go wrong with shrimp fried with lots of garlic and olive oil, but for me the appeal of this dish is less about the shrimp and more about the caramelized garlic and shrimp juices that settle in the bottom of the pan. Put a pan of these in front of me and I could devour a whole loaf of bread, smearing each nibble of browned garlic onto every last piece of crusty bread.
            </p>
            <p>
             <span id="more-14485">
             </span>
             <img alt="Gambas Al Ajillo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14482" height="400" src="http://www.pbs.org/food/files/2012/11/garlic-shrimp-2.jpg" width="600"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Thats why I like using elephant garlic. Its milder than regular garlic, which means you can use a lot more of it before it becomes overpowering. Its also a lot larger than regular garlic (think shallot sized cloves), so its much easier to peel and chop. For this recipe I used half of one giant clove of elephant garlic, but had I been using regular garlic it probably would have taken a whole head of garlic to get the same volume.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Gambas Al Ajillo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14483" height="900" src="http://www.pbs.org/food/files/2012/11/garlic-shrimp-3.jpg" width="600"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             While this dish is usually made with chili peppers in Spain, I like using pimentn because it adds a smoky flavor thats a perfect compliment to the browned garlic and shrimp. Ive also left out the cognac that usually goes into gambas al ajillo in favor of a stove + oven approach that cooks the shrimp more evenly.
            </p>
            <p>
             If for some crazy reason you end up with leftovers, just boil some pasta, drain it, and then toss the leftovers with the hot pasta. The residual heat will warm up the shrimp without making them rubbery, and the shrimp, garlic and oil make for the perfect sauce.
            </p>

             <div class="entry-content">
              <div class="ingredients">
               <h4 class="ingredients">
                Ingredients
               </h4>
               <ul class="ingredients">
                <li class="ingredient">
                 1/3 cup olive oil
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient">
                 50 grams elephant garlic, roughly chopped (1.75 ounces)
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient">
                 1 teaspoon pimentn
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient">
                 450 grams shrimp, peeled and deveined (1 pound)
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient">
                 salt and pepper to taste
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient">
                 1 tablespoon minced parsley to garnish
                </li>
               </ul>
              </div>
              <div class="instructions">
               <h4 class="instructions">
                Directions
               </h4>
               <ol class="instructions">
                <li>
                 <span class="txt">
                  Put your oven rack in the middle position and heat to 400 degrees F (200 C).
                 </span>
                </li>
                <li>
                 <span class="txt">
                  Add the oil and garlic to a cast iron pan and heat over medium high heat.  Fry the garlic until it just starts to turn brown around the edges.  Add the pimentn, and stir to combine.
                 </span>
                </li>
                <li>
                 <span class="txt">
                  Add the shrimp in a single layer, and then sprinkle with salt and pepper.  Put the pan in the oven and cook until the shrimp is pink and opaque all the way through (about 2-3 minutes).
                 </span>
                </li>
                <li>
                 <span class="txt">
                  Remove the pan from the oven and sprinkle with the parsley. Toss the shrimp to coat with the oil and serve with bread.
                 </span>
                </li>
               </ol>
              </div>
              <p class="yield">
               <span class="hrlabel">
                Yield:
               </span>
               <span class="hritem">
                2 servings
               </span>
              </p>
