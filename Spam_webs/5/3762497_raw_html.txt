          The esteemed poet Robert Frost once opined,
          <em>
           Good fences make good neighbors.
          </em>
          If thats the case, then these 12 fiendishly
          <a href="http://weburbanist.com/2011/01/09/stealth-health-american-family-planning-clinic-architecture/">
           offensive fences
          </a>
          must make good neighbors furious. Though haters may class them as classic fails, these fences do succeed at one thing: they never fail to give offense.
          <p>
           <span id="more-28661">
           </span>
          </p>
          <h4>
           Steel This Fence
          </h4>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-28663" height="466" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/offensive_fences_1a.jpg" title="offensive_fences_1a" width="468"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2011/05/01/border-wars-12-offensive-fences-worth-picketing/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/offensive_fences_1a.jpg&amp;description=Border Wars: 12 Offensive Fences Worth Picketing')">
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
            (images via:
            <a href="http://web.mac.com/queenodesign/Detour_Art_Travels/Photo_Gallery/Pages/license_plate_fence,_ar.html">
             Detour Art Travels
            </a>
            )
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Galamba Metals in Sulphur Springs, Arkansas, decided to plate the fence encircling their auto scrap yard one day which they did, using hundreds of license plates formerly affixed to formerly rolling stock. Perhaps someone at the company thought covering the existing fence with license plates would help mask an eyesore. The question now, is how to cover the eyesore covering the eyesore.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-28664" height="489" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/offensive_fences_1b.jpg" title="offensive_fences_1b" width="468"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2011/05/01/border-wars-12-offensive-fences-worth-picketing/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/offensive_fences_1b.jpg&amp;description=Border Wars: 12 Offensive Fences Worth Picketing')">
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
            (images via:
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/digital_idiot/4997078833/in/photostream/">
             Digital Idiot
            </a>
            )
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Sulphur Springs is located in the northwest corner of AR near the states borders with Kansas and Oklahoma. Visitors can find the Galamba Metals yard just steps from the Stateline Liquor store; visits to which are recommended both before and after viewing the license plate fence.
          </p>
          <h4>
           A Fence With Balls
          </h4>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-28666" height="625" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/offensive_fences_2a.jpg" title="offensive_fences_2a" width="468"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2011/05/01/border-wars-12-offensive-fences-worth-picketing/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/offensive_fences_2a.jpg&amp;description=Border Wars: 12 Offensive Fences Worth Picketing')">
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
            (images via:
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/digital_idiot/3091444662/in/gallery-8279953@N05-72157625050217523/">
             Digital Idiot

            <a href="http://www.roadsideamerica.com/tip/14475">
             Roadside America
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.picspile.com/_World_s_Weirdest_and_Bizarre_Fence_57_images-3">
             PicsPile
            </a>
            )
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Most folks like to kick back and relax when they retire, but
           <a href="http://www.roadsideamerica.com/tip/14475">
            Chris Barbee
           </a>
           of Nowata, Oklahoma isnt most folks. Barbee, you see, makes stuff from old bowling balls. Garden sheds, huge kinetic executive toys, fences oh yeah, fences. Since he gave up his day job  possibly as a pin-setter  Barbee has used his free time to build an 80-ft fence strung with dozens of bowling balls suspended from chains. Its a frighteningly beautiful sight to see, even more so when tornadoes are forecast.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-28667" height="480" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/offensive_fences_2b.jpg" title="offensive_fences_2b" width="468"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2011/05/01/border-wars-12-offensive-fences-worth-picketing/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/offensive_fences_2b.jpg&amp;description=Border Wars: 12 Offensive Fences Worth Picketing')">
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
            (images via:
            <a href="http://www.newson6.com/story/11042120/chris-and-amys-bowling-ball-yard-art?redirected=true">
             NewsOn6
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://jpgmag.com/photos/2582136">
             LeAnne Williams
            </a>
            )
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Bowling isnt as popular as it once was and hundreds of former alleys have closed down, which partially explains how Barbee (above) was able to scrounge up so many
           <a href="http://www.newson6.com/story/11042120/chris-and-amys-bowling-ball-yard-art?redirected=true">
            bowling balls
           </a>
           . One wonders, though, what he did with all those bowling pins?
          </p>

            (image via:
            <a href="http://www.real-whitby.co.uk/whitbys-weird-and-wonderful">
             Real Whitby Magazine
            </a>
            )
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           In the olden days, the English would display the heads of beheaded traitors on spikes along London Bridge. Those days have long passed but it seems some traditions die hard in Merry Olde England albeit one doubts the moles above were in any way acting traitorous before they were caught, tried, executed and hung  though not drawn &amp; quartered. Perhaps they were moles from the ministry. So whats up with this gruesome practice? It seems that in the UK,
           <a href="http://www.dumville.org/moles/guestmoles.html">
            Molecatchers
           </a>
           (yes, there is such a profession) are typically paid not by the hour, but according to how many moles they catch. The moles are hung up on the nearest convenient fence for the employer to count at the end of the day. Then one &amp; all enjoy a hot cuppa and scones while admiring the Mrs. new tea cozy. Jolly good!
          </p>
