           The Next Wedding Color is Copper - Who Needs Red Gold When You Can Have Copper?
          </h1>
         </div>

          <a href="http://www.genesisdiamonds.net/blog/author/danielmoss/" rel="author" title="Posts by Daniel Moss">

         <div class="entry">
          <p>
           Wedding trends come and go although some remain eternal. Sure, you may have the occasional bride walking down the aisle in red, black, or cream; although white remains the most popular color of wedding dress. However, when it comes to wedding decorations, details, and accessorizing, there is a lot more flexibility on offer when it comes to use of color.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The new in color for weddings is copper. Gold and silver have become pass and it is the cheaper metal that has come to the fore. And although in the real world copper may cost about $3.20 per pound versus approximately $19,000 per pound of gold, in the world of dreams that is represented by weddings, copper is more than holding its own.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Perhaps it is the popularity of another fantasy setting as seen in Game of Thrones that is driving the popularity of copper but copper has much to recommend it. Copper has a warmth and range of hues that are difficult to match. Aged copper looks antiquated and homely, whereas polished copper is bright and sassy. Of course, there are literally hundreds of variations between these two extremes.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           <b>
            Copper and Your Wedding
           </b>
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Copper_inv" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13407" height="400" src="http://www.genesisdiamonds.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Copper_inv.gif" width="400"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           So how can you integrate copper to your wedding to best effect? These are just a few examples to help fire your imagination:
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <b>
             Copper wedding invitations:
            </b>
            If you decide to go with the copper theme, it is never too soon to start. Integrate copper coloring on your wedding invitations and let the color start to work its magic.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <b>
             Copper table numbers/menu frames/seating cards:
            </b>
            Continue the theme at the reception with copper table numbers, menu frames, or seating cards. Remember, copper is the perfect accompaniment to white or cream but dont overdo it.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.genesisdiamonds.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/copperseatnumbers.png">
            <img alt="copperseatnumbers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13517" height="600" src="http://www.genesisdiamonds.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/copperseatnumbers.png" width="400"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <b>
             Copper colored wedding shoes:
            </b>
            You may have gone for a more traditional colored white dress but that doesnt mean that you cant have copper colored shoes. Your shoes arent on show the whole time and the copper color provides a great contrast when dancing and kicking up your heels.
           </li>
           <p>
           </p>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.genesisdiamonds.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/coppershoes.jpeg">
            <img alt="coppershoes" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-13518" height="306" src="http://www.genesisdiamonds.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/coppershoes-460x306.jpeg" width="460"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <b>
             Copper table centerpiece:
            </b>
            Irrespective of what you decide to be your table centerpiece - a vase with flowers, candelabra, or lantern; copper coloring will add warmth to what is an already warm event.
           </li>
           <p>
           </p>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.genesisdiamonds.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/coppercenterpiece.jpg">
            <img alt="coppercenterpiece" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-13519" height="690" src="http://www.genesisdiamonds.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/coppercenterpiece-460x690.jpg" width="460"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <b>
             Copper seat covers:
            </b>
            If you are having your chairs covered, try a copper colored cloth for maximum impact.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.textilefurnishings.com/gifs/wedding-chair-covers.jpg">
            <img alt="copperwedding-chair-covers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13521" height="229" src="http://www.genesisdiamonds.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/copperwedding-chair-covers.jpg" width="280"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Copper is a metal best known for its use in phone wires. It is often referred to in the financial world as Doctor Copper, as it seems to have a PhD in economics given the predictive powers of its price. So too, copper is proving to have a prophetic ability to set the trend in wedding colors. With its warmth, rich palette, and ability to complement so many other colors, it is hardly surprising.
          </p>
          <br style="clear:both;"/>
