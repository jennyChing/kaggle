           <h1 class="entry-title margin-bottom-30">
            NASA parks Mars Opportunity Rover for the winter
           </h1>
           <div class="entry-meta margin-bottom-30 hidden-print" style="display:none">
            <span class="posted-on">
             <time class="entry-date published updatedpull-left" datetime="2012-01-09T12:27:17+00:00">
              January 9, 2012
             </time>
            </span>
            <span class="byline">
             <span class="author vcard">
              <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.zmescience.com/author/admin/" rel="author">
               Tibi Puiu
              </a>
             </span>
            </span>
           </div>
           <!-- .entry-meta -->
          </header>
          <!-- .entry-header -->
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_11046" style="width: 474px">
            <a href="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/false_colour_image_greeleys_haven.jpg">
             <figure class="hammy-responsive size-full wp-image-11046 " data-media="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2012/01/false_colour_image_greeleys_haven/2582818043.jpg" data-media300="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2012/01/false_colour_image_greeleys_haven/1549383635.jpg" title="false_colour_image_greeleys_haven">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="False colour image of Greeley Haven taken by Opportunity. Credit: NASA/JPL-Caltech/Cornell/Arizona State Univ" height="321" src="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/false_colour_image_greeleys_haven.jpg" title="false_colour_image_greeleys_haven" width="464"/>
              </noscript>
             </figure>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             False colour image of Greeley Haven taken by Opportunity. Credit: NASA/JPL-Caltech/Cornell/Arizona State Univ
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Theres no bigger drag on a busy Monday morning than finding your car under a heap of snow in the driveway. Taking measures and parking it under some kind shelter, if possible, would be advised if you dont plan on driving an ice truck. Take
            <a href="http://www.zmescience.com/tag/nasa">
             NASA
            </a>
            for instance, which, like every year, has routed the precious Opportunity Rover, currently the only transmitting machinery
            <a href="http://www.zmescience.com/space/the-spirit-rovers-last-transmitted-image-from-mars-423343/">
             left on Mars
            </a>
            , to sit out through out the Martian winter at the rim of the massive Endeavourcrater. And, yes, if you were curious
            <a href="http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5jTzY7skJ3raWHWczabqJ4T5GKypQ">
             it does snow on Mars
            </a>
            !
           </p>
           <p>
            The place selected by NASA for the sitout is calledGreeley Haven, in the memory of thelate scientist Ronald Greeley that passed away in October 2011. At the site, Opportunity will be positioned towards the sun-facing slope of the crater to catch the meagre rays of our star on its solar panels.This is the first winter that Opportunity has spent on a slope angling its panels for more collection of sunlight. However, now more than ever, the dust particles collected on top of its solar panels have amounted to a degree such that it makes it necessary for Opportunity to spend the winter there.
           </p>
           <p>
            Naturally, theres never a dull day for Opportunity. While parked, it will continue to study rocks near Greeley Haven, collecting and analyzing samples through out the rim ofEndeavor.Opportunity, which landed on Mars eight years ago, has driven a total of 21 miles (34 kilometers), and it has taken it three years to reach the crater since it first set its course.
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             Greeley Haven provides the proper tilt, as well as a rich variety of potential targets for imaging and compositional and mineralogic studies, said Jim Bell, lead scientist for the Panoramic Camera (Pancam) on the rover.
            </p>
            <p class="entry-stuff clear margin-bottom-15 hidden-print single-post-ads-between center-block text-center">
             <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
             </script>
             <!-- ZME Science -->
             <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-2424323874644721" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="5307795920" style="display:block">
             </ins>
             <script>
              (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
             </script>
            </p>
            <p>
             Weve already found hints of gypsum in the bedrock in this formation, and we know from orbital data that there are clays nearby, too, he stated.
            </p>
            <p>
             Greeley Haven, he said, looks to be a safe and special place that could yield exciting new discoveries about the watery past of Mars.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            <em>
             <a href="http://www.theregister.co.uk/2012/01/09/mars_rover_winters_in_sunny_spot/">
              image credit
             </a>
