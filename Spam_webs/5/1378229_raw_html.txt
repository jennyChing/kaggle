     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Tracks
       </b>
      </i>
      is a 2013 Australian
      <a href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">
       drama film
      </a>
      directed by
      <a href="/wiki/John_Curran_(director)" title="John Curran (director)">
       John Curran
      </a>
      and starring
      <a href="/wiki/Mia_Wasikowska" title="Mia Wasikowska">
       Mia Wasikowska
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Adam_Driver" title="Adam Driver">
       Adam Driver
      </a>
      . It is an adaptation of
      <a href="/wiki/Robyn_Davidson" title="Robyn Davidson">
       Robyn Davidson
      </a>
      's memoir of the same name, chronicling the author's nine-month journey on camels across the Australian desert. It was shown at the
      <a href="/wiki/2013_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2013 Toronto International Film Festival">
       2013 Toronto International Film Festival
      </a>
      (Special Presentation) and the
      <a href="/wiki/70th_Venice_International_Film_Festival" title="70th Venice International Film Festival">
       70th Venice International Film Festival
      </a>
      2013 (in Official Competition).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TIFF2013_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-TIFF2013-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was the opening film at the
      <a href="/wiki/2013_Adelaide_Film_Festival" title="2013 Adelaide Film Festival">
       Adelaide Film Festival
      </a>
      on 10 October 2013. This was the Australian premiere.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFF2013_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-AFF2013-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The film has also been shown at several other film festivals, including London, Vancouver, Telluride, Dubai, Sydney OpenAir, Dublin and Glasgow.
     </p>

      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1977,
      <a href="/wiki/Robyn_Davidson" title="Robyn Davidson">
       Robyn Davidson
      </a>
      travels from
      <a href="/wiki/Alice_Springs" title="Alice Springs">
       Alice Springs
      </a>
      across 2,700 kilometers (1,700 miles) of
      <a href="/wiki/Deserts_of_Australia" title="Deserts of Australia">
       Australian deserts
      </a>
      to the
      <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">
       Indian Ocean
      </a>
      with her dog and four camels.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nma080312_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nma080312-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>

         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Society" title="National Geographic Society">
       National Geographic
      </a>
      photographer
      <a href="/wiki/Rick_Smolan" title="Rick Smolan">

      </a>
      documents her journey.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tracks_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      There were five attempts in the early 1980s and 1990s to turn the book into a film.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sbs120903_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-sbs120903-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1993,
      <a href="/wiki/Julia_Roberts" title="Julia Roberts">
       Julia Roberts
      </a>
      was attached to star in a planned
      <a href="/wiki/Caravan_Pictures" title="Caravan Pictures">
       Caravan Pictures
      </a>
      adaptation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-var931122_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-var931122-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The project eventually fell apart.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nma080312_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-nma080312-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In May 2012, it was reported that Mia Wasikowska would play Robyn Davidson in a John Currandirected adaptation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wasik_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-wasik-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In August, Adam Driver was cast as Rick Smolan.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dl120822_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-dl120822-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The screenplay was written by Marion Nelson.
     </p>
     <p>
      Finance was sourced from
      <a href="/wiki/Screen_Australia" title="Screen Australia">
       Screen Australia
      </a>
      , the South Australian Film Corporation,
      <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Film_Festival" title="Adelaide Film Festival">
       Adelaide Film Festival
      </a>
      , Deluxe Australia, pre-sales, and Canada's Aver Media Finance.
     </p>
     <p>
      With a US$12 million budget,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sbs120903_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-sbs120903-2">

        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fb121008_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-fb121008-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-if121009_12-0">

        <span>

        </span>

      </sup>
      It was filmed in
      <a href="/wiki/South_Australia" title="South Australia">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">
       Box office
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tracks_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Box office">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <i>
       Tracks
      </i>
      received a
      <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">
       limited theatrical release
      </a>
      in North America opening in 4 theaters and grossing $21,544, with an average of $5,386 per theater and ranking #62 at the box office. The widest release for the film was 67 theaters and it ultimately earned $510,007 domestically and $4,368,235 internationally for a total of $4,878,242, below its estimated budget of $12 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">
       Critical response
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tracks_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical response">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <i>
       Tracks
      </i>
      received mainly positive reviews from critics.
      <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">
       Review aggregator
      </a>
      website
      <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">
       Rotten Tomatoes
      </a>
      gives the film has a score of 81% based on 111 reviews with an average score of 7 out of 10. The critical consensus states " What
      <i>
       Tracks
      </i>
      lacks in excitement, it more than makes up with gorgeous cinematography and Mia Wasikowska's outstanding performance."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The film also has a score of 78 out of 100 on
      <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">
       Metacritic
      </a>
      based on 34 critics, indicating "generally favorable reviews".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Maltin" title="Leonard Maltin">
       Leonard Maltin
      </a>
      gave
      <i>
       Tracks
      </i>
      3.5 out of 4 stars, and has stated that it is the last movie (by wide release date) that will be included in the final edition of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Maltin%27s_Movie_Guide" title="Leonard Maltin's Movie Guide">
        Leonard Maltin's Movie Guide
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Word and Film
      </i>
      rated
      <i>
       Tracks
      </i>
      as the best movie adaptation of the year for 2014,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and Sydney Arts Guide awarded Marion Nelson the Best Adapted Screenplay Award 2014.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Mandy Walker won 2014 Best Cinematography awards for
      <i>
       Tracks
      </i>
      from both the Australian Cinematographers Society and the Film Critics Circle of Australia
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
