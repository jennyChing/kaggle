        <div class="entry-content">
         <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="17274249" data-link="http://acadianasthriftymom.com/2013/12/08/candy-cane-wreath/" data-summary="" data-title="Candy Cane Wreath">
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <a href="http://acadianasthriftymom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/candy-cane-wreath1.jpg" target="_blank" title="Candy Cane Wreath">
           <img alt="candy cane wreath1" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27750" height="640" src="http://acadianasthriftymom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/candy-cane-wreath1.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          This project was so much fun and surprisingly EASY! I have seen these all over the place and decided to attempt my own. I did put my own spin on it since I am convinced that this will not last through the Christmas season without the cardboard reinforcement I used. You can decorate these as you see fit. It cost me around $5 to make this one. I would not recommend hanging these on a door as they will be fragile.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Gather your supplies:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li style="text-align: left;">
           20 Candy canes
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: left;">
           Hot glue gun
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: left;">
           1 Cardboard circle 4  6 inches in diameter
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: left;">
           Bows, flowers, beads, ribbon, etc. for decoration
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Get to crafting!:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          1. Glue candy canes in twos back to back until you have 10 sets of 2 like you see here:
          <br/>
          <a href="http://acadianasthriftymom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/candy-cane-wreath2.jpg">
           <img alt="candy cane wreath2" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-27778" height="300" src="http://acadianasthriftymom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/candy-cane-wreath2-206x300.jpg" width="206"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          2. Tie bows, ribbons, etc onto your candy canes in sets of 2.
          <br/>
          3. Glue the candy canes in the shape of a heart until you have a wreath. Glue the tips together and the bottom of the candy canes onto the cardboard for stability like below.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://acadianasthriftymom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/candy-cane-wreath3.jpg">
           <img alt="candy cane wreath3" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-27779" height="300" src="http://acadianasthriftymom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/candy-cane-wreath3-235x300.jpg" width="235"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://acadianasthriftymom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/candy-cane-wreath.jpg">
           <img alt="candy cane wreath" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-27749" height="300" src="http://acadianasthriftymom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/candy-cane-wreath-225x300.jpg" width="225"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          4. Decorate your wreath as you want and make it your own!
          <br/>
          ENJOY!!!!
         </p>
