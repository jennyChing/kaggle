          <div class="post-inner group">
           <h1 class="post-title">
            Top ten google tricks to make your life a little bit easier
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://techtuft.com/author/xpl0iter/" rel="author" title="Posts by Plato Puthur">
             Plato Puthur
            </a>
             September 26, 2014
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <div align="center">
              <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
              </script>
              <!-- 468x60, created 9/9/10 -->
              <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-9862911165611954" data-ad-slot="8557149172" style="display:inline-block;width:468px;height:60px">
              </ins>
              <script>
               (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
              </script>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <p>
              Google has been in the search engine business for quite long and over these years they have changed for the better. The only thing is that most people still dont know how to sue Google search properly to get the info they want. Google when searched in the right way, will present you with beautifully structured data in the easiest way to read and grasp you could imagine. In this articles we will see the top ten Google tricks which will blow your mind away.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #1 : Did you know Google can help you in choosing your food?
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-0" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/1.jpg">
               <img alt="Google food vs food" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-178" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/1.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Just type in your favorite 2 eatables like this: cheeseburger vs applepie. And you will get all the details including neutritions about these two on side by side tables.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #2: Set timer with sound right from your browser
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-1" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/2.jpg">
               <img alt="Google Count down timer" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-179" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/2.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Ever wanted to set a timer while you were on your computer doing something important? You can do this from your browser itself without any addons or extensions. All you need is an updated browser and an internet connection. Open Google.com and type in set timer for 1 minute and the count down will begin.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #3: Find out sunrise or sunset time from Google
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-2" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/sunrise-and-sunset-time-from-Google.jpg">
               <img alt="sunrise and sunset time from Google" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-181" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/sunrise-and-sunset-time-from-Google.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              For this just type in sunrise and sunset &lt;your location&gt; and Google will give you the exact time of sunset and sunrise.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #5: Weather forecast from Google
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-3" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Weather-forecast-from-Google.jpg">
               <img alt="Weather forecast from Google" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-183" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Weather-forecast-from-Google.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Google can also show you the weather forecasts for your favorite location. Just type in &lt;location&gt; forecast!
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #5: Want to do a simple arithmetic operation?
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-4" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-Calculator.jpg">
               <img alt="Google Calculator" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-184" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-Calculator.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              If you want to do a few mathematical operations, Google has got your back. Just type in calculator and Google will open up a calculator right on the webpage. BTW if you ever wanted to know how much you have to tip your waiter you can search for tip calculator and Google will open up a tip calculator for you. Now if you want you can skip typing in the word calculator and start typing your arithmetic problem in Google like 12+45*556-212 and Google will open up the calculator screen with the result for you.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #6: Get flight information directly from Google
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-5" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/flight-routes-Google-search.jpg">
               <img alt="flight routes Google search" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-185" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/flight-routes-Google-search.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              People are getting busy and more and more people have started to use flights for their travels instead of other travel means. Google also has been keeping track of this trend and thats why you can simply search like Cochin to Bangalore flights and Google will list all the flights in that route. Now if you want to get the specifics of a particular flight, you just have to type in the name of the flight and Google will list out the schedules.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #7: Movie and TV show informations
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-6" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/movie-and-tv-show-tricks.jpg">
               <img alt="movie and tv show tricks" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-186" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/movie-and-tv-show-tricks.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Now this trick is really worth knowing. You can just type in like Tom Cruise Movies and it will list out his movies. Another trick is to type in a movie name and cast following it to see the casts of that particular movie. For example Ironman cast. It works for books and writers, songs and singers etc. There are a lot of similar hacks like these, so be sure to try out and share your findings in the comment section below.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #8: Conversions
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-7" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-conversions.jpg">
               <img alt="Google conversions" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-187" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-conversions.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              It can do all type of conversions like inches to feet, miles to kilometers etc. Type in 1 km to miles and it will give you the result.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #9: Want to find out who founded/invented/discovered something, Google has got your back!
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-8" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-wikipedia.jpg">
               <img alt="Google wikipedia" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-188" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-wikipedia.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Now you dont have to go to a wikipedia page just to find out the basic details about really popular brands or people. For example to know who founder Lamborghini you just have to type in Lamborghini founder.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Trick #10: Finding basic definitions, meanings, synonyms etc are way easy with Google now.
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-9" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-Dictionary.jpg">
               <img alt="Google Dictionary" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-189" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-Dictionary.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              You can find out the definition or meaning or synonym of something very easily using Google. For the definition of something you can type in define electric dipole. If you want the meaning of some English word, you can type in obscurantism meaning and it will display the meaning.
             </p>
             <p>
              Bonus Trick: Did you know you can use Google as a translator right from the search box?
             </p>
             <p>
              <a data-rel="lightbox-10" href="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-translatro.jpg">
               <img alt="Google translator" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-190" height="670" src="http://www.techtuft.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Google-translatro.jpg" width="1343"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              For translating something all you have to do is, type in translate in the search box and the translator toolbox will appear right below the search box where you can type in any language you want and get it translated, provided Google supports that language.
             </p>
