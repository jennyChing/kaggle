          <h1 class="title single-title">
           Find-Out Friday: Will this DIY scrub reduce the appearance of cellulite?
          </h1>
          <span class="theauthor single-postmeta">
           Posted by
           <a href="http://getpretty.com.au/author/mea" rel="nofollow" title="Posts by Mea">
            Mea
           </a>
           | In
           <a href="http://getpretty.com.au/category/body-care" rel="nofollow">

           &lt;!--

          <div class="fb-social-plugin fb-like" data-href="http://getpretty.com.au/find-out-friday-will-this-diy-scrub-reduce-the-appearance-of-cellulite.html" data-layout="button_count" data-ref="above-post" data-share="true" data-width="960">
          </div>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <span style="color: #ff99cc;">
            <em>
             If youre telling me that you
             <strong>
              do not
             </strong>
             have
             <strong>
              cellulite
             </strong>
             , youre probably fibbingor youre probably a Victorias Secret Supermodel with a thigh gap.
            </em>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           In case you didnt know, Cellulite is a term used to refer tounderlying fat deposits on certain areas of our bodies. They appear as dimples or lumps on our skin and they are commonly seen on womens tushies (
           <em>
            rump, backside, buns
           </em>
           ) and thighs.There are actually plenty of reasons why cellulite forms. Some of which are poor diet and nutrition, hormones, lack of exercise, body fat percentage and etc.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 574px">
           <a href="getpretty.com.au">
            <img alt="Cellulite - Taking a deeper look" height="422" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-BTBanjMKQFw/UxsP74AWOiI/AAAAAAAAH88/WAnPDnPshu0/s1600/photo+1+(1).JPG" width="564"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Photo courtesy: fitmommydiaries.blogspot.com
           </p>
          </div>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           Almost no one is safe from cellulite so the question that plagues every woman now is:
           <strong>
            Is it reversible? Can it be remedied?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           I have actually read on a few websites that you cannot make cellulite go away totally but there are ways to diminish it.For this, I went on Pinterest to see if I can find a DIY cellulite minimizing skin product. Pinterest has never let me down before and true enough, I found the perfect recipe  simple, easy to do and affordable for everyone.
          </p>
          <p>
           Variations of thisanti-cellulite recipe have been featured in many blogs and Facebook pages. The main ingredient:
           <strong>
            Coffee grinds
           </strong>
           . Its said that the caffeine and the antioxidants in the coffee grinds will help tone and tighten tissues on the areas where you applied it on. Lets find out if it really works.
          </p>
          <h2>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            The Recipe to Test Out
           </span>
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Coffee grinds
           </li>
           <li>
            Sugar
           </li>
           <li>
            Baby oil or olive oil (I used baby oil)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Procedure
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           You can estimate the amount of the ingredients like I did. Ideally, you can use a cup to measure everything. You can try: 1/2 cup of coffee grinds + 1/4 cup of sugar + 1/4 cup of baby oil or olive oil.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Step 1
          </h3>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3143" style="width: 610px">
           <a href="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-1-Prepare-the-coffee-in-a-container.jpg">
            <img alt="Take out the coffee" class="wp-image-3143" height="450" src="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-1-Prepare-the-coffee-in-a-container-1024x768.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Ready the coffee grinds
           </p>
          </div>
          <h3>
           Step 2
          </h3>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3144" style="width: 610px">
           <a href="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-2-Break-down-chunks-of-coffee.jpg">
            <img alt="Break down chunks of coffee" class="wp-image-3144" height="450" src="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-2-Break-down-chunks-of-coffee-1024x768.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Break down chunks using a utensil
           </p>
          </div>
          <h3>
           Step 3
          </h3>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3145" style="width: 610px">
           <a href="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-3-Add-the-sugar.jpg">
            <img alt=" Add the sugar" class="wp-image-3145" height="450" src="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-3-Add-the-sugar-1024x768.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Add the sugar &amp; then mix
           </p>
          </div>
          <h3>
           Step 4
          </h3>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3146" style="width: 610px">
           <a href="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-4-Add-baby-oil-and-then-mix.jpg">
            <img alt="Add baby oil and then mix" class="wp-image-3146" height="450" src="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-4-Add-baby-oil-and-then-mix-1024x768.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Add baby oil &amp; then mix again
           </p>
          </div>
          <h3>
           Step 5
          </h3>
          <p>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3147" style="width: 610px">
           <a href="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-5-Enjoy-it-in-the-shower.jpg">
            <img alt="Enjoy it in the shower" class="wp-image-3147" height="366" src="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Step-5-Enjoy-it-in-the-shower-1024x624.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Enjoy! It could get a little annoying when you have long nails.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Apply on the affected area. Use circular motions. Leave it on for a few minutes.Rinse with warm water and pat area dry with a towel.
          </p>
          <h2>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            My Experience
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           For the sake of another beauty experiment, I will show you my very own leg dimples. Heres the before &amp; after:
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3141" style="width: 610px">
           <a href="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Before-After-DIY-Coffee-Scrub.jpg">
            <img alt="DIY Coffee Scrub" class="wp-image-3141" height="348" src="http://getpretty.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Before-After-DIY-Coffee-Scrub-1024x593.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Before &amp; After
           </p>
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Was there a significant difference in your cellulite?
            </strong>
            Given that my leg dimples dont really show that much, I think I cant really say that their was a significant result. However, the back of my legs were smoother (
            <em>
             probably because of the sugar exfoliation
            </em>
            ) and theyappeared
            <strong>
             <em>
              less
             </em>
            </strong>
            dimple-y. The coffee scrub didnt make it 100% flawless but it lessened the appearance of the dimples.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Was it messy?
            </strong>
            YES! It went everywhere but it was refreshing at the same time.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Would you do this weekly?
            </strong>
            I think I will but Ill probably choose another recipe. I didnt really like the baby oil that was added. My skin felt moisturized after my bath but it also felt greasy to me.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Verdict
            </strong>
             Im giving this one the thumbs up. It did work (a little) but the recipe that I chose, I didnt really like. If it were another recipe that did not make my skin feel greasy, I would give this a two thumbs up. Better yet, Ill just try plain coffee grinds next time.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           I would love to hear about your experience with DIY-ing your very own coffee scrub! How was it and what recipe did you use? Leave your comments below.
