         </h1>

           <p>
            <strong>
             4. God of War
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <a href="http://filmonic.com/10-games-that-should-be-films-2010/hydra" rel="attachment wp-att-9054">
              <img alt="" class="lazy lazy-hidden alignnone size-full wp-image-9054" data-lazy-src="http://filmonic.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/hydra.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="263" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="500"/>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9054" height="263" src="http://filmonic.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/hydra.jpg" width="500"/>
              </noscript>
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            I just recently played through all three
            <strong>
             God of War
            </strong>
            games, and there are some freaking
            <em>
             epic
            </em>
            things going on. The titan Cronos crawls through a desert with the Temple of Pandora on his back. A temple. It takes main character Kratos
            <em>
             three days
            </em>
            to climb up Cronos, to reach this temple. Now
            <em>
             that
            </em>
            is big scale.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             God of War
            </strong>
            is a great big combo of loads of Greek mythology, which is a horrendously imaginative thing. Most of the gods appear, the titans, Icarus, Prometheus, Perseus, Hercules, Hephaestus, the hydra, Cerberus We go to the underworld, Mount Olympus, Athens, Sparta. Clinging onto Gaia as she ascends Olympus at the beginning of the third game is thrilling. The whole series is just brutal, violent, and epic. And its not something that
            <em>
             only
            </em>
            works as a game. Its the sort of fantasy that would be amazing if realised as a film. And a movie, or series of movies, would even filter out the awkward pacing of the games that comes as a result of frustratingly boring puzzles (
            <strong>
             God of War II
            </strong>
            received the prestigious award of First Game to Make Me Actually Throw My PS3 Controller).
           </p>

           <p>
            A
            <strong>
             God of War
             <em>
             </em>
            </strong>
            film has dropped in and out of news  Brett Ratner was attached at one point, then he wasnt, but then he was again. Id like to know just
            <em>
             who
            </em>
            thinks its a good idea to put it in his hands. I hope
            <em>
             his
            </em>
            version doesnt come to any fruition, and also that
            <strong>
             Clash of the Titans
            </strong>
            and
            <strong>
             Immortals
            </strong>
            (whose recent name change from
            <strong>
             War of the Gods
            </strong>
            likely had something to do with
            <strong>
             God of War
            </strong>
            )
            <strong>
            </strong>
            dont impend on this, as itd be a shame to see such an idea left behind because of 3D CG-fests.
           </p>
           <p>
            The greatest challenge though, would be that facing the writers  how to make Kratos
            <em>
             likeable
            </em>
            . Other characters would have to be muchly fleshed out too of course, but in the games, Kratos is just an asswipe and its impossible to sympathise with him in any way, shape or form. And you cant really have a film about someone who sets out basically to kill
            <em>
             everyone
            </em>
            . Can you?
           </p>
