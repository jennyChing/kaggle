       <h1 class="entry-title">
        How To Install a Grommet
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2014-10-13T06:00:33+00:00">
         October 13, 2014
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/author/the-sewing-loft/" rel="author">
           Heather
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/grommet-how-to/#comments">
          11 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         Have you ever used a grommet? If the answer is no, my question to you issimple,Why not? Grommets are one of my favorite types of trims to add on a project. I like to use them on everything from curtains to bags, heck even in home organizational projects. One of my most popular projects is the
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #993300;">
           <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/2014/03/31/scrap-bucket-pattern/" title="Stackable Scrap Bucket Pattern">
            <span style="color: #993300;">
             Scrap Bucket Bag
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets explore:
        </p>
        <h2>
         Grommet
        </h2>
        <p>
         noun
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           an eyelet placed in a hole in a sheet or panel to protect or insulate a rope or cable passed through it or to prevent the sheet or panel from being torn.
          </i>
         </li>
         <li>
          (MEDICINE) a tube surgically implanted to the eardrum to drain fluid from the middle ear.
         </li>
         <li>
          (INFORMAL) a young or inexperienced skier, snowboarder, surfer, or skateboarder
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Origin- Early 17th century (in nautical use in the sense a circle of rope used as a fastening): from obsolete French grommette, from gourmer to curb of unknown ultimate origin. Current senses date from the mid 20th century.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/us/definition/american_english/grommet" target="_blank">
          source
         </a>
         <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Eyelet-tools.jpeg">
          <img alt="Tips for setting grommets onto fabric. The Sewing Loft" class="alignright wp-image-175217" height="350" src="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Eyelet-tools.jpeg" width="230"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A standardgrommet packagewill include:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Eyelet Setter
         </li>
         <li>
          Anvil
         </li>
         <li>
          Eyelets
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Tools needed to install grommets:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          hammer
         </li>
         <li>
          hard flat surface
         </li>
         <li>
          pen for marking
         </li>
         <li>
          small sharp scissors
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The one thing I hear over and over again when teaching this pattern is just how muchpeople are scared to use the hammer. Lets chat about this. Grommets are really easy but they need a good whack of the hammer to be set properly. Here are someeasy steps to help you install a grommet on any project.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Grommet-How-To.jpeg">
          <img alt="Learn how to install a grommet. The Sewing Loft" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-175156" height="450" src="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Grommet-How-To.jpeg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2 style="color: #555555; text-align: center;">
         Grommet How To
        </h2>
        <p style="color: #555555; text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          This sewing tip is for all levels.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="color: #555555; text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Skill Level- 1 Button
         </strong>
        </p>
        <div data-indexer="1" style="color: #555555;">
         <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/" style="color: #c19286;">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter" data-indexer="1" height="38" src="http://thesewingloft.files.wordpress.com/2012/01/button.jpg?w=500" title="Button" width="48"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div data-indexer="1" style="color: #555555;">
        </div>
        <div data-indexer="1" style="color: #555555;">
         <p>
          <strong>
           Basic Instructions:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          1. Position and mark the inside of eyelet on your project. Cut out hole.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Grommet-Install-1.jpeg">
           <img alt="Learn how to install a grommet. The Sewing Loft" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-175271" height="300" src="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Grommet-Install-1.jpeg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          2. Insert the eyelet barrel in hole from the RIGHT side to the WRONG side of fabric. Place washer over barrel of eyelet, teeth side down.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Grommet-Install-3.jpeg">
           <img alt="Learn how to install a grommet. The Sewing Loft" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-175282" height="300" src="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Grommet-Install-3.jpeg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          3. Place anvil on a very hard surface, like the floor. Place eyelet in anvil, grab setter &amp; hammer.
         </p>
         <p>
          4.Position stud end of setter into the barrel of eyelet and hammer away. Give it a few whacks just to be sure.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Grommet-install.jpeg">
           <img alt="Learn how to install a grommet. The Sewing Loft" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-175264" height="378" src="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Grommet-install.jpeg" width="548"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Tip:
           </strong>
           I like to use the sidewalk as my hard surface. Place a towel on the ground to prevent yourproject from getting dirty or scratched.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Its not often to you hear the words grab the hammer in sewing but I promise this tool has a place in your workroom!
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://thesewingloftblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/signature1-e1357093552769.jpg">

         </p>
