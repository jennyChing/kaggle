         </p>

That means that when one body part gets affected, other ones can be influenced by this as well and that it is important to keep yourself as healthy and as clean (on the inside and on the outside) as possible.
         </p>

          Joint pain can be connected to the way you eat, believe it or not. And this is not just about eating the right foods, but also about eating in a balanced way and about avoiding foods that will most likely harm you at a certain point.

Aluminum, arsenic, mercury, bismuth, cadmium, antimony, lead and vanadium are those metal elements that can be very harsh on your body (and this includes your joints as well).
         </p>
         <p>
          These metals can influence the joints in at least three ways. First of all, they can influence the way other minerals, metals and nutrients are accumulated in your body. You should know that your bodys elements should be maintained in perfect balance and that even if one element is essential in your body, you should never excessively add it to your meals. For instance, zinc and copper are in a tight relationship and when the zinc levels fall, the copper levels rise and this can actually lead to the development of arthritis (and thus, to joint pain).
         </p>
         <p>
          Another way in which these metals can influence your joints is by depositing themselves in them. When there are excess amounts of certain metals in your body, they have to go somewhere and they deposit in between the joints, which affects the natural way your joints work.
         </p>

          Thirdly, certain elements can affect your joints and cause pain in their area by creating inflammation. Calcium, copper, manganese, mercury, nickel and other elements can be normally found in the human body, but when they reach excessive levels, they can create inflammation which is usually targeted in the joints between the bones.
