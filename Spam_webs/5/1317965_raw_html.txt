        <div class="body clearfix" data-stock-symbols="$NFLX " id="article_body" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article">
         <div data-part="contents" itemprop="articleBody">
          Our homes rank high on the list of potential money sucks. From heating to cleaning to keeping things in order, homeowners spend a lot of hard-earned cash maintaining their spaces.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Thankfully, that means our homes are also full of the potential to
          <a href="http://affordanything.com/fire" target="_blank">
           save (or even earn) us money
          </a>
          , and even small tweaks can have a
          <a href="http://affordanything.com/2014/06/10/the-ultimate-beginners-guide-to-financial-awesomeness/" target="_blank">
           big impact on your bottom line
          </a>
          . Let's go room by room and explore the savings opportunities under your roof.
          <div>
           <br/>
           <div class="knot-gallery" data-blogid="554" data-share-url="http://www.dailyfinance.com/photos/trim-your-household-budget-room-by-room/" data-slideshow-id="204762" data-slideshow-title="26 Ways to Trim Your Household Costs, Room by Room" data-thumbnails-only="">
           </div>
           <ul class="knot-slideshow-data" style="display:none;">
            <li class="knot-data-item">
             <div class="description">
              <ul>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 TV.
                </strong>
                Downgrade to a cheaper package or eliminate cable/satellite altogether and use services like Hulu and Netflix (
                <a href="http://www.dailyfinance.com/quote/nasdaq/nflx" target="_blank">
                 NFLX
                </a>
                ).
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Electronics.
                </strong>
                Put your electronics on a "smart" power strip to lower electricity costs. Turn off electronics and unplug phone chargers when they're not in use.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Lights.
                </strong>
                Replace your traditional bulbs with CFL or LED bulbs to cut energy costs dramatically.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Furniture.
                </strong>
                Shop second-hand, go to garage sales and comparison-shop online to get the best prices on some great, unusual pieces.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Heating and cooling.
                </strong>
                Get a programmable thermostat to help regulate your home's temperature based on when you're there and when you're away.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <a class="knot-anchor" data-credit="Gettystock" data-media-id="2744669" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/466/9/S2744669/slug/l/mother-and-daughter-playing-with-toys-on-living-room-floor-2.jpg" data-thumbnail-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/466/9/S2744669/slug/s/mother-and-daughter-playing-with-toys-on-living-room-floor-2.jpg" data-tweet-link="" data-type="image" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/466/9/S2744669/slug/l/mother-and-daughter-playing-with-toys-on-living-room-floor-2.jpg">
              Living room
             </a>
             <b class="knot-player">
             </b>
            </li>
            <li class="knot-data-item">
             <div class="description">
              <ul>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Appliances.
                </strong>
                Energy Star appliances may cost more to buy, but they're well worth it when it comes to the
                <a href="http://affordanything.com/2014/01/08/blindfolded-monkey-throwing-darts-stocks-beat-experts/" target="_blank">
                 long-term savings
                </a>
                .
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Water.
                </strong>
                Don't waste money on bottled water. Filter your own tap water with a faucet-mounted or pitcher-style filter and take it with you in a reusable water bottle.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Dishes.
                </strong>
                Only run the dishwasher when it's full, and let the dishes air-dry.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Cleaning.
                </strong>
                Use reusable sponges and dish cloths rather than paper towels. Make your own cleaning solutions from inexpensive household ingredients like ammonia, vinegar and baking soda.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <a class="knot-anchor" data-credit="Shutterstock" data-media-id="2744678" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/467/8/S2744678/slug/l/housewife-is-doing-the-dishes-2.jpg" data-thumbnail-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/467/8/S2744678/slug/s/housewife-is-doing-the-dishes-2.jpg" data-tweet-link="" data-type="image" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/467/8/S2744678/slug/l/housewife-is-doing-the-dishes-2.jpg">
              Kitchen
             </a>
             <b class="knot-player">
             </b>
            </li>
            <li class="knot-data-item">
             <div class="description">
              <ul>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Shower.
                </strong>
                Use a low-flow shower head and limit your shower time. (Sorry, long, hot showers are a no-no.)
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Sink.
                </strong>
                Turn off the water when you're brushing teeth or shaving.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Toilet.
                </strong>
                Invest in a dual-flush toilet, which has separate buttons allowing you to flush with a small amount of water or a large amount, depending on the job at hand.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <a class="knot-anchor" data-credit="Getty Images" data-media-id="2744680" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/0/S2744680/slug/l/father-and-son-brushing-teeth-in-domestic-bathroom-2.jpg" data-thumbnail-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/0/S2744680/slug/s/father-and-son-brushing-teeth-in-domestic-bathroom-2.jpg" data-tweet-link="" data-type="image" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/0/S2744680/slug/l/father-and-son-brushing-teeth-in-domestic-bathroom-2.jpg">
              Bathroom
             </a>
             <b class="knot-player">
             </b>
            </li>
            <li class="knot-data-item">
             <div class="description">
              <ul>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Lights.
                </strong>
                Put lights on a dimmer to use less energy late at night and early in the morning, when bright light is not necessary.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Heating.
                </strong>
                Bundle up in cold months with multiple covers and quilts, and turn the heat down when you're sleeping.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Closet.
                </strong>
                Clean out your closet and sell any unwanted or rarely used clothes and accessories.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <a class="knot-anchor" data-credit="Shutterstock" data-media-id="2744681" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/1/S2744681/slug/l/comfortable-bedroom-with-nice-2.jpg" data-thumbnail-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/1/S2744681/slug/s/comfortable-bedroom-with-nice-2.jpg" data-tweet-link="" data-type="image" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/1/S2744681/slug/l/comfortable-bedroom-with-nice-2.jpg">
              Bedroom
             </a>
             <b class="knot-player">
             </b>
            </li>
            <li class="knot-data-item">
             <div class="description">
              <ul>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Use it more.
                </strong>
                Instead of eating out, cook more dinners at home, and have friends over for a potlucks instead of going out to pricey restaurants.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <a class="knot-anchor" data-credit="Getty Images" data-media-id="2744682" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/2/S2744682/slug/l/happy-hispanic-family-enjoying-dinner-together-in-dining-room-2.jpg" data-thumbnail-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/2/S2744682/slug/s/happy-hispanic-family-enjoying-dinner-together-in-dining-room-2.jpg" data-tweet-link="" data-type="image" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/2/S2744682/slug/l/happy-hispanic-family-enjoying-dinner-together-in-dining-room-2.jpg">
              Dining room
             </a>
             <b class="knot-player">
             </b>
            </li>
            <li class="knot-data-item">
             <div class="description">
              <ul>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Phone.
                </strong>
                Get rid of your landline if you rarely use it. There's no point in having one if you're on your cell all the time anyway.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Internet.
                </strong>
                Get internet service from your phone or TV provider and bundle your services for savings.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <a class="knot-anchor" data-credit="Getty Images" data-media-id="2744683" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/3/S2744683/slug/l/caucasian-mother-working-in-home-office-and-holding-baby-2.jpg" data-thumbnail-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/3/S2744683/slug/s/caucasian-mother-working-in-home-office-and-holding-baby-2.jpg" data-tweet-link="" data-type="image" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/slideshows/images/slides/274/468/3/S2744683/slug/l/caucasian-mother-working-in-home-office-and-holding-baby-2.jpg">
              Home office
             </a>
             <b class="knot-player">
             </b>
            </li>
            <li class="knot-data-item">
             <div class="description">
              <ul>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Work out.
                </strong>
                Create a home gym and cancel your pricey gym membership.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Store food in bulk.
                </strong>
                Get a standalone freezer and create a pantry area with shelving units to store products purchased in bulk or on sale.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Laundry.
                </strong>

           In the meantime, you have shelled out thousands of dollars to buy those new appliances. Then, (hopefully) you have to s-l-o-w-l-y get that money back on lower utility bills. Worse yet, you might have paid for those purchases using a credit card.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           It's always smarter to approach with caution the idea of "saving money" by spending a lot of $$$ on some new purchase.
