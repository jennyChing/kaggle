       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         New Wedding Gift Etiquette: What You Need to Know Before You Send a Present
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="sep">
          Posted on
         </span>
         <a href="http://blog.lakeside.com/2014/06/18/new-wedding-gift-etiquette-what-you-need-to-know-before-you-send-a-present/" rel="bookmark" title="4:31 pm">
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-06-18T16:31:24+00:00">
           June 18, 2014
          </time>
         </a>
         <span class="by-author">
          <span class="sep">
           by
          </span>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://blog.lakeside.com/author/rachel-roszmann/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Rachel Roszmann">
            Rachel Roszmann
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         <a href="http://blog.lakeside.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/WeddingGift.jpg">
          <img alt="Wedding-Gift" class="alignleft wp-image-6874" height="341" src="http://blog.lakeside.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/WeddingGift-1024x681.jpg" width="514"/>
         </a>
         Wedding season is upon us.
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/sandy-malone/outdated-wedding-traditions_b_5497913.html" target="_blank">
          Wedding traditions
         </a>
         are changing. Though the wedding gift is still part of the customs, but wedding gift etiquette isnt what it used to be. Heres what you need to know before you send your gift.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          When
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Popular opinion is wedding gifts
         <i>
          can
         </i>
         be given within a year of the nuptials. That idea may be popular, but its not considered polite. Think about your own standards of etiquette when youre gift shopping. A good rule of thumb would be to give a wedding gift as soon after the wedding as you would expect a
         <a href="http://weddings.about.com/od/bridesandgrooms/a/thankyoucards.htm" target="_blank">
          thank you note
         </a>
         for the gift. You want to be thanked right away, so its only fair that you give the gift right away. If you need a hard deadline, three months is an acceptable amount of time to get your gift in the hands of the happy couple.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://blog.lakeside.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/WeddingGifts.jpg">
           <img alt="Wedding-gifts" class=" wp-image-6875 alignright" height="299" src="http://blog.lakeside.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/WeddingGifts-1024x682.jpg" width="450"/>
          </a>
          Where
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you were invited to the ceremony and you dont attend, its polite to send a gift anyway. If you go to the ceremony, its polite to give a gift  no matter how far you travel. If its a destination
         <a href="http://www.lakeside.com/Gifts/Wedding/cat52176.jmc">
          wedding
         </a>
         and you attend the wedding, its polite to give a gift. The only time its not tacky to
         <i>
          not
         </i>
         give a gift is if its the second wedding for either the bride or the groom and you gave a gift for the first wedding.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What and How Much
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Typically, gift registry information is not included in announcements. Ask the bride and groom where they are registered. How much you spend is mandated only by how much you
         <i>
          can
         </i>
         spend. The amount of money spent on each wedding is different, the income of every guest is different; all people are not expected to spend a set amount. Registries are handy for this; you can select a gift that matches your budget.
         <a href="http://www.realsimple.com/weddings/registry-gifts/great-wedding-gift-ideas-00100000082150/" target="_blank">
          Personal gifts
         </a>
         the couple wont forget or cash are also acceptable gifts. A good way to think about it is to let the relationship you have with the bride and groom  as well as your budget  dictate what kind of gift you give.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://blog.lakeside.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Bride-and-groom.jpg">
          <img alt="Bride-and-groom" class="alignleft wp-image-6876" height="299" src="http://blog.lakeside.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Bride-and-groom-1024x682.jpg" width="450"/>
         </a>
         The rules for giving gifts arent as strict as they once were. Couples are getting married later and have established jobs and live so they dont need a kick start to furnishing their homes the way couples once did. A gift is never expected (at least its not supposed to be), but a token to remember one of the most important days in a couples history is appreciated.
        </p>
