     <h1>
      Black Mr. Gay teaches new guilt theory to liberal white kids
     </h1>
     <div class="author">
      By
      <a href="/author/pedro_gonzales/">
       Pedro Gonzales
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="article_body top">
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         The
         <em>
          New York Times
         </em>
         had a long article about "
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2015/02/22/nyregion/at-new-york-private-schools-challenging-white-privilege-from-the-inside.html" target="_blank">
          diversity training
         </a>
         " at elite private schools in New York. In the finest tradition of Mao's Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution, white students were encouraged to sit around and brainstorm about the oppression caused by white privilege. And leading the sessions was a diversity agitator named Mr. Gay, who is also black. (Is this not the greatest name ever for a diversity trainer? Do you think that's his real name, or just the name he uses for work purposes?)
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          Students gathered in small groups to discuss a variety of social justice issues and participate in workshops ...
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
     </div>
     <div class="article_box_ad">
      <script type="text/javascript">
       &lt;!--

     <div class="article_body bottom">
      <blockquote>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         I'm old enough to remember when workshops used to involve cutting and sanding wood. Now that word is nearly the exclusive property of liberal brainwashers.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          Schools are asking white students and faculty members to examine their own race ... with a special emphasis on ... white privilege. During an exercise called Who Are You? Mr. Gay asked students to create their own identity cards, writing down terms they wanted to be associated with. One girl wrote white, SoHo and Sag Harbor". Then students paired up, with one responding to the question Who are you? The room erupted in noise, with students shouting, black, white, straight, lesbian, Jewish, Spanish and smart.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          Everyone has a card, Mr. Gay told the students. Its called an identity card. Society doesnt value each of these identities equally.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         I love how Mr. Gay inculcates racial animosity into the children. I don't care whether he's black or gay or whatever  he knows his stuff!
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          ... at a few schools, students and faculty members are starting white affinity groups, where they tackle issues of white privilege, often in all-white settings. At the
          <a href="http://www.riverdale.edu/" target="_blank">
           Riverdale Country School
          </a>
          in the Bronx, two white seniors started the Exploring Whiteness club[.]
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         Do you thinkit's racist or progressive for white students to have white-only groups where they talk about the evils of white oppression? Discuss!
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          Today white privilege studies center on the systemic nature of racism as well as the way it exposes minorities to daily moments of stress and unpleasantness 
          <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/22/us/as-diversity-increases-slights-get-subtler-but-still-sting.html" target="_blank">
           sometimes referred to as micro-aggressions.
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/22/us/as-diversity-increases-slights-get-subtler-but-still-sting.html">
           
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         I feel such sympathy for diversity consultants, because it is getting harder and harder to find systematic examples of racism in our society. That's why we need to focus on really subtle examples of racism, whether real or imagined. I'm glad our next generation is de-emphasizing mundane topics like math, science, reading, and writing to focus on the truly important topics, like oppression by race.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         As our workforce gets more and more multicultural, we need to be trained to think about every interaction inracial, class, and gender dimensions. Some shortsighted individuals may say that this will lead to increased tensions and imaginary slights, but isn't that a small price to pay to have a society that lives on a higher plane of social consciousness?
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          The new focus on addressing white privilege has not been an unmitigated success. Dr. Moore, for example, is no longer working with Brooklyn Friends. Acknowledging the inherent tension, he said: Not every student is saying: I want to talk about white privilege. Give me the best book.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         And get someone like George R.R. Martin to write it  compelling, full of easy-to-read fiction, and collectively it can be 5,000 pages or more!
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          At
          <a href="http://www.ecfs.org/" target="_blank">
           the Ethical Culture Fieldston School
          </a>
          , a plan this winter to roll out a racial awareness workshop series for third through fifth graders was met with fierce resistance by parents. Many objected that children as young as 8 were being asked to segregate themselves into race-based affinity groups.. A fifth graders father, called the plan mind-boggling and said his daughter found the entire concept confusing and unsettling.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          At Brooklyn Friends, some white students complained that Dr. Moore was a polarizing figure whose focus on white privilege made them uncomfortable. Both Dr. Moore and a school representative described his departure as amicable.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         It's very sad that these white liberal students are no longer being subjected to self-struggle sessions. I'm very disappointed in their revolutionary consciousness and wonder if their subconscious racism is reasserting itself.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         Anyway, Ihave an even better idea. Why not make students guilty for being leftists? Make them wear names saying "global warming hoaxer," "race-monger," "class warfare specialist," "big-government nanny," and make them explain their guilt for supporting leftist policies.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         <img alt="" src="http://admin.americanthinker.com/images/bucket/2015-02/194166_5_.png" style="margin:4px"/>
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         Exit question: I don't think I've ever seen a white diversity trainer, and certainly not a white male one. If minorities are given preferences for this kind of job, what is the ranking order? Of the following applicants for diversity trainer, which do you think would be most likely to be hired: a black man named Mr. Gay, a gay man named Mr. Black, a Hispanic woman named Mrs. Lesbian, or a lesbian woman named Mr. Transgender?
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>

         " at elite private schools in New York. In the finest tradition of Mao's Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution, white students were encouraged to sit around and brainstorm about the oppression caused by white privilege. And leading the sessions was a diversity agitator named Mr. Gay, who is also black. (Is this not the greatest name ever for a diversity trainer? Do you think that's his real name, or just the name he uses for work purposes?)
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          Students gathered in small groups to discuss a variety of social justice issues and participate in workshops ...
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         I'm old enough to remember when workshops used to involve cutting and sanding wood. Now that word is nearly the exclusive property of liberal brainwashers.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          Schools are asking white students and faculty members to examine their own race ... with a special emphasis on ... white privilege. During an exercise called Who Are You? Mr. Gay asked students to create their own identity cards, writing down terms they wanted to be associated with. One girl wrote white, SoHo and Sag Harbor". Then students paired up, with one responding to the question Who are you? The room erupted in noise, with students shouting, black, white, straight, lesbian, Jewish, Spanish and smart.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          Everyone has a card, Mr. Gay told the students. Its called an identity card. Society doesnt value each of these identities equally.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         I love how Mr. Gay inculcates racial animosity into the children. I don't care whether he's black or gay or whatever  he knows his stuff!
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          ... at a few schools, students and faculty members are starting white affinity groups, where they tackle issues of white privilege, often in all-white settings. At the
          <a href="http://www.riverdale.edu/" target="_blank">
           Riverdale Country School
          </a>
          in the Bronx, two white seniors started the Exploring Whiteness club[.]
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         Do you thinkit's racist or progressive for white students to have white-only groups where they talk about the evils of white oppression? Discuss!
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          Today white privilege studies center on the systemic nature of racism as well as the way it exposes minorities to daily moments of stress and unpleasantness 
          <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/22/us/as-diversity-increases-slights-get-subtler-but-still-sting.html" target="_blank">
           sometimes referred to as micro-aggressions.
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/22/us/as-diversity-increases-slights-get-subtler-but-still-sting.html">
           
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         I feel such sympathy for diversity consultants, because it is getting harder and harder to find systematic examples of racism in our society. That's why we need to focus on really subtle examples of racism, whether real or imagined. I'm glad our next generation is de-emphasizing mundane topics like math, science, reading, and writing to focus on the truly important topics, like oppression by race.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         As our workforce gets more and more multicultural, we need to be trained to think about every interaction inracial, class, and gender dimensions. Some shortsighted individuals may say that this will lead to increased tensions and imaginary slights, but isn't that a small price to pay to have a society that lives on a higher plane of social consciousness?
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          The new focus on addressing white privilege has not been an unmitigated success. Dr. Moore, for example, is no longer working with Brooklyn Friends. Acknowledging the inherent tension, he said: Not every student is saying: I want to talk about white privilege. Give me the best book.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         And get someone like George R.R. Martin to write it  compelling, full of easy-to-read fiction, and collectively it can be 5,000 pages or more!
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          At
          <a href="http://www.ecfs.org/" target="_blank">
           the Ethical Culture Fieldston School
          </a>
          , a plan this winter to roll out a racial awareness workshop series for third through fifth graders was met with fierce resistance by parents. Many objected that children as young as 8 were being asked to segregate themselves into race-based affinity groups.. A fifth graders father, called the plan mind-boggling and said his daughter found the entire concept confusing and unsettling.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:14px">
         <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
          At Brooklyn Friends, some white students complained that Dr. Moore was a polarizing figure whose focus on white privilege made them uncomfortable. Both Dr. Moore and a school representative described his departure as amicable.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         It's very sad that these white liberal students are no longer being subjected to self-struggle sessions. I'm very disappointed in their revolutionary consciousness and wonder if their subconscious racism is reasserting itself.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         Anyway, Ihave an even better idea. Why not make students guilty for being leftists? Make them wear names saying "global warming hoaxer," "race-monger," "class warfare specialist," "big-government nanny," and make them explain their guilt for supporting leftist policies.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         <img alt="" src="http://admin.americanthinker.com/images/bucket/2015-02/194166_5_.png" style="margin:4px"/>
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">
         Exit question: I don't think I've ever seen a white diversity trainer, and certainly not a white male one. If minorities are given preferences for this kind of job, what is the ranking order? Of the following applicants for diversity trainer, which do you think would be most likely to be hired: a black man named Mr. Gay, a gay man named Mr. Black, a Hispanic woman named Mrs. Lesbian, or a lesbian woman named Mr. Transgender?
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size:14px">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif">

        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
