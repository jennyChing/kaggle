      <h1>
       15 Tips for Boosting Your Productivity
      </h1>
      <article about="/accountemps/blog/15-tips-for-boosting-your-productivity" class="node node--national-blog node--full node--national-blog--full" role="article" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">
       <header>
        <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="15 Tips for Boosting Your Productivity" property="dc:title">
        </span>
       </header>
       <div class="node__content">
        <div class="field field--name-field-select-image-style field--type-iss field--label-hidden">
         <div class="field__items">
          <div class="field__item even">
           <img height="298" src="http://www.roberthalf.com/sites/default/files/styles/blog_image_no_style/public/Media_Root/images/blog-at/accountemps_productivity_final_thumbnail_520.jpg?itok=zGLv2eCc" typeof="foaf:Image" width="520"/>

         </span>

        <div class="field field--name-field-body-i18n field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden">
         <div class="field__items">
          <div class="field__item even">
           <p>
            Friday might be fun, but Tuesdays the day to get stuff done. Tuesday once again ranked as the most productive day of the week, according to a new
            <a href="http://www.roberthalf.com/accountemps/about-us" target="_blank">
             Accountemps
            </a>
            survey of managers. The second day of the business week has held the top spot in similar productivity surveys since 1987. Check out our infographic or slideshow for research highlights and other historical productivity statistics.
           </p>
           <p>
            How can you turn every workday into a Tuesday? Here are 15 tips for maximizing your productivity:
           </p>
           <ol>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Axe the excess.
             </strong>
             Too often workers overestimate what they can accomplish and become frustrated by their lack of progress. Try a shorter, more realistic to-do list that leaves room for unexpected projects and interruptions will help you be more productive.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong style="font-size: inherit;">
              Aim for quality, not quantity.
             </strong>
             Multitasking seems like a good way to increase productivity, but repeatedly switching gears is mentally taxing and can slow you down. Do your best to focus on one item at a time.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong style="font-size: inherit;">
              Know your prime time.
             </strong>
             Use your internal clock to your advantage by tackling critical or challenging assignments during the
             <a href="http://accountemps.rhi.mediaroom.com/least_productive" style="font-size: inherit;" target="_blank">
              time of day
             </a>
             when youre most alert and productive.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong style="font-size: inherit;">
              Dodge derailers.
             </strong>
             When working on important assignments, boost productivity by turning off mobile devices and signing out of email and social media. Doing so allows you to give full attention to the task at hand. Also, dont be afraid to occasionally post a do not disturb sign in your workspace.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong style="font-size: inherit;">
              Explore apps.
             </strong>
             Take advantage of the wide selection of software specifically designed to help people increase productivity, keep track of projects, meet deadlines and be more organized.
             <a href="http://evernote.com/evernote/" style="font-size: inherit;" target="_blank">
              Evernote
             </a>
             and
             <a href="http://www.focusboosterapp.com/" style="font-size: inherit;" target="_blank">
              Focus Booster
             </a>
             are two free apps worth trying.
             <br/>
             <!--more-->
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Break down big projects.
             </strong>
             Intimidation can lead to procrastination. So break big, complicated projects into smaller, more manageable tasks. Youll feel less overwhelmed. Plus, youll gain a sense of accomplishment each time you reach an incremental milestone.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Say no.
             </strong>
             Sometimes you simply cant take on another assignment no matter how small. Give yourself permission to say no at times. While you want to build a reputation as a team player, you cant afford to become a doormat in the process.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Closely monitor your online reading habits.
             </strong>
             Keep your focus when online. If youre not disciplined, one second youre reading an industry report, the next youre scanning Facebook and a few minutes later youre staring at a post about
             <a href="http://www.buzzfeed.com/notmonroe/16-glorious-ways-to-make-mac-and-cheese-gg32" target="_blank">
              16 Glorious Ways To Make Mac NCheese
             </a>
             .
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Take care of your health.
             </strong>
             Productivity at work can hinge on what you do
             <em>
              off
             </em>
             the job. Eat healthy, exercise regularly and get adequate sleep. Being out of balance in any of those three areas can throw off your ability to concentrate.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong style="font-size: inherit;">
              Mitigate meeting mania.
             </strong>
             Humorist Dave Barry once observed: If you had to identify, in one word, the reason why the human race has not achieved, and never will achieve, its full potential, that word would be
             <em style="font-size: inherit;">
              meetings
             </em>
             . Rethink routine meetings that have outlived their purpose. Or prune meeting times by 25 percent to force yourself and others to stay on point.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Give yourself a break.
             </strong>
             It sounds counterintuitive, but taking breaks can help you get more done. Solutions to complex problems often surface when were not intently focused on the issue. Likewise, getting some fresh air or engaging in water cooler banter can help you recharge.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Clean up your act.
             </strong>
             If you regularly lose time because youre scrambling to find files, come in early or stay late one day to do a clean sweep of your desk. Then, get into the habit of investing a few minutes each day to tidy up.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Recondition your email reflexes.
             </strong>
             Chances are you read email the instant it arrives. Fight the impulse to immediately react when you hear the pinging sound of an incoming message. Pick specific times to check your inbox throughout the day.
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Bring in reinforcements.
             </strong>
             If everyone is efficient and productive, yet your team is still falling behind, consider bringing in temporary reinforcements. Hiring
             <a href="http://www.roberthalf.com/accountemps/staffing-services" target="_blank">
              specialized temporary professionals
             </a>
             is a cost-effective way to meet rising business demands while lifting the burden off overworked core employees.
             <strong>
             </strong>
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Communicate clearly.
             </strong>
             When relaying information or giving instructions, make sure youre as clear as possible. When in doubt,
             <em>
              overcommunicate
             </em>
             . Covering the details upfront will save you significant time answering questions or making corrections later.
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            <em>
             Do you have other tips for boosting productivity? Dont keep them a secret! Share them in the comments below.
            </em>
           </p>
