                    <img class="image0" id="auto_img_128244" onerror="get_img(this.id,'128244')" src="http://images.speakingtree.iimg.in/files/rsz/fit_s_100x75/files/04-2014/180363/aab3238922bcc25a6f606eb525ffdc56_1397564416.jpg"/>
                   </div>
                  </a>
                 </dfn>
                 <textarea id="desc_128244" style="visibility:hidden; display:none;">
                  Shri Guru Datta is commonly portrayed as being accompanied by 4 dogs of 4 different
colors. These 4 dogs represent the following:
The 4 Vedas: Rig Veda, Sama Veda, Yayur Veda and Atharva Veda
The 4 Yugas: Krita Yuga, Treta Yuga, Dwapara Yuga and Kali Yuga
The 4 states of the Word: Para (beyond), Pasyanti (perception); Madhyama
(conception), Vaikhari (vocal)
The 4 states of Existence: Existence, Awareness, Thought and Speech or Action.
                 </textarea>
                 <div style="float: left; width: 102px; text-align:center; margin-top:3px;">
                  <a class="curnum" href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128244" id="slidenum_128244">

                </li>
                <li cid="325281" id="thumb_128246" sid="128246" title="The four Dogs of Lord Dattatreya">
                 <dfn>
                  <a alt="The four Dogs of Lord Dattatreya" href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128246" imgindex="128246" title="http://images.speakingtree.iimg.in/files/rsz/fit_s_320x240/files/04-2014/180363/9bf31c7ff062936a96d3c8bd1f8f2ff3_1397564418.jpg">
                   <div align="center" onclick="displaySlideshowPopUp(128246)">
                    <img class="image0" id="auto_img_128246" onerror="get_img(this.id,'128246')" src="http://images.speakingtree.iimg.in/files/rsz/fit_s_100x75/files/04-2014/180363/9bf31c7ff062936a96d3c8bd1f8f2ff3_1397564418.jpg"/>
                   </div>
                  </a>
                 </dfn>
                 <textarea id="desc_128246" style="visibility:hidden; display:none;">
                  In the pre-Vedic era, dogs were regarded as auspicious. It was believed
that deities assumed the form of dogs. These animals were also often linked to fidelity
and Veera, the quality of great courage, closely associated with warriors. Dogs can be
both wild and tame, thereby representing the qualities of fidelity and devotion.
The dog is one of the most sublime symbols of the Vedic system. The dog is capable of
sharp auditory capability and has a perception far more advanced than that of human
beings.
                 </textarea>
                 <div style="float: left; width: 102px; text-align:center; margin-top:3px;">
                  <a class="curnum" href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128246" id="slidenum_128246">
                   19

                  <input id="slide_128246" type="hidden" value="18"/>
                  <input id="index_18" type="hidden" value="128246"/>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li cid="325281" id="thumb_128248" sid="128248" title="Dogs and spirituality ">
                 <dfn>
                  <a alt="Dogs and spirituality " href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128248" imgindex="128248" title="https://farm3.staticflickr.com/2866/8748994773_664519ab28_n.jpg">
                   <div align="center" onclick="displaySlideshowPopUp(128248)">
                    <img class="image0" id="auto_img_128248" onerror="get_img(this.id,'128248')" src="https://farm3.staticflickr.com/2866/8748994773_664519ab28_t.jpg"/>
                   </div>
                  </a>
                 </dfn>
                 <textarea id="desc_128248" style="visibility:hidden; display:none;">
                  Greek mythology too believes in a three-headed dog, called Hydra, that keeps vigil at the
gates of heaven and hell. The dog, being a symbol of alertness and vigilance, also
represents the 3 levels of physical sound. The dog is also capable of great love and compassion, which is far beyond the
comprehension of human beings. Cultivating these qualities helps human beings go
beyond their physical and bodily limitations to finally realize the Absolute Truth.
                 </textarea>
                 <div style="float: left; width: 102px; text-align:center; margin-top:3px;">
                  <a class="curnum" href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128248" id="slidenum_128248">

                <li cid="325281" id="thumb_128250" sid="128250" title="The Cow of Lord Dattatreya">
                 <dfn>
                  <a alt="The Cow of Lord Dattatreya" href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128250" imgindex="128250" title="http://images.speakingtree.iimg.in/files/rsz/fit_s_320x240/files/04-2014/180363/c20ad4d76fe97759aa27a0c99bff6710_1397564395.jpg">
                   <div align="center" onclick="displaySlideshowPopUp(128250)">
                    <img class="image0" id="auto_img_128250" onerror="get_img(this.id,'128250')" src="http://images.speakingtree.iimg.in/files/rsz/fit_s_100x75/files/04-2014/180363/c20ad4d76fe97759aa27a0c99bff6710_1397564395.jpg"/>
                   </div>
                  </a>
                 </dfn>
                 <textarea id="desc_128250" style="visibility:hidden; display:none;">
                  Datta is also accompanied by a white cow - in fact, the
cow is protected by the deity. According to Vedic
tradition, the cow embodies creation, which nourishes
its beings with its milk. Drinking the cows milk,
therefore, is symbolic of receiving that nourishment
from the creative cosmic forces; also indicating
growth, satiation, contentment, health and joy. The
cow embodies the nurturing aspect of the Universe
and hence, it is given the utmost respect in Hinduism.
                 </textarea>
                 <div style="float: left; width: 102px; text-align:center; margin-top:3px;">
                  <a class="curnum" href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128250" id="slidenum_128250">

                 </div>
                </li>
                <li cid="325281" id="thumb_128252" sid="128252" title="The Cow of Lord Dattatreya">
                 <dfn>
                  <a alt="The Cow of Lord Dattatreya" href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128252" imgindex="128252" title="http://images.speakingtree.iimg.in/files/rsz/fit_s_320x240/files/04-2014/180363/6ea9ab1baa0efb9e19094440c317e21b_1397564406.jpg">
                   <div align="center" onclick="displaySlideshowPopUp(128252)">
                    <img class="image0" id="auto_img_128252" onerror="get_img(this.id,'128252')" src="http://images.speakingtree.iimg.in/files/rsz/fit_s_100x75/files/04-2014/180363/6ea9ab1baa0efb9e19094440c317e21b_1397564406.jpg"/>
                   </div>
                  </a>
                 </dfn>
                 <textarea id="desc_128252" style="visibility:hidden; display:none;">
                  In this system of thought and philosophy, anything
that is capable of creating should be given the same
respect as of the cow. Hence, Hinduism stipulates that
women, the planet Earth, the solar system and the
entire cosmic system be given the paramount respect.
It is for this very reason that Datta is shown protecting
and nurturing the cow, which bestows happiness,
peace, health and joy.
                 </textarea>
                 <div style="float: left; width: 102px; text-align:center; margin-top:3px;">
                  <a class="curnum" href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128252" id="slidenum_128252">

                <li cid="325281" id="thumb_128254" sid="128254" title="Comparison between Datta and Krishna">
                 <dfn>
                  <a alt="Comparison between Datta and Krishna" href="http://www.speakingtree.in/spiritual-slideshow/seekers/god-and-i/the-secret-life-of-lord-dattatreya/128254" imgindex="128254" title="https://farm1.staticflickr.com/48/113687894_712303c649_n.jpg">
                   <div align="center" onclick="displaySlideshowPopUp(128254)">
                    <img class="image0" id="auto_img_128254" onerror="get_img(this.id,'128254')" src="https://farm1.staticflickr.com/48/113687894_712303c649_t.jpg"/>
                   </div>
                  </a>
                 </dfn>
                 <textarea id="desc_128254" style="visibility:hidden; display:none;">
                  Lord Krishna is also shown being accompanied by a
cow, for the same reason. There are great similarities in their attitudes and
personalities. Through his life story, Lord Krishna ably demonstrated
his threefold ability to create like Lord Brahma, to
preserve like Lord Vishnu and to destroy like Lord
Shiva. Datta too is ascribed these qualities.
While Datta is considered the Master of Yoga, Lord
Krishna taught the Science of Yoga through Bhagavad Gita, which is a
conglomeration of Yoga Vidya, Brahma Vidya and the
Upanishads.
                 </textarea>
