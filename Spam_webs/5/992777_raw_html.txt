             <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
              Sauerkraut Pierogi Skillet
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-38272016049195577" itemprop="description articleBody">
              <center>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                Out of all the pierogi that I make, the sauerkraut is our favorite.
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                This is a quick way to get all the flavors of sauerkraut pierogi without having to make the dumplings. I've seen quite a few recipes for "lazy sauerkraut pierogi" using spiral macaroni and mushroom soup. You could add some sauteed mushrooms to this dish - but no soup -!
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                I used my
                <a href="http://www.comfycook.com/2010/12/grandmas-sauerkraut.html" target="_blank">
                 Mom's Sauerkraut
                </a>
                recipe, but you could use it plain or add a bit of bacon!
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
              </center>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-syDQqe7jbvw/VCBig71KCkI/AAAAAAAAGx4/oFDKnxjPyng/s1600/Noodles%2B%26%2BKraut%2B2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-syDQqe7jbvw/VCBig71KCkI/AAAAAAAAGx4/oFDKnxjPyng/s1600/Noodles%2B%26%2BKraut%2B2.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <center>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                1 - 12 oz. pkg. No Yolks dumpling noodles (or noodles or your choice), cooked according to package directions.
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                1 recipe
                <a href="http://www.comfycook.com/2010/12/grandmas-sauerkraut.html" target="_blank">
                 Grandma's Sauerkraut
                </a>
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                1 medium onion, chopped
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                1 stick unsalted butter
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                Freshly ground black pepper
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                Sour Cream (optional)
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                <br/>
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                Prepare Grandma's Sauerkraut. If it is a little too much sauerkraut for you, save some for hot dogs!
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add chopped onion and saute until light golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                Add cooked noodles, sauerkraut, 1/2 cup of sauerkraut juice and stir well to combine.
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                Season with freshly ground black pepper and a dollop of sour cream.
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                <br/>
               </b>
              </center>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-y8V0wFCo3Pk/VCBp3VtXPAI/AAAAAAAAGyE/Y50L1gYvp8A/s1600/Noodles3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="426" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-y8V0wFCo3Pk/VCBp3VtXPAI/AAAAAAAAGyE/Y50L1gYvp8A/s1600/Noodles3.jpg" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <center>
               <b>
                <br/>
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
               <b>
                Makes a great side dish with pork chops!
               </b>
              </center>
              <center>
