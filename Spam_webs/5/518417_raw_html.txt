          <div class="entry-content">
           <p>
            Bottles of champagne and prosecco begin to disappear from shelves at
            <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/2nd-Street-Liquor-Wine/413234395404636">
             Second Street Discount Liquor and Wine
            </a>
            in Casper about two or three days before New Years Eve, as people amass bottles for parties.
           </p>
           <p>
            Christmas, on the other hand, is good for wine sales, said Andy Czellecz, a managing partner at the store. People desire it with Christmas dinner or purchase fine wines as gifts for friends and family.
           </p>
           <p>
            In Wyoming, people enjoy wines on the sweeter side, according to sales data from the
            <a href="http://eliquor.wyoming.gov">
             Wyoming Department of Revenues liquor division
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Wyoming wine drinkers also like value.
           </p>
           <p>
            Two boxed wines made the top five brands sold in the state this year -- Black Box cabernet sauvignon and Franzia chillable red -- although in some cases, those wines may be the house wine at a bar or restaurant. The liquor division sells wine to bars, restaurants and liquor stores.
           </p>
           <p>
            During the holidays, people enjoy their libations more, Czellecz said.
           </p>
           <p>
            That also includes craft beers, although the state doesnt track sales by brand. While spirits and wine are distributed from a state warehouse in Cheyenne, beer is sold through local companies.
           </p>
           <p>
            Three days before Christmas, Brianna Crowe, of Casper, purchased a bottle of Barefoot moscato, the states fifth-most-popular wine.
           </p>
           <p>
            I like it because its sweet, and it tastes like juice, she said.
           </p>
           <p>
            Among champagnes at Second Street Discount Liquor, the most popular brands are Andre, Cooks and Barefoot, Czellecz said.
           </p>
           <p>
            People get their (Christmas) bonuses, and they want to treat themselves to something nice, Czellecz said. So they get a bottle of wine.
           </p>
