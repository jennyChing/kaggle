     <p>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span>
       les grandes tables by 1024 architecture, boulogne-billancourt,
      </span>
      <span>
       france
       <br/>
       image  designboom
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      before its debut, designboom visited the 
      <a href="http://www.lesgrandestables.com/" target="_blank" title="">
       les grandes tables de lle seguin
      </a>
      , a temporary restaurant on seguin island
      <span>
       in boulogne-billancourt, france, which officially opened to the public on september 12, 2011.
      </span>
      french practice
      <a href="http://www.1024architecture.net/" target="_blank" title="">
       1024 architecture
      </a>
      has completed
      <span>
       the ephemeral structure nestled within an extensive garden provides elevated views of
      </span>
      <span>
       the seine river and
      </span>
      <span>
       surrounding vegetation. after its two year lifespan, the delicate framework will be disassembled and removed without leaving a trace of its existence.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span>
       <br/>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="614" src="/weblog/images/images_2/lauren/les%20grandes/lg02.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <span>
       exterior view
       <br/>
       image  designboom
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span>
       guests enter the interior and outdoor terrace dining areas through an open-air staircase or through the red elevator tower.
      </span>
      steel tubes arranged and connected similar to construction scaffolding support and encase an elongated rectangular wooden volume. a translucent glass facade encompasses the outer shell and becomes a glowing lantern at night.
     </p>
