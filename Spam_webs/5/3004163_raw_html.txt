      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       Amazon Ups Stakes In Payments War, Undercuts Square And PayPal
      </h1>
      <!-- Make individual group author appear -->

      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <figure class="featured_image">
         <img alt="" itemprop="image" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/samanthasharf/files/2014/08/Capture-e1407937144574.jpg"/>
         <figcaption>
          A screenshot of the Amazon Local Register site.
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         In a
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/stevenbertoni/2014/02/12/can-paypal-beat-apple-google-amazon-and-icahn-in-the-wallet-wars/">
          FORBES cover story
         </a>
         earlier this year Steven Bertoni described the race to make money mobile as the wallet wars.
         <a href="/companies/amazon/">
          Amazon.com
         </a>
         <span class="quotecard_hook" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-naturalid="fred/company/196" data-quotes-closing="455.9" data-quotes-now="465.76" data-ticker="AMZN" data-type="organization">
          <span class="wrapper">
           <a href="/companies/amazon/">
            Amazon.com
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         just fired a major shot.
        </p>
        <p>
         Wednesday morning the retail giant introduced Amazon Local Register, a mobile credit card reader designed to directly compete with Square and PayPal Here.
        </p>
        <p>
         We understand that every penny and every minute counts, said Vice President of Amazon Local Commerce Matt Swann in a statement after listing off business that might find the tool useful such as food trucks, contractors and accountants. We want to make accepting payments so easy and inexpensive that it no longer gets in the way of a business owner doing what they love  serving their customers and growing their business.
        </p>
        <p>
         Business that sign up for the point-of-sales system before October 31, 2014 will pay a promotional rate of 1.75% per card swipe on major credit and debit cards until January 1, 2016. Then the price will in increase to 2.5% per swipe. Merchants who sign up after October will also pay the 2.5% rate.
        </p>
        <p>
         Even the eventual higher rate beats the competition. Square, which was founded by Twitter billionaire
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/profile/jack-dorsey/">
          Jack Dorsey
         </a>
         , charges 2.75% per swipe. eBay owned PayPal Here  the digital payment giants physical reader  charges 2.7%.
        </p>
        <p>
         Shares of Amazon were up about 3% as high as $330.50 in morning trading on the news. While eBay shares were down about 1.5% as low as $52.50. Square is privately held.
        </p>
        <p>
         From FORBES February PayPal cover story:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          [Then PayPal president David] Marcus and PayPal have neither the luxury of glitches nor of waiting. Money is going mobile, and the race is on to control the flow of bits and cash across a billion smartphones and at millions of online and physical locations. Research firm Gartner estimates that mobile payments will top $720 billion a year by 2017, up from $235 billion last year. The upside remains enormous: Humans made $15 trillion worth of retail transactions in 2013. Whoever ends up with controlling interests in this new digital ecosystem will reap billions in transaction fees, collect massive amounts of consumer data and control the type of targeted advertising that makes marketers drool. Giants such as Apple, Amazon, Google, Visa and MasterCard all want to be your mobile digital wallet, as do several well-financed startups, including Square, founded by Twitter billionaire Jack Dorsey.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         In June
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/stevenbertoni/2014/06/09/david-marcus-quits-paypal-for-facebook-zuck-must-have-big-plans-for-payment/">
          Marcus left PayPal to run Facebook Messenger
         </a>
         . The move may suggest that Mark Zuckerberg is looking to move into the payment space as well. No matter what Facebook does,Amazon likely wont be the last competitor to jump in.
        </p>
        <p>
