         <h1 class="entry-title">
          No, I Dont Organize Closets For A Living But I Like The Way You Think
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content content">
         <p>
          Acouple of weeks ago
          <a href="http://www.giveandtake.com/Home/AdamGrant" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.giveandtake.com/Home/AdamGrant', 'Adam Grant']);">
           <strong>
            Adam Grant
           </strong>
          </a>
          (who is a total research rock star in my book)wrote
          <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/adam-grant/why-behavioral-economics_b_5491960.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.huffingtonpost.com/adam-grant/why-behavioral-economics_b_5491960.html', 'Why Behavioral Economics Is Cool, and Im Not']);">
           Why Behavioral Economics Is Cool, and Im Not
          </a>
          over atthe Huffington Post. Heres a snippet:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #0000ff;">
            When they think of psychology, they picture Sigmund Freud lying on a couch telling them they failed a driving test because they failed to kill their fathers and sleep with their mothers. (If you object to that, dont worry; youre in denial.)
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #0000ff;">
            Psychology has come a long way since Freud, but the brand hasnt caught up. The new psychological science of the mind and behavior is based on randomized, controlled experiments with measurable behaviors as well as fMRI and physiological data. Ideally, well start rebranding psychology as a source of interesting, rigorous ideas. Alternatively, Kahneman
            <a href="http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2013/04/26/daniel-kahneman-s-gripe-with-behavioral-economics.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2013/04/26/daniel-kahneman-s-gripe-with-behavioral-economics.html', 'proposes']);" style="color: #683db9;" target="_hplink">
             <span style="color: #0000ff;">
              proposes
             </span>
            </a>
            that when it comes to formulating policy, we should stop drawing major boundaries between fields and just call ourselves behavioral scientists.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #993366;">
            <span style="color: #0000ff;">
             Either way, lets say goodbye, Freud.
            </span>
            <br/>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Its a great read  especially for any fellow I-Os. The article got me thinking about the various
          <strong>
           misconceptions folks carry about psychology
          </strong>
          in general, specificallyabout
          <strong>
           I-O psychology.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           If you havent heard of industrial-organizational (I-O) psychology, youre not alone. By a long shot
          </strong>
          . I-O psychology the study of people at work. That includes the things that affect behavior, attitudes, personalities, and motivation at work. People with a background in I-O are often referred to as I-Os (creative, I know). I-Os work in universities as professors and researchers. They also work for organizations focusing on every place that people intersect with their work.
         </p>
         <p>
          A lot of people hearindustrial-organizational psychology try to figure out what it means from the name. Andif you were going off of the name they make total sense. Plus, industrial-organizational psychologyis not very descriptive (unless its explained), its too long, and its still up for debate.
         </p>
         <p>
          Anyway, Ive had three come up that are my all time favorites. If you just hear Industrial-Organizational Psychology, they make total sense so Im not judging. I actually think theyre pretty smart for making the connections. But theyre still wrong and I still think theyre funny, and who couldnt use a little Thursday humor:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           <strong>
            So I should only come to you if Im
            <em>
             really
            </em>
            screwed up? Got it!
           </strong>
           She was thinkingindustrial as inindustrial strength. Love it. But please do not come to me if you so screwed up that the standard grade psychologist couldnt help you if they cant, then I
           <em>
            really
           </em>
           cant. Industrial Strength Psychologyis a good start to atitle for someones bookthough just sayin.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Thank God I met you! Ive needed help for a while with my closet. Whats your going rate?
           </strong>
           Get it? Organizational psychologist? While I dont have any training associated with cleaning out any room of your home, lucky for you I happen to have a secretlove of color coding.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            It must be really hard to find jobs in the U.S. with so many of our companies outsourcing factory work.
           </strong>
           Industrial is often associated with factories. I can work anywhere there is work being done by people  no factory required!
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Adam Grant is right psychology needis in need of some seriousrebranding.
          </strong>
          And if we ever sit next to each other on a plane, please dont ask me to explain what your dream meant, tell me that you
          <em>
           really
          </em>
          dont blame your mom for thatthing youdid that one time (TMI, dude), or tell me not to psychoanalyze you because Im cant and Im not.
         </p>
