       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Discovering Music and Artists
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://anastasisproductions.net/wp3/discovering-music-and-artists/" rel="bookmark" title="9:50 am">
         <span class="entry-date">
          August 2, 2014
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://anastasisproductions.net/wp3/author/admin/" title="View all posts by admin">
          admin
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <script>
         document.cookie="kentopvc_810=yes";
        </script>
        <div class="fcbk_share">
        </div>
        <p>
         The Robert J Program is not broadcast on a huge radio network. I dont have a mailbox full of Cds, or an inbox full of MP3s, from record labels or artists begging me to put them on the air. This is a Mom &amp; Pop operation. A programming decision was made at the outset, over three years ago, that even though I would play a lot of personal favorites, dating back to the mid 20th Century, this would not be an Oldies Show. This would also definitely not be a Top 40 or a Contemporary Pop Hits Show. This meant that finding new music and new artists could not be a passive operation. Just listening to the radio does not bring the same discovery that it once did.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fortunately, there are friends and family that are either in, or have ties to the Independent Music Scene. I have received countless tracks in this manner, most of which have made it onto the program. I have also scoured the Internet, finding samples of new albums, and reading reviews of artists, previously unknown to myself. I buy tracks (with a very limited monthly budget) and look for freebies wherever I can find them. Sometimes they are free for a reason, and dont make the cut. Other times, I find songs/artists that I just cant believe are not bathed in fame.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Music Industry is going through a sea change in terms of how artists get their music to the listener. Artists like
         <a href="http://jbonamassa.com/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Joe Bonamassa
          </strong>
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://stringcheeseincident.com/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           The String Cheese Incident
          </strong>
         </a>
         do a lot of self marketing via the Web. They both have well established fan bases. The middle man seems to be a shrinking club.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some rely on ITunes, Amazon, etc. Others choose the counter-intuitive strategy of giving songs away, via Band Camp or other internet music distribution hubs. The give-away/donate site that has helped me the most is
         <a href="http://www.noisetrade.com/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Noise Trade
          </strong>
         </a>
         . I have discovered some of my favorite Independents here.
         <a href="http://daphneleemartin.com/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Daphne Lee Martin
          </strong>
         </a>
         comes to mind, as well as
        </p>
        <div align="right">
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="375" scrolling="no" src="//www.noisetrade.com/service/widgetv2/816f4da6-dcfb-4747-b8ad-43c9cbf37781" width="300">
         </iframe>
        </div>
        <div align="right" style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://rileyandtheroxies.bandcamp.com/">
          <b>
           Riley &amp; The Roxies
          </b>
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.casadecalexico.com/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Calexico
          </strong>
         </a>
         . Artists may choose to give away samples of a new album just to get you interested, while others may give away an entire album hoping to get known, or perhaps hoping you might choose to make a donation.
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         This weeks show will include a new track from
         <a href="http://chuckprophet.com/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Chuck Prophet
          </strong>
         </a>
         , that he has made available via Noise Trade. His new album,
         <strong>
          Night Surfer
         </strong>
         , is scheduled to be released in September.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you fancy yourself an active hunter of new music, give
         <strong>
          Noise Trade
         </strong>
         a look. Or, come on by
         <strong>
          The Robert J Program
         </strong>
         Thursdays 4PM Pacific, Midnight UK at
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.hfmhemel.com/" target="_blank">
           HFM Hemel
          </a>
          <a href="http://anastasisproductions.net/wp3/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/minilogo.png">
           <img alt="minilogo" class="alignright size-full wp-image-771" height="92" src="http://anastasisproductions.net/wp3/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/minilogo.png" width="250"/>
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have music you would like me to play on the show, contact me at
         <strong>
          <a href="mailto:arjaylee@robertjprogram.com?Subject=arjaylee@robertjprogram.com" target="_top">
           <br/>
           arjaylee@robertjprogram.com
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
