           <h3 class="entry-header">
            Family Movie Matinee
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              Here in Teton Valley, it's an hour drive to a real indoor movie theatre and with a 7 month old that's not somewhere I want to be anyway. We love going to the
              <a href="http://thetobyshow.typepad.com/the_toby_show/2008/07/the-spud-drive-in.html">
               Spud Drive-In
              </a>
              , but the movie starts at dark-thirty and that's not until 9:30ish these days, so that means keeping Huck up over 2 hours past his usual bedtime and THEN starting a movie. It will be a huge thrill when he's a little older but no thanks on that right now. So I thought it would be fun to have a little Matinee Drive-In movie at home.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              I had seen this amazing
              <a href="http://katherinemariephotography.com/blog/archives/2509">
               drive-in idea
              </a>
              on Katharine Marie Photography and knew I'd be stoked to even achieve a small fraction the cuteness of her set up.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/17796366@N06/3747551154/" title="Matinee 3 by jonahlisa, on Flickr">
               <img alt="Matinee 3" height="333" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2671/3747551154_7d0608268d.jpg" width="500"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              We didn't do any of the adorable games or activities, just an Saturday afternoon movie (
              <em>
               Bolt
              </em>
              , which was a little old for him but he still enjoyed), a cardboard box "car", and popcorn in homemade brown paper popcorn bags. Huck thought it was really fun. He enjoyed using real money to pay Pooh Bear, who ran the concession stand. I also made simple tickets out of card stock, which he excitedly carried around for an hour before the show started. Jemima did not watch the movie, we played and nursed instead.
             </p>
             <p>
              I just have to share that while making the popcorn bags and trying to talk to Huck at the same time I had a bit of a slip of the pen and came very close serving my family Porn Corn. Oops. Sometimes multi-taking ain't all it's cracked up to be.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/17796366@N06/3746631569/" title="Popcorn Bags by jonahlisa, on Flickr">
               <img alt="Popcorn Bags" height="333" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2656/3746631569_7ab2b57421.jpg" width="500"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Never made Popcorn from scratch without the microwave or a special gizmo? Here's my simple recipe for perfect popcorn with no burning and very few, if any unpopped kernels..
             </p>
             <p style="font-size: 16px; color: #ff0000; text-align: center; font-family: Arial;">
              <strong>
               Perfect Popcorn
              </strong>
             </p>
             <div style="text-align: center;">
              (though it will now be referred to as Porn Corn at our house)
             </div>
             <p class="blockquote" style="margin-left: 40px;">
              2/3 cup of popcorn kernels
              <br/>
              6 Tbsp of Canola Oil
              <br/>
              Salt
              <br/>
              4 Tbsp Butter (1/2 a stick)
             </p>
             <p class="blockquote" style="margin-left: 40px;">
             </p>
             <p class="blockquote" style="margin-left: 40px;">
             </p>
             <p class="blockquote" style="margin-left: 40px;">
             </p>
             <p class="blockquote" style="margin-left: 40px;">
              This amount almost perfectly fills a large 6 qt dutch oven. We ate it all but for what it's worth my parents say we're "big eaters."
             </p>
             <p class="blockquote" style="margin-left: 40px;">
              Put oil in lidded pot and drop in 2 or 3 kernels. Turn on medium-high heat and wait for the test kernels to pop.
             </p>
             <p class="blockquote" style="margin-left: 40px;">
              Here's the big secret to popping ALL the kernels... Once the test kernels pop,
              <strong>
               remove the pot from heat and dump in all kernels. Give it stir. Wait 30 seconds with it off the heat and then return the pot to the heat.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p class="blockquote" style="margin-left: 40px;">
              No need to shake until they start popping in earnest, then keep the pot moving until the popping slows to 2-3 seconds between pop. Remove from heat. Let cool.
             </p>
             <p class="blockquote" style="margin-left: 40px;">
              In the meantime, melt butter. Pour over popcorn, salt liberally, toss. Eat it up.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Even though I caught my little Porn Corn slip, we still did the thing with our own personal flare.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/17796366@N06/3746634029/" title="Matinee 2 by jonahlisa, on Flickr">
               <img alt="Matinee 2" height="500" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2599/3746634029_ab7c2c3896.jpg" width="333"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
