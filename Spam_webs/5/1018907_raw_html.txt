         <p>
          From Skittles to puppies to Katy Perry: Here's your ultimate Super Bowl preview.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 650px">
          <img alt="Chips sold for the Super Bowl XLIX." class="" height="478" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8634/16177983476_f198eccf43_z_d.jpg" width="640"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           What game? I'm here for the chips. (Mike Mozart / Flickr)
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          This Sunday, 6:30 ET, get ready for Super Bowl XLIX. Whether you're a football fan or are just hanging around the for the chips and dip, here's what you can expect to go down.
         </p>
         <h2>
          The New England Patriots will play the Seattle Seahawks in the 49th Super Bowl.
         </h2>
         <p>
          The game is being played in Glendale, AZ. You can catch the game on NBC or NBC's
          <a href="http://stream.nbcsports.com/liveextra/" target="_blank">
           live stream
          </a>
          . Either way, you'll be joining more than
          <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/cmo/2015/01/12/super-bowl-ad-prices-have-gone-up-75-over-a-decade/" target="_blank">
           100 million people
          </a>
          that are expected to watch. That's almost a third of the entire U.S. population.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 650px">
          <img alt="Playing to win the Super Bowl XLIX title: New England Patriots vs. Seattle Seahawks." class="" height="400" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7394/16165977478_fa0a3fc559_z_d.jpg" width="640"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Playing to win Super Bowl XLIX: New England Patriots vs. Seattle Seahawks. (develop it / Flickr)
          </p>
         </div>
         <h2>
          The pressure's on.
         </h2>
         <p>
          The Seattle Seahawks are defending their Super Bowl title (they beat the Denver Broncos last year), and the New England Patriots are trying to win their fourth Super Bowl. Both teams are pretty good  in the regular season, the Patriots ranked 11th in yards per game with 1,073, while the Seahawks were #1 in the NFL for their defense, allowing only 267.1 yards per game.
         </p>
         <h2>
          If the Seattle Seahawks win ...
         </h2>
         <p>
          Seahawks quarterback Russell Wilson will become the youngest QB in history to win two Super Bowls in a row. Patriots QB Tom Brady currently holds the record.
         </p>
         <p>
          Also, if Seahawks Coach Pete Carroll leads his team to consecutive Super Bowl titles, he would be the
          <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/early-lead/wp/2015/01/29/super-bowl-xlix-preview-and-tv-schedule/" target="_blank">
           seventh to do so
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h2>
          If the New England Patriots win ...
         </h2>
         <p>
          Brady would tie Hall of Famers Joe Montana and Terry Bradshaw for the most Super Bowl rings (they both have four).
         </p>
         <h2>
          Who's expected to win?
         </h2>
         <p>
          Predictions on who will win the game
          <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/playoffs/2014/story/_/id/12244977/super-bowl-xlix-predictions-espn-staff-picks-big-game" target="_blank">
           go both ways
          </a>
          , so expect a close game. (Even Clam the psychic clam
          <a href="http://gawker.com/psychic-clam-predicts-super-bowl-xlix-winner-seahawks-1682659082/+marchman" target="_blank">
           decided not to bet
          </a>
          on this one).
         </p>
         <h2>
          What about deflategate?
         </h2>
         <p>
          The balls used in Sunday's game
          <a href="http://www.bbc.com/sport/0/american-football/31053403" target="_blank">
           will be given extra security
          </a>
          to avoid another instance of deflategate, where
          <a href="http://gokicker.com/2015/01/19/new-england-patriots-accused-of-cheating-by-deflating-balls-deflategate/" target="_blank" title="New England Patriots accused of cheating by deflating balls #DeflateGate">
           the Pats were accused of using under-inflated balls
          </a>
          .
         </p>
