             Bio:
            </div>
            <div class="field-items">
             <div class="field-item odd">
              <p>
               Honored as one of the "
               <strong>
                Ten Standout Stand-Ups Worth Watching
               </strong>
               " by Back Stage magazine, Ray Ellin is comedy's renaissance man: comic, television host, actor, writer and producer/director.
              </p>
              <p>
               Ray began his career as a stand-up comedian at the age of 15. Today, he is a featured performer in NYC's hottest comedy clubs and a regular weekend emcee at the world famous
               <strong>
                Comic Strip Live
               </strong>
               . Known for his ability to ad-lib and improvise with any crowd, Ray has become New York's most sought-after host.
              </p>
              <p>
               Ray is currently directing the feature film "
               <strong>
                The Bourbon Brothers
               </strong>
               " starring Academy-Award winner Mercedes Reuhlhe was also the executive produced and directed the movie "
               <strong>
                The Latin Legends Of Comedy
               </strong>
               ," released by
               <strong>
                20th Century Fox
               </strong>
               . He has racked up an impressive list of television credits including appearances on
               <strong>
                HBO
               </strong>
               's "
               <strong>
                Chris Rock Show
               </strong>
               ,"
               <strong>
                FOX
               </strong>
               's "
               <strong>
                Fox X-Press
               </strong>
               ,"
               <strong>
                ABC
               </strong>
               's "
               <strong>
                Primetime Live
               </strong>
               ,"
               <strong>
                NBC
               </strong>
               's "
               <strong>
                Friday Night
               </strong>
               " and "
               <strong>
                Today Show
               </strong>
               " and England's "
               <strong>
                Mancs vs. Yanks
               </strong>
               ."
              </p>
