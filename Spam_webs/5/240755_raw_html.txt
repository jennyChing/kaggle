       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Peter Pan Collar Shirt Refashion
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2013-09-18T06:00:25+00:00">
         September 18, 2013
        </span>
        By
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://greylustergirl.com/author/greylustergirl/" rel="author">
           greylustergirl
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://greylustergirl.com/peter-pan-collar-shirt-refashion/#comments">
          7 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/diy-peter-pan-collar.jpg">
          <img alt="diy peter pan collar" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1406" height="438" src="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/600x438xdiy-peter-pan-collar-600x438.jpg.pagespeed.ic.hDfO3HIEf7.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         I am a big fan of the peter pan collar shirts these days. I like the vintage nod while at the same time adding a little something to an otherwise plain shirt. The thing that drew me to this next refashion was the bright red peter pan collar. It was definitely an attention grabber. I wanted to refashion a shirt with a peter pan collar that also added a pop of color and stood out.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Peter-Pan-Collar-Shirt-Refashion.jpg">
          <img alt="Peter Pan Collar Shirt Refashion" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1408" height="357" src="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/600x357xPeter-Pan-Collar-Shirt-Refashion-600x357.jpg.pagespeed.ic.utHEG5e2oU.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.sheinside.com/Navy-Beige-Stripe-Peter-Pan-Collar-Short-Sleeve-Zip-Back-Blouse-p-93096-cat-1733.html?src=ls&amp;siteID=QFGLnEolOWg-5lk8uVVgqbsfTotZC1kHHA?src=ls&amp;siteID=QFGLnEolOWg-nd1Rvh3qpjDm5gxP7PdGvg">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I looked through my closet to see what I could find similar to my inspiration picture. No luck. Then I got the idea of maybe flipping it. Instead of doing a patterned shirt with a red peter pan collar, I would do a red shirt with a patterned peter pan collar. I started by making my pattern for the peter pan collar.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Peter-Pan-Collar-Pattern-DIY.jpg">
          <img alt="Peter Pan Collar Pattern DIY" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1409" height="600" src="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/600x600xPeter-Pan-Collar-Pattern-DIY-600x600.jpg.pagespeed.ic.R7xleZ6Y_T.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         I made it in parts and then combined the parts. I traced the front and back neckline of my red shirt so that I could make my collar. I used a jar to round the corners for the front of the collar. I used two pencils rubberbanded together to trace the collar so that I could allow for the seams.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/how-to-make-peter-pan-collar.jpg">
          <img alt="how to make peter pan collar" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1410" height="354" src="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/how-to-make-peter-pan-collar-600x354.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         I had another shirt that had a cute pattern but that was about the only thing it had going for it. I cut out 2 collars using my pattern and added interfacing the the wrong side of one of them. With right sides together, I sewed the outside of the collar and left the inside unsewn. Before you turn it right side out, notch the edges so that it curves well.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/DIY-peter-pan-collar-shirt-redo.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY peter pan collar shirt redo" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1411" height="600" src="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/DIY-peter-pan-collar-shirt-redo-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         Now time to attach the collar. Mark the middle of the front neckline. Turn the shirt inside out and pin the right side of the collar to the wrong side of the shirt. Sew the collar on. Turn the shirt right side out and flip the collar out. Top stitch the collar down.  Quite the quick shirt refashion!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/DIY-Peter-Pan-Shirt-Refashion_before_after.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Peter Pan Shirt Refashion_before_after" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1407" height="372" src="http://greylustergirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/DIY-Peter-Pan-Shirt-Refashion_before_after-600x372.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         From plain red shirt to a fun patterned peter pan collar. If a patterned collar is not your cup of tea, you could check out my
         <a href="http://greylustergirl.com/diy-plain-white-tee-shirt-refashion-lace-front-with-a-peter-pan-collar/">
          Lace Peter Pan shirt refashion
         </a>
         which has a classic black peter pan collar paired with a lace shirt.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Looking for more shirt refashions? Check out my

         that I am

         doing!
