       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Learning To Accept My Muffin Top {In Opposite World}
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-04-21T21:07:01+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          April 21, 2013
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://womenlivingwell.org/author/wome6733/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Courtney
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://womenlivingwell.org/2013/04/learning-to-accept-my-muffin-top-in-opposite-world/#comments">
           100 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <img alt="WLW in Opposite World" height="461" src="http://womenlivingwell.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/WLW-in-Opposite-World-1024x767.jpg" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I hate my muffin top. It makes certain dresses and shirts fit ALL wrong. So I resort toruffled shirts,A-line dresses and anything that hides it.I wish I had a flat stomach a flat stomach is only some self-discipline, exercise and a bit of lipo suction away, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         ButIm learning
        </p>
        <p>
         Im learning that I dont have the discipline it takes to make thismuffin top go away any time soonmaybethat will be next yearsNew Years Resolution{again *sigh*}
        </p>
        <p>
         And maybeyou dont have a muffin top but you carry extra weight somewhere elsemaybein your thighs, arms,chin, or bottom.  And maybe, like me when you look in the mirror, you judge yourself for the food you ate yesterday cause
         <em>
          Opposite World
         </em>
         tells you you really should work on that trouble spot.
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, recently I was comtemplating my muffin top deep thoughts right? lol! I was thinking  why in the world is that still there? Im on the eliptical 5 nights a week and I dont drink milkshakes or eat fries! And then it hit me because God has given me my daily bread. Actually I have a fridge full of food hes given me my weekly bread!
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           My muffin top is evidence of Gods goodness in my life!
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         You see, when I make a meatloaf forour family of four after we each have an appropriate portion for our body size  theres a few slices left. And guess what happens I eata little extra. And after my son finishesa gameof basketball at the gym, we take the kids through the Dairy Queen drive through to cool off (I always get a kids sized cone). And in the fall theres candy corn,at Christmas therescookies, Valentines Day thereschocolate and the list goes on!
         <a href="http://womenlivingwell.org/2009/07/tasty-tuesday-quick-and-easy-pizza/">
          Pizza Bread
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://womenlivingwell.org/2009/05/tasty-tuesday-easy-taco-salad/">
          Crock Pot Macn Cheese
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://womenlivingwell.org/2009/05/tasty-tuesday-easy-taco-salad/">
          Taco Salad
         </a>
         , Burgers and
         <a href="http://womenlivingwell.org/2010/07/tasty-tuesday-hawaiian-meatloaf/">
          Hawaain Meatloaf
         </a>
         grace my dinner table every month.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now all the dieting books say the above eats are the reason I have a muffin top right? You may bethinking, 
         <em>
          goodness  dont make meatloaf andmac n cheese. Those are not on the healthy foodcharts
         </em>
         ! Well, I do cook chicken and fish and lots of veggies too
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://womenlivingwell.org/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         And I get it  I do. I get that I am making choices that keep me from getting rid of my muffin top. So I need to stop whining about it.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Those overflowing plates of food those are evidence of Gods abundant blessings in my life.
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Now you may wonder if I feel this way about others muffin tops. Nope  yours doesnt bother me at all! I have a double standard. Yours looks great andif you complained about it Id tell you, youare beautiful. Id probably point to your children and tell you, they are SO worth the muffin top!!!
        </p>
        <p>
         But
         <em>
          Opposite World
         </em>
         whispers in my ear every time I look in the mirror,
         <em>
          you should not have eaten that yesterday.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          First world problems right? They dont have to set up treadmills in third world countries where women are working themselves to the bone tosurvive or worse starving. Ive read of women in Haiti whose
          <a href="http://news.nationalgeographic.com/news/2008/01/080130-AP-haiti-eatin.html">
           children are so hungry they feed them dirt cakes
          </a>
          just to stop their hunger pains.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <em>
            My muffin top is evidence of Gods abundant blessings.
           </em>
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         And so we bow our heads and give thanks for our daily bread. And we eat not as gluttons, but we never miss a meal and we get to eat an abundance of fruit, vegetables, meat, and bread.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Thank you God for these blessings!
           </em>
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thegospelcoalition.org/blogs/tgc/2013/01/01/new-year-new-self-control/">
          Jen Wilken writes
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          The expectation of physical perfection hits modern females early and often.
          <strong>
           <em>
            In middle school, girls cut themselves
           </em>
          </strong>
          to deal with the pressures of conforming to the ideal.
          <strong>
           <em>
            In middle age, women do, toobut allow the surgeon to hold the knife.
           </em>
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Oh that is so profound  is it not?
        </p>
        <p>
         Jen goes on
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          We carve the record of our self-loathing into the very flesh of our bodiesa self-marring, a literal carving of an idol.
          <em>
           <strong>
            Increasingly, physical perfection is the legacy of womanhood in our culture, handed down with meticulous care from mother to daughter
           </strong>
          </em>
          , with more faithful instruction in word and deed than we can trouble to devote to cultivating kindness, peacemaking, and acceptance that characterize unfading, inner beauty.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         What are wepassing on to our daughters? This weekend I attended the soberingfuneral of mybeautiful Aunt Lori.She died at the age of 54, after a brave5 year battle with cancer. And you know whatno one mentioned her waist size at the funeral.
        </p>
        <p>
         Do we get that no one cares! And certainly God does not care! He is not going to greet me at the pearly gates and say 
         <em>
          you should have never eaten those Christmas cookies!!!
         </em>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           I dont want my kids to look at my picture after my death and say 
           <em>
            our mother was so beautiful
           </em>
           , I want them to say  M
           <em>
            y mother oozed with the love of Jesus. She loved God, loved Daddy, loved us and loved people
           </em>
           ! Thats what I want my legacy to be!
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Christian women should be the most joyous and cheerful women in the neighborhood. We have hope, security and a Heavenly Father who loves us unconditionally! Lets not torture ourselves with the
         <em>
          Opposite Worlds
         </em>
         liesgrumpy because were famished from dieting. I have learned that I need to eat and have energy to serve God heartily.
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh friends and foodies and marathon mamas  I am NOT saying we should not strive to be healthier or eat healthier. I love the way you take care of the temple of the Lord. I love reading your blogs and learning off of you all. I admire your diligence and discipline and lets be honest I dream of having your waist size! This is not a post dissing healthy living.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is for all the women who feel like they are 
         <em>
          less than
         </em>
         because
         <em>
          Opposite World
         </em>
         says strive for your dream weight that isjust.out.of.reachAnd maybe
        </p>
        <p>
         Maybe Im trying to learn toNOT hold that double standard
        </p>
        <p>
         Maybejust maybe -Im trying to tell myself
        </p>
        <p>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Im
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         okay
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         
         <em>
          Charm is deceptive, and beauty is fleeting; but
          <strong>
           a woman who fears the LORD is to be praised
          </strong>
          .
         </em>
         ~ Proverbs 31:30
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Thank you Lord for my abundant blessings that my muffin top represents. Help me to stop fearing food and Opposite Worldand to fear YOU only.
           </em>
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Walk with the King,
         <br/>
         <a href="http://womenlivingwell.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/siggy-1.gif">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-920" height="49" src="http://womenlivingwell.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/siggy-1.gif" title="siggy-1" width="115"/>
