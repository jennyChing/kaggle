        <div class="recipe-info">

        </div>
        Classic risotto Milanese gets its deep yellow color by adding saffron. Isn't that just absolutely amazing? Ready in about 25 minutes.
        <br/>
        <h3>
         Ingredients
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          300 gram risotto rice
         </li>
         <li>
          1 onion
         </li>
         <li>
          1 dl dry white wine
         </li>
         <li>
          6 dl chicken stock
         </li>
         <li>
          1 teaspoon saffron threads
         </li>
         <li>
          olive oil
         </li>
         <li>
          50 gram butter
         </li>
         <li>
          25 gram Parmesan cheese + extra for garnish
         </li>
         <li>
          salt to taste
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1187" style="width: 1034px">
         <a href="http://ohmydish.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Risotto-Milanese-ingredients.jpg">
          <img alt="Risotto Milanese ingredients" class="size-full wp-image-1187" height="768" src="http://ohmydish.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Risotto-Milanese-ingredients.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Risotto Milanese ingredients
         </p>
        </div>
        <h3>
         Kitchen equipment needed
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Cutting board &amp; chefs knife
         </li>
         <li>
          Skillet
         </li>
         <li>
          Spatula
         </li>
         <li>
          Grater
         </li>
         <li>
          Small saucepan
         </li>
         <li>
          Large spoon
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         Preparation for the risotto Milanese  5 minutes
        </h3>
        <p>
         Add the saffron to the chicken stock and let it simmer on low heat for a few minutes. This way the stock will get infused by the saffron and it will get its color, the stock will look orange. Finely dice the onion and grate the parmesan cheese.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1292" style="width: 810px">
         <a href="http://ohmydish.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Risotto-Milanese-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Risotto Milanese" class="size-full wp-image-1292" height="1200" src="http://ohmydish.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Risotto-Milanese-2.jpg" width="800"/>

        <h3>
         Cooking the risotto Milanese  20 minutes
        </h3>
        <p>
         Saut the onion in some olive oil until the onion looks glossy, keep on stirring because you dont want to brown the onion. Add the risotto rice and stir a few times, after a few minutes the risotto rice will look glossy as well. At that point add the white wine, all at ones. Turn the heat down to medium low. When the wine is fully absorbed, start adding the orange chicken stock, a little bit at a time. Do not stir it too much. Its important to keep on tasting before you add more stock, you want to do that to check how far along the risotto rice is already cooked. The rice should be a little bit
         <em>
          al dente
         </em>
         . You dont want to add too much stock, the rice will be overcooked and will get mushy.
        </p>
        <p>
         Stir in the butter and grated Parmesan cheese, this way the risotto will be deliciously creamy. Bring to taste using salt if necessary and top it off with some extra cheese. Thats all there is to it for this classic risotto Milanese.
        </p>
