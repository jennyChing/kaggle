    <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-leftRailSpanner" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn" itemprop="name" style="font-size: 27px;">
      Artichoke Baked Egg
     </h1>
     <img height="526" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Artichoke-Baked-Egg-Main-790x526.jpg" width="790"/>
     <div class="yui3-g">
      <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-well">
       <div class="grub-byline" style="margin-top: 1em;">
        Recipe by
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/113274240511284571445" rel="author">
         Jerry James Stone
        </a>
         January 22, 2013
       </div>
       <div class="grub-mainPostContent" itemprop="recipeInstructions" not-style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 23px;">
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/artichoke-baked-egg-main/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/artichoke-baked-egg-main" rel="attachment wp-att-1253">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Baked eggs are amazing.
         <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/avocado-fried-egg/" title="Avocado Fried Egg">
          Eggs baked in side other foods
         </a>
         are even better! This artichoke baked egg recipe is an easy way to gourmet up your next brunch or breakfast.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="338" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/QgAdx7KeOUo" width="600">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <div class="yui3-g" id="grub-recipeSummary">
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-left">
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul id="grub-ingredients">
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            3 Artichokes
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            6
            <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/ingredient/egg/">
             Eggs
            </a>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup Panko Bread Crumbs
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup parmesan, more for topping
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Lemons
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            6 sprigs of thyme
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            6 Tablespoons Olive Oil
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            Sea salt
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            Cracked pepper
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-right">
          <h2>
           <a class="grub-button" href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/printable/" target="_blank">
            <span class="grub-printIcon">
            </span>
            Printable View
           </a>
          </h2>
          <h2>
           Yield:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6 servings
           </span>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT1H25M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          <h2>
           Total Time
           <div style="float: right;">
            1 hour, 25 minutes
           </div>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT15M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Prep time
           <div style="float: right">
            15 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <meta content="PT1H10M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Cook time
           <div style="float: right">
            1 hour, 10 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Main Ingredients:
           </h2>
           Artichoke,
           <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/ingredient/egg/">
            Egg
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Recipe Type:
           </h2>
           Main Course
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Diet:
           </h2>
           Vegetarian
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         1. Choose 3 artichokes that are about 4 inches wide. This way they will tightly fit into your 9 baking dish. Trim the end of the choke and peel the stem, then half it length-wise.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="737" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/artichoke/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/artichoke" rel="attachment wp-att-1254">
          <img alt="Artichoke" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1254" height="442" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Artichoke-600x442.jpg" title="Artichoke" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="716" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/sliced-artichoke/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/sliced-artichoke" rel="attachment wp-att-1262">
          <img alt="Sliced Artichoke" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1262" height="429" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Sliced-Artichoke-600x429.jpg" title="Sliced Artichoke" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Half one of the 2 lemons and juice it into a large mixing bowl. Then fill that bowl with ice water.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Remove the hairy choke. You cannot eat this, it is really gross. Artichokes are almost color coded so you know which part to remove. I use a pearing knife to cut the choke out and then remove any excess with a small spoon. As a side note, when artichokes bloom, the choke turns this gorgeous indigo color and is simply lovely. But here it is evil and must be destroyed! Place each cleaned artichoke into the lemon water.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/soaking-artichokes-2/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/soaking-artichokes-2" rel="attachment wp-att-1261">
          <img alt="Soaking Artichokes" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1261" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Soaking-Artichokes1-600x399.jpg" title="Soaking Artichokes" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Add 1/2 cup of Panko bread crumbs, 1/4 cup of Parmesan cheese and juice from the other lemon half to a small mixing bowl and mix well.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="733" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/bread-cheese-and-lemon-mix/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/bread-cheese-and-lemon-mix" rel="attachment wp-att-1260">
          <img alt="Bread Cheese and Lemon Mix" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1260" height="439" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Bread-Cheese-and-Lemon-Mix-600x439.jpg" title="Bread Cheese and Lemon Mix" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Slice the remaining lemon.
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Place the artichoke halves sliced-side up in a 9-by-13-inch baking dish, and then fill the cups with some of the bread crumb mix. Add 6 tablespoons of olive oil to the baking dish. Place the lemon slices and 6 sprigs of fresh thyme on top and cover with foil.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="714" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/stuffed-artichokes/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/stuffed-artichokes" rel="attachment wp-att-1258">
          <img alt="Stuffed Artichokes" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1258" height="428" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Stuffed-Artichokes-600x428.jpg" title="Stuffed Artichokes" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="682" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/lemon-stuffed-artichokes/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/lemon-stuffed-artichokes" rel="attachment wp-att-1257">
          <img alt="Lemon Stuffed Artichokes" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1257" height="409" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lemon-Stuffed-Artichokes-600x409.jpg" title="Lemon Stuffed Artichokes" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Bake at 375 degrees F for 50 minutes or until the choke is tender, so you can easily pierce a fork into the thickest part of the artichoke heart. But be sure to not over cook the chokes as we still need to cook them with the eggs.
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Remove from the oven, removing the foil. Crack an egg into each cup, making sure the artichokes halves are level first. If you dont do this, your egg will just pour into the baking dish.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/roasted-artichokes-with-eggs/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/roasted-artichokes-with-eggs" rel="attachment wp-att-1256">
          <img alt="Roasted Artichokes with Eggs" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1256" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Roasted-Artichokes-with-Eggs-600x399.jpg" title="Roasted Artichokes with Eggs" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         9. Bake uncovered for about 15 minutes, or until the eggs have set. Ideally the whites should be fully cooked but the yolks still runny.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/artichoke-baked-eggs-1/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/artichoke-baked-eggs-1" rel="attachment wp-att-1255">
          <img alt="Artichoke Baked Eggs 1" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1255" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Artichoke-Baked-Eggs-1-600x399.jpg" title="Artichoke Baked Eggs 1" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Finish with more cheese, salt and fresh cracked pepper. Enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="1408" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/artichoke-baked-egg-full/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/artichoke-baked-egg-full" rel="attachment wp-att-1259">
          <img alt="Artichoke Baked Egg Full" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1259" height="845" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Artichoke-Baked-Egg-Full-600x845.jpg" title="Artichoke Baked Egg Full" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
