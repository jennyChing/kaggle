        How to Harness the Power of Authentic Worship
       </a>
      </h2>

      <div class="featured_text_nopad">
       <p>
        This article was written by our Guest Blogger Edmond Sanganyado
       </p>
       <p>
        In Acts, we witness miraculous happenings as believers harnessed the power of authentic worship. This leaves a question, how do you harness the power of authentic worship?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Thank you for the salary
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        The economy was in shambles and every Chipo and Tendai on the street corners was a billionaire. At least, I had realized my dream, I wanted to be a millionaire before I turned 30. Unfortunately, I could only afford a loaf of bread with one hundred million dollars.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_604" style="width: 281px">
        <a href="http://www.tehillahworship.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Edmond.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="wp-image-604" height="361" src="http://www.tehillahworship.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Edmond-768x1024.jpg" width="271"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Edmond Sanganyado &amp; His Family
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        You do not have to say, thank you.
       </p>
       <p>
        A month after writing my final exam at college, I got a job as a graduate research assistant. I love research, I love chemistry and I loved my college. Above all, I was earning US $50 per month. Converting that to the local currency made me a quadrillionaire at only 23.
       </p>
       <p>
        Through my life experiences, losing my parents at an early age, going for two weeks with no food and getting kicked out from school for not paying tuition every term, taught me to be thankful of little things. When my boss handed me my paycheck, I always expressed my gratitude. I later learned this made him uneasy.
       </p>
       <p>
        Edmond, you wake up each morning and come here to do your job. You deserve this pay, you earned it. I wish it was more, my boss said handing me the envelope with 500 Rands.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         A Call for Authentic Worship
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        In my relationship to God, I have found everything God does is worth of praise. Everything. Before I received the life of Christ, I was blind, spiritually blind. I could not see the glorious things God was doing in my life and around me. Instead, I was busy blaming Him for every struggle that came my way. The new life opened my eyes and now I could see the praiseworthy work of God (2 Corinthians 4:4).
       </p>
       <p>
        In college, I learned about Erwin Schrdingers thought experiment, if a cat enters a room with poison then the cat is both dead and alive. Yes, God opened my eyes, but that did not mean everyday l saw His works. But does that mean God does stopped His work, or I stopped looking at Him? When praises dry up, it often mean I have removed my eyes from the throne of grace (Hebrews 12:2, Isaiah 26:3).
       </p>
       <p>
        Over the years, through biblical naivety, I was embroiled in spiritual quid pro quo. I ignorantly embraced the teaching worship was the currency we used to pay for Gods blessings. Around 2008, my worship reached hyperinflationary levels. Prolonged fasts, constant all-night prayers and extended hours of worship only secured a trickle of blessings. Something was definitely wrong.
       </p>
       <p>
        A few weeks ago, I attended a church service where one of the musicians was from Zimbabwe. After singing their last song, as people sat and the worship team left the stage, he called them back. This was a bit awkward and unusual. Something was up.
       </p>
       <p>
        We are all used to worshipping God when we need something. We call into God because our child is sick. How easy it is to give God honor when we are looking for a job. We have reduced worship to a con. We want to bait God in to blessing us. But today, let us worship God for who He is. He is good, He is love, He is faithful and He is our God.
       </p>
       <p>
        Although my boss did not think it was fine thanking him for my stipend, I had to since it was a favor. Each day God grants us immeasurable favor, and we respond through authentic worship. Authentic worship is not payback, but an honest reaction to Gods grace. We love Him because He first loved us. But that is not all.
       </p>
       <p>
        When I received the new life, Christ opened my eyes so that I can see His glory. Authentic worship is not limited to a reaction to what God did, but also encompasses who God is. Like the 24 elders, beholding the preeminence of Christ provokes unadulterated worship. Because of who is, not only what He had dinner, Christ deserves all the honor and praise.
       </p>
       <p>
        For some time, I would wake up a trillionaire and the next morning 12 zeros will be gone. It became normal, but one morning it all changed. I lost all my trillions, the Zimbabwean dollar stopped being legal tender. If we reduce worship to spiritual tender, what will happen when we stand before the king of kings at the end of the days? Will you have enough currency to pay for a glorified body? We can harness the power of authentic worship by seeing what the Lord did and knowing who He is.
       </p>

         Grace Musing
        </a>
       </p>
