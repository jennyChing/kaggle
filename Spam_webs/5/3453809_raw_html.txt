       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Puppy Academy
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Recherche Goldens is well-known for its successful 12-week training program called Puppy Academy. Jamel and Charity Hamka, the owners of Recherche Goldens, work with the puppies from the comfort their own home, earning their love, trust, and respect early on. Thanks to the Hamkas experience, consistency, and attention to detail, puppies achieve amazing results by the time they graduate. Most Puppy Academy graduates are better trained at 12 weeks of age than many dogs are at 12
         <i>
          months
         </i>
         ! Keep reading for more information.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="RechercheGoldens" class="aligncenter wp-image-8" height="138" src="wp-content/uploads/recherchegoldens-org/sites/173/Logo1.jpg" width="651"/>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         Week 5-6
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The puppy will begin the crate-training process. By this age, each puppy has already been weaned from its mother which is the turning point from mother provided nutrition to owner provided nutrition. The puppy will now be eating puppy food only. Puppies will only be separated at night and confined to a small crate for sleeping purposes. This teaches the puppy to keep from urinating for an extended period of time and they will associate the process with the crate. The puppy will also learn to sleep at night within the crate. Puppies will be allowed to urinate during the night; however, the increments of time between breaks will increase daily. This will strengthen the puppys bladder and bowel, which is crucial to successful potty training.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         Week 7-8
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-style: normal;">
          <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-style: normal;">
           <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-style: normal;">
            <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-style: normal;">
             At 8 weeks, most puppies will be able to hold their urine for 6-8 hours at night. The puppies develop trust in the trainer for allowing them to potty at appropriate times. Puppies are free to walk around the home, with the trainer nearby watching intently for signs of urination. Once the pup indicates the need to potty, they will immediately betaken outside and rewarded/praised for holding it. Potty training willcontinue in earnest until the pup is taken home at 12 weeks of age.
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         At 7-8 weeks basic obedience training with clickers will help continue training at home and with this the puppy will be able to begin basic commands. Leash training will commence around this time and will take time to develop. This should be introduced with basic commands, such as Sit, Down/Lay, Stay, and Come. By putting all of the basic commands together as well as leash training will help the puppy learn the owners expectations.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Week 9-10
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         At this time it is important to extend the basic obedience commands (Sit, Down/Lay, Stay, and Come) to help hone in the puppys skills.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Each puppy will begin stair training. Many Goldens have a fear of stairs, but if introduced slowly and appropriately at a young age, they will have no problem running up and down almost any type of stairs.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Puppies will need to be car trained and taking the puppy along on field trips which vary in length will help the puppy become accustomed to time in the car as well as meeting new people. This is also a good time to continue practicing leash training.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Proper grooming will take place during this time and will be valuable to accustom the puppy to certain things like bathing, getting blow dried, having nails clipped, experiencing teeth brushing and ear cleaning.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Puppy Academy furthermore teaches puppies how to appropriately interact with people, especially in a home environment. Biting of the hands, feet, or clothing is not allowed. Chewing of the furniture, digging on wood floors, or getting into the trash is forbidden as well. It is important to be consistent with how you respond to the Goldens behavior.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         Week 11-12
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         For countless hours over a 12-week period, a Puppy Academy puppy has been potty trained, crate trained, leash trained, stair trained, car trained, groom trained, basic obedience trained, and extensively socialized. Almost every command is understood by this age, although some may not yet be mastered.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Puppies are tested through repetition during these final two weeks at Recherche Goldens to make sure they are worthy of graduation at the Puppy Academy.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="RechercheGoldens" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-41" height="212" src="wp-content/uploads/recherchegoldens-org/sites/173/Pup1-300x212.jpg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          Repetition is the mother of all learning.
         </em>
        </p>
