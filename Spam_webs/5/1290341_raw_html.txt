         <a href="http://barkpost.com/author/brandon-rhoads/" title="Brandon Rhoads">
          <img alt="Brandon Rhoads" class="avatar avatar-60 wp-user-avatar wp-user-avatar-60 alignnone photo" height="60" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Ricky-Rhoads-1-150x150.jpg" width="60"/>

         <h2>
          <a href="http://barkpost.com/author/brandon-rhoads/" title="Brandon Rhoads">
           Brandon Rhoads
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p class="post-time">
          1 month ago
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Since dogs are family, it can be very easy to treat them that way. And if youre anything like my family, when we play in the pool we play HARD!
         </p>
         <p>
          But your dogs place in the pool is not the same as a persons. Safety is always a must when playing in the pool, but your dogs safety is entirely different issue than the safety of humans.
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are the 11 things you should do for your dog before, during, and after a dip in the pool.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           BEFORE
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1. Make sure your dog knows how to swim.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Duh. Maybe your dog should start in the kiddie pool before jumping into the deep end.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool1" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-46799 has-source" data-sourcename="Eagle Pack" data-sourceurl="http://www.eaglepack.com/blog/?m=201407" height="436" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool1-600x436.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <a class="entry-related-content parent clear ga-track-me" data-tracking='{"category": "click", "action": "In-Article (Sponsored)", "data": "Hilariously Awkward Dog Doesnt Understand How To Swim"}' href="http://barkpost.com/dog-doesnt-understand-how-to-swim/">
          <div class="col-sm-3 col-xs-5 entry-related-content-thumbnail">
           <div class="hide-mobile">
            <img alt="Related: Hilariously Awkward Dog Doesnt Understand How To Swim" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-12-28-at-10.07.03-AM.png"/>
           </div>
           <div class="hide show-mobile">
            <img alt="Related: Hilariously Awkward Dog Doesnt Understand How To Swim" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Screen-Shot-2013-12-28-at-10.07.03-AM.png"/>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="col-sm-9 col-xs-7">
           <div class="meta-label">
            Related
           </div>
           <h4 class="entry-related-content-title">
            Hilariously Awkward Dog Doesnt Understand How To Swim
           </h4>
          </div>
         </a>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2. Understand that personalities determine strong swimmers, NOT breed.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          You may think your dog has a natural inclination to the water because its a [enter random breed here], but your dog will develop a relationship with the water based on
          <em>
           who
          </em>
          your dog is, not
          <em>
           what
          </em>
          your dog is.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-46800 has-source" data-sourcename="Gadoo" data-sourceurl="http://www.gadoo.com.br/entretenimento/21-caes-que-adoram-o-verao/" height="646" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool2.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3. Get a lifevest for your dog.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Find a
          <a href="http://www.kurgo.com/dog-life-jackets/" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
           comfy design
          </a>
          that is both effective and visible.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-46801 has-source" data-sourcename="Kurgo" data-sourceurl="http://www.kurgo.com/dog-jackets/surf-n-turf-dog-life-jacket/" height="600" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool4.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           4. Do some obedience training.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Sit and stay might not have a place in the pool, but come certainly does. Your pup should always come when you call (a simple trick is to ALWAYS ALWAYS ALWAYS reward your dog when it comes) and this goes double in the pool.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-46803 has-source" data-sourcename="Sojos" data-sourceurl="https://www.sojos.com/blog/beyond-basic-commands-tricks-teach-your-dog" height="534" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool3.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5. Learn some pet CPR.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Its one of those things its better to know and not need, than to need and not know.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-46804 has-source" data-sourcename="Brokelyn" data-sourceurl="http://brokelyn.com/your-weekly-planner-doggy-cpr-burgers-for-obama-brewskee-ball/dog-cpr/" height="433" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool5.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           DURING
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           6. Always watch your pup.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Never leave your dog unsupervised. This is key. Your pup could get tired or swallow some water or other such pool hazards, and you need to be ready to spring into action!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool6" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-46806 has-source" data-sourcename="CBS KLUV" data-sourceurl="http://kluv.cbslocal.com/2012/04/11/lifeguard-dog-rescues-puppy/" height="399" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool6.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           7. Make sure your pup can get OUT of the pool.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          The may get tired of swimming or just tired of being in the water. Either way, there needs to be an easy exit for pups to leave the pool.
          <strong>
           ***
           <em>
            This one is very impawtent. If your pup doesnt think there is an exit, they will just stay in the pool until they are dangerously exhausted
           </em>
           .***
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool8" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-46807 has-source" data-sourcename="St. Louis Today" data-sourceurl="http://www.stltoday.com/suburban-journals/metro/life/area-groups-open-facilities-for-dog-swims/article_9853e033-11d3-5ce5-b86b-7b79dfa07d96.html" height="343" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool8-600x343.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           8. Keep your pup cool.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aspca.org/blog/five-water-safety-tips-dogs" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
           According to the ASPCA
          </a>
          , the natural summer heat intensifies near water, meaning you need keep your pup hydrated and have easy access to shade.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool7" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-46808 has-source" data-sourcename="DailyMail" data-sourceurl="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2148709/How-animals-world-taken-tip-cope-hot-weather.html" height="725" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool7-600x725.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           9. Do not let them drink pool water.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Umm, yeah, if it has chlorine in it, dont let them ingest it. Instead, provide alternate sources of water.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool9" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-46809 has-source" data-sourcename="Gifrific" data-sourceurl="http://gifrific.com/dog-holding-cup-falls-into-pool/" height="368" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool9.gif" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           10. Play in the water with your dog!
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          The best way to ensure theyre safe is to join them in the refreshing cool water you prayed for all winter!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool10" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-46810 has-source" data-sourcename="BestBullySticks" data-sourceurl="http://blog.bestbullysticks.com/6dogpoolsafetytips/" height="432" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool10-600x432.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           AFTER
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           11. Rinse with clean water after the pool.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          This removes chlorine and other chemicals which your dog will find unpleasant or harmful.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pool11" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-46812 has-source" data-sourcename="Diary Of Dennis" data-sourceurl="http://diaryofdennis.com/2013/05/10/the-most-commonly-used-meme-font-and-a-tutorial-how-to-create-a-meme-with-photoshop/" height="562" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pool11-600x562.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Learn more in the video below.
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://barkpost.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
