&lt;p&gt;A &lt;b&gt;glitch&lt;/b&gt; is a short-lived &lt;a href="/wiki/Fault_(technology)" title="Fault (technology)"&gt;fault&lt;/a&gt; in a system. It is often used to describe a transient fault that corrects itself, and is therefore difficult to troubleshoot. The term is particularly common in the &lt;a href="/wiki/Computing" title="Computing"&gt;computing&lt;/a&gt; and &lt;a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics"&gt;electronics&lt;/a&gt; industries, and in &lt;a href="/wiki/Circuit_bending" title="Circuit bending"&gt;circuit bending&lt;/a&gt;, as well as among players of &lt;a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game"&gt;video games&lt;/a&gt;, although it is applied to all types of systems including human &lt;a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization"&gt;organizations&lt;/a&gt; and nature.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;&lt;span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology"&gt;Etymology&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection"&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;&lt;a href="/w/index.php?title=Glitch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"&gt;edit&lt;/a&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;table class="mbox-small plainlinks sistersitebox" style="border:1px solid #aaa;background-color:#f9f9f9"&gt;
&lt;tr&gt;
&lt;td class="mbox-image"&gt;&lt;img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Wiktionary-logo-en.svg/37px-Wiktionary-logo-en.svg.png" width="37" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Wiktionary-logo-en.svg/55px-Wiktionary-logo-en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Wiktionary-logo-en.svg/73px-Wiktionary-logo-en.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="1089" /&gt;&lt;/td&gt;
&lt;td class="mbox-text plainlist"&gt;Look up &lt;i&gt;&lt;b&gt;&lt;a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Special:Search/glitch" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:Special:Search/glitch"&gt;glitch&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/b&gt;&lt;/i&gt; in Wiktionary, the free dictionary.&lt;/td&gt;
&lt;/tr&gt;

&lt;p&gt;Some reference books, including &lt;i&gt;Random House's American Slang&lt;/i&gt;, claim that the term comes from the &lt;a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language"&gt;German&lt;/a&gt; word &lt;i&gt;glitschen&lt;/i&gt; ("to slip") and the &lt;a href="/wiki/Yiddish_language" title="Yiddish language"&gt;Yiddish&lt;/a&gt; word &lt;i&gt;gletshn&lt;/i&gt; ("to slide or skid"). Either way it is a relatively new term, as on July 23, 1965, &lt;i&gt;&lt;a href="/wiki/Time_Magazine" title="Time Magazine" class="mw-redirect"&gt;Time Magazine&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/i&gt; felt it necessary to define it in an article: "Glitchesa spaceman's word for irritating disturbances." In relation to the reference by &lt;i&gt;Time Magazine&lt;/i&gt;, the term has been believed to enter common usage during the American &lt;a href="/wiki/Space_Race" title="Space Race"&gt;Space Race&lt;/a&gt; of the 1950s, where it was used to describe minor faults in the rocket hardware that were difficult to pinpoint.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-dictionary_1-0" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-dictionary-1"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;1&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;sup id="cite_ref-etymonline_2-0" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-etymonline-2"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;2&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;&lt;span class="mw-headline" id="Electronics_glitch"&gt;Electronics glitch&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection"&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;&lt;a href="/w/index.php?title=Glitch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Electronics glitch"&gt;edit&lt;/a&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;An &lt;b&gt;&lt;a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics"&gt;electronics&lt;/a&gt; glitch&lt;/b&gt; is an undesired transition that occurs before the signal settles to its intended value. In other words, glitch is an electrical pulse of short duration that is usually the result of a fault or design error, particularly in a &lt;a href="/wiki/Digital_circuit" title="Digital circuit" class="mw-redirect"&gt;digital circuit&lt;/a&gt;. For example, many electronic components, such as &lt;a href="/wiki/Flip-flop_(electronics)" title="Flip-flop (electronics)"&gt;flip-flops&lt;/a&gt;, are triggered by a pulse that must not be shorter than a specified minimum duration; otherwise, the component may malfunction. A pulse shorter than the specified minimum is called a glitch. A related concept is the &lt;a href="/wiki/Runt_pulse" title="Runt pulse"&gt;runt pulse&lt;/a&gt;, a pulse whose amplitude is smaller than the minimum level specified for correct operation, and a &lt;a href="/wiki/Voltage_spike" title="Voltage spike"&gt;spike&lt;/a&gt;, a short pulse similar to a glitch but often caused by &lt;a href="/wiki/Ringing_(signal)" title="Ringing (signal)"&gt;ringing&lt;/a&gt; or &lt;a href="/wiki/Crosstalk" title="Crosstalk"&gt;crosstalk&lt;/a&gt;. A &lt;i&gt;glitch&lt;/i&gt; can occur in the presence of &lt;a href="/wiki/Race_condition" title="Race condition"&gt;race condition&lt;/a&gt; in a poorly designed &lt;a href="/wiki/Digital_data" title="Digital data"&gt;digital&lt;/a&gt; logic circuit.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;&lt;span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_glitch"&gt;Computer glitch&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection"&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;&lt;a href="/w/index.php?title=Glitch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Computer glitch"&gt;edit&lt;/a&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;A &lt;b&gt;computer glitch&lt;/b&gt; is the failure of a system, usually containing a computing device, to complete its functions or to perform them properly.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In public declarations, glitch is used to suggest a minor fault which will soon be rectified and is therefore used as a &lt;a href="/wiki/Euphemism" title="Euphemism"&gt;euphemism&lt;/a&gt; for a &lt;a href="/wiki/Software_bug" title="Software bug"&gt;bug&lt;/a&gt;, which is a factual statement that a programming fault is to blame for a system failure.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;It frequently refers to an error which is not detected at the time it occurs but shows up later in data errors or incorrect human decisions. Situations which are frequently called computer glitches are:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Incorrectly written software (&lt;a href="/wiki/Software_bug" title="Software bug"&gt;software bug&lt;/a&gt;)&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Incorrect instructions given by the operator (&lt;a href="/wiki/ID-Ten-T_Error" title="ID-Ten-T Error" class="mw-redirect"&gt;operator error&lt;/a&gt;) (failure to account for this possibility might also be considered a software bug)&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Undetected invalid input data (this might also be considered a software bug)&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Undetected communications errors&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;&lt;a href="/wiki/Computer_virus" title="Computer virus"&gt;Computer virus&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Computer &lt;a href="/wiki/Exploit_(computer_security)" title="Exploit (computer security)"&gt;exploiting&lt;/a&gt; (sometimes called "hacking")&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Such glitches could produce problems such as:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Keyboard malfunction&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Number key failure&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Screen abnormalities (turned left, right or upside down)&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Random program malfunctions&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Abnormal program registering&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Examples of computer glitches causing disruption include an unexpected shutdown of a &lt;a href="/wiki/Water_filtration" title="Water filtration" class="mw-redirect"&gt;water filtration&lt;/a&gt; plant in New Canaan, 2010;&lt;sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-3"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;3&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt; failures in the &lt;a href="/wiki/Computer_Aided_Dispatch" title="Computer Aided Dispatch" class="mw-redirect"&gt;Computer Aided Dispatch&lt;/a&gt; system used by the police in Austin, resulting in unresponded 911 calls;&lt;sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-4"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;4&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt; and an unexpected &lt;a href="/wiki/Bitwise_operation#NOT" title="Bitwise operation"&gt;bit flip&lt;/a&gt; causing the &lt;a href="/wiki/Cassini%E2%80%93Huygens" title="CassiniHuygens"&gt;Cassini&lt;/a&gt; spacecraft to enter "safe mode" in November 2010.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-5"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;5&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;&lt;span class="mw-headline" id="Video_game_glitches"&gt;Video game glitches&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection"&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;&lt;a href="/w/index.php?title=Glitch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Video game glitches"&gt;edit&lt;/a&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;div class="hatnote"&gt;See also: &lt;a href="/wiki/Software_bug" title="Software bug"&gt;Software bug&lt;/a&gt; and &lt;a href="/wiki/Glitching" title="Glitching"&gt;Glitching&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;

&lt;p&gt;Glitches/bugs are software errors that can cause drastic problems within the code, and typically go unnoticed or unsolved during the production of said software. These errors can be game caused or otherwise exploited until a developer/development team repairs them. Complex software is rarely bug-free or otherwise free from errors upon first release.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Texture/model glitches are a kind of bug or other error that causes any specific model or texture to either become distorted or otherwise to not look as intended by the developers. &lt;a href="/wiki/Bethesda_Game_Studios" title="Bethesda Game Studios"&gt;Bethesda&lt;/a&gt;'s &lt;i&gt;&lt;a href="/wiki/The_Elder_Scrolls_V:_Skyrim" title="The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim"&gt;The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/i&gt; is notorious for texture glitches, as well as other errors that affect many of the company's popular titles.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-6"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;6&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt; Many games that uses &lt;a href="/wiki/Ragdoll_physics" title="Ragdoll physics"&gt;ragdoll physics&lt;/a&gt; for their character models can have such glitches happen to them.&lt;sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;"&gt;[&lt;i&gt;&lt;a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"&gt;&lt;span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)"&gt;citation needed&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/i&gt;]&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Physics glitches are errors in a game's physics engine that causes a specific entity, be it a physics object or an NPC (Non-Player Character), to be unintentionally moved to some degree. These kinds of errors can be exploited, unlike many. The chance of a physics error happening can either be entirely random or accidentally caused.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Sound glitches are in which there is an error with the game's sound. These can range from sounds playing when not intended to play or even not playing at all. Occasionally, a certain sound will loop or otherwise the player will be given the option to continuously play the sound when not intended. Often, games will play sounds &lt;i&gt;incorrectly&lt;/i&gt; due to corrupt data altering the values predefined in the code. Examples include, but are not limited to, extremely high or low pitched sounds, volume being mute or too high to understand, and also rarely even playing in reverse order/playing reversed.&lt;sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;"&gt;[&lt;i&gt;&lt;a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"&gt;&lt;span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)"&gt;citation needed&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/i&gt;]&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Glitches may include incorrectly displayed graphics, collision detection errors, game freezes/crashes, sound errors, and other issues. Graphical glitches are especially notorious in platforming games, where malformed textures can directly affect gameplay (for example, by displaying a ground texture where the code calls for an area that should damage the character, or by &lt;i&gt;not&lt;/i&gt; displaying a wall texture where there should be one, resulting in an &lt;a href="/wiki/Invisible_wall" title="Invisible wall"&gt;invisible wall&lt;/a&gt;.). Some glitches are potentially dangerous to the game's stored data.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-mirror_7-0" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-mirror-7"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;7&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;"&lt;a href="/wiki/Glitching" title="Glitching"&gt;Glitching&lt;/a&gt;" is the practice of players exploiting faults in a video game's programming to achieve tasks that give them an unfair advantage in the game, over NPC's or other players, such as running through walls or defying the game's physics. Glitches can be deliberately induced in certain home video game consoles by manipulating the game medium, such as tilting a &lt;a href="/wiki/ROM_cartridge" title="ROM cartridge"&gt;ROM cartridge&lt;/a&gt; to disconnect one or more connections along the &lt;a href="/wiki/Edge_connector" title="Edge connector"&gt;edge connector&lt;/a&gt; and interrupt part of the flow of data between the cartridge and the console.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-8"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;8&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt; This can result in graphic, music, or gameplay errors. Doing this, however, carries the risk of crashing the game or even causing permanent damage to the game medium.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-9"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;9&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Heavy use of glitches are used in performing a &lt;a href="/wiki/Speedrun" title="Speedrun"&gt;speedrun&lt;/a&gt; of a video game.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-10"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;10&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt; One type of glitch often used for speedrunning is a &lt;a href="/wiki/Stack_overflow" title="Stack overflow"&gt;Stack overflow&lt;/a&gt;, which is referred to "overflowing." Another type of speedrunning glitch, which is almost impossible to do by humans and is mostly made use of in tool assisted speedruns, is &lt;a href="/wiki/Arbitrary_code_execution" title="Arbitrary code execution"&gt;arbitrary code execution&lt;/a&gt; which will cause an object in a game to do something outside of its intended function.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-11"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;11&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Part of the &lt;a href="/wiki/Quality_assurance" title="Quality assurance"&gt;quality assurance&lt;/a&gt; process (as performed by &lt;a href="/wiki/Game_tester" title="Game tester" class="mw-redirect"&gt;game testers&lt;/a&gt; for &lt;a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game"&gt;video games&lt;/a&gt;) is locating and reproducing glitches, and then compiling reports on the glitches to be fed back to the programmers so that they can repair the bugs. Certain games have a cloud-type system for updates to the software that usually repairs coding faults and other errors in the games.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-mirror_7-1" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-mirror-7"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;7&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;&lt;span class="mw-headline" id="TV_glitch"&gt;TV glitch&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection"&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;&lt;a href="/w/index.php?title=Glitch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: TV glitch"&gt;edit&lt;/a&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In broadcasting, a corrupted signal may glitch in the form of jagged lines on the screen, misplaced squares, static looking effects, freezing problems, or inverted colors. The glitches may affect the video and/or audio (usually audio dropout) or the transmission. These glitches may be caused by a variety of issues, interference from portable electronics or microwaves, damaged cables at the broadcasting center, or weather.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-12"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;12&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;&lt;span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_culture"&gt;Popular culture&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection"&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;&lt;a href="/w/index.php?title=Glitch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Popular culture"&gt;edit&lt;/a&gt;&lt;span class="mw-editsection-bracket"&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The 1976 nonfiction book &lt;i&gt;CB Bible&lt;/i&gt; includes glitch in its glossary of &lt;a href="/wiki/Citizens_band_radio" title="Citizens band radio"&gt;citizens band radio&lt;/a&gt; slang, meaning "an indefinable technical defect in CB equipment", indicating the term was already then in use on citizens band.&lt;sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-13"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;13&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt; The 2008 short film &lt;i&gt;The Glitch&lt;/i&gt;, opening film and best science fiction finalist at &lt;a href="/wiki/Dragon_Con" title="Dragon Con"&gt;Dragon Con&lt;/a&gt; Independent Film Festival 2008, deals with the disorientation of late-night TV viewer Harry Owen (&lt;a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Charles_Blamphin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Scott Charles Blamphin (page does not exist)"&gt;Scott Charles Blamphin&lt;/a&gt;), who experiences 'heavy brain-splitting digital breakdowns.'&lt;sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"&gt;&lt;a href="#cite_note-14"&gt;&lt;span&gt;[&lt;/span&gt;14&lt;span&gt;]&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/sup&gt; In the 2012 animated film &lt;i&gt;&lt;a href="/wiki/Wreck-It_Ralph" title="Wreck-It Ralph"&gt;Wreck-It Ralph&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/i&gt;, Vanellope von Schweetz is a glitchy character in the video game &lt;i&gt;Sugar Rush&lt;/i&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;
