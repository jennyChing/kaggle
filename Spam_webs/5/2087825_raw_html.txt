            <h1 class="title">
             Yesterdays transportation meets todays mapping technology: Google employs camels to Street View desert
            </h1>
            <div class="title" style="color: #666666">
             Tech giant makes decision to use native animal so as to not disrupt natural environment
            </div>
            <div>
             <p class="ep_Body">
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              By now, were all familiar with Googles Street View service  a ground-level viewing of local towns and streets, which is part of the companys larger Google Maps and Google Earth products.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Whats probably not known is the extreme lengths the company goes in order to capture these fantastically intimate images of neighborhoods around the world. The Trekker, as it is referred to, is the Googles specially designed camera designed for the purpose of capturing high-quality 360-degree visuals.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              And while its normal home is atop a Google-sponsored vehicle, its also sat on other means of transportation, including bicycles, snowmobiles, and even within backpacks.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The reason? Google needed to record Street View imagery of the Liwa Desert in the United Arab Emirates.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The reason why they went with a camel and not, say, a quad or buggy vehicle? The company wanted to make sure that their efforts in this natural environment caused as little disruption as possible.
             </p>
