        <p>
         <a href="https://seo-hacker.com/losing-tweets">
          <img alt="HTTPS as an SEO Ranking Factor" class="aligncenter wp-image-9040 size-full" data-wp-pid="9040" height="314" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/HTTPS-as-an-SEO-Ranking-Factor.jpg" width="715"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It was a major decision that weve held off for too long. I was tapping my fingers on my computer table contemplating if the risk was worth it. I was about to lose 101,569 recorded tweets and retweets (accounting for 77% of our entire social shares) on SEO Hackers articles. I breathed out a long sigh before finally telling my developer
         <em>
          Lets do it.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-9010">
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9026" style="width: 196px">
         <a href="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/SEO-Hacker-Twitter-Shares.png">
          <img alt="SEO Hacker Twitter Shares" class="wp-image-9026 size-full" data-wp-pid="9026" height="95" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/SEO-Hacker-Twitter-Shares.png" width="186"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Goodbye.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         An hour later, all the recorded Tweets in SEO Hackers website turned to a depressing zero. It felt like an abandoned country ghost town in an old wild west movie. Building this blog for a little over 4 years now with over 300 blog posts, I found myself to be a little lost even after the decision has been made.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Content-Marketing-Tips-Post-Retweets-Gone.png">
          <img alt="Content Marketing Tips Post - Retweets Gone" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9012" data-wp-pid="9012" height="556" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Content-Marketing-Tips-Post-Retweets-Gone.png" width="876"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Lets just hope this works.
         </em>
         I whispered.
        </p>
        <p>
         You see, Ive read all about the new
         <a href="http://googlewebmastercentral.blogspot.com/2014/08/https-as-ranking-signal.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="HTTPS as a ranking signal">
          HTTPS ranking factor
         </a>
         that Google is going to incorporate in their algorithm. To cut the long story short, they said that:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          For now its only a very lightweight signal  affecting fewer than 1% of global queries, and carrying less weight than other signals such as high-quality content
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Its been just a little over two weeks since we made the change. Im excited to say that we already have some valuable data and insights to show for our huge social signal loss. Before I begin, let me qualify the study for you:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Study set:
         </strong>
         46 keywords  all of which we have been tracking for 2 weeks now for this case studys purposes  since we moved the SEO Hacker blog to HTTPS in between that time frame.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3 Competitors
         </strong>
          All of whom are SEO companies in the Philippines with similar business models as ours and are competing for very similar keywords as SEO Hacker. For company interest purposes of the competitors data in this study, we chose not to disclose the names of the individual competitors. Instead, we named them Company 1, Company 2, and Company 3 accordingly.
        </p>
        <p>
         To clarify, these companies did not make the shift to HTTPS. We merely recorded their data to compare against ours in the 2-week timeline since our shift to HTTPS.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Website we used:
         </strong>
         We used seo-hacker.com for the rankings since its the first one we converted to HTTPS. We feel the need to tell you this since we have three company sites.(seo-hacker.net for
         <a href="https://seo-hacker.net/seo-package/" target="_blank" title="SEO Services">
          SEO services and clients
         </a>
         , seo-hacker.org for
         <a href="http://seo-hacker.org" target="_blank" title="SEO School">
          SEO School
         </a>
         , and finally, seo-hacker.com for our
         <a href="https://seo-hacker.com" target="_blank" title="SEO Hacker Blog">
          SEO blog
         </a>
         ).
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Search Engines used in the Study:
         </strong>
         Google (Global), Yahoo and Bing
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Disclaimer:
         </strong>
         I do not accredit the ranking increase solely to our shift to HTTPS. Im aware that no one in the SEO industry will accredit causation to a single factor. However the data speaks quite strongly about the timely correlation of our transfer to HTTPS to our increase in rankings.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Summary of Results:
         </strong>
         SEO Hackers spread of keyword rankings have Increased by as much as
         <strong>
          26%
         </strong>
        </p>

          Keywords that
          <strong>
           increased
          </strong>
          in rankings:
          <strong>
           6
          </strong>
         </li>
         <li>
          Median
          <strong>
           decrease
          </strong>
          of the keywords that ranked
          <strong>
           lower
          </strong>
          over time for Company 3:
          <strong>
           12%
          </strong>
         </li>
         <li>
          Keywords that
          <strong>
           decreased
          </strong>
          in rankings:
          <strong>
           2
          </strong>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Conclusion:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         SEO Hacker increased in keyword rankings by approximately 480% more than the closest competitor (28 keywords from SEO Hacker increased by as much as 26% vs 6 keywords from Competitor 3 who experienced only a maximum ranking increase of 7% ).
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Takeaway:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Was it worth it? As of this point, its inconclusive because of two things:
        </p>
        <p>
         1) The study is just for 2 weeks worth of data.
        </p>
        <p>
         2) 101,569 tweets is a lot to trade off even for as much as 480% increase in rankings against a comparison company.
        </p>
        <p>
         However, after seeing the results, I had HTTPS applied in a heartbeat for our
         <a href="https://seo-hacker.net" target="_blank" title="SEO Services Philippines">
          SEO Services site
         </a>
          which does not have any recorded social signals in any of its pages.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://seo-hacker.net" target="_blank">
          <img alt="SEO Hacker Services" class="aligncenter wp-image-9038 size-large" data-wp-pid="9038" height="306" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/SEO-Hacker-Services-1024x306.png" width="1024"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Consequently, I also had HTTPS applied to my other start-up,
         <a href="https://qeryz.com" target="_blank" title="Qeryz Microsurvey tool">
          Qeryz
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://qeryz.com" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Qeryz Microsurvey Tool" class="aligncenter wp-image-9039 size-full" data-wp-pid="9039" height="584" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Qeryz.png" width="973"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Changing your entire site from HTTP to HTTPS is no easy task. Here are the 12 things Ive learned segmented into categories:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Things me and my developer used to pull this trick off:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         1) Used the
         <a href="https://wordpress.org/plugins/ssl-insecure-content-fixer/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="SSL Insecure Content Fixer">
          SSL Insecure Content Fixer
         </a>
         plugin to make sure that each of our pages will reflect HTTPS properly. Weve tried other plugins but this one works best to our taste. Without it, some of the images and other plugins cease to work properly.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/MaxCDN-Shared-SSL.png">
          <img alt="MaxCDN Shared SSL" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-9032" data-wp-pid="9032" height="334" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/MaxCDN-Shared-SSL-1024x334.png" width="1024"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2) I had to change our
         <a href="https://seo-hacker.com/maxcdn.php" target="_blank" title="Sign up with MaxCDN">
          MaxCDN
         </a>
         pullzone settings from HTTP to HTTPS. Strangely, the CNAME for cdn.seo-hacker.com wasnt working anymore too so I was forced to use a direct CDN address connection to seohacker-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com  which was configured as a shared SSL from MaxCDN
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Purge-Files-MaxCDN.png">
          <img alt="Purge Files MaxCDN" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9033" data-wp-pid="9033" height="170" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Purge-Files-MaxCDN.png" width="584"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3) Purged all caches  including the CDNs cache to pull all the static files from HTTPS instead of HTTP.
        </p>
        <p>
         4) Fixed seo-hacker.coms internal caching system to work with HTTPS. I use the
         <a href="https://wordpress.org/plugins/w3-total-cache/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Caching Plugin for WordPress">
          W3 Total Cache
         </a>
         plugin.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/HTTPS-as-an-HTML-Ranking-Factor.jpg">
          <img alt="HTTPS as an HTML Ranking Factor" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9034" data-wp-pid="9034" height="345" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/HTTPS-as-an-HTML-Ranking-Factor.jpg" width="738"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         5) Reviewed Cyrus Shepards extremely useful post on 
         <a href="http://moz.com/blog/seo-tips-https-ssl" target="_blank" title="The Big List of SEO Tips and Tricks for Using HTTPS on Your Website">
          The Big List of SEO Tips and Tricks for Using HTTPS on Your Website
         </a>
          to make sure I didnt miss out on anything. Cant be too careful as a switch to HTTPS is a major move. It may make or break your rankings depending on things you mightve missed along the way.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Unfortunately, HTTPS is pretty exclusive and not all WordPress plugins will cooperate with an HTTPS site. Here are some consequences Ive faced and sacrificed to finalize our move to HTTPS:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         6) I had to kill nRelate Related Content plugin and sacrifice internally referred Pageviews as it doesnt work with HTTPS.
        </p>
        <p>
         7) Killed Activeshare plugin by OrangeSoda  not really something major since I plan to kill this plugin anyway.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Ultimate-Social-Deux.png">
          <img alt="Ultimate Social Deux" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9043" data-wp-pid="9043" height="485" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Ultimate-Social-Deux.png" width="717"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         8) Killed my manually coded social sharing buttons and switched to
         <a href="http://social.ultimate-wp.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Ultimate Social Deux site">
          Ultimate Social Deux
         </a>
         because it works well with HTTPS. Its actually what were using right now as our social sharing buttons in the site.
        </p>
        <p>
         9) Facing the need to renew our SSL certificate yearly from hereon out. Its an extra expense but if the rankings hold out to increase steadily such as what weve experienced so far, it would be well worth the investment.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Some other positive technical effects of the HTTPS change:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/SEO-Hacker-site-load-speed-with-SPDY.png">
          <img alt="SEO Hacker site load speed with SPDY" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9035" data-wp-pid="9035" height="213" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/SEO-Hacker-site-load-speed-with-SPDY.png" width="610"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         10) We were able to use
         <a href="https://seo-hacker.com/maxcdn.php" target="_blank" title="MaxCDN">
          MaxCDNs SPDY technology
         </a>
          which increased our site load speed like crazy. Imagine, at 2.5mb worth of data and 110 external DNS requests, our site loads as fast as 1.65 seconds! I did a short comparison with Copyblogger (which boasts on running with Synthesis  its own site speed optimized hosting) and the results were quite far off. And they only have 1.5mb worth of data and 68 external requests.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Copyblogger-site-speed.png">
          <img alt="Copyblogger site speed" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9036" data-wp-pid="9036" height="212" src="https://seohackercdn-seohacker.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Copyblogger-site-speed.png" width="611"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Do note that theyre not running on HTTPS either. I have no idea if theyre running on SPDY though. In any case, its a huge difference to behold.
        </p>
        <p>
         11) Our sites transfer protocols security increased dramatically  naturally because of the SSL certificate.
        </p>
        <p>
         12) The social signals well accumulate from this point on as a blog wont be killed off in the future because weve already adopted to HTTPS. No more returning back to a depressing zero. The only way from here is up!
        </p>
        <p>
         Best of all? Weve already experienced a dramatic increase in rankings for our
         <a href="https://seo-hacker.net" target="_blank" title="SEO Services Company Philippines">
          SEO Services Company site
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Hope this entry picked on your brain enough to make the (relatively early) switch to HTTPS. I sincerely hope you wont lose as much Tweets / Retweets as we did!
        </p>
