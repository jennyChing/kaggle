    <h1>
     11 Reasons to Make Megve Your Next Alpine Vacation
    </h1>
    <div class="ssw-content-dek">
     <p>
      We love
      <a href="http://www.cntraveler.com/stories/2015-01-05/a-guide-to-the-best-skiing-and-apres-ski-in-the-alps">
       Courchevel and Verbier
      </a>
      , but the tiny ski town of Megve in the French Alps holds its own against these celebrated Alpine villages with its rich history, excellent cuisine, and unique hotels. Here are 10 reasons to visit.
     </p>
    </div>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The five-star luxury of M de Megve
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       <a href="http://cntraveler.com/hotels/meg-ve/hotel-m-de-megeve">
        M de Megve
       </a>
       finds a balance between Alpine charm and modern design. The hotel, which reopened last year after extensive renovations, now has 42 rooms and suites offering high-end comfort with a warm vibe. The downstairs bar with fireplace is the perfect place to relax aprs-ski, and in-house dining can be done casually at Le Bistrot or more formally at Le SoMMet du M de Megve. The Cinq Mondes-operated spa, with its gorgeous treatment rooms, hammam, sauna, and heated indoor pool, is the icing on the cake.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper portrait">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2015/02/25/54ee05b46450ad091fd9fb76_1-m-de-megeve-stephanie-duval.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The 360 views from L'Alpette
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       At an altitude of 1,895 meters (about 6,200 feet),
       <a href="http://cntraveler.com/restaurants/meg-ve/l-alpette">
        L'Alpette restaurant
       </a>
       offers incomparable views of the nearby mountaintops, including the legendary Mont Blanc. With its red and white napkins and tablecloths, valet service for your skis, and extensive wine menu, the place is much more of a fine dining establishment than we're used to seeing so near the slopes. If you want to enjoy lunch without having to ski up to it, L'Alpette is also accessible via the Rochebrune cable car.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2015/02/25/54ee05b56450ad091fd9fb7f_2-l-alpette-l-design-dr.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      Natural beauty
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Some ski resorts sacrifice natural beauty in favor of well-mapped-out slopes. That's not the case in Megve, where you'll ski past intimidating rock formations and fairytale woods where trees bend under the weight of freshly fallen snow.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2015/02/25/54ee05b9560f0bf2218e8047_3-natural-beauty-stephanie-duval.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The fine dining at Le 1920
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       29-year-old Julien Gatillon won his first Michelin star in 2014 for his work at
       <a href="http://cntraveler.com/restaurants/meg-ve/le-1920">
        Le 1920
       </a>
       at Le Domaine du Mont d'Arbois. He does decidedly modern treatments of classic ingredients such as foie gras, truffles, and scallops as part of his apparent mission to give French haute cuisine a youthful and fresh makeover. Ask for a table by the window, or on the terrace if weather permits, to enjoy the beautiful view.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2015/02/25/54ee05b86450ad091fd9fba4_4-le-1920-courtesy-le-1920.jpg" title=""/>

     <h2>
      The luxurious shopping sprees
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Megve may be a small town, but it has presences from major French luxury brands like Herms, Moncler, and Faonnable. But if you want to expand your shopping range a little bit, head to multibrand boutiques like BluBerry, L'Adress or Blue.Ink.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper portrait">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2015/02/25/54ee05c0560f0bf2218e8074_5-megeve-shopping-1-stephanie-duval.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>

    <section>
     <h2>
      Arts, culture, and a snack in between
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Art lovers take note: Although
       <a href="http://www.scarlett-megeve.com/">
        Scarlett
       </a>
       is technically a
       <em>
        librarie
       </em>
       (aka bookshop), it also sells antiques and art objects, while the Leica-sponsored
       <a href="http://ct-gallery.com/">
        CT Gallery
       </a>
       , not surprisingly, focuses on photographic exhibitions. Take a break between art tours at the newly opened outpost of macaron mecca Ladure.
      </p>

      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2015/02/25/54ee05c0560f0bf2218e807c_5-megeve-shopping-2-stephanie-duval.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>

    <section>
     <h2>
      The quirky charm of L'Htel Mont-Blanc
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       <a href="http://cntraveler.com/hotels/meg-ve/l-hotel-mont-blanc">
        L'Htel Mont-Blanc
       </a>
       is the most centrally located luxury hotel in Megve, which is convenient if you want to walk straight from your hotel to the cable ride that'll bring you up the slopes, or to the town center for some shopping or dining. The Mont-Blanc also has an excellent spa, Pure Altitude, with treatment rooms that look like the inside of an ice cave. The salon de th, finally, is a place to catch your breath after a day of sports, enjoying one of the specially composed teas or their signature spiced hot chocolate.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">

     <h2>
      The lobster roll at Les Enfants Terribles
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       <a href="http://cntraveler.com/restaurants/meg-ve/les-enfants-terribles">
        The in-house restaurant
       </a>
       of L'Htel Mont-Blanc lures you in as much with an original menu as with its unconventional interior thanks to a mural painting by French writer, designer, and filmmaker Jean Cocteau, whose novel
       <em>
        Les Enfants Terribles
       </em>
       gave the restaurant its name. The menu's highlight is a European interpretation of the lobster roll, a dish not often seen outside of the eastern United States.
      </p>

      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2015/02/25/54ee05d3560f0bf2218e80cd_9-les-enfants-terribles-cr-l-di-orio-and-l-brandajs.jpg" title=""/>

     <h2>
      The incomparable rituals of the Spa des Fermes de Marie
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Whether spa culture is the main reason for your trip to Megve or you want to schedule a day of relaxing in between the skiing, the iconic
       <a href="http://www.cntraveler.com/hotels/meg-ve/les-fermes-de-marie">
        Les Fermes de Marie
       </a>
       is sure to offer a treatment to your liking: facials, body wraps, scrubs, baths, and massages. The spa is not only literally big, it is also very popular, so appointments have to be booked well in advance.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper portrait">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2015/02/25/54ee05cc6450ad091fd9fbfe_7-spa-les-fermes-cr-l-di-orio.jpg" title=""/>

     <h2>
      The magical, fairytale like atmosphere of the main square
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Every year the town of Megve commissions a local artist to decorate the gigantic Christmas tree in the middle of the main square. The tree stays up all winter long, and there are twinkling lights in the windows of shops and the local church. The main square is also where locals with horsedrawn carriages assemble each day to take tourists out for a ride through the nearby hills. This season, Ladure added to the charm by placing a beautiful white carousel on the square.
      </p>

Walking into the rooms feels like you've walked into a novel, and you get to play the main character in a historic romance. Even the food is in theme at the Beef Lodge, where Black Angus, Bavarian beef and Simmental beef are the showstoppers in what looks like a hunter's hideout, complete with trophies and animal skins against the walls.
