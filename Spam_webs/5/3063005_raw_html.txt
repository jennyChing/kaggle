     <p>
      <b>
       Waze
      </b>
      is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GPS" title="GPS">
       GPS
      </a>
      -based geographical navigation
      <a href="/wiki/Mobile_app" title="Mobile app">
       application program
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">
       smartphones
      </a>
      with GPS support and display screens which provides
      <a href="/wiki/Turn-by-turn_navigation" title="Turn-by-turn navigation">
       turn-by-turn
      </a>
      information and user-submitted travel times and route details, downloading location-dependent information over
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mobile_telephone_network" title="Mobile telephone network">
       mobile networks
      </a>
      . Waze was developed in
      <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">
       Israel
      </a>
      , funded by early-stage American
      <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">
       venture capital
      </a>
      firm
      <a href="/wiki/BlueRun_Ventures" title="BlueRun Ventures">
       Bluerun Ventures
      </a>
      , and was acquired by
      <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">
       Google
      </a>
      in 2013.
     </p>
     <p>
      Waze won the Best Overall Mobile App award at the 2013
      <a href="/wiki/Mobile_World_Congress" title="Mobile World Congress">
       Mobile World Congress
      </a>
      , beating
      <a href="/wiki/Dropbox_(service)" title="Dropbox (service)">
       Dropbox
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Flipboard" title="Flipboard">
       Flipboard
      </a>
      and others.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globes-bestapp_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-globes-bestapp-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On June 11, 2013,
      <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">
       Google
      </a>
      completed the acquisition of Waze for a reported
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">
       US$
      </a>
      1.3 billion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As part of the deal signed, each of Waze's 100 employees will receive an average of about $1.2 million, which represents the largest payout to employees in the history of Israeli high tech.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Waze supports
      <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">
       Android
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone">
       iPhone
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Symbian_OS" title="Symbian OS">
       Symbian
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/BlackBerry_10" title="BlackBerry 10">
       BlackBerry 10
      </a>
      (except BlackBerry Q10),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Windows_Phone_8" title="Windows Phone 8">
       Windows Phone 8
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Windows_Mobile" title="Windows Mobile">
       Windows Mobile
      </a>
      from version 5.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In July 2013 Waze said that they were planning to support both iPhone and Android, and would consider supporting new platforms. As older platforms (Symbian, WM, BlackBerry) do not support either a full native UI or other
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/API" title="API">
       APIs
      </a>
      they rely on, they could not support them, although existing versions would continue to work.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Waze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      During 2006, a community project was founded by Ehud Shabtai - "FreeMap Israel". The aim of the project was to create, by the community users, a free digital database of the
      <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Israel" title="Geography of Israel">
       map of Israel
      </a>
      , and to ensure its free content, update and distribution, for non-commercial usage, as convenient as possible.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Terms_.26_Conditions_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Terms_.26_Conditions-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      During 2008, the project website stated: We have a new name and address. From now on, we are called Waze.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Parallelly, LinQmap was founded in Israel by Uri Levine, software engineer Ehud Shabtai, and Amir Shinar. During 2009, the company name was changed to Waze Mobile Ltd. In December 2011, Waze employed 80 people, composed of 70 at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ra%27anana,_Israel" title="Ra'anana, Israel">
       Ra'anana, Israel
      </a>
      and 10 in
      <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">
       Palo Alto, California
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2010, the company raised $25 million in the second round of funding.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2011, the company, which planned to monetize through
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Location_based_advertising" title="Location based advertising">
       location based advertising
      </a>
      and to expand into
      <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
       Asia
      </a>
      , raised an additional $30 million in financing.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_16-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-forbes-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_by_Google">
       Acquisition by Google
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Waze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisition by Google">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">
       Facebook
      </a>
      and other companies were interested in purchasing Waze, but did not reach an agreement.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In June 2013 Google bought Waze for $1.1 billion, adding social data to its mapping business.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-investvine_21-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-investvine-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In June 2013 the United States
      <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">
       Federal Trade Commission
      </a>
      started considering whether Google's purchase of Waze might violate
      <a href="/wiki/Competition_law" title="Competition law">
       competition law
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Waze was one of very few competitors in the mobile mapping sector to Google's own
      <a href="/wiki/Google_Maps" title="Google Maps">
       Google Maps
      </a>
      . As of October 2013
      <sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
       <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Waze&amp;action=edit">
        [update]
       </a>
      </sup>
      the FTC has decided that it will not be challenging Google's acquisition of Waze.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The UK
      <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Fair_Trading" title="Office of Fair Trading">
       Office of Fair Trading
      </a>
      and the Israel Antitrust Authority are also investigating.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [

        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">

       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
       Overview
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Waze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Overview">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Waze_3.5_Screen.png">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="640" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/27/Waze_3.5_Screen.png/200px-Waze_3.5_Screen.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/27/Waze_3.5_Screen.png/300px-Waze_3.5_Screen.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/27/Waze_3.5_Screen.png/400px-Waze_3.5_Screen.png 2x" width="200"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Waze_3.5_Screen.png" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The Waze app with visible users, traffic volume, and noted red-light cameras
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Waze differs from traditional
      <a href="/wiki/GPS_navigation_software" title="GPS navigation software">
       GPS navigation software
      </a>
      in that it is community-driven, gathering complementary map data and traffic information from its users.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Like other GPS software it learns from users' driving times to provide routing and real-time traffic updates. It is free to download and use. People can report accidents, traffic jams, speed and police traps, and from the online map editor, can update roads, landmarks, house numbers, etc. Waze also identifies the cheapest fuel station near a user or along their route, provided Waze has enabled gas prices for that country. As of January 2012
      <sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
       <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Waze&amp;action=edit">
        [update]
       </a>
      </sup>
      , the app had been downloaded 12 million times worldwide.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_16-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-forbes-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In July 2012 Waze announced that it had reached 20 million users, half of them recruited in the previous six months.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      According to Yahoo! there were nearly 50 million Waze users as of June 2013.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Waze can be used anywhere in the world but it requires a critical mass of users to have real utility; currently only 13 countries have a full base map, the others are incompletely mapped, requiring users to record roads and edit maps. As of 2013
      <sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
       <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Waze&amp;action=edit">
        [update]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Waze has a complete base map for the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, France, Germany, Italy, Netherlands, Belgium, Israel (claimed to be the best map for that country
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ), South Africa,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Colombia, Ecuador, Chile and Panama, but the company has plans to complete maps for other countries in Europe and elsewhere.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition to turn-by-turn voice navigation, real-time traffic, and other location-specific alerts, Waze simultaneously sends anonymous information, including users' speed and location, back to its database to improve the service as a whole. This
      <a href="/wiki/Crowdsourcing" title="Crowdsourcing">
       crowdsourcing
      </a>
      allows the Waze community to report navigation and mapping errors and traffic accidents simply by running the app while driving.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
         <span title="How does automatic information report errors and accidents? (July 2013)">
          clarification needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Waze uses gaming conventions to engage users and encourage them to provide more information, allowing them to "drive over" icons of cupcakes and other things to earn points. Waze also offers points for traffic or road hazard reports, which can be used to change the user's avatar, and to increase their status in the community.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2011 Waze Mobile updated the software to display real-time, community-curated points of interest, including local events such as street fairs and protests.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In June 2012 Waze launched an update to provide real-time fuel prices. As with all Waze real-time updates, prices are submitted by users, however this feature is not available in all countries.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
       <a href="#cite_note-36">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Since November 2012, in monetizing its app, Waze offers resellers and advertisers a web interface to advertise based on locations where a small icon will appear on a given location for an interested Wazer to engage with the ads.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
       <a href="#cite_note-37">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        37
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It also offers to TV news stations a web interface to broadcast current traffic reports and alerts directly from the Waze app; the service had been used by 25 TV U.S. news stations by June 2013
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
       <a href="#cite_note-38">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        38
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It has also been used in
      <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">
       Rio de Janeiro
      </a>
      inside
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_de_Opera%C3%A7%C3%B5es_Rio&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Centro de Operaes Rio (page does not exist)">
       Centro de Operaes Rio
      </a>
      (Rio's Operations Center) since July 24, 2013,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">
       <a href="#cite_note-39">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        39
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      as well as in
      <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">
       New York
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">
       New Jersey
      </a>
      since 2012.
     </p>
     <p>
      In June 2013, Waze introduced a global localization project that enables future road closures and real-time traffic updates during major events in a given country, for example
      <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">
       Tour de France
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">
       <a href="#cite_note-40">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        40
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Safety_and_security_risk">
       Safety and security risk
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Waze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Safety and security risk">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Some road-safety advocates have voiced concern over the prospect of more drivers using Waze, which they say has the potential to distract them with a flurry of icons and notifications and put them at greater risk of an accident.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">
       <a href="#cite_note-41">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        41
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In March 2014, a successful attempt was made by students from
      <a href="/wiki/Technion_%E2%80%93_Israel_Institute_of_Technology" title="Technion  Israel Institute of Technology">
       Technion-Israel Institute of Technology
      </a>
      to fake a traffic jam.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">
       <a href="#cite_note-42">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        42
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In December 2014, in a letter sent to Google,
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Police_Department" title="Los Angeles Police Department">
       Los Angeles Police Department
      </a>
      Chief
      <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Beck" title="Charlie Beck">
       Charlie Beck
      </a>
      complained about the police locator feature, claiming it could be "misused by those with criminal intent to endanger police officers and the community". It was alleged that Ismaaiyl Brinsley, who
      <a href="/wiki/2014_killings_of_NYPD_officers" title="2014 killings of NYPD officers">
       shot and killed two NYPD officers
      </a>
      that month in retaliation for the police killings of
      <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Eric_Garner" title="Death of Eric Garner">
       Eric Garner
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Shooting_of_Michael_Brown" title="Shooting of Michael Brown">
       Michael Brown
      </a>
      , had used the Waze app prior to the murders and had posted a screenshot from the app on his Instagram account hours before the shootings.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
       <a href="#cite_note-43">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        43
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Users are able to mark the presence of an officer with a small icon and indicate if the officer is visible or hidden. The LAPD, among other police agencies, pressured Google to disable the feature on the application. Google argues knowing the whereabouts of an officer actually promotes safer driving.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">
       <a href="#cite_note-44">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        44
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">
       <a href="#cite_note-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Licensing">
       Licensing
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GNU_General_Public_License_version_2" title="GNU General Public License version 2">

      </a>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">

      <a href="/wiki/OpenStreetMap" title="OpenStreetMap">

      </a>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48">

        <span>
         ]

      </a>

      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roey_Gorodish&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Roey Gorodish (page does not exist)">

      <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">
