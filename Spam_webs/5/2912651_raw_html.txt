       <p>
        <img alt="10448747_682855195117412_4428388512796304532_n" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-320906" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/boingboing.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/10448747_682855195117412_4428388512796304532_n.jpg?resize=680%2C452"/>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        For his project 'Exobiotanica,' artist
        <a href="http://azumamakoto.com/">
         Makoto Azuma
        </a>
        of Japan headed to the  Nevada Black Rock Desert to launch a 50-year-old Japanese white pine bonsai into the stratosphere.
        <span id="more-320894">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="10537428_682854545117477_8357006481827968694_n" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-320900" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/boingboing.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/10537428_682854545117477_8357006481827968694_n.jpg?resize=680%2C452"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="10559841_682855198450745_7217805375146737847_n" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-320905" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/boingboing.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/10559841_682855198450745_7217805375146737847_n.jpg?resize=680%2C452"/>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        With help from Sacramento-based
        <a href="http://www.jpaerospace.com/">
         JP Aerospace
        </a>
        ("a
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/jpaerospace?sk=wall">
         volunteer, DIY space program
        </a>
        "), Azuma sent to near-space a sculptural floral bouquet composed from several dozen types of flowers from around the globe.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="10425071_681111078625157_2413653330973049861_n" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-320897" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/boingboing.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/10425071_681111078625157_2413653330973049861_n.jpg?resize=680%2C453"/>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The project took place on July 15, near the same area where Burning Man devotees will gather to celebrate their own brand of spaced-out art in a few weeks.
       </p>
       <p>
        They used helium balloons to launch the plant matter, and say the bonsai got to about 91,800 feet altitude (27.98 km), and the bouquet got to about 87,000 feet (26.51 km).
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="10403239_681111091958489_6820183718894539955_n" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-320898" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/boingboing.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/10403239_681111091958489_6820183718894539955_n.jpg?resize=680%2C453"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Makoto Azuma:
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/MakotoAzuma">
         Facebook
        </a>
        , web,
        <a href="https://twitter.com/azumamakoto">
         Twitter
        </a>
        . The New York Times has a
        <a href="http://tmagazine.blogs.nytimes.com/2014/07/18/flowers-in-space-azuma-makoto-exobiotanica/?module=BlogPost-Title&amp;version=Blog+Main&amp;contentCollection=Art+Matters&amp;action=Click&amp;pgtype=Blogs&amp;region=Body">
         nice story on the project
        </a>
        . There are some wonderful photos of Azuma's
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/MakotoAzuma/photos_stream">
         past works on his Facebook
        </a>
        --man, I love everything!
       </p>
