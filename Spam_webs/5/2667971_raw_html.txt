        Charlotte Dawson remembered: I didnt know her but I cried for her today.
       </h1>
       <div class="clearfix">
        <div class="author clearfix">
         <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-50 photo" height="50" src="http://2.gravatar.com/avatar/2ec3c95e36d0c69e7565e8100e643512?s=50&amp;d=mm&amp;r=pg" srcset="http://2.gravatar.com/avatar/2ec3c95e36d0c69e7565e8100e643512?s=100&amp;d=mm&amp;r=pg 2x" width="50"/>

      <div class="article-content">
       <p class="leading-paragraph colour-entertainment">
        Australian model and tv host, Charlotte Dawson, passed awayone year ago today.
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         On the anniversary of Charlottesdeath, we remember her beautiful spirit and battles that she fought  most of all with themental illness that stalked her throughout her life.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Bec Sparrow wrote 12 months ago today: I believe Charlotte Dawson was a woman with a big heart who was incredibly gracious and loving. And I worry she died without knowing that she was greatly valued and respected and wanted.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Let this be a reminder to all of us to tell our love ones how much we value them every day and help to keep those with the biggest hearts, those who feel the world so deeply, here with us.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         In memory of Charlotte, we are pleased to share Becs beautiful piece about Charlottes passingonce again.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Last year, Bec Sparrow wrote
        </em>
       </p>
       = display_ad('x18', 'hidden-xs hidden-md mm_incontent', 'MM In Content'); ?&gt;= display_ad('x20', 'visible-xs mm_mob_incontent', 'MM In Content (Mobile)'); ?&gt;
       <p>
        I had a hot and cold relationship with Charlotte Dawson.
       </p>
       <p>
        Dont get me wrong, we never met. But when she first came to my attention on Australias Next Top Model (ANTM) I wasnt a fan.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-02-22 at 1.26.17 pm" class="aligncenter wp-image-413894" height="523" src="http://cdn.mamamia.com.au/wp/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screen-Shot-2015-02-22-at-1.26.17-pm.png" width="720"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        I had vague memories of Charlotte being married to troubled Olympic swimmer Scott Miller at some point and I was aware shed been a sometimes panellist on
        <i>
         Beauty and The Beast
        </i>
        with the late Stan Zemaneck years earlier but Id never given her a passing thought (other than thinking she was extraordinarily beautiful). But when she came to my attention on ANTM? If Im going to be honest, she irritated me.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <strong>
         More:
         <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/social/charlotte-dawson-baclofen/" title="The truth about Charlotte Dawsons death.">
          The truth about Charlotte Dawsons death.
         </a>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Who is this woman? Why is she so mean to the teenage entrants?
       </p>
       <p>
        Thats what I thought of Charlotte Dawson. In a nutshell: not much.
       </p>
       <p>
        But my opinion changed. And I can tell you the exact date it changed:
        <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/entertainment/mia-chats-to-charlotte-dawson-full-interview/">
         28
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         August 2011.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Charlotte sat down with Mia to do a one-on-one interview for this very website and thats when I saw the real Charlotte Dawson  a fragile, seeringly honest, loving, self-deprecating soul with a generous laugh. A woman who just like the rest of us was out there, doing her best and trying hard to be a contributor rather than a critic.
       </p>
       <div class="entry-content-asset">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="641" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/PDQLiMjykQU?feature=oembed" width="1140">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <p>
        I sat at home that day, ready to roll my eyes and instead was mesmerised by her. Instead I wanted to be her friend. There she was, sitting on the floor of the Mamamia office, make-up free, hair pulled back and wearing simple  if not daggy  clothes. She talked openly and honestly  sometimes uncomfortably  about her history of failed relationships and her inability to choose the right partner, her reconciliation with the fact she would never be a mother, her feelings about aging in an industry that values only the very young and the paper thin.
       </p>
       <p>
        I watched her talk freely about her use of Botox, her experiences with online bullying, her fierce support of gay right and the mothering and nurturing (clearly rarely caught on camera) that she gave the ANTM entrants. I would never have guessed it was Charlotte who was the mother hen to those girls, dishing out the tough love and reality checks along with big doses of love to the young entrants.
       </p>
       <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_213868" style="width: 730px">
        <img alt="charlotte-dawson" class="wp-image-213868" height="521" src="http://cdn.mamamia.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/charlotte-dawson.jpg" width="720"/>
        <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
         Charlotte as a judge for Australias Next Top Model.
        </figcaption>
       </figure>
       <p>
        So on 28
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        August 2011, I became a fan. And from then on I went out of my way to read interviews Charlotte gave in the media to learn more about her. Thats where I read about the fact she was sexually abused as a child at the hands of an elderly neighbour. About her teenage birth mother who gave her up for adoption. About her difficult relationship with her stepfather. And her pattern for choosing (according to her therapist) narccisistic men.
       </p>
       <p>
        This morning I heard the news that Charlotte Dawson was
        <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/celebrities/charlotte-dawson-dead/">
         found dead.
        </a>
        And I cried at my desk.
       </p>
       <p>
        Why? I dont even know how to explain it.
       </p>
       <p>
        We werent friends by any means. We exchanged a few messages on Twitter over the years  most frequently a
        <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/health-wellbeing/charlotte-dawson-and-the-twitter-trolls/">
         message of support
        </a>
        from one to the other when things had turned ugly and abuse was flying at either of us for one reason or another. But truth be told she wouldnt have known me in a line-up. I could have walked past her on the street and shed have been oblivious to my presence.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <strong>
         Want more like this? Try:
         <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/news/charlotte-dawson-final-interview-2/" title="6 weeks before she died, Charlotte Dawson gave her final interview.">
          6 weeks before she died, Charlotte Dawson gave her final interview
         </a>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        So why the tears?
       </p>
       <p>
        Because I feel we let her down. As a society. As human beings, quite frankly. She was so frequently pummelled on social media  the target of the most hateful, vicious messages. And having been the target myself on some occasions  let me tell you, its hard for the abuse not to swirl in your head long after youve read it.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="charlotte dawson final interview 2" class="wp-image-297849 aligncenter" height="406" src="http://cdn.mamamia.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/charlotte-dawson-final-interview-2.jpg" width="720"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Its hard for your spirit not to be irrevocably punctured; for your self-esteem not to permanently fractured when people are cajoling you to hang yourself. Or calling you a whore. (And on that subject can I just say how the hell do we expect teenagers to brush off this type of abuse when we, as adults, cant?) Anyway.
       </p>
       <p>
        So Charlotte was frequently attacked online and we tut-tutted and agreed that social media is awful and said, Oh my God, poor Charlotte but then more often than not we followed it with, Well what is she DOING on Twitter? She needs to stop responding. Thats right, we told CHARLOTTE DAWSON to get off social media rather than the parasites and miscreants who have turned platforms like Twitter and Facebook into the cowardly bullys paradise.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <strong>
         Read on:
         <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/career/charlottes-law/" title="On the day of her memorial, a friend pays a personal tribute to Charlotte Dawson.">
          On the day of her memorial, a friend pays a personal tribute to Charlotte Dawson
         </a>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        So why the tears?
       </p>
       <p>
        Because we are so bloody pathetic when it comes to understanding mental illness in this country which is RIDICULOUS. You know why? Because were surrounded by it. At least HALF the people in my friendship group are on some form of anti-depression or anti-anxiety medication. Youd never know, of course. Because still now its this big, shameful secret. But its not rare. Its not an affliction of the weak. Or the fragile. Its one in five Australians. You with me? According to the Black Dog Institute One in five (20%) Australians aged 16-85 experience a mental illness in any year. The most common mental illnesses are depressive, anxiety and substance use disorder.
       </p>
       <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_273301" style="width: 730px">
        <img alt="Charlotte Dawson " class="wp-image-273301" height="414" src="http://cdn.mamamia.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/dawson1.jpg" title="Charlotte Dawson Sunday Night interview" width="720"/>
        <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
         Charlotte, after last years Twitter saga on Sixty Minutes
        </figcaption>
       </figure>
       <p>
        So we dont need all these hushed tones when we talk about depression or
        <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/news/charlotte-dawson-attacked-on-twitter/">
         people with depression
        </a>
        . Most of us have it, have had it or will have it at some point. Of course we will. Life is bloody tough at times. And at some point we will each be brought to our knees. Ive had a bout of depression in my life. Charlotte battled depression for years. Is that any surprise given her childhood?
       </p>
       <p>
        She talked openly and honestly about her struggle with the black dog and I think many of us responded by making her feel
        <i>
         less than
        </i>
        when instead she should have been applauded for her honesty. We should have responded not just with open minds but with open hearts and arms.
       </p>
       <p>
        So why the tears?
       </p>
       <p>
        You know what? I dont know. I guess I worry that this morning her heart ached with pain and loneliness and maybe life looked impossibly bleak. Im sad because I worry that she felt like she was of no use or not wanted or had nothing to offer. Im sad because whether her death was deliberate or accidental  the result is the same: we lost her.
       </p>
       <p>
        I believe Charlotte Dawson was a woman with a big heart who was in fact incredibly gracious and loving. And I worry she died without knowing that she was greatly valued and respected and wanted.
       </p>
       <p>
        I wish I had a clever way to wrap up this column. A wise message. A moral to the story. But I dont. Im just sad because today
        <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/news/trolls-fame-and-blame-helen-razer-on-charlotte-dawson/">
         Charlotte Dawson
        </a>
        is gone.
       </p>

        <p>
         If this post brings up issues for you, or you just need someone to talk to, please call Lifeline on 131 114. You can also visit the Lifeline website
         <a href="http://www.lifeline.org.au/" target="_blank">
          here
         </a>
         and the Beyond Blue website
         <a href="http://www.beyondblue.org.au/index.aspx" target="_blank">
          here
         </a>
        </p>
       </blockquote>
