          <h3>
           Premier League football tips  Arsenal vs Liverpool prediction.
          </h3>
          <p>
           The early kick off sees the two form teams of 2015 face up as Arsenal are home against Liverpool. Arsenal have won six games in a row, conceding only three goals and scoring 13 in that time, while Liverpools last Premier League game saw them lose their first of 2015 at home to
           <a href="http://www.tipsfix.com/premier-league/liverpool-vs-manchester-united-prediction-and-football-tips/" target="_blank" title="Liverpool vs Manchester United Prediction and Football Tips">
            Manchester United
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Arsenal will be looking to continue their run and make it seven in a row, while Brendan Rodgers men will need to pick up a win if theyre to continue putting pressure on the top four.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            When you take a look at the average number of goals scored between both teams over the last 10 games, Arsenals is considerably better with 2 compared to Liverpools 1.4, while both teams concede less than a goal a game with 0.8 and 0.7 respectively.
           </li>
           <li>
            In Arsenals last 10 games, seven of them have involved more than 2.5 goals, whilst Liverpool have been involved in considerably less with four.
           </li>
           <li>
            The last time the two teams met was a very tense affair as Arsenal took a 2-1 lead in the 64th minute with the help of goals from Mathieu Debuchy and Olivier Giroud, before Martin Skrtel did what Martin Skrtel does best and headed to score the equaliser very late in the game.
           </li>
          </ul>

          <p>
           Arsenal fans will certainly be hoping a repeat of the last time the two sides met in a 12.45 Saturday kick off does not happen again, as Liverpool went 4-0 up within 20 minutes and finished the game as 5-1 winners.
          </p>
          <p>
           That definitely wont happen again, Liverpool will do well to pick up any points at the Emirates and at the moment, this seems unlikely.
