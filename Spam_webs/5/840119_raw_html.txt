         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          You Think Bugatti Veyron with 1.000 HP and his Super Sport and
          <a href="http://supercarscorner.com/bugatti-veyron-grand-sport-vitesse-rembrandt/" target="_blank" title="Exotic Bugatti light brown Cognac-Bugatti Veyron Grand Sport Vitesse Rembrandt-VIDEO">

         </p>

          <span id="more-1537">
          </span>
          If you do not believe, just ask the people of tuning house Steve Morris Engines, who is credited with creating the real monster between cars in the form of ordinary Ford Mustang Mach 1 from 70s. This beast, which at first glance does not seem so scary, hiding under the hood V8 engine with a volume of 9,400 cubic meters, two Bulls-eye turbo chargers and Chiseled Performance intercooler. From the results of any modifications to it, you will remain speechless: 3.040 hp at 7.300 rpm and 3.265 Nm maximum torque of 6.000 rpm.
         </p>
         <p>
          I agree that this is a car modified for race track and not for urban driving but in any case, does not diminish the impressiveness of its specifications.
