       <div class="post_content clearfix">
        <h1 class="title">
         7 Things You Must Know to Succeed as a Student That Your Lecturer Cant Teach You!
        </h1>
        <div class="wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertRight" style="float: right;  padding: 5px; margin:5px; float:left;
">
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- In article larger -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle middlead" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7126272587241902" data-ad-slot="3258684833" style="display:inline-block;width: 100%;height:100%; ">
         </ins>
         <script>
          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
        </div>
        <div id="media-pass-tease" style="display:none;">
         You go to university to learn. Everybody knows that. As a student, increasing your level 
        </div>
        <p>
         You go to university to learn. Everybody knows that.
         <strong>
          As a student, increasing your level of intellect week after week is a rewarding feeling.
         </strong>
         This due to the aptitude of your lecturers and the knowledge they impart to you. Be that as it may, there are numerous bits of invaluable know-how that arent in your curriculum. Here are 7 things you must know to succeed as a student.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <strong>
           7. Keep up to date with the industry
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_18638" style="width: 310px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Land.jpg">
          <img alt="LAN don't miss it! Image credit: lenetstanb / shutterstock, modification by Scott Renwick" class="size-full wp-image-18638 " height="216" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Land.jpg" title="lenetstanb / shutterstock, modification by Scott Renwick" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          LAN dont miss it!
          <span style="font-size: 10px;">
           Image credit: lenetstanb / shutterstock.com, modification by Scott Renwick
          </span>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Be aware of whats going on in the world of landscape architecture. Things like projects, news, trends, research, opinions, or topics of concern  the list goes on. Landscape architecture is an industry thats continually evolving and growing, with so many exciting facets to explore and be well informed about. Keep browsing websites like this!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <b>
           6. Start networking
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Be it fellow students, industry professionals, friends of friends, or even the guys in charge of the workshops, you should be making as many social acquaintances in the industry as humanly possible. You never know when you may need a helping hand at university, and getting to know people in the industry may just come in handy when you go searching for work experience. Universities often have organised meet and greets with local professionals, go to them!
        </p>
        <p>
         Learn all about networking with the pros in our feature article
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/10-easy-ways-landscape-architecture-students-network-professionals/">
          10 Easy Ways for Landscape Architecture Students to Network with Professionals
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <b>
           5. Real world office experience
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18639" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Land1.jpg">
          <img alt="Real life experience makes all the difference; image credit: Andy Dean Photography / shutterstock.com" class="size-full wp-image-18639" height="400" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Land1.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Real life experience makes all the difference;
          <span style="font-size: 10px;">
           image credit: Andy Dean Photography / shutterstock.com
          </span>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Your lecturers obviously possess an abundance of knowledge. Regardless, its important that at some stage throughout your studies you actually go and see how things are done in the real world. Firms are often happy to take you on; after all, it (usually) isnt paid. Youll get a feel for what its like to work in a professional environment, start the next semester with a supeme level of knowledge, and put yourself in a great position to be employed as a graduate.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <b>
           4. AutoCAD and Photoshop wont make you great designer
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_18640" style="width: 310px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Land2.jpg">
          <img alt="Do you agree? - Image credit: jovan vitanovski / shutterstock.com, midification by SDR" class="size-full wp-image-18640 " height="224" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Land2.jpg" title="Do you agree? - Image credit: jovan vitanovski / shutterstock.com, midification by SDR" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Do you agree? 
          <span style="font-size: 10px;">
           Image credit: jovan vitanovski / shutterstock.com, midification by SDR
          </span>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         A level of proficiency at AutoCAD and Photoshop is a great resource to have at your disposal, and something you should be aiming for as you progress through your studies. However, upon unearthing the power of these programs, students often implement them too early in the design process, quashing any creative sparks that may have been close to flying. This consociates directing with number 2 below. Keep drawing!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <b>
           3. Ask stupid questions
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle" style="margin: 5px; padding: 0px;">
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- Larger rectangle -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle middlead" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7126272587241902" data-ad-slot="4772444031" style="display:inline-block;width: 100%;height:100%;">
         </ins>
         <script>
          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
        </div>
        <p>
         Asking stupid questions may seem like stupid advice, but its not! Forget about how dumb you might feel  if you dont understand something, stick your hand up. 99% of the time the rest of your classmates will let out a subliminal sigh of relief as they are all wondering the same thing, but too embarrassed to ask. Chances are youre spending a great deal of money to be at university, so make sure you leave class each day with a new level of intelligence, not confusion.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <b>
           2. If you can think it, you can draw it
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         It may take some time to succumb to this notion, but its true. As designers, putting pen to paper and visualising ideas quickly and effectively is essential. If you werent adept at hand drawing when you started university, chances are that unless you keep practicing, nothing much will change. Just sketch stuff, it doesnt have to be pretty! Make the skill of representing ideas on paper second nature.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18644" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Land3.jpg">
          <img alt="Image credit:  Nick Tuttle / Sketchy Saturday entry" class="size-full wp-image-18644" height="424" src="http://landarchs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Land3.jpg" title="Image credit:  Nick Tuttle / Sketchy Saturday entry" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Image credit: Nick Tuttle / Sketchy Saturday entry
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          See more excellent examples of free-hand drawing in our
          <a href="http://landarchs.com/sketchy-saturday-top-10/">
           Sketchy Saturday Top 10
          </a>
          <b>
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Discover your own study/social life balance
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         All student landscape architects know how time consuming and intensive university can be. Despite the high workload, its important to maintain some form of social life while studying! I know, sometimes ditching all that drawing to go out with friends on a Saturday night simply isnt an option. But more often than not, the time you spend procrastinating in front of your desk is better spent just enjoying yourself. Just dont overdo it. Youll come back to your work feeling less stressed and with improved cognizance.
        </p>
        <p>
         Being a student can be great fun, but at times it can seem arduous.
         <strong>
          The aforementioned tips are designed to make your life just that bit easier throughout the semester.
         </strong>
         Admittedly, Im sure there are many more, these being the ones Ive found resonate strongly. I hope they help!
        </p>
