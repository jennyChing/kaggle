                       <div class="content">
                        <a class="active" href="/news/theresa-carter/national-robotics-week-around-chicago">
                         <img alt="National Robotics Week" height="525" src="//dbaxqy6diegw5.cloudfront.net/sites/default/files/styles/featured__600x450_/public/media/1/10177414_10152309120191273_7886405799903377510_n.png?itok=VJ9rgXER" typeof="foaf:Image" width="700"/>

                    <div class="field-items">
                     <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                      <p>
                       When the word robotics was coined in 1941 by Isaac Asimov robots were more the stuff of dreams than reality. Now they're a part of everyday life and becoming even more so. They're a physical manifestation of imagination, pushing boundaries, and turning fiction into fact. Celebrating the science and the art of all things robotics is
                       <a href="http://www.nationalroboticsweek.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                        <strong>
                         National Robotics Week
                        </strong>
                       </a>
                       .
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       In the Chicagoland area the week began on April 2 with the FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) Robotics Competition, an engineering challenge for high school students, at UIC Pavilion. It continues this week with a collection of events that are sure to get your imagination racing.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Bring the kids to the Barrington Library (505 N Northwest Highway, Barringgon, IL) on Wednesday for
                       <a href="http://chicago.thelocaltourist.com/events/lego-wedo-robots" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                        <strong>
                         LEGO WeDo Robots
                        </strong>
                       </a>
                       . This introductory class teaches kids how to build and program robots with LEGOs. While this would be fun for all, it's limited to children in grades 2 through 4.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       The
                       <a href="http://chicago.thelocaltourist.com/business/museum-science-and-industry" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                        <strong>
                         Museum of Science and Industry
                        </strong>
                       </a>
                       (5700 S Lake Shore Dr) is hosting Robot Block Party from Friday through Sunday. There will be 'bots designed by Chicago-area student and university teams and hobbyists all weekend and Saturday is the 2015 Greater Chicago Botball Tournament, an autonomous robotics competition.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Also on Saturday is the
                       <a href="http://chicago.thelocaltourist.com/events/junior-first-lego-league-expo" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                        <strong>
                         Junior FIRST LEGO League Expo
                        </strong>
                       </a>
                       . Teams will have their robots on display at The Raupp Museum in Buffalo Grove beginning at 10am.
                      </p>
