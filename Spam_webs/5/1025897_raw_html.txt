      <h1>
       Highs and Lows of Cinema 2014Part 1
      </h1>
     </figcaption>
     <img alt="" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/themes/fullscreen-2015/images/blank.gif"/>
    </figure>
    <div class="content-section-holder clear">
     <div id="content">
      <div class="entry entry_single">
       <img alt="bellboy and passengers in elevator, in The Grand Budapest Hotel" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64069" height="1200" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-151.jpg" width="1800"/>
       <div class="adv-single-top-leaderboard-box">
        <div class="adv-single-top-leaderboard">
         <div class="bsarocks bsap_b48753df2718669f8dec2f2fc8fc228a" id="bsap_1275142">
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        <span class="intro">
         As a film critic I watch lots of movies. Thats a pretty obvious statement, right? Of course, most of the year is taken up with new releases, but I spend a good amount of time watching older flicks and the ones that slipped through the net last year. I hate it when that happens!
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        So, given that were now past the mid-point of 2014, heres my thoughts on the cinematic landscape so far, and things that have impressed me greatly. The best part of it all? Weve got another six months to go with plenty more left to see and experience.
       </p>
       <h6>
        Top: The coloured coded fantasy of Wes Andersons The Grand Budapest Hotel.
       </h6>
       <img alt="villain in 300: rise of an empire" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64072" height="936" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-16.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Without a single doubt the worst film released so far in 2014.
       </h6>
       <h3>
        The Film I Really Hated:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        300: Rise of an Empire
       </h2>
       <p>
        Did we really need a sequel to 
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/300_(film)" target="_blank">
         300
        </a>
        ? You betcha! Actually, we didnt, but that hasnt stopped Hollywood before and it didnt stop them from making this hideous piece of fascist idiocy. 300: Rise of an Empire was not only repellent in its politics and portrayal of female characters, it was also guilty of being terribly dull.
       </p>
       <img alt="jack ryan chris pine keira knightley" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64043" height="1000" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-02.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Kenneth Branaghs spy thriller was a pretty lacklustre affair.
       </h6>
       <h3>
        The Tired Franchise Re-launch:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        Jack Ryan: Shadow Recruit
       </h2>
       <p>
        As I explained, Hollywood studios love their remakes, sequels and reboots. But who in the hell was excited for Jack Ryan: Shadow Recruit? Nobody. The film was headlined by charisma vacuum
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chris_Pine" target="_blank">
         Chris Pine
        </a>
        , and its plot was straight from a Cold War potboiler that should have been left back in the 1980s. Bored now.
       </p>
       <img alt="under the skin blue water black" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64044" height="844" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-03.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        A deconstruction of the movie star system dressed as an arty sci-fi drama.
       </h6>
       <h3>
        Overrated:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        Under the Skin
       </h2>
       <p>
        Im going to piss off a lot of folk with the following statement:
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonathan_Glazer" target="_blank">
         Jonathan Glazer
        </a>
        s Under the Skin is the most overrated movie of the year so far.
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scarlett_Johansson" target="_blank">
         Scarlett Johansson
        </a>
        stars as an alien roaming Glasgow, Scotland, abducting gullible men so she can eat them. It looks absolutely beautiful, but the self-consciously arty tone does not make it a profound movie experience.
       </p>
       <img alt="ralph fiennes grand budapest hotel" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64037" height="900" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-10.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Ralph Fiennes amuses greatly in Wes Andersons latest cinematic delight.
       </h6>
       <h3>
        Funniest Line of Dialogue:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        The Grand Budapest Hotel
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wes_Anderson" target="_blank">
         Wes Anderson
        </a>
        s movies are full of memorable dialogue and quirky characters.
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ralph_Fiennes" target="_blank">
         Ralph Fiennes
        </a>
        , however, as
        <a href="http://www.imdb.com/character/ch0416257/quotes" target="_blank">
         M. Gustave
        </a>
        , a flamboyant hotel concierge, is a career-high creation. The art-loving main man of The Grand Budapest Hotel is so slick that he has the ability to insult people without them even knowing it. You wouldnt know
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiaroscuro" target="_blank">
         chiaroscuro
        </a>
        from chicken giblets, is the funniest line Ive heard in the movies in a very long time.
       </p>
       <img alt="veronica mars movie camera" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64032" height="900" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-04.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        The power of Kickstarter returned the former teen detective to the big screen.
       </h6>
       <h3>
        Geek Heaven:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        Veronica Mars
       </h2>
       <p>
        
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veronica_Mars" target="_blank">
         Veronica Mars
        </a>
         was a cult television show watched by a handful of nerds who became obsessed with
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristen_Bell" target="_blank">
         Kristen Bell
        </a>
        s super cool teen detective. Of course the television networks cancelled it after three seasons, because theyre idiots, but creator
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rob_Thomas_(writer)" target="_blank">
         Rob Thomas
        </a>
        turned to Kickstarter and was able to give the fans and Veronica the goodbye that they deserved.
       </p>
       <img alt="cheap thrills bar red light" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64033" height="1000" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-06.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        E.L. Katzs horror-comedy explores the power of money and social desperation.
       </h6>
       <h3>
        The Important Second Viewing:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        Cheap Thrills
       </h2>
       <p>
        Sometimes you can watch a movie and its no great shakes. Its always been my policy to give films another chance; a second shot to make an impression (even the ones I hate). E.L. Katzs Cheap Thrills felt like an inferior
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Haneke" target="_blank">
         Michael Haneke
        </a>
        clone/wannabe lacking the great auteurs eye for detail, and just focused on the nastiness. Well, folks, I was dead wrong.
       </p>
       <img alt="little fish cate blanchett" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64034" height="998" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-07.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Little Fish is a beautifully directed, written and performed film.
       </h6>
       <h3>
        The Discovery:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        Little Fish
       </h2>
       <p>
        Everybody watches older movies and wonders why they never bothered with it before, right? Made in 2005
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rowan_Woods" target="_blank">
         Rowan Woods
        </a>
         crime drama, Little Fish, has the best
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cate_Blanchett" target="_blank">
         Cate Blanchett
        </a>
        performance youve quite possibly never seen before. As a recovering heroin addict struggling to get a new business off the ground, the Oscar-winning actress is an absolute sensation.
       </p>
       <img alt="mister john thailand lake aiden gillen" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64035" height="1000" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-08.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Why isnt Aidan Gillen a superstar actor?
       </h6>
       <h3>
        The One I Should Have Loved Last Year:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        Mister John
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aidan_Gillen" target="_blank">
         Aidan Gillen
        </a>
        is one of those actors who has never quite hit the big time in the way I think he deserves. He is phenomenal, but something just hasnt quite worked out. Mister John is the story of a man travelling to the Far East for his brothers funeral. How on earth did this one pass me by? Its so good.
       </p>
       <img alt="the canyons lindsay lohan" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64036" height="844" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-09.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Contrary to popular opinion, The Canyons is a fine neo-noir melodrama.
       </h6>
       <h3>
        The One I Loved but Everybody Else Hated:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        The Canyons
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Schrader" target="_blank">
         Paul Schrader
        </a>
        s neo-noir melodrama, The Canyons, was slated by the critics and played in just one NYC movie house, for like, a whole week. US festivals didnt want it, either. Common opinion is that The Canyons is dreadful. But guess what? It isnt! A rich and rewarding work, if you can stop being a snob for five minutes, give Schraders movie a proper chance. Do it!
       </p>
       <img alt="river of fundament matthew barney detroit" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-64040" height="992" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/review-cinema-13.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Matthew Barneys cine-opera runs to six hours and features some truly extraordinary imagery.
       </h6>
       <h3>
        Event of the Year:
       </h3>
       <h2>
        River of Fundament
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matthew_Barney" target="_blank">
         Matthew Barney
        </a>
        is famous for two things: dating
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bjrk" target="_blank">
         Bjrk
        </a>
        and making epic art movies. River of Fundament is a six-hours-long fantasy saga exploring themes of death and resurrection. Based loosely on the novel 
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Evenings" target="_blank">
         Ancient Evenings
        </a>
        , by author Norman Mailer, its unlike anything Ive ever seen in my life. It was a truly incredible night at the movies.
       </p>
