      <div class="byline">
       The Santa Cruz Wharf may be 100 this year, but the landmark just 73 miles south of San Francisco is still packed with plenty of entertainmentand is more than just a great place to view the sprawling Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk.
      </div>
      <div class="chapter-body">
       <p>
        The Municipal Wharf was constructed in 1914, of more than 2,000 70-foot Douglas fir pilings driven 21 feet into the floor of
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/ca/monterey">
         Monterey
        </a>
        Bay and is the longest pier on the West Coast of the United States (half a mile). Heres how to get the best of the grand dame on a wharf visit:
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h3>
        Greet the Morning at Gilda's
       </h3>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/california/restaurants-santa-cruz/gildas-family-restaurant-12000940?token=1U1AgIAAAAOjp75CZAAAAAAEFcDwAMngMAo4">
         Gildas
        </a>
        has been providing the quintessential fishing-wharf breakfast experience since the 1940s. Choose from classic egg dishes, fish, hotcakes or waffles, and pick a window seat so you can enjoy the view of fishermen making their way out for the day, surfers taking turns at Steamer Lane and wildlife hunting for food. Breakfast ranges from $6.75 to $13.25.
       </p>
       <div class="extra green">
        <h2>
         <span class="icon-Icon-Extras">
         </span>
         INSIDER TIP
        </h2>
        Arrive before 10 a.m. on a weekend and plan to leave after a leisurely dinner (work in a walk up West Cliff Drive to the
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/california/business-santa-cruz/santa-cruz-surfing-museum-265663139" target="_self">
         Santa Cruz Surfing Museum
        </a>
        and the waves of Steamer Lane or down Beach Street to the Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalkboth enjoyable ways to extend your day), or come on a weekday and put the traffic worries behind you.
       </div>
       <h3>
        Go for a Paddle
       </h3>
       <p>
        You can walk on the wharf and check it out from land, but expand your horizons with a water-bound visit when you rent a kayak or stand-up paddleboard from
        <a href="http://www.kayaksantacruz.com/" target="_blank">
         Venture Quest Kayaking
        </a>
        . While youre out, explore kelp beds and catch views of otters and harbor seals. Rates start at $30 for a 3-hour rental.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h3>
        Snack with Sea Lions
       </h3>
       <p>
        No, you cant feed them, but you can swing by
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/california/business-santa-cruz/marinis-candies-303168564" target="_self">
         Marinis Candies
        </a>
        for saltwater taffy or homemade fudge to satisfy your own sweet tooth. Then, walk toward the end of the wharf and peek through cutaway areas at the home of a large colony of California sea lions.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h3>
        Learn to Surf
       </h3>
       <p>
        Leaving the waves at Steamer Lane to the experienced surfers doesnt mean you have to stay out of the water. Get a 2-hour lesson ($90) from
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/california/business-santa-cruz/cowells-beach-n-bikini-surf-shop-11999497" target="_self">
         Cowells Surf Shop
        </a>
        , right within view of the wharf. Even if you just towel-dry afterward, dinner on the wharf is casual.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h3>
        Bask in the Sunset
       </h3>
       <p>
        End your visit with more breathtaking views on the beaches of the
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/ca/santa-cruz">
         Santa Cruz
        </a>
        coastline at
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/california/business-santa-cruz/las-olitas-cantina-grill-304062035" target="_self">
         Olitas Cantina &amp; Grille
        </a>
        . You know you deserve the fresh lime LouieRita with Herradura silver tequila, as well as any of a wealth of tasty daily specialsfrom light seafood to hearty meat dishes.
       </p>
