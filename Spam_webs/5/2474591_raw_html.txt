     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Phoebus cartel
      </b>
      was a
      <a href="/wiki/Cartel" title="Cartel">
       cartel
      </a>
      of, among others,
      <a href="/wiki/Osram" title="Osram">
       Osram
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Philips" title="Philips">
       Philips
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">
       General Electric
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      from December 23, 1924 until 1939 that existed to control the manufacture and sale of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_bulb" title="Light bulb">
       light bulbs
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The cartel is an important step in the
      <a href="/wiki/Economic_history_of_the_world" title="Economic history of the world">
       history of the global economy
      </a>
      because it engaged in large-scale
      <a href="/wiki/Planned_obsolescence" title="Planned obsolescence">
       planned obsolescence
      </a>
      . It reduced competition in the light bulb industry for almost twenty years, and has been accused of preventing technological advances that would have produced longer-lasting light bulbs. Phoebus was a Swiss corporation named "Phoebus S.A. Compagnie Industrielle pour le Dveloppement de l'clairage".
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Members">
       Members
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Phoebus_cartel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Members">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Osram" title="Osram">
       Osram
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Philips" title="Philips">
       Philips
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tungsram" title="Tungsram">
       Tungsram
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Associated_Electrical_Industries" title="Associated Electrical Industries">
       Associated Electrical Industries
      </a>
      , ELIN, Compagnie des Lampes, International
      <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">
       General Electric
      </a>
      , and the GE Overseas Group were members of the Phoebus cartel,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_23Jul1945_PhoebusCartel_p1_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Time_23Jul1945_PhoebusCartel_p1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      holding shares in the Swiss corporation proportional to their lamp sales.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1921 a precursor organisation was founded by Osram, the "Internationale Glhlampen Preisvereinigung". When Philips and other manufacturers were entering the American market, General Electric reacted by setting up the "International General Electric Company" in Paris. Both organisations co-ordinated the trading of patents and
      <a href="/wiki/Market_penetration" title="Market penetration">
       market penetration
      </a>
      . Increasing international competition led to negotiations between all the major companies to control and restrict their respective activities in order not to interfere in each other's spheres.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">
       Purpose
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Phoebus_cartel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Purpose">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The cartel was a convenient way to lower costs and worked to standardise the life expectancy of light bulbs at 1000 hours, while at the same time raising prices without fear of competition. Members' bulbs were regularly tested and fines were levied for bulbs that lasted more than 1000 hours. A 1929 table lists exactly how many
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swiss_francs" title="Swiss francs">
       Swiss francs
      </a>
      had to be paid, depending on the exceeding hours of lifetime.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This was not public knowledge at the time, and the cartel could point to standardization of light bulbs as an alternative rationale for the organization.
     </p>
     <p>
      The cartel claimed that 1000 hours was a reasonable optimum life expectancy for most bulbs, and that a longer lifetime could be obtained only at the expense of efficiency, since progressively more heat and less light is obtained, resulting in wasted electricity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Phoebus Cartel divided the worlds lamp markets into three categories:
     </p>
     <ol>
      <li>
       home territories, the home country of individual manufacturers
      </li>
      <li>
       British overseas territories, under control of
       <a href="/wiki/Associated_Electrical_Industries" title="Associated Electrical Industries">
        Associated Electrical Industries
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Osram" title="Osram">
        Osram
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Philips" title="Philips">
        Philips
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Tungsram" title="Tungsram">
        Tungsram
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       common territory, the rest of the world
      </li>
     </ol>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Demise">
       Demise
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Phoebus_cartel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demise">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In the late 1920s a Swedish-Danish-Norwegian union of companies (the North European Luma Co-op Society) began planning an independent manufacturing centre. Economic and legal threats by Phoebus did not achieve the desired effect, and in 1931 the Scandinavians produced and sold lamps at a considerably lower price than Phoebus.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The original Phoebus agreement was intended to expire in 1955;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      however,
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      greatly disrupted the operation of the cartel.
     </p>
