        <p class="centerImage">
         <strong class="heading">
          Do-It-Yourself Tips
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          <img height="114" src="/media/Images/k/karl2.gif" width="100"/>
          <br/>
         </strong>
         we're tropical
         <br/>
         <strong>
          <strong>
           How To Create A
           <br/>
           Very Special Landscape
          </strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <span class="redText">
          "Your exceptional landscape needs
          <br/>
          foundation plantings. Think first in terms of
          <br/>
          high
          <u>
           quality trees and palms
          </u>
          "
          <br/>
         </span>
        </p>
       </td>
      </tr>
     </tbody>
    </table>
    <table border="0">
     <tbody>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <p>
         Have you ever looked at a home and said...
        </p>
        <p>
         "what a delightful landscape. I just love all those wonderful
         <span class="redText">
          shrubs
         </span>
         "
        </p>
        <p>
         Of course not
        </p>
        <p>
         A quality sub-tropical landscape is based on quality
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/trees.aspx" title="Beautiful trees in Florida">
          trees
         </a>
         and
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/popularpalms.aspx" title="Polular palms in Florida">
          palms
         </a>
         as the "foundation"                     of the
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/landscapes.aspx" title="Tropical landscapes in Florida">
          landscape
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         In Florida, first you consider
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/natives.aspx" title="Beautiful trees in Florida">
          native trees
         </a>
         such                     as
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/liveoak.aspx" title="Florida Live Oak tree, article">
          Live Oak
         </a>
         or Mahogany. Next you select
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/popularpalms.aspx" title="Popular palms in Florida">
          quality palms
         </a>
         . If you can, include flowering                     trees and/or
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/fruit.aspx" title="Fruit trees, fruit shrubs, fruit vines in Florida">
          fruit trees
         </a>
         . Keep seasonal flowering                     and fruiting times in mind. Using significant, valuable, attractive                     foundation plantings is the first step
        </p>
        <p>
         Make it a point to plant at least two or three
         <u>
          important
         </u>
         trees or palms as part of your mix. What's important? We stress trees and palms which not only                     grow but grow more valuable over time
        </p>
        <p>
         For example, a
         <span class="redText">
          Pritchardia pacifica
         </span>
         retails for about $50 per foot. If you plant                     a young four-footer for $200 you'll enjoy seeing it grow into a mature specimen                     palm worth perhaps $4,000 in less than 10 years or about
         <span class="redText">
          20X your $$$ investment
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Planting a fine palm creates a special unique
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/landscapes.aspx" title="Tropical landscapes in Florida">
          landscape
         </a>
         while year after year adding significant value to your property. Common plants                     are...common / ordinary /cheap
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have certain
         <u>
          garbage trees or palms
         </u>
         in your landscape, have them                     all removed before you begin a new landscape plan
        </p>
        <p>
         For example, always extract black olives,
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/benjamina.aspx">
          ficus
         </a>
         and all
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/schefflera.aspx">
          Shefflera
         </a>
         trees. These are on the                     State of Florida's "don't plant" list anyhow. If you have these                     plantings and they're not a problem, rest assured they will be a (bigger) problem                     and more expensive in the future. Just get rid of them all now
        </p>
        <p>
         Most home owners sweat the small stuff - like
         <a class="redText" href="/articles/shrubs.aspx">
          shrubs
         </a>
         and
         <a class="redText" href="/articles/hedges.aspx">
          hedges
         </a>
         and flowers. These plantings are unimportant. Shrubs and flowers are
         <u>
          cheap and easily replaced
         </u>
         . Unlike trees and palms, growth over time rarely enhances their value significantly. You should plant the "small stuff" last because it is the least important
        </p>
        <p>
         Also, not all landscaping should be plants ...."objects" in your yard                     such as swings, benches, large terra cotta pots and statues
         <a class="greenText" href="/media/Images/s/statue01.jpg">
         </a>
         (to name a few) look great integrated
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span class="redText">
           TIPS:
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="redText">
          (1)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Provide the full space required for a mature tree or palm. Don't jam plantings                     so close they will interfere with each other as they grow
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="redText">
          (2)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Provide food. The State of Florida (U. of Florida) now recommends a minimum of 6X                     a year
         <a href="/articles/fertilize.aspx" title="Fertilization and soil, article">
          fertilization
         </a>
         for palms for example.                     You don't use more fertilizer. You use the same amount over the year so you                     feed less each time, but more often
        </p>
        <p>
         Use ONLY the best fertilizers (the most expensive) which include minor elements. Never buy cheap fertilizer
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="redText">
          (3)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Every tree and every palm should be
         <u>
          planted in a planned bed
         </u>
         , not in the grass
        </p>
        <p>
         Our typical St. Augustine grasses creep on runners and soon invade the base of trees                     and palms.
         <a href="/articles/lawnman.aspx" title="Manage and control your lawnman, article">
          Lawnmen
         </a>
         use weed whackers and slash                     the base of trees and palms 2X a month when they cut your grass. In no time they                     have severed the bark which can let in fungus and disease. Weed whacking will eventually                     kill your trees and palms. The only way to protect them is the create a bed, then                     maintain the bed perimeter
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="redText">
          (4)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Don't use
         <a href="/articles/pests.aspx" title="Plant pests, article">
          pesticides
         </a>
         . Almost all attacks                     are temporary and no action is required. Before you use any pesticide, first get                     authoritative professional analysis and advice
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="redText">
          (5)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Look up before you plant. Wires are everywhere and both FPL and Bell South are ruthless                     about hacking trees out of the way of their wires. Select trees and palms that will                     NEVER interfere with their wires
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="redText">
          (6)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="greenText" href="/articles/notinstall.aspx" title="Proper planting, article">
          Plant
         </a>
         everything with (real) manure as a                     soil enhancement. We like Black Cow best and using manure in the planting hole is                     a Master Gardener secret to success. Go ahead and use a whole 50 lb. bag for each                     important tree and palm
        </p>
        <p class="centerText">
         <strong>
          <span class="redText">
           SUMMARY
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Extract all garbage planting before you do anything else
         </li>
         <li>
          Select important foundation tree and palm plantings first
         </li>
         <li>
          Use a minimum of 2-3 unique trees or palms
         </li>
         <li>
          Allow room for each mature specimen's size
         </li>
         <li>
          Feed properly and more feed often
         </li>
         <li>
          Plant everything in beds
          <br/>
         </li>
        </ol>
       </td>
