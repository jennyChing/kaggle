                      <h1 class="times-thin" id="art_headline" style="line-height:48px; font-family:'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight:normal;">
                       Whose Plan Destroys Medicare  Obamas or Romney-Ryans?
                      </h1>
                      <div class="main_image">

                        <p>
                         Stumping in Florida today, Mitt Romney charged President Obamas Affordable Care Act will cut more than $700 billion out of Medicare.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         What Romney didnt say was that his running-mates budget  approved by House Republicans and by Romney himself  would cut Medicare by the same amount.The big difference, though, is the Affordable Care Act achieves these savings by reducing Medicare payments to drug companies, hospitals, and other providers rather than cutting payments to Medicare beneficiaries.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         The Romney-Ryan plan, by contrast, achieves its savings by turning Medicare into a voucher whose value doesnt keep up with expected increases in healthcare costs  thereby shifting the burden onto Medicare beneficiaries, who will have to pay an average of $6,500 a year more for their Medicare insurance, according an analysis of the Republican plan by the non-partisan
                         <a href="http://cbo.gov/sites/default/files/cbofiles/ftpdocs/120xxdoc12033/12-23-selectedhealthcarepublications.pdf">
                          Congressional Budget Office.
                         </a>
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         Moreover, the Affordable Care Act uses its Medicare savings to help children and lower-income Americans afford health care, and to help seniors pay for prescription drugs by filling the so-called donut hole in Medicare Part D coverage.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         The Romney-Ryan plan uses the savings to finance even bigger tax cuts for the very wealthy.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         Spread the word. Dont allow the GOP to get away with this demagoguery.
                        </p>
