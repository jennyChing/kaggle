        <p>
         <em>
          *Updated April 23, 2015
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im all about making my own condiments.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im
         <i>
          not
         </i>
         all about making my own condiments all the time. I just dont have time for that.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sometimes, its just nice to have something with a little more personality! There are tons of great ketchups and barbecue sauces out there, but sometimes I like to spice things up a bit.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Pineapple Brown Sugar Barbecue Sauce: so easy! The perfect sweet and tangy barbecue sauce that's great on chicken, pork, or beef. www.thereciperebel.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2468" height="915" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Pineapple-Brown-Sugar-Barbecue-Sauce-www.thereciperebel.com-1-610x915.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Often Ill make my own sauce when I make pulled pork in the slow cooker, because I have pretty much zero other prep. The same goes with burgers. They are so simple, and I rarely even make a side dish, so its nice to be able to spice things up with homemade barbecue sauce.
        </p>
        <p>
         I feel like this is kind of a new obsession of mine. I think it has a lot to do with how easy it is.
        </p>
        <p>
         Meat and sauce. Maybe a jar of pickles. Some iced tea. The perfect summer meal!
        </p>
        <p>
         So this time I wanted to try something with pineapple. I am also a big fan of sweet on meat. That kind of has a ring to it, doesnt it?
        </p>
        <p>
         I love all things sweet and sour.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Pineapple Brown Sugar Barbecue Sauce: so easy! The perfect sweet and tangy barbecue sauce that's great on chicken, pork, or beef. www.thereciperebel.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2470" height="915" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Pineapple-Brown-Sugar-Barbecue-Sauce-www.thereciperebel.com-2-610x915.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Maple, apple, pineapple, cranberry, brown sugar, tomato, apricot, honey, saskatoons  I love it all in my dinner.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im actually a little embarrassed about my sugar consumption at this point. I feel like a normal person should be able to make dinner without adding fruit or sugar or fruit and sugar.
        </p>
        <p>
         Apparently, I am not.
        </p>
        <p>
         But this sauce.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Pineapple Brown Sugar Barbecue Sauce: so easy! The perfect sweet and tangy barbecue sauce that's great on chicken, pork, or beef. www.thereciperebel.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2471" height="915" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Pineapple-Brown-Sugar-Barbecue-Sauce-www.thereciperebel.com-3-610x915.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         So I added a bunch of random stuff to a pot and simmered it with my pineapple juice. It is
         <i>
          so
         </i>
         good. Its sweet and tangy with just a hint of spice. It pairs perfectly with pulled pork, but it would also be fantastic on chicken or beef!
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Pineapple Brown Sugar BBQ Sauce
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Pineapple-Brown-Sugar-Barbecue-Sauce-www.thereciperebel.com-1-610x915.jpg" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://www.thereciperebel.com/easyrecipe-print/668-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print

          </div>

         <div class="ERSTimes">
          <div class="ERSTime ERSTimeRight">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Cook time

           <div class="ERSTimeItem">

             20 mins

          <div class="ERSTime ERSTimeRight">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Total time

           <div class="ERSTimeItem">

             20 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClearLeft">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          Sweet and sour barbecue sauce with a bit of spice! Delicious on any kind of meat.
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            200ml pineapple juice (1 regular-sized juice box)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup brown sugar, packed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp garlic
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup ketchup
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 pinch red pepper flakes (or more if you like it spicy!)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp onion powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 pinch salt (to taste)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1-2 tbsp corn starch
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1-2 tbsp water
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add all ingredients except for corn starch and water to a medium pot.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bring to a boil and reduce to medium-low, simmering for 10-15 minutes until flavours come together.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Mix equal parts corn starch and water. Slowly add to the sauce and stir, until you reach your desired consistency.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Serve with pork, chicken or beef.
           </li>
          </ol>
