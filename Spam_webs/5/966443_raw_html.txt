          <h1>
           Flip Completely With Olympus E-620
          </h1>
          <p>
           Posted by
           <a href="http://gearcrave.com/author/jared-newman/" rel="author" title="Posts by Jared Newman">
            Jared Newman
           </a>
          </p>
         </header>
         <div class="post-content">
          <p>
           <img alt="e-620__lc_round_print1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13101" height="295" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/e-620__lc_round_print1.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The E-620 from Olympus is a bit above the norm for an entry-level DSLR. Fancy features like the 2.7-inch full flip LCD screen, four selectable aspect ratios, hardware image stabilization and wireless flash capability are paired with some nice basic specs. The sensor is a 12.3-megapixel Live MOS and the lens has a zoom range of 14 mm  42 mm at f3.5/5.6. Another funky perk is the inclusion of Art Filters, which directly apply effects including Pop Art, Grainy Film and Pin Hole. Of course, your newbie-friendly Face Detection and Shadow Adjustment features are included as well. The E-620 will sell for $800, or $700 for the body only, when it hits stores in May. [
           <a href="http://www.dpreview.com/news/0902/09022401olympuse620.asp">
            DPReview
           </a>
           via
           <a href="http://gadgets.boingboing.net/2009/02/24/olympus-e620-flips-o.html">
            BB Gadgets
           </a>
           ]
          </p>
