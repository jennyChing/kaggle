     <p>
      The
      <b>
       International Solidarity Movement
      </b>
      (
      <b>
       ISM
      </b>
      ) is an organization focused on assisting the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palestinian_cause" title="Palestinian cause">
       Palestinian cause
      </a>
      in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Israeli-Palestinian_conflict" title="Israeli-Palestinian conflict">
       Israeli-Palestinian conflict
      </a>
      . ISM is dedicated to the use of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nonviolent" title="Nonviolent">
       nonviolent
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Protest" title="Protest">
       protests
      </a>
      and methods only. It was founded in 2001 by
      <a href="/wiki/Ghassan_Andoni" title="Ghassan Andoni">
       Ghassan Andoni
      </a>
      , a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palestinian_people" title="Palestinian people">
       Palestinian
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Activist" title="Activist">
       activist
      </a>
      ; Neta Golan, a third generation Israeli activist;
      <a href="/wiki/Huwaida_Arraf" title="Huwaida Arraf">
       Huwaida Arraf
      </a>
      , a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palestinian-American" title="Palestinian-American">
       Palestinian-American
      </a>
      ; and George N. Rishmawi, a Palestinian activist.
      <a href="/wiki/Adam_Shapiro_(activist)" title="Adam Shapiro (activist)">
       Adam Shapiro
      </a>
      , an American, joined the movement shortly after its founding and is also often considered one of the founders.
     </p>
     <p>
      The organization calls on civilians from around the world to participate in acts of nonviolent protests against the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Israeli_military" title="Israeli military">
       Israeli military
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/West_Bank" title="West Bank">
       West Bank
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Gaza_Strip" title="Gaza Strip">
       Gaza Strip
      </a>
      .

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Funding">
       Funding
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Solidarity_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Funding">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      According to the ISM's website, "International volunteers who join the ISM are responsible for paying their own way and covering all their expenses in Palestine. The ISM does not receive any funding from any state, government or association. We rely on donations from average people all over the world that support peace and the Palestinian struggle for freedom."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The ISM regularly sends speakers on fund-raising trips and encourages funding drives.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Philosophy">
       Philosophy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Solidarity_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Philosophy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The organization calls on civilians from around the world to participate in acts of
      <a href="/wiki/Nonviolence" title="Nonviolence">
       non-violent
      </a>
      protests against the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Israeli_military" title="Israeli military">
       Israeli military
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/West_Bank" title="West Bank">
       West Bank
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Gaza_Strip" title="Gaza Strip">
       Gaza Strip
      </a>
      . It has been criticised for working alongside other groups to pressure Palestinian artists to boycott the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=One_Voice_Peace_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="One Voice Peace Summit (page does not exist)">
       One Voice Peace Summit
      </a>
      and for helping to undermine the Summit by creating a competing event. ISM and affiliated groups critical of the Summit say that One Voice fails to fully support Palestinian rights guaranteed under
      <a href="/wiki/International_law" title="International law">
       international law
      </a>
      .

       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="ISM.27s_position_on_violence">
       ISM's position on violence
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Solidarity_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: ISM's position on violence">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The ISM's website describes the organization as a "Palestinian-led movement committed to resisting the Israeli occupation of Palestinian land using nonviolent, direct-action methods and principles". It emphasizes international volunteers are not there to "teach nonviolent resistance" but to support resistance through nonviolent direct action, emergency mobilization and documentation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      An article in the
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_newspapers_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of newspapers in the United Kingdom">
       British newspaper
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">
        The Daily Telegraph
       </a>
      </i>
      called ISM "the 'peace' group that embraces violence" because its mission statement recognises "armed struggle" as the "right" of Palestinians.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The statement in question is taken from the ISM Mission Statement:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       As enshrined in international law and UN resolutions , we recognize the Palestinian right to resist Israeli violence and occupation via legitimate armed struggle. However, we believe that nonviolence can be a powerful weapon in fighting oppression and we are committed to the principles of nonviolent resistance.
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      ISM further explains on its website:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       The ISM does not support or condone any acts of terrorism  which is not legitimate armed struggle. The ISM does not associate, support, or have anything to do with armed or violent resistance to the occupation. The ISM does not assist or engage in any kind of armed resistance, no matter what form it may take.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       This right to resist occupation applies not only to the Palestinian people, but to all peoples who are faced with a military occupation. The ISM regards all people as equals with equal rights under international law. We believe that nonviolent action is a powerful weapon in fighting oppression and are committed to the principles of nonviolent resistance.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         5
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      During a
      <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">
       CNN
      </a>
      interview,
      <a href="/wiki/Paula_Zahn" title="Paula Zahn">
       Paula Zahn
      </a>
      with Adam Shapiro and Huwaida Arraf asked about an article they had co-authored which stated: "Palestinian resistance must take on a variety of characteristics, both violent and nonviolent. But most importantly, it must develop a strategy involving both aspects. Nonviolent resistance is no less noble than carrying out a suicide operation." She noted that "some people could lead to the conclusion that you were promoting suicide bombing." Shapiro and Arraf replied:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       The article that we wrote was actually in response to another article written by a Palestinian, who said the Palestinians could not be nonviolent. And so we were addressing within the context of the debate over whether the Palestinians could use violence or could not use nonviolence or could use nonviolence. So it was, first of all, within that context...
       <br/>
      </p>
      <br/>
      There already is violence. Were not advocating it. Its already there. Its on the ground. Were working with people and with Palestinians who want to promote nonviolence, and that was the context of the whole article.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </blockquote>

     <p>
      Past ISM campaigns have used the following tactics:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Acting to deter military operations. Some ISM volunteers object to the use of the term
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Human_shield" title="Human shield">
         human shield
        </a>
       </i>
       to describe their work because, they argue, in a Palestinian context the expression more usually refers to forced use of captive Palestinians by the
       <a href="/wiki/Israel_Defense_Forces" title="Israel Defense Forces">
        Israel Defense Forces
       </a>
       (IDF) when searching Palestinian neighborhoods. They argue that the IDF is far more likely to shoot the darker skinned Palestinian civilians than white western looking activists, given the difference in international response. This tactic is colloquially referred to as the "white-face defense".
      </li>
      <li>
       Accompanying Palestinians to minimize harassment perpetuated by
       <a href="/wiki/Israeli_settlement" title="Israeli settlement">
        Israeli settlers
       </a>
       or soldiers, for example ensuring that queues at Israeli checkpoints are processed efficiently and providing witnesses and intermediaries during annual olive harvests, which are often disrupted by settlers and police.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       Removing roadblocks. These are large unmanned mounds of earth and concrete on roads throughout the
       <a href="/wiki/West_Bank" title="West Bank">
        West Bank
       </a>
       , and sometimes placed at the entrances of Palestinian villages by the IDF, thereby isolating those villages' inhabitants by preventing traffic in or out.
      </li>
      <li>
       Attempting to block military vehicles such as
       <a href="/wiki/Tank" title="Tank">
        tanks
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Bulldozer" title="Bulldozer">
        bulldozers
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       Violating Israeli
       <a href="/wiki/Curfew" title="Curfew">
        curfew
       </a>
       orders enforced on Palestinian areas in order to monitor Israeli military actions, deliver food and medicine to Palestinian homes, or escort medical personnel to help facilitate their work.
      </li>
      <li>
       Interfering with the construction of the
       <a href="/wiki/Israeli_West_Bank_barrier" title="Israeli West Bank barrier">
        West Bank barrier
       </a>
       and placing political graffiti on the wall.
      </li>
      <li>
       Entering areas designated as "closed military zones" by the Israeli military. This is not really a 'strategy' as such, but is a prerequisite for ISM being able to conduct many of the above activities as areas in which the ISM is active are often summarily designated as "closed military zones" by the IDF.
      </li>
      <li>
       Attempting to break the Israeli blockade of the Gaza Strip by supporting and participating in initiatives to send vessels through the naval blockade to Gaza.
      </li>
     </ul>

      . The Israeli government claimed that Sukiya was a senior
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palestinian_Islamic_Jihad" title="Palestinian Islamic Jihad">
       Islamic Jihad
      </a>
      member, and that he was aided by two ISM activists.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78">
       <a href="#cite_note-78">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        78
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79">
       <a href="#cite_note-79">
        <span>

        </span>
        79
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Both parties stated that Sukiya arrived at the ISM's office as he was being pursued through the streets of Jenin by IDF soldiers during an Israeli-imposed curfew. According to the ISM's account, he had been going door to door looking for a place to go, arrived at the building (which is also used by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">
       Red Cross
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9decins_Sans_Fronti%C3%A8res" title="Mdecins Sans Frontires">
       Medecins Sans Frontieres
      </a>
      ) cold and wet, and was offered a chance to dry and warm up by an ISM volunteer.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Israel_Defense_Forces" title="Israel Defense Forces">
       IDF
      </a>
      originally suggested that two
      <a href="/wiki/AK-47" title="AK-47">
       Kalashnikov
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Assault_rifle" title="Assault rifle">
       assault rifles
      </a>
      and a handgun were found on the premises, but subsequently backtracked on the allegation.
     </p>
     <p>
      In May 2003,
      <a href="/wiki/Adam_Shapiro_(activist)" title="Adam Shapiro (activist)">
       Adam Shapiro
      </a>
      from ISM stated that Sukiya was not named a "senior Islamic Jihad terrorist" by any official Israeli military or government source, and was being held in administrative detention in Israel without any charge.
