        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Tata Motors plans to open standalone outlets for Nano
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2011-04-23T20:30:05+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          April 23, 2011
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://indianfusion.aglasem.com/author/angana23/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Angana
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://indianfusion.aglasem.com/6517/tata-motors-plans-open-standalone-outlets-nano/#respond">
           <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="6517 http://indianfusion.aglasem.com/?p=6517">
            Leave a Comment
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         We are exploring appointing full-fledged dealerships, only for the Tata Nano, in small towns, a Tata Motors spokesperson told PTI earlier this week. Taking a leaf out of its runaway success mini truck Ace which is also sold through 600 standalone stores along with normal dealerships across the country, Tata Motors now plans to open exclusive showrooms for its small car Nano in smaller towns. Any further details as to when the first exclusive dealer will be appointed and how many such stores will be opened in total across the country have not yet been divulged by Tata Motors.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The general approach of taking a vehicle closer to where the customer is, was a learning from the Tata Ace, which too, since its launch has been made available through dedicated outlets, in addition to our regular commercial vehicle showrooms, the official said.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         It would be interesting to see if the runaway success of mini truck Ace is repeated once again with Tata Nano.
        </p>
