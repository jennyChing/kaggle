     <p>
      <b>
       DeVotchKa
      </b>
      is a four-piece multi-instrumental and vocal ensemble. They take their name from the
      <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
       Russian
      </a>
      word
      <i>
       devochka
      </i>
      (), meaning "girl". Based in
      <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">
       Denver
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">
       Colorado
      </a>
      , the quartet is made up of Nick Urata, who sings and plays
      <a href="/wiki/Theremin" title="Theremin">
       theremin
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">
       guitar
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Bouzouki" title="Bouzouki">
       bouzouki
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">
       piano
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Trumpet" title="Trumpet">
       trumpet
      </a>
      ; Tom Hagerman, who plays
      <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">
       violin
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Accordion" title="Accordion">
       accordion
      </a>
      , and piano; Jeanie Schroder, who sings and plays
      <a href="/wiki/Sousaphone" title="Sousaphone">
       sousaphone
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Double_bass" title="Double bass">
       double bass
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Flute" title="Flute">
       flute
      </a>
      ; and Shawn King, who plays
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Percussion" title="Percussion">
       percussion
      </a>
      and trumpet.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=DeVotchKa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Originally a backing band for
      <a href="/wiki/American_burlesque" title="American burlesque">
       burlesque
      </a>
      shows, in their early years DeVotchKa also toured with burlesque performer and model
      <a href="/wiki/Dita_Von_Teese" title="Dita Von Teese">
       Dita Von Teese
      </a>
      . Numerous nationwide tours in support of self-released records earned the band an underground following. Their song "How It Ends" introduced the band to a wider audience after being featured in the trailer for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Everything_Is_Illuminated_(film)" title="Everything Is Illuminated (film)">
        Everything Is Illuminated
       </a>
      </i>
      , in a
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Gears_of_War_2" title="Gears of War 2">
        Gears of War 2
       </a>
      </i>
      trailer called "The Last Day", and in an episode of
      <a href="/wiki/Everwood" title="Everwood">
       Everwood
      </a>
      (Season 4). Their performance at the 2006
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bonnaroo" title="Bonnaroo">
       Bonnaroo
      </a>
      music festival was considered a breakout event.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      In between tours, the band was picked by first time film directors
      <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Dayton_and_Valerie_Faris" title="Jonathan Dayton and Valerie Faris">
       Jonathan Dayton and Valerie Faris
      </a>
      to score
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Little_Miss_Sunshine" title="Little Miss Sunshine">
        Little Miss Sunshine
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      a
      <a href="/wiki/2006_in_film" title="2006 in film">
       2006
      </a>
      film that would go on to garner four
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">
       Academy Award
      </a>
      nominations. DeVotchKa, along with composer
      <a href="/wiki/Mychael_Danna" title="Mychael Danna">
       Mychael Danna
      </a>
      , composed and performed the majority of the music for the film's
      <a href="/wiki/Little_Miss_Sunshine_(soundtrack)" title="Little Miss Sunshine (soundtrack)">
       soundtrack
      </a>
      and were nominated for a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards_of_2007" title="Grammy Awards of 2007">
       2006 Grammy Award
      </a>
      for Best Compilation Soundtrack. The main song, "The Winner Is", was also used in a commercial by the French-based environment and energy company
      <a href="/wiki/Suez_(company)" title="Suez (company)">
       Suez
      </a>
      and the Dutch pension fund
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PGGM" title="PGGM">
       PGGM
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Arcade_Fire" title="Arcade Fire">
       Arcade Fire
      </a>
      singer
      <a href="/wiki/Win_Butler" title="Win Butler">
       Win Butler
      </a>
      suggested to DeVotchKa that they cover the
      <a href="/wiki/Siouxsie_and_the_Banshees" title="Siouxsie and the Banshees">
       Siouxsie and the Banshees
      </a>
      song "
      <a href="/wiki/Peepshow_(album)" title="Peepshow (album)">
       The Last Beat of My Heart
      </a>
      ". The musicians found the idea interesting and they recorded that song for the now critically acclaimed
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Curse_Your_Little_Heart" title="Curse Your Little Heart">
        Curse Your Little Heart
       </a>
      </i>
      EP.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The band did a world tour in support of their previous album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/A_Mad_%26_Faithful_Telling" title="A Mad &amp; Faithful Telling">
        A Mad &amp; Faithful Telling
       </a>
      </i>
      (released March, 2008). The album reached No.9 on the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" title="Billboard magazine">
       Billboard
      </a>
      Heatseekers chart and No.29 on the Top Independent Albums chart.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      "New World" and "Head Honcho" were featured in a season 4 episode of the
      <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">
       Showtime
      </a>
      television show
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weeds_(tv_series)" title="Weeds (tv series)">
        Weeds
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      DeVotchKa's latest studio album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/100_Lovers" title="100 Lovers">
        100 Lovers
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      was released in March 2011.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2008, Boston newspaper
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Phoenix_(newspaper)" title="The Phoenix (newspaper)">
        The Phoenix
       </a>
      </i>
      named them the Best New Band from Colorado.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      "How It Ends" reached Number 101 in the UK Singles Charts.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The band supported
      <a href="/wiki/Muse_(band)" title="Muse (band)">
       Muse
      </a>
      at the
      <a href="/wiki/Stade_de_France" title="Stade de France">
       Stade de France
      </a>
      on June 12, 2010, playing to over 80,000 people.
     </p>
