         <div class="blog-post-body">
          <p>
           Last weekend a new link/page was added to
           <a href="http://www.healthcare.gov" target="_blank">
            www.healthcare.gov
           </a>
           . This link is titled 
           <a href="https://www.healthcare.gov/find-premium-estimates/" target="_blank">
            See Plans Now
           </a>
            (see image below  you can click to enlarge). This new feature claimed to let users view plans and rates in their area.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://schealthconnector.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/misled.jpg">
            <img alt="mislead healthcare.gov" class="size-medium wp-image-211 alignright" height="168" src="http://schealthconnector.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/misled-300x168.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
           Unfortunately, the information provided via this tool is
           <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-505269_162-57608843/healthcare.gov-pricing-feature-can-be-off-the-mark/" target="_blank">
            incorrect for nearly all users
           </a>
           . Why, you ask? Because the healthcare.gov rate calculator does not estimate rates based on your exact age. Instead, the it simply asks if you are older/younger than age 49.
          </p>
          <p>
           In the next image, you can see that when you choose to See Plans Now you are simply asked if you are 49 or younger or 50 or older. Not exactly a great baseline for calculating health plan rates.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://schealthconnector.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Age-selection.png">
            <img alt="South Carolina Health Exchange Rates" class=" wp-image-212 alignright" height="147" src="http://schealthconnector.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Age-selection-300x163.png" width="270"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h5>
           The Results:
          </h5>
          <p>
           For this exercise, I chose Charleston County, SC as my area of residence, and selected Age 49 or younger. Of course, I am 35 years old, and my exchange application is still being delayed for Identity verification (2 weeks and counting). So it seems reasonable that I may want to browse rates on the federal exchange while my Identity Verification is processing. Of course, after completing the required information about myself and my family status, I was given a list of every plan available in Charleston county, SC. Unfortunately, the rates given were much lower than what my ACTUAL rates are, based on my
           <a href="http://schealthconnector.org/south-carolina-health-exchange-rates/" target="_blank">
            South Carolina Health Exchange Rates
           </a>
           quote engine! See the image below for the rates I was given:
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://schealthconnector.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/results.png">
            <img alt="South Carolina health exchange quotesengine" class=" wp-image-213 alignright" height="151" src="http://schealthconnector.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/results-300x168.png" width="270"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           As you can see, the healthcare.gov plan finder tool gave me estimated rates of $230.27 for the CoventryOne Silver $10 Copay Roper St Francis Plan.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h5>
           Actual Results: Real numbers from SChealthconnector.org
          </h5>
          <p>
           So after viewing the rates over at healthcare.gov, I proceeded to run quotes for myself here on the schealthconnector.org
           <a href="http://schealthconnector.org/south-carolina-health-exchange-rates/" target="_blank" title="South Carolina Health Exchange Rates">
            South Carolina Health Exchange Rates
           </a>
           page. As expected, my ACTUAL premium was much higher than that given by healthcare.gov $
           <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
            268.50 (See image below  click to enlarge). IN the age of Google, Amazon &amp; Ebay, it seems obvious that transparency should be the #1 priority for a consumer website  hopefully our government can do much better- and SOON!
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://schealthconnector.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Actual-results.jpg" style="line-height: 1.5;">
            <img alt="South Carolina Health Exchange rates" class=" wp-image-215 alignright" height="151" src="http://schealthconnector.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Actual-results-300x168.jpg" width="270"/>
           </a>
          </p>
