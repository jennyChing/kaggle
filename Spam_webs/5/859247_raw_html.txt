       <h2 class="entry-title">
        Who was a detectives best sidekick? The Authors Collection.
       </h2>
       <h3 class="entry-date">
        October 24, 2013
       </h3>
       <h3 class="entry-author">
        <a href="http://venturegalleries.com/author/johnbrantingham/" rel="author" title="Posts by John Brantingham">
         John Brantingham
        </a>
       </h3>
       <div class="full-content group">
        <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_40051" style="width: 500px">
         <a href="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Archie-Goodwin.png">
          <img alt="Timothy Hutton as Archie Godowin in the Nero Wolfe television series." class="size-full wp-image-40051" height="375" src="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Archie-Goodwin.png" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
          Timothy Hutton as Archie Godowin in the Nero Wolfe television series.
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I sat down today to write about my favorite detective sidekicks, and I just couldnt do it. Why? There are too many. So often, the story is really about the sidekicks more than it is about the detective, especially when those detective are of the traditional European variety. Watson is so much more interesting than Holmes. After all, Holmes figures everything out with information we dont really have. He never worries. He never has to deal with conscience. Watson is dealing with bigger issues than Holmes could ever fathom. He has to figure out who did it while at the same time deal with the moral implications of what hes doing.
        </p>
        <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_38746" style="width: 300px">
         <a href="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/John-Brantingham-photo.jpg">
          <img alt="John Brantingham" class="size-medium wp-image-38746" height="225" src="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/John-Brantingham-photo-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
          John Brantingham
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Anyway, there were just too many sidekicks to deal with, so I decided to boil it down to types and say why I love them. Here are a few, but add some if you like. Id love to have a conversation.
        </p>
        <p>
         1. The geniuss assistant: These are generally the less clever pals of brilliant detectives. This is Watson and Archie Goodwin. When these characters are done well, however, they are nearly equal to the detective, but with different skills.
        </p>
        <p>
         Nero Wolfe looks to Archie Goodwin for moral guidance again and again. Watson pushes Holmes towards good even though hes not as brilliant as Archie. He does give us someone to root for however.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. The woman who kicks ass: This is really a new phase of the geniuss assistant, but in recent years  the last decade or so  tough women who help non-violent genius men have become a staple.
         <i>
          Castle, Monk,
         </i>
         and
         <i>
          The Mentalist
         </i>
         among many other books and movies, follow this pattern.
        </p>
        <p>
         I like this pattern, and I will continue to enjoy it until it descends into cliche. I think maybe it has done just that in the new show
         <i>
          Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.
         </i>
         The tough female soldier here is flat and uninteresting. There is nothing to her beyond the supposedly surprising fact that she has courage,
         <i>
          and
         </i>
         shes a woman. As though this is meant to be shocking.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres a wonderful parody of this character in the new Andy Samberg detective comedy,
         <i>
          Brooklyn Nine-Nine.
         </i>
         Shes tough and has almost no affect. Sambergs comedy goes beyond the flat tough female though. Were never quite sure of her motivations, making this parody of a cliche much more interesting than the cliche.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/John-Brantingham/e/B008JH0YES/ref=sr_tc_2_0?qid=1382527231&amp;sr=1-2-ent">
          <img alt="MannOfWar-3dLeft" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-36900" height="300" src="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/MannOfWar-3dLeft-245x300.png" width="245"/>
         </a>
         3. The knights friend: The American detective traditionally is a knight with a code. S/he is someone who might not be much more intelligent than other people, but s/he always acts morally.
        </p>
        <p>
         The sidekick for this character is tough and moral as well. Nora to Nick in
         <i>
          The Thin Man
         </i>
         . T.C., Rick, and Higgins to Magnum. The recently killed Archer to Sam Spade in
         <i>
          The Maltese Falcon
         </i>
         whom because of the dictates of honor, Spade must avenge.
        </p>
        <p>
         The knights friend should be as tough and as good as the knight. We could have a book about the friend alone and it would be just as interesting. Its just that were following the knight in this show. Did you ever see that episode of Magnum, where Higgins is alone in the jungle and taking out a group of bad guys one by one. He could easily have his own show.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. The comic relief. There are a number of good and great detective comedies out there.
         <i>
          Brooklyn Nine-Nine
         </i>
         is one of them. Andy Samberg after all needs to have someone to bounce jokes off. Gus in
         <i>
          Psyche
         </i>
         plays the role well, again avoiding cliche by having complexity.
        </p>
        <p>
         So who are my favorite sidekicks? I dont know. There are too many. There are, after all, more sidekicks than detectives. Each detective has at least one, and many have several. Lets do it this way, below, write down your favorite, and well see if theres any consensus.
        </p>
        <p>
         To start it off, Im going to say that Archie Goodwin is the best sidekick ever written, but you might disagree.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Please click the book cover to read more about John Brantingham and his novels.
         </strong>
        </p>
