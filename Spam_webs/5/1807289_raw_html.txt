      <h1>
       13 Ways the Busiest People Ever Avoid Burnout
      </h1>
      <div class="hidden-xs hidden-sm">
       <div class="grey-buttons" id="share">
        <div class="icon-wrapper favorite-container" data-muse-fav-id="3944" data-muse-fav-type="posts">
        </div>
        <div class="icon-wrapper fb-lg">

          <i class="fa fa-linkedin">

          <i class="fa fa-twitter">
          </i>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="icon-wrapper mail">
         <a class="share-button btn-email-greyscale" href="mailto:?Subject=13 Ways the Busiest People Ever Avoid Burnout &amp;body=Just read this awesome article I thought I would share with you! Check it out here: https://www.themuse.com/advice/13-ways-the-busiest-people-ever-avoid-burnout">
          <i class="fa fa-envelope-o">

         </a>
        </div>
       </div>

      <div class="author">
       By
       <a href="/author/young-entrepreneur-council">
        Young Entrepreneur Council
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="compiled-content share-base">
       <p>
        Sure, you
        <a href="http://www.themuse.com/advice/how-to-seriously-love-your-job">
         love your job
        </a>
        . After all, you wouldn't be doing it if you didn't.
       </p>
       <p>
        But after years of
        <a href="http://www.themuse.com/advice/finally-the-23-unwritten-rules-of-email">
         email upon email
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.themuse.com/advice/the-27-unwritten-rules-of-conference-calls">
         never-ending phone conferences
        </a>
        , even the best of us can face burnout.
       </p>
       <p>
        To find out how to successfully combateven avoidthis phenomenon, we asked 13 startup founders from the
        <a href="http://yec.co">
         Young Entrepreneur Council
        </a>
        (a.k.a., the hardest-working people we know) what strategies they rely on. Here's what they had to say.
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        1. Take a Vacation and Fully Unplug
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          Vacations with loved ones will help entrepreneurs avoid burnout. It's important to put on an out-of-office message, too, and not respond to emails. Another benefit of taking a vacation is you set a good example for hard-working employees.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/jspujji">
          Jesse Pujji
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.ampush.com">
          Ampush
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        2. Forget Balance, Find Harmony
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          I think people who say its all about work-life balance are wrong. I value finding passion and harmony in my work by being connected to and caring about my team and my customers and making a big difference in their lives. I would burn out way faster working five hours a day at a job that was hurting my soul than I would working 15 hours a day at a job that's feeding my soul.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         Dan Price,
         <a href="http://www.gravitypayments.com">
          Gravity Payments
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        3. Know Your Breaking Point
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          I think most entrepreneurs will tell you it's impossible to unplugso burnout is almost inevitable. However, it's important to know when you're close to or at a burnout stage. Something as simple as taking a day off, going for a bike ride, or having a fun night out with friends can help to take the edge off.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/pablopalatnik">
          Pablo Palatnik
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://ShadesDaddy.com">
          ShadesDaddy.com
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        4. Fill Your Day With Joy
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          Our business works to fill our people's day with what they love. When work feels like a job, we redirect those tasks to someone who loves them. Not a great organizer? We have a team member who is. Hate numbers? We've got someone who loves them. We are fueled with so much joy that we have a term around the company called the 'joy hangover.' When work is such a blast, burnout doesn't exist.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/CoreyBlake9000">
          Corey Blake
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.roundtablecompanies.com">
          Round Table Companies
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        5. Schedule Free Time
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          Schedule free time on your calendar, just like you would schedule a meeting, and stick to it. It's crucial to take the time you need for yourself, even if it's just 30 minutes a day. You'll get back to work feeling recharged and inspired, and chances are, you'll accomplish a lot more than you would if you worked straight through the day.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         Evrim Oralkan,
         <a href="http://www.travertinemart.com">
          Travertine Mart
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        6. Travel and Change Your Environment
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          Traveling is the best way to avoid burnout. Take your laptop and spend one to two months working from somewhere else, preferably internationally. With the internet and cloud tools like Dropbox and Skype, there's very little that can't be done from abroad. The change in environment sparks your creativity and allows you to bring new energy into your work.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/leahtn">
          Leah Neaderthal
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.startsomewhere.com">
          Start Somewhere
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        7. Pursue Your Passion
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          Starting a business is a time-consuming endeavor that doesn't end once things start to take off. On the contrary, the more successful you are, the more time you will be asked to contribute toward your enterprise. Thats why its important you focus your time and energy on doing something you enjoy and are passionate about.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/builtinchicago">
          Lane Campbell
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="https://syntress.com">
          Syntress
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        8. Take a Nap
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          Naps are not just for children. Instead of having a second cup of coffee, sleep for 20 minutes. It's the best way to refresh the brain.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/swerdlin">
          Jonathan Swerdlin
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://fdbk.com">
          Fdbk
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        9. Work Out
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          When I feel so mentally burnt out from juggling multiple projects, there's only one thing that can recharge my brain and my enthusiasmworking out. It's a way to unplug and just focus on the task in front of me, whether I go for a long run or a four-hour bike ride around my city. When I'm done, I have a high so powerful that all of the stress from my day-to-day activities is gone.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/AndrewVest">
          Andrew Vest
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.preferling.com">
          Preferling
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        10. Live With Mindfulness
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          Personally, I find the best way to avoid burnout is to have an ongoing focus on mindfulness rather than only when on breaks and vacations. Find ways to detach during the week in a way that works for you. Yoga and exercise work for some, while meditation works for others. Be mindful of those moments. Try to take consistent mini-breaks throughout the week to detach and re-center.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/apthomas">
          Andrew Thomas
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.skybell.com">
          SkyBell Technologies, Inc.
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        11. Find a Hobby
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          The best way to avoid burnout is to find a hobby you can deeply immerse yourself in for a few hours a week. I play ultimate frisbee, and when I'm on the field, I'm definitely not thinking about my company. Hobbies (such as basketball, ceramics, and climbing) can provide a therapeutic release, and you can come to work recharged and ready to go every day!
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/bkparikh">
          Bhavin Parikh
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://magoosh.com">
          Magoosh, Inc.
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        12. Build a Great Team
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          I used to think vacations would recharge me, but I would just come back to piles of work. In the last year, we've built our team to eight people. The quantity doesn't matterthe key is that it's a strong team. I know that whether I'm working or not, great things are being done. Feeling the support from all sides has been critical to my personal happiness.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/ModifyWatches">
          Aaron Schwartz
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://modifywatches.com">
          Modify Watches
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        13. Meditate Daily
       </h2>
       <div class="quote">
        <div class="quote-text">
         <p>
          Even if it's just for 10 minutes, spending the time (especially in the early afternoon) to just breathe has been extraordinarily powerful for me. It keeps me fresh and sharp, and taking self-care seriously sets a great example for the rest of the team.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="quote-source">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <em>
         
         <a href="https://twitter.com/thederek">
          Derek Flanzraich
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://greatist.com">
          Greatist
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
       </p>
