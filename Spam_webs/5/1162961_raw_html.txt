     <p>
      <b>
       Jorwert
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">
       Dutch
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="nl" xml:lang="nl">
       <i>
        Jorwerd
       </i>
      </span>
      ) is a
      <a href="/wiki/Village" title="Village">
       village
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Friesland" title="Friesland">
       Friesland
      </a>
      ,in the municipality of
      <a href="/wiki/Littenseradiel" title="Littenseradiel">
       Littenseradiel
      </a>
      , with a population of 360. The nickname for inhabitants of Jorwert is "Dweilstikken", meaning poor people who are dependent of rich ones. The village is built around a central "
      <a href="/wiki/Artificial_dwelling_hill" title="Artificial dwelling hill">
       terp
      </a>
      ", a man-made mound between 4 and 8 feet tall built to provide relief from the high
      <a href="/wiki/Tide" title="Tide">
       tides
      </a>
      of the sea before
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dike_(construction)" title="Dike (construction)">
       dykes
      </a>
      were constructed around the 10th century.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Book">
       Book
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jorwert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Book">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Dutch writer
      <a href="/wiki/Geert_Mak" title="Geert Mak">
       Geert Mak
      </a>
      used Jorwert as the hook for his book
      <i>
       Jorwerd: The Death of the Village in late 20th century Europe
      </i>
      (
      <a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/1860468039">
       ISBN 1-86046-803-9
      </a>
      ) - though the Dutch title
      <i>
       Hoe God verdween uit Jorwerd
      </i>
      translates as
      <i>
       How God disappeared from Jorwerd
      </i>
      . Mak describes the changes that have affected the village over the last century, and writes eloquently about the people and their experiences.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Church">
       Church
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jorwert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Church">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jorwert_church_2008.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1392" data-file-width="1654" height="185" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Jorwert_church_2008.jpg/220px-Jorwert_church_2008.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Jorwert_church_2008.jpg/330px-Jorwert_church_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Jorwert_church_2008.jpg/440px-Jorwert_church_2008.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Jorwert_church_2008.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The "Redbad Church"
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The centre of the village is the
      <a href="/wiki/Protestant_church_of_Jorwert" title="Protestant church of Jorwert">
       "Redbad church"
      </a>
      , which dates back to the 12th century. The church tower collapsed in 1951, but has since been rebuilt; the action to raise funds for the restoration included an
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.iepenloftspuljorwert.nl/" rel="nofollow">
       open air play
      </a>
      , which is still held every summer in the garden of the local notary.
     </p>
