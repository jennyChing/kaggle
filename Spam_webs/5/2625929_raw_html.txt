           three step chocolate marshmallow cups
          </a>
         </h1>

         <div class="post-content entry-content">
          <p>
           <a href="http://asubtlerevelry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/final-choc.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32890 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://asubtlerevelry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/final-choc.jpg" data-pin-url="http://asubtlerevelry.com/three-step-chocolate-marshmallow-cups" height="392" src="http://asubtlerevelry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/final-choc.jpg" title="Chocolate Marshmallow Cups" width="570"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://asubtlerevelry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/filled-2.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32896 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://asubtlerevelry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/filled-2.jpg" data-pin-url="http://asubtlerevelry.com/three-step-chocolate-marshmallow-cups" height="422" src="http://asubtlerevelry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/filled-2.jpg" title="chocolate cups filled" width="570"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           The coming few weeks will give us ample reason toindulge. Try these sweet and easy chocolate marshmallow cups for a homemade twist during treat season (you dont even need an oven). Therecipemakes just enough to bring for a classroom party, and is rich enough to keep your closest friends satisfied as you turn down the lights for a spooky movie night in.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://asubtlerevelry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/reeses.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32893 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://asubtlerevelry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/reeses.jpg" data-pin-url="http://asubtlerevelry.com/three-step-chocolate-marshmallow-cups" height="1296" src="http://asubtlerevelry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/reeses.jpg" title="Homemade chocolate marshmallow filled cups" width="570"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           To make a classroom size of these marshmallow chocolate cups (about 18) you will need; 8 ounces dark chocolate, coarsely chopped (I used Ghirardelli 60%); 4 ounces milk chocolate, coarsely chopped (I used Hersheys); one jar of Jet-Puffed Marshmallow Creme and decorative mini baking cups.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-32889">
           </span>
           Double layer mini baking cups on a sheet pan. Doubling the cups helps the chocolate cups maintain their shape.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Step one:
           </em>
           set-up a water bath for the chocolates. Combine chocolates in a heat-proof bowl. Set the bowl over a saucepan of simmering water (do not let water touch bottom of bowl), and stir with a rubber spatula until the chocolates have completely melted. The mixture should be smooth.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Step two:
           </em>
           Remove bowl from the saucepan and stir chocolate mixture about 15-20 seconds to release excess heat. With a small spoon, carefully fill the candy cups with about a tablespoon of chocolate. Using the spoon or a pastry brush, brush the chocolate from the bottom of each cup up the sides to completely cover the inside of the cup. Gently tap the sheet pan on the counter to level the chocolate. Place the cups in the fridge to allow the chocolate to harden, about 15 minutes. Set the remaining melted chocolate aside.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Step three:
           </em>
           Remove candy cups from fridge. Using two spoons, fill each cup with about half a tablespoon of marshmallow creme. You can be as liberal as you want with the creme, just make sure it doesnt reach the top of the cup. It does get sticky, thus the use of two spoons. The creme will begin to spread out on the cup bottoms the longer it is kept at room temperature (this is ok). Then spoon a top layer of chocolate onto the marshmallow filling to completely cover it. Gently tap the the pan again and place in fridge to completely set, about 20 minutes.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Recipe and photography done in collaboration with
            <a href="http://athenasplichta.com/" target="_blank">
             Athena Plichta
            </a>
            .
           </em>
