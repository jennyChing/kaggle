         a camera so simple, your dog could use it
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="byline">
        Posted by
        <a href="http://www.shelterrific.com/author/angela-m" rel="author" title="Posts by Angela M.">
         Angela M.
        </a>
        in

      <div class="page_content">
       <p>
        <img alt="pet_camera" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-14351" height="394" src="http://www.shelterrific.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/pet_camera.jpg" title="pet_camera" width="443"/>
        <br/>
        We love our pets at Shelterrific, and often wonder what the world looks like from their point of view. Thanks to the
        <a href="http://www.fredflare.com/customer/product.php?productid=4285&amp;cat=254" target='blank"'>
         Pets Eye View Camera from Fred Flare
        </a>
        , $50, now anyone can get a sneak peak into Fidos world. The water-resistant, lightweight camera clips onto your furry anipals collar. A timer can be set to snap a photo every one, five, or 15 minutes. The internal memory can hold over 40 pictures for you to gush over (and for Fluffy to use to update her Facebook page). Theres already one animal ahead of the proverbial pack. Our furry friend
        <a href="http://www.photographercat.com/" target='blank"'>
         Cooper
        </a>
        , a four-year-old American Shorthair cat who lives in Seattle, has been photographing his day-to-day adventures for more than a year now  hes even had his
        <a href="http://www.photographercat.com/exhibits/chicago/" target='blank"'>
         work exhibited
        </a>
        ! My favorites are the portraits
        <a href="http://www.photographercat.com/2010/01/portraits-by-cooper/" target='blank"'>
         the portraits
        </a>
        that hes snapped of his human family  especially his new baby brother.  Katie D.
       </p>
