        Collective Nouns
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="description">
       <p>
        <a href="http://ckck.tumblr.com/post/11615223059">
         ckck
        </a>
        :
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Even though Ive long been familiar with expressions like a pack of wolves, a murder of crows and a pride of lions I never realized just how many of these there were
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_collective_nouns" target="_blank">
          until now
         </a>
         . Heres a selection, with many more at the link:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          A congregation of alligators
          <br/>
          A shrewdness of apes
          <br/>
          A cloud of bats
          <br/>
          A sleuth of bears
          <br/>
          A clowder of cats
          <br/>
          A bask of crocodiles
          <br/>
          A badling of ducks
          <br/>
          A convocation of eagles
          <br/>
          A gang of elk
          <br/>
          A leash of foxes
          <br/>
          A tower of giraffes
          <br/>
          An array of hedgehogs
          <br/>
          A bloat of hippopotamuses
          <br/>
          A cackle of hyenas
          <br/>
          A mess of iguanas
          <br/>
          A smack of jellyfish
          <br/>
          A leap of leopards
          <br/>
          A suit of mallards
          <br/>
          A parliament of owls
          <br/>
          A pandemonium of parrots
          <br/>
          An unkindness of ravens
          <br/>
          A crash of rhinoceroses
          <br/>
          A scurry of squirrels
          <br/>
          An ambush of tigers
          <br/>
          A knot of toads
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         (Theyd all be excellent band names too, so dibs on Parliament of Owls!)
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        A month or so ago I happened to to use the phrase murder of crows in re-telling of a story that involved an incident from my childhoodto a group of colleagues. Needless to say there were a few interesting reactions just to that particular use of collective noun.
       </p>
       <p>
        And yes, Parliament of Owls sounds like an excellent follow up band to Department of Eagles
       </p>
