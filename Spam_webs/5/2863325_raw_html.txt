    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      The term
      <b>
       population reduction
      </b>
      may be used in relation to many topics, including the following:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Population_decline" title="Population decline">
        population decline
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Depopulation" title="Depopulation">
        depopulation
       </a>
       - reductions in human population levels for reasons such as low birth rate, emigration, disease or war.
      </li>
      <li>
       A euphemism for
       <a href="/wiki/Genocide" title="Genocide">
        genocide
       </a>
       , sometimes used by perpetrators of genocide to disguise their actions.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Population_control" title="Population control">
        population control
       </a>
       - deliberate attempts to control human population levels (typically seeking an initial
       <i>
        reduction
       </i>
       as opposed to an increase), sometimes related to the '
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Optimum_Population" title="Optimum Population">
        Optimum Population
       </a>
       ' concept.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Culling" title="Culling">
        culling
       </a>
       - deliberate reductions in animal or plant population levels, by human action
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        population reduction
       </i>
       - the phase preceding the
       <a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">
        extinction
       </a>
       of a species
      </li>
     </ul>
