                            It depends a lot on whether we're talking early date or a date with your significant other. Some simple ideas would be things that you just wouldn't normally think of for a date...
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            Play tourist for a day with a hop-on-hop-off tour bus. Or hit up the Empire State Building. Those are the types of things that most people only do once (if at all) when they first get to NYC, but could make for excellent dates.
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            Or try to discover things that are a little off the beaten path like The Cloisters or the Little Red Lighthouse. Coney Island makes for a great day-trip as well.
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            Or, if you want to go further afield, there are plenty of places that are an easy train-hop away. Breakneck Ridge (not as scary as it sounds) is one of my favorite hikes and is accessible by train. Or head slightly further up to Beacon NY to explore there.
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            Into Connecticut, you've got Philip Johnson's Glass House which is well-worth a tour if you're interested in architecture and art at all. (Also totally train-accessible.)
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            We've got a ton of date idea recommendations around NYC on Date Night Is... (
                            <span class="qlink_container">
                             <a class="external_link" href="http://www.datenight.is" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                              http://www.datenight.is
                             </a>
                            </span>
                            ) but the list above are some of the first that comes to mind.
