         <h1 id="page-title">
          An Open Letter to the World Music Institute: Do not present Idan Raichel.
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="region" id="content">
         <div class="block block-system no-title odd first last block-count-10 block-region-content block-main" id="block-system-main">
          <article class="node node-web-action article odd node-full 1 ia-n view-mode-full rendered-by-ds clearfix" id="node-1202" role="article">
           <div class="two-66-33 at-panel panel-display clearfix">
            <div class="region region-two-66-33-first">
             <div class="region-inner clearfix">
              <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden view-mode-full">
               <div class="field-items">
                <div class="field-item even">
                 <h3>
                  Tell the World Music Institute that presenting Idan Raichel is not in line with your values or theirs.
                 </h3>
                 <p>
                  This letter was emailed to the World Music Institute on October 2, 2014. After WMI failed to respond it was made public here on October 9.
                 </p>
                 <hr/>
                 <p>
                 </p>
                 <h3>
                  To the staff and board of the World Music Institute:
                 </h3>
                 <p>
                  As organizations and individuals who support the struggle for justice, human rights, equality and democracy in the Middle East, we are contacting you because we do not feel that Idan Raichels presence on the Symphony Space stage (scheduled for November 18, 2014) for a
                  <a href="http://worldmusicinstitute.org/event/toure-raichel-collective/">
                   concert presented by
                  </a>
                  the World Music Institute is in keeping with our values or yours. Idan Raichel has
                  <a href="http://www.richardsilverstein.com/2014/01/06/idan-raichel-israeli-world-music-star-endorses-torture-rape-of-arab-prisoners/">
                   publicly endorsed torture
                  </a>
                  and explicitly describes his role as an artist in terms of uncritical support for the Israeli military and government. He wrote in the Jerusalem Post in June 2014 that In creating this musical project we feel as if
                  <a href="http://www.jpost.com/Features/In-Thespotlight/A-very-special-project-The-keyboardist-composer-and-producer-speaks-out-355234">
                   we are cultural ambassadors for Israel
                  </a>
                  . He added, When I look back over the past few years, I see an Israel I am happy with.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  In 2006, the Israeli government
                  <a href="http://mfa.gov.il/MFA/PressRoom/2006/Pages/International%20Brand%20Israel%20seminar%20to%20be%20launched%20this%20week%20by%20the%20Foreign%20Ministry%2024-Oct-2006.aspx">
                   launched a public relations initiative
                  </a>
                  called "Brand Israel," which
                  <a href="http://forward.com/articles/2070/israel-aims-to-improve-its-public-image/?">
                   uses art and music
                  </a>
                  to whitewash Israels human rights violations. According to Israel's
                  <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2009/03/19/world/middleeast/19israel.html?">
                   Ministry of Foreign Affairs
                  </a>
                  , sending Israeli artists and musicians abroad "show[s] Israel's prettier face, so we are not thought of purely in the context of war." In an October 2013
                  <a href="http://abc7news.com/archive/9284228/">
                   ABC interview
                  </a>
                  with Idan Raichel conducted together with Israels Consul General to the Pacific North-West Andy David, David emphasized Raichels value to the Israeli government, saying. "I think Idan is maybe the best ambassador that Israel has through his music and his inclusiveness." This cynical use of art and music serves to obscure Israel's military occupation of the West Bank and Gaza Strip, as well as Israels overall history of grave human rights abuses, including the collective punishment of Palestinians through home demolitions, kidnappings, bombings and other means.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  As you say in your
                  <a href="http://worldmusicinstitute.org/about/mission/">
                   mission statement
                  </a>
                  , "Music and dance are both a means to communicate social values and a measure of a societys aspirations. . . . WMIs programs are intended to reinforce the cultural values of the community and to communicate to all the unique power of each individual culture." No doubt, Idan Raichel is a popular musician who has persuaded many artists across cultures to collaborate with him. But he hardly represents our social values. Raichel has strong ties to the Israeli military. In his endorsement of the right-wing US organization
                  <a href="http://www.thankisraelisoldiers.org/">
                   Thank Israeli Soldiers
                  </a>
                  ,
                  <a href="http://www.thankisraelisoldiers.org/?CategoryID=169&amp;ArticleID=100">
                   he said
                  </a>
                  that supporting "Israeli soldiers is a fulfillment of the Jewish ideal. Raichel performed for the army
                  <a href="https://www.facebook.com/yediothahronoth/photos/a.163280257033486.41864.137106982984147/895656393795865/?type=1">
                   before
                  </a>
                  ,
                  <a href="http://www.vpmo.gov.il/Communication/DoverMessages/Pages/IdanReichel.aspx">
                   during,
                  </a>
                  and
                  <a href="https://www.facebook.com/yediothahronoth/photos/a.163280257033486.41864.137106982984147/895656393795865/?type=1">
                   after
                  </a>
                  its August 2014 attack on Gaza,
                  <a href="http://blog.amnesty.org.nz/three-steps-to-achieve-a-lasting-truce-in-israel-and-gaza/">
                   which killed over 2100 Palestinians, including 500 children
                  </a>
                  .
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The Idan Raichel Project's 2012 tour of Africa was
                  <a href="http://www.ynetnews.com/articles/0,7340,L-4329210,00.html">
                   initiated and produced by the Israeli Foreign Ministry and Israeli embassies in Africa
                  </a>
                  to portray Israel as a welcoming, multicultural society. Avi Granot, head of the Israeli Foreign Ministrys Africa Division, said, The goal in sending Raichel to Africa was to present Israeli culture at its best. This effort is particularly disingenuous in light of
                  <a href="http://www.hrw.org/news/2013/07/23/israel-new-pressure-asylum-seekers-leave">
                   Israels treatment of Africans seeking political asylum
                  </a>
                  , whom Israeli officials routinely refer to as infiltrators. Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu
                  <a href="http://www.hrw.org/node/128682/section/4">
                   called
                  </a>
                  African asylum seekers a threat to the social fabric of society, our national security, our national identity... [and] our existence as a Jewish and democratic state. Raichels performances in Africa, and with African artists like Vieux Farka Tour, are a calculated distraction from Israels policies and the attitudes of its leaders.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Presenting Idan Raichel runs counter to the very values you espouse in your mission statement. It also ignores
                  <a href="http://www.bdsmovement.net/call">
                   Palestinian civil society's call
                  </a>
                  for
                  <a href="http://bdsmovement.net">
                   Boycott, Divestment and Sanctions (BDS)
                  </a>
                  to target
                  <a href="http://www.pacbi.org/etemplate.php?id=1047">
                   complicit Israeli institutions
                  </a>
                  until Israel respects international law and ceases violating Palestinian rights. This boycott, modeled on the boycott of apartheid South Africa, will continue until Israel ends its military occupation of Palestinian land, grants equal rights to the Palestinians of Israel, and respects refugees right to return to their land.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  We all look forward to the day when WMI can celebrate a post-Apartheid generation of socially conscious Palestinian and Israeli musicians, just as you are doing with
                  <a href="http://worldmusiccentral.org/2014/09/13/new-yorks-world-music-institute-reveals-2014-15-season/">
                   South African musicians
                  </a>
                  . We hope that WMI will act in that spirit now by canceling the Tour-Raichel Collectives planned November 18 concert at Symphony Space.
                 </p>
                </div>
               </div>
