      <br/>
      Leela is the motoring aficionado in the family, having been membership secretary for the Classic Car Club of Hong Kong for many years, and the owner of a Datsun Fair Lady and a couple of MGs that I know of.
      <br/>
      But this is Hong Kong and finding parts is always a matter of shipping and finding good mechanics is always a matter of luck, so ten years ago she sold them all and we bought a Mazda MX-5 which is still running fine.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <center>
       <a href="carparts-mg_grille8557.htm">
        <img alt="MG Grille" border="0" height="115" src="images-carparts/mg_grille/crw_8557s.jpg" width="152"/>
       </a>
      </center>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      These photos were taken at one of the MG Car Club exhibitions, this one in Taikoo Place, Quarry Bay, Hong Kong.
      <br/>
      The weather was perfect so I started photographing bits and pieces of cars -- mainly because photographing whole cars proved to be impossible with all the people and distracting backgrounds.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <center>
       <a href="carparts-mg_bootbag8586.htm">
        <img alt="MG Boot Bag" border="0" height="115" src="images-carparts/mg_bootbag/crw_8586s.jpg" width="152"/>
       </a>
      </center>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      After looking at the MG photos, I started this gallery because Hong Kong is one of the luxury car capitals of the world.
      <br/>
      We have one of the highest ownership rates for Mercedes and there are plenty of Rolls Royces, Lambourghinis, Aston Martins, Ferraris and the like all over the place -- including
      <a href="carparts-ferrari_disabled8844.htm">
       handicapped parking places
      </a>
      . And thanks to domestic helpers and chauffeurs, the average car -- luxury or otherwise -- is usually spotless.
      <br/>
      So I figure since I'm not owning them, I might as well start photographing them. More to come, as they say.
