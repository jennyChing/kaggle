        <p style="text-align: center">
         <b>
          <span style="font-size: 14pt">
           Practical Bindrune Amulets
          </span>
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img align="right" border="0" height="121" hspace="5" src="../images/wares/typicams.jpg" width="243"/>
         A practical bindrune may combine two or more runes to form a single design 
      representing the runic powers of the included runes. The purpose of the 
      bindrune is to enable a controlled release of those runic energies for the 
      benefit of the user, or someone he or she wants to help or influence.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         Bindrunes can only be used to help people, not to harm them. Any attempt to 
    place a curse, prevent a person doing something, or to make something nasty 
    happen to them will inevitably backfire on the user. Don' try it, it isn't 
    worth it, believe me.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         Although a bindrune is a blending of runic characters, each rune included 
      in the design has to retain its own individuality, it has to be visible as 
      an entity within the whole. I find it is best to limit the design to no 
      more than four, or maybe five characters at the most, so that it can still be symmetrical, balanced 
      and pleasing to the eye.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         There are exceptions to this rule, however. Some very ancient and highly 
      effective bindrunes incorporate 6, 7 or even 8 different runes. But 
      generally speaking, the simpler the design, then the more effective the 
      bindrune will be.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         Bindrunes are devised for specific purposes. They can generate mental 
      activity such as memory, logic, emotion, enlightenment, strength of will, 
      courage, fairness, clarity of thought. They can aid physical actions too, 
      such as health, strength, speed, endurance, and all the 5 senses.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img border="0" src="../images/wares/pracbind_color.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         Bindrunes can produce a runic field of energy to protect your person, your 
      home or possessions, your job or your business good-will, and they can 
      enhance relationships by encouraging harmony, partnership, love or sexual 
      attraction.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         You could easily draw a bindrune on a piece of paper, wood, stone or anything and carry it with you hoping that 
      it will have some effect. But the unempowered bindrune will be so weak that you would probably not notice any difference.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         For a bindrune to work effectively it must be empowered (some runemasters refer to this process as 
		'blessing or 'consecration') 
      in the correct way.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         I don't make wooden bindrunes any more because my old fingers just aren't nimble enough these 
		days for the fine 
		detail work required. The exact details of how I used to prepare and empower a bindrune are 
		detailed in my 2008 book
         <b>
          <i>
           <a href="books.shtml">
            Discovering Runes
           </a>
          </i>
         </b>
         (currently out of 
		stock) but I can give you a quick outline 
		here.
        </p>
        <p>
         The choice of wood is important to the effectiveness of a bindrune. When I was still making wooden amulets I 
		used to keep about 20 types of wood so that I could 
      always make one that was compatible with the component runes.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         <img align="right" border="0" height="252" hspace="5" src="../images/wares/keyfob.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #425342" vspace="5" width="150"/>
         During the whole process of cutting, polishing and branding the design, I followed a very ancient ritual for naming and 
      empowering the individual runes. This involved a special environment, chanted invocations 
		which I performed in the old tongue, and the use of 
      certain natural accessories.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         When the bindrune was completed it 
	was then sequestered in a secret place for a 
      period under special conditions. The place I used for this was a ley cross - a point of very high earth energy where two positive ley lines cross each other.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         For the sequesterment, the bindrune must be preserved in a particular way and hidden according to ancient rules for 
      a set length of time that is determined by the component runes and the purpose of the bindrune itself.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         Once the bindrune was released from sequesterment, the imbued power 
		was awakened and invoked on behalf of the user.
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         <img align="left" border="0" height="289" hspace="5" src="../images/wares/amchains.jpg" width="200"/>
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         Practical bindrune amulets 
		were supplied as pendants, keychain fobs or brooches.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Pendants
         </b>
         <br/>
         Leather thongs, silver-plated or gold-plated chain necklaces were 
		available.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>

        <p>
         <b>
          Keychain fobs
         </b>
         <br/>
         <img align="right" border="0" height="177" hspace="5" src="../images/wares/prackeys.jpg" width="226"/>
         Chrome steel split ring, silver-plated or gold-plated chain and ring.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         There was also a choice of pokerwork or painted designs in a range of colors.
        </p>
