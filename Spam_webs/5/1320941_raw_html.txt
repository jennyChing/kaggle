        <h1 class="title">
         Village Voice Makes Money off Child Prostitution
        </h1>
        <div class="entry">
         <!-- ad_automatik_link_tag START 1436811579.6888 -->
         <p>
          This letter from change.org asks that we all sign its
          <a class="autobesttag" href="http://marinagraphy.com/tag/petition/" rel="nofollow" title="See the tag: petition (2 posts)">
           petition
          </a>
          to get Village Voice Media CEO to change his participation in child prostitution, from which the company makes over 20 million dollars via its ads on Backpage.com.
         </p>
         <p>
          Read Groundswells petition, sign it, socialize it, and lets make them stop from making
          <a class="autobesttag" href="http://marinagraphy.com/tag/money/" rel="nofollow" title="See the tag: money (3 posts)">
           money
          </a>
          from this prostitution ring. They should not be allowed to get away with this, let alone make money from
          <a class="autobesttag" href="http://marinagraphy.com/tag/sex/" rel="nofollow" title="See the tag: sex (7 posts)">
           sex
          </a>
          slavery of young girls.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres the letter:
         </p>
         <p>
          By the time Norman Barnes was picked up by Massachusetts state troopers, he had made $19,000 forcing a 15-year-old girl to have sex with up to 8 men a day for 10 days.
         </p>
         <p>
          He couldnt have done it without
          <a href="http://backpage.com/" target="_blank" title="http://backpage.com/">
           Backpage.com
          </a>
          , a classified website owned by Village Voice Media.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://backpage.com/" target="_blank" title="http://backpage.com/">
            Backpage.com
           </a>
           is reported to be the nations #1 online platform for advertising sex with minors.
          </strong>
          A recent letter from 51 Attorneys General to Village Voice Medias lawyers cites over 50 cases (in 22 states) of trafficking or attempted trafficking of minors for sex using the site. Although such use violates
          <a href="http://backpage.com/" target="_blank" title="http://backpage.com/">
           Backpage.com
          </a>
          s terms of service, the site continually fails to identify and remove ads selling sex with children.
         </p>
         <p>
          Driven by the universal moral imperative to end child sex trafficking, a new multifaith action network called
          <strong>
           Groundswell has started a petition on
           <a href="http://change.org/" target="_blank" title="http://change.org/">
            Change.org
           </a>
           .
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.change.org/petitions/tell-village-voice-media-to-stop-child-sex-trafficking-on-backpagecom?utm_medium=email&amp;alert_id=JxrCNTCUog_rJNeHWJNCm&amp;utm_source=action_alert" target="_blank" title="http://www.change.org/petitions/tell-village-voice-media-to-stop-child-sex-trafficking-on-backpagecom?utm_medium=email&amp;alert_id=JxrCNTCUog_rJNeHWJNCm&amp;utm_source=action_alert">
            Sign Groundswells petition now to shut down the adult services section of Backpage.com  the only effective way to end child sex trafficking on the site.
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Village Voice Media executives are fully aware that their site is used as a platform for child sex trafficking, but have done close to nothing to stop it.
         </p>
         <p>
          Instead, after executives were confronted by the Attorneys General, the Village Voice ran a shocking cover story expose that smeared hard-working advocacy groups by presenting sensationally low (and poorly researched) statistics on child sex trafficking.
         </p>
         <p>
          What the story didnt mention is that
          <strong>
           Village Voice Media earns $22.7 million a year from its adult services ads on
           <a href="http://backpage.com/" target="_blank" title="http://backpage.com/">
            Backpage.com
           </a>
          </strong>
          , which it may have to shut down completely (like Craigslist did last year after hearing from 10,000
          <a href="http://change.org/" target="_blank" title="http://change.org/">
           Change.org
          </a>
          members) to effectively prevent the site from being used as a platform for selling sex with children.
         </p>
         <p>
          All Village Voice Media has done so far is throw up a smokescreen to try and hide how theyre providing a platform child sex trafficking on
          <a href="http://backpage.com/" target="_blank" title="http://backpage.com/">
           Backpage.com
          </a>
          . Let Village Voice Media executives know that they cant hide their complicity.
          <strong>
           Sign Groundswells petition to end child sex trafficking on
           <a href="http://backpage.com/" target="_blank" title="http://backpage.com/">
            Backpage.com
           </a>
           now:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.change.org/petitions/tell-village-voice-media-to-stop-child-sex-trafficking-on-backpagecom?utm_medium=email&amp;alert_id=JxrCNTCUog_rJNeHWJNCm&amp;utm_source=action_alert" target="_blank" title="http://www.change.org/petitions/tell-village-voice-media-to-stop-child-sex-trafficking-on-backpagecom?utm_medium=email&amp;alert_id=JxrCNTCUog_rJNeHWJNCm&amp;utm_source=action_alert">
            http://www.change.org/
            <wbr>
             petitions/tell-village-voice-
             <wbr>
              media-to-stop-child-sex-
              <wbr>
               trafficking-on-backpagecom
              </wbr>
             </wbr>
            </wbr>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Thanks for being a change-maker,
         </p>
         <p>
          Amanda and the
          <a href="http://change.org/" target="_blank" title="http://change.org/">
           Change.org
          </a>
          team
         </p>
