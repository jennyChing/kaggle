        <h3>
         The Mobile Movement
        </h3>
        <p>
         As companies continue to develop bigger and more powerful technology, they are simultaneously creating even smaller devices.
         <a href="http://www.computerworld.com/article/2500153/computer-processors/new-tiny-circuit-may-lead-to-smaller--more-powerful-devices.html" target="_blank">
          Computer World
         </a>
         reports how pocket-sized screens are sweeping the world, and theres no denying that these tiny technological advances are exciting and convenient.
        </p>
        <p>
         In addition to relatively traditional phone and computer capabilities  like texting and emailing  we now have access to literally millions of other, more niched apps. Whether its a boredom-combating game, a specialized dating service, or even just an extra-bright flashlight, apps offer us services that, ideally, make it easier to go about our daily lives.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mobile devices have transformed from helpful, convenient tools into wholly necessary ones for working citizens. According to
         <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/02/20/10-mobile-apps-work/#_" target="_blank">
          Mashable
         </a>
         , this increased dependency on mobile devices has encouraged developers to design apps that make working and living easier  useful tools like calendars, calculators, and to-do lists now exist in countless forms, ready for download within seconds.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1884" style="width: 600px;">
         <a href="http://www.appmakr.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Device_Group_shot.jpg">

         <span class="tweetable">

           Because of this astounding surge in app design and development, the mobile marketing industry managed to reach an $18 billion value in 2014,
           <i class="icon-twitter" style="color: #ed2e24;">
           </i>
          </a>
          <span class="sharebuttons">
           <a href="https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?original_referer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.appmakr.com%2Fblog%2Fapp-ads%2F&amp;source=tweetbutton&amp;text=The%20surge%20in%20%23app%20development%20caused%20the%20%23mobile%20%23marketing%20industry%20to%20reach%20%2418bn%20in%202014%2C%20says%20%40mobyaffiliates.&amp;url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.appmakr.com%2Fblog%2Fapp-ads%2F">
            TWEET
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         establishing itself as an extremely profitable industry indeed, according to Moby Affiliates.
        </p>
        <p>
         According to the
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2015/03/09/business/surge-in-smartphones-sets-off-new-wave-of-corporate-self-reinvention.html" target="_blank">
          New York Times
         </a>
         , the exploding mobile market has set new standards for businesses, requiring them to rethink advertising strategies to include a place and a budget for mobile marketing.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Newly Developed, Non-Intrusive Ads
        </h3>
        <p>
         An undeniably obnoxious aspect of everyday app use is the frequent ads that pop up and plaster our screens. Not only do they often take an excessive amount of time to load, but they usually last way longer than we want and are strangely difficult to get rid of.
        </p>
        <p>
         Not to mention that, more often than not, the actual substance of the ads is uninteresting and irrelevant. All this changes with
         <a href="http://www.leadbolt.com/" target="_blank">
          Leadbolt
         </a>
         s newly developed, non-intrusive video ads.
        </p>
        <p>
         This new mobile marketing tool targets a desired audience and plays an ad immediately, with no load time whatsoever. All the video ads are pre-cached  in other words, they are downloaded instead of streamed, so that theyll play with no delay.
        </p>
        <p>
         Not only are Leadbolts video ads both quick to load and relevant, but they can also be swiftly skipped over. This maximizes the amount of time you spend actually using your app and minimizes the time you spend dealing with poorly-designed, irrelevant ads.
        </p>
        <p>
         The results among users demonstrate a drastic increase in both traffic and installation. Non-intrusive video ads have gotten 75% more participation in comparison with ordinary, still-image advertisements.
        </p>
        <p>
         Video ads are indisputably better for all three parties involved: marketing companies, whose profits increase, commercial developers, whose ads perform better, and app users, who ultimately get a smoother, less obnoxious mobile experience.
        </p>
