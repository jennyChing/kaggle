       <h2>
        <a href="http://forzafutbol.com/2013/10/atletico-madrid-the-dark-horses-of-europe.html" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Atletico Madrid:  The Dark Horses Of Europe">
         Atletico Madrid:  The Dark Horses Of Europe
        </a>
       </h2>
      </div>
      <div class="cover">
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://forzafutbol.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/uefa-champions-league-2013-14.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article-', 'http://forzafutbol.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/uefa-champions-league-2013-14.jpg', '']);">
          <img alt="uefa-champions-league-2013-14" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-46780" height="458" src="http://forzafutbol.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/uefa-champions-league-2013-14.jpg" width="611"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The dormant club in Spanish football Atletico Madrid have long been overshadowed by their city neighbours Real Madrid, but this season they have flown out the blocks, winning all eight of their league games and beating neighbours Real in the process. Winning the league could be a stretch for Atletico as they have to compete with Barcelona, the current La Liga favourites with operators including
         <a href="http://casino.betway.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://casino.betway.com/', 'Betway']);">
          Betway
         </a>
         , but they do have a very realistic chance of making waves in Europe, and Champions League glory should not be considered out of their reach.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Atletico have been drawn in group G alongside Porto, Zenit Saint Petersburg and Austria Wien and have seen their winning streak continue in this format, topping the group with six points from two games. They have already beaten Zenit and Porto and face Austria Wien in their next two European fixtures. They should easily win both those games and should be on 12 points from four games. They look very good value to top their group and progress through to the last-16.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Of course it would be foolhardy to suggest that Atletico have the strongest squad in Europe but they do possess a squad of notable calibre. Up front they have Diego Costa and if he can maintain his current goalscoring exploits (ten goals in eight games) he will really be able to propel the club on in this competition. Throw in strike partner David Villa and goals should not be a problem for them.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         They are blessed with an industrial midfield with combative midfielders Tiago, Gabi and Mario Suarez as well as the talented and skilful Raul Garcia, Koke and Arda Turan. Behind the midfield is a solid back four. Centre-backs Diego Godin and Toby Alderweireld are imposing figures and take some beating while full-backs Filipe Luis and Juanfran are defensively sound whilst also providing another avenue of attacking threat.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Above all, the key man for Atleticos chances is young goalkeeper Thibaut Courtois. The 21-year-old is one of the best goalkeepers in Europe and is a solid shot-stopper. This is a squad capable of going far in Europe.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Atletico are well and truly dark horses in the Champions League but if one evokes memories of Liverpools historic 2005 run then their prospects seem a lot more substantial. The Liverpool team then was one of the weakest in recent history but they banded together behind their manager and produced spirited performance after performance.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         In Diego Simeone they have a manager to get behind. He has also played for the club; Simeone was a battler on the pitch and he will be hoping to get that out of his players as they try to advance in this tournament. If Simeone can create a cohesive unit that is high on morale then Atletico have every chance of beating any team. That is the beauty of knock-out football; it is judged over 180 minutes and not an entire season. If Atletico can slug it out then they can win this competition.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
