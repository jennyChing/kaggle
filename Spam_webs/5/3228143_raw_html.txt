       Benjamin Watson's Ferguson Facebook post goes viral

       <p>
        Though he hasn't scored a touchdown, has started just four games and amassed only 88 yards, New Orleans Saints tight end Benjamin Watson has made his presence felt this season.
       </p>
       <p>
        Watson, who serves as a backup to Saints star Jimmy Graham, has had his passionate and hopeful Facebook message about the Ferguson conflict go viral. In the first 12 hours, the status had been shared 97,774 times and liked by 211,688 accounts.
       </p>
       <div class="mostPopular">

       <p>
        The reach is particularly significant given that Watson's account had fewer than 20,000 profile likes as of 10 a.m. Wednesday morning.
       </p>
       <p>
        The 33-year-old Virginia native posted his feelings about the St. Louis County grand jury's decision not to seek an indictment against police Officer Darren Wilson in the shooting death of Michael Brown on
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/BenjaminWatsonOfficial?hc_location=timeline" target="_blank">
         Watson's verified Facebook account
        </a>
        at 9 p.m. Tuesday.
       </p>
       <p>
        Here's the full text of his post:
       </p>
       <div class="photoContainer">
        <a href="/news/nation/guide-to-developments-in-michael-brown-shooting-in-missouri-1.9054285">
         <span class="icon">
          <i class="fa fa-camera">
          </i>
         </span>
         <span class="ovrly">
          <strong>
           Nation
          </strong>
          <em>
           Timeline of events in Ferguson, Mo.
          </em>
         </span>
         <img height="270" src="http://cdn.newsday.com/polopoly_fs/1.10050242.1426169496!/httpImage/image.JPG_gen/derivatives/landscape_640/image.JPG" width="480"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        At some point while I was playing or preparing to play Monday Night Football, the news broke about the Ferguson Decision. After trying to figure out how I felt, I decided to write it down. Here are my thoughts:
       </p>
       <div class="adCont clear">
        <div class="ad">
         <div class="module cubeAd" id="adpos2">
          <div class="adBanner">
          </div>
          <p>
           advertisement |
           <a href="/advertise" name="name">
            advertise on newsday
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        I'M ANGRY because the stories of injustice that have been passed down for generations seem to be continuing before our very eyes.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'M FRUSTRATED, because pop culture, music and movies glorify these types of police citizen altercations and promote an invincible attitude that continues to get young men killed in real life, away from safety movie sets and music studios.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'M FEARFUL because in the back of my mind I know that although I'm a law abiding citizen I could still be looked upon as a "threat" to those who don't know me. So I will continue to have to go the extra mile to earn the benefit of the doubt.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'M EMBARRASSED because the looting, violent protests, and law breaking only confirm, and in the minds of many, validate, the stereotypes and thus the inferior treatment.
       </p>
       <div class="photoContainer">
        <a href="/news/nation/ferguson-protest-photos-after-darren-wilson-not-indicted-for-michael-brown-shooting-1.9650762">
         <span class="icon">
          <i class="fa fa-camera">
          </i>
         </span>
         <span class="ovrly">
          <strong>
           Nation
          </strong>
          <em>
           Protests erupt after Ferguson decision
          </em>
         </span>
         <img height="270" src="http://cdn.newsday.com/polopoly_fs/1.9652116.1416929117!/httpImage/image.JPG_gen/derivatives/landscape_640/image.JPG" width="480"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        I'M SAD, because another young life was lost from his family, the racial divide has widened, a community is in shambles, accusations, insensitivity hurt and hatred are boiling over, and we may never know the truth about what happened that day.
       </p>
       <div class="relatedModule">
        <div id="eplayerHolder" style="width:300px; height:360px; margin:0 auto;">
         <div id="perf3axd01ze71gd1y2c8nbqaudd1">
          <a href="http://www.adobe.com/go/getflashplayer" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Get Adobe Flash player" src="http://images.eplayer.performgroup.com/namedImage/12637/install_flash.gif"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <script src="http://static.eplayer.performgroup.com/flash/js/swfobject.js" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
         <script src="http://static.eplayer.performgroup.com/flash/js/performgroup.js" type="text/javascript">

         </script>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        I'M SYMPATHETIC, because I wasn't there so I don't know exactly what happened. Maybe Darren Wilson acted within his rights and duty as an officer of the law and killed Michael Brown in self defense like any of us would in the circumstance. Now he has to fear the backlash against himself and his loved ones when he was only doing his job. What a horrible thing to endure. OR maybe he provoked Michael and ignited the series of events that led to him eventually murdering the young man to prove a point.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'M OFFENDED, because of the insulting comments I've seen that are not only insensitive but dismissive to the painful experiences of others.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'M CONFUSED, because I don't know why it's so hard to obey a policeman. You will not win!!! And I don't know why some policeman abuse their power. Power is a responsibility, not a weapon to brandish and lord over the populace.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'M INTROSPECTIVE, because sometimes I want to take "our" side without looking at the facts in situations like these. Sometimes I feel like it's us against them. Sometimes I'm just as prejudiced as people I point fingers at. And that's not right. How can I look at white skin and make assumptions but not want assumptions made about me? That's not right.
       </p>
       <div class="photoContainer">
        <a href="/opinion/cartoons-unrest-in-ferguson-1.9086521">
         <span class="icon">
          <i class="fa fa-camera">
          </i>
         </span>
         <span class="ovrly">
          <strong>
           Opinion
          </strong>
          <em>
           Cartoons: Racial tension in America
          </em>
         </span>
         <img height="270" src="http://cdn.newsday.com/polopoly_fs/1.10345327.1430323985!/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/landscape_640/image.jpg" width="480"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        I'M HOPELESS, because I've lived long enough to expect things like this to continue to happen. I'm not surprised and at some point my little children are going to inherit the weight of being a minority and all that it entails.
       </p>
       <div class="adCont clear">
        <div class="ad">
         <div class="module cubeAd" id="adpos3">
          <div class="adBanner">
          </div>
          <p>
           advertisement |
           <a href="/advertise" name="name">
            advertise on newsday
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        I'M HOPEFUL, because I know that while we still have race issues in America, we enjoy a much different normal than those of our parents and grandparents. I see it in my personal relationships with teammates, friends and mentors. And it's a beautiful thing.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'M ENCOURAGED, because ultimately the problem is not a SKIN problem, it is a SIN problem. SIN is the reason we rebel against authority. SIN is the reason we abuse our authority. SIN is the reason we are racist, prejudiced and lie to cover for our own. SIN is the reason we riot, loot and burn. BUT I'M ENCOURAGED because God has provided a solution for sin through the his son Jesus and with it, a transformed heart and mind. One that's capable of looking past the outward and seeing what's truly important in every human being. The cure for the Michael Brown, Trayvon Martin, Tamir Rice and Eric Garner tragedies is not education or exposure. It's the Gospel. So, finally, I'M ENCOURAGED because the Gospel gives mankind hope.
       </p>
