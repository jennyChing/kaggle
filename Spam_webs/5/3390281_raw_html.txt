             <p>
              <strong>
               <em>
                CONTINUED FROM PREVIOUS PAGE
               </em>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://i2.wp.com/journalthis.danoah.com/wp-content/uploads/creeped-out-kid11.jpg">
               <img alt="Creeped out kid!" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-42814" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/journalthis.danoah.com/wp-content/uploads/creeped-out-kid11-830x551.jpg?resize=700%2C465"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <ol start="21">
              <li style="padding-bottom:12px;">
               When my older brother was 2 he wouldnt go by any other name than John and would insist on being called that name (his name is Brent), when he was 4 years old he asked my mom where Yoko was. My mom told him she was doing well and living in New York, once Brent understood this, he was relieved and was okay with continuing on as Brent. My brother has gone on to be a talented musician and artist, with friends in the celebrity realm. Oh, and he was conceived 3 months after John Lennons death
              </li>
              <li style="padding-bottom:12px;">
               I catch the water off Daddys penis in the shower!
              </li>
              <li style="padding-bottom:12px;">
               My 3-yo daughter said, Youre the best mommy I have had so far. I said I am the only mommy you have had! As she walked away she said, Thats what all the other mommies said, too!
              </li>
              <li style="padding-bottom:12px;">
               Sept 11, 2001 was my sons first day of Kindergarten. He was very excited the day before, but when I woke him up, he immediately started crying uncontrollably and said over and over, Mommy, its gonna be such a BAD, BAD DAY!! I tried to convince him otherwise and told him he could watch cartoons while I got ready for work. I sat him (still weeping) on the couch and turned the TV onjust as they showed the second plane hitting the tower on New York.
              </li>
              <li style="padding-bottom:12px;">
               About 2 months ago my 6 yr old son says, Mommy, when you die I will put you in a glass box on my dining room tabledont worry I will make sure my wife knows how to dust you sou you wont get dirty.
              </li>
              <li style="padding-bottom:12px;">
               Mommy, the lady in the white car is okay, she didnt die, there was an angel in the car with her.  my son, age 2. An hour later I turned on the news and a lady in a white Cadillac had driven off the Portland bridge, but swam to safety. She reported seeing a bright light that broke her window and urged her to swim. Emergency personnel said she should have drowned.
              </li>
              <li style="padding-bottom:12px;">
               My Exs son gave me a hug, looked up at me and said I hope it hurts when you die.
              </li>
              <li style="padding-bottom:12px;">
               Dad, my girlfriend is a month late
              </li>
              <li style="padding-bottom:12px;">
               When I was about two weeks pregnant (and didnt even know yet), my 3 year old daughter came and patted my belly and said, great grandpa wants you to know theres a baby boy in there and that you need to take good care of this one so you dont both have to go to heaven when hes born. She had no living great grandparents, and I did end up being pregnant.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              Um.
             </p>
             <p>
              Yeah.
             </p>
             <p>
              Creepy.
             </p>
             <p>
              And hilarious.
             </p>
             <p>
              And sometimes a mixture of both. Hahaha.
             </p>

(her way of saying 'boo'). I asked her what she was doing and she said "I play peek-a-boo Mommy!" I asked her who she was playing peek-a-boo with, and she pointed to the corner of the room where nothing, absolutely nothing was there. I just shrugged it off as kids will be kids, and she was playing peek-a-boo with her own shadow or something. I closed her door, made sure it was closed tight because we have cats, and I went to bed. The next morning, we got up to take my husband to work. My daughter's door is wide open. None of the cats were in the nursery either; what was odd was they were all sitting outside the nursery and looking into the room and their tails were twitching and a couple of them had their ears back (and I'm a crazy cat lady and have five cats, and all five of them were doing this). The cats did not usually refuse to go into the nursery but they were refusing to go in there. I asked my husband why our daughter's door was open and he said 'Because you didn't close it.' Well, I did, and I triple checked the door because I don't want the cats in there when I'm not in there. He didn't open her door, and my daughter was still in a crib back then so she couldnt' climb out and open the door. Once again, I just passed it off as, 'whatever. Maybe I didn't close it.' That very night, I was asleep, and I ended up dreaming about this kid again, the same kid I had seen since I was a little kid, except this time he was once again my same age. In my dream (which seemed incredibly real) I was in my room and right at the foot of my bed, he was standing there and staring at me. He looked the same; dark hair, blue eyes. For some reason I thought back to my "dream kid" and I drew him and colored him in, and I even included the blood caked on the corners of his mouth. Well, I put that picture on my phone because I'm a realistic sketch artist as a hobby and it looked real and I wanted to show my friend. My eighth graders got a total kick out of it and so did my friend. Well, I still have that picture saved as my 'lock screen' picture. Just the other day, I installed a 'Frozen' game on my phone for my daughter and I let her play it. Usually I set it up for her but she wanted to play it so she grabbed my phone off the charger. The lock screen came up, and of course so did the drawing I did around Halloween. She was looking at it for a long time and then she brought me my phone and said, "Mom, his name is Aaron."

And she said, "Yes, he's in my room and when we moved to Texas, he rode in the car with us." Just a couple months ago, we moved from Washington State to Texas. No TV shows I am aware she has watched has a character in them named Aaron (I watch all her TV shows before I let her watch them). So I don't know where on earth she heard the name Aaron.
