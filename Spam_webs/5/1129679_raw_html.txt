         </p>

          <a href="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130354.jpg?a8ba62">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-7793" height="440" src="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130354-588x440.jpg?a8ba62" title="L1130354" width="588"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I could make a meal out of a big peach. Ok, not really. But everything else I eat afterwards just pales in comparison. I really love yellow peaches, which I find have a more intense, although more acidic flavor. The white ones are delicious, of course, and slightly more sweet but just not quite as flavorful. But trust me, Ill eat either any day.
          <a href="/what-to-buy-organic/">
           I always buy organic
          </a>
          peaches since conventionally grown ones are on the Environmental Working Groups
          <a href="http://www.ewg.org/foodnews/list.php">
           Dirty Dozen list
          </a>
          of the most highly contaminated produce. I think its because the pesticides are able to permeate peaches thin skin thereby making it difficult to remove toxins by washing. Pesticides make me nervous.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130376.jpg?a8ba62">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-7797" height="440" src="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130376-588x440.jpg?a8ba62" title="L1130376" width="588"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I have so many regular peach recipes, some that Ive taught in my classes like peach pie, grilled vanilla peaches and this fabulous individual warm peach pie in a jar number I am writing about today. This month I will teach an arugula salad with farro and peaches that is my new favorite. Did you see that crazy delicious breakfast I posted on
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pamelasalzman">
           Facebook
          </a>
          a month ago? Why arent we friends on
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pamelasalzman">
           Facebook
          </a>
          ? Youre missing out. I post something interesting there every single day! I digress. I used some leftovers from this recipe and put it on top of
          <a href='&lt;iframe src="http://rcm-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/cm?t=pamesalzkitcm-20&amp;o=1&amp;p=8&amp;l=as1&amp;asins=B000EDI0Y6&amp;ref=qf_sp_asin_til&amp;fc1=000000&amp;IS2=1&amp;lt1=_blank&amp;m=amazon&amp;lc1=0000FF&amp;bc1=000000&amp;bg1=FFFFFF&amp;f=ifr" style="width:120px;height:240px;" scrolling="no" marginwidth="0" marginheight="0" frameborder="0"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt; '>
           Bobs Red Mill warm, creamy buckwheat porridge
          </a>
          . People, I was like shut the front door. Best. Breakfast. Ever.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130430.jpg?a8ba62">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-7798" height="440" src="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130430-588x440.jpg?a8ba62" title="get those pecans nice and buttery and salty" width="588"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I have a dozen other peach recipes that Im not posting here so lets not even torture you with those. Lets talk about these individual peach pie in a jar thingies that are the quickest, tastiest healthful dessert you cant believe you havent ever made until now. I was inspired by a recipe I saw over at
          <a href="http://roostblog.com">
           Roost
          </a>
          , which is one of the prettiest blogs that mine will never look like. Sigh. Caitlin used apples, which I tried and thought turned out delish, but this peach version is crazy delish.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130469.jpg?a8ba62">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-7799" height="440" src="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130469-588x440.jpg?a8ba62" title="pulse a few times in the food processor until crumbly but not like meal" width="588"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Too many of you have expressed intimidation about
          <a href="/how-to-make-pie-crust/">
           making a pie from scratch
          </a>
          and I get it. Although for me, sometimes I just dont have the time to prepare and bake a pie, especially if Im entertaining and I am making a bunch of other things. Or maybe I have a craving for peach pie and I dont want to tempt myself with an entire pie in the kitchen. I am weak, after all.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130474.jpg?a8ba62">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-7801" height="440" src="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130474-588x440.jpg?a8ba62" title="saute the peaches just until warm" width="588"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This is your answer. You make these amazing buttered, salted pecans which you then crumble up and put on the bottom of a cute glass jar, preferably with a wide opening at the top. Then you saut some chopped peaches with a little honey, cinnamon, nutmeg and the tiniest amount of almond extract possible because like
          <a href="/apricot-cherry-and-almond-crisp/">
           Ive said before
          </a>
          , almond extract makes peach and apricot desserts more peachy and apricot-y. But it has to be the teeniest amount otherwise, whoa. And then just when the peaches are warm, you spoon them on top of the pecans. I am totally serious. But see how not-at-all-bad-for-you this is?? See why I can eat this in the morning and at night? See why I was giving out small portions at my classes so there would be more for me? Weak, I tell you.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130520.jpg?a8ba62">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-7806" height="440" src="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130520-588x440.jpg?a8ba62" title="see how pretty the jars look?" width="588"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130517.jpg?a8ba62">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-7807" height="440" src="http://pamelasalzman.com/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/L1130517-588x440.jpg?a8ba62" title="warm individual peach pie pots | pamela salzman" width="588"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          individual warm peach pie pots
         </h3>
         <p>
          inspired by
          <a href="http://roostblog.com/roost/warm-apple-pie-pots.html">
           Roost
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          serves 6-7, depending on the size of your glasses or jars. Ive used half-pint and pint jars. You dont have to use jars or glasses, but it looks so pretty that way.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Butter Pecan Crust
           </span>
          </em>
          <em>
           :
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           3 cups raw pecans
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           1  Tablespoons unsalted butter or unrefined coconut oil
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
            teaspoon sea salt (this is not a misprint)
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Peach Pie Filling
           </span>
          </em>
          <em>
           :
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           8 cups chopped peaches (peeled or unpeeled), about 12 small-medium peaches
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           2 Tablespoons butter, ghee or unrefined coconut oil
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           2 Tablespoons honey (just eyeball it since its such a pain to measure)
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           1/8 teaspoon almond extract
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           pinch of ground nutmeg
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
            cup almond flour (if necessary to thicken juices)
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Have ready 6 clean  pint or pint jars.
          </li>
          <li>
           To make the crust, preheat the oven to 375 degrees and line a small sheet pan with parchment paper. Melt the butter in a small (1-2 quart) saucepan, turn off the heat and add the pecans. Toss to coat.
          </li>
          <li>
           Pour the buttered pecans onto the sheet pan and sprinkle with  teaspoon salt. Bake for 8-10 minutes. Watch closely so they dont burn! Remove from oven and allow to cool completely.
          </li>
          <li>
           Place the cooled pecans into a food processor and pulse a couple times to form a coarse crumbly mixture. Place a couple tablespoons of crust into the bottom of each jar and set aside.
          </li>
          <li>
           To make the filling, in a large bowl, toss the peaches with the honey, extract, and spices. Melt the butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the peach mixture to the skillet and toss gently to heat through, 2-4 minutes.
          </li>
          <li>
           Turn off the heat and if the mixture is very liquidy, add the almond flour to the peaches and stir to combine.
          </li>
          <li>
           Place several spoonfuls of the peach pie mixture on top of the pecan crust and top with crme fraiche, whipped cream, vanilla ice cream or whipped coconut cream. Also delicious for breakfast on warm porridge or with yogurt. Serve immediately!
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Notes: If you are nut-free, you can still make the peaches alone or serve them over crumbled cookies, like
          <a href="/homemade-graham-crackers-recipe/">
           graham crackers
          </a>
          or gingersnaps.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
