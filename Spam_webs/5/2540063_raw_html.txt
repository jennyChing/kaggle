        <h1 class="entry-title">
         What to Pack on a Haunted Holiday
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="sep">
          Posted on
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/blog/2014/03/what-to-pack-on-a-haunted-holiday/" rel="bookmark" title="1:16 pm">
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-03-18T13:16:52+00:00" pubdate="">
           March 18, 2014
          </time>
         </a>
         <span class="by-author">
          <span class="sep">
           by
          </span>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/blog/author/admin/" rel="author" title="View all posts by admin">
            admin
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div style="float:left;margin-right:10px;">
         <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" scrolling="no" src="http://platform.twitter.com/widgets/tweet_button.html?url=http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/blog/2014/03/what-to-pack-on-a-haunted-holiday/&amp;counturl=http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/blog/2014/03/what-to-pack-on-a-haunted-holiday/&amp;text=What to Pack on a Haunted Holiday&amp;count=vertical&amp;lang=en&amp;via=amabaie&amp;related=" style="width:55px; height:62px;">
         </iframe>
        </div>
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         The main objective for most people on a haunted holiday is to a catch a glimpse of a ghost. Whilst many hotels are known for their frequent apparitions, sightings are never 100% guaranteed.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want to increase your chances of experiencing paranormal activity during your visit, consider packing something related to the resident ghosts in order to trigger the spirits. With the help of Wesley McDermott, weve put together a list of five of the most popular haunted hotels in the US, each of which includes an item you can bring along that might just turn a regular hotel stay into something extraordinary. For more haunted hotel options, you can browse his
         <a href="http://www.hauntedrooms.com" target="_blank">
          Haunted Rooms directory
         </a>
         .
         <span id="more-478">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Glen Tavern Inn, California
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Glen Tavern Inn was built in
         <a href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/directory/278-california/0-travel-web-sites/view.html" target="_blank">
          Santa Paula, Ventura County
         </a>
         , in 1911, when the small hamlet was developing into an up-and-coming oil town. Following the introduction of
         <a href="http://prohibition.osu.edu/" target="_blank">
          prohibition
         </a>
         laws in the town, Glen Tavern Inn suffered greatly and as a result, the inns third floor took on a variety of roles, including a speakeasy, brothel and gambling parlour.
        </p>
        <p>
         The hotels motto is where the past comes to life, so it seems fitting that spirits and ghosts are reported to maintain a vivid presence here. Most of the horrifying stories focus on the third floor and include the murdering of prostitutes and shootouts between unruly gamblers.
        </p>
        <p>
         One of the most commonly seen ghosts at Glen Tavern Inn is known as Calvin, a silent film company employee who was shot dead in a card game on the third floor whilst staying at the hotel in the 1920s. Calvin is often seen in the lobby, wearing a Western-style coat with long hair and a goatee, accompanied by the distinct smell of cigar smoke. If you want to increase your chances of seeing Calvin whilst at the Glen Tavern Inn, consider bringing along a pack of playing cards.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/glen-tavern-outside.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-479" height="319" src="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/glen-tavern-outside.jpg" title="glen-tavern-outside" width="480"/>
         </a>
         If you visit the hotel during October, dont miss out on the chance to take part in the towns
         <a href="http://www.ghostwalk.com/" target="_blank">
          Ghost Walk
         </a>
         . This trip is led by a guide who takes on the role of a popular historic character and guides you around the area, talking about the historic significance of various sites and the mysterious ghost stories which surround them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Queen Mary Hotel, California
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="http://www.hauntedrooms.com/queen-mary-hotel-long-beach-california" target="_blank">
          Queen Mary Hotel
         </a>
         is known as one of the most haunted ships in the world and boasts a rich history. The ship started out life as an ocean liner in 1936, before it served as a troopship throughout WW2. It received numerous awards and broke several records during its life at sea, including being the fastest ship to cross the North Atlantic and carrying the greatest number of people on board a floating vessel. Today, the Queen Mary is docked permanently at
         <a href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/directory/1682-long-beach/0-travel-web-sites/view.html" target="_blank">
          Long Beach, California
         </a>
         and features just short of 500 rooms open to guests.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/n4lteXhQNs4?rel=0" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </center>
        <p>
         With so many terrifying stories and horrifying tales surrounding the Queen Mary Hotel, its no wonder ghostly sightings are a common thing. The first class swimming pool is reported to be haunted by the ghosts of two ladies who drowned there, whilst many other reports include sightings of ghost children who have been spotted all over the ship. If you want to increase your chances of seeing the ghostly children, consider taking along a childrens toy during your visit.
        </p>
        <p>
         Get a hands-on experience of the Queen Mary Hotels most haunted areas and paranormal hotspots by signing up for the Haunted Encounters: The Queen of All Ghost Stories Tour. This tour is held daily and allows you to explore the ship whilst hearing the terrifying and tragic ghost stories which surround the vessel.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/9PcA4q05XIw?rel=0" width="420">
         </iframe>
        </center>
        <strong>
         Baladerry Inn, Pennsylvania
        </strong>
        <p>
         In 1812, Baladerry Inn was built on the site of the former George Bushman farm
         <a href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/directory/879-gettysburg/0-travel-web-sites/view.html" target="_blank">
          near Gettysburg
         </a>
         , where it was used as the home for the tenant farmer before it became the home of George Bushmans son. Like many farms in the region, Baladerry Inn was used as a field hospital during the
         <a href="http://www.civilwar.org/battlefields/gettysburg.html" target="_blank">
          Battle of Gettysburg
         </a>
          the main hall was used to treat civilians and confederate soldiers and was where many primitive amputations were carried out.
        </p>
        <p>
         One of the most common apparitions reported at the Baladerry Inn is of a well-dressed woman who is often seen in the Marigold Room standing at the bottom of the bed. A female re-enactor who staying at the Baladerry Inn reported to have brought some antique jewellery with her, which she kept in a bag on the side of the bed. After a day of working, the re-enactor returned to her room, only to find the jewellery laid out on top of the bag in which she left them in. Many people believe this to have been the work of the ghostly woman who was known for her flamboyant jewellery.Wesley McDermott suggests bringing some antique or vintage jewellery with you, to increase you chances of meeting the ghost.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whilst visiting Baladerry Inn in Gettysburg, why not join one of the
         <a href="http://www.gettysburgghosttours.com/" target="_blank">
          Gettysburg Ghost Tours
         </a>
         ? There are all sorts of tours to choose from, including the Award-Winning Black Cat Tour, Xtreme Ghost Hunt and Intense Ghost Hunts, providing something for everyone  whether youre looking for a family-friendly trip or total scare-fest.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Marreros Guest House, Florida
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Built in 1889 by Francisco Marrero, Marreros Guest Mansion was a key element in the rich cigar makers torrid love triangle. Francisco built the mansion in
         <a href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/directory/664-key-west/0-travel-web-sites/view.html" target="_blank">
          Key West
         </a>
         with the aim of attracting his young lover, Enriquetta, to the island. His mission was successful, as his young love moved into the mansion with him and became his bride. Unfortunately, what Francisco didnt tell Enriquetta was that he was already married to a woman
         <a href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/directory/74-caribbean/0-travel-web-sites/view.html" target="_blank">
          in Cuba
         </a>
         . Following Franciscos death, his first wife heard about his second marriage and his lavish estate. She travelled to Key West, Florida with an army of lawyers which she used to banish Enriquetta and her children from the mansion.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enriquettas final words before leaving the mansion were I will always remain in spirit and its believed that she returned to the estate after her death. Guests and staff alike have reported feeling her presence all over the house, but room 18 is always where the strongest feelings are reported. Many guests who have stayed in this room have reported seeing Enriquetta as she entered the room, searching for her hairbrush whilst she got ready for bed. Shes most commonly seen sitting at the end of the bed whilst the smell of lavender perfume fills the air. If youd like to see the ghost of Enriquetta for yourself, consider staying in room 18 and make sure you take a hairbrush with you.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whilst youre visiting Key West, dont miss the chance to sign up for the
         <a href="http://www.keywestghostandmysteriestour.com/" target="_blank">
          Key West Ghost and Mysteries Tour
         </a>
         . One of the best haunted walking tours in the area, this activity takes place nightly and explores the most haunted and spooky parts of the Key West.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Lamothe Guest House, Louisiana
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Lamothe Guest House began life in 1829 when in was owned by Marie Virginia Lamothe. In 1833, she sold the
         <a href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/directory/726-new-orleans/0-travel-web-sites/view.html" target="_blank">
          New Orleans
         </a>
         property to her brother who built townhouses on the site in 1839. Twenty years later, Frenchmen Paul Rivera and Henry Parlange purchased the property and made numerous renovations, including the changing of the doors and shutters and the addition of a main entrance and hallway. Many other enhancements and additions have since been made to the property, although plenty of original architectural features  such as the ceiling timbers and iron fastenings  still remain in place today and give the property its character.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/lamothe-house-hotel-1.jpg">
          <img alt="Lamothe Guest House" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-481" height="376" src="http://www.wv-travel-directory.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/lamothe-house-hotel-1.jpg" title="Lamothe Guest House" width="500"/>
         </a>
         Guests and staff alike have reported seeing the ghostly figure of a young woman dressed in red who emerges from one of the guest rooms before suddenly disappearing. In addition to the lady in red, the ghostly laughter of children has also been heard in the dead of night, often followed by the sighting of their mother pacing up and down the hallways in search of her children. A great number of guests have caught the faces of ghosts on camera during their stay at the Lamothe Guest House, so if you want to take away a ghostly souvenir, make sure you bring your camera.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/6OwrA9jOEmM?rel=0" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </center>
        If you fancy turning your ghostly experience up a notch, sign up for one of
        <a href="http://www.hauntedhistorytours.com/" target="_blank">
         The Original New Orleans Ghost and Vampire Tours
        </a>
        . There is a huge variety of tours available throughout the year, including general ghost tours, cemetery tours, voodoo tours, underworld tours and vampire tours, guaranteeing something for every type of ghost hunter.
