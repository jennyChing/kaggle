       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Hot Ice Experiment
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        By
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://toysinthedryer.com/author/jamie/" rel="author">
           Jamie
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://toysinthedryer.com/hot-ice-experiment/#respond">
          Leave a Comment
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         You know we love
         <a href="http://toysinthedryer.com/tag/science-activities/" target="_blank">
          science activities
         </a>
         here on Toys In The Dryer. We especially love ones that use things we normally keep on hand in our house or kitchen. This hot ice experiment is one you can do if you have a little time as I guarantee you have the ingredients in your kitchen!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-5-4-22-2015-12-05-PM.png">
          <img alt="hot ice experiment" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5309" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-5-4-22-2015-12-05-PM.png?resize=450%2C599"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Youll need:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1 liter of distilled white vinegar
         </li>
         <li>
          4 tablespoons of baking soda
         </li>
         <li>
          a large sauce pan
         </li>
         <li>
          a bowl with a lid or a bowl with plastic wrap
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Does this experiment sound an awful lot like a
         <a href="http://toysinthedryer.com/volcano-in-a-bottle/" target="_blank">
          volcano experiment
         </a>
         ? It sure does, but in my opinion, the hot ice experiment is MUCH cooler.
        </p>
        <p>
         I must give you fair warning before I give you the directions for hot ice: Youll need time for this experiment as it takes a
         <em>
          minimum
         </em>
         of 2 hours to set up. Youll also need to be prepared for your house to smell like vinegar as you boil it. In fact, Hubby came home from work, took one whiff of the house and asked Nutty if we were doing science experiments that day!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-1-4-22-2015-1-50-PM.png">
          <img alt="Hot Ice Experiment" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5310" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-1-4-22-2015-1-50-PM.png?resize=450%2C338"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         1) Mix the vinegar and baking soda in your sauce pan.
        </h3>
        <p>
         SLOWLY mix them together or youll get the volcano effect and end up with the solution on the outside of your pan vs in it. You do NOT want to lose any mixture over the edge of your pan.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-2-4-22-2015-1-51-PM.png">
          <img alt="hot ice experiment" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5311" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-2-4-22-2015-1-51-PM.png?resize=450%2C338"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         2) Boil the solution over medium heat for about an hour.
        </h3>
        <p>
         You know your solution is done when there is about 100 ml left or when a thin film forms on the top. Dont worry if you over or under boil it. It can be fixed. Its better for your kids if you get it right the first time though
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         Your solution may turn a yellow color. Thats ok.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-3-4-22-2015-1-52-PM.png">
          <img alt="hot ice experiment" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5312" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-3-4-22-2015-1-52-PM.png?resize=450%2C338"/>
         </a>
        </h3>
        <h3>
         3) Cool the solution in the refrigerator
        </h3>
        <p>
         Pour your boiled solution into a new bowl and immediately cover to prevent further evaporation. If crystals form when you pour your liquid, add just enough vinegar to dissolved them then cover the solution and refrigerate.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-10-4-22-2015-12-07-PM.png">
          <img alt="hot ice experiment" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5314" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-10-4-22-2015-12-07-PM.png?resize=450%2C338"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         4) Pour the solution into a new bowl and watch the ice form!
        </h3>
        <p>
         Once completely cooled, pour your solution into a new bowl. It
         <em>
          should
         </em>
         immediately form ice when you pour it. If it doesnt, add a small sprinkle of baking soda to it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-5-4-22-2015-1-55-PM.png">
          <img alt="hot ice experiment" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5313" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/toysinthedryer.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Snapshot-5-4-22-2015-1-55-PM.png?resize=450%2C338"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         5) Notice your ice is hot!
        </h3>
        <p>
         Touch the ice you just formed. Notice something weird? Its hot! Thats why they call this the hot ice experiment!
        </p>
        <p>
         My girls thought this experiment was awesome. Even hubby thought it was really cool.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can heat up (boil) the ice and reuse the solution if you want. Just remember, if crystals form when you dont want them to, add more vinegar. If crystals dont form when you want them to, add more baking soda.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres the hot ice experiment in a video so you can see the ice form and listen to my girls (and husbands) reactions.
        </p>

        </p>

         This video
        </a>
        is also awesome, it is just so pretty!
