     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Tomorrow's Pioneers
       </b>
      </i>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      :
      <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
        
      </span>
      ; also
      <i>
       <b>
        The Pioneers of Tomorrow
       </b>
      </i>
      ) is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Children%27s_program" title="Children's program">
       children's program
      </a>
      , last broadcast on October 16, 2009 on the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palestinian_Authority" title="Palestinian Authority">
       Palestinian
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Hamas" title="Hamas">
       Hamas
      </a>
      -affiliated television station,
      <a href="/wiki/Al-Aqsa_TV" title="Al-Aqsa TV">
       Al-Aqsa TV
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      :
      <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
          
      </span>
      ). The show features young host Saraa Barhoum and her co-host, a large
      <a href="/wiki/Costumed_character" title="Costumed character">
       costumed animal
      </a>
      as they perform skits (or "scenes") and discuss life in Palestine in a talk show fashion with call-ins from children (typically of age 9-13 with some as young as 3).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edwards_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-edwards-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ramadan_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ramadan-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Presented in a children's educational format similar to such shows as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">
        Sesame Street
       </a>
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Barney_%26_Friends" title="Barney &amp; Friends">
        Barney &amp; Friends
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Tomorrow's Pioneers
      </i>
      is highly controversial as it contains
      <a href="/wiki/Antisemitism" title="Antisemitism">
       antisemitism
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Islamism" title="Islamism">
       Islamism
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Anti-Americanism" title="Anti-Americanism">
       anti-Americanism
      </a>
      , and other
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-Western" title="Anti-Western">
       anti-Western
      </a>
      themes.
     </p>
     <p>
      The show, brought to Western attention by pro-Israel organizations such as
      <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_Media_Watch" title="Palestinian Media Watch">
       Palestinian Media Watch
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MEMRI" title="MEMRI">
       MEMRI
      </a>
      , supposedly deals with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic" title="Islamic">
       Islamic
      </a>
      traditions and lifestyles, some as innocuous as the importance of daily prayers and drinking milk, but also advocates messages of Islamism such as "Resistance Jihad," and the loathing of
      <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">
       Israel
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Capitalism" title="Capitalism">
       the capitalist economic system
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">
       western world
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spiegel_1_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-spiegel_1-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      However, according to Palestinian Media Watch, two animal character co-hosts, the
      <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Mouse" title="Mickey Mouse">
       Mickey Mouse
      </a>
      clone Farfour and Nahoul the bee, were used to "champion violence, promote hatred of Israel and preach about world Islamic supremacy." Later in the show's narrative, the characters were, as the show described it, "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martyrdom_in_Islam" title="Martyrdom in Islam">
       martyred
      </a>
      ," with the deaths either directly or implicitly blamed on "The Jews, Israel, Israelis, and/or what they believed to be Zionists."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmw260208_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pmw260208-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The original host of Tomorrow's Pioneers and Farfour,
      <a href="/wiki/Hazim_Al-Sha%27arawi" title="Hazim Al-Sha'arawi">
       Hazim Al-Sha'arawi
      </a>
      , stated that it was his idea that Farfour be killed by an Israeli interrogator, adding that the show wanted to send a message through this character that would fit the reality of Palestinian life.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      El-Sharawi states, A child sees his neighbors killed, or blown up on the beach, and how do I explain this to a child that already knows? The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Israeli_occupation_of_Palestine" title="Israeli occupation of Palestine">
       [Israeli] occupation
      </a>
      is the reason; it creates the reality. I just organize the information for him.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Al-Aqsa TV deputy manager stated, that the program in fact is simply "about Palestinian kids express[ing] their feeling[s] regarding what they witness -- if it's [the] occupation it's about that..."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN1_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-CNN1-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The show is considered to be the spiritual successor to an earlier Hamas-broadcast children's radio program series entitled
      <i>
       Ovan and Branches
      </i>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      :
      <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
        
      </span>
      ). Broadcast weekly on Fridays and running for 85 minutes, the program is also moderated by Hazim Al-Sha'arawi.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Israeli sources have characterized it as "the 'most serious' of martyrdom operations."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ramadan_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ramadan-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attallah_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-attallah-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Arabic sources have said that the broadcasts of
      <i>
       Ovan and Branches
      </i>
      have been
      <a href="/wiki/Radio_jamming" title="Radio jamming">
       jammed
      </a>
      by Israel in the past.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attallah_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-attallah-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <p>
      Saraa Barhoum (
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      :
      <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
        
      </span>

      <a href="/wiki/Hijab" title="Hijab">
       hijab
      </a>
      who hosts
      <i>
       Tomorrow's Pioneers
      </i>
      . She was invited to host the show after entering a singing competition. The show's director, Hazim Al-Sha'arawi, has stated that Saraa was the ideal choice to present the show because "she has witnessed incursions, demolishing of houses, day and night shelling and random continuous firing." In an interview, Saraa revealed that "Israelis [had] hit next door to [her] house with a shell. [She] was wounded on [her] feet and [her] little brother Youssef was wounded in the legs."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edwards_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-edwards-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Despite the tenor of
      <i>
       Tomorrow's Pioneers
      </i>
      , Saraa has pointed out that "We are not terrorists. We do not support terrorism. We are normal people, but we are defending our homeland ... We, as Muslims, are against suicide bombers. We are against the death of civilians on all sides. We are only the enemy of those who took our land and kill us every day."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edwards_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-edwards-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>

        </span>

     <p>

      <i>
       Tomorrow's Pioneers
      </i>

      <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">
       music video
      </a>
      which was hosted for a time at the Palestinian PALN internet network.

         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      :
      <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
          
      </span>
      ) from her first album. The video features Saraa singing a plaintive song about being a mother in Palestine while walking through rubble. Images of gravely injured infants are displayed in the background.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_Barghouti" title="Mustafa Barghouti">
       Mustafa Barghouti
      </a>
      successfully lobbied to have the show shut down for a time although this ban was later lifted. Factors such as these lead to broadcast dates often being irregular.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Criticisms_and_responses">
       Criticisms and responses
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomorrow%27s_Pioneers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Criticisms and responses">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Criticisms concerning the content and methods employed in the broadcast of
      <i>
       Tomorrow's Pioneers
      </i>
      have come from a variety of sources. Israeli officials and critics from the
      <a href="/wiki/Anti-Defamation_League" title="Anti-Defamation League">
       Anti-Defamation League
      </a>
      have denounced the program as incendiary and outrageous in content.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ADL1_23-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ADL1-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Watchdog groups,
      <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_Media_Watch" title="Palestinian Media Watch">
       PMW
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MEMRI" title="MEMRI">
       MEMRI
      </a>
      have described the show as "anti-Israeli," and PMW has commented on the show's "champion[ing of] violence, promot[ion of] hatred of Israel and preach[ing] about world Islamic supremacy."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmw260208_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-pmw260208-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Within the Arabic community, critics have suggested that the show has potential to introduce bias in children at an age when they are unable to properly differentiate between political viewpoints.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75">
       <a href="#cite_note-75">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        75
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Harsher Arabic and Palestinian critics have suggested that the show is nothing short of an attempt at the indoctrination of children.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76">
       <a href="#cite_note-76">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        76
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On the other hand, defenders of the show have suggested that the show's notoriety stands as yet more proof of the political motivations behind the
      <a href="/wiki/Anti-Arabism" title="Anti-Arabism">
       anti-Arabist
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Islamophobia" title="Islamophobia">
       Islamophobic
      </a>
      subjection of Palestinians to the strictest scrutiny and subsequent generalizations of Arabs to support an improper negative stereotype. The argument has been advanced that "Israel is following all the Palestinians, large and small, and placing them under a microscope with a predefined concept of culture, media and religion. It only wants to catch up [Palestinians] and accuse them on themes of terrorism."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ramadan_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-ramadan-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Criticism of the show has been regarded as criticism of
      <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">
       Islam
      </a>
      as a religion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77">
       <a href="#cite_note-77">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        77
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Furthermore, proponents of the show claim that Saraa Barhoum is only an "innocent girl ... incapable of incitement."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afana_78-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-afana-78">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        78
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It has also been suggested by a variety of sources that, holding judgment aside, the show's content is simply reflective of the realities of life in the occupied territory.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ramadan_3-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-ramadan-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN1_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-CNN1-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afana_78-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-afana-78">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        78
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Relatedly, it is argued that
      <a href="/wiki/Zionism" title="Zionism">
       Zionist
      </a>
      critics in particular are disturbed by the fact that the show provides "Arab and Muslim children [with] the opportunity to participate in [a public forum context] to freely express their real feelings [regarding life in] Palestine."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afana_78-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-afana-78">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        78
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Defenders of the show have argued that the reason for the harsh criticism the show has received is that unlike many Arabic television stations Al-Aqsa TV reflects a view of Palestinian thought unfettered by Western social mores.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79">
       <a href="#cite_note-79">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        79
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The timing of certain critical publications has also been considered provocative.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afana_78-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-afana-78">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        78
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h4>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Removal_by_the_Palestinian_Ministry">
       Removal by the Palestinian Ministry
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomorrow%27s_Pioneers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Removal by the Palestinian Ministry">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h4>
     <p>
      After attracting international attention for its use of the
      <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Mouse" title="Mickey Mouse">
       Mickey Mouse
      </a>
      -lookalike, Farfour, Palestinian Information Minister
      <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_Barghouti" title="Mustafa Barghouti">
       Mustafa Barghouti
      </a>
      stated the use of the character was a "mistaken approach" and the program was pulled from Hamas-affiliated Al-Aqsa TV and placed under review at his ministrys request.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msnbc-ap_80-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-msnbc-ap-80">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        80
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Al-Aqsa TV board chairman
      <a href="/wiki/Fathi_Hamad" title="Fathi Hamad">
       Fathi Hamad
      </a>
      replied that the station would neither pull the program, nor change the subject matter. He stated that "[t]his campaign of criticism is part of a plan orchestrated by the West and the occupying power to attack Islam on the one hand and the Palestinian cause on the other."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFP1_81-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-AFP1-81">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        81
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-memriclip_82-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-memriclip-82">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        82
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      An Al-Aqsa TV representative responded to Barghouti's statements, saying that the station will continue to air the show and that "Barghouti misunderstood the issue."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN1_7-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-CNN1-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In a later interview with Al-Aqsa TV, Hamad explained that "the vicious campaign that is being waged by the enemies, and especially the Zionist enemy and American imperialism, is not new. It comes from the framework of the vicious campaign against our Palestinian people as an addition to the siege placed on our people. Therefore, we are not surprised by this. They want the Palestinian people to renounce its Islamic religion and belief. In response, we say that whenever our Palestinian people resorted to its religion, Allah supported it. Therefore, we will not renounce our faith and our belief, and we will move forward for the sake of Allah. We are holding to this religion because it is the secret of our victory. They try, as much as they can... Not just by waging their attack on the religion in this context, in order to prevent the Islamic upbringing of Palestinian children, but they also want this people to renounce all its Islamic foundation. They are the ones who called to remove the Jihad verses that appear in the Palestinian school curricula. Therefore, we cannot accept this."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Memri20070511_83-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Memri20070511-83">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        83
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h4>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_from_Disney">
       Criticism from Disney
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomorrow%27s_Pioneers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Criticism from Disney">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h4>
     <p>
      76-year-old
      <a href="/wiki/Diane_Disney_Miller" title="Diane Disney Miller">
       Diane Disney Miller
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Mouse" title="Mickey Mouse">
       Mickey Mouse
      </a>
      creator
      <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">
       Walt Disney
      </a>
      's last surviving child, commented to the press that, "What we're dealing with here is pure evil and you can't ignore that." She further commented that, "It's not just [about] Mickey, it's [about] indoctrinating children like this, teaching them to be evil. The world loves children, and this is just going against the grain of humanity."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84">
       <a href="#cite_note-84">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        84
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In a response letter by
      <i>
       Tomorrow's Pioneers
      </i>
      director, Fathi Hammad, said: "Before ... [anyone] object[s] to our [program], we are trying to install in our children's memories the interest in [the] lift[ing of] the siege on our people and [the end of] support for the usurper entity of our land and killing our children. ... [T]he Palestinian people continue to struggle not indifferent to such [as] are left with the executioner and victim."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85">
       <a href="#cite_note-85">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        85
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Nevertheless, the show's creators arranged the production of an episode shortly after in which Farfour was martyred off to be replaced by his bumble-bee brother, Nahoul. This arranged martyrdom came in direct response to actions by the Disney family.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86">
       <a href="#cite_note-86">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        86
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Several months later, Hamas television again made use of Disney characters, this time in a five-minute cartoon attacking
      <a href="/wiki/Fatah" title="Fatah">
       Fatah
      </a>
      . The clip depicted the competing movement and its former local leader
      <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Dahlan" title="Mohammed Dahlan">
       Mohammed Dahlan
      </a>
      as corrupt and anti-Islamic rats, while portraying Hamas as a confident lion based on the
      <a href="/wiki/Simba" title="Simba">
       Simba
      </a>
      character from the 1994 film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King" title="The Lion King">
        The Lion King
       </a>
      </i>
      . While Hamas TV executive Hazam Sharawi said the clip was pulled for revising, including removal of the Dahlan depiction, he said there were no plans to remove reference to
      <i>
       The Lion King
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87">
       <a href="#cite_note-87">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        87
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In September 2008, Fatah also began broadcasting a competing show targeting children on the Official
      <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_Satellite_Channel" title="Palestinian Satellite Channel">
       PA TV
      </a>
      network. This program also features use of Mickey and
      <a href="/wiki/Minnie_Mouse" title="Minnie Mouse">
       Minnie Mouse
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marcus_38-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-marcus-38">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        38
        <span>

        </span>

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

        edit

        ]

     <p>
      On April 1, 2008, Rep.
      <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Crowley" title="Joseph Crowley">
       Joseph Crowley
      </a>
      submitted House Resolution No. 1069 in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">
       United States House of Representatives
      </a>
      , a resolution "[c]ondemning the use of television programming by Hamas to indoctrinate hatred, violence, and anti-Semitism toward Israel in young Palestinian children."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88">
       <a href="#cite_note-88">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        88

         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The resolution discusses
      <i>
       Tomorrow's Pioneers
      </i>
      by name, specifically stating that:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       "Whereas Hamas uses their television network, Al-Aqsa TV, to air a children's show 'Tomorrow's Pioneers' to breed new terrorists through hatred for Israel and Western nations; ... Whereas in February 2008, Hamas used a Bugs Bunny look-alike to indoctrinate children by inciting them toward hatred and violence by telling children that he 'will finish off the Jews and eat them'; Whereas in May 2007, Hamas used a Mickey Mouse look-alike in the same children's program to teach terrorist doctrines to children; ... the House of Representatives condemns Hamas for using a children's television program to incite hatred, violence, and anti-Semitism toward Israel; and demands that Hamas immediately suspend all television programming that incites hatred, violence, and anti-Semitism toward Israel ..."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90">
        <a href="#cite_note-90">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         90
         <span>

     </blockquote>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Translation_controversy">
       Translation controversy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tomorrow%27s_Pioneers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Translation controversy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Several commentators, such as
      <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">
       CNN
      </a>
      's Arabic department, have claimed that the transcript of the April 13 show (2007) provided by
      <a href="/wiki/Middle_East_Media_Research_Institute" title="Middle East Media Research Institute">
       MEMRI
      </a>
      contains numerous translation errors and undue emphases.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian1_91-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian1-91">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        91
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Brian_Whitaker" title="Brian Whitaker">
       Brian Whitaker
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">
       Middle East
      </a>
      editor for the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guardian_newspaper" title="Guardian newspaper">
       Guardian newspaper
      </a>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">
       UK
      </a>
      ), wrote in a blog for the newspaper that in the translation of the video, showing Farfour eliciting political comments from a young girl named Sanabel, the MEMRI transcript misrepresents the segment where he asks what she will do, by attributing a sentence said by Farfour, ("I'll shoot"), to the child while ignoring the child's actual reply ("I'm going to draw a picture").
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian1_91-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian1-91">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        91
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Whitaker further criticized MEMRI's translation. He and others commented that a statement uttered by the same child, ("We're going to [or want to] resist"), had been given an unduly aggressive interpretation by MEMRI as ("We want to fight"). Also, where MEMRI translated the girl as saying the highly controversial remark ("We will annihilate the Jews"), Whitaker and others, including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">
       Arabic
      </a>
      speakers used by CNN, insist that based on careful listening to the low quality
      <a href="/wiki/Video_clip" title="Video clip">
       video clip
      </a>
      , the girl is variously interpreted as saying, "The Jews [will] shoot[] us"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian1_91-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian1-91">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        91
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      or "The Jews are killing us."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNNtranscript_92-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-CNNtranscript-92">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        92
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Other sources have also pointed out that MEMRI's translation "I will commit martyrdom" should more accurately have been "I'll become a martyr"  a passive statement rather than an active/aggressive threat.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93">
       <a href="#cite_note-93">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        93
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      CNN's
      <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Beck" title="Glenn Beck">
       Glenn Beck
      </a>
      had planned to run MEMRI's translation on his radio programme but was stopped by his producer. Beck then invited
      <a href="/wiki/Yigal_Carmon" title="Yigal Carmon">
       Yigal Carmon
      </a>
      , a former Colonel of
      <a href="/wiki/Israel_Defense_Forces" title="Israel Defense Forces">
       IDF
      </a>
      Intelligence and founder and President of MEMRI, on to the programme where Carmon denounced CNN's Arabic translation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian1_91-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian1-91">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        91
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      MEMRI defends their translation of the show, Yigal Carmon declared, "Yes, we stand by the translation by the very words, by the context, by the syntax, and every measure of the translation."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNNtranscript_92-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-CNNtranscript-92">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        92
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
