       <header class="article-header single-header">
        <h1 class="h2 entry-title single-title title-container non-thumbnailed" itemprop="headline">
         <div class="title-text-container">
          <span class="title-text">
           25 Ways to Recharge Your Twitter Experience
          </span>
         </div>
        </h1>
        <p class="byline vcard">
         By
         <span class="author">
          <a href="http://blog.shoutem.com/author/ivan-brezak-brkan/" rel="author" title="Posts by Ivan Brezak Brkan">
           Ivan Brezak Brkan
          </a>
         </span>
         on
         <time class="updated" datetime="2009-07-19" pubdate="">
          19/07/2009
         </time>
         <br/>
        </p>
       </header>
       <!-- end article header -->
       <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_510" style="width: 460px">
         <img alt="Recharge Your Twitter Experience" class="size-full wp-image-510" height="113" src="http://blog2.shoutem.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/recharge_front.jpg" title="Recharge Your Twitter Experience" width="450"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Recharge Your Twitter Experience
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Microblogging. Its fun, its useful. Twitter, ShoutEm and other microblogging platforms are fast becoming not just another way to pass the time, but also tools that we use to collaborate with each other on a daily basis. However, it can get tedious when you do it daily.
         <strong>
          Here are 25 simple ways you can get your microblogging habits back on track
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <h2>
         Get out and micro-mingle
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_505" style="width: 460px">
         <img alt="Mingle!" class="size-full wp-image-505" height="208" src="http://blog2.shoutem.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/recharge_3mingle.jpg" title="Mingle!" width="450"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Mingle!
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Every day, the same old followers and fans. With Twitter and every other microblogging network on the planet growing fast, theres a lot of new people you need to tweet with. Get out your 140-character-charm and get going:
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Follow one new user at a time
        </h3>
        <p>
         Theres no need to hurry and follow dozens of new faces immediately. Take your time. Browse your friends follower lists and find people who genuinely interest you.
         <strong>
          Follow and get to know them
         </strong>
         . Only then you should follow others.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. Un-follow casual spammers
        </h3>
        <p>
         You know them. You might even be a casual spammer yourself. Taking about yourself, your projects and what youre doing all the time. Bragging isnt nice, so dont feel bad for un-following people and companies who only tweet about themselves.
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Follow your real life neighbours
        </h3>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Twitter users in your country, town, neighbourhood
         </strong>
         ? You might know a lot of them, but if you dont, there are a few simple ways to find them. The simplest way is to use
         <a href="http://www.search.twitter.com">
          Twitter Search
         </a>
         .
         <a href="http://www.tweelowhood.com">
          Twellowhood
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.twitterlocal.net">
          Twitterlocal
         </a>
         both let you find Twitter users next to you in a few clicks. Do you use an iPhone?
         <a href="http://www.atebits.com/tweetie-iphone/">
          Tweetie
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://tapulous.com/twinkle/">
          Twinkle
         </a>
         and other Twitter applications offer locating nearby Twitter users!
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Find a topic and follow the experts
        </h3>
        <p>
         Interested in web design?
         <a href="http://twitter.com/smashingmag">
          Follow Vitaly of Smashing Magazine
         </a>
         ! Want to start a blog?
         <a href="http://www.twitter.com/problogger">
          Darren Problogger Rowse
         </a>
         is your guy! Love horses?
         <a href="http://www.horsetweet.com">
          Horsetweet
         </a>
         is a must You can
         <strong>
          find experts and insiders
         </strong>
         on almost any topic on Twitter or even on a specialized microblogging network.
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Follow your enemies
        </h3>
        <p>
         You might not agree with them, but their arguments could be valid. The great thing about the web is that you choose what you want to see. Unfortunately,
         <strong>
          the flip side is that we tend to ignore people we dont agree with
         </strong>
         . Why not
         <a href="http://blog.shoutem.com/2009/05/10/twitter-web-interview-andrew-keen/">
          follow Andrew Keen
         </a>
         , the Anti Christ of Silicon Valley? You might end up learning something new.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Tweak your tools
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_506" style="width: 460px">
         <img alt="Tweak Your Twitter Tools" class="size-full wp-image-506" height="194" src="http://blog2.shoutem.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/recharge_4tools.jpg" title="Tweak Your Twitter Tools" width="450"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Tweak Your Twitter Tools
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         On the PC, Mac or mobile, weve found ways to microblog anywhere and anytime. With the hundreds of applications that help us tweet, it might just be time to ease up. Shut down? Check it out:
        </p>
        <h3>
         6. Remove your desktop Twitter client
        </h3>
        <p>
         No, you dont need it. Its a distraction  admit it. Oh, you still have notifications turned on? Including sound notifications? Maybe thats the reason your work is suffering.
         <strong>
          Turn off your desktop Twitter client, uninstall it
         </strong>
         . After the initial shock, doesnt that feel better?
        </p>
        <h3>
         7. Use the web interface
        </h3>
        <p>
         Hey, we didnt say you should quit Twitter. You might have not used the web interface since 2006., but its still here. Weird, isnt it. So simple, and it actually works!
        </p>
        <h3>
         8. Use your mobile phone
        </h3>
        <p>
         iPhone, Nokia or any other mobile phone  you can tweet with it!
         <strong>
          Use the mobile interface or an iPhone application
         </strong>
         , or even (gasp!) send an update via SMS!
        </p>
        <h3>
         9. Check out some Twitter apps
        </h3>
        <p>
         Theres a lot of them around, if you havent noticed.
         <a href="http://www.twittervision.com">
          Twittervision
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.twitterfeed.com">
          Twitterfeed
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.tweetscan.com">
          Tweetscan
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.twistori.com">
          Twistori
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.twitpic.com">
          Twitpic
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.twitscoop.com">
          Twitscoop
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.twittercounter.com">
          Twittercounter
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.tweetlater.com">
          Tweetlater
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.hahlo.com">
          Hahlo
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.twitthis.com">
          TwitThis
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.tweetag.com">
          Tweetag
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://ww.themattinator.com">
          The Mattinator
         </a>
         , TwitLinks,
        </p>
        <h3>
         10. Check out another microblogging platform
        </h3>
        <p>
         Twitter isnt the only microblogging platform out there. Hosted or downloadable, try
         <a href="http://www.shoutem.com">
          creating your own microblogging network
         </a>
         . Why not
         <a href="http://www.shoutem.com">
          try out ShoutEm
         </a>
         ?
        </p>
        <h2>
         The time to tweet is now!
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_507" style="width: 460px">
         <img alt="The Time to Tweet is Now" class="size-full wp-image-507" height="217" src="http://blog2.shoutem.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/recharge_5time.jpg" title="The Time to Tweet is Now" width="450"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The Time to Tweet is Now
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         If youre tired of Twitter, youre probably spending too much time tweeting. Maybe youre spending not enough time tweeting? In any case, heres what you need to do:
        </p>
        <h3>
         11. Schedule when youre going to tweet
        </h3>
        <p>
         The best way to
         <strong>
          get more stuff done is to limit the time you have to get it done
         </strong>
         . The same could be said for tweeting. Schedule a period when youre going to reply to all your DMs and replies, as well as schedule future tweets. This will keep you focused and save valuable minutes you could have been wasting during the rest of the day.
        </p>
        <h3>
         12. Schedule your tweets
        </h3>
        <p>
         Scheduling individual tweets is far more easier with web applications such as
         <a href="http://www.cotweet.com">
          CoTweet
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.tweetlater.com">
          Tweetlater
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://www.hootsuite.com">
          Hootsuite
         </a>
         . In the period youve dedicated to microblogging,
         <strong>
          schedule everything you want to tweet
         </strong>
         . It can be things to go out through out the day, the week, maybe even the month.
        </p>
        <h3>
         13. Dont use Twitter for instant messaging
        </h3>
        <p>
         Its so easy to talk to your friends via replies on Twitter. Multiuser instant messaging? Yep.
         <strong>
          Dont do it
         </strong>
         . Chatting is nice, but you have MSN, Skype, AIM, ICQ and a ton of other services that let you do that more efficiently.
        </p>
        <h3>
         14. Take a break from Twitter
        </h3>
        <p>
         Feeling burned out? Take a break from Twitter for a few days. If you rely on Twitter generating traffic to your website or blog, schedule links for the days youre gone.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Post something a little different
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align:center;">
         <img alt="Post Something Different" class="size-full wp-image-508 aligncenter" height="94" src="http://blog2.shoutem.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/recharge_6different.jpg" title="Post Something Different" width="472"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You might be spending all your time tweeting what youre doing. You might be tweeting with your friends. Whatever youre doing, its a good bet its getting really old, really fast. Shake it up:
        </p>
        <h3>
         15. Post a link
        </h3>
        <p>
         Youre on the web! Its interesting! Post that funny article! Share that video or inspiring photo with your friends Forget e-mail chain mails and tweet it.
        </p>
        <h3>
         16. Livetweet an event
        </h3>
        <p>
         Havent you always wanted to liveblog a conference or concert? Well, heres your chance to do it with your microblog. Put up a hashtag (#eventname) and start tweeting. Oh, and dont forget to tell everyone when youre going to do it.
        </p>
        <h3>
         17. Help other Twitter users
        </h3>
        <p>
         While organizations like
         <a href="http://www.mozilla.com">
          Mozilla
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.comcast.com">
          Comcas
         </a>
         t are going out of their way to solve our problems related to their industries on Twitter, why cant we do the same? Youre a PR professional? Setup a custom search for PR! Live CSS? Do the same for web standards.
         <strong>
          Help your fellow tweep and build your credibility
         </strong>
         in the same time.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Get real with Twitter
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_504" style="width: 460px">
         <img alt="Tweetup Zagreb" class="size-full wp-image-504" height="338" src="http://blog2.shoutem.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/recharge_2getreal.jpg" title="Tweetup Zagreb" width="450"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Tweetup Zagreb
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         We might spend a lot of time online, but we do live in the real world. Why not get real with microblogging and
        </p>
        <h3>
         18. Attend a Tweetup
        </h3>
        <p>
         Tweetups are Twitter meetups. With Twitter users meeting all around the world, from San Francisco to Zagreb, why dont you meet your fellow tweep?
        </p>
        <h3>
         19. Organize a Tweetup
        </h3>
        <p>
         No Tweetups in your area?
         <a href="http://mashable.com/2009/02/25/tweetup">
          Organize your first Tweetup
         </a>
         ! Get the local Twitter users together for beer and conversations of more than 140 characters.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Get serious about microblogging
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_511" style="width: 460px">
         <img alt="Get Serious" class="size-full wp-image-511" height="172" src="http://blog2.shoutem.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/recharge_8pro.jpg" title="Get Serious" width="450"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Get Serious
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Microblogging is a game to some, and a job to others. With Twitter as well as niche microblogging networks fast becoming more and more popular, leverage their popularity:
        </p>
        <h3>
         20. Become a professional Twitter user
        </h3>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Lots of companies are looking for social media professionals
         </strong>
         to get them on the Twitter bandwagon. Why not
         <a href="http://jobs.mashable.com/a/jbb/find-jobs">
          find
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.crunchboard.com/jobs/]">
          one
         </a>
         and go pro with your microblogging know-how?
        </p>
        <h3>
         21. Make a change, champion a cause
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.charitywater.org">
          Charity Water
         </a>
         raised over $250.000 with the
         <a href="http://twestival.com/">
          Twestival
         </a>
         events. Charities have been among the first to realize the power of social media, so why not retrace their steps and raise awareness for charities and causes you support?
        </p>
        <h2>
         Cure microblogging block
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_509" style="width: 460px">
         <img alt="Cure Your Microblogging Block" class="size-full wp-image-509" height="101" src="http://blog2.shoutem.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/recharge_7ideas.jpg" title="Cure Your Microblogging Block" width="450"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Cure Your Microblogging Block
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Stuck on what to tweet? Dont worry, there are actually a couple of ways to get inspired:
        </p>
        <h3>
         22. Be inspired by your fellow Twitter user
        </h3>
        <p>
         Hopefully, youve added a couple of new people to your stream while reading this guide. Now let yourself be inspired by them!
         <strong>
          Follow artists, musicians, writers, journalists,
         </strong>
         Follow people who practice what they preach and whose thoughts on various topics should inspire your own.
        </p>
        <h3>
         23. Be inspired by bloggers
        </h3>
        <p>
         The few, the proud, the bloggers. They were here before the whole microblogging craze. Interestingly,
         <a href="http://www.twitter.com/ev">
          Ev Williams
         </a>
         (co-founder of Twitter) also co-founded Blogger, which as eventually acquired by Google. Blog. Microblog. Anyways,
         <strong>
          blogs are still hot, so dont ignore them
         </strong>
         . In the worse case, theyll give you something to tweet about.
        </p>
        <h3>
         24. Build on the ideas of others
        </h3>
        <p>
         Lots of Twitter applications are trying to power Twitter conversations. Someone starts a topic, others reply and in no time at all  theres a new trend. Hopefully, well see a lot less spam in the trending topics and a lot more of the true conversations that you can participate in, that are going on as we tweet.
        </p>
        <h3>
         25. Give credit where credit is due
        </h3>
        <p>
         Im not just talking about crediting your retweets, but also participating in
         <a href="http://search.twitter.com/search?q=followfriday">
          #followfridays
         </a>
         and other events that highlight your favorite microbloggers. Why?
         <strong>
          People like recommendations
         </strong>
         . By recommending others, you yourself will get noticed. In any case, recommend people that are doing good stuff. Its nice.
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://blog.shoutem.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats it. Use all of the tips or just some of them. Take a break or change what youre doing, but dont stop the tweeting. Microblogging is just getting started!
         <strong>
          What are your tips
         </strong>
         ?
        </p>
       </section>
