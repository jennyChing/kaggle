         Advice For Scheduling Your Grand Canyon Bus Trip
        </legend>

           Tracy Dee

        <div class="span-14 mt1">
         <p>
          Coach tours are one of the cheapest ways to see Grand Canyon National Park. Just be sure to compare all the tours and options so you get the best value for your money.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Starting Point
          <br/>
          <br/>
          First, you need to figure out what city you will begin your tour from. You can take a bus tour to the canyon every day of the year, even holidays, from Las Vegas and Phoenix. If you depart from Vegas, you get to choose between going to the West Rim or the South Rim, but you do not have a choice if you leave from Phoenix, you will automatically go to the South Rim.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Las Vegas
          <br/>
          <br/>
          When you go to the West Rim, you can add upgrades to your tour. One of the most exciting is a bus trip that comes with a helicopter ride to the canyon floor and a float trip down the Colorado River. Of course, the package that includes a walk on the glass Grand Canyon Skywalk is very popular too.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Many people prefer to take the bus trip to the South Rim from Vegas. They're fun, all-day experiences (it takes 5 1/2 hours just to get to the South Rim and another 5 1/2 just to get back to Vegas) that give you up to three hours inside the National Park-enough time to see all the main highlights. You can also take a chopper ride at the South Rim and view the canyon from the air.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Coach Trips From Phoenix
          <br/>
          <br/>
          If you leave from Phoenix, you will get to stop at Sedona, Arizona, which is home to amazing red rock formations. After that, the bus heads to the South Rim, which is so scenic it is often called the 'real' Grand Canyon. Tour passengers have ample time to explore the rim and can add the optional rim-to-rim chopper flight.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          All-Inclusive
          <br/>
          <br/>
          The all-inclusive coach trips come with lunch, tour guide, shuttle service to your hotel, Park fees, and taxes. You don't have to worry about anything but your personal comfort so bring along your sunblock, cell phone, camera, and snacks if you want.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Touring By Van
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Motor coaches hold 40 or more passengers. If you don't like being around that many people, you should book a van tour instead. These tour vans are very comfortable and only hold about 10 people. Since there are less people on these van tours, you get more personal attention too.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Book Early
          <br/>
          <br/>
          I've heard stories about folks (traveling alone) who get away with booking their tours 24 hours ahead, but why risk it? I always suggest booking a week before the date you want. By doing this you can almost guarantee you'll snag a seat. Plus, it will make the last few days before your trip a lot let stressful.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Summing Up
          <br/>
          <br/>
          These tips should help you figure out which tour is best and help you get the best price. First, figure out if you will leave from Vegas or Phoenix. If you pick Vegas you'll need to decide which rim you prefer. I always recommend the South Rim for folks who love magnificent landscapes, but I recommend the West for people drawn to thrills and excitement. Lock down your tour ahead of time. And be sure to buy your seats online. Buying your seats directly on the tour operator's website ensures you get the lowest rate available and the Internet discount too!
         </p>
