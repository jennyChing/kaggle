      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Is there something you know how to do but you are surprised to find out that others have not yet been enlightened? Or maybe a friend does something in front of you that totally blows your mind? For example, I just found out that some people use a fork doskewer an Oreo and dip it in milk. What? I can now dunk and read with ease. Others continually grow green onions on their windowsill (completely awesome). Layered drink how-to is just one of the pieces of useless knowledge that fills my head.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UrFTo9j21dI/T-sbLO-ym0I/AAAAAAAABlk/2wmVkg6iNRo/s1600/Layered+Drinks+July+Fourth+Edition+007+(2)text.jpg">
       <img alt="Layered Drink Tutorial from @katrinaskitchen" border="0" class="aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="480" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UrFTo9j21dI/T-sbLO-ym0I/AAAAAAAABlk/2wmVkg6iNRo/s640/Layered+Drinks+July+Fourth+Edition+007+(2)text.jpg" style="border: 0px;" title="Layered Drink Tutorial from @katrinaskitchen" width="640"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      You can layer drinks in any way you like- the secret is in the sugar content. This works well for red, white, and blue but you can really do this with any color combination. The other secret to success with layered drinks is plenty of ice. After you add your bottom layer fill the glass to the top with ice and pour the second layer slowly and directly on top of an ice cube.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-TDRIBm2eJrY/T-th3mtpi-I/AAAAAAAABlw/qWYUdWKWe5Y/s1600/Layered+Drinks+July+Fourth+Edition+034+(2)wm.jpg">
       <img alt="Layered Drink Tutorial from @katrinaskitchen" border="0" class="aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-TDRIBm2eJrY/T-th3mtpi-I/AAAAAAAABlw/qWYUdWKWe5Y/s640/Layered+Drinks+July+Fourth+Edition+034+(2)wm.jpg" style="border: 0px;" title="Layered Drink Tutorial from @katrinaskitchen" width="480"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      These would be a really fun and easy addition to any picnic you are attending or hosting for the Fourth of July holiday this weekend. I had a few friends ask if they could add alcohol and I dont see why not as long as you note the sugar content in your layers. Since I have the kidlets running around we kept these alcohol-free.
     </p>
     <p>
      But looking back at the photos you can judge for yourself.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-U5h8a9vCkak/T-tlku-t5SI/AAAAAAAABmA/l2TUXBNOGVE/s1600/July+4th+Layered+Drinks+In+Katrina's+Kitchen.jpg">
       <img alt="Layered Drink Tutorial from @katrinaskitchen" border="0" class="aligncenterpib-nohover" height="448" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-U5h8a9vCkak/T-tlku-t5SI/AAAAAAAABmA/l2TUXBNOGVE/s640/July+4th+Layered+Drinks+In+Katrina's+Kitchen.jpg" style="border: 0px;" title="Layered Drink Tutorial from @katrinaskitchen" width="640"/>
      </a>
     </p>

       <h2 itemprop="name">
        July 4th Layered Drinks Recipe
       </h2>
       <p class="printbutton">
        <a class="print" href="http://www.inkatrinaskitchen.com/july-4th-layered-drinks/print/" title="Print Recipe">
         <img alt="Print" class="pib-nohover" src="http://cf.inkatrinaskitchen.com/wp-content/themes/inkatrinaskitchen/images/print.png"/>
         Print
        </a>
       </p>
       <div class="time">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Yield:
         </strong>
         <span itemprop="recipeYield">
          3 cups
         </span>
        </p>
        10 minutes

10 minutes
       </div>
       <div class="summary" itemprop="description" style="clear:left">
        <p>
         Layered drinks in red, white, and blue- perfect for 4th of July! Non-alcoholic and kid-friendly for your next party!
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3 id="ingredients">
        Ingredients:
       </h3>
       <div class="ingredients">
        <ul>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           1 Cup Red CranApple juice
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           1 Cup White Sobe Pina Colada flavored drink
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           1 Cup Blue G2 Gatorade
          </span>
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           Ice
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
       <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
        Directions:
       </h3>
       <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
        <ol>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           Fill your glass 1/3 of the way full with CranApple juice.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           Fill to the top with ice.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           Slowly pour the remaining drinks directly on top of a piece of ice.
          </span>
         </li>
        </ol>
       </div>
       <div class="notes">
        <h3>
         Notes:
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           You MUST pour SLOWLY, DIRECTLY over ice :)
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
       <div class="source">
        <p>
         <em>
          Original recipe and images In Katrina's Kitchen
         </em>
        </p>
