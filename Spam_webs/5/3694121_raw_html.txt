      <h1>
       That's Doctor Jimmy Page to you
      </h1>
      <header>
       <h2>
        Signs instrument for upcoming GuilFest
       </h2>
       <p class="article_info">
        <span itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
         <a class="author" href="/author/joe-bosso">
          <span itemprop="name">
           Joe Bosso
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
        <time data-seconds="1214524500" datetime="2008-6-26" itemprop="datePublished" pubdate="" value="2008-6-26">
         June 26, 2008, 23:55 UTC
        </time>
       </p>
      </header>
      <div id="article-text">
       <p>
        <strong>
         For years he's been hailed as a genius and a rock god, but now Led Zeppelin guitarist
         <a href="http://www.jimmypageonline.com/">
          Jimmy Page
         </a>
         can be called something else: doctor.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        On June 20, the
        <a href="http://portal.surrey.ac.uk/portal/page?_pageid=799,1&amp;_dad=portal&amp;_schema=PORTAL">
         University of Surrey
        </a>
        conferred the honorary degree of Doctor of the University to Jimmy Page for services to the music industry. The guitarist attended the ceremony at Guildford Cathedral. Afterwards, Page signed a guitar which will be given away in a competition at the upcoming
        <a href="http://www.guilfest.co.uk/2008/">
         GuilFest
        </a>
        festival. Surrey's involvement with GuilFest this year celebrates its innovative music programmes that reflect the diversity of contemporary musical practice.
       </p>
       <p>
        This was not Page's first trip to the famed institution - on October 25, 1968, Led Zeppelin played their first gig under that name (having ditched the moniker The New Yardbirds) at the University of Surrey.
       </p>
       <p>
        Although Page is certainly no stranger to picking up platinum albums and music awards, lately he's been the recipient of honors of a different stripe: In 2005 he was awarded an OBE for his charity work with the children of Brazil's slums, and was also made an honorary citizen of Rio De Janeiro.
       </p>
