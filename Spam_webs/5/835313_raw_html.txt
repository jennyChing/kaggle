         <p>
          Aaaand the crystal clear, blue waters of the tropic continue to amaze me! This time; Neguanje, yet another fantastic beach in Colombia and part of Tayrona National Nature Park. We didnt go here during our 7-day camp in Tayrona as it is very far from the other main beaches but drove here with our friends from Santa Marta a few days
          <a href="http://discoveringice.com/travels/south-america/The-One-Place-You-Must-Visit-In-Colombia-Tayrona-National-Natural-Park.html">
           after returning from Tayrona National Park

         </p>

        <p>
         Neguanje, the biggest bay in
         <a href="http://discoveringice.com/travels/south-america-colombia/the-ultimate-guide-to-tayrona-national-natural-park-santa-marta-colombia.html" target="_blank" title="The Ultimate Guide to Tayrona National Natural Park, Colombia">
          Tayrona
         </a>
         , is one of the lesser-visited beaches of the region. The entrance to the beach is about a 45-minute drive from Santa Marta and the winding, mountainous road is another 1 hour almost! The stunning views from the cliffs and mountainsides, of the below ocean helps pass the time however. There are many parking spots along the way for you to stop and enjoy the views, the best one being the beach of Seven Waves (La Playa Siete Olas) and the brilliantly named Mother-In-Law-Slide (El Tobogan De Las Suegras), a steep incline in the cliff which plummets to the ocean below.
        </p>

        <p>
         As Neguanje is part of Tayrona National Nature Park, we had to pay entrance fees again ($35000 COP/20 USD for foreigners, $13000/7 USD for Colombians). My Spanish is far from being at the standard to pass myself as a local just yet. plus the milky white skin is a clear giveaway! The price is pretty hefty for foreigners, especially for just a day trip to a beach but the beaches of Tayrona are spectacular and if you can afford it, Neguanje is well worth it.
        </p>

        <p>
         The water is one of the nicest Ive been in so far. Its so clean that you can see every detail on the ocean floor, which consists mainly of silky soft sand and little else. The waves can get pretty big at times so we made use of them by having human wave surfing competitions.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_510" style="width: 600px">
         <a href="http://discoveringice.com/travels/south-america/neguanje-the-biggest-beach-bay-in-tayrona-national-nature-park-colombia.html/attachment/neguanje-beach-perfect-swimming-water/" rel="attachment wp-att-510">

         </a>

          Neguanje beach - perfect swimming water

        <p>
         There is a nearby beach, Crystal Beach or Playa Cristal, reachable by boat where you can enjoy snorkeling and diving in the unique and diverse corals, one of the most important ecological spots in the entire Caribbean Coast.
        </p>
