    </h1>

     <p>
      President Obamas handling of the beheading of American journalist James Foley at the hand of ISIS terrorists just might prove to be the straw that broke the camels back. Immediately following the Wednesday press conference in which he condemned ISIS actions, the President hit the links at Vineyard Golf Club in Marthas Vineyard.
     </p>

     <p>
      Can that get any worse, Pat asked on radio this morning. Just the appearance of that is horrific. But he couldnt care less. He could not care less. And its just despicable to me. Had that been Bush talking about something like that and then going immediately to a golf course, they would have just berated him.
     </p>
     <p>
      Since Obama jetted to Marthas Vineyard earlier this month, liberals have chastised those who have expressed concern over the president of the United States being on vacation in a time where ISIS is exerting more force in Iraq, Israel and Gaza are locked in a deadly conflict, and  right here at home  Ferguson, Missouri is literally on fire in the aftermath of the shooting death of 18-year-old Michael Brown.
     </p>
     <p>
      But even those Obama sympathizers were at a loss for words on Wednesday.
      <a href="http://www.ijreview.com/2014/08/169897-isis-terrified-of-obama-brief-speech-and-golf-course-retreat/#PrettyPhoto[169897]/5/ ">
       The Independent Journal Review put together a collection
      </a>
      of some of the tweets that emerged once it was confirmed President did indeed go golfing.
     </p>
     <p>
      CBS News Major Garrett was among the first to report the Presidents whereabouts:
     </p>
     <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">

       According to WH travel pool w/POTUS, the president arrived at Vineyard Golf Club at 1:05 p.m., following statement on

        #ISIS
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
        Major Garrett (@MajorCBS)
       <a href="https://twitter.com/MajorCBS/statuses/502142730425081857">
        August 20, 2014
       </a>
      </p>
     </blockquote>

      Not long after, the New York Times Ross Douthat chimed in:
     </p>
     <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
      <p>
       Struggling to reconcile my belief that critiques of presidential hobbies/vacations are stupid with my annoyance at Obama's golfing today.
