           <h1 class="post-title">
            50 Mouth Watering Breakfasts From Around The World
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/author/m-mkmishra/" rel="author" title="Posts by Amanda Saylor">
             Amanda Saylor
            </a>
             May 19, 2014
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">

            <div class="entry-inner">
             <p>
              <strong>
               1. A Turkish breakfast  this normally consists of different cheeses, as well as butter, olives, eggs, tomatoes, cucumbers, jam, honey, and spicy meat.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/50.jpg">
               <img alt="50" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-348" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/50.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <span id="more-347">
               </span>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               2. Israeli Breakfast  Fresh bread, a variety of hard and soft cheeses, fresh juice, olives, jam and butter can all be found on the Israeli breakfast plate.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/49.jpg">
               <img alt="49" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-349" height="360" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/49.jpg" width="540"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               3. Breakfast van de Netherlands  Apple pancakes served with a dark syrup called stroop is a national favorite
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/48.jpg">
               <img alt="48" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-350" height="361" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/48.jpg" width="540"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               4. Polish Breakfast  locally known as Jajecznica, this traditional Polish breakfast consists of scrambled eggs covered with slices of specially prepared kielbasa and two potato pancakes.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/47.jpg">
               <img alt="47" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-351" height="525" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/47-700x525.jpg" width="700"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               5. Quick Spanish breakfast  Pan a la Catalana, or Pan con Tomate, is a simple and popular breakfast dish in Spain. It consists of bread rubbed with fresh garlic and ripe tomato, followed by a quick dash of olive oil and salt, and then topped with cheese, ham or sausage.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/46.jpg">
               <img alt="46" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-352" height="427" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/46.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               6. Moroccan breakfast  different breads with a variety of jams, cheese or butter to spread on top.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/451.jpg">
               <img alt="45" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-353" height="427" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/451.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               7. A healthy Hawaiian breakfast  Lots of delicious fruit, and a nice bagel with some cream cheese for that extra feeling of fullness. This is a quick, simple breakfast that packs a punch.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/441.jpg">
               <img alt="44" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-354" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/441.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               8. Swedish breakfast  Pannkakor is a pancake made from batter and fried on both sides  much like a crepe. It is served with a rich and sweet, fruity filling.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/431.jpg">
               <img alt="43" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-355" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/431.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               9. Icelandic breakfast cuisine  It might not look that magnificent, but the Hafragrautur, or oatmeal, is favored in Iceland for good reason: its a great way to warm yourself up in the cold mornings. Its usually served with brown sugar and a few raisins or nuts on top.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/421.jpg">
               <img alt="42" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-356" height="375" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/421.jpg" width="500"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               10. Breakfast in Portugal  croissants and coffee. A savory morning delight.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/411.jpg">
               <img alt="41" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-357" height="383" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/411.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               11. Breakfast in Australia  its called Vegemite, and apparently the Aussies love it. So much so that a jar of these can often be found in a travelling Aussies backpack!
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/40.jpg">
               <img alt="40" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-358" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/40.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               12. A Brazilian breakfast  a tasty selection of meats, cheeses and bread. A very simple and popular breakfast.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/39.jpg">
               <img alt="39" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-359" height="427" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/39.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               13. An Italian breakfast  Cappuccino and croissant. The italians favor this stylish breakfast, its quick and can be had on the go.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/38.jpg">
               <img alt="38" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-360" height="359" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/38.jpg" width="500"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               14. A Welsh breakfast  Hot bubbling cheese spread over toast. Absolutely delicious.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/37.jpg">
               <img alt="37" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-361" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/37.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong style="color: #3a3531;">
               15. Breakfast in Denmark 
              </strong>
              <span style="color: #3a3531;">
               On a Danes breakfast plate youll often find rye bread, cheeses, salami, ham, pt, honey, jam and sometimes even thin plates of chocolate. It came as a bit of a shock to me but my research has shown that bacon is not actually that popular! Dun dun durrrh. How can this be?
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="Danish breakfast" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-362" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/36.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               16. A Philippines breakfast  its all about the local fruits here. Mangoes are popular fare to keep you regular. As for keeping your energy up rice is the top choice, or the little sausages, known as longganisa, you can see above. When fried with salt and garlic cloves its known as sinangag. The sinangag is then combined with eggs, meats and beans and bobs your uncle, fannys your aunt, a delicious Philippine breakfast is born.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="35" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-363" height="352" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/351.jpg" width="528"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               17. An Alaskan breakfast  featuring reindeer meat and an egg nestled on a pancake. Poor old Rudolph, he wont be able to join in any reindeer games now, will he?
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="34" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-364" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/341.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               18. A traditional German breakfast  wursts, local cheeses and freshly baked bread is the normal fare for a German breakfast. All washed back with a delicious coffee.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="33" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-365" height="426" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/331.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               19. The famous American breakfast  home made thick pancakes with syrup and blueberries, topped off with a few rashers of bacon. Anyone not wishing for a coronary usually opts for a bowl of muesli, so Im told. Pancakes all the way for me!
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="32" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-366" height="427" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/321.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               20. The French breakfast  ah, le croissant, le croissant, how I love le croissant! Pack them with crushed almonds, butter, chocolate or cream, they always taste good.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="31" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-367" height="640" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/311.jpg" width="480"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               21. Breakfast in India  here we have rosemary roasted potatoes, Indian tofu scramble, lentils, veggie sausage and banana pepper toast. Breakfast cuisine in India varies hugely depending on the region but if you think of your Indian breakfast somewhere along these lines, you would be correct.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="30" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-368" height="427" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/30.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               22. A hearty Scottish breakfast  much like a full English and a full Irish, but the countrys USP is the sumptuous slab of haggis served alongside every fat-fried egg. Dont know what haggis is? Scroll down quick if your animal eating habits err on the queasy side. Its sheeps heart, liver and lungs minced with onion, oatmeal, suet, spices, salt and stock
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="29" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-369" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/29.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               23. Thailands breakfast offering  youll find this dish at stalls throughout Thailand. Its a minty spicy fish with a sweet &amp; spicy pork, served with rice. By all accounts it tastes excellent, and its cheap at only 30 Bhat. Thai breakfast fare isnt all that different from what youd eat for lunch and dinner.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="28" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-370" height="428" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/28.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               24. An Argentinian breakfast -usually consists of mate (an infusion drink made with leaves of yerba) or dulce de leche with facturas,a croissant-like typical pastry.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="27" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-371" height="276" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/27.jpg" width="425"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               25. An Irish breakfast  youve had English and Scottish, now its time to learn the Irish USP. That would be white pudding and soda bread.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="26" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-372" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/26.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               26. A Canadian breakfast  that eggy looking section is actually perogies. Perogies are boiled, baked or fried dumplings made from unleavened dough and traditionally stuffed with potato filling, sauerkraut, ground meat, cheese, or fruit. Then youve got some sausages and toast to mop it all up.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="25" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-373" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/251.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               27. Breakfast in Mexico  the delightful plate above consists of beef tips, chilequiles and other assorted goodies eaten in Manzanillo. Nachos, cheese and beans always feature heavily and a delicious, spicy breakfast is the norm.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="24" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-374" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/242.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               28. A Russian breakfast  oladi is the breakfast of choice in Russia. Theyre sort of like pancakes and kind of like Yorkshire puddings, hot, just fried, soft inside and with a crispy edge! Theyre best enjoyed with soured cream, honey, jam or fresh berries.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="23" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-375" height="466" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/232-700x466.jpg" width="700"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               29. Breakfast in Vietnam  usually consists of some meaty treat dropped in a semolina/porridge mixture. What you see above is pork porridge. It features Chinese doughnuts, beansprouts, pork intestine stuffed with peppery pork mince, sliced pork heart, stomach slivers and blood pudding. A bit more interesting than toast and jam anyway.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="22" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-376" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/223.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               30. Breakfast in Peru  ceviche is popular whatever time of day, breakfast, lunch or dinner. Its a seafood dish made from fresh raw fish marinated in citrus juices such as lemon or lime and spiced with chilli peppers. What a feast.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="21" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-377" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/213.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               31. Breakfast in Bolivia  saltenas are a bit like empanadas crossed with Cornish pasties. Theyre the traditional option for a Bolivian breakfast and usually filled with meat and vegetables, and slightly sweetened with sugar.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="20" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-378" height="525" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/202-700x525.jpg" width="700"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               32. An Egyptian breakfast  the breakfast of choice here is Foul Madamas. Its made from fava beans, chickpeas, garlic and lemon. Above youll see the dish topped with olive oil, cayenne, tahini sauce, a hard boiled egg, and some diced green veggies.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="19" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-379" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/192.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               33. Breakfast in Japan  what do you mean youve never had tofu for breakfast? Its a popular choice in Japan, along with fish and rice. Soak it in soya sauce and youve got yourself one delicious, and semi-healthy breakfast.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="18" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-380" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/183.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               34. Breakfast in China  a lot like lunch and dinner in China. Expect noodles, rice, sticky coated chicken and fried veggies.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="17" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-381" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/173.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               35. Malaysian breakfast  A hot bowl of Mee  noodles mixed with egg, vegetable and tasty spices.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="16" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-382" height="423" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/163.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               36. Breakfast in Mongolia  it generally consists of boiled mutton with lots of fat and flour and maybe some dairy products or rice. In western Mongolia they add variety to their diets with horsemeat.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="15" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-383" height="315" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/153.jpg" width="480"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               37. Breakfast in Belize  fry jacks are a staple in Belize breakfast cuisine. Theyre deep-fried pieces of dough that are often accompanied by beans and eggs, or jam and honey.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="14" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-384" height="427" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/143.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               38. A Hungarian breakfast  always consists of Pogcsa. Well, nearly always anyway. Throughout the year there are festivals dedicated to it and the recipe changes region to region. They have a scone-like consistency and as well as a popular breakfast item, theyre also used to bulk up goulash meals.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="13" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-385" height="327" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/132.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               39. A Korean breakfast  breakfast is similar to lunch and dinner in Korea. Youll get a small plate of kimchi, a bowl of rice and a bowl of clear vegetable soup. A good old-fashioned slice of toast is also a popular choice, but that doesnt make for nearly as good a picture.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="12" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-386" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/124.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               40. Breakfast in Pakistan  in Pakistan youll get Aloo Paratha for your breakfast. It s an Indian unleavened flatbread made by pan frying, wholewheat dough on a tava. The dough contains ghee and the bread is usually stuffed with vegetables. Its best eaten with butter, chutney or some other spicy sauce. Its not uncommon to roll it up and dip it in your tea.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="11" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-387" height="427" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/119.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               41. An Estonian breakfast  curd cheese on a wheat bloomer  known locally as cheese on toast. The creamy topping can be supplemented with ricotta or fromage fraiche instead, if you prefer.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="10" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-388" height="300" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/104.jpg" width="400"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               42. Breakfast in Jordan  the choice varies depending on the are and upbringing youre from. Labneh, hummous and falafel are all popular choices and are usually served alongside olive oil, lamb sausage, jam and butter, turkey or beef mortadella.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="9" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-389" height="375" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/94.jpg" width="500"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               43. Breakfast in Venezuela  empenadas are the order of the day. Fill the little pastries with fresh cheese, minced meat or any combination of veggies and beans.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="8" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-390" height="426" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/86.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               44. Breakfast in Colombia  there are a variety of regional staples to keep your stomach grumbles at bay throughout the day. In Cundinamarca this changua dish is very popular. Its made from milk, scallions and cheese.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="7" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-391" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/76.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               45. Breakfast in Ghana  the most popular breakfast item in this African country is waakye. Its basically rice cooked in beans and is found at all the street stalls in Ghana.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <img alt="6" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-392" height="427" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/66.jpg" width="640"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              46. Breakfast in Uganda  like a lot of large countries the typical breakfasts vary region by region. But a popular dish across the country is katogo  its a combination of green cooking bananas mixed in a stew from beef or in a sauce from vegetables. The picture above is banana with cow organs.
              <strong>
               <br/>
               <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/57.jpg">
                <img alt="5" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-393" height="300" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/57.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              47. A Bahamas breakfast  to be a Bahamian breakfast it must contain grits. Grits are dried ground hominy, or corn, for anyone not in the loop. You mix it with boiling water and the grits becomes a porridge. Its popularity came from slavery times when its all the slaves had to eat. Nowadays its topped with fat prawns and meat to spice it up a bit.
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/410.jpg">
               <img alt="4" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-394" height="375" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/410.jpg" width="500"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               48. Breakfast in Costa Rica  Gallo Pinto is the standard breakfast fare in Costa Rica. Its made from black beans, rice, optional soured cream, salsa and a corn tortilla. Costa Ricans will often have a bit of avocado, fried ripe plantain or cold meat on the side.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/310.jpg">
               <img alt="3" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-395" height="640" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/310.jpg" width="427"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              49. Breakfast in the Dominican Republic  you need to try the mangu. Mangu is made from mashing boiled plantains with butter and either salami, cheese or eggs. Top it off with a hot chocolate and youve got yourself some traditional Dominican Republic fare.
              <strong>
               <br/>
               <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/210.jpg">
                <img alt="2" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-396" height="391" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/210.jpg" width="519"/>
               </a>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               50. A full English Breakfast  it must have beans, sausages, bacon, eggs, mushrooms, hash browns and toast. It should all be knocked back with a cup of tea! If youve never experienced a full English breakfast before, do yourself a favor and get to the nearest cafe that serves one!
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/120.jpg">
               <img alt="1" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-397" height="480" src="http://www.hrtwarming.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/120.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
