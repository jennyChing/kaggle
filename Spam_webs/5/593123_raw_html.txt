        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SVIX1EDHEI4BQJC" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FHA/9RYL/HEI4BQF5/FHA9RYLHEI4BQF5.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FHA9RYLHEI4BQF5" data-notes="" href="/file/FHA9RYLHEI4BQF5" id="photoset-link-FOLZ414HELXCXCL-0" style="">

         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SVIX1EDHEI4BQJC" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FYA/594R/HEI4MACS/FYA594RHEI4MACS.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FYA594RHEI4MACS" data-notes="" href="/file/FYA594RHEI4MACS" id="photoset-link-FHHFJVFHELXCXCN-2" style="margin-left:10px">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:295px;height:166px;">
           <img alt="3.jpg" class="photoset-photo id_FYA594RHEI4MACS lazyphoto" data-notes="" data-original="http://cdn.instructables.com/FYA/594R/HEI4MACS/FYA594RHEI4MACS.MEDIUM.jpg" src="/static/img/pixel.png" style="width:295px;"/>

       <div class="txt step-body">
        For this project, I wanted to use some kitchen chemistry and a few common household items to test out a few different methods for making rocket fuel. This type of rocket fuel is commonly referred to as "R-Candy"
        <br/>
        <br/>
        The main components of this composition are a brand of stump remover, which is 100% potassium nitrate (KNO3), and plain white table sugar.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        In addition, I tried using different amounts of corn syrup and homemade rust powder. The rust was made by mixing steel wool (like these scrubbing pads) and vinegar, then letting it rust out over the course of 1-2 weeks.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 3: Mix Intimately and Caramelize
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SDJ0EIUHEI4BQKA" data-entry-url="/id/Making-Rocket-Fuel-With-Kitchen-Chemistry/step3/Mix-Intimately-and-Caramelize/" id="photoset-SDJ0EIUHEI4BQKA">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SDJ0EIUHEI4BQKA" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FBS/GMR5/HEKLLVUS/FBSGMR5HEKLLVUS.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FBSGMR5HEKLLVUS" data-notes="" href="/file/FBSGMR5HEKLLVUS" id="photoset-link-FZRN433HELXCXCS-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:600px;">
           <img alt="Picture of Mix Intimately and Caramelize" class="photoset-photo id_FBSGMR5HEKLLVUS " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FBS/GMR5/HEKLLVUS/FBSGMR5HEKLLVUS.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SDJ0EIUHEI4BQKA" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/F25/T4LO/HEKLLVUX/F25T4LOHEKLLVUX.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/F25T4LOHEKLLVUX" data-notes="" href="/file/F25T4LOHEKLLVUX" id="photoset-link-FKO1BOBHELXCXCT-1" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:600px;">
           <img alt="7.jpg" class="photoset-photo id_F25T4LOHEKLLVUX lazyphoto" data-notes="" data-original="http://cdn.instructables.com/F25/T4LO/HEKLLVUX/F25T4LOHEKLLVUX.MEDIUM.jpg" src="/static/img/pixel.png" style="width:600px;"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="7.jpg" class="photoset-photo" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/F25/T4LO/HEKLLVUX/F25T4LOHEKLLVUX.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>
           </noscript>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        When mixed together in ratios of 60% KNO3/40% Sugar by weight, and placed on medium heat, they melt into a creamy brown liquid. This is because the sugar caramelizes and absorbs the KNO3. The smell is similar to that of making candy, and that's why this is sometimes referred to as "Rocket Candy" or "R-Candy".
        <br/>
        <br/>
        The mixture is volatile, and will violently ignite if brought in contact with a flame, so extreme caution needs to be used at this point.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        When it's runny enough, it can be poured into a casing to cool down and solidify. I'm using these Mega-Block LEGOs. Watch out, it's hot!
