       <div class="perex">
        <span class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_widget hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" data-hs-cos-general-type="widget" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text" id="hs_cos_wrapper_perex_text" style="">

       <span class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text" id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" style="">
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="caption">
         <a href="//cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/431440/file-2950045450-jpg/blog-files/socialrecruiter-300x207.jpg?t=1436883029412">
          <img alt="MSLGROUP Global/flickr" class="size-medium wp-image-4993" height="207" src="//cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/431440/file-2950045450-jpg/blog-files/socialrecruiter-300x207.jpg?t=1436883029412&amp;width=300&amp;height=207" width="300"/>
         </a>
         MSLGROUP Global/
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/mslgroup/15412075404/in/photolist-qqQDju-qoC4Dd-qqJcBi-q9sDVX-ptUV3s-qqUaip-ptUV51-ebNTMQ-ebJgBa-ebHECe-e2Fo69-e2G9qu-pPDNbq-o2EFGs-nDLSsW-fYgXv7-f2JguW-fbwE5W-ayw9LR-78FgEm-qRHQAv">
          flickr
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          With social media platforms proving both efficient and effective at finding the right candidates, do recruiters even need to use job boards anymore?
         </em>
         <!--more-->
        </p>
        <p>
         A survey conducted last year by the
         <a href="https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/employer-perspectives-survey-2014">
          UK Commission for Employment and Skills
         </a>
         (UKCES) of over 18,000 British employers revealed that 7% of UK employers use social media to recruit.
        </p>
        <p>
         Although this number seems quite low, 73% of respondents said they are planning to invest more in this area going forward. Let's unpack the potential benefits of the social and why it might be worth to consider changing your current strategy.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Benefits of Social Media Recruitment
        </h3>
        <p>
         The traditional method of identifying ideal candidates is both time-consuming and
         <br/>
         flawed. Essentially, job portals assume that desirable candidates will somehow stumble across your companys Careers page. In todays increasingly competitive, high-stakes job market, thats an assumption your firm cant afford to make.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is where social recruiting comes in.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobvite.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Jobvite_SocialRecruiting_Survey2014.pdf">
          Jobvite
         </a>
         found that utilising a social platform improved both the quality and quantity of candidates by 44%, with the average time to hire decreasing by 34%.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whats more, hiring managers and recruiters can use social networks at each stage of the hiring process, from top-of-the-funnel activities, which involve
         <a href="http://launchpadrecruits.com/social-engagement/">
          generating brand awareness and promoting a brand strategy
         </a>
         , to bottom-of-the funnel activities, like
         <a href="http://launchpadrecruits.com/how-to-legally-research-an-interview-candidate">
          vetting candidates on social media
         </a>
         before and after theyve been interviewed.
        </p>
        <p>
         Social platforms are highly effective for targeting the passive candidates and the fact that you can engage with them in real time means that you can get an immediate response and that way speed up your recruitment process. In addition, a real time interaction with a potential employer would make a candidate feel more engaged
        </p>
        <p>
         In addition, compared to traditional job portals, recruiting via social media has both a quicker and wider reach. And compared to hiring agencies and signing up for job boards, recruiting through social media costs virtually nothing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Even better, while checking the candidates background, social media sites like LinkedIn give recruiters access to the candidates most relevant information  including references and connections  which can help recruiters decide if someone is a good fit for an organisation.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Top Social Recruiting Sites: Linkedin, Facebook, &amp; Twitter
        </h3>
        <p>
         According to a
         <a href="http://www.stepstone.com/default.cfm?pid=/products-and-services/recruitment-expertise/download-pdf&amp;file=social_media_report">
          Stepstone
         </a>
         report from 2013, LinkedIn is the most commonly used platform for digital recruitment efforts, with 64% of companies using it to find new talent, followed closely by Facebook at 41% and Twitter at 29%.
        </p>
        <p>
         Linkedin offers several tools to aid in tracking and managing potential candidates. The site also provides options for synchronising team activities with shared projects, searches, profiles, and applicant notes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Similarly, Facebook provides you with the opportunity to fill strategic positions that fit your specific needs by incorporating various hiring scenarios, including graduate hiring, seasonal hiring, and niche recruiting, among others.
         <a href="http://branchout.com/">
          BranchOut
         </a>
         , Facebooks professional networking service, brings you access to millions of users.
        </p>
        <p>
         Millions of professionals also use Twitter for networking. With the right tweets, you can build a following on Twitter and attract the right kind of candidates to your brand.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Social Networks: More Than Just a Job Board
        </h3>
        <p>
         With the vast majority of the market yet to tap into its potential, social recruiting presents a huge opportunity for hiring managers across all industries. The benefits are clear from a companys perspective: recruiters get to engage directly with the candidates, build the employer brand, increase referrals and job traffic, and bring a more innovative and personal approach to the process.
        </p>
        <p>
         And for job seekers, the appeal of social recruitment is simple, as the task of finding the perfect fit will get a whole lot easier. With so much to gain and so little to lose, the only question is: whats your company waiting for?
        </p>
       </span>
