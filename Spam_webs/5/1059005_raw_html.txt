&lt;div class="recipe"&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;Carrot Muffins with Cream Cheese Filling&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;I suppose you could also frost these with cream cheese frosting and turn them into cupcakes because if there&#8217;s any cake that&#8217;s the same as a muffin, it&#8217;s carrot cake.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Preheat oven to 350.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Sift together &lt;strong&gt;1 c. all-purpose flour&lt;/strong&gt;, &lt;strong&gt;1 tsp. baking soda&lt;/strong&gt;, &lt;strong&gt;1 tsp.baking powder&lt;/strong&gt;, &lt;strong&gt; tsp. salt&lt;/strong&gt;, &lt;strong&gt;1 tsp. cinnamon&lt;/strong&gt;, &lt;strong&gt; tsp. nutmeg&lt;/strong&gt;, and &lt;strong&gt;1 c. sugar&lt;/strong&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In a separate bowl, beat &lt;strong&gt;3 eggs&lt;/strong&gt; and &lt;strong&gt;2/3 cup vegetable oil&lt;/strong&gt; until lightened. Stir in &lt;strong&gt;1 c. shredded carrots&lt;/strong&gt;, &lt;strong&gt; c. chopped walnuts&lt;/strong&gt;, and &lt;strong&gt; c. raisins&lt;/strong&gt; that have been soaked for 10 minutes in hot water (or warmed rum, if you&#8217;d like).&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;** As a side note, the bestbestbest carrot cake I&#8217;ve ever had in my life was at &lt;a href="http://www.justdesserts.com/"&gt;Just Desserts&lt;/a&gt; in Berkeley. I used to go to the one on College Avenue, order an enormous piece of carrot cake and an espresso and (pretend to) study until they kicked me out. The carrots in Just Desserts&#8217; carrot cake were not normal shreds, like they were done on a box grater, but long and super fine, like carrot &lt;em&gt;threads&lt;/em&gt;. Unfortunately, my little Japanese Benriner cannot make the carrots like that.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In another bowl, cream &lt;strong&gt;4 oz. softened cream cheese&lt;/strong&gt; with &lt;strong&gt;1 egg yolk&lt;/strong&gt;, &lt;strong&gt; c. sugar&lt;/strong&gt;, and &lt;strong&gt; tsp. vanilla&lt;/strong&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Fold the dry ingredients into the egg/oil mixture. Stir until just combined. Fill mini-muffin cups about  full. Plop a generous teaspoon of the cream cheese filling on top of the batter in each muffin cup.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Bake about 20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the cake part comes out clean.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
