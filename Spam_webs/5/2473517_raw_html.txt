                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Artists Perspectives
                      </em>
                      was an initiative launched in 2010, aiming to offer wider linguistic access to Tate by offering articles about the collection in languages other than English. It was initially funded via the World Collections Programme, and the artists approached were from outside Europe and NorthAmerica.
                     </p>
                     <h2>
                      Blog series:200912
                     </h2>
                     <p>
                      Artists were invited to write a short article on a single work  or a small selection of works  in Tates collection, to be published in English and another language of their choice, which was usually their first language. Six texts were completed, by the artists
                      <a href="/node/161599">
                       Nazgol Ansarinia
                      </a>
                      ,
                      <a href="/node/161298">
                       Lamia Joreige
                      </a>
                      ,
                      <a href="/node/161565">
                       Kader Attia
                      </a>
                      ,
                      <a href="/node/161604">
                       Cao Fei
                      </a>
                      ,
                      <a href="/node/161454">
                       Kishio Suga
                      </a>
                      and
                      <a href="http://www.tate.org.uk/art/artists/carla-zaccagnini-14306">
                       Carla Zaccagnini
                      </a>
                      .
                     </p>

                     <h2>
                      Tate Etc. / ArtistsPerspectives
                     </h2>
                     <p>
                      As part of the next stage of
                      <em>
                       Artists Perspectives
                      </em>
                      we will translate a number of texts written by international artists for Tates magazine,
                      <a href="http://www.tate.org.uk/context-comment/articles/search?f%5B0%5D=im_vid_48%3A1881&amp;solrsort=is_end_date%20asc%2C%20is_start_date%20asc%2C%20is_published_date%20desc">
                       Tate Etc
                      </a>
                      . These will be published in late 2013 in Chinese, Arabic, French and Spanish, to sit alongside the existing English versions in TateEtc.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      We will continue to produce new
                      <em>
                       Artists Perspectives
                      </em>
                      in future, commissioning and translating more texts in a wider variety of languages. Alongside artists, other protagonists in the art world might also be invited to write texts. As the project evolves the constant elements willbe:
                     </p>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       Texts will engage with the Tatecollection
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       Authors will be mostly from outside the
                       <span class="caps">
                        UK
                       </span>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       Texts will be published in English and at least one otherlanguage
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       <a href="http://www.tate.org.uk/context-comment/articles/search?f%5B0%5D=im_vid_48%3A1881&amp;f%5B1%5D=im_vid_48%3A6457&amp;solrsort=is_end_date%20asc%2C%20is_start_date%20asc%2C%20is_published_date%20desc">
                        Browse all Tate Etc. / Artists Perspectivesarticles
                       </a>
                      </li>
                     </ul>
