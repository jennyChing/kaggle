         Tiger Loses AT &amp; T Sponsorship, Will Keep NIKE, But Should Consider Hooter's
        </h1>

                     <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                      <div class="field-items">
                       <div class="field-item even">
                        <p>
                         <span class="styles file-styles medium" id="styles-10-0">
                          <img class="media-image" id="10" src="http://anomalies.us/sites/default/files/styles/medium/public/legacy/Tiger%20Woods%20At.?itok=l8VOXjej"/>
                         </span>
                        </p>
                        <p>
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         <span style="font-size: small;">
                          <a href="http://abcnews.go.com/Sports/wireStory?id=10148159">
                           Tiger, who will be returning to golf in the Masters much sooner than expected will not have the luxury of being paid enormous amounts of money for sporting a shiny new logo on his golf bag.
                          </a>
                          <br/>
                          <br/>
                          Poor Tiger.
                          <br/>
                          <br/>
                          First, he gets in a very public car accident which started all of the speculation about his marriage in the first place. Then, the shit-storm hit and Tiger lost countless sponsors, which equaled
                          <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/golf/tigerwoods/6699508/Tiger-Woods-sponsorship-deals-and-endorsements.html">
                           mucho dinero
                          </a>
                          and a tremendous loss of face for the golfer once known as having a semi-stellar reputation. If the loss of money and public face wasnt enough for the unlucky Tiger, he had to go to Sex Re-hab, which is something that I can only envision from the movie 
                          <a href="http://www.foxsearchlight.com/choke/">
                           Choke
                          </a>
                           and the main characters Sex Addicts support group meetings, which always ended up with most of the participants having sex.
                          <br/>
                          <br/>
                          Now, after being dropped by AT &amp; T, Tiger is going to have to sport the old logo from his longest-running sponsor: Nike. How embarrassing is that? Im guessing that most of the golfers will have new sponsors.
                          <br/>
                          <br/>
                          Im sure that he could have done some more creative marketing than that. Last I heard, Hooters was still looking for a golfer to sponsor and I am positive that a semi-stellar guy like Tiger (who did publicly apologize to the entire United States for his sexual liaisons) would love to have a Hooters logo on his bag. Maybe he could even get a couple of cheerleader/waitresses to boot.
                          <span class="styles file-styles medium" id="styles-11-0">
                           <img class="media-image" id="11" src="http://anomalies.us/sites/default/files/styles/medium/public/legacy/Hooters.?itok=Qf1NJtFY"/>
                          </span>
                          <br/>
                          <br/>
                          As to whether or not Tiger will actually fare well in the Masters, Ill leave that to the experts.
                          <a href="http://www.thespread.com/sports-betting-top-stories-900/2010-u.s.-golf-masters-updated-odds-to-win-tiger-woods-favored.html">
                           The bookies are giving ole Tiger three-to-one odds to win in Augusta.
                          </a>
                          For those of you who are concerned about the relative emptiness of Tigers wallet after all of those lap dances, just keep in mind that if
                          <a class="flink" href="http://hotinthe.us/news/tied-4th-and-did-not-lose">
                           Tiger Woods
                          </a>
                          wins the Masters, he will take home a purse of $7.5 million, which is not too shabby.
                          <br/>
                          <br/>
                          Of course, Tiger is still under the sponsorship of Nike indefinitely, who is no slouch in terms of athlete sponsorship in the United States.
                          <br/>
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </p>
