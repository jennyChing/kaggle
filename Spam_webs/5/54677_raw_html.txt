         <h1>
          Minor US Passport
         </h1>
         <p>
          <span class="firstcharacter">
           T
          </span>
          raveling internationally with your child? Your child will need to have a valid minor passport. Child passports valid for five years are issued to US citizens age 15 and younger. At age 16, US citizens are eligible for an adult passport valid for 10 years. Passports for minors cannot be renewed. The procedure to get a child US passport is the same whether or not the child has previously been issued a passport.
         </p>
         <p>
          This guide will show you how to get a passport for minors, including requirements, application procedures, and how to get a minor passport fast.
         </p>
         <p class="expediteServiceLink">
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.rushmypassport.com/child-passport.html" target="_blank">
            Get a Childs US Passport Quickly in 24 to 48 Hours
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Passport for Minors  Eligibility
         </h2>
         <p>
          Child US passports can be issued to any US citizen age 15 or younger. There is no such thing as being too young to get a passport  even newborn babies need a minor passport in order to travel outside the US.
         </p>
         <p>
          Both parents or legal guardians must give their approval for the child to be issued a passport. They can do this in person at the
          <a href="http://passportinfo.com/passport-acceptance-agent/">
           Passport Acceptance Agent
          </a>
          (see Step Two below) by appearing together with their child. If one parent cannot go to the Passport Acceptance Agent, they can give written consent with a notarized government form.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Step-by-Step Guide to How to Get a Passport for Minors
         </h2>
         <h3>
          <label class="stepNum">
           <span>
            1
           </span>
          </label>
          Gather all required documents
         </h3>
         <ul class="stepList">
          <li>
           <a href="http://passportinfo.com/us-passport-help-guide/proof-of-citizenship/">
            Proof of US Citizenship
           </a>
           (birth certificate)  This must be an original or certified copy of a long form birth certificate showing both parents names
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://passportinfo.com/ds-11-application-form-for-minor-passport/">
             DS-11 Application Form for a Minor Passport
            </a>
           </strong>
           , completed offline or filled out online at
           <a href="https://pptform.state.gov/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            https://pptform.state.gov/
           </a>
           and printed. The child passport application form is the same one that is used by adults who need to get a new passport.
          </li>
          <li>
           One
           <a href="http://passportinfo.com/us-passport-help-guide/passport-photos/">
            passport photo
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Photocopy of both parents passports, drivers licenses or other state-issued ID (front and back)
          </li>
          <li>
           Flight itinerary or other
           <a href="http://passportinfo.com/us-passport-help-guide/proof-of-travel/">
            proof of travel plans
           </a>
           (only required if you need to rush a child passport)
          </li>
          <li>
           Previous passport, if the child has been issued one
          </li>
          <li>
           Notarized Statement of Consent
           <a href="http://passportinfo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DS-3053.pdf">
            form (DS-3053)
           </a>
            If one parent is not able to go to the Passport Acceptance Agent, they will need to complete and sign this form and have it notarized. If both parents go with the child to the Passport Acceptance Agent, this form is not needed.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://passportinfo.com/us-passport-help-guide/passport-fee/">
            Minor passport fee
           </a>
            A check for
           <span class="black">
            $95
           </span>
           ($155 if you need expedited service for your childs passport) made payable to US Department of State.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          <label class="stepNum">
           <span>
            2
           </span>
          </label>
          Passport Acceptance Agent
         </h3>
         <p class="stepContent">
          Both parents and the child will need to visit a Passport Acceptance Agent to have the passport application sealed into an official envelope. Passport Acceptance Agents are located at major US Post Offices and at Clerk of the Court offices. To a nearby location, visit
          <a href="http://passportinfo.com/passport-offices/">
           http://passportinfo.com/passport-offices/
          </a>
          . You may need to call to make an appointment with the Passport Acceptance Agent.
         </p>
         <p class="stepContent">
          The child must appear in person, and must be accompanied by at least one parent. If the other parent is not present, the Statement of Consent form is required to show that the absent parent also gives permission for the child to be issued a minor US passport.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <label class="stepNum">
           <span>
            3
           </span>
          </label>
          Have your passport application processed in one of three ways:
         </h3>
         <ol class="stepList">
          <li>
           <strong>
            By Mail
           </strong>
            The Passport Acceptance Agent will mail the sealed envelope to be processed at the National Passport Center.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            At a Passport Agency
           </strong>
            If you are in ahurryfor a new passport, you and your child can visit US Passport Agencies located in major US cities, where the passport will be processed on-site.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            By a Passport Expediting Company
           </strong>
            If are in
           <a href="http://passportinfo.com/fast-passports/">
            rush for child passport
           </a>
           , you can send the sealed envelope to a reputable passport expediting company, who will hand-carry your application to a Passport Agency to obtainthe child passport fast.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p class="callToAction">
          <a href="https://www.rushmypassport.com/step2.html?service=Child Passport" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Expedite child US passport as quickly as same day" class="aligncenter wp-image-9901 size-full" height="90" src="http://passportinfo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Minor-US-Passport.jpg" title="Expedite child US passport as quickly as same day" width="728"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          How to Get a Child Passport Fast
         </h2>
         <p>
          Applying for a child US passport by mail will take up to 8 weeks to receive the passport. Expedited service for a child passport by mail is available for an
          <span class="black">
           additional $60
          </span>
          government fee, and the minor passport will arrive in 2-3 weeks. If you really need to get an expedited child passport, you can either go to a Passport Agency in person or use the services of a
          <a href="http://passportinfo.com/registered-passport-expeditors/">
           <strong>
            passport expediting company
           </strong>
          </a>
          . Thesecompanies can rush a child passport in as little as 24 hours.
         </p>
         <p>
          There are
          <a href="http://passportinfo.com/regional-passport-agencies/">
           26 Passport Agencies located in cities across the US
          </a>
          . To visit one, you must make an appointment to visit one by calling the National Passport Information Center at
          <span class="black">
           877-487-2778
          </span>
          . Using a passport expediting company is super fast and much easier  no appointments needed, and no need to travel to take your child to a Passport Agency.
         </p>
