              <span property="v:description">
               <span itemprop="description">
                <p style="text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://www.techmagnifier.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Dell-Latitude-12.jpg" rel="lightbox[101315]">
                  <img alt="Dell Latitude 12 " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-101316" height="379" src="http://www.techmagnifier.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Dell-Latitude-12.jpg" width="640"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 Its raining heavily in the mountains, temperatures dipping every minute; there could be life threatening situations. But if you still feel like making a work presentation (or at least want your laptop to stay alive), Dell Rugged Extreme series notebooks will be happy to help.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 Dell Latitude 12 and 14 Rugged Extreme are touch screen laptops that would work in freezing cold rain, extreme vibrations, weight pressure of more than 70 Kg and drops from at least shoulder level. This isnt from the specs but what was showcased at the launch event held in Delhi on Wednesday, December 10
                 <sup>
                  th
                 </sup>
                 . The specs say the hard-wearing laptops can also sustain temperatures up to 63 degrees, hazardous influx, explosive atmosphere, 72, 60 and 48 degree transit drops, dust, moisture, thermal shock and other extreme conditions.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://www.techmagnifier.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Dell-Latitude.jpg" rel="lightbox[101315]">
                  <img alt="Dell Latitude" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-101317" height="369" src="http://www.techmagnifier.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Dell-Latitude.jpg" width="640"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 Made from impact-resistant ultra-polymers worked up with magnesium alloy, the laptops look like some serious plumbers toolbox or better, an early mans computer fashioned out of a rock. They have a resistive, glove-ready touchscreen and Latitude 12 REs flips too; its a convertible actually that weighs around 3Kg.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 The core i series based laptops have native serial and other normally available ports accessible through tight shutters placed around its edges; theres a privacy shutter on the webcam and stealth mode and smart card reader, among other security features. There is SSD slot for SSD hard drive, memory card reader (ExpressCard 54 mm or PCMCIA) and rubberized backlit keyboard. The Latitude 14 also has optical drives for option.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 The Dell Latitude 12 Rugged Extreme will be available starting Rs.239999, while the Latitude 14 from Rs.2229999 (taxes extra).
                </p>
