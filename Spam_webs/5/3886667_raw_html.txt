       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Todays senior executives rely on information technology organizations to help their business execute on strategies and improve their operations.
        </p>
        <p>
         As the bedrock for the corporate directory and identity, the Active Directory plays a critical role in this IT environment, by providing access control for servers and applications.
        </p>
        <p>
         At the same time, the threat of compromise to IT infrastructures from external attacks is rapidly growing and evolving in both scope and sophistication. The motivations behind these attacks range from hacktivism (attacks influenced by activist positions) to theft of intellectual property  and the Active Directory environment is not immune from being targeted for compromise.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://aka.ms/bpsadtrd">
          <img alt="" border="0" height="358" src="http://mscorp-staging.azurewebsites.net/wp-content/uploads/sites/13/2013/06/62100.1.jpg" style="float: right;" width="284"/>
         </a>
         Against this backdrop and to help enterprises protect their Active Directory environments, Microsoft IT released a detailed technical reference document, 
         <a href="http://aka.ms/bpsadtrd">
          Best Practices for Securing Active Directory
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         The key tenets of this document are four interrelated strategies Microsoft IT recommends to protect a typical Active Directory environment, including:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Identifying vulnerabilities
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Reducing attack surface
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Monitoring for indicators of compromise
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Developing a long-term security plan
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         The methods we discuss in the document are largely based on Microsofts Information Security and Risk Management (ISRM) organizations experience, which is accountable for protecting the assets of Microsoft IT, other Microsoft Business Divisions, and advising a selected number of Microsofts Global 500 customers.
        </p>
        <p>
         As we strive towards enabling our customers in making their IT environment secure and reliable, we hope that you will find this guidance useful and enlightening.
        </p>

         You can download the technical reference document from the
         <a href="http://aka.ms/bpsad">
          IT Showcase site
         </a>
         , and I encourage you to provide us feedback by sending us email at
         <a href="mailto:bpsad@microsoft.com">
