           Earlier this week, more than 60 seed businesses and organic farmers banded together to file a
           <a href="http://www.scribd.com/doc/52077615/Organic-Seed-Growers-et-al-v-Monsanto">
            federal lawsuit in New York against Monsanto
           </a>
           (yes, again; see
           <a href="http://blogs.riverfronttimes.com/dailyrft/2011/03/roundup_ready_alfalfa_means_curtains_for_organic_beef_and_dairy.php">
            last week's suit in California over alfalfa
           </a>
           ).
           <br/>
           <br/>
           The organic folks, organized partly by the
           <a href="http://www.pubpat.org/">
            Public Patent Foundation
           </a>
           , hope to erect a sort of legal shield against the St. Louis-based agrochemical behemoth this time around.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           If they succeed, Monsanto will not be able to sue them for patent infringement, should Monsanto-engineered crops sprout up unexpectedly in their organic fields.

           </a>
