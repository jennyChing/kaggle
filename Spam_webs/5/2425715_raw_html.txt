							&lt;h2 class="entry_title"&gt;								
								&lt;a href="http://www.soundonsight.org/colleen-coover-illustrates-comics-potential/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Colleen Coover Illustrates Comics&#8217; Potential" &gt;Colleen Coover Illustrates Comics&#8217; Potential&lt;/a&gt;
							&lt;/h2&gt;							&lt;h2 class="entry_title"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.soundonsight.org/colleen-coover-illustrates-comics-potential/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Colleen Coover Illustrates Comics&#8217; Potential" &gt;Female Comics Creators Month&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;
							&lt;p class="top_postmeta"&gt;
								&lt;span class="entrydate metaitem"&gt;Posted on March 17, 2014&lt;/span&gt;
								&lt;span class="entryby metaitem"&gt;By &lt;a href="http://www.soundonsight.org/author/tyler-hayden/"&gt;Tyler Hayden&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;
								&lt;span class="entrycat metaitem last"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.soundonsight.org/comic-book/" rel="category tag"&gt;Comic Book&lt;/a&gt;, &lt;a href="http://www.soundonsight.org/comic-book/features/" rel="category tag"&gt;Features&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;
							&lt;/p&gt;	

							&lt;p&gt;Colleen Coover bridges the gap between the Big Twos muscular multiverses and the menagerie of independent comics. With distinctly cartoonish lines and pallets, Coover creates across genres without ever sacrificing her spirit. From adult books like &lt;i&gt;Small Favors, &lt;/i&gt;to Marvels &lt;i&gt;X-Men: First Class &lt;/i&gt;and the whimsical &lt;i&gt;Bandette, &lt;/i&gt;Coovers work carries her distinct flare. Currently based out of Portland, Oregon as part of Periscope Studio, Colleen Coovers diverse body of work and commitment to vision make her one of the most exciting women working in comics.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Coover honed her comic book story-telling skills in &lt;i&gt;Small Favors,&lt;/i&gt; an erotic adults-only comic chronicling the lives, loves and lusts of Annie and her girlfriend Nibbil. Definitely naughty, &lt;i&gt;Small Favors &lt;/i&gt;recasts sex, specifically lesbian sex, as a healthy, happy thing without giving up the slinky passion. Characters are rare in pornography, well-rounded, likable characters even less so, but &lt;i&gt;Small Favors &lt;/i&gt;pages abound with as many fleshed-out characters as it does characters flesh. &lt;i&gt;Small Favors&lt;/i&gt; illustrates the mediums reach by contrasting Coovers cartoon style with the erotic. Its a bold comic that speaks to the versatility of the medium and Coovers own storytelling ability.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&nbsp;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-236801" alt="SmallFavorsPage22" src="http://www.soundonsight.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/SmallFavorsPage22-192x300.jpeg" width="192" height="300" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Her next major work, &lt;i&gt;Banana Sunday &lt;/i&gt;written by her husband Paul Tobin, takes a decidedly different track. Whereas &lt;i&gt;Small Favors &lt;/i&gt;is decidedly adult, &lt;i&gt;Banana Sunday &lt;/i&gt;is an all-ages comedy, more obviously suited to Coovers cartoon sensibilities. It tells the story of high schooler Kirby Steinberg and three talking monkeys. The transition from erotic comics to a teen comedy for all ages speaks to the surprising utility of her style. Though writing credit goes to Tobin, Coovers lines breath a life into the characters that might seem flat in another artists hands. Her work pops even with just grays. More surprising than her versatility within the indie world is her propensity for super heroes.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-236802" alt="images" src="http://www.soundonsight.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/images.jpg" width="217" height="233" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Under Coovers brush, the heroes and villains populating Marvel Comics take on whimsical lines, a stark contrast with the bulging realism of the modern house styles. In &lt;i&gt;X-Men: First Class&lt;/i&gt;, Coover reimagines the classic X-Men more in tune with their teenage peers. The result is a fun, wacky depiction of the X-Teens downtime adventures. Relatively low-stakes, and specifically enjoyable for it. When Coover works with other Marvel characters, she manages to keep the tone light and idiosyncratic without ever betraying the spirit of the heroes and villains she draws.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-236803" alt="imgres-1" src="http://www.soundonsight.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/imgres-1.jpg" width="182" height="277" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;A self-described comics geek, Coovers just as at home illustrating the whimsical &lt;i&gt;Bandette, &lt;/i&gt;creating queer comics or telling classic stories with Marvel staples. As a comics artist, Coover illustrates the depth and breadth of the medium. Tying together the experimental fringes with the colorful classics, Colleen Coover represents the best thing about comics: they can tell every kind of story, and have barely scratched the surface.&lt;/p&gt;
