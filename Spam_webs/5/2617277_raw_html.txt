            <p>
             <em>
              Aspiring nurses have
multiple factors in their favor to achieve career success. The Bureau of Labor
predicts
              <a href="http://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/registered-nurses.htm">
               nursing occupations
to grow
              </a>
              at a faster than average rate of 26%
              <strike>
              </strike>
              by 2020, due to the
              <a href="http://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/registered-nurses.htm">
               advances in
technology, a growing aging population, and an increased emphasis on
preventative care.
              </a>
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              While some nurses have
practiced with earning a associates degree, recent legislative efforts may
require higher education for nurses. The
              <a href="http://www.iom.edu/Reports/2010/The-Future-of-Nursing-Leading-Change-Advancing-Health/Report-Brief-Education.aspx">
               Institute
of Medicine
              </a>
              recommends that 80%of the nursing workforce should have a baccalaureate degree by 2020, making
Registered Nurse to Bachelor of Science in Nursing (RN-BSN) programs an ideal
way to meet new demands and advance their career.
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              Like most health care
related fields, nursing programs can be a large expense for those looking to
return to school.
              <strike>
               .
              </strike>
              Fortunately, government and
non-profit organizations provide a bounty of nursing scholarship resources
worth exploring to help with the financial aspect of nursing school.
             </em>
            </p>
            <h2>
             Starting the Scholarship Search
            </h2>
            <p>
             It may be difficult to know where to begin finding nursing scholarship programs. A great place to begin is through the
             <a href="http://studentaid.ed.gov/fafsa" target="_blank">
              Federal Student Aid program
             </a>
             , provided by the U.S. Department of Education. This agency provides more than $150 billion annually in scholarships and grants to college students.
            </p>
            <p>
             Nursing students can submit a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA), which is a free application that assesses your eligibility for school and private financial help. Its often a pre-requisite for other financial opportunities. For example, you must also fill out a FAFSA form before
             <a href="http://studentaid.ed.gov/types/grants-scholarships/pell" target="_blank">
              applying for a Pell Grant
             </a>
             . Regardless of a nursing students age, grades or financial situation, the Federal Student Aid program encourages all students to complete a FAFSA for the reason that students may qualify for more than they expect.
            </p>
            <h2>
             Additional Scholarship Resource Options
            </h2>
            <p>
             Depending on a students qualifications such as education level, area of interest, financial situation and academic record, it is in your best interest to consider researching scholarship resources.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              National Student Nurses Association
             </strong>
             <br/>
             The
             <a href="http://www.nsna.org/foundationscholarships.aspx" target="_blank">
              National Student Nurses Association
             </a>
             gives out approximately $345,000 annually in scholarship money in increments of $1,000 to $5,000 for every eligible student. Available for associate to doctorate students, these scholarships are awarded based on a variety of factors including financial need, involvement in nursing organizations and academic record.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              NURSE Corps Scholarship Program (NCSP)
             </strong>
             <br/>
             The
             <a href="http://www.hrsa.gov/loanscholarships/scholarships/nursing/" target="_blank">
              NURSE Corps Scholarship Program
             </a>
             offers scholarships, tuition assistance, and aid for program related expenses for students enrolled in diploma, associate, baccalaureate or graduate nursing programs. The NCSP was developed by the U.S. Government with the intention of eliminating the shortage of nurses in the U.S. Eligible students must be enrolled, full time, in an accredited undergraduate or graduate level nursing program.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Minority Nurse Scholarship Program
             </strong>
             <br/>
             Sponsored by
             <a href="http://www.minoritynurse.com/" target="_blank">
              Minority Nurse Magazine
             </a>
             , the
             <a href="http://www.minoritynurse.com/blog/joey-stern/minority-nurse-scholarship-program" target="_blank">
              Minority Nurse Scholarship Program
             </a>
             offers $1,000 and $3,000 scholarships to racial and ethnic minority students who are enrolled in an accredited BSN or MSN program in the United States. Eligible students will have earned a 3.0 or better GPA and be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              National Institutes of Health Undergraduate Scholarship Program
             </strong>
             <br/>
             The National Institutes of Health (NIH) Undergraduate Scholarship Program (UGSP) offers scholarships to disadvantaged students who are pursuing an undergraduate degree in biomedical, behavioral, and social science health-related programs. The program offers scholarship support, paid research training, and paid employment and training at the NIH upon graduation. Students must be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident enrolled or accepted as a full-time student at an accredited four year institution in the U.S.
            </p>
            <p>
             Upon researching nursing scholarships, students will often find that many organizations are generous to award nurses a financial head start on pursuing their degree. Students are encouraged to research all scholarship opportunities and discuss their eligibility with the awarding organization and their program contact.
            </p>
            <p>
             With an
             <a href="/rn-bsn/online-rn-bsn-degree-overview">
              online Registered Nurse to Bachelor of Science in Nursing degree
             </a>
             (RN to BSN) from Concordia University, Nebraska online, students will be given the knowledge and skills to become nurse leaders in the health care industry. The faith-based BSN curriculum prepares students to meet the demands of todays health care environment and provide quality patient care. Concordia Nebraskas online RN-BSN program offers a flexible class schedule to accommodate working nurses who demonstrate leadership skills and to wish to advance their education and nursing career. To learn more about Concordia University, Nebraskas faith-based online RN-BSN program, visit
             <a href="http://onlineprograms.cune.edu/">
              onlineprograms.cune.edu
             </a>
             .
            </p>
