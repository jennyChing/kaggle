         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Oldest male stellar sea lion bids final adieu to Oregon Zoo
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-02-03T18:35:16+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           February 3, 2014
          </time>
          Posted by
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.utahpeoplespost.com/author/contributor/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
            <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
             Contributor
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          Julius, the oldest male stellar sea lion in the United States, has finally said goodbye to the Oregon Zoo as it has succumbed to its diseases.
         </p>
         <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
         <div style="float:left;margin:4px 4px 4px 0;">

         <p>
          The 26-year-old sea lion was suffering from macular degeneration in both eyes and arthritis.
         </p>
         <p>
          Notably, the life span of sea lion is between 20 and 30 years and they rarely live beyond their teens.
         </p>
         <p>
          Julius originally belonged to Connecticuts Mystic Aquarium from where he was shifted to Portland in 2000 after the zoo opened its Stellar Cove marine life habitat.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.utahpeoplespost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/9e39684564b7d238319bfdef12cf95f0.jpg">
           <img alt="9e39684564b7d238319bfdef12cf95f0" class="size-full wp-image-1594 aligncenter" height="350" src="http://www.utahpeoplespost.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/9e39684564b7d238319bfdef12cf95f0.jpg" width="523"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Recalling the26-year-old sea lion, Oregon Zoo workers said Julius had a sense of tough independence.
         </p>
         <p>
          Julius was an amazing, bold and confident animal. He learned quickly, and always seemed ready to move on to the next thing. We will remember him that way  with energy, confidence and bravado, said Amy Cutting, curator of the zoos North America section. 
         </p>
         <p>
          Gus, a fellow sea lion who is also 26, has now take the title as the oldest in any U.S. zoo or aquarium.
         </p>
