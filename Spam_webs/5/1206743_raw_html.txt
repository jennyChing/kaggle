     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Mortal Kombat X
       </b>
      </i>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Fighting_game" title="Fighting game">
       fighting video game
      </a>
      developed by
      <a href="/wiki/NetherRealm_Studios" title="NetherRealm Studios">
       NetherRealm Studios
      </a>
      . It is the tenth main installment in the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat" title="Mortal Kombat">
        Mortal Kombat
       </a>
      </i>
      video game series and was released on April 14, 2015 for
      <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">
       PlayStation 4
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Xbox_One" title="Xbox One">
       Xbox One
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">
       Microsoft Windows
      </a>
      , scheduled to be ported to
      <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">
       PlayStation 3
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">
       Xbox 360
      </a>
      by
      <a href="/wiki/High_Voltage_Software" title="High Voltage Software">
       High Voltage Software
      </a>
      . NetherRealm's mobile team developed a version for
      <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">
       iOS
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">
       Android
      </a>
      devices.
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat X
      </i>
      was officially announced in June 2014.
     </p>
     <p>
      Like previous
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat
      </i>
      games,
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat X
      </i>
      's gameplay consists of two players, or one player and the CPU, fighting against each other with their selected character, using a large, varied array of attacks. The game contains several modes such as a story mode, which takes place twenty years after the previous
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_(2011_game)" title="Mortal Kombat (2011 game)">
        Mortal Kombat
       </a>
      </i>
      game, numerous online modes, and the 'Krypt', a mode played in a
      <a href="/wiki/First_person_(video_games)" title="First person (video games)">
       first-person
      </a>
      perspective where players explore the areas unlocking useful items.
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat X
      </i>
      received positive reviews from critics upon release. Most praise was directed at the game's controls, graphics, story, and characters, with some reviewers calling it the best game in the
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat
      </i>
      series. Some critics particularly disliked the tutorials,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microtransactions" title="Microtransactions">
       microtransactions
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Downloadable_content" title="Downloadable content">
       downloadable content
      </a>
      , and online playability. The PC version of the game was especially criticized due to numerous technical issues.
     </p>

      See also:
      <a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat#Gameplay" title="Mortal Kombat">
       Gameplay of the Mortal Kombat series
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat X
      </i>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Fighting_game" title="Fighting game">
       fighting game
      </a>
      in which two players fight against each other using a variety of attacks, special moves, and gruesome
      <a href="/wiki/Fatality_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Fatality (Mortal Kombat)">
       Fatalities
      </a>
      . The energy meter, first introduced in
      <a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_(2011_video_game)" title="Mortal Kombat (2011 video game)">
       the previous
       <i>
        Mortal Kombat
       </i>
       game
      </a>
      , allows players to perform techniques such as "X-Ray" special moves.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-:0-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Similarly to
      <a href="/wiki/NetherRealm_Studios" title="NetherRealm Studios">
       NetherRealm Studios
      </a>
      ' previous title,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Injustice:_Gods_Among_Us" title="Injustice: Gods Among Us">
        Injustice: Gods Among Us
       </a>
      </i>
      , fighters are able to interact with the environment, bouncing off areas to reposition themselves or using parts of the environment as weapons.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sn_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-sn-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In addition, each fighter has three different variations, each featuring a different set of moves they can use during the fight: for example, the character
      <a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Scorpion (Mortal Kombat)">
       Scorpion
      </a>
      features a
      <a href="/wiki/Ninjutsu" title="Ninjutsu">
       Ninjutsu
      </a>
      variation in which he wields dual swords, a Hellfire variation which relies more on fiery special moves, and an Inferno variation which allows him to summon hellspawn minions to aid him in the fight.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gb_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-gb-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The sprint gauge, which was last seen in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_4" title="Mortal Kombat 4">
        Mortal Kombat 4
       </a>
       /
       <a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_Gold" title="Mortal Kombat Gold">
        Gold
       </a>
      </i>
      , returns in this game.
      <i>
       MKX
      </i>
      features two new types of finishing moves: Quitality, which will instantly kill a player's character if they quit during a multiplayer match;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and Faction Kills, finishing moves tied to each of the game's five factions. Brutality finishing moves make a comeback, although different from the ones featured in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Mortal_Kombat_3" title="Ultimate Mortal Kombat 3">
        Ultimate Mortal Kombat 3
       </a>
      </i>
      . Brutalities now take the form of enhanced versions of certain moves that are triggered when said move is used as the final attack when winning the final round in a fight, provided certain conditions are met.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In addition, the game features stage Brutalies (largely equivalent to the Stage Fatalities featured in previous games), which are triggered when certain environment interactions are used to finish the opponent.
     </p>
     <p>
      The game's play modes include Story, 1 vs. 1 Ranked, King of the Hill, Survivor, and Test Your Luck. A new addition called Living Towers is an evolved form of
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat'
      </i>
      s Challenge Tower where the play conditions will be changing every hour. Another new mode is Faction Wars, where the players will have to choose one from the five factions (Black Dragon, the Brotherhood of Shadow, the Lin Kuei, Special Forces, and White Lotus) to align with and join a persistent online
      <a href="/wiki/Cross-platform" title="Cross-platform">
       cross-platform
      </a>
      competition with the others, winning points for their faction to contribute in the conflict between them, ranking up personally, and earning special rewards such as faction-specific finishing moves (one of which is unlocked to the player if their faction win at the end of any given week).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mortal_Kombat_X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Two years after the
      <a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_(2011_video_game)" title="Mortal Kombat (2011 video game)">
       defeat
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Shao_Kahn" title="Shao Kahn">
       Shao Kahn
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Shinnok" title="Shinnok">
       Shinnok
      </a>
      attacks Earthrealm with his army of Netherrealm demons, as well as the Earthrealm warriors who were killed during Kahn's invasion and are now resurrected as revenants under
      <a href="/wiki/Quan_Chi" title="Quan Chi">
       Quan Chi
      </a>
      's control. After fighting their fallen comrades, a strike team led by
      <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Cage" title="Johnny Cage">
       Johnny Cage
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sonya_Blade" title="Sonya Blade">
       Sonya Blade
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Kenshi" title="Kenshi">
       Kenshi
      </a>
      open a portal to Raiden's Sky Temple and head to the Jinsei Chamber, the source of Earthrealm's life force, where
      <a href="/wiki/Raiden_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Raiden (Mortal Kombat)">
       Raiden
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Fujin" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">
       Fujin
      </a>
      are seen battling Shinnok and his Netherrealm forces. When Shinnok tries to kill Sonya, Johnny rushes to defend her, inadvertently awakening ancient superhuman powers within himself. Using his newly-found powers, Johnny holds Shinnok at bay long enough for Raiden to steal Shinnok's amulet and imprison him inside it, but Quan Chi escapes. Johnny, Raiden, and Sonya track him to his lair in the Netherrealm and defeat him, successfully restoring
      <a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Scorpion (Mortal Kombat)">
       Scorpion
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sub-Zero_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Sub-Zero (Mortal Kombat)">
       Sub-Zero
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Jax_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Jax (Mortal Kombat)">
       Jax
      </a>
      to human form in the process, though Quan Chi escapes again. In the years that follow, Johnny and Sonya marry and have a daughter,
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Cassie_Cage" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">
       Cassie
      </a>
      , though they later divorce. Scorpion reinstates his Shirai Ryu clan while mentoring Kenshi's son
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Takeda_Takahashi" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">
       Takeda
      </a>
      , and Sub-Zero becomes the new Grandmaster of the Lin Kuei after killing
      <a href="/wiki/Sektor" title="Sektor">
       Sektor
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Twenty years after Shinnok's defeat, Johnny assembles a team of fighters composed of Cassie, Takeda, Jax's daughter
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Jacqui_Briggs" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">
       Jacqui
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Kung_Lao" title="Kung Lao">
       Kung Lao
      </a>
      's cousin
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Kung_Jin" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">
       Kung Jin
      </a>
      . After undergoing a training exercise with Sub-Zero, they are deployed to Outworld to resolve a civil war between former empress
      <a href="/wiki/Mileena" title="Mileena">
       Mileena
      </a>
      , who has obtained Shinnok's amulet from
      <a href="/wiki/Kano_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Kano (Mortal Kombat)">
       Kano
      </a>
      , and Outworld's current emperor
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kotal_Kahn" title="Kotal Kahn">
       Kotal Kahn
      </a>
      , with whom Earthrealm has a peace treaty. Meanwhile, in an Outworld refugee camp in Earthrealm, Sonya captures Kano and forces him to reveal Mileena's location. Using this information, Cassie's team aids Kotal in recovering Shinnok's amulet and capturing Mileena, who is subsequently executed on Kotal's orders. No longer believing Earthrealm capable of keeping the amulet safe, Kotal decides to keep it in Outworld's hands, and takes Cassie and her friends hostage as leverage against Raiden. Kotal's second-in-command
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#D.27Vorah" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">
       D'Vorah
      </a>
      , who is revealed as a double agent for Quan Chi, steals the amulet. Cassie and her team escape captivity, and inform Earthrealm of D'Vorah's intentions.
     </p>
     <p>
      Hoping to stop Quan Chi from getting the amulet, Jax and Kenshi, aided by
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Sareena" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">
       Sareena
      </a>
      , lead an assault on the Netherrealm. After fighting his way through the revenants, Jax captures a weakened Quan Chi, who is brought to Earthrealm and held at an Outworld refugee camp. Scorpion, having learned from Sub-Zero the truth of Quan Chi's involvement in the deaths of his family and clan, infiltrates the refugee camp alongside his clan with the intent of assassinating Quan Chi, defeating Kenshi, Johnny and Sonya in the process. As Scorpion prepares to kill Quan Chi, D'Vorah arrives with the amulet. Quan Chi manages to finish a spell moments before being beheaded by Scorpion, releasing Shinnok from captivity. Weakened by the Shirai Ryu's previous attack, Johnny is taken hostage by Shinnok and D'Vorah. With the aid of Quan Chi's revenants, Shinnok and D'Vorah assault the Sky Temple, and subdue Raiden in the process. Shinnok then enters the Jinsei and corrupts it, transforming himself into a powerful demon in the process.
     </p>
     <p>
      Cassie's team pursues Shinnok, only to be intercepted by Kotal Kahn and his army. They inform him of Shinnok's return and Kotal decides to kill them, hoping to appease Shinnok and buy time to bolster Outworld's defenses, but Sub-Zero and his Lin Kuei warriors appear just in time to repel the Outworld forces. At the Sky Temple, Jacqui and Takeda occupy the revenants, while Cassie and Jin enter the Jinsei chamber. Cassie defeats D'Vorah, though Jin is knocked out, leaving her to face Shinnok alone. When Shinnok begins torturing Johnny, Cassie gains her father's superhuman powers, which she uses to defeat Shinnok. A grievously-wounded Raiden then purifies the Jinsei, stripping Shinnok of his powers and forcing the revenants to withdraw. Sonya and her soldiers arrive to take Shinnok and D'Vorah away, and the Cage family reunites.
     </p>
     <p>
      Some time later, the revenants
      <a href="/wiki/Liu_Kang" title="Liu Kang">
       Liu Kang
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Kitana" title="Kitana">
       Kitana
      </a>
      are revealed to have assumed leadership of the Netherrealm. Raiden, now in possession of Shinnok's amulet and corrupted in the process of purifying the Jinsei, warns them they will face "fates worse than death" if they threaten Earthrealm. Before departing, Raiden leaves behind Shinnok's severed, yet still living head as a final warning.
     </p>

        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>

       Mortal Kombat X
      </i>
      received positive reviews. Aggregating review websites
      <a href="/wiki/GameRankings" title="GameRankings">
       GameRankings
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">
       Metacritic
      </a>
      gave the Xbox One version 85.86% based on 14 reviews and 85/100 based on 20 reviews,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRXONE_58-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GRXONE-58">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        58
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCXONE_61-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-MCXONE-61">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        61
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the PlayStation 4 version 84.15% based on 55 reviews and 83/100 based on 77 reviews
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRPS4_59-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GRPS4-59">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        59
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCPS4_62-1">

        </span>
        62
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the Microsoft Windows version 76.50% based on 4 reviews and 78/100 based on 9 reviews.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRPC_60-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GRPC-60">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        60
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCPC_63-1">

        63

     <p>
      Brian Shea of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Game_Informer" title="Game Informer">
        Game Informer
       </a>
      </i>
      called
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat X
      </i>
      "more than the continuation of NetherRealm's successful vision for the franchise; it's one of the best fighting games in years", adding that the core gameplay was complex and varied while he enjoyed the multiplayer options.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GI_66-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GI-66">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        66
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/GameSpot" title="GameSpot">
       GameSpot
      </a>
      's Peter Brown enjoyed the overall gameplay, saying it had "the best fighting mechanics of any game in the series" but was critical of the downloadable content as "there's so much to love about the new
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat
      </i>
      that it's a shame to see such blatant
      <a href="/wiki/Monetization" title="Monetization">
       monetization
      </a>
      practices overlap with your experience".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSpot_68-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GSpot-68">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        68
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Lucas Sullivan of
      <a href="/wiki/GamesRadar" title="GamesRadar">
       GamesRadar
      </a>
      described the story mode as "ceaselessly entertaining", having "great voice-acting and a surprisingly enjoyable script", and "a pretty tough act to follow", criticizing the other single-player modes outside of the main story, and "sparse" tutorial options.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRadar_69-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GRadar-69">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        69
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Michael Huber of
      <a href="/wiki/GameTrailers" title="GameTrailers">
       GameTrailers
      </a>
      called it a "superb entry in the franchise", noting the gameplay, story mode and new online components.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GT_70-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GT-70">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        70
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Vince Ingenito of
      <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">
       IGN
      </a>
      gave it a score of 8.4. He claimed that
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat X
      </i>
      was the best
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat
      </i>
      game in the franchise's history, lauding it as deeper, mechanically richer and more fully featured than the previous
      <i>
       Mortal Kombat
      </i>
      games. He also praised the presence of the new characters as the major roster shakeup made the game feel new and exciting to longtime series fans and casual fans.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_72-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-IGN-72">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        72
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Michael McWhertor of
      <a href="/wiki/Polygon_(website)" title="Polygon (website)">
       Polygon
      </a>
      called the game "one of the best fighting games in the series", praising the gameplay in addition to the newer systems in place and overall presentation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Poly_74-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Poly-74">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        74
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Stephen Kleckner of
      <a href="/wiki/VentureBeat" title="VentureBeat">
       VentureBeat
      </a>
      was more critical of the game, citing in his opinion confusing move animations, a system favoring close range mid-to-high combos, having to use the block button to execute a run, poor online play, and easy Fatality DLC.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VentureBeat_76-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-VentureBeat-76">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        76
