           <h3 class="entry-header">
            Oil prices in currencies other than the USD
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              I've received a request to update my irregular series of graphs of movements in oil prices in currencies other than the USD. There's been a certain amount of movement in the oil and forex markets over the past few months, so I'm happy to oblige:
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://worthwhile.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451688169e20120a60fa8d2970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Oil_currencies_10_09" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83451688169e20120a60fa8d2970b " src="http://worthwhile.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451688169e20120a60fa8d2970b-800wi" style="width: 800px;"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              The CAD and the euro have appreciated by about 20% with respect to the USD and the yen since February. And inflation is below target in both Canada and the euro area. So it may not be a coincidence that
              <a href="http://blogs.ft.com/maverecon/2009/10/time-for-the-ecb-to-get-serious-about-the-overvalued-euro/" target="_blank">
               Willem Buiter's recommendation to the ECB
              </a>
              looks a lot like
              <a href="http://worthwhile.typepad.com/worthwhile_canadian_initi/2009/10/disinflation-continues.html" target="_blank">
               my recommendation to the Bank of Canada
              </a>
              .
              <br/>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>

And while I agree with you that a strengthening Loonie is deflationary (or at least I did agree), there's a problem with the line of argument comparing the relative strength of the USD, Yen, Euro and CAD, and correlating that strength to inflation being below target. Perhaps the relative strength of CAD and the Euro mere reflective of the higher inflation rates in those two currency areas?
