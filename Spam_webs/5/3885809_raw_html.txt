             <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
              Where Are the Women Of New Atheism And Why Are They Not Recognized As Much As Their Male Counterparts?
             </h1>
             <div class="meta-info">
              <ul class="td-category">
               <li class="entry-category">
                <a href="http://natskep.com/category/atheism/">
                 Atheism
                </a>
               </li>
               <li class="entry-category">
                <a href="http://natskep.com/category/znatskep/">
                 zNatskep
                </a>
               </li>
              </ul>
              <time class="entry-date updated" datetime="2013-09-09T12:00:37+00:00" itemprop="dateCreated">
               Sep 9, 2013
              </time>
              <meta content="UserComments:0" itemprop="interactionCount"/>
              <div class="entry-comments-views">
               <span class="td-sp td-sp-ico-comments td-fake-click" data-fake-click="http://natskep.com/where-are-the-women-of-new-atheism-and-why-are-they-not-recognized-as-much-as-their-male-counterparts/#comments">
               </span>
               0
               <span class="td-sp td-sp-ico-view">

               <span class="td-nr-views-1746">
                442
               </span>
              </div>
             </div>
            </header>
            <div class="td-post-text-content">
             <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1999" style="width: 600px;">
              <a href="http://natskep.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Sam-Harris-Menof-Atheism-2b.jpg">
               <img alt="Men of Atheism, Sam Harris" class="size-full wp-image-1999" height="369" src="http://natskep.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Sam-Harris-Menof-Atheism-2b.jpg" width="600"/>
              </a>
              <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
               Men of Atheism, Sam Harris
              </figcaption>
             </figure>
             <p>
              In an
              <a href="http://www.salon.com/2013/07/21/from_hitchens_to_dawkins_where_are_the_women_of_new_atheism/">
               article for Salon
              </a>
              , Katie Engelhart discussed how the new movements of secular churches are disregarding female leadership just as the old religious churches have done throughout history.
              <br/>
              Engelhart poses the question of whether the New, New Atheism a stronger movement with the intention of building an atheist church, will include women. When thinking back to the names that are considered founders or non-belief leaders of the atheist movement, Richard Dawkins, Christopher Hitchens, Sam Harris, Peter Singer, A.C. Grayling, Daniel Dennett, etc., she asks, Where were the women?
              <br/>
              Around the time that the Dawkins-Hitchens-Harris tripartite published its big wave of Atheist critique, historian Jennifer Michael Hecht published Doubt and journalist Susan Jacoby published Freethinkersboth critically acclaimed, writes Engelhart, And yet, these women, and many others, failed to emerge as public figures, household names, she adds.
             </p>
             <p>
              She continues on listing some of the reasons that people have given on the so-called absence of female atheist leadership, such as that women in general are more devout and less atheist than men according to statistical accounts  and data; that women arent as militant in defending their non-beliefs; or as Susan Jacoby argued that atheists have not supported womens causes as militantly.
             </p>
             <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
             <div style="float:left;margin:20px 20px 20px 0;">
              Click Here to Buy from Amazon!
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/044654034X/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=044654034X&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=httpwwwhub0db-20">
               <img src="http://ecx.images-amazon.com/images/I/51ty6xJ8PlL._SY344_PJlook-inside-v2,TopRight,1,0_SH20_BO1,204,203,200_.jpg"/>
              </a>
             </div>
             <p>
              In a critique to her article Hemant Mehta of Pathos asked, Is it the movements fault for not elevating women to higher status (whatever that means), or is it societys fault for not more strongly embracing (or even rejecting) outspoken atheist women? While considering the number of female atheists that are publically acclaimed such Engelharts exampes Hecht and Jacoby, he poses the question, At what point does a prominent female atheist become prominent enough.
             </p>
             <p>
              The debate raised by Engelhart and the questions that Mehta demands to be asked, are nothing new in the whole debate of feminism and overall female achievement in comparison to males.
             </p>
             <p>
              In the new film Before Midnight, Julie Delpy says that women arent as competitive as men, because we dont have much to compare ourselves with, as women struggled for much longer than men to be recognized. But today we women do not compete only with women, and do not compare ourselves only with women; we compare ourselves and we compete with males.
             </p>
             <p>
              But the reality is that if we just discuss society alone, theres no question that women have been marginalized for centuries, and it still continues today. Women are still not as represented in politics, still not as represented in science, still not as represented by the Nobel Prize.
             </p>
             <p>
              As a matter of fact the Nobel Prize has been awarded 807 times to men and 44 times to women. Curt Rice writes that the Nobel Peace Prize Committee Chair, Thorbjrn Jagland, has said repeatedly that
              <br/>
              The sex of the candidates is irrelevant; the award goes to the individual making the greatest contribution. And yet Rice asks, is it that men have done 85% of the meritorious work or is that women are not seen.
             </p>
             <p>
              The debate of the atheist leadership of women can go endlessly just as the debate of womens political leadership, and scientific achievement, and womens success in every other field. When Mehta asks when does the prominent female atheist become prominent enough, I believe Engelhart has inadvertently answered that question, when she demands that the names of secular thinkers of the female gender be recognized on the same level with the names of the white boys club.
             </p>
             <p>
              The question in our minds remains, is that women arent doing the work, or is that they are not being recognized for the work?
             </p>
             <p>
              Dr Helena Cronin, co-director of the Centre for Philosophy of Natural and Social Science at the London School of Economics, argues that from the beginning of sexual reproduction 800 million years ago one sex specialized slightly more in competing, and the other slightly more in caring, and this goes on 800 million years of natural selection later. Not just our species but all species divided by sex, the variance in males is higher, meaning that the difference between the largest and the smallest is higher, while females tend to cluster around the average, around the mean much more, she explains in a Podcast for the Guardian. People look at the means, and they wonder why when on average girls are as good as boys, why boys shoot up the scale in the university and get the firsts and then go on and get the PhDs. But then she points out, that while women succeed on average, while men get the higher levels of success, men also get the lower ones. Among males there are more Nobels, but there are more Dumbbells too.
             </p>
             <p>
              So if Cronin is correct and biologically speaking, and because of natural selection, women tend to be more caring and less competitive, theres one reason why there are so many women who are doing the heavy work of secular leadership, but arent in the highest levels of leadership. Maybe, women are doing the heavy lifting in the trenches, whereas men are taking the spotlight.
             </p>
             <p>
              Regardless of biological, cultural, or historical reasons of why women arent recognized as much in the atheist debate, if that is really so, and not just a product of the media, theres not much debate in what needs to be done now to make the women just as prominent as men, and that is that women are going to have to stand up, proclaim themselves as atheists, and use the media to rise to prominence. When it comes to women issues, were just going to have to open ours roads ourselves, and climb to the top ourselves; just as always.
             </p>
