         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="A_pre-guide_note">
           <u>
            A pre-guide note
           </u>
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/The_Guitar_Resource_Guide?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit A pre-guide note section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>

          To get the most out of this Guitar Resource Guide it would

           help

          </li>
         </ul>

          </li>
         </ul>

          To the guide!

         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Things_to_acquire">
           Things to acquire
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/The_Guitar_Resource_Guide?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit Things to acquire section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <b>
            A tuner
           </b>
           , this is essential as it keeps the guitar sounding in great pitch. BONUS = Being able to tune your guitar by ear (but for now and important times, such as before a performance, you can't go wrong with a digital tuner).
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <b>
            A Metronome
           </b>
           , one of the most useful tools you will come across. When starting out/practicing it helps build your natural rhythm for the future so you can keep in time when playing to a backing track or with another guitarist for example.
           <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://onlinemetronome.co/" rel="nofollow">
            Online Metronome
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <b>
            Guitar Pro / Tux Guitar
           </b>
           Guitar Pro offers more than TuxGuitar such as an more aesthetically pleasing interface and a few more features but essentially the two applications do the same job of being
           <b>
            notation programs
           </b>
           . Why is this awesome? You can note down your own riffs and download files in the
           <b>
            Guitar Pro
           </b>
           format from
           <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.ultimate-guitar.com/" rel="nofollow">
            Ultimate-Guitar
           </a>
           of songs and albums which shows the music in both
           <b>
            tabulature
           </b>
           and
           <b>
            standard notation
           </b>
           . Other advantages include being able to compose MIDI songs and slowing it down to learn the music at a slow tempo before you play it full speed. LINKS:
           <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://tuxguitar.herac.com.ar/" rel="nofollow">
            TuxGuitar
           </a>
           <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.guitar-pro.com/" rel="nofollow">
            Guitar Pro
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Essential_Resources">
           The Essential Resources
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/The_Guitar_Resource_Guide?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit The Essential Resources section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.zentao.com/guitar/guitar-lessons.html" rel="nofollow">
           Zentao Guitar Lessons
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This website is awesome. If you want to be great at playing the guitar, use this website. It gives you the basic theory you need to get started on the beautiful instrument, both left and right hand techniques, tons of chords, and it's all laid out for you neatly and simply
          <b>
           for free
          </b>
          , so there's no excuse not to use it. TIP: Only advance to the next lesson once you have completely understood the previous one, this will make for more efficient learning.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.justinguitar.com/" rel="nofollow">
           Justin Guitar
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          It would be incredibly difficult to find a better (largely) free guitar education website. Justin Sandercoe has hundreds of video lessons taking you from the moment you unpackage that beauty of a new guitar to when you are wielding that axe with skill and precision. It's a great idea to start with his beginner's course, but feel free to skip around if a real interesting technique catches your eye.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.mediafire.com/?imnwyjkdiqm50tm" rel="nofollow">
           Steve Vai's 30 Hour Workout
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          WafflesWaffles being the good guy he is, uploaded this important workout routine to his mediafire account for fellow guitarists and musicians to make use of. Finger exercises are some of the most important yet frustrating things you will ever have to do as a guitarist. These will help fight the steep learning curve for co-ordination, speed and dexterity.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.guitar-pro.com" rel="nofollow">
           Guitar Pro 6 Software
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Guitar Pro is a really useful software, that allows you to edit, visualize and share tabs. It offers lots of tools that make learning songs faster (scale-validating tool, tuner, metronome, guitar fretboard), and is also good for tabbing out your own songs.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.all-guitar-chords.com/" rel="nofollow">
           All Guitar Chords.com
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This is pure gold, I recommend that you spend a couple of hours on this website to see how awesome it is.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.jazzguitar.be/jazzguitar_lessons.html" rel="nofollow">
           Jazz Guitar.be Lessons
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Probably more useful for those who have already grasped the basics, goes over some of the more applicable areas of music theory such as throwing 7th chords into your playing as an example. It also explains all the 'difficult stuff you never understood' like modes etc.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.andrewwasson.com/index.php" rel="nofollow">
           Andrew Wasson
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This guy is a god. Bookmark this, even homepage it if you fancy! He offers online guitar theory lessons, technique lessons, podcasts and information about the music business.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.mediafire.com/?zmttjy2zdjn" rel="nofollow">
           William Russo - Composing Music
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This .pdf book is helpful for writing tighter, more memorable melodies and music. If you feel like your songwriting needs the extra oompf then give this a read.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.4shared.com/dir/31493207/b15ad282/Music_Books.html" rel="nofollow">
           4shared archive of Music Books
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           TONS
          </b>
          of music books, ready to download. What else can I say, you've hit the jackpot.
         </p>
         <p>
          There you go! Thanks for reading, I hope its helpful.
         </p>

          Thanks to:
