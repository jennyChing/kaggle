     <h1>
      <a href="http://gapersblock.com/drivethru/2009/09/22/how_far_to_mcdonalds/">
       How Far to McDonald's?
      </a>
     </h1>
     <!--START If Author is not Drive Thru, Provide Byline-->
     <p class="byline">
      <em>
       By
      </em>
      Andrew Huff
     </p>
     <!--END If Author is not Drive Thru, Provide Byline-->
     <p>
      <a href="http://www.weathersealed.com/2009/09/22/where-the-buffalo-roamed/">
       <img alt="mcdonalds map" border="0" height="363" src="http://gapersblock.com/drivethru/mcdonaldsmap.jpg" width="500"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      In most of the United States, not far at all. The image above is
      <a href="http://www.weathersealed.com/2009/09/22/where-the-buffalo-roamed/">
       a map of all 13,000 McDonald's restaurants
      </a>
      , with color indicating distance to the nearest Golden Arches. Unsurprisingly, The area around Chicago is particularly bright; if you zoomed in on Chicagoland, the dots stay pretty damn dense:
     </p>
     <p>
      <iframe frameborder="0" height="350" marginheight="0" marginwidth="0" scrolling="no" src="http://maps.google.com/maps?f=q&amp;source=s_q&amp;hl=en&amp;geocode=&amp;q=McDonald's&amp;sll=41.848337,-87.650642&amp;sspn=0.928855,2.469177&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;rq=1&amp;ev=zo&amp;ll=41.848337,-87.650642&amp;spn=0.928855,2.469177&amp;output=embed" width="500">
      </iframe>
      <br/>
      <small>
       <a href="http://maps.google.com/maps?f=q&amp;source=embed&amp;hl=en&amp;geocode=&amp;q=McDonald's&amp;sll=41.848337,-87.650642&amp;sspn=0.928855,2.469177&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;rq=1&amp;ev=zo&amp;ll=41.848337,-87.650642&amp;spn=0.928855,2.469177" style="color:#0000FF;text-align:left">
        View Larger Map
       </a>
      </small>
     </p>
     <p>
      Where's the spot farthest from a McDonald's?
      <a href="http://maps.google.com/maps?f=q&amp;source=s_q&amp;hl=en&amp;geocode=&amp;q=45.45955,+-101.91356&amp;sll=41.898188,-87.648926&amp;sspn=1.856273,4.938354&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;ll=45.469762,-101.914673&amp;spn=0.922605,2.469177&amp;z=9&amp;iwloc=A">
       Glad Valley, SD
      </a>
      , at 145 miles by car.
     </p>
     <p>
      UPDATE: Stephen Von Worley, the creator of the McDonald's map, has
      <a href="http://www.weathersealed.com/2009/10/20/the-hungry-midwest/">
       produced a follow-up
      </a>
      focusing on the Midwest (really, more like the Great Lakes region). Fun fact: there are 424 McDonald's within 50 miles of the Sears Tower!
     </p>
