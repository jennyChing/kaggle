       <h1>
        :: Crochet A Paper Chain
       </h1>
       <span class="date">
        December 9, 2014
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="post-img">
       <a href="http://meetmeatmikes.com/crochet-a-paper-chain/">
        <img alt="crochet-chain-a-la-pip" class="attachment-full-thumb wp-post-image" height="500" src="http://meetmeatmikes.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/crochet-chain-a-la-pip.jpg" width="700"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="post-entry">
       <p>
        <img alt="crochet-chain-a-la-pip-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31330" height="500" src="http://meetmeatmikes.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/crochet-chain-a-la-pip-2.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <div class="page" title="Page 49">
        <div class="layoutArea">
         <div class="column">
          <p>
           Its time to decorate the house. Are you getting all festive and sparkly at yours? We are slowly getting things sorted. Weve put the tree up and the advent too. And weve done our Christmas window, of course! I am going to make a few other bits and bobs over the next week Something about making things well and truly cements the tinselly season for me. Do you feel a bit like that too?
          </p>
          <p>
           Im doing other stuff with the 25th and New Year in mind. I bought a new diary. Were planning our break (staying home!) Ive almost finished my shopping AND weve nearly drawn up the menu and accompanying shopping list too. Im feeling pretty cosy and organised and feeling glad that I have lovely people in my life.
          </p>
          <p>
           If youd like to make this cute crocheted chain, its super easy. You can use my pattern as a guide, or just go nuts and work it out for yourself. Sometimes just seeing an example of something cute and Christmassy is enough to get you inspired to do it your own way, isnt it? Heres how to do it my way, if you so desire
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="crochet-chain-a-la-pip-again" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31329" height="500" src="http://meetmeatmikes.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/crochet-chain-a-la-pip-again.jpg" width="700"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Make a crocheted chain a la Pip
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Using 8ply yarn and a 3mm hook  or whatever you prefer
          </p>
          <p>
           Im using Australian/UK crochet terminology (go here to convert to US!)
          </p>
          <p>
           Lets go!
           <br/>
           Chain 9 stitches
           <br/>
           Skip the first stitch and double crochet once into each chain until the end of the row
          </p>
          <p>
           *Double crochet once into each chain until the end of the row. Chain one*
           <br/>
           Turn repeat pattern between * and * until your rectangle measures approx. 10cm.
          </p>
          <p>
           Make as many of these as you like.
           <br/>
           Sew the first rectangle into a loop using matching yarn. Loop the next rectangle link through the first and stitch together neatly.
           <br/>
           Continue like this until your chain is the length you prefer.
          </p>
          <p>
           Add tassels too, if you like. I made mine by wrapping the yarn around a small book. Then I snipped the bottom row ofwrapped yarn  at the bottom edge of the book  and carefully manoeuvred it off the book. NextI tied the
           <strong>
            unsnipped yarn
           </strong>
           at the
           <strong>
           </strong>
           top edge of the book together with a length of yarn (about an inch from the fold) Thats all there is to tassels, really!
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           I hope youre having a nice lead up to Christmas. Im pushing on, despite a bit of a tough yesterday. Today is a new day, right?
          </p>
          <p>
           x Pip
          </p>
