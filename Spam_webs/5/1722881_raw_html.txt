       <div class="entry-content brandviews">
        <p>
         <img alt="Great-Infographic" class="attachment-featured wp-post-image aligncenter wp-image-1135631 size-full" height="350" src="http://cdn2.business2community.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Great-Infographic.png.png" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Did you know that we remember 80% of what we see, yet only 20% of what we read? The
         <a class="external" href="https://www.wyzowl.com/blog/power-visual-communication-infographic/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.wyzowl.com/blog/power-visual-communication-infographic/', 'power of visual content']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          power of visual content
         </a>
         is hard to ignore.
        </p>
        <p>
         And thats why an infographic is such a valuable piece of content.
        </p>
        <p>
         Not only is it one of most engaging ways to tell your story, but businesses who market with infographics actually gain on average
         <a class="external" href="http://blog.wishpond.com/post/70300587846/10-reasons-visual-content-will-dominate-2014" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://blog.wishpond.com/post/70300587846/10-reasons-visual-content-will-dominate-2014', '12%']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          12%
         </a>
         more traffic than those who dont.
        </p>
        <p>
         So what makes a good infographic? Well, youre about to find out
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. A Targeted Audience
        </h3>
        <p>
         As with any piece of content, if you want your infographic to be a success then you need to create one for your target audience. When coming up with the concept, do your research and find out what your audience likes, so that you can create an infographic that they will just have to share because its so on-point with their thoughts.
        </p>
        <p>
         Think about it like this. Your target audience has a problem (otherwise you wouldnt exist) and your job is to solve that problem. When coming up with concepts, your goal should be to show your audience that you are their solution, using the creativity of an infographic.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you dont have one, make sure you create a buyer persona so you know exactly who you are creating your infographic for.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Exclusive Bonus:
         </strong>
         <a class="external" href="https://my.leadpages.net/leadbox/14657b173f72a2%3A10c3425cbb46dc/5700305828184064/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://my.leadpages.net/leadbox/14657b173f72a2%3A10c3425cbb46dc/5700305828184064/', 'Download']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Download
         </a>
         an awesome template to help you create your own buyer persona today!
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. A Compelling Theme
        </h3>
        <p>
         If you check out the largest online community for infographics 
         <a class="external" href="http://visual.ly/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://visual.ly/', 'Visual.ly']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Visual.ly
         </a>
          then youll see that each one comes with a different theme. Your theme is essentially your story and it needs to correlate with your brand, as your infographic will become another footprint in your content marketing journey.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you check out this example below from Home Food Safety, you can see that theyve really nailed their theme. The aim is to teach people the importance of cooking safety to help avoid food poisoning. Their theme is set within the various focus areas of the kitchen, and it really matches their brand too!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-01-21 at 15.08.57" class=" aligncenter wp-image-1135632 size-full" height="791" src="http://www.business2community.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Screen-Shot-2015-01-21-at-15.08.57.png.png" width="684"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You can check out the full
         <a class="external" href="https://www.wyzowl.com/content-infographic-food-safety-grill.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.wyzowl.com/content-infographic-food-safety-grill.html', 'infographic']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          infographic
         </a>
         here.
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Actionable Data
        </h3>
        <p>
         Next step should be to find the right data for your infographic, and this should be a heavy focus throughout. Dont just create one because you think it looks cool. For people to want to share it, it needs to feature stats that will back up your case.
        </p>
        <p>
         The key here is to find stats that will help prove to your audience that your product or service is the solution that they need in their life.
        </p>
        <p>
         As you can see from this snippet of one of our infographics below. As a video company, were looking to attract marketers who are interested in creating an explainer video, so weve used stats to show how powerful video can be.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-01-21 at 15.02.01" class=" aligncenter wp-image-1135633 size-full" height="638" src="http://www.business2community.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Screen-Shot-2015-01-21-at-15.02.01.png.png" width="538"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You can check out the full
         <a class="external" href="https://www.wyzowl.com/content-infographic-online-video-by-the-billions.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.wyzowl.com/content-infographic-online-video-by-the-billions.html', 'infographic']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          infographic
         </a>
         here.
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Awesome Graphics
        </h3>
        <p>
         This is an obvious point to make, but your infographic
         <em>
          has
         </em>
         to look good! The graphics you use within it will help guide the viewer through the story, and if you havent put much effort into it then youll never see any shares. If you dont have an in-house design team then think about
         <a class="external" href="https://www.wyzowl.com/content-marketing.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.wyzowl.com/content-marketing.html', 'outsourcing your infographic']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          outsourcing your infographic
         </a>
         , because this is too important not to get right.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some of our top tips from our infographic designers include:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Keep your graphics simple
         </li>
         <li>
          Tell your story in sections so it flows
         </li>
         <li>
          Dont use too many colors
         </li>
         <li>
          Make it readable
         </li>
         <li>
          Match up your visuals with data
         </li>
         <li>
          Stay true to your brand
         </li>
         <li>
          Dont be too repetitive
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         This infographic below is a great example of simple graphics split into sections to effectively tell the story
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.business2community.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Screen-Shot-2015-01-21-at-16.02.37.png.png" rel="nofollow">
          <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-01-21 at 16.02.37" class=" aligncenter wp-image-1135634 size-full" height="548" src="http://www.business2community.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Screen-Shot-2015-01-21-at-16.02.37.png.png" width="481"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         You can check out the full
         <a class="external" href="https://www.wyzowl.com/content-infographic-10-types-of-store-owners.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.wyzowl.com/content-infographic-10-types-of-store-owners.html', 'infographic']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          infographic
         </a>
         here.
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Powerful Copy
        </h3>
        <p>
         Yep thats right, its not all about how it looks. The copy on your infographic is just as important! Were not saying make it copy-heavy but If you want to share it on social then you need to come up with powerful headlines to really convey the message.
        </p>
        <p>
         Your headline needs to be strong so that people will be compelled to share it. Keep it simple, concise and relevant to the theme. Quite like blog posts, a great way to grab attention and invite people to check out your infographic is to create a how-to headline or one that asks a question.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres an example of one that we created with the headline:
         <strong>
          How much do average apps make?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-01-21 at 16.26.38" class=" aligncenter wp-image-1135635 size-full" height="388" src="http://www.business2community.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Screen-Shot-2015-01-21-at-16.26.38.png.png" width="689"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You can check out the full
         <a class="external" href="https://www.wyzowl.com/content-infographic-how-much-do-apps-make.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.wyzowl.com/content-infographic-how-much-do-apps-make.html', 'infographic']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          infographic
         </a>
         here.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Whats Next?
        </h3>
        <p>
         So once youve put together your infographic with all of the above points in mind, the next step is to start outreaching.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sure you can promote it on your social, but if you really want to make it go viral then you need to put the effort in. There are plenty of bloggers and influencers out there who will be looking for an infographic just like yours, so start putting a strategy in place to contact them and see if they will feature it on their site.
        </p>
