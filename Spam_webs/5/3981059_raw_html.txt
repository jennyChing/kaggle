       Taking inspiration from the
       <a href="http://www.dexigner.com/news/23851">
        C-X16 concept
       </a>
       unveiled in 2011, the front of the F-TYPE features a new interpretation of the bold angular Jaguar grille from which flows the muscular clamshell bonnet with its signature 'power bulge.'

       <a class="imglink" data-image="22928/Jaguar_F_Type_13" href="http://media.dexigner.com/article/22928/Jaguar_F_Type_13.jpg" style="height:304px;width:552px" target="_blank">

       </a>

       <br/>
       <a class="imglink" data-image="22928/Jaguar_F_Type_14" href="http://media.dexigner.com/article/22928/Jaguar_F_Type_14.jpg" style="height:255px;width:552px" target="_blank">
        <img alt="Jaguar F Type 14" class="aimg" height="274" src="http://cdx.dexigner.com/article/22928/Jaguar_F_Type_14_thumb.jpg" width="550"/>
       </a>
       <br style="clear:left;"/>
       <br/>
       The all important heartline begins its journey in the blade dissecting the shark-like gills on either side of the grille before leading the eye to the top of the front wing, emphasised by the bi-xenon headlamp and integral LED daytime running lights, into the door and towards the rear of the car where it gracefully disappears.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <a class="imglink" data-image="22928/Jaguar_F_Type_10" href="http://media.dexigner.com/article/22928/Jaguar_F_Type_10.jpg" style="height:316px;width:552px" target="_blank">
        <img alt="Jaguar F Type 10" class="aimg" height="335" src="http://cdx.dexigner.com/article/22928/Jaguar_F_Type_10_thumb.jpg" width="550"/>
       </a>
       <br style="clear:left;"/>
       <br/>
       <a class="imglink" data-image="22928/Jaguar_F_Type_11" href="http://media.dexigner.com/article/22928/Jaguar_F_Type_11.jpg" style="height:300px;width:552px" target="_blank">
        <img alt="Jaguar F Type 11" class="aimg" height="319" src="http://cdx.dexigner.com/article/22928/Jaguar_F_Type_11_thumb.jpg" width="550"/>
       </a>
       <br style="clear:left;"/>
       <br/>
       The F-TYPE's interior features a driver-focused philosophy, with engaging controls encased in dramatic architecture. Unique technical grains tailor the trim materials, further differentiating the dual-cockpit cabin.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <a class="imglink" data-image="22928/Jaguar_F_Type_12" href="http://media.dexigner.com/article/22928/Jaguar_F_Type_12.jpg" style="height:318px;width:552px" target="_blank">
        <img alt="Jaguar F Type 12" class="aimg" height="337" src="http://cdx.dexigner.com/article/22928/Jaguar_F_Type_12_thumb.jpg" width="550"/>
       </a>
       <br style="clear:left;"/>
       <br/>
       Taking inspiration from cockpits of fighter airplanes, the controls are ergonomically grouped by function. Further aeronautical inspiration can be found in the joystick-shaped SportShift selector controlling the eight-speed transmission. The air vents on top of the dashboard will only deploy when instructed to by either the driver or complex control algorithms, staying tucked discreetly out of sight in other circumstances.
