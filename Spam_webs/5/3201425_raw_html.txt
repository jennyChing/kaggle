       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Queen of Hearts Tarts  Fit for Your Royal Family
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.lifeinpleasantville.com/author/karen/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Karen Humphrey
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.lifeinpleasantville.com/queen-hearts-tarts-fit-royal-family/#comments">
           2 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Its a weird world we live in, this age of technology. With the connectivity of Facebook you can keep in touch with people that you might normally lose track of, and it can bring back things youd never expect. The other day I made this tarts and stashed them in the fridge where my 19 year old found them. If you know 19 year old boys, you know that they eat just about anything and everything. Kevin was chattering away, rummaging in the fridge for sustenance, and suddenly he stopped cold.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cherry-tarts-00.jpg">
          <img alt="Cherry Tarts" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16059" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cherry-tarts-00.jpg?fit=700%2C525"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         These are SO good! Where did you get the idea? he mumbled through a mouthful of tart.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mrs. Koebel,  I called back.
        </p>
        <p>
         Wait. What? Mrs. Kobel? You chat with Mrs. Koebel?Two towns and a full 14 years ago, its been a long time since she sang her way through a day in kindergarten with Kevin. On his first Valentines Day in school, she baked him these very tarts-although she used canned pie filling, which is equally as tasty. I never would have thought to put cinnamon hearts in the filling, but the spice of the cinnamon adds some great flavour to the cherry. Try them warm with a scoop of ice cream, and you will thank me later.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mmm Kevin shoved the rest of the tart in his mouth. Tell her thanks. Those are awesome.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Queen of Hearts Tarts
        </h2>
        <p>
         Ingredients:
        </p>
        <p>
         one 600 gram package of frozen dark sweet cherries, thawed (Presidents Choice has a good brand)
         <br/>
         3 tbsp sugar
         <br/>
         2 tbsp cornstarch
         <br/>
         1/4 tsp almond extract
         <br/>
         12 tart shells
         <br/>
         50-60 cinnamon heart candies
        </p>
        <p>
         Directions:
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cherry-juice.jpg">
          <img alt="queen of hearts tarts, cherry juice, tarts" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16060" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cherry-juice.jpg?fit=700%2C525"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         1. First, pour off the cherry juice into a small dish and whisk it with the corn starch until smooth. Set aside.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cherry-pie-filling-3.jpg">
          <img alt="queen of hearts tarts, cherry pie filling 3" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16082" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cherry-pie-filling-3.jpg?fit=700%2C525"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Mix your cherries and sugar in a saucepan over medium heat until its bubbly. Stir it constantly so it doesnt burn. Gently stir in the cornstarch juice and continue to stir until its nice and thick. Remove from heat and let cool.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/tarts-and-hearts2.jpg">
          <img alt="queen of hearts tarts, tarts and hearts2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16083" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/tarts-and-hearts2.jpg?fit=700%2C525"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Set the tart shells on a cookie sheet and pre-heat the oven to 375 F. Fill the tart shells with the cherry pie filling, and press 3-5 cinnamon hearts in each tart.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Bake the tarts for about 15-20 minutes, until the pastry is lightly browned and the filling is slightly bubbly. Remove the tarts from the oven and let cool. They will keep 2-3 days in the fridge.
        </p>
