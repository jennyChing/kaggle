              <p>
               It's a good day for Elevate, which was just named
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/apple/">
                Apple
               </a>
               's favorite app of 2014.
              </p>
              <p>
               The app, which helps you improve memory and focus, was Apple's top pick of the year. The runner-up was Instagram's
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/08/26/hyperlapse-app-instagram/">
                Hyperlapse
               </a>
               , which speeds up shaky videos and turns them into time lapses in just two taps.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/12/01/smart-home-gifts-2014/">
                 10 gifts to make your home a 'smart home'
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               Apple compiled its annual list of what it considers to be the year's most standout apps, with categories ranging from "best apps" to "best games."
              </p>
              <p>
               Some notable inclusions are
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/01/07/yahoo-news-digest/">
                Yahoo News Digest
               </a>
               , Facebook's
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/01/30/facebook-announces-paper/">
                Paper
               </a>
               and the SwiftKey Keyboard app. With
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/09/23/best-things-about-ios-8/">
                iOS 8
               </a>
               , which debuted in September, Apple allowed iPhone users to pick alternative keyboard options from third-party developers; SwiftKey was quickly heralded as one of the best.
              </p>
              <p>
               Uber, which has come under a great deal of scrutiny over the past month for such things as "
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/11/19/uber-probing-josh-mohrer/">
                God view
               </a>
               " and
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/11/17/uber-executive-revenge/">
                floating the notion
               </a>
               of spending $1 million on digging up dirt on journalists, also appears on the list.
              </p>
