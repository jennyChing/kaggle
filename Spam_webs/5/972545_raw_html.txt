      <h1 class="post-title">
       Why No Woman Should Ever Be Scared of Turning 30
      </h1>
      <div class="post-meta">
       Posted on January 2, 2014
      </div>
      <div class="post-content">
       <h4 id="pressboard-ad-sponsorship-tag" style="margin-bottom: 35px;">
       </h4>
       <p>
        When I was a teenager, I couldnt see myself past the age of 30. In my diary, I routinely recorded that I would win my first Oscar by 23 years old, be married by 27 (either to Ben Affleck or David Duchovny) and push out my first baby by 29. Beyond that was just darkness; a horribly scary void that meant the end of supple skin, a high metabolism and acceptable irresponsibility masked as just bein young, son.
       </p>
       <p>
        Well, Im 31 now (actually, Im writing this to you on my 31
        <sup>
         st
        </sup>
        birthday), and suffice it to say, I didnt marry David Duchovny and pop out one of his kids by 30 (which is probably a blessing given hes apparently more like Hank Moody than Fox Mulder. Ugh!).
       </p>
       <p>
        But last year, I wasnt nearly as cool as I am now. I got sad and mad about the things that I wanted for myself but didnt have (my dream job, the dream husband, the Oscar, a real goddamn Louis Vuitton wallet). I had mini-meltdowns as I consulted those annoying must-have before 30 lists that womens magazines annually churn out, and thought: what the hell is wrong with me?!
       </p>
       <p>
        Well, as it turns out, nothing is wrong with me. Or you, fellow thirty-somethings/almost-thirty-somethings.
       </p>
       <p>
        Now that Im freshly post-thirty, I can tell you, with a pretty large amount of certainty, that everything youve read and/or been brainwashed about turning 30, is utter bullshit.
       </p>
       <p>
        Throw out your timelines and your to do lists, ladies. Youre not supposed to have it all figured out, despite what all those scary email chain letters say. This is what thirty really feels like.
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Less bullshit.
         </strong>
         Small talk with anyone can be brutal. But guess what? When youre thirty, you dont have to put up with that crap anymore. Suddenly you feel less of a people-pleaser and more of an adopter of the Aint Nobody Got Time For That philosophy. Want to stop talking to The Annoying Person at the Networking Event/Party/Bar? Then, stop! This attitude also extends to those toxic friends, co-workers and family members youve been meaning to shake, but felt you couldnt because of niceties and what will people think of me? syndrome. Youre not being unkind; its called finding your backbone.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <div>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Yes, your body is changing; let it.
         </strong>
         Maybe you cant eat a bag of Jalapeno Cheetos in one sitting anymore without witnessing the aftermath moments later on your thighs and butt, but thats probably for the best anyway because of cholesterol and sodium intake  things you actually start considering in your 30s. You will also notice these annoying chin hairs that suddenly sprout rapidly like youre some kind of Chia Pet; waxing or a good pair of tweezers will take care of them. Boobs start to sag (yes, even on us flat-chested gals) and smile crinkles appear around your eyes. Your back starts to hurt. Feet, too. Oh, yeah, and adult acne becomes this real, awful thing that usually spreads its wicked ugliness around
         <em>
          that
         </em>
         time of the month (treating it holistically with Sea Buckthorn Oil usually does the trick). I know this all sounds scary, but the cool thing about 30 is that you just sort of accept it; you start accepting yourself as is. Your awesome body is the only one you have and its gotten you this far, so it really does deserve major props.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <div>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Dating, relationships and sex at 30 is better.
         </strong>
         Telling the truth when it comes to matters of the heart and being vulnerable becomes easier at 30 mainly because youre not afraid to say what you want and need inside  and outside  of the bedroom. Theres nothing more refreshing or powerful than telling someone I like you/that/it or Get out of my apartment, dirt bag! All the truth-bombing is probably due to that pesky ticking biological clock, and the fact that youve been around the block a few times and know whats up. You know what you like and what you dont like, and because youre no longer suffering from what will people think of me? syndrome, youre more likely to speak your mind. Better to be hurt and validated than not having said anything at all and remain stuck in the same rut. Im not saying its all roses and couples costumes, but things just become less dramatic. Hallelujah!
        </li>
       </ul>
       <div>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Youre on your own journey.
         </strong>
         So you looked at Facebook recently and your newsfeed is a sea of engagement announcements and wedding pictures and baby bumps. Real grown up shit. When everyone is getting married and traveling and, you know, doing BIG things around you, its definitely easy to catch a case of the have-nots and feel sorry for yourself with visions of spinsterhood and a house filled of cats dancing in your head when youre single and still living in your parents basement. But this neednt be the case. The only thing you need to worry about is your own happiness. If youre single, youre single because youre fucking single. Not because youre picky or like to stay home and watch Netflix in your sweats and its definitely not because theres something wrong with you. Youre single because you havent found him, or her. Its not your time yet. And maybe its not your time to own a condo or give birth to another human, either. Thats OK, too. Thirty looks different to everyone. The best thing (besides not comparing your life to those on your Facebook) is to celebrate your victories. Maybe it was finally getting that promotion or paying off your student loan or winning a short story competition. Dont categorize your victories as big or small  theyre yours and yours alone, and thats what makes them dope.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <div>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Wisdom, you got it.
         </strong>
         You suddenly become this Yoda-like deity of sage advice for people younger than you, like interns, and its weirdly gratifying. All the so-called mistakes you made in your twenties, all the woulda/shoulda/couldas? Well, they actually, finally, come in handy. Apartment rental advice? You got this! How to properly iron a shirt? You got this! Credit card debt advice? Unfortunately, you got this one too. But, its also oddly satisfying to tell someone in her early twenties that you saw Backstreet Boys live in concert when she was a toddler. Well, kind of satisfying, and also mildly depressing (I didnt say turning 30 didnt totally suck).
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        Theres a reason why I got do you. tattooed on my left arm last May (I included the period in the tat because, to have left it unstated, seemed to welcome all sorts of awkward connotations). I had quit my full-time editorial job without a back-up plan and, thus, received a lot of backlash from other people who didnt understand my decision. Why on earth would I give up benefits, vacation time and a parking spot? They asked.
       </p>
       <p>
        To the outside world, I was supposedly living the adult dream when, really, I wasnt. I was miserable. The thing about turning 30 is that time really becomes of the essence. And, no, I dont mean that aforementioned pesky biological clock. What I mean is, I came to the point where I had to look at myself in the mirror and ask: is this where I want to be in my life? Is this who I want to be? Am I on the right path to where and who I want to be, professionally and personally? When I couldnt answer yes to ANY of those questions, I knew what I had to do: I quit my job and began this freelancing life, while pursuing my passion of writing for TV and film. I did me. And it was the best decision I ever made.
       </p>
       <p>
        If theres something you want to do at 30, do it. Try that improv class! Start writing that book! Plan that business venture youve always dreamed of! Its not too late at all; its actually the best time ever. Forget about the haters. Fuck, Facebook. Remember: you aint got time for that. Because, honestly, you wouldnt want return to your twenties for a million bucks (unless it was to pay off that credit card) and forty is just around the corner, which is when, Im guessing, we figure all this shit out.
       </p>

              <p>
               Turning 30 (still hard to say it) feels tougher than i though. I assumed it would all work out magically and every to-do on my list would have been done. Here i am counting days like a hopeless 29 year old. Fearing the countdown. Hating every passing moment as it's not what it was supposed to be.
               <span>
                People tell me to not be this hopeless about the approaching decade but i can't help but wonder do they really
               </span>
               <span>
                know how i feel?
               </span>
               ? The hopeful plans of the wonderful twenties are undone and time just flew. Years went by and life has changed. Yes, the wisdom continues but it sucks to be the one with all the answers and yet have none for yourself. The changes in the body are just the beginning of the fear, leaving your twenties behind with no clarity of what life would be when the thirties approach. The biological clock (how i hate it) ticking in my ears every morning i wake up and yeah the unlimited supply of Facebook posts of new engagements, wedding, baby showers and birthdays don't help at all. I am sure cutting myself off social media won't help. I will have to brave this and shine like i always did but HOW is the question of the hour. I don't know HOW to not feel this way. HOW do i get over myself and brace the new decade. HOW do i know what i should do next? HOW..HOW..HOW? I agree turning 30 is better than turning 40 but turning 20 wasn't that hard. I want to go back to my glory years and fix all i can so that when i reach the doorstep of 30 i know better than to ask HOW.
              </p>
