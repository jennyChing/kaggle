               <p id="eow-description">
                The Indigenous Marathon Project (IMP) is a health-promotion charity that changes the lives of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Island people by conquering the New York City Marathon off the back of only seven months of training. During their involvement with IMP, squad members also undertake a Certificate IV in Health and Leisure, and use their experiences to inspire their family, friends, communities and the wider Australian population.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Australia's own marathon legend Robert de Castella mentors young Indigenous Australians on their journey and takes a group of inspirational men and women to participate in the New York City marathon each year.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                30 year old Charlie Maher from Alice Springs in the Northern Territory became the first Indigenous Australian to ever run the New York Marathon when he crossed the finish line in 2010 in a time of 3 hours 32 minutes.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Charlie is an inspirational individual, changing the lives and minds of other Indigenous men and women who aspire to follow in his footsteps.
                <br/>
                <br/>
