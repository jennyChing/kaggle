        <div class="post-content">
         <p>
          <img alt="kegerator" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-243593" height="368" src="http://cdn.foodbeast.com.s3.amazonaws.com/content/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/kegerator.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          You might want to sit down for this one. The folks at Dream Arcades have invented the
          <strong>
           Kegerator Pro 60
          </strong>
          , aka the "ultimate party machine." No, it's not a keg with your favorite brew nor is it a nostalgic video game marathon. It's not even an HDTV screen of epic proportions.
         </p>
         <p>
          It's all damn three, and a fridge, because why f*ckin' not.
         </p>
         <p>
          We apologize for any fainting spells of disbelief that may have caused.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="kegerator-video-games" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-243388" height="537" src="http://cdn.foodbeast.com.s3.amazonaws.com/content/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/kegerator-video-games.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The Kegerator Pro 60
          <strong>
           features a built-in fridge, a keg with three separate taps, a 60-inch HDTV screen and more than 140 classic video games including Street Fighter, Pac-Man and Centipede.
          </strong>
          This beer-flowing arcade hybrid also comes equipped with a DVD player, MP3 software and internet capability. So, when you're not furiously lightning kicking into the air, you can watch TV or listen to music.
         </p>
         <p>
          Think
          <a href="http://foodbeast.com/content/2013/02/18/finish-him-the-last-barfighter-arcade-game-pours-the-winner-a-beer/" target="_blank">
           The Last Barfighter
          </a>
          , whichdebuted in February, but more games and booze.
         </p>
         <p>
          In an email to
          <a href="http://news.cnet.com/8301-10797_3-57599642-235/kegerator-fuses-vintage-video-games-with-a-beer-keg/" target="_blank">
           <em>
            CNET
           </em>
          </a>
          , the company promised that "players never have to stop the action to refill their drink" and thanks to the arcade's two built-in cup holders, "players can keep their drinks topped off while keeping one hand on the joystick."
         </p>
         <p>
          Sadly, as Nobel Prize-worthy as the Kegerator is, it also comes with a $5000 price tag. Dream Arcades indeed.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Kegerator Pro 60
           </strong>
           , $5000 @
           <a href="http://www.dreamarcades.com/tables.shtml" target="_blank">
            Dream Arcades
           </a>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>

         </p>
