     <p>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender" title="Avatar: The Last Airbender">
        Avatar: The Last Airbender
       </a>
      </i>
      is a 61-episode,
      <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">
       Emmy Award
      </a>
      -winning
      <a href="/wiki/Television_in_the_United_States" title="Television in the United States">
       American
      </a>
      animated television series written and created by
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dante_DiMartino" title="Michael Dante DiMartino">
       Michael Dante DiMartino
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Konietzko" title="Bryan Konietzko">
       Bryan Konietzko
      </a>
      . It first aired on February 21, 2005, on
      <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">
       Nickelodeon
      </a>
      with a one-hour series premiere
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iceberg_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Iceberg-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and concluded its run with a two-hour TV movie on July 19, 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVGuide_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-TVGuide-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <i>
       Avatar: The Last Airbender
      </i>
      franchise refers to each season as a "Book", in which each episode is referred to as a "chapter". Each "Book" takes its name from one of the elements that
      <a href="/wiki/Aang" title="Aang">
       Aang
      </a>
      , the protagonist, must master:
      <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">
       Water
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
       Earth
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">
       Fire
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iceberg_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Iceberg-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The show's first two seasons each consisted of 20 episodes, while the third season had 21. In addition to the three seasons, there were two recap episodes and three "shorts". The first recap summarized the first eighteen episodes while the second summarized season two. The first self-parody was released via an online flash game. The second and third were released with the Complete Second Season Box Set DVD.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Box2_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Box2-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The entire series has been released on DVD in
      <a href="/wiki/DVD_region_code" title="DVD region code">
       Region One
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/DVD_region_code" title="DVD region code">
       Region Two
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/DVD_region_code" title="DVD region code">
       Region Four
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In the
      <i>
       Avatar: The Last Airbender
      </i>
      universe, there are people who are able to manipulate, or "bend", the four elements:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air" title="Air">
       Air
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">
       Water
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
       Earth
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">
       Fire
      </a>
      . Along with the four elements, there are four nations that correspond with each element. Not everyone can bend an element, and those that can can only bend one. However, the Avatar is a being able to manipulate all four elements as well as communicate with the spirits. The Avatar is also born into one nation, and after dying, is reincarnated into another nation following the pattern of Air, Water, Earth, and Fire. The series takes place 100 years after the Fire Nation declares war against all other nations and has killed off all airbenders in search of finding the Avatar, who has been reincarnated as an airbender named Aang. The Avatar, trapped in ice for 100 years, knows nothing of the war. The series starts with Aang being accidentally freed by Katara, a waterbender. The series then primarily follows the adventures of Aang and his companions, Sokka, Katara, and later Toph and Zuko, as he tries to master all four elements and defeat the Fire Nation. There is also a strong secondary focus on
      <a href="/wiki/Zuko" title="Zuko">
       Zuko
      </a>
      , the banished and disinherited
      <a href="/wiki/Crown_prince" title="Crown prince">
       crown prince
      </a>
      of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_Nation" title="Fire Nation">
       Fire Nation
      </a>
      . Zuko was scarred in a duel with his own father, the current Fire Lord, and is obsessed with trying to capture Aang to regain his father's favor and in doing so restoring his honor.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="DVD_releases">
       DVD releases
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender_episodes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: DVD releases">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The first
      <i>
       Avatar: The Last Airbender
      </i>
      DVD set became available on January 31, 2006. The first season had five DVD sets, each containing four episodes. For season two and three, four DVD sets were released, with five episodes on each. The only exception to the release pattern was the last DVD set of season three, which contained a sixth episode. At the end of each season, a box set was released, containing all of the episodes from the season. Each box set contains an additional disc of bonus features not available as an individual disc release like every episode of the series.
     </p>
