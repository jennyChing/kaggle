       <h2>
        Got Virgin ISP?  Cancel it.
       </h2>
       <small>
        April 15th, 2008 by Nicholas Reville
       </small>
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         Cory Doctorow (PCF board member) has canceled his internet account with Virgin in response to comments by their CEO that net neutrality is a load of bollocks and that they will be slowing down traffic from sources that dont pay extra.  These companies need to hear from the public and this is the language they understand.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cory writes: Theoretically, Im locked into a Virgin plan for another six months, but as far as Im concerned, theyve just announced that theyre violating the agreement by announcing that the services I can reach will be systematically slowed down unless they pay Virgin extra. That means that were now null and void. Ill be calling to cancel today.  Whos with me?
        </p>
        <p>
         Got Virgin for internet?  Cancel it and tell them why.
        </p>
        <p>
         At our Worcester office, we have a Charter cable internet connection.  Weve been noticing that they appear to be restricting BitTorrent traffic and were canceling our service this month.  Miro, of course, supports evasionary encryption to get around such blocking, but a technology arms race is no substitute for real public protections.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.boingboing.net/2008/04/13/virgin-media-ceo-net.html">
          Corys full post.
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Update:
         </strong>
         Folks have been asking what Cory is going to switch to.  He tells me:Ive got two lines at home, BT and Virgin, so when I manage to kill Virgin, Ill keep BT for the rest of the year, then have a look around Im not fond of BT (theyre participants in Phorm), but Im locked into them, too.
        </p>
        <p>
         At the PCF office, we have both Charter and Verizon.  Well be using our Verizon account for now, as they seem to be more neutrality positive so far.
        </p>
