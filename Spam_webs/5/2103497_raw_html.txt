          <img alt="Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="640" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Vintage-Key-Wall-Art1.png" title="Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" width="456"/>

        <p>
         <i>
          <span style="font-size: x-small;">
           Materials for this project were provided by Plaid Crafts and Mod Podge Rocks! All opinions are most definitely my own!
          </span>
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its time for a Math lesson. I hate Math, so no worries: this ones easy!
        </p>
        <div style="text-align: center;">
         <b>
          <span style="color: #a64d79;">
           Mod Podge+ stencils+ glitter+ Amy Anderson = Mod Podge Rocks Stencils
          </span>
         </b>
        </div>
        <p>
         Amy Anderson, the awesome creative force behind the
         <a href="http://modpodgerocksblog.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <span style="color: #cc0000;">
           Mod Podge Rocks!
          </span>
         </a>
         blog, came up with a fantastic way to stencil designs easily with glitter. How? Using Mod Podge, of course, with her new line of adhesive-backed stencils!
        </p>
        <p>
         These stencils fill a definite need in crafting with glitter, and come in six trendy designs:
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-stencils1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Mod Podge Rocks Stencils come in 6 designs." border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-stencils1.jpg" title="Mod Podge Rocks Stencils come in 6 designs."/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Arent they fun?
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive seen quite a few projects on Pinterest using these stencils and was super-excited to get a chance to try them out myself.
        </p>
        <p>
         I chose the Skeleton Keys stencil for my project.
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencil-Skeleton-Keys1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;">
          <img alt="Supplies needed to make your own Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="480" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencil-Skeleton-Keys1.jpg" title="Supplies needed to make your own Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <b>
          Other supplies needed for this project include:
         </b>
         Mod Podge Spouncers, Mod Podge Podgeable Glitter, and a Mod Podge formula of your choice  I used Antique Mod Podge. I also used a very small paint brush  think small makeup brush  to brush away excess glitter, which Ill discuss in just a bit.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres a closer look at a
         <b>
          Mod Podge Spouncer
         </b>
         , which is used to apply the Mod Podge to the stencil:
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Spouncer1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Mod Podge Spouncer" border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="400" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Spouncer1.jpg" title="Mod Podge Spouncer" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         The
         <b>
          Mod Podge Podgeable Glitter
         </b>
         comes in a small tube. Remove the cap and you have an open point, as shown below. This pointed cap
         <i>
          <b>
           also
          </b>
         </i>
         unscrews, so any extra glitter can easily be funneled back into the tube. No waste!
         <i>
          And who wants to waste glitter?
         </i>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Podgeable-Glitter-Purple1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Mod Podge Podgeable Glitter" border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="400" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Podgeable-Glitter-Purple1.jpg" title="Mod Podge Podgeable Glitter" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Whats also simply awesome about these small glitter tubes is that when gently squeezed, they sort of poof out little spurts of glitter in a controlled way. I was surprised at how effective this not-really-impressive-to-look-at little tube of glitter was. A really great idea!
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <b>
          Vintage Key Wall Art Tutorial
         </b>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         When I received these materials and saw the purple glitter, I wasnt sure what Id come up with. But after staring at my paper stash for quite awhile, my eyes landed on a box of shelf paper which is in my stash because I love the vintage-style print. It was a perfect match.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/vintage-style-shelf-paper1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Vintage-style printed shelf paper is a great decorative paper to craft with!" border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="640" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/vintage-style-shelf-paper1.jpg" title="Vintage-style printed shelf paper is a great decorative paper to craft with!" width="480"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <b>
          Step One:
         </b>
         Using Antique Mod Podge, I decoupaged the shelf paper onto an 810 artist canvas, wrapping the paper around onto the back frame of the canvas as shown below. Smooth it out as best you can and let it dry. If theres any wrinkles, however, it will just add to the vintage look of this piece!
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <i>
          <b>
           Handy tip:
          </b>
          I always use wax paper on top of any Mod Podged flat surface when Im smoothing it out: glue cant stick to my fingers, and any glue that gets onto the wax paper wont stick back onto the project either.
         </i>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/shelf-paper-mod-podged-onto-canvas1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Use wax paper on top of a Mod Podged flat surface when smoothing it out: glue can't stick to fingers!" border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="640" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/shelf-paper-mod-podged-onto-canvas1.jpg" title="Use wax paper on top of a Mod Podged flat surface when smoothing it out: glue can't stick to fingers!" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <b>
          Step Two:
         </b>
         Using a sanding block, sand the paper to give it more of a weathered and worn appearance. I sanded more heavily in some areas than others, and love how it began to resemble vintage wallpaper. Wipe away any sanding dust with a soft white cloth.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Once the decoupaged canvas was dry and sanded, it was time to stencil!
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <b>
          Step Three:
         </b>
         Using a spouncer, I applied the Antique Mod Podge onto the stencil. Work quickly, so the Mod Podge doesnt begin drying. As soon as the Mod Podge is applied, carefully lift up the stencil and set aside.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Using-Mod-Podge-Spouncer1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Mod Podge Spouncers are the best way to apply Mod Podge onto the new Mod Podge Rocks Adhesive Stencils." border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="480" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Using-Mod-Podge-Spouncer1.jpg" title="Mod Podge Spouncers are the best way to apply Mod Podge onto the new Mod Podge Rocks Adhesive Stencils." width="640"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <b>
          Step Four:
         </b>
         Using the Podgeable Glitter, cover the Mod Podge stenciled image with glitter. Make sure to have paper underneath your project to make it easy to catch the excess glitter when youre tapping and brushing it off later.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencil-skeleton-key-glitter-just-applied1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Close up of a just-glittered stencil design before glitter is brushed away." border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="480" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencil-skeleton-key-glitter-just-applied1.jpg" title="Close up of a just-glittered stencil design before glitter is brushed away." width="640"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <b>
          Step Five:
         </b>
         Take your small brush and carefully brush away as much of the excess glitter as you can, without touching the wet Mod Podged areas. Heres what mine looked like after my initial brushing:
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencil-skeleton-key-glitter-brushed-away1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Close up of a glittered stencil design after glitter is brushed away, but before the design is dry." border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="480" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencil-skeleton-key-glitter-brushed-away1.jpg" title="Close up of a glittered stencil design after glitter is brushed away, but before the design is dry." width="640"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <b>
          Step Six:
         </b>
         Once everything is dry, use the same tiny brush and remove the rest of the excess glitter. What I loved about this step is that I was able to use my brush
         <b>
          <i>
           gently
          </i>
         </b>
         even on the stenciled keys, without losing any of the glitter on the designs.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Heres a close-up shot of one of the cleaned-up keys:
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencil-skeleton-key-detail1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Close up of a glittered stencil design after it has dried and extra glitter is brushed away completely." border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="640" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencil-skeleton-key-detail1.jpg" title="Close up of a glittered stencil design after it has dried and extra glitter is brushed away completely." width="640"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Thats all there is to it!
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         In a short amount of time, you have a pretty stenciled design in glitter! I spaced out the different keys across my canvas, moving from left to right, so there wasnt any waiting time needed for each key to dry.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <i>
          <b>
           Optional tip:
          </b>
          to avoid glitter coming off your project, spray with a light coat of sealer.
         </i>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Heres the canvas hung on one of our mocha-colored living room walls:
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Vintage-Skeleton-Key-Wall-Art-DIY1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="640" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Vintage-Skeleton-Key-Wall-Art-DIY1.jpg" title="Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" width="524"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         And heres a detail shot so you can see how the glittered skeleton key design looks up close:
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Vintage-Key-Wall-Art-Tutorial1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="640" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Vintage-Key-Wall-Art-Tutorial1.jpg" title="Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         I love how it turned out, and that I was able to make something using glitter that still retained a vintage feel! Because the stencils are adhesive-backed, they wont let any glue seep underneath and you can also use these stencils on curved surfaces like mason jars and vases.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Vintage-Key-Wall-Art-using-Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencils1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" border="0" data-jpibfi-description="" height="640" src="http://www.petscribbles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Vintage-Key-Wall-Art-using-Mod-Podge-Rocks-Stencils1.jpg" title="Vintage Key Wall Art using Mod Podge Rocks Stencils" width="480"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <p>
         The stencils can be used multiple times, as long as you take care to wash them immediately after using. Just use soap and water to clean the stencils, nothing fancy. I let mine air dry  adhesive side up  on a scrap piece of paper and then placed it back onto its adhesive backing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <i>
          <b>
           Fun tip:
          </b>
          Try using sand instead of glitter, for a different effect!
         </i>
         <br/>
