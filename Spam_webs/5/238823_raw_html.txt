      <h1 class="omc-post-heading-standard">
       Cloud + tech predictions for 2014
      </h1>
      <p>
       <a id="dd_start">
       </a>
       Experts and pundits in every industry love to read the tea leaves and predict what the rest of us will see in the year to come; its an especially enjoyable endeavor for experts because we so seldom hold them accountable (or at least gently make fun of them) when their predictions flop. After reading approximately 2014 posts about the year 2014, I rounded up several provocative and plausible predictions. Do you see something in your own crystal ball? Please chime in.
      </p>
      <h5>
      </h5>
      <h5>
       Mobile proliferation leads to increased security risks for businesses without BYOD plans or policies
      </h5>
      <p>
       The trend has been that mobile was winning. Its now won, Google big cheese Eric Schmidt said recently. Hes right; anyone predicting the rise of mobile isnt predicting so much as retroactively
       <i>
        observing
       </i>
       . But what does this actually mean for our businesses? For one, the move toward a BYOD (bring your own device) world poses some challenges for your IT department  technical challenges, financial challenges and/or legal challenges. As progressive as BYOD might seem, its anathema to the majority of IT departments, being a world away from the structured familiarity of traditional IT hardware policy, James Gardner wrote in his 
       <a href="/2013/03/everything-you-need-to-know-about-byod/" target="_blank">
        Everything you need to know about BYOD
       </a>
        post for us earlier this year.
      </p>
      <p>
       It is recommended that companies better define expectations for employee-owned hardware to balance flexibility with confidentiality and privacy requirements,
       <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/peterhigh/2013/10/14/gartner-top-10-strategic-technology-trends-for-2014/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.forbes.com/sites/peterhigh/2013/10/14/gartner-top-10-strategic-technology-trends-for-2014/', 'wrote Peter High for Forbes']);" target="_blank">
        wrote Peter High for
        <i>
         Forbes
        </i>
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h5>
      </h5>
      <h5>
       IT will come to terms with the cloud; other departments will come to terms with ITs involvement
      </h5>
      <p>
       Cloud computing is set to become mainstream computing, period, Joe McKendrick says, calling that his 
       <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/joemckendrick/2013/12/19/my-one-big-fat-cloud-computing-prediction-for-2014/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.forbes.com/sites/joemckendrick/2013/12/19/my-one-big-fat-cloud-computing-prediction-for-2014/', 'One Big Fat Cloud Computing Prediction for 2014']);" target="_blank">
        One Big Fat Cloud Computing Prediction for 2014
       </a>
       . Technologist Larry Freeman predicts 2014 will be the year of 
       <a href="http://blogs.computerworld.com/cloud-computing/23291/2013-review-cloud-normal-real-work-cfbdcw" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://blogs.computerworld.com/cloud-computing/23291/2013-review-cloud-normal-real-work-cfbdcw', 'cloud immersion']);" target="_blank">
        cloud immersion
       </a>
        and cloud first policies.
      </p>
      <p>
       The reality of employees expecting to work anywhere at any time  and access their files any time on any device  will catch up to IT departments slow to embrace and incorporate the cloud into their plans and policies. As a result, it may become harder for other departments to go rogue by adopting cloud solutions without the involvement of their colleagues in IT. Ultimately, though,
       <a href="/2012/11/why-you-should-keep-it-off-your-cloud/" target="_blank">
        that should be good news for your business
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
        The cloud has put tremendous pressure on CIOs to reinvent themselves and their role in order to remain relevant, said Sharon Florentine and Thor Olavsrud in their
        <a href="http://www.cio.com/article/745155/2014_Forecast_for_Cloud_Computing?page=2&amp;taxonomyId=3024" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.cio.com/article/745155/2014_Forecast_for_Cloud_Computing?page=2&amp;taxonomyId=3024', 'cloud forecast for CIO.com']);" target="_blank">
         cloud forecast for CIO.com
        </a>
        . If they want to remain innovative and drive a cutting-edge organizationthey must educate their teams, help them get to the cloud faster, and be on the front lines of innovation.
       </span>
      </p>
      <h5>
      </h5>
      <h5>
       Passwords will evolve (or die)
      </h5>
      <p>
       I believe that biometric technology will become [a] much more common way for users to authenticate themselves over the course of the coming year, PayPal CTO James Barrese
       <a href="http://www.wired.com/insights/2013/12/four-tech-predictions-2014-paypals-cto/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.wired.com/insights/2013/12/four-tech-predictions-2014-paypals-cto/', 'wrote in Wired']);" target="_blank">
        wrote in
        <em>
         Wired
        </em>
       </a>
       , explaining that a silver lining of some prominent recent security breaches (Target, et al.) may be a radical rethinking of usernames and passwords. The days of the password is password' may be drawing to a close.
      </p>
      <h5>
      </h5>
      <h5>
       Flexible workflows and collaboration on the go
      </h5>
      <p>
       Were big believers in
       <a href="http://www.centraldesktop.com/workflow" target="_blank">
        the power of a well-designed workflow
       </a>
        and as it becomes easier to reduce the amount of busywork clogging your day-to-day, it will increasingly become necessary to do so. Collaboration will continue to evolve, next with an emphasis on flexible workflows,
       <a href="http://www.information-age.com/technology/mobile-and-networking/123457459/mobile-collaboration-in-2014--4-big-predictions" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.information-age.com/technology/mobile-and-networking/123457459/mobile-collaboration-in-2014--4-big-predictions', 'Ben Rossi predicts']);" target="_blank">
        Ben Rossi predicts
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h5>
      </h5>
      <h5>
       Melancholy and the infinite scroll
      </h5>
      <p>
       <em>
        The Atlantic
       </em>
       s Alexis Madrigal predicts that people will start to experience stream fatigue and gravitate instead toward 
       <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2013/12/2013-the-year-the-stream-crested/282202/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2013/12/2013-the-year-the-stream-crested/282202/', 'structure and endings']);">
        structure and endings
       </a>
       . There is a melancholy to the infinite scroll, he says. Will it be time to rethink the ways we consume information from our myriad networks?
      </p>
      <p>
       If you still havent gotten your future fix: we asked our Collabosphere keynote speaker, Jacob Morgan, to look
       <em>
        ten years
       </em>
       down the road. Read his
       <a href="/2013/11/jacob-morgan-6-bold-predictions-future-work/" target="_blank">
        bold predictions for the future of work
       </a>
       .
