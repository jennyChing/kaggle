          <h1 class="post-content__title">
           <a href="http://blog.lanterncrm.com/2014/08/work-life-balance-vacation/">
            Work-Life Balance: Working on Vacation
           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="post-content__text">
           <h3>
            Im writing this on a four hour train ride from Ancona to Venice, Italy. This is the last leg of a two-week backpacking trip, before a week in Rhode Island with my family.
           </h3>
           <p>
            If youre properly consumed in your work, you should love it enough to be thinking about it without having it be a chore. One of our investors and lead mentors, Jason Cohen,
            <a href="http://blog.asmartbear.com/value-time.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://blog.asmartbear.com']);">
             once wrote a post
            </a>
            stating that a startup should take all of your time. Thats true, but it shouldnt get in the way of life experiences. If you have the chance to backpack Europe for a few weeks, take it now. Tomorrow is always in the future that never comes.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <h2>
            Fresh Thoughts and Ideas
           </h2>
           <p>
            Luckily at LanternCRM, we have a take all the time you need vacation policy. As I set up my three week trip, I looked at Alex, our CEO, and asked if I should shorten the trip. His answer: why would you do that? The real point about vacation is getting away from the daily grind that can hinder creativity and accelerate burnout, while shuffling up the team to cover job roles theyre not used to, thus bringing fresh thought.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Since Ive been gone, Alex has stopped coding the app to work specifically on landing pages, SEO, A/B testing, social, and ads. Some of these things Ive done in the past, but daily necessary customer activities prevented me from going further. With all of Alexs time dedicated to these unfamiliar job functions, hes excited and energized about learning the best he can (and is doing a fantastic job!). Hes brought new thought to something I couldnt have on my own, which is in and of itself, very exciting. David has been working with customers primarily, and just yesterday sent me a HipChat saying, I forgot how much I love talking to customers. Only a few months ago, he was burned out from it. Andrew is focusing on what Andrew has always done best, squashing bugs like a champion. Jenni, the best intern ever, has taken on a major marketing project all on her own, which she is impressively nailing to the wall. The Ultimate CRM Guide she is making will be a cornerstone of our marketing strategy for the next 12 months.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <h2>
            Working On-the-Go
           </h2>
           <p>
            I had planned to work on the trip from the beginning. I have found that a week is generally more than enough time away from productive work before you start craving the problem-solving and critical thinking that work provides. Working while abroad is easier said than done, though. With wifi only available at my hostels and a seven hour time difference, it hasnt always been easy. Once I left, I took about a solid four days off without any connection to the teamminus bragging with photos. On day five, I was already missing my work. Again, since Alex, David, and Jenni were picking up my slack, I was able to choose exactly what I worked on, which made it much more enjoyable. I did research for and drafted out some blog posts, checked our stats and analytics, brainstormed with the team while we were on HipChat at the same time, communicated with our designer, brainstormed long term marketing plans while on long walks around confusing cities, and put some work into our knowledge base. I also checked in with a few customers to see how they were doing and scheduled time with my teammates if necessary. I did all of this on my own time, when I wanted. There were some days I worked 20 minutes and dumped thoughts out to someone or into Evernote; others I crushed five hours in front of my laptop, some days, I didnt work at all. I was always available to my team via text, but they didnt bother too often, unless they really needed something.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            There was actually a funny Instagram post where my friends and I were on a long hike to a remote eating locale. Theyre all socializing while I get caught at the back of the line (looking like Im texting), but im actually writing notes into Evernote thanks to the spark of a fresh idea.
            <nerd alert="">
             <br/>
            </nerd>
