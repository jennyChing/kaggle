        <div id="triberr_endorsement">

        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         He has trekked across Victoria Falls in Zambia, andsat in Devils Pool, became a dive-master in Dahab, and hiked the likes of Ankor Wat in Cambodia and Machu Picchu, Peru.He has hitch-hiked across ALL of Europe, beach hopped in Western Australia, and even spent time
         <a href="http://www.mappingmegan.com/detained-in-egypt/" target="_blank" title="Interview: Detained In Egypt. My Time in an Egyptian Prison.">
          detainedin an Egyptian prison
         </a>
         cell.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         A true nomad who once visited South Africa on a university trip and never returned home, Justin Carmack entertains us all with his exploits across the globe. He documents his nomadic lifestyle in inspiring articles on his blog, and shares beautiful photos from each new destination he finds.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Though there is one thing we havent yet seen from Justin Carmack, and thats a travel web series.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           What do you love the most about travelling?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Its a toss up between two things. First of all is the freedom.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I dont have to wake up at 6 am to get to a shitty job, and I dont have to scrape the ice off my car while I freeze.I can wake up when I want, ask myself where I should go that day, and go. No one is telling me what to do, where to go, or how to do it.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_19265" style="width: 810px">
         <a data-slb-active="1" data-slb-group="19251" data-slb-internal="0" href="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/img_3117.jpg">
          <img alt="Photos from Guatemala" class="size-full wp-image-19265" height="691" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/img_3117.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Deciding to visitGuatemala.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I am only responsible for myself and thats awesome. Even if its only a 2 week trip, it still applies. You can just forget about the rat race for a while.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The second reason is the excitement. Every time I look at a map I see 1,000 places I havnt gone, which all look awesome. Every time I step off a bus or plain in a whole new place, far from anyone I know or any sort of support system, its a huge rush.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I know Im on my own and that the world is mine.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           What is the True Nomads Web series?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         This year I want to take my blog to another level. I want to start documenting all the places I go with video. I feel like I have really missed out on that opportunity. I have taken photos and written about places that I might never go to again, though I walked away without video.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Take Victoria Falls for example. While I took photos of this amazing place in Zambia, I could have had an aerial drone and gotten some incredible video footage. I see this as a missed opportunity.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_19267" style="width: 650px">
         <a data-slb-active="1" data-slb-group="19251" data-slb-internal="0" href="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/5921311744_1bd6ec6615_z.jpg">
          <img alt="Victoria Falls. Photo CC " class="size-full wp-image-19267" height="476" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/5921311744_1bd6ec6615_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Victoria Falls. Photo CC
          <a class="owner-name truncate" data-rapid_p="35" data-track="attributionNameClick" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jurvetson/" title="Go to Steve Jurvetson's photostream">
           Steve Jurvetson
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         So thats my resolution for this year  to create video out of my travels and share them the best way possible.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The series will be part documentary, part reality show, and all shot in amazing locations around the world, through the eyes of True Nomads everywhere. So long as my Kickstarter project is successful and I can get the right equipment,Guatemala will be the location for the first episodes.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           What does it mean to be a True Nomad?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The definition of a nomad is someone who wanders the land with no fixed home, who follows the seasons. I think a TRUE nomad is someone who also integrates easily into every culture, gets to know a country through the locals and customs, and steers away from the tourist track and guided tours.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Its someone who is always willing to step out of their comfort zone and meet new and possibly scary experiences head on.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 547px">
         <img alt="" class="" height="409" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Netherlands1.jpg" width="537"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The definition of a nomad: someone who wanders with no fixed home.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         It is not the weekend tourist or the cruise ship specialist who never leaves the resort area. True nomads are the broke backpackers who look at a map and get excited to see somewhere barely known and new to them.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         THESE are the people that make the best memories and tell the best stories.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           Why are you launching a web series? What is the aim behind jumping into making travel documentaries?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I think video is the best way to share my travels. I will never charge for my videos, and will feature many other locals and nomads, documenting unique places that are not shown on TV.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I have fallen in love with the drone videos you see on YouTube. I think if I could use one in the amazing locations I visit it would make for the best videos ever. I mean imagine aerial footage of the Pyramids of Egypt, or Mayan ruins in Guatemala, or Iguazu Falls.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         These are places that not a lot of people carry drones to, but which would be awesome to see.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           Which countries will you be documenting in your series?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Thats hard to say exactly. Guatemala obviously. But the whole thing depends on the success of the
         <a href="http://truenomads.com/2015/01/true-nomads-webisode-series-kickstarter/" target="_blank">
          Kickstarter campaign
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         With its success I can get professional under water camera equipment for the dive coverage I plan to do, as well as the arial drone which will make the series stand out. If all goes well, featured countries would also be around Central America. El Salvador, Honduras, Belize, Nicaragua, etc.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_19264" style="width: 1034px">
         <a data-slb-active="1" data-slb-group="19251" data-slb-internal="0" href="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Lake-Atitlan-Guatemala.jpg">
          <img alt="Lake Atitlan, Guatemala" class="size-full wp-image-19264" height="768" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Lake-Atitlan-Guatemala.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Lake Atitlan, Guatemala.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Mytravel plans for 2015 are big. In the summer I fly to Europe, and am planning an overland trip to Asia.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         From London to Beijing, through the Silk Route. Across Europe to Turkey. Then a ferry from Azerbaijan to Turkmenistan. Through all the Stans in Central Asia. Crossing from Kazakhstan to Western China. Across China and down into Vietnam.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Ill travel around SE Asia some before finally landing in the Philippines where I plan to stay and explore extensively and make some great diving documentaries about the beautiful country. So this much travelling in 2015 will ensure I have plenty of video footage from amazing locations around the world.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           What will we see in this web series which we dont normally see?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Most travel videos I see are always the same. A bunch of people drinking at the hostel, playing card games, going out on a pub crawl, the next day taking a tour together to the local sites. Maybe one of the dudes has a GoPro strapped to his head. Nothing too special.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I want to combine all my favorite travel shows and make a great one. Shows like
         <a href="http://www.madventures.tv/en/index.php" target="_blank">
          Madventures
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://descendingtv.com/" target="_blank">
          Descending
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Madventures is a hilarious TV series where they travel to crazy places and really step outside of their comfort zones to try new and crazy things.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Descending is a show by the same guys from Departures and its mostly about diving. It combines diving and traveling as they travel the world documenting the best dive sites on the planet. They combine amazing underwater footage with aerial drone footage when they arent in the water.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_19262" style="width: 970px">
         <a data-slb-active="1" data-slb-group="19251" data-slb-internal="0" href="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Diving-Dahab.jpg">
          <img alt="C" class="size-full wp-image-19262" height="639" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Diving-Dahab.jpg" width="960"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Combining diving with travel.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         So I would like to combine it all: Awesome people and strange customs and locations, as well as underwater exploration and inspiring video throughout. Places off the tourist track which are not generally shown on TV.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           Tell us about your plans for Guatemala  what do you plan to film?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Obviously I will film the main attractions like Tikal and Antiguas volcanoes, but then there is a lot more to Guatemala than most people realize.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Like when you think of Guatemala, you dont think about amazing beaches, but there are. I want to film these off the beaten path attractions and destinations along with the epic lakes, volcanoes, jungles, coffee and peanut plantations,waterfalls and much more.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         There is so much more to see than the bars in Antigua and the ruins of Tikal.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           Why Guatemala specifically?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Why not? I like Central America. It really has everything. Jungles with monkeys and sloths coupled with world class beaches, diving and much more.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Its easy to get to for most Americans and Europeans to travel to Central America, and when people see my videos they will know that visiting there isnt so out of the question.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I just wrote an article called
         <a href="http://truenomads.com/2015/01/10-reasons-travel-guatemala/" target="_blank">
          10 reasons why you should visit Guatemala
         </a>
         which got shared on Facebook 15k times in two days. Apparently people agreed.I would like to show them an even more interesting side.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           What are some facts about Guatemala we probably dont know?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         People rave about Tikal or Chichenitza in Mexico, but El Mirador in Guatemala is said to be one of the biggest Mayan ruin complexes in Central America.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_19268" style="width: 650px">
         <a data-slb-active="1" data-slb-group="19251" data-slb-internal="0" href="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/4272305705_3bcde90d8e_z.jpg">
          <img alt="El Mirador in Guatemala" class="size-full wp-image-19268" height="425" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/4272305705_3bcde90d8e_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          El Mirador in Guatemala. Photo CC by
          <a class="owner-name truncate" data-rapid_p="35" data-track="attributionNameClick" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/archer10/" title="Go to Dennis Jarvis's photostream">
           Dennis Jarvis
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         They dont know for sure because its still mostly covered in jungle.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           Why is it so important to educate people about how to travel like a local?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         When you takeguided tours and pre-set packages you are seeing what someone wants you to see, on a set route, that many before you have also taken. This means everyone knows this route and they set up souvenir shops and nice pretty things to please the tourists.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         You see a watered down version of the country amd not the real culture.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Do you think the locals in Fiji just dance all day in grass skirts and spit fire and all that? No, its for your benefit. Its their job to go all tribal and entertain you.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_19263" style="width: 1510px">
         <a data-slb-active="1" data-slb-group="19251" data-slb-internal="0" href="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guatemalans.jpg">
          <img alt="Guatemalans" class="size-full wp-image-19263" height="1125" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guatemalans.jpg" width="1500"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Locals in Guatemala
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Go find out how they really live. Go fishing with a local farmer who doesnt speak english and has never met a tourist. Go ask a dive shop to take you to the dive sites THEY prefer to dive, not just the ones they take the customers to all day to ruin.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         THATs getting the most out of your travels.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           What are some of your most memorable adventures as a nomad to date?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         There is so many. Trekking across Victoria Falls in Zambia was one. It is the biggest water fall on Earth and we sat in Devils Pool which is right on the edge of the falls. Incredibly scary.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Another would be experiencing NYE in Rio De Janiero. Ive never seen such amazing fireworks and in such an awesome setting. We were all on Copacabana beach and they were shooting the fireworks off of 3 big boats off-shore. It was amazing.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 810px">
         <img alt="" class="" height="534" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/t_praia5.jpg" width="800"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Rio beaches are spectacular.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I think my most memorable accomplishment to date was becoming a divemaster in Dahab, Egypt. I started getting into underwater photography, and its the most addicting thing Ive ever begun. I dont plan to ever stop that.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         More memories include hiking Angkor Wat in Cambodia and Machu Picchu in Peru. Learning salsa dancing in Cali, Colombia. Hitchhiking ALL of Europe. Beach hopping in Western Australia. Exploring Zanzibar.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         And maybe my first ever trip abroad, sitting on
         <a href="http://truenomads.com/2014/11/best-beaches-in-costa-rica-for-a-low-budget-holiday/" target="_blank">
          the best beaches in Costa Rica
         </a>
         for three months, doing nothing but snorkel and watch monkeys.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           What is your kick-starter campaign about?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The campaign is so I can get started on the travel series. I want to make documentaries and offer them free on my site, and I know that with the places I visit these could be amazing. But I realise that other vloggers and documentary makers currently have one thing over me: Good equipment.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Money raised from the campaign will go directly for that. I need better dive housings and strobes, a aerial drone, and a few other expensive things I cant manage alone. There is also help for editing and things like that.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Most of the similar campaigns I see on Kickstarter are less about producing a unique travel series, and more about trying to get people to fund their dream trip, and in exchange they will make some videos. I dont think thats a cool project. I am already a full time blogger and traveler, and I am going to these places already. I dont need travel funds.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_19261" style="width: 610px">
         <a data-slb-active="1" data-slb-group="19251" data-slb-internal="0" href="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Coffee-Plantation-in-Guat.gif">
          <img alt="Coffee Plantation in Guat" class="size-full wp-image-19261" height="400" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Coffee-Plantation-in-Guat.gif" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Coffee Plantation in Guatemala.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         You arent donating to help me live a good life traveling. I already do that. All the funds go directly into equipment and production, insuring that I can produce the best qualityvideo webisodes possible.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           How can people get involved?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Head on over to my
         <a href="http://truenomads.com/2015/01/true-nomads-webisode-series-kickstarter/" target="_blank">
          official page
         </a>
         where I give more detail on the project, and see for your self if it is something you would like to see succeed. See if its something you would be willing to help out with and even have your name on.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         If it is, then there are two ways you can help. First is by donating. I offer rewards for different size donations, and you can see what they are and what you can afford to help out with. Any donation would be amazing and very touching to me.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://truenomads.com/2015/01/true-nomads-webisode-series-kickstarter/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="The-True-Nomads-Travel-Series" class="alignleft wp-image-19275 size-full" height="200" src="http://www.mappingmegan.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/The-True-Nomads-Travel-Series.png" width="1000"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The second way is to share the campaign on your Facebook and with your friends. I need all the help I can get because if the campaign doesnt reach its goal, even if its $3 short, I get nothing. But, even if 1 out of 20 of my readers and fans donate just $1, it will be a success!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         So please see if its for you, and help if you are willing!
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           When can people access your first episode?
          </strong>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Lets assume the campaign succeeds, then it ends on Feb 10 2015. We will go straight into filming as I am already in Guatemala and ready to go.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Maybe two or three weeks of shooting and another one or to to edit and produce. So, by the end of March we should have our first episode!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         And then with all the equipment on hand, I would just roll on to the next country!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
        </p>
