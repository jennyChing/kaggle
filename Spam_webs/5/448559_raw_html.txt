     <p>
      <b>
       Maglemosian
      </b>
      (c. 9000 BC  6000 BC) is the name given to a
      <a href="/wiki/Archaeological_culture" title="Archaeological culture">
       culture
      </a>
      of the early
      <a href="/wiki/Mesolithic" title="Mesolithic">
       Mesolithic
      </a>
      period in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Europe" title="North Europe">
       North Europe
      </a>
      . In
      <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">
       Scandinavia
      </a>
      , the culture was succeeded by the
      <a href="/wiki/Kongemose_culture" title="Kongemose culture">
       Kongemose culture
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The actual name originates from the
      <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">
       Danish
      </a>
      archeological site
      <i>
       Maglemose
      </i>
      , situated near
      <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B8rlev" title="Grlev">
       Grlev
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B8ng" title="Hng">
       Hng
      </a>
      on western
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zealand_(Denmark)" title="Zealand (Denmark)">
       Zealand
      </a>
      , southwest of lake
      <a href="/wiki/Tiss%C3%B8" title="Tiss">
       Tiss
      </a>
      . Here the first settlement of the culture was excavated in 1900, by George Sarauw.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      During the following century a long series of similar settlements were excavated from
      <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">
       England
      </a>
      to
      <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">
       Poland
      </a>
      July and from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sk%C3%A5ne" title="Skne">
       Skne
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">
       Sweden
      </a>
      to northern
      <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
       France
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The Maglemosian people lived in forest and wetland environments, using fishing and hunting tools made from wood, bone, and flint
      <a href="/wiki/Microlith" title="Microlith">
       microliths
      </a>
      . It appears that they had domesticated the
      <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">
       dog
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2008)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Some may have lived settled lives, but most were nomadic.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2008)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Huts made of bark have been preserved, and the tools were made of
      <a href="/wiki/Flint" title="Flint">
       flint
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Bone" title="Bone">
       bone
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Horn_(anatomy)" title="Horn (anatomy)">
       horn
      </a>
      . A characteristic of the culture are the sharply edged
      <a href="/wiki/Microlith" title="Microlith">
       microliths
      </a>
      of flintstone, used for spear and arrow heads.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Another notable feature is the "leister", a characteristic type of fishing spear, used for
      <a href="/wiki/Gigging" title="Gigging">
       gigging
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      When the Magesmosian culture reigned, sea levels were much lower than now and Europe and Scandinavia were landlocked with Britain. The cultural period overlaps the end of the
      <a href="/wiki/Weichselian_glaciation" title="Weichselian glaciation">
       last ice age
      </a>
      , when the ice retreated and the glaciers melted. It was a long process and sea levels in
      <a href="/wiki/Northern_Europe" title="Northern Europe">
       Northern Europe
      </a>
      , did not reach current levels until almost 6000 BC, by which time they had inundated large territories previously inhabited by Maglemosian people. Therefore, there is hope that the emerging discipline of
      <a href="/wiki/Underwater_archaeology" title="Underwater archaeology">
       underwater archaeology
      </a>
      may reveal interesting finds related to the Maglemosian culture in the future.
     </p>
