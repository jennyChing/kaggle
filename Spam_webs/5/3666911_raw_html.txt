       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Oven Baked Steak Fries
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date time published" title="2014-06-22T09:00:06-0400">
         <span class="day">
          22
         </span>
         <span class="month">
          Jun
         </span>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Oh, french fries. How I love thee. Curly, seasoned fries thin, crispy, shoestring fries and definitely
         <a href="http://lovegrowswild.com/2014/06/crispy-baked-sweet-potato-fries-chipotle-lime-aioli/" target="_blank">
          sweet potatoes fries
         </a>
         with chipotle lime aioli. Mmmmhmm. As much as I love a sinfully delicious fried potato, if Im making friesat home I always opt for these easierand MUCH healthier Oven Baked Steak Fries. Theyre thick cut, extra crispy, and
         <em>
          perfect
         </em>
         for dipping. Did I mention how ridiculously delicious they are?? Go ahead and try to eat just one of these bad boys I dare ya!
        </p>
        <p>

        <p>
         Heres one
         <em>
          very
         </em>
         important trick Ive learned for making the perfect batch of french fries: Soak em. Soak em good!
        </p>
        <p>
         Soaking your potatoes will help remove a lot of the starch, which allows them to crisp up on the outside while keeping a soft potato texture on the inside. If Im in a hurry, Ill just soak them for an hour, but I generally like tolet them sit for several hours to overnightif Im smart enough to plan ahead that day. Which rarely happens, but hey, I try!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cf.lovegrowswild.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Oven-Baked-Steak-Fries-4.jpg">
          <img alt="These Oven Baked Steak Fries are thick, crispy, and ready for dipping! Much healthier than fried and SO addicting! | LoveGrowsWild.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3361" height="991" src="http://cf.lovegrowswild.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Oven-Baked-Steak-Fries-4.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         A little bit of oil and a simple seasoning mixture are all you need for delicious baked fries that will go with just about any meal.
         <a href="http://lovegrowswild.com/2014/06/big-mac-burger/" target="_blank">
          Burgers
         </a>
         and fries, for sure.
         <a href="http://lovegrowswild.com/2013/10/slow-cooker-italian-beef-sandwiches/" target="_blank">
          Italian beef
         </a>
         sammies and fries, oh yeah.
         <a href="http://lovegrowswild.com/2013/01/spaghetti-sauce-with-italian-sausage/" target="_blank">
          Spaghetti
         </a>
         and fries um, why not?
        </p>
        <p>
         Sometimes I like to jazz up my fries with a little extra spice, some garlic, or a sprinkle ofcheese, butthis is a great basic recipethat I promise you and your family will love. Make sure you have plenty of ketchup or your favorite dipping sauce on hand, and you might even want to make a double batch they
         <em>
          will
         </em>
         disappear quickly!
        </p>

          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Liz Fourez
           </span>

           <span itemprop="recipeCategory">

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 large or 4 medium russet potatoes
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tablespoons canola oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon cornstarch
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon seasoned salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon black pepper
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cut potatoes lengthwise into " thick slices. Then cut each slice lengthwise into " sticks. Fill a large bowl with cold water and soak the potato slices for at least 1 hour. Drain the potatoes and pat them dry with paper towels.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 425F. In a large bowl, toss the potatoes with canola oil to coat. Mix the cornstarch, seasoned salt, salt, and pepper in a small bowl, and add to potatoes. Toss until potatoes are evenly coated in the seasoning mixture.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place potatoes in a single layer on parchment-lined baking sheets and bake for 45 minutes, flipping the potatoes halfway through. Check the potatoes and continue baking another 5-15 minutes until desired crispness is reached. Serve with your favorite dipping sauce and enjoy!
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           - Bake time will depend on your oven, how thick you slice the potatoes, and how much you crowd the baking sheets. Keep a close eye on the potatoes after 40-45 minutes, as they can burn quickly!
