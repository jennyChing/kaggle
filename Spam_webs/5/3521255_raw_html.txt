     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Midnight Sun
       </b>
      </i>
      is an unreleased companion
      <a href="/wiki/Novel" title="Novel">
       novel
      </a>
      to the book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Twilight_(novel)" title="Twilight (novel)">
        Twilight
       </a>
      </i>
      by author
      <a href="/wiki/Stephenie_Meyer" title="Stephenie Meyer">
       Stephenie Meyer
      </a>
      . The work retells the events of
      <i>
       Twilight
      </i>
      , but is written from the perspective of
      <a href="/wiki/Edward_Cullen" title="Edward Cullen">
       Edward Cullen
      </a>
      instead of that of the series' usual narrating character
      <a href="/wiki/Bella_Swan" title="Bella Swan">
       Bella Swan
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-midnight_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-midnight-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Meyer stated that
      <i>
       Twilight
      </i>
      was to be the only book from the series that she planned to rewrite from Edward's perspective.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      To give them a better feel of Edward's character, Meyer allowed
      <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Hardwicke" title="Catherine Hardwicke">
       Catherine Hardwicke
      </a>
      , the director of the film adaptation of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Twilight_(2008_film)" title="Twilight (2008 film)">
        Twilight
       </a>
      </i>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Robert_Pattinson" title="Robert Pattinson">
       Robert Pattinson
      </a>
      , the actor playing Edward, to read some completed chapters of the novel while they shot the film.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-release_history">
       Pre-release history
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      On August 28, 2008, Meyer halted the writing of
      <i>
       Midnight Sun
      </i>
      in response to the leak of twelve chapters of the unfinished manuscript on the Internet. She stated, "If I tried to write
      <i>
       Midnight Sun
      </i>
      now, in my current frame of mind, James would probably win and all the Cullens would die, which wouldn't dovetail too well with the original story. In any case, I feel too sad about what has happened to continue working on
      <i>
       Midnight Sun
      </i>
      , and so it is on hold indefinitely."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-midnight_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-midnight-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She made the twelve-chaptered draft available on her website in fairness to her readers, now that the novel has been compromised before its intended publication date.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Meyer also stated that she doesn't believe the manuscript was leaked with any malicious intent, and would not give any names.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-midnight_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-midnight-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In a November 2008 interview, Meyer said that, "It's really complicated, because everyone now is in the driver's seat, where they can make judgment calls. [...] I do not feel alone with the manuscript. And I cannot write when I don't feel alone."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ewinterview_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ewinterview-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She said that her goal was to go for around two years without hearing about
      <i>
       Midnight Sun
      </i>
      , and she thought that she would begin working on the novel again once she was sure that "everyone's forgotten about it".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ewinterview_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ewinterview-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
