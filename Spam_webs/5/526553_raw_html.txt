        <article class="post-22918 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-red-sox-mlb category-mlb category-news tag-allen-craig tag-baseball tag-boston-red-sox tag-h4-entertainment-com tag-h4tv tag-hal-bent tag-joe-kelly tag-john-lackey tag-jon-lester tag-mlb tag-yoenis-cespedes" id="post-22918">
         <div class="entry-content">
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22924" style="width: 725px">
           <a href="http://www.h4-entertainment.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/i-5.jpeg">
            <img alt="Jon Lester was just one of Boston's many players traded at the deadline -  Mandatory Credit: Jim Rogash/Getty Images via ESPN.com" class="size-full wp-image-22924" height="483" src="http://www.h4-entertainment.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/i-5.jpeg" width="715"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Jon Lester was just one of Bostons many players traded at the deadline  Mandatory Credit: Jim Rogash/Getty Images via ESPN.com
           </p>
          </div>
          <p dir="ltr">
           The Boston Red Sox set off the trade deadline fireworks with a number of moves to completely change the fabric of the franchise.
          </p>
          <p dir="ltr">
           After their bizarre inability to workout a long-term deal with their veteran ace Jon Lester, the Red Sox traded the centerpiece of their starting rotation of the past seven seasons to the American League West-leading Oakland Athletics, in exchange for power-hitting Cuban outfielder Yoenis Cespedes. Far from done, after sending away starting pitcher Jake Peavy to San Francisco the previous week, the Red Sox unloaded veteran starter John Lackey to St. Louis, left-handed power reliever Andrew Miller to division rival Baltimore, and veteran shortstop Stephen Drew to the Yankees.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            The Red Sox dealt all of their major pieces with expiring contracts during the trade deadline. Peavy was not going to be coming back to Boston, and was really a rental for just 2013 which should have been dealt this past offseason; Lackey was not pitching for the one-year veterans minimum deal brought about from his missing a season with Tommy John surgery; Miller is in line for a big money extension that Boston would never pay him, as he has harnessed his control and fulfilled some of his vast potential as a dominant late-innings match-up nightmare for left-handed batters; Drew was a mistake to sign in the first place, and he likely would have been designated for assignment had the Yankees not been desperate for infield help.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Lester being dealt to Oakland as his contract comes to a close this season was the biggest domino dropping. A loyal Red Sox starter, the big lefty topped 200 innings pitched in five of his six full seasons in the major leagues. His one season not reaching 200 innings saw him throw
           </span>
           <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/l/lestejo01.shtml" style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            191 innings in 2011
           </a>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            . This season, he was well on his way to 200 with 143 innings pitched in Boston, with two months left to go in the season.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Lesters greatest strength has been his postseason performances.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Since 2007, Lester has been taking Red Sox nation by storm, after coming back from a non-Hodgkins lymphoma diagnosis and treatment that season. Not only did he pitch  and win  the clinching game of the World Series that season, in his time in Boston he won two World Series titles with a career 1.97 ERA in 11 playoff starts. Lester was lights-out this last October, as he had a 0.59 ERA in his two starts versus the St. Louis Cardinals. He earned trips to three All-Star Games, threw a no-hitter at Fenway Park in 08, and finished fourth in American League Cy Young Award voting in 2010 (statistics from
           </span>
           <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/l/lestejo01.shtml" style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Baseball-Reference.com
           </a>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            ).
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Negotiations broke down with Lester on a long-term deal this spring after the Red Sox made an insulting offer of four-years for $70 million dollars, after Lester stated his first choice was to stay in Boston and
           </span>
           <a href="http://espn.go.com/boston/mlb/story/_/id/11280544/agent-boston-red-sox-pitcher-jon-lester-denies-report-contract-demands-led-trade-talks" style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            he would be willing to take a hometown discount
           </a>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            to stay. Rather than risk having Lester leave Boston with nothing but a compensation pick in return, the Red Sox front office moved the teams quiet, professional leader of the pitching staff for a young, expensive, free-swinging power-hitter in Cespedes.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            While Lester and the Red Sox have both expressed a desire to reconnect in free agency, such a deal is extremely unlikely. If Boston had wanted to keep him, they had exclusive negotiating rights his entire tenure with the team. If they could not come to a number of years and dollars prior to free agency, they have little chance to see any hometown discount once the other 29 clubs are involved with negotiations for a hard-throwing, durable left-handed starter with a fantastic track-record in the postseason. Right now, six-years at $150 million seems to be the realistic starting point on the open market for a pitcher of Lesters pedigree.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Oakland is not likely to be a competitor for Lesters services just because general manager Billy Beane historically does not sign players to those kind of deals being in a small market. Once Lester hits the market, the Yankees and Dodgers automatically become the favorites for his services. Both have deep pockets, and the Yankees may have a little more motivation due to the success of left-handed pitchers pitching in Yankee Stadium (short porch for left-handed batters negated by the match-up), and they have had a front row seat to Lester pitching in the division for their top rivals.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            In return for these players, the Red Sox made some interesting acquisitions.
           </span>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Rather than grab a load of prospects with an eye for 2016 or 2017, Boston took back major league talent.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Cespedes is going to be given every chance to settle into right field and provide Boston a 30-homer bat at one of the corner outfield positions. Boston is betting on Cespedes recapturing his 12 numbers (23 home runs, 82 RBIs, 16 stolen bases, .292 batting average, and .896 OPS), compared to his 13 (.294 OBP and .737 OPS) and this season so far (.303 OBP and .764 OPS), where his strikeouts have risen, and slugging percentage dropped (stats from
           </span>
           <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/c/cespeyo01.shtml" style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            baseball-reference.com
           </a>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            ).
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            The Red Sox got two major-league ready players from the Cardinals in exchange for Lackey. Left fielder Allen Craig, currently signed to a five-year $31 million contract, has had a tough 2014 in St. Louis. Boston is betting on him returning to his previous production in 12 and 13 where he hit over .300 and had OPS of .830 and .876 respectively. This year Craig hit just .237 in St. Louis in 97 games in 2014. His slugging dropped to .348 as he just could not get the ball rolling(stats from
           </span>
           <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/c/cespeyo01.shtml" style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            baseball-reference.com
           </a>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            ). Playing left field at Fenway and taking aim at the Green Monster could revitalize Craig in 2015 and beyond.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Red Sox seriously upgraded their woeful offense.
          </p>
          <p>
           Craig in left field is a big upgrade over Jonny Gomes while Cespedes in right field brings power back to a position in need of it. If Shane Victorino is ever healthy playing him in center field gives Boston a dynamic offense after suffering through the Gomes-Jackie Bradley-Daniel Nava weak-hitting lineup most of 2014. Having Bradley and Nava at their more natural role as backups makes for a stronger bench in 2015. Also, it allows them to move rookie Mookie Betts back to the infield where he is more comfortable.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            The Red Sox got pitcher Joe Kelly from St. Louis as well. Kelly is not a star pitcher, but is a solid middle-of-the-rotation pitcher, who is under team control through 2018, and has a career 3.25 ERA in the National League. Boston got a decent prospect back for Miller from Baltimore in Double-A pitcher Eduardo Rodriguez, who was a top pitching prospect for the Orioles organization. Finally, there were some interesting prospects back from San Francisco for Peavy. Pitching prospect Edwin Escobar is already at Triple-A, and relief pitcher Heath Hembree is a power bullpen arm for 16.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            The other part of the deal that improves Boston is in the infield.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Trading Drew away frees up shortstop for phenom Xander Bogaerts. The Red Sox panicked and moved Bogaerts out of the shortstop position. Not turning 22 until the end of the season, Boston made a strategic mistake jerking Bogaerts to third base. If they truly believe he is the future at shortstop, they need to keep him there, and let him mold into the player they see he can be.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            However, the Red Sox will still have to go out in the offseason and find two starting pitchers via trade and free agency to supplement their plethora of young arms getting a tryout down the stretch in 14.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Brandon Workman, Rubby De La Rosa, Allen Webster, and Anthony Ranaudo will all get multiple chances to pitch for the big club down the stretch this season. With two or three rotation spots open, theres a chance for the young arms to step-up and grab a role for next season.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Replacing Lester and Lackey is not an easy task for Boston. However, a smart trade and a savvy free agent signing could have them right back in the thick of the race in the AL East for 15.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            After that, it is a matter of simply getting a little bit of the 13 Red Sox luck to get everything to fall into place.
           </span>
          </p>
         </div>
