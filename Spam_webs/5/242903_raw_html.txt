     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Have You Lived Before This Life
       </b>
      </i>
      is a non-fiction book published by
      <a href="/wiki/L._Ron_Hubbard" title="L. Ron Hubbard">
       L. Ron Hubbard
      </a>
      in 1960. It is one of the
      <a href="/wiki/Canon_law" title="Canon law">
       canonical
      </a>
      texts of
      <a href="/wiki/Scientology" title="Scientology">
       Scientology
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      It purports to be a collection of "forty-one actual case histories" of
      <a href="/wiki/Reincarnation" title="Reincarnation">
       reincarnation
      </a>
      and past-life experiences, gleaned from
      <a href="/wiki/Auditing_(Scientology)" title="Auditing (Scientology)">
       auditing
      </a>
      with an
      <a href="/wiki/E-meter" title="E-meter">
       e-meter
      </a>
      at the
      <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scientology" title="Church of Scientology">
       Church of Scientology
      </a>
      's "Fifth London Advanced Clinical Course" held in 1958.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LRHStore_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-LRHStore-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Scientology's official website says of the book: "The major portion of the book is devoted to the auditing case histories of individuals, detailing their memories of past lives. These case histories graphically show how a persons attitudes and actions in present time can be affected by incidents in his or her past lifetimes. They also document the improvements that occurred when such incidents were addressed and run out in auditing." Their promotional text for the book also goes on to note that the book answers important questions such as:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <ul>
       <li>
        "How does a
        <a href="/wiki/Thetan" title="Thetan">
         thetan
        </a>
        behave when the body dies?"
       </li>
       <li>
        "When and how does a thetan pick up a new body?"
       </li>
       <li>
        "What causes someone to have a "famous person" fixation?"
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LRHStore_2-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-LRHStore-2">
          <span>
           [
          </span>
          2
          <span>
           ]
          </span>
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      The book has been consistently in print until 1989. It is still sold in Church bookstores but it is not currently offered for sale today by Scientology's
      <a href="/wiki/Bridge_Publications_(Scientology)" title="Bridge Publications (Scientology)">
       Bridge Publications
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Era_Publications" title="New Era Publications">
       New Era Publications
      </a>
      websites.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Have_You_Lived_Before_This_Life&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In her book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Scandal_of_Scientology" title="The Scandal of Scientology">
        The Scandal of Scientology
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Paulette_Cooper" title="Paulette Cooper">
       Paulette Cooper
      </a>
      examined the often fantastical nature of the past lives described, referred to by Hubbard as "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_opera_(Scientology)" title="Space opera (Scientology)">
       space opera
      </a>
      ":
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       "Hubbard has devoted a special book called
       <i>
        Have You Lived Before This Life: A Scientific Survey
       </i>
       just to past-life case histories of Scientologists. The preface of this book also contains the names and addresses of the people who took part in the experiment so that the cynical could check its facts ... Most of the Scientologists who relived their past lives believed that they had once been plain people, or very often space people, and for plots, their histories read like a type of science-fiction sadomasochism."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cooper_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-cooper-3">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         3
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Unpublished_sequel">
       Unpublished sequel
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Have_You_Lived_Before_This_Life&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Unpublished sequel">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      According to Cooper, Hubbard announced a sequel called
      <i>
       Where Were You Buried?
      </i>
      was in the works. He instructed his auditors to check all preclears for recent deaths, and then to physically locate their place of burial.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cooper_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-cooper-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Proper_title">
       Proper title
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Have_You_Lived_Before_This_Life&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Proper title">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Some editions of the book have a question mark in the title, but most, including the first printing, do not. Later editions added the proper question mark missing from its title in the original printings. Adding to the confusion, some editions use the question mark on the dust jacket but not on the book itself.
     </p>
