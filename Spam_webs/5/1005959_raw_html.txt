        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Choosing a Goal Weight Based on Body Fat
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_177" style="width: 1210px">
         <a href="http://mikienglish.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/goal_weight_based_on_bodyfat.png">
          <img alt="Goal Weight Based On Body Fat" class="size-full wp-image-177" height="800" src="http://mikienglish.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/goal_weight_based_on_bodyfat.png" width="1200"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Original photo courtesy of
          <a href="http://www.freedigitalphotos.net/images/slim-woman-measuring-her-waist-photo-p177869  ">
           stockimages
          </a>
          at FreeDigitalPhotos.net
         </p>
        </div>
        <br/>
        One of the best methods Ive found for figuring out a healthy goal weight is the Goal Weight Based on Body Fat method. Its a little more complex than just picking a number on the scale, but a heck of a lot more realistic for we who sport more curves and muscles.
        <p>
         When it comes to body weight, its important to watch more than just the numbers on the scale. Why? Because that bitch lies to you. Not about how many pounds/kilos your body weighs (unfortunately), but about
         <strong>
          how healthy you are based on your weight
         </strong>
         . The last thing you want to be is
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/dr-mark-hyman/skinny-fat_b_1799797.html" target="_blank" title="Skinny fat">
          skinny fat
         </a>
         (AKA MONW, or metabolically obese normal weight). Tracking your body fat can help you monitor how much fat AND muscle you are losing. In my (admittedly limited) opinion, it offers a more realistic way to lose weight and maintain a healthy body composition. Even if you use one of the more inaccurate body fat calculation methods, it can still be an effective tool if you use the same method consistently.
        </p>
        <p>
         Another thing to remember:
         <strong>
          muscle weighs more than fat
         </strong>
         . Fat loss = good. Muscle loss = bad. If youve been hitting the weights hard and watching your diet, you may notice that the scale shows youve gained a pound or two. Dont panic! Just throw the scale out the window. Really. Track your body size with a tape measure, body fat calipers or even your cloths  not by the scale. Youll save yourself a lot of frustration.
        </p>
        <p>
         For your convenience, Mr. E developed a Goal Weight Based on Body Fat Calculator that you can use to quickly find a target weight.  To use the calculator, you will need to know your
         <strong>
          current body fat percent
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          your current weight
         </strong>
         and
         <strong>
          your goal body fat
         </strong>
         . You can choose your goal body fat % based on the table below. To keep from getting discouraged in your weight loss efforts, I would suggest picking a body fat goal percentage just below your current body fat number. Play around with different goals until youre satisfied with the numbers.
        </p>
        <p>

        </p>

        <h3>
         Conclusion
        </h3>
        <p>
         Give the body fat method of tracking weight loss a try and remember, losing weight is a long game, not a quick fix. Having realistic weight loss goals can keep you from getting discouraged and make you feel and look healthier in the long run. To hold on to that precious lean muscle, balance weight training, cardio and a good diet (with plenty of protein). Weight training to lose weight might seem counter intuitive to some of you, but I promise it works! And, for those of you who think weight training is relegated to the gym along side those muscle bound hard core lifters, think again. Weight training can be done with only your body weight, medicine balls, kettle bells and lots of other fun equipment you can use right in your own home!
        </p>
