										&lt;h2&gt;Trendy Tea for Skin&lt;/h2&gt;
										
					&lt;p&gt;What is the healthiest drink in the world?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;If you said water, youre almost right. There is a very strong argument to be made that tea could take home that prize. Tea consumption has long been associated with anti-aging and anti-cancer effects, and &lt;span style="color: #990033;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;new research suggests that drinking certain teas may benefit our skin.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;&lt;img class="aligncenter  wp-image-13646" title="Green tea" src="/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Tea-1024x682.jpg" alt="Natural spread of dried and fresh green tea leaves in small ramekins with a cup of steeped green tea and a white teapot on rustic white wood surface." width="614" height="409" /&gt;Drinking tea is an excellent way to consume more water and stay hydrated. &lt;strong&gt;&lt;span style="color: #990033;"&gt;The skin-benefits of tea are linked to the antioxidant, anti-inflammatory and anti-carcinogenic effects of bioactive plant compounds found in tea, called polyphenols.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/strong&gt; Green tea in particular, has high levels of a potent polyphenol known as EGCG. Additional protective polyphenols are also found in other types of tea including white tea and black tea.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;When polyphenol-rich tea is consumed, or even applied topically to the skin, these natural compounds have been shown to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation caused by UV exposure. It is this oxidative stress and inflammation that can lead to premature wrinkles, as collagen in the skin breaks down and the elasticity of the skin degrades. It should be noted that while tea has been linked to sun protection, &lt;span style="color: #990033;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;there is no replacement for sunscreen to protect from damaging UV rays&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/span&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;With spring in full swing and summer around the corner, &lt;span style="color: #990033;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;now is the perfect time to stock up on some organic tea at your natural health food store&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/span&gt;. Throw a cube of ice in it and stay refreshed as you add a new layer of skin protection.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;For even more fun with tea, &lt;strong&gt;&lt;a href="/refresh-your-skin-naturally/diy-body-care-recipes/"&gt;try one of our simple DIY natural skin care recipes&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;. This refreshing green tea and raw sugar scrub acts as an excellent exfoliant, designed to help get your skin ready for summer. Try making it at home.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;img class="wp-image-13650 aligncenter" title="IMG_3363" src="/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_3363-1024x1024.jpg" alt="" width="511" height="511" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Green Tea and Raw Granulated Sugar Scrub&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;strong&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Brew a very strong 1/4 cup of green tea (using 1/4 cup of water).&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Let steep for a half hour.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Remove the tea bag and empty the brewed tea leaves into a small bowl.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Mix in 1 tbsp of course, granulated sugar.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Rub gently into skin, let sit for 5-10 minutes.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Wash off with warm water&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p&gt;For more on natural skin health, check out our&lt;span style="color: #990033;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Top Tips for Naturally Radiant Skin:&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;ol&gt;

&lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;&lt;a href="/refresh-your-skin-naturally-your-skin-your-story/opt-for-natural-oils/"&gt;Opt for natural oils&lt;/a&gt;; &lt;/strong&gt;&lt;span style="color: #990033;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;coconut oil, sea buckthorn oil, omega-3s&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/span&gt; and &lt;span style="color: #990033;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;argan oil&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/span&gt; help to lock in moisture and heal damaged skin.&lt;/li&gt;
