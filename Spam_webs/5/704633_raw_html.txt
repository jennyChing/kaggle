         <div class="at-above-post addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Utica Man Among Six Charged With Conspiracy &amp; Providing Material support ISIS" data-url="http://unir1ny.com/utica-man-among-six-charged-conspiracy-providing-material-support-isis/">
         </div>
         <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
         </div>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           The Justice Department announced late Friday the arrests of six people charged with conspiracy and providing material support to terrorists from the ISIS network in Iraq and Syria. One of the six suspects is from Utica.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Nihad Rosic, 26, is named in the indictment. The others are Ramiz Zijad Hodzic, 40, his wife Sedina Unkic Hodzic, 35, and Armin Harcevic, 37, all of St. Louis County, Missouri; Mediha Medy Salkicevic, 34, of Schiller Park, Illinois; and Jasminka Ramic, 42, of Rockford, Illinois.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Rosic is accused of sending $500 via Western Union from Utica to the alleged ringleader of the operation in St. Louis.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Also, Rosic and the suspects are accused of
          </span>
          <span style="font-family: System; font-size: small;">
           <span style="font-family: System; font-size: small;">
            sending
           </span>
          </span>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           materials including U.S. Military clothing, night vision equipment, boots and more.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           In addition, Federal Agents say Rosic attempted to board a
          </span>
          <span style="font-family: System; font-size: small;">
           <span style="font-family: System; font-size: small;">
            Norwegian Airways
           </span>
          </span>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           flight out of JFK Airport last
          </span>
          <span style="font-family: System; font-size: small;">
           <span style="font-family: System; font-size: small;">
            July
           </span>
          </span>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           to join the ISIS right in Syria.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Rosic, of Utica, is also charged with conspiring to kill and main persons in a foreign country, according to the Justice Department.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           All six suspects are natives of Bosnia who immigrated to the United States.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           If convicted, the penalties range up to 15 years on each count and/or fines of up to $250,000.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           The crime Rosic is charged with, conspiring to kill and maim persons in a foreign country, carries a penalty of up to life in prison.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Aman by the same name, Nihad Rosic,has a criminal history in Utica. He was arrested in 2012 accused of beating a woman with a belt. In 2011 he was charged with punching a woman in the face as the victim was holding a child.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Todays charges and arrests underscore our resolve to identify, thwart, and hold accountable individuals within the United States who seek to provide material support to terrorists and terrorist organizations operating in Syria and Iraq, said Assistant Attorney General Carlin. Preventing the provision of supplies, money, and personnel to foreign terrorist organizations like ISIL remains a top priority of the National Security Division and our partners in the law enforcement and intelligence communities. I want to thank the many agents, analysts and prosecutors responsible for this case.
         </p>
