                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Witches - Gothic Fashion Label Launch in L.A.
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4301709403414651337" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <a href="http://www.notcot.com/images/2009/03/witches3.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.notcot.com/images/2009/03/witches3.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; width: 600px; height: 445px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.notcot.com/images/2009/03/witches8.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.notcot.com/images/2009/03/witches8.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; width: 600px; height: 607px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.notcot.com/images/2009/03/witches13.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.notcot.com/images/2009/03/witches13.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; width: 600px; height: 564px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     From celebrity playground, the Chateau Marmont in Los Angeles, designers Lauren Alexander and Gabby Applegate launched a gorgeous collection for the modern sorceress, "Witches".
                     <br/>
                     The collection, as described by the designers is a "modern take on Goth, Grunge, and Victorian dressing. The entire line is comprised of black lace, velvet, silk, and spandex".
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The collection launch event (on a Friday, the 13th) was a dramatic presentation in Gothic opulence.  Ravens, etched mirrors, black ribbons and vintage morbid postcards adorned elegant antique set pieces.  The pieces are generally priced at an accessible $200-$400 for such sophisticated offerings and will be available for purchase Fall 2009.  (No jokes about Hollywood bloodsuckers though).
