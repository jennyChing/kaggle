        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Human Trafficking in Italy Worsening  UN Reports
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-10-24T12:00:54+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 24, 2013
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://findingjustice.org/author/ericdye/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Eric Dye
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://findingjustice.org/human-trafficking-in-italy-worsening-un-reports/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <img alt="Human Trafficking in Italy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12931" height="390" src="http://findingjustice.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Human-Trafficking-in-Italy-Screenshot.png" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The UN says that Italyshuman trafficking problem is getting worse.Having
         <a href="http://findingjustice.org/sex-trafficking-in-italy/" title="Sex Trafficking in Italy">
          researched this in the past
         </a>
         , it does not surprise me to hear that.
        </p>
        <p>
         There were concerns over Italys human trafficking problem, even way
         <a href="http://findingjustice.org/italys-dark-underground-sex-trafficking/" title="Italys Dark Underground: Sex Trafficking [Video]">
          back in the 1990s
         </a>
         .Yet nothing has been aggressively done to finally put an end to it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-12930">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Italy has two main reasons why its a human trafficking favorite:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Its geography is ideal to enter the country and then there is access for all of Europe.
         </li>
         <li>
          There are already established crime networks throughout the country.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         Think about the geography of Italy for a moment. Many come by way of boatfrom Africa. The coastline is extremely difficult to closely monitor. Others are trafficked over land, from impoverished nearby countries such as Albania, Romania and Moldova.
        </p>
        <p>
         When it comes to sex trafficking specifically, Italys legalized prostitution makes it ridiculously easy to conduct business. To make matters worse, Italy, a vacation destination, has become a popular place for sex tourists as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are some voices in the crowd that question whether or not Italy even has a trafficking problemwho would want to admit to such a horror, right? Often times these critics point at the lack of statistical data, but its hard to measure something thats hiding. I have been living in Italy for nearly four years now and I can personally confirm that human trafficking is indeed occurring in Italy. I have spoken with victims myself.
        </p>
        <p>
         Of course, I cant help but think that those who so adamantly deny the existence of a human trafficking problem in Italy are perhaps the very people profiting from this outrageous injustice.Even if we were to suppose, however, that Italy might not have a human trafficking problem, what harm could it possibly do to put the proper mechanisms in place to deal with it? You know, just in case.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How about we start by outlawing prostitution?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         SOURCE:
         <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-24183737" target="_blank">
          BBC
         </a>
        </p>
