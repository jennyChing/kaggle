           <h1>
            Running them back to school
           </h1>
           <p>
            A back to school check list so you're not rushing at the last minute. A painless back to school with all the important things covered.
           </p>
           <div class="no-print" id="social">
            <div class="row collapse">

          <section class="text">
           <p>
            Ah, school holidays. We were all only too happy to shrug off the routine of school and slip into something a little more comfortable. At my place weve done not much at all except swim every single day somewhere or other  our pool, friends pools, the local pool, various beaches, the creek, the aquatic centre, the river. Its been a gloriously wet summer (as opposed to the other kind of wet summer, which is surely not so glorious).
           </p>
           <p>
            But its time, isnt it? Time to send them back? As much as I relish the holidays, I also relish being able to work in daylight hours (and I know Im lucky being able to keep working through the hols at all!). Im looking forward to having a day that doesnt start at 6.30am and wind up well after midnight. And Im really, really looking forward to going to the toilet without having to call out, Im in the toilet every single time. Its the little things, isnt it?
           </p>
           <div class="advertisement" id="mrec-mobile">
           </div>
           <p>
            Despite the fact that Im racing them back to school, I acknowledge that reentry is tough. Theres the angst over the newness of it all  new teachers, new classrooms, new class  and even more angst over the sameness of it all  same school drop off, same lunch boxes, same routine, same 3pm daily curfew.
           </p>
           <p>
            I confess that I quite like the preparation involved in getting ready to return. I do like a list. Here are all the things Im doing to get my trio prepped for reentry in just a couple of weeks
           </p>
           <h3>
            1. Lunch box preparation
           </h3>
           <p>
            After four years on the job, I like to think Ive got the lunch box conquered. Thats why each time Im baking in the next few weeks, Ill be making a little extra to freeze for the kids lunches. So far Ive got neat little piles of
            <a href="http://www.kidspot.com.au/best-recipes/Breakfast+1/Cheesy-bacon-and-corn-muffins-recipe+2825.htm">
             cheesy bacon and corn muffins
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.taste.com.au/recipes/5546/zucchini+slice">
             zucchini slice
            </a>
            and
            <a href="/13-fun-recipes-to-cook-with-kids/">
             apple muffins
            </a>
            , all individually frozen in grease-proof paper and ready to pop in the lunch box when its all systems go. Im also planning to make
            <a href="/ham-pinwheels-muesli-bars/">
             chia seed bars
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.actuallyamy.com/2013/01/milo-and-coconut-cookies.html">
             milo and coconut cookies
            </a>
            . The lunch box pressure is off for the first few weeks of school at least!
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/ham-pinwheels/" rel="attachment wp-att-64367">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42311" height="435" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Ham.pinwheels.jpg" title="Ham.pinwheels" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <h3>
            2. Uniform once-over
           </h3>
           <p>
            This is one job that I cant stand leaving until the last minute, so I get it done the day after school lets out. So, December saw me
            <a href="http://www.kidspot.com.au/Back-to-School-For-parents-How-to-hem-trousers-and-skirts+7435+153+article.htm">
             fixing hems
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.kidspot.com.au/Back-to-School-For-parents-How-to-sew-on-a-button+7436+153+article.htm">
             buttons
            </a>
            before giving the uniforms a good soak,
            <a href="http://www.kidspot.com.au/Back-to-School-For-parents-How-to-make-white-socks-white-again+7440+153+article.htm">
             boiling the white socks
            </a>
            , cleaning the hatsandwashing the school bags (I just throw them in the washing machine with my standard washing liquid and a handful of borax, wash themon the delicate settingand drip dry them on the line  they dont seem to mind it at all). Once everything is gleaming, I pack it all away ready for February.
           </p>
           <h3>
            3. School shoes a go-go
           </h3>
           <p>
            The whole school shoe thing is just so important. Our kids are in their shoes for more than 30 hours a week (when you take into account before and after school time). Most of us do a once, maybe twice, a year school shop and its almost always at the beginning of the school year.I dont know why the kids feet grow so much over the six weeks of summer holidays, but they do. Perhaps its because I limp them over the end-of-the-year line in their old shoes?
           </p>
           <p>
            My son, who is a giant of a child and requires complex shodding up to three times a year (I kid you not  he wears a
            <em>
             mens size eight
            </em>
            and he is only nine years old) is on first-name basis with our local Athletes Foot guy, Anthony.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot/" rel="attachment wp-att-64337">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42330" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-18/" rel="attachment wp-att-64343">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42324" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-18.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-18" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            We started going to
            <a href="http://www.theathletesfoot.com.au/back-to-school">
             Athletes Foot for school shoes
            </a>
            for all the kids when Max was half-way through Year 1 (he heads into Year 4 this year) and we have never gone anywhere else again. They stock Clarks and Ascent, both of which are the only school shoes that last the distance. Believe me, when you are buying three pairs of shoes a year for your child due to size alone, the last thing you want is to have to replace a pair that he kicked the toe out of or that simply fell apart. Weve never had that happen with either Clarks or Ascent  both my girls had their Clarks school shoes for the full year last year and they are still in great condition.
           </p>
           <p>
            Such good condition, in fact, that its tempting to hand down Arabellas shoes to Lottie and save myself the expense of buying a new pair. Tempting, but its not something that I would do. All other kinds of clothes go from one girl to the other, but not shoes  years of watching the fitting process at Athletes Foot has meant Im all too aware of how different each childs feet actually are. The shoes that are right for Arabella are not the shoes that Lottie needs to adequately support her feet.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-64338">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42329" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-2.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-2" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-13/" rel="attachment wp-att-64350">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42318" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-13.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-13" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-14/" rel="attachment wp-att-64351">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42317" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-14.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-14" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            This has become even more apparent with the
            <a href="http://www.theathletesfoot.com.au/blog/new-in/fitzi-has-arrived-experience-it-now">
             new FITZI fitting system
            </a>
            that Athletes Foot have introduced. Its just amazing! The girls thought it was super-fun watching their little legs walking on the video, but it simply demonstrated to me that each child deserves to have a proper fitting of their own school shoes.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-9/" rel="attachment wp-att-64346">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42322" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-9.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-9" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-10/" rel="attachment wp-att-64347">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42321" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-10.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-10" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-11/" rel="attachment wp-att-64348">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42320" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-11.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-11" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-16/" rel="attachment wp-att-64353">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42315" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-16.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-16" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Anthony showed me where each girl exerted the most pressure on the ground when she walked and the girls patterns were very different. Anthony is an Athletes Foot master fit technician, which means hes had extensive training and can recommend the right kind of support for each of the girls different needs. He determined that Arabella would actually be better off in the Ascent shoes and Lottie in the Clarks. Arabella was thrilled that she got to choose the Ascent flower shoes and Lottie was a little non-plussed in her plain-soled Clarks but even she agreed that the Clarks were far more comfortable for her.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-6/" rel="attachment wp-att-64341">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42326" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-6.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-6" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-3/" rel="attachment wp-att-64339">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42328" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-3.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-3" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-8/" rel="attachment wp-att-64345">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42323" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-8.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-8" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Max didnt join us on this occasion  he didnt quite make the end-of-year shoe-limp last year, requiring new shoes at the end of October (nearly broke my heart  just two months to go until the end of the school year!). What was beyond fabulous, however, was that in October Anthony once again managed to source Max a pair of school shoes with Velcro fastenings, rather than laces. I didnt think that was going to be possible this time  now that were well into mens shoe territory, I felt for certain he would have to go the laces-option. He was absolutely thrilled; all of the boys in his class still have Velcro and he just wanted to keep fitting in for a little while longer. So, you see, Athletes Foot do all kinds of fits geddit? Yes? No? Fair enough.
           </p>
           <p>
            Before I stop waxing lyrical about school shoes (who knew I could even do that!) I thought Id mention that I didnt quite manage to escape Athletes Foot unscathed. All my photo taking and chatting with Anthony left me with my guard down. The girls begged me to let them try on a colourful pair of trainers each and once they were on there was no getting them off. Now they are practically living in them  Lottie did actually wear her pair to bed two nights in a row. This is actually a good thing. Anthony fitted those trainers just as carefully as he fitted the school shoes and I know Id rather they were wearing them all summer than thongs!
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-7/" rel="attachment wp-att-64342">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42325" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-7.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-7" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-17/" rel="attachment wp-att-64354">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42314" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-17.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-17" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <h3>
            4. Books, books, books
           </h3>
           <p>
            Last year I thought Id save money by cobbling together parts of the school book list that I knew we already had around the house. The coloured pencils here, a ruler there, that pair of scissors, this whiteboard marker big mistake.
           </p>
           <p>
            Firstly, I entered the book list in with our local supplier online, untagging the items that I didnt want. Unfortunately I untagged way too many lines and ended up not even having half the things on the list that I thought I had.
           </p>
           <p>
            Then I got confused as to what they were supplying and what I was supplying. Was I supposed to find an empty display book or a scrapbook? Was it the green pen or the purple pen that I thought we already had?
           </p>
           <p>
            Finally, there was the issue of the wrong scissors being sent in  I dont want scissors with blunt ends, I want scissors with pointier ends like
            <em>
             all the other kids in my class
            </em>
              but I choose not to think about that one too much.
           </p>
           <p>
            So, long story short, just get the full list. Book it, pick it up, done.
           </p>
           <h3>
            5. Earth to contact, come in contact
           </h3>
           <p>
            Isnt contact paper the suckiest thing known to woman (because its always we women contacting the books, isnt it? Ive never, ever known a man to tackle the contact so if youve heard of one, please send details!)? I have two pieces of advice to share when it comes to
            <a href="http://www.kidspot.com.au/Back-to-School-For-parents-How-to-cover-books-in-contact-without-bubbles+7433+153+article.htm">
             contact and books
            </a>
            :
           </p>
           <p>
            (1) Work fast. The faster you lay that book down the less air seems to get trapped. I jam the spine down onto my neatly cut piece of contact and then I let the book fall  bang. Then I check and  no bubbles! If by some freaky chance (because this is contact we are dealing with) you find you have bubbles, dont sweat it, simply iron them out with the edge of a ruler, smoothing along as you go.
           </p>
           <p>
            (2) Keep the backing paper in place until you are absolutely ready to work with the contact beneath. Ripping off the backing paper willy-nilly will surely result in clumped contact locking your childs pristine spelling book shut forever and you will never, ever,
            <em>
             ever
            </em>
            be forgiven.
           </p>
           <h3>
            6. Two big helpers
           </h3>
           <p>
            There are two things that help me keep on top of the whole school thing and they are my schedule and my calendar.
           </p>
           <p>
            The schedule is a day-by-day list for each child of whats on, what time, what they need to pack and other important notes. So on Maxs Tuesday we know theres a trombone lesson and its a sports day. So on the things I need list it says sports uniform, trombone, band folder bag and other notes say leave trombone and folder at school. All so clear that Max could pack his own school bag every single day. He really could!
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/running-them-back-to-school/athletes-foot-19/" rel="attachment wp-att-64363">
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-42313" height="427" src="http://cdn.kidspot.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Athletes-Foot-19.jpg" title="Athlete's Foot-19" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            My second big helper is my calendar. I honestly dont know how anyone gets through a year without a family calendar, showing who is doing what and when. Ours is so written on it looks like a giant mess, but I know exactly what everything means and there isnt a thing that goes on that doesnt go on that calendar. Its there for everyone to see (ie, husband) so they also know whats going on and where theyre meant to be (theoretically).
           </p>
           <p>
            So, thats the low-down at our place as I get ready to take the kids back to school. Its not secret that Im running them there  I adore the kicking-around school holidays, but boy is it great to hear that school bell ring and know that a little structure is coming back into our lives.
           </p>
           <h5 style="text-align: center">
            Whats on your back-to-school To Do list?
           </h5>
