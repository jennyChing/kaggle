         Start a Brew Club, Change Your Life
        </a>
       </h2>
       <span class="postMonth" title="2011">
        Mar
       </span>
       <span class="postDay" title="2011">
        29
       </span>
       <div class="postSubTitle">

       <p>
        <a href="http://ncsdbeer.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/BrewClub1.png" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/downloads/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/BrewClub1.png');">
        </a>
        <a href="http://ncsdbeer.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/BrewClub1.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/downloads/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/BrewClub1.jpg');">
         <img alt="Brew Club" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-175" height="429" src="http://ncsdbeer.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/BrewClub1.jpg" title="BrewClub1" width="600"/>
        </a>
        While sipping the sweet fruits of our labor at our last Brew Club meeting one thought kept nagging at me, Why havent we been doing this for the past 10 years instead of the last 6 months?. Its fairly inexpensive, highly rewarding, mildly addicting, perfectly legal, and a great excuse to get together and have a few pints with friends whom you have shared the experience of brewing with. Based on my deep seeded regret for not starting earlier I figured I should pass on the knowledge I wish was given to me many cases ago. While brewing techniques and preferences are as varied as their are backyard brewers, here is a crash course in starting your own Brew Club. I am not going to get into how to brew beer, there are plenty of resources available online for that purpose.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Overview
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        The main purpose of a brew club should be to learn about brewing, have fun, and drink beer. The way we operate is that we brew about every two months. At the current meeting we brew while we drink what we brewed at the previous meeting and if there is any left over people can bring beer home in a growler. So far there has not been any left over. We started by brewing two 5 gallon batches, but are now doing 3 batches per meeting. So far people have suggested what we should brew and the leaders have chosen what we will brew at the meeting. Next meeting we will be assigning a person to be in charge of each batch. That person will pick what is brewed including style, ingredients, techniques, etc. This way everyone will get more familiar with the process.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Choose Your Leaders
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Behind every great beer is a passionate brewer and your goal is to make great beer. We had four guys get this club started. Two have been brewing for a while and two have been wanting to brew for a while. If you dont have a seasoned professional to get things started then you have to have someone who is willing to do the leg work of deciding what equipment to buy or borrow, where you can brew, take care of the finances and decide who should be invited to your group. I think having a few people to spread these responsibilities out to is better than one person doing all the work.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Finances
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        There are many ways to get the money and equipment needed. What our club did is that the four guys who started the club had some equipment and the other things we needed they split the cost on. At every meeting the other members of the club chip in $10 for the brewing ingredients and the people that started the club are slowly reimbursing themselves with the leftover money. After they are reimbursed we will use the leftover money to replace/upgrade equipment. Because we had 20 to 30 people interested in the club they originally got enough equipment to brew and ferment two 5 gallon batches per meeting, though we decided to up it to 3 batches because we had enough people chipping in and 10 gallons of beer goes quickly with 20 or so people hanging out on a Saturday.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Equipment
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Basic home brew equipment is not all that expensive. You can get away with spending as little as $150 but I would suggest planning on $300-$400 to get decent equipment. You can buy many products online but shop around and visit your local home brew store like
        <a href="http://motherearthbrewco.com/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/motherearthbrewco.com/');" target="_blank" title="Mother Earth Brew Company">
         Mother Earth Brew Company
        </a>
        or
        <a href="http://www.hydrobrew.com/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.hydrobrew.com/');" target="_blank" title="Hydrobrew">
         Hydrobrew
        </a>
        . Both these home brew stores are very helpful and can help you figure out what you need. When I started brewing I shopped around and used a combination of online shopping and visiting local home brew and kitchen stores to get the equipment I wanted. Below is a list of what you need and some things you may want to consider for making 5 gallon extract batches of beer.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>

          Brewpot
         </a>
        </strong>
        (At least 16 quart capacity made of stainless steel or enamel-coated metal)
        <br/>
        <strong>
         2.
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0007R6J10/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0007R6J10" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0007R6J10/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0007R6J10');" target="_blank" title="Glass Carboy">
          Primary Fermenter
         </a>
        </strong>
        (I recommend a glass 6 gallon carboy)
        <br/>
        <strong>
         3.
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000E60G2W/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000E60G2W" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000E60G2W/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000E60G2W');" target="_blank" title="Airlock">
          Airlock
         </a>
         and Stopper
        </strong>
        (A blow off for the CO2 created during fermentation)
        <br/>
        <strong>
         4. Plastic Hose
        </strong>
        (Food grade hose for siphoning and blow off)
        <br/>
        <strong>
         5.
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000E62H8I/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000E62H8I" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000E62H8I/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000E62H8I');" target="_blank" title="Bottling Bucket">
          Bottling Bucket
         </a>
        </strong>
        (5 gallon bucket with spigot)
        <br/>
        <strong>
         6.

         7.

        <br/>

         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0019R4HQQ/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0019R4HQQ" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0019R4HQQ/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0019R4HQQ');" target="_blank" title="Kitchen Thermometer">

        <br/>
        <strong>
         9.
         <a href="http://www.northernbrewer.com/brewing/brewing-equipment/cleaning-chemicals-equipment/sanitation-cleaning/saniclean.html" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.northernbrewer.com/brewing/brewing-equipment/cleaning-chemicals-equipment/sanitation-cleaning/saniclean.html');" target="_blank" title="Sanitizers">
          Sanitizers
         </a>
        </strong>
        (Very important)
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         What You Should Consider Buying
        </strong>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         1.
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004D3T1KA/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004D3T1KA" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004D3T1KA/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004D3T1KA');" target="_blank" title="Wort Chiller">
          Wort Chiller
         </a>
        </strong>
        (To quickly chill the wort after the boil)
        <br/>
        <strong>
         2.
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000E60JF6/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000E60JF6" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000E60JF6/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000E60JF6');" target="_blank" title="Auto Siphon">
          Auto Siphon
         </a>
        </strong>
        (Makes moving beer much easier)
        <br/>
        <strong>
         3.
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000291GBQ/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000291GBQ" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000291GBQ/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000291GBQ');" target="_blank" title="Outdoor Burner">
          Outdoor Burner
         </a>
        </strong>
        (So you dont have to brew in the kitchen)
        <br/>
        <strong>
         4.
         <a href='"http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000E60U6Y/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=ncco05-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000E60U6Y' target="_blank" title="Triple Scale Hydrometer">
          Triple Scale Hydrometer
         </a>
        </strong>
        (To measure thealcoholcontent)
       </p>
       <p>
        This is just a basic list. Make sure to go over everything with your local brew store or a friend that has brewed before brewing your first batch. There are always new toys to buy to make brewing easier or to use different processes.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bottles or Kegs
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        It is cheaper to start with bottles but our group went straight to kegging. Because we drink what we brewed at the previous meeting, this is much simpler for us. We bought a cheap fridge off of craigslist and bought a
        <a href="http://stores.kegconnection.com/Detail.bok?no=431" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/stores.kegconnection.com/Detail.bok?no=431');" target="_blank" title="Basic 2 keg setup">
         basic 2 keg setup with a CO2 tank
        </a>
        . They are available online or at local shops. If you are willing to shell out the money, this is the way to go.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Choosing What To Brew
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Choosing what to brew can be the hardest part. Start with ales in the beginning and remember that you will be doing this many times so you will get a chance to try different things. We either buy supplies from a local brew store or we get extract kits from
        <a href="http://www.northernbrewer.com" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.northernbrewer.com');" target="_blank" title="Northern Brewer">
         Northern Brewer
        </a>
        online. The extract kits are nice to start with because they have everything you need pre-measured and ready to go, but as you learn about brewing it is a lot more fun to experiment with recipes.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Dont Forget
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Remember the main goal is to have fun and learn something new. Even if you have been brewing for 10 years, brewing with other people teaches you new things you may not learn without some outside input. Keep it light, be open to suggestions and enjoy the process. If you start a brew club let me know, I would love to hear about it and see pictures (and if you send me some of your beer I wont complain either).
       </p>
       <p>
        Cheers
       </p>
