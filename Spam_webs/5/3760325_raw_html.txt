     <p>
      <b>
       Inuit Ataqatigiit
      </b>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kalaallisut_language" title="Kalaallisut language">
       Greenlandic
      </a>
      for "Community of the People") is a
      <a href="/wiki/Left-wing_politics" title="Left-wing politics">
       leftist
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Separatism" title="Separatism">
       separatist
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Greenland" title="List of political parties in Greenland">
       political party in Greenland
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcnews_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-bbcnews-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-reuters-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The party, founded in 1976, was born out of the increased youth
      <a href="/wiki/Political_radicalism" title="Political radicalism">
       radicalism
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">
       Denmark
      </a>
      during the 1970s. The party strives to make Greenland an independent state.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-latimes-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenlandic_parliamentary_election,_2005" title="Greenlandic parliamentary election, 2005">
       2005 elections
      </a>
      , the party won 22.6% of the popular vote and seven out of 31 seats.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-copenhagen_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-copenhagen-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Four years later, Inuit Ataqatigiit won a 44% plurality of the popular vote and doubled their share of parliamentary seats to 14 out of 31 seats in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenlandic_parliamentary_election,_2009" title="Greenlandic parliamentary election, 2009">
       June 2009 elections
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-reuters-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Inuit Ataqatigiit is represented in the
      <a href="/wiki/Folketing" title="Folketing">
       Folketing
      </a>
      , the Danish parliament, by
      <a href="/wiki/Sara_Olsvig" title="Sara Olsvig">
       Sara Olsvig
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/Kuupik_Kleist" title="Kuupik Kleist">
       Kuupik Kleist
      </a>
      , its current president, replaced
      <a href="/wiki/Josef_Motzfeldt" title="Josef Motzfeldt">
       Josef Motzfeldt
      </a>
      as president in 2008. In the Danish
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danish_parliamentary_election,_2007" title="Danish parliamentary election, 2007">
       2007
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danish_parliamentary_election,_2011" title="Danish parliamentary election, 2011">
       2011 parliamentary elections
      </a>
      , it retained one of Greenland's two seats in the 179-seat Danish Folketing.
     </p>
     <p>
      Inuit Ataqatigiit made a major electoral breakthrough in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenlandic_parliamentary_election,_2009" title="Greenlandic parliamentary election, 2009">
       2009 Greenlandic parliamentary election
      </a>
      . Making gains from the 2005 Greenlandic parliamentary election, it doubled its total number of seats in the Parliament from 7 to 14 seats  just two seats short of a majority  and nearly doubled its total vote from 22.4% to 43.7%. It supplanted both its coalition partners, shifting the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forward_(Greenland)" title="Forward (Greenland)">
       Forward
      </a>
      party from first to second and the
      <a href="/wiki/Democrats_(Greenland)" title="Democrats (Greenland)">
       Democrats
      </a>
      party from second to third.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcnews_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-bbcnews-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-latimes-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At the 2014 elections the party maintained its 11 members in the Greenlandic parliament.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Formerly a left-wing socialist party, it has developed towards supporting
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Privatisation" title="Privatisation">
       privatisation
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Market_economy" title="Market economy">
       market economy
      </a>
      , believing that an independent Greenland should be
      <a href="/wiki/Competitiveness" title="Competitiveness">
       competitive
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
