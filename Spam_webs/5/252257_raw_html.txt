        <h3>
         <em>
          Child, he replied, that is why all the rest are now a horror to her. That is what happens to those who pluck and eat fruits at the wrong time and in the wrong way. The fruit is good, but they loathe it ever after.
         </em>
        </h3>
        <p>
         This is how Aslan explained to Polly in C.S. Lewis first
         <em>
          Narnia
         </em>
         story,
         <em>
          The Magicians Nephew,
         </em>
         the
         <strong>
          unintended consequences
         </strong>
         Jadis the witch would experience after eating in rebellion from the tree which gave to the Narnians
         <em>
          joy and life and health.
         </em>
         The
         <strong>
          consequence
         </strong>
         ? To Jadis, the tree became
         <em>
          death and horror and despair.
         </em>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Defining Words
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Consequences.
         </strong>
         Merriam-Webster Dictionary says,
         <em>
          something that happens as a result of a particular action or set of conditions.
         </em>
         Similar words?
         <em>
          Aftermath. Aftereffect.
         </em>
         <strong>
          How about
         </strong>
         <em>
          <strong>
           backwash.
          </strong>
          <span style="color: #0000ff;">
           <strong>
            
           </strong>
          </span>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Unintended
         </strong>
         is defined as
         <em>
          Happening by chance. Inadvertent. Unintended. Unplanned.
          <span style="color: #0000ff;">
           <strong>
            
           </strong>
          </span>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         So Im seeing and experiencing the
         <em>
          unintended, unplanned aftermath
         </em>
         of a specific result of someones actions. What this is making me realize is that when I myself take action, whatever it may be, there are consequences, some of which I may never see. Some of these aftereffects are encouraging to others. Some of the effects my actions have had on others are better defined as
         <em>
          aftermath.
         </em>
         Like when a tornado is unleashed on a community kind of effect. And sadly, I may never see or know what effects my actions have had on others. The trouble with us humans is that we can be such a selfish bunch.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Good Consequences and Bad
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Consequences in our modern English language tend to bring to mind images of negative things, but there can be positive consequences to our actions. In this life journey, particularly for the Christian, there are two roads to travel:
         <em>
          obedience and rebellion.
         </em>
         And both carry
         <em>
          consequences,
         </em>
         good or bad.
        </p>
        <p>
         John Piper once said very accurately of Paul,
         <em>
          All my life has been like a fight and like a race. Keeping faith has been a struggle. Trusting Gods promises (Romans 4:20) and walking by faith in the Son of God (Galatians 2:20), resting in the easy yoke of Jesus (Matthew 11:28), being anxious for nothing (Philippians 4:6) has been like a war all my life.
         </em>
         Piper continues that Paul,
         <em>
          made clear that this view of his own life was his view of the Christian life everybody is called to live.
          <span style="color: #0000ff;">
           <strong>
            
           </strong>
          </span>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Doesnt that make the Christian who is facing consequences of their own or as the result of another persons actions, or one who is in the midst of struggle, despair or even rebellion cry out with a resounding
         <em>
          Why?
         </em>
         I sure wonder why all of the struggle and the constant wars over battles already won by Christ on the cross. Death has been defeated, the end of the book written and the enemy overcome. So why such a struggle? Why, as Paul says of the Christian life,
         <em>
          Why the race, the struggle, the constant warring against anxiety?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         You could rightly say, Im exhausted. Im in pain. I am a mess and I can hardly get up and walk and you want to remind me I need to engage a
         <em>
          race
         </em>
         <em>
          ?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         The short answer is
         <em>
          yes,
         </em>
         but
         <a href="http://genewhitehead.com/truth-consequences/" title="Truth and Consequences">
          before we go there in my next post
         </a>
         , where we will take a closer look at this
         <em>
          why
         </em>
         question, I encourage you to get a head start by reading
         <a href="https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=heb+12&amp;version=LEB" target="_blank">
          Hebrews chapter 12
         </a>
         a few times over in the next couple of days. It speaks about the example of Jesus suffering and provides
         <em>
          a serious warning against refusing God.
         </em>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Deeper Still
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Back to Narnia. Polly assumed that because Jadis used the fruit of life
         <em>
          in the wrong way
         </em>
         that it simply wouldnt work for her.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #333333;">
          <em>
           Alas, said Aslan, shaking his head. It will. Things always work according to their nature. She has won her hearts desire all get what they want; they do not always like it.
          </em>
         </span>
        </h3>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #333333;">
          Your thoughts so far on
          <em>
           unintended consequences?
          </em>
          Leave your comments below.
         </span>
        </h4>
