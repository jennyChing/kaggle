	&lt;header class="entry-header"&gt;
		&lt;h1 class="entry-title"&gt;Review: DC31 Animal from Dyson&lt;/h1&gt;

		&lt;ul class="post-details clearfix"&gt;
            &lt;li&gt;6 years ago by &lt;a href="http://www.blisstree.com/author/pegrow/" title="Posts by Peggy Rowland" rel="author"&gt;Peggy Rowland&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/li&gt;
            &lt;li&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.blisstree.com/2009/09/11/sex-relationships/review-dc31-animal-from-dyson/#comments" title="Comment on Review: DC31 Animal from Dyson"&gt;&lt;span class="dsq-postid" rel="156493 http://www.blisstree.com/?p=111778"&gt;5 Comments &lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/li&gt;
			&lt;li&gt;
                &lt;a href="#top" onclick="show_modal_window('share-tip-window');"&gt;Share a Tip&lt;/a&gt;			&lt;/li&gt;
            &lt;li&gt;&lt;/li&gt;
        &lt;/ul&gt;
	&lt;/header&gt;&lt;!-- .entry-header --&gt;

	&lt;div class="entry-content"&gt;
		&lt;p&gt;Just released in the US in September, the &lt;a href="http://www.dyson.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.dyson.com']);"&gt;Dyson&lt;/a&gt; &lt;strong&gt;DC31 Animal&lt;/strong&gt; is designed to make easy work of cleaning &lt;a href="http://www.blisstree.com/articles/cat-interlopes-computer/" &gt;pet&lt;/a&gt; hair, dust, dirt, even those cardboard scraps from pet &lt;a href="http://www.blisstree.com/articles/contain-mess-with-scratchpal/" &gt;scratching pads&lt;/a&gt;. I received one to review for free.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Before using the DC31, I had long abandoned my former &lt;strong&gt;handheld vacuum&lt;/strong&gt;. It wimped out far too much and needed more charging than I had patience for.  However, I&#8217;ve become a bit addicted to the DC31. It&#8217;s not perfect, but it&#8217;s far better than other handhelds I&#8217;ve experienced. It retails for $269.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-111821" src="http://www.blisstree.com/files/2009/09/DC31-Animal.jpg" alt="DC31 Animal" width="500" height="272" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Likes&lt;/strong&gt;:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Makes cleaning pet hair and other messes from furniture, corners, stairs, windowsills and anywhere much easier. My toughest cleaning job was getting all the pet hair off the tile floor under my desk. It was difficult and awkward to reach with a broom and dustpan, but the DC31 has made it a breeze.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;The &lt;strong&gt;battery is fade-free&lt;/strong&gt;. In other words, it has constant power, even when it&#8217;s getting low of charge. An indicator light tells you when power is about to run out. The DC31 also has a fairly quick recharge: 3.5 hours.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;The &lt;strong&gt;lifetime filter&lt;/strong&gt; doesn&#8217;t require replacing! You simply wash it under the tap once a month and let it dry 12 hours before use. This is a big benefit to me. I detest buying replacement filters.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;The &lt;strong&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.dyson.com/technology/ddmtabbed.asp" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.dyson.com']);"&gt;powerful digital motor&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/strong&gt; uses less energy than conventional motors, all without losing suction. It spins up to 104,000 times a minute and doesn&#8217;t emit carbon dust.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Attachments are easy to remove or add on.  One attachment features a motorized brush to &lt;strong&gt;easily pick up pet hair&lt;/strong&gt;. (Shown in photo above.) A combination accessory tool converts to a duster with soft bristles at the tip. A crevice attachment is also included for reaching tight gaps.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;It weighs less than three pounds for easy handling.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Other features you might like&lt;/strong&gt;:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt; Dual power mode lets you choose between 38 or 65 airwatts. You get 6 minutes of use per charge with 65 watts or 10 minutes of use per charge with 38 watts. For most jobs, the 65 watt mode won&#8217;t be necessary.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Clear bin lets you know when it&#8217;s time to empty.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Can also be used as a car vacuum, but it&#8217;s not recommended for outdoor uses or around wet areas like the bathroom.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Dislikes&lt;/strong&gt;:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;I would have liked more details from the owner&#8217;s manual. They don&#8217;t name the attachments or their recommended uses.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;It&#8217;s not horribly loud, but it could certainly be quieter. My &lt;strong&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.blisstree.com/articles/keep-black-cats-safe-in-october/" &gt;cat&lt;/a&gt; ran for the hills&lt;/strong&gt; each time I turned it on. My husband also commented that it was &#8220;loud.&#8221; However, it&#8217;s not as loud as your standard upright vacuum.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;The clear bin is easy to empty by simply pressing the red lever. The bottom will fall out of the bin, emptying the contents into your trash. However, you must be careful to hold the bin carefully just above the trash or you&#8217;ll likely end up with the contents back in the floor. That only happened to me once, but I learned my lesson. Also, sometimes not everything falls out and you have to reach in and help. That usually happened when long hair was vacuumed up, forming a bit of a nest.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Overall, I like the Dyson DC31 Animal. It&#8217;s lightweight, powerful and handy for pet owners. Because of its digital motor and sturdy user-friendly design, it&#8217;s one of the best handhelds out there. It won&#8217;t stay hidden in the closet!&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Have you tried the DC31? Please tell us what you thought.&lt;/p&gt;

           <p>
            By far the best cordless hand-held vacuum Ive ever used.  I use it as my primary vacuum unless Im cleaning the whole house (for which I use the Oreck XL21).
           </p>
           <p>
            I have two cats, one with long hair, and the DC31 has made cleaning the sofa much easier.  It works well for spot cleaning the carpet as well.
           </p>
           <p>
            My complaints are:
            <br/>
            1. The canister pops open.  I would rather have the option of removing the canister when I empty it.
            <br/>
            2. The spare battery has been out of stock at dyson.com for a while.
            <br/>
            3. It costs as much as other brands upright vacuum cleaner.
           </p>
           <p>
            Overall, I highly recommend this vacuum as an addition to the full size vacuum, especially if you have pets around your house.
