      When You Go To Sephora For A Birthday Gift
     </div>
     <div class="copy">
      <p>
       -I am only going to get a gift for my friend
      </p>
      <p>
       -Oh, but I dont have
       <i>
        this
       </i>
       red
      </p>
      <p>
       -I have a lot of orangey-reds, but I dont have a tangerine red like this one. It is like tangerine, and not orange. Not even clementine. NOT EVEN.
      </p>
      <p>
       -You know, even though I get told my eyebrows are on fleek on Insta comments, nobody ever actually tells me that in real life, so do I need 84 Benefit pencils or new friends? What the hell is going on?
      </p>

       -This skincare is 75 dollars?! I am getting older, but I am more at the age of40-50 dollar skincare. Thats really where I am right now in my life and my choices.

       -Lately, Ive been craving glossy lips, and also to throw my money in a garbage can.
      </p>

       -Even though I SAY I want lip gloss, I also could just smear jelly all over my face and hair and call it a damn day
      </p>
      <p>
       -Ah yes, bronzer, the dirt of the makeup world, we meet again
      </p>

       -Screw you bronzer! Now its highlighter. I want to look like I am the gold dude in 300.
      </p>

       -How can I look like Chrissy Teigen though? What mother f**&amp;&amp;^** blush will do that HUH
      </p>

       -If I buy this, its really only one the cost of one brunch. Oh, its NARS. So its the cost of eight brunches and thirty bottles of Trader Joes wine.
      </p>
      <p>
       -How do I match my foundation to my face. But what if, great idea, I just bathe in it, like that Milk Scene when Charlize Theron was the Evil Witch in Snow White
      </p>
      <p>
       -This perfume smells like Mandy Moore used one of Alicia Keys pens in Clueless, it is so candy
      </p>
      <p>
       -This perfume smells like a cigarette got put out onto flowers, and I probably need it
      </p>
      <p>
       -This thing by the register is
       <i>
        19 dollars?
       </i>
       What are you, fuckin nuts?
      </p>
      <p>
       -I bet if I use this face mask and drink like ONE juice a week, itll pretty much cancel out how I eat like a hog
      </p>

     <div class="title">
      On Crop Tops
     </div>
     <div class="copy">
      <p>
       Everybody has a least favorite part of their body, and mine just happens to be my stomach.
      </p>
      <p>
       Im a relatively confident person, one who has overcome years of disordered eating, heavy dieting, and self-hatred. After applying enough mental analgesic (selfies! body confidence movements! Dove commercials!) to my wounds over the years, I can now say things likeI love myself! and take selfies and even get insulted on the internet without taking steps backwards.
      </p>
      <p>
       Still, I wont wear crop tops, and Ive never worn a bikini.
      </p>
      <p>
       What my body ACTUALLY looks like doesnt really matter in this specific instance, or how heavy I am or am not. Sometimes it does, because the way we treat and handle fat people who are happy with themselves (or not happy, or just existing) is completely different than the way we handle those who are thinner. But in the world of self-confidence, and self-care, It doesnt matter if youre thin or overweight or skinny or downright fat when it comes to how deep the idea of aperfect body can run in your blood, and how alienated you can feel when you do not have it, and how deeply insecure it can make you, and just how much it can affect your good time. It doesnt matter what you look like when you feel like you look less than good. I got better at it, but Im not there yet.
      </p>
      <p>
       Years ofgetting better is kind of a ridiculous concept, just in the way I mean that I spent years simply just trying to, at the very least,
       <i>
        accept
       </i>
       the body I was born in and have only one of, but here we are. I had to spend years working on liking myself. That is bonkersto work on yourself enough to get to the point where you can put on an outfit and smile and just feel nice that you are alive in the figure you are in. Its CRAZY minimal, but it is still a struggle. And its a struggle enough for me that even though I am happy most of the time, and feel beautiful, and can admit that nowIm still afraid to go outside in a crop top. Is it because I feel you should only wear a crop top if you have a six-pack? Nope. I dont go out in a crop top because I have not yet overcome the fact that
       <i>
        my
       </i>
       stomach isnt flat, and am still not confident enough to accept it, even though i want to. Im still not over it all.
      </p>
      <p>
       So. Now we get to Oprah Mag, and the crop top debacle. What it does is reiterate the fact that if (and only if) I look exactly how I already know the world wants me to look, than I can wear a crop top, too. It restricts me in the way I already restrict myself. And it makes me feel correct in my choice. And it keeps that negative thought in my head even though a) I wear dark lipstick even though I hear its not feminine and b) I wear short shorts even though I have stretch marks and c) I wear high-waisted skirts even though there was another article on how ugly THAT is and d) I leave the house in outfits I love even though Im not perfect looking, for fucks sake.
      </p>
      <p>
       But its not them that stops me. Its not news to open up a magazine or step out into the world, finding rules on how to dress if you dont fit an ideal.
       <i>
        I
       </i>
       stop me from ignoring those rules and enjoying my own life.
      </p>
      <p>
       And then I see the backlash, and I see beautiful chubby women, and beautiful skinny women, and women with stomachs much bigger and much smaller than mine, and I am reminded of another thing: bodies, and how different they are, and how inoffensive they are, and how regular they can be. And I am reminded that we think more about how we look than somebody else does, and I am reminded how many women can have fun in crop tops, and another thing. Me not wearing a crop top isnt a world problem, it is only a me problem. At this point, I wear outfits I only feel 100% confident in, and Im not sure if thats even a bad thing. I feel more beautiful in a tank top and my favorite black shorts, and thats who I am for now. We cant be perfect body-positive role models, and I am no exception. But still, I remember:
      </p>
      <p>
       I can wear a crop top, too. If (and ONLY if), I feel confident enough to do just that.
      </p>
      <p>
       Its okay if you dont, but dont forget to put something on that makes you feel damn good. Just think of that alone when youre getting dressed in the morning.
      </p>

     <div class="title">
      Things I Learned From The Move
     </div>
     <div class="copy">
      <p>
       -you will leave bobby pins in every room youve ever been in
      </p>
      <p>
       -throw out your birthday cards
      </p>
      <p>
       -throw that out, too
      </p>
      <p>
       -dont throw out any pictures
      </p>
      <p>
       -dont order furniture from a startup you forgot to research (moveloot, Im lookin at you)
      </p>
      <p>
       -dust bunnies are called bunnies because they multiply
      </p>
      <p>
       -loose change adds up, even though there is almost no change in your purse
      </p>
      <p>
       -if you havent worn it in two years and you didnt even remember having it till you found it, please just give it away
      </p>
      <p>
       -you will never wear that again
      </p>
      <p>
       -you are not meant to lift this many boxes
      </p>
      <p>
       -there is never enough packing tape in the roll
      </p>
      <p>
       -when you look at all the things on the floor, you will want to both read and set on fire that book about objects thatspark joy
      </p>
      <p>
       -the necklace is too knotty to fix
      </p>
      <p>
       -it is a bittersweet feeling to close a chapter
      </p>
      <p>
       -the turquoise flip flop was underneath the bed
      </p>
      <p>
       -the french bulldog sock was also underneath the bed
      </p>
      <p>
       -you can actually snap Ikea furniture in half
      </p>
      <p>
       -friends become your family after a while, and you can drink to that
      </p>
      <p>
       -family, both real and created, is worth more than all the bobby pins in all the rooms you have left behind
      </p>
      <p>
       -your air conditioner is filled with water
      </p>
      <p>
       -air conditioning is akin to godliness
      </p>
      <p>
       -your grandmothers clock is now on the wall, and you wish you could tell her that
      </p>
      <p>
       -dont wear sandals
      </p>
      <p>
       -cold showers and cold beers are like air and water when you move in the summer
      </p>
      <p>
       -all things pass
      </p>
      <p>
       -you can never have too much money or too much love
      </p>
      <p>
       -let some things go, and never let some things go, and trust you know the difference
      </p>
