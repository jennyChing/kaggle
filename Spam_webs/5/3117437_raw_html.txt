           <p>
            RUSH: It is time, my friends. Never a dull moment. Everybody tuned in to the EIB Network waiting for America's Real Anchorman, Rush Limbaugh, well-known Hall of Fame broadcaster. I've been doing this for a long time.
           </p>
           <p>
            JOHNNY DONOVAN: And now, from sunny south Florida, it's Open Line Friday!
           </p>
           <p>
            RUSH: I don't know. I guess I shouldn't let it get to me. But I am steaming. I am steaming over the son of the potbellied dictator being able to determine what movies I can see. This is outrageous. And the AP has a story saying that our options are limited! Our options are limited. I'll tell you, America's days as a superpower are effectively over, if this is our attitude.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img class="img_middle" id="eZObject_100165" src="http://www.rushimg.com/cimages//media/images/rushom/1334183-1-eng-GB/RushOM.jpg"/>
            The
            <a href="http://www.pbs.org/newshour/rundown/u-s-sanctions-way-sony-hack/" target="_blank">
             Associated Press
            </a>
            : "Suspicions that North Korea was behind a destructive hacking attack against Sony Pictures and a threat against movie theaters are intensifying calls for tougher US steps to cut that country's access to hard currency and declare it once more as a state sponsor of terrorism." Well, that'll really scare 'em. That's what we need. I'll tell you what we need, let's get Marie Harf. Let's get that little -- sorry -- let's get the spokesbabe over at the state, Marie Harf, and let's have her stand up at a press conference and declare once more that the Norks are a state-sponsor of terrorism, and then we'll watch Kim Jong-un quake in fear. Right.
           </p>
           <p>
            "At first glance," says the AP, "US options for responding to the hacking attack are limited. Bringing the shadowy hackers to justice appears a distant prospect." Of course. We're never gonna get to the bottom of it. Meanwhile, if we were being consistent here, Sony executives would be in jail today. If you remember last September on the 11th of September, it was in Cairo, the State Department, in our embassy in Cairo,
            <a href="http://www.rushlimbaugh.com/daily/2014/06/18/benghazi_perp_set_up_to_blame_video" target="_blank">
             issued an apology for something that hadn't happened
            </a>
            . They issued a preemptive apology for a video made by "Bacula Alasul nose hair" or whatever his name was, that nobody had ever seen, but was thought to inspire anti-US violence on the part of Islamic jihadists if they did see it.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.rushlimbaugh.com/daily/2014/06/18/benghazi_perp_set_up_to_blame_video" target="_blank">
             <img class="img_middle" id="eZObject_100167" src="http://www.rushimg.com/cimages//media/images/obamahillarylyingtoamericabenghaziperp/1334189-1-eng-GB/ObamaHillaryLyingtoAmericaBenghaziPerp.jpg"/>
            </a>
            So we issued this apology in advance that was supposed to quell any protests. By apologizing in advance, we were admitting guilt and hopefully the anti-American protesters in Cairo would stand down. Well, we all know what happened. Obama and Hillary and Susan Rice for weeks blamed this video, and they said, "We cannot tolerate this. We cannot tolerate these kinds of insults to decent people."
