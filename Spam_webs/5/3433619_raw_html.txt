        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Dominique King: Interview with a Travel Blogger
        </h1>
        <div class="comments-link">
         <a href="http://www.visitflyovercountry.com/2015/02/dominique-king-interview-with-a-travel-blogger.html#comments">
          3 Replies
         </a>
        </div>
        <!-- .comments-link -->

       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Dominique King of Midwest Guest is one of the most focused travel bloggers I know: she rarely deviates from her niche of Midwest travel. One of the longest running blogs dedicated to the Midwest is
         <a href="http://www.midwestguest.com/">
          Midwest Guest
         </a>
         , her personal travel blog that offers a distinct flavor of our home region. Dominique writes for several other print and online media, and is one of the most well-respected Midwest travel writers around. Although I have followed her travels for years, I just recently took some time to learn a bit more about her and her Midwest Guest site.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_838" style="width: 235px">
         <a href="http://www.visitflyovercountry.com/wp-content/uploads/profile-picture.jpg">
          <img alt="Dominique King at Midwest Guest" class="wp-image-838 size-full" height="300" src="http://www.visitflyovercountry.com/wp-content/uploads/profile-picture.jpg" width="225"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Midwest Guest
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Quick Stats
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">
          Name: Dominique King
          <br/>
         </span>
         Blog Name: Midwest Guest
         <br/>
         <span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">
          Website Address:
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.midwestguest.com" style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">
          http://www.midwestguest.com
          <br/>
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">
          Blogging Since: 2008
          <br/>
         </span>
         <span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">
          Blog Niche: Midwest travel, history, culture
         </span>
         <span id="more-837">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Tell me a bit about yourself?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <em>
          We live in Metro Detroit. I graduated from Wayne State University in Detroit with a Bachelors degree inPolitical Science and a Masters degree in Speech, Communication and Rhetoric. Our family owned anindustrial grinding business, where I worked from high school until we sold the business in late 2012. Iworked for more than a decade as a freelance business writer for local print publications. I also had aweekly On the Street column for a series of local weeklies for several years, wrote advertorial copy for amajor Detroit glossy magazine, wrote Chamber of Commerce newsletters and did other one-offassignments for local publications and business clients. Tim, who is my in-house photographic guru andIT guy, and I like to travel where we can explore our special interests in photography and history. Timrecently became president of metro Detroits Motor City Camera Club and publishes occasional travelposts at
          <a href="http://www.photowanderer.typepad.com/">
           The Wandering Photographer
          </a>
          and images at
          <a href="http://photowanderer.smugmug.com/">
           SmugMug
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Why did you start your own travel blog?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <em>
          I always wanted to write about regional travel and occasionally managed to sell a travel-related article tothe print media where I worked as a freelance business writer. As the print market for freelancers reallydried up in the Detroit area around 2008, I established Midwest Guest to concentrate on writing andpublishing the regional travel stories I really love doing.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Why do you choose to write about the Midwest?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <em>
          We live in the Midwest and did a lot of weekend travel even as we both still had full-time jobs in differentfields. There also seemed to be very few people blogging about Midwest travel at that time, so it was agreat niche for me.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Since you started travel blogging, what has been your most memorable Midwest trip?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <em>
          Theyre all memorable, but I particularly enjoy the trips where Tim and I are able to spend more than afew days in one location. We traveled to Michigans Isle Royale a few years ago, which had always beenon our bucket list. We stayed in a cabin on the island for a few days and coupled that trip with a few daysin Copper Harbor at the tip of Michigans Keweenaw Peninsula.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What Midwest destinations do you most commonly recommend when asked?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <em>
          People tend to ask me about Michigan or Ohio as I do a lot of stories about both of those states and canoften point to stories Ive done about specific locations there.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What is your favorite post of your own?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.midwestguest.com/2013/04/follow-the-north-star-at-conner-prairie.html">
           Follow the North Star at Conner Prairie (Indiana)
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What destinations are on your Midwest bucket list?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <em>
          Milwaukee, Madison, Spring Green and other destinations in Wisconsin. Art museums in Akron,Milwaukee, Dayton, Flint (Michigan) and the Gallery of Costume at the Kent State University Museum(Ohio). Wed also like to spend some time in Columbus (Ohio), Columbus (Indiana), Galena (Illinois) andrevisit Chicago. This list is definitely just for starters!
         </em>
        </p>

    date.setTime(date.getTime()+(days*24*60*60*1000));
    var expires = "; expires="+date.toGMTString();
  } else {
    var expires = "";
  }
  document.cookie = name+"="+value+expires+"; path=/";
}

function readCookie(name) {
  var nameEQ = name + "=";
  var ca = document.cookie.split(';');
  for(var i=0;i &lt; ca.length;i++) {
    var c = ca[i];
    while (c.charAt(0)==' ') {
      c = c.substring(1,c.length);
    }
    if (c.indexOf(nameEQ) == 0) {
      return c.substring(nameEQ.length,c.length);
    }
  }
  return null;
}

function eraseCookie(name) {
  createCookie(name,"",-1);
}

    if (accordion==1) {
      var theDiv = jQuery(this).parent().parent().find('span.collapse').parent().children('div');
      jQuery(theDiv).hide('normal');
      jQuery(this).parent().parent().find('span.collapse').removeClass('collapse').addClass('expand');
      createCookie(theDiv.attr('id'), 0, 7);
      jQuery(this).parent().parent().find('span.sym').html(expandSym);
      //jQuery('#' + id + ' .expand .sym').html(expandSym);

    return false;
  });
  jQuery('#' + id + ' span.collapse').live('click', function() {
    collapseArch(this, expandSym, collapseSym);
    return false;
  });
}
