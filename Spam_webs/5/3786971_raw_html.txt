Each age-appropriate title will be made available from the publishers' online libraries, though there's no word on whether the e-title will be given away or simply checked out as traditionally lent books are.      <h1 class="h1" itemprop="headline">
      Obama to provide 10,000 free e-books through your library
     </h1>
     <p class="byline">
      <span class="byline-container">
       <img alt="blogger-avatar" class="avatar" height="24" src="http://o.aolcdn.com/dims-global/dims/GLOB/5/24/24/http://www.blogcdn.com/www.engadget.com/media/2015/03/imag0392.jpg" width="24"/>
       <span class="by">
        by
       </span>
       <a data-image="http://o.aolcdn.com/dims-global/dims/GLOB/5/24/24/http://www.blogcdn.com/www.engadget.com/media/2015/03/imag0392.jpg" href="/about/editors/andrew-tarantola/">

         Andrew Tarantola
        </strong>

       <span class="bar">

        May 1st 2015 at 9:01am

       <span class="time" data-swiftype-name="published_at" data-swiftype-type="date" style="display:none">
        May 1st 2015 9:01 am
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="permalink-social-tools">
      <a class="aol-share-permalink" data-bid="21" data-pid="21178648" href="mailto:yourfriend@email.com?subject=Obama%20to%20provide%2010%2C000%20free%20e-books%20through%20your%20library&amp;body=www.engadget.com/2015/05/01/obama-10-000-free-e-books/" title="Obama to provide 10,000 free e-books through your library">

      <a class="comment-count" href="#comments">

       <span class="livefyre-commentcount" data-lf-article-id="21178648" data-lf-site-id="296092" data-swiftype-name="lf-count" data-swiftype-type="integer" itemprop="interactionCount">

    <div class="copy post-body" data-author="andrew-tarantola" data-mn-header="93319229" data-mn-onvis="93431222" data-mn-rail-topper="93310581" data-mn-sponsor-logo="" data-mobile-onvis="93431222" data-mobile-placement="348-14-15-1679" data-original-publication-date="05-01-2015" data-postid="21178648" data-prop19="BarackObama|connectED|Digital Public Library of America|e book|e books|e reader|e readers|e-book|e-books|e-reader|e-readers|Hachette|HarperCollins|NewYorkPublicLibrary|Penguin Random House|SimonSchuster|UnitedStates" data-sourceid="original_content" data-title="Obama to provide 10,000 free e-books through your library" data-url="www.engadget.com/2015/05/01/obama-10-000-free-e-books/" data-videopresence="video_novideo" itemprop="text">
     <div class="article-content" data-swiftype-name="body" data-swiftype-type="text">
      <p class="image-container" style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2015/04/30/obama-10-000-free-e-books/">
        <img alt="Children Using E-Readers" data-caption="WAYLAND - JULY 20: The Levy kids read books on their e-readers. Kids and teens are using e-readers, helping to make them a popular back-to-school item. Sam Levy reads in his room. (Photo by Suzanne Kreiter/The Boston Globe via Getty Images)" data-credit="Boston Globe via Getty Images" data-mep="802788" src="http://o.aolcdn.com/dims-shared/dims3/GLOB/crop/4896x3263+0+0/resize/630x420!/format/jpg/quality/85/http://hss-prod.hss.aol.com/hss/storage/midas/512ba36ca6e4aea597024b223aff28f8/201935092/120106045.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2015/03/09/obama-techhire/">
        President Barack Obama
       </a>
       announced a new program on Thursday aimed at delivering access for more than 10,000 e-books to financially strapped schoolchildren throughout the United States. The $250 million program will feature titles from numerous publishers including Macmillan, Simon &amp; Schuster, Penguin Random House, HarperCollins and Hachette, selected by volunteers from Digital Public Library of America. The
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2014/03/31/new-york-public-library-free-maps/">
        New York Public Library
       </a>
       has signed on to develop the free app. "It's very different than from our generation," Cecilia Muoz, Obama's domestic policy adviser, told
       <a href="http://www.reuters.com/article/2015/04/30/us-usa-obama-ebooks-idUSKBN0NL13X20150430">
        <em>
         Reuters
        </em>
       </a>
       . "More and more, you're going to be seeing kids using devices, and what we're doing is making sure that there's more books available on those devices."
      </p>
      <p>
       As the president's top economic advisor Jeff Zients pointed out to
       <em>
        Reuters
       </em>
       ,
       <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/blog/2015/04/30/connected-open-books-and-open-doors">
        research shows
       </a>
       that some 80 percent of low-income children are behind the rest of their grade in terms of reading skills. Few of them have books at home. That's why Obama's program will also work with local libraries, boosting their enrollment of local kids in order to provide them with hardware necessary to enjoy these books. Each age-appropriate title will be made available from the publishers' online libraries, though there's no word on whether the e-title will be given away or simply checked out as traditionally lent books are. Still, any excuse to
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2015/04/06/hulu-perfect-gif-library/">
        get kids into the library
       </a>
       is a good one.
      </p>
