        <h1>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
          This is where updates to a
          <span style="color: #000080;">
           <a href="http://mosswoodconnections.com/plentiful-living-with-a-minimalist-approach-to-life/" style="color: #000080;">
            Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach to Life
           </a>
          </span>
          will be posted.
         </span>
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          Each year my family adopts a theme to live by.in 2014 our theme was Living with a Grateful Heart. We made it a daily practice to take inventory of all of the love and goodness around us, and before long we began to realize how abundantly blessed we are. Through this process, I also began to realize that there were too many distractions in our lives, both physical and emotional, and that it was time to employ a minimalist approach to life by eliminating the excess and the unnecessary clutter. This would allow us to focus on what we really care about.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h1 style="text-align: center;">
         <span class="text_exposed_show" style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          <strong>
           Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach to Life Continued
          </strong>
         </span>
        </h1>
        <p>
         <img alt="Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach Continued" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5928" height="620" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/620x620xPlentiful-Living-with-a-Minimalist-Approach-Continued.jpg.pagespeed.ic.ai0vTjL6hb.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         <span class="text_exposed_show" style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          <strong>
           Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach to Life Updates:
          </strong>
         </span>
        </h2>
        <h4>
         <strong>
          The Purge Continues-
         </strong>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          L really wanted to buy the new Kids Bop Cd so I suggested that he go through his books and choose some items to take to the book re-seller in downtown Campbell. They didnt buy them all but he did sell 20 books and made $20.00. Almost enough for his CD. We donated the remaining books to the various free little libraries in our neighborhood. This is a win/win scenario as L gets to buy his CD and our house continues to lose weight
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach Day 8" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5930" height="693" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/520x693xPlentiful-Living-with-a-Minimalist-Approach-Day-8.jpg.pagespeed.ic.Q2m74k10ol.jpg" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          <strong>
           Spending Time with Friends is Free-
          </strong>
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          It was a great day, we spent our day in the company of some of our favorite people and finished the night with an amazing meal prepared by Tom who will NEVER be a minimalist in the kitchen but we like that. Thanks Tom, for a fantastic Michelin star worthy meal!
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          Tomorrow is the first day of my challenge to not buy anything other than consumables (like food, we have to eat.
          <img alt="" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-edit/plugins/emoticons/img/smiley-smile.gif"/>
          )and Im excited to see what I will discover about myself in the process.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach Day 9" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5947" height="640" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Plentiful-Living-with-a-Minimalist-Approach-Day-9.jpg" width="480"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          <strong>
           Oh, the Home Office
          </strong>
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          Today I tackled the home office. I filled an entire laundry basket with unsolicited offers for credit cards, home loans, gym memberships etcI wonder if theres a way to get off marketing lists to avoid the waste of paper production, which Im assuming (surely?) has a larger carbon footprint than what I imagine.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          I did have a little bit of a hard time today when we stopped at Walgreens for some eye drops, and saw Christmas ornaments on sale 90%off! I picked them up, held them, and wrestled with ways to make it okay to buy them but, alas, I stuck to my goal and walked out of the store sans cheap ornaments. Truth is, we have more ornaments than we need and my favorites are the ones we made at home, which are like little time capsules that take me back to past Christmases.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach Day 10" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5957" height="429" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Plentiful-Living-with-a-Minimalist-Approach-Day-10.jpg" width="648"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          <strong>
           Too Many Obligations-
          </strong>
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          I havent had any trouble eliminating clutter from my home but my schedule is a wreck! It seems like I have so many obligations this week and hardly any time to relax. I cant do anything about this week but next week will have a very different pace. On a positive note, I havent made any nonessential purchases. Though I did order a delicious coffee alternative beverage from Amazon called Teeccino, but my morning cup is indeed essential.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          Have a great night everyone
         </span>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          <strong>
           <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
            The Minimalist Manifesto
           </span>
           <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
            
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #808080; font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          Thanks to all for following my journey. Your interest is both encouraging and makes me accountable to you all. With all of the newcomers to the page, I thought it apropos to repost my manifesto in case you missed it the first time.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="color: #808080; font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          I was out of the house the whole day so I didnt get to work on organizing my home but I accomplished so much both on a professional front as well as a personal front. Most importantly, after several weeks of trying different forms of martial arts, L (my little one) has finally settled on Jiu-Jitsu! Now that thats settled, we can finally ease into our new after school schedule. Good night!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach Day 12" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5958" height="607" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Plentiful-Living-with-a-Minimalist-Approach-Day-12.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
           Going Paperless
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          Earlier this week I tackled the home office and I was amazed by the amount of junk mail that we receive. We get a few of our bills electronically already but I have few more that I need to switch over. Im also planning to cancel all magazine subscriptions . It feels so good to get rid of all this paper clutter
          <img alt="" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-edit/plugins/emoticons/img/smiley-laughing.gif"/>
          If you want help getting started on going paperless check out this article:
          <a href="http://www.moneycrashers.com/going-paperless-home/" target="_blank">
           <span style="color: #0000ff;">
            How to Go Paperless at Home
           </span>
          </a>
          .
         </span>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="474" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/cpkRvc-sOKk" width="904">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
           What minimalism has taught me so far.
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          1. Laundry is way more manageable when you own fewer articles of clothing.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          2. Theres no need to save the fancy stuff (China, crystal etc..) for special occasions. After 15 years of storage, I have finally incorporated our China into our daily use dish wear. So what if it breaks.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          3. Now that the clutter is mostly gone, Im spending less time cleaning up the house.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          4. Speaking of time, time management is something I really need to work
          <span class="text_exposed_show">
           on.
           <br/>
          </span>
          <span class="text_exposed_show">
           5. Sharing this goal of minimalism with my husband has brought us closer. I appreciate that hes actively participating in becoming a minimalist family.
          </span>
          <span class="text_exposed_show">
           <br/>
           6. I have way more makeup than I will ever use. Time to clean out my makeup bag and embrace my imperfections.
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          <img alt="Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach Continued" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5990" height="827" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Plentiful-Living-with-a-Minimalist-Approach-Continued1.jpg" width="620"/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <h4>
        </h4>
        <h4>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
           Attacking the craft supplies:
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          Before L was born I had a voracious addiction to scrapbooking and card making. I often bought my supplies when they were on sale and I always ended up with more than I could use. Once my son arrived, I didnt have the time or the desire to scrapbook. My supplies ended up with the same fate as my China. Ignored and forgotten in a closet in the garage. The other night L and his friend L were making puppets. Thats when I decided that it was time to to clean out my supplies and so I gave them free range to all of my expensive paper and embellishments. In the past I would have weighed the cost of the paper against the intended use but now I could really care less. I cant hang on to things indefinitely and watching L and L
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          get creative was so worth it.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          <strong>
           Time to Party!
          </strong>
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          Theres nothing minimalist about celebrating a half birthday but thats just what we did this past Friday. In the birthdays past I spent too much time planning the party and preparing for the event. It created so much stress for me and my family that after the party I often felt like I did when I ran a triathlon.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          L started talking about his half birthday back in December (we celebrate half birthdays because hes a summer baby and most of his friends travel during the summer
          <span class="text_exposed_show">
           months making attendance slim) he usually has a strong opinion about the theme of his party. Lets see, weve had a super hero disco dance party, a parade party, a Mardi Gras Mambo party, etc This year he had lots of ideas but never settled on one. In the past few weeks he hadnt much mentioned a party until 4 days before his half birthday. He wanted to have it at a very expensive childrens party center. Somehow this didnt seem to fit well with our theme for the year so 3 days before his half birthday, I decided to plan a surprise picnic at one of his favorite knolls in the foothills. I was tempted to bake a cake and prepare picnic goodies but with so little time I decided instead to purchase a cake, strawberries, a bag of chips and a couple of cases of water. No fancy pulled pork sandwiches, no Jambalaya, no stress. We hung a few lanterns and a piata from an oak tree and we pinned a pin the tail on the donkey poster on the trunk of one of the trees. We provided a few picnic blankets to sit on and that was all. No goody bags, no expensive party locale, and no gifts (although a few people broke the rules). It was a free range party with very little structure.The kids had a great time running around and L said it was his best party yet. The total cost of the party was $110.00 which this day in age is awfully inexpensive.
           <br/>
           I look forward to more low stress, free range, minimalist parties with L and his friends!
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Plentiful Living with a Minimalist Approach Continued" class="aligncenter wp-image-6031 size-full" height="674" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Plentiful-Living-with-a-Minimalist-Approach-Continued2-e1422296231599.jpg" width="472"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
           Toys or Time?
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          L has a few favorite toys but until recently he preferred playing with me over anything else. Now he loves playing with friends which is appropriate but I sure do miss our eternal play dates.
          <img alt="" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-edit/plugins/emoticons/img/smiley-smile.gif"/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          <strong>
           HOW MANY HOUSEHOLD CLEANERS DO WE REALLY NEED?
          </strong>
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          Am I the only one with a billion cleaners under the kitchen sink? I hate to throw it all out but Im sure its all highly toxic. My goal for tomorrow is to replace the 777 bottles of cleaners with no more than 5 non-toxic, multipurpose homemade cleaners. Ill be sure to share the formulas after I test them out.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://eartheasy.com/live_nontoxic_solutions.htm" target="_blank">
          Non-Toxic Home Cleaning
         </a>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <strong>
          HOW TO BE MINIMIZE WHILE SHARING:
         </strong>
        </h4>
        <p>
         Im in love with all of the little free libraries that are sprinkled through my neighborhood. I also love the fruit thats shared on the sidewalks but my favorite is the free trading post that one of my neighbors started a few years ago. Hooray for sharing.
         <img alt="" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-edit/plugins/emoticons/img/smiley-laughing.gif"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: helvetica; font-size: 12pt;">
          <strong>
           <img alt="Plentiful Living Continued Collage" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6076" height="600" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Plentiful-Living-Continued-Collage.jpg" width="600"/>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <h4>
        </h4>
        <h4>
         <strong>
          COLLECT MEMORIES NOT THINGS-Anonymous
         </strong>
        </h4>
        <p>
         L loves to make videos and Im always game for any flavor of silliness. We laughed so hard the night we made this movie that my abs actually hurt from all of the laughter. Im looking forward to making lots more happy deposits into our family memory bank
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe height="460" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/ARLBeXIUTcM" width="640">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its been a while since my last update and Ive had many moments of weakness but Ive greatly reduced the amount of stuff that I bring into the home. L has the next few days off of school so Ive decided to break out all of the arts and crafts to see what he can create. Some of the stuff he used in todays creations were things I bought 10 years ago! Im glad hes bringing new life to forgotten materials.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Crafts and painting with kids." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6175" height="621" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Plentiful-Living-with-a-Minimalist-Approach-Continued5.jpg" width="621"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I havent forgotten my goal but life has a funny way of interfering with plans. I have a few hurdles in front of me that I need to deal with but Ill be back at it in no time. I can proudly report that I can walk through my garage!!! And! I found a dress that I bought for NYE two years ago (its never been worn!!) that I thought Id lost!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Minimalist Manifesto Spiritual Growth" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6330" height="465" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Plentiful-Spiritual-Growth.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Have you ever noticed how much trash you generate while on vacation? Its mind boggling! Weve been in Arizona for two days and weve already filled the trash can in our condo. The challenge for the rest of our time here is to eliminate buying food served on disposable plates/cups/cutlery or anything that comes in packaging.
         <br/>
         Wish us luck!
         <img alt="" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-edit/plugins/emoticons/img/smiley-foot-in-mouth.gif"/>
        </p>
