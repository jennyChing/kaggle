         <h1 class="title entry-title">
          3 Words to Beat Your Doubting Brain
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="small">
          By
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://makemomentum.com/author/deirdre-m/" rel="author" title="Posts by Deirdre Maloney">
            Deirdre Maloney
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="small">
          on
         </span>
         <abbr class="date time published updated" title="2013-02-19T21:48:44-0700">
          February 19, 2013
         </abbr>
         <span class="small">
          in
         </span>
         <span class="categories">
          <a href="http://makemomentum.com/category/uncategorized/" title="View all items in Uncategorized">

          </a>

        <section class="entry">
         <p>
          A few months ago I facilitated a multi-day planning session for a group of scientists inMichigan.
         </p>
         <p>
          Id worked hard to get the projectand worked even harder once Id landed it. Many hours were spent on planning with the CEO, who was waiting for me when I arrivedon the big day.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ready? she asked, a bit expectantly.
         </p>
         <p>
          I smiled at her, looked her square in the face and said I sure am!
         </p>
         <p>
          The truth? I was nervous. Really, really nervous.
         </p>
         <p>
          This was my first time working with scientists, working in The Great Lakes State, working with this exact kind of process. And there was a lot at stake.
         </p>
         <p>
          And when Im nervous, I tend to show it.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3445" height="225" src="//makemomentum.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/108/2013/02/fear-pic-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Yet I knew that in this case, as nervous as I felt, I couldnt show it. I hadnt been hired to be nervous. Id been hired to use my experience and skills to do a job, one that Id prepared for and was ready for.
         </p>
         <p>
          My creeping doubts were notthe CEOsproblem.
         </p>
         <p>
          Instead of showing my anxiety I needed to act as if I had every second in controlas if I wasnt feeling one single pang of anxietyas if I knew with certainty that it was all going to work out amazingly fine.
         </p>
         <p>
          I had to
          <em>
           act as if
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          We have a lot of firsts in our lives, times when we try something new or expand on a project, skill or activity.
         </p>
         <p>
          We spend a lot of time worrying about how things will gowhether we will be able to pull off a complicated presentation on time, whether we will be able to handle our kids teenage years, whether well survive our first time in New York rush hour.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3446" height="202" src="//makemomentum.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/108/2013/02/will-ferrell-300x202.jpg" width="300"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Worry has found a nice, warm, safe home in the brain. Our thoughts go in millions of directions about the various things that can go wrong.
         </p>
         <p>
          When this happens we often try tochange our thinking. We affirm that we know what were doing, convince ourselves that were ready, tell ourselves all will be fine.
         </p>
         <p>
          And sometimes it works.
         </p>
         <p>
          But there are plenty of other times when the brain doesnt quite buy it. Its not ready for reprogramming yet, and prefers to battle this one out, forcing our fears into our thoughts in a steady stream.
         </p>
         <p>
          And thats when we need to forget about trying to change the brain and focus instead on our actions.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats when we need to
          <em>
           act as if
          </em>
          and trust the brain to catch up later.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Acting as if
          </em>
          means
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Showing confidence when diving into a new project head first, even though our brain tells us theres no way we can do it as well as the other guy
          </li>
          <li>
           Walking into a meeting with our head held high and engaging in conversation, even though we feel like everyone around us is more accomplished, knowledgeable and smarter than we are
          </li>
          <li>
           Arriving on the first day of a new job with enthusiasm and energy, even though we wonder if we actually snowed our new boss into hiring us
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <em>
           Acting as if
          </em>
          isnt about pretending to be more confident just for the good of others. Its about changing our own thinking so that we are more confident in ourselves.
         </p>
         <p>
          Great leaders are masters at this.
         </p>
         <p>
          When we
          <em>
           act as if
          </em>
          , we go full steam ahead into a situation, refusing to let the doubts in our mind win over. Eventually, as our actions show that we actually know what were doing  our mind catches up.
         </p>
         <p>
          It saysoh, I guess I really could do this. How about that?
         </p>
         <p>
          These three simple words 
          <em>
           act as if
          </em>
           work in all kinds of situationsnew responsibilities at work, new hobbies, new commitments to give up a bad habit we cant imagine living without.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its similar to another commonphrase:
          <em>
           fake it til you make it
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          But I like this one better becausewere not really faking anything.
          <em>
           Acting as if
          </em>
          isnt really about acting. Its about action. Its about forging ahead (knowing were smart and prepared). Its about learning from what went right so we can do it again next time. Its about learning from what went wrong so we can do it even better.
         </p>
         <p>
          No
          <em>
           acting as if
          </em>
          wont guarantee a victory every timethough sometimes well get there.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3448" height="224" src="//makemomentum.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/108/2013/02/will-ferrell-2-300x224.jpg" width="300"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Either way, thats life. The tragedy is when we allow our anxieties to keep us from trying something we are truly ready for.
         </p>
         <p>
          Like facilitating a multi-day planning session for a group of scientists in Michigan. Was it perfect? No. But did it meet the objectives? Sure did. Was the CEO pleased with the results? Sure was.
         </p>
         <p>
          And now every part of me knows I can do it.
         </p>
         <p>
          Even my mindwhich eventually caught up.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           This week
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          As you embark on doing something differently for the first time, recognize the doubts and forge ahead anyway.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Act as if
          </em>
          . Your mind will catch up.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now go do goodand do it
          <em>
           well
          </em>
          .
         </p>
        </section>
