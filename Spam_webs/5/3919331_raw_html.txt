        All Natural Products for Your Skin

         <div class="ob-text">
          <p>
           Most of the people know that it is time to visit their dermatologist when their skin get rough and lose its complexion. Worse to it is that most people will run for over the counter prescriptions which offer temporal remedies and the problem therefore persists. All these are uncalled for since with the following natural products you can take good care of your skin at a much less cost. The first natural product you can use is olive oil cleanser that works both as a cleanser and a moisturizer to restore the moisture content of your skin. All you need is to pour small amount of the oil into your hand and then rub it into your skin. You also need to drape a relatively warm and damp piece of cloth over your face. Keep draping until the skin cools and then wipe the excess oil using a dry piece of cloth. Another great natural product is avocado mask. First clash one half of the avocado. Add one tablespoonful of olive oil. For dry skin add honey into the mixture and apply the mask formed to your skin. Then, you need to leave it for more than 15 minutes after which you will wash it off.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Other all natural products for your skin
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The next natural products call for you to mix half cup sugar with two tablespoon olive oil. If essentials oil is available, add small amounts of it into the mixture. An example of an essential oil that you can add is Lavender. Rub the mixture gently into your skin and then clean it off. To rehydrate your dry skin use oatmeal soak. After using oatmeal soak, your skin will be able to retain its moisture level after bathing. You can also use an oatmeal honey mask for rehydrating your dry skin. Add two tablespoon oats to a tablespoonful of honey. The mixture needs some warming hence you need to add some few drops of water. Just like an avocado mask, rub the oatmeal honey mask into your skin and then wash it off after a period of not less than 15 minutes. This will leave your skin rehydrated and smooth.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            How to use natural products for your skin
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Treatments is far better than cure hence you need to take a proactive strategy to ensuring that your skin remain smooth and is free from dehydration. For this, always use a natural product that will moisturize your skin every time you bath. You should make skin moisturizing a part of your to-do-list. Thinker moisturizers are better. You should also limit the time that you take your shower to at most 10 minutes. Very warm water and long periods of showering are habits that expose the skin to dehydration as warm water is known to take away moisture from the skin. To supplements the efforts of organic products in ensuring that you have a smooth skin, always drink a lot of water. Natural products have numerous benefits such as prevention of skin aging and skin problems such as irritation and allergies. Also these products have numerous benefits to the environment as they usually dont contain any chemicals that can pollute air or water. Most skins are allergic to certain chemical hence when one
           <a class="popup" href="http://organiccheckout.com/">
            uses natural products
           </a>
           they are assured that there will be no allergic reactions.
          </p>

        h('p.ob-info', [
          h('span.ob-avatar', avatar),
          h('span.ob-user', [
            h('span.ob-name', [
              comment.user.url
                ? h('a.ob-website', {rel: "nofollow", href: comment.user.url}, comment.user.name)
                : h('span.ob-website', comment.user.name)
            ]),
            h('span.ob-date', comment.date)
          ]),
          allowReply
            ? h('span', {className: 'ob-button ob-reply'}, h('button', {onclick: replyFn}, I18N.reply))
            : null
        ]),
        h('p.ob-message', [
          h('span.ob-text', renderText(comment.comment))
        ]),
        h('.ob-reply-moderated', I18N.moderated),
        h('.ob-reply-published', I18N.published),
        replyContainer,
      ]),
      repliesContainer,
      loadMore
    ])
  }

  function renderForm(commentId) {
    return h('iframe#ob-comments-iframe-' + commentId + '.ob-comments-iframe', {
      allowtransparency: true,
      src: REPLY_URL.replace("__id_comment__", commentId)
    })
  }

  function rpc(url, params) {
    var fn = "_"+((new Date().getTime())*Math.random() &gt;&gt;&gt; 0)
    window[fn] = params.callback

    url += '/' + fn
    url += '/' + params.id
    url += '/' + params.offset
    url += '/' + params.limit

    document.body.appendChild(h('script', {src: url}))
  }

  function fetchComments(parentId, offset, cb) {
    rpc(RPC_COMMENTS, {
      callback: cb,
      id: parentId,
      offset: offset,
      limit: COMMENT_LIMIT,
    })
  }

  function fetchReplies(parentId, offset, cb) {
    rpc(RPC_REPLIES, {
      callback: cb,
      id: parentId,
      offset: offset,
      limit: COMMENT_LIMIT,
    })
  }

  var mainReplyContainer = document.querySelector("#main-reply")
  if (mainReplyContainer) {
    mainReplyContainer.appendChild(renderForm(0))
  }

  var rootContainer = document.querySelector('.ob-list')
  var rootLoadMore = h('div.load-more-comments', {onclick: rootLoadMoreFn}, [
    h('span.load-more-comments-link', I18N.more_comments)
  ])

  fetchComments(POST_ID, 0, function(data) {
    rootContainer.innerHTML = ''
    for (var i = 0; i &lt; data.length; i++) {
      rootContainer.appendChild(renderComment(data[i].comment, data[i].replies || [], ALLOW_REPLY))
    }
    if (data.length === COMMENT_LIMIT) {
      rootContainer.parentNode.appendChild(rootLoadMore)
    }
  })

  function rootLoadMoreFn() {
    offsets[0] += COMMENT_LIMIT
    fetchComments(POST_ID, offsets[0], function(comments) {
      if (comments.length !== COMMENT_LIMIT) {
        removeChild(rootLoadMore)
      }
      for (var i = 0; i &lt; comments.length; i++) {
        rootContainer.appendChild(renderComment(comments[i].comment, comments[i].replies || [], ALLOW_REPLY))
      }
    })
  }

  function renderText(text) {
    var parts = text.split(/&lt;br ?\/?&gt;/)
    var children = []
    for (var i = 0; i &lt; parts.length; i++) {
      children.push(parts[i], h('br'))
    }
    children.pop()
    return children
  }
