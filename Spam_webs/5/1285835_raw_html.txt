          <h1 class="entry-title">
           The College Recruiting Process in 2014: What every Athlete needs to know
          </h1>
          <div class="post-meta">
           <ul>
            <li>
             <time class="entry-date updated" datetime="2014-01-23T15:00:24+00:00">
              23. Jan
             </time>
            </li>
            <li class="divider">
             /
            </li>
            <li>
             <a href="http://www.prolater.com/blog/category/college-athletics/" title="View all posts in College Athletics">
              College Athletics
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.prolater.com/blog/category/guest-posts/" title="View all posts in Guest Posts">
              Guest Posts
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.prolater.com/blog/category/recruiting/" title="View all posts in Recruiting">
              Recruiting
             </a>
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          There are an endless number of articles, columns, and posts about the dos and donts of the college recruiting process, but it can almost impossible to separate the good from the bad. Weve taken the time to read (almost) everything thats out there and have included nothing but the most essential facts in this list of what every aspiring college athlete needs to know. Read on to find out how your approach to the college recruiting process measures up.
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Follow the rules.
         </h4>
         <p>
          If you cant maintain your eligibility, even the best strategy in the world wont get you recruited. Get familiar with the
          <a href="http://web1.ncaa.org/ECWR2/NCAA_EMS/NCAA.jsp" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://web1.ncaa.org']);" target="_blank" title="NCAA Eligibility Center">
           NCAA Eligibility Center
          </a>
          and confirm that you meet the relevant requirements.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Stand out from the crowd.
         </h4>
         <p>
          For the majority of athletes, getting recruited is going to be completely up to you. If you go into the college recruiting process with the attitude that coaches will find you on their own, youre already starting off on the wrong foot. A few athletes will have the luxury of many opportunities to chose from but most, almost all, will have to be proactive and advocate on behalf of themselves.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Understand your game.
         </h4>
         <p>
          You need to accurately evaluate your own talent. Whether youre overestimating your ability and targeting schools that may be out of your range or underestimating your ability and selling yourself short, its important to have a clear idea of where your game is and where its going in order to find the right program for you. There are plenty of professionals who you can rely on to help assess your skill level  whether it be a coach, trainer, scout, or somebody you trust. We recommend finding someone who is not only a professional in his or her field, but who you also know will be honest with you.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          <span id="more-1095">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Know your options.
         </h4>
         <p>
          A full division 1 scholarship is rare, and there are tons of academic institutions to pick from. At the beginning of your recruiting process try to get your name into as many places as possible. You can limit your choices once you have a lot of options. Be knowledgeable about all levels of play and the scholarships, grants and financial packages that can be offered at these levels, from junior colleges all the way to top division 1 programs.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Be patient, but proactive.
         </h4>
         <p>
          The college recruiting process as a whole typically will take several months and possibly more than a year. It takes time to have a coach see you play, talk to him or her about their school, discuss potential financial aid, get accepted to the school, and then decide to actually enroll.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Start your college recruiting process early.
         </h4>
         <p>
          Freshman year might be too early for going on college visits and pursuing coaches, but you should be thinking about the schools you want to attend and the camps that can give you the right exposure long before you start applying.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Hit the books.
         </h4>
         <p>
          Academics are important, no matter what level or division in which you plan on playing. This should go without saying, but being on top of your game in the classroom is just as important as on the court or field.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Be mindful of what you post to social media.
         </h4>
         <p>
          Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram are all beautiful things, but we all know that whatever goes on the internet is there forever. You might be able to take down or delete posts, but if somebody got a screenshot or digs deeply enough through cyberspace, they can find whatever you posted. If you wouldnt want your grandma or your employer to see what you posted, definitely dont put it on the internet.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Get to know the school.
         </h4>
         <p>
          When you finally pick your school, make sure its one that you can see yourself at regardless of athletic standing. As a student athlete, your schedule will be filled with sports requirements, but you want to be in an environment that youll love even when youre away from the field. Websites like the
          <a href="http://www.princetonreview.com/college-rankings.aspx" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.princetonreview.com']);" target="_blank" title="The Princeton Review">
           Princeton Review
          </a>
          can be a great way to get a sense for the personality of various schools.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Play your game.
         </h4>
         <p>
          When playing at a showcase for coaches, play naturally and within yourself. This doesnt mean dont play hard, but coaches notice when you try to do too much. They want to see your work ethic and your attitude more than anything, but they also want to get the full picture of how you play. Dont try to force an all-world performance, just play your game.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h4 style="margin-top:30px;">
           Always be yourself.
         </h4>
         <p>
          Make sure your athletic resume is honest. Coaches can easily find out the truth about you and your career. Lying or overselling your game is not the way to find success.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <a href="www.prolater.com/danlev">
            Dan Lev
           </a>
           is Prolaters college recruiting analyst and a member of the Oberlin mens soccer team.
           <br/>
          </em>
         </p>
