        </h1>

         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I read dozens of business and marketing books every year, and am always on the look out for the next exceptional publication to inspire me and help in my work. Amongst all these I definitely find my favourites.
        </p>
        <p>
         In this context, I thought it would be fascinating to find out what books have most inspired some of the worlds most prominent content marketing experts. So hundreds of emails later Im very grateful to the industry insiders who took part and answered this simple question:
        </p>
        <blockquote>

         <p>
          The book Ive learned from the most is not specifically about content marketing, but the psychology of persuasion. Its called
          <strong>
           Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion
          </strong>
          by Robert B. Cialdini.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres a powerful quote from the book that, in a nutshell, reveals exactly how you should approach content marketing:
         </p>
         <p>
          One of the most potent of the weapons of influence around us is the rule for reciprocation. The rule says that we should try to repay, in kind, what another person has provided us. If a woman does us a favour, we should do her one in returnby virtue of the reciprocity rule, then, we are obliged to the future repayment of favours, gifts, invitations, and the like.
         </p>
         <p>
          In the digital marketing world, if you wantto get eyeballs to your content, you must first give eyeballs to other peoples content. If you link to peoples resources, tweet their posts out to your followers, like them on Facebook, etc, you will invoke the rule ofreciprocation.
         </p>
         <p>
          So next time you have an important article you want to get out to the world, when you reach out to those people you did a favour for, they will be more than happy to do one in return  market the hell out of your content!

           <img alt="Heidi Cohen" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-2458 alignright" height="150" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/heidicohen-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
           Heidi Cohen
          </b>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://heidicohen.com/content-marketing-definition/" target="_blank">
           HeidiCohen.com
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Heidi says:
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are my top content marketing book recommendations:
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Positioning
          </strong>
          : The Battle for Your Mind by Al Ries and Jack Trout Its an oldie but goodie.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Made To Stick
          </strong>
          by Chip &amp; Dan Heath  Its a great book for how to present ideas.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Contagious
          </strong>
          by Jonah Berger  It shows how ideas catch on based on research by a Wharton professor.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Epic Content Marketing
          </strong>
          by Joe Pulizzi  Its an action plan for content creators.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Content Rules
          </strong>
          by Ann Handley and CC Chapman  Its a must have book on content marketing.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="#top">
          Back to top of list
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Conclusion
        </h3>
        <p>
         So there we have it folks, one of the most comprehensive lists of its kind Ive seen anywhere online.
         <a href="#list">
          Click here
         </a>
         if you want to go back to the reading list.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whats been great about putting this together is that although there are clearly some very popular books out there  many of which Ive already read or heard of  theres also a whole load of books, online resources and blogs offering massive value on content marketing that I didnt have a clue about. Im really happy to have found all these, its like opening a toy box and Ill definitely be stocking up for Christmas
         <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As content marketing grows as an industry (especially in the UK), so does the number of high quality publications in the space  which you would obviously hope to see happening. However, this proliferation does also show that content marketing is becoming more accepted by mainstream business owners, marketers and online entrepreneurs  who now more than ever can see its true value.
        </p>
        <p>
         Again, thanks to everyone for taking part, I appreciate your time. If Ive missed out your own personal favourite, let me know in the comments below
