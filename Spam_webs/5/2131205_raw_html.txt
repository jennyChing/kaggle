        5 Reasons Having Big Balls Makes You A Better Dad
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Screen-Shot-2013-09-29-at-10.30.53-AM.png">
          <img alt="bigger balls dad" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-105155" height="260" src="//goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Screen-Shot-2013-09-29-at-10.30.53-AM.png" width="468"/>
         </a>
        </h2>
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <strong>
           Charlie Capen feels discriminated against by recent findings that say there is a direct relationship between testicular grandeur and fatherhood.
          </strong>
         </em>
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         
        </p>
        <p>
         Recently a
         <a href="http://healthland.time.com/2013/09/09/study-choose-dads-with-smaller-nads/#ixzz2fDOCIkrT" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://healthland.time.com/2013/09/09/study-choose-dads-with-smaller-nads/#ixzz2fDOCIkrT', 'Time magazine article');" target="_blank" title="Study: Choose Dads With Smaller Nads">
          Time magazine article
         </a>
         reported on a scientific study that men with smaller mansicles make better fathers. Testicular volume is likely to be a more stable measure than testosterone  okay? Well, I felt discriminated against and Id like to set the record straight on this pseudo-scientific buffoonery.
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are my five reasons having bigger balls makes you a better dad
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          1. Workspace
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_105149" style="width: 190px">
         <a href="http://goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/saw-table.jpg">
          <img alt="The Right Tool for the Job" class="size-full wp-image-105149" height="180" src="//goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/saw-table.jpg" width="180"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The Right Tool for the Job
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Need a workbench? Want a flat area to fix one of your kids toys? If you have big balls, you have an instant surface to fix toys like a boss or eat your lunch between shifts of running after your kids. Sure, huge balls make it harder to run after them, but writing this post on my laptop is so much easier with my huge nuts.
        </p>
        <p>
         Because blogging also makes a better dad. Obviously.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          2. Scare Tactics
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <div>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_105150" style="width: 190px">
          <a href="http://goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/two-balls.jpg">
           <img alt="Fear is Your Friend" class="size-full wp-image-105150" height="180" src="//goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/two-balls.jpg" width="180"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Fear is Your Friend
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Have you seen an angry set of rhino testicles charging at you? No? Well, if youre lucky you might see a pair of frustrated dadballs charging at you if youre screaming at my kid on the playing field. Seriously, a giant pair can really give you the advantage if another dad is an agro sports father. You can saunter up and politely tell him to call down. No time flat. Or round. Whatever.
         </p>
         <p>
          Balls are pretty scary, generally.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           3. Being Different
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_105152" style="width: 190px">
          <a href="http://goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/yellow-different.jpg">
           <img alt="Little. Hairy. Different." class=" wp-image-105152 " height="180" src="//goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/yellow-different.jpg" width="180"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Little. Hairy. Different.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Average testicle size is 1.2 inches in diameter, but you have huge balls. You know the value and stress of being different. You relate to outsiders who look or act differently.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thus, you can teach your children about the value of being different, the power of accepting others as they are, just as youve accepted that you have magnificently overgrown man marbles, and the burden of possessing heavy dude jewels.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           4. Superior Swimming
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_105153" style="width: 190px">
          <a href="http://goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/splash.jpg">
           <img alt="Dive! Dive! Dive!" class="size-full wp-image-105153" height="180" src="//goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/splash.jpg" width="180"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Dive! Dive! Dive!
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Okay, fine. Maybe you wont be the fastest swimmer, but youll have the biggest cannonball of any other dad there. And that should count for something.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           5. MacGyver Satchel
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_105154" style="width: 190px">
          <a href="http://goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Macgyver.jpg">
           <img alt="Duct Tape Won't Solve This" class="size-full wp-image-105154" height="180" src="//goodmenproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Macgyver.jpg" width="180"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Duct Tape Wont Solve This
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Listen, with great balls comes great scrotal responsibility. Sure, when its a warm out and youre wearing wool underwear, so there may be some extra dough lying around. Big testes mean a bigger bag. You can use it to carry small items for later and it isnt a fanny pack. Win/win. Its like natures little pocket.
         </p>
         <p>
          In the end, I really dont have any idea why people were compelled to measure their testicles or find this study remotely useful. We all know the best fathers write blogs about fatherhood and love geeky stuff like
          <a href="http://amzn.to/17VPuNK" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://amzn.to/17VPuNK', 'Star Wars light saber night lights');" target="_blank" title="Light Saber Night Light">
           Star Wars light saber night lights
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
