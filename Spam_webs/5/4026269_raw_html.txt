           <h2 class=" small_title " style="color:#">
            Which Jewelry Trend Suits Your Personality?
           </h2>
           <h3>
           </h3>
          </div>
          <div class="contentWrapper">
           <p>
            Maybe you prefer big pav clusters that are priced upon request, a la
            <a href="http://www.elizabethstreet.com/style/birthday-elizabeth-taylor">
             Elizabeth Taylor
            </a>
            . Or perhaps you're more of an
            <a href="http://www.elizabethstreet.com/style/best-statement-necklaces">
             Audrey Hepburn
            </a>
            type, with a penchant for studs and a strand of pearls. Whatever you're drawn to,
            <a href="http://www.elizabethstreet.com/tags/jewelry">
             jewelry
            </a>
            should always be thought of as a personal statement, an accessory that shows a glimpse of your character. When shoes are meant to cover your feet and coats are meant to keep you warm, earrings and bracelets are the farthest things from utilitarian, performing no function aside from adding embellishment to your ensemble, which is why it is the most telling item you can wear.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            There are three things extremely hard: steel, a diamond, and to know one's self, said
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poor_Richard%27s_Almanack">
             Benjamin Franklin
            </a>
            . He may not have been a great admirer of gemstones as Taylor and Hepburn, but Franklins philosophy perfectly describes how one should shop for jewelry: They should have a dash of brilliance (the diamond), a durable setting (the steal) and impart some aspect of your inner psyche.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em style="font-size: 13px;">
             Click through the slideshow to shop the jewelry trend you most identify with.
            </em>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <em>
             By Barry Samaha
            </em>
           </p>
