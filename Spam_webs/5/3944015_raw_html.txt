     <h1>
      28 incredibly useful packing hacks
     </h1>
     <div class="post-inner clearfix">
      <div class="post-meta">
       <div class="post-meta-author">
        by
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/author/matthershberger/">
         Matt Hershberger
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="post-meta-date">
        April 18, 2014
       </div>
       <div class="post-avatar">
        <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-100" height="100" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/authors/matthershberger.jpg" width="100"/>
       </div>
       <div class="post-share-wrapper post-share--vertical" data-post-id="244393" data-sticky="" data-sticky-max-width="991" data-sticky-offset-top="69" data-sticky-parent-contain="" data-sticky-parent-selector=".page-section">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        <span style="text-transform:uppercase;">
         Packing for travel
        </span>
        is an art, and its a minimalist art. Experienced travelers will have, over time, developed either a system for packing lightly and efficiently, or insanely strong back, arm, and neck muscles. But if you dont want to look like Tom Hardy in
        <em>
         The Dark Knight Rises
        </em>
        , then there are a bunch of other little tricks  other than just cutting back on clothing or shoes you arent going to need  that can help make you a more space-efficient traveler.
       </p>
       <p>
        Lately, many budget airlines, like EasyJet or Ryanair, are charging pretty outrageous amounts for bags that exceed a certain size. Most luggage stores will know what their luggage requirements are, and will sell you a decent bag within their acceptable range, but its still not a ton of space. Heres how you can get a full weeks worth of clothes into that bag:
       </p>
       <h5>
        Vacuum seal
       </h5>
       <div class="captionright">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/gCE9TD6mJWc" width="560">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <p>
        As a courtesy to those of you out there who just frantically Googled packing tips the night before leaving for your trip and found this page, well start with the vacuum seal, because its the last resort  if you cant arrange and jam your stuff into your luggage otherwise, vacuum sealing is the way to go. There are a lot of products for sale that can help you vacuum pack your bags. Vacuum packing is never a bad idea for packing tightly, because a good chunk of the space in your bag is going to be empty air. First, you can get the specialized bag and pump.
       </p>
       <p>
        It works for sure, but then youre carrying the pump everywhere you go. This is solved if you get the space bags, which basically allow you to put your luggage in the bags and then roll the air out of them manually.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.dailymotion.com/video/xdgby7_travel-space-bags-no-vacuum-needed_school">
         Heres another
        </a>
        , not altogether horrible option, but you need to spend on the bags themselves. A third option  probably the best for the budget traveler  is the DIY vacuum seal. There are a couple of good ways to do this:
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         The dip-it and zip-it
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_244500" style="width: 610px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/vacuum-sealer2.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="vacuum sealer2" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/vacuum-sealer2-600x450.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-medium wp-image-244500 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/vacuum-sealer2-600x450.jpg" height="450" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="600"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://www.seriouseats.com/2010/04/cook-your-meat-in-a-beer-cooler-the-worlds-best-sous-vide-hack.html/">
          via
         </a>
         ), (
         <a href="http://makezine.com/2011/05/18/how-to-vacuum-seal-cheap-ziploc-bags/">
          via
         </a>
         ), (
         <a href="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/1/14/Make-Your-Own-Vacuum-Sealed-Storage-Bags-Step-3.jpg/670px-Make-Your-Own-Vacuum-Sealed-Storage-Bags-Step-3.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        First, fold and put your item into a regular, sealable plastic bag. Then dip it in a sink or tub full of water. This forces the air out, and you can seal it yourself. This is a technique usually used for food, and you might be constrained when it comes to larger items.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you dont want to risk the possibility of getting all of your clothes wet, try this instead:
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         The ghetto vacuum pack
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Put your item in a Ziploc bag, leaving it slightly open. Then, put that bag in a larger Ziploc bag, and insert a regular vacuum hose, sealing the larger bag around it as much as possible. Turn the vacuum on, and itll suck the air out of both bags. Close the smaller bag manually (without taking it out or turning the vacuum off), and voila!
       </p>
       <p>
        The issue with vacuum packing is that its going to either be time consuming, expensive, equipment-dependent, or its going to get your shit all wet. So here are some other tricks to conserve space.
       </p>
       <h5>
        Packing efficiently
       </h5>
       <p>
        There are a lot of schools of thought about
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/notebook/1-trick-that-will-change-the-way-you-pack-forever/">
         how best to pack a bag
        </a>
        . Im going to leave that up to you  I dont think theres a definitive way. I tend to be in the make space at all costs school, but some people are more concerned with neatness preventing wrinkles, and theres an entirely different set of rules for that. That said, here are some space savers:
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Roll em up.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_244406" style="width: 910px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/roll-em-up.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="roll em up" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/roll-em-up-940x701.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-large wp-image-244406 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/roll-em-up-940x701.jpg" height="701" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/-R0JbfJriYFo/UygKT_MliwI/AAAAAAABGno/Xz1F_ByRzN0/w2048-h1529/Hu6Jz.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Sure, it can wrinkle the clothing, but veteran packers know that rolling all of your clothing up makes it way more space efficient than laying it in flat. An additional tip is to wrap the clothing in rubber bands to make sure its not loose, and to keep some semblance of organization in a bag full of rolled-up clothes.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Wear what you cant fit.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244413" style="width: 648px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/wear-what-you-cant-fit.png">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="wear what you can't fit" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/wear-what-you-cant-fit.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-full wp-image-244413 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/wear-what-you-cant-fit.png" height="471" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="638"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://blog.cybex-online.com/blog/safety/cold-season-tips-be-careful-when-wearing-thick-and-heavy-winter-clothes-in-the-car-seat/">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Travel with as much of the bulky clothes on as you can. Even if you prefer light clothes on planes, remember that you arent limited in how much clothes you can be wearing on a plane, and you can take off a couple of layers and put them in the overhead compartment or under the seat if you have to. All its doing in your luggage is taking up space.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Use all of the space. Even in your shoes.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244487" style="width: 910px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/socks-in-boots.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="socks in boots" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/socks-in-boots-940x626.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-large wp-image-244487 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/socks-in-boots-940x626.jpg" height="626" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://www.organizationquest.com/.a/6a00e553e72cae883401156f424dd3970b-pi">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        If you must pack an extra pair of shoes, remember that there is space inside of them for socks and underwear.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Shaving cream is not necessary.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244421" style="width: 310px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/conditioner-shaving-cream_300.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="conditioner-shaving-cream_300" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/conditioner-shaving-cream_300.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-full wp-image-244421 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/conditioner-shaving-cream_300.jpg" height="357" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://roadventureworld.blogspot.com/2013/07/travel-hacks-you-need-to-know.html">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        If you dont want to bring a bulky thing of shaving cream, you can use soap, conditioner, or sunscreen as shaving cream instead.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>

       <p>
        When youre trying to figure out what to take out and what to put in, remember this: Unless youre on a business trip, you dont need more than one nice set of clothes. But you will absolutely need a lot of underwear. Seriously, guys  trust me on this.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Light clothes go on top.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244426" style="width: 910px">

         </noscript>
         <img class="size-large wp-image-244426 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Packing-a-Business-Trip-Suitcase-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">

         <a href="http://lifehacker.com/5788501/how-to-pack-your-suitcase-as-efficiently-as-a-flight-attendant">

         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Flight attendant
        <a href="http://lifehacker.com/5788501/how-to-pack-your-suitcase-as-efficiently-as-a-flight-attendant">
         Heather Poole suggests
        </a>
        putting your heavy clothes and shoes in first, and then moving up towards the lighter clothes. That way, when you have to close the suitcase, you arent trying to push down on the bulkiest items.
       </p>
       <h5>
        Finding good containers
       </h5>
       <p>
        If you dont want to buy handfuls of travel-size items when you already own the stuff you need, just in too large of a container, you can use regular household items to fit stuff in.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Everything fits in medicine bottles.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244443" style="width: 910px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/q-tips-storage.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="q tips storage" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/q-tips-storage-940x940.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-large wp-image-244443 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/q-tips-storage-940x940.jpg" height="900" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-2ZjZ8v4iP-U/UHxECHJUf_I/AAAAAAAAGMA/e9SM5TJZAqc/s1600/PicMonkeyd+Collage.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Q-tips and bobby pins will fit nicely into medicine bottles, as will jewelry. I would suggest finding a more opaque bottle for jewelry, or possibly lining it with paper if you dont want it to get stolen. Another obvious use is just put in more pills  possibly multivitamins. But make sure you can explain that you dont need a prescription for the multivitamins.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Youll find shampoo. You wont find moisturizer.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244445" style="width: 650px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/moisturizer-shampoo.jpg">

         </noscript>
         <img class="size-full wp-image-244445 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/moisturizer-shampoo.jpg" height="640" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="640"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LJbNyQMLZp8/UHxEAPiSvDI/AAAAAAAAGLw/dU5psEThNNc/s640/PicModnkey+Collage.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Though I can usually find shampoo or conditioner to use while traveling, unless Im staying in a nice hotel, I always have a hard time finding moisturizer.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Make your own dry toothpaste.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244446" style="width: 610px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/toothpaste-dots-hacks.png">

        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Toothpaste really isnt a worry when youre traveling  you can find it anywhere. But if you really want to give yourself that little extra space, the site
        <a href="http://herpackinglist.com/">
         Her Packing List
        </a>
        suggests making little non-liquid toothpaste capsules. Simple enough: Squeeze a drop of non-gel toothpaste onto a sheet of foil, sprinkle it with baking soda, and then wait a few days until its dry and pop it into a bag. When youre using it, just pop it in your mouth, rub it against your teeth with your tongue, and then brush.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>

        </strong>
       </p>

       </p>
       <h5>
        Keeping things clean and orderly
       </h5>
       <p>
        One of the problems with packing so tightly is that if something smells, then in a very short while, everything will smell. Another problem, with all of the jostling, is that you might squeeze that toothpaste right out of its tube and all over your clothes and toiletries. Here are some tricks to help prevent that:
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Take no chances with fluids.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_244495" style="width: 610px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/packinghacks1.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="packinghacks1" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/packinghacks1-600x450.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-medium wp-image-244495 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/packinghacks1-600x450.jpg" height="450" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="600"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://media-cache-ec0.pinimg.com/236x/a7/ef/7f/a7ef7f46a0ef37fff5f265728e917630.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Before leaving, unscrew your bottles with liquids or gels inside them, cover them in cling wrap, and then screw the cap back on. Not only does this help the lids stay on tighter, but it provides another layer of defense between the gel and all of your clothes.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Jewelry tangles. Straws dont.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        To keep jewelry from tangling, try running it through a straw.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Keep your collar straight.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        If you want to keep a shirt collar neat and not smashed, you can wrap your belt around the inside. It also creates a nice spot for your belt.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Keep your earrings from falling into the deepest crevice of your bag.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Earrings can be held together using buttons.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Protect your clothes from your shoes.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244453" style="width: 650px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/protect-clothes-from-shoes.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="protect clothes from shoes" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/protect-clothes-from-shoes.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-full wp-image-244453 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/protect-clothes-from-shoes.jpg" height="360" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="640"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://lifehacker.com/5874035/pack-shoes-in-hotel-shower-caps-to-keep-your-luggage-contents-clean">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Stinky or dirty shoes? Put them in a shower cap. Its not a perfect seal, but itll keep them together and will be at least a slight barrier between them and the clothes.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Protect your clothes from wrinkling.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244454" style="width: 660px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/protect-clothes-from-wrinkling.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="protect clothes from wrinkling" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/protect-clothes-from-wrinkling.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-full wp-image-244454 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/protect-clothes-from-wrinkling.jpg" height="433" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="650"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://www.wendyslookbook.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/Packing-9-650x433.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        To keep clothes from wrinkling, roll it or fold it over tissue paper.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Protect your clothes from travel stink.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244455" style="width: 710px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/dryer-sheets-in-luggage.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="dryer sheets in luggage" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/dryer-sheets-in-luggage.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-full wp-image-244455 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/dryer-sheets-in-luggage.jpg" height="525" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="700"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Bounce-Back-3.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        You can avoid a bad-smelling suitcase by throwing in a couple of dryer sheets.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Keep your headphones untangled.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244456" style="width: 580px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/headphones-paper-clip-hack.gif">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="headphones paper clip hack" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/headphones-paper-clip-hack.gif" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-full wp-image-244456 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/headphones-paper-clip-hack.gif" height="321" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="570"/>

       <p>
        Headphone wires wrap nicely around paperclips / binder clips.
       </p>

       </p>

       <h5>
        Space-saving gadgets
       </h5>
       <p>
        For the most part, Im not an advocate of getting travel gadgets. They take up extra space, are wasteful, and dont really help all that much. But there are a few gadgets that are spectacularly useful in terms of space conservation when it comes to travel.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Get an e-reader.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244461" style="width: 910px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/e-reader-travel-hack.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="e-reader travel hack" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/e-reader-travel-hack-940x670.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-large wp-image-244461 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/e-reader-travel-hack-940x670.jpg" height="670" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://i1-news.softpedia-static.com/images/news2/Sony-Gives-Up-on-eReader-Business-in-the-US-Kobo-Takes-Over-424742-2.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        The e-reader is essential for people who like to read when they travel. Ive had a Kindle for about 4 years now, and its my favorite possession on the road. The newer versions fit into most jacket or cargo pockets, and they can replace hundreds of books, which used to be the primary source of excess luggage weight when I traveled. If you go the tablet route, youre further combining your computer and music into the same device.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Use one plug to charge all devices.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244463" style="width: 510px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/universal-power-plugs-cords.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="universal power plugs cords" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/universal-power-plugs-cords.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="size-full wp-image-244463 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/universal-power-plugs-cords.jpg" height="350" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="500"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         (
         <a href="http://dandygadget.com/simplify-your-laptop-and-mobile-device-charging-by-a-new-max-series-from-igo-igo-everywheremax/">
          via
         </a>
         )
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        There are a number of universal travel adapters out there, and they tend to be on the pricier side  usually at least over $100  but if you spend enough time traveling, and if you travel with a lot of electronics, its worth getting. Its a single adapter for your computer, your tablet, your phone, your e-reader, your electric shaver, and so on.
       </p>
       <h5>
        A final bonus cheat
       </h5>
       <p>
        Okay, this is a little shitty, but you can game the system, and it will keep your luggage from being harmed and will also get you out of baggage claim faster.
       </p>

         Label it Fragile.
        </strong>
       </p>

          <p>
           If you are talking about packing hand luggage (carry-on bags), then be aware that in Europe you cannot take liquids, gels, creams etc in containers of more than 100ml (so no wine bottles!) and you have to have them in a separate plastic ziplock bag to show at security. You can buy small empty containers and small tubes and tubs of toothpaste, shampoo, handcreams etc in many pharmacies and other shops.
           <br/>
           Airlines also advise that you dont put jewelry or valuables in your hold luggage so that should always be in your carry-on bag anyway.
           <br/>
           And for ladies (and some gentlemen)  your handbag (purse) has to fit into your hand luggage as well, so if you dont want to be separated from your essentials, use one of those bags that you tie round your waist or hang on a belt  but it needs to be a fairly small one.
           <br/>
           And always wear onto the plane clothes with lots of pockets so you can have your puzzle books, pens, pack of sweets, ebook etc with you and not have to disturb anyone by getting up and searching for them in your bag in the overhead lockers.
          </p>

          <p>
           A simple solution for evacuating air from ziplocks or vacuum bags is to fill them, seal them (except for the air-opening), then lay on them to squish the air out.  If youre exceptionally thin or otherwise not soft, put a pillow between you and the bag.  When its squished as much as you can, seal it.
           <br/>
           Take half as much underwear as you expect to need, and do hand-wash.  Take more socks that you plan to need  they dont drip dry quickly.  LUSH sells solid shampoo bars that are great because theyre not liquid and wont leak.  They also sell solid moisturizer bars.  Pack baby wipes and facial wipes in ziplocks for the plane.  They are great for a quick freshen-up.
           <br/>
           I pack my carry-on (underseat) bag with crackers, cheese, carrots and celery.  The vegetables make a great supplement to the airplane food as well as freshening your mouth.  Cheese and crackers make a good protein source.
           <br/>
           I also pack a small down pillow, in a ziplock with the air squished out, and several pashmina shawls.  The shawls make excellent blankets, pillows, head coverings (important for visiting churches in conservative places), and skirts in case of wardrobe malfunction.
