        <h1 class="entry-title">
         America the Offended: Im Offended That Youre Offended By Me Being Offended
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         By
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://shenkitup.com/author/about-shenkitup/" rel="author" title="Nate Shenk">
            Nate Shenk
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="date published time" title="2014-01-19T22:22:20+00:00">
          January 19, 2014
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://shenkitup.com/2014/01/19/america-the-offended-im-offended-that-youre-offended-by-me-being-offended/#comments">
           14 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="categories">
          Filed Under:
          <a href="http://shenkitup.com/category/humor/" rel="category tag">
           Humor
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           <a href="http://shenkitup.com/wp-content/uploads/paula-deen-offended-america.jpg" rel="nofollow">
            <img alt="paula deen offended america" height="262" src="http://shenkitup.com/wp-content/uploads/paula-deen-offended-america.jpg" style="border: 0pt none; float: right; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" title="" width="353"/>
           </a>
           For nearly a hundred years, America has been known as the land of the free and the home of the brave, thanks to Francis Scott Keys The Star-Spangled Banner, which became our national anthem in 1931.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           However, in more recent years, America has become not only the land of the free, but also the land of the perpetually offended.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           Please dont say Merry Christmas  its offensive. Please dont state your opinion on gay marriage, or other social &amp; political issues  its offensive. And religion? Oh hell no  dont even think about it, because, you guessed it  its offensive. In Europe, they actually make fun of us for causing such a big scene over silly issues, such as displaying Christmas trees on public property.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           A few days ago, I came across an article about how
           <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/femail/article-2303960/Target-dress-apology-Website-forced-remove-Manatee-Gray-plus-size-dress-backlash.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             Target publically apologized
            </strong>
           </a>
           for labeling a dress in their plus-size section as Manatee Gray. Apparently, a large group of people were offended by Targets blatant comparison of plus-size women to manatees, which, just FYI, is also known as the sea cow. 
           <em>
            Moooooo
           </em>
           . Just kidding, thats offensive.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           Forget the fact that the dress itself was a similar (aka identical) color to that of a manatee, hence the term manatee gray, no, Target was clearly trying to offend all its plus-size customers who were in the market for a gray maxi dress. Shame on you, Target, your actions are inexcusablenow apologize to the manatees/sea cows.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           The examples of when and how Americans have been getting offended in recent years could fill a book (
           <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/oprah-winfrey-breaks-silence-paula-deen-controversy-article-1.1419098" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             Paula Deen
            </strong>
           </a>
           ,
           <span class="st">
            Juan Pablo Galavis, etc.)
           </span>
           and I dont know about you, but Im getting tired of it. Theres a fine line between being reasonably politically correct and absurditybut dont tell the manatees that  theyre still upset.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           Lets examine the situation that unfolded last month when Phil Robertson, from A&amp;Es Duck Dynasty, was quoted in a GQ interview stating his negative views on homosexuality. The entire country was in an uproar; some were mortified over the things he said, while others applauded him.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           Im not going to get into what I thought about his comments, but the thing that really got me was the fact that people seemed to be actually surprised by his response. I mean, sure, I can understand why it made people upset, but surprised? Really? What did you honestly think a 67-year-old, openly devout Christian man from the woods of Louisiana would say when asked about homosexuality? Being gay is perfectly fine. In fact, I wish I was gay! #TeamGay yaynot a chance.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           Since when did
           <a href="http://shenkitup.com/category/american/" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             America
            </strong>
           </a>
           become a place where you cant be open about your beliefs? Whether or not those beliefs are considered as socially acceptable by the masses shouldnt be an issue. I mean, isnt that the point of living in a democratic country? No matter how badly you might want the United States to be a politically correct utopia, its never going to happen.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           Achieving a perfect state of political correctness is an absurd ideology that the mainstream media uses as a bedtime story for those who cannot grasp the concept of someone not agreeing with them.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           There will always be people who celebrate Christmas and use the term Merry Christmas. There will always be those who hate, or disapprove of gay people. There will always be people who dont vote for the same political party as you do and there will always be people who go to a different church than you (or not at all, for that matter).
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           The only thing youre achieving when
           <a href="http://shenkitup.com/2012/12/09/offended-american-nativity-scenes-are-offensive/" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             you get offended
            </strong>
           </a>
           by the slightest things (i.e. Target comparing you to an aquatic mammal, seeing a Christmas tree at the city park, etc.) is elevated blood pressure and/or a bad case of heartburn. My advice to you is quite simpleget over it. If you think you look comparable to a sea cow, do something about it  perhaps changing your diet, or exercising more would suffice.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           Instead of exploding from ridiculous levels of being offended, look at the situation and change how you feel about it. Youll never convince everyone in the United States that they should be Christian, nor will you convince everyone that being gay is acceptable.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: helvetica;">
           What you should be doing (if you find yourself getting offended by a number of things in your environment) is surrounding yourself with people who love and care about you. Concentrate
           <a href="http://shenkitup.com/2011/12/06/if-at-first-you-dont-succeed-try-a-little-less-a/" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             on the positive
            </strong>
           </a>
           and focus your energy away from trying to make the world a politically correct place because thats just not going to happen.
          </span>
         </p>

          <span style="font-family: helvetica;">
           America the Offended: Its Time We Got Over It
          </span>
