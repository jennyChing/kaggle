           <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
            Chocolate Orange Fruit Bark--From BHG
           </h3>
           <div class="post-header">
            <div class="post-header-line-1">
            </div>
            <div class="date-outer">
             <h2 class="date-header">
              <span>
               19 December 2014
              </span>
             </h2>
             <div class="date-posts">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8573847117220947765" itemprop="articleBody">
              <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
               <tbody>
                <tr>
                 <td style="text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-PBS9OYTDOoA/VIJ3xEGFAMI/AAAAAAAAO4g/JNnR6M8Uof8/s1600/chocolate-orange-fruit-bark-43-o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                   <img alt="Chocolate Orange Fruit Bark | bakeatmidnite.com | #bhg #bark #christmas #candy #chocolate" border="0" height="478" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-PBS9OYTDOoA/VIJ3xEGFAMI/AAAAAAAAO4g/JNnR6M8Uof8/s1600/chocolate-orange-fruit-bark-43-o.jpg" title="Chocolate Orange Fruit Bark | bakeatmidnite.com | #bhg #bark #christmas #candy #chocolate" width="640"/>
                  </a>
                 </td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                 <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                  Chocolate Orange Fruit Bark
                 </td>
                </tr>
               </tbody>
              </table>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               A fruit-filled chocolate bark with a hint of orange. Perfect candy for the Christmas holidays or any time!
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <a name="more">
              </a>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               I'm having a really nice walk down memory lane with the new Better Homes &amp; Gardens cookbook I was sent to review.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
               <tbody>
                <tr>
                 <td style="text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-idbHzXJns8o/VHvkIMSbSEI/AAAAAAAAOyo/BFXdCHqmAi4/s1600/BHG-New-Cookbook.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                   <img alt="Better Homes and Gardens New Cookbook #bhg #cookbooks" border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-idbHzXJns8o/VHvkIMSbSEI/AAAAAAAAOyo/BFXdCHqmAi4/s1600/BHG-New-Cookbook.jpg" title="Better Homes and Gardens New Cookbook #bhg #cookbooks" width="426"/>
                  </a>
                 </td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                 <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                  16th Edition of Better Homes &amp; Gardens Cookbook
                 </td>
                </tr>
               </tbody>
              </table>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               Years ago, when I first got married, I made my first candy recipe,
               <a href="http://www.bakeatmidnite.com/2014/12/english-toffee.html" target="_blank">
                English Toffee
               </a>
               , out of that book. It got me hooked on candy making!
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               Barks were not in the old book--guess because they were not popular 30-odd years ago, but they are included in the newest edition. There's a basic recipe and many variations on the side of the page. Here's a couple of variations~~
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
               <tbody>
                <tr>
                 <td style="text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-5BUVkBVuPt8/VIkLQ-hMMUI/AAAAAAAAPAI/EE3vVJIWDIc/s1600/double-layer-bark-o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                   <img alt="Peppermint Bark | bakeatmidnite.com | #candy #bark #christmas" border="0" height="400" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-5BUVkBVuPt8/VIkLQ-hMMUI/AAAAAAAAPAI/EE3vVJIWDIc/s1600/double-layer-bark-o.jpg" title="Peppermint Bark | bakeatmidnite.com | #candy #bark #christmas" width="400"/>
                  </a>
                 </td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                 <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                  Double Decker-melt dark as in original recipe; add 2 drops oil of peppermint; chill, then melt white, spread over and top with crushed candy canes.
                 </td>
                </tr>
               </tbody>
              </table>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
               <tbody>
                <tr>
                 <td style="text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2qYFThpeZB8/VIkLiWz22hI/AAAAAAAAPAQ/tHNwa3fV58A/s1600/marble-bark-o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                   <img alt="Peppermint Bark | bakeatmidnite.com | #candy #bark #christmas" border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2qYFThpeZB8/VIkLiWz22hI/AAAAAAAAPAQ/tHNwa3fV58A/s1600/marble-bark-o.jpg" title="Peppermint Bark | bakeatmidnite.com | #candy #bark #christmas" width="400"/>
                  </a>
                 </td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                 <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                  Marble--melt chocolates as in original recipe and add 2 drops oil of peppermint to both colors. Drop one color randomly on prepared sheet, then drop other color in the spaces and swirl lightly with a knife. Top with crushed candy canes.
                 </td>
                </tr>
               </tbody>
              </table>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               And this is the one I used of these variations to make this. It's the "dried fruit" bark and I chose to use dried cherries. Absolutely fantastic!
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
               <tbody>
                <tr>
                 <td style="text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-WrqTiw4ND1c/VIJ5gn2GnkI/AAAAAAAAO4s/7PNl2xf6ULw/s1600/chocolate-orange-fruit-bark-1-pin.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                   <img alt="Chocolate Orange Fruit Bark | bakeatmidnite.com | #bhg #bark #christmas #candy #chocolate" border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-WrqTiw4ND1c/VIJ5gn2GnkI/AAAAAAAAO4s/7PNl2xf6ULw/s1600/chocolate-orange-fruit-bark-1-pin.jpg" title="Chocolate Orange Fruit Bark | bakeatmidnite.com | #bhg #bark #christmas #candy #chocolate" width="426"/>
                  </a>
                 </td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                 <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                  Smooth as silk texture
                 </td>
                </tr>
               </tbody>
              </table>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               And I learned something new from this book; the reason my other attempt at making bark was a minor fail. You MUST use confectioner's coating--at least 50%--to get that smooth, silky appearance and texture. Confectioner's coating is also known as candy melts (that's a Wilton trade name), almond bark, candy coating or summer coating.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               I decided to drizzle some melted vanilla candy coating on top of my bark just for a dramatic look, but it's totally not necessary. I just melted that according to package directions and used about 4 oz of the chips.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               <b>
                <i>
                 <u>
                  <br/>
                 </u>
                </i>
               </b>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: large;">
               <b>
                <i>
                 <u>
                  Chocolate Orange Fruit Bark
                 </u>
                </i>
               </b>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <ul class="ingredients">
               <li>
                <i style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                 <span style="font-size: large;">
                  6 oz semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
                 </span>
                </i>
               </li>
               <li>
                <i style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                 <span style="font-size: large;">
                  6 oz chocolate -or- vanilla-flavor candy coating, chopped
                 </span>
                </i>
               </li>
               <li>
                <i style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                 <span style="font-size: large;">
                  1 tbs shortening
                 </span>
                </i>
               </li>
               <li>
                <i style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                 <span style="font-size: large;">
                  1-2 tsp finely shredded orange or lemon peel (optional)
                 </span>
                </i>
               </li>
               <li>
                <i style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                 <span style="font-size: large;">
                  1 1/2 cups dried fruit (raisins, cranberries, cherries, etc)
                 </span>
                </i>
               </li>
              </ul>
              <span style="font-size: large;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <span style="font-size: large;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                <i>
                 <br/>
                </i>
               </span>
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                <i>
                 Line a large baking sheet with heavy foil; grease foil and set aside.
                </i>
               </span>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-size: large;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                <i>
                 <br/>
                </i>
               </span>
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                <i>
                 In a large microwave-safe bowl combine candy coating, chocolate, and shortening. Microwave, uncovered on 100% power (high) for 1 1/2 to 2 minutes or until chocolate melts, stirring every 30 seconds. If desired, add citrus peel; mix in fruit.
                </i>
               </span>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-size: large;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                <i>
                 <br/>
                </i>
               </span>
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                <i>
                 Pour mixture onto the baking sheet. Spread about 1/4 inch thick.
                </i>
               </span>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-size: large;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                <i>
                 <br/>
                </i>
               </span>
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                <i>
                 Chill candy about 30 minutes or until firm. Use foil to lift candy; carefully break into pieces. Store between sheets of waxed paper in an airtight container. Refrigerate for up to 2 weeks.
                </i>
               </span>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-size: large;">
               <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                <i>
                 <br/>
                </i>
               </span>
              </span>
              <i style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
               <span style="background-color: white; color: #222222; line-height: 1.4em;">
