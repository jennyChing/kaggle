        <h1 class="entry-title single-title" itemprop="headline">
         Spring Break! How to Help Keep Your Home Safe While Youre Gone
        </h1>
        <p class="byline vcard">
         Posted
         <time class="updated" datetime="2015-03-13" pubdate="">
          March 13, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="author">
          <a href="http://www.protectyourhome.com/blog/author/alrmadmin/" rel="author" title="Posts by Admin">
           Admin
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="amp">
          &amp;
         </span>
         filed under
         <a href="http://www.protectyourhome.com/blog/category/home-safety/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Home Safety">
          Home Safety
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </header>
       <!-- end article header -->
       <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
        <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article">
         <span itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           <a href="http://alarmsecuritynow.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/home.png">
            <img alt="home" class="wp-image-2048 alignleft" height="262" src="http://alarmsecuritynow.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/home.png" width="349"/>
           </a>
           Spring break is one of the best times to go on vacation and get away from it all. While enjoying a quick getaway is relaxing, you need to make sure that your house and everything in it are safe. There are many things you can do to help ensure that your home and all of your possessions are safe while youre gone. Here are ten tips on helping to keep your home safe while youre off
           <a href="http://www.travelchannel.com/interests/spring/articles/top-10-family-spring-break-vacations">
            making the most of spring break
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <h2>
           1. Get a House and Pet-Sitter
          </h2>
          <p>
           Having a house sitter is probably the best way to help ensure that your home is safe while youre gone. If you find someone you can trust, ask them to spend the duration of your trip in your home. Set some guidelines and provide careful directions for any plant or home care. Make it worth their while  give them access to your fridge or pay them  and they will likely do an even better job of taking care of your home than expected.
          </p>
          <p>
           If you have pets, a house sitter is an especially sound idea. Even if the sitter cant spend all their time in your home, having someone come by to check on your pet daily may save you some money and provide peace of mind. Boarding can be very expensive for long periods of time and your pet will be less unsettled if someone is in the house with them.
          </p>
          <h2>
           2. Have Someone Come Get Your Mail and Newspaper
          </h2>
          <p>
           A sure sign of someone gone on vacation is the buildup of mail in the mailbox. If a thief sees that someones mailbox is full, they know that person isnt home. This is another reason to hire a house sitter. The sitter can bring in the mail and newspapers as they arrive.
          </p>
          <h2>
           3. Program Your Lights
          </h2>
          <p>
           Before you leave, buy some timers for the lights in your home. A house that looks lived in will be less likely to be broken into.
           <a href="https://www.homedefender.com/how-it-works/optional-features">
            Many modern timers for light switches can be controlled from your smartphone
           </a>
           , giving you control wherever you are.
          </p>
          <h2>
           4. Keep the Grass Trimmed
          </h2>
          <p>
           If you would usually cut the grass during the time youre going to be gone, then you need to have someone stop by and trim the grass. A neighbor or friend will help if you dont already pay a lawn service. If you do pay a lawn service, make sure they plan to come by during the time youll be gone.
          </p>
          <h2>
           5. Lock Windows and Doors
          </h2>
          <p>
           Locking your doors and windows seems obvious, but many people forget these simple steps. Windows are especially easy to forget. No matter where they are on the house (first or second floor) make sure the windows are shut and locked.
          </p>
          <h2>
           6. Keep Specifics Off Social Media
          </h2>
          <p>
           Its fun to post things about your trip on social media in real time, but its a smart idea to
           <a href="https://cdt.org/blog/over-sharing-and-location-awareness/">
            keep the pictures and other vacation events quiet
           </a>
           until you return. Broadcasting your schedule out to the world will tell the thieves when and where youll be while theyre breaking in.
          </p>
          <h2>
           7. Home Security System
          </h2>
          <p>
           Installing a home security system can solve many problems. If you have a monitored security system, you have someone keeping an eye on your home. While this is a big plus, it doesnt mean that youre going to be safe from all threats. Even if you have a reliable, monitored security system, you need to rely on other measures to stay safe.
          </p>
          <h2>
           8. Let the Police Know
          </h2>
          <p>
           Although this may seem like overkill, notifying the police of your absence isnt necessarily a bad idea. They may be able to send a patrol car by your house on a regular basis.
          </p>
          <h2>
           9. Remove Your Spare Key
          </h2>
          <p>
           Many people hide a house key outside in case they lose their own key or lock themselves out. That key wont help you while youre away, so bring your outdoor spare key indoors and put it away until you return home.
          </p>
          <h2>
           10. Notify Your Landlord
          </h2>
          <p>
           If you rent your home, let your landlord know that youre going to be gone for an extended period of time. The landlord may be able to drive by every so often and make sure that the house is still safe.
          </p>
          <p>
           These are some of the things to do before you leave for your spring break vacation. If you prepare ahead of time, you should be all set to go away and enjoy a much-needed holiday.
          </p>
