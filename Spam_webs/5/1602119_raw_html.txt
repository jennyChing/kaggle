                     <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop/chicklitdesigns" target="_blank">
                      Chick-Lit Design's Etsy shop
                     </a>
                     . In addition to $165 clutches made out of vintage editions of
                     <em>
                      Catcher in the Rye
                     </em>
                     and
                     <em>
                      Pride and Prejudice
                     </em>
                     (some of which have a pocket big enough for your Kindle), the Gilbert, Ariz.--based designer also makes a range of e-reader and iPad covers in the same style.

An average pair is 5 5/8" from temple-to-temple, but smaller and larger sizes are available by request. Glasses, which come in multiple patterns, cost between $72 and $85 per pair.

While studying fine art in Florence in 2000, she fell in love with American fashion and began making cloche hats. She now has her own shop in Hudson, N.Y. and sells her hand-draped creations in her Etsy shop.

Rings start at $17; the most expensive item in the shop is a $720 fiber and metal necklace. She'll also work with you to make a custom item.

                    </p>

                   </div>
                  </div>

                     <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/90137080/taco-barrettes" target="_blank">
                      taco-adorned barettes
                     </a>
                     and
                     <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/89584280/hot-fudge-sundae-ring" target="_blank">
                      a ring
                     </a>
                     in the form of a hot fudge sundae.
                    </p>

                   </div>

                     <em>
                      Sex and the City
                     </em>
                     movie. She also sells beautiful
                     <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop/emmakisstina?section_id=6424428" target="_blank">

                     </a>
                     for $148.

                   </div>
