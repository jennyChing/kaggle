&lt;div id="WNStoryBody" class=" withRelated right"&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;News9.com&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;WOODWARD, Oklahoma -- The Woodward Fire Department was called out at 7:24 Tuesday morning to rescue an 8-year-old boy whose tongue was frozen to a pole.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Firefighter Shane Pingry said firefighters arrived on the scene at 7:27 a.m. to find the boy standing on his tiptoes with his tongue frozen to the pole of a stop sign across the street from the Woodward Middle School.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;A teacher saved the day with a bottle of drinking water. Firefighters poured it on the boy&#39;s tongue and it took about a minute before the boy was free.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Pingry estimated the boy had been stuck to the pole for about 10 minutes. He was taken to the hospital for treatment.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;When asked why he&#39;d stuck his tongue to the pole in such cold weather, the boy said it was because his older brother had dared him to do it.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The boy&#39;s name was not released.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Tuesday evening, the Ft. Supply Fire Department said they were dispatched to a call of another child whose tongue was frozen to a pole. The child was able to free his tongue before crews arrived.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;!--END WNStoryBody--&gt;
