        <p>
         In the early 1900s a single woman was considered a spinster at age 25. But modern technology like telephones, televisions and automobiles helped to change all that. Now fast forward to the turn of the
         <em>
          new
         </em>
         century. Oh, how things have changed. People are now living longer lives  and finding love at any age, thanks to online dating.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Internet allows people to find true love in the privacy of their own homes. No longer do you have to rely on the matchmaking efforts of your sister or best friend  or worse yet, your mother. No more joining groups you arent really interested in. Or lingering in the produce aisle hoping that cute guy or gal by the apples will bump into your cart. Nope  the world has changed. Enter online dating.
        </p>
        <p>
         The concept may have started with the younger set in mind, but it soon became clear that theres no age limit on love. People of all ages who were never married, divorced or widowed are signing up to meet their match. And finding love.
        </p>
        <p>
         Are you ready to take the leap? Sometimes that first step will prove to be the hardest. I have one friend who has talked about it for two years, but as far as I know, she hasnt signed up. Another friend has the site picked out but hes waiting until, well, Im not quite sure when.
        </p>
        <p>
         For many people, choosing the right dating website (for them) is the hardest. There are so many sites out there today, that the choices are endless. Asking around and hearing about others experiences with various sites is a good place to start. Keep in mind, however, that there are a lot of things that might make
         <em>
          your
         </em>
         experience different than theirs. It could be that you (or they) have a better photo or bio posted. It might be that youre each just looking for something different.
        </p>
        <p>
         So lets start there. Ask yourself honestly what youre looking for. Do you want companionship  someone to go to dinner and movies with? Or are you hoping to get married? Some sites might lean more toward one objective or the other. Evaluate the sites  some offer
         <em>
          more
         </em>
         matches; others boast a better success rate in terms of
         <em>
          lasting
         </em>
         romance.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you fall into a specific demographic or have compelling interests, those things might take priority over choosing a site geared to seniors. For instance, there are dating sites aimed at people who are Christian, Jewish, farmers and people who love dogs, to name a few. If you own four dogs and wouldnt look twice at a partner who doesnt love animals, well, then you might have the best success on a site for people who love dogs. Ditto if your faith is more important than your desire for a dinner date.
        </p>
        <p>
         For the rest of you, heres a look at some of the best online dating sites for seniors (in no particular order). Read on  and take the leap!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Zoosk
         </strong>
          Who knows what the name means, but it sound fun, right? The growing number of users get daily match updates. And those matches are a result of your preferences (and the secret algorithm). Seniors will love the easy-to-use site that includes the ability to change some of your choices. For instance if youve been set up with too many old fogeys, one click will allow you to specify that you want to limit your matches to those under 55. Free to sign up, then $19.98 per month for a three-month membership or $12.49 per month for six months.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Match.com
         </strong>
          This site has the largest number of senior users in the world, which certainly increases the number of possible matches. With features such as winks, chats and interest stirs there are a number of ways to meet your match. Match.com demonstrates its concern for user safety by utilizing a fraud detection program, which scans the site for suspicious activity. No cost to sign up. A one-month membership is $42.99, but the cost drops to $23.99 for a three-month membership and $19.99 per month if you sign on for a year.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          eHarmony
         </strong>
          Their Compatibility Matching System narrows the number of matches to those that are found to be your most compatible. You may get less quantity but the quality is what counts. EHarmony claims to be the matchmaker behind five percent of all U.S. marriages, and the most of any online dating service. Free to sign up. The cost of a one-month membership is a hefty $59.95 but that drops to just $11.95 per month for 12 months.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Christian Mingle
         </strong>
          This is the perfect site for like-minded singles whose faith is important to them. Youll find questions about the church you were raised in, a prayer wall and Bible studies, but that doesnt mean theres no fun stuff. Christian Mingle also gives users the chance to talk about their favorite music, hobbies, etc. Free to sign up. A one-month membership will run you $29.99 but a mere $13.99 per month will get you six months on the site.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          OurTime
         </strong>
          This is one of the most popular among the over-50-year-olds  probably because it was created for the senior set. Questions such as are you a punctual person? are more apt to hit home with seniors than the younger generation. Its easy to use, but offers fewer bells and whistles. Free to sign up. Itll cost you $23.99 to join for a month but just $13.99 for a six-month membership.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          OKCupid
         </strong>
          Heres an easy-to-use site for people of all ages. The profile is question-based, but its OK to skip over ones that dont apply. Again, not a lot of extras, but it might be a good way to test the waters. Its free to sign up and FREE to use for 6 months! The catch? There are a lot of pop-up ads. If those are putting a damper on romance, you can become an A-List member for $14.95 a month  with no ads.
        </p>
        <p>
         Pick the one thats best for you. It just might lead to The One whos best for you!
        </p>
