     <h2 itemprop="name">
      CAT EYE LITE
     </h2>
     <meta content="2014-11-06" itemprop="datePublished"/>
     <div class="entry">
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_16831" style="width: 522px">
       <a href="http://thebeautydepartment.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWingFB.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-16831" height="572" src="http://97.74.65.162/wp-content/uploads-c/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWingFB.jpg" title="TBDMiniSmokedWingFB" width="512"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        TUTORIAL + PHOTOGRAPHY BY AMY NADINE, GRAPHIC DESIGN BY EUNICE CHUN
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Sometimes a little smoked wing is all you need! Who says a cat eye has to be done with liquid liner? This soft version is ultra-feminine and flirty and a very modern take on a retro look. And for those of you who are still intimidated by liquid liner, this is a perfect way to master the shape of the flick. Lets break it down step by step:
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        TOOLS:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        A Nude Shadow  pick one that matches your own skin color.
       </li>
       <li>
        A Black Matte Shadow  not that a shimmer version wouldnt be pretty too! But I used this one from
        <strong>
         <a href="http://bit.ly/1uyIZhX" target="_blank">
          my favorite matte shadow palette
         </a>
        </strong>
        on Magali above.
       </li>
       <li>
        A Long-Edged Angled Liner Brush  if you dont have
        <strong>
         <a href="http://bit.ly/1xgXS6b" target="_blank">
          this long-edged angled liner brush
         </a>
        </strong>
        in your arsenal yet, youre missing out! It takes the guess work out of lining and allows you to stamp and sweep.
       </li>
       <li>
        Mascara  I used
        <strong>
         <a href="http://bit.ly/1xaI4Dz" target="_blank">
          this big volume mascara
         </a>
        </strong>
        and really loved the way it coated each lash.
       </li>
       <li>
        A Cotton Swab  it can be a regular one or a pointed tip.
       </li>
       <li>
        Optional: Half-Strip False Lashes  I used and love
        <strong>
         <a href="http://bit.ly/10xLR1q" target="_blank">
          these lash accents
         </a>
        </strong>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       STEPS:
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://thebeautydepartment.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWingSTEPS1.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16832" height="1174" src="http://97.74.65.162/wp-content/uploads-c/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWingSTEPS1.jpg" title="TBDMiniSmokedWingSTEPS1" width="512"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       1. Apply the nude shadow all over your lid and crease.
      </p>
      <p>
       2. Press the liner brush into the shadow and give it a tap against the counter to release any excess.
      </p>
      <p>
       3. Determine the angle of the wing by imagining an line from the end of your eye to the end of your brow. Stamp the brush along that angle.
      </p>
      <p>
       4. Move the brush towards the lashline and stamp again. Lift the brush up and stamp horizontally above it to connect it to your lashline.
      </p>
      <p>
       5. Now stamp along the lashline and stop about 3/4s of the way across.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://thebeautydepartment.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWingSTEPS2.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16833" height="767" src="http://97.74.65.162/wp-content/uploads-c/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWingSTEPS2.jpg" title="TBDMiniSmokedWingSTEPS2" width="512"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       6. Take the cotton swab and gently smear the line back and forth to smoke it out.
      </p>
      <p>
       7. Press the brush back into the shadow and give it another tap against the counter to remove any excess. Go back and stamp the wing to perfect the edges a little. You dont have to do this step, I just couldnt help myself!
      </p>
      <p>
       8. Add a couple coats of mascara.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://thebeautydepartment.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWingSTEPS3.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16834" height="601" src="http://97.74.65.162/wp-content/uploads-c/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWingSTEPS3.jpg" title="TBDMiniSmokedWingSTEPS3" width="512"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       9. This step is optional but you can always add a half strip of lashes for extra drama. Revisit my
       <strong>
        <a href="http://bit.ly/TBDlashstrips" target="_blank">
         Quick Tips For Lash Strips Tutorial
        </a>
       </strong>
       for secrets to getting it right every time.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://thebeautydepartment.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWing2.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16844" height="467" src="http://97.74.65.162/wp-content/uploads-c/2014/11/TBDMiniSmokedWing2.jpg" title="TBDMiniSmokedWing2" width="512"/>
       </a>
      </p>
