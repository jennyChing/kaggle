             <h1>
              The Secrets To Success
              <span style="color: #ff6600;">
               :
              </span>
              Bootlegging Disney
             </h1>
             <p>
              Before anyone panics, this is not an article about stealing Disney Classics, its about duplicating the success of one of the worlds greatest Entrepreneurs. Its about Walt Elias Disney himself. Have you ever wondered how he managed to create such an overwhelmingly successful enterprise? Of course, we know he was tenacious and hard working but so are many others who dont
              <a href="http://addicted2success.com/success-advice/the-5-great-lessons-walt-disney-taught-us/" title="Walt Disney's Success">
               achieve the same level of success as Walt Disney
              </a>
              . What are some of the thoughts and actions that made Walt, the man, so special and could we duplicate them in todays business environment with the same upward mobility?
              <span id="more-21601">
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              CONTINUING EDUCATION
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Walt Disney Picture Quote" class="lazy alignleft size-full wp-image-21603" data-original="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Walt-Disney-Picture-Quote.png" height="245" src="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/plugins/jquery-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" width="306"/>
             </p>
             <noscript>
              <img alt="Walt Disney Picture Quote" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-21603" height="245" src="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Walt-Disney-Picture-Quote.png" width="306"/>
             </noscript>
             By continuing education, I simply mean reading. Walt once said,
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               There is more treasure in books than in all the pirates loot on Treasure Island.
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
              Theres little doubt that Walt loved books, the legacy he left behind is like his own treasure map to the things he loved. The level of success beyond college graduation is often in direct correlation to the amount of time spent reading. Read fiction, non-fiction, biographies and 
              <a href="http://addicted2success.com/success-advice/how-to-think-to-get-what-you-want/" title="How To">
               how-to
              </a>
              s .just never stop learning. Power comes from knowledge and knowledge comes from books. It always has and always will.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              GET MOVING, KEEP TRYING, WORK HARD
             </h2>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               The way to get started is to quit talking and begin doing.
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
              I have no idea what the circumstances were when Walt said that but its a golden nugget of truth. We love to dream and talk about plans. Walt dreamed, talked and planned but he actually got up and worked too.
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               Everyone falls down. Getting back up is how you learn.
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
              In todays world, were often hampered by difficult regulations, costs, bad business partners and/or any number of negative events. Is this truly different than Walts day? Probably not as much as we would like to think. The rights to his first cartoon were more or less stolen from him. He didnt pout in his easy chair for the rest of his life. He got back up and Mickey Mouse was born.
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               People often ask me if I know the secret of success and if I could tell others how to make their dreams come true. My answer is, you do it by
               <i>
                working
               </i>
               .
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
              This is, or should be, common sense. After all, Walt grew up in an average working class family and saw his dad go to work daily.
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Dont look for shortcuts.
              </li>
              <li>
               Be willing to invest time and effort into a job well done and success will follow.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              ASK FOR HELP
             </h2>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               All youve got to do is own up to your ignorance honestly, and youll find people who are eager to fill your head with information.
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
              Plain and simple, he means ask for help. You cant possibly know everything and should stop acting like you do. During Walts lifetime, America was changing constantly and becoming an industrial world leader. He lived through two world wars and saw the invention of countless gadgets and machines designed to make life easier. No one person could keep up with it all. Walt knew this and asked for help and advice from experts. I think he must also have been a good listener.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              WHAT YOU KNOW
             </h2>
             <p>
              Writers are told to 
              <em>
               write what you know
              </em>
               because it gives authenticity to their writing. The same is true of business. Use your own life experience and personal knowledge to your advantage.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              KNOW YOUR PRIORITIES AND STOP WORRYING
             </h2>
             <p>
              <a href="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Walt-Disney-Picture-Quote-Best-you-Can-quote.png">
               <img alt="Walt Disney Picture Quote Best you Can quote" class="lazy alignleft size-full wp-image-21602" data-original="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Walt-Disney-Picture-Quote-Best-you-Can-quote.png" height="268" src="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/plugins/jquery-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" width="336"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Walt Disney Picture Quote Best you Can quote" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-21602" height="268" src="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Walt-Disney-Picture-Quote-Best-you-Can-quote.png" width="336"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
              All the success in the world wont be worth anything if your priorities are backwards.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                A man should never neglect his family for business
               </strong>
              </em>
              is one of the best quotes from Walt Disney. He knew what his priorities were and so should you. What good is financial gain if you lose your family in the process?
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               Why worry? If youve done the very best you can, worrying wont make it better.
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              This is excellent but difficult advice to follow. However, youll save yourself time, energy and possibly an anxiety attack if you do.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              DREAMS REALLY DO COME TRUE
             </h2>
             <p>
              Hundreds, possibly thousands, of
              <a href="http://addicted2success.com/quotes/40-magnificent-quotes-by-the-worlds-most-successful-billionaires/" title="Billionaire Quotes">
               quotes
              </a>
              are attributed to Walt Disney but one of my favorites is:
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               All our dreams can come true if we have the courage to pursue them.
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
              I know better than to think all my dreams will come true. I have some fantastic dreams. But the gist of the saying is spot on. It takes courage to fail and try again until success occurs. If you have courage, or can cultivate it, you are on your way to your dreams of wealth and success coming true.
             </p>
