     <p>
      <b>
       Stella D'oro
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">
       brand
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Cookie" title="Cookie">
       cookies
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Breadstick" title="Breadstick">
       breadsticks
      </a>
      owned by
      <a href="/wiki/Snyder%27s-Lance" title="Snyder's-Lance">
       Snyder's-Lance
      </a>
      . Stella D'oro means "star of gold" in
      <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
       Italian
      </a>
      , and the cookies are inspired by Italian baking. Its products include
      <a href="/wiki/Breadstick" title="Breadstick">
       breadsticks
      </a>
      , Swiss Fudge and other
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cookies" title="Cookies">
       cookies
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Biscotti" title="Biscotti">
       biscotti
      </a>
      , and their S-shaped breakfast treats.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Stella D'oro Biscuit Company was established in 1930 by Italian immigrant Joseph Kresivich. The family also operated a restaurant next door to its manufacturing facility on West 237th Street at the north end of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingsbridge,_The_Bronx" title="Kingsbridge, The Bronx">
       Kingsbridge
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">
       the Bronx
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York City
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt2008-12-26_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nyt2008-12-26-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1992 the family sold the business for $100 million
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytbatch_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nytbatch-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      to
      <a href="/wiki/Nabisco" title="Nabisco">
       Nabisco
      </a>
      , which became part of
      <a href="/wiki/Kraft_Foods" title="Kraft Foods">
       Kraft Foods
      </a>
      in 2000.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt2008-12-26_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-nyt2008-12-26-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2006, Kraft sold Stella D'oro to a
      <a href="/wiki/Private_equity" title="Private equity">
       private equity
      </a>
      firm,
      <a href="/wiki/Brynwood_Partners" title="Brynwood Partners">
       Brynwood Partners
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fishman_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-fishman-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      for $17.5 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytbatch_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-nytbatch-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Brynwood in turn sold the company to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lance,_Inc" title="Lance, Inc">
       Lance
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in 2009 for $23.9 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stella_D%27oro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Joseph Kresivich, an immigrant from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trieste,_Italy" title="Trieste, Italy">
       Trieste, Italy
      </a>
      , began working in
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York City
      </a>
      bakeries after his arrival in the U.S. in 1922, and developed his own recipes. He married his wife, Angela, "an expert baker in her own right" in 1928. The two later founded the Stella D'Oro bakery, which evolved into the Stella D'oro Biscuit Company.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The biscuits produced were less sweet than other Italian baked goods and marketed as an accompaniment to coffee and tea. Early on they made their cookies "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parve" title="Parve">
       parve
      </a>
      " (with neither meat nor dairy products), which appealed to a large and loyal New York kosher market. This early success led the company to relocate to a building at 237th and Broadway, in the
      <a href="/wiki/Kingsbridge,_Bronx" title="Kingsbridge, Bronx">
       Kingsbridge
      </a>
      section of the Bronx.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frazier_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Frazier-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Felice L. (Phil) Zambetti later became the company's
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO">
       CEO
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Buyout.2C_strike_and_closing">
       Buyout, strike and closing
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stella_D%27oro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Buyout, strike and closing">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 2006,
      <a href="/wiki/Brynwood_Partners" title="Brynwood Partners">
       Brynwood Partners
      </a>
      bought the Stella D'oro Biscuit Co. from Kraft Foods, Inc. (now
      <a href="/wiki/Mondel%C4%93z_International" title="Mondelz International">
       Mondelz International
      </a>
      ) .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fishman_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-fishman-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On August 14, 2008, two weeks after their contract expired, 138 workers of the
      <a href="/wiki/Bakery,_Confectionery,_Tobacco_Workers_and_Grain_Millers%27_International_Union" title="Bakery, Confectionery, Tobacco Workers and Grain Millers' International Union">
       Bakery, Confectionery, Tobacco Workers and Grain Millers' International Union
      </a>
      , Local 50, went on strike citing proposed pay and benefit cuts, and later picketed the company's attempt to bring in replacement workers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt1_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nyt1-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nypoststrikes_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nypoststrikes-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt3_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nyt3-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt4_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nyt4-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After more than 11 months on striking by its workers, the company was ordered by the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Labor_Relations_Board" title="National Labor Relations Board">
       National Labor Relations Board
      </a>
      to reinstate the workers, give them back pay, and restart collective bargaining.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      That same month, the company announced it would close its facility.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gt1_16-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-gt1-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In September 2009, Brynwood announced the sale of Stella D'oro to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lance,_Inc" title="Lance, Inc">
       Lance
      </a>
      , a large manufacturer of snack foods, which intended to relocate Stella D'Oro's production to a non-union facility in
      <a href="/wiki/Ashland,_Ohio" title="Ashland, Ohio">
       Ashland, Ohio
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytbatch_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-nytbatch-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Brynwood earned negative attention for its role in the work stoppage and sale of Stella D'oro, including a reference in an op-ed piece by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AFL-CIO" title="AFL-CIO">
       AFL-CIO
      </a>
      's Richard Trumka, published in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">
        The Wall Street Journal
       </a>
      </i>
      in April 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wsj2_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-wsj2-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:277px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stella_Doro_for_sale_E_jeh.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3168" data-file-width="4224" height="206" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Stella_Doro_for_sale_E_jeh.jpg/275px-Stella_Doro_for_sale_E_jeh.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Stella_Doro_for_sale_E_jeh.jpg/413px-Stella_Doro_for_sale_E_jeh.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Stella_Doro_for_sale_E_jeh.jpg/550px-Stella_Doro_for_sale_E_jeh.jpg 2x" width="275"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stella_Doro_for_sale_E_jeh.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Longtime Bronx bakery for sale
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      On September 8, 2009, Lance announced it was purchasing the Stella D'oro brand as well as certain manufacturing equipment and inventory. It thereafter began manufacturing Stella D'oro products in Lance's
      <a href="/wiki/Ashland,_Ohio" title="Ashland, Ohio">
       Ashland, Ohio
      </a>
      , bakery. The Bronx manufacturing location, which was not included in the sale, closed after the sale, and was demolished in spring 2012.
     </p>
     <p>
      In early September 2010, a 2 to 1 ruling by the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Labor_Relations_Board" title="National Labor Relations Board">
       National Labor Relations Board
      </a>
      affirmed the June 2009 decision of an administrative law judge that Stella D'oro violated federal labor law by refusing to furnish detailed financial statements to the workers' union to support claims of needing contract concessions to survive. The board ordered the company to furnish back pay with interest, as well as benefits for the two-month period after employees offered to return to work in May 2009, and before the company took them back in July.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On March 28, 2013, the Second Circuit Court of Appeals reversed the NLRB's decision, finding that Stella D'oro had not violated any federal labor law.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">
       Operations
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stella_D%27oro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Operations">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Stella D'oro's bakery was located on West 237th Street at the north end of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingsbridge,_The_Bronx" title="Kingsbridge, The Bronx">
       Kingsbridge
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">
       the Bronx
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York City
      </a>
      , but this was closed in October 2009 after disputes with labor unions and not included in the sale to Lance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      By late December 2006, the company had moved part of its manufacturing and cookie production to New Jersey.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The company's Swiss fudge cookies being made without milk or butter, Stella D'oro has a devoted following among
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orthodox_Jews" title="Orthodox Jews">
       Orthodox Jews
      </a>
      who follow Jewish dietary guidelines.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt2008-12-26_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-nyt2008-12-26-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The cookies are nicknamed
      <a href="/wiki/Shtreimel" title="Shtreimel">
       shtreimels
      </a>
      , the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yiddish" title="Yiddish">
       Yiddish
      </a>
      term for round fur hats worn on the Sabbath by some
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hasidic_Jew" title="Hasidic Jew">
       Hasidic Jews
      </a>
      , and are
      <a href="/wiki/Pareve" title="Pareve">
       pareve
      </a>
      , a category of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kosher" title="Kosher">
       kosher
      </a>
      food that contains neither meat nor dairy ingredients.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt2003-01-12_22-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nyt2003-01-12-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After buying the company, Kraft removed the pareve designation from Stella D'oro's baked products, but returned it after declining sales and complaints.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt2003-01-12_22-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-nyt2003-01-12-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
