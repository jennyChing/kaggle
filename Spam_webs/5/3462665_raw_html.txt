         </h1>

         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           <span style="font-size: 18pt; color: #000000; font-family: 'courier new', courier;">
            Khalil Joseph
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           The videography of Khalil Joseph. I recently visited LA for the first time and I went to the MOCA. On exhibition was the video Double Consciousness treated to Kendrick Lamars album m.A.A.d city. The video is fifteen minutes long and played on two split screens displaying the dichotomy of the beauty and the horror of black life in LA. Thoughtful and often cryptic (no pun intended) Joseph brings to life the young, old, innocent and tainted characters of Kendrick Lamars lyrics. I had never heard of Khalil Joseph until I stepped foot into MOCA and now I want to know so much more about the fiercely private director of short films and music videos. I was unable to find and share thedouble consciousness video (if youre in LA go to the MOCA and see it!) but this video directed by
           <del>
            my husband
           </del>
           I mean Khalil for Flying Lotus Until the Quiet Comes shows the amazing grace he brings to grit.
          </span>
         </p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/-pVHC1DXQ7U" width="560">
         </iframe>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           <span style="font-size: 18pt; color: #000000; font-family: 'courier new', courier;">
            Kendrick Lamars Alright
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/kendrick-video-9_xyaf0l.jpg">
           <img alt="kendrick-video-9_xyaf0l" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6472" height="440" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/kendrick-video-9_xyaf0l.jpg" width="670"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <span style="color: #000000; font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
          Which brings me to ol boy Kendrick Lamar. He is doing what so many muthafuckas want to do but cant because they lack the artistic ability and affinity that comes so naturally to Lamar. For example, no matter what Kanye or Jay Z try to offer as their original art (Marina Abramovic, Givenchy designs, clothing, and often times music) falls flat to me. It always seems to miss the mark, feels two dimensional, feigned and selfish. But when I listen to Kendricks albums, the characters he creates, the many moods he sets when I watch his collaboration with
          <del>
           my husband
          </del>
          I mean Khalil Joseph it is obvious the lil man has a distinct, altruistic message that he is attempting to share. Thats what real artist do, they have something to say and they twist, turn, stretch, sketch, type, film, paint and syncopate to get the message across in mediums that are sometimes hard to digest and understand. I dig that shit man.
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
         </span>
         <p class="none">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           I watched his new video Alright and immediately shared it with my videographer/filmmaker friend. Initially, he was pissed that the piece was so cryptic and senseless but because he knows that nothing within an artists work is unintentional he studied it and came up with an interpretation which I think is spot on. The beginning scenes are hellish, chaotic and a reflection of the times and state of consciousness of the masses. The upside down Kendrick represents the cocooned Kendrick, the growing of consciousness and resistance, the floating Kendrick is the butterfly, evolved, flying high who perhaps can be a hopeful inspiration extended to the black race which the oppressors/evil dont want to see thus the shooting and bringing down of Kendrick but it aint over. What do you guys think? What are your interpretations? Did you like the video?
          </span>
         </p>

         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           I related a lot to this article. I didnt relate to the addiction to seduction part. I dont pursue, I dont meddle, I dont change for men nor am I feign for attention. What I do relate to is the pattern of overlapping, serial monogamous relationships. I suppose I love love, of experiencing the variances of being in like to the reckless, all consuming madness of losing myself in another until now. I have met my match  myself. I have surrendered to being a lone. No distractions. Just me, my God and the current of my fears, hopes, pain and dreams which bubble within me like a cold, thick stew being warmed. I can relate with Gilbert in the contemplative, difficult journey of finding the savory in alone-ness. (Hardest shit I have ever done son.)

          <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/memoir-writing-worskshop-reflect-remember-release/" rel="bookmark" title="4:21 pm">

           by
           <span class="author vcard">

        <div class="entry-content">
         <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/tumblr_mo4cdmQNOY1s8x8t4o1_500.png">
          <img alt="tumblr_mo4cdmQNOY1s8x8t4o1_500" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6452" height="375" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/tumblr_mo4cdmQNOY1s8x8t4o1_500.png" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
          <strong>
           A excerpt from a piece I am working on for this workshop:
          </strong>
         </span>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             I am fourteen years old. My parents are arguing again in the backyard as I watch them from my bedroom window. My fathers black frame is tight. His right arm rises and falls like a karate chop with each word he yells. My mother stands in front of him, chest out, red faced and crying. He must seem threatening to my neighbors  this rigid black man yelling at the top of his lungs at a hysterical white woman. I am worried that they will call the cops on them. I want to bring them inside but I dont interfere this time. I just watch.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             My dad soon comes into my bedroom. He is late for work. He apologizes for the scene. He tells me he feels hopeless and that he cant do it anymore. I tell him they should just get a divorce. I dont feel very sad. I dont cry like I usually do when my parents fight. This time I dont care. I feel numb.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             I recently started high school. Each day I wake up early and get off to school by myself. It takes me about an hour to get to school each day. Its an agricultural high school. We learn about plants, farm animals and the environment. I really enjoyed the orientation that I had to go to during the summer. I got all As and won awards. I was surprised when they called my name for an award the first time but I was embarrassed by the third.I dont have the same focus that I did back in the summer because now I think I am in love
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="none" style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             He pages my beeper. I have been waiting for it all day. Once I get home from school, I shower, oil my body with coconut oil, slick my curls into a high ponytail, put on new Baby Phat panties I bought from Burlington Coat Factory and dress into a new outfit. I have a job at a gourmet food shop that pays me in cash each week. I spend most of my money on clothes. I have six pairs of Timberland boots in different colors and a fresh pair of air force 1s in all white and red and white. I am the only girl in my high school to wear a Roc A Wear valor sweat suit. I think all of the girls are jealous of me. I think all of the boys like me. I dont have many friends.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             I call him back.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             Yo, meet me on Wayne Ave.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="none" style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             My heart is racing. I rush to leave my home. I am happy to get away from the heavy, dark space it has become. My father left a month ago. He moved into a new apartment five blocks away. I dont know where my mom is. She has started a new teaching job and often comes home late and tired. She cries a lot at night.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             I walk the four blocks to meet Ali. He is sixteen. He drives a car, has a Nextel, white teeth, light skin and freckles. He isnt like my last boyfriend. Ali isnt my friend. We dont stay up late talking on the phone. I dont know his mothers name. We dont laugh very often. He says I talk funny, like a white girl so I talk less and curse more.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="none" style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             He is the first boy I ever French kiss. We sit on the steps of a church and pass gum between our mouths swapping his Big Red for my Winter Fresh. His tongue is wet and forceful like his hands which squeeze my small breasts and push in between my thighs.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             I dont know what I am doing but I feel grown. I feel like the older girls who have full breasts, big hips, asses and who have had sex.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="none" style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             I meet Ali at a park. Fall is here and the sun is setting earlier. It is nearly dark. My mom flashes into my mind but I push her tired, worried image into the back of my thoughts.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             Ali looks over my body and chews loudly on his Big Red. In between the interruption of his Nextel which barks random Yoooos and Where you ats, he kisses me and touches my hair.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="none" style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             He wants me to go to his cousins house down the street. A house I have never been to. I feel nervous but I play it cool and agree. I know why he wants me to go to this random house. He wants to have sex.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #000000;">
             I am a virgin and a lot of my friends from middle school have already had sex. They tell me about the way it feels and how many boys they have been with. I want to experience it. I like the way my body responds when he kisses me and touches me but my young, fourteen year old self is in conflict.
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="none">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/final-writing-promo.jpg">
            <img alt="final writing promo" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6453" height="377" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/final-writing-promo-1024x603.jpg" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           This is memoir writing. It comes from memory about experiences and moments that make deep impressions and grooves into the woodwork of our lives. For me, writing is an act of healing. It helps me to reflect, remember and release moments that bring me confusion, hurt and shame. Writing helps me to accept myself and all my varied circumstances so that I can forgive others (and most importantly myself) and grow.
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="none">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           I know that there are other young, old, experienced and fresh writers out there with their own stories that have been stagnant in their psyches for so long. It is time to loosen the hardened material and allow the beautiful alchemy of words to unfurl.
          </span>
         </p>
         <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
          I have facilitated a Memoir Writing Workshop here in Philadelphia beginning July 7th-21 lead by Maleka Fruean. Every Tuesday for two hoursin this short writing workshop, we will explore our memories and our lifes narrative- from the first time we remember feeling fear to the last time we ate cotton candy. Well use humor, sadness, and everything in between to begin creating polished non-fiction pieces that read like great stories. Well explore our own unique writing voice in the world through in-class writing prompts and exercises, and short assignments to work on at home. This workshop is forwomen,andfor all levels of writers. Our work will be shared and workshopped in a safe and encouraging environment.
         </span>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">

         <div class="entry-meta">
          Posted on
          <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/ultas-love-your-hair-campaign-product-reviews-2/" rel="bookmark" title="5:47 am">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-05-21T05:47:08+00:00" pubdate="">
            May 21, 2015
           </time>
          </a>
          <span class="byline">
           by
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/author/antoinette/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Antoinette">
             Antoinette
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>

        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ulta-post-thumbnail.jpg">
           <img alt="ulta post thumbnail" class="size-large wp-image-6413" height="427" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ulta-post-thumbnail-1024x683.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           My Turn! As you know Ulta is having their in-store and online, Love Your Hair Event all month long. Shanti and I were gifted with some products to review and as much as we hate trying new products this wasnt so bad.
          </span>
         </p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-20-at-1.59.14-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-05-20 at 2.11.55 PM" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6411" height="408" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-20-at-2.11.55-PM.png" width="394"/>
          </a>
         </span>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           <a href="http://www.ulta.com/ulta/a/_/Ntt-k-pak/Nty-1?Dy=1&amp;ciSelector=searchResults" target="_blank">
            K-Pak
           </a>
           is a product line that is starting to get a lot of attention and for good reason. I was first introduced to this line at the
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/essence-best-in-black-beauty-awards/" target="_blank">
            Best in Black Beauty Essence Event
           </a>
           . This intense hydrator is essentially a deep conditioner stocked with amino acids that offers immediate hydration in only 5 minutes. It leaves your hair soft, detangled, hydrated and manageable and only for $4.99. Child, you cant be that price. Mucho bang for your buck. However, please note that this product contains silicones which are highly effective at smoothing and taming frizz but can also create product build up. So, be sure to monitor your build up and cleanse regularly if you use this product in excess.
          </span>
         </p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-20-at-1.59.14-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-05-20 at 1.59.14 PM" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6409" height="396" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-20-at-1.59.14-PM.png" width="294"/>
          </a>
         </span>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           <a href="http://www.ulta.com/ulta/browse/productDetail.jsp?productId=xlsImpprod3780009&amp;skuId=2238659&amp;_requestid=738790" target="_blank">
            Ouidad Moisture Lock Leave-In Conditioner
           </a>
           ended up being a winner in the end but I was skeptical. Ouidads claim is that it  instantly defines and hydrates curls while keeping frizz at bay (it) creates a moisture foundation by infusing hair with vital conditioning proteins. Prickly pear extract draws in moisture from the air and seals it in for superior hydration while antioxidant green tea and vitamin B5 help protect and promote shine. Arnica extract lends a smoothing touchsealing the cuticle for frizz-free results.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           I was initially turned off by this product because it didnt detangle my hair well or have much slip when I applied it. I added quarter size amount to my wet hair and began to let it air dry. About 10 minutes later I examine my strands and the product was so lightweight, I couldnt tell if my hair was actually being hydrated or not. So, I added another quarter size amount to my hair and still it felt like nothing was applied. I couldnt see or feel the product in my hair. When I scrunched my strands together to mold them into the perfect curl there was no white film no product residue on my hands nothing. It was strange.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           So, I mentally prepared myself for a horrible hair day and got my top knot supplies ready. About 30 minutes later I looked at my hair and to my surprise, I loved the way my hair looked. My curls were incredibly happy, weightless and full of movement, body and bounce. I wasnt mad at all. Frizz free? No. Not really. But I dont like frizz free hair anyway so me no care.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           Overall,I like this conditioner because it doesnt create a lot of build up and it allows my natural curl to be a little more wild and free. With that said,I would definitely recommend it to curlies looking for a weightless leave-in who have looser, fine curls like mine. This is that, hair blowing in the wind leave-in.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           *Side note*
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           I just dyed my hair ombre blonde and feel like I need something with a little more slip for the time being because my bleached strands are a bit stiff and ratchet. Ill continue to use this product again once my hair health is restored.
          </span>
         </p>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-20-at-2.01.09-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-05-20 at 2.01.09 PM" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6410" height="425" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-20-at-2.01.09-PM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </span>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           Now you already know I couldnt resist trying out a product called
           <a href="http://www.ulta.com/ulta/a/_/Ntt-big+sexy+hair?Ns=topRated%7C1&amp;ciSelector=searchResults" target="_blank">
            Big Sexy Hair Spray and Play
           </a>
           . This is essentially hair spray. Its not much different from any other hair spray Ive used besides the fact that it comes in this fun packaging. It has a lot of hold and a lot of power so if you like movement, I would use a very small amount. I flipped my hair to the opposite side in which I part it, sprayed the roots a little, fluffed and enjoyed some added body. Boom. Its that simple. Not much to review here. It did its job the way I expected it to do. I do want to try their volume and texturizing powder though.
          </span>
         </p>

           Ultas Love Your Hair Campaign  Product Reviews
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          Posted on
          <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/ultas-love-your-hair-campaign-product-reviews/" rel="bookmark" title="6:07 am">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-05-20T06:07:21+00:00" pubdate="">
            May 20, 2015
           </time>
          </a>
          <span class="byline">
           by
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/author/antoinette/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Antoinette">
             Antoinette
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           As mentioned before in
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/ultas-love-your-hair-campaign-cheap-cheap-prices/" target="_blank">
            this post
           </a>
           , Ulta has gifted Antoinette and I (my account is not working for some reason, so I am posting under her name) a bunch of products to review for
           <span style="font-size: 18pt;">
            Ultas Love Your Hair Campaign
           </span>
           . You can get great
           <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
            hair products on sale at all Ulta stores from May 10-30.
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           The products I choose to review were,
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/2282900.jpeg">
            <img alt="2282900" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6353" height="400" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/2282900.jpeg" width="400"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
            
            <a href="http://www.ulta.com/ulta/a/_/Ntt-biolage%20cleasing/Nty-1?Dy=1&amp;ciSelector=searchResults" target="_blank">
             Maxtrix Biolage Cleansing Conditioner
            </a>
           </span>
            a combination of soap-free cleansers and superfruits which slide away dirt and residue without stripping the hair. For
           <strong>
            Medium
           </strong>
           hair (whatever the hell that is).
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <strong>
            Antifrizz, suitable for color treated.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           And
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
            
            <a href="http://www.ulta.com/ulta/browse/productDetail.jsp?productId=xlsImpprod3780011#reviews" target="_blank">
             Ouidad Climate Control
            </a>
            
           </span>
           formulated to expand in contact with moisture, enveloping cuticles to prevent frizz without altering style. When humidity lessens, the polymers contract creating natural, non-sticky curls (whatever the hell that means).
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ouidad_climatecontrolgel_250ml_new_900x900.jpg">
            <img alt="ouidad_climatecontrolgel_250ml_new_900x900" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6354" height="460" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ouidad_climatecontrolgel_250ml_new_900x900.jpg" width="460"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           I was super excited to try the combination due to the fact that I am looking for a new co-wash and hoping to find my holy grail holding product for this coming summers heat.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           My Slept-On, Un-styled Hair (Judge Your Mutha)
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DSC_3642.jpg">
            <img alt="DSC_3642" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6351" height="424" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DSC_3642-1024x678.jpg" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DSC_3644.jpg">
            <img alt="DSC_3644" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6355" height="424" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DSC_3644-1024x678.jpg" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           After Showering Using
           <a href="http://www.ulta.com/ulta/a/_/Ntt-matrixbscollection1/Nty-1?Dy=1&amp;ciSelector=searchResults" target="_blank">
            Biolage Cleansing Conditioner
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DSC_3653.jpg">
            <img alt="DSC_3653" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6356" height="424" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DSC_3653-1024x678.jpg" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="color: #000000; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           What I loved about the Biolage was that it was moisturizing. I could feel the product cleansing my hair but it never felt streaky and drying. I wash and go mostly every single day. I add tons of product to my hair every single day. It was nice to have found a product that will rinse away yesterdays residue while also replenishing much needed moisture. I can tell that the product was indeed moisturizing because my curls are well formed and clumped rather than frizzy and separated.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DSC_3667.jpg">
            <img alt="DSC_3667" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6357" height="424" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DSC_3667-1024x678.jpg" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino;">
           <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DSC_3674.jpg">

           Senegalese Twist Crown Up Do
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">

        <div class="entry-content">
         <a href="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/senegalese-twist-.jpg">
          <img alt="senegalese twist" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6345" height="640" src="http://aroundthewaycurls.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/senegalese-twist--1024x1024.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
         This video is a simple updo for senegalese twists. All you need to complete this style are bobby pins.
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Keep in mind you want to hydrate your real hair while they are in the twists. The easiest way to do that is to create a hydrating mist and spray your hair both at night and in the morning.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
           <strong>
            DYI Hydrating Mist Recipe:
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          2-TBSP Leave-In Conditioner
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          1 TBSP Coconut Oil
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          1 TBSP Aloe Vera Juice
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Remainder Water
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Shake Well and Spray
         </p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/3nwwMaPPpfY" width="560">
         </iframe>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          *Disclaimer*
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          If you follow us on instagram youll know that while I loved my twists, they unfortunately, were not for me. My hair was far too thin and fine for them. I left the twists in for about a month and after, I did I experience a lot of breakage due to the weight of the hair and the amount of updos I wore for work. They didnt feel too tight but at times, they felt too heavy. There was just too much pulling at my roots. Oddly enough however, the hair that was not being ripped from my scalp was pretty happy and healthy. LOL. This told me that the moisturizing mist worked but the weight of the twists did not. So, I still recommend them as a protective style just not for women with fine or damaged hair. You must have that real good healthy stuff to rock these. Oh well, you cant win them all. I tried and my hard headed/big headed self will probably try again and complain about being bald when I take them out.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Whateva. At the end of the day its just hair. Right?
         </p>
