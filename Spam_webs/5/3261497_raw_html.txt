        <h1 class="posttitle">
         Prenda Hit With $12,000 In Sanctions Over Its Bogus Defamation Suit
        </h1>
        <h3>
         from the
         <i>
          Assclown-Law-racks-up-another-debit
         </i>
         dept
        </h3>
        <div class="postbody">
         The spectacular
         <a href="https://www.techdirt.com/blog/?company=prenda+law" target="_blank">
          Prenda flameout
         </a>
         continues. As was
         <a href="https://www.techdirt.com/articles/20140123/18272425973/yet-another-prenda-gambit-fails-judge-agrees-to-sanctions-against-paul-duffy-over-bogus-defamation-lawsuit.shtml" target="_blank">
          reported here
         </a>
         back in January, US District Judge John Darrah approved Paul Godfread and Alan Cooper's request for sanctions at the tail end of Prenda's disastrous defamation suit (one John Steele himself
         <a href="http://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2014/02/judge-slams-prenda-for-sophistry-in-defamation-suit-sanction/" target="_blank">
          did plenty to undermine
         </a>
         ).
         <a href="http://blog.muddlawoffices.com/mloblog/2014/06/13/sanctions-affirmed-against-prenda-law/" target="_blank">
          Now, we have a dollar amount
         </a>
         . (via
         <a href="http://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2014/06/prenda-lawyers-who-sued-over-assclown-taunt-must-pay-12k-in-fees/" target="_blank">
          Ars Technica
         </a>
         )
         <blockquote>
          <i>
           Today, Judge Darrah in the Northern District of Illinois granted yet another sanctions award against copyright troll Prenda Law. Paul Godfread and Alan Cooper have been awarded $11,758.20 as reimbursement for a portion of the fees they have spent on attorneys in defending themselves against Prendas defamation lawsuit.
          </i>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <i>
           The Court awarded the sanctions under both Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 11 as well as its inherent power to sanction provided in 28 U.S.C.  1927. In the Courts own words, the sanctions award was [b]ased on Prendas misrepresentations in this Court and the Defendants incurred fees were occasioned by the misconduct of Prenda Law and Paul A. Duffy in this Court.
          </i>
         </blockquote>
         This will be added to the half-million in
         <a href="http://wiki.ktetch.co.uk/index.php?title=Monetary_awards" target="_blank">
          (mostly uncollected) sanctions levied
         </a>
         against Prenda
         <a href="https://www.techdirt.com/articles/20130722/12295523887/more-sanctions-issued-against-team-prenda.shtml" target="_blank">
          since the beginning
         </a>
         of last year. Prenda tried to argue that the hourly fee requested was ridiculously high.
         <blockquote>
          <i>
           According to the American Intellectual Property Law Associations 2013 Report of the Economic Survey, intellectual property attorneys based in Chicago and Boston within the first quartile bill $400 per hour, in line with the amount billed by Defendants counsel, who are based in Chicago and Boston. (Dkt. No. 61, Ex. B.) Yet,
           <b>
            Prenda argues that this rate is exorbitant because Defendants attorney Erin Russell is not registered before the United States Patent and Trademark Office and, therefore, not an intellectual property attorney
           </b>
           .
          </i>
         </blockquote>
         "Apropos of what," Judge Darrah doesn't even bother asking.
         <blockquote>
          <i>
           Prenda cites no law supporting this contention, and its claim of exorbitant rates fails.
          </i>
         </blockquote>
         Prenda also argued that some of the fees incurred were "self-inflicted" and not the direct result of its bogus lawsuit/endless dicking around. Darrah dismantles this argument as well.
         <blockquote>
          <i>
           The basis of Prendas mitigation argument is that Defendants chose not to trust Prenda to withdraw its motion to remand.
           <b>
            Defendants failure to trust Prenda was not unreasonable, given Prendas vigorous defense of its motion to remand on the same day it filed its motion to withdraw the motion
           </b>
           . In any event, Defendants response to Prendas motion to remand was sufficiently quick and efficient.
           <b>
            Not only did Defendants file their response within six days, they filed it on the same day that Prenda presented its motion to withdraw
           </b>
           . Prendas opposition with respect to Defendants fees being self-inflicted or avoidable is denied.
          </i>
         </blockquote>
         Prenda did manage to come out of this in slightly better shape than it could have. Darrah only awarded the attorney's fees racked up in his court, not including anything that occurred in a separate court in 2013. That decision cut the awarded amount roughly in half.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         This appears to be the final moments for Prenda Law, a sordid episode of legal abuse that is ending with lots of (gavel) banging and (Prenda partner) whimpering.
