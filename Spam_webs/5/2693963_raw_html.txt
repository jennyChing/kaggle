         <p>
          A few notes about this recipe. First of all, I looked up several other pound cake recipes to compare and this one wasn't like any of the others. The more other recipes I looked at, the more I worried that this one would be a colossal failure! However, I mostly followed the recipe and instructions and it turned out just lovely. In fact, it was a huge hit, and the whole family loved it.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-39479">
          </span>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Old Fashioned Pound Cake Recipe
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What You Need:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           1 cup butter
          </li>
          <li>
           1 cup sugar
          </li>
          <li>
           5 eggs, separated
          </li>
          <li>
           1/4 tsp nutmeg
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tbsp lemon juice
          </li>
          <li>
           2 cups flour
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tsp baking powder
          </li>
          <li>
           1/2 tsp baking soda
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tsp vanilla extract
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What To Do:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
          </li>
          <li>
           In small bowl, mix together flour, baking powder, and baking soda. Set aside
          </li>
          <li>
           In large mixing bowl, cream Butter. Add sugar and beat well.
          </li>
          <li>
           Stir in beaten egg yolks and nutmeg.
          </li>
          <li>
           Alternately add dry ingredients and fold in egg whites, adding lemon juice with egg whites
          </li>
          <li>
           Stir in vanilla extract
          </li>
          <li>
           Add to loaf pan or bundt ban and bake at 350 degrees for 1 hr.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Typically, pound cake is served without icing because of it's richness. However, I had some strawberries that were calling my name and I just couldn't help myself, so I made a strawberry glaze to go with ours.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Old Fashioned Pound Cake Recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40266" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Pound-Cake.jpg" height="501" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="560"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Old Fashioned Pound Cake Recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40266" height="501" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Pound-Cake.jpg" width="560"/>
         </noscript>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Strawberry Glaze
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What You Need:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           1/2 cup butter, softened
          </li>
          <li>
           2 cups powdered sugar
          </li>
          <li>
           1/2 tsp vanilla extract
          </li>
          <li>
           2-3 tbsp milk
          </li>
          <li>
           3 large strawberries, pureed.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What To Do:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Cream butter in a mixing bowl. Add 1 cup powdered sugar, and mix well. Add remaining powdered sugar, alternating with milk until desired consistency is reached. Finally, mix in 1 tsp vanilla extract and strawberry puree. If glaze is too thick, add additional milk 1 tsp at a time. If it is too thin, add additional powdered sugar 1 tbsp at a time.
         </p>
         <p>
          Enjoy this delicious pound cake with your favorite fruit, with the strawberry glaze above, or plain with a bit of whipped cream!
         </p>
