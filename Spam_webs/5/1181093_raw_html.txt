       <h1 class="title">
        7 Recipes That Use Pumpkin
       </h1>
       <div class="post-meta">
        <span class="small">
         Published on
        </span>
        <abbr class="date time published" title="2013-10-31T17:41:15+0000">
         October 31, 2013 |
        </abbr>
        <span class="small">
         in
        </span>
        <span class="categories">
         <a href="http://blog.aprons.com/apron/category/food-tips/">
          Food Tips
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="small">
         By
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://blog.aprons.com/apron/author/adminsingle/" rel="author" title="Posts by Aprons Team">
           Aprons Team
          </a>
         </span>
         |
        </span>
        <div class="icon-comment">
        </div>
        <span class="post-comments comments">
         <a href="http://blog.aprons.com/apron/7-recipes-use-pumpkin/#comments">
          0 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         <img alt="7 Recipes That Use Pumpkin" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-100" height="392" src="http://blog.aprons.com/apron/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/7-Recipes-That-Use-Pumpkin.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The air is getting crisper, the leaves are turning and a definite sense of change is upon us. Autumn is officially in full swing, and with it comes a bounty of fresh new ingredients we foodies can take advantage of in the kitchen.
        </p>
        <p>
         At the top of our list this seasonbig, bright pumpkins. Besides being packed with vitamins, we love pumpkins because they work wonderfully in both sweet and savory dishes.
        </p>
        <p>
         So put on your apron and taste the flavors autumn with these 7 delicious pumpkin recipes.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Pumpkin Pancakes
        </h1>
        <p>
         Who doesnt love a big stack of pancakes on a lazy Sunday morning? This
         <a href="http://www.food.com/recipe/light-and-fluffy-pumpkin-pancakes-187362" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.food.com/recipe/light-and-fluffy-pumpkin-pancakes-187362', 'Light and Fluffy Pancake recipe from Food.com']);">
          Light and Fluffy Pancake recipe from Food.com
         </a>
         make a perfect breakfast that will keep you full for hours.
        </p>
        <p>
         Start with six tablespoons of canned pumpkin puree, melted butter, some nutmeg, ginger, cinnamon and a pinch of clove, along with some other essential ingredients, and mix everything into a batter. Then, pour batches of your batter onto a heated and greased skillet, cooking each pancake for about 3 minutes per side. Serve them up with some butter and syrup, and wash down with a fresh glass of OJ.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Pumpkin Fritters
        </h1>
        <p>
         Delicious and easy to make, these
         <a href="http://www.ivillage.com/pumpkin-fritters/3-r-72722http://www.ivillage.com/pumpkin-fritters/3-r-72722" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ivillage.com/pumpkin-fritters/3-r-72722http://www.ivillage.com/pumpkin-fritters/3-r-72722', 'Pumpkin Fritters from iVillage.com']);">
          Pumpkin Fritters from iVillage.com
         </a>
         are perfect for breakfast, lunch or dinner. Theyre made with one 15-ounce can of pumpkin puree, a 1/2 cup of flour, one egg, butter, ground cinnamon and a few other essential ingredients.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once your ingredients are combined in a large bowl, drop spoonfuls of your mixture onto a hot skillet and lightly flatten with a spatula. Cook about 4 minutes on each side or until golden. Sprinkle hot fritters with cinnamon and sugar before serving.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Pumpkin Soup
        </h1>
        <p>
         Theres nothing like drinking a warm bowl of soup on a cool autumn evening. We especially love this
         <a href="http://www.marthastewart.com/336106/roasted-pumpkin-soup" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.marthastewart.com/336106/roasted-pumpkin-soup', 'Roasted Pumpkin Soup from MarthaStewart.com']);">
          Roasted Pumpkin Soup from MarthaStewart.com
         </a>
         because its easy to make and uses a bevy of flavor-packed ingredients like garlic, onions, shitake mushrooms, freshly ground pepper and vegetable stock.
        </p>
        <p>
         To make it, start by cutting your pumpkin into 2-inch pieces. Combine with mushrooms, garlic and onion before tossing it all into a 450-degree oven. Roast about 30 minutes, or until pumpkin is tender. Then put everything in a saucepan and cook with vegetable stock. Slowly add batches of your mixture to a blender and puree until smooth. Enjoy a bowl of soup with a big piece of crusty bread for the ultimate lunch or dinner.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Pumpkin Ravioli With Sage Butter
        </h1>
        <p>
         If youre looking for a recipe thats both a little more challenging and unique, try this
         <a href="http://www.williams-sonoma.com/recipe/pumpkin-ravioli-with-sage-butter.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.williams-sonoma.com/recipe/pumpkin-ravioli-with-sage-butter.html', 'Pumpkin Ravioli With Sage Butter featured on Williams-Sonoma.com']);">
          Pumpkin Ravioli With Sage Butter featured on Williams-Sonoma.com
         </a>
         . According to the website, it features handmade pasta and a Sauce of lightly browned butter and fresh sage, which heightens and contrasts with the sweetness of the pumpkin in the filling. This is certainly a dish that will delight your family and friends at your next dinner party.
        </p>
        <p>
         For the filling, youll need ingredients such as a 1-lb. piece of Cheese or Sugar Pie pumpkin, grated Parmigiano-Reggiano or grana padano cheese, freshly grated nutmeg, dried breadcrumbs, fresh sage leaves and a few other essential ingredients. Check out the website for the full step-by-step recipe.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Turkey Pumpkin Chili
        </h1>
        <p>
         We certainly love traditional recipes with a bit of a twist, especially when they boast added nutrients without sacrificing flavor. Thats why this
         <a href="http://www.wholefoodsmarket.com/recipe/turkey-pumpkin-chili" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.wholefoodsmarket.com/recipe/turkey-pumpkin-chili', 'Turkey Pumpkin Chili from WholeFoodsMarket.com']);">
          Turkey Pumpkin Chili from WholeFoodsMarket.com
         </a>
         is next up on our list. According to the website, Besides adding a sweet nutty flavor to dishes, pumpkin is a ready source of vitamin A, which boosts the nutrition content of this offbeat chili.
        </p>
        <p>
         Start with a can of pumpkin puree, a pound of dark meat turkey, a yellow onion, a green bell pepper, two jalapeos, a clove of garlic, a can of diced tomatoes, a can of kidney beans, a cup of water and some spices (salt, pepper, cumin and chili powder). Then heat two tablespoons of olive oil in a large pot over high heat. Toss in your ingredients (according to recipe), stirring occasionally for about 30 minutes. Cover and simmer for about 30 minutes more. Serve and enjoy!
        </p>
        <h1>
         15-Minute Pumpkin Risotto
        </h1>
        <p>
         The next time youre looking for a creative side dish that wont take hours to make, try this
         <a href="http://www.myrecipes.com/recipe/15-minute-pumpkin-risotto-10000000523117/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.myrecipes.com/recipe/15-minute-pumpkin-risotto-10000000523117/', '15-minute Pumpkin Risotto from MyRecipes.com']);">
          15-minute Pumpkin Risotto from MyRecipes.com
         </a>
         . Its packed with warm fall flavors and pairs perfectly with grilled chicken or fish.
        </p>
        <p>
         Start with one cup of canned pumpkin, dry white wine, chicken broth, onion, margarine, cornstarch, precooked white rice, parmesan cheese and some essential spices. Combine everything in a 4- to 5-quart pan (according to recipe) and top it off with some cheese before serving.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Old-Fashioned Soft Pumpkin Cookies
        </h1>
        <p>
         Now you didnt think we would go through seven pumpkin recipes and not address dessert, did you? Once you try these
         <a href="http://www.verybestbaking.com/recipes/32364/old-fashioned-soft-pumpkin-cookies/detail.aspx" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.verybestbaking.com/recipes/32364/old-fashioned-soft-pumpkin-cookies/detail.aspx', 'Old-Fashioned Soft Pumpkin Cookies from VeryBestBaking.com']);">
          Old-Fashioned Soft Pumpkin Cookies from VeryBestBaking.com
         </a>
         , were sure youll reconsider the traditional pumpkin pie at your Thanksgiving dinner this year.
        </p>
        <p>
         To make them, youll need a can of pumpkin puree, flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, sugar, nutmeg, butter, vanilla extract and one large egg. After combining everything in a bowl, Drop rounded spoonfuls of your batter onto a greased baking sheet. Bake for about 15 minutes in a pre-heated 350-degree oven. Let cool and enjoy.
        </p>
        <p>
         From breakfast to dessert, and all meals in between, pumpkins are a perfect ingredient to showcase the warm and delicious flavors of autumn.
        </p>
        <p>
         Do you have a favorite pumpkin recipe? Tell us about it.
        </p>
