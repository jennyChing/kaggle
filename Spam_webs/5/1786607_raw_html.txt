             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               For the second time in one week, a video has shed new light on a fatal police shooting, raising questions about the use of deadly force by police against minorities in the United States.
              </p>
              <p>
               Earlier this month, Eric Harris was shot and killed by a white police officer in Tulsa, Oklahoma, after allegedly attempting to illegally sell a pistol to an undercover police officer. The officer claims he accidentally fired his handgun at Harris, confusing it for his taser.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/04/12/north-charleston-people-government/">
                 In North Charleston, the people's bond with their government is broken
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               While pinned to the ground, Harris can be heard saying, "I'm losing my breath."
              </p>
              <p>
               One of the officers replies, "F*ck your breath." Harris later died.
              </p>
              <p>
               The video, which
               <a href="http://www.tulsaworld.com/news/crimewatch/family-of-man-killed-by-undercover-reserve-deputy-asks-sheriff/article_e1921f2b-c4a1-523a-a38c-8e182cd4d338.html" target="_blank">
                Harris' family pressed the Tulsa Police Department to release
               </a>
               , has reignited outrage over police brutality towards minorities. The clip was first released by police on Friday. A raw version of the footage, posted by
               <em>
                New York Daily News
               </em>
               , can be seen, below:
              </p>
              <p>
               <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/FEhhcgFf5RQ?enablejsapi=1&amp;" width="640">
               </iframe>
              </p>
              <p>
               The Tulsa County Sheriff's Office told reporters on Friday that 73-year-old reserve deputy Robert Charles Bates fatally shot Harris during an undercover operation on April 2, after pulling out his service weapon instead of a stun gun.
              </p>
              <p>
               Bates is an insurance company executive assigned to the Violent Crimes Task Force. The sheriff's office said Bates spent time with the Tulsa Auxiliary Police, and is a former full-time officer.
              </p>
              <p>
               Maj. Shannon Clark of the sheriff's office said Harris met with an undercover officer to sell him a pistol and ammunition, ran when deputies tried to arrest him, then fought with another deputy while he was shot.
              </p>
              <p>
               The sheriff's office released
               <a href="http://www.tulsaworld.com/news/crimewatch/reserve-deputy-who-shot-killed-man-thought-he-drew-his/article_67394595-5b09-59d1-a791-ae543e4cfcd1.html" target="_blank">
                a statement
               </a>
               two days after the incident, saying Harris had struggled when a deputy tried to restrain him.
              </p>
              <p>
               "During the rapidly evolving altercation, the reserve deputy had what he believed was his taser from his tactical carrier and attempted to render aid in subduing the suspect. Initial reports have determined that the reserve deputy was attempting to use less lethal force, believing he was utilizing a taser, when he inadvertently discharged his service weapon, firing one round which struck Harris," the statement said.
              </p>
              <p>
               Reaction on social media to the newly released video was swift, with many shocked by the initial response of the officers after Harris was shot and was pinned to the ground.
              </p>

                <br/>

                Seeking help. Shot.
                <br/>
                Sleeping. Shot.
               </p>
               <p>
                What can black folks do?
               </p>
               <p>
                 You'on Luh God? (@Pants_So_Short)

               </p>
