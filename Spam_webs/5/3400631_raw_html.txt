        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Painted Vinyl/Linoleum Floors
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         Ok, so the product isnt technically meant to stick to vinyl or linoleum floors, but over the weekend, I decided that I was so sick of staring at the
         <a href="http://www.uglyducklinghouse.com/2010/08/bathroom-floor.html">
          hideous floor
         </a>
         in my
         <a href="http://www.uglyducklinghouse.com/2011/01/bathroom-tour.html">
          master bathroom
         </a>
         that I simply couldnt take it anymore. I needed a quick fix.
        </p>
        <div>
         <img alt="How to paint linoleum floors" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="512" src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/-cnfzTvGIuis/UFCl9dA0VJI/AAAAAAAAEII/BoPUXdSiVq4/s640/paint%2520linoleum%2520floors.png" style="border: 0px;" title="How to paint linoleum floors" width="384"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Since I had the paint around anyway for my plans to paint the laminate countertops in both the master and guest bathrooms (to complete the
         <a href="http://www.uglyducklinghouse.com/2011/03/bathroom-changes-using-rustoleum.html">
          cabinet painting project
         </a>
         I started two weeks ago), I figured hey, why stop there?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="640" src="//lh4.googleusercontent.com/_3uy1nrLi_Vw/TVizDR-WcwI/AAAAAAAAAx4/bhMDsRD_K5Q/s640/IMG_8937.JPG" style="border: 0px;" width="480"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Youve heard me talk about the products before when I
         <a href="http://www.uglyducklinghouse.com/2011/02/i-painted-my-kitchen-countertops.html">
          painted my kitchens laminate countertops
         </a>
         last month, but Rustoleum makes two completely different countertop painting kits  one is
         <a class="external" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B006Z6AQK2/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B006Z6AQK2&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theuglducho09-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Rustoleum Countertop Transformations Kit">
          an actual kit
         </a>
         which contains about sixteen different components to turn a laminate countertop into a painted laminate countertop with flecks of some kind of material on top  I am guessing, but its probably confetti. And the kits cost (roughly $250)seems to be just short of what it would cost to actually replace the countertop with butcher block from IKEA. In other words, Id rather spend mytwo hundredplusbones on an actual new counter rather than just slop paint on the old one and toss confetti on top. The alternative, also made by Rustoleum, is
         <a class="external" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003NEM98Q/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B003NEM98Q&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theuglducho09-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Rustoleum Countertop Coating">
          a simple $20 can of paint
         </a>
         that is supposed to stick to laminate and Ive already tested it out in my kitchen. So far, it seems to be bonding pretty well, so I spent another twenty bucks on a new color to try out in the bathroom (the original gray was a little too blue, so I wanted to test out a muddier, darker color in the bathrooms in hopes it would be a more tolerable color during the months Ill be saving up for full countertop replacement).
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets face it; bathrooms are places where you clean yourself, but when it comes to cleaning the room itself, its nothing short of total ick. But, it must be done. So, after getting down on my hands and knees and scrubbing behind a thirty-year-old toilet (one that I plan to replace ultimately during the big bathroom reno TBD), I decided it was the perfect time to test out the paints abilities on the floor. If the paint sticks to laminate, I figured it might have a similar bonding experience with the newly cleaned, albeit hideous vinyl/linoleum.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="507" src="//lh4.googleusercontent.com/_3uy1nrLi_Vw/TX2P4Yf1K1I/AAAAAAAAA8I/R1ZQ0TXz8hQ/s640/IMG_9258.JPG" style="border: 0px;" width="380"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         When it comes to painted surfaces, you want the paint and the surface to bond like gum to hot asphalt. So, after giving the floor a good general cleaning, I did a once-over with
         <a class="external" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000I1TJW6/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000I1TJW6&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theuglducho09-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="TSP substitute">
          TSP substitute
         </a>
         (which is what Id recommend you do before painting any surface  it degreases and deglosses to help the paint stick better, and doesnt require pre-rinsing before applying paint).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" src="//lh3.googleusercontent.com/_3uy1nrLi_Vw/TX2P3r9P4AI/AAAAAAAAA8E/pqGmtBhfed4/s640/IMG_9256.JPG" style="border: 0px;" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Next up was putting painters tape where I didnt want the paint to stick, such as on the bedroom carpet transition strip and around the ol toilet. Even thoughallof it (minus the carpet) isgoing to be replaced later this year (I hope), I didnt want it looking too sloppy in the interim.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="400" src="//lh3.googleusercontent.com/_3uy1nrLi_Vw/TX2P28gibpI/AAAAAAAAA8A/JMNE4OmUFRE/s400/IMG_9254.JPG" style="border: 0px;" width="300"/>
         <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="400" src="//lh3.googleusercontent.com/_3uy1nrLi_Vw/TX2P5ZQwrCI/AAAAAAAAA8M/BB2ojVC26MY/s400/IMG_9268.JPG" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Next,it was time to don the safety gear and prep for paint application. And while I usually dont bother when it comes to the usual low-VOC latex (I use BEHR for just about everything), this stuff is super strong-smelling and requires both gloves and a breathing mask. I had a simple breathing mask on hand, but if I had to do it over again, I recommend something a little heavier such as a
         <a class="external" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001G8WRLU/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001G8WRLU&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theuglducho09-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="3m Painting Respirator">
          charcoal filtered mask
         </a>
         . Seriously folks, these fumes arent kidding around (and of course be sure to protect yourself as much as possible when handling any kind of harmful chemicals).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="640" src="//lh6.googleusercontent.com/_3uy1nrLi_Vw/TX2P6NmQVUI/AAAAAAAAA8Q/aBcsL4aeH7s/s640/IMG_9274.JPG" style="border: 0px;" width="480"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The paint separates rather easliy in the can, so stirring the paint prior to application is key. It only takes a few minutes to get a smooth color consistency, so taking the extra time here will save you a lot of re-application time (and the added frustration)later.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whether you choose a foam brush or foam roller (I used both for cutting in and the larger areas), you want a smooth finish and a thick application (so you dont have to do a second coat and wait a week to start using the bathroom again). And remember to start in the corner furthest away from the door and work your way out of the room! I have the luxury (ironic?) of having a small enough bathroom that I didnt have to worry too much about trapping myself in, but take your time when planning your brush strokes. And get everything you need out of the room before you ever dip the brush into the can  trust me, its much easier to get to your toothpaste when you dont have to do the splits at cabinet level.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="painted linoleum floors" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="375" src="//lh6.googleusercontent.com/_3uy1nrLi_Vw/TX2P619R5tI/AAAAAAAAA8U/xZBMC7Sjw6A/s640/IMG_9280.JPG" style="border: 0px;" title="painted linoleum floors" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Once the last of the floor was covered, I made sure the window was open and closed the bathroom door. Since its right next to the master bedroom (where we sleep of course), I wanted to make sure that the night would be warm enough to leave the windows open and let the paint properly dry without destroying our brain cells as we slept. The open windows aired out the fumes rapidly and we were able to sleep normally. We still had to shower and get ready for bed in the guest bathroom, which I didnt realize until now how much it bothered Scott  I guess he has a thing for the extra large showerhead we have in the master and hates the low-flow one in the guest room.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="375" src="//lh3.googleusercontent.com/_3uy1nrLi_Vw/TX2P7cGcNQI/AAAAAAAAA8Y/04m4U9itMyA/s640/IMG_9282.JPG" style="border: 0px;" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         In my opinion, three days of guest bathroom use is a small price to pay for a much-improved floor. While the honeycomb pattern is still visible due the texture (see above), I am contemplating adding a pattern like chevron stripes or a stencil print just for funsies.
        </p>
        <p>
         The photos in these pics are still as the paint was wet (and as usual, at night), but Ill take more later this week to show you the finished look and sheen. For only $20 though, this product is quickly climbing the charts as one of my faves. If only it werent so toxic, Id use it for just about everything.
        </p>
        <h5>
         UPDATED
        </h5>
        <p>

         </a>
         too. Want to see how they both held up? Click

        </p>

I used American Pride/Mystic primer  works great for anything and then however you want it to look after that  (always no VOC paint)  sponge painting with natural colors works real well for a stone look.
