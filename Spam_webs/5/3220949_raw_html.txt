                  <h1>
                   The Simpsons Take on Burning Man
                  </h1>
                  <div class="author-date">
                   <span class="date">
                    Nov 12, 2014
                   </span>
                   <span class="author">
                    / Alexander Dias
                   </span>
                  </div>
                  <div class="content">
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" src="//cdn-insomniac.s3.amazonaws.com/images/news/thesimpsonstakeonburningman_653x367.jpg" style="width: 653px; height: 367px;"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    The Simpsons are going to Burning Man!
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Well give you a second to collect yourselves.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    The synopsis of the episode goes something like this: Bart gets a new teacher, played by Willem Dafoe, who is a spirit-crushing bully. Bart schemes to bring him down, and somewhere along the way, the family takes a trip to Burning Man. While we arent entirely sure how that plays into the plot, we are sure that the writers will do a great job of making it work in only the most hilarious way.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    How on earth did
                    <em>
                     The Simpsons
                    </em>
                    manage to incorporate Burning Man into an episode? Well, besides the fact that for 25 years, the show has managed to lampoon every bit of pop culture, show director David Silverman is a longtime burner. He hinted at this one back in August, while at the Burn.
                   </p>
                   <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en" xml:lang="en">
                    <p>
                     Missed the Marathon here at Burning Man, poor tv reception and all -- Simpsons go to BM 11/16/14 fyi --
                     <a href="//t.co/CovOAZyHwT">
                      pic.twitter.com/CovOAZyHwT
                     </a>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      David Silverman (@tubatron)
                     <a href="https://twitter.com/tubatron/status/504653275602223104">
                      August 27, 2014
                     </a>
                    </p>
                   </blockquote>
                   <script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js">
                   </script>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" src="//cdn-insomniac.s3.amazonaws.com/images/news/thesimpsonstakeonburningman_margehomer_653x367.jpg" style="width: 653px; height: 367px;"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Joining our favorite four-fingered, dysfunctional, yellow family will be a couple of Black Rock Citys iconic art cars,
                    <em>
                     <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Dusty-Rhino/1471181606494475" target="_blank">
                      The Dusty Rhino
                     </a>
                    </em>
                    and
                    <a href="https://www.facebook.com/ElPulpoMecanico" target="_blank">
                     <em>
                      El Pulpo Mecanico
                     </em>
                    </a>
                    . Judging from the promo images posted by Fox, this promises to be a wild and hilarious show, with Homer getting into some trouble (DOH!). Buy it cant be any worse than that time he ate those insanity peppers.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     The Simpsons
                    </em>
                    Burning Man episode airs this Sunday, November 16, on Fox. Check your local TV time listings.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" src="//cdn-insomniac.s3.amazonaws.com/images/news/thesimpsonstakeonburningman_margehomerday_653x367.jpg" style="width: 653px; height: 367px;"/>
