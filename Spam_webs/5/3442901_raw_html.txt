              <span class="field-content">
               <h1>
                Vote on the Army's New Service Handgun
               </h1>
              </span>
             </div>
             <div class="views-field views-field-field-people">
              <div class="field-content">
               by
               <a href="/people/david-e-petzal">
                David E. Petzal
               </a>

          <!--&lt;/div&gt;-->

         <div class="panel-pane pane-block pane-post-social-post-social-block">

           </a>
           <span class="pw-counter-custom1">
            <span class="pw-button-counter_custom1">
             <b class="customCounter" style="font-weight: normal!important;">
              0
             </b>
            </span>

          </div>
          <script src="http://i.po.st/static/v3/post-widget.js#publisherKey=4monpeu0unshpr5v936p" type="text/javascript">

         </div>
         <div class="panel-pane pane-entity-field pane-node-body">
          <!--&lt;div class="pane-content"&gt;-->
          <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
           <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
             <p>
              Attention to orders.
              <br/>
              Subject: Voting for new Army sidearm
              <br/>
              To: All interested readers
             </p>
             <p>
              The
              <a href="http://www.fieldandstream.com/blogs/the-gun-nuts/how-you-can-help-the-army-pick-a-new-handgun">
               first post on this subject
              </a>
              provided some excellent nominations. Some of the nominations, however, were from people who obviously deserved to be beaten about the head and shoulders with one of the terrifically cool batons sold by Arizona Ironwood. There were 14 finalists, but, to simplify matters, I have combined some entries. For example, we had nominations for the Colt 1911, the Kimber 1911, and the Springfield Armory 1911. All of these are essentially the same handgun, so Ive simply put it as Model 1911 .45ACP and omitted the manufacturer.
             </p>
             <p>
              In the case of SIG/Sauer, we had the SIG P220, P226, and P227. Though they are similar, theyre not identical, so Ive listed all three. Same with the Glock 17 and 21.
             </p>
             <p>
              Carry on.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               M9 Beretta
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <em>
               9mm. DA/SA.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298116 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" data-src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/M9-pistolet_0.jpg?itok=_Xry_MPJ" height="456" src="/sites/fieldandstream.com/themes/bonnier_theme/images/placeholder.gif" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
             </p>
             <div class="no-script-image">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298116 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" height="456" src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/M9-pistolet_0.jpg?itok=_Xry_MPJ" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <p>
              No one quite understood why this one was selected in 1985. The best you can say about it is that its not bad enough to provoke a Congressional investigation, and is chambered for a cartridge the FBI had deemed inadequate for its agents.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Model 1911
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <em>
               .45ACP. SA.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298104 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" data-src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/_1911.png?itok=vhcx_6hC" height="426" src="/sites/fieldandstream.com/themes/bonnier_theme/images/placeholder.gif" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
             </p>
             <div class="no-script-image">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298104 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" height="426" src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/_1911.png?itok=vhcx_6hC" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <p>
              Based on its 70-year-plus record, the best service pistol ever, and the most effective pistol round. There are plain 1911s and fancy 1911s. For our purposes consider a plain one with an accessory rail and Tritium sights.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               SIG/Sauer P220
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <em>
               .45 ACP. DA/SA.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298118 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" data-src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/sigp220.png?itok=tz4oScmc" height="392" src="/sites/fieldandstream.com/themes/bonnier_theme/images/placeholder.gif" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
             </p>
             <div class="no-script-image">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298118 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" height="392" src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/sigp220.png?itok=tz4oScmc" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <p>
              Vintage of 1976, a very modern handgun with all the necessary bells and whistles.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               SIG/Sauer P226
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <em>
               .40 S&amp;W. DA/SA
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298120 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" data-src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/sigp226_0.png?itok=PxerQOsf" height="402" src="/sites/fieldandstream.com/themes/bonnier_theme/images/placeholder.gif" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
             </p>
             <div class="no-script-image">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298120 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" height="402" src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/sigp226_0.png?itok=PxerQOsf" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <p>
              Carried by the SEALs and Texas Rangers, among others.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               SIG/Sauer P227
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <em>
               .45ACP. DA/SA.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298121 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" data-src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/sigp227.png?itok=GJqpRLlx" height="398" src="/sites/fieldandstream.com/themes/bonnier_theme/images/placeholder.gif" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
             </p>
             <div class="no-script-image">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298121 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" height="398" src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/sigp227.png?itok=GJqpRLlx" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <p>
              Newest of the SIGs, this one holds an extra-long magazine that packs 14-plus rounds. Despite this, the grip is slim enough to be held by human hands.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Glock 17
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <em>
               9mm.
              </em>
              <em style="line-height: 20.7999992370605px;">
               DA (Striker Fire).
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298111 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" data-src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/_glock17.jpg?itok=2JcoRjEJ" height="438" src="/sites/fieldandstream.com/themes/bonnier_theme/images/placeholder.gif" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
             </p>
             <div class="no-script-image">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298111 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" height="438" src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/_glock17.jpg?itok=2JcoRjEJ" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <p>
              The most widely used law-enforcement handgun in the world, very easy to learn to shoot and to maintain. Very reliable, and now in its fourth generation. On the negative side its a 9mm, and is the choice of many members of the New York City Police Department.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Glock 21
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <em>
               .45ACP.
              </em>
              <em style="line-height: 20.7999992370605px;">
               DA (Striker Fire).
              </em>
              <em>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298112 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" data-src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/_glock21.jpg?itok=Mo-ITDrx" height="456" src="/sites/fieldandstream.com/themes/bonnier_theme/images/placeholder.gif" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
             </p>
             <div class="no-script-image">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298112 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" height="456" src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/_glock21.jpg?itok=Mo-ITDrx" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <p>
              Nearly the same as the Glock 17, except chambered for an effective cartridge. Not authorized by the NYPD, which is a major endorsement.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               HK 45
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <em>
               45ACP. DA/SA.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298124 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" data-src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/newhk.jpg?itok=m8tt4QYJ" height="467" src="/sites/fieldandstream.com/themes/bonnier_theme/images/placeholder.gif" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
             </p>
             <div class="no-script-image">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298124 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" height="467" src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/newhk.jpg?itok=m8tt4QYJ" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="680"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <p>
              Originally designed to replace the M9 under the Joint Combat Pistol Program, which was suspended in 2006. Figure that one out. Lots of bells and whistles, including a threaded barrel extension on which to screw a suppressor. Lots of attention paid to ergonomics. Made in New Hampshire.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Smith &amp; Wesson M&amp;P
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <em>
               .40 and .45ACP. DA (Striker Fire).
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298123 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" data-src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/news%26w.png?itok=iArW6kQE" height="405" src="/sites/fieldandstream.com/themes/bonnier_theme/images/placeholder.gif" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="598"/>
             </p>
             <div class="no-script-image">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" class="media-element file-default attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__298123 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__title__ attr__data-image_style__article_image_full" data-image_style="article_image_full" height="405" src="http://www.fieldandstream.com/sites/fieldandstream.com/files/styles/article_image_full/public/news%26w.png?itok=iArW6kQE" title="" typeof="foaf:Image" width="598"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <p>
              Very recent design [2005] intended for law enforcement. Comes in a bewildering number of variations and calibers. Compact, light, and reliable.
             </p>
