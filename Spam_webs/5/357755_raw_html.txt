     <p>
      <b>
       Heffer Wolfe
      </b>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Confidential_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Confidential-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">
       fictional character
      </a>
      on the cartoon
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Rocko%27s_Modern_Life" title="Rocko's Modern Life">
        Rocko's Modern Life
       </a>
      </i>
      and the comic book series of the same name.
      <a href="/wiki/Tom_Kenny" title="Tom Kenny">
       Tom Kenny
      </a>
      provided the voice of the anthropomorphic steer. His catchphrase, which can be heard in the series' opening credits, is "That was a hoot!"
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Conception_and_development">
       Conception and development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heffer_Wolfe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Conception and development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Joe_Murray_(animator)" title="Joe Murray (animator)">
       Joe Murray
      </a>
      , creator of
      <i>
       Rocko's Modern Life
      </i>
      , partially based Heffer on an adopted friend who enjoyed
      <a href="/wiki/Bologna_sausage" title="Bologna sausage">
       bologna
      </a>
      sandwiches and "had an interesting take on life."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CharMuseum_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-CharMuseum-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Heffer first appeared on an ID spot aired on
      <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">
       MTV
      </a>
      in 1989; the ID spot depicts Heffer as flying out of a television with the MTV logo branded on his
      <a href="/wiki/Buttocks" title="Buttocks">
       buttocks
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Murray_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Murray-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tom_Kenny_in_2009.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="482" data-file-width="469" height="226" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Tom_Kenny_in_2009.jpg/220px-Tom_Kenny_in_2009.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Tom_Kenny_in_2009.jpg/330px-Tom_Kenny_in_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Tom_Kenny_in_2009.jpg/440px-Tom_Kenny_in_2009.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tom_Kenny_in_2009.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Tom_Kenny" title="Tom Kenny">
         Tom Kenny
        </a>
        voiced Heffer and was chosen for the role in a large casting call in
        <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
         Los Angeles
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Murray originally wrote "Sucker for the Suck-O-Matic" as the pilot episode; the executives decided that Heffer would be "a little too weird for test audiences." Murray, instead of removing Heffer from "Sucker for the Suck-O-Matic," decided to write "Trash-O-Madness," an episode without Heffer, as the pilot episode.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Murray_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Murray-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Originally, Murray did not include any information about Heffer's origins in his pitch to Nickelodeon. Vince Calandra wrote the Heffer's origin plotline in Season 1.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MaestriDigitalCharAnimation3_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-MaestriDigitalCharAnimation3-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Murray auditioned
      <a href="/wiki/Tom_Kenny" title="Tom Kenny">
       Tom Kenny
      </a>
      in a large casting call in
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
       Los Angeles
      </a>
      and chose him as the voice actor for Heffer.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Murray_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Murray-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Kenny based Heffer's voice on the voice of a nephew of his; at the time the nephew was a teenager.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The boy, who was 13 when the Heffer voice started, was the son of Kenny's brother. Kenny said that the voice was "[n]ot dead on, but some of his quirks I incorporated into the Heffer audition."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Peitzman_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Peitzman-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Jeff_%22Swampy%22_Marsh" title='Jeff "Swampy" Marsh'>
       Jeff "Swampy" Marsh
      </a>
      , a storyboard writer, says that Heffer's right eye and left nostril are "notched at the bottom" due to Murray's design style. Marsh added that the animators found keeping the sides straight "a little tricky at first" and that they referred to the design as "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tombstone" title="Tombstone">
       Tombstone
      </a>
      -shaped."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Character">
       Character
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heffer_Wolfe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Character">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Best friend of
      <a href="/wiki/Rocko" title="Rocko">
       Rocko
      </a>
      , Heffer was raised by wolves in O-Townas seen by his
      <a href="/wiki/Family_name" title="Family name">
       family name
      </a>
      who originally were going to eat him, and thus began to fatten him. Eventually the family grew to love Heffer, so they instead raised him, not letting him know that he was adopted until Rocko accidentally revealed the truth when the Wolfe family invited him for dinner. The mark which Heffer believed to be a "
      <a href="/wiki/Birthmark" title="Birthmark">
       birthmark
      </a>
      " on his buttocks is actually the plotting lines showing where the wolves were going to divide him.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:139px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HefferInComic.PNG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="209" data-file-width="137" height="209" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/f/fa/HefferInComic.PNG" width="137"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HefferInComic.PNG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Heffer Wolfe in the
        <i>
         Rocko's Modern Life
        </i>
        comic book
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The Nickelodeon UK page describes Heffer as a "devoted friend" who "loves life."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Heffer's lack of fear mostly stems from stupidity instead of genuine bravery.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aus2_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Aus2-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_South_East_Asia" title="Nickelodeon South East Asia">
       Nickelodeon South East Asia
      </a>
      website describes him as an "overgrown slob."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SEAsiaProfile_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-SEAsiaProfile-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Despite being obese, he can drive; unlike Rocko he does not wear
      <a href="/wiki/Seat_belt" title="Seat belt">
       seat belts
      </a>
      , mostly because the seat belts do not extend enough for him to wear them comfortably. Also he seems to be a hopeless romantic at times as mentioned in "Sugar Frosted Frights," where he asks Filburt's shell while in an eight-ball mode if he will ever find true love.
     </p>
     <p>
      Heffer holds several jobs: tree farmer, greenskeeper at a golf course, mail carrier, security guard, waiter at a caf, and cashier. Despite his size, he can be very nimble at times, most notably when he is roller-skating. At the local skating rink, he is known as "The King," and performs his own skating routine at the request of the patrons.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Heffer's large size often has him hungry or eating. Another
      <a href="/wiki/Running_gag" title="Running gag">
       running gag
      </a>
      is Heffer being called a cow, which is usually corrected by him or his father calling Heffer a
      <b>
       steer
      </b>
      . Heffer's name is taken from the word "
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/heifer" title="wikt:heifer">
       heifer
      </a>
      ".
     </p>
     <p>
      It is revealed in an episode (Specifically the episode 'Who's for Dinner?', where Heffer's "birthmark" is also revealed) that Heffer's biological father is still alive with a girl cow named Joyce living in a trailer in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canoga_Park,_Los_Angeles,_California" title="Canoga Park, Los Angeles, California">
       Canoga Park
      </a>
      section of the
      <a href="/wiki/San_Fernando_Valley" title="San Fernando Valley">
       San Fernando Valley
      </a>
      region of
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
       Los Angeles
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CharMuseum_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-CharMuseum-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, Heffer's father seems to have very little patience for his son, even to the point of dropping the short-tempered bombshell that "your mother's a car seat in
      <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">
       Illinois
      </a>
      ." This seems to stem from his claim that he's had dozens of children like Heffer who always come seeking his affection
     </p>
