      <div class="lead item_left">
       Our cool technology is "reshaping solar energy" in a very literal way: Most of today's solar energy systems take the form of flat panels or heavy mirrors or metal boxes-with-lenses and require significant amounts of heavy, expensive materials. Our inflated solar concentrators, on the other hand, are primarily made of inexpensive and free materials. This design approach radically reduces material requirements as well cost and time.

    <div class="content item_right">
     <div class="view view-cpv-product view-id-cpv_product view-display-id-block_1 container view-dom-id-21265a8fc2651c8ba944a83851d43047">

        <div class="views-field views-field-field-header-photo">
         <div class="field-content">

        <div class="views-field views-field-body">
         <div class="field-content">
          <div style="text-align:center;">
           <h2>
            Our Inflated Solar Concentrator
           </h2>
           <p>
            Solar Concentrators Focus the Sun. Our inflated, tube-shaped concentrators are key to Cool Earth's innovative design. Each 3 foot diameter concentrator is made of plastic film - similar to that used commercially for packaging and shipping. When inflated with air, the concentrator naturally forms a shape that focuses or concentrates sunlight onto a PV cell placed at the focal point. This means we need fewer solar cells and other more expensive materials to produce a lot more electricity. In fact, a single cell in our concentrator generates up to 30 times the electricity of a solar cell without a concentrator.
           </p>
           <p>
            The inflated structure is naturally strongstrong enough to support a person's weightand aerodynamically stable, able to withstand high wind speeds. Finally, the inflated tube protects the PV cell and receiver from the environment, including rain and snow, as well as insects and dirt.
           </p>
           <p>
            Each inflated concentrator includes, in addition to the solar cell receiver, a small air pump for maintaining air pressure and a simple heat sink to handle the concentrated energy from the sun.
           </p>
           <h3>
            A Support System Holds It All in Place.
           </h3>
           <p>
            The concentrators are so light and aerodynamic that they require a equally light weight and inexpensive supporting structure and solar tracker. The resulting system uses a minimum amount of material, has a small footprint, and causes the least disruption to the natural environment of any solar power plant.
           </p>
           <h3>
            And, That's It!
           </h3>
           <p>
            All in all, a simple, elegant and cool solution.
           </p>

           <div class="faq">
            <h2>
             FAQ
            </h2>
            <div class="col col-lg-12">
             <!-- faq1
	======================================== -->
             <div class="accordion" id="faq1">
              <div class="accordion-group">
               <!-- div.accordion-heading&gt;a.accordion-toggle.collapsed[data-toggle="collapse"] -->
               <!-- div.accordion-body.collapse&gt;div.accordion-inner -->
               <div class="accordion-heading">
                <a class="accordion-toggle collapsed" data-toggle="collapse" href="#answer1">
                 <span class="fa fa-plus ">
                 </span>
                 How does your system work and how does it differ from other CPV systems?
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="accordion-body collapse" id="answer1">
                <div class="accordion-inner">
                 Our system works like a conventional concentrated photovoltaic cell (CPV) system, but we take an unconventional approach to forming our ultralight optics and tracking the sun. Instead of using rigid aluminum or glass structures to focus light, we use very thin plastic films. And, instead of using ribs, trusswork, or material heft to maintain the optic concentrator shape, we use active inflation of air. Our concentrators are firmly attached to our own proprietary tracking system to track the daily and seasonal passage of the Sun.
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="accordion-group">
               <div class="accordion-heading">
                <a class="accordion-toggle collapsed" data-toggle="collapse" href="#answer2">
                 <span class="fa fa-plus">
                 </span>
                 Why are your solar concentrators inflatable?
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="accordion-body collapse" id="answer2">
                <div class="accordion-inner">
                 Serendipitously, inflation air allows us to make an effective concentrator from nothing but thin plastic films. The inflated structure is lightweight, strong enough to survive high winds, and designed so the concentrator can produce electricity in winds up to 40 mph. We optimize the optical properties of the concentrator by actively controlling its inflation. The inflated tube also forms a protective barrier around our PV receivers and cells.
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="accordion-group">
               <div class="accordion-heading">
                <a class="accordion-toggle collapsed" data-toggle="collapse" href="#answer3">
                 <span class="fa fa-plus">
                 </span>
                 Do you have a way to store energy for later use?
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="accordion-body collapse" id="answer3">
                <div class="accordion-inner">
                 The amount of electricity generated by our systems is already highly correlated to peak electric demand load. This means the electricity is available when it is needed the most and is, therefore, immediately used, so storage is not often an issue. Taking a larger view, solar presently serves less than 1 percent of the world's energy needs. Solar needs to be serving well over 50 percent of the world's needs before storage is a significant global concern.
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="accordion-group">
               <div class="accordion-heading">
                <a class="accordion-toggle collapsed" data-toggle="collapse" href="#answer4">
                 <span class="fa fa-plus">
                 </span>
                 Can I install your system onto my rooftop? My home? A commercial building?
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="accordion-body collapse" id="answer4">
                <div class="accordion-inner">
                 Our system is designed to only be installed on the ground. We are currently in the prototype and pilot plant stages, but after our system is fully certified, we plan to make it available for commercial and industrial use.
                </div>
