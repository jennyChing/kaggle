           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             <span class="large-cap">
              I
             </span>
             have been wanting to make some ankle bells for my sons for ages. So, armed with a groovy ball of wool and some bells I set to work. At the end of it I was pretty chuffed; My first knitting project was complete and I was surrounded by two foot stomping, jingly-jangly boys to boot!
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Homemade musical instruments: Ankle bells" class="alignleft" height="589" src="http://www.minieco.co.uk/images/aug10/ankle-bells-1.jpg" title="Homemade musical instruments: Ankle bells" width="570"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             This project is especially great if you are just learning to knit (like me) as its dead easy and very quick  compared to knitting a cardigan or jumper anyway!
            </p>
            <p>
             If knitting is not your thing then you can attach some bells to a felt strip and put a velcro fastening at either end.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Homemade musical instruments: Ankle bells" class="alignleft" src="http://www.minieco.co.uk/images/aug10/ankle-bells-2.jpg" title="Homemade musical instruments: Ankle bells" width="570"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Click below to read the how-to. In the meantime I would love to here from you if you have other ideas for simple kids knitting projects.
            </p>
            <p>
             <span id="more-1301">
             </span>
            </p>
            <h3>
             How to
            </h3>
            <p>
             To make your ankle bells you will need:
            </p>
            <p>
              Ball of wool
             <br/>
              Knitting needles
             <br/>
              Jingle bells (1.8mm)  I used five for each cuff
             <br/>
              Needle
            </p>
            <p>
             1) To begin with you need to create a cuff to sew the bells onto. If you are a complete newbie to knitting then check out this pdf from
             <a href="http://www.the-little-experience.com/web_site/pdfs/free_pages/how_to_knit.pdf">
              The Little Experience
             </a>
             . Its very helpful. I also used
             <a href="http://www.knitsimplemag.com/node/7">
              this article at knitsimplemag
             </a>
             . Its very useful and explains how to knit cuffs.
            </p>
            <p>
             Cast on a multiple of 4 stitches plus 2 extra  I cast on 36 stitches but you may need more or less depending on the size of your little ones ankles  my little boy is pretty skinny in the leg department!
             <br/>
             Row 1 (right side) Knit two, *purl two, knit two; repeat from * to end.
             <br/>
             Row 2 Purl two, knit two, purl two; repeat from * to end.
             <br/>
             Repeat rows 1 and 2 until your cuff is about 2 inches wide.
             <br/>
             Cast off
            </p>
            <p>
             2) Sew the sides together
            </p>
            <p>
             3) Attach 5 bells at regular intervals around the cuff and Bobs your uncle!
            </p>
            <p>
             WARNINGS: Please remember to attach the bells securely and make sure you supervise little ones as the bells could be a choking hazard.
            </p>

                 I know this is a really old thread  but I thought Id chime in here for the sake of someone coming in for the first time {these have been on my to-do list for.ever!}. Acrylic yarn doesnt have the memory that wool yarn will have so will not stretch back into shape and will, in short order, be more stretched out than youd probably want. Stick to wool if you can. Otherwise make the acrylic somewhat smaller to make up for the fact that they will stretch and stay more stretched than wool.
