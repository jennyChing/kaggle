       <h2 class="entry-title">
        Five Tips for Making New Years Resolutions You Can Keep
       </h2>
       <div class="post-content">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Five Tips for Making New Years Resolutions You Can Keep" data-url="http://www.amli.com/blog/five-tips-making-new-years-resolutions-can-keep/">
        </div>
        <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amli.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Five-Tips-for-Making-New-Years-Resolutions-You-Can-Keep.png">
          <img alt="Five Tips for Making New Year's Resolutions You Can Keep" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-10864" height="225" src="http://www.amli.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Five-Tips-for-Making-New-Years-Resolutions-You-Can-Keep-300x225.png" width="300"/>
         </a>
         Happy New Years Eve!
        </p>
        <p>
         As 2014 draws to a close, it is time to reflect on your year and think about changes you want to make in 2015.
        </p>
        <p>
         Do you have
         <a href="http://www.inc.com/sims-wyeth/3-best-new-year-s-resolutions-for-leaders.html" target="_blank" title="Inc. New Year's resolutions for leaders">
          goals for yourself on the work front
         </a>
         ? How about in your personal life? If you need an extra push to achieve these goals, makesome New Years resolutions to start down the right track.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you havent had much
         <a href="http://qz.com/162534/a-harvard-professor-reveals-how-to-make-new-years-resolutions-that-you-can-actually-keep/" target="_blank" title="QZ.com New Year's resolution tips Harvard professor">
          success keeping them
         </a>
         in the past, here are some tips to keep in mind as you make your New Years resolution(s) for 2015.
         <span id="more-10797">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Be Realistic
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Reaching for the stars enables us to dream big. But if youre like most people, New Years resolutions will call for changes in habits or behavior you arent exactly over-the-moon about making. Set the bar high, but also think about your past experience. If you havent carried out most of the resolutions youve made, consider why.
        </p>
        <p>
         Are youoverambitious in general? It is possible you simply have too much else to tackle to realistically fulfill your resolution?
         <a href="http://health.howstuffworks.com/mental-health/human-nature/behavior/5-resolutions-you-can-keep.htm" target="_blank" title="HowStuffWorks New Year's resolutions you can keep">
          Keep your resolutions simple
         </a>
         , yet sincere. Choose themthoughtfully and lay out a plan for how to make the changes you want in 2015.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Createa Timeline
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Have you donean excellent job staying on top of your New Years resolution for a month or two, only to see your success fizzle as the year wears on? As long as your resolutions are manageable, one of the best ways to see them throughis to think about and outline the
         <a href="http://psychcentral.com/lib/10-sure-ways-to-keep-your-new-years-resolutions/0005" target="_blank" title="PsychCentral.com tips for keeping New Year's resolution">
          steps you need to take throughout the year
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         For some, this means consistent deadlines forgoals tobe met. For example, if youre trying to get in shape and improve your fitness, try to run 100 miles each month. Others will want to hit the ground running and slow down the ferocity later on in the year. If you want to lose 25 pounds this year through a combination of
         <a href="http://www.amli.com/blog/5-healthy-eating-trends-you-should-embrace/" target="_blank" title="AMLI Residential blog healthy eating trends">
          healthy eating
         </a>
         and exercise, create a plan to work towards that goal instead of feeling like a failure come March. For example, you canshoot to lose 10 pounds by March 1, 20 pounds by July 1, and 25 pounds by the end of the year. Those who are especially worried about failing shouldstart out with smaller goals and gradually move on to larger onesas the year rolls on.
        </p>
        <p>
         No matter which method isbest for you,
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/stevemeyer/2014/11/18/your-new-years-resolutions-will-fail-again-unless-you-do-this/" target="_blank" title="Forbes New Year's resolution tips">
          make measurable goals and write them down
         </a>
         to stay ontrack.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Hold Yourself Accountable
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         If youre good at sticking to self-imposed deadlines, regularly checking your timeline may be all you need to
         <a href="http://www.gretchenrubin.com/happiness_project/2009/10/six-tips-to-hold-yourself-accountable-for-keeping-your-resolutions/" target="_blank" title="GretchenRubin.com New Year's resolution accountability">
          hold yourself accountable
         </a>
         . If you perform more effectively when someone keeps tabs on you, then do just thathave someone else help keep you ontrack. This someone could be a loved one, a personal trainer, a doctor, or someone you trust to keep tabs on your progress and call you out when youre slacking. These days, you can even take advantage of
         <a href="http://mashable.com/2010/12/29/social-media-resolutions/" target="_blank" title="Mashable tips for keeping New Year's resolution with social media">
          social media check-ins and updates
         </a>
         . You know better than anyone else what is necessary for you to stay on track. Whatever itis, just make sureit happens.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Reward Yourself
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         For a select few, knowing that desired results can be achieved if youkeep pressing onis enough to keep you on track to meet your goals. But for many of us, the knowledge the hard work we put inwill lead to better health or some other improvement in our life isnt enough. A bit of positive reinforcement can go a long way. If you crave this gratification, reward yourself each time you meet a short-term goal. Just make sure the rewards
         <a href="http://alifeofproductivity.com/resolutions/chapter11/" target="_blank" title="ALifeofProductivity.com New Year's resolution rewards">
          dont completely unravel the work
         </a>
         youve already put in.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Dont Give Up
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         As people realize they wontsuccessfully conquer their New Years resolutions, they tend to give up on them entirely. If you really want to make some changes, dont let this happen. Remind yourself a little bit of progress is better than none at all. Maybe you wont live upto the resolutions you set, but this doesnt mean you shouldnt try. Dont fall into the common trap of abandoning your New Years resolutions under the pretenses you can just try again next year. Keep trying this year!
        </p>
        <p>
         Whatever New Years resolutions you make and however you plan to go about tackling them
         <a href="http://www.amli.com/" target="_blank" title="AMLI Residential luxury apartments">
          AMLI Residential
         </a>
         wishes you the best as you work towards keeping your resolutions. We encourage you to reach for your goals, but dontlet the pursuit of them keep you from enjoying yourself and the company of your loved ones. Taking the time to indulge in your interests is important to maintaining the motivation necessary to realizing your resolutions.
        </p>
        <p>
         <i>
          What New Years resolutions are you making this year? Share in the comments!
         </i>
        </p>
