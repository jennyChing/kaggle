      <h2 class="post-title">
       Anatomy of a Comeback: How the Rangers Ousted the Penguins
      </h2>
      <div class="postmeta">
       <span class="meta-date">
        Posted on
        <a href="http://fulltiltnyr.com/anatomy-comeback-rangers-ousted-penguins/" rel="bookmark" title="12:23 pm">
         <time datetime="2014-08-18T12:23:56+00:00">
          August 18, 2014
         </time>
        </a>
       </span>
       <span class="meta-author sep">
        by
        <a href="http://fulltiltnyr.com/author/jorfthnban/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Jorge Bannister">
         Jorge Bannister
        </a>
       </span>
       <span class="meta-comments sep">
        <a href="http://fulltiltnyr.com/anatomy-comeback-rangers-ousted-penguins/#respond">
         Leave a comment
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <img alt="Lundqvist took out Crosby in game 7 in 2014 (AP Photo/Gene J. Puskar)" class="attachment-featured_image wp-post-image" src="http://i0.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/i-51.jpeg?resize=715%2C280"/>
      <div class="entry clearfix">
       <script>
        &lt;!--
dynamicgoogletags.update();
//--&gt;
       </script>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_7067" style="width: 725px">
        <a data-rel="lightbox-0" href="http://i1.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/i-11.jpeg">
         <img alt="Derick Brassard (16), left, scores during the second period of Game 6 (AP Photo/Seth Wenig)" class="size-full wp-image-7067" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/i-11.jpeg?resize=715%2C578"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Derick Brassard (16), left, scores during the second period of Game 6 (AP Photo/Seth Wenig)
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Trailing, 3-1, after four games in the second round of the Stanley Cup Playoffs, the Broadway Blueshirts looked flat after winning Game 1 against the Pittsburgh Penguins.
        <span id="more-11997">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        The Rangers needed a spark, better team defense and goaltending.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          The Spark
         </em>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Newcomer Martin St. Louis provided all the spark the Rangers needed just by showing up. The death of his mother the day before didnt stop the forward from showing up for a pivotal Game 5.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12228" style="width: 460px">
        <a data-rel="lightbox-1" href="http://i1.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/tumblr_n5fuh5ynXC1sna8pso1_500.gif">
         <img alt='"Thank You" - Marty St. Louis' class="size-full wp-image-12228" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/tumblr_n5fuh5ynXC1sna8pso1_500.gif?resize=450%2C251"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Thank You  Marty St. Louis
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        I know deep down my mother would have wanted me to play this game, St. Louis told the media after Game 5. Shed be proud of me coming here to help as much as I can.  I owed it to her to do it. I know she would want me to do it.
       </p>
       <p>
        Marc Staal said, You want to play hard for a guy like that whos coming from such a tragedy. You want to rally around it.
       </p>
       <p>
        It officially started about midway through the first period of Game 5. The Rangers were on a power play, and Derek Stepan won an offensive zone faceoff. Ryan McDonough had a clear, hard shot from the left point after Chris Kreider dove to tip it to the point, it hit hard off Marc-Andre Fleury right pad and onto the stick of Kreider who scrambled back onto his skates. Kreider buried the puck on a bad-angle wrist shot and the Rangers were up, 1-0.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12229" style="width: 980px">
        <a data-rel="lightbox-2" href="http://i0.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/s.jpg">
         <img alt="Chris Kreider scores a huge goal in game 5 (photo-Andrew Theodorakis)" class="size-full wp-image-12229" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/s.jpg?resize=860%2C574"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Chris Kreider scores a huge goal in game 5 (photo-Andrew Theodorakis)
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Derick Brassard, who scored four goals in the series, had two in Game 5. Mats Zuccarello and Benoit Pouliot each had a goal in the series, as the second line scored six of the fifteen goals for the Rangers.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          Tough Defense, Excellent Goaltending
         </em>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        While 10 different players scored for the Rangers, one name left off the scoresheet for much of the series was Sidney Crosby. The Kid had a goal in Game 3 and two assists in Game 4, but was shutout in every other game, including Game 7.
       </p>
       <p>
        Alain Vigneault ran his top two defensive pairs  Ryan McDonough-Dan Girardi and Marc Staal-Anton Stralman  for many of Crosbys shifts. And in Game 7, despite the Penguins having the last change at the whistle because of home ice, the top two pairs were on the ice for 23 of Crosbys 25 even-strength shifts. The top NHL goal scorer was shut down, especially on the power play.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_7046" style="width: 860px">
        <a data-rel="lightbox-3" href="http://i1.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/marc-staal-sidney-crosby-henrik-lundqvist-nhl-stanley-cup-playoffs-pittsburgh-penguins-new-york-rangers.jpg">
         <img alt="Marc Staal blanketed Sidney Crosby (nhl)" class="size-full wp-image-7046" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/marc-staal-sidney-crosby-henrik-lundqvist-nhl-stanley-cup-playoffs-pittsburgh-penguins-new-york-rangers.jpg?resize=850%2C566"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Marc Staal blanketed Sidney Crosby (nhl)
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        While the Penguins did have a balanced approach with the man-advantage, the Rangers coaching staff correctly planned for everything to run through Crosby. Because of the scouting, Pittsburgh was 1 for 20 on the power play, a far cry from the success in its previous series (6 for 29) against Columbus.
       </p>
       <p>
        While Henrik Lundqvist allowed four goals on 27 shots in Game 4, he allowed just three goals on 105 shots in Games 5,6 and 7.
       </p>
       <p>
        In Game 7, Lundqvist showed everyone why he is The King. With 5:55 left and a bad clear by Staal, Lundqvist fought off Lee Stempniaks tip off a shot from Matt Niskanen and denied Evgeni Malkin on the doorstep immediately after and froze the puck preserving the Rangers 2-1 lead.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_7134" style="width: 685px">
        <a data-rel="lightbox-4" href="http://i2.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/RANGERS1-master675.jpg">
         <img alt="Rangers defeat Pens in 7 (Charles Leclaire/USA Today Sports)" class="size-full wp-image-7134" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/fulltiltnyr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/RANGERS1-master675.jpg?resize=675%2C457"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Rangers defeat Pens in 7 (Charles Leclaire/USA Today Sports)
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Conclusion
         </strong>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        The Rangers needed something to rally around being down 3-1. They got that from a tragedy when Marty St. Louis mom passed. The spark was not her death but Martys response.
       </p>
       <p>
        To a man, every Ranger stepped up and followed his lead. That was good for game 5 and the opening of game 6. However, the Rangers won the series because of defense and goaltending more than anything else.
       </p>
       <p>
        Henrik Lundqvist played heroin game 7 and was the bottom line difference in the series. Coupled with the Rangers ability to shut down the games best player in Crosby  the anatomy of a comeback is clear cut.Total team effort.
       </p>
