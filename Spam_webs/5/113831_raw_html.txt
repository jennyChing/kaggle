     <p>
      <b>
       George Vernon Hudson
      </b>
      (20 April 1867  5 April 1946) was a
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
       British
      </a>
      -born
      <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">
       New Zealand
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Entomology" title="Entomology">
       entomologist
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">
       astronomer
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Born in
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">
       England
      </a>
      , Hudson was the sixth child of Charles Hudson, an artist and stained-glass window designer. By the age of 14 he had built up a collection of British insects, and had published a paper in
      <i>
       The Entomologist
      </i>
      . In 1881 Hudson moved with his father to
      <a href="/wiki/Nelson,_New_Zealand" title="Nelson, New Zealand">
       Nelson
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">
       New Zealand
      </a>
      . He worked on a
      <a href="/wiki/Farm" title="Farm">
       farm
      </a>
      , and in 1883, aged 16, he began working at the
      <a href="/wiki/Post_office" title="Post office">
       post office
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Wellington" title="Wellington">
       Wellington
      </a>
      , where he eventually became chief clerk, retiring in 1918.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Hudson was a member of the
      <a href="/wiki/1907_Sub-Antarctic_Islands_Scientific_Expedition" title="1907 Sub-Antarctic Islands Scientific Expedition">
       1907 Sub-Antarctic Islands Scientific Expedition
      </a>
      . The main aim of the expedition was to extend the magnetic survey of New Zealand by investigating
      <a href="/wiki/Auckland_Islands" title="Auckland Islands">
       Auckland
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Campbell_Island_group" title="Campbell Island group">
       Campbell islands
      </a>
      but botanical, biological and zoological surveys were also conducted. The voyage also resulted in rescue of the castaways of the shipwreck the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dundonald_(ship)" title="Dundonald (ship)">
        Dundonald
       </a>
      </i>
      in the Auckland Islands.
     </p>
     <p>
      Hudson is credited with proposing modern-day
      <a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time" title="Daylight saving time">
       daylight saving time
      </a>
      . His
      <a href="/wiki/Shift_work" title="Shift work">
       shift-work
      </a>
      job gave him leisure time to collect insects, and led him to value after-hours daylight.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNZB_Hudson_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-DNZB_Hudson-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1895, he presented a paper to the Wellington Philosophical Society proposing a two-hour daylight-saving shift,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and after considerable interest was expressed in
      <a href="/wiki/Christchurch" title="Christchurch">
       Christchurch
      </a>
      , he followed up in an 1898 paper.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Hudson's collection of
      <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">
       insects
      </a>
      , the largest in New Zealand, is housed in the
      <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_New_Zealand_Te_Papa_Tongarewa" title="Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa">
       Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Hudson's wife, Florence, died in 1935. They left one daughter. Hudson died on 5 April 1946 at his home in the
      <a href="/wiki/Wellington" title="Wellington">
       Wellington
      </a>
      suburb of
      <a href="/wiki/Karori" title="Karori">
       Karori
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNZB_Hudson_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-DNZB_Hudson-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
