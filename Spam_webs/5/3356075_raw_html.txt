        <p>
         If you work from home, your home office is the place where you spend majority of your time during the day. If this space is a complete mess with all kinds of stuff piled up on your desk, you know that its doing nothing more than hurting your productivity.
        </p>
        <p>
         Do you enjoy hunting for things under piles of paper? Arent you tired of torturing yourself when you cant find that tiny but very important thumb drive because its buried? Most of us go crazy when we cant find things and such craziness could be an everyday routine for a person working from a messy and cluttered home office.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know youve been thinking about cleaning it up but have been constantly overpowered by fear, laziness and of course, procrastination. So its time to pull up your socks and get set to rejuvenate your messy home office.
        </p>
        <ol>
         <ol>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 130%">
            <span>
             Identify your needs
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           The first step should be to identify what are the most essential materials for your work.
           <div style="margin-left:-7px;float:left;padding-right:10px;margin-top:-5px;overflow:hidden;">
            <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
            </script>
            <!-- DLM 336 Article Top -->
            <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-2684800653163841" data-ad-slot="5946187531" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
            </ins>
            <script>
             (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
            </script>
           </div>
           For example, a USB thumb drive or a microphone could be more important to you than that bulky printer sitting on your desk. So take a pen and paper and make a note of just the very essential things. Those are the things that will get placement on your desk (i.e. in plain view).
           <span style="font-size: 14px;line-height: 1.5em">
           </span>
          </li>
         </ol>
        </ol>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 130%">
           <span>
            Start with an empty room
           </span>
          </span>
          <br/>
          It will be wise to start by completely removing
          <span style="font-weight: bold">
           all
          </span>
          your belongings from the room. Once youve got an empty room you can get an idea of the vacant space and then plan the placement of different objects. Youve already sorted out the most essential objects so you can start by placing them first, nearest to your desk.
          <span style="font-size: 130%;line-height: 1.5em">
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 130%">
           <span>
            Go wireless
           </span>
          </span>
          <br/>
          Wires or cords are the most prominent contributor to clutter in a home office. Hence going wireless should take care of most of that mess. You should start by investing in a wireless keyboard and mouse and then plan to get rid of more cords or at least group them together in a way that they look neat. LifeHackers Adam Pash has a nice article on
          <a href="http://lifehacker.com/software/workspaces/hack-attack-the-cordless-workspace-sort-of-179911.php" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://lifehacker.com/software/workspaces/hack-attack-the-cordless-workspace-sort-of-179911.php']);">
           setting up a cordless workspace
          </a>
          .
          <span style="font-size: 14px;line-height: 1.5em">
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span>
           Keep the desk clean
          </span>
          <br/>
          You should try and keep the desk as clean as possible and devoid from any clutter. Organize your filing cabinet and put all the important papers and files in that. Dont let the printer or fax machine sit on your desk. Instead allocate them space below your desk . For other important stuff like thumb drives, I am hoping that your desk has a drawer attached to it and its time to make use of it. Dont let these small things occupy space on your desk, when you already have a separate space for them.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span>
           <span style="font-weight: bold">
            Go paperless
           </span>
          </span>
          <br/>
          Yes, this had to be the next obvious option. If you are dealing with lots of paper everyday then your home office is bound to turn messy again. Hence its better to go paperless and make use of the unending world of tools and apps available on the internet. What and why are you printing? Can you receive electronic bills or invoices instead of paper ones?
          <span style="font-size: 130%;line-height: 1.5em">
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 130%">
           <span>
            Use the wall space
           </span>
          </span>
          <br/>
          You could make use of the wall space beside your desk by putting things like a calendar and a small wall clock on it. Apart from making your work space look attractive, it also makes sure that you dont keep these on your desk and add to the clutter.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span>
           <span style="font-weight: bold">
            Get the perfect chair
           </span>
          </span>
          <br/>
          A
          <a href="http://freshome.com/2007/05/11/computer-chair-buying-guide-a-step-by-step-guide/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://freshome.com/2007/05/11/computer-chair-buying-guide-a-step-by-step-guide/']);">
           quality chair
          </a>
          plays an important role in the ergonomics of your home office, helps you maintain good posture, and also helps you to stay away from common health problems like back pain. Hence if you are not satisfied with your chair, you should invest some time and money and make the purchase. Read on as Jeff Atwood explains you more about
          <a href="http://www.codinghorror.com/blog/archives/000938.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.codinghorror.com/blog/archives/000938.html']);">
           workstation ergonomics
          </a>
          .
          <span style="font-size: 14px;line-height: 1.5em">
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 130%">
           <span>
            Improve the lighting
           </span>
          </span>
          <br/>
          Make way for some more sunlight to pass into your room during the day so you can keep the other lights switched off. Its healthy, energizing and also saves electricity.
          <span style="font-size: 14px;line-height: 1.5em">
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span>
           Go green
          </span>
          <br/>
          Well, youve already gone green by allowing more sun light and saving electricity during the day time. Now add some real plants to your home office and water them every day. Your room will always have a fresh odor and hence improve the work environment.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span>
           <span style="font-weight: bold">
            Keep it fresh !always !
           </span>
          </span>
          <br/>
          Okay, so were done. We have completely removed the clutter and given a new and fresh look to our beloved home office. Now, you gotta swear that youll never make a mess out of it again and keep it clean and fresh. Always !
          <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.dumblittleman.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
          <span style="font-size: 14px;line-height: 1.5em">
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Hope the above tips help you in improving your personal workspace. Do you have any more suggestions ? Do share them in comments.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cheers,
        </p>
        <p>
         Abhijeet
        </p>
