     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Hatepe eruption
      </b>
      , named for the Hatepe Plinian
      <a href="/wiki/Pumice" title="Pumice">
       pumice
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tephra_layer" title="Tephra layer">
       tephra layer
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      sometimes referred to as the
      <b>
       Taupo eruption
      </b>
      and dated to around 180
      <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">
       AD
      </a>
      , was
      <a href="/wiki/Lake_Taupo" title="Lake Taupo">
       Lake Taupo
      </a>
      's most recent major
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eruption" title="Eruption">
       eruption
      </a>
      . It is considered
      <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">
       New Zealand
      </a>
      's largest eruption during the last 20,000 years. The eruption ejected some 120km
      <sup>
       3
      </sup>
      (29cumi), a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volcanic_Explosivity_Index" title="Volcanic Explosivity Index">
       VEI
      </a>
      7 eruption,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      of which 30km
      <sup>
       3
      </sup>
      (7.2cumi) was ejected in the space of a few minutes. This makes it one of the most violent eruptions in the last 5000 years, comparable to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianchi_eruption" title="Tianchi eruption">
       Tianchi eruption
      </a>
      of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baekdu_Mountain" title="Baekdu Mountain">
       Baekdu
      </a>
      at around 1000AD and the 1815 eruption of
      <a href="/wiki/Mount_Tambora" title="Mount Tambora">
       Tambora
      </a>
      . The resulting
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ash_(volcanic)" title="Ash (volcanic)">
       ash
      </a>
      turned the sky red over
      <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">
       Rome
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">
       China
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wilson1980_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Wilson1980-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Stages_of_eruption">
       Stages of eruption
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatepe_eruption&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Stages of eruption">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The eruption went through several stages, with six distinct
      <a href="/wiki/Marker_horizon" title="Marker horizon">
       marker horizons
      </a>
      identified. Despite the uniform composition of the erupted
      <a href="/wiki/Magma" title="Magma">
       magma
      </a>
      , a wide variety of eruptive styles were displayed, including weak
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phreatomagmatism" title="Phreatomagmatism">
       phreatomagmatism
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Plinian_eruption" title="Plinian eruption">
       Plinian eruptions
      </a>
      , and a huge
      <a href="/wiki/Pyroclastic_flow" title="Pyroclastic flow">
       pyroclastic flow
      </a>
      .
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhyolitic" title="Rhyolitic">
       Rhyolitic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Lava_dome" title="Lava dome">
       lava domes
      </a>
      were extruded some years or decades later, forming the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Horomatangi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Horomatangi (page does not exist)">
       Horomatangi
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">
       reefs
      </a>
      and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Waitahanui&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Waitahanui (page does not exist)">
       Waitahanui
      </a>
      bank.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The main pyroclastic flow devastated the surrounding area, climbing over 1,500m (4,900ft) to overtop the nearby
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaimanawa_Ranges" title="Kaimanawa Ranges">
       Kaimanawa Ranges
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Mount_Tongariro" title="Mount Tongariro">
       Mount Tongariro
      </a>
      , and covering the land within 80km (50mi) with
      <a href="/wiki/Ignimbrite" title="Ignimbrite">
       ignimbrite
      </a>
      . Since New Zealand was not settled by the
      <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Mori people">
       Mori
      </a>
      until more than a thousand years later, the area had no known
      <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">
       human
      </a>
      inhabitants when the eruption occurred.
      <a href="/wiki/Tsunami_deposit" title="Tsunami deposit">
       Tsunami deposits
      </a>
      of the same age have been found on the central New Zealand coast, evidence that the eruption caused local tsunamis, but much more widespread waves may have been generated (like those observed after the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1883_Krakatoa_eruption" title="1883 Krakatoa eruption">
       1883 Krakatoa eruption
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Hatepe eruption further expanded the lake that had formed after the much larger
      <a href="/wiki/Oruanui_eruption" title="Oruanui eruption">
       Oruanui eruption
      </a>
      around 26,500 years ago. The previous outlet was blocked, raising the lake 35 metres above its present level until it broke out in a huge flood, flowing for more than a week at roughly 200 times the
      <a href="/wiki/Waikato_River" title="Waikato River">
       Waikato River
      </a>
      's current rate.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Dating_the_event">
       Dating the event
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatepe_eruption&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Dating the event">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Taupo eruption was at one time dated to approximately 130AD based on
      <span class="chemf" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon14" title="Carbon14">
        <span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.2em;font-size:70%;text-align:left">
         14
         <br/>
        </span>
        C
       </a>
      </span>
      from carbonized
      <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">
       vegetation
      </a>
      enclosed in the eruption products. However, the 22 selected samples used to obtain an average date of 1,81917 years BP (131) had much larger
      <a href="/wiki/Standard_deviation" title="Standard deviation">
       standard deviations
      </a>
      than the average date itself.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wilson1980_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Wilson1980-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Most if not all
      <a href="/wiki/Geologist" title="Geologist">
       geologists
      </a>
      now accept that the
      <a href="/wiki/Tephra" title="Tephra">
       tephra
      </a>
      or pumice fall from the eruption was far greater than previously thought, approximately 150km
      <sup>
       3
      </sup>
      (36cumi) instead of 14km
      <sup>
       3
      </sup>
      (3.4cumi). This means the event would have been visible from China and Rome. It was therefore posited by
      <a href="/wiki/Colin_J._N._Wilson" title="Colin J. N. Wilson">
       Colin J. N. Wilson
      </a>
      et al. (and is now generally accepted) that the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorological" title="Meteorological">
       meteorological
      </a>
      phenomena described by
      <a href="/wiki/Fan_Ye_(historian)" title="Fan Ye (historian)">
       Fan Ye
      </a>
      in China and by
      <a href="/wiki/Herodian" title="Herodian">
       Herodian
      </a>
      in Rome
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      were due to this eruption, which therefore can be dated exactly to 186AD.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, recent radiocarbon dating by R. Sparks has put the date at 233AD13 (95% confidence).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Humans had not settled in New Zealand at the time, and would not for another thousand years, The nearest humans at the time may have been those in Australia, more than 2,000 kilometres (1,200mi) to the west.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
