              <span style="font-family: Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; color: #444444; font-size: 12px">
               You studied it. You debated it. You forwarded it to your friends. But you never did know for sure what
               <a href="http://links.namgclubs.net/ctt?kn=19&amp;m=4615322&amp;r=NjU0ODYxODE2NQS2&amp;b=3&amp;j=MTY2NjQzMDc0S0&amp;mt=1&amp;rt=1" name="link1" target="_blank">
                the real story
               </a>
               was behind this famous pike picture, did you?
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Well, here you gothe day after Halloween seems like a good time to clear up
               <a href="http://links.namgclubs.net/ctt?kn=16&amp;m=4615322&amp;r=NjU0ODYxODE2NQS2&amp;b=3&amp;j=MTY2NjQzMDc0S0&amp;mt=1&amp;rt=1" name="link2" target="_blank">
                the mystery around this monster
               </a>
               ...
               <br/>
               <br/>
               For starters, it wasn't caught on Rainy Lake, Ontario, and it didn't attack a 36-inch pike that was already hooked, as many popular e-mail chains claimed. In truth, it wasn't even caught on this side of the Atlantic Ocean.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Dutch angler
               <a href="http://links.namgclubs.net/ctt?kn=17&amp;m=4615322&amp;r=NjU0ODYxODE2NQS2&amp;b=3&amp;j=MTY2NjQzMDc0S0&amp;mt=1&amp;rt=1" name="link3" target="_blank">
                Ewout Blom
               </a>
               caught the 42-pound pike, and spilled the beans on
               <a href="http://links.namgclubs.net/ctt?kn=12&amp;m=4615322&amp;r=NjU0ODYxODE2NQS2&amp;b=3&amp;j=MTY2NjQzMDc0S0&amp;mt=1&amp;rt=1" name="link4" target="_blank">
                all the details on a British website
               </a>
               . He re-lives the thrilling battle, mentions how long the fish measured and even reveals
               <a href="http://links.namgclubs.net/ctt?kn=13&amp;m=4615322&amp;r=NjU0ODYxODE2NQS2&amp;b=3&amp;j=MTY2NjQzMDc0S0&amp;mt=1&amp;rt=1" name="link5" target="_blank">
                which lure he was using
               </a>
               .
               <br/>
               <br/>
               He also makes the understatement of the day: "When we managed to get the fish on board, we could both see it was a fairly big fish."
              </span>
