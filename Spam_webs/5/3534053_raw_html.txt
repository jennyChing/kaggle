     <p>
      <b>
       Blueberry Hill
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">
       restaurant
      </a>
      and music club located in the
      <a href="/wiki/Delmar_Loop" title="Delmar Loop">
       Delmar Loop
      </a>
      neighborhood in
      <a href="/wiki/University_City,_Missouri" title="University City, Missouri">
       University City
      </a>
      , a suburb of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" title="St. Louis, Missouri">
       St. Louis, Missouri
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Berry" title="Chuck Berry">
       Chuck Berry
      </a>
      performs there the third Wednesday of each month, in the Duck Room.
     </p>
     <p>
      The restaurant is considered a St. Louis landmark,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      drawing tourists and locals during the day, and then becoming a popular hangout for university students in the evening, mostly from nearby
      <a href="/wiki/Washington_University_in_St._Louis" title="Washington University in St. Louis">
       Washington University
      </a>
      . The restaurant is famous for its decor, its
      <a href="/wiki/Cheeseburger" title="Cheeseburger">
       cheeseburger
      </a>
      and its
      <a href="/wiki/Darts" title="Darts">
       darts
      </a>
      room.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is frequently mentioned by St. Louis-based announcer
      <a href="/wiki/Bob_Costas" title="Bob Costas">
       Bob Costas
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Joe_Edwards_(St._Louis)" title="Joe Edwards (St. Louis)">
       Joe Edwards
      </a>
      and Linda Edwards opened Blueberry Hill on September 8, 1972. Since opening, the restaurant has expanded into the adjacent spaces on the east and the west, and it now occupies an entire block of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delmar_Boulevard_(St._Louis)" title="Delmar Boulevard (St. Louis)">
       Delmar Boulevard
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Outside the restaurant is the
      <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Walk_of_Fame" title="St. Louis Walk of Fame">
       St. Louis Walk of Fame
      </a>
      , the work of Joe Edwards. The Walk lines the sidewalks on both sides of Delmar, and is made up of bronze stars and informative biographical plaques honoring individuals from the St. Louis area who have made major national contributions to US cultural heritage.
     </p>
