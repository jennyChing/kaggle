          My Favorite Productivity Tools
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="cusdate">
         10.04.10
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">

        <p>
         <a href="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/chalkboard.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10162" height="637" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/chalkboard-430x637.jpg" title="chalkboard" width="430"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive had a couple questions recently about how I like to keep things organized, so I thought Id take a day and tell you all about it. Ready or not, here are my favorite ways for staying on top of things (or trying anyway!)
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. My mini-goals chalkboard
         </strong>
         by
         <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop/MaryKateMcDevitt" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.etsy.com']);">
          MaryKate McDevitt
         </a>
         . I like erasing what ever I wrote yesterday and having two simple goals up for today. To tell you the truth, some days even two goals seems ambitious.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Google Reader.
         </strong>
         I could not live my life without having all my favorite blogs and websites come to me each morning instead of me going to visit each of them. If you have never tried Google Reader, I will tell you truly, you are missing out.
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0klgLsSxGsU" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.youtube.com']);">
          This 3.5minute video
         </a>
         is the best explanation Ive seen and will help you understand why Google Reader is better than having a golden retriever who gets you your paper and slippers every morning.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. Gmail.
         </strong>
         Especially all the fun stuff in
         <strong>
          <a href="http://mail.google.com/support/bin/answer.py?hl=en&amp;answer=29418" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://mail.google.com']);">
           Gmail Labs
          </a>
          .
         </strong>
         You can turn on different features that the good people at Google are experimenting with in Beta. Some of them rock. Learn how to turn on Gmail Labs right
         <a href="http://mail.google.com/support/bin/answer.py?hl=en&amp;answer=143242" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://mail.google.com']);">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3.
          <a href="http://www.evernote.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.evernote.com']);">
           Evernote.
          </a>
         </strong>
         (Thank you, Jenny!) I can save any picture or text from any website I like, or from my phone, or from my email. This is my new way for keeping recipes.
         <a href="https://www.evernote.com/about/download/web_clipper.php" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.evernote.com']);">
          This video
         </a>
         explains a little about saving things from websites. I love you, Evernote people! Whoever you are.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. My hand-written piece of paper to-do list.
         </strong>
         You may not believe it after reading everything else I just wrote, but I am a paper girl at heart. I like to draw out my weekly list with a row for errands, phone calls, computer chores, and things to do around home.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5. My AmberLee binder.
         </strong>
         This is the binder where I keep all my favorite lists. Lists like things to do when Im old, a spring cleaning list, recipes I can throw random vegetables into, the perfect Disneyland meal plan, the list of things I want to teach my children, and so on. If my house were burning down, I would go back for this binder of lists.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          6. Teau-Deux.

        <p>
         Have you ever noticed many candlesticks have a hole that goes all the way through? Do a little searching and Im sure youll find several. On my last trip I picked up five. They are ripe for the picking, my friends. If you dont like your color options, pick up a spray can of Krylon at the hardware store and do a quick paint job when you get home.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5165" rel="attachment wp-att-5165">
          <img alt="" height="291" src="../wp-content/uploads/2010/01/tutorial.jpg" title="-tutorial" width="430"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Often youll need to unscrew something. It wouldnt hurt to have a Philips and flathead with you when you go.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5166" rel="attachment wp-att-5166">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5166" height="287" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/DIY-wooden-dowel.jpg" title="-DIY wooden dowel" width="430"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The next stop is the craft store. Pick out a wooden dowel that makes a snug fit. Mine was pretty snug. The wood was soft enough that I just screwed it right into the base. If your dowel is a little thin, you have options. Pick up a wooden disk while youre at the craft store. Drill a hole so you can use the wooden disk as your base, then plant the dowel in the hole (again, make sure its a snug fit) and slide the candlestick right over. You also have the choice of picking up one of these from the hardware store, theyre called allthreads and work great for this project if you prefer them to a dowel.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5167" rel="attachment wp-att-5167">
          <img alt="" height="287" src="../wp-content/uploads/2010/01/screws.jpg" title="-screws" width="430"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5172" rel="attachment wp-att-5172">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5172" height="287" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/ribbon-organizer2.jpg" title="-ribbon organizer" width="430"/>
         </a>
         Thats it. Once you find a dowel or rod that fits, theres nothing left to do but stack the candlestick back together, and display in your studio, office, or right on your mantle.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5162" rel="attachment wp-att-5162">
          <img alt="" height="287" src="../wp-content/uploads/2010/01/DIY-ribbon-organizer.jpg" title="-DIY ribbon organizer" width="430"/>
         </a>
        </p>

        <h2 class="entry-title">
         <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?p=4981" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent link to Simplifying &amp; Organizing Gift Wrap, my 7 tips">
          Simplifying &amp; Organizing Gift Wrap, my 7 tips
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="cusdate">
         01.21.10
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <table class="pib-count-table pib-count-table-horizontal">
          <tbody>
           <tr>
            <td>
             <a class="pin-it-button-no-iframe pin-it-button-user-selects-image" count-layout="horizontal" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.giverslog.com%2F%3Fp%3D4981&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fgiverslog.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2010%2F01%2Fgift-wrap1-430x645.jpg&amp;description=Simplifying%20%26%23038%3B%20Organizing%20Gift%20Wrap%2C%20my%207%20tips" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://pinterest.com']);" rel="nobox">
              <img border="0" class="pib-count-img" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/PinExt.png" title="Pin It"/>
             </a>
            </td>
            <td class="pib-count-cell">
             <div class="pib-count-bubble">
             </div>
            </td>
           </tr>

         </table>

        <p>
         As youve read, Im purging my gift and wrapping closet. I wanted to take some pics to share with you, and I found out I had quite a lot to say on the topic. So here it goes, the first of three posts about your gift stash. I also would love to hear, what do you do at your house to keep all these things organized? Any tips would be great.
        </p>
        <p>
         As a sidenote, a rather shameless one, I had Brent add a share this button to the bottom of my posts. (This he did after fixing our well system and folding a giant heap of clothes. Hes great like that.) So if you like something I write here, please click the button so your friends see it or maybe your mother or someone else important.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now on to gift wrap.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5007" rel="attachment wp-att-5007">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5007" height="645" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/gift-wrap1-430x645.jpg" title="-gift wrap" width="430"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         When it comes to wrapping gifts, Ive learned to make one assumption:
         <strong>
         </strong>
         I will have five minutes at the most to gift wrap. That includes cleanup time. Im learning this lesson a little better each year. And Ive managed to simplify my wrapping by doing a few things. I thought you might like to hear them, so here they are.
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          My 7 tips to organize and simplify gift wrap
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Print and punch tags ahead:
         </strong>
         As a new year starts out fresh, I print and punch a
         <a href="http://giverslog.com/wrapthegift/?cat=14">
          stack of labels and tags
         </a>
         in advance. Its so much more fun to go to the closet and pick out a pretty tag than to wrestle with the printer minutes before having to walk out the door with your gift in hand. ???And punching tags from scraps is sort of a fun artsy activity.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Pick a color scheme:
         </strong>
         Its like planning a wedding. Doesnt that sound fun? I realized one day that I didnt need to be prepared with wrap, ribbon, and tags for every possible color scheme. I could just stick with one or two. And it could be whatever color scheme my whim dictated. So I choose a color scheme a year. This year Im leaning towards with red, turquoise, and blue. But I f ind its also great to keep versatile staples around, like a roll of brown kraft paper and a stack of
         <a href="http://giverslog.com/?p=2750">
          glassine
         </a>
         envelopes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. Collect scraps:
         </strong>
         If you dont already collect scraps, just make a place to keep them and they will start showing up. Youll be amazed how theyll collect and youll find uses for bits of ribbon, old maps, a piece of graph paper, last years Christmas cards. Make sure to get yourself a big circle punch or tag-shaped punch and youll be able to make a tag out of almost anything.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Wrap green:
         </strong>
         <a href="../wrapthegift/?cat=8">
          Reuse household containers
         </a>
         or wrap a gift in another gift (thanks, Carly!) like a tea towel or pair of socks. Or go with just a ribbon and a tag, like
         <a href="http://samanthaleelee.blogspot.com/2010/01/thoughts-on-gifting-and-winner-blog.html">
          this lovely lady did.
         </a>
         A
         <a href="../wrapthegift/?cat=14">
          pretty tag
         </a>
         can go a long way, dont you agree?
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5. Make the container fit the need:
         </strong>
         Pick out an appropriate sized box or container for everything you like to wrap with. This way you know when the box is full to stop buying that supply, so you dont end up with 15 different packages of crinkle wrap and every single gift bag youve every received in your life. Plus when the box is getting empty you know its time to restock. The same rule works for gifts. My baby gifts, for example, go in a sweater box. Just the right size to keep a few at a time.
         <br/>
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          6. Be real about your space:
         </strong>
         Im done buying rolls of wrapping that measure up to my chest. Seriously. I have a small house, and plenty of other things to store. I trust that if I have something that big to wrap, my ingenuity will kick in and Ill find a way.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          7. Organization doesnt have to cost you:
         </strong>
         Save your money for the wrapping, you dont have to spend it all on organizing. Look around the house for containers you already have that you can use to turn your unwieldy mess into a tame, happy, wrapping corner. A few of my favorites below.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5008" rel="attachment wp-att-5008">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5008" height="287" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/ribbon-organizer.jpg" title="-ribbon organizer" width="430"/>
          </a>
         </strong>
         Im telling you, these
         <strong>
          <a href="http://jas.familyfun.go.com/crafts/reusable-lunch-containers-785113/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://jas.familyfun.go.com']);">
           containers made from milk cartons
          </a>
         </strong>
         are the best for organizing everything. Theyre perfect for saving scraps. Ive gotten so I can transform a milk carton into one of these babies in under two minutes with my trusty craft knife.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5009" rel="attachment wp-att-5009">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5009" height="287" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/organizing-gift-wrap3.jpg" title="-organizing gift wrap3" width="430"/>
          </a>
          Pizza boxes
         </strong>
         turned inside out are another favorite of mine (original idea from
         <a href="http://familyfun.go.com/crafts/pizza-box-portfolio-710497/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://familyfun.go.com']);">
          here
         </a>
         , I also used this idea
         <a href="http://giverslog.com/?p=2203">
          here
         </a>
         ). I love to use tissue paper for wrapping, and I find pizza boxes protect it nicely. Sweater boxes are also great for a stash of gifts.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5010" rel="attachment wp-att-5010">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5010" height="287" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/yarn-organizer.jpg" title="-yarn organizer" width="430"/>
         </a>
         These
         <strong>
          boxes once held salad.
         </strong>
         Now theyre keeping my yarn from becoming a tangled heap. Im planning to punch a hole in the side to pull the yarn through.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5011" rel="attachment wp-att-5011">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5011" height="287" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/organizing-gift-wrap.jpg" title="-organizing gift wrap" width="430"/>
          </a>
          Cereal boxes
         </strong>
         are the best for filing gift bags by size or color or theme.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5012" rel="attachment wp-att-5012">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5012" height="287" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/organizing-gift-wrap2.jpg" title="-organizing gift wrap2" width="430"/>
          </a>
         </strong>
         Ive found that
         <strong>
          hair pins
         </strong>
         work great for keeping gift box tops paired with their bottoms.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.giverslog.com/?attachment_id=5013" rel="attachment wp-att-5013">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5013" height="287" src="http://giverslog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/art-organizer.jpg" title="-art organizer" width="430"/>
         </a>
         One final piece. I cut a section off a
         <strong>
          hanging toy organizer
         </strong>
         to stuff full of my crinkle wrap. As you can see, its time for me to stop buying crinkle wrap for a while.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have one final
         <strong>
          trick for storing ribbon,
         </strong>
         and Ill be sure to share in a day or two once I can get a tutorial together. Also
         <strong>
          coming soon:
         </strong>
         all my favorite gift wrap supplies. It really is a lot of fun taking pictures of ribbons, labels, and paper. See you soon.
        </p>

         <a href="http://www.kirtsy.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.kirtsy.com']);">
          Kirtsy
         </a>
         . Wahoo. If youre thinking of adding this friendly button to your blog, it took only a few minutes.)
