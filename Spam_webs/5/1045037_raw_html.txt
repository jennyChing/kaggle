             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://www.freegenealogytools.com/2015/06/ancestors-who-came-from-or-thru-germany.html">
               Ancestors Who Came From -- Or Thru -- Germany
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8755376873396021457">
              <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
              </script>
              <!-- fgt leaderboard -->
              <br/>
              <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-5449807520906997" data-ad-slot="4331773522" style="display: inline-block; height: 90px; width: 728px;">
              </ins>
              <script>
               (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
              </script>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-vjzVrAokkm8/VXMoVd07fhI/AAAAAAAACiw/QJU6ThjtBlU/s1600/german%2Bemigrants%2B1850.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                <img alt="Emigrants leaving Bremerhaven, Germany 1850" border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-vjzVrAokkm8/VXMoVd07fhI/AAAAAAAACiw/QJU6ThjtBlU/s1600/german%2Bemigrants%2B1850.jpg" title="Emigrants leaving Bremerhaven, Germany 1850"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              If your ancestors immigrated to the United States from Germany, they almost certainly traveled by boat. Even ancestors who came here from other European countries -- France, Russia, Italy, Sweden -- may well have left the continent from a German port.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              In either case, here's an important resource worth checking. The
              <a href="http://www.dad-recherche.de/hmb/index.html" target="_blank">
               German Emigrants Database
              </a>
              (the elegant-souding
              <i>
               Deutsche Auswanderer Datenbank
              </i>
              in German) is a joint project of German and American organizations, specifically:
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Castle Garden Immigration Research (New York)
               </li>
               <li>
                Historisches Museum (Bremerhaven)
               </li>
               <li>
                Frderkreis Historisches Museum (Bremerhaven)
               </li>
              </ul>
              <br/>
              Here's a direct link to the
              <a href="http://www.dad-recherche.de/hmb/index_engl.asp" target="_blank">
               search form
              </a>
              (though you may want to click through the site first for background info). You can search on surnames as well as gender and a range of dates. The data is mostly compiled from a large (and growing) collection of shipping manifests.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              The German Emigrants Database includes other useful content: information on ships, research tools, and a collection of almost a century's worth of photographs covering the period from 1840-1938.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              On a related note, the
              <i>
               Staats Archiv
              </i>
              in nearby Bremen also has
              <a href="http://www.passagierlisten.de/" target="_blank">
               searchable passenger lists for 1920-1939
              </a>
              (the page is in German, but there's a button for
              <i>
               English
              </i>
              at the top right of the page).I'm not clear on how much overlap (if any) there is with the Bremerhaven lists. If anyone knows, please post a comment.
              <br/>
              <br/>

              The
              <b>
               Families in British India Society
              </b>
              hosts the
              <a href="http://www.fibis.org/" target="_blank">
               FIBIS website
              </a>
              to celebrate, if that's the right word, all things related to Colonial India. If your roots hark back to the UK or to India, this can be a valuable site for family research. As the FIBIS genealogy motto says:
              <i>
               Your brick wall is in India
              </i>
              .
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Their main tool is a
              <a href="http://search.fibis.org/frontis/bin/" target="_blank">
               searchable database
              </a>
              of more than a million names. These look to be mostly British names, but there are a substantial number of Indian names as well. The database was amassed from newspaper mentions, shipping records, property records, government files and so on.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Don't overlook the rest of the site. Although not as comprehensive as the names database, there is ample material here like photographs, reference sources and research advice, all worth looking into. You can register at FIBIS at no charge for access to additional materials.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Enjoy your exploration!

        <div class="widget-content">
