              <a href="http://chezbeeperbebe.blogspot.com/2014/03/almost-springtime-in-paris.html">
               Almost Springtime in Paris
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-hKbBsvDeYCM/UxzKpr97u8I/AAAAAAAAHKU/YhHApCP_NaM/s1600/12910314275_094e40812c_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-hKbBsvDeYCM/UxzKpr97u8I/AAAAAAAAHKU/YhHApCP_NaM/s1600/12910314275_094e40812c_o.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               I was recently in Europe for work (Geneva and Amsterdam), and decided to stay a little longer and take the train down to Paris for the weekend. Arriving in the Gare du Nord is like emerging into some sort of old world state of grace. To me, everything just feels right in Paris--the food, the art, the parks, the architecture, the streets--I found myself moving more slowly, breathing more deeply, looking at my surroundings with dreamy eyes. Paris is like an antidote for the hurry and unpoetic details of day to day life. It is a balm made up of pain au chocolat, beautiful boulevards, sidewalk cafes, french wine, teeny shops, late dinners and the art of Rodin, Seurat, Matisse and Monet.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               Here is some of the loveliness found in a weekend of wandering and lingering...

              <a href="http://chezbeeperbebe.blogspot.com/2014/02/make-this-valentines-balms.html">
               Make This: Valentine's Balms
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-NaxvzXV1Orw/UvxETOY586I/AAAAAAAAHIk/wSnyUzWmgh0/s1600/jumble+of+balms.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-NaxvzXV1Orw/UvxETOY586I/AAAAAAAAHIk/wSnyUzWmgh0/s1600/jumble+of+balms.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               Beat this: what's more classic for Valentine's Day than chocolate and roses? Yep--not much else out there more classic than this pairing...but the debbie-downer-side to these things is that chocolates make you fat and lazy while roses just die and drop their sad wilted petals all over the place.
               <i>
                These
               </i>
               chocolate and roses though add neither calories nor detritus to your home...
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aSdcwIt4Wpk/UvxEoL71kAI/AAAAAAAAHJE/-_C0Bh5cydc/s1600/Two+Balms6.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aSdcwIt4Wpk/UvxEoL71kAI/AAAAAAAAHJE/-_C0Bh5cydc/s1600/Two+Balms6.jpg" style="cursor: move;" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               Yep--Valentine's balms--one for the lips, the other for all the places you would apply perfume. You come out tasting great and smelling awesome. And, as it turns out, making balms is so crazy easy. Once you have your supplies, you can whip a dozen of these up in less than an hour. Seriously.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-wHxLQtJAjgE/UvxDqldG2vI/AAAAAAAAHIA/FWx9Yk2Jq84/s1600/Balms+and+Tape.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-wHxLQtJAjgE/UvxDqldG2vI/AAAAAAAAHIA/FWx9Yk2Jq84/s1600/Balms+and+Tape.jpg" style="cursor: move;" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               Add some colorful washi tape to the tops and they instantly say love and romance and other tasty sentiments (Plus, don't you love the simple look of a strip of washi tape on just about anything? How can a strip of tape add so much? The washi has little bits of magic embedded in it, I think...)
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-5y-KtG672Q4/UvxExMROUWI/AAAAAAAAHJU/zEUlbZFFIrA/s1600/washi+tape+on+the+sill3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-5y-KtG672Q4/UvxExMROUWI/AAAAAAAAHJU/zEUlbZFFIrA/s1600/washi+tape+on+the+sill3.jpg" style="cursor: move;" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               Oh, and that? Well, that is just reflective of my washi tape love....did someone say addiction? Obsession? OCD hoarding? No, no--it is just love. Plain and simple.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               Happy Valentine's Day.

             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://chezbeeperbebe.blogspot.com/2014/01/eat-this-polar-vortex-doughnuts.html">
               Eat This: Polar Vortex Doughnuts
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-TlIGzsS31Ko/UsxHbfJEkoI/AAAAAAAAHHQ/7ATyTEtkG7s/s1600/Polar+Vortex+Doughnuts6.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-TlIGzsS31Ko/UsxHbfJEkoI/AAAAAAAAHHQ/7ATyTEtkG7s/s1600/Polar+Vortex+Doughnuts6.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               You may have heard: it's really, really cold in Minnesota right now--some sort of polar vortex weather phenomena is hovering over the state--just like a dementor waiting to suck out all of your happy. Luckily, I have figured out that doughnuts are the perfect antidote to happiness-sucking weather. Makes perfect sense, no?  When a tornado hits, head to the basement, and when a besieged by a polar vortex, make doughnuts.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-2KJmKucj0HE/UsxGdnSSwuI/AAAAAAAAHGM/lDKw-szoovs/s1600/Coconut+Toasted.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-2KJmKucj0HE/UsxGdnSSwuI/AAAAAAAAHGM/lDKw-szoovs/s1600/Coconut+Toasted.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-m4jCKjODkwk/UsxGlkJHCKI/AAAAAAAAHGU/R5a5lHOzbPs/s1600/Mixed+Dry+and+Wet+Ingredients.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-m4jCKjODkwk/UsxGlkJHCKI/AAAAAAAAHGU/R5a5lHOzbPs/s1600/Mixed+Dry+and+Wet+Ingredients.jpg" style="cursor: move;" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               So, I dug out my as-yet-unused doughnut baking pan (best $8 investment ever...), toasted up some coconut and got down to business. I actually started thinking about making something like this
               <a href="http://chezbeeperbebe.blogspot.com/2011/06/where-do-doughnut-muffins-fit-in.html" target="_blank">
                back when I made Joy's doughnut muffins
               </a>
               ...and through some adaptation of the doughnut recipe found on the back of the actual doughnut pan packaging, came up with this recipe.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-N9Z55rszzJk/UsxGTaAm2RI/AAAAAAAAHF8/7GxEFvY0ens/s1600/Coconut+Doughnut+Dough.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-N9Z55rszzJk/UsxGTaAm2RI/AAAAAAAAHF8/7GxEFvY0ens/s1600/Coconut+Doughnut+Dough.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-whe80xiPduo/UsxGOjVw_BI/AAAAAAAAHF0/r6Y0-LJt4hw/s1600/Doughnut+Glaze+and+Coconut2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="267" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-whe80xiPduo/UsxGOjVw_BI/AAAAAAAAHF0/r6Y0-LJt4hw/s1600/Doughnut+Glaze+and+Coconut2.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               This dough comes together in about 5 minutes, and the glaze took about the same. You bake the doughnuts for 15 minutes and suddenly there you have it--ta-da!--cake doughnuts! No standing over hot frying oil, little extra fat beyond what you choose to grease the pan with, and so little time invested!
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-EA63KjgNRsg/UsxG0wX8SnI/AAAAAAAAHGk/AR5yyr2WqJk/s1600/Unglazed+Doughnuts3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-EA63KjgNRsg/UsxG0wX8SnI/AAAAAAAAHGk/AR5yyr2WqJk/s1600/Unglazed+Doughnuts3.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-doTyjweA1nM/UsxG7zLaxxI/AAAAAAAAHGs/jY_SWZYloxQ/s1600/Glazing+the+Doughnuts.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-doTyjweA1nM/UsxG7zLaxxI/AAAAAAAAHGs/jY_SWZYloxQ/s1600/Glazing+the+Doughnuts.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               I will caution you to only fill your doughnut pan about 3/4 of the way full--while I did leave a little space in mine, they cooked over and actually eclipsed their holes.  They also have what I think of as a very narrow doughnut tutu. No matter, I just got a paring knife and cut out the holes and gobbled those up while still warm. I then triple-dipped them in the vanilla bean glaze and sprinkled heavily with the toasted coconut. I would recommend letting them set up for an hour so the glaze will not ooze all over your fingers, dropping a party of coconut confetti after each bite--but this did not deter my family. We oozed, we partied, we tucked into the doughnuts with wild abandon while shaking our fists out the window at the scowling polar vortex.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-MbvYRBtQKWo/UsxHM6TdovI/AAAAAAAAHG8/XejQ-To_Zg4/s1600/Polar+Vortex+Doughnuts.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-MbvYRBtQKWo/UsxHM6TdovI/AAAAAAAAHG8/XejQ-To_Zg4/s1600/Polar+Vortex+Doughnuts.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               So, what are you waiting for? You do not need a polar vortex of your own to make these--just a doughnut baking pan and 30 minutes and you are on your way to world filled with warm and happy thoughts no matter the weather. Now go on...
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

                DOWNLOAD BAKED COCONUT DOUGHNUTS RECIPE

             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://chezbeeperbebe.blogspot.com/2014/01/christmas-plushies-made.html">
               Christmas Plushies Made
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-nt8oBqg-luc/UsneGwGdhCI/AAAAAAAAHFA/u_068n3wPbU/s1600/Clothtopus3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-nt8oBqg-luc/UsneGwGdhCI/AAAAAAAAHFA/u_068n3wPbU/s1600/Clothtopus3.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               Hey. How were your holidays? Mine were a cocktail of quietly happy moments (fireside reading in
               <a href="http://www.rhbabyandchild.com/catalog/product/product.jsp?productId=rhbc_prod364339&amp;categoryId=rhbc_cat377264" target="_blank">
                my fave faux-fur beanbag
               </a>
               , mad games of
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Parker-Brothers-Vintage-Collection-Exclusive/dp/B00285GXKG/ref=sr_1_5?s=toys-and-games&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1388962166&amp;sr=1-5&amp;keywords=clue" target="_blank">
                Clue
               </a>
               with Beeper, and listening to lots of new vinyl...) and random incidents of crazy frustration (water dripping from above in the dining room and subsequent tear down of half of the ceiling to get at a pipe failure, usual extended-family dramatics, last minute pre-Christmas biz trip to Brazil for my day job...).
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               I did still manage to whip up a couple of handmade items (and bought a butt load of handmade from
               <a href="http://www.etsy.com/?ref=si_home" target="_blank">
                Etsy
               </a>
               ...maybe will share some of that later because there was so much goodness to give and you may want to know about it)--like the Clothtopus, above. I actually refer to him as the Magic Rainbow Coloctopus in homage to his color-wheel legs, but
               <a href="http://www.hopskipjump.typepad.com/" target="_blank">
                Fiona
               </a>
               calls him a Clothtopus in her book,
               <i>
                <a href="http://www.hopskipjump.bigcartel.com/product/hop-skip-jump-fiona-dalton" target="_blank">
                 Hop Skip Jump: 20 Eco-Friendly Toys to Sew
                </a>
                .
               </i>
               I blogged about the book some time ago--
               <a href="http://chezbeeperbebe.blogspot.com/2012/01/two-penguins-and-hop-skip-jump-book.html" target="_blank">
                here
               </a>
               --and today, I would have to name it my all-time favorite plushie-making book. Really. I pretty much want to make every toy in there and plan to keep finding children to who I can give these things so I have a reason.
               <br/>
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-pkSTCL5QGsg/Usnpz4AiRNI/AAAAAAAAHFg/IeKvPkNtNCw/s1600/Clothtopus6.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-pkSTCL5QGsg/Usnpz4AiRNI/AAAAAAAAHFg/IeKvPkNtNCw/s1600/Clothtopus6.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               Anyway, I made the octopus for our friend's daughter, Zoey. She is a curious child and I thought she deserved a little something as interesting as she is.  I used Robert Kaufman's
               <i>
                <a href="http://www.robertkaufman.com/fabrics/quilters_linen/" target="_blank">
                 <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-style: normal;">
                  Quilter's Linen
                 </span>
                </a>
                ,
               </i>
               which I love for the color-pop selection and the linen look without all the stringiness of actual linen (am planning to make a couple of sets of
               <a href="http://chezbeeperbebe.blogspot.com/2010/01/tutorial-and-pattern-counting-bean-bags.html" target="_blank">
                my counting beanbags
               </a>
               from this same fabric soon--watch
               <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop/beeperbebe" target="_blank">
                my shop
               </a>
               for those).
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SKKlNyMhQg8/UsneInHszZI/AAAAAAAAHFM/FH5em1wH8Bg/s1600/Socrates+Plush3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SKKlNyMhQg8/UsneInHszZI/AAAAAAAAHFM/FH5em1wH8Bg/s1600/Socrates+Plush3.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               I also have been wanting to make a black cat plushie for Beeper for a couple of years now. You see, we have a black cat--
               <a href="http://chezbeeperbebe.blogspot.com/2010/03/please-meet-socrates.html" target="_blank">
                Socrates
               </a>
               --and Beeper is mad-adoring of him so, I thought I would make him a plush Socrates who could travel with him whenever we are away from home since he always misses Socrates like he is his actual blood-brother or something.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--gtyr8tPV9I/UsneMcKahNI/AAAAAAAAHFU/HwWw7rjXUhE/s1600/Make+Your+Own+Toys+Cover.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="266" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--gtyr8tPV9I/UsneMcKahNI/AAAAAAAAHFU/HwWw7rjXUhE/s1600/Make+Your+Own+Toys+Cover.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               It is a very rustic and simple design--it came from the book,
               <i>
                <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Make-Your-Own-Toys-Bunnies/dp/0307586448/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1388962996&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=make+your+own+toys+sue+havens" target="_blank">
                 Make Your Own Toys
                </a>
               </i>
               by Sue Havens. The book focuses on using reclaimed materials to make colorful, mismatched-patchworky-plush--all of the designs are far more colorful than my black cat plush above--which complements the simplicity of the designs. I really like this book--it actually reminds me quite a bit of
               <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/beeperbebe/sets/72157603226777596/" target="_blank">
                my earliest plush designs
               </a>
               --but she uses all manner of fabrics (sweaters, tees, shirts) and designs (plaids, stripes, prints) in combinations I never would have thought to. The effect is very clever and fetching. I would recommend this book if you are new to plushie-making or want to begin working with reclaimed materials--the designs are simple enough and the instructions are clear with good illustrations.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               Here's to a new year of inspiring, creating, making, and finding new ways to incorporate a rainbow of color into your life...
              </div>
