                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Movie Review: L'Ecume des Jours (2013)
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      <span class="post-labels">
                       Labels:
                       <a href="http://www.jhanzey.net/search/label/Movie" rel="tag">
                        Movie
                       </a>
                       ,
                       <a href="http://www.jhanzey.net/search/label/Review" rel="tag">
                        Review
                       </a>
                      </span>
                      <span class="post-comment-link">
                      </span>
                      <span class="post-icons">
                       <span class="item-control blog-admin pid-106288826">
                        <a href="https://www.blogger.com/post-edit.g?blogID=4788372542694914358&amp;postID=5104202435967217157&amp;from=pencil" title="Edit Post">
                         <img alt="" class="icon-action" height="18" src="//img2.blogblog.com/img/icon18_edit_allbkg.gif" width="18"/>
                        </a>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5104202435967217157" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      Although it doesn't look like it, I am honestly in love with films. I may not be a 'know it all' as much as the other film enthusiasts, but I do enjoy and love watching compelling and visually stimulating films. I'm more of an artsy fartsy viewer, and would rather enjoy films that would make me think than those that would make me laugh and wouldn't leave a mark after. Not that I'm not a fan of comedy -- trust me, I love a good laugh.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Anyway, a few months back, I went on binge movie downloading session at home of some films that I've listed down during a session of my trailer/movie hunt on Youtube. Most of them are in French, while the others are part of Sundance Film Festivals. I've also downloaded a few Oscar's nominated films like Dallas Buyers Club, which I still haven't watched. Despite my crazy schedule, I was able to squeeze in some time to enjoy a good film or two, and one of my personal favorites would be Michael Gondry's L'Ecume des jours (Mood Indigo). I've watched the film a few weeks back, but it definitely has something in it that makes it remarkable. Here's a short review of the film and some spoilers... I'm sorry.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Pn-yHKRPkQg/VCtyF1-ZJLI/AAAAAAAAA9A/I85ngWQ-_ng/s1600/MOOD_INDIGO_Underwater.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="225" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Pn-yHKRPkQg/VCtyF1-ZJLI/AAAAAAAAA9A/I85ngWQ-_ng/s1600/MOOD_INDIGO_Underwater.jpg" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        L'Ecume des Jours (2013)
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Director: Michael Gondry
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Starring Romain Duris and Audrey Tautou
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Mood Indigo (L'Ecume des Jous) is the latest adaptation of Boris Vian's novel, which was published in 1947. It follows the adventure of the young and wealthy Colin (Duris) and his search for happiness. Set in a surreal world where pianoctails exist and lilies grow inside the body, L'Ecume des Jours would definitely satisfy your hunger for a wonderful cinematography, of course by no other than Michael Gondry.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-fdszgRQ5RVM/VCt9r6wYY3I/AAAAAAAAA9Q/6koLWff2eeg/s1600/MOOD_INDIGO_Biglemoi_Look.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="225" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-fdszgRQ5RVM/VCt9r6wYY3I/AAAAAAAAA9Q/6koLWff2eeg/s1600/MOOD_INDIGO_Biglemoi_Look.jpg" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       The movie starts out in a happy and carefree tone, with Colin and his best friend Chick (Gad Elmaleh) finding the love of their lives. Chick meets Alise (Aissa Maiga) at a party through their common interest of reading Jean Sol-Partre's masterpieces, which spruces up Colin's curiosity of settling down and finding a girl himself. Through the help of his cook and attorney, Nicolas (Omar Sy), he finally met Chloe (Tautou) and fell into the worst roller coaster relationship imaginable.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Irs0jkH-L98/VCt-AlOPZ6I/AAAAAAAAA9Y/U9CyJ6KDF_k/s1600/MOOD_INDIGO_Storm.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="225" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Irs0jkH-L98/VCt-AlOPZ6I/AAAAAAAAA9Y/U9CyJ6KDF_k/s1600/MOOD_INDIGO_Storm.jpg" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       During their honeymoon, Chloe caught a waterlily into her heart, which can only be cured by surrounding herself with fresh flowers. Despite having a 'cure', her demise was still inevitable, and this caused Colin to be in great despair. From the happy, colorful hues, the movie began to backlash into black and white, heightening the emotions of each character.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       It's a tragic yet exquisitely executed film. If you are looking for a feel good movie, I highly suggest that you stay away from L'Ecume des Jours, for it will wreck your heart and slowly trap you in the world of adversity where the characters are.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Rating: 6.5/10
                       </b>
                       (because it was heartbreaking and it wrecked me after)
                      </div>
