                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.biblegems-pastorcoleman.blogspot.com/2015/07/can-god-look-on-evil.html">
                      Can God Look On Evil?
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1781572888123961505" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         Biblegems #235
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         Question
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        : What does the Bible mean when it
says that God cannot look upon evil?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        The question
refers to this biblical quote:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <b>
                         Hab. 1:13
                        </b>
                        <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                        </span>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         Your
eyes are too pure to look on evil; you cannot tolerate wrong
                        </i>
                        .
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        Unfortunately,
this passage is often misquoted, misunderstood and therefore misapplied. The
language in this verse makes use of metaphor to make a very strong point: God
cannot treat evil as something acceptable.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        To convey
that idea, God is described metaphorically as having physical eyes, which of
course, He does not. God is Spirit (Jn. 4:24).
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         Your
                         <u>
                          eyes,
                         </u>
                        </i>
                        says the prophet Habakkuk,
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         are too pure to look on evil.
                        </i>
                        In addition, Habakkuk makes this
statement as part of a question of complaint against God. The second half of
the verse reads:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         
                         <span style="color: #46260d;">
                          Why do you tolerate wrong? Destruction and violence are
before me; there is strife, and conflict abounds.
                          <o:p>
                          </o:p>
                         </span>
                        </i>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        A
cardinal rule of sound biblical interpretation is that
                        <u>
                         doctrinal truth
cannot be derived from a metaphor
                        </u>
                        . A second rule of interpretation is that
                        <u>
                         doctrinal
truth cannot be established on the basis of a single Scriptural reference
                        </u>
                        .
A third rule of interpretation is that
                        <u>
                         doctrinal truth cannot be established
from a question
                        </u>
                        . Habakkuk 1:13 contains
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         all
three
                        </i>
                        of these elements! Since nowhere else in Scripture is the idea
specifically taught that God cannot look upon evil, and since the language is
clearly metaphorical, and since the statement introduces a question of
complaint against God, this verse clearly means something else.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        The Bible
tells us that it was because God
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         saw
                        </i>
                        the wickedness of mankind that He enacted the global Flood as judgment:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <b>
                         Gen. 6:5
                        </b>
                        <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                         <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                         </span>
                        </b>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         The LORD saw how great mans
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          wickedness
                         </span>
                         on the earth had become, and that every inclination of the thoughts of his
heart was only evil all the time
                        </i>
                        .
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        Likewise,
when Scripture metaphorically describes God as turning His
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         face
                        </i>
                        against those who do evil, it means He sees evil and confronts
it head on:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none; text-indent: .5in;">
                       <b>
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                         Ps. 34:16
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the face of the LORD is against those who do
evil, to cut off the memory of them from the earth
                        </i>
                        .
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        In order
to correctly interpret Gods Word it is always important to understand
Scripture in the light of what Scripture teaches elsewhere. No one verse can be
taken out of context to teach something the Word of God as a whole does not support.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        The truth
is, God does see our sin:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <b>
                         Jer. 13:27b
                        </b>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          I have seen
                         </span>
                         your
detestable acts on the hills and in the fields
                        </i>
                        .
                        <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                        </span>
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        He
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         remembers
                        </i>
                        our wickedness:
                        <o:p>

                       </span>
                      </div>

                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>

                         Jer. 14:10

                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        Habakkuk
used a common figure of speech to convey the impeccable purity of Gods
holiness. Imagine someone who doesnt understand the common English figure
speech, hes out of his mind, trying to interpret those words literally! As
noted above, the meaning of Habakkuk 1:13 is that our holy God cannot treat
evil as acceptable. That is a solidly biblical concept!

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.biblegems-pastorcoleman.blogspot.com/2015/06/kingdom-of-heaven-kingdom-of-god4.html">
                      Kingdom Of Heaven &amp; the Kingdom Of God?4
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2347543764200374024" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         Biblegems #234
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         Question
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        : Is there a difference between the
Kingdom of Heaven and the Kingdom of God? Also, what does it mean that
"the kingdom of heaven is near," "theirs is the kingdom of
heaven," "Your kingdom come. . . on earth as it is in heaven,"
"seek first His kingdom," etc. Are all of these kingdoms the same? Is
it a state of mind? An actual place?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        This last
installment on the kingdom of God / heaven zeros in on the apparent disparity
between statements such as
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         "Your
kingdom come. . . on earth as it is in heaven"
                        </i>
                        and
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         "seek first His kingdom."
                        </i>
                        Is
the Kingdom a state of mind or an actual place?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        Is
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the kingdom
                        </i>
                        just an attitude of the
heart?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                       </span>
                       <b>
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                         Luke
17:20
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                        </span>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         Once, having been asked by the
Pharisees when the
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          kingdom of God
                         </span>
                         would
come, Jesus replied, The
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          kingdom of
God
                         </span>
                         <u>
                          does not come with your careful observation,
                         </u>
                         nor will people say,
Here it is, or There it is, because
                         <u>
                          the
                          <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                           kingdom of God
                          </span>
                          is within
                         </u>
                         you.
                        </i>
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        The truth
is, the kingdom encompasses
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         both
                        </i>
                        the physical and spiritual realms. It is more than a spiritual idea, more than
a state of mind or attitude of the heart. One day in the not too distant
future the kingdom of God will occupy planet earth as its geographical center,
and citizens of the kingdom will enjoy with Jesus the fruitful harvest of the
land:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <b>
                         Mark 14:25
                        </b>
                        <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                        </span>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         Truly I
tell you, I will not drink again from the fruit of the vine until that day when
I drink it new in the
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          kingdom of God
                         </span>
                         .
                        </i>
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        However,
as Jesus pointed out in Luke 17:20, if the Kingdom of God is not already
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         within
                        </i>
                        us then we cannot expect to be
in the kingdom at Christs appearing. Its physical coming will be a joy only
for those who already belong to Him because the physical Kingdom is an outward
manifestation of the spiritual Kingdom.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        Heaven is
a place (Jn. 3:3) inhabited by God and the Lamb, thousands of angels, and
many other creatures:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none; text-indent: .5in;">
                       <b>
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                         Rev.
11:15
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                         <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                         </span>
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                         The
seventh angel sounded his trumpet, and there were loud voices
                         <u>
                          in
                          <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                           heaven
                          </span>
                         </u>
                         , which said: The kingdom
of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord and of his Christ, and he will
reign for ever and ever.
                        </span>
                       </i>
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                        </span>
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        That
heavenly kingdom will appear on earth with Jesus coming:
                       </span>
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none; text-indent: .5in;">
                       <b>
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                         2Tim. 4:1
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                         In
the presence of God and of Christ Jesus, who will judge the living and the
dead, and
                         <u>
                          in view of his appearing and his
                          <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                           kingdom
                          </span>
                         </u>
                        </span>
                       </i>
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none; text-indent: .5in;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        And
though the kingdom of God
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         is not a
matter of eating and drinking
                        </i>
                        (Rom. 14:17), nor
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         a matter of talk but of power
                        </i>
                        (1Cor. 4:20), yet here will be plenty
of feasting and conversation in the kingdom of heaven:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                       </span>
                       <b>
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                         Luke
22:29
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <span style='font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: " "; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast;'>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         And I confer on you a
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          kingdom
                         </span>
                         ,
just as my Father conferred one on me, so that you may eat and drink at my
table in my
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          kingdom
                         </span>
                         and sit on
thrones, judging the twelve tribes of Israel
                        </i>
                        .
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        Gods Kingdom
is where the material and immaterial expressions of life are perfectly married.
As invisible forms of energy radiate through buildings, your body, even the
earth, so too does the spiritual realm intersect and interact with the physical
realm. The Kingdom of God is where men and women of earth become immortal
citizens of heaven, and where water and spirit describe our birth into each of these
realms:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>

                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         Biblegems #233
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         Question
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        : Is there a difference between the
Kingdom of Heaven and the Kingdom of God? Also, what does it mean that
"the kingdom of heaven is near," "theirs is the kingdom of
heaven," "Your kingdom come. . . on earth as it is in heaven,"
"seek first His kingdom," etc. Are all of these kingdoms the same? Is
it a state of mind? An actual place?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        The first
two posts on this subject focused on the phrase,
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the kingdom of heaven,
                        </i>
                         used almost exclusively by Jesus in the
book of Matthew. In contrast to this, the phrase,
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the kingdom of God
                        </i>
                        occurs 66 times in the New Testament. Most of
these are in the Gospels (52X), then Acts (6X), 1 Corinthians (4X), followed by
Romans, Galatians, Colossians and 2 Thessalonians (1X each). Of all of these,
the term is found most frequently in the Gospel of Mark and used almost
exclusively by Jesus.
                        <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                        </span>
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        But here
is where things get really interesting. Just as Jesus taught that the kingdom
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         of heaven
                        </i>
                        has already come near (i.e.,
because of Jesus presence as heavens royal representative on earth), He also
taught exactly the same way about
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the
kingdom of God:
                        </i>
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <b>
                         Mark 9:1
                        </b>
                        <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                        </span>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         And he
said to them, Truly I tell you, some who are standing here will not taste
death before they see that the
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          kingdom
of
                          <u>
                          </u>
                          God
                         </span>
                         <u>
                          has come
                         </u>
                         <span style="mso-text-raise: 4.0pt; position: relative; top: -4.0pt;">
                         </span>
                         with power
                        </i>
                        .
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        The
kingdom
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         of
                        </i>
                        God means the kingdom
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         from
                        </i>
                        God, just as the kingdom
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         of
                        </i>
                        heaven means the kingdom
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         from
                        </i>
                        heaven. The fact is, God, who is
Spirit (Jn. 4:24), has established a location in His creation from which He governs
all that exists:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none; text-indent: .5in;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         Ps.
2:4
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                         </span>
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         The One
                         <u>
                          enthroned in
                          <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                           heaven
                          </span>
                         </u>
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                         </span>
                         laughs; the Lord scoffs at them
                        </span>
                       </i>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        .
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        Gods
throne
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         in heaven
                        </i>
                        is distinct from
the heavens we commonly refer to as intergalactic space:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                         P
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          s. 8:1
                         </span>
                         <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                         </span>
                        </b>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         O LORD, our Lord, how majestic
is your name in all the earth! You have set your glory
                         <u>
                          above the
                          <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                           heavens
                          </span>
                         </u>
                        </i>
                        .
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         The kingdom of God,
                        </span>
                       </i>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        then, means
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the authority
                        </i>
                        of (or
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         from
                        </i>
                        )
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         God
                        </i>
                        as the Supreme Ruler of creation
whose throne is in heaven, beyond the physical and spatial universe. Consequently,
when Jesus told the three disciples they would 
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         see that
                         <u>
                          the
                          <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                           kingdom of God
                          </span>
                          has
come
                         </u>
                         <span style="mso-text-raise: 4.0pt; position: relative; top: -4.0pt;">
                         </span>
                         with
power
                        </i>
                         (Mk. 9:1), He was referring to the demonstration of Gods power and
authority they were about to witness on the Mount of Transfiguration (Mk.
9:2-8).
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        But
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the kingdom of God
                        </i>
                        refers to more than
simply His authority and power. It also refers to a community, a unique culture
that perfectly reflects Gods nature, Gods values and Gods character. It is
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         Gods
                        </i>
                        kingdom; and those who inhabit His
kingdom now, and those who will enter His kingdom in the future must likewise reflect
His nature, values and character. Sadly, not everyone will enter in:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <b>
                         Luke 13:28-29
                        </b>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         There
will be weeping there, and gnashing of teeth,
                         <span style="mso-text-raise: 4.0pt; position: relative; top: -4.0pt;">
                         </span>
                         when you see Abraham, Isaac and Jacob
and all the prophets in the
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          kingdom of
God
                         </span>
                         , but you yourselves thrown out. People will come from east and west
                         <span style="mso-text-raise: 4.0pt; position: relative; top: -4.0pt;">
                         </span>
                         and north and
south, and will take their places at the feast in the
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          kingdom of God
                         </span>
                        </i>
                        .
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        For this
reason, Jesus frequently gave examples from life and from nature on how to enter
the kingdom of God / kingdom of heaven:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <b>
                         Mark 4:26
                        </b>
                        <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                        </span>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         He also
said, This is what the
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;">
                          kingdom of God
                         </span>
                         is
like. A man scatters seed on the ground.
                        </i>
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.biblegems-pastorcoleman.blogspot.com/2015/06/the-kingdom-of-heaven-kingdom-of-god-2.html">
                      The Kingdom of Heaven &amp; The Kingdom Of God 2
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4407604428479035019" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         Biblegems #232
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                         Question
                        </span>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        : Is there a difference between the
Kingdom of Heaven and the Kingdom of God? Also, what does it mean that
"the kingdom of heaven is near," "theirs is the kingdom of
heaven," "Your kingdom come. . . on earth as it is in heaven,"
"seek first His kingdom," etc. Are all of these kingdoms the same? Is
it a state of mind? An actual place?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        The second
part of this series on the Kingdom of Heaven focuses on what is meant by the
statement,
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the kingdom of heaven is near"
                        </i>
                        Matt. 3:2; 4:17;10:7). As we saw in the previous post, both John the Baptist
and Jesus implored people to repent now, while there is still time, because
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the kingdom of heaven is
                         <u>
                          near
                         </u>
                         .
                        </i>
                        Or
to put it in reverse,
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the kingdom of
heaven is near,
                        </i>
                        so you had better repent!
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        The
phrase 
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         is near
                        </i>
                        means something much
deeper than
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         is coming,
                        </i>
                        or
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         is almost here.
                        </i>
                        In fact, the English
language does not even have the same verb tense used in the Greek New Testament
(Perfect Active Indicative) to translate the verb
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         eggike
                        </i>
                        appropriately. The Greek perfect tense points to the present
state of affairs as the result of some past action. For example, if someone
should ask, Where is the bag of chips I gave you? and I reply, I have eaten
them, that person understands the chips are
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         <u>
                          now
                         </u>
                         gone
                        </i>
                        based upon the fact that I
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         <u>
                          had
                         </u>
                         eaten
                        </i>
                        them earlier.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        Thats
the idea behind the perfect tense translated as
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the kingdom of heaven
                         <u>
                          is near
                         </u>
                         .
                        </i>
                        It essentially means that
the kingdom is near
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         now!
                        </i>
                        It is
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         already
                        </i>
                        near. That is a present reality
based upon some event that brought it about. What is that event? It is the
arrival of the King Himself to planet earth, Jesus the Lord of heaven, who
alone gives us access to heaven:
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                        </span>
                        <b>
                         Matt. 7:21
                        </b>
                        <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                        </span>
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         Not
everyone who says to me, Lord, Lord, will enter the kingdom of heaven, but
only he who does the will of my Father who is in heaven.
                        </i>
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        Jesus,
the King of heaven, has brought the kingdom of heaven to earth. It is
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         near
                        </i>
                        because He is here!
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-autospace: none;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 14.0pt;">
                        Literally,
the phrase Jesus uses is
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the kingdom of
                        </i>
                        (or
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         from
                        </i>
                        )
                        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
                         the heaven
                         <u>
                          s
                         </u>
                         .
                        </i>
