       <h1 class="headline">
        The GEM Debate: Bullying: Should You Make Your Kid The Target?
       </h1>
       <span class="post-byline">
        By
        <a href="http://www.goodenoughmother.com/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by admin">
         admin
        </a>
        on May 20, 2013
       </span>
      </div>
      <!--title-main-->
      <div id="home-main">
       <div class="post-29549 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-family-home category-the-gem-debate category-news tag-bullying tag-good-enough-mother tag-how-do-you-solve-the-problem-of-bullying tag-rene-syler tag-when-your-kid-is-the-bully" id="post-area">
        <div id="social-box">
         <ul class="post-social">
          <li>
           <div class="fb-like" data-layout="button_count" data-send="false" data-show-faces="false" data-width="90">

        <!--social-box-->
        <div id="content-area">
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_29567" style="width: 513px">
          <a href="http://www.goodenoughmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Picture-106.jpg">
           <img alt="Picture 106" class=" wp-image-29567 " height="402" src="http://www.goodenoughmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Picture-106.jpg" title="The GEM Debate: Bullying: Should You Make Your Kid The Target?  " width="503"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
          </p>
          <center>
           Photo Credit: Fox29 Facebook Page
           <center>
           </center>
          </center>
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          There are a lot of bullies out there, and making my kid learn a lesson at the expense of them becoming a target is a no-noNO.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres the scoop. A local Fox affiliate in Philly posed
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151611360523855&amp;set=a.274398993854.143073.59404413854&amp;type=1">
           this question on their Facebook page
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Wow! Did this mom go too far? She made her daughter dress like this for school for two days! Why? Because her daughter was bullying another 4th grade girl in school  teasing her about the way she dresses. So the mom gave her a taste of her own medicine.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          I have to say it again. This is a no-no. Firstly, lets start with the little girl (lets call her Girl A) who was the target of this childs (lets call he r Girl B) bullying. Basically this mom has sentenced Girl A to relive the bullying encountered by Girl B since now (and I think this was the point) Girl B has to experience the teasing. And if I remember my teasing days the kids are going to lead off with, Wow, Girl B, your mom made you dress like Girl A. How horrible for you! or something stupid like that. Of course, they will do it in Girl As earshot thus making her still feel bad.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Secondly, lets say you want to prove to someone shooting with guns is bad. You dont shoot them with a gun. Yes, you will prove your point, but I do believe as the mother of a toddler that that cant be right. Joelle climbs on things, I dont push her off to show her how it feels to fall. She is infatuated with blowing out candles (and the gas stove to her is just another candle), but I dont let her burn her little lips or ignite the curtains just to prove to her how destructive this behavior is either. Same goes with this mom. Shes hurting her childs feelings in a specific way to teach her child how something feels. I gotta say thats a lesson you leave for a conversation, not an actual demonstration.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Thirdly, mom is setting her daughter up for
          <a href="http://www.goodenoughmother.com/2013/03/guest-posting-how-a-little-bullying-went-a-long-way/">
           long-term bullying
          </a>
          . See, she may think she just sent her to school for two days of teasing, but what she really did is put a proverbial kick me sign on her daughters back. Kids dont forget this stuff and even if Girl B is only slightly joshed about this for the days and weeks to come it can turn into deeper feelings of inadequacy and being ostracized catapulting her daughter into how bullied kids feel. Teen egos are too fragile to teach a lesson that could lead to
          <a href="http://www.goodenoughmother.com/2011/12/what-would-you-say-to-him/">
           emotions kids may not be able to control
          </a>
          or handle on their own.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Lastly, I feel like, in a way, the mom is teasing the other little girl. I dont think it is deliberate. However, and stay with me here, on some level the mom is saying that the clothes the other little girl is wearing deserve teasing. Thats not the message you want to send. You want to tell your kids that everyone is equal no matter what is on the outside because what counts is on the inside. She should make her daughter include Girl A in her activities or invite her over for an afternoon so they can talk about things. Girl Bs mom might even want to
          <a href="http://www.goodenoughmother.com/2012/04/ask-rene-my-son-is-being-bullied-video/">
           reach out
          </a>
          to Girl As mom or the principal and start a conversation there about getting the two of them into each others circles a little more often (Ive bonded quite well with some I didnt understand after we had to work together). Mocking the little girl in this way doesnt really seem to solve anything and doesnt bring the kids together. Understanding is a much better road to change in these instances.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          So what do you think? Has mom committed a no-no or would you teach your child to stop bullying in this way? What has worked in your communities? Let us know!
         </p>
