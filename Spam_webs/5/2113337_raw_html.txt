      <p class="MsoNormal">
       While its certainly important to be grateful for the things
(e.g. family, friends, material possessions), I believe its equally if not
more important to be grateful for no particular reason; to be grateful for THIS
moment, which is all we ever really have no matter how much we try to delude
ourselves into believing otherwise.
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       As I was stuffing my face yesterday on Turkey day, I had a
deeper realization that NO AMOUNT of food could ever possibly fulfill
me in the long run. The same holds true with anything material. My colleague
Mark once said, You never know when it could end, so we should enjoy it while
it lasts.
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       The alternative is being in a constant state of fear of loss
or having an insatiable appetite for MORE of ANYTHING to cover up the
undeniable feeling of emptiness that arises when are not simply grateful for
our own existence. Either one interferes with our ability to fully enjoy what
the material world has to offer.
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       Even though we all know peace comes from within, we are
culturally conditioned to look outside of ourselves for temporary fixes. Perhaps
this is never more evident than on Black Friday (which now starts on
Thanksgiving in many places) when millions of consumers spend BILLIONS of
dollars buying things that, at least in the short term, do make us feel good.
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       Im not suggesting that we should not embrace and enjoy the
gift of giving and receiving this holiday season, but reminding us that
       <b>
        <i>
         the
most precious gift we can give ourselves and others is simply our
        </i>
       </b>
       <b>
        <i>
         Undivided
Attention
        </i>
       </b>
       <b>
        <i>
         to the Present Moment
        </i>
       </b>
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       As much as we like to think things (e.g. people, food, and
material possessions) make us feel good, its really our attention to the
moment that we secretly enjoy. We've all had the experience of not enjoying things,
activities, and people we typically like and the reason has everything to do
with simply being distracted.
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       While practicing gratitude is one of the healthiest things
we can do,
       <b>
        <i>
         the truth is that gratitude is really who we are at our core
        </i>
       </b>
       . When we are truly in the present moment, we
aren't practicing gratitude, we ARE gratitude.
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       Id like to invite you to pay attention to this truth as we
enter into this holiday season. When we are truly present, we are grateful.
When we are grateful, we dont need anything to feel good. When we dont need
anything to feel good, we are literally free to fully enjoy the gift of giving
and receiving.
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       We also free up a great deal of energy that can have a
tremendous influence on the quality of service we provide.
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">
       <o:p>
       </o:p>
