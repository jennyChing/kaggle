          </a>

         <div class="media-body">

In this graphic, material shown in blue is moving toward the observer, while material in red is traveling away.

      <div class="editorial-body">
       <div class="module module-multi module-series inset ">
        <div class="module-title">
         <div class="title-inner">
          <span class="span-0">
           Related
          </span>
          <span class="span-1">
           Links
          </span>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="module-body">
         <div class="module-body-wrap">
          <div class="fragment-media module-member">
           <div class="media-body-wrap">
            <div class="media-title">
             <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 1 : Link" data-module-name="Related Links" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/space/astronomy/dark-matter-and-gamma-ray-lobes-light-up-the-visible-universe-120508.htm">
              Milky Way's Gamma-ray Bubbles Shaped by Dark Matter?
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="fragment-media module-member">
           <div class="media-body-wrap">
            <div class="media-title">
             <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 2 : Link" data-module-name="Related Links" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/space/galaxies/mystery-galactic-gamma-ray-bubbles-still-defy-explanation-140801.htm">
              Mystery Galactic Gamma-ray 'Bubbles' Still Defy Explanation
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="fragment-media module-member">
           <div class="media-body-wrap">
            <div class="media-title">
             <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 3 : Link" data-module-name="Related Links" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/space/history-of-space/gamma-ray-bubbles-black-hole.htm">
              Giant Bubbles Found in Space
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="series-footer">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        Giant bubbles of gas that erupted from the core of the Milky Way galaxy millions of years ago are expanding out into space at mind-blowing speeds, according to new observations that may help reveal how the strange balloon-like lobes formed.
       </p>
       <div class="fragment-media secondary-image media-vertical video-over">
        <div class="media-hero-wrap">
         <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 1 : Image" data-module-name="left rail : linked video" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/videos/dung-beetles-use-milky-way-as-compass.htm">
          <img class="media-hero" src="http://static.ddmcdn.com/gif/dung-beetle-ignoble-456.jpg"/>
          <div class="over-main">
           <img height="38px" src="http://static.ddmcdn.com/en-us/news/img/video-icon.png" width="38px"/>
           <span>
            Play Video
           </span>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="media-body-wrap">
         <div class="media-title">
          <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 1 : Link" data-module-name="left rail : linked video" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/videos/dung-beetles-use-milky-way-as-compass.htm">
           Dung Beetles Use the Milky Way to Navigate
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="media-body">
          Poop ball making Dung Beetles actually use the Milky Way for navigation. So why does an animal who runs around hunting for poop need to navigate so accurately? Trace has the answers.
         </div>
         <div class="media-sub">
          Emily Baird
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        Scientists using the
        <a href="http://www.space.com/20802-hubble-space-telescope-quiz.html">
         Hubble Space Telescope
        </a>
        have clocked the speed of gas bubbles, known as Fermi bubbles, at a whopping 2 million mph (3.2 million km/h). The giant structures now extend 30,000 light-years above and below the plane of the Milky Way.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://news.discovery.com/space/history-of-space/top-10-space-stories-of-2014-readers-choice-141231.htm">
         Top 10 Space Stories of 2014: Readers' Choice
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        "A few million years ago, there was a very energetic event at the galactic center, and we're seeing a remnant," lead author Andrew Fox, of the Space Telescope Science Institute in Baltimore, Maryland, said at a press conference this month. [
        <a href="http://www.space.com/9699-top-10-strangest-space.html">
         The Strangest Things in Space
        </a>
        ]
       </p>
       <p>
        Fox presented the new Hubble observations at the
        <a href="http://www.space.com/28222-american-astronomical-society-meeting-2015-full-coverage.html">
         225th meeting of the American Astronomical Society
        </a>
        in Seattle, revealing the , and agethe Fermi bubbles.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Piercing the Dust and Gas
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.space.com/19099-milky-way-galaxy-giant-geysers.html">
         Fermi bubbles
        </a>
        were first discovered in 2010 by scientists using NASAs Fermi Large Area Telescope, which revealed two lobes of material protruding from the center of the Milky Way. Since then, the features have been studied in the X-ray and radio wavelengths.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://news.discovery.com/space/videos/pluto-flyby-and-black-holes-top-space-events-for-2015-video-150105.htm">
         VIDEO: Pluto Flyby and Black Holes: Top Space Events for 2015
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Fox and his team paired Hubble's Cosmic Origins Spectrograph with a distant quasar to measure the speed and composition of the billowing bubbles. A quasar is a very bright source of light generated by fast-moving particles near a supermassive black hole inside a distant galaxy. Light from the quasar is so strong that it outshines its parent galaxy. The scientists measured how the ultraviolet light from quasar PDS 456 shifted as it passed through the base of the northern bubble.
       </p>
       <p>
        With the help of the
        <a href="http://www.space.com/17262-quasar-definition.html">
         bright quasar
        </a>
        , the team determined that material on the near side of the northern lobe is streaming toward the sun, while material on the far side is zipping away. The material is gushing out of the Milky Way at approximately 900 to 1,000 kilometers per second, or about 2 million mph.
       </p>
       <p>
        At this rate, the scientists calculated that the event that created the lobes occurred between 2.5 and 4 million years ago.
       </p>
