           <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
            5 Healthy Smoothies Your Taste Buds, Body AND Waistline Will LOVE!
           </h1>
          </header>
          <aside class="post-author">
           <time class="time" datetime="2012-08-27T21:03:47+00:00">
            August 27, 2012
           </time>
           <em>
            by
           </em>
           <a href="http://theindiechicks.com/author/leanne-beyer/" rel="author" title="Posts by Leanne Beyer">
            Leanne Beyer
           </a>
          </aside>
          <div class="post-content-container">
           <figure class="post-gallery">
            <img alt="5 Healthy Smoothies Your Taste Buds, Body AND Waistline Will LOVE!" height="500" src="http://theindiechicks.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/5healthysmoothiesrecipes-e1346116522805-800x500.jpg" width="800"/>
           </figure>
           <div class="post-content entry-content cf" data-first="W">
            <p>
             <!-- BEGIN THEIA POST SLIDER -->
            </p>
            <p>
             We all love a good smoothie right?! Too bad many store bought smoothies are packed with empty calories from the added sugars (honey included) with lack of fiber and protein. These healthy and EASY smoothie recipes dont only taste delicious, theyre extremely good for you AND your waistline! So dust of your blender, get your lovely ass to the store and get these simple ingredients. Blend away and ENJOY!
            </p>
            <h2>
             <strong>
              1.
             </strong>
             <strong>
              Flaxberry
             </strong>
            </h2>
            <p>
             <em>
              Packed with essential
             </em>
             <em>
              omega-3 fatty acids
             </em>
             <em>
              from the flax seeds, lots of fiber and protein to keep so fuller longer and a healthy amount of anti-oxidants from the berries All that for only 130 calories per person!
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             Note:
             <em>
              Preferably grind your own flax seeds in a coffee / spice grinder. This will keep the nutrition value optimal and keep the flax seeds from going rancid.
              <span id="more-3726">
              </span>
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             1  cups blueberries
             <br/>
              cup plain fat-free Greek yogurt
             <br/>
              cup unsweetened almond milk
             <br/>
             2 tablespoons ground flax seeds
             <br/>
             Few drops of stevia
             <br/>
             1 cup ice cubes
            </p>
            <p>
             Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth  add extra water if needed.
            </p>
            <p>
             Serves 2
             <br/>
             Calories per person: 130; Fat: 4g; Protein: 5.5g; Carb: 18.5g; Fiber: 5.4g
            </p>
            <h2>
             2. PB&amp;Jerries
            </h2>
            <p>
             <em>
              These berries are packed with fiber and peanut butter adds heart-healthy fat. The combination of fats with carbs will help your body to keep your blood sugar steady.
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             1 cup raspberries
             <br/>
              cup cherries  pitted!
             <br/>
             2 tablespoons unsweetened almond milk
             <br/>
             2 tablespoons natural smooth peanut butter
             <br/>
             Few drops of stevia
             <br/>
             1 cup ice cubes
            </p>
            <p>
             Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth  add extra water if needed.
            </p>
            <p>
             Serves 2
             <br/>
             Calories per person: 160; Fat: 9.5g; Protein: 5.5g; Carb: 17g; Fiber: 6g
            </p>
            <h2>
             <strong>
              3.
             </strong>
             <strong>
              Stofuberry
             </strong>
            </h2>
            <p>
             <em>
              The soft tofu gives this smoothie a silky texture as well as satisfying protein. I promise you; you wont be able to tell theres tofu in your drink!
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             1  cups strawberries without the stem
             <br/>
              cup soft tofu
             <em>
              (approx. 4 ounces)
             </em>
             <br/>
             2 pitted dates
             <br/>
             2 tablespoons ground flax seeds
             <br/>
             2 tablespoons unsweetened almond milk
             <br/>
             1 cup ice cubes
            </p>
            <p>
             Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth  add extra water if needed.
            </p>
            <p>
             Serves 2
             <br/>
             <em>
              Calories per person: 142; Fat: 5.7g; Protein: 7g; Carb: 18g; Fiber: 5.1g
             </em>
            </p>
            <h2>
             4. Spiced Banadate
            </h2>
            <p>
             <em>
              This smoothie will give you instant energy from the simple sugars in dates and banana. To balance out the sugars, you get a healthy amount of protein from the cottage cheese. The oats and cinnamon will help to keep your blood sugar steady. One of my favorite pre-workout snacks!
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
              cup fat-free cottage cheese
             <br/>
             1 medium very ripe banana
             <br/>
             3 pitted dates
             <br/>
             2 tablespoons old-fashioned rolled oats
             <br/>
             Generous pinch ground cinnamon (to taste)
             <br/>
             Pinch ground ginger (to taste)
             <br/>
             Even smaller pinch ground nutmeg (to taste)
             <br/>
             1 cup ice cubes
            </p>
            <p>
             Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth  add extra water if needed.
            </p>
            <p>
             Serves 2
             <br/>
             Calories per person: 150; Fat: &lt;1g; Protein: 7.6g; Carb: 30g; Fiber: 3g
            </p>
            <h2>
             <strong>
              5.
             </strong>
             <strong>
              Almondberry Chocolate
             </strong>
            </h2>
            <p>
             <em>
              Greek yogurt + almond butter + blackberries + chocolate?! Does this even need an explanation on why to drink this? For me personally; it doesnt get much better than this  Super yummy! This is an all-round winner: protein  healthy fats  lots of fiber and packed with anti-oxidants, vitamins and minerals!
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             1  cups blackberries
             <br/>
              cup plain fat-free Greek yogurt
             <br/>
             2 tablespoons almond butter
             <br/>
             1 tablespoon raw cacao powder
             <br/>
             Few drops of stevia
             <br/>
             1 cup ice cubes
            </p>
            <p>
             Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth  add extra water if needed.
            </p>
            <p>
             Serves 2
             <br/>
             Calories per person: 195; Fat: 10.5g; Protein: 8.5g; Carb: 19g; Fiber: 8g
            </p>
            <p>
             These are just a few of the MANY healthy options out there! I hope youre inspired to fix yourself a healthy smoothie and for more tips and tricks on food, health and overall wellbeing please visit my blog at
             <a href="www.eatandgetmoving.wordpress.com">
              www.eatandgetmoving.wordpress.com
             </a>
            </p>
