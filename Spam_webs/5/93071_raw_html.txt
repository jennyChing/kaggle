             <h1>
              Sneak Peak: Top 10 Things to Know for Your Next Oracle Audit
             </h1>
            </div>
            <div class="bi_date">
             March 16 2015
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="description">
          <p>
           Receiving a notification letter from Oracle
           <sup>
            
           </sup>
           informing you that you have been selected for a license audit can be daunting. Many questions start buzzing through your head - who pays for the cost of the audit? If we are found non-compliant will Oracle be lenient with us? Knowing what steps you can take to prepare will help ease your mind and streamline the process. Below is a preview of our
           <a href="http://emerset.com/resources">
            Downloadable Whitepaper
           </a>
           . Find out the answers to the 10 most frequently asked questions in this guide to Oracle audits and license reviews.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           An Oracle license audit, also referred to as a license review, is done in order to:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Understand oracle asset ownership rights and limitations
           </li>
           <li>
            Identify potential requirements in meeting regulatory standards
           </li>
           <li>
            Plan and budget accurately for future projects
           </li>
           <li>
            Observe and enhance oracle asset utilization
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What happens during an Oracle License Audit?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The audit begins with a notification letter telling you that you have been selected for the license audit and which programs will be reviewed. The letter will also let you know which license management service partner will perform your audit. There will be an introduction meeting where auditors will fill you in on the next steps of the audit process. Project expectations and deadlines for requested information will also be decided on and discussed. Once this data is gathered, it is shared with Oracle and analyzed. After any follow-up questions, the analysis is gathered into a final report. Any issues of non-compliance are to be resolved within 30 days.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What software programs are typically included in an Oracle License Audit?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Any of Oracle's software programs are subject to a license audit; however an audit team tends to focus on:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Oracle Database - Database Options and Database Enterprise Managers
           </li>
           <li>
            Oracle Application Server
           </li>
           <li>
            SOA
           </li>
           <li>
            Weblogic
           </li>
           <li>
            And more ...
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           On average you can expect to be audited every three to four years. Being prepared will help you contain costs and simplify the overall process of your next Oracle license audit. To learn more about what you should know for your next Oracle Audit, download our whitepaper "
           <a href="http://emerset.com/resources" target="_blank">
            Top 10 Things to Know for Your Next Oracle Audit
           </a>
           ."
          </p>
