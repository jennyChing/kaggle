              <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;display:block;">
               When looking for a way to build a website, most people sign up with a web host and use whatever comes with the hosting. If you like Wordpress or you just want a blog, this is great. But if you want something more for a website that's easy to use and maintain, you will need something different to build your website.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               WORDPRESS
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Wordpress is everyone's favorite. It was built as a blogging platform and continues to this day to be very popular even though it is also used as a website. Here are a few questions that you can answer when considering Wordpress:
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Are you tech savvy?
               <br/>
               Do you just want to blog?
               <br/>
               Are you familiar with short codes?
               <br/>
               How much money do you want to spend on Wordpress?
               <br/>
               Do you have a lot of time to spend on your blogging platform?
               <br/>
               <br/>
               If you want a straight forward blog and you don't mind occasionally dealing with code or plugins for your features to extend the use of your Wordpress site, then Wordpress or Blogger is for you! Make sure you can handle expired plugins and fix any backend problems on your own, you will need some sort of technical knowledge to handle your Wordpress site, or you can always hire someone to fix your blog if you ever need to perform fixes.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               WEEBLY FOR WEBSITES AND BLOGS
               <br/>
               <br/>
               As a designer, I often hear these statements after viewing my Weebly demos: "Can I have all these features with Wordpress?" and "I don't know much about websites" and "I need it to be easy to add my own content". I always tell them that they need a Weebly website. I have tested and used many website builders. I have a few (very expensive) builders that I like, but Weebly is the only builder that's affordable, easier to use and offers just as many features as the expensive builders. Once my clients start using Weebly, I get comments like "I love my new website!" Many hosting companies that offer Wordpress have discovered Weebly and you can check to see if your hosting company offers

                Weebly
               </a>

                Where To Host Your Domain Name
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p class="blog-date">
               <span class="date-text">
                8/1/2013
               </span>
              </p>
              <p class="blog-comments">
               <a class="blog-link" href="http://www.boutique-website-design.com/blog/where-to-host-your-domain-name#comments">

              <div>
               <div align="right" class="wcustomhtml" id="862999866516123387" style="width: 100%; overflow-y: hidden;">
                <a data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonPin" href="//pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.boutique-website-design.com%2F2%2Fpost%2F2013%2F08%2Fwhere-to-host-your-domain-name.html&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.boutique-website-design.com%2Fuploads%2F8%2F9%2F0%2F6%2F8906613%2F1955434.jpg&amp;description=Where%20To%20Host%20Your%20Domain%20Name">

              <span class="imgPusher" style="float:right;height:0px">
              </span>
              <span style="z-index:10;position:relative;float:right;max-width:100%;;clear:right;margin-top:0px;*margin-top:0px">
               <a>
                <img alt="Picture" class="galleryImageBorder wsite-image" src="/uploads/8/9/0/6/8906613/1955434.jpg" style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 10px; margin-right: 0px; border-width:0;"/>
               </a>
               <span class="wsite-caption" style="display: block; font-size: 90%; margin-top: -10px; margin-bottom: 10px; text-align: center;">
               </span>
              </span>
              <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;display:block;">
               Your online business will grow and what works for you in the next three years may not work in the fourth year. Who knows what technical upgrades are in the future? Online businesses move around from host to host all the time. The secret is to keep your domain name stable and secure if you move your website or blog around on the net.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Just think of a big store that has moved out of your neighborhood, they don't take the building with them, but they take the store's name and logo ... wherever they go.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Godaddy.com (and any registrar) allows your domain name
               <em>
                to follow you
               </em>
               wherever you go. You simply point it wherever you have moved your website.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Actually, the easiest way to get a domain name is to signup for it when you are signing up for website hosting, this is recommended for beginners. But if you ever decide to move away from that web host you will need to transfer your domain name away from your current host to a registrar or your new web host. Transferring domain names can be a timely procedure.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Godaddy.com is not the only registrar that is available to host your domain, but it is highly recommended because most, if not all, web hosts include simple instructions for using godaddy.com.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <span>
                It is just easier to move your website and
                <em>
                 point
                </em>
                your domain name to your new website. Having this flexibility makes moving and
                <em>
                 growing
                </em>
                your business faster and easier.
               </span>
               You may keep your website and domain name with your current host for years, but if you ever want to move your website, godaddy.com makes it easier so you don't have to move your domain name too.
