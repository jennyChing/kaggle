          <div style="width:614px;">
           <h3 class="subtitle">
            What to do
           </h3>
           <p class="bodytext">
            First try protecting the egg using just a strong box.
           </p>
           <p class="bodytext">
            Cut the bottle open around the widest part.
           </p>
           <p class="bodytext">
            Put an egg in the bottle and tape the bottle back up.
           </p>
           <p class="bodytext">
            Try dropping it from about waist height.
           </p>
           <p class="bodytext">
            Now try filling the bottle with salty water. Keep adding salt until the egg starts to float.
           </p>
           <p class="bodytext">
            Try dropping the bottle from waist height. Do you get the same result?
           </p>
          </div>
          <div style="width:614px;">
           <h3 class="subtitle">
            What may happen
           </h3>
           <p class="bodytext">
            The egg just in the bottle will smash, but in the salty water it should mostly survive.
           </p>

          <div style="width:614px;">
           <h3 class="subtitle">
            Why does it happen?
           </h3>
           <p class="bodytext">
            If you drop an egg on the floor its speed changes from about 3m/s to stopped in a very short period of time, this means that is accelerates (decelerates) very rapidly. A large acceleration requires a large force and because this force is concentrated in one place the eggshell isn't strong enough to support this force and it breaks.
           </p>
           <table cellpadding="1" cellspacing="1" class="" style="BORDER-RIGHT: 0px solid; BORDER-TOP: 0px solid; BORDER-LEFT: 0px solid; WIDTH: 100%; BORDER-BOTTOM: 0px solid">
            <tbody>
             <tr>
              <td>
               <p class="bodytext">
                <img alt="Egg hitting the ground" src="uploads/RTEmagicC_EggWomb-normal-impact.png.png" style="WIDTH: 300px; HEIGHT: 330px" title="If an egg hits the ground it feels a very large force in one place (c) Dave Ansell"/>
               </p>
              </td>
              <td style="VERTICAL-ALIGN: bottom">
               <p class="bodytext">
                <img alt="Egg afterwards" src="uploads/RTEmagicC_EggWomb-normal-after.png.png" style="WIDTH: 300px; HEIGHT: 128px" title="The concentrated force breaks the egg  Dave Ansell"/>
               </p>
               <p class="bodytext">
               </p>
              </td>
             </tr>
             <tr>
              <td>
               <p class="bodytext">
                When the egg hits the floor it is slowed by a large force from the floor.
               </p>
              </td>
              <td>
               <p class="bodytext">
                This large force causes the shell to break.
               </p>
              </td>
             </tr>
            </tbody>
           </table>
           <p class="bodytext">
           </p>
           <p class="bodytext">
            If you put the egg in a bottle the egg still stops rapidly and the force is still concentrated in one place so the egg still breaks.
           </p>
           <table cellpadding="1" cellspacing="1" class="" style="BORDER-RIGHT: 0px solid; BORDER-TOP: 0px solid; BORDER-LEFT: 0px solid; WIDTH: 100%; BORDER-BOTTOM: 0px solid">
            <tbody>
             <tr>
              <td>
               <p class="bodytext">
                <img alt="Egg hitting the ground in a bottle" src="uploads/RTEmagicC_EggWomb-normal-bottle.png.png" style="WIDTH: 300px; HEIGHT: 333px" title="The acceleration in a bottle is the same and the force is also concentrated  Dave Ansell"/>
               </p>
              </td>
              <td style="VERTICAL-ALIGN: bottom">
               <p class="bodytext">
               </p>
               <p class="bodytext">
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <img alt="Egg broken in a bottle" src="uploads/RTEmagicC_EggWomb-bottle-after.png.png" style="WIDTH: 300px; HEIGHT: 256px" title="The concentrated force again breaks the egg  Dave Ansell"/>
              </td>
             </tr>
             <tr>
              <td>
               <p class="bodytext">
                In a bottle the egg is still stopped very rapidly by a large force in one place.
               </p>
              </td>
              <td>
               <p class="bodytext">
                Again this breaks the egg
               </p>
              </td>
             </tr>
            </tbody>
           </table>
           <p class="bodytext">
            However if the egg is floating in a fluid then the force that slows down the egg is upthrust, the same force which was making it float in the first place, which gets larger the larger the acceleration of the bottle.
           </p>
           <table cellpadding="1" cellspacing="1" class="" style="BORDER-RIGHT: 0px solid; BORDER-TOP: 0px solid; BORDER-LEFT: 0px solid; WIDTH: 100%; BORDER-BOTTOM: 0px solid">
            <tbody>
             <tr>
              <td style="VERTICAL-ALIGN: bottom">
               <p class="bodytext">
                <img alt="Egg in water hittng floor" src="uploads/RTEmagicC_EggWomb-water-impact.png.png" style="WIDTH: 300px; HEIGHT: 365px" title="The force is transferred to the egg evenly by the liquid so the egg is not broken  Dave Ansell"/>
               </p>
              </td>
              <td style="VERTICAL-ALIGN: bottom">
               <p class="bodytext">
                <img alt="Egg floating afterwards" src="uploads/RTEmagicC_EggWomb-water-after.png.png" style="WIDTH: 300px; HEIGHT: 266px" title="The egg survives  Dave Ansell"/>
               </p>
              </td>
             </tr>
             <tr>
              <td>
               <p class="bodytext">
                The egg is accelerated by the upthrust from the liquid which is an evenly distributed force.
               </p>
              </td>
              <td>
               <p class="bodytext">
                The egg is good at resisting this type of force and survives.
               </p>
              </td>
             </tr>
            </tbody>
           </table>
           <p class="bodytext">
            This is one of the reasons that an unborn baby is surrounded by salty amniotic fluid because the baby is a similar density to the amniotic fluid it is floating, so if there are any impacts to the mother the forces are evenly transferred to the baby not doing it any damage.
           </p>
