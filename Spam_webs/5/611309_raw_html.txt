         <h2>
          Fertility MoTs for City staff at lunch
         </h2>

             Hannah Summers

         <p>
          THE largest IVF clinic in Europe will open in London later this month, 
targeting busy City workers with 200 fertility MoTs that they can have 
during their lunch hours.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Create Fertility centre near St Pauls also aims to carry out up to 4,000 
IVF cycles each year, increasing capacity in the capital by an estimated 30%.
         </p>
         <p>
          The move comes amid growing demand for IVF as more and more NHS hospitals 
ignore voluntary guidance that says women under 40 should be offered three 
rounds of IVF and women aged 40-42 one round. Critics say the NHS service is 
a postcode lottery.
         </p>
         <p>
          Geeta Nargund, Create Fertilitys founder, believes the new clinic will 
provide cheaper and more accessible treatment for women and men, with a 
focus on natural-cycle IVF.
         </p>

          But some experts have urged people to exercise caution, saying that success 
rates for natural-cycle IVF were lower than for treatment in which
