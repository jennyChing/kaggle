         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         My Mothers Day this year was pretty laid back. We hung out at home, I sort of slept in, my mom came over for brunch and we went to DHs moms house for the afternoon/dinner. Thats usually how it goes every year. I like as little stress and as little activity as possible, since so much of our normal day-to-day is constant on-the-go. Our moms are the same way. But I hear the stories. Fellow moms talk about the travel, stress, nagging unreal expectations out of kids. All I can think about when the venting ends is Girl, you
         <em>
          need
         </em>
         a spa day, which brought me to creating a list of items you need for
         <strong>
          Post Mothers Day Survival
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.ricola.com/en-us/About-Ricola" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <img alt="Mother's Day Survival Kit #swissherbs" class="aligncenter wp-image-36424 size-full" height="473" src="http://www.blushingnoir.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Mothers-Day-Survival-Kit-swissherbs.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Step 1:
          </strong>
         </em>
         First and foremost you want to focus on items you already have at home. After all of that drama over the weekend who wants to go to the store? Drop the kids at school, take the day off of work if you must and head to the most local of all stores (only if necessary) to pick up anything you may need. Gather that as well as the items you have in the cabinets and get it organized. You dont want to be in the middle of a soak and suddenly remember that you forgot your fave scrub.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.ricola.com/en-us/About-Ricola" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <img alt="Shopping List #swissherbs" class="aligncenter wp-image-36422 size-full" height="462" src="http://www.blushingnoir.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Shopping-List-swissherbs.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Step 2:
          </strong>
         </em>
         Run the bath water to your desired temp. Mine borders on scalding. Throw in a bath bomb, oils, bubbles.. whatever you like. Eucalyptus always relaxes me and I love lavender also. Sink in.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Step 3:
          </strong>
         </em>
         While all of that is percolating apply a mask. I tend to love those mud masks that dry on your face and leave little holes where all of your pores are. IDK why but I love the feeling of it drying and then seeing all that junk.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Step 4:
          </strong>
         </em>
         My bathtub area isnt all that big so there isnt room to set things like a beverage or candle nearby. Candle wise I light something that will coordinate with whatever scent Im using in the tub. I hold off on a beverage (itll come later, trust) and opt instead for
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ricola.com/en-us/About-Ricola" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Ricola Revitalizing Herb Drops
          </a>
         </strong>
         . Maybe its from talking all weekend, maybe its a bit of allergies maybe youre just exhausted but the throat gets dry and it gets scratchy.
         <strong>
          Ricola Revitalizing Herb Drops
         </strong>
         are famous for their unique delicious taste and are effective in providing soothing relief (the secret is in the
         <a href="http://www.ricola.com/en-us/Chrueterchraft" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Chrterchraft
         </a>
         !).
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.ricola.com/en-us/About-Ricola" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <img alt="pampering tools" class="aligncenter wp-image-36423 size-full" height="568" src="http://www.blushingnoir.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/pampering-tools.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Step 5:
          </strong>
         </em>
         Remove the mask and hop out of the tub (not literally, because youll fall) and into the biggest, fluffiest, softest robe ever. Or a silk robe, because maybe youre into that  As soon as Im all dry and comfy I pop on a set of eye gels to take care of any signs of puffiness or exhaustion that may be left over.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Step 6:
          </strong>
         </em>
         As your eye gels do their magic slather on your fave lotions or creams alllll over your body. I like to take my time and really make sure Im not missing any areas and that Im working all of the cream into my skin.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Step 7:
          </strong>
         </em>
         This step is more of a preference. Mani/Pedi time. If you want to leave the house, get someone at your favorite salon to do them. If you dont want to leave the house and/or want to save some cash DIY it with a mani kit and a bright, happy nail color.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Step 8:
          </strong>
         </em>
         If youre still at home, get dressed. I know. Wearing pants sucks. Alas, so does jail so if youre picking up your kids youll need to put some on. Kick back with some tea, lemonade.. whatever youre feeling and do something where you dont have to think. Read a novel, watch Ellen NAP.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.ricola.com/en-us/About-Ricola" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Love Mom #swissherbs" class="aligncenter wp-image-36421 size-full" height="640" src="http://www.blushingnoir.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Love-Mom-swissherbs.jpg" width="480"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         No matter what goes on youve always got love for mom, but you need some love too and you cant be the best
         <em>
          you
         </em>
         without some pampering and taking care of yourself.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          How do you decompress from a stressful holiday?
         </em>
        </p>
