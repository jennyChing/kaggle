                                &lt;div class="post_contents"&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;img class="aligncenter wp-image-8540" src="http://www.havahartwireless.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/flea-and-ticks-feature.jpg" alt="flea and tick prevention feature" width="579" height="241" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Is your poor pooch constantly scratching and licking certain parts of their body? Perhaps your pet has been restless lately, like something is bugging them.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;It could be a lot of little things bugging themfleas and ticks!&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Weve created a graphic with everything you need to know about these pests including how to get rid of fleas and ticks on your pet and in your home.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;When is Flea and Tick Season?&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;While fleas and ticks are active year round, pets are most susceptible to becoming a host to these tiny pests from May to September.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Fleas prefer warmer temperatures but ticks are hardierand can survive colder climates.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Check out the graphic below to see if your state is one of the worst places to live for dogs and cats in regards to fleas and ticks.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;img class="aligncenter wp-image-8539" src="http://www.havahartwireless.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Fleas-and-ticks3-01-1.png" alt="Flea and Tick prevention on dogs" width="580" height="4766" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Whats the Difference Between Fleas and Ticks?&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Though both pests look similar, fleas and ticks have very different life cycles and habits. Fleas tend to stick to one host for the duration of their lives, which can last for more than 100 days. Adult fleas lay 20-40 eggs per day for several weeks and these eggs are shed wherever the host goes.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Ticks, on the other hand, have multiple hosts throughout their life, which can last up to 3 years. Female ticks can lay thousands of eggs but only once in their life and immediately afterwards they die.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Ticks can also transmit deadlier diseases than fleas. Fleas spread bartonellosis and tapeworm. Ticks spread Lyme disease and Rocky Mountain spotted fever.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Can Humans Get Fleas?&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Its not just your pets who are at risk. Fleas bite humans, too. Their bites look like small, red bumps with a red halo around the center. Most often, youll find their itchy bites around your ankles or waist.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Most humans dont contract any diseases from flea bites but thats not the case for your pets.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;How to Get Rid of Fleas and Ticks?&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Its important to keep your lawn cut short and to vacuum your home at least once a week to keep fleas and ticks away.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Here are a few tips for &lt;a href="http://www.havahartwireless.com/blog/how-to-protect-your-dog-from-fleas-and-ticks.php"&gt;flea and tick prevention on dogs and cats&lt;/a&gt;:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Wash your pets bedding 1-2 times per week.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Always check pets for fleas and ticks after they come in from outside.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Provide a flea collar, topical or chewable flea preventives for your pets.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Keep your pet away from dense shrubbery and tall grass.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p&gt;If you think your pet already has fleas, wash them with a flea and tick shampoo and then use a flea comb to get the little pests out of their fur. If youve found a tick on your pet, &lt;a href="http://www.havahartwireless.com/blog/keeping-ticks-off-your-dog.php"&gt;follow the instructions here to remove it quickly.&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

Perhaps your pet has been restless lately, like something is bugging them.It could be a lot of little things bugging themfleas and ticks!Weve created a graphic with everything you need to know about these pests including how to get rid of fleas and ticks on your pet and in your home.When is Flea and Tick Season?While fleas and ticks are active year round, pets are most susceptible to becoming a host to these tiny pests from May to September.Fleas prefer warmer temperatures but ticks are hardierand can survive colder climates.Check out the graphic below to see if your state is one of the worst places to live for dogs and cats in regards to fleas and ticks.Whats the Difference Between Fleas and Ticks?Though both pests look similar, fleas and ticks have very different life cycles and habits. Fleas tend to stick to one host for the duration of their lives, which can last for more than 100 days. Adult fleas lay 20-40 eggs per day for several weeks and these eggs are shed wherever the host goes.Ticks, on the other hand, have multiple hosts throughout their life, which can last up to 3 years. Female ticks can lay thousands of eggs but only once in their life and immediately afterwards they die.Ticks can also transmit deadlier diseases than fleas. Fleas spread bartonellosis and tapeworm. Ticks spread Lyme disease and Rocky Mountain spotted fever.Can Humans Get Fleas?Its not just your pets who are at risk. Fleas bite humans, too. Their bites look like small, red bumps with a red halo around the center. Most often, youll find their itchy bites around your ankles or waist.Most humans dont contract any diseases from flea bites but thats not the case for your pets.How to Get Rid of Fleas and Ticks?Its important to keep your lawn cut short and to vacuum your home at least once a week to keep fleas and ticks away.Here are a few tips for flea and tick prevention on dogs and cats:Wash your pets bedding 1-2 times per week.Always check pets for fleas and ticks after they come in from outside.Provide a flea collar, topical or chewable flea preventives for your pets.Keep your pet away from dense shrubbery and tall grass.If you think your pet already has fleas, wash them with a flea and tick shampoo and then use a flea comb to get the little pests out of their fur.
