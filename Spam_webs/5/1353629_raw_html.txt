          <h1 class="entry-title">
           The Hackaball: Programmable Playtime Is Here!
          </h1>
          <div class="ad ad-single ad-single-after-title">
           <!-- The Creative Ham - Text Link Ads (Blog) -->
           <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-0844911714157510" data-ad-slot="2435525190" style="display:inline-block;width:468px;height:15px">
           </ins>
           <script>
            (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
           </script>
          </div>
          <div class="entry-summary">
           <p>
            <a href="https://cdn1-thecreativeham.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Hackaball.jpg">
             <img alt="Hackaball - Half App, Half Ball" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4633" height="360" src="https://cdn1-thecreativeham.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Hackaball.jpg" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Todays parents are constantly torn between two dilemmas.
            </strong>
            Dilemma A: The Ward Cleaver Principle, and Dilemma B: The Edward Zuckerberg Principalyup, even Mark has a dad.
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Dilemma A
             </strong>
             is essentially the struggle to prevent your offspring from becoming chubby, diabetic zombies attached to the teet of technology and fueled by sugar water. This is what drives parents to put a time limit on the XBOX controller, and kick their kids out of the house to go play (read: awesome parents).
            </li>
            <li>
             <strong>
              Dilemma B
             </strong>
             , on the other hand, essentially acknowledges that theres a baconload of money to be made by the young entrepreneur out there who can establish career skills prior to establishing facial hairsee
             <a href="http://nypost.com/2015/02/19/meet-the-15-year-old-babysitting-boss-poised-to-make-millions/" target="_blank" title="Poised To Maek Millions">
              Noa Mintz
             </a>
             for reference.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            Now lets face it, as selfless as we all are (read: not actually that selfless), if we can capitalize on our offsprings genius while teaching them to be successful entrepreneurs, weve essentially just won at life. And men, its only an added bonus that during the process, we are guaranteeing that we can retire at an age prior to the man-boobs setting in.
           </p>
           <h3>
            Yet, heres the problem:
           </h3>
           <p>
            Never does one find themself learning to code whilst running a mean
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curl_%28route%29" target="_blank" title="For Those Of You ho Don't Know What A Button Hook Is">
             button hook
            </a>
            , nor does one find themself rounding 3rd base with an iPad in hand.
            <strong>
             Enter the Hackaball.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div class="wp-video" style="width: 600px; ">
            <!--[if lt IE 9]&gt;&lt;script&gt;document.createElement('video');&lt;/script&gt;&lt;![endif]-->
            <video class="wp-video-shortcode" controls="controls" height="337" id="video-4602-1" preload="metadata" width="600">

             </a>
            </video>
           </div>
           <p>
           </p>
           <h3>
            Hidden under the guise of
            <em>
             active game play
            </em>
            , Hackaball embodies the foundational building blocks for open source design.
           </h3>
           <p>
            <a href="https://medium.com/@stueccles/how-we-took-our-hackaball-side-project-all-the-way-to-kickstarter-3086c2f32465" target="_blank" title="Side Project Gone Viral">
             Originally a side project of Made by Many
            </a>
            , a product innovation studio in London, Hackaball is gaining exponential traction. With Hackaball, your kids can build and hack their own games, and then get off their butts to go reap the physical benefits/fun of their mental prowess. In short, Dilemma A is solved by Dilemma B, and somewhere Nerf is crying.
            <strong>
             Meanwhile, people are talking:
            </strong>
