                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     The Lacquer Legion June 2014: On Holiday!
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6271947230467717649">
                     Hi everyone! Another prompt from
                     <b>
                      <a href="https://www.facebook.com/thelacquerlegion">
                       The Lacquer Legion
                      </a>
                     </b>
                     , and this one was so much fun:
                     <i style="font-weight: bold;">
                      On Holiday
                     </i>
                     ! As with all of the The Lacquer Legion prompts, this can be interpreted in many ways, but I decided to do a manicure inspired by my family's favorite vacation spot: Sunset Beach, North Carolina. Sunset Beach is at the very southern point in North Carolina, so it's pretty close to Myrtle Beach and all of the fun stuff there, but it's much quieter and more quaint. I've been going with my husband's extended family for seven years now, and I love it there!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Next to Sunset Beach, there is a small island called Bird Island, which is part of the North Carolina Coastal Reserve. You can walk there from Sunset Beach (just pack some water and sunscreen!), and it is just beautiful. You start out surrounded by families and beach houses, and then eventually it's just dunes and sea turtle nests and the occasional beach comber! On Bird Island is the famous Kindred Spirit mailbox...it's quite a journey to find it, but there is a mailbox up on the dune, with a bench beside it. People place letters, dreams, wishes, and prayers inside it. Usually, there are notebooks and pens inside it, and you can add your thoughts to it. It's so peaceful and beautiful and has become a treasured tradition for me and the family. Read more about The Kindred Spirit [
                     <a href="http://travelgirlsblog.wordpress.com/2012/02/24/453/">
                      here
                     </a>
                     ].
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/--XuMYBIOIbg/U5XK9T7_XxI/AAAAAAAAZE8/aht1al-FLHo/s1600/kindredspiritCollage.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="260" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/--XuMYBIOIbg/U5XK9T7_XxI/AAAAAAAAZE8/aht1al-FLHo/s1600/kindredspiritCollage.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     So I set out to create a manicure inspired by The Kindred Spirit! I love how it turned out :)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-vdPLLlXP-rc/U5XLki9NnhI/AAAAAAAAZFM/KHWnWCQgnPU/s1600/1-P6091742.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-vdPLLlXP-rc/U5XLki9NnhI/AAAAAAAAZFM/KHWnWCQgnPU/s1600/1-P6091742.JPG" width="638"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     I started out by painting my nails with the sandy color and then used my striping nail art brush to paint the sky in. Then I added all the detail! The sand was done in
                     <b>
                      OPI My Vampire Is Buff
                     </b>
                     with detail in
                     <b>
                      OPI You're So Vain-illa
                     </b>
                     . The sky is
                     <b>
                      Zoya Blu
                     </b>
                     with clouds in
                     <b>
                      Wet N Wild Wild Shine French White
                     </b>
                     . The dune grasses were done in
                     <b>
                      Zoya Tilda
                     </b>
                     and
                     <b>
                      Zoya Hunter
                     </b>
                     . The mailbox and bench were done in
                     <b>
                      Wet N Wild Wild Shine Black Creme
                     </b>
                     ,
                     <b>
                      Wet N Wild Wild Shine Red Red
                     </b>
                     , and the black mixed with French White. The birds were done with Black Creme. I sealed everything in with two coats of
                     <b>
                      Glisten &amp; Glow HK Girl
                     </b>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-0RVbL1hrGnI/U5XLky12VNI/AAAAAAAAZFY/eegYZdAMLzQ/s1600/2-P6091747.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-0RVbL1hrGnI/U5XLky12VNI/AAAAAAAAZFY/eegYZdAMLzQ/s1600/2-P6091747.JPG" width="638"/>
                      </a>

                     <b>
                      Wet N Wild Wild Shine Black Creme

                      Wet N Wild Wild Shine Red Red
