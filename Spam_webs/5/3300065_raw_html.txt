        <p>
         There is a saying,
         <em>
           When in New Zealand, do as the Kiwis do
         </em>
         .errr or was that Rome?
        </p>
        <p>
         Either way, New Zealand, a country, packed full of adventures such as bungee jumping, skydiving,
         <a href="http://www.abackpackerstale.com/blogs/hauntingly-beautiful-glow-worm-caves-waitomo/" target="_blank" title="The Hauntingly Beautiful Glow Worm Caves of Waitomo">
          glow worms caves
         </a>
         , and rafting, is a mecca to
         <a href="http://www.abackpackerstale.com/blogs/my-scariest-adventure-travel-moments-and-why-i-love-them/" target="_blank">
          adventure travelers
         </a>
         like myself.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           That is.Normally!
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         As the propeller of the tiny pink plane turned over, I felt a not so adventurous shiver run down my spine. The plane started its slow crawl onto the runway before picking up speed in preparation for its 20 minute ascent to 15,000 ft.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Skydiving in Lake Tapou" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4750" height="677" src="http://www.abackpackerstale.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/skkk.jpg" title="" width="902"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Somehow I had decided that this altitude was just right for jumping out of a perfectly good aircraft.
         <em>
          *Slap hand against forehead*
         </em>
         . At least I got see see the vibrant rolling hills of
         <a href="http://www.abackpackerstale.com/travel-blog/hobbiton-a-tour-through-the-shire-new-zealand">
          Hobbiton
         </a>
         before I die I thought.
        </p>
        <p>
         Yep, I was about to make my first skydive over the gorgeous mountains of Taupo, New Zealand. It was a perfect day for Skydiving in New Zealand.
        </p>
        <p>
         I never before realized that twenty minutes is an extremely long time to contemplate your immediate future. I quickly reflected on my life
         <a href="http://www.abackpackerstale.com/travel-blog/20-things-you-learn-from-traveling-in-your-20s" title="20 Life Lessons You Learn Traveling the World in Your 20s">
          travelling the world
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         I kept repeating
         <a href="http://www.skydivetaupo.co.nz/" target="_blank">
          Skydive Taupos
         </a>
         logo which was plastered on the side of the plane,
         <em>
          <strong>
           Fear Is Temporary. Achievement is Permanent.
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-w24NgCk/A" title="Photo &amp; Video Sharing by SmugMug">
          <img alt="Photo &amp; Video Sharing by SmugMug" src="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-w24NgCk/0/L/Skydive%20Tapou-L.jpg" title="Photo &amp; Video Sharing by SmugMug"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         My skydive instructor, a tall Australian with a beard that would rival Zeus himself, scooted close and started fiddling with buckles, tightening straps, and checking cords. At least I wasnt alone in this, I thought.
        </p>
        <p>
         It felt like a group of espresso fueled butterflies were running a marathon in my stomach, but overall I wasnt nearly as nervous as I envisioned myself.
        </p>
        <p>
         Forcing the butterflies to subside, I tuned my head to face my dive partner, Amos, and got up the nerve to ask,
         <em>
          How many jumps have you done?.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Closing my eyes, I turned back around and held my breath, I hadnt been this nervous about someones answer since
         <a href="http://www.abackpackerstale.com/blogs/the-day-i-went-to-lunch-in-the-back-of-a-russian-cop-car/" target="_blank" title="The Day I Went to Lunch in the Back of A Russian Cop Car">
          the cops said they werent going to takeme in Russia.
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         If his answer was anything under 100, I knew it would thrust my fear level from proceed with caution to abort mission status.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          9,000,
         </em>
         he replied as I exhaled a sigh of relief. I could hear the smile in his voice as he said, You have nothing to worry about.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is my office. He motioned out the window to the beautiful cloud covered mountains, the deep blue lake, and the rolling green hills of New Zealand. 
         <em>
          Damn. I thought my nomadic office of beaches, coffee houses, and hostel common rooms were good. This guys got it made! Now this is a
          <a href="http://www.abackpackerstale.com/travel-blog/10-travel-jobs-that-give-you-freedom-to-travel-the-world" title="10 Travel Jobs That Give You the Freedom to Travel The World">
           travel job that I want!
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="View from my Skydiving Plane" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4748" height="677" src="http://www.abackpackerstale.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/skydive.jpg" title="" width="902"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         He pointed to the snow covered mountain jutting past the clouds.
        </p>
        <p>
         The sun reflecting on the snow made the mountain appear half gold.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Its Mt. Doom from Lord of the Rings,
         </strong>
         he said. Being a massive Lord of the Rings fan, the thought of skydiving over Mt Doom replaced all my nervousness with excitement. Then the plane door slid open.
        </p>
        <p>
         A gust of cold air hit me like a hard slap in the face. The sobering reality of the situation clamped down on every synapse in my brain. Suddenly this skydiving thing took on a new level of realness.
        </p>
        <p>
         I watched almost in a daze, as three different skydiving pairs sat on the edge and then in a split second, disappear on a speedy descent towards the massive, solid object below  earth.
        </p>
        <p>
         From behind, my tandem partner pushed me across the seat in a massive scoot, waking me from my stupefied state. Instantly, a wave of emotions shot through my head. Anxiety pulsated through my veins. Strangely, I was conscience that my body was numb while my mind urgently screamed that this was totally insane.
        </p>
        <p>
         All my determination went into not shouting,
         <em>
          Hey Beardman,
          <strong>
           STOP
          </strong>
          scooting us to the edge of the door!.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         After a couple more scoots, we were unbelievably sitting on the ledge. Looking down, I zeroed in on my legs dangling 15,000 feet above the world.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          I knew I had to get control.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Closing my eyes and taking a deep breath, I cleared my head of everything but one simple thought.
         <em>
          I want to live this moment entirely
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         I lifted my head as the adrenaline of life conquered all my fear. I felt myself start to lean forward and ever so gently, somersault out of the plane.
        </p>
        <p>
         I was flying!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-9WHf7Lj/A" title="">
          <img alt="" src="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-9WHf7Lj/0/L/Skydive%20NZ-L.jpg" title=""/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It took a second to register and to come to terms with the rush of my thoughts and feelings.
        </p>
        <p>
         First, I noticed that it felt more like floating than falling. Fear was conquered by a wave of massive adrenaline and yet at the same time, peacefulness.
        </p>
        <p>
         As I took in the landscape, the wind beat across my face. My lips curved into a smile and I started screaming excitedly.
         <em>
          (Which is why, my face, in the video is red and looks like I am going to pass out).
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-vD57shR/A" title="">
          <img alt="" src="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-vD57shR/0/L/ydive%20tapou-L.jpg" title=""/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         My guide and I tossed and turned as we took in the unrivaled view of Lake Taupoin the early morning sun, a view that only New Zealand can provide.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then we started playing with the camera, giving thumbs up, gnarly signs, and showing off the writing on my hands
         <em>
          (Adventure Rocks)
         </em>
         . Garth made a gesture and I knew he was asking if I was comfortable enough to do some spins.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-cHnFBzn/A" title="">
          <img alt="" src="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-cHnFBzn/0/L/post-L.jpg" title=""/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Yes please!,
         </em>
         I gestured. We spun around the lake and mountains behind us and the bright green hills in front of us. In the 60 seconds of free fall, I felt free and alive.
        </p>
        <p>
         I felt a tiny jerk as the parachute caught us.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-BKwdHVn/A" title="">
          <img alt="" src="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-BKwdHVn/0/L/Parachute-L.jpg" title=""/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Now we were gliding slowly back to the ground, which was perfect since I wasnt in any rush.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-bhRWnbQ/A" title="">
          <img alt="" src="http://stephenschreck.smugmug.com/New-Zelaand-Skydive/i-bhRWnbQ/0/L/koa%20Toa-L.jpg" title=""/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         As we landed, I eagerly thought,
         <em>
          Lets do that again!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Skydiving lets you live in the moment. You dont think about everyday life, worries, or any problems you are facing. They simply dont exist there. It just you, your guide, adrenaline, and one hell of a view. It is one of the most exciting and liberating things I have ever done.
        </p>
        <p>
         The adrenaline buzz lasted all day for me. I was a little uneasy going into skydiving, but now I cant recommend it enough.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/5zP2NZ9865M" width="560">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you are ever in New Zealand, I suggest doing your skydive at LakeTaupo for a few reasons.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           1-
          </strong>
         </em>
         Skydive Taupo was extremely safe. The staff is friendly and the dive partners make you feel comfortable throughout the whole process.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          2-
         </em>
         It is the cheapest place to skydive in New Zealand.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           3-
          </strong>
         </em>
         The views are amazing! Lord of the Rings fans get the opportunity to skydive over Mt. Doom.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           4-
          </strong>
         </em>
         If something goes wrong with the filming equipment or you get less than 300 pictures, they refund your video cost.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           5-
          </strong>
         </em>
         They Pick you up in a Limo!
        </p>
        <h3>
         So what do you think now? Would you skydive?
        </h3>
