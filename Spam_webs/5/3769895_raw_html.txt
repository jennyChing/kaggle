           <h3>
            The Three Tenors
           </h3>
           <p>
            Comprised of Placido Domingo, Jos Carreras and Luciano Pavarotti, the Three Tenors were a talented opera trio of the 1970s and 80s, and, arguably the most famous opera singers in history. They held their debut concert in Rome, July 1990, to an astounding international viewership of over one billion. They found success after touring a number of big cities, and by 1994 they were already well known. Years later they would go on to release their own album, "Carreras, Domingo, Pavarotti: The Three Tenors in Concert", which sold upwards of 10 million copies, and placed them firmly at the top of the classical music scene.
           </p>

           <h3>
            The Three Stooges
           </h3>
           <p>
            This comical trio started out in 1925 as part of a relatively unknown act called "Ted Healy and His Stooges". They were
            <i>
             discovered
            </i>
            in 1930 when they appeared in their debut film, "Soup to Nuts" - a film which, by all accounts, was considered a miserable failure. Despite this, the group, consisting of brothers Moe and Curly Howard and childhood friend Larry Fine, kept on pursuing their dream, and things quickly started looking bright for the trio.
           </p>
           <p>
            From around 1925 until the 1980s, the trio defined a form a slapstick comedy that was unknown at the time. They were loud, obnoxious, and funny as hell. Finally, during the 1980s the trio started receiving the attention they deserved, and critical acclaim followed. They have starred in over 220 films and are loved the world over, filling the older generation with nostalgia and delight, as well as gaining younger fans due to their timeless antics.
           </p>
