     <a class="header_title" href="/the-kudlow-report/">
      Kudlow's Corner
     </a>
     <div class="menu" id="pageHeadNav">
      <ul>
       <li class="first selected">
        <a href="/the-kudlow-report/">
         Kudlow's Corner
        </a>
       </li>
       <li class="">
        <a href="/id/100250309">

        </a>
       </li>
       <li class=" last">
        <a href="/larry-kudlow/">
         Larry Kudlow
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
     </div>
    </h2>
    <div class="story-top">
     <div class="story-header-left twoCol">
      <h1 class="title" itemprop="name">
       What if Obama defended American business for once?
      </h1>
      <div class="source" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
       <a href="/larry-kudlow/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
        Larry Kudlow
       </a>
       |
       <span class="twitter-url">
        <a href="http://twitter.com/Larry_Kudlow">
         @Larry_Kudlow
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <time class="datestamp" datetime="2014-07-25T17:48:05-0400" itemprop="datePublished">
       Friday, 25 Jul 2014 |  5:48  PM ET
      </time>
      <span class="sourcelogo" id="sourcelogo-cnbccom" itemprop="sourceOrganization">
       CNBC.com
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="story-header-right">
      <div>
       <div class="share-count-container hide">
        <div class="share-total">
        </div>
        <div class="share-text">
         SHARES
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="gigyasharebar" id="social_bar_top">
       </div>
      </div>
      <a data-comments-label="Article" data-disqus-identifier="blogpost_101868673" href="#">
      </a>
     </div>
    </div>
    <section class="cols2">
     <div class="unit col1">
      <article>
       <div class="story">
        <div class="content" id="article_body">
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="player embed-container cnbcvideo autoplay">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="player" data-src="http://player.cnbc.com/cnbc_global?byGuid=3000295908&amp;autoPlay=true&amp;endCardGuids=3000383239|3000383217|3000383353" frameborder="0" height="298" id="player_0_0" mozallowfullscreen="" msallowfullscreen="" oallowfullscreen="" scrolling="no" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="533">
            <p>
             Kudlow: Make America tax competitive
            </p>
            <p>
             President Obama blamed Congress for not acting on tax reform. CNBC's Larry Kudlow and Steve Liesman debate if Congress is really to blame. Kudlow says the president is wrong when he says the companies that invert don't pay any taxes.
            </p>
           </iframe>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            Just for once, wouldn't it be great if President Obama actually
            <em>
             defended
            </em>
            American business, instead of
            <em>
             attacking
            </em>
            it?
            <span>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Just once?
            </em>
           </p>
           <br/>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            Wouldn't it be great if Obama
            <em>
             acknowledged
            </em>
            that U.S. firms are overburdened by the highest corporate tax rate among developed countries, and as a result are becoming less and less competitive?
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/07/24/obama-may-be-lots-of-things-that-are-legal-but-not-the-right-thing-to-do-by-the-country.html" target="_self">
             Obama: What's legal might not be right for US
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             Wouldn't it be great if he said he wants to fix this tax imbalance in order to grow the economy faster and give U.S. businesses a leg up on the global scene?
            </span>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             <em>
              Couldn't he just say that?
             </em>
            </span>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            He could say it like this: "Look, I don't want to drive businesses away. I want to keep businesses here. I understand the importance of business. I understand that you can't have a good job without a thriving business. And I get that businesses require capital investment. And I understand that the investment and the business must have a high rate of return,
            <em>
             after-tax
            </em>
            . When that's the case, the company expands, jobs grow, and families have more income to pay for health care, education, and the good life."
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="embed-container image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
           <img alt="" height="298" src="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2014/07/25/101868703-Obama.530x298.jpg?v=1406324351" width="530"/>
           <meta content="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2014/07/25/101868703-Obama.jpg?v=1406324351" itemprop="image"/>
           <meta content="width='530'" itemprop="width"/>
           <meta content="height='298'" itemprop="height"/>
           <meta content="2014-07-25T17:39:11-0400" itemprop="datePublished"/>
           <meta content="2014-07-25T17:38:53-0400" itemprop="dateCreated"/>
           <meta content="2014-07-25T17:39:13-0400" itemprop="dateModified"/>
           <meta content="2014-07-25T17:38:53-0400" itemprop="uploadDate"/>
           <meta content="2099-12-31" itemprop="expires"/>
           <!-- TODO: defaulting to 2099 until we get the expiry date from toolbelt -->
           <meta content="jpg" itemprop="encodingFormat"/>
           <meta content="Getty Images" itemprop="author"/>
           <meta content="Getty Images" itemprop="publisher"/>
           <div class="attribution">
            Getty Images
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            <span>
             Instead, Obama mocks businesses. And now he's hauling out an election-year populist whine about "economic patriotism." He's attacking companies that merge with foreign businesses and reincorporate in lower-tax foreign countries. It's called inversion. And Obama wants to stop it in the name of patriotism.
            </span>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/07/18/is-patriotism-a-virtue-in-businesstax-inversioncommentary.html" target="_self">
             Is patriotism a virtue in business?
            </a>
            <span>
             <br/>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             In an interview with my CNBC colleague Steve Liesman, Obama talked about how businesses are ungrateful for "the range of benefits that have helped to build companies, create value, [and] create profits." He complained that these firms are moving their "technical address simply to avoid paying taxes."
            </span>
            <span>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            That sounds a bit like, "If you've got a business  you didn't build that. Somebody else made that happen."
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             But the issue isn't avoiding all taxes. If a U.S. business reincorporates overseas it still pays the same record-breaking 35-percent tax rate on its U.S. income. The difference is that it doesn't pay 35 percent on profits made in foreign countries. That's the double tax it is trying to avoid.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             We are the only nation that has this goofy system. And it reduces after-tax profits and renders our companies less competitive. That's why nearly $2 trillion in cash from American companies is sitting overseas.
            </span>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             But Obama could turn this around in a blink. He could say, "We need that cash to grow the economy, create more jobs, and yes, to build more infrastructure. So I'm going to grant a small repatriation fee of 5 percent to bring that money back and put it to work at home."
            </span>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            He could then say, "And to make American business number one, we're gonna drop the corporate tax rate from 35 percent to 20 percent  with full cash expensing for new investment  and scrap
            <em>
             all other deductions and special credits
            </em>
            . In other words, we're gonna end crony capitalism and corporate welfare, just as I pledged in my 2008 campaign. And that includes the Ex-Im bank. "
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/07/25/taxes-will-go-up-if-inversions-rise-mark-cuban.html" target="_self">
             Taxes will go up if inversions rise: Mark Cuban
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            He could then say he understands from numerous studies that 70 percent of the benefits of a corporate tax cut will flow to working wage earners while the rest will flow to lower-priced consumers. And he could add that we're going to allow pass-through S-corps and LLCs who now pay the personal tax rate to reincorporate and take advantage of the new lower C-corp rates.
            <span>
            </span>
           </p>
           <br/>
           <span>
            <br/>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container last">
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            And
            <em>
             that's
            </em>
            when he could argue that American companies must come home and stay home.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             Nobody, including myself, likes the idea of foreign-based inversions or foreign-parked cash. But
             <em>
              corporate tax reform
             </em>
             is the solution, not
             <em>
              corporate punishment
             </em>
             .
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             The president sometimes gives lip service to corporate tax reform. But he never follows through. There have been a dozen good proposals down through the years. But Obama keeps blaming Republicans in Congress, even though he has been completely disengaged from any serious talks about tax reform.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             Five years ago, Simpson-Bowles had a good corporate tax reform. The president ignored it. Democratic Senator Max Baucus developed a corporate tax-reform package when he was chair of the Senate Finance Committee. It was completely disregarded by the White House. And Republican Dave Camp, chairman of the Ways and Means Committee, delivered a comprehensive tax reform that included corporations. It wasn't perfect, but the White House overlooked that too.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             And now, it looks like corporate tax reform is dead again this year. The Obama Democrats will try to push through a bill to stop foreign re-incorporations. It will never pass. And they'll keep trashing business and turning up the flame on the class-warfare burner. Divide the country and bash success. The same old tired themes.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             It's a pity. Why isn't it patriotic to stand by large and small businesses, which are at the heart of the American free-market economy? Just this once, can't we get the story right?
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             By CNBC's Larry Kudlow.
            </em>
           </p>
          </div>
