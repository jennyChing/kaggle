    </p>

    <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
     <strong>
      Overview/History:
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p style="margin-top: 0;">
     Rock n Roller Coaster Starring Aerosmith is one of Disney's more exhilarating rides. This extreme coaster is teamed with the exciting 
                      sound of Americas legendary rock band, Aerosmith, creating 
                      a high-speed thrill ride that will literally rock n roll
                      guests.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     This indoor roller 
          coaster features a high-speed launch of 0-60 mph in 2.8 
          seconds, three inversions, rock-concert lighting and a specially 
          created Aerosmith soundtrack blasting from 120 onboard speakers 
          in each coaster train -- all firsts for a Walt Disney World 
        attraction.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="http://photopost.wdwinfo.com/showphoto.php/photo/44520">
      <img alt="Rockin' Roller Coaster" src="http://photopost.wdwinfo.com/data/970/medium/Rock-n-Roller-Coaster_02.JPG" width="620"/>
     </a>
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Rock n Roller Coaster Starring Aerosmith, located near 
                        The Twilight Zone Tower
     <i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal">
     </i>
     of Terror on Sunset Boulevard, represents yet another 
                        breakthrough in Disney theme park rides.
     <span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
     </span>
     Guests board a limo and  blast off- you're rockin' at five Gs as you head to the first upside down loop on the track.
     <span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
     </span>
     By comparison, astronauts aboard the Space Shuttle 
                      experience three Gs at liftoff.
     <o:p>
     </o:p>
     <br/>
     <br/>
     The attraction begins with a tour of the fictional G-Force 
          Records. Here guests encounter Aerosmith finishing 
          up a studio recording session.
     <span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
      They notice all of their "fans" and invite you to join them in their limo as they head to a concert.
     </span>
     You all head to the boarding area  and climb into a 24-passenger stretch limo and
                        swiftly accelerate into the Hollywood night.
     <o:p>
     </o:p>
    </p>
    <p>
     The enclosed experience is dimly lit while Southern 
                      California landmarks illuminate different twists and turns 
        throughout the ride -- 365 days a year, rain or shine.
    </p>
    <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
     <strong>
      Fastpass:
     </strong>
     Yes
    </p>
    <p style="margin-top: 0;">
     <strong>
      Handicapped Accessibility:
     </strong>
     Guests in ECVs must transfer to a wheelchair to go through the queue.
    </p>
    <p class="box-reviews" style="margin-top: 0;">
     <strong>
      Fun Fact:
     </strong>
     For awhile, when this attraction first 
opened, there was a real person who was part of the Aerosmith video 
pre-show scene. He'd pick up a guitar for the band and bring it to their
 "limo". Day in, day out, for each endless group of riders, it was the 
same thing over and over. Fortunately, they set the poor guy free. 
(Rumor has it that he was last seen running down the expressway yelling 
"I didn't study Shakespeare for this.")
    </p>
