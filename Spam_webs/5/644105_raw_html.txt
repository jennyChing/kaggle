         <p>
          <a href="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/give-your-bathroom-tiles-a-makeover.png">
           <img alt="give-your-bathroom-tiles-a-makeover" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5666" data-lazy-src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/give-your-bathroom-tiles-a-makeover.png" height="486" src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="717"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="give-your-bathroom-tiles-a-makeover" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5666" height="486" src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/give-your-bathroom-tiles-a-makeover.png" width="717"/>
           </noscript>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Most of us would never dream of going out with our friends wearing the same ra-ra skirt, fluorescent tights and frilly blouse that we were wearing in 1988. Many of us however are stuck with a bathroom which has not been updated for decades, and as bathroom fashions change just as clothing fashions do, it can look seriously outdated. Replacing a whole bathroom is not cheap though, and if you are living in rented property you may not have the option. If however youve decided you just cant live with those old-fashioned bathroom tiles any more, there are some cheap and cheerful methods of bringing your bathroom into the 21
          <sup>
           st
          </sup>
          century. If youre greatly improving the appearance of the bathroom, chances are the landlord wont object either.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           Painting
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/white-bathroom-with-funky-lego-style-colourful-tiles.jpg">
           <img alt="white bathroom with funky lego style colourful tiles" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5668" data-lazy-src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/white-bathroom-with-funky-lego-style-colourful-tiles.jpg" height="478" src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="717"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="white bathroom with funky lego style colourful tiles" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5668" height="478" src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/white-bathroom-with-funky-lego-style-colourful-tiles.jpg" width="717"/>
           </noscript>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The best way of making over your dated bathroom tiles is with paint. You cant just use any old paint for this though, you will need a specialist product such as Ronseal One Coat Tile Paint, Dulux Tile Paint or Fortress Tile Paint. Measure your walls to work out how much paint youll need. Coverage is detailed on the packaging, but a 250ml can costing around 10 should be enough for 2 square metres. You can also choose between matte, satin and gloss paints depending on the look you are trying to achieve.
         </p>
         <p>
          Using the paint is very easy. With most products there is no need for priming the existing tiles first, and you just paint one even coat all over the surface of the tiles. Allow the tiles to dry out completely before using the bath or shower, and make sure you keep the room well ventilated as you work. Tile paint is available in a wide range of colours, so if you want to get creative by painting a border of a different colour around the wall or doing some other sort of design, you are only limited by your imagination. Floor tiles can also be painted, but you will need to take extra care as the tiles on the floor have to stand up to much more wear and tear than those on the wall.
         </p>
         <p>
          Once you have painted the tiles on the floor  and even if you are using specialist tile paint  you will have to give it at least three coats of varnish to ensure it keeps looking good. Many of the manufacturer and retailer websites have short videos demonstrating how to use these tile painting products and its a good idea to watch these before ploughing ahead with the job.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           Stickers and Transfers
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/bathroom-stencils.jpg">
           <img alt="bathroom stencils" class="aligncenter wp-image-3604" data-lazy-src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/bathroom-stencils.jpg" height="538" src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="717"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="bathroom stencils" class="aligncenter wp-image-3604" height="538" src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/bathroom-stencils.jpg" width="717"/>
           </noscript>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          If your landlord isnt keen on the idea of painting, or if you just want a quicker fix to makeover your bathroom, stickers or transfers could be the perfect solution. Not all stickers are suitable for using in bathrooms, so look for ones which are marked as suitable for rooms with high humidity. If you are trying to cover up tiles which are heavily patterned or brightly coloured with stickers this can prove tricky, but if you shop for stickers which are more darkly coloured or which have a stronger, more modern pattern, you should be able to cover up the old tiles.
         </p>
         <p>
          Stickers and transfers are also great for injecting a bit of personality into a bland, boring bathroom which has plain white wall to ceiling tiles. There are dozens of internet sites selling stickers and showing you how to use them, so browse around for inspiration.
         </p>
         <p>
          For more information on stencils, see
          <a href="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/articles/stencils-in-the-bathroom">
           our article about using stencils in the bathroom
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           Wall Boards
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Reco-Bathroom-Tiling-1.jpg">
           <img alt="Reco Bathroom Tiling" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2794" data-lazy-src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Reco-Bathroom-Tiling-1.jpg" height="280" src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="340"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="Reco Bathroom Tiling" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2794" height="280" src="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Reco-Bathroom-Tiling-1.jpg" width="340"/>
           </noscript>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.plumbworld.co.uk/blog/articles/bathroom-tiling-technology-set-eliminate-mould-good">
           Waterproof wall boards are a modern innovation in the bathroom market
          </a>
          and have many advantages, one of which is that most of them can simply be fixed over the old tiles. Wall boards come in large sheets and in a range of styles and colours. As they are completely waterproof they can be used in a shower cabinet or above the bath, and just need to be sealed around the edges with special strips which can be bought from the retailer. The boards are just trimmed to size then glued on top of the existing tiles so a competent joiner or carpenter should be able to completely transform your bathroom in just a couple of hours. Wall boards are more expensive than paint or transfers, but still a lot cheaper than replacing all of your tiles.
         </p>
