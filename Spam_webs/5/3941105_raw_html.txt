   <b>
    Explanation:
   </b>
   What strange world is this?
   <a href="http://www.josleys.com/show_image.php?galid=347&amp;imageid=9913">
    Pictured above
   </a>
   is no real place but rather a purely mathematical visualization of a
generalization of a fractal into three dimensions.

Classical
   <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/Fractal.html">
    fractal
   </a>
   diagrams are typically confined to the two dimensions inherent in the
   <a href="http://www.mathsisfun.com/numbers/complex-numbers.html">
    complex number
   </a>
   <a href="http://www.mathsisfun.com/algebra/complex-plane.html">
    plane
   </a>
   , 
demarking regions where an
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julia_set">
    iterative function
   </a>
   diverges.

Recently explored additions expand the
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandelbrot_set">
    Mandelbrot set
   </a>
   of fractals to
three dimensions with prescriptions dubbed
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandelbox">
    Mandelbox
   </a>
   and
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandelbulb">
    Mandelbulb
   </a>
   sets.

The results are often visually stunning creations of
   <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/0704.0646">
    virtual worlds
   </a>
   with
limitless detail, some of which you can
   <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EFSyG_FZD7E">
    fly through
   </a>
   .
   <a href="http://www.josleys.com/show_image.php?galid=347&amp;imageid=9913">
    Pictured above
   </a>
   is one such
   <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D2xYjiL8yyE">
    mathematical
   </a>
   <a href="http://qntm.org/trollpi">
    fantasy
   </a>
   , possibly reminiscent of 
some sort of
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steampunk">
    steam
   </a>
   -
   <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/renalou/steampunkian/">
    punkian
   </a>
   ,
   <a href="http://vzn1.files.wordpress.com/2013/02/quantum_corral_nise.jpg">
    quantum-mechanical
   </a>
   landscape.
  </p>
