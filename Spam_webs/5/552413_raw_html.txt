       <section class="photo">
        <p>
         <img alt="news, benefits, britain, migrant, romania, family, home, council, UKIP, government" src="http://cdn.images.express.co.uk/img/dynamic/1/590x/23n05familyTC-490708.jpg"/>
         <span class="photo-caption nointellitxt ctx_blocked">
          Parents Veronica and Mihai with baby Elizabeth and 13 of their 14 children yesterday [Tim Clarke]
         </span>
        </p>
       </section>
       <!-- ENDMAINIMAGE -->
       <section class="text-description">
        <p>
         The super-sized Toma family from Romania were so desperate for a new life they lied so their brood could live together in one of the worst cases of overcrowding ever seen.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         They claim they stand to pocket 55,200 a year in benefits while dreaming of a bigger, taxpayer-funded home. But ina move that will be welcomed by millions of hard-working families, council chiefs have told them: Youre not being housed by us.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Tomas, devout Christians, arrived here from Prahova in Romania in 2012, lured by the prospect of a better life for their children. Hard-working father Mihai, 47, says he earns 1,800-a-month as an electrician.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         But turned away by two letting agencies because of the size of their family, they told a private landlord they were a family of six to secure the property in September 2013.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.express.co.uk/pictures/galleries/2157/Migrants-Sleeping-rough-in-London-s-exclusive-Park-Lane" rel="next" target="_blank">
          <span style="color: #ff0000;">
           <strong>
            **GALLERY: MIGRANTS SLEEPING ROUGH IN EXCLUSIVE AREAS OF LONDON**
           </strong>
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It was only after neighbours raised the issue that the familys extraordinary domestic life was exposed. Housing inspectors said the cramped semi-detached property in Harrow, north-west London, is one of the worst cases of overcrowding they have ever seen.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mihai pays 1,700-a-month rent but the size of his and wife Veronicas brood means they receive a series of benefits. The family say they get 2,500 in tax credit, 1,400 in housing benefit and 700 in child benefit which totals 4,600 a month.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Governments 26,000-a-year benefits cap is not believed to applyto the family as the father is in work and entitled to the tax credits. When Mihais wage is added to the handouts, the family say they enjoy a monthly income of 6,400, or 76,800 a year.
        </p>
        <p>
         To take this home a worker would have to be paid a salary, before tax, of around 125,000. Critics last night said the high figure shows why Britain is now the destination of choice for poor eastern European migrants.
        </p>
       </section>
       <section class="photo">
        <p>
         <img alt="news, benefits, britain, migrant, romania, family, home, council, UKIP, government" src="http://cdn.images.express.co.uk/img/dynamic/1/590x/secondary/183814.jpg" title="news, benefits, britain, migrant, romania, family, home, council, UKIP, government"/>
         <span class="photo-caption">
          The familys three-bedroom house in north-west London yesterday  [Tim Clarke]
         </span>
        </p>
       </section>
       <section class="pull-quote on-left">
        <blockquote class="nointellitxt ctx_blocked" id="nointellitxt">
         <p class="quote">
          The council has promised to help the family search for larger accommodation but yesterday they were told they will not get a council house.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
       </section>
       <section class="text-description">
        <p>
         Speaking to the Daily Express through her daughter, Daniela, 19, mother Veronica, 37, said: The council is worried about us. We hope to have more space. We dont expect 10 bedrooms, but more than three.
        </p>
        <p>
         People will judge us but we have not done bad because we have come here to get a better life.
        </p>
        <p>
         Two agencies didnt accept us because we were too big. We lied to the landlord, saying there were six of us. Lying was the only way we could get a house. Its not good to do it but we didnt have a chance.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its a better life than in Romania. Education is good and there are more opportunities here.
        </p>
        <p>
         The council has promised to help the family search for larger accommodation but yesterday they were told they will not get a council house. Space is at such a premium inside the hot and humid home that the 15 children, aged between four months and 19, are forced to share beds.
        </p>
        <p>
         Two other grown-up children live abroad. Eight of the children are in local state schools while fluent English speaking Daniela is studying business at college. Environmental health officers have visited the house and said the property is statutorily overcrowded, as prescribed by the Housing Act, but the landlord is not to blame.
        </p>
        <p>
         Last night former Harrow Council leader Susan Hall said: This highlights the need not only for continuing reform to our benefits system, but the need to eradicate the sense of entitlement some feel towards it  which has contributed to Britain being an easy target for welfare tourism.
        </p>
        <p>
         The council said the family failed to show up for a meeting last month so would not be looking for alternative housing for them. The family is not eligible to be placed on the housing register as they have not been resident in Harrow for five years. But if they do find somewhere larger they will be eligible for increased housing benefit payments.
        </p>
        <p>
         Harrow housing chief Glen Hearnden said: We are talking to the landlord to try and resolve this informally. If you choose to take your very large family overseas for work, you have a duty to ensure you can afford to house them properly. We just dont have five and six-bedroom houses available to hand out.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ukip MEP Tim Aker said: This simply illustrates Britains soft touch approach.
        </p>
       </section>
