      <h1 class="remove-bottom half-bottom-padding border-bottom entry-title">
       You can now use bitcoins to buy digital content from Microsoft
      </h1>
      <p class="half-bottom meta category byline reviewer vcard">
       <time class="date dtreviewed" datetime="Thursday, 11 December 2014 13:50:33" pubdate="" title="11 December 2014 13:50:33 PM">
        December 11th, 2014, 13:50
       </time>
       <strong>
        by
       </strong>
       <a class="meta author" href="http://blog.gsmarena.com/author/ansh/" rel="author">
        <span class="author fn n">
         Ansh
        </span>
       </a>
       <a class="comment-count meta comments right" href="http://blog.gsmarena.com/can-now-use-bitcoins-buy-digital-content-microsoft//#disqus_thread" rel="comment">
        <span>
         <span class="dsq-postid" rel="77244 http://blog.gsmarena.com/?p=77244">
          13
         </span>
        </span>
        comments
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="entry half-bottom" id="blog-entry">
       <p>
        Nearly a year after
        <a href="http://blogs.bing.com/search/2014/02/10/bitcoin-conversion-now-live/" target="_blank">
         adding
        </a>
        a Bitcoin currency converter to its Bing search engine, Microsoft has started accepting the digital currency as a mode of payment for apps, games, and other digital content on Windows, Windows Phone, Xbox Games, Xbox Music, and Xbox Video stores. A
        <a href="http://www.coindesk.com/microsoft-adds-bitcoin-payments-xbox-games-mobile-content/" target="_blank">
         Coindesk report
        </a>
        says that the software giant has partnered with Bitpay to enable Bitcoin payments.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.gsmarena.com/pics/14/12/microsoft-bitcoin/gsmarena_001.jpg" title="" width="660"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        According to the Redmond-based companys payments information page, which contains detailed steps on how to add money to your Microsoft account with Bitcoin, the feature is currently available to US-based customers who can now use Bitcoin to add money to their Microsoft account.
        <span id="more-77244">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        A couple of points worth mentioning here is that you can only use Bitcoins to purchase digital goods, which means that you cant use them to purchase Microsoft products and services at this time, and money added to your Microsoft account using Bitcoin cannot be refunded.
       </p>
       <p>
        The news also comes around five months after Dell
        <a href="http://www.coindesk.com/computer-giant-dell-now-accepts-bitcoin/" target="_blank">
         announced
        </a>
        that the company would begin accepting the digital currency for most products and services.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="https://commerce.microsoft.com/PaymentHub/Help/Right?helppagename=CSV_BitcoinHowTo.htm" target="_blank">
         Source
        </a>
        |
        <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2014/12/11/microsoft-now-takes-bitcoins/" target="_blank">
         Via
        </a>
       </p>
