          <h3 class="entry-header">
           Your Friday Tutorial: paper sachets
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p style="font-size: 18px; color: #482c1b; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <span style="font-size: 15px; color: #898a49;">
              Aren't these fun? They were ever so much faster and easier to make than the hand-embroidered variety I shared yesterday ... but they smell just as sweet! To make one or two of your own, just follow the simple step
             </span>
             <span style="font-size: 15px; color: #898a49;">
             </span>
             <span style="font-size: 15px; color: #898a49;">
              s below -
             </span>
            </p>
            <p style="font-size: 16px; color: #482c1b; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <span style="font-size: 15px; color: #898a49;">
             </span>
             <a href="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535ed610a970b-popup" onclick="window.open(this.href,'_blank','scrollbars=no,resizable=yes,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0'); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="DSC_0001_edited-2" border="0" class="at-xid-6a010534d1ef94970c010535ed610a970b image-full " src="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535ed610a970b-800wi" title="DSC_0001_edited-2"/>
             </a>
             <strong>
              materials
             </strong>
             patterned paper (My Mind's Eye) + felt flowers and brads (Making Memories) + paper sachets by Kim Morgan, Gilbert AZ
            </p>
            <p style="font-size: 16px; color: #482c1b; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             1. Print the sachet pattern onto the back of your patterned paper.You can find it(along with several other fabulous card and envelope patterns) at
             <a href="http://www.ruthannzaroff.com/mirkwooddesigns/hexagon.htm" target="_blank">
              this
             </a>
             website.
             <a href="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535f3e0a1970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="DSC_0006_edited-1" border="0" class="at-xid-6a010534d1ef94970c010535f3e0a1970c image-full " src="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535f3e0a1970c-800wi" title="DSC_0006_edited-1"/>
             </a>
             2. Cut out along the solid lines. Lightly score at the base of each petal (where the dotted lines fall, onlydo iton the
             <strong>
              front
             </strong>
             side of the paper).
            </p>
            <p style="font-size: 16px; color: #482c1b; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <a href="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535f3e106970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="DSC_0007_edited-1" border="0" class="at-xid-6a010534d1ef94970c010535f3e106970c image-full " src="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535f3e106970c-800wi" title="DSC_0007_edited-1"/>
             </a>
             3. Fold back each petal and, one at a time, run a line of glue along only the right edge, from the tip to the base.
            </p>
            <p style="font-size: 16px; color: #482c1b; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <a href="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535ed622d970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="DSC_0008_edited-1" border="0" class="at-xid-6a010534d1ef94970c010535ed622d970b image-full " src="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535ed622d970b-800wi" title="DSC_0008_edited-1"/>
             </a>
             4. Place approximately 1 tablespoon of potpourri in the center of the sachet (don't over-fill, potpourri needs room to breathe).
            </p>
            <p style="font-size: 16px; color: #482c1b; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <a href="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535f3e16f970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="DSC_0011_edited-1" border="0" class="at-xid-6a010534d1ef94970c010535f3e16f970c image-full " src="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535f3e16f970c-800wi" title="DSC_0011_edited-1"/>
             </a>
             5. Fold in petals, working to the right. Tuck in the last petal and firmly press over the whole sachet to secure.
            </p>
            <p style="font-size: 16px; color: #482c1b; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <a href="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535ed62a9970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="DSC_0012_edited-1" border="0" class="at-xid-6a010534d1ef94970c010535ed62a9970b image-full " src="http://simplescrapbooks.typepad.com/.a/6a010534d1ef94970c010535ed62a9970b-800wi" title="DSC_0012_edited-1"/>
             </a>
             6. Decorate as desired (or leave just as is), and you're done!
            </p>
           </div>
