         Chocolate Oreo Cupcakes with Cookies and Cream Frosting
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-06-09T12:30:24+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fbackforseconds.com%2Fchocolate-oreo-cupcakes-with-cookies-and-cream-frosting%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fbackforseconds.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2013%2F06%2FChocolate-Cookies-and-Cream-Cupcakes.jpg&amp;description=Chocolate%20Oreo%20Cupcakes%20with%20Cookies%20and%20Cream%20Frosting" rel="nobox">

        <p>
         The
         <strong>
          best chocolate cake
         </strong>
         has collided with one of the best cookies 
         <strong>
          Oreos
         </strong>
         ! The cake alone is amazing, but the frosting is outrageous!
         <strong>
          Chocolate Oreo Cupcakes and Cookies and Cream Frosting
         </strong>
         are a match made in heaven!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://backforseconds.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Chocolate-Cookies-and-Cream-Cupcakes.jpg">
          <img alt="Chocolate Oreo Cupcakes with Oreo Frosting! http://backforseconds.com  #recipe #cupcake #chocolate #oreo #frosting" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2903" height="748" src="http://backforseconds.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Chocolate-Cookies-and-Cream-Cupcakes.jpg" title="Chocolate Oreo Cupcakes with Oreo Frosting! http://backforseconds.com  #recipe #cupcake #chocolate #oreo #frosting" width="525"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is such an easy cupcake recipe. There is no reason to buy a cake mix when chocolate cake from scratch is this easy and this scrumptious! Toss in some chopped Oreos for a little something special, and swirl on a generous portion of Oreo frosting, and you will be in cupcake heaven, friend!
        </p>
        <p>
         I made these cupcakes for a birthday party, so I added some edible bling to make them fancy. That part is totally optional but who doesnt love a fancy cupcake?
         <span id="more-2902">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://backforseconds.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Oreo-cupcake-recipe.jpg">
          <img alt="Chocolate Oreo Cupcakes with Oreo Frosting! http://backforseconds.com  #recipe #cupcake #chocolate #oreo #frosting" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2905" height="805" src="http://backforseconds.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Oreo-cupcake-recipe.jpg" title="Chocolate Oreo Cupcakes with Oreo Frosting! http://backforseconds.com  #recipe #cupcake #chocolate #oreo #frosting" width="525"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Things are starting to settle down around here. We moved into our new house a week ago (our second move in 2 weeks) and Im making some progress in the unpacking department.
        </p>
        <p>
         This house is quite a bit larger than our last house, and we need to buy a lot of furniture to fill it up and make it look like someone actually lives here
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://backforseconds.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
         I also need to do some decorating. I need help in this areaany takers?? I have some awesome ideas, but I dont know how to execute them. In my head its amazing, but I dont know how to bring my ideas to life. And then there are rooms that I just have no clue how to arrange and decorate. Someone please come help me!!
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://backforseconds.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
         Would any of you be interested in seeing before and after photos as I tackle each room? Or maybe I will share a photo of how a room looks now and some of you brilliant decorators can tell me what to do! yes?
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyway, back to the cupcakes. If you never make cupcakes from scratch, please try these! They seriously are so simple, and theres nothing quite like a homemade cupcake  especially if its slathered in Oreo frosting! This frosting is not your usual too sweet buttercream. Part of the butter is replaced with cream cheese, and theres just a pinch of salt to keep this luscious frosting from being too sweet. Swirl in some crushed cookies and you have the perfect compliment to the chocolate Oreo cupcakes!
        </p>

         <div class="gmc-recipe-description" itemprop="description">
          Rich chocolate homemade cupcakes get a fun twist with Oreos in the batter! The cookies and cream frosting is the perfect compliment to these scrumptious cupcakes!
         </div>
         <div class="gmc-recipe-ingredients">
          <h2 class="gmc-recipe-subtitle">
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul class="gmc-ingredient-list">
          </ul>
          <h3 class="gmc-recipe-subtitle">
           Cake
          </h3>
          <ul class="gmc-ingredient-list">
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup butter (melted)
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup canola oil
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 1/2 cup sugar
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            3/4 cups cocoa powder
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 1/4 cups flour
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 teaspoon salt
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 teaspoons baking soda
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 eggs
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 3/4 cup buttermilk
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon vanilla extract
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup chopped Oreos
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h3 class="gmc-recipe-subtitle">
           Frosting
          </h3>
          <ul class="gmc-ingredient-list">
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            3/4 cups butter (softened)
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            4oz cream cheese (softened)
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon vanilla extract
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/8 teaspoon salt
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 1/2 cups powdered sugar
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            4-6 heaped tablespoons milk
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/3 cup crushed Oreos
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="gmc-recipe-steps">
          <h2 class="gmc-recipe-subtitle">
           Directions
          </h2>
          <table class="gmc-step-list">
           <tr>
            <td class="gmc-group-list-title" colspan="2">
             Cake
            </td>
           </tr>
           <tr>
            <td class="gmc-step-list-title" colspan="2">
             Step 1
            </td>
           </tr>
           <tr class="gmc-step-list-item">
            <td class="gmc-step-desc" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Preheat oven to 350
             <br/>
             In a large mixing bowl combine all cake ingredients except the Oreos. Mix well. Stir in the Oreos by hand. Fill lined cupcake tins about 2/3 full. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Allow cupcakes to cool completely before frosting.
            </td>
           </tr>
           <tr>
            <td class="gmc-group-list-title" colspan="2">
             Frosting
            </td>
           </tr>
           <tr>
            <td class="gmc-step-list-title" colspan="2">
             Step 2
            </td>
           </tr>
           <tr class="gmc-step-list-item">
            <td class="gmc-step-desc" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             In a mixing bowl beat the butter, cream cheese, vanilla and salt until creamy and smooth. Add the powdered sugar and 4 TBS of milk. Beat well. If too thick add more milk, one TBS at a time. Stir in Oreos. Spread or pipe onto cooled cupcakes. Store covered at room temp for up to two days or refrigerate up to 5 days.
            </td>
           </tr>
          </table>

              <br/>
              1.The oreos dont dissolve or get soggy in the cupcakes, but they do soften a bit. They arent crunchy in the finished cupcake.
              <br/>
              2.The frosting does taste like the oreo cream, but the cookie crumbles really make it great. You could leave out the cookies, but I reccomend leaving them in  it tastes awesome!
              <br/>
              3.I added crumbs of the whole cookie  cream filling and all. If you are wanting to add only the cream centers I would try microwaving them first to soften enough that they mix in smoothly. However, I think it would only add more sweetness to the frosting which really isnt necessary. I wouldnt bother adding just the cream filling.
