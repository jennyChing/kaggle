              <p>
               A home isnt the only thing you can rent these days, especially as consumers look to make less of a financial commitment to a variety of life's needs and wants. (Thats code for: Were all
               <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/article/smart-spending/bargains/deals/5-easy-phone-calls-can-save-you-money">
                looking for deals
               </a>
               , even if it means not taking ownership.)
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Rentals are everywhere. You just need to know where to look.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <strong>
                1. Clothing.
               </strong>
               Why spend hundreds of dollars on an evening dress from Saks (Stock Quote:
               <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/stocks.html?symbol=sks">
                SKS
               </a>
               ) that youll likely only wear once this year? My first stop would be to shop in a friends closet. Next, check local rental boutiques. They exist! At
               <a href="http://ilus-nyc.com/shop/home.php" target="_blank">
                Ilus
               </a>
               , a shop in New York City, you can pick out a designer dress to wear for up to three days. (If you live outside the city, you can arrange to have a dress shipped to you.) Prices range from $60 to $400. At
               <a href="http://www.alexandriasformal.com/page/page/1394292.htm" target="_blank">
                Alexandrias Formal
               </a>
               in Roseville, Calif., you can rent strapless bras, prom and cocktail dresses, bridal gowns and jewelry.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <strong>
                2. Accessories.
               </strong>
               The new Louis VuittonTivoli handbag (Stock Quote:
               <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/stocks.html?symbol=LVMH">
                LVMH
               </a>
               ) retails  for $935, but if youre a member of
               <a href="http://www.bagborroworsteal.com/" target="_blank">
                Bag, Borrow or Steal
               </a>
               , itll cost you $43 for the week or $125 per month. You can choose from a plethora of designer purses and accessories. Prices vary per item. At
               <a href="http://www.frombagstoriches.com/rentbag/pc/index.asp" target="_blank">
                FromBagstoRiches.com
               </a>
               , a similar strategy exists where fancy bag rental prices range from $22.95 to $299.95, depending on the designer and make.
               <br/>
               <strong>
                <br/>
                3. Electronics.
               </strong>
               Want to entertain guests at your next party, but dont want to burn
               <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/article/smart-spending/technology/wii-fit-worth-money">
                $300 on a Wii
               </a>
               ?
               <a href="http://us.zilok.com/" target="_blank">
                Zilok
               </a>
               offers Microsofts Xbox 360 (Stock Quote:
               <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/stocks.html?symbol=MSFT">
                MSFT
               </a>
               ) and Nintendo Wii rentals. While youre there, find rental listings in your ZIP code for computers, phones and PDAs.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <strong>
                4. Athletic Gear.
               </strong>
               Its hard to commit to $400 skis, especially if youre only going on a few ski trips this season. In fact, when it comes to skis, bikes, snowboards, tennis racquets and golf clubs, theres usually no need to invest in owning the stuff if youre not an avid player. Most athletic facilities and shops offer a selection of rental gear all year round. Try also searching for athletic equipment rentals online.
              </p>

              <br/>
              <strong>
               5. Furniture.
              </strong>
              Renting furniture is a great way to inexpensively stage your home for an
              <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/article/moneyinvesting/real-estate/how-recession-proof-your-home-sale">
               open house
              </a>
              or to provide seating for an upcoming business meeting or party. Billionaire investor Warren Buffetts office and home furniture rental company,
              <a href="http://www.cort.com/" target="_blank">
               Cort.com
              </a>
              , offers temporary furniture including conference tables, desks, couches, chairs and lamps. Other national furniture rental companies include
              <a href="http://www6.rentacenter.com/site/page/pg4285.html" target="_blank">
               Rent-A-Center
              </a>
              (Stock Quote:
              <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/stocks.html?symbol=RCII">
               RCII
              </a>
              ) and
              <a href="http://www.rentfurniture.com/" target="_blank">
               American Furniture Rentals
              </a>
              .
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <strong>
               6. Books.
              </strong>
              It may take your local library a while to stock the newest Patricia Cornwell hardcover and it costs $18.45 on
              <a href="http://www.amazon.com" target="_blank">

              </a>

              <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/stocks.html?symbol=amzn">
               AMZN
              </a>
              ). Theres a cheaper alternative. At
              <a href="http://www.bookswim.com/" target="_blank">
               BookSwim
              </a>
              , the Netflix for books, $10 a month gets you unlimited book rentals. Its so far the only web-based book rental company that offers newly-released hardcover titles, along with older books in paperback. College students can also rent textbooks here and at
              <a href="http://www.chegg.com/" target="_blank">
               Chegg.com
              </a>
              , saving an average of 60% to 85% on books per semester. Meantime, theres also
              <a href="http://www.booksfree.com/" target="_blank">
               BooksFree.com
              </a>
              , which offers plans also starting at $10 a month.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <strong>
               7. Home.
              </strong>
              With so many people losing their homes due to foreclosure, and potential first-time buyers turned off by the recent housing debacle, renting living space may be a more suitable choice for some. The timing couldnt be better. Apartment rental rates are dropping across the country, as inventory skyrockets. Thats great news for new tenants and existing renters. Its also a silver lining for folks whove lost their homes due to foreclosure and are desperate to find a new place to live. For new renters, definitely ask for a deal, along with some concessions like free parking or discounts to a local gym. In New York City, where a majority of residents rent their apartments, rates have fallen five to six percent in the last year and there are anecdotes of people negotiating deals 20% less than the asking price.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <strong>
               8. Cars.
              </strong>
              Maybe you work mostly from home or maybe everything you need is within walking distance.
              <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/article/smart-spending/autos/back-basics-7-ways-save-your-next-car">
               Buying a car
              </a>
              or leasing one for a few years may be more of a commitment than you can afford to make right now. Companies such as
              <a href="http://www.connectbyhertz.com/" target="_blank">
               ConnectByHertz.com
              </a>
              and
              <a href="http://www.zipcar.com/" target="_blank">
               ZipCar
              </a>
              , which operate pay-as-you-go car agencies, are becoming more popular. Members pay $8.50 an hour for using a car at Connect By Hertz (Stock Quote:
              <a href="http://www.mainstreet.com/stocks.html?symbol=HTZ">
               HTZ
              </a>
              ). At ZipCar, share cars and pay roughly $10 an hour during the week.
