        <section class="zn zn-body-text zn-body zn--idx-0 zn-has-multiple-containers zn-has-32-containers" data-containers="32" data-eq-pts="xsmall: 0, medium: 460, large: 780, full16x9: 1100" data-vr-zone="zone-1-0" data-zn-id="body-text" id="body-text">
         <div class="l-container">
          <div class="el__leafmedia el__leafmedia--storyhighlights el__leafmedia--">
           <div class="el__storyhighlights_wrapper">
            <div class="el__storyhighlights">
             <h3 class="el__headline">
              Story highlights
             </h3>
             <ul class="el__storyhighlights__list">
              <li class="el__storyhighlights__item el__storyhighlights--normal">
               Certain types of depressive thinking can spread from roommates, a new study shows
              </li>
              <li class="el__storyhighlights__item el__storyhighlights--normal">
               Researchers studied young adults living in a college dorm
              </li>
              <li class="el__storyhighlights__item el__storyhighlights--normal">
               Brooding can be contagious between those living closely, the study found
              </li>
             </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="ad ad--epic" data-ad-text="show">
             <div class="ad-ad_ns_btf_01" id="ad_ns_btf_01">
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           We don't think of emotional states as passing from one person to another, but a new study suggests some depressive thoughts can go viral.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Researchers studying a group of college students found that certain types of depressive thinking can spread from close-living roommates like a lingering flu.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Although many people see
           <a href="http://topics.time.com/depression/" target="_blank">
            depression
           </a>
           as a chemical imbalance in the brain, scientists say social context and the way you see yourself and the world can be critical in causing and sustaining the illness, which affects around 10% of college-age adults.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           "Thinking styles are a really important factor in risk for depression," says the study's lead author Gerald Haeffel, associate professor of clinical psychology at Notre Dame University. "How one thinks about life
           <a href="http://topics.time.com/stress/" target="_blank">
            stress
           </a>
           and negative moods is one of the best predictors that we have of future depression."
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Haeffel and his colleagues recognized that starting life in a college dorm -- with students transitioning from the familiarity of high school and family and venturing into a completely new social setting -- would serve as an ideal real-world laboratory for studying how social connections and thinking styles of some students can influence others, and how these interactions can affect depression.
          </p>
