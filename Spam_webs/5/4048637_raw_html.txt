          ad:tech NY keynote rundown
         </h1>

        <div class="author">
         <img class="avatar" src="http://45.55.255.229/jcPJvOvnsd8nbudyGSnb6w0S02I=/34x34/smart/http://45.55.255.229/user/image/509051/profile_image-509051.jpg"/>

        <div class="bodycopy">
         <p>
          The ad:tech event in New York is just around the corner so its about that time that you start thinking about how youre going to schedule yourself. As usual, the keynote presentations look pretty stacked with some of the best panels possible talking about the future of digital advertising as 2014 leads to 2015.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           The Modern CMO
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          The first days keynote is happening on the afternoon of Nov. 5. Its entitled The Modern CMO and the Evolution of Agency Relevancy and collects a number of viewpoints about what being a chief marketing officer means in the digital era.
         </p>
         <p>
          The role of the CMO is changing, and we couldnt be more excited to have two leading marketing experts share their insights on how marketing technology and innovation are driving the need for a digitally driven and creative CMO, Kimberly Dunn, marketing director for ad:tech, said in a release back in August when the keynote panelists were announced.
         </p>
         <p>
          ad:tech, of course, strives to predict where the industry is headed in the near future as well as long-term.
         </p>
         <p>
          Attendees will walk away from ad:tech New York 2014 with a fresh perspective on what it means to be a modern marketer, Dunn continued.
         </p>
         <p>
          The first keynote will feature industry insiders Alan Cohen, co-founder of Giant Spoon, and John Nardone, EVP and general manager of Rocket Fuel.
         </p>
         <p>
          Cohens relatively recent venture, Giant Spoon, is a marketing agency targeting idea-centric strategy and lives where content, digital, technology and innovation collide, according to ad:tech.
         </p>
         <p>
          Nardones marketing insight has been tapped by some of the biggest brands in the world, and he has pioneered interactive advertising and other fields in digital.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Ignite Innovation
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          The following morning starts with a 9 a.m. keynote featuring executives from Wal-Mart and Unilever called Cut the Crap: Igniting Innovation in Your Brand.
         </p>
         <p>
          Many companies claim to innovate, ad:tech notes in its description of the discussion, yet in reality, most lack the internal processes to operationalize innovation and make it real.
         </p>
         <p>
          There will definitely be plenty of advice to be gleaned from this keynote considering the brands and people at play. Brian Monahan, the vice president of marketing for Walmart.com, will be talking to Babs Rangaiah, Unilevers vice president of Global Media Innovation &amp; Ventures.
         </p>
         <p>
          Visual Storytelling
         </p>
         <p>
          The afternoon keynote for Nov. 6 is all about image. Graphics and video are undeniably crucial mediums for marketers in our age of Instagram, Snapchat and Vine. The final keynote features two more powerful speakers, the head of operations for Pinterest, Dan Faul, and Richard Turley, MTVs senior vice president of Visual Storytelling and deputy editorial director.
         </p>
