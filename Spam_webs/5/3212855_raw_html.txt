     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Erminie
       </b>
      </i>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Comic_opera" title="Comic opera">
       comic opera
      </a>
      in two acts composed by
      <a href="/wiki/Edward_Jakobowski" title="Edward Jakobowski">
       Edward Jakobowski
      </a>
      with a
      <a href="/wiki/Libretto" title="Libretto">
       libretto
      </a>
      by Claxson Bellamy and Harry Paulton, based loosely on
      <a href="/wiki/Charles_Selby" title="Charles Selby">
       Charles Selby
      </a>
      's 1834
      <a href="/wiki/Melodrama" title="Melodrama">
       melodrama
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Robert_Macaire" title="Robert Macaire">
        Robert Macaire
       </a>
      </i>
      . The piece first played in
      <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">
       Birmingham
      </a>
      , England, and then in London in 1885, and enjoyed unusual international success that endured well into the twentieth century.
     </p>

     <p>
      <i>
       Erminie
      </i>
      opened at the Grand Theatre,
      <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">
       Birmingham
      </a>
      , England, on 26 October 1885. It transferred to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comedy_Theatre" title="Comedy Theatre">
       Comedy Theatre
      </a>
      in London, then under the management of
      <a href="/wiki/Violet_Melnotte" title="Violet Melnotte">
       Violet Melnotte
      </a>
      , opening on 9 November 1885 and playing for a total original run of 154 performances.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stone_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Stone-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It starred
      <a href="/wiki/Florence_St._John" title="Florence St. John">
       Florence St. John
      </a>
      who, being pregnant, ceded the role to a young
      <a href="/wiki/Marie_Tempest" title="Marie Tempest">
       Marie Tempest
      </a>
      in December; Melnotte took the role of Cerise. On 18 February 1886, the piece moved to the
      <a href="/wiki/Gaiety_Theatre,_London" title="Gaiety Theatre, London">
       Gaiety Theatre, London
      </a>
      , and by the end of 1885 another cast had begun touring the piece in the British provinces.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adams_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adams-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It soon toured throughout the world, reaching Australia by 1889.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">
       Broadway
      </a>
      , New York production was extraordinarily successful, opening at the
      <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Casino_Theatre" title="Casino Theatre">
       Casino Theatre
      </a>
      on 10 May 1886 and running for 571 performances.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It starred
      <a href="/wiki/Pauline_Hall" title="Pauline Hall">
       Pauline Hall
      </a>
      as the title character and
      <a href="/wiki/Francis_Wilson_(actor)" title="Francis Wilson (actor)">
       Francis Wilson
      </a>
      as Cadeaux.
      <a href="/wiki/DeWolf_Hopper" title="DeWolf Hopper">
       DeWolf Hopper
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Gerrish" title="Sylvia Gerrish">
       Sylvia Gerrish
      </a>
      later joined the cast.
      <i>
       Erminie
      </i>
      enjoyed many UK, US and foreign productions and was revived on Broadway in 1893, 1899, 1903 and in 1921 with Wilson and Hopper again assuming their 1886 roles.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adams_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adams-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Amateur productions were also mounted between 1895 and 1921.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       Erminie
      </i>
      was performed as a staged concert production in July 2008 by the Lyric Theatre of San Jose in
      <a href="/wiki/Mountain_View,_California" title="Mountain View, California">
       Mountain View, California
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lyric_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Lyric-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Erminie-Theatre_Royal_2.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="902" data-file-width="600" height="271" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/97/Erminie-Theatre_Royal_2.jpg/180px-Erminie-Theatre_Royal_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/97/Erminie-Theatre_Royal_2.jpg/270px-Erminie-Theatre_Royal_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/97/Erminie-Theatre_Royal_2.jpg/360px-Erminie-Theatre_Royal_2.jpg 2x" width="180"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Erminie-Theatre_Royal_2.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Poster from the 1894 production in Edinburgh
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <dl>
      <dt>
       Act I
      </dt>
     </dl>
     <p>
      Erminie is the daughter of the Marquis de Pontvert. She has been promised, in an arranged marriage, to Ernest, Vicomte de Brissac. He is a young nobleman and the son of an old war comrade of Erminie's father. Erminie and Ernest have never met each other. Erminie, however, is secretly in love with her father's secretary, Eugne, and Ernest is secretly in love with Cerise Marcel, who is Eugene's sister and Erminie's friend. Eugne and Cerise are orphans who were taken into his household by Erminie's father. Eugene feels that his subservient position makes it impossible for him to ask the Marquis for Erminie's hand in marriage.
     </p>
     <p>
      On his way to the betrothal ceremony, Ernest runs into a pair of crafty "philanthropists" (thieves), Ravennes and Cadeau, who steal his money and wardrobe and tie him to a tree. They disguise Ravennes as Ernest and Cadeau as a Baron and blunder into the Lion d'Or for the betrothal, saying that they were waylaid by thieves. Seeing the wealthy wedding guests, they scheme to make off with a lot of money. Cadeaux gets drunk, however, and his bad manners nearly spoil the scheme. Ernest eventually escapes his bonds and arrives late and in disordered attire at the Lion d'Or. Seeing Ernest, Ravennes cries "Seize the villain," claiming that Ernest is the thief who attacked them earlier in the day. Ernest is arrested and tossed in jail.
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dt>
       Act II
      </dt>
     </dl>
     <p>
      At the Chateau Pontvert that evening, Ravennes, still disguised as Ernest, pretends sympathy for Erminie's love for Eugene. He promises to help her to elope. At the same time, she unwittingly helps him to rob the house and the wedding guests. Eventually, however, in a farcical comedy of errors and mistaken identities, the plan fails, and the robbers are arrested. Nevertheless, their scheming inadvertently rescues Erminie from the arranged marriage, as both pairs  Eugene and Erminie, and Ernest and Cerise  are happily united.
     </p>
