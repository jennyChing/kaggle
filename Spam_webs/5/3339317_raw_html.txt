       <h1 class="the-title">
        14 Characteristics Of Highly Successful Traders
       </h1>
       <div class="cf post-meta-container">

         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/are-wealthy-investors-always-the-most-intelligent-investors-10785.html" title="Are Wealthy Investors Always The Most Intelligent Investors?">
          achieving success

         in all aspects of your life.

         </strong>
        </p>

        <p>
         But what Im talking about here specifically are the characteristics that relate to trading for a living. During my time at
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/about">
          Deutsche Bank and BB&amp;T
         </a>
         I had the opportunity to watch and be coached by some very successful traders. Then when I started coaching people 4 years ago it only reaffirmed the qualities that separate the successful from the unsuccessful traders.
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Discipline Is All Or Nothing
        </h3>
        <p>
         One cannot be
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/the-14-stages-of-investor-emotions-and-trading-psychology-10433.html" title="The 14 Stages Of Investor Emotions And Trading Psychology">
          disciplined up to a certain point
         </a>
         and yet call themselves disciplined. Its all or nothing. Either you are going to be a trader or you are not. Discipline needs to be exercised at all times and not sometimes only. This doesnt mean you focus 100% of your day on trading  that would be crazy  but that you are 110% committed to becoming better over time.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. Losing Is Welcomed To Learn From
        </h3>
        <p>
         You will lose money  and the sooner you accept this fact the quicker you move on. Since everyone losses money, the thing that separates great traders from bad ones is that great traders learn from the losses. Welcome the opportunity to advance your strategy or adjust your trading plan after a loss.
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/analyzing-new-trading-strategies-an-example-of-one-that-didnt-go-so-well-10912.html" title="Analyzing New Trading Strategies  An Example Of One That Didnt Go So Well">
          Heres an example of one that happened to me recently
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Never-Ending Student Of The Markets
        </h3>
        <p>
         The markets are changing every day and you need to fully understand how it works. As its changing you have to learn to evolve and change with it. Sometimes this requires
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/rebalancing-your-portfolio-to-control-risk-exposure-10753.html" title="Rebalancing Your Portfolio To Control Risk Exposure">
          adjustments on your part
         </a>
         . The moment you engage to learning new things, there will always be good opportunities which will be opening up.
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Unique Trading Personality
        </h3>
        <p>
         You are an individual and your trading should reflect that. Trading has a way of uncovering the personality of individuals by making them trade outside their comfort zones. So it is very crucial to match your
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/10-golden-rules-for-trading-success-regardless-of-what-you-trade-10300.html" title="10 Golden Rules For Trading Success Regardless Of What You Trade">
          personality
         </a>
         with the right kind of trading.
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Can Separate News Hype From Reality
        </h3>
        <p>
         It is a crucial trait for you to know how to think for oneself without being influenced by the
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/the-2-most-important-secrets-or-principals-in-the-stock-market-10021.html" title="The 2 Most Important Secrets Or Principals In The Stock Market">
          media and the news
         </a>
         . All the great traders normally know how to think for themselves on some level. This is why I tell every person who signs up for a members that I dont want them around for 5-10 years (that means they havent learned anything). You need to be honest with yourself,
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/following-the-herd-mentality-and-how-it-hurts-your-portfolio-9688.html" title="Following The Herd Mentality And How It Hurts Your Portfolio">
          make personal decisions
         </a>
         and reach your own conclusions.
        </p>
        <h3>
         6. Simple, Yet Clear Planning
        </h3>
        <p>
         It is more likely for a trader to be
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/why-most-people-fail-miserably-at-options-trading-and-how-to-avoid-it-9744.html" title="Why Most People Fail Miserably At Options Trading (And How To Avoid It)">
          successful if you have a plan
         </a>
         than if you dont have one. What are your goals and how will you get there? How will your protect yourself from losses and at what point will you take profits? What will you do with your winnings? There are countless questions to ask here.
        </p>
        <h3>
         7. Proactive And Not Re-Active
        </h3>
        <p>
         Probably the most important on this list! A trader needs to know how dangerous it can be if you overreact and make quick decisions
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/the-8-step-guide-to-trading-stock-market-corrections-successfully-10009.html" title="The 8 Step Guide To Trading Stock Market Corrections Successfully">
          without thinking
         </a>
         . The ability for you to become a better decision maker will arise from experience gained in trading. Be proactive in knowing every possible move ahead of time and dont just react to what the market gives you.
        </p>
        <h3>
         8. Commitment To Learning What You Fear
        </h3>
        <p>
         The most attractive things in life comes from the most unattractive activities in life. Most successful traders have developed over the years a sense of
         <strong>
          status-quo
         </strong>
         in their plan. They got burned by options before and will NEVER trade them again. Well, you need to learn about options and futures and stocks and forex so that you understand how they all work to affect each other. Dont be afraid to re-learn!
        </p>
        <h3>
         9. Being Patience With The Markets
        </h3>
        <p>
         A successful trader will be quick to take losses and slow to make profits.
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/5-steps-to-create-an-ironclad-options-trading-system-9439.html" title="5 Steps To Create An Ironclad Options Trading System">
          Beginners take profits quickly
         </a>
         because theyve never had them before and fear losing them. Wealth isnt built in a day or a week  it is slowly accumulated with consistent trading strategies.
        </p>
        <h3>
         10. Remaining Extremely Humble
        </h3>
        <p>
         Okay so I goofed and this one does apply to life also. But still holds true that you need to be a persona of humility and respect.
         <em>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Give to others and you will receive
          </span>
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <h3>
         11. Keeping Accurate Trading Records
        </h3>
        <p>
         Having the right records to refer from will avoid a trader from making wrong decisions and thus avoiding losses. Keep accurate records of your trading book.
        </p>
        <h3>
         12. Balancing Your Life Outside
        </h3>
        <p>
         The ultimate goal off any trader is to make money and be
         <a href="http://optionalpha.com/3-ways-at-home-traders-can-be-more-profitable-than-professionals-9966.html" title="3 Ways At Home Traders Can Be More Profitable Than Professionals">
          financially independent
         </a>
         (or at least thats my goal). However, it should be understood that life needs to be enjoyed and not suffered in the name of sacrificing for the sake of trading. Go to the gym, take a walk, do something other than sit in front of the trading screen all day for Gods sake!
        </p>
        <h3>
         13. Trade To Live, Dont Live To Trade
        </h3>
        <p>
         If youre serious about trading for a living, working to incorporate these traits is a step closer towards your goal. Starting small and determining why you want to become a trader in the first place will help drive you through the tough times as they are inevitable.
         <strong>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Just as I mentioned in #1, the determined and disciplined trader will make it in the end!
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h3>
         14. Readers Choice
        </h3>
        <p>
         Now its your turn to add your comments and let me know what YOU think makes a successful trader. Im sure that there are qualities here that I havent even thought of yet. Maybe there are some characteristics that bad traders exhibit which makes them unsuccessful
        </p>
