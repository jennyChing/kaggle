        Lunch at Mexicano LA!
       </h1>

         <img alt="" src="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex-620x264.png" title="Mex"/>
        </a>

       </div>
       <p>
        What comes to mind when you think of Mexican food? If yourelike me,it might be chain restaurants &amp; drive thrus offering 99 cent tacos made in a nacho chip tortilla! While Mexican is easily my favorite kind of cuisine, I was introduced to it in the most authentic way possible this past weekend on behalf of a luncheon at Mexicano arranged by The International Food, Wine &amp; Travel Writers Association (IFWTWA).
       </p>
       <p>
        Located in the heart of Los Angeles at The Baldwin Hills/Crenshaw Plaza, Owners/Chefs, Jaime &amp; Ramiro expand their repertoire for a new audience and continue to redefine what many people think constitutes Mexican cuisine; When we create a dish, it is always rooted in our history, Jaime says. Perhaps its inspired by the tamales my grandmother used to cook every Sunday, or the way Ramiros mother would keep tortillas warm in a wicker basket when bringing them down to the river to her husband after he was done fishing.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex.png" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Mex" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4180" height="502" src="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex.png" width="669"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        After years of cooking bold and traditional Mexican food together at La Casita Mexicana restaurant in Bell, California, Jaime Martin Del Campo and Ramiro Arvizu are affectionately known jointly as Jaime and Ramiro. Among some of the biggest stars in the Mexican cooking world, this wonderful duo has brought the authentic flavors and heirloom family recipes of their signature Los Angeles-area restaurant to Baldwin Hills.
       </p>
       <p>
        Walking into Mexicano, I felt transformed to a beautiful &amp; inviting cantina &amp; fine diner, with the walls adorned with gorgeous, vibrant colors &amp; paintings to ponder over as their friendly bar staffpour you on of their signature cocktails. Starting out, I began with a Placero, which boasts lime, cactus syrup, chicharron, pork rinds(!) &amp; for the alcohol of choice, Mezcal. I will admit, the pork rinds threw me! While I removed them right out, I do feel they, along with the chicharron, made the savory flavors this drink has. Upon first smell, it had a very strong leather-likescent yet it turned out to be quite satisfying. When I decided to try out a Anticuado, with Tequila, orange bitters agave, orange &amp; lime zest, I was expecting a very Mary drink; citrus infused with a subtle taste of the tequila.folks? This drink will blow your socks off! The strong tequila along with the orange bitters make for a very intense drink.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4183" style="width: 386px">
        <a href="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex3.png" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Did you know crickets are also common on traditional margaritas?!" class="size-full wp-image-4183" height="502" src="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex3.png" width="376"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Did you know crickets are also common on traditional margaritas?!
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        As we moved on to a heavenly first course of appetizers Queso Fundido , Guacamole Al Tequila &amp; Ceviche, I had to give in &amp; find myself a chorizo free zone on the topped queso! I managed &amp; it was absolute perfection; 3 traditional, Mexican queso cheese melted fondue style. It was very creamy, but not overly heavy. Ceviche I had awhile back, &amp; this dish made me wonder why I have not had it more often. With jalapeo peppers, carrots, onions, tomato and cilantrosoaking inthis bowl of raw white fish, this is a great lighter option to consider. And, as far as the guacamoleI must sayJaime &amp; Rimero are GENIUS to be so generous with the tequila! It added an extra pop to a well-balanced favorite, &amp; made it go even further on the Must have list.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4182" style="width: 386px">
        <a href="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex2.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Guacamole &amp; fresh hard corn tortillas" class="size-full wp-image-4182" height="502" src="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex2.jpg" width="376"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Guacamole &amp; fresh hard corn tortillas
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        For lunch, I got to try their Shrimp Enchiladas, wrapped in fresh corn tortillas &amp; covered in salsa verde. The salsa verde tasted fresh, &amp; the tortillas warm &amp; soft. Next up, they brought out their Valentina Shrimp. Prepared with an aromatic tomato sauce, it was a hard decision to try to decide if Valentina or the Al Mexcal Shrimp won my heart more. Cooked with sweet and sour orange, chile de rbol and agave, I felt this was slightly more compatible on my palette after the drinks &amp; enchiladas Id enjoyed.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4186" style="width: 386px">
        <a href="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex6.png" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Valentina Shrimp" class="size-full wp-image-4186" height="502" src="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex6.png" width="376"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Valentina Shrimp
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        And, whats an authentic Mexican lunch without dessert?! Following all the countless plates of home-made goodness, we were treated to coffee with cinnamon, churros, 3 cheese flan &amp; Grandmas Rice Pudding. The rice pudding was flavored with caramel &amp; cinnamon, making me awfully tempted to pour some in my coffee, or the other way around. As a huge lover of flan, this was definitely one of the best Ive ever had; loaded with creamy, full flavored cheeses &amp; covered in sauce, it melted in my mouth with subtle notes of vanilla.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4185" style="width: 679px">
        <a href="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex5.png" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Flan &amp; rice pudding. Yum!" class="size-full wp-image-4185" height="502" src="http://alongcomesmaryblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mex5.png" width="669"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Flan &amp; rice pudding. Yum!
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Im already planning a return visit to Mexicano. Theyre located at 3650 West Martin Luther King Blvd, their web-site can be found
        <a href="http://www.mexicanola.com/">
         HERE
        </a>
        . xo
       </p>
       <p>
        (*Disclosure: I am a proud member of The IFWTWA, who arranged this luncheon on behalf of a feature. All opinions are my own.)
       </p>
