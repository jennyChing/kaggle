        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Pulled Pork Noodle Bowls
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-10-13T08:00:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 13, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.grumpyshoneybunch.com/author/honeyb" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Shelby
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
          I really love an Asian Noodle Soup and this
          <b>
           PulledPork Noodle Bowl
          </b>
          was no exception!  A slow cooked Pulled Pork seasoned with oregano, salt, red pepper and chicken broth brings great flavor to the meat that is added to this Noodle Bowl!
          <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://52.24.202.75/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pulled-Pork-Noodle-Bowl1-683x1024.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
            <img alt="Pulled Pork Noodle Bowl" border="0" height="640" src="http://52.24.202.75/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pulled-Pork-Noodle-Bowl1-683x1024.jpg" title="Pulled Pork Noodle Bowl " width="426"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          </div>
          <p>
           With the cooler weather hitting us, I feel the need for soups. When I came across a recipe for this noodle bowl on my recipes.com I knew that I had to make it. I grabbed a 2-1/2 pound pork loin from Trader Joes. I love Trader Joes meat selection and I have never had a pork roast from there that was not delicious!
          </p>
          <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://52.24.202.75/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pork-Noodle-Bowl-PT1-683x1024.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
            <img alt="Pulled Pork Noodle Bowl" border="0" height="640" src="http://52.24.202.75/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pork-Noodle-Bowl-PT1-683x1024.jpg" title="Pulled Pork Noodle Bowl" width="425"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <p>
           I came home and immediately put the roast in my crockpot and slow cooked it for 4 hours on high, At the end of 4 hours it was perfect for pulling.
          </p>
          <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://52.24.202.75/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pork-Noodle-Bowl-sq1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
            <img alt="Pulled Pork Noodle Bowl" border="0" height="640" src="http://52.24.202.75/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pork-Noodle-Bowl-sq1.jpg" title="Pulled Pork Noodle Bowl" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
           I find it kind of funny that Grumpy loves these noodle bowls also. He loved the
           <a href="http://www.grumpyshoneybunch.com/2013/01/spicy-asian-chicken-soup.html">
            Spicy Asian Chicken Soup
           </a>
           so I was really hoping he would love this one and guess what? He did! This is really simple, just a few different steps. Once you have your cooked pulled pork, you have it made.
          </div>
          <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
          </div>
          <p>
           <a class="ziplist-button add-recipe small" href="http://www.ziplist.com/webkitchen/button/add_recipe?as_partner=grumpyshoneybunch" target="_blank">
            <img src="http://asset1.ziplist.com/wk/add_recipe-small.png"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <div class="hrecipe">
           <h1 class="fn">
            Pulled Pork Noodle Bowl
           </h1>
           <p>
            slightly adapted from
            <a href="http://http//www.myrecipes.com/recipe/pork-noodle-bowls-50400000134874/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             myrecipes.com
            </a>
            <br/>
            Published 10/13/2014
            <br/>
            <img alt="Pulled Pork Noodle Bowl" class="photo" src="https://images-blogger-opensocial.googleusercontent.com/gadgets/proxy?url=http%3A%2F%2F1.bp.blogspot.com%2F-X7dzhV6swPs%2FVDenmvr4rLI%2FAAAAAAAAPuE%2F0oqD5e9DYws%2Fs1600%2FPork%252BNoodle%252BBowl%252Bsq.jpg&amp;container=blogger&amp;gadget=a&amp;rewriteMime=image%2F*" style="border-width: 0px; height: 140px; width: 140px;"/>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <div class="summary">
            I used the 5-ingredient slow cooker pulled pork recipe on myrecipes.com for this recipe and it was perfect
            <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.grumpyshoneybunch.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
           </div>
           <h2>
            Ingredients
           </h2>
           <ul>
            <li class="ingredient">
             1 8.8 ounce package thin rice noodles
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient">
             1 8 ounce package sliced baby portobello mushrooms
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient">
             2 teaspoons olive oil
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient">
             2 cups
             <a href="http://www.myrecipes.com/recipe/slow-cooker-pulled-pork-50400000134872/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              5-ingredient slow cooker pulled pork
             </a>
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient">
             1 cup angel hair coleslaw mix
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient">
             2 green onions, white and green parts, sliced
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient">
             1/2 cup loosely packed fresh cilantro leaves, roughly chopped
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient">
             6 cups chicken broth
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient">
             1 teaspoon fresh grated ginger
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient">
             lime wedges
            </li>
           </ul>
           <h2>
            Instructions
           </h2>
           <ol>
            <li class="instructions">
             Cook rice noodles according to package instructions.
            </li>
            <li class="instructions">
             Sautee mushrooms in olive oil until moisture has released and mushrooms begin to brown slightly.  Add pulled pork and cook until pork is warmed.
            </li>
            <li class="instructions">
             While sauteeing mushrooms, add ginger to chicken broth and bring chicken broth to a simmer.
            </li>
            <li class="instructions">
             To serve, divide noodles between 4 bowls.  Place 1/8 cup coleslaw on top of noodles, then place mushroom and pork mixture on top of coleslaw.
            </li>
            <li class="instructions">
             Pour 1-1/2 cup of chicken broth over each noodle bowl.  Top with green onions and cilantro leaves and squeeze with lime juice if desired.
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            Yield: 4
            <br/>
            Prep Time: 00 hrs. 45 mins.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          Total time: 45 mins.
