     <h2>
      I Just Got Fired. Was It Illegal? Do I Have a Claim?
     </h2>
     <h3>
      By Donna Mack
     </h3>
     <h4>
      Wednesday, February, 18th, 2015
     </h4>
     <div class="at-above-post addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="I Just Got Fired. Was It Illegal? Do I Have a Claim?" data-url="http://skfmlaw.com/blog/i-just-got-fired-was-it-illegal/">
     </div>
     <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
     </div>
     <p>
      Were letting you go.
      <img alt="businesswoman getting fired office" class=" wp-image-632 alignright" height="190" src="http://skfmlaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/businesswoman-getting-fired-office.jpg" width="286"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      No one likes hearing these words. (Why cant managers just say Youre fired?) Theyre almost guaranteed to make you feel shocked, indignant, and upset.
     </p>
     <p>
      If youve been fired,
      <b>
       Im sorry
      </b>
      . Its frustrating enough even when its legal. If you were fired illegally, youre probably furious. But you do have options.
     </p>
     <p>
      If your employer broke the law by firing you, an employment lawyer will hold them accountable and help you defend your rights. Keep reading to find out if you have a case for wrongful termination.
     </p>
     <h2 style="text-align: left;">
      <b>
       When Is It Illegal To Fire Someone?
       <img alt="job loss fired" class=" wp-image-633 alignright" height="365" src="http://skfmlaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/job-loss-fired.jpg" width="207"/>
       <br/>
      </b>
     </h2>
     <p>
      So was it illegal? That depends on the situation. In many cases, youre legally protected from discrimination on the job. You cant be fired solely due to your race, ethnicity, national origin, gender, religion, disability, or age (or sexual orientation, in Washington and
      <a href="http://www.lgbtmap.org/equality-maps/non_discrimination_laws" target="_blank">
       20 other states
      </a>
      ). Its also illegal for your boss to fire you based on one of these reasons:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       You were pregnant.
      </li>
      <li>
       You had an abortion or considered having an abortion.
      </li>
      <li>
       You took approved FMLA leave.
      </li>
      <li>
       You were about to collect a big sales commission.
      </li>
      <li>
       You had jury duty.
       <i>
       </i>
      </li>
      <li>
       You took time off work to vote.
      </li>
      <li>
       You were a whistleblower because the company was doing something illegal or dangerous.
      </li>
      <li>
       You filed a sexual harassment charge against someone at work.
      </li>
      <li>
       You were hurt on the job and filed for workers compensation.
      </li>
      <li>
       You refused to lie or break the law when your boss asked you to.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      However, the details make all the difference.
      <a href="http://skfmlaw.com/contact/">
       Talk to an employment lawyer
      </a>
      about the specifics of your case.
     </p>
     <h2 style="text-align: left;">
      <b>
       <img alt="pregnant woman fired discrimination" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-634" height="475" src="http://skfmlaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/pregnant-woman-fired-discrimination.jpg" width="567"/>
       Can I Sue My Employer For Firing Me?
      </b>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Washington is an at will employment state, which means that your employer can fire you anytime (and also that you can quit anytime) absent an agreement to the contrary. If you have an employment contract that guarantees you work through a certain date or project, you may have grounds for a lawsuit. And of course, if you were fired out of discrimination, you might as well.
     </p>
     <p>
      So can you  or
      <i>
       should
      </i>
      you  sue? That depends. In some cases, you might get your former employer to agree to give you a severance package (including medical and dental coverage) on the condition that you wont sue. Thats something your employment lawyer can help you decide. In other cases, you may want to file a wrongful termination lawsuit.
     </p>
     <p>
      An employment attorney will help you decide if a lawsuit is worth it. In some cases, you might just want to move on. As one career coach
      <a href="http://jobs.aol.com/articles/2009/06/04/think-you-were-fired-unfairly/" target="_blank">
       put it
      </a>
      , You have to decide if you can emotionally handle listening to someone talk about your shortcomings in a public forum. Sometimes thats hard to take.
     </p>
     <p>
      On the other hand, no one wants a company to get away with discrimination. For example,
