           <h1 class="post-title">
            When is the Best Time to Rent an Apartment in NYC?
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="https://revaluate.com/blog/author/maxgalka/" rel="author" title="Posts by Max Galka">
             Max Galka
            </a>
             February 21, 2015
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry share">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <p>
              <a href="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/moving-day-may-1st-new-york1.png">
               <img alt="Moving Day in New York City, May 1st, 1906" class="aligncenter wp-image-905 size-full" height="486" src="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/moving-day-may-1st-new-york1.png" width="646"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h4 style="text-align: center;">
             </h4>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h4 style="text-align: center;">
              <em>
               When is the Best Time to Rent an Apartment in NYC?
              </em>
             </h4>
             <p>
              Had you lived during the three centuries stretching fromNew Yorks colonial days tothe time of World War II, you would only have one option.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Until about 70 years ago,
              </strong>
              <strong>
               <em>
                everyone
               </em>
               in New York moved on the same day,
              </strong>
              May 1, known then as Moving Day.
             </p>
             <p>
              As the legend goes, Dutch settlers established this odd traditionincelebration ofMay 1st, 1626, the day theyfirst set out to colonizeManhattan (later buying it fromtheLenape on May 4thfor a measly$23). Each year thereafter,the settlers would memorialize the tripby moving houses, and the tradition stuck.
             </p>
             <p>
              There may no longer be a Moving Day, but many aspects of moving inNew York City still do vary from season to season. Hereare fourseasonal trends you should be familiar with when deciding when to move.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Search Volume
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/nyc-rental-search-queries-google.png">
               <img alt="NYC Rentals Google Search Queries By Month" class="aligncenter wp-image-881 size-full" height="341" src="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/nyc-rental-search-queries-google.png" width="767"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              The chart above, from Google Trends, shows how the volume ofapartment searches online varies during the year.
              <strong>
               The low pointsare in December
              </strong>
              , ostensibly because ofthe holidays.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               The high points areconsistently in July or August
              </strong>
              , due in part to the flood of college grads that moveto the City after graduation.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Time on Market
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/days-on-market-nyc-2014.png">
               <img alt="Time spend on market, nyc apartments" class="alignleft wp-image-909 size-medium" height="187" src="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/days-on-market-nyc-2014-300x187.png" width="300"/>
              </a>
              A similar seasonal trend emergeswhen you look at the amount of time apartments stay on the market before being rented. As shown in this chart,
              <strong>
               apartments get snapped up fasterduring the summer.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              All else being equal, more time on market is of course better. Not only does it mean less pressure to close right away, but it can also afford you more flexibility with your move-in date, as well as more bargaining power when negotiating the terms of the lease.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Listing Inventory
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Listing-Inventory-Manhattan-By-Month.png">
               <img alt="number of rental listings by month in nyc" class="alignright wp-image-901 size-medium" height="175" src="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Listing-Inventory-Manhattan-By-Month-300x175.png" width="300"/>
              </a>
              As this chart illustrates, there are
              <strong>
               more apartments available insummer than in winter
              </strong>
              . Summer is the most common time for leases to begin and expire, leading to a largerinventory andmore options to choose from.
             </p>
             <p>
              With the availability of apartments in NYC being very limited to begin with, this is a clearlyan important issue to consider, especially if the apartment you are looking for is one with particular or uncommon qualities.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Pricing
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Rental-Prices-By-Month-NYC.png">
               <img alt="rental prices by month in NYC" class="alignleft wp-image-920 size-medium" height="215" src="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Rental-Prices-By-Month-NYC-300x215.png" width="300"/>
              </a>
              The truth is that most people are just looking for a decent placethey can afford, making price the most important factor.Thischart, created by
              <a href="http://www.renthop.com/" target="_blank" title="Renthop">
               Renthop
              </a>
              , illustrateshow the price of a typical1 bedroom rental unit varies during the year.
             </p>
             <p>
              The price swing between the high and the low points is about 4%. So, for a $4,000-per-month unit,
              <strong>
               renting inwinter could save you as much a$160 per month
              </strong>
              versusrenting the same apartment insummer.
             </p>
             <p>
              $160 per month may seem like splitting hairs, since itwould representonly slightly more than a onedays worth of rent. But over the course ofa one-year lease, the total wouldamount toa savings of $1,920.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               So, when is the best time to rent an apartment in New York City?
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              For most people, renting in winter is probably the better option. You are likely to get better value for your money and more flexibility with move in date. You also have less chance ofending up in a situation where you feel pressured to make a rushed decision.
             </p>
             <p>
              On the other hand, if you you plan to live in the apartment for a while, having a bigger selection from which to choose may outweigh everything else, making summer the better time to move.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/may-day-in-new-york.png">
               <img alt="May Day in New York" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-926" height="184" src="https://revaluate.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/may-day-in-new-york-300x184.png" width="300"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              In either case,when you start feeling frustrated with your apartment search, just remember,most people who have ever moved to New York had to do it in competition with everyone else in theCity.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
