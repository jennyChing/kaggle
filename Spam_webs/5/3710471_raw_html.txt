          <h2>
           Product Description:
          </h2>
          <p class="lead">
           This "Best in Class" Countertop Home Water Distiller comes with a One Gallon, Break-Resistant, Food-Grade, (BPA-Free), Polypropylene Collection/Serving Pitcher, (Carafe). It is a very dependable water distiller with a Stainless Steel main body exterior and a High Quality 100% Stainless Steel interior steam chamber and condensing coil; and also features single-button start, automatically turns off, and is very easy to clean.
          </p>
          <p>
           Our "Best in Class" Models 100, 100SS, 300 and 300SS are identical except for your choice of exterior, White Baked-Enamel metal or Stainless Steel; and the Collection/Serving pitcher, which is Break-Resistant Food-Grade BPA-Free Polypropylene with the Model 100 and 100SS; or a specailly designed Glass Carafe and glass nozzle insert with the Model 300 and 300SS. Other than those variations, Models 100, 100SS, 300 and 300SS all share the same top quality features of our tried and true system that has been
sold to thousands of satisfied customers.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Features &amp; Convenience:
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Top Quality:
            </strong>
            The entire Steam Chamber including Dome and Condensing Coil is 100% Stainless Steel, Grade 304, (also known as 18/8), the most widely used and versatile of all the stainless grades. All plastics used are Food-Safe and BPA-Free. (NO POLYCARBONATE COLLECTION CONTAINER or other parts that may touch the water.) All our stainless steel boilers also have a metal exterior. Some other makes are plastic.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Optimal size 565 Watt heating element.
            </strong>
            Bigger is NOT better. Larger heating elements in one gallon units can cause water to mix with VOCs and other contaminates. Some manufacturers use a splash shield with a larger heating element which is ineffective in preventing contamination.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Best Value:
            </strong>
            More economical by far. Some one-gallon units can cost twice as much and aren't as good. Other makes can cost less initially, but end up costing much more due to poor quality and repair issues.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
