               <p>
                Sure, backyard chickens are popular. In addition to giving you a constant supply of omega-3-rich eggs, they make really cool pets. But so do ducks, which can be a perfect addition to your backyard flock if you like to garden, love eggs, hate bugs, or just desire some unique entertainment. Heres why this is one quack remedy you should consider:
               </p>

      &lt;section class="field-body"&gt;

&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;1. Duck eggs are delicious and nutritious&lt;/strong&gt;. Theyre richer-tasting than chicken eggs and contain more protein, calcium, iron, potassium, and pretty much every major mineral. The yolks are big and orange, and the whites are pure white. You cant beat them for baking and making cakes rise high and airy, plus theyre divine soft boiled, poached, or over easy. (The yolks are like satiny custard.) Many breeds of ducks also lay more eggs per year than most breeds of chickens do. And if youre allergic or sensitive to chicken eggs, you may be able to eat these (but check with your doctor first).&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;&lt;img alt="low-maintenance" class="media-image attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__1986 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__field_file_image_alt_text[und][0][value]__low-maintenance attr__field_file_image_title_text[und][0][value]__low-maintenance" src="http://www.eatclean.com/sites/eatclean.com/files/getty-200182402-001-lowmaintenance-Chris-Windsor.jpg" title="low-maintenance" typeof="foaf:Image" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;2. Ducks are low maintenance.&lt;/strong&gt; They thrive on chickenfeed, but they graze and forage for a higher percentage of their own food, which means less work for you. They also don't take up much space. You'll need a secure shelter to protect them from predators, but a 3- to 4-square-foot pen is enough.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;&lt;img alt="ducks in garden" class="media-image attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__1991 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__field_file_image_alt_text[und][0][value]__ducks in garden attr__field_file_image_title_text[und][0][value]__ducks" src="http://www.eatclean.com/sites/eatclean.com/files/getty-181800856-goodforgarden-Sotiris-Filippou.jpg" title="ducks in garden" typeof="foaf:Image" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;3. Ducks are great for the garden.&lt;/strong&gt; They're relentless hunters of pests, including slugs, snails and hornworms, which can destroy tomatoes and hostas. Unlike chickens, which love to scratch the dirt and upend soil, ducks generally don't disturb garden plants. Two exceptions are lettuces and ripe strawberries, which they love. Their flat feet will also squash seedlings.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;&lt;img alt="funny ducks" class="media-image attr__typeof__foaf:Image img__fid__1996 img__view_mode__default attr__format__default attr__field_file_image_alt_text[und][0][value]__funny ducks attr__field_file_image_title_text[und][0][value]__funny" src="http://www.eatclean.com/sites/eatclean.com/files/getty-88636361-ducksarefunny-Martha-Catherine-Ivey.jpg" title="funny ducks" typeof="foaf:Image" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;4. Ducks are funny.&lt;/strong&gt; Theyll swim in anything, from a dishpan to a kiddie pool, dunking their entire heads with great gusto and frequency. They also make surprisingly good companions.&lt;br /&gt;
Intrigued? Convinced? Here's what youll need to start a brood:&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;ul&gt;&lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Plan on a pair.&lt;/strong&gt; Ducks are social creatures, and one will be very lonely unless it gets to pad around with you all day (which can get old, even if you are very lonely). If you have a smallish space, don't get more than four, as that can result in smelly poop slicks in their favorite places.&lt;/li&gt;
	&lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Know your breeds.&lt;/strong&gt; There are at least a dozen different domestic ducks. One popular variety is called Runners, so named because theyre long, skinny and stand up almost like bowling pins when they run. Theyre also smallish birds that come in a variety of colors: fawn and white, chocolate, blue (gray), and black. They lay white or bluish eggs. Khaki Campbells are another good choice. Theyre medium-brown birds, a little larger than Runners, with a more typical duck shape. They almost always lay white eggs and are among the most prolific layers in the duck world.&lt;/li&gt;
	&lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Find a breeder.&lt;/strong&gt; Metzer Farms in California ships ducklings all over the country and has a great duck-breed comparison chart on it website so you can learn all about the different types. This particular hatchery will ship as few as two ducklingsjust be prepared to spend extra on a special shipping carton and heat pack to keep the wee birdies comfy on their way to you. But the ducks themselves are not that expensive (generally less than $10 apiece). They are only available, however, at certain times of year.&lt;/li&gt;
