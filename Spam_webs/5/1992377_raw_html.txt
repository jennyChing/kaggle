               History Of Windows
              </h1>
              <div class="header_font" id="single_blog_meta">
               <div class="single_blog_meta_div" id="samba_single_date">

               </div>
               <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_8564" style="width: 477px">
                <a href="http://i2.wp.com/creativesolvibrations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/history_of_window.jpg">
                 <img alt="Photo credit:  myblogguest.com/forum/uploads/articles/2015/2/history_of_window.jpg" class=" wp-image-8564" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/creativesolvibrations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/history_of_window.jpg?resize=467%2C309"/>
                </a>
                <p class="wp-caption-text">
                 Photo credit:
                 <br/>
                 myblogguest.com/forum/uploads/articles/2015/2/history_of_window.jpg
                </p>
               </div>
               <p>
                Windows seem like a simple innovation that should be as old as the concept of the home itself, but interestingly, windows took hundreds of years of innovation before it looked like the version that we know of today. The development of windows isnt that straightforward as history demonstrates.
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: center;">
                <strong>
                 Early windows
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                In the earlier days, some windows were not closed, while others were covered with fabric. Wooden boards are often used to close off the window in order to prevent wind currents or rain to enter the home. In hot regions, damp cloth was hung next to the windows and as the air moved past the building, evaporation would cool the inside of the room.
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: center;">
                <strong>
                 Roman glass-making
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                The Romans are famous for being relatively innovative compared to other populations from around that time. At the time, the Romans were forming crude glass panes. Glassblowers would form a cylinder, remove the bottom, and then unfurl the hollow tube. The flattened pane of glass is affixed to a wooden frame and then inserted into the window. The panes of glass were opaque and it would take centuries before the trades would be able to make the clear glass as we know it today.
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: center;">
                <strong>
                 Progressing backwards during the Dark Ages
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                The knowledge and practice of glass-making for windows was lost or discontinued during this time and many builders returned to using animal hide to cover openings in the walls. The hide would be smeared with oil or grease making it translucent. Unfortunately, the construction was drafty, so homeowners would need to line the edges of the opening with drapes or other material to reduce airflow. Due to the inefficiency, the openings were often small and they had to rely heavily on the indoor lighting.
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: center;">
                <strong>
                 Mixing the new with the old
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                Although glass-making was crude, it still existed at the time and
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_glass_%28window%29%22" target="_blank">
                 it was called crown glass
                </a>
                . While hot, the material was spun out into round, flat discs that were great for cutting into a diamond shape. Consequently, many windows from the Tudor era contain diamond or circular lattices. The glass had a noticeable center that was a different color and thickness than the rest, but it was a large improvement.
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                At the same time, the Roman techniques of flattening cylindrical glass were revised, which yielded small, square panes that only the rich could afford.
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: center;">
                <strong>
                 Leaded windows
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                Intricate lattices were in use by the 17th century, which were made of lead. People were unaware of the dangers of the metal during that time. Wooden sashes worked as both a decorative improvement and protection against draft. Glass was still expensive and wasnt as commonly used in households as it is today.
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                By the 18th century, glass was clear and relatively easy to work with. Taxes on glass-work in Britain were repealed and building construction changed so that windows and walls didnt have to bear the weight of the structure.
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                Today,
                <a href="http://www.clerawindows.com/windows/" target="_blank">
                 glass windows and doors have evolved and can be categorized
                </a>
                according to their designs, energy efficiency, glass type, colors etc. &amp; everyone can have their own choices and their walls will reflect their unique style.
               </p>

               </p>
