         <p>
          To obtain a six-packiis necessary to strengthen our lower back to get the right balance and have adequate support. For thatwe can include isometric back extension in our
          <a href="http://exerciseforsixpackabs.com/">
           <strong>
            core exercise workout
           </strong>
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          Steps to properlydoIsometric Back Extension
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ready, Set:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Lie facedown on the floor with a rolled-up towel beneath your navel and your legs about shoulder-width apart.
          </li>
          <li>
           Lift your torso and rest your weight on your forearms, as if you were lying on the floor with a newspaper spread out in front of you.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           GO:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Slowly lift your forearms off the floor and out to your sides while keeping your elbows flexed and your torso in the same position.
          </li>
          <li>
           Hold at this position for 4 or 5 seconds,
          </li>
          <li>
           Then, in a controlled motion, slowly lower your forearms back to the floor.
          </li>
          <li>
           Let some ot the tension release from your lower back, and repeat.
          </li>
          <li>
           this is one of the exercises in our program of
           <a href="http://exerciseforsixpackabs.com/exercise-for-six-pack-abs-6-week-program/">
            six pack ab workouts
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>

         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          Isometric Back ExtensionPerformance Tips
         </h2>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Hold at the up position for 4 seconds the first week and 5 the second.
          </li>
          <li>
           Make sure you keep your torso and neck in the same position throughout the exercise.
          </li>
          <li>
           Breathe normally. Use your breath to count the 4 or 5 second hold at the elbows up position, one strong breath per second.
          </li>
          <li>
           If you have a strong lower back, you may need to hold for longer than the 4 or 5 seconds prescribed to feel the exercise do its work.
          </li>
         </ul>
