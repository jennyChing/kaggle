           <h1 class="h1">
            <span class="headline">
             Marvels Netflix Series: What To Read To Get Ready
            </span>
           </h1>
           <div class="subhead">
            With four new shows on the way, we tell you where to learn more about Daredevil, Jessica Jones, Luke Cage and Iron Fist.
           </div>
          </div>
          <section class="entry-content clearfix">
           <div class="hr">
           </div>
           <span class="byline">
            by
            <span class="author">
             <a href="http://www.mtv.com/news/author/sullivak/" rel="author" title="Posts by Kevin P. Sullivan">
              Kevin P. Sullivan
             </a>
            </span>
            <span class="date">
             11/8/2013
            </span>
           </span>
           <div class="sm4ShareWidget sm4Widget" data-contenturi="id::mgid:uma:content:mtv.com:1717110::og::http://www.mtv.com/news/1717110/marvels-netflix-series-what-to-read-to-get-ready/?date_last_modified=1383936391" data-widget="postShare">
           </div>
           <p>
            Marvel made big waves Thursday when it
            <a href="http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1717021/marvel-netflix-tv-series.jhtml">
             announced its partnership with Netflix
            </a>
            , which will include four new series, each featuring one of the companys beloved heroes, culminating in a crossover miniseries.
           </p>
           <p>
            And while Daredevil, Jessica Jones, Luke Cage and Iron Fist have hardcore fans within the comics community, they are certainly less well-known than their Avengers counterparts. So if youre excited to see what Marvel will unleash on Netflix starting in 2015 but arent entirely sure why, heres where you should start with each individual hero.
           </p>
           <p>
            <big>
             <b>
              Daredevil
             </b>
            </big>
            <br/>
            <b>
             Where to Start:
            </b>
            Mark Waids run, beginning with Daredevil, Vol.3 #1
            <br/>
            <b>
             What Youll Get:
            </b>
            While Daredevil rose in popularity thanks to a darker tone applied to him by writer Frank Miller, Waid took him back into a lighter direction, and the series quickly gained a reputation as being one of the best current Marvel series. Though the run will end early next year, this remains a great jumping-off point for newly interested fans.
            <br/>
            <b>
             Where to Buy It:
            </b>
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Daredevil-Vol-1-Mark-Waid/dp/0785152385" target="_blank">
             Amazon
            </a>
            ,
            <a href='http://www.comixology.com/Daredevil-By-Mark-Waid-Vol-1/digital-comic/28992"' target="_blank">
             Comixology
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <big>
             <b>
              Jessica Jones
             </b>
            </big>
            <br/>
            <b>
             Where to Start:
            </b>
            At the beginning. Alias only ran for a totally straightforward 28 issues, so theres no confusing what the good stuff is.
            <br/>
            <b>
             What Youll Get:
            </b>
            For one thing, Alias has always been famous for being Marvels most adult series. The first issue opens with the F-word  the companys first use of it ever  and includes material adult enough to justify the series R rating. But aside from that, Alias is a smart, modern take on the superhero genre that still manages to score cameos from some of Marvels most famous heroes.
            <br/>
            <b>
             Where to Buy It:
            </b>
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Alias-Vol-Brian-Michael-Bendis/dp/0785111417/ref=sr_1_3?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1383929409&amp;sr=1-3&amp;keywords=alias" target="_blank">
             Amazon
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <big>
             <b>
              Luke Cage
             </b>
            </big>
            <br/>
            <b>
             Where to Start:
            </b>
            In a lot of ways, Luke Cage is at his best during Alias, but for more fun, check out the second volume of The New Avengers.
            <br/>
            <b>
             What Youll Get:
            </b>
            Captain America handed the reins of this Avengers splinter group to Cage, who was then joined by Wolverine, Spider-Man, the Thing, Iron First, Jessica Jones, Mockingbird, Ms. Marvel and Victoria Hand.
            <br/>
            <b>
             Where to Buy It:
            </b>
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Avengers-Vol-Brian-Michael-Bendis/dp/0785148736/ref=sr_1_5?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1383930648&amp;sr=1-5&amp;keywords=new+avengers" target="_blank">
             Amazon
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.comixology.com/New-Avengers-By-Brian-Michael-Bendis-Vol-1/digital-comic/23430" target="_blank">
             Comixology
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <big>
             <b>
              Iron Fist
             </b>
            </big>
            <br/>
            <b>
             Where to Start:
            </b>
            The Immortal Iron Fist #1 by Ed Brubaker and Matt Fraction
            <br/>
            <b>
             What Youll Get:
            </b>
            This beloved run won an Eisner Award in 2008 for Best New Series and was part of the reason Brubaker won Best Writer the same year. It is widely considered to be the best series featuring the character and greatly expands on the mythology of the martial-arts master.
            <br/>
            <b>
             Where to Buy It:
            </b>
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/The-Immortal-Iron-Fist-Vol/dp/0785124896/ref=pd_sim_b_13" target="_blank">
             Amazon
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.comixology.com/Immortal-Iron-Fist-Vol-1-The-Last-Iron-Fist-Story/digital-comic/22497" target="_blank">
             Comixology
            </a>
           </p>

	}

	return size;
