             <h1 class="entry-title">
              Caf Du Monde
             </h1>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="entry-content self-clear">
            <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6204 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/CafeDuMonde_001.jpg" title="CafeDuMonde_HungryNative_001" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             Situated in the French Market District,
             <a href="http://www.cafedumonde.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','www.cafedumonde.com']);" target="_blank">
              Caf Du Monde
             </a>
             was established in 1862 and has become one of, if not the, most iconic eateries in New Orleans. While a well-known tourist destination, it retains its local customers and color.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6175 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/CafeDuMonde_006.jpg" title="CafeDuMonde_HungryNative_006" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             Caf Du Monde is world-famous for beignets (a type of French doughnut) and their caf au lait, which is made with a coffee-chicory blend. The process of mixing coffee and chicory has its roots in the French civil war, when coffee was scarce and chicory, the ground root of the Endive plant, was added to help stretch their meager supplies. Chicory has a slight bitter flavor and adds an almost powdery mouth-feel to the coffee.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6176 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/CafeDuMonde_026.jpg" title="CafeDuMonde_HungryNative_026" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             Caf Du Mondes caf au laits are made with a fifty-fifty split of warm milk and coffee, which tempers the bitterness, and makes for a smooth, creamy cup. If coffee isnt your thing, orange juice, hot chocolate and milk are also available, but almost everyone is there for the coffee and beignet experience.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6177 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/CafeDuMonde_009.jpg" title="CafeDuMonde_HungryNative_009" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             The Beignets are the only food item on the menu, but they are done right. Served hot, in orders of three, they arrive absolutely covered in powdered sugar. Despite many patrons Herculean efforts to the contrary, everyone winds up with powdered sugar on their clothes and faces. That said, wearing dark clothing to Cafe Du Monde is not advised. Also, laughing, coughing or even breathing wrong is liable to raise a cloud of white powdery topping, but thats all part of the fun. They have a bit more chew than your average doughnut, and arent as sweet, but the mound of sugar on top more than makes up for that.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:933px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6178 p3-downsized" height="933" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/CafeDuMonde_039.jpg" title="CafeDuMonde_HungryNative_039" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             Being such a popular spot, Cafe Du Monde can get quite busy at times, so expect a wait during peak hours. If you can, grab one of the outside tables under the awning to enjoy the view and perhaps the sounds of street muscians. They are open 24 hours a day, every day except Christmas.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6187 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/beignets_017.jpg" title="beignets_HungryNative_017" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             When KD visited, she purchased a package of their
             <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?ie=UTF8&amp;keywords=cafe%20du%20monde%20Beignet&amp;tag=hungnati-20&amp;index=aps&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','www.amazon.com']);" target="_blank">
              Beignet Mix
             </a>
             <span style="color: #ffffff;">
              <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=hungnati-20&amp;l=ur2&amp;o=1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
              .
             </span>
             and a couple cans of
             <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?ie=UTF8&amp;keywords=cafe%20du%20monde%20coffee&amp;tag=hungnati-20&amp;index=aps&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','www.amazon.com']);" target="_blank">
              Coffee
             </a>
             <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=hungnati-20&amp;l=ur2&amp;o=1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
             <span style="color: #ffffff;">
              .
             </span>
             (and a mug!) so we could try our hands at re-creating this classic at home.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:693px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6188 p3-downsized" height="693" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/beignets_023-32.jpg" title="beignets_HungryNative_023-32" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             The coffee and chicory blend comes pre-ground in a traditional coffee can. While it was ground a little fine for a French press, that is what we had, so we heated up some milk and gave it a whirl.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6189 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/beignets_036.jpg" title="beignets_HungryNative_036" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             The results are what one would expect from pre-ground coffee, a little flat tasting, but fine. The chicory adds perhaps a hint of musty bitter-chocolate flavor but its hardly noticeable with all the milk in the mug.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6190 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/beignets_005.jpg" title="beignets_HungryNative_005" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             Making the beignets couldnt be easier, combine two cups of the mix with seven ounces of water in a bowl and stir until blended.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6192 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/beignets_042.jpg" title="beignets_HungryNative_042" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             The dough is then rolled out on a floured surface until its about 1/8 of an inch thick. Cut into 2 and a 1/4 squares (roughly) and place into 370 oil.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6193 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/beignets_047.jpg" title="beignets_HungryNative_047" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             Youll know your oil is hot enough if they rise to the top in 8-10 seconds.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6194 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/beignets_050.jpg" title="beignets_HungryNative_050" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             Once they do, baste them with oil, while they continue to cook, until puffy and golden brown. Next, drain them and dust with powdered sugar while still hot, which helps the sugar stay were you want it (sorta).
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="p3-img-protect p3-img-protect-alignnone" style="width:800px;">
             <img class="p3-overlay" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/themes/prophoto3/images/blank.gif" style="width:800px;height:533px;"/>
             <img alt="Hungry Native Martha's Vineyard Food Blog New Orleans Cafe Du Monde French Quarter Coffee Chicory Beignets" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6195 p3-downsized" height="533" src="http://hungrynative.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/beignets_098.jpg" title="beignets_HungryNative_098" width="800"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             Surprisingly, the taste was fairly close to the real thing, crispy on the outside and a bit chewy on the inside. Theyre definitely on the savory rather than sweet side, but that can be adjusted by how much sugar you decide to top them with. Also, because they arent tooth-achingly sweet, youll feel less guilty when you eat a half-dozen for breakfast. With Mardi Gras on everybodys mind this time of year, it was fun to have a little bit of New Orleans in our own kitchen.
            </p>
