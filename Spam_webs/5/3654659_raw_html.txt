       <p>
        <strong>
         WHY GO?
        </strong>
        Thought Salem was all about witches? Perish the thought and make a beeline to the electrifying Peabody Essex Museum. Though Salem is most associated with the Witch Trials, you can spend a few days here without experiencing anything witch-related at all. One of the nations best  and dare we say quirkiest  museums, The Peabody Essex, should be your Stop #1. In the late 1700s, early 1800s Salem was the epicenter of the America/ China/India Spice Trade, and that history too often gets lost in Salem Witch Mania. Also believe it or not, Salem, the former hotbed of joy-denying Puritanism, is becoming known for its locavore, sustainable, and yes, joyful farm-to-table cuisine; and were not talking wing of bat and eye of newt. (Should you wish to satisfy your interest in the witchy side of Salem, check out this
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/salem-ma/">
         Getaway Mavens post
        </a>
        ).
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1480" style="width: 370px">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Mariner-Hall-Peabody-Essex-Museum-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="Huge, colorful figureheads lunge into great museum hall | Great Mariner Hall, Peabody Essex Museum MA" class="size-medium wp-image-1480" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Mariner-Hall-Peabody-Essex-Museum-Salem-MA-360x480.jpg" title="Great Mariner Hall, Peabody Essex Museum MA" width="360"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Great Mariner Hall, Peabody Essex Museum
        </p>
       </div>
       <h2>
        Things To Do in Salem, MA
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         VISIT:
         <a href="http://www.pem.org/">
          Peabody Essex Museum
         </a>
         (PEM).
        </b>
        Heralded as one of the top 20 museum in the USA, the Peabody Essex is also the oldest continuously operating museum in the country with the nations first collection of Asian Export artifacts, one of the best maritime collections and a top-ten childrens interactive museum. Going through its third major expansion now, you can spend a weekend in a slew of galleries, participate in a selection of innovative programs, and only scratch the surface. People move to Salem specifically because this museum is here.
       </p>
       <p>
        But why Salem? Following Independence, American ships were barred from most European ports, necessitating more far-flung expeditions. Our young and intrepid citizenry set course for unexplored lands, leading to trade with China and India, and creating fortunes for many. In 1799 a group of young merchants and explorers met over drinks to discuss the establishment of a museum to showcase collected artifacts from their world travels.The original Greek Revival Mariners Hall, built in 1825, still displays these early gleanings, and one of the most curious is a taxidermied penguin with a goose-long neck. In the early 1800s, the taxidermist had never seen a penguin and assumed that the dead creatures neck had shrunk in transit  so he made adjustments based on the birds he did know.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1473" style="width: 370px">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Andalusian-Cape-Peabody-Essex-Museum-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="Shimmery cape made from Sea Lion intestines | Peabody Essex Museum | Salem MA" class="size-medium wp-image-1473 " height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Andalusian-Cape-Peabody-Essex-Museum-Salem-MA-360x480.jpg" title="Andalusian Cape, Peabody Essex Museum, Salem MA" width="360"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Shimmery cape made from Sea Lion intestines, PEM
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        PEM curators are masters of the obscure, and have put on display some incredible artifacts. Stare agog at a wedding dress made completely from shells and a shimmery gossamer Andalusian cape created out of sea-lion intestines. And do not leave before touring the
        <strong>
         Yin Yu Tang Chinese House
        </strong>
         a two hundred fifty year old 16-room remote village family complex shipped stone by stone, carved wall by carved wall, numbered then rebuilt intact on PEM grounds just ten years ago.
        <i>
         Tues.  Sun. 10-5, $15. Its an extra $5 with admission for a 30-min. self-guided tour of the Yin Yu Tang House.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Salem-Food-Tours-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="Salem Food Tour guide holding up platter of fresh, thick sandwiches" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1482" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Salem-Food-Tours-Salem-MA-640x480.jpg" title="Salem Food Tours, Salem, MA" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         TOUR:
         <a href="http://www.salemfoodtours.com/">
          Salem Food Tour
         </a>
        </b>
        <a href="http://www.salemfoodtours.com/">
         .
        </a>
        Yes, there is a food scene in Salem. I was skeptical, too, but no more. Karen Scalia, SAG Actor and foodie extraordinaire takes groups of up to 12 (8-10 is her sweet spot) on a four-hour, five stop, eye-opening cultural, epicurean and historical walking tour of Salem. FYI  you will be well fed. Start on Pickering Wharf, part of the
        <a href="http://www.nps.gov/sama/index.htm">
         <b>
          Salem Maritime Historical Site
         </b>
        </a>
        , to learn about Salems role in the Spice Trade and of course try a few said spices at David Bowies spice store, The Pickle Pot, to wake up your senses. (Yes, David Bowie, but not that one.) Seventy five percent of what we taste is through the nose, Scalia states, as she opens the door to
        <b>
         <a href="http://www.aromasanctum.com/">
          AromaSanctum Perfumes
         </a>
        </b>
        where guests are encouraged to sniff apothecary bottles filled with musks, florals and undetermined scents. Next onto some of Scalias favorite restaurants, visits with chefs, and a kitchen tour and quick cooking class. According to a certain review site that Will Not Be Named, this is Salems Number One Attraction, so book early.
        <i>
         Call or refer to website for reservations and rates
        </i>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         VISIT:
         <a href="http://www.7gables.org/">
          House of the Seven Gables
         </a>
        </b>
        . Nathaniel Hawthorne did not live here, but he visited his cousin who did, and this informed his classic book,
        <em>
         The House of the Seven Gables
        </em>
        . Hawthornes home (from birth to 4 years old) was originally down the street but transported to Seven Gables property as part of the museum complex. Kids love the secret staircase, and literary types might enjoy a bit of Hathorne Witch Trial history.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Harbor-Sweets-Sweet-Sloops-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt='"Sweet Sloops" - triangles of nut brittle enrobed with melted white chocolate' class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1477" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Harbor-Sweets-Sweet-Sloops-Salem-MA-640x480.jpg" title="Harbor Sweets Tour, Salem, MA" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         TOUR:
         <a href="http://www.harborsweets.com/tour.asp">
          Harbor Sweets Factory Tour
         </a>
        </b>
        . Come to Harbor Sweets, home of the original Sweet Sloop  and get a gander at how this specialty chocolate factory enrobes triangles of decadent butter crunch in white chocolate then hand dips each in dark chocolate and nuts. Don a hair net and watch ironically svelte employees mold, dip and wrap each piece. Known for its unique packaging and custom candies, Harbor Sweets has a very loyal customer base, though Id wager most have never been here. Come for a Factory Tour, savor a free sample, and you may walk out a loyal customer too. Prices are reasonable for hand-crafted chocolates; Sweet Sloops, 24 for $19.50, Sand Dollars, 10 for $19.50, Sweet Shells, 20 for $19.50.
        <i>
         Tues or Thurs at 11am, Shop open every day including weekends.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         SHOP:
         <a href="http://www.pemshop.com/">
          Peabody Essex Museum Store
         </a>
        </b>
        . Huge, colorful, creative and magnificently curated, youll find everything from jewelry to books, clothing, home goods and accessories at reasonable prices.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Aromasanctum-Perfumes-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="Owner of Aromasanctum Perfumes with array of apothecary jars filled with unusual oils and scents" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1474" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Aromasanctum-Perfumes-Salem-MA-640x480.jpg" title="Aromasanctum Perfumes Salem, MA" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         SHOP:
         <a href="http://www.aromasanctum.com/">
          Aromasanctum Perfumes
         </a>
        </b>
        . Essential oil fragrances like Pirate and Celestial Smoke and an infinite number of others (custom blended) cost $28 per  oz (lasts a year) or $45 for a scent made just for you.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Milk-and-honey-salem-ma.jpg">
         <img alt="Shop sign with cow and bee (milk and honey)" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1486" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Milk-and-honey-salem-ma-595x480.jpg" title="Milk and Honey shop, Salem, MA" width="595"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         PICNIC:
         <a href="http://www.milkandhoneysalem.com/">
          Milk and Honey Green Grocer
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://salemwineimports.com/">
          Salem Wine Imports
         </a>
         .
        </b>
        There are plenty of green spaces in Salem to set out a picnic, and the best purveyors of sandwiches and wine are right next door to each other. Slow-roasted (read; unprocessed) meats and fresh-as-just-picked produce form delectable baguette sandwiches at Milk and Honey, a small Whole Foods, green grocer. Pick some up along with cheeses and other prepared foods, then head next door to
        <b>
         Salem Wine Imports
        </b>
         a little slice of Italy. Owner Eric Olson loves recommending the fruit of Mom and Pop vineyards.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Finz-Restaurant-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="oysters on halfshell on ice at full service bar" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1476" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Finz-Restaurant-Salem-MA-640x480.jpg" title="Finz Restaurant Salem, MA" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h2>
        Where To Eat in Salem, MA
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         EAT:
         <a href="http://www.hipfinz.com/">
          Finz
         </a>
        </b>
        . Oyster-lovers will adore Oh So Hip Seafood  Finz. Select from and extensive oyster menu (85% come post haste from Cape Cod) and get them naked (6 for $11.99) doused in wasabi and Stoli (6 for $14.99) or fried ($13.99). Follow up with the signature Deconstructed Tuna Sushi entre ($24.99) or top seller Tequila-Lime Enchiladas ($15.99).
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Bill-Fogarty-Scratch-Kitchen-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="big guy chef, Bill Fogarty, at stove in his open kitchen restaurant" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1475" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Bill-Fogarty-Scratch-Kitchen-Salem-MA-492x480.jpg" title="Bill Fogarty, owner, Scratch Kitchen Salem MA" width="492"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         EAT:
         <a href="http://www.scratchkitchensalem.com/">
          Scratch Kitchen
         </a>
        </b>
        . Dont let the casual coffee shop dcor fool you. This is serious foodie territory. Bill Fogarty, the Paul Prudhomme-girthed chef/owner sprinkles bacon dust on French Fries (hes big on the three Bs  Bacon, Butter and Beer), adds nuggets of pork to his mussel broth, sources from attributed local farms, and cooks, New Orleans style, in a window so that passers by may watch. You can be sure everything set before you is farm-fresh and baconlicious. Given his predilection for pig (signature pulled pork, $9.50), Fogarty also serves up an amazingly toothsome veggie Black Bean Burger ($8.50).
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Life-Alive-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="Shot of colorful counter at raw food restaurant Salem MA" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1479" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Life-Alive-Salem-MA-640x480.jpg" title="Life Alive Cafe Salem MA" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         EAT:
         <a href="http://www.lifealive.com/">
          Life Alive
         </a>
        </b>
        . Owners of this crunchy kale and granola fast-real-food caf want to connect you to where your food comes from and put you in touch with your senses. They achieve this brilliantly through bowls of vibrantly hued raw or blanched veggies that come to your table within four minutes. Signature dish, The Goddess (bowl, $8.55), with Ginger Nama Soya sauce, is a satisfying, healthful combination of colors, flavors and textures  enhancing Mother Nature just a tad.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Salad-Capts-Waterfront-Grill-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="Fresh lettuce and avocado salad wrapped in cucumber" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1481" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Salad-Capts-Waterfront-Grill-Salem-MA-640x480.jpg" title="Capt's Waterfront Grill Signature Salad, Salem MA" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         EAT:
         <a href="http://www.capts.com/">
          Capts Waterfront Grill
         </a>
        </b>
        <a href="http://www.capts.com/">
         .
        </a>
        A recent change in ownership freshened up this waterfront restaurant and its menu. Now, the chic Captain Salad comes garden-fresh, encased in a sliced cucumber shell ($7.99); beautiful to look at, better to eat. Try the signature Charcoal Steamed Lobster, split, stuffed with herbs, wrapped in foil and placed on coals. Break into it without getting splashed by hot water and enjoy its unadulterated taste (1  lb $29.99). If you just want to enjoy a 44 oz specialty martini ($21.99, for two or three people) on the deck, thats OK, too.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/62-Restaurant-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="Handkerchief Pasta with Sauted Asparagus, Ricotta, Toasted Breadcrumbs in Shallot Vinaigrette " class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1472" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/62-Restaurant-Salem-MA-640x480.jpg" title="62 Restaurant Salem MA" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         EAT:
         <a href="http://www.62restaurant.com/">
          62 Restaurant
         </a>
        </b>
        <a href="http://www.62restaurant.com/">
         .
        </a>
        If youre looking for spaghetti and meatballs, look elsewhere. This Modern Italian Trattoria rolls out around twenty five pounds of fresh pasta every day, and what you
        <i>
         will
        </i>
        find are excellent dishes like Handkerchief Whole Wheat Pasta with Sauted Asparagus, Ricotta, Toasted Breadcrumbs in Shallot Vinaigrette ($12 for half portion, $24 entree).
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         EAT/LOCAL JOINT:
         <a href="http://www.comidasalem.com/">
          Comida Mexican Taquria
         </a>
        </b>
        . Owners grow their own ingredients for homemade salsas at this tiny popular Mexican taccoria.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         EAT/LOCAL HANGOUT:
         <a href="http://www.redssandwichshop.com/">
          Reds Sandwich Shop
         </a>
        </b>
        . Once the 1698 London Coffee House, a Patriot meeting place prior to the Revolution, its now called Reds and serves up fluffy omelets and $6.95 lunch specials (like Shrimp Scampi) diner style.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Hawthorne-Hotel-Salem-MA.jpg">
         <img alt="overstuffed chairs and table lamps set comfy old fashioned mood in Historic Salem hotel" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1478" height="480" src="http://www.getawaymavens.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Hawthorne-Hotel-Salem-MA-640x480.jpg" title="Lobby of Hawthorne Hotel, Salem MA" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h2>
        Where To Stay in Salem, MA
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         STAY:
         <a href="http://www.hawthornehotel.com/">
          Hawthorne Hotel
         </a>
        </b>
        <a href="http://www.hawthornehotel.com/">
         .
        </a>
        Money was raised by Public Stock Offering in 1925 to build lodging in Salem upscale enough to entertain clients of the Hy-Grade Electric Co. (which became Sylvania), and thus was born the Hawthorne Hotel, now a member of
        <strong>
         Historic Hotels of America
        </strong>
        . Youll find brass chandeliers, flat screen TVs and luxury-level bedding in antique-filled rooms. In the morning, soft classical music plays while you sip coffee on overstuffed lobby chairs. Its so grandeur-of-a-time, you may be surprised to find that the Hawthorne was one of the first hotels to have a
        <a href="http://www.hawthornehotelblog.com/">
         blog
        </a>
        , and has 4,500 Facebook fans (and growing). This is partly due to the fact that it is a pet-friendly hotel; monkeys, dogs, and even a box turtle have checked in
        <i>
         . $140-$350 (suite) per night includes coffee in lobby, free parking, free wi-fi.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         STAY:
         <a href="http://www.salemwaterfronthotel.com/">
          Salem Waterfront Hotel
         </a>
        </b>
        . Salems newest full service hotel and marina, rooms are large, modern and nautical chic.
        <i>
         $160-$220 per night includes free parking, free wi-fi.
        </i>
       </p>
