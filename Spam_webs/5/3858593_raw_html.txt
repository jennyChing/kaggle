              <meta content="UserComments:0" itemprop="interactionCount"/>
              <div class="entry-comments-views">
              </div>
             </div>
            </header>
            <div class="td-post-text-content">
             <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
             <p>
              Travelling is fun. Airplanes are not. But in order to actually get somewhere, chances are youre going to have to deal with the
              <a href="http://destinationfemme.com/google-flight-explorer-save-money-fly/">
               plane ride
              </a>
              first. Here are our best tips on how to conquer theflight.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Airplane food
             </h2>
             <p>
              <a href="http://destinationfemme.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/airplane-food.jpg">
               <img alt="Travel Tips: Your Airplane Survival Guide" class="size-full wp-image-12096 aligncenter" height="382" src="http://destinationfemme.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/airplane-food.jpg" width="650"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Is it just me, or does all airplane food smell the same? If the food they serve isnt your favourite, bringof your own with you. You can bring cup noodles for example, and ask an attendant to fill it with hot water for you. Oftentimes, theyll have sandwiches and snacks at the back of the plane too; you just have to ask.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Must-haves foryour carry-on
              <br/>
              <a href="http://destinationfemme.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/musthave1.jpg">
               <img alt="Travel Tips: Your Airplane Survival Guide" class="size-full wp-image-12040 aligncenter" height="350" src="http://destinationfemme.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/musthave1.jpg" width="650"/>
              </a>
             </h2>
             <p>
              1.
              <strong>
               <a href="http://ca.loccitane.com/shea-mini-hand-cream,19,1,1334,27176.htm">
                Hand Cream
               </a>
              </strong>
              Something about the air in an airplane makes your skin super dry, so pack along a mini hand cream.
              <br/>
              2.
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.sephora.com/rosebud-salve-in-a-tube-P266126">
                Lip balm
               </a>
              </strong>
               For the same reason as above, but for your lips. For this and the hand cream, pack them in a clear ziplock bag.
              <br/>
              3.
              <strong>
               <a href="http://gravol.ca/en/products/for-adults/gravol%C2%AE-filmkote%C2%AE-tablets/?cat=14">
                Nausea meds
               </a>
              </strong>
               We allknow how woozy the shaky ride can make us. Even if you personally dont experience motion sickness  bring some along anyway for the person next to you, if you know what I mean.
              <br/>
              4.
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.polyvore.com/yesstyle.com_women_style_berry_buttoned/thing?id=5584342">
                Cardigan
               </a>
              </strong>
               It can get a little chilly on the plane sometimes. A cardigan is easy to throw on, as well as work as a makeshift blanket/cover up
              <br/>
              5.
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.bestbuy.ca/en-CA/product/beats-by-dr-dre-beats-by-dr-dre-urbeats-in-ear-headphones-900-00066-01-black-900-00066-01/10228331.aspx?path=3f94637990314c6cafc9465735b76b80en02">
                Earphones
               </a>
              </strong>
               Dont be forced to buy one of their cheap headsets! Bring your own! Make sure yours are the kind to block out sound, because the engine can get pretty loud. So can babies.
              <br/>
              6.
              <strong>
               Your phone
              </strong>
               Of course. A smartphone is the ultimate multi-tasker. Its a music player as well as handheld gameconsole.
              <br/>
              7.
              <strong>
               A good book
              </strong>
               Youre going to want to bring a paperback, just cause its smaller and lighter. Make sure its an interesting read as well; you dont want to get bored halfway through. So get recommendations or reread a favourite.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Boredom
             </h2>
             <p>
              Boredom  the ultimate obstacle during a plane ride. Read that book youve packed in your carry-on. Play with your phone. Watch some movies. If you have a laptop, its a good idea to download (from
              <a href="http://www.apple.com/ca/itunes/">
               iTunes
              </a>
              , of course) some shows or movies that you can binge watch during the flight beforehand. Nowadays, planes usually provide outlets for charging your devices too.
             </p>
             <p>
              Another good tip is to just get out of your seat and take a (very) short (and limited) walk about the plane. It wont seem like much, but its a break from sitting, and youll feel refreshed.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Turbulence
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <a href="http://destinationfemme.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/airplane-e1411050569918.jpg">
               <img alt="Travel Tips: Your Airplane Survival Guide" class="size-full wp-image-12095 aligncenter" height="364" src="http://destinationfemme.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/airplane-e1411050569918.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </h2>
             <p>
              Turbulence is most possibly the most horrifying experience of a plane ride, because in your head
              <em>
               you are going to die
              </em>
              . In reality, youre not. Youre panicking inside your head but trying not to look too uncool on the outside. In cases of major turbulence, you need to distract yourself. Blast your music and lose yourself in a world where the plane is not falling. Grip the seat.
             </p>
             <p>
              The most important thing to help your fears is to tell yourself that
              <em>
               you are absolutely safe.
              </em>
             </p>
             <h2>
              In the event of a crash
             </h2>
             <p>
              First of all, crashing is
              <em>
               extremely
              </em>
              unlikely. So you really dont have to worry. But it doesnt hurtto know right? Before the plane takes off, the flight attendants will demonstrate all the safety procedures necessary for you to know in an emergency situation. Step 1 is to pay attention.
             </p>
             <p>
              Make sure your seatbelt is fitting snuggly. The looser it is, the more G-force youre going to feel during the fall. During the crash, youll need to adopt whats called the brace position, depicted here:
              <br/>
              <a href="http://destinationfemme.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/brace-position.jpg">
               <img alt="Travel Tips: Airplane Survival Guide" class="size-full wp-image-12048 aligncenter" height="360" src="http://destinationfemme.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/brace-position.jpg" width="650"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              If youve made it to the end of this article, that means youre ready. Youre ready for to take an amazing trip, and you now know everything you need in order to survive your next airplane ride.
             </p>
             <p>
              Congratulations. Go forth and have fun.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Tell us:
             </h3>
             <blockquote>
              <p style="">
               What are your tips for a great plane ride?
              </p>
             </blockquote>
