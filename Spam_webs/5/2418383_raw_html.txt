&lt;h1 style="text-align: center"&gt;How To Draw A Tree&lt;/h1&gt;
&lt;!-- start: shared_blocks.25621281#below-h1 --&gt;

&lt;div class="" style="width: 100%; box-sizing: border-box"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;!-- end: shared_blocks.25621281#below-h1 --&gt;
You want to know how to draw a tree because you are great nature lover. It is very good. We often forget about how trees are essentially important for our survival on the planet.
&lt;br&gt;
&lt;br&gt;
&lt;div class="AdsenseAd" style="float:left;padding:15px;"&gt;
&lt;script async src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js"&gt;

  If you are learning how to draw landscapes or nature in general then drawing trees will be an indispensable skill.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  I will show you a few simple examples of the process of drawing a tree with a pencil.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Basically if you learn this process, which is deceptively simple, you can
  <b>
   draw any tree
  </b>
  you like regardless on shape or size.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Before you start to draw a tree it is good to know a few simple facts about structure of a tree. I made this simple sketch in order to understand better the
  <b>
   Tree Structure
  </b>
  .
  <br/>
  <br/>
  <center>
   <b>
    <h3>
     Do You Love Drawing Nature?
    </h3>
   </b>
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/your-nature-scene-drawings.html#INV ">
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     <b>
      <h3>
       <u>
        Share Your Favorite
        <br/>
        Nature Scene Drawings HERE
       </u>
      </h3>
     </b>
    </p>
   </a>
  </center>
  <br/>
  <br/>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree-structure.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/xdraw-a-tree-structure01.jpg.pagespeed.ic.jmnUZSX8pW.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <center>
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </center>
  <br/>
  Knowing about tree structure will help you tremendously while you observe a tree (real or photo) as the object of your drawing.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  <center>
   <b>
    <h3>
     Draw A Tree Step By Step
    </h3>
   </b>
  </center>
  <br/>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree05.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/xdraw-a-tree05-1.jpg.pagespeed.ic.N-6Wzuf6oY.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  This is a standard type of a leaf tree. You can find similar trees in any forest or park around you.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree01.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/xdraw-a-tree01s.jpg.pagespeed.ic.LUB5nOHQJh.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Draw the ground line first. That will be the starting point from where the trunk is coming out from the ground.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Keep in mind when you draw a tree that branches of (especially) leaf trees tend to grow more upward than to the sides, as it is with coniferous trees.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Begin to draw from the trunk upward. Thickness of the trunk would determine the size of the tree.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Observe carefully how the branches are growing upward and copy them into your sketchbook. This is the initial sketch, so please make only weak and gentle pencil lines.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree02.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/xdraw-a-tree02s.jpg.pagespeed.ic.T8yWhlJJCY.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Draw a contour of the tree crown. Use your creativity here and, decide what shape of crown your tree will have.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  It is basically round but not too even. Often branches are popping out from the crown.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Mind the total balance and proportion with the trunk and branches.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree03.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/xdraw-a-tree03s.jpg.pagespeed.ic.L2SIQLCk6l.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Draw wavy irregular contours of the leaves on the branches. Here too, use your creativity. You can copy my drawing and make it better.
  <b>
   Lower part
  </b>
  of crown is always
  <b>
   slightly darker
  </b>
  than the top which comes into direct contact with the sunlight. Remember that, when you draw a tree.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree04.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree04s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Darken the lower part of crown. Do it gradually, make only weak strokes first. Draw shade under the tree.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree05.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree05s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  You can do some final touch now. Draw the trunk a bit darker. With the tip of your pencil draw sharper contour outline.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree10.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree10-1.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  This tree is very similar to the previous. The crown expands more to the sides
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree06.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree06s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  The process of the tree drawing is the very same as above. Draw the ground line, the trunk and a couple of branches.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Notice the branches here, as if they would creep up toward the sky. They are so eager to reach high out there. Can you see and feel that?
  <br/>
  <br/>
  When you draw plants or animals or any living thing, it is good if you can sense how that living object feels at the moment.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree07.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree07s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Shape of the crown is a little edgy. Crown expands to the both sides. This is one of the examples of how shape of the crown determines the impression of the whole tree.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  When you draw a tree, always watch out for this important feature. It is quite simple in fact, you only have to consciously train yourself to
  <b>
   see it
  </b>
  .
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree08.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree08s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  By irregular (still) weak pencil strokes draw the leaves. It will look like a bundle of shades but it is OK.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree09.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree09s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Darken the trunk and the branches. Gradually put more shade irregularly here and there on the parts of crown.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree10.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree10s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Finally, draw shade under the tree and make a final contour of the crown.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  <center>
   <b>

     Do You Love Drawing Nature?

   </b>

     <b>

    </p>
   </a>
  </center>

  </center>
  <br/>
  <br/>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree14.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree14-1.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  This is a coniferous tree example. It resembles to a huge Cedar Tree. Lets imagine that this is an old tree.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree11.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree11s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Draw the ground line. By weak pencil line indicate the center of the trunk and its height. That
  <b>
   center line
  </b>
   a kind of axis  will be your
  <b>
   guideline
  </b>
  .
  <br/>
  <br/>
  It is very easy to get derailed from the correct proportion balance while you are totally absorbed into your drawing. Such guideline then helps a lot.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Make an approximate outline contour sketch of the crown. Be creative a little here; draw your own shape while keeping the basic conical character.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree12.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree12s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Now when you have ready the thick trunk  which is also conical  draw a couple of branches here and there. Draw them all by weak pencil lines.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  When you draw a tree, especially a coniferous one, notice that many of them usually have one
  <b>
   long and high trunk
  </b>
  that looks like a
  <b>
   flagpole
  </b>
  .
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree13.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree13s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  Now decide which branches will remain visible and which will be covered inside the crown.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  So go ahead and do it.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Hold the pencil loose and tilted and lightly shade the entire crown, all in the same color tone.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Then draw darker spots here and there in the area of crown.
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <a href="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree14.jpg" target="_blank">
    <img border="2" src="http://www.easy-drawings-and-sketches.com/images/draw-a-tree14s.jpg"/>
   </a>
  </p>
  <p style="text-align: center;">
   <b>
    click the image to enlarge
   </b>
  </p>
  <b>
   Darken
  </b>
  a bit more the trunk and the branches. Draw a sharper contour outline around the crown. Draw shade under the tree and youre done.
  <br/>
  <br/>
  Find some ideas about sketching trees on my friend
  <a href="http://www.allaboutdrawings.com/tree-drawings.html" target="_blank">
   <b>
    Kerrys website
