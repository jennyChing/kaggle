    <h2 class="cnbc-clearfix pagehead" id="page_header">
     <a class="header_title" href="/cnbc-fed-survey/">
      CNBC Fed Survey
     </a>
    </h2>
    <div class="story-top">
     <div class="story-header-left twoCol">
      <h1 class="title" itemprop="name">
       Crude oil bottom seen at $40: CNBC Fed survey
      </h1>
      <div class="source" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
       <a href="/elizabeth-schulze/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
        Elizabeth Schulze
       </a>
       |
       <span class="twitter-url">
        <a href="http://twitter.com/eschulze9">
         @eschulze9
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <time class="datestamp" datetime="2015-01-28T09:16:49-0500" itemprop="datePublished">
       Wednesday, 28 Jan 2015 |  9:16  AM ET
      </time>
      <span class="sourcelogo" id="sourcelogo-cnbccom" itemprop="sourceOrganization">
       CNBC.com
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="story-header-right">
      <div>
       <div class="share-count-container hide">
        <div class="share-total">
        </div>
        <div class="share-text">

        </div>

    <section class="cols2">
     <div class="unit col1">
      <article>
       <div class="story">
        <div class="content" id="article_body">
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="player embed-container cnbcvideo autoplay">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="player" data-src="http://player.cnbc.com/cnbc_global?byGuid=3000349924&amp;autoPlay=true&amp;endCardGuids=3000383239|3000383217|3000383353" frameborder="0" height="298" id="player_0_0" mozallowfullscreen="" msallowfullscreen="" oallowfullscreen="" scrolling="no" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="533">
            <p>
             Fed Survey: Bottom line on oil
            </p>
            <p>
             CNBC's Steve Liesman reports on the effect of lower oil prices, according to the results of the CNBC Fed Survey.
            </p>
           </iframe>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            Crude oil's free fall may be close to bottoming, according to CNBC's January Fed Survey.
           </p>
           <p>
            Wall Streeters in the survey forecast that the lowest price for WTI crude in the current downturn will be $40 on average per barrel, about $6 below its current price. Among the 33 economists, money managers and investment strategists surveyed, the range of bottom estimates was $25 to $48.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Crude oil futures have tumbled nearly 60 percent from the recent peak of $107 in June.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container last">
          <div class="embed-container image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
           <img alt="A worker prepares to lift drills by pulley to the main floor of Endeavor Energy Resources LP's Big Dog Drilling Rig 22 in the Permian basin outside of Midland, Texas." height="298" src="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2015/01/28/102375690-460496974.530x298.jpg?v=1422453060" width="530"/>
           <meta content="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2015/01/28/102375690-460496974.jpg?v=1422453060" itemprop="image"/>
           <meta content="width='530'" itemprop="width"/>
           <meta content="height='298'" itemprop="height"/>
           <meta content="2015-01-28T08:51:00-0500" itemprop="datePublished"/>
           <meta content="2015-01-28T08:50:07-0500" itemprop="dateCreated"/>
           <meta content="2015-01-28T08:51:00-0500" itemprop="dateModified"/>
           <meta content="2015-01-28T08:50:07-0500" itemprop="uploadDate"/>
           <meta content="2099-12-31" itemprop="expires"/>
           <!-- TODO: defaulting to 2099 until we get the expiry date from toolbelt -->
           <meta content="jpg" itemprop="encodingFormat"/>
           <meta content="Brittany Sowacke | Bloomberg | Getty Images" itemprop="author"/>
           <meta content="CNBC" itemprop="publisher"/>
           <meta content="A worker prepares to lift drills by pulley to the main floor of Endeavor Energy Resources LP's Big Dog Drilling Rig 22 in the Permian basin outside of Midland, Texas." itemprop="caption"/>
           <div class="attribution">
            Brittany Sowacke | Bloomberg | Getty Images
           </div>
           <div class="caption">
            A worker prepares to lift drills by pulley to the main floor of Endeavor Energy Resources LP's Big Dog Drilling Rig 22 in the Permian basin outside of Midland, Texas.
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            Fifty-six percent respondents say the steep slide in oil prices is primarily the result of excess supply. Only 13 percent say plunging oil is a result of weak demand, while 28 percent say it is equal parts a supply and demand issue.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2015/01/27/rate-hike-seen-in-september-2015-cnbc-fed-survey.html" target="_self">
             Rate hike now seen in September: CNBC Fed survey
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            The Fed has said lower oil prices continue to be a net positive for the economy as consumers are left with more money to spend from their savings on gas. Plunging oil is seen boosting U.S. growth, with respondents predicting lower oil prices adding 0.27 percent to the gross domestic product in 2015. Meanwhile, they expect lower oil will reduce core inflation, which does not include food and energy, by 0.28 percent.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            But the steep slide in commodities is not without risks, particularly their impact on inflation.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/2015/01/26/gold-rises-on-soft-dollar-ahead-of-fed-rate-meeting.html" target="_self">
             Gold rises on soft dollar ahead of Fed rate meeting
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            "Low wage growth and a lack of inflation pose more than idle threats to economic growth," said survey respondent Kevin Giddis, head of fixed income capital markets at  Raymond James/Morgan Keegan. "If progress isn't made on both of these fronts, then there is a risk, coupled with a global slowdown, that the U.S. growth rate would retreat to levels that could be compared to a recession."
           </p>
           <p>
            Beyond the effect on the domestic economy, geopolitical risks from lower oil prices could drag on growth.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            "The biggest risk, from the decline in oil and commodity prices, is the transmission of a Russian recession to Europe, China, and beyond although it is unlikely to derail the U.S. expansion," said another survey respondent, Hugh Johnson, founder of Hugh Johnson Advisors. "With luck, we will muddle through."
           </p>
