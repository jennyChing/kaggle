        <p>
         <img alt="Newark teachers and students protest outside Newark Public Schools meeting." class="caption" height="112" src="/images/cache/70b8d4940d48120180a05c7acbc8da8a_w200_h112_sc.jpg" style="background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); line-height: 20px; height: 112px; width: 200px; margin: 3px; float: right;" title="Newark teachers and students protest outside Newark Public Schools meeting." width="200"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          May 22, 2014
         </em>
         <br/>
         <em>
          By Marc Bussanich
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          Newark, NJNewark students staged a dramatic sit-in during the School Advisory Board meeting after schools superintendent Cami Anderson joined the meeting, prompting her and the board to end its meeting early.
         </span>
         <a href="http://laborpress.org/sectors/education/3731-newark-students-sit-in">
          <strong style="line-height: 1.7em;">
           <em>
            WATCH VIDEO OF SIT-IN
           </em>
          </strong>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ms. Anderson has not been participating in the school boards public meetings because the community has been demanding for her resignation as she implements the One Newark plan that calls for expanding charter schools.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          As she entered the room, students with the Newark Student Union immediately called for her again to resign. They then sat down and said they would not move until their demands are addressed.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          We will not move until we have justice, shouted Newark Student Union President Kristin Towkaniuk.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          Ms. Anderson was heckled by the members of the community who also demanded she resign.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          When asked if the meeting could have been more productive, Antoinette Baskerville-Richardson, the school boards president, said that politically the meeting was productive.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          In the sense of making it clear to people as to what this superintendent is about I think this was a very productive meeting, said Baskerville-Richardson.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          When asked if there was a missed opportunity to hear from Ms. Anderson, Ms. Towkaniuk said they heard from Ms. Anderson clearly when she reportedly disparaged Newark when she spoke in April at the
         </span>
         <a href="http://edinnovation.gsvadvisors.com/" style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          Education Innovation Summit
         </a>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          in Arizona.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          She said Newark is horrible, that its the craziest job in the world. Weve heard enough, its time for [her] to leave Newark if its so bad and crazy, said Towkaniuk.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          The students were still sitting-in two hours after the board members left. Rashon Hasan, the school boards chairman, tried to persuade the students to leave by trying to arrange a Wednesday or Thursday meeting with Ms. Anderson.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          With the students and parents singing the folk song Keep Your Eyes on the Prize, Mr. Hasan said he and his colleagues were making arrangements to ensure the safety of the students should they decide to stay overnight. He assured that the students wouldnt be forced to leave.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.7em;">
          Safety at this point and time is the number one concern for us, said Hasan.
         </span>
        </p>
