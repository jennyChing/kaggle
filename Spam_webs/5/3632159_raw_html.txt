                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     Jack E. Leonard - Rock And Roll Music For Kids Over Sixteen (LP, 1956)
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2892511000653135274">
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.excitingsounds.com/img/jack-e-leonard-rock-and-roll-music-for-kids-over-sixteen.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="317" src="http://www.excitingsounds.com/img/jack-e-leonard-rock-and-roll-music-for-kids-over-sixteen.jpg" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      Jack E. Leonard
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      Rock And Roll Music For Kids Over Sixteen
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      RCA Vik LX-1080, mono [1956]
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     A mid-fifties album with a somewhat enticing and provocative title, Rock And Roll Music For Kids Over Sixteen is definitely a journey. In many ways, it is also a freakish (but certainly lovable) artifact of the Rockabilly era, because nothing quite similar enough to it exists on this planet.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     For those of you who arent familiar,
                     <a href="http://www.jackeleonard.net/" target="_blank">
                      <b>
                       Jack E. Leonard
                      </b>
                     </a>
                     was a comedian, and fairly well-known at that. In his time, he appeared extensively on television, recorded several albums, acted in movies, and was the narrator for
                     <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0059921/" target="_blank">
                      <i>
                       The World Of Abbott And Costello
                      </i>
                     </a>
                     (a compilation film released in 1965). Fortunately for the listener, Mr. Leonard performed this recording in a fully unrestrained fashion, and I can say with confidence that most people will get a chuckle or two, at the very least. Personally, I was cracking up the first time I spun this, as well as the second and third time. The humor is such that the content, tone AND musical structure amuse the listener equally, making it an all-around valuable record in terms of its entertainment potential.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     This album carries a consistent bad boy theme, in the classic
                     <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0048545/" target="_blank">
                      <i>
                       Rebel Without A Cause
                      </i>
                     </a>
                     meets Fonzie kind of way. It is, however, also self-deprecating, as Mr. Leonard repeatedly refers to himself as fat, middle-aged and goofy. Thats the charm of this recording in a nutshell, though  it is quite obviously a parody album, and not meant to be taken seriously on any level.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     To say a word or two about the label this album was released on,
                     <a href="http://www.excitingsounds.com/rca-vik/" target="_blank">
                      <b>
                       Vik Records
                      </b>
                     </a>
                     was a subsidiary of
                     <b>
                      RCA Victor
                     </b>
                     , and was where most of their more doubtful acts (in terms of monetary potential) found their record deal. Personally, Im a fan of Vik Records, and I find their catalogue intriguingly obscure and no less imaginative than the average mainstream release by RCA Victor. Needless to say, this album could never have matched the success of Elvis Presley or Bill Haley And His Comets, but Jackie Gleason DID write the liner notes and that counts for something, right? ;)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     All in all, I think youre in for a delightfully novel experience with this Novelty record, I certainly was myself. :)
