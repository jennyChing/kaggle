    <h3>
     Preparing For Sport
    </h3>
    <br/>
    <!--&lt;p class="post-meta"&gt;Posted &lt;span&gt;11 May 2015&lt;/span&gt; by &lt;a href="#"&gt;Charlotte&lt;/a&gt; in &lt;a href="http://www.deadseabathcare.co.uk/tag/exercise/" rel="tag"&gt;exercise&lt;/a&gt;&lt;br /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;-->
    <figure>
     <img alt="sports preparation" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="722" src="http://www.deadseabathcare.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-11-at-16.13.45.png" width="903"/>
    </figure>
    <div class="at-above-post addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Preparing For Sport" data-url="http://www.deadseabathcare.co.uk/preparing-for-sport/">
    </div>
    <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
    </div>
    <p>
     The weather is getting better. Its lighter in the evenings, lighter in the mornings and theres more daylight to be out and about in.
    </p>
    <p>
     Which means that were all
     <em>
      more inclined to be active
     </em>
     . And with the prospect of showing off more flesh on the beach and in summer clothes, you might feel prompted to shape up a bit, drop a few pounds and tone up.
    </p>
    <p>
     Its classic that after Wimbledon you get to see more people out on public tennis courts, knocking up. When the rest of the year they dont venture onto a court at all.
    </p>
    <h2>
     The trick is not to overdo things too quickly
    </h2>
    <p>
     When you see an athlete or a perfectly honed body, what you have to remember is that it didnt happen overnight.
    </p>
    <p>
     Building up a six pack and perfect legs takes time and effort.
    </p>
    <p>
     So dont throw yourself into a faddish exercise and diet regime because it wont last  and you cant expect quick results either.
    </p>
    <p>
     Slow and steady is the way to go.
    </p>
    <p>
     Start every day with some gentle stretches. Work out how you can fit exercise into your schedule in the longterm. Rather than go on a crash diet, research healthy eating patterns that you are going to be able to stick with.
    </p>
    <p>
     And as far as exercise goes, if you go all out for a run, tennis game or gym session when youre not used to it, youre to pull a muscle or damage a joint and then youll be out of the game for ages.
    </p>
    <p>
     So take some professional advice, have a session with a personal trainer to see whats doable and work out how much weight you want to lose and how long its going to take you (a 1lb a week is healthy). Oh, and remember to drink plenty of water during your sessions to make sure you are hydrated.
    </p>
    <h2>
     When youre on a fitness and health regime, youre going to want to pamper yourself and reward yourself too
    </h2>
    <p>
     So treat yourself occasionally  maybe some chocolate, a glass of wine, your favourite pudding.
    </p>
    <p>
     And after exercising, the perfect way to reward your body is with a soak in our
     <a href="http://www.deadseabathcare.co.uk/work/genuine-dead-sea-bath-salts/" title="Genuine Dead Sea Bath Salts">
      Genuine Dead Sea Bath Salts (
     </a>
     which dont involve you taking on board any calories!).
    </p>
    <p>
     Let the bath salts work their centuries-old magic by relaxing and soothing tired muscles. And if youd like to soak in water thats fragranced, try our
     <a href="http://www.deadseabathcare.co.uk/work/aromatherapy-bath-salts-with-frankincense-oil-rose-petals/" title="Aromatherapy Bath Salts with Frankincense oil &amp; rose petals">
      Bath Salts wtih Frankincense and Rose Petals
     </a>
     or
     <a href="http://www.deadseabathcare.co.uk/work/aromatherapy-bath-salts-with-jasmine-oil-rose-petals/" title="Aromatherapy Bath Salts with Jasmine oil &amp; rose petals">
      Jasmine and Rose Petals
     </a>
     .
    </p>
    <p>
     Afterwards, smooth some of our
     <a href="http://www.deadseabathcare.co.uk/work/natural-mineral-body-lotion/" title="Natural Mineral Body Lotion">
      Natural Dead Sea Mineral Body Lotion
     </a>
     into your skin. (
     <a href="http://www.deadseabathcare.co.uk/testimonials/" title="Testimonials">
      Which has also been reported to help
     </a>
     with eczema, psoriasis and other skin conditions.)
    </p>
    <p>
     You can rest assured that youre avoiding any artificial chemicals  all our products are 100% natural, vegan and sls free  they only contain naturally occuring minerals from the Dead Sea, a source of comfort for skin conditions and tired bodies for centuries past.
    </p>
    <p>
     You can buy our Bath Salts and other products here
     <a href="http://www.deadseabathcare.co.uk/products/" title="Products">
      online at Boots
     </a>
     or in the High Street.
    </p>
    <p>
     So when youre preparing for sport, make sure were part of your post-exercise wind down regime!
    </p>
