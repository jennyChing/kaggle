      <p>
       Its not easy to stand out in the startup world, but a great promo video makes for a good start. Here are some of our favorites from across the web.
      </p>
      <h2>
       1. Dollar Shave Club
      </h2>
      <p>
       One startup that got a lot of
       <a href="http://mashable.com/2012/03/06/youtube-startup-dollar-shave/">
        attention
       </a>
       thanks to a super video was
       <a href="http://www.dollarshaveclub.com/">
        Dollar Shave Club
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/ZUG9qYTJMsI?rel=0" width="560">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <h2>
       2. Snazzy Room
      </h2>
      <p>
       The team at
       <a href="http://snazzyroom.com">
        Snazzy Room
       </a>
       played on Dollar Shave Clubs popularity in this recruitment ad.
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/KULAkpNXBD8?rel=0" width="560">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <h2>
       3. Active Building
      </h2>
      <p>
       What could have been an average software video from
       <a href="http://www.activebuilding.com/default.aspx">
        Active Building
       </a>
       has been spiced up with some animated zombies.
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="http://player.vimeo.com/video/40689075" width="560">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <h2>
       4. Panorama 9
      </h2>
      <p>
       Panorama 9s IT-Man 8-bit video is a fun introduction to the product, complete with damsels in distress and giant dragons.
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/oQWSceSd1jI?rel=0" width="560">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <h2>
       5. Do
      </h2>
      <p>
       In the over-saturated market of task-management apps,
       <a href="http://do.com">
        Do.com
       </a>
       used a school election campaign as the premise for a slick promo video.
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="http://player.vimeo.com/video/33419341?title=0&amp;byline=0&amp;portrait=0" width="560">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <h2>
       6. Jotly
      </h2>
      <p>
       Believe it or not,
       <a href="http://www.jotly.co/">
        Jotly
       </a>
       started out as just a parody video making fun of the abundance of mobile rating-and-suggestion apps. It proved to be so popular that the Jotly app was created after all!
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/QIWpbfZHHzc?rel=0" width="560">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <h2>
       7. Nosh
      </h2>
      <p>
       Co-founded by the very guy who makes fun of these kind of apps with Jotly, food-review app
       <a href="http://www.nosh.com/">
        Nosh
       </a>
       used a similar brand of humor for this video.
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/oFdvqe13u34?rel=0" width="560">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <h2>
       8. Vooza
      </h2>
      <p>
       Putting together every cliche weve come to expect from a startup video,
       <a href="http://vooza.com/">
        Vooza
       </a>
       created this hilarious promo of how they built Vooza.
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe frameborder="0" height="270" scrolling="auto" src="http://content.bitsontherun.com/players/ykEIdbyX-DnUnfcRt.html" width="480">
       </iframe>
      </p>
