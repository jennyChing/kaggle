       <h1>
        Get Your Homes Groove Back  Retro Interiors Baby!
       </h1>
       <span class="post-meta">
        By
        <a href="http://www.eatsleeplive.co.uk/blog/author/andrea/" rel="author" title="Posts by Andrea Morgan">

       <p>
        A couple of weeks ago I shared with you a number of key
        <a href="http://www.eatsleeplive.co.uk/blog/features/interior-trends-for-2015/">
         interior trends of 2015
        </a>
        , one of which was Modern/Mid-century Modern elements. This trend is a throwback to modern design elements of the 1950s through the 1970s and requires you to take a key piece and allow it to work with the design of your room. Think of g plan designs and shapes with their beautiful range of colours. You couldnt get more loud a design statement than the 1970s, come on those psychedelic patterns! But we do have love of the retro, whether it is a piece of furniture that has been passed down through the generations or a token piece that enhances your contemporary dcor. Yes the 1970s may have been bright but many of its designs are timeless treasures that are being re-invented for modern day living.
       </p>
       <p>
        One of the most recognisable pieces of the 1970s was Hornsea pottery. Known for its distinctive patterns it was a staple throughout homes in the UK. You can still find pieces of Hornsea pottery today, such as this Tea Storage Jar on ebay.
