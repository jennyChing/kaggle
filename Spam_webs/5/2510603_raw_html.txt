          Top Frequently Asked Questions From Home Buyers
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-thumbnail">
        <img alt="Home Buying FAQs" class="attachment-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="150" src="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Top-Frequently-Asked-Questions-from-Home-Buyers-150x150.png" width="150"/>

       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="11227602" data-link="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/top-frequently-asked-questions-home-buyers/" data-summary="" data-title="Top Frequently Asked Questions From Home Buyers">
        </div>
        <div class="pf-content">
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_2391" style="width: 460px">
          <img alt="Home Buying FAQs" class="lazy wp-image-2391" data-original="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Top-Frequently-Asked-Questions-from-Home-Buyers-682x1024.png" height="675" src="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-images-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" width="450"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
          </p>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="Home Buying FAQs" class="wp-image-2391" height="675" src="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Top-Frequently-Asked-Questions-from-Home-Buyers-682x1024.png" width="450"/>
          </noscript>
          Top Frequently Asked Questions from Home Buyers
         </div>
         <p>
          Whether a buyer is purchasing their first home or their fifth home, the home buying process can createmany emotions and feelings. One of the best ways to ensure the process is not overwhelming for a buyer is to be well educated and properly prepared for the process.
         </p>
         <p>
          There are normally many questions that home buyers will have throughout the process. Even an experienced home buyer can forget exactly how the process works and what the proper steps are to ensure the process is relatively smooth.
         </p>
         <p>
          When buying a home, one of the most important things to understand is that no question is a dumb question. If youre unsure of something when buying a home, ask!
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           There are many questions that home buyers seem to ask quite often. Some questions are before starting the home buying process, during a house hunt, while writing a contract, or after an offer is accepted. Here are the top frequently asked questions from home buyers.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #000080;">
           <strong>
            <span style="font-size: 18pt;">
             Questions Asked Before Starting The Home Buying Process
            </span>
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              Should Italk with a bank before looking at homes?
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          The answer to the question is YES! There are tons ofreasons why you should talk with a bank and
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/when-and-why-should-i-get-pre-approved-for-a-mortgage/" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank" title="When and Why Should I Get Pre-Approved For A Mortgage?">
            get pre-approved before looking at homes
           </a>
          </span>
          . First and foremost, talking with a bank before looking at homes can help you understand exactly how much you can afford. There is no reason to look at homes that are listed for $250,000 if you can only afford up to $200,000.
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre a first time home buyer, talking with a bank before looking at homes is strongly suggested, as there are many
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/first-time-home-buyer-programs/" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank" title="First Time Home Buyer Programs">
            first time home buyer programs
           </a>
          </span>
          available. These programs can vary from state to state and county to county, so knowing exactly whats available to you, is critical.
         </p>
         <p>
          Another important reason to talk with a bank before looking at homes is so you understand exactly what
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/hidden-costs-fees-buying-home/" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank">
            costs are associated with buying a home
           </a>
          </span>
          . There are many home buyers who dont understand the difference between a down payment, pre-paid items, and escrows, which can be thoroughly explained by a mortgage professional. A mortgage professional can give you advice on the type of financing you should be looking to obtain and also whether or not you should request the seller to contribute towards your closing costs, also known as a sellers concession.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #b8911d;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               Should I buy or continue to rent?
              </span>
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Buying a home can be a very solid investment. This being said, renting can also be a better option for some, depending on the circumstances. The current interest rates are incredible. A 30-year FHA mortgage can be locked in at a rate of around 3.5%. Since the interest rates are so low, it actually can be cheaper to pay a mortgage right now than paying rent.
         </p>
         <p>
          There are questions that you should ask yourself before deciding to buy a home. One of the most important things to consider is the length you plan on staying in a home, if you were to purchase. If the answer is only a few years, its likely the better decision is to continue renting. Another question to ask yourself is whether you are ready to take on the additional responsibilities of owning a home. When owning a home there will be general home maintenance that should be done, are you ready for that?
         </p>
         <p>
          Buying a home is a great option in many cases, but not always.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               Can I find a rent-to-own property?
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Can you find a needle in a haystack? Of course you can, but the probability isnt very high. The same can be said about a rent-to-own property. A common question from home buyers is whether rent-to-owns exist or whether an owner would consider that option. They are out there, but there are somethings that you need to know before agreeing to a rent-to-own.
         </p>
         <p>
          When an owner is offering rent-to-own as a possible financing option, they are taking on a high risk since in most cases, a rent-to-own buyer has a credit score that is not impeccable. Since an owner is taking a higher risk the terms for a rent-to-own must be considerably favorable for the owner. This often leads to less than favorable terms for a buyer. When looking at a rent-to-own as an option you can expect to provide a considerable amount of money down and a higher interest rate than what a lender is currently offering.
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre able to purchase a home by financing through a bank or lender, you will be better offbecause the terms will be more favorable.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               I own a home, should I buy another before selling my current home?
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          There is truly no concrete correctanswer to this question. There are pros and cons to
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.maxrealestateexposure.com/buying-home-selling-existing-property/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.maxrealestateexposure.com/buying-home-selling-existing-property/', 'buying a home before selling your current home');" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank">
            buying a home before selling your current home
           </a>
          </span>
          and the same can be said about selling your current home before buying another.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
           <strong>
            <em>
             Buying a home before selling your current home
            </em>
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          The biggest benefit to buying a home before selling your current home is the fact that you have a suitable property lined up. This can reduce the stress and pressure of having to find a home once your current home is sold. This however also can create disappointment and heartbreak. If you are unable to purchase a new home without having to sell your current home, youre purchase offer is going to be
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/4-common-contract-contingencies-when-buying-a-home/" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank">
            contingent upon sale and transfer
           </a>
          </span>
          of title of your current home. If your current home does not sell in a timely manner, this can lead to you getting bumped by a non-contingent buyer and you losing out on the home youre looking to purchase, which can be devastating.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
           <em>
            <strong>
             Selling your current home before buying a newhome
            </strong>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          The time it takes to sell your current home is unpredictable. There is no crystal ball that exists that can tell you exactly how many days it will take. Selling your current home before buying a newhome will put you in an ideal position to negotiate on the new home youre purchasing due to the fact you are purchasing without the sale contingency of your current home.
         </p>
         <p>
          One risk of selling your current home without buying a new home first is the chance of not being able to have a place to live. There are options if your current home sellers before buying another though. A rent-back can sometimes be negotiated with the buyer of your current home. A rent-back would allow you to retain possession of your current home for a certain number of days after closing at the expense of paying the buyers mortgage. A rent-back allows for additional time to find a new home.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_2393" style="width: 460px">
            <img alt="When Buying a Home Hire a Realtor" class="lazy wp-image-2393" data-original="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/When-Buying-a-Home-Hire-a-Realtor-682x1024.png" height="675" src="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-images-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" width="450"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">

            </noscript>
            When Buying a Home Hire a Realtor
           </div>
           <p>
            <span style="color: #b8911d;">
             <em>
              <strong>
               <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
                Do Ireally need a Realtor when buying a home?
               </span>
              </strong>
             </em>
            </span>
           </p>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          When buying a home, its strongly recommended you have a Realtor. There are many reasons why you should have a Realtor represent your best interests when buying a home. Keep in mind, all Realtors are not the same! When choosing a buyers agent, make sure you know how to
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/how-to-interview-a-buyers-agent-when-buying-a-home/" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank" title="How To Interview A Buyers Agent When Buying a Home">
            properly interview prospective Realtors when buying a home
           </a>
          </span>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Attempting to buy a home without a Realtor can really make the home buying process more difficult. Having a Realtor is always recommended when buying a home. One thing not to do when buying a home is calling the listing agent because you dont want to bother your Realtor. This is one
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.maxrealestateexposure.com/things-buyers-do-that-real-estate-agents-hate/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.maxrealestateexposure.com/things-buyers-do-that-real-estate-agents-hate/', 'thing that real estate agents hate');" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank">
            thing that real estate agents hate
           </a>
          </span>
          .
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              Who pays the Realtor fees when buying a home?
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          One reasons why buyers ask the question about the need of having a Realtor when buying a home is because they dont understand
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.greatcoloradohomes.com/blog/who-pays-the-realtor-fees-in-a-real-estate-transaction.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.greatcoloradohomes.com/blog/who-pays-the-realtor-fees-in-a-real-estate-transaction.html', 'who pays the Realtor fees when buying a home');" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank">
            who pays the Realtor fees when buying a home
           </a>
          </span>
          . There are no guarantees, however, in most cases the seller pays the Realtor fees.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 18pt; color: #000080;">
           <strong>
            Questions Asked While House Hunting
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               What is a short sale?
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Before getting involved with a short-sale, its important you understand exactly what it is and what to expect from a short sale. The easiest wayto understand ashort sale is the sale ofa homein which the proceeds from the sale are less than the balance of debts secured by liens against the property and the home owner cannot afford to pay the liens in full.
         </p>
         <p>
          Before
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/seven-things-to-consider-when-purchasing-a-short-sale/" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank">
            purchasing a short sale, you should consider
           </a>
          </span>
          things such as the time it can take for a short sale response, the fact that a foreclosure is still possible, and that many short sale properties are in disarray. Short sales are not impossible to buybutyou must be patient and be in no immediate rush to move.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              What is a foreclosure?
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Believe it or not, foreclosures can actually be a smoother transaction than a short sale. A foreclosure, sometimes referred to as a REO, is a property that is owned by a lender. If youre considering the purchase of a foreclosure, its important to understand that most are sold as-is. Foreclosures, if not purchased by an owner occupant, are often purchased by investors, fixed up, flipped, and sold to a owner occupant.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              How is the neighborhood/area?
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          When buying a home, a common question home buyers have is regarding the neighborhood/area. As a real estate professional, there are rules against steering and providing personal insight into specific areas and neighborhoods. This doesnt mean that your Realtor cannot provide you with
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/9-tips-to-help-choose-right-neighborhood-when-buying-home/" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank">
            tips to help you choose the right neighborhood
           </a>
          </span>
          when buying a home. Many buyers wonder about the growth of the local economy, crime statistics, taxes, and local amenities. If you have a top Realtor when buying a home, you should be able to receive all of the pertinent information to allow you to make an educated decision on areas and neighborhoods.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              How are the schools?
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          This is another question that Realtors should tread very lightly with. There is no doubt that
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.maxrealestateexposure.com/how-schools-can-impact-home-prices/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.maxrealestateexposure.com/how-schools-can-impact-home-prices/', 'schools impact property values');" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank">
            schools impact property values
           </a>
          </span>
          . Just like tips for selecting a neighborhoods, a top Realtor should be able to provide you with names or websites where you can find information on the local schools so that you can determine whether or not the schools are acceptable to you or not.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               What are the average utility bills?
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          When buying a home, its important to know what additional costs will be in addition to the monthly mortgage payment. Utility bills are just one of the additional costs to consider when buying a home. Utility bills can be obtained from the home owner and in some cases, from the local utility company, who can provide averages over the past 12 months. Keep in mind, everyone prefers to have their home temperature different, so the average bill could be different if you were to purchase the home.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_2394" style="width: 460px">
            <img alt="How many homes should you look at before buying one" class="lazy wp-image-2394" data-original="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/How-many-homes-should-you-look-at-before-buying-one-682x1024.png" height="675" src="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-images-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" width="450"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
            </p>
            <noscript>
             <img alt="How many homes should you look at before buying one" class="wp-image-2394" height="675" src="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/How-many-homes-should-you-look-at-before-buying-one-682x1024.png" width="450"/>
            </noscript>
            How many homes should you look at before buying one?
           </div>
           <p>
            <span style="color: #b8911d;">
             <strong>
              <em>
               <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
                Whats the age of the _?
               </span>
              </em>
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          When looking at homes, many buyers want to know the ages of specific items in a home. The most popular items in a home that buyers want to know about are the major mechanical items, such as the roof, furnace, water heater, and air conditioning (if applicable). An experienced Realtor should be able to find the dates of a furnace, water heater, and air conditioning unit by looking at the serial numbers. The roof age is often known by the home owner. If not, the age usually can be approximately determined by looking at the roof characteristics, such as the sagging areas and the way the shingles are laying.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               How many homes should I look atbefore putting in a purchase offer?
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          This question is often asked and is a simple answer. The answer is, there is no specific number of homes you should look at before buying a home. Dont feel that if you were to purchase the first home you look at that youre making a mistake. Same can be said if it takes you looking at 25 homes.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #000080;">
           <strong>
            <span style="font-size: 18pt;">
             Questions Asked While Placing A Purchase Offer
            </span>
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              How much should I offer the sellers?
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          When buying a home, you are the only one who can determine how much you should offer a seller. Certainly its suggested you ask for your Realtors advice and thoughts, but ultimately you are the only person who can determine how much you should offer.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #b8911d;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               What is an earnest money deposit?
              </span>
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          An earnest money deposit is also frequently referred to as a good faith deposit. When a buyer purchases a home, they provide the sellers real estate company a deposit to hold in their escrow account. The primary purpose of this deposit is to show a seller you are serious about purchasing their home. The amount that is deposited is subtracted from the final figure that a buyer pays at the closing table. In most cases, the larger the deposit, the stronger a purchase offer looks to a seller.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #b8911d;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               How long does the seller have to respond to myoffer?
              </span>
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          There is not a standard answer to this question. A purchase offer will have a life. The life of the offer can vary from 12 hours to 3 or 4 days. There are many circumstances that can effect the length of the life of the offer. Your Realtor should know how long of a life to give to your offer. If youre looking to purchase a home that is newly listed and the possibility of multiple offers exists, a shorter life is recommended. If the home youre looking to purchase has been on the market for 3 months and the seller is located out of town, a 2 day life maybe necessary and/or recommended.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              What if my offer is rejected?
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          When a purchase offer is submitted to the seller there are generally four possibleresponses. The first is an accepted offer, the second is a counter offer, the third is a rejected offer, and the final is an offer that is not responded to. If your offer is rejected, meaning the seller says no and doesnt counter, you have the right to place another offer. Its not very common an offer is rejected or not responded to, unless a seller is offended by a low-ball offer.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              Do I have the option to have any inspections?
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          When buying a home, you have the option to perform several types of inspections. The purchase offer you write can be contingent upon a satisfactory home inspection, pest inspection, chimney inspection, radon test, and many other inspections. In most cases, its recommended that when buying a home, you at the bare minimum have a home inspection. There are
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/top-10-common-home-inspection-findings/" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank" title="Top 10 Common Home Inspection Findings">
            home inspection findings
           </a>
          </span>
          that are more common than others, however, no two homes are the same so its a great idea to get the home inspected.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #000080;">
           <strong>
            <span style="font-size: 18pt;">
             Questions Asked After A Purchase Offer Is Accepted
            </span>
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_2395" style="width: 460px">
            <img alt="Home Buying FAQ - When is the closing date" class="lazy wp-image-2395" data-original="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Home-Buying-FAQ-When-is-the-closing-date-682x1024.png" height="675" src="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-images-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" width="450"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">

             <img alt="Home Buying FAQ - When is the closing date" class="wp-image-2395" height="675" src="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Home-Buying-FAQ-When-is-the-closing-date-682x1024.png" width="450"/>
            </noscript>
            Home Buying FAQ  When is the closing date?
           </div>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
             <em>
              <strong>
               Whats the next step?
              </strong>
             </em>
            </span>
           </p>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Congratulations! Your
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.rochesterrealestateblog.com/offer-accepted-now/" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank" title="Offer Accepted!  Now What?">
            offer was accepted, now what
           </a>
          </span>
          ? Between contract acceptance and the closing date, there are many things that need to be completed. In a nutshell, after an offer is accepted, generally any inspections will be completed. After the inspections, you complete a formal mortgage application and last but not least, the title, abstract, survey, and any miscellaneous paperwork is completed. When buying a home, finding the perfect home is only one part of actually becoming a homeowner. Throughout the mortgage process, you should expect the bank to require documentation, letters, and other items from you to satisfy the bank conditions, so dont be upset or surprised when this happens.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #b8911d;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
               Do I need todo a final walk-through?
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          As a buyer, you have the option to perform a final walk-through. Is a final walk through a requirement?  NO. Is a
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           <a href="http://www.thelasvegasluxuryhomepro.com/blog/is-a-final-walk-thru-really-necessary.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.thelasvegasluxuryhomepro.com/blog/is-a-final-walk-thru-really-necessary.html', 'final walk throughnecessary');" style="color: #99cc00;" target="_blank">
            final walk throughnecessary
           </a>
          </span>
          ? YES. Generally when buying a home several weeks go by between when you last walked through your home. Lots of things can change during that time. When doing a final walk through a few things you should check is thatfurnace is working, the toilets are flushing properly, and there is hot water.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #b8911d;">
            <em>
             <strong>
              When is the closing date?
             </strong>
            </em>
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          When buying a home, the excitement level is extremely high. Its important to understand that the closing date in the purchase offer is a target and not a guarantee. Before you hire the movers and take time off from work, know that the closing date in the contract isnt necessarily the date you will own your new home. Many buyers will ask their Realtor this question, however, it isnt up to the Realtors when a closing will be. The attorneys are the ones who have to set the closing date and time.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #000080;">
           <strong>
            <span style="font-size: 18pt;">
             Final Thoughts
            </span>
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          When buying a home, being prepared and well educated can really make a huge difference. Again, no question is a dumb question and if you are unsure of something, ask! The home buying process begins before you ever look at one home and the process continues all the way up to the final walk-through. When buying a home, its critical youre on your game, stay organized, and remain focused throughout the entire process. After reading thesefrequently asked questions, you should now have a stronger understanding of what to expect throughout the process.
