        <p>
         "Beloved" isn't a word you'd use to describe the Smart fortwo -- not in the U.S., anyway. To those of us used to America's wide open spaces and the super-sized portions of American restaurants, the fortwo seems dangerously, unsatisfyingly petite. Our own John Voelcker scored it a cautious
         <a href="http://www.thecarconnection.com/overview/smart_fortwo_2014">
          six out of ten
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         If the fortwo were going to be popular anywhere in the country, though, we'd assume it would be San Francisco. SF is generally considered forward-thinking, eco-friendly, Euro-ish -- all qualities shared by the fortwo (although the pint-sized model's eco-credentials aren't all they should be, thanks to so-so fuel economy). Surely, San Franciscans show the fortwo some love, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         Wrong.
        </p>
        <div class="ad floating-ad" id="adsense1" style="width:468px;height:80px;">
         <script type="text/javascript">
          //
		google_ad_client = "pub-4956733744046268";

		google_ad_height = 80;
    //
         </script>
         <script src="http://pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/show_ads.js" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
        </div>
        <p>
         As our colleagues at
         <a href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1091313_new-san-francisco-sport-smart-tipping-cows-are-so-passe">
          Green Car Reports
         </a>
         wrote, there was a spate of "Smart tippings" in SF over the weekend -- three cars rolled onto their front or rear ends Sunday night. A witness described seeing between six and eight individuals carrying out one of the dastardly deeds. Given the fortwo's 1800-pound curb weight, that sounds about right.
        </p>
        <p>
         Why is this happening?
        </p>
        <p>
         It could be that SF is finally catching up to other parts of the world, where fortwo tipping has been a "thing" since 2009. Or perhaps SF has been infested with a bunch of urban cowboys who mistakenly think that
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/09/10/cow-tipping-real-prank_n_3897303.html" target="_blank">
          cow tipping is real
         </a>
         . But the most likely bet is that the city's growing hatred for high-tech "
         <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2014/02/19/tech/mobile/google-glasshole/" target="_blank">
          Glassholes
         </a>
         " has spilled over from
         <a href="http://blog.sfgate.com/techchron/2014/04/01/google-bus-blocked-by-dancing-protesters-in-s-f-mission-district/" target="_blank">
          boycotting buses
         </a>
         to flipping cars.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Moral of the story
         </strong>
         : if
         <a href="http://www.thecarconnection.com/news/1076166_oprah-says-you-get-a-car-you-get-a-car-you-get-a-smart-fortwo">
          Oprah gives you a Smart fortwo
         </a>
         and you live in the Bay Area, keep it locked in the garage.
        </p>
