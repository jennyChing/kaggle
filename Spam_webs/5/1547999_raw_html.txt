Cole tells Sera,&ldquo;there are songs in every part of you&hellip; waiting for you to hear them&rdquo; and &ldquo;the sky wants to say something, it&rsquo;s trying,&rdquo; plus, Sera experiencesdj vu (Cole says it&rsquo;s memories slipping to her across the Veil) and hears whispers that other elves do not (sounds similar to the mostly unintelligible whispers the Inquisitor hears if she drinks from the Well). Flemeth has accepted and integrated Mythal (&ldquo;she is a part of me, no more separate than your heart from your chest&rdquo;) so she can fully understand the knowledge, memories, and power that comes from Mythal, but Sera might be scared and angry because she&rsquo;s trying to suppress or avoid these feelings and the wisp is probably still too weak to manifest more strongly.

So here&rsquo;s the big question: if Sera contains the wisp of an ancient elf &lsquo;god,&rsquo; then why is she so hateful toward 'elfy&rsquo; elves? (elfy is an important distinction because she seems to judge people for how they act and not simply what they are) There are a few possibilities. If Sera is getting memories from Andruil&rsquo;s wisp she could be reacting to Andruil owning slaves and slaves&rsquo; horrible treatment at the hands of their elven masters in general. She could have some understanding of the truth about the ancient elves and the atrocities committed by the 'gods,&rsquo; so she&rsquo;d be angry at anyone who worshiped and glorified them. On top of this, she can&rsquo;t even explain it to anyone because she&rsquo;d have to reveal (and admit to herself) what&rsquo;s inside of her. Lastly, her rage against elfy elves could be so extreme because of self-hate and guilt if the wisp inside of her embodies everything she thinks is wrong with the world.
 Here is animportantcodex entry onAndruilfrom the mosaic in theToM: 
 Let&rsquo;s break this codex down: 

&ldquo;Yet even a god should not linger there, and each time she entered the Void,Andruilsuffered longer and longer periods of madness after returning.&rdquo;and &ldquo;ButMythal&rsquo;smagic sappedAndruil&rsquo;sstrength, and stole her knowledge of how to find the Void. After this, the great hunter could never make her way back to the abyss, and peace returned.&rdquo; 

Thiscould explain why Sera doesnt know what she is (whatever that is),where she came from, and her apparent loss of memory.She remembers being a child in the alienage but says she doesnt remember her parents and doesn&rsquo;t care to remember where she was born.




&ldquo;One dayAndruilgrew tired of hunting mortal men and beasts. She began stalking The Forgotten Ones, wicked things that thrive in the abyss.&rdquo;and &ldquo;Mythalspread rumors of a monstrous creature and took the form of a great serpent, waiting forAndruilat the base of a mountain.&rdquo; 

The Forgotten Ones may be dragons or,likeFlemeth,take dragon form. The evidence for this is shaky but there is more ancient elven text that suggests the greater gods and their chosen could.  
If true, it could explain why the game goes to lengths toshowhow much Sera enjoys hunting dragons. From her dialogue when you encounter dragons, to war table notes,and this from her completed codex entry:





 &ldquo;Yet even a god should not linger there, and each time she entered the Void,Andruilsuffered longer and longer periods of madness after returning.&rdquo;

When you take Sera to The Fade and go to the area with the gravestones Seras specifically says thather greatest fear isTheNothing, which is strange unless you consider that The Nothing could be another way to describe The Void.





Before we leave this codex behind, take one last look at the overallstory ofMythalluringAndruilinto a ambush and theirfight that ends inMythalstealing her strength and knowledge. In her companion quest,Sera is lured into an ambush by a nobleman and,if certainoptions are chosen,she goesout of her mindwith rage andwreckshim. Her reaction was unnervingly brutal and felt extremeeven in those circumstances, but it could&rsquo;ve been an echo ofAndruil&rsquo;srage in the same way thatFlemethis still angry andwants revenge for being betrayed personally and for Mythal being betrayed in the past.   Another quote from Cole reinforces the idea that Sera is a vessel for something:

&ldquo;Fleet-footed and free, the arrow that caught the miller&rsquo;s sack, but no longer shot alone, a quiver in a quiver.&rdquo;

The 'quiver in a quiver&rsquo; part may be significantifbothAndruiland Sera areempty of most oftheir significant memories(essentially a hollow container within another hollow container)and the arrowcaught in the sack representsAndruil&rsquo;spurpose.If the arrow was meant to pierce the sack to tear a hole,open the floodgates, andallow 'grain&rsquo; to flow out, then being stuck in the sack shows that something went wrong and Andruil&rsquo;s purpose was thwarted. Grain and arrows also comes up in the writing from theToMbelow, in whichAndruilgathers 'grains of light&rsquo; into an arrow that seems meant for some terrible use in the future.  


 Cole also says the following, which might tie into her purpose. 

&ldquo;She hurts, but helping hurts more. She sees the strings that pull me, eyes like raisins in a stale cookie.&rdquo; 

Also how would Sera see the strings that pull Cole? I don&rsquo;t think she is hiding being a mage, but the part of Sera that is Andruil may be capable of it.





Sera seems to be resisting Andruil&rsquo;swispandgets vague feelings/messages/power from it that she doesn&rsquo;t quite understand and possibly even tries to suppress (whispers, dreams, natural ability with the bow,etc). Look at Sera&rsquo;s involvement with the Friends ofRed Jenny,Andruil&rsquo;sWay of the Three Trees (WotTT: be swift and silent, bend but don&rsquo;t break, be stronger together/mindful of the gifts of the hunt), and the story ofAndruilandGhilan'nain. 


TheWotTTmight as well be the motto of the Friends of Red Jenny and the story ofGhilan'nainis a perfect illustration of how the Friends of Red Jenny actually operates. 



&ldquo;AndGhilan'nainprayed to the gods for help. Ghilan'nain(a Friend) calls toAndruil(Red Jenny) for help, who sends hares (the Little People), thatchew through her bonds to set her free (Little People acting within their limited power to help the Friends of Red Jenny as a whole), which eventually leads to the target being brought to justice in some way (the Friends will harass nobles, steal, upset plans, even kill those they think deserve it). If Sera felt echoes from Andruil&rsquo;s wisp inside of her she might&rsquo;ve felt compelled to act in certain ways, and joining the Red Jennies is like following a skewed version of Andruil&rsquo;s WotTT. 

 Now let&rsquo;s see how Sera&rsquo;s tarot card, the &ldquo;grains of light&rdquo; text, andAndruil&rsquo;svallaslincome crashing together with surprising clarity.

Now compare Sera&rsquo;s tarot card to thetext from theToM: whole stars appear at the top of the card and streaks of light clearly center on a glowing arrow with a pale golden shaft. 
The stars, grains of light, glowing spear (a projectile, just like an arrow), andAndruil&rsquo;svallaslinare so clearly depicted in Sera&rsquo;s very deliberate pose on her tarot card that it&rsquo;s hard to believe this was anything but intentional.
