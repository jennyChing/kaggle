      A Local Woman Discovers a Weird Red Blob That Washed Up on The Beach She Wrapped It Up And Took It Home

        <div class="slide_content">
         <p>
          Viewing 1 of 5
         </p>
         <h2 class="Heading4 title">
         </h2>
         <p>
          A weird  aquatic creature was deposited by the tide on the shore at  Mudjimba Beach on the Sunshine Coast. The creature was sited on the sand on Monday morning by Debbie Higgs, who was taking a walk on the beach. The bizarre, bright red creature has ribbing, no limbs, eyes or mouth, and has what seems like frills around it.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="oneByOne_item">
        <div class="slide_img">
         <img alt="" src="http://www.todayoutlook.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/2192.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="slide_content">
         <p>
          Viewing 2 of 5
         </p>
         <h2 class="Heading4 title">
         </h2>
         <p>
          'It's surely not a plant, it appears porous like a jelly fish, Ms Higgs told Daily Mail Australia, 'and it is foul-smelling.' 'I knew nothing about it so I clicked a picture of it and  tweeted it,' she said 'Tons of people retweeted and responded speculating what it was.'
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="oneByOne_item">
        <div class="slide_img">
         <img alt="" src="http://www.todayoutlook.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/3173.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="slide_content">
         <p>
          Viewing 3 of 5
         </p>
         <h2 class="Heading4 title">
         </h2>
         <p>
          After spotting the 'blob' Ms Higgs clicked it and walked off the beach, but after receiving heaps of tweets she came back for it. 'I used a  tea towel to pick the 25 cm long creature, and took it home. I let it float in a bucket of salty water.'
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="oneByOne_item">
        <div class="slide_img">
         <img alt="" src="http://www.todayoutlook.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/4165.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="slide_content">
         <p>
          Viewing 4 of 5
         </p>
         <h2 class="Heading4 title">
         </h2>
         <p>
          She wanted to know what it was so she kept on  posting images of the weird sea creature on social media. 'I've never come across anything like it and a walk on the beach is my daily routine. People tweeted that it is probably a Spanish Dancer Slug,' Ms Higgs said. Spanish Dancer slugs are usually found in the warmer tropical waters of the Indian Ocean.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="oneByOne_item">
        <div class="slide_img">
         <img alt="" src="http://www.todayoutlook.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/5159.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="slide_content">
         <p>
          Viewing 5 of 5
         </p>
         <h2 class="Heading4 title">
         </h2>
         <p>
          Ms Higgs' Twitter feed has been overwhelmed with clues about what the bright red creature is. 
People  retweeted and tagged  experts --- while some made it into a comedy saying it is 'The red Teletubby' or 'whale placenta' and one person brazenly likened it to Prime Minister Abbott in budgie smugglers. As per observations of Queensland Museum it's most likely a sea slug. The CSIRO has till now not been able to identify the mysterious creature.
         </p>
        </div>
