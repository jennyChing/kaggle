          <div class="hg-read-more-container" id="hg-read-more-container-29781">
           <p>
            Last year I shared some great ideas for
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <a href="http://www.homegoods.com/blog/2013/09/05/back-to-school-lunch-ideas-for-you-and-your-kids/">
              creative back to school lunches
             </a>
            </span>
            and I thought it might be a good idea to give you some fresh inspiration for this year. Here are a few tips to help you organize your childrens school lunches:
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             1. Create a lunch list.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Lets start with the
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <a href="http://www.mayoclinic.org/healthy-living/childrens-health/in-depth/nutrition-for-kids/art-20049335">
              Mayo Clinics Guidelines
             </a>
            </span>
            for a healthy dietfor kids. According to these experts, children need these nutrient-dense foods:
            <br/>
            <strong>
             -Protein:
            </strong>
            Seafood, lean meat and poultry, eggs, beans, peas, soy products and unsalted nuts and seeds.
            <br/>
            <strong>
             -Fruits:
            </strong>
            A variety of fresh, canned or dried fruits.
            <br/>
            <strong>
             -Vegetables:
            </strong>
            Especially dark green, red and orange vegetables, beans and peas.
            <br/>
            <strong>
             -Grains:
            </strong>
            Choose whole grains, such as whole-wheat bread, oatmeal, popcorn, quinoa or brown or wild rice.
            <br/>
            <strong>
             -Dairy:
            </strong>
            Fat-free or low-fat dairy products, such as milk, yogurt, cheese or fortified soy beverages.
           </p>
           <p>
            So, how might thislook like in a kids lunchbox? Well, it couldlook like
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <a href="http://www.chefshayna.com/journal/2014/8/22/back-to-school-part-2-lunches">
              Chef Shayna
             </a>
             s
            </span>
            creation. She boughtthe little red box at HomeGoods.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/lunchbox.jpg">
             <img alt="lunchbox" class="alignnone wp-image-30325 size-medium" height="490" src="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/lunchbox-490x490.jpg" width="490"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Or maybe like this deli boxby MOMables
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/laura-fuentes/healthy-school-lunches_b_2474389.html?utm_hp_ref=fb&amp;src=sp&amp;comm_ref=false">
              Laura Fuentes
             </a>
            </span>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/2013-01-14-delibox.jpg">
             <img alt="2013-01-14-delibox" class="alignnone wp-image-29785 size-medium" height="347" src="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/2013-01-14-delibox-490x347.jpg" width="490"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            But lets face it, lunches dont make themselves. So after youve shopped, how can you get organized to put together fivegreat lunches for the week?
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             2. Create BIG lunch boxes for your supplies.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Jen at
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <a href="http://iheartorganizing.blogspot.com/2013/08/back-to-school-organization.html">
              I Heart Organizing
             </a>
            </span>
            keeps a separate bin in the fridge for refrigerated lunch foods  this makes it easy to keep track of what you have on hand (and what you need to buy) and also makes it easy to grab what you need when you are pulling a lunch together.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Back_To_School_Organization_10.jpg">
             <img alt="Back_To_School_Organization_10" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-30333" height="326" src="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Back_To_School_Organization_10-490x326.jpg" width="490"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            And she keeps one for dry lunch snacks too!
           </p>
           <p>
            By organizing your lunch contents in the BIG lunch boxes once a week, it will be easy for the kids to help put their own lunch boxes or bags together the night before. Just pull out the big lunch bins and let them choose ONE item from each food category.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             3. Keep your lunch bags and containers all in one place.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Its easier to pack lunches if you have all your tools in one place: lunch bags, plasticbaggies, plastic containers, utensils, napkins, etc. Find a drawer or cupboard that can hold them all in an organized way so its easy to grab what you need.
           </p>
           <p>
            DaNitafrom
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             <a href="http://blog.delightfulorder.com/2010/10/peak-inside-my-pantry.html">
              Delightful Order
             </a>
            </span>
            has each boys lunch bag hung on a hook in the pantry with all of her other lunch stuff in bins nearby.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/100_9569.jpg">
             <img alt="100_9569" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-29790" height="640" src="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/100_9569.jpg" width="480"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            So take your shopping list, organize what you buy into easy-to-grab portions (enough to last the week), then grab what you need each night to pack it up and ship it out the next morning!
           </p>
