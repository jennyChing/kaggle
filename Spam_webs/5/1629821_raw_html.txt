        <img alt="Ecwid vs WooCommerce: Job Interview" height="381" src="http://www.shopping-cart-migration.com/images/articles/August2014/ecwid-vs-woocommerce-job-interview.jpg" title="Ecwid vs WooCommerce: Job Interview" width="650"/>
        <p align="justify">
         Starting an online store to some people may seem an affair not only expensive, but also very time-and effort consuming. They might imagine an installation that takes no less than a rocket scientist to accomplish, heaps of complicated settings, months of product uploading and so on. Dreadful In fact, the era of e-Commerce for the chosen ones is over. Now, starting an online store can take literally minutes, is free and the level of difficulty hardly exceeds that of using a text editor. There are shopping cart platforms that are both rich featured and easy to use, like
         <a href="http://www.shopping-cart-migration.com/supported-carts/9491-ecwid" title="Ecwid">
          Ecwid
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.shopping-cart-migration.com/supported-carts/4418-woocommerce" title="WooCommerce">
          WooCommerce
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         The idea to start your own business online just struck you and you cant wait to put it to life? Thats easy!  Lets take a closer look at the resumes, compare
         <a href="http://www.shopping-cart-migration.com/shopping-cart-migration-options/24809-ecwid-to-woocommerce-migration" title="Ecwid to WooCommerce">
          Ecwid to WooCommerce
         </a>
         and find out whos the best candidate for the job.
        </p>
        <center>
         <h2>
          Ecwid Resume
         </h2>
        </center>
        <img align="right" alt="Ecwid vs WooCommerce: Job Interview" height="140" src="http://www.shopping-cart-migration.com/images/articles/August2014/ecwid-200.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #ccc; margin: 5px 0px 5px 5px;" title="Ecwid" width="140"/>
        <p>
         <b>
          Name:
         </b>
         Ecwid
         <br/>
         <b>
          Birth date:
         </b>
         2009
         <br/>
         <b>
          Type of platform:
         </b>
         hosted, SaaS
         <br/>
         <b>
          Pricing model:
         </b>
         Freemium (theres one free plan available, the rest are paid on a monthly basis)
         <br/>
         <b>
          Job experience:
         </b>
         Ecwid is integrated with any website via API, major settings and product uploading is done in your account. The store can then be mirrored in Facebook or the other website.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Installation
         </b>
         <br/>
         You need to register an account on Ecwid website, then pick the suitable plan - youll get an HTML code string to insert into your existing site  and youre good to go. Ecwid will be tied to your site, so you can go ahead and start tuning it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Management
         </b>
         <br/>
         Similarly to other hosted carts, Ecwid target audience are non-technical users, who dont like to bother much about techie stuff and spend weeks mastering the software. For a regular Internet user it will take a few hours of playing around with the settings to get the hang of major operations.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Features
         </b>
         <br/>
         The platform in general provides multiple functions to make a store work like a clock. It allows to sell different types of products, including variable products, cross-sell, etc. It also comes integrated with a number of payment methods, like PayPal, Stripe, Sage, check etc. As to shipping, you can either establish a flat rate table or get real time fees from UPS, USPS and FedEx. Theres no one-step checkout, but the process is very clear and thanks to breadcrumbs, you always know how many steps are left.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Customization
         </b>
         <br/>
         Theres no much space for customization since developers can make changes only through API instead of having access to the source code. You can change themes though, and with CSS knowledge- add the necessary touches to the design of your store.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          SEO
         </b>
         <br/>
         Ecwid uses the Google's AJAX indexing specification supported by the most popular search engines: Google, Yahoo, Bing, Yandex for indexing. If merchant uses ready plugins for Wordpress, no additional measures are needed - if  a paid Ecwid account, your store products will automatically get to search results. Moreover, on each product page, the product information is structured using the special SEO-friendly format recommended by Google (so-called microdata), which helps search engines to produce rich snippets for your product pages in the search results.
        </p>
        <center>
         <h2>
          WooCommerce Resume
         </h2>
        </center>
        <img align="right" alt="Ecwid vs WooCommerce: Job Interview" height="140" src="http://www.shopping-cart-migration.com/images/articles/August2014/woocommerce-200.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #ccc; margin: 5px 0px 5px 5px;" title="WooCommerce" width="140"/>
        <p>
         <b>
          Name:
         </b>
         WooCommerce
         <br/>
         <b>
          Birth date:
         </b>
         2011
         <br/>
         <b>
          Type of platform:
         </b>
         open-source plugin for WordPress
         <br/>
         <b>
          Pricing model:
         </b>
         Free
         <br/>
         <b>
          Job experience:
         </b>
         WooCommerce only works with WordPress
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Installation
         </b>
         <br/>
         If you already have got  WordPress site running, it will be a piece of cake to install WooCommerce- just download it from the official repository and install in a few clicks.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Management
         </b>
         <br/>
         WooCommerce interface very much resembles the look and feel of WP, so for users with hands-on experience with the latter mastering WooCommerce will be a breeze. Even if you just start getting familiar with WordPress and its most popular ecommerce plugin, it wont take much time for you to learn. Ease of use has always been one of its major strengths.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Features
         </b>
         <br/>
         By default, WooCommerce is enough to plunge into online sales with no interruptions  its got powerful reporting system, convenient inventory management, ready to work with all types of products, presents a number of marketing tools like coupons and has integration with major shipping (UPS, USPS, FedEX) and payment methods (PayPal, BACS, and Cash on Delivery). If thats not enough  youre welcome to get the needed extensions at the official repository  there are hundreds of them, free and paid, to any taste.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Customization
         </b>
         <br/>
         As every open-source software, WooCommerce can be modified and tweaked to the extend you want it to be, the only limit is your skills. Similarly, you can change the design via changing the code or just getting one of thousands themes available.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          SEO
         </b>
         <br/>
         WooCommerce, like WP itself, works great with SEO  it provides every opportunity for you to make your webshop loved by search engines. Apart from the out of the box functionality, you can get more sophisticated SEO features via the available robust extensions.
        </p>
        <h2>
         So, who gets the job?
        </h2>
        <p>
         Its actually for you to decide, based on the above resumes. If youre a WordPress user or wouldnt like to pay for the ecommerce software, WooCommerce might be the better variant. In case you want something simple for your site, are ok with the monthly fees and dont plan on many customization, Ecwid would suit you well. The final word is yours.
        </p>
        <p>
         <i>
          P.S. In case you already run your store and would like to
          <a href="http://www.shopping-cart-migration.com/supported-carts/9491-ecwid" title="migrate from Ecwid">
           migrate from Ecwid
          </a>
          to WooCommerce, Cart2Cart will do it in a few clicks on your side, easily and accurately with no coding skills needed.
         </i>
        </p>
