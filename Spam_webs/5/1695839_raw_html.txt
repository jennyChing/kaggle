         <p>
          The vast majority of industry professionals in Connecticut see the current commercial real estate market as fair to poor, but a key index shows some modest improvements over the past nine months, according to a survey released Tuesday.
         </p>
         <p>
          Fifty-two percent of survey respondents called commercial real-estate conditions fair, while 32 percent branded them poor. Only 16 percent said conditions were good or excellent.
         </p>
         <p>
          "The latest data reflects a commercial real estate market that is still facing challenges in the form of cautious consumer spending and higher energy and oil prices," according to the report issued by the Connecticut Business &amp; Industry Association, the nonprofit Connecticut Economic Resource Center and research firm DataCore Partners. "Moreover, area retailers are facing rising competitive pressures from Internet retailers with lower overhead costs."
         </p>
         <p>
          The third quarterly statewide survey said Connecticut commercial real estate "has struggled in many areas due to sluggish job expansion and meager income growth."
         </p>
         <p>
          Still, the Farmington Bank/O'Connor Davies Munns &amp; Dobbins Commercial Real Estate Index, which tracks market conditions, has shown an upward trend in the past two quarters, according to the report. And future expectations have increased during the past six months as well.
         </p>
         <p>
          "The consensus is that economic recovery is apt to strengthen during 2011," said John Patrick, president and chief executive of Farmington Bank.
         </p>
         <p>
          The statewide report, which includes data from New London County, also noted that Connecticut's economy is on the mend, having added 30,000 jobs since its employment ebb last year.
