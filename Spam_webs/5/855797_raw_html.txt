        <p>
         Youve added a cohost to your podcast, and you want to record in front of a live Internet audience. Weve previously talked about
         <a href="http://theaudacitytopodcast.com/tap036-3-ways-to-record-multiple-onsite-podcast-hosts/" title="TAP036: 3 Ways to Record Multiple Onsite Podcast Hosts">
          recording multiple onsite cohosts
         </a>
         . But if your cohost is offsite, there are ways you can record and live-stream with just a few extra cables, or do it easiest with a mixer and two computers.
        </p>
        <p>
         Before continue, make sure youve heard the previous episodes in our live-streaming series.
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          <a href="http://theaudacitytopodcast.com/tap042-how-to-podcast-live-five-reasons-four-cautions/" title="TAP042: How to Podcast Live: 5 Reasons, 4 Cautions">
           5 Reasons, 4 Cautions
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://theaudacitytopodcast.com/tap043-how-to-live-stream-yourself-while-podcasting/" title="TAP043: How to Live-Stream Yourself While Podcasting">
           How to Live-Stream Yourself While Podcasting
          </a>
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         As I write and speak, Ill use the term PC in reference to both of the major personal computers (PCs): OS X and Windows. These same principles will also apply to Linux PCs. As youll notice, we cover many of the same things as when youre podcasting alone, but adding cohosts, especially through Skype, can complicate your setup.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thanks to Dr. Vibe from
         <a href="http://thevibeandvegasshow.wordpress.com/">
          The Vibe in Vegas Show
         </a>
         for his voicemail asking how to do this same thing!
        </p>
        <h2>
         Record and live-streaming from the same PC
        </h2>
        <p>
         If youre already recording into your computer, then its no extra step to broadcast that to the Internet through live-streaming.
        </p>
        <h2>
         One PC
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/one_pc1.png">
          <img alt="Live-streaming with one PC" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2979" height="367" src="//theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/one_pc1-600x367.png" title="Live-streaming with one PC" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Connect your mic to a splitter.
          <ol>
           <li>
            First line goes to your PCs first input for Skype.
           </li>
           <li>
            Second line heads toward PC.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </li>
         <li>
          Split that second line.
          <ol>
           <li>
            First line accepts the Skype output.
           </li>
           <li>
            Second line continues toward PC.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </li>
         <li>
          Split the line again.
          <ol>
           <li>
            First line connects headphones.
           </li>
           <li>
            Second line continues to the PC.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect the final output connects to PCs second input (or USB) for recording/streaming.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h2>
         Sounds and one PC
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/sounds-one_pc1.png">
          <img alt="Live-streaming with sounds and one PC" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2980" height="338" src="//theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/sounds-one_pc1-600x338.png" title="Live-streaming with sounds and one PC" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Connect your mic to a splitter.
          <ol>
           <li>
            First line accepts sounds from your PCs second output (or USB)
           </li>
           <li>
            Second line heads toward PC.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </li>
         <li>
          Split the line again.
          <ol>
           <li>
            First line connects to PCs first input for Skype.
           </li>
           <li>
            Second line continues toward PC.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </li>
         <li>
          Split the line again.
          <ol>
           <li>
            First line accepts Skype from your PCs first output.
           </li>
           <li>
            Second line continues toward PC.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </li>
         <li>
          Split the line again.
          <ol>
           <li>
            First line connects to headphones.
           </li>
           <li>
            Second line continues to PC.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect the final output connects to PCs second input (or USB) for recording/streaming.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h2>
         Mixer, sounds, and one PC
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/mixer-sounds-one_pc1.png">
          <img alt="Live-streaming with a mixer, sounds, and one PC" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2978" height="373" src="//theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/mixer-sounds-one_pc1-600x373.png" title="Live-streaming with a mixer, sounds, and one PC" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Connect your mic to channel 1 of mixer.
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect PCs first output to channel 2 of mixer for Skype.
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect PCs second output (or USB) to channel 3/4 of mixer for sounds.
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect headphones to the mixers headphones jack.
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect Aux Out (may also be called Aux Sends, FX Out, or FX Sends) back to PCs first input in mix-minus setup (see below).
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect Main Out or Tape Out to PCs second input (or USB) for recording/streaming.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h2>
         Mixer, sounds, and two PCs
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/mixer-sounds-two_pcs1.png">
          <img alt="Live-streaming with a mixer, sounds, and two PCs" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2983" height="349" src="//theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/mixer-sounds-two_pcs1-600x349.png" title="Live-streaming with a mixer, sounds, and two PCs" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Connect your mic to channel 1 of mixer.
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect Skype PCs output to channel 2 of mixer for Skype.
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect recording/streaming PCs output to channel 3/4 of mixer for sounds.
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect headphones to the mixers headphones jack.
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect Aux Out (may also be called Aux Sends, FX Out, or FX Sends) back to Skype PCs input in mix-minus setup (see below).
         </li>
         <li>
          Connect Main Out or Tape Out to recording/streaming PCs input.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h2>
         Overview of Skype mixer mix-minus
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/mixer_mix_minus.png">
          <img alt="Skype Mixer Mix Minus" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2972" height="552" src="//theaudacitytopodcast.com/files/2011/08/mixer_mix_minus-600x552.png" title="Skype Mixer Mix Minus" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Connect equipment as described above and illustrated here.
         </li>
         <li>
          Adjust the FX (or Aux) to 0dB (12 oclock position) on the mics channel (1)and the sounds channel (5/6 in this illustration).
         </li>
         <li>
          Adjust the FX (or Aux) to fully counterclockwise on the Skype channel (3/4 in this illustration).
         </li>
         <li>
          Use everything else as you normally would.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h2>
         How I made these illustrations
        </h2>
        <p>
         After realizing how complicated my oral explanation was becoming, I decided to illustrate each of these setups. Rather than load up Adobe Illustrator, I wanted a simpler solution that could turn out results quickly.
        </p>
        <p>
         I found
         <a href="http://bit.ly/oXcCo8">
          LucidChart
         </a>
         as a free (with paid options) online flowchart-making tool.
        </p>
