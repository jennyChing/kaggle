       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         38+ Freelancing Success Tips (What You Really Need to Do as a Freelancer)
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-03-21T00:39:39+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          March 21, 2013
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://freelancefolder.com/author/laura/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Laura Spencer
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/cptspock/2087480739/">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft frane" height="404" src="http://freelancefolder.com/wp-content/uploads/freelancing-success-tips.jpg" title="freelancing-success-tips" width="270"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Are you just starting out as a freelancer? Have you been freelancing for a while, but now youre stuck?
        </p>
        <p>
         Either way, reviewing this post will help. In it, I share over 38 freelancing tips in our biggest list of freelancing success tips ever.
        </p>
        <p>
         Freelancing can be hard.
         <a href="http://freelancefolder.com/why-freelancing-is-hard/">
          Really hard
         </a>
         . Youll get discouraged. You may even be tempted to quit.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are challenges to be met. There are problems to solve. And its hard to remember everything.
        </p>
        <p>
         This post can help you stay on track. It works great as a quick reminder for the experienced freelancer or as a checklist for a new freelancer.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you liked this post, you will probably also like
         <a href="http://freelancefolder.com/seven-days-of-freelancing-tips/">
          Seven Days of Freelancing Tips
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-22042">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Success Tips for Freelancers
        </h3>
        <p>
         For the first time, Im sharing this giant list of over 38 freelancing success tips.
        </p>
        <p>
         (Ive arranged them in the order that makes the most sense to me, but feel free to apply them as needed.)
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are the tips:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Dont stop marketing.
          </strong>
          Marketing is the key to a steady flow of new and repeat clients. Dont stop, even when you are busy.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Get social.
          </strong>
          Social media and other online communities are a great way to get your freelancing business in front of prospective clients.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Establish an effective website.
          </strong>
          Having a freelancing website is almost a given these days. Make sure yours conveys your message.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Blog to establish expertise.
          </strong>
          Blogging is a great way to show that you really are knowledgeable about what you do.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Know whats unique about your business.
          </strong>
          Base your marketing on how your freelancing business is better than similar businesses.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Keep your portfolio up to date.
          </strong>
          Remember to review and periodically update your portfolio so that your best work is always showcased.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Dont be afraid to cold call.
          </strong>
          You dont have to wait for clients to come to you. Its okay to reach out to prospective clients.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Research potential clients.
          </strong>
          Dont enter a client agreement without knowing something about the company you will be doing business with.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Listen carefully and ask questions.
          </strong>
          Dont jump to any conclusions about what you think your client wants.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Be polite.
          </strong>
          We live in a rude society, but that doesnt make it right. Being courteous will make you stand out in a good way.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Negotiate.
          </strong>
          Dont just accept whatever terms a prospect suggests. Nearly all aspects of a freelancing agreement can be negotiated.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Define scope.
          </strong>
          Make sure that you understand exactly what the client wants. Be as specific as possible.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Price fairly.
          </strong>
          Dont base your freelancing business on being able to provide low cost services, but rather on the value you add for the client.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Dont be afraid to say no.
          </strong>
          Not every freelancing opportunity will be right for you. Dont be afraid to turn work down.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Get it in writing.
          </strong>
          A contract or freelancing agreement is a must-have. Dont start work without one.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Minimize distractions.
          </strong>
          Make sure that your work environment is conducive to maximum productivity.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Set a routine.
          </strong>
          While you dont have to keep traditional hours, most freelancers are more productive if they schedule regular working hours.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Keep your office neat.
          </strong>
          A messy office can slow you down and lower your morale.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Eat right.
          </strong>
          The last thing most freelancers want is to lose work time due to illness.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Get enough exercise.
          </strong>
          Youll feel better and work more efficiently too.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Take regular breaks.
          </strong>
          It goes against common sense, but working too long can actually reduce your productivity.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Meet or exceed expectations.
          </strong>
          Dont disappoint your clients. Deliver on time and make sure that you deliver what the client wanted.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Invoice promptly.
          </strong>
          Once youve delivered work, its time to send an invoice. If you wait, you or the client may forget.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Say thank-you.
          </strong>
          Again, people who say thank-you are rare. If you want to stand out in a good way, say thanks.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Follow up.
          </strong>
          Too many freelancers are afraid to check back with existing clients, but follow up is an important part of the process.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Set measurable goals.
          </strong>
          Without goals, there is no way to measure how well your business is doing.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Have a plan.
          </strong>
          Once youve set goals, devise a realistic plan to achieve those goals (and revisit it often).
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Delegate low-level tasks or specialized tasks.
          </strong>
          Just because you freelance doesnt mean you have to do everything yourself.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Keep learning.
          </strong>
          Keep your skills and knowledge current. Regular learning is not optional for freelancers.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Invest in your business.
          </strong>
          In the end, it is worthwhile to spend money on a high quality machine, pay for software updates, and high-speed Internet.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Use the right tools.
          </strong>
          While some freeware and shareware software is quite useful, there are times when you will need to invest in the right tool to get the job done.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Build a support group.
          </strong>
          Freelancers usually work alone, so its up to you to maintain and grow your friendships.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Get testimonials.
          </strong>
          An endorsement from a past or current client is a powerful selling tool.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Create products.
          </strong>
          You can create an additional income stream by developing and selling products such as eBooks, WordPress themes, and training modules.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Stay positive.
          </strong>
          A good attitude is a great asset for the freelancer.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Believe in yourself.
          </strong>
          If you dont think you are the right person for the job, why would you expect a client to believe that you are?
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Try new things.
          </strong>
          Dont be afraid to experiment.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Monitor your online reputation.
          </strong>
          What you say and what is said about you online is important.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Bonus Tip: Prepare for the future.
          </strong>
          Save money for those famine periods and have a strategy for retirement.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h3>
         Your Turn
        </h3>
        <p>
         What tips would you add to the list?
        </p>
        <p>
         Share your tips in the comments.
        </p>
