       <h1 class="headline">
        A Matter of Magic by Patricia C. Wrede
       </h1>
       <p class="byline">
        <span class="post_author_intro">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="post_author">
         Allison (AllureofBooks)
        </span>
        <span class="post_date_intro">
         on
        </span>
        <span class="post_date" title="2012-06-29">
         June 29, 2012
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="post_content">
       <p>
        <strong>
         A Matter of Magic
        </strong>
        by
        <strong>
         Patricia C. Wrede
        </strong>
        is actually a duology; it contains
        <a href="http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/245727.Mairelon_the_Magician">
         Mairelon the Magician
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/169885.Magician_s_Ward">
         Magicians Ward
        </a>
        . It has been on my radar for quite awhile now because I love the historical fantasy genre. Since Im currently trying to get some reading done on my
        <a href="http://theallureofbooks.com/project-fill-in-the-gaps" title="Project Fill in the Gaps">
         Fill in the Gaps challenge
        </a>
        , I decided to finally pick it up!
       </p>
       <p>
        You guys, if I had known how much I was going to love
        <strong>
         A Matter of Magic
        </strong>
        , Id have picked it up ages ago! I had so much fun while reading it! Other than
        <a href="http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/64207.Sorcery_and_Cecelia_or_The_Enchanted_Chocolate_Pot">
         Sorcery &amp; Celia
        </a>
        , I havent read anything else by
        <strong>
         Patricia C. Wrede
        </strong>
        . If all her books are this enchanting, that will be changing very quickly. The characters and plot both held me riveted.
       </p>
       <p>
        Kim grew up on the streets, disguised as a boy for safety. When she is caught poking around where she doesnt belong, she ends up assistant to a magician. Mairelon ends up dragging her into a web of intrigue involving lost magical artifacts. Then, in the second half of
        <strong>
         A Matter of Magic
        </strong>
        , Kim is living as his ward when further intrigues develop.
       </p>
       <p>
        Hilarious, witty, captivatingI was left completely and utterly charmed. Some of the hijinx that Mairelon and Kim find themselves in had me shaking my head and snorting with laughter. The personalities of both left me withsimilarreactions even when the situations werent meant to be especially funny.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://theallureofbooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/A-Matter-of-Magic-by-Patricia-C.-Wrede.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2729" height="300" src="http://theallureofbooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/A-Matter-of-Magic-by-Patricia-C.-Wrede-200x300.jpg" title="A Matter of Magic by Patricia C. Wrede" width="200"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         A Matter of Magic
        </strong>
        reminded me why it is best not to get caught up in only reading current young adult fantasy. It lacks the qualities of books that are a little older. When all you read is too focused on romance and elaborate supernatural situations  you can end up missing out! (Not that this book doesnt have a cute romance sub-plotIm not hating on anything, I love modern YA fantasy too!) Diversity my friends, diversity.
       </p>
       <p>
        The simplicity of
        <strong>
         Patricia C. Wredes
        </strong>
        story is so beguiling, yall. I really cant recommend reading this enough!
       </p>
