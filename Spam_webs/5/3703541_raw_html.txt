   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Search for Osama bin Laden
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
     <span class="mw-redirectedfrom">

     <p>
      <span style="font-size: small;">
       <span id="coordinates">
        <a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">
         Coordinates
        </a>
        :
        <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion">
         <a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Search_for_Osama_bin_Laden&amp;params=18_N_66_E_type:waterbody">

           <span style="display:none">

            <span class="geo">

         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
      </span>
      <a href="/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden" title="Osama bin Laden">
       Osama bin Laden
      </a>
      , the founder of
      <a href="/wiki/Al-Qaeda" title="Al-Qaeda">
       al-Qaeda
      </a>
      , went into hiding following the start of the
      <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001present)">
       War in Afghanistan
      </a>
      in order to avoid capture by the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      and its allies for his role in the
      <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">
       September 11, 2001 attacks
      </a>
      , and having been on the
      <a href="/wiki/FBI_Ten_Most_Wanted_Fugitives" title="FBI Ten Most Wanted Fugitives">
       FBI Ten Most Wanted Fugitives
      </a>
      list since 1999.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      After evading capture at the
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tora_Bora" title="Battle of Tora Bora">
       Battle of Tora Bora
      </a>
      in December 2001, his whereabouts became unclear, and various rumours about his health, continued role in al-Qaeda, and location were circulated. Bin Laden also released several
      <a href="/wiki/Videos_and_audio_recordings_of_Osama_bin_Laden" title="Videos and audio recordings of Osama bin Laden">
       video and audio recordings
      </a>
      during this time.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the decade following his disappearance, there were many attempts made by the United States government to locate bin Laden. In December, 2009,
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">
       U.S. Army
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/General_(United_States)" title="General (United States)">
       General
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stanley_McChrystal" title="Stanley McChrystal">
       Stanley McChrystal
      </a>
      said that bin Laden would need to be "captured or killed" in order for the U.S. to "finally defeat al-Qaeda."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In August 2007, the U.S.
      <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">
       Central Intelligence Agency
      </a>
      located a
      <a href="/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden%27s_compound_in_Abbottabad" title="Osama bin Laden's compound in Abbottabad">
       compound in Abbottabad
      </a>
      in Pakistan and identified it as the likely location of bin Laden. On May 1, 2011,
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy_SEALs" title="United States Navy SEALs">
       United States Navy SEALs
      </a>
      of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Naval_Special_Warfare_Development_Group" title="United States Naval Special Warfare Development Group">
       Naval Special Warfare Development Group
      </a>
      (DevGru) carried out an assault on the compound on orders from
      <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">
       U.S. President
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">
       Barack Obama
      </a>
      . During a 40-minute raid,
      <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Osama_bin_Laden" title="Death of Osama bin Laden">
       bin Laden was killed
      </a>
      by one bullet above the left eye and another to the chest.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The SEALs overpowered the compound's remaining residents, killing several, and extracted bin Laden's body (which was subsequently
      <a href="/wiki/Burial_at_sea" title="Burial at sea">
       buried at sea
      </a>
      ) as well as computer
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hard_drive" title="Hard drive">
       hard drives
      </a>
      , documents, and other material. After bin Laden's identity was positively confirmed, President Obama announced the operation in a national address.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Bin_Laden.27s_life_between_2001_and_2011">
       Bin Laden's life between 2001 and 2011
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Search_for_Osama_bin_Laden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Bin Laden's life between 2001 and 2011">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote">
      Further information:
      <a href="/wiki/Bin_Laden_Issue_Station" title="Bin Laden Issue Station">
       Bin Laden Issue Station
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      New information of Bin Laden's location has been emerging since his death and the arrest of his wives. According to one of his wives,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      bin Laden was united with his family the first time after the 9/11 attacks in the second half of 2002 in
      <a href="/wiki/Peshawar" title="Peshawar">
       Peshawar
      </a>
      . After this, bin Laden took his family into rural mountain areas of northwest Pakistan (and very notably, not in the tribal belt where main US attention was focused.) First they stayed in the Shangla district in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swat_valley" title="Swat valley">
       Swat valley
      </a>
      , where they stayed in two houses for eight to nine months. In 2003 they moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Haripur,_Pakistan" title="Haripur, Pakistan">
       Haripur
      </a>
      , a small town close to Islamabad, where they stayed in a rented house for two years. In mid-2005, Bin Laden and his family moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Abbottabad" title="Abbottabad">
       Abbottabad
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Tracking">
       Tracking
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Search_for_Osama_bin_Laden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tracking">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      American intelligence officials discovered the whereabouts of Osama bin Laden by tracking one of his couriers. Information was collected from
      <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Bay" title="Guantnamo Bay">
       Guantnamo Bay
      </a>
      detainees, who gave intelligence officers the courier's
      <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">
       pseudonym
      </a>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Abu_Ahmed_al-Kuwaiti" title="Abu Ahmed al-Kuwaiti">
       Abu Ahmed al-Kuwaiti
      </a>
      , and said that he was a
      <i>
       protg
      </i>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Khalid_Sheikh_Mohammed" title="Khalid Sheikh Mohammed">
       Khalid Sheikh Mohammed
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Rumors_and_speculation_about_bin_Laden.27s_whereabouts_from_2001_until_2011">
       Rumors and speculation about bin Laden's whereabouts from 2001 until 2011
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Search_for_Osama_bin_Laden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Rumors and speculation about bin Laden's whereabouts from 2001 until 2011">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Bin Laden's location and state of health were a continuing topic of speculation since his disappearance from
      <a href="/wiki/Tora_Bora" title="Tora Bora">
       Tora Bora
      </a>
      . It has become clear that most of these rumors and speculation were not based on fact. First, rumors surfaced that bin Laden was killed or fatally injured during U.S. bombardments, most notably near
      <a href="/wiki/Tora_Bora" title="Tora Bora">
       Tora Bora
      </a>
      , or that he died of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_causes" title="Natural causes">
       natural causes
      </a>
      . According to
      <a href="/wiki/Gary_Berntsen" title="Gary Berntsen">
       Gary Berntsen
      </a>
      , in his 2005 book,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jawbreaker:_The_attack_on_bin_Laden_and_al-Qaeda" title="Jawbreaker: The attack on bin Laden and al-Qaeda">
        Jawbreaker
       </a>
      </i>
      , a number of al-Qaeda detainees later confirmed that bin Laden had escaped Tora Bora into
      <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">
       Pakistan
      </a>
      via an eastern route through snow-covered mountains in the area of
      <a href="/wiki/Parachinar" title="Parachinar">
       Parachinar
      </a>
      , Pakistan. The media reported that bin Laden suffered from a
      <a href="/wiki/Kidney" title="Kidney">
       kidney
      </a>
      disorder requiring him to have access to advanced medical facilities, possibly
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kidney_dialysis" title="Kidney dialysis">
       kidney dialysis
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/Ayman_al-Zawahiri" title="Ayman al-Zawahiri">
       Ayman al-Zawahiri
      </a>
      , al-Qaeda's second-in-command and a close bin Laden associate, is a
      <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">
       physician
      </a>
      and may have provided medical care to bin Laden.
     </p>
     <p>
      Between 2002 and 2011, the most common suggestion from U.S. national security officials and others was that "their best intelligence suggested that bin Laden was living along the mountainous, ungoverned border of
      <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">
       Pakistan
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">
       Afghanistan
      </a>
      ," such as in the
      <a href="/wiki/Federally_Administered_Tribal_Areas" title="Federally Administered Tribal Areas">
       Federally Administered Tribal Areas
      </a>
      (an area that includes
      <a href="/wiki/Waziristan" title="Waziristan">
       Waziristan
      </a>
      ) or volatile regions in
      <a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">
       North-West Frontier Province
      </a>
      (now known as Khyber Pakhtunkhwa), where an
      <a href="/wiki/War_in_North-West_Pakistan" title="War in North-West Pakistan">
       ongoing insurgency
      </a>
      has taken place. Several experts and former officials expressed surprise when bin Laden was instead revealed to have been hiding in the urban city of
      <a href="/wiki/Abbottabad" title="Abbottabad">
       Abbottabad
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Less common suggestions were that bin Laden had died (either by illness or military attack), or that he was alive and living in countries other than Pakistan, such as Afghanistan or
      <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">
       Iran
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2004">

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       February 27: Iranian news agency IRNA reported that bin Laden had been caught some time earlier in Pakistan. The news was spread by Asheq Hossein, director of the state-sponsored radio station, who mentioned two sources. The first source was a reporter of the Pakistani newspaper "The Nation," Shamim Shahed, who denied ever telling this to Hossein. The second source was "someone closely related to intelligence agencies and Afghan tribal elders." Both the Pentagon and a spokesperson of the Pakistani armed forces have denied the capture of bin Laden. Similar rumours have appeared from time to time since the start of U.S. military operations in Afghanistan but none have been confirmed.
      </li>
      <li>
