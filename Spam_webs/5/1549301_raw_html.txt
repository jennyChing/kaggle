     <h1>
      Fukushima Spews, Los Alamos Burns, Vermont Rages &amp; We Almost Lost Nebraska
     </h1>
     <div class="meta">
      By
      <a href="http://hawaiinewsdaily.com/author/harvey-wasserman/" rel="author" title="Posts by Harvey Wasserman">
       Harvey Wasserman
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="entry">
      <iframe allowtransparency="true" class="fbLikeContainer" frameborder="0" id="fbLikeIframe" name="fbLikeIframe" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2Fhawaiinewsdaily.com%2F2011%2F06%2Ffukushima-spews-los-alamos-burns-vermont-rages-we-almost-lost-nebraska%2F&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=true&amp;width=300&amp;height=25&amp;action=like&amp;font=arial&amp;colorscheme=light" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:300px; height:25px; display:inline;">
      </iframe>
      <p>
       Humankind is now threatened by the simultaneous implosion, explosion, incineration, courtroom contempt and drowning of its most lethal industry.
      </p>
      <p>
       We know only two things for certain:worse is yet to come, and those in charge are lying about itat least to the extent of what they actually know, which is nowhere near enough.
      </p>
      <p>
       Indeed, the assurances from the nuke power industry continue to flow like the floodwaters now swamping the Missouri Valley heartland.
      </p>
      <p>
       But major breakthroughs have come from a Pennsylvania Senator and New Yorks Governor on issues of evacuation and shut-down.And a public campaign for an end to loan guarantees could put an end to the US industry once and for all.
      </p>
      <p>
       FUKUSHIMA:The bad news continues to bleed from Japan with no end in sight.The light at the end of the tunnel is an out-of-control radioactive freight train, headed to the core of an endangered planet.
      </p>
      <p>
       Widespread internal radioactive contamination among Japanese citizens around Fukushima has now been
       <a href="http://www.tgdaily.com/general-science-brief/56884-fukushima-residents-have-radioactive-urine" title="Fukushima residents have radioactive urine">
        confirmed
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Two whales caught some 650 kilometers from the melting reactors have shown
       <a href="http://blog.alexanderhiggins.com/2011/06/15/japan-nuclear-radiation-whales-caught-650-km-fukushima-31-becquerels-kilogram-cesium-detected-27701/" title="According to Japanese news sources, Japanese whalers tested 6 of 17 whales captured 650 kilometers north-east of the Fukushima nuclear reactor. Of the 6 whales tested 2 were found to contain cesium radiation which must have come from the Fukushima nuclear reactor. ">
        intense radiation
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Plutonium, the deadliest substance known to our species,
       <a href="http://www3.nhk.or.jp/daily/english/05_21.html" title="Plutonium found outside Fukushima plant">
        has been found
       </a>
       dangerously far from the site.
      </p>
      <p>
       Tokyo Electric and the Japanese government have admitted to three 100% meltdowns but cant confirm with any reliability the current state of those cores.Theres reason to believe one or more have progressed to melt-throughs in which they burn through the thick stainless steel pressure vessel and onto the containment floor.
      </p>
      <p>
       The molten cores may be covered with water.But whether they can melt further through the containments and into the ground remains unclear.
      </p>
      <p>
       Possibilities may include a China Syndrome scenario in which one or more still-molten cores does melt through the containment and hits ground water.That could lead to a steam explosion that could blow still larger clouds of radioactive steam, water and debris into the atmosphere and ocean.
      </p>
      <p>
       At least three explosions have occurred, one of which may have involved criticality.
      </p>
      <p>
       There is no doubt at least two containments were breached very early in the disaster.Unit Four is cracked and sinking.The status of its used radioactive fuel pool, which has clearly caught fire, is uncertain.Also unclear is the ability of the owners to sustain the stability of Units Five and Six, which were shut when the quake/tsunami hit.
      </p>
      <p>
       That stability depends on continued power to run cooling systems, which could disappear amidst seismic aftershocks many believe are inevitable.A very substantial quake hit after the tremors that led to Indonesias devastating tsunami, and few doubt it could happen againsoonat Fukushima.
      </p>
      <p>
       All the above is dependent on reports controlled primarily by Tokyo Electric and the Japanese government.There is every reason to believe the situation is worse than it seems, and that those in charge dont really know the full of the extent of the damage or how to cope with it.
      </p>
      <p>
       Just five years ago a quake shut seven reactors at Kashiwazaki.The entire nation of Japan sits on a wide range of fault lines.Tsunami is a Japanese word
      </p>
      <p>
       Radiation from Fukushima has long since been detected throughout the northern hemisphere, with health effects that will be debated forever.
      </p>
      <p>
       Some fifty reactors still operate in Japan.According to some, the Japanese public has the legal right to shut them all.
      </p>
      <p>
       Let us pray they do.Yesterday.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        LOS ALAMOS:
       </strong>
       A massive wildfire has swept at least to the outskirts of the national laboratory that was at the core of the program that built the Atomic Bomb.
      </p>
      <p>
       The first explosion irradiated a nearby valley on July 16, 1945.Then came the two that obliterated Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
      </p>
      <p>
       There are significant quantities of stored radioactive material in and around Los Alamos.How much there is, where it is, how badly it is threatened, how much (if any) has already been engulfed in flames remains to be seen.Evacuations are underway.
      </p>
      <p>
       Official reassurances are not reliable.
      </p>
      <p>
       Nor are estimates of the potential for radioactive fallout to spread throughout North America and beyond.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        VERMONT YANKEE:
       </strong>
       Entergy, owner of the one reactor in Vermont, has sued to shred a solemn public contract.
      </p>
      <p>
       The one thing certain here is the companys contempt for the sanctity of its own word.
      </p>
      <p>
       Years ago Entergy sought official permits at VY.It promised in return that the state could choose to shut the reactor on March 21, 2012, which its now done.
      </p>
      <p>
       In recent years VY has spewed tritium into groundwater and the Connecticut River, in some cases from underground pipes whose existence the company denied.A cooling tower has collapsed.
      </p>
      <p>
       But the Nuclear Regulatory Commission has
       <a href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2011-06-16/nrc-sides-with-entergy-in-vermont-plant-debate-sanders-says-1-.html" title="NRC Sides With Entergy in Vermont Plant Debate, Sanders Says">
        extended the reactors license
       </a>
       and asked the federal Justice Department to intervene on behalf of the utility.
      </p>
      <p>
       The request trashes any credibility retained by the NRC.The Commission was established in the mid 1970s to be a disinterested party on which the public could rely.For it to now take a partisan stand on behalf of a reactor owner its bound to regulate thoroughly contaminates the core of its existence.
      </p>
      <p>
       Entergy has sued so it can buy some $65 million in radioactive fuel the people of Vermont do not want burned on their land.
      </p>
      <p>
       This will go to the US Supreme Court, where the future legal sanctity of any and all public contracts signed by any corporation, nuclear or otherwise, may be determined.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        NEBRASKA:
       </strong>
       The flooding Missouri River continues to threaten at leasttwo heartland reactors.
      </p>
      <p>
       Late reports indicate Cooper may still be running, with public assurances it could be shut very quickly.What might happen if the operators are a little bit late has not been explained.
      </p>
      <p>
       Nor is there much to go on about the impacts of flooded cores and fuel cooling ponds on the Missouri and Mississippi Rivers or the eco-systems along the way to a Gulf of Mexico still reeling from BPs toxic dose.
      </p>
      <p>
       But an almost surreal set of circumstances surrounds the true nature of design specifications and protections in place (or not) at
       <a href="http://www.counterpunch.com/giambrone06272011.html" title="Nuclear Catastrophe Imminent in Nebraska?">
        Fort Calhoun
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       They may be best summarized by what happened to a flood berm meant to protect Ft. Calhoun.This huge rubberized water-filled sausage was sixteen feet at the base and eight feet high.
      </p>
      <p>
       But CNNhas quoted a company representative as saying a some sort of equipment
       <a href="http://articles.cnn.com/2011-06-26/us/nebraska.flooding_1_nuclear-power-plant-omaha-public-power-district-berm" title="Flood berm bursts at Nebraska nuclear plant">
        came in contact
       </a>
        with the berm and punctured it.
      </p>
      <p>
       Not to worry:the same level of protection is in place as had been prior to the installation of the berm.
      </p>
      <p>
       In other words, the device was installed to protect the reactor.Then somebody punctured it.But things are as they were before so they must not have needed that berm in the first place.Got it?
      </p>
      <p>
       Its as yet unclear whether flood waters will continue to rise at these two reactors, whether the operators can protect them, and what will happen if they cant.
      </p>
      <p>
       The corporate media is carrying virtually zero coverage of any of the above stories.All are subject to rapid, dangerous changes about which we may have little reliable information.
      </p>
      <p>
       But we do know for sure that US Senator Robert Casey, Jr. (D-PA) now wants to see more deeply into one of the key holes in the nuclear faade:evacuation.
      </p>
      <p>
       After Three Mile Islands 1979 partial melt-down, new federal legislation allegedly gave states more power over how to get people out of the path of a melting nuke.
      </p>
      <p>
       But after an as-yet unopened Perry reactor was damaged by a 1986 earthquake, Ohios then-Governor Richard Celeste sued to keep Perry shut pending a state evacuation study.
      </p>
      <p>
       The NRC refused and won in federal court.Perry opened.Ohios official study then said evacuation was virtually impossible.
      </p>
      <p>
       A quarter-century later, Casey wants to see what it might now take to move downwinders out of harms way from a TMI, Perry, Chernobyl, Fukushima, Vermont Yankee, Cooper, Ft. Calhoun..you name it.
      </p>
      <p>
       Caseys being joined by New York Governor Andrew Cuomo, whose
       <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/06/29/nyregion/cuomo-emphasizes-aim-to-close-indian-point-plant.html" title="One of Gov. Andrew M. Cuomos top advisers met with the operators of the Indian Point nuclear plant last week and told them that the governor was determined to close the plant.">
        demands for the shut-down of Indian Point
       </a>
       , 35 miles north of Manhattan, have left its owners shaken.
      </p>
      <p>
       Cuomo and Casey might do well to join governors of states like Vermont, Massachusetts, California and others in testing the law on evacuation planning.Populations have vastly increased at virtually all US reactor sites since TMI.And the ugly realities that define the so-called Peaceful Atom are still making themselves all too apparent.
      </p>
      <p>
       Whether the US will now turn with Germany, Japan, Italy, Switzerland, Israel and others away from atomic power and toward a green-powered Earth is up to us.The Solartopian technologies of wind, solar, tidal, geothermal, ocean thermal, bio-fuels, increased efficiency and conservation are now demonstrably cheaper, safer, cleaner, more reliable, more job-producing and quicker to install than anything atomic energy can promise.
      </p>
      <p>
       A $36 billion loan guarantee give-away still mars the proposed 2012 federal budget.
       <a href="http://nukefree.org/bonnie-jackson-graham-we-may-be-brink" title="We may be on the brink of stopping the US nuclear industry from building new reactors.">
        Constant pressure on Congress and the White House
       </a>
       can kill that, and any other proposed funding for still more of these nightmares.
      </p>
      <p>
       The stream of reactor disasters spewing from this dying industry is certain to escalate.The toll rises with each leak at Fukushima, every flame at Los Alamos, each legal brief at Vermont Yankee, every foot of Nebraska floodwater.
      </p>
      <p>
       The need to stop the madness grows more desperate every day.
