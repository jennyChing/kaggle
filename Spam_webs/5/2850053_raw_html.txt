      <h2>
       Free Crochet Pattern: Loose Cowl
      </h2>
      By Rachel Choi 
      <a href="http://www.crochetspot.com/free-crochet-pattern-loose-cowl/#comments">
       64 Comments
      </a>
      <div class="entry">
       <table>
        <tr>
         <td>
          This cowl is meant to not choke you!  For some reason whenever I wear something around my neck it feels a little too tight, but finally here is something that isnt.  Its great for when the weather gets warmer or to add some flare to an outfit.  The cowl is made with a big crochet hook to work up fast and use a small amount of yarn.  Feel free to adjust the size of the cowl as you see fit!
         </td>
         <td width="200">
          <img alt="crochet cowl" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1579" height="169" src="http://www.crochetspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/loose-cowl.jpg" title="crochet cowl" width="200"/>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
       <table>
        <tr>
         <td>
          <strong>
           Skill Level:
          </strong>
         </td>
         <td width="130">
          <img alt="beginner crochet skill level" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-86" height="36" src="http://www.crochetspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/01-beginnersmall.jpg" title="beginner crochet skill level" width="125"/>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Finished Size
        </strong>
        : 8 (20 cm) tall, 19 (48 cm) around
        <br/>
        Note: to adjust size, follow directions within pattern
       </p>
       <table>
        <tr>
         <td>
          <strong>
           Materials
          </strong>
          :
          <br/>
          Medium Weight yarn (approximately 90 yards)
          <br/>
          Crochet Hook N (10.00 mm)
         </td>
         <td valgin="bottom" width="50">
          <img alt="crochet yarn size 4" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-84" height="45" src="http://www.crochetspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/4-mediumsmall.jpg" title="crochet yarn size 4" width="50"/>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Gauge
        </strong>
        : not really important
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Crochet Pattern: Cowl
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Start by chaining 35 or as long as you want your cowl.
        <br/>
        Row 1: ch 1, sc in second ch from hook and in each ch across: 35 sc
        <br/>
        Row 2: ch 2, turn, dc in each sc across: 35 dc
        <br/>
        Row 3: ch 1, turn, sc in each dc across: 35 sc
       </p>
       <p>
        Repeat rows 2  3, seven more times or until cowl is as tall as you desire.
       </p>
       <p>
        Slip stitch or sew the shorter sides of the cowl together and finish off.  You may also choose to use buttons as a closure instead of sewing ends together.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you need help with crocheting this cowl, let me know and I would love to help!
       </p>

Have fun with it!

           Hi Diana, a 10mm hook can be marked as either N or P depending on the company that makes it.  Thats what I dont like about the letters on the hooks, they arent always consistent.  Its best to always go by the mm measurement before looking at the letter.  If you dont have a bigger hook, you can always add more chains at the beginning of the pattern to adjust the size of your cowl.  Id recommend wrapping it around your neck to make sure it fits before you go further.
