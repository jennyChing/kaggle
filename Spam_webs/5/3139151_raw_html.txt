           <header>
            <h2 datatype="" property="dc:title">
             Natural Light: The Photographers Ally
            </h2>
           </header>
           <div class="content">
            <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
             <div class="field-items">
              <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
               <p>
                Black and white photographer Keith Moss talks to us about how he made the most of daylight during a fashion shoot.
               </p>
               <p>
                When Andrea, owner and designer of The Beehive Brand, approached Keith to shoot their inaugural fashion shoot, it was music to his ears.
               </p>
               <p>
                Many of us are familiar with fashion photography, be it in magazines, advertising or even online shopping. The enhanced colours, studio lighting and retouched images are not a new sight for any of us, but Keith decided to approach the project differently.
               </p>
               <p>
                I decided that I would only use natural light - no flash - to show the garments and tell the story of the brand, reveals Keith. This was a bold move, because when shooting fashion, the light is usually well and truly manufactured and the finished images are often retouched. I wanted none of that; NO retouching, just the glorious use of light and shadow to create emotion and drama.
               </p>
               <p>
                By 3am on the day of the shoot, the 16-strong crew were at a secluded location on the outskirts of Darlington probably known only to the 'Dawn Army' - the factory workers who used to make their way to work at dawn silently and en masse, by pedal power or on foot.
               </p>
               <p>
                The lighting at 3am may sound like a stark difference to the bright studio lights many fashion shoots take place under, but for Keith, that was part of the excitement: I love early morning light! Its beautiful; sometimes soft, sometimes harsh, but always beautiful, demanding and a real challenge.
               </p>
               <p>
                My objective was to show the knitwear in the best light possible showing its beauty and texture, positioning each model so the natural light falls perfectly on the garment and not the model.
               </p>
               <p>
                Despite the fact that the attitude to lighting may have been different to other shoots, one thing was the same: the pace. It is always fast moving, scampering from one part of the location to another, from one model to a group of models. But this time it involved following the light and shade of what natural light was available.
               </p>
               <p>
                I am a street photographer, finishes Keith. Natural light is my ally, my partner in recording life on the streets. Always exciting, always challenging, always fulfilling.
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
              </div>
