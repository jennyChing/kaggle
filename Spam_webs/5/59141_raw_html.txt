       <h2 class="entry-title">
        <a href="http://ltoventures.org/2014/05/nevada-autism-commission-5-year-plan-for-autism/" rel="bookmark">
         Nevada Autism Commission 5-Year Plan for Autism
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://ltoventures.org/2014/05/nevada-autism-commission-5-year-plan-for-autism/" rel="bookmark" title="10:07 AM">
         <span class="entry-date">
          May 20, 2014
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://ltoventures.org/author/marklolson/" title="View all posts by Mark L. Olson">
          Mark L. Olson
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         The
         <a href="http://dhhs.nv.gov/Autism_Commission.htm" target="_blank">
          Nevada Commission on Autism Spectrum Disorders
         </a>
         is developing a 5-year plan for addressing autism in the State, and has issued a Call for Subcommittee Participation [
         <a href="http://ltoventures.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/NV-Autism-Commission-5-Year-Plan-Marketing-Flier-051914.pdf" target="_blank">
          read/download flier
         </a>
         ]. The initiative was introduced at the May 2, 2014 Commission meeting [
         <a href="http://ltoventures.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/NV-Autism-Commission-Five-Year-Strategic-Plan-Development-Proposal-updated-050914.pdf" target="_blank">
          read/download proposal
         </a>
         ]. The new plan will build on the findings and recommendations of the
         <a href="http://dhhs.nv.gov/autism/TaskForce/2008/2008_NV_Autism_Task_Force_Report.pdf" target="_blank">
          2008 Autism Task Force
         </a>
         , with near-term and full-term goals. The Commission emphasized its desire to have self advocates and parent advocates lead the plan development effort.
        </p>
       </div>
