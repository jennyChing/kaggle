             <h1 class="entry-title">
              Spray-In Bedliner Costs  A National Survey of Line-X and Rhino Installers
             </h1>
             <ul class="entry-meta fix">
              <li class="entry-date">
               June 1, 2015
              </li>
             </ul>
            </div>
            <!--/pad-->
           </header>
           <div class="main fix sidebar-right">

             <article class="entry fix post-2288 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-truck-news" id="entry-2288">
              <div class="pad fix">
               <div class="text">
                <p>
                 Late last month, we hired a marketing company to help us determine just how much spray-in installers (both LINE-X and Rhino Linings) were charging for bedliners. The information we got back was so interesting that we decided to turn it into a handy graphic
                 <span id="more-2288">
                 </span>
                 (see below).
                </p>
                <p>
                 The big picture (as of August, 2014):
                </p>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  248 spray-in bedliner installers were contacted by phone and asked for their best price to install a spray-in liner in a
                  <a href="http://www.dualliner.com/shopping/dualliner-truck-bed-liners/ford/dualliner-bed-liner-2004-to-2014-ford-f-150-without-factory-tailgate-step-xlt-regular-6-foot-6-inch-bed.html">
                   2014 F150
                  </a>
                  (6.5 bed, no tailgate step)
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  The Line-X installers quoted an average of
                  <strong>
                   $481.77
                  </strong>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  The Rhino Linings installers quoted an average of
                  <strong>
                   $470.41
                  </strong>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  The graphic below shows how much spray-in installations cost (on average) in select cities, as well as across regions of the USA
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <p>
                 As youll see, spray-in bedliner installation costs vary greatly from region to region. Residents of Texas, for example, may pay hundreds of dollars less for a spray-in bedliner than residents in New York.
                </p>
                <p>
                 As far as DualLiner is concerned,
                 <b>
                  this data shows that we offer a better bedliner for a better price
                 </b>
                 . Unlike spray-in liners, the DualLiner can absorb dents, the ZeroSkid rubber floor on our liner keeps cargo solidly in place, and our price is always the same.
                </p>
                <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_2291" style="width: 930px">
                 <a href="http://www.dualliner.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/spray-in-cost-survey-graphic-FINAL-blue.png">
                  <img alt="Bedliner Cost Survey" class="size-full wp-image-2291" height="3277" src="http://www.dualliner.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/spray-in-cost-survey-graphic-FINAL-blue.png" width="920"/>
                 </a>
                 <p class="wp-caption-text">
                  A survey of 248 spray-in bedliner installers across the USA reveals information about pricing, installation, and wait times. Click for the full-size image.
                 </p>
                </div>
                <div class="features">

                   Youve seen the difference
                  </strong>
                  <br/>
                  <a class="button" href="/buy-now.html" style="margin-left: 20px; font-size: 20px;">
                   Buy Now
                  </a>
                 </h3>

                <hr/>
                <p>
                 <b>
                  Other Interesting Notes:
                 </b>
                </p>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  Installation times varied
                  <i>
                   greatly
                  </i>
                  , with some installers saying that they could complete a spray-in installation in less than 4 hours, and some saying they needed a full day.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  The average wait time between calling for an appointment and getting a spray-in installed was about 3.5 days, with 40% of installers having a wait of more than 4 days.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  The lowest quoted price of $335 came from a spray-in installer in Nome, Texas, and the highest quoted price of $667 came from an installer near Omaha, Nebraska
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Amazingly, almost 10% of the installers that were called (23 out of 248) did not answer their phones (despite three separate call attempts!)
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <h2>
                 The Key Takeaway
                </h2>
                <p>
                 Aside from providing information about spray-in bedliner installation costs across the US, this data underlines a theme that DualLiners customer service staff hear from our customers every day:
                 <b>
                  Spray-in bedliner installers are a mixed bag
                 </b>
                 . For every installer who does a good job at a fair price, theres an installer who demands $669, or an installer who rushes to install a spray-in bedliner in 3 hours.
                </p>
                <p>
                 So, if we cant convince everyone reading to buy a DualLiner, at the very least
                 <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                  we encourage everyone to screen spray-in bedliner installers very carefully
                 </span>
                 . With so much variance in pricing and installation times, there are bound to be installers taking shortcuts to make an extra buck.
                </p>
