       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Summer Squash Casserole
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-08-29T06:00:37+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          August 29, 2014
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/author/tekesha-geelgmail-com/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Tekesha
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/summer-squash-casserole/#comments">
           11 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1097 size-full" height="484" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-final-2-ribbet.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Remember when it used to be hell on earth to get a recipe out of someone? Everyones recipe was a secret and there was no way they could give it to you without some family member rolling in their grave. The first time I had summer squash was at an office potluck and it was served with a ton of onions and swimming in butter. Sooo good! The woman who brought it refused to say exactly how she made it. Thanks to the internet I later learned the secret family recipe was a totally standard recipe for yellow squash and made all over Florida. (Squash + Onion + Butter + Water = Boom. Good grief lady, THAT is what you were trying to keep under wraps?!) Since I learned the recipe, Ive made it hundreds of times and I always clean my plate. I was not even tempted to try it another way until I came across
         <a href="http://www.gourmetgirlcooks.com/2013/06/sundays-chicken-and-yellow-cheddar.html" target="_blank">
          Gourmet Girls Yellow Cheddar Squash Casserole
         </a>
         the other day and its the perfect twist.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1098 size-full" height="510" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-final-4.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The squash is sauted and then baked in a cheesy sauce and topped with an almond flour crumble. Once it has cooled a bit, you can slice it up and serve it with whatever protein you have on hand or even just a big salad. It keeps for a long time in the fridge or freezer so its perfect to bake at the beginning of the week and then slice up throughout the week as needed for a side dish. Ive adapted her incredible recipe to suit my tastes. Okay. Here we go:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          5 yellow squash
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 white onion
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tbsp olive oil
         </li>
         <li>
          2 sprigs of fresh thyme
         </li>
         <li>
          2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 cup parmesan cheese
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 cup heavy whipping cream
         </li>
         <li>
          1 egg
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tsp garlic powder
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tsp onion powder
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tsp cayenne pepper
         </li>
         <li>
          1 stick of butter, room temperature
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup of almond flour
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tsp paprika (optional)
         </li>
         <li>
          Salt and Pepper
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Preheat your oven to 350.
        </p>
        <p>
         Slice your squash and onion and set out your thyme.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1099 size-full" height="553" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-1.jpg" width="679"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Add the squash, onion, and thyme to a skillet with a little olive oil and salt and pepper. You do not need to remove the thyme leaves from the stem.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1100 size-full" height="680" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-2.jpg" width="655"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Cover and saut on medium heat for approximately 15 minutes, until the onion is translucent and the squash is tender, stirring frequently.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1101 size-full" height="510" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-3.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Remove the thyme stems from the skillet and drain off any water or oil still in the pan.
        </p>
        <p>
         In a small bowl, add almond flour and softened butter
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1102 size-full" height="531" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-4.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         combine with a fork or your hands until it turns into a paste.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1103 size-full" height="510" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-5.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         In a large bowl, combine cooked squash and onion mixture, cheddar cheese, mozzarella cheese, parmesan cheese, heavy whipping cream, egg, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper. Stir well to combine.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1104 size-full" height="648" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-6.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Pour into a baking dish.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1105 size-full" height="510" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-7.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Top with dollops of the almond paste and a sprinkle of paprika.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1106 size-full" height="510" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-8.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Bake for approximately 40 minutes, until the top is golden brown and bubbly.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1107 size-full" height="510" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-9.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Let it cool and then slice and serve.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1108 size-full" height="510" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-final-1.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I like to serve my casserole with baked BBQ chicken tenderloins.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Summer Squash Casserole" class="alignnone wp-image-1109 size-full" height="510" src="http://www.eatmorefoodproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/summer-squash-casserole-final-3.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
