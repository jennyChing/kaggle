          <section class="text">
           <p>
            This is Las Vegas, you're almost required to have a hangover at some point while you're here. But after a bout of overindulgence, your body has revolted and demanded that you feed it some form of sustenance, ideally with nutritional value. Or else. So to make it easy to identify which dishes are ideal for your purposes, we tracked down the ones that had "hangover" in the title or at least hangover-easing effects. This way, if it's really bad, you can simply point to the word on the menu and the server will totally understand.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Hangover bloody Mary at Guy Fieri's Vegas Kitchen &amp; Bar
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            Pretty much any item on the new breakfast menu from
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/guy-fieris-vegas-kitchen-bar-opens/7837671/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/guy-fieris-vegas-kitchen-bar-opens/7837671/">
             Guy Fieri's Vegas Kitchen &amp; Bar
            </a>
            is going to cure what ails you, especially when paired with a hangover bloody Mary, a sort of build your own cocktail where you get to pick your poison. Start by choosing which vodka  there's also one tequila available if you're feeling saucy  then level of spiciness, before deciding on which five garnishes from about a dozen that include cucumber, pineapple and pickles. The crowning achievement is the protein that adorns the whole shebang: Will it be candied bacon? A shrimp? Or maybe you'll go savory with pepperoni or chicken apple sausage.
           </p>
           <p>
            However you build it, your healthy-sized bloody Mary is ideal to wash down the hunger-busting Dragon's Breath Chili Cornbread Benedict. Sweet cornbread is topped with two soft poached eggs, Fieri's house chili, white cheddar cheese sauce and scallions. After you feel a little human again, you can walk it off through
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/linq-is-a-breath-of-fresh-air-on-the-las-vegas-strip/7254035/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/linq-is-a-breath-of-fresh-air-on-the-las-vegas-strip/7254035/">
             The LINQ
            </a>
            , think about getting on the
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/las-vegas-high-roller-tallest-observation-wheel-in-the-world/7072015/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/las-vegas-high-roller-tallest-observation-wheel-in-the-world/7072015/">
             High Roller
            </a>
             or on second thought, retreat back into the comforting darkness of the casino.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Hangover omelet at Serendipity 3
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            There's a cheeriness when you walk into
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/listings/serendipity-3/2033163/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/listings/serendipity-3/2033163/">
             Serendipity 3
            </a>
             maybe it's because everyone there is hopped up on Frrrozen Hot Chocolate  but they do have a secret weapon for anyone not feeling especially cheery thanks to a night out on the town. A three-egg omelet is folded over its filling of bacon, French fries, mushrooms, and cheddar and jack cheeses. The hangover omelet was built with efficiency in mind: protein, starches, a kind-of vegetable and fat all consumable in one bite.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Hangover brunch at Citizens Kitchen &amp; Bar
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            Recover from a doozy at Light Nightclub with a meal at
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/listings/citizens-kitchen-bar/2033985/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/listings/citizens-kitchen-bar/2033985/">
             Citizens Kitchen &amp; Bar
            </a>
            , located right across the hallway at
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/video/escape-to-paradise-at-mandalay-bay/3491447/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/video/escape-to-paradise-at-mandalay-bay/3491447/">
             Mandalay Bay
            </a>
            . Keep in mind this a restaurant with something called the Fatty Melt on its menu, consisting of two cheeseburger patties sandwiched between two grilled cheese sandwiches. So when it comes to brunch, you'll undoubtedly find the cure to what ails you. The a la carte menu ranges from classic, such as eggs Benedict, to savory and hearty, as in chicken chilaquiles and corned beef hash skillet, to sweet, as in crme brulee French toast. Toss in bottomless Bloody Marys or mimosas for $25, because hey, it's Vegas.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Fried chicken at Brooklyn Bowl
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            If there's ever a need for a hangover meal, it's after a night at a concert. So it wouldn't be unheard of if you'd spent the previous evening rocking out to a live act at
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/brooklyn-bowl-brings-bowling-food-and-music-to-the-linq/6366979/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/brooklyn-bowl-brings-bowling-food-and-music-to-the-linq/6366979/">
             Brooklyn Bowl
            </a>
            , only to return the following morning (or midday) to the scene of the crime. If you feel like a little bit of salty and sweet, check out the Jim Morrison, a vinyl record-sized pancake served with Brooklyn Bowl's famous fried chicken, available in dark, white or mixed.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Hangover oxtail soup at Stewart + Ogden
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            Clear soups are not unheard of to settle the stomach, and since fatty, rich foods can cure your hangover, a simple, flavorful broth should hit the spot. Hawaiians swear by the healing properties of this particular savory stock: a meaty and rich, but clean soup made from slow-braising oxtails. The bone, along with diced vegetables, makes an appearance in the soup for you to pick the meat from, if you're so inclined. And the best part is, no matter what time you finally roll out of bed, the healthy option is available all day in this 24-hour restaurant in
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/downtown-vegas-gets-brand-new-hotel/3431671/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/vegas-buzz/downtown-vegas-gets-brand-new-hotel/3431671/">
             Downtown Grand
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Hangover Brunch at Bar + Bistro
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            If you're intent on paying for your sins off-Strip, the weekend hangover brunch at the
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/listings/bar-bistro/2033601/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/listings/bar-bistro/2033601/">
             Arts Factory
            </a>
            features live bluegrass, though we're not exactly sure how that will interact with a headache. Fans seem to love the internationally inspired  la carte brunch items, such as flan French toast and quiche, but one of the biggest reasons people seem to head here is for the hair o' the dog with bottomless mimosas and Bloody Marys for $24 ($12 each).
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Rou jia mo at China Poblano
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            We're pretty sure rou jia mo doesn't mean "hangover cure" in Mandarin, but eating one does certainly help. Sandwiches aren't that popular in Chinese culture, but this classic street food can be found late nights at hawker stalls in Asia, not to mention from the walk-up window at
            <a href="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/listings/china-poblano/2033483/" title="http://experience.usatoday.com/las-vegas/listings/china-poblano/2033483/">
             China Poblano
            </a>
            . The bread is sort of a crossover between a steamed bun and a griddled English muffin, with sweet, red braised pork stuffed into it. Think of it as an Asian-spiced pulled pork sandwich, fatty and rich with the juices soaking into the bun. More of a snack before you turn in for the night, it will definitely curb the hunger pangs and hopefully act as a last defense before you realize what you've done in the morning.
           </p>
           <p>
            <i>
             Or just call the Hangover Heaven bus to come to you. Watch how it works:
            </i>
           </p>
