        <h1>
        </h1>
        <h1>
         <span id="docs-internal-guid-dff93168-5d61-108d-6281-0038bfb8af58" style="font-weight: normal;">
          Paris' 10 Stunning Patisseries You'll Fall in Love With
         </span>
        </h1>
        <h2>
         <span id="docs-internal-guid-dff93168-5d61-a90f-835e-0c1c0f7f3dac" style="font-weight: normal;">
          The city of love, art and creativity,
          <a href="http://theculturetrip.com/europe/france/paris/">
           Paris
          </a>
          has much to offer in the way of beauty, romanticism and, of course, gourmet cuisine. With a lengthy tradition for creating and presenting the very best pastries, foods and wines, it is often an almost impossible task to whittle the selection down.  We discover ten of the best ptisseries in the city of light that really satisfy the sweet tooth.
         </span>
        </h2>
       </div>
       <div class="imageWrapper inline-image imageFull">
        <figure>
         <img alt="The Culture Trip" src="http://static.theculturetrip.com/images/56-295957-2.-carl-marletti.jpg"/>
         <figcaption>
          <em>
           Interior of the Carl Marletti boutique |  Carl Marletti
          </em>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
       </div>
       <div class="text-container">
        <p>
         <span id="">
          <h3 id="">
           <span id="" style="font-weight: normal;">
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             Carl Marletti
            </span>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p dir="ltr" id="">
           Established by a connoisseur in the art,
           <a href="http://www.carlmarletti.com/" id="">
            Carl Marletti
           </a>
           is a fantastic ptisserie that fuses all that is required to create the perfect product, focusing on beautiful presentation, absolute precision and an incredible flavour. Perfect for those wishing for aesthetic and gastronomic perfection, Carl Marletti is unusual in its combination of colour and scent  in honour of his florist partner, Marletti often adds scent to traditional pastries to create something entirely new, such as the 'Lily Valley' (a St Honor cake with the scent of violet). Though it is his Lemon Tart that won an award in 2010 for its incredible quality, there are other products that are definitely worth trying within the shop itself, such as his specialty vanilla millefeuilles, clairs, and the signature 'Marletti' (dacquoise biscuit with coconut, vanilla cream and red fruits).
          </p>
          <em>
           <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/Carl+Marletti/@48.839325,2.350465,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0xc301491513f2deb7?sa=X&amp;ei=vXGJVN6zGcv0UqWKhNAL&amp;ved=0CIQBEPwSMA0" id="">
            Carl Marletti, 51 Rue Censier, Paris, France
           </a>
           , +33 1 43 31 68 12
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
       </div>

       <div class="text-container">

        <h3 id="">
         <span id="" style="font-weight: normal;">
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Bl Sucr
          </span>
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p dir="ltr" id="">
         Small yet renowned, Bl Sucr has reached a quasi-cult status for all those itinerant gourmands who wish to try the very best of breads and cakes in Paris. Placed in a 'quaint' and picturesque part of Paris, the superior quality of the products served, combined with a charming atmosphere (especially if you eat on their terrace overlooking the Square Trousseau), and excellent, friendly service make Bl Sucr a delight. Special mention must go to their glazed madeleines, croissants, and dark chocolate slabs, all of which are said to be so good that they are worthy of a trip to Paris in themselves. Bl Sucr is therefore a must-visit ptisserie for those wishing to indulge their sweet tooth.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em id="">
          <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/Bl%C3%A9+Sucr%C3%A9/@48.850612,2.376803,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0xda8fb6358465b3c8?sa=X&amp;ei=qXGJVP2JCML5UKD_gIgO&amp;ved=0CHwQ_BIwCw" id="">
           Bl Sucr, 7 Rue Antoine Vollon, Paris, France
          </a>
          , +33 1 43 40 77 73
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

         <img alt="The Culture Trip" src="http://static.theculturetrip.com/images/56-295962-3.-pain-d-epices.jpeg"/>

         <span id="">
          <h3 id="">
           <span id="" style="font-weight: normal;">
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             Des Gteaux et Du Pain
            </span>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p dir="ltr" id="">
           Acclaimed by a happy customer who described stepping into the ptisserie as being similar to 'stepping into a jewellery shop', this opinion is very much reflective of
           <a href="http://www.desgateauxetdupain.com/" id="">
            Des Gteaux et Du Pain
           </a>
           's ethos of creating exciting, feminine, delicate and yet gorgeous new pastries. Claire Damon is the creative force behind the entire enterprise, she has been acclaimed across France for her successful fusions of traditional and modern, making something refreshing. A notable example of her successes include the 'Lipstick Clair-Obscur' (a mixture of crushed almonds, coffee mousseline, orange blossom flavoured crme brle, ladyfingers, and coffee chantilly cream), which is praised for the harmony of its ingredients and its refreshing flavour.
          </p>
          <em>
           <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/Des+G%C3%A2teaux+et+du+Pain/@48.841242,2.314636,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x83ffeb3e732db1b7?sa=X&amp;ei=el6HVPnsN4LoUqTZgbAN&amp;sqi=2&amp;ved=0CJABEPwSMA8" id="">
            Des Gteaux et Du Pain, 63 Boulevard Pasteur, Paris, France
           </a>
           , +33 1 45 38 94 16
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>

          <h3 id="">
           <span id="" style="font-weight: normal;">
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             Htel Plaza Athne
            </span>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p dir="ltr" id="">
           Established in 1911 and boasting a beautiful view overlooking the Champs-Elysees and Eiffel Tower, the
           <a href="http://www.dorchestercollection.com/en/paris/hotel-plaza-athenee" id="">
            Htel Plaza Athne
           </a>
           houses the beautiful and renowned La Galerie restaurant, a venture mainly powered by the acclaimed
           <a href="http://www.christophemichalak.com" id="">
            Christophe Michalak
           </a>
           and his team of ptissiers. With a wide range of choices, from breakfasts, to light lunches, and little sweet treats, La Galerie is best known for its very own version of Afternoon Tea, which offers a mouth-watering array of meringues, little cakes, brioches and iced fancies. Perfect for those wishing for that pleasant blend of chic beauty, conviviality and liveliness, La Galerie joins the elements of ambience, quality and splendour to create an unforgettable experience.
          </p>
          <em>
           <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/Hotel+Plaza+Athenee+Paris/@48.866194,2.304272,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x646bce5738139a" id="">
            Htel Plaza Athne, 25 Avenue Montaigne, Paris, France
           </a>
           , +33 1 53 67 66 65

         </span>

             Hugo &amp; Victor
            </span>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p dir="ltr" id="">
           Having obtained a reputation for creativity and elegance,
           <a href="http://www.hugovictor.com/" id="">
            Hugo &amp; Victor
           </a>
           is a very popular ptisserie that prides itself on the intensity of flavours and the duality of its creations, a duality that strives to merge the principles of the innovative 'Hugo' and the classic 'Victor'. Classy and beautifully presented to the extreme, the quality of its baked goods is enhanced by the fantastic beauty of its presentation boxes and packages, some of which are a compulsory experiences in themselves (especially the Moleskin notebook-esque boxes for their gourmet chocolates). The classic yet creative simplicity of their products, such as the praline escargot pastry, are in a class of their own and simply have to be sampled.
          </p>
          <em>
           <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/Hugo+et+Victor/@48.852461,2.326332,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x23146b2caea6704?sa=X&amp;ei=dV2HVKTXKsbmUp3kgsgL&amp;ved=0CJABEPwSMA4" id="">
            Hugo &amp; Victor, 40 Boulevard Raspail, Paris, France
           </a>
           , +33 1 44 39 97 73

         <span id="" style="font-weight: normal;">
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           La Bague de Kenza
          </span>
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p dir="ltr" id="">
         A leader in its field,
         <a href="http://www.labaguedekenza.com/" id="">
          La Bague de Kenza
         </a>
         is noted for its dazzling array of sweets and pastries that are specifically inspired by and based upon
         <a href="http://theculturetrip.com/africa/algeria/" id="">
          Algerian
         </a>
         fare. Offering a range of over two hundred baked goods, the explosive flavour arising from the simplest ingredients of sticky honey, nuts, semolina, and dates, seasoned with fragrant hints of oranges and roses, is an olfactory and palatable delight. The enduring popularity with which the ptisserie is blessed also ensures that the products on sale are correspondingly fresh, with products being almost always made on the day. A wonderful combination of the exotic and the sweet, this ptisserie is the perfect choice for those looking for something a little outside of traditional French fare.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em id="">
          <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/search/la+bague+de+kenza/@48.851106,2.3392645,14z/data=!3m1!4b1?sa=X&amp;ei=CWSHVK7xAoHuUL2lhKAK&amp;ved=0CJMBELYD" id="">

        <h3 id="">
         <span id="" style="font-weight: normal;">
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           L'clair de Gnie
          </span>
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p dir="ltr" id="">
         In a world that is often overwhelmed by the more aesthetically pleasing pastries, such as macarons, cronuts and brles, the legendary
         <a href="http://leclairdegenie.com/" id="">
          L'clair de Gnie
         </a>
         manages the not inconsiderable feat of bringing eclairs back into the realm of popular taste. Though an enduring favourite with the French since the 19th century, in modern times it has often been eclipsed by prettier treats, dismissed as being rather old school. This is not the case with Christophe Adam however, the pioneer of L'clair de Gnie, who has been hugely successful in his modernisation of the classic clair, bringing a sophistication and newness to it that has reinvigorated the entire 'franchise' of the humble clair. With noteworthy new flavours such as the yuzu, salted caramel and popcorn clairs, Adam has outdone himself in his creativity, a creativity that ought to be properly savoured and experienced.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/L'%C3%A9clair+de+G%C3%A9nie/@48.856189,2.360603,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x7855b55b5b030ace?sa=X&amp;ei=YVyHVLvHO8T5UKW0gbgM&amp;ved=0CI8BEPwSMA0" id="">
           L'clair de Gnie, 14 Rue Pave, Paris, France
          </a>
          , +33 1 42 77 85 11
         </em>

        <h3 id="">
         <span id="" style="font-weight: normal;">
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Maison Desgranges
          </span>
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p dir="ltr" id="">
         The ultimate in Parisian modishness,
         <a href="http://www.maisondesgranges.com/actualites-article.php?id=13" id="">
          Maison Desgranges
         </a>
         is famed for its ability to produce the simplest of products: the classic baguette. Deceptively simple to create and yet difficult to perfect, Maison Desgranges has won awards and acclaim for its success in baguettes. Nevertheless, it would be unfair to praise it on its breads alone, with a charming array of cakes and pastries offering unparalleled beauty and class  such as their flaky almond cakes and their macarons. They are deservedly dubbed the 'architects of tastes', blending differing textures, colours, contrasts and flavours together in unusual and exciting ways. Maison Desgranges is thus the heart of Paris itself and simply cannot be missed.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/6+Rue+de+Passy,+75016+Paris,+France/@48.8584808,2.2844332,17z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m2!3m1!1s0x47e66ffdeb388f4d:0xaec4ff068b012023" id="">
           Maison Desgranges, 6 Rue de Passy, Paris, France
          </a>
          , +33 1 42 88 35 82
         </em>
        </p>

           <span id="" style="font-weight: normal;">
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             Pain de Sucre
            </span>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p dir="ltr" id="">
           Literally named 'Sugarloaf' in French,
           <a href="http://www.patisseriepaindesucre.com/" id="">
            Pain de Sucre
           </a>
           is an explosion of all that is exciting and ever so slightly quirky in the world of pastry. They are especially well-known for the exciting range of tastes in marshmallows, such as chicory, saffron, chilli pepper, angelica, and whiskey, just to name a very small few. This principle of extraordinariness and overwhelming flavour can also be seen in their other products, such as their breads, which are simply bursting with the flavours of fruits, nuts and grains, and their cakes, which are made with a painstaking effort and skill evident in the freshness and flavour of their fruit tarts and individual baba au rhums.
          </p>
          <em>
           <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/Pain+de+Sucre/@48.860539,2.355673,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x5a9598b75eeb1863?sa=X&amp;ei=y16HVOOyBcisU8nbgZgI&amp;sqi=2&amp;ved=0CJEBEPwSMA0" id="">
            Pain de Sucre, 14 Rue de Rambuteau, Paris, France
           </a>
           , +33 1 45 74 68 92
          </em>

          <h3 id="">
           <span id="" style="font-weight: normal;">
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             Stohrer
            </span>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p dir="ltr" id="">
           A national treasure in itself,
           <a href="http://www.stohrer.fr/?lang=en" id="">
            Stohrer
           </a>
           partially earns its fame from being the oldest ptisserie in Paris, founded in 1730 after Marie Antoinette travelled to France, bringing her trusted pastry chef 'Stohrer' with her, who founded the shop five years after settling in the French capital. In keeping with its tradition of quality and dedication, Stohrer earns the rest of its reputation from the excellent quality of its cakes and its innovation, being the birthplace of the 'baba au rhum' and connoisseurs of chocolate garnish and tart crusts. Its historical heritage and dedication to perfection thus make it the go-to place for any hungry or curious tourist.
          </p>
          <em>
           <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/Stohrer/@48.86527,2.346775,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x8fd70c0719b6ceab" id="">
            Stohrer, 51 Rue Montorgueil, Paris, France

           , +33 1 42 33 38 20
