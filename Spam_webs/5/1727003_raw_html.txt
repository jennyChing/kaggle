         <h1 class="entry-title">
          In Florida: Registered Sex Offender Wins Lottery
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <div class="meta">
          <span class="meta_posted_by">
           Posted by:
          </span>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a href="http://sportsmasher.com/author/sportsmasher/" rel="author">
            SportSmasher
           </a>
          </span>
          ,
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-12-11T11:46:19+00:00">
           December 11, 2014
          </time>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://sportsmasher.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/IMG_6050.jpg">
           <img alt="IMG_6050.JPG" class="size-full aligncenter" src="http://sportsmasher.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/IMG_6050.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.orlandosentinel.com/news/breaking-news/os-sex-predator-lottery-winner-millions-20141210-story.html">
           OrlandoSentinel
          </a>
          <em>
           At the Florida Lottery headquarters, Timothy Poole, 43, posed for a photo with an oversized check after claiming his $3 million Scratch-Off prize. But when the image hit the Internet, others recognized the 450-pound man as a registered sexual predator. In 2001, the Orange County Sheriffs Office arrested Poole on charges of sexual battery on a victim under 12, records show. Before that, he was arrested in 1991 for stealing almost $20,000 in food stamps from Safeco Inc. of Ocala, where he worked as a guard. arrested 12 times in Florida on charges ranging from grand theft to forging a check, records show. Poole served three months in prison in 1996 and did a second stint from 2003 to 2006, according to the Florida Department of Corrections. This week, Poole claimed his winnings from the Florida Lottery in a one-time lump-sum payment of $2,219,807.90. He played the Super Millions scratch-off game which costs $20 a ticket. The game offers 12 top prizes of $3 million and, with Pooles winner, five remain, spokeswoman Keri Gero said.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Does anyone else think that we should have some sort of law on the books that makes convicted felons ineligible to win the lottery? Arent convicted felons the last people we want to hand millions of dollars to with no questions asked? I do not think that the victim or their family is going to be thrilled that a registered sex offender just got a check from the government for millions of dollars, and maybe the government should put a law on the books, where they can step in these situations and donate this money to a charity for abused kids.
         </p>
         <p>
          Congratulations Timothy Poole on your winnings, you can now afford the newest and shiniest (or dustiest, whichever is better) windowless van on the block, good for you.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://sportsmasher.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/IMG_6051.jpg">

          </a>
         </p>

           Fucking Florida

          tag on this page.
         </p>
