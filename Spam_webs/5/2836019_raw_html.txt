       <h2>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Cities">
         <a href="/wiki/Skyrim:Cities" title="Skyrim:Cities">
          Cities
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/w/index.php?title=Skyrim:Places&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cities">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Each city is located in a different
        <a href="/wiki/Skyrim:Holds" title="Skyrim:Holds">
         hold
        </a>
        , and is home to that hold's ruler, or
        <a href="/wiki/Skyrim:Jarl" title="Skyrim:Jarl">
         jarl
        </a>
        . The five largest cities (major cities) are walled, allowing their maps (or
        <i>
         cells
        </i>
        as they are called in the
        <a href="/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Kit" title="Skyrim:Creation Kit">
         Creation Kit
        </a>
        ) to be treated separately from the main map.
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skyrim:Carriage" title="Skyrim:Carriage">
         Carriages
        </a>
        are available for hire outside each major city to transport the player to any other city.
       </p>
       <p>
        Fast traveling to a major city places you just inside its main gate, but each such city also has a keep or palace where the jarl resides, which is a separate fast-travel destination and is listed below. Additionally, Riften has a third fast-travel destination once you have joined the
        <a href="/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(faction)" title="Skyrim:Thieves Guild (faction)">
         Thieves Guild
        </a>
        , namely the entrance to its
        <a href="/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(place)" title="Skyrim:Thieves Guild (place)">
         headquarters
        </a>
        .
       </p>
