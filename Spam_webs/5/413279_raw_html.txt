         <h2>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Tips to Have a Better Credit Score
         </p>
         <p>
          By:
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <hr class="transcript-hr"/>
       <div class="Transcript">
        <div class="col-xs-3">
         <img alt="Tips to have a better credit score" class="img-responsive" data-src="http://image2.slideserve.com/5045384/slide2-t.jpg" id="lazyimg" src="http://www.slideserve.com/new/img/default-thumb.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="col-xs-9">
         <p style="float:left">
         </p>
         <p>
          Your credit score is totaled based on five major categories  your payment pattern, your payment history, your debts, the types of your credit, and new credits in your account. Now, lets discuss each of these credit score categories.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <hr class="transcript-hr"/>
       <div class="Transcript">
        <div class="col-xs-3">
         <img alt="Your payment pattern" class="img-responsive" data-src="http://image2.slideserve.com/5045384/your-payment-pattern-t.jpg" id="lazyimg" src="http://www.slideserve.com/new/img/default-thumb.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="col-xs-9">
         <p style="float:left">
         </p>
         <h3>
          YourPayment Pattern
         </h3>
         <p>
          Obviously, how good of a payer you are will affect your credit score greatly. To analyze your payment pattern, simply ask yourself the following questions:
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Do you pay all your bills promptly?
           <p>
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           Are you in the habit of delaying on your payments?
           <p>
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           Do you have past due bills in your account?
           <p>
            Nevertheless, although you usually try your best to pay on time, there may be instances when you are forced to delay on your payments. When this happens, make sure that you get in touch with your creditors right away. Remember, you can request from your creditors not to report your late payments as long as it doesnt exceed thirty days. If you think you cant pay off your debt within that 30 day limit, try to make an arrangement with your creditor.
           </p>
          </li>
         </ul>
        </div>
       </div>
       <hr class="transcript-hr"/>
       <div class="Transcript">
        <div class="col-xs-3">
         <img alt="Your payment history" class="img-responsive" data-src="http://image2.slideserve.com/5045384/your-payment-history-t.jpg" id="lazyimg" src="http://www.slideserve.com/new/img/default-thumb.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="col-xs-9">
         <p style="float:left">
         </p>
         <h3>
          YourPaymentHistory
         </h3>
         <p>
          How long youve had credit also plays a role in your credit report. That is why it is very important to establish credit as early as possible. It adds up to your credibility as a borrower. This is also the reason why you should never close your account from your old credit cards even when you dont use frequently use them. If your old credit cards come with high interest, use them occasionally to buy inexpensive items just to keep them from automatically closing out.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <hr class="transcript-hr"/>
       <div class="Transcript">
        <div class="col-xs-3">
         <img alt="Your debts" class="img-responsive" data-src="http://image2.slideserve.com/5045384/your-debts-t.jpg" id="lazyimg" src="http://www.slideserve.com/new/img/default-thumb.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="col-xs-9">
         <p style="float:left">
         </p>
         <h3>
          YourDebts
         </h3>
         <p>
          The debts you owe will determine whether you are a high risk borrower or not. If you are in the habit of maximizing your credit limit, then creditors will automatically regard you as high risk. Ideally, borrowers should never go beyond 30%-40% of their allowable credit.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <hr class="transcript-hr"/>
       <div class="Transcript">
        <div class="col-xs-3">
         <img alt="Types of your credit" class="img-responsive" data-src="http://image2.slideserve.com/5045384/types-of-your-credit-t.jpg" id="lazyimg" src="http://www.slideserve.com/new/img/default-thumb.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="col-xs-9">
         <p style="float:left">
         </p>
         <h3>
          Types of YourCredit
         </h3>
         <p>
          Whether you have credit card accounts, car loans or mortgage loans also affect your credit score. A mortgage loan can increase your credit standing because it is considered as debt that appreciates in value over time. Nonetheless, keep in mind that it will only boost your credit if you are able to keep up with your mortgage.
         </p>
         <p>
          How you manage your new credit is just as important. Remember that each time you submit a credit application, a new inquiry is made on your credit history. Too many inquiries can lower down your credit score. Refused credit applications can also be a red flag in your report. To avoid damaging your credit, send an application only if you really plan on obtaining it.
         </p>
        </div>
