   <h1>
    Australia's best pub apps
   </h1>
   <!-- google_ad_section_end -->
   <p>
    <cite>
     Christine D'Mello -
     <small>
      Dec 12, 2013
     </small>
    </cite>
   </p>
   <div class="wof">
    <img alt="Apps on tap: find the best beers in town." src="/content/dam/images/2/z/5/y/z/image.related.articleLeadwide.300x169.2z2xa.png/1386851377047.jpg" title=""/>
    <p>
     Apps on tap: find the best beers in town.
    </p>
   </div>
   <!-- class:wof -->
   <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
   <p>
    Beer on tap could take on a whole new meaning.
   </p>
   <p>
    With the ubiquitous smartphone in the palm of our hands, options for checking out a new watering hole or having a night out are a mere swipe away.
   </p>
   <p>
    One of the entrepreneurs quenching this customer thirst is former professional basketballer Adrian Bauk with The Pub Crawl Pages app.
   </p>
   <p>
    The idea came to Bauk, 28, after he travelled through Europe two years ago and didn't know what to do for a night out. He found out there's a range of pub crawl companies you can go to in a given city.
   </p>
   <p>
    Back in Australia, Bauk's research led him to believe there was a market for an app that promotes and advertises pub crawls around the world to make it easier for you to find a night out which is safe and guided.
   </p>
   <p>
    It took about a year and a half to get the data. There are more than 100 pub crawls on Bauk's app and it is being updated daily.
   </p>
   <div id="rest">
    <p>
     Bauk, who won the NBL title with the Sydney Kings, tried to get his app made in Australia but it was "a monstrous amount of money". He then opted for a freelancer in India.
    </p>
    <p>
     A friend designed the app. "I sent the files off to the Indian company and they finished it in a month and a half.
    </p>
    <p>
     "They gave me milestones so I didn't have to pay the full cost upfront."
    </p>
    <p>
     All this set him back just under $4000.
    </p>
    <p>
     Sydney-born Bauk's target market is huge  travellers, backpackers, people wanting a good night out, buck's and hen's parties.
    </p>
    <p>
     The app, which is available on Android and iOS devices, provides updated information on pub crawls. Bauk says many of the featured companies offer a discount if a booking is made through the app.
    </p>
    <p>
     "The app's 99 cents ... but in Sydney alone you are getting five dollars off one of the pub crawl's price, you're getting an extra free cocktail with another."
    </p>
    <p>
     As part of his business model, he does not charge the companies to put their product on the app. "However, if a user goes to the door and shows the app, and they buy a ticket, I get 10 per cent of that ticket."
    </p>
    <p>
     Almost 1000 users have downloaded the app, which was launched in October. Bauk's target is 20,000 users within the first year and a half.
    </p>
    <p>
     The idea for the BarBait app came to Melbourne duo Gautam Paalep and Kristofer Tan when they were walking down a part of the city with which they were not familiar.
    </p>
    <p>
     "There are apps out there with a 'what's around me' sort of thing. That wasn't enough for us." Paalep says they wanted to know what "the owner of that bar was doing to get me to walk through the doors".
    </p>
    <p>
     The pair, who both hold full-time jobs, conceptually ran the idea by venues and "what they liked about it is that they had full control over the promotion."
    </p>
    <p>
     Co-owners Paalep and Tan, former school friends, faced a major challenge early on. They engaged "a company which developed the first build for us and we launched, but unfortunately they had on-sold the code and idea and concept to someone else."
    </p>
    <p>
     Instead of engaging lawyers, they decided to change the application.
    </p>
    <p>
     "That was a defining moment for us because that happened a month after we launched. So we engaged our own developer and gave him a stake in the business."
    </p>
    <p>
     Paalep, 28, says the app  which costs $2.99 and is available on iPhone and Android  enables the user to pop into a bar or restaurant they have not visited before and often take advantage of an offer.
    </p>
    <p>
     He says from the venues' point of view there are many advantages. For instance, they can generate foot traffic to BarBait's 15,000 users via promos they can run on the app.
    </p>
    <p>
     "When those users come and redeem [an offer] at that venue, they are tagged in the database, so then any future communication can happen between the venue and those users directly through our push notification."
    </p>
    <p>
     Rob Murray-Lyon believes design is an important factor for a successful app. "Design is fundamentally important. If you can't design it properly, you've got no chance."
    </p>
    <p>
     His Sydney Bars app has consistently ranked in the top 100 in the iTunes paid charts.
    </p>
    <p>
     Murray-Lyon runs a company Nasda Studios that makes apps. He did the coding in two to three weeks. His wife Kerrin, who came up with the idea, did the research. "There was a lot of legwork in going around to all the bars.
    </p>
    <p>
     "There is really a huge amount of information in the app, in terms of addresses, phone numbers, websites, photos and the cost of drinks."
    </p>
    <p>
     The Sydney Bars app lists 250 to 300 venues, costs $1.99 and has about 5000 users in Australia.
    </p>
    <p>
     His puts emphasis on the visual, too. "A picture tells a thousand words, I can look at a picture and say I want to go to that bar or I don't want to go to that bar."
    </p>
    <p>
     "We have a decent track record of making apps that rank well. This [pubs app] is the market that was begging for something to be made. Now there are about five or six apps and ours is still ranking well ahead of everybody else."
    </p>
   </div>
