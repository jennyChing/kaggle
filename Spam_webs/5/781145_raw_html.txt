       <h1>
        The Strongest Boy Names in the Top 20
       </h1>
       <table class="info entry-meta">
        <tr>
         <td class="date">

         </td>
         <td>
          <span class="filledunder">

        <p>
         <img alt="dumbbells" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-118532" height="200" src="http://www.nancy.cc/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dumbbells.jpg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Youd think the top 20 boy names in the nation would be strongly masculine names, wouldnt you? That theyd be used almost entirely by boys, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats the case with most of them, but not with all of them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Below, on the left, are the top 20 baby boy names in the U.S. On the right, Ive re-ordered the names according to relative masculinity (strongest to weakest).

        <p>
         Masculinity was determined by dividing the number of boys with each name by the total number of babies (male + female) with each name, then multiplying by 100 to come up with a percentage.
        </p>
        <p>
         The percentages are all very high (99+) until you get down to Ryan, Logan and Jayden:
        </p>
        <table>
         <tr>
          <td>
           Ryan
          </td>
          <td>

          </td>
          <td>

          </td>

        <p>
         And this analysis doesnt account for alternative spellings. If Id considered variants like Noa, Aidan and Jadan, some percentages would have been even lower.
        </p>
        <p>
         All 20 of these names are still boy namesIm not claiming that any of them are girl namesbut several might be mistaken for girl names once in a while. So if youre expecting a boy and youre aiming for an unambiguously male name, be wary of Noah, Ryan, Logan, Aiden and Jayden. (And all the other
         <a href="http://www.nancy.cc/2010/07/26/how-many-baby-girls-got-ayden-names-in-2009/">
          -ayden names
         </a>
         , just to be on the safe side.)
        </p>
