       <h1 class="entry-title">
        A Post for the Mommas
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2012-09-10T07:54:17+00:00">
         September 10, 2012
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Last week I read
         <a href="http://momastery.com/blog/2012/09/07/baby-sisters/" target="_blank">
          this post at Momastery
         </a>
         . She included two letters from sister moms of infants and she wanted her readers to leave some comments with hope on how to survive the younger years. For whatever reason, I didnt comment. Maybe because I knew nearly 500 other people would.
        </p>
        <p>
         Saturday night we babysat my nephews daughter. Shes two and a half. Shes as sweet as she can be. Shes funny. Shes also independent, as many 2 year olds are. I was transported back 5 years to when my now 7 and 9 year olds were that age.
        </p>
        <p>
         And I was glad theyre 7 and 9.
        </p>
        <p>
         Yesterday I was at church with a couple that had a 2 year old and an infant. The dad was keeping track of the 2 year old and the mom was feeding the wee one standing right in the middle of the hallway.
        </p>
        <p>
         While we were standing there, a frustrated momma came by with a squirming toddler on each hip. I wanted to take one off her hands, help them to nursery and then give her a nice back massage.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im convinced those of you reading with young ones need to hear some encouragement.
         <strong>
          You can do this. It wont always be hard like right now. Sure, it will be hard but in different ways.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A day is coming where you dont have to worry about anyones poop schedule but your own.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_56012.jpg">
           <img alt="100_5601" border="0" height="400" src="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_5601_thumb1.jpg" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-top: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-right: 0px" title="100_5601" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         You wont even have to look at poop all day if you dont want to.
        </p>
        <p>
         In fact, there will be weeks that go by where you dont even care if anyone else is pooping.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A day is coming when you get to eat a meal where you dont have to scoop even one bite of food into someone elses mouth.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_75851.jpg">
          <img alt="100_7585" border="0" height="400" src="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_7585_thumb1.jpg" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-top: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-right: 0px" title="100_7585" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Youll get to eat every single bite of your own food.
        </p>
        <p>
         And better yet, none of the food will come from your own body!
        </p>
        <p>
         In fact, since youve learned how to eat a dinner in 90 seconds, youll actually finish before anyone else instead of being the lone survivor at the dinner table.
        </p>
        <p>
         And yes, theres a day when all of you will sit in normal chairs around an actual dinner table. And youll ask questions about their day and theyll tell you and youll all laugh and actually enjoy this part of your day.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A day is coming when you dont have to be in the same room to bathe your children.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_65491.jpg">
          <img alt="100_6549" border="0" height="400" src="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_6549_thumb1.jpg" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-top: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-right: 0px" title="100_6549" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         You actually get to say grab a towel and go get in the shower.
        </p>
        <p>
         And they do it. And they know how to shampoo and conditioner their hair. AND brush it when they get out.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its coming, momma.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A day is coming when you dont have to go through gymnastics to get your child to sleep.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_60801.jpg">
          <img alt="100_6080" border="0" height="400" src="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_6080_thumb1.jpg" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-top: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-right: 0px" title="100_6080" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Youll be able to say bedtime and they know what that means. Theyll know how to brush their teeth and get their own pajamas on and read to themselves.
        </p>
        <p>
         In fact, youll have to remind yourself to spend time with them at night just so you dont miss the best part of the day. Yes! Bedtime will one day be wonderful.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A day is coming when they sleep through the night.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         And sometimes, theyll throw up during the night and theyll just tell you about it in the morning. Mom, I even made it to the toilet.
        </p>
        <p>
         Doesnt that sound glorious?
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A day is coming when you wont even know what room theyre in when theyre playing.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_9820.jpg">
          <img alt="100_9820" border="0" height="400" src="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_9820_thumb1.jpg" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-top: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-right: 0px" title="100_9820" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Youll have to walk around and find them and ask what theyve been doing. And the best part is it wont be fingerpainting the walls, unbuckling their diaper and smearing it on carpet or rubbing diaper cream all over their face.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A day is coming when you dont have to pack 80 things to leave the house.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_04081.jpg">
          <img alt="100_0408" border="0" height="401" src="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_0408_thumb1.jpg" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-top: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-right: 0px" title="100_0408" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         In fact, the kids will want to grab a few of their favorite things and you can just say, Come out to the car when youre ready and shut the door on your way out.
        </p>
        <p>
         And when they come out, you dont have to buckle a single person except yourself.
        </p>
        <p>
         And while youre driving, you dont have to entertain a single soul to keep them from crying.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A day is coming when they wont say no every time you tell them to do something.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_47011.jpg">
           <img alt="100_4701" border="0" height="600" src="http://amyjbennett.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/100_4701_thumb1.jpg" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-top: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-right: 0px" title="100_4701" width="400"/>
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         In fact, sometimes theyll even ask you what they should do.
        </p>
        <p>
         All of this and more is coming. It gets easier. Parenting gets funner. Yes, funner. But it gets that way because you did the hard work.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theyll know how to shower because you taught them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theyll know how to play because you taught them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theyll know how to eat because you taught them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Hang on, mommas. I know these are hard years, but theyre good years. You can do it.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
