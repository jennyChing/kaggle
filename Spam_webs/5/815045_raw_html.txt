    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      <b>
       Past life regression
      </b>
      is a technique that uses
      <a href="/wiki/Hypnosis" title="Hypnosis">
       hypnosis
      </a>
      to recover what practitioners believe are
      <a href="/wiki/Memory" title="Memory">
       memories
      </a>
      of past lives or
      <a href="/wiki/Reincarnation" title="Reincarnation">
       incarnations
      </a>
      , though others regard them as fantasies or delusions or a type of
      <a href="/wiki/Confabulation" title="Confabulation">
       confabulation
      </a>
      . Past life regression is typically undertaken either in pursuit of a
      <a href="/wiki/Spirituality" title="Spirituality">
       spiritual
      </a>
      experience, or in a
      <a href="/wiki/Psychotherapy" title="Psychotherapy">
       psychotherapeutic
      </a>
      setting. Most advocates loosely adhere to beliefs about reincarnation,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skepdic_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Skepdic-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      though religious traditions that incorporate reincarnation generally do not include the idea of
      <a href="/wiki/Repressed_memory" title="Repressed memory">
       repressed memories
      </a>
      of past lives.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cordon_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cordon-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Past_life_regression&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mythology">
       Mythology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Past_life_regression&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mythology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/History_of_India" title="History of India">
       ancient Indian
      </a>
      literature, the
      <a href="/wiki/Upanishads" title="Upanishads">
       Upanishads
      </a>
      mention past-life regression,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but the
      <a href="/wiki/Yoga_Sutras_of_Patanjali" title="Yoga Sutras of Patanjali">
       Yoga Sutras of Patajali
      </a>
      discuss the concept in greater detail. Written during the 2nd century BC, the
      <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">
       Hindu
      </a>
      scholar
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pata%C3%B1jali" title="Patajali">
       Patajali
      </a>
      discussed the idea of the
      <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">
       soul
      </a>
      becoming burdened with an accumulation of impressions as part of the
      <a href="/wiki/Karma" title="Karma">
       karma
      </a>
      from previous lives.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Patajali called the process of past-life regression
      <i>
       prati-prasav
      </i>
      (literally "reverse birthing"), and saw it as addressing current problems through memories of past lives. Some types of
      <a href="/wiki/Yoga" title="Yoga">
       yoga
      </a>
      continue to use
      <i>
       prati-prasav
      </i>
      as a practice.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the religious
      <a href="/wiki/Mythology" title="Mythology">
       mythology
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
       China
      </a>
      the deity
      <a href="/wiki/Meng_Po" title="Meng Po">
       Meng Po
      </a>
      , also known as the "Lady of Forgetfullness", prevents souls from remembering their past lives: she gives them a bittersweet drink that erases all memories before they climb the wheel of reincarnation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_era">
       Modern era
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Past_life_regression&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Modern era">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In the modern era, it was the works of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madame_Blavatsky" title="Madame Blavatsky">
       Madame Blavatsky
      </a>
      , co-founder of the
      <a href="/wiki/Theosophical_Society" title="Theosophical Society">
       Theosophical Society
      </a>
      , which brought it a new found popularity, especially in
      <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">
       the West
      </a>
      . French educator
      <a href="/wiki/Allan_Kardec" title="Allan Kardec">
       Allan Kardec
      </a>
      also researched into past life regression in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Spirits_Book" title="The Spirits Book">
        The Spirits Book
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Heaven_and_Hell_(Allan_Kardec)" title="Heaven and Hell (Allan Kardec)">
        Heaven and Hell
       </a>
      </i>
      . Past life regression therapy has been developed since the 1950s by
      <a href="/wiki/Clinical_psychology" title="Clinical psychology">
       psychologists
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Psychiatry" title="Psychiatry">
       psychiatrists
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Mediumship" title="Mediumship">
       mediums
      </a>
      . The belief gained credibility because some of the advocates possess legitimate credentials, though these credentials were in areas unrelated to religion, psychotherapy or other domains dealing with past lives and mental health. Interest in the phenomenon started due to American housewife Virginia Tighe reporting and recounting the alleged memories of a 19th-century Irish woman named
      <a href="/wiki/Bridey_Murphy" title="Bridey Murphy">
       Bridey Murphy
      </a>
      ; later investigation failed to support the existence of such a woman and the memories were attributed to Tighe's childhood during which she spent time living next to an Irish immigrant with great similarity to the character she described.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skepdic_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Skepdic-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1988,
      <a href="/wiki/Brian_Weiss" title="Brian Weiss">
       Brian Weiss
      </a>
      , an American psychiatrist, started using past-life regression using hypnosis on his patient, later published, best-selling
      <i>
       Many Lives, Many Masters
      </i>
      , and has since been prominent writer on the topic.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rl1xxx_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-rl1xxx-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daily_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-daily-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He has appeared twice in interview with
      <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">
       Oprah Winfrey
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daily_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-daily-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Technique">
       Technique
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Past_life_regression&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Technique">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In the
      <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" title="Western culture">
       West
      </a>
      , past-life regression practitioners use
      <a href="/wiki/Hypnosis" title="Hypnosis">
       hypnosis
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Suggestion" title="Suggestion">
       suggestion
      </a>
      to promote recall in their patients, using a series of questions designed to elicit statements and memories about the past life's history and identity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyclopedia_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-encyclopedia-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Some practitioners also use bridging techniques from a clients current-life problem to bring "past-life stories" to conscious awareness.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Practitioners believe that unresolved issues from alleged past lives may be the cause of their patients' problems.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The technique is not taught as part of any medical internships.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skepdic_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Skepdic-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Luis Cordn states that this can be problematic as it creates
      <a href="/wiki/Delusion" title="Delusion">
       delusions
      </a>
      under the guise of therapy. Memories can vary from harmless to actually increasing suffering in the patient or their families. The memories are experienced as vivid as those based on events experienced in one's life, impossible to differentiate from true memories of actual events, and accordingly any damage can be difficult to undo.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cordon_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cordon-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Numerology" title="Numerology">
       Chinese numerologists
      </a>
      use the
      <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">
       Buddhist
      </a>
      /
      <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">
       Taoist
      </a>
      text the
      <i>
       Three Lives Book
      </i>
      to describe details of past lives.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Teachers of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_religion" title="Eastern religion">
       Eastern religion
      </a>
      claim to be able to use
      <a href="/wiki/Siddhi" title="Siddhi">
       siddhi
      </a>
      or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abhijna" title="Abhijna">
       abhijna
      </a>
      abilities to regress past lives (pubbenivsnussati
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">
       Criticism
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Past_life_regression&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Criticism">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The technique used during past life regression involves the subject answering a series of questions while hypnotized to reveal identity and events of alleged past lives, a method similar to that used in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recovered_memory_therapy" title="Recovered memory therapy">
       recovered memory therapy
      </a>
      and one that similarly misrepresents memory as a faithful recording of previous events rather than a constructed set of recollections. The use of hypnosis and
      <a href="/wiki/Suggestive_question" title="Suggestive question">
       suggestive questions
      </a>
      makes the subject particularly likely to hold distorted or false memories.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyclopedia_12-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-encyclopedia-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The source of the memories is more likely
      <a href="/wiki/Cryptomnesia" title="Cryptomnesia">
       cryptomnesia
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Confabulation" title="Confabulation">
       confabulations
      </a>
      that combine experiences, knowledge, imagination and
      <a href="/wiki/Suggestion" title="Suggestion">
       suggestion
      </a>
      or guidance from the hypnotist than recall of a previous existence. Once created, the memories are indistinguishable from memories based on events that occurred during the subject's life.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skepdic_1-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Skepdic-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cordon_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cordon-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Memories reported during past life regression have been investigated, and revealed historical inaccuracies that are easily explained through a basic knowledge of history, elements of popular culture or books that discuss historical events. Experiments with subjects undergoing past life regression indicate that a belief in reincarnation and suggestions by the hypnotist are the two most important factors regarding the contents of memories reported.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skepdic_1-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-Skepdic-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>

        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

       <a href="#cite_note-Spanos-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sumner_19-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Sumner-19">
        <span>
         [

     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Sources_of_memories">
       Sources of memories
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Past_life_regression&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sources of memories">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Scientific consensus is that the memories are the result of
      <a href="/wiki/Cryptomnesia" title="Cryptomnesia">
       cryptomnesia
      </a>
      , narratives created by the subconscious mind using imagination, forgotten information and suggestions from the therapist.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skepdic_1-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-Skepdic-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cordon_2-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cordon-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyclopedia_12-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-encyclopedia-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Memories created under hypnosis are indistinguishable from actual memories and can be more vivid than factual memories.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cordon_2-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cordon-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The greatest predictor of individuals reporting memories of past lives appears to be their beliefs about the existence in reincarnationindividuals who believe in reincarnation are more likely to report such memories, while skeptics or disbelievers are less so.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skepdic_1-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-Skepdic-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sumner_19-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Sumner-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Examinations of three cases of apparent past life regression (Bridey Murphy, Jane Evans and an unnamed
      <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">
       English
      </a>
      woman) revealed memories that were superficially convincing. However, investigation by experts in the languages used and historical periods described revealed flaws in all three patients' recall. The evidence included speech patterns that were "...used by movie makers and writers to convey the flavour of 16th century English speech" rather than actual
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">
       Renaissance
      </a>
      English, a date that was inaccurate but was the same as a recognized printing error in historical
      <a href="/wiki/Pamphlet" title="Pamphlet">
       pamphlets
      </a>
      , and a subject that reported historically accurate information from the Roman era that was identical to information found in a 1947 novel set in the same time as the individual's memories, with the same name reported by the person regressed. Other details cited are common knowledge and not evidence of the factual nature of the memories; subjects asked to provide historical information that would allow checking provided only vague responses that did not allow for verification, and sometimes were unable to provide critical details that would have been common knowledge (e.g. a subject described the life of a Japanese fighter pilot during
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      but was unable to identify
      <a href="/wiki/Hirohito" title="Hirohito">
       Hirohito
      </a>
      as the
      <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">
       Emperor of Japan
      </a>
      during the 1940s).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spanos_18-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Spanos-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Experimental_studies">
       Experimental studies
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Past_life_regression&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Experimental studies">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A 1976 study found that 40% of
      <a href="/wiki/Hypnotic_susceptibility" title="Hypnotic susceptibility">
       hypnotizable
      </a>
      subjects described new identities and used different names when given a suggestion to regress past their birth.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spanos_18-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Spanos-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the 1990s a series of experiments undertaken by
      <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Spanos" title="Nicholas Spanos">
       Nicholas Spanos
      </a>
      examined the nature of past life memories. Descriptions of alleged past lives were found to be extremely elaborate, with vivid, detailed descriptions. Subjects who reported memories of past lives exhibited high hypnotizability, and patients demonstrated that the expectations conveyed by the experimenter were most important in determining the characteristics of the reported memories. The degree to which the memories were considered credible by the experimental subjects was
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Correlation" title="Correlation">
       correlated
      </a>
      most significantly to the subjects' beliefs about reincarnation and their expectation to remember a past life rather than hypnotizability. Spanos' research leads him to the conclusion that past lives are not memories, but actually social constructions based on patients acting "as if" they were someone else, but with significant flaws that would not be expected of actual memories. To create these memories, Spanos' subjects drew upon the expectations established by authority figures and information outside of the experiment such as television, novels, life experiences and their own desires.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spanos_18-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Spanos-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
