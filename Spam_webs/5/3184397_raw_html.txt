        Ever Had a Yogasm? Yoga and Sex Talk Gets Oh So Interesting
       </h1>
       <span class="post_author_intro">

       </span>
       <span class="post_author" itemprop="author">

       </span>
       <meta content="2011-09-29" itemprop="datePublished"/>

       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_24332" style="width: 490px">
        <a href="http://yogadork.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/rex-manning-empire-records-yoga.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-24332" height="300" src="http://yogadork.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/rex-manning-empire-records-yoga.jpg" title="rex-manning-empire-records-yoga" width="480"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Zexxxay
        </p>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
       </p>
       <p>
        Oh yes, yes! The semi-popular semi-taboo yoga and sex talk comes swinging around the block once again with its saucy seducing scent of tea tree oil and
        <a href="http://youtu.be/zLq2-uZd5LY" target="_blank">
         Sex Panther
        </a>
        . This time its the Oh word: yOgasm. We didnt make it up.
       </p>
       <p>
        So, is this yogasm for real? Like the 
        <a href="http://youtu.be/jxB9mnKYr4M" target="_blank">
         ecstatic birth
        </a>
         trend, are orgasms yogas best kept secret? The experts weigh in, via
        <a href="http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2011/09/28/yogasms-on-the-rise-instructors-say-yoga-related-orgasms-are-real.html" target="_blank">
         Daily Beast
        </a>
        :
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
        <strong>
         The sexologist:
        </strong>
        Its another way that a woman can bring herself to orgasm without touching herself, says Dr. Jeffre TallTrees.
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
        <strong>
         The Yoga Teacher:
        </strong>
        The first time it happened to me I was in Sharon Gannons class at Jivamukti, and I was in forward bend, expresses Kelly Morris, a popular yoga instructor in New York  City. I was breathing and concentrating and suddenly, Whoa!
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
        <strong>
         The Lucky Student:
        </strong>
        I was in lotus pose, focusing on breathing and lifting the muscles of  my pelvic floor, she said. She wasnt prepared for what happened after  her instructor pressed his body against her back and synchronized his  breath with hers, lifting her ribs as she inhaled, and pushing down on  her thighs as she exhaled. I was tingling all over! she gushed.
       </p>
       <p>
        Mmmmyogasm or mula bandha contact high? Does it matter?
       </p>
       <p>
        The greater subject here may be how yoga affects, as in enhances, your sexual experience. No not just on the mat! Good grief. The thing is, the subject seems to be less taboo than it once was (read: those
        <em>
         wild and crazy
        </em>
        tantra antagonists) and freedom to talk about your hooha in relation to yoga is actually healthy and normal.
       </p>
       <p>
        Indeed. Marco Rojas, the same yoga teacher who implores us to feel the now (sometimes
        <a href="http://yogadork.com/news/blindfolded-yoga-we-dare-you-to-close-your-eyes/" target="_blank">
         blindfolded
        </a>
        ), is highlighted in the DB article as an experienced yoga and better-sex guide of sorts.
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         One woman who attended a class where we focused a lot on
         <em>
          mula bandha
         </em>
         came  back the next day and told me she was able to orgasm for the first time  with her boyfriend of six years, says Rojas, seeming slightly  flabbergasted himself. So yes, things can happen! To infinity and  beyond.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        Marco doesnt explicitly teach yoga for sex (yet) but weve taken his class and have heard enough interesting and
        <em>
         liberating
        </em>
        sounds to believe people are finding the pleasure in the pain. And its not just more fun for the ladies.
       </p>
       <p>
        Alan Finger, prestigious yogi and founder of ISHTA (Integrated Science of Hatha, Tantra and Ayurveda) Yoga tells us:
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         The man starts at a disadvantage because his orgasm is outwards, which  makes it briefer and shorter than a womans. But if he can engage
         <em>
          mula bandha
         </em>
         during sex, then he can prolong his climax and make the entire experience more powerful.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        Another reason for
        <a href="http://yogadork.com/news/mens-classes-broga-male-only-retreats-where-the-boys-are-in-yoga/" target="_blank">
         the dudes to practice
        </a>
        ?
       </p>
       <p>
