       <h2 class="headline" itemprop="name">
        <a href="http://neighborfoodblog.com/2014/09/fish-taco-salads-with-sriracha-lime-dressing.html" rel="bookmark">
         Fish Taco Salads with Sriracha Lime Dressing
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="byline small">
        <meta content="2014-09-04" itemprop="datePublished"/>
        <meta content="2015-03-07" itemprop="dateModified"/>
        <span class="post_date_intro">
         on
        </span>
        <span class="post_date" title="2014-09-04">
         September 4, 2014
        </span>
        <span class="post_author_intro">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="post_author" itemprop="author">
         Courtney
        </span>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="post_content" itemprop="articleBody">
       <p>
        In food, as in life, sometimes you dont get it right the first time. These Fish Taco Salad Bowls took a few times to get just right, but trust me when I tell you they were worth the wait!
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Fish Taco Salad Bowls with a Creamy Sriracha Lime Dressing!" class="aligncenter wp-image-6977 size-full" height="820" src="http://neighborfoodblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fish-taco-salad-2.jpg" title="Fish Taco Salads" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        I remember the first timeI heard about fish tacos. I was in college (I know, I know, I was late to the fish taco game) and the Mr. was telling meabout one of his favorite foods, the crunchy fish tacos at Del Taco. At the mention of a fish taco, Im pretty sure my nose curled up in disgust. A fish
        <em>
         taco?
        </em>
        I couldnt wrap my mind around the idea. We didnt have Del Taco in Ohio, and I wasnt particularly adventurous in my pre-college days so the whole fish taco thing was completely foreign to me.
       </p>
       <p>
        It was probably another year before I actually tried one. The Mr. and I were out on a date at a fun little Mexican restaurant, and he convinced me to try a bite of his beloved fish tacos. It was love at first bite. I probably ended up eating more of his tacos than my own meal. The combination of crispy fish, crunchy cabbage, and spicy creamy dressing was addicting.From that night on, I was sold.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="All the best parts of a fish taco packed in a healthy salad!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6979" height="820" src="http://neighborfoodblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fish-taco-salad-4.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Ive had these Fish Taco Salad Bowls on the brain for awhile now. I wanted to recreate my favorite tacos, but in a form that was healthy enough to enjoy every night of the week. It took me a few tries to get this just right. The first time was good, but the Mr. and I both agreed it was missing something. I originally tried this with a cilantro lime vinaigrette, but the dressing just wasnt enough to pull the salad together. The Mr. and I both decided a creamier dressing was in order.
       </p>
       <p>
        The second time around, I amped up the flavor of the fish and whipped up a quickcreamy dressing featuring the sweet heat of Sriracha, tangy lime, and cilantro. The dressing made all the difference. This salad went from good to AH-mazingwith just that simple swap.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="All the fixings for a crunchy, colorful Fish Taco Salad Bowl!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6980" height="820" src="http://neighborfoodblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fish-taco-salad.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        To call this salad LOADED would be an understatement. I made these when the garden was overflowing with produce, so I threw just about everything in there- cabbage, bell pepper, red onion, jalapeno, tomato, and avocado. The second time around I also added a sprinkle of queso fresco and a few crunchy tortilla strips. You can feel free to mix it up with your own favorites, or set out bowls of toppings and let everyone create their own ultimate fish taco salad.
       </p>
       <p>
        A quick note about the fish. Fish is, after all, kind of the star of this dish. I used mahi mahi fillets for this, but any firm white fish would be great. I like to let the fish marinate for 15 minutes or so while I chop and prep the other ingredients, but its not completely necessary. Lately, Ive started broiling fish, and I love this method for getting a nice browned exterior and keeping the interior nice and flaky. The fish is perfectly done in about 8 minutes, which means you can have these big ol salad bowls on your table in less than 30 minutes. Thats what I call winning the weeknight.
        <br/>
        <span id="more-6856">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Fish Taco Salad Bowls with a creamy Sriracha Lime Dressing!" class="aligncenter wp-image-6978 size-full" height="820" src="http://neighborfoodblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fish-taco-salad-3.jpg" title="Fish Taco Salad Bowls" width="600"/>
        <em>
         P.S. If you make this recipe (which I really, really hope you do!), just note the dressing wont look like these photos. The dressing in the picture is the original lime vinaigrette. I photographed these before changing the dressing, sowhat you see isthe lime vinaigrette in all its green glory.
        </em>
        <br/>
       </p>

          <span itemprop="recipeYield">
           4-6

        <div class="ERSIngredients">
         <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
          Ingredients
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           For the fish:
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           4 white fish filets (I used mahi mahi)
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 Tablespoon olive oil
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           Juice of 1 large lime or 2 small limes
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 teaspoon cumin
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 teaspoon chili powder
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           Kosher salt and pepper
          </li>
         </ul>
         <div class="ERSSectionHead">
          For the salad:
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           5 cups shredded romaine lettuce
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           3 cups shredded cabbage
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 sweet bell pepper, chopped
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           2 jalapenos, seeded and diced
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           2 tomatoes, chopped
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           2 avocados, sliced
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           4 ounces queso fresco, crumbled (optional)
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           Tortilla strips
          </li>
         </ul>
         <div class="ERSSectionHead">
          For the dressing:
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            cup greek yogurt
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            cup mayo
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 teaspoon sriracha
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            cup cilantro, chopped
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           Juice of 1 lime
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           2-4 Tablespoons milk
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           Salt
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           Pepper
          </li>
         </ul>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="ERSInstructions">
         <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
          Instructions
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSectionHead">
          For the fish:
         </div>
         <ol>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Place the fish filets in an oven safe baking dish lined with foil. Squeeze the lime juice and olive oil over top and sprinkle both sides evenly with cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Refrigerate for 20 minutes. When ready to cook, preheat the broiler. Place the filets under the broiler and broil for 7-8 minutes or until fish is no longer translucent and flakes easily.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <div class="ERSSectionHead">
          For the salad:
         </div>
         <ol>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Toss together the romaine and cabbage and divide it evenly between 4-6 bowls. Top each bowl with bell pepper, jalapenos, tomatoes, avocado, and queso fresco if desired.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <div class="ERSSectionHead">
          For the dressing:
         </div>
         <ol>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Whisk together the yogurt, mayo, sriracha, cilantro, and lime. Add milk until desired consistency is reached. Salt to taste.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           To serve, place a filet on top of each salad and cover with dressing. Garnish with tortilla strips, if desired.
          </li>
         </ol>
