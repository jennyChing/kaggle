        <h1 class="blog-headline">
         How To House-Sit Your Way Around The World
        </h1>
       </div>
       <table cellspacing="5" class="entry-info">
        <tr>
         <td class="sharetools" valign="middle">

       <div class="entry-detail">
        <div class="blogphoto photosize635 panoramic" style="width:635px;">
         <img alt="house sitting" class="" src="http://images.budgettravel.com/house-sitting-7312014-11028_panoramic.jpeg"/>
         <div class="entry-photo-caption caption">
          <p>
           Try house-sitting, a great way to see the world for less.
          </p>
          <span class="credit">
           (Courtesy
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jedavillabali/4976812818/" target="_blank">
            Bart Speelman/Flickr
           </a>
           )
          </span>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <strong>
           Youve heard about
          </strong>
          <strong>
           petsitting
          </strong>
          , but what about
          <em>
           house
          </em>
          sitting to save money while traveling? Dalene and Peter Heck are one Canadian couple who did just that: five years ago, they sold everything for the sake of travel, and started a website,
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/" target="_blank">
            Hecktic Travels
           </a>
          </strong>
          , about how they saved over $30,000 in accommodations costs by housesitting their way around the world.
         </p>
         <p>
          The basic idea is reciprocity: keep an eye on someone's home while they're away, and you get to stay in it for free. It's a winwin since the owners get the peace of mind in knowing their houses (and sometimes pets) are safe, and you get to take the price of accommodations out of your vacation budget. (You'll also save money on food, since your lodgings now include a kitchen.) Jobs can last anywhere from two weeks to six months and give new meaning to the term culture immersion.
         </p>
         <p>
          "The best part about the whole experience has been the ability to really dig in to a destination and get to understand the culture. We get to know people and visit places that regular tourists never would," said Dalene Heck.
         </p>
         <p>
          A number of websites, such as
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.housesittersamerica.com/" target="_blank">
            House Sitters America
           </a>
          </strong>
          ,
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.caretaker.org/" target="_blank">
            The Caretaker Gazette
           </a>
          </strong>
          ,
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.mindmyhouse.com/" target="_blank">
            Mind My House
           </a>
          </strong>
          , and
          <a href="http://www.trustedhousesitters.com/t/" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            TrustedHousesitters.com
           </strong>
          </a>
          among others, provide listings for a fee (ranging from $20 to $60 depending on the membership), but consider this an investment. The couple recommends creating an account on multiple websites to increase your chances of being chosen for a coveted housesit job.
         </p>
         <p>
          Planning ahead is the key, since it can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to fully flesh out the details of a contract. Housesitting hopefuls from the U.S. should remember to check Visa requirements for countries they plan to apply for, Dalene warns. "In 28 countries of Europe, Americans are only allowed 90 days total at a time, so the dream of bouncing around from housesit to housesit indefinitely isn't really an option there."
         </p>
         <p>
          How did they find out about this underground travel trend? During a trip to Ecuador in 2009, the two met an elderly American couple who had been housesitting around the world for a whopping 16 years! They inspired Dalene and Peter to apply for their first gig in British Columbia, Canada, and shortly afterward, the couple took on a sixmonth long stint in Roatan, Honduras. Over the last three years, they've also been housesitters in Ireland, Belgium, England, Spain, Turkey, and the United States.
         </p>
         <p>
          For more details about housesitting contracts, the best ways to find opportunities, and tips on how to be a good housesitter, purchase the couple's ebook, "
          <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/housesitting" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            How to Become a HouseSitter and See The World
           </strong>
          </a>
          " for $19.99 on their website.
         </p>
