              Wood to Glass
             </b>

          The strongest choice would be:
          <blockquote>
           <a href="/glue/silicone_ii.shtml">
            <font size="+1">
             General Electric Silicone II
            </font>
           </a>
           <br/>
          </blockquote>
          A less toxic alternative would be:
          <blockquote>
           <a href="/glue/weldbond.shtml">
            <font size="+1">
             Weldbond
            </font>
           </a>
           <br/>
          </blockquote>
          but it isn't as strong.
          <br/>
          The fastest and also less toxic recommendation would be:
          <blockquote>
           <a href="/glue/hotglue.shtml">
            <font size="+1">
             Hot Glue
            </font>
           </a>
           <br/>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <img src="/icon/tip.gif"/>
           Whenever you are gluing glass you must consider
the visibility of the
adhesive.   If the glass is translucent or transparent, you will want a glue that dries as
clear as possible.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="/icon/tip.gif"/>
           Before gluing any glass bond be sure that the glass is clean, free of any
oil (even from your fingers) and dry.
          </p>
