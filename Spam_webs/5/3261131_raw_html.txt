      <h1 class="blog_post_title">
       "I'll Eat You Last:" Salt Lake Acting Company Dishes Up Dirt on '80s Hollywood
      </h1>
      <div style="float: right;">
       <div style="float: right;">
        <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.mercolocal.com%2Fblog%2Fill-eat-you-last-salt-lake-acting-company-dishes-up-dirt-on-80s-hollywood.php&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=false&amp;width=200&amp;action=like&amp;font=arial&amp;colorscheme=light&amp;height=25" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:200px; height:25px;">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <div style="float: right;">
        <a class="twitter-share-button" data-count="horizontal" href="http://twitter.com/share">
         Tweet
        </a>
        <script src="http://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" type="text/javascript">
        </script>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="blog_post">
       <p dir="ltr">
        <span>
         <img alt="I'll Eat You Last Sue Mengers" height="467" src="http://www.mercolocal.com/uploads/files/2009/large/suemain.jpg" width="700"/>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        After another successful season of Saturdays Voyeur, the
        <a href="http://www.mercolocal.com/merchants/salt-lake-acting-company-events-salt-lake.php">
         Salt Lake Acting Company
        </a>
        will keep up the pace with yet another culturally significant, delightfully edgy production:
        <a href="http://www.saltlakeactingcompany.org/this-season/i-ll-eat-you-last">
         Ill Eat You Last: A Chat with Sue Mengers.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <span>
         We spoke with director Robin Wilks-Dunn, the brains behind SLACs interpretation of Tony award-winner John Logans script. We also spoke with lead actress Camille G Van Wagoner, whose vivacity and glowing presence will come in handy as she goes it alone on stage in this 90-minute one-woman production.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <span>
         Camille stars as Sue Mengers, the prolific Hollywood agent from the 70s-early 80s. The play is a snapshot of Mengers as she sits in her living room awaiting a call from Barbara Streisand, one of her biggest clients. Though it is a seemingly simple setup and story, the real complexity and intrigue comes from the character of Mengers herself.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <img alt="I'll Eat You Last Warning" height="450" src="http://www.mercolocal.com/uploads/files/2010/large/warnings.jpg" style="float: right;" width="300"/>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <span>
         She could be crass. She swore, she drank. She smoked pot constantly, she smoked cigarettes constantly, Camille said of her character. She was fierce, she was funny, she was absolutely unafraid. And she would take on anybody. While Camille laughs at her faults and quirks, she speaks warmly and with emotion about Mengers incomparable strengths that led her to be not only the most successful female agent, but the number one most successful agent in Hollywood at the time.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <span>
         But every starlet sees their own downfall, and Ill Eat You Last depicts those twilight years where Mengers began to glimpse her careers mortality. Camille quotes her line Ive seen the future, kiddies, and its not a lot of laughs with a morbid cackle. As the minutes pass in wait for this monumental phone call, Mengers character opens up to the audience, sharing childhood reflections as well as true stories concerning her dealings with movie stars and celebrities of the time (including clients like Cher, Michael Caine, and many others). Its hard to say whether or not this dirt, or these revealing stories are all completely true all were true by Sue Mengers, however, and they are now the stuffs of legend.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <span>
         Robin spoke excitedly of the upcoming production debuting in September. One thing weve tried to recreate is that 1981 [look]. So weve recreated her living room, weve recreated her look The shape of her nails, the shape and size of her glasses, the telephone she used. So we really want to create that little slice of life. SLAC has worked tirelessly in creating a set that pays proper tribute to the life and times of an affluent 80s Hollywood agent.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <span>
         When asked about the unique title of the play, Robin and Camille explained that it was based on a personal joke of Sue Mengers where she professed her plans to write an autobiography titled Ill Eat You Last: A Cannibal Love Story. When we asked whether Ill eat you last was a threat made out of love or aggression, Robin and Camille exchanged looks and replied Probably both.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <span>
         This is a perfect testament to the complexity of Mengers character. Spending an hour and a half with the Hollywood starlet is sure to be a treat for anyone who likes good storytelling by a truly colorful storyteller.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="docs-internal-guid-2a09b51f-5ff7-9374-2faf-d0e1c2998c95">
         This production begins on September 17th and will run through October 26th at their Chapel Theatre. Check back for deals through
         <a href="http://www.mercolocal.com/merchants/salt-lake-acting-company-events-salt-lake.php">
          Merco Local
         </a>
         , and buy your tickets on
         <a href="http://www.saltlakeactingcompany.org/this-season/i-ll-eat-you-last">
          SLACs website.
         </a>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>

       </p>
