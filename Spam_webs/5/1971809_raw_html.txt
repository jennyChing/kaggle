                           <p>
                            I have a wooden pencil box that has been a fixture on my desk since grade school. I believe my sister gave it to me... or perhaps I stole it from her; my memory is a little hazy when it comes to how things once hers came into my possession. A new pencil box instantly sends me back to crisp fall days dressed in even-crisper white shell-button blouses, itchy wool skirts and droopy knee socks. What does all this have to do with our cool little corduroy zippered pencil case with the cute front-buttoned pocket? Nothing really. But it was a sweet story, wasn't it?
                            <!--break-->
                           </p>
                           <p>
                            Our cool little corduroy zippered pencil case with the cute front-buttoned pocket is an ideal matching accessory for our
                            <a href="/projects/storage-solutions/back-school-perfect-slouchy-book-bag" title="BTW book bag">
                             Back to School: Perfect Slouchy Book Bag
                            </a>
                            . Make them both, then give them to your sister... you probably owe it to her for all that stuff you stole.
                           </p>

                            Fabric and Other Supplies

                           <p>

                             <em>

                             <a href="http://www.fatquartershop.com/store/stores_app/Browse_Item_Details.asp?Shopper_id=148883184351488&amp;Store_id=499&amp;page_id=23&amp;Item_ID=18379&amp;Parent_Ids=" target="_blank">

                             <br/>
                             NOTE:
                             <em>
                              If you don't have enough scraps on hand, you will need  yard of exterior fabric (based on a horizontal corduroy wale;  yard will work if you use a plain fabric) and  yard of lining fabric.

                             </a>

                           <h1>
                            Getting Started
                           </h1>
                           <ol>
                            <li>
                             From the exterior bag fabric (
                             <em>
                              Valori Wells' Nest Corduroy from Free Spirit Fabrics in Autumn Paisley in our sample)
                             </em>
                             , cut the following:
                             <br/>
                             NOTE:
                             <em>
                              We wanted the corduroy's wale to run horizontally for our pencil case, and so cut all our pieces accordingly.
                              <br/>
                             </em>
                             TWO 10" wide x 7" tall rectangles for case body.
                             <br/>
                             ONE 8" wide x 6" tall rectangle for the front pocket.
                             <br/>
                             ONE 4" x 4" square for the front pocket loop.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             From the lining fabric
                             <em>
                              (Moda Fabric's Bella Solids in Natural in our sample)
                             </em>
                             , cut TWO 10" x 7" rectangles.
                            </li>
                           </ol>
                           <h1>
                            At Your Sewing Machine &amp; Ironing Board
                           </h1>
                           <h2>
                            Making and placing pocket and loop
                           </h2>
                           <ol>
                            <li>
                             Find the 8" x 6" pocket piece.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Press under " along both 8" sides and one 6" end.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             On the opposite 6" end, make a simple 1" hem. To do this, press under ", then press under again 1", pin in place, and stitch close to the fold to finish.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Sew the button at the exact center of this hem line on the front of the pocket.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Find the 4" x 4" loop piece.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Fold it in half diagonally, right sides together, to form a triangle.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Stitch " away from the fold. Folding and stitching on the diagonal will make the loop bias-cut, which will allow it to curve without wrinkling.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-101-3_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-101-3.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Trim the seam allowance to " and turn right side out with a safety pin, loop turner or
                             <a href="/tips-resources/sewing-tips-tricks/quick-tip-tiny-tube-turning-hemostat" title="turning with a hemostat">
                              hemostat
                             </a>
                             . Press flat.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Place the pocket, right side up, on the right side on one 10" x 7" exterior piece. It should be positioned approximately 2" from each end and 1" from the top and bottom. Slip the folded loop in place and test its position with the button. Pin everything in place.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-102-4_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-102-4.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Adjust the loop as needed to best fit the button, also making sure the top edge of the pocket stays flat beneath the loop. When it fits just right, pin the ends in place.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-103-5_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-103-5.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Sew the loop ends in place with a line of stitching, going back and forth several times to secure.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             <a href="/glossary/edgestitch" title="edgestitch">
                              Edgestitch
                             </a>
                             the pocket in place along both sides and across the bottom, pivoting at the corners.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-104-6_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-104-6.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                           </ol>
                           <h2>
                            Inserting the zipper
                           </h2>
                           <ol>
                            <li>
                             The process outlined below is very similar to how we inserted the zipper in our
                             <a href="/projects/storage-solutions/wristlet" title="wristlet">
                              wristlet tutorial
                             </a>
                             . If you are new to inserting zippers, you might want to review this tutorial; there are several additional photos, which help you walk through the process.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Place the finished front of the case right side up on your work surface.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Lay your zipper upside down on top of it (teeth facing down on the right side of the fabric). The edge of the zipper tape should be even with the fabric's raw edge.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-105-7_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-105-7.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Lay a 10" x 7" piece of lining, right side down, on top of the front piece, sandwiching the zipper in between the two layers of fabric. As above, line up the top raw edge with the edge of the zipper tape. Pin all three layers together, being careful to pin through just the top of the zipper. You need to be able to open and close the zipper; you can't do that if you've pinned through the whole thing.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-106-8_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-106-8.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Fold back the lining to reveal the zipper, and zip it open about half way.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Fold the lining back down into position and take the assembled layers to your machine. Attach your zipper foot. Your needle should be in the left-most position.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Stitch as close to the zipper as the foot will allow, removing the pins as you sew.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-107-9_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-107-9.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Go slowly. When you get to the middle, where you can start to feel you're approaching the zipper pull, stop with your needle in the down position. Twist your fabric around slightly and open up the layers so you can access the zipper. Be gentle! Carefully close the zipper. Re-position your fabric and finish sewing to the end.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             When finished, your lining piece should be on one side of the zipper and your front piece on the other. Fold the lining and front piece wrong sides together, so the zipper stands straight up, and press.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-108-10_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-108-10.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Repeat to attach the other side of the zipper between the back exterior panel and the other piece of lining.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-109-11_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="282" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-109-11.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                           </ol>
                           <h2>
                            Final assembly
                           </h2>
                           <ol>
                            <li>
                             Find your 3" piece of ribbon. Fold it in half, wrong sides together.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Pin it to the right side of the front of the case just below the zipper tape, as shown below. The raw edges should be flush and the loop facing in. Machine baste it in place.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-111-12_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-111-12.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Un-zip the zipper about half way and fold the case right sides together.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Align the raw edges of the sides and the bottom, all layers, and pin in place.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Using a " seam allowance, stitch down both sides and across the bottom, pivoting at the corners. Clip the corners and turn right side out through the zippered opening.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Push out the corners and press well.
                            </li>
                           </ol>
                           <h3>
                            Optional French seam
                           </h3>
                           <ol>
                            <li>
                             The final assembly steps above work just dandy, but do leave a raw-edged seam allowance on the inside of your case. I made a
                             <a href="/tips-resources/sewing-tips-tricks/finishing-raw-seams-part-one--sewing-machine-finishes" title="finishing raw seams">
                              French Seam
                             </a>
                             to create a finished inside edge.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-112-13_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-112-13.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             After attaching the ribbon loop as described above, un-zip the zipper about half way and fold the case WRONG sides together.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Align the raw edges of the sides and the bottom, all layers, and pin in place.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Using a " seam allowance, stitch down both sides and across the bottom, pivoting at the corners.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-113-14_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-113-14.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Trim the seam allowance back very close to your stitching  to about 1/8".
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Turn wrong side out through the zippered opening.You'll need to carefully poke out the bottom corners with a blunt tool, like a large knitting needle or a chopstick, but don't be rough or you'll poke right through the seam.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Using a 3/8" seam allowance, stitch AGAIN down both sides and across the bottom, pivoting at each bottom corner and being careful tostart and finish as close as possible to the head and tail of your zipper.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-114-15_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-114-15.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Turn the case right side out again through the zippered opening. Poke out those bottom corners again... carefully. And you have a lovely French seam for a clean inside finish.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-photo-115-16_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="408" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-photo-115-16.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                            </li>
                           </ol>
                           <h2>
                            Zipper pull and carabiner
                           </h2>
                           <ol>
                            <li>
                             Clip the carabiner onto the ribbon loop.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Find your length of waxed cord.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Cut one end at a diagonal to make it thin enough to thread through the zipper pull.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                             Once through the pull, thread a bead on each end and make a knot on each side of each bead to hold them in place. Tie both ends in a knot against the zipper pull to secure.
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-116-17_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-Photo-116-17.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-photo-117-18_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="344" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-photo-117-18.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="500"/>
                             </a>
                             <br/>
                             <a class="colorbox" href="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-DSCN3942-19_b.jpg">
                              <img alt="Click to Enlarge" height="623" src="/sites/sewmk.mervideo.com/files/images/articles/0603-DSCN3942-19.jpg" style="margin-top: 0px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 15px; margin-right: 0px;" title="Click to Enlarge" width="599"/>

                            </li>
