       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Team selling  more prevalent, more important and as difficult as ever
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://salestrainingconnection.com/2014/03/31/team-selling-more-prevalent-more-important-and-as-difficult-as-ever/" rel="bookmark" title="6:49 am">
         <span class="entry-date">
          March 31, 2014
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://salestrainingconnection.com/author/dick-ruff/" title="View all posts by Richard Ruff">
          Richard Ruff
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <!-- Start Shareaholic LikeButtonSetTop Automatic -->
        <!-- End Shareaholic LikeButtonSetTop Automatic -->
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_3549" style="width: 160px">
         <a href="http://salestrainingconnection.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/stock-footage-female-businesswomen-presenting-business-plan-to-banking-executives-for-consideration.jpg">
          <img alt="Team selling" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-3549" height="150" src="http://salestrainingconnection.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/stock-footage-female-businesswomen-presenting-business-plan-to-banking-executives-for-consideration-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Team selling
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         In major accounts there are a number of different strategic situations where a
         <a href="http://salestrainingconnection.com/category/team-selling/">
          team sale
         </a>
         is preferred. The salesperson might want to bring along someone from top management either because of the purpose of the meeting or because of who is attending from the customers side.
        </p>
        <p>
         Another common situation is when the salesperson is engaged in a long sales cycle and given the calls agenda brings a person with expertise in a specific area such as: technical support, manufacturing, or customer service.
        </p>
        <p>
         Do these situations sound familiar? These types of situations are becoming increasingly more common because the customers are looking for their suppliers to provide the expertise need to creatively address their challenges.
        </p>
        <p>
         The growth in team selling comes not only from customer demands but also from the increased complexity of the sale, as well as, customers using teams. Customer teams often take the form of buying committees or purchasing teams.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #008080;">
           The classic trap in situations like these is not having a team attending the meeting. Instead, its just two people who happen to be in the same room at the same time.
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         To avoid this trap, weve found these eight best practices to be helpful:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Successful teams have a
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            compelling clear vision of the sales teams purpose
           </span>
          </strong>
          that is shared by everyone on the team.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            Everyone must believe there is benefit to the company  and to them personally
           </span>
          </strong>
           for working as a team.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          All of the roles to succeed are represented on the sales team and each team member is
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            clear about their role in the team and the expectations.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          The sales team members recognize talent alone does not guarantee team success.
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            Attitude is critical.
           </span>
          </strong>
          Positive attitudes can lead to a sales team performing at its peak; bad attitudes can rip a team apart.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          There is a
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            team leader.
           </span>
          </strong>
          Without a leader, all teams can lose their way.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Sales teams often struggle making poor decisions, no decisions, or decisions by edict  led by the loudest voice  because they have no processes in place
          <b>
           .
          </b>
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            Successful sales teams adopt or create their own team processes that guide how they operate.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Successful sales teams
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            adjust, adapt, and keep track.
           </span>
          </strong>
          They make effective strategic adjustments as the sales teams collective knowledge grows and insights are gained from the customer.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Last,
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            without trust, the team members dont believe they can count on each other. Sales teams like these cannot possibly achieve their shared purpose
           </span>
          </strong>
          . Trust is not something that automatically happens automatically.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Want to take a deeper dive into team selling? Take a look at this Team Selling
        </p>
