         As St. Patricks Day draws closer, well soon be searching for that green Shamrock shirt, scanning Pinterest for (easy) Corned Beef recipes and picking up a little whiskey or Guinness to get ready for the celebration.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sure we love to celebrate St. Patricks Day, but where did these traditions come from and how are we celebrating them today? Weve picked some of the most interesting St. Patricks Day facts and tidbits for you to share in your party conversations!
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are 15 of our favorite facts and tidbits about St. Patricks Day:
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          1. St. Patricks Day is an Irish national holiday with banks, stores, and businesses closing for the day.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/13/world/st-patricks-day-fast-facts/">
          http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/13/world/st-patricks-day-fast-facts/
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          2. The first St. Patricks Day celebration in the United States was held in Boston (1737).
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/13/world/st-patricks-day-fast-facts/">
          http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/13/world/st-patricks-day-fast-facts/
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Shamrocks are the national flower of Ireland" height="800" src="http://a1128.g.akamai.net/7/1128/497/0001/origin-d5.scene7.com/is/image/ProvideCommerce/3-8_StPatricksDay-Body_Shamrocks-3_blog140308" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          3. Shamrocks are the national flower/emblem of Ireland.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/13/world/st-patricks-day-fast-facts/">
          http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/13/world/st-patricks-day-fast-facts/
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          4. The color of St. Patricks Day was originally blue.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Wearing green has become a staple of St. Patricks Day, but the holiday was originally associated with the color blue. Its thought that the shift to green happened because of Irelands nickname The Emerald Isle, the green in the Irish flag and the shamrock, or clover. Green ribbons and shamrocks were worn as early as the 17th century.
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.livescience.com/27957-st-patricks-day-5-facts.html">
          http://www.livescience.com/27957-st-patricks-day-5-facts.html
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          5. Beer is one of the most widely consumed beverages on St. Patricks Day.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source: Every St. Patricks Party, ever. And this article:
         <a href="http://www.livescience.com/27957-st-patricks-day-5-facts.html">
          http://www.livescience.com/27957-st-patricks-day-5-facts.html
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          6.Legend says that each leaf of the clover has a meaning: Hope, Faith, Love and Luck.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.whsv.com/seasonal/misc/40129602.html">
          http://www.whsv.com/seasonal/misc/40129602.html
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Green Chicago River" height="800" src="http://a1128.g.akamai.net/7/1128/497/0001/origin-d5.scene7.com/is/image/ProvideCommerce/3-8_StPatricksDay-Body_Chicago-7_blog140308" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="800"/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          7. 1962 marked the first time Chicago dyed their river green for St Patricks Day.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://news.nationalgeographic.com/news/2012/03/120316-saint-patricks-day-2012-march-17-facts-ireland-irish-nation/">
          http://news.nationalgeographic.com/news/2012/03/120316-saint-patricks-day-2012-march-17-facts-ireland-irish-nation/
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="St. Patrick's Day Guiness Facts" height="800" src="http://a1128.g.akamai.net/7/1128/497/0001/origin-d5.scene7.com/is/image/ProvideCommerce/3-8_StPatricksDay-Body_Guiness-8_blog140308" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          8. Guiness is one of the most popular drinks on St. Patricks Day. Here are 34 recipes
         </span>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.buzzfeed.com/rachelysanders/eat-cook-with-guinness-stout-beer-recipes-st-patricks-day">
          http://www.buzzfeed.com/rachelysanders/eat-cook-with-guinness-stout-beer-recipes-st-patricks-day
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          9. Shamrock shakes are also very popular (and tasty!):
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://gothamist.com/2013/02/15/shamrock_shakes_return.php">
          http://gothamist.com/2013/02/15/shamrock_shakes_return.php
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          10. There are 34.7 million U.S. residents with Irish ancestry. This number is more than seven times the population of Ireland itself.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.history.com/topics/st-patricks-day/st-patricks-day-facts">
          http://www.history.com/topics/st-patricks-day/st-patricks-day-facts
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          11. The real St. Patrick wasnt Irish. He was born in Britain around A.D. 390 to an aristocratic Christian family.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://news.nationalgeographic.com/news/2012/03/120316-saint-patricks-day-2012-march-17-facts-ireland-irish-nation/">
          http://news.nationalgeographic.com/news/2012/03/120316-saint-patricks-day-2012-march-17-facts-ireland-irish-nation/
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          12. Your odds of finding a four-leaf clover are about 1 in 10,000.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.wsaw.com/seasonal/misc/40129602.html">
          http://www.wsaw.com/seasonal/misc/40129602.html
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          13. The worlds shortest St. Patricks Day parade is held in an Irish village. It lasts only 100 yards, between the villages two pubs.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.livescience.com/27957-st-patricks-day-5-facts.html">
          http://www.livescience.com/27957-st-patricks-day-5-facts.html
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          14. To celebrate St. Patricks Day, Chicago dyes the river green for a few hours.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/europe/ireland/5005127/St-Patricks-Day-Top-10-facts.html">
          http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/europe/ireland/5005127/St-Patricks-Day-Top-10-facts.html
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          15. St. Patrick never got canonized by a pope, making his saintly status somewhat questionable.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://www.biography.com/blog/st-patrick-little-known-facts">
          http://www.biography.com/blog/st-patrick-little-known-facts
         </a>
        </p>
