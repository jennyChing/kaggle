      <h2 class="pagetitle">
       Slow Cooker Gooey Chocolate Cake
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2682 pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/2011/11/slow-cooker-gooey-chocolate-cake.html" height="413" src="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Final1-620x413.jpg" title="Final" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Im meeting a new friend for lunch today. And an old friend for line-dancing tonight. And Im feeling rather spoiled about it all. I mean, moms shouldnt have TWO social events on the calendar for the same day, should we?
      </p>
      <p>
       Which brings me to the point of what moms should be doing anyway.
      </p>
      <p>
       For a long time, I measured myself by what I remember of my own mom. I remember my mom always being awake when I woke up in the morning. Terrycloth robe wrapped around her waist, soft pink slippers, a nice short haircut that wasnt too fussy but still showed terrific style. She was a mid-thirty something year-old for as long as I can remember. Old enough that Id never know what it was like to be as old as her.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span id="more-2671">
       </span>
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2673 pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/2011/11/slow-cooker-gooey-chocolate-cake.html" height="413" src="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/23-620x413.jpg" style="border-style: initial; border-color: initial;" title="2" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The odd thing is, Ive now reached the age in my life where I am exactly as old as she was. And I dont feel the least bit old at all. I was chatting it up with my sons piano teacher late Monday night. We were just two hilariously adorable young gals, catching up after a week of absence. I turned my head and looked at my son and realized, he didnt see it. He nudged his nose a little and I swear I caught his eyes roll and I was surprised to realize he doesnt find me the least bit young, or lively, or cool. Im just his mom. That nice old lady that greets him in the morning and tucks him in at night and buys him sneakers and makes him chocolate cake in a crockpot to greet the biting chill of an oncoming winter.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2675 pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/2011/11/slow-cooker-gooey-chocolate-cake.html" height="413" src="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/53-620x413.jpg" title="5" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Such is the fate of all moms. No matter how skinny we are, or how many abbreviations we can text, or how many Zumba moves we can shimmy through, to our kids were just the old, wise people in their lives.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2677 pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/2011/11/slow-cooker-gooey-chocolate-cake.html" height="413" src="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/72-620x413.jpg" title="7" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       And I realized then that everyday is really a bit like living in an imaginary world. Because Im not even old. And Im not really all that wise. Yet no matter how much I try to let my children in on these facts, Im older than they are and wiser than they are. So that makes me ancient. Except Im not, duh.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2678 pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/2011/11/slow-cooker-gooey-chocolate-cake.html" height="413" src="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/82-620x413.jpg" title="8" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Because, ancient people dont do lunch with new friends.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2679 pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/2011/11/slow-cooker-gooey-chocolate-cake.html" height="413" src="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/9-620x413.jpg" title="9" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       And they definitely dont join in a on rousing night session of line-dancing with old friends.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2680 pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/2011/11/slow-cooker-gooey-chocolate-cake.html" height="413" src="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/10-620x413.jpg" title="10" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       They dont make oatmeal in the morning in bare feet and spandex shorts. Terrycloth robes, maybe. But spandex? For nos.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2681 pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/2011/11/slow-cooker-gooey-chocolate-cake.html" height="413" src="http://www.cheekykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/111-620x413.jpg" title="11" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       And ancient people DEFINITELY do not fill their days with a slew of other snappy, bodacious activities. Like calling sisters to talk about shopping in the grocery store with crying babies. Or organizing closets in descending rainbow order. Or going on hot dates with husbands to Outback Steakhouse to order low-calorie foods. Or taking secret baths and reading archived Readers Digest articles. Or visiting the dermatologist for a random red bump on our eyelids.
      </p>
      <p>
       See?
      </p>
      <p>
       Im crazy hip. Spry and youthful. Totally mom inappropriate.
      </p>
      <p>
       Whatever are my kids thinking?
      </p>
      <p>

        </a>
       </p>
       <span class="item">
        <h2 class="fn">
         Slow Cooker Chocolate Cake
        </h2>
       </span>
       <h3 style="clear:left">
        Ingredients:
       </h3>
       <div class="ingredient">
        <p>
         4 eggs
         <br/>
         1 cup brown sugar
         <br/>
         3/4 cup cocoa powder
         <br/>
         1/2 cup melted butter
         <br/>
         1 packet chocolate pudding
         <br/>
         1 cup flour
         <br/>
         1/2 cup milk
         <br/>
         8 ounces plain Greek yogurt or sour cream
         <br/>
         1 1/2 cups chocolate chips
         <br/>
         1 teaspoon vanilla
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3 style="clear:left">
        Directions:
       </h3>
       <div class="instructions">
        <p>
         Spray a large crock pot with nonstick cooking spray. Plop all ingredients into the crockpot and mix together well combines. Pop the lid on the crockpot and cook for 4 hours on low. Check the cake by inserting a knife in the center. It should come out fairly clean. You'll know the cake is done when the edges begin separating from the sides of the pot. Your first cake you make might require some checking until you get to know how quickly your slowcooker actually cooks the cake.
        </p>
        <p>
         Turn the crockpot off and allow the cake to rest for up to 30 minutes. Slice and serve with ice cream or whipped cream or sprinkle with powdered sugar to garnish.
        </p>
       </div>
