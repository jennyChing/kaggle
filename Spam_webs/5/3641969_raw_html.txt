         <br/>
         Today, we are featuring my youngest son,
         <strong>
          <a href="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/kids-cooking">
           Alex
          </a>
          ,
         </strong>
         making a recipe he came up with 
         <strong>
          Triple Fruity Freeze
         </strong>
         , a healthy, frosty and refreshing drink, perfect for a hot summer day.
         <a href="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/07/kids-cooking-channel-video-healthy-mixed-berry-popsicles-for-cancer.html">
          Steve Jobs, the duck (the Kids Cooking Channel mascot)
         </a>
         , had a sip and loved it too!
        </p>
        <p>
         During the summer, its especially important to keep hydrated, so what better way than to use whole fresh fruits as a natural way to replenish your body with water and nutrients. Watermelon is about 92% water by weight, making it ideal for blending into a drink to rehydrate your body.
        </p>
        <p>
         My four boys love to play basketball two-on-two, so you can imagine how hot and sweaty theyve been lately after their games. Although I encourage them to drink cold water, having a refreshing icy fruity drink is a real treat.
        </p>
        <p>
         I often simply blend watermelon chunks, a squeeze of lime juice and ice cubes to make a
         <a href="http://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=382089031848270&amp;set=a.105215959535580.4079.103882016335641&amp;type=1">
          frosty watermelon drink
         </a>
         during the summer, but Alex came up with this combination today of watermelon, cherries and bananas and named it the
         <strong>
          Triple Fruity Freeze
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         I love it when kids use their creative juices to concoct their own recipes, especially when theyre healthy (and even healthier than my own concoction). This frosty treat is full of
         <a href="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/07/warm-braised-beet-salad-with-beet-greens-and-yogurt-dressing-power-food.html">
          Power Foods
         </a>
         !
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/07/triple-fruity-freeze-recipe-video.html/triplefruityfreeze-2" rel="attachment wp-att-7674">
          <img alt="Triple Fruity Freeze" class="aligncenter wp-image-7674 pib-hover-img" data-pin-description="Kids Cooking Channel Video  Triple Fruity Freeze Recipe ~ http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com" data-pin-url="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/07/triple-fruity-freeze-recipe-video.html" height="444" src="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/TripleFruityFreeze1.jpg" title="Triple Fruity Freeze" width="518"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I had never really thought watermelon was that nutritious, but it is an excellent source of
         <strong>
          vitamin C
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          vitamin A
         </strong>
         , and a good source of
         <strong>
          potassium
         </strong>
         and
         <strong>
          magnesium
         </strong>
         . Watermelon is also a very concentrated source of the antioxidant
         <strong>
          lycopene
         </strong>
         , which is protective again
         <strong>
         </strong>
         prostate cancer, breast cancer, endometrial cancer, lung cancer and colorectal cancers. In addition,
         <strong>
          watermelon
         </strong>
         is exceptionally high in an amino acid called
         <strong>
          citrulline
         </strong>
         which our bodies use to make another amino acid, arginine, which helps remove ammonia from the body.
        </p>
        <p>
         This
         <strong>
          Triple Fruity Freeze
         </strong>
         also contains
         <a href="http://www.livestrong.com/article/523212-how-do-sweet-raw-cherries-benefit-your-body/">
          brain-boosting
          <strong>
           cherries
          </strong>
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.webmd.com/diet/features/eat-smart-healthier-brain?page=2">
          <strong>
           pomegranate
          </strong>
          juice
         </a>
         , which are full of antioxidants, and
         <strong>
          bananas
         </strong>
         which are rich in vitamin C, B6, manganese, fiber, potassium, and magnesium. Whats not to like about a frosty treat that not only rehydrates your body, but is full of nutrients and helps protect brain health. Drink up!
        </p>
        <p>
         If youd like to see some fun videos that Alex has made (all by himself) featuring his duck, Steve Jobs, check out
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pL3hl0IjLpQ&amp;feature=plcp">
          Steve Jobs Life Cycle (Fictional Story), Steve Jobs the Duck

          The Magical Steve Jobs Commercial

          8 Ducks of Luck Movie Trailer
         </a>
         . Can you tell that Alex likes ducks?
        </p>
