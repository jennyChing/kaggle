           <h2>
            Coalition kills second renewable project in Geelong /Surf Coast region
           </h2>
           <div class="byline">
            Posted by
            <span class="linked-signup-name">
             Phil Evans
            </span>
            <span class="pc">
             559sc
            </span>
            on February 20, 2014
           </div>
          </div>
          <div id="content">
           <div class="intro" id="intro">
            <div class="content">
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="http://melbourne.foe.org.au/files/images/frontpage/geothermal-energy.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 5px; float: left;"/>
              Plans for a 12MW geothermal energy installation to power the industrial city of Geelong and Surf Coast region has been scrapped after the Napthine Government withdrew a $25 million grant for the project.
             </p>
             <div class="content">
              <p>
               Plans for a 12MW geothermal energy installation to power the
               <br/>
               industrial city of Geelong and Surf Coast region has been scrapped after
               <br/>
               the Napthine Government
               <a href="http://reneweconomy.com.au/2013/geelong-geothermal-project-cancelled-after-victoria-halts-funding-94788">
                withdrew
               </a>
               a $25 million grant for the project.
              </p>
              <p>
               So, what does the move tell Victorians about the Napthine governments energy policy?
              </p>
              <p>
               The Napthine government is starting to look like its anti-renewable
               <br/>
               energy. Its decision to pull funding from a geothermal energy project
               <br/>
               comes on top of Ted Baillieus anti-wind farm laws which have
               <a href="http://yes2renewables.org/2012/06/15/anti-windfarm-laws-what-are-they-costing-victoria/">
                cost the state
               </a>
               thousands of jobs and hundreds of millions worth of investment.
              </p>
              <p>
               The scrapped funding for a geothermal power station is the second
               <br/>
               renewable energy project to be killed off in the Geelong region since
               <br/>
               the Coalition took office.
               <span id="more-5817">
               </span>
              </p>
              <p>
               In 2011, the
               <a href="http://sceg.org.au/">
                Surf Coast Energy Group
               </a>
               s dream of a community-owned wind farm modelled on the
               <a href="http://reneweconomy.com.au/2012/hepburn-wind-wins-global-industry-gong-36109">
                award-winning Hepburn Wind farm
               </a>
               was
               <a href="http://surfcoast.starcommunity.com.au/indy/2011-05-30/wind-farm-dream-dies/">
                killed
               </a>
               when then Premier Ted Baillieu flagged wind farms would be
               <a href="http://yes2renewables.org/2011/09/19/maps-show-where-you-cant-build-a-wind-farm-in-victoria/">
                banned
               </a>
               around Geelong, along the coast and Bellarine Peninsula.
              </p>
              <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5814" style="width: 604px;">
               <img alt="nogozones" class="size-large wp-image-5814" src="http://yes2renewables.files.wordpress.com/2013/10/nogozones.jpg?w=594&amp;h=411"/>
               <p class="wp-caption-text">
                Arbitrarily established no-go zones ban wind farms in the Geelong, Bellarine Peninsula and Surf Coast area.
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               The governments record on helping to get renewable energy projects off the ground in the region is poor.
              </p>
              <p>
               The localmember of parliament Andrew Katos (Liberal party)
               <br/>
               commitment to the growth of renewable energy in the region is
               <br/>
               questionable. Many of his constituents must be wondering whether Mr
               <br/>
               Katos even went in to bat for local renewable energy projects.
              </p>
              <p>
               The decision to withdraw funding will disappoint local groups who support renewable energy, such as
               <a href="http://angleseaairaction.org/">
                Surf Coast Air Action
               </a>
               .
               <br/>
               SCAA are campaigning to address polluting emissions which risk the
               <br/>
               health of residents of Anglesea. The Alcoa coal mine and power plant
               <br/>
               located just 2 kilometres from the town.
              </p>
              <p>
               The geothermal energy facility which was to be located at the Holcim
               <br/>
               Ltd Moriac quarry, 11km north west of Anglesea, would have emitted only
               <br/>
               water vapour.
              </p>
              <p>
               <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                <strong>
                 TAKE ACTION
                </strong>
               </span>
              </p>
              <p>
               The renewable energy projects which have been killed off by Coalition
               <br/>
               government policies would have created local jobs, investment, and
               <br/>
               helped power Geelong without polluting emissions.
              </p>
              <p>
               If youre a local and thinkMr Katos should stand up for renewable energy in the Coalition party room, nows the time to
               <strong>
                send a message
               </strong>
               :
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Send Andrew Katos MP an email,
                <a href="mailto:andrew.katos@parliament.vic.gov.au">
                 andrew.katos@parliament.vic.gov.au
                </a>
               </li>
               <li>
                Call his electorate office, (03) 5244 2288.
               </li>
               <li>
                Leave a message on Mr Katos
                <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/I-support-Andrew-Katos-for-South-Barwon/297161751379" target="_blank">
                 Facebook page
                </a>
                .
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
