                <p>
                 <a href="http://now.snopes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/okines.jpg">
                  <img alt="okines" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1542" height="434" src="http://now.snopes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/okines.jpg" width="772"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 Progeria awareness advocate Hayley Okines
                 <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/uk-england-sussex-32174131" target="_blank">
                  died
                 </a>
                 on 2 April 2015, her mother confirmed on Facebook.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Okines, 17, was initially not expected to live past the age of 13 due to a genetic disorder known asHutchinson-Gilford Progeria Syndrome.The very rare disease strikes just one in four million people, with few available treatments to extend the lifespan of those afflicted.
                </p>
                <p>
                 On Facebook, Okines mother Kerry published a status update on 2 April 2015:
                </p>
                <blockquote>
                 <p>
                  My baby girl has gone somewhere better. She took her last breath in my arms at 9.39pm
                 </p>
                </blockquote>
                <p>
                 The Progeria Research Foundation expressed condolences a few hours later:
                </p>
                <div class="fb-post" data-href="https://www.facebook.com/ProgeriaResearch/posts/1085461001486351:0" data-width="500">
                 <div class="fb-xfbml-parse-ignore">
                  <blockquote cite="https://www.facebook.com/ProgeriaResearch/posts/1085461001486351:0">
                   <p>
                    The entire Progeria family mourns together with many as we say goodbye to Hayley Okines, our smart, beautiful and
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Posted by
                    <a href="https://www.facebook.com/ProgeriaResearch">
                     Progeria Research Foundation
                    </a>
                    on
                    <a href="https://www.facebook.com/ProgeriaResearch/posts/1085461001486351:0">
                     Thursday, April 2, 2015
                    </a>
                   </p>
                  </blockquote>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 Accent Press, the publisher that distributed Okines book, shared a video memorial:
                </p>
                <p>
                 <br/>
                 <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/goibmzRAEAk" width="560">
                 </iframe>
                </p>
                <p>
                 Okines died ofpneumoniacomplications.
                </p>
