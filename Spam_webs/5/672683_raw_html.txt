         <h2>
          Recipes Using Bean Flour
         </h2>
         <p>
          Today we are exploring a few dishes you can make and use
          <a href="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/making-and-using-bean-flour.html" target="_blank">
           homemade bean flour
          </a>
          as the thickener. (You can read all about
          <a href="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/making-and-using-bean-flour.html" target="_blank">
           making homemade bean flour
          </a>
          here, how to do it and why you might want to!) They had originally called for flour as the thickener, but the bean flour is easily substituted and provides a healthier alternative. (and gluten free!) The bean flour that is used as a thickener is from white type beans (great northern or lima beans). There is no bean taste to adding in these as thickeners and it is never lumpy, but rather very smooth. These recipes are both made in the crockpot, but probably could be made on the stove top. I just generally like putting everything together first thing in the morning and then forgetting about it until supper time.
         </p>

         <h3>
          <em>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Crockpot Chicken Rice Soup
           </span>
          </em>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What You Need:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           6 T. butter
          </li>
          <li>
           3 T. diced onion
          </li>
          <li>
           7 c. chicken broth
          </li>
          <li>
           2 c. cooked, diced chicken (or turkey)
          </li>
          <li>
           1 c. brown rice or wild rice blend
          </li>
          <li>
           2 c. diced carrots
          </li>
          <li>
           1/3 c. white bean flour
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Place in crock pot on low for 4 -6 hours or until the vegetables are tender. Turn to "keep warm" setting and add in:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           1 c. milk (can add in more, depending on consistency that you like)
          </li>
          <li>
           2 c. shredded cheese
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Serve when cheese is melted.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="soup2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41657" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/soup2.jpg" height="337" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="450"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="soup2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41657" height="337" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/soup2.jpg" width="450"/>
         </noscript>
         <h3>
          <em>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Crockpot Cheeseburger Soup
           </span>
          </em>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What You Need:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           1 lb. browned hamburger
          </li>
          <li>
           3/4 c. diced onion
          </li>
          <li>
           3/4 c. shredded carrots
          </li>
          <li>
           3/4 c. diced celery
          </li>
          <li>
           1 t. basil
          </li>
          <li>
           1/4 t. parsley
          </li>
          <li>
           4 T. butter
          </li>
          <li>
           4 c. chicken broth
          </li>
          <li>
           4 c. diced potatoes
          </li>
          <li>
           1/4 c. white bean flour
          </li>
          <li>
           1/4 t. pepper
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Place in crockpot and cook on low for 4-6 hours or until all vegetables are tender.
          <br/>
          Turn to "keep warm" setting and add in:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           8 ounces shredded cheese
          </li>
          <li>
           1 1/2 c. milk (can add in more, depending on the consistency you like)
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Serve after cheese has melted. Can add in 1/4 c. sour cream right before serving.
         </p>
         <p>
          I have also used the pinto bean flour in making a taco dip. I used the recipe from the
          <a href="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/making-and-using-bean-flour.html" title="Making and Using Bean Flour">
           Making and Using Bean Flour post
          </a>
          for making the refried bean mixture. I added in
          <a href="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/recipe-for-taco-seasoning.html" target="_blank" title="Taco Seasoning Recipe">
           taco seasoning
          </a>
          to taste. Spread the cooled bean/spice mixture onto a large platter. Add a layer of sour cream and a layer of salsa. Shred up lettuce and cheese to add on top. Serve with tortilla chips.
         </p>
         <p>
          I hope this provides you with some inspiration for using bean flours. They are an excellent substitute for flour used as a thickener in soups or gravies. If you have questions about using beans or the bean flour, please let us know. We are devoting this month to beans and rice and would love your input or if you have suggestions on what you would like covered.
         </p>
