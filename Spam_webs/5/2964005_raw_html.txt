         <img alt="These smashed potatoes are a delicious, easy side dish that goes with lots of different meals.         <p>
         Smashed potatoes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive been seeing these all over Pinterest lately. I love all things potato (Irish girl here), so Im always excited to try a new spin on this humble veggie.
        </p>
        <p>
         My brother and sister-in-law made a version of these over the holidays, and I could have eaten the whole platter. They were so good.
        </p>
        <p>
         I put together this easy recipe for smashed potatoes to make a side dish that works with many types of meals. You can season them with any fresh herbs you like, and adjust the salt and onion powder to suit your tastes. Cook them a little longer if you want them extra crispy. You really cant mess these up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="These smashed potatoes are a delicious, easy side dish that goes with lots of different meals. Try this healthy recipe to dress up your potatoes." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15567" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/realfoodrealdeals.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/smashed-potatoes-600.jpg?resize=600%2C600"/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <link href="http://realfoodrealdeals.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Smashed-Potatoes-rfrd.jpg" itemprop="image"/>
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Smashed Potatoes
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://realfoodrealdeals.com/easyrecipe-print/15564-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            8
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 lbs. small red or white potatoes (approx. 2-inch diameter)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tbs. olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. onion powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 sprigs rosemary
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 scallions, chopped
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat the oven to 425 degrees.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Boil the potatoes until fork-tender. Transfer the cooked potatoes to 2 baking sheets. You may want to let them cool a bit, then smash each potato gently with a spatula to a thickness of about  inch.
           </li>
           <li class="ERSInstructionImage">
            <img alt="Smashed potatoes spatula" class="aligncenter size-full" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/realfoodrealdeals.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/smashed-potatoes-smashing-rfrd.jpg?resize=600%2C450" title="smashed potatoes smashing rfrd"/>
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a small bowl, stir together the salt and onion powder. Drizzle the olive oil over the potatoes, then sprinkle half the salt mixture over the potatoes (save the other half for later). Break apart the rosemary from its stalk and sprinkle it over the potatoes.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake the potatoes for 10 minutes on each side. When you flip them, sprinkle the remaining salt/onion powder mixture over them.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Transfer the roasted potatoes to a serving dish. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and serve warm.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
