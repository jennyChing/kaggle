      <div class="article-description">
       <p>
        The nation watched in horror this week as riots broke out in the streets of Baltimore following a funeral service for Freddie Gray, a 25-year-old African-American man who died after suffering a spinal cord injury when he was taken into police custody earlier this month.
       </p>
       <p>
        The damage is done, but the unrest tells a bigger story.Virtually no show in the history oftelevision has better captured the complicated, desperate, broken, utterly entrenched, and down right dirty realities of Baltimore than HBO's "The Wire."
       </p>
       <p>
        <span>
         As William "Bunk" Moreland on "The Wire,"
        </span>
        <strong>
         Wendell Pierce
        </strong>
        <span>
         lived and worked in Baltimore for years.Click on the audio player above to hear Piercereflecton the fictionalized world of "The Wire," and the real-life problems facing the city today.
        </span>
       </p>
