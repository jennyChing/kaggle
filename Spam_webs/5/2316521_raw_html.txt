             <h2 style="text-align: left;">
              Tweeting Fortune 500 CEO's, Thought Leaders and Entrepreneurs
             </h2>
             <img alt="" class="mceHSmore" data-mce-resize="false" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7" style="float: left;"/>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              As a social platform,
              <a href="/5-day-twitter-kickstart-kit" target="_blank">
               Twitter still remains the number one network for sharing
              </a>
              .
              <img align="right" alt="twitter_nashville" src="http://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/160334/file-832196155-jpg/twitter_nashville.jpg" style="width: 275px; float: right;" width="275"/>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              According to a study done by Umpf, for every 100M users on social networks, 197.3 Twitter users were likely to share an online story. This is a huge number compared to just 41.8 on Facebook, 15.2 on LinkedIn, and 6 on Google+.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              With over 500M accounts, 142M of which are in the United States, using Twitter in a social media strategy is still good business.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              However, with social media making such a huge impact on the way businesses are marketing and in how people make buying decisions, it is also surprising to learn that only 1.8% of the CEOs of Fortune 500 companies have a presence on Twitter.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              We took a look at the Fortune 500 CEOs on Twitter, as well as a few of the top influencers on Twitter and have compiled a list of the people you need to be following.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              Why?
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              These CEOs, Presidents, Entrepreneurs, Business Owners and Thought Leaders all have the same characteristics that you do. They have drive, skill, education, ideas and knowledge about starting up, running and keeping a business going.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
