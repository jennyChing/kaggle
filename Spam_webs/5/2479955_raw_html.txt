       <h1 itemprop="headline name">
        Turkey: inside the new global economic growth machine
       </h1>
       <h2 itemprop="alternativeHeadline description">
        The finalists in the 2014 HSBC Global Connections competition discovered an 
  economic giant in the making when they visited Istanbul, says James Hurley
       </h2>

           <sup>

         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           Being greeted by a wall of traffic as you arrive in a new city for the first time normally spells frustration for the weary traveller. For Edward Naylor, however, sluggish progress through Istanbuls packed roads proved as welcome a sight as any of the citys famous attractions.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           Mr Naylor, chief executive of the South Yorkshire manufacturer
           <a href="http://www.naylor.co.uk/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Naylor Industries
           </a>
           , is one of 33 entrepreneurs and executives from some of Britains fastest growing mid-sized businesses who travelled to Turkeys largest city this month to network, and meet local businesses and trading experts to help them sell more overseas.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           One of Naylor Industries products allows underground pipes to be laid without digging up roads. Seeing the vastness of Istanbul has left the family companys fourth-generation chief executive in no doubt about the size of the opportunity Turkey presents.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           What weve got is so on the money for Istanbul, where youve got significant population growth and heavy traffic flow  they need infrastructure but dont want to be digging up all the roads to develop it, he says.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class=" related_links_inline" id="tmg-related-links">

         <div class="body">
          <p>
           On this trip, Turkey has moved in my own mind from a market that I hadnt focused on to one I need to be putting real time into. The opportunities are here, now I just need the right contacts.
          </p>
          <p>
           The trade mission has already helped Naylor get the ball rolling in that respect. It was organised by HSBC for the regional finalists of the banks
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sponsored/business/global-connections/" target="_blank">
            Global Connections
           </a>
           competition to allow the companies to meet successful local business leaders and their advisers;
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sponsored/business/global-connections/10902890/Turkey-business-lessons-Istanbul.html">
            learn about the economic and cultural background of Turkey
           </a>
           and its surrounding regions; and visit local companies to get practical advice on how to export.
          </p>
          <p>
           Why Turkey? Martin Spurling, chief executive of HSBC Turkey, admits that he was frequently asked what Id done wrong when he first took the position in the country.
          </p>
          <p>
           While he knew the cynics were wrong to underestimate Turkey, even Mr Spurling has been surprised by the pace of growth and economic progress in the frequently misunderstood market. Turkey is both fiercely proud and grossly underestimated, he says.
          </p>
          <p>
           Turkey has long been identified as an emerging economic giant, one of the so-called Mint countries along with Mexico, Indonesia and Nigeria which could go on to replicate the growth over the past decade enjoyed by the Brics  Brazil, Russia, India and China.
          </p>
          <p>
           Yet to call Turkey, which saw the rise of a middle class back in the Eighties, an emerging market is unfair, Mr Spurling believes. Turkey is already Europes sixth and the worlds 16th largest economy. Of its population of 77 million, 27.9 million are part of the workforce.
          </p>
          <p>
           In 2000, average income per head in Turkey was 2,400. By 2012 that had grown to 6,300 and by 2050 it is expected to leap to 28,700, according to figures from the IMF and Goldman Sachs. The OECD believes that it will be the second-fastest growing market in the world by 2020.
          </p>
          <p>

          <p>
           Once the centre of the Ottoman empire, Turkey has for centuries exploited its location on the historic Silk Road, straddling Europe and Asia. Its status as a trading hub between regional markets in the Middle East and Africa as well as Europe and Asia is growing, and should make the country of particular interest to British exporters.
           <span style="float:right; display: inline-block; background: url(http://s.telegraph.co.uk/sponsored/pullquote/v1/img/quote-mark.png) no-repeat 0 20px transparent; width:210px; margin: 10px 20px 0 20px; padding: 20px 0; font: Georgia, Times, serif; font-size:16px; font-style:italic; text-indent:25px; border-top: 1px solid #aeaaa9; border-bottom: 1px solid #aeaaa9;">
            Turkey has arrived on the international stage and is on the cusp of something. Its empire could be about to be reborn by a takeover of the trading world
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           However, its a lot more than a gateway or refreshment stop on a trading route, Mr Spurling notes. Turkey is on the cusp of something. Turkeys empire could be about to be reborn  not them invading left, right and centre, but by starting to take over the trading world. Turkey has transformed itself from the sick man of Europe to 10 years of [relative] stability and consistency  its arrived on the international stage.
          </p>
          <p>
           Melis Metiner, Turkey economist with HSBC in Istanbul, says that the country has four key strengths: a young and rapidly growing population; a large consumer economy; low debt, encompassing households, strong banks and good fiscal discipline; and its geographical position.
          </p>
          <p>
           Modern exploitation of its singular trading location is perhaps best shown by the fact that Turkish Airlines serves more cities than any other air operator in the world.
          </p>
          <p>
           Trade between the UK and Turkey is growing rapidly and has increased by about 40pc since 2009. It is now worth more than $11bn (6.54bn) a year.
          </p>
          <p>
           UK Trade &amp; Investment (UKTI) says that sectors such as infrastructure, oil and gas, renewable energy and retail offer particular opportunities for British firms willing to come to Turkey. However, UKTIs Sayed Zubair Alam Shah says that while Turkish companies want to work with British businesses, they dont see them enough.
          </p>
          <p>
           That may be down to some misapprehensions about Turkey as a market, he says. We are playing catch-up with Germans and others [in Turkey]. Britain as a brand is strong here, but our view of each other needs modernising.
          </p>
          <p>
           While the countrys attempts to join the European Union have stalled  a fact thats been welcomed by many in the countrys business community  trade deals mean that working with a Turkish business need not feel more complex for a British company than working with a partner in Europes single market.
          </p>
          <p>
           Its a market with a modern business environment and modern practices, says Mr Shah. For example, intellectual property rules are similar to the ones that youll find in the UK.
          </p>
          <p>
           Inevitably, there are numerous challenges of which businesses need to be mindful.
          </p>
          <p>
           Politics and business are intertwined in Turkey and can be something of a minefield, Mr Shah warns.
          </p>
          <p>
           The country is currently facing its most unstable political period since Prime Minister Recep Tayyip Erdogan and his AK Party came to power in 2002. Its first direct presidential elections are due to take place in August, while a general election is scheduled for next year.
          </p>
          <p>
           Voting will be held in the wake of street protests in Istanbul and a corruption scandal which led to the resignation of four cabinet members.
          </p>
          <p>
           The recent mining disaster which claimed the lives of more than 300 workers in the west of the country has prompted a debate not only about safety standards in the sector, but also over long-term energy security. Turkey has the worlds second highest energy imports bill, after China, and the cost of energy is one of the most significant issues facing the countrys businesses.
          </p>
          <p>
           Other challenges include inflation and sudden changes to legislation and regulations without warning.
          </p>
          <p>
           Reha Demirdag, chief executive of Reha Textile, an Istanbul-based textile manufacturer, has seen his business thrive in recent years, partly thanks to a wise move into precision manufacturing of performance fabrics as a defence against the rise of low-cost textile manufacturing in the Far East.
          </p>
          <p>
           All of the companys sales are exports since the goods it produces cost too much for the local market. Last year, his settlement with staff was 13pc (inflation was around 8pc), but the move was offset by depreciation of the Turkish lira.
          </p>
          <p>
           When asked what the biggest challenge his 21-year-old business faces, his response is telling. Politics, he says. Were positioned between the Middle East and Europe. That means that at any time, something big can happen.
          </p>
          <p>
           Yet for British companies such as Naylor Industries, which are willing to treat Turkey as a unique market that needs close attention, the rewards are there.
          </p>
          <p>
           The adaptability and SME spirit that Mr Spurling says define Turkish industry may even suit nimble mid-sized exporters over their larger counterparts.
          </p>
          <p>
           When I arrived in Turkey and said, lets put in a five-year plan, my colleagues looked at me like I was from Mars. They said, the whole world in Turkey changes in six months. Turkey has no original word for strategy. You need to be able to adapt.
          </p>
          <p>
           Indeed, local small and medium-sized companies dominate the corporate landscape  very few multinational companies lead their sectors in Turkey.
          </p>
          <p>
           Chris Gaunt, chairman of the British Chamber of Commerce in Turkey, says this should be music to the ears of ambitious British exporters such as the Global Connections finalists.
          </p>
          <p>
           No, the legal system is not perfect and politically there are issues, he admits, but Turkey is far more advanced than many emerging markets.
          </p>
          <p>
           If you want to trade in your comfort zone, in developed markets, what growth can you see? You need to trade in emerging markets and Turkey is the place to start.
          </p>

        /*
         Read Next Widget

        var readNextWidget = (function(){

            var settings = {
                articleImage: "",
                articleTitle: "",
                articleUrl: "#",
                articleId: "",
                currentArticleId: "",
                articleList: [],
                articleListToSkip: [],
                readNextCookieName: "tmg_read_next",
                openButton: "#readNextOpen a",
                closeButton: "#closeBtn a",
                articleContainer: "#readNextArticleContainer",
                widgetContainer: "#readNextWidget",
                displayThumbnail: false,
                panelOpen: false,
                autoOpen: true
            };

            var renderTemplate = function() {
                var template = '&lt;div id="readNextWidget" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextOpen"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div id="readNextOpenInner"&gt;&lt;a href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Next Story&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="icn"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextArticleContainer" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticle"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleInnerWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;p id="readNextLabel"&gt;MOST VIEWED IN NEWS&lt;/p&gt;';
                        
				if(settings.articleImage &amp;&amp; settings.articleImage!="null" &amp;&amp; settings.displayThumbnail){
					template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageThumbnail" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;img src="'+ settings.articleImage +'"/&gt;&lt;/a&gt;';
				}
                        
				template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageTitle" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;' + settings.articleTitle + '&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="closeBtn"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a href="#"&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;iframe style="border:none;position:absolute;top:0;left:0; height:100%;width:100%;z-index:-1;" id="iefix" src="about:blank"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;' + //fix for IE bug
                        '&lt;/div&gt;';
                return template;
            };


            var init = function(currentArticleId, articleList, displayThumbnail) {

                settings.articleList = articleList;
                settings.currentArticleId = currentArticleId;
                settings.displayThumbnail = displayThumbnail;

                settings.articleListToSkip = unescape($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName)).split(',');

                if ($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName) != null) {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    updateCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                } else {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    createCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                }


                if (settings.articleId != "") {

                    $(renderTemplate()).appendTo("body");

                    $("#nextArticleWrapper a, #readNextOpenInner a").click(function () {
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_headline', 'version_1']);

                        getNavPos($(this).attr("href"), "wayfarerReadNext");
                    });

                    bindUIActions();

                    var displayed = false;

                    setTimeout(function () {
                        if (!displayed) {
                            displayed == true;
                            readNextWidget.display();
                        }

                    }, 10000);

                    $(window).scroll(function () {
                        if (isScrolledIntoView("#tmg-related-links")) {
                            if (!displayed) {
                                displayed == true;
                                readNextWidget.display();
                            }
                        }

                        if(isScrolledIntoView("#shareBottom") || isScrolledIntoView("#newGigyaShareBottom")) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }

                        if($(window).scrollTop() + $(window).height() == $(document).height()) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }
                    });
                }
            };

            var display = function () {
                $(settings.widgetContainer).fadeIn(2000, function () {
                    //animation complete
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_closed', 'version_1']);
                });
            };

            var bindUIActions = function () {
                $(settings.openButton).mouseenter(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'hover_closed', 'version_1']);
                    openArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.widgetContainer).mouseleave(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    closeArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.closeButton).click(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_close', 'version_1']);
                    updateCookie(settings.articleId, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    closeArticleContainer();
                    settings.autoOpen = false;
                });
            };

            var openArticleContainer = function () {
                if (!settings.panelOpen) {
                	$("#readNextArticleContainer").show();//show/hide because height is calculated incorrectly for elements with display:none
                    $("#readNextArticleContainer").hide();
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_open', 'version_1']);
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                    settings.autoOpen = false; //set auto open to false once user has viewed this once
                }
                return false;
            };

            var closeArticleContainer = function () {
                if (settings.panelOpen) {
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                }
                return false;
            };


            var isScrolledIntoView = function(elem) {
                if($(elem).length) { //check if element exists
                    var docViewTop = $(window).scrollTop();
                    var docViewBottom = docViewTop + $(window).height();

                    var elemTop = $(elem).offset().top;
                    var elemBottom = elemTop + $(elem).height();

                    return ((elemBottom &lt;= docViewBottom) &amp;&amp; (elemTop &gt;= docViewTop));
                } else {
                    return false;
                }
            };

            var unique = function(array) {
                return array.filter(function (el, index, arr) {
                    return index == arr.indexOf(el);
                });
            }

            var createCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        articleListToSkip[0] = currentArticleId;
                        $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        updateCookie(currentArticleId, articleListToSkip);
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookie = function(articleIdToSkip, articleListToSkip) {
                articleListToSkip.push(articleIdToSkip);
                articleListToSkip = unique(articleListToSkip);
                $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
            }

            var chooseNextArticle = function (currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if ((article.id != currentArticleId) &amp;&amp; ($.inArray(article.id, articleListToSkip) === -1)) {

                        //set article
                        settings.articleTitle = article.title;
                        settings.articleUrl = article.url;
                        settings.articleId = article.id;
						settings.articleImage = article.imgUrl;
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            //public API
            return {
                settings: settings,
                init: init,
                display: display,
                openArticleContainer: openArticleContainer,
                closeArticleContainer: closeArticleContainer
            }
        })();

    };
