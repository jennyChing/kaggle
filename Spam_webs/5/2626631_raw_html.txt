         <p>
          The new uniforms for Colombia's women's cycling team are raising eyebrows for all the wrong reasons.
         </p>
         <aside>
          <div class="aside relatedSidebar">

         <p>
          The team's six cyclists donned the new kits for an event in Italy late last week. They're red and yellow, but feature
          <span>
           a wide, flesh-toned panel extending from mid-torso to the upper thighs.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           In other words, it makes the women look like they're exposing themselves.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           Not surprisingly, a photo of the team has gone viral.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           Some versions actually have a black bar over the flesh-colored fabric. And Twitter deemed the image risque enough to warrant a warning: "The following media may contain sensitive material."
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           Brian Cookson, president of the Union Cycliste Internionale,
          </span>
          condemned the suggestive uniforms, calling them
          <span>
           "unacceptable by any standard of decency."
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           Turns out, the uniforms were designed by one of the team's members. While cyclist Angie Tatiana Rojas Suarez hasn't weighed in on the controversy, she did retweet a social media comment that said, "The uniform may not be the most beautiful and we may not like it, but there's no need for certain comments."
          </span>
         </p>
