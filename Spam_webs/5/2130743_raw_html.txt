        <h1 class="entry-title">
         What Causes Swollen Feet or Ankles?
        </h1>
        <div class="comments-link">
         <a href="http://footanklealliance.com/blog/los-angeles-foot-doctors-california/#respond" title="Comment on What Causes Swollen Feet or Ankles?">
          <span class="leave-reply">
           Leave a reply
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
        <!-- .comments-link -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-2795" height="297" src="http://footanklealliance.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/specialists-foot-doctors-los-angeles.png" title="specialists-foot-doctors-los-angeles" width="261"/>
         Foot and ankle swelling has many causes, including long periods of standing or sitting, being overweight, high blood pressure and arthritis.
        </p>
        <p>
         This type of swelling usually happens when theres a buildup of fluid in the veins, or when the bodys cells begin retaining water.
        </p>
        <p>
         What can be done about swollen feet and ankles, keep reading!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-2790">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         (
         <em>
          Image by Buzzle.com
         </em>
         )
        </p>
        <p>
         Temporary cases of swollen feet and ankles often have to do with a persons diet (i.e. eating a lot of salty foods) or putting persistent weight on the feet.
        </p>
        <p>
         Since the feet and ankles sit nearest the ground, they bear the majority of a persons weight, and the pull of gravity is often strongest here. This sort of swelling is usually relieved when a person changes position.
        </p>
        <p>
         People who spend a lot of time standing, such as sales clerks or bank tellers, may notice they have varicose veins, which are purplish, spider-like veins, on or around their ankles. This is when the body forces the blood to re-route itself down the leg and into the foot, which is called venous insufficiency.
        </p>
        <p>
         People with this sort of swelling are usually able to relieve the problem by wearing special compression stockings or socks and elevating the feet and ankles every couple of hours.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some people experience swelling in their feet and ankles after long airline flights, which is usually in response to the high pressure of most commercial airline cabins.
        </p>
        <p>
         When the air is pressurized, blood vessels are more likely to constrict. When this is combined with sitting still for hours, it often leads to puffiness and water retention in the feet.
        </p>
        <p>
         In most cases, this is harmless and will disappear on its own once on the passenger is on the ground. However, people who are at risk for blood clots or are on blood thinning medications, may be at risk fordangerous side effects.
        </p>
        <p>
         Wearing compression stockings, drinking plenty of fluids, and trying to walk around or change position regularly are some of the best ways to avoid this.
        </p>
        <p>
         Injuries can also lead to temporary swelling as the body rushes blood and other fluids to the site of the injury. In these cases, the swelling means something is wrong on the inside, and needs to be treated by a
         <a href="http://www.footanklealliance.com/contact-foot-doctors.html">
          <strong>
           foot and ankle doctor
          </strong>
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have foot pain, do not let it go untreated or it could very well get worse. Get your foot looked at today so that tomorrow is pain-free.
        </p>
        <p>

          Please call 626-447-2184 (Arcadia) or 818-408-2800 (San Fernando)

         <a href="http://www.footanklealliance.com/contact-foot-doctors.html">
          <strong>
           foot and ankle doctor
          </strong>
         </a>
         about your foot and/or ankle needs.
        </p>
