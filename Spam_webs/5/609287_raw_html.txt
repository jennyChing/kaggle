              Turn That Garden Into Your Own Little Piece of Paradise
             </h2>

             <div class="itemIntroText">
              <p>
               Whether simple, exotic, wild or luxurious, gardens not only fit their owners lifestyle, they are also a reflection of their personality.
              </p>
             </div>
             <!-- Item fulltext -->
             <div class="itemFullText">
              <p>
               Garden design is an art in itself, and with so many days of sunshine, the
               <strong>
                French Riviera
               </strong>
               is definitely a great place to cultivate beautiful gardens, resplendent with all sorts of plants and flowers. We recently met with a young designer,
               <strong>
                Stefana Savin
               </strong>
               (
               <em>
                pictured above),
               </em>
               who, two years ago, launched her own company,
               <a href="http://www.riviera-gardens.com/en/" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Riviera Gardens
                </strong>
               </a>
               , specialising in creating gardens throughout the
               <strong>
                Cte dAzur
               </strong>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 Hello, Stefana, first of all, can you tell us a little about yourself, your background? How did you become professionally involved in garden design?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               I grew up in the
               <strong>
                North-Eastern Romanian
               </strong>
               <strong>
                countryside
               </strong>
               where as children, my sister and I enjoyed collecting flowers a lot pressing them into books totally at random. Every time I came back home from a walk in nature my hands were filled with
               <strong>
                colourful bouquets
               </strong>
               . What a delicious surprise it was to find months, even years later sometimes, a forgotten pressed leaf or petal in a thick dictionary or an adventure novel. The smell of it still lingers, I can still remember the perfume.
              </p>
              <p>
               My dream at that time was to become a
               <strong>
                painter
               </strong>
               , then a
               <strong>
                doctor
               </strong>
               , then a
               <strong>
                lawyer
               </strong>
               . But when it came to taking a decision at the age of 18, I chose
               <strong>
                Horticulture and Landscape Design
               </strong>
               . And, without any doubt, it was one of my best decisions ever.
              </p>
              <p>
               After graduating from university, I had practical training in a
               <strong>
                Baroque garden
               </strong>
               near
               <a href="http://www.riviera-buzz.com/features/travel/item/269-dresden.html" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Dresden in Germany
                </strong>
               </a>
               and then later, almost by accident, I started working in a
               <strong>
                landscape design office
               </strong>
               in
               <strong>
                Nice
               </strong>
               . It all happened so fast. My initial plan was to create a landscaping company in my hometown. I was only supposed to spend two months on the Riviera but guess what? I am still here today, almost five years later. That is the
               <strong>
                Cte d'Azur effect
               </strong>
                there is an undeniable attraction.
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Riviera Gardens design your perfect garden" src="/images/stefana-savin-004.jpg"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 Two years ago you created your own company, Riviera Gardens. Can you tell us a bit about it?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               Founded in 2011,
               <a href="http://www.riviera-gardens.com/en/" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Riviera Gardens
                </strong>
               </a>
               aims at recreating the aura of the mythical
               <strong>
                Cte d'Azur
               </strong>
               . All five senses are embraced and given a rhythm with style.
               <strong>
                Riviera Gardens
               </strong>
               has a touch of that fervour of the collectors who bring rare plants from far away places. The garden is seen as a temple; plants become sacred, almost human, one-step closer to eternity. It is the place that
               <strong>
                brings people together
               </strong>
               in the most enchanting way. Isn't this what life is all about?
               <strong>
                Riviera Gardens
               </strong>
               tells a story about an era of
               <strong>
                abundance
               </strong>
               ,
               <strong>
                voyages
               </strong>
               and
               <strong>
                discovery
               </strong>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 What kind of gardens do you work on - what do people ask you to do?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               From sumptuous to low maintenance, there is diversity in the demand.In places like
               <strong>
                Cap Ferrat
               </strong>
               or
               <strong>
                <a href="http://www.riviera-buzz.com/features/food-drink/item/263-restaurant-de-bacon.html" target="_blank">
                 Cap d'Antibes
                </a>
               </strong>
               , we are expected to create that 
               <strong>
                wow
               </strong>
                factor in the garden conceptual design. Exotic plant varieties, a splash of colours next to a lush greenery wall, all together projected on to the blue canvas of the
               <strong>
                Mediterranean
               </strong>
               .The countryside is more appropriate to
               <strong>
                Provenal retreats
               </strong>
               , where time stands still, light is filtered through the leaves of
               <strong>
                Millenarian olive trees
               </strong>
               , and you have the continuous cacophony of crickets all summer long.There are also the contemporary and minimalist roof terraces in
               <a href="http://www.riviera-buzz.com/on-the-town/entertainment/item/479-angel-awards-2013.html" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Monaco
                </strong>
               </a>
               or
               <strong>
                Cannes
               </strong>
               , where you get the perfect setting for an
               <strong>
                urban cocktail party
               </strong>
               .I love
               <strong>
                diversity
               </strong>
               and believe that every garden must be
               <strong>
                unique
               </strong>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 And so far, which projects rank among your favourites?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               Oh, that is hard to answer. My team and I use the 
               <em>
                passion ingredient
               </em>
               for all of the projects we are working on. Every project has its own particular story attached to it, since we spend hours and hours of our life on it. Personally, I have a particular interest in an on-going project in
               <a href="http://www.riviera-buzz.com/features/local-buzz/item/289-villa-santo-sospir.html" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Saint-Jean Cap Ferrat
                </strong>
               </a>
               . Part of a waterfront property is going to become a
               <strong>
                vertical garden
               </strong>
               with a
               <strong>
                breathtaking waterfall
               </strong>
               . Another favourite is a public square project in
               <strong>
                Cagnes-sur-Mer
               </strong>
               , the challenge being that it needs to give the feeling that the square existed for centuries.
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Garden design by Riviera Gardens" src="/images/stefana-savin-001.jpg"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="For your perfect garden, contact Riviera Gardens" src="/images/stefana-savin-003.jpg"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 From where do you get your inspiration?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               From the beauty that surrounds me, the
               <strong>
                Cte dAzur
               </strong>
               landscape with its dramatic volumes and colours. Simply looking into the heart of a flower can yield some fantastic ideas. I have a particular fondness for flowers, as they represent the '
               <em>
                love'
               </em>
               period in the life of a plant. As
               <strong>
                Princess Grace of Monaco
               </strong>
               said in '
               <a href="http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/2175855.My_Book_of_Flowers" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 My Book of Flowers
                </strong>
               </a>
               ', "...
               <em>
                flowers have a language of their ownthat transcend international barriers. As with music, there is also timelessness about flowers and a sense of communication.
               </em>
               "
               <strong>
                Travelling
               </strong>
               is definitely an endless source of inspiration. There is a scene that remains very clear in my mind ever since the journey - a rocky slope on the
               <strong>
                Greek Mediterranean coast
               </strong>
               covered with
               <strong>
                bay-laurel bushes
               </strong>
               ,
               <a href="http://www.2020site.org/trees/cypress.html" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 cypresses
                </strong>
               </a>
               and
               <strong>
                towering twisting umbrella pines
               </strong>
               . This was one of those landscapes with such a harmony in scale and arrangement, which any designer tries to reach in their compositions.And, last, but by no means least, the precious people that surround me,
               <strong>
                friends and partners
               </strong>
               . They often teach me about
               <em>
                generosity, dedication, courage,
               </em>
               and
               <em>
                wisdom
               </em>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 Do you have favourite plants that you frequently work into your landscape designs? What are they and why are they a must in your designs?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               In the rich world of plants having favourites sounds like discrimination. Nevertheless,
               <strong>
                cypresses
               </strong>
               and
               <strong>
                palm trees
               </strong>
               are often integrated into the conceptual design as they offer that
               <strong>
                vertical architectural feature
               </strong>
               that is reminiscent of columns in a temple. There are also the fragrant plants that should be in any garden: sweetly scented
               <strong>
                citrus blossoms
               </strong>
               ,
               <strong>
                jasmines
               </strong>
               ,
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliotropium" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 heliotropes
                </strong>
               </a>
               ,
               <strong>
                roses
               </strong>
               ,
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honeysuckle" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 honeysuckle
                </strong>
               </a>
               and
               <strong>
                mimosas
               </strong>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Garden on the French riviera by Riviera Gardens" src="/images/stefana-savin-002.jpg"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 What would you say are the most challenging aspects of your job?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               Making people understand that a
               <strong>
                sustainable garden
               </strong>
               needs time to develop. Making them understand the
               <strong>
                real cost and value of a beautiful garden
               </strong>
               in terms of time spent planning, dedication maintaining it, working with the best materials and people.
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 And the most rewarding?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               Contemplating my own creations. I keep walking in that new garden repeating to myself: it is so beautiful, so beautiful, then seeing the big smile on the owner's faces.
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 What are some of your professional goals for the future?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               To create gardens in different parts of the world that will form an
               <strong>
                exquisite collection
               </strong>
               of views, styles and ambiances. The kind of portfolio that will make you feel you are travelling simply by looking at it.
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Stunning garden on the French Riviera by Riviera Gardens" src="/images/stefana-savin-005.jpg"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 Finally, can you tell us in a few words what you like so much about Monaco and the French Riviera?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               Here you get bits of everything: landscape, lifestyle, cultural events, all kinds of people, high mountains, azure skies and the sea. The
               <strong>
                generous sun
               </strong>
               is embracing the coast so many days a year that growing beautiful flowering plants require less effort than anywhere else. As the
               <strong>
                French writer
               </strong>
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colette" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Colette
                </strong>
               </a>
               said, "
               <em>
                Monaco is the country where the frontiers are only flowers"
               </em>
               
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               <span style="font-size:11px;">
                <em>
                 <strong>
                  All photographs courtesy Stefana Savin and  Riviera Gardens
                 </strong>
                </em>
               </span>
              </p>
