     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        The Super Mario Bros. Super Show!
       </b>
      </i>
      is an
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       American
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">
       television series
      </a>
      based upon
      <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">
       Nintendo
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Bros." title="Super Mario Bros.">
        Super Mario Bros.
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Bros._2" title="Super Mario Bros. 2">
        Super Mario Bros. 2
       </a>
      </i>
      video games. It is the first of three TV shows based on the video game series, with the other being
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Super_Mario_Bros._3" title="The Adventures of Super Mario Bros. 3">
        The Adventures of Super Mario Bros. 3
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_World_(TV_series)" title="Super Mario World (TV series)">
        Super Mario World
       </a>
      </i>
      . It was originally broadcast via
      <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_syndication" title="Broadcast syndication">
       first-run syndication
      </a>
      from September 4, 1989, to December 1, 1989, with reruns continuing until September 6, 1991.
      <a href="/wiki/ABC_Family" title="ABC Family">
       The Family Channel
      </a>
      picked up the series on September 23, 1991,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and aired it until August 26, 1994.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The show was produced by
      <a href="/wiki/DIC_Entertainment" title="DIC Entertainment">
       DiC Animation
      </a>
      and was distributed by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viacom_Enterprises" title="Viacom Enterprises">
       Viacom Enterprises
      </a>
      in association with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nintendo_of_America,_Inc." title="Nintendo of America, Inc.">
       Nintendo of America, Inc.
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/DHX_Media" title="DHX Media">
       DHX Media
      </a>
      , the successor company of
      <a href="/wiki/Cookie_Jar_Group" title="Cookie Jar Group">
       Cookie Jar Entertainment
      </a>
      and DiC, is the current distributor.
     </p>

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">

     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mario_Bros._Plumbing">
       <i>
        Mario Bros. Plumbing
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Super_Mario_Bros._Super_Show!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mario Bros. Plumbing">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The first and last parts of each episode were live segments which showed
      <a href="/wiki/Mario" title="Mario">
       Mario
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">
       WWE
      </a>
      Hall of Famer Captain
      <a href="/wiki/Lou_Albano" title="Lou Albano">
       Lou Albano
      </a>
      ) and
      <a href="/wiki/Luigi" title="Luigi">
       Luigi
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Danny_Wells" title="Danny Wells">
       Danny Wells
      </a>
      ), two
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian-American" title="Italian-American">
       Italian-American
      </a>
      plumbers living in
      <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">
       Brooklyn
      </a>
      , where they would often be visited by celebrity guest stars.
     </p>
     <p>
      Some of the celebrity guest stars were popular television stars, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Nedra_Volz" title="Nedra Volz">
       Nedra Volz
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Norman_Fell" title="Norman Fell">
       Norman Fell
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Donna_Douglas" title="Donna Douglas">
       Donna Douglas
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Eve_Plumb" title="Eve Plumb">
       Eve Plumb
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Vanna_White" title="Vanna White">
       Vanna White
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jim_Lange" title="Jim Lange">
       Jim Lange
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Danica_McKellar" title="Danica McKellar">
       Danica McKellar
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Eggert" title="Nicole Eggert">
       Nicole Eggert
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Clare_Carey" title="Clare Carey">
       Clare Carey
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Brian_Bonsall" title="Brian Bonsall">
       Brian Bonsall
      </a>
      or professional athletes such as the Late
      <a href="/wiki/Lyle_Alzado" title="Lyle Alzado">
       Lyle Alzado
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Magic_Johnson" title="Magic Johnson">
       Magic Johnson
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/WWE_Hall_of_Fame" title="WWE Hall of Fame">
       WWE Hall of Famers
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Roddy_Piper" title="Roddy Piper">
       Roddy Piper
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Sgt._Slaughter" title="Sgt. Slaughter">
       Sgt. Slaughter
      </a>
      . In one episode,
      <a href="/wiki/Ernie_Hudson" title="Ernie Hudson">
       Ernie Hudson
      </a>
      appeared as a Slimebuster, a parody of his
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Ghostbusters" title="Ghostbusters">
        Ghostbusters
       </a>
      </i>
      persona
      <a href="/wiki/Winston_Zeddemore" title="Winston Zeddemore">
       Winston Zeddemore
      </a>
      and on another occasion Mario and Luigi receive a visit from
      <a href="/wiki/Inspector_Gadget" title="Inspector Gadget">
       Inspector Gadget
      </a>
      , performed live by
      <a href="/wiki/Maurice_LaMarche" title="Maurice LaMarche">
       Maurice LaMarche
      </a>
      . There was also another episode with
      <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Peterson" title="Cassandra Peterson">
       Cassandra Peterson
      </a>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Elvira" title="Elvira">
       Elvira
      </a>
      , Mistress of the Dark, but the episode was not included in the DVDs. In an interview for the first DVD release of the show, Lou Albano talked about filming these live action skits, which mainly involved he and Wells getting a central plot and mostly improvising the dialogue as they went along.
     </p>
     <p>
      In one episode, Lou Albano portrays himself, forcing his regular character to leave the scene in order for himself to appear. In the episode in question, pop star
      <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Lauper" title="Cyndi Lauper">
       Cyndi Lauper
      </a>
      states she's looking for Lou Albano because he's missing, due to the note she got from him (although there is an important part missing from the note). Mario exclaims how much he wants to meet Lou, and later Lou appears as himself supposedly while Mario's out shopping for pizza. As a result, Luigi gets to meet Lou, but Mario does not.
     </p>
     <p>
      Lou Albano and Danny Wells also once played female versions of themselves, Marianne and Luigeena (their cousins), and also two
      <a href="/wiki/Hillbilly" title="Hillbilly">
       hillbilly
      </a>
      cousins, named Mario Joe and Luigi Bob.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Super_Mario_Bros.">
       <i>
        Super Mario Bros.
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Super_Mario_Bros._Super_Show!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Super Mario Bros.">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Each
      <i>
       Super Mario Bros.
      </i>
      cartoon served as the second segment of every show, following the introduction and first few minutes of the episode's live-action segment.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The cartoon featured characters and situations based upon the
      <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Nintendo Entertainment System">
       NES
      </a>
      games
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Bros." title="Super Mario Bros.">
        Super Mario Bros.
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Bros._2" title="Super Mario Bros. 2">
        Super Mario Bros. 2
       </a>
      </i>
      , as well as several sound effects and musical cues from the two games. Each episode featured Mario, Luigi,
      <a href="/wiki/Toad_(Mario)" title="Toad (Mario)">
       Toad
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Princess_Peach" title="Princess Peach">
       Princess Toadstool
      </a>
      defending the Mushroom Kingdom from the reptilian villain
      <a href="/wiki/Bowser_(character)" title="Bowser (character)">
       King Koopa
      </a>
      , often in a movie or pop-culture parody. Getting into the spirit of these parodies, Koopa often used
      <a href="/wiki/Alter_ego" title="Alter ego">
       alter egos
      </a>
      fitting the current theme.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theme_song" title="Theme song">
       theme song
      </a>
      for the cartoon segments revealed that the Super Mario Brothers were accidentally warped into the Mushroom Kingdom while working on a bathtub drain in
      <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">
       Brooklyn
      </a>
      . After traveling via the warp drain, the Super Mario Brothers defeated King Koopa's
      <a href="/wiki/Koopa_Troopa" title="Koopa Troopa">
       Koopa Troopas
      </a>
      , saved Princess Toadstool and halted Koopa's plan to conquer the Mushroom Kingdom. At the beginning of every cartoon segment, Mario recites an entry into his "Plumber's Log", a parody of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain%27s_Log" title="Captain's Log">
       Captain's Log
      </a>
      from
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">
        Star Trek
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      When Mario and Luigi are in danger, they often do their Patty-Cake routine: "Patty-cake, Patty-cake, Pasta-Man! Gimme Pasta power as quick as you can!" then everything becomes possible.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       Super Mario Bros.
      </i>
      cartoons aired four days a week, from Monday through Thursday.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Legend_of_Zelda">
       <i>
        The Legend of Zelda
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Super_Mario_Bros._Super_Show!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Legend of Zelda">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda_(TV_series)" title="The Legend of Zelda (TV series)">
       The Legend of Zelda (TV series)
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      On every Friday episode of
      <i>
       The Super Mario Bros. Super Show
      </i>
      , a cartoon based on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda_(video_game)" title="The Legend of Zelda (video game)">
        The Legend of Zelda
       </a>
      </i>
      video game series was featured instead of the
      <i>
       Super Mario Bros.
      </i>
      cartoons. The elf-like hero
      <a href="/wiki/Link_(The_Legend_of_Zelda)" title="Link (The Legend of Zelda)">
       Link
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Princess_Zelda" title="Princess Zelda">
       Princess Zelda
      </a>
      battled against the forces of the evil wizard
      <a href="/wiki/Ganon" title="Ganon">
       Ganon
      </a>
      . Scenes from each episode of the show were shown during the sitcom segments on the preceding
      <i>
       Super Mario Bros. Super Show!
      </i>
      episodes during the week, and then broadcast as sneak peeks. The
      <i>
       Zelda
      </i>
      cartoons consisted of thirteen episodes, which ended when the
      <i>
       Super Mario Bros. Super Show!
      </i>
      ended its initial broadcast run. The characters of Link, Zelda and Ganon, along with their respective voice actors (
      <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Potts" title="Jonathan Potts">
       Jonathan Potts
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Cynthia_Preston" title="Cynthia Preston">
       Cynthia Preston
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Len_Carlson" title="Len Carlson">
       Len Carlson
      </a>
      ), were later featured in an episode of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Captain_N:_The_Game_Master" title="Captain N: The Game Master">
        Captain N: The Game Master
       </a>
      </i>
      , based on the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Zelda_II:_The_Adventure_of_Link" title="Zelda II: The Adventure of Link">
        Zelda II: The Adventure of Link
       </a>
      </i>
      NES game, another animated series based on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NES" title="NES">
       NES
      </a>
      video games, and also produced by
      <a href="/wiki/DIC_Entertainment" title="DIC Entertainment">
       DiC Animation
      </a>
      around the same period, airing on
      <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">
       NBC
      </a>
      as part of its
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saturday_morning_cartoon" title="Saturday morning cartoon">
       Saturday morning cartoon
      </a>
      schedule.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Club_Mario">
       <i>
        Club Mario
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Super_Mario_Bros._Super_Show!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Club Mario">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In the 1990-91 season after the original animated series had ended, the
      <a href="/wiki/Lou_Albano" title="Lou Albano">
       Albano
      </a>
      /
      <a href="/wiki/Danny_Wells" title="Danny Wells">
       Wells
      </a>
      live-action sequences were replaced on September 3, 1990 with new continuity under the title
      <i>
       Club Mario
      </i>
      . The
      <i>
       Super Mario Bros.
      </i>
      cartoons (as well as
      <i>
       The Legend of Zelda
      </i>
      on Fridays) remained intact but the live-action format now featured Mario-obsessed teenagers Tommy and Tammy Treehugger, along with Cool MC
      <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_signal_intrusion" title="Broadcast signal intrusion">
       'commandeering' the 'satellite signal'
      </a>
      of the
      <i>
       Super Show
      </i>
      using a satellite dish atop their apartment building (despite the reality of the show going out on tapes to stations well in advance), goofing around, and in at least one episode, running around the studio and harassing DiC executive and executive producer
      <a href="/wiki/Andy_Heyward" title="Andy Heyward">
       Andy Heyward
      </a>
      . Cool MC also had to deal with his
      <a href="/wiki/Evil_twin" title="Evil twin">
       evil twin
      </a>
      Eric (played by the same actor), who attempted fruitlessly to take over the show. An additional added segment was a one-to-two-minute viewing of
      <i>
       Space Scout Theater
      </i>
      /
      <i>
       Spaced Out Theater
      </i>
      , hosted by Princess Centauri, a green alien woman, which was sourced and edited from the
      <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">
       science fiction
      </a>
      children television series,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Photon_(TV_series)" title="Photon (TV series)">
        Photon
       </a>
      </i>
      . The segments had an unpopular reception and further distribution of the series after the 1990-91 season has solely featured only the original cut of the show with the Albano/Wells live-action sequences.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mario_All_Stars">
       <i>
        Mario All Stars
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Super_Mario_Bros._Super_Show!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mario All Stars">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1993,
      <a href="/wiki/ABC_Family#Early_history" title="ABC Family">
       The Family Channel
      </a>
      aired the show in a package named
      <i>
       Mario All Stars
      </i>
      , consisting of time compressed versions of
      <i>
       The Super Mario Bros. Super Show!
      </i>
      cartoon segments and the
      <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_World_(TV_series)" title="Super Mario World (TV series)">
       <i>
        Super Mario World
       </i>
       cartoons
      </a>
      . It ran for 39 episodes in double episode format, and was promoted as "the Super Mario Bros. in 40 brand new adventures". Although clips from
      <a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Super_Mario_Bros._3" title="The Adventures of Super Mario Bros. 3">
       the
       <i>
        Super Mario Bros. 3
       </i>
       cartoons
      </a>
      were used in promos for the show, none of the show's episodes were featured.
      <i>
       All Stars
      </i>
      was later seen on the
      <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">
       USA Network
      </a>
      from January 6June 6, 1997, when it was replaced by
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(TV_series)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (TV series)">
        Sonic the Hedgehog
       </a>
      </i>
      reruns. Before being re-edited for
      <i>
       All Stars
      </i>
      in 1993, Family Channel played the episodes slower than their normal speed and included the live action segments. The package's title is most likely inspired by the title of the video game compilation
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_All-Stars" title="Super Mario All-Stars">
        Super Mario All-Stars
       </a>
      </i>
      that was released the previous year.
     </p>

      At some point in the cartoon segments, a song would be played to go along with the scene. These were usually notable singles from famous singers, songwriters, and musical artists of the era. When the program was re-released on
      <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">
       DVD
      </a>
      in North America, the songs were replaced by instrumentals of songs from
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Super_Mario_Bros._3" title="The Adventures of Super Mario Bros. 3">
        The Adventures of Super Mario Bros. 3
       </a>
      </i>
      and one song from the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_World_(TV_series)" title="Super Mario World (TV series)">
        Super Mario World
       </a>
      </i>
      episode ,
      <i>
       The Wheel Thing
      </i>
      . Episode/song combinations are shown on the
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mario_television_episodes" title="List of Mario television episodes">
       list of Mario television episodes
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Home_video_releases">
       Home video releases
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Super_Mario_Bros._Super_Show!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Home video releases">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      From 1989 to 1990, Kids Klassics released episodes of the series on VHS. Starting in 1991, Kids Klassics' parent company
      <a href="/wiki/GoodTimes_Entertainment" title="GoodTimes Entertainment">
       GoodTimes Entertainment
      </a>
      continued releasing episodes on VHS up through 1993.
     </p>
     <p>
      1989 Releases (Each volume begins and ends with a complete live-action segment, with the featured complete animated segment in the middle)
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Mario's Magic Carpet
      </li>
      <li>
       Mario Meets Koop-zilla
      </li>
      <li>
       King Mario of Cramalot
      </li>
      <li>
       The Great Gladiator Gig
      </li>
      <li>
       Butch Mario &amp; the Luigi Kid
      </li>
      <li>
       The Great BMX Race
      </li>
      <li>
       The Ringer (
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda_(TV_series)" title="The Legend of Zelda (TV series)">
         The Legend of Zelda
        </a>
       </i>
       )
      </li>
      <li>
       Sing for the Unicorn (
       <i>
        The Legend of Zelda
       </i>
       )
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      There were also 3 VHS tapes only available through a mail-in offer with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nestle" title="Nestle">
       Nestle
      </a>
      Quik. They follow the same format as the volumes listed above.
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       The Bird! The Bird!
      </li>
      <li>
       Pirates of the Koopa
      </li>
      <li>
       Kiss'N Tell (
       <i>
        The Legend of Zelda
       </i>
       )
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      1990 Releases (Each volume has 1 complete live action segment and 3 complete animated segments)
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Koopa Claus (plus "Santa Claus Is Coming to Flatbush", "Stars in Their Eyes", and "Too Hot to Handle")
      </li>
      <li>
       Count Koopula (plus "Vampire Until Ready", "Koopenstein", and "Robo Koopa")
      </li>
      <li>
       The Missing Link [
       <i>
        The Legend of Zelda
       </i>
       ] (plus "Captain Lou Is Missing", "Cold Spells", and "The Moblins Are Revolting")
      </li>
      <li>
       Princess, I Shrunk The Mario Brothers (plus "Rowdy Roddy's Rotten Pipes", "Rollin' Down The River", and "Brooklyn Bound")
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      1991 Releases (The Kids Klassics logo is retained although these volumes were released by GoodTimes) (Each volume has 4 complete animated segments and no live-action segments)
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Two Plumbers and a Baby (plus "On Her Majesty's Sewer Service", "The Great Gold Coin Rush", and "Flatbush Koopa")
      </li>
      <li>
       Robo Koopa (plus "Bad Rap", "Karate Koopa", and "The Koopas Are Coming! "The Koopas Are Coming!")
      </li>
      <li>
       Hooded Robin and His Mario Men (Plus "Plummers Academy", "Quest for Pizza", and "Escape from Koopatraz")
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      1993 Release (Released in a clamshell packaging, contains 6 complete animated sgements and no live-action segments)
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Super Mario Bros. Super Show ("Jungle Fever", "Mario and the Beanstalk", "20,000 Koopas Under the Sea", "Mario of the Apes", "Mario of the Deep", and "King Mario of Cramalot")
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      In 2006,
      <a href="/wiki/Shout!_Factory" title="Shout! Factory">
       Shout! Factory
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_BMG_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony BMG Music Entertainment">
       Sony BMG Music Entertainment
      </a>
      released the series on two 4-disc DVD sets.
     </p>
