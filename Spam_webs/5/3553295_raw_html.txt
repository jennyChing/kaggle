        <div class="p402_premium">
         <p>
          At my house I am not the only fan of craft beer, my lovely wife Jessica shares my love of beer. Jessica is the Maine chapter Chair of
          <a href="http://www.girlspintout.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Girls Pint Out,
          </a>
          a National organization whosegoal is to empower and educate women in the ways of craft beer, because its not a boys club its a beverage. Usually its me whos heading out during the week to tap takeovers and brewery visits and such, but Jessica has her events and I completely support her, so on her nights I stay home, cook dinner, give the boys baths and put them to bed so that she can be out doing her thing, I asked her to take the next step though and write me a guest post about the event shes been planning with Geaghan Brothers Brewing, which sounds like it was a success.
         </p>
         <p>
          Cheers
         </p>
         <p>
          Chaddah
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="IMG_4211" class="aligncenter size-thumbnail wp-image-2943" height="250" src="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4211-e1414450828706-250x250.jpg?quality=99&amp;w=100" width="250"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          On Sunday October 19th Maine Girls Pint Out held their first Beer Pairing Dinner at
          <a href="http://www.geaghanspub.com/ordereze/Content/9/Summary.aspx" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Geaghans Pub &amp; Craft Brewery i
          </a>
          n Bangor, Maine, the event was sold out with nearly thirty women in attendance. The dinner was four courses each paired with a Geaghans Brothers beer, before we started we were introduced to Andy Geaghan, head brewer and Matthew Rideout, pub chef.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2944" style="width: 460px">
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4210.jpg?ref=inline">
           <img alt="IMG_4210" class="wp-image-2944 size-medium" height="337" src="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4210-450x337.jpg?quality=99&amp;w=100" width="450"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Andy Geaghan, head brewer and Matthew Rideout, pub chef.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The pairings were all excellent; I heard many women mention how they were not normally a fan of a particular type of beer but when paired correctly they really enjoyed it.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2941" style="width: 260px">
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4212-e1414450813347.jpg?ref=inline">
           <img alt="The Menu" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-2941" height="250" src="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4212-e1414450813347-250x250.jpg?quality=99&amp;w=100" width="250"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The Menu
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Course I  Butternut Squash and Apple Soup paired with Smiling Irish Bastard
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4213-e1414450795519.jpg?ref=inline">
           <img alt="IMG_4213" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2939" height="450" src="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4213-e1414450795519-337x450.jpg?quality=99&amp;w=100" width="337"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This was a sweet and savory contrasting pairing, I was a little unsure about the soup but I really enjoyed it, I would have loved more but I knew we still had three more courses.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Course II  Spinach Salad with crumbled blue cheese &amp; candied pecans, roasted garlic balsamic dressing paired with Higgings Irish Red Ale
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4215-e1414450869857.jpg?ref=inline">
           <img alt="IMG_4215" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2945" height="450" src="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4215-e1414450869857-337x450.jpg?quality=99&amp;w=100" width="337"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          A complementary pairing, the Irish Red is a malt forward beer which is sweet like the candied pecans but a contrast to the blue cheese.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Course III  Braised Beef Brisket locally sourced beef brisket braised in Bangor Brown Ale, cracked peppercorns, &amp; onions served au jus, mashed sweet potatoes &amp; coleslaw paired with Captain Kool IPA
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4217-e1414450881783.jpg?ref=inline">
           <img alt="IMG_4217" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2946" height="450" src="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4217-e1414450881783-337x450.jpg?quality=99&amp;w=100" width="337"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The beef, sweet potatoes and coleslaw were all fantastic but what I really remember is Andy talking about the woman Captain Kool is named for, its named for a woman who was a Pub regular. Myrtle Molly Kool was from New Brunswick, Canada, she was North Americas first female sea captain and spent her final years in Bangor where she took the bus to the pub to drink whiskey. Geaghans named their biggest, hoppiest beer after the whiskey drinking female captain.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Course IV  Pumpkin Cheesecake paired with Bangor Brown Ale
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4220-e1414450776195.jpg?ref=inline">
           <img alt="IMG_4220" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2937" height="450" src="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_4220-e1414450776195-337x450.jpg?quality=99&amp;w=100" width="337"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Everyone was excited for the cheesecake, rightfully so, it was a great way to finish a great meal but by the fourth course I was so full I only had a couple bites, dont worry the rest went home with me.
         </p>
         <p>
          At the dinner Geaghans sold special Maine Girls Pint Out T-shirts for a minimum $20 donation with all proceeds benefiting
          <a href="http://www.emmc.org/splash_cancercareofmaine.aspx" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Cancer Care of Maine,
          </a>
          we have since raised over $400! If you didnt get one that night there are still a few available at the Pub, stop by to get yours before they are gone.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_3937.jpg?ref=inline">
           <img alt="IMG_3937" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2940" height="450" src="http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/126/files/2014/10/IMG_3937-450x450.jpg?quality=99&amp;w=100" width="450"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          I cant wait until our next beer pairing dinner at Geaghans, there will be more!
         </p>
         <p>
          For more information about events and goings on with Maine Girls Pint Out like us on
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/MaineGPO" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

          </a>
          and
          <a href="https://twitter.com/mainegpo" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

         </p>

			if ( width &lt; parentWidth ) {
				console.log('.parent()');
				return parentWidth;
			} else if ( width &lt; grandParentWidth ) {

				return grandParentWidth;
			} else if ( width &lt; ancestorWidth ) {

				return ancestorWidth;

			var parentElement = element.parent();

			if( !parentElement || parentElement.length &lt;= 0 )

			if( !parentElement.is(':visible') || parentElement.width() &lt; width )
