     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Lawnmower Man 2: Beyond Cyberspace
       </b>
      </i>
      is a 1996
      <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_film" title="Science fiction film">
       science fiction film
      </a>
      , and the
      <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">
       sequel
      </a>
      to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Lawnmower_Man_(film)" title="The Lawnmower Man (film)">
        The Lawnmower Man
       </a>
      </i>
      , which was titled after a
      <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">
       Stephen King
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/The_Lawnmower_Man" title="The Lawnmower Man">
       short story of the same title
      </a>
      , but the stories are unrelated. Retitled
      <i>
       <b>
        Lawnmower Man 2: Jobe's War
       </b>
      </i>
      for video release, the film stars
      <a href="/wiki/Matt_Frewer" title="Matt Frewer">
       Matt Frewer
      </a>
      as Jobe along with
      <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Bergin" title="Patrick Bergin">
       Patrick Bergin
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Austin_O%27Brien" title="Austin O'Brien">
       Austin O'Brien
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawnmower_Man_2:_Beyond_Cyberspace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The founder of virtual reality, Dr. Benjamin Trace (
      <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Bergin" title="Patrick Bergin">
       Patrick Bergin
      </a>
      ), has lost a legal battle to secure a
      <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">
       patent
      </a>
      on the most powerful worldwide communications chip ever invented. Touted as the one
      <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">
       operating system
      </a>
      to control all others, in the wrong hands the "
      <a href="/wiki/Chiron" title="Chiron">
       Chiron
      </a>
      Chip" has the potential to dominate a society dependent on computers.
     </p>
     <p>
      When corporate
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tycoon" title="Tycoon">
       tycoon
      </a>
      and virtual reality entrepreneur Jonathan Walker (
      <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Conway_(actor)" title="Kevin Conway (actor)">
       Kevin Conway
      </a>
      ) takes over development of the Chiron Chip, he and his team discover Jobe Smith (
      <a href="/wiki/Matt_Frewer" title="Matt Frewer">
       Matt Frewer
      </a>
      ) barely alive after the explosion at the end of the first film. Reconstructing his face and hooking him up to their database, they plan to use Jobe to help them perfect the Chiron Chip.
     </p>
     <p>
      Six years later, a now 16-year-old Peter Parkette (
      <a href="/wiki/Austin_O%27Brien" title="Austin O'Brien">
       Austin O'Brien
      </a>
      ) is a computer hacker and lives in the subways of Los Angeles with his girlfriend and two other friends. While hooked into cyberspace, Jobe reconnects with Peter and asks him to find Dr. Trace for him. After Peter finds Trace, they discover that Jobe is back to his old ways and plots to finish what he started in the first film.
     </p>
     <p>
      Joining forces with Trace's former lover, Trace, Peter and his friends must go on a race against time to save the world from Jobe's diabolical scheme and face him in one last battle in cyberspace. In the end, Trace defeats the villain with help from Jobe who turns back into his former good self.
     </p>
