            <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
             Technology Tool Arsenal for a Home Office
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta entry-meta-above">
             Posted on
             <time class="entry-date published updated" datetime="2013-11-18T13:30:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
              November 18, 2013
             </time>
             by
             <span class="entry-author vcard" itemprop="author" itemptype="http://schema.org/Person" itemscope="">
              <a class="author-link fn nickname url" href="http://rabidofficemonkey.com/author/guest-author/" itemprop="url" title="View all posts by Contributor">
               <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
                Contributor
               </span>
              </a>
             </span>
             
             <a class="entry-comments" href="http://rabidofficemonkey.com/technology-tool-arsenal-for-a-home-office/#comments" title="Technology Tool Arsenal for a Home Office  Comments">
              0 Comments
             </a>
            </div>
            <!-- .entry-meta -->
           </header>
           <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
            <div id="triberr_endorsement">
            </div>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/09/18/opinion/invitation-to-a-dialogue-working-from-home.html?_r=0" target="_blank">
              Working from home
             </a>
             is becoming an increasingly common practice. Parents needing to look after young children or workers burned out from the commute can benefit immensely from working from home when their employer allows it. Small business owners also often start out working out of their homes. In order to get the most out of working from home, its important to have a well-equipped home office complete with all of the essential technology tools. While you may get by with a basic laptop and your cell phone for a while, you will soon see how important some of these other tools can be.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              Skype
             </b>
            </p>
            <p>
             If you are in a business that requires making lots of phone calls, your family probably wont appreciate you eating up all of the minutes on the familys cell phone plan. Instead, go for a cheaper alternative like
             <a href="http://www.skype.com" target="_blank">
              Skype
             </a>
             . Of course, you will also need some kind of headset or a quality microphone on your computer to not sound like you are yelling at a speakerphone while you are on your calls, so get the whole set up fixed up, so you can focus on making that sale, not your phone bill or getting the other person to understand what you are saying.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              Printer
             </b>
            </p>
            <p>
             A printer is a pretty basic device, but it is one every home office should have. Consider getting one with a wireless setup, so you can print from anywhere. They also have printers now with their own email addresses, so instead of programming the printer to each device, you simply send the printer an email with an attachment and out pops the document.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              Computer Software
             </b>
            </p>
            <p>
             Having the right computer software is another must. One cheap alternative is to use
             <a href="https://drive.google.com/" target="_blank">
              Google Drive
             </a>
             to make documents, spreadsheets and presentations. Of course, these dont offer all of the features as the standard Microsoft Office, so take into considerations how nice your reports and presentations need to be. If you are the one that presents to clients, spring for the better software. A
             <a href="http://www.adobe.com/products/acrobat/pdf-editor-pdf-files.html" target="_blank">
              PDF editor
             </a>
             is another handy tool since many documents are sent in PDF format these days that require you to fill in information.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              Webcam
             </b>
            </p>
            <p>
             Finally, one part of telecommuting are those video conference calls with your boss or co-workers. Make sure you have a quality webcam that will pick up your voice well and not wash you out and make you look like you are dying from something. While you are thinking about this, set up a background behind you that looks professional and wont be distracting during the meeting.
            </p>
