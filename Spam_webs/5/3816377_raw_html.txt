     Greg George Junior could be the Northern Territory's youngest cowboy.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Greg George Junior lives at Kelly's ranch just outside Tennant Creek, NT.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Junior spends his time working on the farm, 'feeding the chickens, feeding the cows, feeding the horses and filling the water for the galahs."
     <br/>
     <br/>
     When not feeding the animals or chasing the ducks, Junior works with the horses and explains, "I have to dress like a real cowboy."
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Junior may be only 8 years and needs the help of a milk crate to saddle up on his horse but the NT has another cowboy in waiting.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Just ask experienced cattleman Gerry Kelly, a local (Warumungu) Aboriginal, who was born at Alroy Downs Station in the Barkly Region, and is a Traditional Aboriginal Owner from the region.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     "Yeah, he'll fit in all right, he gets up at 5am and does his farm work and is now going to school. He loves the horses but also loves kicking his footy around, being on his push bike and roller skating... he's a good kid," he said.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     This video postcard is created by
     <a href="http://www.mediamob.net.au/">
      <strong>
       MEDIA MOB
      </strong>
     </a>
     and supported by an
     <a href="http://www.abc.net.au/heywire/stories/2013/03/3708056.htm">
      <strong>
       FRRR ABC HEYWIRE Youth Innovation Grant
      </strong>
     </a>
     , these digital postcards follow the achievements of young individuals and groups living in Tennant Creek and The Barkly Region, giving them the opportunity to promote the positive aspects of their community.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Media Mob is a small group of indigenous youth from Tennant Creek (NT) who, under the guidance of
     <strong>
      <a href="http://barklyarts.com.au/">
       Barkly Regional Arts
      </a>
     </strong>
     , receive training in digital media.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     The filmmakers have produced a series of 15 video postcards that will be posted online throughout the month of July, 2014.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Media Mob video producer Sean Bahr-Kelly has proudly mentored the younger kids during the entire production and couldn't be prouder of the result.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     "This is the best way to show that good things do happen here in Tennant Creek and we are good people.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     "Hopefully some of the people who watch our video postcards will start thinking big and expand their horizons," he said.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Each video postcard will follow the achievements of young individuals and groups, giving them the opportunity to tell their stories, and promote the positive aspects of their community.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     This project is surely going a long way towards 'Getting the Balance Right' and building a balanced and positive representation of youth in the media, as
     <a href="http://www.abc.net.au/heywire/stories/2013/03/3703640.htm">
      envisaged by the 2013 Heywire participants
     </a>
     .
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Keep track of all the video postcards on our
