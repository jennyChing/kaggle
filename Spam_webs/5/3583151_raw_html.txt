       <h1>
        What Should I Do If My Car is Sideswiped?
       </h1>
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         For most drivers on the road, a sideswipe seems to fall easily in to the same category as a rear-ending: it should be clear who is at fault in this situation. However, without witnesses or proper evidence, it can be difficult to prove your car accident case in the event of a sideswiping unless you seek proper legal representation. In order to ensure that you are treated fairly in the aftermath of a sideswiping, follow these essential tips from Bruce Robinson &amp; Associates.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.marylandaccidentattorney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/sideswipe.jpg">
           <img alt="sideswipe" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-388" height="200" src="http://www.marylandaccidentattorney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/sideswipe-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          The 5 Essential Steps to Dealing with a Sideswipe Car Accident
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Contact Emergency Services:
         </strong>
         If you have been injured during the sideswiping, immediately contact emergency services.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Call Your Insurance Company and Your Attorney:
         </strong>
         Getting in touch with the people who can help you is a vital step to take in the aftermath of a sideswiping. Your attorneys will be able to offer advice right away and help you in the evaluation of the damages done to both you and your vehicle.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Evaluating the Damage:
         </strong>
         Whether your car is sideswiped while it is parked or while you are driving, the first thing it will be necessary to do is to evaluate the damage done. Make sure that you pull over to a safe spot out of the way of traffic (and that the driver responsible for the sideswiping does as well) and get out of your car. You can do a cursory examination of the side that was hit in order to see what has happened, making sure to take pictures if you can for the insurance companys records and for your attorney.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          List Your Questions and Observations:
         </strong>
         In the moment, many thoughts run through your head concerning what to do next, and you also have the most recent, accurate observations of what exactly happened. Do whatever works best for you to list these questions and thoughts- record them on your phone, jot them down on a notepad, etc. This way, when it comes time to sit down with your attorney and get your questions answered, you wont forget about any of the details, no matter how small they may seem. Every little bit helps when it comes to winning your car accident case.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Stay Calm:
         </strong>
         Any accident, big or small, can be alarming, shocking, frustrating, and frightening. The important thing to do is to stay calm and rational. You can put your trust in your car accident attorney, who will be able to help you through this difficult time.
        </p>
