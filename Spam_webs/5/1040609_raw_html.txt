    <p>
     I constructed a book in the fall. Was not satisfied with it, so took it up again with more ambition in the Spring. Why its taken this long to post here, I couldnt say really.
    </p>
    <p>
     This was something initially inspired by a book Id picked up at
     <a href="http://www.wellerbookworks.com/" target="_blank" title="Weller Book Works">
      Sam Wellers
     </a>
     when visiting Salt Lake City last summer. (incidentally. go visit
     <a href="http://stephanieleitch.com/section/268863.html" target="_blank" title="Stephanie Leitch">
      Stephanie Leitchs site
     </a>
     . Id actually only walked into the store to say oh hey hello to her.) The book in question was a study published in 1950 by les Presses Universitaires de France. Enough observational data had been, at this point, compiled and debated to suggest that the universe was not static, but expanding rapidly. The French within was lucid enough, but the math took me extra time to decipher. I found that expressing the equations visually helped. So. What began as a means to better understand the equations to which Couderc alluded became a primary occupation. My book, a photo montage companion to the original, melds the geometry, emotion of expansion, degrees of understanding and poetry of my experience reading
     <em>
      Lexpansion de lunivers
     </em>
     .
    </p>
