                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <b>
                      Strawberry Cheesecake Pops
                     </b>
                     {#SummerOfThePopsicle}
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2120505171951565050" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-GqLRSJLqPfc/UjBEk7A3_OI/AAAAAAAAUS0/ARvNYotsX2I/s1600/strawberry+cheesecake+pops.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Strawberry Cheesecake Pops {#SummerOfThePopsicle} | www.girlichef.com" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-GqLRSJLqPfc/UjBEk7A3_OI/AAAAAAAAUS0/ARvNYotsX2I/s1600/strawberry+cheesecake+pops.jpg" title="Strawberry Cheesecake Pops {#SummerOfThePopsicle} | www.girlichef.com"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Well, summer is winding down. Though summer doesn't seem to realize that...it's been mid to upper 90's so far all this week. And today is not the exception. So yeah, summer is
                     <i>
                      supposed
                     </i>
                     to be winding down. But it's not going without a fight. I prefer to believe that that's because it hasn't seen enough popsicles yet! I can hardly argue with that.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Yes, today is Wednesday. And during this
                     <a href="http://www.girlichef.com/2013/05/SummerOfThePopsicleTheReturn.html" target="_blank">
                      <span style="color: #e06666;">
                       <b>
                        Summer of the Popsicle
                       </b>
                      </span>
                     </a>
                     , Wednesday has meant that a guest-popsicler would be stopping by to share a tasty creation with us. And after 15 amazing weeks of even more amazing guests, I'm jumping in with a pop of my own! I decided to nestle myself...and these ice pops I've been sitting on all summer long
                     <i>
                      (wouldn't that be a glorious mess)
                     </i>
                     ...into this Wednesday slot.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Now, it's funny. I've been testing out new ice pop, popsicle, and paleta recipes all summer long, but this is one I made way back towards the end of May. At the beginning of summer. And since then, I've noticed a trend. A CHEESECAKE POPS trend! Must be something in the air this year. Three of my guest popsicles have shared cheesecake-inspired frozen pops. Even more people have linked some up to the Summer of the Popsicle.
                     <br/>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-mIHK9VFG41I/UjBEks_jL-I/AAAAAAAAUS8/fo2BXwdY5xg/s1600/strawberry+cheesecake+pops+3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Strawberry Cheesecake Pops {#SummerOfThePopsicle} | www.girlichef.com" border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-mIHK9VFG41I/UjBEks_jL-I/AAAAAAAAUS8/fo2BXwdY5xg/s1600/strawberry+cheesecake+pops+3.jpg" title="Strawberry Cheesecake Pops {#SummerOfThePopsicle} | www.girlichef.com"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Obviously this many people can't be wrong!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Initially I started out with a New York style cheesecake in mind, and sort of built up from there. I contemplated a thin sour cream layer on top of the cheesecake center, but didn't wind up using it. Logistically I'll figure out how to swing that next time, because I think it would be pretty cool. But that said, we all devoured these. Because yes, they were ridiculous! So cheesecakey. So creamy. So perfect.

Use a hand mixer to blend everything together until creamy and smooth.  Start slow so that everything doesn't splatter all over the place.
                       </div>

                       </div>

                       </div>

                        <br/>
                       </div>
