     <p>
      Looking for an easy one pot meal? You know I love Mexican food, and Ive done it again, just when I thought I couldnt make a better Mexican dish. I made this one-pot
      <strong>
       <em>
        skillet Mexican Chicken &amp; Rice
       </em>
      </strong>
      dish. The chicken is cooked in the skillet and then removed followed by a nice saute of veggies like poblano peppers. The rice is then added in, and its pure heaven. I ultimately wanted to make a risotto, but it didnt work out that night since I didnt have any arborio rice on hand.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <img alt="Skillet Mexican Chicken &amp; Rice from KatiesCucina.com" class="aligncenter" height="362" src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-l6gWh1G7Wrs/Ud_l30GlrHI/AAAAAAAAhSc/6kwklp2tKeE/s912/DSC_0582.JPG" title="Skillet Mexican Chicken &amp; Rice from KatiesCucina.com" width="547"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      The chopped chicken on top with broiled melty cheese is to die for! I placed the skillet on the table and we helped our selves to dinner that night. I paired this one-pot
      <em>
       skillet Mexican chicken &amp; rice
      </em>
      recipe with my favorite
      <a href="http://www.katiescucina.com/2012/05/classic-guacamole/" target="_blank" title="Classic Guacamole">
       classic guacamole
      </a>
      recipe and my
      <a href="http://www.katiescucina.com/2010/08/home-made-salsa/" target="_blank" title="Home-made Salsa">
       homemade salsa
      </a>
      . We chowed down on dinner and took breaks in between bites to fill up our tortilla chips with more guac and salsa.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      <img alt="Skillet Mexican Chicken &amp; Rice from KatiesCucina.com" class="aligncenter" height="362" src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/-jxawTLH5DD8/Ud_l8nS_OII/AAAAAAAAhSo/7RrIeq4HtfU/s912/DSC_0605.JPG" title="Skillet Mexican Chicken &amp; Rice" width="547"/>
      Although Im not an advocate for turning your oven on during the summer months, this dish is really worth it in the end. Just a few minutes in the broiler and your house shouldnt heat up that much. I hope you love this
      <strong>
       <a href="http://www.foodfanatic.com/2013/07/skillet-mexican-chicken-and-rice-a-weeknight-winner/" target="_blank">
        <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         Skillet Mexican Chicken &amp; Rice: A Weeknight Winner!
        </span>
       </a>
      </strong>
     </p>
