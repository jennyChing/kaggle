  decamp to Balmoral - but who is in the running to be the godparents?

        <div id="mainBodyArea" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           With the baby Princess safely home and the Duke and Duchess looking forward to a month of family time in Norfolk, their thoughts will soon turn to the matter of choosing her godparents.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           The Princess is likely to be christened in the Chapel Royal at St Jamess Palace, like her brother, before the Royal family decamp to Balmoral for their summer holiday.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           Prince George was given
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/prince-george/10399013/Prince-George-christening-profiles-of-the-godparents.html">
            <strong>
             no fewer than seven godparents
            </strong>
           </a>
           , so the Duke and Duchess will have plenty of thinking to do before their daughters next big appearance.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           Zara Tindall was the only member of the Royal family to be asked to be a godmother to
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/prince-george/">
            <strong>
             Prince George
            </strong>
           </a>
           , so bookmakers William Hill have installed Princesses Beatrice and Eugenie as the joint favourites for the Princess, at 1/5 on.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           Mrs Tindalls brother Peter Phillips is an even-money bet, with
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/prince-harry/">
            <strong>
             Prince Harry
            </strong>
           </a>
           at longer odds of 2/1, perhaps because he missed out last time despite being such an obvious choice. Similar odds are being offered on the Duchesss sister Pippa.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 class="was-h6">
           Possible godparents - the odds
          </h3>
          <ul class="storylist">
           <li>
            Princess Beatrice - 1/5
           </li>
           <li>
            Princess Eugenie - 1/5
           </li>
           <li>
            Peter Phillips - 1/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Tiggy Pettifer - 5/4
           </li>
           <li>
            Sam Waley-Cohen - 6/4
           </li>
           <li>
            William Van Cutsem - 6/4
           </li>
           <li>
            Alice St John Webster - 2/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Alicia Fox-Pitt - 2/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Eddie Van Cutsem - 2/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Pippa Middleton - 2/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Prince Harry - 2/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Thomas van Straubenzee - 2/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Mike Tindall - 3/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Lady Iona Douglas-Home - 4/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Earl Spencer - 5/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Zara Phillips - 6/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Guy Pelly - 8/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Ben Fogle - 16/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Victoria or David Beckham - 25/1
           </li>
           <li>
            Boris Johnson - 100/1
           </li>
           <li>
            David Cameron - 100/1
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <sup>
            <strong>
             Source: William Hill
            </strong>
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           The other godparents are likely to be harder to predict. With Prince George, the couple went for a mixture of loyal staff, aristocrats and personal friends, including Jamie Lowther-Pinkerton, the Dukes former private secretary, Julia Samuel, who was a close friend of Diana, Princess of Wales, and Emilia Jardine-Paterson  a visitor to Kensington Palace yesterday  who was at school with the Duchess.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alttext="The Duke and Duchess of Cambridge have named (top row) Zara Tindall, Jamie Lowther-Pinkerton, Oliver Baker (bottom row) Emilia Jardine-Paterson, Earl Grosvenor, Julia Samuel and William van Cutsem as the godparents of their son George" caption="The Duke and Duchess have named (top row) Zara Tindall, Jamie Lowther-Pinkerton, Oliver Baker (bottom row) Emilia Jardine-Paterson, Earl Grosvenor, Julia Samuel and William van Cutsem as the godparents of their son George" copyright="Mark Stewart Photography/Rex/PA" declared-caption="" height="387" name="george-christening-8-way" photographer="" refid="2710856" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/02710/george-christening_2710856b.jpg" version="b" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Tiggy Pettifer, nee Tiggy Legge-Bourke, the 50-year-old former nanny to Princes William and Harry, is considered one of the most likely choices for the honour, together with Sam Waley-Cohen, the amateur jockey and businessman who has been credited with getting the Duke and Duchess back together after their brief split in 2007 by inviting them both to a party at his Oxfordshire mansion.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ben Fogle, the television presenter who has become a personal friend of the Duke and Duchess since filming a documentary about the Dukes conservation charity work in Africa, is considered a 16/1 outsider to be a godparent, while David Beckham, who has become a firm friend of the Duke through their work with the Football Association, of which the Duke is president, is a 100/1 shot, as is his wife Victoria. Both were guests at the Royal wedding in 2011.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alttext="Adventurer Ben Fogle plots political journey" caption="Ben Fogle is considering following his friend James Cracknell into a career in politics" copyright="Rex Features" declared-caption="" height="387" name="ben-fogle" photographer="" refid="2628660" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/02628/ben-fogle_2628660b.jpg" version="b" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <sup>
            <strong>
             Ben Fogle is considered a 16/1 outsider to become a godparent (Rex Features)
            </strong>
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           Of the Duke and Duchesss long-term friends, William van Cutsem, whose family used to live in Anmer Hall, the Duke and Duchesss country home in Norfolk and who has been a friend of the Duke since early child hood, is a strong contender, together with his brother Eddie.
          </p>
          <p>
           Thomas van Straubenzee, who was at Ludgrove Prep School with the Duke and is married to the Duke of Northumberlands daughter Lady Melissa Percy, could also get the call.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alttext="The Duke and Duchess of Cambridge pose for the formal portrait with their son Prince George and their immediate families" caption="The Duke and Duchess of Cambridge pose for the formal portrait with their son Prince George and their immediate families" copyright="JASON BELL/CAMERA PRESS" declared-caption="" height="387" name="george" photographer="" refid="2713290" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/02713/george_2713290b.jpg" version="b" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <sup>
            <strong>
             A family portrait after Prince George's christening (Jason Bell/Camera Press)
            </strong>
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           Other names in the frame include Alicia Fox-Pitt and Alice St John Webster, who were at Marlborough College with the Duchess, and Lady Iona Douglas-Home, another friend of the couple.
          </p>
          <p>
           Zara Tindalls husband Mike, the former England rugby player, is likely to keep his phone by his side over the coming weeks, as is Guy Pelly, the impish nightclub-owning friend of the Duke to whom he has remained fiercely loyal despite a number of controversies.
          </p>
          <p>
           The Duke may also consider turning to his late mothers side of the family. His uncle, Earl Spencer, could be a candidate, together with his maternal aunts Lady Sarah McCorquodale and Baroness Fellowes.
          </p>
