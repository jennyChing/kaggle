               In an Octopuss Garden with You (for Five Years)
              </a>
             </h1>
             <div class="meta">
              <span class="meta-date">
               By
               <a href="http://discovermagazine.com/authors?name=Elizabeth+Preston" style="color:#555">

             <div class="shareIcons" style="border-top:1px solid #999;border-bottom:1px solid #999;padding:7px 0 7px 0;margin-bottom:20px;">

             <p>
              <a href="http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/inkfish/files/2015/05/photo.jpg">
               <img alt="photo" class="aligncenter wp-image-2404" height="457" src="http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/inkfish/files/2015/05/photo-1024x928.jpg" width="505"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Hi friends,
             </p>
             <p>
              Earlier this month, while I was busy taking screenshots of autofill suggestions for
              <a href="http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/inkfish/2015/05/12/dont-trust-doctor-google/#.VWR4jpNViko">
               medical searches on Google
              </a>
              , something shocking happened:Inkfish had its five-year anniversary.
             </p>
             <p>
              Five years!!Its about twice the lifespan of a pet hamster. Its closer tothree times the lifespan of a common octopus. In that time Ive written 519 posts, had three different online homes, and somehow managed to learn almost no HTML.
             </p>
             <p>
              To mark the occasion, I dug through my analytics and foundthe most-viewed story fromeach year of Inkfishs existence.
              <span id="more-2401">
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Year Five:
              </strong>
              <a href="http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/inkfish/2015/03/06/the-women-who-stare-at-babies/#.VWR9T5NViko">
               The Women Who Stare at Babies
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Year Four:
              </strong>
              <a href="http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/inkfish/2014/03/28/scientists-convince-people-their-hands-are-rocks/#.VWR9hJNViko">
               Scientists Convince People Their Hands Are Rocks
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Year Three:
              </strong>
              <a href="http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/inkfish/2012/10/01/enslaved-ants-get-even-by-killing-captors-babies/#.VWR9uZNViko">
               Enslaved Ants Get Even by Killing Captors Babies
              </a>
              (and
              <a href="http://inkfish.fieldofscience.com/2012/10/enslaved-ants-get-even-by-killing.html">
               heres
              </a>
              the original version, at the blog network Field of Science)
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Year Two:
              </strong>
              <a href="http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/inkfish/2012/04/17/human-dung-wins-interspecies-taste-test/#.VWR-k5NViko">
               Human Dung Wins Interspecies Taste Test
              </a>
              (original
              <a href="http://inkfish.fieldofscience.com/2012/04/human-dung-wins-interspecies-taste-test.html">
               here
              </a>
              )
             </p>
             <p>
              In
              <strong>
               Year One
              </strong>
              I had a blogspot address, which no longer exists. My two most popular posts were tied with78 pageviews. One was
              <a href="http://inkfish.fieldofscience.com/2010/06/daring-to-discuss.html">
               Daring to Discuss
              </a>
              , a complaint about a column in the New York Times. The other was an
              <a href="http://inkfish.fieldofscience.com/2010/09/toot-toot.html">
               announcement
              </a>
              about a byline I had in National Geographic, which I thinkhighlights how many of my readers were related to me.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thanks to all of you who, even if were not related,are readingtoday. And thanks to Lisa Raffensperger for inviting me to blog at Discover.Five years ago I sat on a secondhand couch and decidedit would be fun to share a few science stories with my friends. Everyone whos given me a reason to keep doing this has my gratitude, in oceans.
             </p>
             <p>
              Elizabeth
             </p>

              PS: If you want to get me a blogday gift, you could

              <a href="http://elizabethgpreston.com/">
               at my website

              . Or, you know, cupcakes aregood.
