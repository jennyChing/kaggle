      <div id="articleText">
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        The fantasy football season started off with a bang in a week when many players put up some ridiculous numbers. FOURTEEN quarterbacks threw for over 300 yards, leading to thirteen wide receivers with 100+ yards receiving. Despite all the excitement through the air, seven running backs still managed one hundred yards or more on the ground.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         About these rankings
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         Fantasy Football MVP Rankings
        </i>
        is a list of the thirty-two top players for the 2011 season. Even though this list clearly places a higher emphasis on games played, what players are projected to do in the coming weeks is still taken into account.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        With only one week of play to look at, this list could look very different this time next week.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <div style="page-break-after: always; display: none;">
        <!---PAGEBREAK-->
       </div>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         1. Tom Brady
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         517 pass yards, 4 pass TDs, 1 INT
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        An easy choice. Brady threw for his most yards ever against the Dolphins and became just the 11th quarterback to throw for 500 or more yards in a game.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         2. Drew Brees
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         419 pass yards, 3 TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Brees throwing the ball 49 times in a game won't be the norm, but he was phenomenal Thursday night.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         3. Aaron Rodgers
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         312 pass yards, 3 pass TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Rodgers gets the edge over several QBs who had more yards than he did because of his near perfect play; he completed 77.1% of his passes.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         4. Matthew Stafford
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         305 pass yards, 3 pass TDs, 1 INT
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Next week, he gets to play home against the Chiefs, who Ryan Fitzpatrick threw four touchdowns against.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         5. Ray Rice
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         107 rush yards, 1 rush TD, 42 receiving yards, 1 rec TD
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Rice owners complained last year about his lack of touchdowns. Well, it's no longer last year.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         6. LeSean McCoy
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         122 rush yards, 1 rush TD, 15 rec yards, 1 rec TD
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        McCoy has all the tools to be a top three fantasy running back this year. Plus, he's on a team that'll score more points than the teams of Adrian Peterson, Jamaal Charles, and Chris Johnson.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         7. Calvin Johnson
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         88 rec yards, 2 rec TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Megatron has always been a beast. With a healthy Stafford, he'll be the best wide receiver in the league.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         8. Michael Vick
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         187 pass yards, 2 pass TDs, 98 rush yards, 1 Fumble lost
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Vick starts the year ranked 9th in rushing yards. If he doesn't get hurt, there's a chance he'll be every bit the fantasy stud he was last year.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         9. Chad Henne
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         416 pass yards, 2 pass TDs, 1 INT, 59 rush yards, 1 rush TD
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Well, that was unexpected. Maybe Henne's not as bad as Miami fans thought he was.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         10. Cam Newton
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         422 pass yards, 2 pass TDs, 1 INT, 18 rush yards, 1 rush TD
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        It was an insane fantasy game, but it came against the Cardinals. We'll get to see just how good Newton is next week when he plays the Packers.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         11. Darren McFadden
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         150 rush yards, 6 rec yards
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        He averaged 6.8 yards per carry. Just wait till he starts getting touchdowns.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         12. Mike Tolbert
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         35 rush yards, 1 rush TD, 58 rec yards, 2 rec TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Tolbert is a touchdown machine. He had 11 last year and, in the season's first game, he had three, more than any other running back or wide receiver.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         13. Wes Welker
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         160 rec yards, 2 rec TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        He'd be much higher if 99 of his 160 yards didn't come all in one play.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         14. Kenny Britt
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         136 rec yards, 2 TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        If the first game is any indication, it looks like Britt's third season really might be his breakout year.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         15. Steve Smith
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         178 rec yards, 2 TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Just when you thought Steve Smith would never be fantasy relevant again he has a game like this. Unfortunately for Smith owners, four of the five next opponents for the Panthers were playoff contenders last year (Packers, Bears, Saints, Falcons).
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         16. Matt Forte
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         68 rush yards, 90 rec yards, 1 rec TD
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        A 56 yard touchdown reception in the 1st quarter helped Forte have a great fantasy game.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         17. Cedric Benson
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         121 rush yards, 1 rush TD, 2 rec yards
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Benson will be the centerpiece of the Bengals offense all year. He has one of the easier schedules for running backs so, even if he doesn't average many yards per carry, he could have a very good year.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         18. Philip Rivers
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         355 pass yards, 2 pass TDs, 2 INTs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Rivers threw the ball 48 times and will have many passing attempts this season.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         19. Tony Romo
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         342 pass yards, 2 pass TDs, 1 INT, 1 Fumble lost
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Felix Jones doesn't look like he'll do a lot for the Cowboys running game. With Miles Austin, Dez Bryant, and Jason Witten to throw to, Romo could duplicate his fantasy performance against the Jets (possibly the league's best defense) many times in 2011.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         20. Maurice Jones-Drew
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         97 rush yards, 1 rush TD
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        The average game you can expect from Jones-Drew if he doesn't get injured.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         21. Adrian Peterson
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         98 rush yards, 6 rec yards
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        The safest running back pick in fantasy football was good, but not great, in Week 1.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         22. Andre Johnson
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         95 rec yards, 1 TD
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        He's elite, but I don't thin he'll be the top fantasy football wide receiver this year.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         23. Ryan Fitzpatrick
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         208 pass yards, 4 pass TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Four TDs gets Fitzpatrick, who doesn't get enough credit in many fantasy football leagues, the #23 spot.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         24. Tim Hightower
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         72 rush yards, 1 rush TD, 25 rec yards
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Hightower looked good very good against the Giants.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         25. Jay Cutler
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         312 pass yards, 2 pass TDs, 1 INT
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Cutler didn't play great last year and the Bears still made it to the NFC Championship Game. On Sunday, he looked much better.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         26. Kevin Kolb
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         309 pass yards, 2 pass TDs, 1 Fumble lost
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        With Larry Fitzgerald to throw to, Kolb has great fantasy football potential this season.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         27. Joe Flacco
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         224 pass yards, 3 pass TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Three touchdowns allowed Flacco to put up lots of points for fantasy teams, but don't expect that every week.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         28. Mark Sanchez
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         335 pass yards, 2 pass TDs, 1 INT, 1 Fumble lost
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Expect the Jets to return to the running game next week in a big way.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         29. Rex Grossman
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         305 pass yards, 2 pass TDs, 1 Fumble lost
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Another Week 1 QB surprise.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         30. Miles Austin
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         90 rec yards, 1 TD
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        Austin played well going up against the league's best secondary.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         31. Fred Jackson
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         112 rush yards, 5 rec yards
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        If the BIlls offense plays as good as they did against the Chiefs in the coming weeks, Jackson is set for success.
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica; min-height: 14px;">
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <b>
         32. Ted Ginn Jr
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        <i>
         268 return yards, 2 return TDs
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="margin: 0px; font: 12px Helvetica;">
        His two TD returns earns him a spot on this list. Don't expect him to make the cut next week.
       </p>
