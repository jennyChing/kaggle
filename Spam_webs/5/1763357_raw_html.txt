      <h1>
       Whats It Going to Take for You to Be Rich?
      </h1>
      <h4>
       June 17th, 2014
       <!-- by Meredith Simonds -->
       
       <a href="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/wordpress/2014/06/17/whats-going-take-rich/#comments">
        No Comments
       </a>
       
       <a href="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/wordpress/category/your-money/" rel="category tag">
        Your Money
       </a>
      </h4>
      <img alt="Meredith Simonds" class="photo" height="80" src="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/userphoto/44.thumbnail.jpg" width="69"/>
      <p>
       by Meredith Simonds
      </p>
      <div class="entry">

       <p>
        <a href="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Fotolia_59262544_XS.jpg">
         <img alt="Whats It Going to Take for You to Be Rich?" class="alignleft wp-image-17676 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Fotolia_59262544_XS-300x300.jpg" title="Whats It Going to Take for You to Be Rich?" width="300"/>
        </a>
        In a survey of 1,000 Americans, roughly 1 in 4 said they believe they could be rich one day. The other 3 out of 4, not so much.
       </p>
       <p>
        This begs the question.
       </p>
       <p>
        How much money do you need to have to consider yourself rich?
       </p>
       <p>
        While gender, relationship status, and other demographic factors seem to influence optimism about being rich one day, how much money you need to be rich likely depends most on how much money youre already making.
       </p>
       <p>
        As
        <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/06/17/upshot/definition-of-rich-changes-with-income.html?_r=0">
         reported
        </a>
        by
        <em>
         The New York Times
        </em>
        , the surveyby research firm YouGov revealed the following.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you make an annual household income of:
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Under $25,000 then you need to increase that income to $293,000 a year to be rich
        </li>
        <li>
         $25,000 to $60,000 then you need to increase that income to $394,000 a year to be rich
        </li>
        <li>
         $60,001 to $120,000 then you need to increase that income to $426,000 a year to be rich
        </li>
        <li>
         Greater than $120,000 then you need to increase that income to $501,000 a year to be rich
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        This begs a second question.
       </p>
       <p>
        What does being rich really mean?
       </p>
       <p>
        Bankrate delved deep into the subject for its own
        <a href="http://www.bankrate.com/finance/financial-literacy/do-you-think-you-will-be-rich-one-day-1.aspx">
         survey
        </a>
        in 2009:
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         33 percent said being rich means having just enough money not to worry
        </li>
        <li>
         26 percent said being rich means having enough money that you dont need to work
        </li>
        <li>
         17 percent said being rich means a net worth of $1 million or more
        </li>
        <li>
         11 percent said being rich means a six-figure annual income
        </li>
        <li>
         7 percent said being rich is determined by possessions (i.e., houses, cars, boats)
        </li>
        <li>
         3 percent cite other reasons
        </li>
        <li>
         3 percent dont know
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        The good news is that the number one answer on the list  having just enough money not to worry  is well within the realm of possibility. The bad news is, most people arent taking the necessary steps to get them there.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/a0e3826231b798153cacc36977055945.jpg">
          <img alt="All money is a matter of belief. ~Adam Smith" class="alignleft wp-image-18239 size-full" height="275" src="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/a0e3826231b798153cacc36977055945.jpg" title="All money is a matter of belief. ~Adam Smith" width="236"/>
         </a>
         How To Get Rich
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        If you define rich as having just enough money not to worry, the recipe for getting there is a pretty straightforward one:
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/wordpress/2014/01/02/hate-budgets-6-steps-lovable-one/">
          Live below your means
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/budgeting/myths-about-saving.shtml">
          Pay yourself first
         </a>
         (i.e., save)
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/debt/">
          Stay out of debt
         </a>
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        Yes, it is far easier said than done. But the point is, it is doable. Not 10 years down the road, or even 10
        <em>
         months
        </em>
        from now, but today.
        <a href="http://www.creditinfocenter.com/budgeting/">
         Heres how.
        </a>
       </p>
