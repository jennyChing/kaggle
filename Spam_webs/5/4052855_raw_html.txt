           <span style="color: blue">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Hi Guys,
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="color: blue">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             I have had a couple request for more information on the Primadonna's built in the late 70's/early 80's.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="color: blue">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             This is the story, from
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: blue">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Jack
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: blue">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Atkinson
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: blue">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             (Designer, Builder).
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="color: blue">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             I added the photo's to illustrate the story. There are hundreds more photo's but this should give you the idea.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             THE PRIMADONNA Z
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             AN AUTO-BIOGRAPHY
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             There has been a little debate and some misinformation over the years about the
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Primadonna
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Z.
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Since the dreamer of this creation does not talk much, Ill fill anyone in who knows of its existence. Anyone who thinks they know how or why certain aspects of the design came to be are strictly speculating.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img179.imageshack.us/img179/2593/z111mz1.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             Long before I was a Primadonna Z, I was just a 240Z. My name was ZBAIT. A name the dreamer gave me because nobody could catch me on the rolling hills above
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Redwood City
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             ,
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             California
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             where I lived.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img180.imageshack.us/img180/7461/1144cp8.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             I was adopted in September of 1972 and it didnt take long for him to start messing with me. At first, he said I needed just a little tweaking. However, in the early 70s he used to take me to
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Laguna
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Seca
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             and Sears Point Raceways to show me what I could be. He started me off with little bubble flares, an air dam and a rear spoiler. Strictly, off the shelf stuff. At first, I liked it but then at the next years races I saw these new flares on the Walt Maas Far Performance 240Z. We realized I would be hotter with this setup so the bubbles were popped off and I had a new look.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             We were happy for a couple more years when I noticed a change in him. He started showing a lot of interest in this thing called the 935. He told me how he fell in love with a German Boxer.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             I felt so inadequate. Then one day he stripped me down and said it could take a couple of years to rebuild me but when he was done, it would take 20 years for me to be fully appreciated.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img179.imageshack.us/img179/7497/thebox1ab0.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img251.imageshack.us/img251/2846/thebox2ex6.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img179.imageshack.us/img179/6331/plotoh5.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img175.imageshack.us/img175/4086/sandingnk8.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             He was only 25 working on me outside of his apartment. He didnt have a shop or a garage, just an aluminum box he built that he slipped me in. I fit like a glove and my box fit perfectly in his parking stall. He didnt have fancy tools, not even a workbench, just worked off the curb. It was here where I was truly born. My designer and a young neighbor friend would sit for hours talking about how to make me the ultimate Z.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img175.imageshack.us/img175/378/balconydn1.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             Then one day he rolled me out of the metal box and fit me with the most exotic tires and wheels imaginable. I had an extended windshield, a huge whaletail and wing just like those 935s.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img175.imageshack.us/img175/4807/1stdriveff2.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             It was at this time he met his real love
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Yolanda
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             and together they took me everywhere. I created quite a stir whenever Id stop. Other makes were always trying to get a closer look on the road and their owners would wave, smile and try to make me go faster. I was a gunslinger.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img175.imageshack.us/img175/2717/z115rc6.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             We were well known in the Z Owners of Northern California and were very active leading many other Zs on tours from
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Oregon
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             to
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Death Valley
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             ,
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             California
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             . Then things started to change.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img175.imageshack.us/img175/725/scan0020fk9.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             It was 1983 now. Thats when I saw my little sister for the first time. She had different hips, they were square. I was white and she was yellow. She was named Primadonna Z and her metal plates said Prima Z. Up until now, it was fun for everybody then we learned our owner was to sell clones of us to anyone that wanted to be ahead of his time.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img180.imageshack.us/img180/7461/1144cp8.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             Little did we know another dreamer was looking for us. Never seeing a picture only hearing about the Great White Z. Then
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             One day someone told this dreamer where he had seen the myth. He ran home, fired up his own creation and together they found us.
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Michael
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             was his name and his car was a flat rusty red.
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Rusty
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             must have been very upset because there was a deep roar coming from under his hood. Something called a V8 was lurking under there with a lot of other stuff I had never seen before. We became friends. Rusty would eventually look just like me, white, only with a lot more attitude. Jettlag was on his nameplate. By now, our bodies were very precise. Within a year, my yellow sister got a face-lift and became a real black beauty.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img161.imageshack.us/img161/3752/scan0014tw9.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img371.imageshack.us/img371/472/normalblkzprimafsvhh2.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img161.imageshack.us/img161/2116/53618852ud5.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             In about 1985 the last change I received appeared. A new precise set of clothes, a stretched windshield, a whaletale without the wing and a flashy Mars Red paint job made me the sexiest Primadonna yet! Thats when my owner, designer and builder had to part with me and pass me on to another. A decision he would be saddened by. A year later, my black sister was adopted by
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Alan
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             , a wonderful owner. So good, he still loves the black Prima Z after nearly 20 years. Shes in great hands!
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img371.imageshack.us/img371/5967/88cw2.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img451.imageshack.us/img451/5395/7zm9.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             <span rel="lightbox">
              <img alt="Posted Image" class="bbc_img" src="http://img161.imageshack.us/img161/4818/scan0026ji1.jpg"/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Michael
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             kept Jettlag for another 14 years but in 2000, after 20 years, realized he could no longer give Jettlag the attention needed, he reluctantly parted with him. He also has felt the loss. Someone named
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Hoover
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             took Jettlag never really loving or understanding what he had. With the help of the internet, Jettlag was sent off to a place called
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Tennessee
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             to another
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Mike
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             . Only this
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Mike
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             cared less than
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Hoover
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             . Jettlag had an uncertain future falling in disrepair with not much hope facing a slow death parked in the grass.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             Then a small miracle happened.
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Roger
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             appeared. Not really knowing why, other than he had to rescue Jettlag. Jettlag was brought to
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Florida
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             and is now being nurtured back to life. Hes in great hands with an owner who commands attention to detail and understands what he has.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             You say just a car! With
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Roger
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             almost accidentally finding Jettlag, it led to him just as accidentally meeting the original dreamer and telling his story of how he found Jettlag. All of which brought two old friends back together again after many years.
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Jack
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             and
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Michael
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             after all have a lot in common and a lot of pictures and memories to share. Both realizing what they had and what they had lost.

           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             Epilogue
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             The Primadonna Z was never intended to be a business, just a personal custom car and only from the evolution of creating it and using it, was the inevitable thought of duplication. With todays acceptance of radical concepts theres almost limitless possibilities. The Primadonna Z fits in with todays concepts but 25 years ago, no one knew what Bling was!
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             The last generation molds and jigs from the original design were sold to a friend in the early 90s. Space being at a premium at home and our lives going in another direction, I thought they were in good hands.
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Miles
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Garcia
            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             saw the early stages of development to the last paint job. He wanted to keep the Primadonna alive.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             Unfortunately, he didnt have THE RIGHT STUFF and after trying to re-sell the molds to the new owners, he inexplicably,
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             without fully comprehending the consequences cut up, destroyed and dumped every piece.
            </span>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <span style="color: black">
             After nearly 9 years of designing, building and perfecting, the final   thick molds that were built to last and to perhaps make many parts, were history. Fortunately, not all art is treated this way. The only way the Primadonna can endure is if the current owners are smart enough to pull new molds or drive very carefully. Quite expensive but its the only insurance.
            </span>
           </span>

            </span>
           </span>
           <span style="color: black">
            <span style="font-family: Verdana">
             Vince
            </span>

             cannot be located. The vin numbered was last registered in 1997 and the plates ZBAIT not in use and no record of it being salvaged. It may be just sitting under a cover in someones garage or yard and they dont know what it is or what to do with it.
