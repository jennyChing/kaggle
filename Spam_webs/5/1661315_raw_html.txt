        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         7 Home Remedies for Upset Stomach
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-11-24T16:49:23+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          November 24, 2012
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://commonsensehome.com/author/laurieneverman/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Laurie Neverman
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://commonsensehome.com/home-remedies-for-upset-stomach/#comments">
           16 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pf-content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://commonsensehome.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/home-remedies-upset-stomach-2.jpg">
           <img alt="Home remedies for upset stomach and indigestion that may help sooth your aching belly, plus recommendations to prevent stomach upset." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9040 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://commonsensehome.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/home-remedies-upset-stomach-2.jpg" data-pin-url="http://commonsensehome.com/home-remedies-for-upset-stomach/" height="600" src="http://commonsensehome.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/home-remedies-upset-stomach-2.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The holiday season seems to leave many of us with upset stomach, indigestion, heart burn, diarrhea and other tummy troubles. If youre coping with stomach flu symptoms, you should take a look at
          <a href="http://commonsensehome.com/coping-with-stomach-flu-symptoms-why-the-brat-diet-may-not-be-your-best-choice/" title="Why the BRAT diet may not be your best choice for stomach flu">
           this post
          </a>
          to find out why the BRAT diet is probably not your best option. For extra protection against food-borne illness, check out 
          <a href="http://commonsensehome.com/protect-yourself-from-salmonella-and-e-coli-naturally/" title="Protect Yourself from Salmonella and E coli naturally">
           Protect Yourself from Salmonella and E Coli Naturally
          </a>
          . For general indigestion, Ive compiled this home remedies for upset stomach that may help sooth your aching belly, plus recommendations on how to avoid it in the first place.
          <span id="more-3703">
          </span>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Home Remedy for Upset Stomach #1  Apple Cider Vinegar (ACV)
         </h2>
         <p>
          When it comes to home remedies,
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001I7MVG0?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B001I7MVG0&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=commosensehom-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="organic apple cider vinegar">
           apple cider vinegar
          </a>
          has got to be near the top of the list as a go to for just about everything. For stomach upset, most folks put a shot of ACV (roughly 1 teaspoon to 1 tablespoon) in a glass of room temperature water and drink it down. Some add a bit of honey to mellow the flavor. As we age, many of us produce less stomach acid, which bogs down digestion. A little extra acid helps get things moving again.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Home Remedy for Upset Stomach #2  Raw Honey
         </h2>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000Z93FQC?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B000Z93FQC&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=commosensehom-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="raw honey">
           Raw honey
          </a>
          contains the enzymes diastase, invertase, catalase, glucose oxidase, acid phosphatase and inulase, which aid in digestion and assimilation. (See
          <a href="http://www.enzyme-facts.com/raw-honey-enzymes.html" target="_blank" title="Raw honey enzymes">
           raw honey enzymes
          </a>
          for more details.) Honey has also been found to be
          <a href="http://www.whfoods.com/genpage.php?tname=foodspice&amp;dbid=96" target="_blank" title="Honey at World's Healthiest Foods">
           effective against E. coli and Candida albicans
          </a>
          . Take a spoonful on its own or combined with your ACV drink (remember  raw honey will have the most active enzymes).
         </p>
         <h2>
          Home Remedy for Upset Stomach #3  Papaya
         </h2>
         <p>
          Papaya fruit or
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00013Z13M?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B00013Z13M&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=commosensehom-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="papaya enzyme">
           papaya enzyme capsules
          </a>
          are both effective at assisting with digestion. Papaya contains the enzymes papain and chymopapain, which are similar to digestive enzymes. Papain is used in many commercial meat tenderizers because it helps break down proteins. Enjoy some fresh papaya or take some papaya enzymes capsules, but dont expect the same results from dried papaya. The Worlds Healthiest Foods indicates that
          <a href="http://whfoods.org/genpage.php?tname=dailytip&amp;dbid=210" target="_blank" title="Dried papaya does not have enzymes">
           dried papaya is likely to be low on enzymes
          </a>
          , which are heat sensitive. Look for organic papaya, as much of the papaya crop (roughly 80%) of the papaya crop is genetically modified.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Home Remedy for Upset Stomach #4  Activated Charcoal
         </h2>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0006LCQ4Q?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B0006LCQ4Q&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=commosensehom-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Activated Charcoal for Stomach Problems">
           Activated charcoal
          </a>
          is more likely to be effective for extracting problem substances than treating general upset stomach. Within hours of consuming the problem food,
          <a href="http://www.healthguidance.org/entry/15696/1/Activated-Charcoal-for-Stomach-Problems.html" target="_blank" title="Activated Charcoal for Stomach Problems">
           Health Guidance.org
          </a>
          recommends keeping some activated charcoal on hand so that it can be consumed in the scenario that a family member should ingest something potentially poisonous such as a medicine overdose, a household cleaner, gone off food, sedatives or narcotics. This will then reduce the bodys absorption by up to 60% which could save their life.  It should be consumed with plenty of water because it may cause dehydration, and should not be mixed with something like chocolate milk or syrup.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Home Remedy for Upset Stomach #5  Baking Soda
         </h2>
         <p>
          Just add one half to one teaspoon of sodium bicarbonate (baking soda) to a glass of water, mix well and drink it down. Best for occasional use only, this fizzy drink can bring on a burp that may ease stomach pressure and will reduce acidity in the stomach.
          <strong>
           Caution: Do not use baking soda if your stomach upset is due to excessive overeating, as there has been at least one incidence of a
           <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/1991/11/27/news/in-rare-cases-of-indigestion-baking-soda-may-be-a-peril.html" target="_blank" title="mans's stomach explodes from baking soda">
            mans stomach exploding from taking baking soda
           </a>
           on a stunningly full stomach.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Home Remedy for Upset Stomach #6  Spices
         </h2>
         <p>
          Cardamom, coriander, fennel, ginger, fenugreek, caraway (top right in photo above) and other herbs have a history of use as digestive aids. Simply chew a few seeds or nibble some candied ginger to help relieve nausea, soothe intestinal spasms and help you flatulate. (Better out than in, after all.)
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <a href="https://www.mountainroseherbs.com/?AID=120969&amp;BID=4100" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Shop Mountain Rose Herbs">
           <img alt="Mountain Rose Herbs. A Herbs, Health &amp; Harmony Com" border="0" class="alignnone pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://commonsensehome.com/home-remedies-for-upset-stomach/" height="250" src="http://mountainroseblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/1-250x250.jpg" width="250"/>
          </a>
         </center>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <em>
           Mountain Rose Herbs stocks a great selection of organic herbs, teas, spices and much more.
          </em>
          <br/>
          <em>
           Your MRH purchase helps to support this site. Thank you!
          </em>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Home Remedy for Upset Stomach #6  Herbal Teas
         </h2>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003D4MW38?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B003D4MW38&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=commosensehom-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="peppermint tea">
           Peppermint tea
          </a>
          was my go to drink for pregnancy nausea, and I still find it soothing for mild stomach upset. Other herbal teas recommended for upset stomach include chamomile, catnip, cinnamon, fennel, ginger, green, lemon balm, licorice, sage, valerian and yarrow. (Read more at 
          <a href="http://www.myindigestionremedies.com/herbal-teas-that-soothe-an-upset-stomach" target="_blank" title="Herbal Teas that Sooth Upset Stomach">
           My Indigestion Remedies
          </a>
          .) In a pinch, if you dont have tea handy, strong mints (like Altoids) may have a similar effect.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Home Remedy for Upset Stomach #7  Live Culture Foods/ Probiotics
         </h2>
         <p>
          If I end up eating a less than stellar meal that does not sit well, the first thing I do is grab for a bottle of
          <a href="http://commonsensehome.com/healthy-homebrew-kombucha/" title="How to Brew Kombucha">
           kombucha
          </a>
          when I get home. Kombucha is a live culture food, which means that it is loaded with friendly bacteria that help your body do its job. Milk kefir,
          <a href="http://commonsensehome.com/water-kefir-versus-kombucha/" title="Water kefir versus Kombucha">
           water kefir
          </a>
          , yogurt,
          <a href="http://commonsensehome.com/how-to-make-sauerkraut/" title="How to Make Sauerkraut">
           kraut
          </a>
          , kimchi or any fermented food will act to boost the populations of good microorganisms in your digestive tract. These are also very good to eat with a meal to avoid the stomach upset in the first place.
         </p>
         <p>
          You can make your own live cultured foods for pennies on the dollar with starter cultures from Cultures for Health or other culture providers. Some cultured food can be made with nothing but the base ingredient (such as vegetables) and salt. (to make sauerkraut). If youd like more information on cultured foods, you can visit the
          <a href="http://commonsensehome.com/recipes/#Live_Culture_FoodsProbioticsFerments" title="Live Cultured Foods">
           live cultured foods section of our recipe page
          </a>
          or check our the book 
          <a href="http://commonsensehome.com/the-complete-idiots-guide-to-fermenting-foods/" title="The Complete Idiot's Guide to Fermenting Foods">
           The Complete Idiots Guide to Fermenting Foods
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h2>
          How to Avoid Upset Stomach  An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure
         </h2>
         <p>
          If you pay attention to your body, you can probably figure out what triggering your upset stomach, and work to heal it or avoid it in the first place.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Common food related causes of upset stomach may include:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           food poisoning
          </li>
          <li>
           overeating ( or drinking too much)
          </li>
          <li>
           greasy foods
          </li>
          <li>
           spicy foods
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Consuming probiotics with your meal (as mentioned above) may help ease digestion. Also consider adding some acid to your meal, such as a vinaigrette dressing or a splash of vinegar or lemon juice. Bitter herbs and greens (such as chicory, endive, dandelion or other strong flavored greens) help stimulate digestive juices. Enjoy in moderation, dont stuff yourself. Chew slowly and thoroughly, giving your stomach time to recognize that its full.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Common lifestyle related causes of upset stomach may include:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           stress
          </li>
          <li>
           smoking
          </li>
          <li>
           excess alcohol or caffeine
          </li>
          <li>
           motion sickness
          </li>
          <li>
           medication
          </li>
          <li>
           pregnancy (the good thing about this one is that it is generally temporary, plus you get a baby
           <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://commonsensehome.com/home-remedies-for-upset-stomach/" src="http://commonsensehome.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
           )
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Do try to relax when you get the chance, and avoid the other negative factors as you are able. When pregnant, be extra careful with anything you ingest. Mint tea is okay, but large amounts of chamomile, for instance, are not recommended.
         </p>
