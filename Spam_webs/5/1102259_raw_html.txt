          <h1 class="article__headline">
           Paul Greengrass To Direct 1984 Adaptation
          </h1>
          <time class="article__date" datetime="2014-11-20">
           20 Nov 2014
          </time>
         </header>
         <div class="article__content">
          <div class="widget__wrapper js-widget js-widget--desktop js-widget--tablet js-widget--mobile visible--desktop visible--tablet visible--mobile" data-widget-id="5acbbe255657b12b3ed857dc75bb1697">
           <section class="widget widget--html">
            <p>
             If you want a picture of the future, imagine a boot stamping on a human face - directed by Paul Greengrass.
            </p>
            <p>
             No, the Academy Award-nominated director isn't launching plans on world domination. Word comes from
             <a href="https://deadline.com/2014/11/1984-paul-greengrass-george-orwell-scott-rudin-1201289630/" target="_blank">
              <em>
               Deadline
              </em>
             </a>
             thatGreengrass (
             <em>
              The Bourne Supremacy, Captain Philips
             </em>
             ) and Sony Pictures are looking to adapt George Orwell's dystopian classic
             <em>
              1984
             </em>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             The source of familiar tropes such asBig Brother, Orwell's
             <em>
              1984
             </em>
             novel tells the bleak story of Winston Smith, an editor responsible for historical revisionism in the all-seeing Ministry of Truth. Struggling to exist in the controlling future state of Oceania, Smith begins a personal rebellion against the terrifying ruling party (the Party) andfalls in love with the wrong girl.
            </p>
            <p>
             Previous adaptations of the novel include the1956 film of Michael Anderson and the 1984 release which starred John Hurt.Orwell's inspired vision still hasmajorinfluences in contemporaryscience fiction works, with this week's release of
             <em>
              The Hunger Games: Mockingjay
             </em>
             a prime example.
            </p>
            <p>
             Sony Pictures has already lined up James Graham (
             <em>
              Finding Neverland
             </em>
             ) to pen the script, while Scott Rudin will produce, having previously worked with Greengrass on the Oscar-nominated
             <em>
              Captain Philips
             </em>
             . Here's hoping they do it justice.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              (Image: Rex)
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              [Via:
              <a href="https://deadline.com/2014/11/1984-paul-greengrass-george-orwell-scott-rudin-1201289630/" target="_blank">
               Deadline
              </a>
              ]
             </em>
            </p>
           </section>
