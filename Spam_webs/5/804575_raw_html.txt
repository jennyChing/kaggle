         <span class="content" data-reactid=".1jf4rhzln9c.5.1:2.1.0.0.5">
          <p>
           Art influences fashion. (See Burberry Prorsums nouveau fall 2014 runway.) And fashion influences art. (See Quentin Joness Almost Human exhibit at the New Museum or Kehinde Wileys Modern Kings series.)
          </p>
          <p>
           Since the Renaissance era, this auspicious connection has held true. Both fields manifest imaginary worlds, emit a statement, and are engineered for self-expression. Its a centuries-old love affair with craftsmanship and beauty that influences and affects one another. This notion is reemphasized in fall 2014 fashions, from Alexander McQueens ivy-print jacquard velvet pants to Givenchys butterfly silk dress.
          </p>
          <p>
           To honor this symbiotic relationship, one American specialty department store has masterfully spun an annual fashion testament for 20 consecutive years. Each year,
           <em>
            <a href="http://www.neimanmarcus.com/Art-of-Fashion/cat51100738_cat41020734_cat000001/c.cat">
             The Art of Fashion
            </a>
           </em>
           is presented by Neiman Marcus as an ode to those who create the beautiful, the imaginative, and the unforgettable; in other words, those who marry art and fashion.
          </p>
          <p>
           Its a project that not only focuses on the most exquisite fashion designers; it celebrates the most sought-after fashion photographers, as well.
          </p>
          <p>
           The photographers are also creative thinkers, explains Georgia Christensen, executive creative director of Neiman Marcus. They see things that dont exist yet. Its shocking at times.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Past
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           This disruptive nature was the spark behind the projects inception in 1994. The campaign hinged on industry great Richard Avedon, 10 years before he passed away. He photographed German model Nadja Auermann in a highly coveted, luxurious style that would become a true first for any luxury retailer: until that point, no fashion players had so firmly placed their trust in the concept of an artistic lens and a living legend.
          </p>
          <div class="embed image portrait row">
           <figure class="embedded-image">
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos.vanityfair.com/2015/01/29/54caae4c494254fc0996aa21_image.jpg" title=""/>
           </figure>
          </div>
          <p>
           After two decades, this vision has proven worthwhile. For me, it ties back to Neiman Marcuss roots in creative thinking, Christensen says. 
           <em>
            The Art of Fashion
           </em>
           has a distinct point of view.
          </p>
          <p>
           And with the likes of legendary photographers including Helmut Newton and Annie Leibovitz at the helm over the years, it has to. Thats why Eddie Nunns, the vice president of Neiman Marcus brand creative, says its more and more challenging each year to pinpoint who the next photographer to bring a unique perspective will be. We ask ourselves, Who will look at this project entirely differently? Nunns says. In the end, theres something alluring about the unexpectedin fashion
           <em>
            and
           </em>
           in art.
          </p>
          <p>
           The ability to relentlessly experiment is a shared maxim and one that Christensen instills in the photographers she chooses. I want
           <em>
            The Art of Fashion
           </em>
           to be them. To be their theater, she emphasizes. This is one way it has continued to entice highly collected photographers to the project.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Present
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           This years lauded photographer, Nadav Kanderthe 42nd to take up the projectwas drawn to the revolutionary series. I was inspired by the legacy of it, Kander explains. The clothing in this falls campaign was amazing. Art is a real response to society, and fashion hits a cultural note in the same way art does. How we present ourselves, for better or for worse, is very relevant. Culturally, fashion has never been more important.
          </p>
          <p>
           Following tradition, the just-shot photography will appear in the September edition of
           <em>
            The Book,
           </em>
           Neiman Marcuss biannual dossier of fashions best designer looks. After all, as retail stores become pop-up galleries, spectacularly curated fashion campaigns (especially ones that clock in at a noteworthy 26 pages) become short-run art books. Collectors, welcome.
          </p>
