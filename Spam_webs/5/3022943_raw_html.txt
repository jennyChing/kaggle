      <h2 align="center" style="color: #00476B ">
       Almond Cookie Guide
      </h2>
      <br/>
      <h3 align="left" style="color:grey">
       <a name="paste">
       </a>
       <strong>
        Almond paste cookies
       </strong>
      </h3>
      There are many types of
      <a href="http://www.gluten-free-around-the-world.com/almond-paste-cookies.html">
       almond paste cookies
      </a>
      , including some
      <a href="http://www.gluten-free-around-the-world.com/almond-macaroons.html">
       almond macaroons
      </a>
      , almond petit fours, and others. In general there are 2 types of recipes for making them.
      <ol>
       <li>
        Recipes calling for prepared almond paste, which may have additional flavorings and additives, including gluten.
       </li>
       <li>
        Cookies made with the almond paste method. These require first processing equal amounts of almonds and sugar together, then mixing in unbeaten egg white. Basically you are making your own almond paste that has the appropriate ingredients for that particular cookie.
       </li>
      </ol>
      Check out this amazing list of
      <a href="http://www.gluten-free-around-the-world.com/almond-paste-cookies.html">
       almond paste cookies
      </a>
      .
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <h3 align="left" style="color:grey">
       <a name="meringue">
       </a>
       <strong>
        Almond meringue cookies
       </strong>
      </h3>
      The cookies in this category are made by first beating the egg whites, or at least half of them, with sugar to make a meringue. Then it's combined with the almonds and other ingredients.

      <br/>
      Some almond macaroons fall in this category, such as the famous Gerbets: 
the smooth-surfaced, and often filled and brightly colored French macaroons of Paris. 

But there are many other types of almond meringue cookies. Just a sampling:
      <br/>
      <br clear="all"/>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Almond Boulders
        </strong>
        are made with Swiss meringue, a method that requires heating the meringue as it's being whipped. Toasted sliced almonds are added, then the cookies are piped into little peaked boulder shapes, or dropped with spoons.
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Almond Souffls
        </strong>
        (*French) are piped into little fluted bon bon cups.
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://italianmade.com/recipe-details/desserts-amaretti-91-888.html" target="_blank">
         <strong>
          Amaretti
         </strong>
        </a>
        The classic Italian cookie, a cousin of the crackled macaroon, made with sweet and bitter almonds. They tend to more crisp than the French version. Like the macaroon, there are also almond paste recipes for amaretti.
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Cinnamon sticks
        </strong>
        , made with a cinnamon flavored meringue and almonds, and rolled into little finger sized sticks.
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Khedives
        </strong>
        Although they have a Persian name, these are French cookies, made with a chocolate almond meringue, and topped with almond meal.
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.gluten-free-around-the-world.com/zimtsterne.html">
         <strong>
          Zimtsterne
         </strong>
        </a>
        (Cinnamon Stars) These are Christmas cookies from Switzerland, often hung on the Christmas Tree.
       </li>
      </ul>

       Almond cookies using other methods

         <em>

         <strong>

        <a cinnamon-stars.html="" href-http:="" www.gluten-free-around-the-world.com="">

        . They are cut-out cookies made from almonds, egg whites, sugar, and cinnamon, and optional flavorings.
       </li>

      <h3 align="left" style="color:grey">
       <a name="eggfree">
       </a>
       Egg free almond cookies, some vegan
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Massafan
        </strong>
        Iraqi almond cookies that are vegan: just almonds, sugar, and rose water. You can find a recipe in Jane Brody's
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0553352954/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=glutenfreenomad-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0553352954" target="_blank">
         Good Food Gourmet

        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=glutenfreenomad-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0553352954" style="border:none !important; margin:0px !important;" width="1"/>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Mandelchen
        </strong>
