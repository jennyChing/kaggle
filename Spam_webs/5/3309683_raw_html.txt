      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       Two More Gruber Videos: Obamacare Architect Boasts Of Law's 'Exploitation' Of The American Voter
      </h1>
      <!-- Make individual group author appear -->
      <div class="bylines">
       <cite class="entity_block type_inline_block">
        <span class="attach_user_popup" data-user-id="" data-user-json="">
         <a class="avatar" href="http://blogs.forbes.com/people/aroy/">
          <img alt="Avik Roy" itemprop="image" src="http://0.gravatar.com/avatar/63e50440e403d1dcd4c8f8b263b51216?s=40&amp;d=http%3A%2F%2F0.gravatar.com%2Favatar%2Fad516503a11cd5ca435acc9bb6523536%3Fs%3D40&amp;r=G"/>
         </a>
         <span>
          <a class="user" href="http://blogs.forbes.com/people/aroy/">
           Avik Roy
          </a>
          <span class="desc">
           ,Forbes Staff
          </span>
         </span>
        </span>
       </cite>

      <div class="mninst">

         <div class="follow_unireg">
          <a class="follow_unireg_link" href="javascript://follow">

     <div class="article_body article" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         <!--donotpaginate-->
        </p>
        <p>
         On Tuesday, it came out that MIT economist Jonathan GruberObamacares architecthad said that he and his Democratic colleagues had concealed the health laws true costs from the public in order to take advantage of the stupidity of the American voter and pass the bill. Gruber then went on MSNBC and claimed that his comments were off the cuff and should be disregarded. But two new videos have emerged: one in which he says that Americans were too stupid to understand Obamacares main tax hike, and another in which he boasts of Obamacares exploitation of the lack of economic understanding of the American voter.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its clear from these videos that Grubers cracks about the intelligence of the American voter werent a spontaneous off the cuff outburst, but rather a regular punch line in his stump speech to audiences around the country. The first video is from a panel discussion at the
         <a href="http://ldi.upenn.edu/ahec2013/agenda">
          University of Pennsylvania
         </a>
         in October 2013; the second from another October 2013 event at
         <a href="http://stream.nts.wustl.edu/R131004001/">
          Washington University in St. Louis
         </a>
         ; the third from a lecture at the
         <a href="http://www.uri.edu/hc/2012/20121030_Gruber_VIDEO.html">
          University of Rhode Island
         </a>
         on November 1, 2012, just before the presidential election of that year.
        </p>
        <iframe class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="365" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/Adrdmmh7bMo" type="text/html" width="485">
        </iframe>
        <div style="padding: 20px 0pt;margin: 20px 0pt;border-bottom: 1px solid #DDDDDD;border-top: 1px solid #DDDDDD">
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Case-Against-Obamacare-Avik-Roy-ebook/dp/B00R22VZ80/?utm_source=article&amp;utm_medium=direct&amp;utm_campaign=againstobamacareebook" style="text-decoration:none;color:#900;font: bold 20px/26px Georgia">
          The Case Against Obamacare: An eBook From Forbes
         </a>
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Case-Against-Obamacare-Avik-Roy-ebook/dp/B00R22VZ80/?utm_source=article&amp;utm_medium=direct&amp;utm_campaign=againstobamacareebook" style="text-decoration:none;font: 15px/18px  Georgia;color:#000">
          Dont be fooled. The new health law has disrupted coverage for millions, and driven up costs for millions more.
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         The second and third videoswhich emerged on Wednesday and Thursday, from
         <a href="http://www.bloomberg.com/politics/articles/2014-11-11/meet-the-mildmannered-investment-advisor-whos-humiliating-the-administration-over-obamacare">
          Rich Weinstein
         </a>
         focus on the origins of Obamacares so-called Cadillac tax on expensive employer-sponsored health insurance plans. Unlike the policies Gruber was discussing at the Penn event, the general concept of paring back the tax break for employer-sponsored health insurance
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/theapothecary/2012/05/14/how-obamacare-tackled-the-pitfalls-of-employer-sponsored-insurance-reform/">
          enjoys bipartisan support
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The Cadillac tax was a direct violation of a 2008 Obama campaign pledge
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Nonetheless, in the Cadillac tax videos, Gruber mocks the lack of economic understanding and stupidity of the American voter, because a more straightforward tax on employer-sponsored coverage encountered political resistance, while the Cadillac taximposed on employers, rather than on workers directlymade it through.
        </p>
        <p>
         This allowed President Obama and his allies to falsely claim that Obamacare wouldnt increase taxes on the middle class, despite the fact that the Cadillac tax clearly affects middle-income Americans,
         <a href="http://www.politicsdaily.com/2009/12/17/cadillac-tax-in-health-plan-would-hit-middle-class-hard/">
          especially unionized industrial laborers
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <iframe class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="365" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/UJVMWjTQh_Y" type="text/html" width="485">
        </iframe>
        <p>
         This was a direct violation of Senator Obamas 2008 vow not to raise middle-class taxes. I can make a firm pledge,
         <a href="http://www.atr.org/president-obama-alters-middle-class-tax-a7123">
          said
         </a>
         Obama on September 12, 2008. Under my plan, no family making less than $250,000 a year will see
         <em>
          any form of tax increase
         </em>
         . Not your income tax, not your payroll tax, not your capital gains taxes,
         <em>
          not any of your taxes
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          In 2008, Obama excoriated John McCains version of the Cadillac tax
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is no small deal. Senator Obamas Republican opponent in 2008, Sen. John McCain (Ariz.), put forth a plan for universal coverage that involved
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/theapothecary/2012/06/20/mitt-romney-outlines-his-plan-to-replace-obamacare/">
          replacing
         </a>
         the tax exclusion for employer-sponsored coverage with a universal tax credit that the uninsured could use to buy health insurance.
        </p>
        <p>
         Obama
         <a href="http://www.politico.com/blogs/bensmith/1008/Obama_attacks_radical_McCain_health_plan.html">
          excoriated
         </a>
         the McCain plan. So heres John McCains radical plan in a nutshell: he taxes health care benefits for the first time in history, said Obama. I dont think thats rightI reject the tired old debate that says we have to choose between two extremes: government-run health care with higher taxesor insurance companies without rules denying people coverage.
        </p>
        <iframe class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="365" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/0Ywc1kSRVPM" type="text/html" width="485">
        </iframe>
        <p>
         So, this begs a question for Jonathan Gruber. Was Senator Obama one of the stupid American voters with a lack of economic understanding? Or was Obama knowledgably, and disingenuously, attacking a feature of Sen. McCains proposal that he later adapted for his own?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center">
         * * *
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          UPDATE
         </strong>
         : At a briefing in Burma on Thursday, White House press secretary Josh Earnest
         <a href="http://insider.foxnews.com/2014/11/13/video-white-house-spox-earnest-finally-addresses-stupid-americans-obamacare-remark" target="_blank">
          said
         </a>
         that he disagrees vigorously with [Gruber's] assessment that Democrats were intentionally deceptive in the way they passed the health law.
        </p>
