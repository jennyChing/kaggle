              <h1 class="documentFirstHeading">
               National Stolen Art File
              </h1>
              <p class="documentDescription">
               <span class="" id="parent-fieldname-description-bb4f914c05083881d223ea39a8622d20">
                A new online tool enables quick and easy searches of our art theft database.
               </span>
              </p>
              <div class="plain" id="parent-fieldname-text">
               <p class="graphicboxheader" style="text-align: center; ">
                <b>
                 Tracking Stolen Art
                </b>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 <b>
                  <br/>
                  New Resource for Investigators and the Public
                 </b>
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 12/2/2010
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </p>
               <table align="left" border="0">
                <tbody>
                 <tr>
                  <th>
                   <img alt="Info  Belcher Engraved Bottle  Caddo Nation engraved ceramic bottle with incised design, evidence of wear" class="image-inline" src="https://www.fbi.gov/news/stories/2010/december/national-stolen-art-file/image/info-belcher-engraved-bottle-caddo-nation-engraved-ceramic-bottle-with-incised-design-evidence-of-wear-1" title="Missing Caddo Indian Pottery Stolen from Southern Arkansas University"/>
                  </th>
                  <th style="width: 5px;">
                  </th>
                 </tr>
                 <tr>
                  <td style="text-align: center; ">
                   <span class="blackgraphtx10">
                    One of the missing Caddo Nation items.
                    <br/>
                    See the
                    <a class="external-link" href="http://www.fbi.gov/about-us/investigate/vc_majorthefts/arttheft/NSAF/020714/@@search-artcrimes?getCrimeCategory=&amp;maker=&amp;AdditionalCatalogedData=&amp;TitleAndDescription=caddo&amp;period=&amp;form.submitted=1&amp;form.button.search=Search">
                     entire list
                    </a>
                    .
                    <br/>
                   </span>
                  </td>
                  <td>
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                </tbody>
               </table>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 More than four years after 26 pieces of Caddo Indian pottery were stolen from Southern Arkansas University there is still no trace of the rare bottles, pots, or
                 <b>
                 </b>
                 bowls. But now there is a new tool that could allow the art communityand the publicto help solve the case.
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 <b>
                  With the recent redesign of FBI.gov, we debuted the
                  <a class="internal-link" href="https://www.fbi.gov/about-us/investigate/vc_majorthefts/arttheft/national-stolen-art-file">
                   National Stolen Art File (NSAF)
                  </a>
                 </b>
                 . This free online tool allows anyone to quickly and easily search a database of thousands of stolen artworks and contact us if they have information about the items.
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 Its impossible for the FBI to look for every piece of stolen art in the country, said Bonnie Magness-Gardiner, who manages our art theft program. Having this online, searchable database accessible to the public as well as local law enforcement takes advantage of our technological resources to help curb the age-old problem of art theft.
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 Currently, the database contains about 7,000 images. Some were already online, but not in an easy-to-use format. Now, anyone can search a variety of categoriesfrom books and bowls to stamps and stained glassor enter keywords such as an artists name, period date, or artwork title.
                </span>
               </p>
               <table align="right" border="0">
                <tbody>
                 <tr>
                  <th style="width: 5px;">
                  </th>
                  <th>
                   <a class="internal-link" href="https://www.fbi.gov/about-us/investigate/vc_majorthefts/arttheft/national-stolen-art-file">
                    <img alt="National Stolen Art File search" class="image-inline" src="https://www.fbi.gov/news/stories/2010/december/national-stolen-art-file/image/national-stolen-art-file-search" title="National Stolen Art File Search"/>
                   </a>
                  </th>
                 </tr>
                 <tr>
                  <td>
                  </td>
                  <td style="text-align: center; ">
                   <span class="blackgraphtx10">
                    Using the new search tool pictured above, you can sort stolen objects by
                    <br/>
                    90+ different types (paintings, sculptures, etc.) and by other categories.
                    <br/>
                    To begin your search, visit the
                    <a class="internal-link" href="https://www.fbi.gov/about-us/investigate/vc_majorthefts/arttheft/national-stolen-art-file">
                     National Stolen Art File webpage
                    </a>
                    .
                    <br/>
                   </span>
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                </tbody>
               </table>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 <b>
                  Search for Monet, for example, and several stolen works are returned.
                 </b>
                 Click on an image and it enlarges. In many cases, details are provided about the artwork, including the works dimensions and other identifying information.
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 For those considering buying and selling works of artgallery owners, brokers, and private collectorsthe database is one way to make sure their potential purchases are legitimately for sale. If a collector or gallery owner checks our site and sees that a piece of art is stolen, thats an immediate red flag, and they can contact us, Magness-Gardiner said.
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 For law enforcement, particularly local agencies that investigate burglaries and other thefts, the database will eventually be accessible through the Law Enforcement Online (LEO) website, so local police departments can add to the list. As the database grows, Magness-Gardiner said, the number of cases that can be solvedand artworks recoveredwill increase. And as a result, we will help keep stolen art out of the marketplace.
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 <b>
                  Items in the database such as the Caddo Indian pottery contain images of the original worksnot copies or duplicatesand they all have uniquely identifiable characteristics, such as an artists signature, damage marks, or other one-of-a-kind traits.
                 </b>
                 Any object stolen within the U.S. with a value of more than $2,000 can be listed. There are some international pieces in the file, but this is primarily a resource for reporting the theft of artwork within the United States.
                </span>
               </p>
               <table align="center" border="0">
                <tbody>
                 <tr>
                  <th>
                   <img alt="Largo: Image of woman playing a cello (screen shot)" class="image-inline" src="https://www.fbi.gov/news/stories/2010/december/national-stolen-art-file/image/largo-image-of-woman-playing-a-cello-screen-shot" title="Largo: Image of Woman Playing a Cello (Screen Shot)"/>
                  </th>
                 </tr>
                 <tr>
                  <td style="text-align: center; ">
                   <span class="blackgraphtx10">
                    Click on any thumbnail image in the National Stolen Art File to enlarge it
                   </span>
                   <span class="blackgraphtx10">
                    an example is
                   </span>
                   <span class="blackgraphtx10">
                    shown above.
                    <br/>
                   </span>
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                </tbody>
               </table>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 <br/>
                 We want to solicit any and all information from the public about the stolen material, Magness-Gardiner said. And we in turn will provide this information to a much wider audience. This is a first step, and as we grow, we look forward to working with local law enforcement, the art community, and the public.
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 If you have information about an item in the National Stolen Art File, contact your
                 <a class="internal-link" href="https://www.fbi.gov/contact-us/field">
                  local FBI office
                 </a>
                 or
                 <a class="external-link" href="https://tips.fbi.gov">
                  submit a tip
                 </a>
                 at FBI.gov. Requests to have items added to the database must come through a law enforcement agency accompanied by a physical description of the object, a photograph if available, and a copy of any police reports or other relevant information. Additionally, notification is required when works on the list have been recovered. Failure to notify NSAF may result in recovered works remaining on the list.
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="blackgraphtx">
                 <b>
                  Resources
                  <br/>

                 </b>
                 <a class="internal-link" href="https://www.fbi.gov/about-us/investigate/vc_majorthefts/arttheft/national-stolen-art-file">
                  <span class="external-link">
                   Search the National Stolen Art File
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 <br/>
                 -
                 <a class="internal-link" href="https://www.fbi.gov/about-us/investigate/vc_majorthefts/arttheft">
                  Art Theft website
                 </a>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </p>
