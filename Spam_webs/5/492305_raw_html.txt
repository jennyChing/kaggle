        <p>
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          They may not be quite as impressive as gazing into the Grand Canyon or marvelling at the Pyramids.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          But for those who cant afford to spend thousands fulfilling their lifetime ambitions, here is a set of homegrown suggestions  the Great British Bucket List.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          From seeing Tower Bridge being raised to watching the sun come up over Stonehenge on midsummers day, the 50 things to do before you die compilation is likely to appeal to the more patriotic among us
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">

          <font style="font-size:1.2em;">

        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          Put together from a survey of 1,000 British adults, the list is headed by the simple but truly British pleasure of tucking into fish and chips while sitting on a pier in a typical seaside resort.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          The poll, carried out by search engine Ask Jeeves, revealed that our favourite homegrown activities range from the cultural to the culinary and sporting, with plenty of sight-seeing thrown in for good measure.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          For those who cant afford to swim with dolphins or climb Kilimanjaro, it recommends seeing whales off the coast of Wales and climbing Ben Nevis in Scotland.And there are plenty of world class musical experiences available in the UK, from the Glastonbury festival to the Proms.
         </font>
        </p>

         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          For sports lovers, the list suggests holding the FA Cup, seeing a Brit play at Wimbledon, or lining the streets to cheer on runners in the London Marathon. Or for something different but still very British, it urges us to take in the unique atmosphere of the world darts championships which are held annually in the UK.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          Meanwhile, foodies are encouraged to dine at a Gordon Ramsay restaurant, eat haggis on Burns Night or simply try a deep fried Mars Bar. Other uniquely British pursuits include seeing a panto and having a pint in the Rovers Return  the pub in Coronation Street  as part of a guided tour of the soaps TV set.
         </font>
        </p>
