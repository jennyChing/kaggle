         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <em>
           Potstickers are unbelievablyeasy to make. Best of all, theyre freezer-friendly, perfect for those busy weeknights!
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Sesame Chicken Potstickers - These are unbelievably easy to make. And they're freezer-friendly too, perfect for those busy weeknights!" class="aligncenter wp-image-9681 size-full" height="975" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/IMG_6242edit.jpg" width="650"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://damndelicious.net/2014/08/08/sesame-chicken-potstickers/&amp;media=http://damndelicious.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/IMG_6242edit.jpg&amp;description=Sesame Chicken Potstickers')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Whenever I have a chance, I make huge batches of potstickers. Theyre one of my most favorite things to eat, so its always super convenient to have a batch of homemade potsticker right in the freezer. And its always helpful to have a helping hand. Plus, its even more fun when you compare your pretty potsticker folding skills while your helping partnerdestructs them.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-9680">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Sesame Chicken Potstickers - These are unbelievably easy to make. And they're freezer-friendly too, perfect for those busy weeknights!" class="aligncenter wp-image-9682 size-full" height="975" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/IMG_6241edit.jpg" width="650"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://damndelicious.net/2014/08/08/sesame-chicken-potstickers/&amp;media=http://damndelicious.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/IMG_6241edit.jpg&amp;description=Sesame Chicken Potstickers')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Still, pretty or not, these potstickers have quickly become a favorite of mine. Ive tried
          <a href="/2013/02/20/shrimp-dumplings/">
           shrimp
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/2014/03/01/potstickers/">
           pork potstickers
          </a>
           both of which I love  but the chicken has quicklybecome a favorite. These chicken potstickers are also more budget friendly, which become super helpful when you double/triple the recipe for freezing.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Sesame Chicken Potstickers - These are unbelievably easy to make. And they're freezer-friendly too, perfect for those busy weeknights!" class="aligncenter wp-image-9683 size-full" height="975" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/IMG_6247edit.jpg" width="650"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://damndelicious.net/2014/08/08/sesame-chicken-potstickers/&amp;media=http://damndelicious.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/IMG_6247edit.jpg&amp;description=Sesame Chicken Potstickers')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Best of all, you can completely customize these fillings by adding or omitting your preferred veggies. Just be sure to make extra to freeze  these will come in super handy when you have those Asian potsticker cravings!
         </p>

             <div class="b-b h-1 strong" id="zlrecipe-title" itemprop="name">
              Sesame Chicken Potstickers
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="zlmeta zlclear">
             <div class="fl-l width-50">
              <p id="zlrecipe-prep-time">
               Prep Time
               <span content="PT20M" itemprop="prepTime">
                20 minutes
               </span>
              </p>
              <p id="zlrecipe-cook-time">
               Cook Time
               <span content="PT10M" itemprop="cookTime">
                10 minutes
               </span>
              </p>
              <p id="zlrecipe-total-time">
               Total Time
               <span content="PT30M" itemprop="totalTime">
                30 minutes
               </span>
              </p>
             </div>
             <div class="fl-l width-50">
              <p id="zlrecipe-yield">
               Yield
               <span itemprop="recipeYield">
                36 potstickers
               </span>
              </p>
             </div>
             <div class="zlclear">
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="img-desc-wrap">
             <p class="t-a-c hide-card">
              <img alt="Sesame Chicken Potstickers" class="photo" itemprop="image" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/IMG_6241edit.jpg" style="width: 180px;" title="Sesame Chicken Potstickers"/>
             </p>
             <div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description">
              <p class="summary italic">
               Potstickers are unbelievably easy to make. Best of all, they're freezer-friendly, perfect for those busy weeknights!
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
             Ingredients
            </p>
            <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 pound ground chicken
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
              3 ounces shiitake mushrooms, diced
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 cloves garlic, pressed
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 green onions, thinly sliced
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 tablespoons reduced sodium soy sauce
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 tablespoon sesame oil
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 teaspoon rice vinegar
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/4 teaspoon white pepper
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
              36 2-inch won ton wrappers
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 tablespoons vegetable oil
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
              Soy sauce, for serving
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
             Instructions
            </p>
            <ul class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              In a large bowl, combine chicken, mushrooms, garlic, green onions, soy sauce, sesame oil, ginger, rice vinegar and white pepper.*
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              To assemble the dumplings, place wrappers on a work surface. Spoon 1 tablespoon of the chicken mixture into the center of each wrapper. Using your finger, rub the edges of the wrappers with water. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape, pinching the edges to seal.*
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add potstickers in a single layer and cook until golden and crisp, about 2-3 minutes per side.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Serve immediately with soy sauce, if desired.
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-notes">
             Notes
            </p>
            <div id="zlrecipe-notes-list">
             <p class="notes">
              <span class="italic">
               *The filling in the potstickers does not have to be cooked prior to wrapping.
              </span>
              <span class="italic">
               <br/>
               *To freeze, place uncooked potstickers in a single layer on a baking sheet overnight. Transfer to freezer bags.
               <br/>
