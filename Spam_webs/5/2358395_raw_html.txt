       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Stuffed Peppers Soup
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-01-13T09:46:12+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 13, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/author/lyuba" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            lyuba
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/2014/01/stuffed-peppers-soup.html#comments">
           57 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14893145" data-link="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/2014/01/stuffed-peppers-soup.html" data-summary="" data-title="Stuffed Peppers Soup">
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         This is a comforting
         <strong>
          Stuffed Peppers Soup
         </strong>
         thats made with ground beef, fresh vegetables and rice. This
         <strong>
          homemade soup
         </strong>
         holds all the wonderful flavors you will find in
         <strong>
          stuffed peppers
         </strong>
         and is so easy to make.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Stuffed Peppers Soup | from willcookforsmiles.com #soup #groundbeef #stuffedpeppers" class="size-full wp-image-5146 aligncenter" height="750" src="http://willcookforsmiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Stuffed-Peppers-Soup-from-willcookforsmiles.com-soup-groundbeef-stuffedpeppers.jpg" width="496"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         I love
         <strong>
          stuffed peppers
         </strong>
         and Ive experimented with different stuffing several times. I still find the original
         <strong>
          ground beef
         </strong>
         and rice combination the best. Making a
         <strong>
          soup
         </strong>
         with those flavors was amazing. I really needed to make a soup that used something other than chicken as meat.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         As I mentioned in several posts before, I like to use as many fresh ingredients as much as possible and I like to make my own stock. Great thing about ground meats (particularly red meats like pork and beef) is that it gives out a lot of juice. When I make chili, I barely have to add any stock, just a little bit. Since we are making a soup, we will add some stock but a lot of flavor with come from
         <strong>
          ground beef
         </strong>
         juices.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         I also like to use different colors of bell peppers. First of all, it adds a touch of color the to soup. Second of all, each bell pepper type has a different flavor. Using 2-3 different colors of bell peppers will add extra flavor complexity to the soup.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          **
         </strong>
         If you are thinking about what to do with the leftover peppers, I like to make a quick and delicious salad. I just dice the remaining peppers and dice a tomato. Mix the peppers, tomato, some feta cheese and a little bit of olive oil and balsamic vinegar. Salt and pepper to taste and you got yourself a delicious and healthy quick salad.
         <strong>
          **
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Stuffed Peppers Soup 3 from willcookforsmiles.com #soup #groundbeef #stuffedpeppers" class="size-full wp-image-5145 aligncenter" height="496" src="http://willcookforsmiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Stuffed-Peppers-Soup-3-from-willcookforsmiles.com-soup-groundbeef-stuffedpeppers.jpg" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Stuffed Peppers Soup
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://willcookforsmiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Stuffed-Peppers-Soup-3-from-willcookforsmiles.com-soup-groundbeef-stuffedpeppers.jpg" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/easyrecipe-print/5142-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 lb ground beef
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             medium yellow onion, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 tbs olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 large tomato, peeled and diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             red bell pepper, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             yellow bell pepper, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             green bell pepper, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 cups of beef broth
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tbs tomato paste
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup of dry Jasmine rice (that's my favorite but you can use whichever you like)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp dry basil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp dry oregano
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            salt and pepper to taste
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a large pot, over medium heat, heat up the oil and add diced onion, tomato and peppers. Saute for a few minutes, until fragrant and onion starts to be transparent.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the ground beef and mix well. Cover and cook, stirring occasionally, until the beef is mostly cooked.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the beef broth and tomato paste. Stir well until all the tomato paste is incorporated.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add basil, oregano, salt and pepper. Taste to make sure you have enough salt.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cook for about 15 minutes, covered.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add rice and lower the heat to low. Cover and wait until the rice is done.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Serve once the rice is cooked.
           </li>
          </ol>
