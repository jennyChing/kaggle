        <div class="body " data-context="5286920" data-friendly-name="Article Inline Link Tracking" data-max-times-to-track-links="5" data-widget="tm.omniture.tracking" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          If youre planning a spring clean, its tempting to sweep everything into the bin bags. But what if your clutter is hiding a few gems?
         </p>
         <p>
          We asked Drew Hunter, the presenter of Salvage Hunters, what to look for:
         </p>
         <h3>
          1. Jewellery
         </h3>
         <p>
          The difference between a gemstone and paste is very little to the untrained eye. I once saw something that was in a junk jewellery box that went for nearly 5,000.
         </p>
         <h3>
          2. Paintings and drawings
         </h3>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <figure class="inline-image clearfix ">
           <div class="image-credit-wrapper">
            <span class="image-credit">
             Getty
            </span>
            <img alt="Dresses designed by the fashion house 'Biba' are display at the Victoria and Albert museum" class="captify" height="409" src="http://i1.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article774758.ece/ALTERNATES/s615/Dresses%20designed%20by%20the%20fashion%20house%20'Biba'%20are%20display%20at%20the%20Victoria%20and%20Albert%20museum" title="Designs by fashion house Biba go down well" width="615"/>
           </div>
           <figcaption>
            Designs by fashion house Biba go down well
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </p>
         <p>
          They turn up everywhere. It really helps if it is by a known artist who is
          <a href="http://www.apr.ac.uk/artists/home.htm" rel="noFollow">
           on an art register
          </a>
          . If your parents or grandparents are in their late 80s, they will have been buying things in the 60s. The chance of finding an Old Master is slim - look for good quality prints from a name like Biba.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h3>
          3. Garden furniture
         </h3>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Some garden antiques are not moved because they are very heavy. Every year I find one or two Georgian and Victorian garden sets. Dont think about how it looks - you would be covered with lichen if you were outside for 150 years. Whatever you do, dont clean an antique. You need to sell it as it is.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h3>
         </h3>
         <h3>
          4. Postcards
         </h3>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <figure class="inline-image clearfix portrait">
           <img alt="" height="776" src="http://i1.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article5286965.ece/BINARY/Postcard.jpg" title="Old postcards can have more than sentimental value" width="467"/>
           <figcaption>
            Old postcards can have more than sentimental value
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </p>
         <p>
          Postcards have a low value, but it is a value. Saucy postcards from the Edwardian period do well. And postcards that have not been written on.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h3>
          5. Mistakes
         </h3>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Look for anything that is a mistake or is genuinely wrong. It could just be spelt wrong. Mugs dont have much value, but if you had the abdicated King Edwards coronation mug it might be worth a few extra pounds.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h3>
          6. Pop culture
         </h3>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <figure class="inline-image clearfix portrait">
           <img alt="" height="409" src="http://i3.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article5286993.ece/ALTERNATES/s615/Harry-Potter-toys.jpg" title="The new Star Wars?" width="615"/>
           <figcaption>
            The new Star Wars?
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </p>
         <p>
          Harry Potter models will be the new Star Wars. The people who collected these are in their mid-20s now. When they get into their 30s and start having children they will want them back. Anything to do with the first Big Brother is valuable, because it was a social phenomenon.
         </p>
         <p>
          For more on valuable toys,
          <a href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/money/8-types-old-toy-could-5010229">
           check out this guide
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h3>
          7. Anything Georgian (18th century)
         </h3>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Victorians created mass production. But in the Georgian era they made things to be one off. So these things are now rare and more desirable. Even a pair of shoes could be worth money.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h3>
          I think Ive found an antique - what do I do next?
         </h3>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Go to your local auction house -
          <a href="http://www.auctionhouse.uk.net/auctioneers" rel="noFollow">
           you can find it here
          </a>
          . They have free valuation days. Go to the larger ones if possible. They will have a look at it they wont charge you for it. Always get a second opinion.
         </p>

          The new series of Salvage Hunters begins on Quest from 18 March.
