       <h1 class="headline" itemprop="name">
        Deus Ex: The Eyeborg Documentary
       </h1>
       <div class="byline small">
        <span class="post_author_intro">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="post_author" itemprop="author">
         <a href="https://www.singularityweblog.com/author/Socrates/" rel="nofollow">
          Socrates
         </a>
        </span>
        <meta content="2011-09-03" itemprop="datePublished"/>
        <meta content="2015-01-20" itemprop="dateModified"/>
        <span class="post_date_intro">
         on
        </span>
        <span class="post_date" title="2011-09-03">
         September 3, 2011
        </span>
        <a class="num_comments_link" href="https://www.singularityweblog.com/deus-ex-the-eyeborg-documentary/#disqus_thread" rel="nofollow">
         Comments
        </a>
       </div>

      <div class="post_content" itemprop="articleBody">
       <div class="pf-content">
        <p>
         It has been some time ago that I first posted the
         <a href="https://www.singularityweblog.com/deus-ex-stunning-trailer-for-human-revolution/" title="Deus Ex Stunning Trailer for Human Revolution">
          Deus Ex: Human Revolution
         </a>
         original trailer about the stunning video game from Eidos. The clip is so visually captivating that I am forced to re-watch it every once-in-a-while and ponder the myriad of profound issues that it touches on. Even more so, a reader of this blog once told me that it was seeing the Deus Ex trailer for the first time that made him aware of
         <a href="http://www.singularitysymposium.com/transhumanism.html" title="Transhumanism">
          transhumanism
         </a>
         and consequently pushed him to devote his life to studying it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enter Rob Spence. Those who missed
         <a href="https://www.singularityweblog.com/the-eyeborg-rob-spence-on-singularity-1-on-1/" title="I Am Eyeborg: Rob Spence on Singularity 1 on 1">
          my interview with Rob
         </a>
         should know that he is known as the Eyeborg  a self proclaimed cyborg who lost an eye in an accident and replaced it with a wireless video camera. Rob is a wacky documentarian and recently joined forces with the gang from Square Enix to create
         <strong>
          Deus Ex: The Eyeborg Documentary
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.singularityweblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/Eyeborg.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-10954" height="68" src="https://www.singularityweblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/Eyeborg-300x68.jpg" title="Eyeborg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         When I posted the original trailer I asked: What are the odds of those clips becoming reality?
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, as it turns out, this 15 minute documentary attempts to reply to that (rhetorical) question by investigating the latest in prosthetics, cybernetics and human augmentation.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, how far are we from the future presented to us in Deus Ex: Human Revolution?
        </p>
        <p>
         Check out the movie to find out!
        </p>
