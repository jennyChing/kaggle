     <p>
      <b>
       HMS
       <i>
        Cockchafer
       </i>
      </b>
      was a
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">
       Royal Navy
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insect_class_gunboat" title="Insect class gunboat">
       Insect-class
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Gunboat" title="Gunboat">
       gunboat
      </a>
      . She was built by
      <a href="/wiki/Barclay_Curle" title="Barclay Curle">
       Barclay Curle
      </a>
      and launched on 17 December 1915 as the fifth Royal Navy ship to carry this name.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-battleships-cruisers_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-battleships-cruisers-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Insect_Class_gunboats_1_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Insect_Class_gunboats_1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Insect class was originally designed for service on the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danube_River" title="Danube River">
       Danube River
      </a>
      but most of them spent much of their service on Chinese rivers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="First_World_War">
       First World War
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Cockchafer_(1915)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: First World War">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      During the
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">
       First World War
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      was assigned to the defence of the south east coast of England, based at
      <a href="/wiki/Brightlingsea" title="Brightlingsea">
       Brightlingsea
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-battleships-cruisers_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-battleships-cruisers-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      During the
      <a href="/wiki/Russian_Civil_War" title="Russian Civil War">
       Russian Civil War
      </a>
      , she served with some of her sister ships as part of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allied_Intervention_in_the_Russian_Civil_War" title="Allied Intervention in the Russian Civil War">
       British intervention forces
      </a>
      fighting in support of
      <a href="/wiki/White_movement" title="White movement">
       White Russian
      </a>
      forces on the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Dvina" title="Northern Dvina">
       Dvina River
      </a>
      from 19181919.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On 17 January 1920, the Insect-class ships
      <a href="/wiki/HMS_Cricket_(1915)" title="HMS Cricket (1915)">
       <i>
        Cricket
       </i>
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/HMS_Moth_(1915)" title="HMS Moth (1915)">
       <i>
        Moth
       </i>
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Mantis_(1915)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Mantis (1915) (page does not exist)">
       <i>
        Mantis
       </i>
      </a>
      and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Cicala(1915)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Cicala(1915) (page does not exist)">
       <i>
        Cicala
       </i>
      </a>
      set out from
      <a href="/wiki/Chatham_Dockyard" title="Chatham Dockyard">
       Chatham
      </a>
      , England for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912-49)" title="Republic of China (1912-49)">
       China
      </a>
      .
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      was stationed on the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River">
       Yangtze River
      </a>
      where her duties were patrolling and protection of British nationals and interests in China.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Wanhsien_Incident">
       Wanhsien Incident
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Cockchafer_(1915)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wanhsien Incident">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      One significant event which
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      was involved in was the Wanhsien Incident
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in August and September 1926. Wanhsien, now known as
      <a href="/wiki/Wanzhou_District" title="Wanzhou District">
       Wanzhou District
      </a>
      , is a port on the Yangtze River about 1,500 miles (2,400km) upstream from
      <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">
       Shanghai
      </a>
      . The local
      <a href="/wiki/Warlord" title="Warlord">
       warlord
      </a>
      , Marshal
      <a href="/wiki/Wu_Peifu" title="Wu Peifu">
       Wu Pei Fu
      </a>
      controlled the area and his local commander was General
      <a href="/wiki/Yang_Sen_(1884%E2%80%931977)" title="Yang Sen (18841977)">
       Yang Sen
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      General Yang's troops seized the British
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merchant_ship" title="Merchant ship">
       merchant ship
      </a>
      , SS
      <i>
       Wanhsien
      </i>
      in August 1926,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tolley.Wanhsien_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Tolley.Wanhsien-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      which belonged to the
      <a href="/wiki/The_China_Navigation_Company" title="The China Navigation Company">
       The China Navigation Company
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SL_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-SL-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The crew of
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      heard the British crew calling for help and sent an officer and boarding party to
      <i>
       Wanhsien
      </i>
      to investigate. They found the ship occupied by 100 Chinese soldiers. The Navy party obtained the release of the ship after a heated argument.
     </p>
     <p>
      On 29 August 1926,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      China Navigation Co. ship, the SS
      <i>
       Wanliu
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SL_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-SL-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      suddenly made a U turn while a wooden boat full of Chinese soldiers, guns, bullets and allowances passed by. The wave caused by the movement of
      <i>
       Wanliu
      </i>
      capsized the wooden boat. 58 soldiers were drowned. Thousands of bullets, hundreds of guns and some allowances were lost. The
      <i>
       Wanliu
      </i>
      steamed upstream while the Chinese soldiers aboard attempted to capture the ship. They were unable to do so by the time the
      <i>
       Wanliu
      </i>
      reached Wanhsien where
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      sent a boarding party to remove the soldiers.
     </p>
     <p>
      The reports about the escape of the
      <i>
       Wanliu
      </i>
      reached General Yang whose troops captured SS
      <i>
       Wanhsien
      </i>
      again. The British officers were held aboard.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tolley.Wanhsien_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Tolley.Wanhsien-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Another British merchant ship SS
      <i>
       Wantung
      </i>
      was also captured. Chinese troops with artillery gathered on the shore. General Yang seized several of
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      's Chinese crewmembers who were ashore and one was killed in full view of the rest of the crew. Yang refused to negotiate with the commander of
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      and the senior officer on the Upper Yangtze, commander of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Widgeon_(1904)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Widgeon (1904) (page does not exist)">
       <i>
        Widgeon
       </i>
      </a>
      headed for Wanhsien while
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      remained at Wanhsien in a standoff with the overwhelming numbers of Chinese troops.
     </p>
     <p>
      On 1 September 1926
      <i>
       Widgeon
      </i>
      arrived at Wanhsien but negotiations did not go well and the rear admiral on the Yangtze decided that the matter would have to be settled by force. A British merchant ship, SS
      <i>
       Kiawo
      </i>
      , was
      <a href="/wiki/Camouflage" title="Camouflage">
       camouflaged
      </a>
      and armoured and manned by a naval crew gathered from
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      , the light cruiser
      <a href="/wiki/HMS_Despatch_(D30)" title="HMS Despatch (D30)">
       <i>
        Despatch
       </i>
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       Scarab
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Mantis
      </i>
      boarded
      <i>
       Kiawo
      </i>
      and she sailed on 4 September 1926.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tolley.Wanhsien_6-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Tolley.Wanhsien-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the evening of 5 September 1926 the
      <i>
       Kiawo
      </i>
      arrived in sight of Wanhsien. The plan was to board and re-take SS
      <i>
       Wanhsien
      </i>
      and SS
      <i>
       Wantung
      </i>
      . While
      <i>
       Widgeon
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      would provide covering fire.
      <i>
       Kiawo
      </i>
      came under fire from the Chinese troops ashore. She came alongside
      <i>
       Wanhsien
      </i>
      and boarded under fire. The boarding party rescued the British seaman held onboard after fierce fighting.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the meantime, Chinese troops onshore and aboard
      <i>
       Wantung
      </i>
      opened fire on
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Widgeon
      </i>
      which returned fire. The boarding party aboard SS
      <i>
       Wanhsien
      </i>
      suffered a number of casualties including the senior British officer from
      <i>
       Despatch
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/Sub-lieutenant" title="Sub-lieutenant">
       sub-lieutenant
      </a>
      who were killed. Having rescued the British merchant seamen onboard SS
      <i>
       Wanhsien
      </i>
      the attacking force retired to SS
      <i>
       Kiawo
      </i>
      . After an hour of fighting, the action was discontinued and the two merchant ships were abandoned. The British ships then retired having rescued the crews.
     </p>
     <p>
      The British ships caused casualties of nearly a thousand Chinese civilians and soldiers in the Wanhsien Incident. Thousands of shops and homes were destroyed by shells. In the end, General Yang was pressured to release SS
      <i>
       Wanhsien
      </i>
      and SS
      <i>
       Wantung
      </i>
      first. It was unclear whether British made any compensations.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Second_World_War_service">
       Second World War service
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Cockchafer_(1915)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Second World War service">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1939
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      started the war still on Yangtze River patrol. After consideration for conversion to a
      <a href="/wiki/Minelayer" title="Minelayer">
       minelayer
      </a>
      , she was then transferred to the East Indies Squadron. In 1941 she assisted in the landings of British and
      <a href="/wiki/British_Indian_Army" title="British Indian Army">
       Indian Army
      </a>
      troops at
      <a href="/wiki/Basra" title="Basra">
       Basra
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Iraq" title="Kingdom of Iraq">
       Iraq
      </a>
      during the
      <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Iraqi_War" title="Anglo-Iraqi War">
       Anglo-Iraqi War
      </a>
      . She played host to the
      <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">
       regent
      </a>
      of Iraq,
      <a href="/wiki/%27Abd_al-Ilah" title="'Abd al-Ilah">
       Amir Abdul Illah
      </a>
      who had been deposed and fled an
      <a href="/wiki/Assassination" title="Assassination">
       assassination
      </a>
      plot in
      <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">
       Baghdad
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Playfair.Iraq_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Playfair.Iraq-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jackson.Iraq_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Jackson.Iraq-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      She also played a part in the
      <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Soviet_invasion_of_Iran" title="Anglo-Soviet invasion of Iran">
       Anglo-Soviet invasion of Iran
      </a>
      , which was the invasion of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_State_of_Iran" title="Imperial State of Iran">
       Iran
      </a>
      by
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
       British and Commonwealth forces
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
       Soviet Union
      </a>
      , codenamed Operation Countenance, from 25 August 1941 to 17 September 1941. The purpose of the invasion was to secure Iranian
      <a href="/wiki/Oil_field" title="Oil field">
       oil fields
      </a>
      and ensure
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supply_line" title="Supply line">
       supply lines
      </a>
      (see
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Persian_Corridor" title="Persian Corridor">
        Persian Corridor
       </a>
      </i>
      ) for the Soviets fighting against
      <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">
       Nazi Germany
      </a>
      on the
      <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Front_(World_War_II)" title="Eastern Front (World War II)">
       Eastern Front
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jackson.Iraq_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Jackson.Iraq-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1943,
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      was transferred to the
      <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">
       Mediterranean
      </a>
      Fleet at
      <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">
       Malta
      </a>
      . She took part in support operations for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operation_Husky" title="Operation Husky">
       Operation Husky
      </a>
      , the invasion of
      <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">
       Sicily
      </a>
      . Following the invasion of
      <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">
       Italy
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Elba" title="Invasion of Elba">
       invasion of Elba
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       Cockchafer
      </i>
      was employed on harbour duties in
      <a href="/wiki/Taranto" title="Taranto">
       Taranto
      </a>
      in late 1944. In 1945, she was despatched to the Eastern Fleet in the
      <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">
       Indian Ocean
      </a>
      again to support operations in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Burma" title="British Burma">
       Burma
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      After the end of the war against
      <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">
       Japan
      </a>
      in August 1945, she was sent to
      <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">
       Singapore
      </a>
      where she was placed in Reserve. In 1949 as the last surviving Insect class gunboat, she was sold for scrap and broken up.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Insect_Class_gunboats_1_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Insect_Class_gunboats_1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
