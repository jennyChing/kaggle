         <p>
          The other morning I stood bleary eyed in line at my favorite coffee stand awaiting my turn to pay an exorbitant amount of money for an artisan coffee. I recognize the absurdity in making a habit out of something that you recognize the absurdity in, but we all have our vices and a guy I met who lives on a livestock farm in Eastern Washington told me to never trust a man without a vice. So Im a woman who finds deep enjoyment in the occasional five dollar latte. I will exercise my right to bear vice and I will enjoy it damnit. However, this is all beside the point. While I held position in line I began to observe what one could speculate was a first date. The tentative social gestures, the overzealous pity laugh, the awkward Im trying to impress you but attempting to make it seem like Im not conversation. Weve all been there, and while often painful for all parties involved and all who bear witness, these bumbling social interactions are necessary when cultivating meaningful ones later.
         </p>
         <p>
          But something happened amidst the excessive hair touching and hand fumbling that I didnt expect to strike me so. The suitor asked his female counterpart something relatively nondescript, to which she replied with firm resolve Im not like most girls. 	    url = url.replace(/^https?:\/\//, '');
	    link = "&lt;a href='" + address + "' target='_blank' rel='nofollow'&gt;" + url + "&lt;/a&gt;";
	    if(brEnd) {
	    	return link + '&lt;br/&gt;';
	    } else {
	    	return link;
	    }
	  });
	};

	formatTextForHTML = function(content) {
	  var safeContent;
	  safeContent = ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION(content);
	  safeContent = linkify(safeContent);
	  safeContent = safeContent.replace(/(?:\r\n|\r|\n)/g, '&lt;br /&gt;');
	  //safeContent = emojify(safeContent);
	  return MARKSAFE_FUNCTION(safeContent);
	};

	Handlebars.registerHelper('formatTextForHTML', formatTextForHTML);
  </script>
