         <h2>
          Homemade Pancake Syrup
          <a href="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/little-house-living-project-rating-system">
           <img alt="Intermediate" class="size-full wp-image-41179 alignright" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Intermediate.png" height="92" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="92"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="Intermediate" class="size-full wp-image-41179 alignright" height="92" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Intermediate.png" width="92"/>
           </noscript>
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          I've been working on new recipes and ideas lately that focus on using up food scraps in the kitchen. Yes, my goats appreciate when I bring them a scrap, but if that same food scrap can feed us instead I'm going to go for that option first! This recipe is fairly simple and will use up the scraps leftover from making homemade applesauce or canning apples. You can use any kind of apples for this recipe. The amount of syrup that you harvest will be based on how many apple peelings that you cooked down in the first place, so it can vary from recipe to recipe. Just have a good amount of clean jelly jars handy!
          <span id="more-41480">
          </span>
          I used jelly jars in my recipe since we don't eat a large amount of syrup at a time, but if you have a bigger family or think you will use quite a bit, simply use pint jars instead.
         </p>

         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41488" height="677" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup9.jpg" width="450"/>
         </noscript>
         <h3>
          Homemade Pancake Syrup
         </h3>
         <p>
          What You Need:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Water Bath Canning Equipment
          </li>
          <li>
           Apple Cores and Peelings
          </li>
          <li>
           Water
          </li>
          <li>
           Sugar
          </li>
          <li>
           Candy Thermometer
          </li>
          <li>
           Jars and Lids
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41492" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/up2.jpg" height="677" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="450"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41492" height="677" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/up2.jpg" width="450"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Start by placing any apple peelings and cores that you have in a large stockpot or saucepan. Fill the pot the rest of the way with water until the water covers the peelings. Turn the burner on a medium/high heat and simmer the mixture.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41491" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup34.jpg" height="677" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="450"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41491" height="677" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup34.jpg" width="450"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Let the peelings cook in the water until the cores are mushy.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41483" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup4.jpg" height="677" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="450"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41483" height="677" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup4.jpg" width="450"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Place another stockpot or bowl under a strainer and place a piece of cheesecloth in the strainer to catch any smaller pieces. Pour the entire mixture through the strainer so you are just left with the liquid.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41484" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup5.jpg" height="677" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="450"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41484" height="677" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup5.jpg" width="450"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Place the liquid back on the burner on a high heat. Add in sugar. You will need roughly 4 to 5 cups of sugar for every 10 cups of liquid. Continue to heat the liquid until boiling.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41485" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup6.jpg" height="677" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="450"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41485" height="677" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup6.jpg" width="450"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Using the candy thermometer, heat up the liquid until it reaches 218 degrees. Once at this temperature, let the mixture boil until it begins to thicken and form a syrup.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41486" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup7.jpg" height="677" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="450"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41486" height="677" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup7.jpg" width="450"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Pour the syrup into clean jelly or pint jars. leave at least 1/2 inch of headspace in the jars.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41487" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup8.jpg" height="372" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="560"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41487" height="372" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup8.jpg" width="560"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Clean off the rims of the jars and place new lids and clean rims on tightly.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41490" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup11.jpg" height="409" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="560"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41490" height="409" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup11.jpg" width="560"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Process the jars in a hot water bath for 20 minutes. Remove the jars when finished and let them cool undisturbed until the lids have sealed.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41489" data-lazy-original="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup10.jpg" height="677" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAAAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==" width="450"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Homemade Pancake Syrup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41489" height="677" src="http://www.littlehouseliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/syrup10.jpg" width="450"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Use your homemade Pancake Syrup on pancakes or use in the place of syrup in recipes!
         </p>
