       <h1 class="post-title">
        The First Ever Tribal Marijuana Conference Is Tomorrow. Please Join Us.
       </h1>
       <span class="post-byline">
        By
        <a href="http://www.cannalawgroup.com" rel="author external" title="Visit Canna Law Blogs website">
         Canna Law Blog
        </a>
        on
        <time class="post-date" datetime="2015-02-26">
         February 26, 2015
        </time>
       </span>
       <span class="post-categories">
        Posted in
        <a href="http://www.cannalawblog.com/category/events/">
         Events,
        </a>
        <a href="http://www.cannalawblog.com/category/federal-law-and-policy/">
         Federal law and policy
        </a>
       </span>
      </header>
      <section class="post-content">
       <p>
        The nations first ever
        <a href="https://www.eventbrite.com/e/the-tribal-marijuana-conference-tickets-15462558930">
         Tribal Marijuana Conference
        </a>
        will be taking place tomorrow  this Friday, February 27 at the
        <a href="http://www.tulalipresortcasino.com">
         Tulalip Resort Casino
        </a>
        , just North of Seattle. We are expecting around 400 people from around 35 states and representing around 75 tribes. There will also be a large contingent of national reporters covering the event.
        <a href="http://www.cannalawblog.com/files/2015/02/Tulalip.jpg">
         <img alt="Tulalip" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-7310" height="225" src="http://www.cannalawblog.com/files/2015/02/Tulalip-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Our all-star cast of speakers, tribal leaders, public policy experts, and leading tribal and cannabis lawyers from both the public sector and the private sector will be discussing the legalization of marijuana in Indian tribal jurisdictions. Needless to say, if you are interested in tribal marijuana, this will also be a criticalevent for networking.
       </p>
       <p>
        The
        <a href="http://www.cannalawblog.com/files/2015/02/Tribal-Marijuana-Conference-Brochure.pdf">
         Tribal Marijuana Conference Brochure
        </a>
        contains the complete agenda, along with the full list of speakers and their biographies.
       </p>
       <p>
        There is still time to purchase tickets to this event through Eventbrite.com or at the door (via credit card or cash only). If you have any further questions please contact Megan at
        <a href="mailto:megan@harrismoure.com">
         megan@harrismoure.com
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        On the Saturday morning following the conference, there will be a meeting for conference participantsto discuss forming a
        <a href="http://indiancountrytodaymedianetwork.com/2015/02/18/tribal-leaders-discuss-formation-tribal-cannabis-association-159272">
         Tribal Cannabis Association
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        If you want to read more about the conference and some of the issues related to it., please check out some of the articles that have been written on it (so far) below:
       </p>
