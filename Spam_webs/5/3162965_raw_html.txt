                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     OREO Cookie Ball Game Day Cupcakes
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6543877572170261832" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
                       <i>
                        This shop has been compensated by Collective Bias, Inc. and its advertiser. All opinions are mine alone. #OREOCookieBalls #CollectiveBias
                       </i>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Score a touchdown on game day with this fun and delicious
                      <a href="http://www.oreo.com/recipes/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                       OREO Cookie Balls
                      </a>
                      Game Day Cupcakes recipe! Recipe includes football-shaped OREO Cookie Balls atop a turf inspired cupcake.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-2er8ZtIdPLg/VLxPqt0fAsI/AAAAAAAASKI/Zplz2pbSkP8/s1600/OREO%2BCookie%2BBall%2BGame%2BDay%2BCupcakes%2B1a.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="Score a touchdown on game day with this fun and delicious OREO Cookie Balls Game Day Cupcakes recipe! Recipe includes football-shaped OREO Cookie Balls atop a turf inspired cupcake. #OREOCookieBalls #ad" border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-2er8ZtIdPLg/VLxPqt0fAsI/AAAAAAAASKI/Zplz2pbSkP8/s1600/OREO%2BCookie%2BBall%2BGame%2BDay%2BCupcakes%2B1a.png" title="Score a touchdown on game day with this fun and delicious OREO Cookie Balls Game Day Cupcakes recipe! Recipe includes football-shaped OREO Cookie Balls atop a turf inspired cupcake. #OREOCookieBalls #ad"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <a name="more">
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      So .... we finally know what teams will be playing in the big game on February 1st. Are you happy? Scrollingthrough my Facebook feed yesterday, it seems like a lot of my friends are! :)
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      I got a text message last night from my 18-year-old bragging about his beloved East Coast team. Just because I like to instigate a little bit of a rivalry, I had to do some trash-talking with him. To be honest, I don't care about the teams - I'll only be watching the big game for some more trash-talking fun and for the food.
                      <i>
                       Obviously.
                      </i>
                      Hehe!!
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-RA73qEQ39fk/VLxPy0ELRTI/AAAAAAAASKQ/6_JNem-Qwm8/s1600/OREO%2BCookie%2BBall%2Bin%2Bstore%2B2.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="Score a touchdown on game day with this fun and delicious OREO Cookie Balls Game Day Cupcakes recipe! Recipe includes football-shaped OREO Cookie Balls atop a turf inspired cupcake. #OREOCookieBalls #ad" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-RA73qEQ39fk/VLxPy0ELRTI/AAAAAAAASKQ/6_JNem-Qwm8/s1600/OREO%2BCookie%2BBall%2Bin%2Bstore%2B2.png" title="Score a touchdown on game day with this fun and delicious OREO Cookie Balls Game Day Cupcakes recipe! Recipe includes football-shaped OREO Cookie Balls atop a turf inspired cupcake. #OREOCookieBalls #ad"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      We were out and about on Saturday. We took the kiddos to the Phoenix Zoo and had a blast. On the way home, I wanted to make a quick stop at Walmart and pick some things up for dinner. I didn't get anything out to thaw since I wasn't exactly sure when we would be home. You know how it goes ... that trip for a few things for dinner becomes a shopping cart full of stuff! It happens more times than I would like to admit!
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      I passed this OREO display in the center aisle and noticed the
                      <a href="http://deliciousmomentsofjoy.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                       OREO Cookie Balls
                      </a>
                      recipe on the side of the display. You don't even have to tell me twice, two packages of OREO cookies go into the cart. I went with traditional OREO cookies this time. Since I knew there was a couple of games this past weekend, I wanted to make a fun treat inspired by the
                      <a href="http://www.oreo.com/recipes/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                       OREO Cookie Balls
                      </a>
                      recipe. Football shaped OREO Cookie Balls seemed like an obvious choice - but I wanted to take it one step farther and use them as a cupcake topper. It's like getting two treats in one!
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-qoCGTfHueFo/VLxP99VWIOI/AAAAAAAASKY/6WD_RE4z-vo/s1600/OREO%2BCookie%2BBall%2B-%2Bmolds%2B2.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="Score a touchdown on game day with this fun and delicious OREO Cookie Balls Game Day Cupcakes recipe! Recipe includes football-shaped OREO Cookie Balls atop a turf inspired cupcake. #OREOCookieBalls #ad" border="0" height="426" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-qoCGTfHueFo/VLxP99VWIOI/AAAAAAAASKY/6WD_RE4z-vo/s1600/OREO%2BCookie%2BBall%2B-%2Bmolds%2B2.png" title="Score a touchdown on game day with this fun and delicious OREO Cookie Balls Game Day Cupcakes recipe! Recipe includes football-shaped OREO Cookie Balls atop a turf inspired cupcake. #OREOCookieBalls #ad" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       I had a hard time finding the size of candy mold that I wanted - and I already had these football sucker shaped molds on hand, so I went with it.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       You're going to start by using just a little bit of white chocolate and painting the laces in the mold. Once those have dried (it doesn't take long!), you will need to cover the rest of the mold with the chocolate. You may need a couple of coats - I did 3 coats of chocolate and then went back and filled in spots that looked thin. I used a paint brush for food to make it easier.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-uy5dLFebdLg/VLxQE3ZEKJI/AAAAAAAASKg/uoD_84bw6wY/s1600/OREO%2BCookie%2BBall%2B-%2Bmolds%2Bfilled%2B2.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="Score a touchdown on game day with this fun and delicious OREO Cookie Balls Game Day Cupcakes recipe! Recipe includes football-shaped OREO Cookie Balls atop a turf inspired cupcake. #OREOCookieBalls #ad" border="0" height="426" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-uy5dLFebdLg/VLxQE3ZEKJI/AAAAAAAASKg/uoD_84bw6wY/s1600/OREO%2BCookie%2BBall%2B-%2Bmolds%2Bfilled%2B2.png" title="Score a touchdown on game day with this fun and delicious OREO Cookie Balls Game Day Cupcakes recipe! Recipe includes football-shaped OREO Cookie Balls atop a turf inspired cupcake. #OREOCookieBalls #ad" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Once the mold is coated in chocolate, it will need to harden before it can be filled with the
                       <a href="http://deliciousmomentsofjoy.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                        OREO
                       </a>
                       mixture. Press small amounts of the OREO mixture into the mold leaving just a bit of space to cover the top with more chocolate.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Refrigerate the
                       <a href="http://www.oreo.com/recipes/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                        OREO Cookie Ball
                       </a>
                       footballs for about an hour. Carefully remove the cookie balls from the mold. If you're messy like me, you may need to trim off the stray pieces of chocolate from the edges. Use them atop your favorite cupcake recipe! I used chocolate for mine. I even tinted canned frosting and tried to pipe it to look like grass! You may have to use your imagination a bit! ;)
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       I also want to note, that
                       <i>
                        you don't necessarily need the football mold
                       </i>
                       to make this recipe. You can just as easily shaped the OREO mixture into football shapes, then dip them into the chocolate. Using a food-safe paintbrush, paint in the white laces!
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Although this recipe does take a little bit of time, they were a big hit at our get-together yesterday! I hope you'll give them a try!
                      </div>

                        </span>

                        <ul class="single_recipe_text" id="ingr" style="color: #4f372f; padding: 0px;">
                         <li class="ingredient" style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
                          <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                           1 pkg (8 oz.) brick cream cheese, softened
                          </span>
                         </li>
                         <li class="ingredient" style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
                          <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                           36 OREO Cookies, finely crushed (about 3 cups)
                          </span>
                         </li>
                         <li class="ingredient" style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
                          <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                           4 pkg. (4 oz. each) semi-sweet baking chocolate, melted
                          </span>

                         <li class="ingredient" style="margin-bottom: 3px;">

                       </div>
                       <div id="instructions">
                        <div class="single_recipe_header" id="inst_header" style="color: #3d9d8b; padding: 0px; text-decoration: none;">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <i>
                          <b>
                           <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                            Instructions
                           </span>
                          </b>
                         </i>
                         <br/>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <span class="single_recipe_text" style="color: #4f372f; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; font-weight: bold; margin-left: 7px;">
                         (FOOTBALL MOLD VERSION) For the OREO Cookie Balls:
                        </span>
                        <br/>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          1. Mix cream cheese and cookie crumbs until blended.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          2. Use the white chocolate chips to "paint" the laces in your football mold. Allow to harden. Paint the footballs using the semi-sweet baking chocolate. You may need a few coats - let harden in between coats. Freeze 10-15 minutes or until hardened.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          3. Press small amounts of the OREO mixture into the mold leaving just a bit of space to cover the top with more chocolate. Spread some of the melted chocolate over the top of the OREO mixture to seal the football.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          4. Refrigerate the OREO Cookie Ball footballs for about an hour. Carefully remove the cookie balls from the mold. If you're messy like me, you may need to trim off the stray pieces of chocolate from the edges. Keep refrigerated until ready to use.
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <span class="single_recipe_text" style="color: #4f372f; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; font-weight: bold; margin-left: 7px;">
                         (HAND-SHAPED VERSION) For the OREO Cookie balls:
                        </span>
                        <br/>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          1. Mix cream cheese and cookie crumbs until blended.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          2. Shape into 24 footballs. Freeze 10 min.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          3. Dip footballs in melted chocolate; place in single layer in shallow waxed paper-lined pan.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          4. Refrigerate 1 hour or until firm.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          5. Using a food-safe paint brush, paint the laces onto the footballs using the white chocolate chips.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="instruction">
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                          6. Refrigerate 10 minutes or until firm. Keep refrigerated until ready to use.
