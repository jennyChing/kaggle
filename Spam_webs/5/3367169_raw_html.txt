          <p align="center">
           <strong>
            OCU PREVAILS 80-63 FOR SECOND STRAIGHT NATIONAL TITLE
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            INDEPENDENCE, Mo. 
            <a href="http://www.ocusports.com" target="_blank">
             Oklahoma City University
            </a>
            powered its way to an 80-63 victory over Campbellsville (Ky.) to repeat as NAIA Division I womens basketball champion Tuesday at the Independence Events Center.
            <a href="http://oklahomasports.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/NAIA-WBB-2015-Tournament-Logo-A-OCU.jpg">
             <img alt="NAIA-WBB-2015-Tournament-Logo-A-OCU" class="alignright wp-image-20993" height="113" src="http://oklahomasports.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/NAIA-WBB-2015-Tournament-Logo-A-OCU.jpg" width="201"/>
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Second-ranked Oklahoma City (30-2) captured its third national title in four years and eighth overall national crown to lead NAIA womens basketball. OCU reached 30 wins for the 11th time in 15 years.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            OCU now has 59 national championships in all sports. The Stars have won national titles in NAIA mens cross country, cheerleading and now womens basketball in 2014-15.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Fourth-ranked Campbellsville ended its season 33-4 with its finest national finish ever. The Stars knocked Campbellsville out of the 2014 national tournament in the quarterfinals.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Rateska Brown, a senior from Alvin, S.C., boosted Oklahoma City with 29 points, eight rebounds, five assists and three steals. Brown provided OCU with 19.2 points, 4.0 rebounds and 2.00 steals a game during the tournament to earn first-team all-tournament accolades.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            OCUs Yvonte Neal, a senior from Spring Valley, Calif., pitched in 11 points and five rebounds. Neal took tournament most valuable player as she posted 18.0 points, 7.0 rebounds and 2.00 thefts a contest during the Stars championship run.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            The Stars Ouleymatou Coulibaly, a senior from Bamako, Mali, West Africa, produced 19 points, 14 rebounds and five assists. Coulibaly became a second-team all-tournament selection with her tournament numbers of 13.6 points and 8.2 rebounds a game with 56.4 percent shooting from the field.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Marisha Wallace, a senior from Midwest City, Okla., added 11 points and nine rebounds, and Janay Borum, a senior from Upper Marlboro, Md., picked up four points, four rebounds, nine assists and three steals for Oklahoma City.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            The Stars owned the glass, piling up a 56-24 rebounding advantage and 27 offensive boards. OCU built advantages in points in the paint (40-16) and second-chance points (16-5).
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            I knew one of our strengths was offensive transition, so we had to get one and done, OCU coach Latricia Trammell said. If we kept rebounding and pushing it, we had a great chance at this. We always preach offensive rebounding.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Trammell took her second consecutive Phyllis Holmes coach of the year award. She improved to 85-10 in three season as OCU coach. Trammell earned her 150th victory as a college coach, moving to 150-86 in seven years, including four as Western State (Colo.) boss.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            It is absolutely amazing, Trammell said. It feels just as good as it did last year, especially with this group of seniors. I am so proud of our girls. They really fought this game.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            We wanted it, and we knew this was it. I knew we had to continue to rebound and do what we do best  and that is transition  and I thought we did a really good job with that.
            <a href="http://oklahomasports.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/NewOCU2A.jpg">
             <img alt="NewOCU2A" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-6986" height="150" src="http://oklahomasports.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/NewOCU2A-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            OCU used eight unanswered points to create a first-half 12-point lead. Coulibaly rejected a Lindsey Burd shot, then scored in transition off Borums assist. Brown hit free throws, then Catherine Kruschke knocked in a putback. Brown capped the run with a bucket off Neals setup to put the Stars in front 29-17 with 5:32 left in the first half.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            The Stars tacked on a 10-1 spree. Brown ignited the run by jab-stepping, then nailing a 3-pointer. Neal drilled a trey, and Brown tallied a fastbreak basket off Borums steal and feed. Neal had a breakaway layup using Browns help for a 39-23 OCU advantage with 1:25 to go before halftime.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            In the second half, Neal canned a trey to put the Stars ahead 46-28 with 17:03 to go. OCU matched that 18-point cushion when Brown buried a 3 for a 65-47 advantage at the 7:04 mark.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Campbellsville chipped away the OCU lead. Kristen Jacobs jumper cut the Stars margin to 70-60 with 3:47 showing.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Wallace answered with four free throws to fortify the OCU lead. Brown and Borum tacked on foul shots to salt away the Stars championship.
           </span>
          </p>

         <div class="author-description">
          Joey McWilliams lives in Durant and provides the bulk of the content for OklahomaSports.Net.  He is a long-time sports journalist, having covered sports via radio, newspaper, television and the Internet since 2000.  He was the color analyst for the Southeastern Savage Storm football and basketball teams from 2001-2011.  He currently does play-by-play for high school football, basketball, softball and baseball on radio for Mix 96.1 in Durant.  He can also be seen on the GAC Sports Network.
Joey is an alum of both Southeastern Oklahoma State University and Oklahoma State University.  He completed his second bachelors degree in 2012, a journalism/broadcasting degree at OSU in sports media.  He earned first place in the television sports play-by-play category in the 2012 OAB/OBEA competition.
