     <p>
      Moneyball, the Hollywood types would brand it. Picking players based on stats to produce results. Down in Chiefs country they just call it winning rugby.
     </p>
     <p>
      And that winning continued last night as Dave Rennie's Super Rugby-leading Chiefs shook off moments of lethargy to complete a bonus point victory over competition doormats the Lions in front of 12,364 fans at their Pukekohe home base.
     </p>
     <p>
      It was not the bloodbath that some predicted on a relatively pleasant May night, but the Chiefs dealt efficiently and at times entertainingly with an outmatched Lions outfit as they ran in four tries to three to storm back to the top of the table with their ninth victory in 10 matches.
     </p>
     <p>
      To say the Chiefs are on a roll - they have now won nine straight since that opening-round defeat - is an understatement. They are playing a fabulous mix of hard, committed and efficient rugby up front, mixed with some slick and scintillating stuff out wide when the opportunities present.
     </p>
     <p>
      Last night they absorbed their fair share of pressure from a Lions side who played with plenty of aggression and intent. All three of the visitors' tries came off driving mauls via lineout possession close to the line.
     </p>
     <p>
      But the Chiefs dished out a lesson for the South Africans in how to punish off turnover, coasting to a victory that was never ever in doubt from early in the second half.
     </p>
     <p>
      It wasn't exactly a classic display from the competition's form side, who rested a few tired bods this week and may have paid a momentum price for it. The three tries were the most the competition's best defence has conceded this season, and they rather lost their way in the final quarter when things got decidedly messy.
     </p>
     <p>
      But the Chiefs, with two tries in each half, banked the bonus point, and emerged unscathed on the injury front. They will have Liam Messam and Brodie Retallick back next week for a Sunday visit to the Queensland Reds, and are already eyeing that contest as a great litmus test.
     </p>
     <p>
      Of course it is no fluke that these Chiefs finally have some substance to go with the style that's been a characteristic of theirs for a few years now.
     </p>
     <p>
      It's all very well having arguably the world's best midfield, and such classy operators as Aaron Cruden, Lelia Masaga and Brendon Leonard in your backline. But you've got to have the forwards to lay the foundation up front.
     </p>
     <p>
      "We picked a lot of guys based on work ethic," Rennie said of his forwards for 2012. "We trawled through all the stats from the NPC to find guys we thought would graft and work hard. I think we're seeing the benefits of that."
     </p>
     <p>
      The Chiefs forwards last night did the job splendidly. Even missing such form men as Messam, Sona Taumalolo (who came off the bench), Retallick and Alex Bradley. The show rolled on, with the likes of Kane Thomson, Toby Smith and Sam Cane slotting in as though they'd been there all year.
     </p>
     <p>
      Rennie reckoned stats provided a valuable insight in certain work-related positions. "We're not looking for locks who play a wide game, we're looking for guys who get in and do some graft, move bodies and make tackles. We want to be combative and we want to commit numbers around the edges."
     </p>

     <p>
      They have also set high standards for themselves. Thus some rather glum faces when they came off the paddock. "Pretty flat," was Rennie's verdict on the performance. "There certainly wasn't the same sort of intensity we've played with recently."
     </p>
     <p>
      Defending off the driving maul, discipline and execution off the scrum will be areas looked closely at this week, said a rather stern-faced coach.
     </p>
     <p>
      But that's also an indication of the goals this group now have. "We've got things set down we want to achieve in the way we play the game and the way we want to defend," said skipper Craig Clarke. "We've had lapses in that tonight."
     </p>
     <p>
      The Chiefs led 20-7 at halftime, through tries to Cruden and Clarke and coasted home on a couple of second-spell gems to Masaga and Leonard.
     </p>
     <p>
      Masaga's 48th-minute try was created by some Cruden magic off the boot, extending the margin to 20, while Leonard's, in the 56th minute, was a fabulous piece of opportunism created by the halfback's excellent vision and committed chase.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Lions, though, hung tough and punished their hosts on the drive, replacement Cobus Grobbelaar (twice) and tireless skipper Josh Strauss powering over.
     </p>
     <p>
      So the Chiefs have some adjustments to make, even as their roll continues unabated. Be sure of one thing: the formula won't change.
     </p>
