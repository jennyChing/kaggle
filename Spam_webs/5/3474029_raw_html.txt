     <h1 style="text-align:center;font-family:Arial;font-size:11pt;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      <strong>
       <span style="text-align: center;font-size: 24pt; font-family: Arial">
        FunVax: Fundamentalist Vaccine
       </span>
       <br/>
       <span style="text-align: center;font-size: 16pt; font-family: Arial">
        Pentagon Video, Document Reveal Aerial Vaccination Plan
       </span>
      </strong>
     </h1>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 16pt 0 11pt 0">
      <b>
       "In non-conflict zones, effectiveness [of the fundamentalist vaccine] could be measured by a decrease in the people attending religious activities."
      </b>
      <br/>
      -- Quarterly Report on the Pentagon's FunVax (Fundamentalist Vaccine), 6/1/07
     </p>
     <p style="text-align:justify;font-family:Arial;font-size:11pt;margin: 16pt 0 11pt 0">
      Dear friends,
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      The Pentagon may vaccinate large populations in the Middle East with what is being called FunVax  a fundamentalist vaccine.
      <strong>
       As explained by Pentagon researchers, the FunVax uses an airborne virus to indiscriminately infect populations considered high risk for religious fundamentalism.
      </strong>
      The virus in this vaccine purportedly has been tested and shown to reduce fundamentalism and religiosity in all who are infected by damaging what is called the "God gene."
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      Though this may sound like some bizarre science fiction novel,  the four-minute Pentagon video presentation and  official FunVax report  below show that this vaccine against fundamentalism is actively being tested for implementation. As it has been a few years since this date of this video and report, it is entirely possible this program has already been started.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      <strong>
       Four-minute Pentagon video presentation of FunVax  dated April 13, 2005
       <br/>
       <a href="http://www.personalgrowthcourses.net/video/health/pengtagon_vaccine_modify_behavior" target="_blank">
        http://www.personalgrowthcourses.net/video/health/pengtagon_vaccine_modify_behavior
       </a>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      <strong>
       Government report on the fundamentalist vaccine: Quarterly FunVax Review, June 1, 2007
       <br/>
       <a href="http://www.wanttoknow.info/health/funvax070601.pdf" onclick="javascript:urchinTracker('/health/funvax070601.pdf');" target="_blank">
        http://www.wanttoknow.info/health/funvax070601.pdf
       </a>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      There is some possibility that the above video and document are faked. But if they are, it is  very intelligent  disinformation. Who would spend all of the time and money required to come up with such a sophisticated and coherent story? And why? Knowing how science and the military often work together, a program like this is all too possible. I once attended a lecture by Al Gore's science advisor in which he said he had been invited to see some of the most secret biological research programs being undertaken by the U.S. military. He said it gave him nightmares for months.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      For those who doubt the government would  carry out such a campaign, consider a July 12, 2011
      <a href="http://www.wanttoknow.info/a-vaccination-ruse-used-pursuit-bin-laden" target="_blank">
       <em>
        New York Times
       </em>
       report
      </a>
      that the CIA organized a fake vaccine drive to get Osama bin Laden's family DNA. It appears that health care workers were used knowing or unknowingly on a clandestine mission as part of a deliberate policy of the U.S. government. An article in the
      <em>
       <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/cia-defends-running-vaccine-program-to-find-bin-laden/2011/07/13/gIQAbLcFDI_story.html" target="_blank">
        Washington Post
       </a>
      </em>
      quotes a senior U.S. official as stating, "The vaccination campaign was part of the hunt for the world's top terrorist, and nothing else."
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      According to the FunVax report above, the fundamentalist vaccine is prepared using "a viral vector that will inhibit/decrease the expression of VMAT2 within a human population. The VMAT2 gene, also referred to as the "
      <a href="http://www.medicalnewstoday.com/releases/16378.php" target="_blank">
       God gene
      </a>
      ," has received wide coverage in the media. It is the gene which has been shown to have the most relationship to spirituality.
      <em>
       Time
      </em>
      magazine  featured the God gene story on it's
      <a href="http://www.time.com/time/covers/0,16641,20041025,00.html" target="_blank">
       front cover
      </a>
      on Oct. 25, 2004. For the fascinating article on VMAT2 in that issue of
      <em>
       Time
      </em>
      , titled, "Religion: Is God in Our Genes?",
      <a href="http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,995465,00.html" target="_blank">
       click here.
      </a>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      <strong>
       So the Pentagon has found a way to decrease religious fervor using an airborne vaccine. What are the ethical implications of this? Do we really want to get involved in  manipulating people's genes, especially when those subjected to this are not being informed? Do we really want to decrease spirituality in unknowing citizens through a vaccine? And why isn't this disturbing news being reported and debated in the major media?
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      The Pentagon report  states that tests have been conducted on six different methods of dispersing the virus: "high altitude release, water supply release, insect transmission, diffusion by a ground level object such as a car, diffusion from a stationary object such as a bottle and infection of food supply such as cattle or produce." You have to wonder where these tests were conducted and on whom. The minutes of the report also mention discussion of a "proposal for a suicide gene."
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      Here's a quote from the FunVax report's recommendations section:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
       <em>
        Of the
        <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16537595" target="_blank">
         600 variants
        </a>
        of
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vesicular_stomatitis_virus" target="_blank">
         Vesicular Stomatitiis Virus
        </a>
        tested, VSV287 had the greatest decrease of VMAT2 within mice. However, this may not be the case for human subjects. All 600 strains of VSV should be retested on human subjects in the clinical group.
       </em>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      Once again, we have to ask who these human subjects will be. Will they be informed of what is happening and give their consent? Would anyone in their right mind give consent to have one of their key genes altered? We know in the past that low-ranking soldiers were used for  tests like this and recruited with special bonuses, but not told about the real objectives of the test.
      <strong>
       For a long list of secret government tests in which soldiers, prisoners, and even the general public were unknowingly treated like human guinea pigs
      </strong>
      ,
      <a href="http://www.wanttoknow.info/humanguineapigs" target="_blank">
       click here
      </a>
      (links provided to verify each case).
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial;margin: 0 0 11pt 0">
      Of the behavioral indicators which would determine effectiveness of the vaccine mentioned in the report, one states, "In non-conflict zones, effectiveness could be measured by a decrease in the people attending religious activities." A second indicator mentions "measurable increase in ... telephone, email, and other forms of communication that express discontent with religion or God.
