         <h2>
          Google Maps Dragon Mode
         </h2>
         <p>
          Google has always had fun with their Google Maps site, especially for Geeks. Remember
          <a href="http://www.thehobbitblog.com/journey-through-middle-earth-in-a-new-google-chrome-experiment/" target="_blank">
           Middle Earth
          </a>
          found in Google Maps? What about
          <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2013/08/13/tardis-google-maps/">
           tours of the TARDIS
          </a>
          from
          <em>
           Doctor Who
          </em>
          , and for
          <em>
           Lord of the Rings
          </em>
          fans, you could search for
          <a href="http://techland.time.com/2011/12/21/google-maps-easter-egg-one-does-not-simply-walk-into-mordor/" target="_blank">
           directions to Mordor
          </a>
          . Now their latest easter egg is for all of those
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0944947/">
            Game of Thrones
           </a>
          </em>
          fans.
         </p>
         <p>
          Try searching for directions from Snowdon, Wales, to Brecon Beacons in South Wales. Here you will be given an option for
          <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/+GoogleMaps/posts/JxgAP9nc5Vq" target="_blank">
           dragon travel
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Google Maps Dragon Mode" data-caption="" data-credit-label="" data-credit-link="https://plus.google.com/u/0/+GoogleMaps/posts/JxgAP9nc5Vq" data-credit-name="Google+" data-credit-provider="Custom type" data-fragment="m!bc38" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzA2LzAzL2Y5L0RyYWdvbk1vZGUuYWRhMDMucG5nCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/98066e71/d3b/Dragon-Mode.png" data-micro="1" data-width="506" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzA2LzAzL2Y5L0RyYWdvbk1vZGUuYWRhMDMucG5nCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/98066e71/d3b/Dragon-Mode.png" title="Google Maps Dragon Mode"/>
          <br/>
          IMAGE:
          <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/+GoogleMaps/posts/JxgAP9nc5Vq" target="_blank">
           GOOGLE+
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Judging by Google Maps time for travel, going by dragon, the 122-mile journey would take you only 21 minutes. Sounds pretty fuel efficient to me!
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="lightbox-expand for-lightbox">
           <img alt="googlemaps_dragon" data-caption="" data-credit-label="" data-credit-link="" data-credit-name="Google Maps" data-credit-provider="Custom type" data-fragment="m!785b" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzA2LzAzL2IyL2dvb2dsZW1hcHMuOTU5YzMucG5nCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/50cb53c1/168/googlemaps_dragon.png" data-micro="1" data-width="1130" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzA2LzAzL2IyL2dvb2dsZW1hcHMuOTU5YzMucG5nCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/50cb53c1/168/googlemaps_dragon.png" title="googlemaps_dragon"/>
          </span>
          <br/>
          IMAGE: GOOGLE MAPS
         </p>
         <p>
          We speculate that
          <strong>
           Google Maps Dragon Mode
          </strong>
          used this location possibly because of the Welsh flag iconic symbol of a red dragon. If we get more clarification, we will be sure to post here.
         </p>
         <p>
          Let us know your thoughts on the location and anything else you find.
         </p>
