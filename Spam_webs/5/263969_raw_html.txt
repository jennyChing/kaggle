     <h1>
      19 epic road trips through Scotland
     </h1>
     <div class="post-inner clearfix">
      <div class="post-meta">
       <div class="post-meta-author">
        by
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/author/katiescottaiton/">
         Katie Scott Aiton
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="post-meta-date">
        April 15, 2014
       </div>
       <div class="post-avatar">
        <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-100" height="100" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/authors/katiescottaiton.jpg" width="100"/>
       </div>
       <div class="post-share-wrapper post-share--vertical" data-post-id="243985" data-sticky="" data-sticky-max-width="991" data-sticky-offset-top="69" data-sticky-parent-contain="" data-sticky-parent-selector=".page-section">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="post-content">
       <div class="subtitle">
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/trips/30-images-scotland-cant-stop-looking/">
         Scotland
        </a>
        is one of THE best countries in the world to take a road trip. Here are 20 of my favourite routes, many of them easily linkable for longer trips.
       </div>
       <p>
        <em>
         Note: Place names located above the central belt of Scotland have been translated into Scottish Gaelic to help with navigation.
        </em>
       </p>
       <h5>
        1. Selkirk  Moffat (Scottish Borders, A708)
       </h5>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244019" style="width: 910px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Yarrow Valley" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/yarrow-valley-matador-seo.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class=" lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/yarrow-valley-matador-seo.jpg" size-full="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900 class=" wp-image-244019=""/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Moffat Valley. Photo:
         <a href="">
          Jonathan Combe
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        From Selkirk, cruise up the side of
        <a href="http://visitstmarysloch.org.uk/">
         St Marys Loch
        </a>
        , at the head of the Yarrow Valley in the heart of the Scottish Southern Uplands. Stop at
        <a href="http://tibbieshiels.com/?doing_wp_cron=1397402633.8705239295959472656250">
         Tibbie Shiels Inn
        </a>
        for an excellent pub lunch. The surrounding area forms a nature reserve spanning 922 hectares, owned by the National Trust for Scotland. To work off lunch, climb up the pass and drop down to view one of Scotlands finest waterfalls, the Grey Mares Tail. The Southern Uplands are a wonderland for botanics, bird watchers, and hill walkers.
       </p>
       <h5>
        2. Ayr  Turnberry (Ayrshire, A719)
       </h5>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244020" style="width: 910px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Culzean Castle" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/culzean-castle-matador-seo.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-full wp-image-244020 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/culzean-castle-matador-seo.jpg" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Culzean Castle. Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/jamiewyllie/">
          Jamie Wyllie
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        As you leave the town of Ayr (
        <em>
         Inbhir ir
        </em>
        , Mouth of the River Ayr), head south on the A719 and visit the birthplace of
        <a href="http://www.burnsmuseum.org.uk/">
         Robert Burns
        </a>
        , Scotlands most famous bard. Jump back on the coastal main road and check out the Electric Brae, or Croy Brae as its known locally. The hill is an optical illusion where you appear to be going uphill when actually youre traveling down. Next, stop at Culzean (pronounced Culain)
        <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/info/see-do/culzean-castle-and-country-park-p248671">
         Castle and Country Park
        </a>
        , where General Dwight D. Eisenhower had an apartment during the Second World War. Finish at Turnberry and enjoy a walk down the magnificent beach bordered by a
        <a href="http://www.turnberry.co.uk/">
         championship golf course
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h5>
        3. Fort William  Inverness (A82)
       </h5>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244021" style="width: 910px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Urquhart Castle" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/urquhart-castle-matador-seo.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-full wp-image-244021 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/urquhart-castle-matador-seo.jpg" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Urquhart Castle and Loch Ness beyond. Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/ifl/">
          Peretz Partensky
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Follow the
        <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/info/see-do/caledonian-canal-from-fort-augustus-p521641">
         Caledonian Canal
        </a>
        north from Fort William on the A82. The canal links the lochs of the
        <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/blog/the-highlands/hiking-through-scotlands-great-glen-way/">
         Great Glen
        </a>
        to form a maritime route from west to east, precluding the need to sail round the often stormy seas off the north coast. Stop at the Commando Memorial just north of
        <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/en-us/info/towns-villages/spean-bridge-p236651">
         Spean Bridge
        </a>
        , which commemorates the men of the original British Commando Forces of World War II. Look back south and on a clear day youll see
        <a href="http://www.visit-fortwilliam.co.uk/ben-nevis-in-fort-william-is-the-highest-mountain-in-the-uk">
         Ben Nevis
        </a>
        , Scotlands highest mountain.
       </p>
       <p>
        Continue north along the banks of Loch Lochy (
        <em>
         Loch Lochaidh
        </em>
        ) and Loch Oich (
        <em>
         Loch Omhaich
        </em>
        ) to lunch in
        <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/info/towns-villages/fort-augustus-p236671">
         Fort Augustus
        </a>
        . As you go further north aside Loch Ness, keep an eye out for the elusive monster, Nessie. Stop at the visitor centre just after
        <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/info/see-do/urquhart-castle-p245811">
         Urquhart Castle
        </a>
        in Drumnadrochit (
        <em>
         Druim na Drochaid
        </em>
        ) to learn about the hunt for the monster. Continue north along the loch to Inverness, where the canal finally joins the sea spilling into the Moray Firth.
       </p>
       <h5>
        4. Crail  Elie (Fife, A917)
       </h5>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244022" style="width: 910px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Anstruther Fish Bar" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/anstruther-matador-seo.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-full wp-image-244022 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/anstruther-matador-seo.jpg" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Anstruther Fish Bar. Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/hagdorned/">
          Magnus Hagdorn
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Drive southwest along the A917 and visit the fishing villages of the
        <a href="http://www.eastneukwide.co.uk/">
         East Neuk of Fife
        </a>
        . Dont miss the
        <a href="http://www.anstrutherfishbar.co.uk/">
         Anstruther Fish Bar
        </a>
        down at the harbour, one of the best chippies in Scotland. Journey on to Pittenweem and St Monans, finishing up at
        <a href="http://www.ship-elie.com/?page_id=56">
         Ship Inn
        </a>
        in Elie, which overlooks the natural harbour.
       </p>
       <h5>
        5. Lochgilphead  Tayvallich (Argyllshire, A816/B841/B8025)
       </h5>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244024" style="width: 910px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Sound of Jura" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/sound-jura-matador-seo.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-full wp-image-244024 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/sound-jura-matador-seo.jpg" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Sound of Jura. Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/scottmarley/">
          Scott Marley
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Trace the
        <a href="http://www.scottishcanals.co.uk/our-canals/crinan-canal">
         Crinan Canal
        </a>
        north up the A816 and turn off at Cairnbaan (
        <em>
         An Crn Bn
        </em>
        ). Follow the country road all the way to Crinan (
        <em>
         An Cronan
        </em>
        ), where the canal opens into the Atlantic. Walk around the little harbour and look northwest across the Sound of Jura (
        <em>
         An Linne Rosach
        </em>
        ) to the Gulf of Corryvreckan (
        <em>
         Coire Bhreacain
        </em>
        ); whirlpools stir the sea here. Return partway along the B841 and turn south down the B8025 to
        <a href="http://www.tayvallich.com/">
         Tayvallich
        </a>
        (
        <em>
         Taigh a Bhealaich

        <img class="size-full wp-image-244046 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/north-scotland-matador-seo.jpg" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         End of the mainland. Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/23899307@N05/">
          Ant Jackson
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Drive the route along the roof of mainland Scotland. From Durness (
        <em>
         Diranais
        </em>
        ), go east on the A838, past Tongue (
        <em>
         Tunga
        </em>
        ); Bettyhill (
        <em>
         Am Blran Odhar
        </em>
        ), which has a great pub; and then onto Thurso (
        <em>
         Inbhir Thersa
        </em>
        ). Take a detour left to Dunnet Head (
        <em>
         Ceann Dnaid
        </em>
        ), the northernmost point on mainland UK. On a clear day you can see the Orkney Islands to the north. Visit the
        <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/en-us/info/see-do/the-castle-and-gardens-of-mey-p245781">
         Castle of Mey
        </a>
        , the former holiday home of the Queen Mother, before finishing up at John OGroats (
        <em>
         Taigh Iain Ghrt
        </em>
        ).
       </p>
       <h5>
        19. Banchory  Blairgowrie (Aberdeenshire, A93)
       </h5>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_244047" style="width: 910px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Balmoral Castle" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/balmoral-castle-matador-seo.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-full wp-image-244047 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/balmoral-castle-matador-seo.jpg" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="900"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Balmoral Castle. Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/82134796@N03/">
          denisbin
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Start at Banchory (
        <em>
         Beannchar
        </em>
        ) and drive west up upper Deeside to Ballater (
        <em>
         Bealadair
        </em>
        ), then onto Crathie and past
        <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/info/see-do/balmoral-castle-p245771">
         Balmoral
        </a>
        , the Scottish holiday home of the British Royal family, which they visit every summer. Go on to Braemar, then south past the ski slopes and over the Spittal of Glenshee before dropping down into Perthshire and finishing at
        <a href="http://www.visitscotland.com/en-us/info/towns-villages/blairgowrie-p241891">
         Blairgowrie
        </a>
        , the raspberry capital of Scotland.
       </p>
