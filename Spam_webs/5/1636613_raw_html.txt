         Top 30 Thursday: 30 Variations Of Burpees You Cant Wait To Try
        </h1>

        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         Its Thursday! Does anyone ever wake up and do the happy dance? If you havent, youre missing out.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thursdays are my only mornings to sleep in past 5:15am, so you better believe I prance around the house as if I am on Dancing With The Stars just waiting for Nigel to tell me how amazing I am.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dan loves it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Not really. My a.m. chipperness annoys him. And I think he would much rather do 10 reps of
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          all
         </span>
         the burpees below than to have to deal with my morning-loving self.
        </p>
        <p>
         Remember on Monday how I mentioned my challenge to
         <a href="http://www.liftingrevolution.com/5-never-heard-of-burpees-why-i-dont-eat-tofu/">
          come up with 30 different variations of burpees
         </a>
         ?
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, I did it and now I get to share them ALL with you.
        </p>
        <p>
         Instead of Top 10 Thursday, this is your lucky day:
        </p>
        <h3>
         Top 30 Thursday: 30 Amazing Variations Of Burpees
        </h3>
        <p>
         If burpees are new to you, youre about to get a fast lesson. They are one of the best (and most intense) exercises you can do.
        </p>
        <div class="bpsubsubhead">
         Why Do Burpees Rock?
        </div>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          They require no equipment (at least the basic burpee)
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          They work every BIG muscle in your body
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          They raise your heart rate, providing both a toning and cardio workout
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div class="bpsubsubhead">
         Your Challenge:
        </div>
        <p>
         Before I jump in to showing you all 30 funtastical burpees, I propose a challenge.
        </p>
        <div class="bpcallout">
         Choose 15 of the following 30, and complete 10 reps of each. Yes, that is a combined total of 150 burpees. But I know you can do it.
        </div>
        <p>
         Are you in?
        </p>
        <p>
         Let me know in the comments below. And if so, lets get started
        </p>
        <h3>
         30 Different Burpee Variations
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.liftingrevolution.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/burpee-header.jpg">
          <img alt="30 burpees" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7882" height="308" src="http://www.liftingrevolution.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/burpee-header.jpg" width="550"/>
         </a>
         <a data-pin-config="above" data-pin-do="buttonPin" href="//pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.liftingrevolution.com%2F30-variations-of-burpees&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.liftingrevolution.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2013%2F05%2Fburpee-header.jpg&amp;description=30%20different%20variations%20of%20burpees.%20Can%20be%20done%20anywhere%20and%20an%20easy%20way%20to%20get%20a%20full%20body%20workout%20anywhere.">
          <img alt="" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Hurpee
         <em>
          (also called a 1/2 burpee)
         </em>
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="hurpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/herpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         It has a strange name but youll remember it!
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          The hurpee is simply a burpee but without a push-up. Some people call this a full burpee its not.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          To be called a burpee, it must include a push-up.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         2. Standard Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/regular-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The little guy responsible for this top 30 list!
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Start standing, squat all the way down to the ground, jump feet back and do a push-up.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Thrust your feet back to your hands and explode up to the sky.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div class="bpsubsubhead">
         Modification tips:
        </div>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Step your feet back and forward if jumping is too difficult.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Push-ups can be performed on knees (for any of the burpees here).
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         3. 8-Count Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="8-count burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/8-count-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Start like a normal burpee, jumping back to a push-up position with feet together.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete a plank jack (jump feet apart and back together)
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Do a push-up and finish off with the thrust and squat jump.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         4. Box Jump Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="box jump burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/box-jump-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Use a bench, table or stepper (like above) and set to a height you feel comfortable with.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Instead of finishing with a squat jump, end the burpee with a box jump. Land softly on top and jump back down to repeat.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         5. Candlestick Burpee (My personal favorite)
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="candlestick burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/candlestick-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is my favorite as it combines a classic gymnastics training exercise with my beloved burpee.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete a candlestick by rolling back and pushing toes to the sky.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Use core to roll forward and place hands in front of you.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete the burpee like normal.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         6. Dumbbell Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="dumbbell burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/db-squat-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          This is a variation of the deadlift burpee (as seen below) but the placement of the hands makes it much different.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Use a dumbbell large enough that your hands can stay on the ends.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Finish, by grabbing the handle and deadlifting the weight up. (see tips below). No jumping.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         7. Deadlift Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="deadlift burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/deadlift-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Grab a barbell or pre-weight bar (like above).
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete the first part of the burpee with hands on the bar, keeping the core extra tight.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          The push-up should have your chest coming to the bar.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Thrust to standing and keeping back straight, head up, complete a deadlift.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         8. Deadman Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/deadman-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Lower all the way on to the ground.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Use your core and arms to lower down in one straight line. And push-up off the ground the same way.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Avoid rolling up or down.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         9. Decline Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="decline burpees" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/decline-pushup-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Make sure you have a nice sturdy box, chair, or bench.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete burpee by jumping your feet onto the bench (after putting hands on the ground).
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete a decline push-up.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Thrust feet between hands and jump up as normal.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         10. Divebomber Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="divebomber burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/divebomer-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Perhaps one of the most challenging variations but one of the prettiest
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Jump back into a downward dog (instead of a plank)
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Scoop forward as if there is a piece of wire youre going under.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Reverse back to downward dog before finishing with the squat jump.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         11. Double Thrust Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="double thrust burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/double-thruster-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          The thrust is the part of the burpee where you jump your feet to your hands.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete this twice before jumping up off the ground.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Keep the core tight and start and finish in a straight plank position.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         12. Jump Over Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="jump over burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/jump-over-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Use a box or bench that you are confident you can jump over (as you get tired it gets harder)
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Once over the box, turn and repeat.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         13. Traveling Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="long jump burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/jumping-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Make sure you have about 10 feet in front of you.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete the burpee as normal.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          At the end, jump up and out as far as possible.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Immediately drop back down and repeat moving forward.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          If space is tight, just turn around and repeat the burpee going back to where you started.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         14. Lateral Jump Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="lateral burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/lateral-jump-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Stand beside a stepper top, dumbbell or even a jump rope.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          At the end, jump laterally over the object and quickly go straight into the next rep.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         15. Lunge Jump Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="lunge jump burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/lunge-jump-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          The burpee stays the same, all the way through
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          After finishing with the squat jump, complete a lunge jump on each leg.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          <strong>
           Warning
          </strong>
          : its a butt burner!
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         16. Med Ball Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/med-ball-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          This burpee required some tricep and core strength.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Grab a medicine ball and perform the entire ground part with your hands on the ball.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Keep a tight core to ensure the ball doesnt slip out.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         17. Mt Climb Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="mt climb burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/mt-climber-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          After the push-up, complete 5 mountain climbers.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Finish like normal!
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         18. Uneven Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="uneven burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/off-set-pushup-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Use a stepper top, push-up handle or a dumbbell.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          One hand will go on the ground, the other on the box or dumbbell handle. Complete an uneven push-up before finishing like normal.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Repeat on the other side, alternating.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         19. 1-legged Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="1 legged burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/one-leg-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Start balanced on one foot, jump back and complete the entire burpee on one foot.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Switch feet at the top, and repeat.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         20. Plyo Push-up Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/plyo-pushup-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          In place of the push-up, step it up by doing a clapping push-up.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          If you cant clap, modify by jumping straight off your hands as much as possible.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         21. Pull Through Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="pull through burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/pull-through-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Youre going to want a somewhat heavy weight.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Make sure youre on ground that will allow the weight to slide.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Start off with it beside you, do your push up
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Reach underneath, grab the weight and pull it to the other side of your body.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Repeat in the opposite direction.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         22. Star Jump Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="star jump burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/star-jump-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         What a fun burpee!
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Replace the squat jump at the end with a star jump.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Explode up, jumping your feet wide apart, arms up, and quickly bringing everything back in for the landing.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         23. Tuck Jump Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="tuck jump burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/tuck-jump-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          At the end of the burpee, finish with a tuck jump. Where you bring your knees up to your chest.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Keep your head forward and shoulders back.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         24. Twister Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="twister burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/twistie-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          On the squat jump up, add a rotation (making it a twister squat jump) so you face backwards.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete a burpee here.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Make sure to rotate in opposite directions so you
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           dont get dizzy
          </span>
          .
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         25. Double Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="double burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/two-pushup-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete the burpee just like normal, except perform 2 push-ups.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          You can also add an extra squat jump if youre feeling frisky!
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         26. Up Down Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="hell raiser burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/up-down-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Before doing the push-up complete a plank up-down (also known as a hell raiser
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Make sure to alternate which arm is the lead arm pushing back up to the top of a push-up.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         27. Upright Row Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="upright row burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/upright-row-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Grab a barbell (regular or pre-weighted)
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Keeping your hands on the bar, core tight so that the bar doesnt roll, complete the burpee.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Instead of jumping, stand up (deadlift the bar) and complete the upright row.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         28. Walking Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="walking burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/walking-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Make sure you have about 5 feet of space to the side of you.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Start the burpee, complete the push-up and complete 3-5 steps of a plank walk.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Thrust the feet up and finish with the squat jump.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Repeat in the other direction.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         29. Step Up Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="step-up burpee" class="aligncenter" height="169" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/lifting-revolution/gifs/stepup-burpee.gif" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete a burpee behind a bench, box or step-up.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Do a burpee and replace the squat jump with a step-up.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Repeat and step-up with the other foot.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         30. Pull-up Burpee
        </h3>
        <p>
         Unfortunately I was not able to get a picture of this because our pull-up bar was being used.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="listread">
          Complete a burpee underneath a pull-up bar.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          At the end, while jumping up from the ground, grab the pull-up bar and complete a pull-up.
         </li>
         <li class="listread">
          Jump down and repeat.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         These make me feel the most badass of all the variations.
        </p>
