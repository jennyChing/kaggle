            <h1 class="entry-title">

             </a>
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta">
             on December 31, 2011 by Paul Caridad
            </div>
            <!-- .entry-meta -->

           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/?p=30443">
              <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-30446" height="507" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/20111231-134950.jpg" title="20111231-134950.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              From the creative mind of
              <a href="http://loldwell.com">
               Caldwell Tanner
              </a>
              , illustrator of
              <a href="http://www.collegehumor.com/">
               College Humor
              </a>
              , writer of his own captivating and hilarious illustration blog, and graphic artist/comedian extraordinaire, now comes
              <a href="http://www.collegehumor.com/article/6670326/the-internet-justice-league">
               The Internet League of Justice
              </a>
              illustration series!
             </strong>
             Imagine if the most popular online websites and social media networks were superheroes! What powers would they have?
             <span id="more-30443">
             </span>
            </p>
            <div class="see-also">
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2011/05/31/real-life-disney-princesses/" title="REAL LIFE DISNEY PRINCESSES">
             </a>

             </p>
             <div class="clearfix">
             </div>
            </div>
            <p>
             Who would their nemeses be? Thanks to Tanners amazing wit and humor, we dont have to guess anymore! He has created superpowers and archenemies for each imagined, internet superhero ie, Facebook, Google, Amazon, etc. Depicting Google as an overpowered hero with near infinite abilities, Caldwell draws us into his fascinating and humorous fantasy of a world immersed in a perilous battle for internet domination!. Can you envision Amazon as a super hero/villain(?) with amazing powers to drain bank accounts and provide super saver shipping, whose worst nemesis is Craigslist? Or perhaps, Google + as a noble superhero with good intentions, who yet may not have much value. See all of Caldwell Tanners internet superhero
             <a href="http://www.collegehumor.com/article/6670326/the-internet-justice-league">
              illustrations
             </a>
             for more amusing descriptions of these online heroes. More information about Tanner and his image-based laughtermagic can be found on
             <a href="https://plus.google.com/105330595818765767730/posts">
              Google +
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.facebook.com/loldwell">
              FaceBook
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.linkedin.com/pub/h-caldwell-tanner/12/277/642">
              LinkedIn
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://vimeo.com/caldy">
              Vimeo
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://blogwell.tumblr.com/">
              Tumblr
             </a>
             , and
             <a href="http://twitter.com/caldy">
              Twitter
             </a>
             . Hmm does it seem, perhaps, that Tanner and his graphic humor, could be said to be dominating the internet, as well?!
            </p>
