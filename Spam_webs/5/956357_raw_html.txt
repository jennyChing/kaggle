        <h2>
         Holiday Tips
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.youngentrepreneur.com/blog/managing-your-business-in-the-holiday-season/" target="_blank" title="Managing Your Business in the Holiday Season ">
          The wheels of progress stop for no one
         </a>
         . And that includes your clients and customers, who look for 24/7 consistency no matter how many holiday obligations or gatherings you may have scheduled. So how do you, as an entrepreneur, face the seasons hectic workload with aplomb? Here are a few tips on how to manage your holiday workload.
         <em>
          <strong>
           Youngentrepreneur
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.bizcompare.com/blog/small-business/small-business-owners-and-vacation-an-oxymoron/" target="_blank" title="Small business owners and vacation: An oxymoron?">
          Plan to be away, or de-stress with a working holiday
         </a>
         . As an entrepreneur, its hard to scale back completely at this time of yearyou usually cant just walk away (and you usually dont want to). But you
         <em>
          can
         </em>
         set plans in motion to minimize the footprint of business buzz during this time of year, while still maintaining a state of receptiveness. Or, maybe now is the optimal time to catch up on e-mail, re-evaluate back-burner projects, or use the atmosphere as an inspiration for creative thinking.
         <em>
          <strong>
           BizCompare
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Marketing
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.canuckseo.com/index.php/2011/12/online-marketing-program-4-0-monitoring-analytics-tasks/" target="_blank" title="Online Marketing Program: 4.0 Monitoring &amp; Analytics Tasks">
          Start making sense
         </a>
         ! Youve collected a lot of data on your business, but now its time to make sense of it all. You need to transform it into meaningful information you can use as a GPS to see where your business stands, right here and now. But how is this achieved? Heres a great strategy along with some recommendations for choosing the right analytics tools.
         <em>
          <strong>
           CanuckSEO
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://smallbusinessbonfire.com/street-walking-do-the-opposite-of-everyone-else" target="_blank" title="Street Walking: Do the Opposite of Everyone Else">
          Be contrary to popular thought
         </a>
         . Sometimes its better to jump off the bandwagon and head in the opposite direction when it comes to defining your product or service. Jason Gregory offers this brief but poignant insight to illustrate that divergence can be a beautiful thing in business.
         <em>
          <strong>
           Small Business Bonfire
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Self-development
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.yourofficeanywhere.com/12-days-of-collaboration-day-8-%E2%80%93-how-to-be-open-minded/" target="_blank" title="12 Days of Collaboration: Day 8 - How to Be Open Minded">
          Dont get caught inside the box
         </a>
         . Teams can sometimes get trapped into predictable patterns of thought and behavior, impeding the groups effectiveness for problem solving and innovation. But sometimes all thats needed is a change of scenery to liberate thinking and let the ideas soar.
         <em>
          <strong>
           YourOfficeAnywhere
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://martinairing.com/blog/reflections-on-the-entrepreneurial-journey/" target="_blank" title="Reflections on the entrepreneurial journey">
          Yoga for entrepreneurs
         </a>
         . Really, its not that strange, since meditation, analysis, positive thinking, and visualization are all necessary components of the entrepreneurial journey. Take a deep breath and join us in this post for a moment of reflection on your small business goals.
         <em>
          <strong>
           Small Business Bliss
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Management
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.inc.com/rudina-seseri/how-to-build-a-great-e-commerce-company.html" target="_blank" title="As an investor, I see dozens of e-commerce companies each week. These four qualities separate the great ones from the flops.">
          What makes a spectacular e-commerce startup
         </a>
         ? Find out what differentiating characteristics one venture capitalist looks for when deciding whether to fund new e-commerce ventures, and see whether your small business is leveraging these four pillars of success to cut through the clutter of the competition.
         <em>
          <strong>
           Inc.com
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://smallbiztechnology.com/archive/2011/12/why-good-design-makes-great-business-looking-good-is-not-just-for-big-brands.html/" target="_blank" title="Why Good Design Makes Great Business  Looking Good Is Not Just for Big Brands">
          Visual creatures, we humans
         </a>
         . First impressions are often the result of what we see. Since the internet is first and foremost a visual medium, its doubly important that you incorporate great design elements into your small business Website. But no matter what youre doing now, here are a few examples of what
         <em>
          not
         </em>
         to do.
         <em>

          <p>
           As the Christmas season nears heres something businesses should remember, whether its an online store or a brick-and-mortar establishment  think from the point of view of the customer, plan ahead, and give them what they want and need. Heres an example. One of the things many shoppers are concerned with amidst the Christmas shopping rush is how to dress their gifts beautifully. Its a hassle to have to look for a ribbon or accessory that will match the chosen wrapping paper. If youre talking about gifts for many recipients, multiply the hassle by the number of people youre giving gifts to. A business can make shopping easy for its customers by offering different packaging options, each one color-coded, with something suitable for every kind of recipient (grandma, grandpa, spouse, boss, daughter, son, grandchildren, etc). If a business thinks of its customers needs and goes out of its way to solve their problems, the customers will  keep coming back, year after year.
