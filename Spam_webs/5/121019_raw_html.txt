        <p>
         <em>
          The best homemade vanilla cake and strawberry frosting  perfect for a party or birthday! The little cakes are made with a biscuit or cookie cutter so theyre easier than they look.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Tell me: what is your ideal birthday food?
        </p>
        <p>
         For me? Im all about tradition. I want cake.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Mini Vanilla Layer Cakes with Strawberry Swiss Meringue Buttercream: the perfect moist vanilla cake and fluffy not-too-sweet strawberry frosting! Great for any birthday or Spring celebration. www.thereciperebel.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2400" height="915" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Mini-Vanilla-Layer-Cakes-with-Strawberry-Swiss-Meringue-Buttercream-www.thereciperebel.com-3-3-610x915.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         And not some fancy, new-age birthday cake. I want vanilla or chocolate  the simpler, the better. A good thick layer of not-too-sweet frosting and maybe some sprinkles for good measure.
        </p>
        <p>
         Confession: I have
         <em>
          always
         </em>
         preferred store-bought birthday cake over homemade.
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont know what it is.
        </p>
        <p>
         But I suspect part of the reason is that light, fluffy bakery frosting. Theres just (almost!) nothing like it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Mini Vanilla Layer Cakes with Strawberry Swiss Meringue Buttercream: the perfect moist vanilla cake and fluffy not-too-sweet strawberry frosting! Great for any birthday or Spring celebration. www.thereciperebel.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2401" height="915" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Mini-Vanilla-Layer-Cakes-with-Strawberry-Swiss-Meringue-Buttercream-www.thereciperebel.com-4-3-610x915.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I used to work in a store bakery when I was in high school, and I ate a ridiculous amount of sweets. I could eat the cake frosting straight from a spoon, sans cake.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres a time and a place for classic buttercream frosting (in fact I can think of
         <em>
          plenty
         </em>
         ). But its just so sweet, that sometimes I want something a little fluffier and a little less sweet.
        </p>
        <p>
         And then I found Swiss Meringue Buttercream. And my life has never been the same.
        </p>
        <p>
         I think its my favorite frosting. Ever.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Mini Vanilla Layer Cakes with Strawberry Swiss Meringue Buttercream: the perfect moist vanilla cake and fluffy not-too-sweet strawberry frosting! Great for any birthday or Spring celebration. www.thereciperebel.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2402" height="406" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Mini-Vanilla-Layer-Cakes-with-Strawberry-Swiss-Meringue-Buttercream-www.thereciperebel.com-5-3-610x406.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         But Im not going to lie  it takes a bit of time. And a bit of equipment. But its so totally worth it if youre going for something extra special, or if you just like your frosting a little on the milder side.
        </p>
        <p>
         To make the cakes, I baked my favorite cake recipe (adapted slightly from
         <a href="http://addapinch.com/cooking/the-best-white-cake-recipe/#.Ukgwxoa-1I5" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://addapinch.com/cooking/the-best-white-cake-recipe/#.Ukgwxoa-1I5', ' Add a Pinch']);" target="_blank">
          Add a Pinch
         </a>
         ), but I spread the batter between a large sheet pan and a 913 pan. I wanted fairly thin layers, and I think they could even be a little bit thinner. I lined the pans with parchment so I could easily remove them from the pans to cut them.
        </p>
        <p>
         I baked them ahead of time, so I covered in plastic wrap and stuck the whole pans in the freezer for a couple days. Then I removed them and let them thaw slightly while I made the frosting.
        </p>
        <p>
         I cut them out with a biscuit cutter (about 2.5 in diameter), and I got about 22 circles, or 11 2-layer mini cakes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Mini Vanilla Layer Cakes with Strawberry Swiss Meringue Buttercream www.thereciperebel.com 2" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2403" height="407" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Mini-Vanilla-Layer-Cakes-with-Strawberry-Swiss-Meringue-Buttercream-www.thereciperebel.com-2-610x407.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         And dont even worry about the extra cake! I got plenty of scraps, and I used them to make a trifle. I threw them all in a large container and stuck them in the freezer until I was ready to make my trifle. Super easy! Then you have a quick dessert for company when you dont have a lot of time to bake later on.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Mini Vanilla Layer Cakes with Strawberry Swiss Meringue Buttercream www.thereciperebel.com 1" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2404" height="915" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Mini-Vanilla-Layer-Cakes-with-Strawberry-Swiss-Meringue-Buttercream-www.thereciperebel.com-1-610x915.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         To make the frosting, youll need a double boiler, a thermometer, and an electric mixer (I recommend stand mixer, because it takes a bit of time to whip!). Ive never owned a real double boiler. You can see my make-shift double boiler above  I take a small pot and a small glass bowl and just make sure the bowl doesnt sit too low in the pot. You dont want the bottom of the bowl touching the water in the pot.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its going to take a bit of time to come to the right temperature, and its going to take some time after that to whip to stiff peaks. Just have patience!
        </p>
        <p>
         When its done, you can just slather it on the little vanilla layer cakes, or grab a piping bag and a simple tip and give it a swirl. I chose not to frost the sides, because (a) its less work and (b) its so rare that the sides of a cake are so perfect, why not show them off?!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Mini Vanilla Layer Cakes with Strawberry Swiss Meringue Buttercream: the perfect moist vanilla cake and fluffy not-too-sweet strawberry frosting! Great for any birthday or Spring celebration. www.thereciperebel.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2405" height="915" src="http://cf.thereciperebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Mini-Vanilla-Layer-Cakes-with-Strawberry-Swiss-Meringue-Buttercream-www.thereciperebel.com-6-3-610x915.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Vanilla cake:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1  cups butter, softened
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 cups granulated sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            5 eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup milk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tsp vanilla
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 cups all-purpose flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tsp baking powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp salt
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Strawberry Meringue Buttercream
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1  cups diced strawberries
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1  cup granulated sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 large egg whites
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 sticks butter (1 ) cups, room temperature, cubed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            -1 cup powdered icing sugar, optional
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Vanilla cake:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Prepare a large pan and a 9x13 pan by spraying with non-stick spray, then line with parchment (the non-stick helps the parchment to stay down), and spray the parchment with non-stick spray.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In the bowl of a stand mixer, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in eggs until well combined. Add milk and vanilla and beat until smooth.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add flour, baking powder and salt and mix just until combined and smooth.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Divide between both pans  you want an even, thin layer in both pans.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean or with moist crumbs (baking time will vary based on pan size, so be sure to check often).
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cool completely. Then you can either freeze the cakes (as I mentioned above) or cut out your mini cakes with a regular biscuit cutter (about 2.5 diameter).
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Strawberry Meringue Frosting:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Puree strawberries in a blender or food processor (I used a Magic Bullet because it gets things the smoothest for me). Set aside.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In the bottom of your double broiler (see notes and photo above in post if you do not have one), put about  - 1 of water (make sure that the bottom of your bowl will not touch the water when its boiling). Set on the stove and bring to a boil.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In the top of your double broiler (your bowl), off the heat, whisk together the sugar and egg whites. Place over your pot of boiling water and reduce heat to medium or medium-low. Continue cooking and whisking until the mixture reaches 160 degrees F and sugar is dissolved (this took a while for me  10-15 minutes).
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pour mixture into the bowl of a stand mixer and whip with a whisk on medium-high speed until stiff peaks form and mixture is room temperature (about 10 minutes).
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add butter, one cube at a time, beating until incorporated. Add the pureed strawberries gradually, making sure that they do not dilute your frosting and make it runny (I used the full amount and it was not runny at all  this is just a precaution). Beat until combined (if your frosting begins to look soupy or curdled after adding the butter and strawberries, continue beating 3-5 minutes on medium-high and it will come together! This happens every time I make it).
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            If you find that its not as sweet as you hoped, add in optional powdered sugar.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Frost cakes as desired. Store in the refrigerator or freezer until ready to serve.
           </li>
          </ol>
