         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Whats Your Style?
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="sep">
           Posted on
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.cabinetsanddesigns.net/2013/10/15/3140-whats-your-style/" rel="bookmark" title="1:19 pm">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2013-10-15T13:19:01+00:00" pubdate="">
            October 15, 2013
           </time>
          </a>
          <span class="byline">
           <span class="sep">
            by
           </span>
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.cabinetsanddesigns.net/author/sjohnson/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Cabinets and Designs">
             Cabinets and Designs
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          There are a lot of reasons why we recommend working with a
          <a href="http://www.cabinetsanddesigns.net/meet-our-staff/">
           professional designer
          </a>
          ; their expertise is invaluable when it comes to designing what is arguably the most important room in your home. One of the best reasons to work with a designer, however, is their ability to help you determine and refine your style.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Cabinet Design" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-3141" height="150" src="http://www.cabinetsanddesigns.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/cabinet-design-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           A Designers Eye
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Let your designers eye be the lens of the camera through which you view your remodel. They can focus on the details when needed, or zoom out to show you the big picture when perspective is required. During the early stages of a remodeling project, its easy to get distracted by the sheer number of choices and decisions: colors, textures, countertops, appliances, flooring, lighting, windows! And thats just for starters. Your designer will keep you on track, focusing on options that fall within your budget and suit your aesthetic.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Make it Personal
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Your designer needs to understand your style, but they also need to know how you liveafter all, this is a room you are going to live and work in. Talk about your familys routines and lifestyle, which will dictate many choices, including flooring and fabrics.
         </p>
         <p>
          Take a look around your own house, and take note of any repeated patterns or colors; if you have a few favorite signature pieces, share those with your designer. They are clues that will help your designer not only achieve the look you want, but to make it feel organic to younew, but still home.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Get Visual
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Perhaps it goes without saying, but it is always a good idea to show your designer photos of rooms whose aesthetic youfind appealing. You can talk to the designer about what appeals to you, which will help give them an idea in what direction to take your current dcor.
         </p>
         <p>
          Are you ready for a remodel, but you dont know where to start? Visit us at Cabinets &amp; Designs today! Our design team is ready to help you achieve the remodel of your dreams.
         </p>
        </div>
