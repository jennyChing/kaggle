           <span>
            Bull sharks are mainly found in the warmer and shallower coastal waters of the southern hemisphere. The bull shark is one of the most common species of shark found worldwide.
            <p>
             The bull shark is able to inhabit both salt and
             <a href="/reference/freshwater/">
              freshwater
             </a>
             sources just as happily, with one bull shark known to have swum 4,000 km up the Amazon River in South America, finally ending up in the mountainous Peru.
            </p>
            <p>
             The bull shark is well known for its unpredictable and aggressive temperament and is thought to be one of the most dangerous sharks towards
             <a href="/animals/human/">
              humans
             </a>
             . This is probably because the bull inhabits coastal waters and rivers and is therefore more likely to come into contact with swimmers and water sports enthusiasts.
            </p>
            <p>
             The bull shark is an effective and dominant
             <a href="/reference/glossary/#Predator">
              predator
             </a>
             in its environment and so has no real natural
             <a href="/reference/glossary/#Predator">
              predator
             </a>
             itself. Bull sharks are occasionally hunted by the bigger shark species such as the
             <a href="/animals/tiger/">
              tiger
             </a>
             shark and the infamous great white shark.
            </p>
           </span>
