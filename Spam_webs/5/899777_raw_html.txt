            <p>
             For many people the hardest part about working out is simply getting motivated to start. A lot of men and women alike tend to view exercise as a chore as well as something that is optional. When it comes to actually getting off your ass and doing it many often get the typical its too hard, Im not in the mood, I dont have time, or I can always do it tomorrow mentality in their head.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.workoutbliss.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Workout-Motivation.jpg">
              <img alt="Workout Motivation" class="aligncenter wp-image-20" height="582" src="http://www.workoutbliss.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Workout-Motivation.jpg" width="400"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             This is nonsense. If you continue to think negatively about it youll only continue to delay any progress. Whether you want to lose weight or just get in better shape, exercise is a MUST. So how can you get motivated to keep working out even when you dont feel like it?Try using the tips listed below.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              1. Dont Do the Same Routine Every Day
             </strong>
            </p>
            <!--Ad Injection:random-->
            <div style="">
             <center>

            <p>
             A lot of people are guilty of this. Most of us like to form a specific routine and just stick to it. This can be a daily jog, a 30 minute swim, lifting weights, or whatever it may be. Doing the exact same thing day in and day out can get to be painfully boring and fuel your negativity toward your workouts. Mix up your routines so your body and mind dont get bored.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              2. Grab a Friend
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             If a workout seems too dull for you simply because you hate doing it alone then rope a friend or family member into doing your routines with you. This way youll have someone to talk to, keep you on track, or even compete with. This makes a workout feel a bit more like fun than work.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              3. Keep Track of Your Progress
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             One of the best motivation tools available is simply keeping track of the progress youre making. Whether you looking to shed a few pounds or build up endurance to run for greater distances  track it. Watch your progress each day and try to set a goal to burn a slightly higher number of calories with each workout or run 1-3 minutes longer every day. Nothing compares to besting yourself with each workout.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              4. Follow a Program
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             If youre trying to burn fat, build muscle, or improve your endurance and you find yourself hitting a plateau or just struggling in general it may be time to check out a program. Theres an effective and detailed program available for ANYTHING you want to do health-wise. For example,

             is one of the best. It provides an easy-to-follow diet and a daily workout schedule that will help you reach your fat loss goals in no time. Just make sure you find a credible program, not just any junk fad that Dr. Oz or other professionals endorse.
            </p>
