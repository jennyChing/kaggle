          <h1 class="article-title">
           MARVEL'S AGENT CARTER!!
           <br/>
           What Make The Critics Of ABCs Peggy-Centric Series Sequel To CAPTAIN AMERICA??
          </h1>
          <p class="article-metainfo">
           Published at: Jan. 6, 2015, 10 p.m. CST by
           <a href="https://twitter.com/hercaicn">
            hercules
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="article-heading-right">
          <div class="fb-like" data-action="like" data-href="http://www.aintitcool.com/node/69938" data-layout="box_count" data-share="false" data-show-faces="false" data-width="50">
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="article-content">
         <p>
          <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/9l5Kt_c1-7U" width="560">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="mailto:Herculesaicn@yahoo.com">
           I am  Hercules!!
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Set in 1946 as Captain America secretly continued his career as a giant ice-cube, Agent Carter follows Steve Rogers girlfriend Peggy (still played by the fabulous Hayley Atwell) as she makes coffee and does filing for the agents of SHIELD predecessor Strategic Scientific Reserve  and carries out secret missions for Iron Mans falsely-accused fugitive dad Howard Stark (still played by Dominick Cooper). Theres also a human version of Stark butler Jarvis played by James DArcy to liaison between Carter and Stark. James Urbaniak (the voice of Dr. Thaddeus Venture on The Venture Bros.) recurs as one Miles Van Ert.
         </p>
         <p>
          Christopher Markus &amp; Stephen McFeely, who wrote the two Captain America movies that introduced Peggy, wrote tonights first hour.
         </p>
         <p>
          Showrunners on tonights second hour and beyond are Tara Butters and Michele Fazekas, who wrote the hilarious Reaper pilot before Avengers writer-director Joss Whedon hired them to script Dollhouse. (Dollhouse star Enver Gjokaj plays a fellow SSR agent and potential romantic interest for Peggy. Ray Wise, who played the devil in Reaper, also turns up.)
         </p>
         <p>
          (If all of this somehow fails to excite you, know that wedged between the acts of tonights episodes is a big trailer for Marvels Ant Man movie due this July. Atwell plays the 1960s version of Peggy in Ant Man alongside the 1960s version of Michael Douglas Hank Pym and John Slatterys Iron Man 2 version of Howard Stark.)
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.hitfix.com/whats-alan-watching/review-marvels-agent-carter-swings-into-the-big-band-era">
           Hitfix says
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <strong>
           ... Atwell effortlessly moves from the wings to center stage. She has physical presence to spare, a fine deadpan comic delivery, and projects just enough vulnerability to remind you that this is not a woman with superpowers, even if she's doing her best to live up to the example of a man who was very super indeed. She also works very well alongside D'Arcy  on the whole, Atwell is so good, and the show has so much fun with the period setting, that it's a really promising start for "Agent Carter"  and also a very encouraging sign for all the upcoming Marvel TV shows.
          </strong>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2015/01/06/arts/television/marvels-agent-carter-debuts-on-abc.html?_r=0">
           The New York Times says
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <strong>
           ... a fairly entertaining conglomeration of nostalgia, postwar intrigue, comic-book science fiction and screwball comedy (with frequent interludes of bone-crunching violence). 
          </strong>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://communityvoices.post-gazette.com/arts-entertainment-living/tuned-in/item/38614-agent-carter-in-action-on-abc">
           The Pittsburgh Post-Gazette says
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <strong>
           ... a welcome, interesting entry, a far cry from the Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. large ensemble and a welcome opportunity to put a woman front and center. In addition, the 1940s production design is admirably detailed and deep, from costumes to computer-generated sets and backgrounds. 
          </strong>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.sfgate.com/tv/article/Agent-Carter-review-Cool-addition-to-5990386.php">
           The San Francisco Chronicle says
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <strong>
           ... a heck of a lot of fun  Atwell is terrific in the title role, as capable in the efficient, unflappable skin of Peggy as she is when pretending to be a blond American floozy in a gin joint. James DArcy is a model of amusing dry wit as Jarvis, Starks butler and his official liaison with Peggy. 
          </strong>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.usatoday.com/story/life/tv/2015/01/05/agent-carter-review/21269507/">
           USA Today says
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <strong>
           ... What matters is Atwell's ability to project both Peggy's sense of loss and her determination, and the show's skill at highlighting the problems women faced while still retaining the pleasures of a comic-book pulp adventure. It's hard not to fall a little in love with a woman who knows how to use her co-workers' sexism against them  and who can disguise herself as a blond femme fatale while stapling a bad guy out of her way. 
          </strong>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://variety.com/2015/tv/reviews/tv-review-marvels-agent-carter-1201389031/">
           Variety says
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <strong>
           ... the combination of the British actress and post-World War II setting make the Marvel-branded vehicle, Agent Carter, considerable fun, and in some ways more promising than the series its replacing, the uneven Agents of SHIELD. While theres no assurance this spinoff will have legs, the opening salvo is worthy of a hearty Hail, Carter.
          </strong>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          8 p.m. Tuesday. ABC.
         </p>
