         <header class="entry-header">
          <h1 class="entry-title">
           Is Someone Getting On Your Nerves? Try This
          </h1>
          <div class="byline-meta">
           <i class="icon-pencil">
           </i>
           <span class="author vcard">
            <span class="fn">
             <a href="http://www.havingtime.com/author/banu/" rel="author">
              Banu Sekendur
             </a>
            </span>
           </span>
           <i class="icon-calendar">
           </i>
           <time class="date time published" title="2014-10-06T18:46:49+0000">
            06/10/2014
           </time>
           <span class="post-comments">
            
            <a href="http://www.havingtime.com/is-someone-getting-on-your-nerves-try-this/#comments">
             <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="3984 http://www.havingtime.com/?p=3984">
              Leave a Comment
             </span>
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </header>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p class="p1">
           <a href="http://www.havingtime.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dont-worry-be-happy.jpg">
            <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
             <img alt="don't worry be happy" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3998" height="471" src="http://www.havingtime.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dont-worry-be-happy.jpg" width="700"/>
             <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.havingtime.com/is-someone-getting-on-your-nerves-try-this/&amp;media=http://www.havingtime.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dont-worry-be-happy.jpg&amp;description=Is Someone Getting On Your Nerves Try This')">
             </span>
            </span>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p class="p1" style="text-align: center;">
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #000080;">
             You are the sky. Everything else  its just the weather. 
            </span>
           </strong>
           <span style="color: #000080;">
            <em>
             Pema Chdrn
            </em>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p1" style="text-align: justify;">
           If you are in a human body and you are not the
           <a class="snppopup" href="http://www.dalailama.com/">
            Dalai Lama
           </a>
           , it is possible that you have experienced an intense
           <a href="http://www.havingtime.com/how-to-diffuse-an-argument-3-helpful-tips/">
            negative reaction
           </a>
           to someone at some point in your life. In some cases, the feeling is so strong that you might feel guilty for having it. As if there is an evil twin inside of you, trying to break through your chest and wrap its hands around this others persons neck and shake them up a bit. Or worse.
          </p>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            That is the shadow energy in all of us. The shadow consists of unnamed and disowned parts of us that were deemed unacceptable by society. It is not OK to be lazy, greedy, jealous, competitive or even to have a high sex drive. Yet, as we are all pieces of the mosaic of life, these traits, feelings and tendencies exist inside us, hiding in the dark corners of our psyche.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            <span style="color: #333399;">
             <strong>
              When we have such a strong negative emotional reaction to someone it is an amazing opportunity for our personal growth.
             </strong>
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            It is an opportunity to accept and love a part of us that we were taught to reject. I certainly have had more than a handful of experiences where
            <a href="http://www.havingtime.com/recovering-from-a-toxic-friendship-dos-and-donts/">
             I felt guilty for the negative feelings
            </a>
            about the other person that plagued me.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            Or I was irritated by them so much that it was hard to hide my feelings. Prickly words would come out of my mouth unconsciously or Id find myself engaging in passive-aggressive behaviours in order to get back at this person and to relieve my psychic tension, to no avail. Because the solution wasnt about changing them, it was about developing the courage to look at a shadow side of me and freeing up the energy for it to express itself authentically by accepting it.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            Eventually, originating from Byron Katies The Work, I came up with a simple exercise that helps me work through the feelings that come up and set the stage for me to take back the projection from that person.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            I do this for two reasons: a) I want to get to know, accept and love all of me (lifelong process).
            <br/>
            b) I know the power of the Shadow and its ability to destroy relationships and lives.
           </span>
          </p>
          <h1 class="p3">
           Is Someone Getting On Your Nerves? Try This
          </h1>
          <h3 class="p3">
           <span style="color: #333399;">
            <strong>
             <span class="s1">
              1.Come up with one short sentence that can express your
              <i>
               grief
              </i>
              about this person. Example: He is selfish
             </span>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.havingtime.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/life-is-an-echo.jpg">
            <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
             <img alt="life is an echo" class="aligncenter wp-image-3785" height="538" src="http://www.havingtime.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/life-is-an-echo-1024x1009.jpg" width="546"/>
             <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.havingtime.com/is-someone-getting-on-your-nerves-try-this/&amp;media=http://www.havingtime.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/life-is-an-echo-1024x1009.jpg&amp;description=Is Someone Getting On Your Nerves Try This')">
             </span>
            </span>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h3 class="p3">
           <span style="color: #333399;">
            <span class="s2">
             <b>
              2.
             </b>
            </span>
            <span class="s1">
             Turn the sentence around and say each of these statement to yourself (out loud is even better):
            </span>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <ol class="ol1">
           <li class="li4">
            <span class="s1">
             I am selfish
            </span>
           </li>
           <li class="li4">
            <span class="s1">
             I used to be selfish
            </span>
           </li>
           <li class="li4">
            <span class="s1">
             I wish I was selfish,
            </span>
           </li>
           <li class="li4">
            <span class="s1">
             I could be selfish.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            Sit with each statement until you are able to feel the truth of them in your body/mind consciousness.
           </span>
          </p>
          <h3 class="p3">
           <span style="color: #333399;">
            <span class="s2">
             3.
            </span>
            <span class="s1">
             The third step is for opening the heart and flexing the compassion muscle. This is also when we arrive at new truths and decisions for ourselves and access the compassion behind our irritation.
            </span>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            <i>
             Behind every behavior is one intention: to feel good
            </i>
            . This is universal and it includes animals. Applying this question to the selfish friend situation, your new interpretation and inner dialogue could look like this:
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            He believes that, unless he puts himself first, his needs do not get met. He wants to make sure that he is taken care of. I dont know him well enough to understand where his behaviour comes from. People do what they do in order to feel good, loved, effective and enough. Maybe by taking care of himself, he feels like he is relieving people from the responsibility of making him happy. Maybe there is wisdom for me to learn here. Maybe what I consider selfish is his normal. I am willing to see a deeper truth.
           </span>
          </p>
          <h3 class="p3">
           <span style="color: #333399;">
            <span class="s2">
             <b>
              4.
             </b>
            </span>
            <span class="s1">
             What do I need to be more of or less of?
            </span>
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            The truth is, even if this person is really too selfish for your taste, your intense reaction is not about their selfishness. In my experience, we react that way because we are doing the same thing and cant accept it or we need to do more of it but hold ourselves back due to our learned beliefs.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="p3">
           <strong>
            <span class="s1" style="color: #000080;">
             In this case, there are a few questions left to ask ourselves to clear the way:
            </span>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol class="ol1">
           <li class="li4">
            <span class="s1">
             In which ways could I be more selfish?
            </span>
           </li>
           <li class="li4">
            <span class="s1">
             How is my lack of making myself a priority affecting my happiness?
            </span>
           </li>
           <li class="li4">
            <span class="s1">
             How have I been selfish in the past that I havent forgiven myself for?
            </span>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p class="p3">
           <span class="s1">
            I admit that it is an uncomfortable exercise to do in the beginning yet incredibly liberating. As you make this into a habit and get better at being with the discomfort of being a selfish, see how it has been useful), you will be less fearful others judging you and start carrying yourself with
            <a href="http://www.havingtime.com/i-dont-have-self-esteem-is-a-myth/" title="improve self-esteem">
             more confidence
            </a>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
