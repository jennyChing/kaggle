         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Book releases that have caught my eye  April 2015
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-29T17:13:42+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           March 29, 2015
          </time>
          by
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/author/admin" itemprop="url" rel="author">
            <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
             Jo
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="entry-comments-link">
           <a href="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/2015/03/book-releases-that-have-caught-my-eye-april-2015.html#respond">
            Leave a Comment
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="7829344" data-link="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/2015/03/book-releases-that-have-caught-my-eye-april-2015.html" data-summary="" data-title="Book releases that have caught my eye - April 2015">
         </div>
         <p>
          Here are just a fewof the titles being released in April2015 that have caught my eye.
         </p>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <em>
            <img alt="black-rock-white-city" class="alignright size-grid-featured wp-image-18059" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/black-rock-white-city-196x300.jpg" width="196"/>
            Black Rock White City
           </em>
           is a novel about the damages of war, the limits of choice, and the hope of love.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           During a hot Melbourne summer Jovans cleaning work at a bayside hospital is disrupted by acts of graffiti and violence becoming increasingly malevolent. For Jovan the mysterious words that must be cleaned away dislodge the poetry of the past. He and his wife Suzana were forced to flee Sarajevo and the death of their children.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Intensely human, yet majestic in its moral vision,
           <em>
            Black Rock White City
           </em>
           is an essential story of Australias suburbs now, of displacement and immediate threat, and the unexpected responses of two refugees as they try to reclaim their dreams. It is a breathtaking roar of energy that explores the immigrant experience with ferocity, beauty and humour.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: left;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/Black-Rock-White-City-Patric/9781921924835">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/black-rock-white-city-a-s-patric/prod9781921924835.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Black-Rock-White-City-Patric-ebook/dp/B00UWDJRYU/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1427612073&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=black+rock+white+city">
            Amazon
           </a>
           |
           <a href="https://store.kobobooks.com/en-US/ebook/black-rock-white-city">
            Kobobooks
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <p>
          <span id="more-18056">
          </span>
         </p>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px; margin: 0px 0px 25px; text-align: left; background-color: #f2f2f2;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="wp-image-18058 size-grid-featured alignleft" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/the-thrilling-adventures-of-lovelace-and-babbage-232x300.jpg" width="232"/>
           <em>
            The Thrilling Adventuresof Lovelaceand Babbage
           </em>
           . . . in which Sydney Padua transforms one of the most compelling scientific collaborations into a hilarious series of adventures.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Meet Victorian Londons most dynamic duo: Charles Babbage, the unrealized inventor of the computer, and his accomplice, Ada, Countess of Lovelace, the peculiar protoprogrammer and daughter of Lord Byron. When Lovelace translated a description of Babbages plans for an enormous mechanical calculating machine in 1842, she added annotations three times longer than the original work. Her footnotes contained the first appearance of the general computing theory, a hundred years before an actual computer was built. Sadly, Lovelace died of cancer a decade after publishing the paper, and Babbage never built any of his machines.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           But do not despair!
           <em>
            The Thrilling Adventures of Lovelace and Babbage
           </em>
           presents a rollicking alternate reality in which Lovelace and Babbage do build the Difference Engine and then use it to build runaway economic models, battle the scourge of spelling errors, explore the wilder realms of mathematics, and, of course, fight crime-for the sake of both London and science. Complete with extensive footnotes that rival those penned by Lovelace herself, historical curiosities, and never-before-seen diagrams of Babbages mechanical, steam-powered computer,
           <em>
            The Thrilling Adventures of Lovelace and Babbage
           </em>
           is wonderfully whimsical, utterly unusual, and, above all, entirely irresistible.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: left;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/Thrilling-Adventures-Lovelace-Babbage-Sydney-Padua/9780307908278">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/the-thrilling-adventures-of-lovelace-and-babbage-sydney-padua/prod9780307908278.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Thrilling-Adventures-Lovelace-Babbage-Computer/dp/0307908275/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1427612185&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=Thrilling+Adventures+of+Lovelace+and+Babbage">
            Amazon
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/the-thrilling-adventures-of-lovelace-and-babbage-sydney-padua/1120420212?ean=9780307908278">
            B&amp;N
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="wp-image-18057 size-grid-featured alignright" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/the-truth-and-other-lies-197x300.jpg" width="197"/>
           Famous bestselling author, loving husband, generous friend  Henry Hayden is a pleasant person to have around. Or so it seems. And when his mistress, who is also his editor, becomes pregnant, his carefully constructed life threatens to fall apart.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           So Henry works out an ingenious plan. Craftily and cold-bloodedly, he intertwines lies and truths and all the shades of grey in-between.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           But when he tries to get rid of his mistress, Henry makes a terrible mistake. Not only are the police soon after him, but his past, which he has painstakingly kept under the carpet, also threatens to catch up with him with deadly consequences.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: left;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/Truth-Other-Lies-Sascha-Arango/9781471139710">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/the-truth-and-other-lies-sascha-arango/prod9781922182777.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Truth-Other-Lies-Novel/dp/147679555X/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1427612382&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=the+truth+and+other+lies">
            Amazon
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/the-truth-and-other-lies-sascha-arango/1119855949?ean=9781476795553">
            B&amp;N
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <a href="http://www.tkqlhce.com/8k101ft1zt0GONINJMIGIIMPIKJN" target="_blank">
           <img alt="TheReadingRoom" border="0" src="http://www.lduhtrp.net/mf98qmqeki3BA5A6953559C576A"/>
          </a>
         </center>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-18067 size-grid-featured" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/quicksand-197x300.jpg" width="197"/>
           The literary event of 2015. Steve Toltz follows his extraordinary debut, the Booker-shortlisted
           <em>
            A Fraction of the Whole
           </em>
           , with a novel thats just as edgy, hilarious and compelling:
           <em>
            Quicksand
           </em>
           , at once unmistakeably Toltzean and unlike anything thats come before.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Why should I let you write about me?
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Because youll inspire people. To count their blessings.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Aldo has been so relentlessly unlucky  in business, in love, in life  that the universe seems to have taken against him personally. Even Liam, his best friend, describes him as a well-known parasite and failure. Aldo has always faced the future with optimism and despair in equal measure, but this last twist of fate may finally have brought him undone.Theres hope, but not for Aldo.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Liam hasnt been doing much better himself: a failed writer with a rocky marriage and a dangerous job he never wanted. But something good may come out of Aldos lowest point. Liam may finally have found his inspiration. Together, maybe they can turn bad luck into an art form.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           What begins as a document of Aldos disasters develops into a profound story of love lost, found and betrayed; of freedom and incarceration; of suffering and transcendence; of fate, faith and friendship; of taking risks  in art, work, love and life  and finding inspiration in all the wrong places.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <em>
            Quicksand
           </em>
           is a fearlessly funny, outrageously inventive dark comedy that looks contemporary life unblinkingly in the eye. It confirms Steve Toltz as one of our most original and insightful novelists.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/Quicksand-Steve-Toltz/9781473606067">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/quicksand-steve-toltz/prod9781926428680.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Quicksand-Steve-Toltz/dp/147679782X/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1427612667&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=quicksand+toltz">
            Amazon
           </a>
           |
           <a href="https://store.kobobooks.com/en-US/ebook/quicksand-21">
            Kobobooks

           |
           <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/quicksand-steve-toltz/1120678765?ean=9781476797823">
            B&amp;N
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="alignright wp-image-18066 size-grid-featured" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/where-the-bird-sings-best-201x300.jpg" width="201"/>
           The magnum opus from Alejandro Jodorowsky-director of
           <em>
            The Holy Mountain
           </em>
           , star of Jodorowskys
           <em>
            Dune
           </em>
           , spiritual guru behind
           <em>
            Psychomagic and The Way of Tarot
           </em>
           , innovator behind classic comics
           <em>
            The Incal and Metabarons
           </em>
           , and legend of Latin American literature.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           There has never been an artist like the polymathic Chilean director, author, and mystic Alejandro Jodorowsky. For eight decades, he has blazed new trails across a dazzling variety of creative fields. While his psychedelic, visionary films have been celebrated by the likes of John Lennon, Marina Abramovic, and Kanye West, his novels-praised throughout Latin America in the same breath as those of Gabriel Garcia Marquez-have remained largely unknown in the English-speaking world. Until now.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Where the Bird Sings Best tells the fantastic story of the Jodorowskys emigration from Ukraine to Chile amidst the political and cultural upheavals of the 19th and 20th centuries. LikeOne Hundred Years of Solitude, Jodorowskys book transforms family history into heroic legend: incestuous beekeepers hide their crime with a living cloak of bees, a czar fakes his own death to live as a hermit amongst the animals, a devout grandfather confides only in the ghost of a wise rabbi, a transgender ballerina with a voracious sexual appetite holds a would-be saint in thrall. Kaleidoscopic, exhilarating, and erotic,
           <em>
            Where the Bird Sings Best
           </em>
           expands the classic immigration story to mythic proportions.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: left;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/Where-Bird-Sings-Best-Alejandro-Jodorowsky/9781632060280">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/where-the-bird-sings-best-alejandro-jodorowsky/prod9781632060280.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Where-Bird-Sings-Alejandro-Jodorowsky/dp/1632060280/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1427612857&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=where+the+bird+sings+best">

            B&amp;N
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-18065 size-grid-featured" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/the-dream-lover-206x300.jpg" width="206"/>
           New York Times bestselling author Elizabeth Berg has written a lush historical novel based on the sensuous Parisian life of the nineteenth-century writer George Sand-which is perfect for readers of Nancy Horan and Elizabeth Gilbert.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           At the beginning of this powerful novel, we meet Aurore Dupin as she is leaving her estranged husband, a loveless marriage, and her familys estate in the French countryside to start a new life in Paris. There, she gives herself a new name-George Sand-and pursues her dream of becoming a writer, embracing an unconventional and even scandalous lifestyle.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Paris in the nineteenth century comes vividly alive, illuminated by the story of the loves, passions, and fierce struggles of a woman who defied the confines of society. Sands many lovers and friends include Frederic Chopin, Gustave Flaubert, Franz Liszt, Eugene Delacroix, Victor Hugo, Marie Dorval, and Alfred de Musset. As Sand welcomes fame and friendship, she fights to overcome heartbreak and prejudice, failure and loss. Though considered the most gifted genius of her time, she works to reconcile the pain of her childhood, of disturbing relationships with her mother and daughter, and of her intimacies with women and men. Will the life she longs for always be just out of reach-a dream?
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Brilliantly written in luminous prose, and with remarkable insights into the heart and mind of a literary force,
           <em>
            The Dream Lover

           An infamous financier courts an ethereal young woman who lives in a secret walled garden in Shanghai.           <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Ranging from the wicked noir Shanghai of 1946, to the echo chambers of Hollywood, to remote, snow-covered mountains, This Is How it Really Sounds follows three men, each in search of a different life. Small-town Alaskan Harry Harrington is a legend in a small circle, once the worlds greatest extreme skier, racing avalanches and knocking back flips off of cliffs. Peter Harrington is a world-famous financier, hated across the globe for making hundreds of millions of dollars on his hedge fund, and fleeing New York to begin a new venture in Shanghai. Finally, there is Pete Harrington, a middle-aged rock star, now touring third-tier venues and fleeing bankruptcy, but hoping that one great new song can rescue him. All are seeking something that has slipped awayyouth, power, purpose, magic; all are wonderful creations, whose strangely familiar lives and dreams become unforgettable.
