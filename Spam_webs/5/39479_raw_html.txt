       <a href="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Be_Good_Blog.jpg">
        <img alt="Be_Good_Blog" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6840 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Be_Good_Blog.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        Each holiday season, we gift our partners and publishers with something that reflects the work we do and the values we hold. Whether its combining our love of data visualization with coffee to create an interactive poster or sharing our hard-earned knowledge through our book, we pour our hearts and souls into choosing and creating these gifts. This year, we sent our partners a unique set of fine art limited-edition letterpress prints inspired by one of our
        <a href="http://www.columnfivemedia.com/about/mission" target="_blank">
         Five Columns
        </a>
        : Be Good to Each Other.
        <span id="more-3874">
        </span>
       </p>
       <img alt="FND_6032-2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6793 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/FND_6032-2.jpg"/>
       <div>
        <em>
         The Column Five 2013 gift: A unique three-print series
        </em>
       </div>
       <p>
        The Concept
       </p>
       <p>
        When we began the creative process for this project, we started ruminating on our working relationships, particularly with our partners. We are deeply grateful for our partners; they are the reason we get to do what we love each day. Together, we create beautiful work, learn new things and build lasting relationships. We believe in honoring the relationships that help us succeed, which is why we ascribe to the 
        <a href="http://www.columnfivemedia.com/c5s-five-columns-be-good-to-each-other" target="_blank">
         Be Good to Each Other
        </a>
         philosophy.
       </p>
       <a href="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/begoodtoeachotherre.jpg">
        <img alt="begoodtoeachotherre" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6845 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/begoodtoeachotherre.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        <em>
         Each of the Five Columns adorns our office walls, reminding us to practice what we preach.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        But being good to each other isnt always easy. Relationship dynamics can be complicated. In some cases, conflict and frustration occur, while charity and generosity triumph in others. In ideal cases, we work together symbiotically to achieve a greater good. But always at the core is the push and pull, the struggle of compromise, the balance between collaboration and conflict, while striving to be good to each other. This was the complexity that we were eager to explorethus it became the foundation of our concept: to showcase the evolution of relationships, from enemy, to ally, to symbiotic partner.
       </p>
       <p>
        Because we are all ultimately animals, we turned to nature for inspiration, searching for examples of animal kingdom relationships that best represent the spectrum of these relationship dynamics. We delved into research, considering well-known animal relationships (like the age-old conflict between dog and cat) and reading about lesser-known and more unusual pairings that seem almost inconceivable. We finally settled on three unique animal relationships:
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 1em;">
         <strong>
         </strong>
         Red Fox &amp; Gray Fox
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span style="font-size: 1em;">
         Lioness &amp; Baby Antelope
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span style="font-size: 1em;">
         Wolf &amp; Raven
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        Each of these relationships teaches us a valuable lesson about interacting together.
       </p>
       <p>
        The Artwork
       </p>
       <p>
        Once we selected our animal pairs, we searched for the perfect way to present each as a visual metaphor; the yin-yang juxtaposition immediately came to mind. Although it depicts polar oppositeslight and dark, good and evilit symbolizes the interconnectedness and interdependency of two life forces. We knew showcasing our chosen animal pairings within this yin-yang formation was the ideal way to complete the visual metaphor.
       </p>
       <p>
        We then asked some of our favorite fine artists,
        <a href="http://dvdandrea.com/" target="_blank">
         David DAndrea
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://ericawilliamsillustration.com/" target="_blank">
         Erica Williams
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="http://mcdonaghillustration.com/" target="_blank">
         Tim McDonagh
        </a>
        , to turn our ideas into beautiful illustrations that would serve as powerful inspiration in our partners lives. Each artist was assigned an animal relationship depiction and directed to present the pair in a yin-yang formation to create a cohesive look and keep the focus on the illustrations. (It was auspicious that these three artists, as well as our selected printing company, all use animals in their own logos). The results:
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Red Fox &amp; Gray Fox
        </strong>
       </p>
       <a href="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Be_Good_Red_Gray_Fox.jpg">
        <img alt="Be_Good_Red_Gray_Fox" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6847 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Be_Good_Red_Gray_Fox.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        <em>
         Illustration byDavid DAndrea
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        As humans, we often overlook our similarities, focusing instead on our differencesa mindset that serves to stoke and perpetuate conflict, rather than unite. This destructive dynamic mirrors the plight of the Red and Gray foxes, both common in North America. Deforestation has led to a reduction in territory, increasing competition between these animals. Despite the animals many similarities and common enemies, they view each other as opponents in a war for food, territory and dominancea lesson in the benefits of working together, rather than at odds.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lioness &amp; Baby Antelope
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Be_Good_Lioness_Antelope.jpg">
          <img alt="Be_Good_Lioness_Antelope" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6848 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Be_Good_Lioness_Antelope.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </strong>
        <em>
         Illustration by Erica Williams
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        One of the human species most noble traits is our ability to empathize and show compassionkey in building healthy dynamics. Beautiful examples are also evident in the animal world, even among natural-born enemies. Such is the case of a lioness in Kenyas Samburu National Reserve who famously adopted newborn antelopes, protecting them from other animals and humans and even showing signs of grief upon their deathshowing that our nature and interactions are what we make of them.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Wolf &amp; Raven
        </strong>
       </p>
       <a href="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Be_Good_Wolf_Raven.jpg">
        <img alt="Be_Good_Wolf_Raven" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6849 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Be_Good_Wolf_Raven.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        <em>
         Illustration by Tim McDonagh
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        The strongest human relationships are those that work symbiotically. In the same manner, we admire the unusual partnership between wolf and raven, which has long been recognized in folklore and in modern study. Ravens have been known to travel with wolf packs, and wolves are said to watch for their circling as an indicator of food. They have also been observed sharing meals, as wolves invite the ravens to partake in their kills by opening the carcasses to expose the meat. In this way, working together serves both species greatest needs.
       </p>
       <a href="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/postcard_set.jpg">
        <img alt="postcard_set" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6804 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/postcard_set.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        <em>
         Each gift set included the three prints, a thank you letter and postcards featuring information about the project concept and artist bio.
        </em>
       </p>
       <a href="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Print_det3.jpg">
        <img alt="Print_det3" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6805 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/Print_det3.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        <em>
         Each print was impressed on environmentally friendly paper made from 100% cotton linters.
        </em>
       </p>
       <a href="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/FND_5964-2.jpg">
        <img alt="FND_5964-2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6836 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/FND_5964-2.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <a href="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/FND_5819.jpg">
        <img alt="FND_5819" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6808 full-width" src="http://wp_media-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/app/uploads/2014/01/FND_5819.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        <em>
         To ensure each print was personalized, Column Five team members hand-signed, titled, and numbered each poster.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Column Fives chop mark featured a feather, based on our new logo. Birds are social creatures that communicate in a variety of ways, including visually, across great distances. The feather is symbolic of the bird and our own creative endeavors: the wisp of an idea, words written with a feather quill, the journey from conception to execution.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Debossed printer and artists chop marks.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        In total, we produced 650 limited-edition prints. At times, the projects creation itself tested the Be Good to Each Other philosophy, begetting concept disagreements, aesthetic critiques, and printer errors. But, in the end, this years gifts serves as a beautiful reminder to work together and, above all, be good to each other.
       </p>
