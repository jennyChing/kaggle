        <h1 class="title" id="page-title">
         How To Improve Eyesight With The Right Eye Vitamins
        </h1>
        <div class="block block-system block-main block-system-main odd block-without-title" id="block-system-main">
         <div class="block-inner clearfix">
          <div class="content clearfix">
           <article about="/blog/how-improve-eyesight-right-eye-vitamins" class="node node-basic-blog node-published node-not-promoted node-not-sticky author-webby5 odd clearfix" id="node-basic-blog-669" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">

             <div class="sharethis-buttons">
              <div class="sharethis-wrapper">

             <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
              <div class="field-items">
               <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                <p>
                 By: Dr. Michael E. Rosenbaum, MD
                </p>
                <p>
                 Would you like to improve your eyesight? Let me tell you Velvas story . . . and how the right eye vitamins helped her keep her sharp vision intact.
                </p>
                <p>
                 See, Velvas eye doctor told her to get ready for deteriorating eyesight. He told her it was a natural part of aging.
                </p>
                <p>
                 But she didnt buy this.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Instead she focused on eating well and taking care of her health. And now shes knitting, crocheting and embroidering  and she still doesnt need glasses at age 81.
                </p>
                <p>
                 In fact, her vision is so keen, shes made a total of 76 afghan blankets  and counting.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Along with a healthy lifestyle, Velva thanks eating lots of green chlorella for helping her eyes
                 </strong>
                 . And research is bearing her out.
                </p>
                <h2>
                 How Chlorellas Carotenoids Can Help Your Eyes
                </h2>
                <p>
                 <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/media/img_blog_20150303_02.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 200px; border-width: 0px; border-style: solid; margin: 0px 0px 10px 10px; float: right;"/>
                 In 2008, a study published in the
                 <em>
                  American Journal of Ophthalmology
                 </em>
                 showed that simply by eating
                 <a href="https://www.sunchlorellausa.com/blog/why-super-greens-are-so-good-you">
                  nutritious greens
                 </a>
                 like kale and collards
                 <em>
                  once per month
                 </em>
                 , women decreased their chance of having eye problems later in life by 69%.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <em>
                  Just once a month!
                 </em>
                </p>
                <p>
                 If just some greens once a month can make that kind of a difference, imagine what it can do for your eyes to have a
                 <em>
                  super
                 </em>
                 green food
                 <em>
                  every day
                 </em>
                 like
                 <a href="https://www.sunchlorellausa.com/shop/sun-chlorella-tablets">
                  chlorella
                 </a>
                 .
                </p>
                <p>
                 See, like collards and greens,
                 <strong>
                  chlorella is packed with a rich supply of carotenoids  special plant pigments that feed your eyes and nerves.
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  These carotenoids include
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Lutein.
                  </strong>
                  Lutein is so important to your health that doctors can assess how good your vision is simply by measuring the concentration of the pigment in your retina.Now a normal serving of chlorella wont give you the full amount of lutein recommended in studies. But with 3 mg of this special pigment per 15 tablets, it gives you a good start.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Beta-carotene
                  </strong>
                  , the natural precursor for
                  <a href="https://www.sunchlorellausa.com/blog/four-reasons-eat-foods-vitamin">
                   vitamin A
                  </a>
                  . Vitamin A became recognized as a vitamin  by definition, a nutrient essential for life  thanks to its essential role in eye health.When it comes to the natural form of Vitamin A, chlorella gives you a solid 18% of the RDA for this vitamin.
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <p>
                 But just because chlorella has a good supply of the carotenoids your eyes need to work well, doesnt mean your eye can use them. Heres why . . .
                </p>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  First, carotenoids compete for absorption. When you have too much of beta-carotene, for example, your body wont take in as much lutein. However, chlorellas carotenoids are perfectly balanced as in nature so your body will absorb them all in optimum amounts.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Secondly, with chlorella you know youre getting the real deal. Luteins molecular structure can vary depending on its source and if its synthesized or natural. Chlorellas form of lutein is the same natural form used in studies on eye health.
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <p>
                 But thats not all chlorella offers your eyes . . .
                </p>
                <h2>
                 Omega-3 Fatty Acids May Keep Your Eyes Sharp
                </h2>
                <p>
                 <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/media/img_blog_20150303_03_0.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 200px; border-width: 0px; border-style: solid; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; float: left;"/>
                 According to research published in the
                 <em>
                  American Journal of Clinical Nutrition
                 </em>
                 , your body doesnt do much with lutein unless you take it with some
                 <a href="https://www.sunchlorellausa.com/blog/big-change-heart-healthy-diet-recommendations">
                  healthy fats
                 </a>
                 . When you take chlorella, along with lutein, you get healthy omega-3 and omega-6 fats so your body can absorb all of luteins eye-saving nutrition.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Omega-3 fatty acids
                 </strong>
                 may play a central role in eye health.While its found throughout your body, the highest concentrations are in your retina, according to the American Optometric Association. Because of this, eye health experts are eager to see how much of a difference it could make in eye health. In 2013, the National Eye Institute initiated a clinical trial combining omega-3 fatty acids with eye health carotenoids. Preliminary studies suggest the results may be promising.[1]
                </p>
                <p>
                 Bear in mind, chlorella will not give you enough omega-3 fatty acids to meet eye health recommendations. One serving of chlorella has only 52 mg. The National Eye Institute trial is using doses of 1000 mg, which is on the low end for therapeutic recommendations.[2]
                </p>
                <p>
                 In addition to using nutrition, you can do a couple other things to help improve your eyesight . . .
                </p>
                <h2>
                 Exercise For Eye Health
                </h2>
                <p>
                 <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/media/img_blog_20150303_04.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 200px; border-width: 0px; border-style: solid; margin: 0px 0px 10px 10px; float: right;"/>
                 Preliminary research has shown that exercise can increase the delivery of a growth hormone to the retina of the eye. This growth hormone seems to help the eye defend itself against damage from UV rays. It also seems to help the retina respond better to light.[3]
                </p>
                <h2>
                 Give Your Eyes A Break
                </h2>
                <p>
                 We spend more and more of our time looking at screens. All this screen time damages our eyes in a number of ways. We blink less, refreshing our eyes less. Our eyes strain to focus closeup (just like with reading a book). And the light can also be tough on the eyes. To combat the eye strain from computers, experts recommend you follow the 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, take 20 seconds and look at something 20 feet away.
                </p>
                <h2>
                 Improve Your Eyesight With These Natural, Healthy Techniques
                </h2>
                <p>
                 Your eyes are your windows to the world. You depend on them to assess your environment, interact with people and get around town. If they stop performing well for you, life gets a lot more difficult. But with them working well, you can enjoy life that much more.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The key to healthy eyes is to take good care of them. Eat right, exercise, get outdoors and rest them. Give your eyes the attention they deserve so they can keep serving you well. Like Velva, you may find you can defy the naysayers. With healthy eyes, nothing will stop you from following your vision and doing what you love to do well into your senior years.
                </p>
