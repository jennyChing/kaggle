       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         The ULTIMATE Bodyweight Guide to Getting Lean and Ripped
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://ashotofadrenaline.net/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Todd Kuslikis
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         on
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-27T13:11:53+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          March 27, 2015
         </time>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://ashotofadrenaline.net/the-ultimate-bodyweight-guide-to-getting-lean-and-ripped/#comments">
           8 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <img alt="getting ripped" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14954" height="886" src="http://1tljmj43yo4m291xrgtfdtsq.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/3893650182_fd48214c33_b.jpg" width="1024"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          *This is a guest post by my good friend
          <a href="https://www.thescienceofgettingripped.com/" target="_blank">
           Raza Imam
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         For lots of people, getting lean and ripped seems like an unachievable goal.
        </p>
        <p>
         But it isnt. Not by a long shot.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im a busy father of two young boys. I have a 2 hour daily commute. I have a very demanding IT job at a hospital. So I can relate to the average busy guy.
        </p>
        <p>
         What Im going to do today is show you a very deliberate formula and list of inclusions that I follow to help you get the body that you want, in as little time as possible.
        </p>
        <p>
         Of course, everyone is different, and your results will vary, but I can guarantee that if you start to incorporate these tips into your daily routine, youll be well on your way to getting ripped; without expensive gym memberships, scammy supplements, or gimmicky fitness gadgets.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sound good? Lets get started.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Why Only Use Bodyweight Exercises?
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="flip" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14956" height="296" src="http://1tljmj43yo4m291xrgtfdtsq.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/2654318868_f16d9065f0.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Okay, so let me do a bit of explaining.
        </p>
        <p>
         Imnotsaying that using bodyweight exercises is the only way to get ripped. So its not like this guide is the ONLY way to get ripped.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its not.
        </p>
        <p>
         In fact, I like to lift weights and have been doing so for quite a while. But the cold, hard truth is that strength coaches like
         <a href="https://strengthcamponline.com/" target="_blank">
          Elliott Hulse
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://startingstrength.com/" target="_blank">
          Mark Rippetoe
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://jasonferruggia.com/" target="_blank">
          Jason Ferruggia
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.jimwendler.com/" target="_blank">
          Jim Wendler
         </a>
         , (not to mention Todd Kuslikis) understand the value of
         <a href="http://ashotofadrenaline.net/10-ways-muscle-bodyweight-exercises/" target="_blank" title="10 Irrefutable Ways to Build Muscle Using Only Bodyweight Exercises">
          bodyweight exercises
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         I have been interested in fitness ever since I was a teenager, and like Todd, I was actively involved in martial arts. So I always appreciated the value of bodyweight exercises for overall fitness and strength and getting lean and ripped. Ive tried a lot of different things, but I keep coming back to the importance of bodyweight training.
        </p>
        <p>
         Today all I am doing is sharing the tactics, strategies, and tricks that I have seen make a big difference, especially for busy guys that dont have time to slave away in a gym.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im hoping that you might read something new, apply it to your own life and see a big difference in your own results.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 1: Visualize Your Goal
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="visualize" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14958" height="332" src="http://1tljmj43yo4m291xrgtfdtsq.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/3891484456_ab96d01498.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets get started by discussing the importance of goals. Setting realistic goals is crucial to accomplishing anything worthwhile in life whether you want to save for your retirement, get a Masters degree, educational goal, or learn a new language.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fitness is no different.
        </p>
        <p>
         Part of having a goal is to have a plan to achieve that goal. That means coming up with exact, specific steps you can need to take to get the body you want.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres a quick goal that I wrote and put in my phone. I have it popup everyday at 6:30am. I read it to myself and visualize the steps I need to take to hit my goal. You can create something like this for yourself:
        </p>
        <p>
         I have the body I want because I workout ___ times per week. I do _____ each day. I do _____ minutes of cardio/HIIT on _____ days.
         <br/>
         I eat _______ for lunch and drink ______g of protein per day and _____ g fat and carbs per day.
        </p>
        <p>
         It sounds simple, but defining an end goal, and the exact steps you need to take to achieve your goal will set you on the right track.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 2: Plan Out Your Diet
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="food" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14959" height="357" src="http://1tljmj43yo4m291xrgtfdtsq.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/6930467624_10f56759ab.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Weve all heard the phrase abs are made in the kitchen, not the gym so if you want to get lean and muscular, youre going to have to focus on what you eat.
        </p>
        <p>
         In fact, its not a stretch to say that diet accounts for 80% of your results. But dont worry, Im going to show you a simple template for eating that not only tastes good, but helps you get lean and muscular.
         <br/>
         Between kids birthday parties, family gatherings, neighborhood barbeques, andbusiness lunches, how in the world is the average guy supposed to eat right?
        </p>
        <p>
         The key is to plan what you eat. My wife is a dietitian, and one of the first things she tells new clients to do is to keep a journal of everything they eat for a few days. Every packet of sugar in their coffee, every handful of healthy trail mix, every serving of salad dressing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Like they say you cant manage what you dont measure, so simply seeing what you eat is very eye-opening to a lot of people.
        </p>
        <p>
         But were going to do the same process in REVERSE.
        </p>
        <p>
         So instead of looking at what youve already eaten, youre going to make a plan of what youre going to eat. This puts you in control of the process. See how effective this can be?
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres what you need to do:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Make a List of Your Favorite Foods
         </strong>
         : For me its Greek yogurt w/ chocolate covered almonds, Steak w/ rice and asparagus, Salmon w/spinach and capers, Grilled chicken w/sweet potatoes and broccoli, Buffalo chicken salad, and French toast.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Calculate Your Daily Caloric Needs
         </strong>
         : Then calculate how many calories your body needs on a daily basis based on your age, gender, and current activity levels.If you want to gain muscle, youll eat 10%above your caloric needs, if you want to burn fat, youll eat 10%-20% below your daily caloric needs. Then just cook your favorite foods, making sure that you calculate the right amount of calories.
        </p>
        <p>
         Just make sure you cook your meals on your day off and youre good to go.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres an example of how I eat during a typical work day:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          9:00am black coffee w/ Stevia and 24 ounces of ice water
         </li>
         <li>
          10:00am black coffee w/ Stevia and 24 ounces of ice water
         </li>
         <li>
          11:00am black coffee w/ Stevia and 24 ounces of ice water
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Workout during lunch (take 10 grams of branch chain amino acids)
         <br/>
         1:00pm 50 grams of protein powder with 1 cup almond milk (or water), 2 cups of Greek yogurt with chocolate almonds and frozen blueberries, Turkey sandwich
         <br/>
         6:00pm Whatever my wife makes for dinner
        </p>
        <p>
         This way I can hit my protein numbers (roughly 160 grams per day) and have a big, satisfying dinner. Drinking coffee in the morning helps curb my hunger and keeps me sharp and alert at work (when I have breakfast Im starving by the time I get to work!)
        </p>
        <p>
         You can make your own diet plan from any of your favorite foods: Lean sources likebeef,turkey,chicken,bison,salmon,tuna,greek yogurt,andeggsshould be your main sources of muscle-building protein.
        </p>
        <p>
         Carbs such assweet potatoes,white potatoes,rice,quinoa,sprouted bread,fruits, andvegetables(kale, spinach, broccoli, cauliflower, green beans, green peppers, carrots, squash, eggplant, etc.) can give you the vital nutrients and energy you need.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fats likealmonds,almond butter(and other nut butters),avocado,coconut oil,fish oil, andolive oilgive you the necessary essential fatty acids you need to produce testosterone
        </p>
        <p>
         The get ripped quick diet is really as simple as that. This is the #1 secret to to burn fat and start building muscle.No extreme dieting. No insane cravings. No long hours in the gym. Youre eating all of your favorite foods AND burning fat.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 3: Do Simple Bodyweight Workouts
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://1tljmj43yo4m291xrgtfdtsq.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/3050897429_17b1c88282.jpg">
          <img alt="pull up" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14960" height="332" src="http://1tljmj43yo4m291xrgtfdtsq.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/3050897429_17b1c88282.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         With a solid goal and a well-planned out meal plan (that youll actually like), youre on track to getting ripped. Now we get to the workouts.
        </p>
        <p>
         I like to lift weights these days, but Im in love with bodyweight exercises. Pushups, pullups, burpees, jump squats, mountain climbers, and my all time favorite; dips. I cant get enough of them.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know Todd has a TON of great workouts, so I wanted to share the one that I personally do. If youre just getting started, dont worry because were going to take it slow in the beginning. But over time well add more repetitions. Youll notice yourself getting stronger and leaner in the process.
        </p>
        <p>
         Each week, you want to add 3-5 more reps and 1 more set. There are no hard and fast rules here. The point is to do a little bit more each week than you did the previous week.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Heres a sample week:
        </h4>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Monday: 10 pushups followed by 10 burpees followed by 5 pullups. Rest one minute and repeat 2 more times (the following week you can do 15 pushups, 15 burpees, and 6 pullups or the you can do 4 total rounds with the same number of repetitions, and so forth each week)
         </li>
         <li>
          Tuesday: 5 burpees followed by 5 split squats followed by 5 mountain climbers, followed by 5 high knees. Rest one minute and repeat 2 more times (again, the next week you can add more repetitions or more
         </li>
         <li>
          Wednesday: 10 pushups followed by 10 burpees followed by 5 pullups. Rest one minute and repeat 2 more times (the following week you can do 15 pushups, 15 burpees, and 6 pullups or the you can do 4 total rounds with the same number of repetitions, and so forth each week)
         </li>
         <li>
          Thursday: 5 burpees followed by 5 split squats followed by 5 mountain climbers, followed by 5 high knees. Rest one minute and repeat 2 more times (again, the next week you can add more repetitions or more
         </li>
         <li>
          Friday: 10 pushups followed by 10 burpees followed by 5 pullups. Rest one minute and repeat 2 more times (the following week you can do 15 pushups, 15 burpees, and 6 pullups or the you can do 4 total rounds with the same number of repetitions, and so forth each week)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         As you get stronger, add more reps, or even another exercise like dips, or wall pushups, or pistol squats on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday or to cut your rest time from 1 minute to 30 seconds. The key is to progressively make the workouts harder as you get stronger.
        </p>
        <p>
         This workout is so easy that it can be done in less than 25 minutes. If you have kids (like me), you can do this with them at the park, or at home. Youre going to have to figure out how to keep them from jumping on your back when doing pushups though! (sorry, no suggestions for you there)
        </p>
        <p>
         The benefit of doing this workout is that you naturally combine resistance training with high intensity cardio; allowing you to build muscle and burn fat.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Wrapping It All Up
        </h2>
        <p>
         Whew, weve covered a lot. But its literally everything youd need to know to get lean and ripped using body weight exercises. To sum it all up, you have to focus on the 3 key areas:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Concrete Goals with Action Steps
         </li>
         <li>
          Healthy Foods You Like to Eat
         </li>
         <li>
          Simple Workouts You Can Actually Do
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         I know it sounds simple, but thats because it is. All you need to do is put the plan into action.
        </p>
