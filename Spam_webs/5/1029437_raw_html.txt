              <p>
               We all remember the disappointment that was evident on the Doctors face when he went to the coordinates given to him by Missy in hopes of finding Gallifrey at the end of Death in Heaven. While he had to assume that Missy was lying, since 99% of anything the Master/Missy says is for his/her own gain, it still had to hurt.
              </p>
              <p>
               However, there remains the possibility that Missy was actually telling the truth about the whereabouts of Gallifrey. After all, right after seeing that Gallifrey was not there, who should the Doctor see but Santa Claus? When he asked the Doctor what he wanted for Christmas, it led to a realm of possibilities that could have proven to be quite interesting.
              </p>
              <p>
               The only problem with that is that Santa Claus was not really there. As shown in Last Christmas, his presence was the result of a deeply ingrained dream. In fact, the Doctor may not have even gone to those coordinates, as he was shown pulling the dream crab off of his face in what appeared to be the same cavern in whichClara thought that she threw the keys to the TARDIS into the lava. False memories abound.
              </p>
              <div class="related-category">
               <strong>
                More From
                <span>
                 Opinion

                <li>

                 </a>
                </li>
                <li>
                 <a href="http://doctorwhowatch.com/2015/07/09/doctor-who-could-use-a-male-companion/?utm_source=FanSided&amp;utm_medium=Network&amp;utm_campaign=RelatedCategory">
                  7/9 - Doctor Who Could Use a Male Companion in Series 10
                 </a>
                </li>
               </ul>

              <p>
               Since the Doctor woke up in that cavern, did he even go to those coordinates given by Missy? Is it possible that Gallifrey is really there, and not in some pocket dimension or hidden away in a parallel universe? In fact, as that cavern he awoke in appears to be the lava cave from Dark Water, could it be that the events of the two part finale, including Claras betrayal, never actually happened?
              </p>
              <p>
               If that is the case, the realm of possibilities are endless. While Danny Pink would still be dead, as the Doctor and Clara went to the caves after he was killed, both Missy and Osgood could still be alive. The Cyberman army, and both Danny and the Brigadier as Cybermen, may have never happened. In fact, the Nethersphere could still be functioning, ready to serve as a setting for yet another episode or two.
              </p>
              <p>
               Typically, having the results of an episode and relegating it to a dream status is just a way to have a do-over, to pretend that some truly awful season, movie or reboot never happened. In this case, having the finale be nothing more than a dream would instead be an interesting way to keep the Doctors quest for Gallifrey alive, and to bring back a truly popular character and potential future companion in
               <a href="http://doctorwhowatch.com/2014/11/18/popular-consensus-bring-back-osgood/" target="_blank">
                Osgood
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               Gallifrey may still be out there. Maybe, just maybe, in those dreams, if they were dreams, Missy gave the Doctor the right coordinates. How would Dream-Missyknow that Gallifrey would still be there, you ask? Because Moffat logic. Hopefully, he comes up with a good explanation and the Doctor takes another look.
              </p>
