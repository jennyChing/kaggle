     <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->

     </ul>
     <p>
      Hundreds of casual Australian Tax Office workers in Albury will lose their jobs as the site winds down its paper processing, according to the public sector union.
     </p>
     <p>
      The ATO announced it would begin outsourcing paper processing from September this year, but no permanent jobs would be lost or redundancies offered.
     </p>
     <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
      <img alt="Hundreds of casual jobs will go from the Albury tax office." src="/content/dam/images/1/m/v/q/w/e/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.1mvqt6.png/1430270073232.jpg" title=""/>
      <p>
       Hundreds of casual jobs will go from the Albury tax office.
      </p>
     </div>
     <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
     <p>
      The Community and Public Sector Union national president Alistair Waters said the move had failed to consider the pool of 600 casual staff who work out of the Albury office.
     </p>
     <div class="ad adAlignRight" id="adspot-300x250-pos3" style="display:none;">
      <small>
       Advertisement
      </small>
      <script>
       titan.requestAd.push("adspot-300x250-pos3");
      </script>
     </div>
     <p>
      According to one insider, up to 50 of those were employed in the past two months.
     </p>
     <p>
      The number employed varies depending on the work available but about 200 are now working in paper processing.
     </p>
     <p>
      "Some casual staff have been working for the ATO for over a decade and they deserve to be treated with respect," Mr Waters said.
     </p>
     <p>
      "This is a terrible blow for them and their families and the decision will have a wider impact on the Albury community."
     </p>
     <p>
      The union is now seeking a meeting with the ATO to get more information on the cuts and options for staff.
     </p>
     <p>
      The ATO has said the 63 permanent staff members affected would be moved into alternative roles within the agency, while casual staff would remain in the casual "pool" and be considered for temporary positions as they arose.
     </p>
     <p>
      ATO assistant commissioner Kath Anderson said Albury's 600 casual staff worked in a variety of areas including "(paper) capture processing, debt collection, exceptions processing and finance activities".
     </p>
     <p>
      Ms Anderson, who is based in Albury, said the decision to outsource reflected the "significant decrease in the number of paper interactions".
     </p>
     <p>
      Last year there was a 30per cent decrease in the number of hard copy tax lodgements.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ms Anderson said this downward trend was expected to continue as the ATO's online options improve.
     </p>
     <p>
      "This is a strategic business decision," she said.
     </p>
     <p>
      "We remain committed to Albury, as demonstrated by the move to new purpose-built offices in December 2012."
     </p>
     <p>
      But Mr Waters said cutting ATO staff "doesn't make sense at a time when collecting the revenue that we need to fund essential services is so crucial".
     </p>
     <p>
      Farrer MP Sussan Ley said the ATO "has to adapt to the changes this digital age is throwing up".
     </p>
     <p>
      "Any local reduction in staff is disappointing however management in Albury is doing an outstanding job at minimising the impact," she said.
     </p>
