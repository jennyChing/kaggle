            <p data-textannotation-id="9d5099db9d9596048e6c89bcde9e1144">

            <p data-textannotation-id="aca800f565d75ee26c01a282295615b2">
             Cleaning your air conditioning unit prolongs its lifespan, saving you the trouble of early repairs and replacements.
             <a href="http://homeguides.sfgate.com/clean-outdoor-central-air-unit-20242.html" target="_blank">
              SFGate recommends cleaning your home AC unit twice a year
             </a>
             : once in spring and again at the end of summer. A clean AC unit also results in more efficient performance, saving you $70-$100 for extra service inspections and repairs for a poorly performing or malfunctioning system.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="c63a590c7011db0a40697d6db25fff66">
             All you have to do to is turn off the power, remove large debris, and spray the unit down with a hose. Make sure you wear protective goggles. You can see exactly how it's done in the video above from
             <a href="http://www.entergy.com/" target="_blank">
              Entergy
             </a>
             . If you clean your unit and it still seems to be performing poorly, you may have been a little late with cleaning.
             <a href="http://lifehacker.com/5315097/give-your-air-conditioner-a-check-up">
              Inspect it yourself
             </a>
             to see if there's something else you can fix, or otherwise call a professional for help.
            </p>
            <h2 data-textannotation-id="2b40bead3940e9b4077736dbda16645b">
             Your Refrigerator's Condenser Cords
            </h2>
            <p data-textannotation-id="47f17ac0590a5dd0e492fccd0df8f281">
             <span class="clear-both flex-video widescreen">
              <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" class="youtube" data-chomp-id="RJ7ykYOHmME" frameborder="0" height="360" id="youtube-RJ7ykYOHmME" mozallowfullscreen="mozallowfullscreen" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/RJ7ykYOHmME?wmode=transparent&amp;rel=0&amp;autohide=1&amp;enablejsapi=1&amp;showinfo=0" webkitallowfullscreen="webkitAllowFullScreen" width="640">
              </iframe>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="0feaf261b6fe81eeb5a3f5ed9021d6ed">
             According to Family Handyman,
             <a href="http://www.familyhandyman.com/appliance-repair/refrigerator-repair/how-to-avoid-refrigerator-repairs/view-all" target="_blank">
              clogged condenser cords cause 70 percent of service calls
             </a>
             for refrigerators. Your refrigerator won't cool the food quite as well, and will eventually break down if these are left unmanaged. This can run you $70 per hour plus parts for a repair, when you could alternatively spend about five to ten minutes cleaning the cords. Clean them twice a year, or more if you have pets.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="41ed68d968495aff1f4c67e6a6503712">
             Grab a
             <a data-amazonasin="B0046OEAHK" data-amazonsubtag="[p|1666421408[a|B0046OEAHK[au|1530118464" data-amazontag="lifehackeramzn-20" href="http://www.amazon.com/Flexible-Dryer-Vent-Refrigerator-Brush/dp/B0046OEAHK?tag=lifehackeramzn-20&amp;ascsubtag=[t|link[p|1666421408[a|B0046OEAHK[au|1530118464" onclick="window.ga('send', 'event', 'Commerce', 'lifehacker - The Dirty Spots that Can Damage Your Home (and How to Clean Them)', 'B0046OEAHK');" target="_blank">
              coil cleaning brush
             </a>
             and unplug the refrigerator. Remove the grill and then brush the coils to loosen and remove the debris, dirt and dust. Afterward use a vacuum hose to suck all the gunk out. The video above from YouTube user
             <a href="https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC-qk91J4pvGetPvjU7vrT6A" target="_blank">
              uxwbill
             </a>
             shows you exactly how it's done.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="9945d66047c66baa7647684d9a320b9d">
             Note: while you're at it, it's a good idea to unplug and pull your other appliances out from the walls for a good cleaning as well. At minimum, this will improve the air quality in your home and save you from mold.
            </p>
            <h2 data-textannotation-id="1d4b02c43393668784b028b9088cb34d">

              <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" class="youtube" data-chomp-id="2_9WJSzV6fw" frameborder="0" height="360" id="youtube-2_9WJSzV6fw" mozallowfullscreen="mozallowfullscreen" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/2_9WJSzV6fw?wmode=transparent&amp;rel=0&amp;autohide=1&amp;enablejsapi=1&amp;showinfo=0" webkitallowfullscreen="webkitAllowFullScreen" width="640">

            <h2 data-textannotation-id="b69c0946f593d49cc93ef252fe74997b">
             Your Furnace Filter
            </h2>
            <p data-textannotation-id="4f511af85cf4bab65a6c7f07249c65c0">
             <span class="clear-both flex-video widescreen">
              <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" class="youtube" data-chomp-id="-lO4xAsTyqM" frameborder="0" height="360" id="youtube--lO4xAsTyqM" mozallowfullscreen="mozallowfullscreen" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/-lO4xAsTyqM?wmode=transparent&amp;rel=0&amp;autohide=1&amp;enablejsapi=1&amp;showinfo=0" webkitallowfullscreen="webkitAllowFullScreen" width="640">
              </iframe>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="d0b4126c4e0785f99a96e7687bf3e126">
             A dirty furnace filter will restrict airflow and cause the burners to shut down from overheating. A repair will get the burners working again, but Clarke Contractors warns that
             <a href="http://www.clarkecontractors.com/2011/10/importance-of-changing-your-furnace-filter/" target="_blank">
              the burners will eventually shut down for good
             </a>
             . A clogged HVAC system or the worse, a fire, could be the end result as well. The U.S. Fire Administration reported 50,100 residential heating fires between 2008 and 2010, with furnace fires included in the statistic. It's the second leading cause to residential fires after cooking fires.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="cf67c4adcc7fbd4fb9b5ff3179d7b3e9">
             Bob Villa (of
             <em>
              This Old House
             </em>
             fame) recommends
             <a href="http://www.bobvila.com/articles/change-a-furnace-filter/" target="_blank">
              replacing a standard fiberglass or paper filter every one to two months
             </a>
             , while electrostatic or HEPA filters last twice as long. If you have pets, smoke indoors,
             <a href="http://lifehacker.com/5175243/make-your-home-dust-proof" x-inset="1">
              notice excessive dust bunnies
             </a>
             , or simply check your filter and notice dust buildup, you should change the filter more frequently.
            </p>
