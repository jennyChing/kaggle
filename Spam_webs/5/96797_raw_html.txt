       <p class="author">
        Posted by the Mass Office of Travel and Tourism
       </p>
       <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
       <p>
        Many cities across the country have their own unique holiday celebrations, but even after traveling far and wide, youd be hard-pressed to find one like Bright Nights at Forest Park in Springfield.
       </p>
       <p>
        This year marks the 20
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        anniversary of the annual Western Massachusetts lights extravaganza, which is the biggest of its kind in New England and showcases stunning illuminated displays along two-mile trail.
       </p>
       <p>
        For families, couples and anyone who just enjoys a good show,
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/business/637/" target="_blank">
         Bright Nights at Forest Park
        </a>
        is a great time.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres what visitors will find on a trip to the enchanted grounds.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Lit-trees.jpg">
         <img alt="Lit trees" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6897" height="2048" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Lit-trees.jpg" width="1371"/>
        </a>
        <i>
        </i>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <i>
         Photo: Illuminated trees at Bright Nights
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        This year, the opening night for Bright Lights will be Wednesday, November 26. Theres a special price for the inaugural evening, with tickets running just $6. From there on out, attendance will cost $18 Monday through Thursday, when the park is open from 5 p.m. to 9 p.m.
       </p>
       <p>
        On Friday, Saturday, Sunday and holidays, tickets will cost $21, with the park staying open from 5 p.m. to 11 p.m. Bright Nights is set to last through January 4 of the New Year, although it will take two off-days on December 1 and 2.
       </p>
       <p>
        Inside the park, there will be a world to explore. Actually, several! In total, there are more than a
        <a href="http://www.brightnights.org/tour/index.html" target="_blank">
         dozen different light displays
        </a>
        , ranging from the flowery Poinsettia Fantasy to the charming Victorian Village.
        <a href="http://www.brightnights.org/tour/04seussland/index.html" target="_blank">
         Seussland
        </a>
        , a fitting tribute to one of Springfields most famous native sons, is simply not be missed.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Seussland.jpg">
         <img alt="Seussland" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6898" height="474" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Seussland.jpg" width="717"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <i>
         Photo: Seussland at Bright Nights
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        In addition to the displays, there are quite a few activities at Bright Nights. Friday and weekend nights offer
        <a href="http://www.brightnights.org/activities/WagonRides.html" target="_blank">
         wagon and carriage rides
        </a>
        , departing every half hour.
        <a href="http://www.brightnights.org/activities/SantasCottage.html" target="_blank">
         Santa
        </a>
        will also be on the scene, keeping cozy in his cottage, where guests can make their holiday wishes and grab a souvenir or two. To see the full catalog of activities, click
        <a href="http://www.brightnights.org/activities/index.html" target="_blank">
         here
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Santa.jpg">
         <img alt="Santa" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6899" height="1443" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Santa.jpg" width="2048"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <i>
         Photo: Santa in good spirits outside of his cottage
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        In terms of visitation, Bright Nights takes place very close to downtown Springfield, so its
        <a href="http://www.brightnights.org/Directions/indexDirections.html" target="_blank">
         quite accessible
        </a>
        . Thanks to its popularity, Bright Nights is also a popular for
        <a href="http://www.brightnights.org/bustours/index.html" target="_blank">
         bus tours
        </a>
        , which present another good way to visit.
       </p>
       <p>
        While Bright Nights is an excellent place to spend a few evenings, its one of
        <a href="http://www.brightnights.org/areaInfo.html" target="_blank">
         many events
        </a>
        going on in the Greater Springfield area around the holidays. If you decide to stay in town for a whole weekend or longer,
        <a href="http://www.naomisinn.net/" target="_blank">
         Naomis Inn
        </a>
        is well-suited for overnights, and was recently one of the best B&amp;Bs in Western Mass. You can also peruse more lodging choices
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/stay/by-region/?stype=1&amp;cid=11&amp;tid=0&amp;gid=0&amp;sid=0&amp;rid=0&amp;attr1=0&amp;attr2=0&amp;so=0&amp;map=0&amp;pnum=1&amp;dnum=20&amp;mid=397" target="_blank">
         here
        </a>
        .
       </p>
