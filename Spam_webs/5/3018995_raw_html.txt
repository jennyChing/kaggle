         <p>
          During the last two decades, the concept of electronic commerce has changedthe way people buy and sell goods all over the world. It has had an invaluable impacton the worlds business practices. Consumers are no longer restricted todriving to a physical store in order to get access to products they want.
         </p>
         <p>
          Online retailers affect the
          <a href="http://urbantimes.co/2013/02/beyond-consumption/" title="traditional market">
           traditional market
          </a>
          by offering lower prices for everyday items, sparking healthy competitionbetween markets. Any budding entrepreneur ought to be familiar with the leadersin their industry and learn from their accomplishments. Here are four of themany need-to-know names in e-commerce.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Jeff Bezos
         </h2>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.achievement.org/autodoc/page/bez0bio-1" target="_blank" title="Jeff&nbsp;Bezos">
           JeffBezos
          </a>
          is the founder and CEO of
          <a href="http://urbantimes.co/2012/03/explore-the-amazon-with-google/" title="Amazon.com">
           Amazon.com
          </a>
          .He graduated from Princeton University in 1986 and worked on Wall Street beforestarting Amazon, which was first marketed as an online bookstore. Through his expertise,the online store became the worlds largest online retailer. It has become thego-to framework for budding sites as they build their enterprises. He startedAmazon in his garage in 1994, and it has since grown exponentially as itsstocks continue to skyrocket and sales continue to climb.
         </p>
         <p>
          Today Amazon is a leading provider of cloud computing services, among a hostof other features, including streaming and downloading music and movies, theKindle e-book reader and the Kindle Fire tablet, as well as nearly any otherretail item imaginable.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Pierre Omidyar
         </h2>
         <p>
          Internet entrepreneur
          <a href="http://www.omidyar.com/team/pierre-omidyar" target="_blank" title="Pierre Omidyar">
           Pierre Omidyar
          </a>
          is the founder and chairman of the onlineauction site, ebay.com. He earned his bachelors degree in computer sciencefrom Tufts University in Medford/Somerville, Mass. in 1988, and quickly began acareer in the computer industry. After working for Apple Computer subsidiaryClaris, he cofounded a company called Ink Development that was later renamedeShop.
         </p>
         <p>
          With a vision in mind of an online person-to-person auction service forcollectible items, Omidyar began writing the original code for eBay in 1995. Heat first named it Auction Web and hosted it on his website that he hadpreviously established to house information on the ebola virus.
         </p>
         <p>
          The site quickly evolved into a massive online auction portal, with around250,000 auctions hosted that first year alone. By its second year it hosted anastounding 800,000 auctions every single day, and it snowballed from there.Today eBay is a multi-billion dollar company, operating out of more than thirtycountries worldwide, and has become an undisputed household name.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Tej Kohli
         </h2>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.tejkohli.com/" target="_blank" title="Tej Kohli">
           Tej Kohli
          </a>
          is anemerging name in the e-commerce world. Hes a thriving entrepreneur who hasfounded and invested in several commercial and philanthropical organisationsaround the world. He founded Grafix Softech, which offers next-generationpayment-processing services, headquartered in Costa Rica, with offices invarious countries. Grafix Softech has become one of the most successful techcompanies in the country.
         </p>
         <p>
          His wide-ranging philanthropic efforts focus on enhancing lives by providing food and educational scholarships to those in poverty, and combating blindnessthrough corneal transplants, helping 1,000 people in India each year throughthe entirely self-funded
          <a href="http://www.tejkohlifoundation.com/" target="_blank" title="Tej Kohli Foundation">
           Tej Kohli Foundation
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h2>
          Jerry Yang
         </h2>
         <p>
          In the early days of the Internet revolution, Jerry Yang created Jerryand Daves Guide to the World Wide Web in 1994 with his buddy
          <a href="http://www.forbes.com/profile/david-filo/" target="_blank" title="David Filo">
           David Filo
          </a>
          .It was a website that served as a directory for other websites as a way to helppeople navigate around the giant web that is the internet. Sound familiar? Itought to; that website directory grew to be what we now know as Yahoo! Inc.
         </p>
         <p>
          Yahoo! Inc. is now a massive, multi-platformed, multi-faceted, multi-billiondollar media giant that caters to more than half a billion consumers eachmonth. Its ventures include a highly successful email platform, as well asinstant messaging and mobile blogging. Of the many sites and services owned byYahoo! Inc, some of the most successful are Flickr, FoxyTunes, and Tumblr,along with a host of Yahoo! branded services.
         </p>
         <p>
          Yang has since stepped away from Yahoo!, but remains a major player in theworld of e-commerce.
         </p>
         <p>
          There you have it. Four of the most influential players in the game of e-commerce.Each of these entrepreneurs saw an opportunity during the infancy phase of theInternet. They each applied their personal experience, education, and expertiseto create something that changed the way people use the Internet, and in manyways, the way we live our lives every single day. What other major e-commerceplayers came to mind as you read this article? Please feel free to name otherprominent individuals and discuss their importance inthe comments below.
