     <h3>
      Melty Chocolate Phone from Japan Looks Good Enough To Eat
     </h3>
     <p class="byline">
      December 1, 2009, 11:35 am
      <a class="co" href="http://www.eatmedaily.com/2009/12/melty-chocolate-phone-from-japan-looks-good-enough-to-eat/#comments" rel="nofollow">
       2 Comments
      </a>
     </p>
     <div class="tmb">
      <div class="tweetmeme_button" style="">
       <iframe frameborder="0" height="20" scrolling="no" src="http://api.tweetmeme.com/button.js?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.eatmedaily.com%2F2009%2F12%2Fmelty-chocolate-phone-from-japan-looks-good-enough-to-eat%2F&amp;source=eatmedaily&amp;style=compact&amp;service=bit.ly" width="90">

     <p>
      <img alt="japanese-chocolate-phone-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-36503" height="274" src="http://www.eatmedaily.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/japanese-chocolate-phone-1.jpg" title="japanese-chocolate-phone-1" width="540"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      The NTT Docomo Melty Chocolate phone is waterproof, has an eight-megapixel camera, digital TV tuner, etc, but it's the design of the phone, made to look like a melting bar of chocolate, that makes it impressive. Even the menus and default wallpaper on the phone are "chocolate-themed." It's a collaboration between Sharp and
      <a href="http://www.q-pot.jp/">
       Q-Pot
      </a>
      , the Tokyo-based accessory brand founded by the designer Wakamatsu Tadaaki that makes pastry-inspired jewelry and accessories. In addition, the phone has a
      <a href="http://shop.q-pot.jp/app.php?do=A05001PRDCATSAH&amp;code=028">
       whole series
      </a>
      of optional straps that look like cupcakes and macarons. His and hers! Japan-only, duh.
     </p>
