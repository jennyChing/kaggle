                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://moptopmaven.blogspot.com/2010/03/diy-frayed-fabric-rosette-neclace.html">
                      DIY Frayed Fabric Rosette Neclace
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      <span class="post-author vcard">
                       Penned by
                       <span class="fn">
                        Mop Top Maven
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <span class="post-timestamp">
                       at
                       <a class="timestamp-link" href="http://moptopmaven.blogspot.com/2010/03/diy-frayed-fabric-rosette-neclace.html" rel="bookmark" title="permanent link">
                        <abbr class="published" title="2010-03-02T08:51:00-08:00">
                         8:51 AM
                        </abbr>
                       </a>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S406ChjORHI/AAAAAAAABRs/ZREwE2nseUU/s1600-h/DSC_0367-1.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="476" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S406ChjORHI/AAAAAAAABRs/ZREwE2nseUU/s640/DSC_0367-1.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      I love rosette bib statement necklaces, however buying them can cost a pretty penny. This necklace can easily be made with scrap fabric for about 1/6th of the retail cost.
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      You can make this out of any fabric, however I recommend using cottons over silky fabrics, as cotton frays easily. Also keep in mind a color scheme when picking out your fabrics, using a print with a solid color is always visually appealing.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <b>
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <b>
                       You will need:
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      Scraps of fabric [45" in length]
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      Felt
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      Glue Fun
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      Extra Fabric [to cover felt]
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      Cord/Braid/Trim/Ribbon
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      Scissors
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      End Fasteners
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S407gXB0ysI/AAAAAAAABR0/fKAvTirc2to/s1600-h/rosette1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="212" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S407gXB0ysI/AAAAAAAABR0/fKAvTirc2to/s640/rosette1.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Above are a few different fabric options; Notice how I have a matching solid to go with my prints - Pick your fabric and "rip" pieces that are about an inch wide. Don't worry, it does not have to be perfect.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S408GtMo2CI/AAAAAAAABR8/eT2-ZKERlCo/s1600-h/rosette2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="432" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S408GtMo2CI/AAAAAAAABR8/eT2-ZKERlCo/s640/rosette2.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     After making your strips, fold the strip in half. Make a knot in the middle of the strip [you will have two free ends], then begin to twist the two ends together.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     After twisting the free ends, begin to wrap the twisted section around the knot, and hot glue the rosette as you go.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S4088YrxeGI/AAAAAAAABSM/bFbgvnqHHHI/s1600-h/rosette3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S4088YrxeGI/AAAAAAAABSM/bFbgvnqHHHI/s640/rosette3.jpg" width="578"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      Cut your pattern for your bib necklace out of felt -
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S408uPrfEjI/AAAAAAAABSE/C7s3HkRIRBw/s1600-h/rosette3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S408uPrfEjI/AAAAAAAABSE/C7s3HkRIRBw/s640/rosette3.jpg" width="500"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      Take your rosettes and begin to glue them onto the felt, use smaller sizes of rosette's to help fill in gaps; don't worry about the white areas around the edges of the necklace, you will cut that off when you are done.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S41Amr7uT5I/AAAAAAAABSk/DahZ7P8mT0E/s1600-h/rosette5.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S41Amr7uT5I/AAAAAAAABSk/DahZ7P8mT0E/s640/rosette5.jpg" width="490"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Now trim away all of the excess felt around the edges of the necklace. At this point, hot glue your cord across the back of the necklace. - If you would like to make the back of the necklace a little neater, hot glue some scrap fabric on top of the back, then trim away the edges.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Add your end fasteners, and you are good to go!
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S41B3l5P_II/AAAAAAAABSs/emOT8jG_1DM/s1600-h/DSC_0368.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="428" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S41B3l5P_II/AAAAAAAABSs/emOT8jG_1DM/s640/DSC_0368.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S41B-VHWzFI/AAAAAAAABS0/ohyZARlF5bQ/s1600-h/DSC_0372.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="420" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_XVP0MrP2pDI/S41B-VHWzFI/AAAAAAAABS0/ohyZARlF5bQ/s640/DSC_0372.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
