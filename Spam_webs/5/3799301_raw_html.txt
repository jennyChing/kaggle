              <article id="article-body" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article" section="articleBody">
               <meta content="http://cnet2.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2013/10/21/3ea22d23-4de3-11e3-945a-14feb5ca9861/thumbnail/770x433/331bc8cdc8c9ef5aa1e01d1e66e777be/Micro_Surface_2_35827900-3721.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl"/>
               <meta content="Tablets, Tech Industry, Cameras" itemprop="keywords"/>
               <meta content="http://www.cnet.com/news/how-the-top-ipad-air-alternatives-compare-with-apples-latest-tablet/" itemprop="url"/>
               <div class="article-main-body row" data-component="lazyloadImages" data-use-autolinker="true" itemprop="articleBody">
                <p>
                 Tim Stevens'
                 <product id="35781421">
                  <a data-is-autolink="true" href="/products/apple-ipad-air/">
                   iPad Air
                  </a>
                  review
                 </product>
                 is up and -- big surprise! -- it's more iPad. An evolution as opposed to a true revolution in
                 <a data-is-autolink="true" href="/topics/tablets/">
                  tablets
                 </a>
                 . As such, it's entering a much more competitive market than any iPad before it. There are quite a few viable -- and most times cheaper -- alternatives to the iPad Air. And more are still coming.
                </p>
                <p>
                 That said, Apple still kills it when it comes to app availability and quality, and the iPad Air epitomizes high-end tech. Also, the iOS interface -- although not nearly as customizable as some of its competitors -- is fast, simple, and often problem-free.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Still, if you aren't dead set on an iPad purchase, here are a few large-size alternatives, with their advantages and disadvantages compared with the iPad Air in tow.
                </p>
                <hr/>
                <figure class="image image-large pull-none" section="shortcodeImage">
                 <span class="imageContainer">
                  <img alt="" class="" itemprop="image" src="http://cnet3.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2013/10/21/108b45a5-6de7-11e3-913e-14feb5ca9861/resize/620x/e469e34a29184a8cb3b3f22d5bf1d604/Micro_Surface_2_35827900-3721.jpg"/>
                 </span>
                 <figcaption>
                  <span class="caption">
                   The Surface 2 may win the value argument against the iPad Air.
                  </span>
                  <span class="credit">
                   Josh Miller/CNET
                  </span>
                 </figcaption>
                </figure>
                <p>
                 <b>
                  <span class="link" section="shortcodeLink">
                   <a href="/products/microsoft-surface-2-windows-rt/">
                    Microsoft Surface 2
                   </a>
                  </span>
                 </b>
                 (starts at $449 for 32GB)
                 <br/>
                 <b>
                  Advantages versus iPad Air:
                 </b>
                 Price starts at $50 less for twice the storage space; comfortable keyboard accessory with an included touch pad; free full
                 <a data-is-autolink="true" href="/products/microsoft-office-365-home-premium/">
                  Office 2013
                 </a>
                 included; deep photo-editing options.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <b>
                  Disadvantages versus iPad Air:
                 </b>
                 App support is dismal compared with iOS; gaming performance not quite as impressive.
                </p>
                <hr/>
                <figure class="image image-large pull-none" section="shortcodeImage">
                 <span class="imageContainer">
                  <img alt="" class="lazy " data-original="    http://cnet1.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2013/10/01/108cefdd-6de7-11e3-913e-14feb5ca9861/resize/620x/bb794e2b0377379b0b62dc1283a8e605/Amazon_Kindle_Fire_HDX_7_35828167-9771.jpg" src=""/>
                  <noscript>
                   <img alt="" class="" itemprop="image" src="http://cnet1.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2013/10/01/108cefdd-6de7-11e3-913e-14feb5ca9861/resize/620x/bb794e2b0377379b0b62dc1283a8e605/Amazon_Kindle_Fire_HDX_7_35828167-9771.jpg"/>
                  </noscript>
                 </span>
                 <figcaption>
                  <span class="caption">
                   Gaming performance, customer service, and a robust media ecosystem are the Fire HDX 8.9's strong suits. Pictured above is the Kindle Fire HDX 7.
                  </span>
                  <span class="credit">
                   Josh Miller/CNET
                  </span>
                 </figcaption>
                </figure>
                <p>
                 <b>
                  <span class="link" section="shortcodeLink">
                   <a href="/products/amazon-kindle-fire-hdx-8-9/">
                    Amazon Kindle Fire HDX 8.9
                   </a>
                  </span>
                 </b>
                 (starts at $379 for 16GB)
                 <br/>
                 <b>
                  Advantages versus iPad Air:
                 </b>
                 Available with 64GB for $20 less than the iPad Air, which has 16GB; unbeatable synergy with your books, movies, TV shows, and music; unparalleled personal customer service with video-chat-enabled Mayday feature; display runs at a higher resolution with more pixels per inch.
                </p>
                <div class="ad-inpage-video-top" data-ad="inpage-video-top" id="inpage-video-top-55a58969ca7d1">
                 <script>
                  window.console &amp;&amp; console.log &amp;&amp; console.log("ADS: queuing inpage-video-top-55a58969ca7d1 for display");
        var cbsiGptDivIds = cbsiGptDivIds || [];
        cbsiGptDivIds.push("inpage-video-top-55a58969ca7d1");
                 </script>
                </div>
                <p>
                 <b>
                  Disadvantages versus iPad Air:
                 </b>
                 The heavily curated app store results in plenty of missing apps; costs an extra $15 to remove ads; design doesn't hold a candle to the Air's high-end look and feel.
                </p>
                <hr/>
                <figure class="image image-large pull-none" section="shortcodeImage">
                 <span class="imageContainer">
                  <img alt="" class="lazy " data-original="    http://cnet3.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2013/09/11/108fb666-6de7-11e3-913e-14feb5ca9861/resize/620x/0c4243c4a1148a61d86496a923ad4456/Asus-Transformer-Pad-Infinity-TF701-3192.jpg" src=""/>
                  <noscript>
                   <img alt="" class="" itemprop="image" src="http://cnet3.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2013/09/11/108fb666-6de7-11e3-913e-14feb5ca9861/resize/620x/0c4243c4a1148a61d86496a923ad4456/Asus-Transformer-Pad-Infinity-TF701-3192.jpg"/>
                  </noscript>
                 </span>
                 <figcaption>
                  <span class="credit">
                   James Martin/CNET
                  </span>
                 </figcaption>
                </figure>
                <p>
                 <b>
                  <span class="link" section="shortcodeLink">
                   <a href="/products/asus-transformer-pad-tf701t/">
                    Asus Transformer Pad TF701
                   </a>
                  </span>
                 </b>
                 (price is unannounced)
                 <br/>
                 <b>
                  Advantages versus iPad Air:
                 </b>
                 Higher resolution and brighter screen; Android 4.3 is a much more open and customizable OS than iOS.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <b>
                  Disadvantages versus iPad Air:
                 </b>
                 Some top apps not available on Android; despite the TF701's power, iOS versions of games tend to look better
                </p>
                <hr/>
                <figure class="image image-large pull-none" section="shortcodeImage">
                 <span class="imageContainer">
                  <img alt="" class="lazy " data-original="    http://cnet2.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2013/09/30/1093d0ab-6de7-11e3-913e-14feb5ca9861/resize/620x/65641c4373309a20c720a3345d0ee01f/Samsung_Galaxy_Note_10.1_35827193-0667.jpg" src=""/>
                  <noscript>
                   <img alt="" class="" itemprop="image" src="http://cnet2.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2013/09/30/1093d0ab-6de7-11e3-913e-14feb5ca9861/resize/620x/65641c4373309a20c720a3345d0ee01f/Samsung_Galaxy_Note_10.1_35827193-0667.jpg"/>
                  </noscript>
                 </span>
                 <figcaption>
                  <span class="caption">
                   The Galaxy Note 10.1 displaying its biggest advantage over the iPad Air.
                  </span>
                  <span class="credit">
                   Josh Miller/CNET
                  </span>
                 </figcaption>
                </figure>
                <a href="http://reviews.cnet.com/tablets/samsung-galaxy-note-10/4505-3126_7-35827193.html">
                 <b>
                  Samsung Galaxy Note 10.1 2014 Edition
                 </b>
                </a>
                (starts at $549 for 16GB)
                <br/>
                <b>
                 Advantages versus iPad Air:
                </b>
                The S Pen stylus is a more natural way to interface with a touch screen; its 2,560x1,600 resolution is higher; plenty of fun and useful camera options; the TouchWiz UI is extremely customizable.
                <p>
                 <b>
                  Disadvantages versus iPad Air:
                 </b>
                 Starts at $549 with only 16GB of storage; a high learning curve to using the stylus.
                </p>
