             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              You gotta check out this list of most stunning beaches in the world, you might change your vacation plans after having a look at them. Some of the most breath taking islands and beaches known to human till today. Im sure after having a look at them, you would never want to miss any one of em. You may plan your trip for these locations and have wonderful vacations with your love ones.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <font size="3">
                Whitehaven Beach
               </font>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="Whitehaven Beach" border="0" height="364" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-j2NwxrdYoCI/TguKarvi7QI/AAAAAAAAH9g/vhNEZBFv6G4/Whitehaven%252520Beach_thumb%25255B4%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Whitehaven Beach" width="485"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Whitehaven Beach is located on Whitsunday Island, the largest island in the Whitsunday group. The beach is almost 6 km long and consists of a very fine white sand that is 98% pure silica. The sand is in fact so pure that is has been used in the construction of the lens for the Hubble telescope. At the northern end of Whitehaven Beach is Hill Inlet, a beautiful cove where the tide shifts the sand and water to create a beautiful fusion of colors.
             </p>
             <span class="fullpost">
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 Tapuaetai
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Tapuaetai" border="0" height="364" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-zkukITyHkgY/TguKbl82BDI/AAAAAAAAH9k/eZTmfSC_gbY/Tapuaetai_thumb%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Tapuaetai" width="485"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               Tapuaetai (One Foot Island) is a small islet in the Aitutaki atoll of the Cook Islands. It offers the visitor some great views of the Aitutaki lagoon and depending on the tide one is able to walk on a sandbank a decent distance away from Tapuaetai. The beach was awarded Australasias Leading Beach at the World Travel Awards held in Sydney in June 2008.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 The Baths
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="The Baths 01" border="0" height="364" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-KQLB5jA4B5o/TguKc9kn0lI/AAAAAAAAH9o/UoufX9uJqfY/The%252520Baths%25252001_thumb%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="The Baths 01" width="485"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               The Baths are situated at the southern tip of Virgin Gorda, the third largest island of the British Virgin Islands. Huge granite boulders lie in piles on the beach, forming spectacular tunnels and grottoes that are open to the sea and flood at high tide. The sandy beaches are lined with tropical palms, adding to the dramatic effect.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 Navagio Beach
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Navagio Beach 00" border="0" height="364" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-_xLBnP1pSsg/TguKeD7j7MI/AAAAAAAAH9s/50ic4dWaJA4/Navagio%252520Beach%25252000_thumb%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Navagio Beach 00" width="485"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               Navagio Beach is an isolated sandy cove on Zakynthos island only accessible by boat. It is one of the trademark beaches in Greece, frequently featured on postcards. Navagio Beach is home to the wreck of an alleged smuggler and features sheer limestone cliffs, white sandy beaches, and clear blue water. No wonder this stunning beach attracts thousands of tourists every year.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 Zlatni Rat
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh5.ggpht.com/-L720I3arcb4/TguKfg0jTtI/AAAAAAAAH9w/xuOGKvDMyOc/s1600-h/Zlatni%252520rat%252520beach%25255B7%25255D.jpg">
                <img alt="Zlatni rat beach" border="0" height="356" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-kp0DYunyI7A/TguKg6yPspI/AAAAAAAAH90/3lJdPKQra5Q/Zlatni%252520rat%252520beach_thumb%25255B10%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Zlatni rat beach" width="485"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               Zlatni Rat (Golden Cape) is among Europes most famous beaches and one of the top tourist attractions in Croatia. Situated near the city of Bol on the southern coast of the island of Brac, Zlatni Rat is a narrow white pebble beach. The amazing shape of the beach shifts with the changes in tide, currents and wind. In 2009, it was named as one of the top ten best beaches in the world by Lonely Planet.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 Oldeniz
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 <img alt="Oldeniz" border="0" height="364" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-v06LtBEkmrg/TguKh4Ifa_I/AAAAAAAAH94/2fj4QL3ZOCk/Ol%2525C3%2525BCdeniz_thumb%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Oldeniz" width="485"/>
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               ldeniz is a small village located on the south west coast of Turkey on the Aegean Sea. It has a secluded sandy bay at the mouth of ldeniz, on a blue lagoon. This beach is famous for its shades of turquoise and remains one of the most photographed beaches on the Mediterranean. ldeniz is also regarded as one of the best places in the world to paraglide due to its unique panoramic views.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 Maya Bay
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-x1OhX2Jl1dE/TguKjXHZp2I/AAAAAAAAH98/pNFCuqraMVQ/s1600-h/Maya%252520Bay%25252001%25255B5%25255D.jpg">
                <img alt="Maya Bay 01" border="0" height="364" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-jKRrztDXdWE/TguKkt3GYYI/AAAAAAAAH-A/PnpsmHLzofQ/Maya%252520Bay%25252001_thumb%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Maya Bay 01" width="485"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               Maya Bay is a shallow bay surrounded by a ring of steep limestone hills on Koh Phi Phi Leh, the second largest island of the Phi Phi Islands. The bay has superb marine life and is a favorite spot for divers. Maya Bay became world famous after the 2000 movie The Beach was filmed there and today many tourists come to Koh Phi Phi just to see this beach.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 Trunk Bay
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Trunk Bay" border="0" height="364" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-nZyhCJp2fbc/TguKl2RlKbI/AAAAAAAAH-E/a4qSorHpoJg/Trunk%252520Bay_thumb%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-right: 0px; border-top: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-left: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-bottom: 0px" title="Trunk Bay" width="485"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               Maya Bay is a shallow bay surrounded by a ring of steep limestone hills on Koh Phi Phi Leh, the second largest island of the Phi Phi Islands. The bay has superb marine life and is a favorite spot for divers. Maya Bay became world famous after the 2000 movie The Beach was filmed there and today many tourists come to Koh Phi Phi just to see this beach.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 Tulum
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Tulum" border="0" height="323" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-MQI64x4h4Ew/TguKm3o7FMI/AAAAAAAAH-I/pDmK7olRYw0/Tulum_thumb%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-right: 0px; border-top: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-left: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-bottom: 0px" title="Tulum" width="485"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               Tulum is situated on the east coast of the Yucatn Peninsula. It once served as the major port of the Mayan city of Coba. Tulum was built around 1200 AD when the Mayan civilization was already in decline and therefore lacks the elegance of some other famous sites. The tropical beach backdrop however makes this one of the top tourist attractions in Mexico that is a heaven for the photographer or artist.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="3">
                 Anse Source D'Argent
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Anse Source D'Argent" border="0" height="364" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-AMIIfvQBLF8/TguKoB77i7I/AAAAAAAAH-M/ewmO9ACbeRY/Anse%252520Source%252520D%252527Argent_thumb%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-right: 0px; border-top: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-left: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-bottom: 0px" title="Anse Source D'Argent" width="485"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               Anse Source DArgent is located on La Digue, one of the smaller islands of the Seychelles. It is one of the most photographed beaches in the world and featured in countless travel magazines. One of the worlds top beaches, Anse Source DArgent contains a winning combination of large granite boulders studding the coastline, coupled with pink sand beaches and coconut palms.
              </p>
