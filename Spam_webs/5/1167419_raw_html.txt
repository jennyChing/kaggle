       <h1 id="printHeader" style="display:none">
        How-To Geek
       </h1>
       <!-- abovepost -->
       <div class="thepost" id="post-6499">
        <div style="max-width:650px;width:100%;margin-bottom:0px;margin-top:-10px;min-height:24px;">
         <div style="clear:both;">
         </div>
        </div>
        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/6499/stupid-geek-tricks-find-hidden-messages-in-web-site-headers/" rel="bookmark" title="Stupid Geek Tricks: Find the Secret Messages in Web Site Headers">
          Stupid Geek Tricks: Find the Secret Messages in Web Site Headers
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="thecontent">
         <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
         <p>
          Todays stupid geek trick takes us to the unseen technical world beneath the surface of your favorite websites, and shows us how real geeks hide secret messages in the HTTP protocol headers. Were talking
          <em>
           Serious
          </em>
          geek cred here.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Slashdot's Hidden Message" border="0" height="340" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/image58.png" style="border-right-width: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px" title="Slashdot's Hidden Message" width="615"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What Are You Talking About?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <div id="div-1342-1" style="margin-top:10px;padding-top:10px;border-top:1px solid #CCC;margin-bottom:10px;border-bottom:1px solid #CCC;">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           tmntag.adTag('div-1342-1');
          </script>
         </div>
         <p>
          The thing you probably dont realize is that behind the scenes, your web browser sends a request to the web server using the
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypertext_Transfer_Protocol">
           HTTP protocol
          </a>
          , which is nothing more than a pre-defined set of commands in text format so the browser and web server can communicate.
         </p>
         <p>
          but thats really boring. Which is why the geeks that run various web sites decided to hide messages in those headers just to see if anybody would notice. And some of them are rather fun.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           How to See the Hidden Headers
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Theres a number of ways to access these headers, the easiest for real geeks is to pop open the command prompt and use the curl utility, available on Linux, Mac OS X, or even Windowsif you dont mind
          <a href="http://curl.haxx.se/download.html">
           downloading an additional utility.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The other way to view them is with the
          <a href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/3829">
           Live HTTP Headers
          </a>
          add-on for Firefox.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you are using the curl method, you can simply type in the following command (thats a dash and capital i, not an L)
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           curl I sitename.com
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          For instance, if you type in curl I slashdot.org, youll see that they embed random quotes from Futurama into the headers:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" border="0" height="212" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/image59.png" style="border-right-width: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px" title="image" width="620"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The blogging platform
          <a href="http://wordpress.com">
           WordPress
          </a>
          uses the header for a useful purposethey recommend contacting them about a job if youve been able to locate the headers:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           curl I wordpress.com
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" border="0" height="153" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/image60.png" style="border-right-width: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px" title="image" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          My buddy Ross over at
          <a href="http://www.simplehelp.net/">
           SimpleHelp.net
          </a>
          expresses his music taste in his header by now Im sure you can figure out the command.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" border="0" height="224" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/image61.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="image" width="405"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Over at the
          <a href="http://www.online-tech-tips.com">
           Online Tech Tips
          </a>
          site, theyve got a very important tip for you:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" border="0" height="212" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/image62.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="image" width="473"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The
          <a href="http://cybernetnews.com">
           CybernetNews
          </a>
          blog tells us that you should tape your thumbs to your hands. I mean, everyone is doing it!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" border="0" height="243" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/image63.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="image" width="518"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://mattiasgeniar.be/">
           Mattias Geniar
          </a>
          wants everybody to know who rocks. Turns out, its him!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" border="0" height="159" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/image64.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="image" width="455"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The
          <a href="http://www.tuxgames.com/">
           Tux Games
          </a>
          site is using subliminal advertising. BUY MORE GAMES!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" border="0" height="155" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/image65.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="image" width="526"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Add a Hidden Message To Your Own Web Site
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          For those of you rocking a blog running on an Apache web server, and using a .htaccess file, you can actually add your own secret hidden message to your own HTTP headers.
         </p>
         <p>
          Just open up your .htaccess file, and add the following line (near the beginning for best effect).
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Header set X-Nerd Something Here
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          You can change whatever is after the X- to anything you want, like X-Yay or X-SecretStuff, and you can also put whatever message you want between the quotesthough I would keep it nice and short.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Added Bonus: Pull a Random Futurama Quote from Slashdot
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          So now weve figured out how to see the quoteswith a little more command-line magic you can make a script that pulls a random quote from the Slashdot servers. Yes, this script is pretty useless, but its fun!
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           curl -sI slashdot.org | grep -E X-(B|L|F)
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" border="0" height="119" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/image66.png" style="border-right-width: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px" title="image" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Theres lots of other sites with hidden messages in the headers
         </p>
