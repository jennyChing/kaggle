                   <em>
                    <span property="v:summary" style="color: #938953;">
                     I was really happy to discover orzotto in the Lombard cuisine. It is a wonderful alternative to risotto and as easy to prepare. Barley is rich in Iron and dietary fiber and should be a constant part of ones diet. Perhaps it is not as versatile as rice, but still nifty enough to prepare quick and delicious meals  Mediterranean style.

                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                   <span style="font-family: georgia; font-size: 18px;">
                    <strong>
                     <span style="color: #244061; font-size: 20px;">
                      INGREDIENTS
                     </span>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                  </p>
                  <span style="font-family: georgia; font-size: 18px;">
                  </span>
                  <p>
                  </p>
                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                   <span rel="v:ingredient" style="color: #244061; font-family: georgia; font-size: 18px;">
                    <em>
                     Bunch of green parsley
                     <br/>
                     Olive oil
                     <br/>
                     5 cloves garlic
                     <br/>
                     500g/18oz clams de-shelled and defrosted
                     <br/>
                     Juice of  lemon
                     <br/>
                     2 cups pearl barley
                     <br/>
                     Salt and pepper
                     <br/>
                     Fish stalk
                     <br/>
                    </em>
                   </span>
                  </p>

                   DIRECTIONS
                  </span>
                 </td>

                 <td colspan="2" style="padding: 5px; font-family: tahoma; font-size: 18px; background-color: #ffffff;">
                  <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                  </span>
                  <p style="text-align: justify;">
                   <span style="color: black; font-family: tahoma, sans-serif; font-size: 16px;">
                    If you remember, soak the barley for a few hours. Otherwise you will need to cook it longer.
                   </span>
                  </p>
                  <p style="text-align: justify;">
                   <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   </span>
                  </p>
                  <p style="text-align: justify;">
                   <span style="color: black; font-family: tahoma, sans-serif; font-size: 16px;">
                    Wash and chop the green parsley. Place half of the parsley in a pan with olive oil, along with chopped garlic. Saut briefly. Next add the defrosted clams and cook for about five minutes. Add juice of lemon and then the pre-soaked and drained barley. Stir to combine with all the ingredients in the pan. Season with salt and pepper.
                   </span>
                  </p>
                  <p style="text-align: justify;">
                   <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   </span>
                  </p>
                  <p style="text-align: justify;">
                   <span style="color: black; font-family: tahoma, sans-serif; font-size: 16px;">
                    Next, pour some fish stalk (or water) and continue cooking just like risotto, letting the liquids evaporate, adding more stock and repeating the same a few times, until the barley expands. Finally add enough stalk to almost cover the barley; place a lid on top, lower the heat and cook until the grains are soft, for about 15-20 minutes.
                   </span>
                  </p>
                  <p style="text-align: justify;">
                   <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                   </span>
                  </p>
                  <p style="text-align: justify;">
                   <span style="color: black; font-family: tahoma, sans-serif; font-size: 16px;">
                    Finally, combine the orzotto with the remaining chopped parsley, garnish with some
                    <em>
                     grana padano
                    </em>
                    cheese if you like, and serve.
                   </span>
                  </p>
