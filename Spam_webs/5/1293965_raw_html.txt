       <h1>
        Hurry Up And Wait In The VA System
       </h1>
       <div style="color:#666666;margin-bottom:10px;">
        Saturday, February 14, 2015
       </div>
       <div class="articleStory">
        <p>
         If you have ever been in the military one of the biggest sayings is hurry up and wait. As a veteran I am sick and tired of waiting for VA medical appointments. The Veterans Health Administration is Americas largest integrated health care system with over 1,700 sites of care, serving 8.76 million veterans each year. With all those assets why are veterans having to wait for months at a time for appointments and in some cases veterans are dying while on the waiting list.
        </p>
        <div class="pageRichAd">
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="270" scrolling="no" src="http://ads.chattanoogan.com/AdPage.aspx?a=8&amp;c=1" width="300">
         </iframe>
        </div>
        <p>
         I have documented bad knees from being in the military I called them to make an appointment and the earliest day I could get in to see a doctor was five weeks later. I called my civilian job primary doctor, got an appointment the same day, he referred me to an orthopedic doctor, they called, got appointment, went to appointment, they schedule me for a MRI, for a few days later, had MRI, got results back from the doctor, but still had to wait three more weeks before I could get an initial visit to see an orthopedic doctor through VA.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now fortunately I had civilian health insurance, as to where I could get seen and diagnosed in a reasonable amount of time. But for some of our veterans who do not have this option and are waiting on the VA for their appointments, they should not have to be put on a waiting list for appointment and surgeries.
        </p>
        <p>
         In my instance it was just my knee, but what if it was diabetes, or my high blood pressure. The VA hospitals all over the U.S. are having issues of long waiting lists because they are so far behind and it is going to take several years for them to catch up. With that being said they did trying to pass a $169 billion budget for FY 2016 which is great; the issue is trying to hire all the health care professional needed to fill in the gaps. I would purpose that the President along with the Senate and HOR work with the governors of each state and create a way that for the next two years veterans would be able to see a list of doctors thats approved by the VA to take care of the veterans issues. By doing this that will give the VA hospitals the needed time to get staff in place to handle the call load of veterans. It will also get doctors the ability to give back to veterans for service to our country. After the two years are up the VA should be able to sustain, because it would not be a huge backlog of veterans trying to get appointments.
        </p>
        <p>
         Hurry up and wait, for what, I did not have to wait when they deployed me to combat areas, or when I was away from my families for multiply deployments. Lets change the negative stigma that comes with hurry up and wait and implore a reasonable amount of wait time for appointments for our veterans.
        </p>
        <p>
         I am tired of waiting for better service.
        </p>
        <p>
         Shawn Hickey
        </p>
