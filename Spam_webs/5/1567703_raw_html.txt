      <h1 class="name post-title entry-title" itemprop="itemReviewed" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Thing">
       <span itemprop="name">
        Alstom Powering Thailand With Cleaner Lignite
       </span>
      </h1>
      <p class="post-meta">
       <span>
        Posted by:
        <a href="http://www.establishmentpost.com/author/john-lefevre/" title="">
         John Le Fevre
        </a>
       </span>
       <span>
        April 16, 2015
       </span>
       <span>
        in
        <a href="http://www.establishmentpost.com/subjects/industry-business/" rel="category tag">
         Business
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.establishmentpost.com/special/side-story/" rel="category tag">
         Featured
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.establishmentpost.com/special/headlines/" rel="category tag">
         Headlines
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.establishmentpost.com/subjects/infrastructure/" rel="category tag">
         Infrastructure
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.establishmentpost.com/special/picture-of-the-week/" rel="category tag">
         Picture of the Week
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.establishmentpost.com/countries/thailand/" rel="category tag">
         Thailand
        </a>
       </span>
      </p>
      <div class="clear">
      </div>
      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        France-based multinational power generation, power transmission and rail infrastructure company Alstom has been appointed by the Electricity Generating Authority of Thailand (Egat) to build it the first ultra-supercritical lignite-fired power plant in Asia.
       </p>
       <p>
        The contract, signed last month, will see Alstom supply and build a 600 MW plant at the Mae Moh power plant in Lampang province, northern Thailand to replace four existing 150 MW generators (units 4-7) installed there between 1984 and 1985.
       </p>
       <p>
        For its  520 million (BT 18.368 billion / US$ 565.578 million) portion of the  950 million (Bt 33.558 billion / US$ 1.033 billion) engineering, procurement and construction (EPC) contract signed in consortium with Japanese Marubeni Corporation, Alstom will supply its state-of-the-art technology products such as:
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_21117" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.establishmentpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Alstom-Mae-Moh_3D_1.jpg">
         <img alt="Alstom 3D model of Asias first ultra-supercritical lignite-fired power plant to be built at Mae Moh power plant" class="wp-image-21117" height="323" src="http://www.establishmentpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Alstom-Mae-Moh_3D_1-446x360.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="credit">
          copyright Alstom, not for commercial use
        </p>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Alstom 3D model of Asias first ultra-supercritical lignite-fired power plant to be built at Mae Moh power plant
        </p>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li>
         ultra-supercritical boiler with integrated Selective Catalytic Reduction of NOx (SCR), ultrasupercritical steam turbine and generator
        </li>
        <li>
         an air quality control system comprising a Wet Flue Gas Desulphurisation (WFGD) which reduces sulphur oxides by 98 per cent, and an Electrostatic Precipitators (ESP) designed to capture particulate and dust emissions with an efficiency of 99.9 per cent
        </li>
        <li>
         The Mae Moh power plant is built on the Mae Moh basin where abundant amounts of lignite, a soft brownish coal showing traces of plant structure, were first discovered 1953. The plant currently houses 10 generators with a total capacity of 2,400 MW.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_21118" style="width: 460px">
        <a href="http://www.establishmentpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Ratanachai-Namwong-EGAT-deputy-governor-----Power-Plant-Development.jpg">
         <img alt="Ratanachai Namwong, EGAT deputy governor - Power Plant Development: A primary consideration was efficient and reliable pollution control " class="size-full wp-image-21118" height="446" src="http://www.establishmentpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Ratanachai-Namwong-EGAT-deputy-governor-----Power-Plant-Development.jpg" width="450"/>
        </a>
        <p class="credit">
         Electricity Generating Authority of Thailand (Egat)
        </p>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Ratanachai Namwong, EGAT deputy governor  Power Plant Development: A primary consideration was efficient and reliable pollution control
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Ratanachai Namwong, EGAT deputy governor  Power Plant Development said the lignite to be used to fuel the new generating plant was the lowest quality grade of coal with a three per cent sulfur content and 40 per cent calcium oxide. A primary consideration in the TOR was efficient and reliable pollution control and a requirement for a high efficiency boiler.
       </p>
       <p>
        EGATs pollution control was regulated to be better than the standard of the Pollution Control Department for the optimum efficiency of the power production with the least impact on the community and environment. This responds to the requirement of the community on pollution control Mr Namwong said.
       </p>
       <p>
        Andreas Lusch, senior vce president Steam Business at Alstom said the 2,400MW Mae Moh power plant is essential to Thailands generation capacity, and produces about 15 per cent of Egats total output from local lignite.
       </p>
       <p>
        This new project will allow Egat to replace units 4 to 7 with a new, state of the art lignite fired unit, maintaining a sustainable use of the local lignite sources, while reducing significantly the environmental footprint of the plant.
       </p>
       <p>
        Mr Lusch said that while the existing generators at the Mae Moh power plant were currently equipped with Alstom boilers the new unit will operate at ultra-supercritical conditions which leads to a higher overall plant efficiency, an improvement of close to 20 per cent in the heat rate, increased power output, as well as a reduction of more than 20 per cent in CO2 emissions per unit of fuel burned compared to current installations.
       </p>
       <p>
        We are proud to be able to work with Egat to provide more sustainable and cleaner power to Thai customers and businesses, Mr Lusch added.
       </p>
       <p>
        The EPC for the Mae Moh power plant refurbishment project comes on top of a long term service agreement (LTSA) Alstom signed with Egat in January for two of its upgraded GT26 gas turbines it is installing under a separate contract for the North Bangkok combined-cycle power plant (CCPP) Block 2. (

       </p>
