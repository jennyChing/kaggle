       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Bacon Cheddar Apple Scones
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.lifeinpleasantville.com/author/candace/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Candace Derickx
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.lifeinpleasantville.com/bacon-cheddar-apple-scones/#comments">
           32 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Nothing says lovin like baked goods and these Bacon Cheddar Apple Scones scream love. For starters, who doesnt love warm scones from the oven? Secondly, bacon. Third, cheese. Honestly, need I say more? I either had you at bacon or cheddar, but either way you know these babies are right up your alley.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats why I chose these Bacon Cheddar Apple Scones for my February
         <a href="http://www.lifemadedelicious.ca/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lifemadedelicious.ca/', 'Life Made Delicious']);" target="_blank">
          Life Made Delicious
         </a>
         post, since February is all about the love. Were loving each other on Valentines day and loving our families on Family Day, and loving the world as we celebrate the Olympics. Its a very sentimental month, so why not bring on the foods we love too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="bacon cheddar apple scones" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10842" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/bacon-cheddar-apple-scone-e1392731125396.jpg?fit=700%2C466"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Bacon Cheddar Apple Scones
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         2 1/4 cups flour
         <br/>
         3 tbsp. plus 1 tsp sugar, divided
         <br/>
         1 tbsp. baking powder
         <br/>
         6 tbsp. cold butter, cut into 1/2 pieces
         <br/>
         2 slices of bacon, cooked crisp and crumbled
         <br/>
         1 Gala apple (cored and diced, I leave the skin on, you may choose to remove)
         <br/>
         1 cup plus 1 tbsp buttermilk, divided
         <br/>
         1 cup grated old cheddar cheese
        </p>
        <p>
         Preheat oven to 425F. Combine flour, sugar and baking powder in a food processor using pulse function. Add butter and using pulse function, process until coarse crumbs appear. Transfer contents to a large bowl. Add bacon, cheese and apple and toss well. Stir in 1 cup buttermilk until soft dough forms.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="bacon cheddar apple scones" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10851" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/scones-e1392732359282.jpg?fit=700%2C466"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Turn dough out onto floured surface and knead five to six times. Divide dough in half and shape each half into a 6 wide circle. Cut each circle into eight wedges. Place wedges 1 apart on baking sheet. Brush tops with remaining 1 tbsp buttermilk and sprinkle with 1 tsp of sugar. Bake for 20 minutes. Makes 16 scones.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can freeze uncooked scones (and this applies to any scone recipe) and then bake them straight from the freezer. Wrap well on sheet pan lined with parchment paper until youre ready to use, or freeze in a container with layers separated by parchment.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="bacon cheddar apple scones" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10846" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.lifeinpleasantville.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/bacon-cheddar-apple-scones.jpg?fit=700%2C700"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You may also like these
         <a href="http://www.lifeinpleasantville.com/raspberry-scones/" target="_blank">
          Raspberry Scones
