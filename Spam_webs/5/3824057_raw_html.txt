       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Ok so youve been a user of WordPress.com for a while now. Things have been pretty nice so far, but youve come to a point where youre ready to go beyond WordPress.com. You want to install plugins and customize your themes. You want to really start monetizing your site and be in full control. Well you are at the right place. While todays article wont discuss the
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/beginners-guide/self-hosted-wordpress-org-vs-free-wordpress-com-infograph/" target="_blank" title="Self Hosted WordPress.org vs Free WordPress.com">
          differences between WordPress.com and WordPress.org
         </a>
         , we will take a look at a question that a lot of users have been asking us: how to move your existing WordPress.com blog to a self-hosted WordPress.org site. In this step by step tutorial, we will show you how to properly move your blog from WordPress.com to WordPress.org. We will cover how to transfer all of your WordPress.com content to a WordPress.org site (yes this include images). We will also cover how to keep all of your search engine rankings along with how to redirect your existing users to your new site. Are you ready? Lets start transferring your WordPress.com blog over to your new self-hosted site.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Things You Need Before You Start
        </h4>
        <p>
         You will need to have a domain name and a
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/wordpress-hosting/" target="_blank" title="How to Choose the Best WordPress Hosting">
          good web hosting company
         </a>
         that knows WordPress. We highly recommend that you use
         <a class="thirstylink" href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/refer/bluehost/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Bluehost">
          Bluehost
         </a>
         because they will give you a free domain and 50% off their hosting plan (special for WPBeginner users).
         <a class="thirstylink" href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/refer/bluehost/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Bluehost">
          Bluehost
         </a>
         is also an officially recommended hosting provider of WordPress.
        </p>
        <p>
         If your WordPress site already has a custom domain name, then dont worry. We will cover that as well. Lastly, you will need access to your WordPress.com account, so you can easily move all of your posts, pages, images, comments, and other data over to your self-hosted WordPress site.
        </p>
        <p>
         Before you get started with this migration tutorial, we want to let you know that we can migrate your site for FREE as part of our
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/free-wordpress-blog-setup/" title="Free WordPress Blog Setup">
          Free WordPress Blog setup service
         </a>
         . This will be less hassle for you, but if you are someone who likes learning and doing things yourself, then follow our step by step tutorial below.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Video Tutorial
        </h4>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/zln3ZkCd00E?feature=oembed" width="500">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you dont like the video or need more instructions, then continue reading.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Step 1: Exporting Data from WordPress.com
        </h4>
        <p>
         Sign in to your WordPress.com account and go to your blogs Dashboard. Click on Export under Tools menu.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Export Tool in WordPress.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27745" height="229" src="http://cdn2.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/export-wordpressdotcom.jpg" title="Export Tool in WordPress.com" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Clicking on the Export tool will take you to a new page where you will be asked to choose between Free or Guided Transfer.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Free or Guided Transfer from WordPress.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27746" height="257" src="http://cdn4.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/export-free-paid.png" title="Free or Guided Transfer from WordPress.com" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Choose the Free option by clicking on Export. On the next screen you will be asked which data you want to export. Select All Content and press the Download Export File button. An XML file will be downloaded in your computer which will contain all of your posts, pages, images, comments, custom fields, categories, tags, navigation menus and other information.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Exporting data from WordPress.com in XML format" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27747" height="225" src="http://cdn.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/export-options.png" title="Exporting data from WordPress.com in XML format" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <h4>
         Step 2: Setting up WordPress
        </h4>
        <p>
         Now that you have exported your WordPress.com data, the next step is to set up a fresh WordPress install on your web hosting. WordPress is very easy to install, and it only takes a few clicks. We have a step-by-step tutorial on
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/how-to-install-wordpress/" target="_blank" title="How to Install WordPress on your Site">
          how to install WordPress within 5 minutes
         </a>
         . Once you have installed WordPress, it is time to import your content into your new self-hosted WordPress website.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Step 3: Importing Content into Self Hosted WordPress Site
        </h4>
        <p>
         To import your old content from WordPress.com to your new site, you need to click on Tools  Import option in your self-hosted WordPress.org dashboard.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Importing WordPress.com content into WordPress.org" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27748" height="223" src="http://cdn3.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/wordpressdotcom-import.png" title="Importing WordPress.com content into WordPress.org" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Next click on WordPress. You will be asked to install the
         <a href="http://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/wordpress-importer/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="WordPress Importer Plugin">
          WordPress Importer
         </a>
         plugin. Once you install it, activate and run the importer plugin. It will take you to a screen where you will be asked to upload your WordPress.com XML file that you downloaded in step 1 of this tutorial.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Import WordPress" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27749" height="230" src="http://cdn4.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/import-wordpress.png" title="Import WordPress" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Note: if your file size is larger than 2MB, then you have two options. One is to ask your web hosting company to increase that limit temporarily, so you can get on with the process. The other option would be to split your file by using a
          <a href="http://www.rangerpretzel.com/content/view/20/19/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="WXR File Splitter">
           WXR file splitter
          </a>
          .
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         When you start importing, you will have the option of assigning your old content to a current user or create a new user. Youll also have the option of importing your old attachments (you want to make sure that you check this box to ensure that your images are imported).
        </p>
        <h4>
         Step 4: Importing Your Blogroll Links
        </h4>
        <p>
         If you had been using the Links feature on your WordPress.com blog to store blogroll or other links, then you need to follow these instructions to export and import them. Users who were not using this feature can skip to Step 5.
        </p>
        <p>
         Blogroll links are exported in OPML format. It is an XML format which allows you to export and import your links and link categories. Your WordPress.com links have an OPML file located at an address like this:
        </p>
        <p>
         http://example.wordpress.com/wp-links-opml.php
        </p>
        <p>
         Replace example with your WordPress.com blogs subdomain. If you are using a custom domain with your WordPress.com blog then your OPML file can be accessed by visiting a URL like this:
        </p>
        <p>
         http://www.example.com/wp-links-opml.php
        </p>
        <p>
         Your OPML file will open in your browser window and you need to save it to your desktop. Press CTRL+S (Command+S on Mac) to save the file on your computer.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now that you have a backup of your WordPress.com links, the next step is to import them into WordPress. However, self hosted WordPress does not have a link manager enabled by default.
        </p>
        <p>
         First thing you need to do is install and activate the
         <a href="https://wordpress.org/plugins/link-manager/" target="_blank" title="Link Manager">
          Link Manager
         </a>
         plugin. The plugins page says that it hasnt been updated in last two years. Thats because it didnt need updating and you can confidently install this plugin.
        </p>
        <p>
         Upon activation, the plugin will add a new menu item in your WordPress admin bar labeled Links.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Links manager in WordPress" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27744" height="313" src="http://cdn4.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/links-manager.png" title="Links manager in WordPress" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Your WordPress site is now capable of managing links and blogrolls and you can safely import them. Visit
         <strong>
          Tools  Import
         </strong>
         and click on Blogroll to install the OPML importer.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blogroll Importer" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27740" height="267" src="http://cdn4.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/blogroll-importer.png" title="Blogroll Importer" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Upon installation you will be redirected to the importer. Where you need to upload the OPML file you saved earlier and click on import OPML file button.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Importing an OPML file in WordPress" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27741" height="235" src="http://cdn4.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/importing-opml-file.png" title="Importing an OPML file in WordPress" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         WordPress will now import your links and link categories from the OPML file. You will be able to see the progress and on completion you will see the success message.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Successful completion of blogroll link import" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27742" height="254" src="http://cdn4.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/success.png" title="Successful completion of blogroll link import" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <h4>
         Step 5: Setting your WordPress.com blog to private
        </h4>
        <p>
         The final step would be to set your WordPress.com blog to private. If you dont want to redirect your old users to your new site, then go ahead and follow this step. Go to the Reading page under Settings tab, from your WordPress.com Dashboard and select the last option,
         <em>
          I would like my blog to be private, visible only to users I choose
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Making a WordPress.com Blog Private" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27750" height="210" src="http://cdn3.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/privacy-wpdotcom.png" title="Making a WordPress.com Blog Private" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Attention:
         </strong>
         If you have been writing for sometime, and you have a loyal audience, then it does not make sense to leave them hanging. Furthermore, if your blog has been out there for a while, then chances are that it is indexed in Google and other search engines. You should keep all the search engine juice and easily redirect your old users to your new blog.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Step 6: Redirecting Visitors and Preserving SEO
        </h4>
        <p>
         Redirecting users to new location with a 301 header is the standard solution to keep search rankings while moving a site from one location to another. Since you dont have access to .htaccess on WordPress.com, you can not make any changes by yourself to retain search engine rankings. However, WordPress.com offers a paid upgrade, Site Redirect, which provides this functionality.
        </p>
        <p>
         Go to WordPress.com blogs dashboard and click on the Store menu item. The upgrade you are looking for is called Site Redirect. At the time of writing this tutorial, this update was reasonably priced at $13 USD per year. This upgrade provides 301 redirect which allows your sites visitors and search engines to be automatically redirected to your new site.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="WordPress.com site redirect" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27751" height="230" src="http://cdn3.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/wpcom-site-redirect.png" title="WordPress.com site redirect" width="520"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Some of you will ask how long should I keep paying for this Offsite redirect feature? The answer is for as long as you want to. However, we personally think that 2 years would be enough time for your old users to memorize your new domain name
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/plugins/classic-smilies/img/icon_smile.gif"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you are changing domains, then another thing you want to do is update all in post URLs. If you ever inter-linked your posts, then those links have to be updated. Use our article on
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/plugins/how-to-update-urls-when-moving-your-wordpress-site/" title="How to update URLs when moving your WordPress site">
          how to update URLs when moving your WordPress site
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h4>
         Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
        </h4>
        <p>
         We wrote this article in January 2013 and since then weve received tons of great questions. A lot of which weve answered via email or in comments, so thought it would be nice to compile it at one place where everyone can see them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What happens with my WordPress.com Subscribers?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thankfully, WordPress.com allows you to migrate your subscribers with one condition. You must use
         <a href="http://wordpress.org/plugins/jetpack/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Jetpack plugin">
          Jetpack plugin
         </a>
         which adds the same subscribe functionality that you had at WordPress.com. Once you install and activate the Jetpack plugin, you would need to
         <a href="http://jetpack.me/contact-support/" target="_blank" title="Contact Jetpack Support Team">
          contact the WordPress.com team
         </a>
         and ask them to migrate the subscribers for you. Our hope is that in the later versions of Jetpack, this will become easier and users will be able to do it themselves.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Can you help me transfer from WordPress.com to WordPress.org for FREE?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Absolutely. We offer migration from WordPress.com as part of our
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/free-wordpress-blog-setup/" title="Free WordPress Blog Setup">
          free WordPress blog setup service
         </a>
         . If you need our assistance, then simply signup. Its free
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.wpbeginner.com/wp-content/plugins/classic-smilies/img/icon_smile.gif"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What are the costs of switching to WordPress.org?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         WordPress is free. However, there are some minimal
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/wordpress-hosting/" title="WordPress Web Hosting">
          web hosting
         </a>
         costs. We recommend that you read this article on:
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/beginners-guide/why-is-wordpress-free-what-are-the-costs-what-is-the-catch/" title="Why is WordPress free?">
          Why is WordPress free? What are the costs and what is the catch?
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will my images break?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         No they will not. When you transfer using the import file, all attached images are downloaded and the link is updated. However there is one caveat that we noticed. If your image URL starts with files.wordpress.com, then they will not be converted. If you notice that the image URL has not changed and still points to WordPress.com, then we recommend that you use
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/plugins/how-to-import-external-images-in-wordpress/" title="Import External Images in WordPress">
          import external images
         </a>
         plugin, and it will take care of that.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          I registered my domain through WordPress.com, can I still move?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yes you can. WordPress believe in empowering users and giving them full control of their content. If you already have a domain name through WordPress.com, then all you need is a web hosting account. We recommend that you setup with
         <a class="thirstylink" href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/refer/bluehost/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Bluehost">
          Bluehost
         </a>
         or any of these other
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/wordpress-hosting/" title="WordPress hosting">
          WordPress hosting
         </a>
         providers. During the signup phase, you will be asked do you have a domain or want to register a new one. Simply select the option that I have a domain name, and insert the domain that you registered at WordPress.com. Next thing you would have to do is change the nameservers to point to the hosting provider. Were able to assist with this as part of our free setups service. You can also ask your web hosting provider for support.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will my website go down when I switch?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you do it correctly, then NO. The method weve suggested above will ensure that your website never goes down. If youre worried, then know that we are here to help. Youre welcome to use our
         <a href="http://www.wpbeginner.com/free-wordpress-blog-setup/" title="Free WordPress Setup">
          free setups service
         </a>
         at anytime.
        </p>
        <p>
         We hope that you found our guide on switching from WordPress.com to self-hosted WordPress.org to be helpful. If you have any questions, then please let us know by leaving a comment below.
        </p>
