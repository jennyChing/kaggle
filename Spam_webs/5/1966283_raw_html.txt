         <h2 class="headline" itemprop="name">
          <a href="http://www.abestindustrialsupply.com/blog/preventing-hydraulic-pump-failure/" rel="bookmark">
           Preventing Hydraulic Pump Failure
          </a>
         </h2>
         <div class="byline small">
          <meta content="2014-08-30" itemprop="datePublished"/>
          <meta content="2014-10-17" itemprop="dateModified"/>
          <span class="post_date_intro">
           Posted on
          </span>
          <span class="post_date" title="2014-08-30">
           August 30, 2014
          </span>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post_content" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          The hydraulic pump life is the main vessel of any hydraulic tools and equipment and machineries such as rams, presses, cylinders and pullers by basically converting mechanical power to hydraulic energy. Without the aid of this chunk of formidable equipment, large scale hydraulic tasks such as lifting, pressure holding, pushing, pulling and pressing would be close to impossible, especially for industrial and auto-mechanical applications. That is why maintenance must be top priority to ensure full functionality, promote safety and lessen the cost of repairs effectively.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmkEnSt49wM" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Untitled" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-145" height="174" src="http://www.abestindustrialsupply.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Untitled-300x174.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          With that being said, here are some things you should know to prevent hydraulic pump failure.
         </h2>
         <ol>
          <li>
           <h3>
            Oil Keeps the Good Flow Going
           </h3>
           <p>
             The mechanism of
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.abestindustrialsupply.com/" target="_blank">
              hydraulic tools and equipment
             </a>
            </strong>
            ultimately rely on oil. Thats why regular checking of oil is needed. Lacking oil in its reservoir reduces oil flow and leads to lower performance in providing ample pressure to lift, spread, press and pull. Moreover, contaminants are the number one cause of deterioration of seals and gaskets that can result to leak and oil blockage.
           </p>
           <h4>
            You need to:
           </h4>
           <p>
            Check the oil level regularly. Never let its reservoir run dry. Also, when replacing the used hydraulic fluid with a new one, be sure to follow the recommended ISO and/or AW rates for viscosity and density. This will greatly affect the performance of your pump in terms of volumetric efficiency, tolerance in friction and temperature, as well as resistance from seal corrosion.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <h3>
            Seal the Deal
           </h3>
           <p>
             Seals, gaskets and caps prevent oil spillage. Most of the time, these small components are the first ones to go, as they are more prone to deterioration. A minor damage to any of these components may give you a rather big headache in repair costs if you dont give equal importance to these small parts.
           </p>
           <h4>
            You need to:
           </h4>
           <p>
            See if the seals are still intact. The moment you found even the smallest of damage to the seals, it means that you should replace it as soon as possible. This goes also for the screws, washers and bolts of the pump.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <h3>
            Know Your Limit
           </h3>
           <p>
             Theres a reason why every hydraulic pump has its own specifications such as pressure limit and recommended tool compatibility. Using it for equipment that exceeds the pressure limit or for the wrong hydraulic tool will force this
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.abestindustrialsupply.com/hydraulic-pumps.html" target="_blank">
              hydraulic apparatus
             </a>
            </strong>
            to exert more pressure that may lead to damages such as overheat and overload or worse, accidents.
           </p>
           <h4>
            You need to:
           </h4>
           <p>
            Read the manual to know the specific functionalities and correct usage of your hydraulic pump. Never exceed the recommended pressure limit to enforce safety.
           </p>
           <div style="text-align: center;">
            <blockquote style="margin: 0 0 0 40px; border: none; padding: 0px;">
             <div style="text-align: center;">
              <iframe align="center" allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/MmkEnSt49wM" width="420">
              </iframe>
             </div>
            </blockquote>
           </div>
          </li>
         </ol>
