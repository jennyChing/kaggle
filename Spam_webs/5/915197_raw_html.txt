          <h1 class="blog-title">
           A small guide on soft contact lenses
          </h1>
         </header>
         <section class="content">
          <section class="blog-content">
           <div class="content-wrap clearfix">
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              What should you know?
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             They are made from Hydrogel (a plastic) and 40-80% water. They are very much flexible and provide a lot of comfort for the users. They are corrective lenses that are worn directly on the eye (they rest on the cornea). They are used for correcting various visual disorders that include but not just limited to Hyperopia (farsightedness), Myopia (nearsightedness), Astigmatism, Presbyopia etc. They can have less image distortion and can offer greater field of view than commonly used glasses. There are special contacts too which are used for the changing the color of the eye or to improve the appearance of an abnormal eye.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Your doctor can help you:
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Your eye care professional will perform many tests to help you get the best lenses that can fit your eyes. He may measure the curvature of the cornea and may ask you to put on trial contacts of various brands to find out that can be the best for you. Only your doctor can demonstrate well how you can insert them or how you can remove or disinfect them etc. If there is someone who is using them for the first time then he / she may take at least a few days to get used to them. In the beginning, there can be some discomfort but it will go away with the time.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Care for your contacts:
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             There is a need to focus on cleaning too. If you do not focus on this then there can be an eye infection. Many of the doctors suggest that users should clean or disinfect their them daily. Users can make use of the most branded solutions for the same purpose. One of the best solutions which are available in the market is ReNu Multipurpose Solution which can be very effective.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Consult your doctor:
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             People who wear contacts need to consult their doctor if they feel any continual discomfort. If eyes become red or if there is any sort of pain then it is essential to get in touch with the doctor. There can be possibility of development of infection in some cases. Infection may occur due to less care. People should visit their doctor for the examination at least once in a year even if they do not find any problem. Your doctor might examine the health of the cornea and may let you know if there is a need to continue or not. Your doctor may even correct problem if there is any.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Are contacts for everyone?
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Although, they are worn by many patients, yet they may not be right for everyone. Many of the patients who suffer from eye infections frequently or those who have dry eye syndrome or other eye allergies should not make use of them. This can lead to more serious problems for them.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
