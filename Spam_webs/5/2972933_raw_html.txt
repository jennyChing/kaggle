        <div class="post-body__content">
         <figure>
          <img alt="" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-Final.jpg"/>
         </figure>
         <p>
          Geometric print designs have been quite the trend as of late. Using the
          <strong>
           Blend Tool (W)
          </strong>
          , the
          <strong>
           Appearance
          </strong>
          panel in Adobe Illustrator, and a trendy color palette we'll create a hexagonal design of our own, ready for the printers.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <span class="sectionnum">
           1.
          </span>
          Draw Your Hexagons
         </h2>
         <h3>
          Step 1
         </h3>
         <p>
          The first step is simple: draw a hexagon. To do so easily use the
          <strong>
           Polygon Tool
          </strong>
          to create a
          <strong>
           6-sided
          </strong>
          polygon with a
          <strong>
           Radius
          </strong>
          of
          <strong>
           100pts
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <figure>
          <img alt="Hex-001" height="280" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-001.jpg" width="600"/>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h3>
          Step 2
         </h3>
         <p>
          Draw a second, smaller hexagon next to the first. This one has a
          <strong>
           Radius
          </strong>
          of
          <strong>
           20pts
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-002.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-002" height="362" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-002.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h2>
          <span class="sectionnum">
           2.
          </span>
          Blend the Hexagons
         </h2>
         <h3>
          Step 1
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Select
          </strong>
          both hexagons and
          <strong>
           Align
          </strong>
          their centers (both
          <strong>
           Vertically
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           Horizontally
          </strong>
          ). Using the
          <strong>
           Blend Tool (W)
          </strong>
          , select both shapes and apply a blend of
          <strong>
           6 Steps
          </strong>
          . I change the colors of the hexagons before applying the blend so the blend is easier to see.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-003.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-003" height="552" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-003.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h3>
          Step 2
         </h3>
         <p>
          With your newly blended group selected, go to
          <strong>
           Object &gt; Expand
          </strong>
          , and
          <strong>
           Expand
          </strong>
          the group so you have
          <strong>
           eight
          </strong>
          stacked objects.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="http://wp-vector.tutsplus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Hex-004.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-004" height="755" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-004.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h2>
          <span class="sectionnum">
           3.
          </span>
          Divide into Sections
         </h2>
         <h3>
          Step 1
         </h3>
         <p>
          Use the
          <strong>
           Line Segment Tool (\)
          </strong>
          , draw a straight path in the center of the hexagon, bisecting it horizontally, from left corner to right corner. Draw two additional line segments from the top left corner to the bottom right corner and another from the top right corner to the bottom left corner.
          <strong>
           Zoom
          </strong>
          in as needed to get the placement of the paths just right.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-005.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-005" height="810" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-005.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h3>
          Step 2
         </h3>
         <p>
          Select the lines and hexagons and hit
          <strong>
           Divide
          </strong>
          in the
          <strong>
           Pathfinder
          </strong>
          panel. You should be left with each of the
          <strong>
           eight
          </strong>
          hexagons having been cut into
          <strong>
           six
          </strong>
          individual sections.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-006.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-006" height="835" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-006.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h2>
          <span class="sectionnum">
           4.
          </span>
          Coloring the Sections
         </h2>
         <h3>
          Step 1
         </h3>
         <p>
          Before we change the color of each of the hexagon's sections, let's figure out a color palette. The one I've chosen is as follows:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Blue:
           <strong>
            C
           </strong>
           65
           <strong>
            M
           </strong>
           23
           <strong>
            Y
           </strong>
           35
           <strong>
            K
           </strong>
           0
          </li>
          <li>
           Taupe:
           <strong>
            C
           </strong>
           13
           <strong>
            M
           </strong>
           13
           <strong>
            Y
           </strong>
           30
           <strong>
            K
           </strong>
           0
          </li>
          <li>
           Peach:
           <strong>
            C
           </strong>
           0
           <strong>
            M
           </strong>
           32
           <strong>
            Y
           </strong>
           54
           <strong>
            K
           </strong>
           0
          </li>
          <li>
           Salmon:
           <strong>
            C
           </strong>
           0
           <strong>
            M
           </strong>
           64
           <strong>
            Y
           </strong>
           42
           <strong>
            K
           </strong>
           0
          </li>
          <li>
           Rose:
           <strong>
            C
           </strong>
           30
           <strong>
            M
           </strong>
           79
           <strong>
            Y
           </strong>
           36
           <strong>
            K
           </strong>
           4
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          I chose print-ready CMYK colors in order to make sure this design would be ready with the colors I wanted for printing later.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-007.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-007" height="348" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-007.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h3>
          Step 2
         </h3>
         <p>
          Ungroup the hexagon shapes and sections. Select three of the first hexagon's sections and set the color to the blue from the palette in the previous step. Select the other three and set the fill color to the taupe from the step above. Continue selecting three pieces at a time changing their colors so no two colors of the same hue touch (aside from at their corners).
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-008.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-008" height="552" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-008.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h3>
          Step 3
         </h3>
         <p>
          As you select pieces and apply new fill colors change up the order in which they appear in each hexagon. For instance, sometimes the color order may go taupe, salmon, taupe and other times it could be a successions of different colors like blue, peach, and rose.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-009.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-009" height="537" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-009.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h3>
          Step 4
         </h3>
         <p>
          The number of steps you applied in the blend in
          <strong>
           Section 2, Step 1
          </strong>
          contributes directly to the amount of sections you'll have to recolor. The more sections and the more colors you add, the more complex your design will be. When using muted colors like these, having a more complex design reads well and it's too busy, vibrant, or loud. If you're using brighter colors you might want to limit the palette and number of sections. This, of course, is entirely up to you.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-010.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-010" height="729" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-010.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h2>
          <span class="sectionnum">
           5.
          </span>
          Final Touches and Pattern
         </h2>
         <h3>
          Step 1
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Group (Control-G)
          </strong>
          together your hexagon sections once you've finished recoloring them.
          <strong>
           Copy (Control-C)
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           Paste (Control-V)
          </strong>
          the hexagon group. We'll call the first group
          <strong>
           Hexagon A
          </strong>
          and the copied group
          <strong>
           Hexagon B
          </strong>
          .
          <strong>
           Align
          </strong>
          groups A and B.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-011.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-011" height="455" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-011.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h3>
          Step 2
         </h3>
         <p>
          Apply a
          <strong>
           Linear Gradient
          </strong>
          to
          <strong>
           Hexagon B
          </strong>
          with the
          <strong>
           Gradient
          </strong>
          panel going from Purple (
          <strong>
           C60 M86 Y45 K42
          </strong>
          ) to Creamy Yellow (
          <strong>
           C0 M13 Y57 K0
          </strong>
          ). Note how many of the gradients are angled in different directions. This is intentional.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-012.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-012" height="466" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-012.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h3>
          Step 3
         </h3>
         <p>
          In the
          <strong>
           Transparency
          </strong>
          panel reduce
          <strong>
           Hexagon B's
          </strong>
          <strong>
           Opacity
          </strong>
          to
          <strong>
           53%
          </strong>
          and change its
          <strong>
           Blending Mode
          </strong>
          to
          <strong>
           Color Burn
          </strong>
          .
          <strong>
           Group
          </strong>
          together
          <strong>
           Hexagons A
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           B
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-013.jpg">
           <img alt="Hex-013" height="972" src="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-013.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
         </figure>
         <h3>
          Step 4
         </h3>
         <p>
          Select your unified hexagon group and in the
          <strong>
           Pattern Options
          </strong>
          panel go to
          <strong>
           Options
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           Make Pattern
          </strong>
          . Name your new pattern and choose
          <strong>
           Hex by Column
          </strong>
          as the
          <strong>
           Tile Type
          </strong>
          . At the top of your document's window you should have the option to save this pattern; do so.
         </p>
         <figure>
          <a href="https://cdn.tutsplus.com/vector/uploads/2014/03/Hex-014.jpg">
