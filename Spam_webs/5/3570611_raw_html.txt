         </h1>

         <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="4927911" data-link="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/2015/05/01/things-to-do-in-vaucluse-france/" data-summary="As picturesque vineyards, brilliant lavender fields, and lively festivals are attracting tourists to Provence for the spring and summer peak season, I'm reflecting on our wonderful visit there in December and particularly the Vaucluse area in the northwestern part of the region." data-title="Off-Season Provence: Things to Do in Vaucluse">
         </div>
         <p>
          As picturesque vineyards, brilliant lavender fields, and lively festivals are attractingtourists to Provence for the spring and summer peak season,Im reflecting on our wonderful visit there in December and particularly the
          <a href="http://www.provenceguide.co.uk/" target="_blank">
           Vaucluse area
          </a>
          in the northwestern part of the region.You may have already read other stories about our trip that began in
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/2015/02/18/capital-of-culture-highlights-of-marseille/" target="_blank">
           Marseille
          </a>
          and ended in
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/2015/02/02/cassis-france-by-the-sea/" target="_blank">
           Cassis
          </a>
          with many memorable placesin between. Here are a few highlights of things to do inVaucluse that made our trip special.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Our top things to do in Vaucluse
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Visit UNESCO World Heritage Sites
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Key to Avignons designation as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Palais des Papes (Palace of the Popes) is an interesting and very important part of European and papal history.
         </p>
         <p>
          The story of the popes in Avignon begins in the 13
          <sup>
           th
          </sup>
          century when the King of France had a feud with Pope Boniface VIII. After the popesdeath and months later the death of his successor, Clement V, a Frenchman, was elected pope and he chose to stay in France rather than move to Rome. Between1309 to 1377, seven popes lived in the
          <a href="http://www.palais-des-papes.com/en" target="_blank">
           Palais des Papes
          </a>
          in Avignon, not Rome. During this period, Avignonbecame a major capital of the world with its population rising from about 5,000 to over 30,000.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22189" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Palaid-des-Papes.jpg">
           <img alt="Cathedral of Notre Dame des Doms and Palais des Papes (Palace of the Popes), Avignon" class="wp-image-22189 size-full" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Palaid-des-Papes.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Cathedral of Notre Dame des Doms and Palais des Papes (Palace of the Popes), Avignon
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Surrounded by 4.5-km of ancient ramparts, the palace took almost 20 years to build during the papacies of Benedict XII and Clement VI, and its the largest Gothic palace in Europe.There are 25 rooms open to the public, including the private apartments of the pope and grand rooms that were used for formal ceremonies and banquets. The adjacent Romanesque Cathedral of Notre Dame des Doms is also included in the UNESCO World Heritage designation.
         </p>
         <p>
          Frescoes in the papal apartments, sculptures, and other design features represent the work of manyartistswhocame to Avignon during Pope Clement VIs papacy.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22214" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/palais-des-papes-art.jpg">
           <img alt="Mosaic tiles, frescoes and sculptures: St. Catherine of Siena (bottom right) was instrumental in bringing the papacy back to Rome" class="wp-image-22214 size-full" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/palais-des-papes-art.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Mosaic tiles, frescoes and sculptures (St. Catherine of Siena [right] was instrumental in bringing the papacy back to Rome.)
          </p>
         </div>
         Below leftis one of my favorite scenes from inside the palace where I could imagine the popes sitting and gazing through the window at the Provenal countryside. The Window of Indulgences on the right is where the pope blessed the faithful in the central courtyard outside below.
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22481" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Palais-des-Papes-windows1.jpg">
           <img alt="Windows overlooking the Provenal countryside; Window of Indulgences in the Palais des Papes in Avignon, France" class="wp-image-22481 size-full" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Palais-des-Papes-windows1.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Windows overlooking the Provenal countryside; Window of Indulgences
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Another famous and beautiful site included in the Avignon UNESCO designation is the Pont Saint-Bnzet, known as Pont dAvignon. You may have heard of the15
          <sup>
           th
          </sup>
          century childrens song about the Pont dAvignon. It begins,
          <em>
           Sur le pont dAvignon on y danse, on y danse
          </em>
          which translates to
          <em>
           
          </em>
          On the bridge of Avignon, we all dance . The song addedto the allure of the bridge for me.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22192" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC04790.jpg">
           <img alt="Pont St. Benezet (Pont d'Avignon)" class="wp-image-22192 size-full" height="260" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC04790.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Pont St. Benezet (Pont dAvignon) .. On the bridge of Avignon, we all dance there in a ring
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Legend has it that Saint Benezet was divinely inspired as a 12 year old boy to build abridge in Avignon and that a very large stonehe inexplicably threw into the water became the support for the first arch. Originally built with wood in the 12
          <sup>
           th
          </sup>
          century, it wasrebuilt in stone in the 13
          <sup>
           th
          </sup>
          century. Successiveflooding of the Rhne River continued to damage the bridge and now only 4 of the original 22 arches remain.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Browse and shop at Les Halles
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          40 local retailers sell Provenalfresh produce at the
          <a href="http://www.avignon-leshalles.com" target="_blank">
           coveredmarket of Les Halles
          </a>
          in Avignon, open Tuesday to Sunday mornings. Its quite a sight to see the vertical garden created by botanistPatrick Blanc on one of the facades. Inside wequickly surveyed the many wonderful vendors of food, wine, flowers and muchmore, wishing we would be staying long enough to partake of the offerings.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22457" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Les-Halles.jpg">
           <img alt="Covered market of Les Halles in Avignon, France" class="size-full wp-image-22457" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Les-Halles.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Covered market of Les Halles
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Walk around Avignon
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Of course, like many of Europes old cities, itwas atreat to just walk around experiencing the
          <a href="http://www.avignon-tourisme.com" target="_blank">
           essence ofAvignon
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22458" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Walking-around-Avignon.jpg">
           <img alt="Walking around Avignon" class="size-full wp-image-22458" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Walking-around-Avignon.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Walking around Avignon
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          We strolled through many of the narrow cobbled streets during the day and in the evening, passing buildings that have been standing for centuries, and crossing public squares quiet on a winters day. The beautiful Opra Grand Avignon (top right above) on the Place de lHorloge
          <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
           ,is the vibrant scene of theatrical, dance, musical, and variety performances.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Indulge in Provenal cuisine and ambiance
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Two of our favorite meals in Provence were in Avignon which was especially nice sincewe were there onMr. TWSs birthday.
         </p>
         <p>
          Librairie lAmi Voyage,a small restaurantwith an upstairs bookshop, was bustling with locals as we ate a hearty lunch accompanied with a local Ctes du Rhne (one of Vaucluses three appellations)vintage from the village of Cairanne in Vaucluse. Lunch was topped off with a special cheesecake that was served with a candle for the birthday boy. It was as much the cozy atmosphere and friendly service as the delicious food that made the meal quite memorable.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22493" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/lunch-avignon.jpg">
           <img alt="Hearty lunch of sausage stew and cheesecake at Librairie L'Ami Voyage, a brasserie in Avignon, France" class="size-full wp-image-22493" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/lunch-avignon.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Hearty lunch of sausage stew and cheesecake at Librairie LAmi Voyage
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Continuing the celebration that evening at
          <a href="http://www.la-fourchette.net/" target="_blank">
           Restaurant La Fourchette
          </a>
          , we had an elegant selection of Provenal dishes, a few shown below. The foodand the presentation were top quality,but again the atmosphere, service and the companyof local diners made it one of our favorites. Mr. TWS was once again treated to dessert with a candle on top.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22495" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/La-Fourchette1.jpg">
           <img alt="Celebrating a birthday at La Fourchette: Scallops St. Jacques, Beef Stew Avignonnaise, sea bream, and chocolate mouse cake" class="size-full wp-image-22495" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/La-Fourchette1.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Celebrating a birthday at La Fourchette: Scallops St. Jacques, Beef Stew Avignonnaise, sea bream, and chocolate mouse cake
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Thewonderful meals and welcoming atmosphere at both restaurants really helped to make Mr. TWSsday special, as if being in Avignonitself wasnt special enough!
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Take a side trip to Carpentras
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Located about 16 miles from Avignon in the heart of the rich agricultural and historical centers of Vaucluse, Carpentras is a maingateway to Mount Ventoux (known as the Giant of Provence and one of the steepest routes of the Tour de France). In addition to the
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/2014/12/23/christmas-in-provence/" target="_blank">
           Christmas festivities and traditions
          </a>
          that we focused on during our December visit, there is much to see and doin Carpentras  museums, markets, parks, and significant landmarks, such as the oldest active synagogue in France. Well look forward to many of these ona future trip.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22460" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Carpentras.jpg">
           <img alt="Carpentras, France at Christmas" class="size-full wp-image-22460" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Carpentras.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Carpentras
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          We stopped at the
          <a href="http://carpentras-ventoux.com" target="_blank">
           Carpentras tourist office
          </a>
          where there is a shop with an impressive selection of fine wines and food products of the region, some of which are available for sampling, such as Sautel, an apritif that you can see I was thoroughly enjoyingbelow.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22488" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Carpentras-tourist-office-shop.jpg">
           <img alt="From top left: honey, Sautel (an apritif), Wines of Provence, coing (quince), and olive oils" class="size-full wp-image-22488" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Carpentras-tourist-office-shop.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           From top left: honey, Sautel (an apritif), Wines of Provence, coing (quince), and olive oils
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Our short time inCarpentras was definitely delicious as we made sure to visit
          <a href="http://www.patisserie-jouvaud.com/" target="_blank">
           PtisserieJouvaud
          </a>
          to admire and sample their confections and pastries.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22523" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Aix-Avignon-Carpentras-Dec-10-20148.jpg">
           <img alt="Confections and pastries at Ptisserie Jouvaud in Carpentras, France" class="size-full wp-image-22523" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Aix-Avignon-Carpentras-Dec-10-20148.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Confections and pastries at Ptisserie Jouvaud
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Visit an olive mill
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Provence is blessed with a plentitude of olive groves. In Gordes, we visited one of the many mills,Moulin du Clos des Jeannons, where we got a private tour of the olive mill byownerAndr Horardwho talked about the ancient processes of olive oil production still used by the mill today.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22468" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/olive-groves-gordes.jpg">
           <img alt="Olive groves of Moulin du Clos des Jeannons in Gordes, Vaucluse department of France" class="size-full wp-image-22468" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/olive-groves-gordes.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Olive groves of Moulin du Clos des Jeannons in Gordes
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          He also talked about the history of the groves and challenges they have faced at times when the fruit has been damaged by weather conditions, but have continued to produce only quality oils.
         </p>
         <p>
          We returned to the unique building designed by Andre that housed a large shop and afforded a view of the olive oil production. Hishospitality couldnt have been more gracious as we sampled three distinctly different oils accompanied by one ofProvences signatureRos wines. We would definitely recommend a stop here if youre in Provence.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22469" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/DSC04832.jpg">
           <img alt="With Andr Horard at Moulin du Clos des Jeannons in Gordes, France" class="size-full wp-image-22469" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/DSC04832.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           With Andr Horard at Moulin du Clos des Jeannons
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Indulge your sweet tooth
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          I think that sweets are always a good idea, but Christmas is a very special time in Provence with a holiday tradition of the 13 Desserts. At the Confiserie Saint Denisin the village of Les Beaumettes, we learned about one of the primary 13 Desserts, crystallized fruit, their specialty, and we also learned about the involved process of making it. We also tried a few of the verysweet samples, but since it was just before lunch we refrained from too many of them!
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_21823" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Avignon-and-Salon-December-11-20141.jpg">
           <img alt="Crystallized fruit from soaking to ready-to-eat at Confiserie Saint Denis in Les Beaumettes, France" class="wp-image-21823 size-full" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Avignon-and-Salon-December-11-20141.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Crystallized fruit from soaking to ready-to-eat at Confiserie Saint Denis
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           See the quiet side of Cavaillon and the Vaucluse countryside
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Our drivefrom Les Baumettes to Cavaillon was lovely, passing vineyards and farms along the way.
          <a href="http://cavaillon-luberon.com" target="_blank">
           Cavaillon is well-known
          </a>
          for its amazing melons and draws tourists for its produce and festivals especially mid-June to September.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22472" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Cavaillon-countryside.jpg">
           <img alt="Sunny winter day in the countryside near Cavaillon, in the Vaucluse department of France" class="size-full wp-image-22472" height="481" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Cavaillon-countryside.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Sunny winter day in the countryside near Cavaillon
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          In the town of Cavaillon, I imagined how busy the streets would be in peak season while we got a glimpse of the townwherelocals were going about their business with no tourists in sight (besides us) that I could see.We took a few minutes to step inside Maison Jarry and talk to the owner while surveying the mouth-watering cakes and chocolates for which the shop is well-known inthe area.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22500" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Maison-Jarry.jpg">
           <img alt="Sonia and her delicious creations at Maison Jarry in Cavaillon" class="size-full wp-image-22500" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Maison-Jarry.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Sonia and her delicious creations at Maison Jarry in Cavaillon
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Les Ths au Soleil in Cavaillon was a surprise in its colorful and eclecticdecor. And the food and wine were delicious. As in Avignon at this time of year, the meals were hearty, and we left fully satisfied. Then it was time to leave the Vaucluse area and head toward our next destination, Salon de Provence.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22475" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Cavaillon-restaurant.jpg">
           <img alt="Les Ths au Soleil, restaurant and tearoom in Cavaillon, France" class="size-full wp-image-22475" height="480" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Cavaillon-restaurant.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Les Ths au Soleil, restaurant and tearoom in Cavaillon
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Idencourage you to think about a visit to Vaucluseanytime, but if youre thinking about going in the less-busy off-season as Mr. TWS and I did, now is the time to start planning.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Tips:
         </h3>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_22453" style="width: 310px">
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Wall-of-Avignon.jpg">
           <img alt="Ancient ramparts and city gate of Avignon, France" class="size-medium wp-image-22453" height="201" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Wall-of-Avignon-300x201.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Ancient ramparts and city gate of Avignon
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Driving and Parking:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          In Avignon, the large indoor parking lot serving the Palais des Papes and several hotels is right inside the city gates, but we actually missed it! Too focused on our iPhones Siri directions to the palace itself, we didnt even see it and ended up driving the narrow streets through the old district. I definitely do not recommend doing this, especially at a slightly busier time of year.
         </p>
         <p>
          In most allof the old cities of France, avoid driving in the historic city centers as these streets and alleys were not designed for the large vehicles and two-way traffic of our day. Use main parking structureslike the one mentioned above, locate smaller lots just on the outskirts of the city, or travel by train or bus between your destinations.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Take time:
          </strong>
          Our timein Avignon and other locations in Vauclusewas intentionally brief due to the nature of our
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/2014/12/23/christmas-in-provence/" target="_blank">
           Christmas tour in Provence
          </a>
          tripand to enable us toprovide our readers with a wide-reaching introduction to the area in the off-season. ButI recommend taking as much time as you can to see more of the main sites, relish the ambiance of Provence in its villages and countryside, and interact with the locals.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Hotel-de-lhorloge.jpg">
           <img alt="Hotel de l'Horloge" class="alignleft wp-image-22502 size-medium" height="300" src="http://travelingwithsweeney.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Hotel-de-lhorloge-225x300.jpg" width="225"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Accommodations:
          </strong>
          There are many accommodation options throughout Vaucluse, but from our brief experience, we know that Avignon is a good choice as a base for visiting much of the area. Staying in the city center of Avignon was ideal for our itinerary of visiting the Christmas markets and historical sites of the city.
          <a href="http://www.hotel-avignon-horloge.com/uk/hotel-luxe-avignon-centre-ville-provence-site-officiel.php" target="_blank">
           Htel de LHorloge
          </a>
          (shown on the left) is a small, independently-owned 4-star hotel in a 19th-century building just off Avignons main square, Place de LHorloge. It was very convenient for our activities. Our room was simply designed, comfortable, and had old-world ambiance. The breakfast buffet was wonderfully diverse with cereals, fruit, and pastries to start our day.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           And dont forget to dance on the Pont dAvignon!
          </strong>
         </p>
