     <p>
      <b>
       Uwe Boll
      </b>
      (
      <small>
       German pronunciation:
      </small>
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">
        [uv bl]
       </a>
      </span>
      ; June 22, 1965)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Curr_Bio_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Curr_Bio-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is a German
      <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
       director
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">
       producer
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">
       screenwriter
      </a>
      based in Canada, whose work includes several films adapted from
      <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">
       video games
      </a>
      . He finances his own films through his
      <a href="/wiki/Boll_KG" title="Boll KG">
       Boll KG
      </a>
      and Event Film Productions production companies. Many of his films are produced on low budgets.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uwe_Boll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Boll was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Wermelskirchen" title="Wermelskirchen">
       Wermelskirchen
      </a>
      . He studied at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cologne" title="University of Cologne">
       University of Cologne
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Siegen" title="University of Siegen">
       University of Siegen
      </a>
      , and holds a
      <a href="/wiki/Doctorate" title="Doctorate">
       doctorate
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Literature" title="Literature">
       literature
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      He first decided to go into the movie business at ten years old, after seeing
      <a href="/wiki/Marlon_Brando" title="Marlon Brando">
       Marlon Brando
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Mutiny_on_the_Bounty_(1962_film)" title="Mutiny on the Bounty (1962 film)">
        Mutiny on the Bounty
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lrr_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-lrr-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Career">
       Career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uwe_Boll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Films">
       Films
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uwe_Boll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Films">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Boll's first two major releases were the horror movie
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Blackwoods_(film)" title="Blackwoods (film)">
        Blackwoods
       </a>
      </i>
      and the drama
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Heart_of_America_(film)" title="Heart of America (film)">
        Heart of America
       </a>
      </i>
      , both of which he directed and co-wrote.
     </p>
     <p>
      Boll is best known for loosely adapting
      <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">
       video games
      </a>
      into movies, having directed and produced a number of such adaptations, including
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/House_of_the_Dead_(film)" title="House of the Dead (film)">
        House of the Dead
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Alone_in_the_Dark_(2005_film)" title="Alone in the Dark (2005 film)">
        Alone in the Dark
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Alone_in_the_Dark_II_(film)" title="Alone in the Dark II (film)">
        Alone in the Dark II
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/BloodRayne_(film)" title="BloodRayne (film)">
        BloodRayne
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BloodRayne_II:_Deliverance" title="BloodRayne II: Deliverance">
        BloodRayne II: Deliverance
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/BloodRayne:_The_Third_Reich" title="BloodRayne: The Third Reich">
        BloodRayne: The Third Reich
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/In_the_Name_of_the_King:_A_Dungeon_Siege_Tale" title="In the Name of the King: A Dungeon Siege Tale">
        In the Name of the King: A Dungeon Siege Tale
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Postal_(film)" title="Postal (film)">
        Postal
       </a>
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Far_Cry_(film)" title="Far Cry (film)">
        Far Cry
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In the opening credits to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Seed_(2007_film)" title="Seed (2007 film)">
        Seed
       </a>
      </i>
      (2007), Boll used footage of animal abuse and torture he acquired from
      <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">
       PETA
      </a>
      to underscore the film's
      <a href="/wiki/Nihilism" title="Nihilism">
       nihilism
      </a>
      . He has also promised to donate 2.5 percent of his net profits from
      <i>
       Seed
      </i>
      to PETA.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In September 2010, a trailer for Boll's film, titled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Auschwitz_(film)" title="Auschwitz (film)">
        Auschwitz
       </a>
      </i>
      , about the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auschwitz" title="Auschwitz">
       concentration camp
      </a>
      , was posted on YouTube. The trailer, in which Boll appears as an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS" title="SS">
       SS
      </a>
      gas-chamber guard, contains explicit scenes of the brutalization and killing of concentration camp inmates.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Boll has been quoted as saying that films such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Schindler%27s_List" title="Schindler's List">
        Schindler's List
       </a>
      </i>
      "no longer had the ability to reach young people and that it was his duty as a German to make the film as a way of confronting the past."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Boll is the subject of a documentary film titled
      <i>
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Raging_Boll_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Raging Boll (film) (page does not exist)">
        Raging Boll
       </a>
      </i>
      , directed by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Lee_West&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dan Lee West (page does not exist)">
       Dan Lee West
      </a>
      , which premiered at the
      <a href="/wiki/Austin_Film_Festival" title="Austin Film Festival">
       Austin Film Festival
      </a>
      in October 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wrap_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-wrap-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <h3>

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Boll's films have often performed poorly at the box office in the United States and around the world.
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/House_of_the_Dead_(film)" title="House of the Dead (film)">
        House of the Dead
       </a>
      </i>
      , which was budgeted at $12 million, made $5.73 million in its opening weekend,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Alone_in_the_Dark_(2005_film)" title="Alone in the Dark (2005 film)">
        Alone in the Dark
       </a>
      </i>
      , which was budgeted at $20 million, made $5.1 million,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/BloodRayne_(film)" title="BloodRayne (film)">
        BloodRayne
       </a>
      </i>
      , which was made for $25 million, made $2.42 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The least profitable commercial performance of his career was
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/In_the_Name_of_the_King:_A_Dungeon_Siege_Tale" title="In the Name of the King: A Dungeon Siege Tale">
        In the Name of the King: A Dungeon Siege Tale
       </a>
      </i>
      , which made barely $10 million worldwide at the box office on a $60 million budget.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the DVD commentary of
      <i>
       Alone in the Dark
      </i>
      , Boll explains how he funds his films: "Maybe you know it but it's not so easy to finance movies in total. And the reason I am able to do these kind of movies is I have a tax shelter fund in Germany, and if you invest in a movie in Germany you get basically fifty percent back from the government."
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Response_to_criticism">
       Response to criticism
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uwe_Boll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Response to criticism">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Boll does not shy away from his critics. In the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Alone_in_the_Dark_(2005_film)" title="Alone in the Dark (2005 film)">
        Alone in the Dark
       </a>
      </i>
      DVD commentary, he responds to criticism that his adaptations make significant changes to the plot and style of the source material: "Fans are always totally flipping out and I understand that the fan of a video game has his own agenda in his head and has his ideas about what is a good movie and what is a bad movie." Referring to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/House_of_the_Dead_(film)" title="House of the Dead (film)">
        House of the Dead
       </a>
      </i>
      , Boll said: "I think I made a perfect
      <i>
       House of the Dead
      </i>
      movie, because it really shows how the game is. It's a lot of fun, it's over-the-top action."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EuroGamer_26-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-EuroGamer-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Boll is especially critical of his Internet detractors. Referring to two
      <a href="/wiki/Ain%27t_It_Cool_News" title="Ain't It Cool News">
       Ain't It Cool News
      </a>
      critics who negatively reviewed his work, Boll said, "
      <a href="/wiki/Harry_Knowles" title="Harry Knowles">
       Harry
      </a>
      (Knowles) and Quint (Eric Vespe) are retards."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1Up_27-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-1Up-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Boll also criticizes the game companies themselves for not providing support to the production following the selling of the film rights. He cites the cross-promotion and support which comic book-based properties adapted for the screen receive, whereas video game companies often "sell off the license and then forget about it."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EuroGamer_26-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-EuroGamer-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He argues that this is the reason video game adaptations are not well received by critics and audiences.
     </p>
     <p>
      When
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Wired_(magazine)" title="Wired (magazine)">
        Wired
       </a>
      </i>
      published a negative review of
      <i>
       Postal
      </i>
      , Boll responded with an e-mail claiming that the critic didn't "understand anything about movies and that you are an untalented wannabe filmmaker with no balls and no understanding what POSTAL is. You don't see courage because you are nothing. and go to your mom and fuck her ...because she cooks for you now since 30 years ..so she deserves it" [sic].
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SwearingWired_28-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-SwearingWired-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Boll stated that this angry email was sparked not by the review, but because the
      <i>
       Wired
      </i>
      editor told Boll in person that the editor loved the movie then published a negative review.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UweExplains_29-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-UweExplains-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Critic_boxing_matches_.28Raging_Boll.29">

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Boll made headlines by challenging his critics to "put up or shut up". In June 2006, his production company issued a press release stating that Boll would challenge his five harshest critics each to a 10-round boxing match. Invitations were also open to film directors
      <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">
       Quentin Tarantino
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Roger_Avary" title="Roger Avary">
       Roger Avary
      </a>
      . To qualify, critics had to have written two extremely negative reviews of Boll, in print or on the Web. In 2005, footage from the fights were to be included on the DVD of his upcoming film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Postal_(film)" title="Postal (film)">
        Postal
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sknr1_30-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-sknr1-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On June 20, 2006,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rich_%22Lowtax%22_Kyanka" title='Rich "Lowtax" Kyanka'>
       Rich "Lowtax" Kyanka
      </a>
      stated on
      <a href="/wiki/Something_Awful" title="Something Awful">
       Something Awful
      </a>
      that he had been invited by Boll to be the first contestant, after Kyanka reviewed
      <i>
       Alone in the Dark
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SomethingAwful2_31-0">

         [
        </span>

       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/GoldenPalace.com" title="GoldenPalace.com">

      <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">

      </a>
      film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Raging_Bull" title="Raging Bull">
        Raging Bull
       </a>
      </i>

      <i>
       Rue Morgue
      </i>
      magazine writer Chris Alexander, webmaster of
      <i>
       Cinecutre
      </i>
      Carlos Palencia Jimenez-Arguello,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ain%27t_it_Cool_News" title="Ain't it Cool News">
        Ain't it Cool News
       </a>
      </i>
      writer Jeff Sneider, and Chance Minter, amateur boxer and website critic.

        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>

       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/Estepona" title="Estepona">
       Estepona
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
       Spain
      </a>
      , against Carlos Palencia.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>

        34
        <span>
         ]

       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

     <p>
      After Kyanka lost his match, he would go on to make several allegations against Boll, including the fact that Boll refused to fight against Chance Minter who was an experienced amateur boxer. However, Boll fought Minter as his fourth opponent.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">

        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]

        <span>

      <i>

He also stated that Boll was an "insane, two-fisted rogue, and a shockingly honest one at that, someone who absolutely adores film, knows its history and truly lives for what he does."

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Petition_to_retire">
       Petition to retire
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uwe_Boll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Petition to retire">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In April 2008,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">
        The Guardian
       </a>
      </i>
      ran an article claiming Boll had promised to retire if an online petition at PetitionOnline.com asking him to do so received 1,000,000 signatures.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">
       <a href="#cite_note-42">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        42
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On May 7, 2008, the makers of
      <a href="/wiki/Stride_(gum)" title="Stride (gum)">
       Stride gum
      </a>
      announced they would give each signer a digital coupon for a pack of gum if the petition obtained the required 1 million signatures by May 14, 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
       <a href="#cite_note-43">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        43
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      As of July 22, 2012, there are around 353,835 signatures.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">
       <a href="#cite_note-44">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        44
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In an interview with Mike Gencarelli of the
      <i>
       Movie Mikes
      </i>
      website on March 22, 2010, Boll stated that he would not retire, should the petition receive one million signatures, commenting:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       I think no, it has been too long. If they would have made it to a million in like 2 months, then they would have had something. They even got sponsored by that gum factory. I felt like it's three years later, forget it. I also felt that people signed numerous times on the petition so it is probably only like 150,000 people that actually signed it.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gencarelli_45-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-gencarelli-45">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         45
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <h4>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Response">
       Response
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uwe_Boll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Response">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h4>
     <p>
      As part of a publicity stunt for
      <i>
       Postal
      </i>
      , Uwe Boll released a video stating that he is "the only genius in the whole fucking [movie] business" and that other directors such as
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bay" title="Michael Bay">
       Michael Bay
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Eli_Roth" title="Eli Roth">
       Eli Roth
      </a>
      are "fucking retards".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">
       <a href="#cite_note-46">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        46
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He promised that his film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Postal_(film)" title="Postal (film)">
        Postal
       </a>
      </i>
      would be "way better than all that social-critic
      <a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">
       George Clooney
      </a>
      bullshit that you get every fucking weekend". In response to an "Anti-Uwe Boll" online petition, Boll has also expressed hopes that somebody will start a Pro-Uwe Boll petition, which he would expect to hit a million signatures. As of July 22, 2012, the pro-Uwe Boll petition with the most signatures is the
      <i>
       Long Live Uwe Boll
      </i>
      poll with a total of 7,631 signatures.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47">
       <a href="#cite_note-47">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        47
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bay responded to the "fucking retards" comment by calling Boll "a sad being" and stated that he did not care "in the slightest" about the remark,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48">
       <a href="#cite_note-48">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        48
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      while Roth facetiously described Boll's comments as the "greatest compliment ever". Boll later noted that the comments were meant to be a generic picture over Hollywood, and that he has nothing against the people mentioned.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49">
       <a href="#cite_note-49">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        49
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Boll then appeared on Thursday, April 10's episode of G4's
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attack_of_the_Show" title="Attack of the Show">
        Attack of the Show
       </a>
      </i>
      , where he was interviewed in the nude regarding his controversial online retort. In the interview, he said (in regard to Michael Bay and Eli Roth responding to his criticisms) that Roth "has a sense of humor" and that Bay "has no sense of humor". He also jokingly stated that he believes that his upcoming adaptation of
      <i>
       Postal
      </i>
      (from the video game of the same name) could beat
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Jones_and_the_Kingdom_of_the_Crystal_Skull" title="Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull">
        Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull
       </a>
      </i>
      at the box office.
     </p>
     <p>
      On April 27, 2008, Boll responded to Bay's "not caring about Boll" comment. "To prove who is the better director", Boll offered to challenge Bay to a boxing match at Mandalay Bay in Las Vegas. If Bay accepted, the match would last for 12 rounds and would take place in September.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      In response to Boll's comments, Bay again posted a statement, this time saying:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       I never even heard his name till last week when he made threats and rants. The guy is a fucking idiot, making threats to me, Clooney, Eli Roth, says he has a doctoratebut uses the word "retard" in his vocabulary, come on. When you look at his videos, what is interesting are the backgrounds. I guess his low rent offices, with 15 year old 3/4 machines, archaic computers, this is just some dumb chump trying to get some fame when he has none, so he has to make YouTube lame quality anger rants. This guy just wants attention because he can't get any for the so called movies he makes. Nothing sadder when he had his screening in LA to an over half empty movie house.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50">
        <a href="#cite_note-50">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         50
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      Recently, Boll told everyone to "fuck yourself" in a viral video on YouTube, targeting those who did not fund his film;
      <i>
       Rampage 3
      </i>
      , on
      <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">
       Kickstarter
      </a>
      as he was unable to secure enough funds.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51">
       <a href="#cite_note-51">
        <span>
