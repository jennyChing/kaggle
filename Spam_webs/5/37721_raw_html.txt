         <div class="entry">
          <p>
           <i>
            In case you missed it
           </i>
            Dungeons and Dragons co-creator Dave Arneson has died.
          </p>
          <div class="widget ad_position widget-ad-position-A300 widget-fake">
           <div class="hdn-ad-position ad-A300 hdn-ap-visible-mobile">
            <div class="juice-ad-position juice-ad-position-A300">
             <script id="A300-0-96211300-1436887747" type="text/javascript">
              window.ad_position_widget.place_named_ad('A300', 'A300-0-96211300-1436887747');
             </script>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           It was the second blow to fans of the addictive role-playing gamein about a year. Gary Gygax, the more famous face behind the 25-year-old classic,
           <a href="http://blog.seattlepi.com/thebigblog/archives/133422.asp">
            died in March of last year
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Gygax, 69, had been suffering from various health problems. Arneson, 61, died of cancer. He passed away shortly after 11 p.m. April 7, according to a statement sent by his family to a couple
           <a href="http://grognardia.blogspot.com/2009/04/sadly-this-is-accurate.html">
            gaming
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.dorktower.com/2009/04/08/rest-in-peace-dave/">
            bloggers
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Arneson was a driving force behind D&amp;Ds creation and his contribution to the world of adventure gaming should not be underestimated, wrote Matt Blum of
           <a href="http://blog.wired.com/geekdad/2009/04/rip-dave-arneso.html">
            Wired Magazine
           </a>
           . It was Arnesons spark that transformed Gygaxs game Chainmail into the first edition of D&amp;D, and begat everything that followed.
          </p>
         </div>
