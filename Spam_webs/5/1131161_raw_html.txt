        <h1 class="headline">
         How a Distance Runner Turned a Shoe Company into a Billion-Dollar Brand with Angel Martinez
        </h1>
        <a class="num_comments_link" href="http://lewishowes.com/podcast/angel-martinez/#comments" rel="nofollow">
         <span class="num_comments">
          13
         </span>
         comments

       <div class="post_content">
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignright" height="269" src="http://lewishowes.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/SchoolOfGreatnessPodcastArt.jpg" width="269"/>
         You probably havent heard of Deckers Outdoors Corporation.But you probably have heard of Ugg (think boots). And Teva (hiker sandals). And Sanuk (beach flip flops).
        </p>
        <p>
         So, indirectly, youve heard of one of the most successful footwear brands in existence right now.
        </p>
        <p>
         Today on
         <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-school-of-greatness/id596047499" target="_blank" title="The School of Greatness">
          The School of Greatness
         </a>
         you get you hear the amazing story of how this billion-dollar footwear company, Deckers Brands, came to be from the CEO himself,
         <strong>
          Angel Martinez
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         If you havent heard me mention it yet, Im writing a book (
         <em>
          and its going to be big
         </em>
         ).
        </p>
        <p>
         I wanted to interview Angel for part of my book, so I got the privilege to sit down with him a few months ago to hear his story.
        </p>
        <p>
         After being left by both his parents as a toddler in Cuba, Angel was brought by his guardians to New York City, where he grew up as a distance runner.
        </p>
        <p>
         He later became one of the first running store owners on the West Coast, but his real talent for building something big happened after that.
        </p>
        <p>
         He became a sales rep for Reebok in the eighties (when it was nothing) and turned it into a more successful brand than Nike.
        </p>
        <p>
         But his accomplishments and wisdom go far beyond that.
        </p>
        <p>
         Angels story is a fascinating one, and in this interview he opens up and shares both personal and business insights that are invaluable if youre trying to build something big.
        </p>
        <p>
         I want you to take notes because theres no one better to learn solid business and life principles from than this man.
        </p>
        <p>
         Welcome to the incredible story of
         <strong>
          Angel Martinez
         </strong>
         in Episode 124.
        </p>

          <strong style="font-size: 1.5em; line-height: 19px;">
           The School of Greatness Podcast

        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Angel Martinez on The School of Greatness" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3568" height="620" src="http://lewishowes.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/111-The-School-of-Greatness-Angel-Martinez-Lewis-Howes.png" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://ctt.ec/w4gbU" target="_blank">
           If you dont understand what it takes to live a life with some integrity, youre not going to be successful in business.
          </a>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <h2>
         Some questions I ask:
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          What was the dream for you growing up?
         </li>
         <li>
          Do you apply the same principles from running to your business?
         </li>
         <li>
          Who was your most valuable mentor growing up?
         </li>
         <li>
          How do you get people to do what you need them to do without being loud about it?
         </li>
         <li>
          How does a great leader continue to have a huge vision and be successful when they go through an extremely emotional experience?
         </li>
         <li>
          How do you keep from getting comfortable once youre so successful?
         </li>
         <li>
          What are some of your blind spots as a leader?
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         In This Episode, You Will Learn:
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          What running in school taught him about having no limits
         </li>
         <li>
          His opinion on why long-distance runners are so self-directed
         </li>
         <li>
          How the two rules of cross-country running have stayed with him:
          <br/>
          1- No walking
          <br/>
          2- You cant drop out of the race
         </li>
        </ul>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://ctt.ec/bc22G" target="_blank">
           Running is that constant confrontation of a challenge every day.
          </a>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <ul>
         <li>
          About his involvement in the very beginnings of the running shoe industry in America (before Nike was called Nike)
         </li>
         <li>
          How he got the job of managing the first running shoe specialty store on the West Coast
         </li>
         <li>
          The story of how he got introduced to Reebok and took their sales from $13 million to $300 million in a year and a half
         </li>
        </ul>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://ctt.ec/dB8aw" target="_blank">
           Its easier to come to a place than come from a place.
          </a>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <ul>
         <li>
          That women bought eight times the amount of shoes men did in the 80s (probably still true)
         </li>
         <li>
          How aerobics was a vehicle for women to discover the power they could get from physical activity
         </li>
         <li>
          His thoughts on how Reebok lost its lead over Nike
         </li>
         <li>
          About the product he created and sold to 3M (who invented Post-Its)
         </li>
         <li>
          Plus much more
         </li>
        </ul>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://ctt.ec/8pAa6" target="_blank">
           We need to become more givers, not just takers and makers.
          </a>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
