      <h1 class="post-single__title">
       Are all banks the same?
      </h1>
      <div class="post-single__content">
       <p>
        This is a question people ask me all the timeand its not an easy one to answer, but using your reviews, we can have a go at answering it.
       </p>
       <p>
        We'vefocused on the Big Four banks. Why? Well, because they dominate the banking market in the UK - they have 77% of the personal current account market and 85% of the business current account market. This means that for many of us, these banks are all we know.
       </p>
       <p>
        To help us answer the question weve used the Value Disciplines Model, see the geeky bit below if you want to know more about this model.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         So heres what youve told us:
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         The shape for each of the Big Four banks is roughly the same.
         <strong>
          This means that for most customers, there really isnt much difference these banks
         </strong>
         . One may be slightly better, but they all kind of look the same
        </li>
        <li>
         Your customer reviews have shown that
         <strong>
          none of these banks have demonstrated strong leadership in any one of the three value disciplines
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         While
         <strong>
          Barclays comes out top across all 3 value disciplines
         </strong>
         , it was a fairly close run result
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          The
         </strong>
         <strong>
          biggest difference can be seen in the Product Leadership
         </strong>
         value discipline (the lowest score here was HSBC with 1.2. Barclays come out top with 2.9)
        </li>
        <li>
         This suggests that
         <strong>
          banks are working hard to differentiate their products first and then their brands
         </strong>
         . Weve seen this to be particularly true in the competitive current account space, were cashback, extra benefits such as travel insurance, and good interest rates are helping to win customers. Take a look at your most recommended current accounts:
         <a href="http://smartmoneypeople.com/compare?query=currentaccount&amp;filter=rating">
          http://smartmoneypeople.com/compare?query=currenta...
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         The geeky bit!
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Weve used your reviews and the Value Disciplines Model (Michael Treacy and Fred Wiersema). The model says that there are three key value disciplines for companies, Operational Excellence, Product Leadership and Customer Intimacy. Companies must choose one of the value disciplines and act upon it consistently. This doesnt mean that the other 2 dimensions dont matter, just that being ok is enough.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          Operational Excellence:
         </em>
        </strong>
        Great operations/execution, with a focus on providing a reasonable quality at a low price
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          Produce Leadership:
         </em>
        </strong>
        Very strong innovation and marketing. This normally isnt a long-term source of advantage as competitors just copy what works well
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          Customer Intimacy:
         </em>
        </strong>
        Customer service and attention rock! Aim to match and exceed customer expectations
       </p>
       <p>
        How does your bank or insurance company differentiate? Tell us:
        <a href="http://smartmoneypeople.com/search?review">
         http://smartmoneypeople.com/search?review
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Our Mission
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Our mission is to increase trust and transparency in the Financial Services industry; to help make it work better for everybody. After just 4 weeks, your reviews are helping other customers to make better informed decisions.
       </p>
       <p>
        Natasha
       </p>
