        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Paris in 3 days ! What to do
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <a class="author" href="http://vellichor.org/archives/author/swaran" rel="author">
          Swaran
         </a>
         /
         <a class="entry-date" href="http://vellichor.org/archives/954">
          18/10/2014
         </a>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <span class="wpsr_floatbts_anchor" data-offset="40">
        </span>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Art collections
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Paris is the capital of art collections. You will find all forms of art, from all times, from all around the world. The gates of Babylon and the famous winged bulls, the most beautiful Apsaras from Angkor Wat, the Mona Lisa and the most representative works of the 20th century, theyre all in Paris !
        </p>
        <p>
         Unfortunately, you shall not have enough time so here is what we think you should see
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
          Musee dOrsay
         </strong>
          The Museum dOrsay is THE must-see museum in Paris thanks to the single most impressive collection of Impressionist period paintings on earth! Wander from room to room and gawp at the works from the most famous of artists including Edouard Manet, Claude Monet and Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec. Within the former railway station there are also works by Pierre-Auguste Renoir, Edgar Degas, Vincent van Gogh, Camille Claudel and many many more!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 646px">
         <img class="" height="427" src="https://c2.staticflickr.com/2/1250/4725795882_e6d290ea57_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Le Muse dOrsay
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Centre Pompidou
         </strong>
          The Renzo Piano and Richard Rogers designed Centre Pompidou (also known as Beaubourg) is one of the most iconic and colourful pieces of architecture in Paris. Inside youll find the largest collection of modern art in Europe  some 50,000 works of art by 5,000 artists, representing Primitivism, Fauvism, Cubism, Dadaism, Surrealism, American Color-Field painting and Abstract Expressionism, of which only a fraction  about 600 works  can be seen for real at any one time.
         <br/>
         Avoid the queues by coming after 6pm (the museum is open until 9pm  sometimes later during temporary exhibitions).
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 561px">
         <img class="" height="370" src="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/image3.jpg" width="555"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Le Centre Pompidou
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Musee de lOrangerie  Its an art gallery featuring various impressionist and post-impressionist works and above all  Claude Monets famous Water Lilies. Some of the huge paintings are exposed in the same room, in order to see Monets point of view in his own garden in Giverny at different times of the day.
         <br/>
         This imposing building is located beside the Tuileries Gardens on Paris Place de la Concorde.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 3082px">
         <img class="" height="2304" src="http://bebgalerie.voila.net/images/photos/nympheas_sud.JPG" width="3072"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Monets Water Lillies
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Monuments
         </strong>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 606px">
         <img class="" height="400" src="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Admire-the-view-of-Paris-from-the-Notre-Dame-Cathedral-in-France.jpg" width="600"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          View from Notre Dame
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Notre Dame Cathedral  The iconic Notre Dame Cathedral has featured in many films thanks to its handsome Gothic architecture, which was built between the 12th and the 14th century. Catch mass here, admire the intricate gargoyles and climb the 400 steps up to that infamous belltower.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 606px">
         <img class="" height="400" src="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Notre-Dame-Cathedral-and-the-River-Seine-in-Paris.jpg" width="600"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          View of the river Seine and Notre Dame
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Sacre-Coeur  Sitting proudly atop the Montmartre hill is the mighty Sacred Heart Basilica of Montmartre, aka Sacre-Coeur. Admire the panoramic views of Paris below and if youre feeling energetic climb the basilicas dome for an elevated view.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 329px">
         <img class="" height="468" src="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Le-Sacre-Coeur-Basilica-in-Montmarte-in-Paris.jpg" width="323"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          La Basilique du Sacr Coeur
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Pont Alexandre III Bridge  Walk over the historic Pont Alexandre III arch bridge that crosses the River Seine and connects the Champs-lyses district with the Invalides and Eiffel Tower district. The bridge was built in 1896 and reflects the architecture of the Grand Palais which is located adjacent to the bridge. Featuring lavish lamps in Art Nouveau style as well as winged horses the bridge was named in honour of Russian Tsar Alexander III.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 760px">
         <img class="" height="501" src="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/pont_alexandre_3_tour_eiffel_3.jpg" width="750"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Alexandre III bridge and the Eiffel tower
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Restaurants
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Le Fumoir
         </strong>
          French&amp;International cuisine. There arent many places in Paris which can compete with this elegant local institution: neo-colonial fans whirr lazily and oil paintings adorn the walls. A sleek crowd sips martinis or reads papers at the long mahogany bar (originally from a Chicago speakeasy), giving way to young professionals in the restaurant and pretty things in the library. It feels a wee bit try-hard and resolutely well behaved, but the cocktails get tongues wagging soon enough, and food is consistently top notch. Price : 35 / person + drinks (20-30 for a bottel of wine). 6 Rue de lAmiral de Coligny, 1er Paris.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          LIlot
         </strong>
          Seafood. Here, you dont have to pay Parisian prices for the best catch of the day. The venue is tiny but beautiful, with big slate menus, earthenware pots and white parquet, a bay window, a few photos on the walls and a terrace for nice days  it all has a solid, comfortable charm. Perch yourself on a stool and order your white wine, then browse the menu: 5 for a serving of taramasalata or tuna or salmon rillettes, 4.50 to 9.50 for pink or grey Madagascan prawns, 6.50 for whelks and 8 for a half crab (14 for the whole). 4 rue de la Corderie, 3e, Paris.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Le Flamboire
         </strong>
          Barbecue &amp; Meat. Lovers of barbecues and long-braised meats, Le Flamboire is for you. Located on Pigalles Rue Blanche, its no New-York-style trendy new opening, but a straightforward meat lovers paradise with kitsch flames painted on the walls and a no-nonsense carnivorous menu that really delivers. Price : between 30-40 pers person + drinks. Highly recommended. 54 rue Blanche, 9e, Paris.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Clubbing
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Club Haussman (Doctor Love)  This is the place for young people to party ! 23 rue Taitbout, 9e.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 559px">
         <img class="" height="370" src="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/image4.jpg" width="553"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Doctor Love  Club Haussman
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Concrete  This is the thing for real electronic music ! Events all around Paris (check
         <a href="http://www.concreteparis.fr" title="Concrete Paris">
          http://www.concreteparis.fr
         </a>
         ) and the headquarters is on a boat : 69 Quai de la Rape, 12e.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 730px">
         <img class="" height="480" src="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/fr-0908-512400-205471-back.jpg" width="720"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The Concrete Party on the Seine
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Bario Latino  If you are not very young neither an electronic music fan you can check out the Bario Latino. 46 Rue du Faubourg Saint-Antoine, 12e, Paris.
         <a href="http://www.barrio-latino.com/" title="Bario Latino">
          http://www.barrio-latino.com/
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/41.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-988">
          <img alt="4" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-988" height="142" src="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/41-300x142.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/11.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-989">
          <img alt="1" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-989" height="142" src="http://vellichor.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/11-300x142.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We let you decide
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Eiffel Tower  Parisians live a whole life in Paris whithout climbing up in the n1 tourist attraction. Still, climbing up in the tower will give you twos sets of incredible sight perspectives above Paris and La Defense Skyline. The only hinch is that its a 4 hours deal, IF there is a medium queue.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Things that you will do the next time
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Louvre  Well, we think you should leave the Louvre for the next time (unless you are an Art Historian). Otherwise, you will spend a whole day inside and you will remember one painting, the Mona Lisa. Maybe Venus De Milo. And you shall certainly forget the other 399.998 works of art exposed in the Louvre.
        </p>
        <p>
         Disneyland  Well, you got plenty of time for that in the US !
        </p>
