        <p>
         <em>
          This pearapplesauceis a sweet treat made with no added sugar. Its easy and quick to make in the pressure cooker
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         My sweet neighbor gave me a bag of gorgeouspears. Ive been wanting to make applesauce. Its the perfect treat on cold days. So I decided to change it up and make pear applesauce.
        </p>
        <p>
         The pears were sweet so I decided to pair the pears with some tart Granny Smith apples. My neighbor got the pears from her CSA, so Im not sure what kind of pears they are. Maybe someone more familiar with pears can tell what kind they are from the picture?

        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pressurecookingtoday.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Pressure-Cooker-Pear-Applesauce-3-Pressure-Cooking-Today.jpg">
          <img alt="Quick and Easy Pressure Cooker Pear Applesauce recipe from Pressure Cooking Today" class="aligncenter wp-image-3591 size-full" height="947" src="http://www.pressurecookingtoday.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Pressure-Cooker-Pear-Applesauce-3-Pressure-Cooking-Today.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Just a little disclaimer to this recipe. Some manuals dont recommend making applesauce in your pressure cooker because it foams. Also, this recipe does not use the minimum liquid recommendedby most pressure cookers. The fruit is so juicy and releases plenty of liquid as it cooks, so this recipe works well for me in my electric pressure cookers. Ive made applesauce in the pressure cookermany times and never had a problem.
        </p>
        <p>
         Any time you think a recipe isnt cooking as expected, dont be afraid to unplug the pressure cooker, or remove it from the heat. If something smells like its burning, you need to check it. Dont just keep cooking it because the time isnt up. Perhaps you didnt put the lid on quite right, or you need a new gasket, or something else went wrong. Trust your instincts.
        </p>
        <p>
         This recipe requires a natural pressure release because the hot fruit needs some rest time, so it doesnt start to come up out of the valve when you release the pressure. Any time any thing but steam is coming out of the valve, you need to close it and just let the pressure cooker rest for a while. You dont need to be scared if this happens, just rememberthat the contents is under pressure and the pressure needs to be released more slowly.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pressurecookingtoday.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Pressure-Cooker-Pear-Applesauce-2-Pressure-Cooking-Today.jpg">
          <img alt="Quick and Easy Pressure Cooker Pear Applesauce from Pressure Cooking Today" class="aligncenter wp-image-3590 size-full" height="960" src="http://www.pressurecookingtoday.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Pressure-Cooker-Pear-Applesauce-2-Pressure-Cooking-Today.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         If youd rather, you can also
         <a href="http://www.barbarabakes.com/2009/10/crockpot-applesauce/" target="_blank">
          cook applesauce
         </a>
         in aslow cooker. In fact, one of these days I should try slow cooking applesauce using the slow cooker setting on mypressure cooker.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you havent made homemade applesauce yet, you need to give it a try. So much better than store bought applesauce, and even more fun with the addition of pears.
        </p>

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 large Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored, and quartered
             <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="//stats.ziprecipes.net/piwik.php?idsite=1&amp;rec=1" style="width: 1px; height: 1px; border:0" width="1"/>
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             8 pears, peeled, cored and quartered
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup apple juice or water
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Directions
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Place the apple and pear pieces, apple juice, and cinnamon in the pressure cooker and stir to combine. Select High Pressure and set cook time for 4 minutes.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             After timer beeps, turn off pressure cooker, and let the pressure release naturally. When the valve drops, carefully remove the lid.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Blend the applesauce using an immersion blender in the pot until you've achieved your desired consistency. I like to leave it a little bit chunky. You could also mashed it with potato masher, or even put it in the blender if you like your applesauce very smooth.
            </li>
           </ol>
