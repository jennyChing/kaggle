           <h2>
            OSHA Training and Safety at G &amp; M Services
           </h2>
           <div class="entry">
            <p>
            </p>
            <h1 class="western">
             <a href="http://www.gmservices.ws/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/OSHA.jpg">
              <img alt="OSHA training" class="aligncenter wp-image-561 size-full" height="227" src="http://www.gmservices.ws/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/OSHA.jpg" width="222"/>
             </a>
            </h1>
            <p>
             OSHA training is essential for most workers in the construction and maritime fields as well as those in the general industry. Most credible construction companies, like G &amp; M Services, require their employees to undergo a training course through OSHA to ensure they are aware of their rights as employees as well as the dangerous hazards they will be facing in their line of work. At G &amp; M Services, we are proud to say that all of our employees have completed the 10 hour Safety Course program offered by OSHA.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <h2 class="western">
             OSHA Training- Why so Important?
            </h2>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             The Occupational Safety and Health Administration has made different safety courses available to employers and their employees in order to help both parties as well as our overall productivity as a nation. The courses are offered in to time increments; ten hours and thirty hours. The ten hour course, taken by G &amp; M employees, is designed for employees working in an entry-level position. The thirty hour course is designed for supervisors or employees with a larger amount of responsibility within their job title. According to the
             <span style="color: #000080;">
              <span lang="zxx">
               <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                <a href="https://www.osha.gov/">
                 OSHA official website
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
             , the course is focused on educating employees and employers alike on the following areas:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Health Hazards
             </li>
             <li>
              Avoidance
             </li>
             <li>
              Safety Hazards
             </li>
             <li>
              Abatement
             </li>
             <li>
              Recognition
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             Employers and employees are also trained and educated on the responsibilities of the employers as well as the rights of the employees. Employees will also be trained on how to properly file a complaint in their workplace. The tools both employees and employers learn during their training is imperative to their performance within their job.
            </p>
            <p>
             According to the statistics available through
             <span style="color: #000080;">
              <span lang="zxx">
               <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                <a href="https://www.osha.gov/oshstats/commonstats.html">
                 OSHA,
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
             in 2012 alone, 4,175 deaths that occurred within a private industry. Of these deaths, deaths within the construction industry accounted for 806 of them. OSHA then released the top four causes of deaths in the construction industry. They are:
            </p>
            <ol>
             <li>
              Falls- Of the 806 deaths, 279 of them were falls.
             </li>
             <li>
              Struck by Object- Of the total deaths, 79 were due to being struck by an object.
             </li>
             <li>
              Electrocution- 66 of the 806 deaths were caused by electrocution.
             </li>
             <li>
              Caught in/between- 13 of the 806 deaths were caused by being caught in or between equipment.
             </li>
            </ol>
            <p>
             G &amp; M Services is proud of our employees and the dedication they have for safety in the work place. Our employees are also rewarded for positive safety practices. We also incorporate weekly and monthly discussions and meetings to ensure that all involved are aware of how to handle equipment and potential situations that could occur during a project.
            </p>
