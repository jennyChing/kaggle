       <h1 class="entry-title">
        The Blogging 101 Seminar
       </h1>
       <p class="meta post-meta">
        Posted on
        <span class="updated">
         13/08/2014
        </span>
        by
        <span class="vcard author">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://10percentinspired.com/blog/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by CJ">
           CJ
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        in
        <a href="http://10percentinspired.com/blog/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag">
         Uncategorized
        </a>
        //  4 Comments
       </p>
      </header>
      <div class="entry clearfix">
       <div class="post-thumbnail">
        <img alt="Nominee badge Ausmumpreneur Awards" src="http://10percentinspired.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Nominee.jpg" title="Nominee badge Ausmumpreneur Awards"/>
       </div>
       <p>
        On Monday this week, I held a blogging 101 seminar right here in Hobart. Many minds blown! Hobart is often the forgotten state capital when it comes to things like this, and thats a crying shame.
       </p>
       <p>
        We covered basic things, things that for some reason the internet likes to just assume you know. As thought you should have been born knowing the difference between widgets and plugins, what hosting is, how to use mail chimp, and what WordPress is for. There was a little bit of nervousness on the part of my attendees, they didnt necessarily want to ask the questions they knew they needed to, for fear of the question being silly.
       </p>
       <p>
        Its terrible and ridiculous that the online environment has made people feel that way. There are no silly questions. Every single one was cogent and realistic, and they were things these women
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         <strong>
          needed
         </strong>
        </span>
        to know about if they were going to navigate the world of business blogging.
       </p>
       <p>
        The experience has only reinvigorated my determination to demystify all this online stuff. To lay it out so that more people can join the conversation and feel comfortable and confident in doing so!
       </p>
       <h3>
        Seminar outcomes for 10%
       </h3>
       <ol>
        <li>
         More classes of this kind. Small numbers, cups of tea and maybe a cake or two, and any and all questions that you couldnt ask at a big venue.
        </li>
        <li>
         Explore the possibility of large events as well, progressing into some more advanced or nuanced subjects.
        </li>
        <li>
         Compile an e-book covering the basics  the things most asked about in the 101 classes for people who would like to get into blogging but find the whole business confusing.
        </li>
       </ol>
