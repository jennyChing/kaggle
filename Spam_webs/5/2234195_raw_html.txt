          I love turkey on Thanksgiving, but then I need to take a little break from it for a while -- and making something to freeze for later is the perfect solution. Tossing the turkey breast in olive oil keeps it tender and moist, and the shallot, lemon zest, parsley, and vermouth give the final pie a little extra sparkle.
         </span>
         
         <a class="username" href="/users/6814-jennifer-ann">
          Jennifer Ann
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="recipe-note">
          <strong>
           Food52 Review:
          </strong>
          WHO: Jennifer Ann is an early Food52-er and works in international patent research.
          <br/>
          WHAT: A perfect way to use up turkey leftovers -- and to save them for another day.
          <br/>
          HOW: Simmer all of the goodness together on the stove, then transfer to a casserole, top with puff pastry, and bake.
          <br/>
          WHY WE LOVE IT: Jennifer Ann calls for turkey breast in the ingredients, but we think it would work well with all parts of the turkey. The recipe is heavy on the lemon (which we loved), but feel free to adjust to taste. We'd eat this today -- and, of course, on another day!
         </span>

          The Editors
         </abbr>

           <span class="item-name">

           <span class="quantity">

           </span>
           <span class="item-name">
            cup heavy cream or half-and-half
           </span>
          </li>
          <li class="has-quantity " itemprop="ingredients">
           <span class="quantity">
            3/4
           </span>

            cup frozen peas
           </span>

          <li class="has-quantity no-measurement" itemprop="ingredients">
           <span class="quantity">
            1
           </span>
           <span class="item-name">
            sheet of puff pastry, thawed
           </span>
          </li>
          <li class="has-quantity no-measurement" itemprop="ingredients">
           <span class="quantity">
            1
           </span>
           <span class="item-name">
            egg, lightly beaten
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ol>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           In a medium bowl, mix together the turkey, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, shallots, lemon zest, and parsley; set aside.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Melt 1 tablespoon of butter with 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a dutch oven. Saut the mushrooms over medium-high heat until cooked through and lightly browned; transfer to a bowl, and set aside.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat in a clean dutch oven; add leeks, carrots, and thyme and cook until vegetables begin to soften (about 8 minutes). Raise the heat slightly, add the dry vermouth, and cook until most of the liquid has evaporated.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Add flour to the carrot mixture, stirring constantly for about 1 minute. Whisk in the stock and cream, then simmer until the sauce thickens (about 10 minutes); remove from heat and allow to cool to room temperature.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Mix the cooled sauce with the lemon-parsley turkey, mushrooms, and frozen peas; divide the mixture between 4 mini-loaf pans.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Cut 4 pieces of puff pastry large enough to cover each loaf pan with 1/2 inch hanging over on each side. Crimp the edges with the tines of a fork to seal and tightly wrap each pie in several layers of plastic wrap and a layer of aluminum foil. Freeze for up to 2 months.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           To cook the frozen pies: Preheat oven to 400 F. Brush the tops of the unwrapped frozen pies with egg and bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until the tops are golden brown. Let stand for 5 to 10 minutes before serving.
          </li>
         </ol>
