     <h1>
      Why Audiobooks Are Our New Road Trip Hobby
     </h1>
     <div class="meta">
      <span>
       By
       <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/author/cazcraig/" rel="author" title="Posts by Caz">
        Caz
       </a>
      </span>
      <span class="date">
       Monday, June 23rd, 2014
      </span>
      <span class="comments">
       11 Comments
      </span>
     </div>
     <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
     <p style="text-align: right;">
      <em>
       Brought to you byAudible.com.au and
       <a href="http://www.nuffnang.com.au/blogger/nuffnang-policy-on-sponsored-posts.">
        Nuffnang
       </a>
      </em>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Road trips offer the perfect space forconversations and rocking tunes.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Theres one problem, I get tired of my music playlists and radio sucks, especially breakfast radio and that time slot between 4-6pm.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Repetitive songs, constant ads, and annoying DJs talking crap and trying way too hard to be cool.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Dont get me started on the negative news interruptions every half an hour too. In many parts of Australia (i.e all places outside of major cities, i.e most of Australia) you cant find a channel that doesnt play country or church songs.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Travelling full-time with our kids and running a full-time business doesnt leave much opportunityfor our learning or reading time.So our car has become a school classroom and library.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      We were recently introduced to
      <a href="http://adfarm.mediaplex.com/ad/ck/27125-203412-54918-0?mpt=[CACHEBUSTER]" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       Audible.com.au
      </a>
      , an online membership site for audiobooks.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      I think its hard to replace a book, but I have a list of books a mile long and I only get to read for 10 minutes a night before I fall asleep. As a result, Im missing out on so much valuable wisdom immersion.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Audible is proving to be a worthy solution and slotting in nicely with our long drives.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Audible-audiobooks.png">
       <img alt="Audible audiobooks" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-42961" height="710" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Audible-audiobooks-473x710.png" width="473"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <h2 style="text-align: left;">
      Thriving
     </h2>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      We allhave a lot of wasted time throughout our day. Moments of nothingness that we spend on things like watching the news, stuffing our faces, gossiping, or scrolling through Facebook feeds to see what people we rarely speak to in real life are up to.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      This year I made a deal with myself to fill wasted moments with more enriching ones. To better my mind, soul and body and make life more meaningful.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      We cant do that without learning from others and being inspired to try new things.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/thrive.jpg">
       <img alt="Thrive Audible" class="alignleft wp-image-42956 size-full" height="175" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/thrive.jpg" width="175"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      I first heard of the book
      <strong>
       <em>
        Thrive
       </em>
      </strong>
      by Arianna Huffington through an interview she did with Marie Forleo. I was fascinated with what she had to say in regards to doing less to do more. After suffering a major health crisis she realized the intense way in which she ran her business was destroying her body and her relationships so she set out to make a change and began to thrive in a new way.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Ive been wanting to read the book for ages,but because of our crazy and nomadic life can never seem to get to the book shop, nor find the time to read it.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      Enter Audible
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      They have a
      <strong>
       free month membership trial
      </strong>
      at the moment so I created an account and downloaded the book.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      This past week, weve had a couple of half day drives and were absorbing and loving her wisdom. I love how the book is narrated by someone who sounds just like her, so I feel as if Arianna herself is talking to me.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Its so easy to follow and absorb what shes saying. I can still even take it in if the girls are making a noise in the back seat and grabbing for my attention. Unlike a book where Id read the same sentence over and over again.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Audible could be quite addictive.
     </p>
     <h2 style="text-align: left;">
      Audible Membership
     </h2>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      With the
      <a href="http://adfarm.mediaplex.com/ad/ck/27125-203412-54918-0?mpt=[CACHEBUSTER]" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       Audible membership
      </a>
      you pay $15 a month which entitles you to one free book download.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      Benefits to Audible membership
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Your membership gives you 1 credit which you can use to purchase any book, no matter the price.
      </li>
      <li>
       You can purchase smaller priced books and leave your credits for the more expensive ones.
      </li>
      <li>
       If you dont like a book you can exchange it for another.
      </li>
      <li>
       Its cheaper than buying a book most of the time (at least in Australia!).
      </li>
      <li>
       You can listen to your book on your smartphone or iPod devices.
      </li>
      <li>
       Youll also receive exclusive emails about promotions, discounts, and sales.
      </li>
      <li>
       You know exactly how long it will take to read a book, which makes it easier toschedule in.
      </li>
      <li>
       No delivery time. You can download in an instant.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2 style="text-align: left;">
      Benefits of Listening to Audiobooks
     </h2>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      Quicker to consume
     </h3>
     <p>
      If I waited to buy and read a printed version of
      <em>
       Thrive,
      </em>
      it would have been Christmas at least untilI read it.
     </p>
     <p>
      Now, Ive digested the book in 9 hours AND can implement all Ive learned straight away, which can only help my business and personal growth.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      More than one person can read at a time
     </h3>
     <p>
      Talk about making the most out of your time.
     </p>
     <p>
      Craig and I get to read the book at the same time when we listen in the car, which means we discuss the book right there on the spot and brainstorm anything that we want to implement from what we learned. Theres our weekly meeting done!
     </p>
     <h3>
      You can multi-task
     </h3>
     <p>
      Its almost impossible to read and do something else at the same time, but with Audibleits pretty easy. Multi-tasking is essential to help me get through the endless checklist.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      Lessweight to carry
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      We tend to pack more books than anything else when we travel. Its too much to manage, so were madly trimming out the fat.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Books, especially when backpacking, are far too heavy to carry around. I love digital solutions like Audible.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      Audiobooks dontget trashed
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Theres nothing more devastating than to have your favourite book float away in the flash flooding caused by torrential, or have lotions from your bag spill on it.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      With Audible the books stay in your
      <strong>
       Audible cloud
      </strong>
      so you can access themno matter if you lose your phone or device.
     </p>
     <h2 style="text-align: left;">
      5 Ways to Listen to Audiobooks When You Travel
     </h2>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      1.Getting around
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      You know how quickly a headache and nausea can set in when bouncing around on a bus in Asia. I used to love catching up on my reading on those long journeys, but it was often very difficult and then when it was night time and lights out forget it.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Now you can just pop your headphones on and listen to those books you want to read.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      2. Lazing around on a beach
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      I loved reading books on a beach (notice I said loved  kids soon took away that past time) but, it was extremely painful as the sun would hurt my eyes, and holding the book up meant Id get sore arms, and if I fell asleep the book would land with a thud on my head.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Now with audiobooks you can lie backand enjoy the warmth of the sun witha story told to you by someone else.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      3. Exercising
     </h3>
     <p>
      Ive never understood how people can walk on a treadmill while reading a book! Now you can just listen to one instead and avoid the motion sickness and potential injury.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      4. Road tripping
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      No more songs about dead dogs and empty whiskey bottles! Turn the road trip into one long lesson or story. You could even listen to an audiobook related to the area you are visiting or driving through.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      Why not team up your Aussie road trip withBill Brysons
      <em>
       Down Under
      </em>
      , or for the kids,
      <em>
       Are we there yet? A journey around Australia. (
      </em>
      Its got to be better than reruns of The Wiggles)
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: left;">
      5. Cooking
     </h3>
     <p>
      Cooking is the only space in the day I get to myself. (Well most of the time). I love to set the kids and Craig with some tasks, while I whack on the headphones and listen to some podcasts or my
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/bschool1" target="_blank">
       B-School training.
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Now I can listen to some of those books Marie is always recommending. I can get them straight away on Audible rather than waiting to find a bookstore, or trying to get a book delivered to an address I dont have.
     </p>
     <p>
      A couple of clicks and Ive got it in my
      <a href="http://adfarm.mediaplex.com/ad/ck/27125-203412-54918-0?mpt=[CACHEBUSTER]" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       Audible.com.au
      </a>
      store.
     </p>
     <h2>
      Try Audible for FREE Now
     </h2>
     <p>
      Why not make use of the free trial membership today.
     </p>
     <p>
      Just sign up
      <a href="http://adfarm.mediaplex.com/ad/ck/27125-203412-54918-0?mpt=[CACHEBUSTER]" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       here
      </a>
      , download that book youve been dying to read with your free credit and listen to it instead. If the audiobook doesnt feel like your cup of tea you can easily cancel your membership.
     </p>
     <p>
      I think youll be quite surprised at how much youll like it and how much more productive youll feel. After our first session listening to
      <em>
       Thrive
      </em>
      , Craig emphatically stated,
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       <em>
        I think Ive just discovered how Ill read books from now on. I just never get time to learn anything otherwise. Radio is just full of crap, Id much rather fill the space with inspirational wisdomlike this.I learned quite a bit from her and Im motivatedto implement what she talks about.
       </em>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      I think our new road trip hobby has begun!
     </p>
     <p>
      Better just download some stories for the girls to keep them happy too! Yeah for Audible.
     </p>

      Tell us:
     </h3>

      What audiobook should we download next month?
     </p>
