                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7400730405485167899" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div style="background: white; margin-bottom: 5.75pt; margin-left: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-top: 5.75pt; mso-line-height-alt: 15.55pt; text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       <span style="line-height: 15.55pt;">
                        You have
                       </span>
                       <span style="line-height: 20.7333335876465px;">
                        heard
                       </span>
                       <span style="line-height: 15.55pt;">
                        the phrase Dont let the bed bugs bite, it is often said to wishsomeone a good nights sleep. If you are not bitten by
                       </span>
                       <span style="line-height: 20.7333335876465px;">
                        irritating
                       </span>
                       <span style="line-height: 15.55pt;">
                        bed bugs, you
willsurely get a good and quality sleep. But if your home will be bed bug
infested they will not let you sleep comfortably. They will bite you on your
body it will cause itching and result in disturbance of your sleep. In this
article I'll provide you some basic information
                       </span>
                       <span style="line-height: 20.7333335876465px;">
                        about
                       </span>
                       <span style="line-height: 15.55pt;">
                        bed bugs and Ill also
let you know aboutbest bed bug treatment.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="background: white; line-height: 15.55pt; margin-bottom: 5.75pt; margin-left: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-top: 5.75pt; text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="background: white; line-height: 15.55pt; margin-bottom: 5.75pt; margin-left: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-top: 5.75pt; text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <strong>
                       <span style='color: #0070c0; font-family: "Verdana","sans-serif";'>
                        What are Bed Bugs?
                       </span>
                      </strong>
                      <span style='color: #0070c0; font-family: "Verdana","sans-serif";'>
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="background: white; line-height: 15.55pt; margin-bottom: 5.75pt; margin-left: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-top: 5.75pt; text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-MKCwDoUlPpk/VKPl5pMQhiI/AAAAAAAAADQ/fxpLuLsgwnI/s1600/Bed%2BBugs%2BBites%2BSymptoms.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-MKCwDoUlPpk/VKPl5pMQhiI/AAAAAAAAADQ/fxpLuLsgwnI/s1600/Bed%2BBugs%2BBites%2BSymptoms.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style='font-family: "Verdana","sans-serif";'>
                       First of all,
what are bed bugs? They are flat and wingless bugs about 0.6cm long. Bed bug color
ranges from white to brown and it turns red after they feed on their human host. They are given the
name of bed bugs due to the reason that they lives in beddings and
mattresses. They are usually nocturnal in nature and bites during nights but
they never miss an opportunity to bite in day time. They are very dangerous for
infant children because they spend most of time while sleeping.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="background: white; line-height: 15.55pt; margin-bottom: 5.75pt; margin-left: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-top: 5.75pt; text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="background: white; line-height: 15.55pt; margin-bottom: 5.75pt; margin-left: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-top: 5.75pt; text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <strong>
                       <span style='color: #0070c0; font-family: "Verdana","sans-serif";'>
                        What are Bed Bug Bites?
                       </span>
                      </strong>
                      <span style='color: #0070c0; font-family: "Verdana","sans-serif";'>
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="background: white; line-height: 15.55pt; margin-bottom: 5.75pt; margin-left: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-top: 5.75pt; text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Verdana","sans-serif";'>
                       You may easily
mistaken a bed bugs bite for a mosquito or flea bite because its symptoms are usually

                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       Bedbugs prefer to bite when a host is
sleeping as that provides maximum feeding opportunity and they do need to
repeat. So they tend to hang out in locations near a sleeping host and try to
be as covert as possible and strike at night mostly. However if hungry bugs will
attack in other locations and reside in other things besides the bed, such as
in couches, chairs and anything close by to one, basically anywhere a host
resides for some longer periods. The
newly hatched nymphs of bed bugs can't travel as far as a adult bedbug so their
harborage tend to be close to where hosts resides for extended periods than in
other areas.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>

                      </a>
                      <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       Early stage larva are nearly invisible to
the naked eye, most only see the adults. Thus one cannot go by looks alone but
assume they are there and wipe and clean everything. Approximately 50x
magnifier works well to see them, there is no need to buy a microscope in the
75x and up range.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 2.9pt; margin-left: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-top: 2.9pt; text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <br/>

need to breed and if they are very hungry (more like thirsty) and will come for
you in the daytime at sitting locations to crawl up your legs or onto you via
seats or anything you are touching. Workplaces, movie studios and even vehicles
can become infested this way if there are enough people involved. What sometimes
occur with this desperate act is they get stuck or trapped on clothing when a
person suddenly moves and they just hang on for the ride home. So you can
become an unexpected carrier of bedbugs to your home.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>

                       <span style='background: white; font-family: "Verdana","sans-serif"; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial;'>
                        <a href="http://wecleanbedbugs.ca/">
                         Heat Treatment
                        </a>
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <b>
                       <span style='font-family: "Verdana","sans-serif";'>
                        <o:p>

                       </span>
                      </b>

                      </a>
                      <span style='background: white; font-family: "Verdana","sans-serif"; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-weight: bold;'>
                       Mostly people rely on chemical pesticides to kill bed bugs. Here I want
to bring in your notice that researches by many top health institutes proved
that bed bugs have immunity (resisting power) against different types of
pesticides available in markets. Some recent researches by CDC confirmed that
bed bugs build a protein protective code around their bodies to resist against
pesticides. Another drawback of chemical sprays is that it only kills bed bugs
with direct contact. Pesticides usually have no effects on bed bugs eggs. So
with the passage of time re-infestation occurred in pesticides treated homes.
