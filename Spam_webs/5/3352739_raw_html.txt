          <p class="graf--p graf--first" id="dda2" name="dda2">

          <p class="graf--p" id="91aa" name="91aa">
           He accused the blue helmets of being a security burden on the Sudanese military because they cant protect themselves. Whats absurdbut not at all funnyis that Al Bashirs own troops and agents often pose the greatest threat to the very same peacekeepers.

          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="6ac7" name="6ac7">
           Al Bashir addressed that in his remarks, too. He blamed foreign agents for spreading the reports.
          </p>

          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="e239" name="e239">
           Al Bashir empowered Bin Laden and Kony to fight against the black Christian rebels in South Sudan. As the southerners gained autonomy in the 2000s, Darfuri rebels in the west launched an uprising of their own, hoping for similar results.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="43e0" name="43e0">
           Al Bashir responded by arming ethnic Arab Janjaweed militias in Darfur, promising them payment and development for their communities if they helped quell the rebellion. Rape, looting and the destruction of crops and livestock became normal tactics for the Janjaweed and Sudanese military.
          </p>
          <blockquote class="graf--pullquote pullquote" id="edda" name="edda">
           The war has killed more than 400,000 peopleand counting.
          </blockquote>
          <p class="graf--p" id="c8f5" name="c8f5">
           The Sudanese government always denied backing the Janjaweeds campaign, but African Union military observers intercepted radio chatter of Sudanese helicopters reporting refugee movements to Janjaweed on the ground.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="9c8d" name="9c8d">
           And U.S. Marine veteran Brian Steidleon loan to the A.U. through the U.S. State Department
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            took photos
           </em>
           of Sudanese troops burning homes and crops.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="a54f" name="a54f">
           The U.N. took over peacekeeping duties after Darfuri rebels launched a deadly night time raid on an A.U. peacekeeping base in Haskanita in September 2007. But the U.N. force has struggled with insufficient manpower and equipment and a lack of aircraft.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="9a03" name="9a03">
           Although they can protect civilians from bandits and rogue rebels, the lightly armed U.N. force has never been able to stand up to the most powerful and dangerous factionthe Sudanese government itself.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="2011" name="2011">
           In fact, UNAMID has frequently refrained from even
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            mentioning
           </em>
           Sudanese troops involvement in war crimes for fear of reprisal.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="section-inner u-sizeFullWidth">
          <figure class="graf--figure graf--layoutFillWidth" id="82fe" name="82fe">
           <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked">
            <div class="aspect-ratio-fill" style="padding-bottom: 66.7%;">
            </div>
            <img class="graf-image" data-height="2400" data-image-id="1*ohYIZPTmMCVDjP99OSDVfg.jpeg" data-width="3600" src="https://medium2.global.ssl.fastly.net/max/2000/1*ohYIZPTmMCVDjP99OSDVfg.jpeg"/>
           </div>
           <figcaption class="imageCaption">
            A woman after her village was destroyed by a group of heavily-armed men in Darfur. U.N. photo
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </div>
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <h4 class="graf--h4" id="cb66" name="cb66">
           Playing both sides
          </h4>
          <p class="graf--p" id="4904" name="4904">
           This year, things have gone from bad to worse all over Sudan. In Darfur, more that 400,000 people have fled their homes since January, adding to the already horrible refugee crisis.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="2433" name="2433">
           In South Sudan in December 2013, civil war broke out against between ethnic Dinkas loyal to president Silva Kiir and Nuers answering to former vice president Riek Machar. The conflict has continued into 2014, occasionally interrupted by
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="https://medium.com/war-is-boring/south-sudans-peace-deal-is-not-worth-the-paper-dd9c0c7411a0" href="https://medium.com/war-is-boring/south-sudans-peace-deal-is-not-worth-the-paper-dd9c0c7411a0">
            half-hearted ceasefires
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="91cd" name="91cd">
           Machar has fought both for and against Al Bashir over the years, more than once turning on his fellow South Sudanese when he doesnt get his way.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="630d" name="630d">
           Recently when journalist and adventurer Robert Young Pelton
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="https://medium.com/war-is-boring/who-can-save-south-sudan-ec16c85b5f77" href="https://medium.com/war-is-boring/who-can-save-south-sudan-ec16c85b5f77">
            interviewed Machar and his troops
           </a>
           , he wrote that they were having shady phone conversations in Arabic and sporting new equipment that any rebel army would have a hard time getting hold of.
          </p>
          <blockquote class="graf--pullquote pullquote" id="b63f" name="b63f">
           Pelton speculated that Machar was once again working with Al Bashir.
          </blockquote>
          <p class="graf--p" id="ca85" name="ca85">
           In the last few months, the South Sudanese government has accused Al Bashirs forces of launching a series of air strikes in South Sudancharges Khartoum denies.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="201b" name="201b">
           Peacekeepers in the South have also
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="https://medium.com/war-is-boring/under-siege-in-south-sudan-119600c57b95" href="https://medium.com/war-is-boring/under-siege-in-south-sudan-119600c57b95">
            struggled to keep civilians safe
           </a>
           , just as their counterparts have done in Darfur. But theyve had much greater success maintaining refugee camps inside their bases.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="5263" name="5263">
           The U.N. security council, unconvinced of Machar and Kiirs ability to keep to ceasefires, voted last month to extend the peacekeepers mandate in South Sudan.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="e03a" name="e03a">
           But Sudanese belligerence could spell the end of the peacekeeping mission in Darfur. And that would leave Darfurs most vulnerable people with no
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
           </em>
           protection at all.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="863d" name="863d">
           Meanwhile, Al Bashir spouts absurdities without anyone questioning him. He slams peacekeepers for being unable to protect themselves, when more often than not
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            his
           </em>
           forces are the ones attacking them.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="b036" name="b036">
           Recently the Sudanese foreign ministry flipped the discussion about the Tabit case, accusing
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            peacekeepers
           </em>
           of raping Darfuri womenand claiming that UNAMID commanders have taken no action.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="9069" name="9069">
           In contrast to the investigation in Tabit, which has focused on a specific location, time and number of victims, the Sudanese counter-accusation was tellingly vague.
          </p>
         </div>
