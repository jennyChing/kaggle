           <p>
            It takes a special type of person to make it in the Special Forces. Upper body strength plays a role, you need good abs, strong legs, a fantastic core. But that's not enough. You also need to have the kind of spirit that simply refuses to quit. The Special Forces workout will nudge you towards the right direction.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Instructions:
            </strong>
            Repeat each move with no rest in between until the set is done, rest up to 2 minutes and repeat the whole set again 10 times (or as many times as you can) in total depending on your fitness level.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             What it works:
            </strong>
            Lats (latissimus dorsi muscle), traps (trapezius muscle), deltoids, biceps, triceps, chest, core, obliques, upper abdominals, lower abdominals, front hip flexors, cardiovascular system.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Make it better:
            </strong>
            When executing any ab-training exercise, consciously tighten the lower abs, pulling the muscles in slightly, towards the spine and aligning them so that they work harder.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Make it harder:
            </strong>
            Reduce rest time between sets to just 45 seconds for a true boot camp training experience.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Body types this can work for:
            </strong>
            The special forces do not discriminate. They take in all sorts of body types with the right attitude and aptitude and turn them into the right stuff!
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Perfect for:
            </strong>
            Shadow warriors ready to join Bravo team or Delta company. It is also great exercise for anyone looking for a solid workout with an edge.
           </p>
