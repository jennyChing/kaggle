       <div itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         The nonprofit Thoroughbred retirement center Old Friends will be presented the Special Eclipse Award at ceremonies Jan. 17 at
         <span class="bloodhorse-racetrack">
          <a href="http://www.bloodhorse.com/horse-racing/racetracks/15/gulfstream-park" title="Gulfstream Park | BloodHorse.com Track Profile">
           Gulfstream Park
          </a>
         </span>
         in Hallandale Beach, Fla.
        </p>
        <p>
         The award selection of Old Friends was announced Dec. 19 by event sponsors National Thoroughbred Racing Association,
         <em>
          Daily Racing Form
         </em>
         , and the National Turf Writers and Broadcasters. It honors extraordinary service, individual achievements in, or contributions to, the sport of Thoroughbred racing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Founded in 2003 by former Boston
         <em>
          Globe
         </em>
         film critic Michael Blowen, the Georgetown, Ky., farm cares for more than 150 rescued and retired horses, including several pensioned stallions. This year Old Friends became the new home for two of racing's biggest stars from the past 20 years, bringing 1997 Kentucky Derby (gr. I) hero Silver Charm back to the U.S. after retiring from stud duty in Japan, and acquiring three-time Santa Anita Handicap (gr. I) winner
         <span class="bloodhorse-horse" id="horse-8052275">
          <a href="http://www.bloodhorse.com/horse-racing/thoroughbred/game-on-dude/2007?source=BHonline" title="Game On Dude | BloodHorse.com Horse Profile">
           Game On Dude
          </a>
         </span>
         after the 7-year-old gelding finished racing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Other grade I winners currently residing at the main farm include
         <span class="bloodhorse-horse" id="horse-7773667">
          <a href="http://www.bloodhorse.com/horse-racing/thoroughbred/amazombie/2006?source=BHonline" title="Amazombie | BloodHorse.com Horse Profile">
           Amazombie
          </a>
         </span>
         , Affirmed Success,
         <span class="bloodhorse-horse" id="horse-1362030">
          Afternoon Deelites
         </span>
         , Bonapaw, Commentator, Dinard, Early Pioneer, Fabulous Strike, Geri, Gulch, Hidden Lake, Kiri's Clown, Kudos, Ogygian,
         <span class="bloodhorse-horse" id="horse-4638583">
          Sarava
         </span>
         ,
         <span class="bloodhorse-horse" id="horse-7714745">
          <a href="http://www.bloodhorse.com/horse-racing/thoroughbred/sean-avery/2006?source=BHonline" title="Sean Avery | BloodHorse.com Horse Profile">
           Sean Avery
          </a>
         </span>
         , Seek Gold, Special Ring, Tinners Way, Wallenda, and
         <span class="bloodhorse-horse" id="horse-1312810">
          You and I
         </span>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         "This is just unbelievable," Blowen said. "We started with one horse just over 10 years ago and now we have 166 retirees. We have several Eclipse Award winners already at the farm; I can't wait to show them mine!"
        </p>
        <p>
         Additional fan favorites include
         <span class="bloodhorse-horse" id="horse-7704652">
          <a href="http://www.bloodhorse.com/horse-racing/thoroughbred/rapid-redux/2006?source=BHonline" title="Rapid Redux | BloodHorse.com Horse Profile">
           Rapid Redux
          </a>
         </span>
         , winner of 19 consecutive races in 2011, and Little Silver Charm, the operation's miniature mascot and the farm's only non-Thoroughbred. Old Friends also operates Old Friends at Cabin Creek: The Bobby Frankel Division near Saratoga Springs N.Y., where past Travers winners Thunder Rumble and Will's Way greet the public, along with 100-time loser Zippy Chippy.
        </p>
        <p>
         Even while working full-time for the
         <em>
          Globe
         </em>
         as a staff writer, Blowen would spend mornings at
         <span class="bloodhorse-racetrack">
          <a href="http://www.bloodhorse.com/horse-racing/racetracks/25/suffolk-downs" title="Suffolk Downs | BloodHorse.com Track Profile">
           Suffolk Downs
          </a>
         </span>
         working for veteran trainer Carlos Figueroa. Mucking stalls and walking hots aren't typical side jobs for journalists, but for Blowen they were excuses to satisfy his growing fascination with horses.
        </p>
        <p>
         Shortly after Blowen retired from the
         <em>
          Globe
         </em>
         , he accepted a position in Kentucky as operations director for the Thoroughbred Retirement Foundation.
        </p>
        <p>
         In 2002, the news that 1986 Kentucky Derby winner Ferdinand had perished in a slaughterhouse spurred Blowen to form his own operation, with an emphasis on retired stallions. Old Friends would be accessible to the public as a way of raising awareness for racehorse retirement and improving racing's image. The presence of high-profile stars would help to raise money and give comfortable homes to lesser names.
        </p>
        <p>
         "It allows us to take other horses that really didn't do as much but deserve a retirement just as well," Blowen said. "I want people to come away with really good feelings about the sport of horseracing because I adore it and we've made some inroads in that direction."
        </p>
        <p>
         The farm is run by five full-time employees, four part-time employees, and a dedicated group of 24 volunteers. Blowen praised his team, particularly the cast of volunteers.
        </p>
        <p>
         "Old Friends is like a great baseball team. We have talent at every position. I'm just the lucky manager," Blowen said. "The staff and volunteers are the ones that do it all and, of course, what keeps the team together are our great athletes."
        </p>
        <p>
         "Old Friends is a special place," said recently retired jockey and Old Friends board member Rosie Napravnik. "It is such a well-run organization and Michael Blowen is one of the most genuine people I've ever met in racing. When I pull up there, I'm practically sprinting up the driveway I'm so excited. I don't really get starstruck by people, but I do get starstruck when I visit Old Friends."
        </p>
        <p>
         Old Friends offers daily tours and attracts up to 1,000 visitors per week during its busiest times of the year.
        </p>
        <p>
         "Our industry has made significant progress in allowing former racehorses to live out dignified retirements thanks to Old Friends and likeminded organizations," said NTRA president Alex Waldrop. "We are grateful to Michael and his dedicated team of staff and volunteers for the positive impact they've had on our sport's commitment to aftercare and on the lives of hundreds of Thoroughbreds."
        </p>
        <p>
         The Special Award will be given for the 24th time since it was inaugurated in 1971 to honor Robert J. Kleberg, manager of the famed King Ranch.
        </p>
        <p>
         Subsequent recipients have included racing luminaries such as Bill Shoemaker, C.V. Whitney, Edward J. DeBartolo, Richard Duchossois, Russell Baze, Laffit Pincay Jr., Dale Baird, Team Zenyatta, and last year's recipient, the Thoroughbred Aftercare Alliance.
        </p>
        <p>
         Old Friends will be the third aftercare-related recipient, following Monique Koehler, founder of the Thoroughbred Retirement Foundation, in 2009, and the TAA.
        </p>
