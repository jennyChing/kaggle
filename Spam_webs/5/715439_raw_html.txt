          <h1 class="entry-title" data-content-field="title">
           <a href="/blog/asquimom.com/2015/06/kids-camp-stone-age-time-travelers-at.html">
            Kids Camp: Stone Age Time Travelers at Coyote Hills
           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="article-meta-top">
           <div class="location">
            <a href="//maps.google.com/maps?q=37.5539363,-122.09175720000002" target="_blank" title="View map in new window">
             <i class="icon-map-marker">
             </i>
             Coyote Hills Regional Park, 8000 Patterson Ranch Road, Fremont, CA 94555, USA
            </a>
           </div>
           <span class="date">
            <a href="/blog/asquimom.com/2015/06/kids-camp-stone-age-time-travelers-at.html">
             <time class="published" datetime="2015-06-18">
              June 18, 2015
             </time>
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="author">
            <a href="/?author=55960852e4b0b41e796a9487" rel="author">
             by Melissa Avery
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </header>
         <!--POST BODY-->
         <div class="body entry-content">
          <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" id="item-55960e22e4b0f9314f070a10">
           <div class="row sqs-row">
            <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
             <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-55960e39e4b0f9314f07135b">
              <div class="sqs-block-content">
               One of my favorite local Bay Area parks is
               <a href="http://www.ebparks.org/parks/coyote_hills" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                <b>
                 Coyote Hills Regional Park
                </b>
               </a>
               in Fremont, CA. If you glance at my
               <a href="https://instagram.com/ChasquiMom/" target="_blank">
                <b>
                 Instagram feed
                </b>
               </a>
               you will see that I'm there very often! This is my go-to-park for spending time outdoors with my children by hiking, biking, nature walks and visiting the Butterfly Garden.
               <br/>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                 <a href="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a12/1435897378587/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                  <img border="0" height="435" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a13/1435897378590/1000w/" width="640"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                Not only does Coyote Hills provide great outdoor activities, they also have many family events throughout the year and are very involved with the local community! If you are located in the San Francisco Bay Area, Coyote Hills has a special kid summer program that I'm very excited to tell you about...
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <b>
                  <span>
                   Kids Camp: Stone Age Time Travelers
                  </span>
                 </b>
                </div>
                <br/>
                <table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="float: left; margin-right: 1em; text-align: left;">
                 <tbody>
                  <tr>
                   <td style="text-align: center;">
                    <a href="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a14/1435897378590/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                     <img border="0" height="374" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a15/1435897378590/1000w/" width="640"/>
                    </a>
                   </td>
                  </tr>
                  <tr>
                   <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                    Photo by East Bay Regional Park District
                   </td>
                  </tr>
                 </tbody>
                </table>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <span>
                  <br/>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <span>
                  <br/>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <span>
                  <br/>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <span>
                  <br/>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <span>
                  <br/>
                 </span>
                 <span>
                  <br/>
                 </span>
                 <span>
                  <br/>
                 </span>
                 <span>
                  <b>
                   Play outdoors and practice skills once needed to survive everyday life in the ancient wilds!
                  </b>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  <span style="text-align: center;">
                   Learn to create shelter, fire, cordage and tools.
                  </span>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Discover edible and useful
plants.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Play games of the past and visit an Ohlone Village site.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Embark on a
fun-filled Stone Age journey through hands-on wilderness skills and nature
awareness practices.
                  <a name="more">
                  </a>
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <span>
                  <a href="http://www.ebparks.org/parks/coyote_hills" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                   Coyote Hills Regional Park
                  </a>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <a href="https://www.google.com/maps/place/8000+Patterson+Ranch+Rd,+Fremont,+CA+94555/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x808fbde450431883:0x59b9fbcbb609ac3f?sa=X&amp;ei=YfeCVajkLsHaoASdoYLoAw&amp;ved=0CB4Q8gEwAA" target="_blank">
                  8000 Patterson Ranch Rd, Fremont, CA 94555
                 </a>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 When: July 20-23, 2015 [4 days]
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 Time: 9:00 a.m. - 3:00 p.m.
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <o:p>
                </o:p>
                At this outdoor day camp event kids (ages 9-13), can play and learn local outdoor skills that bring confidence and boost self-esteem. As a parent, it's always exciting to see my kids learn new skills especially when it's a skill that doesn't require modern technology. From making Cattail mats to fire-making pump drills, this is fun day camp for kids to learn and be active outdoors this summer.

           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="article-meta-top">
           <span class="date">
            <a href="/blog/asquimom.com/2015/04/confessions-of-pregnant-outdoor-mom.html">

         <div class="body entry-content">
          <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" id="item-55960e22e4b0f9314f070a23">

                <b>

                </b>
               </div>
               <div style="text-align: center;">
                <b>
                 <br/>
                </b>
               </div>
               <div style="text-align: center;">
                If you followed me on social media over my recent pregnancy, you know I stayed active outdoors throughout my entire pregnancy. I loved all the encouragement from everyone from the social media world and people on the trail but....
               </div>
               <br/>
               <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                <tbody>
                 <tr>
                  <td style="text-align: center;">

                    <img border="0" height="400" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a24/1435897378601/1000w/" width="400"/>
                   </a>
                  </td>

                  <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">

                  </td>

               I'm not going to lie, it was difficult at times to even get out the door. With two kids in tow already, an ever growing pregnant belly and the fact that I just wanted to sleep, heading outdoors didn't seem as enticing. As usual the rewards of heading outdoors, especially during pregnancy always made me better and I can honestly say I never came back saying "Well I wish I hadn't gone outdoors!". Yet here are a few pregnancy confessions this outdoor mom would love to share with you.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <div style="text-align: center;">
                <i>
                 <span>
                  NOTE: I am not a medical health professional but these are my own personal observations and suggestions based on my three HEALTHY pregnancies with the approval of my personal doctor.
                 </span>
                </i>
               </div>
               <a name="more">
               </a>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <b>
                Confession #1: Staying Active Outdoors ~ I Don't Want To
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Before I became pregnant I was hiking, running, biking and lifting weights, but as my pregnancy progressed I slowly had to stop doing certain activities or reduce the intensity of these activities. By the end of my pregnancy I was left with light hiking and walking but I vowed that I was going to stay active, especially in the outdoors.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                <tbody>
                 <tr>
                  <td style="text-align: center;">
                   <a href="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a26/1435897378601/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                    <img border="0" height="300" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a26/1435897378601/1000w/" width="400"/>
                   </a>
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                 <tr>
                  <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                   In my first trimester biking with the kids.
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                </tbody>
               </table>
               My confession is that once the pregnancy hormones hit the fan, I just wanted to take a nap but there was always a force driving outdoors. Most of the time it was my kids needing to go outside so they could run around like wild animals or the fact that I needed fresh air because every smell in my house made me want to vomit. Whatever the reason even though I just wanted to nap, staying active outdoors was one of the best things I did in this pregnancy.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               I had stopped hiking around 36 weeks but I continued walking EVERY day between 1-2 miles. For more details about hiking while pregnant, please visit my post on Backpacker Magazine, "
               <a href="http://www.backpacker.com/skills/beginner/kids-how-to-guides/bun-in-the-oven-pregnant-hiking/#bp=0/img1" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                <b>
                 Bun in the Oven: Pregnant Hiking
                </b>
               </a>
               ".
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <b>
                Confession #2: The Aches and Pain ~ Everything Hurts!
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Everything hurts more when you are pregnant, so when you mix an outdoor activity it is hard to admit but every little joint hurts more than normal! Plus it takes longer to recover even from short hikes due to the "wonderful" ligament laxity that is produced during pregnancy. Whether I stayed home or headed outdoors everything still hurt, so Tylenol was my best friend.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                <tbody>
                 <tr>
                  <td style="text-align: center;">
                   <a href="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a28/1435897378601/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                    <img border="0" height="300" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a28/1435897378601/1000w/" width="400"/>
                   </a>
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                 <tr>
                  <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                   36 weeks ~ I was trying to hike the baby out. [Photo by Daniel Barrantes]
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                </tbody>
               </table>
               Early in my pregnancy I continued road biking, but as my belly got larger different areas of my body started hurting more than I could take and sadly I had to stop biking in my second trimester. Oddly enough, hiking and walking helped minimize the swelling in my hands, feet and other areas of my body. Even though it took me longer to recover, I felt the "good" soreness of a workout and swelling was almost a non-issue in my last pregnancy.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <b>
                Confession #3: Emotional "Stuff" ~ I Just Want to Cry
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               I'm not a really emotional person, but pregnancy changes everything. It's those pregnancy hormones that make you worse than a Bridezilla. Just kidding. But only sort of. I just wanted to cry some days, whether it was because of a stupid sappy commercial, my kids driving me insane, or simply the fact I couldn't see my feet anymore.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a2a/1435897378601/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="305" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a2a/1435897378601/1000w/" width="400"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <br/>
               The outdoors helped regain my emotional stability on days when pregnancy, motherhood, and just plain life seemed like a big blur. Personally, the outdoors is my time I spend talking with God, my husband and kids. It sets the scene so my emotional status can get in check. So on days when I felt like crying, I headed outdoors to get perspective on things and I always came back with a smile on my face.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <b>
                Confession #4: Labor &amp; Delivery Day ~ It Was Easy, Sort of...
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               I gave birth outdoors...just kidding! But have you seen those ladies who do? It's a little much for me but there's a movement out there. I can't directly say the outdoors made my labor easy, but I was active in the outdoors much longer than in my previous pregnancies and the labor compared to my others were "easy". Anyways, for those who are interested here's my "Labor Story":
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                <tbody>
                 <tr>
                  <td style="text-align: center;">
                   <a href="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a2c/1435897378601/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                    <img border="0" height="400" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a2c/1435897378601/1000w/" width="400"/>
                   </a>
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                 <tr>
                  <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                   5 minutes before birth!
                  </td>
                 </tr>
                </tbody>
               </table>
               Giving birth for the third time was easy, like to the point where the baby practically fell out of my body.  Waiting for the labor to actually start on the other hand was not easy. I was 38 weeks pregnant and just miserable, everything took four times as long to do and I was constantly scared of peeing my pants in public. Whether because I was actually going to pee my pants or afraid that water was going to break. I was to the point where people just stared at my huge belly...I was "scary" pregnant.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               My husband had decided that it was too much for me already to deal with our two kids and life so he decided to stay home until after I gave birth. I remember a few hours before I went into labor, we were driving home from my son's preschool, I cried and prayed to God that I'd go into labor that night.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <a href="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a2f/1435897378601/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="400" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/55960854e4b0eb5f837b323d/55960e21e4b0f9314f070a0c/55960e22e4b0f9314f070a2f/1435897378601/1000w/" width="300"/>
               </a>
               As usual, I had fake contractions the rest of the afternoon, but I distracted myself with housework due to the ferocious "nesting" stage I was in. I was cleaning the house....I even had this urge to clean the windows on the outside of the house. I just needed to do something, I wanted to go for a run but I just physically could not.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               The "fake contractions" continued even through a video conference call I had for Latino Outdoors and we joked about that I might be in labor.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Later that night, after the kids went to bed, my husband and I were watching TV re-runs, when I finally admitted to my husband I thought I was in labor because things were getting a little painful. I stretched, laid down a towel on the couch (thank goodness) before I decided to spend the rest of the night on the couch. I sat down on the couch and immediately my water broke.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               After that it was kind of a blur, but the women from my familia came over (and my dad) and we headed over to the hospital. Our beautiful baby boy was born before sunrise!
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               But for those who want to know....
               <br/>
               <ol>
                <li>
                 Medicine: I went into labor naturally but I didn't want to feel ANY pain so I had an epidural and the lowest amount of pitocin to get things rolling.
                </li>
                <li>
                 Labor: I pushed the baby out in ONE push. It took longer to prepare the room than to get the baby out.
                </li>
                <li>
                 Recovery: My recovery has been amazing. More to come on this but I was released early from the hospital and it's been a faster and better recovery than my previous births.
                </li>
               </ol>
               <span style="background-color: white;">
                I confess, I'm so glad I'm done being pregnant but I will always cherish the lovely outdoor memories and adventures I had with this pregnancy. The love of the outdoors starts from the womb and I know this little guy already loves it.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style="background-color: white;">

            Food Reviews: Greenbelly Bars ~ Fuel for the Active Family
           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="article-meta-top">
           <span class="date">
            <a href="/blog/asquimom.com/2015/03/food-reviews-greenbelly-bars-fuel-for.html">
             <time class="published" datetime="2015-03-03">
              March 03, 2015
             </time>
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="author">

         <div class="body entry-content">
          <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" id="item-55960e22e4b0f9314f070a33">
           <div class="row sqs-row">
            <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
             <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-55960e39e4b0f9314f07135f">
              <div class="sqs-block-content">
               <div style="text-align: center;">
                We are an active adventurous family. Whether we are hiking, backpacking or cycling there is one thing as outdoor parents that is a major concern for the entire family....
                <br/>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="text-align: center;">
                <span>
                 <b>
                  Food = Energy
                 </b>
                </span>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

                Greenbelly
               </a>
               has created a new meal bar that was calling out to Chasqui Mom and family! We might not hike or bike major distances because our of children but we do burn a lot of calories due to the amount of gear we carry in addition to "little humans"!
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Greenbelly meal bars were created by founder, Chris Cage after his amazing hiking, backpacking and cycling adventures all throughout Asia, New Zealand and the Appalachian Trail. Cage understood the necessity of quality, healthful nutrition that can keep an adventurer full. Chasqui Mom and family tested all three flavors in three different activities: hiking, snow play and cycling.

                 <span>
                  The Pluses!
                 </span>
                </b>

               <div>
                <br/>

               <div>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  <b>
                   Meal Replacement or Added Snack
                  </b>
                  ~ Honestly, my husband and I are always in a "calorie deficit" when we go on long treks with our kids and Greenbelly meal bars are a great addition. This will going in our backpacking food list and hopefully we don't wake up in the middle of the night hungry anymore!
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <b>
                   All Natural for the Whole Family
                  </b>
                  ~ Since we do everything as a family, my kids eat whatever we eat on the trail. I gladly shared my Greenbelly meal bars with my kids knowing that it's all natural, not to mention that I was pregnant when I ate these. I love giving my family nutritious natural foods.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <b>
                   Flavorful
                  </b>
                  ~ Many energy bars taste, "blah" and even though they might provide the calories and energy needed, I just don't like eating them. Greenbelly meal bars are flavorful and taste like the food listed on the packaging because they are made from REAL FOOD! I thought Cranberry Almond would be my favorite, but I truly loved the Peanut Apricot even though I'm not a big peanut fan!
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <b>
                   Socially Conscious
                  </b>
                  ~ For every meal bar that is purchase, Greenbelly
                  <a href="http://www.greenbelly.co/pages/buy-1-feed-1" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                   <i>
                    donates a meal
                   </i>
