         <a href="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/chocolate_covered_strawberry_granola.jpg">
          <img alt="Chocolate-Covered Strawberry Granola" height="900" src="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/chocolate_covered_strawberry_granola.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Its true  Im a granola fiend. Ever since I discovered how to
         <a href="http://www.kitchentreaty.com/how-to-make-granola/" target="_blank">
          make my own granola at home
         </a>
         (and just how unbelievably easy it is), Ive been a bit crazy for it. Every week or two, you can usually find me in my kitchen whipping up a new batch, almost always doing something to tweak it here or there. Because I can never leave well enough alone.
        </p>
        <p>
         This Chocolate-Covered Strawberry Granola, however, is the first time Ive gone all crazy chocolate bonkers with granola. It will
         <em>
          so
         </em>
         not be the last.
        </p>
        <p>
         Rolled oats, coconut, and almonds tossed in a sweet, chocolatey glaze and baked to toasty aromatic perfection. Then we add strawberriestart freeze-dried strawberries, to be exact. Because fresh strawberries would lead to soggy granola, and nobody wants that.
        </p>
        <p>
         And
         <em>
          then
         </em>
         , THEN, a dark chocolate drizzle. Just to ensure that this granola is complete and utter chocolate-covered strawberry bliss.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/chocolate_covered_strawberry_granola_3.jpg" style="line-height: 1.5em;">
          <img alt="Chocolate-Covered Strawberry Granola" height="900" src="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/chocolate_covered_strawberry_granola_3.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
          If ever granola could be considered romantic, well  I think this is a good start. I, for one, am completely in love with it.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh, and its a nice, flexible recipe, too. Its still plenty delicious if youd rather omit the chocolate drizzle. Andif you prefer a vegan version, a couple of swaps will get you there: Just substitute vegetable oil or more coconut oil for the butter and replace the honey with pure maple syrup.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

            <div class="b-b h-1 strong" id="zlrecipe-title" itemprop="name">
             Chocolate-Covered Strawberry Granola
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="zlmeta zlclear">
            <div class="fl-l width-50">
             <p id="zlrecipe-prep-time">
              Prep Time:
              <span content="PT10M" itemprop="prepTime">
               10 minutes
              </span>
             </p>
             <p id="zlrecipe-cook-time">
              Cook Time:
              <span content="PT30M" itemprop="cookTime">
               30 minutes
              </span>
             </p>
             <p id="zlrecipe-total-time">
              Total Time:
              <span content="PT40M" itemprop="totalTime">
               40 minutes
              </span>
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="fl-l width-50">
             <p id="zlrecipe-yield">
              Yield:
              <span itemprop="recipeYield">
               About 8 cups
              </span>
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="zlclear">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="img-desc-wrap">
            <p class="t-a-c hide-print">
             <img alt="Chocolate-Covered Strawberry Granola" class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/chocolate_covered_strawberry_granola_2.jpg" title="Chocolate-Covered Strawberry Granola"/>
            </p>
            <div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description">
             <p class="summary italic">
              Hearty, chocolatey, and addicting. Tart freeze-dried strawberries dot this decadent granola; a dark chocolate drizzle sends it over the top.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup coconut oil (solid, not melted)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup unsalted butter
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup honey
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup golden brown sugar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup Dutch process cocoa powder
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoon vanilla extract
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
             3 cups rolled oats
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 cups sliced almonds
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup sweetened shredded coconut
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 1/2 cups (1.2 ounces) freeze-dried strawberries
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 (4-ounce) 60% cacao bittersweet chocolate bar (or substitute another chocolate bar or chips of your chosing)
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Preheat oven to 325F. Line a large rimmed cookie sheet with parchment paper or a
             <a class="instruction-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00008T960/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00008T960&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ohmyveggies0c-20" target="_blank">
              silicone mat
             </a>
             .
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add the coconut oil, butter, and honey to a small saucepan over medium-low heat. Heat, stirring occasionally, until the butter and coconut oil melt. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar and cocoa powder. Pour this into the coconut oil/butter/honey mixture and stir with a whisk, stirring constantly until smooth, about 30 seconds. Remove from heat and stir in the salt and vanilla extract.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             In a large bowl, toss together the rolled oats, almonds, and coconut. Pour the wet mixture over the top and stir well with a wooden spoon, until most of the dry ingredients are coated.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Spread onto the cookie sheet and bake, stirring every 10 minutes, until the granola is fragrant and beginning to turn golden brown--about 30 minutes total. Remove from the oven and let the granola cool to the touch, about 10 minutes. Break the pieces up with your fingers, then let the granola cool the rest of the way.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Once cool, add the strawberries, breaking them into smaller pieces with your hands as you mix them into the granola.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             To melt the chocolate, use a double boiler or place an oven-proof bowl over a medium saucepan of barely simmering water. Break the chocolate into chunks and place them into the double boiler/bowl, stirring constantly just until melted. Drizzle the chocolate over the granola and allow to cool until hardened, 1 to 2 hours.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             With your hands, break up the granola to distribute the chocolate and transfer to a bowl or storage container.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two weeks. Note: don't let it get too warm, or the chocolate may melt and you'll have messy granola on your hands (literally)!
            </li>
           </ol>
