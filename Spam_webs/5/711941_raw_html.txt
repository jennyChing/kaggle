        <h1 class="entry-title">
         How To. Make a Flint Axe and Hatchet
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-date">
          <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/how-to-make-a-flint-axe-and-hatchet/" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-04-05T20:17:02+00:00">
            April 5, 2014
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/author/admin/" rel="author">
            George
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/how-to-make-a-flint-axe-and-hatchet/#respond">
           Leave a comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Sometimes a flint knife or adze is just not enough and you need something with a bit more clout. At times like these, what you need is a flint axe.
         <br/>
         Heres how I constructed the large flint axe you can see below, with a few pictures at the end about its little brother, the hatchet.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3279" style="width: 424px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-15-33-47.png" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 15 33 47" class="size-large wp-image-3279" height="1024" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-15-33-47.png" width="424"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Two good tools
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I made these tools on the
         <a href="http://www.woodcraftschool.co.uk/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Woodcraft School Primitive Technology
          </strong>
         </a>
         course and had some excellent tuition from John Rhyder and
         <a href="http://www.flintknapping.co.uk/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           John Lord
          </strong>
         </a>
         .
         <br/>
         John Lord gave an excellent demonstration on knapping a flint axe head. It was a joy to watch this master take a lump of flint and transform it into a work of art.
         <br/>
         When it came to the turn of us students to knap out our axe heads John gave everyone lots of one-to-one tuition. If it hadnt been for this (and John knapping the tricky bits) I would have been lucky to have ended up with an arrow head, never mind a large axe head.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3266" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-13-58-40.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 13 58 40" class="size-large wp-image-3266" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-13-58-40.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Some tuition from the master Mr Lord
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I like to think that this axe head has some of me in it but truth be told its more John Lord than me. I did however have a great time seeing this axe head appear out of the flint knowing at least part of it is me.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3265" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-13-04-11.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 13 04 11" class="size-large wp-image-3265" height="934" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-13-04-11-710x1024.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          One knapped flint axe blade
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I used a well-seasoned piece of yew for the handle as that was what I had available at the time.
        </p>
        <p>
         Initially I used my flint
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/how-to-make-and-use-a-flint-adze/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           adze
          </strong>
         </a>
         to try and shape the yew, but although the bark came off easily enough it didnt make much of an impression on the hard wood beneath.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can see the tool marks left on the wood by the adze in the right-hand picture below. As a Time Team enthusiast I have watched the archaeologists discuss such marks on many ancient pieces of worked wood they have found so it was good to see it in action for myself.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3267" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-13-59-38.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 13 59 38" class="size-large wp-image-3267" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-13-59-38.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Stripping the handle
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         At first I used short chopping motions to tear away at the bark and wood with the adze. Ever experimenting, I tried a few strikes with a bit more force and eventually took a chip out of the adze blade. I was able to re-sharpen the edge of the adze by pressure flaking it but decided that the yew was just too tough for the adze (notwithstanding my lack of patience and skill).
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3268" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-00-01.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 00 01" class="size-large wp-image-3268" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-00-01.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Too tough for the flint adze
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I reverted to my modern axe which took the excess wood off easily. In the picture on the right below you can see the very different tool marks left by the iron axe in comparison to the flint adze marks.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3269" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-01-05.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 01 05" class="size-large wp-image-3269" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-01-05.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Time for an iron axe
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         This piece of yew had a slight curve to it which I thought would give added strength to the handle. I left the handle fairly rough, just ensuring I would have a comfortable grip and not get any splinters.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3270" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-01-29.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 01 29" class="size-large wp-image-3270" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-01-29.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          One rough handle
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I initially started using a discoid all-flint knife to cut out the socket for the axe head but I did not take any pictures of this part. I did not do this for long as the yew was just too hard and the flint blade kept slipping. A few of the other students did just use flint for this stage but since the woods they were using were slightly softer they had more success. I ended up using my little palm gauge for the job and it worked very well.
        </p>
        <p>
         I did try and burn out the wood with embers but soon got put off this with the fumes (yew wood being highly toxic).
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3271" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-02-44.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 02 44" class="size-large wp-image-3271" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-02-44.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Carving and burning
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The socket finished  front and back.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3272" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-03-07.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 03 07" class="size-large wp-image-3272" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-03-07.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          The finished socket
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         A good fit but too loose for use.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3273" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-03-41.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 03 41" class="size-large wp-image-3273" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-03-41.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Test fit
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I wrapped the flint axe head in a piece of rawhide to see if that would secure the axe head in the socket (apologies for the poor quality of the pictures), but with one piece wrapped around it the axe head would not fit into the socket.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3281" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-05-43.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 05 43" class="size-large wp-image-3281" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-05-43.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Prepping rawhide
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Instead I cut up some strips of rawhide and held it all in place with some string. As the rawhide dried out it really gripped the axe head and the wood of the socket.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3274" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-06-42.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 06 42" class="size-large wp-image-3274" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-06-42.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Securing with rawhide
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         After that it was a case of wrapping a load more rawhide around the axe head and leaving it to dry for a couple of days.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3275" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-08-00.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 08 00" class="size-large wp-image-3275" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-08-00.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Drying and adding more rawhide
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         After the rawhide had dried out it became almost translucent but it was a very strong hold.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3278" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-18-03.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 18 03" class="size-large wp-image-3278" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-18-03.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Side profiles
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Top and bottom profiles of the axe head.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3276" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-10-41.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 10 41" class="size-large wp-image-3276" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-10-41.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Top and bottom profiles
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I tested the axe out on an old stump in my garden. Like the adze, the axe tears into the wood as opposed to slicing into it as a modern iron axe would do. It was still very effective in its own way.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3277" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-12-38.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 12 38" class="size-large wp-image-3277" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-12-38.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Leaves the wood torn
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The blade edge is not particularly sharp and has a good shoulder area behind it. This shoulder area really supports the edge so that it does not break off when the axe is used.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3282" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-09-54.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 14 09 54" class="size-large wp-image-3282" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-14-09-54.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Up close
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I had a small flint axe blade I had made at the same time as the larger one, so I just scaled everything down to make a hatchet.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3284" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-16-12-22.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 16 12 22" class="size-large wp-image-3284" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-16-12-22.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Hatchet blade jammed in with rawhide
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         As well as using rawhide I filled the socket with some spruce pitch to fill up any gaps and to help secure the head more.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3285" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-16-13-56.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 16 13 56" class="size-large wp-image-3285" height="648" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-16-13-56.jpg" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Gaps filled with resin
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I have never used this tool on anything  it would have been used mostly to dispatch small game that had been caught in traps. I do like it a lot though, in some ways more than the larger axe.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3286" style="width: 700px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-16-14-13.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 16 14 13" class="size-full wp-image-3286" height="700" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-16-14-13.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Rawhide wrap
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         This hatchet sits nicely on my primitive belt order.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3283" style="width: 489px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/paul-12.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="paul 12" class="size-large wp-image-3283" height="652" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/paul-12.jpg" width="489"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          On the belt order
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Finally, my whole primitive order kit:
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3280" style="width: 648px;">
         <a href="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-15-33-58.png" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Photo 26-03-2014 15 33 58" class="size-large wp-image-3280" height="864" src="http://www.bushcraftdays.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Photo-26-03-2014-15-33-58.png" width="648"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          My collection
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Cheers
        </p>
        <p>
         George
        </p>
