       <article class="post-96334 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-opinion tag-diana-may-waldman tag-original-worldwidehippies-web-content tag-worldwide-hippies" id="post-96334">

         <h1 class="entry-title">
          <a href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/2015/07/14/im-backkkkk/" rel="bookmark">
           Im Backkkkk!
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="entry-date">
           <a href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/2015/07/14/im-backkkkk/" rel="bookmark">
            <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-07-14T10:26:04+00:00">
             July 14, 2015
            </time>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="byline">
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/author/worldwide-hippies/" rel="author">
             Worldwide Hippies
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
          <span class="comments-link">
           <a href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/2015/07/14/im-backkkkk/#comments">
            2 Comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <div>
          <strong>
           By Diana May-Waldman
          </strong>
          /
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/treehugger.jpg">
           <img alt="treehugger" class="alignright wp-image-96335" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/treehugger.jpg?resize=212%2C365"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div>
          WWH-Ive been out of the writing loop for a while. In fact, I have been kind of out of the loop all together. Ive been off somewhere dealing with shit.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          My father died and that seemed to unleash of boat load of unwanted baggage.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          My ovaries dried up, leaving me an emotional wreck with nothing to gauge the already boat load of crap I was dealing with..and then our house burned down.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          It has been a time of confusion, anger, sadness, and of course, appreciation.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          Ive come full circle. Finally.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          My grandson and I were in the fire together. I can still see him sleeping peacefully, with his little foot sticking out of the blanket, when I snatched him up and ran from house.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          Everyone says that I saved his life.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          The truth is, he saved mine.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          I was in a bad place.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          Every single day he was happy to see me. No matter what. He didnt care that I had days in which I was wearing the sweats that I slept in, sitting on the couch, chewing on a deer hoof dipped in chocolate.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          He instinctively knew that I wasnt ready to write again. Every time I tried, he would crawl up on my lap and press random keys on my keyboard, arch his back and scream for me to watch a cartoon on YouTube.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          He protected me from reading bad news, by ripping my reading glasses off of my head every time he saw them.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          He made sure my self-esteem was intact by letting the world know that he thought I was the boss. If he didnt get his way he ran to me. If Grandma says it is okay, then its okay. And it usually was.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          He taught me not to sweat the small stuff and convinced me that apple pie for breakfast, is still fruit and it has nutritional value.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          He protects my assets and wont let anyone drink coffee but me. His mother drinks coffee in the bathroom, because he thinks all the coffee in the world is mine. And if he sees you drinking coffee, he is going to try and wrestle you for the mug. If that doesnt work, he will throw himself on the floor and cry until you give the coffee back to me.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          He encouraged me to listen to music. Every day. The same song over and over for about two years.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          He taught me that being barefoot is still a simple pleasure in life and so is not wearing pants.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/jumping.jpg">
           <img alt="jumping" class="alignleft wp-image-96336" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/jumping.jpg?resize=109%2C261"/>
          </a>
          No matter what this kid made me laugh every single day. Even when he was throwing temper tantrums because he didnt understand why we couldnt have a bowling alley in the kitchen or why his cat wouldnt talk to him.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          He lifted my sadness when his little fingers would point to the sky and he would say, Sky, trees, rainbow.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          We hugged trees and played in the rain. We caught raindrops on our tongues.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          We read books and baked cookies.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          We ate chips and dip and watched episodes of Hoarders. (Dont tell his mom).
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          We were there for each other when my dad died and his left.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          We snuggled a lot.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          He sang and recited plays and I was his audience.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          We went to playgrounds, water parks, beaches and got doughnuts with sprinkles.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          We stared out the window during winter storms and watched the squirrels.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          We made our own rain with the garden hose and used our umbrellas.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          There just wasnt any time to be sad or angry. Only time to heal and laugh and love.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          So, Im back and I am ready to write again. Well, kind ofsort of
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          I just looked over my shoulder to see what the kid was doing and he is dumping an entire bottle of bubble bath into the bathtub.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
          Shit.
         </div>
         <div dir="ltr">
         </div>

          Neil Young played Hippie Dream for the first time in 18 years during this show at Pinnacle Bank Arena, Lincoln, Nebraska.
         </p>
         <p>
          The show took place on July 11, 2015 during Youngs Rebel Content tour with Promise Of The Real.
         </p>
         <p>
          Hippie Dream appeared on Youngs 1986 album, Landing On Water. Young last played it on August 24, 1997 at the Coral Sky Amphitheatre, West Palm Beach, Florida.
         </p>
         <p>
          During his 29 song set, Young also played Bad Fog Of Loneliness for the first time since 2008, Words and Out On The Weekend for the first time since 2009.
         </p>
         <p>
          Young has also dusted down a number of other deep cuts on this current tour. On the opening night on July 5, 2015 at Marcus Amphitheater, Milwaukee, Wisconsin, he played Dont Be Denied for the first time in 12 years, Greendales Double E for the first time in 10 years and performed Ragged Glory track, White Line, live for only the sixth time.

         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           <strong>
            <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/dailynewstrump.jpg">
             <img alt="dailynewstrump" class="alignleft wp-image-96325" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/dailynewstrump.jpg?resize=274%2C306"/>
            </a>
            By Sherry Pasquarello/
           </strong>
           WWH 
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           I have to say this- I have more regard for the honest bigots; the ones that come out loud and proud of their hatred than the ones that hide theirs with stock phrases and mock concern. I have NO respect for the politicians and the Republican passengers in the Presidential wanna be clown car.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           I know that the pundits on the left are having a field day with Trump right now and the handlers on the right are getting ulcers trying to clean up after Trump vomits out his mess of racism and arrogance. What worries me are the answers the crowds that gather to hear Trump give when asked why they support him.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a name="_GoBack">
          </a>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           The answers range for thinly vailed racism to the ridiculous. Why on earth would anyone consider voting for this clown simply because they are tired of politicians? The one thing I can give politicians, is that the know how to BE politicians (except for the Tea Party fanatics). They know procedure. They understand how the government is SUPPOSED to work. Trump? He knows how to work the system in place for big business. Hes used it often enough in filing and getting bankruptsy declared for his businesses- more than a few times. The government is not a business. Its agencies are run under far different (thankfully) rules. Its budget is not a household budget. So the only issue I can see that has carried Trump this far is his bigotry.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           Trump may wear expensive clothes but he isnt any different than the guys I see with the confederate flag on beat up, rusty trucks here in the Burgh. The confederate flag means racist here no matter what anyone says in public. If anyone here had kin in the Civil War- they fought for the north. He just cloaks his racism in concern over all of those supposed criminals and rapists that he has chosen as his issue to run on.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           I have more of a respect for the honest bigots in the trucks that an evil clown in an expensive suit. At least the jagoffs in the trucks arent lying.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           Peace 
          </span>
          and politics. We cant afford not to participate this time. everyone has dues to pay. Its our turn again.
         </p>

           Granary Art Centers Rat Fink! tells story of countercultural icon-turned-LDS convert
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="entry-date">
           <a href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/2015/07/13/granary-art-centers-rat-fink-tells-story-of-countercultural-icon-turned-lds-convert/" rel="bookmark">

        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/1565540.jpg">
            <img alt="1565540" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-96321" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/1565540.jpg?resize=310%2C430"/>
           </a>
           By Scotti Hill/
          </strong>
          For the Deseret News
         </p>
         <p>
          EPHRAIM, Utah  Granary Art Centers current exhibition Rat Fink! is a far cry from what one generally encounters in a traditional art museum. Curated by Laura Allred Hurtado, the exhibition showcases the life and art of Ed Big Daddy Roth, a pioneer of Californias Custom Car movement of the 1960s, who later moved to Manti, Utah, after converting to The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.
         </p>
         <p>
          Visitors will discover a wide selection of Roths art, such as sculptures, drawings, T-shirts and skateboards. The inclusion of such items broadens the parameters of traditional art exhibitions and paints a nuanced picture of an artist that relished in American counterculture.
         </p>
         <p>
          Born in 1932, Roth was a California native whose diverse career included work as a cartoonist, custom car designer and pinstriper. Roth is considered an innovator in Southern Californias Kustom Kulture movement, a term used to designate the style and accessories of those who crafted and rode custom cars and motorcycles starting in the 1950s.
         </p>
         <p>
          In 1963, Roth created the character Rat Fink, a figure that would serve as an icon of the hot rod movement.
         </p>
         <p>
          According to Hurtado, Conceived as an anti-Mickey Mouse, Rat Fink is a miscreant, a bulging-eyed, grotesque rodent with sharp teeth and a large mouth. The characters deliberately shocking appearance and grotesque green or gray color became a poster symbol of an entire counterculture movement, summarizing, in his deviance, the ethos of outsider.
         </p>
         <p>
          So what was the motivation for Roths rebelliousness?
         </p>
         <p>
          The answer can be found by examining the context of his creations. During the 1960s, pop art took the country by storm, introducing the American public to artists such as Andy Warhol, Roy Lichtenstein and Claes Oldenburg. Pop ushered in a new phase of art that eliminated the boundaries of high and low culture, using household items, advertisements and imagery from popular sources. This facet of pop, granting artistic legitimacy to lowbrow, functional items, indelibly influenced Roths work.
         </p>

         <h1 class="entry-title">
          <a href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/2015/07/12/stand-up-speak-out-2/" rel="bookmark">
           Stand Up. Speak Out
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="entry-date">
           <a href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/2015/07/12/stand-up-speak-out-2/" rel="bookmark">
            <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-07-12T08:56:03+00:00">
             July 12, 2015
            </time>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="byline">
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/author/worldwide-hippies/" rel="author">
             Worldwide Hippies
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
          <span class="comments-link">
           <a href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/2015/07/12/stand-up-speak-out-2/#comments">
            4 Comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_22132" style="width: 150px;">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/134773_1516356512596_1344979786_31241756_1355651_o1-150x150.jpg">
           <img alt=" Diana May-Waldman" class="size-full wp-image-22132" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/134773_1516356512596_1344979786_31241756_1355651_o1-150x150.jpg?resize=150%2C150"/>
          </a>
          <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
           Diana May-Waldman
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>
          <strong>
           By Diana May-Waldman
          </strong>
          /WWH- There has been a lot going on in the world during the past couple of weeks. Sometimes, its hard to take it all in and sometimes it scares the hell out of us.
         </p>
         <p>
          I suspect our government and the powers that be like it or prefer us to be in a state of fear. Kind of like our parents did, when they didnt want us to get into trouble or do something wrong. They put the fear of God into us. They made examples of out us.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive been losing sleep lately, unable to turn off my brain at night thinking about all that is going on around us. Way too may disasters and way too many children dying from child abuse. Nearing an election year and the arguing, hatefulness and back stabbing appear. The media picks and chooses what will be the top stories and as we know, guns and murder are always a good selection to scare the hell out of us.
         </p>
         <p>
          See, that is when I see people taking a stand. When it comes to guns. Another shooting and people get up in arms. No pun intended. Then it dies down, until the next time.
         </p>
         <p>
          We gripe and bitch and then we move on. I think about those who are standing up and speaking outonly to resign and take off running. Running as fast as they can, because they know whats coming next. Whistle blowers get the shit kicked out of them, one way or another.
         </p>
         <p>
          So, here you have half of us shouting and screaming about injustice and the other half of us huddled in a corner, hoping not to be noticed.
         </p>
         <p>
          I dont like injustice. I like things fair and balanced. I dont think too often in black and white, but mostly in shades of gray. I dont like mean people and I dont like blatant liars. I want to believe that you are telling me the truth. I want to believe that you are fair. Ill believe you until you give me reason not to. And I want to believe that you are committed to something.
          <br/>
          My mother used to say that I never left well alone.
         </p>
         <p>
          My father used to beat my mother. He was a raging alcoholic who thought nothing of beating the crap out of his wife. Sometimes, he would get so drunk, he would go into the basement and slit his wrists. Wed all have to end up going to the hospital and my mother would warn us not to say anything to upset him. Of course, being the big mouth that I am, I could never resist telling him what a shit-bag he was. I mean, really, go slit your wrists, hack your damn hands off, at least you will stop hitting my mother. I dont care. Call alcoholism a disease, and make excuses for why those hands have the right to hurt others? They dont. You cant make a right out of a wrong. You still have to be held accountable.
         </p>
         <p>
          I can still hear the clicking of my mothers shoes, walking behind me and asking me, Why? Why must you upset him?
         </p>
         <p>
          It wasnt that I wanted to upset him. I just didnt want to keep quiet about it. I didnt want to keep the secret or give the message that what he was doing was okay, because it wasnt. And I sure as hell wasnt going to be quiet out of fear.
         </p>
         <p>
          Fear rarely stops me.
         </p>
         <p>
          If I think what your doing is wrong, Im going to call you out on it. If it walks like duck, quacks like duck, then its a fucking duck period. I say it like it is and I make no apologies for it. Call me a loud mouth, a radical, as I have said before, I dont care.
         </p>
         <p>
          I prefer to think of myself as passionate. My passion overrides my fear.
          <br/>
          I can take a little heat. Its the real hot stuff that scares me, but it usually doesnt stop me. I mean, I can take a jab here and there, I just dont enjoy getting the shit beat out of me.
         </p>
         <p>
          My point being if we dont act now and do something to change things, pretty soon we arent going to have the right. The little bit of fear that you had, the fear that prevented you from doing something, will grow into a big old panic monster when all is said and done.
         </p>
         <p>
          We arent moving forward anymore. In fact, were going backward. The disheartening part of all of this is that we have the knowledge of what is going on around us. We have the means to get the truth, the real storysomething we couldnt always do.
         </p>
         <p>
          And I suppose if all else fails, we can pray. But, God isnt going to fix this. Only we can fix this. We operate on free will. We cant remain huddled in a corner, waiting for this to pass.
         </p>
         <p>
          We cant pretend that we dont know that the Republicans have no respect for women and would rather us go back to being barefoot and pregnant, even if it means carrying the child of a rapist. We cant pretend that we feel Veterans need help, but then bitch about the guys living on the street and asking for money. We can pretend that jobs are plentiful and people are just lazy and if their parents are good for nothing, then kids dont deserve to eat.
         </p>
         <p>
          Weve long forgotten about Bradley Manning. Bradley, who? Think about it.If they can do that to him, then what stops them from breaking down our doors and taking us away? Nothing. Nothing at all. See, thats the problem. They have the power to do that and we know itand thats what keeps us stuck in the fear.
         </p>
         <p>
          I dont want to over throw the government. I want them to stop lying, stealing, and hurting us. I want them to listen to what we have to say. I want what is fair and just. I want balance. I want peace and love. And I want them to know that we are watching them too and we demand accountability.
         </p>
         <p>
          We cant do that if we dont speak out. We cant do that if were afraid. And we cant do it alone. We are long overdue for change. We have to exercise our rights while we still have them and its time to say we dont like the way things are going. Time to spit out the bad taste.
         </p>
         <p>
          Weve got to stand for something. Pick a cause. Whether its domestic abuse, child abuse human trafficking, animal abuse, union rights, civil rights, womens rights, politics.what ever it is, pick something and fight for it. Speak out. Dont be afraid. Change it. Make a difference. Stop hiding and be heard, because somewhere out there, someone needs your voice. Someone needs you to advocate for them. Do it. Do it, now.
         </p>
         <p>
          I am a Poet because
          <br/>
          I love words and thoughts
          <br/>
          because words tangle in my mind
          <br/>
          and smooth on paper
          <br/>
          I am a Poet because
          <br/>
          my father was a Poet
          <br/>
          and because my mind wanders
          <br/>
          and my fears rise like bath water
          <br/>
          and sometimes I am afraid to feel.
          <br/>
          I am a Poet because I am defiant
          <br/>
          made raw and real
          <br/>
          by love and nature
          <br/>
          and sometimes I hurt until it festers
          <br/>
          breaks loose
          <br/>
          and because
          <br/>
          I love to lie in my bed
          <br/>
          with papers and pens
          <br/>
          nailing love- cries to my heart
          <br/>
          and because I sigh
          <br/>
          under the baggage of the past
          <br/>
          and I am a Poet because
          <br/>
          I have lived with ice blue shackles
          <br/>
          and the pink eggshells of anger
          <br/>
          and it needs to be told
          <br/>
          and because I can breed moods
          <br/>
          dramatic and still
          <br/>
          and I am Poet because
          <br/>
          I ache in secret and heal out loud
          <br/>
          I am a Poet because
          <br/>
          German men killed baby Jews
          <br/>
          and left mothers
          <br/>
          with drooping, aching, empty breasts
          <br/>
          and I am a Poet because
          <br/>
          someone killed John Lennon
          <br/>
          for the fame of a skeletal moment
          <br/>
          and I am Poet because
          <br/>
          death makes us stupid with grief
          <br/>
          and unbuttons vulnerability
          <br/>
          making us cry glass tears
          <br/>
          and I am a Poet because
          <br/>
          women are still ripped into sparks
          <br/>
          and scattered like stale bread
          <br/>
          and I am a Poet because
          <br/>
          I feel terror when I sleep
          <br/>
          an insomniac woman
          <br/>
          with a cottonmouth tongue
          <br/>
          demanding to be heard
          <br/>
          and I am a Poet because
          <br/>
          Sylvia Plath stuck her head in an over
          <br/>
          leaving babies with bread and jam
          <br/>
          and because Anne Sexton had more to say
          <br/>
          and I am a Poet because
          <br/>
          racism needs to be thrown off like a cloak,
          <br/>
          holding our hearts
          <br/>
          and because bombs explode
          <br/>
          and planes crash
          <br/>
          and young boys are buried with American flags
          <br/>
          and the world is still in secondary love
          <br/>
          I am a Poet because babies are thrown in trash cans,
          <br/>
          left in bathrooms
          <br/>
          because someone wished they were someone else
          <br/>
          and because people are hungry
          <br/>
          while others with meat-cutting knives
          <br/>
          stab into human flesh
          <br/>
          and because there is no apology for smoke billowing factories
          <br/>
          spreading poison on playgrounds
          <br/>
          where children laugh in homeboy-style
          <br/>
          and 30 years later, die stiff-lunged
          <br/>
          and I am a Poet, because homophobes
          <br/>
          hide behind velvet hats pressing hatred
          <br/>
          against their lips
          <br/>
          and the white crusted mouth sores of A.I.D.S.,
          <br/>
          seal bodies in polished oak
          <br/>
          and we make quilts to remember the silent sleepers
          <br/>
          and because even though this world can be dangerous and unforgiving,
          <br/>
          offensive and frantic,
          <br/>
          I still believe still believe in you
          <br/>
          and still believe in me
          <br/>
          I still believe in love.
         </p>

          <span class="entry-date">
           <a href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/2015/07/10/mmj-is-here-to-stay-and-is-legal-but-it-depends-on-what-public-agency-you-talk-to-and-that-needs-to-change/" rel="bookmark">

          <span class="byline">
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.worldwidehippies.com/author/worldwide-hippies/" rel="author">
             Worldwide Hippies

           </span>

          <span class="comments-link">

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/713_3599256.jpg">
            <img alt="713_3599256" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-96312" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.worldwidehippies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/713_3599256.jpg?resize=474%2C244"/>
           </a>
           By Aari Ruben
          </strong>
          /
          <a href="http://www.tucsonweekly.com/tucson/mmj-opinion/Content?oid=5442381" target="_blank">
           tucsonweekly.com
          </a>
          
         </p>
         <p>
          Friday, July 10 will go down in history but not for the same reasons I would have wished. There was a state of the art social event planned that had to be cancelled, well not entirely cancelled, just the state of the art part.
         </p>
         <p>
          Counter Culture Events AZ planned and promoted the Fire and Ice 7/10 dab party. For those who dont know July 10 is the new 4/20. The reason is if you turn 710 upsidedown and backwards it spells OIL. Hash oil is one name for some of the popular and potent cannabis extracts in the market these days. The event features three local bands including Alter Der Ruine who is about to embark on a nationwide tour opening for Haujobb, other local acts Mothers Lament and Intertwine, who have a Native American radio station, will also play.
         </p>
         <p>
          The portion of the event that has been cancelled had to do with medical marijuana, big surprise. This event is not, and never was, open to the public. Everyone who attends must purchase a ticket making it a private event. State certified medical marijuana patients were to be allowed to purchase VIP access to a medicated area, where they could sample and share cannabis and cannabis-infused products with other like-minded patients. This type of activity has happened in places around Arizona, such as Phoenix and other private events and clubs here in Tucson. In and of itself, consuming cannabis at a private event is legal. Sharing amongst those with the right to consume and possess cannabis is legal. Talking about and considering the merits of different cannabis with like-minded folks is legal. But the powers that be apply pressure wherever and however they can.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.tucsonweekly.com/tucson/mmj-opinion/Content?oid=5442381" target="_blank">

          </a>
         </p>
