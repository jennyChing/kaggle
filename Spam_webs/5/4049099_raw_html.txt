           <h3>
            CAT 2014: Learning lesson from first day CAT; manage time &amp; speed
           </h3>
           <p class="date">

        <div class="genericModule">
         <div class="articleFull">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            CAT-2014 second day exam will be taken by thousands of test    takers on Nov 22, 2014. This is the good opportunity for the second day CAT    2014 test takers to learn from the experience of first day CAT 2014 and avoid    any repetition of mistakes which were knowingly or unknowingly made by the test    takers on Nov 16, 2014.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            More important however, is to learn what to make most out of    the second day and how to attempt this coveted examination on November 22, 2014.    Number of aspirants are yet to take CAT 2014 and it will be helpful also to    know beforehand what should be the right strategy on the D day. Follow, understand    and march on the path of success with the learning lessons shared by Prof    S K Agarwal, expert and mentor on CAT preparation.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             More questions less time per question
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            You will have to manage the time with faster speed and higher    accuracy level to crack CAT 2014 with a high percentile of 99+, overall as well    as sectional. Most of the CAT 2014 test takers were found gasping for more time    as they found that the questions could not be solved in the allotted overall    time. There was also no sectional time constraint that could prevent them    from remaining stuck to one section.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            With the increase in number of questions from 30 to 50 in each    of two sections, total number of questions stands increased from 60 to 100,    a 66% increase. But the time to solve these questions has not so much increased.    Earlier it was 140 minutes for 60 questions in CAT 2013 and now it is 170 minutes    for 100 questions.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            If we calculate, there is no proportionate increase in time    to solve the questions. On the contrary the time allotted per question to solve    has been reduced by 1 minute. CAT aspirants earlier had 2.32 minutes at their    disposal to solve one question and now it stands reduced to 1.7 minutes per    question. Now you need to solve more questions in less time.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            Improvement in speed and accuracy within the allotted time    is the strategic focus now for CAT 2014 test takers appearing in CAT on Nov    22, 2014.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             First day CAT takers: 40-50 questions
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            The first day CAT 2014 takers could hardly go beyond 45-50    questions in 170 minutes. It implies that they took more than 3 minutes per    question to solve. On an average one question needs 3 minutes to solve. You    have to read it carefully, think over it and then try to arrive at the right    answer option. The process is not so short as is perceived. It takes time but    you have to maximise the score within the allotted time.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            The CAT 2014 takers on Nov 16, 2014 despite finding moderate    Quant and Verbal could not complete even the 25 questions out of 34 and didnt    even touch DI-LR. Number of candidates could not even attempt the DI and LR    problems as they found them too tricky and too lengthy.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            If you are serious enough and wish to pursue the MBA/PGDM from    a top rated B school after being successful in CAT 2014, you will have to manage    the time judiciously to score high.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Keep watch over the sectional movement
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            CAT 2014 has removed the restriction of moving between the    two sections. Now the candidates may switch from one section to another within    the allotted time of 170 minutes.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            They can solve questions according to their preference in either    section. But the caution, not to focus on only one section shall have to be    exercised. Sectional cut off still applies to CAT 2014. Also you need a high    balanced sectional percentile to be a winner.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            CAT 2014 test takers will need to get ready with following    3 things that can help them ace CAT 2014 on its second day.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            1. Faster speed
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            2. High Accuracy level
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            3. Management of time
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Experts advice: 3 steps of a question
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            For those who wish to emerge as one of the winners in this    greatest show of MBA/PGDM entrance tests for the admission year 2015-16, the    following key suggestions from Prof S K Agarwal, expert on Verbal Ability for    CAT 2014 preparation would be highly instrumental to help you achieve    your goal.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            Three implications could be attributed to improving the speed.    The first- improving the speed of reading the questions with understanding in    Quant, DI, Verbal Ability, LR. Second, Solving the questions quickly and    getting to the most appropriate answer option. Third, Marking the correct answer    option or keeping the same under review on the computer screen.
            <br/>
            All the 3 steps cited above when completed more quickly than you used to do    earlier will mark the improvement in your speed. You are supposed to cope with    the reduced proportionate time and this is possible only with improving the    speed to read, understand and attempt the question. If you are able to read    the sentences, paragraphs, parajumbles, Reading comprehension passages faster    and understand them, you will be able to solve them more quickly.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Attempt the favourites
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            What is the use of choosing the question which is not well    understood by you or is found tricky or lengthy. All the questions carry equal    marks, it will be better to opt for those ones which you can solve with less    strain on you and in less time.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p align="justify">
           <font size="2">
            There is no need to attempt more questions but to attempt whatever    you can with accuracy.
           </font>
          </p>
