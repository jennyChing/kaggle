        <div class="post-inner">
         <h1 class="name post-title entry-title" itemprop="itemReviewed" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Thing">
          <span itemprop="name">
           2014 Volkswagen Passat: Unheralded, worthy sedan
          </span>
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta">
          <span class="post-meta-author">
           <i class="fa fa-user">
           </i>
           <a href="http://theweeklydriver.com/author/james/" title="">
            James Raia
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="tie-date">
           <i class="fa fa-clock-o">
           </i>
           June 11, 2014
          </span>
          <span class="post-views">
           <i class="fa fa-eye">
           </i>
           162 Views
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>
         <div class="entry">
          <p>
           The 2014 Volkswagen Passat is a family sedan that for years has fallen into an odd category. It doesnt turn heads via an overtly handsome exterior. And neither are its performance nor interior styling spectacular.
          </p>
          <p>
           But for more than four decades the Volkswagen Passat has done a lot right and little wrong. That places the car in a category among few family sedans, particularly those in the high $20,000 to low $30,000 range. Its hard to find anything wrong with the car.
          </p>
          <div class="ad-inserter ad-inserter-3" style="margin:8px 0px;">
           <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
           </script>
           <!-- TWD 2015 third paragraph -->
           <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-8886632308747568" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="3192084963" style="display:block">
           </ins>
           <script>
            (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
           </script>
          </div>
          <p>
           The sixth and current generation debuted in 2011 and was named the 2012 Motor Trend Car of the Year. The Passat has a unique position the auto industry. Despite its Tennessee assemblage, its the only European-engineered family sedan in the segment.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Weekly Driver Test Drive
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           This was my second test week with the Passat SE. The more I drove it, the more I liked it. Its a good choice for a vehicle whose price rests at the average new price of a car in the United States.
          </p>
          <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_22723" style="width: 550px;">
           <a href="http://theweeklydriver.com/wp-content/uploads/vw5.jpg">
            <img alt="The 2014 Volkswagen Passat does a lot right and little wrong." class="size-full wp-image-22723" height="313" src="http://theweeklydriver.com/wp-content/uploads/vw5.jpg" width="550"/>
           </a>
           <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
            The 2014 Volkswagen Passat does a lot right and little wrong. Images  James Raia/2014
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
          <p>
           Offered in four models 2.5L, 1.8T, TDI and V6 models, the 2014 Volkswagen Passat is further categorized in four trims, Wolfsburg, SE, Sport and SEL.
          </p>
          <p>
           The latter trim, with the new, more fuel-efficient 1.8-liter, 170-horsepower, four-cylinder turbocharged engine was my Weekly Driver on both occasions.
          </p>
          <div class="gallery galleryid-22722 gallery-columns-3 gallery-size-thumbnail" id="gallery-2">
           <figure class="gallery-item">
            <div class="gallery-icon landscape">
             <a href="http://theweeklydriver.com/wp-content/uploads/vw23.jpg">
              <img alt="The 2014 Volkswagen Passat has clean, unspectacular exterior styling." aria-describedby="gallery-2-22725" class="attachment-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://theweeklydriver.com/wp-content/uploads/vw23-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text gallery-caption" id="gallery-2-22725">
             The 2014 Volkswagen Passat has clean, unspectacular exterior styling.
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <figure class="gallery-item">
            <div class="gallery-icon landscape">
             <a href="http://theweeklydriver.com/wp-content/uploads/vw31.jpg">
              <img alt="The 2014 Volkswagen Passat has a sharped angled hood and front lights perspective." aria-describedby="gallery-2-22726" class="attachment-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://theweeklydriver.com/wp-content/uploads/vw31-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text gallery-caption" id="gallery-2-22726">
             The 2014 Volkswagen Passat has a sharped angled hood and front lights perspective.
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <figure class="gallery-item">
            <div class="gallery-icon landscape">
             <a href="http://theweeklydriver.com/wp-content/uploads/vw51.jpg">
              <img alt="The interior of the 2014 Volkswagen has clean, efficient and spacious interior." aria-describedby="gallery-2-22727" class="attachment-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://theweeklydriver.com/wp-content/uploads/vw51-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text gallery-caption" id="gallery-2-22727">
             The interior of the 2014 Volkswagen has clean, efficient and spacious interior.
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
          </div>
          <div class="ad-inserter ad-inserter-7" style="margin:8px 0px;">
           <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
           </script>
           <!-- midpost -->
           <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-8886632308747568" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="1640416568" style="display:block">
           </ins>
           <script>
            (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
           </script>
          </div>
          <p>
           Its only offered this year in the SEL and Sport model. It will be introduced progressively in other models as the standard configuration, replacing the 2.5-liter, five-cylinder offerings.
          </p>
          <p>
           The Volkswagen Passat in all trims offers an extensive standard features list and in the SEL model a pleasant presentation of no option packages.
          </p>
          <p>
           The SEL model, like other top-line car trims included all of the other trim offers and host of other features: navigation system with a larger screen, hard-drive music storage and traffic updates, keyless ignition/entry, remote ignition, eight-way power front seats with driver memory functions, partial leather upholstery, wood-grain interior trim, dual-zone automatic climate control, HD radio and a separate ski pass-through for the 60/40 rear seat.
          </p>
          <p>
           Driving the Passat is a treat. Its not classified as a luxury car, but it has those tendencies. Its extraordinarily quiet when idling and at high speeds. Theres near-expansive front and rear seat room.
          </p>
          <p>
           The 18-inch alloy wheels and the electronic stability control combine for a smooth rider with bumps, divots and other road nuisances are handled without issue. The Passats steering and maneuverability exceeds a good share of higher-priced vehicles.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Likes:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Smooth, quiet highway drive.
          </p>
          <p>
           Spacious interior, front seats and back.
          </p>
          <p>
           Superior comfort.
          </p>
          <p>
           Modern exterior design.
          </p>
          <p>
           Steady powerful performance.
          </p>
          <p>
           All inclusive equipment list. No overpriced option packages.
          </p>
          <p>
           Retro analog clock. Simply presented. Why not?
          </p>
          <p>
           Clean, horizontal paneling design.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Dislikes:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Blind spot over drivers left shoulder.
          </p>
          <p>
           Acceleration lag.
          </p>
          <p>
           Off-white colored leather interior and carpet can discolor easily.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Facts &amp; Figures: 2014 Volkswagen Passat
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Acceleration: 0-60 mph, 7.7 seconds.
           <br/>
           Airbags: (6).
           <br/>
           Fuel economy: 24 mpg (city), 34 mpg (highway), 28 (combined). six-speed automatic transmission.
           <br/>
           Horsepower: 170
           <br/>
           Manufacturers Suggested Retail Price: $30,895.00
           <br/>
           Manufacturers Web site:
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.vw.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.vw.com', 'www.vw.com');" target="_blank">
             www.vw.com
            </a>
           </strong>
           .
           <br/>
           Price As Tested: $31,715.00
           <br/>
           Warranty: Bumper to bumper, 3 years/36,000 miles; Powertrain, 5 years/60,000 miles; Corrosion, 12 years/unlimited miles; Roadside Assistance, 3 years/36,000 miles.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What Others Say:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           In a segment dominated by Korean, Japanese, and American powerhouses, Volkswagens Passat stands proudly as a solid German alternative to the midsize competition. Although its styling is safer and personality a tad more restrained, the base Passats improved powertrain and agreeable interior make it a satisfying choice for drivers who prefer German  and savoring all the driver-focused functionality that comes with it. 
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.popularmechanics.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.popularmechanics.com', 'Popular Mechanics');" target="_blank">
             Popular Mechanics
            </a>
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Taken with its improved manners and drivability, the volume Passat now finds itself in a position to snag more mid-size market share. 
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.caranddriver.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.caranddriver.com', 'Car and Driver');" target="_blank">
             Car and Driver
            </a>
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Other family sedans may be flashier, but the Passat provides one of the best all-around combinations of performance, features and value. 
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.autotrader.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.autotrader.com', 'AutoTrader');" target="_blank">
             AutoTrader
            </a>
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Set apart by its European-style refinement, roomy interior and fuel-efficient diesel engine option, the 2014 Volkswagen Passat is a solid choice for a family sedan. 
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.edmunds.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.edmunds.com', ' Edmunds');" target="_blank">
             Edmunds
            </a>
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Weekly Drivers Final Words:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Theres plenty of room for a quality, fairly priced family sedan with a good share of performance and not from Japanese of South Korean manufacturer. The German-engineered, American-assembled Volkswagen Passat is a prime example.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
