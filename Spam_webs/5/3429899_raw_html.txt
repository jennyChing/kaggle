           <div class="content-body">
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Palms are clammy. Buttocks are clenched. The mood, in short, istense.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/UK/tags/Porsche">
              Porsche
             </a>
             has just released details on the 911 GT2 RS  its fastest, most powerful road car ever  and we are frankly a littleuneasy.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.topgear.com/uk/photos/porsche-99-gt2-rs" title="GT2 RS">
              See more pics of the GT2RS
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.topgear.com/uk/photos/porsche-99-gt2-rs" title="GT2 RS">
             </a>
             To remind you, the
             <em>
              normal
             </em>
             911 GT2 is already one of the most monstrous cars on the road, with a 523bhp twin-turbo six feeding the rear wheels and a reputation for going sideways and never coming back. They call it TheWidowmaker.
            </p>
            <p>
             And now Porsche has made The Widowmaker even more evil. Here come the headline stats: the 911 GT2 RS produces 611bhp  yep, still all through the rear wheels  weighs 1,370kg and will go from standstill to 124mph in
             <em>
              under ten seconds
             </em>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Thats 88bhp more powerful and 70kg lighter than the GT2. Thats 8bhp more than the Carrera GT. Thats a power-to-weight ratio of
             <em>
              435bhp per tonne
             </em>
             .Sheesh.
            </p>
            <p>
             Porsche says the RS will, in the hands of someone far, far braver than us, lap the Nuburgring in 7m18s - 19 seconds quicker than the very, very fast
             <a href="http://www.topgear.com/UK/photos/porsche-911-turbo-s-first-drive" title="911 Turbo S">
              911 Turbo S
             </a>
             .
            </p>

            <p>
             0-60mph? 3.4 seconds  point-two quicker than the GT2. Top speed is 205mph. Those are some entirely wall-lickingnumbers.
            </p>
            <p>
             The extra power comes from an increase in boost pressure to the GT2s 3.6-litre twin turbo engine, while the weight loss is down to an intensive carbon fibre diet: the GT2 RSs front and rear spoilers, bonnet and rear arch vents are made of thestuff.
            </p>
            <p>
             Theres extra downforce from a taller rear wing and redesigned front spoiler. In the cabin, you get lightweight bucket seats, fabric door pulls and lots ofalcantara.
            </p>
            <p>
             Porsche is keen to point out that the GT2 RS is a deeply green car, returning 23.7mph and emitting just 284g/km of CO2  better figures than the GT2 despite the increasedpower.
            </p>
            <p>
             Just 500 GT2 RSs will be built, with prices starting at  one more big number for you  a whopping 164,000. Thats about 33,000 more than a regular GT2. We suspect potential owners shall notcare.
            </p>
            <p>
             The GT2 RS wont be formally unveiled until August. Well get our hands on one as soon as possible and give you the full verdict. We are a little tiny bit entirely terrified at thisprospect.
