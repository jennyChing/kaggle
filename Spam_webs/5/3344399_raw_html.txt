        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
         Grilled Pork Tenderloin with Peaches
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <meta content="UserComments:27" itemprop="interactionCount"/>
         <span class="entry-commentcount">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="recipe-description" itemprop="description">
         <p>
          Summer peaches, pork, grill, herbs, yum! Theres something wonderful about this combination. As you know, pork plays well with fruit. Here were pairing pork tenderloin with fresh peaches, and grilling them both for smoky goodness.
         </p>
         <p>
          Tender as it is, pork tenderloin on its own isnt the most flavorful of pork cuts. But it lends itself to all sorts of spice rubs, in this case herbes de Provence, a mixture of herbs typical of the south of France.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Grilled Pork Tenderloin with Peaches" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-18196" height="427" src="http://www.simplyrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/grilled-pork-tenderloin-peaches-horiz-a-640.jpg" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          The mix Im using includes thyme, rosemary, tarragon, and lavender. The floral notes of the herbs work great with both the peaches and the pork, and act as a flavor bridge between the two.
         </p>
         <p>
          In preparing for this recipe we experimented with a few different rubs, with the herbes de Provence winning out over the others. Do you have a favorite spice rub for pork tenderloin? If so, please let us know about it in the comments.
         </p>

         <h2>
          Grilled Pork Tenderloin with Peaches Recipe
         </h2>
         <div class="recipe-meta">
          <ul>
           <li class="recipe-prep">
            <span class="recipemeta-label">
             Prep time:
            </span>
            <span class="preptime" content="PT00H30M" itemprop="prepTime">
             30 minutes
             <span class="value-title" title="PT00H30M">
             </span>
            </span>
           </li>
           <li class="recipe-cook">
            <span class="recipemeta-label">
             Cook time:
            </span>
            <span class="cooktime" content="PT00H15M" itemprop="cookTime">
             15 minutes
             <span class="value-title" title="PT00H15M">
             </span>
            </span>
           </li>
           <li class="recipe-yield">
            <span class="recipemeta-label">
             Yield:
            </span>
            <span class="yield" itemprop="recipeYield">
             Serves 2, can can be doubled.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="entry-details recipe-intronote">
          <p>
           Herbes de Provence is a mixture of dried herbs typical of the Provence area of France. The mixture I used has chervil, basil, rosemary, tarragon, garlic, lavender, marjoram, savory, thyme and parsley. This somewhat floral herb mixture works great with the peaches and the pork. If you don't have herbes de Provence, you can use an Italian herb seasoning, or any favorite combination of dried herbs, salt, and pepper.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="entry-details recipe-ingredients">
          <div class="yummly-button">
           <a class="YUMMLY-YUM-BUTTON" href="//yummly.com" rel="nofollow">
            Yum
           </a>
          </div>
          <h3>
           Ingredients
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 pork tenderloin
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon kosher salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tbsp
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbes_de_Provence">
             herbes de Provence
            </a>
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1-2 yellow peaches or nectarines, quartered or cut into thick slices
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="widget-ad ad-mid-callout" id="widget-ad-mid-callout">
          <!-- ADSENSE || Mid-Callout Ad -->
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-5527714554151844" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="1194169405" style="display:inline-block;width:300px;height:250px;">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
         </div>
         <div class="entry-details recipe-method instructions">
          <h3>
           Method
          </h3>
          <div itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           <p>
            <b>
             1
            </b>
            Coat the tenderloin with olive oil, then sprinkle with salt. Roll the tenderloin in the herbes de Provence and set aside at room temperature for 30 minutes.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             2
            </b>
            Prepare your grill for high direct heat on one side, and low heat on another side. A tenderloin is thick on one end and thin on the other. You will want to cook the thinner end on the cooler side of the grill while the thicker end is on the hot part o the grill.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             3
            </b>
            Grill the tenderloin until until a meat thermometer inserted into the center of the thickest part reads 140F, about 5 minutes per side. Remove and let rest while you cook the peaches.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="herbed-grilled-tenderloin-1.jpg" height="140" src="http://www.simplyrecipes.com/photos/herbed-grilled-tenderloin-1.jpg" width="210"/>
            <img alt="grilled-peaches-1a.jpg" height="140" src="http://www.simplyrecipes.com/photos/grilled-peaches-1a.jpg" width="210"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             4
            </b>
            Paint the peaches with a little olive oil and grill on high direct heat for 1-2 minutes per side, just enough to get some good grill marks.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             5
            </b>
            Slice the tenderloin into 1/4-inch pieces and drizzle any juices released from the cutting the meat over them. Serve hot with the peaches.
           </p>
          </div>
