            <div class="what">
             <p>
              Please sack Jeremy Clarkson due to his continual offensive remarks and behaviour. Paying a presenter to court outrage is no way to run a publicly funded broadcast station.
             </p>
            </div>
            <h3 class="subtitle">
             Why is this important?
            </h3>
            <div class="why">
             <p>
              Update: It is reported today that Clarkson has been suspended for allegedly assaulting a TV producer. With this catalogue of misdemeanors it's now time for him to go.
             </p>
             <p>
              The Daily Mirror reported that Clarkson and his family business sold golliwogs and is reportedly very proud of the fact. He has zero awareness as to how totally offensive this could be for a great number of people.
             </p>
             <p>
              It seems Clarkson has been up to it again, he's now reportedly used the 'n' word:
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/media/2014/may/01/jeremy-clarkson-begs-forgiveness-n-word-top-gear">
               http://www.theguardian.com/media/2014/may/01/jeremy-clarkson-begs-forgiveness-n-word-top-gear
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              It must be remembered that the BBC sacked Carol Thatcher for refering to a tennis player backstage as a 'golliwog'. So there is no excuse in keeping Clarkson on is there?
             </p>
             <p>
              As recent newspaper reports show in October 2009 he ridiculed TV bosses for being obsessed with having black Muslim lesbians on shows to balance out the number of white heterosexual men. These comments followed on from the Strictly Come Dancing issue over Anton Du Beke calling his partner Laila Rouass a Paki.
             </p>
             <p>
              In November 2008 he made a joke about lorry drivers murdering prostitutes during an episode of Top Gear. Despite numerous complaints to the BBC and Ofcom, and a call from an MP for him to lose his job, he gave his usual mock apology on the following weeks show.
             </p>
             <p>
              He has called Minister Gordon Brown a one-eyed Scottish idiot", but only apologised about the remark about Mr Browns appearance and he didnt apologise about calling him a Scottish Idiot.
             </p>
             <p>
              In July 2010, he made a remark about a woman in a burka wearing a red g-string while discussing dangerous driving conditions on the show. Later that month Alastair Campbell revealed on his blog that, in un-aired comments, Clarkson had rebuffed claims that he wasnt very sound on gay rights by replying Oh yes I am. I demand the right not to be bummed.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              In August 2010 Clarkson was comparing two Ferraris and described the older one as a simpleton that should be called the 430 Speciale needs instead of its real name 430 Speciale, which prompted a backlash from the National Autistic Society.
             </p>
             <p>
              In April this year, The BBC was forced to apologise to the Mexican ambassador after Clarkson branded Mexicans lazy, feckless and flatulent in an episode of Top Gear. Ofcom received 157 complaints about the comments on the BBC2 show, which viewers complained were derogatory, cruel, xenophobic, discriminatory and racist.
             </p>
             <p>
              And now this week he has made an offensive remark about Asians.
             </p>
             <p>
              The BBC should sack Clarkson, just like they have done in the past with other presenters who have caused offense. The recent remark about Asians is in contravention of the Equality Act 2010 and lawyers have threatened to take the issue to Ofcom and consider legal action.
             </p>
             <p>
              Casual racism in the media by established BBC stalwarts is constantly brushed aside; each time presenters casually laugh it off or make some insincere, veiled apology and the BBC 'reprimand' them. But nothing changes.
             </p>
             <p>
              The BBC has an additional duty under the Equality Act, in that it is deemed a public body and therefore has an additional "Equality Duty"; It also requires that public bodies: "...Have due regard to the need to eliminate discrimination, advance equality of opportunity and foster good relations between different people when carrying out their activities"
             </p>
            </div>
            <h3 class="subtitle">
             How it will be delivered
            </h3>
            <div class="delivery">
             <p>
              I will send the completed petition to the BBC and ask for them to take the appropriate action against Clarkson.
             </p>
