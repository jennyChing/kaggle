                  <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                   <span>
                    Rainbow Oats {How to Dye Oats for Sensory Play}
                   </span>
                  </h3>
                  <div class="post-header">
                   <div class="post-header-line-1">
                   </div>
                   <div style="margin: 5px 0; border-bottom: 0px solid; padding: 5px;">
                    By
                    <a class="g-profile" href="https://plus.google.com/108363111569505763643" rel="author" title="author profile">
                     Dyan Robson
                    </a>
                    on Monday, July 07, 2014
                    <span class="post-comment-link" style="Float:right;">
                    </span>
                   </div>
                   <span class="post-author vcard">
                   </span>
                  </div>
                  <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3627860408313566341" itemprop="articleBody">
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                    <i>
                     This post contains affiliate links for your convenience.
                    </i>
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                    <i>
                     <br/>
                    </i>
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                    It's been awhile since we've done a good old fashioned
                    <a href="http://www.andnextcomesl.com/search/label/Sensory%20Bins" target="_blank">
                     sensory bin
                    </a>
                    , mostly because our sensory bin container was in dire need of replacement. Well, thankfully, I remembered to pick up a
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002BA5F4I/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002BA5F4I&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=PPQJRFLQRWUL7RVJ" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     new sensory bin container
                    </a>
                    while running errands. Then we kicked off our return to
                    <a href="http://www.andnextcomesl.com/search/label/Sensory%20Bins" target="_blank">
                     sensory bins
                    </a>
                    with a colorful (new-to-us) base. I haven't used
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     oats
                    </a>
                    as a
                    <a href="http://www.andnextcomesl.com/search/label/Sensory%20Bins" target="_blank">
                     sensory bin
                    </a>
                    filler before. And while I'm sure that the boys would have enjoyed playing with plain old
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     oats
                    </a>
                    on their own, I always like to add a bit of color if I can. So the boys and I spent a morning dyeing
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     oats
                    </a>
                    in all colors of the rainbow, left them in the sun to dry, and were playing with them a few hours later.
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                    <br/>
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                    <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JkF16aoCSWI/U7tsIId3YBI/AAAAAAAANac/MFtk2Xe6iJ8/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-4.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                     <img alt="Rainbow oats - how to dye oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" border="0" height="424" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JkF16aoCSWI/U7tsIId3YBI/AAAAAAAANac/MFtk2Xe6iJ8/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-4.JPG" title="Rainbow oats - how to dye oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" width="640"/>
                    </a>
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                    <br/>
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                    Dyeing the
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     oats
                    </a>
                    was very straightforward. Since we are passed the mouthing stage in our house, I went the non-edible route. However, you could easily make edible rainbow oats by using
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004G7PLJI/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B004G7PLJI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     food coloring
                    </a>
                    . To dye the
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     oats
                    </a>
                    , you will need:
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                    <br/>
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                   </div>
                   <ul>
                    <li>
                     6 cups of
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                      large flake whole grain oats
                     </a>
                     (1 cup for each color)
                    </li>
                    <li>
                     Water
                    </li>
                    <li>
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0042SYTV2/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B0042SYTV2&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                      Liquid watercolors
                     </a>
                     or
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004G7PLJI/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B004G7PLJI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                      food coloring
                     </a>
                    </li>
                    <li>
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00HFQ2T06/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00HFQ2T06&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=ADZ5FJXZOFSVCPJ4" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                      Paper towel
                     </a>
                    </li>
                   </ul>
                   <div>
                    <br/>
                   </div>
                   <div>
                    In a bowl, I mixed approximately 1.5 tablespoons of water with a few drops of
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0042SYTV2/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B0042SYTV2&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     liquid watercolors
                    </a>
                    (if you are using
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004G7PLJI/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B004G7PLJI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     food coloring
                    </a>
                    , you will need likely 8-10 drops depending on how vibrant you want them). You want just enough liquid to coat the oats. You do not want them to be drenched. I then added the
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     rolled oats
                    </a>
                    . Once all the
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     oats
                    </a>
                    were evenly coated, I dumped the mixture onto
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00HFQ2T06/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00HFQ2T06&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=ADZ5FJXZOFSVCPJ4" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     paper towel
                    </a>
                    and left it in the sunroom to dry. Drying time was only about an hour.
                   </div>
                   <div>
                    <br/>
                   </div>
                   <div>
                    I then arranged the colored
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     oats
                    </a>
                    into a rainbow. Isn't it pretty?
                   </div>
                   <br/>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                    <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Nxkc79u31V0/U7tsCFQZXeI/AAAAAAAANZw/5KUreAD__Ts/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-1.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                     <img alt="Rainbow oats - how to dye oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" border="0" height="423" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Nxkc79u31V0/U7tsCFQZXeI/AAAAAAAANZw/5KUreAD__Ts/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-1.JPG" title="Rainbow oats - how to dye oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" width="640"/>
                    </a>
                   </div>
                   <br/>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                    <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Kg3y7jj_v-o/U7tsFd2UmdI/AAAAAAAANaM/P9MTxGkqFyw/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-2.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                     <img alt="Rainbow oats - how to dye oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Kg3y7jj_v-o/U7tsFd2UmdI/AAAAAAAANaM/P9MTxGkqFyw/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-2.JPG" title="Rainbow oats - how to dye oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" width="424"/>
                    </a>
                   </div>
                   <br/>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                    <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VL-CUK3qGgU/U7tsGc8ZESI/AAAAAAAANaU/oUkcJ1_wLSY/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-3.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                     <img alt="Rainbow oats - how to dye oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VL-CUK3qGgU/U7tsGc8ZESI/AAAAAAAANaU/oUkcJ1_wLSY/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-3.JPG" title="Rainbow oats - how to dye oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" width="423"/>
                    </a>
                   </div>
                   <br/>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                    Of course, all the colored
                    <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002UT5H3E/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002UT5H3E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=annecol-20&amp;linkId=N5QNJUH64GIFXHYB" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                     oats
                    </a>
                    will be thoroughly mixed in a matter of seconds, leaving a bin full of colorful confetti-like sensory fun.
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                    <br/>
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                    <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-xZ4o2WfIyYs/U7tsJ0WImiI/AAAAAAAANas/UDkRPaIgRPE/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-6.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                     <img alt="Close up of rainbow dyed oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" border="0" height="423" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-xZ4o2WfIyYs/U7tsJ0WImiI/AAAAAAAANas/UDkRPaIgRPE/s1600/rainbow-dyed-oats-how-to-sensory-play-for-kids-toddlers-6.JPG" title="Close up of rainbow dyed oats for sensory play from And Next Comes L" width="640"/>
                    </a>
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                    <br/>
                   </div>
                   <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                    The rainbow oats were irresistible to my boys! J loved to draw shapes, letters, and numbers in them. And he loved to scoop them up and let them fall gently between his fingers.
                   </div>
                   <br/>

                    </a>

                   Doesn't it look just like confetti?
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   K, on the other hand, loved to explore the rainbow oats with his entire body. I've said it many times before, but I will say it again. No
                   <a href="http://www.andnextcomesl.com/search/label/Sensory%20Bins" target="_blank">
                    sensory bin
                   </a>
                   is complete until there's a child inside the bin itself!
