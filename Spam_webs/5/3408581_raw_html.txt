      <div style="background: none repeat scroll 0% 0% rgb(238, 238, 238); border: 1px solid rgb(204, 204, 204); padding: 5px 10px; text-align: justify;">
      </div>
      <img alt="" src="http://cdn2.intellitradersnetwork.com/upload/images/FibonacciSpiral%283%29.jpg" style="margin-bottom:15px; width: 570px; height: 250px;"/>
      <h2>
       So What's Fibonacci Trading?
      </h2>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Every foreign exchange trader uses
Fibonacci Retracements at some stage of his professional career.
This is an extremely useful tool that can help you to set your
business in the right direction and has the ability to serve you
for long times. However, you need to use this method correctly and
wisely no matter when or how often you use it. In this regard,
there are some common mistakes you need to avoid while applying
Fibonacci retracements. The most important of them are explained
below.
      </p>
      <p style="margin-left: 4.5pt; text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Never
Ignore Long Term Trends:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Relatively newer users do not
consider the bigger picture and tend to calculate and measure
important pullbacks and moves in short time period. This short term
perspective usually misguides the traders about the nature of the
market. On the other hand, they will be able to apply Fibonacci
retracements in the right direction of the momentum if they
concentrate on the long term trends that can open the doors to
endless opportunities for them.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The short trends often last only
for five minutes and therefore, traders usually have short term
opportunity after the run-up in any currency pair. On the contrary,
the long term trends allow the traders to trade in currency pair
for longer periods of time by applying the Fibonacci at the correct
time. Therefore, it is always better to pick your trade
opportunities in the long term trends as it will also prevent you
from fighting the trends.
      </p>
      <p style="margin-left: 4.5pt; text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Never
Mix Fibonacci Reference Points:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       It is always important for any
trader to keep his reference point consistent while applying
Fibonacci retracements to price action. This simply means that the
best price should be available at the top of the trend, from candle
body to candle body, wick to wick or within the body of the candle,
if you are referencing the lowest price in the trend through body
of the candle or close of a session.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The mixture of reference points
lead to misguidance and miscalculations such as going from candle
of the body to the wick. The resistance and support levels on the
chart become clear to the eyes if the Fibonacci reference point is
kept consistent. This technique will also enable you to analyze
quickly and ultimately quicken the trade.
      </p>
      <p style="margin-left: 4.5pt; text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Never
Rely on Fibonacci Alone:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Fibonacci is really helpful as far
as providing trade setups that are reliable but it cannot do it
without any sort of confirmation. As a matter of fact, you will
increase your chances of good trade if you complement Fibonacci by
using other tools such as stochastic oscillators and MACD. If you
do not use these tools alongside Fibonacci retracements then you
can only hope to make most of your trade setups. These tools can
help you to confirm the apparent price in the market helping you
nobly to do good trade.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Conclusion:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Fibonacci retracements or rules for
binary trading options are really helpful if used correctly and in
combination with other price confirming methods. Therefore, it is
necessary for any trader to master the Fibonacci rules if he wants
to experience real success in binary trading.
      </p>
