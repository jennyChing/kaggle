          <h2>
           Holiday Savings Guide: Extreme Couponers Share Secrets to Super Cheap but Fabulous Holidays
          </h2>
          <p>
           Love celebrating holidays in a big way, but on a budget? With a little cyclical planning, savvy shoppers can piggyback their holiday bargains and make them stretch all year long!
          </p>
          <p>
           Of course, we all know couponing is a surefire way to save moneyhowever, there are other things you can do throughout the year to make your $ go even further.
          </p>
          <p>
           We have queried our network of expert extreme couponers, and compiled a list thatll guide you through the calendar. Ready to start the seasonal fun? Lets take a trip through a years worth of savings!
          </p>
          <h3>
           Lets Start with the Official Holiday Crunch Time: Thanksgiving!
          </h3>
          <h3>
           <span style="line-height: 1.6em; font-size: 14px;">
            Nows the best time to start your cyclical round of annual savings. On your mark, get set, grab your coupons!
           </span>
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Surprise! Turkey isnt cheaper after the holidays. Its actually the cheapest the week of Thanksgiving.
            </span>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Stores like
             <a href="http://www.grocerycouponnetwork.com/view/walmart">
              Walmart
             </a>
             and
             <a href="http://www.grocerycouponnetwork.com/view/kroger">
              Kroger
             </a>
             use turkeys and other meats as a loss leader to get shoppers in to buy other groceries.
            </span>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             So grab a couple on sale: Eat one and freeze one for later.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Everythings on sale this week. Check specials on canned and shelf-stable items.
            </span>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Dont forget to use coupons/double coupons, and youll pay virtually nothing.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Were you clever and bought up some pumpkins on the cheap November 1? Pumpkin-flavored desserts and stews work for Thanksgiving and Christmas.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Leftover cooked turkey can be frozen for several months.
            </span>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Freeze with a bit of turkey broth to keep the pieces moist, and youll have picnic sandwiches for Fourth of July!
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Youll get your best deals on food pre-Thanksgiving, but it goes without saying that big retail days such as
             <a href="http://www.grocerycouponnetwork.com/community/black-friday-2013-store-deals-round/">
              Black Friday super deals
             </a>
             , Cyber Monday and Small Business Saturday are a fine place to begin Christmas shopping.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h3>
           December Holiday Season
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Everyone loves turkey: Have it again for Christmas! Take out the extra bird you bought at Thanksgiving.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Or, take out the extra ham you purchased at Easter.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Didnt buy then? The week of Christmas will have excellent prices, too.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             If you made soup from your November bird, serve it along with the ham for variety.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Flesh out soup with homemade dumplings (cheap and tasty).
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Celebrating Hanukkah? Matzoh is a great economic soup stretcher as well!
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Day-old jelly doughnuts for your Hanukkah party will save bigcut each into quarters to show off pretty red centers, and arrange decoratively with gelt and cookies.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Still have champagne from earlier in the year? Make a festive Yuletide punch for open houses.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Got any red or white candy left over from February? Serve at parties.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Looking for a deal on an artificial tree? Consider negotiating with stores on their floor models. Most stores want to clear these early in December.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Live tree lots usually wont mind a bit if you haul off stray pine branches. Free decorand it smells great!
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Post-Christmas, stock up on super-cheap seasonally colored candy (M&amp;Ms, foil-wrapped chocolates).
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Nows the time to grab holiday greeting cards, wrapping paper, and home dcor. Fill up your closet directly following the holiday season when prices are rock-bottom.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             If you find a card or paper design you love on sale, buy enough for several years to comewhy not? Its not like it goes out of style
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Stock up on as many cheap shiny ball ornaments as you can. Use them to fill bowls for centerpieces, attach to bows on presents, even create clever wreaths and mantle decor. All for basically pennies!
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Dont forget to buy some red candles, red paper plates, and red napkins on sale!
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h3>
           New Years Eve
          </h3>
          <p>
           A few weeks before the big night, buy an extra bottle or two of champagne on sale.Store the champagne in a cool place, such as your pantry, and on its side to keep the cork wet.
          </p>
          <p>
           Everyones still in a holiday mood, so dont be afraid to use some of your leftover Christmas or Hanukkah decorsilver, gold, etc.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Super Bowl Sunday
          </h3>
          <p>
           Stock up on football-related dcor on sale at the start of the regular season and show it off at the Super Bowl party.
          </p>
          <p>
           Need an economical crowd-pleaser recipe? Buy several containers of economy-sized salsa and sour cream, plus a few bags of tortilla chips. These items always have good coupons. Pull out some leftover turkey, and treat your football lovin friends to turkey nachos. There should be no leftovers here.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Valentines Day
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Stock up on Halloween and Christmas candy the days following those holidays when all the stores have them on sale! Fasten to your kids homemade classroom valentines for sweet treats.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Bought an extra bottle of bubbly to ring in the New Year? Treat your honey to a romantic dinner with the champers.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Use red candles bought cheap post-Christmas to round out the fun.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             You can also use red napkins and paper plates purchased at Christmas for classroom parties.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Post-holiday, hit the stores and buy up a variety of rock-bottom-priced paper valentines for the kids to exchange next year.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Small stuffed toys, bath sets, perfumes, and other post-Valentines discounted goods can be snapped up and stored for Christmas stocking stuffers.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Buy up red and white-colored or wrapped chocolates/candies on sale and throw them in the freezer for future celebrations.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h3>
           Easter
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Ham is a cooked and cured meat that freezes beautifully, so buy extra while its on sale.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Get a little creative here: Did you buy Christmas-colored M&amp;Ms? Pick out the green ones. Theyll make a great nest for your chocolate bunnies and chicks.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Love Marshmallow Peeps? Believe it or not, you can use them all year round, and they have a crazy-long shelf life. Buy em on salecheck Pinterest for creative dessert recipes.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Off-season, buy reusable wicker baskets at a discount home goods store. Slap on some re-used ribbon you have lying around and youve got yourself some cute Easter baskets.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Again, as with Valentines Day, look for small toys that dont scream Easter at the post-holiday sales. Perfect for those December stockings
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h3>
           Fourth of July
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Did you buy any blue Peeps at Easter? Use with red and white M&amp;Ms from Christmas to bake cookies or fun cupcakes (check Pinterest for recipes).
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Take out some of the turkey and/or ham you have in the freezer from Christmas and/or Easter. Make picnic sandwiches or bulk up a macaroni salad.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Any Xmas red paper plates/napkins left over? Combine with white and blue dcor.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Keep an eye out for any post-4th of July holiday meats on sale you can throw into the freezer and use for back-to-school lunches in two months.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             On the non-edible side, pool toys and bathing suits will be discounted to almost nothing now. Pick them up for next year.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Planning to buck tradition and go somewhere warm for the holidays? Wrap up these discounted summer items for Christmas presents.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             School supplies will be hitting the stores soon. Stock up your cupboards for the entire year.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h3>
           Halloween
          </h3>
          <p>
           The average American spends $75.03 on Halloween decorations, costumes and candy. And a whole lot of people are spending even more. Theres no need to spend that much!
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Give out generic, non-holiday branded candy such as Fun Size chocolate. The fancy Halloween packing usually costs more.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Halloween costumes also go way down in price right after the holiday. Purchase a size ahead for the kids next year.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Afraid your tyke wont want to be a witch next year? Buy anyway, and hold a swap party with like-minded friends the following October.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Get creative. A little fake blood and some dark eyeshadow for under the eyes goes a long way to create that perfect dead look. Get your hair wet and rub flour in it to get that crusty look. Take the flour and water mixture and rub it on your face in small clumps, let it dry and crack, and youre an automatic zombie whos been dead and dry for a while.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Light up the night. Save those milk jugs, paint ghost faces on them, cut out the bottom and stick a candle in there, then line your driveway with them to welcome to trick or treaters in.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Halloween
             <a href="http://www.grocerycouponnetwork.com/view/candy-dessert">
              candy goes on super-sale
             </a>
             the days after the holiday. Stock up now, freeze and save year round! You can use these for Christmas stockings, Valentines parties, Easter baskets, etc.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Do you like pumpkin? Theres lots of lonely (and cheap) pumpkins just waiting to find their home in muffins, pies, breads, etc. post Halloween!
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div>
           Happy saving and we hope you have some fabulous parties!
          </div>
