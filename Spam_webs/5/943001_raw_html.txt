             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               Lionel Messi was awarded the Golden Ball for
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/world-cup/">
                World Cup
               </a>
               MVP. Did he deserve it? Maybe not, but he's still an all-time great, and don't you forget it.
              </p>
              <p>
               Messi scored four goals in the 2014 World Cup, but none in the knockout stage. Thomas Mller, meanwhile, scored five goals for Germany en route to the World Cup title.
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/06/28/james-rodriguez-world-cup-colombia/">
                James Rodriguez
               </a>
               was transcendent and revelatory in crashing the global soccer scene while taking the Golden Boot award for most goals, with six.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/07/13/world-cup-final-germany-argentina-live/">
                 World Cup Final: Germany Beats Argentina 1-0 in Extra Time
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               But Messi carried more weight and pressure during this World Cup than anyone other than the
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/07/08/brazil-germany-world-cup-loss/">
                Brazilian national team
               </a>
               . He may not have been the
               <em>
                most
               </em>
               deserving Golden Ball candidate, but he was in the conversation.
              </p>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="THUMB-emomessifixed" class="fullwidth" data-caption="" data-credit-link="" data-credit-name="" data-credit-provider="" data-width="300" src="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzA3LzEzL2IyL1RIVU1CZW1vbWVzLjU3N2QyLmdpZgpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/e09b8012/d32/THUMB-emomessifixed.gif" title="THUMB-emomessifixed"/>
               </p>
              </figure>
              <p>
               Still, look at the GIF above. Look how sad he looks accepting the Golden Ball just after Argentina's last-minute loss to Germany in the World Cup final. That's not a happy man.
              </p>
              <p>
               We know how this narrative plays out. We see it happen time and time again  perhaps the best recent analogy is
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/lebron-james/">
                LeBron James
               </a>
               , who is so mercilessly scrutinized by media and fans alike for anything his team fails to achieve or he does in a perceived-as-graceless manner.
              </p>
              <p>
               Messi's talent, his heart, his moment-ness, his
               <em>
                legacy
               </em>
               will be dissected ad nauseam in the coming days. Some will come to his defense. Some will tear him apart as little but the product of an FC Barcelona dynasty.
              </p>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="THUMB-sadmessigoldenball" class="fullwidth" data-caption="" data-credit-link="" data-credit-name="" data-credit-provider="" data-width="300" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzA3LzEzLzQzL1RIVU1Cc2FkbWVzLjU5MmQ4LmdpZgpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/60b0fa1c/e39/THUMB-sadmessigoldenball.gif" title="THUMB-sadmessigoldenball"/>
               </p>
              </figure>
              <p>
               But let's get one thing straight here: Lionel Messi is an all-time soccer great. He has been for years, and he always will be. Sure, the World Cup trophy would have cemented his "legacy" in the 2014 hot-takes ecosystem  and for good reason. But he didn't need it to prove his worth.
              </p>
              <p>
               Messi is still a transcendent player to be appreciated and admired as a magician and gift to soccer fans the world over.
              </p>
              <p>
               We're lucky to get to watch him play this game. Even if his face while accepting the Golden Ball carries all of the sadness of Argentina's disappointed fans.
              </p>
