         <h1 class="articletitle">
          Designing a website? Here are the mistakes to avoid
         </h1>
        </div>
        <div class="author">
         <img class="avatar" src="http://45.55.255.229/YHOefuHvMls9uoh0LJUqBryte6Q=/34x34/smart/http://45.55.255.229/user/image/611066/profile_image-611066.jpg"/>

          <span class="name">
           Eric  Schaal
          </span>
          on Nov 05, 2014
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="bodycopy">
         <p>
          Every website has an expiration date. While the cost and effort to design a replacement may seem daunting for business owners of any size, you could usher in a new era for your brand with the right approach.
         </p>
         <p>
          On the first day of ad:tech New York, website design was the focus of a creative session by Stephanie Balderrama,President, Consumer Insights Group, LLC. Balderrama offered key insights she learned from working with the world's top brands for"
          <a href="http://ad-tech.com/ny/creativesession7" rel="nofollow">
           What Not to Wear: Website Edition
          </a>
          ."
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are 10 mistakes to avoid in your website design.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           1. Ignoring the target
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          If there were one thing to remember when designing a website, it would be focusing on your target market. "Knowing your consumers is hands-down the way to win the game," Balderrama said. Studying your customer' likes, dislikes, preferences online and everything else about your audience should come first.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           2. Lacking a clear purpose
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you are selling something, there should be a simple, direct path for consumers to identify and buy it. Balderrama noted how many sites merely infer what visitors should do. A better approach is presenting a clear course of action to whatever the site's purpose may be.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           3. Presenting weak visuals
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Consumers expect to be entertained as well as sold goods when they visit a website, so building one without captivating visuals is a mistake. Companies should focus on visuals that are appropriate and enhance the brand's relevance in the industry. However, this strategy can backfire when the visuals come at the expense of functionality.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           4. Overwhelming at the home page
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Balderrama noted how busy home pages often mean a quick exit from the website. While it is OK to highlight content and grab attention, allow visitors to find the content with simple navigation. Grabbing attention and flattening it are different things.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           5. Hindering access to content
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Blocking users' access to content with auto-play ads, excessive pagination and other tired tactics will end up backfiring with today's instant-gratification audience. Allow an easy path to your site content and trust its quality will deliver on the monetization front.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           6. Passing on winning details
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          There are many ways to keep visitors on pages while offering them more information. Balderrama noted how Netflix and HBO GO use the hover feature to great effect. Users get a preview of the content without having to click through. In a more general bit of advice, she advised using tabs in website design. Tabs are widely considered more user-friendly than alternatives.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           7.Botching search functions
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Every site user is one click away from Google or Bing, so it is important for websites to get search functions right. According to Balderrama, the right approach is offering clear search at the top of the site with broad search terms. She also noted the drawbacks of global search.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           8.Forgetting about screens
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Most Web traffic is being viewed on mobile devices, so neglecting this screen (or tablets or desktops) in the design process is a huge mistake. Make sure your site is functional in every platform.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           9.Missing out on curated content
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Studies have shown consumers love curated content when handled correctly. It offers a sense of personalization and individual service. When it is poorly curated, on the other hand, Balderrama highlighted how it can feel creepy or self-serving.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           10. Changing too much in a redesign
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          During a website redesign, you don't want to alienate your regular visitors. Balderrama suggested keeping familiar elements and letting visitor behaviors evolve when launching the new version. No matter how great it is, visiting the site should not feel like work to loyal customers.
         </p>
         <p>
          What not to do with a website might sound like common sense, but some of the world's best brands have made these mistakes. You owe it to your brand to get it right the first time.
         </p>
