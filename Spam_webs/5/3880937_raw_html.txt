         <p style="text-align: center;">

          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Last year, I spent the day observing internationally acclaimed stylists
          <a href="http://www.pearlandgodiva.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.pearlandgodiva.com/', 'Pearl &amp; Godiva');" target="_blank">
           Pearl &amp; Godiva
          </a>
          set up for a beautiful wedding in an old Irish estate (in that gorgeous image above!). I arrived earlier on the morning of the wedding and lent a hand while everything for the wedding tables was being unpacked and unwrapped. Just that phase felt like it took forever! And it did, in part because it was a large wedding with lots of tables to set, but also because the stylists at Pearl &amp; Godiva are masters of their craft. It was such a treat to watch them work and I tried to pick up as many tips for styling
          <a href="http://www.weddingpartyapp.com/blog/2014/04/30/wedding-reception-seating-arrangements-pros-cons-table-layout/" target="_blank">
           wedding tables
          </a>
          as I could throughout the day.
         </p>
         <p>
          As youre planning your wedding, youre probably scanning photo after photo of beautiful tables, like the ones in this post. But you might be finding it difficult to figure out exactly why a tables style works so well. Well, Ive broken down five tips for styling wedding tables that will make your planning process a little simpler. Lets look at what makes the most beautiful wedding tables so youll know what to look for in yours!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://media01.weddingpartyapp.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Borris-House-Wedding-by-Brosnan-Photographic-20-e1408474584817.jpg?74e275">
           <img alt="5 tips for styling wedding tables" class="aligncenter wp-image-29106 size-full" height="820" src="http://media01.weddingpartyapp.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Borris-House-Wedding-by-Brosnan-Photographic-20-e1408474584817.jpg?74e275" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.brosnanphotographic.com/2013/07/audrey-mikes-wedding-at-borris-house/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.brosnanphotographic.com/2013/07/audrey-mikes-wedding-at-borris-house/', 'Brosnan Photographic');" target="_blank">
           Brosnan Photographic
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">

          </span>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Layers
         </h2>
         <p>
          If theres one thing I learned watching the pros, its that the most beautiful wedding tables are built with layers  I thought the stylists were finished styling when these tables were only half finished. But when they were done, I realized thats the difference between a good and a great table. They started with a clean, blank canvas, and they began layering various textiles that complemented each other, metals that reflected when the candles were lit, and everything at different levels to create interest. They also, however, know where to stop. Too many things on a table can make it seem cluttered, so youll want to take a step back and make sure you havent gone over board.
         </p>
         <p>
          One of the best tipsI can recommend if youre planning and styling your own tables is to set one full table as fully as you can ahead of time so you can see roughly how it will look.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://media01.weddingpartyapp.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/wedding-table-styling-pinecone.jpg?74e275">
           <img alt="Style wedding table decor pinecone rustic DIY" class="aligncenter wp-image-29104 size-full" height="900" src="http://media01.weddingpartyapp.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/wedding-table-styling-pinecone.jpg?74e275" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.brooketrexler.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.brooketrexler.com/', 'Brooke Trexler Photography');" target="_blank">
           Brooke Trexler Photography
          </a>
          via
          <a href="http://www.weddingchicks.com/gallery/beautifulspringwedding/?pid=40725" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.weddingchicks.com/gallery/beautifulspringwedding/?pid=40725', 'Wedding Chicks');" target="_blank">
           Wedding Chicks
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Height
         </h2>
         <p>
          Another key to amazing wedding tables is getting the height right. Ive been to too many weddings where the flower arrangement was giant and exactly at eye level, making conversation across the table completely impossible. You want your guests to be able to enjoy each others company, so keep your flower arrangements just below average eye level.
         </p>
         <p>
          Do, however, mix in different height arrangements and accessories. If everything decorating the table is the same height  low or high  the table will look flat and static. You can also mix up the heights of arrangements from table to table, so each table has a different feel as you look out across the room. This can also help guests find their tables when theyre coming off the dance floor later in the evening!
         </p>
         <h2>
          Candles
         </h2>
         <p>
          You can never have too many candles, especially at a wedding. But just like the height of your arrangements and accessories, mix up your candles. Tea lights and pillar candles are inexpensive and can add so much ambiance to your table arrangements. Stock up somewhere like Ikea or a wholesale supplier  any extras you might have can be used on window sills and in entryways to give added glow to those areas.
         </p>
         <p>
          You can add height with candelabras, but beware of the bare silver candlestick look. Make sure to soften the look by draping bunting, beads or even ivy around the candlesticks. My brother-in-law recently got married and the hotel provided silver candelabras for each table. We were shocked at the difference adding a little wild ivy made to the look of the entire room. It went from stark and a little tacky to rustic and inviting in a snap.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://media01.weddingpartyapp.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/wedding-table-styling-2.jpg?74e275">
           <img alt="5 tips for styling wedding tables" class="aligncenter wp-image-29100 size-full" height="900" src="http://media01.weddingpartyapp.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/wedding-table-styling-2.jpg?74e275" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.bethhelmstetter.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bethhelmstetter.com/', 'Beth Helmstetter');" target="_blank">
           Beth Helmstetter
          </a>
          via
          <a href="http://www.stylemepretty.com/gallery/picture/155624/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.stylemepretty.com/gallery/picture/155624/', 'Style Me Pretty');" target="_blank">
           Style Me Pretty
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Textures
         </h2>
         <p>
          Part of your layering should involve adding in different textures, with fabrics, accessories or even stationery. You want a few things to stand out on the table and pull your guests in to look closer. Textiles and fabrics instantly add a warmer texture and can also be an easy and inexpensive way to add color to a plain old white tablecloth. At my wedding, we found patterned fabric and used it to make sort of large square table runners in the center of the round tables. It added cheerful color as a base to layercandles and flower arrangements onto.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Details
         </h2>
         <p>
          The last layer of the equation involves the little details thatcan add that last bit of personality to your wedding tables. Like the pinecones in the image above, you can continue to tie in any theme you might have with smaller details on your wedding tables. Your table numbers can add that detail, whether theyre in frames or sitting up on their own (like
          <a href="http://www.confetti.ie/article/super-sweet-diy-table-numbers" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.confetti.ie/article/super-sweet-diy-table-numbers', 'these');" target="_blank">
           these
          </a>
          ). Or adding little favors at each plate adds a unique detail as well.
         </p>
         <p>
          So what do you think, have we demystified the art of styling wedding tables just a little for you? Are you ready to try out your own to practice for your big day  or even an upcoming dinner party?
