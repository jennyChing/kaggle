        <h1 itemprop="name">
         Sichuan-style chicken stir-fry
        </h1>
        <div class="headerDetails">
         <!-- Copyright Fairfax Media 2013. -->
         <!-- Author Photo and Name -->
         <div class="authorPhoto">
          <img alt="Neil Perry" height="90" src="http://images.goodfood.com.au/2012/11/28/3842911/Neil_Perry.jpg?rand=1354070227880" width="90"/>
          <div class="authorDetails">

          Sichuan pepper heats things up in this classic Chinese-style stir-fry.  Add chopped fresh chillies or a spoon of chilli bean paste for extra bite. I also like it with snow peas or baby corn. You could change up the dish by replacing the chicken with thinly sliced beef or pork.

        <div class="cN-simpleList listIngredients" itemprop="ingredients">
         <h3>
          Ingredients
         </h3>
         <p>
          450g skinless, free-range chicken breasts, cut into 3cm pieces
         </p>
         <p>
          1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns
         </p>
         <p>
          peanut oil, for deep frying (about 3 cups)
         </p>
         <p>
          1 cup fresh basil leaves
         </p>
         <p>
          4 long green shallots, finely sliced
         </p>
         <p>
          1 tbsp shaoxing (Chinese wine)
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           For the marinade
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          1 tbsp oyster sauce
         </p>
         <p>
          1/2 tbsp shaoxing wine
         </p>
         <p>
          1 1/2 tsp fish sauce
         </p>
         <p>
          1 1/2 tsp sesame oil
         </p>
         <p>
          1 tbsp Chinese dark soy sauce
         </p>
         <p>
          1 tsp chopped ginger, pounded
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           For the sauce
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          1/4 cup fresh chicken stock
         </p>
         <p>
          1 tbsp oyster sauce
         </p>
         <p>
          1 1/4 tsp corn flour
         </p>
         <p>
          1/2 tsp castor sugar
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </div>
        <!-- class:cN-simpleList listIngredients -->
        <div itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         <h3>
          Method
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Mix together
          </strong>
          the marinade ingredients. Coat the chicken. Set aside for 30 minutes.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Combine
          </strong>
          the sauce ingredients and set aside.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Dry roast
          </strong>
          the peppercorns in a hot wok for 20   seconds, then lower the heat and stir for about a minute until their   fragrance is released. Cool, then coarsely crush them in a mortar with a   pestle.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Heat the peanut oil
          </strong>
          in a wok to 150C (use a   thermometer). Ensure the basil is completely dry, then in two batches   fry the leaves for 2-3 minutes until crispy and bright green. Carefully   remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towel.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Heat the oil again,
          </strong>
          this time until smoking (about   180C). Drain the chicken from the marinade, then deep-fry it, turning   the pieces constantly until golden, about 3 minutes. Turn off the heat   and lift out the chicken, draining on paper towel.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Pour off
          </strong>
          most of the oil from the wok into a   saucepan, leaving 2 tablespoons behind. Heat the wok and as the oil just   begins to smoke, add the pounded peppercorns and half the shallots.   Stir-fry for about 30 seconds, return the chicken and stir-fry for a   minute, deglazing with the shaoxing. Make a well in the centre and add   the sauce. Stir fry for a final 2 minutes until the sauce is absorbed.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Turn off
          </strong>
          the heat and transfer the chicken to a platter. Garnish with remaining shallots and fried basil, then serve with steamed rice.
         </p>
         <p>
