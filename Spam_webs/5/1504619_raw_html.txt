         <p>
          We love cocktails, a lot. And although the
          <a href="http://vinepair.com/wine-blog/history-cosmopolitan-birth-craft-cocktail-culture/" title="The History Of The Cosmopolitan And The Birth Of Craft Cocktail Culture">
           reemergence of cocktail culture
          </a>
          means there are hundredsof great cocktail bars across the world whipping up new cocktails every night of the week, we still love the classics. But when acocktail attainsclassic status, its often hard to remember how and where it originated in the first place. Which is why we made this map. We dove through history books  and made a few trips to our favorite bars in the name of research  to uncover the originsof more than 30 famous cocktails. Now head to the birthplaces of these great cocktails and enjoy them even more.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://vinepair.com/features/external/map-famous-cocktail-birthplaces-1300px.jpg">
           <img alt="MAP: The Birthplaces Of More Than 30 Famous Cocktails" class="alignnone" height="2190" src="http://vinepair.com/features/external/map-famous-cocktail-birthplaces-1300px.jpg" width="1300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Famous Cocktails Whose Origins Are In Dispute:
         </h3>
         <p>
          While we were able to dig up the history on more than 30 cocktails, the originsof some of the worlds most iconic drinks have been lost to time. Heres a quick rundown on why four of those cocktails were impossible to map:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Cosmopolitan
           </strong>
            Where this
           <a href="http://vinepair.com/wine-blog/history-cosmopolitan-birth-craft-cocktail-culture/" target="_blank" title="The History Of The Cosmopolitan And The Birth Of Craft Cocktail Culture">
            <em>
             Sex and the City-c
            </em>
            onnected cocktail was born is an East Coast-West Coast argument
           </a>
           that predates Biggie and 2Pac.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Margarita
           </strong>
            We recently dove into
           <a href="http://vinepair.com/wine-blog/the-mystery-behind-who-really-created-the-margarita/" target="_blank" title="The Mystery Behind Who Really Created The Margarita">
            the history of the margarita
           </a>

            restored to prominence by
            <em>
             Mad Men
            </em>
            -name checking
           </a>
           ) is hiding in a 19th century fog.
