           <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
             <p>
              Not long ago, I wrote a post about
              <a href="http://www.yummymummyclub.ca/blogs/gwen-leron-50-shades-of-green/20140112/what-is-butylated-hydroxytoluene-bht" target="_blank">
               Butylated Hydroxytoluene (BHT)
              </a>
              , why it may be in your favourite cereals, and how to avoid it. Since then, I have received a few messages from readers saying that they would like to start buying cereals that do not contain this and other artificial preservatives but they werent sure where to start or how to find versions of their childrens favourites (and theirs!) that were BHT-free.
             </p>
             <p>
              A good tip is that if a cereal is certified organic (meaning, the packaging carries one of the official certified organic seals), then it does not contain BHT or any other artificial additives or preservatives.
             </p>
             <p>
              Here is a list of healthier substitutes to get you started:
             </p>
             <h2>
              Crispy Rice Cereal
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/crispy_rice_cereal.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 304px;"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://ca-en.naturespath.com/product/crispy-rice-cereal" target="_blank">
               Natures Path Crispy Rice Cereal
              </a>
              is all organic along with all of their other products. There are many reasons to support this conscientious Canadian company, two of them are that their products are all GMO-free and they
              <a href="http://ca-en.naturespath.com/about/our-minifesto" target="_blank">
               take care and pride
              </a>
              in all the ingredients they put into their products.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Wheat Squares
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/organic_wheat_squares.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 273px;"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.presidentschoice.ca/content/pc/en_CA/products/productlisting/pc_organics_wheat_squares10748.html" target="_blank">
               PC Organics Wheat Squares cereal
              </a>
              is just one of the organic cereals offered in this line.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Corn Flakes
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/corn_flakes.jpg" style="width: 201px; height: 286px;"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Another Natures Path favourite is their
              <a href="http://ca-en.naturespath.com/product/fruit-juice-sweetened-corn-flakes" target="_blank">
               fruit juice sweetened Corn Flakes
              </a>
              . If you like your corn flakes on the sweeter side, they also make
              <a href="http://ca-en.naturespath.com/product/honeydr-corn-flakes" target="_blank">
               Honeyd Corn Flakes
              </a>
              that are sweetened with evaporated cane juice and, you guessed it, honey.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Wheat Biscuits
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/kashi_organic_promise_cinnamon_harvest.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 299px;"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://www.kashi.com/our-foods/cold-cereal/kashi-cinnamon-harvest-whole-wheat-biscuits" target="_blank">
               Kashis Cinnamon Harvest Whole Wheat Biscuits
              </a>
              make an excellent organic (and GMO-free) substitute if this type of cereal is a fave in your home.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Raisin Bran
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/organic_raisin_bran.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 304px;"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Not many children like Raisin Bran cereal, but many parents do.
              <a href="http://ca-en.naturespath.com/product/flax-plusr-raisin-bran-flakes" target="_blank">
               Natures Paths Flax Plus Raisin Bran
              </a>
              is a great stand-in.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Whole Os
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/whole_os.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 296px;"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Kids love this type of cereal, and again, Natures Path comes through with a healthier organic version,
              <a href="http://ca-en.naturespath.com/product/whole-ostm-cereal" target="_blank">
               Whole Os cereal
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <h2>
              Granola
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/love_grown_cereal.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 316px;"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              There are many excellent organic granola cereals on the market, but my absolute favourite is by a company called
              <a href="http://lovegrownfoods.com/products" target="_blank">
               Love Grown Foods
              </a>
              . Their cereals are not certified organic, but they are made with quality ingredients and contain no artificial preservatives or additives, just straight-up wholesome ingredients.
             </p>
             <p>
              All of these cereals can be found either in the natural section of your grocery store or at your local health food store.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Have you found any other preservative-free cereals that have become new family favourites?
              </strong>
             </p>
