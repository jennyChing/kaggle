           <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
            A US court today granted a teenage girl all trademark rights to the mobile photo phenomena of selfies. We are yet to learn who this individual is but we are sure the whole world will find out very soon.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            This trademark means that anyone who takes, or wants to be part of, a selfie must pay the trademark owner a royalty. It is a small fee at 0.01 per image, but at the rate selfies are uploaded to social networks, this savvy teenager will be the richest individual in the world approximately 14 minutes after the trademark goes live.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            However, there is a loophole TechDump has discovered. The trademark does not cover taking selfies of selfies. It will of course mean you will need a second camera phone, and the original selfie cannot be used. The technique of the 'selfie selfie' is simple, whilst you're taking a selfie with camera phone 1, use camera phone 2 to take a second selfie of yourself taking the original selfie. Both images must be taken at the same time but only the image taken with camera phone 2 can be used..
            <br/>
            <br/>
            So you've heard it here first folks, 'selfie selfies' will be the next hottest trend to hit an Instagram account near you soon.
