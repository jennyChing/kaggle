          <section class="story cf">
           <p>
            This is an oldie, but a goodie. Ive seen more timelapses than there are frames in one, but this is easily one of my favorites. I can only describe it as a euphoric tilt shift dream that encompasses all of humanity and joy. Just watch the video and youll understand.
           </p>
           <p>
            <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" src="//player.vimeo.com/video/49753231" width="500">
            </iframe>
           </p>
           <p>
            Photographer
            <a href="http://keithloutit.com/#personal">
             Keith Loutit
            </a>
            has developed a tilt-shift timelapse combo and hes been working on experimental focus and light transition techniques.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="6" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-186143" height="348" src="http://www.slrlounge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/61.jpg" width="650"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            From an interview with Planet5D Loutit says
           </p>
           <p>
            Because its the first time Ive released in this style, the film is hard hitting, and full of effects more so than if I were releasing a story based concept. Many of the scenes are not really tilt shift.. theyre what I call clean shocks, or tilt shocks, depending on whether I choose to keep the tilt shift effect, and these are the focus of most of my experiments now going forward.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-186142" height="350" src="http://www.slrlounge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/51.jpg" width="650"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            For camera bodies Loutit uses the
            <a class="slr-aff-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002SQKVD0/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B002SQKVD0&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=slrlounge01-20" rel="nofollow external" target="_blank">
             Nikon D3S
            </a>
            ,
            <a class="slr-aff-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B006U49XM6/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B006U49XM6&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=slrlounge01-20" rel="nofollow external" target="_blank">
             Nikon D4
            </a>
            , and the
            <a class="slr-aff-link" href="http://www.bhphotovideo.com/c/product/583953-REG/Canon_2764B003_EOS_5D_Mark_II.html/BI/5982/KBID/6868/kw/CAE5D2/DFF/d10-v2-t1-xCAE5D2" rel="nofollow external" target="_blank">
             Canon 5D Mark II
            </a>
            . For camera lenses, this is where it gets interesting. Loutit uses large format bellows, classic Rodogon lenses, classic Nikon lenses, and modified enlarger lenses.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-186141" height="349" src="http://www.slrlounge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/42.jpg" width="650"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Loutit also mentions I havent fully made the transition from optical to post processed effects yet, as I find the lenses often survive the color grade better.
           </p>
           <p>
            Even with his list of gear I can still only imagine how he got these amazing shots. I can only hope for a behind-the-scenes video. Let me know how you think he shot this timelapse in the comments!
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-186139" height="350" src="http://www.slrlounge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/2.jpg" width="650"/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-186138" height="353" src="http://www.slrlounge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/1.jpg" width="650"/>
           </p>
