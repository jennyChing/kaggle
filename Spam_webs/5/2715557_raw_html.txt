       Broadway Box Office 3/22: "Finding Neverland" Takes Flight
      </h1>

      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         The grosses from last week are in, and the new musical
         <em>
          Finding Neverland
         </em>
         was
         <a href="http://www.broadwayworld.com/grosses.cfm?sortby=diffdollars&amp;orderby=desc#.VRB0jPnF9Js" target="_blank">
          the clear winner
         </a>
         . Though it didnt nab the number onespot (
         <em>
          Lion King, Wicked,
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          Book of Mormon
         </em>
         continued their musical chairs act at the top of the list), it played to standing-room crowds at a higher price point than any other new show, raking in over $1 million off only 7 performances.A much publicized
         <a href="http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2015/01/23/after-dispute-harvey-weinstein-parts-ways-with-the-press-agent-for-finding-neverland/?_r=0" target="_blank">
          change of press agency
         </a>
         seems to have paid off for the film adaptation, and word of mouth is strong. The show is produced by Hollywood titan Harvey Weinstein.
        </p>
        <p>
         Otherwise it was mostly business as usual. Heavy hitters
         <em>
          Matilda, Kinky Boots, Fish In The Dark,
         </em>
         <em>
          Aladdin
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          The Audience
         </em>
         all pulled in over $1 million each.
         <em>
          Its Only A Play
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          Honeymoon in Vegas
         </em>
         continued their recent lackluster performances, and
         <em>
          Hedwig and the Angry Inch
         </em>
         took the weeks biggest tumble to the tune of almost $200k  a surprising shortfall given the strong performance of late.
        </p>
        <p>
         TheVanessa Hudgens vehicle
         <em>
          Gigi
         </em>
         had a strong first week taking in almost 50% of its potential weekly gross from only its first fourperformances. The two parts ofBritish import
         <em>
          Wolf Hall
         </em>
         had an even higher gross off the same number.
        </p>
        <p>
         Next week will see the addition of four new musicals on the docket:
         <i>
          Something Rotten, Dr. Zhivago, Fun Home,
         </i>
         and
         <em>
          The Visit.
         </em>
         Overall gross will probably decline as a result, as ticket buyers face market saturation.
        </p>
        <p>
         Total gross for all shows running was $26.million, up an impressive $4 million from last week. With spring officially sprung, audiences are coming out in droves to see the new crop of Tony hopefuls.
        </p>
