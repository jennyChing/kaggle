     <div class="story-header-left twoCol">
      <h1 class="title" itemprop="name">
       Barclays claims of investor protection a 'sham': NY AG
      </h1>
      <div class="source" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
       <a href="/matthew-j-belvedere/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
        Matthew J. Belvedere
       </a>
       |
       <span class="twitter-url">
        <a href="http://twitter.com/Matt_Belvedere">
         @Matt_Belvedere
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <time class="datestamp" datetime="2014-06-26T10:12:34-0400" itemprop="datePublished">
       Thursday, 26 Jun 2014 | 10:12  AM ET
      </time>
      <span class="sourcelogo" id="sourcelogo-cnbccom" itemprop="sourceOrganization">
       CNBC.com
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="story-header-right">
      <div>
       <div class="share-count-container hide">
        <div class="share-total">
        </div>

    <section class="cols2">
     <div class="unit col1">
      <article>
       <div class="story">
        <div class="content" id="article_body">
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="player embed-container cnbcvideo autoplay">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="player" data-src="http://player.cnbc.com/cnbc_global?byGuid=3000287826&amp;autoPlay=true&amp;endCardGuids=3000383239|3000383217|3000383353" frameborder="0" height="298" id="player_0_0" mozallowfullscreen="" msallowfullscreen="" oallowfullscreen="" scrolling="no" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="533">
            <p>
             Barclays' 'dark pool' protection a 'sham': NY AG
            </p>
            <p>
             NY Attorney General Eric Schneiderman, discusses allegations that Barclays demonstrated a disturbing disregard for "ordinary" investors and operated a "dark pool" that favored high-frequency traders.
            </p>
           </iframe>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            Investors thought they were diving into "safe waters" when using the so-called dark pool at
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="10720" data-inline-quote-symbol="BARC-GB" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/BARC-GB" target="_self">
             Barclays
            </a>
            , but those waters were "full of predators," New York Attorney General Eric Schneiderman told CNBC on Thursday.
           </p>
           <p>
            "Dark pools theoretically are places where large investors will go to protect themselves from high-frequency traders because there's less disclosure," he said. "A lot of these are big funds that are, in fact, trading for average people on the street. They're trading for pension funds."
           </p>
           <p>
            Schneiderman announced Wednesday a lawsuit against Barclaysaccusing the British bank of
            <asset_inline nid="101791206" target="_blank">
             giving an unfair edge
            </asset_inline>
            in the U.S. to high-frequency trading clients, even as it claimed to be protecting large investors from such traders.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            "This is supposedly a way that high-frequency traders can't tell what you're doing," he said. "We found in this case that in fact Barclays [was] providing huge informational advantages to high-frequency traders and deceiving ordinary investors about how safe the pool was."
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="embed-container image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
           <img alt="Attroney General Eric T. Schneiderman to sue Barclays Bank over securities fraud on June 25, 2014." height="298" src="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2014/06/25/101789989-photo[4].530x298.?v=1403729898" width="530"/>
           <meta content="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2014/06/25/101789989-photo[4].?v=1403729898" itemprop="image"/>
           <meta content="width='530'" itemprop="width"/>
           <meta content="height='298'" itemprop="height"/>
           <meta content="2014-06-25T16:58:18-0400" itemprop="datePublished"/>

           <meta content="2014-06-25T16:58:19-0400" itemprop="dateModified"/>
           <meta content="2014-06-25T16:54:40-0400" itemprop="uploadDate"/>
           <meta content="2099-12-31" itemprop="expires"/>

           <meta content="" itemprop="encodingFormat"/>
           <meta content="Trusha Chokshi | CNBC" itemprop="author"/>

           <meta content="Attroney General Eric T. Schneiderman to sue Barclays Bank over securities fraud on June 25, 2014." itemprop="caption"/>
           <div class="attribution">
            Trusha Chokshi | CNBC
           </div>
           <div class="caption">
            Attroney General Eric T. Schneiderman to sue Barclays Bank over securities fraud on June 25, 2014.
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            The suit
            <a href="/2014/06/25/ny-ag-levels-serious-allegations-at-barclays.html" target="_blank">
             alleges specific instances of outright fraud
            </a>
            by the Barclays Equities Electronic Trading Division, which operated a so-called dark pool alternative trading system.
           </p>
           <p>
            "They had this very elaborate presentation to investors about how safe their dark pool was  to protect their investors from what they called 'toxic, predatory, aggressive traders,' meaning high-frequency traders," Schneiderman said. "In fact, the whole system was a sham."
           </p>
           <p>
            Barclays said it's taking these allegations "very seriously," while cooperating with authorities and looking at the matter internally.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/06/25/ny-ag-levels-serious-allegations-at-barclays.html" target="_self">
             NY AG levels serious allegations at Barclays
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            The suit delivers another blow to CEO Antony Jenkins, who is trying to restore the bank's reputation after a series of scandals, including the alleged manipulation of
            <a href="/id/43266422" target="_blank">
             Libor
            </a>
            benchmark interest rates.
           </p>
           <p>
            The investigation has been progressing for up to a year but took on additional urgency in recent months, after best-selling author Michael Lewis released the book "Flash Boys," which said markets were rigged in favor of high-speed traders.
           </p>
           <p>
            "We alleged in the complaint that Barclays invited the predators [traders] in," Schneiderman said. "They gave information advantage. They charged them less per trade."
           </p>
           <p>
            Barclays did so to increase the trading volume in its pool, he said. "They wanted their pool to make money, but also there's a certain amount of prestige to be the largest in the United States."
           </p>
           <p>
            He said he has all the documents "showing that was what they were pushing for." Barclays called the dark pool inside the company, "The Franchise," he added.
           </p>
          </div>
