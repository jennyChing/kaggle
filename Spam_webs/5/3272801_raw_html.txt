        <h1 class="article-title">
         These Lamps Are Made Out Of Refurbished Cassettes
        </h1>
        <div class="article-meta">
         <span class="author">
          By Bogar Alonso
         </span>
          May 31 2013
        </div>
        <div class="header-socials share-widget" data-media="" data-permalink="http://thecreatorsproject.vice.com/blog/lamps-made-out-of-refurbished-cassettes" data-title="The Creators Project" data-topic="" data-tweet="The Creators Project" data-twitter-username="CreatorsProject" data-url="http://thecreatorsproject.vice.com/blog/lamps-made-out-of-refurbished-cassettes" id="">

         <img alt="These Lamps Are Made Out Of Refurbished Cassettes" class="srcN lazy" data-breakpoints="100% 320px,100% 640px,100% 1000px" data-hires-compression="90" data-lowres-compression="10" data-sources="//thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/article/lamps-made-out-of-refurbished-cassettes/f2978be4a4043c34febbf4c68e4cf0e5.jpg?crop=1xw:0.6445115810674723xh;*,*&amp;resize=320:* 320px,//thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/article/lamps-made-out-of-refurbished-cassettes/f2978be4a4043c34febbf4c68e4cf0e5.jpg?crop=1xw:0.6445115810674723xh;*,*&amp;resize=640:* 640px,//thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/article/lamps-made-out-of-refurbished-cassettes/f2978be4a4043c34febbf4c68e4cf0e5.jpg?crop=1xw:0.6445115810674723xh;*,*&amp;resize=1000:* 1000px" src="//thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/article/lamps-made-out-of-refurbished-cassettes/f2978be4a4043c34febbf4c68e4cf0e5.jpg?crop=1xw:0.6445115810674723xh;*,*&amp;resize=1000:*"/>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="These Lamps Are Made Out Of Refurbished Cassettes" class="srcN lazy" src="//thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/article/lamps-made-out-of-refurbished-cassettes/f2978be4a4043c34febbf4c68e4cf0e5.jpg?crop=1xw:0.6445115810674723xh;*,*&amp;resize=1000:*"/>
         </noscript>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="article-content">
        <p class="p1">
         <em>
          Cassette tapes floor lamp
         </em>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Whether turning computer keyboards into
         <a href="http://thecreatorsproject.vice.com/blog/trojan-rocking-horse-sculpture-created-using-computer-keys" target="_blank">
          a rocking horse
         </a>
         or old electronics into
         <a href="http://thecreatorsproject.vice.com/blog/these-coffee-tables-contain-futuristic-neon-cityscapes-made-from-recycled-electronics" target="_blank">
          futuristic coffee tables
         </a>
         , outdated technology can be a handy material to reappropriate into decorative pieces and interior furnishings. Your standard compact cassette might not be of much use anymore, but people will always need a way to brighten an apartment. Which is why
         <a href="http://www.cassetteisnotdead.com/" target="_blank">
          Cassette Is Not Dead
         </a>
         s idea to pair refurbished cassettes with lamps is so dazzling, as well as winning us all over by playing the nostalgia card too.
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         As a tribute to this old school format and to do its bit to help the earth from becoming one giant landfill, Cassette Is Not Dead turn old tapes into new lampsand they come in a variety of designs:
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <em>
          You can get hold of a box lamp for your empty at-home office desk.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <img alt="" src="http://assets2.thecreatorsproject.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/506dbc1ebdc942c296c323eb1b99a2cd.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 554px;"/>
        </p>
        <p class="p4">
        </p>
        <p class="p4">
         <em>
          Or, if youre cramped for space a small lamp might be optimal.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <img alt="" src="http://assets2.thecreatorsproject.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/9eb037f374804d86ad1cff96d289938f.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 379px;"/>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
        </p>
        <p class="p6">
         <em>
          Those with minimal counter space can choose to hang their lights...
         </em>
        </p>
        <p class="p6">
         <img alt="" src="http://assets2.thecreatorsproject.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/e66e5aad4198c3fb6eacb67d03b53a3c.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 484px;"/>
        </p>
        <p class="p6">
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <em>
          ...or set them on floor stands.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p class="p4">
         <img alt="" src="http://assets2.thecreatorsproject.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/1ac4cbcf3a07e542ab59ae1e08b459a9.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 640px;"/>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         The
         <a href="http://ooomydesign.bigcartel.com/" target="_blank">
          <span class="s3">
           option
          </span>
         </a>
         to dole out cash to buy these lamps exists (and they're not cheap), but Cassette Is Not Deads barter philosophy is far more inviting. As their website shows, a free lamp will be sent your way if you provide the adequate numbers and types of tapes:
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <a href="%22http://i.imgur.com/3Y7ctTw.png%22%20%5Ch">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/3Y7ctTw.png" style="width: 640px; height: 354px;"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="p4">
         Based in Valencia, Spain the designers behind Cassette Is Not Dead seem genuinely eager to help the world by repurposing unused goods into useful art and decorations to brighten your day. Plus, you can turn that collection of dusty old tapes into something genuinely usefulit's time to say goodbye to them, you can do it.
        </p>
