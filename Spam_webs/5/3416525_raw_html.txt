    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      <b>
       Black Jazz Records
      </b>
      was a
      <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">
       jazz
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">
       record label
      </a>
      founded in
      <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">
       Oakland, California
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allrecordlabels.com_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-allrecordlabels.com-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      during the early 1970s by jazz pianist Gene Russell.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allrecordlabels.com_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-allrecordlabels.com-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Russell founded the label as an alternative to traditional jazz invoking a more political and spiritual tone
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackjazz.com_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-blackjazz.com-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      often with funk overtones.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Black Jazz released various types of music including,
      <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">
       funk
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Free_jazz" title="Free jazz">
       free jazz
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Soul_jazz" title="Soul jazz">
       soul jazz
      </a>
      . According to a 1974 Billboard Magazine, one of the label's more successful artists, Doug Carn, sold more records than
      <a href="/wiki/Dave_Brubeck" title="Dave Brubeck">
       Dave Brubeck
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Ramsey_Lewis" title="Ramsey Lewis">
       Ramsey Lewis
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackjazz.com_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-blackjazz.com-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The label existed for six years during its first run and ended following the death of Russell.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackjazz.com_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-blackjazz.com-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
