     <p>
      The
      <b>
       GMG
      </b>
      (
      <i>
       Granatmaschinengewehr
      </i>
      or "grenade machine gun") is an
      <a href="/wiki/Automatic_firearm" title="Automatic firearm">
       automatic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Grenade_launcher" title="Grenade launcher">
       grenade launcher
      </a>
      developed by
      <a href="/wiki/Heckler_%26_Koch" title="Heckler &amp; Koch">
       Heckler &amp; Koch
      </a>
      for the
      <a href="/wiki/German_Army" title="German Army">
       German Army
      </a>
      . It is also often referred to as
      <b>
       GMW
      </b>
      or
      <b>
       GraMaWa
      </b>
      (
      <i>
       Granatmaschinenwaffe
      </i>
      ).

        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Design_details">
       Design details
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heckler_%26_Koch_GMG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design details">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      It fires
      <a href="/wiki/40_mm_grenade" title="40 mm grenade">
       40 mm grenades
      </a>
      at a rate of about 340 rounds per minute. The GMG is
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belt_(firearm)" title="Belt (firearm)">
       belt-fed
      </a>
      , and can be loaded from either side, making it easy to mount on most platforms. With a variety of day and night sights available, the GMG can be used for most medium range infantry support situations.
     </p>
     <p>
      The weapon is 1.09 m long and has a 415mm rifled barrel; the ammunition box has dimensions of 470x160x250 mm. The gun operates on a recoil operated blow back basis. It weighs 29kg; the tripod is an additional 11kg.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Testing_and_operation">

        edit

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The HK GMG was tested in the
      <a href="/wiki/Yuma,_Arizona" title="Yuma, Arizona">
       Yuma
      </a>
      desert in
      <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">
       Arizona
      </a>
      in 1997 in order to compete for future
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      contracts.
     </p>
