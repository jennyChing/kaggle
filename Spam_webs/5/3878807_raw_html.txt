     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Funj Sultanate of Sennar
      </b>
      (sometimes spelled
      <b>
       Sinnar
      </b>
      ), known in Sudanese traditions as the
      <b>
       Blue Sultanate
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      :
      <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">
        ;
       <i>
        As-Saltana az-Zarqa
       </i>
      </span>
      ),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      was a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultanate" title="Sultanate">
       sultanate
      </a>
      in the north of
      <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">
       Sudan
      </a>
      , named Funj after the
      <a href="/wiki/Funj_people" title="Funj people">
       ethnic group
      </a>
      of its dynasty or Sinnar (or Sennar) after
      <a href="/wiki/Sennar" title="Sennar">
       its capital
      </a>
      , which ruled a substantial area of northeast Africa between 1504 and 1821.
     </p>

       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sennar_(sultanate)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In the 15th century the part of
      <a href="/wiki/Nubia" title="Nubia">
       Nubia
      </a>
      formerly controlled by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Makuria" title="Makuria">
       Makuria
      </a>
      was home to a number of small states and subject to frequent incursions by desert nomads. The situation in
      <a href="/wiki/Alodia" title="Alodia">
       Alodia
      </a>
      is less well known, but it also seems as though that state had collapsed. The area was reunified under
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Abdallah_Jamma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Abdallah Jamma (page does not exist)">
       Abdallah Jamma
      </a>
      , the gatherer, who came from the eastern regions that had grown wealthy and powerful from the trade on the
      <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">
       Red Sea
      </a>
      . To him is ascribed the capture of Soba, which sank into unimportance: according to Reubeni, in the time of Amara Dunqas it was in ruins. Abdallahs status as Muslim hero is confirmed by traditions representing him marrying the daughter of a Hijazi holy man called Alshikh Hamd Abou Dunana who was burned in Abu Delaig, and as the eponymous ancestor of the ruling clan, the Abdallab.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Abdallah's empire was short lived as in the early 16th century the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Funj" title="Funj">
       Funj
      </a>
      people under
      <a href="/wiki/Amara_Dunqas" title="Amara Dunqas">
       Amara Dunqas
      </a>
      arrived from the south, having been driven north by the
      <a href="/wiki/Shilluk_people" title="Shilluk people">
       Shilluk
      </a>
      . The Funj defeated Abdallah and set up their own kingdom based at
      <a href="/wiki/Sennar" title="Sennar">
       Sennar
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">
       Religion
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sennar_(sultanate)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Religion">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Funj had originally practiced a religious mix of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_Traditional_Religion" title="African Traditional Religion">
       African Traditional Religion
      </a>
      and Christianity. Islam also had an important influence, and in 1523 the Sennar monarchy officially converted to Islam, though many elements of the previous beliefs continued.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_and_conflicts">
       Expansion and conflicts
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sennar_(sultanate)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expansion and conflicts">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Sennar expanded rapidly at the expense of neighboring states. Its power was extended over the
      <a href="/wiki/Al_Jazirah_(state)" title="Al Jazirah (state)">
       Gezira
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Butana" title="Butana">
       Butana
      </a>
      , the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayuda" title="Bayuda">
       Bayuda
      </a>
      , and southern
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kordofan" title="Kordofan">
       Kordofan
      </a>
      . This caused immediate tensions with its neighbours.
      <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">
       Ethiopia
      </a>
      felt it was much threatened but its internal problems prevented intervention. Newly
      <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">
       Ottoman
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">
       Egypt
      </a>
      also saw the new state as a threat and invaded in force, but then failed to conquer the area, so the Ottoman forces fortified the border and consolidated their hold on northern Nubia. This border would hold until 1821.
     </p>
     <p>
      Relations with Ethiopia were more strained as both states competed over lowlands between their two states. Eventually the Ethiopians moved their capital to nearby
      <a href="/wiki/Gondar" title="Gondar">
       Gondar
      </a>
      and secured their influence over these areas. Conflicts with the
      <a href="/wiki/Shilluk_people" title="Shilluk people">
       Shilluk
      </a>
      to the south continued, but later the two were forced into an uneasy alliance to combat the growing might of the
      <a href="/wiki/Dinka_people" title="Dinka people">
       Dinka
      </a>
      . Under Sultan
      <a href="/wiki/Badi_II" title="Badi II">
       Badi II
      </a>
      , Sennar defeated the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Taqali" title="Kingdom of Taqali">
       Kingdom of Taqali
      </a>
      to the west and made its ruler (styled Woster or Makk) his vassal.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Military_culture">
       Military culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sennar_(sultanate)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Military culture">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The armies of Sennar relied most on
      <a href="/wiki/Heavy_cavalry" title="Heavy cavalry">
       heavy cavalry
      </a>
      : horsemen drawn from the nobility, armed with long
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadsword" title="Broadsword">
       broadswords
      </a>
      as the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toe_stirrup" title="Toe stirrup">
       toe stirrups
      </a>
      they used did not permit the use of
      <a href="/wiki/Lance" title="Lance">
       lances
      </a>
      . These riders were armoured with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chain_mail" title="Chain mail">
       chain mail
      </a>
      while the horses were covered in thick
      <a href="/wiki/Quilt" title="Quilt">
       quilts
      </a>
      and copper headgear. A greater mass of troops were infantry carrying swords and armoured. This permanent standing army, the largest in East Africa until the 1810s, was garrisoned in castles and forts throughout the sultanate. Reliance on a standing army meant that the professional armies fielded by Sennar were usually smaller, but highly effective against their less organized rivals.
     </p>
     <p>
      The sultanate was heavily divided along geographic and racial/ethnic lines. The society was divided into six racial groups. There was a sharp division between those who were the heirs of the ancient kingdom of
      <a href="/wiki/Alodia" title="Alodia">
       Alodia
      </a>
      and the rest of Sennar. The Alodians adopted the mantle of the defeated
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Abdallah_Jamma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Abdallah Jamma (page does not exist)">
       Abdallah Jamma
      </a>
      and came to be known as the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Abdallab&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Abdallab (page does not exist)">
       Abdallab
      </a>
      . In the late 16th century they rose in revolt under
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ajib_the_Great&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ajib the Great (page does not exist)">
       Ajib the Great
      </a>
      . Ajib alkafuta routed the Kings of Sennar, first making them his vassals and then seizing almost the entire kingdom in 1606 and they fled until they reached Abyssinia in the eastern region. The Sennar monarchy regrouped under
      <a href="/wiki/Adlan_I" title="Adlan I">
       Adlan I
      </a>
      , defeating Ajib in a pair of decisive battles. Eventually a compromise was reached whereby Ajib and his successors would rule the Sennar province of
      <a href="/wiki/Dongola" title="Dongola">
       Dongola
      </a>
      with a great deal of autonomy. One of the famous Abdallab leaders in 1798 is Alamin Musmar Wad Agib who defeated Hamaj in different battles, besides his victory against Abyssinia, Alamin Musmar killed both Badi Abuelkilk and his cousin Rajab in different battles.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Trade">
       Trade
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sennar_(sultanate)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Trade">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The capital Sennar, prosperous through trade, hosted representatives from all over the Middle East and Africa. The wealth and power of the sultans had long rested on the control of the economy. All caravans were controlled by the monarch, as was the gold supply that functioned as the state's main currency. In time this power was eroded. Foreign currencies became widely used by merchants breaking the power of the monarch to closely control the economy. The thriving trade created a wealthy class of educated and literate merchants, who read widely about Islam and became much concerned about the lack of orthodoxy in the kingdom. The monarchy of Sennar had long been regarded as semi-divine, in keeping with ancient traditions, but this idea ran strongly counter to Islam. Many festivals and rituals also persisted from earlier days, and a number them involved massive consumption of alcohol. These traditions were also abandoned.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Decline">
       Decline
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sennar_(sultanate)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Decline">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Sennar was at its peak at the end of the 16th century, but over the seventeenth it began to decline as the power of the monarchy was eroded. The greatest challenge to the authority of the king was the merchant funded
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulema" title="Ulema">
       ulema
      </a>
      who insisted it was rightfully their duty to mete out justice.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1762,
      <a href="/wiki/Badi_IV" title="Badi IV">
       Badi IV
      </a>
      was overthrown in a coup launched by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Likayik&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Abu Likayik (page does not exist)">
       Abu Likayik
      </a>
      of the red
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hamaj&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hamaj (page does not exist)">
       Hamaj
      </a>
      from the northeast of the country. Abu Likayik installed another member of the royal family as his puppet sultan and ruled as regent. This began long conflict between the Funj sultans attempting to reassert their independence and authority and the Hamaj regents attempting to maintain control of the true power of the state.
     </p>
     <p>
      These internal divisions greatly weakened the state and in the late 18th century Mek Adlan II, son of Mek Taifara, took power during a turbulent time at which a Turkish presence was being established in the Funj kingdom. The Turkish ruler,
      <a href="/wiki/Al-Tahir_Agha" title="Al-Tahir Agha">
       Al-Tahir Agha
      </a>
      , married Khadeeja, daughter of Mek Adlan II. This paved the way for the assimilation of the Funj into the Ottoman Empire.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1821,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ismail_bin_Muhammad_Ali&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ismail bin Muhammad Ali (page does not exist)">
       Ismail bin Muhammad Ali
      </a>
      the general and son of the
      <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">
       nominally Ottoman
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Khedive" title="Khedive">
       khedive
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">
       Egypt
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Ali_of_Egypt" title="Muhammad Ali of Egypt">
       Muhammad Ali
      </a>
      , led an army into Sennar; he encountered no resistance from the last king, whose realm was promptly absorbed into Ottoman Egypt. The region was subsequently absorbed into the
      <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Egyptian_Sudan" title="Anglo-Egyptian Sudan">
       Anglo-Egyptian Sudan
      </a>
      and the independent Republic of Sudan on that country's independence in 1956.
     </p>
