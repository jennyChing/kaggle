         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Crayon Fire Sculptures by Herb Williams
          <span class="post-date">
           by
           <a href="http://thisiscolossal.com/about" rel="author external" title="Visit Christopher Jobsons website">
            Christopher Jobson
           </a>
           on October 13, 2011
          </span>
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-15765" height="400" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/crayon-1-600x400.jpg" title="crayon-1" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15773" height="600" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/crayon-1-1.jpg" title="crayon-1-1" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-15767" height="900" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/crayon-3-600x900.jpg" title="crayon-3" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-15768" height="900" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/crayon-4-600x900.jpg" title="crayon-4" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-15769" height="491" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/crayon-5-600x491.jpg" title="crayon-5" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-15766" height="352" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/crayon-2-600x352.jpg" title="crayon-2" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Artist
          <a href="http://www.herbwilliamsart.com/">
           Herb Williams
          </a>
          is one of the only people in the world to have an account with Crayola. I imagine him whipping out his cell phone, speed dialing Crayola Headquarters and saying I need 40,000 Screamin Greens and 20,000 Tickle Me Pinks.
          <em>
           Tonight
          </em>
          . Im not sure if thats exactly how it works, but lets go with it.
         </p>
         <p>
          This latest work by Williams,
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.facebook.com/portraitofwildfire">
            Unwanted Visitor: Portrait of Wildfire
           </a>
          </em>
          , just opened at the National Ranching Heritage Center at Texas Tech University in Lubbock. The five swirling flames made of thousands of crayons are meant to resemble the
          <a href="/2011/09/the-speed-of-fire/">
           recent wildfires
          </a>
          that ravaged the state several weeks ago. Over time, the sculptures themselves will be ravaged by the hot Texas heat, and will gradually begin to melt, turning the already brilliant color gradation into a dripping, gooey mess. Awesome right? The project began as a small
          <a href="http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/nrhc/unwanted-visitor-portrait-of-wildfire">
           proof-of-concept on Kickstarter
          </a>
          only a month ago and is now open to the public at
          <a href="http://www.depts.ttu.edu/ranchhc/">
           NRHC
          </a>
          . Special thanks to Emily Arellano, Herb Williams, and photographer
          <a href="http://www.ashtonthornhill.com/">
           Ashton Thornhill
          </a>
          who captured the images above. (via
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kellypodz/6221206477/">
           kelly podzemny
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <ul id="social-list">
