        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Use bias tape for a neckline or arm hole facing
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="onDate">
          <a href="http://so-sew-easy.com/use-bias-tape-neckline/" rel="bookmark" title="6:00 am">
           <span class="entry-date">
            April 22, 2014
           </span>
           <span class="entry-time">
            - 6:00 am
           </span>
          </a>
          <span class="bl_sep">
           |
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="bl_categ">
          <a href="http://so-sew-easy.com/category/sewing/" rel="tag" title="View all posts in Sewing">
           Sewing
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://so-sew-easy.com/category/add-to/sewing-tutorials-page/" rel="tag" title="View all posts in Sewing tutorials page">
           Sewing tutorials page
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://so-sew-easy.com/category/tutorials/" rel="tag" title="View all posts in Tutorials">
           Tutorials
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <img alt="OLYMPUS DIGITAL CAMERA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9734" height="500" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Bias-facing-020b.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Do you have garments youve either made or bought that have facings? How do you like them? Some of mine seem to work well, others are too big, floppy, irritating, show through to the right side, or just keep getting in the way somehow.  So how do you finish the edge of a neckline or armhole without facings or doing a full lining?
         </p>
         <p>
          Have you come across sewing patterns that tell you to simply fold over the raw edge to the inside and stitch in place, either on the neckline or armhole? Thats fine with knit fabrics that stretch, but on wovens  really! How is that going to work then, when you have to turn the shorter edge in and make it lie flat against a longer edge? Well, I can tell you that it just doesnt work and Ive ended up with all sorts of puckering and nastiness.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is where the bias tape facing can be a real solution. Its not all big and floppy or loose like the traditional facing, yet it provides a very neat and smooth finish and makes that raw edge easy to turn in. You can do it in a matching self-made bias tape or use a commercial or contrast tape. Its up to you.
         </p>
         <p>
          Lets take a look.
         </p>
         <h2>
          How to sew a bias facing for a neckline.
         </h2>
         <p>
          I didnt have a current project to work on, so I cut a practice piece of fabric to represent a neckline to be finished. I also used a couple of very different fabrics and a white thread so you can see where I am working, but you might bind with the same fabric, and a matching thread. I drew on an approximation of my neckline seam line using a disappearing marker.
         </p>
         <p>
          TIP: Use a soft and fine fabric that moves easily for your binding. In this example, I was using some scraps and the binding fabric I chose was too thick really and didnt hold a crease. Suggested fabrics, satin, charmeuse, cotton, viscose, rayon etc
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="How to add a bias tape binding to a neckline or armhole to finish the raw edge.  Not too difficult and gives a super-smart finish." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9723" height="325" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Neckline-binding-1.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          First measure your neckline, along the seam line and add on about 4-6 inches to give yourself something to work with. Then youll need a piece of bias binding. You can buy this in a plain contrasting fabric, or you can make your own using the fabric of your garment. Take a look at these tutorials on how to make your own bias tape.
         </p>
         <table>
          <tbody>
           <tr>
            <td>
             <a href="http://so-sew-easy.com/making-continuous-bias-binding-tape/" title="Making continuous bias binding tape">
              <img alt="Easy to follow steps for making continuous bias binding tape from a square of fabric. No more fiddly sewing strips together." class="aligncenter size-thumbnail wp-image-8140" height="150" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Bias-corners-012-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
             </a>
            </td>
            <td>
             <a href="http://so-sew-easy.com/continuous-bias-binding-calculator/" title="Continuous Bias Binding Calculator">
              <img alt="Continuous bias binding calculator. Work out how much you will get from a square, or what size square you will need." class="aligncenter size-thumbnail wp-image-8106" height="150" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Graphic2-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
             </a>
            </td>
            <td>
             <a href="http://so-sew-easy.com/turning-corners-with-bias-binding/" title="Turning corners with bias binding">
              <img alt="How to turn sharp corners with bias binding" class="aligncenter size-thumbnail wp-image-8152" height="150" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Bias-corners-027b-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
             </a>
            </td>
           </tr>
          </tbody>
         </table>
         <p>
          Fold your tape in half along the length and press, without stretching it out of shape. This is single-fold bias tape. This is what we will use around the neckline and armhole edges in place of a full facing. Im using a 2 inch strip folded in half to make 1 inch single fold tape.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="How to add a bias tape binding to a neckline or armhole to finish the raw edge. Not too difficult and gives a super-smart finish." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9726" height="325" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Neckline-binding-2.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://so-sew-easy.com/staystitching/" title="Staystitching  what is it and why do it?">
           Stay-stitch
          </a>
          the curve, neckline or armhole. Normal stitch length, just inside the 1/2 inch seam line. This will help to stop the curve stretching out as you work on it. Do handle your fabric carefully and avoid letting it hang or these curved edges can stretch and then getting a smooth finish will be much harder.
         </p>
         <p>
          Start and end at center back, leave a tail, a couple of inches or so from center.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="How to add a bias tape binding to a neckline or armhole to finish the raw edge. Not too difficult and gives a super-smart finish." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9728" height="325" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Neckline-binding-3.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Keep raw edge of binding even with raw edge of neckline. Sew half way across the width of the tape, taking a half inch of tape on either side of the stitch line. Stitch slowly and carefully around the curve, stopping where you need to, to lift the presser foot and re-position the tape. Dont stretch the bias as you sew, but do concentrate of getting the fabric under the presser foot flat, and dont worry if it creates some small ripples on the
          <em>
           inside
          </em>
          of the curve, thats fine.
         </p>
         <p>
          NOTE  The fabric should be flat along the stitching/seam line and can ripple a bit on the inside. If you follow the raw edges, keeping these flat, when you turn later, the binding could be a little too tight.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="How to add a bias tape binding to a neckline or armhole to finish the raw edge. Not too difficult and gives a super-smart finish." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9729" height="325" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Neckline-binding-4.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Arrive back 2 inches from center, join the tape, cut and press seam open. Finish sewing the binding in place between the areas where you started and finished the stitching. On an armhole, start and finish at the underarm seam.
         </p>
         <p>
          Trim back through all layers half the width of the seam allowance. Clip around the curves to help your mini-facing to lie flat.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="How to add a bias tape binding to a neckline or armhole to finish the raw edge. Not too difficult and gives a super-smart finish." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9730" height="325" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Neckline-binding-5.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Press all layers up towards the facing.
         </p>
         <p>
          Turn the facing now towards the inside, into the neckline and press again. Allow the facing to turn about 1/16th to 1/8th of an inch inside the seam line so that it wont be visible from the outside once sewn. Steam and pressing is your friend here.
         </p>
         <p>
          Pin in place. For the best possible result, hand baste the facing in place, or if you are confident, you can just pin.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="How to add a bias tape binding to a neckline or armhole to finish the raw edge. Not too difficult and gives a super-smart finish." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9731" height="325" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Neckline-binding-6.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Sew all the way around the neckline, binding facing upwards, close to the inside fold and keeping your stitching an even distance from the neckline edge. Give the entire neckline a final press and you are done.
         </p>
         <p>
          This method of finishing can be used on both necklines and armholes.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="How to add a bias tape binding to a neckline or armhole to finish the raw edge. Not too difficult and gives a super-smart finish." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9732" height="500" src="http://cf.so-sew-easy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Neckline-binding-7.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          So why am I telling you all this? I have a feeling you might be using this method soon. The Easy Breezy Blouse pattern is currently in development, and all being well, will be released in June. To get the perfect finish on your armholes, youll want to use this mini bias facing.
         </p>
