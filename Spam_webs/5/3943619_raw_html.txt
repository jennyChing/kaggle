        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Surprise in a Tequila Bottle
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-05-13T20:56:22+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          May 13, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.chabernet.com/author/chabernet/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            chabernet
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.chabernet.com/surprise-in-a-tequila-bottle/#comments">
           10 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
          and it is not a worm.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.chabernet.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Old-Bottle.jpg">
          <img alt="Pitanga Vodka" class="size-large wp-image-140 aligncenter" height="780" src="http://www.chabernet.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Old-Bottle-1024x780.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The hand-blown Patron bottle is a thing of beauty, and the Patron Silver Tequila is not bad either (see below for our perfect Margarita recipe). So, it came as no surprise when my wife asked me to retrieve an empty bottle from our recycle bin. What to do with an empty tequila bottle? Why, you re-fill it with home-made fruit-flavored Vodka. Id expect nothing less from the woman who coined the word Chabernet. This time she substituted Vodka infused with foraged Pitanga (Surinam Cherry), but any edible fruit might work.
         <a href="http://www.clubclipper.com/">
          A club recalling the days of the Pan Am Clippers
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         In Hawaii, Guava also grows wild, as does Lilikoi (passion fruit), and Mountain Apple. In
         <a href="http://www.foragingtexas.com/2009/07/plum-wild.html" target="_blank">
          Texas, Wild Plum trees
         </a>
         abound, as do wild Persimmon and Mulberries. The first time I encountered a wild plum tree while walking along a Texas creek, I smelled the fragrance of the fruit before I spotted the tree. The smell is indescribable and Ive sniffed it before in wild fruit like the Pitanga. How amazing that such a fleeting perfume can be captured and savored in a Vodka infusion. Conveniently,
         <a href="https://flic.kr/p/6QA14R" target="_blank">
          wild yeast grows on the skin of the plum
         </a>
         making it perfect for handcrafted wine or your own sourdough bread starter yeast (but that is another story).
         <br/>
         In the Pacific Northwest, wild berries are our choice  blackberry, raspberry, currants, goose berry, and huckleberry  just to name a few. I recall a sweet golden raspberry that we foraged in Oregon and its color and taste infusing Vodka might be a clever alternative to Chambord.
         <br/>
         In any case,
         <a href="http://www.beyondveg.com/billings-t/fruit-table/wild-cultiv-fruit-1a.shtml" target="_blank">
          wild fruit is a renewable and healthy resource
         </a>
         . Impress your guests by hauling out your own homemade hooch then offer a toast to their good health.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.chabernet.com/pitanga-flavored-vodka/" target="_blank">
          Recipe for Surinam Cherry Vodka
         </a>
         is here. In our typical Chabernet style, we replace expensive spirits with inexpensive but good quality Costco Signature Vodka. By the way, we dont add sugar or sweetener to our home-made flavored Vodka.
        </p>
        <p>
         Quote from Patron regarding their beautiful bottle.
         <br/>
         Each bottle is individually crafted by a glass artisan from recycled glass and is hand-numbered.  See more at: http://www.patrontequila.com/bottle#sthash.UqDwyMiV.dpuf
         <br/>
         If you dont drink Tequila, empty Patron bottles are sold on e-Bay.
        </p>
        <div class="hrecipe recipress-light" id="recipress_recipe">
         <h2 class="fn">
          Perfect Margarita
         </h2>
         <p class="seo_only">
          By
          <span class="author">
           chabernet
          </span>
          Published:
          <span class="published updated">
           May 13, 2014
           <span class="value-title" title="2014-05-13T20:56:22+00:00">
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <ul class="recipe-details">
          <li>
           <b>
            Yield:
           </b>
           <span class="yield">
            1 Servings
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Prep:
           </b>
           <span class="preptime">
            <span class="value-title" title="PT5M">
            </span>
            5 mins
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p class="summary">
          Inspired by the House Margarita at Uncle Julios in Dallas, TX (our favorite Tex-Mex chain).
         </p>
         <h3>
          Ingredients
         </h3>
         <ul class="ingredients">
          <li class="ingredient">
           <span class="amount">
            2 Oz.
           </span>
           <span class="name">
            <a href="http://www.chabernet.com/ingredient/lime-juice/">
             Lime Juice
            </a>
           </span>
           <i class="notes">
            Freshly Squeezed
           </i>
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient">
           <span class="amount">
            1 Teaspoon
           </span>
           <span class="name">
            <a href="http://www.chabernet.com/ingredient/agave-syrup/">
             Agave Syrup
            </a>
           </span>
           <i class="notes">
            Or, Simple Syrup
           </i>
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient">
           <span class="amount">
            1 Teaspoon
           </span>
           <span class="name">
            <a href="http://www.chabernet.com/ingredient/triple-sec/">
             Triple Sec
            </a>
           </span>
           <i class="notes">
           </i>
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient">
           <span class="amount">
            3 Oz.
           </span>
           <span class="name">
            <a href="http://www.chabernet.com/ingredient/tequila/">
             Tequila
            </a>
           </span>
           <i class="notes">
            Patron Silver
           </i>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Instructions
         </h3>
         <ol class="instructions">
          <li>
           Serve on the rocks in a glass with a salted rim (optional).
My wife likes hers tart and not too sweet, so these are her proportions
           <br/>
          </li>
          <li>
           The Agave Syrup is a clever touch since Tequila is also made from the Agave plant. Simple Syrup may be substituted.
           <br/>
          </li>
         </ol>
