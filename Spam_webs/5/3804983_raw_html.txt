        </a>
       </h2>
       <img src="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/themes/threads/images/postbreak.png" width="600px"/>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="San Francisco + Napa + Hail Hail 31" data-frizzlyposturl="http://1000threadsblog.com/2015/06/28/san-francisco-napa-hail-hail-31/" type="hidden" value=""/>

        </p>
        <p>
         Which is not to say Im good at it. Weve spent the last two weekends traveling and the weeks in between sewing napkins and delivering pies.

        </a>
       </h2>
       <img src="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/themes/threads/images/postbreak.png" width="600px"/>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="Salt &amp; vinegar roasted potatoes" data-frizzlyposturl="http://1000threadsblog.com/2015/06/08/salt-vinegar-roasted-potatoes/" type="hidden" value=""/>

         I grew up on the coast, with a vast ocean just a few miles from my home. I felt strangled in my small town, and when I stared at the ocean in the clean, crisp, salt air, I saw nothing but possibility. I never even considered a school in my own state

        </p>
        <p>
         Here, the beaches have boardwalks. The smell of Old Bay, malt vinegar, and sunscreen fills the air and for Mark, that smell is every bit as nostalgic as my own.

       </h2>
       <img src="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/themes/threads/images/postbreak.png" width="600px"/>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="Turning that black thumb green" data-frizzlyposturl="http://1000threadsblog.com/2015/05/29/turning-that-black-thumb-green/" type="hidden" value=""/>
        <p>
         <a href="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/green-thumb-thousand-threads-3.jpg">

         Ive never fancied myself much of a gardener. My grandmother? Yes. My mother? Hell yes.
        </p>
        <p>

        </p>
        <p>

        </p>
        <p>
          Im completely spoiled. And so are our  very healthy  plants.

        </a>
       </h2>
       <img src="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/themes/threads/images/postbreak.png" width="600px"/>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="52 self portraits // 14" data-frizzlyposturl="http://1000threadsblog.com/2015/05/26/52-self-portraits-14/" type="hidden" value=""/>
        <p>
         <a href="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/thousand-threads-greenhouse-portrait-2.jpg">
          <img alt="52 portraits // a thousand threads" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15251" data-wp-pid="15251" src="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/thousand-threads-greenhouse-portrait-2.jpg" width="600"/>

        </p>

        </p>

          of course Im trying not to think of where they mightve gone.

        </p>
        <p>
          Im also trying not to think about that.

        </a>
       </h2>
       <img src="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/themes/threads/images/postbreak.png" width="600px"/>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="My grandmothers date bars // Archiving with HP Sprout" data-frizzlyposturl="http://1000threadsblog.com/2015/05/20/my-grandmothers-date-bars-archiving-with-hp-sprout/" type="hidden" value=""/>
        <p>
         <a href="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/date-bars-sprout-thousand-threads-1.jpg">
          <img alt="my grandmother's date bars // a thousand threads" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15228" data-wp-pid="15228" src="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/date-bars-sprout-thousand-threads-1.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         There was a time when I thought we led a pretty minimalist existence  the little voice that told me I could live in a tiny house was strong.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then I moved.
        </p>
        <p>
         In moving spaces, it suddenly occurs to a person just how much stuff theyve managed to accumulate over the years. Clothes, linens, props, 40 place settings for the occasional gathering.
        </p>
        <p>
          okay those last two might be unique.
        </p>
        <p>
         But whatever you might keep stuffed in your own closets and corners, there is one thing we all share  we all have a lot of stuff we dont really need.
        </p>
        <p>
         The truly precious items, the ones wed mourn if they were somehow misplaced in the move, are fewer and farther between. And thats all the more reason to do whatever we can to make sure theyre protected.
        </p>
        <p>
          as we settle into our new home and I settle into a new job, a lot of feelings seem to sneak up and overwhelm me on a daily basis. Worry, excitement, general terror, elation okay mostly just the two. But life and the people we love somehow feel more precious in these times of increased intensity, and the lost connection to those weve held dear more stark.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have only a few items to remember my grandmother, who, growing up, lived just through a trail in the woods and some of the most precious items I own are her handwritten recipes.
        </p>

       <h2 class="entry-title">
        <a href="http://1000threadsblog.com/2015/05/12/52-self-portraits-13/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to 52 self portraits // 13">
         52 self portraits // 13
        </a>
       </h2>
       <img src="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/themes/threads/images/postbreak.png" width="600px"/>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="52 self portraits // 13" data-frizzlyposturl="http://1000threadsblog.com/2015/05/12/52-self-portraits-13/" type="hidden" value=""/>
        <p>
         <a href="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/52-portraits-thousand-threads-13-1.jpg">
          <img alt="52 self portraits // a thousand threads" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15184" data-wp-pid="15184" src="http://1000threadsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/52-portraits-thousand-threads-13-1.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         We bought a fixer upper. And everything about the idea seemed fun.
        </p>
        <p>
         Carpeted bathrooms, purple toilets, and gardens full of weeds were all just opportunities to put our own spin on the perfect home we knew it could be. After all, most of the updates in the newer homeswe could afford werent our style  this was the best way to make it ours.
        </p>
        <p>
          then came the move.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dust, dirt, stained walls, wrinkled carpets, broken attempts to dry our clothes, and an ant colony (not just ants) in the mailbox all serve as daily reminders that weve only just begun.
        </p>
        <p>
         Add a new job to the mix andeverything seems suspended in space  or, more accurately, in boxes. Ifeel myself becominga terrible friend, a cut-rate blogger, and a hack of a wife as I unpack my life in more ways than one tallying the hours, days, months, in my head it will take until were settled back into a normal routine.
        </p>
        <p>
         But slowly, when Im able to step back from it all, Ill admit thatwith every day that goes by, it feels a little more like home.
        </p>
        <p>
         Our counters are ever so slightly cleaner, our bedrooms have beds we even have a thing or two put away.
        </p>
        <p>
          and hey, the carpets in two out of three of the bathrooms are gone.
        </p>
        <p>
         The summer sun is beginning to beat down and, because I cant help being me, I feel the pull to make things happen now. All of the things so I can run off and treat my white legs to some warmth.
        </p>
        <p>
         But I have to remember that it will all happen in time. The dream house we know well love is hiding under a few deep breaths and a little bit of time.
        </p>
        <p>
         For now, Ill just have to work on enjoying the ride.
        </p>
        <p>
          and breaking up that ant colony so I can pick up my mail.
        </p>
