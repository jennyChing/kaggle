          <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
           <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
             <p>
              <em>
               "I think I can. I think I can."
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              We all know mantras can be helpful in reaching goals. Whether it's powering through a plateau to lose those last 10 pounds or pushing yourself to get through the last mile of that 5k, for fitness and weight loss, positive self-talk can be essential. Think about it - you'd never tell a friend or family member to just give up and go home, would you? So why is it okay to speak to yourself that way?
             </p>
             <p>
              But before you can start thinking positive, you must clear away the negative clutter floating around your mind. Though you may not realize it, you have running conversations with yourself all the time, says Dr. Allen Weg, Ed.D, A New Jersey-based cognitive behavioral therapist and former Weight Watchers member. "You think to yourself, This is too hard. I can't do it,'" Weg explains. "But think about it realistically. How do you know that just because you haven't done something before, it's truly impossible?"
             </p>
             <p>
              Sometimes the awareness starts organically. It can be an "a-ha" moment that jolts people into reality, Weg says. "There's no magic formula that can make people see success, but if a doctor tells you you're on the path to diabetes or if you realize you can no longer fit into your favourite dress, that might be the day you decide once and for all you can and will get healthy."
             </p>
             <p>
              This is why positive self-talk is important. "Try keeping a journal or ask yourself to list positive things about yourself," he says. If that seems difficult, step outside the box. What would your mother or your best friend say about you?" The important thing is to not let yourself run away with the negative thoughts in your head. Instead, tune that Negative Nelly
              <em>
               OUT.
              </em>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Match the mantra to the moment
             </h2>
             <p>
              Repeating positive mantras or sayings can help. We chose our favourites from our Weight Watchers Community. for specific roadblocks. Try them!
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/lips_icon.gif" style="width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
               When you don't feel like working out:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              "Yesterday you said, 'tomorrow.'"
             </p>
             <p>
              "Losing weight is a journey, not a destination."
             </p>
             <p>
              "If it is to be, it's up to me."
             </p>
             <p>
              "You can't make footprints in the sands of time if you're sitting down."
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <strong>
                <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/lips_icon.gif" style="width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
               </strong>
               While grocery shopping:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              "You can't eat it if you don't have it."
             </p>
             <p>
              "Eat good, feel good!"
             </p>
             <p>
              "Nothing tastes as good as healthy feels."
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <strong>
                <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/lips_icon.gif" style="width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
               </strong>
               When you have a disappointing weigh-in:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              "If you trip down one stair, don't throw yourself down the whole flight."
             </p>
             <p>
              "You only fail if you quit."
             </p>
             <p>
              "You didn't gain the weight overnight; you won't lose it overnight, either."
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <strong>
                <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u18/lips_icon.gif" style="width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
               </strong>
               When you're feeling down:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              "It doesn't matter how slow you go, only that you do not stop."
             </p>
             <p>
              "Weight loss isn't a sprint, it's a marathon."
             </p>
             <p>
              "Take pride in how far you have come, and have faith in how far you can go."
             </p>
             <p>
              "Shoot for progress, not perfection."
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                Do you have any positive mantras you use to get yourself through tough situations? Share them in the comments!
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>
