          <p>
           Serving Ohio State Universitys Wexner Medical Center  Otolaryngologist and surgeon, Dr. Eugene Chio explains the process of diagnosing Sleep Apnea. He describes the surgical evaluation process for patients who may not get relief any other way.  Dr. Chio performs the majority of surgeries for moderate and severe Obstructive Sleep Apnea suffers.
          </p>
          <p>
           He states that surgery is usually reserved for surgery considerations for OSA patients who have failed their Sleep Apnea treatment program. This includes patients who could not become accustomed to CPAP Therapy, or other alternative treatment methods.
          </p>
          <p>
           Surgery, as a last resort for the worst of Sleep Apnea sufferers, is at the extreme end of all treatment options.  It is not the only option and most sleep specialists will try to help patients to adjust to challenges inherent with any OSA treatment program. They know that even an invasive surgery carries no guarantee that it will cure Sleep Apnea.
          </p>
