       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Trial of the Bat-Witch!
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="sep">
          Posted on
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.the-isb.com/?p=20" rel="bookmark" title="10:59 pm">
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2007-05-13T22:59:56+00:00" pubdate="">
           May 13, 2007
          </time>
         </a>
         <span class="by-author">
          <span class="sep">
           by
          </span>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.the-isb.com/?author=5" rel="author" title="View all posts by Chris Sims">
            Chris Sims
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Every now and then, Ill run across a story that I am
         <i>
          convinced
         </i>
         is The Craziest Thing Ever Published.
        </p>
        <p>
         It happened when I read the issue of
         <a href="http://the-isb.blogspot.com/2006/01/blue-blazes.html" target="_blank">
          <i>
           Metamorpho
          </i>
         </a>
         where he fights off a two-foot tall galactic conqueror by using a guitar that shoots laser beams; it happened when I read about
         <a href="http://the-isb.blogspot.com/2006/02/crank-file-part-2.html" target="_blank">
          the little man with a radio
         </a>
         that lived in Sun Boys ankle and spied on the Legion; and it happenedof coursein the story that brought us both
         <a href="http://the-isb.blogspot.com/2006/08/can-nothing-stop-computo.html" target="_blank">
          Bizarro Computo and Hate Face
         </a>
         .  And each time, I found something that was even
         <i>
          more
         </i>
         nuts to fixate on for a few weeks.
        </p>
        <p>
         But this time?  I dont know if anythingll
         <i>
          ever
         </i>
         top this:
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img src="http://www.the-isb.com/images/WorldsFinest186.jpg" title="Mmm... Batwich..."/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Why is Superman hanging with a bunch of Puritans and accusing Batman of consort with the Author of All Lies?  Because Bob Kanigher.
         <b>
          Thats
         </b>
         why.
        </p>
        <p>
         Yes, springing from the pen of madness itself with pencils by the late, great Ross Andru is 1969s
         <i>
          Worlds Finest
         </i>
         #186, wherein Kanigher apparently read
         <i>
          The Crucible
         </i>
         and decided that it just didnt have enough super-heroes, a theory that a sixteen year-old Christopher J. Sims would later pose in Ms. McDonalds 10th grade English class.  Unfortunately, this ones actually a two-parter that ends in
         <i>
          WF
         </i>
         #187, which I couldnt find, but I think theres enough here to be getting on with.
        </p>
        <p>
         The whole thing gets started atwhere else?Stately Wayne Manor, where we find Bruce Wayne brushing up on his family history with a volume about Revolutionary War hero Mad Anthony Wayne, a choice of recreation that, by the sheer coincidence that made up a good three quarters of the storytelling back in the 60s, is going to be important in about three pages.
        </p>
        <p>
         In fact, no sooner has he finished reminiscing aloud about his famous Redcoat-hating ancestor than Commissioner Gordon gives him a ring on the hotline, asking him to guard a priceless bust thats just been donated by an anonymous collector, because apparently, crime had been completely eradicated by this point in history.  Of course, due to the fact that the businesses of Gotham City are staffed
         <i>
          entirely
         </i>
         by incompetent maroons, his services are rendered unnecessary when the curator drops the bustwhich has been covered in a sheet and never seen by
         <i>
          anyone
         </i>
         , including the museum staff, before it went on public displayand shatters it into a million pieces.
        </p>
        <p>
         Clearly, this is a job for Superman.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://www.the-isb.com/images/BatWitch01-Big.jpg">
          <img src="http://www.the-isb.com/images/BatWitch01.jpg" title="Remember what I said about all crime being eradicated?  Yeah."/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         (
         <i>
          Click for a larger image
         </i>
         )
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The way I see it, there are essentially two possibilities here:  A), that whomever sculpted said bust went through the trouble of carving out an exact duplicate of Anthony Waynes head, then (somehow)
         <i>
          made a Batman mask out of marble
         </i>
         and managed to fit it over; or B), which will seem far more likely after the next few pages, that Supermans just messing with Batman.  Either way, the fact that Superman shows Gothams museum-going public that Batman looks an awful lot like Bruce Wayne underneath his mask is never brought up again.
        </p>
        <p>
         Needless to say, this sparks Batmans curiosity, so after Superman whips up a couple of Colonial outfits out of Wayne Manors drapesno,
         <i>
          really
         </i>
         its off on a trip through the Time Barrier to Colonial New England, which somehow manages to be
         <i>
          slightly
         </i>
         less historically accurate than, say, Colonial Williamsburg.  No sooner have they landed, however, then they are immediately mistaken for British spiesprobably because Batmans drapes were made of a soft lavender, when everybody knows that earth tones were in during the summer of 75and attacked by Mad Anthony himself:
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img src="http://www.the-isb.com/images/BatWitch02.jpg" title="Apparently an affinity for capes runs in the family."/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Worlds Greatest Detective, ladies and gentlemen.
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh, and then Batman fights a horse:
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img src="http://www.the-isb.com/images/BatWitch03.jpg" title="No horses were harmed in the making of this comic.  Dammit."/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         During the scuffle, Anthony Ol Stabby Wayne manages to rip Supermans Colonial outfit, and gets the idea from Supermans red
         <i>
          cape
         </i>
         that hes fighting a couple of red
         <i>
          coats
         </i>
         , which leads Our Heroes to the completely logical conclusion that theyll be far more inconspicuous if they just wander around in their super-hero outfits.  The side effect of all this?
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          The Single Greatest Panel Of All Time
         </b>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img src="http://www.the-isb.com/images/SForShakespeare.jpg" title="SUPERMAN: MASTER OF DECEPTION"/>
         <br/>
         
         <i>
          Im going to play Hamlet!  See?  S For Shakespeare!
         </i>
         
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sadly, all copies of the Eighth Folio version of
         <i>
          Hamlet
         </i>
         , which included Laertes seeking vengeance as a Bat-Man, were lost to the ages.
        </p>
        <p>
         The townspeople are pretty easy to convince, but to be fair, theyre a little distracted by the fact that
         <b>
          theyve got a witch trial going on
         </b>
         , apparently completely oblivious to the fact that theyre actually living almost a full century
         <i>
          after
         </i>
         the famous Salem witch trials, but hey, theyve got a long way to go before they get TV, and theres got to be
         <i>
          something
         </i>
         to kill the time, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         The lady in questionas is so often the case with this sort of thingis actually completely innocent of witchcraft, a fact Batman is able to instantly determine from the evidence of her astonishing hotness.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://www.the-isb.com/images/BatWitch04-Big.jpg">
          <img src="http://www.the-isb.com/images/BatWitch04.jpg" title="All Real!  Not Code-Approved!  The Hottest Girls of the Revolution!"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Rather than let Sylviathe soaking wet blonde in the previous imagedie, Superman leaps into action faster than the eye can see, biting through the dunking stool in the hopes that the superstitious townsfolk will leaphowever illogicallyto the conclusion that she was freed by an underwater serpent.  Seriously.  Thats his plan.  And amazingly, it works, although it doesnt quite solve the problem of keeping a woman tied to a plank of wood underwater from drowning.
        </p>
        <p>
         No, thats the kind of thing that requires a Batman, and once he drags her to the shore and unties her, she rewards him with some good ol fashioned 18th Century makeouts, thus proving that every romance novel cover I have ever seen is
         <b>
          100% Accurate
         </b>
         .  Superman finds
         <i>
          his
         </i>
         attempts to get on some of that, however,
         <b>
          totally shot down
         </b>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img src="http://www.the-isb.com/images/BatWitch05.jpg" title="Ooh, the dreaded Kryptonite Cockblock!"/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         That is
         <b>
          just how Batman rolls
         </b>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Superman doesnt take this sort of thing lying down though, and while Batmans off making time with Sylvia in a tavern, Supes sets out to
         <b>
          have his best friend convicted of witchcraft and burned at the stake
         </b>
         . How?  By dyeing his costume and flying around on a broom and using Super-Ventriloquism to make it look like a black cat was talking to him, thus reminding us all that when it comes to Silver-Age Dicketry,
         <i>
          nobody
         </i>
         does it better than the Last Son of Krypton.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img src="http://www.the-isb.com/images/BatWitch06.jpg" title="Clark Kent, you are COLD AS ICE!"/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The townspeople immediately find Batman guilty and sentence him to be burned at the stake at noon the next day.  Thus, Batman is left in the stocks to contemplate his impending death, and since he apparently forgot that
         <b>
          hes fucking Batman
         </b>
         and could free himself from an 18th century pillory in less time than it takes me to make a sandwich, things are looking pretty grim for the Caped Crusader.
        </p>
        <p>
         Is there anyone
         <i>
          anyone
         </i>
         who could help?!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <img src="http://www.the-isb.com/images/BatWitch07.jpg" title="It's all about the Benjamins, WHAT!"/>
        </center>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yes,
         <b>
          Benjamin Franklin
         </b>
         shows up, and hits on the brilliant idea of freeing Batman from the stocks by tying a kite to the lock and waiting for lightning to strike it.  One can assume that after failing that, he wouldve attempted to spring Bats by burning him out with a pot-bellied stove, focusing light through a pair of bifocals, or banging a couple of French broads.
        </p>
        <p>
         It doesnt quite work out that way, though, and with Superman around to
         <i>
          stop the lightning from reaching the lock
         </i>
         , Franklins forced to call it a night and go back to founding the Post Office, leaving Batman to be burned at the stake in the next exciting issue.
        </p>
        <p>
         But like I said, I dont have that one, and after having my mind
         <i>
          irrevocably shattered
         </i>
         by the first appearance of the Batman/Ben Franklin team, I can only assume that everything works out okay.  If I had to hazard a guess, though, Id say that it involves a deception resulting in Anthony Wayne being mistaken for Batman, an appearance by Kryptonite that somehow pre-dates the explosion of Krypton, and the stunning revelation that Batman was making out with his own great great grandmother about five paragraphs ago.
        </p>
        <p>
         Time Travel, folks:
         <b>
          Never A Good Idea
         </b>
         .
        </p>
       </div>
