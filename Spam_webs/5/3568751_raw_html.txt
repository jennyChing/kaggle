        Manual time tracking systems need manpower and the help of many HR department employees, biometric time tracking systems do not need any manpower and thus help a company save a lot of money which can be spent on paying for extra manpower, increasing productivity.
       </li>
       <li style="margin-left: 35px; text-align: justify;">
        Biometric systems or fingerprint time attendance clocks can be expensive and difficult to install but there is no installation or setting up cost in manual paper based systems. This is one of the advantages of using manual systems over computer based ones.
       </li>
       <li style="margin-left: 35px; text-align: justify;">
        In case of a manual payroll calculation system, there is a huge probability of error but biometric systems are completely accurate and error-free. They can thus help in avoiding the extra cost that may sometimes be paid to employees due to calculation errors.
       </li>
       <li style="margin-left: 35px; text-align: justify;">
        Some biometric systems are made in such a way that all the data recorded in them is always backed up through cloud storage and thus one doesnt need to worry about data theft or loss but on the other hand, in case of a manual payroll system, data loss is common and happens in many cases.
       </li>
       <li style="margin-left: 35px; text-align: justify;">
        Fingerprint or retina scanning time attendance systems help employers in accessing data of any employee from anywhere using just a mobile phone or a computer but this facility is not provided in the case of a manual system.
       </li>
       <li style="margin-left: 35px; text-align: justify;">
        Another point of difference between the two is that manual systems usually require employees to carry punch cards which can be used by others as well to gain entry into an office but in case of a retina or fingerprint scan, only authenticated employees can enter and probability of unwanted access or proxy is zero.
       </li>
      </ul>
