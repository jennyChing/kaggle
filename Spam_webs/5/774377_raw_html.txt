          <h1>
           Halloween Vocabulary: Spooky Idioms and Ghostly Phrases
          </h1>
         </header>
         <p>
          For hundreds of years, the transition between summer and winter has been celebrated in one way or another.Although traditions and customs have changed, it seems that the summers end and the coming of the harsh, cruel and long winter have always instilled some dark thoughts in people.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           SEE ALSO:
          </strong>
          <a href="//saundz.com/trick-or-treat-origins-of-halloween/" title="Trick or Treat: the Origins of Halloween">
           Trick or treat: The Origins of Halloween
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Obviously, Halloween is more than just trick-or-treating.It is said that this is the time when people have to overcome their deepest, darkest fears, and fight against malevolent spirits and unnatural forces.Hence, it is not surprising that the first things to pop up into our mind upon mentioning this festivity are usually spooky and scary images depicting witches, cats, bats, ghosts, and cold weapons.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="//saundz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/halloween1.jpg">
           <img alt="halloween" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-17778" height="443" src="//saundz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/halloween1-800x443.jpg" width="800"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          In honor of Halloween, we have prepared a short guide through the darkest parts of
          <a href="//saundz.com/english-srsly/" target="_blank" title="English, srsly">
           the English vocabulary
          </a>
          .Take a deep breath, because we are going to take a closer look into dusty pages of our English dictionary in search for Halloween vocabulary, and discuss spooky idioms and ghostly phrases, all of which, of course, include Halloween imagery.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Lets start from scratch.
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Did you know that
          <strong>
           <em>
            terrible
           </em>
          </strong>
          is used to describe something extremely bad, and
          <strong>
           <em>
            terrific
           </em>
          </strong>
          , though similar in form, means marvelous? However, both,
          <strong>
           <em>
            horrible
           </em>
          </strong>
          and
          <em>
           <strong>
            horrific
           </strong>
          </em>
          are scary words.
          <em>
           <strong>
            Horrible
           </strong>
          </em>
          simply means
          <em>
           awful
          </em>
          , and when you describe something as
          <em>
           <strong>
            horrific
           </strong>
          </em>
          , you are saying that is really
          <em>
           frightening
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           upsetting
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <div class="blockquote_left">
          <p>
           Did you know that the word haunted first appears in Shakespeares Midsummers Night Dream? This play is filled with ghosts, spirits, fairies, and other otherworldly creatures that wander around.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Adjectives such as
          <em>
           <strong>
            scary
           </strong>
          </em>
          ,
          <em>
           <strong>
            creepy
           </strong>
          </em>
          , and
          <em>
           <strong>
            spooky
           </strong>
          </em>
          are regularly used to describe something frightening.However, there some nuances in their meanings.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you describe something as
          <em>
           <strong>
            creepy
           </strong>
          </em>
          , in addition to being frightened, you are also implying that you are feeling rather nervous.Also, dont forget that
          <em>
           <strong>
            spooky
           </strong>
          </em>
          is always related to something supernatural.
         </p>
         <p>
          For instance, if you hear about
          <em>
           <strong>
            a spooky old house
           </strong>
          </em>
          , then, this house is most probably occupied by ghosts and other supernatural beings.In other words, it is
          <strong>
           <em>
            haunted
           </em>
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          A person can be haunted, too.Yet, it doesnt mean that the person is bothered by ghosts.On the contrary, a haunted person is someone who is sad or upset, that is, bothered by thoughts.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Cats, bats, owls, and rats
         </h2>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Curiosity killed the cat
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          How come this happened when it is widely known that cats have nine lives? Apparently, the Internets favorite animal acted inquisitive and got into trouble.
          <br/>
          <a href="//saundz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/cat.jpg">
           <img alt="Cat and a Pumpkin" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-17755" height="196" src="//saundz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/cat-300x196.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          The saying has been present for centuries, although it first went on in a slightly different form:
          <em>
           care killed the cat
          </em>
          .However, at the time when the expression was coined,
          <em>
           care
          </em>
          used to mean
          <em>
           a big worry
          </em>
          .Obviously, being overly concerned over some issues is by no means good.
         </p>
         <p>
          Todays form stands as a warning: if you want to avoid dangerous situations, then you should control your strong desires to know everything, especially on Halloween.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Like a bat out of hell
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Once upon a time all the bats were imprisoned in Hell.Then, one day, the gate was left open, and they all escaped.Even today, bats have wings like the devil.
         </p>
         <p>
          At least, this is how the story goes.The imagery is vivid, and its easy to make a connection with the saying and its intended meaning.If you run away from something quickly and without looking back, then youre running like a bat out of hell.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            A night owl
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <a href="//saundz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/owl.jpg">
          <img alt="Night Owl" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-17765" height="200" src="//saundz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/owl-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <p>
          Anyone who either stays up late or regularly works at night is a night owl.This expression has its roots in the well-known habit of owls.Namely, owls are nocturnal beings, that is, they are active at night.Because of its mystical behavior, the owl is considered a symbol of Halloween.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Are you a night owl? Or, a morning person?
          </em>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Like a rat abandoning a sinking ship
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          While it is true that Halloween is a night when all sorts of weird occurrences may take place, it is by no means allowed to act like a rat abandoning a sinking ship.In other words, if you notice that some extraordinary events are rising, deal with them with courage and decision.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/R-VRAemIvbI? wmode=transparent" width="420">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Ghosts, skeletons, and witches
         </h2>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Skeleton in the closet
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          There comes a time when some hidden truths must be revealed, no matter how shocking they are.To put it simply, we all have skeletons in our closets.
         </p>
         <p>
          It is not difficult to see why skeleton is used in this phrase.It is gruesome, savage, sickening.The father of mystery, Edgar Allan Poe, in his short story
          <em>
           The Black Cat
          </em>
          creates astonishing imagery and shows us how it looks like when the truth finally comes to surface.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Gentlemen, I delight to have allayed your suspicions, and here, through the mere frenzy of bravado, I rapped heavily upon that very portion of the brick-work behind which stood the corpse of the wife of my bosom.The wall fell bodily.The corpse, already greatly decayed, stood erect before the eyes of the spectators.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Pale as a ghost
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Sometimes, it is fully acceptable to show that you are afraid, especially on the evening when spooky stories are being told and when ghosts and goblins are freely wandering around.If you notice that a friend of yours is pale as a ghost, most certainly, he experienced some frightening moments.Remember, a friend in need is a friend indeed.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Witch hunt
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <a href="//saundz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/witch.jpg">
          <img alt="Witch hunt" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-17767" height="287" src="//saundz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/witch-300x287.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <p>
          Salem, Massachusetts was once the most frightening place on the planet.
         </p>
         <p>
          From February 1692 to May 1693, twenty women were accused of witchcraft, persecuted and sentenced to death.This means that the phrase
          <em>
           <strong>
            witch hunt
           </strong>
          </em>
          once meant a true search for witches.
         </p>
         <p>
          Of course, witches do not exist.
         </p>
         <p>
          Todays witch trials are unfair persecutions of some individuals or a group of people.The modern meaning of the phrase was first recorded in George Orwells
          <em>
           Homage to Catalonia
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           What about you? Have you ever heard of a modern witch hunt? Do you assume that someone has a skeleton in the closet? Dont be afraid, share!
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Dont forget that Halloween is just about the perfect time for dealing with the darkest human fears.
         </p>
