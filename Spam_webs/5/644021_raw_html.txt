          <h2 class="hide">
           How a Breakfast Badass Makes Eggs: Scrambled AND Hard-Boiled (Without Cracking the Shell)
          </h2>
          <p>
           Eggs are one of the most versatile foods imaginable. They can be cooked in tons of different ways, eaten for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, and go with just about anything. Most of us have hard-boiled and scrambled eggs before, but have you ever thought about combining the two?
          </p>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="51380610docPartGal880003" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
              <img alt="How a Breakfast Badass Makes Eggs: Scrambled AND Hard-Boiled (Without Cracking the Shell)" height="532" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/72/45/63498705009807/0/breakfast-badass-makes-eggs-scrambled-and-hard-boiled-without-cracking-shell.w654.jpg" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;" width="532"/>
             </figure>
            </div>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p>
           That looks pretty good, doesn't it? But it's not exactly the kind of scrambled and hard-boiled eggs combo I'm referring to. And thankfully,
           <a href="http://wtfoto.wonderhowto.com/inspiration/wtfotos-day-10-bizarre-foods-millions-people-love-0133471/">
            this
           </a>
           isn't what I'm talking about either...
          </p>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="43087912docPartGal880005" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
              <img alt="How a Breakfast Badass Makes Eggs: Scrambled AND Hard-Boiled (Without Cracking the Shell)" height="532" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/23/50/63498705186587/0/breakfast-badass-makes-eggs-scrambled-and-hard-boiled-without-cracking-shell.w654.jpg" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;" width="532"/>
             </figure>
            </div>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <small>
           Image via
           <a href="http://tfster.com" rel="nofollow">
            tfster.com
           </a>
          </small>
          <p>
           No, even though
           <a href="http://tag.wonderhowto.com/balut/">
            balut
           </a>
           may look like a scrambled mess inside of an egg (yuck!), I'm talking specifically about what's called a "Golden egg", which you can see below.
          </p>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="58580929docPartGal880007" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
              <img alt="How a Breakfast Badass Makes Eggs: Scrambled AND Hard-Boiled (Without Cracking the Shell)" height="532" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/29/72/63498706132947/0/breakfast-badass-makes-eggs-scrambled-and-hard-boiled-without-cracking-shell.w654.jpg" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;" width="532"/>
             </figure>
            </div>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <small>
           Image via
           <a href="http://instructables.com" rel="nofollow">
            instructables.com
           </a>
          </small>
          <p>
           Instructables user
           <em>
            ATTILAtheHUNgry
           </em>
           spent some time in Japan and learned the art of
           <a href="http://www.instructables.com/id/Scrambled-Eggs-still-in-the-Shell-/?ALLSTEPS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            scrambling an egg without cracking the shell
           </a>
           . His process doesn't require mucha raw egg and one leg from a pair of stockingsbut you'll probably have to practice a few times to get it right.
          </p>
          <p>
           If you succeed, this is what you'll end up with:
          </p>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="24028054docPartGal880009" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
              <img alt="How a Breakfast Badass Makes Eggs: Scrambled AND Hard-Boiled (Without Cracking the Shell)" height="532" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/27/71/63498706804731/0/breakfast-badass-makes-eggs-scrambled-and-hard-boiled-without-cracking-shell.w654.jpg" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;" width="532"/>
             </figure>
            </div>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <small>
           Image via
           <a href="http://instructables.com" rel="nofollow">
            instructables.com
           </a>
          </small>
          <p>
           Start by placing the egg inside the stocking, right in the middle. Grab each end and twist it 20-25 times, then pull the ends tight so it spins. Repeat this about ten times.
          </p>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="10296744docPartGal880011" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
              <div id="docPartGal8800110">
               <object height="324" width="532">
