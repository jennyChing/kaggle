        <div class="body " data-context="1150004" data-friendly-name="Article Inline Link Tracking" data-max-times-to-track-links="5" data-widget="tm.omniture.tracking" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          YOU might be able to find one a bit cheaper, but after a quick surf of the web I found a Porsche Boxster for sale at 3,650.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its a 1997 car, one of the oldest youll find (the Boxster was launched in 1996) and only the 205bhp 2.5-litre version.
         </p>
         <p>
          But what a load of car  and badge  for under four grand.
         </p>
         <p>
          The more exciting S model will cost you a bit more but even these are a bargain today.
         </p>
         <p>
          So perhaps in 15 years time well look at this weeks road test car and use words like bargain and its a steal.
         </p>
         <p>
          At 45,384 the latest Boxster S isnt exactly a steal, especially when you add a few desirable options to the mix.
         </p>
         <p>
          With its extras, our yellow S comes out at 52,731.
         </p>
         <p>
          But lets not talk about money for a while and instead concentrate on this spectacularly good car.
         </p>
         <p>
          The first thing you need to know is this latest Boxster is a lot more than a facelift and is almost completely new.
         </p>
         <p>
          The bodyshell is now more than half aluminium, the rest of it high-strength lightweight steel and magnesium.
         </p>
         <p>
          The result is a drop in weight of around 35kg. Thats impressive, considering the car is bigger than it used to be.
         </p>
         <p>
          <figure class="inline-image clearfix ">
           <div class="image-credit-wrapper">
            <span class="image-credit">
             Free
            </span>
            <img alt="New Porshe Boxster S" class="captify" height="409" src="http://i4.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article1150052.ece/ALTERNATES/s615/New%20Porshe%20Boxster%20S" title="Sleek: It's now longer and lower" width="615"/>
           </div>
           <figcaption>
            Sleek: It's now longer and lower
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </p>
         <p>
          The wheelbase is 60mm longer, the overall length has grown by 32mm and both front and rear tracks are wider.
         </p>
         <p>
          On top of that the bodyshell is 40% stiffer than before, not that I noticed the old one was bendy at all.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Boxster has never been a beautiful car. Its always looked a bit short and dumpy to me.
         </p>
         <p>
          The new one, however, looks fantastic. Its one of the best restyling jobs Ive seen in years.
         </p>
         <p>
          I love the side air intakes, obviously influenced by the ones on the Carrera GT supercar. Even the new lettering on the tail looks really nice.
         </p>
         <p>
          As well as being a bit longer than before, the new Boxster is lower and that gives it a less-podgy look.
         </p>
         <p>
          New outside, new inside. The interior style Porsche used on its Panamera saloon has recently been used on the new 911 and now a version of it is in the Boxster.
         </p>
         <p>
          The first thing current owners will notice is the conventional handbrake has been replaced by an electric one.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats to free up space on the centre console so switches can live there without the unit having to be wider.
         </p>
         <p>
          <figure class="inline-image clearfix ">
           <div class="image-credit-wrapper">
            <span class="image-credit">
             Free
            </span>
            <img alt="New Porshe Boxster S" class="captify" height="409" src="http://i3.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article1150051.ece/ALTERNATES/s615/New%20Porshe%20Boxster%20S" title="Open roads: It has a new inside and a new outside" width="615"/>
           </div>
           <figcaption>
            Open roads: It has a new inside and a new outside
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </p>
         <p>
          The quality of all the trim and the components has been improved so now the Boxster feels just as posh as any of Porsches more-expensive models.
         </p>
         <p>
          The soft top has also been completely changed. It stretches back further than it used to, has even better insulation and has a bigger back window that folds flat when the roofs down and doesnt have a hard cover that folds down on top of it (binning it saves 12kg).
         </p>
         <p>
          Testing the Boxster for its sunshine performance has not been possible but we have carried out comprehensive rainfall tests.
         </p>
         <p>
          We can tell you that unless you try driving the new Boxster to France not using the tunnel or ferry, you will not get wet inside it or hear rain hammering on its roof.
         </p>
         <p>
          Porsche has left the engine more or less as it was. Theres an extra 5bhp in the 3.4-litre flat-six, raising its power to 315bhp.
         </p>
         <p>
          But youd be a liar if you told me you could spot the difference in performance.
         </p>
         <p>
          <figure class="inline-image clearfix ">
           <div class="image-credit-wrapper">
            <span class="image-credit">
             Free
            </span>
            <img alt="New Porshe Boxster S" class="captify" height="409" src="http://i2.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article1150050.ece/ALTERNATES/s615/New%20Porshe%20Boxster%20S" title="Cockpit: Inside the new Porsche Boxster S" width="615"/>
           </div>
           <figcaption>
            Cockpit: Inside the new Porsche Boxster S
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </p>
         <p>
          By a stroke of luck, Porsche sent us a manual Boxster to test.
         </p>
         <p>
          The semi-automatic PDK gearbox is brilliant but I still dont reckon you can beat a manual for pure fun.
         </p>
         <p>
          One of the options fitted to our car is Porsches latest PASM (Porsche Active Suspension Management) system.
         </p>
         <p>
          It costs 971 but it does allow you to cruise around in luxury saloon comfort and then flick to a sports setting for more serious progress.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its money better spent than the 599 parking sensors, which should be chucked in for free on a 45k car.
         </p>
         <p>
          Mind you, you could always buy the standard 2.7-litre Boxster, which starts at 37,589.
         </p>
         <p>
          It still does 164mph and 0-62mph in 5.8sec (0.7sec slower than the S) and still looks the business.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats if you cant be bothered to wait 15 years until its three grand.
         </p>
