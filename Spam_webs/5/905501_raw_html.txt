            <h1 class="blog-title">
             <a href="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/oatmeal-cream-pie-bars/">
              Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars
             </a>
            </h1>
            <div class="blog-info-wrapper">
             <div class="blog-tag">
              <span class="blog-info-head">
               tag :
              </span>
              <a href="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/tag/bars/" rel="tag">
               Bars
              </a>
             </div>
             <div class="blog-author">
              <span class="blog-info-head">
               by :
              </span>
              <a href="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/author/kristan/" rel="author" title="Posts by Kristan">
               Kristan
              </a>
             </div>
             <div class="blog-comment">
              <span class="blog-info-head">
               comment :
              </span>
              <a href="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/oatmeal-cream-pie-bars/#comments" title="Comment on Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars">
               16
              </a>
             </div>
             <div class="clear">
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="blog-content">
             <p>
              If you like Oatmeal Cream Pies, youll love these Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars! They are simple to whip up and taste better than the store bought treats.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/oatmealcreampie.jpg">
               <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
                <img alt="Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars Photo" class="aligncenter wp-image-10329" height="773" src="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/oatmealcreampie-584x876.jpg" width="515"/>
                <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/oatmeal-cream-pie-bars/&amp;media=http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/oatmealcreampie-584x876.jpg&amp;description=Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars')">
                </span>
               </span>
              </a>
              My husband is currently building a man cave.
             </p>
             <p>
              Of course, hes not calling it a man cave. Hes calling it a shop. I know he thinks hes invented the whole call something by a work sounding name so no flags are raised thing, but I shop at Sephora and get to write it off on my taxes. Trust me  Im familiar with how this game works.
             </p>
             <p>
              When I ask where MY shop is, my husband tells me that I get the whole house.
             </p>
             <p>
              WHAT DOES THAT EVEN MEAN?
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_9462-2.jpg">
               <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
                <img alt="Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars Picture" class="aligncenter wp-image-10328" height="773" src="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_9462-2-584x876.jpg" width="515"/>
                <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/oatmeal-cream-pie-bars/&amp;media=http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_9462-2-584x876.jpg&amp;description=Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars')">
                </span>
               </span>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Do you know who ELSE gets the whole house? Everyone. The other day I walked into my bedroom only for my son to yell from my master bathroom, IM POOPING, YOU NEED TO LEAVE. Supposedly the kitchen is mine, but it doesnt feel very sacred when a toddler is hanging from my leg while I attempt to make spaghetti sauce on the stove. Also? Someone keeps losing the lids to the good pens I keep in my desk. And Im very particular about the kinds of pens I like, so this is kind of a whole big thing.
             </p>
             <p>
              What Im saying is, I dont want the whole house. I want my own woman cave. That has a door that LOCKS FROM THE INSIDE.
             </p>
             <p>
              I texted my husband a link to these
              <a href="http://lightersideofrealestate.com/real-estate-life/cool-stuff/introducing-she-sheds" target="_blank">
               cute little things called She Sheds
              </a>
              and he was all, What are you going to do with one? Drink wine in it?
             </p>
             <p>
              First of allyes. Second of all, I will definitely do other things in it. Like watch Ryan Gosling movies and eat Hot Cheetos and not change diapers. This is all so obvious, I dont understand his confusion.
             </p>
             <p>
              So while I am in the midst of championing this cause (SHE SHEDS FOR EVERYONE!!), Im not above baked good bribery. Its pretty much the best kind of bribery, except for new shoe bribery, which Im pretty partial to.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_9459-2.jpg">
               <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
                <img alt="Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars Image" class="aligncenter wp-image-10327" height="772" src="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_9459-2-584x876.jpg" width="515"/>
                <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/oatmeal-cream-pie-bars/&amp;media=http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_9459-2-584x876.jpg&amp;description=Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars')">
                </span>
               </span>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/2014/04/raspberry-zinger-cake/" target="_blank">
               Snack cakes
              </a>
              are a particular
              <a href="http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/2013/07/twinkie-strawberry-cheesecake-shortcake/" target="_blank">
               passion of mine
              </a>
              , and I couldnt believe Id never made Homemade Oatmeal Cream Pies on this site! So I decided to do one better and make Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars. All of the flavor, less of the fuss. Done and done.
             </p>
             <p>
              These are really good. Much better than the store bought version and so simple to whip up. Try them!!
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <div class="zlrecipe-container-border" id="zlrecipe-container-337">
              <div class="serif zlrecipe" id="zlrecipe-container" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
               <div id="zlrecipe-innerdiv">
                <div class="item b-b">
                 <div class="zlrecipe-print-link fl-r">
                  <a class="butn-link" href="javascript:void(0);" onclick="zlrPrint('zlrecipe-container-337', 'http://www.confessionsofacookbookqueen.com/wp-content/plugins/ziplist-recipe-plugin/'); return false" title="Print this recipe">
                   Print
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <div class="zl-recipe-link fl-r zl-rmvd" id="zl-recipe-link-337">
                 </div>
                 <div class="b-b h-1 strong" id="zlrecipe-title" itemprop="name">
                  Oatmeal Cream Pie Bars
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="zlmeta zlclear">
                 <div class="fl-l width-50">
                  <p id="zlrecipe-prep-time">
                   Prep Time:
                   <span content="PT20M" itemprop="prepTime">
                    20 minutes
                   </span>
                  </p>
                  <p id="zlrecipe-cook-time">
                   Cook Time:
                   <span content="PT20M" itemprop="cookTime">
                    20 minutes
                   </span>
                  </p>
                  <p id="zlrecipe-total-time">
                   Total Time:
                   <span content="PT40M" itemprop="totalTime">
                    40 minutes
                   </span>
                  </p>
                 </div>
                 <div class="fl-l width-50">
                  <p id="zlrecipe-yield">
                   Yield:
                   <span itemprop="recipeYield">
                    12-15 servings
                   </span>
                  </p>
                 </div>
                 <div class="zlclear">
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="img-desc-wrap">
                 <div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description">
                  <p class="summary italic">
                   **very heavily adapted from Just a Pinch
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
                 Ingredients
                </p>
                <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
                  Bars:
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1 cup (2 sticks) salted butter, slightly softened
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
                  3/4 cup light brown sugar
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1/2 cup granulated sugar
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1 teaspoon vanilla extract
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
                  2 large eggs
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1 teaspoon baking soda
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1/8 teaspoon ground cinnamon
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1 1/2 cup quick cooking oats
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
                  Frosting:
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1 stick salted butter, slightly softened
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1/4 cup heavy whipping cream
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-13" itemprop="ingredients">
                  1 tsp vanilla extract
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-14" itemprop="ingredients">
                  7 oz jar marshmallow creme
                 </li>
                 <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-15" itemprop="ingredients">
                  3 cups powdered sugar
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
                 Instructions
                </p>
                <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
                 <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                  Preheat oven to 350. Line a 9x13 pan with foil. Spray with nonstick spray and set aside.
                 </li>
                 <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                  In a medium bowl combine flour, baking soda, and cinnamon and set aside.
                 </li>
                 <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                  In the bowl of your mixer, beat butter and both sugars on medium speed until smooth and fluffy, about 1 1/2 minutes. Add vanilla extract and eggs, beating until smooth and scraping the sides of the bowl as needed.
                 </li>
                 <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                  Add flour mixture and beat until fully combined. Beat in oats until mixed. Press mixture evenly into prepared pan and bake for 15 - 20 minutes until edges are golden brown. Remove from oven and let bars cool completely.
                 </li>
                 <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                  Prepare frosting:
                 </li>
                 <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                  In the bowl of your mixer, beat butter, vanilla and whipping cream on medium low speed. Add marshmallow cream and beat on medium until smooth. Slowly beat in powdered sugar until just barely mixed. Increase speed to high and beat for one minute, until fluffy. Frost cooled bars. Chill bars until firm, then remove from pan, peel off foil, and cut into bars.
                 </li>
                </ol>

                 3.1

              These are the perfect after school treats. Enjoy and thanks for reading!
