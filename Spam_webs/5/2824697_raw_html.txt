        <header>
         <h1 class="title">
          How-to Change the Outlook 2007 Color Scheme
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/howto/author/mrgroove/" rel="author" title="Posts by Steve Krause">
            Steve Krause
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         |
         <abbr class="date time published" title="2009-07-30T23:17:41-0700">
          July 30, 2009
         </abbr>
         <span class="small">
          in
         </span>
         <span class="categories">
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/microsoft/outlook/">
           Outlook
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <section class="entry">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/tag/microsoft/outlook/" rel="tag" title="Microsoft Outlook 2007 How-To Articles and Tutorials">
           <img align="left" alt="Outlook 2007" border="0" height="100" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/image10.png" style="margin: 0px 10px 5px 0px;" title="Outlook 2007" width="100"/>
          </a>
          Many people dont realize it but its possible to change the
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Outlook Color Scheme
          </span>
          with just a few simple clicks. If your like me and you stare at Outlook ALL DAY, this is nice little feature as ANY change is good change. 
         </p>
         <p>
          <br/>
          <em>
           Update: See this groovyPost for the step-by-step for
           <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/howto/howto/change-office-2010-color-scheme/">
            Changing the Color Scheme using
            <strong>
             Outlook 2010
            </strong>
           </a>
           .
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Lets start things off with a groovy How-To Video
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h3>
          How-To Change the Outlook 2007 Theme Color (Video)
         </h3>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="510" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/x8z93u2WOtY?rel=0" width="640">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          And now the Step-by-Step with Screen Shots!
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           How-To Change the Outlook 2007 Theme Color
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1.
          </strong>
          <em>
           Open
          </em>
          <strong>
           Outlook,
          </strong>
          <em>
           Click
          </em>
          <strong>
           Tools, Options
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_44.png">
           <img alt="Outlook 2007 Tools, Options Menu" border="0" height="269" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_thumb_34.png" style="margin: 5px 0px; display: inline; border-width: 0px;" title="Outlook 2007 Tools, Options Menu" width="244"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2.
          </strong>
          <em>
           Click
          </em>
          <strong>
           Mail Format
          </strong>
          Tab, then
          <strong>
           Click
          </strong>
          the
          <strong>
           Editor Options
          </strong>
          Button
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_52.png">
           <img alt="Select Outlook 2007 Color Scheme - Click Editor Options" border="0" height="269" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_thumb_42.png" style="margin: 5px 0px; display: inline; border-width: 0px;" title="Select Outlook 2007 Color Scheme - Click Editor Options" width="228"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3.
          </strong>
          Under the
          <strong>
           Popular
          </strong>
          Tab,
          <em>
           Click
          </em>
          the
          <strong>
           Color Scheme Down Arrow
          </strong>
          .
          <em>
           Select
          </em>
          between
          <strong>
           Blue
          </strong>
          ,
          <strong>
           Silver
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           Black.
          </strong>
          <em>
           Click
          </em>
          <strong>
           OK
          </strong>
          to Apply
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_62.png">
           <img alt="Select Outlook 2007 Color Scheme" border="0" height="269" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_thumb_52.png" style="margin: 5px 0px; display: inline; border-width: 0px;" title="Select Outlook 2007 Color Scheme" width="354"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           All Done!
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          My personal Favorite is Silver but I normally change the color around every 30 days or so just to mix things up. I know
          <strong>
           Im WILD
          </strong>
          !!! 
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           (Default) Blue Silver Black
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_71.png">
           <img alt="Default Outlook 2007 Blue Theme" border="0" height="148" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_thumb_62.png" style="margin: 5px 0px; display: inline; border-width: 0px;" title="Default Outlook 2007 Blue Theme" width="200"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_8.png">
           <img alt="Outlook 2007 Silver Theme" border="0" height="148" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_thumb_7.png" style="margin: 5px 0px; display: inline; border-width: 0px;" title="Outlook 2007 Silver Theme" width="200"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_9.png">
           <img alt="Outlook 2007 Black Theme" border="0" height="148" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/image_thumb_8.png" style="margin: 5px 0px; display: inline; border-width: 0px;" title="Outlook 2007 Black Theme" width="200"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Very groovy indeed! Now go, rush off and tell your cube mates about this very groovy tip! And yeah dont forget to mention
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com" rel="tag" target="_blank">
           groovyPost.com
          </a>
          !!! 
         </p>

            <p>
             I never like Outlook 2007 color scheme on the month view. It only use one color tone for everything (background, events etc). Just found a way to color the events so they standout.
             <br/>
             -Right click on any event, select Automatic formating
             <br/>
             -Click Add, then Condition to open the Filter panel.
             <br/>
             -Select Advanced then Field, Frequently Used Fields to open the field list.
             <br/>
             -Select Start near the bottom of the list, then click Add To List
             <br/>
             -Click OK to close the Filter panel then click on Color to select a color for the event.
             <br/>
             -Click OK then voila, your events now can be a different color than the background.
             <br/>
             The only problem with this is some events seems to change the background color too. I dont know why. If you have a fix please post, but overall the month view looks a lot less confusing.
            </p>
