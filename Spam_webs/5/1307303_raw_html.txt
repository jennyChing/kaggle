        <h1 class="entry-title">
         What do good failures look like?
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2013-04-12T14:00:39+00:00">
          April 12, 2013
         </span>

         <p>
          <img alt="what do good failures look like?" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6259" height="396" src="http://www.game-changer.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/what-do-good-failures-look-like.jpg" width="737"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Question-to-innovate Series: This the nineteenth of a series of weekly posts where I will answer a few
            <a href="http://www.game-changer.net/category/question-to-innovate/" target="_blank" title="common questions about innovation">
             common questions about innovation
            </a>
            . Please feel free to add your own response. Also, if you have any questions you think we should discuss, let me know.
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          A few weeks ago I set the record straight about the relationship between innovation and failure:
          <a href="http://www.game-changer.net/2013/03/18/failure-isnt-the-goal-for-innovation/" target="_blank" title="Failure isn't the goal, but it is part of the process of innovation">
           Failure isnt the goal, but it is part of the process of innovation
          </a>
          .
          <span id="more-6180">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          The goal of experimentation is probe for answers you cant predict. When well done, mistakes will be made and learning will be had. Framing failure as learning is key to understanding its importance, because well then view it as productive trial-and-error. So, how do we know when weve failed productively?
         </p>
         <p>
          Simply put, good failures have two defining characteristics:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           You know why you failed and you have gained knowledge relevant to the next project
          </li>
          <li>
           Good failures happen fast enough and arent big enough to compromise your brand
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Pretty simple huh?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Bottom line:
          </strong>
          Without experimentation there is no innovation. We have to
          <a href="http://www.inc.com/howard-tullman/the-only-two-words-an-innovator-needs-to-know.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.inc.com/howard-tullman/the-only-two-words-an-innovator-needs-to-know.html', 'think about failure as learning');" target="_blank" title="think about failure as learning">
           think about failure as learning
          </a>
          .
         </p>
