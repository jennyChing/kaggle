                 <p>
                  <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/player/stats/_/id/12563/michael-crabtree">
                   Michael Crabtree
                  </a>
                  will try to salvage his NFL career by switching teams while remaining in the San Francisco Bay Area.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The free-agent veteran wide receiver has agreed to a one-year contract with the
                  <a href="/nfl/team/_/name/oak/oakland-raiders">
                   Oakland Raiders
                  </a>
                  , the team announced Monday.The deal is for $3 million with an additional $2 million in incentives, a league source told ESPN NFL Insider Adam Schefter.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Crabtree, the
                  <a href="/nfl/team/_/name/sf/san-francisco-49ers">
                   San Francisco 49ers
                  </a>
                  ' No. 10 pick of the 2009 draft, confirmed his decision on Twitter.
                 </p>
                 <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
                  <p>
                   It's official.. I'm a Raider
                  </p>
                  <p>
                    Michael Crabtree (@KingCrab15)
                   <a href="https://twitter.com/KingCrab15/status/587806896372842496">
                    April 14, 2015
                   </a>
                  </p>
                 </blockquote>
                 <script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js">
                 </script>
                 <p>
                  Crabtree's production waned last season, and he appeared to lose explosiveness after a 2013 Achilles injury that caused him to miss most of that season. Crabtree has had just two 100-yard-receiving games in the 24 games since his return from the injury, and despite playing in all 16 games in 2014, he dealt with nagging foot injuries. His highest-yardage total in a 2014 game was 85 yards, and he had 68 catches.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <aside class="inline editorial float-r" data-behavior="article_related">
                   <h1>
                    Editor's Picks
                   </h1>
                   <ul>
                    <li>
                     <h2>
                      <a class="" href="/blog/oakland-raiders/post/_/id/10508/five-thoughts-on-the-raiders-agreeing-to-terms-with-michael-crabtree">
                       Williamson: Crabtree addresses area of need
                      </a>
                     </h2>
                     <p>
                      Don't expect Michael Crabtree to be a huge difference-maker for the Raiders, but he does make a weak receiver group better.
                     </p>
                    </li>
                   </ul>
                  </aside>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Crabtree, 27, has a chance to be a starter for Oakland, which badly needs receiving weapons for second-year quarterback
                  <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/player/_/id/16757/derek-carr">
                   Derek Carr
                  </a>
                  . The Raiders tried to sign big-ticket free agents
                  <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/player/_/id/14053/randall-cobb">
                   Randall Cobb
                  </a>
                  (Green Bay) and
                  <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/player/_/id/12579/jeremy-maclin">
                   Jeremy Maclin
                  </a>
                  (Kansas City), yet they are settling for Crabtree nearly five weeks into free agency.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The Raiders still could take Alabama receiver
                  <a href="http://insider.espn.go.com/nfl/draft/player/_/id/38026/amari-cooper">
                   Amari Cooper
                  </a>
                  or West Virginia receiver
                  <a href="http://insider.espn.go.com/nfl/draft/player/_/id/37288/kevin-white">
                   Kevin White
                  </a>
                  with the No. 4 pick in the NFL draft, which begins April 30.
                 </p>

                 <p>
                  Even after saving a game for the Niners with a late 51-yard catch on fourth-and-10 at New Orleans last season, Crabtree derisively referred to himself as a third-down receiver or a fourth option. And after entering the season with a 12.9 yards-per-catch career average, he averaged a career low, which would have been 9.7 yards without the 51-yarder at the Superdome.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Crabtree caught four touchdown passes, the third-highest single-season total of his career. And according to Pro Football Focus, the 49ers' 91.1 passer rating when targeting Crabtree was third-best on the team, behind
                  <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/player/_/id/4512/anquan-boldin">
                   Anquan Boldin
                  </a>
                  (103.5) and
                  <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/player/_/id/11458/stevie-johnson">
                   Stevie Johnson

                  (102.0).
