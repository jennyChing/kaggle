            <div class="body label-hidden entry-content entry-content">
             <p>
              Wine: Sainte-Croix Syrah-Merlot
             </p>
             <p>
              Grapes: Syrah (50 percent), merlot (50 percent)
             </p>
             <p>
              Region: Vin de Pays DOc, France
             </p>
             <p>
              Vintage: 2013
             </p>
             <p>
              Price: $4.99
             </p>
             <p>
              Availability: Trader Joes
             </p>
             <p>
              In the glass: Sainte-Croix is a fine semitranslucent violet-red color with a firm ruby-red core going out into a fine light pink rim definition with medium viscosity.
             </p>
             <p>
              On the nose: It exudes bright red peppery fruit with freshly crushed strawberries, cranberries and red currants, followed by phenolically laced red pepper skins, vanilla beans and raspberry sorbet with just touches of fresh wood and wet stone.
             </p>
             <p>
              On the palate: This wine collides with the taste buds in the most dramatic fashion, partly because of its relative youth, but also because it is just a rockin little wine full of crushed red fruits. Its as if you took raspberries, strawberries and cranberries and mashed them up with cracked black pepper and a touch of calcium powder, youd have the hypothetical equivalent of this wine. It also has a nice red berry finish with firm dryish tannins, but all goodness in the glass.
             </p>
             <p>
              Odds and ends: It would be hard to claim that 2013 was a successful vintage in all of France. Bordeaux was really mediocre at best on average, while Burgundy made superbly charming, red-fruit forward lovely drinking wines. Champagne was nearly rained out, and then theres the south, where this wine comes from. Amid the sun-kissed vines of the Languedoc region where the grapes ripen more evenly like in California, superb everyday drinking wines such as this one from the great co-op La Bergerie are produced. Syrah is one of the successful grape varieties grown here and then merlot for the blend, making for a nice concentrated mix of a classic Rhone grape variety and a Bordeaux one. The result is this phenomenally well-priced bottle of wine. I noticed they were floor stacking this upfront in the Trader Joes stores, meaning itll sell like hotcakes, so grab a case before its gone. It would make a great companion to prime rib with au jus and potatoes. Drink it through 2018.
             </p>
             <p>
