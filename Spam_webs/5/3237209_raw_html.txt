       <div class="headline_area">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         AgainBWF Banned From Facebook: The Offensive Placenta
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           Mrs. BWF
          </span>
         </span>
         on
         <abbr class="published" title="2012-02-08">
          February 8, 2012
         </abbr>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.birthwithoutfearblog.com">
          <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-4622 aligncenter" height="421" src="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/299098_10150414187981894_593971893_10063417_1746517505_n-460x421.jpg" title="Tree of Life Placenta" width="460"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Thats right.
         <a href="http://www.facebook.com/birthwithoutfear" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.facebook.com/birthwithoutfear', 'Facebook']);" target="_blank">
          Facebook
         </a>
         found this picture taken by
         <a href="http://www.facebook.com/?ref=logo#!/Doula.Seana" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.facebook.com/?ref=logo#!/Doula.Seana', 'Seana Berglund']);" target="_blank">
          Seana Berglund
         </a>
         (of Ruthies placenta) to be in violation of their ethics. How? I dont know. It is awesome!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I honestly am laughing. Im not surprised. I have seen this happen to many pages. While I have been careful what pictures I post on
         <a href="http://www.facebook.com/birthwithoutfear" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.facebook.com/birthwithoutfear', 'Facebook']);" target="_blank">
          Facebook
         </a>
         , posting the rest on
         <a href="http://www.birthwithoutfearblog.com" target="_blank">
          my blog
         </a>
         instead, it still happened. The picture was removed, I was banned from posting for 24 hours and told if I violate again, my whole profile/page can be deleted. Two years of work in the page and supporting women. Gone, just like that, if they want.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.birthwithoutfearblog.com">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-4642" height="194" src="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/images-225x194.jpg" style="margin: 5px;" title="oh no baby" width="225"/>
         </a>
         On one hand, I find it frustrating, but on the other hand, I get it. Facebook owns Facebook. Just as I can ban someone on
         <a href="http://www.facebook.com/birthwithoutfear" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.facebook.com/birthwithoutfear', 'my page']);" target="_blank">
          my page
         </a>
         , Facebook can ban who they want. We are using it for free, as guests, and can be kicked out at anytime. I can respect that. Do I like it? No. But I get it.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         This has actually been on my mind lately, as I watch other natural and birth pages put in time out or detention and even scolded or deleted. Im not surprised it is my turn. The thing is, this is
         <a href="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/about-bwf/" target="_blank">
          MY blog
         </a>
         and it is not going anywhere. BWF is now a strong community of women that inform and support each other in TTC, pregnancy, labor,
         <a href="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/category/birth-stories/" target="_blank">
          birth
         </a>
         , post partum,
         <a href="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/category/breastfeeding/" target="_blank">
          breastfeeding
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/category/parenting-without-fear/" target="_blank">
          parenting
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/2011/11/10/mothers-and-our-families-never-perfect-but-always-great/" target="_blank">
          womanhood
         </a>
         and more!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I have asked myself what to do to ensure Facebook does not have control over our community of women (and some dads) and
         <a href="http://www.birthwithoutfearblog.com" target="_blank">
          Birth Without Fear
         </a>
         . There have been many ideas thrown around and this slap on the hand has made us (myself and my husband) decide.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I will continue to blog, but Facebook will not, can not, be the main source of our community. Women supporting women can not happen freely there. We propose
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <strong>
           a private BWF Forum
          </strong>
         </span>
         . Not only can we share openly and safely, but with less
         <a href="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/2011/11/03/compassion-and-love-goes-further-than-fighting-and-hate/" target="_blank">
          drama
         </a>
         . Only those that really want to be part of the BWF Family will be there. Also, you can
         <strong>
          post your own questions
         </strong>
         for support. I cant tell you the anxiety I can let go of knowing my emails will significantly decrease (I currently receive 20-100/day)! We have had a forum sitting there for months, time to get it up and going. Give us a few days and we will launch it!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Will this change the dynamics of BWF a bit? Yes, but I say, lets do this! Lets take this lemon and make some kick ass lemonade!!!!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         *What can you do? Other than everyone deleting Facebook, which wont happen, you can sign this
         <a href="http://www.signon.org/sign/facebook-let-us-post.fb1?source=c.fb&amp;r_by=2138543" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.signon.org/sign/facebook-let-us-post.fb1?source=c.fb&amp;r_by=2138543', 'petition']);" target="_blank">
          petition
         </a>
         . See you on the BWF Forums soon!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          UPDATE
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, apparently FB doesnt like the thumbnail version either as I got this notice:
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.birthwithoutfearblog.com">
          <img alt="banned from facebook" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4801" src="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/banned-1-cropped-2.bmp" title="banned 1 cropped 2"/>
         </a>
         Ive been suspended
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4800" src="http://birthwithoutfearblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/banned-2-cropped.bmp" title="banned 2 cropped"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The
         <a href="http://www.facebook.com/Doula.Seana" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.facebook.com/Doula.Seana', 'photographer']);" target="_blank">
          photographer
         </a>
         also had the picture removed and a warning
        </p>
