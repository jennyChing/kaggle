           Dear The Other Woman,
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           You are not the other woman. You are just a woman who has chosen to put yourself into an equation and position that you have no business being in. When I first discovered your existence, I sat frozen in pain; pain that moved me to my knees, and pain that caused me to sob so forcefully that my body had not one drop of energy left to even produce a sound.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I sat there, on the floor of my childs room unmovable. I wanted to scream in anger, and I wanted to call you and tell you how you ruined my marriage, but that wouldnt be true. You didnt ruin my marriage, you ruined yourself, and as much as I wanted to hate you, I couldnt. Gosh, I sure tried, and let me tell you, I came close.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           Then, my daughter came into the room and saw my tears pouring over a phone in my hand. She put her arms around me and told me it would all be OK- it would be OK, mommy- and I thought. One day she will be a woman herself. And on her journey, she will either be a woman like you, or a woman like me, and I didnt want her to be a woman like
          </div>
          <div>
           either of us at that moment.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I took out the evidence, and read your emails and texts to my husband. I viewed the photos you sent, and I counted the calls on our phone bill. As I read over and over, your professing of love and admiration for my husband, I became sad- for you. I am not proud to admit that I have been on both sides in this situation, but as I believe all life experiences can grow us or destroy us, I want to share with you what I learned in my regretful role as the other woman.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           When I reached out to men from my past, or entertained men from my present, I never did so with the intention of making them feel good. In fact, it wasnt about the man at all. It was about me, and it was selfish. Sometimes, I was able to distort the facts enough to actually convince myself that I had the most innocent of intentions; I just wanted to catch up with an old friend, I just wanted him to know that when I turned him down years ago- it was me, not him. I wanted to just see how he was doing and let him know how much I respected his marriage and family life- but those were all lies.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           If there was any truth to those mirages of my mind, I would not have pursued the man, nor would I have shared with him any past, or present feelings. If I truly loved him, I would have celebrated in his life and the choices he made willing, even if they didnt involve me. I wouldnt have told him anything that would cause him to reflect and reevaluate his current relationship status. I would never have put him in a place that would cause him to question the vows he willing spoke.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           Instead, I reached out to a man because I was lonely, just as I suspect you are. I filled his ears and mind with beautiful words and painted pictures of blue skies and tender moments we had once exchanged, and I shared with him the moments I hoped we could one day have as well. I was unhappy with myself , and with my life, and with the lack of my accomplishments in my life, so I came to him with hope; to feel wanted, validated, and thought of- just as you hope communicating with my husband will do for you. And while you may feel that he has that say in that decison, to entertain your pursuits or not, he is not the only one in this marriage who can refuse or reject your propositions.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           You see, my husband does not belong to you, nor does he belong to me. He belongs to Christ. And one day, we choose to leave all others and be bound to one another as the same, and God handed him to me; a man and a woman to become one under the Authority of Christ. Every time you correspond with my husband, you correspond with me, and even more importantly, you correspond with Christ- in whose hands our marriage is held.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I know you once loved him, and I know you still do, and always will- I read that several times, but your actions are not those done in love. You have no place to share what feelings your heart has for my husband. It is not honoring to me, to him, or to God. If you truly loved my husband, you would not speak things that may cause him to stumble. Your absence and lack of romantic confessions would have shown that you respected and honored him as a man, and as someones husbands.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           Instead, you acted selfishly, and went seeking validation, attention, and affection from a man who possibly could have have given you those things years ago, and even though he is married now, you hoped his offer would still stand, or at least sustain you until you found a love to call your own.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I want to hate you. I want to be angry, and bitter, and I want to fall apart. I wish that I could erase all that my eyes have soaked in as they play like tapes in my mind each time he is away from me, and each time his cell phone alert chimes. I wish that I never knew you existed. But I do, so- now what?
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           Do I label him as untrustworthy and file for a divorce, or do I try to just forget this all as if it were a bad dream? Do I tell you that I have known of your existence over the years, and that I have seen your photos- and read every word that you never meant for me to see? I wish. But, as a Christian woman, I am left with few options, and while your actions and confessions nearly caused me to act out of anger, God has given me the strength to act in love.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I have decided to pray for you; your heart, your emotions, your family, and your view of self. I have decided to view you as a woman in need, and I have decided to call you by name, and not by mistress. I have decided to tell you the things that other woman in my situation have not been strong enough to do, or simply had no words to express the pain that a woman like you have caused.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I love you not because you pursued my husband, but because my Heavenly Father has commanded me to do so. I have chosen to overlook your wrongs and indiscretions, not because I want to, but because I must; just as God extended His grace and mercy to me, I must extend that to you. This does not mean that I accept or welcome your involvement or friendship in my marriage. Your presence has no place here, and never will.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           As a little girl, I sat on my bedroom floor with my ear pressed to my parents bedroom wall. I could hear my father talking as if it was harmless and friendly interactions, but my mother, oh, my mother- she sobbed just as I did the day I read your letter to my husband, and I felt the heartbreak she had felt- the heartbreak that occurs from a man entertaining the affections and words from a woman- not his wife
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I pray that today, as you read this, you will delete his number and all means of contact from every source you have. I pray that you will release him, just as I am releasing you. And when he calls to see if youre OK, and where youve been, as I know that he will do,- I ask that you find the courage to say goodbye, just as Ive found the courage to forgive.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I know that he will never forget you, and I know that you will never forget him, but I ask that you forget the possibility of him being anything other than my husband- because on earth and in heaven, we are bound by a vow that no man, or woman, can separate, and tonight, I put all faith in my God to salvage the damage that has been done. There is no going back, and no way to undo what is- but I refuse to bring this into my future. In the almighty name of Jesus, your role in our lives, ends here.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I pray that as you continue on in life, you never find yourself needing to write a letter like this. I pray that you never know the pain that comes with such betrayal and break of trust, and I pray that one day you will be the dedicated wife that refused to let another woman take what is not theirs, nor yours- but Gods.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           I forgive-you.
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           Wholeheartedly,
          </div>
          <div>
           his bride.
          </div>
