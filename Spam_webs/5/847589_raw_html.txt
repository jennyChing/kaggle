                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     More Great Baby Blanket ideas and tutorials
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2035979805703803249" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      There are so many awesome baby blankets out there and I just didn't have time to make them all. I know, I Know, I am such a slacker. :) Here are a few more really great baby blankets and some great tutorials.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_w-H7EU9qR0w/Sg2uA2HdcEI/AAAAAAAAAaA/VSe3lamVHF8/s640/Sewing+and+Crafts+064.JPG" width="480"/>
                     </div>
                     Check out the double layer ric rac trimed receiving blanket tutorial at
                     <a href="http://sewmuchado.blogspot.com/2009/05/ric-rac-receiving-blanket-tutorial.html">
                      Sew Much Ado
                     </a>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_w-H7EU9qR0w/SqlJHCc2WaI/AAAAAAAAAhs/bpm2z1kyGh8/s400/IMG_5348.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_w-H7EU9qR0w/SqlJHCc2WaI/AAAAAAAAAhs/bpm2z1kyGh8/s640/IMG_5348.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Or you can check out this raw edge blanket also at
                     <a href="http://sewmuchado.blogspot.com/2009/09/rag-edge-receiving-blanket-tutorial.html">
                      Sew Much Ado
                     </a>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-355cHkbLdp4/TWMr5ur2zKI/AAAAAAAABUY/ReiwBwGSits/s320/DSCF2671.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-355cHkbLdp4/TWMr5ur2zKI/AAAAAAAABUY/ReiwBwGSits/s640/DSCF2671.JPG" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I don't know how to crochet. I did, but now I don't. But this blanket makes me want to learn again. I think my very most favorite baby blanket is a crochet blanket that someone made for my mom when she was pregnant with my little sister, that my mom then gave to me, and I have used with all my babies. I just love how stretchy and soft it is. Perfect for getting a nice cozy swaddle. Anyway enough rambling from me, this lovely was made by Jill at
                     <a href="http://www.oursevendwarfs.com/2011/02/baby-isaacs-crocheted-gifts.html">
                      Our Seven Dwarfs
                     </a>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_-TWSiReqFCU/TJKiStJxQKI/AAAAAAAAICE/Axs2D5U8c-4/s320/Froggie+Blanket.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="480" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_-TWSiReqFCU/TJKiStJxQKI/AAAAAAAAICE/Axs2D5U8c-4/s640/Froggie+Blanket.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I love the wide binding on the blankets from
                     <a href="http://www.sewdangcutecrafts.com/2010/09/baby-blanketwide-binding-tutorial.html">
                      Sew Dang Cute
                     </a>
                     . She used minky on the back, which I LOVE, but you could use this same tutorial with just about ant fabrics.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://i260.photobucket.com/albums/ii35/minxo55/Prudent%20Baby%20Assets%202/IMG_8398.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="498" src="http://i260.photobucket.com/albums/ii35/minxo55/Prudent%20Baby%20Assets%202/IMG_8398.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I think next time I have a baby I will have to give these fancy shmancy swaddle blankets a try. They look like they would work great and they are so cute. You can get the tutorial for these two at
                     <a href="http://www.prudentbaby.com/2010/05/new-improved-diy-swaddle-blanket.html">
                      Prudent Baby
                     </a>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     And last but not least the self binding baby blanket.
                     <br/>
