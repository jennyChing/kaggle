         <h2 class="title">
          Shots Fired! Dame Dash Exposes Funkmaster Flex, Lyor Cohen, Steve Stoute &amp; Many More! [AUDIO]
         </h2>
         <div class="postmeta-primary">
          <span class="meta_date">
           June 07, 2014
          </span>
          <span class="meta_author">
           Word On Da Street
          </span>
          <span class="meta_comments">
           <a href="http://wordondastreet.com/shots-fired-dame-dash-exposes-funkmaster-flex-lyor-cohen-steve-stoute-many-audio/#respond">
            <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="17267 http://wordondastreet.com/?p=17267">
             No comments
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="entry clearfix">
          <img alt="funk-flex-dame-dash" class="alignleft featured_image wp-post-image" height="180" src="http://wordondastreet.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/funk-flex-dame-dash-300x180.jpg" width="300"/>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             Dame Dash has been letting certain industry heads have it over the past week or so?! Dame aired out his grievances with major industry figures Lyor Cohen, Funkmaster Flex, Steve Stoute, and Joie Manda.
            </strong>
            <strong>
             Flex responded the other day going on a lengthy on-air rant telling Dash to stick to artwork and clothing and slippers, and accusing him of owing large amounts of money to Curren$y and Beanie Sigel. Dash then told his side of the story on the Combat Jack show and WENT IN on Flex! Check it out below
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-17267">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe frameborder="no" height="400" scrolling="no" src="https://w.soundcloud.com/player/?visual=true&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fapi.soundcloud.com%2Ftracks%2F153128697&amp;show_artwork=true&amp;maxwidth=500&amp;maxheight=750" width="500">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p dir="ltr">
           What I do is I look at other peoples perspectives, he said. You have guys likeEbroand Im not calling him out. You got guys like Flex who have jobs. Theyre paid. They dont have any equity in anything that theyre working for. A guy like Flex has had the same exact job for 25 years straight. Hes 46 years old. A guy like Ebro I dont knowbut again hes employed by somebody. Hes told what to do. Hes told to protect something that he doesnt own.
          </p>
          <p dir="ltr">
           He went on to explain that this is a much different outlook than that of an artist or entrepreneur. His mentality is completely different than Chuck Ds, he continued. Chuck D is a man that fights for freedom. He fights for what he believes in. For a person with a jobHot 97 is owned by a parent company and those people culturally have nothing to do with Hip Hop. They make money from it all day long. Other people that work for Hot 97 fight for them but they own nothing. They have a secure salary. They dont know about putting up their own money. They dont know about fighting for what they believe in. Theyre safe.
          </p>
          <p dir="ltr">
           Dash also addressed more of his issues with label and industry figureheads. These CEOs have made so much money off rap beef for so long, he said. Its not like when two rappers have a problem with each other they sit them down and squash it. They actually put a battery in their backsort of like how a battery might have been in Flexs backand have them beef with each other. Then they make money off the winner. As opposed to what I do when I see two people having a problem, I sit them in a room and try to talk it through.  Via
           <a href="http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/dame-dash-responds-to-funkmaster-flex-news.10840.html">
            MTV
           </a>
          </p>
