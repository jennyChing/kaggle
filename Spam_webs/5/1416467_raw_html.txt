         <h1 class="entry-title">
          13 Easy Ways To Detox Your Home
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="posted-on">
           Posted on
           <a href="http://www.chadwicks.ie/blog/13-easy-ways-detox-home/" rel="bookmark">
            <time class="entry-date published updated" datetime="2015-03-04T11:37:53+00:00">
             March 4, 2015
            </time>
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <strong>
           13 easy ways to detox your home
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          We often undertake detox diets to cleanse our bodies after a time of excess food and drink but what about our homes? Research shows the average home contains between 500-1000 chemicals. Cleaning products are packed full of harsh chemicals and chances are you have a few bottles for each and every room. If you have to wear rubber gloves and a gas mask to apply certain cleaning products is that really something you want to slather all over the surfaces in your home? Luckily it turns out its very easy to make your own natural products, save money and have a healthier home while youre at it.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           <strong>
            All purpose spray
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          You can make a long-lasting, all purpose spray with the following ingredients:2 cups Hot water, 2 tablespoons of white vinegar, half teaspoon of Borax, 1/2 teaspoon of washing soda crystals and 1/2 teaspoon of natural liquid soap. Add some drops of your favourite essential oils too. Alternatively you can use just three ingredients: white vinegar, natural liquid and water.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Image-19.jpg">
           <img alt="Image 1" class="aligncenter wp-image-2392 " height="290" src="http://www.chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Image-19.jpg" style="border: 2px solid #000000;" width="435"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://enviropureottawa.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/home-cleaning-ottawa.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://enviropureottawa.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/home-cleaning-ottawa.jpg', 'Image Source');" rel="nofollow">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="2">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Baking Soda
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Baking soda is great on pans with stubborn stains along with a coarse salt which acts as an abrasive. Baking soda and white vinegar work well together as a toilet cleaner. You can also use your baking soda just like the Shake-n-vac on your carpets for a fresh result.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="3">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Cookware
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Avoid non-stick pans as the chemicals used in the coating have been linked to cancer. Yes it may mean you end up scrubbing a little longer but the peace of mind far outweighs this. Look for stainless steel, glass or iron pans instead.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="4">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Drinking water
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Apply a water filter to your taps as they are a gateway in to your home for hundreds of chemicals.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="5">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Black mould
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Using clove oil and eucalyptus oil together works well on black mould in your bathroom. Just leave on overnight then wash off with white vinegar and baking soda in the morning.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="6">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Windows and mirrors
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          White vinegar, corn starch and warm water will have your windows and mirrors sparking in no time.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="7">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Showers
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          White vinegar, natural liquid soap and water work magic together in the shower. Swap your vinyl shower curtain for a cotton one. Vinyl is said to contain chemicals that become gaseous at room temperature.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="8">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Shoes off
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          It may sound pedantic but dirty shoes can bring in all kinds of pesticides and bacteria so make a space by the door for this new healthy habit.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="9">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Plastics
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Opt for cardboard or glass packaging over plastic where possible. Certain plastics can leak toxic chemicals when heated in dishwasher or microwave.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="10">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Bleached products
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          You dont need your paper towel and loo roll to be a bright white as this often means they are bleached products which contain carcinogenic chemicals. Look for unbleached products.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="11">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Clean air
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Ventilate as much as you can, weather permitting, as indoor air can build up its own pollution after a while.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Image-21.jpg">
           <img alt="Image 2" class="wp-image-2393 aligncenter" height="373" src="http://www.chadwicks.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Image-21.jpg" style="border: 2px solid #000000;" width="435"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.sjah.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/curtain_in_breeze.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.sjah.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/curtain_in_breeze.jpg', 'Image Source');" rel="nofollow">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="12">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Fresh floors
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Carpets often contain many toxins. If possible replace all your carpets with wood flooring, tiles or laminate.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ol start="13">
          <li>
           <strong>
            Gas levels
           </strong>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Lastly have a professional check your home for carbon monoxide leaks or radon gas levels.
         </p>
        </div>
