           <h1 itemprop="itemReviewed">
            Review: #1 For $1: Brain Boy  Psychic Warfare
           </h1>
           <span class="author">
            by
            <span itemprop="author">
             Jideobi Odunze
            </span>
           </span>
           <div class="featured-panel">
            <div class="inner-content">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="the-content">
            <p id="yui_3_16_0_1_1418659857712_137470">
             Dark Horse continues their promotion of favored titles selling one full issue for only one dollar. This time that title being Brain Boy. That telepath the United States Secret Service sends in to stop assassinations before the killers even decided to buy a gun. An interesting concept for such a character and an even more interesting character as well just for his attitude. One which seems to manifest most if you call him Brain Boy.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.geekedoutnation.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/1for1brain-boy-p2.jpg">
              <img alt="1for1brain boy p2" class="alignright size-large wp-image-34891" height="400" src="http://www.geekedoutnation.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/1for1brain-boy-p2-259x400.jpg" width="259"/>
             </a>
             Matt Prices story starts with one of his various missions. A story that takes us straight to the end, and then rewind to a point where we catch up on everything that happens up to that moment. You enjoy Brain Boy because this is as arrogant as you can get for someone who can read anyones mind and much more if you test him. Matt Price is a character you learn to like because like characters of the like he has that attitude that would turn you sour to him at first introduction. Not hard to understand why and that is all a part of who he is which is addressed later on. Especially since Fred Van Lente wastes no time diving straight into Matt Prices past. You almost sympathize with the way that his life has been planned for him with little say in the matter. To see how they molded him into the valued agent and asset that he is now.
            </p>
            <p>
             As a whole this first issue shows you just how dangerous things can get when the area of protection turns into psychic warfare. Especially when there is no trusting that someone right next to you can be able to do the same thing or if they can, do exactly as we saw near the end of this issue. Now with that said, the uphill fight that this series does have to conquer and will have to conquer consistently is bringing in villains who are stronger than Matt. Something which should be a challenge when Matt is already deemed and called the worlds most powerful telepath.
            </p>
            <p>
             Next to the story, the interior art is the most appealing of this issue. Clean lines, inking, and great detail goes into everything from the characters to the scenery. Especially that between the highway scene and the gore seen at the end. Now what really stands out is the coloring. Mainly due to how the colorist not only takes advantage of solid colors, but also meshes them together very well, shows variation, and uses shades of color to bring out depth in the characters. Not only this, in general there is a unique display of telepathic abilities which drives that message home that this is a Psy Vs. Psy fight. It wasnt enough to say that Matt was powerful, they made him look powerful with every entrance he made.
            </p>
            <p id="yui_3_16_0_1_1418659857712_137453">
             This first issue for Brain Boy is a real page turner. Turning everything that might seem dull or cliche about telepaths and creating a world where people really take advantage of that kind of capability. Enough so that the so-called strongest begins to wonder whether he knows everything or nothing at all. At the end of this first issue there is still room to find favor in Brain Boy to grow on you, but a lot of potential is seen from where this series can go.
            </p>

	function equalHeightColumns(group) {
		tallest = 0;
		width = jQuery(window).width();							
		group.each(function() {
			jQuery(this).removeAttr('style');			
			thisHeight = jQuery(this).height();
			if(thisHeight &gt; tallest) {
				tallest = thisHeight;
			}
		});
		if(width &gt; 767) {	
			group.height(tallest);	
		}
	}
