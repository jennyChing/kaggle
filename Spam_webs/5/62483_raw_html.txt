         <a href="http://smashface.com/blog/2010/04/celebrating-the-web.php">
          Celebrating The Web: Take 2
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="byline">
         Posted by Steve Woolf  
	April 16, 2010 
         <a href="http://smashface.com/blog/2010/04/celebrating-the-web.php">
          Permalink
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://smashface.com/blog/2010/04/celebrating-the-web.php#discuss">
          Discuss (3)
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="content_body" style="margin-bottom:30px;">
         <div class="img_center">
          <img src="http://smashface.com/gfx/blog/zadi-streamys-2010-530.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/thebuibrothers/4520592526" target="_blank">
           Photo by The Bui Brothers
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          Last Sunday Epic Fu was honored with a
          <a href="http://streamys.org" target="_blank">
           Streamy Award
          </a>
          for Best Web Series Host.  It was the first award of the night, and Zadi and I were obviously thrilled and rather surprised given the calibur of people who were nominated in the same category.
          <a href="http://www.kevinpollakschatshow.com/" target="_blank">
           Kevin Pollak
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/buck" target="_blank">
           Michael Buckley
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.diggnation.com/" target="_blank">
           Alex Albrecht
          </a>
          , and
          <a href="http://redcarpetcloset.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">
           Kristyn Burtt
          </a>
          are all people who either have well-established followings or are in the process of building them.  So that was an honor.  We did not expect to be nominated, so the win was truly unexpected and immensely appreciated.
         </p>
         <p>
          Since that eventful night, the Streamy Awards themselves have been the
          <a href="http://news.tubefilter.tv/2010/04/12/the-streamys-post-show-community-feedback-and-discussion/" target="_blank">
           subject
          </a>
          <a href="http://newteevee.com/2010/04/12/the-streamy-awards-a-producers-apology-and-its-three-fails/" target="_blank">
           of
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.mediapost.com/publications/?fa=Articles.showArticle&amp;art_aid=125919" target="_blank">
           much
          </a>
          <a href="http://sandeepparikh.tumblr.com/post/520184004/response-to-streamy-producers-apology" target="_blank">
           discussion
          </a>
          .  Suffice it to say there were technical problems and tonal problems and length problems and other problems that left much of the online video world confused and dismayed.  This blog post isn't about that night.  This is about last Thursday at an event called the Streamy Winners Celebration, a.k.a.
          <a href="http://www.celebratetheweb.com/" target="_blank">
           Celebrate the Web
          </a>
          , organized by
          <a href="http://twitter.com/kimevey" target="_blank">
           Kim Evey
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://twitter.com/jennipowell" target="_blank">
           Jenni Powell
          </a>
          , and sponsored by
          <a href="http://blip.tv" target="_blank">
           Blip.tv
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://jinx.com" target="_blank">
           Jinx
          </a>
          .  Celebrate the Web was designed to wash away some of the bad taste left in the mouths of attendees and viewers of the Streamys by getting a community of content creators together in an intimate atmosphere to enjoy the things that got us here, with no distractions of pageantry, awards-style comedy bits, or pretension.
         </p>
         <p style="font-weight:bold;">

          </a>
         </p>
