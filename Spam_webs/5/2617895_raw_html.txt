      <h1 class="entry_title">
       6 Things to Eat and Drink While Watching the 2014 Winter Olympics
      </h1>
      <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14604792" data-link="http://honestcooking.com/what-to-consume-while-watching-the-2014-winter-olympics-in-sochi/" data-summary="Here are a few Russian dishes that will put you in the mood to watch the icy action." data-title="6 Things to Eat and Drink While Watching the 2014 Winter Olympics ">
      </div>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Russian cuisine is notably diverse, here are a few traditional dishes that will put you in the mood to watch the icy action.
       </strong>
       <br/>
       By Annelise McAuliffe
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Pirozhki
       </strong>
       <br/>
       After a long day of watching athletes compete in the cold, there is nothing quite like a baked or fried pirozhki. This bun-like food can be filled with meat, fish, vegetables or even sweet fillings such as cottage cheese or fruits. Check out this
       <a href="http://www.saveur.com/article/Recipes/Pirozhki" target="_blank">
        savory variation
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/9339099794_e0fc55c420_b.jpg">
        <img alt="9339099794_e0fc55c420_b" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-89213" height="911" src="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/9339099794_e0fc55c420_b.jpg" width="635"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <center>
       <em>
        http://www.melangery.com
       </em>
      </center>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Salo
       </strong>
       <br/>
       This year the Olympic torch traveled farther than ever making its debut in outer space. Without traveling the distance, enjoy this traditional Russian snack of cured pork fat. Introduced to the country from the Ukraine, this versatile staple can be served fried, cold, or boiled. Try the brined meat thinly sliced atop toasted rye bread with garlic and vodka on the side.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/salo-1.jpg">
        <img alt="Salo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-89220" height="762" src="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/salo-1.jpg" width="624"/>
       </a>
       <br/>
      </p>
      <center>
       <em>
        http://claireannsmith.blogspot.com
       </em>
      </center>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Olivier Potato Salad
       </strong>
       <br/>
       French chef Olivier, created this dish at his acclaimed Moscow restaurant, Hermitage, that served Russian cuisine with a touch of France for 53 years. While always mayonnaise and potato based, this salad has morphed over time to include everything from apples to cucumbers, veal tongue, caviar, or capers. Check out
       <a href="http://www.melaniecooks.com/russian-potato-salad-olivie-recipe-2/865/" target="_blank">
        this version
       </a>
       and express your style with your own additions as you watch the new 2014 event of figure skating teams get creative on the ice.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/olivie-potato-salad-1.jpg">
        <img alt="russian Olivier salad potato" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-89290" height="414" src="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/olivie-potato-salad-1.jpg" width="635"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <center>
       <em>
        http://www.melaniecooks.com
       </em>
      </center>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Borscht
       </strong>
       <br/>
       It may be their first time hosting the Olympics as Russia, but it will not be the first time they have served so much of this common soup. It is estimated that approximately 70,000 gallons of borscht will be dished out during the games. Dont worry, if your next activity of the day is ski jumping
       <a href="http://thenakedbeet.com/ode-to-borscht/" target="_blank">
        this meal
       </a>
       wont weigh you down. High in sugar and vitamins, beets are a great source of natural energy. Get the recipe at
       <a href="http://thenakedbeet.com/ode-to-borscht/" target="_blank">
        <strong>
         The Naked Beet
        </strong>
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_2929-Borscht500x358.jpg">
        <img alt="borscht olympics" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-89186" height="455" src="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_2929-Borscht500x358.jpg" width="635"/>
       </a>
       <br/>
      </p>
      <center>
       <em>
        www.thenakedbeet.com
       </em>
      </center>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Old Fashioned Russian Bagels
       </strong>
       <br/>
       Every dedicated athlete of the fifteen different events knows they need to start the day with a quality carb load. What better way than with a traditional
       <a href="http://honestcooking.com/old-fashioned-russian-bagels/" target="_blank">
        Russian bagel
       </a>
       ? These sweet breads are easy to make and consume even if youre not competing for gold. Get the recipe
       <a href="http://honestcooking.com/old-fashioned-russian-bagels/" target="_blank">
        <strong>
         here
        </strong>
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/img_3320-495x330.jpg">
        <img alt="img_3320-495x330" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-89189" height="423" src="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/img_3320-495x330.jpg" width="635"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <center>
       <em>
        www.eatalready.com
       </em>
      </center>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Kvass
       </strong>
       <br/>
       Made of bread meal, generally rye, this low-alcohol, fermented and fizzed drink was described by Tolstoy as being the drink of Russian soldiers. Because of its fermented enzymes, this beverage is known to help digestive functions. If you have any pre-game butterflies, indulge in kvass. Make your own or try out
       <a href="http://www.beaverbrewingcompany.com" target="_blank">
        <strong>
         Beavers Brewing Company
        </strong>
       </a>
       in Pennsylvania.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Mint_bread_kvas.jpg">
        <img alt="kvass" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-89203" height="415" src="http://honestcooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Mint_bread_kvas.jpg" width="635"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <center>
       <em>
        www.wikipedia.org
       </em>
      </center>
      <br/>
