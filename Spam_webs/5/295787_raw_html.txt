       <div id="Introduction">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Introduction" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Introduction">

        <h2>
         Introduction
        </h2>
        <p>
         Taking an
         <strong>
          overland border crossing
         </strong>
         into
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/China/">
          China
         </a>
         can be fun. Most of the border crossings in China are in far flung regions of the empire and show it. Just remember that the regulations for border crossings change frequently and randomly. Also with most Chinese borders make sure you have your visa and other papers in order before arrival.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <div id="Hong_Kong">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Hong Kong" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Hong Kong">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Hong Kong
        </h2>
        <p>
         There are six overland border crossings between Hong Kong and China. These are Lo Wu, Lok Ma Chau Spur Line, Lok Ma Chau, Man Kam To, Sha Tau Ko and Shenzhen Bay. Lo Wu is a train and pedestrian crossing; Lok Ma Chau is a pedestrian crossing; Lok Ma Chau and Sha Tau Kok are road, bus and pedestrian crossings, Man Kam To and Shenzhen Bay bridge are road and bus crossings.
         <a class="external" href="http://www.td.gov.hk/en/transport_in_hong_kong/land_based_cross_boundary_transport/index.html" rel="nofollow" title="This is an external link">
          Cross Boundary Coaches
         </a>
         goes to several destinations in China, including
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Shenzhen/">
          Shenzhen
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Guangzhou/">
          Guangzhou
         </a>
         and its airports.
         <br/>
         <a class="external" href="http://www.mtr.com.hk/eng/intercity/index.html" rel="nofollow" title="This is an external link">
          MTR
         </a>
         operates intercities across the border to Shenzhen, Guangzhou, but also places further afield like
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Beijing/">
          Beijing
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Shanghai/">
          Shanghai
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <div id="Kazakhstan">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Kazakhstan" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Kazakhstan">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Kazakhstan
        </h2>
        <p>
         There is a year round 24-hour border crossing at Khorgos. There are buses from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Urumqi/">
          Urumqi
         </a>
         and Yining in
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Xinjiang/">
          Xinjiang
         </a>
         to
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Almaty/">
          Almaty
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Kazakhstan/">
          Kazakhstan
         </a>
         . Remember to get your
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Kazakhstan/">
          Kazakhstan
         </a>
         visa ahead of time.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <div id="Kyrgyzstan">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Kyrgyzstan" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Kyrgyzstan">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Kyrgyzstan
        </h2>
        <p>
         From June to September it is possible to cross the
         <strong>
          Torugart Pass
         </strong>
         to
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Kyrgyzstan/">
          Kyrgyzstan
         </a>
         . This is a very rough road and it is best to arrange transport from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Kashgar/">
          Kashgar
         </a>
         . Make sure to have all the visa arrangements lined up before hand and even then there can still be problems. Be prepared for random closures and other unusual events.
        </p>
        <p>
         There is also a crossing from Irkeshtam, which is south of the Torugart Pass and is considered much easier to get through, meaning that it is hassle-free. The Torugart Pass requires prearranged transport and is supposedly a pain to get through but the route is said to be stunning.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <div id="Laos">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Laos" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Laos">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Laos
        </h2>
        <div class="photo_container">
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/photos/stream/photoID/25389/orderByID/">
          <img alt="Border Crossing" class="photo" height="147" src="http://photos.travellerspoint.com/41305/thumb_NAMEO BORDER.jpg" width="200"/>
         </a>
         <h4>
          Border Crossing
         </h4>
         <p>
           All Rights Reserved
          <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/users/danalasta/">
           danalasta
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         There is a year round border crossing between
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Laos/">
          Laos
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/China/">
          China
         </a>
         . The easiest way to cross from Laos is to take a morning bus across the border from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Luang_Namtha/">
          Luang Namtha
         </a>
         to Mengla,
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/China/">
          China
         </a>
         . If going from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/China/">
          China
         </a>
         take a bus from Jinghong Yunnan to Mengla then take a bus to Lunag Namtha. It is possible to buy a
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Laos/">
          Laos
         </a>
         visa at the border.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <div id="Macau">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Macau" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Macau">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Macau
        </h2>
        <p>
         There are several options to get to/from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Macau/">
          Macau
         </a>
         , both overland as well as by boat. There are two vehicular entry points into Macau from China. They are the Portas do Cerco in the northern part of the Macau Peninsula which connects with Gongbei in Zhuhai, and the Lotus Bridge (officially the Cotai Frontier Checkpoint) which links the Cotai Strip with the Wanzai district of Zhuhai. Most people either cross by foot or take a longdistance bus to/from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Guangzhou/">
          Guangzhou
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Shenzhen/">
          Shenzhen
         </a>
         and Dongguang City in
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Guangdong/" rel="nofollow">
          Guangdong
         </a>
         province. These buses also go to the Zhuhai busstation, from where you can walk into Macau, which can save some money. There are also connections betweeen Macau Airport and these cities and between the Shenzhen and Guangzhou Airports and Macau.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <div id="Myanmar">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Myanmar" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Myanmar">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Myanmar
        </h2>
        <p>
         At this time to cross overland from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/China/">
          China
         </a>
         to
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Myanmar/">
          Myanmar
         </a>
         is only one way. It requires arranging for transport, a guide and paying for a visa in
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Kunming/">
          Kunming
         </a>
         . The trip takes several days and crosses some of the beautiful areas of
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Yunnan/">
          Yunnan
         </a>
         . This process is actually more expensive then just flying from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Kunming/">
          Kunming
         </a>
         to
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Yangon/">
          Yangon
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Mandalay/" rel="nofollow">
          Mandalay
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <div id="Mongolia">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Mongolia" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Mongolia">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Mongolia
        </h2>
        <p>
         The only open border for foreigners to cross is at Erenhot Inner Mongolia. This dusty town has little town has little to offer other then being the jumping point between
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Inner_Mongolia/">
          Inner Mongolia
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/China/">
          China
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Mongolia/">
          Mongolia
         </a>
         . There is a train line but on the Chinese side the train only runs a few days week. On the Mongolian side there are multiple trains a day to
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Ulan_Bator/">
          Ulan Bator
         </a>
         . The best ways to get there or leave on the Chinese side are long distance sleeper buses or taking the
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Trans-Mongolian/" rel="nofollow">
          Trans-Mongolian Railway
         </a>
         . There are many buses a day to
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Beijing/">
          Beijing
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Hohhot/">
          Hohhot
         </a>
         from the bus station. One cool feature is that on the highway out of town there are many large bronze dinosaur sculptures for a few kilometres.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>
        </a>
       </p>

         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Nepal" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Nepal">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Nepal
        </h2>
        <div class="photo_container">
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/photos/stream/photoID/164478/orderByID/">
          <img alt="Border Chaos" class="photo" height="150" src="http://photos.travellerspoint.com/99049/thumb_Border-Chaos.jpg" width="200"/>
         </a>
         <h4>
          Border Chaos
         </h4>
         <p>
           All Rights Reserved
          <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/users/jacquiedro/">
           jacquiedro
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Transports can be arranged between
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Lhasa/">
          Lhasa
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Nepal/">
          Nepal
         </a>
         during the summer time. In order to do this you must hire a driver and a car in
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Lhasa/">
          Lhasa
         </a>
         that will drive you to the border. Most people arrange a few stops along the way, such as
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Mount_Everest/">
          Mount Everest
         </a>
         . If travelling from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Nepal/">
          Nepal
         </a>
         you must arrange transport, a guide and a group visa, which can be a group of one. There is a Nepali Consulate in
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Lhasa/">
          Lhasa
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </div>

        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=North Korea" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: North Korea">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         North Korea
        </h2>
        <p>
         It is possible to cross overland into
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/North_Korea/">
          North Korea
         </a>
         . This must be done with a prearranged tour in
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Beijing/">
          Beijing
         </a>
         . Many times travelers to
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/North_Korea/">
          North Korea
         </a>
         fly in one way and take the train out the other way. The train from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Pyongyang/">
          Pyongyang
         </a>
         back to Beijing takes about 24 hours and is a great trip.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>

       <div id="Pakistan">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Pakistan" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Pakistan">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Pakistan
        </h2>
        <p>
         From May to early November it is possible to travel over the Karakoram Highway to and from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Pakistan/">
          Pakistan
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Xinjiang/">
          Xinjiang
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/China/">
          China
         </a>
         . The Khunjerab Pass is one of the highest in the world at 4800 metres. It is best to arrange transport in
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Urumqi/">
          Urumqi
         </a>
         or in
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Kashgar/">
          Kashgar
         </a>
         . Remember to have your Pakistani visa ready before you arrive.
        </p>
       </div>

        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>

       </p>
       <div id="Russia">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Russia" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Russia">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Russia
        </h2>
        <div class="photo_container">
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/photos/stream/photoID/222822/orderByID/">
          <img alt="Manzhouli Train platform" class="photo" height="150" src="http://photos.travellerspoint.com/139729/thumb_Manzhouli_..latform.jpg" width="200"/>
         </a>
         <h4>
          Manzhouli Train platform
         </h4>
         <p>
           All Rights Reserved
          <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/users/Lavafalls/">
           Lavafalls
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Officially you can only take the train across the border at
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Manzhouli/">
          Manzhouli
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Inner_Mongolia/">
          Inner Mongolia
         </a>
         , although if you have the proper
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Russia/">
          Russian
         </a>
         papers one can walk across pretty easily. Remember that visas must be arranged in Beijing first. Also the town of
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Manzhouli/">
          Manzhouli
         </a>
         is pretty interesting and might be worth a day or two. The grasslands surrounding the town are some of the largest natural prairies left in the world. Running through
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Russia/">
          Russia
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/China/">
          China
         </a>
         , the Trans-Manchurian train leaves the
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Trans-Siberian_Railway/">
          Trans-Siberian Railway
         </a>
         route near
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Chita/" rel="nofollow">
          Chita
         </a>
         , continuing via
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Shenyang/">
          Shenyang
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Harbin/">
          Harbin
         </a>
         in China.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="screen_only">
        <a class="link_to_top" href="#">
         <img alt="Top" src="http://1.tpstc.com/img/arrow-green-up.gif"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <div id="Vietnam">
        <span class="section_edit_link">
         <a href="/wiki_edit.cfm?title=Overland%5FBorder%5FCrossings%5FIn%5FChina&amp;section=Vietnam" rel="nofollow" title="Edit section: Vietnam">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <h2>
         Vietnam
        </h2>
        <p>
         Crossing at
         <strong>
          Friendship Pass
         </strong>
         , between
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Vietnam/">
          Vietnam
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/China/">
          China
         </a>
         , is very easy. The border is open every day of the year 7:00am to 4:00pm.  The best way is to arrange transport from
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Hanoi/">
          Hanoi
         </a>
         to the border. After crossing the border take a mini bus to Pingxiang in
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Guangxi/">
          Guangxi
         </a>
         Province. From there it is very easy to buy a bus ticket to
         <a href="http://www.travellerspoint.com/guide/Nanning/">
          Nanning
         </a>
         , which run every half an hour all day long. Make sure you arrange for your Vietnam visa (visa upon arrival is not available at overland borders).
        </p>
       </div>
