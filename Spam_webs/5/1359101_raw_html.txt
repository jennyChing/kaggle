        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Ranch Tofu Salad
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="author vcard">
          Article by
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://fitblogger.ca/author/kristina/" rel="author">
            Kristina - SpaBettie
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_0041.jpg">
           <img alt="" height="325" src="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_0041.jpg?w=490&amp;h=325" title="ranch tofu salad" width="490"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           I have been craving fresh veggies lately. Ive also been wanting to make more raw recipes. Remember my
           <a href="http://spabettie.com/2010/06/29/thats-dippy/">
            cashew tomato dip
           </a>
           ? I made another one that was raw, and while I was making that I had an idea Ive thought about for a month or so
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ranch dressing. Raw ranch dressing.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1 1/2 cups raw cashews
           <br/>
           1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
           <br/>
           1/4 cup cold-pressed olive oil
           <br/>
           1 clove garlic, finely minced
           <br/>
           5 chives, finely chopped
           <br/>
           1 teaspoon onion powder
           <br/>
           1 teaspoon oregano
           <br/>
           sea salt
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Pulse cashews in food processor to fine powder. Add onion powder, oregano, sea salt  process. Add acv, olive oil, garlic, chives  process until thoroughly combined.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Yum.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           I pressed some tofu. I am patiently awaiting the arrival of my
           <a href="http://shopopensky.com/spabettie/buy/tofuxpress-gourmet-food-press">
            tofu press
           </a>
           ; until then, this is my method:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_00272.jpg">
            <img alt="" height="334" src="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_00272.jpg?w=490&amp;h=334" title="tofu press !" width="490"/>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ha! It works. I cannot WAIT to use my tofu press!
           <img alt=":)" src="http://s2.wp.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif?m=1293710947g"/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           I then coated my pressed tofu with my raw ranch.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_00281.jpg">
            <img alt="" height="325" src="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_00281.jpg?w=490&amp;h=325" title="tofu" width="490"/>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Baked tofu for 17 minutes at 375.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Our salads contained:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           chopped romaine
           <br/>
           chopped iceberg
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_00311.jpg">
            <img alt="" height="325" src="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_00311.jpg?w=490&amp;h=325" title="lookit my new veggie keeper!!" width="490"/>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           (I love my new
           <a href="http://astore.amazon.com/spabettie-20/detail/B000OUY2QO">
            lettuce keeper
           </a>
            I want two of these!)
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           chopped red bell pepper
           <br/>
           broccoli
           <br/>
           Daiya cheese!
           <br/>
           marcona almonds
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Salads topped with warm baked ranchy tofu. Paired with raw carrot crackers from Pure Market Express:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_00362.jpg">
            <img alt="" height="325" src="http://spabettie.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/dsc_00362.jpg?w=490&amp;h=325" title="carrot !" width="490"/>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           This salad was so very tasty. I love this ranch, I love baked tofu, I love warm toppings on salad, I love marcona almonds! I *finally* found Daiya cheese, and it is as tasty amazing as everyone has said  almost buttery in flavor. Daiya needs to make a goat cheese and a brie, and I could possibly be completely dairy free!
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Salads:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
