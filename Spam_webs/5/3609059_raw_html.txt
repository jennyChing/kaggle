           Description
          </h3>
          <p>
           This low hours fuel injected 24ft. Sport-Cuddy Baja has all the upgrades, is fully maintained, and sleeps 3 adults comfortably, Any marine survey service will tell you the first thing to go on big boats is the steering, and this may be the most expensive fix, luckily this boat has addressed all the known issues, lots of $$$ spent to get it done right.
          </p>
          <p>
           Steering: Warlock hydraulic race steering ($2500), a must have if you want your steering to last in the (rough) great lakes, keeps the boat dead straight and easily controllable in any kind of water and help saves the gimbal bearing. New gimbal ring with complete new swivel shaft, u bolts, and bushing($3500)
          </p>
          <p>
           Engine has been upgraded to a 365HP (not 310hp like they all come). There is185 hours on engine (total hours on engine), runs excellent, no oil consumption, this motor should be good for 1000 hours. Makes approx. 400hp with the stainless marine manifolds and upgraded steering.
          </p>
          <p>
           Exhaust: Thru hull with internal baffles (legal all the time, no need for silent choice) mated to Stainless Marine Headers that were $3500 +25hp/tq, a nice upgrade for any boat.
          </p>
          <p>
           Outdrive: Bravo 1 mated to a SS Mirage plus 23 pitch (mint, with baja engraving), also has a custom drive shower installed to keep upper half of outdrive cool for less wear.
          </p>
          <p>
           New drop down bolster seats (drop down bolster seats cost me over $1000 alone to get done professionally, 30 hours of labour), plus the rest of the cockpit done, looks great, thousands spent.
          </p>
          <p>
           Trim tabs: New lines, new fluid, double hydraulic instatrim tabs with indicator.
          </p>
          <p>
           Stereo  Professional setup and installed, new MB quartz mids/highs, 2 10 inch subs, amp, and all supporting electronics,etc, over $2500 spent (great sound balance), I also upgraded the alternator for more AMP output, and installed 2 brand new batteries with 1900 CCR (combined).
          </p>
          <p>
           Aluminum I beam trailer in mint shape, a very nice piece.
