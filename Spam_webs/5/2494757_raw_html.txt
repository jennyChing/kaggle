        <h1 class="single-title" itemprop="headline">
         Glamping, California Style
        </h1>
       </div>
       <p class="meta">
        <time datetime="2014-06-11" pubdate="">
         June 11, 2014
        </time>
       </p>

      <section class="post_content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
       <p class="lead">
        Modern-day gourmands and hedonists have embraced camping by turning it into glamping  glamorous camping.
       </p>
       <p>
        This version of the traditional back-to-nature experience eliminates much of the rough from roughing it. The trend extends to luxury camping supplies, including top-quality coffee setups, stoves, sometimes even the kitchen table.
       </p>
       <p>
        If youd like to try a taste of the outdoor high life, youll find glamping opportunities near Los Angeles in
        <a href="http://www.elcapitancanyon.com/accommodations/safari-tents/" target="_blank">
         El Capitan Canyon
        </a>
        (11560 Calle Real, Santa Barbara; [805] 685-3887). On-site safari tents come complete with personal kitchenettes, soaking tubs and double beds dressed in fine linens. Add to that a country store carrying ready-to-eat gourmet meals as well as an array of organic goods and luxury accessories like Voluspa travel candles.
       </p>
       <a href="http://quintessentialca.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/1_Glamping_Camper.jpg">
        <img alt="1_Glamping_Camper" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-257" src="http://quintessentialca.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/1_Glamping_Camper.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        If a tent still seems too outdoorsy, skip it altogether and rent a well-appointed trailer instead. The most alluring rigs can be found through year-old
        <a href="http://airstream2go.com/" target="_blank">
         Airstream 2 Go
        </a>
        (1212 East Las Tunas Drive, San Gabriel; [800] 780-9880). These stylish (and highly recognizable) silver-bullet recreational vehicles sell for up to $100,000, but you can rent one starting at $5,300 per week. Inside your aluminum temporary home, youll find a gourmet mini kitchen, a barbecue grill and an iPad packed with ideas about where to stop and what to cook while youre on the road. These tricked-out rigs sport posh interiors designed by architect Chris Deam and will make you the envy of your fellow glampers.
       </p>
      </section>
