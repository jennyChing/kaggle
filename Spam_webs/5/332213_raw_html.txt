             <span class="meta-inner">
              <a href="http://immigrationimpact.com/category/business/" rel="category tag">

              </a>

              <a href="http://immigrationimpact.com/category/economics/" rel="category tag">

             </span>

               </span>
              </a>

           </div>
          </div>
          <a id="dd_start">
          </a>
          <p>
           <a href="http://immigrationimpact.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/14103406263_4bbd89a885_b.jpg">
            <img alt="14103406263_4bbd89a885_b" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15379" height="682" src="http://immigrationimpact.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/14103406263_4bbd89a885_b.jpg" width="1024"/>
           </a>
           June marks the first annual
           <a href="http://welcome.us/about/">
            Immigrant Heritage Month
           </a>
           , a time to gather and share inspirational stories of how the United States has been fueled by our immigrant tradition. As such, in a
           <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/carlschramm/2014/06/01/why-immigration-is-crucial-to-the-revival-of-americas-cities/">
            June 1 post in
            <em>
             Forbes
            </em>
           </a>
           , Carl Schramm describes immigrations historical role in American cities industrial growth, as well as the role immigrants continue to play in cities economic growth and development today and into the future. The United States industrial history is intimately connected with the more complex story of American immigration, Schramm
           <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/carlschramm/2014/06/01/why-immigration-is-crucial-to-the-revival-of-americas-cities/">
            describes
           </a>
           . The concentration of an industry influenced who came to work and the influx of immigrants from a particular nation, in turn, often made new industries All over the nation, different industries brought immigrants from different parts of the globe. Italians came, in part, to build the masonry cities of the eastTodays New York dairy farmers trace their roots back to the five thousand Scotch-Irish who came to build the Erie Canal in the 1820s. San Francisco has Americas largest Chinese diaspora largely because of the enormous need for labor to build the transcontinental railway.
          </p>
          <p>
           For cities pursuing a variety of strategies to reignite their economies, Schramm
           <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/carlschramm/2014/06/01/why-immigration-is-crucial-to-the-revival-of-americas-cities/">
            suggests
           </a>
           examining a citys historic relationship between the businesses that were created there and its immigrant experience as a way to develop insights into future initiatives. In essence, Schramm argues that cities should identify the kinds of industries and businesses they want to attract and recruit immigrant entrepreneurs who can once again transform the nation.
          </p>
          <p>
           Theres certainly an appetite for Schramms advice. In
           <a href="http://www.cleveland.com/business/index.ssf/2014/05/jobsohio_plans_to_partner_with.html">
            Cleveland
           </a>
           , for example, organizations are partnering to attract newcomers to northeast Ohio. Local leaders in
           <a href="http://triblive.com/news/adminpage/6189396-74/campbell-immigrant-pittsburgh#axzz33DPu9x10">
            Pittsburgh
           </a>
           are pursuing strategies to attract more immigrants to their city. In the nations quintessential immigrant gateway, New York Citys Small Business Services recently launched a new
           <a href="http://www.nycppf.org/html/dca/downloads/pdf/PR_CitiImmigrantOwnedBusinesses_FINAL.pdf">
            Immigrant Business Initiative
           </a>
           . New immigrant destinations across the South are also pursuing immigrant integration. Atlanta, which officially became a welcoming city last fall, recently
           <a href="http://www.wtvm.com/story/25637640/atlanta-group-formed-to-help-integrate-immigrants">
            launched
           </a>
           the
           <a href="http://www.atlantaga.gov/index.aspx?page=672&amp;recordid=2855">
            Welcoming Atlanta Working Group
           </a>
           to help integrate immigrant communities. In North Carolina, the
           <a href="http://charmeck.org/city/charlotte/cic/getinvolved/pages/immigrant-integration-task-force.aspx">
            Charlotte Immigrant Integration Task Force
           </a>
           began hosting
           <a href="http://wfae.org/post/task-force-public-how-can-city-be-more-immigrant-friendly#.U4iu3JTD44c.twitter">
            community listening sessions
           </a>
           to learn how to make the city more friendly to
           <a href="http://www.immigrationpolicy.org/just-facts/entrepreneurship-and-innovation-resource-page">
            immigrant entrepreneurs
           </a>
           , while
           <a href="http://www.wral.com/raleigh-city-council-approves-issuing-bonds/13660931/">
            Raleigh
           </a>
           officially became the latest welcoming city in the state.
          </p>
          <p>
           The rise of such initiatives is so great in fact, that it has prompted a regional convening of interested cities. The
           <a href="http://globalgl.wordpress.com/">
            Global Great Lakes Network
           </a>
           Convening, which is occurring June 12 in Pittsburgh, highlights immigrant integration efforts throughout the broader Great Lakes and Midwest regions. The second annual
           <a href="http://globalgl.wordpress.com/2014-agenda/">
            convening
           </a>
           brings together representatives of cities and organizations in the upper Midwestern region working on immigrant-related economic development initiatives to share their success stories and encourage discussion of next steps.
          </p>
          <p>
           As Immigrant Heritage Month began those who are spearheading the effort
           <a href="http://welcome.us/about/">
            noted
           </a>
           , the entrepreneurial drive and spirit of our country is built on our diversity of origins. It is what drew the first people to the U.S. and what continues to drive American business. American success is a result of our many distinct experiences, not in spite of it. Despite the
           <a href="http://www.immigrationpolicy.org/just-facts/entrepreneurship-and-innovation-resource-page">
            sizeable contributions
           </a>
           immigrants make to American cities, and despite the growing number of places large and small that are proactively working to attract and welcome immigrants, political dithering in Washington is costing the very places that contribute the most to the nations economycities and metropolitan regionsthe opportunity to become even stronger. While the U.S. and its cities have benefited greatly from entrepreneurial immigrants in the past, the country can no longer afford to squander away an opportunity to help those places become more successful.
          </p>

          </p>
