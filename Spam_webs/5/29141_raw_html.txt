           <header class="entry-header">
            <h1 class="entry-title">
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/06/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/" rel="bookmark">
              The First Ever Compassion and Technology Conference and Contest at Stanford University
             </a>
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta">
             on December 6, 2013 by Shawn Saleme
            </div>
            <!-- .entry-meta -->
           </header>
           <!-- .entry-header -->
           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/?p=99186">
             </a>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/compassion-technology-poster-for-web_72/" rel="attachment wp-att-99327">
              <img alt="Compassion-Technology-Poster-for-web_72" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-99327" height="776" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Compassion-Technology-Poster-for-web_72.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              In a world where each day more people are becoming familiar with technology and its social utilities,
              <a href="http://www.stanford.edu/">
               Stanford University
              </a>
              is playing host to the first ever
              <a href="http://ccare.stanford.edu/event-registration/?ee=98">
               Compassion and Technology conference
              </a>
              , which will bring social entrepreneurs, engineers and scientists together for discussion about technology designed to enhance compassion.
             </strong>
             <span id="more-99186">
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             The conference is presented by the
             <a href="http://ccare.stanford.edu/about/mission-vision/">
              Center for Compassion and Altruism Research and Education (CCARE)
             </a>
             in collaboration with
             <a href="https://www.facebook.com/facebook/info">
              Facebook
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://1440.org/">
              1440 Foundation
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.hopelab.org/">
              HopeLab
             </a>
             , and
             <a href="http://www.dalailamafoundation.org/">
              The Dalai Lama Foundation
             </a>
             . The conference will include esteemed presenters and panelists including the Director of Engineering at Facebook,
             <a href="http://ccare.stanford.edu/event-registration/?ee=98">
              Arturo Bejar
             </a>
             , President of Kiva,
             <a href="http://www.kiva.org/about/team">
              Premal Shah
             </a>
             , and other notable social innovators and academics. It will also conclude a contest in which designers, innovators and engineers were invited to present their ideas on how a technology design and/or product would help people learn, practice, or improve one or more of the following qualities: compassion, empathy, social connectedness and altruism.
             <!--more-->
            </p>
            <p>
             The
             <em>
              <strong>
               ten finalists
              </strong>
             </em>
             that have been selected will present their ideas at the conference this Friday December 6, and three will be selected to receive cash prizes (one $10,000 and two $5,000) to assist with their projects. They also will receive a one-hour consultation from the operating partner of The
             <a href="http://bbcollaborative.com/">
              Bridge Builders Collaborative
             </a>
             and the opportunity to meet His Holiness the 14th
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/03/06/dj-dalai-lama-his-holiness-bumps-that-bass/">
              Dalai Lama
             </a>
             during his visit to the San Francisco Bay Area in February 2014. See more information about the Compassion and Technology Conference
             <a href="http://ccare.stanford.edu/event-registration/?ee=98">
              here
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/?p=99186">
             </a>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/stanford-compassion/" rel="attachment wp-att-99321">
              <img alt="stanford-compassion" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-99321" height="370" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/stanford-compassion.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Below are the ten finalists and their projects:
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/ccare_compassion_technology_1/" rel="attachment wp-att-99291">
              <img alt="CCARE_Compassion_Technology_1" class="alignnone size-vn_content_img wp-image-99291" height="241" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_1-600x241.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Gabriel-Angelo Ajayi
             </strong>
             : Gabriel-Angelo Ajayi (Angelo) is the founder of MoodPik, a sentiment discovery network. Along with his co-founders, he created MoodPik out of an idea rooted in emotional connection; believing that emotion is one of the forces that fundamentally connects people. Angelo comes from a finance background, and holds a BS- business- from American University (06), and a MSF from Suffolk University (10).
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/ccare_compassion_technology_2/" rel="attachment wp-att-99292">
              <img alt="CCARE_Compassion_Technology_2" class="alignnone size-vn_content_img wp-image-99292" height="201" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_2-600x201.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Lauren Haynes
             </strong>
             : Lauren Haynes is Product Manager at GiveForward where the job description has the tag line Quirky, Joy-making Start-Up Desperately Seeking Meticulous, Do-Gooder with a Small Ego and Huge Heart.oh and a commitment to product metrics so some of those words might describe her. Shes a geek with heart and passionate about increasing diversity in design fields. Lauren spent nearly two years at the Ounce of Prevention Fund as IT Manager and 4 years at Accenture consulting with the Technology Labs. She focuses on human computer interaction, usability, technology adoption, and collaboration. And she swing dances.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/ccare_compassion_technology_3/" rel="attachment wp-att-99293">
              <img alt="CCARE_Compassion_Technology_3" class="alignnone size-vn_content_img wp-image-99293" height="238" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_3-600x238.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Kasley Killam
             </strong>
             : Kasley recently graduated with a B.S. in Psychology at Queens University in Canada. She is passionate about helping people lead healthy, fulfilling, and meaningful lives and aspires to ultimately become a clinical psychologist or medical doctor. Over the past two years, Kasley has conducted social psychology research at the University of Pennsylvania, which led to her first two academic publications, and worked with individuals who have severe mental illnesses while at her alma mater. An interesting fact about Kasley is that she took a year off halfway through her degree to attend acting school in Paris and live in a Buddhist monastery in Nepal. Inspired by a project she completed earlier this year, in which she did one compassion action every day for 108 consecutive days, and by her involvement as the student representative for the Canadian Positive Psychology Association, Kasley is dedicated to launching the Compassion-in-Motion campaign and thereby to promoting feasible, effective, and fun ways for people to enhance their compassion and social-connectedness.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/discovergiving3/" rel="attachment wp-att-99294">
              <img alt="discovergiving3" class="alignnone size-vn_content_img wp-image-99294" height="125" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_4-600x125.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Karli Larson
             </strong>
             : Karli Larson is a Pacific Northwest native with a background in marketing and communications for technology-based Inc. 500 companies. She is also a certified Roots of Empathy instructor, and for the past four years has taught weekly lessons to elementary school students about empathy and emotional literacy. As the Education &amp; Charities Manager for TisBest Philanthropy, a nonprofit organization based in Seattle, Karli has developed the DiscoverGiving program, a classroom philanthropy project that introduces students to the concepts of generosity and altruism.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/ccare_compassion_technology_5/" rel="attachment wp-att-99295">
              <img alt="CCARE_Compassion_Technology_5" class="alignnone size-vn_content_img wp-image-99295" height="338" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_5.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Belinda Liu
             </strong>
             : As a former Teach For America teacher, China Fulbright scholar, Harvard Graduate School of Education alumna, and Startup Chile entrepreneur, Belinda is deeply embedded in diverse local and global communities and is often known in her circles as a connector and leader. Her gift is the ability to identify the strengths of individuals and form strong teams that are effective, synergistic, and diverse. She also enjoys facilitating groups to take conversations to a deep place and coaching people to find their talent and express their inner voice. She lives and leads with her heart. In the past 10+ years, she has successfully coached education leaders, principals, and teachers to close racial achievement gaps in many urban schools across the country. She transformed public education systems by building strong practices around inquiry, data-driven reflection, collaboration, and strategic planning. Belindas own self and life inquiry is what inspired her to create Tech urSelf, a Bay Area startup focused on leveraging technology to help individuals and communities develop their inner purpose, passion, and well being.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/ccare_compassion_technology_11/" rel="attachment wp-att-99304">
              <img alt="CCARE_Compassion_Technology_11" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-99304" height="450" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_11.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Isabell Loeschner
             </strong>
             :Isabell is a PhD Student in Sociology at the London School of Economics with a Bachelors and Masters degree from Lancaster University. Her research interest is the study of technology in organizations focusing on use practices and ethical implications. She has a management background with international work experience in 4 countries and five organizations including multinational companies such as Adidas, Siemens and Booz&amp;Company. These experiences together with her academic background have allowed her to develop a large network including both world-leading academics and industry leaders which she will utilize in realizing the Altrubox vision. Additionally, she is affiliated with Rotary International, a world renown service organisation, together with which she has previously founded and chaired Lancaster University Rotaract Club (Rotary Organisation for Adults between 18  30) with the idea to promote service above self and to bridge cultural differences by bringing people from all kinds of backgrounds together. She has a passion for leveraging the potential of new technologies, such as crowdsourcing platforms, while ensuring that these innovations are used ethically and in the best interest of humankind. Altrubox is one of these potentials she wants to exploit by bringing the best of existing platforms together into one.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/ccare_compassion_technology_7/" rel="attachment wp-att-99297">
              <img alt="CCARE_Compassion_Technology_7" class="alignnone size-vn_content_img wp-image-99297" height="453" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_7.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Aimee Prasek
             </strong>
             : Aimee Prasek is currently a PhD student in the School of Nursing at the University of Minnesota (UMN). Her research is focused in integrative healing practices to alleviate symptoms of stress, depression, and anxiety. Aimee serves as a research assistant and co-director of yoga programming for the Tibetan Healing Initiative at the UMN. Aimee received her MA in Holistic Health Studies from the University of St. Catherine and an MA in Health Journalism and Communications from the UMN. Aimee has also gathered certifications in Healing Touch, Reiki, personal training, group fitness, yoga therapy (PYT-500), and is an E-RYT 500 level yoga teacher. Both inside and outside academia, Aimee has saturated herself in research and hands-on exploration of integrative healing practices. A personal search for peace and healing led her to these practices and the profound transformation she experienced now motivates her to share these practices with others. Fueled by a passion for a more compassionate approach to wellness, Aimee founded Sherman Project. She was selected as a 2012-2013 Dalai Lama Fellow and received funding to initiate the projects first phase of design and development.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/ccare_compassion_technology_8/" rel="attachment wp-att-99298">
              <img alt="CCARE_Compassion_Technology_8" class="alignnone size-vn_content_img wp-image-99298" height="270" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_8-600x270.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Sam Reiss
             </strong>
             : Sam Reiss is the founder of X-change the World. He is a Senior at Crossroads High School in Santa Monica, CA. Sam discovered his passion for service when a group of Tanzanian orphans visited his school in 2007. This encounter prompted him to co-found Kids for Tunahaki, an organization that raised funds to build a much needed home for these children. In 2010, Sam raised the funds to sponsor a soccer program for orphans in Rwanda, which later went on to win numerous local awards. In 2011, Sam founded X-Change the World, a project that uses an online educational platform to connect students in America with students across the world. It aims to bring English speaking skillsand other educational opportunitiesto underserved youth, and life-long, cross-cultural friendships to students both here and abroad. He has recently been named a Finalist for the Gloria Barron Award for Young Heroes, and the Jefferson Awards for Public Service recently awarded X-Change The World 2nd place in the Education and Literacy category of the National Youth Service Challenge. He is a Creative Activist with the Creative Visions Foundation.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/ccare_compassion_technology_9/" rel="attachment wp-att-99299">
              <img alt="CCARE_Compassion_Technology_9" class="alignnone size-vn_content_img wp-image-99299" height="224" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_9.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Enrique Solis
             </strong>
             : Enrique Solis is an Associate Research Specialist at the Center for Investigating Healthy Minds at the Waisman Center, UW-Madison. Enrique is the manager for the Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation funded Games &amp; Well-Being Study which aims to investigate the impact of prosocial games using neuroscientific methods. Enrique has completed the Facial Action Coding System (FACS) training with Erika Rosenberg and is also involved in a study examining the relationship between self-concept, well-being and altruism. He is interested in using behavioral economic measures as a way to assess altruism. Enriques previous work includes studies of emotion recognition within different ethnicities.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/12/04/stanford-university-holds-technology-innovation-competition-and-conference-to-promote-public-compassionate-action/ccare_compassion_technology_10/" rel="attachment wp-att-99300">
              <img alt="CCARE_Compassion_Technology_10" class="alignnone size-vn_content_img wp-image-99300" height="450" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/CCARE_Compassion_Technology_10-600x450.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Ralph Vacca
             </strong>
             : Ralph has been collaboratively designing and researching transformative learning experiences for the past decade. He believes technology is a tool that can be a source of empowerment for populations that too often are left out of conversations for positive social change. He has extensive experience designing and facilitating the design of digital learning experiences, including working with NYC public school teachers, teaching game design in after-school programs, co-founding a for-profit digital simulation design company, and designing games for social change. He has completed a masters degree in Educational Leadership, Politics and Advocacy at NYU, where he was an NYU Reynolds Fellow in Social Entrepreneurship and an Education Pioneers Fellow. In addition, he has taught university courses on digital learning design and spoken frequently in conferences about the future of interactive learning media. Currently, Ralph is a doctoral student at New York University focused on the use of technology to support emotion regulation and civic engagement.
            </p>
