             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               A
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/facebook/">
                Facebook
               </a>
               group created for posting pictures of random dogs is being accused of tyranny across the Internet.
              </p>
              <p>
               You may have never heard of it, but "
               <a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/10487409466/" target="_blank">
                Dogspotting
               </a>
               " is one of the Internet's most popular sports. With nearly 30,000 members on the main Facebook group and thousands more on separate, tributary groups and websites, Dogspotting has quickly become very serious business. With the institution of a new, strict set of rules, it's also becoming very controversial. Kind of.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/08/01/stoner-dogs/">
                 25 Dogs That Are Totally Stoned Out of Their Minds Right Now
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                Dogspotting is one of the most competitive groups on facebook don't let cute dog pictures fool you
               </p>
               <p>
                 tru spooky mom 2k14 (@yogurtfairy)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/yogurtfairy/status/519569518452961282" target="_blank">
                 October 7, 2014
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               For the uninformed, we've created the following explainer, which will break down the various rules and techniques, and the semi-sarcastic, deeply complicated history of Dogspotting.
              </p>
              <h2>
               The Rules
              </h2>
              <p>
               Dogspotting is a simple sport. To play, you must spot (i.e., see) an unknown dog (no pets, neighbors' pets or otherwise known dogs) and report that spot to the group. Photographs are entirely optional, so as not to discriminate against those without cameras.
              </p>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="Text dogspot" class="" data-caption="" data-credit-label="Screenshot" data-credit-link="" data-credit-name="Facebook, Dogspotting" data-credit-provider="Custom type" data-fragment="m!7239" data-image="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzEwLzA4LzFiL3RleHRiYXNlZGRvLjBhODE0LmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/fe5a25ec/7c6/text-based-dogspot.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="892" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzEwLzA4LzFiL3RleHRiYXNlZGRvLjBhODE0LmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/fe5a25ec/7c6/text-based-dogspot.jpg" title="Text dogspot"/>
               </p>
               <div class="image-credit">
                <p>
                 Image: Screenshot Facebook,  Dogspotting
                </p>
               </div>
              </figure>
              <p>
               Based on a series of rules, your spot is then given points. If the dog spots you spotting it, all points go to the dog. If the dog is acting violently, points are subtracted from your total. It's simple Dogspotting, guys  get on board.
              </p>
              <p>
               If you think Dogspotting sounds like a pleasant and irreverent way to pass the time, think again.
               <span class="microcontent" data-description="There is a war raging within Dogspotting  a war that is shaking the very foundation of the sport" data-fragment="there-is-a-war" data-micro="1">
                There is a war raging within Dogspotting  a war that is shaking the very foundation of the sport
               </span>
               and replacing the friendly sharing of cute dog pics with bitterness and vitriol.
              </p>
              <p>
               Many Dogspotters believe that points should be attributed freely, in an unorganized and creative fashion. Herein lies the root of the controversy. The world of Dogspotting has become polarized between two very different spotting methods: the "Boruffian Orthodox" and the "Free Point System."
              </p>
              <h2>
               The Free Point System vs. Boruffian Orthodox
              </h2>
              <p>
               In 2006, John Savoia established the sport of Dogspotting and created the Free Point System, essentially a free-for-all, creativity-driven form of spotting in which points can be awarded for a limitless number of things. For example, a dog who is squinting could receive a "+4 skeptical dog" bonus. A dog at the beach could receive a "+1 summer dog" bonus.
              </p>
              <p>
               This technique gives purpose to the Facebook group, as the loose method allows members to attribute humorous and creative points to spotted dogs.
              </p>
              <p>
               "The original vision  if you can call it that  for Dogspotting was the gamification of seeing dogs," Savoia told
               <em>
                Mashable
               </em>
               in an email. "The cute factor was never considered, especially since online cute culture didn't really exist."
              </p>
              <p>
               At the root of the Free Point System is the simple desire to share pictures of dogs and have a bit of fun competition.
              </p>
              <p>
               Enter Josh Boruff.
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                I am now the most hated man in all of dogspotting.
               </p>
               <p>
                 Josh (@Livestock)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/Livestock/status/519722208038363138" target="_blank">
                 October 8, 2014
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               A writer at Electronic Arts and
               <em>
                Something Awful
               </em>
               , Boruff entered the sport of Dogspotting in 2008, after reading a
               <em>
                Something Awful
               </em>
               forum post by Savoia.
              </p>
              <p>
               "[Boruff] was present when I first started talking about Dogspotting online," says Savoia. "He and most of our current admin team go back years, albeit many of those years we were't really thinking about Dogspotting."
              </p>
              <p>
               Over time,
               <a href="http://www.somethingawful.com/news/dogspotting-orthodox-rules/" target="_blank">
                Boruff claims
               </a>
               , he became dissatisfied by the "happy-go-lucky" spotting popularized by Savoia, and, along with David Thorpe, established a stricter set of guidelines, known as orthodox. From 2006 to Oct. 5, 2014, the Free Point System trumped Boruff's orthodox style, and spotters were allowed to assign points where and when they saw fit.
              </p>
              <p>
               After a recent influx of new users following an explosion of popularity in Australia  a phenomenon Savoia has no explanation for  the Facebook group adopted Boruff's orthodox method on Oct. 6. The new rules are harsh, and the admins will ban any user who resists the change.
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                DOGSPOTTING HAS CONVERTED TO ORTHODOX. THE DAY HAS FINALLY COME. RESIST AND BE BANNED.
               </p>
               <p>
                 David Thorpe (@Arr)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/Arr/status/519701294202646528" target="_blank">
                 October 8, 2014
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               So why the sudden change? Savoia has two answers.
              </p>
              <p>
               "One is what you've read in the group, that we held a vote and orthodoxy won," he says. "The other answer is that it's all been carefully planned and executed and all of the admin crew is in on it, but I don't think that could be true."
              </p>
              <p>
               Whatever the truth is, the new rules were set in place and made public.
              </p>
              <p>
               <iframe class="scribd_iframe_embed" data-aspect-ratio="undefined" data-auto-height="false" frameborder="0" height="600" id="doc_51679" scrolling="no" src="https://www.scribd.com/embeds/242300820/content?start_page=1&amp;view_mode=scroll&amp;show_recommendations=true" width="100%">
               </iframe>
              </p>
              <p>
               Under these new rules, Dogspotting scores can be more easily quantified. The picture at the top of this article, for example, would be awarded 48 points.
              </p>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="dog-spot-orthodox" class="" data-caption="" data-credit-label="Mashable Composite" data-credit-link="https://www.flickr.com/people/kpaulus/" data-credit-name="Flickr, Kristine Paulus" data-credit-provider="Custom type" data-fragment="m!de28" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzEwLzA4L2NlL2RvZ3Nwb3RvcnRoLmIyMDNiLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/1c32e8d3/fff/dog-spot-orthodox.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1920" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzEwLzA4L2NlL2RvZ3Nwb3RvcnRoLmIyMDNiLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/1c32e8d3/fff/dog-spot-orthodox.jpg" title="dog-spot-orthodox"/>
               </p>
               <div class="image-credit">
                <p>
                 Image: Mashable Composite
                 <a href="https://www.flickr.com/people/kpaulus/" target="_blank">
                  Flickr
                 </a>
                 ,  Kristine Paulus
                </p>
               </div>
              </figure>
              <p>
               While the orthodox method may allow for accurate and organized tabulation, it removes the creativity found in the Free Point System. Because a spotter can now accurately determine their own score, the Facebook group, if left under Boruffian rule, will become merely a way of keeping track of points and standings.
              </p>
              <p>
               The most controversial rule within the Boruffian orthodox method is the subtraction of points for small dogs. This anti-small dogs stance infuriates loyal spotters, who have voiced their discontent on social media.
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                The introduction of Boruffian Orthodox rules into the
                <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/dogspotting?src=hash" target="_blank">
                 #dogspotting
                </a>
                community is a travesty in my opinion
               </p>

               Today, the battle between Free Points and Boruffian Orthodox rages on. Can the thousand screaming voices of small-dog enthusiasts be silenced, or will creativity and freedom win the day? Savoia is certain his group will prevail, but doesn't want to get too detailed about the sport's future.
