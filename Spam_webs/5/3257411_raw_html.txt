    <h1 id="single-title">
     Meet The Rockabillies, 21st Century Americans Living In The 50s
    </h1>
    <div class="post-top-meta">
     <div class="date" style="float: left; display: block;">
      Published 1 year ago
     </div>
     <div class="like" style="float: right;">
      <span>
       Like Demilked on Facebook:
      </span>
      <div class="fb-like" data-action="like" data-href="http://facebook.com/demilked" data-layout="button_count" data-share="false" data-show-faces="false" id="single-top-like">
      </div>
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="content">
     <div class="desktop-ad" style="width: 336px;height: 280px; padding-right: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px; float: left;">
     </div>
     <div class="desktop-ad content-ad" id="">
      <div id="div-gpt-ad-1433929196547-0">
       <script type="text/javascript">

     </div>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Whether you think theyre amazing, fashionable, fake or genuine, the Rockabilly communities living all across America being photographed by Jennifer Greenburg are definitely a sight to see.
     </p>
     <div class="mobile-fold">
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       This photographer spent an entire decade photographing people who look as if they live in the 50s.
       <em>
        It is not as easy as going down to the shopping centre and buying a look off the rack,
       </em>
       the photographer told DailyMail  theyve modelled their entire lives around mid-century aesthetics.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The rockabilly community has its own philosophy based on the long-forgotten values of the 50s.
       <em>
        We did not have a disposable Made in China culture like we do now. When you bought a toaster, it worked for decades, and it looked good just as long. If it broke, you had it repaired. The middle of the 20th Century in the United States was a time when design was in its heyday.
       </em>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Greenburg, who has collected the clothing and jewelry from the 50s herself since she was a child, didnt feel like a tourist in their community.
       <em>
        Happiness, I believe, is everyones primary full-time job. And living a life that resembles, visually, the 1950s helps make that just a little easier.
       </em>
      </p>
