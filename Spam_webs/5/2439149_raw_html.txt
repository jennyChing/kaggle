              <h1 class="title">
               Senate Democrats Softening on Opposition to New Medicare Bill
              </h1>
              <div class="post-meta">
               <p class="meta-info">
                Posted  by
                <a href="http://www.johnbilletdoux.com/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by admin">
                 admin
                </a>
                on Apr 8, 2015 in
                <a href="http://www.johnbilletdoux.com/category/news-and-information/medicare/" rel="category tag">
                 Medicare
                </a>

                <a href="http://www.johnbilletdoux.com/category/news-and-information/" rel="category tag">
                 News and Information
                </a>
                |
                <a href="http://www.johnbilletdoux.com/senate-democrats-softening-on-opposition-to-new-medicare-bill/#respond" title="Comment on Senate Democrats Softening on Opposition to New Medicare Bill">
                 0 comments
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <!-- end .post-meta -->
              <div class="thumb">
               <img alt="Senate Democrats Softening on Opposition to New Medicare Bill" class="single-thumb" height="200" src="http://www.johnbilletdoux.com/wp-content/uploads/et_temp/US-Senate-1402788_200x200.jpg" width="200"/>
               <span class="overlay">
               </span>
              </div>
              <!-- end .thumb -->
              <p>
               The House bill that will bring sweeping changes to Medicare is beginning to gain momentum in the United States Senate  Democrats who were originally opposed to this bill are starting to come around. The bipartisan bill, which will be voted on later this week with confidence from leaders that it will pass in the House, proposes changes in how Medicare will pay physicians as well as grant an extension of the popular Childrens Health Insurance Program, more commonly known as CHIP.
              </p>
              <p>
               According to an article posted on
               <a href="http://www.politico.com/story/2015/03/senate-democratic-opposition-to-medicare-pay-bill-softening-116405.html" target="_blank">
                Politico.com
               </a>
               , on Wednesday President Barrack Obama put pressure on Senate Democrats to consider the changes, saying he looks forward to signing a good bipartisan bill. This bill will induce changes to the current Medicare formula and will help to put an end to the doc fixes that Congress has been passing for the last decade. With strong support from Nancy Pelosi and John Boehner in the House, there is also wide spread support for the bill in many outside organizations.
              </p>
              <p>
               Initially, Senate Democrats have two main objections to the changes to Medicare this new House bill would bring. First, although these changes to Medicare would extend the Childrens Health Insurance Program for an additional two years, Democrats are pushing to double to extension of the program to four years. Additionally, Democrats were also opposed to including the Hyde Amendment to the bill which would place restrictions on abortion funding for community health centers.
              </p>
              <p>
               Despite these concerns, support for the bill has begun to grow among Senate Democrats  one of the reasons for this change is the combined effort and involvement between the Houses top Republican and Democratic representatives. Additionally, Democrats are beginning to get on board with fixing the problems in the Medicare payment structure as well as being able to support the continuation of CHIP. This extension would give doctors and hospitals more time to plan ahead for the end of the program.
              </p>
              <p>
               According to the Houses proposed Medicare bill, the changes put into place would end the current formula being used to pay physicians and the Sustainable Growth Rate, which both political parties have agreed is a flawed process. The new bill would initiate a new payment process that will reward physicians based on their performance and the quality of the care they provide to patients instead of on the volume of patients they see  this will help to bring a positive shift in American medicine.
              </p>
              <p>
               While there is still some speculation about the proposed new Medicare bill, including the amount of money it changes will add to the federal deficit, but political leaders are confident that the bill will pass in the House. With Senate Democrats now getting on board with the changes this bill will bring, a bipartisan passing in the House will likely add momentum to passing the bill in the Senate.
              </p>
