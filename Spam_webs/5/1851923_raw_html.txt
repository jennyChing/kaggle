      <h1>
       Why start a site like this for writers and bloggers?
      </h1>
      <hr class="wh-gray under-post-headline"/>
      <div class="author-info">
       <a class="blue" href="/my-page/121017-1/cameron-kellock">
        Cameron Kellock
       </a>
       ,

    
        Auckland - Mar 12, 2013
      </div>
      <div class="share-info">
       (3744 views  / 36 comments / 41 shares)
      </div>
      <div class="post-content">
       <div class="post-photo-container pull-right" id="postPhotoContainer">
        <img class="post-photo vertical" src="/media/photos/opinion/514027b8fe7e31465ebdc8a0"/>
        <div class="post-photo-source">
         <b>
          Source:
         </b>
         Florian Klauer via Upsplash.com
        </div>
       </div>
       I dont know about you, but when I sit down with my laptop at my dining table, way down here midway between California and Antarctica, I feel decidedly outside the Silicon Valley inner circle.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       It seems there is a world of insider code and secret handshakes that is increasingly, deliberately being manufactured and manipulated by a few to be out of reach for the majority.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       For those with the right connections doors open, invitations arrive, secrets are shared and Angels smile down from the heavens.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       The rest of us are expected to just tag along and make up the numbers - or should I say metrics - as customers, subscribers, eyeballs to be sold to advertisers.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Dont get me wrong, one of the amazing things about the Internet is the fact that any teenager, or fifty year old for that matter, can create something from almost nothing. With an idea, a bit of business acumen and an off-the-shelf template just about any savvy entrepreneurial type can take on the world. Throw in a modicum of programming knowledge and the opportunities increase exponentially.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Of course, every industry has its hub of activity, and its core group of connected experts who work to both promote their collective efforts and protect their individual interests.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       But shouldnt this industry be different? It was, after all, created for all of us.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       To
       <a href="http://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Tim_Berners-Lee" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        quote
       </a>
       Sir Tim Berners-Lee: The Web is more a social creation than a technical one. I designed it for a social effect  to help people work together  and not as a technical toy. The ultimate goal of the Web is to support and improve our weblike existence in the world.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       So the web was created as the ultimate community service, the technological embodiment of democracy. As such, it should serve the needs of all people, not just a few. In many respects it does.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       But not when it comes to blogging.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       By that, I mean real blogging: writing an opinion, expressing an idea, sharing information. Not a mere couple of sentences posted on the fly. Certainly not just posting a link to someone elses writing to make yourself appear well-read. And definitely not posting pictures of the meal youre about to devour, or the deserted beach youre on. These messages all have their place, and they add greatly to the fabric of our daily communications, but we should not demean writers by categorizing them as blogging.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Blogging is writing. Blogging is important. The exchange of opinions is the fundament of human communication. Self expression through writing is, therefore, one of the most fundamental purposes of the web, yet it seems the more we try to evolve it, the more elitist it becomes.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        Read this! Please?
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       It began with the very best of intentions. A page on the Internet, for anyone who wanted one, to say whatever you wanted, however your wanted. Equal opportunity to speak and be heard.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Unfortunately, what began as an exemplar of free speech has devolved to become a world monopolized by big publishers and disproportionately connected insiders.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       There are a few writers who have gained recognition and a large audience the old fashioned way, by being very good at what they do. But they are a small minority.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       The rest? Destined to be mere whispers in the wilderness, undiscovered, unread.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       By my reckoning, around 90% of all blogs - and there are in the region of 200 million worldwide according to
       <a href="http://blog.nielsen.com/nielsenwire/online_mobile/buzz-in-the-blogosphere-millions-more-bloggers-and-blog-readers/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        NM Incite
       </a>
       - have less than 100 followers.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       (By the way, its almost impossible to find real statistics for average number of readers per blog. Try it yourself. I obtained my number from proprietary research I conducted via SurveyMonkey. I hypothesize that its not in the best interests of the major blogging platforms to publicize how few readers the vast majority of their members actually have. If anyone has real numbers, Id like to know.)
       <br/>
       <br/>
       As a result, for most bloggers attracting readers has become a preoccupation and skill as labor intensive as the act of writing itself. Tricks and tips on how to get more followers dominate any Google search using the term blogging.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       And what about the readers? To find all the information on a subject, to get many and varied points of view, a reader has to jump all over the web, from site to site, blog to blog, page to page. How many of us have the patience for that? So we settle for two or three regular sources we trust.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Few blogging pioneers would have envisioned how lopsided the sphere would become.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       However, the worst thing is that recent attempts at transformation appear to perpetuate, even promote the disparity.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        In the New Wave, it's who you know
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       I find it ironic that the new wave of anti-establishment blogging platforms-cum-publishers (Medium, Svbtle and Branch being the most notable) are as closed as the very institutions they challenge. They are either invitation only, or the first wave of contributors, carefully chosen friends and colleagues of the founders, vet you for worthiness to be a part of their conversation. How is that any different from the New York Times, or Vanity Fair, or any Conde Naste publication with staff writers and editorial control?
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Its not that I dislike these services. I find them elegant, informative and impressive. I simply disagree with their founding premise: I dont see how replacing one closed system with another improves anything.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Anil Dash, in his opinion piece,
       <a href="http://dashes.com/anil/2012/08/you-cant-start-the-revolution-from-the-country-club.html" target="_blank">
        You Cant Start The Revolution From The Country Club
       </a>
       , challenges the notion of a Silicon V/Alley elite starting a new kind of blogging platform filled with posts written by cohorts. He writes:
       <br/>
       <br/>
       ... the fundamental value which has given blogging and social media its moral grounding and its most significant impact:
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        The urge to make tools for communication and community more inclusive, more participatory and more democratic.
       </strong>
       To my surprise, this goal has been the part of the social web that has succeeded best, empowering and enriching the lives of many people who arent privileged by geography, wealth, inheritance, social standing, or identity. While far from perfect, its inarguable that people of many less privileged groups have participated in the social web from the start, and have been able to impact the world around them, and that counts for a lot.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Despite being an admitted insider himself, Mr Dashs article is a passionate, cogent argument for breaking down the walls:
       <br/>
       <br/>
        innovating on the core values of tools and technology while ignoring the value of inclusiveness is tantamount to building a gated community.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       If we believe Tim Berners-Lee, that the Web is to support and improve our weblike existence in the world, how can we now build for anything but a full embrace of diversity, for all its strengths and sins?
       <br/>
       <br/>
       And if Mr Dash sees that, sitting comfortably on the inside, imagine how it looks from out here.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       So when I sit in front of my laptop, opinions sharpened, I have to ask myself: Is my voice equally loud, equally valid, equally as important as everyone elses?
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Technically, yes. In reality, no.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        How to reclaim what is rightfully ours
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Create a platform that will give equal voice to all, in an environment that values all voices equally.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Create a structure that will  help people work together  support and improve our weblike existence ...
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Its that simple.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       And that difficult.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Lets face it, giving everyone a chance to say whatever they want is a recipe for chaos. But the world is chaotic. Naturally. And from that chaos we have always been able to distill, develop and evolve. Without chaos, without juxtaposition, without the collision of ideas and philosophies, wed not be debating blogging at all.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Yes, Im a believer in chaos. Because Im also a believer in the best aspects of human nature.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       So, for better or worse, freedom of speech and participation for all are the foundation stones upon which WriteHere is built.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       And here it is: a virtual Speakers Corner for the Internet; open to anybody with more than 140 characters to write, on any topic; a forum where posts will be organized thematically, facilitating discussion and debate around issues and interests; available to every reader.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       It's the everyday opinions and stories of everyday people, for everyday readers.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Part blog, part discussion board, part YouTube.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Its for people who prefer to write themselves, rather than link to someone elses. Its for people who appreciate that sometimes a picture cannot tell the story. Its for people who are passionate, expressive and opinionated.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Quite frankly, its uncertain, perilous work that will probably keep you up past your bedtime.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        There, and Here
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Rest assured, Im not advocating abandoning your blog or your website to WriteHere. Quite the contrary, Im offering WriteHere as an additional outlet to post your writing. Somewhere to put forward your argument, where it will be read with, and compared to, the opinions of others writing on the same topic. Its a forum where you can initiate, or participate in a conversation. And hopefully WriteHere will be a place where you can attract more readers.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       So, please, post your words as always on your blog or site if you have one, then simply copy and paste your text to WriteHere. (Dont worry, therell be an API to automate the process soon. One step at a time.)
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        Now, everyone can WriteHere
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       So there you have it, Im hacking blogging for everybody, not just a handful of somebodies.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Im putting my efforts firmly behind two ideals I feel strongly about: freedom of speech and participation for all.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Its not intentioned as a political statement. Rather, it is a simple statement of intent.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       My dining table is 6,525.36 miles from The Country Club to which Anil Dash refers - 10,501.27 kilometres, if youre that way inclined. Im as far from the inner circle as I could be. And Im an explorer, not an expert. So Im intentionally colliding with the status quo, hoping to ignite an evolution.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Are you in?
       <br/>
       <br/>
       WriteHere is now live. No waiting list, invitation or secret code is required. There are no editors or advertisers to conciliate. Just community standards of common sense, common courtesy and common decency.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       So I ask: Do you have an opinion? Is there an issue you're passionate about? A story or poem you'd like to share?
       <br/>
       <br/>
       If so, you are welcome to WriteHere.
       <br/>
       <br/>

var getSelectedText = function() {
    var t = '';
    if (window.getSelection) {
        // one tab open

        var sel = window.getSelection();
        if (!sel.rangeCount) return;
        var rng = sel.getRangeAt(0);
        expandToWord(rng);
        t = rng.toString();
    }
    else if (document.getSelection)  {
        // multiple tabs open
        var sel = document.getSelection();
        if (!sel.rangeCount) return;
        var rng = sel.getRangeAt(0);
        expandToWord(rng);
        t = rng.toString();
    }
    else if (document.selection) {
        // IE
        var rng = document.selection.createRange();
        // expand range to enclose any word partially enclosed in it
        rng.expand("word");
        t = rng.text;
    }

    // convert newline chars to spaces .replace(/\r?\n/g, " ") , collapse whitespace, and trim non-word chars
    return t.replace(/\r?\n/g, " ").replace(/\s+/g, " ").replace(/^\W+|\W+$/g, '');
};

function expandToWord(range) {
    if (range.collapsed) return;
    if (range.toString().replace(/\s+/g, " ") == '') return;

    while (range.startOffset &gt; 0 &amp;&amp; range.toString()[0].match(/\w/)) {
        range.setStart(range.startContainer, range.startOffset - 1);
    }

    while (range.endOffset &lt; range.endContainer.length &amp;&amp;
            range.toString()[range.toString().length - 1].match(/\w/)) {
        range.setEnd(range.endContainer, range.endOffset + 1);
    }
}

var showCommentDialog = function(html, extract) {
    if (typeof(extract) === 'undefined') return;

    if (extract !== '') {
        var templateContext = {
            extract : extract.replace(/"/g, '&quot;'),
            html : html.replace(/"/g, '&quot;')
        }
        var commentTemplate = _.template($('#commentTemplate').html());
        return commentTemplate(templateContext);
    }

    return false;
};

var renderComment = function($comment) {
    var commentTemplate = _.template($('#commentDisplayTemplate').html());
    var templateContext = {
        'author': $comment.attr('data-author'),
        'author_avatar': $comment.attr('data-author_avatar'),
        'author_location': $comment.attr('data-author_location'),
        'author_my_page_url': $comment.attr('data-author_my_page_url'),
        'formatted_date': $comment.attr('data-formatted_date'),
        'comment': $comment.attr('data-comment'),
        'comment_id': $comment.attr('data-comment_id'),
        'comment_user_id': $comment.attr('data-comment_user_id'),
        'thumb_count': $comment.attr('data-thumb_count')
    }
    return commentTemplate(templateContext);
}

            $('.opinion-arrow-left').live('click', function(e){
                var opinionId = $(this).parent().attr('data-opinion_id');
                var topicId= $(this).parent().attr('data-topic_id');
                var skip= $(this).parent().attr('data-skip');
                var that = this;

                $.ajax({
                    'url': '/json/opinion/previous',
                    'dataType': 'json',
                    'type': 'GET',
                    'async': false,
                    'data': {opinion_id: opinionId, topic_id: topicId, skip: skip},
                    'success': function(data) {
                        if(data['result'] === true){
                            var $currentOpinionMarker = $(that).parent().find('.current-opinion').first();
                            var currentOpinion = parseInt($currentOpinionMarker.text()) - 1;
                            $currentOpinionMarker.html(currentOpinion);

                            var opinionExtractTemplate = _.template($('#opinionExtractTemplate').html());
                            var opinionExtract = opinionExtractTemplate(data);

                            var photoImage = $(that).parent().parent().find('.photo-image').first();
                            if (photoImage.length &gt; 0) {
                                if (photoImage.hasClass('portrait-image') &amp;&amp; data['opinion_photo_orientation'] == 'vertical') {
                                    photoImage.find('.opinion-img').attr('src', data['opinion_photo_url']);
                                }
                                if (photoImage.hasClass('landscape-image') &amp;&amp; data['opinion_photo_orientation'] == 'horizontal') {
                                    photoImage.find('.opinion-img').attr('src', data['opinion_photo_url']);
                                }
                            }

                            $(that).parent().find('.opinion-extract').first().html(opinionExtract);
                            $(that).parent().attr('data-opinion_id', data['new_opinion_id']);
                            $(that).parent().attr('data-skip', data['skip']);

                            if (!$('#gridContainer').hasClass('normal-page')) {
                                $('#gridContainer').isotope('reLayout');
                            }
                        }
                    }
                });
                e.preventDefault();
            });
