      <h1 id="post-5135">
       <a href="http://www.everydayfiction.com/tethered-by-kevin-shamel/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: TETHERED  by Kevin Shamel">
        TETHERED  by Kevin Shamel
       </a>
      </h1>
     </div>
     <div class="postspace2">
     </div>
     <!--content with more link-->
     <p>
      Wyn floated with his pod above the churning sea. His umbilicus untwisted itself, and he spun lazily, staring down at the circling wings below. Nimble fliers, those wings. Wyn remembered not long after ascending how frightened he was of the little creatures zipping between the umbilical tethers of his people. He worried that their beaks would tear through his taut skin and send him soaring into the clouds. He did not want to go up.
     </p>
     <p>
      But Wyns tether was strong  it held him through storms, snapper swarms, and island rollovers. He could withstand a thousand tiny wing-beaks at once. Wyn didnt worry about such things as he gained experience.
     </p>
     <p>
      The storm swept southward. Swirling winds whispered goodbyes. Wyn watched the suns fade behind swelling thunderheads, and a gentle red glow suffused the world. He stopped spinning.
     </p>
     <p>
      Below, the walking islands crept along their spiraling journey through the seas, tugging Wyn and his people with them by their roots.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ebo spoke to Wyn. That was a big storm.
     </p>
     <p>
      Weve come into the Buffets, Wyn answered.
     </p>
     <p>
      So we have, said Ebo, staring down at the calming sea. After a long pause, where Wyn could hear his own umbilicus stretching, Ebo said, I thought about letting go.
     </p>
     <p>
      Wyn raised his head. Letting go?
     </p>
     <p>
      Its nearly time for me.
     </p>
     <p>
      Oh, Ebo. No. Ill miss you. Wyn shifted to face his friend, lifting his left fin.
     </p>
     <p>
      Yes. But well meet again. Above. The older floater flexed his whiskers  smiling.
     </p>
     <p>
      Maybe. Unless going up is the end.
     </p>
     <p>
      Have you been listening to Vyxis? Ebo chuckled. Hell soon find out for himself.
     </p>
     <p>
      Wyn looked up at the cloud bottoms. They were relatively calm, especially for just after a storm. Only sheet lighting. Youre not much older than me, Ebo. My pod ascended just after yours.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ebos eye twinkled. He looked down toward their roots. Yes. And your island is getting big. Eating a lot. Your pod is gaining plenty of buryl. Youll follow me soon, Wyn.
     </p>
     <p>
      But Im only just realizing my strengths! Learning the ways of nature. The mysteries of life.
     </p>
     <p>
      I feel the same, Wyn! Im only just reaching the strongest Ive ever been.
     </p>
     <p>
      And youre not sad about going up? Being cut off in your prime?
     </p>
     <p>
      Were you afraid to leave the ocean, Wyn? When you knew your umbilicus was long enough  strong, and ready to hold you? When your head breached the surface of the water, was it fear you felt? Or exhilaration? Longing. Hope. Excitement.
     </p>
     <p>
      All of that. And yes I was afraid to leave the quiet, comfortable sea and rush into the sky, Ebo. I was afraid my umbilicus would snap and Id keep going up. I worried Id choke on the air, or get hit by lightning. Yes, Ebo. I was afraid.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ebo swept over and nudged his companion. Its going to be fine, Wyn. I can feel it. I
      <em>
       want
      </em>
      to let go.
     </p>
     <p>
      Youre just high on buryl.
     </p>
     <p>
      I truly am.
     </p>
     <p>
      Im going to sleep.
     </p>
     <p>
      Pleasant dreams, Wyn. Savor every day.
     </p>
     <p>
      Wyn awoke to another storm.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ebo shouted, chasing away the memory of a dream. Wyn! Im letting go. Im going up! I wanted to let you know.
     </p>
     <p>
      Lightning snapped nearby, illuminating Ebo. He seemed to vibrate.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ebo!
     </p>
     <p>
      Goodbye, Wyn. See you soon!
     </p>
     <p>
      Dont let go!
     </p>
     <p>
      Ebo shook as tendrils of lightning shot from the clouds above. Wyn looked down to the darkened sea. He strained to see the end of Ebos long tether. And from the jagged mist of rain below, it came whipping past.
     </p>
     <p>
      Wyn looked up to see his friend illuminated just under the clouds  a fit of lightning surrounding him. Within moments, only the flayed-wide tendrils of Ebos roots remained below the constant cloud-cover. Then he was gone.
     </p>
     <p>
      The rest of Ebos pod followed. Six floaters withdrew their life-sustaining anchors threaded deep within the rock of their island, and went up. Before he knew it, there was empty space to Wyns left  something that had never been. The storm raged. Wyn was hit with all its force.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      ***
     </p>
     <p>
      Many storms passed. Wyn spent each of them thinking about his friend, and how much he missed him. Ebo had always been beside him, even closer than anyone in his own pod. He could barely speak to just two of his brethren across the wide sky between them, and only on calm days. He remembered Ebos words. He savored every day, even sad ones.
     </p>
     <p>
      Each storm made the lightning into an electric garden. The clouds above grew less threatening. Wyn still did not want to go up. He felt strong, and dove through air currents, tugging on his tether, nearly as agile as a wing. For the first time since just after birth, Wyn was aware of his roots.
     </p>
     <p>
      A new pod ascended from Ebos island. A floater came to rest beside Wyn. She was very beautiful.
     </p>
     <p>
      Im Aryl, she told him.
     </p>
     <p>
      They fell in love.
     </p>
     <p>
      The couple spent time talking and singing, wrapped around each others umbilicals. They soared through winds and danced. They made love. Aryl dropped spores into the ocean. Wyn thought about the cycle of life. He felt complete.
     </p>
     <p>
      One day Wyn said to Aryl, Im going to let go when this storm hits. He watched one gathering to the north.
     </p>
     <p>
      Oh, no! Aryl said. No, Wyn, our time together has been so short. What if its the end, going up? Please dont leave me.
     </p>
     <p>
      Wyn marveled at his lack of fear. He felt buryl rushing through his tether. He drew on its stores inside his wide body. He felt it hardening his umbilicus, turning it into a tail. Wyn looked up to the canopy of clouds, laced with lightning. He longed to fly.
     </p>
     <p>
      Rain pattered against his skin. Wyn flexed his roots.
     </p>
     <p>
      He told Aryl, Ill see you above the clouds.
     </p>
     <p>
      Lighting reached down for Wyn. He looked up and the canopy parted  he could see through. Fliers swam in a clear, deep sky.
     </p>
     <p>
      Wyn let go.
     </p>
