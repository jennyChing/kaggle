           <p>
            <span style="font-size:28px;">
             <strong>
              The 11 Forces of Tarot
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            To understand Tarot you must understand the 11 forces. These are the 4 Elements and the 7 Planets.
           </p>
           <p>
            After you have an understanding of the basic meanings of the 4 Elements and 7 Planets,
           </p>
           <p>
            it is important to understand the meanings of the combinations of these 11 forces.
           </p>
           <p>
            This is how the art and science of Tarot was developed and discovered.
           </p>
           <p>
            When you choose any two of these 11 forces a unique set of energies is described.
           </p>
           <p>
            For most, but not all, of these interactions, a Tarot card is the result.
           </p>
           <p>
            For example:
           </p>
           <p>
            If SATURN is joined with WATER, the 3 of Cups is the result.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://sunmoontarot.com/images/sat.png" style="width: 100px; height: 100px;"/>
            <img alt="" src="http://sunmoontarot.com/images/water.png" style="height: 100px; width: 100px;"/>
            <img alt="" src="/home/nits/public_html/team5/sunmoontaroot/admin/ckfinder/userfiles/images/3ofcups(2).jpg" style="width: 75px; height: 139px;"/>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Why ? Because the suit of Cups = Water and the number 3 = Saturn.
           </p>
           <p>
            What does this mean ? It is the union of TIME (Saturn) and EMOTION (Water)
           </p>
           <p>
            It can sometimes describe how ones sense of time changes during emotional situations.
           </p>
           <p>
            Time seems to slow down or speed up at times depending on our emotional state.
           </p>
           <p>
            Saturn + Water describes the effect of TIME on the EMOTIONS and
           </p>
           <p>
            The effect of the EMOTIONS on TIME.
           </p>
           <p>
            The people dancing in this Tarot card do not care what time it is. They may
           </p>
           <p>
            want this moment of happiness to last forever.
           </p>
           <p>
            If you think about these root energies at the basis of any Tarot card, the true meaning of the card
           </p>
           <p>
            is revealed no matter which Tarot deck you use or which Tarot spread you use.
           </p>
           <p>
            This is true for ANY Tarot deck regardless of what the artwork looks like.
           </p>
           <p>
            The root forces behind the 3 of Cups are always Saturn and Water.
           </p>
           <p>
            These are the forces that should be emphasized in any depiction of the 3 of Cups.
           </p>
           <p>
            If a Tarot artist is unaware of these underlying forces,
           </p>
           <p>
            how can he possibly understand or create a TRUE Tarot card ?
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Another example of combining the forces is :
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            AIR is joined with AIR you will get the following three Tarot cards.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://sunmoontarot.com/images/sky.png" style="height: 100px; width: 100px;"/>
            <img alt="" src="http://sunmoontarot.com/images/sky.png" style="height: 100px; width: 100px;"/>
            <img alt="" src="/home/nits/public_html/team5/sunmoontaroot/admin/ckfinder/userfiles/images/fool.jpg" style="width: 75px; height: 139px;"/>
            <img alt="" src="/home/nits/public_html/team5/sunmoontaroot/admin/ckfinder/userfiles/images/knightofswords.jpg" style="width: 75px; height: 139px;"/>
            <img alt="" src="/home/nits/public_html/team5/sunmoontaroot/admin/ckfinder/userfiles/images/aceofswords(1).jpg" style="width: 75px; height: 139px;"/>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            All three of These Tarot cards = AIR + AIR
           </p>
           <p>
            All three of these Tarot cards describe the same energy, AIR + AIR,
           </p>
           <p>
            but the direction of flow is different in each case.
           </p>
           <p>
            By understanding the subtleties in the various energy combinations,
           </p>
           <p>
            ANY Tarot card from ANY Tarot deck can be analyzed properly.
           </p>
           <p>
            So, in summary, every Tarot card is the union of 2 of the 11 forces.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            If you understand what the individual meanings of the 11 forces are with their combinations,
           </p>
           <p>
            there is no limit to your ability to decode the message of Tarot.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            What about the combination of MARS + SATURN or SUN + MOON ?
           </p>
           <p>
            Well, there are no Tarot cards that have 2 different planets expressed.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            What does this mean ?
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            There are 4 different types of Tarot cards. They are :
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Pure Planet Tarot cards. Both Forces are the same planet. Examples =  SUN + SUN or JUPITER + JUPITER There are 7 of these
           </p>
           <p>
            Pure Element Tarot cards Both forces are the same element. Examples =  EARTH + EARTH or FIRE + FIRE There are 10 of these
           </p>
           <p>
            Zodiac cards (1 Planet + 1 Element)  Examples = JUPITER + FIRE or SUN + EARTH There are 40 of these
           </p>
           <p>
            Two Element cards 2 different elements Examples =  Water + FIRE or AIR + FIRE There are 21 of these
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            For a Total of 78 Cards.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
