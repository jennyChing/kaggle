         <a href="http://www.savingcranes.org/whats-new/2015/02/5307/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to ">
          Suspected Shooting of a Whooping Crane in Aransas Bay, Texas
         </a>
        </h1>
        <span class="post-meta">
         <span class="post-date">
          February 3, 2015
         </span>
        </span>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <div id="fb-root">
        </div>
        <script src="http://connect.facebook.net/en_US/all.js#appId=206980846023709&amp;xfbml=1">

        <a href="http://www.savingcranes.org/whats-new/feed/" target="_blank">

        <table border="0" cellpadding="10">
         <tbody>
          <tr>
           <td valign="top" width="580">
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             <img alt="" class=" aligncenter" src="http://www.savingcranes.org/images/stories/site_images/whats_new/whooping_cranes_texas_smith600.jpg" width="400"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              The U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service and Texas Parks and Wildlife Department have announced the recentdeath of an Endangered Whooping Crane in Texas. With just over 300 birds in the western population, every bird is critical for the survival and health of the species. Photo by Dave &amp; Liz Smith
             </em>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             Media Contact:
             <a href="mailto:media@savingcranes.org" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/mailto/media@savingcranes.org');" target="_blank">
              media@savingcranes.org
             </a>
             , 608-356-9462
            </p>
            <p>
             Only two weeks after the announcement of a tragic Whooping Crane shooting in Louisiana this fall, the U.S. Fish &amp; Wildlife Service and Texas Parks &amp; Wildlife Department (TPWD) have announced the recent death of a Whooping Crane in Texas, also likely due to a shooting. The crane was found on January 4, 2015 along the central Texas coast near Sand Lake in Aransas Bay. ICF is contributing to a $27,500 reward pledged for information leading to a conviction for this crime.
             <a href="http://www.fws.gov/southwest/docs/WhoopingCraneReward_NR_020315.pdf" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.fws.gov/southwest/docs/WhoopingCraneReward_NR_020315.pdf');" target="_blank">
              Click here
             </a>
             to read the USFWS press release.
            </p>
            <p>
             The killing ofa Whooping Crane in Texas is unforgivable, said Dr. Elizabeth Smith, ICF Texas Program Director. As this population struggles to survive in the face of climate change, coastal development, fresh-water shortages, and other threats, we must protect these iconic birds from senseless and preventable acts of vandalism.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the face of such tragedy, ICFs collaborative work with partners in Texas includes increasing public awareness of this rarest crane in the world. ICFhas partnered with the TPWD, San Antonio Bay Partnership, and others to install educational signage at 18 public access areas and boat ramps within the Whooping Cranes wintering area in the Aransas and San Antonio Bays (below, click on the image to enlarge).
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.savingcranes.org/images/stories/site_images/whats_new/texas_whooping_crane_sign800.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/downloads/images/stories/site_images/whats_new/texas_whooping_crane_sign800.jpg');" target="_blank">

             </a>
             ICF staff are currently conducting an inventory to identifysigns for replacement, as well as locating potential new sites for signage within the Lavaca-Matagorda Bays to the northeast. Each winter, more Whooping Cranes are documented in this new area and are staying for longer periods an indication of the populations expansion along the Texas coast. Proactive awareness activities such as these are linked toICFs continuing work to identify potential newcrane habitats under future sea-level rise and changing climate conditions.
