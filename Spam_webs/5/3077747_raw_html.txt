          <p>
           <img alt="Valentines Day" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11829" height="400" src="http://www.24saltlake.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Valentines-Day.png" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Believe it or not, Valentines Days roots date back to an ancient Roman Empire and the holiday was not created by a greeting card company as a marketing ploy.
          </p>
          <p>
           The name Valentines Day comes from the priest Valentine who was put to death on Feb. 14, 270 AD because he secretly married couples against the will of Emperor Claudius II. Claudius had a tough time finding soldiers for his army and thought that by banning marriages and engagements in Rome he would gain a stronger army.
          </p>
          <p>
           When Valentine was in jail, he wrote a love letter to his beloved and signed it From your Valentine. The expression stuck and is still relevant today. Valentine was named a saint after his death and around 498 AD, Feb. 14 was declared St. Valentines Day to honor the martyr.
          </p>
          <p>
           Valentines Day traditions vary across the country and world. Here are a few of the different ways people recognize the holiday:
          </p>
          <h2>
           Valentines Day in Mexico
          </h2>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11831" style="width: 610px">
           <img alt="Puerto Vallarta Mexico" class="size-full wp-image-11831" height="398" src="http://www.24saltlake.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Puerto-Vallarta-Mexico.jpg" width="600"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Credit:
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/mcbarnicle/" target="_blank">
             SMcGarnigle
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Valentines Day is very popular in Mexico. Mexicans celebrate the day to acknowledge the feelings they have for loved ones every day. Flowers are a huge part ofValentines Day in Mexico. Floral arrangements are sent to friends, family members and lovers and flowers are used as elaborate decorations. Traditional Mexican balls and dances are also a popular way to celebrate this day.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Valentines Day in Colombia
          </h2>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11832" style="width: 610px">
           <img alt="Colombia" class="size-full wp-image-11832" height="400" src="http://www.24saltlake.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Colombia.jpg" width="600"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Credit:
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/lehnin78/" target="_blank">
             Andreas Lehner 2013
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Valentines Day in Colombia is celebrated on the third Friday and Saturday in September and is known as Love and Friendship Day. The tradition of Amigo secreto (secret friend) is where friends give anonymous gifts to friends and is very popular among kids. These gifts are given wrapped and mention a clue about who gave the gift. Throughout the entire month of September, snack baskets, chocolate and flowers are given to aunts, uncles, friends and anyone you love.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Valentines Day in Italy
          </h2>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11833" style="width: 610px">
           <img alt="Gondola Venice" class="size-full wp-image-11833" height="400" src="http://www.24saltlake.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Gondola-Venice.jpg" width="600"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Credit:
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pyride/" target="_blank">
             Orchids love rainwater
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Valentines Day in Italy is exclusively for couples and it is an extremely romantic holiday in Italy. Italians will give each other love notes, flowers, perfumes and diamonds as an expression of their love.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Valentines Day in India
          </h2>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11834" style="width: 610px">
           <img alt="Camels" class="size-full wp-image-11834" height="400" src="http://www.24saltlake.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Camels.jpg" width="600"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Credit:
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/krayker/" target="_blank">
             wildxplorer
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Valentines Day in India is popular among young people. Teddy bears, personalized photo collages and gift baskets are given as a token of love from boys to girls. Gold ornaments, which are created with elaborate designs, are given to women as a symbol of love. Games and competitions are arranged around shopping malls to engage the community in this holiday.
          </p>
