          <h2>
           Location of Angthong Marine National Park
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Angthong Marine National Park (sometimes also spelled separately as Ang Thong, which means
            <em>
             golden bowl
            </em>
            )
           </strong>
           is a string of 42 islands in the Gulf of Thailand, located on the Northwest of Koh Samui and West of Koh Phangan and Koh Tao islands. It is a protected nature area of around 250 square kilometres which is home for at least 54 species of birds, 16 species of mammals, 14 species of reptiles and 5 species of amphibians. It is a place for nature lovers.
           <br/>
           All the islands are of different size and shape and most of them are made of limestone and covered with the tropical rainforest.
           <strong>
            Most of the islands are inhabited and completely undeveloped. Just the island Koh Paluay is the place of Sea Gypsies who dedicate their life to fishing.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/42-islands-of-Anghtong-Marine-National-Park-in-Thailand-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="42 islands of Anghtong Marine National Park in Thailand" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3935" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/42-islands-of-Anghtong-Marine-National-Park-in-Thailand-copia.jpg" title="42 islands of Anghtong Marine National Park in Thailand" width="533"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           How to Get to Angthong Marine National Park
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           There are day trips organized by travel agencies on all the three islands: Koh Samui, Koh Tao and Koh Phangan going to Angthong which is a unique experience and a must see if you have some free time in Samui archipelago.
          </p>
          <p>
           I spent 6 days on Koh Phangan island and have done already all
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/koh-phangan-things-to-see-and-do/" title="Koh Phangan - things to see and do">
            the things you can do on Koh Phangan
           </a>
           . A day trip to Angthong was also exactly what I personally did after I explored
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/koh-phangan-best-beaches/" title="Koh Phangan  best beaches">
            the best Koh Phangan beaches
           </a>
           and even now after 2 weeks, I still cannot forget about this charming place.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            There is an entrance fee of 200 baht
           </strong>
           as the national park is one of the best places around to dive and snorkel because of undamaged corals and weird sea snakes, turtles and some lovely fish species. Two metre giant black corals can be found in the area. The entrance fee is usually included in the packages that travel agencies offer so just do not worry about it.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/islands-of-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="islands of Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3942" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/islands-of-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="islands of Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           What to Do in Angthong Marine National Park
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The standard trip to Angthong National Park is one day trip that leaves from Thongsala pier early morning (if you are on Koh Phangan island). I arranged it with
           <a href="http://www.safariboat.info" target="_blank" title="Safari Boat">
            Safari Boat
           </a>
           travel agency who picked us all up and before taking us to the speedboat, we enjoyed a light breakfast in their office. Oh yes, watermelon and bananas!
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/on-a-safari-boat-going-to-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg">
            <img alt="on a safari boat going to Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3949" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/on-a-safari-boat-going-to-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg" title="on a safari boat going to Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Then it was around 1 hour on the speed boat to get to
           <strong>
            the northernmost tip of the national park where the snorkeling is done by the Koh Tay Plaow island or Koh Wao
           </strong>
           . The visibility is good here as these are the best spots to observe the underwater life in the Angthong Marine National Park. You can feed fish with banana or watermelon to attract all of them to one place. The pictures of the Angel fish, Parrot fish and Butterly fish are much better with all of them around you and not just one or two fish swimming by
           <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/boats-that-took-us-to-Angthong-National-Park-from-Thongsala-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="boats that took us to Angthong National Park from Thongsala" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3955" height="400" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/boats-that-took-us-to-Angthong-National-Park-from-Thongsala-copia.jpg" title="boats that took us to Angthong National Park from Thongsala" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/underwater-during-snorkeling-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park1.jpg">
            <img alt="underwater during snorkeling in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3957" height="400" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/underwater-during-snorkeling-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park1.jpg" title="underwater during snorkeling in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/snorkel-and-feeding-fish-with-a-banana-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg">
            <img alt="snorkel and feeding fish with a banana in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3956" height="400" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/snorkel-and-feeding-fish-with-a-banana-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg" title="snorkel and feeding fish with a banana in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            After the snorkeling, you will be taken to another island Koh Mae Ko (Mother island) where you have to climb up a lot of very weird uncomfortable steps.
           </strong>
           But then you will be rewarded with the best view of most of the 42 islands of Angthong. I could spend there the whole day just staring at the beauty all around! Absolutely marvelous is the view of the islands so close to each other. Frankly, the scenery is breath-taking!
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/heading-to-Koh-Mae-Ko-island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="heading to Koh Mae Ko island in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3941" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/heading-to-Koh-Mae-Ko-island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="heading to Koh Mae Ko island in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/climbing-the-steps-to-the-viewpoint-of-Koh-Mae-Ko-in-Angthong-National-Park-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="climbing the steps to the viewpoint of Koh Mae Ko in Angthong National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3939" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/climbing-the-steps-to-the-viewpoint-of-Koh-Mae-Ko-in-Angthong-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="climbing the steps to the viewpoint of Koh Mae Ko in Angthong National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Koh-Mae-Ko-island-beach-from-the-viewpoint-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="Koh Mae Ko island beach from the viewpoint in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3945" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Koh-Mae-Ko-island-beach-from-the-viewpoint-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="Koh Mae Ko island beach from the viewpoint in Angthong Marine National Park " width="533"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           And on the other side of the viewpoint, there is this inner lake of saltwater which has incredible green coloured water and it really seems so unreal.
           <strong>
            The lake is called Talay Nai (sometimes seen as Thale Nai)  Emerald Lake and is hidden inside the island
           </strong>
           , surrounded by the limestone cliffs and connected to the sea by an underground tunnel. The lake is visible only once you get to the viewpoint. So do not miss the chance to climb up to the viewpoint even if you are boiling hot and the water at the beach looks so appealing! The emerald lake is a natural wonder you honestly cant skip on your way. Just look at the pictures! Unfortunately, it is not allowed to swim in there. But from the lowest viewpoint you can get to see barracudas and some small fish swimming close by if you are lucky.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Talay-Nai-Emerald-lake-on-Koh-Mae-Ko-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg">
            <img alt="Talay Nai Emerald lake on Koh Mae Ko in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3951" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Talay-Nai-Emerald-lake-on-Koh-Mae-Ko-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg" title="Talay Nai Emerald lake on Koh Mae Ko in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            There are very few beaches where tourists can camp in Angthong National Park, one of them is at Koh Mae Ko island.
           </strong>
           Camp fire, BBQ and relaxing in a hammock on a deserted island must be the experience of the lifetime. This is for those, who would like to enjoy the natural beauty of the marine park for longer and in a different way.
           <br/>
           Still with the orgasmic feeling from the inner lake, the speedboat took us around the islands and we saw some islands that look like animals, hence their names; such as
           <strong>
            Monkey island
           </strong>
           in the shape of a monkey, or the
           <strong>
            Lion island
           </strong>
           which is unfortunately breaking down slowly as some cliffs fell down and it does not really look like a lion any more (they will have to rename it according to some other animal then, I guess.)
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Monkey-Island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg">
            <img alt="Monkey Island in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3962" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Monkey-Island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg" title="Monkey Island in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Monkey-island-from-the-close.jpg">
            <img alt="Monkey island from the close" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3961" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Monkey-island-from-the-close.jpg" title="Monkey island from the close" width="533"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           After holding your breath at all the beauty everywhere around you, enjoying the wind in your hair on the speedboat and sun on your skin,
           <strong>
            we got to the main island of the whole national park  Koh Wua Talap.
           </strong>
           A very tasty lunch was provided at the beach restaurant. You can play beach volleyball here. There are also a few bungalows and tourist information at this beach. And a couple of viewpoints, but take your shoes with you and a lot of water when climbing up.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Koh-Wua-Talap-island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="Koh Wua Talap island in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3948" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Koh-Wua-Talap-island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="Koh Wua Talap island in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/great-lunch-at-Koh-Wua-Talap-restaurant-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg">
            <img alt="great lunch at Koh Wua Talap restaurant in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3940" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/great-lunch-at-Koh-Wua-Talap-restaurant-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park.jpg" title="great lunch at Koh Wua Talap restaurant in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Koh-Wua-Talap-beach-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="Koh Wua Talap beach in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3946" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Koh-Wua-Talap-beach-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="Koh Wua Talap beach in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Koh-Wua-Talap-beach-volleyball-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="Koh Wua Talap beach volleyball in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3947" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Koh-Wua-Talap-beach-volleyball-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="Koh Wua Talap beach volleyball in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Just in front of Koh Wua Talap island, there is a small island Koh Pee.
           </strong>
           <strong>
            You can actually go around it kayaking
           </strong>
           and see some little caves and a lot of other islands around. It is the best place to do some kayaking and see caves, rainforest,white sandy beaches and dramatic rock formations.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/caves-of-Koh-Pee-island-in-Angthong-National-Park-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="caves of Koh Pee island in Angthong National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3938" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/caves-of-Koh-Pee-island-in-Angthong-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="caves of Koh Pee island in Angthong National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/kayaking-to-Koh-Pee-island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg">
             <img alt="kayaking to Koh Pee island in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3944" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/kayaking-to-Koh-Pee-island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="kayaking to Koh Pee island in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/kayaking-around-Koh-Pee-island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg">
             <img alt="kayaking around Koh Pee island in Angthong Marine National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3943" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/kayaking-around-Koh-Pee-island-in-Angthong-Marine-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="kayaking around Koh Pee island in Angthong Marine National Park" width="533"/>
            </a>
           </strong>
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/a-cave-of-Koh-Pee-island-seen-when-kayaking-in-Angthong-National-Park-copia.jpg">
            <img alt="a cave of Koh Pee island seen when kayaking in Angthong National Park" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3936" height="353" src="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/a-cave-of-Koh-Pee-island-seen-when-kayaking-in-Angthong-National-Park-copia.jpg" title="a cave of Koh Pee island seen when kayaking in Angthong National Park" width="533"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           But even if you dont stay camping in Angthong, it will still be a great adventure and I bet you will have the same amazing fulfilling feeling each time you will remember the day in the Angthong Marine National Park. The same nice feeling that you had when there or that I had during
           <a href="http://www.crazysexyfuntraveler.com/ko-phangan-holidays-that-never-end/">
            the holidays on Koh Phangan that never end
           </a>
           . Sometimes one day is enough to fall in love with a place. Which was exactly what happened to me.
          </p>
