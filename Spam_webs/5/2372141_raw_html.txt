       <div class="article-body">
        <div id="intro">
         <p>
          99 Tons. That is a outrageous amount of weapons. A minimum of 4 planeloads lost? Anyone who knows the Military, knows that the one thing they do in a anal fashion is flight records. The who what where and when a plane takes off and lands is documented up the wahzoo. Knowing this to be true, where did 99 Tons of weapons scheduled to land in Baghdad really land ? No one seems to know.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           When the U.S. government needed to fly four planeloads of seized weapons from an American base in Bosnia to Iraqi security forces in Baghdad in August 2004, they used a Moldovan air cargo firm tied to Bout's aviation empire.
           <strong>
            The problem is that the planes apparently never arrived. When Amnesty International investigators tried two years later to trace the shipment of more than 99 tons of AK-47s and other weapons, U.S. officials admitted they had no record of the flights landing in Baghdad.
           </strong>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p class="divider-doodle">
        </p>
        <div class="article-body" id="body">
         <p>
          While it appears some would just as soon this was chalked up to more incompetence, it could be more sinister. Surprised ? No, I wasn't either. Once again our President got cozy with questionable people. His constant " the ends justify the means" way of doing things is one of the major reasons our country is in the shape it is. This time we got in bed with a gent our own country has put on the Terrorist watch list. Who is this Arms Dealer?
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Consider the case of one particular bad guy, Viktor Bout -- a stout, canny Russian air transporter who also happens to be the world's most notorious arms dealer...
          </p>
          <p>
           That's because Bout is about as bad as bad guys get. For more than a decade before he landed on U.S. payrolls, Bout's air cargo operations delivered tons of contraband weapons -- ranging from rifles to helicopter gunships -- to some of the world's most dangerous misfits.
          </p>
          <p>
           He stoked wars across Africa, supplying Charles Taylor, the deposed Liberian president now on trial for war crimes.
           <strong>
            He ferried $50 million in guns and other cargo, and he even sold air freighters to the Taliban, whose mullahs shared their lethal inventories with Al Qaeda's terrorists in Afghanistan.
           </strong>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          The resume is much longer, with many examples of why we should of never been doing business with this Putz, yet we did. Fair Use limits me from listing all of them. What happen to these Weapons is not known yet, but I'd bet they are being used against our own troops in Iraq and Afghanistan.
         </p>
         <p>
          Worse in my book is the fact that we knew we were in bed with this crook, yet made him a important "valued U.S. contractor". The LATimes reports we knew this in the 90s and still hired him. Also reported is that in 2004 when some were trying stop our use of Bout, stopping him was sidetracked until he finished few choice tasks. So Bouts went from being a candidate for Rendition to being "second tier contractor" and then once we were done with him did we arrest him ? Did we put him out of business at least ? Hell no.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Last summer, a jumbo Il-76 flying the Khazakh flag swooped down to a landing in Mogadishu to unload arms for radical Islamic leaders who briefly seized control of Somalia. It was one of Bout's planes, concluded U.S. military intelligence officials
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.latimes.com/news/opinion/la-oe-braun13aug13,0,1196900.story?coll=la-opinion-center">
           http://www.latimes.com/...
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I will finish by pointing out this article is buried on the OpEd page of the LATimes and is not likely to be big news unless we bring attention to it. Pass this on to anyone you know that might have the ear of our leaders or the Press that is still willing to do the right thing.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           P.S. Please don't let this be buried by the rollout of Roves departure.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Update : Over at TPMuckraker is another story of guns being run in the same areas by Italians and Maliki. Could they be related ? We don't know that yet, there sure seem to be a lot of guns flying around and not to the USArmy.
