        <p>
         <strong>
          Instructions
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         1. Whether you have your own table saw or are having the hardware store cut your wood and PVC, use the diagram below to measure and cut all the pieces you need for your garden cart. Even though I have my own saws, I often have Home Depot cut large pieces of wood so theyll fit in my car. It also keeps my workshop sawdust-free!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-2" rel="attachment wp-att-172903">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172903" height="678" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-2.jpg" title="garden cart-2" width="500"/>
         </a>
         2. Well upholster the cart in fabric and cover again in plastic so its extra cleanable and durable. Use the diagram below to draw and cut the fabric and plastic pieces needed for the cart. Use a pen to draw on the plastic.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-3" rel="attachment wp-att-172904">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172904" height="511" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-3.jpg" title="garden cart-3" width="500"/>
         </a>
         3. By using a furniture dolly, we start with a strong frame and casters ready for action. Remove the carpet and staples from the furniture dolly with a staple remover and pair of pliers.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-7" rel="attachment wp-att-172905">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172905" height="712" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-7.jpg" title="garden cart-7" width="500"/>
         </a>
         4. Apply glue on the bottom bars of the furniture dolly and clamp the bottom piece of wood in place. Drill pilot holes through the bottom piece and bottom bars of the furniture dolly in the middle and corners of the two long sides.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-17" rel="attachment wp-att-172906">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172906" height="350" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-17.jpg" title="garden cart-17" width="500"/>
         </a>
         5. Screw the bottom piece to the bottom bars of the furniture dolly through the pilot holes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-22" rel="attachment wp-att-172907">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172907" height="750" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-22.jpg" title="garden cart-22" width="500"/>
         </a>
         6. Now well attach the short sides of the cart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-23" rel="attachment wp-att-172908">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172908" height="338" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-23.jpg" title="garden cart-23" width="500"/>
         </a>
         7. Place the cart on its side and repeat steps 4 and 5 to attach the short sides to the base of the cart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-32" rel="attachment wp-att-172909">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172909" height="737" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-32.jpg" title="garden cart-32" width="500"/>
         </a>
         8. Turn the cart upside down and repeat steps 4 and 5 to attach the long sides to the base of the cart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-39" rel="attachment wp-att-172910">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172910" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-39.jpg" title="garden cart-39" width="500"/>
         </a>
         9. At the corners, screw the long sides into the short sides where they touch.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-42" rel="attachment wp-att-172911">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172911" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-42.jpg" title="garden cart-42" width="500"/>
         </a>
         10. Measure and mark a line 16 3/4 from the left side on the front and back sides of the cart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-60" rel="attachment wp-att-172912">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172912" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-60.jpg" title="garden cart-60" width="500"/>
         </a>
         11. Insert the divider and line up its right side with the lines marked in step 10.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-65" rel="attachment wp-att-172913">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172913" height="750" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-65.jpg" title="garden cart-65" width="500"/>
         </a>
         12. Double check the measurements inside the cart to make sure the bottom of the divider is 15 1/4 from the inside left edge.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-72" rel="attachment wp-att-172914">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172914" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-72.jpg" title="garden cart-72" width="500"/>
         </a>
         13. Clamp the divider against the sides to hold it in place and mark a vertical line on the outside of the cart even with the center of the divider.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-74" rel="attachment wp-att-172915">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172915" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-74.jpg" title="garden cart-74" width="500"/>
         </a>
         14. Drill pilot holes and screw the divider to the sides of the cart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-75" rel="attachment wp-att-172916">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172916" height="712" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-75.jpg" title="garden cart-75" width="500"/>
         </a>
         15. With the 3/8 drill bit attached, drill several holes in the sides of the cart so air can circulate through the padding and fabric.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-82" rel="attachment wp-att-172917">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172917" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-82.jpg" title="garden cart-82" width="500"/>
         </a>
         16. For the fabric on the base of the cart, align the left side of one piece with the right side of the other (with good sides facing) and sew them together. Repeat this step to sew together the other two edges.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-87" rel="attachment wp-att-172918">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172918" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-87.jpg" title="garden cart-87" width="500"/>
         </a>
         17. Using the diagram from step 2, fold the fabric along the dotted line (with good sides facing) and sew the cut out notch together.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-89" rel="attachment wp-att-172919">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172919" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-89.jpg" title="garden cart-89" width="500"/>
         </a>
         18. Follow the instructions from Upholstery Basics:
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2012/02/upholstery-basics-boxed-ottoman.html" target="_blank">
          Boxed Ottoman
         </a>
         to sew the kneeling pad fabric together. Since Im covering this in plastic, I nixed the welt cord.
        </p>
        <p>
         19. Repeat steps 16-18 for the for the plastic.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-97" rel="attachment wp-att-172920">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172920" height="750" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-97.jpg" title="garden cart-97" width="500"/>
         </a>
         20. Staple Dacron to the top edge and sides of the cart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-101" rel="attachment wp-att-172921">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172921" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-101.jpg" title="garden cart-101" width="500"/>
         </a>
         21. Slip the sewn sides over the base of the cart and pull it into place.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-104" rel="attachment wp-att-172922">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172922" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-104.jpg" title="garden cart-104" width="500"/>
         </a>
         22. With the corners snug and in place, smooth the fabric around the top edge and staple the fabric to the inside of the cart. Make cuts to release the fabric around the divider (steps 4 and 5,
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2011/09/upholstery-basics-constructing-coil-springs-part-2.html" target="_blank">
          Upholstery Basics: Constructing Coil Seats  Part 2
         </a>
         ).
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-133" rel="attachment wp-att-172924">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172924" height="750" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-133.jpg" title="garden cart-133" width="500"/>
         </a>
         23. Smooth and staple the fabric to the bottom of the cart.
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-110" rel="attachment wp-att-172923">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="750" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-110.jpg" title="garden cart-110" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         24. Refer back to
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2012/02/upholstery-basics-boxed-ottoman.html" target="_blank">
          Upholstery Basics: Boxed Ottoman
         </a>
         to pad and attach the fabric to the kneeling pad. I used 2 thick outdoor foam for mine.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-138" rel="attachment wp-att-172925">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172925" height="750" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-138.jpg" title="garden cart-138" width="500"/>
         </a>
         25. Repeat steps 16-24 to cover the fabric in plastic.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-153" rel="attachment wp-att-172926">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172926" height="750" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-153.jpg" title="garden cart-153" width="500"/>
         </a>
         26. Cut off excess fabric close to the staple line and staple cardboard tack strip on top of the cut edges. On the bottom of the kneeling pad, screw in the corner blocks on all four corners 1 1/4 from the edges.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-161" rel="attachment wp-att-172927">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172927" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-161.jpg" title="garden cart-161" width="500"/>
         </a>
         27. Staple both ends of the shorter rope to one side of the bottom of the kneeling pad. Keep the stapled ends 1 1/4 inside the edge of the kneeling pad so the rope can lay inside the cart when the kneeling pad is in place.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-169" rel="attachment wp-att-172928">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172928" height="681" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-169.jpg" title="garden cart-169" width="500"/>
         </a>
         28. Place a piece of PVC in the back right and front right corners of the cart and make a mark on the wood just inside the PVC. Staple the ends of the longer rope to these marks.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-174" rel="attachment wp-att-172929">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172929" height="333" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-174.jpg" title="garden cart-174" width="500"/>
         </a>
         29. Insert the cut PVC tubes into the right side of the cart and place your kneeling pad over the cubby on the left side.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-181" rel="attachment wp-att-172930">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-172930" height="750" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/garden-cart-181.jpg" title="garden cart-181" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <strong>
          Garden Cart Tips
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          To keep your cart fresh for many seasons to come, use outdoor or marine grade fabric and plastic.
         </li>
         <li>
          Use nylon thread for sewing.
         </li>
         <li>
          Consider marine foam for better moisture resilience.
         </li>
         <li>
          Store your cart in a covered area.
         </li>
         <li>
          Wipe clean with a wet rag.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/2013/04/upholstery-basics-garden-cart.html/garden-cart-197" rel="attachment wp-att-172931">
