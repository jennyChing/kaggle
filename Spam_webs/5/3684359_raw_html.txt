       <div class="headline_area">
        <h2 class="entry-title">
         Gluten Free Vegan Garlic Herb Cheese Spread Recipe
        </h2>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard fn">
          Carol Kicinski
         </span>
         on
         <abbr class="published" title="2013-06-05">
          June 5, 2013
         </abbr>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <!-- AddThis Button BEGIN -->
        <div class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style " style="float:left;">
         <a class="addthis_button_pinterest_pinit">
         </a>
        </div>
        <script src="//s7.addthis.com/js/300/addthis_widget.js#pubid=xa-51b837450a3b6ce5" type="text/javascript">
        </script>
        <span style="vertical-align: top">
         <g:plusone annotation="none" href="https://simplygluten-free.com/blog/2013/06/gluten-free-vegan-garlic-herb-cheese-spread-recipe.html">
         </g:plusone>
        </span>
        <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2F/simplygluten-free.com/blog/2013/06/gluten-free-vegan-garlic-herb-cheese-spread-recipe.html&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=false&amp;width=450&amp;action=like&amp;colorscheme=light&amp;height=35" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:450px; height:35px;">

        <img alt="Post image for Gluten Free Vegan Garlic Herb Cheese Spread Recipe" class="post_image aligncenter" height="402" src="https://simplygluten-free.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/gluten-free-vegan-garlic-and-herb-cheese.jpg" width="600"/>
        <br/>
        Lately I have been consumed with the idea of making my own cheese. It started with homemade ricotta (easy peasy and for my dairy eating readers I will post that recipe soon) and then I decided to try my hand at vegan nut cheese.
        <p>
         What I was after was a garlicky, herby, spreadable cheese that would be good for entertaining not just vegans but anyone.
        </p>
        <p>
         I didnt invent this method by any means. I got the idea from my friend
         <a href="http://www.lexieskitchen.com/lexies_kitchen/2012/4/10/raw-cultured-cashew-cheese.html" target="_blank">
          Lexie
         </a>
         who got it from someone who got it from someone else who got it from someone else And so it goes with recipes; people take them and make them their own.
        </p>
        <p>
         Making nut cheese is ridiculously easy. It is a little time consuming as you have to wait while your nuts soak and then wait for your mixture to culture and then finally wait for all the flavors to meld in the fridge. But it is not like you are doing anything when waiting. Unless you are me who has to peek at it every few minutes in case I miss some strange and miraculous occurrence. For the record you can skip the peeking, absolutely nothing appears to be happening. Notice I say appears  stuff happens you just cant see it.
        </p>
        <p>
         The basic recipe for cultured cashew cheese is just raw cashews, water, and probiotics. Some recipes call for rejuvelac which is a fermented liquid that typically contains gluten in it. I just used water  simple!
        </p>
        <p>
         If you can buy cashew pieces instead of whole, do it. They are typically cheaper and you are just going to grind them up anyway. You can soak your cashews anywhere from 4 to 12 hours. I (being impatient) opted for 4 hours. No idea if this makes any difference in the overall product.
        </p>
        <p>
         You need probiotic capsules. Make sure they are allergen free and shoot for having something around 10  15 billion organisms total.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have a high speed blender like a Vitamix, great. If not, dont worry you can still make this with a regular blender it will just take a little more work to get your mixture really smooth. Just keep scraping and blending, you will get there.
        </p>
        <p>
         When blending the cashews try to use as little water as possible. Start with a little and just add a bit each time until the mixture is really smooth. This is really the only step that requires just a touch of patience  well except for the waiting part.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can culture your cheese (dont get scared, all that means is just let it sit) for 14  16 hours or longer if you want a riper cheese. I was trying to combat the natural sweetness of cashews so I actually let my cheese ripen for 24 hours. At least thats the story I will stick with, actually I just sort of forgot about it for a little bit.
        </p>
        <p>
         At this point you can form into rounds and let harden in the fridge as it. As I said I was looking for an herby, garlicky spread so I doctored it up with lemon juice, a touch of vinegar, some nutritional yeast, salt, pepper, garlic, and herbs. I used a combination of fresh thyme, chives, oregano, and parsley but you could use the herbs you prefer and if you dont have fresh, dried will work well too.
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh, and you need some cheese cloth. Duh!
        </p>
        <p>

        <h2>
         Gluten Free Vegan Garlic Herb Cheese Spread Recipe
        </h2>
        <h3>
         Ingredients
        </h3>
        <p>
         3 cups raw cashews
         <br/>
         3 probiotic capsules
         <br/>
          cup water (more or less)
         <br/>
         2 teaspoons lemon juice
         <br/>
          teaspoon white wine vinegar
         <br/>
         1 tablespoons fresh herbs, minced
         <br/>
         2 large cloves garlic (or more if you like), grated or finely minced
         <br/>
         2 green onions, finely minced
         <br/>
         1 teaspoons Kosher or sea salt
         <br/>
          teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
         <br/>
         2 teaspoons nutritional yeast
         <br/>
         More minced herbs for coating if desired
        </p>
        <h3>
         Directions
        </h3>
        <p>
         Soak the cashews in clean, cool water for 4  12 hours. Drain well. Put in blender with the powder from the probiotics (discard the casings) and a little water. Blend until smooth, adding water a little at a time to keep things moving and blending. Use only as much water as is needed for a smooth texture.
        </p>
        <p>
         Line a colander with a double layer of cheesecloth and place over a bowl. Scrape the cheese mixture into the cheesecloth, cover with cheesecloth and then place a towel over the whole thing and let sit in a warm spot in your kitchen for 12  24 hours. The longer it sits the more tart it becomes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Put the cheese in a mixing bowl, stir in the rest of ingredients except for the herbs for coating. Transfer mixture to an airtight container and let firm up in the fridge for a few hours.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want you can mold your cheese. Line 2 ramekins (8 ounces each) with cheesecloth or plastic wrap, fill with the cheese, cover and refrigerate for several hours. Unmold the cheese are press minced herbs into the sides and on top. It will be on the soft side, similar to Boursin Cheese.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cheese keeps for 3  4 days, in airtight container, refrigerated.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Servings:
        </h3>
        <p>
         A gluten free recipe that makes about 2 cups of vegan cheese spread.
        </p>
