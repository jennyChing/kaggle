          <h1 class="post-title single">
           <a href="http://gather.com/standard-poors-strips-united-states-of-prestigious-spot/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Standard &amp; Poors Strips United States of Prestigious Spot">
            Standard &amp; Poors Strips United States of Prestigious Spot
           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="meta">
           <span class="meta-cats">
            Filed in
            <a href="http://gather.com/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag">
             Uncategorized
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="meta-author">
            by
            <a href="http://gather.com/author/fredyt123/" rel="author" title="Posts by Fred Thomas III">
             Fred Thomas III
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="meta-date">
            on August 6, 2011
           </span>
           <span class="meta-comments">
            
            <a href="http://gather.com/standard-poors-strips-united-states-of-prestigious-spot/#comments" rel="bookmark" title="Comments for Standard &amp; Poors Strips United States of Prestigious Spot">
             0 Comments
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
          <p>
           Tonight, Standard &amp; Poors, the highly acclaimed credit agency, did what some never thought it would do. For the first time in the history of the United States of America, its credit rating has been reduced to AA+ status. Previously it has always held the cherished gold-plated AAA rating.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.standardandpoors.com/servlet/BlobServer?blobheadername3=MDT-Type&amp;blobcol=urldata&amp;blobtable=MungoBlobs&amp;blobheadervalue2=inline%3B+filename%3DUS_Downgraded_AA%2B.pdf&amp;blobheadername2=Content-Disposition&amp;blobheadervalue1=application%2Fpdf&amp;blobkey=id&amp;blobheadername1=content-type&amp;blobwhere=1243942957443&amp;blobheadervalue3=UTF-8" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Standard &amp; Poors
           </a>
           justified its downgrade by stating the recently approved debt ceiling plan does not go far enough to correct the fiscal woes of the U.S. Part of the negotiations of the plan were
           <em>
            budget items
           </em>
           , including the hotly debated gesture to cut spending without implementing policies to increase revenues. The announcement was received via e-mail from subscribers of
           <a href="http://www.marketwatch.com/story/us-loses-triple-a-rating-from-sp-2011-08-05?siteid=bnbh" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            MarketWatch
           </a>
           and other financial newswire services.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Economist Warned Politicians
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Just several weeks ago, Federal Reserve Chairman
           <strong>
            Ben Bernanke
           </strong>
           <em>
            warned
           </em>
           politicians to not take lightly the need to increase the debt ceiling. He stressed to lawmakers they should
           <em>
            concentrate
           </em>
           on initiating economic policies which would improve market confidence. His sentiments were echoed by Treasury Secretary
           <strong>
            Tim Geithner
           </strong>
           , as he pleaded with any politician who would listen that partisanship aside, it was crucial for the United States to do whatever is necessary to jumpstart the economy. In 2007, before President Obama took office, the nation was rocked with an economic crisis that is still lingering today.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center">
           <a class="label" href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/photos-and-video/photogallery/august-2011-photo-day" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Treasury Secretary Tim Geithner explaining impact of S &amp; P downgrade">
            <img alt="Treasury Secretary Tim Geithner with President Obama" height="265" src="http://media-files.gather.com/images/d727/d57/d747/d224/d96/f3/full.jpg" style="margin-bottom: 5px;margin-left: auto;margin-right: auto" width="472"/>
           </a>
           Treasury Secretary Tim Geithner discussing impact with President Obama
           <a class="label" href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/photos-and-video/photogallery/august-2011-photo-day" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Treasury Secretary Tim Geithner explaining impact of S &amp; P downgrade">
            <br/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Instead their viewpoints were micro-managed or otherwise marginalized.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Calling Bluff Instead of Heeding Advice
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           For the past several weeks, the nation saw gamesmanship from both political parties. However, since President Obama is a Democrat, the Republicans, led by House Majority Leader John Boehner and Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell, apparently were calling Bernankes and Geithners
           <strong>
            bluff
           </strong>
           , as they refused to heed their warnings.
          </p>
          <p>
           The debt ceiling was eventually approved before the drop-dead deadline of midnight August 2; however, by then it was like a heavy weight brawl, as both sides were exhausted by all the antics.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Debt Ceiling is Traditionally Very Routine
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Surprisingly, this was the first time politicians have used the debt ceiling as political fodder. It is a very basic part of administrative finances, yet the Republicans are eying removing President Obama in the upcoming 2012 elections and thought it made sense to cloud the issue, versus putting every ounce of energy into improving the economy.
          </p>
          <p>
           Standard &amp; Poors announcement came after the markets closed. Over the weekend and until they open Monday morning, there will be lots of finger-pointing and the usual punditry.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.standardandpoors.com/about-sp/main/en/us" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Standard &amp; Poors
           </a>
           is known to investors worldwide as a leader of financial-market intelligence. Their ratings are crucial in analyzing institutional creditworthiness. Fitch and Moodys, the other two institutional credit rating services, have not made an announcement.
          </p>

             Pete Souza
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
