                <h1 class="entry-title">
                 3 Web Designing Essentials to Be Tucked in
                </h1>
                <div class="entry-meta">
                 <time class="entry-date updated" datetime="2014-03-05T18:21:26+00:00">

               <div class="entry-content">
                <div align="center" style="padding-left:5px; padding-right:5px; padding-bottom:5px; padding-top:5px; margin-left:auto; margin-right:auto; ">
                 <script type="text/javascript">
                  &lt;!--

                <p>
                 <span style="color: #000000;">
                  <strong>
                   <em>
                    <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
                     Guest Post By Maria Mincey
                    </span>
                   </em>
                  </strong>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  Internet is unquestionably a place that can open you to an altogether new market, but one where you find an increasing number of relevant customers. So, if you decide to start a website of your own, a site that represents your business online, you have already taken that first step towards empowered marketing. But the buck doesnt stop there.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  To be sure that your website does attract the sort of results you have been expecting, there are number of approaches to be taken. These approaches are what will determine if your website can gain leverage over the growing competition and make a separate identity for itself:
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="color: #000000; font-size: large;">
                  <strong>
                   <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
                    Invest as Much Time and Money as You Can On the Layout Design
                   </span>
                  </strong>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  <div class="tb-lightbox-shortcode aligncenter" style="max-width: 400px">
                   <a class="themeblvd-lightbox mfp-image tb-thumb-link image thumbnail" href="http://www.onemillionwaystomakemoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/ID-100122960.jpg" title="ID-100122960">
                    <img alt="ID-100122960" src="http://www.onemillionwaystomakemoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/ID-100122960.jpg" width="400"/>
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <em>
                  Featured Image Credits: Freedigitalphotos.net, Stuart Mile
                 </em>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  Granted, the current crop of CMSs like WordPress, Drupal and Joomla facilitate setting up websites free of cost. And they have a number of free templates and themes for you to decide the tone and style of your website.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  Having said that, you may still find yourself struggling for what you precisely want. When it comes to having a particular website design in mind and you cant see that materializing using the free templates, you have to go for custom website development. And that would require some investment to be made. Custom website development would give you the freedom of including everything on your website that you had ever wanted.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  The layout can be designed in a way you deem right, without relying on the constraints that free templates come with. If you wish to have just an image on the landing page with minimal text, you can use a WordPress theme, but you would require a wed designing expert to bring that feature into effect.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="color: #000000; font-size: large;">
                  <strong>
                   <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
                    Create a Logo that Gels with Your Brand
                   </span>
                  </strong>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <div class="tb-lightbox-shortcode aligncenter" style="max-width: 400px">
                 <a class="themeblvd-lightbox mfp-image tb-thumb-link image thumbnail" href="http://www.onemillionwaystomakemoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/ID-100105873.jpg" title="ID-100105873">
                  <img alt="ID-100105873" src="http://www.onemillionwaystomakemoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/ID-100105873.jpg" width="400"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
                <p>
                 <em>
                  <a href="http://www.freedigitalphotos.net/images/Other_Business_Conce_g200-Brand_Under_Magnifying_Glass_p105873.html" target="_blank">
                   Image Credits: Freedigitalphotos.net,David Castillo Dominici
                  </a>
                 </em>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  Logo is your first point of contact with customers and Internet users. If they feel it is just a spin off from some popular logo design, they wont have high regards for your brand either. So, be original and create a logo sends out the message in the right tone.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  Keep the colors on the logo subtle and clutter-free. And when it comes to keeping it clean and easy on the eyes, you also need to steer clear of using those fancy fonts that the readers cant make heads and tails out of. And please, do not use clip art on your logo. That would be too juvenile.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="color: #000000; font-size: large;">
                  <strong>
                   <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
                    Make Navigation Easy
                   </span>
                  </strong>
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  One thing that has been overstated is the fact that Internet users do not stick around on a site if they find that navigating through it is a pain. with thin patience, they would instead jump to some other URL and that would only increase the bounce rate on your site, and decrease the avg. time. That is not the kind statistic you would want.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  So, make sure that users are able to find the pages they are looking to read easily.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: medium; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #000000;">
                  It might sound too mainstream but your website is the online face of your brand. Make it presentable enough.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
