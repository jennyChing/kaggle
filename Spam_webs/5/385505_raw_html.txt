        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Beet, Cranberry and Ginger Smoothie
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-01-11T08:56:31+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 11, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://veggiedesserts.co.uk/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Kate Hackworthy
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://veggiedesserts.co.uk/beet-cranberry-and-ginger-smoothie/#comments">
           35 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="16202087" data-link="http://veggiedesserts.co.uk/beet-cranberry-and-ginger-smoothie/" data-summary="" data-title="Beet, Cranberry and Ginger Smoothie">
        </div>
        <p>
         <img alt="Beet, Cranberry and Ginger Smoothie | Veggie Desserts" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1984" height="876" src="http://veggiedesserts.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/BeetCranberrySmoothie1-683x1024.jpg" width="584"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive been eating more vegetables for January. Ive never really been one for New Years resolutions, but this year I decided to take part in
         <a href="http://veggiedesserts.co.uk/jumpstart-2015-squashing-vegetables-and-feeling-healthier/" target="_blank" title="Jumpstart 2015  Squashing Vegetables and Feeling Healthier">
          Jumpstart15
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.veganuary.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.veganuary.com/', 'Veganuary']);" target="_blank" title="Veganuary">
          Veganuary
         </a>
         . Im so glad that I did. Ive been squashing vegetables, squishing vegetables, juicing them, pureeing them, chomping on them raw. I always know that eating more veg will make me feel better, but after hitting the cheeseboard with too much gusto over Christmas it has been great to go back to my beloved veggies. No calorie counting, and not dieting. Just eating vegetable smoothies in the mornings, soups for lunch and making sure its all vegan.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive been a vegetarian for years, but now Ive gone completely vegan for January. I really thought that it would restrict what I eat more than it has. Ive had my usual smoothies and soups and for dinner we have had Moroccan chickpea and squash stew, hearty bean and kale soups, adzuki bean and spinach chilli, wholemeal pasta with cauliflower alfredo sauce Ive swapped out the sneaky bites of cheese that I sometimes had for snacks and have a steady supply of veggies to dip into hummous. Treats? I have dark chocolate, dark chocolate covered rice cakes (I LOVE these) and there are plenty of vegan biscuits in the shops should I so desire. Almond Dream kindly sent me some samples
         <a href="http://instagram.com/p/w6KesGkQhg/?modal=true" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://instagram.com/p/w6KesGkQhg/?modal=true', 'vegan almond-milk ice creams']);" target="_blank" title="Veggie Desserts on Instagram">
          vegan almond-milk ice creams
         </a>
         that are delicious. I often have nut milks in the house, store-bought or homemade in the blender, but I havent found one that I like in my coffee so Ive started to have it black. Ive been trying to start my days with hot water, lemon and ginger and also to remember to drink more water. So far, going vegan has been quite easy. Wait, there was that blip in a cafe the other day when I forgot and had a cappuccino that wasnt vegan. But I forgive myself.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-1983">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         My beloved
         <a href="http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=74&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1574" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=74&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1574', 'Froothie Optimum']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Froothie Optimum 9400">
          Froothie Optimum
         </a>
         high-speed blender has been making it so easy to stick to my healthy eating for January (yes, I know its only the 11th!). Smoothies in seconds. Perfect before work. Plus it whizzes vegetables into soups and even heats them up.
        </p>
        <p>
         I had a load of cranberries left over from Christmas that I didnt quite get around to making cranberry sauce out of. Instead of letting them go to waste, I dropped them into my morning smoothie. The tartness contrasts well with the smooth sweetness of the beet and the warmth from the ginger. This is a zesty, tangy and earthy smoothie that the whole family really loved. Its beautifully vibrant and quite filling.
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
