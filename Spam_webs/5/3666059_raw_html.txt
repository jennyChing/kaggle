         <p>
          <em>
           UNIQLO offers innovative clothing for men, women, and kids. Check out our i am OTHER collection designed by Pharrell Williams and our UT t-shirts featuring your favorite artists! Discover more at
           <a href="http://uniqlo.com/" shape="rect" target="_blank">

         <p>
          By now you must have heard it millions of timesbeing an independent artist in this market is tougher than it has ever been. The money exists in fewer and fewer places and the budgets have fewer and fewer zeros. Luckily for artists there is a bright spot in live performancespecifically on college campuses. Colleges and universities nationwide provide a variety of opportunities, with many different types of facilities to suit artists from all genres and backgrounds. The coveted college circuit may be difficult to break into, but once you do youre well taken care of.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres a few reasons why bands love to play on your college campus:
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1. The Rooms
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Touring as an independent artist, you play a lot of strange places. Strange places that arent always intended to be live music venues. Basements? Living rooms? Cold storage sheds? Vintage store front? These are mainstay performance spaces of emerging artists.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now as fun as that all sounds, bands are trying our best to sound good. Thats kind of the whole point. But if youre constantly traveling from one non-acoustically-treated room to the next, with sounds bouncing around concrete rooms every night, its pretty tough to get the consistency you need to lock your sound in tight. Colleges have budgetsfor this. If youre a string quartet, theyve got a nice ballroom for you. If youre an a cappella group, they have a choir room. And if youre a punk band youll fit right into their student center or modern art space. As an artist and a professional, it feels good to be afforded a higher standard of tools and amenities.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-11.08.51-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-05-13 at 11.08.51 PM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-220951" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-11.08.51-PM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2. The Tour Stop Where Youll Actually Get Paid
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Most on-campus gigs are paid for with funds collected from student fees. Some facilities are run like actual clubs and need to make money to keep it going, but most campus activities dont make money. Instead, committees are allocated budgets by the university, and if they want to get the same amount or more money the following fiscal year, they have to show that the money was needed. Meaning theyve gotta spend it if they want to keep getting it. Its a vicious cycle, that fortunately works in the artists favor because it means larger guarantees for artists. Artists that might command $200 at an average coffee shop gig could make $3,000 at a good college show in the
          <em>
           exact
          </em>
          <em>
           same
          </em>
          <em>
           town
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-11.16.40-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-05-13 at 11.16.40 PM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-220952" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-11.16.40-PM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3. Centrally located amplification opportunities
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Clever and hardworking bands want (and need) to work as much as they possibly can, as efficiently as they possibly can. This means constantly engaging in activities that will grow their brand. On tour, you dont want to just load in and play one show, you want to book press, possibly film a music video on a your day off, book a recording session, or track a demo in the back of the van or bus while driving to the next city. On a college campus, this is extremely simple because nearly every college has a radio station, a student paper, and various student organizations that are constantly looking for new talent to showcase and create content around. Most importantly, artists dont have to wander far from the venue to participate.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/1379720_510799319011259_152274751_n.jpg">
           <img alt="1379720_510799319011259_152274751_n" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-220954" height="237" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/1379720_510799319011259_152274751_n.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           4. The Crowds
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Universities like to promote artists shows as free for students. If theres one thing you know, its that college kids love free. Of course, as we mentioned above, a lot ofgigs are paid for with funds collected from student fees, so technically they already paid for the show. But regardless, more often then not these shows are a very easy, cheap form of entertainment for kids who are still in the the discovery phases of their lives, excited to learn about new artists and genres, making for some very satisfactory turnouts.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-11.51.19-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-05-13 at 11.51.19 PM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-220955" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-11.51.19-PM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          And last but not least
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5. Catering
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          One of the biggest problems emerging artists face on tour is staying properlynourished. Performers are often left to subsist on gas station food or fast casual dining at best just because its cheap and easy. Its hard to stay healthy as it is with most touring schedules, but its nearly impossible when your fuel is ramen noodles made with hot water from the coffeemaker at Kum N Go. With college budgets, comes higher-quality fareand a better chance at arriving home post-tour without anemia or scurvy.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-11.54.15-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-05-13 at 11.54.15 PM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-220956" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-11.54.15-PM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
