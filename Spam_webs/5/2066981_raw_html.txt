          <div class="entry_widget_large entry_widget_right" id="asset-13068183">
           <span class="adv-photo-large">
            <img alt="package.jpg" class="lazy" data-original="http://media.al.com/breaking/photo/13068183-large.jpg" src="https://blog.advance.net/static/common/img/blank.gif" style="display: block;"/>

          <p>
           <span style="font-size: 1em;">
            GUNTERSVILLE, Alabama - A liquid believed to have made two Guntersville postal workers ill Tuesday was determined to be
            <a href="http://www.k-y.com/">
             K-Y Intense Arousal Gel
            </a>
            that leaked from packages addressed to someone in the entertainment industry, an inspector said.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           The liquid, which authorities said after initial tests Tuesday was likely non-hazardous methanol, has ingredients to "intensify sensation," including propylene glycol.
          </p>
          <p>
           The post office was evacuated at about 9 a.m. and two workers were taken to the hospital after coming into contact with the liquid that spilled from a package.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://blog.al.com/breaking/2013/07/police_on_scene_at_guntersvill.html">
            Read initial story here
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Guntersville Police and Hazmat teams responded, retrieving a bag containing several packages with the substance on it.
          </p>
          <p>
           The packages were addressed to someone in the entertainment industry, although that person's name was not released, said Tony Robinson, a USPS postal inspector.
          </p>
          <p>
           Robinson said he is not sure how the non-toxic substance made the workers ill. He added that, according to USPS policy, the sender would be contacted and advised how to better pack mail in the future.
          </p>
          <p>
           According to the
           <a href="http://www.atsdr.cdc.gov/substances/toxsubstance.asp?toxid=240">
            Agency for Toxic Substance and Disease Registry
           </a>
           , propylene glycol is used in cosmetics, medicines and foods and the FDA classified it as "generally recognized as safe" for use in food.
          </p>
