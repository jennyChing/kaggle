     <p>

     <div class="entry clearfloat">
      <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14242034" data-link="http://dollarstorecrafts.com/2009/10/jack-o-lantern-jars/" data-summary="" data-title="Make Jack-o'-Lantern Jars">
      </div>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://dollarstorecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/09/jackolanternjars.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="300" src="//dollarstorecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/09/jackolanternjars.JPG" style="max-width: 800px;" width="450"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Have extra jars on hand? I absolutely love these Jack-o'-Lanterns made from baby food containers by
       <a href="http://meandmyinsanity.blogspot.com/2009/08/jack-o-lantern-jars.html">
        My Insanity
       </a>
       - adorable! What's even better is that they are super-simple, and you probably have almost everything you need on hand--that's an award winning recipe for dollar store craftin', in my book! They'd be a great activity for kiddos (with a little help, depending on their ages), and you can use any size of jar or old vase to make them. You have to check the My Insanity blog for a super cute black bat jar, too! [
       <a href="http://meandmyinsanity.blogspot.com/2009/08/jack-o-lantern-jars.html">
        click here for project details
       </a>
       ]
      </p>
      <p>
       Oh, and while doing some recon recently, I found that W*l-mart has spray paint in basic colors for $.97, so I can legitimately claim that you can spend a buck for spray paint... not sure if they have orange, though! You can also substitute acrylic paint for this project, it will just have a more mottled, brushed-on look. You can get battery-operated tealights at the dollar store 2 for $1.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        Project Estimate:
       </span>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Recycled glass jars (or vases), on hand
       </li>
       <li>
        Spray paint, $1
       </li>
       <li>
        Mailing labels, hole reinforcement stickers, on hand or $1
       </li>
       <li>
        Tea lights or battery-operated tealights, on hand or $1
       </li>
      </ul>

       <strong>
        Total: FREE and up
       </strong>
