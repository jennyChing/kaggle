        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I almost wimped out on sharing a #15MinuteSuppers recipe this week because I thought that I was completely out of ideas
        </p>
        <p>
         And then I remembered a service activity that I attended this past month where we made omelets in a bag. As soon as I saw the process of making an omelet in a bag, I KNEW that my 2 youngest kids would go crazy over them. They are so easy to throw together and a tasty but quick way to feed a bunch of people customized eggs all at the same time. Just fit your pot of water to the size of the group youre feeding, and youre set.
        </p>
        <p>
         Perhaps the best part? When youre done, theres no mess to clean up. Just throw the bags away!
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step-by-step instructions to Build-your-own OMELET in a bag
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/build-your-own-omelet-in-a-bag.jpg">
          <img alt="build-your-own omelet in a bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21694" height="400" src="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/build-your-own-omelet-in-a-bag.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ingredients needed:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Eggs (about 2 per person)
         </li>
         <li>
          Salt and pepper
         </li>
         <li>
          Omelet ingredients (ham, bacon, shredded cheese, chopped green onions, salsa, mushrooms, etc)
         </li>
         <li>
          Quart sized zipper freezer bags (one per person)
         </li>
         <li>
          Big pot of boiling water
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          STEP 1
         </strong>
          Add a few of your favorite omelet ingredients to some small bowls. Gently beat eggs in a large bowl (about 2 per person on average) with some milk (OPTIONAL  I add about 1 Tablespoon or so per egg.but I never measure).
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-1.jpg">
          <img alt="build-your-own omelet in a bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21703" height="400" src="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-1.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          STEP 2
         </strong>
          Write family member names on ziploc bags (I ran out of quart-sized bags, so I used gallon freezer bags and used twisty ties to make them smaller).
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-2.jpg">
          <img alt="build-your-own omelet in a bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21702" height="400" src="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-2.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          STEP 3
         </strong>
          Let each person fill their bags up with the ingredients they want in their omelet. The amount of egg doesnt matter too much herejust take what youll eat. Obviously, a larger bag of eggs will take a little longer to cook than a smaller bag of eggs.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-6.jpg">
          <img alt="build-your-own omelet in a bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21698" height="400" src="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-6.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          STEP 4
         </strong>
          Seal bags and mix ingredients together (this is the point of the process where Reese realized wed be COOKING the eggs in the bags. Wow!).
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-7.jpg">
          <img alt="build-your-own omelet in a bag" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-21697" height="700" src="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-7-466x700.jpg" width="466"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          STEP 5
         </strong>
          Add each bag to the pot of boiling water and
         <strong>
          boil for about 12 minutes
         </strong>
         (be sure to check the doneness of your eggs before removing from bags).
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-3.jpg">
          <img alt="build-your-own omelet in a bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21701" height="400" src="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-3.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          STEP 6
         </strong>
          Remove finished omelet from ziploc bags and enjoy! I find mine more appealing when I smash it up a bitmaking it more of a scrambled egg in a bag (but Im not going to worry too much about that!).
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-8.jpg">
          <img alt="build-your-own omelet in a bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21696" height="400" src="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-8.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ta-da! A customized omelet for everyone in the family in just a few minutes! Youll be amazed at how tasty they are, too!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-9.jpg">
          <img alt="build-your-own omelet in a bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21695" height="400" src="http://betterinbulk.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/omelet-in-a-bag-9.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
