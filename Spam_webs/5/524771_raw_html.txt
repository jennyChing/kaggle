          For those looking for spectacular coral reefs that offer great scuba diving opportunities, then there are 3 coral reefs that are known throughout the SCUBA diving community for spectacular sights. These 3 notable coral reefs are well-known for unspoiled ecosystems and attract millions of undersea enthusiasts annually.
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          Great Barrier Reef: Australia
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_33730" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/briangratwicke/5421442899/sizes/z/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/briangratwicke/5421442899/sizes/z/', '');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           <img alt="coral reef dive destinationl" class="size-full wp-image-33730" height="480" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/great-barrier-reef.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           via flickr/brian.gratwicke
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The most well-known and popular coral reef dive destination is the Great Barrier Reef. The Great Barrier Reef stretches for 1,553 miles and is the largest coral reef on the planet. The Great Barrier Reef consists of over 2900 individual reefs, 300 coral cays, and 600 islands, which make it the planets largest individual structure that houses a variety of living organisms. The Great Barrier Reef is also one of the 7 wonders of the world and has over 3,000 individual reef systems.This world famous reef is something that every diver should experience.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          Red Sea Coral Reefs: Egypt
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_33731" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/dkeats/6312814080/sizes/z/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/dkeats/6312814080/sizes/z/', '');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           <img alt="coral reef dive destination" class="size-full wp-image-33731" height="480" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/red-sea-coral.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           via flickr/Derek Keats
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The Red Sea Coral Reefs, in the Indian Ocean, is another top choice for SCUBAdivers. The reef is 1,180 miles in length and houses more than 200 soft and hard corals. The warm saltwater temperatures allow the coral reefs to flourish and grow. A combination of the water temperature and limited rainfall provides extraordinary clarity, which is a primary reason the reef is so popular among divers. The Red Sea Coral Reefs also have 1,200 species of coral reef fish, ten percent of which are not found anywhere else on the globe.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          New Caledonia Barrier Reef: New Caledonia
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_33732" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/philippeamiot/7041695717/sizes/z/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/philippeamiot/7041695717/sizes/z/', '');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           <img alt="coral reef dive destination" class="size-full wp-image-33732" height="480" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/new-caledonia.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           via flickr/Philippe AMIOT
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Located in the Pacific Ocean close to New Caledonia, the New Caledonia Barrier Reef is the second longest double barrier reef. The reef is 932 miles in length and houses an array of fish and invertebrates. The New Caledonia Barrier Reef is home to one of the major nesting sites for loggerhead, green, leatherback, and hawksbill turtles. This reef has more than 600 species of sponges and more than 350 species of algae for divers to observe, making it one of the top coral reef diving destinations in the world.
         </p>
