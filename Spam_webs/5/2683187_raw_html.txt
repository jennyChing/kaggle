        <p>
         No one wants to be away from loved ones on any holiday, much less Valentines Day. This year, we have close family members deployed overseas. Being away during a special time is hard for everyone. To help ease the distance, mom and kiddo spent a few days at home making a care package filled with fourteen gifts  one for each day of February leading up to the 14th. It still wont be the same, but he certainly knows he is loved. Their little girl is almost 3, and she loves to see Daddy open the gifts via Skype.
        </p>

         <strong>
          6. Tissue Paper Heart Collage
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Tissue paper collages always turn out so well. I love the texture and colors. If you are saving space, the collage can be on paper, either as-is or made into a card. Or, you can collage onto an old baby food jar to make a tealight candle holder.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          7. I Love You Hand
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is a unique way to use the kids handprints  turn them into an
         <a href="http://www.kidscount1234.com/monthlycalendars.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.kidscount1234.com/monthlycalendars.html', ' I Love You']);" target="_blank">
          I Love You
         </a>
         sign-language sign, just the size their Valentine remembers seeing it from them.
