   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Rapid shallow breathing index
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       rapid shallow breathing index
      </b>
      (RSBI) is a tool that is used in the weaning of
      <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_ventilation" title="Mechanical ventilation">
       Mechanical ventilation
      </a>
      on
      <a href="/wiki/Intensive_care_unit" title="Intensive care unit">
       intensive care units
      </a>
      . The RSBI is defined as the ratio of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Respiratory_frequency" title="Respiratory frequency">
       respiratory frequency
      </a>
      to
      <a href="/wiki/Tidal_volume" title="Tidal volume">
       tidal volume
      </a>
      (f/VT). People on a ventilator who cannot tolerate independent breathing tend to breathe rapidly (high frequency) and shallowly (low tidal volume), and will therefore have a high RSBI.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YangTobin_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-YangTobin-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Equation">
       Equation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rapid_shallow_breathing_index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Equation">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <img alt="RSBI = \frac{f}{V_T} " class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline tex" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/math/2/f/b/2fbb6f328a30b82b2403675499eb7a9b.png"/>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Measurement">
       Measurement
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rapid_shallow_breathing_index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Measurement">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Measurement is done with a handheld
      <a href="/wiki/Spirometer" title="Spirometer">
       spirometer
      </a>
      attached to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endotracheal_tube" title="Endotracheal tube">
       endotracheal tube
      </a>
      while a patient breathes room air for one minute without any ventilator assistance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid2023603_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pmid2023603-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Example">
       Example
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rapid_shallow_breathing_index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Example">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As an example, a patient who has a respiratory rate of 25 breaths/min and a tidal volume of 250 mL/breath has an RSBI of (25 breaths/min)/(0.25 L) = 100 breaths/min/L
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rapid_shallow_breathing_index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The concept was introduced in a 1991 paper by physicians from the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_Health_Science_Center_at_Houston" title="University of Texas Health Science Center at Houston">
       University of Texas Health Science Center at Houston
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Stritch_School_of_Medicine" title="Stritch School of Medicine">
       Stritch School of Medicine
      </a>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/Loyola_University_Chicago" title="Loyola University Chicago">
       Loyola University
      </a>
      in Chicago.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YangTobin_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-YangTobin-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Weaning_Readiness">
       Weaning Readiness
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rapid_shallow_breathing_index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Weaning Readiness">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      An RSBI score of less than 65
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid11742961_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pmid11742961-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      indicating a relatively low respiratory rate compared to tidal volume is generally considered as an indication of weaning readiness.
     </p>
