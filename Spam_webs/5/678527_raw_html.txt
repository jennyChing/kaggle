         <div class="post-content description " itemprop="articleBody">
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Should Be the Hall of Shame!
          </h1>
          <h2 style="text-align: center;">
           Brought to you by:
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <a href="http://sethsrockreport.com/" target="_blank">
             Seths Rock Report
            </a>
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 246px">
           <img alt="" class="" height="177" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/56/KISS_in_concert_Boston_2004.jpg" width="236"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
             KISS
            </span>
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
            The RARHOF is a disgrace. I cant put it any other way. They are not credible in any way, shape or form. Those of you who know me, know that I have written and campaigned, joined petitions, etc. for the hall of shame to change its ways. Mainly, I was trying to get
            <a href="http://www.kissonline.com/" target="_blank">
             KISS
            </a>
            in for years. KISS qualified in 1998 and didnt get in until 2014. Even though they finally did get in and yet the Hall still didnt get it right. But I will get into that later. Lets set up what the process is from their
            <a href="http://www.rockhall.com/" target="_blank">
             website
            </a>
            :
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
             Artists become Eligible for induction 25 years after the release of their first record. Criteria include the influence and significance of the artists contributions to the development and perpetuation of rock and roll.
            </span>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
            Simple enough, right? Makes you wonder how so many bands are missed. The first class inducted was in 1986. I would definitely agree with this class, as most were part of the influence of what was rock and roll at that time. The inductees which followed this class not so much. They have country artists, pop artists, and rappers and so many that arent even in the realm of rock. For the record, I do consider Blues part of the rock family, as it influenced what rock became.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
            Why do I point this out? The fact that other genres are in the rock hall of shame? I do so because there are so many others that should be there, have been qualified for years and are still not in. Maybe if they were, I wouldnt be so hard on them. I will list the bands that I think should be later on in this article. Another thing that drives me crazy is that even when they get to actually inducting a band, they choose which members get in, not all line ups
            <em>
             (save the bands who just had a member replaced in the last couple of years)
            </em>
            , and it is just crazy. You want to scream,
            <i>
             Who the hell do they think they are?
            </i>
           </span>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 245px">
           <img alt="" class="" height="171" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e9/Bob_Welch_and_Jimmy_Robinson_at_the_Record_Plant_in_Sausilito_CA.jpg" width="235"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
             (Left)Bob Welch and Jimmy Robinson
            </span>
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
            Let me give you some examples,
            <a href="http://www.fleetwoodmac.com/splash" target="_blank">
             Fleetwood Mac
            </a>
            but no
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bob_Welch_%28musician%29" target="_blank">
             Bob Welch
            </a>
            . He was on 5 albums all charted in the top 100 on Billboard, but still not inducted.
            <a href="http://www.santana.com/" target="_blank">
             Santana
            </a>
            , well there are way too many to list but there are a few names sakes, most notably
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neal_Schon" target="_blank">
             Neal Schon
            </a>
            .
            <a href="http://www.aerosmith.com/" target="_blank">
             Aerosmiths
            </a>
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jimmy_Crespo" target="_blank">
             Jimmy Crespo
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rick_Dufay" target="_blank">
             Rick Dufay
            </a>
            , both on
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_in_a_Hard_Place" target="_blank">
             Rock In A Hard Place
            </a>
            , charted and kept the band going. Not in.
            <a href="http://www.steelydan.com/" target="_blank">
             Steely Dan
            </a>
            , yes the band is known for
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Becker" target="_blank">
             Walter Becker
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Fagen" target="_blank">
             Donald Fagan
            </a>
            , but what about
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeff_Baxter" target="_blank">
             Jeff Skunk Baxter
            </a>
            ?Another band with a huge list, but just the two of them got in. Very sad. He and others were on the first several albums but they were a band at that time. They should be in.
           </span>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 246px">
           <img alt="" class="" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a7/Ronnie_James_Dio_in_Concert.jpg" width="236"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
             Ronnie James Dio
            </span>
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
            <a href="http://www.acdc.com/us/rockorbust" target="_blank">
             AC/DC
            </a>
            , no
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Evans_%28musician%29" target="_blank">
             Mark Evans
            </a>
            or
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simon_Wright_%28musician%29" target="_blank">
             Simon Wright
            </a>
            ?
            <a href="http://www.blacksabbath.com/" target="_blank">
             Black Sabbath
            </a>
            with no
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ronnie_James_Dio" target="_blank">
             Ronnie James Dio
            </a>
            , are you kidding me?
            <a href="http://www.van-halen.com/" target="_blank">
             Van Halen
            </a>
            , where is
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sammy_Hagar" target="_blank">
             Sammy Hagar
            </a>
            ???? My favorite, KISS, yes the original line up had the impact, but
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Carr" target="_blank">
             Eric Carr
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Kulick" target="_blank">
             Bruce Kulick
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinnie_Vincent" target="_blank">
             Vinnie Vincent
            </a>
            and so on. They were part of the history of the band that kept it going. I think I made the case here. You get the point.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
            Explain to me how
            <a href="http://www.rundmc.com/" target="_blank">
             Run DMC
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.publicenemy.com/" target="_blank">
             Public Enemy
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.donnasummer.com/" target="_blank">
             Donna Summer
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ABBA" target="_blank">
             Abba
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.madonna.com/" target="_blank">
             Madonna
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grandmaster_Flash" target="_blank">
             Grandmaster Flash
            </a>
            , get in before the following list? This list should make you cringe. They arent in and most havent even been nominated. Here it is
            <em>
             (not in any specific order)
            </em>
            :
           </span>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://judaspriest.com/" target="_blank">
             <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
              Judas Priest
             </span>
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.ironmaiden.com/" target="_blank">
             <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
              Iron Maiden
             </span>
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.deeppurple.com/" target="_blank">
             <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
              Deep Purple
             </span>
            </a>
           </li>
