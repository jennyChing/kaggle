          <h1 class="title single-title">
           The Inventor Of The Keurig Says He Feels Guilty For Creating It
          </h1>
          <div class="post-info">
           <span class="thetime updated">
            <i class="icon-calendar">
            </i>
            March 4, 2015
           </span>
           <span class="thecategory">
            <i class="icon-tags">
            </i>
            Uncategorized
           </span>
          </div>
         </header>
         <!--.headline_area-->
         <div class="post-single-content box mark-links">
          <div class="topad">
           <a href="https://www.facebook.com/sharer/sharer.php?u=&lt;?php echo(urlencode(get_permalink( $id ))); ?&gt;" target="_blank">
            <img src="http://viralbubble.viralbubblellc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/shareonfacebook.jpg"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <p>
           John Sylvan said he doesnt even own one of his very creations because they are kind of expensive to use.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" height="417" src="http://s3-ec.buzzfed.com/static/2015-03/4/15/enhanced/webdr14/enhanced-10739-1425502373-5.jpg" width="625"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <small>
            Shannon Stapleton / Reuters
           </small>
          </p>
          <p>
           The inventor of the Keurig now says he feels guilty for creating the coffee-making device because it creates a ton of waste.
          </p>
          <p>
           In an interview with the
           <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/health/archive/2015/03/the-abominable-k-cup-coffee-pod-environment-problem/386501/">
            <i>
             Atlantic
            </i>
           </a>
           published Monday, John Sylvan said that he feels he created a monster.
          </p>
          <p>
           "I feel bad sometimes that I ever did it," Sylvan said.
          </p>
          <p>
           Sylvan created the Keurig in 1992 with his college roommate Peter Dragone. He said he believed they would only be used in offices.
          </p>
          <p>
           He told the
           <i>
            Atlantic
           </i>
           he was pretty sure the device, which allows the user to brew a single cup of coffee in seconds by inserting a pod into a machine, would be a hit.
          </p>
          <p>
           "It's like a cigarette for coffee, a single-serve delivery mechanism for an addictive substance," he said.
          </p>
          <p>
           Sylvan sold his share of the company in 1997 for $50,000. However, he told the
           <i>
            Atlantic
           </i>
           he now regrets being a part of it at all, because the pods are bad for the environment.
          </p>
          <p>
           "No matter what they say about recycling, those things will never be recyclable," he said.
          </p>
          <p>
           According to the
           <i>
            Atlantic,
           </i>
           the number of pods that were buried in 2014 could circle the Earth an estimated 12 times.
          </p>
          <p>
           The company has received backlash online in viral videos and petitions for creating a huge amount of waste.
          </p>
          <p>
           On its
           <a href="http://www.keuriggreenmountain.com/en/Sustainability/SustainableProducts/Overview.aspx">
            website
           </a>
           , Keurig says it has committed to making its pods 100% recyclable by 2020. The company did not respond to a request for comment from BuzzFeed News.
          </p>
          <p>
           "We believe that our products can be designed, sourced, and manufactured in ways that are good for people and good for the environment," it says. "We are dedicated to creating high-quality products that are more sustainable."
          </p>
          <p>
           Sylvan said he had an idea for how to make the pods more environmentally friendly, but it fell on deaf ears.
          </p>
          <p>
           "I told them how to improve it, but they don't want to listen," he told the
           <i>
            Atlantic.
           </i>
           "There's a much better way of doing it."
          </p>
          <p>
           In another show of disapproval, Sylvan said that he doesn't even have one of his own creations.
          </p>
          <p>
           "They're kind of expensive to use," he told the
           <i>
            Atlantic
           </i>
           . "Plus it's not like drip coffee is tough to make."
          </p>
          <p>
           <small>
           </small>
          </p>
