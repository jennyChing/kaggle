        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Smoked Salmon Avocado &amp; Dill Tartine
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-11-17T07:49:30+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          November 17, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.howtocookgoodfood.co.uk/author/howtocookgoodfoodcou/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Laura Scott
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.howtocookgoodfood.co.uk/2014/11/smoked-salmon-avocado-dill-tartine/#comments">
           13 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Here is a simple little dish that was inspired by an evening spent enjoying the delights of Norwegian food cooked by
         <a href="https://www.facebook.com/Scandilicious" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.facebook.com/Scandilicious', 'Signe Johansen']);" target="_blank" title="Scandilicious-Signe Johansen">
          Signe Johansen
         </a>
         . My smorbrod (Scandinavian style open sandwich) or Tartine (French open sandwich)) as I like to call it, is something that hardly qualifies as a recipe but is more a collection of Scandi flavours piled onto a spelt, rye and oat loaf.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10561" style="width: 540px">
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.howtocookgoodfood.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/smoked-salmon-tartine-single.jpg">
          <img alt="dill tartine " class="wp-image-10561" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.howtocookgoodfood.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/smoked-salmon-tartine-single.jpg?resize=530%2C623"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Smoked salmon avocado &amp; dill tartine
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         We created our own versions of this simple starter once Signe had introduced us all to the delights of what Norway has to offer in terms of its food culture. The event was organised by
         <a href="http://www.seafoodfromnorway.co.uk" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.seafoodfromnorway.co.uk', 'The Norwergian Seafood Council']);" target="_blank" title="Norwegian Seafood Council">
          The Norwergian Seafood Council
         </a>
         and held at
         <a href="http://aveqia.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://aveqia.com', 'Aveqia']);" target="_blank" title="Aveqia">
          Aveqia
         </a>
         ,so naturally the menu leaned towards fish. Most importantly salmon. Take a look at the menu below to see what Signe created for us to enjoy with the help of Poppy.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Smoked salmon on Peters Yard cripsbread
        </p>

        </p>

        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">

        <p style="text-align: center;">
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Highlights of the menu for me were many, and no it really did not matter to me that we feasted on smoked salmon and then baked salmon. I would eat both again gladly when they are cooked and prepared as well as they were, much in the same way I would enjoy beef carpaccio followed by steak frites. If there was one standout dish of the evening, it would have to be the Fyrstekake. Buttery rich pastry, cardamom spiced sticky centre (frangipane-like) and crunchy almonds complimented perfectly by the cherry glogg. Forget mulled wine, I am now serving this as my festive drink of choice.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10562" style="width: 519px">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/www.howtocookgoodfood.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Norwegian-seafoood-council-collage.jpg">
          <img alt="dill tartine" class="wp-image-10562" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.howtocookgoodfood.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Norwegian-seafoood-council-collage.jpg?resize=509%2C509"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Norwegian Seafood Event with Signe Johansen. Pictures courtesy of The Norwegian Seafood Council.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Norwegian food is not something I had ever tried before nor Scandinavian food in general  I have always had an obsession with the flavours of Asia. The impression I get is one of freshness, simplicity and a love of spices, fruits, cured fish and meats as well as dairy. Signe told us about her love of butter, lots of it and creme friache especially from Normandy. I am assuming cheese is also a favourite even though it did not feature as part of our meal and not forgetting good breads of course.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10563" style="width: 545px">
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.howtocookgoodfood.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/smoked-salmon-tartine.jpg">
          <img alt="dill tartine" class="wp-image-10563" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.howtocookgoodfood.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/smoked-salmon-tartine.jpg?resize=535%2C405"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Smoked salmon avocado &amp; dill tartine
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         The bread I used for my tartine was a recipe I found in Signes book Scandilicious for an oatmeal loaf. I added a combination of rye, spelt and regular white bread flour along with jumbo porridge oats soaked in water. It made for a wonderfully textured loaf perfectly suited to being topped with a range of Scandi inspired ingredients. Toppings are a matter of personal preference but if you love fresh light flavours why not try this recipe out? Lunch will feel all the more decadent for it.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Helen at Fuss Free Flavours created these wonderful salmon ceviche &amp; cranberry canaps having been inspired by the evenings cooking. You can read her recipe
