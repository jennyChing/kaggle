             <p>
              Prime Minister Stephen Harpers decision to meet first with Toronto Mayor John Tory has not put her off a face-to-face with the countrys leader, Premier Kathleen Wynne says.
             </p>
             <p>
              I dont take those things personally. I dont think theres any room in politics for that, Wynne said in a year-end interview with the Toronto Sun. I think that you really have to rise above that kind of thing and not get caught in that kind of pettiness.
             </p>
             <p>
              Wynne has been seeking a meeting with Harper for several months now to discuss common interests such as infrastructure support for the provincial economy. Yet, the newly-sworn-in Tory had no trouble getting an hour-long confab with Harper last week to pitch his transit proposals.
             </p>
             <p>
              Despite a failure to pencil her into his calendar, Wynne said shes optimistic because the Prime Minister has indicated a willingness to do so at some point.
             </p>
             <p>
              With a federal election almost a year away in October, Wynne said she believes there will be an opportunity early in the new year to meet with Harper.
             </p>
             <p>
              Im not sitting around wringing my hands, Ive got lots to do, Im very busy and theres lots of projects that we are working on, she said. Some of them like infrastructure, like the Ring of Fire, it would be really good to have a conversation with the Prime Minister and to be able to find a way to work together, but were going to carry on nonetheless.
             </p>
             <p>
              I feel very strongly that this isnt a personal thing, this isnt about Kathleen Wynne and Stephen Harper. This is about the Office of the Premier and the Office of the Prime Minister having a good working relationship.
             </p>
             <p>
              Harper had earlier indicated a reluctance to meet with a premier seeking confrontation.
             </p>
             <p>
              The Ontario government has been highly critical of the federal governments level of financial support, devoting a chapter in its budget this year to the topic, after equalization payments to the province dropped.
             </p>
             <p>
              Wynne has more recently spoken out with concern about the federal prostitution bill C-36, setting her attorney general to determine if the law is constitutional.
             </p>
             <p>
              The Premier suggested that politicians need to have thicker skins.
             </p>
             <p>
              Anyone in this life, anyone in politics, has to know that there will be criticism and there will be criticism from other levels of government, there will be criticism from the public, she said. That is the norm, and to be able to hear criticism in a way thats constructive, I think thats our responsibility.
             </p>

              <p>
               Prime Minister Stephen Harpers decision to meet first with Toronto Mayor John Tory has not put her off a face-to-face with the countrys leader, Premier Kathleen Wynne says.
              </p>
              <p>
               I dont take those things personally. I dont think theres any room in politics for that, Wynne said in a year-end interview with the Toronto Sun. I think that you really have to rise above that kind of thing and not get caught in that kind of pettiness.
              </p>
              <p>
               Wynne has been seeking a meeting with Harper for several months now to discuss common interests such as infrastructure support for the provincial economy. Yet, the newly-sworn-in Tory had no trouble getting an hour-long confab with Harper last week to pitch his transit proposals.
              </p>
