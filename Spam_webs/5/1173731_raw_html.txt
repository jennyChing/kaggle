   <h3 id="canvas-results">
    Canvas Fingerprinting
   </h3>
   <div class="media">
    <img alt="Different images printed to canvas by various canvas fingerprinting scripts" class="media-object pull-left" src="canvas_fig.png"/>
    <div class="media-body">
     <h4 class="media-heading">
      Background
     </h4>
     <p>
      Canvas fingerprinting is a type of browser or device fingerprinting technique that 
				was first presented by
      <a href="http://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~hovav/papers/ms12.html" title="Pixel Perfect: Fingerprinting Canvas in HTML5">
       Mowery and Shacham in 2012
      </a>
      . 
				The authors found that by using the Canvas API of modern browsers, one can 
				exploit the subtle differences in the rendering of the same text to extract a
				consistent fingerprint that can easily be obtained in a fraction of a second without user's awareness.
     </p>
    </div>
   </div>
   <div class="media">
    <img alt="Different images printed to canvas by various canvas fingerprinting scripts" class="media-object pull-right" src="canvas_imgs.png"/>
    <div class="media-body">
     <h4 class="media-heading">
      Results
     </h4>
     <p>
      By crawling the homepages of the top 100,000 sites we found that more than 5.5% of 
			the crawled sites include canvas fingerprinting scripts. 
			Although the overwhelming majority (95%) of 
			the scripts belong to a single provider (addthis.com), 
			we discovered a total of 20 canvas fingerprinting provider domains, 
			active on 5542 of the top 100,000 sites.
     </p>
     <p>
      On the right, collage of the images printed to canvas by various fingerprinting scripts discovered during the study. 
			The images are intercepted using a modified browser (by instrumenting the
      <code>
       ToDataURL
      </code>
      method). 
			Some blank space was cropped from images to save space.
     </p>
    </div>
   </div>

   <h3 id="evercookies-results">
    Evercookies &amp; Respawning
   </h3>
   <div class="media">
    <img alt="Steps of respawning" class="media-object pull-left" src="respawning.png"/>
    <div class="media-body">
     <h4 class="media-heading">
      Background
     </h4>
     <p>
      Evercookies are designed to overcome the "shortcomings" of the traditional tracking mechanisms.
					By utilizing multiple storage vectors that are less transparent to users and may be more difficult to clear, 
					evercookies provide an extremely resilient tracking mechanism, and have been found to be used by many popular 
					sites to circumvent deliberate user actions
      <a href="http://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1446862">
       <sup>
        1,
       </sup>
      </a>
      <a href="http://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1898390">
       <sup>
        2,
       </sup>
      </a>
      <a href="http://ashkansoltani.org/2011/08/11/respawn-redux-flash-cookies/">
       <sup>
        3
       </sup>
      </a>
      .
     </p>
    </div>
   </div>

   <h3 id="cookiesync-results">
    Cookie Syncing
   </h3>
   <div class="media">
    <div class="col-md-6">
     <img alt="Steps of cookie syncing" class="media-object pull-right" src="cookie_sync_dia.png"/>
    </div>
    <div class="col-md-6">
     <div class="media-body">
      <h4 class="media-heading">
       Background
      </h4>
      <p>
       Cookie synchronization or cookie syncing is the practice of tracker domains 
					passing pseudonymous IDs associated with a given user, typically stored in 
					cookies, amongst each other.
      </p>
      <p>
       Read the blog post that explains cookie syncing and our findings with animated diagrams:
       <a href="https://freedom-to-tinker.com/blog/englehardt/the-hidden-perils-of-cookie-syncing/">
        The hidden perils of cookie syncing
       </a>
       (Freedom to Tinker)
      </p>
     </div>
    </div>
   </div>
   <div class="clearfix">
   </div>
