              <header>
               <h1 class="entry-title page-title">
                10 Rarest Horse Breeds
               </h1>
              </header>
              <div class="entry-meta">
               <span class="date updated icon-clock">
                <a href="http://www.horsenation.com/2013/08/31/10-rarest-horse-breeds/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to 10 Rarest Horse Breeds">
                 <time class="entry-date" datetime="2013-08-31T20:11:27+00:00">
                  August 31, 2013
                 </time>
                </a>
               </span>
               <span class="author vcard icon-user">
                <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.horsenation.com/author/carla/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Carla Lake">
                 Carla Lake
                </a>
               </span>
               <span class="categories-links icon-folder-open">
                <a href="http://www.horsenation.com/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag">
                 Uncategorized
                </a>
               </span>
               <span class="tags-links icon-tag">
                <a href="http://www.horsenation.com/tag/lists/" rel="tag">
                 #LISTS
                </a>
               </span>
               <span class="date icon-bubbles">
                <a href="http://www.horsenation.com/2013/08/31/10-rarest-horse-breeds/#comments">
                 8 Comments
                </a>
               </span>
              </div>
              <!-- .entry-meta -->
              <p>
               This Ahal-Teke picture has been circulating the Internet as the most beautiful horse in the world. Thats debatable, but the breed is one of the rarestfind out the rest of the top 10.
               <span id="more-38382">
               </span>
              </p>
              <p>
               (top image via
               <a href="https://www.facebook.com/AmazingThingsInTheWorld" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.facebook.com/AmazingThingsInTheWorld', 'Amazing Things in the World']);">
                Amazing Things in the World
               </a>
               Facebook group)
              </p>
              <p>
               If any of your non-horsey friends are like mine, they blissfully send you horse-related things that are wrong at best or REALLY REALLY wrong at worst. From his ridonkulous ewe-neck to his over-at-the-knee-ness, the Ahal Teke appeals to me more as a Whats Wrong With This Picture? more than for his beauty.
              </p>
              <p>
               However, there are fewer than 5000 Ahal Tekes in the world, which puts them on the
               <a href="http://www.livestockconservancy.org/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.livestockconservancy.org/', 'Livestock Conservancy ']);">
                Livestock Conservancy
               </a>
               Threatened Breeds list. The breeds on the Critical list have fewer than 500 animals.
              </p>
              <h4>
               Critical Breeds (&lt;500 worldwide)
              </h4>
              <p>
               <strong>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/ziebell.jpg">
                <img alt="ziebell" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-38385" height="232" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/ziebell-300x232.jpg" width="300"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://www.acdha.org/history.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.acdha.org/history.html', 'American Cream Draft Association']);">
                American Cream Draft Association
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                American Cream
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               The American Cream is the only American breed of draft horse, and is known for its pink skin, amber eyes and cream-colored coat. The breed was developed in the early 1900sright as cars and farm machinery were becoming mass-produced. Not the best time for draft horses in general, and the Cream in particular, but those who love them continue to breed them in a way that is consistent with the old-style heavy draft type. Today they are used for riding and driving.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Introd3.jpg">
                <img alt="Introd3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38386" height="197" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Introd3.jpg" width="284"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://www.caspianhorses.org/introduction.asp" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.caspianhorses.org/introduction.asp', 'International Caspian Society']);">
                International Caspian Society
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Caspian
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Caspians are considered Irans national treasure, and though images of them appeared in art dating from 3000 B.C., they were believed to be extinctuntil 1965. Thats when an Iranian aristocrat, Narcy Firouz, and his American wife, Louise Laylin Firouz, went on an expedition to find horses or ponies for children to ride in their Tehran-based riding school. They heard that these small horses were kept by the Caspian Sea, and found that the breed was still alive. They stand no higher than 12.2 hands, and have a similar temperament and hardiness to Arabians.
              </p>
              <p>
               Political turmoil in Iran made her efforts to maintain the breed difficult, but Louise Firouz created a national Caspian stud farm, Persicus Farm, which is now run by the government, and also exported several Caspians to the U.K.. Today the largest population of Caspians outside Iran continues to be in the U.K.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/cb.png">
                <img alt="cb" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-38387" height="235" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/cb-300x235.png" width="300"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://www.clevelandbay.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.clevelandbay.com/', 'Cleveland Bay Horse Society']);">
                Cleveland Bay Horse Society
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Cleveland Bay
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               As you might have guessed, Cleveland Bays are a bay breed of horse. These warmblood-type horses are Englands oldest breed. Theyre very athletic and their sensible temperament means they were used for anything and everything. So how did they become rare? World War II, thats how.
              </p>
              <p>
               But then Queen Elizabeth said, Look under your chairsyou get a Cleveland Bay! You get a Cleveland Bay! EVERYONE GETS CLEVELAND BAYS! Or
               <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=stVIWf03XrQ" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=stVIWf03XrQ', 'maybe Im mixing her up with Oprah']);">
                maybe Im mixing her up with Oprah
               </a>
               . Anyway, she took the preservation of the breed quite seriously, and though their numbers are still small, theyve been quite successful at the top levels of competition, making appearances in World Games and the Olympics.
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/marsh_tacky_200.jpg">
                <img alt="marsh_tacky_200" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38389" height="223" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/marsh_tacky_200.jpg" width="200"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               Marsh Tacky from
               <a href="http://www.equisearch.com/resources/breeds/marsh_tacky_062408/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.equisearch.com/resources/breeds/marsh_tacky_062408/', 'Equisearch']);">
                Equisearch
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Colonial Spanish Strains
               </strong>
               <br/>
               Bankers, Belskys, Cerbats, Choctaws, Florida Crackers, Carolina Marsh Tackies, New Mexicos, Pryors, Santa Cruzes, Sulphurs and Wilbur-Crucesall of these closely related breeds originated from Spanish stock, and all of them have fewer than 500 existing horses. Nearly all colors appear in the Spanish strains, and many are gaited.
               <br/>
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/horse1.jpg">
                <img alt="horse1" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-38390" height="200" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/horse1-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://hackneysociety.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://hackneysociety.com/', 'American Hackney Horse Society']);">
                American Hackney Horse Society
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Hackney Horse
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               The high-stepping Hackney Horse was originally a roadster breed valued for its hardniess, style and speed. In the 1800s Hackneys often competed in 100-mile harness races, finishing in about 10 hours. Unfortunately, 10mph is no longer the gold standard when it comes to high-speed road vehicles, and demand for the British breed declined, and was further diminished by the World Wars. Now Hackney Horses (and ponies, though their genetic makeup is slightly different) often compete in driving classes, though they are athletic and versatile.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/46.jpg">
                <img alt="46" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-38391" height="208" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/46-300x208.jpg" width="300"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://www.newfoundlandpony.com/photo_gallery/view4325.html?id=46&amp;limit=8&amp;category_id=5" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.newfoundlandpony.com/photo_gallery/view4325.html?id=46&amp;limit=8&amp;category_id=5', 'Newfoundland Pony Society']);">
                Newfoundland Pony Society
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Newfoundland Pony
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Like many other rare breeds, these hardy little ponies were originally bred from colonial stock, then were specialized for a task that became outmoded as farm machinery advanced. Originally the breed did a little bit of everything that was needed around the farm, from driving to riding to plowingand they did it all while surviving harsh Canadian winters. Currently only around 400 Newfoundland Ponies exist in the world!
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/800px-Shire_horse.jpg">
                <img alt="800px-Shire_horse" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-38392" height="225" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/800px-Shire_horse-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
               </a>
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Shire_horse.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Shire_horse.jpg', 'Wikipedia']);">
                Wikipedia
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Shire
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               No, it doesnt have anything to do with Middle EarthShires get their name from rural British counties called shires. They are one of the largest breeds of draft horse, reaching 19 hands or more.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/parkbowler.png">
                <img alt="parkbowler" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-38393" height="225" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/parkbowler-300x225.png" width="300"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://suffolkhorsesociety.org.uk/stallion-list/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://suffolkhorsesociety.org.uk/stallion-list/', 'Suffolk Horse Society']);">
                Suffolk Horse Society
               </a>
              </p>
              <h3>
               Suffolk Punch
              </h3>
              <p>
               Another breed outsourced by machinery, the Suffolk is a draft horse that only comes in shades of chestnut and liver. Currently there are about 600 Suffolks in the U.S.their moderate height of 15-16 hands makes them more versatile than some of the other endangered draft breeds.
              </p>
              <h4>
               Threatened Breeds (&lt;5000 worldwide)
              </h4>
              <p>
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Dirkhan.jpg">
                <img alt="Dirkhan" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-38394" height="227" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Dirkhan-300x227.jpg" width="300"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Dirkhan.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Dirkhan.jpg', 'Wikimedia Commons']);">
                Wikimedia Commons
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Akhal-Teke
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Oh, the Ahal-Teke. Considered the Greyhound of the horse world, I just think they look weirdand so do many other people, which is likely why theyre rare. This breed from Turkmenistan does have some interesting characteristics, such as having a unique hair structure that gives their coat a metallic sheen. The breed is older than the Arabian, and was developed to be suited for nomadic tribes traveling long distances.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/JimZipperStand.jpg">
                <img alt="JimZipperStand" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-38395" height="300" src="http://875357559f655c0fd9842374.eventingnation.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/JimZipperStand-198x300.jpg" width="198"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://www.chhaps.org/photos.htm" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.chhaps.org/photos.htm', 'Canadian Horse Heritage &amp; Preservation Society']);">
                Canadian Horse Heritage &amp; Preservation Society
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Canadian
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               The versatile little iron horse comes from French stock that Louis XIV exported to Canada. The breed is rugged, strong and quick, but after the U.S. Civil War, so many Canadian horses were imported to the U.S.and killed in combat that the breed nearly died out, and has struggled since.
              </p>
              <p>
               Go Riding.
              </p>
