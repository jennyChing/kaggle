      <h2>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pure9/2795414963/" rel="external nofollow" style="font-size: 14px; line-height: 1.5em;">
        <img alt="Most-beautiful-sailing-destinations in Europe" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-598546" height="424" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Most-beautiful-sailing-destinations1.jpg" title="Most-beautiful-sailing-destinations in Europe" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <em>
        Chartering a boat and sailing around Europe has been on our travel wish list for a while now, so when we heard from Antonios Fiorakis,co-founder of
        <a href="http://incrediblue.com/" rel="external nofollow">
         incrediblue.com
        </a>
        a kind ofAirbnb for boat rentals we were more than a little intrigued. The principle is based on the fact that the average boat owner utilizes their boat only a couple of weeks per year. The website aims to bring all those boats to your fingertips and is the first travel startupto build a trusted community marketplace for boat rentals  a perfect and often inexpensive option for your summer vacation. So all you have to do now is choose your beautiful sailing destination. If Europe is your first stop, here are some of the best
       </em>
      </p>
      <h2>
       Ionian Islands, Greece
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/robwallace/89912103/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="Sailing around the Ionion islands" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-598502" height="480" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Sailing-around-the-Ionion-islands.jpg" title="Sailing around the Ionion islands" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Island hopping around the
       <a href="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/destinations/europe/10-best-greek-islands-travel-snobs/" title="Greek islands">
        Greek islands
       </a>
       is at the top of many a travellers bucket list and the famously beautiful Ionian islands are a great place to begin. Start from Corfu or Lefkada and then meander through turquoise seas stopping off at each of the seven large islands and some of the smaller ones along the way (Paxoi and Antipaxoi make excellent sailing destinations). Expect all the usual cliched Greek images such as harbours filled with coloured fishing boats, white-washed villages, beautiful beaches,rugged mountains andtraditional tavernas.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Azores, Portugal
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/8640561@N07/526547249/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="Azores - beautiful sailing destinations" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-598495" height="480" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Azores.jpg" title="Azores - beautiful sailing destinations" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.visitazores.com/en" rel="external nofollow">
        The Azores
       </a>
       are a cluster of tiny Portuguese-owned islands located in the Atlantic Ocean. The volcanic islands are known for their lush green vegetation and dramatic lunar-esque landscapes and are perfect for exploring by boat (Horta is a good starting point). Their remote location means they are unspoilt by the tourist masses and if youre lucky enough youll also get to see the odd whale or dolphin along the way.
      </p>
      <h2>
       The Dalmatian Coast, Croatia
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/planeta_roig/6124054272/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="Vis Island Croatia - most beautiful sailing destinations" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-589093" height="427" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/Vis-Island-Croatia.jpg" title="Vis Island Croatia - most beautiful sailing destinations" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Croatia is a travel destination that is definitely having a moment. Hailed as everything from the new Greece to the new Riviera, this part of Europe offers beautiful beaches and a traditional way of life. There are no fewer than 1185 islands along the crystalline Adriatic coast, and one of the best ways to explore them is by boat. Head off from the historic cities of Split and Dubrovnik and make sure you include the upmarket island of Hvar, timeless Vis island and the more remote islands of Kornati or Elafiti. Expect traditional fishing villages, beautiful sun-soaked beaches and a plethora of attractive hidden coves.
      </p>
      <h2>
       The Baltic Islands, Germany and Scandinavia
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/werkunz/3973647532/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="The Baltic Islands" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-598506" height="224" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/The-Baltic-Islands.jpg" title="The Baltic Islands" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       The brackish inland sea is bound by the Scandinavian Peninsula, Europe mainland and the Danish islands. Although most of us would not think of going island-hopping in Germany, it offers some really unique locations. Start at Stralsund and include the wild sweeping landscapes of the car-free Hiddensee island and the deserted white sand beaches of Rugen island. If you can, extend your trip to include the beautiful Stockholm archipelago and the Danish Islands which includes Isle of
       <a href="http://www.langeland.dk/?cc=uk" rel="external nofollow">
        Langeland
       </a>
       where wild horses roam.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Sporades Islands, Greece
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/ioarvanit/9533736886/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="Sporades Islands sailing" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-598507" height="429" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Sporades-Islands-sailing.jpg" title="Sporades Islands sailing" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Another beautiful Greek sailing route takes you through the less famous Sporades islands which are located off the East coast. There are eleven islands in total but if you start at Volos or Skiathos, youve got a good chance of visiting quite a few. Known as the emerald of the Aegean, expect rich vegetation, unspoiled green blue waters and a more traditional way of life. Highlights include the picturesque island of Skopelos, the party-island Skiathos and the Marine Park of Alonissos which is dotted with secret coves.
      </p>
      <h2>
       The French Riviera, France
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/destinations/europe/48-hours-cannes/">
        <img alt="48-Hours-in-Cannes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-594613" height="427" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/48-Hours-in-Cannes1.jpg" title="48-Hours-in-Cannes" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Many may think the glitzy
       <a href="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/destinations/europe/big-pictures-cote-dazur/">
        South of France
       </a>
       is a victim of its own popularity but its still one of the most beautiful sailing destinations in Europe, if not the world. Start at celebrity haunt Saint-Tropez and make your way along the celebrated coastline stopping off at Cannes, Nice and the millionaire playground, Monaco. If you want to fit in, pack your finest clothes, charter a huge yacht and pose artfully on the deck every time you moor up.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Bay of Naples, Capri and the Amalfi Coast, Italy
      </h2>
      <h2>
       <a href="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/destinations/europe/10-reasons-visit-amalfi-coast/">
        <img alt="Amalfi-Coast" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-598112" height="480" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Amalfi-Coast1.jpg" style="font-size: 14px; line-height: 1.5em;" title="Amalfi-Coast" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </h2>
      <p>
       For the most uniquely beautiful coastline in Europe, set sail from Naples and head down the coast towards
       <a href="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/destinations/europe/10-reasons-visit-amalfi-coast/" title="Amalfi">
        Amalfi
       </a>
       and the exclusive island of Capri. Expect sunny shores, pretty towns, plenty of food and wine and gorgeous isles from this part of Italy. Make sure you stop at the famous Blue Grotto, the honeymooners favourite the Isle of Capri, the volcanic island of Ischia and the photogenic little coastal community, Positano.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Turkish Blue Coast &amp; Dodecanese islands in Greece
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/rhh/7990753914/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="Turkey sailing" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-598512" height="319" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Turkey-sailing.jpg" title="Turkey sailing" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       For more Mediterranean finery, start at the cosmopolitan beach resort Bodrum which is situated along the glistening Turkish Blue Coast and make your way to the sun-drenched Greek Dodecanese islands. This part of Europe is known for its ancient amphitheatres, rocky coastline and sunken villages. Stop off at one of the countless bays to swim or snorkel, and make sure you include a stop to at traditional island of Leros. Other island highlights include Aspronisia, Tiganakia &amp; Marathi which are all serene and unspoiled.
      </p>
      <h2>
       The Dorset and East Devon Coast, England
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/steinsky/2157897313/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="Jurassic Coast Devon sailing" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-598513" height="426" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Jurassic-Coast-Devon-sailing.jpg" title="Jurassic Coast Devon sailing" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       You might not always get the good weather but this part of the UK equals many of its European rivals in beauty. Over 95 miles of uniquely formed ancient coastline stretch all the way from East Devon to Dorset. Otherwise known as the Jurassic Coast, some of the rock formations here are 185 million years old and its Englands first natural World Heritage Site. Set sail from Weymouth Harbour and stop off at all the local beauty spots  Durdle Door, Lulworth cove and countless historic coastal villages.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Balearic islands, Spain
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/anieto2k/7788294442/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="Sailing Majorca" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-596673" height="250" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Unspoilt-Majorca.jpg" title="Sailing Majorca" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       This is a much more touristy option but the Balearics still make a beautiful European sailing destination, and you can avoid the crowds if you know how. The cluster of Spanish islands include Ibiza, Mallorca, Menorca and Formentera; all major holiday spots known for their sun-drenched beaches and sparkling blue waters. Also include a few stops to the pretty, unspoilt towns of Deia in Mallorca and the secluded Ibizan bay of Cala Llentrisca.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Corsica, France
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/erbajolo/8597080871/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="Corsica sailing" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-598517" height="426" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Corsica.jpg" title="Corsica sailing" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       With over 200 beaches,
       <a href="http://www.corsicatravelguide.com/towns.php" rel="external nofollow">
        chic coastal resorts
       </a>
       and fine weather, Corsica is one of the best kept secrets of the Western Mediterranean. Its a fairly isolated spot that has kept the tourist masses away so expect a more traditional way of life and plenty of peace and quiet. The coastline is also pretty special with unspoilt beaches, hidden coves and secluded bays which are best appreciated from the deck of a yacht. Highlights include the beautiful town of Ajaccio which is encircled by mountains and Bonifacio, a major port with a restaurant-lined harbour.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Norwegian Fjords
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/ewixx/8514550289/" rel="external nofollow">
        <img alt="sailng Norway Fjords" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-592184" height="427" src="http://www.globalgrasshopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Sognefjord-Norway1.jpg" title="sailng Norway Fjords" width="640"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       A sailing trip here will offer you some of the most breathtaking scenery in Europe. The
       <a href="http://www.visitnorway.com/uk/What-to-do/Attractions-Culture/Unesco-World-Heritage-Sites/" rel="external nofollow">
        World Heritage listed fjords
       </a>
       were formed by glaciers (during the last ice age) and today they are a dramatic sight where tranquil blue waters gently lap at lush green shores which rise to majestic granite peaks. Gaze at picturesque villages, isolated farms and gushing waterfalls on your journey through the deep dark waters.
      </p>
