        <h1 class="entry-title">
         What Is Brainwave Entrainment?
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/brainwave_entrainment.jpg">
           <img alt="brainwave entrainment" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-184" height="228" src="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/brainwave_entrainment-300x228.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Youve probably heard about
          <strong>
           brainwave entrainment
          </strong>
          , or binaural beats, or maybe even
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/what-are-isochronic-tones/" title="What are Isochronic Tones?">
           isochronic tones
          </a>
          . Thats why youre here, right? Well, theres a lot of products out there right now, but to date, there are very few good information resources for people like us. So thats what this article and this website is about, providing information and resources about the benefits of brainwave entrainment to those of us who arent engaged in neuroscience research. You dont need to know anything already about alpha waves or binaural beats or isochronic tones, because Im going to walk you through it, and you can decide for yourself whether its something you want to make part of your lifestyle.
         </p>
         <div class="linkads">
          <script async="" src="http://pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
          </script>
          <!-- Article Ads Google -->
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-3896657708179157" data-ad-slot="5841298758" style="display:inline-block;width:468px;height:15px">

         </div>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           What Brainwave En
          </strong>
          <strong>
           trainment is
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Alright, so first off, what the heck is this brainwave entrainment stuff anyway? Well, we all have some sort of idea what a brainwave is, and entrain simply means to carry along. The idea behind the technology is to use specific methods, such as binaural beats or isochronic tones to carry your brainwaves from one electrochemical state to another. So, the essential idea is that
          <strong>
           using brainwave entrainment changes your brainwaves to a desired state
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          You see, your brain under normal circumstances produces four main types of brainwaves: alpha, beta, delta, and theta.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           Alpha Waves
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Alpha wa
          <img alt="alphawave" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-43" height="45" src="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/alphawave.jpg" width="120"/>
          ves have a frequency from 8-13Hz and originate from your brains thalamus during
          <strong>
           light relaxation
          </strong>
          and with your eyes closed. They are also generated during REM sleep
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           Beta Waves
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Beta wa
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/betawave.jpg">
           <img alt="betawave" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-46" height="45" src="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/betawave.jpg" width="120"/>
          </a>
          ves have a frequency between 12-30Hz. These waves are generated by your brain when youre in an alert and wakeful state . Theyre associated with
          <strong>
           active concentration
          </strong>
          , or busy or anxious thinking.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           Theta Waves
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/thetawave.jpg">
           <img alt="thetawave" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-48" height="45" src="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/thetawave.jpg" width="120"/>
          </a>
          Theta waves have a frequency of between 6-10Hz and occur mainly, but not exclusively, in the hippocampus region of your brain. These waves are generated mainly during sleep or during
          <strong>
           deep meditation
          </strong>
          , although to a certain extent theyre always present and the research suggests they help regulate a great many bodily functions.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           Delta Waves
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/deltawave.jpg">
           <img alt="deltawave" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-47" height="45" src="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/deltawave.jpg" width="120"/>
          </a>
          Delta waves have a frequency between 0.1-4Hz and are what your brain produces during
          <strong>
           deep sleep
          </strong>
          , also known as slow-wave sleep. This is the bare minimum amount of activity your brain can sustain.
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre interested, you can read all about these and other types of brainwaves at the
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neural_oscillation">
           Wikipedia page on neural oscillations
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Now that you know what the main types of brainwaves are, what about the entrainment part? Well, in the most simple terms, brainwave entrainment is the process of using an external frequency to cause your brains internal frequency to
          <strong>
           produce a desired response
          </strong>
          . Another term for brainwave entrainment is frequency following response. Thats exactly what your brain does when exposed to certain frequencies, it follows them. You can grasp the concept even more simply if you think about a song that always causes you a reaction when you hear it. Ever heard a song that was just so catchy that your body started tapping your foot along with it, almost without you even being aware of it? Brainwave entrainment technology like binaural beats or isochronic tones works in a similar, although much more scientifically advanced way. So what are these binaural beats and isochronic tones I keep talking about?
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Binaural Beats
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/headphones.jpg">
           <img alt="headphones" class="size-medium wp-image-78 alignright" height="189" src="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/headphones-300x189.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Put simply, a binaural beat is
          <strong>
           two different tones
          </strong>
          at two different pitches that you listen through headphones, so each ear is hearing a different tone. Your brain then attempts to combine the two, and
          <strong>
           what you actually hear is one tone
          </strong>
          . Now, you remember talking about brainwaves, and how they operate at different frequencies, well these tones in a binaural beat are specifically designed to cause your brainwaves to adjust to certain frequencies of their own, which are useful for achieving different goals.
         </p>
         <p>
          For example, if you have an anxiety problem, there are binaural beats which target specific frequencies in the alpha wave range which can help alleviate the symptoms of a panic attack that youre having, and over an extended period of usage, they can actually cause you to experience panic attacks less frequently and more mildly. There are a wide range of things that binaural beats can do, some of which Ill go into later on in the article
         </p>
         <p>
          Be aware that most reputable binaural beats are produced by professional companies like
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/idoser.php" target="_blank" title="i-Doser">
           i-Doser
          </a>
          or
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/transparent.php" target="_blank" title="Transparent Corp.">
           Transparent Corp
          </a>
          . There are a lot of people who make their own, but they havent done the research to know how to produce quality beats, so if youve tried binaural beats before and they havent worked for you, chances are you found a knock-off made by someone hoping to cash in on the popularity or who meant well but simply didnt know what they were doing.
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre technically minded, you can read this
          <a href="http://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/00029157.2003.10403543#.Uh7Bfz-DVAN">
           research paper on binaural beats
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Isochronic Tones
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/speaker.jpg">
           <img alt="speaker" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-79" height="225" src="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/speaker-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          An isochronic tone is a little bit different from a binaural beat. Isochronic tones
          <strong>
           dont require headphones
          </strong>
          , and they engage not just your brain, but your body as well. Ever been to a concert and you could feel the music, not just hear it. Isochronic tones work in a similar manner. This can be amazingly effective in all sorts of ways well get into in
          <a href="http://www.isochronicbeats.net/what-are-isochronic-tones/" title="What are Isochronic Tones?">
           an article specifically on the subject of isochronic tones
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          One of them Ill share now though, is one that I use myself pretty frequently, and thats headache relief. Normally, delta waves are only produced during deep sleep, but with isochronic tones, your brain can be induced to generate them while youre awake, and after about 5 minutes of listening to certain tones in the delta wave range, my headaches vanish as quickly as they came.
         </p>
         <p>
          Like binaural beats, there have been a number of people hoping to cash in on the isochronic tone phenomenon by making shoddy products without the benefit of research and development that are designed solely to take your money. Occasionally youll find a free one or two that are worth your time, but its like looking for a needle in a haystack and youre far better off just going with a professionally produced product like
          <a href="http://0a8504bbx84kz5tbgata1sgo8s.hop.clickbank.net/" target="_blank" title="Meditation Power">
           Meditation Power
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Again, if youre technically minded, heres a
          <a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304394007007938">
           research paper on isochronic tones
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           What Are the Benefits of Brainwave Entrainment
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Alright, so youve got the concept now, right? You have an idea of how this entrainment stuff works, but what does it actually do? My first question when I ran across it was, What exactly is the benefit to this? Well, the benefits deserve an entire article all on their own, but heres a short list of the things that brainwave entrainment can do for you:
