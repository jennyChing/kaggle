          <article class="content widget widget-alt expert-content expert-content-text" itemprop="articleBody">
           <div class="content-img-wrapper ">
            <figure class="content-img content-img-small right-gutter">
             <div class="content-img-wrapper">
              <img alt=" - " class=" addPinit" data-description="" data-pinit-img-url="http://f.tqn.com/y/friendship/1/S/e/C/-/-/83826995.jpg" height="255" itemprop="image" src="http://f.tqn.com/y/friendship/1/W/e/C/-/-/83826995.jpg" width="385"/>
             </div>
             <figcaption class="content-img-subtitle">
             </figcaption>
            </figure>
           </div>
           <div class="by-line-top hide-desktop">
           </div>
           <p>
            Having a grateful attitude about life can help you in a variety of areas, including friendship. Gratefulness can transform your friendships to create a deeper connection. The more grateful you are, the more you pay attention to the little things that make a friendship special. You also behave differently as a friend when you're grateful.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             You Put Less Demands On Your Friends
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Are you
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Conflicts_With_Friends/a/How-To-Handle-A-Clingy-Friend.htm">
             clingy
            </a>
            ?
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="2" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Setting_Boundaries/a/How-Do-You-Deal-With-A-Selfish-Friend.htm">
             Selfish
            </a>
            ? Allow gratitude to transform your perception about the relationships in your life.
           </p>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="3" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense1">
           </div>
           <div class="radlinks ads-half" data-character-limit="null" data-display-inline="true" data-display-label="false" data-num-links="5" id="radlinks3">
           </div>
           <div data-defer="onPageLoad" id="videoPlaceholderLarge">
           </div>
           <p class="cb-split">
            Grateful people look for the good in people and situations, so behaviors like wondering why a friend hasn't called or
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="3" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Toxic_Friends/f/Its-All-About-Her.htm">
             demanding your own way
            </a>
            are minimized.
           </p>
           <p>
            If you're grateful, you're
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="4" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Improving_Friendships/tp/5-Ways-To-Be-Happier-In-Your-Friendships.htm">
             happy with the time spent with a friend
            </a>
            so you focus on that instead of the fact that you won't see them again for a week.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             You Put More Effort Into Your Friendships
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Gratefulness helps you develop your
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="5" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Keeping-Friendships-Strong/a/How-Self-Esteem-Affects-Friendship.htm">
             self-esteem
            </a>
            , so you feel more confident
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="6" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Setting_Boundaries/f/I-Always-Have-To-Be-The-First-One-To-Ask-My-Friends-To-Do-Something.htm">
             being the one to call up friends more often
            </a>
            , or approach that new person and start up a conversation. You make the effort because you feel it is worth doing, regardless of what happens after.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             You Enjoy the Moment When You're With Your Friends
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            The whole concept behind our
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="7" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Friendships_Offline_Boot_Camp/a/Take-Friendship-Offline-Boot-Camp.htm">
             Friendship Boot Camp
            </a>
            was helping people see their friends in person more often. There is value in that. Face-to-face contact is so much more beneficial to your friendship than online communication.
           </p>
           <p>
            But gratitude makes time spent together even better, because you will be present, and not distracted by your busy life.
           </p>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="2" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense2">
           </div>
           <p class="cb-split">
            You'll
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="8" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Improving_Friendships/a/The-Importance-Of-Active-Listening-In-A-Friendship.htm">
             listen more fully
            </a>
            , and instead of "cramming in some friend time" to your schedule, you'll push the rest of the world away so you can just enjoy the moment with your friend.
           </p>
           <p>
            When you do this, you also have the benefit of re-living these types of moments later on. Instead of a blurred memory of seeing your friend quickly over coffee, you'll remember her smile, the funny joke he shared, or how she made you feel so happy over the fact that you have someone there to support you. This memory will be clearer because you'll have savored it while it happened.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             You Stop Assuming So Much About Friends
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            When you have a grateful attitude, you look at things more honestly. So if a friend does something nice for you, you appreciate what they did and leave it at that. You don't read more into the situation, or
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="9" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Keeping-Friendships-Strong/tp/5-Things-Never-To-Assume-About-A-Friend.htm">
             assume
            </a>
            that your friend is only being nice because they want something.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             You'll Be Less Jealous of Friends
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            If you've been
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="10" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Setting_Boundaries/a/5-Signs-Youre-Jealous-Of-Your-Friend.htm">
             jealous of a friend
            </a>
            in the past, gratitude can help with that. You'll appreciate your own individual blessings more, and as a result you won't feel threatened when other people succeed. You'll also be happy for friends more genuinely because you'll be in touch with your own level of contentment. Since you know what it's like to feel those small moments of joy, you'll be happy for your friend because they're able to experience the same type of positive emotions also.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             You'll Make Friends Easier
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Grateful people tend to give off a healthy vibe. They are very often folks who have that mysterious something that
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="11" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Making-New-Friends/ss/Characteristics-That-Make-It-Easier-To-Make-Friends.htm">
             draws people in
            </a>
            , and they make friends much more easily. This means they're more relaxed and natural when meeting people so their friendships tend to be more genuine.
           </p>
           <p>
            They're also the people you want to call up to share your good news or when you need to commiserate. They'll happily celebrate your success but they'll also point out the positives in a bleak situation. Either way, you seek them out.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             You'll Will Move on From Friendships That Have Ended More Gracefully
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            The
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="12" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Breaking_Up_With_a_Friend/Breaking-Up-With-A-Friend.htm">
             end of a friendship
            </a>
            , even a bad one, is rough. It's especially rough if you didn't see it coming. You can get caught up in the "why" of the end of the friendship and even begin to look at the relationship negatively. You might wonder if someone was a friend to you or not.
           </p>
           <p>
            The problem with this thinking is that friendships do tend to be temporary. You can have friends that stay in your life forever, and that's great. But the reality is that many of them don't. So appreciate your friends for whatever they bring to your life now. Cultivating gratitude will help you do that.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Do You Practice Gratefulness?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            If you need to get acquainted with gratefulness, consider starting a
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="13" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/Friendship-In-Culture/ht/How-To-Create-A-Friendship-Journal.htm">
             friendship gratitude journal
            </a>
            or blog about them and make a practice of recording your thoughts of gratitude on a regular basis.
           </p>
