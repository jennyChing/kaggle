       <div class="txt step-body">
        Professional bike repair stands run in hundreds of dollars. But I have seen a few DIY versions of those made from ground up with either PVC or steel plumbing pipes. They still cost you atleast $50. I wanted to make a quick and dirty stand with couple of things I already had at home. As bike owners most of you will have these too.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        At home I always hang my bike on the garage wall with couple of hooks. I had a couple of them to spare. And I transport my bike with a trunk mounted bike rack. These are the components I've used in this instructable.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        - 2 bike hanging hooks (like
        <a href="http://www.acehardwaresuperstore.com/bell-bike-storage-hooks-for-hanging-bikes-either-p-65583.html" rel="nofollow">
         these
        </a>
        )
        <br/>
        - 1 Trunk mounted bike rack like
        <a href="http://www.rei.com/product/767996" rel="nofollow">
         this
        </a>

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 1: Steps to build
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SAT900MFZQ1GP1S" data-entry-url="/id/Bike-Repair-Stand-Ultra-Cheap/step1/Steps-to-build/" id="photoset-SAT900MFZQ1GP1S">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SAT900MFZQ1GP1S" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FM7/VJMG/FYTCHXT5/FM7VJMGFYTCHXT5.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FM7VJMGFYTCHXT5" data-notes="" href="/file/FM7VJMGFYTCHXT5" id="photoset-link-FPF07J7FZQ1GPB5-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:600px;">
           <img alt="Picture of Steps to build" class="photoset-photo id_FM7VJMGFYTCHXT5 " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FM7/VJMG/FYTCHXT5/FM7VJMGFYTCHXT5.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        <strong>
         Steps:
        </strong>
        <br/>
        - Drill a couple of holes (slightly smaller in diameter to the hooks) in a sturdy part of the wall (maybe studs), just enough apart that the hooks can still hold the bike rack. Make sure they are high enough (say, slightly above eye level) from the floor, so the bike is at a comfortable working level and with sufficient clearance.
        <br/>
        - Screw the bike hooks into the holes, and end them facing up (duh)
        <br/>
        - Now mount your bike rack with top arm in the hooks, and the bottom arm supported by the wall itself.
        <br/>
        - Load your bike (as you would to transport it in your car), and work away.
        <br/>

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 2: Pros/Cons
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="S6FZLZ0FZQ1GP1T" data-entry-url="/id/Bike-Repair-Stand-Ultra-Cheap/step2/ProsCons/" id="photoset-S6FZLZ0FZQ1GP1T">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-S6FZLZ0FZQ1GP1T" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FX9/76LM/FZQ1GP9G/FX976LMFZQ1GP9G.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FX976LMFZQ1GP9G" data-notes="" href="/file/FX976LMFZQ1GP9G" id="photoset-link-F5BPWI2FZQ1GPB9-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="">
           <img alt="Picture of Pros/Cons" class="photoset-photo id_FX976LMFZQ1GP9G " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FX9/76LM/FZQ1GP9G/FX976LMFZQ1GP9G.MEDIUM.jpg" style="height:600px;"/>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        <strong>
         Advantages:
        </strong>
        <br/>
        - Pretty cheap. Only about $4 if you already have the bike rack.
        <br/>
        - Height is adjustable depending on your bike rack.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Limitations:
        </strong>
        <br/>
        - It is not portable, It is fixed in one place, choose it wisely :-)
        <br/>
        - It is close to the wall, when I want to work on the other side of the bike, I just flip it around.
        <br/>
        - The bike is not secured very tight, it will wobble when you run the wheel vigorously (like working on derailluers)
        <br/>
