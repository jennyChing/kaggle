         <div class="articleBody">
          <p>
           This is a city with numerous galleries and museums. Always do some pre-trip research and compile yourowntop ten listing of 'must do' activities. For helpful links, click on the name of the museum or attraction.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.folkartmuseum.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="American Folk Art Museum">
             American Folk Art Museum
            </a>
            ,
            <i>
             new location:
            </i>
           </b>
           One Broadway. Devoted to folk art in America over the ages, in the past, the collection included early American paintings, weather vanes, boxes, tools, samplers, advertising, handpainted furniture, and the work of many newer folk artists. Quaint, amusing, ingenious, beautiful.
          </p>
          <p>
           In transition, now, they are not charging an entrance fee and the gift shop sells miscellaneous trinkets and has very little to see. Maybe some quilts on the wall for decorations.
          </p>
          <p>
           Many of the things on display at the former location are on loan to the Metropolitan Museum of Art who also had purchased some of the things. They also had things on loan to other Museum for display.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.movingimage.us/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="American Museum of the Moving Image">
             American Museum of the Moving Image
            </a>
           </b>
           (in Astoria, Queens), 36th St. and 35th Ave. Movie/film lovers' paradise.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.amnh.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="American Museum of Natural History">
             American Museum of Natural History
            </a>
           </b>
           , Central Park West @ 79th St. Dinosaurs,rocks and gems, lizards, frogs, bears, whales, stars and so much more. A favorite of children from 2 to 102.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.asiasociety.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Asia Society and Museum">
             Asia Society and Museum
            </a>
           </b>
           , 725 Park Ave. @ 70th St. Directions: #6 train to 68th St. If you think you're not too interested in Asian art, literature, film or performing arts, check it out.
           <a href="http://www.asiasociety.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://www.asiasociety.org">
            http://www.asiasociety.org/
           </a>
           .Even if you don't have enough time to enjoy one of the exhibits, try to check out theMuseum store, one of the best in the City.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.bgc.bard.edu/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Bard Graduate Center for Studies in the Decorative Arts, Design and Culture">
             Bard Graduate Center for Studies in the Decorative Arts, Design and Culture
            </a>
           </b>
           18 West 86th Street.After you've taken in the dinosaurs and gems at the Am. Museum of Natural History, you may wanta "snack" of great design. The recent exhibition of Castellani jewels at this 3-floor townhouse was a baby blockbuster. Current exhibitions details on the website.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.aviewoncities.com/nyc/batterypark.htm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Battery Park">
            <b>
             Battery Park
            </b>
           </a>
           <b>
           </b>
           It is located at the bottom of Manhattan Island. This is the place that you must go through to embark on the ferries for the Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island. The # 1 Subway will get you there just fine. Also if you are on the tour buses that let you get off and on they stop at the edge of the park to pick you up again.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www1.bloomingdales.com/media/about/history.jsp" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Bloomingdale's Department Store">
            <b>
             Bloomingdale's Department Store
            </b>
           </a>
           <b>
            -
           </b>
           1000 Third Avenue ( 59th Street &amp; Lexington Avenue ). Phone: 212-705-2000.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadway" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Broadway">
             Broadway
            </a>
           </b>
           - Walk as much as possible along this famous street, one of the very oldest thoroughfares in the United States. Great synopsis of its history to be foundon the website.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.bronxzoo.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Bronx Zoo">
             Bronx Zoo
            </a>
            ,
           </b>
           Bronx River Pkwy at Fordham Rd. A children's magical landof all the wonderful animals they've never met.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.bbg.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Brooklyn Botanic Garden">
            <b>
             Brooklyn Botanic Garden
            </b>
           </a>
           <b>
            ,
           </b>
           900 Washington Ave., Bklyn. Takethe 2 or 3 subway to Eastern Parkway stop. Next door to the Bklyn Museum of Art,its grounds arethe site of many a New York wedding. Doesn't get more lovely than this in NYC.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.endex.com/gf/buildings/bbridge/bbridgefacts.htm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Brooklyn Bridge ">
             Brooklyn Bridge
            </a>
           </b>
           - Take the time to walk across the bridge. It doesn't take too long and provides excellent views of the city, especially at sunset. After sunset, no view of the City'slights is better.Subway 4, 5, 6 or J, M, Z trains to Chambers and Centre Sts. You can also get there on the #15 bus to get there and you will get to view of many different neighboorhoods like Chinatown. Also when you walk, make sure you stay on the walking path as the bikers get very upset when they almost hit you as the whiz by on their bikes going to and fro to work. Make sure you take water with you specially on sunny summer days when there is a heat wave. N.B. Very interesting web site with loads of facts and figures.
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.brooklynmuseum.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Brooklyn Museum of Art ">
             Brooklyn Museum of Art
            </a>
           </b>
           , 200 Eastern Parkway, Bklyn. (Subway same as for Brooklyn Botanic Garden) Housing one of the most extensive collections of Egyptian art in the world, outside Egypt, the BMA is quieter than most Manhattan museums, serves a delicious lunch, and is right next door to the romantic Bklyn Botanic Garden.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.carnegiehall.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Carnegie Hall">
             Carnegie Hall
            </a>
            -
           </b>
           57th Street &amp; 7th Avenue
           <b>
            .
           </b>
           Visit this website and take a virtual tour of this famous building.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.stjohndivine.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Cathedral of St. John the Divine">
             Cathedral of St. John the Divine
            </a>
            ,
           </b>
           1047 Amsterdam Ave @ 112th St. Take 1 or 9 train to 110th St.. Couple yourvisit with Columbia University (4 blocks North at 116th St.). World's largest cathedral (St. Peter's Basilica is technically not a cathedral) -- awesome.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.centralparknyc.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Central Park">
             Central Park
            </a>
            .
           </b>
           Bounded on the South by59th St. (SouthWest corner -- Columbus Circle and Central Park West; SouthEast corner --5th Ave.), running an astounding 50 blocks North to 110th St. (both East and West).
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.aviewoncities.com/nyc/chrysler.htm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Chrysler Building">
            <b>
             Chrysler Building
            </b>
           </a>
           <b>
            -
           </b>
           It is located at 405 Lexington Ave. Subway 4, 5, 6 to 42nd Grand Central.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.circleline.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Circle Line Cruises / Ferry ">
            <b>
             Circle Line Cruises/Ferry
            </b>
           </a>
           <b>
            -
           </b>
           This site has lots of information about fares and schedules.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.aviewoncities.com/nyc/cityhall.htm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="City Hall">
             City Hall
            </a>
           </b>
           - It faces south to the City Hall Park. It islocated in Lower Manhattan on Murray Street between Broadway and Park Row.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.metmuseum.org/events/ev_cloisters.asp?HomePageLink=collections_cloisters_l" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="The Cloisters of the Metropolitan Museum of Art">
            <b>
             The Cloisters
            </b>
           </a>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.metmuseum.org/events/ev_cloisters.asp?HomePageLink=collections_cloisters_l" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="The Cloisters of the Metropolitan Museum of Art">
             of the Metropolitan Museum of Art
            </a>
            -
           </b>
           located in Fort Tryon Park.A separate site of the Metropolitan devoted exclusively to European medieval art. Directions.A subway to 190th St., then change forM4 bus.At theCloisters you will feel like you're back in time. Undisturbed peace, quiet,spirit.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.columbia.edu/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Columbia University in the City of New York">
