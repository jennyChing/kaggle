           <p>
            Across the planet, new technologies and business modelsare decentralizing power and placing it in the hands of communities and individuals.
           </p>
           <p>
            "We are seeing technology-driven networks replacingbureacratically-driven hierarchies," says VC and futurist Fred Wilson, speaking on what to expect in the next ten years.View the entire 25-minute video below (it's worth it!) and then check out the 21 innovations below. Thanks to
            <a href="https://sudoroom.org/wiki/User:Tunabananas">
             Jenny Ryan
            </a>
            whoseOpen Garden newsletter inspired this post.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <div class="media-youtube-video media-image media-youtube-1">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="media-youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="340" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/R43OKYmGbhU?wmode=opaque" title="R43OKYmGbhU" width="560">
             Video of R43OKYmGbhU
            </iframe>
           </div>
           <p>
            Here are 21innovations that will help make it happen:
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             1.
             <a href="http://opengarden.com/">
              Open Garden
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Screenshot%202014-02-13%2022.52.07.png?itok=RU7bihOT" style="width: 480px; height: 230px;" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Decentralized technology will become
            <a href="http://opengarden.net/2013/12/2014-decentralized-technology/">
             mainstream in 2014
            </a>
            , according to the
            <a href="http://opengarden.net/about-us/">
             Open Garden Foundation
            </a>
            , a San Francisco-based startup dedicated to net neutrality and internet access for everyone.Open Garden, a product of the Open Garden Foundation, provides mesh networking through an app that creates a wirelesh mesh network, enabling everyone to have faster downloads, and stronger and cheaper connectivity.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             2.
             <a href="http://commotionwireless.net/">
              Commotion Router
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/download.jpeg?itok=FbuK9S3C" style="width: 480px; height: 332px;" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Commotion Router is free, open source software that allows for communities to build their own mesh networks.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             3.
             <a href="http://twister.net.co/">
              Twister
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Twister_dial.jpg?itok=kG-S3sBb" style="width: 500px; height: 500px;" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Do you really want to trust your data to Twitter?
            <a href="http://twister.net.co/">
             Twister
            </a>
            is a peer-to-peeralternative social network operating on a decentralized framework, designed in a way that prevents other usersfrom knowing too much about your whereabouts and online habits.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             4.
             <a href="https://www.google.com/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=1&amp;cad=rja&amp;ved=0CCkQFjAA&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.intel.com%2Fcontent%2Fwww%2Fus%2Fen%2Fdo-it-yourself%2Fedison.html&amp;ei=pVX-Ut_iK8nOyQGu44CICg&amp;usg=AFQjCNHEAgBeKXPGh0svBkeeHv5gJlGjDw&amp;sig2=mhapOD0l1YG2esZPYlPHkw&amp;bvm=bv.61190604,d.aWc">
              The Edison
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/edison.jpg?itok=XQub_eZ3" style="width: 500px; height: 333px;" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            The future of devices is micro and wearable.Intel announced this SD-sizedcomputer in January, and bloggers are already talking about the potential for
            <a href="http://readwrite.com/2014/01/07/meet-edison-intel-tiny-plan-to-power-the-next-generation-of-wearables#awesm=~ovRVO4gyjarp6M">
             freedom from mobile
            </a>
            devices and
            <a href="http://readwrite.com/2014/01/08/intel-edison-tiny-computers-everywhere#awesm=~ovRITUX0IKzN2e">
             making dumb gadgets smart
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             5.
             <a href="https://github.com/wetube/bitcloud/blob/master/bitcloud.org">
              BitCloud
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/cloud.jpeg?itok=xSzrjfVo" style="width: 420px; height: 245px;" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            BitCloud is only a concept for now, but it's basically a
            <a href="http://www.wired.co.uk/news/archive/2014-01/27/bitcloud?_tmc=SJuEhvST5aU6IiIbDDYL4zepC1PoiQ5bJX0Nbz93V1M">
             proposal to replace the entire internet
            </a>
            with a decentralized system.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             6.
             <a href="http://readwrite.com/tag/internet-of-things#awesm=~ovWesGKwW7oar9">
              The Internet of Things (IoT)
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/20130522%20AMS-IX_infographic%20Internet%20of%20Things%20final.jpeg?itok=JXKdSMZE" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            The number of "things" that will be connected to the Internet of things is about to explode,
            <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/growth-in-the-internet-of-things-market-2-2014-2">
             reports
            </a>
            Business Insider. The smartification of formerly "dumb" objects like parking meters, home thermostats, appliances, and automobilesis set to rival the number of computers and mobile devices currently connected to the internet, creating trillions in economic value.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             7.
             <a href="https://www.dragoninnovation.com/projects/35-wunderbar-by-relayr">
              WunderBar
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/wundebar.jpeg?itok=UqYRPjpR" style="width: 600px; height: 343px;" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            WunderBar is a starter kit for developers, hackers and makers looking to test out ideas and contribute to the Internet of Things. Created by European startup
            <a href="http://www.relayr.de/">
             relayr
            </a>
            , the kit will include six modules with sensors for temperature, distance, light, color, humidity, a gyroscope, an accelerometer, a infrared remote control,a Grove connector, and one additional sensorto be determined by a crowdfunding campaign vote.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             8.
             <a href="https://reg.wirelessregistry.com/">
              The Wireless Registry
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/wirelssreg.png?itok=n9qdIdZC" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            In the Internet of Things, each thing will need an address and a name, just like regular computers and mobile devices. While the regular Internet associates URLs with IPaddresses via the DNS system, The Wireless RegistryassociatesService Set Identifiers(SSIDs) with 32-character names that convey the users identity in the wireless world.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             9.
             <a href="https://dotbit.me/">
              Dot-Bit
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Namecoin_Logo_2.png?itok=TmAclvVl" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Based on cryptocurrency software (see next section),
            <a href="https://www.namecoin.org/">
             Namecoin
            </a>
            is an alternative domain name registration system (and potentially an alternative internet) for .bit top-level domain. It operatesindependently of
            <a href="https://www.google.com/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=1&amp;cad=rja&amp;ved=0CCkQFjAA&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.icann.org%2F&amp;ei=9hcAU5T8MfDLsQTB94HwBw&amp;usg=AFQjCNHLbyAW_-BRZqFSzs-9VZre6ifgcQ&amp;sig2=hLK09Nz9otgJPxHdOyaQPA&amp;bvm=bv.61535280,d.cWc">
             ICANN
            </a>
            , and because it is based on adecentralized network, it is theoretically immune from internet censorship.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             10.
             <a href="https://bitcoin.org/en/">
              Bitcoin
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Cryptocurrency.jpg?itok=a40t5XIG" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Cryptocurrencyis adigital medium of exchange not controlled by any one group or agency and secured by cryptography.Bitcoin has been trading since 2009, and in recent years more than 60 currencies have been launched.
            <a href="http://www.coindesk.com/information/what-is-bitcoin/">
             Here's a good layman's guide to cryptocurrencies
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             11.
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.ethereum.org/">
              Ethereum
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Screenshot%202014-02-15%2020.40.44.png?itok=DIJV-QTT" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Ethereum is a decentralized, cryptographic ledger that allows users to encode transactions, contracts and applications, including custom currencies and financial derivatives.The tool includes its ownprogramming language to create custom transactions.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             12. Smart Contracts
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/smartcontracts.jpg?itok=W2EA03FD" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            What if contracts could enforce themselves, obviating the need for contract law and lawyers? The idea of smart contracts was
            <a href="http://szabo.best.vwh.net/smart.contracts.html">
             introduced by Nick Szabo
            </a>
            in the1990s, but recent advancements in cryptography may be moving the
            <a href="http://siliconangle.com/blog/2014/02/12/can-ethereum-kill-bitcoin-with-self-executing-contracts/">
             idea closer to reality
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             13.Smart Property
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Commercial-Eviction-for-Unpaid-Rent.jpg?itok=cPiVmy8S" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            What if the property behind property and lease contracts ceased to function or be accessible, obviating the need for repo men and lengthy eviction processes? An important subset of smart contracts is
            <a href="http://www.coindesk.com/smart-property-colored-coins-mastercoin/">
             smart property
            </a>
            , built on self-executing contracts atopcryptocurrency frameworks.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             14. Peer toPeer Payments
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Chord_route.png?itok=-_GCbl30" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Services like
            <a href="https://square.com/cash">
             Square Cash
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="https://venmo.com/">
             VenMo
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://www.dwolla.com/">
             Dwolla
            </a>
            are cutting out the bank by creating a platform and framework for online payments between peers.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             15. Peer to Peer Lending
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/lending.jpeg?itok=g_rlFBHD" style="width: 480px; height: 318px;" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Services like
            <a href="https://www.lendingclub.com/">
             Lending Club
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.zopa.com/">
             Zopa
            </a>
            , and
            <a href="http://www.prosper.com/">
             Prosper
            </a>
            are cutting out institutional lenders and bigbanksby enabling people to make loans directly to each other.
            <a href="http://www.lendacademy.com/p2p-lenders-2013-loan-volume/">
             Over $2.4 billion in peer-to-peerloans were issued in 2013
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             16.
            </strong>
            <strong>
             Crowdsourcing Civic Engagement
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Screenshot%202014-02-15%2006.34.09.png?itok=eckBiwvY" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            What ifgetting your city planner's attention was as easy as tacking anote to a digital bulletin board? With crowdsourcing platforms like
            <a href="http://dearcity.org/">
             Dear City
            </a>
            ,citizens can do just that, and others can up-vote or down-vote the concern.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             17. Civic Crowdfunding
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Screenshot%202014-02-15%2023.00.02.png?itok=KP7gzwDQ" style="width: 500px; height: 308px;" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            With public budgets slashed, urban planners and community groups are turning to sites like
            <a href="http://www.citizinvestor.com/">
             Citizinvestor
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.citysourced.com/zenfunder">
             ZenFunder
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://www.google.com/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=3&amp;cad=rja&amp;ved=0CD8QFjAC&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fneighbor.ly%2F&amp;ei=1yYAU6Aj5tSwBMrlgOgP&amp;usg=AFQjCNGnoPrAH4cubiG2uGpwD51avUiRRg&amp;sig2=iocrv8X3sb4xWEcWzGRC-g&amp;bvm=bv.61535280,d.cWc">
             neighbor.ly
            </a>
            to pay forcivic projects and infrastructure.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             18. Decentralized Urban Farming
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Tobago_herb_garden.jpg?itok=g7-JFghi" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Our food system is extremely centralized; Walmart sells 25 percent of all the groceries in the United States, and that number is far higher in rural areas.Services like
            <a href="http://www.bkfarmyards.com/">
             BK Farmyards
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://landshare.channel4.com/">
             LandShare
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://hyperlocavore.ning.com/">
             hyperlocavore
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.sharingbackyards.com/">
             Sharing Backyards
            </a>
            use social media and Google maps to connect urban landowners with urban farmers. But this is just scratching the surface, an entirealternative agriculture system is emerging that's based on local production, processing, and distribution.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             19. FarmHack
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <div class="media-vimeo-outer-wrapper" id="media-vimeo-1" style="width: 480px; height: 360px;">
            <div class="media-vimeo-preview-wrapper" id="media_vimeo_73504647_1">

              </embed>
             </object>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            <a href="https://www.google.com/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=1&amp;cad=rja&amp;ved=0CCkQFjAA&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Ffarmhack.net%2F&amp;ei=qkwDU-LfEs_J0AGI_YCIBA&amp;usg=AFQjCNFhOg_aHoMMG-rcld8SKXT4zeACNQ&amp;sig2=YDaMBnjakUWbFNeslBXyDw&amp;bvm=bv.61535280,d.dmQ">
             FarmHack
            </a>
            is an open-source, online platform for peer-to-peer innovation targeted toward developing tools and systems for resilent, sustainable agriculture.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             20. MOOCS and Online Learning Platforms
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/bigstock_Online_Education_11092172.jpg?itok=8qNxKvl3" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Technology-based
            <a href="http://www.ciesin.columbia.edu/decentralization/English/Issues/Education.html">
             decentralization of education
            </a>
            promises to be a game-changing, disruptive force for the future.Massive Online Open Courses (MOOCS) have the potential to be
            <a href="http://www.slate.com/articles/life/education/2014/02/degree_of_freedom_project_earning_a_one_year_b_a_through_moocs.html">
             great equalizers
            </a>
            , decentralizing education to the extent that collegebecome accessible to everyone, not just those who can afford it,while online learning communities like
            <a href="https://www.duolingo.com/">
             Duolingo
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.codecademy.com/">
             CodeAcademy
            </a>
            have established peer networks to help people learn from each other.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             21. Coming Soon: Identity, Trust and Data
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="media-image" src="http://www.shareable.net/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Screenshot%202014-02-16%2000.02.35.png?itok=VyuILacO" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            When we use Facebook or Twitter to login, we give our data over to Facebook and Twitter in exchange for the convenience of not having to remember a password. Fred Wilson believes we will see a Bitcoin-like protocol for internet identity in the very near future. Stay tuned.
           </p>
