             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://www.gwennypenny.com/2011/04/out-to-eat-place-mat-tutorial.html">
               Out to Eat Place Mat Tutorial
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              I guest posted on
              <a href="http://www.liberatecreativity.com/" target="blank">
               Liberate Creativity
              </a>
              with this tutorial a few weeks ago...
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Today I'm going to share a sewing tutorial for an "Out to Eat Place Mat". I'm guilty of feeding my 14-month-old daughter her finger food right on the table at restaurants. Gross? Yes... have you seen them wipe down the tables before? I decided to make a place mat that I could easily tuck in my bag to bring with us when we go out to a restaurant.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-oRL2ED4AzCA/TZJ-mVrs98I/AAAAAAAABY8/nJr3DNNPPiY/s1600/IMG_4214.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-oRL2ED4AzCA/TZJ-mVrs98I/AAAAAAAABY8/nJr3DNNPPiY/s1600/IMG_4214.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              I included a little pocket to hold some toddler-sized silverware and ties to wrap the entire place mat up into a compact little roll.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ya4zgS2j_Kk/TZFMabDn8KI/AAAAAAAABWo/haIgVjyTDtM/s1600/IMG_4202-Z.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ya4zgS2j_Kk/TZFMabDn8KI/AAAAAAAABWo/haIgVjyTDtM/s1600/IMG_4202-Z.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              By the way, did you know that place mat is two words? Looked it up in the dictionary... who knew?
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Want to make one for your little one? Here's the how-to:
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Materials:
              </b>
              <br/>
              <ul>
               <li>
                front fabric (12" x 18")
               </li>
               <li>
                back fabric (12" x 18")
               </li>
               <li>
                pocket fabric (6" x 9")
               </li>
               <li>
                double fold bias tape (purchased or
                <a href="http://www.dana-made-it.com/2008/07/technique-understanding-bias-and-making.html" target="blank">
                 make your own
                </a>
                ) (2.5 yds)
               </li>
               <li>
                light to medium weight fusible interfacing (11.5" x 17.5")
               </li>
               <li>
                thread
               </li>
              </ul>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Instructions:
              </b>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 1:
              </i>
              Cut out all of your fabric to the dimensions listed in the materials list above.Center your fusible interfacing on the
              <i>
               wrong side
              </i>
              of the fabric you are using for the front of your place mat, and iron it in place following the manufacturer's directions.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-BfZRDYTfW6I/TZFR4iRzbSI/AAAAAAAABW0/lq6Bo2RF7Mo/s1600/IMG_4145.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="242" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-BfZRDYTfW6I/TZFR4iRzbSI/AAAAAAAABW0/lq6Bo2RF7Mo/s400/IMG_4145.jpg" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 2:
              </i>
              Next you need to prepare the pocket. Take your pocket fabric and fold it in half (bringing the shorter sides together) with
              <i>
               right sides together
              </i>
              . Sew around the three open sides, using a 1/2" seam allowance and leaving a 2" opening for turning.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-cBQf_KTIKTA/TZFSAXm23ZI/AAAAAAAABW4/22dZKrs1bZM/s1600/IMG_4149.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-cBQf_KTIKTA/TZFSAXm23ZI/AAAAAAAABW4/22dZKrs1bZM/s1600/IMG_4149.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              Clip the corners, trim the seams down, and turn the pocket right side out. Press the pocket, making sure to tuck the edges of the opening inside the pocket.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nDMg7i4qt3w/TZFSLdgVnCI/AAAAAAAABW8/c29m_AlmhEA/s1600/IMG_4153.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nDMg7i4qt3w/TZFSLdgVnCI/AAAAAAAABW8/c29m_AlmhEA/s1600/IMG_4153.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 3:
              </i>
              Sew across the top of the pocket (the folded edge) about a 1/4" from the top edge.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rZJI7XqQaB4/TZFSh2DR7CI/AAAAAAAABXA/cFUtt4VSj1E/s1600/IMG_4154.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rZJI7XqQaB4/TZFSh2DR7CI/AAAAAAAABXA/cFUtt4VSj1E/s1600/IMG_4154.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 4:
              </i>
              Mark the center of the pocket by drawing a line from top to bottom (I used a water soluble disappearing ink pen).Pin the pocket in place on the front of your place mat. The top of the pocket should be 4" down from the top of the place mat, and the left side of the pocket should be 1.5" from the left edge of the place mat. Sew around the left, bottom, and right sides of the pocket (1/4" from edges), then sew along the line you drew down the center of the pocket.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-BGb17hoiRTY/TZFTCwJts9I/AAAAAAAABXE/tOF-e3Q0Glk/s1600/IMG_4155.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-BGb17hoiRTY/TZFTCwJts9I/AAAAAAAABXE/tOF-e3Q0Glk/s1600/IMG_4155.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-jdzNBLgK--E/TZFTNQkdvMI/AAAAAAAABXI/Vbc048FLyPs/s1600/IMG_4160.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-jdzNBLgK--E/TZFTNQkdvMI/AAAAAAAABXI/Vbc048FLyPs/s1600/IMG_4160.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 5:
              </i>
              Take your front and back pieces of place mat fabric and line them up with
              <i>
               wrong sides together
              </i>
              . Baste (using a very long running stitch) all around the outside edges using a 1/8"-1/4" seam allowance.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Z7aXuimF35M/TZFTTycKJLI/AAAAAAAABXM/rGH80-3PZHM/s1600/IMG_4161.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Z7aXuimF35M/TZFTTycKJLI/AAAAAAAABXM/rGH80-3PZHM/s1600/IMG_4161.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 6:
              </i>
              Make sure you reset your machine to a shorter stitch length. Before you sew on the binding, you can make some little ties to add to the place mat so that you can roll it up. Cut a piece of bias tape that is 24" long. Open up one end of the bias tape. Fold in the end about 1/4".
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1yyjpyJZHvg/TZFTj4HSzhI/AAAAAAAABXQ/32S7i6NVkYM/s1600/IMG_4174.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1yyjpyJZHvg/TZFTj4HSzhI/AAAAAAAABXQ/32S7i6NVkYM/s1600/IMG_4174.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              Refold the bias tape. Repeat on the other end.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-xX-6bG7-acU/TZKAF5N_aXI/AAAAAAAABZA/bBHqkwklrxs/s1600/IMG_4176.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-xX-6bG7-acU/TZKAF5N_aXI/AAAAAAAABZA/bBHqkwklrxs/s1600/IMG_4176.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              Sew the ends and length of the bias tape closed by sewing very close to the edge. Set this piece aside.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-16i47SY7WMs/TZKAOqMr0WI/AAAAAAAABZE/rFrKKJ3N7Yw/s1600/IMG_4180.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-16i47SY7WMs/TZKAOqMr0WI/AAAAAAAABZE/rFrKKJ3N7Yw/s1600/IMG_4180.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ktd3qIrF1cc/TZFT_4-Yc-I/AAAAAAAABXc/YrwbcZf1NwU/s1600/IMG_4181.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ktd3qIrF1cc/TZFT_4-Yc-I/AAAAAAAABXc/YrwbcZf1NwU/s1600/IMG_4181.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 7:
              </i>
              Time to bind your place mat. Take your bias tape and open it up. Starting along the bottom edge of the back of the place mat, pin one edge of your open bias tape even with the raw edge of the place mat. Leave about a 6" tail of bias tape at the very beginning.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-wXP7DKeukcA/TZFUrojf5wI/AAAAAAAABXg/BPdBnPW4q5g/s1600/IMG_4185.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-wXP7DKeukcA/TZFUrojf5wI/AAAAAAAABXg/BPdBnPW4q5g/s1600/IMG_4185.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              Sew right in the crease of your bias tape that is closest to the outside edge of the place mat. Stop sewing and backstitch 1/4" before you reach the edge of the adjacent side of the place mat.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-UX63z7tZ8dk/TZKBQEKkV9I/AAAAAAAABZQ/0gH9GKisOG8/s1600/IMG_4186.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-UX63z7tZ8dk/TZKBQEKkV9I/AAAAAAAABZQ/0gH9GKisOG8/s1600/IMG_4186.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              Fold your bias tape up at a 90 degree angle.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ob7darUOdMY/TZFVVWzgy1I/AAAAAAAABXo/-IxSiyiRHpY/s1600/IMG_4187.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ob7darUOdMY/TZFVVWzgy1I/AAAAAAAABXo/-IxSiyiRHpY/s1600/IMG_4187.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <span class="Apple-style-span" style="background-color: white;">
               Fold the bias tape back down over itself. Start pinning the bias tape along this side, even with the raw edge of the place mat.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-y9xMFxfw7e4/TZKAZxR_x4I/AAAAAAAABZI/7XI5btI5Jbg/s1600/IMG_4188.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-y9xMFxfw7e4/TZKAZxR_x4I/AAAAAAAABZI/7XI5btI5Jbg/s1600/IMG_4188.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 8:
              </i>
              If you are now at the side of the place mat opposite the side with the pocket, you need to attach the ties you made earlier. Fold the 24" piece of bias tape in half and slip the folded edge under the bias tape you are attaching around the place mat, centering the ties on the side of the place mat.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-9O5RDT1HG94/TZFZmiWAq_I/AAAAAAAABYM/WHsPyksS8v4/s1600/IMG_4183.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-9O5RDT1HG94/TZFZmiWAq_I/AAAAAAAABYM/WHsPyksS8v4/s1600/IMG_4183.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 9:
              </i>
              Start sewing in the outermost crease of the bias tape again, starting 1/4" from the edge of the place mat and stopping 1/4" from the opposite end. Repeat the folding from
              <i>
               Step 7
              </i>
              for all four corners. Stitch back and forth over the tie attachment a few times.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 10:
              </i>
              When you get all four corners done, continue sewing the bias tape along the bottom of the place mat, stopping when you are about 6" from where you started attaching your bias tape.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/--umEE-nEooQ/TZFWdV12m0I/AAAAAAAABXw/dyGbqjD7nDE/s1600/IMG_4189.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/--umEE-nEooQ/TZFWdV12m0I/AAAAAAAABXw/dyGbqjD7nDE/s1600/IMG_4189.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 11:
              </i>
              Fold over each tail of your bias tape so that they touch in the center. Pin in place and finger press.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aorDHHlUjWA/TZFWs8JiJNI/AAAAAAAABX0/fYtiHws8Sic/s1600/IMG_4190.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aorDHHlUjWA/TZFWs8JiJNI/AAAAAAAABX0/fYtiHws8Sic/s1600/IMG_4190.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              Remove the pins and match up the creases you just made with
              <i>
               right sides together
              </i>
              . Pin.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-AcI2ekXwgxs/TZFXDIylwkI/AAAAAAAABX4/kf7t9f-Qk80/s1600/IMG_4191.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-AcI2ekXwgxs/TZFXDIylwkI/AAAAAAAABX4/kf7t9f-Qk80/s1600/IMG_4191.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              Sew across the crease you just made (I lightly marked it with a pen in the photo above). Trim the seam to 1/4", and press the seam open.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-hmpWYPWWsow/TZFXYrHoIII/AAAAAAAABX8/Y4-GrO4COoQ/s1600/IMG_4192.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-hmpWYPWWsow/TZFXYrHoIII/AAAAAAAABX8/Y4-GrO4COoQ/s1600/IMG_4192.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 12:
              </i>
              Line up the edge of the bias tape along the raw edge of the place mat. Finish attaching the bias tape by sewing in the outermost crease of the bias tape again.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-JnjOUEH3zGY/TZFY4xM7nFI/AAAAAAAABYI/1XoLaqXkio0/s1600/IMG_4193.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-JnjOUEH3zGY/TZFY4xM7nFI/AAAAAAAABYI/1XoLaqXkio0/s1600/IMG_4193.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Step 13:
              </i>
              Now that your bias tape is attached all the way around the outside, it's time to fold it over to the front of your place mat. Refold the bias tape and pull it over the edge of the place mat to the front so that it just covers your stitches. I use LOTS of pins to hold bias tape in place.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AC9-eXEBygk/TZFaGSDn5OI/AAAAAAAABYQ/utPqDlcLi4w/s1600/IMG_4194.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AC9-eXEBygk/TZFaGSDn5OI/AAAAAAAABYQ/utPqDlcLi4w/s1600/IMG_4194.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              Carefully miter each corner. It may take a few tries to get it right.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-hTSBQeDp5UE/TZFawhe4iQI/AAAAAAAABYU/WkUd_v_bZNs/s1600/IMG_4196.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-hTSBQeDp5UE/TZFawhe4iQI/AAAAAAAABYU/WkUd_v_bZNs/s1600/IMG_4196.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ItIaNF1B6LU/TZFa5_9pWSI/AAAAAAAABYY/_e1kYmM9U1k/s1600/IMG_4198.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ItIaNF1B6LU/TZFa5_9pWSI/AAAAAAAABYY/_e1kYmM9U1k/s1600/IMG_4198.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               This is what the back corners will look like when you fold the bias tape over to the front...
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-uTvSrBEyJbw/TZFbJP2RksI/AAAAAAAABYc/lUPw0oTZnXs/s1600/IMG_4199.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-uTvSrBEyJbw/TZFbJP2RksI/AAAAAAAABYc/lUPw0oTZnXs/s1600/IMG_4199.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <i>
               Step 14:
              </i>
              Once you have the bias tape pinned to the front all the way around, sew the bias tape down close to the inside edge of the bias tape. (
              <i>
               TIP:
              </i>
              Pin your ties to the center of your place mat so they don't get caught up in the bias tape when sewing.)
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-e8VOoi2zzfk/TZKAnrtdziI/AAAAAAAABZM/QU-qfdm9V7A/s1600/IMG_4200.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-e8VOoi2zzfk/TZKAnrtdziI/AAAAAAAABZM/QU-qfdm9V7A/s1600/IMG_4200.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <span class="Apple-style-span" style="background-color: white;">
               You're all done! Now insert some silverware, fold the top and bottom edges toward the center, roll it up, and tie it.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-c_pKhOSQENU/TZFdCfkUlOI/AAAAAAAABYs/ZnVour4F7bs/s1600/IMG_4202.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-c_pKhOSQENU/TZFdCfkUlOI/AAAAAAAABYs/ZnVour4F7bs/s1600/IMG_4202.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ELr-V4tOqRw/TZFcTpe2EEI/AAAAAAAABYk/PRVgr_TNrwQ/s1600/IMG_4206.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ELr-V4tOqRw/TZFcTpe2EEI/AAAAAAAABYk/PRVgr_TNrwQ/s1600/IMG_4206.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BoZg6D4Yxog/TZFcZyD0K0I/AAAAAAAABYo/xWfNYmzRkV0/s1600/IMG_4207.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BoZg6D4Yxog/TZFcZyD0K0I/AAAAAAAABYo/xWfNYmzRkV0/s1600/IMG_4207.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <span class="Apple-style-span" style="background-color: white;">
               Now you have a cute little place mat to bring with you to the restaurant for your little one, and you don't have to worry about what was on the table before you sat down with your family. Cover that place mat with Goldfish crackers and enjoy!
              </span>
              <br/>

               <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-t4Dn_hyBSEU/TY_0uUq8L2I/AAAAAAAABVg/IKUdwt73OxE/s1600/Gwen_New_Signature.png"/>
