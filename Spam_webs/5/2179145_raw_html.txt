         AUSTRIA reforms its 1912 Islam Law; Muslims outraged
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <h2 class="introduction" id="story_continues_1">
         <img alt="982057322-Anti-Islam-Group-PEGIDA-Holds-First-Austria-March" class="alignright size-full wp-image-285501" height="203" src="http://www.barenakedislam.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/982057322-Anti-Islam-Group-PEGIDA-Holds-First-Austria-March-e1424976656336.jpg" width="360"/>
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
          The Austrian parliament has passed controversial reforms to the countrys century-old law on Islam.The bill, which is partly aimed at tackling Islamic radicalism, bans foreign funding for mosques and imams.Austrian Foreign Minister Sebastian Kurz: We dont want our Muslim community to be dependent on foreign funding.
         </span>
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_285502" style="width: 760px">
         <img alt="Muslims in Austria protest the new laws as discriminatory to muslims. So leave!" class="size-full wp-image-285502" height="507" src="http://www.barenakedislam.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/1424891670-h_51815177-1.jpg" width="750"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          <strong>
           Muslims in Austria protest the new laws as discriminatory to muslims. So leave!
          </strong>
         </p>
        </div>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #073763;">
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-31629543" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-31629543', 'BBC']);">
            BBC
           </a>
          </span>
          <span style="color: #999999;">
           (h/t Mark Christians)
          </span>
          Austrias Integration Minister, Sebastian Kurz, defended the reforms but Muslim leaders say they fail to treat them equally.The 1912 law made Islam an official religion in Austria.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #073763;">
          Muslim groups say the ban on foreign funding of msoques is unfair as international support is still permitted for the Christian and Jewish faiths. They say the legalization reflects a widespread mistrust of Muslims and some are planning to contest it in the constitutional court.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #073763;">
          Mr Kurz told the BBC the reforms were a milestone for Austria and aimed to stop certain Muslim countries using financial means to exert political influence.What we want is to reduce the political influence and control from abroad and we want to give Islam the chance to develop freely within our society and in line with our common European values, he said.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #073763;">
          Meanwhile the legislation has drawn wide reaction from Muslims across the world, with Turkeys head of religious affairs, Mehmet Gormez, adding his condemnation on Tuesday.Austria will go back 100 years in freedom with its Islam bill, Mr Gormez said, according to Turkeys state-funded Anadolu news agency.
          <span style="color: #ff0000;">
           <em>
            (One can only hope)
           </em>
          </span>
         </span>
        </h4>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #073763;">
          Roughly half a million Muslims live in Austria today, around 6% of the population. Many of them have Turkish or Bosnian roots.
         </span>
        </h4>
