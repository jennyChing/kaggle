       Natalie Martinez Talks Broken City Blu-Ray And Video Games
      </h1>

      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         <a href="http://b-i.forbesimg.com/johngaudiosi/files/2013/05/natalie-martinez.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-5335" height="199" src="http://b-i.forbesimg.com/johngaudiosi/files/2013/05/natalie-martinez-300x199.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         Actress Natalie Martinez has been busy working on the new Steven Spielberg and Stephen King collaboration for CBS, Under the Dome. She plays a cop trying to come to terms with the mysterious dome that has cut her town off from the rest of the world in the June TV series.
        </p>
        <p>
         Martinez is also starring in the new 20
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         Century Fox Home Entertainment Blu-ray and DVD release Broken City, alongside Mark Wahlberg and Russell Crowe. She worked with director Allen Hughes on the big screen feature. The actress, who also stars in CSI New York and has appeared in Hollywood films like End of Watch and Death Race, talks about her love of video games in this exclusive interview.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What was it was like working on Broken City?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         It was kind of a crazy experience. I got to meet Russell Crowe and Mark Wahlberg and Catherine Zeta-Jones and most of the cast and everybody was just great. Knowing that Im part of a film with such great actors of such a high caliber, it really puts you on youre a game. I worked a lot with Mark Wahlberg. He was very comfortable, very cool with Alan (Hughes). He was with a great director too, and you see that with the actors. We had a lot of rehearsal time, and it was actually really cool working on that film and it was definitely a learning experience.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Whats the atmosphere like on the set when youre on a movie like that and what do you guys do when youre not acting?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Theyll get you ready for what youre going to do. You talk amongst yourselves and you hang out and sometimes late nights and things like that you get a little delirious and start playing these little jokes. The kind you cant really explain but need to be there for. Everybody just kind of gets along together from working 12 to 14 hours a day sometimes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://b-i.forbesimg.com/johngaudiosi/files/2013/05/natalie-martinez-premiere-end-of-watch-01.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-5358" height="300" src="http://b-i.forbesimg.com/johngaudiosi/files/2013/05/natalie-martinez-premiere-end-of-watch-01-200x300.jpg" width="200"/>
          </a>
          When you were making Broken City did you play video games with anyone?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Myself and some of the producers on Broken City and were playing Cogs on iPhone. Its this game where you have to turn the wheels so that everything works properly. Its just kind of crazy.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How does the ability to play games now on iPhone and tablets help kill time when you have a lot of breaks on set?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Just imagine sitting in your trailer for an hour with no game. It helps you immensely. Youre occupied. Youre doing something and the next thing you know, that hour comes so much quicker youre so involved in your game youre like, Okay, Ill be there in five minutes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What games do you like to play?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I like to play Battleship and I also like Wordle on iPhone. These are good things to play while youre on set. Words with Friends is also great.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Did you play video games growing up?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I think the last game console I had was Super Nintendo. I remember once I played the Sega Genesis. But Super Nintendo was my last game device. I played outside more. I liked kickball and baseball. I liked playing outside in the neighborhood and stuff and by the time I had been through middle school and high school, we were hanging out at friends houses and we were doing things. But I have a Playstation now and an Xbox and I mostly have the games for when people come over.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What do you play when people come over?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Dance Dance Revolution. Thats a lot of fun when youre having a dinner party and you just want to be a little silly.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://b-i.forbesimg.com/johngaudiosi/files/2013/05/chunli.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-5359" height="168" src="http://b-i.forbesimg.com/johngaudiosi/files/2013/05/chunli.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          When you had the old Super Nintendo what did you play back then?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I was a huge Street Fighter fan and I actually still am. The only game I really was good at was Street Fighter.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Street Fighter is a pretty hardcore fighting game.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its always fun and I even have a Chun Li costume with the white lace-up boots almost up to my knees.
        </p>
