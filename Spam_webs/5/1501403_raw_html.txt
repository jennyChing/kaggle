            5 Tips for Surviving the Audition Waiting Room
            <span class="post-format-icon minor-meta">
            </span>
           </a>
          </h1>
          <span class="post-meta-infos">
           <time class="date-container minor-meta updated" datetime="2014-05-08T07:41:47+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">

          <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;">
           <a href="http://takelessons.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Act.jpg">
            <img alt="Audition Waiting Room Tips" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-19006" data-wp-pid="19006" height="306" src="http://takelessons.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Act.jpg" width="720"/>
           </a>
           <em>
            Ready for your big break? Auditions can be nerve-wracking  and the time you spend sitting in the waiting room before the audition is often where your nerves get the best of you. To help you get prepared, take a look at these 5 acting audition tips from Brooklyn, NY teacher
            <a href="http://takelessons.com/profile/caitlin-gra?utm_source=blog-student&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_content=post&amp;utm_campaign=tl-blog">
             Caitlin G.
            </a>
            :
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           If youre an actor going on an audition, you already have enough to worry about. What am I wearing? Is my resume updated? Do I know where Im going?
          </p>
          <p>
           And then theres the
           <a href="http://takelessons.com/performing-arts/acting-lessons?utm_source=blog-student&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_content=post&amp;utm_campaign=tl-blog">
            acting
           </a>
           part of it.
          </p>
          <p>
           On top of all this, the audition waiting room can be a difficult ordeal, to say the least. Picture it: youre sitting with five other actors, all of whom look just like you and most of whom are much more experienced. And theyve all got personalities. One of them is reading the audition lines out loud  really loud. Two others are exchanging stories of their latest gigs, with no regard for how long its been since the last one you landed. Another is doing vinyasa yoga on the floor, and the last one is openly staring daggers at you.
          </p>
          <p>
           How are you supposed to focus with all of that going on?
          </p>
          <p>
           Well, here are a few acting audition tips that might help:
          </p>
          <h2>
           1. Arrive as early as possible.
          </h2>
          <p dir="ltr">
           Getting to the audition site on time  whether its an actual rehearsal studio or a college students dorm room  is half the battle. Getting there early will ensure that youre in true fighting shape. 15-20 minutes should suffice, but half an hour is golden. Youll have time to use the restroom, make sure your face is on straight, and find a comfortable, quiet spot to settle in and center yourself. If the audition is a cold reading, this should give you plenty of time to look over your lines.
          </p>
          <p>
           And theres an added bonus: if the casting team happens to be running ahead of schedule, or the auditioner before you hasnt arrived, you may get to go in early and skip the waiting room altogether. Score.
          </p>
          <h2 dir="ltr">
           2. Dont socialize.
          </h2>
          <p dir="ltr">
           It can be tempting, and theres always one actor at the audition who wants to make friends. Trust me. Sometimes that actor is just nervous and they figure having a friendly conversation will calm their nerves. Sometimes its the actors way of subtly sussing out the competition. You never know what youre going to get, so avoid it if you can. You may end up in a conversation you dont want to be having, especially not before your audition.
          </p>
          <p dir="ltr">
           My advice: bring an iPod, put in those earbuds, and (quietly) rock out to any playlist that makes you feel confident and energized. You can always pause-and-play as auditioners come and go, just to make sure you dont miss your appointment. If possible, find a quiet corner where you can be all by your lonesome and get in the zone with no distractions. As long as it doesnt take you too far away from the audition monitor, of course.
          </p>
          <h2 dir="ltr">
           3. Be helpful and kind.
          </h2>
          <p dir="ltr">
           As I said above, dont socialize  but dont flat-out ignore other auditioners when they ask for help. If someones lost or needs a simple question answered, be courteous and give them the help they need. Pay it forward. Dont be the jerk whos too competitive to tell a fellow auditioner where the restroom is. Youll set the tone for the whole room, and everyone will appreciate it.
          </p>
          <h2>
           4. Stay loose.
          </h2>
          <p dir="ltr">
           I know youre nervous, but dont sit in the same position for too long, especially if you follow the Tip #1 and get there half an hour early. Get up and walk around every once in a while. Have a stretch, especially if youre doing a theater audition. You want your body to be warm, and sitting in one spot can mean stiffness when you finally do get up to audition.
          </p>
          <p dir="ltr">
           If you do opt to stretch, I recommend finding a quiet place to do it, as a courtesy to the other actors in the room. Your power squats are impressive, but they may intimidate others.
          </p>
          <h2 dir="ltr">
           5. Remember: you deserve to be there.
          </h2>
          <p dir="ltr">
           Always remember, youre there for a reason. You belong at this audition. Dont let anyone else tell you differently. The kind of auditioners who would insinuate such a thing are the exception rather than the rule, but they will cross your path from time to time. Dont let them get to you. Youre just as qualified to be there as they are.
          </p>
          <p>
           The acting
           <a href="http://takelessons.com/blog/2012/11/expert-audition-tips-takelessons-exclusive/?utm_source=blog-student&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_content=post&amp;utm_campaign=tl-blog">
            audition tips
           </a>
           above may not work for everybody, and thats okay. Whats most important is discovering what works best for you. If youre not sure, dont worry: itll come with practice. The more auditions you go on, the closer youll be to figuring out the ritual that makes you the most comfortable, confident, and capable of delivering the best audition possible.
          </p>
          <p>
           Break legs!
          </p>
          <p>
