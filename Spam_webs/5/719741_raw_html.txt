         <img align="left" alt="" border="1" hspace="4" src="http://www.blogcdn.com/realestate.aol.com/blog//media/2010/10/500xambanihouse.jpg" vspace="4"/>
         Mukesh Ambani, India's richest man according to
         <em>
          Forbes
         </em>
         , just moved his family into a $1 billion Mumbai skyscraper -- occupying all 27 stories.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/asia/india/8063385/Indias-richest-man-Mukesh-Ambani-moves-into-630m-home.html">
          The 53-year-old tycoon's new home is larger than the Palace of Versailles
         </a>
         and needs a staff of 600 to keep things running. The building has underground parking for 160 cars, three helicopter pads and a theater that seats 50. The Ambani family will occupy all 27 stories, which include a ballroom, several lounges, a health club, a dance studio and an elevated garden large enough to accommodate trees. Everybody needs a little greenery, right?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Some have suggested that the billionaire, like many of his billionaire compadres, was simply
         <a href="http://nymag.com/daily/intel/2010/10/indias_richest_man_builds_priv.html">
          buying himself a private island
         </a>
         . Indeed, he chose to name the structure Antilia, which is the name of a mythical island.
        </div>
