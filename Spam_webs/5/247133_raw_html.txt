     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Labor omnia vincit
       </font>
      </b>
      - Work conquers all things. (Virgil)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lacrima Christi
       </font>
      </b>
      - Christ's tear
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lapsus calami
       </font>
      </b>
      - A slip of the pen
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lapsus linguae
       </font>
      </b>
      - A slip of the tongue
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lapsus memoriae
       </font>
      </b>
      - A slip of the memory
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lares et penates
       </font>
      </b>
      - Household gods
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Latet anguis in herba
       </font>
      </b>
      - A snake lies in the grass. (Vergil)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Latine dictum
       </font>
      </b>
      - Spoken in Latin
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Laudant illa, sed ista legunt
       </font>
      </b>
      - Some (writing) is praised, but other is read. (Martialis)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Laudatores temporis acti
       </font>
      </b>
      - Praisers of time past
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Laudem virtutis necessitati damus
       </font>
      </b>
      - We give to necessity the praise of virtue finding the benefit in what's needful
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Laus Deo
       </font>
      </b>
      - Praise be to God
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lectio brevior lectio potior
       </font>
      </b>
      - The shortest reading is the more probable reading
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lector benevole
       </font>
      </b>
      - Kind reader
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lectori Salutem (L.S.)
       </font>
      </b>
      - Greetings to the reader
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Legatus a latere
       </font>
      </b>
      - Advisor from the side
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lege atque lacrima
       </font>
      </b>
      - Read 'em and weep
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Legum servi sumus ut liberi esse possimus
       </font>
      </b>
      - We are slaves of the law so that we may be able to be free. (Cicero)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Leve fit, quod bene fertur, onus
       </font>
      </b>
      - The burden is made light which is borne well. (Ovid)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lex domicilii
       </font>
      </b>
      - The law of a person's home country
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lex fori
       </font>
      </b>
      - The law of the forum (country)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lex loci
       </font>
      </b>
      - The law of the place
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lex malla, lex nulla
       </font>
      </b>
      - A bad law is no law. (St. Thomas Aquinas)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lex non scripta
       </font>
      </b>
      - The unwritten (common) law
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lex scripta
       </font>
      </b>
      - The written law
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lex talionis
       </font>
      </b>
      - The law of revenge
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Libenter homines id quod volunt credunt
       </font>
      </b>
      - Men gladly believe that which they wish for. (Caesar)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Liberae sunt nostrae cogitationes
       </font>
      </b>
      - Our thoughts are free. (Cicero)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Libera te ex inferis
       </font>
      </b>
      - Save yourself from hell
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Libertas inaestimabilis res est
       </font>
      </b>
      - Liberty is a thing beyond all price. (Corpus Iuris Civilis)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Liberum arbitrium
       </font>
      </b>
      - Free will
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Libra solidus denarius (L.S.D.)
       </font>
      </b>
      - Pounds, shillings, pence
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Licentia liquendi
       </font>
      </b>
      - Liberty of speaking
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Licentia poetica
       </font>
      </b>
      - Poetic licence. (Seneca)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Licet
       </font>
      </b>
      - It is allowed
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lingua franca
       </font>
      </b>
      - Universal tongue
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Literati
       </font>
      </b>
      - Men of letters
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Litterae humaniores
       </font>
      </b>
      - The humanities
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Loco citato (lc)
       </font>
      </b>
      - In the passage just quoted
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Locum tenens
       </font>
      </b>
      - One occupying the place (used as an English noun meaning 'deputy')
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Locus classicus
       </font>
      </b>
      - The most authoritative source, Classical passage
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Locus delicti
       </font>
      </b>
      - The scene of the crime
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Locus desperatus
       </font>
      </b>
      - A hopeless passage
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Locus enim est principum generationis rerum
       </font>
      </b>
      - For place is the origin of things. (Roger Bacon)
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Locus in quo
       </font>
      </b>
      - The place in which something happens
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Locus poenitentiae
       </font>
      </b>
      - A place for repentance
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Locus sigilli (l.s.)
       </font>
      </b>
      - The place of the seal
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Locus standi
       </font>
      </b>
      - Place of standing
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Longo intervallo
       </font>
      </b>
      - After a long gap
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Loquitur (loq.)
       </font>
      </b>
      - He/she speaks
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Luctor et emergo
       </font>
      </b>
      - I struggle but I'll survive
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lumen naturale
       </font>
      </b>
      - Natural light
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lupus est homo homini
       </font>
      </b>
      - Man is wolf to man
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <font color="#fdeeb3">
        Lupus in fabula
       </font>
      </b>
      - The wolf in the tale (i.e. Speak of the wolf, and he will come) (Terence)

      <b>
