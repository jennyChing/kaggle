&lt;h2&gt;&lt;a title="A Shadow of Lilies" href="http://www.eclecticcycle.com/a-shadow-of-lilies/" rel="bookmark"&gt;A Shadow of Lilies&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;

&lt;p align="CENTER"&gt;&lt;b&gt;Prologue&lt;/b&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p align="LEFT"&gt;The ancient demon watched her through the glass. He had forgotten how exquisite she was.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p align="LEFT"&gt;And how urgent his &lt;i&gt;need&lt;/i&gt; of her was.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p align="LEFT"&gt;He pressed his long fingers against the glass. He would collect her soon.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p align="LEFT"&gt;&lt;i&gt;Very&lt;/i&gt; soon.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p align="CENTER"&gt;&lt;b&gt;One&lt;/b&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira held her tears back because of stubborn pride. He was late again. She did not even know if he would come home tonight.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She finally sat up, wiping off mascara that had been ruined when the tears made their escape. Tonight, she had thought, they could work things out from their fight. Her anger lashed out and around the empty room. Finding nowhere to settle, it slid back into her heart.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira heard a click, and their bedroom door eased open. He slipped into the room, stinking of too much cologne and far too much wine.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She kept her back stiff to him and stared out of the floor-length windows. Rain had been lashing against the glass for hours now. The dark glory of the city was lost between the blur of her tears and the fury of nature.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Matt undressed as if everything was fine, as if he did not notice her rigid backbone, her silence, and her carefully cultivated brooding air. She turned and locked her dark eyes on his lighter ones.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She asked, Where were you?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Matt shrugged and said, The gallery opening ran later than the owner thought. She really had to drag this one out. Some billionaire collector was perusing, and she needed to make a sale.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;He slid under the silken sheets of their bed, the fumes from his breath assaulting her nostrils. She had heard a dozen variations of his excuses for being out so late. Either he claimed he was at his art studio downtown, a new show opening, or chasing his muse.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;All were veiled lies that he used to cover his meetings with his real muse.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She wondered why he even stayed with her, and why she stayed with him. Her heart cramped and pounded at the thought of his leaving her alone in this steel jungle.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She had nowhere else to go.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She turned back to admire the drumming rain, already defeated. With Matt, you could never quite get your hands on the slippery semblance of truth but she would perform the fruitless labor to ease her own heart. She had to at least try to defend herself against his constant assault of lies.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira said casually, So this gallery opening was so fascinating that six hours flew by? Six hours in which you did not bother to contact me?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Matt feigned a look of pain when she glanced back to gauge his reaction. Im sorry. But if you expect me to keep you in this luxury, I have to be seen at all these functions. The art world would leave me behind if I didnt give my all. He hiccuped low under his smugness and slid closer to her.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira whipped her head around to glare at him. No man &lt;i&gt;kept&lt;/i&gt; her.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She arched a thin eyebrow and tried to stay calm. I see. Yet you insist I not attend any of these important gallery openings. I am forever told that they are too dull.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;They are very dull. The wine is mediocre and the company pretentious.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira ground her toes into the lush carpet and willed her tone to be neutral. She said, You went to Anthony's show, right?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Yes. His new art is riveting. Really ahead of the curve.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;You were there for six whole hours?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Yes. Call Anthony if you dont believe me. The slightest petulance crept into his tone.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I actually stopped by to see his new paintings. I was there for over an hour. Cant say that I saw you.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Silence greeted her. Matt had gone very still, his eyes wide. He shivered, and made a bleary attempt to turn the tables on her. He snapped, You dont trust me. You were spying on me!&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I was going to surprise you. I wanted to say I was sorry. Her voice finally caught in her thickening throat. Where &lt;i&gt;were&lt;/i&gt; you?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;He lowered his eyes and said nothing. He rolled away after a moment, clinging to the edge of their bed with limp fingers.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira waited with as much patience as she could muster for him to say something, her heart pounding a dull tattoo to his unspoken betrayal. She let her watering eyes admire the lean beauty of him.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She wondered if she dared to tell him that idle gossip had brought his lover's name to her waiting ears. Or if she dared to tell him that the distance between them was becoming a gulf that she could not cross.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;When had he become such a vain shadow of a man?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She saw a glimmer of moisture on his pillow. Pity rose up in her, and she turned back towards the windows and slipped down to the floor, resigning herself to spending another night in the guest room.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Her heart stopped when she saw who was looking at her.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Or, &lt;i&gt;what&lt;/i&gt; was looking at her.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;A man stood on the narrow ledge of their window, rain plastering his jet black hair to his pale face. His eyes sought hers, and she was unable to resist when their gazes locked.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;He grinned with wicked mirth and winked at her.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She continued to stare into his obsidian eyes, unable to comprehend how somebody could be out there when they lived on the fifty second floor. No fire escape or balcony marred their breathtaking view of the city. There was no way to just stand out there without rope or scaffolding.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Yet there he stood, without the slightest aid.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The man stared at her for an eternity as she stood, petrified. He began to push against the glass with his giant hands, fingers splayed like dead spiders against the window.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira snapped out of her numb horror long enough to scramble back in a panic. She turned to grab Matt, her anger with him forgotten in sheer animal fear. She found her voice as she pulled on his arm. She hissed, Get out, get out, we have got to get out before he breaks the glass!&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She shoved hard against Matt where he lay in his drunken stupor. He glanced at the window behind her, confusion etched on his face. He asked, What are you talking about? Who is breaking what?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;His bleary eyes roved over the window and made no sign of recognition. Abira turned back as fear unfurled delicate petals throughout her shaking body. She stopped and gaped at what she saw, too incredulous to scream.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The dark haired man was now standing in their room, dripping rain on the carpet. He wore a tailored black suit. His eyes and hair were the same midnight ebony, and his teeth flashed a brilliant white as he smiled at Abira and bowed to her.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Behind him, the glass remained intact, as if he had pushed his way through solid matter.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;He was the most graceful and frightening thing she had ever seen in her life. As she continued to study him, she thought she might be having a breakdown. He was just too bigat least seven feet talland just too &lt;i&gt;there&lt;/i&gt;, where he should not be.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Was this hallucination the result of too much wine and heartbreak?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Matts high scream told her that he now saw the same thing. She turned to him as he scrambled out of bed, clutching the sheets to his bare chest. His eyes were round, his mouth making a primal sound of base terror. His bloodshot gaze swerved to meet her somewhat calmer one.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;He mouthed, What. Is. That.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira shook her head and turned back to the intruder, who looked confused. Abira heard a gasping fumble at the door behind her, and then a slam.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Matt had left her all alone to deal with this.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;What a coward. The man grinned at her again, his eyes lighting up as he fully took her in. I would have taken you and fled. You are much too delectable to leave behind. His voice was cultured refinement, vaguely antiquarian, the sort of voice that only generations of money and power leave behind.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;He took a towering step toward her, still dripping rainwater. Heat spread off of him in waves, caressing her cold skin. Tendrils of his heat wriggled tender fingers up her thighs. Serenity began to ease into her with a shivery-ticklish sensation. It was a serenity that was not her own, but his. She started to feel a pleasant sense of detachment from this insane moment.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;What of it if a man stepped through glass to loom over her?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira shook her head, confounded. She regarded him for a long moment as her knees stopped shaking, and the semblance of calm settled her racing heart. They stared at each other as Abira tried to find a logical explanation for all of this.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She pushed back against the unwelcome serenity. It whispered that she need not think for herself, that all would be well. He had not hurt her yet, but that meant nothing. He could still be wanting to harm her. It felt as if he was using some strange influence to lure her with kindness.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;And only the most clever of predators lured.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She pulled her thoughts back to the questions she wanted to ask. They would not come back to her at first, but even as his warmth continued to caress her, she gathered her iron will and shoved his heat away. She asked, How did you get in here? Who are you?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The giant man stopped, still and hard as a marble statue. Surprise seemed to flit across his face. You do not know me, Empress? I am your First Prince. Is this some new game you would play?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Her jaw dropped. Of all the unbelievable things that could come from between those sculpted lips, that answer was beyond her reasoning. She tried to ask again, but instead closed her mouth and feebly attempted to regain a bit of composure. Then she shook her head, speechless.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She was beginning to think that she was really was having a breakdown. No nightmare could be so vivid.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The man continued his silent appraisal, his dark eyes moving over her flushed face, her nightie, her long hair. There was something feral and wanton in his gaze, under the polished manners.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira stood her ground and stared back, determined not to flee this mystery nor bow before his prying eyes. Then the huge man began to speak in a guttural language, sweeping his hands around the room, his tone pleading for understanding.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira shook her head and shrugged. I dont understand what you are saying. I dont speak that language.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The giant man took a quick step over to her and lifted his left hand. An almost invisible iridescence trailed from his fingers and drifted over to her. She jumped back from his unwelcome approach too late. The strange shivering, tickling sensation that she had experienced just a moment ago enveloped her again, working through her with sensuous ease as the iridescence penetrated her skin.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The man spoke again, sans the dramatic hand flailing. Can you understand me now?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira nodded, surprised. She heard the deep guttural language underneath the words, but nonetheless she could comprehend them. She responded, I can. How is that possible?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira clamped her mouth shut when she heard her own voice. That odd language, so reminiscent of crumbling churches gone long to dust, was escaping her own throat. She trembled, feeling violated. This man had used his strange influence to penetrate her twice now, with effects she did not understand.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Rage began to tickle at the corners of her spirit.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The mans disappointment at her ignorance registered with a slow frown. I cannot take the risk, he murmured to himself. He reached out and slid a long finger down her face, tilting her chin up, his eyes probing hers. I must bring you with me.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira jerked away from his blazing skin. She was always cold, and his warmth shot through her with too much force. She said, You arent taking me anywhere.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The man chuckled deep in his throat. And how do you propose to stop me?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Before she could respond, his arms shot out and he threw her over his massive shoulder. Her abductor turned with lightning speed, and ran straight through the window as if it was not there. When they passed through the glass, she felt no resistance.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;They began to fall.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She screamed with no sound as the wind snatched her voice away. They fell fifty two stories, her hair whipping behind, her heart stopping&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Her abductor's feet slammed into the slick pavement, and he ran so fast that they were out of the rain in a blink, the dreary streets blurring past her terrified gaze. Abira squeezed her eyes shut, trying to think, trying to understand as he ran on. She had never believed the old legends her Nana had told her, but&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Either she was having a bizarre nightmare, a breakdown, or this man was not human.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abiras curiosity got the better of her as they flew through the night, on and on through shadows and cold. She willed herself to face her fate, and opened her eyes. She had to know where they were going so she could find her way back to safety.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Hard as she tried, she could not suppress her mounting terror so that she could focus. Grey nothings flowed past, some almost resembling people or streets. The grey lightened to white as Abira blinked hard against the creeping mists that surrounded everything they flew past.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;He was taking her against her will through a ghost of a place, a hint of a dimension. She risked a look behind her and saw nothing but roiling mists.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;There was no trail back to follow.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The abductor slowed down and set her on her feet with gentle care. She clutched his arm in spite of herself. Disorientation made her head swim, made her mind thick with woolen thoughts.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;As her head cleared, Abira felt a scream building up in the back of her throat. She glanced up and saw a purplish sky, bruised as if with old wounds. A dim sun was just rising from the horizon to cast a shadow on the strange mist.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The mist slowly parted to reveal a looming darkness just ahead, the outlines suggesting a gate. The mist teased her with this mysterious glimpse, then began to fold back in on itself. It chuckled at her befuddlement while whispering to her of cloaked mysteries, mocking her ignorance in the face of dark miracles.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira kept her eyes on the gate, trying to ignore the invading mist. She made a brave attempt to ask a simple question, and her throat seized up. She worked some saliva around and tried again. She whispered, Where are we?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She clamped her mouth shut before a scream could escape on her querys tail.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She felt the smooth sliding of his muscles as her abductor cocked his head. Do you truly not know?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Abira shook her head, wondering how she was going to convince this giant that she was not an empress. She stood there, waiting for him to explain.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She let go of him as soon as her disorientation cleared, her hands limp by her nightie. The mist swirled back to reveal a gargantuan gate before closing in on itself again.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;He did not respond, but instead continued to stare at her. She barely noticed, however, as all of her attention was fixed on the architecture ahead. The gate was obscene in scale, crowded with iron-wrought figures of serpents and apples. Exquisite detail was hinted at before the mist settled in once again. The silence of the mist was once more penetrated by voices. They built back up again, sending subtle jabs at her psyche, gibbering, mumbling, laughing at her confusion&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She looked down at her bare feet to spy a rough rock path. So medieval, like the gate to a fairy land&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The man startled her from her observations with a slow sigh. He said, If you are as ignorant as you claim, I may have made an error. We are at my home, which is now your home. The empire of Hades. Most likely referred to as Hell in your realm. My name is Baphomet.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;His voice began to fade away as she crumbled to the stone ground. The last thing she felt was his flaming touch as he caught her. Soft, beautiful darkness wrapped around her inert frame.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;She drifted with the cruel mists, laughing right along with them....&lt;/p&gt;
