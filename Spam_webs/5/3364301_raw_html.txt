     <p>
      <b>
       Moombahton
      </b>
      (
      <span class="nowrap">
       <span class="IPA nopopups">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">
         /
         <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
          <span title="// primary stress follows">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'m' in 'my'">
           m
          </span>
          <span title="/u/ long 'oo' in 'food'">
           u
          </span>
          <span title="'m' in 'my'">
           m
          </span>
          <span title="'b' in 'buy'">
           b
          </span>
          <span title="// 'a' in 'about'">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'t' in 'tie'">
           t
          </span>
          <span title="// short 'o' in 'body'">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'n' in 'no'">
           n
          </span>
         </span>
         /
        </a>
       </span>
      </span>
      ,
      <span class="Unicode" title="English pronunciation respelling">
       <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Wikipedia:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i>
         <b>
          <span class="smallcaps">
           <span class="SMALLCAPS" style="FONT-VARIANT:SMALL-CAPS;">
            <span class="NOCAPS" style="TEXT-TRANSFORM:LOWERCASE;">
             MOOM
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </b>
         -b-ton
        </i>
       </a>
      </span>
      ) is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fusion_genre" title="Fusion genre">
       fusion genre
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">
       house music
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Reggaeton" title="Reggaeton">
       reggaeton
      </a>
      that was created by American
      <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">
       DJ
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_producer" title="Music producer">
       producer
      </a>
      Dave Nada
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washdc_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-washdc-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      (born David Villegas) in Washington, D.C., in 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Identifying characteristics of moombahton include a thick and spread-out bass line, dramatic builds; and a two-step pulse, with quick drum fills.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washdc_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-washdc-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Occasionally moombahton includes ravey synthesizers and acappella rap samples.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washdc_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-washdc-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Nada coined the name as a
      <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">
       portmanteau
      </a>
      of "Moombah" (a track by Dutch house DJ
      <a href="/wiki/Chuckie_(DJ)" title="Chuckie (DJ)">
       Chuckie
      </a>
      ) and
      <i>
       reggaeton
      </i>
      (itself derived from combining the term
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">
        reggae
       </a>
      </i>
      with the Spanish suffix
      <i>
       -on
      </i>
      signifying "big").
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">
       Origins
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moombahton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Moombahton was created by Dave Nada in late 2009 while DJing his cousin's high school cut party in Washington D.C. He blended the
      <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">
       house
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_music" title="Club music">
       club music
      </a>
      he had planned to play with the
      <a href="/wiki/Dancehall" title="Dancehall">
       dancehall
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Bachata_(music)" title="Bachata (music)">
       bachata
      </a>
      the guests were previously listening to by slowing down
      <a href="/wiki/Afrojack" title="Afrojack">
       Afrojack
      </a>
      's remix of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Silvio_Ecomo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Silvio Ecomo (page does not exist)">
       Silvio Ecomo
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Chuckie_(DJ)" title="Chuckie (DJ)">
       Chuckie's
      </a>
      song "Moombah" from 128BPM to 108BPM, to create the basis of the genre.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Between late 2009 and early 2010, Nada worked on a five track
      <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">
       extended play
      </a>
      of moombahton tracks that was released in March 2010, with the support of the DJ Ayres and the
      <a href="/wiki/Tittsworth" title="Tittsworth">
       DJ Tittsworth
      </a>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/T%26A_Records" title="T&amp;A Records">
       T&amp;A Records
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Though not referred to as moombahton, the concept of combining reggae/
      <a href="/wiki/Dancehall" title="Dancehall">
       dancehall
      </a>
      /reggaeton percussion with electronic elements dates back further than Dave Nada.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Examples of artists which previously fused Latin and electronic dance music include
      <a href="/wiki/Nadia_Oh" title="Nadia Oh">
       Nadia Oh
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/El_General" title="El General">
       El General
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Masters_at_Work" title="Masters at Work">
       Masters at Work
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Munchi_(DJ)" title="Munchi (DJ)">
       Munchi
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Luny_Tunes" title="Luny Tunes">
       Luny Tunes
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jowell_%26_Randy" title="Jowell &amp; Randy">
       Jowell &amp; Randy
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Moombahton has also been incorporated into existing styles of music, creating derivative genres such as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moombahcore" title="Moombahcore">
       moombahcore
      </a>
      , a genre fusing the tempo and percussion of moombahton with the distorted sounds of modern
      <a href="/wiki/Dubstep" title="Dubstep">
       dubstep
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_developments">
       Recent developments
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moombahton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recent developments">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In January 2012,
      <a href="/wiki/Beatport" title="Beatport">
       Beatport
      </a>
      created a chart for the top moombahton songs of 2011.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Moombahton creator Nada and collaborator Matt Nordstrom as Nadastrom (who toured with
      <a href="/wiki/Skrillex" title="Skrillex">
       Skrillex
      </a>
      in 2011) are currently based in Los Angeles but return regularly to DC to play the
      <i>
       Moombahton Massive
      </i>
      parties they established in 2010 at U Street Music Hall.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <p>
      Moombahcore is a style of different moombahton incorporating dubstep influences. As well as incorporating elements of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_house" title="Dutch house">
       Dutch house
      </a>
      as moombahton does, moombahcore also fuses elements of
      <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_hardcore" title="Mainstream hardcore">
       gabber
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Breakcore" title="Breakcore">
       breakcore
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Techstep" title="Techstep">
       techstep
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Dubstep" title="Dubstep">
       dubstep
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Moombahcore tracks often feature
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wobble_bass" title="Wobble bass">
       wobble bass
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FM_synthesis" title="FM synthesis">
       FM synthesis
      </a>
      , distorted basslines, aggressive sounds, violent snares and leads, and complex percussion patterns.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
