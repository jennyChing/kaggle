     <p>
      <b>
       -Butyrolactone
      </b>
      (
      <b>
       GBL
      </b>
      ) is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hygroscopic" title="Hygroscopic">
       hygroscopic
      </a>
      colorless liquid with a weak characteristic
      <a href="/wiki/Odor" title="Odor">
       odor
      </a>
      . Soluble in water, GBL is a common
      <a href="/wiki/Solvent" title="Solvent">
       solvent
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Reagent" title="Reagent">
       reagent
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">
       chemistry
      </a>
      as well as being used as a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flavouring" title="Flavouring">
       flavouring
      </a>
      , as a cleaning solvent, as a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Superglue" title="Superglue">
       superglue
      </a>
      remover, and as a
      <a href="/wiki/Solvent" title="Solvent">
       solvent
      </a>
      in some wet aluminium electrolytic capacitors. In humans it acts as a
      <a href="/wiki/Prodrug" title="Prodrug">
       prodrug
      </a>
      for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%CE%93-hydroxybutyric_acid" title="-hydroxybutyric acid">
       -hydroxybutyric acid
      </a>
      (GHB), and it is used as a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recreational_drug" title="Recreational drug">
       recreational
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intoxicant" title="Intoxicant">
       intoxicant
      </a>
      with effects similar to
      <a href="/wiki/Ethanol" title="Ethanol">
       alcohol
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Occurrence">
       Occurrence
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Occurrence">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      GBL has been found in extracts from samples of unadulterated wines.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid11569560_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pmid11569560-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This finding indicates that GBL is a naturally occurring component in some wines and may be present in similar products. The concentration detected was approximately 5 g/mL and was easily observed using a simple extraction technique followed by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gas_chromatography-mass_spectrometry" title="Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry">
       GC/MS
      </a>
      analysis. GBL can be found in cheese flavourings but typically results in a content of 0.0002% GBL in the final foodstuff.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HO_circular_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-HO_circular-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation">
       Preparation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparation">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      GBL is produced industrially by dehydrogenation of 1,4-butanediol.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ull_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ull-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This route proceeds via
      <a href="/wiki/Dehydration_reaction" title="Dehydration reaction">
       dehydration
      </a>
      of GHB.
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Synthesis_gamma-Butyrolactone.svg">
        <img alt="Synthesis gamma-Butyrolactone.svg" data-file-height="116" data-file-width="620" height="75" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Synthesis_gamma-Butyrolactone.svg/400px-Synthesis_gamma-Butyrolactone.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Synthesis_gamma-Butyrolactone.svg/600px-Synthesis_gamma-Butyrolactone.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Synthesis_gamma-Butyrolactone.svg/800px-Synthesis_gamma-Butyrolactone.svg.png 2x" width="400"/>
       </a>
      </dd>
     </dl>
     <p>
      In the laboratory, it may also be obtained via the oxidation of
      <a href="/wiki/Tetrahydrofuran" title="Tetrahydrofuran">
       tetrahydrofuran
      </a>
      (THF), for example with aqueous
      <a href="/wiki/Sodium_bromate" title="Sodium bromate">
       sodium bromate
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">
       Reactions
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reactions">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As a
      <a href="/wiki/Lactone" title="Lactone">
       lactone
      </a>
      , GBL is
      <a href="/wiki/Hydrolysis" title="Hydrolysis">
       hydrolyzed
      </a>
      under
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basic_(chemistry)" title="Basic (chemistry)">
       basic
      </a>
      conditions, for example in a
      <a href="/wiki/Sodium_hydroxide" title="Sodium hydroxide">
       sodium hydroxide
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Solution" title="Solution">
       solution
      </a>
      into
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sodium_gamma-hydroxybutyrate" title="Sodium gamma-hydroxybutyrate">
       sodium
       <i>
        gamma
       </i>
       -hydroxybutyrate
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Sodium" title="Sodium">
       sodium
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Salt_(chemistry)" title="Salt (chemistry)">
       salt
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Gamma-Hydroxybutyric_acid" title="Gamma-Hydroxybutyric acid">
       <i>
        gamma
       </i>
       -hydroxybutyric acid
      </a>
      . In acidic water, a mixture of the lactone and acid forms coexist in an
      <a href="/wiki/Chemical_equilibrium" title="Chemical equilibrium">
       equilibrium
      </a>
      . These compounds then may go on to form the
      <a href="/wiki/Polymer" title="Polymer">
       polymer
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Polyhydroxybutyrate" title="Polyhydroxybutyrate">
       poly(4-hydroxybutyrate)
      </a>
      . When treated with a non-nucleophilic base, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Lithium_diisopropylamide" title="Lithium diisopropylamide">
       lithium diisopropylamide
      </a>
      , GBL undergoes deprotonation alpha to the carbonyl. The related compound
      <a href="/wiki/Caprolactone" title="Caprolactone">
       caprolactone
      </a>
      can be used to make a polyester in this manner.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Pharmacology">
       Pharmacology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pharmacology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gamma-hydroxybutyrate#Pharmacology" title="Gamma-hydroxybutyrate">
       gamma-hydroxybutyrate  Pharmacology
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      GBL is not active in its own right; its mechanism of action stems from its identity as a
      <a href="/wiki/Prodrug" title="Prodrug">
       prodrug
      </a>
      of GHB.
     </p>
     <p>
      The hypnotic effect of GHB is enhanced by combination with alcohol. A 2003 rat study showed that GBL in combination with ethanol showed a potentiated hypnotic effect, as the sleep-timing measure was longer than both of the individual components combined.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Pharmacokinetics">
       Pharmacokinetics
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pharmacokinetics">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>

     <p>
      GBL is rapidly converted into GHB by
      <a href="/wiki/Paraoxonase" title="Paraoxonase">
       paraoxonase
      </a>
      (lactonase) enzymes, found in the blood.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forchem_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Forchem-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">

        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Animals which lack these enzymes exhibit no effect from GBL.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forchem_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Forchem-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      GBL is more lipophilic (fat soluble) than GHB, and so is absorbed faster and has higher
      <a href="/wiki/Bioavailability" title="Bioavailability">
       bioavailability
      </a>
      . Because of these pharmacokinetic differences, GBL tends to be more potent and faster-acting than GHB, but has a shorter duration; whereas the related compound
      <a href="/wiki/1,4-Butanediol" title="1,4-Butanediol">
       1,4-butanediol
      </a>
      (1,4-B) tends to be slightly less potent, slower to take effect but longer-acting than GHB.
     </p>
     <p>
      The levels of lactonase enzyme can vary between individuals, meaning that first-time users can show unpredictable results, even from small doses. In many this manifests as slow onset of effects, followed by headaches, semi-consciousness which is distinct from GBL sleep in normal users. If the user decides to try again at a later date, they appear to be able to enjoy the effects normally.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Use_as_a_nutritional_supplement">
       Use as a nutritional supplement
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Use as a nutritional supplement">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Gamma-Hydroxybutyric_acid#Sports_and_athletics" title="Gamma-Hydroxybutyric acid">
       Gamma-Hydroxybutyric_acid  Sports_and_athletics
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Due to its property of being a
      <a href="/wiki/Prodrug" title="Prodrug">
       prodrug
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Gamma-Hydroxybutyric_acid" title="Gamma-Hydroxybutyric acid">
       GHB
      </a>
      which increases sleep related growth hormone (GH) secretion,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      GBL was sold as a nutritional supplement after the scheduling of GHB, under the names Revivarant and Renewtrient
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erowid_rev_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-erowid_rev-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in the U.S. at least until the end of 1999.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Recreational_use">
       Recreational use
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Recreational use">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      GBL is a
      <a href="/wiki/Prodrug" title="Prodrug">
       prodrug
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Gamma-Hydroxybutyric_acid" title="Gamma-Hydroxybutyric acid">
       GHB
      </a>
      and its recreational use comes entirely as a result of this.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      To bypass GHB restriction laws, home synthesis kits were introduced to transform GBL and/or 1,4-B into GHB.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GBLjugs.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="295" data-file-width="550" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/GBLjugs.jpg/350px-GBLjugs.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/GBLjugs.jpg/525px-GBLjugs.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/07/GBLjugs.jpg 2x" width="350"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GBLjugs.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Jugs of seized GBL.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dangerous_dietary_supplements.gif">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="725" data-file-width="546" height="398" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Dangerous_dietary_supplements.gif/300px-Dangerous_dietary_supplements.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Dangerous_dietary_supplements.gif/450px-Dangerous_dietary_supplements.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/34/Dangerous_dietary_supplements.gif 2x" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dangerous_dietary_supplements.gif" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        FDA warning against products containing GHB and its prodrugs, such as GBL.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      GBL overdose can cause irrational behaviour, severe sickness,
      <a href="/wiki/Coma" title="Coma">
       coma
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">
       death
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      GBL has a distinctive taste and odour, described as being comparable to stale water, synthetic melon aroma or burnt plastic. This differs significantly from GHB, which is described as having a decidedly "salty" taste.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Dangers">
       Dangers
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Dangers">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      If taken undiluted by mouth, GBL can cause esophageal and gastro-intestinal irritation. It is possible for oral ingestion of GBL to cause nausea and other similar problems, possibly more so than with GHB.
     </p>
     <p>
      GHB has biphasic effects, a euphoric effect at low doses (the reason for the term liquid ecstasy), and a sedative effect
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      at higher doses. As a result of this sedation it can cause unconsciousness.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraphdeath_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-telegraphdeath-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      When combined with alcohol the increased sedation and risk of vomiting results in a high risk of fatality. Many harm reduction organisations suggest never mixing the two drugs as a result.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      There have been news reports of several deaths associated with GBL, usually in combination with alcohol or other depressants.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcdeath_20-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-bbcdeath-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Addictiveness">
       Addictiveness
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Addictiveness">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Frequent use of GHB/GBL, even when taken long-term and in moderate doses, does not appear to cause significant physical dependency in the greater majority of its users. In many people, quitting or temporarily abstaining from use of the drugs is achieved with minimal or no difficulty. However, when consumed in excessive amounts with a high frequency of dosing, physical and psychological dependence can develop.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      There are some reports of GHB/GBL users adopting a '24/7' dosing regime.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This is where the user has become tolerant to the effects of the drug, increasing the dosage and frequency of dosage simply to avoid withdrawal symptoms.
     </p>
     <p>
      For those users who do report withdrawal symptoms upon quitting the use of GHB/GBL, symptoms seem to depend on the dosage and the length of time the drug was used for. Light to moderate users often experience insomnia and sleep-related problems, whereas heavy, prolonged use can cause severe withdrawal symptoms similar to
      <a href="/wiki/Benzodiazepine_withdrawal_syndrome" title="Benzodiazepine withdrawal syndrome">
       Benzodiazepine withdrawal syndrome
      </a>
      (BWS).
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Dose">
       Dose
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Dose">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A milliliter of pure GBL metabolizes to the equivalent 1.65g of
      <a href="/wiki/Gamma-Hydroxybutyric_acid" title="Gamma-Hydroxybutyric acid">
       NaGHB
      </a>
      , the common form, so doses are measured in the single milliliter range, either taken all at once or sipped over the course of a night.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_status_of_GBL">
       Legal status of GBL
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamma-Butyrolactone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Legal status of GBL">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <b>
       Australia:
      </b>
      GBL is a border controlled substance and is illegal to import into Australia without a permit. The importation of a commercial quantity of a border controlled drug (over 1kg of GBL) is punishable by up to life imprisonment and/or an $825,000 fine.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Canada:
      </b>
      GBL is a Controlled Substance under Schedule VI of the "Controlled Drugs and Substances Act" in Canada. Schedule VI of the "Controlled Drugs and Substances Act" requires vendors to collect information regarding purchases of GBL. The Act also prohibits the import and export of GBL into or out of Canada classifying it as either an indictable offense punishable with up to 10 years in prison or an offense punishable on summary conviction liable to imprisonment for up to eighteen months.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is not illegal for an individual to possess GBL in Canada.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Germany:
      </b>
      GBL is not listed in the narcotics law, but its distribution is controlled. Possession is not illegal, but may be punished according to the Medicines Act, when intended to be sold for human consumption or synthesis of GHB. In recent years, an increase of GBL consumption has been observed due to the prohibition of GHB.
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Hong Kong SAR:
      </b>
      GBL is a dangerous drug controlled under Schedule 1 of the Dangerous Drugs Ordinance, Cap.134 (with exemption clause at Paragraph 16D). Any person who is found to have in his possession of it not in accordance with this Ordinance can be liable, on conviction upon indictment, a fine of HK$1,000,000 and to imprisonment for 7 years.
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Israel:
      </b>
      GBL was classified as a proscribed substance from 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       The Netherlands
      </b>
      : GBL has been placed on list 1 of the "Opiumwet" (freely translated: "Opiumlaw") and is therefore illegal to manufacture, possess and deal. However, use of illegal drugs is considered legal in the Netherlands, to maintain the user's safety when hospitalized.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Poland:
      </b>
      GBL is not classified as a drug and can be purchased in chemistry shops as a solvent.
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Russia
      </b>
      : GBL is classified as psychotropic substance since 22 February 2012, its traficking is limited, and non-licensed selling, buying or any other using is imprisoned up to 20 years.
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Sweden:
      </b>
      GBL is not classified as a drug but as a health-endangering substance. Although recently passed legislation to enter into force on 1 April 2011 will make it possible to handle narcotics for industrial purposes will enable GBL and 1,4-Butanediol to be classified as controlled substances.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       United Kingdom:
      </b>
      Because of their legitimate uses, regulation 4B of the 2001 regulations makes it lawful to import, export, produce, supply, offer to supply or possess GBL and
      <a href="/wiki/1,4-Butanediol" title="1,4-Butanediol">
       1,4-BD
      </a>
      . Except where a person does so knowing or believing that they will be used for the purpose of human ingestion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HO_circular_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-HO_circular-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       United States:
      </b>
      GBL is regulated as a List 1 controlled chemical. As a GHB analog, it is treated as a controlled substance under Schedule I of the "Controlled Substances Act" if intended for human consumption.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
