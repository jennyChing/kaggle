         <div class="article__content">
          <p>
           If you're thinking about starting a business, you're not alone.
          </p>
          <p>
           According to the
           <a class="saveLink" href="profiles/company/us/ca/laguna_beach/global_entrepreneurship_institute/401983">
            Global Entrepreneurship
           </a>
           Monitor, entrepreneurial activity in the U.S. is at a 13-year high. But taking that leap is no easy step.
          </p>
          <p>
           With that in mind, we recently asked our readers and social media followers what's the one piece of advice they would give to someone thinking of starting their own business.
          </p>
          <p>
           Here are some of our favorite responses:
          </p>
          <p>
           From our Facebook page:
          </p>
          <p>
           "If you cannot state both your business idea and why it occupies a unique place in the market in twenty seconds or less, then you don't have a business idea. For you haven't given anybody a reason to move their business to you from some other guy." -
           <a href="bizjournals/search/results?q=Chris Goldschmidt">
            Chris Goldschmidt
           </a>
           ,
           <a class="saveLink" href="profiles/company/us/al/birmingham/the_forte_marketing_group_llc/27613">
            Forte Marketing Group
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           "Find a mentor who is willing to share his/her experiences and provide you with honest advice." -
           <a href="bizjournals/search/results?q=Scott Schablow">
            Scott Schablow
           </a>
           , Hip Brand Group
          </p>
          <p>
           "Keep your ego and lose your pride! Confidence is great but arrogance is not." -
           <a href="bizjournals/search/results?q=Tess Lynes">
            Tess Lynes
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           "I would give my standard advice. Attorney, Banker and Accountant from the very beginning." - Robin Elizabeth Hunt, ThinkData Solutions Inc.
          </p>
          <p>
           From our readers:
          </p>
          <p>
           "I recommend reading the book '
           <em>
            The E-myth Revisited
           </em>
           .' It's a story about why small businesses fail and what to do about it. Most problems an entrepreneur faces are not new. Don't reinvent the wheel trying to solve each problem on your own. Let a professional (like a CPA) help." -
           <a href="bizjournals/search/results?q=Keith Barfield">
            Keith Barfield
           </a>
           ,
           <a class="saveLink" href="profiles/company/us/al/birmingham/barfield_murphy_shank_%26_smith_pc/3275950">
            Barfield Murphy Shank &amp; Smith PC
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Craft a well-written business plan and focus on filling a void in the marketplace. Think strategically and set clear goals. Most importantly, follow your passion and work harder and smarter than you've ever worked in your life.
           <a href="bizjournals/search/results?q=William White">
            William White
           </a>
           , Entrepreneurs Organization Birmingham
          </p>
