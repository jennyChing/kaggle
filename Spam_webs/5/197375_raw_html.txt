                 <h2>
                  <a href="/deeplinks/2015/07/top-five-takeaways-todays-hearings-encryption">
                   Our Top Five Takeaways From Today's Hearings on Encryption
                  </a>
                 </h2>
                </div>
                <div class="views-field views-field-term-node-tid">
                 <span class="field-content">
                  <a href="/issues/security">
                   Security
                  </a>
                  <a href="/issues/calea">
                   CALEA
                  </a>
                  <a href="/encrypt-the-web">
                   Encrypting the Web
                  </a>
                 </span>

                <div class="views-field views-field-body">
                 <div class="field-content">
                  <p>
                   Despite
                   <a href="https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2014/10/90s-and-now-fbi-and-its-inability-cope-encryption">
                    all of
                   </a>
                   the
                   <a href="https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2014/10/eff-response-fbi-director-comeys-speech-encryption">
                    evidence
                   </a>
                   to the
                   <a href="https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2015/07/fbis-revival-crypto-wars-part-ii-continues-two-hearings-congress">
                    contrary
                   </a>
                   , FBI Director Comey wants you to know that he doesn't want another crypto war. As he said today in hearings before the
                   <a href="http://www.judiciary.senate.gov/meetings/going-dark-encryption-technology-and-the-balance-between-public-safety-and-privacy">
                    Senate Judiciary Committee
                   </a>
                   and
                   <a href="http://www.intelligence.senate.gov/hearings/counterterrorism-counterintelligence-and-challenges-going-dark">
                    Senate Select Committee on Intelligence
                   </a>
                   (SSCI), he just wants a discussion. Of course, it's hard to have a discussion when you're not listening to anyone else.
                  </p>

                 <h2>
                  <a href="/deeplinks/2015/07/fbis-revival-crypto-wars-part-ii-continues-two-hearings-congress">
                   FBI's Revival of Crypto Wars Part II Continues At Two Hearings in Congress
                  </a>
                 </h2>
                </div>
                <div class="views-field views-field-term-node-tid">
                 <span class="field-content">
                  <a href="/issues/privacy">
                   Privacy
                  </a>
                  <a href="/issues/calea">
                   CALEA
                  </a>
                  <a href="/issues/law-enforcement-access">
                   Law Enforcement Access
                  </a>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="views-field views-field-body">
                 <div class="field-content">
                  <p class="MsoNormal">
                   The FBI wants to ensure everyday people can't use strong encryption. For over nine months FBI Director James Comey has been pushing the FBI's
                   <a>
                   </a>
                   <a href="https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2014/10/90s-and-now-fbi-and-its-inability-cope-encryption">
                    twenty-year-old talking points
                   </a>
                   <!--[if !supportAnnotations]-->
                   about why he wants to reduce the security in your devices, rather than help you increase it. Director Comey will appear at two hearings about cryptography on July 8: The first in
                   <a href="http://www.judiciary.senate.gov/meetings/going-dark-encryption-technology-and-the-balance-between-public-safety-and-privacy">
                    front
                   </a>
                   of the Senate Judiciary Committee, followed by
                   <a href="http://www.intelligence.senate.gov/hearings/counterterrorism-counterintelligence-and-challenges-going-dark">
                    another
                   </a>
                   in front of the Senate Intelligence Committee.
                  </p>
