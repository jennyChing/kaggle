       <p>
        <br/>
        Up is down. Day is night. Dogs and cats living together. Mass hysteria.
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
        <style>
         .embed-container { position: relative; padding-bottom: 56.25%; padding-top: 30px; overflow: hidden; max-width: 100%; height: auto; } .embed-container iframe, .embed-container object, .embed-container embed { position: absolute; top: 0; left: 0; width: 100%; height: 100%; }

        </iframe>
       </div>
       <p>
        The Patriots are 27th in the NFL in total offense and second in total defense. You read that right. There's no real wrath of god stuff going on but man, it's weird.
       </p>
       <p>
        Those numbers make perfect sense in the aftermath of Sunday's 16-9 Houdini act against the Oakland Raiders, who would have won by three touchdowns if they weren't, you know, the Oakland Raiders. The Pats were rescued by their defense as the offense continued to run in mud with Tom Brady dodging getting squashed into oblivion the way Roger Goodell dodges accountability for the crapstorm that is the current NFL. It wasn't settled until a pinballed interception by Vince Wilfork with 59 seconds left. It was "The Great Escape" but no one was nearly as cool as Steve McQueen.
       </p>
      </div>
      <p class="story-ad-txt overline-weak">

      <div class="l-story-txt no-flow entry-txt">
       <p>
        A win is still a win though, and the Pats do have an extra day to try to figure out how to take the next step, thanks to their match-up against KC coming on Monday Night Football. They probably will. But that doesn't mean we don't have to take one more look at Sunday's horror show. Let's just get it over as quickly as possible and then forget all about it, OK? So with that, here's this week's report card.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         OVERALL TEAM GRADE: C-
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         OFFENSE
        </strong>
        - If it didnt need so much work on every facet of the operation, Sundays performance by the offense would be one of those burn the tape situations.
       </p>
       <p>
        Although there were some positive signs, starting with the Pats posting four drives of double digit plays and more outstanding work from Julian Edelman, as has been made abundantly clear a million times over already, the trouble starts and ends with the offensive line.
       </p>
       <p>
        This unit is in shambles, a combination of some new personnel, a complete lack of identity or leadership (this is the first year neither
        <a href="http://www.patriots.com/media-center/videos/Matt-Light-Career-Highlights/24643bf4-9312-4694-b095-d6c757bf0f3b">
         Matt Light
        </a>
        nor Logan Mankins - two born leaders - hasnt been a part of the O-line since 2000, which, in case you forgot, was before the Pats started winning or contending for championships every year), unfamiliar coaching and well, I dont know what else. Its inexplicable how bad this group has been and all the talk about Bradys chemistry with his receivers or a calf injury or his age or whether the team should commit more to the run or what musical instrument Jordan Devey played in high school is useless until everyone involved figures out how to perform better and more cohesively.
       </p>
       <p>
        As lost as Devey has been, as lousy as Dan Connolly has looked no matter where he lines up for well over two years now (his shotgun snap straight into the turf near the end of the first half was Damien Woody-esque), the key here is Nate Solder.
       </p>
       <p>
        Raiders rookie Khalil Mack owned him all day on Sunday, and if Solder hadnt also been similarly horrid trying to contain Cameron Wake in Week 1, perhaps we could look at it as an aberration or a bad matchup. But something isnt right across the board with this guy, who should be the anchor of the entire group but is more like dead weight
        <a href="https://www.profootballfocus.com/blog/2014/09/22/refo-raiders-patriots-week-3/">
         (Pro Football Focus grades him out at -7.8 for the year
        </a>
        and in the bottom five among the 65-plus tackles who have played at least one down this season). There was more than one occasion when it looked like Brady might not get up and Solder was the behemoth standing by with his hands on his hips.
       </p>
       <p>
        <style>
         .embed-container { position: relative; padding-bottom: 56.25%; padding-top: 30px; overflow: hidden; max-width: 100%; height: auto; } .embed-container iframe, .embed-container object, .embed-container embed { position: absolute; top: 0; left: 0; width: 100%; height: 100%; }
        </style>
       </p>
       <div class="embed-container embed-responsively">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/3f0FqXX69zI">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <p>
        So blame Brady, who looks somewhat fearful for his life out there, if you want. Blame Stevan Ridley for only managing 2.8 yards per rushing attempt. Blame Danny Amendola if you need to be part of that herd. Blame Brandon LaFell for not being able to make it through a game without committing a crushing penalty. Blame the coaches for burying Aaron Dobson or blame Dobson for not doing enough to avoid being buried. Again the Pats are 27th on offense and have scored three TDs since halftime of Week 1. Its tough to believe. But the proof is right there where every play begins. At this point, it doesnt matter why or whos responsible. It just needs to get fixed.
        <strong>
         GRADE: D
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         DEFENSE
        </strong>
        - If there was any kind of silver lining in Sundays pitch dark cloud, the D, which continues to lead this team while the offense desperately grasps for something, wins the prize. The Raiders are better on offense than one might have expected and they were able to put rookie quarterback Derek Carr in very manageable situations throughout the game. The fact that hes mobile and able to make throws outside the pocket will serve him well as he grows into his role as a starting NFL QB. And it certainly kept the Pats from being able to apply the same kind of pressure as they did in the Minnesota game. But overall, the D looked sharp enough to spearhead the win.
       </p>
       <p>
        It all started with Donta Hightower,
        <a href="https://www.profootballfocus.com/blog/2014/09/22/refo-raiders-patriots-week-3/">
         who earned himself a tidy +5.1 grade from PFF
        </a>
        and continues to be the most consistently impressive performer on this defense (not to take anything away from Chandler Jones, who has been sensational at times  Hightower has simply been a little more steady). Hes clearly most comfortable rushing the quarterback, which hes doing more of this season, thanks to his quickness and how easily he can get leverage when he gets a standing head start. He blew up more than one running play, too, but what was perhaps most impressive - and for the second straight week - he looked decisive and aggressive in space and was able to properly read a couple of short passes and stop them before they got started. He even covered a long sideline throw and didnt look like a giant moose trapped in someones kitchen doing it.
       </p>
       <p>
        Ideally, Hightower continues to spend the majority of his time coming off the edge and tormenting opposing QBs. But its a real comfort to see how much better he is at doing everything else he may be asked to do (PFF notes he was at his best in coverage, and if you respond to that by saying you could have predicted it, youre full of bleep) than hes been during his first two seasons.
       </p>
       <p>
        Jones had another very nice afternoon, getting his giant football demon paws on a couple of throws. Rob Ninkovich looked as fresh and active as in any of the first three games, putting some pressure on Carr and dropping into coverage at times effectively. And Wilfork held down the fort admirably once Sealver Siliga got hurt, even before his right-place-at-the-right-time pick to solidify the Pats escape. If Siliga is out for an extended period, not only will that decrease Joness reps on the outside (remember Week 1 when he played Richard Seymour so much thanks to Chris Jones being out?) but it will put more of a burden on Big Vince to play like his old self.
       </p>
       <p>
        And while were discussing old selves, please, please dont get too down on Darrelle Revis. Hes not been quite what we expected in two of the first three games for sure but Carr, who was fearless in this game,
        <a href="https://twitter.com/jeffphowe/status/513775819228184576">
         completing 1,000 percent if his passes when throwing at Revis
        </a>
        is a slightly misleading stat. Revis was right there on all those completions and no one got anywhere near beating him in or near the end zone. The secondary wasnt as stout as in Minnesota, and Logan Ryan wasnt as dynamic as he was against the Vikings either (although the late pass interference call against him was garbage) but its still miles ahead of where its been for years. And after years of Wheatleys, Wilhites, Butlers and Dowlings, thats OK with everyone, right? Thought so.
        <strong>
         GRADE: B
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         COACHING/SPECIAL TEAMS/INTANGIBLES
        </strong>
        - Big time ups to the Pats' special teams, which produced another stellar showing in all areas. Ryan Allen is one of the best punters in the league, Stephen Gostkowski drilled three more field goals and Matthew Slater provided one his four or five yearly reminders as to why hes not only a mainstay for this team, but a captain because of his tremendous skill and savvy covering kicks. After suffering the indignity of having the first punt of the year blocked down in Miami, there have been nothing but positives in this phase of the game for the Pats.
       </p>
       <p>
        <style>
         .embed-container { position: relative; padding-bottom: 56.25%; padding-top: 30px; overflow: hidden; max-width: 100%; height: auto; } .embed-container iframe, .embed-container object, .embed-container embed { position: absolute; top: 0; left: 0; width: 100%; height: 100%; }
        </style>
       </p>
       <div class="embed-container embed-responsively">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/b7jX-cwVya0">
        </iframe>
       </div>
       <p>
        The same cant be said for the coaching staff. From personnel decisions to play calling to time management, it all felt at least slightly off. You could make the argument that all of these factors were also, at least in part, limited due to the nightmare of the offensive line, which couldnt even run block in this game (four negative runs and four for no gain in 29 rushing attempts - gross.), thus hindering the possibility of both leaning on the ground game and using more play action. On D, it felt odd at times that the coaches werent more inclined to do the things that have proved to work best this season (having Revis shadow one receiver while Chandler Jones almost exclusively rushes the passer as was the case in Minnesota) more often.
       </p>
       <p>
        It pays to be unpredictable, of course, but not a week goes by when there isnt a time or two when Belichick or Josh McDaniels seems to outthink himself (in McDaniels case, it was starting the game with three straight pass plays against a defense that had allowed 400 yards rushing in two games).
       </p>
       <p>
        Perhaps everything needs to be streamlined again. There were
        <a href="https://twitter.com/DougKyedNESN/status/514098253613522945?refsrc=email">
         18 different personnel groups used on offense
        </a>
        and while the Pats have always been best on that side of the ball when they are diverse, that mindset could be hurting them right now, especially considering the troubles up front. Whether thats it or if its something else, the Pats are not going to beat many teams playing the way they played on Sunday. History says theyll figure everything out and it will happen soon (
        <a href="http://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201309290atl.htm">
         see Week 4, 2013
        </a>
        ).
       </p>
       <p>
        Cross your fingers.
        <strong>
         GRADE: C
        </strong>
       </p>
