Binsar, Uttarakhand: Simple Life in Birding Paradise
Package Highlights


History: One night stay at Khali Estate, explore the erstwhile home of Mahatma Gandhi and Lord Ramsay

Birding: Explore Binsar Bird Sanctuary and its unmatched avi fauna.

Culture: Spend 2 (or 1) night in a scenic Himalayan Village, trying not to fall in love with the simple life

Adventure: Explore footpaths in the sanctuary, as you hike from village to village

Note: Park closes at 6 pm daily. Guests are advised to arrive on time to avoid holiday mood crushers.
Gypsy Byte
While on our visit we saw 51 species of hill birds and photographed 26 species. The Village experience is sure to leave one's heart one size bigger. We enjoyed the home-cooked delicious food, and were treated like long lost relatives! The stay in Village Guesthouses situated on hillsides with great views of snow capped peaks is rustic but never fails to charm.
Khali Estate has a vibrant history that the friendly owner lovingly called Mamaji will be happy to share with you. Dont miss the Jamini Roy paintings that are a part of the heritage of this wonderful property.
About Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary
In the foothills of the Himalayas, UttarakhandsBinsarWildlife Sanctuaryreigns green, wild and supreme. The Sanctuary is ahaven for Bird Watchersand home to more than 200 species of native and migratory birds ofexotic varietiessuch asWoodpeckers, Forktail, Blackbirds,Laughing Thrush, Kalij Pheasant,Nuthatches,Parakeets, Monal and Koklas. The Sanctuary's chir pine and oak forest hold abundant trails for trekking. It is unlikely that you will see another human being during most of your treks. Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary also holds a thriving population of leopards, Black Himalayan Bear , Wild boars, Kakars, Monkeys Red Fox, Jackals etc.

Itinerary
Our journey begins at the Khali Estate in Binsar. On the next two days, we will go bird watching in the Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary and hike to a nearby village. 
Day 1: Arrive, Relax and Explore Khali Estate
Find yourself in the paradise of Khali Estate and its 25 acres of serene and tranquil Deodar grove. Once the home of Sir Henry Ramsay (the then Commissioner of Kumaon) who built a small cottage here, Khali charms one with its panoramic view of the snow-clad mountains and its lively avi-fauna. Such is the serenity of this place that even Mahatma Gandhi was captivated by it, and built Shail Ashram.  Dinner and Overnight Stay at the Estate
Day 2, Day 3: Explore Binsar Bird Sanctuary and hike to a Village
After Breakfast, leave Khali Estate for the warm welcomes of the nearby villages. Accompanied by your professional yet friendly local guide, set off to explore Binsar Bird Sanctuary as you hike to your next port of call, a local village. Your guide will choose a course for you depending upon your fitness and appetite for adventure. Your walk can be an easy 45 minute saunter through pine forests or a 5 hour trek down into the valley and up again into a village. Porters will be available to carry your luggage should you need their help. Reach the village guest house after building up an appetite. Enjoy lunch prepared by your village hosts.The village communities pride themselves in how well they take care of their guests. Everyone will pitch in to make you feel welcome and comfortable in the village. Explore the homes and fields but don't forget to keep a lookout for bird-life that congregates at the fringes of the village. You can explore the forests around the village accompanied by your guide. Note: Those opting for the 3 night itinerary will be hiking to the next village on the 3rd Day. Each village has its own charm and character and the treks from village to village take you through pristine pine and oak forests. Packed Lunch, Dinner and Overnight Stay at Village Guesthouse.
Final Day: Take Leave and Promise to Come Back
It's breakfast time  and your last chance to enjoy the simple yet tasty village meals. After your vibrant days of trekking through villages and enjoying the views of the Himalayan ranges - its time to leave this bird haven and the village lives. Don't forget to promise yourself to return here someday. Lunch can be provided depending on your schedule. Please share your time of departure with your guide beforehand.

Accommodation




At the Estate, 1N


:


Double or twin well appointed rooms with private bathroom. The rooms are charmingly built like barrels standing on their sides. Your bedroom is a circular affair, with wooden interiors. We were amazed how they fit a spacious crescent shaped bathroom into the affair.
Another secret of the room is a secret passageway that connects two rooms should you be booking more than one room for your group.
Meals are served in the Khali Estate dining room, the staff are friendly and take good care to ensure your comfort.




At the Village Guesthouse, 2N or 3N


:


The Village Guesthouse is a basic accommodation, charming yet simple. Your bed will be of stylish charpoys, that we loved sleeping in. Restroom and shower-room attached but accessible from outside. Food provided will be lovingly cooked by the villagers.

Getting There





Nearest Airport



Domestic and International


Binsar is not directly connected via airways, so you have to reach there either by the railways or roadways. Delhi Airport thus would be the nearest one. From Delhi by road it is through Moradabad  Haldwani  Binsar (410 km)
To book flights to Delhi, click here.




Nearest Railway Station:


Kathgodam (126 kms)is the nearest railhead from Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary. If you want to visit Binsar from New Delhi then take the overnight Ranikhet Express from Old Delhi railway station to reach Kathgodam. From Kathgodam you have to take a taxi to reach Binsar.
For trains to Kathgodam, seat availability and fare, click here.




By Road


Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary is around33 km from Almoraand 96 km from Nainital.You can first go to Kathgodam or Almora and then start for Binsar from any of these places exploring the places on the way. From Delhi by road it is through Moradabad  Haldwani  Binsar (410 km)
To book buses to Haldwani, click here.

         <h2>
          About Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary
         </h2>
         <p>
          In the foothills of the Himalayas, Uttarakhands
          <strong>
           Binsar
          </strong>
          Wildlife Sanctuary
          <strong>
           reigns green, wild and supreme
          </strong>
          . The Sanctuary is a
          <strong>
           haven for Bird Watchers
          </strong>
          and home to more than 200 species of native and migratory birds of
          <strong>
           exotic varieties
          </strong>
          such as
          <strong>
           Woodpeckers
          </strong>
          , Forktail, Blackbirds,
          <strong>
           Laughing Thrush
          </strong>
          , Kalij Pheasant,
          <strong>
           Nuthatches
          </strong>
          ,
          <strong>
           Parakeets
          </strong>
          , Monal and Koklas. The Sanctuary's
          <strong>
           chir pine and oak forest
          </strong>
          hold abundant trails
          <strong>
          </strong>
          for trekking. It is unlikely that you will see another human being during most of your treks. Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary also holds a thriving population of leopards, Black Himalayan Bear , Wild boars, Kakars, Monkeys Red Fox, Jackals etc.
         </p>

         <h2>
          Itinerary
         </h2>
         Our journey begins at the Khali Estate in Binsar. On the next two days, we will go bird watching in the Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary and hike to a nearby village.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <dl class="accordion">
          <dt>
           <a href="#">
            <small class="right">
             +
            </small>
            Day 1: Arrive, Relax and Explore Khali Estate
           </a>
          </dt>
          <dd>
           Find yourself in the paradise of Khali Estate and its 25 acres of serene and tranquil Deodar grove.
           <br/>
           Once the home of Sir Henry Ramsay (the then Commissioner of Kumaon) who built a small cottage here, Khali charms one with its panoramic view of the snow-clad mountains and its lively avi-fauna. Such is the serenity of this place that even Mahatma Gandhi was captivated by it, and built Shail Ashram.
           <strong>
           </strong>
           <br/>
           Dinner and Overnight Stay at the Estate
          </dd>
          <dt>
           <a href="#">
            <small class="right">
             +
            </small>
            Day 2, Day 3: Explore Binsar Bird Sanctuary and hike to a Village
           </a>
          </dt>
          <dd>
           After Breakfast, leave Khali Estate for the warm welcomes of the nearby villages.
           <br/>
           Accompanied by your professional yet friendly local guide, set off to explore Binsar Bird Sanctuary as you hike to your next port of call, a local village. Your guide will choose a course for you depending upon your fitness and appetite for adventure. Your walk can be an easy 45 minute saunter through pine forests or a 5 hour trek down into the valley and up again into a village. Porters will be available to carry your luggage should you need their help.
           <br/>
           Reach the village guest house after building up an appetite. Enjoy lunch prepared by your village hosts.The village communities pride themselves in how well they take care of their guests. Everyone will pitch in to make you feel welcome and comfortable in the village. Explore the homes and fields but don't forget to keep a lookout for bird-life that congregates at the fringes of the village. You can explore the forests around the village accompanied by your guide.
           <br/>
           Note: Those opting for the 3 night itinerary will be hiking to the next village on the 3rd Day. Each village has its own charm and character and the treks from village to village take you through pristine pine and oak forests.
           <br/>
           Packed Lunch, Dinner and Overnight Stay at Village Guesthouse.
          </dd>
          <dt>
           <a href="#">
            <small class="right">
             +
            </small>
            Final Day: Take Leave and Promise to Come Back
           </a>
          </dt>
          <dd>
           It's breakfast time  and your last chance to enjoy the simple yet tasty village meals. After your vibrant days of trekking through villages
           <span style="line-height: 1.2; font-size: 14px;">
            and enjoying the views of the Himalayan ranges - its time to leave this bird haven and the village lives. Don't forget to promise yourself to return here someday.
           </span>
           <br/>
           Lunch can be provided depending on your schedule. Please share your time of departure with your guide beforehand.
          </dd>
         </dl>
