       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Dead Men Walking: Oddsmakers Say 6 NFL Coaches In Real Trouble
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="wp-socializer-buttons clearfix">
         <span class="wpsr-btn">
          <!-- Start WP Socializer Plugin - Facebook Button -->
          <div class="fb-like" data-action="like" data-colorscheme="light" data-href="http://www.bettingsports.com/dead-men-walking-oddsmakers-say-6-nfl-coaches-in-real-trouble/" data-layout="button_count" data-share="false" data-show-faces="0">
          </div>
          <!-- End WP Socializer Plugin - Facebook Button -->
         </span>
         <span class="wpsr-btn">
          <!-- Start WP Socializer Plugin - Retweet Button -->

          </a>
          <!-- End WP Socializer Plugin - Retweet Button -->
         </span>
         <span class="wpsr-btn">
          <!-- Start WP Socializer Plugin - +1 Button -->
          <div class="g-plusone" data-href="http://www.bettingsports.com/dead-men-walking-oddsmakers-say-6-nfl-coaches-in-real-trouble/" data-size="medium">

        </div>
        <div id="fb-root">
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_54183" style="width: 585px">
         <a href="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Burning_Couch.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Now THAT'S a hot seat." class="size-full wp-image-54183" height="350" src="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Burning_Couch.jpg" width="575"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Now THATS a hot seat.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         With less than 48 hours until the
         <a href="http://www.bettingsports.com/nfl/teams/denver/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Denver Broncos
         </a>
         and defending Super Bowl champion
         <a href="http://www.bettingsports.com/nfl/teams/baltimore/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Baltimore Ravens
         </a>
         kick off the start of the season,
         <a href="http://sports.bovada.lv/sports-betting/football-player-props.jsp" target="_blank">
          Bovada Sports released future odds
         </a>
         on the six NFL head coaches most likely to be fired during 2013 regular season.
        </p>
        <p>
         So if profiting financially from the professional and personal failures of others is something that appeals to you, this is something you might want to take a stab at. If anything, it gives you a vested interest in something outside personal sports allegianceskind of like fantasy footballit if was less fun and more mean.
        </p>
        <p>
         As far as the list goes, there really arent any surprises. That being said, lets take a look at a few coaches who really need to consider polishing up the ol resume. Although it seems that only two of them are seriously in jeopardyat the moment.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_54186" style="width: 604px">
         <a href="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Jim%2BSchwartz%2BDetroit%2BLions%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BGiants%2BolxWx5-Ep6Il.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Hopefully he's been working on his anger management issues. " class="size-full wp-image-54186" height="396" src="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Jim%2BSchwartz%2BDetroit%2BLions%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BGiants%2BolxWx5-Ep6Il.jpg" width="594"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Hopefully hes been working on his anger management issues.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.pro-football-reference.com/coaches/SchwJi0.htm" target="_blank">
           Jim Schwartz
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.bettingsports.com/nfl/teams/chicago/ http://www.bettingsports.com/nfl/teams/detroit/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Detroit Lions
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          12/1 or 7.69 percent
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its not a surprise to see Lions coach Jim Schwartz on this list. The real surprise is that his job has been safe this long, given that hes gone well under .500 in three of his four years in Detroit.
        </p>
        <p>
         Considering what a tragic mess the Lions had been for a decade before Schwartz arrived, they did improve vastly in each of his first three seasons. Obviously thats what kept him safe, despite a serious backslide in 2012.
        </p>
        <p>
         After finishing 10-6 and making the playoffs in 2011, last year the Lions went a dismal 4-12, just two games better than his first season in 2009. But Schwartz will be fine as long as he bounces back wit
         <em>
         </em>
         h no worse than an 8-8 record.
        </p>
        <p>
         If Schwartz manages to make the playoffs, he will likely be safe though the end of his contract in 2015.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_54187" style="width: 585px">
         <a href="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/6872818.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="This guy better get off to a fast start. " class="size-full wp-image-54187" height="350" src="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/6872818.jpg" width="575"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          This guy better get off to a fast start.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.pro-football-reference.com/coaches/GarrJa0.htm" target="_blank">
           Jason Garrett
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.bettingsports.com/nfl/teams/dallas/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Dallas Cowboys
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          15/2 or 11.76 percent
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ah yes, Cowboys coach Jason Garrett, the most decidedly average head coach in the NFLbased on his record. He finished 5-3 after taking over for Wade Phillips in 2010, but went 8-8 the last two seasons and has yet to make the playoffs.
        </p>
        <p>
         Garrett having two years left on his contract means absolutely nothing to his famously impatient, and infamously micromanaging owner, Jerry Jones.
        </p>
        <p>
         Although Jones claimed Garretts job was safe during training camp, if it was really safe a contract extension would have been negotiated. That
         <a href="http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/nfl/news/20130720/cowboys-jason-garrett.ap/" target="_blank">
          never happened
         </a>
         . Probably because Jones doesnt want to promise anything more to a guy he doesnt even trust to call plays.
        </p>
        <p>
         Garrett was publicly stripped of his play calling duties in January, with Jones effectively promoting Bill Callahan to offensive coordinator. Of course the fact that it took six months for Garrett to
         <a href="http://profootballtalk.nbcsports.com/2013/06/12/jason-garrett-finally-admits-that-he-wont-call-plays/" target="_blank">
          acknowledge
         </a>
         the move publicly is also very telling.
        </p>
        <p>
         Which is exactly why the line on Garrett doesnt seem quite right. If his Cowboys are worse than 5-5which is certainly possibleat their Week 11 bye in November, they will have a very tough road to the playoffs, given
         <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/team/schedule/_/name/dal/dallas-cowboys" target="_blank">
          their schedule
         </a>
         . In that scenario its hard to imagine hell return in Week 12.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_54188" style="width: 585px">
         <a href="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/359186620-20214800.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Are his fortunes tied to those of QB Cam Newton's?" class="size-full wp-image-54188" height="350" src="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/359186620-20214800.jpg" width="575"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Are his fortunes tied to those of QB Cam Newton?
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.pro-football-reference.com/coaches/RiveRo0.htm" target="_blank">
           Ron Rivera
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.bettingsports.com/nfl/teams/carolina/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Carolina Panthers
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          11/2 or 15.38 percent
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Panthers head coach Ron Rivera has been taking a lot of heat for a guy who has been in Carolina for just two seasons. He was brought in after the teams disastrous 2-14 season in 2010 and went 6-10 and 7-9 his first two years.
        </p>
        <p>
         At this point the real issue isnt with Rivera, but rather Panthers owner Jerry Richardsons lack of enthusiasm for third year starting quarterback Cam Newton, who was selected by the team No. 1 overall in the 2010 NFL Draft. Newton had a record breaking rookie season with Carolina and concern over his supposed drop off last season was greatly exaggerated.
        </p>
        <p>
         This past offseason GM Dave Gettleman
         <a href="http://www.catscratchreader.com/2013/7/31/4574654/cam-newton-panthers-win-now" target="_blank">
          was asked
         </a>
         if Newton was the QB to build around and, after a long pause, he replied, Yes, he is. But now its time to win. Which seemed to echo the same kind of vague reservation Richardson expressed
         <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/story/_/id/6894818/carolina-panthers-jerry-richardson-cam-newton-no-tats-piercings" target="_blank">
          when dictating
         </a>
         the terms of Newtons physical appearance in August 2011.
        </p>
        <p>
         Until these guys are sold on Newton, theyre not going to be sold on Rivera. Even so, neither coach or quarterback will get the boot before the end of the season, sorry oddsmakers.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_54189" style="width: 585px">
         <a href="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Mike%2BMunchak%2BHouston%2BTexans%2Bv%2BTennessee%2BTitans%2BDpm59JSxDydl.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="If his name doesn't ring a bell, you're not alone. " class="size-full wp-image-54189" height="350" src="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Mike%2BMunchak%2BHouston%2BTexans%2Bv%2BTennessee%2BTitans%2BDpm59JSxDydl.jpg" width="575"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          If his name doesnt ring a bell, youre not alone.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.pro-football-reference.com/coaches/MuncMi0.htm" target="_blank">
           Mike Munchak
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.bettingsports.com/nfl/teams/tennessee/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Tennessee Titans
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5/1 or 16.67 percent
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         What can you say about Titans head coach Mike Munchak? He was hired to replace the departing Jeff Fisher in 2011 and went 9-7 and 6-10 his first two years in Nashville.
        </p>
        <p>
         Munchaks first big move was drafting quarterback Jake Locker out of Washington with the No. 8 overall pick of the 2011 NFL Draft. He has started 16 games in his first two seasons with the Titans, but has garnered little confidence that hes their longterm solution at the position.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its hard to imagine owner Bud Adams will pull the plug on either of them before the end of the season, if only because there will be few, if any, great options if he does. Which should give both Munchak and Locker 16 more games to prove themselves worthy of an extension.
        </p>
        <p>
         With just one and two years left on their respective contracts at the end of this season, and with Lockers not being fully guaranteed, Adams wont be on the hook for anything excessive should he decide to kick one or both of them to the curb.
        </p>
        <p>
         And considering Adams is worth $1.1 billion, but 90-years-old, hes going to be more likely to pull the trigger and part with a few dollars than sit by patiently and wait to see if conditions improve.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_54190" style="width: 585px">
         <a href="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Dennis%2BAllen%2BOakland%2BRaiders%2BIntroduce%2BNew%2BQjPVcfchKDQl.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="A Raiders coach on the hot seat? Shocker! " class="size-full wp-image-54190" height="350" src="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Dennis%2BAllen%2BOakland%2BRaiders%2BIntroduce%2BNew%2BQjPVcfchKDQl.jpg" width="575"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          A Raiders coach on the hot seat? Shocker!
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.pro-football-reference.com/coaches/AlleDe0.htm" target="_blank">
           Dennis Allen
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.bettingsports.com/nfl/teams/oakland/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Oakland Raiders
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3/1 or 25 percent
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Dennis Allen was brought in as the Raiders head coach for the 2012 season, which means hes already on borrowed time. In the last 11 years only one coach has survived more than 32 games in OaklandTom Cable stuck around for 44 games, mostly because the late Al Davis wasnt about to pay
         <em>
          another
         </em>
         guy for not working.
        </p>
        <p>
         They Raiders parted ways with Hugh Jackson, who went 8-8 in 2011, to make way for Allen, who went 4-12 in his first season. It doesnt take a genius to see those numbers are going to spell trouble for him if this team doesnt vaguely resemble an NFL football team when their bye rolls around in Week 7
         <a href="http://espn.go.com/nfl/team/schedule/_/name/oak/oakland-raiders" target="_blank">
          their schedule
         </a>
         only gets tougher from there.
        </p>
        <p>
         Considering their ongoing nightmare at the quarterback position and over a decade of abject failure, Allen
         <a href="http://profootballtalk.nbcsports.com/2013/06/14/dennis-allen-no-matter-what-the-experts-say-i-like-this-team/" target="_blank">
          maintains
         </a>
         he likes what he sees in this team and insists he doesnt give a damn about what all those idiot experts have to say.
        </p>
        <p>
         That positive attitude should serve him well while seeking new employment. Allen may very well be fired before midseason, but hell
         <em>
          definitely
         </em>
         be out on his ass come January.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_54191" style="width: 585px">
         <a href="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/rex-ryan-2e92b416e69a7129.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Sorry, Rex. Your days with the Jets are numbered. " class="size-full wp-image-54191" height="345" src="http://cdn.bettingsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/rex-ryan-2e92b416e69a7129.jpg" width="575"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Sorry, Rex. Your days with the Jets are numbered.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.pro-football-reference.com/coaches/RyanRe0.htm" target="_blank">
           Rex Ryan
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.bettingsports.com/nfl/teams/nyjets/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           New York Jets
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3/2 or 40 percent
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         To say things have been bad for Jets coach Rex Ryan recently would be an understatement. After leading this team to an AFC Championship (loss) in his first two seasons, everything he worked to build in his first two years has come crashing down around him in his second.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once the coach every player in the NFL wanted to play for, Ryans friend-first mentality has become a liability in the locker room. With no accountability coming from the top down, disgruntled Jets have taken to venting their frustration in the mediaanonymously. Not an ideal situation.
        </p>
        <p>
         After trying last season to tone down the over-the-top persona he cultivated early in his tenure, Ryan has followed the lead of his players and stopped holding back in recent weeks. With his first round selection of quarterback Mark Sanchez in 2009 still hanging like an albatross around his neck, Ryan has become increasingly combative and defiant with the New York sports media.
        </p>
        <p>
         Particularly when pressed about the starting QB position by reporters, who have been anxiously awaiting a decision on whether it will be Sanchez or rookie Geno Smith starting this weekend. Right now it looks like Smith will get the nod, but considering
         <a href="http://ftw.usatoday.com/2013/09/rex-ryan-clemson-new-york-jets/" target="_blank">
          Ryan ditched
         </a>
         the Jets final cut day to fly to South Carolina for a college football game last weekend, whos to say whats going on in that head of his.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ryan went to tailgate at Clemson and left his dirty work to someone else. A man who is legitimately invested in keeping his job would never do that. Meaning this is a man who is not overly concerned with keeping his job.
        </p>
        <p>
         When GM Mike Tannenbaum got that ax earlier this year, Ryan was lucky to have survived. Of course it probably had more to do with owner Woody Johnson being a cheapskate than any confidence in his head coach, which has clearly been deteriorating since 2012.
        </p>
        <p>
         Right now it seems like Rayn can read the writing on the wall as well as the rest of us. Barring some kind of miracle, this is his final act in New York
        </p>
        <p>
         And Ryans recent behavior seems to suggest hed actually prefer to have it end sooner, rather than later.
        </p>
