   <h1 id="microdata-recipe-name" itemprop="name">
    Self-Rising Crunchy Sugar Cookies
   </h1>
   <img id="microdata-recipe-image" itemprop="photo" src="/shop-img/1337180465344.jpg"/>
   <span id="microdata-recipe-author" itemprop="author">
    Sue Gray
   </span>
   <time datetime="2012-04-30" id="microdata-recipe-published" itemprop="published">
    November 5, 2009
   </time>
   <span id="microdata-recipe-summary" itemprop="summary">
    These cookies are delectably light and crunchy, thanks to the mellow protein level of our self-rising flour.
   </span>
   <span id="microdata-recipe-review" itemprop="review" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/Review-aggregate">
    <span id="microdata-recipe-rating" itemprop="rating">
     4.8
    </span>
    <span id="microdata-recipe-count" itemprop="count">
     11
    </span>
   </span>
   <time datetime="PT20M" id="microdata-recipe-preptime" itemprop="prepTime">
   </time>
   <time datetime="PT10M" id="microdata-recipe-cooktime" itemprop="cookTime">
   </time>
   <time datetime="PT60M" id="microdata-recipe-totaltime" itemprop="totalTime">
   </time>
   <span id="microdata-recipe-yield" itemprop="yield">
    4 dozen cookies
   </span>
   <div id="microdata-recipe-instructions" itemprop="instructions">
    1) Beat together the vegetable shortening or butter (see "tips," below), sugar, and vanilla until fluffy.
    <br/>
    2) Mix in the egg, then the flour. Scrape the bottom and sides of the bowl, then mix again, until thoroughly combined.
    <br/>
    3) Chill the dough for 30 minutes or longer, to make it less sticky and easier to work with.
    <br/>
    4) Near the end of the chilling period, preheat the oven to 375F.
    <br/>
    5) Grease or line with parchment two baking sheets.
    <br/>
    6) Place 1/4 cup granulated or coarse white sparkling sugar in a bowl.
    <br/>
    7) Use a cookie scoop or a spoon to make 1" to 1 1/2" balls. Drop them into the sugar, then place the balls sugar side up onto the baking sheets, leaving about 2" between cookies; they?ll spread as they bake.
    <br/>
    8) Bake the cookies for 8 to 10 minutes, until the edges are lightly browned. The cookies will puff up and look soft, but they?ll fall and become crisp/crunchy as they cool.
    <br/>
    9) Remove the cookies from the oven. Let them rest on the pan for 5 minutes before transferring them to a rack to cool completely. Store well-wrapped cookies at room temperature for a week or so; freeze for longer storage.
    <br/>
    Yield: 4 dozen cookies.
    <br/>

       <section class="sect6">
        <h3>
         Directions
        </h3>
        <!-- wrap button float in block 'group' -->
        <p class="group noprint">
        </p>
        <div id="InstructionSection">
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           1) Beat together the vegetable shortening or butter (see "tips," below), sugar, and vanilla until fluffy.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           2) Mix in the egg, then the flour. Scrape the bottom and sides of the bowl, then mix again, until thoroughly combined.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           3) Chill the dough for 30 minutes or longer, to make it less sticky and easier to work with.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           4) Near the end of the chilling period, preheat the oven to 375F.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           5) Grease or line with parchment two baking sheets.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           6) Place 1/4 cup granulated or coarse white sparkling sugar in a bowl.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           7) Use a cookie scoop or a spoon to make 1" to 1 1/2" balls. Drop them into the sugar, then place the balls sugar side up onto the baking sheets, leaving about 2" between cookies; they?ll spread as they bake.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           8) Bake the cookies for 8 to 10 minutes, until the edges are lightly browned. The cookies will puff up and look soft, but they?ll fall and become crisp/crunchy as they cool.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           9) Remove the cookies from the oven. Let them rest on the pan for 5 minutes before transferring them to a rack to cool completely. Store well-wrapped cookies at room temperature for a week or so; freeze for longer storage.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="group" id="InstructionSet">
          <span id="Instructions">
           Yield: 4 dozen cookies.
          </span>
         </p>
