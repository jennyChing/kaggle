           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://ad.doubleclick.net/ad/Inhabitat/recycled-materials;article=articlename;kw=content1;sz=300x250;ord=123456789?"/>
          </a>
         </noscript>
         <!-- end ad tag -->
         <img alt="UPS, packaging, green, eco, cardboard, paper, shipping, is it green, sung" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-114451" height="357" src="http://assets.inhabitat.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2010/05/Universal-Packaging-System_by-Patrick-Sung_2.jpg" title="Universal-Packaging-System_by-Patrick-Sung_2" width="537"/>
         <p>
          We could see how the concept would not be the most practical for all applications, but it could be really great for mailing a surprise gift to a friend! Soft items like clothing or
          <a href="http://inhabitat.com/2010/04/13/fuseproject-and-puma-revolutionize-the-shoe-box/">
           shoes
          </a>
          , or even products that are rigid, like a
          <a href="http://inhabitat.com/2010/03/12/karim-rashid-unveils-water-filtering-bobble-bottle/">
           funky reusable water bottle
          </a>
          , could be perfect for this packaging. Not to mention that the perforated lines give the package an interesting graphic pattern style. There is something to be said about the efficiency of boxes that stack, which is why it is great that the sheet can also be folded into standard 6-sided boxes.
         </p>
         <p>
          Sung has branded his concept the
          <em>
           UPACKS
          </em>
          (Universal Packaging System). Although it is not related to the shipping carrier
          <a href="http://www.ups.com">
           United Parcel Service
          </a>
          , the naming likeness could attract some attention for this waste reducing,
          <strong>
           <em>
            inside the box
           </em>
          </strong>
          packaging concept.
         </p>

             </p>
             <p>
              By perforating/creasing the corrugated board, you remove ALL the strength a traditional box provides. Corrugated sheets derive ALL their strength via the CORRUGATIONS (where the packaging material gets its name). Are you form fitting the product? Yes. However, are there better, more environmentally friendly materials, lighter weight materials, less manual labor intensive practices that can accomplish this same task? Yes! If I were Patrick, I would bring this idea to a UPS or FedEx and they would in turn show him just how aggressive a sorting facility will be on this product/package.
             </p>
