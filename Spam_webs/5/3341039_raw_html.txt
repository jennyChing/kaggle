        </h1>

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          At the end of a long day, I can fall pretty much instantly to sleep.
         </p>
         <p>
          Sometimes, the lovely bride will roll over a few hours later and ask, Whats that scratching sound? It sounds like something enormous is dragging its claws along the entire side of the house.
         </p>
         <p>
          And then, she goes back to sleep. If she was even awake in that moment to begin with.
         </p>
         <p>
          Fast forward to me, prowling armed through my house at three in the morning, when virtually everything seems to be making scratching noisesor preparing to. And naturally, when the family dog sees me, walking slowly and silently down the hallway, he reacts with an air of alarm. Which furthers my paranoia. Which makes him more alarmed. Which leaves us both staring at each other in a dark kitchen at three a.m, before I tell him to stop being such a sally before I scurry back to bed.
         </p>
         <p>
          This time, my hunt for the elusive North American Scratchbeast yielded no results, but I
          <strong>
           will
          </strong>
          be victorious.
         </p>
         <p>
          At the very least, I know I can look forward to hearing Angelas description of it echo in my ears the next time I try to fall asleep.
         </p>
