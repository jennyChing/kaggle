           6 Common Diseases of Miniature Schnauzers
          </h1>

        <div class="format_text entry-content">
         <p>
          Miniature schnauzers are an extremely
          <a href="http://www.alldogbreedsonline.com/types-dogs-make-great-pets/" target="_blank">
           popular breed
          </a>
          around the country today. They are often classified as energetic, spirited and intelligent, and dont forget about how cute they are. The sad thing is they have a number of health conditions and diseases that tend to be quite common among the breed. When you know what conditions are common in this
          <a href="http://www.alldogbreedsonline.com/dogs/miniature-schnauzer/" target="_blank">
           small dog breed
          </a>
          , you can identify any potential problems with your pet early on. If problems develop later on down the line, you will be prepared to handle them as they arise.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alldogbreedsonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Miniature_schnauzer.jpg" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Miniature schnauzer 288x216 f improf 288x216 6 Common Diseases of Miniature Schnauzers" class="size-medium wp-image-1678 aligncenter" height="216" src="/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/_d_improd_/Miniature_schnauzer-288x216_f_improf_288x216.jpg" title="" width="288"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Portosystemic Shunt
         </h3>
         <p>
          These small dog breed puppies are prone to liver shunts. The disease strikes when the blood doesnt flow properly to the liver. Toxins end up accumulating within the blood when this happens. Symptoms can include blindness, deafness, seizures, vomiting, drooling, bladder stones, excessive drinking and urination and diarrhea.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Bladder Stones
         </h3>
         <p>
          Bladder stones tend to be quite common in Miniature Schnauzers. It could be the result of a bladder infection or mineral excretion from the kidneys. Signs of bladder stones are the need to urinate more frequently, trouble urinating and blood in the urine. An x-ray at the veterinarian will help to diagnose whether your dog has bladder stones or not.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Hyperlipidemia
         </h3>
         <p>
          Miniature schnauzers have the tendency to become fat. They are known for having exceptionally high cholesterol levels. Hyperlipidemia is when there are extremely high fat levels in the blood, which leads to seizures, pancreatitis and eye problems. Symptoms of this condition include vomiting, lethargy, diarrhea, seizures, tender abdomen and a white discoloring of the eye.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alldogbreedsonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Miniature-Schnauzers.jpg" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Miniature Schnauzers 288x191 f improf 288x191 6 Common Diseases of Miniature Schnauzers" class="size-medium wp-image-1679 aligncenter" height="191" src="/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/_d_improd_/Miniature-Schnauzers-288x191_f_improf_288x191.jpg" title="" width="288"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Pancreatitis
         </h3>
         <p>
          Another common disease in this small dog breed is that of pancreatitis. The disease is an inflammation of the pancreas. This often occurs the dog has consumed something their stomach couldnt handle, such as a fatty food. The dog might have pancreatitis if they have lethargy, diarrhea, vomiting or a tender abdomen. Many of these small dog breeds are plagued with the condition throughout their entire lives, while some will experience more than one bout of the condition.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Eye Diseases
         </h3>
         <p>
          Eye diseases tend to be another one of the common problems for Miniature Schnauzers. The two most common types of eye diseases this breed can get is progressive retinal atrophy and cataracts. Signs of cataracts are loss of vision, blindness and a cloudiness in one of the eyes or both at the same time.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Skin Conditions
         </h3>
         <p>
          This small dog breed is predisposed to any number of different skin conditions such as allergic dermatitis and comedone syndrome. Most of these small dogs will struggle with some type of skin problem within their lifetime.
         </p>
         <p>
          Comedone syndrome is often distinguished by small pimple-like bumps appearing on the back of the dog. They tend to be prone to infection. If the blackhead areas are to become infected, they can become itchy.
         </p>
         <p>
          Allergic dermatitis means that the dog is allergic to something or another that contacted their skin. Symptoms of a reaction include scratching, itching, inflamed skin, rubbing, hair loss and a lot of licking to the areas that are affected.
         </p>
