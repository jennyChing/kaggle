     <h1 class="no_bottom">
      What If Everybody in Canada Flushed At Once?
     </h1>
     <p class="date">
      Written by Pats Papers | Monday, 8 March 2010 2:42 PM
     </p>
     <img alt="" class="imgborder" src="http://www.patspapers.com/images/uploads/flush_game.jpg"/>
     <br/>
     <p>
      The water utility in Edmonton, EPCOR, published the most incredible graph of water consumption last week. By now youve probably heard that up to 80% of Canadians were watching last Sundays gold medal Olympic hockey game. So I guess it stands to reason that theyd all go pee between periods.
     </p>
     <p>
      But stillthe degree to which the water consumption matches with the key breaks in the hockey game is stunning.
     </p>
     <p>
      Its been 20 years since my days as a beat reporter at CFRN (old screen shot below) and CITV in Edmonton, so it was nice to get an Edmonton news tip. Thanks to
      <a href="http://twitter.com/robertgorell" title="@robertgorell">
       @robertgorell
      </a>
      <a href="http://twitter.com/tcollen" title="@tcollen">
       @tcollen
      </a>
      and
      <a href="http://twitter.com/yoni" title="@yoni">
       @yoni
      </a>
      for sending this my way on Twitter. After our PatsPapers.com story swirled around the internet Monday, a reporter at the
      <a href="http://www.theglobeandmail.com/news/national/bathroom-breaks-could-wait-during-gold-medal-match/article1494358/" title="Globe and Mail">
       Globe and Mail
      </a>
      picked up the story and contributed an interview with EPCOR. (Ignore the fact that the Globe misread EPCORs ML as millilitres instead of megalitres.)
     </p>
