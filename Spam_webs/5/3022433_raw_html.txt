        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Car Dealership Staff Berates Pizza Driver, Internet Gets Revenge Via Scathing Reviews (VIDEO/SCREENSHOTS)
        </h1>
       </div>
      </header>
      <div class="wrapper">
       <div class="grids">
        <div class="grid-8 column-1">
         <div class="entry-media">
         </div>
         <div class="single-box clearfix entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">
          <!--Ad Injection:top-->
          <div style="">
           <center>
            <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
            </script>
            <!-- IYON 336 x 280 Large Rec Before All Articles -->
            <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-9292789304436098" data-ad-slot="8808602446" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
            </ins>
            <script>
             (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
            </script>
           </center>
          </div>
          <p>
           A Massachusetts car dealership is discovering the power of the internet as its reputation on
           <em>
            Yelp
           </em>
           descends into the one-star zone after posting video of staff berating the man who delivered their pizza, and stiffing him for a tip to boot.
          </p>
          <p>
           The fine people at used car dealershipF&amp;R Auto Sales pooled money to buy a pizza from Palace Pizza, the unluckiest business in the statethat day. The order came to just over $42, and the employeesgave the driver, Jarrid Tansey, $50  two twenties and two fives.
          </p>
          <p>
           Obviously, as he had been handed $45 plus another $5, the driver assumed he had been given a generous tip. He thanked his customers and left. According to Tansey, no one asked for change.
          </p>
          <p>
           However, all was not well at F&amp;R. Expecting change from the 50 bucks they handed him for their $42 order, the staff was furious that they had not received the extra five dollar bill and change from the other $45 back.
          </p>
          <p>
           Unfortunately, what may seem to mostlike a reasonable, $7 tip for a $42 order, was a matter of life and death to the staff at F&amp;R. The owner of the dealership called the pizza delivery place and complained to the manager.
          </p>
          <p>
           The driver was forced to use his own resources to return to the used car dealership and return the $7  and the
           <em>
            fine
           </em>
           folks at F&amp;R Auto Sales were ready.
          </p>
          <p>
           In a security camera recording that was posted to YouTube under the title, irate pizza driver (The video
           <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zOF_6L5TRYA" target="_blank">
            has since been made private
           </a>
           ,but was preserved on
           <a href="http://www.liveleak.com/view?i=030_1421237536" target="_blank">
            Liveleak
           </a>
           ), the staff of the car dealership began by ganging up on the delivery man.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <center>
           <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="360" src="http://www.liveleak.com/ll_embed?f=8c8e8fde26f2" width="640">
           </iframe>
          </center>
          As though they expected him to somehow possess the ability to read minds, F&amp;R employees chastised Tansey for not possessing the telepathic powers necessary to know that they wanted him to hand back the extra $5 they gave him, as well as change. By the end of the video, the mob is berating, and even threatening, the driver.
          <p>
           As Tansey says in the video:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            It just doesnt make sense why youd hand me a bill that you were just gonna have me drive back here to give you back anyway.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           An employee replies:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            Because I gave you everything that they gave me so I would get the correct change.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           Tansey then asks:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            So, you handed me a five dollar bill that you were going to ask for back?
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           Correct, replied the employee. You ever gone to the supermarket and you give them a certain amount of money, so they give you back your change?
          </p>
          <p>
           Not a whole extra bill, Tansey responded.
          </p>
          <p>
           As Tansey attempted to leave, one employee tells him, Lets not be mad.
          </p>
          <p>
           Im not mad, I just had to waste my resources coming back here, the driver replies.
          </p>
          <p>
           So listen: The manager apologized once for you. Do you want him to apologize again for you? the employee in blue presses.
          </p>
          <p>
           Its okay, you got your $7 back, so the world is right now. Tansey says as he finally was able to make his exit.
          </p>
          <h2>
           The last straw
          </h2>
          <p>
           As Tansey is leaving, a female worker threatens:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            Out the door before I put my foot in your ass.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           After the unfortunate driver gets the hell out of there, the employees continue to belittle him. One man indicates that he wants Tansey fired,
           <em>
            even though he and his fellow car dealership employees are the ones who acted inappropriately
           </em>
           :
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            Get the f*cking owner and the manager on the phone, I want that mother-f*cker done. I want him fired.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           Fortunately, our hero will not be fired.Pizza Palace has elected not to discipline the driver, and some have
           <a href="http://www.gofundme.com/xx9js?fb_action_ids=1001771459852130&amp;fb_action_types=og.shares&amp;fb_ref=fb_g_my_t" target="_blank">
            begun raising money for Tansey
           </a>
           . The pizza shop has elected not to punish Tansey, according to managerAdam Willoughby.
          </p>
          <p>
           This isnt the first time we have had problems with F&amp;R Auto, Willoughby explained. He says the owner of F&amp;R and his son stopped by Pizza Palace to apologize on Wednesday.
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            I was the manager on that night, and today they came and spoke to me, profusely apologized.We both want to make things right between us. We dont want any bad blood.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           Naturally, the car dealership proudly posted the video online (with the intent of humiliating the poor driver), but the internet responded in a way they surely didnt expect. F&amp;Rs Yelp page is overrun with one-star reviews, many of which are pretty damn funny:
          </p>
