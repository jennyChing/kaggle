         <img alt="Siblings" src="http://learn.livingdirect.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/siblings.jpg" style="width:100%;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         In many families, separate bedrooms for siblings are simply not feasible. And yet, decorating a shared room can be a challenge, especially if you want to allow children to express their own personalities and interests. After all, how do you combine ponies and Transformers or Scooby Doo and The Incredible Hulk?
        </p>
        <p>
         It sounds challenging. But with some out-of-the-box thinking and creative use of furniture, paint and dividers, you can create a shared space that reflects the individuality of each child.
         <br/>
         <span id="more-1339">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Create Fun Combinations
        </h3>
        <p>
         Even when children have different interests, you may be able to find ways to combine them in fun ways. Do you have a couple of tweens who each have their own favorite celebrities? Create a celebrity themed room that gives equal space to posters of each of their favorites.
        </p>
        <p>
         Does one of your sons love cars while the other loves outer space? Decorate the bottom half of walls with decals and posters of cars and the top half with decals and posters of rocket ships and other things space-related.
        </p>
        <p>
         Is one of your daughters a ballerina while the other loves princesses? Look for dancing princesses or mix up the princesses and ballerinas throughout the room. Youll have a fun space that celebrates each childs own favorite passions.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. Use Color to Identify Each Childs Space
        </h3>
        <p>
         If your childrens interests are more diverse (or if they cant agree on some creative combinations), divide the space and allow each child their own section of the room.
         <a href="http://learn.livingdirect.com/painting-tips/">
          Painting the room
         </a>
         is a quick and easy way to accomplish this.
        </p>
        <p>
         Paint can create a visual divide in the room. Use a neutral color (black, tan or white, for example) to create a large stripe on the walls and ceiling where you want to divide the room. Then paint and decorate each side of the room based on that childs interests. You might have pastel pink with Disney princesses on one side and purple with jungle animals on the other side. Or, blue with a sports theme on one side and pink with unicorns on the other.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want a more classic look for the room, purchase three paint colors  allowing each child to choose a color for the wall that is solely on their side of the room and the third color for the opposite walls that will be shared by the children. You can define a color palette ahead of time to ensure that the colors blend well together, or allow for a more eclectic color combination.
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Physically Divide the Room
        </h3>
        <p>
         There are many ways to physically divide a shared room. For younger children, a gate that stretches across the length of the room can be an easy and simple way to create a division. For older children who object to having a baby gate in their room, a half wall might be a solution if youre willing to spend the time and money to build it.
        </p>
        <p>
         For a less permanent solution, use a curtain or hanging beads suspended from the ceiling to create a wall. Or use privacy screens or hinged room divider panels to create false walls. Room dividers come in materials ranging from paper and fabric to wood or bamboo with a variety of designs so you should be able to find one that fits your dcor and household style.
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Make Creative Use of Furniture
        </h3>
        <p>
         Bookshelves or dressers can be placed across the center of the room to create division between each childs personal space. Be sure to check that the furniture is sturdy and cant be easily tipped over, especially if your children are young.
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Use Vertical Space
        </h3>
        <p>
         Unless your childrens bedroom is spacious, you may have a challenge finding space for all of their belongings. Add bookcases, install shelving units, and use the walls. Decorative hooks can hold your daughters jewelry and hairbands. Those same hooks can hold baseball caps and belts. Hanging storage organizers (such as those used for shoe storage) can be used to store small toys or other items.
        </p>
        <p>
         Using taller furniture or wall space can be especially helpful when there is a significant age difference between children. You can store items for your 10 year old out of reach of a 4-year-old sibling. Not only will that make the room safer for the younger sibling, but it can help prevent the inevitable conflict that happens when a younger sibling accidentally breaks your older childs favorite possession.
        </p>
        <p>
         You may not be able to give each child their own room. But, with some imagination and resourcefulness, you can give them each a space that feels like their own and shows off their individual personalities  the next best thing to having their own rooms.
        </p>
        <p>
         A little creativity can create separate space for each child. What are your tips for separating a room amongst siblings?
        </p>
