  <meta content="Each table is assigned a chef from the open kitchen at Vue de Monde At $60 for the 1 hour lunch at Vue de Monde it is not the cheapest in town by far. But it adds up to so much more than two courses and a glass of wine as an experience; I defy you to not order more or want to return. The whole experience is high theatre and extreme camp from the ornate cutlery, the colourful chaise lounges and the many flourishes that make this such an enjoyable experience while enjoying the view from the top of the Rialto Towers. Really to make the most of it, I should have dressed like Karl Lagerfeld. The room is minimal with a wide open kitchen. The tables are broad and wide and stretched with kangaroo leather, the seats are backed by Skippy fur. In the centre of each table sits a collection of stones and some twigs from a Penfolds vineyard. Prawn Russian salad or salade Rusee de crevettes. Youll need to book in advance and you should ensure prompt arrival to ensure you maximise you allocated time, although we were able to enjoy an extra glass of wine in the Lui Bar after. Then another, some cheese, a bottleit got messy although after seven hours there we got our cost per minute down to around 50 cents. The twigs become a rest for your ornate cutlery while one of the stones is revealed to be hollowed out to contain salt and pepper. Bread is unlimited and placed in the centre of the arrangement in a kangaroo hide bucket while quenelles of butter are scooped from a large wooden bucket imported from France. First take your pestle The menu gives two choices for each course, cheese being one for dessert. The big surprise is when a pestle arrives with the cutlery for the second course followed by a mortar of herbs and flowers. Our personal chef pours in liquid nitrogen and we are invited to crush, before mixing with a cucumber sorbet. Wagyu beef, dried apricot, Jerusulem artichoke, smoked bone marrow. Sauted potatoes of sorts arrive but there is a twist with them packaged in newspaper and neatly tied with string. Surgical scissors cut the string as the pack is folded out and secured with stones.           <h1 class="entry-title single-title" itemprop="headline">
           Lunch at Vue de Monde at $1 a minute
          </h1>
          <p class="byline vcard">
           Posted
           <time class="updated" datetime="2011-11-2" pubdate="">
            November 2nd, 2011
           </time>
           by
           <span class="author">
            <a href="http://tomatom.com/author/chingo/" rel="author" title="Posts by Ed">
             Ed
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="amp">
            &amp;
           </span>
           filed under
           <a href="http://tomatom.com/category/eat-streets/melbourne/" rel="category tag">
            Melbourne
           </a>
           .
          </p>
         </header>
         <!-- end article header -->
         <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tomatom/6300617230/" title="PA074140 by gastrotom, on Flickr">
            <img alt="PA074140" height="375" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6036/6300617230_f3cbcd463d.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           <em>
            Each table is assigned a chef from the open kitchen at Vue de Monde
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="drop_cap">
            A
           </span>
           t $60 for the 1 hour lunch at
           <a href="http://www.vuedemonde.com.au/">
            Vue de Monde
           </a>
           it is not the cheapest in town by far. But it adds up to so much more than two courses and a glass of wine as an experience; I defy you to not order more or want to return.
          </p>
          <p>
           The whole experience is high theatre and extreme camp from the ornate cutlery, the colourful chaise lounges and the many flourishes that make this such an enjoyable experience while enjoying the view from the top of the Rialto Towers. Really to make the most of it, I should have dressed like Karl Lagerfeld.
          </p>
          <p>
           The room is minimal with a wide open kitchen. The tables are broad and wide and stretched with kangaroo leather, the seats are backed by Skippy fur. In the centre of each table sits a collection of stones and some twigs from a Penfolds vineyard.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tomatom/6300614192/" title="PA074135 by gastrotom, on Flickr">
            <img alt="PA074135" height="375" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6231/6300614192_21581d5f48.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           <em>
            Prawn Russian salad or salade Rusee de crevettes.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           Youll need to book in advance and you should ensure prompt arrival to ensure you maximise you allocated time, although we were able to enjoy an extra glass of wine in the
           <a href="http://www.tomatom.com/2011/08/lui-bar-vue-de-monde/">
            Lui Bar
           </a>
           after. Then another, some cheese, a bottleit got messy although after seven hours there we got our cost per minute down to around 50 cents.
          </p>
          <p>
           The twigs become a rest for your ornate cutlery while one of the stones is revealed to be hollowed out to contain salt and pepper. Bread is unlimited and placed in the centre of the arrangement in a kangaroo hide bucket while quenelles of butter are scooped from a large wooden bucket imported from France.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tomatom/6300618800/" title="PA074144 by gastrotom, on Flickr">
            <img alt="PA074144" height="375" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6046/6300618800_90bf73ff97.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           <em>
            First take your pestle
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           The menu gives two choices for each course, cheese being one for dessert.
          </p>
          <p>
           The big surprise is when a pestle arrives with the cutlery for the second course followed by a mortar of herbs and flowers. Our personal chef pours in liquid nitrogen and we are invited to crush, before mixing with a cucumber sorbet.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tomatom/6300620386/" title="PA074147 by gastrotom, on Flickr">
            <img alt="PA074147" height="375" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6042/6300620386_0ce2bd253e.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           <em>
            Wagyu beef, dried apricot, Jerusulem artichoke, smoked bone marrow.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           Sauted potatoes of sorts arrive but there is a twist with them packaged in newspaper and neatly tied with string. Surgical scissors cut the string as the pack is folded out and secured with stones.
          </p>
          <p>
           We got carried away with the theatre and went over the top. Ill be back but perhaps with a little more self discipline.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tomatom/6300088111/" title="PA074149 by gastrotom, on Flickr">
            <img alt="PA074149" height="375" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6211/6300088111_07c74c51e9.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           <em>
            Pure Theatre
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tomatom/6300088711/" title="PA074150 by gastrotom, on Flickr">
            <img alt="PA074150" height="375" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6107/6300088711_0d608f6136.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tomatom/6300089611/" title="PA074152 by gastrotom, on Flickr">
            <img alt="PA074152" height="375" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6227/6300089611_57aa37bd08.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tomatom/6300623218/" title="PA074157 by gastrotom, on Flickr">
            <img alt="PA074157" height="375" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6107/6300623218_a6caceb7e0.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           <em>
            Old Telegraph Road (second from left) is the only Australian cheese.
           </em>
          </p>
          <a href="http://www.urbanspoon.com/r/71/762025/restaurant/CBD/Vue-de-monde-Melbourne">
           <img alt="Vue de monde on Urbanspoon" src="http://www.urbanspoon.com/b/link/762025/biglink.gif" style="border:none;width:200px;height:146px"/>
