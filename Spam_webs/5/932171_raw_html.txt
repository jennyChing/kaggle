      <h1>
       Cross cultural relationships.
      </h1>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.barendspsychology.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/cross-cultural-relationships.jpg">
        <img alt="cross cultural relationships" class="alignright wp-image-1801 size-medium" height="216" src="http://www.barendspsychology.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/cross-cultural-relationships-e1424614097693-300x216.jpg" width="300"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       To some extend everyone deals with
       <strong>
        cross cultural relationships
       </strong>
       , because two different families come together and always need to adjust at least a little bit. For the majority overcoming cultural differences comes naturally, for the minority the cultural differences may be difficult to understand and overcome. In
       <strong>
        c
       </strong>
       <strong>
        ross cultural relationships
       </strong>
       both partners have to give up some of their own culture and adjust to another cultures beliefs, habits, parenting tactics, and they may even need to deal with the others (not supportive) family.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Cross cultural relationships
       </strong>

Through communication (understanding and listening to each other) we learn about one anothers stories, overcome cultural differences and start focusing on the future. This article is for those people with

      <h2>
       Cross cultural relationships  dealing with differences.
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Do not make assumptions
        </strong>
        .
        <br/>
        In
        <strong>
         cross cultural relationships
        </strong>
        itis common to defend your own culture and to make assumptions about your partners culture. We simply do not like change that much, since change means adjustment and adjustment can be scary. But its these assumptions about your partners culture you make that makes your partners culture look more scary, silly or even weird. Cultural stereotypes are a source of misunderstanding and often kill the discussion even before it even started. Instead, focus on your partners personality, beliefs and opinions and figure out which parts of your partners culture is important to your partner. Find out why your partners culture has certain habits, beliefs or traditions, before even comparing it to your own culture.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Respect differences
        </strong>
        .
        <br/>
        Living with differences is one thing, but respecting each others differences is something else. In some
        <strong>
         cross cultural relationships
        </strong>
        couples do live with each other but have some passive aggressive feelings towards their partners different lifestyle due to their culture. Your partner feels your passive aggressiveness and does not make the two of you grow closer as a couple. In stead of judging your partners different lifestyle, or beliefs, habits and opinions, try to understand where they are coming from and respect that not everyone has the same opinion and lifestyle as you do. Sometimes reading into your partners culture and history make it easier for you to understand your partner.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Understand and explore
        </strong>
        .
        <br/>
        Your partners culture is a part of your partner. So getting to know your partner means that you have to get to know your partners culture. A huge advantage of
        <strong>
         cross cultural relationships
        </strong>
        is that you have an expert of that culture who can tell you about all the ins and outs. Your expert can tell you about the delicious food, beautiful countryside, ways of celebrating national holidays, birthday parties and much more. The more you get to know about your partners culture, the easier it becomes to understand your partner. By showing your partner that you are curious, your partner will automatically also show more interest in your culture. And remember: you dont have to adopt everything your partners culture has to offer.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Dont be offended by criticism
        </strong>
        .
        <br/>
        In relationships, especially
        <strong>
         cross cultural relationships
        </strong>
        , it is normal to criticize one another from time to time. It may get very personal if your partner criticizes your culture or the way youve been raised. In
        <strong>
         cross cultural relationships
        </strong>
        criticism on the partners culture is often an attempt to defend and protect their own culture. By responding angry, your criticizing partner will probably become more defensive and the discussion will lead nowhere.In stead of being offended and angry, try to laugh about your partners criticism, because your partner is probably ignorant. And then ask why your partner thinks the way he does. Criticism often provides an opening to talk about differences in opinion and to get to know each other better.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Keep what matters most to you
        </strong>
        .
        <br/>
        Living with your partner doesnt mean you have to adopt every single habit, belief or ritual. Often, in
        <strong>
         cross cultural relationships
        </strong>
        habits, rituals and beliefs are so diverse that it is impossible for you or your partner to adopt every single one of them. Especially, since at the same time it would mean that you have to give up some of your own. The best thing to do is to pick those rituals, habits and beliefs that make you happy or more complete and respect the ones you do not like so much.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Be patient
        </strong>
        .
        <br/>
        Every person has its own rhythm and pace in a relationship. For some people it is easier to adjust to changes and new cultural elements in a relationship, whereas for other this may take a little longer. Be patient, because rushing will only put more pressure on your partner and will either push away your partner or make her walk on egg shells. If you think your partner is worth it, then patience should not be a problem at all.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Plan for the future
        </strong>
        .
        <br/>
        Making plans for the future can be tricky. For some it is normal to talk about children after one month, for others it is never a good time to talk about children. However, especially in
        <strong>
         cross cultural relationships
        </strong>
        it is important to talk about your plans for the future, so you will not be surprised in the end. Think of the way your partner wants to marry, how many children, where he wants to live and so on It might not seem important in the beginning, but after a while it may be these differences in opinion that can drive the two of you apart.
       </li>
      </ul>
