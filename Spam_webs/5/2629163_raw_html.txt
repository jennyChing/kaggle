        Some linky goodness
       </h1>

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         I know that I need to tell you about Plettenberg Bay (as promised
         <a href="http://6000.co.za/road-trip-oudtshoorn/" target="_blank" title="Road Trip post part 1">
          here
         </a>
         ), but all in good time. In the meanwhile (whats a meanwhile?) here are some interesting stories for your perusal.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is not to be confused with a Wednesday Ephemera post, despite the obvious similarities.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Fracking news
         </strong>
         : In
         <a href="http://biznewz.com/cool-heads-please-sa-contemplates-discovery-equal-310-years-oil-consumption/" target="_blank" title="Link">
          a post
         </a>
         that is more about the disgraceful hounding of the late Tony Twine than our energy reserves or whether or not we should be extracting them, comes the (repeated) news that theres LOADS of gas under the Karoo and even if we can get at just a bit of it, it will have a HUGE effect on the country.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          The potential was so mind-boggling Twine tried to tone down expectations. He ran his econometric model on just 10% of what the USs Energy Information Administration (EIA) had published as the Karoo Basins recoverable reserves.Even at this conservative level, the model concluded shale gas would create 850 000 jobs and, for a minimum of 25 years, generate annual economic growth equivalent to 9.6% of 2010s GDP.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         In a country so bogged down by youth unemployment, a frankly terrifying economic outlook and widespread pessimism WHY ARE WE STILL WAITING to move on this?
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Things which are connected to Fracking news
         </strong>
         : While the US economy continues to recover, use of clean shale gas instead of coal has assisted in reducing that countrys
         <a href="http://arstechnica.com/science/2013/10/us-carbon-emissions-hit-lowest-level-since-1994-despite-economic-growth/" target="_blank" title="Link">
          carbon emissions
         </a>
         to levels not seen since 1994:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Last year, the US saw its lowest carbon emissions since 1994, continuing a downward trend that began in 2008 during the economic crisis. It marks the second year in a row that carbon emissions have dropped despite a growth in gross domestic product. Prior to the last few years, economic growth had been closely tied to increased carbon emissions.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <a href="http://6000.co.za/?s=fracking&amp;searchsubmit=Search" target="_blank" title="Link">
          Other fracking related posts
         </a>
         on
         <em>
          6000 miles
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Todays bizarre story of the day
         </strong>
         : A man who forgot to book the venue for his wedding decided that rather than admitting this to his wife-to-be, hed phone the place just before the ceremony and
         <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-merseyside-24624639" target="_blank" title="Link">
          claim that there was a bomb
         </a>
         on the premises.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          as she got dressed on the morning of the wedding he slipped out of the house, went to a phone box and, disguising his voice, told the receptionist at Liverpool Register Office, which is part of St Georges Hall:
         </p>
         <p>
          This is not a hoax call.Theres a bomb in St Georges Hall and it will go off in 45 minutes.
         </p>
         <p>
          The call, 11 days after the Boston Marathon bombing, provoked terror and the building was immediately evacuated and emergency services called.He was given a 12-month jail sentence after admitting making the hoax call.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         And yes, theyre still together. Awww.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Bascule Bar at the Cape Grace is now a bit rubbish
         </strong>
         : According to
         <a href="http://waterfrontwhisky.wordpress.com/2013/10/22/the-bascule-whisky-bar-capegrace-has-collapsed-its-time-to-rebuild/" target="_blank" title="Link">
          this post
         </a>
         , anyway. I havent been there for years, but they do (did?) stock
         <a href="http://www.tamdhu.com/" target="_blank" title="Link">
          Tamdhu
         </a>
         , which is my whisky of choice when I have a spare9 to drop on a shot. Would it be worth putting up with the (allegedly) rubbishy chairs, the lack of soul, the poorly motivated staff and the thieving prostitutes though?
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          What is worse is that prostitutes are camped out at the bar. Im not nave  Bascule is at The Cape Grace Hotel, a superb 5 star property. Ladies for hire are a fact of life at many 5 star hotels in tourist cities. But at Bascule, they have taken over. There are many stories around of customers being robbed and accosted by the women. A few Friday evenings ago I went with an old friend  someone who at one stage shared a wine locker with me That Friday it resembled a seedy pool bar, without the pool tables. There was a 19-year-old prostitute at the bar  and she left with an elderly German tourist. Another woman at the bar stole a bottle of cider  slipped it into her handbag. There was not a glass of whisky in sight.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Actually, I think it probably would. Especially if they get some pool tables in.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Nigel Clough is the new Blades manager
         </strong>
         : a little over 20 years after United ended his Dads managerial career with a 2-0 win at the City Ground (Glyn Hodges and Brian Gayle the scorers, if memory serves) in a game that he played in, Nigel Clough has been announced as the
         <a href="http://www.sufc.co.uk/news/article/20131023-nigel-clough-appointed-1130477.aspx" target="_blank" title="Link">
          new Sheffield United manager
         </a>
         . The board at the club have said some lovely things about him:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Nigel was the clear, first choice of the board. He brings an approach to the game that is well suited to what we are trying to achieve at Sheffield United. Nigel has enjoyed managerial success in the game. He knows how to inspire first team players to give their all and has a proven track record of making an academy a key ingredient to the clubs success. We count ourselves lucky to have Nigel aboard.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Just as they did about David Weir a few months back:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          We are thrilled to appoint David and the three-year contract emphasises the fact that he will assist in a change in club culture.I have stated before that the club needs to act differently and David is young, has a great pedigree and will bring a new vision and leadership to the club.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Weir was rubbish and lasted just 13 league games.
         <br/>
         So what of Nigel? Well surely keep you informed.
        </p>
