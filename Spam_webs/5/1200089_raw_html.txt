        <h2 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
         <a href="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/yummy-alsace-food-delicacies/" rel="bookmark" title="Yummy Alsatian Delicacies">
          Yummy Alsatian Delicacies
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <div class="post-content">
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         France is well known for its high culinary standards and variety  and Alsace food is no exception. Its history is tinted with both French and German heritage, creating a very rich mix of flavors, for the pleasure of our taste buds. Most meals out happen at the closest
         <em>
          winstub (
         </em>
         the Alsatian equivalent of British pubs, but with checkered table cloths), which offers a laid-back atmosphere in a convivial decor where locals gather to enjoy local delicacies and wines. Good enough for me!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4092" style="width: 522px">
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/eurotriptips/6146798452/in/set-72157624130221750">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Alsace food, riesling wine" class="size-full wp-image-4092 " height="342" src="http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/alsace1.jpg" title="riesling wine" width="512"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/yummy-alsace-food-delicacies/&amp;media=http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/alsace1.jpg&amp;description=Yummy Alsatian Delicacies')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Local riesling
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <strong>
           Riesling wine
          </strong>
         </span>
         : probably my favorite discovery during the
         <span style="color: #99cc00;">
          <a href="http://www.eurotriptips.com/alsace-roadtrip-itinerary/">
           <span style="color: #99cc00;">
            roadtrip
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         . This type of wine is rather dry and just a little bit acid, but very refreshing and aromatic. I had the chance to visit a winery and bring a few bottles back home and Ive been cherishing every sip since then! Alsatian Riesling is always served in these types of glasses, with a long, green stem and a wide bowl, which is said to be best for the floral scents of the wine. Other local and popular wines are
         <span style="color: #99cc00;">
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinot_gris#Alsace">
           <span style="color: #99cc00;">
            pinot gris
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         and
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gew%C3%BCrztraminer#France">
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           gewurztraminer
          </span>
         </a>
         .
         <em>
          Sant!
         </em>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4210" style="width: 416px">
         <a href="http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/alsace21.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Alsace food, salty bretzels" class="size-full wp-image-4210 " height="546" src="http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/alsace21.jpg" title="bretzels" width="406"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/yummy-alsace-food-delicacies/&amp;media=http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/alsace21.jpg&amp;description=Yummy Alsatian Delicacies')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Saltly pretzels!
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <strong>
           Pretzels
          </strong>
         </span>
         : a delicacy very well known throughout Germanic Europe, history says that pretzels date back as far as the
         <span style="color: #99cc00;">
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pretzels#History">
           <span style="color: #99cc00;">
            12th century
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         ! Every country has its own recipe, for both the daily treats and the special holidays, sometimes salty and sometimes sweet  in Alsace, it is baked traditionally and decorated with salt crystals. Most often than not, pretzels are sold in street cars or small bakeries, and arent very expensive (usually around 1 euro), making it the perfect treat after a long walk!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4211" style="width: 522px">
         <a href="http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/IMG_1470.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="flammekueche, alsace food" class="size-full wp-image-4211 " height="384" src="http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/IMG_1470.jpg" title="IMG_1470" width="512"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/yummy-alsace-food-delicacies/&amp;media=http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/IMG_1470.jpg&amp;description=Yummy Alsatian Delicacies')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Flammekueche
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <strong>
           Flammekueche
          </strong>
         </span>
         : probably the most iconic dish of the Alsace food repertoire, this pizza lookalike is usually composed of a very thin bread dough, onions, fresh cream and lardons. Although the French translation (tarte flambe) suggests that it is flamed with alcohol, it is actually cooked in a traditional wood-fire oven for a deliciously crispy taste. It has several variations (even sweet ones!), but the traditional version remains my favorite. If you are familiar with the chain
         <span style="color: #99cc00;">
          <a href="http://www.les3brasseurs.com/">
           <span style="color: #99cc00;">
            Les Trois Brasseurs
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         , its their emblem dish!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4212" style="width: 522px">
         <a href="http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/IMG_9721.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Sauerkraut, alsace food" class="size-full wp-image-4212 " height="331" src="http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/IMG_9721.jpg" title="IMG_9721" width="512"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/yummy-alsace-food-delicacies/&amp;media=http://www.eurotriptips.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/IMG_9721.jpg&amp;description=Yummy Alsatian Delicacies')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Sauerkraut
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <strong>
           Sauerkraut
          </strong>
         </span>
         : this widely known dish is often associated with Germanic Europe, and Eastern France is no exception. Sauerkraut is basically fermented cabbage served with sausages, but the Alsatian twist adds other pork meats (bacon in this case) and potatoes. And if you cant bear the energy of making one yourself, visit any French grocery store and buy the slightly-less-appetizing-but-ready-to-microwave version!
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Have you ever tasted Alsace food? Whats your favorite? Have you ever been into a winstub? Or do you prefer traditional French food?
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
