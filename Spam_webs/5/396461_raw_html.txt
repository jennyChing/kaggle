    <div class="single-title">
     <h1>
      Your Sustainability Profession: A Small Piece of a Big Puzzle
     </h1>
     <div class="clear">
     </div>
     <div class="single-info">
      <span class="single-date">
       <strong>
        Published On
       </strong>
       14/02/2014 |
      </span>
      <span class="single-author">
       By Laura Elizabeth Clayton Paul |
      </span>
      <span class="single-category">
       <strong>
        <em>
         <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/category/urban-design/sustainable-living/" rel="category tag">
          Sustainable Living
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/category/urban-design/" rel="category tag">
          Urban Design
         </a>
        </em>
       </strong>
      </span>
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="clear">
    </div>
    <!-- Single Content -->
    <div class="single-content">
     <p>
      Green roofs: one mention, by a fellow student, was literally the only thing even remotely sustainably minded in my Civil Engineering bachelor degree. I longed not only for technical sums to be solved, but also world issues.
     </p>
     <p>
      Was I in the wrong field?
     </p>
     <p>
      It certainly seemed so 10 years ago, as I stepped tentatively into my freshman year. Yet now, sustainability has become increasingly recognized as an essential part of any
      <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/triple-bottom-line-the-modern-business-model/" target="_blank" title="Triple Bottom Line  The Modern Business Model">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         responsible
        </strong>
       </span>
      </a>
      <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/vertical-forest-answer-urban-sustainability/" target="_blank" title="Is This Vertical Forest the Answer to Urban Sustainability?">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         building
        </strong>
       </span>
      </a>
      <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/biomimetics-natural-problem-solving/" target="_blank" title="Biomimetics: Natural Problem Solving">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         design
        </strong>
       </span>
      </a>
      . Its been heartwarming to see the industry change so rapidly, and Ive been blessed to have a front row seat on the action.
     </p>
     <p>
      The US EPAs
      <a href="http://www.epa.gov/climatechange/ghgemissions/sources.html" target="_blank" title="Inventory of Greenhouse Gas Emissions and Sinks">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         Inventory
        </strong>
       </span>
      </a>
      of Greenhouse Gases (GHG) and Sinks,identifies electricity production as the number one cause of GHGs, making up over 1/3 of all emissions. This does not touch on all of the other environmental loads caused by the building sector, such as: material extraction, transport, and ongoing maintenance. Its impossible to overlook the magnitude of impact the built environment has on the resilience of the planet. We all have vested interest in buildings, spending the majority of our time living and
      <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/office-lifestyle-assessment-organically-balanced-offices/" target="_blank" title="Office Lifestyle Assessment  Organically Balanced Offices">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         working
        </strong>
       </span>
      </a>
      in them.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1859" style="width: 661px">
      <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/future-of-green-building.jpg">
       <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
        <img alt="future-of-green-building" class="size-full wp-image-1859" height="375" src="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/future-of-green-building.jpg" width="651"/>
        <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/your-sustainability-profession-small-piece-of-big-puzzle/&amp;media=http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/future-of-green-building.jpg&amp;description=Your Sustainability Profession: A Small Piece of a Big Puzzle')">
        </span>
       </span>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       <em>
        The future of green building  scifi city settlement. Image via LeArchitecto,
        <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic-131026658/stock-photo-scifi-city-settlement-in-space-concept-illustration.html?src=bS9eljND-2IpEg1J4GgMbg-1-88" target="_blank" title="Shutterstock">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <strong>
           Shutterstock
          </strong>
         </span>
        </a>
        .
       </em>
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      This cannot be just a movement for the elite or for the sustainability specialists  we all have a role to play from Urban Planners, Architects, Engineers, Builders, Product Manufacturers, Facility managers, Owners, Tenants, to the General Public.
     </p>
     <p>
      Most of my heroes are unsung. They are the people who quietly, without fanfare, roll up their sleeves and live out their beliefs. Some are young, some poor, some with little to no building design knowledge, but through their individual actions, the world is changing.
     </p>
     <p>
      Never underestimate the positive impact you can make, first in your own life and then slowly increasing your circle of influence to your family, your career, your workplace, your community, your city, your country, the world. Over the past couple of years, I have saved up and DIY-ed myself to a more
      <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/the-difference-between-going-green-and-being-sustainable/" target="_blank" title="The Difference Between Going Green and Being Sustainable">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         sustainable
        </strong>
       </span>
      </a>
      home, recording some of my journey on my
      <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://lauraecpaul.wordpress.com/" target="_blank" title="Laura EC Paul">
         blog
        </a>
       </strong>
      </span>
      . Im not close to being finished, but there is beauty and satisfaction in the translation of theory to your own journey.
     </p>
     <p>
      If you are an Engineer  what is the embodied energy of the materials you are specifying? If youre an Architect  do you consider deconstruction during design? If you are a home owner  what energy reducing initiatives are you implementing? If you are a manufacturer  what is the cradle to cradle impact of your product? If you are a student  have you rallied for environmental improvement of facilities? Whats one action you can commit to start pursuing today?
     </p>
     <p>
      Im not in the wrong field, and neither are you.
     </p>

      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="textwidget">
      <p>
       As the editor of PowerHouse Growers Group (PHG), I strive to ensure that our content is timely, well-researched, and on the cutting edge of urban agriculture and sustainability.
      </p>
      <p>
       I edit for PHG because I admire its vision, and I love being a part of moving that vision forward in a way that positively impacts individuals and organizations in all areas. In the future, I aim to further the cause of green building practices by developing sustainable homes and buildings that go above and beyond certifications like LEED - structures that not only serve our basic human needs, but provide healthy habitation while maintaining modern design.
      </p>
