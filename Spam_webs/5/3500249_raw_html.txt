        <h1 class="entry-title single-title clearfix" itemprop="headline">
         Who Has the Most Web Servers?
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- end article header -->
       <div class="social-buttons clearfix">
        <p class="entry-meta clearfix imageDate">
         <span>
          by
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/archives/author/richm/" rel="nofollow external">

       <div class="entry-content clearfix clear" itemprop="articleBody">
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4832" style="width: 480px">
         <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4832 " height="313" src="http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/1and1serverroom.jpg" title="1and1serverroom" width="470"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          A look inside one of the data centers operated by 1&amp;1 Internet, which is among the companies with the most web servers.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Which company has most web servers? We first examined the question in April 2009, prompted by news that
         <a href="http://www.rackspace.com">
          <strong>
           Rackspace
          </strong>
         </a>
         now had 50,000 servers. We went looking for other companies that publicly report their server counts, and put together a list of those whose numbers we know, along with a rundown of companies that have extensive Internet infrastructure but dont disclose their server counts. Heres a look at our list of
         <strong>
          Who Has The Most Servers
         </strong>
         , gleaned from public reports and partial data from a recent
         <a href="http://news.netcraft.com/hosting-provider-server-count">
          Netcraft server count
         </a>
         report.
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Updated
         </strong>
         , in July 2013 to add new Microsoft count, Facebooks estimate, eBays DSE number and reflect continuing growth at OVH, Akamai and Rackspace:
        </p>
        <div class="in-content">
         <script type="text/javascript">
          if (!window.inet_ord) { inet_ord = Math.random()*100000000000; }
		if (!window.inet_tile) { inet_tile = 1; }
		document.write('&lt;script type="text/javascript" src="http://ad.doubleclick.net/adj/inet.dck/hosting;pos=mrec_content;sz=300x250;article=10509;tile='+inet_tile+';ord=' + inet_ord + '?" &gt;&lt;/scr' + 'ipt&gt;');
		inet_tile++;
         </script>
        </div>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Microsoft
          </strong>
          has more than 1 million servers, according to CEO Steve Ballmer (July, 2013)
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Facebook
          </strong>
          has hundreds of thousands of servers (Facebooks Najam Ahmad,
          <a href="http://gigaom.com/2013/06/19/did-facebook-finally-let-its-server-count-slip/">
           June 2013
          </a>
          )
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.ovh.co.uk/aboutus/">
           <strong>
            OVH
           </strong>
          </a>
          : 150,000 servers (company, July, 2013)
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Akamai Technologies
          </strong>
          : 127,000 servers (company, July 2013)
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.softlayer.com/">
            SoftLayer
           </a>
          </strong>
          : 100,000 servers (company,
          <a href="http://www.softlayer.com/press/release/1501/softlayer-achieves-record-setting-revenues-for-third-quarter">
           December 2011
          </a>
          )
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Rackspace
          </strong>
          : The strong growth of the Rackspace Cloud has boosted the total for this San Antonio-based provider to 94,122 servers as of March 31, 2013 (Source:
          <a href="http://ir.rackspace.com/phoenix.zhtml?c=221673&amp;p=irol-newsArticle&amp;ID=1817218&amp;highlight=">
           Company press release
          </a>
          )
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Intel
          </strong>
          : 75,000 servers (
          <a href="http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/archives/2011/08/03/intel-it%E2%80%99s-top-5-reasons-for-implementing-cloud/">
           company
          </a>
          , August, 2011)
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.1and1.com">
            1&amp;1 Internet
           </a>
          </strong>
          : More than 70,000 servers (
          <a href="http://order.1and1.com/xml/order/Facts;jsessionid=6871CAD0838D6C042319C702F701A437.TCpfix141b?__frame=_top&amp;__lf=Static">
           company
          </a>
          , Feb. 2010)
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           eBay
          </strong>
          : 54,011 servers (
          <a href="http://dse.ebay.com/">
           DSE dashboard
          </a>
          , July 2013)
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           LeaseWeb
          </strong>
          : 36,000 servers (company, Feb. 2011)
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Intergenia
          </strong>
          : (PlusServer/Server4You), 40,000 servers (
          <a href="http://www.plusserver.net/unternehmen/">
           company
          </a>
          , 2013)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Of course, there are a number of providers who have at least 50,000 servers and dont publish the information. Who else is in the club?
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-10509">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres a list of companies we believe arerunning at least 50,000 servers:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Google:
          </strong>
          The search giants server count has long been the focus of speculation. The company doesnt release numbers, but a recent report from energy expert Jonathan Koomey estimated that Google had
          <a href="http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/archives/2011/08/01/report-google-uses-about-900000-servers/">
           900,000 servers
          </a>
          , based on an extrapolation from data Google provided on its total energy usage. Googlesrecently revealed
          <a href="http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/archives/2009/04/01/google-unveils-its-container-data-center/">
           container data center
          </a>
          holds more than 45,000 servers, and thats a single facility built in 2005.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong style="font-size: 13px;">
           Amazon:
          </strong>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           It runs the worlds largest online store and one of the worlds largest cloud computing operations. Amazon saysvery little about its data center operations, but we know that it bought
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/archives/2009/03/20/amazon-86-million-in-servers-in-2008/" style="font-size: 13px;">
           $86 million inservers
          </a>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           from Rackable in 2008, and stores
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/archives/2009/02/26/amazon-s3-now-stores-40-billion-objects/" style="font-size: 13px;">
           40 billion objects
          </a>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           in its S3 storage service. A 2009 analysis by Randy Bias estimates that
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/archives/2009/10/01/report-ec2-running-40000-servers/" style="font-size: 13px;">
           40,000 servers
          </a>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           are dedicated to running Amazon Web Services EC2.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong style="font-size: 13px;">
           Yahoo
          </strong>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           :While its data center infrastructure isnt quite as enormous as those for Google and Microsoft, the third major search portallikelyhas more than 100,000 servers in operation to support its large free hosting operation as well as its paid hosting service and Yahoo Stores.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           GoDaddy:
          </strong>
          Itsthe worlds largest domain registrar with more than 35 million domains under management, but effective cross-selling of its hosting plans has also made GoDaddyone of the largest shared hosting operations in the world.Its infrastructure is probably similar in scope to that of 1&amp;1 Internet.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           HP/EDS
          </strong>
          :While server ownership is less distinct with system integrators, EDS has an enormous data center operation.
          <a class="broken_link" href="http://www.eds.com/services/hosting/downloads/webhosting_aag.pdf">
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Company documents
           </span>
          </a>
          (PDF) say EDS is managing 380,000 servers in 180 data centers. (Thanks, Bruce for the link).
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           IBM
          </strong>
          : With more than 8 million square feet of data center space, IBMalso houses an enormous number of servers in its data centers, both for itself and its customers.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Have we missed anyone obvious? Let us know in the comments.
        </p>
