       Imagine, then, the shock, utter disappointment, and disgust when the local ABC news affiliate, KSTP
       <a href="http://kstp.com/news/stories/S3612199.shtml?cat=1">
        ran a news story on Thursday night
       </a>
       claiming that Mayor Hodges dangerously flashed a gang sign with a known criminal and has put officers in harms way. Follow the trail below.
      </div>
      <div class="body">
       <p>
        First, ABC reporter Jay Kolls
        <a href="https://twitter.com/JayKollsKSTP/status/530567839246475264">

It's so outrageous and has such a phony feel to it that it seems almost like a practical jokeexcept it's completely real. In it, current and retired police officers claim that Mayor Hodges has put the safety of officers in serious risk by her deliberate and flagrant action.

        <br/>
        Does the Minneapolis ABC affiliate KSTP not have a filter in which something that smells this bad gets flagged for poor journalism?

       <p>
        Sadly, only racism allows such an ugly story and stereotype to be advanced about a young man who was clearly not flashing a gang sign with the mayor of Minneapolis. He deserves a public apology and heads should roll at this station for even allowing it to ever make it to the air.
       </p>
