    <h2 class="cnbc-clearfix pagehead" id="page_header">
     <a class="header_title" href="/european-central-bank/">
      European Central Bank
     </a>
    </h2>
    <div class="story-top">
     <div class="story-header-left twoCol">
      <h1 class="title" itemprop="name">
       ECB firing 'lots of small bullets': El-Erian
      </h1>
      <div class="source" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
       <a href="/drew-sandholm/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
        Drew Sandholm
       </a>
       |
       <a href="/katy-barnato/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
        Katy Barnato
       </a>
      </div>
      <time class="datestamp" datetime="2014-06-05T12:47:20-0400" itemprop="datePublished">
       Thursday,  5 Jun 2014 | 12:47  PM ET
      </time>
      <span class="sourcelogo" id="sourcelogo-cnbccom" itemprop="sourceOrganization">
       CNBC.com
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="story-header-right">
      <div>
       <div class="share-count-container hide">
        <div class="share-total">
        </div>
        <div class="share-text">
         SHARES
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="gigyasharebar" id="social_bar_top">
       </div>
      </div>
      <a data-comments-label="Article" data-disqus-identifier="cnbcnewsstory_101734950" href="#">
      </a>
     </div>
    </div>
    <section class="cols2">
     <div class="unit col1">
      <article>
       <div class="story">
        <div class="content" id="article_body">
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="player embed-container cnbcvideo autoplay">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="player" data-src="http://player.cnbc.com/cnbc_global?byGuid=3000280405&amp;autoPlay=true&amp;endCardGuids=3000383239|3000383217|3000383353" frameborder="0" height="298" id="player_0_0" mozallowfullscreen="" msallowfullscreen="" oallowfullscreen="" scrolling="no" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="533">
            <p>
             El-Erian's ECB reaction
            </p>
            <p>
             CNBC's Rick Santelli speaks to Mohamed El-Erian, Allianz chief economic advisor, about the authority of the European Central Bank. El-Erain also explains why the euro is higher after today's ECB measures..
            </p>
           </iframe>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            With a series of measures announced Thursday, the European Central Bank has fired a "lots of small bullets" at the
            <span>
             euro zone's growth-sapping disinflation,
            </span>
            <span>
             but so far, the markets are not sure it will work, f
            </span>
            ormer Pimco co-CEO Mohamed El-Erian told CNBC on Thursday.
           </p>
           <p>
            The central bank decided to take the unprecedented step of imposing a negative interest rate on banks for their deposits as part of an array of initiatives to spur economic growth.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/06/05/ecb-cuts-main-interest-rate-to-015-from-025-cuts-deposit-rate-to-010.html" target="_self">
             Draghi announces host of economic measures
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            "So they move simultaneously on lots and lots of fronts, knowing that the experience away from them hasn't been great on a standalone," El-Erian said on "
            <a href="/squawk-on-the-street/" target="_self">
             Squawk on the Street
            </a>
            ." "They're trying a package approach hoping that somehow they're going to get to critical mass."
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            But, the
            <span>
             chief economic adviser at
            </span>
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="9178" data-inline-quote-symbol="ALV-DE" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/ALV-DE" target="_self">
             Allianz
            </a>
            <span>
             added
            </span>
            <span>
             , the moves are "a little bit like firing lots and lots of small bullets" rather than a big bazooka.
            </span>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container last">
          <div class="embed-container image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
           <img alt="Mohamed El-Erian, during the Bretton Woods Committee annual meeting at World Bank headquarters in Washington May 21, 2014." height="298" src="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2014/06/05/101734889-RTR3Q7S0.530x298.jpg?v=1401978824" width="530"/>
           <meta content="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2014/06/05/101734889-RTR3Q7S0.jpg?v=1401978824" itemprop="image"/>
           <meta content="width='530'" itemprop="width"/>
           <meta content="height='298'" itemprop="height"/>
           <meta content="2014-06-05T10:33:44-0400" itemprop="datePublished"/>
           <meta content="2014-06-05T10:31:51-0400" itemprop="dateCreated"/>
           <meta content="2014-06-05T10:33:44-0400" itemprop="dateModified"/>
           <meta content="2014-06-05T10:31:51-0400" itemprop="uploadDate"/>
           <meta content="2099-12-31" itemprop="expires"/>
           <!-- TODO: defaulting to 2099 until we get the expiry date from toolbelt -->
           <meta content="jpg" itemprop="encodingFormat"/>
           <meta content="Reuters" itemprop="author"/>
           <meta content="CNBC" itemprop="publisher"/>
           <meta content="Mohamed El-Erian, during the Bretton Woods Committee annual meeting at World Bank headquarters in Washington May 21, 2014." itemprop="caption"/>
           <div class="attribution">
            Reuters
           </div>
           <div class="caption">
            Mohamed El-Erian, during the Bretton Woods Committee annual meeting at World Bank headquarters in Washington May 21, 2014.
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            ECB President Mario Draghi, speaking at the central bank's June monetary policy meeting, said the rate on its deposit facility for banks has dropped from 0 percent to minus 0.10 percentthe first time a major global central bank has moved rates into negative territory.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             It also cut its main interest rate to from 0.25 percent to 0.15 percent, and cut the rate on its marginal lending facility by 35 basis points to 0.4 percent from 0.75 percent.
            </span>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            The ECB announced other initiatives, too, including longer-term refinancing operations (LTROs), in which the central bank lends to banks at low interest rates in order to encourage them to lend to households and nonfinancial corporations.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            In addition, he said the ECB had undertaken "preparatory work" in order to conduct a form of U.S.Federal Reserve-style bond-buying by purchasing asset-backed securities (ABS) from banks.
           </p>
           <p>
            Furthermore, Draghi said the ECB would cease sterilizing the liquidity injected from its Securities Markets Program. The program involved the purchase of bonds from troubled "peripheral" euro zone countries.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/06/05/live-blog-ecbs-draghi-poised-to-unveil-stimulus.html" target="_self">
             Live blog: ECB cuts rates, more stimulus on the way
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Basically, the ECB is simply "buy[ing] time for the system to heal," El-Erian explained.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            The central bank had no choice but to take these actions, too, he added, because European policymakers have so far failed to do the things that "really matter," like improve productivity and competitiveness. The new strategy is an attempt by the ECB to put more credit and money into the system, but only few market observers think it will work, he said.
           </p>
           <p>
            "What people are basically betting on is, for the time being ... this is a tranquil environment," he said. "Of course there is a fallacy of composition here ... If we all believe it and we all go too far, in terms of leveraging risk factors, at the end of the day, we may find ourselves in a rather unpleasant situation and that is the risk right now. It's financial stability versus using an imperfect policy instrument."
           </p>
           <p>
            To El-Erian, time is of the essence because Europe's economy is so fragile and there are only so many actions left to take.
           </p>
           <p>
            "There are very few spare tires left on this car, and this car is navigating a bumpy road."
           </p>
          </div>
