         <a href="http://lh6.ggpht.com/_tDp8wecNH-Y/S2btKGa7IlI/AAAAAAAAGMI/tvs52P7fE9A/s1600-h/OrangeStickyBuns3%5B6%5D.jpg">
          <img alt="Orange Sticky Buns" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="321" src="http://lh5.ggpht.com/_tDp8wecNH-Y/S2btK467_sI/AAAAAAAAGMM/07Cr2AiEDdc/OrangeStickyBuns3_thumb%5B4%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="display: border: 0px;" title="Orange Sticky Buns" width="467"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Is there anything more wonderful than waking up to the aroma of sweet rolls baking in the oven? My sweet friend Jamie,
         <a href="http://lifesafeast.blogspot.com/2010/01/focaccia-farcita-stuffed-focaccia.html">
          Lifes a Feast
         </a>
         , asked us to bake bread for her birthday. So for her virtual Birthday/Bread Baking Day #26, I woke up early and baked her Whole Wheat Orange Cranberry Sticky buns.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="250" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_JjKJSk4FUpE/S0RRTWB6BfI/AAAAAAAAGxg/kWBRnLsYqT8/s400/breadbakingday26.jpg" width="130"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Ive been seeing lots of scrumptious cinnamon rolls on the web lately, but since one of my sons isnt crazy about cinnamon I decided to make orange rolls instead. I Googled orange rolls and came across a fun blog I hadnt visited before,
         <a href="http://frugaldrmom.blogspot.com/">
          Frugal Dr. Mom
         </a>
         . She had an
         <a href="http://frugaldrmom.blogspot.com/2008/11/orange-sticky-buns.html">
          Orange Sticky Bun
         </a>
         recipe that I knew my troops would love.
        </p>
        <p>
         I substituted my favorite
         <a href="http://www.barbarabakes.com/2008/12/favorite-dinner-rolls.html">
          dinner roll recipe
         </a>
         for the buns. I used whole wheat pastry flour, bread flour and vital wheat gluten, but it works just as well with all purpose flour if youd rather. I also used a simple orange glaze instead of the sour cream and butter glaze that Dr. Mom used and sprinkled on sweetened dried cranberries. These rolls were scrumptious  sweet and light and fluffy. My boys and I managed to quickly devour an entire pan full in one sitting!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="OrangeStickyBuns" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="339" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/_tDp8wecNH-Y/S2btNM0s2SI/AAAAAAAAGMc/o8Ha_Eq1_JI/OrangeStickyBuns_thumb%5B4%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="OrangeStickyBuns" width="461"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Jamie and I were born in the same year and this is a milestone birthday for us. Be sure and stop by her blog on February 5 and wish her Happy Birthday and check out all of the scrumptious birthday treats the blogosphere baked up for her. Happy Birthday Jamie!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

            <div class="b-b h-1 strong" id="zlrecipe-title" itemprop="name">
             Whole Wheat Orange Cranberry Sticky Buns

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <div class="ingredient no-bullet-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0">
             Filling:
             <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="//stats.ziprecipes.net/piwik.php?idsite=1&amp;rec=1" style="width: 1px; height: 1px; border:0" width="1"/>
            </div>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             3/4 cup sugar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 Tablespoons grated orange rind
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 cup sweetened dried cranberries (Craisins)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 -2 tablespoons butter, softened
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
            <div class="ingredient no-bullet-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6">
             Buns:
            </div>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 cups bread flour
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup whole wheat pastry flour
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 teaspoons vital wheat gluten
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 package rapid-rise (instant) yeast
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup milk (120 to 130)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup sugar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-13" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup butter
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-14" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoon salt
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-15" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 egg
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-16" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
            <div class="ingredient no-bullet-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-17">
             Glaze:
            </div>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-18" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup powdered sugar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient no-bullet" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-19" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 tablespoons fresh orange juice
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Directions
           </p>
           <span class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Filling: In a small bowl, mix  cup of sugar with orange rind; set aside.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             In a large mixing bowl, mix 1 cup bread flour and 1 cup whole wheat pastry flour, wheat gluten, and yeast. Heat and stir milk, sugar, 1/4 cup butter, and salt until butter is melted and milk is between 120 to 130. Add to flour mixture, add egg. Beat at low speed for 1/2 minute. Beat 3 minutes at high speed.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Mix in the remaining flour to make a moderately stiff dough. (Add more or less if necessary.) Knead until smooth (6 to 8 min.). Shape into a ball. Place in greased bowl, turn. Cover, let rise until double (45 to 60 Min.).
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Preheat oven to 350. Punch dough down and turn out onto floured surface. Divide into 2 equal parts. Roll each part into a 12? circle. Brush circles with softened butter. Sprinkle with orange-sugar mixture. Sprinkle with cranberries. Cut each circle into 8 pie-shape wedges. (I like to use a pizza cutter for this.)
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Cover the filling with a piece of wax paper and gently press the filling into the dough. Starting at wide end, roll up wedges. Place point side down in greased 913? pan. Cover and let rise in warm place until almost double in size, about 1 hour.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Bake, uncovered, 20 minutes or until golden brown.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Remove from oven; cool 15 minutes. Combine powdered sugar and orange juice. Drizzle over rolls.
            </p>
           </span>

            If you're using fresh cranberries, cut them in half and simmer them in a saucepan with a couple of tablespoons of water and the greater amount of sugar for 8 to 10 minutes. Keep the heat low and give the mixture an occasional stir.
            <br/>
            If you're using dried cranberries, put aside one quarter of the filling ingredients for later.
           </p>
