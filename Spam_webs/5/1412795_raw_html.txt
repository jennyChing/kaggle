      Gamers Pirating The Sims 4 in Protest Are Being Trolled By a Full Screen Censor
     </h1>
    </header>

         <h2 class="article-excerpt">
          You wanna play The Sims 4 for free, do ya? Well, I hope you like a blurry mess.
         </h2>
         <span class="division">
         </span>
         <div class="meta">
          <time class="featured-main-date date" datetime="2014-09-03">
           <i class="icon-clock ir">
           </i>
           September 3rd, 2014
          </time>
          <span class="featured-main-author post-author">
           <i class="icon-user ir">
           </i>
           Jonathan Leack
          </span>
          <div class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style">
           <a class="addthis_button_facebook_like" fb:like:show_faces="false">
           </a>
           <a class="addthis_button_tweet" tw:count="horizontal" tw:via="craveonline">
           </a>
           <a class="addthis_button_google_plusone" g:plusone:count="true" g:plusone:size="medium">
           </a>
          </div>
          <script>
           var addthis_config = { "data_track_addressbar": false };
          </script>
          <script src="//s7.addthis.com/js/300/addthis_widget.js#pubid=ra-5140fa9a2e73387c&amp;async=1">

         <div class="js-article-content article-content">
          <p>
           As with virtually any gamesadly, there is a pirated version of
           <em>
            The Sims 4
           </em>
           floating around the internet. Although pirates have been successful in acquiring the game for free, its at a cost, albeit not a monetary one.
          </p>
          <p>
           The current cracked version of
           <em>
            The Sims 4
           </em>
           has anissue that is reminding pirates that they cant always get away with stealing. When players with the hacked version send their Sim to go to the bathroom or take a shower, either the full screen or the Sim is permanently censored. In the case of the former, the game is a blurry mess.
          </p>
          <p>
           This has caused some funny circumstances on the internet, where players are prone to asking for help when bugsare encountered. There are examples of users posting on forums asking for help, questioning why their screen is blurred. These users have become the laughing stock of communities onthe official forums, fan sites, and even Reddit.
          </p>
          <p>
           Here is what the mentioned issue lookslike based on screenshots submitted by three users who were caught red handed:
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Dk1LHUn" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-full wp-image-754163" data-lazy-src="http://cdn3-www.craveonline.com/assets/uploads/2014/09/Dk1LHUn.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="370" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="658"/>
          </p>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="Dk1LHUn" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-754163" height="370" src="http://cdn3-www.craveonline.com/assets/uploads/2014/09/Dk1LHUn.jpg" width="658"/>
          </noscript>
          <p>
           <img alt="TLLWmja" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-full wp-image-754167" data-lazy-src="http://cdn3-www.craveonline.com/assets/uploads/2014/09/TLLWmja.png" data-lazy-type="image" height="370" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="658"/>
          </p>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="TLLWmja" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-754167" height="370" src="http://cdn3-www.craveonline.com/assets/uploads/2014/09/TLLWmja.png" width="658"/>
          </noscript>
          <p>
           <img alt="PgM0M4n" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-full wp-image-754165" data-lazy-src="http://cdn3-www.craveonline.com/assets/uploads/2014/09/PgM0M4n.png" data-lazy-type="image" height="370" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="658"/>
          </p>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="PgM0M4n" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-754165" height="370" src="http://cdn3-www.craveonline.com/assets/uploads/2014/09/PgM0M4n.png" width="658"/>
          </noscript>
          <p>
           <em>
            The Sims 4
           </em>
           has been particularly prone to piracy given its controversy. Many argue that Maxis has removed a substantial sumof features from the series that should be in the game, and replaced them with what they argue isnt enough to justify the purchase.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: center;">
           Also See:
           <a href="http://www.craveonline.com/gaming/articles/755313-sims-4-review-close-cigar">
            The Sims 4 Review
           </a>
          </h2>
          <p>
           Whatever the case is, there is no circumstance where piracy is okay. If you want to play the game, buy it. Downloading games is stealing, which is illegal, and hurts the gaming industry. If you really want to protest, go on Metacritic and Amazon and leave a fake review like most people are doing. Thatll show them/sarcasm.
          </p>
