        <h1>
         Lembert Dome - Yosemite - Hiking California
        </h1>
        <div class="region region-content">
         <div class="block block-system" id="block-system-main">
          <article class="node node--blog node--promoted node--full node--blog--full clearfix" id="node-314" role="article">
           <section class="node-submitted">
            <span class="submitted">
             By
             <span class="username">
              Barry
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="date">
             21 / Oct / 2011
            </span>
           </section>
           <div class="node-content">
            <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
             <div class="field-items">
              <div class="field-item even">
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Lembert Dome
                </strong>
                is an
                <em>
                 accessible granite monolith
                </em>
                in the
                <strong>
                 High Sierra
                </strong>
                of
                <strong>
                 Yosemite
                </strong>
                <strong>
                 National Park
                </strong>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                <img alt="" class="image-large" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/styles/large/public/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_13.jpg?itok=pP5FDstc" style="height:480px; width:640px"/>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Yosemite National Park
                </strong>
                is a place of
                <strong>
                 Granite Domes
                </strong>
                .
                <strong>
                 Mel and I
                </strong>
                drive in early morning from
                <strong>
                 Lee Vining
                </strong>
                , through
                <strong>
                 Inyo National Forest
                </strong>
                , up
                <strong>
                 Tioga Road
                </strong>
                ,across
                <strong>
                 Tioga Pass
                </strong>
                , and past immaculate
                <strong>
                 Dana Meadows
                </strong>
                to the parking area for trailsto
                <strong>
                 Lembert Dome
                </strong>
                and
                <strong>
                 Dog Lake
                </strong>
                . We park in the busy lot.
                <strong>
                 Lembert Dome
                </strong>
                looms largedirectly in front of us.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_01.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8686" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_01.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                The trailhead to
                <strong>
                 Lembert Dome
                </strong>
                is visible and nearby. Thetrail is interrupted with a section over rock and we miss the next trail sign which is tucked in behind trees. After wandering about aimlessly for a minute or two, common sense kicks in and we are on our way over well-used, good path withvery modestelevation.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_02.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8687" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_02.jpg" style="height:432px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_03.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8688" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_03.jpg" style="height:426px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_04.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8689" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_04.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_05.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8690" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_05.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_06.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8691" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_06.jpg" style="height:408px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                The trail swings around the back side of
                <strong>
                 Lembert Dome
                </strong>
                . Beneath the sheer cliff face there is a pristine pond a short way off-trail that is easy to miss.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_07.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8692" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_07.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                A spur trail delivers us to the approach after we have hiked about 3/4 of the way around
                <strong>
                 Lembert Dome
                </strong>
                .
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_08.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8693" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_08.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                There is no trail, not even a wear pattern on the surface of the dome. The surface appears smooth but is surprisingly gritty with good 'stick'. Appropriate footwear is essential. Weenjoy perfect weather.
               </p>
               <p>
                If there was a possibility of rain, I wouldbe reluctant towalk on this surface. It is rock withplentiful lichen growth on the surface. In the presence of wet and windy weather the surface slopes could become very slippery and would be at a risk level I would not personally assume.
               </p>
               <p>
                In a thunderstorm, lightning could be a serious and fatal issue. It will not be a factor this day and we climb straightforward tothe second ridge then keep left to find the line which will moderate ascent angle as much as possible. This takes us past the summit but provides a reasonable line to the top with a single switchback. The possibilities are plentiful. It is a matter of personal comfortcombined with alevel of risk which can be adjusted from safe to higher risk.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_09.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8694" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_09.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_10.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8695" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_10.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_11.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8696" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_11.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
               </p>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_12.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="size-full wp-image-8697" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_12.jpg" style="height:444px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                Dog Dome adjacent to and below the top of Lembert Dome.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <p>
                Elevation gain from the parking area to the summit of
                <strong>
                 Lembert Dome
                </strong>
                is 850 ft (259 m) on a round trip route of about 3.2 miles (5.1 KM). The glacial-polished granite dome is named after
                <strong>
                 Jean (John) Baptiste Lembert
                </strong>
                whobuilt a cabin nearby in
                <strong>
                 Tuolumne Meadow
                </strong>
                and raised goats here in the late 1800's. The 9,450 ft (2,880 m) summit offerspremiere viewsof
                <strong>
                 Tuolumne Meadows
                </strong>
                and surrounding peaks including
                <strong>
                 Unicorn Peak, Cathedral Peak, Mount Conness, Mount Dana
                </strong>
                and
                <strong>
                 Mount Gibbs
                </strong>
                . The top is characteristically breezy. Tie down the hat and glasses.
               </p>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_13.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="size-full wp-image-8698" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_13.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                The final switchback approach to the summit of Lembert Dome.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_14.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="size-full wp-image-8699" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_14.jpg" style="height:458px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                Sweeping 360 degree view of Tuolumne Meadows and surrounding mountains from the summit of Lembert Dome.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_15.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="size-full wp-image-8700" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_15.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                Dog Dome below from the summit of Lembert Dome.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <p>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_16.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8701" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_16.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
                Without a route to follow the down hike is interesting but we find something similar to our ascent with a couple ofadjustments. Directly below us are a couple of small granite domes on the other side of
                <strong>
                 Tioga Road
                </strong>
                . Downside footing is less comfortable than climbing. Normal.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_18.jpg">
                 <img alt="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8703" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/Lembert_18.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="Lembert Dome - Yosemite National Park, California, USA"/>
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>

As we progress through other locations and features of Yosemite National Park, I hope the photographs bring back some good memories for you,  Good to hear from you, D.
