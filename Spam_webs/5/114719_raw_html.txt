       <div class="entry" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         We decorate our homes for the holidays. We decorate our offices and stores, too. But the question is: are youalso decorating your online place of business, i.e., your website?
        </p>
        <p>
         You may have noticed that were already decorated here for the Christmas holidays.
         <a href="http://timgrahl.com">
          Tim Grahl,
         </a>
         the
         <em>
          Small Business Trends
         </em>
         webmaster, put a Santa cap on the blue globe in the
         <em>
          Small Business Trends
         </em>
         logo:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Small Business Trends logo for Christmas" class="pin-it pin-it" src="http://www.smallbiztrends.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/12/sbtchristmaslogo329x83.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Every year we have tried to do something to acknowledge the Christmas season. In past years weve put holiday images in posts. Weve addedChristmas lights along the top of the site or sidebar. Weve even createdspecial holiday messages and holiday-themed articles.
        </p>
        <p>
         But this year I decided to take a page out of Googles book and actually decorate the logo. Google does this for major (and not so major) holidays each year. Here are two Google Christmas logos from years past:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Google logo for Christmas" class="pin-it pin-it" src="http://www.smallbiztrends.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/12/googleholiday1.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Google logo for Christmas 2" class="pin-it pin-it" src="http://www.smallbiztrends.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/12/googleholiday2.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yahoo also has a history of decorating its logo. Here is a Yahoo Christmas logo from 2006:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Yahoo logo for Christmas" class="pin-it pin-it" src="http://www.smallbiztrends.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/12/yahooholidaylogo.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yet I had to ask myself: with all the projects planned for this site, why did I feel compelled to decoratethe website, of all things? One answer to this question comes from no less an authority than Jakob Nielsen, the website usability guru.
         <a href="http://www.useit.com/alertbox/20021028.html">
          On the topic of decorating websites for holidays, Nielsen notes
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          There are two main reasons for websites to recognize holidays and special events, and both reasons fall under the same general category: To
          <strong>
           respect users as human beings
          </strong>
          , rather than simply as eyeballs or a source of e-commerce transactions. Commemorating special events is a way for websites to connect to users and be seen as welcoming environments, rather than places focused solely on money grubbing.
         </p>
         <p>
          More specifically, the first reason for a website to commemorate a holiday or special occasion is to
          <strong>
           appear current and up-to-date
          </strong>
          . Major holidays and special events are important to users, and they constantly see these special days reflected in the physical dcor of their surroundings. A website that doesnt reflect whats currently topical and important to users will feel out of touch. Worse, it might feel stale, and users might think its outdated in other respects as well. * * *
         </p>
         <p>
          The second reason for a website to feature holiday or event decorations is to
          <a class="old" href="http://www.useit.com/alertbox/20020707.html" title="Alertbox July 2002: User Empowerment and the Fun Factor">
           increase joy of use
          </a>
          . Even simple ornamentations like a Christmas wreath, a Valentines heart, or a soccer ball can create a small moment of happiness for users as theyre reminded of the greater happiness the holiday or special occasion brings. A user might be engaged in the dreary task of researching enterprise solutions, for example, and be reminded that its Christmas and a happy time of year.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Yes, anything toput a smile on our faces .
        </p>
        <p>
         So, back to the question I started with:have you decorated your online place of business for the holidays (Christmas oranyseasonal theme)?
        </p>
        <p>
         If so, leave a comment below with your websiteURL. Or just point us toward any sitethats been decorated.
        </p>
        <p>
         I will then add the URLs to this post.
         <a href="http://www.smallbiztrends.com/2007/12/sites-decorated-for-the-holidays.html">
          a list of decorated websites over here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         That way we can allvisitand check out the holiday decorations. It will be like driving aroundthe neighborhood looking at holiday displays. But instead of driving well surf aroundwebsites.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          PS: If you havent decorated yet but are thinking about it, I found a
         </em>
         <a href="http://forums.digitalpoint.com/showthread.php?p=5452769">
          <em>
           forum post featuring a designer who is offering to decorate your logo
          </em>
         </a>
         <em>
