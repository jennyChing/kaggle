          <div class="hg-read-more-container" id="hg-read-more-container-26808">
           <p>
            I am a huge fan of mixing elegant and rustic in design, especially when it comes to a simple idea for place settings for a table.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26813" style="width: 437px">
            <a href="http://frenchcountrycottage.blogspot.com/2013/08/1-diy-framed-burlap-place-settings.html">
             <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-26813" height="640" src="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_3048-1.jpg" width="427"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
            </p>
            <p class="wp-credit-text">
             Photo by
             <a href="http://frenchcountrycottage.blogspot.com/2013/08/1-diy-framed-burlap-place-settings.html" target="_blank" title="credit">
              French Country Cottage
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            This has a little bit of elegance with its silver frames and a little bit of rustic appeal with burlap. These little place setting numbers are so simple and inexpensive to make and you can personalize them with names or messages as well.
           </p>
           <p>
            Heres what you need: Small frames (choose the size and style you would like); leftover pieces of burlap; glue, stencil and a marker
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26814" style="width: 310px">
            <a href="http://frenchcountrycottage.blogspot.com/2013/08/1-diy-framed-burlap-place-settings.html">
             <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-26814 " height="400" src="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/photo-1.jpg" title="photo-1" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
            </p>
            <p class="wp-credit-text">
             Photo by
             <a href="http://frenchcountrycottage.blogspot.com/2013/08/1-diy-framed-burlap-place-settings.html" target="_blank" title="credit">
              French Country Cottage
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Start by taking your frames apart and using the back of the frame as a guide for the size of burlap you need. Cut your burlap to size for your frames and then glue the burlap to the piece of cardboard that was inside the frame  I used plain old school glue.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26815" style="width: 310px">
            <a href="http://frenchcountrycottage.blogspot.com/2013/08/1-diy-framed-burlap-place-settings.html">
             <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-26815" height="400" src="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/photo.jpg" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
            </p>
            <p class="wp-credit-text">
             Photo by
             <a href="http://frenchcountrycottage.blogspot.com/2013/08/1-diy-framed-burlap-place-settings.html" target="_blank" title="credit">
              French Country Cottage
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Once the glue dried, I used a stencil and a black permanent marker to mark each one with a number. You could use an initial for a monogram or small words depending on the size of your frame and the orientation of how you would like to use them. These were pretty small, so I decided on just numbering them.
           </p>
           <p>
            When they are finished drying, place the numbered burlap back into the frames and set your table.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26812" style="width: 437px">
            <a href="http://frenchcountrycottage.blogspot.com/2013/08/1-diy-framed-burlap-place-settings.html">
             <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-26812" height="640" src="http://www.homegoods.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_3012.jpg" width="427"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
            </p>
            <p class="wp-credit-text">
             Photo by
             <a href="http://frenchcountrycottage.blogspot.com/2013/08/1-diy-framed-burlap-place-settings.html" target="_blank" title="credit">
              French Country Cottage
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            These are perfect to send home with guests as a little gift, too, especially if youre using their names on them.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26811" style="width: 437px">
