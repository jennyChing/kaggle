     <h1 class="post_title">
      No Bake Cookies Recipe
     </h1>
     <div class="singlepost entry">
      <div class="tw_button" id="tweetbutton5241" style="">
       <a class="twitter-share-button" href="http://twitter.com/share?url=http%3A%2F%2Fdealinanddishin.com%2F2013%2F05%2Fno-bake-cookies-recipe%2F&amp;via=DealinandDishin&amp;text=No%20Bake%20Cookies%20Recipe&amp;related=&amp;lang=en&amp;count=horizontal&amp;counturl=http%3A%2F%2Fdealinanddishin.com%2F2013%2F05%2Fno-bake-cookies-recipe%2F" style="width:55px;height:22px;background:transparent url('http://dealinanddishin.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-tweet-button/tweetn.png') no-repeat  0 0;text-align:left;text-indent:-9999px;display:block;">
        Tweet
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="fcbk_share">
       <div class="fcbk_like">
        <fb:like href="http://dealinanddishin.com/2013/05/no-bake-cookies-recipe/" layout="button_count" share="false" show_faces="false" width="450">
        </fb:like>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       This is a recipe I saw in a Sharing Recipes cookbook, which was my Grandmas book and a collection of recipes from ladies from the Dubois County (Indiana) Extension Homemakers. This book was published 1984-85, and she gave this to me in 1994. It is still a favorite of mine. I always recommend cookbooks like this from real home cooks.
      </p>
      <p>
       I had to fix this recipe a bit to get it like I want it.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://dealinanddishin.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/NoBakeCookies.jpg">
        <img alt="NoBakeCookies" class="aligncenter wp-image-5243" height="360" src="http://dealinanddishin.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/NoBakeCookies.jpg" width="480"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        No Bake Cookies
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        1 Stick Margarine
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        2 Tbsp. Cocoa powder
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        2 cups sugar
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        1/2 c. milk
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Boil the above ingredients for 1 minute. Be sure to stir to avoid sticking. Remove from heat and add:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        1/2 c. peanut butter
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        3 c. quick oats
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        1 tsp. vanilla.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Add the vanilla, peanut butter, and then oats. Add the oats 1 cup at a time to get to consistency you like. I like thick cookies so I upped it to 3 cups, and that was good for me. Also the recipe originally called for crunchy peanut butter, but Im a creamy fan myself.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        You can drop by teaspoonful on wax paper if you wish.
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        I prefer to line a 912 cookie sheet with wax paper and pour the mixture all over it.
        <a href="http://dealinanddishin.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/cutcookies.jpg">
         <img alt="cutcookies" class="aligncenter wp-image-5249" height="405" src="http://dealinanddishin.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/cutcookies.jpg" width="540"/>
        </a>
        <a href="http://dealinanddishin.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/cookiesinpan.jpg">
         <img alt="cookiesinpan" class="aligncenter wp-image-5248" height="405" src="http://dealinanddishin.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/cookiesinpan.jpg" width="540"/>
        </a>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Once it cools and sets some you can easily cut it with a plastic spatula thus protecting your cookie sheets from scratches.
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       I can eat these all day long, and am still trying to convince myself thats okay since they have oats and peanut butter.
      </p>
