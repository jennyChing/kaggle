    <h1>
     Welcome to ADAPT!
    </h1>
    ADAPT is a national grass-roots community that organizes disability rights activists to engage in nonviolent direct action, including civil disobedience, to assure the civil and human rights of people with disabilities to live in freedom.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    <h1>
     <span style="color: red">
      <u>
       <strong>
        NEW
       </strong>
      </u>
     </span>
     Community Integration Act To Be Introduced!
    </h1>
    <p>
     As we celebrate the 25th anniversary of the signing of the Americans with Disabilities Act, we know there is still much work to get done so that people with disabilities can live in the community rather than be forced into nursing facilities and other institutions.  To assure that people with disabilities have a real choice to avoid or leave institutional placement, ADAPT is meeting with members of Congress to coordinate introduction of the Community Integration Act.
    </p>
    <p>
     The proposed legislation creates a new Title of the ADA, which requires states to change their policies, provide community-based services first, and offer HCBS to people currently in institutions.
    </p>
    <a href="http://www.adapt.org/main.ciamain" rel="nofollow">
     Learn more about the proposed legislation here.
    </a>

    <h1>
     Community First Choice Option
    </h1>
    <p>
     Several states have already indicated that they will be selecting the option.
     <a href="http://www.cdcan.info/sites/default/files/2011-11-15%20-%20Community%20First%20Choice%20Option%20Council%20-%20Power%20Point%20Presentation%20(November%2017,%202011).pdf" rel="nofollow">
      California
     </a>
     has formed a CFC Development and Implementation Council and has an aggressive timeline for CFC Implementation.
     <a href="http://www.health.ny.gov/facilities/long_term_care/solicitation_community_first_choice_options.htm" rel="nofollow">
      New York
     </a>
     has announced that it will be selecting the option.  Maryland has confirmed that the state will select the option.  Alaska, Rhode Island and Washington all indicated in a
     <a href="http://www.kff.org/medicaid/upload/8248.pdf" rel="nofollow">
      Families USA survey
     </a>
     that they would be selecting the CFC Option in the coming year.  In the same survey, Arizona has indicated that the state is considering selecting the option.
    </p>
    <p>
     Whether your state is moving forward with the CFC Option or you are advocating that they do, ADAPT's Community First Choice Option
     <a href="http://cdrnys.org/files/CFC%20OVERVIEW.pdf" rel="nofollow">
      Fact Sheet
     </a>
     explains the basics for you.  We have also developed
     <a href="http://cdrnys.org/files/CFC-FAQ-122211.pdf" rel="nofollow">
      Answers to Frequently Asked Questions
     </a>
     about CFC as well as a
     <a href="http://www.adapt.org/main/cfcletter" rel="nofollow">
      sample letter
     </a>
     you can edit to send to your State Medicaid Director to ask for a meeting about your state selecting the CFC Option.
    </p>
    <h1>
     Managed Care
    </h1>
    <p>
     The expansion of Medicaid managed care that includes long term services and supports for people with disabilities and older people confronts us with the challenge of how to assure that the advances we have made in developing a person centered, consumer directed, community based system is not diminished or eliminated.  Managed care has some positive opportunities as well as potential for negative outcomes.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     States are developing Requests for Proposals that HMOs must respond to.  The HMOs selected to administer the program then must sign a contract with the state that outlines what they must provide, how it is delivered, how the state will evaluate their performance and how the HMOs will be reimbursed.  Advocates should be at the table telling the state what best practices to put in the RFP.  In addition there must be some accountability in the managed care contract to assure that community integration is the outcome we want.
    </p>
    <p>
     Two resources for advocates at the state level are
     <a href="http://www.adapt.org/main/ahip" rel="nofollow">
      Guiding Principles for Serving Individuals with Disabilities through Medicaid Health Plans
     </a>
     that National ADAPT negotiated with the American Health Insurance Programs (AHIP)in 2007 and proposed
     <a href="http://www.adapt.org/main/cipi" rel="nofollow">
      Community Integration Performance Indicators
     </a>
     (CIPI) that ADAPT of Texas has recently developed to assess how their managed care program in Texas (StarPlus) is doing in providing LTSS.
    </p>
