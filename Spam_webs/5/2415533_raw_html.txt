            <p>
             Can you believe Christmas is almost here?! Like every year, I countdown to Christmas but this year is very special for me because Kitana is starting to understand whats happening around her. Im extremely excited to teach her family traditions and create new ones.
            </p>
            <p>
             Every family as their own traditions and today Im sharing some of mine with you.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.youbabyandi.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/10-Family-Christmas-Traditions.png">
              <img alt="10 Family Christmas Traditions" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7065" height="768" src="http://www.youbabyandi.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/10-Family-Christmas-Traditions.png" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              1. Christmas Tree/Decorations
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             This is the first thing I do when December starts, it always gets me in the mood and excited for the holidays. Its tradition that we put up the Christmas tree together. This year it was all about Kitana, she was overwhelmed at the amount of decoration we had and was so happy to hang them up. We dont get real Christmas trees here so we bought this one that can be used yearly.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.youbabyandi.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Christmas.jpg">
              <img alt="Christmas tree" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7061" height="761" src="http://www.youbabyandi.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Christmas.jpg" width="1142"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              2. Baking
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             Nothing is better than a home that smells festive. Its a great way to bond with your child and have some fun in the kitchen. Ive already baked one batch of cookies and plan to bake more this week. Kitana loves helping me in the kitchen.
            </p>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              3. Advent Calendar
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             The perfect way to countdown to Christmas is with a special advent calendar. I bought the one with chocolates so everyday Kitana has a little treat.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.youbabyandi.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/MG_0009.jpg">
              <img alt="advent calendar" class="aligncenter wp-image-7071" height="747" src="http://www.youbabyandi.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/MG_0009.jpg" width="498"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              4. A letter to Santa
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             I never wrote a letter to Santa but I really want to encourage Kitana to believe in him. Every child needs some fantasy. We plan on leaving some milk and cookies for Santa under the tree this year.
            </p>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              5. Christmas Movies
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             I absolutely love Christmas movies and can watch them over and over again. Every afternoon Kitana and I catch up on a movie.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.youbabyandi.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/MG_0038.jpg">
              <img alt="Christmas movies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6993" height="761" src="http://www.youbabyandi.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/MG_0038.jpg" width="1142"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              6. Give to others
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             Every year we donate to charity. Its a great tradition to teach kids about sharing and the importance of giving.
            </p>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              7. Christmas Carols
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             Whats Christmas without some carols ? Hope this gets you in the mood too! I love listening to music in the kitchen while I prepare meals for thefamily.
             <br/>
             <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
              <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="379" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/Y-9uoxZoLH8?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="620">
              </iframe>
             </span>
            </p>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              8. Christmas Books
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             Teach your child about the festive season with a book that teachesthe importance of Christmas. Many of us forget the true meaning behind the celebration and sometimes a book can remind us how special it is.
            </p>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              9. Christmas Pictures
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             Capture those perfect moments with pictures and create your own album. Every year we take out tons of pictures and now weve created a collection from the past 2 years with our daughter.
            </p>

            <h4>
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              10. Christmas Eve
             </span>
            </h4>
            <p>
             I got so many plans for Christmas eve! Im so excited to be starting new traditions with my family this year. From toddler crafts to reading the bible to learn about this special day. I will be giving Kitana
             <em>
              a night before Christmas box
             </em>
             filled with some lovely goodies including a bible for toddlers.
            </p>
            <p>
             Id love to learn about yourChristmas traditions. Please share them in the comment section below.
