           <h2 class="entry-title">
            FOCUS : Paris
           </h2>
           <div class="entry-meta">
            <span class="posted-on">
             <i class="fa fa-calendar">
             </i>
             <a href="http://avocadoplease.com/focus-paris/" rel="bookmark" title="5:12 pm">
              <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2015-02-16T17:12:18+00:00">
               February 16, 2015
              </time>
             </a>
            </span>
            <div class="entry-categories">
             <span class="entry-cats">
              <a href="http://avocadoplease.com/category/focus-2/" rel="category tag">
               FOCUS
              </a>
              <span class="comma">

              </span>

           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             For each and every FOCUS we present to you ouraim is to ensure that they include authentic, genuine and varied tips, with the hope that they will stand out from the crowd and provide you with all the citys gems. For this FOCUS we have teamed up with our friends at
             <a href="https://www.38hrs.com/">
              38HOURS
             </a>
              the sleek travel guide, curated with insider tips from local people, stunning imagery and their distinctlyhumorous tone  providingyouwith a completely refreshed approach to our FOCUS series. For just10 Euros you can pick up your own copy of their entire guide, available online and in-store:
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             LONDON:
             <em>
              Foyles, Shreeji, Stanfords, Walther Knig
              <br/>
             </em>
             BERLIN:
             <em>
              Do You Read Me, Dussman, Creators of Berlin, Voo.
             </em>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             (Visit
             <a href="https://www.38hrs.com/">
              38hrs.com
             </a>
             for the full list)
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             From the Haussmann facades to the historic bridges over the Seine, Paris hasremained very synonymous withart and style. With that, it has always remained cutting-edge and at times a trailblazer, despite being criticized at times forresting on its laurels and relying too much on its heritage; butfollowing on from their 1980a  1990s hiatus, Paris is once again very much inventing itself.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             This renouveau can be noted through the changes in gastronomy  Michelin starred dining, complete with pristine white table cloths, is being transformed into a more local, simplistic affair  the focus now falling heavily on the quality of ingredients. Meanwhile, the huge fashion houses that once dominated the scene in Paris are allowingsmaller, independent designers to make waves, creating an entirely new fashion in itself. The whole renouveau is actually part of what makes Paris, Paris. Herearesome top tips for navigating the City of Love in true Parisian fashion.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <em>
               Fast Facts:
              </em>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              The population of the city of Paris is 2.34 million people. However, the population of the surrounding suburbs is estimated to be 10.5 million.
             </li>
             <li>
              The Eiffel Tower weighs about 7,000t and uses 50 tonnes of paint every seven years. The Eiffel tower swayed 13 cm during a storm in 1999, which blew at 240 kph.
             </li>
             <li>
              Weather wise: July is the warmest month of the year in Paris, experiencing on average 10 hours sunshine and highs of 20 degrees
             </li>
             <li>
              The coldest month of the year is December,the average amount of sunshine is three hours and temperatures rarely exceed 4 degrees
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-0" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/10731440_1595510204019018_1513760380_n.jpg">
              <img alt="10731440_1595510204019018_1513760380_n" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3034" height="640" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/10731440_1595510204019018_1513760380_n.jpg" width="640"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-1" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/10946352_868467989883843_796458054_n.jpg">
              <img alt="10946352_868467989883843_796458054_n" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3025" height="640" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/10946352_868467989883843_796458054_n.jpg" width="640"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-2" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/914509_324977761045666_1291990879_n1.jpg">
              <img alt="914509_324977761045666_1291990879_n" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3036" height="640" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/914509_324977761045666_1291990879_n1.jpg" width="640"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              <strong>
               Ici Cest Paris: Hidden spots &amp; Local Life
              </strong>
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             Few places in Paris are as popular as Montmartre, the hill that rises above the north of the city. Home to the white-domed Basilica of the Sacre Cur its a magnet for tourists. Countless artists lived here through the years and there are still painters in the streets today. But step a bit away from the crowds and you can discover an almost magical place that seems to be suspended in time; down the hill and around the west side of the Basilica lies a secret spot that is easily overlooked: A small vineyard in the middle of Montmartre.It is, like many of the best vineyards, on a steep slope.
            </p>
            <p>
             If that isntenough to unleash your inner Parisian thenmaybe the markets can, the food markets to be exact. This is where locals mingle and buy groceries for dinner, tourists snap pictures and businessmen sit together for a quick lunch. The Marche des Enfants Rouge is one of
             <strong>
              Paris most beautiful food markets
             </strong>
             right in the heart of the Marais, and its the oldest covered market in the city. Apart from fresh produce and flowers, youll find a big selection of warm dishes that are served by the vendors  go there at lunchtime to trythecouscous or fresh handmade pasta!
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-3" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/market.jpg">
              <img alt="market" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3049" height="4755" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/market.jpg" width="7125"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-4" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/paris1.jpg">
              <img alt="paris1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3060" height="1317" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/paris1.jpg" width="1581"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-5" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0031.jpg">
              <img alt="DSC_0031" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3108" height="2592" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0031.jpg" width="3872"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-6" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0042.jpg">
              <img alt="DSC_0042" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3109" height="2592" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0042.jpg" width="3872"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-7" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/belles.jpg">
              <img alt="belles" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3051" height="4617" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/belles.jpg" width="6917"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a data-rel="lightbox-8" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Paris_Fashion_Avocado_Please.jpg">
              <img alt="Paris_Fashion_Avocado_Please" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3053" height="800" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Paris_Fashion_Avocado_Please.jpg" width="1021"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             Above images: Florent Michel; Megan Wellington;Katie Taylor, Katie Taylor, Sren Jepsen, Sren Jepsen.
            </p>
            <p>
             To be in Paris and not indulge in their offerings of couture would mean one would not be truly indulging in the infamousParisian offerings. We discovered a gem: following its opening in 2013 this shop soon came the place to be, and to this day fashionistas flock to
             <strong>
              The Broken Arm
             </strong>
             when they are in town. The owners stock a carefully selected range of the seasons styles from, brands like Raf Simons, Carven and Kenzo. There are sneakers in rare colour combinations, accessories for women and men, and art books and magazines in the basement. The cafe in the front serves fantastic coffee and is a perfect place to have lunch while exploring Le Marais (or the perfect place to entertain your guest while you have a quick browse?).
            </p>
            <p>
             Aside from Parisian fashion, fashion and coffee are two things that go hand-in-hand; but for a city that is so famous for its cafe culture, the coffee is actually fairly terrible. Until recently. A group of young, well-travelled barristers have invaded the city and started what is known as the coffee boom. Highly recommended:
             <em>
              <strong>
               <a href="https://www.facebook.com/TenBelles">
                Ten Belles
               </a>
               .
              </strong>
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-9" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/83027ad2a49911e381f0122ef83043a2_8.jpg">
              <img alt="83027ad2a49911e381f0122ef83043a2_8" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3027" height="640" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/83027ad2a49911e381f0122ef83043a2_8.jpg" width="640"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-10" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/11290015.jpg">
              <img alt="11290015" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3015" height="3583" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/11290015.jpg" width="2376"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-11" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/11290023.jpg">
              <img alt="11290023" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3020" height="3583" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/11290023.jpg" width="2376"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-12" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/000035.jpg">
              <img alt="000035" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3019" height="2137" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/000035.jpg" width="3224"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             Images:first, Paris Bielby; remainder,
             <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/karl_hab/">
              Karl Hab
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             As the blissful Parisian days soon meander into decadent nights and the landscape deepens, shadows lengthen, the air cools, and the lipstick upgrades to an ultra sultry red, where do you then spend the nights? A trend thats making big waves in Paris are
             <strong>
              foodie wine bars.
             </strong>
             They are nowfoodie destinations  with great wine!Refusingtostickto the traditional charcuterie and classiccheese boards  theyre re-inventing themselves as hip destinationsfor great wines, evening nibbles and a variety of entertainment. Could we pick favourites? Sure!
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.frenchie-restaurant.com/en/menu_bar_en">
              Frenchie Bar a Vins
             </a>
             will please the busier traveler whohasnt had time to make a reservation, but without having to make anyunnecessary compromises. The small plates 
             <strong>
              tapas style
             </strong>
              suit Marchands cooking style to a tee. And, of course, the wine list: a selection of wines from France and the New World.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://verjusparis.com/fr-bar-a-vins/">
              Verjus Bar a Vins
             </a>
              offerings rangefrom Mediterranean (marinated feta, roast tomato vinaigrette, purslane and olives), to Japanase (gyozas of celeri root with toasted peanuts), to Cajun (buttermilk fried chicken). The options are eclectic, but executed toperfection. A true foodie dream.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://leverrevole.fr/">
              Le Verre Vole
             </a>
             is also a haven ofgreat wine and nibbles. Plus, location-wise its alongsidethe hip Canal Saint-Martin and conveniently open every day; this cave a vin is a place to go for cool nights, at times loud ones, that are alwaysfilled with an undeniably infectious spirit.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-13" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/bckg_bar.jpg">
              <img alt="bckg_bar" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3063" height="683" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/bckg_bar.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-14" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/pointephemere.jpg">
              <img alt="FOCUS_Paris_38hrs_AvocadoPlease" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2935" height="1047" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/pointephemere.jpg" width="1589"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a data-rel="lightbox-15" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0258.jpg">
              <img alt="DSC_0258" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3040" height="2000" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0258.jpg" width="3008"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             Images:Frenchie Bar a Vins;Le Point Ephemere; Gonzalo Perea
            </p>
            <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;">
             Just a stones throw fromLe Verre Vole is a renovated formed warehouse known as
             <strong>
              Emphemeral Nights.
             </strong>
             The place is a real gem, where a set of rotatingartists use the space for concerts several times a week. But, ironically, the real draw here is the terrace and bar. Right by the watersideof the Canal Saint-Martin it isthe perfect place to enjoy a cold beer or a glass of wine after exploring the neighbourhood. If the weather is nice, it can get quite crowded here, so, come early!
            </div>
            <p>
             The entrance of
             <a href="http://www.lecomptoirgeneral.com/">
              Le Comptoir General
             </a>
             is tricky to find, but persevereand youll be happy you did. This place is a laid-back art museum is dedicated to ghetto culture: creativity that springs up in marginalised places all over the world, especially from Africa. This vast space is strewn with artifactsand the interior looks like a movie set; several of the shops sell records, second hand clothes andaselection of books.Periodically they showfilms, exhibitions and other events (see above).
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a data-rel="lightbox-16" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/comptoir01.jpg">
              <img alt="comptoir01" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3067" height="633" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/comptoir01.jpg" width="950"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a data-rel="lightbox-17" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/comptoir03.jpg">
              <img alt="comptoir03" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3068" height="633" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/comptoir03.jpg" width="950"/>
             </a>
             Images: Caroline Kurze
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              <strong>
               Favouritestay:
              </strong>
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Hotel de Temps:
             </strong>
             Restful white colours, unique vintage furniture and raw materials create a delicate charm. The bar attracts a steady local crowd with great cocktails and secret party venue in the basement. See pictures below;
             <strong>
              <b>
               <i>
                <a href="https://www.38hrs.com/paris.html">
                 Book now
                </a>
                .
               </i>
              </b>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-18" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/10671_398135646978433_1916394359448692353_n.jpg">
              <img alt="10671_398135646978433_1916394359448692353_n" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2937" height="773" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/10671_398135646978433_1916394359448692353_n.jpg" width="960"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a data-rel="lightbox-19" href="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/10940450_398125390312792_634288712572732342_n.jpg">
              <img alt="FOCUS_Paris_38hrs_AvocadoPlease" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2936" height="773" src="http://avocadoplease.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/10940450_398125390312792_634288712572732342_n.jpg" width="960"/>
