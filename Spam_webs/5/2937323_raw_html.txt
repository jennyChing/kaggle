        <div class="article-content clearfix">
         <p>
          Kathryn Stockett, author of The Help, made news recently when she tweeted out the confirmation number for her flight:
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/203515/author-gets-deadly-confirmation-code-for-plane-flight/">
           GONER5
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          But superstitious passengers flying Finnair had it way worse on Friday, hitting a hat trick of dark omens:
          <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/world/2013/sep/13/friday-13th-flight-666-hel">
           Flight #666, on Friday the 13th, to HEL
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          It has been quite a joke among the pilots, said pilot Juha-Pekka Keidasto. Its hopefully smooth skies, he added before the flight, risking a perilous jinx.
         </p>
         <p>
          But the short flight from Copenhagen to Helsinki was nearly sold out. Passengers courage was rewarded with clear skies and a smooth flightthe plane landed safely in Helsinki in the afternoon.
         </p>
         <p>
          Every Friday the 13th costs nearly $1 billion in lost business worldwide as superstitious customers avoid travel (and walking under ladders). But seriously, even for the non-superstitious among us, flying flight 666 to HEL on Friday the 13th is pretty hardcore.
         </p>
