             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              Santa and Reindeer Stick Puppets
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1847269805095197200">
              <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                Every day when I read stories with my preschooler she likes to read each book twice. I read it to her the first time and then she reads it back to me. She doesn't actually know how to read yet, so more than anything it's a great comprehension exercise with her re-telling me the story. We made some
                <b>
                 Santa and Reindeer Stick Puppets
                </b>
                to go along with a book we've been reading to make her retelling of the story an even more fun experience.
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>

                 <b>
                  SANTA STICK PUPPET
                 </b>
                </span>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <b>
                 <u>
                  <br/>
                 </u>
                </b>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <b>
                 <u>
                  Supplies you will need:
                 </u>
                </b>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <ul style="text-align: left;">
                 <li>
                  <a href="http://craftprojectideas.com/index.php/products/basic-elements/wood-sticks-a-dowels/41-jumbo-wood-sticks-75ct" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                   jumbo craft stick
                  </a>
                  (we received ours from Craftprojectideas.com)
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  red cardstock paper
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  white cardstock paper
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00IZGY4DA/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00IZGY4DA&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=iheacrathi04-20&amp;linkId=DVJR423JCA27NMNZ">
                   white cupcake liner
                  </a>
                  <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=iheacrathi04-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00IZGY4DA" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  apricot colored crayon
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  small white pom (We used
                  <a href="http://craftprojectideas.com/index.php/products/fabric-crafts/poms/84-glitter-poms-assorted-sizes-75-pack" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                   Glitter Poms
                  </a>
                  received from Craftprojectideas.com)
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  small red pom(We used
                  <a href="http://craftprojectideas.com/index.php/products/fabric-crafts/poms/84-glitter-poms-assorted-sizes-75-pack" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                   Glitter Poms
                  </a>
                  received from Craftprojectideas.com)
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0074MHV7W/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B0074MHV7W&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=iheacrathi04-20&amp;linkId=OEHIC3RAKLJXB5ZG">
                   googly eyes
                  </a>
                  <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=iheacrathi04-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B0074MHV7W" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  glue stick
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00178KLEY/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00178KLEY&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=iheacrathi04-20&amp;linkId=Y75UNCYS43VAXAAL">
                   tacky glue
                  </a>
                  <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=iheacrathi04-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00178KLEY" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-vVf7ZbLL_yg/VITQ317YCAI/AAAAAAAAUYA/YZ0Fv0BE3TI/s1600/Materials%2BSanta%2BPuppet.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                  <img border="0" height="512" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-vVf7ZbLL_yg/VITQ317YCAI/AAAAAAAAUYA/YZ0Fv0BE3TI/s1600/Materials%2BSanta%2BPuppet.jpg" width="640"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  <u>
                   Directions:
                  </u>
                 </b>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  (We made these just like we did our Paper Plate Santa Craft.)
                 </b>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 1. Start by cutting 1/3 off the top of your white cupcake liner. Color the inside circle section with your apricot crayon.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Gm7_Z9qyWzs/VITVHO_1vmI/AAAAAAAAUYM/n24uzqH-BJE/s1600/Santa%2Band%2BReindeer%2BPuppets%2B1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                  <img border="0" height="320" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Gm7_Z9qyWzs/VITVHO_1vmI/AAAAAAAAUYM/n24uzqH-BJE/s1600/Santa%2Band%2BReindeer%2BPuppets%2B1.jpg" width="640"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 2. Cut a triangle out of your red cardstock paper. Glue your cupcake liner in the middle of your red triangle.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 3. Cut a small circle and a thin strip from your white cardstock paper. Cut the circle in half.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 4. Glue on all the finishing features to your Santa: A white pom at the top of the hat, the white strip for the brim of the hat, googly eyes, the two half circles for a mustache and your small red pom for the nose. (We used tacky glue to glue on our poms.)
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-QIpTH0guKQE/VITVz8SDQyI/AAAAAAAAUYU/6lNcdGEzV94/s1600/Santa%2BPuppet.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                  <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-QIpTH0guKQE/VITVz8SDQyI/AAAAAAAAUYU/6lNcdGEzV94/s1600/Santa%2BPuppet.jpg" width="456"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 5. Cover your jumbo craft stick half way down with tacky glue and then attach your Santa to it. Let it dry completely before playing with it.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 You can also omit the jumbo craft stick and use your Santa as a Gift tag also. Simply punch a hole in the hat of your Santa, write who the gift is to on the back, and attach it to a gift.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JeuDNMZHNV4/VITWnYVJb9I/AAAAAAAAUYg/iujKktuWYjQ/s1600/Santa%2BGift%2BTag.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                  <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JeuDNMZHNV4/VITWnYVJb9I/AAAAAAAAUYg/iujKktuWYjQ/s1600/Santa%2BGift%2BTag.jpg" width="512"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  <span style="font-size: large;">
                   REINDEER STICK PUPPET
                  </span>
                 </b>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  <br/>
                 </b>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  <u>
                   Supplies you will need:
                  </u>
                 </b>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <ul style="text-align: left;">
                  <li>
                   <a href="http://craftprojectideas.com/index.php/products/basic-elements/wood-sticks-a-dowels/41-jumbo-wood-sticks-75ct" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    jumbo craft stick
                   </a>
                   (we received ours from Craftprojectideas.com)
                  </li>
                  <li>
                   brown cardstock paper
                  </li>
                  <li>
                   <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0074MHV7W/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B0074MHV7W&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=iheacrathi04-20&amp;linkId=GZHD4XKX3E7ESRKC">
                    googly eyes
                   </a>
                   <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=iheacrathi04-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B0074MHV7W" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                  </li>
                  <li>
                   <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00COFV7GK/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00COFV7GK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=iheacrathi04-20&amp;linkId=KRGET4UEGBDKGYDC">
                    brown pipe cleaner
                   </a>
                   <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=iheacrathi04-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00COFV7GK" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                  </li>
                  <li>
                   red pom(We used
                   <a href="http://craftprojectideas.com/index.php/products/fabric-crafts/poms/84-glitter-poms-assorted-sizes-75-pack" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Glitter Poms
                   </a>
                   received from our friends at Craftprojectideas.com)
                  </li>
                  <li>
                   glue stick
                  </li>
                  <li>
                   <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00178KLEY/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00178KLEY&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=iheacrathi04-20&amp;linkId=Y75UNCYS43VAXAAL">
                    tacky glue
                   </a>
                   <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=iheacrathi04-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00178KLEY" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                   (or glue gun)
                  </li>
                 </ul>
                </div>
               </center>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-FzjHYvbxIpc/VITY4uTQEMI/AAAAAAAAUY0/Zsh5DN1XR3E/s1600/Reindeer%2BMaterials.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="512" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-FzjHYvbxIpc/VITY4uTQEMI/AAAAAAAAUY0/Zsh5DN1XR3E/s1600/Reindeer%2BMaterials.jpg" width="640"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <b>
                 <u>
                  <br/>
                 </u>
                </b>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <b>
                 <u>
                  Direct
                 </u>
                </b>
                <b>
                 <u>
                  ions:
                 </u>
                </b>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                1. Start by cutting a triangle out of your brown cardstock paper. I cut a square out about 4-inches by 4-inches and then cut out my triangle from that. Also cut out two small ears (I went with a leaf shape). Glue the ear onto the side of the reindeer face (triangle pointing down).
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                2. Cover your jumbo craft stick half way down with tacky glue and glue on your triangle with the point at the bottom.
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </center>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-KNZORnhC5Ow/VITbUZBu7OI/AAAAAAAAUZA/QLcoruDKGFo/s1600/Santa%2Band%2BReindeer%2BPuppets%2B2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="320" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-KNZORnhC5Ow/VITbUZBu7OI/AAAAAAAAUZA/QLcoruDKGFo/s1600/Santa%2Band%2BReindeer%2BPuppets%2B2.jpg" width="640"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                3. Use your tacky glue to glue on your two googly eyes, and your red pom nose.
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                4. Cut your brown chenille stem in half, then those two pieces in half again. Cut two of those sections in half one more time. You will end up with 6 pieces. Twist two of the small pieces around one of the bigger pieces one time and then point the ends upwards to make antlers. Do the same with the other pieces.
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </center>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-0R3Pvt23FR8/VITcNDSU7YI/AAAAAAAAUZI/z-ncUTgjneY/s1600/Santa%2Band%2BReindeer%2BPuppets%2B3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="320" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-0R3Pvt23FR8/VITcNDSU7YI/AAAAAAAAUZI/z-ncUTgjneY/s1600/Santa%2Band%2BReindeer%2BPuppets%2B3.jpg" width="400"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                5. Use a generous amount of tacky glue or a hot glue gun to glue your antlers to the back of your reindeer. Let your puppet dry completely before playing with it.
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </center>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-0YlRpl9OLho/VITXjO9eS7I/AAAAAAAAUYo/Rtf96ufHUPE/s1600/Reindeer%2BPuppet.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-0YlRpl9OLho/VITXjO9eS7I/AAAAAAAAUYo/Rtf96ufHUPE/s1600/Reindeer%2BPuppet.jpg" width="456"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                You can also use this cute little guy as a gift tag by making it without gluing the jumbo stick on the back. Simple punch a hole in the corner of it and attach it to a gift.
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </center>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

                 <img border="0" height="510" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cLBHedEwOu8/VITd_dtytfI/AAAAAAAAUZU/8sqeNTo1od8/s1600/Santa%2Band%2BReindeer%2BPuppets.jpg" width="640"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
               </center>
               <center style="text-align: left;">
