       <h1>
        Surprise winner in Toronto deli duel
       </h1>
       <p class="author">
        Posted by
        <a href="/author/staff">
         Staff
        </a>
        <span class="date">
         / June 21, 2011
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="stuff">
        <span class="comments">
         <a href="#comments">
          22 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
        <div class="clear">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
       <div class="content">
        <p>
         <img alt="Toronto Deli Duel" height="391" src="http://www.blogto.com/upload/2011/06/20111906-deli-8.jpg" width="531"/>
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/city/2008/11/in_photos_artscape_wychwood_barns_brings_together_artists_and_community/">
          The Wychwood Barns
         </a>
         played host to the
         <a href="http://www.thestop.org/event/19-jun-2011">
          2nd annual Deli Duel
         </a>
         this past weekend, pinning some of the city's best smoked meat against each other in a head-to-head competition, with all proceeds going to charity.
        </p>
        <p>
         An estimated 1,000 people made their way to the historic warehouse to sample smoked meat sandwiches from
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/restaurants/stockyards-smokehouse">
          Stockyards
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.goldins.ca/" target="_blank">
          Goldin's
         </a>
         , and reigning champion
         <a href="http://www.blogto.com/restaurants/caplanskys">
          Caplansky's
         </a>
         . Mini sandwiches cost $3 each, and those who sampled all three were given an opportunity to vote for their favorite.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Toronto Deli Duel" height="392" src="http://www.blogto.com/upload/2011/06/20111906-deli-2.jpg" width="590"/>
         With last year's trophy on display the lineup in front of Caplansky's was considerably longer than their competitors for much of the day. The rival delis nevertheless brought their a-game, with Goldin's serving up thick slices of meat between sample-size slices of rye while Stockyard's took a riskier approach with a stronger peppercorn seasoning accompanied with Dijon mustard.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Toronto Deli Duel" height="439" src="http://www.blogto.com/upload/2011/06/20111906-deli-3.jpg" width="590"/>
         After a long afternoon of meat carving this year's crown went to Goldin's which was awarded a bottle of champagne for their victory, not to mention a year's worth of bragging rights.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Toronto Deli Duel" height="347" src="http://www.blogto.com/upload/2011/06/20111906-deli-4.jpg" width="590"/>
         Last year's event raised approximately $10,000 for
         <a href="http://www.thestop.org" target="_blank">
          The Stop Community Food Centre
         </a>
         , a non-profit organization which strives to increase access to healthy food through a variety of outreach programs.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Toronto Deli Duel" height="392" src="http://www.blogto.com/upload/2011/06/20111906-deli-6.jpg" width="590"/>
         "This is our nod to the delis in the city," said Roz Heintzman, corporate fundraiser for The Stop. "This is a great event, and the people are just so happy to be here, it's amazing."
        </p>

             Hi. First of all, all deli should come with a pickle. Gherkins are not expensive to make or buy and Caplansky's has always been cheap for giving one third of a pickle with their sandwiches (or maybe its a quarter now) instead of a whole or even half. Cheap.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Anyways, everyone hated the Stockyards so not surprising: they've never been very good at the best of times.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             I'll judge not on eating but on a few factors: smell, appearance and service.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             For smell, Goldins is the winner with by far the least "hotdog" like smell of all of them. It smells more like the roast you would smoke at home than one done in a giant industrial smoker with pucks of sawdust...
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Appearance has Goldins tied with Stockyards. Both have a darker brown color with a bit of red around the edge (the smoke ring) and visible are the spices like coriander and peppercorns. Caplansky's, as you can see in the picture, is very pink looking, almost like baby beef. A bit too much of the Sodium Nitrate. Perls delicatessen used to do the same thing. It looks unnatural: brisket should not look like baby beef.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Service was Caplansky's forte for the day. Fresh sliced meat cannot be beat in terms of presentation, but it is no guarantee of taste. Part of the problem with Caplansky's consistent failure to serve good sandwiches is that their meat isn't always marbled with fat. When they try and sell you a fat sandwich rather than the proper marbelling it just means they leave on a piece of the fat cap on top, which isn't as tender as the interconnective tissue you want in a sandwich. Slow meat cooking is all about creating gelatine out of the collagen in these tissues but lacking these tissues you might as well be eating turkey. It's just not going to be moist enough. The best solution by the way? It isn't smoking it for 12 hours. It's smoking for 12 minutes. Cook the meat perfectly in the oven, boiling, or sous vide and them cover it in pepper and smoke the living crap out of it for ten-twenty minutes (can do it on a grill with a smoke pouch, in a wok, whatever). Trust me, you'll never have to sit outside all day next to a smoker again. Or go downtown to a deli ;)
