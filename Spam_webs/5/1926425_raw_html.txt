         <h1 class="title">
          HOTEL RESHAPED 88 TIMES
         </h1>
         <div class="post-content clear-block">
          <p>
           Architectural photographer Victor Enrich has taken photography of buildings to the next level by exploring the various formal possibilities of the NH Deutscher Kaiser Hotel in Munich.He has reshaped it in 88 different ways. Here are some examples.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2300" height="530" src="http://www.revolutionaustralia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_1.jpg" title="victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_1" width="530"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2301" height="530" src="http://www.revolutionaustralia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_2.jpg" title="victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_2" width="530"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2302" height="530" src="http://www.revolutionaustralia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_3.jpg" title="victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_3" width="530"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2303" height="530" src="http://www.revolutionaustralia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_4.jpg" title="victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_4" width="530"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2304" height="530" src="http://www.revolutionaustralia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_5.jpg" title="victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_5" width="530"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2305" height="530" src="http://www.revolutionaustralia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_6.jpg" title="victor_enrich_reshapes_hotel_88_times_6" width="530"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           From Photographer:
           <br/>
           This is a push forward to test my own limits, playing with the very simple geometry of the building I chose: the NH Deutscher Kaiser Hotel in Munich, Germany.
          </p>
          <p>
           In previous projects, I always felt I did not make enough formal explorations. So, I squeezed my brains and tried to push myself towards more. It seems that right now we are in the Time Lapse era, so I tried to make some sort of antagonist to these wonderful photographic productions of todays contemporary artists. In fact, the video I have made is, both, a time lapse and an anti-time lapse.
          </p>
          <p>
           In order to place limits on myself, and not fall into boredom, I decided to make 88 pictures (since 88 is the number of keys that modern pianos have.) I am a piano player; so I found it beautifully poetic to connect two distinct artistic disciplines such as photography and computer graphics with the classical piano.
          </p>
          <p>
           The choice of the building subject comes from my stay in Munich, a city in which I lived for a year working as an assistant for people with disabilities. During the first two months I couch-surfed the city, staying at friends apartments as a guest. This is something I do often when I am new to a place. It allows me to get introduced to the city, explore its limits and make pictures of Architecture.
          </p>
          <p>
           To be comfortable, I placed all my big luggage in the lockers of the central train station. I had to visit every three days, the maximum period that one can pay in advance. The building subject is in front of the train station. I quickly became fond of the building, having to pass by it several times during those three-day cycles. Also, important to note, I like skyscrapers. Though the subject is not necessarily tall, it is much taller than its neighbours.
          </p>
          <p>
           You can see all 88 renders in his website:
           <a href="http://victorenrich.com/?product_tag=nhdk">
            victorenrich.com
           </a>
          </p>
