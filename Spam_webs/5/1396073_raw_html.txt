     <div class="block-system-main block block-system" id="block-system-main">
      <div class="block__content">
       <div class="t7-clear-me">
       </div>
       <div class="node node-blog-entry node-promoted node-165984 comment-2 clearfix" data-nid="165984" data-type="blog_entry">
        <div class="node__content mode__full">
         <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
          <div class="field__item">
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            The desire for true
            <a class="inline-links topic-link" href="/basics/relationships" title="Psychology Today looks at love">
             love
            </a>
            runs deep. It is so profound a need, the satisfaction or deprivation thereof has the power, for better or worse, to fuel the entire universe.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Most folks would probably agree that true love means unconditional and lasting, giving and accepting. This seems though to describe dogs and babies more than it aligns with the notion of true love we are conditioned to seek.
           </p>
           <div class="insertArea">
            <div class="image-article-inline-half">
             <div class="insert-inner">
              <div class="insert-image">
               <img alt="" height="228" src="https://cdn.psychologytoday.com/sites/default/files/styles/article-inline-half/public/blogs/154333/2014/12/165984-170720.jpg?itok=fun8lnbU" width="320"/>
              </div>
              <div class="subtext insertArea--origin">
               <a href="http://desktopbackgroundshq.com/">
                http://desktopbackgroundshq.com/
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            In the search for a mate, there are criteria. Mr. Right is tall, dark, handsome and rich, (or some variation thereof) and the perfect woman is beautiful, smart and sexy, but not too much so as to detract from her future role as mother. On top of that, true love in our society implies that true means singular and romantic, and that finding it equates with living happily every after.
            <em>
             Once.
            </em>
            No wonder love can be so difficult to find and sustain with a definition as narrow and unforgiving as this.
           </p>
           <p>
            We are told what love is and what it should look like. But love-as-
            <a class="inline-links topic-link" href="/basics/marriage" title="Psychology Today looks at marriage">
             marriage
            </a>
            is a model, not an
            <a class="inline-links topic-link" href="/basics/empathy" title="Psychology Today looks at understanding">
             understanding
            </a>
            , and one that has shown its flaws. This is because it is a manmade social construct, that upon its creation, had nothing to do with love. It was designed to establish position, ensure control and create hierarchy 
            <em>
             by men
            </em>
            . If you want to read an interesting account that starts with an analysis that dates all the way back to biblical text, read,
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Dont-Contrarian-History-Marriage/dp/B0046LUUZ4" target="_blank">
             <em>
              I Dont
             </em>
             , by Susan Squire
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            But the desire for love changed that. We evolved. In the process, marriage became a fantasy-based proposition. We bought into an ideal because it repositioned marriage as a promise to deliver love forever, which we know it only actually does a fraction of the time.
           </p>
           <p>
            So, in search for true love, while we may or may not find everything (or anything) on our checklist, marriage or not, at our core, we want to be loved for who we are. In order for that to be possible though, personal truths - everything hidden underneath the person we create to survive (and succeed) in the world - need to be made available to share with whomever we want to exchange love. Eventually, then, the inner person, the true person, under the outer shell begins to emerge. And when you find someone who loves
            <em>
             that
            </em>
            unexposed individual,
            <em>
             thats
            </em>
            true love.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             I hope you'll join me on
             <a href="https://www.facebook.com/DonnaFlaggAuthorPage" target="_blank">
              Facebook
             </a>
             .
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
