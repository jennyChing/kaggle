            <h1 class="entry-title">
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/04/01/magical-landscapes-by-a-legend-eyvind-earle/" rel="bookmark">
              Magical Landscapes by a Legend: Eyvind Earle
             </a>
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta">
             on April 1, 2013 by Benjamin Starr
            </div>
            <!-- .entry-meta -->
           </header>
           <!-- .entry-header -->
           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/?p=80983">
              <img alt="Eyvind Earle 1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-80984" height="397" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Eyvind-Earle-1.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              The work of Eyvind Earle is truly timeless, looking so current it could have been painted yesterday by a mature artist wed flock to gallery openings for but Earle passed away almost 13 years ago.
             </strong>
             His lifetime of work continues to inspire with its imaginative, dreamlike scenery; confetti-like array of colors across the canvas; and for the many influential film works he helped create. If these landscape paintings look familiar, well tell you why.
             <span id="more-80983">
             </span>
            </p>
            <div class="see-also">
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/03/02/trees-in-nature-with-white-canvas-backgrounds/" title="Trees in Nature with White Canvas Backgrounds">
             </a>
             <p>
              See Also
              <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/03/02/trees-in-nature-with-white-canvas-backgrounds/" title="Trees in Nature with White Canvas Backgrounds">
               Trees in Nature with White Canvas Backgrounds
              </a>
             </p>
             <div class="clearfix">
             </div>
            </div>
            <p>
             Born in New York in 1916, Earle got his start in art at the young age of 10 when his father challenged him to choose between reading a 50 page book or painting a picture each day  showing early vigor, the young Earle chose to do both. Just 4 years later, at 14, he had his first solo show in France. Living his life in a bohemian fashion, he later bicycled across the US, from Hollywood to New York, paying his way with 42 watercolors. It was in that year, at age 21, that he began to move away from the realism that had dominated his work and on to the style we know him for today.
            </p>
            <p>
             It was in 1937 that Earles career really took off: he opened a solo show in New York to a highly enthusiastic audience, and two years later sold out the same gallery. Then, in 1951 he joined Walt Disney studios as an assistant background painter. Impressed with his work, Disney put him on projects like
             <em>
              Peter Pan
             </em>
             , and
             <em>
              Lady and the Tramp
             </em>
             ; but to see his work fully expressed, check out Sleeping Beauty, where he created the styling, backgrounds and colors for the entire film. You can learn more about this master of the 20th century at
             <a href="http://www.eyvindearle.com/Default.aspx">
              eyvindearle.com
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              For 70 years, Earle wrote in 1996, Ive painted paintings, and Im constantly and everlastingly overwhelmed at the stupendous infinity of Nature. Wherever I turn and look, there I see creation. Art is creating Art is the search for truth.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
