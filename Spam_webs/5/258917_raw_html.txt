          <a class="entry-title" href="http://amyclarkefilms.com/creative-burnout/" rel="bookmark" title="Creative Burnout">
           Creative Burnout
          </a>
         </h1>

        <div class="entry fix">
         <p>
          <a class="fancyboxgroup" href="http://amyclarkefilms.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/unmotivated-bear.jpg" rel="gallery-3331">
           <img alt="unmotivated bear" class="alignnone wp-image-3333" height="399" src="http://amyclarkefilms.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/unmotivated-bear.jpg" width="598"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          You may have noticed that I havent written anything for the past 2 weeks. The reason being is that I am in the middle of a Creative Burnout. These are the best words I can use to describe my state of mind. Sometime 2 weeks ago I felt suddenly unmotivated and unable to work at all.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is where normal people say that work is not meant to be fun, its work and you should just do it anyway. But for the creative sort of person this is not possible, the whole reason I work twice as hard to do what I want to do is because I enjoy it. And if I cant work for no apparent reason then something is wrong.
         </p>
         <p>
          It started 5 months ago when I quit Script supervising (simple because you are worked too hard in that job for very little respect and thanks). I needed a break and oddly enough the first thing I wanted to do when I got back was paint a picture. Im not good at painting and its not something I would normally do, but I guess I really needed to feed my creativity which I had unknowingly ignored for a year.
         </p>
         <p>
          I have been working on my History Documentary project for the past 2 months ,which is now mostly complete. But I guess a history documentary was not quite enough creativity to keep me motivated.
         </p>
         <p>
          Im lacking a few things right now, enough exercise, a highly creative project to get my imagination going, enough outdoors in general, a sense of adventure and excitement about life. Im mainly spending my days inside my rented magnolia painted house and sleeping.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Some Ideas for changes
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
           Go running once a week
         </p>
         <p>
           Wake up early every day
         </p>
         <p>
           Spend 5 days working a week, not 7
         </p>
         <p>
           Work outside and in cafes (no more than 2 days in a row working at home)
         </p>
         <p>
          Try not to give myself too much to do, small steps to start off with. I wonder how long it will take before I get over this.
         </p>
        </div>

             <span class="says">

            </div>
            <div class="comment-meta commentmetadata">
             <a href="http://amyclarkefilms.com/creative-burnout/#comment-768">
              September 2, 2013 at 5:19 pm
             </a>

           </div>
           <p>
            What youre going through is a totally normal  inevitable, really  part of the creative process.  Nobody can keep the pedal-to-the-metal 24/7, week in and week out.  Sooner or later you need to back off the throttle, get out of the car, and take a long walk to let your own creative batteries recharge.  Youve been pushing pretty hard for a while now  feature after feature, then the documentary, and thats enough to put anybody in a creative coma.
           </p>
           <p>
            It think it was Phillip Kaufman (director of The Right Stuff, among other films) who said something like You start out every film with the highest hopes, but by the end are reduced to a desperate struggle just to get the thing finished.
           </p>
           <p>
            Theres some truth in that  a lot of truth  so finish up your doc, get it out on the market, and take a break.  Do something or go somewhere new  shake things up a bit to replenish your creative well and shift your perspective.  The creative desire and drive will come back, I promise  its just a matter of time.
           </p>
          </div>
