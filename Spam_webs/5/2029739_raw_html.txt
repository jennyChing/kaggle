                  <span>
                   Thursday, November 27, 2014
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="3409782137860414365" itemprop="blogId"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     THE CHINA SYNDROME
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1253117300762392774" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     Like all good Americans on the day before Thanksgiving, I have spent a large part of my day at the supermarket buying food and then at home prepping for the creation of the traditional Thanksgiving feast. I've defrosted and brined a turkey. I've made pies. I've made casseroles. I've made cornbread for the cornbread stuffing. Today was simply the pre-preparation for tomorrow's official cook-a-palooza. I take Thanksgiving very seriously.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Thanksgiving is my favorite holiday. I love Christmas. I like the 4th of July. Memorial and Veterans day are inspirational. President's Day and Martin Luther King Day are good. Thanksgiving however, in my opinion, is the most American of American holidays. Where else but in the United States of America would we celebrate the generosity of the indigenous people (knowing full well the atrocities that would later be perpetrated upon them) by filling our gullets with enough food to feed a small African village for a week? I never feel more American than when I am force feeding myself a last bit of mashed potatoes, turkey, and stuffing. In my head Springsteen's "
                     <a href="https://video.search.yahoo.com/video/play;_ylt=A2KIo9Rt1XZUgUcA0J37w8QF;_ylu=X3oDMTByZ2N0cmxpBHNlYwNzcgRzbGsDdmlkBHZ0aWQDBGdwb3MDMg--?p=born+in+the+usa+lyrics+meaning&amp;vid=2d864aa8367f4e15bd9475efe70479df&amp;l=6%3A27&amp;turl=http%3A%2F%2Fts4.mm.bing.net%2Fth%3Fid%3DVN.608047527015025275%26pid%3D15.1&amp;rurl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.youtube.com%2Fwatch%3Fv%3DqzV-sjjtXqI&amp;tit=%3Cb%3EBorn+in+the+USA+%3C%2Fb%3E-+Bruce+Springsteen+%5BDVD+Live+%3Cb%3Ein+%3C%2Fb%3EBarcelona+2002%5D+%28+Subtitles+%26+%3Cb%3Elyrics+%3C%2Fb%3E%29&amp;c=1&amp;sigr=11aj2flk3&amp;sigt=13csqjoes&amp;age=0&amp;fr2=p%3As%2Cv%3Av%2Cm%3Asa&amp;fr=yfp-t-251-s&amp;tt=b" target="_blank">
                      Born in the USA
                     </a>
                     " is playing and I may utter to myself a "suck it,
                     <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J80R3gCGXsQ" target="_blank">
                      Drago
                     </a>
                     ." As I have never been in the military, Thanksgiving is my way of serving my country by eating more food in one sitting than can be imagined by most people around the world. USA! USA! USA!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     That is a long way of saying that I had Chinese food tonight. With all of the work and preparation for Thanksgiving's eat-off I had no intention or desire to cook dinner tonight. Instead I ordered Chinese food. Unfortunately, my "frugality" at times can lead to undesired consequences when it comes to my gastronomical choices.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     A good friend of mine once told me that Chinese food shouldn't be expensive. I wish he would have also told me that it shouldn't be cheap. The greasy Chinese food that I consumed wreaked havoc on my body. The ramifications of my thriftiness could only be called explosive. I experienced a meltdown in my nuclear core and the resulting mushroom cloud led to a nuclear winter that prevented the habitation of the bathroom for over an hour.
