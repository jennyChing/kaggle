          Everything I Needed to Know About Content Marketing, I Learned from Mark Twain

         <div class="article-body" dir="ltr" itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           <em>
            <span>
             Samuel Langhorne Clemens (November 30, 1835  April 21, 1910), better known by his pen name
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Twain" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              Mark Twain
             </a>
             , was an American author and humorist. He wrote The Adventures of Tom Sawyer (1876) and its sequel, Adventures of Huckleberry Finn (1885), the latter often called "the Great American Novel."
            </span>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            Yesterday there were thousands and thousands of blog posts written about content marketing. I ignored them all.
            <strong>
             Sometimes its best to go back to see ahead.
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            <strong>
             <img alt="" class="center" data-li-src="https://media.licdn.com/mpr/mpr/p/2/005/04b/2e1/2ca2880.jpg" src="//:0"/>
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The secret of getting ahead is getting started.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            Think its too late to start a blog now? Why even bother creating those YouTube videos, theres just too much competition, right?
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            Ignore all of those evil thoughts.
           </span>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://blog.bufferapp.com/the-habits-of-successful-people-they-start-before-they-feel-ready" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Start now
            </a>
            .
           </strong>
           <em>
            Its the only way to make a difference tomorrow.
           </em>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The secret of getting started is breaking your complex, overwhelming tasks into small, manageable tasks, and then starting on that first one.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            Content marketing itself can be overwhelming. You start thinking about EVERYTHING that needs to get done, all of the different channels that you NEED to be creating content for, all of the different people you HAVE to connect with and it becomes too much for some people. So they try to do a little bit of everything and never really give anything their ALL.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            It doesnt have to be like this.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            Start with aggressive but realistic goals and then work backwards. What is the most important thing you need to do?
           </span>
           <a href="http://zenhabits.net/purpose-your-day-most-important-task/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <span>
             Whats your MIT
            </span>
           </a>
           <span>
            ? Do that.
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Don't go around saying the world owes you a living. The world owes you nothing. It was here first.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            Your audience doesnt owe you anything. Thats if you are lucky enough to even have an audience. Give them something of value. Show them you appreciate THEM.
           </span>
           <a href="http://moz.com/blog/8-ways-to-delight-the-pants-off-your-community-whiteboard-friday" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <span>
             Delight the pants off them
            </span>
           </a>
           <span>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             It is better to remain silent and be thought a fool than to open one's mouth and remove all doubt.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            <span>
             <span>
              Can you imagine how much different the Internet would be if people truly adopted a quality over quantity approach to content?
             </span>
            </span>
            <br/>
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             If you tell the truth, you don't have to remember anything.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            <span>
             <span>
              <span>
               <span>
                Dont just
               </span>
               <span>
                try
               </span>
               <span>
                to be authentic. Actually be authentic. Be honest. It makes everyones job easier.
               </span>
              </span>
              <br/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            <span>
             <span>
              <iframe allowfullscreen="" data-li-src="//www.youtube.com/embed/-60R0rC7GU4" frameborder="0" height="315" src="about:blank" width="560">
              </iframe>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Be careful about reading health books. You may die of a misprint.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            Experiment and test for yourself. Dont just rely on what you read from
           </span>
           <em>
            experts
           </em>
           <span>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Whenever you find yourself on the side of the majority, it is time to pause and reflect.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            If everyone loves everything you create, you are doing something wrong. Dont be afraid to take chances. Take a stand on the the issues that matter to you.
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The most interesting information comes from children, for they tell all they know and then stop.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            <span>
             Be honest. Be curious. Be succinct. Great ideas,
            </span>
            <a href="http://mintaka.sdsu.edu/faculty/wfw/CLASSES/ASTROBIO/science.kids" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             <span>
              great explanations
             </span>
            </a>
            <span>
             and great content come from these tenets.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             It ain't what you don't know that gets you into trouble. It's what you know for sure that just ain't so.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            <span>
             <span>
              <span>
               <span>
                Is there anything worse than that person who is so sure of themselves and can only see from their viewpoint?
               </span>
               <span>
                The one who wont listen to anything anyone else has to say and is only too happy to tell everyone about it?
               </span>
               <span>
                Yes, there is:
                <strong>
                 a brand that does this.
                </strong>
               </span>
              </span>
              <br/>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Humor is mankind's greatest blessing.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            Noone said that your content hasnt to always be serious. Make people laugh. Help brighten their day. Maybe even help them
           </span>
           <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/what-laughter-can-do-you" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <span>
             live longer
            </span>
           </a>
           <span>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             You can't depend on your eyes when your imagination is out of focus.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            Get outside of your bubble as often as you can. When you are too close to something, what you think you see may not actually be what is there.
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             I can live for two months on a good compliment.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            <span>
             <span>
              <span>
               <span>
                <span>
                 A big part of content marketing success is the social aspect. Are you doing enough to make your audience feel good? What about your employees? Other creators? When was the last time you
                 <a href="http://www.positivityblog.com/index.php/2008/03/26/5-kick-ass-reasons-to-give-a-genuine-compliment-and-how-to-do-it/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  complimented someone
                 </a>
                 ? Do it everyday. Let someone ride high, you will feel better too.
                </span>
                <strong>
                 <br/>
                </strong>
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Do the right thing. It will gratify some people and astonish the rest.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            <span>
             <span>
              <span>
               <span>
                <span>
                 <span>
                  <span>
                   People take notice when you
                  </span>
                  <a href="http://lifehacker.com/always-do-right-this-will-gratify-some-people-and-ast-1300779343" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                   <span>
                    do the right thing
                   </span>
                  </a>
                  <span>
                   . Sometimes it doesnt feel like it and its hard but when you do the right thing, whether it's going the extra mile to make your content truly remarkable or simply being honest and truly transparent, people will notice. Some will be pleased you're doing it, others will be surprised, some will hate it because it goes against so much of what they see each day.
                   <a href="https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10152590150818012&amp;set=a.10150209284848012.374230.500013011&amp;type=1&amp;stream_ref=10" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Do it anyway
                   </a>
                   <strong>
                    .
                   </strong>
                  </span>
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Substitute 'damn' every time you're inclined to write 'very'; your editor will delete it and the writing will be just as it should be.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            <span>
             <span>
              <span>
               <span>
                <span>
                 <span>
                  <span>
                   <span>
                    Simply put, this is
                    <del class="strikethrough">
                     very
                    </del>
                    damn good advice.
                   </span>
                   <strong>
                    <br/>
                   </strong>
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </span>
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <strong>
             All generalizations are false, including this one.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span>
            Take the title of this post for example. I obviously didnt learn everything I know about content marketing by reading Mark Twains works, his quotes or his biographies. I didnt learn everything from anyone. I have to learn by doing. By
           </span>
           <a href="http://chetztogom.com/legend/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/mark-twain-frog.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <span>
             eating that frog
            </span>
           </a>
           <span>
            every morning.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            <span>
             <span>
              <span>
               <span>
                <span>
                 <span>
                  <span>
                   <span>
                    <span>
                     <span>
                      The lessons that I have learned from standing on the shoulders of giants like Mark Twain have impacted me greatly and remind me that
                      <em>
                       great ideas and principles are timeless.
                      </em>
                     </span>
                    </span>
                    <br/>
                   </span>
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </span>
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What are some of your favorite quotes? Why? Please share them in the comments below so we can all continue to learn and see things from a different perspective.
           </strong>
          </p>
