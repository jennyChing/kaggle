        <div id="heading">
         <span class="title-pink">
          Stay Motivated and keep fit this winter!
         </span>
        </div>
        <small>
         Posted on Friday, June 14, 2013
        </small>
        <br/>
        <div style="margin-top:15px;">
         <span class="st_fblike_hcount" displaytext="Facebook Like">
         </span>
         <span class="st_twitter_hcount" displaytext="Tweet" st_via="firstforwomen">
         </span>
         <span class="st_linkedin_hcount" displaytext="LinkedIn">
         </span>
         <span class="st_pinterest_hcount" displaytext="Pinterest">
         </span>
         <span class="st_googleplus_hcount" displaytext="Google +">
         </span>
        </div>
        <div>
         <p>
          As winter closes in the days get shorter and temperatures start  dropping. Sticking to an exercise routine seems almost impossible when  just the thought of changing into your gym clothes is enough to send you  straight back to the couch and that cup of hot chocolate you were  planning. Dare to be different this winter and exercise your way to  summer. Heres how.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="276" src="/Global/Blog-Winter-Exercise-%23190BB(1).jpg" width="529"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Stay motivated
          </strong>
          <br/>
          The key to success lies in keeping yourself motivated.
          <strong>
           Write down your goals
          </strong>
          for your winter fitness plan and remind yourself of them when the going  gets tough. Maybe you want to run a half marathon in summer or even  just fit into your summer wardrobe when the weather warms up without  having to invest in a new pair of jeans!
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Another good way to stay motivated and ready for action is to
          <strong>
           remind yourself of the benefits of exercise
          </strong>
          ,  from the obvious health benefits to maintaining a toned and lean body  and of course keeping those winter blues at bay. Exercise also gets the  blood pumping and will warm you up faster than that high calorie mug of  hot chocolate you were planning! And dont forget to
          <strong>
           reward yourself
          </strong>
          for  sticking to your routine. Whether it is a new winter jacket, a weekend  away or a giant slice of carrot cake, working towards a reward is a  sure-fire way to keep yourself on your toes.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Lastly, consider working out with
          <strong>
           an exercise buddy
          </strong>
          for the winter months. Exercising with a friend is more fun and it is  harder to make excuses when there is somebody waiting for you at the  gym!
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Get organised
          </strong>
          <br/>
          A successful winter exercise plan needs to be carefully organised:
         </p>
         <div id="bullets2">
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: small;">
             Where: some experts recommend exercising from home in the winter months
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: small;">
             How: consider shaking up your routine and finding a new way to exercise, for example a dance class or martial arts
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: small;">
             When: draw up a realistic routine that accommodates family and work and stick to it!
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <strong>
           Remember that nobody is perfect! Dont let a few missed sessions  derail you. Keep reminding yourself of your goals and keep exercising!
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Beyond exercise
           </strong>
           <br/>
           And a final world of advice Remember that good health it not just  about exercise, it is also about your diet, how you manage stress and  regular medical check-ups!
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Hot Tip
           </strong>
           <br/>
           Try the MyFitnessPal app  itll help you monitor your calories,  regulate your exercise and achieve some of your daily diet and fitness  goals.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <em>
            "It is health that is real wealth and not pieces of gold and silver."
            <br/>
            Mahatma Gandhi
           </em>
          </p>
