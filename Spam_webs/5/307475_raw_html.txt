     <p>
      <b>
       John McClane
      </b>
      (born circa 1955) is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">
       fictional character
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">
       protagonist
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Die_Hard_(film_series)" title="Die Hard (film series)">
       <i>
        Die Hard
       </i>
       film series
      </a>
      . He is portrayed by
      <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">
       Bruce Willis
      </a>
      . He is known for his sardonic one-liners, including the famous catchphrase "Yippee-ki-yay, motherfucker".
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Character_portrayal">
       Character portrayal
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=John_McClane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Character portrayal">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      John McClane was originally based on the fictional character Detective Joe Leland from
      <a href="/wiki/Roderick_Thorp" title="Roderick Thorp">
       Roderick Thorp
      </a>
      's bestselling novel,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothing_Lasts_Forever_(1979_novel)" title="Nothing Lasts Forever (1979 novel)">
        Nothing Lasts Forever
       </a>
      </i>
      , along with another character, Frank Malone from
      <a href="/wiki/Walter_Wager" title="Walter Wager">
       Walter Wager
      </a>
      's novel
      <i>
       58 Minutes
      </i>
      (later adapted as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Die_Hard_2" title="Die Hard 2">
        Die Hard 2
       </a>
      </i>
      ) and is somewhat based on and inspired by
      <a href="/wiki/Harry_Callahan_(character)" title="Harry Callahan (character)">
       Dirty Harry
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Die Hard
      </i>
      villain Hans Gruber describes him as "just another American.... who thinks he's
      <a href="/wiki/John_Wayne" title="John Wayne">
       John Wayne
      </a>
      ,"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      to which McClane replies that he "was always partial to
      <a href="/wiki/Roy_Rogers" title="Roy Rogers">
       Roy Rogers
      </a>
      ."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He is described as being a "foul-mouthed, wisecracking, no-nonsense New York cop with an itchy trigger finger ... and a never-say-die maverick spirit."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-empire_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-empire-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      McClane's marriage is in a constant state of crisis, his
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vigilantism" title="Vigilantism">
       vigilantism
      </a>
      and disregard for authority have put him in danger of losing his job more than once, and he is a
      <a href="/wiki/Chain_smoking" title="Chain smoking">
       chain-smoker
      </a>
      who is described by Inspector Cobb in
      <i>
       Die Hard with a Vengeance
      </i>
      as being "two steps away from becoming a full blown
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alcoholic" title="Alcoholic">
       alcoholic
      </a>
      ", which McClane jokingly corrects him saying only "one step".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      McClane is consistently portrayed as a
      <a href="/wiki/Reluctant_hero" title="Reluctant hero">
       reluctant hero
      </a>
      who, with little or no assistance from others, is required against his wishes to thwart the elaborate plans of a group of like-minded villains because no one else is in a position to do so. The trailer for the first
      <i>
       Die Hard
      </i>
      film states, "The last thing McClane wants is to be a hero, but he doesn't have a choice." In the second film, he is told "You're the wrong guy in the wrong place at the wrong time", to which he replies, "The story of my life." In the fourth film, he says he gets involved in dangerous situations "because there is nobody else to do it."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the first
      <i>
       Die Hard
      </i>
      movie, McClane carries a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beretta_92F" title="Beretta 92F">
       Beretta 92F
      </a>
      as his duty weapon. In the next two movies, his service pistol is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beretta_92FS" title="Beretta 92FS">
       Beretta 92FS
      </a>
      , even though the NYPD has never issued the Beretta 92 for duty use. He later uses a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sig_P220" title="Sig P220">
       Sig P220
      </a>
      as his duty gun in the fourth and fifth movies of the series.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      </a>

     </p>
     <p>
      Holly moved to Los Angeles several months earlier to pursue a career, leading to their separation. McClane stayed behind, officially to cope with open cases, unofficially hoping she would relent. Their two children, Lucy and John, are in her custody.
     </p>
     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Eve" title="Christmas Eve">

      <a href="/wiki/Bearer_bond" title="Bearer bond">

      </a>

     </p>
     <p>
      McClane escapes detection, hides throughout the building, and kills the terrorists one by one. When McClane meets the terrorists' leader, Hans Gruber, a climactic battle ensues. McClane shoots Gruber, who then falls out a high window.

McClane discovers a conspiracy between the mercenaries and an active military unit to rescue a notorious dictator from being imprisoned for crimes against humanity. He foils their plans and provides a visual landing signal for the circling aircraft by exploding the plane the villains were using for their exit strategy.

With the help of Carver, McClane tracks Simon to the Canadian border. In a decisive final confrontation in a helicopter, McClane ends Simon's plan and his life with a handgun and a power line.

      <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_Bedelia" title="Bonnie Bedelia">

      </i>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nakatomi_Plaza" title="Nakatomi Plaza">

      <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">

      </a>

      <i>

      </i>

      <i>
       Die Hard with a Vengeance
      </i>
      , she is mentioned in conversation. McClane makes an attempt to telephone her, and her voice is heard briefly on the phone. She still resides in Los Angeles and is separated from, but still married to McClane.

      <i>

      </i>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Catchphrase">
       Catchphrase
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=John_McClane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Catchphrase">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Throughout the films, McClane is known for his catchphrase, "Yippee-ki-yay, motherfucker". He frequently says it to taunt his adversaries, or in the moments before killing them.
     </p>
     <p>
      McClane adopts the phrase in the first film,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Die_Hard" title="Die Hard">
        Die Hard
       </a>
      </i>
      , when villain Hans Gruber calls him a cowboy and asks if he thinks he stands a chance. McClane replies, "Yippee-ki-yay, motherfucker". At the end of the film, before his death at the hands of McClane, Gruber echoes the phrase before McClane overpowers him.
     </p>
     <p>
      McClane repeats the phrase in the two following films. At the end of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Die_Hard_2" title="Die Hard 2">
        Die Hard 2
       </a>
      </i>
      , he delivers it before lighting a trail of fuel to destroy the enemy airplane. At the end of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Die_Hard_with_a_Vengeance" title="Die Hard with a Vengeance">
        Die Hard with a Vengeance
       </a>
      </i>
      , he says it in a low voice after shooting down the enemy helicopter.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the fourth film,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Live_Free_or_Die_Hard" title="Live Free or Die Hard">
        Live Free or Die Hard
       </a>
      </i>
      , McClane is held captive by cyber-terrorist Thomas Gabriel. Gabriel taunts him, claiming that McClane's tombstone will read "always in the wrong place at the wrong time". McClane replies, "How about 'yippee-ki-yay, motherfu'?" as he shoots himself to kill Gabriel. The last two syllables are drowned out by the gunshot in the only film in the series to receive a PG-13 rating. In the unrated version, the "Yippee-ki-yay, motherfucker" line is left uncensored as he fires right after it.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the fifth film,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/A_Good_Day_to_Die_Hard" title="A Good Day to Die Hard">
        A Good Day to Die Hard
       </a>
      </i>
      , McClane says the catchphrase before driving a truck out of an airborne helicopter in the final confrontation in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chernobyl_(city)" title="Chernobyl (city)">
       Chernobyl
      </a>
      .
     </p>
