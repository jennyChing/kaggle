        <h1 class="entry-title">
         5 Ways to Start Saving Money NOW!
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard fn">
          Kristi
         </span>
         on
         <abbr class="published" title="2010-10-21">
          October 21, 2010
         </abbr>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://spendlessandsavemore.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/piggybank_santa_hat.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3599" height="135" src="http://spendlessandsavemore.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/piggybank_santa_hat.jpg" title="piggybank_santa_hat" width="128"/>
         </a>
         With the holidays quickly approaching, you are probably starting to think about how you are going to afford buying all those Christmas gifts. It is not too late to start saving. Try to do the following in the next month &amp; see how much your able to save:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Stop dining out (this means for lunch too). I want you to think about how much you spend each time you go out to eat. If you spend $25 a week on dining out, in one months time, you will have
          <strong>
           saved $100
          </strong>
          ! If you spend more than $25 on dining out a week, you will save even more!
         </li>
         <li>
          Stop buying coffee. An average coffee at Starbucks can run about $4. If you get 2 coffees per week, you will
          <strong>
           save around $16 a month
          </strong>
          !
         </li>
         <li>
          Stop going to the movies &amp; rent one instead. If a family of 4 goes to the movies. The average cost of this trip is $32 just on tickets. Include drinks &amp; popcorn and you can easily
          <strong>
           spend $50
          </strong>
          . Go without the kids? Your spending just as much if you have to hire a babysitter!
         </li>
         <li>
          Stop Grocery Shopping! Well, at least a little. If you can cut out shopping for one week, the average family can save nearly $100! Often we overlook what is in our pantry and our freezer. Get creative! Look online for recipes by doing a search by ingredients on a site like allrecipes.com.
         </li>
         <li>
          Dont buy one item of clothing this month. Im not sure what your family spends per month on clothing, but I would imagine that you spend something. Clothing is easy to justify spending money on especially because kids outgrow clothes so fast. If you absolutely must buy something, really consider shopping at Goodwill, Thrift Store or Consignment shop.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         <em>
          What other ways can you think of to save money between now &amp; the holidays?
         </em>
        </p>
