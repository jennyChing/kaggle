             <p>
              Recent History Department graduate Philip Van Huizen has been awarded this year's
              <a href="http://pcb.cgu.edu/prizes.htm" target="_blank">
               W. Turrentine Jackson Dissertation Prize
              </a>
              from the Pacific Coast Branch of the American Historical Association. The prize is awarded "to the author of the most outstanding dissertation on any aspect of the history of the American West in the twentieth century."
             </p>
             <p>
              Phil formerly won theW. Turrentine Jackson Prize for his article,Building a Green Dam: Environmental Modernism and the Canadian-American Libby Dam Project,Pacific Historical Review, 79 3 (2010), 418  453.
