      <h1 class="page-header">
       Getting Real: Long-chained Fatty Acids in Grass-fed Beef
      </h1>
      <div class="region region-content">
       <section class="block block-system clearfix" id="block-system-main">
        <div about="/blog/getting-real-long-chained-fatty-acids-grass-fed-beef" class="node node-article node-promoted clearfix" id="node-110" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">
         <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="Getting Real: Long-chained Fatty Acids in Grass-fed Beef" property="dc:title">
         </span>
         <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="0" datatype="xsd:integer" property="sioc:num_replies">
         </span>

         <div class="content">
          <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
           <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
             <p>
              Farming is at the heart of every culture, and animal husbandry is an art that has stayed the same for hundreds of years. But modern science is changing the way we raise our animals as well as how we eat.
             </p>
             <p>
              Today, farmers have a big choice to make, and consumers are included in that decision too. Consider this for a moment: by raising our livestock in a natural pasture environment, and forgoing pesticides, hormones, and restrictive caging practices, we are carrying on a way of raising cattle that has been practiced for centuries.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <!--break-->
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.blackdiamondmeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/happy_cows.jpg" style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; color: rgb(80, 80, 80); text-decoration: none;">
               <img alt="happy_cows" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-122" src="http://www.blackdiamondmeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/happy_cows-1024x768.jpg" style="border:1px solid rgb(233, 232, 215); clear:both; display:block; height:auto; margin:0px auto 12px; max-width:100%; padding:3px; vertical-align:top; width:845px"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Plus, consumers are seeking wholesome meats that have been raised using the cleanest, most traditional, methods possible. And they are justified in doing so, because as we learn more about the pitfalls of modern agriculture, the more the benefits of traditional methods become clear.
             </p>
             <p>
              The reason that consumers are returning to these traditional and proven methods of raising livestock is that modern science, despite being the catalyst for many unhealthy methods of agriculture, is showing that the natural way is best. One benefit of pastured meat, which science has revealed, is elevated levels of long-chained fatty acids  compounds that help to control inflammation, weight, and other crucial functions in our bodies. For example, Conjugated Lineolic Acid, or CLA, is a long-chained fatty acid that helps our bodies build muscle and burn fat. In fact, it is so effective that it is commonly sold as a dietary supplement.
             </p>
             <p>
              However, grass-fed and grass-finished beef is naturally rich in CLA, in addition to a host of other beneficial and essential fatty acids, so why supplement when you can go straight to the source?
             </p>
             <h3>
              The Details
             </h3>
             <p>
              Todays natural food advocate is far more informed about the science of nutrition than the average consumer. On the topic of livestock, advocates highlight the following benefits of grass-fed beef: lower concentrations of toxins, less impact on cholesterol levels, and long-chained fatty acids like Omega-3, 7, and 9. Although the terminology surrounding these topics can be puzzling to some, these are the healthful details that separate grass-fed beef from its conventionally raised alternative. Upon further investigation, these topics are actually quite simple, and they can empower us to make better decisions.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.blackdiamondmeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Cow_eating_grass.jpg" style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; color: rgb(80, 80, 80); text-decoration: none;">
               <img alt="Cow_eating_grass" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40" src="http://www.blackdiamondmeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Cow_eating_grass.jpg" style="border:1px solid rgb(233, 232, 215); clear:both; display:block; height:auto; margin:0px auto; max-width:100%; padding:3px; vertical-align:top; width:845px"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p align="center">
              <em>
               Photo by
               <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/dizzo/2168242107/sizes/m/" style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; color: rgb(80, 80, 80); text-decoration: none;" target="_blank" title="David Izzo">
                David Izzo
               </a>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Scientists and farmers alike are becoming more and more aware of the benefits of grass-fed beef. The Standard American Diet is high in Omega-6, a pro-inflammatory fatty acid that can hamper your weight-loss efforts as well as promote chronic disease when consumed in excess. Grass-fed beef contains the anti-inflammatory fatty acids, Omega-3 for example, that our bodies need to balance our high Omega-6 intake.
             </p>
             <h3>
              All About Chains
             </h3>
             <p>
              It turns out that there are some plant-based sources of Omega-3 as well, such as flaxseed and walnuts, but theres a catch. Not all omega-3 fatty acids are created equally. Most plant-based sources are found to be short-chained instead of long-chained, and research has found that humans benefit most from long-chained fatty acids.
             </p>
             <p>
              Our bodies will benefit most from long-chained Omegas, and thats exactly what you get from the top-quality grass-raised and grass-finished beef provided by
              <a href="http://www.blackdiamondmeats.com/" style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; color: rgb(80, 80, 80); text-decoration: none;">
               Black Diamond Meats
              </a>
              .
             </p>
