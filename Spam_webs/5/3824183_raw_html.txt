           <h1>
            <a href="/new-seo-tools">
             New SEO Tools
            </a>
           </h1>
           <div class="info">
            <div class="ribbon-fold-left">
             <img src="/img/ribbon-green-left.png"/>

            <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
             <div class="field-items">
              <div class="field-item even">
               <p>
                <a href="http://getlisted.org/">
                 Get Listed
                </a>
                is a cool tool for seeing how your website looks in local search, and to aid you in submitting your site to local search engines.
               </p>
               <p>
                Wordtracker announced
                <a href="http://www.wordtracker.com/blog/new-wordtracker-keyword-tool-is-coming/">
                 they will be launching a new version of their keyword tool
                </a>
                soon.
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="http://www.fairshare.cc/fairshare/">
                 FairShare
                </a>
                helps you track scrapers publishing your content.
               </p>
               <p>
                WebReader makes it easy for people to click a button that
                <a href="http://webreader.readspeaker.com/">
                 makes your blog speech enabled
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                Wiep
                <a href="http://wiep.net/talk/tools-plugins/buzzstream-beta/">
                 highlighted
                </a>
                a new link building and public relations tool called
                <a href="http://www.buzzstream.com/">
                 BuzzStream
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                SEOmoz announced
                <a href="http://www.seomoz.org/blog/backlink-anchor-text-analysis-powered-by-linkscape">
                 they retooled their backlink anchor text analysis tool
                </a>
                using their Linkscape data, which they also
                <a href="http://www.seomoz.org/blog/announcing-seomozs-100-free-linkscape-api-the-500-most-linkedto-domainspages-on-the-web">
                 released an API for
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                Majestic SEO created
                <a href="http://www.majesticseo.com/research/neighbourhood-checker.php?d=seobook.com">
                 a neighboring sites search
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="http://www.wolframalpha.com/">
                 Wolfram Alpha
                </a>
                is to
                <a href="http://blog.wolfram.com/2009/03/05/wolframalpha-is-coming/">
                 launch in May
                </a>
                as an answer engine. I predict that launch strategy will create branding issues (like not being able to outsource the blame for their poor algorithms onto spammers - like Google does) but if the tool is decent it might be a great tool to use when researching content generation ideas. The new
                <a href="http://cashkeywords.ourtoolbar.com/">
                 CashKeywords toolbar
                </a>
                also looks quite useful for researching content ideas.
               </p>
               <p>
                In January Google started offering a new, more-advanced
                <a href="http://googlewebmastercentral.blogspot.com/2009/01/new-google-sitemap-generator-for-your.html">
                 sitemap generator
                </a>
                , and
                <a href="http://www.zengestrom.com/blog/2009/01/signal-and-noise-on-jaiku-this-week.html">
                 made Jaiku (a Twitter-like tool) open source
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="http://labs.mozilla.com/2009/01/test-pilot-vision/">
                 Mozilla is testing collecting usage data to create open research
                </a>
                , hopefully they don't
                <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2006/08/09/technology/09aol.html?ex=1312776000">
                 pull an AOL with the data
                </a>
                though.
               </p>
               <p>
                Some developers are
                <a href="http://radar.oreilly.com/2009/03/wikirank-zeitgeist-wikipedia.html">
                 creating new tools to analyze Wikipedia
                </a>
                .
               </p>
