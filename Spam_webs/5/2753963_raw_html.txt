     <p>
      <b>
       Editorial independence
      </b>
      is the
      <a href="/wiki/Free_will" title="Free will">
       freedom
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Copy_editing" title="Copy editing">
       editors
      </a>
      to make decisions without interference from the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Owners" title="Owners">
       owners
      </a>
      of a
      <a href="/wiki/Publication" title="Publication">
       publication
      </a>
      . Editorial independence is tested, for instance, if a newspaper runs
      <a href="/wiki/Article_(publishing)" title="Article (publishing)">
       articles
      </a>
      that may be unpopular with its
      <a href="/wiki/Advertising" title="Advertising">
       advertising
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clientele" title="Clientele">
       clientele
      </a>
      or critical of its ownership.
     </p>
