           <div class="s-element-content s-text emojify">
            <p>
             Anyone who
loves working with sounds and instruments, and wants to present it to the world
as music, is a good candidate to take up a
             <a class="" href="http://www.education.gov.uk/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              <b>
               sound engineering course
              </b>
             </a>
             .
This teaches them about the art of recording, capturing, copying, mixing
reproducing and editing sound, using various mechanical and electronic devices.
There are numerous options to help you qualify.
            </p>
            <p>
             <br/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <i>
              <b>
               Production
and Post Production Options
              </b>
             </i>
            </p>
            <p>
             <br/>
            </p>
            <p>
             The process
can either be the actual recording, which is termed as production, or it could
be the process of adding a magic touch, mixing and editing on what has been
already recorded. The latter is called post production, and both processes are
equally important. You can keep your preference in mind when you choose a
particular line of study and register for
             <a class="" href="http://london.s-s-r.com/course/audioengineering" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              <b>
               sound engineering courses
in London
              </b>
             </a>
             <b>
              music schools
             </b>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             <br/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <i>
              <b>
               Qualifying
for the Course
              </b>
             </i>
            </p>
            <p>
             Theres no
pre-qualification for registering in a sound engineering course, but generally,
you ought to have a good ear, capable of paying attention to details, and have interest
in music. Opportunities abound in the
music industry, video production, sound broadcasting, films and advertising.
You should be willing to work long hours, though, as this industry does not have
fixed work schedules. A basic knowledge of physics and mathematics will
definitely come in handy as acoustic engineers need to calculate a lot.
            </p>
            <p>
             <br/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <i>
              <b>
               After the
Learning
              </b>
             </i>
            </p>
            <p>
             <br/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Once you
finish the
             <a class="" href="http://homerecording.about.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              <b>
               sound engineering course
              </b>
             </a>
             , you can work in a studio or as a freelancer.
Its a very competitive field and it will help if you specialise as a studio
engineer, broadcast engineer, recorder or editor, dialogue editor, mix
engineer, or any other acoustic-related jobs. You can either work in recording
studios making records for commercial releases or engineer the acoustics for
performing bands, musicians and theatre artists when they perform for a live
audience.
            </p>
            <p>
             <br/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Many audio-visual
rental companies are always on the lookout for qualified professionals to
operate the mixing desk for a conference, presentation or exhibition, where an
audio video system has been rented out. Cruise liners also need competent
engineers for their bands while cruising.
Some acoustic engineers even purchase their own equipment and offer both
their equipment and services for hire.
            </p>
            <p>
             Everybody
loves a good music, and as long as everybody wants to listen to something good,
sound engineers will always be there to provide support and their expertise.
            </p>
