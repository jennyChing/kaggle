     <p>
      <b>
       Nicholas Roerich
      </b>
      (
      <span class="nowrap">
       <span class="IPA nopopups">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">
         /
         <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
          <span title="// primary stress follows">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'r' in 'rye'">
           r
          </span>
          <span title="/r/ 'ir' in 'bird'">
           r
          </span>
          <span title="// short 'i' in 'bid'">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'k' in 'kind'">
           k
          </span>
         </span>
         /
        </a>
       </span>
      </span>
      ; October 9, 1874  December 13, 1947)  known also as
      <b>
       Nikolai Konstantinovich Rerikh
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
       Russian
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="ru">
         
      </span>
      )  was a Russian painter, writer,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaeologist" title="Archaeologist">
       archaeologist
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theosophist" title="Theosophist">
       theosophist
      </a>
      , perceived by some in
      <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">
       Russia
      </a>
      as an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enlightener" title="Enlightener">
       enlightener
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Philosopher" title="Philosopher">
       philosopher
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Public_figure" title="Public figure">
       public figure
      </a>
      , who in his youth was influenced by a movement in Russian society around the occult. He was interested in
      <a href="/wiki/Hypnosis" title="Hypnosis">
       hypnosis
      </a>
      and other
      <a href="/wiki/Spiritual_practice" title="Spiritual practice">
       spiritual practices
      </a>
      and his paintings are said to have hypnotic expression.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Born in
      <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">
       Saint Petersburg
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Russia" title="Imperial Russia">
       Russia
      </a>
      to the family of a well-to-do
      <a href="/wiki/Notary_public" title="Notary public">
       notary public
      </a>
      , he lived in various places around the world until his death in
      <a href="/wiki/Naggar" title="Naggar">
       Naggar
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Himachal_Pradesh,_India" title="Himachal Pradesh, India">
       Himachal Pradesh, India
      </a>
      . Trained as an artist and a lawyer, his main interests were literature, philosophy,
      <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">
       archaeology
      </a>
      , and especially art. Roerich was a dedicated activist for the cause of preserving art and architecture during times of war. He earned several nominations for the
      <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">
       Nobel Peace Prize
      </a>
      . The so-called
      <a href="/wiki/Roerich_Pact" title="Roerich Pact">
       Roerich Pact
      </a>
      was signed into law by the United States and most nations of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan-American_Union" title="Pan-American Union">
       Pan-American Union
      </a>
      during April 1935.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Roerich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">

     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nicholas_Roerich,_Guests_from_Overseas.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1300" data-file-width="1770" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Nicholas_Roerich%2C_Guests_from_Overseas.jpg/240px-Nicholas_Roerich%2C_Guests_from_Overseas.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Nicholas_Roerich%2C_Guests_from_Overseas.jpg/360px-Nicholas_Roerich%2C_Guests_from_Overseas.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Nicholas_Roerich%2C_Guests_from_Overseas.jpg/480px-Nicholas_Roerich%2C_Guests_from_Overseas.jpg 2x" width="240"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nicholas_Roerich,_Guests_from_Overseas.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <i>
         Guests from Overseas
        </i>
        , 1901 (
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Varangian" title="Varangian">
         Varangians
        </a>
        in Rus').
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Raised in late 19th century St. Petersburg, Roerich matriculated simultaneously at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Petersburg_University" title="St. Petersburg University">
       St. Petersburg University
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Academy_of_Arts" title="Imperial Academy of Arts">
       Imperial Academy of Arts
      </a>
      during 1893. He received the title of "artist" during 1897 and a degree in law the next year. He found early employment with the Imperial Society for the Encouragement of the Arts, whose school he directed from 1906 to 1917. Despite early tensions with the group, he became a member of
      <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Diaghilev" title="Sergei Diaghilev">
       Sergei Diaghilev
      </a>
      's "
      <a href="/wiki/World_of_Art" title="World of Art">
       World of Art
      </a>
      " society; he was president of the society from 1910 to 1916.
     </p>
     <p>
      Artistically, he became known as his generation's most talented painter of Russia's ancient past, a topic that was compatible with his lifelong interest in archaeology. He also succeeded as a stage designer, achieving his greatest fame as one of the designers for Diaghilev's
      <a href="/wiki/Ballets_Russes" title="Ballets Russes">
       Ballets Russes
      </a>
      . His best-known designs were for
      <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Borodin" title="Alexander Borodin">
       Borodin's
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Prince_Igor" title="Prince Igor">
        Prince Igor
       </a>
      </i>
      (1909 and later productions), and costumes and set for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Rite_of_Spring" title="The Rite of Spring">
        The Rite of Spring
       </a>
      </i>
      (1913),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYT-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      composed by
      <a href="/wiki/Igor_Stravinsky" title="Igor Stravinsky">
       Igor Stravinsky
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Another of Roerich's passions was architecture. His acclaimed publication "Architectural Studies" (19041905)  the dozens of paintings he completed of fortresses, monasteries, churches, and other monuments during two long trips through Russia- inspired his decades-long career as an activist on behalf of artistic and architectural preservation. He also designed religious art for places of worship throughout Russia and
      <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">
       Ukraine
      </a>
      : most notably the
      <i>
       Queen of Heaven
      </i>
      fresco for the Church of the Holy Spirit which the patroness
      <a href="/wiki/Princess_Maria_Tenisheva" title="Princess Maria Tenisheva">
       Maria Tenisheva
      </a>
      built near her
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Talashkino&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Talashkino (page does not exist)">
       Talashkino
      </a>
      estate; and the stained glass windows for the
      <a href="/wiki/Datsan_Gunzechoinei" title="Datsan Gunzechoinei">
       Datsan Gunzechoinei
      </a>
      during 19131915.
     </p>
     <p>
      During the first decade of the 1900s and in the early 1910s, Roerich, largely due to the influence of his wife Helena, developed an interest in eastern religions, as well as alternative (to Christianity) belief systems such as
      <a href="/wiki/Theosophy" title="Theosophy">
       Theosophy
      </a>
      . Both Roerichs became avid readers of the Vedantist essays of
      <a href="/wiki/Ramakrishna" title="Ramakrishna">
       Ramakrishna
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vivekananda" title="Vivekananda">
       Vivekananda
      </a>
      , the poetry of
      <a href="/wiki/Rabindranath_Tagore" title="Rabindranath Tagore">
       Rabindranath Tagore
      </a>
      , and the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Bhagavad_Gita" title="Bhagavad Gita">
        Bhagavad Gita
       </a>
      </i>
      . The Roerichs' commitment to occult mysticism increased steadily. It was especially intense during
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">
       World War I
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Russian_Revolution" title="Russian Revolution">
       Russian revolutions of 1917
      </a>
      , to which the couple, like many Russian intellectuals, accorded apocalyptic significance. The influence of
      <a href="/wiki/Theosophy" title="Theosophy">
       Theosophy
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Vedanta" title="Vedanta">
       Vedanta
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">
       Buddhism
      </a>
      , and other mystical topics can be detected not only in many of his paintings, but in the many short stories and poems Roerich wrote before and after the 1917 revolutions, including the
      <i>
       Flowers of
       <a href="/wiki/Morya_(Theosophy)" title="Morya (Theosophy)">
        Morya
       </a>
      </i>
      cycle, begun during 1907 and completed 1921.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Revolution.2C_emigration.2C_and_the_United_States">
       Revolution, emigration, and the United States
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Roerich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Revolution, emigration, and the United States">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:M_Sherling_Portrait_Roerich.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1380" data-file-width="930" height="297" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/M_Sherling_Portrait_Roerich.jpg/200px-M_Sherling_Portrait_Roerich.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/M_Sherling_Portrait_Roerich.jpg/300px-M_Sherling_Portrait_Roerich.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/M_Sherling_Portrait_Roerich.jpg/400px-M_Sherling_Portrait_Roerich.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:M_Sherling_Portrait_Roerich.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Nicholas Roerich. 1916.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      After the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/February_Revolution_of_1917" title="February Revolution of 1917">
       February Revolution of 1917
      </a>
      and the end of the czarist regime, Roerich, a political moderate who valued Russia's cultural heritage more than ideology and party politics, had an active part in artistic politics. With
      <a href="/wiki/Maxim_Gorky" title="Maxim Gorky">
       Maxim Gorky
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Benois" title="Aleksandr Benois">
       Aleksandr Benois
      </a>
      , he participated with the so-called "Gorky Commission" and its successor organization, the Arts Union (SDI). Both attempted to gain the attention of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provisional_Government" title="Provisional Government">
       Provisional Government
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Petrograd_Soviet" title="Petrograd Soviet">
       Petrograd Soviet
      </a>
      on the need to form a coherent cultural policy and, most urgently, protect art and architecture from destruction and vandalism. At the same time, however, illness forced Roerich to leave the capital and reside in Karelia, the district bordering Finland. He had already quit the presidency of the World of Art society, and he now quit the directorship of the School of the Imperial Society for the Encouragement of the Arts. After the October Revolution and the acquisition of power of Lenin's
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolshevik_Party" title="Bolshevik Party">
       Bolshevik Party
      </a>
      , Roerich became increasingly discouraged about Russia's political future. During early 1918, he, Helena, and their two sons George and Sviatoslav emigrated to Finland.
     </p>
     <p>
      Two unresolved historical debates are associated with Roerich's departure. First, it is often claimed that Roerich was a major candidate to direct a people's commissariat of culture (the Soviet equivalent of a ministry of culture) which the Bolsheviks considered establishing during 19171918, but that he refused to accept the job. In fact, Benois was the most likely choice to direct any such commissariat. It seems that Roerich was a preferred choice to manage its department of artistic education; the topic is rendered moot by the fact that the Soviets elected not to establish such a commissariat. Second, when he wished to reconcile with the USSR, Roerich maintained later that he had not left Soviet Russia deliberately, but that he and his family, living in
      <a href="/wiki/Karelia" title="Karelia">
       Karelia
      </a>
      , had been isolated from their homeland when
      <a href="/wiki/Finnish_Civil_War" title="Finnish Civil War">
       civil war
      </a>
      began in
      <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">
       Finland
      </a>
      . However, Roerich's extreme hostility to the Bolshevik regime  prompted not so much by a dislike of communism as by his revulsion at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lenin" title="Lenin">
       Lenin
      </a>
      's ruthlessness and his fear that Bolshevism would result in the destruction of Russia's artistic and architectural heritage  was amply documented. He illustrated
      <a href="/wiki/Leonid_Andreyev" title="Leonid Andreyev">
       Leonid Andreyev
      </a>
      's anti-communist polemic "S.O.S." and had a widely published pamphlet, "Violators of Art" (19181919). Roerich believed that "the triumph of Russian culture would come about through a new appreciation of ancient myth and legend".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After some months in Finland and Scandinavia, the Roerichs relocated to London, arriving during mid-1919. Engrossed with Theosophical mysticism, the Roerichs now had millenarian expectations that a new age was imminent, and they wished to travel to India as soon as possible. They joined the English-Welsh chapter of the Theosophical Society. It was in London, during March 1920, that the Roerichs initiated their own school of occultism,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Agni_Yoga" title="Agni Yoga">
        Agni Yoga
       </a>
      </i>
      , which they referred to also as "the system of living ethics." To earn passage to India, Roerich worked as a stage designer for
      <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Beecham" title="Thomas Beecham">
       Thomas Beecham
      </a>
      's
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Covent_Garden_Theatre" title="Covent Garden Theatre">
       Covent Garden Theatre
      </a>
      , but the enterprise ended unsuccessfully during 1920, and the artist never received full payment for his work. Among the notable people Roerich befriended while in England were the famed British Buddhist
      <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Humphreys" title="Christmas Humphreys">
       Christmas Humphreys
      </a>
      , philosopher-author
      <a href="/wiki/H._G._Wells" title="H. G. Wells">
       H. G. Wells
      </a>
      , and the poet and Nobel laureate
      <a href="/wiki/Rabindranath_Tagore" title="Rabindranath Tagore">
       Rabindranath Tagore
      </a>
      (whose grand-niece
      <a href="/wiki/Devika_Rani" title="Devika Rani">
       Devika Rani
      </a>
      would later marry Roerich's son Sviatoslav).
     </p>
     <p>
      Later, a successful exhibition resulted in an invitation from a director at the
      <a href="/wiki/Art_Institute_of_Chicago" title="Art Institute of Chicago">
       Art Institute of Chicago
      </a>
      , offering to arrange for Roerich's art to tour the United States. During the autumn of 1920, the Roerichs traveled to America by sea.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NicholasRoerichcar.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/NicholasRoerichcar.jpg/220px-NicholasRoerichcar.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/NicholasRoerichcar.jpg/330px-NicholasRoerichcar.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/NicholasRoerichcar.jpg/440px-NicholasRoerichcar.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NicholasRoerichcar.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Car of Nicolas Roerich in his museum at Naggar
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The Roerichs remained in the United States from October 1920 until May 1923. A large exhibition of Roerich's art, organized partly by U.S. impresario Christian Brinton and partly by the Chicago Art Institute, began in New York during December 1920 and toured the country, to San Francisco and back, during 1921 and early 1922. Roerich befriended acclaimed soprano
      <a href="/wiki/Mary_Garden" title="Mary Garden">
       Mary Garden
      </a>
      of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago_Opera" title="Chicago Opera">
       Chicago Opera
      </a>
      and received a commission to design a 1922 production of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rimsky-Korsakov" title="Rimsky-Korsakov">
       Rimsky-Korsakov
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Snow_Maiden" title="The Snow Maiden">
        The Snow Maiden
       </a>
      </i>
      for her. During the exhibition, the Roerichs spent significant amounts of time in Chicago, New Mexico, and California.
     </p>
     <p>
      They settled in New York City, which became the base of their many American operations. The Roerichs initiated several institutions during these years:
      <i>
       Cor Ardens
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Corona Mundi
      </i>
      , both of which were meant to unite artists around the globe in the cause of civic activism; the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Master_Institute_of_United_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Master Institute of United Arts (page does not exist)">
       Master Institute of United Arts
      </a>
      , an art school with an exceptionally versatile curriculum, and the eventual home of the first
      <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Roerich_Museum" title="Nicholas Roerich Museum">
       Nicholas Roerich Museum
      </a>
      ; and an American Agni Yoga Society. They also joined various theosophical societies, and their activities with these groups dominated their lives.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Asian_Expedition_.281925.E2.80.931929.29">
       Asian Expedition (19251929)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Roerich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Asian Expedition (19251929)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Family_Roerich.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="300" height="307" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Family_Roerich.JPG/230px-Family_Roerich.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d2/Family_Roerich.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d2/Family_Roerich.JPG 2x" width="230"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Family_Roerich.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <center>
         Roerich's family. (Kullu valley, India).
        </center>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      After leaving New York, the Roerichs  together with their son
      <a href="/wiki/George_de_Roerich" title="George de Roerich">
       George
      </a>
      and six friends  began the five-year-long 'Roerich Asian Expedition' that, in Roerich's own words:
      <i>
       "started from Sikkim through Punjab, Kashmir,
       <a href="/wiki/Ladakh" title="Ladakh">
        Ladakh
       </a>
       , the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karakoram_Mountains" title="Karakoram Mountains">
        Karakoram Mountains
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khotan" title="Khotan">
        Khotan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Kashgar" title="Kashgar">
        Kashgar
       </a>
       , Qara Shar,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urumchi" title="Urumchi">
        Urumchi
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irtysh" title="Irtysh">
        Irtysh
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Altai_Mountains" title="Altai Mountains">
        Altai Mountains
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Gorno-Altai_Autonomous_Oblast" title="Gorno-Altai Autonomous Oblast">
        Oryot
       </a>
       region of Mongolia, the Central
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gobi" title="Gobi">
        Gobi
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kansu" title="Kansu">
        Kansu
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tsaidam" title="Tsaidam">
        Tsaidam
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">
        Tibet
       </a>
       "
      </i>
      with a detour through Siberia to Moscow during 1926. Roerichs' Asian expedition attracted attention from the foreign services and intelligence agencies of the USSR, the United States, Great Britain, and Japan. In fact, prior to this expedition, Roerich himself solicited help of Soviet government and Bolshevik secret police to assist him in his expedition, promising in return to monitor British activities in the area, but received only a lukewarm response from Meer Trilisser, the chief of the Soviet foreign intelligence at that time. On the one hand, the Bolsheviks assisted him with logistics when Roerich was traveling through Siberia and Mongolia. Yet, on the other hand, they refused to totally commit themselves to his reckless utopian project of the Sacred Union of the East  a spiritual utopia that boiled down to Roerich ambitious attempts to stir the Buddhist masses of inner Asia to create a highly spiritual cooperative commonwealth under the patronage of Bolshevik Russia. The official mission of this expedition, as Roerich put it, was to act as the embassy of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Buddhism" title="Western Buddhism">
       Western Buddhism
      </a>
      to Tibet. However, for Western media his expedition was presented as an artistic and scientific enterprise;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Between the summer of 1927 and June 1928 the expedition was thought to be lost, since communication with them ceased for a year. They had been attacked in Tibet and only the
      <i>
       "superiority of our firearms prevented bloodshed... In spite of our having Tibet passports, the expedition was forcibly stopped by Tibetan authorities."
      </i>
      The expedition was detained by the government for five months, and forced to live in tents in sub-zero conditions and to subsist on meagre rations. Five men of the expedition died during this time. During March 1928 they were allowed to leave Tibet, and trekked south to settle in
      <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
       India
      </a>
      , where they initiated a research center, the Himalayan Research Institute.
     </p>
     <p>
      During 1929 Nicholas Roerich was nominated for the
      <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">
       Nobel Peace Prize
      </a>
      by the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Paris" title="University of Paris">
       University of Paris
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He received two more nominations during 1932 and 1935.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His concern for peace resulted in his creation of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pax_Cultura" title="Pax Cultura">
       Pax Cultura
      </a>
      , the "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">
       Red Cross
      </a>
      " of art and culture. His work for this cause also resulted in the United States and the twenty other nations of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan-American_Union" title="Pan-American Union">
       Pan-American Union
      </a>
      signing the
      <a href="/wiki/Roerich_Pact" title="Roerich Pact">
       Roerich Pact
      </a>
      on April 15, 1935 at the White House. The Roerich Pact is an early international instrument protecting cultural property.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Manchurian_expedition">
       Manchurian expedition
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicholas_Roerich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Manchurian expedition">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      During 19341935, the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Agriculture" title="United States Department of Agriculture">
       U.S. Department of Agriculture
      </a>
      (then headed by Roerich admirer
      <a href="/wiki/Henry_A._Wallace" title="Henry A. Wallace">
       Henry A. Wallace
      </a>
      ) sponsored an expedition by Roerich and USDA scientists H. G. MacMillan and James F. Stephens to
      <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">
       Inner Mongolia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">
       Manchuria
      </a>
      , and China. The expedition's purpose was to collect seeds of plants which prevent the destruction of benign layers of soil.
     </p>
     <p>
      The expedition consisted of two parts. During 1934, they explored the
      <a href="/wiki/Greater_Khingan" title="Greater Khingan">
       Greater Khingan
      </a>
      mountains and Bargan plateau in western Manchuria. During 1935, they explored parts of Inner Mongolia: the
      <a href="/wiki/Gobi_Desert" title="Gobi Desert">
       Gobi Desert
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ordos" title="Ordos">
       Ordos
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Helan_Mountains" title="Helan Mountains">
       Helan Mountains
      </a>
      . The expedition found almost 300 species of
      <a href="/wiki/Xerophyte" title="Xerophyte">
       xerophytes
      </a>
      , collected herbs, conducted archeological studies, and found antique manuscripts of great scientific importance.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_and_World_War_II">
       Later life and World War II
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <p>
      Roerich was in India during the Second World War, where he painted Russian epic heroic and saintly themes, including:
      <i>
       Alexander Nevsky
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       The Fight of Mstislav and Rededia
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Boris and Gleb
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leek2005_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Leek2005-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      During 1942, Roerich received
      <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru" title="Jawaharlal Nehru">
       Jawaharlal Nehru
      </a>
      at his house in Kullu and Nehru's daughter,
      <a href="/wiki/Indira_Gandhi" title="Indira Gandhi">
       Indira Gandhi
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Together they discussed the fate of the new world:
      <i>
       "We spoke about Indian-Russian cultural association
      </i>
      ,  Roerich wrote, 
      <i>
       it is time to think about useful and creative cooperation ...
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Gandhi would later recall about several days spent together with Roerich's family:
      <i>
       "That was a memorable visit to a surprising and gifted family where each member was a remarkable figure in himself, with a well-defined range of interests."
      </i>
      ...
      <i>
       "Roerich himself stays in my memory. He was a man with extensive knowledge and enormous experience, a man with a big heart, deeply influenced by all that he observed"
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      During the visit,
      <i>
       "ideas and thoughts about closer cooperation between India and USSR were expressed. Now, after India wins independence, they have got its own real implementation
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">

           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       . And as you know, there are friendly and mutually-understanding relationships today between both our countries
      </i>
      ".

     <p>
      Vice President of the United States
      <a href="/wiki/Henry_A._Wallace" title="Henry A. Wallace">
       Henry A. Wallace
      </a>
      was a frequent correspondent and sometime advocate of Nicholas Roerich's teachings. Wallace became attracted to the idea of Sacred Union of the East, a spiritual and geopolitical utopia Nicholas and Helena Roerich contemplated to establish in the heart of Asia. Based on spiritual ideas, which the Roerichs claimed they received from otherworld masters, this utopia was to show the humankind a blueprint of ideal society. As the US Secretary of Agriculture, Wallace became so much interested in the whole project that he decided to sponsor the second Roerich expedition to Asia in 19331934. In the meantime, Helena Roerich was corresponding with US President Franklin D. Roosevelt, who was intrigued by her "fiery letters." The whole project ended in a disaster and resulted in energetic efforts by Wallace and FDR to cut their ties with the Roerichs. The whole incident later partially resurfaced and became controversial when Wallace campaigned for President during 1948 and portions of the correspondence were printed by columnist Westbrook Pegler, becoming known as the Guru Letters,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

       </a>

     <p>
      Presently, the
      <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Roerich_Museum" title="Nicholas Roerich Museum">
       Nicholas Roerich Museum
      </a>
      in New York City is a major institution for Roerich's artistic work. Numerous Roerich societies continue to promote his theosophical teachings worldwide. His paintings can be seen in several museums including the Roerich Department of the State Museum of Oriental Arts in Moscow; the Roerich Museum at the International Centre of the Roerichs in Moscow; the Russian State Museum in Saint Petersburg, Russia; a collection in the
      <a href="/wiki/Tretyakov_Gallery" title="Tretyakov Gallery">
       Tretyakov Gallery
      </a>
      in Moscow; a collection in the Art Museum in
      <a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk" title="Novosibirsk">
       Novosibirsk
      </a>
      , Russia; an important collection in the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Gallery_for_Foreign_Art" title="National Gallery for Foreign Art">
       National Gallery for Foreign Art
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">
       Sofia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">
       Bulgaria
      </a>
      ; a collection in the Art Museum in
      <a href="/wiki/Nizhny_Novgorod" title="Nizhny Novgorod">
       Nizhny Novgorod
      </a>
      Russia;
      <a href="/wiki/National_Museum_of_Serbia" title="National Museum of Serbia">
       National Museum of Serbia
      </a>
      ; the Roerich Hall Estate in Nagar village in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kullu_Valley" title="Kullu Valley">
       Kullu Valley
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
       India
      </a>
      ; the
      <a href="/wiki/Sree_Chitra_Art_Gallery" title="Sree Chitra Art Gallery">
       Sree Chitra Art Gallery
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Thiruvananthapuram" title="Thiruvananthapuram">
       Thiruvananthapuram

        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in various art museums in India; and a selection featuring several of his larger works in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Latvian_National_Museum_of_Art" title="The Latvian National Museum of Art">
       The Latvian National Museum of Art
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Roerich's biography and his controversial expeditions to Tibet and Manchuria have been examined recently by a number of authors, including two Russians, Vladimir Rosov and Alexandre Andreyev, American (Andrei Znamenski), and the German Ernst von Waldenfels.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H.P._Lovecraft" title="H.P. Lovecraft">
       H.P. Lovecraft
      </a>
      referred to the "strange and disturbing paintings of Nicholas Roerich" in his Antarctic horror story
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/At_the_Mountains_of_Madness" title="At the Mountains of Madness">
        At the Mountains of Madness
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Minor_planet" title="Minor planet">
       minor planet
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4426_Roerich" title="4426 Roerich">
       4426 Roerich
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">
       Solar System
      </a>
      was named in honor of Roerich.
     </p>
     <p>
      During June 2013 during
      <a href="/wiki/Russian_Art_Week" title="Russian Art Week">
       Russian Art Week
      </a>
      in London, Roerich's
      <i>
       Madonna Laboris
      </i>
      sold at auction at
      <a href="/wiki/Bonhams" title="Bonhams">
       Bonhams
      </a>
      shop for 7,881,250 inc. buyer's premium, making it the most valuable painting ever sold at a Russian art auction.
