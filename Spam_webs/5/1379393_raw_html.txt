     <p>
      <b>
       The Disinformation Company
      </b>
      (abbreviated as
      <b>
       Disinfo
      </b>
      ) is a
      <a href="/wiki/Privately_held_company" title="Privately held company">
       privately held
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Private_company_limited_by_shares" title="Private company limited by shares">
       limited
      </a>
      American
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Publishing_company" title="Publishing company">
       publishing company
      </a>
      that focuses on current affairs titles and seeks to expose alleged
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conspiracy_theories" title="Conspiracy theories">
       conspiracy theories
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occultism" title="Occultism">
       occultism
      </a>
      , politics, news oddities and purported
      <a href="/wiki/Disinformation" title="Disinformation">
       disinformation
      </a>
      . It is headquartered in
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York City
      </a>
      , New York. Arguably, its most visible publications to date are
      <i>
       50 Things You're Not Supposed to Know
      </i>
      and the
      <i>
       Everything You Know About (subject) is Wrong
      </i>
      series, both by the company's
      <a href="/wiki/Editor-at-large" title="Editor-at-large">
       editor-at-large
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Russ_Kick" title="Russ Kick">
       Russ Kick
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McNICHOL2003_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-McNICHOL2003-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Disinfo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1996,
      <a href="/wiki/Tele-Communications_Inc." title="Tele-Communications Inc.">
       Tele-Communications Inc.
      </a>
      (now
      <a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">
       Comcast
      </a>
      ) funded a
      <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">
       Hollywood
      </a>
      -based internet initiative responsible for online projects like the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Getty_Museum" title="Getty Museum">
       Getty Museum
      </a>
      and an internet-based political humor soap opera entitled
      <i>
       Candidate 96
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2008)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      The initiative launched its own interactive website, featuring the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tag_line" title="Tag line">
       tag line
      </a>
      ; "everything you know is wrong". Soon after the site's launch, TCI cancelled funding and support for the site
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2008)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The founding team kept it going, winning an award for politics in the first
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Webby_Awards" title="Webby Awards">
       Webby Awards
      </a>
      ceremony.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2008)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      The Disinformation Company, as it was now known, was then acquired by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avenue_A/Razorfish" title="Avenue A/Razorfish">
       Razorfish
      </a>
      . Eventually, the Disinformation Company became privately held.
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition to publishing books, the company also has a home video division as well as multimedia/internet projects.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2000, Disinfo organized
      <b>
       DisinfoCon
      </b>
      , a 12 hour event featuring
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_Metzger" title="Richard Metzger">
       Richard Metzger
      </a>
      , rock musician
      <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Manson" title="Marilyn Manson">
       Marilyn Manson
      </a>
      , underground filmmaker
      <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Anger" title="Kenneth Anger">
       Kenneth Anger
      </a>
      , painter
      <a href="/wiki/Joe_Coleman_(painter)" title="Joe Coleman (painter)">
       Joe Coleman
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Rushkoff" title="Douglas Rushkoff">
       Douglas Rushkoff
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Pesce" title="Mark Pesce">
       Mark Pesce
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Grant_Morrison" title="Grant Morrison">
       Grant Morrison
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Robert_Anton_Wilson" title="Robert Anton Wilson">
       Robert Anton Wilson
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.fargonebooks.com/bio.html" rel="nofollow">
       Todd Brendan Fahey
      </a>
      and others.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2002, the company produced a four-episode documentary series called
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Disinformation_(TV_series)" title="Disinformation (TV series)">
        Disinformation
       </a>
      </i>
      (also alternatively titled as
      <i>
       Disinfo Nation
      </i>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2008, the Disinformation Company itself was accused of spreading propaganda (such as 9/11 "Truth" material) by Louis Proyect.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
