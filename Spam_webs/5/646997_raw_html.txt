        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Grilled Salmon w/ Citrus Salsa Verde
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <a class="time" href="http://rickbakas.com/grilled-salmon-w-citrus-salsa-verde">
          <time class="date published updated" datetime="2011-07-15">
           July 15, 2011
          </time>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-3389 aligncenter" height="614" src="http://rickbakas.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/salmoncitrus.jpg" title="salmoncitrus" width="461"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           This recipe is modified from a Giada De Laurentis recipe
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           INGREDIENTS
          </strong>
          <br/>
          1 Salmon filet, I prefer Norwegian
          <br/>
          2 scallions, sliced thin
          <br/>
          juice &amp; zest from two oranges
          <br/>
          juice &amp; zest from one lemon
          <br/>
          1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
          <br/>
          2 TBSP capers
          <br/>
          1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
          <br/>
          1 TSP white wine vinegar
          <br/>
          3 TBSP fresh mint leaves, chopped
          <br/>
          1 TSP red pepper flakes
         </p>
         <p>
          To make the salsa, first zest and juice the oranges and lemon in a small bowl.  Add olive oil, cilantro, scallions, mint, pepper flakes, capers, salt and pepper (to taste).  Set aside.
         </p>
         <p>
          For salmon, brush flesh side of salmon with olive oil, then salt and pepper.  Giada suggests also brushing with agave nectar but thats up to you (itll caramelize a little, which is nice).  Turn on grill to medium-high heat.
          <a href="http://rickbakas.com/grilling-salmon-what-side-first" target="_blank">
           In another blog post about grilling salmon
          </a>
          I suggest putting the salmon on the grill skin side up while the grill is heating up. This prevents the fish from overcooking while giving a nice char texture. Grill the salmon for about 5 minutes. Flip the fish over skin side down and cook the rest of the way until the salmon has a flaky texture.
         </p>
         <p>
          Serve with salsa on the side or spooned over. Pat yourself on the back for eating healthy and actually enjoying it.
         </p>
