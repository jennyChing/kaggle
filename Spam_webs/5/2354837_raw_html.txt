            <p>
             Twitter has now entered the live-streaming market with its new app Periscope which is now available to download from the App Store.
            </p>
            <p>
             Periscope allows you to wither broadcast live from your phone and also record the event for replays so viewers can watch later. Users can add comments while the event is live and can also share the event via Twitter itself. Private events can also be set up where specific users can only be invited. Notifications for specific people you follow can also be set up so you can keep up to date on your favourite accounts.
            </p>
            <p>
             The Most Loved feature shows the most popular users on Periscope. Like Twitters Favourite and Facebooks Like buttons respectively, you can send hearts to the accounts you may like to make them more popular. Even now there are accounts with nearly 5 million hearts for their content.
            </p>
            <p>
             The launch of Periscope comes after Twitter acquired the firm for an undisclosed sum, but believed to be around 100 million, earlier this year.
            </p>
            <p>
             Periscopes direct competition is Meerkat, a similar app that allows live streaming. However Meerkat was built and launched within just two weeks. Since its launch Meerkat has gained a considerable user base, but it is likely that the founders will have to keep on their toes as Twitter looks to gain a share in the live-streaming market.
            </p>
            <p>
             Both Meerkat and Periscope are pre-dated by other attempts at live-streaming apps, all of which fell by the wayside. But now, with more open Wi-Fi networks and faster 4G mobile networks than ever before, live streaming looks set to become as regular a part of our lives as Googling and Snapchatting
            </p>
