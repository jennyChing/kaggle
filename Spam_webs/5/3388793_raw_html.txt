         <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden clearfix">
          <div class="field-items">
           <div class="field-item even" property="schema:articleBody content:encoded">
            <p>
             Bernie Wells was steamrolling over the New York Giants, talking trash and backing it up with three touchdowns. However when push came to shove, the Giants pushed back harder and pulled out a thrilling 31-27 victory over the
             <a class="inline_link" href="/topic/arizona-cardinals">
              Arizona Cardinals
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Wells had 2 touchdowns in the third quarter, putting Arizona ahead 20-10 going into the deciding frame. The Giants cut the lead to 20-17 on a Brandon Jacobs run early in the fourth, but Wells and Arizona answered, stretching their lead to 27-17. The Giants seemed listless and defeated, but found momentum behind
             <a class="inline_link" href="/topic/eli-manning">
              Eli Manning
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Manning connected with tight end Jake Ballard to cut the score to 27-24 with 3:37 remaining. Under a minute later the Eli and the Giants were finding paydirt again, this time on a 29-yard score from Manning to Hakeem Nicks. Right when it seemed that the Giants were done, they fought back and Kevin Kolb seemed to fold under the pressure. A good portion of that pressure came from the
             <a href="http://www.examiner.com/new-york-giants-in-new-york/tuck-out-umenyiora-as-giants-take-on-cardinals">
              returning Osi Umenyiora
             </a>
             , who seemed to not miss a beat in gathering two sacks.
            </p>
            <p>
             The Giants, beaten and battered entering the season, find themselves tied on top of the NFC East with the Washington Redskins. The
             <a href="http://www.examiner.com/new-york-giants-in-new-york/giants-take-out-vick-eagles-29-16-win">
              Philadelphia Eagles
             </a>
             have fallen into "Miami Heat" territory, as their big offseason moves haven't paid off into victories and are at the bottom of the heap at 1-3.
            </p>
            <p>
             The Giants head home to take on the 1-3 Seattle Seahawks next Sunday at 1pm. Seattle is coming off a close 30-28 loss to the Atlanta Falcons.
            </p>
            <p>
             <u>
              Stats and Notes:
             </u>
            </p>
            <p>
             - Manning was 27/40 for 321 yards and two touchdowns.
            </p>
            <p>
             - Hakeem Nicks caught 10 of those passes for 162 yards and the winning touchdown.
            </p>
            <p>
             - Dave Tollefson matched Osi Umenyiora with 2 sacks, his first of the season.
            </p>
            <p>
             - Mario Manningham, returning from injury, had 1 reception for 10 yards.
            </p>
            <p>
             - Arizona's Bernie Wells had 27 carries for 138 yards and 3 touchdowns.
            </p>
            <p>
             - The Giants destroyed Seattle last season in their only meeting, winning 41-7
            </p>
