     <h2 class="title">
      <a href="http://dinnerwithjulie.com/2014/10/13/sweet-potato-lentil-soup-apple/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: Sweet Potato &amp; Lentil Soup with Apples">
       Sweet Potato &amp; Lentil Soup with Apples
      </a>
     </h2>
     <div class="pf-content">
      <p>
       <img alt="IMG_0017" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-17805" height="780" src="http://julievr.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0017-585x780.jpg" width="585"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       I take a lot of pictures on my phone. Right now there are approximately 4,375 on there (no exaggeration) and yet theyre not quite good enough to post here. And so I finally knocked one thing off my to-do list that has been hanging out there for at least a year (I have plenty of years-old to-do items, sadly) and got up to speed with my phone, which means I can finally (maybe) try to catch up on
       <a href="http://instagram.com/dinnerwithjulie" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://instagram.com/dinnerwithjulie', 'Instagram']);">
        Instagram
       </a>
       and not have to lug my regular camera around and then have to connect it to my computer to upload images.. so archaic! Look at me all cutting edge!
      </p>
      <p>
       So this post serves as a test, to see if the new toy is up to snuff. I did also want to share this soup.
      </p>
      <p>
       We had three turkey dinners this weekend  a tryptophan triple header. The first was at our house, with friends who have no family in town, which involved me making the stuffing in an electric skillet and then running across the street to put the turkey in my sisters oven, and us and our friends eating dinner on the couch because our dining room is filled with a fridge and stuff. The second was out at Mikes cousins house, where they covered their bird with bacon and barbecued it in an aluminum roasting pan. The third was tonight at my moms house, and I was charged with soup  something I could do in the slow cooker on my dining room table  and so I tucked a couple sweet potatoes in the oven alongside the turkey on Friday night and then kept them in the fridge in their jackets until this morning, when I peeled and tossed them into the pot. It isnt much different from a carrot-lentil-sweet potato soup Ive been making for years  but this comes from the
       <a href="http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/13416/sweet-potato-and-lentil-soup" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/13416/sweet-potato-and-lentil-soup', 'BBC']);">
        BBC
       </a>
       . I do like their recipes.
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Sweet Potato &amp; Lentil Soup with Apple
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        2 Tbsp. canola or olive oil
        <br/>
        2 onions, chopped or grated
        <br/>
        3 garlic cloves, crushed
        <br/>
        1 Tbsp. grated ginger
        <br/>
        1 Tbsp. curry powder or paste
        <br/>
        1/4-1/2 cup chopped cilantro (stems too)
        <br/>
        2 medium dark-fleshed sweet potatoes, roasted and peeled or peeled and chopped
        <br/>
        1 L chicken or vegetable stock
        <br/>
        2 cups water
        <br/>
        1/2 cup dry red lentils
        <br/>
        1 tart apple, peeled, cored and grated
        <br/>
        1 cup half &amp; half or coconut milk
        <br/>
        juice of a lime
        <br/>
        salt
       </p>
       <p>
        If you have a stovetop, heat a drizzle of oil in a medium pot; add the onions and saute for 4-5 minutes, until soft. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for another minute. Add the curry powder or paste and cook for another minute; then the cilantro.
       </p>
       <p>
        Add the sweet potatoes, stock, water, lentils and apple; bring to a simmer and cook for 30 minutes, or until everything is very soft. (Alternatively, toss everything in the slow cooker and turn it on low for 8 hours.)
       </p>
       <p>
        Puree the soup in the pot using a hand-held immersion blender; add the half &amp; half, lime juice and salt, blend again and adjust seasonings. Serve hot.
       </p>
       <p>
        Serves 8.
       </p>
      </blockquote>
