       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Caramel Rolo Rice Krispie Treats
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2013-10-29T08:22:40+00:00">
         October 29, 2013
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://www.lifeloveandsugar.com/2013/10/29/caramel-rolo-rice-krispie-treats/#comments">
          33 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.lifeloveandsugar.com%2F2013%2F10%2F29%2Fcaramel-rolo-rice-krispie-treats%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.lifeloveandsugar.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2013%2F10%2FCaramel_Rolo_Rice_Krispie_Treats.jpg&amp;description=Caramel%20Rolo%20Rice%20Krispie%20Treats" rel="nobox">
          <img data-pib-button="true" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         These rice krispie treats arent just any rice krispie treats. No, these treats dont even use marshmallows. Instead the sugary gods of sweetness hold these babies together with an entire bag of caramels. And the caramel train doesnt stop there. Theyre also filled with an entire bag of Rolos.
         <strong>
          Caramel
         </strong>
         +
         <strong>
          Rolos
         </strong>
         +
         <strong>
          rice krispies
         </strong>
         =
         <strong>
          crunchy caramel heaven
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Can you tell Im good at math? 
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Caramel Rolo Rice Krispie Treats" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5046" height="874" src="http://cf.lifeloveandsugar.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Caramel_Rolo_Rice_Krispie_Treats.jpg" width="575"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Well growing up, I actually was pretty decent at math. At least until I hit Calculus. Then I got lost. I no longer do math.
        </p>
        <p>
         The other thing I got good at as a kid was hiding things. My favorite things. Dessert things.
        </p>
        <p>
         My younger brother and I would always hide our favorite foods from the other when we didnt want the other to be able to eat them all. Usually it was frozen treats of some sort and wed have to find some stealthy spot behind the peas where the other wouldnt venture to look. Then we got a second fridge in the garage and it opened up a whole new possibility of hiding places.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Caramel Rolo Rice Krispie Treats" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5070" height="761" src="http://cf.lifeloveandsugar.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Caramel_Rolo_Rice_Krispie_Treats21.jpg" width="575"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         And it wasnt just the freezer. My parents still live in the same house and its got a nice big pantry. The sides actually go back into the walls a bit so its like theres deep, dark caves on either side of the shelves. You know what those deep, dark caves are good for? Hiding stuff. Like food. Particularly the Thin Mint Girl Scout cookies you want all to yourself.
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh yea, that happened.
        </p>
        <p>
         And the hiding wasnt limited to myself and my brother.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Caramel Rolo Rice Krispie Treats" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5049" height="675" src="http://cf.lifeloveandsugar.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Caramel_Rolo_Rice_Krispie_Treats3.jpg" width="575"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         On any given day you could find stashes of candy that my parents had hidden (and still do hide) for themselves. Problem was my dads hiding spot became known. I knew just where to find that licorice candy he was keeping from me. And if I only took a couple pieces at a time
        </p>
        <p>
         Not that long ago, the hubs and I were visiting them (my parents) and theyd recently discovered a new favorite cookie. A chocolate covered marshmallow thing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Well my dad couldnt find them in the pantry and had been looking for a couple days when he finally asked my mom what happened to them. She went into the pantry and came right back out with them.
        </p>
        <p>
         After that, about 5 minutes of heckling and joking with her began as my dad accused her of hiding them from him. It was pretty fun to watch. And you know shes using those deep, dark caves to her advantage. 
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Caramel Rolo Rice Krispie Treats" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5048" height="547" src="http://cf.lifeloveandsugar.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Caramel_Rolo_Rice_Krispie_Treats4.jpg" width="575"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Well deep, dark caves is what you will need when you make these. They are so delicious and you will want to eat as many of them as you can, but you wont be the only one. If you arent careful someone might just eat them right out from under you!
        </p>
        <p>
         They were devoured at our small group and at home. They didnt last a full day after Id taken pictures of them and declared them fair game.
        </p>
        <p>
         Having the caramel replacing the marshmallows was a smart move on my part. And every caramel krispie treat bite is only made better by the chocolate covered caramel pockets formed by the Rolos. If youre a fan of caramel, chocolate and some crunch, these are a no brainer.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Caramel Rolo Rice Krispie Treats" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5071" height="780" src="http://cf.lifeloveandsugar.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Caramel_Rolo_Rice_Krispie_Treats51.jpg" width="575"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Plus, theyre made right on the stove just like a regular rice krispies. Which means no turning on the oven, my friend. It also means theyre quick to make. Just melt the caramel, butter and milk together and add in the rice krispies and Rolos. The hardest part is waiting for them to cool to cut into squares.
        </p>
        <p>
         And they would be a great way to use up some leftover Halloween candy. Any candy with caramel and/or chocolate would be tasty in these  milky ways, snickers, twix, M&amp;Ms  the possibilities are endless! And so tasty!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

             Caramel Rolo Rice Krispie Treats

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <span id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup milk
             <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="//stats.ziprecipes.net/piwik.php?idsite=1&amp;rec=1" style="width: 1px; height: 1px; border:0" width="1"/>
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 cup of butter
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             one 14 oz bag Kraft caramels
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             5-6 cups rice krispies
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             one 12 oz bag Rolos
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup butterscotch chips, optional
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips, optional
            </div>
           </span>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <span class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             1. Line a 9x13 pan with parchment paper.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             2. Cut all Rolos into halves.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             3. Melt caramels, butter and milk together over medium-low heat.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             4. When melted and smooth, remove from heat, then add rice krispies and halved Rolos and mix well. Don't over mix, as the Rolos will soften from the heat and you don't want them to lose too much of their shape.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             5. Press into pan. Don't press down too much or the rice krispies will be hard when cool.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             6. Allow to cool, lift out of the pan with the parchment paper.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             7. Melt butterscotch chips in a bowl in the microwave, using 30 second increments so that it doesn't burn .
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             8. Drizzle melted butterscotch across the top of the rice krispie treats.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             9. Repeat steps 7 and 8 with the semi-sweet chocolate chips.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-9" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             10. Allow to cool, then cut into squares.
            </div>
           </span>
