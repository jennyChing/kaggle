         <h1 class="entry-title">
          The Can Cannon is becoming a reality (VIDEO)
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <a href="http://www.guns.com/2014/09/24/the-can-cannon-is-becoming-a-reality-video/" rel="bookmark" title="4:07 pm">

           </span>

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="371" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/HihYF9JDoJo" width="660">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          X Products, a prominent name in drum magazines, has been teasing us with its Can Cannon, an AR-15 upper that lets users launch unopened 12-ounce canned drinks 100 yards or more when used with 5.56/.223 blanks for a while now but theyve finally set up a pre-order page.
         </p>
         <p>
          Can Cannon is not taking pre-orders just yet but its one step closer to making this must-have upper a reality. No one wants this to be vaporware.
         </p>
         <p>
          Part of the delay has been due to the wait for Federal approval. After all, this is a very big bore device, but its no firearm even if it uses AR-15 lowers.
         </p>
         <p>
          Why would you launch a soda can, because its fun, plus its an incredibly fast and fun decoy to shoot at, explains X Products. Every demonstration leads to more smiles and laughs than any product weve ever introduced. Expect upon BATFE approval (due in 3 weeks) a variety of parts and accessories, from net launchers, to decoy dog training devices.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you feel the need for a Can Cannon in your bones
          <a href="http://www.xproducts.com/ar-15-soda-can-launcher-accessories-launcher" target="_blank">
           head over to the pre-pre-order page
          </a>
          and hit refresh every so often. Its coming.
         </p>
