           <h1 class="h1">
            <span class="headline">
             We Owe Jaden And Willow Smith A Major Apology
            </span>
           </h1>
           <div class="subhead">
            The Smith siblings are truly otherworldly and these movie cameos prove it.
           </div>
          </div>
          <section class="entry-content clearfix">
           <div class="hr">
           </div>
           <span class="byline">
            by
            <span class="author">
             <a href="http://www.mtv.com/news/author/Macleana/" rel="author" title="Posts by Andrew MacLean">
              Andrew MacLean
             </a>
            </span>
            <span class="date">
             11/26/2014
            </span>
           </span>
           <div class="sm4ShareWidget sm4Widget" data-contenturi="id::mgid:wordpress:post:articles.mtv.com:2007866::og::http://www.mtv.com/news/2007866/willow-jaden-smith-time-space-interstellar-future/?date_last_modified=1417019982" data-widget="postShare">
           </div>
           <p>
            <em>
             Images by Kurt Woerpel
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            People of the world, weve made a terrible mistake. Last week in an interview with the
            <a href="http://tmagazine.blogs.nytimes.com/2014/11/17/jaden-and-willow-smith-exclusive-joint-interview/" target="_blank">
             <em>
              New York Times T Magazine,
             </em>
            </a>
            Willow and Jaden Smith were kind enough to sit down and discuss their otherworldly views of, well, the world.
           </p>
           <p>
            And after graciously giving up their time (more on that in a second) and energy, the Internet sat back and scoffed at them. After some investigative journalism, though, MTV News realized how foolish and naive anyone was to ever doubt the sincerity of their words.
           </p>
           <p>
            It turns out that not only are the Smiths correct in understanding the relationship between space and time, but theyve also used their superior intelligence to camouflage themselves into the worlds of our favorite films just to prove their point.
           </p>
           <p>
            We uncovered a few notable examples, but were sure there are more. Be sure to tweet
            <a href="https://twitter.com/mtvnews" target="_blank">
             @MTVNEWS
            </a>
            if you discover any other Willow or Jaden cameos, using the hashtag #findthesmiths.
           </p>
           <p>
            <big>

           <p>
            What better place to begin our investigation than The Godfather of time-travel movies? We found this easter egg of Willow and Jaden at the Twin Pines Mall (or The Lone Pine Mall as its called now) and now our minds are racing with questions.
           </p>
           <p>
            Was Earth Angel playing When Will Smith met Jada Pinkett? Has Jadens hand ever begun to disappear for no reason? Were Jaden and Willow responsible for their parents meeting and thus the masters of their own creation? These are the questions that only Jaden and Willow can answer.
           </p>
           <p>
            <big>
             <strong>
              2. Bill &amp; Teds Excellent Adventure

           <p>
            Strange things are afoot when two of the most excellent dudes in history are joined by the Smiths. We have witnessed many strange things at MTV but nothing as bodacious as seeing Willow and Jaden join Bill and Ted on an educational voyage through history.
           </p>
           <p>
            We should have seen this easter egg coming as the Smiths have been outspoken about their own education with Jaden saying, Heres the deal; School is not authentic because it ends. Its not true, its not real. Our learning will never end. The school that we go to every single morning, we will continue to go to. With Willow adding, Forever, til the day that were in our bed.
           </p>
           <p>
            <big>
             <strong>
              3. Looper
             </strong>
            </big>
            <br/>
           </p>

           <p>
            At one point during the interview with
            <em>
             T Magazine,
            </em>
            Willow refers to being inspired by her
            <em>
             own
            </em>
            work.
           </p>
           <p>
            Thats what I do with novels. Therere no novels that I like to read so I write my own novels, and then I read them again, and its the best thing, she said, with Jaden adding, Willows been writing her own novels since she was 6.
           </p>
           <p>
            This had us scratching our heads. Not because we dont believe that Willow would have the ability to write novels at the age of six but because this type of looping inspiration seems to indicate a higher echelon of understanding. And like a lightbulb going off it hit us: Willow and Jaden are Loopers.
           </p>
           <p>
            Theyve lived their entire lives only to then re-inspire themselves with their own work.
           </p>

             <strong>
              4. Donnie Darko

           </p>
           <p>
            Yes, Willow and Jadens fingerprints are all over the shocking time-travel saga Donnie Darko. And while it may be difficult to imagine traveling through a wormhole just to shock audiences, we should have learned by now to never underestimate Willow and Jaden.
           </p>
           <p>
            The only way to change something is to shock it. If you want your muscles to grow, you have to shock them. If you want society to change, you have to shock them. said Jaden, whose time-traveling accomplice added, Thats what art is, shocking people. Sometimes shocking yourself.

             holodeck

function postClickCallback (evt, target)
{
	//it's a category click
	if ($(target).closest(".category").length &gt; 0)
	{
		return "NewsFeed:"+$(target).text();
	}
	else
	{
		//finding the main post container.
		var $post  = $(target).closest("article").parent();
		var $media = $post.find(".item");

		var position;
		//we calculate the relative position of the post, within its group.
		var relative_position = parseInt($post.attr("class").match(/post-\d+/)[0].replace('post-', ''), 10);

		//if it was loaded dynamically by ajax, we calculate how many post were before and add the relative position.
		var ajax_classes = $post.parent().attr("class").match(/mtvn-ajax-placeholder-\d+/);
		if (ajax_classes !== null)
		{
			var post_per_page = $post.parent().parent().children(".post").length;
			var ajax_count = parseInt(ajax_classes[0].replace('mtvn-ajax-placeholder-', ''), 10);
			position = (ajax_count - 1) * post_per_page + relative_position;
		}
		else
		{
			position = relative_position;
		}

		//selectiong the size
		var size = getSize($media);

		if ($("body").hasClass("home"))
		{
			section = "NewsFeed:";
		}
		else
		{
			section = "CategoryFeed:";
		}

		return section+position+":"+size;
	}
}

function getSize($media)
{
	var size;

	if ($media.hasClass("item-md"))
	{
		size = "medium";
	}
	else if ($media.hasClass("item-sm"))
	{
		size = "small";
	}
	else if ($media.hasClass("item-lg"))
	{
		size = "large";
	}
	else if ($media.hasClass("postcard-left") || $media.hasClass("postcard-right"))
	{
		size = "small";
	}

	return size;
}
