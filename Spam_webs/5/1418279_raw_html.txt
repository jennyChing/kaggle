       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         3 Ways to Grow Vegetables  Even if You Dont Have Time, Energy or Space for a Garden
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-04-08T12:37:13+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          April 8, 2013
         </time>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://calmhealthysexy.com/healthy-eating-strategy-grow-some-vegetables/#comments">
           22 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="http://calmhealthysexy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Garden-tomatoes-with-text.jpg">
          <img alt="growth red tomato in Greenhouse" class="alignright size-full wp-image-4782" height="459" src="http://calmhealthysexy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Garden-tomatoes-with-text.jpg" width="377"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://calmhealthysexy.com/healthy-eating-101-part-1-get-organized-and-make-a-plan/">
          Eating well
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://calmhealthysexy.com/smallstep-6-replace-2-processed-foods-with-real-foods/">
          increasing the number of real foods you eat
         </a>
         are key strategies to living a healthier life. Now that Spring is here, try a fun and (relatively) easy strategy for doing both  grow some vegetables! Gardening provides wonderful physical and mental health benefits  fresh air, sunshine, exercise and delicious food. Its a great family activity  most children love to dig in the dirt, plant, water, and pick fresh vegetables.
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are three approaches to growing your own vegetables, from the simplest, in terms of time, money and energy, to the most involved:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Grow vegetables in containers
          </strong>
           The easiest and least intimidating way to start is to grow some tomatoes, peppers, herbs, strawberries or other container-friendly plants in large pots. This strategy works well if you have very little space or time. Place the containers in a sunny location, ideally in a spot thats convenient for watering and monitoring them, fill with organic soil, and plant. Some plants are advertised as container or patio varieties; ask for help at your garden center if you arent sure which varieties grow well in containers.
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Create a raised bed garden
          </b>
           If want to go bigger than container gardening,
          <a href="http://calmhealthysexy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/May-1-C.jpg">
           <img alt="OLYMPUS DIGITAL CAMERA" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2178" height="300" src="http://calmhealthysexy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/May-1-C-245x300.jpg" width="245"/>
          </a>
          but dont want to deal with an in-ground garden, consider planting vegetables in raised beds. I grow my vegetables and herbs in raised beds and think its the best way to produce a lot of delicious food with a minimum of time, effort and hassle. I started with two 4 x 4 beds a number of years ago and now have expanded to four beds. Raised beds cut down on the work required to set up and maintain a garden  especially the weeding  and allow you to grow lots of vegetables and herbs with minimal effort. If someone in your family is handy, he or she can build 4 x 4 beds fairly easily. If not, you can buy them ready-to-assemble at most garden centers. (Make sure theyre made of untreated wood  you dont want chemicals to leach into the soil.) Starting with one 4 x 4 bed is a great way to ease into gardening. The downside to raised bed gardening is that it costs money the first year to set up the beds and fill them with organic, weed-free soil. But youll use them for a number of years, and the work required to maintain the gardens is minimal, so for me it was worth the investment.
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Plant a traditional in-ground garden
          </b>
           If you have space and time and want to garden in a big way, consider planting a traditional in-ground garden. On the plus side, you can grow a lot of vegetables for minimal cost. On the down side, youll need a tiller, a large space in full sun, and a lot of time and energy to establish and maintain it.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Once youve decided how youll garden,
         <b>
          decide what you want to grow
         </b>
         . Pick vegetables your family enjoys, plus one or two new ones. Add some favorite herbs, such as basil, mint and oregano. Then
         <b>
          determine the best time to plant
         </b>
         . This
         <a href="http://cdo.ncdc.noaa.gov/cgi-bin/climatenormals/climatenormals.pl?directive=prod_select2&amp;prodtype=CLIM2001&amp;subrnum%20to%20Freeze/Frost%20Data%20from%20the%20U.S.%20Climate%20Normals">
          National Climactic Data Center site
         </a>
         lets you select your state and city to determine the probability of freezing temperatures. Or contact the nearest
         <a href="http://www.csrees.usda.gov/Extension/">
          Extension Service
         </a>
         office about the best time to plant in your area. Next,
         <b>
          shop for plants at your garden center, greenhouse or farm supply store
         </b>
         . (Next year, consider starting some of your plants from seed, which saves money and expands the range of vegetables you can grow.) Then, the fun part 
         <b>
          plant your garden
         </b>
         ! If you have children, be sure to involve them; make it a fun project, not a chore. Gardening is a great way to help children learn to love healthy,
         <a href="http://calmhealthysexy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Girl-in-garden.jpg">
          <img alt="Girl in garden" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2179" height="241" src="http://calmhealthysexy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Girl-in-garden-300x300.jpg" width="241"/>
         </a>
         nutritious foods. If theyve helped to grow it, theyre going to want to eat it! Finally,
         <b>
          tend your garden
         </b>
         . Pull weeds as soon as they appear and watch for bugs. Many bugs can be removed by hand, but you may need to use organic pesticides if they become a problem. Check out
         <i>
          <a href="http://www.organicgardening.com/">
           Organic Gardening
          </a>
         </i>
         for tips on preventing and treating bugs and other garden problems.
        </p>
        <p>
         Do you grow vegetables? If so, whats your best tip? Please share your thoughts in the Comments  I would love to hear from you. I also would love for you to subscribe to Calm.Healthy.Sexy. by email (enter your email address in the sidebar under Subscribe to Blog Via Email). Thanks. Gaye
        </p>
