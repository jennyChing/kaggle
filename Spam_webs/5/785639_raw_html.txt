     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        I Am a Strange Loop
       </b>
      </i>
      is a 2007 book by
      <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Hofstadter" title="Douglas Hofstadter">
       Douglas Hofstadter
      </a>
      , examining in depth the concept of a
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Strange_loop" title="Strange loop">
        strange loop
       </a>
      </i>
      to explain the sense of "I". The concept of a
      <i>
       strange loop
      </i>
      was originally developed in his 1979 book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6del,_Escher,_Bach" title="Gdel, Escher, Bach">
        Gdel, Escher, Bach
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <table align="center" class="cquote2" role="presentation" style="border-collapse:collapse; border-style:none; background-color:transparent;">
      <tr>
       <td style="vertical-align:top;color:#B2B7F2;font-size:40px;font-family:serif;font-weight:bold;text-align:left;padding:10px 10px;" width="20">
        
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align:top;padding:4px 10px;">
        In the end, we are self-perceiving, self-inventing, locked-in mirages that are little miracles of self-reference.
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align:bottom;color:#B2B7F2;font-size:40px;font-family:serif;font-weight:bold;text-align:right;padding:10px 10px;" width="20">
        
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align:top;">
        <div style="line-height:1em;text-align: right">
         <cite style="font-style:normal;">
           Douglas Hofstadter,
          <i>
           I Am a Strange Loop
          </i>
          p.363
         </cite>
        </div>
       </td>
      </tr>
     </table>
     <p>
      Hofstadter had previously expressed disappointment with how
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6del,_Escher,_Bach" title="Gdel, Escher, Bach">
        Gdel, Escher, Bach
       </a>
       ,
      </i>
      which won the
      <a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize" title="Pulitzer Prize">
       Pulitzer Prize
      </a>
      in 1980 for general nonfiction, was received. In the preface to its 20th-anniversary edition, Hofstadter laments that the book was perceived as a hodgepodge of neat things with no central theme. He states: "GEB is a very personal attempt to say how it is that animate beings can come out of inanimate matter. What is a self, and how can a self come out of stuff that is as selfless as a stone or a puddle?"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Hofstadter seeks to remedy this problem in
      <i>
       I Am a Strange Loop
      </i>
      by focusing and expounding on the central message of
      <i>
       Gdel, Escher, Bach
      </i>
      . He demonstrates how the properties of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self-referential" title="Self-referential">
       self-referential
      </a>
      systems, demonstrated most famously in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G%C3%B6del%27s_Incompleteness_Theorem" title="Gdel's Incompleteness Theorem">
       Gdel's Incompleteness Theorem
      </a>
      , can be used to describe the unique properties of
      <a href="/wiki/Mind" title="Mind">
       minds
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      As an exploration of the sense of "I", Hofstadter explores his own life, and those to whom he has been close.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>

        </span>

        <span>
