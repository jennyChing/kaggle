        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Content Planning: The Pros and Cons of Evergreen, Newsjacking and Thought Leadership Posts
        </h1>

        <p>
         When you start to think about it, the ideaof writing a blog (or any content for that matter) is daunting.To stay top of mind, you need to create new content on a regular basis. And to create new content, you have to either come up witha new idea, spin an old one, or latch on to a topic that someoneelse is aggressively pushing forward. As youbrowse the Google machinefor ideas while scratching your head, a familiar thought pops up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          What should I write about today?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im going to attempt to help you answer that question. But not with one of your typical buzzfeed-breed 555 blogs you should be writing type of posts. Instead, Ill break the art of content developmentdown into three distinct categories,each with its own unique benefitsand challenges.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="content-planning-evergreen-newsjacking-web" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1147" data-lazy-src="http://i2.wp.com/redbamboomarketing.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/content-planning-evergreen-newsjacking-web.jpg?resize=700%2C238" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/redbamboomarketing.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="content-planning-evergreen-newsjacking-web" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1147" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/redbamboomarketing.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/content-planning-evergreen-newsjacking-web.jpg?resize=700%2C238"/>
        </noscript>
        <h3>
         Evergreen Content
        </h3>
        <p>
         Like the wooded trees it shares a name with, evergreen content lasts through the seasons. Content is considered evergreen when its subject-matter is relevant for a significant amount of time. What was relevant a year ago will still be relevant today, and may last for years to come.
        </p>
        <p>
         Evergreen contentoften explains a fundamental truth or a widelyaccepted point of view. Because of this, there will always be a built-in audience. For example, you may write a blog post that explains the difference between white goldand platinum.These two metals have distinctly differentphysical properties, and barring a seismic shift in the way we view chemistry, the contentwill be useful and accurate for years to come.
        </p>
        <p>
         Because of its stickiness, evergreen content is a safe bet when your goal is tocollect traffic over the long run. An evergreen blog post you write today may still get you website traffic in 2019. But before you stop reading and start planning your entire content strategy around this first type of content, you have to realize theres a catch: There is a LOT of evergreen content already out there. And that means your contentislikely not going to give you strong short-term gains.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Pros
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Long life span
         </li>
         <li>
          Generally accepted ideas, making it easier to research
         </li>
         <li>
          Wide audience appeal
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Cons
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Can be highly competitive
         </li>
         <li>
          Lacks urgency
         </li>
         <li>
          Need a lot of posts to see big returns
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          My Advice
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         When writing evergreen content, swing for the fences. Try to create something so valuable and unique that it becomes a de facto resource for interested readers. One of my favorite examples is Avinash Kaushiks blog post explaining the
         <a href="http://www.kaushik.net/avinash/digital-marketing-and-measurement-model/">
          basics of web analytics
         </a>
         .He hit a grand slam with this post, and it has consistently ranked on the top of hundreds of related Google searches since 2011.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Topical Content
        </h3>
        <p>
         Topical content focuses on weaving a timely story into your content to capitalize on public interest. This type of content isfleeting in nature, attempting to cash in on a popular topic to get big traffic returns in a short amount of time. For example, a blog post highlighting the 2015 Academy Awards runway fashion may spike in the daysfollowing the event, but will likely find itself six feet under soon after.
        </p>
        <p>
         While not the only approach,
         <strong>
          Newsjacking
         </strong>
         may be the most well-known variant of topical content.
         <a href="http://blog.hubspot.com/blog/tabid/6307/bid/32983/The-Inbound-Marketer-s-Complete-Guide-to-Newsjacking.aspx">
          According to Hubspot
         </a>
         , newsjackingis defined as the practice of capitalizing on the popularity of a news story to amplify your sales and marketing success.
        </p>
        <p>
         Newsjacking can be wildly successful (just look at Buzzfeed) when done right, drivingincredible amounts of traffic to your website in a short time span. But aside from its short shelf life and ultra-specific topics, theres a big issue with newsjacking: Its really hard. For your post to go viral, it must presenta good spin on a current story and do it quickly to boot. This kind of fast turnaround can leave room for big mistakes. And even worse, you may put in a lot of effort but still miss the boat completely.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_1144" style="width: 610px">
         <img alt="newsjacking" class="size-full wp-image-1144" data-lazy-src="http://i2.wp.com/redbamboomarketing.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/newsjacking.png?resize=600%2C437" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/redbamboomarketing.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif"/>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="newsjacking" class="size-full wp-image-1144" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/redbamboomarketing.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/newsjacking.png?resize=600%2C437"/>
         </noscript>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Credit: HubSpot Blog
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Pros
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Ability to go viral
         </li>
         <li>
          Posts can be short and sweet
         </li>
         <li>
          Less competition when well-timed
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Cons
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Speaking of timingits critical (and difficult) to get right
         </li>
         <li>
          Often has less sources available to cite
         </li>
         <li>
          Extremely short shelf life
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          My Advice
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont lose your soul. One of the biggest pitfalls of newsjacking (and timely content in general) is that you can often cross into a moral gray area in an effort toride the wave of the story. Like when Kenneth Cole tweeted this during Egyptian protests in 2011:
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_1145" style="width: 310px">
         <img alt="Source: http://www.getspokal.com/" class="size-medium wp-image-1145" data-lazy-src="http://i1.wp.com/redbamboomarketing.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/kenneth-cole-tweet.png?resize=300%2C132" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/redbamboomarketing.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif"/>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Source: http://www.getspokal.com/" class="size-medium wp-image-1145" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/redbamboomarketing.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/kenneth-cole-tweet.png?resize=300%2C132"/>
         </noscript>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Source: http://www.getspokal.com/
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Yeah. Dont do that.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Thought Leadership
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         This last content category is a little less well-defined, because technically it can fit into either category above. However, I see enough differentiation to let it exist on its own.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thought leadership content is a high-risk, high-reward style that is arguably the most difficult to write. When writing thought leadership content, you must forge new ground, establishing yourself as an industry expert through the use of insight, forecasting and trend analysis.
        </p>
        <p>
         First, let metackle the flaws. Writing thought leadership content requires an intimate understanding of your subject matter, as well as incorporating original ideas that belong to you. This makes it extremely difficult to create thought leadership pieces.And repeatedly doing so? Thats reserved for theelite pioneers of industry.
        </p>
        <p>
         Butlets sayyou do create mind-melting, jaw-dropping, damn-good content. Theres still a good chance that NOBODY WILL READ IT. Becauseyoure pushingan original thought, there likely isnt a large audience searching for your content. Which really sucks.
        </p>
        <p>
         So why would anyone risk their time writing content that is difficult to create and likely to never be seen? Because the rewards of becoming a thought leader are tremendous. Thought leaders grab our attention. They cut through the clutter like thunder through the silence. They move markets and define popular opinion. To cut my rhetoric short, its damn nice to be recognized as a thought leader.
        </p>
        <p>
         For example, take a look at HubSpot, one of my favorite companies over the last 5 years. They pioneered the term inbound marketing and built an entire methodology around it when everyone else was focusing on fragmented tactics like SEO and social media. By doing this, they were able to differentiate from the masses and become the gold standard for inbound marketing  a term now synonymous with digital and content marketing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Pros
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Catapults you to the forefront of your industry
         </li>
         <li>
          Builds a loyal following
         </li>
         <li>
          Makes you a baller
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Cons
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Extremely difficult to research  its up to you!
         </li>
         <li>
          You can easilylook like ahack instead of a hero
         </li>
         <li>
          No guarantees that it will drive traffic
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          My Advice
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont be afraid to take risks. The hardest part about writing thought leadership pieces is having the confidence to show vulnerability and share your thoughts with the world. Should all your content be thought leadership pieces? No  that would be too risky for 99% of businesses. But strive to work an original piece in every once in a while, and dont quit if successdoesnt show her face immediately.
        </p>
        <p>
         So there you go. I went into this post with a sliver of an idea, and it quickly turned into a 1000+ word essay. I hope it gives you a different frame of mind when planning your next blog posts. And who knows  maybe this will be one of my evergreen posts!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What do you think?
         </strong>
         Id love to hear some of your thoughts in the comments below.
        </p>
