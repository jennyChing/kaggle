    <h1 class="entry-title">
     How to Install Android Lollipop on Phones and Tablets
    </h1>
    <div class="articleInfo author vcard">
     <a class="fl" href="https://plus.google.com/102065047990026539206/">
      <img alt="Wondershare Editor" height="50" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-OrjUuTRGY5Q/VQl0Nk-C19I/AAAAAAAAAsw/6n9ChirlpG0/w140-h140-p/1ec45baf_o.jpeg" width="50"/>
     </a>
     <div class="text">
      <p class="mt5">
       <abbr class="updated" title="Dec 02,2014 9:22 am">
        Dec 02,2014 9:22 am
       </abbr>
       / Posted by
       <a class="url fn" href="https://plus.google.com/102065047990026539206/" rel="author">
        Selena Lee
       </a>
       to
       <a href="/android-data-recovery/" rel="category">

       <a class="follow" href="https://twitter.com/WS_selena">
        Follow @WS_selena

      </div>
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="cont">
     <p>
      Google has released Android 5.0  Lollipop. And the
      <a href="https://plus.google.com/102065047990026539206/posts/Q9rv2Y6aKUa" rel="nofollows" target="_blank">
       survey
      </a>
      we have done shows that more than 94% of people are going to install Android 5.0 on their phones and tablets. Most of them want to test the Lollipop, after all, it's a brand new OS for Android Phones and tablets. However, it is not that easy to update Android 5.0 or install Android 5.0 on your Phones and tablets. Before doing that, you have some things to pay attention to:
     </p>
     <p class="related-articles">
      <a href="#part1">
       Part 1. Check out whether your Android device is officially permitted to install Android 5.0 or not
      </a>
      <br/>
      <a href="#part2">
       Part 2. How to Update to Android 5.0 with official OTA
      </a>
      <br/>
      <a href="#part3">
       Part 3. How to Install Android 5.0 Lollipop to Nexus Series Manually
      </a>
      <br/>
      <a href="#part4">
       Part 4. How to Flash Android 5.0 ROM to your Android device
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      It sounds it's very complicated to update to Android Lollipop, right? Don't worry. You can figure out where you are and what you're supposed to do by reading the following details.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="how to upgrade to android 5.0" class="maxW" src="http://images.wondershare.com/images/android/android-5-0-lollipop.jpg"/>
     </p>
     <a name="part1">
     </a>
     <h2>
      Part 1. List of devices that officially confirmed can be updated to Android 5.0
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       1.	Flagship devices from Google: Nexus 4, Nexus 5, Nexus 7, Nexus 10 and Google Play edition devices
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Developers with a Nexus 5 or Nexus 7 have had the access to Android Lollipop since June 26. As for the rest of the users, the good news is that Android Lollipop is already available for Nexus 4, Nexus 5, and Nexus 7. And the OTA will be available for Nexus 10 shortly. If you haven't received the Andoid 5.0 updateOTA for your Nexus device and don't want to wait, you can directly download the
      <a href="https://developers.google.com/android/nexus/images" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       factory image from Google
      </a>
      now.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       2.	Samsung Galaxy S5, Samsung Galaxy Note 4, Galaxy Alpha, and Galaxy Note 3
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Samsung Galaxy S5 will be the first device from Samsung to get update for Android 5.0.  Samsung Galaxy Note 4 was released too early to launch with Android Lollipop, but there will be an update as well. It is said that Galaxy Alpha and Galaxy Note 3 will get the update too.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="how to install android 5.0" class="maxW" src="http://images.wondershare.com/images/android/samsung.JPG "/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       3.	HTC One (M8) and HTC One (M7)
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      HTC officially confirmed that they will begin rolling out updates to the HTC One family worldwidely within 90 days of receiving final software from Google. Besides the mentioned 2 devices, it's very likely that HTC Desire Eye, HTC One Mini 2, HTC One Mini and perhaps HTC One Max will get the update too.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="update to android 5.0" class="maxW" src="http://images.wondershare.com/images/android/htc-one.jpg "/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       4.	Moto X Pure Edition and second-g Moto G
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Motorola has confirmed there will be an update for Moto X Pure Edition and second-g Moto G. It is still in processing now.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       5.	LG G3
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      The first device that is going to get Android 5.0 update is LG G3. The manufacturer announced that LG3 users in Poland will be the first to download the Android 5.0  Lollipop.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       6.	Sony Xperia Z, Xperia ZL, Xperia ZR, Xperia Tablet Z, Xperia Z1, Xperia Z1S, Xperia Z Ultra, Xperia Z1 Compact, Xperia Z2, Xperia Z2 Tablet, Xperia Z3, Xperia Z3v, Xperia Z3 Compact and Xperia Z3 Tablet Compact
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Sony has declared that they will release Android 5.0 update for the Xperia Z series.
     </p>
     <p>
      There will be Android 5.0 updates for the above mentioned devices. If your device is in the list, what you need to do is to wait for the official OTA. It might takes 1, 2 or 3 months for the update arrives depending on your manufacturer. My suggestion for you is to wait for the official update for Android 5.0. when it is available, you can
      <a href="#part2">
       upgrade to Android 5.0 easily
      </a>
      . If you insist on installing Android 5.0 on your device before the official OTA comes, the only way is to
      <a href="#part3">
       flash Android 5.0 ROM
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Like to test Android Lollipop, only to find that your manufacturer doesn't have any plan to release Android 5.0 update OTA for your device? The only way for you to install Android Lollipop is to flash Android 5.0 ROM.
     </p>
     <div class="line">
     </div>
     <a name="part2">
     </a>
     <h2>
      Part 2. How to Update to Android 5.0 with official OTA
     </h2>
     <p>
      If Android 5.0 arrives, there will an OTA message on your phone or tablet, telling there is an update. Right now, Android Lollipop update OTAs are available for Moto X Pure Edition, second-g Moto G, Nexus 7 (Wi-Fi and Mobile version), Nexus 9 (Wi-Fi), Nexus 5 (GSM/LTE) and Nexus 10. If you have seen the OTA message for Android 5.0 Lollipop on your device, you can follow the simple steps below to install it.
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 1. Backup your device. Restart your device and enter the recovery mode: hold down the Power and Volume button. Find the Backup or Nandroid section to confirm the backup.
      <br/>
      Step 2. Ensure your Wi-Fi or cellular data is stable.
      <br/>
      Step 3.Click the update or install button to download the installation package on your phone and follow the prompts to install Android 5.0 on your device.
     </p>
     <div class="line">
     </div>
     <a name="part3">
     </a>
     <h2>
      Part 3. How to Install Android 5.0 Lollipop to Nexus Phones and Tablet Manually
     </h2>
     <p>
      Some people have mentioned that though their Nexus device is in the list that will be updated to Android Lollipo, they haven't received the OTA yet. Don't worry! If you really can't wait to install Android Lollipop on your Nexus phone or tablet, try the following steps.
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 1. Download and install the
      <a href="http://www.wugfresh.com/nrt/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       Nexus Root Toolkit
      </a>
      on your computer. It only works on Windows PC. If you have a Mac, please try to use it in the virtual Windows PC. Please note that this tool might void your warranty. If you'r worried about that, then you should just wait for the official OTA mentioned in Part 2.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="manually install Android 5.0" class="maxW" src="http://images.wondershare.com/images/android/nexus-root-toolkit.png"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 2. Tap Settings &gt; About Phone on your Nexus device. Under the Build number, find your device build and remember it.
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 3. Tap Settings &gt; About Phone &gt; click Build Number to enable Developer mode. In the Developer mode options, enable USB debugging. Only by enabling USB debugging, your Nexus device will be detected by the Nexus Root Toolkit.
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 4. Connect your Nexus device with your computer via its digital cable. Launch the Nexus Root Toolkit and select the device you're using and it's current build. When the tool tries to connect your device, there will be a pop-up on your phone. You should allow the connection. If the tool can't recognize your phone, you're supposed to click the Full Driver Installation Guide on the toolkit to install software drivers.
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 5. In the Nexus Root Toolkit, click the Unlock button &gt; OK &gt; Yes. During the process, your device will reboot.
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 6. On your Nexus device, click Settings &gt; Build number to enable Developer Mode and select USB debugging.
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 7. Go to Google offcial site to
      <a href="https://developers.google.com/android/nexus/images" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       download the 5.0 factory image file
      </a>
      . Please select the right image file to download according to your device model. Save the image file on your local drive.
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 8. In the Nexus Root Toolkit, click Flash Stock + Unroot. In the prompt select the image file you've downloaded in step 7, copy and past the MD5 checksum number which is next to the image file you downloaded on Google official site.
     </p>
     <p>
      Step 9. Wait for your phone rebooting. That's it!
     </p>
     <div class="line">
     </div>

     </p>
     <h2>
      Part 4. How to Flash Android 5.0 ROM to your Android device
     </h2>
     <p>
      For users who will never get Android 5.0 update from the manufacturer, flashing Android 5.0 ROM to the device is the only way. Unfortunately, the 3 variants of Android 5.0 Lollipop ROMs are only available for Nexus 5 now. Other device owners have to wait for the ROMs to come. Below are the details to flash Android 5.0 ROM for Nexus 5.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="flash a ROM to Android 5.0" class="maxW" src="http://images.wondershare.com/images/android/nexus5.jpg"/>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <strong>
       Prep work:
      </strong>
     </h3>
     <ul class="dot">
      <li>
       Ensure 80% of Nexus 5 battery charge
      </li>
      <li>
       Backup data and settings with a tool, like ClockworkMode or TWRP.
      </li>
      <li>
       Make sure your phone can be connected with your computer successfully with its USB cable;
      </li>
      <li>
       Ensure the Nexus 5 is rooted with TWRP recovery installed and its bootloader is unlocked.
      </li>
