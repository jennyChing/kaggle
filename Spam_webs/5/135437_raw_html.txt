            <h1>
             Press Releases
            </h1>
            <h2>
             <a href="/articles/press-releases/powa-downhill-machine">
              Powa Downhill Machine
             </a>
            </h2>
            <dl class="article-info">
             <dt class="article-info-term">
              Details
             </dt>
             <dd class="category-name">
              Category:
              <a href="/articles/press-releases">
               Press Release
              </a>
             </dd>
             <dd class="createdby">
              Written by Powa
             </dd>
            </dl>
            <h1 align="center">
             <br/>
             <em>
              <strong>
               Powa Downhill Machine
              </strong>
             </em>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Active Image" height="300" src="http://images.silverfishlongboarding.com/images/stories/Powa/IMG_0008_2.jpg" width="450"/>
            </h1>
            <p>
             Created by designer Nuno Pereira for Powa, it s a project that goes way far from the conventional skate concept it became a downhill machine
            </p>
            <p>
             The deck is made for speed, more area on the front, to position the front foot, less area on the back to the tip of the back foot
            </p>
            <p>
             Rails are chamfered, it improves aerodynamics and gives a better feeling when grabbing the deck
            </p>
            <p>
             The purpose of an adjustable skate is that we can have a setup for each hill, according to the hill and the personal preferences of the rider.
            </p>
            <p>
             You can adjust the
             <a href="http://www.silverfishlongboarding.com/gear-reviews/truck" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="trucks">
              trucks
             </a>
             between 0 and 90 degrees, you can put one truck with a very aggressive angle, as you can put it with zero turning. Each truck adjustment is independent. When you adjust the truck you are also adjusting the wheelbase. You can adjust the ground clearance, truck width, truck angle, wheelbase and weight
            </p>
            <p>
             These trucks have the same precision that CNC machined trucks have.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: center">
             <img alt="Active Image" height="300" src="http://images.silverfishlongboarding.com/images/stories/Powa/IMG_0013_2.jpg" width="450"/>
            </div>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            You can create lift or downforce, making a variation between  the front and back truck. On a very fast hill you can create lift to  achieve higher speed, on a more technical one you can create downforce  to have more corner support
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <div style="text-align: center">
             <img alt="Active Image" src="http://images.silverfishlongboarding.com/images/stories/Powa/inv1.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div style="text-align: center">
              <img alt="Active Image" src="http://images.silverfishlongboarding.com/images/stories/Powa/inv2.jpg"/>
             </div>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             You can distribute the weight on the  front or back, if you want the back end more loose, or the front, you  just add or reduce weight where you wish. You can have a heavier skate  on fast hills, to maintain momentum or you can reduce it on twisty ones.
            </p>
            <p>
             With a conventional skate, if you want to have this possibilities  you would have to own a lot of decks, trucks, baseplates, hangars,  risers, etc until you get to the perfect combination. That combination  would never be perfect because a rider is constantly evolving, today  needs are never the tomorrow needs. With the downhill machine, you just  have to unscrew and screw some bolts and you are ready to go. To adjust  the ground clearance you add or take carbon fiber shims, this assure  precision and ease on the adjustment. To adjust the truck angle you  replace carbon fiber plates. You can experience almost infinite  combinations.
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: center">
             <img alt="Active Image" src="http://images.silverfishlongboarding.com/images/stories/Powa/IMG_0011_2.jpg"/>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             You can have a drop deck, a top mount, a drop  trough, evo setup, etc
            </p>
            <p>
             The rider adjusts the skate according to  the hill, and his needs in that precise moment, optimizing the  performance.
            </p>
            <p>
             This skate was not thought as a deck to mount  other trucks, it was projected as one.
            </p>
            <p>
             At the end, this is  giving the adjustability that you have on a racing car or motorcycle to a  skate, thats why it is a downhill machine
            </p>
            <p>
             The trucks are made  from aerospace grade aluminium, the axles are machined from solid block  of chromium vanadium steel
            </p>
            <p>
             The deck has a birch and airex core  and it is vacuum laminated with carbon fiber and epoxy resin.The  standard deck has very little longitudinal and torsional flex but this  can be fine tuned for each rider preference
            </p>
            <p>
             New decks with other  materials are being tested
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: center">
             <img alt="Active Image" src="http://images.silverfishlongboarding.com/images/stories/Powa/IMG_2602.JPG"/>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <h2 align="center">
             Specs
            </h2>
            <div align="center">
            </div>
            <p align="center">
             <a href="http://www.silverfishlongboarding.com/gear-reviews/truck" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Truck">
              Truck
             </a>
             width: 190-215
            </p>
            <div align="center">
            </div>
            <p align="center">
             Ground clearance:20-90mm
            </p>
            <div align="center">
            </div>
            <p align="center">
             Truck angle: 0-90
            </p>
            <div align="center">
            </div>
            <p align="center">
             weight:  4.7kgs (adjustable)
            </p>
            <div align="center">
            </div>
            <p align="center">
             deck lenght: 800mm
            </p>
            <div align="center">
            </div>
            <p align="center">
             deck width:255mm
            </p>
            <div align="center">
            </div>
            <p align="center">
             londitudinal flex: very little
            </p>
            <div align="center">
            </div>
            <p align="center">
             torsional flex: no flex
            </p>
            <div align="center">
            </div>
            <p align="center">
             wheelbase: variable
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             At Powa, We dont like  selling to clients and we dont like to call our products objects, we  dont like mass production without meaning
            </p>
            <p>
             We like to provide  pieces of well thought design and engineering to people have fun and  enjoy life. These are much more than objects, are extensions of the  human soul We put a piece of our souls in each project, our passion  and excitment is present in each one.
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: center">
             <img alt="Active Image" src="http://images.silverfishlongboarding.com/images/stories/Powa/powaaa.jpg"/>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             We dont do disposable  products, our products are designed to last. Our products are  not expensive, what you pay is a part of the work, the rest of is paid  with enthusiasm, the stoke we get when we see someone rocking and loving  our extensions of the soul
            </p>
            <p>
             Nuno can make custom
             <a href="http://www.silverfishlongboarding.com/gear-reviews/longboard" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="boards">
              boards
             </a>
             , but  there is not guaranteed that he will make every project. Each project is  selected by him, or has he likes to say, he is selected by the  projects. He chooses the projects based on if he likes the project, and  the people. He wont make copies or simple re-designs, if you ask him to  do something difficult and innovative, it will probably do it if the  likes you. In this case , the product is one of a kind, the person who  asked for it gets a masterpiece.
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: center">
             <img alt="Active Image" src="http://images.silverfishlongboarding.com/images/stories/Powa/IMG_2605.JPG"/>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Our products are made by  riders for riders,. Each product is tested by the design/engineering  team and by our team riders. The first to ride is the one who created.  We trust what we do.
            </p>
            <p>
             Nunos design is minimalist, functionalist,  and therefore sustainable too. Each element has a function and is well  thought, nothing is left by chance. The aim is always to use the less  possible material and make the products last. He his always looking for  new ways of making , thinking, and new materials. There are no  boundaries, you can see materials of all areas come to others, where  normally wouldnt be found. There are no limits, or limitations.
            </p>
            <p>
             Each  product have a long design development, physical models, 3d modeling,  prototypes ,etc We use technology, it saves time and material, but each  product is handcrafted to perfection, and at the end of the day, the  most important is the human part.
            </p>
            <p>
             Several decks are being  developed, a street luge, a gravity bike, conventional precision trucks,  and wheels, but there are always new projects coming up
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: center">
             <img alt="Active Image" src="http://images.silverfishlongboarding.com/images/stories/Powa/IMG_6659.JPG"/>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             For  now we have two available , the Downhill Machine and the Speedfeel
            </p>
            <p>
             A  video of the downhill machine and the speedfeel is being produced
            </p>
            <p>
             The website,
             <a href="http://www.dapowa.com" target="_blank">
              http://www.dapowa.com
             </a>
             , is currently being updated but you can  check us on
             <a href="http://www.facebook.com/pages/Powa/298922875875?v=info#!/pages/Powa/298922875875" target="_blank">
              facebook
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
