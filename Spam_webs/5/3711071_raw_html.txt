        <p>
         Greetings from Saigon and happy new year! Here is this weeks edition of Nomadic News.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Digital Nomad Conference
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.dnxglobal.com/" target="_blank">
          DNX GLOBAL
         </a>
         is billed as the first international conference for Digital Nomads, and will take place in Berlin in July/August. There is a great list of speakers (including
         <a href="http://sivers.org/" target="_blank">
          Derek Sivers
         </a>
         ) and it goes to show that the whole working remotely lifestyle is gaining momentum.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Location Independence
        </h2>
        <p>
         AsiaLife Magazine covers the
         <a href="http://www.asialifemagazine.com/vietnam/digital-nomads/" target="_blank">
          digital nomad movement in Saigon
         </a>
         , interviewing myself,
         <a href="http://jonmyers.com/" target="_blank">
          Jon Myers
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.legalnomads.com" target="_blank">
          Jodi Ettenberg
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.mattbieber.net/" target="_blank">
          Matt Bieber
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://global-goose.com/digital-nomad-lifestyle/freelance-travel-the-world/" target="_blank">
          How I built a successful freelance writing business so I could work from anywhere and travel the world
         </a>
          By Kelly from Global Goose.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Living Abroad
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://america.aljazeera.com/watch/shows/real-money-with-alivelshi/articles/2014/12/30/quitting-americagivingupcitizenshiptosavemoneyontaxes.html" target="_blank">
          Quitting America: Giving up citizenship to save money on taxes
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.wsj.com/articles/an-expat-writer-still-looking-for-home-1419451264" target="_blank">
          An expat writer, still looking for home
         </a>
          Good article if you can access it (boo paywalls!)
        </p>
        <h2>
         Cost Of Living
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://findingthefreedom.com/how-to-live-on-the-beach-cheap/" target="_blank">
          Living on a beach in India for under $500 a month
         </a>
          By Alex Bradbeer.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Office Of The Week
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Nomadic Notes - Travel photos: Ho Chi Minh City &emdash; " height="600" src="http://photos.nomadicnotes.com/img/s1/v5/p608999300-4.jpg" width="800"/>
         <br/>
         [Loft  2nd floor, 95 Pasteur, District 1, Ho Chi Minh City]
        </p>
        <p>
         This is the second
         <a href="http://loftcafe.vn/" target="_blank">
          Loft Cafe
         </a>
         to open in Saigon, which is just as stylish as the first.
        </p>

          </p>
          <p>
           First off, sitting in a beach in Goa, you dont see the Indian ocean but see the Arabian sea instead.
          </p>
          <p>
           Secondly, the author says that the prices in his restaurant are on the higher side, while I, from India dont think they are. Yes you can get cheaper food in India but the prices mentioned are no way near the normal rates at a good shack in Goa.
          </p>
          <p>
           Coffee  Rs.20 ($0.40) at a road side shop. A coffee shop will charge you $1-2.
           <br/>
           Garlic Nan (bread)  The price given is for one. An average adult would need 3-4 Nans per meal, along with curry of course.
           <br/>
           Beer  $1 only if you purchase it from a shop and have it in the room. At a restaurant or a bar, it will be at least three times the price.
          </p>
          <p>
           And finally, the hut where the author stays is at the low end of the spectrum. Forget wifi, you will not have 24hr electricity, proper toilets or hot water at this price range.
          </p>
          <p>
           You can live in a beach in India for $500 but it will be no paradise as the article states. For a backpacker yes, but definitely not for a digital nomad.
          </p>
