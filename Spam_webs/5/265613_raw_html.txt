            <div class="articlehead">
             <header>
              <span>
              </span>
              <h1>
               Survival Tips for Parents of Children With ADHD
              </h1>
             </header>
             <aside class="disclaimer">
             </aside>
             <h2>
              Parenting a child with ADHD can be stressful. These survival tips can help you cope, even on the most difficult days.
             </h2>
             <div class="bottom">
              <div class="byline">
               By
               <a href="/contributing-writers-and-editors.aspx">
                Beth W. Orenstein
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="reviewedby">
               Medically reviewed by
               <a href="/medical-reviewers.aspx">
                Pat F. Bass III, MD, MPH
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="clear">
             </div>
            </div>
            <div id="video-player-top">
            </div>
            <aside class="aside">
             <img alt="7 Survival Tips for Parents of Children With ADHD" class="left" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/cs_adhd_survival_tips_for_parents_article.jpg"/>
            </aside>
            <p>
             Another morning, another scene: Your child with attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (
             <a href="/health-report/adhd-guide-for-parents.aspx">
              ADHD
             </a>
             ) wont brush his teeth, get dressed, or
             <a href="/health-report/adhd-guide-for-parents/diet-and-exercise-natural-adhd-therapies.aspx">
              eat breakfast
             </a>
             , and the school bus comes in 15 minutes. Your meeting starts in an hour, and you have a 30-minute commute.
            </p>
            <p>
             Its an understatement to say that, at times, life can be a challenge when you have a child with ADHD. On top of this, parents often blame themselves for their childs impulsive, aggressive behaviors.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the stress of the moment, it can be easy to overreact, but lashing out at your child or blaming yourself helps no one, says Billi Bittan, PhD, an ADHD coach and therapist in private practice in Tarzana, Calif. Parents need to take care of themselves first to be able to
             <a href="/adhd-pictures/help-adhd-child-succeed.aspx">
              help a child with ADHD
             </a>
             . Like the flight attendant says: Put the oxygen mask on yourself first and then help your child, Bittan advises.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              7 Survival Tips to Help You Cope
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             Heres how you can help yourself cope, even on the most difficult days:
            </p>
            <ol>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Learn as much as you can about ADHD.
              </strong>
              The more you know about ADHD, the better you can deal with your
              <a href="/health-report/adhd-guide-for-parents/discipline-strategies-for-adhd.aspx">
               childs behavior
              </a>
              and help her to succeed in life. You will feel empowered if you know what to do in various situations, especially when your child acts out. Resources abound on the Internet and in bookstores. Write down your questions and take them to your childs next doctor or counselor appointment.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Dont think you have to do it all alone.
              </strong>
              Seek professional help, especially if you are feeling depressed and frustrated or exhausted by your parenting responsibilities. Join a support group for
              <a href="/health-report/adhd-guide-for-parents/parenting-child-with-adhd-tracies-story.aspx">
               parents of children with ADHD
              </a>
              . Sharing ideas and listening to others facing similar issues can be very helpful and reassuring. If you dont have a support group in your area, think about starting one or join one online.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Stay calm.
              </strong>
              Remember that even when you are having difficulty controlling your childs behavior, you can still control your own. Getting frustrated and angry when your child doesn't listen or is forgetful wont help. It will likely only make matters worse. You are your childs role model, Bittan says, so act the way you would like her to act.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Schedule me time.
              </strong>
              You need to pay attention to your health, both physical and mental, to be better able to take care of your child. You cant take care of your child if youre sick, Bittan says. Take time each day to do a favorite activity, whether thats reading a book, doing yoga, or talking on the phone. Eat right, exercise, and manage your stress.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Focus on the positives.
              </strong>
              Make a list of all the things that are positive, valuable, and unique about your son or daughter. The more you believe that your child can learn and succeed, the more likely it will be to happen. Think about this list every morning when you wake up, and you will start your day with a smile.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Have realistic expectations.
              </strong>
              If you set high expectations that your child wont be able to achieve, youre setting him  and yourself  up for failure. You wouldn't ask a child in a wheelchair to climb the stairs to his room, says Bittan. Know your childs limitations and dont expect the impossible. You can raise the bar, she explains, but do it slowly and be sure to celebrate the achievements your child makes along the way
              <em>
               together
              </em>
              .
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Simplify your life.
              </strong>
              As a parent of a child with ADHD, you automatically have a lot on your plate. Dont agree to take on more outside duties than you can handle. If you limit your commitments, you wont feel as harried and will be better able to concentrate on whats important for your child.
             </li>
            </ol>
            <p>
             While parenting a child with ADHD can be stressful, focusing on the positives, seeking support from others, and taking care of yourself can make your job easier and more rewarding.
            </p>
