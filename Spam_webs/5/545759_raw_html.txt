           The Last Days of Internet ?
          </span>
         </h1>
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- 650*90 -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-5396744638567400" data-ad-slot="6996961973" style="display:inline-block;width:650px;height:90px">

         <div class="entry">
          <a id="dd_start">
          </a>
          <p>
           London : Internet has become the basic need for everyone from a school going kid to a resting old man. Humans can live without food, but cant live without having Internet. Every single task is related to Internet. Internet has created such a strong impact on all of us.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.tollywoodone.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/internet.jpg">
            <img alt="The Last Days of Internet ? ,  net neutrality, net neutrality in india, net neutrality in london, A Case for Net Neutrality, net neutrality pros and cons " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-302" height="275" src="http://www.tollywoodone.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/internet.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           But, sadly these days seem to be the last days of Internet. According to Professor Andrew Ellis of London, Internet speeds have increased by 50 fold in the last decade alone. Optical fibres have reached capacity and cant transfer any more light. Laying down more cables may solve problem but this will increase costs. It may become very hard to secure Internet.
          </p>
          <p>
           Social networking sites has been buzzed with Net Neutrality for the last few days. Now, it might move to this side with the excessive usage of Internet.
          </p>
