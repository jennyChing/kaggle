     <div class="article-body pagination-first">
      <p>
       In the late 1980s, India's government announced a plan to release speciallybred flesh-eating turtles into the Ganges river. According to Hindu faith, there is salvation in being cremated on the banks of the riverand then dumped into it. But sometimes the corpses are only partially burned, and
       <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/magazine-28112403">
        according to the BBC
       </a>
       , the corpses of those who cannot afford to be cremated are put in the river, too.
      </p>
      <div class="associated-container">
      </div>
      <p>
       "Hundreds of years ago, such turtles flourished in the Ganges until they were killed by hunters for meat,"
       <a href="http://articles.latimes.com/1987-08-09/news/mn-470_1_holiest-river">
        the
        <em>
         Los Angeles Times
        </em>
        reported
       </a>
       . "Now, the government has begun breeding them on a farm near Lucknow." The turtles were raised on dead fish, "so that they wouldn't develop a taste for the living,"
       <a href="http://www.atlasobscura.com/articles/what-became-of-india-s-corpse-eating-turtles">
        reports Atlas Obscura
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Some 25,000 flesh-hungryturtles, and$32 million dollars later, the plan was a failure, Atlas Obscura reports:
      </p>

       </div>

       </div>
      </div>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        Itwasplaguedby corruption and mismanagement, and though plenty offorethought was put into raising the turtles, not so much attention was paid to seeing that they survived in the wild after their release, and as a result, they werepoachedand killed in large numbers.
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Today,
       <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/magazine-28112403">
        corpse pollution is still a problem
       </a>
       for the Ganges. As is turtle disappearance. To protect the population from being depleted by poachers and smugglers,each year
       <a href="http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/varanasi/34000-tortoises-released-into-Ganga/articleshow/8553068.cms">
        over a thousand ordinary turtles
       </a>
       are released in into the river.
