&lt;img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-15929" alt="Pumkin Pie Frosting" src="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/Pumkin-Pie-Frosting-Collage-2-done-512x1024.jpg" width="512" height="1024" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Pumpkin pie frosting is not frosting that is kinda sorta like pumpkin pie. It&#8217;s not a buttercream with some pumpkin mixed in, or a cream cheese frosting with a dash of pumpkin pie spice. Pumpkin pie frosting is honest-to-goodness pumpkin pie (minus the crust &#8211; although you could add that too if you wanted) piped onto cupcakes.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div&gt;&lt;a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/__IP6S4qrDTw/TL2-I1PsbHI/AAAAAAAADVI/-KGjuLkOhUI/s1600/Pumpkin+Pie+Frosting+03.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="aligncenter" alt="" src="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/Pumpkin+Pie+Frosting+03.jpg" width="400" height="266" border="0" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;It turns out that pumpkin pie is perfectly pipe-able. Try out this pumpkin pie frosting as the ideal complement for any Fall cupcake flavor. I piped mine onto &lt;a href="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/2010/10/apple-cobbler-cupcakes-with-pumpkin-pie.html"&gt;apple cobbler cupcakes&lt;/a&gt;. But, for other Fall cupcake inspiration, check out my &lt;a href="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/2007/02/my-ten-favorite-fall-cupcake-recipes_7898.html"&gt;top ten Fall cupcake recipes&lt;/a&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;/div&gt;&lt;div class="img-desc-wrap"&gt;&lt;p class="t-a-c hide-card hide-print"&gt;
			  &lt;img class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/Pumpkin+Pie+Frosting+05.jpg" title="Pumpkin Pie Frosting" alt="Pumpkin Pie Frosting" style="width: 250px;" /&gt;
			&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;p id="zlrecipe-ingredients" class="h-4 strong"&gt;Ingredients&lt;/p&gt;&lt;ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list"&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"&gt;3/4 C sugar&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"&gt;    1/2 tsp salt&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"&gt;    1 1/2 tsp pumpkin pie spice&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"&gt;    2 large eggs&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"&gt;    15 oz canned pumpkin (pure pumpkin, not pumpkin pie filling)&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"&gt;    12 oz evaporated milk&lt;/li&gt;&lt;/ul&gt;&lt;p id="zlrecipe-instructions" class="h-4 strong"&gt;Instructions&lt;/p&gt;&lt;ol id="zlrecipe-instructions-list" class="instructions"&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;Preheat oven to 425 F.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;    In a large bowl, beat eggs.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;    Stir in sugar, salt, and pumpkin pie spice.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;    Mix in pumpkin.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;    Mix in evaporated milk.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;    Pour into 9" pie plate (no crust needed).&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;    Bake for 15 minutes.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;    Reduce heat to 350 F and bake for another 40-50 minutes, until a toothpick comes out dry.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions"&gt;    Remove from oven and leave on counter for two hours before use.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;/ol&gt;&lt;p id="zlrecipe-notes" class="h-4 strong"&gt;Notes&lt;/p&gt;&lt;div id="zlrecipe-notes-list"&gt;&lt;p class="notes"&gt;While you can load a slice of pie directly into the piping bag (as shown in the second photo of this post), I found that the pie piped better if I put it in a large mixing bowl and beat it for about thirty seconds to evenly distribute any moisture before loading it into my bag.  &lt;/p&gt;&lt;p class="notes"&gt;
Final Tip&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p class="notes"&gt;As you might guess, this technique works just as well with other similar pies, like &lt;a href="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/2009/01/inauguration-cupcakes-sweet-potato-pie.html" class="notes-link" target="_blank"&gt;sweet potato pie&lt;/a&gt;!&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div class="ziplist-recipe-plugin" style="display: none;"&gt;3.0&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a id="zl-printed-permalink" href="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/2010/10/pumpkin-pie-frosting.html"title="Permalink to Recipe"&gt;http://www.cupcakeproject.com/2010/10/pumpkin-pie-frosting.html&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
