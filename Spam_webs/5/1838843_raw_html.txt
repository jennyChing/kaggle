          <h2 style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://therapywithliz.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Sitting-in-Darkened-Room.jpg">
            <img alt="Head Held in Hands in a Dimmed Room Representing the Gloom of Migraine" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-294" height="241" src="http://therapywithliz.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Sitting-in-Darkened-Room.jpg" style="padding-right: 30px; padding-bottom: 5px;" title="Sitting in Darkened Room" width="209"/>
           </a>
           Ive tried everything!
          </h2>
          <p>
           Does this sound familiar? Do you suffer from migraine and have tried all the painkillers, the preventative medications, the herbal remedies, meditating, alternative health treatments and still are feeling intense pain?
          </p>
          <p>
           Perhaps some or all of these treatments have been effective in the short term but they havent taken away the pain on a more permanent basis.
          </p>
          <p>
           Psychotherapy can offer a further level of support.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The pain of migraine is real. There is no question about that. It is a sensation that ravages and incapacitates. At times you may be able to think of nothing but the pain. The body may go rigid, and the brain may feel like jelly (or you have brain fog). But what is migraine exactly?
          </p>
          <p>
           Many different kinds of explanations have attempted to explain its origin. It is:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <ul>
            <li>
             A Biochemical Imbalance
            </li>
            <li>
             Confusion (see my Blog post
             <a href="http://therapywithliz.co.uk/life-after-migraine/" title="Life After Migraine">
              Life After Migraine
             </a>
             )
            </li>
            <li>
             A Conversion headache, the explanation being that psychological distress is converted into a more manageable physical symptom (see my Blog Post
             <a href="http://therapywithliz.co.uk/not-another-headache/" title="Not Another Headache">
              Not Another Headache
             </a>
             )
            </li>
            <li>
             Caused by depression or it develops as the result of depression.
            </li>
            <li>
             A Grief reaction
            </li>
            <li>
             Caused by Personality traits such as neuroticism and perfectionism
            </li>
            <li>
             The result of Suppressed emotions such as anger, resentments, dissatisfaction and anxiety
            </li>
           </ul>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <a href="http://therapywithliz.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/White-Flower.jpg">
            <img alt="White Flowers Meeting as Minds Meet in Psychotherapy" class="size-medium wp-image-978 alignright" height="199" src="http://therapywithliz.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/White-Flower-300x199.jpg" style="padding-right: 30px; padding-bottom: 5px;" title="White Flowers in the Sunlight" width="300"/>
           </a>
           With so many possible causes for migraine, who is to say what can help?
          </p>
          <p>
           The good news is that while it is very common for migraine to have its root in unexpressed emotions, those emotions can be worked through in psychotherapy. Pain
           <em>
            can
           </em>
           dramatically lessen, if not disappear entirely. This is not to suggest that the physical pain you are experiencing is not real, is self-inflicted or is minor. Rather, the fact that physical pain and emotions are so intertwined demonstrates how the body and mind work powerfully together.
          </p>
          <p>
           Psychotherapy and counselling can help.
          </p>
          <p>
           Experiences can be unravelled. Headaches can be investigated. Emotions can be explored.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <strong>
            <em>
             During the first part of therapy I had won health, now slowly I was beginning to discover myself (Marie Cardinal)
            </em>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           For help exploring and resolving the roots of
