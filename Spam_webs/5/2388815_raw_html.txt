       <div class="module module-multi module-series inset ">
        <div class="module-title">
         <div class="title-inner">

        <div class="module-body">
         <div class="module-body-wrap">
          <div class="fragment-media module-member">
           <div class="media-body-wrap">
            <div class="media-title">
             <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 1 : Link" data-module-name="Related Links" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/earth/oceans/found-new-underwater-volcano-discovered-in-hawaii-140519.htm">
              Found! New Underwater Volcano Discovered in Hawaii
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="fragment-media module-member">
           <div class="media-body-wrap">
            <div class="media-title">
             <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 2 : Link" data-module-name="Related Links" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/earth/baby-volcano-island-eats-its-older-neighbor-140405.htm">
              Baby Volcanic Island Eats Its Older Neighbor
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="fragment-media module-member">
           <div class="media-body-wrap">
            <div class="media-title">
             <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 3 : Link" data-module-name="Related Links" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/earth/earth-madness-vote-on-nasas-top-shots-photos-140311.htm">
              Earth Madness: Vote on NASA's Top Shots: Photos
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="series-footer">
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        Lurking some 3.2 miles (5.1 kilometers) beneath the Pacific Ocean, a massive mountain rises up from the seafloor, say scientists who discovered the seamount using sonar technology.
       </p>
       <p>
        The seamount is about two-thirds of a mile high (1.1 kilometers), researchers said. Seamounts, rocky leftovers from extinct,
        <a href="http://www.livescience.com/15282-hottest-deepest-volcano-earth-underwater.html">
         underwater volcanoes
        </a>
        , are found on ocean floors around the world. The newly discovered seamount is about 186 miles (300 km) southeast of Jarvis Island, an uninhabited island in a relatively unexplored part of the South Pacific Ocean, experts said.
       </p>
       <div class="fragment-media secondary-image media-vertical video-over">
        <div class="media-hero-wrap">
         <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 1 : Image" data-module-name="left rail : linked video" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/earth/videos/the-biggest-volcano-out-there-video.htm">
          <img class="media-hero" src="http://static.ddmcdn.com/gif/videos/volcano1.jpg"/>
          <div class="over-main">
           <img height="38px" src="http://static.ddmcdn.com/en-us/news/img/video-icon.png" width="38px"/>
           <span>
            Play Video
           </span>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="media-body-wrap">
         <div class="media-title">
          <a class="event-click-tracking" data-link-position="Pos 1 : Link" data-module-name="left rail : linked video" data-track-rule="module" href="http://news.discovery.com/earth/videos/the-biggest-volcano-out-there-video.htm">
           Found: The BIGGEST Volcano Out There!
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="media-body">
          Scientists believe they've discovered the largest volcano on Earth.
         </div>
         <div class="media-sub">
          DCI
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        "These seamounts are very common, but we don't know about them, because most of the places that we go out and map have never been mapped before," James Gardner, a University of New Hampshire research professor who works at the university's NOAA Center for Coastal and Ocean Mapping/Joint Hydrographic Center,
        <a href="http://www.unh.edu/news/releases/2014/09/bp02seamount.cfm">
         said in a statement
        </a>
        . [
        <a href="http://www.livescience.com/47663-new-pacific-seamount-discovered-photos.html">
         See Images of the Newfound Pacific Ocean Seamount]
        </a>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <a href="http://news.discovery.com/earth/oceans/new-japanese-island-forming-in-pacific-ocean-pictures.htm">
         PHOTOS:New Japanese Island Forming In Pacific Ocean
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Gardner's team found the seamount on Aug. 13, less than five days into an expedition to map the outer limits of the U.S. continental shelf. They used a 12-kHzmultibeam echo sounder, which uses sonar to detect contours on the ocean floor. Late that night, the seamount appeared "out of the blue," Gardner said in the statement.
       </p>
       <p>
        The
        <a href="http://www.livescience.com/27498-red-sea-canyon-discovered.html">
         multibeam echo sounder
        </a>
        gave the researchers an advantage over other mapping methods. Low-resolution satellite data have revealed images for most of the earth's seafloor, but the technique is not advanced enough to capture most seamounts.
       </p>
       <p>
        "Satellites just can't see these features and we can," Gardner said.
       </p>
       <p>
        The researchers have yet to explore the effects of the as-yet-unnamed seamount on the surrounding environment, but these underwater mountains often host diverse marine life, such as commercially important fish species, research finds. However, the newly found seamount is too deep underwater to provide a home for rich fisheries, he said.
       </p>
       <p>
        Still, because the seamount is so far underwater it won't be a navigational hazard. The United States has jurisdiction over the volcanic seamount and the waters above it, Gardner added.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <a href="http://news.discovery.com/games/volcano-explorer.htm">
         Game: Volcano Explorer
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        "It's probably 100 million years old," Gardner said, "and it might have something in it we may be interested in 100 years from now."
       </p>
       <p>
        The group made its discovery aboard the R/V Kilo Moana, a 186-foot (57 meter) vessel owned by the U.S. Navy and operated by the University of Hawaii Marine Center.
       </p>
