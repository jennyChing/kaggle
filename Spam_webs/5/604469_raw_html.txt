             <header class="entry-header">
              <h2 class="post-title entry-title">
               <a href="http://www.growingajeweledrose.com/2015/02/rock-candy-experiment.html">
                Rock Candy Experiment
               </a>
              </h2>
             </header>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="entry-meta" style="margin-bottom:10px;">
              Posted by Crystal Underwood
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              Making rock candy at home is really easy and lots of fun! This activity is a beautiful Science experiment and a yummy treat all in one. My kids LOVED checking on their jars each day to see if the rock crystals had grown. Here is an easy tutorial for how we conducted our rock candy experiment.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: x-large;">
                <b>
                 Rock Candy Experiment
                </b>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-hTYuCmHi2Hs/VNZdbCWw1UI/AAAAAAAAkpA/5YjKVruv3aU/s1600/Rock%2BCandy%2BExperiment.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <a name="more">
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <a data-pin-color="white" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" data-pin-height="28" href="https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/">
                <img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pinit_fg_en_rect_white_28.png"/>
               </a>
               <!-- Please call pinit.js only once per page -->
               <script async="" defer="" src="//assets.pinterest.com/js/pinit.js" type="text/javascript">
               </script>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               {Follow Growing a Jeweled Rose
               <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/JeweledRose/" target="_blank">
                on Pinterest
               </a>
               for all the best kids activities}
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: center;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
               <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                <u>
                 Materials
                </u>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: left;">
              </div>
              <ul>
               <li>
                <span style="font-size: large;">
                 2-3 cups of sugar
                </span>
               </li>
               <li>
                <span style="font-size: large;">
                 1 cup of water
                </span>
               </li>
               <li>
                <span style="font-size: large;">
                 <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000UJ0Z10?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B000UJ0Z10&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=groajewros-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  Skewers/candy sticks
                 </a>
                </span>
               </li>
               <li>
                <span style="font-size: large;">
                 A jar or glass
                </span>
               </li>
               <li>
                <span style="font-size: large;">
                 A large saucepan
                </span>
               </li>
               <li>
                <span style="font-size: large;">
                 Clothespins
                </span>
               </li>
              </ul>
              <div>
               <span style="font-size: large;">
                <u>
                 Optional additions
                </u>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div>
               <ul>
                <li>
                 <span style="font-size: large;">
                  <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B007EMYD8M?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B007EMYD8M&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=groajewros-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                   Food coloring
                  </a>
                 </span>
                </li>
                <li>
                 <span style="font-size: large;">
                  <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0018AMYPA?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B0018AMYPA&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=groajewros-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                   Candy flavoring
                  </a>
                 </span>
                </li>
               </ul>
               <div>
                <span style="font-size: large;">
                 Note:
                </span>
                You can easily double and triple the above recipe. We wanted to make a whole rainbow of rock candy colors, so we ended up using 10 cups of water and close to 30 cups of sugar. As long as the sugar to water ration is close to 3:1 the rock candy will grow.
               </div>
              </div>
              <div>
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div>
               <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                <u>
                 Method
                </u>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div>
               <ul>
                <li>
                 Combine equal parts of sugar and water in a saucepan and heat until all of the sugar is dissolved
                </li>
               </ul>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DPOScProXnI/VNQqlOrZR3I/AAAAAAAAkm8/PMzxgOjFIX4/s1600/candy.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <ul>
                <li>
                 Then slowly add more sugar in small amounts until it will no longer dissolve in the water
                </li>
               </ul>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-mAM4WoWjQg4/VNQsQvLVxrI/AAAAAAAAknI/vL_0JhSdEt4/s1600/candy%2B1.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <ul>
                <li>
                 The water should start to look a little cloudy. That is the point when no more sugar is dissolving and the perfect sugar saturation has been reached.
                 <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                  Basically, you are creating a
                  <i>
                   saturated
                  </i>
                  sugar solution (a solution in which no more sugar can dissolve at a particular temperature)
                 </span>
                 <span style="font-family: Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: x-small;">
                 </span>
                 The amount of sugar verses water used should be roughly 3:1. Add
                 <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0018AMYPA?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B0018AMYPA&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=groajewros-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  candy flavoring
                 </a>
                 if desired, and then continue to heat the water until it comes to a simmer.
                </li>
               </ul>
               <ul>
                <li>
                 Remove the sugar-water from the heat, and allow it to cool. While it is cooling you can prepare your skewers or candy sticks. Cut the
                 <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000UJ0Z10?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B000UJ0Z10&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=groajewros-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  skewers
                 </a>
                 to a desirable size for the jars you are using. Then dip the sticks in water and roll them in sugar
                </li>
               </ul>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cKkUw1kwibQ/VNZUji-4onI/AAAAAAAAkn4/o1glo8K4HlQ/s1600/candy%2B50.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               </div>
               <ul>
                <li>
                 Allow the sugar coated sticks to dry. While those are drying you can prep your jar(s). Once your sugar-water is cool enough pour it into jars and add food coloring if desired. Then, once the sticks are dry place them in the jar(s).
                </li>
               </ul>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-JEX_Hvt_Re0/VNZVJuzl2SI/AAAAAAAAkoE/eu1o-moT4Hs/s1600/rock%2Bcandy%2B31.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <ul>
                <li>
                 You want to make sure that the sugar coated sticks are completely dry before placing them in the jars. The rock candy needs the sugar to grow on, and if the sugar on the sticks isn't dry it will dissolve in the water. It is also important to make sure that the sticks are not touching the bottom or sides of the jar
                </li>
               </ul>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-F4Juqn4yV7Y/VNZUjt1Pm5I/AAAAAAAAkn0/hZ0HKay5ctI/s1600/rock%2Bcandy.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                That's it! It is now time to sit back and observe the jars. Rosie and Jewel loved checking on their jars each day. This was what our rock candy looked like after just one day
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-bs7WM5j2Hx8/VNZV6KuPWAI/AAAAAAAAkoQ/d3R5XAZd-Dg/s1600/rock%2Bcandy%2B1.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                 After three days
                </span>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-gZ3n3NDjNWw/VNZWA1FQPQI/AAAAAAAAkoY/EfioQYeGXiY/s1600/rock%2Bcandy%2B21.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-BwmTC5hYwSU/VNZWGPQyUCI/AAAAAAAAkog/z49iDPfvkvk/s1600/rock%2Bcandy%2B8.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                 After one week
                </span>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.  My kids loved checking on their jars each day to see if the rock candy had grown!" border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3CvC2p-EFYw/VNZqoteosHI/AAAAAAAAkpc/YOqIX3rQISs/s1600/rock%2Bcandy%2B265.jpg" title=""/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                After a week we were happy with how much our rock candy had grown, but you could leave yours in the jars longer if desired. Once you are happy with the growth just remove the candy sticks from the jars, and place them on a plate to dry. Once dry you will have a yummy treat to enjoy
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <img alt="ROCK CANDY EXPERIMENT: A beautiful Science experiment &amp; a yummy treat all in one.
