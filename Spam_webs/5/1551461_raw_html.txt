     <p>
      Around for nearly every childhood, Lego is a staple of the creative kid. Few other toys give you practically unlimited options to build and play like Legos do, making them one of the most popular toys ever. The simple brick is ubiquitous, moving from humble toy to an art medium, an adult architectural model, and even a base for advanced robotics. Yet, even the tiniest bricks hold huge secrets that will make you stop and rethink what a toy
      <em>
       really
      </em>
      is!
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="LEGO_logo.svg" src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/24/LEGO_logo.svg"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <em>
       Via wikimedia
      </em>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       1. Legos aren't your father's plastic
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Today's legos are made from
      <span style="line-height:1.45em;">
       ABS, a
      </span>
      highly
      <span style="line-height:1.45em;">
       resilient and, most importantly, non-toxic form of plastic common in the industry. Lego engineers conduct high-level stress and flow tests on plastic samples, carefully calculating the exact shape and feel of every block. You may think that the plastic is melted down and poured into the moulds, but it is actually warmed to the consistency of dough and injected under hundreds of pounds of pressure, a process close to extrusion. The end product is
       <em>
        extremely
       </em>
       durable: Lego has produced over 400 billion bricks in 40 years, most of which are still in use.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="yfwBWE8.jpg" src="http://i.imgur.com/yfwBWE8.jpg"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <em style="line-height:1.45em;">
       <a href="http://pixabay.com/en/lego-brick-yellow-orange-red-90868/" target="blank">
        Via Pixabay
       </a>
      </em>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       2. Lego (technically) produces the most tires in the world!
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      If you take every tire made, no matter the size, and count it as one tire, you might expect the giants like Firestone or Goodyear to easy produce the most tires per year. However, they overlook the humble, tiny Lego tire, a miniature recreation of the full sized wheel. Lego pumps out 306
      <strong>
       million
      </strong>
      of these tiny tires each year, roughly 18% of the
      <strong>
       entire
      </strong>
      global tire market. Just don't try to drive on Lego's tires - they aren't actually rubber!
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="4335320868_7e4d9d7e73.jpg" src="https://farm3.staticflickr.com/2692/4335320868_7e4d9d7e73.jpg"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <em>
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/ishanmanjrekar/4335320868/" target="blank">
        Via flickr
       </a>
      </em>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       3. Lego lives in the world of high art
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Most people see bright Lego bricks as children's toys and immature fun, but more and more see them as tools for art! Shows across the world have opened over the past decade, showcasing lifelike art and abstract pieces constructed entirely out of Lego bricks. The most famous, a man pulling his chest open to reveal piles of unused bricks, has toured the world. Lego has even entered into the world of buildings with the launch of its Lego Architecture sets, aimed at adults and professionals. Feeling like the next brick Picasso? You can even pick up a set of all white minimalist bricks, letting you design your world as you see fit.
      <img alt="42530236.jpg" src="http://img.rakuten.com/PIC/42530236/0/1/500/42530236.jpg" style="line-height:1.45em;"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="line-height:1.45em;">
       <strong>
        4. In a few years, Lego "minifigs" will outnumber humans!
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      The common minifig, Lego's name for its tiny human figures, is as popular as the brick itself. That popularity has finally reached close to a spectacular hurdle: by 2019, there will be more minifigs produced than humans alive in the world! The
      <a href="http://xkcd.com/1281/" target="blank">
       always-perfect XKCD crunched the numbers
      </a>
      , confirming what we all feared: mankind has surpassed itself. We can only hope that our ABS plastic overlords take pity on us for all of those times that we crashed their Lego cars...
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="5491549442_6d1deaf61c.jpg" src="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5213/5491549442_6d1deaf61c.jpg"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/mac_filko/5491549442/" target="blank">
        Via flickr
       </a>
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
       <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
        <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
         <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
          <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
           <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
            <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
             <strong>
              5. Lego has spawned its own video game series, not even based on a movie
             </strong>
            </i>
           </i>
          </i>
         </i>
        </i>
       </i>
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
       <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
        <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
         <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
          <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
           <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
            <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
             <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
              <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
               <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
                <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
                 <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
                  <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
                   <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
                    <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
                     <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
                      <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
                       <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
                        Originally, Lego was well known for its hilarious, family-friendly games, based on movies and pop culture. Now, however, Lego is moving into games entirely existing in the Lego universe, drawing in a huge range of characters and missions and introducing a new world. It takes an incredible amount of effort and skill to successfully launch a franchise without any direct link to adult culture, but Lego seems to have nailed the formula.
                       </i>
                      </i>
                     </i>
                    </i>
                   </i>
                  </i>
                 </i>
                </i>
               </i>
              </i>
             </i>
            </i>
           </i>
          </i>
         </i>
        </i>
       </i>
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
       <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
        <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
         <i style="line-height:1.45em;">
