     <div class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Yallingup has been named as the country's top hotel holiday destination." src="/content/dam/images/1/1/t/l/c/e/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.11tgt6.png/1416896693520.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Yallingup has been named as the country's top hotel holiday destination.
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       Yallingup, 300km south of Perth, has been named as the country's top hotel holiday destination, beating out interstate hot spots including Byron Bay, the Barossa Valley and Mission Beach.
      </p>
      <p>
       And husband and wife-run bed and breakfast, Baudins of Busselton, was named as the top rated hotel in Western Australia and an overall 12th in the country.
      </p>
      <p>
       Linda and Greg Leuzzi were thrilled with their award which will partner their number one rankings from a host of other leading hotel search engines.
      </p>
      <p>
       "We were stoked. We took over Baudins over two years ago. Since then we've refurbished and added some extra luxuries into the rooms and upgraded with brand name products," Greg said.
      </p>
      <div class="ad adAlignRight" id="adspot-300x250-pos3" style="display:none;">
       <small>
        Advertisement
       </small>
       <script>
        titan.requestAd.push("adspot-300x250-pos3");
       </script>
      </div>
      <p>
       "I think people like us because we don't rush them. Check-out is nominally at 10am but we can still all be sitting around the breakfast table at 11.30am," he said.
      </p>
      <p>
       The 2014 Australian Reputation Ranking by online hotel search site, Trivago, also ranked another five WA holiday spots in its national top 50 including: Denmark, 4th; Margaret River 17th; Broome, 30th; Exmouth, 43rd; and Busselton, 48th.
      </p>
      <p>
       Other top rating regional WA hotels included Windmills Break, Yallingup; The Beach House at Bayside, Albany; Seashells, Mandurah; and Blue Seas Resort, Broome.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Beach Manor B&amp;B and The Richardson Hotel and Spa topped the list in the metropolitan area.
      </p>
      <p>
       Perth was the most-searched destination in WA and third nationally behind surprise leaders, Adelaide and Canberra. Within regional WA, Margaret River was the most searched followed by Mandurah, Busselton, Albany and Broome.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Trivago ranking only rates hotels with over 200 ratings and the survey was based on data from October 2014.
      </p>
      <p>
       Trivago spokesperson, Bianca Delbao, said that some people might be surprised that the big hotel brands did not feature, but that was a reflection of user experience.
      </p>
      <p>
       "The best of the smaller operators really personalise their service and that's what people remember in their reviews. That's why they top the rankings," she said.
      </p>
