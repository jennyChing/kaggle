          <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
           Song 58: Damita Jo  If You Go Away [originally: Jacques Brel  Ne Me Quitte Pas]
          </h1>
          <div class="byline">
           <time class="published" datetime="2015-03-01T20:25:29+00:00" title="Sunday, March 1st, 2015, 8:25 pm">
            March 1, 2015
           </time>
            by
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://everythingwithatwist.com/author/sunscreen/" rel="author" title="sunscreen">
             sunscreen
            </a>
           </span>
            in
           <span class="category">
            <a href="http://everythingwithatwist.com/category/animation/" rel="tag">

            <a href="http://everythingwithatwist.com/category/music/" rel="tag">
             Music
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <p>
            <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
             <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="390" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/XC3ThUuqNrc?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="640">
             </iframe>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             The song
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ne_me_quitte_pas" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ne_me_quitte_pas', 'Ne me quitte pas']);" target="_blank">
              Ne me quitte pas
             </a>
             was originally written in 1959 by
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Brel" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Brel', 'Jacques Brel']);" title="Jacques Brel">
              Jacques Brel
             </a>
             , a Belgian song writter who 
             <em>
              composed and performed literate, thoughtful, and theatrical songs that generated a large, devoted following, initially in Belgium and France, later throughout the world
             </em>
             .The song was adapted in many languages from
             <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qHGYGgRLY7Y" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qHGYGgRLY7Y', 'Greek']);" target="_blank">
              Greek
             </a>
             to
             <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HJI-Clnd5O8" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HJI-Clnd5O8', 'Arabic']);" target="_blank">
              Arabic
             </a>
             .Notable artists have been performed the song such as Nina Simone, Allison Moyet, Sting, Barbara Streisand andShirley Bassey to name some.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             TheEnglish adaptation was first made by
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rod_McKuen" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rod_McKuen', 'Rod McKuen']);" title="Rod McKuen">
              Rod McKuen
             </a>
             . Damita Joreleased the English version in 1966 inan album with the same name. I have listened to
             <strong>
              Ne me quitte pas
             </strong>
             in many versions, though neverfrom Jo. It is worth a try. Enjoy.
            </span>
           </p>
