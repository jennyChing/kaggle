        <div class="field-item">
         Built from Legos and running on Android, the Cube Stormer II is faster than any human at solving the worlds most confounding puzzle.
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="field">
        <div class="field-item">
         <div>
          <div class="right image">
           <img alt="" class="lazyload" data-src="/sites/ember/files/spark/rubix2.png"/>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p class="p1">
          Dubbed the Cubestormer II, the worlds fastest Rubiks Cubesolving robot drew big crowds in the exhibit area at the 2013 FIRST Competition in St. Louis (a robotics competition for kids ages 6 to 18). Built and programmed by UK-based Mike Dobson and David Gilday, the robot is constructed from four
          <a href="http://mindstorms.lego.com/en-us/Products/default.aspx" target="_blank">
           Lego Mindstorm NXT
          </a>
          kits, controlled by a tricked-out Android app running on a smartphone.
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s2">
           As reported by
           <a href="http://www.wired.co.uk/news/archive/2011-11/11/cubestormer-ii-world-record" target="_blank">
            <span class="s1">
             Wired UK
            </span>
           </a>
           in a November 11, 2011 report, 
          </span>
          The robot, in the presence of the editor-in-chief of Guinness World Records, shaved a massive 58.73 seconds off the previous world record of 64 seconds, which was
          <a href="http://community.guinnessworldrecords.com/_Rubiks-cube-solving-robot/video/497454/7691.html" target="_blank">
           set two years ago
          </a>
          . The time to beat now stands at 5.270 seconds.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/GK08y2MP_hw" width="560">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Read more about the FIRST Robotics Competition:
         </p>
         <p>

           Dean Kamen's Young, Robot-Building Army
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
