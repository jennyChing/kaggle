        <p>
         Paint chips are great for making all sorts of crafts and activities with because they come in so many colors and their thickness makes them easy to work with and easy for little hands to handle. There is really no end to what you can do with paint chips.
        </p>
        <p>
         I use busy bags with the younger kids and have made several with paint chips as they are free and due to our recent renovations, we have a lot of them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="7 Busy Bags with Paint Chips" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6674" height="600" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/7-Busy-Bags-with-Paint-Chips.jpg" width="450"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         Paint Chip Sight Word Match
        </h1>
        <p>
         Materials needed:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          paint chips
         </li>
         <li>
          permanent marker
         </li>
         <li>
          medium or large resealable bag
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="Paint Chip Sight Word Match" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6663" height="338" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Paint-Chip-Sight-Word-Match.jpg" width="450"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This easy activity is great for reenforcing sight words. I made ours with the sight words the kids are currently learning and can continue to add more. These are so easy to make and dont cost anything.
        </p>
        <p>
         To create these cards, take two paint chips per word and punch large holes in one of them. I used the
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002V15F24/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002V15F24&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FMM2BNR6EOFPF4PT" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002V15F24/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002V15F24&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FMM2BNR6EOFPF4PT', 'heart shaped punch');" target="_blank">
          heart shaped punch
         </a>
         again, but regular squares will do. Lay it on top of the other card so that you line up the words properly. Write lowercase letters on one card, through the holes made and uppercase letters on the other. Place them all into a bag.
        </p>
        <p>
         The activity is much more challenging if you use different colours so that the kids have to match them up based on the letters and not based on colour.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Paint Chip Sight Word Activity" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6675" height="450" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Paint-Chip-Sight-Word-Activity.jpg" width="450"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         Paper Clip Colour Match
        </h1>
        <p>
         Materials needed:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          paint chips in various colours
         </li>
         <li>
          paper clips in corresponding colours
         </li>
         <li>
          sandwich bag
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="paper clip paint chip busy bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6667" height="397" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/paper-clip-paint-chip-busy-bag.jpg" width="450"/>
         It doesnt get much more simple than this! Throw some paint chips and paper clips of the same colour into a sandwich bag and toss it into your busy bag box. Your child can then attach the paper clips onto their same coloured paint chips for colour matching and fine motor skill practise.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Paint Chip Colour Matching
        </h1>
        <p>
         Materials needed:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          paint chips (2 of each colour necessary)
         </li>
         <li>
          wooden clothespins
         </li>
         <li>
          glue
         </li>
         <li>
          large resealable bag
         </li>
         <li>
          optional: large hole punch
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="Paint Chip Colour Match Busy Bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6668" height="338" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Paint-Chip-Colour-Match-Busy-Bag.jpg" width="450"/>
         In order to create this activity bag, you will need to take two identical paint chips and cut a shape out of each shade of colour in one. I used a
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002V15F24/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002V15F24&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FMM2BNR6EOFPF4PT" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002V15F24/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002V15F24&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FMM2BNR6EOFPF4PT', 'heart shaped hole punch');" target="_blank">
          heart shaped hole punch
         </a>
         . Glue these shapes onto the ends of wooden clothespins and place the intact paint chips and the clothespins into a resealable bag.
        </p>
        <p>
         This activity is good as a more advanced colour matching game for kids as some of the shades can be similar and difficult to differentiate.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Paint Chip Color Sort Busy Bag
        </h1>
        <p>
         Materials needed:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          paint chips in various colours
         </li>
         <li>
          pompoms
         </li>
         <li>
          feathers
         </li>
         <li>
          medium or large resealable bag
         </li>
         <li>
          optional: tweezers for fine motor practise
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="Paint Chip Color Sort Busy Bag" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6670" height="598" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Paint-Chip-Color-Sort-Busy-Bag.jpg" width="450"/>
         This is a simple bag to put together. Just throw some paint chips and colored pompoms and feathers in a resealable bag. I added some plastic tweezers in case the kids wanted to use those for placing the pompoms on the paint chips. I also threw in three sizes of pompoms so that if they wanted to, they could create patterns with the sizes.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Paint Chip Color Swatch
        </h1>
        <p>
         Materials needed:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          paint chips
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001KKTDDE/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001KKTDDE&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FLLIIQXECT2UDNPD" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001KKTDDE/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001KKTDDE&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FLLIIQXECT2UDNPD', 'binder ring');" target="_blank">
           binder ring
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          possibly: small hole punch
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="Paint Chip Color Swatch" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6672" height="450" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Paint-Chip-Color-Swatch.jpg" width="450"/>
         I was able to find paint chips that already had a hole punched in the corner but if using paint chips that dont have this feature, punch a hole into each paint chip, thread them through a
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001KKTDDE/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001KKTDDE&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FLLIIQXECT2UDNPD" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001KKTDDE/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001KKTDDE&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FLLIIQXECT2UDNPD', 'binder ring');" target="_blank">
          binder ring
         </a>
         and it is ready to go! When using this activity, your child can find objects inside or outside that match up with the colors in their color swatch.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Paint Chip Patterning
        </h1>
        <p>
         Materials needed:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          paint chips in various colours
         </li>
         <li>
          glue
         </li>
         <li>
          medium or large resealable bag
         </li>
         <li>
          optional: shaped hole punch
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="Paint Chip Patterning" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6661" height="600" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Paint-Chip-Patterning.jpg" width="450"/>
         <br/>
         To create these, cut out shapes from paint chips (I used a
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002V15F24/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002V15F24&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FMM2BNR6EOFPF4PT" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002V15F24/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002V15F24&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thechaandthec-20&amp;linkId=FMM2BNR6EOFPF4PT', 'heart hole punch');" target="_blank">
          heart hole punch
         </a>
         in two different sizes but you could cut out squares or any other shape) and glue them onto another paint chip. It works best if the paint chip you glue them onto is a long solid coloured one. Cut out identical shapes and colours to enclose in the bag so that your child can recreate the pattern on the cards.
         <img alt="patterning with paint chips" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6662" height="450" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/patterning-with-paint-chips.jpg" width="450"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         Paint Chip Notebooks Busy Bags
        </h1>
        <p>
         Materials needed:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          paint chips
         </li>
         <li>
          white paper
         </li>
         <li>
          staples
         </li>
         <li>
          stickers and/or markers or crayons
         </li>
         <li>
          resealable bag
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Last year, we made tiny notebooks out of paint chips. The kids each made their own but afterwards, we didnt do anything with the notebooks until I thought of creating them into easy busy bags. I threw notebooks into resealable bags with stickers or with markers or crayons for the perfect little activity bag on the go!
        </p>
        <p>
         You can read about how we created our
         <a href="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/archives/2219" target="_blank">
          paint chip notebooks
         </a>
         (by clicking on the highlighted words) and create your own. The kids love them more than other mini notebooks because they are so proud to have made them themselves!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Paint Chip Notebook Busy Bags" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6673" height="300" src="http://thechaosandtheclutter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Paint-Chip-Notebook-Busy-Bags.jpg" width="600"/>
         If you are looking for other busy bag ideas, you may be interested in following my
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/sharlajk/activity-bags/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.pinterest.com/sharlajk/activity-bags/', 'Activity Bags Board');" target="_blank">
          Activity Bags Board
         </a>
         on Pinterest. You might also want to
         <a href="http://mad.ly/signups/101586/join" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://mad.ly/signups/101586/join', 'sign up for email updates');" target="_blank">
          sign up for email updates
         </a>
         as I will be sharing many of our simple and inexpensive busy bag ideas in the coming weeks.
        </p>
        <p>
         Busy bags are perfect for bringing on road trips, using in waiting rooms or giving to younger kids while you do homeschooling or homework with older ones.
        </p>
