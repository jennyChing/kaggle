                <p>
                 Welcome to the next post in our interior painting color series! Last time I talked about
                 <a href="http://www.mosaikdesign.com/2014/09/guide-interior-painting-color-palette/">
                  choosing an interior painting color palette
                 </a>
                 . We covered the color wheel, analogous and complementary colors, and how different colors affect your mood. Its worth a quick skim if you missed it.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Today well focus on what color you should paint your kitchen. Theres no one perfect kitchen color, but some are better than others. (The short version: White, cream, and light citrus and blues work well; avoid navy, black, and bright yellow.) Ill suggest a few kitchen color schemes based on what looks good as well as how the colors affect your mood.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <img alt="kitchen color" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2625" height="515" src="http://mosaikdesign.flywheelsites.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/red-brown-kitchen-color.jpg" width="700"/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 Lets start with three questions.
                </p>
                <h4>
                 <span style="color: #333399;">
                  <b>
                   1. What Color Are You Working Around?
                  </b>
                 </span>
                </h4>
                <p>
                 Most people have a dominant and/or neutral color in their kitchen. To figure out yours, look at your cabinets or flooring. (Or think about the new ones youve already chosen.) Theyll usually dictate the rest of your kitchen color scheme. If youre still struggling, just look at the biggest surface in your kitchen.
                </p>
                <h4>
                 <span style="color: #333399;">
                  <b>
                   2. Is Your Palette Warm or Cool?
                  </b>
                 </span>
                </h4>
                <p>
                 Now that youve identified your kitchens dominant feature, determine if its more blue or red. For instance, if your cabinets are bright white (blue undertones), a cream-and-yellow color scheme will look off. Youll want to use cooler hues like silver and black, with touches of something bright like red as an accent. Blues and greens are excellent choices as well.
                </p>
                <p>
                 If your cabinets or floors are a warm mahogany (red undertones), youll want to build a sunny color scheme. Pale banana yellow, burgundy, cherry tomato, and pumpkin are all strong choices. Cream, beige, and tan paint will look better than a chilly white. The same is true if copper, brass, or gold dominate your kitchen. Save cool tones for small accents.
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <img alt="kitchen color schemes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2612" height="251" src="http://mosaikdesign.flywheelsites.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/warm-vs-cool-neutral-paint-colors.png" width="604"/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <i>
                  Sources:
                 </i>
                 <a href="http://www.fancyhouseroad.com/2011/08/18/the-perfect-neutral-gray/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.fancyhouseroad.com/2011/08/18/the-perfect-neutral-gray/', 'Fancy House Road']);" target="_blank">
                  <i>
                   Fancy House Road
                  </i>
                 </a>
                 <i>
                  ,
                 </i>
                 <a href="http://www.seldin.com/blog/home-decorating-tips-add-color-without-paint/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.seldin.com/blog/home-decorating-tips-add-color-without-paint/', 'Seldin']);" target="_blank">
                  <i>
                   Seldin
                  </i>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <h4>
                 <span style="color: #333399;">
                  <b>
                   3. Whats Your Color Trend or Scheme?
                  </b>
                 </span>
                </h4>
                <p>
                 Now that youve figured out what your main color is, whether its warm or cool, and one or two possible coordinating colors, its time to figure out your overall color trend. The main three color trends popular today are
                 <a href="http://www.dtelepathy.com/blog/design/color-theory" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.dtelepathy.com/blog/design/color-theory', 'pastels, brights, and monochromatic colors']);" target="_blank">
                  pastels, brights, and monochromatic colors
                 </a>
                 .
                </p>
                <p>
                 <b>
                  Pastels
                 </b>
                 are calm, Easter-egg colors: light blue, chiffon yellow, and dusty lavender. (An extreme version is a baby nursery.)
                 <b>
                  Brights
                 </b>
                 are high-saturation hues that make an impression. Designers often use them in small doses or tone them down with neutrals. And
                 <b>
                  monochromatic
                 </b>
                 colors are all in the same family, just lighter and darker variations of each other.
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <img alt="kitchen color schemes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2613" height="574" src="http://mosaikdesign.flywheelsites.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/sherwin-williams-kitchen-color-schemes.jpg" width="700"/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.certapro.com/certapro-kitchens-palette-paint-colors.aspx" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.certapro.com/certapro-kitchens-palette-paint-colors.aspx', 'Source: CertaPro Painters']);" target="_blank">
                  <i>
                   Source:
                  </i>
                  <i>
                   CertaPro Painters
                  </i>
                 </a>
                 <i>
                  using Sherwin-Williams colors
                 </i>
                </p>
                <p>
                 So if your cabinets are a creamy white and your floors are warm wood, you have several paint options for your color trend:
                 <strong>
                  <strong>
                  </strong>
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  <b>
                   Pastel:
                  </b>
                  sunny yellow or coral
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  <b>
                   <b>
                    Bright:
                   </b>
                   tropical teal or a similar gemstone
                  </b>
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  <b>
                   Monochromatic
                  </b>
                  : tans that are a lighter tint of the flooring or a darker shade or tone than the cabinet
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <p>
                 If you have a hard time choosing paint colors, you can return to complementary or analogous color schemes.
                </p>
                <p>
                 If you remember
                 <a href="http://www.mosaikdesign.com/2014/09/guide-interior-painting-color-palette/">
                  from last time
                 </a>
                 ,
                 <b>
                  analogous color schemes
                 </b>
                 use colors that are next to each other on the color wheel. They
                 <a href="http://www.dtelepathy.com/blog/design/color-theory" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.dtelepathy.com/blog/design/color-theory', 'express']);" target="_blank">
                  express
                 </a>
                 consistency and uniformity. If youre new to kitchen color schemes or feel overwhelmed, analogous colors are a safe choice.
                </p>
                <h5>
                 <em>
                  Continue reading to learn about complementary color schemes
                 </em>
                </h5>
