      <h1 class="entry-title post-title">
       Portrait  Steve Jobs is Apple: UPDATED
      </h1>
      <div class="post-meta">
       <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author posted">
        Posted on
       </span>
       <a href="http://www.cultofmac.com/5350/portrait-steve-jobs-is-apple/" rel="bookmark" title="11:44 pm">
        <span class="timestamp updated">
         December 1, 2008
        </span>
       </a>
       <span class="byline">
        by
       </span>
       <span class="author vcard">
        <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.cultofmac.com/author/lonnie-lazar/" title="View all posts by Lonnie Lazar">
         Lonnie Lazar
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="post-entry">
       <p>
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/27498333@N08/2558355602/sizes/o/in/set-72157608304064272/">
         <img alt="" class="lazy lazy-hidden alignnone size-full wp-image-5351" data-lazy-src="http://cdn.cultofmac.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2558355602_680561655e.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="500" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" title="2558355602_680561655e" width="500"/>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5351" height="500" src="http://cdn.cultofmac.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2558355602_680561655e.jpg" title="2558355602_680561655e" width="500"/>
         </noscript>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Image 
        <a href="http://www.dripbook.com/tsevis">
         Charis Tsevis
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         UPDATE: With thanks to reader James of RetroMacCast, credit is due to the original creator of this and many other wonderful
         <a href="http://www.dripbook.com/tsevis/book/tsevis-mozaix-and-synthetix/">
          mosaix-technique portraits
         </a>
         , Athens, Greece-based artist, Charis Tsevis. You can download and listen to a podcast interview with Tsevis
         <a href="http://media.libsyn.com/media/retromaccast/RetroMacCast_62__Mosaics_by_Charis_Tsevis.m4a">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        Flickr user mic.imac has a fascinating portrait up of Steve Jobs, comprised entirely of artfully arranged Apple products. The portrait echoes a theme that runs through the upcoming
        <em>
         Welcome to Macintosh
        </em>
        documentary my colleague Nicole Martinelli
        <a href="http://cultofmac.com/welcome-to-macintosh-doc-now-on-dvd/5296">
         wrote about on Monday
        </a>
        , which is that the people who work at Apple give themselves entirely to the work of designing and producing the products the company makes.
       </p>
       <p>
        Of no one is that statement more true than Steve Jobs. As CoM founder Leander Kahney says in the documentary, Apple
        <em>
         is
        </em>
        Steve Jobs.
       </p>
