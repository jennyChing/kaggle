     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Quadrophenia
       </b>
      </i>
      is a 1979 British film, loosely based on the 1973
      <a href="/wiki/Rock_opera" title="Rock opera">
       rock opera
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Quadrophenia" title="Quadrophenia">
       of the same name
      </a>
      by
      <a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">
       The Who
      </a>
      . The film stars
      <a href="/wiki/Phil_Daniels" title="Phil Daniels">
       Phil Daniels
      </a>
      as Jimmy, a 1960s
      <a href="/wiki/Mod_(subculture)" title="Mod (subculture)">
       Mod
      </a>
      , supported by
      <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Ash" title="Leslie Ash">
       Leslie Ash
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Toyah_Willcox" title="Toyah Willcox">
       Toyah
      </a>
      and, towards the end of the film,
      <a href="/wiki/Sting_(musician)" title="Sting (musician)">
       Sting
      </a>
      . It was directed by
      <a href="/wiki/Franc_Roddam" title="Franc Roddam">
       Franc Roddam
      </a>
      in his feature directing dbut. Unlike the film adaptation of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tommy_(1975_film)" title="Tommy (1975 film)">
        Tommy
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Quadrophenia
      </i>
      is not a
      <a href="/wiki/Musical_film" title="Musical film">
       musical film
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Quadrophenia_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The film, set in 1964, follows the story of Jimmy Cooper (
      <a href="/wiki/Phil_Daniels" title="Phil Daniels">
       Phil Daniels
      </a>
      ), a London Mod. Disillusioned by his parents and a job as a post room boy in an advertising firm, Jimmy finds an outlet for his teenage
      <a href="/wiki/Angst" title="Angst">
       angst
      </a>
      with his Mod friends Dave (
      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Wingett" title="Mark Wingett">
       Mark Wingett
      </a>
      ), Chalky (
      <a href="/wiki/Phil_Davis_(actor)" title="Phil Davis (actor)">
       Philip Davis
      </a>
      ) and Spider (
      <a href="/wiki/Gary_Shail" title="Gary Shail">
       Gary Shail
      </a>
      ). One of the Mods' rivals, the Rockers, is in fact Jimmy's childhood friend, Kevin (
      <a href="/wiki/Ray_Winstone" title="Ray Winstone">
       Ray Winstone
      </a>
      ). An assault by aggressive Rockers on Spider leads to a retaliation attack on Kevin. Jimmy participates in the assault, but when he realises the victim is Kevin, he doesn't help him, instead driving away on his scooter.
     </p>
     <p>
      A
      <a href="/wiki/Bank_holiday" title="Bank holiday">
       bank holiday
      </a>
      weekend provides the excuse for the rivalry between
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mods_and_Rockers" title="Mods and Rockers">
       Mods and Rockers
      </a>
      to come to a head, as they both descend upon the seaside town of
      <a href="/wiki/Brighton" title="Brighton">
       Brighton
      </a>
      . A series of running battles ensues. As the police close in on the rioters, Jimmy escapes down an alleyway with Steph (
      <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Ash" title="Leslie Ash">
       Leslie Ash
      </a>
      ) a girl on whom he has a crush and they have sex. When the pair emerge, they find themselves in the middle of the melee just as police are detaining rioters. Jimmy is arrested, detained with a violent, charismatic Mod he calls 'Ace Face' (
      <a href="/wiki/Sting_(musician)" title="Sting (musician)">
       Sting
      </a>
      ), and later fined the then-large sum of 50. When fined 75, Ace Face mocks the magistrate by offering to pay on the spot, to the amusement of fellow Mods.
     </p>
     <p>
      Back in London, Jimmy becomes increasingly depressed. He is thrown out of his house by his mother, who finds his stash of
      <a href="/wiki/Amphetamine" title="Amphetamine">
       amphetamine
      </a>
      pills. He then quits his job, spends his severance package on more pills, and finds out that Steph has become the girlfriend of his friend Dave. After a brief fight with Dave, the following morning his rejection is confirmed by Steph and with his beloved
      <a href="/wiki/Lambretta" title="Lambretta">
       Lambretta
      </a>
      scooter accidentally destroyed, Jimmy takes a train back to Brighton. In an attempt to relive the recent excitement, he revisits the scenes of the riots and of his encounter with Steph. To his horror, Jimmy discovers that his idol, Ace Face, is in reality a lowly
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bellboy" title="Bellboy">
       bellboy
      </a>
      at a Brighton hotel. Jimmy steals Ace's scooter and heads out to
      <a href="/wiki/Beachy_Head" title="Beachy Head">
       Beachy Head
      </a>
      , crashing the scooter over a cliff, which is where the film begins with Jimmy walking back from the cliff top in the sunset back drop.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production_notes">
       Production notes
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Quadrophenia_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production notes">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alley_Between_East_Street_and_Little_East_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1116449.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="469" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Alley_Between_East_Street_and_Little_East_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1116449.jpg/220px-Alley_Between_East_Street_and_Little_East_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1116449.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Alley_Between_East_Street_and_Little_East_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1116449.jpg/330px-Alley_Between_East_Street_and_Little_East_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1116449.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Alley_Between_East_Street_and_Little_East_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1116449.jpg/440px-Alley_Between_East_Street_and_Little_East_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1116449.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alley_Between_East_Street_and_Little_East_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1116449.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Alley between East Street and Little East Street, Brighton, England, made famous as the location where Jimmy Cooper and Steph have sex in
        <i>
         Quadrophenia
        </i>
        . The small yard is to the right of the "Chris from Margate" graffiti, a spot often visited and left with graffiti to mark that visit. Turning left will take you to East Street.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Several references to
      <a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">
       The Who
      </a>
      appear throughout the film, including an anachronistic inclusion of a repackaged Who album that was not available at the time, a clip of the band performing "
      <a href="/wiki/Anyway,_Anyhow,_Anywhere" title="Anyway, Anyhow, Anywhere">
       Anyway, Anyhow, Anywhere
      </a>
      " on the TV series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Ready_Steady_Go!" title="Ready Steady Go!">
        Ready Steady Go!
       </a>
      </i>
      , pictures of the band and a "Maximum R&amp;B" poster in Jimmy's bedroom, and the inclusion of "
      <a href="/wiki/My_Generation" title="My Generation">
       My Generation
      </a>
      " during a party gatecrashing scene. The film was almost cancelled when
      <a href="/wiki/Keith_Moon" title="Keith Moon">
       Keith Moon
      </a>
      , the drummer for The Who, died, but in the words of Roddam, the producers, Roy Baird and Bill Curbishley, "held it together" and the film was made.
     </p>
     <p>
      Only one scene in the whole film was shot in the studio; all others were on location.
      <a href="/wiki/Beachy_Head" title="Beachy Head">
       Beachy Head
      </a>
      , where Jimmy may or may not have tried to kill himself at the end of the film, was the location of a real-life suicide that supposedly influenced the film's ending.
     </p>
     <p>
      The stunt coordinators underestimated the distance that the scooter would fly through the air after being driven off Beachy Head. Franc Roddam, who shot the scene from a helicopter, was almost hit.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeff_Dexter_(DJ)" title="Jeff Dexter (DJ)">
       Jeff Dexter
      </a>
      , a club dancer and disc jockey fixture in the Sixties London music scene was the DJ in the club scenes, and was the uncredited choreographer of 500 extras for the ballroom and club scenes. He also choreographed Sting's feet in his dance close-ups. Dexter managed
      <a href="/wiki/America_(band)" title="America (band)">
       America
      </a>
      whose first major gig at "Implosion" at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Roundhouse" title="The Roundhouse">
       The Roundhouse
      </a>
      , Chalk Farm, was the opening act to
      <a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">
       The Who
      </a>
      on 20 December 1970.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="DVD_releases">
       DVD releases
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Quadrophenia_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: DVD releases">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Universal first released the film on DVD in 1999 with an 8-minute montage featurette. It used the VHS print, resulting in a much lower-quality video than expected.
     </p>
     <p>
      Following this in the US was a special edition by Rhino, which included a remastered
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Letterbox" title="Letterbox">
       matted
      </a>
      wide screen transfer, a commentary, several interviews, galleries, and a quiz. However, it was a shorter cut of the film, with several minutes of footage missing.
     </p>
     <p>
      On 7 August 2006, Universal improved upon their original DVD with a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DVD_region_2" title="DVD region 2">
       Region 2
      </a>
      two-disc special edition. The film was digitally remastered and included a brand new commentary by Franc Roddam, Phil Daniels and Leslie Ash. Disc 2 features an hour-long documentary and a featurette with Roddam discussing the locations.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Unlike their previous DVD, it was the complete, longer version, and it was matted to the correct aspect ratio.
     </p>
     <p>
      On 1 January 2012, The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Criterion_Collection" title="Criterion Collection">
       Criterion Collection
      </a>
      hinted in their annual New Year's drawing that they would be releasing a special edition version of this movie, presumably on both DVD and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-Ray" title="Blu-Ray">
       Blu-Ray
      </a>
      formats.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This edition was released on 28 August 2012.
     </p>
