     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Republic of the Rio Grande
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
       Spanish
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
       <i>
        Repblica del Ro Grande
       </i>
      </span>
      ) was an independent nation that insurgents against the Central Mexican Government sought to establish in northern
      <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
       Mexico
      </a>
      . The rebellion lasted from January 17 to November 6, 1840 and the Republic of the Rio Grande was never officially recognized.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
       Background
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Rio_Grande&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      After a decade of strife, Mexico won its
      <a href="/wiki/Mexican_War_of_Independence" title="Mexican War of Independence">
       independence
      </a>
      from the Kingdom of
      <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
       Spain
      </a>
      in 1821. After a failed attempt at a
      <a href="/wiki/First_Mexican_Empire" title="First Mexican Empire">
       monarchy
      </a>
      , Mexico adopted a new constitution, the
      <a href="/wiki/1824_Constitution_of_Mexico" title="1824 Constitution of Mexico">
       1824 Constitution
      </a>
      . This new constitution established los
      <i>
       Estados Unidos Mexicanos
      </i>
      , or "the United Mexican States," as a
      <a href="/wiki/Federal_republic" title="Federal republic">
       federal republic
      </a>
      , similar to the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1833, General
      <a href="/wiki/Antonio_L%C3%B3pez_de_Santa_Anna" title="Antonio Lpez de Santa Anna">
       Antonio Lpez de Santa Anna
      </a>
      was elected to his first term as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Mexico" title="List of Presidents of Mexico">
       president
      </a>
      and was, at the time of his election, in support of the federal republic. However, after some members of government angered Santa Anna's political allies, Santa Anna decided to start a
      <a href="/wiki/Centralized_government" title="Centralized government">
       centralized government
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Santa Anna suspended the constitution, disbanded Congress and made himself the center of power in Mexico. This led to the eruption of a number of uprisings and secessionist movements throughout the country, the most successful being the
      <a href="/wiki/Texas_Revolution" title="Texas Revolution">
       Texas Revolution
      </a>
      . Less successful secession movements were attempted by the Republic of
      <a href="/wiki/Zacatecas" title="Zacatecas">
       Zacatecas
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Republic of Yucatn">
       Republic of Yucatn
      </a>
      . At the same time there was
      <a href="/wiki/Filibuster_(military)" title="Filibuster (military)">
       filibuster
      </a>
      activity in the country that sought to expand slavery in Mexico. Many of the
      <a href="/wiki/Caudillo" title="Caudillo">
       caudillos
      </a>
      that initiated and participated in the rebellion also participated in later violent secessionist movements.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Rebellion">
       Rebellion
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Rio_Grande&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rebellion">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      On January 17, 1840 a meeting was held at the Orevea Ranch near
      <a href="/wiki/Laredo,_Texas" title="Laredo, Texas">
       Laredo
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A group of notables from the states of
      <a href="/wiki/Coahuila" title="Coahuila">
       Coahuila
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo Len">
       Nuevo Len
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">
       Tamaulipas
      </a>
      advocated a rebellion seeking secession from Mexico and formation of their own federal republic with
      <a href="/wiki/Laredo,_Texas" title="Laredo, Texas">
       Laredo
      </a>
      as the capital. However, those states' own congresses and governments never took any action to support the insurgents, and requested the help of the Central government in
      <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">
       Mexico City
      </a>
      to aid the local state armies.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Texas_Mexico_Disputed_Border_1836_to_1848.PNG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="521" data-file-width="460" height="340" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2d/Texas_Mexico_Disputed_Border_1836_to_1848.PNG/300px-Texas_Mexico_Disputed_Border_1836_to_1848.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2d/Texas_Mexico_Disputed_Border_1836_to_1848.PNG/450px-Texas_Mexico_Disputed_Border_1836_to_1848.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/2d/Texas_Mexico_Disputed_Border_1836_to_1848.PNG 2x" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Texas_Mexico_Disputed_Border_1836_to_1848.PNG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The Republic of Rio Grande was a brief attempt to create an independent nation inside northern Mexico. The insurgency lasted from January 17 to November 6, 1840.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The insurgents designated their own officials. They were:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_de_C%C3%A1rdenas" title="Jess de Crdenas">
        Jess de Crdenas
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">
        president
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Canales_Rosillo" title="Antonio Canales Rosillo">
        Antonio Canales
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Commander-in-chief" title="Commander-in-chief">
        commander-in-chief
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Army" title="Army">
        army
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Nepomuceno_Molano&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Juan Nepomuceno Molano (page does not exist)">
        Juan Nepomuceno Molano
       </a>
       , council representative for Tamaulipas.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Francisco_Vidaurri_y_Villase%C3%B1or&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Francisco Vidaurri y Villaseor (page does not exist)">
        Francisco Vidaurri y Villaseor
       </a>
       , council representative for Coahuila.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Mar%C3%ADa_de_Llano" title="Manuel Mara de Llano">
        Manuel Mara de Llano
       </a>
       , council representative for Nuevo Len.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Jes%C3%BAs_Carbajal" title="Jos Mara Jess Carbajal">
        Jos Mara Jess Carbajal
       </a>
       , council secretary.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      After the meeting, the insurgents temporarily moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Nueva_Ciudad_Guerrero" title="Nueva Ciudad Guerrero">
       Guerrero
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">
       Tamaulipas
      </a>
      , for security reasons. After the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Morales_(1840)" title="Battle of Morales (1840)">
       Battle of Morales
      </a>
      in March 1840, the government moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_Texas" title="Victoria, Texas">
       Victoria
      </a>
      inside the
      <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Texas" title="Republic of Texas">
       Republic of Texas
      </a>
      , where it remained until the rebellion collapsed later that year.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Insurgent_operations">
       Insurgent operations
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

       </span>

     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rio_Grande_Republic_Capitol.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2128" data-file-width="2848" height="187" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Rio_Grande_Republic_Capitol.jpg/250px-Rio_Grande_Republic_Capitol.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Rio_Grande_Republic_Capitol.jpg/375px-Rio_Grande_Republic_Capitol.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Rio_Grande_Republic_Capitol.jpg/500px-Rio_Grande_Republic_Capitol.jpg 2x" width="250"/>
       </a>

        <div class="magnify">

         </a>
        </div>
        This building, the capitol of the Republic of the Rio Grande, is now the
        <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Rio_Grande_Museum" title="Republic of the Rio Grande Museum">
         Republic of the Rio Grande Museum
        </a>
        in
        <a href="/wiki/Laredo,_Texas" title="Laredo, Texas">

        . It showcases the history of the rebellion and the restoration of three rooms in a period
        <a href="/wiki/Hacienda" title="Hacienda">

        .

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_Santa_Rita_de_Morelos_.28March_24.E2.80.9325.2C_1840.29">
       Battle of Santa Rita de Morelos (March 2425, 1840)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Rio_Grande&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Battle of Santa Rita de Morelos (March 2425, 1840)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Santa_Rita_de_Morelos" title="Battle of Santa Rita de Morelos">
       Battle of Santa Rita de Morelos
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The respective armies of the insurgents General Antonio Canales and Mexican General
      <a href="/wiki/Mariano_Arista" title="Mariano Arista">
       Mariano Arista
      </a>
      met at Morales, Coahuila, on March 24 and 25 1840. The Centralist Mexican forces defeated the insurgent forces. Included in this defeat was the trial and execution of 23 members of the insurgents' cavalry, including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colonel_Jose_Antonio_de_Zapata" title="Colonel Jose Antonio de Zapata">
       Colonel Jose Antonio de Zapata
      </a>
      , the commander of the cavalry, on March 29.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      General Canales and the remaining insurgents that survived the Battle of Morales sought refuge in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Antonio,_Texas" title="San Antonio, Texas">
       San Antonio
      </a>
      , Texas.
     </p>
     <p>
      General
      <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Canales_Rosillo" title="Antonio Canales Rosillo">
       Antonio Canales Rosillo
      </a>
      traveled throughout Texas to build awareness of the rebellion for which he now fought. He also toured Texas to ask
      <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
       Texans
      </a>
      to volunteer to fight his cause.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_Saltillo_.28October_25.2C_1840.29">
       Battle of Saltillo (October 25, 1840)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Rio_Grande&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Battle of Saltillo (October 25, 1840)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Saltillo" title="Battle of Saltillo">
       Battle of Saltillo
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      General Canales was in
      <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">
       Austin
      </a>
      the second half of April 1840, where he met with President
      <a href="/wiki/Mirabeau_B._Lamar" title="Mirabeau B. Lamar">
       Mirabeau B. Lamar
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is said that President Lamar privately supported the new republics actions, but felt that public endorsement would hamper Texas efforts to secure recognition of its own independence from Mexico. General Canales left Austin for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">
       Houston
      </a>
      on May 2 to continue to build awareness and seek volunteers.
     </p>
     <p>
      While official recognition from the Republic of Texas was not obtained, General Canales' tour was met with some success. On June 1, he arrived in
      <a href="/wiki/San_Patricio,_Texas" title="San Patricio, Texas">
       San Patricio
      </a>
      , where the rebellion forces had been reorganizing. In addition to 300 volunteers, the army had grown to include 140 Texan and 80 Native American volunteers. The Texan volunteers were led by Colonel
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_W._Jordan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel W. Jordan (page does not exist)">
       Samuel W. Jordan
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      General Canales sent Colonel Jordan and approximately 90 Texan volunteers south into disputed territory in late June; they crossed the
      <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande" title="Rio Grande">
       Rio Grande
      </a>
      , and took
      <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Victoria" title="Ciudad Victoria">
       Ciudad Victoria
      </a>
      , the capital of
      <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">
       Tamaulipas
      </a>
      , without any resistance. A few of those who were guiding Colonel Jordan were still loyal to the Centralist government of Mexico and were trying to lead the Texans towards
      <a href="/wiki/San_Luis_Potos%C3%AD" title="San Luis Potos">
       San Luis Potos
      </a>
      , where a Mexican ambush awaited. Colonel Jordan suspected such and diverted his troops to
      <a href="/wiki/Saltillo" title="Saltillo">
       Saltillo
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Coahuila" title="Coahuila">
       Coahuila
      </a>
      , where, on October 25, he was attacked by Mexican General
      <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A1fael_V%C3%A1squez_(Mexican_general)" title="Rfael Vsquez (Mexican general)">
       Rfael Vsquez
      </a>
      . Despite the fact that part of his troops deserted, Colonel Jordan was still able to defend himself and return to Texas.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEAnonymous2014_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAnonymous2014-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <p>
      In November, representatives of Generals Canales and Arista met to discuss the war. During this meeting, the Mexican government offered General Canales the position of brigadier general in the Mexican army in exchange for his abandoning the cause of the rebellion. General Canales accepted the offer on November 6. Upon this event, the Republic of the Rio Grande rebellion failed.

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_republic_as_a_tourist_attraction">
       The republic as a tourist attraction
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Rio_Grande&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The republic as a tourist attraction">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Remnants of the republic's effect can be seen in:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Zapata_County,_Texas" title="Zapata County, Texas">
        Zapata County, Texas
       </a>
       , as well as the city of
       <a href="/wiki/Zapata,_Texas" title="Zapata, Texas">
        Zapata
       </a>
       are named in honor of Republic of the Rio Grande cavalry commander,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colonel_Jose_Antonio_de_Zapata" title="Colonel Jose Antonio de Zapata">
        Colonel Jose Antonio de Zapata
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Rio_Grande_Capitol_Building_Museum" title="Republic of the Rio Grande Capitol Building Museum">
        Republic of the Rio Grande Capitol Building Museum
       </a>
       is located in
       <a href="/wiki/Laredo,_Texas" title="Laredo, Texas">
        Laredo, Texas
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       As an addition to the historical
       <a href="/wiki/Six_flags_over_Texas" title="Six flags over Texas">
        six flags over Texas
       </a>
       , the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Laredo_Morning_Times" title="Laredo Morning Times">
         Laredo Morning Times
        </a>
       </i>
       newspaper adds a seventh flag to its banner: the flag for the Republic of the Rio Grande.
      </li>
     </ul>
