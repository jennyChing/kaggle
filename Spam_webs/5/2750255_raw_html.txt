             <h1>
              Benjamin Netanyahu granted two-week extension on forming government
             </h1>
            </header>
            <footer class="meta">
             <p class="byline">
              By
              <span class="vcard">

          <p>
           Israels president has granted Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu a 14-day extension to form a new government.
          </p>
          <p>
           Four weeks ago, Netanyahu and his Likud Party experienced resounding electoral success, winning the largest number of seats of any party and securing a mandate from President Reuven Rivlin to form the next governing coalition. But in 28 days of negotiations, Netanyahu has been unable to reach an agreement with his so-called natural allies on the right  two ultra-Orthodox parties, two far-right parties, and the center-right party, Kulanu.
          </p>
          <p class="story-continued">
           Story Continued Below
          </p>
          <div class="story-interrupt format-s pos-alpha predetermined">
           <div class="interrupt-item ad">
            <div class="ad-slot orient-vertical" id="core-twentyten-story_pol-05">
            </div>
           </div>
           <!--/.interrupt-item-->
          </div>
          <p>
           We have moved forward and we are on the way but I need additional time so that it will be a stable government, Netanyahu said Monday, according to
           <a href="http://news.yahoo.com/israels-rivlin-gives-netanyahu-extra-14-days-form-074326676.html" target="_blank">
            AFP
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           These sorts of extensions are by no means unusual in Israeli politics.
          </p>
          <p>
           Recently, rumors have circulated that the center-left Zionist Union, the second largest party after election day, might take part in a national unity government with Netanyahus Likud. Zionist Union leader Isaac Herzog has denied these rumors and restated his partys intention to join the opposition.
          </p>
          <p>
           According to the
           <a href="http://www.timesofisrael.com/pm-to-request-14-day-extension-to-form-government/" target="_blank">
            Times of Israel
           </a>
           , though, Herzog has not entirely dismissed the possibility of entering a coalition with Netanyahus Likud.
          </p>
