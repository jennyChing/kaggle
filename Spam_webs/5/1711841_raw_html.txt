                     </div>
                     <div style="color: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.701961); font-family: UICTFontTextStyleBody; -webkit-composition-fill-color: rgba(130, 98, 83, 0.0980392); text-decoration: -webkit-letterpress;">

                      I also don't use major seasoning. I tend to stick with a tiny bit of salt and pepper, minced garlic, and lemon juice.
                     </div>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://qtcblog.blogspot.com/2013/09/johnson-johnson-infant-motrin-recall.html">
                      Infant Motrin Recall
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2295699295707766962" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     Johnson &amp; Johnson is recalling 200,000 bottles of Motrin Infants (1/2 ounce) 
formula due to the risk that they contain tiny plastic particles.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-PUSUApC0dP0/UiuIwMvjLGI/AAAAAAAAAtY/f7tPgaiHFAU/s1600/babymotrindropsRecall.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="306" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-PUSUApC0dP0/UiuIwMvjLGI/AAAAAAAAAtY/f7tPgaiHFAU/s320/babymotrindropsRecall.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <blockquote class="tr_bq">
                      <b>
                       FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
                      </b>
                      - September 6, 2013 - McNeil 
Consumer Healthcare Division of McNEIL-PPC, Inc. (McNeil) is 
voluntarily recalling at the retail level three lots, approximately 
200,000 bottles, of Concentrated MOTRIN Infants Drops Original Berry 
Flavor 1/2 fl oz bottles distributed in the United States (see full 
product list below). This recall is being conducted with the knowledge 
of the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). McNeil is asking 
retailers to remove the affected lots from store shelves, and is asking 
consumers to stop using and dispose of any product they may have that is
 included in this recall.
                     </blockquote>
                     <blockquote class="tr_bq">
                      After releasing these three lots of Concentrated MOTRIN Infants Drops 
Original Berry Flavor 1/2 fl oz into the market, tiny plastic particles 
(approximately 1 mm in size or about the size of a poppy seed) were 
identified in a different product lot during manufacturing. This lot was
 not released to the market. It was determined that the particles 
originated in a shipment from a third party supplier of ibuprofen, the 
active ingredient in Concentrated MOTRIN Infants Drops Original Berry 
Flavor 1/2 fl oz. Out of an abundance of caution, McNeil is voluntarily 
recalling the three lots released to the market made with the same batch
 of active ingredient. McNeil has worked with the third party to ensure 
that corrective measures are currently in place and are effective. The 
potential for adverse medical events related to the reason for this 
recall is not likely. Concentrated Infants MOTRIN Drops Dye-Free Berry
 Flavor 1 fl oz is not included in this recall. Childrens or Adult 
MOTRIN products are not included in this recall.
                     </blockquote>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://www.fda.gov/Safety/Recalls/ucm367550.htm">
                       www.FDA.gov
                      </a>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://qtcblog.blogspot.com/2013/08/diy-contoured-nursing-pads-without.html">
                      DIY Contoured Nursing Pads (Without a Serger)
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4720598527493975682" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     If you're like me, you like to save money (who doesn't?) When it comes to things that I can make myself, I definitely give it a try. Once our daughter arrives, I plan on breastfeeding. I have a few disposable nursing pads ready but I really like the idea of not having to buy any disposable or washable if I can really just make my own.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <u>
                      <b>
                       What you'll need:
                      </b>
                     </u>
                     <br/>
                     Sewing machine
                     <br/>
                     Flannel fabric, or breathable cotton. (I've used an old nursing blanket)
                     <br/>
                     A CD or a small bowl (to make the pattern)
                     <br/>
                     Scissors
                     <br/>
                     Pen/pencil
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ukVgslORG1Q/UgWOi-fMr9I/AAAAAAAAAsA/2qZ_1F59zEo/s1600/NursingPad1.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="427" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ukVgslORG1Q/UgWOi-fMr9I/AAAAAAAAAsA/2qZ_1F59zEo/s640/NursingPad1.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I've used a tiny bowl with a 4.5 in. diameter. I've also folded the old swaddeling blanket in half, to cut my time in half *no pun intended :P*
                     <br/>
                     I will be using 3 layers per nursing pad. You can add more layers if you think you'll leak through or don't want to change them as often and you can also reduce the amount of layers if you don't leak as much, but I think 3 layers is perfect since I will be changing them often and I live in Florida (heat!!!). Keep in mind, you need your nips to breathe.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-4h1D3Ei0vtM/UgWOcz7sNII/AAAAAAAAAr4/KnYoyGYm3T4/s1600/NursingPad2.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="428" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-4h1D3Ei0vtM/UgWOcz7sNII/AAAAAAAAAr4/KnYoyGYm3T4/s640/NursingPad2.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     To contour the pad, fold it in half and measure a triangle 3/4 in. across and 1 3/4 in. in length. Go ahead and sew a straight stitch across your marking (I've marked it in pink above)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-bxu0sughPW8/UgWOlZNejlI/AAAAAAAAAsI/ChiSlXiWb9Y/s1600/NursingPad3.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="428" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-bxu0sughPW8/UgWOlZNejlI/AAAAAAAAAsI/ChiSlXiWb9Y/s640/NursingPad3.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Once you have sewn a straight stitch across your marking, cut off the left over fabric triangle (I have marked in black to show where I stitched above, my white thread isn't visible)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-7Cl0C-nwNqc/UgWOrf8zr4I/AAAAAAAAAsQ/iv9gCIVu3tY/s1600/NursingPad4.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="428" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-7Cl0C-nwNqc/UgWOrf8zr4I/AAAAAAAAAsQ/iv9gCIVu3tY/s640/NursingPad4.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Like I said before, you'll need 3 layers (or as many as you'd like). You will need to do the contouring step for all layers. Stack the layers and use a zig zag stitch on the edges all the way around.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-YW7kbrO9l_k/UgWOsB7EMMI/AAAAAAAAAsY/yYKvGMmMxvc/s1600/NursingPad5.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="428" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-YW7kbrO9l_k/UgWOsB7EMMI/AAAAAAAAAsY/yYKvGMmMxvc/s640/NursingPad5.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     If you use a thin fabric, the edges might crinkle a little but I don't mind, I want my tatas to breathe (Florida girl). If you would like more protection you can always add some fleece in the middle layer. There ya' go! Free nursing pads *or much cheaper than store bought anyways ;)*
