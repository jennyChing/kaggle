        <h1 class="entry-title">
         100+ Recipe Substitutions  Replacements
        </h1>
        <h2 class="entry-meta">
        </h2>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         Im sure you have all been in this situation. Its time to cook and you have found the perfect recipe. Except for one tiny detail. You are missing one of the ingredients. Or perhaps you are trying to lose weight and want some lower calorie replacements. Many are adapting to Vegetarian, Paleo or Gluten free diets and need replacements, too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="100+ Recipe Substitutes and Replacements - thegardeningcook.com/recipe-substitutes-replacements" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14221" height="988" src="http://thegardeningcook.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/100recipesubstitutes1.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
        <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
         <center>
          <!--TBN BTF 300x250 pos 4 STARTS-->
          <div id="tbn-2023b47e-aed3-457b-85af-40aa870397e4">
           <script type="text/javascript">
            window.$tbn = window.$tbn || {"d":function(){}}; $tbn.d("2023b47e-aed3-457b-85af-40aa870397e4");

         </center>
        </div>
        <p>
         Ive been there so often that I cant count. But dont worry. Most baking and recipe ingredients can be replaced by substitutes without changing the taste of texture of a dish. And there are loads of other replacements that will fit the diet you are trying to stick to.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive combined some of my favorite substitutes and also some of those from my fans of the
         <a href="https://www.facebook.com/RecipesJust4U" target="_blank">
          Gardening Cook on Facebook
         </a>
         . (their replacements are in bold and red below.) Some of the replacements can be used for any cooking technique or recipe, others are better suited to limited uses. (I will show this in brackets if this is the case.)
        </p>
        <p>
         There is also a section at the end of the list which talks about healthy substitutes, replacements for weight loss and gluten free substitutes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its time to rescue that recipe!
        </p>
        <h2>
         <img alt="Baking substitutes" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14211" height="500" src="http://thegardeningcook.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/food-substitutes.jpg" width="500"/>
         BAKING SUBSTITUTES:
        </h2>
        <ol>
         <li>
          1 tsp baking powder = 1/4 tsp baking soda + 1/2 tsp cream of tartar
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tsp lemon juice = 1/2 tsp vinegar
         </li>
         <li>
          1 ounce unsweetened chocolate = 4 tbsp cocoa powder + 1 tbsp butter
         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 cup cocoa = 1 oz square of unsweetened chocolate
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tsp cream of tartar = 2 tsp lemon juice or vinegar
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup cake flour = 1 cup all purpose flour less 2 tbsp
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup self raising flour = 7/8 cup all purpose flour = 1 1/2 tsp baking powder + 1/2 tsp salt
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tsp arrowroot = 1 tbsp flour or 1 tsp cornstarch
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tbsp gelatin = 2 tsp
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000MGSJ5A/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000MGSJ5A&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thegarcoo-20&amp;linkId=J5O6AH4PWQA3U676" target="_blank">
           agar agar
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tsp lemon zest = 1/2 tsp lemon extract
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup of cake flour = 2 tbsp cornstarch and all purpose flour to fill one cup
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tbsp tapioca = 1 1/2 tbsp all purpose flour
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup self raising flour = 1 cup all purpose flour + 1/2 tsp salt + 1 1/2 tsp baking powder (suggested by page
          <span style="color: #993300;">
           <em>
            <strong>
             fan Linda Kelly Lee
            </strong>
           </em>
          </span>
          )
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h2>
         RECIPE SUBSTITUTES:
        </h2>
        <ol>
         <li>
          1 tsp cornstarch = 2 tsp flour (for thickening)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tbsp butter or margarine = 1/2 tsp of extra virgin olive oil (frying, sauteing or grilling)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 clove of garlic = 1/8 tsp garlic powder
         </li>
         <li>
          red wine = replace with equal amounts of grape juice or beef broth
         </li>
         <li>
          white wine = replace with equal amount so apple juice or chicken broth
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup of beer = 1 cup of chicken broth
         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 cup of brandy = 1 tsp brandy extract plus enough water to make 1/4 cup
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup ketchup = 1 cup tomato sauce + 1 tsp vinegar + 1 tsp sugar
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup mayonnaise = 1 cup plain yoghurt
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 cup Parmesan cheese = 1/2 cup grated Asiago cheese or 1/2 cup grated Romano cheese
         </li>

         </li>

          </span>
         </li>

         </li>

        <ol>

         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup powdered sugar = 1 cup sugar + 1/3 tsp cornstarch (blend in a blender in batches)

         </li>
         <li>
          1 tbsp sugar = 1/8 tsp
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0078DP5XE/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0078DP5XE&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thegarcoo-20&amp;linkId=WUJ7WXXPTMXT4CLZ" target="_blank">

          (affiliate link)

          1 cup sugar = 2/3 cup agave nectar
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup corn syrup = 1 1/4 cup white sugar + 1/3 cup of water
         </li>
        </ol>

         </li>
         <li>

         </li>
         <li>

        <h2>
         <img alt="Egg Substitutes" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14205" height="320" src="http://thegardeningcook.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/eggs.jpg" width="500"/>

        <ol>

         </li>

         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 cup applesauce (batters, breads, brownies)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 egg = 1/4 cup pureed tofu + 1 tbsp flour (for vegans)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 egg = 1/4 cup yoghurt (brownies)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 egg = 1/2 cup buttermilk (cakes)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 egg = 3 tbsp pureed fruit (cakes)
         </li>
         <li>

         </li>
         <li>
          1 egg = 1/4 cup vegetable oil (cookies)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 egg = 1 tbsp cornstarch + tbsp water (cookies)
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h2>

         </li>

          1/2 cup sour cream = 1/2 cup pureed silken tofu, blended
         </li>

         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup whole milk = 1 cup fat free milk + 1 tbsp canola oil
         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 cup butter = 1/4 cup baby prunes (dark cakes or brownies)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup butter = 1/2 cup buttermilk + 1/2 cup applesauce (cakes, breads, muffins)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup cream cheese = 1 cup fat free ricotta cheese
         </li>
        </ol>

         </li>
