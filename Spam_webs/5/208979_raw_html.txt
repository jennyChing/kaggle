        <p>
         The resemblance was striking. The long, stringy, platinum blond hair. The gleaming forehead and gravity-defying cheeks. The skin as taut as a drum.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fredric Brandt apparently recognized himself in Dr. Grant, a fictional character on the popularNetflix TV comedy show Unbreakable Kimmy Schmidt. Andthe parody helped drive him to despair.
        </p>
        <p>
         On Sunday morning, Brandthanged himself in his Miami mansion. The pioneering dermatologist, who kept celebrities like Madonna looking forever young, was 65.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the two days since his death, debate has swirled over how much of a role the TV caricature played in Brandts suicide. One of Brandts publicists said he had beendevastated by the show.
        </p>
        <p>
         He did not commit suicide because of the show, another publicist, Jacquie Tractenberg,
         <a href="http://www.miamiherald.com/news/local/obituaries/article17488187.html" target="_blank">
          told the Miami Herald
         </a>
         . The show didnt help. It was mean. He felt bullied. It was mean-spirited picking at the way he looked for no reason at all. But he suffered from depression before that.
        </p>
        <p>
         Brandt was one of the nations best-knowndermatologists, recognized as much forhis odd look  described as 
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/30/fashion/Dr-Fredric-Brandt-Cosmetic-Dermatologist-to-the-Rich-and-Famous.html" target="_blank">
          unnervingly ageless
         </a>
          by the New York Times andandrogynous on 
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bQwT5LxmAt0" target="_blank">
          Unbreakable Kimmy Schmidt
         </a>
          as for his decades of innovation with fillers and creams.
        </p>
        <p>
         He grew up in Newark, the son of candy store owners. He was a different child who took apart appliances to see how they worked, he
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/30/fashion/Dr-Fredric-Brandt-Cosmetic-Dermatologist-to-the-Rich-and-Famous.html" target="_blank">
          told the Times
         </a>
         last year. His parents died when he was young, and Brandt attended medical school in Philadelphia before opening a dermatology practice in Miami. He also flew frequently to New York to see patients. When Madonna began praising his needle work, Brandtsbusinessboomed. If I have nice skin, Madonna told the Times, I owe a lot to him.
        </p>
        <p>
         Brandt developed his own line of skin-care products but also boasted of being his own client.Ive been kind of a pioneer in pushing the limits to see how things work and what the look would be,
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/30/fashion/Dr-Fredric-Brandt-Cosmetic-Dermatologist-to-the-Rich-and-Famous.html" target="_blank">
          he said
         </a>
         . Would I change anything Ive done? I might not have used as much Botox, because you dont want to look quite as frozen.
        </p>

        <p>
         <script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js">
         </script>
         The dermatologist wasnt attention-shy, often appearing on magazine covers and at parties in Miami and New York. But people close to him said he had been depressed lately and that being parodied on Unbreakable Kimmy Schmidt only made things worse. On the show, Dr.Grant (pronounced Franff) is played by actor Martin Short.After a few seconds inspecting Kimmy Schmidt, the Botox-loving dermatologist offers to zapher already youthful looking face. Susan Biegacz, one of Brandts publicists,
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2015/04/07/us/dr-frederic-brandt-65-celebrity-baron-of-botox-is-dead.html" target="_blank">
          said
         </a>
         the dermatologisthad been devastated by the portrayal. He was depressed, another source close to Brandt
         <a href="http://www.people.com/article/unbreakable-kimmy-schmidt-not-cause-dr-brandt-apparent-suicide" target="_blank">
          told People magazine
         </a>
         . Did the show upset him? Yes. It was a mean characterization. He was a human being, no one would like that. It was making fun of him for the way he looked and it was mean and it was bullying. It was an illness, he suffered from depression. He always seemed like the life of the party, he would sing and rap his way through the day. It wasnt the only thing troubling him, it was just one factor, the source told People. It definitely hurt him. He was absolutely upset, who would want that? But that was not why he committed suicide. But it didnt help. Another Brandt client told the
         <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/confidential/fredric-brandt-troubled-crying-suicide-source-article-1.2175537" target="_blank">
          New York Daily News
         </a>
         that the parody had driven the dermatologist to tears.His nature was so sweet and delicate, he couldnt take it. Who cares if he had too much filler? the clientsaid. Im not surprised that a Martin Short spoof would upset him. He was depressed and had been for a while, but the Kimmy Schmidt parody pushed him over the edge,one of Brandts friends
         <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/confidential/fredric-brandt-troubled-crying-suicide-source-article-1.2175537" target="_blank">
          told the Daily News
         </a>
         . He thought, well, that was on Netflix, its not somebody making fun of you behind your back, its international. Whatever the cause of his despair, Brandt succumbed either late Saturday night or Sunday morning. A friend found Brandts bodyhanging from a yellow cord in the garage of his$1.5 million Miami mansion. According to a Miami police report, the friend wasstaying with Brandt due to suicidal concerns for the deceased. The police report also stated that a psychiatrist had been treating Brandt for 10 days for depression. Many of Brandts celebrity clients took to Twitter to express sadness over the death of the Baron of Botox. https://twitter.com/KellyRipa/status/585184339576627204 https://twitter.com/Cat_Marnell/status/584933983747715072
        </p>

        <p>
         Someseemed to be struggling to reconcile memories of the happy, singing doctor with his sad demise. It was a paradox underlined by Brandts own mantra.
        </p>
        <p>
         I want people to feel they havent given up on life and are still in the game, he once
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/30/fashion/Dr-Fredric-Brandt-Cosmetic-Dermatologist-to-the-Rich-and-Famous.html">
          told the Times
         </a>
         . The realistic thing is to look refreshed and happy, not sad, not fatigued.
        </p>
