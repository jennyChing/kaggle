      <section class="js-could-have-report-project js-project-content js-project-description-content project-content" data-project-state="canceled">
       <div class="NS_projects__description_section">
        <div class="py6 bg-white">
         <div class="container-flex px2">
          <div class="row">
           <div class="col col-8">
            <div class="mb6">
             <h3 class="normal mb4 mobile-hide">
              About this project
             </h3>
            </div>
            <div class="full-description js-full-description responsive-media formatted-lists">
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="1703886">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/001/703/886/4c1ab7eaef521f62961fa4d38ee95ef8_original.png?v=1393931078&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;lossless=true&amp;s=f0817dab6290fddeba1215bc89d903e0"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              To put it simply, Strife is a multi-player card game where players assume the role of a mighty champion looking to test their mettle in the World's Arena. This project has been stuck in my head for the past decade or so, but it's only been until recently that I've been able to get things into gear and get the ball rolling. Self publishing a card game, well, any kind of game, really, by yourself, is an extremely difficult task. After getting together an estimated price from a publisher, from an artist and a graphic designer, as well as running estimates on what shipping will be, I kind of sat there and scratched my head. I thought my dream had been dashed, but then I discovered the wonder of KickStarter!
             </p>
             <p>
              The goal of this KickStarter is to secure pledges to pay for art and graphic assets (i.e. logo and card template designs) as well as cover the publisher's minimum order amount. Tossed into the final total are the KickStarter charges, as well as the Amazon charges. This brings the total required minimum to launch at a whopping 65,000 dollars. Why so much?
             </p>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="1703894">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/001/703/894/8bef3a6bd823c8ec22b80a70f915508e_original.png?v=1393931153&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;lossless=true&amp;s=db3c1984ddc2902609c94e52a8a45858"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              Well, I have an answer for you. I'm partnering with Cartamundi, USA, to print this card game! Cartamundi prints many things, but most importantly they print Magic: the Gathering. The quality of those cards are bar-none, and I will settle for nothing but the best when it comes to printing this game.
             </p>
             <p>
              The main reason this is going to cost so much is because of the 
publisher's minimum order quantity of 1,000 sets of cards, coming to a 
grand total of 35,000 dollars by itself. 15,000 will go to the artist 
and the graphic designer, and the remaining 15,000 will go to covering 
all the shipping costs, and the other KickStarter/Amazon charges.
             </p>
             <p>
              Now that you know how much the project is going to cost me, I bet you're wondering what's going to be inside of the game, right?
             </p>
             <p>
              Inside of Strife, you will find:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               320 - 400 Cards, the final number is not yet determined
              </li>
              <li>
               Instruction Manual
              </li>
              <li>
               Glass Bead Counters
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              ...and more, if stretch goals are met. All of this is wrapped up in a fancy hard cardboard box which will act as carrying case for the game itself.
             </p>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="1703900">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/001/703/900/09c100842382a0c9700c18a5c6f1b1c1_original.png?v=1393931222&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;lossless=true&amp;s=aa68ef3e78a5a5895cf9a9f95abff30a"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              I like to consider Strife unique in many ways. First and foremost it is a multiplayer game for 2 - 6 players. Strife will have a few different methods of play, or, as I like to call them, "Game Modes", to keep the experience fresh and exciting. The main game mode is called Tournament of Champions.
             </p>
             <p>
              Tournament of Champions is a long game type where the players pick one Champion from the roster and one ally, round robin style. Then, the remaining cards in the set are shuffled up and divided evenly amongst the players into three separate piles per player. Players will "draft" cards from each pile until they end up with the same amount as before, and then they will craft their deck and prepare for battle.
             </p>
             <p>
              I went this route because I feel it would be an interesting shift from other sorts of card games, where you have pre-constructed decks of the greatest cards. This mode tests your deck building ability and when in battle, your strategic resource usage.
             </p>
             <p>
              At the end of the tournament, the winner is the last man standing. Congratulations, you are the Champion!
             </p>
             <p>
              The second game mode is called Free For All. You and your opponents each select a champion and an ally, and then shuffle the remaining cards, keeping the monsters in a separate pile. Each player then wages war against the other players and the monsters summoned each turn, earning trophies for player or monster kills. Trophies are used to purchase cards to lay waste to your foes. In this mode, your character can die between 3 - 5 times, I haven't worked out the final number, before you are eliminated. This game mode is not really complete yet, and is subject to change by release.
             </p>
             <p>
              The big thing, though, that makes Strife unique is the manner in which you play cards from your hands. Strife uses a system I liked to call "Sacrifice".
             </p>
             <p>
              Each card has a Sacrifice value, which will be found in one of the corners (final placement pending). In order to play cards from your hand, you must make a Sacrifice in any of the following ways:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Discarding a card currently in play
              </li>
              <li>
               Sacrificing health on your champion
              </li>
              <li>
               Discarding cards from your hand
              </li>
              <li>
               Discarding cards off of the top of your deck, or removing discarded cards from play.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              The Sacrifice mechanic allows the game to maintain a quick, frenetic pace. Battles in the arena are quick and hard-fought, and only one champion will remain at the end.
             </p>
             <p>
              Speaking of Champions... let's meet a few of them, shall we?
             </p>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="1703925">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/001/703/925/8dcba62b31e4fef72ae1eadf3c1a7415_original.png?v=1393932219&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;lossless=true&amp;s=271629d146c74c4ea3dd4b3f9f2de56d"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              This is Valaina, Ascendant Pyromancer. This fierce, fiery dragoness seeks the massive fortune that is awarded to the Champion who earns the coveted title of Lord of the Arena!
             </p>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="1703933">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/001/703/933/538cda2c40cc410d3302a62ed9483a4e_original.png?v=1393932323&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;lossless=true&amp;s=8747d28ec4f54da096f19390e4263b1c"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              This is Omnipotus, the swirling elemental comprised entirely of arcane energy. Rumor has it that Omnipotus was once a man who was driven mad by his lust for power, and was eventually consumed and domineered by the very magic he sought to control.
             </p>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="1703945">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/001/703/945/61828d0cfea77270d3bfada5f424af83_original.png?v=1393932622&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;lossless=true&amp;s=fdecf20a9268ab6c4b806f2070aaa286"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              This is Anoriand, a Grand Abolisher within the Church of the Sun God, Thalos. Anoriand wishes to battle in the arena to test his mettle more so than anything, as his religious ways taught him that material goods are of least importance to a man such as he.
             </p>
             <p>
              Wait... what did you say about monsters?!
             </p>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="1703947">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/001/703/947/8f539540a5100c47e82bc5764e0fccdf_original.png?v=1393932643&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;lossless=true&amp;s=b9bc8799a975135e9234c3c61a943696"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              This is the Infernal Machine, a creature from the deepest depths of the world hellbent on laying waste to all things living.
             </p>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="1703955">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/001/703/955/49ca5579bdc09c36310ec9b294072b56_original.png?v=1393933069&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;lossless=true&amp;s=1554d7c916e4802bd74806df7d046b5d"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              I am a writer, a gamer, a collector, a game designer, and music fanatic, but above all I am a man. I have been dreaming of the day where I will be able to make my own card game, and hopefully my dream can become a reality. Thank you for glancing over my Kickstarter. If you pledged, I thank you greatly!
             </p>
             <p>
              The video will be up in a few days. Also, all character art within this Kickstarter are my own characters, save for the last one, which belongs to the artist doing the art for the game. The text images are placeholder and will NOT be used on the final project.
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="mb6 mb2">
             <div class="mobile-show py2">
             </div>
             <h3 class="normal mb4">
              Risks and challenges
             </h3>
             <p>
              Some of the risks and challenges with the product mainly have to deal with the collaboration between myself and the artist and graphic designer. The workload might become a tad bit too much, and as such, we might have to push back the date even further, but I don't see this as being too much of an issue.
             </p>
             <p>
              Also, understand, these costs are just purely estimations done to the best of my ability. I may be off on shipping charges, for example, which would delay shipment on a few orders. However, this won't be an issue, either, as I made sure there is enough breathing room within the funding goal.
             </p>
