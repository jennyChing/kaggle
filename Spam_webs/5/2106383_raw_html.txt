       <h2 class="title">
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/mexican-layered-dip">
         Mexican Layered Dip
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="meta noprint">
        Filed under:
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes/snackfood/party-food" rel="category tag">
         party food
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes" rel="category tag">
         Recipes
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes/sauces-spices/sauces" rel="category tag">

        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes/snackfood/snacks" rel="category tag">
         snacks
        </a>
         Tags:
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/cayenne" rel="tag">
         cayenne
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/flat-leaf-parsley" rel="tag">
         flat leaf parsley
        </a>

        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/guacamole" rel="tag">
         Guacamole
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/jalapenos" rel="tag">
         Jalapeos
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/kidney-beans" rel="tag">
         kidney beans
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/mexican-layered-dip" rel="tag">
         Mexican layered dip
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/salt" rel="tag">
         salt
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/shallot" rel="tag">
         shallot
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/sour-cream" rel="tag">
         sour cream
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/taco-seasoning" rel="tag">
         taco seasoning
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/tomato" rel="tag">
         tomato
        </a>
          Kay @ 7:44 am
       </div>
       <div class="article">
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3585/3476254331_ddc4f4f300.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Nothing beats spending a Friday (or Saturday) evening vegging out on the couch. After Ive tucked my little guy in, picked up the roughly 7.342 toys scattered around the floor, wiped the peanut butter smudges off of my furniture and clothes, its 
         <em>
          me
         </em>
         time. 
         <em>
          Me time
         </em>
          usually translates to popping a chick flick in the dvd player, opening up a bottle of cold white wine and a big bag of Doritos.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tortilla chips have to be dipped. They
         <em>
          need
         </em>
         to be dipped. Theres absolutely no point in eating them unless you have a big bowl of
         <span style="text-decoration: line-through;">
          fattening
         </span>
         yumminess to dip them in. If its up to our Dutch supermarkets, Id be dipping my Doritos in a bowl of flavorless, artificially colored pre-made dip. I still havent recovered from trying their nacho cheese dip. It was traumatizing, I tell you. Ill never be the same again.
        </p>
        <p>
         So heres the Mexican layered dip I usually whip up. Its relatively fast, simple, so flavorful and definitely has the 
         <em>
          wow
         </em>
          factor if you want to impress your guests.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-3579">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ingredients:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/?p=2287" style="text-decoration:none" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           guacamole
          </strong>
         </a>
         <br/>
         2 cans kidney beans (16 oz)
         <br/>
         2 tsp taco seasoning
         <br/>
         1/4 tsp cayenne
         <br/>
         1 big juicy tomato
         <br/>
         1 small shallot (or red onion)
         <br/>
         flat-leaf parsley
         <br/>
         jalapeos (canned)
         <br/>
         8 oz sour cream
         <br/>
         salt
         <br/>
         tortilla chips
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Directions:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         A layered dip is a
         <em>
          very
         </em>
         undutch thing to eat. Not sure why. Dont windmills and Mexican sombreros match?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3090/2601321206_a9a72a5c0c.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive recently discovered refried beans in our local supermarket! It was shocking to see them there. Havent tried them in a dip yetand judging by how they taste, its not likely Imgoing to either. Sticking tothe recipe as Ive been making it all along. That includes 2 basic cans of kidney beanspinto beans are pretty much impossible to buy over hereIve rinsed about 16 oz (450gr) of them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3325/3476253995_40e3467be3.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         You can mash them by hand, but you know how lazy I am; I use my food-processor. Mash the kidney beans with 2 tsp taco seasoning and 1/4 tsp cayenne.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3658/3477061300_0372deb529.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Put the mashed beans in a bowl,check the seasoning and adjustwhen needed. Set the bowl aside.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3394/3477061180_06c8797ac9.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Get yourself a big bowl filled with delicious guacamole,itsthe ultimate comfort food to me. No need for me to repost a recipe Ive already posted, you can find my guacamole recipe right
         <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/?p=2287" style="text-decoration:none" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           here
          </strong>
         </a>
         . Its real basic stuff.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3402/3477060660_0198b47ba6.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Move on to the next stage. Big, red and juicy tomatoes definitely have a place in this dip. Im making a simple salsa with them. You can take the skin off, but I never do. I just wash and dice them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3545/3477060552_9ef514bd6c.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sadly enough; this is the only way for me to get my hands on Jalapeos since they dont sell them here. Pity me. Ive chopped a small handful of canned Jalapeos in stead.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3562/3476252061_06683aa4a3.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         After I found out I ran out of red onions (imagine that), I simply opted for a small shallot. It worked like a charm.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3552/3477059262_b8477e4b7e.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         And Ive chopped a really good handful of flat-leaf parsley. I would have used coriander if I hadnt thought it to be the absolute foulest taste in the world. Strangely enough I do like coriander seeds.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3342/3476250815_9b405130cc.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Throw everything in a big bowl. Squeeze the juice of half a lime in there and season with a generous amount of salt. Giveit a good stir. Theres my basic tomato
         <em>
          salsa
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3663/3477058748_2fd64f64b2.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Start layering your ingredients and work your way in, leaving a small part ofeachlayer exposed. Begin with the kidney bean mash, top with a thin layer of sour cream then add the
         <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/?p=2287" style="text-decoration:none" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           guacamole
          </strong>
         </a>
         and sprinkle some of the salsa on top.Serve with lots and lots of tortilla chips. My favorite bag isDoritos Spicy Sweet Chili.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3297/3477058630_85aa7103f6.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         This has got to be the best Doritos dip ever.Not easy on the thighs, but if you have to cheat on your diet, at least do it right! Whats your favorite dip?
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3640/3477058496_365dd583f8.jpg"/>
        </p>
