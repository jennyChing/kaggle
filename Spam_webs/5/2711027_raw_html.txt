             <h1 class="entry-title">
              How smart devices and BYOD can bridge the generation gap
             </h1>
             <div class="post-info">
              <span class="date published time" title="2014-03-13T10:44:10+00:00">
               March 13, 2014
              </span>
              <span class="author vcard">
               <span class="fn">
                <a href="https://letstalk.globalservices.bt.com/en/author/stevemasters/" rel="author">
                 Steve Masters
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
              <span class="post-comments">
               <a href="https://letstalk.globalservices.bt.com/en/2014/03/smart-devices-byod-can-bridge-generation-gap/#respond">
                <span class="number">
                 0
                </span>
               </a>
              </span>
             </div>
             <div class="entry-content">
              <p>
               <strong>
                By Steve Masters, VP Customer Innovation and Design, BT Global Services
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                BT has a history of innovation dating back to the 1800s; we have a reputation for developing a list of technological firsts. Most notably, the single-mode optical fibre  the hair-thin strands of glass that power our communications networks globally  is an innovation which now underpins almost every network-based interaction you have, including any phone call or credit card transaction you make, and increasingly, how you watch TV.
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Staying at the forefront of the innovation curve is critical for us. Adastral Park  BTs nerve centre of innovation  is where we manage over 4,000 patents, house around 3,500 research and development people from BT, and 500 people from our 45 high-tech partner companies, such as Cisco and Microsoft. But innovation isnt just about inventing, its about being creative with existing technology. Its no secret that when used effectively, technology can create a competitive advantage in business.
               <span id="more-12280">
               </span>
              </p>
              <p>
               Its a fact of life that IT is not what it used to be. Im not very old but I remember the days when youd go to work, clock-on and sit at a desk. In my first job the only tools were a Kalamazoo filing system, a phone and a pen with a terminal hard-wired to the mainframe.
              </p>
              <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 183px">
               <img alt="Steve Masters" height="177" src="https://letstalk.globalservices.bt.com/en/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Steve-Masters.jpg" width="173"/>
               <p class="wp-caption-text">
                Steve Masters, VP Customer Innovation and Design, BT Global Services
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               Since then, things have changed; these days, IT allows us to work anywhere, at anytime. Being able to work from home or on the other side of the globe allows people to be more efficient, flexible and it reduces travel costs  giving us a raft of exciting and worrying challenges to deal with.
              </p>
              <p>
               One such challenge is the range of ages that make up the modern workforce  2014 will see five generations in the work place; traditionalists are working alongside the young whippersnappers of Generation Y. Managing that blend is challenging as each of them have their own expectations of what their workplaces should be like.
              </p>
              <p>
               The concept of BYOD also presents its own challenges; most people have a smart device and we need to embrace this. The key is to exploit the fact that its a useful communication device which can be used to harness better collaboration. Its important to identify the type of user that you are working with and what they need and are allowed access to, so that you can set up and provide the right security levels and deliver the best experience to them. If a senior executive wants voice and video on an iPhone, you must be able to provide them with a consistent experience regardless of where and when they use it.
              </p>
              <p>
               IT and particularly the consumerisation of IT are now part of peoples every day social lives and not just tied to a single device. They have access to all of their apps, such as Facebook, Instagram and Whatsapp, to name a few. A tool like Google+ pulls together a users apps into one feed so that they have an effective way of communicating and collaborating. But at work, its typically very different; the users are locked down applications are silos and collaboration tools are patchy or non existent. The secret is harnessing the trends and technologies we all use in our social lives and deliver them within the business in a robust and secure fashion. Many organisations feel that they are a million miles from this but in reality getting the infrastructure right from the start is the best step you could make towards building the social enterprise.
              </p>
              <p>
               Find out more about
               <a href="http://www.bt.com/beyondyourdevice">
                BTs BYOD solutions
               </a>
               and watch our
               <a href="http://www.virtualshowcase.bt.com/?v=introduce-byod-into-your-organisation">
                BYOD demonstration
               </a>
              </p>
