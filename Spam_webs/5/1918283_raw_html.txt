            <h1 class="entry-title">
             <a href="http://www.logodesignlove.com/obama-08-logo-design-options" rel="bookmark">
              Obama logo ideas that werent chosen
             </a>
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta">
             <span class="posted-on">
              <i class="fa fa-calendar">
              </i>
              <a href="http://www.logodesignlove.com/obama-08-logo-design-options" rel="bookmark">
               <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2008-12-12T12:26:40+00:00">
                December 12, 2008
               </time>
               <time class="updated" datetime="2015-04-13T22:27:12+00:00">
                April 13, 2015
               </time>
              </a>
             </span>
             <span class="byline">
              <i class="fa fa-user">
              </i>
              <span class="author vcard">
               <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.logodesignlove.com/author/davidairey">
                David Airey
               </a>
              </span>
             </span>
            </div>
            <!-- .entry-meta -->
           </header>
           <!-- .entry-header -->
           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             Sol Sender, who led a design team for the Obama 08 logo, was interviewed about the project. Heres a look at the various logo options, with some of Sols thoughts.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Option #1
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-2.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Option #2
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Option #3
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-3.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              We did see the O as standing for something beyond just the first initial of the candidates name.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Option #4
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-4.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              There was an idea of unity that emerged, particularly when we began to look at the red and blue, and how they intersected.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Option #5
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-5.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              Before we even arrived at the final identity we were aware of developing a number of options that had an element that stood separate from the typography.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Option #6
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-6.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              We developed a lot of logos. Usually we only develop two to four, maybe five. There were fifteen, sixteen options, and we focused on three.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <h3>
             Three finalist logos
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Obama logo finalist #1
             </strong>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              This masking of different photos with the O shape became an opportunity to say different things in different moments The O could contain all these different ideas, possibilities and feelings.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-7.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-8.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-9.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-10.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-11.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Finalist #2
             </strong>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              There was a lot of excitement about this. People felt this was really something new, something different. It was a kind of populist expression  everyones excited about Obama, people are talking (but) it was a little too far out of the box. We felt that having a little more tradition in the mark was the smart way to go.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-12.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-13.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-14.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Finalist #3
             </strong>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              For me personally, this was always the one.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-15.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-16.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-17.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              Originally the stripes were kind of symmetrically expressed across the horizon, and as we went into final refinements we felt that giving it a little bit more dimension, a little bit more motion, ways to enter into it a little bit more for the viewer was a better way to go.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             <img alt="Obama 08 logo design" src="http://www.logodesignlove.com/images/monograms/obama-08-logo-18.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Its interesting to note that Sol had never worked on a political logo before. A nice reference for when that
             <a href="http://www.zeldman.com/2008/12/04/20-signs-you-dont-want-that-web-design-project/" title="Zeldman's 20 reasons why you don't want that web project">
              Russian nesting doll client
             </a>
             asks you how many Russian nesting doll logos youve created  dont worry about saying, None.
            </p>
            <p>
             Amanda Gentry and Andy Keene are two of the designers at Sender LLC who worked on the project.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the second part of
             <a href="http://www.vsapartners.com/news.asp?article=70" title="Sol Sender interview about the Obama 08 logo">
              Sols interview
             </a>
             , he talks about the viral nature of the campaign, and the many places the Obama logo appeared.
            </p>
            <p>
             One last quote from the intervew:
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              The strongest logos tell simple stories.
             </p>
            </blockquote>

             <div class="comment-content">
              <p>
               As a libertarian, I was heartbroken to see the Obama branding  it was near perfection.  I am confident that the branding effectively won the election for Obama.  I think the GOP leadership saw this as well, as there were numerous attempts late in the campaign and shortly afterwards to associate the imagery with Nazism where possible  see the seal that was used after the election.
              </p>
              <p>
               It was also interesting to see the play on the 8 and B  that would normally have worked, but I think a previous poster hit it on the head when he said that it could also be read Osama.  The names were close enough to be a disadvantage anyhow, and the play with typography made it worse.
              </p>
              <p>
               Finalist #2 is too trendy and cliched.  Look at the Charles Schwab Ask Chuck campaign  it already seemed outdated and old, the first time you saw it.
              </p>
              <p>
               I actually liked option #5, but it was very vertically oriented, and would have been a real challenge to use on the web and for many strongly horizontal media.
              </p>
