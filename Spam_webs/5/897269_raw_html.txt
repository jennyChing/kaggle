          <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
           New Music of the Day: The Rentals  1000 Seasons
          </h1>
          <div class="byline">
           <time class="published" datetime="2014-07-24T13:54:18-04:00" title="Thursday, July 24th, 2014, 1:54 pm">
            07/24/2014
           </time>
            by
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.fuzzyheadphonez.com/author/oso-borracho/" rel="author" title="Angel J Melendez">
             Angel J Melendez
            </a>
           </span>
            in
           <span class="category">
            <a href="http://www.fuzzyheadphonez.com/category/new-music-2/" rel="tag">
             New Music
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <p>
            <a data-imgid="9854" data-postid="fsg_post_9853" href="http://i0.wp.com/www.fuzzyheadphonez.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/The-Rentals.png">
             <img alt="The Rentals" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9854" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.fuzzyheadphonez.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/The-Rentals.png?resize=750%2C540"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            After 15 years of experimenting with one-off acoustic solo projects, ambitious mini-albums, dozens of rotating band members, and sporadic tours and overseas concerts,
            <a href="http://www.therentals.com/" target="_blank">
             The Rentals
            </a>
            are officially back.
           </p>
           <p>
            The brainchild of former
            <a href="http://www.fuzzyheadphonez.com/web-wednesday-new-music-videos-weezer-bleached-angus-julia-stone/" target="_blank">
             Weezer
            </a>
            bassist Matt Sharp, The Rentals released two albums in the 90s,
            <em>
             Returns of the Rentals
            </em>
            and
            <em>
             Seven More Minutes
            </em>
            , before Sharp dissolved the band to work on solo material. A decade and a half later, Sharp has a new record label in Polyvinyl and his third proper studio LP,
            <em>
             Lost In Alphaville
            </em>
            , in hand.
           </p>
           <p>
            For this latestincarnation of The Rentals, Sharp roped in some talented friends including Jess Wolfe and Holly Laessig of
            <a href="http://www.fuzzyheadphonez.com/saturdays-sweetest-sounds-new-music-videos-from-above-beyond-lucius-sia-the-head-and-the-heart-more/" target="_blank">
             Lucius
            </a>
            on vocals, Ryen Slegr of Ozma on guitars, Lauren Chipman of The Section Quartet on strings, and Patrick Carney of
            <a href="http://www.fuzzyheadphonez.com/the-10-best-albums-of-2014-so-far-spring-to-summer-edition/" target="_blank">
             The Black Keys
            </a>
            on drums. This isnt the first time Sharp has called on rock and roll buddies; in the past, true to their name, The Rentals have borrowed members from Blur, Elastica, Pixies, and We Are Scientists to name a few.
           </p>
           <p>
            Last month, The Rentals debuted the lead single from the forthcoming album,
            <a href="https://soundcloud.com/polyvinyl-records/the-rentals-thought-of-sound?in=polyvinyl-records/sets/the-rentals" target="_blank">
             Thought Of Sound,
            </a>
            a blast from the past that immediately connects the fuzzy power pop of the bands previous hit singles,
            <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wi-H6ohY37k" target="_blank">
             Friends of P
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VW15vdcNSSk" target="_blank">
             Waiting,
            </a>
            to 2014.
           </p>
           <p>
            Today we get another Moog-flavored, sweet and bouncy pop gem from Sharp and company. 1000 Seasons squeals with delight (as do the keyboards) while Sharp and the Lucius girls go back forth in perfect girl/boy harmony. For fans of nerd punk bands like Nerf Herder, early
            <a href="http://www.fuzzyheadphonez.com/we-were-there-ash-lathers-up-central-florida-into-a-rock-frenzy-the-hard-rock-live-orlando/" target="_blank">
             Ash
            </a>
            ,
            <em>
             Blue Album
            </em>
            Weezer, and of course, The Rentals, it might seem like not much has changed here with Sharp  and thats a good thing. On 1000 Seasons, The Rentals still make our heads bob in concert with the beat and our mouths sing along to the catchy, finger snapping chorus. Thats something we welcome back wholeheartedly.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Lost In Alphaville
            </em>
            is out August 26th on
            <a href="https://www.polyvinylrecords.com/#store" target="_blank">
             Polyvinyl Records.
            </a>
           </p>
