  <meta content="I had the good fortune of being exposed to numerous cultures and countless 
history lessonswhile growing up,and because of that I think I was given 
the opportunity to see the world through many different perspectives. Now 
in my early 30s, I continue to travel and expose myself to as much of the 
world as I can. Whether it's close to home or in the middle of nowhere in 
South America, there are cultures and traditionsworth experiencing and 
memories waiting to be created.

Colonia, Uruguay

When you first embark on a quest, whether it's one of curiosity or one of 
travel,you start to notice that there are two different breeds of people. 
The first of the two consists of thosethat liketo stay close to home and 
areeither afraid or haveno desire to experience the world beyond what 
they've become accustomed to. They stay within their bubble and if they do 
happen to venture out they appear uncomfortable and remain tethered to home 
in one way or another. If and when they travel,they tend to stick to all 
the typical sites found in a travel guide and never venture out to 
experience real local culture. If they're in New York they'll visit the 
Empire State Building, the Eiffel Tower in Paris, Big Ben in London, etc . 
. . and they'll eat at major chain restaurants which they probably frequent 
back home. This is an extremely boring way to travel and see the world, 
yet when these people return to their homes they will speak of their 
experience as if it were really exciting and recommend the same trip to 
everyone they talk to. The reality behind this kind of travel and this 
breed of individual is that they really aren't trying to learn anything new 
because they lack true curiosity and have no interest inexperiencing local 
life. They enjoy telling people where they've been, even though in most 
cases they know nothing about those places. Instead of exposing themselves 
to new experiences, theybring what they know with them on their journey 
and return home as the exact same person they were when they first set out.

The Narrows | Zion National Park

The other breed consists of the more adventurous types. This doesn't mean 
they're roaming around the globeseeking near death experiences, this just 
means they're comfortable being uncomfortable and willing to immerse 
themselves in whatever it is they're doing. If they travel somewhere new, 
even if only for a day, they live there for that day. They don't visit a 
place, they make it their home for the duration. People of this breed make 
new lifelongfriends wherever they go. When Icross paths with one of 
these individuals I immediatelynotice something different, yet familiar. 
It's as if we both were part of the same fraternity or grew up in the same 
town. Although we may have never met before, we feel an instant 
connection. The reason for this is because we're both part of what I'll 
call the Global Society. People that are members instantly recognize each 
other without saying a word. Even though we may have spawned from 
different corners of the globe, our roots are embedded everywhere. Some 
people that are part of this worldwide club may have never traveled a day 
in their life, but they possess an inquisitive nature and desire that 
expands their horizon beyond where the sun sets. It's always refreshing to 
stumble upon such individuals and share stories while at the same 
timeadding another chapter to your ownadventurous life story. When 
parting ways you both feel like you've learned something new and know that 
you've made a friend for life, a friend that will most likely relate to you 
more than most of the friends you grew up with. The Global Society is the 
closest thing you'll find to autopia, it's a place where race,religion, 
and politicshave no relevance.

Mendenhall Glacier | Alaska

Members of the Global Society are some of the wealthiest people you'll ever 
meet, regardless of the monetary value of all their assets or what they 
have in the bank. These individuals are a wealth of knowledge and 
experience. These people keep you humble and modest and constantly remind 
you to ditch the blinders and look at the world with eyes wide open." property="og:description"/>

        <h1 class="entry-title" data-content-field="title">
         <a href="/read/2015/3/6/the-global-society">
          The Global Society
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="body entry-content">
         <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" data-updated-on="1425704084630" id="item-54fa76b8e4b0297e020a36dc">
          <div class="row sqs-row">
           <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-b975703a3b1cf179ed92">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p>
               I had the good fortune of being exposed to numerous cultures and countless history lessonswhile growing up,and because of that I think I was given the opportunity to see the world through many different perspectives. Now in my early 30s, I continue to travel and expose myself to as much of the world as I can. Whether it's close to home or in the middle of nowhere in South America, there are cultures and traditionsworth experiencing and memories waiting to be created.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image sqs-col-6 span-6 float float-right" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_6_1430164690511_7554">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <div class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-below ">
               <div class="intrinsic" style="max-width:2500.0px;">
                <div class="image-block-wrapper " data-description="&lt;p&gt;Colonia, Uruguay&lt;/p&gt;" style="padding-bottom:66.95999908447266%;">
                 <noscript>
                  <img alt="Colonia, Uruguay" src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54c6a76de4b0bf89531631a9/t/553e9822e4b01c68ac00590b/1430165667229/"/>
                 </noscript>
                 <img alt="Colonia, Uruguay" class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54c6a76de4b0bf89531631a9/t/553e9822e4b01c68ac00590b/1430165667229/" data-image-dimensions="2500x1674" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="553e9822e4b01c68ac00590b" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54c6a76de4b0bf89531631a9/t/553e9822e4b01c68ac00590b/1430165667229/" data-type="image"/>
                </div>
                <div class="image-caption-wrapper">
                 <div class="image-caption">
                  <p>
                   Colonia, Uruguay
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_6_1430164690511_7773">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p id="yui_3_17_2_6_1430164690511_7520">
               When you first embark on a quest, whether it's one of curiosity or one of travel,you start to notice that there are two different breeds of people. The first of the two consists of thosethat liketo stay close to home and areeither afraid or haveno desire to experience the world beyond what they've become accustomed to. They stay within their bubble and if they do happen to venture out they appear uncomfortable and remain tethered to home in one way or another. If and when they travel,they tend to stick to all the typical sites found in a travel guide and never venture out to experience real local culture. If they're in New York they'll visit the Empire State Building, the Eiffel Tower in Paris, Big Ben in London, etc . . . and they'll eat at major chain restaurants which they probably frequent back home. This is an extremely boring way to travel and see the world, yet when these people return to their homes they will speak of their experience as if it were really exciting and recommend the same trip to everyone they talk to. The reality behind this kind of travel and this breed of individual is that they really aren't trying to learn anything new because they lack true curiosity and have no interest inexperiencing local life. They enjoy telling people where they've been, even though in most cases they know nothing about those places. Instead of exposing themselves to new experiences, theybring what they know with them on their journey and return home as the exact same person they were when they first set out.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image sqs-col-6 span-6 float float-left" data-aspect-ratio="66.13636363636364" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_6_1430164690511_5380">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <div class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-below ">
               <div class="intrinsic" style="max-width:1500.0px;">
                <div class="image-block-wrapper has-aspect-ratio" data-description="&lt;p&gt;The Narrows | Zion National Park&lt;/p&gt;" style="padding-bottom:66.13636016845703%;">
                 <noscript>
                  <img alt="The Narrows | Zion National Park" src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54c6a76de4b0bf89531631a9/t/553e9760e4b0561f901249af/1430165426420/"/>
                 </noscript>
                 <img alt="The Narrows | Zion National Park" class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54c6a76de4b0bf89531631a9/t/553e9760e4b0561f901249af/1430165426420/" data-image-dimensions="1500x994" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="553e9760e4b0561f901249af" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54c6a76de4b0bf89531631a9/t/553e9760e4b0561f901249af/1430165426420/" data-type="image"/>
                </div>
                <div class="image-caption-wrapper">
                 <div class="image-caption">
                  <p>
                   The Narrows | Zion National Park
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_6_1430164690511_7133">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p>
               The other breed consists of the more adventurous types. This doesn't mean they're roaming around the globeseeking near death experiences, this just means they're comfortable being uncomfortable and willing to immerse themselves in whatever it is they're doing. If they travel somewhere new, even if only for a day, they live there for that day. They don't visit a place, they make it their home for the duration. People of this breed make new lifelongfriends wherever they go. When Icross paths with one of these individuals I immediatelynotice something different, yet familiar. It's as if we both were part of the same fraternity or grew up in the same town. Although we may have never met before, we feel an instant connection. The reason for this is because we're both part of what I'll call the Global Society. People that are members instantly recognize each other without saying a word. Even though we may have spawned from different corners of the globe, our roots are embedded everywhere. Some people that are part of this worldwide club may have never traveled a day in their life, but they possess an inquisitive nature and desire that expands their horizon beyond where the sun sets. It's always refreshing to stumble upon such individuals and share stories while at the same timeadding another chapter to your ownadventurous life story. When parting ways you both feel like you've learned something new and know that you've made a friend for life, a friend that will most likely relate to you more than most of the friends you grew up with. The Global Society is the closest thing you'll find to autopia, it's a place where race,religion, and politicshave no relevance.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image sqs-col-6 span-6 float float-right" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_6_1430164690511_8912">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <div class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-below ">
               <div class="intrinsic" style="max-width:2500.0px;">
                <div class="image-block-wrapper " data-description="&lt;p&gt;Mendenhall Glacier | Alaska&lt;/p&gt;" style="padding-bottom:80.76000213623047%;">
                 <noscript>
                  <img alt="Mendenhall Glacier | Alaska" src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54c6a76de4b0bf89531631a9/t/553e9922e4b017006f18b09f/1430165828262/"/>
                 </noscript>
                 <img alt="Mendenhall Glacier | Alaska" class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54c6a76de4b0bf89531631a9/t/553e9922e4b017006f18b09f/1430165828262/" data-image-dimensions="2500x2019" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="553e9922e4b017006f18b09f" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54c6a76de4b0bf89531631a9/t/553e9922e4b017006f18b09f/1430165828262/" data-type="image"/>
                </div>
                <div class="image-caption-wrapper">
                 <div class="image-caption">
                  <p>
                   Mendenhall Glacier | Alaska

               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_6_1430164690511_9131">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p id="yui_3_17_2_6_1430164690511_8785">
               Members of the Global Society are some of the wealthiest people you'll ever meet, regardless of the monetary value of all their assets or what they have in the bank. These individuals are a wealth of knowledge and experience. These people keep you humble and modest and constantly remind you to ditch the blinders and look at the world with eyes wide open.
              </p>
             </div>
