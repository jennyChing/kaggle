        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Riches from RAGS
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         In
         <em>
          Riches from RAGS
         </em>
         , eleven artists from the Reston Art Gallery and Studios (RAGS) exhibit a variety of art ranging from traditional landscapes, to abstract art, to photographic exploration in complimentary disciplines of pastel, collaged paper, printmaking, copper and acrylics. Thevisually rich and thematically diverse collection is on exhibit Tuesday, July 29 to Sunday, August 24, 2014. The Reston Art Gallery and Studios is a privately-owned cooperative gallery and studio in the historical part of Reston known as Lake Anne.
         <a name="top">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Meet the artists during the Artists Reception on Saturday, August 2, 2014 from 7 to 9 p.m. at ArtSpace Herndon.
        </p>

         <a name="Erwin">
         </a>
        </p>
        <h1>
         <a href="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Axtell-Erwin-2012-12-31-23.00.00-703.jpg">
          <img alt="Axtell-Erwin 2012-12-31 23.00.00-703" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-4153" height="225" src="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Axtell-Erwin-2012-12-31-23.00.00-703-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         Gail Axtell Erwin
        </h1>
        <p>
         Mixed Media Paintings
        </p>
        <p>
         A native to the Reston  Herndon area, Gail has been involved in the arts her whole life. She specializes in minute detail in both her painting and jewelry making. In painting she creates highly realistic scenes using acrylic paint in fine washes over textured rice paper which is adhered to her canvas. She also uses Casein, ink, metallic foils and marbleized papers if the painting requires it. In addition to art, she creates exquisite jewelry made from semi-precious gems and druse combined with fine macram, needlework and beads. Ms. Axtell Erwin pulls together many different components to create rich and complex work in both her paintings and jewelry.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="#top">
          top
         </a>
         <br/>
         <a name="Corbett">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         A native to the Reston  Herndon area, Gail has been involved in the arts her whole life. She specializes in minute detail in both her painting and jewelry making. In painting she creates highly realistic scenes using acrylic paint in fine washes over textured rice paper which is adhered to her canvas. She also uses Casein, ink, metallic foils and marbleized papers if the painting requires it. In addition to art, she creates exquisite jewelry made from semi-precious gems and druse combined with fine macram, needlework and beads. Ms. Axtell Erwin pulls together many different components to create rich and complex work in both her paintings and jewelry.
        </p>
        <h1>
         <a href="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Corbett-2012-12-31-23.00.00-721.jpg">
          <img alt="Corbett 2012-12-31 23.00.00-721" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-4154" height="225" src="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Corbett-2012-12-31-23.00.00-721-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         Lassie Corbett
        </h1>
        <p>
         Traditional Watercolors and Mixed-Media Abstracts
        </p>
        <p>
         Lassie is a member of a number of art groups, and served on the Council for the Arts of Herndon. She is a signature member of the Potomac Valley Watercolor Society as well as the Virginia Watercolor Society. Lassies work is exhibited regularly at various Art in the Workplace exhibits, the Vienna Arts Society, and at the Reston Art Gallery. She teaches private classes and workshops for local art groups. Most of her paintings are traditional, transparent watercolors. Subjects come from her foreign and adventure travel and her experiences as a watercolor workshop teacher. She also paints a number of floral paintings in season, as well as local landscapes.
        </p>
        <p>
         I begin my paintings with a series of detailed drawings. While traveling, I do a number of large drawings on tracing paper. I also take a sketch book for quick sketches, and journaling. I take a few photos as color references, but rarely draw from them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Lassie also works in and teaches acrylics (traditional and abstract), collage and Chinese brush painting, and watercolor, at the Herndon Community Center.
        </p>
        <p>
         Lassie has had her paintings published in three books:
         <em>
          The Creative Edge
         </em>
         , by nationally-known artist Mary Todd Beam;
         <em>
          The Potomac Valley Watercolorist
         </em>
         , a juried watercolor organization; and
         <em>
          His Legacy, Our Heritage, Professor Chen Bing Sun.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         This latter book honors Professor Chen, who is renowned for his Chinese Brush painting. This book also includes the work of artists, such as Lassie, who have studied under the professor and have gone on to teach Chinese Brush painting to other artists. Lassie originally studied under Professor Chen for four years in the Philippines.

        <h1>
         Dorothy Donahey
        </h1>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_3897" style="width: 310px">
         <a href="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/riches-from-rags/donahey-night-storm-mixed-media-on-canvas-42x54/" rel="attachment wp-att-3897">
          <img alt="Night Storm (mixed media on canvas) by Dorothy Donahey" class="wp-image-3897 size-medium" height="217" src="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Donahey-Night-Storm-mixed-media-on-canvas-42x54-300x217.jpeg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Night Storm (mixed media on canvas) by Dorothy Donahey

        <p>
         Acrylic Realism and Abstract Paintings
        </p>
        <p>
         Ms. Donahey received her BFA from Ohio State University and has continued to pursue her fine arts career at the Reston Art Gallery starting in 1993. Working in watercolor, Acrylic or oils. Donahey specializes in representational abstraction and realism. Currently she is working on acrylic paintings of landscapes the Northeastern United States.
        </p>
        <p>
         Donahey has won numerous awards including a Party Animal by the DC Commission of the Arts and Humanities. Awareness Donkey resided on Pennsylvania Avenue in Washington DC. He is now part of the permanent collection at American University.

         <a name="Jolles">
         </a>
        </p>
        <h1>
         <a href="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Jolles-05.jpg">
          <img alt="Jolles 05" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-3899" height="300" src="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Jolles-05-122x300.jpg" width="122"/>
         </a>
         Ronni Jolles
        </h1>
        <p>
         Paper Paintings
        </p>
        <p>
         Ronni grew up in Rochester, New York, and Washington, D.C., and graduated from Cornell University with a degree in psychology and art. Ronni was an art educator for 18 years before she became a full-time professional artist.
        </p>
        <p>
         The artwork of Ronni Jolles is something that is truly unique and innovative. The primary medium used in the work is paper. Hundreds of kinds of paper from all over the world are cut, torn, and layered to create uniquely textured scenes. An acrylic sealant is applied to every layer to protect and enhance textures, and sometimes acrylic paints are used to add variation of color. It could be called collage or mixed media, but in fact, it doesnt really fit in either category. The reality is, what you are looking at is really more of a sculptural art form. Each piece is one-of-a-kind.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ronni finds inspiration in quite a variety of subjects, ranging from Italian landscapes, trees by the water, dancers preparing to go on stage, or country gardens. Ronnis work has been featured in galleries and art spaces throughout the country, including a recent show in San Francisco. You can see more about Ronni Jolles at
         <a href="http://www.ronnijolles.com/">
          www.RonniJolles.com
         </a>

         <a name="Kautter">
         </a>
        </p>
        <h1>
         <a href="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Kautter-DSC_2157.jpg">
          <img alt="Kautter DSC_2157" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-3902" height="273" src="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Kautter-DSC_2157-300x273.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         Kathy Kautter
        </h1>
        <p>
         Photography
        </p>
        <p>
         Kathy Kautter is a current member of the Reston Art Gallery. She began her art career as a painter but recently has focused on photography as an art form. She loves to capture the abstract quality of nature, be it a close up of a pattern on a leaf or the shapes formed by waves or clouds, or the negative spaces created by the intersection of branches on a tree. Reflections in water are another favorite subject.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kathy has a degree in Political Science from Wilkes University, and a Masters degree in Urban and Regional Planning from The George Washington University, Washington, DC. Her first love has always been art, so she redirected her eduction to take art classes at NOVA, as well as classes from various professional artists in the Northern Virginia area.
        </p>
        <p>
         One of her photos of a cherry tree was selected as one of 100 for a special exhibit of the FotoDC National Cherry Blossom Festival photography exhibit in 2011. Another photo was accepted by FotoDC in 2012 for an on-going exhibit at the Crystal City Business District. She recently had an abstract photo accepted into the 4th Annual Joseph Miller Abstract Photography Exhibit of the Northern Virginia Alliance of Camera Clubs.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kathy is a past president of the League of Reston Artists, as well as a past board member. She is also a founding member of the Herndon Old Town Gallery. She exhibits in many local art shows and her work can also be found on several online photography websites.

         I lovestrong, bright colors,andthe interplay ofshapes,patterns, and glowing colors as they emerge and take on a life of their own. Ienjoy exaggerating the attitude of the humanfigure to capture the persons inner spirit.Inspiredby Hawaiis flowing tropical vegetation, and its illuminated and dazzling colors, Icreatedpaintings last winter thatfilled my heart with great joy. I never see black or gray. My personal world is filled with shimmering color!

        <h1 style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Samper-2012-12-31-23.00.00-715.jpg">
           <img alt="Samper 2012-12-31 23.00.00-715" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-4157" height="225" src="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Samper-2012-12-31-23.00.00-715-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          Claudia Samper
         </strong>
        </h1>
        <p>
         Oil and Acrylic Paintings
        </p>
        <p>
         Claudia Samper was born in Buenos Aires, Argentina. She holds an Advanced Degree in Architecture from the University of Buenos Aires and also studied graphic design, photography, and ceramics. After being involved in the design field for many years her interest went beyond that into fine arts focusing on oil and acrylic painting.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sampers goal is to create a space to encourage a dialogue between the viewer and her work. She looks for this interaction, to be able to trigger a feeling, a thought or a wonder. Kindness, joy, ambiguities, hopes, disappointments, perceptions, are all embedded in her paintings. Colors, textures, lines, shapes, and composition all work together to express what one could find inside ones mind, the definite as well as the uncertain.
        </p>
        <p>
         Samper has participated in many juried group and solo shows around the Washington Greater area and the United States. Her pieces can be found in collections in Spain, Argentina, and the United States.

In printmaking I have produced work in silkscreen, intaglio, relief, aquatint, monoprint, monotype and mezzotint. I am presently working on drypoint etchings (intaglio), relief prints (woodcut and linoleum), paintings and recycling some previous works with collage and transfers.

As a self  taught artist, she works with different media to discover the different properties in each and how they change the look of what she wants to achieve. I did study with several local artists as well as intensively studying art books and going to art shows and museums to learn how other artists work.           <a href="http://www.artspaceherndon.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Thole-2012-12-31-23.00.00-709.jpg">

          Roberta Thole
         </strong>
        </h1>
        <p>
         Contemporary Abstracts in Layers of Copper and Fresco
        </p>
        <p>
         With a collegiate teaching background in Art History, Humanities and Drawing, Roberta Thole developed a strong interest in Classical symbolism. Her painting challenge: to visually contain time past and time present, using the imagery and ideas of Greco-Roman antiquity, while creating work that is distinctly contemporary and abstract.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tholes canvasses combine geometric and architectural elements that are richly layered and textured, resembling ancient frescoed walls and ruins. Exploring the use of copper and fresco, and returning to a love of nature, have expanded the range and sense of the timelessness of these complex works. All of her art expresses her commitment to the process of painting.

         <strong>
          Reston Art Gallery &amp; Studios
         </strong>

Throughout the year, the artists latest works are featured in special shows each month. Childrens Art Workshops are offered at no charge at RAGS every Saturday from 10 am until 12 pm. RAGS is open to the public every weekend, from 10 am until 5 pm on Saturdays, and 12 pm until 5 pm on Sundays at 11400 Washington Plaza, Reston, VA 20190, at historic Lake Anne Plaza. Through the week, RAGS is open by chance or by appointment. Located on the ground floor of the 15-story Heron House, RAGS is a privately-owned cooperative of artists of various media, including: Pat Macintyre; Roberta Thole; Joan Kelly;
