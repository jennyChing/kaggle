              <p>
               Protect your noggin because the
               <em>
                Scooby-Doo
               </em>
               gang wants a bite of your brain.
              </p>
              <p>
               Photographer
               <a href="http://www.jeffzoet.com/" target="_blank">
                Jeff Zoet
               </a>
               has reimagined the classic animated series, pitting the supernatural investigators against villains much scarier than the ghosts they've encountered before.
              </p>
              <p>
               The epic photo series, titled "Scooby-Doo vs. the Zombie Apocalypse," places truly badass versions of Shaggy, Scooby, Velma, Fred and Daphne in a bloody fight against attacking
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/zombies/" target="_blank">
                zombies
               </a>
               .
              </p>

              <p>
               Zoet told Mashable that he had been a fan of
               <em>
                Scooby-Doo
               </em>
               since he was a child. After seeing a rerun on television recently, he came up with the concept for the zombie photo shoot.
              </p>
              <p>
               "If you just step back and take a look at the core of the TV show, it was about a group of kids that solve mysteries of the strange and unexplainable," he said. "Since it was a children's cartoon, things obviously never got too serious or life-threatening. So, I decided to set up a photo shoot with these characters that my entire generation was familiar with, and drop them into a more 'realistic' world where their lives could actually be in mortal danger."
              </p>
              <p>
               Zoet said that he's done other shoots inspired by pop culture, adding that, "I love to put my own spin on things that I'm a fan of."
              </p>

               For more of Zoet's work, check out his

               page.
