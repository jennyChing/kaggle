              <div class="viral-video-lead">
               <p>
                <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/OoyCRlqiHHg?enablejsapi=1&amp;" width="640">

              <p>
               Swarms of protesters took to the streets of Budapest, Hungary, on Tuesday night, waving their smartphones in the air to demonstrate against a planned tax on Internet use. The protest, which was the
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/10/27/hungary-internet-tax-protest/">
                second in about a week
               </a>
               , was largely organized through a
               <a href="https://www.facebook.com/Ne.legyen.Internetado" target="_blank">
                Facebook page
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               Estimations put the number of demonstrators against Prime Minister Viktor Orbns policy at
               <a href="https://www.yahoo.com/tech/100-000-hungarians-take-to-streets-to-protest-internet-101264259254.html" target="_blank">
                close to 100,000
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               The proposed
               <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/oct/29/hungarians-protest-internet-tax-plan-orban" target="_blank">
                monthly tax
               </a>
               was 700 forints, or $2.89, on each Internet user. After an initial protest on Sunday, the Hungarian government changed the proposal to cap the tax, but the movement has only grown in size. This breathtaking time-lapse shows hoards of protesters crossing the Erzsebet Bridge in Budapest.
              </p>
