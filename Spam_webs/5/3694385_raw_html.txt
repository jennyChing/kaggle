        <h1>
         How to Turn a Hobby into a Home Business
        </h1>
        <div class="post-meta">
         Sep 13, 2012 | Written by:
         <a href="/smallbusiness/author/ddonovan/" rel="author" title="Posts by Dave Donovan">
          Dave Donovan
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="entry">
         <h2>
          Three Important Questions You Need to Ask Before You Try
         </h2>
         <p>
          <img alt="turning a hobby into a business" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-449" height="168" src="http://www.dandb.com/smallbusiness/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/jewelry-maker-300x168.png" width="300"/>
          It is the dream of every hobby enthusiast to turn their passion into a home business that can support their lifestyle and family. After all, how many business experts tell you that until you are doing something you are truly passionate about, you will fail to reach the level of success that awaits you?
         </p>
         <p>
          Unfortunately, in some cases, reality sets back in, and the dreamer spends a lot of time and money trying to build their business  only to discover that their hobby is just that, a hobby. It is not something that transfers easily over to being a full-time business.
         </p>
         <p>
          But the failures of others should not be what keeps you from chasing your dream, if you think your hobby could be a sustainable home business. Before you go all in, however, be sure to ask yourself the following questions to ensure the dreams you are chasing are at least possible.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #1  Is There a Market for Your Product, and How Much Will They Pay for It?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          When you start off on this journey, you are probably not going to have access to business professionals who can guide you in the ways of determining whether or not there is a market for your product, so here is how you can do it yourself.
         </p>
         <p>
          First, determine how much you would have to charge for your product in order to make a profit on it, and then ask your friends if they would be willing to pay that amount for it. If your suggested amount is too high, then ask them what they would be willing to pay for it, and determine if that is a realistic amount in order for you to make a profit.
         </p>
         <p>
          Next, find a few people you do not know, and ask them the same questions. If yes beats no by a significant number, then there might be a viable market for your product.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #2  How Are You Going to Market Your Product?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Todays hobbyists have a number of resources available to them that can help them to build their business. From Facebook advertising, to making YouTube videos, to online storefronts like
          <a href="http://www.etsy.com/" target="_blank">
           Etsy
          </a>
          , getting the word out is not as difficult as it used to be, but it is still a time-consuming and strategically-demanding process.
         </p>
         <p>
          A good marketing campaign is a financial investment, so knowing whether or not you can afford to market your product effectively will be a deciding factor.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #3  Can This Hobby Really Support My Lifestyle?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Most hobbyists who manage to turn their passions into lifestyle-supporting businesses do so gradually. These types of businesses usually start off small, with the owner using the tools mentioned above. At some point, they realize that their home business has grown to the degree that they can afford to quit the day job.
         </p>
         <p>
          If all of your research leads to there being a market for your product, and you start utilizing the dozens of free and low-cost marketing tools, then with dedication and time, your business should start to grow. The moment you decide that you can make it your full-time job is something only you can determine.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
