        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.fryguy.net/2015/07/07/it-is-time-to-drop-the-ccie-written/" rel="bookmark">
          It is time to drop the CCIE written
         </a>
        </h2>
        <small>
         In
         <a href="http://www.fryguy.net/category/ccie-2/" rel="category tag">
          CCIE
         </a>
         on
         <strong>
          July 7, 2015
         </strong>

        <div class="entry">
         <p>
          <img alt="" border="0" class=" aligncenter" src="https://networkingnerd.files.wordpress.com/2012/09/ccierouting_and_switching_uselogowr_edited-1.gif?w=150" title=""/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Back in 1993 the CCIE Cisco Certification,the first Cisco certification, was created and tested.Yes, the CCIE certification came years before the CCNA certification (1998) and thus Cisco needed a way to weed out candidates who were not ready for theCCIE lab exam. What they came up with was a Written pre-qualification exam to show that the candidate wasready to attempt theCCIE Lab exam. The CCIE Written is not a certification in itself, even though many job seekers seem to think that it is, it is just a qualifier to show that the candidate is ready toattempt the CCIE lab exam. To this day theCCIE written is still needed, regardless if thecandidate does not have or has other Cisco certifications. Today though, with all the Cisco certifications, training, and the amount of Cisco networking gear in the enterprise, it is quite different and begs the question, Does the CCIE Writtenstill make sense today?
         </p>

          Cisco Live 2015  Monday
         </a>
        </h2>
        <small>
         In
         <a href="http://www.fryguy.net/category/cisco-live-2/" rel="category tag">
          Cisco Live
         </a>
         on
         <strong>
          June 9, 2015
         </strong>

         <strong>

        <div class="entry">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.fryguy.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/JC.jpg">
           <img alt="JC" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-4606" height="300" src="http://www.fryguy.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/JC-296x300.jpg" width="296"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Day two is now in the books, and all I have to say is What a Keynote! I have been coming to Cisco Live for over 10 years now and I will say that this keynote did have something special to it. I think knowing that this was his list keynote as CEO of Cisco, it just had something extra to, maybe it was OK GO opening up, maybe it was the seating, or just the energy in the room. Mr. Chambers owned the room and engaged with the people in the audience.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.fryguy.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/DSC03184-Medium.jpg">
           <img alt="DSC03184 (Medium)" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-4607" height="199" src="http://www.fryguy.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/DSC03184-Medium-300x199.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          His successor,Chuck Robbins, was able to do a nice tribute video to Mr. Chambers that covers many of the keynotes, Netvet receptions, and World of Solutions engagements over the past years. Mr. Robbins is preparing to take Cisco into the future of this industry and help it to maintain its leadership.
         </p>
