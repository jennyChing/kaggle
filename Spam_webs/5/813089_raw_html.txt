          <h1>
           Joe Torre, Bobby Cox and Tony La Russa All Headed To Baseball Hall of Fame
          </h1>
          <div class="post_meta">
           <span class="author_avatar">
            <a href="http://wibx950.com/author/jeff/" title="Jeff Monaski">
             <img src="http://wac.450f.edgecastcdn.net/80450F/wibx950.com/files/2013/10/Jeff.jpg?w=40&amp;h=40&amp;zc=1&amp;s=0&amp;a=t&amp;q=89"/>
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="the_author">
            <span>
             By
            </span>
            <a href="http://wibx950.com/author/jeff/" title="Jeff Monaski">
             Jeff Monaski
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="the_date">
            December 9, 2013 3:12 PM
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="social-group-top large">
           <div class="social_icons size_large">
            <a class="facebook share_link" href="http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?u=http%3A%2F%2Fwibx950.com%2Fjoe-torre-bobby-cox-and-tony-larussa-all-headed-to-baseball-hall-of-fame%2F%3Ftrackback%3Dfbshare_top&amp;t=Joe+Torre%2C+Bobby+Cox+and+Tony+La+Russa+All+Headed+To+Baseball+Hall+of+Fame" target="_blank" title="Share on Facebook">
             <div class="social_icon_large">
             </div>
             <span class="social_icon">
              Share on Facebook
             </span>
            </a>
            <a class="twitter share_link" href="http://twitter.com/intent/tweet?text=Check+out+Joe+Torre%2C+Bobby+Cox+and+Tony+La+Russa+All+Headed+To+Baseball+Hall+of+Fame+http%3A%2F%2Fwibx950.com%2Fjoe-torre-bobby-cox-and-tony-larussa-all-headed-to-baseball-hall-of-fame%2F%3Ftrackback%3Dtwitter_top+%40WIBXnews" target="_blank" title="Share on Twitter">
             <div class="social_icon_large">

            <span class="social-submenu">
            </span>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="the_content clearfix">
           <figure class="alignleft" id="attachment_286512">
            <img alt="LOS ANGELES, CA - JUNE 04: Atlanta Braves manager Bobby Cox and Los Angeles Dodgers manager Joe Torre talk prior to the start of the game at Dodger Stadium on June 4, 2010 in Los Angeles, California. (Photo by Jeff Gross/Getty Images)" class="size-large wp-image-286512" height="438" src="http://wac.450f.edgecastcdn.net/80450F/wibx950.com/files/2013/12/101630607-630x438.jpg" title="LOS ANGELES, CA - JUNE 04: Atlanta Braves manager Bobby Cox and Los Angeles Dodgers manager Joe Torre talk prior to the start of the game at Dodger Stadium on June 4, 2010 in Los Angeles, California. (Photo by Jeff Gross/Getty Images) " width="630"/>
            <figcaption style="width:630px;">
             Jeff Gross, Getty Images
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            Three of the major leagues greatest coaches will be inducted in the National Baseball Hall of Fame this summer.
            <span id="more-286510">
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Joe Torre, Bobby Cox and Tony La Russa will all be enshrined in Cooperstown, NY,
            <a href="http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article/mlb/tony-la-russa-joe-torre-bobby-cox-elected-to-baseball-hall-of-fame?ymd=20131209&amp;content_id=64493980" target="_blank">
             according to MLB.com
            </a>
            :
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             The three, who accumulated 7,558 regular-season wins, 17 pennants and eight World Series titles, were elected unanimously by the 16-member Expansion Era Committee during a lengthy meeting on Sunday. The announcement by Hall of Fame chairman Jane Forbes Clark was made on Monday morning as the annual Winter Meetings began.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            Let me say that I think all three coaches are obviously very deserving of this honor, so Im not trying to downplay their selection.
           </p>
           <figure class="alignleft" id="attachment_286513">
            <img alt="ARLINGTON, TX - OCTOBER 23: Manager Tony La Russa stands in the dugout prior to Game Four of the MLB World Series against the Texas Rangers at Rangers Ballpark in Arlington on October 23, 2011 in Arlington, Texas. (Photo by Rob Carr/Getty Images)" class="size-medium wp-image-286513" height="300" src="http://wac.450f.edgecastcdn.net/80450F/wibx950.com/files/2013/12/130145308-200x300.jpg" title="ARLINGTON, TX - OCTOBER 23: Manager Tony La Russa stands in the dugout prior to Game Four of the MLB World Series against the Texas Rangers at Rangers Ballpark in Arlington on October 23, 2011 in Arlington, Texas. (Photo by Rob Carr/Getty Images)" width="200"/>
            <figcaption style="width:200px;">
             Rob Carr, Getty Images
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            However,
            <a href="http://baseballhall.org/news/press-releases/o%27day-ruppert-white-inducted-hall-fame-class-2013-sunday-cooperstown" target="_blank">
             for fear of a repeat of 2013
            </a>
            , when no modern era players made the cut and the honorees included names most fans had never heard of, I almost think this is an insurance induction. Bring three legendary figures to town so in the case that no big name modern era guys get in, at least you still have a reason to go to Cooperstown.
           </p>
           <p>
            I could be wrong but I just think this may not have happened
            <em>
             now
            </em>
            without the cloud of steroids tainting ballots.
           </p>
           <p>
            Good for all three skippers though, again, its hard to argue about their worthiness as all three are in the top 5 all time for managerial wins, according to
            <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/managers/" target="_blank">
             baseballreference.com
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            La Russa ranks third (2,728), Cox is fourth (2,504) and Torre is fifth (2,326).
