             </a>
             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://latenightboutique.blogspot.com/2010/12/why-not-make-your-own-butter.html">
               Why not make your own Butter
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2optvRRBI/AAAAAAAAAUM/gOZARNdsMOQ/s1600/DSCF1561.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="300" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2optvRRBI/AAAAAAAAAUM/gOZARNdsMOQ/s400/DSCF1561.JPG" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              I always thought making butter was a longprocess, but did you know it only takes 3.5 minutesand twoingredients for lightly salted butter.
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2otiq7QHI/AAAAAAAAAUQ/VzbhYhoKR_Q/s1600/DSCF1552.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="300" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2otiq7QHI/AAAAAAAAAUQ/VzbhYhoKR_Q/s400/DSCF1552.JPG" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              You need a foodprocessor, mixer or blender. I use a food processor. Pour 2 cups of whipping cream in your food processor and 1/2 to 1 teaspoon sea salt. Start your food processor and let it run until you hear slushing and see the butterseparatefrom the buttermilk, this will take about 3 to 3 1/2 minutes.
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2o50yIjmI/AAAAAAAAAUU/vaV4umLUWq8/s1600/DSCF1553.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="300" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2o50yIjmI/AAAAAAAAAUU/vaV4umLUWq8/s400/DSCF1553.JPG" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              Pour the butter and the buttermilk in a bowl lined with a cheese cloth or thin cloth napkin..
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2pEJ9yIbI/AAAAAAAAAUY/XUMC9yiFQyU/s1600/DSCF1554.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="240" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2pEJ9yIbI/AAAAAAAAAUY/XUMC9yiFQyU/s320/DSCF1554.JPG" width="320"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2pKHYD8ZI/AAAAAAAAAUc/pxAjjH4Rkxo/s1600/DSCF1555.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="240" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2pKHYD8ZI/AAAAAAAAAUc/pxAjjH4Rkxo/s320/DSCF1555.JPG" width="320"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2pNzDOTUI/AAAAAAAAAUg/BtqGF9LG01E/s1600/DSCF1556.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="150" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2pNzDOTUI/AAAAAAAAAUg/BtqGF9LG01E/s200/DSCF1556.JPG" width="200"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2pRxgVr6I/AAAAAAAAAUk/XowcHO0muik/s1600/DSCF1557.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
               <img border="0" height="150" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_3t8pEp7PniU/TP2pRxgVr6I/AAAAAAAAAUk/XowcHO0muik/s200/DSCF1557.JPG" width="200"/>
              </a>
              Gather your cloth andgentlysqueeze the buttermilk out.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Place the butter in a dish andrefrigerate.
              <br/>

                     <p class="comment-content" id="bc_0_6MC">
                      Um, are you sure that's a pound of butter?  I make my butter all the time from the cream skimmed from raw cow's milk.  I do anywhere from 2-4 cups of cream at a time in my blender and usually only end up with about 3/4 to 1 1/2 cups of butter.  A pound of butter that you buy at the store contains 2 cups, so I just go by that instead of weighing it.  I just never imagined it would be cost effective to make homemade butter from cream you had to buy at the store with the meager yield I get!
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Also, I always 'rinse' my butter in fresh water (squish the butter around in the water), to help remove some additional buttermilk.  The buttermilk goes rancid rather quickly at room temp., which will ruin your butter.  So the more you remove, the longer your fresh butter will taste great!
