       <h1>
        8 cool things you can do on Google
       </h1>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <span class="intro">
         I love geeks. I particularly love the ones over at Google (who were
         <a href="https://www.google.com/partners/#a_profile;idtf=2903709300;" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          partnered with
         </a>
         , by the way). Not only are they so awe-inspiringly smart, I admire their sense of humour and appreciate the fact that their handy inventions make life a little simpler and procrastinating more fun
        </span>
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <p dir="ltr">
        I will forever be indebted to the search engine giants synonyms and define features for saving me from writers block at uni, as well as their Google Maps tool for keeping me entertained; its always nice to know you can take a virtual trip to the Antarctic to look at the penguins.Every once-in-a-Google moon, the geniuses put their algorithm-changing, user-enhancing, spam-fighting activities on hold to create something weird, wonderful or useful. Who said computer geeks were boring? Not me! Here are 8 more cool things you can do on Google, which Im pretty fond of
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <h2 dir="ltr">
        <strong>
         1. Let me Google that for you (LMGTFY)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p dir="ltr">
        For those people in the office who are right in front of a computer, but find it more convenient to bother you with a question rather than ask Google and get an answer in 0.22 seconds, Google invented the wonderfully facetious
        <a href="http://lmgtfy.com/" target="_blank">
         Let me Google that for you
        </a>
         tool. You simply type the question that youve just been asked into the tools search bar and press the Type a question. Click a button button. This generates a link to the answer to your lazy colleagues question, which you can email to them. Voil! Point proven. Who said the art of conversation is dead?
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <img alt="8 cool things you can do on Google: Let me Google that for you" height="402" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/lmgtfy.png" width="666"/>
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <h2 dir="ltr">
        <strong>
         2. Film reviews
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p dir="ltr">
        Fancy watching a film, but not sure whats on at the cinema? Google Movies has got you covered. Just head over to
        <a href="http://www.google.com/movies" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Google.com/movies
        </a>
        and youll be presented with a list of films and screening times at the cinemas closest to your current location. Or, if theres a flick you think you might like to see, but youre wary of wasting your time and popcorn money on it, simply type the name of the film into the search bar and hit enter. Googles search results will present you with an information-rich box, featuring ratings, a release date, running time, images of the cast, movie stills and a film blurb along the right-hand side of the page. Heres an example from when I typed in Captain America: The Winter Soldier:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/captainamerica.png">
        </a>
        <a href="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/captainamerica.png">
         <img alt="8 cool things you can do on Google: film reviews" class="alignnone wp-image-2170 size-full" height="950" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/captainamerica.png" width="461"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        In the same search, Google presents further information at the top left-hand side of the page, including a link to the films trailer and location-specific information, such as local cinemas that screen the film.
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <img alt="8 cool things you can do on Google: film showing times" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2173" height="273" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/CaptainAmericaShowingTimes.png" width="558"/>
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <h2 dir="ltr">
        <strong>
         3. Google Books Ngram Viewer
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p dir="ltr">
        The Google Books geeky Ngram viewer tool lets you analyse the rise and/or fall of a specific keyword or keywords over a 500-year time span. You can search for one keyword to view its history or search for a few to compare their popularity. Heres an example of a search for just one keyword, digital:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/GoogleBooksNgramViewer.png">
         <img alt="8 cool things you can do on Google: Ngram viewer" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2176" height="925" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/GoogleBooksNgramViewer.png" width="1847"/>
        </a>
        Lets give it a go with digital and marketing:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/GoogleBooksNgrramViewer1.png">
         <img alt="8 cool things you can do on Google: Ngram viewer" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2178" height="938" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/GoogleBooksNgrramViewer1.png" width="1819"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        To compare the popularity of two keywords, simply add a comma in between each one. Click on the dates at the bottom of the page to be directed through to search results, which contain Google books written within that specified era. This is a valuable tool for gathering research data and gaining cultural insights.
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <h2 dir="ltr">
        <strong>
         4. Think With Google
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <a href="http://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Think With Google
        </a>
        is one of my favourite recent discoveries; its a huge help when doing research for articles and its a great resource for anyone in the digital marketing industry. The site is brimming with insights, trends and research in digital marketing as well as inspiration, statistics, industry intelligence and best practices for businesses. The drop-down menus along the top of the page allow you to narrow your research down by industry, marketing objective and ad types. The perspectives tab gives you free, unlimited access to viewpoints from industry professionals, while the creative sandbox rounds up the top creative ideas within the technology industry  a great source of inspiration.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/ThinkWithGoogle.png">
         <img alt="Think With Google" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2179" height="966" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/ThinkWithGoogle.png" width="1882"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <h2 dir="ltr">
        <strong>
         5. Weather forecast
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p dir="ltr">
        Is it a shorts day or a jeans day? Google knows. Simply type weather into the search bar and you will be presented with a comprehensive weather guide, featuring details of precipitation levels, humidity and wind speed in your area.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/GoogleWeather.png">
         <img alt="GoogleWeather" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2181" height="498" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/GoogleWeather.png" width="574"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <h2 dir="ltr">
        <strong>
         6. Make Google beatbox
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p dir="ltr">
        Go to
        <a href="http://translate.google.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Google Translate
        </a>
        and paste the following text into the translation box:
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        pv zk pv pv zk pv zk kz zk pv pv pv zk pv zk zk pzk pzk pvzkpvpvzk kkkkk bsch
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        Translate it into German and press the listen button (which will now say beatbox when you hover over it). Thanks to whoever had too much time on their hands and discovered that little gem.
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         7. Cool tricks
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p dir="ltr">
        Do a barrel roll, zerg rush tilt and askew, elgooG. If you havent already typed these into Google, you should do it now. Anyone whos played Nintendos Star Fox game will understand the barrel roll. Zerg rush makes Googles oos act like zerglings from the Starcraft game. Tilt and askew are pretty self-explanatory.
       </p>
       <p dir="ltr">
        <a href="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/tilt.png">
         <img alt="Google tilt" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2182" height="961" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/tilt.png" width="1887"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/zergrush.png">
         <img alt="google Zerg rush" class="alignnone wp-image-2183 size-full" height="357" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/zergrush.png" width="675"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         8. Adjust reading level
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p dir="ltr">
        Googles Reading Level option is a handy tool to know about, particularly if you have children or youre researching a particularly complex topic. For the sake of this example, I will use the phrase digital marketing to show you how it works, if you dont know already.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/readinglevel.png">
         <img alt="Adjust Google reading level" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2184" height="586" src="http://cdn.click.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/readinglevel.png" width="988"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
