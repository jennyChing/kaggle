                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         <img alt="40 YEAR OLD VIRGIN" src="http://ibizbook.com/live/images/62/40_YEAR_OLD_VIRGIN.jpg" width="100%"/>
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         Everyone knows one. Whether it's a friend or family member, there's one person in everyone's life who remains a virgin into adulthood and beyond.
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         A new study from the Archives of Sexual Behavior has some new theories on the matter. While there are people who remain abstinent into their 20s and 30s for religious reasons, the study indicates that for some people, prolonged virginity is unintentional. For others, lack of interest in sex prompts them to remain abstinent well beyond adolescence.
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         The study, which was the first to delve into the reasons behind adulthood abstinence, followed 2,857 men and women from adolescence through their early 30s. Of those who were still virgins at the age of 18, three percent were still abstinent by the time they reached their late 20s and early 30s.
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         Researchers found that some participants were abstinent due to an inability to attract members of the opposite sex. Some factors cited as potential causes of adulthood abstinence are lower cognitive performance, obesity, and lower parental educational levels. One researcher stated that in many cases, respondents to this particular survey who were obese were more likely to fall into the "inexperienced" category based on responses.
                        </p>
