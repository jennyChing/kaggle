          <h1 class="asset-name">
           <span class="bigletters">
            Pirates try to capture anti-pirate navy ship
           </span>
          </h1>
          <div class="asset-meta">
           <span class="byline">
            May  5, 2009  6:34 PM
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="asset-content">
          <div class="asset-body">
           <span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image">
            <img alt="200x190.jpg" class="mt-image-left" height="190" src="http://newsliteimgs.s3.amazonaws.com/090505_pirate1.jpg" style="margin: 0pt 20px 20px 0pt; float: left;" width="200"/>
           </span>
           <b>
            Hapless Somali pirates got more than they bargained for when the ship they were trying to hijack turned out to be an anti-pirate navy ship.
            <br/>
           </b>
           <br/>
           It is thought the bungling gang had mistaken the French navy ship - filled with weapons and angry commandos - for a cargo ship, and tried to attack it.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           But as they approached the NIVSE, 500 nautical miles east of Mombassa, it dawned on the pirates they had made a mistake.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           To be honest it was probably the helicopter firing warning shots and the two boats of commandos dispatched to bring them in, that gave it away.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           The pirates tried to turn and flee but were soon captured with all 11 surrendering ... and wishing they had taken a better pair of binoculars.
          </div>
          <div class="asset-more" id="more">
           <span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image">
            <img alt="200x190.jpg" class="mt-image-center" height="235" src="http://newsliteimgs.s3.amazonaws.com/090505_pirate2.jpg" style="margin: 0pt auto 20px; text-align: center; display: block;" width="480"/>
           </span>
           A spokesperson for the French navy said: "The pirate fleet was composed of a "mother ship", a boat of 10 metres long, from which the skiffs were launched and sustained, and two assault skiffs of 8 metres long."
           <br/>
           <br/>
           The NIVSE was in the area a part of Operation Atlanta, a EU operation to improve safety at sea, accompanying ships of the World Food Program.
           <br/>
           <br/>
