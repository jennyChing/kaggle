         <span class="rpr_recipe_notes" itemprop="post">
          I am always trying to come up with unique healthy recipes and this one was perfect on a weeknight after a long day of work. I really wanted something hearty but was trying to eat healthy. You guys know how much I love noodles and vietnamese flavors and cauliflower so as you can imagine I devoured it.
          <div align="center">
           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.thefoodinmybeard.com/content/tempehnoodles/tempehnoodles1.JPG"/>
           First mix the noodles with carrot and cucumber. I also added mint, cilantro, fish sauce, and lime juice.
           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.thefoodinmybeard.com/content/tempehnoodles/tempehnoodles2.JPG"/>
           Tempeh and cauliflower ready to go.
           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.thefoodinmybeard.com/content/tempehnoodles/tempehnoodles3.JPG"/>
           Brown up the tempeh and cauliflower, and add a paste of garlic, ginger, and chiles. Then some sugar goes in.
           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.thefoodinmybeard.com/content/tempehnoodles/tempehnoodles4.JPG"/>
           The cauliflower and tempeh are really hearty, spicy, savory and sweet, while the noodles are sour bright and fresh. They compliment each other perfectly.
           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.thefoodinmybeard.com/content/tempehnoodles/tempehnoodles5.JPG"/>
           I like the chewy nuttyness of the tempeh and it matches great in this dish and with the vietnamese inspired flavors.
           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.thefoodinmybeard.com/content/tempehnoodles/tempehnoodles6.JPG"/>

          <h3>
           Instructions
          </h3>
          <!-- INFO LINE (Times) -->
          <ol class="recipe-instructions">
           <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <span class="recipe-instruction ">
             Shred the carrot and cucumber with a julienne peeler. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions.  Strain and rinse.  Mix with the carrot, cucumber, mint, cilantro, lime, and fish sauce.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <span class="recipe-instruction ">
             Blend the garlic, ginger, and chiles to form a paste.  Remove the seeds if you wanna be a wimp about it.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <span class="recipe-instruction ">
             Break up the cauliflower and cut the tempeh into a similar size.  Cook in some oil on high heat tossing occasionally to brown well.  Lower the heat and add in the ginger garlic chile paste.  Cook 2 minutes.  Add in the sugar and cook 2 minutes stirring often and making sure not to burn the sugar. Remove from heat. Add in the splash of fish sauce and water.  Stir well.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <span class="recipe-instruction ">
             To serve, plate the noodles and top with the cauliflower mixture.  Top with the rice powder if you have it. I had some leftover and I put it on pretty much everything now.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ol>
