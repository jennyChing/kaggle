         <h1>
          What Heroes, Villains, And Freaks Teach Us About Success Pt. 1
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta">
          by
          <a href="http://akannelson.com/author/akannelson/" rel="author" title="Posts by Akan">
           Akan
          </a>
          | Feb 4, 2015 |
          <a href="http://akannelson.com/category/productivity/" rel="category tag">
           Productivity
          </a>
          |
          <span class="comments-number">
           <a href="http://akannelson.com/heroes-villains-freaks-teach-us-success-pt-1/#respond">
            0 comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <img alt="What Heroes, Villains, And Freaks Teach Us About Success Pt. 1" height="675" src="http://akannelson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/7749808022_f1dc3e6484_o-1080x675.jpg" width="1080"/>

         <div class="entry-content">
          <span class="wpsr_floatbts_anchor" data-offset="25">
          </span>
          <p>
           World culture is obsessed with the idea of the superhuman  the super smart, super athletic, super rich, super selfless  we are fascinated with people who operate at the extremes of human achievement, because
           <strong>
            at the core of each and every single one of us is the desire for complete mastery
           </strong>
           , of ourselves and of our environments.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            We want to
            <strong>
             win
            </strong>
            .
           </li>
           <li>
            We want to
            <strong>
             be better
            </strong>
            .
           </li>
           <li>
            We want to
            <strong>
             leave a legacy
            </strong>
            after we die.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Think about why cultures around the world have spent decades imagining and reimagining stories of exceptional people doing the unimaginable, disrupting the universe, and overcoming what we assume to be the emotional and physical limits of human ability.
          </p>
          <p>
           Think about why we collectively spend millions to see the next Denzel Washington movie, watch Floyd Mayweather beat down another boxer, or see Rihanna perform live on stage.
          </p>
          <p>
           Think about why we idolize athletes who compete in The World Cup and The Olympics.
          </p>
          <p>
           Think about Serena Williams, Binyavanga Wainaina, and The Incredible Hulk.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            We love these people because they are all succeeding at the boundaries of human expectations
           </strong>
           <strong>
            . They are superhumans.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The superhuman, often presented as a hero, villain, or freak, serves as a reminder of what we can achieve as individuals
           </strong>
            a reminder that incredible progress is possible if only you applied yourself with consistent pressure, and believed in your heart that you have the power, with time, to achieve anything your mind can conceive.
          </p>
          <p>
           Super heroes, villains, and freaks (real and imagined) are the ultimate success stories, and offer a lot of information about what it really means to live as a successful person.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong style="line-height: 1.5;">
            1. Purpose  How do you plan to change the world?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           There isnt a single hero, villain, or freak out there that we care about who doesnt have a
           <strong>
            clear, defined reason
           </strong>
           for doing what they do. There isnt a single one whose reason(s) for waking up every morning doesnt immediately come to mind when you hear their name.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Usain Bolt wakes up every morning to be the Worlds Fastest Man.
           </li>
           <li>
            Spiderman wakes up every morning to save New York.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 461px">
           <img alt="" class="" height="508" src="http://moveoveraverage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/phil.jpg" width="451"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Phil Heath wakes up every morning to be Mr. Olympia
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <strong>
            You cannot begin the process of transforming yourself if you dont have a reason outside yourself to wake up every day.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Some people say they that they dont have any specific passions or reasons for doing what they do; they say things like, I just want to live and be happy, or Im interested in doing too many things, I cant pick one purpose for my life.
          </p>
          <p>
           Dont be one of these people.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Happiness is self-defined
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Usain Bolt is probably happier than most of the world because he gets to do what he loves every day. For him, happiness is the thrill of trying to beat his best times on the track every day. He might care about poverty, over-optimistic housing markets, or philosophy, but what Bolt really loves is running; and hes gone ahead and made himself a millionaire by doing it.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Everyone has one or two dreams that stand above all their other aspirations
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Deciding what your definitive passion is will be uncomfortable, confusing, and hard, but have courage.
          </p>
          <p>
           Be brave enough to embrace the uncertainty of your future, and work yourself into a position where you are ready to make the best of that life-changing opportunity when it comes your way.
          </p>
          <p>
           Peter Parkers life-changing opportunity was the spider bite, which turned him into Spiderman;Barack Obama had been active in US politics for 11 years before his life-changing opportunity  the chanceto run for president in 2008.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Know your purpose, and work towards it every day
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Be ready for opportunity when it comes
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            How do you plan to change the world? What can you do to build up your natural strengths and strengthen your natural weaknesses? What is the first thing you want people to know you for?
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            2. Know Yourself, Accept Yourself, Love Yourself
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           At their most effective, heroes, villains, and freaks have a deep understanding of their capabilities, character, emotions, and motivations.
          </p>
          <p>
           They are unapologetic about their strengths, and accept their weaknesses as they push to be better at the things they do.They hold themselves in high esteem, and appreciate themselves for who they are, good and bad.
          </p>
          <p>
           To be successful  to live as a superhuman  you must:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Know yourself
            </strong>
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Accept yourself
            </strong>
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Love yourself
            </strong>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           For instance, Ive come to recognize that I have a super short fuse under prolonged periods of high-stress. My temper is explosive and easily triggered, and I end up doing and saying things that I regret.
          </p>
          <p>
           Im currently at a place where Ive accepted this as part of who I am, and moved from trying to fix my degree of irritability to learning to identify the kinds of situations that set me off. This way I can better catch myself before I lose self-control.
          </p>
          <p>
           And the truth is even with this knowledge I dont catch myself all the time. Sometimes Im a cutthroat monster, and all I can do in those situations is damage control: forgive myself, apologize to whomever, and try harder next time.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Self-love is one of the most important keys to personal achievement.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           You cannot and should not hate yourself for every poor decision youve ever made. Everyone makes mistakes.
          </p>
          <p>
           Do not let anyone make you apologize for continuing to think highly of yourself after youve madea mistake. People who do this are negative, and trying to control you in a harmful way.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="570" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/Ge33hrlN2Uc?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;start=14&amp;end=49&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="960">
            </iframe>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           When Kanye West says, I am a God, it is unlikely that he thinks that he is actual deity. Kanye West does not think people pray to him at night. To interpret his words as such is to miss the point.
          </p>
          <p>
           Rather,
           <strong>
            Kanyes words are a wink and a nod to the unrelenting and unapologetic level of self-acceptance and self-love required to survive at the highest levels of society.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Kanye West might be a real-life villain, but the world will continue to love him  accept his genius and his insanity  because he does so himself.
          </p>
          <p>
           If you want to be happy you want to
           <strong>
            know yourself, love yourself, and accept yourself
           </strong>
           without apology every single day.
          </p>
          <p>
           Start with the
           <a href="http://personality-testing.info/tests/OEJTS/" target="_blank">
            Jungian/Myers-Briggs Personality Type test
           </a>
           , which is based on four preferences, that when combined give you a coherent personality type. I find the test to be strikingly accurate, and have taken it at least once a year for the last three years.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Im an INTJ
           </strong>
           . Im Introverted (center my energy and attention inside my body), Intuitive (pay more attention to the patterns and possibilities I see in information I receive), Thinking (evaluate my goals by objective principles), and Judging (prefer a structured lifestyle and the use of feeling and thinking).
          </p>
          <p>
           The Jungian/Myers-Briggs test will give you a strong base to start building a greater degree of personal understanding and awareness.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Focus on knowing and accepting yourself first. Take the Jungian/Myers-Briggs test. Practice active reflection in the shower, as you fall asleep, or whenever else you have free time.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Understand that the world will perceive and treat you how you perceive and treat yourself.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            3. Success Is Isolating  Youre easiest to hit when youre at the top
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           As you climb up the ladder of success many of people you know who are not achieving at similar levels will begin to resent you.
          </p>
          <p>
           They will accuse you of thinking you are better than everyone else, and describe you as having become a different person since you achieved whatever goal it was.
          </p>
          <p>
           They will not be able to understand how you have been able to achieve what you have achieved and will highlight your flaws, which you already acknowledge and accept, in an attempt to discredit your accomplishments.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            You will not care.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            You will not be affected.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            You will understand that harsh, undeserved criticism is the price of being a winner.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 616px">
           <img alt="" class="" height="404" src="http://i4.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article754170.ece/alternates/s2197/mario-balotelli.jpg" width="606"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Mario Balotelli is one of the best players on the pitch with or without your opinions about his bad behavior.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           I remember when Usain Bolt smashed the world record in the 100m for the second time in 2009. Before the end of the daythe media was already speculating that he might be on steroids.
          </p>
          <p>
           Theycould not understand how anyone could run so fast andso they accused him of cheating, ignoring the years of hard work and dedication he put in before the world knew who he was.
          </p>
          <p>
           (On a tangent  steroid and performance enhancing drug abuse is rampant in the world of commercial sport. If every Olympian athlete on aperformance enhancing drug was caught there would be no one to compete. Even on performance enhancers you still need to train at extreme levels to be Olympics worthy).
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Loneliness, anxiety, and depression are common themes in the lives of successful people.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Theres always a point in the moviewhere the superhero is hunted down by the police for being a threat to thepublic, and has toseek solace in some underground lair or hide behind a mask.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Prolonged success attracts attention, and attention is never neutral.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           If youre serious about being a winner
           <strong>
            you need to have thick skin
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           You are the easiest target when youve reached the top of the hill because everyone can see you. You get the most stones thrown at you when youre at the top of the hill because everyone wants your spot.
          </p>
          <p>
           Take the negative attention and use it as extra motivation to keep you going.
          </p>
          <p>
           You need to be prepared to have people throw unrequested opinions in your face, and talk about you and the things you are doing as if theyve known you for years.
          </p>
          <p>
           Dont waste your time on the opinions of people who dont understand your goals.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Understand that some of the relationships you value now will become unsustainable as you move up in life. Identify the people who you can trust for support and advice. Who is a part of your inner circle? Understand that receiving criticism is a necessary part of being a winner.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            When the negative attention gets too muchseek comfort in your family and inner circle.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           As you become more successful and move to higher levels in life more and more people will begin to identify you as another superhuman  another hero, villain or freak pushing the boundaries of human achievement.
          </p>
          <p>
           Start working now to identify your purpose, and cultivate self-knowledge, self-acceptance, and self-love in yourself. Train your mind to filter out malicious feedback. Be great.
          </p>
          <hr/>
          <p>
           Part 2 of this post will explorethree more observations about heroes, villains, and freaks and what they tell us about success. Look out for it in a few days!
          </p>
