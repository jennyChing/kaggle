       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         No-Bake Chocolate Cookies
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-01-19T16:43:15+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 19, 2015
         </time>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          Simple and delicious- thats how its supposed to be- these chocolate no-bake cookies are gluten-free and full of all things yummy.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.hotchocolatehits.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/homemade-no-bake-cookie-recipe.jpg">
          <img alt="A no-bake chocolate cookie with all things delicious in under 10 minutes. Simple and delicious- that's how it's meant to be. #cookies #nobake #glutenfree" class="aligncenter wp-image-866 size-full" height="567" src="http://www.hotchocolatehits.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/homemade-no-bake-cookie-recipe.jpg" width="850"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Hi there.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its been a while.
        </p>
        <p>
         My exams have been keeping me busy, then I had Christmas break for 3 weeks, which I spent in India. My Chemistry exam was today, so I dont feel like working. Instead Im procrastinating.
        </p>
        <p>
         Today I bring you the N0-Bake Chocolate Cookie. A cookie that lies somewhere between
         <a href="http://www.hotchocolatehits.com/2014/12/double-chocolate-peppermint-fudge.html" title="Double Chocolate Peppermint Fudge">
          fudge
         </a>
         and somewhere between a good ol
         <a href="http://www.hotchocolatehits.com/2014/10/apple-pie-cookies.html" title="Apple Pie Cookies">
          cookie
         </a>
         . Its full of all things good- peanut butter, coconut, oats and chocolate.
        </p>
        <p>
         I first tried no-bake cookies at a bake sale a while ago, but didnt appreciate its splendor back then. I made these a couple months ago, they were gone in 10 minutes, this barely gave them time to cool. Bite after glorious bite, so difficult to resist. Soft, sweet, nutty, chewy. Isnt that what constitutes a good cookie?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.hotchocolatehits.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/how-to-make-no-bake-cookies.jpg">
          <img alt="A no-bake chocolate cookie with all things delicious in under 10 minutes. Simple and delicious- that's how it's meant to be. #cookies #nobake #glutenfree" class="aligncenter wp-image-874 size-full" height="567" src="http://www.hotchocolatehits.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/how-to-make-no-bake-cookies.jpg" width="850"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The only purely healthy ingredient is the oats; however, you can make the cookies healthier by using almond butter instead of peanut butter, using a little less sugar if you like things a little less sweet and reducing the size of each cookie. The coconut in this recipe is optional; however, I wouldnt do away with it- the flavor and texture it adds is unbeatable. Drizzling with dark chocolate? Not necessary, but a must. It lookslike theyve taken hours to make- but in reality, it was only minutes. One of my favorite cookies  a must-have in your recipe binder.
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/e3F6_ZuFTvI" width="560">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          No-Bake Chocolate Cookies
         </div>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups granulated sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup cocoa powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup unsalted or salted butter- either works, or margarine
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup milk (skim, whole, evaporated- either works as long as it's not sweet)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            pinch salt (if the butter is unsalted)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 cups quick cooking oats
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            -1/2 cup desiccated coconut
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup crunchy peanut butter
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp vanilla extract (PLEASE make sure that you're using vanilla extract not essence, essence is a lot more powerful in taste so you'll have to reduce the amount you add!)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a saucepan, combine the sugar, cocoa, butter and milk.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bring these ingredients to a boil on medium heat, boil for 2 minutes, then turn off the heat.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Stir in the oats coconut, peanut butter, salt and vanilla.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Spoon 2 tbsp sized mounts of the mixture onto a parchment paper lined/ greased tray and flatten into the shape of a cookie. Work quickly as the mixture sets- just like fudge.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cool for about 10 minutes, drizzle with melted chocolate and sprinkle atop some crushed peanuts and desiccated coconut for a fancier finish.
           </li>
          </ol>
