        <p>
         Sure, pretty much everyone could
         <a href="http://greatist.com/grow/surprising-benefits-therapy">
          benefit from therapy
         </a>
         . But not everyone can afford it. Thankfully, theres a whole world of free or affordable mental health care out there designed to help you with just about every issue, whether thats
         <a href="http://greatist.com/health/ultimate-guide-quitting-smoking">
          kicking an addiction
         </a>
         , managing your emotions, finding a group of like-minded peers, or recovering from trauma. Even better? Some of these resources are available whenever you need them. (No need to schedule an appointment between the hours of 9 and 5.) Support groups, hotlines and call centers, websites and online forums, and even apps can be put into action when you have a crisis or just need extra support.
        </p>

         <h3>
          Mental Health Apps
         </h3>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="media-g_oversize_inline_image media-element-container">
           <img alt="A Better Way to Think About Work-Life Balance" src="http://greatist.com/sites/default/files/styles/article_main/public/Smartphone-feature.jpg?itok=NQMdPG4O"/>
          </div>
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/act-coach/id804247934?mt=8" target="_blank">
             1. ACT Coach
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            ACT Coach teaches users how to tolerate negative thoughts and feelings by virtually guiding them through awareness exercises and giving tips on how to ditch self-doubt. With an extra focus on
            <a href="http://greatist.com/mindfulness">
             mindfulness
            </a>
            , this app also provides a log to track your progress. (Free;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/act-coach/id804247934?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://rockapproll.com/app/aetas" target="_blank">
             2. AETAS
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Designed by therapist Rosemary Sword, this app uses Time Perspective Therapy, a method developed to unglue us from unhelpful or obsessive thoughts. Chock-full of visual aids to encourage relaxation and self-soothing, AETAS also arms users with a
            <a href="http://psychcentral.com/lib/whats-your-time-perspective/0001365">
             time perspective inventory
            </a>
            that helps them understand how they view the past, present, and future will either help or hinder their happiness. ($4.99;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/aetas/id954977409?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://t2health.dcoe.mil/apps/breathe2relax" target="_blank">
             3. Breathe2Relax
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Sometimes, all we need to de-stress is take a few
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/breathing-exercises-relax">
             deep breaths
            </a>
            . Created by the National Center for Telehealth and Technology, this app teaches users how to do
            <a href="http://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases_conditions/hic_Understanding_COPD/hic_Pulmonary_Rehabilitation_Is_it_for_You/hic_Diaphragmatic_Breathing">
             diaphragmatic breathing
            </a>
            . Features include educational videos on the stress response, logs to record stress levels, and customizable guided breathing sessions. (Free;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/breathe2relax/id425720246?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.t2health.breathe2relax&amp;hl=en">
             Android
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/dbt-diary-card/id479013889?mt=8" target="_blank">
             4. DBT Diary Card and Skills Coach
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            This app works as a daily mood and thought
            <a href="http://greatist.com/health/keep-food-diary">
             diary
            </a>
            . But it also has a coaching module that gives tips on sticky emotional situations, like how to ask for what you need without drama or how to successfully resolve conflict. And users get positive reinforcement when they're consistent with their entries. The app also includes a super helpful
            <a href="http://psychcentral.com/lib/an-overview-of-dialectical-behavior-therapy/0001096">
             DBT
            </a>
            reference section for more info on coping skillsall backed by research. ($4.99;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/dbt-diary-card/id479013889?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.excelatlife.depression&amp;hl=en" target="_blank">
             5. Depression CBT Self-Help Guide
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Need help managing the blues?  Monitor dips in your mood, learn about clinical depression and treatments, try guided
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/40-ways-relax-5-minutes-or-less">
             relaxation
            </a>
            techniques, and learn strategies to challenge negative thinking with this app. It's all just a few taps and swipes away. (Free;
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.excelatlife.depression&amp;hl=en">
             Android
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/ecbt-calm/id356997070?mt=8" target="_blank">
             6. eCBT calm
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Implementing some of the many strategies of
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness-therapy-explained">
             cognitive behavioral therapy
            </a>
            , this app helps users assess their stress levels, practice mindfulness and relaxation skills, and connect their thoughts to feelings and behaviors. The end result is more calm in your everyday life and more awareness of your actions and emotions. ($0.99;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/ecbt-calm/id356997070?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.happify.com/" target="_blank">
             7. Happify
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Want to kick negative thoughts, nix worry, and dial down stress? The array of engaging games, activity suggestions, and gratitude prompts makes Happify a useful shortcut to a good mood. Designed with input from 18 health and happiness experts, Happifys positive mood-training program is psychologist approved. Even cooler? Its website links to bonus videos that are sure to make you smile. (Free;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/happify/id730601963?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://gethowareyou.com/en/" target="_blank">
             8. How Are You
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Tracking your moods can help you fight the blues and teach you to tune into positive things. That's the premise behind this app. But as a bonus, it also allows you to compare your mood with worldwide averages, see which emotions you feel the most, and export your mood tracking data so you can share it with a mental health professional or trusted friend. ($9.99-$12.99;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/how-are-you-mood-tracker/id691974213?mt=8&amp;ign-mpt=uo%3D4">
             iOS
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=co.quantumlab.howareyou">
             Android
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.anxietybc.com/mobile-app" target="_blank">
             9. MindShift
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            This straightforward stress management tool helps users re-think whats stressing them out through a variety of on-screen prompts. At the same time, the app encourages new ways to take charge of anxiety and tune into body signals. (Free;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/mindshift/id634684825?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.bstro.MindShift&amp;hl=en">
             Andriod
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/ca/app/operation-reach-out/id478899653?mt=8%20PTSD%20Coach%20-%20http://www.ptsd.va.gov/public/materials/apps/PTSDCoach.asp" target="_blank">
             10. Operation Reach Out
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            This mood tracker and resource locator was designed by Emory University researchers to aid in suicide prevention. The setup is simple: Users create a personal profile that includes emergency contact information, current medications, safety plans, and reminders for appointments or medications. Plus the app uses GPS to locate mental health care services nearby, should any user enter crisis mode. (Free;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/ca/app/operation-reach-out/id478899653?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=suicide.prevention.app&amp;hl=en">
             Android
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.ptsd.va.gov/public/materials/apps/PTSDCoach.asp" target="_blank">
             11. PTSD Coach
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            If you suffer from PTSD symptoms, this 24-hour tool that's linked directly with support services is a valuable thing to download. Available as an app or on the Web, PTSD Coach lets users select the specific issue they want to deal with (from anxiety and anger to
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/secret-better-sleep-infographic">
             insomnia
            </a>
            and alienation), and then gives them guidance on how to lift their mood, shift their mindset, and reduce stress. (Free;
            <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/ptsd-coach/id430646302?mt=8&amp;ign-mpt=uo%3d2">
             iOS
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=gov.va.ptsd.ptsdcoach&amp;hl=en">
             Android
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/quit-it-3.0-stop-smoking/id285538794?mt=8" target="_blank">
             12. Quit It
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            If youre a smoker, you probably already know all about the nasty
            <a href="http://www.drugabuse.gov/publications/research-reports/tobacco/what-are-medical-consequences-tobacco-use">
             health consequences
            </a>
            . But it probably doesnt stop you from lighting up. But this app's approach is different. It shows you the hit
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/ways-save-money">
             your wallet takes
            </a>
            every time you get another pack. Even better: Quit It calculates how much money you save each time you
            <em>
             dont
            </em>
            smoke. Think of it as extra financial incentive to kick nicotine and tobacco (and save for something far better!). ($1.99;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/quit-it-3.0-stop-smoking/id285538794?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/quit-pro-stop-smoking-now/id336786774?mt=8" target="_blank">
             13. Quit Pro
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Think of this as a
            <a href="http://greatist.com/health-and-fitness-tracking">
             fitness tracker
            </a>
            for your smoking habit. By monitoring your cravings over time, the places you puff the most, the triggers that lead you to light up, and the money you save by resisting a cigarette, this comprehensive app is a much better thing to have in your back pocket than a pack of smokes. (Free;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/quit-pro-stop-smoking-now/id336786774?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.bitsmedia.android.quitpro">
             Android
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://sam-app.org.uk/" target="_blank">
             14. SAM
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            How do you know whats pushing you over the edge and reel yourself back in? SAMs approach is to monitor
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/reduce-anxiety">
             anxious thoughts
            </a>
            , track behavior over time, and use guided self-help exercises to discourage stress. SAM takes it to the next level by offering a Social Cloud feature that allows users to confidentially share their progress with an online community for added support. (Free;
            <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/app/sam/id666767947">
             iOS
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.uwe.myoxygen">
             Android
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://stepaway.biz/" target="_blank">
             15. Step Away
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            A study funded by the National Institute of Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism found that this pro-sobriety app helped reduce heavy drinking among users by 60 percent. Step Away offers tips on maintaining sobriety, encouragement, and strategies to avoid drinking during stressful times. Youll also be able to plug in your top drinking triggers to prepare yourself before facing down any tricky situation. (Free;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/step-away-mobile-intervention/id688669242?mt=8&amp;ign-mpt=uo%3D4">
             iOS
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://stopbreathethink.org/" target="_blank">
             16. Stop, Breathe, Think!
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Got five minutes? Thats enough time to cultivate mindfulness, which can improve your mood, lower stress, and help you feel more compassion toward yourself and the world. Skeptical? Well, consider that
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/how-to-be-happy-pursuit-of-happiness">
             mindfulness and happiness
            </a>
            tend to go hand-in-hand. And as added incentive, this app can also improve your focus. (Free;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/id778848692">
             iOS
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.stopbreathethink.app">
             Android
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/stop-drinking-andrew-johnson/id365566955?mt=8" target="_blank">
             17. Stop Drinking
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Relying on the powers of relaxation, visualization, and positive suggestions, this pro-sobriety app has the goal of calming your mind and geting it to a less stressed placewhere youll be less likely to crave a drink. Take advantage of the reminder feature that gives periodic chimes to prompt you to breathe and focus on the good throughout the day. ($2.99;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/stop-drinking-andrew-johnson/id365566955?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            and
            <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.hivebrain.andrewjohnson.drinking&amp;hl=en">
             Android
            </a>
            )
           </p>

         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/stress-anxiety-companion-beautifully/id786238252?mt=8" target="_blank">
             18. Stress and Anxiety Companion
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Sure, we know that releasing
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/thinking-negative-thoughts-mindfulness-meditation">
             negative thoughts
            </a>
            , practicing relaxation techniques, and engaging in mindful awareness is good for our wellbeing. But that doesnt mean we actually do it. This app can help make the process a lot easier by guiding you through proven techniques to reduce those off-kilter thoughts and emotions while cultivating a much more present mindset. Additional features allow you to identify anxiety triggers to make sure they dont catch you off guard. ($4.99;
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/stress-anxiety-companion-beautifully/id786238252?mt=8">
             iOS
            </a>
            )
           </p>
          </div>

          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Bet you didnt think you could chat with a
            <a href="http://greatist.com/therapy">
             therapist

          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            The granddaddy of support groups, AA has been helping alcoholics since 1935. Founded by two former drinking buddies, the program was loosely modeled on a popular religious movement bent on owning your errors, assessing your character, and making amends. Today it boasts over two million members worldwide and welcomes folks of any age and all political, sexual, and gender orientations. No dues or fees required.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.al-anon.org/" target="_blank">
             55. Al-Anon
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Sometimes the issue is not your drinking, but a friend's or family member's whose issues with alcohol have disrupted your life. Al-Anon supports individuals affected by others alcoholism and even offers a specialized program for teens (Alateen).
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.ca.org/" target="_blank">
             56. Cocaine Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Started in Los Angeles in 1982, Cocaine Anonymous counts around 30,000 members across the globe. Like its name implies, CA is modeled after the 12 steps and peer-support design of AA. People wrestling with addictions to other substances in addition to cocaine are also welcome to address that here. Meetings are free and open to all. The only requirement: You want to stop using.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.crystalmeth.org/" target="_blank">
             57. Crystal Meth Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Crystal Meth Anonymous was also born out of AA. After witnessing an upsurge of crystal meth addicts joining AA to get sober, one former addict began this offshoot in 1994. He figured meth addicts could benefit from a 12-step model, but they needed their own tailored version of support. Turns out, that hunch was right, as today you can find over 600 CMA meetings worldwide.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://draonline.qwknetllc.com/index.html" target="_blank">
             58. Dual Recovery Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Dual Recovery Anonymous offers a specialized 12-step program for folks grappling with chemical dependencies on top of emotional and psychological disorders. Similar to other 12-step peer support programs, the only requirement for entry is a desire to get sober and, in this case, a desire to manage your mood.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.gamblersanonymous.org/ga/locations/" target="_blank">
             59. Gamblers Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            The 12-step system doesnt just apply to substances. People who find themselves frequently in debt or otherwise stressed by excessive gambling habits have made good use of this support group. And it's no newcomer; its been on the scene since 1957.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://lifering.org/" target="_blank">
             60. LifeRing
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            LifeRing doesnt involve any official steps. And theres no need for sponsorship here, either. The organization does, however, provide forums and face-to-face meetings to help people who wish to be
            <a href="http://greatist.com/connect/what-I-learned-from-giving-up-alcohol">
             sober
            </a>
            design their own recoveries in a way that makes sense for them.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.marijuana-anonymous.org" target="_blank">
             61. Marijuana Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Sure, pot is now legal in some states, but like other legal substances (ahem, alcohol) that doesnt mean it wont
            <a href="http://greatist.com/play/myths-about-marijuana">
             trigger addiction
            </a>
            issues. If your tokes are getting in the way of your life, these national meetings can help bring back some balance.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.na.org/" target="_blank">
             62. Narcotics Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Designed for drug addicts grappling with all types of chemical dependencies, NA models itself after, you guessed it, the traditions and steps of Alcoholics Anonymous.  And its been in the business of keeping folks around the world drug-free since 1953.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.nar-anon.org/naranon/" target="_blank">
             63. Nar-Anon
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Similar to Al-Anon and Alateen, Nar-Anon offers support to the family members and friends of people struggling with addiction. Meetings give a safe space for people to sort out their feelings and make sense of their loved ones addictive behavior and its impact on their lives.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.oa.org/" target="_blank">
             64. Overeaters Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Yes, you can get hooked on the
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/science-why-we-binge">
             highs associated with food
            </a>
            . And if you are, youre not alone: About 11 percent  of us are addicted to food
            <cite class="citation-reference" data-cite-reference="">
             <a data-cke-saved-href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3207274/" href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3207274/">
              How Prevalent is &ldquo;Food Addiction&rdquo;?
             </a>
             Meule, A. Frontiers in Psychiatry, 2: 61.
            </cite>
            . Thankfully, there are over 6,500 OA meetings across the globe designed in the 12-step spirit of AA to help people manage compulsive eating habits and cultivate a healthier relationship with food.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="https://saa-recovery.org/" target="_blank">
             65. Sex Addicts Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Were all for a healthy and
            <a href="http://greatist.com/health/health-benefits-of-sex">
             happy sex life
            </a>
            . But sometime people use sex to self-medicate, self-destruct, and, in the process, wreak havoc on their own and others wellbeing. Researchers estimate between 3 and 6 percent of the population is at risk
            <cite class="citation-reference" data-cite-reference="">
             <a data-cke-saved-href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3134413/" href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3134413/">
              Prevalence of the Addictions: A Problem of the Majority or the Minority?
             </a>
             Sussman, S., Lisha, N., Griffiths, M. Evaluation and the Health Professions, 2011 Mar; 34(1): 3-56.
            </cite>
            . Men and women can learn to manage their behavior, gain insight into their impulses, and start their recovery through peer support with 12-step SAA meetings hosted across the globe.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.sossobriety.org/home.html" target="_blank">
             66. Secular Organizations for Sobriety
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            An alternative to AA, this support network is for anyone seeking sobriety. SOS backs individual empowerment while also declaring a strong respect for science and healthy
            <a href="http://www.psmag.com/books-and-culture/75-years-alcoholics-anonymous-time-admit-problem-74268">
             skepticism surrounding treatment
            </a>
            . Meetings are held across the U.S. and abroad.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.smartrecovery.org/" target="_blank">
             67. SMART Recovery
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Twelve-step programs not your thing? No problem. SMART recovery offers another alternative to AA and its offshoots. Modeled after research-based
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness-therapy-explained">
             cognitive behavioral therapy
            </a>
            strategies, SMART meetings do not require you to identify as an addict or alcoholic. Its also less steeped in spirituality and puts greater emphasis on empowering members. The group isn't exclusively for alcoholics; SMART doors are open to individuals struggling with all types of addictions.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.womenforsobriety.org" target="_blank">
             68. Women For Sobriety
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Women For Sobriety is based on the belief that theres a bit of a gender divide when it comes to getting clean. Rather than emphasizing humility and lack of control over your drinking habits, WFS aims to bolster womens self-worth, personal responsibility, and problem-solving skills. And instead of 12 steps, WFS offers a variety of strategies to practice acceptance and avoid getting strung up on the past. Groups can be found in the U.S. and Canada. Ladies only, please.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <h3>
          Other Support Groups
         </h3>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="media-g_oversize_inline_image media-element-container">
           <img alt="Therapy Feature" src="http://greatist.com/sites/default/files/styles/article_main/public/TherapyFeature.jpg?itok=gNhdMiO9"/>
          </div>
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.afsp.org/coping-with-suicide-loss/find-support/find-a-support-group" target="_blank">
             69. American Foundation for Suicide Prevention
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            If youve lost someone you love to suicide, AFSP support groups will give you a place to discuss your feelings and manage grief in the company of others who get itbecause theyve been there too. While some meetings take place during a set time span, others are ongoing and open to attendees showing up as frequently as they wish.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.adaa.org/supportgroups" target="_blank">
             70. Anxiety and Depression Association of America Support Groups
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            ADAA offers an extensive, searchable list of free or affordable resources that cater to specific anxieties, phobias, and
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/34-ways-bust-bad-mood-ten-minutes">
             mood issues
            </a>
            . The organization also offers resources for general support for faulty thinking and behavior patterns, relationship problems, and self-esteem issues.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://coda.org/" target="_blank">
             71. Co-dependents Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            If you struggle with low self-esteem and find yourself frequently sucked into
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/unhealthy-relationship-exercise">
             relationships
            </a>
            where your needs remain unmet or minimized, this support group can help you set healthy boundaries. CoDa meetings are modeled after AAs 12 steps and seek to empower individuals to break free from self-destructive habits and develop healthier relationships.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.dbsalliance.org/site/PageServer?pagename=peer_support_group_locator" target="_blank">
             72. Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            This alliance offers over 700 national groups with peer support. Its a judgment-free discussion zone where you can open up about lifes challenges brought on by living with
            <a href="http://greatist.com/happiness/can-exercise-help-treat-depression">
             depression
            </a>
            or bipolar disorder. The best part? All groups are totally free.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://allone.com/12/ea/" target="_blank">
             73. Emotions Anonymous
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Even if you don't have an addiction, you can still apply the 12-step model to manage negative thinking,
            <a href="http://greatist.com/grow/downside-self-esteem">
             self-esteem issues
            </a>
            , loneliness, and other destructive feelings with the support of over 1,000 EA meetings worldwide. (And if you
            <em>
             are
            </em>
            wrestling with substance or behavioral addictions, youre still welcome to attend.)
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.glbtnearme.org/" target="_blank">
             74. GLBT Near Me
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            The GLBT National Resource Database offers over 1,000 support services for people of all genders, sexual orientations, races, and ages. Plug your zip code into their handy local resource finder and voil: Youll be connected to an affordable (if not entirely free) support group catered to your needs that's close to home.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.anad.org/eating-disorders-get-help/eating-disorders-support-groups/" target="_blank">
             75. National Association of Anorexia Nervosa and Associated Disorders
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            These eating disorder support groups come in a few different formats. Most are led by a psychotherapist, while others are run by a
            <a href="http://greatist.com/eat/holiday-healthy-eating-tips">
             nutritionist
            </a>
            . The vast majority are free. The main goal of these programs is to offer a safe space for people struggling with disordered eating to openly discuss their turmoil and receive guidance on how they can heal. To find the closest one to you, click your state on ANADs support groups page.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.nationaleatingdisorders.org/find-treatment/support-groups-research-studies" target="_blank">
             76. National Eating Disorders Association
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Like ANAD, NEDA offers an extensive list of support groups for individuals with eatingd disorders. All you have to do is plug your state into their search engine and find groups nearby. For those who want more personalized peer support, check out
            <a href="http://www.nationaleatingdisorders.org/neda-navigators">
             NEDA Navigator
            </a>
            , a program that connects individuals looking to overcome disordered eating with a person whos been there and can act as a guide during recovery.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://pflag-chapter-map.herokuapp.com/" target="_blank">
             77. Parents, Families, and Friends of Lesbians and Gays
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            If youre a friend, family member, or parent of someone who identifies anywhere on the LGBTQ spectrum, PFLAG is your go-to resource for all things related to education, advocacy, and social outreach. PFLAG can also help those struggling to come to terms with a loved ones sexual or gender orientation. Plug in your hometown to their search engine to find a local chapter.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://healgrief.org/grief-support-resources/NATIONAL/" target="_blank">
             78. Heal Grief
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            At some point in our lives, all of us will have to wrestle with the many stages of grief. But it helps if weve got people to talk to about our lossespecially someone in the midst of a similar grieving process or someone who have come through to the other side. Heal Griefs support services extend across the U.S. and can be found, organized by state, via the drop down menu on the group's website.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://iocdf.org/find-help/" target="_blank">
             79. International OCD Foundation
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Obsessive Compulsive Disorder affects millions of people across the globe. About 2.3 percent of Americans have the disorder
            <cite class="citation-reference" data-cite-reference="">
             <a data-cke-saved-href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2538471/" href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2538471/">
              An Epidemiological Perspective of Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder in Children and Adolescents
             </a>
             . Fogel, J. The Canadian Child and Adolescent Psychiatry Review, 2003 Mar; 12(2): 33-36.
            </cite>
            . Thankfully, theres a ton of support out there, and its likely closer than you think. Check out the listings on the International OCD Foundations website to find a group near you.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.sidran.org/help-desk/get-help/" target="_blank">
             80. Sidrans HelpDesk
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            The Sidran Institute offers services for all kinds of people grappling with PTSD, from
            <a href="http://greatist.com/fitness/military-uses-yoga-cure-ptsd-121712">
             military vets
            </a>
            to white-collar workers. Trauma can trigger a huge amount of emotional suffering, and without help, some people can be debilitated by their symptoms. If you're wrestling with these shockwaves, contact Sidran to get more info on support groups.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            <a href="http://www.trich.org/treatment/support-groups.html" target="_blank">
             81. Trichotillomania Learning Center
            </a>
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Trichotillomania is an obsessive compulsive disorder where sufferers compulsively pull out their hair or incessantly pick at their skin to the point of bruising. Symptoms can be damaging, but so can the isolation people feel with this disorder. This organization's support groups can help people manage their impulses, find better coping skills for their anxiety, and find company in their struggle.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
