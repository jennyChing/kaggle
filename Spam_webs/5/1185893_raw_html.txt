        <hr/>
        <div class="dmg-words">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Dogster-Monday-Miracle-badge_49_0_0_0_21.jpg">
           <img alt="Dogster-Monday-Miracle-badge_49_0_0_0_2" class=" size-full wp-image-312615 alignright" height="250" src="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Dogster-Monday-Miracle-badge_49_0_0_0_21.jpg" width="250"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          When Julie LeRoy first laid eyes on a five-month-old puppy with an extreme underbite, long front legs, and a short, curved spine, the animal control officer knew she was looking at a dog who needed saving. LeRoy didnt yet know anything about short-spine syndrome, the congenital anomaly that made the puplook so different, but she knew thedog was special.
         </p>
         <p>
          They brought her out on a leash made of a piece of twine, LeRoy says, remembering that fateful day in Durham, North Carolina, when she first met the dog she would later adopt, Cuda.
         </p>
         <p>
          The couple  complainants [in a case unrelated to Cuda] had asked me if I wanted to see her, she recalls. They had just had her for only two weeks, and they had bought her on Craigslist from a backyard breeder.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_312910" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda3-with-mom.jpg">
           <img alt="It was love at first sight for Julie LeRoy and Cuda.  (Photo courtesy Chariot Creative)" class="size-full wp-image-312910" height="480" src="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda3-with-mom.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           It was love at first sight for Julie LeRoy and Cuda. (Photo courtesy Chariot Creative)
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          When the little puppy was brought out, LeRoy couldnt believe her eyes. The couple had mentioned the dogs underbite, but they hadnt prepared LeRoy for the extent of Cudas differences. She was shocked, and LeRoy knew this pupwould not survive for long in the shelter system.
         </p>
         <p>
          I just thought, I cannot bring this dog to the shelter; theyre not going to understand. It was a high-kill shelter  theyre going to euthanize her.
         </p>
         <p>
          The couple told LeRoy they needed to get rid of the dog right away, so she promised to come back after her shift. In the meantime, she began texting her husband, who was initially against the idea as the LeRoys already had four dogs at home.
         </p>
         <p>
          Eventually, her spouse relented over text message, and LeRoy began mentally preparing herself to go back for the strange-looking puppy. Unsure of the extent of the dogs special needs, LeRoy began to feel nervous. A small part of her hoped that the couple would decide to keepCuda after all.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_312911" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda1.jpg">
           <img alt="(Photo courtesy Chariot Creative)" class="wp-image-312911 size-full" height="480" src="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda1.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Cuda has a distinctive profile. (Photo courtesy Chariot Creative)
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          But there she was, and I just put her in my arms, says LeRoy, who has never regretted her decision to adopt Cuda.
         </p>
         <p>
          Within two days of taking Cuda home, LeRoy sought medical attention for the pup, who needed to be spayed.
         </p>
         <p>
          The vet couldnt find anything else odd, she remembers, adding that Cuda was not in any pain, despite her compressed appearance.Her new pup was happy and healthy, but LeRoy still wondered if her physical deformities would shorten her life or cause her pain in the future.
         </p>
         <p>
          We thought she was the only one in the world for the longest time, explains LeRoy. I had just chocked it up to inbreeding.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_312915" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda-xray2.jpg">
           <img alt="Cudas spine is fused and curved." class="size-full wp-image-312915" height="339" src="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda-xray2.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Cudas spine is fused and curved.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Eventually, LeRoy created a
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/cudacares/timeline" target="_blank">
           Facebook profile
          </a>
          for her unique pup, documenting Cudas daily life. Social media proved to be the key to finding out more about what caused Cuda to look the way she did.
         </p>
         <p>
          I found another dog like her about seven months after I first put her online. That dogs located in Italy, says LeRoy, who also found out about
          <a href="http://www.dogster.com/the-scoop/dog-rescue-adoption-akita-pig-half-a-body-whole-heart-pictures-photos">
           Pig
          </a>
          , another famous short-spine dog, through the social network.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Italian dogs human helped LeRoy learn more about short-spine syndrome and track down other dogs with the condition. LeRoy now knows of 11short-spine dogs worldwide, the oldest one being 13years old.
         </p>
         <p>
          They dont know what causes it. Its a congenital anomaly, explains LeRoy, who has done extensive research on short-spine syndromeandsays the rare condition was first identified centuries ago.
         </p>
         <p>
          There was actually an 18th or 19th century painter who featured one in a painting.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_312943" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda4-left-space.jpg">
           <img alt="cuda4 left space" class="wp-image-312943 size-full" height="480" src="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda4-left-space.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           This photo of Cuda would actually make a pretty painting.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Humans in the 21st century are just as interested in looking at short-spine dogs as that painter was, and in 2011 LeRoy and her husband took Cuda to a place where plenty of people could get a good look.
         </p>
         <p>
          When she was about a year-and-a-half old, we took her to California for the Worlds Ugliest Dog contest, LeRoy remembers.
         </p>
         <p>
          Cuda didnt win, but did get third place in the pedigree division.
         </p>
         <p>
          She wasnt feeling well on the way back from California, and I couldnt figure out why. She kept getting worse and worse.
         </p>
         <p>
          When the LeRoys got home, young Cuda was diagnosed with diabetes.None of the other short-spines have it. Shes been on insulin since she was about 18 months old.
         </p>
         <p>
          Managing Cudas diabetes has been the biggest challenge in caring for the unique dog. The disease, in combination with her short-spine deformities, has created some additional health issues for Cuda. According to LeRoy, Cudas extreme underbite made some of her bottom teeth loose from being constantly exposed to air.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_312919" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda2.jpg">
           <img alt="Cudas bottom teeth get a lot of air. (Photo courtesy Chariot Creative)" class="size-full wp-image-312919" height="480" src="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/cuda2.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Cudas bottom teeth get a lot of air. (Photo courtesy Chariot Creative)
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          We proactively had them removed so that she didnt develop an infection from the diabetes, so shes now missing three front teeth.
         </p>
         <p>
          Cudas special needs dont stop there. She also has a curly trachea and esophagus  a physical difference that caused great concern for her humans.
         </p>
         <p>
          One incident occurred where something was lodged and we couldnt get it out and she passed out. My husband actually breathed into her mouth and dislodged whatever was there. It was the most terrifying thing.
         </p>
         <p>
          To ensure that doesnt happen again, Cuda eats from a raised bowl and can only eat certain treats.
         </p>
         <p>
          Despite her special needs, Cuda gets along perfectly with her animalsiblings.
         </p>
         <p>
          Shes always trying to engage them in play, says LeRoy. Based on what Ive heard from the other short-spine [dog] parents, they all have a very playful personality.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_312924" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Cuda-and-the-cat.jpg">
           <img alt="Cuda is one of the smaller animals in her household." class="size-full wp-image-312924" height="538" src="http://www.dogster.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Cuda-and-the-cat.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Cuda is one of the smaller animals in her household.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          These days, LeRoy is busy chasing after Cuda and helping other people with special needs animals.
         </p>
         <p>
          I just want to educate people, says LeRoy, who wants people to have all the necessary information before they adopt a special needs dog.I want to develop a network so that if people have a short-spine dog they can pick up the phone and give someone a call.
         </p>
         <p>
          Putting Cuda online and creating her own network helped LeRoy clear up many of the mysteries about why Cuda was so different, which included her genetic background.
         </p>
         <p>
          We had her DNA tested when she was about a year old. One of her fans actually purchased the test just to see, and she was classified as a
          <a href="http://www.dogster.com/dog-breeds/Staffordshire_Bull_Terrier">
           Staffordshire Terrier
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Unlike most Staffordshire Terriers, little Cuda just happens to be smaller than the family cat.
         </p>

	function addApiToVideos() {
	    //find all iframes
	    var ifs = document.getElementsByTagName("iframe");
	    //each iframe
	    for (var i in ifs) {
	        var iframe = ifs[i];
	        //if it has a src
	        if (iframe.src) {
	            //assume we don't already have a query
	            var append = "?";
	            //if we find a ?, we use a &amp;
	            if (iframe.src.search(/\?/) != -1) {
	                append = "&amp;";
	            }
	            //if it's a vimeo page
	            if (iframe.src.search(/^http[s]?:\/\/.+\.vimeo.com/) != -1 &amp;&amp; iframe.src.search("api=1") == -1) {
	                //reload the video with the new query
	                iframe.src += append + "api=1";
	            }
	            //if it's a youtube page
	            else if (iframe.src.search(/^http[s]?:\/\/.+\.youtube.com/) != -1 &amp;&amp; iframe.src.search("jsapi=1") == -1) {
	                //reload the video with the new query
	                iframe.src += append + "jsapi=1";
	            }
	        }
	    }
	}
	addApiToVideos();
