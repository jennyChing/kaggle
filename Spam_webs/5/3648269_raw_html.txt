      <header class="post-header">
       <h1 class="post-title">
        <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/2014/12/tips-for-doing-business-in-china.html" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Tips For Doing Business in China">
         Tips For Doing Business in China
        </a>
       </h1>
       <span class="post-byline">
        By
        <a href="http://harrismoure.com/our-team/dan-harris/" rel="author external" title="Visit Dan Harriss website">
         Dan Harris
        </a>
        on
        <time class="post-date">
         December 23rd, 2014
        </time>
       </span>
       <span class="post-categories">
        Posted in
        <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/basics-of-china-business-law/" rel="category tag">
         Basics of China Business Law
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/china-business/" rel="category tag">
         China Business
        </a>
       </span>
      </header>
      <section class="post-content">
       <p>
        A while ago I was interviewed by Laurel Delany for her article,
        <a href="http://importexport.about.com/od/DoingBusinessIn/a/Legal-Tips-For-American-Small-Business-Owners-Doing-Business-In-China.htm?utm_term=Doing%20Business%20in%20China&amp;utm_content=p1-main-1-title&amp;utm_medium=sem-rel&amp;utm_source=msn&amp;utm_campaign=adid-e9874090-1778-4ec7-806d-7ef41f10fa48-0-ab_mse_ocode-5994&amp;ad=semD&amp;an=msn_s&amp;am=exact&amp;q=Doing%20Business%20in%20China&amp;dqi=&amp;o=5994&amp;l=sem&amp;qsrc=6&amp;askid=e9874090-1778-4ec7-806d-7ef41f10fa48-0-ab_mse">
         Legal Tips for American Small Business Owners Doing Business in China
        </a>
        Laurel wrote the book,
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1430257911/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=1430257911&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkId=FHS6MV3Q2VSFW4PH">
         Exporting: The Definitive Guide to Selling Abroad Profitably
        </a>
        , a truly excellent book on importing and exporting.
       </p>
       <p>
        My interview (modified slightly) is below.
        <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/files/2014/12/Doing-Business-in-China.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-22371" height="300" src="http://www.chinalawblog.com/files/2014/12/Doing-Business-in-China-274x300.jpg" width="274"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         LD:
        </strong>
        What are the top five legal pitfalls for small business owners doing business in China?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         DH:
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         Its a very different legal system. Little to nothing is implied.
        </li>
        <li>
         You generally must
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/danharris/2014/12/22/how-to-guard-against-china-counterfeiting/">
          register your intellectual property in China
         </a>
         to protect it there.
        </li>
        <li>
         You absolutely must have
         <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/2010/04/china_employment_contracts_ten.html">
          written contracts in Chinese with your employee
         </a>
         s.
        </li>
        <li>
         For
         <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/2014/05/china-contracts-that-work.html">
          contracts
         </a>
         in general, specificity is critical.
        </li>
        <li>
         Foreign companies are under a microscope; you will be treated differently than domestic companies.
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/danharris/2014/11/30/foreign-businesses-five-ways-to-measure-your-china-risk/">
          Know your risks.
         </a>
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        LD:What are the three essential elements to crafting an effective
        <a href="http://importexport.about.com/od/DevelopingSalesAndDistribution/a/What-To-Do-An-Overseas-Sales-Agent-Distributor-Or-Wholesaler-Doesn-T-Perform.htm">
         supplier contract
        </a>
        with a Chinese company?
       </p>
       <p>
        DH:
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/2014/05/china-contracts-that-work.html">
          Jurisdiction of disputes
         </a>
         should generally be in China. If you win a lawsuit in most countries outside of China (like the United States), you cannot enforce your judgment in China.
        </li>
        <li>
         Be sure to protect intellectual property. You should make clear in your contract what belongs to you and what your supplier must do to protect your physical and intellectual property. Use an
         <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/2013/12/china-nnn-agreements-watching-the-sausage-get-made-2.html">
          NNN Agreement
         </a>
         or an NNN provision for this.
        </li>
        <li>
         Be incredibly specific about all requirements. If it isnt in your contract, the courts generally will not put it there by implication. Also, if its not in your contract, your supplier likely wont do it.
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        <strong>
         LD:
        </strong>
        Is there a definitive statement that should be declared in a
        <a href="http://importexport.about.com/od/ChoosingImportProductSupplier/a/An-Inside-Guide-On-Sourcing-In-China.htm">
         sourcing
        </a>
        contract to ensure the buyer has a good chance of prevailing should there ever be an issue with the seller?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         DH:
        </strong>
        <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/2014/07/china-commercial-contracts-writing-the-damage-provision.html">
         Contract damages provisions
        </a>
        are usually the best way both to ensure compliance and to increase the chances of prevailing in litigation. If there is ever litigation, is it better to take place in China or the United States? Almost always it makes sense to have your contract in Chinese with disputes to be resolved in a Chinese court. Winning against a Chinese company in a US court has virtually no value unless that Chinese company has assets outside China, and it usually doesnt. Know
        <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/2014/10/how-to-sue-china-companies.html">
         how to sue a Chinese company
        </a>
        before you write your contract.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         LD:
        </strong>
        Based on your experience, whats the single biggest mistake small businesses make when doing business in China? How do you overcome it?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         DH:
        </strong>
        The biggest mistake is believing that China has no laws. American companies that believe China has no laws fail to take the legal steps necessary to protect themselves in China. Those companies end up without protections and they then end up with big problems.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         LD:
        </strong>
        With all its challenges, why should small businesses do business in China at all?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         DH:
        </strong>
        Because year in and year out, something like 90% of Western businesses doing business in China operate profitably inChinaand because China has 1.3 billion people, most of whom are getting richer every year.
       </p>
      </section>
