        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Lost My Name Personalised Books For Children
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-07-28T07:33:05+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          July 28, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.frugalmomeh.com/author/elizabeth" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Elizabeth
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.frugalmomeh.com/2014/07/lost-name-personalised-books-children.html#comments">
           15 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <script>
         &lt;!--
dynamicgoogletags.update();
//--&gt;
        </script>
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="18498706" data-link="http://www.frugalmomeh.com/2014/07/lost-name-personalised-books-children.html" data-summary="" data-title="Lost My Name Personalised Books For Children">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.frugalmomeh.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/IMG_7192.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_7192" class="aligncenter wp-image-7703" height="409" src="http://www.frugalmomeh.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/IMG_7192-1024x682.jpg" width="614"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im sure Ive mentioned it before but books are a big thing in my house. Keira and Ava have a giant collection of books, some are new and some are from my own childhood. They even have a few personalised books which are sure bets on capturing Keiras attention and produces a giggle at every mention of her name.
        </p>
        <p>
         We were sent a new personalised book for each of them from
         <a href="http://www.lostmy.name/#" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          LostMy.Name
         </a>
         to check out and share with you. The book started as a DIY project by three dads and an uncle and it turned into something really great!
        </p>
        <p>
         Unlike other kids personalised books that are in the market where the childs name is in the story,the childs name IS the story. Every book is as special (and almost as wonderful) as the child it is made for and is based on the letters in their names.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.frugalmomeh.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/IMG_7188.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_7188" class="aligncenter wp-image-7706" height="409" src="http://www.frugalmomeh.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/IMG_7188-1024x682.jpg" width="614"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Each Lost My Name book features the story of a child who had lost his or her name. Off the child goes on an adventure to track down all the letters of their lost name from a whole host of different creatures, until finally the child is able to spell their name!
        </p>
        <p>
         Ordering the books is easy. There isnt much to do but input your childs name and sex then off you go to preview the story prior to ordering.
        </p>
        <p>
         When the two books arrived I immediately fell in love with them; the stories are cute and fun with lots of different creatures and characters to meet along the way. The illustrations are pretty gorgeous too!
        </p>
        <p>
         I was wondering if there would be a huge length difference between Keiras and Avas books but noticed that they threw in some filler type pages into Avas book that arent found in Keiras story so they end up around the same length. I thought that was pretty smart and showed how much thought has gone into this whole project.
        </p>
        <p>
         Another detail taken care of is multiples of a single letter in a name. For example, Ava has two As in her name. In her book, the little girl encounters an Aardvark, a Viking and an Angel.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.frugalmomeh.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSCN0261.jpg">
          <img alt="DSCN0261" class="aligncenter wp-image-7705" height="461" src="http://www.frugalmomeh.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSCN0261-1024x768.jpg" width="614"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I love how varied and not-so-typical the characters involved are. In Keiras book the little girl encounters a Knight, an Elephant, an Inuit man, a Robot, and an Aardvark.
        </p>
        <p>
         The books themselves are A4 landscape sized with nice thick pages to help with resist being torn by eager little hands. Speaking of little hands, the books are intended for ages 2-6 but I think they make pretty marvellous gifts for newborns and infants as well. They do get older dont they? Ava loves being read to and since the books have thicker pages she isnt able to tear it apart the way she has ruined others.
        </p>
        <p>
         I really love this unique approach to personalised books and am totally amazed that they are self-published and self-funded. They created over 150 illustrations, to be able to make books for every name.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Buy it:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         A personalised
         <a href="http://www.lostmy.name/#" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Lostmy.name
         </a>
         book costs $27.99 including free world-wide shipping.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
