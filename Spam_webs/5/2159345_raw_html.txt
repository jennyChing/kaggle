           <h2 class="onePostTitle">
            This Crazy Device is About to Completely Transform the Wine Industry
           </h2>
          </div>
          <div class="postDetails">
           <a href="">
           </a>
           By
           <span class="poster">
            Jessica Bourne at Coravin
           </span>
           |  2 Comments
          </div>
          <div class="postContent">
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.thesavory.com/drink/crazy-device-about-completely-transform-wine-industry.html?utm_source=facebook&amp;utm_medium=bitly">
             <img alt="" src="http://www.coravin.com/media/wysiwyg/p190s6s5i01d4ksgle1jg918nv4.jpg"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            "Starting at around $40 and climbing as high as your credit limit will allow, for most people a bottle of wine at a restaurant represents a significant financial gamble. Sure you can enlist the help of a well-versed sever or sommelier, but you can never be sure that the bottle you want to choose is right for you. If only there was a way to taste the wine without actually opening the bottle
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The Coravin, a futuristic wine opening device created by medial equipment developer Greg Lambrecht, is a new piece of technology that can extract wine from the bottle without opening it. The device uses a fine needle to pierce the cork and extracts the wine using a complex pump system. The wine is then returned to the shelf completely unaffected by the process, and continues to age as if nothing ever happened."
           </p>
