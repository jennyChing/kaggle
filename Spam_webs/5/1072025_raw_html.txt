      <h1>
       Fall Isnt Too Late for a Crab Feast; We Ship Crabs Overnight
      </h1>
      <div class="entry">
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <a href="https://www.marylandseafooddirect.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Steamed-Crabs-Overnight.jpg">
         <img alt="Crabs Overnight" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-191" height="300" src="https://www.marylandseafooddirect.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Steamed-Crabs-Overnight-199x300.jpg" width="199"/>
        </a>
        Now that fall is here, do you find yourself missing summer? Do you miss the barbeques, the beach, and the crab feasts? Waitwe can help you out with the last one! Fall isnt too late for a crab feast! You still have time before it gets too cold to have your friends and family over and gather around the table to enjoy one of Marylands favorite traditions. Crabs are actually still in season through the end of October!
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        So call your friends and family and invite them over! Last minute plans always turn out to be the best times, so thats why Maryland Seafood Direct ships crabs overnight! Weve even compiled a list of items for you, and a few tips for your crab feast:
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Newspaper
         </strong>
         . Cover the table with old newspapers before the feast to avoid messing up a tablecloth. Plus, it makes for easy cleanup.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Garbage
         </strong>
         <strong>
          bags
         </strong>
         . Lay these down on the table underneath of the newspaper to prevent moisture from seeping through the newspaper onto the table; it makes for even easier cleanup!
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Wooden
         </strong>
         <strong>
          mallets
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Metal
         </strong>
         <strong>
          shell
         </strong>
         <strong>
          crackers
         </strong>
         . These make the process a little easier, and cause a little less mess than mallets.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Knives
         </strong>
         . Knives can be very helpful when trying to get the savory claw meat out of the shell.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Paper
         </strong>
         <strong>
          towels
         </strong>
         . Paper towels are stronger than napkins and can be much more effective when trying to wipe your hands off.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Corn on
         </strong>
         <strong>
          the
         </strong>
         <strong>
          cob
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Beer
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          water
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          and
         </strong>
         <strong>
          other
         </strong>
         <strong>
          beverages
         </strong>
