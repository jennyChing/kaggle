     <section id="article-section-1">
      <p>
       The paintings of fanatical late medieval artist Hieronymus Bosch were popular for their little nightmare details: a man playing a flute made out of his own nose, or a bird-monster devouring sinners and pooping them into the gaping maw of a pit. King Philip II of Spainpatron of the Inquisitionkept one of Boschs grim paintings in his bedroom and would meditate on it for hours at a time.
      </p>
      <p>
       Boschs best-known painting remains
       <em>
        The Garden of Earthly Delights
       </em>
       . The center of the triptych shows a horde of naked humans in shocking tantric contortions. The right panel shows Hell, the natural destination of the denizens of the center panel.
      </p>
      <p>
       Now, thats all been turned into
       <a href="http://www.randomhouse.de/paperback/Coloring-Book-Hieronymus-Bosch/Sabine-Tauber/e446045.rhd?pub=58500">
        a product targeted at young museum gift-shop goers
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <figure style="max-width: 570.0px;">
       <picture style="position:relative; width:100%; height:0; overflow:hidden; padding-bottom:57.0175438596%;">
        <img alt="" class="lazyload" data-src="http://cdn.theatlantic.com/assets/media/img/posts/2014/10/Hieronymous_Bosch/f89a9cb52.jpg" style="position:absolute; width:100%; height:100%; top:0; left:0; border:0;"/>
       </picture>
       <figcaption class="caption">
        <em>
         The Garden of Earthly Delights
        </em>
        , 1490-1510 (Hieronymus Bosch/Wikimedia)
       </figcaption>
      </figure>
      <p>
       I have to admit that I was a bit skeptical when I was first asked to design a coloring book on Bosch with
       <em>
        The Garden of Earthly Delights
       </em>
       as the main piece, author Sabine Tauber told me. It does not seem appropriate for children at first sight. Tauber focused on lighter scenes and natural details to make the book more accessible. Its title font matches Dr. Seuss.
      </p>
     </section>
     <div class="ad-boxinjector-wrapper">
      <div class="ad" data-ad-starts-hidden="true" data-injected-ad="true" id="ad-boxinjector1">
       <noscript>
        <div>
         <a href="http://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/jump?iu=%2F4624%2FTheAtlanticOnline%2Fchannel_entertainment&amp;t=src%3Darticle%26pos%3Dboxinjector1&amp;sz=300x250%7C320x350%7C728x90%7C768x350%7C970x250%7C1024x350&amp;c=857697270&amp;tile=16" title="">
          <img alt="" src="http://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/ad?iu=%2F4624%2FTheAtlanticOnline%2Fchannel_entertainment&amp;t=src%3Darticle%26pos%3Dboxinjector1&amp;sz=300x250%7C320x350%7C728x90%7C768x350%7C970x250%7C1024x350&amp;c=857697270&amp;tile=16"/>
         </a>
        </div>
       </noscript>
      </div>
     </div>
     <section id="article-section-2">
      <p>
       It makes a good companion to
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/0152622101/ref=rdr_ext_tmb">
        <em>
         Pish Posh, Said Hieronymus Bosch
        </em>
       </a>
       . That childrens book takes the perspective of Boschs housekeeper, who's fed up with the mess his wild monsters make around the house.
      </p>
      <p>
       Those two products speak to the fact that despite Bosch dying almost 500 years ago, his dark visions seem more popular than ever. He has a giant, attractive new coffee table
       <a href="http://www.taschen.com/pages/en/catalogue/art/all/01102/facts.hieronymus_bosch_complete_works.htm">
        book
       </a>
       from art publisher Taschen. In 2007, his hometown of Den Bosch finally gave him a
       <a href="http://www.jheronimusbosch-artcenter.nl/">
        museum
       </a>
       . He is on a new line of
       <a href="http://beautifuldecay.com/2014/05/02/dr-martens/">
        Doc Martens
       </a>
       , and on
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Yizzam--Hieronymus-Garden-Leggings-1498-Large/dp/B00IS9FM3A/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=undefined&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=hieronymus+bosch+leggings">
        leggings
       </a>
       ,
       <em>
        and
       </em>
       on
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/ArtsyClothingCo--Hieronymus-Garden--Tagless--1497-X-Large/dp/B00F2M8CKG/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=undefined&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=hieronymus+bosch+tank+top">
        cutoffs
       </a>
       just so no one at your favorite brunch spot forgets about the way of all flesh. The guy even has his own line of
       <a href="http://www.boom-art.com/en/16-j-bosh-sk8-box-delights-3-skateboards.html">
        skateboards
       </a>
       . How did this happen?
      </p>
      <div style="text-align:center">
       <figure style="display: inline-block;max-width: 565.0px; width: 100%;">
        <picture style="position:relative; width:100%; height:0; overflow:hidden; padding-bottom:66.5486725664%;">
         <img alt="" class="lazyload" data-src="http://cdn.theatlantic.com/assets/media/img/posts/2014/10/hieronymus_bosch_x_dr_martens_01_565x376/f48bfdb3f.jpg" style="position:absolute; width:100%; height:100%; top:0; left:0; border:0;"/>
        </picture>
        <figcaption class="credit">
         Doc Martens
        </figcaption>
       </figure>
      </div>
      <p>
       Bosch enjoyed great popularity while he was alive. He inspired so many imitations that its challenging to define his original canon. But by the heady Counter-Reformation days of the early 17th century, Bosch was out and Baroque was in.
      </p>
      <p>
       "The Catholic Church was reasserting itself, and it wanted to emphasize the church and salvation and saints, which was not exactly what Bosch focused on, Larry Silver, a professor of art history at the University of Pennsylvania, said over the phone. Take Rubens. Theres just no way that Bosch and Rubens can be simultaneously alive. That's one of the things that spelled the end for him, that turn from pessimism to the lighter side.
      </p>
      <p>
       That state of affairs persisted until the early 20th century. Scholars like Carl Justi showed a musty sort of interest, but it took sexy avant-garde types like Andr Bretons Surrealists to begin Boschs reintroduction to popular society. The Surrealists praised imagination and the unconscious. They also delighted in Sadism and blasphemy, railing against organized religion and bourgeois morality.
      </p>
      <p>
       This sort of bohemian delight became a constant presence in Bosch history. A persistent thesis, first advanced by Wilhelm Fraenger in 1947, holds that Bosch was a member of a secret cult of sexpot heretics called The Brethren of the Free Spirit. In this interpretation, the central panel of the
       <em>
        Garden
       </em>
       doesnt show a fallen and sinful world, but the sect enjoying glorious tantric delights in a state-of-nature cornucopia of free love. It might not come as a surprise to learn that the painting gets a mention in Chapter 37 of
       <em>
        The Da Vinci Code
       </em>
       .
      </p>
     </section>
     <div class="ad-boxright-wrapper" data-pos="boxright">
      <div class="ad" data-ad-starts-hidden="true" data-injected-ad="true" id="ad-boxright1">
       <noscript>
        <div>
         <a href="http://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/jump?iu=%2F4624%2FTheAtlanticOnline%2Fchannel_entertainment&amp;t=src%3Darticle%26pos%3Dboxright1&amp;sz=300x250%7C300x600&amp;c=857697270&amp;tile=12" title="">
          <img alt="" src="http://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/ad?iu=%2F4624%2FTheAtlanticOnline%2Fchannel_entertainment&amp;t=src%3Darticle%26pos%3Dboxright1&amp;sz=300x250%7C300x600&amp;c=857697270&amp;tile=12"/>
         </a>
        </div>
       </noscript>
      </div>
     </div>
     <section id="article-section-3">
      <p>
       The sex-cult thesis was even more popular than the idea that Bosch had been on a bad trip from eating moldy rye bread. According to author Walter Bosing, it acted like a problem-solving wonder drug both for Bosch scholarship at institutes of higher learning and for the sensational appetites of certain audiences. A bizarre example is 60s philosopher Norman O. Brown, a sex therapist who connected Freuds anal eroticism theories to Martin Luthers doctrine of salvation by faith alone. He cited Boschs
       <em>
        Garden of Earthly Delights
       </em>
       as an illustration of his own theories put into practice.
      </p>
      <p>
       These interpretations, such perfect fits with modern stereotypes of the unhinged psychedelic artist, are a joke to todays experts. Bosing calls them scholarly nonsense, and all the academics I interviewed laughed when I brought them up.
      </p>
      <aside class="pullquote instapaper_ignore">
       I call him the first heavy-metal artist.
      </aside>
      <p>
       I want to say around the 60sIm not sure, maybe that would be too good a storypeople got tired of repeating the moralizing things and started to wonder if there wasnt a wilder intellect at work, said Craig Harbison, professor emeritus in art history at UMass. Dr. Silver agreed: In the Hugh Hefner era, you can imagine in the 60s and with the birth-control pill, all the sex seemed very enlightened and very liberating."
      </p>
      <p>
       These theories, while lacking the potency of the hippy days, are still ingrained in the popular imagination. In an
       <a href="http://pitchfork.com/features/interviews/7553-fleet-foxes/">
        interview
       </a>
       with
       <em>
        Pitchfork
       </em>
       , Fleet Foxes lead Robin Pecknold digressed on Flemish art: Bosch is the weirdest, too. He has LSD stuff before LSD was even invented. You can understand why the image persists. It takes no effort to accept that Bosch was a man totally outside of his time, attending
       <em>
        Eyes Wide Shut-
       </em>
       style orgies and dabbing maniacally at his canvas under the moonlight, days-old moldy rye crumbs peppering his beard.
      </p>
      <p>
       We do have the types of the starving artist, the visionary artist. That originated with Van Gogh, who is really the poster child of the romantic idea that individual difference and mental unbalance are necessary for the greatest artists, Silver said. But todays literature is anything but supportive of that. "The solitary genius idea is holding up less and less under current research. We are definitely breaking down the stereotype of Bosch as a crazed, drugged individual."
      </p>
      <figure style="max-width: 570.0px;">
       <picture style="position:relative; width:100%; height:0; overflow:hidden; padding-bottom:113.157894737%;">
        <img alt="" class="lazyload" data-src="http://cdn.theatlantic.com/assets/media/img/posts/2014/10/image-1/62cf1218a.jpg" style="position:absolute; width:100%; height:100%; top:0; left:0; border:0;"/>
       </picture>
       <figcaption class="caption">
        A production still from the upcoming film
        <em>
         Hieronymous
        </em>
        (Courtesy of Erik van Schaaik)
       </figcaption>
      </figure>
      <p>
       In any case, hes now a muse to some of todays most important creators. Director
       <a href="http://www.evolver.at/reloaded/Hellboy_2_Interview_Del_Toro/">
        Guillermo Del Toro
       </a>
       (
       <em>
        Pans Labyrinth
       </em>
       ,
       <em>
        Pacific Rim
       </em>
       ) cites Bosch as an inspiration for his famous surreal imagery. The late Alexander McQueen featured him in his final
       <a href="http://jezebel.com/5491341/mcqueens-final-work-includes-feathered-cherub-shoes">
        collection
       </a>
       . Best-selling author Michael Connelly named his star detective after the painter, and keeps a print of Boschs
       <em>
        Hell
       </em>
       painting above his writing desk.
      </p>
     </section>
     <div class="ad-boxinjector-wrapper">
      <div class="ad" data-ad-starts-hidden="true" data-injected-ad="true" id="ad-boxinjector2">
       <noscript>
        <div>
         <a href="http://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/jump?iu=%2F4624%2FTheAtlanticOnline%2Fchannel_entertainment&amp;t=src%3Darticle%26pos%3Dboxinjector2&amp;sz=300x250%7C320x350%7C728x90%7C768x350%7C970x250%7C1024x350&amp;c=857697270&amp;tile=16" title="">
          <img alt="" src="http://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/ad?iu=%2F4624%2FTheAtlanticOnline%2Fchannel_entertainment&amp;t=src%3Darticle%26pos%3Dboxinjector2&amp;sz=300x250%7C320x350%7C728x90%7C768x350%7C970x250%7C1024x350&amp;c=857697270&amp;tile=16"/>
         </a>
        </div>
       </noscript>
      </div>
     </div>
     <section id="article-section-4">
      <p>
       His relevance now may come from the fact that the present day shares some things with Boschs times, according to Harbison. [His age] was a little bit like today, people feeling like everything is sort of falling apart, he said. It leads to an artistic burst of fantasy and imagination, Over and over, Bosch painted the Biblical idea of man being born to trouble as the sparks fly upward. He held profound distrust of historic institutions. He pilloried the gilded clergy of his day. He had our
       <a href="http://blogs.indiewire.com/theplaylist/is-this-the-end-get-to-know-your-2013-apocalypse-movies-20130613">
        millenarian
       </a>
       taste for
       <a href="http://www.christianitytoday.com/ct/2014/july-web-only/everythings-turning-up-apocalypse.html">
        apocalypse
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Erik van Schaaik, the writer and director of
       <a href="http://hieronymus-movie.com/">
        <em>
         Hieronymus
        </em>
       </a>
       , a promising animated feature now in development, discovered Bosch while he was a teenager interested in horror movies.
      </p>
      <p>
       I call him the first heavy-metal artist, said Schaaik. His script embraces that interpretation. Its a story about a young artist whos real rock and roll, but living in an oppressed world where he cant get his visions out.
      </p>
      <figure style="max-width: 570.0px;">
       <picture style="position:relative; width:100%; height:0; overflow:hidden; padding-bottom:70.8771929825%;">
        <img alt="" class="lazyload" data-src="http://cdn.theatlantic.com/assets/media/img/posts/2014/10/image_1-1/eb261a1cd.jpg" style="position:absolute; width:100%; height:100%; top:0; left:0; border:0;"/>
       </picture>
       <figcaption class="caption">
        Sketches for the forthcoming
        <em>
         Hieronymous
        </em>
        (Courtesy of Erik van Schaaik)
       </figcaption>
      </figure>
      <p>
       There was a weird proverb in Boschs day: The Devils ass is Hells gateway. It was the inspiration for this little
       <a href="http://humidfruit.files.wordpress.com/2012/09/bosch-temptations-of-saint-anthony-left-panel-18.jpg">
        detail
       </a>
       in the left panel of
       <em>
        The Temptation of the Saint Anthony
       </em>
       . Its an example of how Bosch laced his iconoclasm and hellfire with humor, a hallmark of the grotesque form. He draws the worlds sins in the sand and then pauses to set off a whoopee cushion. His style
       <em>
        is
       </em>
       a dark sort of rock and roll, fronted not by a drug addict but a fiery Old Testament prophetand somehow perfect for a coloring book at the same time.
      </p>
     </section>

        <h3 class="hed">
         Why the Iran Deal Makes Obama's Critics So Angry
        </h3>
       </a>
       <ul class="metadata">
        <li class="byline">
         <a href="/author/peter-beinart/" title="Peter Beinart">
          Peter Beinart
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <div class="article-content ">
        <p class="dek">
         The nuclear agreement highlights the limits of American powersomething the presidents opponents wont accept.
        </p>
        <div class="excerpt">
         <p>
          Mankind faces a crossroads,
          <a href="http://www.skeptic.ca/Woody_Allen_Speech_to_Graduates.htm">
           declared
          </a>
          Woody Allen. One path leads to despair and utter hopelessness. The other, to total extinction. Let us pray we have the wisdom to choose correctly.
         </p>
         <p>
          The point is simple: In life, what matters most isnt how a decision compares to your ideal outcome. Its how it compares to
          <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/international/archive/2015/04/alternatives-obama-iran-nuclear-deal-israel/389751/">
           the alternative at hand
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          The same is true for the Iran deal,
          <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-33518524">
           announced Tuesday
          </a>
          <span>
           between Iran and six world powers

          . As Congress begins debating the agreement, its opponents have three real alternatives. The first is to kill the deal, and the
