         <a href="http://peasandcrayons.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/rainbow-spiralized-veggie-salad-broccoli-stalk-beet-carrot-cucumber-recipe-680-0089.jpg">
          <img alt="Spiralized Veggie Salad: Eat the Rainbow!" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6251" height="1002" src="http://peasandcrayons.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/rainbow-spiralized-veggie-salad-broccoli-stalk-beet-carrot-cucumber-recipe-680-0089.jpg" width="680"/>

        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Empty your crisper with this rainbow spiralized veggie salad!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <em>
          Or is it a slaw?
         </em>
         Ever since
         <a href="http://peasandcrayons.com/2015/02/madras-lentil-soup.html" target="_blank">
          the great soup/stew debate
         </a>
         [
         <em>
          hello dramatic!
         </em>
         ]Ive been doubting my title choices. Im officially the most indecisive person ever.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Heres the deal, if you serve it atop some yummy grilled chicken or salmon, or as a side to a rocking veggie burger, then its a slaw. If you pile it in a bowl and dive in with your favorite dressing and a big ole fork, then its a salad.
         <em>
          Deal? Deal!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Ive been making a ton of recipes with broccoli florets lately, and Im 100% opposed to tossing the stalks. I just cant do it! They make fabulous broccoli slaw, a great addition to fresh pressed juice, and now theyre getting the extra-fancy red carpet treatment in this spiralized veggie salad.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://peasandcrayons.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/healthy-spiralized-rainbow-veggie-slaw-and-salad-recipe-0058.jpg">

         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Wash and peel your carrots, beet, and cucumber.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Remove the tops from your broccoli if you haven't already used them, and set aside for snacking or stirring into your favorite dish.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Since thicker vegetables make the best spirals, remove the thin ends of the carrot as well as a sliver of the carrot top, to make a flat base on both ends. You'll only want the thickest part of the carrot, so save the tip for grating into muffins or for snacking.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Break out the spiral slicer and spiralize all four veggies, using the thinnest setting.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pile into a bowl and top with chopped nuts and seeds along with your favorite dressing.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            I used ranch for mine [the craving struck!] but I'll throw some links to other dressing recipes at the bottom of this post for ya!
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Enjoy!
           </li>
