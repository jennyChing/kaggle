         <img alt="Content marketing road ahead" class="size-full wp-image-5581" data-wp-pid="5581" height="439" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/content-marketing-road-ahead.jpg" width="640"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Content marketing can often seem like a long road ahead
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Some time ago I ran a half marathon in Cambridge, UK  a beautiful, historic city with some amazing old college buildings that date back centuries.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was great fun, but theres quite a story behind it  a story that has helped me in my content marketing
         <span id="more-5580">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I like to think Im fairly fit, and had trained for six months on my fancy new running machine before the race  and even ran the whole 13.1 mile distance during a training run.
        </p>
        <p>
         My time was pretty slow but it didnt worry me, and I convinced myself I was bound to do it faster on the big day.
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyway, the big day came around very fast, and after buying some new trainers and running kit to look the part  I found myself lining up on the start line with my brother-in-law, sister and a few friends.
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets stick together until at least half way if we can, said my brother-in-law. OK! I replied enthusiastically.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Bang! The starters gun sounded and we were off!
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         That was the last I saw of my brother-in-law, my sister, and all my friends for that matter.
        </p>
        <p>
         And as they disappeared into the distance, I settled into the familiar pace of my training runs.
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh crap! I admitted to myself, Im not as fast as I thought I was!
        </p>
        <p>
         By the 10K mark my feet were burning, and by 15K it felt as though my knees might give way.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5585" style="width: 610px">
         <img alt="Cambridge, UK" class="size-large wp-image-5585" data-wp-pid="5585" height="450" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/bridge-374048_640-600x450.jpg" width="600"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Cambridge, UK
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         As I ran along a cobbled street past one of the old Cambridge University college buildings, the pain shot up my legs like needles  but I carried on trudging forward.
        </p>
        <p>
         How long dyou reckon weve got left mate? I panted to another struggling guy next to me.
        </p>
        <p>
         He looked me in the eye and said convincingly, Not long buddy, just a couple of miles  you can do it!
        </p>
        <p>
         As he too ran off into the distance and I was overtaken by a sprightly man in his seventies, I began to think this may not have been such a great idea after all.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Especially with painful arthritis.
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         I was diagnosed in my thirties, and despite being otherwise fit and healthy, my arthritis is a constant unwanted companion in my life  and flares up badly when you least expect it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyway, it was doing its best to flare up on this particular day, and it felt like someone had stuck nails in the soles of my feet  and in my knees  and set fire to each joint for good measure
         <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yet as I jogged from the shadow of the old college building back into the hazy sunshine, I never once considered giving up.
        </p>
        <p>
         Because I remembered who I was doing this for  my baby daughter Maryann.
        </p>
        <p>
         My wife Anna and I have three lovely children, two boys Ewan and Callum, and our little girl.
        </p>
        <p>
         Both boys came into the world with no problems at all  but with Maryann it was different.
        </p>
        <p>
         There were serious complications at her birth that meant she stopped breathing for several minutes.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was the most vividly shocking day of my life.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5582" style="width: 490px">
         <img alt="Maryann getting better" class="size-full wp-image-5582" data-wp-pid="5582" height="640" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/IMG_1225.jpg" width="480"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Maryann in intensive care
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Yet happily, miraculously, skillfully  the amazing work of the hospital medical team brought her back to us, and for that we are very lucky and eternally grateful.
        </p>
        <p>
         Shes since made a full recovery, for which we feel blessed.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2614" style="width: 342px">
         <img alt="Maryann and daddy" class="size-full wp-image-2614" data-wp-pid="2614" height="442" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/LozandMaryann.jpeg" width="332"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Maryann fully recovered with Daddy
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         So thats why I was running, to raise money for the intensive care unit where Maryann was cared for in the days after her birth.
        </p>
        <p>
         And thats why the whole way round the half marathon, despite the pain, it never occurred to me to give up.
        </p>
        <p>
         It meant so much to cross that line that I kept going right to end, and finished with a flourish amongst the tail-enders  my arms held high like Rocky running up those steps in the film.
        </p>
        <p>
         I couldnt walk for a week but thats another matter
         <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           So whats all this got to do with content marketing you might be asking?
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Well it seems to me that persistence, hard work and digging deep when you cant even see the finishing line, are all great traits to carry over into your business life.
        </p>
        <p>
         These characteristics are especially analogous to the process of content marketing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Provided you have done your preparation and have clearly defined goals in mind, then you can get to work in the confidence that every step you take will carry you that little bit further towards achieving your goals.
        </p>
        <p>
         In this context, its important to appreciate that when you commit to using content marketing as part of your overall marketing mix  you arent going to see results overnight.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is a long-term game but the results can be exponential.
        </p>
        <p>
         Youll have to research your business goals, target audience and marketplace, set out what metrics you want to measure and attribute a value to them, define your ideal customer journey and plan content for their consumption  then promote your content aggressively before you can analyse your results and do more of whats working.
        </p>
        <p>
         And if all that sounds like hard work  it is.
        </p>
        <p>
         Name one thing in life worth achieving that isnt?
        </p>
        <p>
         However, if you stick at it and run the race with your content marketing efforts, the rewards for your business can be great  and will certainly help you overtake your competitors (if not leave them far behind).
        </p>
        <p>
         The sense of achievement I felt crossing the finishing line in that half marathon was fantastic, because despite everything I didnt give up, and successfully reached my goal at the end.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can do the same thing for your business with content marketing, but only if you commit to taking coherent, documented action on what will be a long (but profitable) road ahead.
        </p>
        <h2>
         In Conclusion
        </h2>
        <p>
         If youll allow me  there is just one more purpose to this blog post (which is after all a piece of content marketing in itself).
        </p>
        <p>
         In running the half marathon I raised over 1,000 for the Colchester Hospitals Charity who looked after Maryann so well (proof below).
        </p>
        <p>
         If youd like to support their work with whatever you can give, it will help many other babies and their families in future. Thank you.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.justgiving.com/cohoc" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Donate to Colchester Hospitals Charity" class=" size-full wp-image-5592 aligncenter" data-wp-pid="5592" height="53" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Donate_JG_White_Large.gif" width="217"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5594" style="width: 510px">
          <img alt="Fundraising proof" class="wp-image-5594" data-wp-pid="5594" height="555" src="http://www.contentchampion.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/fundraising.jpg" width="500"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           It was all so worth it :-)
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
