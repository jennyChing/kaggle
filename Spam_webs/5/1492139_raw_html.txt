       <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article">
        <h1 class="BlogPTitleDetail" itemprop="headline">
         Argentina v. The Netherlands: Battle of the beef recipes
        </h1>
        <div class="BlogPImage">
         <span st_image="https://alintaenergy.com.au/savewithgas/getattachment/6c1a50fa-202f-4f35-a904-93a536798a08/Argentina-v-The-Netherlands-Battle-of-the-beef-rec?maxsidesize=700">
          <img alt="Argentina v. The Netherlands: Battle of the beef recipes " itemprop="image" src="/savewithgas/getattachment/6c1a50fa-202f-4f35-a904-93a536798a08/Argentina-v-The-Netherlands-Battle-of-the-beef-rec?maxsidesize=700"/>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="BlogPBodyDetail textContent">
         <div class="summary">
          <div class="BlogPostSummaryText" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            When it comes to meaty snacks, there are few who can infuse succulent beef with rich and dreamy flavours like Argentina. One bite of an Argentinian empanada and you lose all sense of reality. But if Bitterballen are anything to go by, the Netherlands is a worthy contender. These little nuggets of fun are simply irresistible! Get stuck into these two local recipes.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <h2>
          Argentinian Beef Empanadas
         </h2>
         <p>
          Argentinian Beef Empanadas are essentially little meat pies with a sultry South American edge. There are two main styles of Argentinian empanadas  the Tucuman (fried) or the Salta (baked). The below recipe is for fried empanadas, because theres nothing like biting into that crispy crunchy crust. Serve them hot or cold as a snack, appetizer, lunch dinneryou wont need an excuse to tuck into these divine little pastry pockets.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Ingredients
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Pastry
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           450g plain flour
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tsp salt
          </li>
          <li>
           2 tsp baking powder
          </li>
          <li>
           60 g butter (diced)
          </li>
          <li>
           60 g lard (diced)
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Filling
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           400g minced beef
          </li>
          <li>
           2 large hard-boiled eggs, peeled and chopped
          </li>
          <li>
           2 tbsp olive oil
          </li>
          <li>
           1 large onion, finely chopped
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tsp paprika
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tsp dried chilli flakes
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tsp cumin
          </li>
          <li>
           30g raisins
          </li>
          <li>
           Pinch of salt
          </li>
          <li>
           Vegetable oil for frying
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tsp caster sugar
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <br/>
          Preparation time: 45 mins
          <br/>
          Cooking time: 10-30 mins
          <br/>
          Makes 12
         </p>
         <h3>
          To prepare
         </h3>
         <ol>
          <li>
           To make the pastry, mix the flour, baking powder and salt in a bowl. Rub the diced butter and lard into the flour using your fingertips. Stop when you have the texture of fine breadcrumbs. Add cold water gradually (about 3-6 tbsps), mixing with a table knife until a soft dough comes together. Roll this into a ball, wrap with cling wrap and put it in the fridge for an hour.
          </li>
          <li>
           For the filling, heat the olive oil in a frying pan over a gas stove. Add the onion and cook on a low heat until the onion is soft and transparent.
          </li>
          <li>
           Add the minced beef and fry over a high heat until the meat is browned all over, then stir in the paprika and chilli flakes. Remove from the heat.
          </li>
          <li>
           Add the raisins, cumin and chopped eggs to the meat mixture. Season with salt and pepper as desired.
          </li>
          <li>
           Remove the pastry from the fridge and roll it out on a lightly floured surface until it is roughly 3mm thick. Cut circles of about 10-12cm.
          </li>
          <li>
           Put 2 teaspoons of filling into one half of each circle. Wet the edge of the pastry with a little water, fold the pastry over and press the edges to seal. You can use a fork to crimp the edges.
          </li>
          <li>
           To cook, heat the vegetable oil in a large pan and fry the empanadas in small batches until beautifully golden on both sides. Drain on paper towels and serve with a dusting of caster sugar.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <h2>
          Bitterballen
         </h2>
         <p>
          Bitterballen (plural of bitterbal) have all the trappings of a great snack  fast and easy to make and fast and easy to eat. Typically, this deep-fried bite-sized ball contains a mixture of beef or veal with a surprising hint of nutmeg and even curry powder, and is eaten with good mustard. We recommend you make a few batches as theyre incredibly moreish!
         </p>
         <h3>
          Ingredients
         </h3>
         <ul>
          <li>
           50 g butter
          </li>
          <li>
           125 g plain flour
          </li>
          <li>
           750 ml beef stock
          </li>
          <li>
           3 tbsps onion, finely diced
          </li>
          <li>
           1 tbsp fresh parsley, finely diced
          </li>
          <li>
           1 cups minced beef, cooked
          </li>
          <li>
           Salt and pepper to taste
          </li>
          <li>
           Nutmeg
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           For the breading
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           60 g flour
          </li>
          <li>
           2 eggs, beaten
          </li>
          <li>
           120 g breadcrumbs
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          To prepare
         </h3>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Slowly melt the butter in a pan and add the flour little by little. Stir into a thick paste, or roux.
          </li>
          <li>
           Add the stock into the roux, until the roux absorbs the liquid. Simmer for 2-3 minutes over a low heat while you stir in the onion, parsley and minced beef. Add salt, pepper and nutmeg to taste.
          </li>
          <li>
           Pour your meat gravy into a container and put into the fridge for a few hours, or until solidified.
          </li>
          <li>
           To prepare the balls, remove your container from the fridge. Take a heaped tablespoon of the gravy and quickly roll it into a small ball. Roll lightly through the flour, then the egg and then the breadcrumbs, making sure the ball is completely covered. Repeat with all the mixture and refrigerate until your frying pan is hot enough to cook (test with a small piece of bread  if it turns golden brown within 10 seconds, youre ready!)
          </li>
          <li>
           Fry 5-6 balls at a time until golden brown all over. Serve with good, spicy mustard.
          </li>
         </ol>
