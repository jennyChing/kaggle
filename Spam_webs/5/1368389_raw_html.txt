       <h1 class="title">
        <a href="http://zipso.net/how-to-get-cheap-stock-photos-using-google-image-search/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to How to get Cheap Stock Photos using Google Image Search">
         How to get Cheap Stock Photos using Google Image Search
        </a>
       </h1>
       <div class="top-meta clearfix">
        <span>
         March 11, 2014 at 5:24 am
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry">
        <!--
						&lt;div style="float:right; display:block;width:180px; height:150px; border:1px solid #000;"&gt;
						&lt;/div&gt;
-->
        <p>
         <img alt="night-bench" class="size-post-large wp-image-101 alignnone" height="240" src="http://static1.zipso.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/night-bench-650x240.jpg" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <h3 class="nomargin">
         Searching for the perfect pic for your new website or blog?
        </h3>
        <h3 class="nomargin">
         Maybe youve got something in mind but the cost of photos on stock sites is adding up?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Weve got some clever tips on how to save money on high-quality images from stock photo sites.
        </p>
        <div style="float:right;margin:8px 0px 8px 8px;">
         <div style="margin-right:9px">
          <!-- zipso post para2 -->
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-8959601970688211" data-ad-slot="1606386652" style="display:inline-block;width:240px;height:160px">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         But first
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Microstock sites
         </strong>
          Websites that hold large collections of photos for commercial use, typically for posting online. Photo licenses are usually royalty-free which simply means that you dont need to pay a royalty to the owner every time you use them. However, the photos themselves cant be re-sold.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Traditional stock photo sites
         </strong>
          These are photo licensing agencies such as
         <a href="http://www.corbisimages.com/" target="_blank">
          Corbis
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://gettyimages.com" target="_blank">
          Getty Images
         </a>
         that typically offer higher-tier photos and licensing for higher prices.
        </p>
        <p>
         For the purposes of this article, were just going to look at Microstock sites, since theyre more affordable for general use.
        </p>
        <p>
         However, you could use the same approach for any photo sites.
        </p>
        <h3>
         How to save money on individual photos
        </h3>
        <p>
         Were just going to compare prices for individual photos, instead of subscription packages.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you use a lot of stock photos, the subscriptions are often a better deal, however if you are just an occasional user its probably better to pay as you go.
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets get started.
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Find your ideal picture on one of the sites
        </h3>
        <p>
         We suggest looking at some of the sites with a larger selection of images, such as
         <a href="http://fotolia.com" target="_blank">
          Fotolia.com
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://shutterstock.com" target="_blank">
          ShutterStock.com
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://dreamstime.com" target="_blank">
          dreamstime.com
         </a>
         etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         For this technique to work, youll ideally want to navigate to the info/purchase page for a specific image (with larger thumbnail and download prices) before going to the next step.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. Compare prices across different stock sites with Google Image search
        </h3>
        <p>
         So youve found your ideal photo on one of the sites, but before you click that Buy button  are you getting the best deal?
        </p>
        <p>
         What you might not know is that exactly the same photo is often available on a number of sites, and the prices can be much different.
        </p>
        <p>
         To check where your photo is available, youll need to use Google Image search with the photos URL.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some sites make it difficult to find the direct URL for a picture, however there are a number of tools that can help here.
        </p>
        <p>
         Well look at two Google Chrome extensions as an example, but you can find similar tools for other browsers. To use these, youll need to be using the Chrome browser and signed in to a Google account (at least initially to install them):
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Google Image Search extension
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="search-by-image" class="size-full wp-image-458 alignnone" height="200" src="http://static1.zipso.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/search-by-image.png" style="margin-top: 15px; margin-bottom: 15px;" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/search-by-image-by-google/dajedkncpodkggklbegccjpmnglmnflm?utm_source=chrome-ntp-icon" target="_blank">
           Google Image Search
          </a>
           This useful extension adds a way to search by right-clicking on an image and selecting Search Google with this image. This will then bring you to the Google Image search results (see below).
          <br class=" sb-br"/>
          Note however that this wont work on all sites, especially where you can pan and zoom over the image. In this case, we need to first find the image URL (see next extension below).
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Image Downloader extension
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="image-downloader" class="size-full wp-image-459 alignnone" height="196" src="http://static1.zipso.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/image-downloader.png" style="margin-top: 15px; margin-bottom: 15px;" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/image-downloader/cnpniohnfphhjihaiiggeabnkjhpaldj?utm_source=chrome-ntp-icon" target="_blank">
           Image Downloader
          </a>
           Since were just interested in the photo previews on the stock sites, we dont actually want to use the download functionality of this extension, however, it provides a way to more easily see the URL for a specific image.
          <br class=" sb-br"/>
          <br/>
          After installing, navigate to the info page for a specific photo, click on the downloader icon in the top-right beside the browser address bar.
          <br class=" sb-br"/>
          <br/>
          <img alt="image-downloader-button2" class="size-full wp-image-469 alignnone" height="126" src="http://static1.zipso.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/image-downloader-button2.png" style="margin-bottom: 25px;" width="271"/>
          <br/>
          The extension will now show a list of all images on the page. Scroll down to your photos thumbnail, click on the URL box, then right-click, Select All and copy the URL.
          <br class=" sb-br"/>
          <br/>
          <img alt="image-downloader-selection" class="size-full wp-image-470 alignnone" height="246" src="http://static1.zipso.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/image-downloader-selection.png" style="margin-bottom: 25px;" width="368"/>
          <br/>
          A slight quirk of Image Downloader is that it doesnt show the mouse cursor when you click on the URL box, however youre still able to select the URL text after clicking.
          <br class=" sb-br"/>
          <br/>
          You can now go to Google Image search and use this URL. Go to Google.com and click on Images. A searchbox will appear, with a small camera icon to the right.
          <br class=" sb-br"/>
          <img alt="image-search1" class="size-full wp-image-463 alignnone noborder" height="202" src="http://static1.zipso.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/image-search1.png" style="margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 15px;" width="650"/>
         </li>
         <li>
          Click on the camera icon to search by image. This will open a different box where you can paste in the image URL. The image URL will usually end in .png, .jpg or .gif.
          <br class=" sb-br"/>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="image-search2a" class="size-full wp-image-468 alignnone noborder" height="321" src="http://static1.zipso.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/image-search2a.png" style="margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 15px;" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Click on Search by image to see the results.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Google Image search results
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now that youve searched for your image using either of the above two methods, you can try to see if any of the results point to other stock photo sites.
        </p>
        <p>
         Youll want to scroll down to 
         <strong>
          Pages that include matching images
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         However, the results here may not all be stock photo sites  but there is a trick to return more relevant sites.
        </p>
        <p>
         Simply go to the searchbox at the top and delete whatever text Google has auto-inserted as the best guess. Make sure to keep the photo icon widget there. Now replace the text with royalty free and search again.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="royalty-free" class="size-full wp-image-481 alignnone" height="47" src="http://static1.zipso.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/royalty-free.png" style="margin-top: 15px; margin-bottom: 15px;" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You should now see more of the stock photo sites in the results.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Savings
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         We did a test using
         <a href="http://us.fotolia.com/id/56904867" target="_blank">
          this photo
         </a>
         on Fotolia and found a saving of $4 on another stock site with exactly the same photo when purchasing the47523168 resolution.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whether you will see similar savings is hard to tell since the results will vary considerably depending on the photo, however you have a much better chance of finding the best price for a given image.
        </p>
        <p>
         See the next section for another way to save using Vector format images.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Note:
         </strong>
         For some sites, e.g. Dreamstime, you will need to be logged in first before you can see prices.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Dont choose a high-res option if there is a vector format available
        </h3>
        <p>
         As the average devices display resolution keeps increasing, some of the small and even medium size options available for download seem a bit lacking. The problem is that the price of hi-res downloads doesnt appear to show any sign of decreasing.
        </p>
        <p>
         However, if you need hi-res, but can use an illustration instead of a photo, you can save a lot by choosing a vector download format. A vector file is in a format such as .AI, .EPS or .SVG which is resolution independent.
        </p>
        <p>
         You will most likely want to convert to JPG or PNG formats for use on the web  the great thing with vector formats is that you can convert the image to these formats at any resolution of your choice, without making the image blurry.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_433" style="width: 660px">
         <img alt="fotolia-pricing" class="size-full wp-image-433 noborder " height="334" src="http://static1.zipso.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/fotolia-pricing.png" style="margin-top: 15px;" width="650"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Example of format pricing on Fotolia. Vector is much better value if you need Large res. or higher.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Photoshop or
         <a href="http://www.gimp.org/">
          GIMP
         </a>
         will open .eps files and let you save as a raster .jpg or .png.
        </p>
        <p>
         You might not even need to convert it though  Fotolia.com also includes a hi-res JPG in the zip file when you download in vector format. And funnily enough, this option is significantly cheaper compared to selecting a hi-res download, even though youre getting the same thing!
        </p>
        <p>
         Do you have any tips for finding cheap stock photos?
        </p>
        <p>
         Maybe you want to find free images instead?
        </p>
        <p>
         Stay tuned for our upcoming article on how to find public domain and Creative Commons image sources.

          MWQNDJDUW6AT

        </p>
