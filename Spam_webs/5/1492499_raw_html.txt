        <h1 class="post-title" data-content-field="title">
         #FitnessFriday - Intro
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" data-updated-on="1429498071365" id="item-55346834e4b003aefe430b33">

             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p>
               Cardio is depressing to me because I run for thirty minutes or so and feel like I am going to hack up a lung and keel over and die and then I realized I barely burned off my latte. I have dreams of being the girl that is "going for a run to clear her head" but as of now, I am the girl who is "binging on netflix to clear her head."
              </p>
              <p>
               Anyway, I really wanted to be a more fit person, and definitely used this blog as motivation to start working out for #fitnessfriday posts. Ifound some workouts on
               <a href="http://www.instagram.com/TheCrownFox" target="_blank">
                instagram
               </a>
               and
               <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/TheCrownFox" target="_blank">
                pinterest
               </a>
               and realized they could give me better results, quicker. The "secret" is strength training. I feel silly because many people have told me that I should lift weights and I said the most stereotypical response:
               <em>
                I dont want to get bulky.
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               So, newsflash, apparently
               <strong>
                you won't get bulky
               </strong>
               . As a female its really difficult for us to get bulky since we dont have enough testosterone. I have definitely heard professionals and trainers tell me to do lighter weights/longer reps for a long lean look. But fear not ladies. Pick up something heavy. Hell, pick up something REALLY heavy and get strong and powerful and look good and feel like a badass.
              </p>
              <p>
               The best part about getting strong muscles is cardio becomes less necessary. Like, okay, you should do cardio because its good for your heart  but you dont have to depend on cardio to actually lose weight. Strong muscles burn calories and a lot of them. A muscular derriere will burn a lot of calories. I know youve heard about those pro athletes eating like 6k calories a day because their bodies need so much fuel to work. I dont need to be as muscular or calorie-needing as them. But if I can be muscular enough to not feel terrible about eating my chipotle bowl (with brown rice. And no sour cream.)then thats great.
              </p>
              <p>
               Consider this an intro post, and look for more every other Friday about our favorite workouts, workout gear, playlists, etc. Don't forget to check out our
               <a href="http://www.TheCrownFox.Etsy.com/" target="_blank">
                Etsy
               </a>
               shop and follow us on social media!
              </p>
