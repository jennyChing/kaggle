          <h3 class="Standard">
           It is an industry that dominates Asian cinema, it is the Eastern equivalent to Hollywood, creates fashion trends, gives new meanings to songs and delivers some top grossing blockbusters.
          </h3>
          <p class="Standard">
           It is the cinematic juggernaut that goes by the name of Bollywood. However while Bollywood cinema is big with Asian audiences and massive in countries like India, it does not have the crossover appeal Hollywood or British films do. So why is Bollywood a foreign concept in these different markets?
          </p>
          <p class="Standard">
           Firstly let us have a look at what Bollywood cinema is about. I was a big follower of Bollywood films when I was growing up but sadly as time moved on I did become more westernised and neglected these films. Although that does not mean I dont appreciate it as a major player in the industry. The films consist of fantasised drama, everything is over the top and exaggerated for entertainment purposes, it includes stuff that wouldnt happen in real life.
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p class="Standard">
            This is similar to all movies to an extent, but if you take away the realism factor, it is an enjoyable viewing experience and still contains plots that are relatable to audiences, just with added spice I suppose.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p class="Standard">
           The films usually feature an array of colourful, vibrant, Indian costumes especially on the female cast members that add that some glamour to proceedings, the extravagant settings used during the song and dance numbers are another reason to watch. Bollywood films could be labelled as musicals as every film contains bursts of song and dance. Usually during the happy and sad moments of the film, the positive bits are accompanied by energetic dance routines and the more sombre scenes have songs with dramatic montages. Bollywood cinema excels at Romantic Comedies, they provide great, chemistry-filled love stories and some golden moments. However, the more dramatic and action-packed additions to the genre appear somewhat lacklustre in comparison.
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p class="Standard">
            They come across as fluffy and not gritty enough, and the fight scenes in the action films are too far-fetched to look convincing.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p class="Standard">
           Some world-class actors have come from the Bollywood with Sharukh Khan, Sunny Deol, Rani Mukerjee and Priyanka Chopra being just a few names that showcase the talent that Bollywood has to offer. These actors have the looks and talent to make it big in other markets but they havent had that chance to make that leap, as they are not enough roles for Asians in mainstream cinema. There needs to be because most of these actors are a triple threat in terms of acting, singing and dancing.
           <a href="http://cinemajam.com/mag/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/bollywood-aishwarya-rai-lovely-sari.jpg">
            <img alt="bollywood-aishwarya-rai-lovely-sari" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-1385" height="300" src="http://cinemajam.com/mag/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/bollywood-aishwarya-rai-lovely-sari-223x300.jpg" width="223"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p class="Standard">
           There are many reasons as to why Bollywood hasnt made that jump to mainstream, the language barrier being one, like most Asian cinema productions the films are in a different language but there is always subtitles. Another reason would be that Bollywood is too fantasised. For me personally, I delve into Bollywood from time to time as I like to have a couple of hours away from reality and enjoy the spectacle of the all-singing and dancing show Bollywood produces.
          </p>
          <p class="Standard">
           However Bollywood could be big and is big in its own right across Asia. Yet this is down to how you have been brought up. If you were raised in an English-speaking country and have watched Hollywood films from a young age, that does not change when you grow up.
          </p>
          <p class="Standard">
           I am a British Asian and I mainly stick to Hollywood blockbusters and British classics, as I have been brought up around that. But from time to time I make an effort to remember my roots and get excited over the latest Bollywood extravaganza.
          </p>
          <div style="clear: both;">
          </div>
          <div class="authorinfo" style="overflow: hidden; background: #F9F9F9; border: 1px solid #E01B5D; padding: 10px 15px; margin: 10px 0;">
           <div style="float:left; margin: 0 15px 5px 0;">
            <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-96 wp-user-avatar wp-user-avatar-96 photo avatar-default" height="96" src="http://0.gravatar.com/avatar/887b372ca1445e8521a7a1f4a62b4017?s=96&amp;d=&amp;r=G" width="96"/>
           </div>
           <h4 style="margin: 0 0 12px; background: none; display: inline-block;">
            <a href="http://cinemajam.com/mag/author/Juggy-Sohal" rel="author" style="font-size: 22px;color: #E01B5D">
             Juggy Sohal
            </a>
           </h4>
           <p style="margin: 0 0 8px; clear:none;">
            Juggy Sohal is a writer from Bedford, who having achieved a BA in English and FIlm/Television at Brunel University in London, has been looking to make a name for himself in the writing world. Being a film and television fanatic, he has written many scripts in his spare time ranging from short films to sitcoms and one day hopes for his work to be played out on the big screen.
           </p>
