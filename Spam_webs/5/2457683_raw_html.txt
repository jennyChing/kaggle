     <span class="item">
      <h1 class="fn">
       Green Juice Recipe
      </h1>
     </span>
     <div class="entrydate col-sm-12 col-xs-12 text-center">
      <div class="dateinfo">
       <a href="http://www.101cookbooks.com/archives/green-juice-recipe.html">
        <span class="published">
         September 17, 2014
         <span class="value-title" title="2014-09-17">
         </span>
        </span>
       </a>
       |
       <a href="http://www.101cookbooks.com/archives/green-juice-recipe.html#comments">
        35 Comments
       </a>
      </div>
     </div>
     <br class="clear"/>
     <div class="entrybody">
      <p>
       Everyone should have a green juice recipe in their repertoire, and this one is a ringer. Its heart and soul is straight green, not at all sweet, with a good amount of lemon-lime tang, and invigorating ginger lift. Like many green juices, it makes you sing from the inside, and is reason enough to invest in a juicer, or dust one off. When I traveled to Paris last year, I spent a beautiful first day and night there, then came down with a (particularly miserable) cold - throat on fire, raspy cough. Being sick away from home is hard, begin sick in a city like Paris, when you're there for just a short time, is spiritually deflating. So, in preparation for
       <a href="http://www.101cookbooks.com/archives/momo-dumplings-recipe.html">
        my most recent trip
       </a>
       , I became a vigilant hand-washing, snotty three-year avoiding, soup eating individual with a juicer on the counter. I religiously drank a glass of this green juice every day, punctuated with a gingery version of this
       <a href="http://www.101cookbooks.com/archives/turmeric-tea-recipe.html">
        turmeric tea
       </a>
       a few times along the way. It's a variation on one of the (many) green juices highlighted in Pressed Juicery's
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1607746271/heidiswanson-20">
        Juice: Recipes for Juicing, Cleansing, and Living Well
       </a>
       , a book I purchased recently, before realizing we share the same editor!
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Green Juice Recipe" border="0" src="http://www.101cookbooks.com/mt-static/images/food/green_juice_recipe_2.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Juicing can be time intensive. I think that is why so many people are inclined to purchase fresh juices instead of making them at home. Quite honestly, I do both. There are stretches when I don't get around to making fresh juice, and other times I make it regularly. I like how fresh juice makes me feel.
      </p>
      <p>
       A few things to consider to make green juice easier.
      </p>
      <p>
       - Wash, trim, and prepare your produce when you come home from the market. Then refrigerate. This way, it's straight into the juicer when you're ready.
      </p>
      <p>
       - Make more then you need in one go. Certainly, fresh, immediate juice is the best. That said, I make a three day supply, and keep it refrigerated (you can even freeze it). And it's good. With this juice in particular, you can use it as a soup broth, I even used it to make dumpling wrappers in place of water.
      </p>
      <p>
       - Clean up as soon as you're finished.
      </p>
      <p>

     <div id="recipe">
      <h1>
       Green Juice
      </h1>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        2 to 3 kale leaves, to taste
        <br/>
        large handful of spinach
        <br/>
        1 head romaine
        <br/>
        2 celery stalks
        <br/>
        1 small bunch of parsley, trimmed
        <br/>
        1/2 lemon, peeled, deseeded
        <br/>
        1/2 lime, peeled, deseeded
        <br/>
        1 1-inch cube of peeled ginger
        <br/>
        2 small cucumbers, peeled
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Combine the above in a juicer - serve, refrigerate, or freeze immediately. Refrigerated, I've found the green juice to be pretty great on day two, as well as the morning of day three.
      </p>

       <p>
        Prep time:
