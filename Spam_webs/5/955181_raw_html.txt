&lt;p&gt;When in the mood to relive your golden years, there's a pretty good chance you'll reach for an equally golden beverage: &lt;strong&gt;a 40oz&lt;/strong&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Maybe you used to drink 'em in your friend Brad's basement, or maybe you used to drink 'em at your friend Brad's friend Todd's lake house, either way, &lt;strike&gt;what was your deal with Brad?!?&lt;/strike&gt; we know you've slugged one of these. Most likely with Brad. But which was your 40 of choice? And were you really picking the best-tasting elixir? We wondered (&lt;a href="http://www.thrillist.com/drink/nation/the-best-two-buck-chuck-at-trader-joe-s-cheap-wine-thrillist-nation" target="_blank"&gt;like how we wondered about all that seriously cheap wine&lt;/a&gt;), and decided to bring in &lt;strong&gt;Anne Becerra, a certified cicerone&lt;/strong&gt; &lt;strong&gt;and bartender at the NYC-based &lt;a href="http://www.gingerman-ny.com/" target="_blank"&gt;Ginger Man&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;We presented her with four classic, kind of warm 40s: &lt;strong&gt;Olde English&lt;/strong&gt;, &lt;strong&gt;Colt 45&lt;/strong&gt;, &lt;strong&gt;St. Ides&lt;/strong&gt;, and &lt;strong&gt;Crazy Stallion&lt;/strong&gt;. Then we had her blindly taste 'em from cups and straight outta the bottle (carbonation is greater in bottles, which can mask flavor, while pouring into a cup brings out flavors and aromas), throw down some serious tasting notes, and rank 'em from worst to best. This is her story.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;strong&gt;For chugging out of the bottle&lt;/strong&gt; (the way God intended), &lt;strong&gt;grab that OE&lt;/strong&gt; and get down. St. Ides and Colt 45 won't be too tough to swill either, though. But &lt;strong&gt;STAY AWAY FROM THAT CRAZY STALLION&lt;/strong&gt;.&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;strong&gt;Closing remarks:&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
"When Im teaching about beer, one of my main points is drink out of a glass -- &lt;strong&gt;you wanna see the head retention, color, the clarity -- its all a part of it when drinking nice beer&lt;/strong&gt;. When Im talking about this, &lt;strong&gt;its really important to do the opposite&lt;/strong&gt; -- what you don't know cant hurt you, you dont wanna be smelling creamed corn from the cafeteria. But if they're ice cold in a bag and youre hanging out with friends outside listening to music, drink out of the bottle and share it and make sure everyone taps it for good luck. &lt;strong&gt;In this, you really get to taste all the defects&lt;/strong&gt;, and they clearly pair better with [LEGALLY PURCHASED DRUGS!]."&lt;br /&gt;
