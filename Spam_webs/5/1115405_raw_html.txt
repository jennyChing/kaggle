      <h1>
       Just what time of the year is better to graduate?
      </h1>
      <div class="blogAuthorImg floatLeft" style="background-image:url(/assets/img/uploaded/avatars/2015-04/226508644562626.jpg);">
      </div>
      <p class="lighter">
       Posted on 7 September 2014 by
       <a href="/blog/author/josh-booth-10471/">
        Josh Booth
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="clear">
      </div>
      <p class="larger">
       <b>
        <a href="/blog/">

        </a>
       </b>
      </p>
      <br/>
      <div class="blogArticleInnerShareInteractive">

       </div>
       <div style="left:120px;">
        <a class="twitter-share-button" data-count="none" data-size="small" data-text="Just what time of the year is better to graduate?" data-url="http://bahgum.com/blog/48/" data-via="BahGumOffical">
        </a>
       </div>
       <div style="left:200px;">

      <p class="larger">
       We take a look at the possible advantages to graduating later in the year!
      </p>
      <img alt="Just what time of the year is better to graduate?" class="blogImg" src="/assets/img/uploaded/blog/2015-05/6911684_orig_80110.jpg"/>
      <br/>
      <p>
       Most of your friends are graduates now. And theyre not going to let you forget it any time soon. Their universitys graduation ceremonies were in July, so theyre so much better than you, right? Wrong!
       <br/>
       <br/>
       For starters, you get to use the title graduand for more than a couple of weeks. If youve not been calling yourself a graduand, start now - it means one who has left university but has not yet graduated. Go around telling people youre a graduate and theyll be like yeah, yeah, sos everybody else. But a graduand? How mysterious and exotic and Latin and intellectual. Milk it for all its worth.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Your extended graduand status also allows for far more preparation time; how many of your graduate friends had their special day ruined by having to run around, getting things organised at the last moment? Therell be no such problems for you, with your 3 months of preparation.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Unless, of course, youre too busy doing something else. Like working. At your job. Which you didnt even need to graduate to get. If you actually have a job, you can lord it over your graduate friends all summer, and then probably over those youre graduating with too.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       But better yet, if you do have a job, youll get the day off to graduate! What are those July graduates missing on their big day? Just another lovely summer day. Whereas you get to replace a day at work with a day of celebration, without missing out on that July sunshine.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Which, incidentally, is clearly the worst weather to graduate in. Layer upon layer of gowns over your suit or best dress is hardly suitable summer attire. But graduating later in the year, youll actually be grateful for those layers, and you wont look all sweaty and flustered in your photos.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Those photos, by the way, make great Christmas presents. July graduates dont really get that luxury, but for November graduates, Grandmas present for this year is sorted.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       But perhaps the very best (and certainly the most cheesy) reason for late graduations is that you get to see your friends again, having had the whole summer to miss them. For those graduating in July, its probably only been a matter of days since you last saw your university friends - you might even be fed up of them. But by the end of the summer, youll be dying to see them again, especially as youve got a summer load of fun to catch up on. By the end of the year, youll all have an idea of what your immediate futures are going to look like, making it much easier for you to make plans and stay in touch.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       So heres to you, you long-term graduands, the envy of graduates everywhere! Enjoy your graduations, they will certainly be worth the wait. If you are worried about your graduation you can take a look at our
       <a href="https://bahgum.com/blog/10-tips-to-graduating-13/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        top 10 tips on how to graduate!
       </a>
      </p>
