           <h2>
            Music Licensing  an interview with the minds behind Girth Music
           </h2>
          </div>
         </div>
         <a class="post_comment_number" href="http://www.weraddicted.com/music-licensing-interview-minds-behind-girth-music/">
          0 Comment
         </a>
         <div class="post_detail">
          <img class="middle" src="http://www.weraddicted.com/wp-content/themes/hades/images/clock.png"/>
          30 Sep 2013
          <img class="middle" src="http://www.weraddicted.com/wp-content/themes/hades/images/author.png"/>
          Written by Nadia Elkharadly
         </div>
         <br class="clear"/>
         <div class="post_img">
          <a href="http://www.weraddicted.com/music-licensing-interview-minds-behind-girth-music/" title="Music Licensing  an interview with the minds behind Girth Music">
           <img alt="" class="" src="http://www.weraddicted.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/girthmusic.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="post_inner_wrapper">
          <p>
           Its nighttime, and youre watching television, curled up on your couch. A commercial with a kicky tune in the background crosses your screen. Or maybe a particularly poignant part of a show episode is punctuated by a tear jerking song playing. Whether you know it or not, music plays a huge part in how video, be it commercials, television series, or films, impact a viewing audience. Its up to hard working and meticulous music supervisors to perfectly pair music and video to produce a desired effect. And thats exactly what the talented crew at
           <b>
            <a href="http://www.girthmusic.com/">
             Girth Music
            </a>
           </b>
           does.
          </p>
          <p>
           Formed in Toronto as part of the
           <b>
            Eggplant Collective
           </b>
           of media gurus, Girth Musics purpose is to provide music supervision and clearance services. In essence, they help their wide variety of clients (including advertising agencies like Leo Burnett, JWT, Blammo Worldwide, MacLaren McCann, Cossette and Taxi) find particular music and songs to suit campaigns for their various clients. Girth Music has licensed music for big names like Ikea, Kelloggs and Canadian Tire, and in doing so have not only created aurally and visually pleasing content, but have helped to expose the Canadian population to music they may never have heard otherwise. Along with commercials, Girth also places music in television shows like
           <i>
            The LA Complex
           </i>
           (MuchMusic, CTV, CW) and
           <i>
            The United States of Tara
           </i>
           (Showtime), and films such as
           <i>
            A Year in Mooring
           </i>
           and
           <i>
            Six Month Rule
           </i>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Since music licensing kind of sounds like my dream job, I chatted with Girth Music guys Rich Brisson and Scotty Taylor about their awesome jobs and what music licensing is all about. Take a listen below, and be sure to visit Girths
           <a href="http://www.girthmusic.com/">
            website
           </a>
           for more information.
          </p>
