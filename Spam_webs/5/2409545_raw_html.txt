   (function(window, document){
		// Object that provides helper functions for
		// use with Smart ad server
		window.OzySAS = {

			//get populated with {name,value} objects
			//which get passed through to SAS
			targets: [],

			getTarget: function() {
				this.reset();

				var string = "";
				for(var i = 0; i &lt; this.targets.length; i++) {
					var target = this.targets[i];
					string += this.formatTargetParameter(target.name, target.value);
				}
				return string;
			},

			reset: function(){
				this.targets = [];
				this.initialize();
			},

			formatTargetParameter: function(name, value){
				return name + "=" + value + ";";
			},

			queryStringToArray: function(string) {
				var pairs = string.slice(1).split('&amp;');
				var results = [];
				if(string){
					for(var i = 0; i &lt; pairs.length; i++) {
						var pair = pairs[i].split('=');
						var name = pair[0];
						var value = pair[1];
						results.push({name: name, value: decodeURIComponent(value || '')});
					}
				}
				return results;
			},

			addQueryStringToTarget: function(){
				this.targets.push.apply(this.targets, this.queryStringToArray(location.search));
			},

			addSasCookiesToTarget: function(){
				this.targets.push.apply(this.targets, this.sasCookiesToArray(document.cookie));
			},

			sasCookiesToArray: function(string) {
				var pairs = string.split('; ');
				var results = [];
				if(string){
					for(var i = 0; i &lt; pairs.length; i++) {
						var pair = pairs[i].split(/=(.+)?/);
						var name = pair[0];
						if(this.isSasCookie(name)){
							var value = unescape(pair[1]).split('=')[1];
							results.push({name: name, value: value });
						}
					}
				}
				return results;

				// return [{name: 'sasOzyHasIgnoredPopup', value: 'true'}];
			},

			isSasCookie: function(string){
				return string.indexOf("sasOzy") == 0;
			},

			initialize: function(){
				// Push existing user subscriptions as a target
				OzySAS.targets.push({ name: 'subscribed', value: localStorage.getItem('subscribed') });
				OzySAS.targets.push({ name: 'ignored', value: null }); //removed ignored logic
				// Push query string parameters to target
				OzySAS.addQueryStringToTarget();
				// Push sas cookies to target
				OzySAS.addSasCookiesToTarget();
			}
		};

	})(window, document);

           <div class="storytext">
            <p>
             With a name like Tamara Gonzalez Perea youre not expecting a Pole. As in: from Poland. And youre really not expecting her to be a fashion barometer whose proclamations are watched and listened to by people all over the world who are paying attention to fashion. But they are. Oh, yes, they are.
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: 15.6000003814697px;">
              
             </span>
             <span>
              Celebrities are beating down her door because of how she uses accessories and color, says
             </span>
             <a href="http://petryczko.pl/" target="_blank">
              Monika Petryczko
             </a>
             , a former arts/culture critic at Polskie Radio Szczecin for the past decade. And how her choices challenge how such things should be used: Its both unique to not only Poland but present fashion in general. So its nice to see her recognized internationally. Tamara Gonzalez Perea, who makes her home in Szczecin (pronounced Stet-chin), Polands seventh largest city, not only just won Fashion Magazines award for best fashion blog of 2012 for her
             <a href="http://www.macademiangirl.com/" target="_blank">
              Macademian Girl
             </a>
             blog, but also took a first place win from Press Magazine for fashion blogs. In fast fashion, those wins were followed by spreads in Glamour , Elle and Cosmo this summer. And its not just the fashion blogging that makes her interesting. Nor is it simply her wild pastiche of found/repurposed materials.
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: 15.6000003814697px;">
              A sentiment seconded by the willowy 24-year old Perea, an interior design student at Szczecins Art Academy and a product of a Polish mother and a Panamanian father (with a Native American grandmother), even if Pereas processing of different inputs and influences had not always been so welcome in Poland, a country thats 98 percent racially homogenous. Beaten nearly to death by kids in her class when she was younger for being a nigger, Perea had a lot of alone time. On her own inside, she grew her interests in fashion, interior design and writing.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             My biggest success, outside of what the awards recognize though, said Perea, is overthrowing all of the fashion stereotypes that Polish people love so much. America too. For example a trend you all are late to would be colors in metallic versions, like in the SS 2013 Burberry Prorsum collection. Beautiful.
            </p>
            <blockquote class="inlineLeft">
             <blockquote class="quote">
              <p>
               Fashion for me is like art  Thats what I want in my clothing. And thats what I want in my life.
              </p>
              <p class="quotesource">
                Tamara Gonzalez Perea
              </p>
             </blockquote>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: 15.6000003814697px;">
              So her love of fashion, design and writing is now channeled into a business model that lets you buy what she wears and blogs about for prices that are ridiculously affordable even by Polish standards (Poland, though part of the EU, still uses the
             </span>
             <em>
              zloty
             </em>
             <span style="line-height: 15.6000003814697px;">
              as their primary currency). But Perea finds the cash much less interesting than the fact that her readership in Poland is spreading to fashion hot spots in France, Italy and beyond  her blog pulls in about 100,250 unique visitors a month.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             TV has come calling, too, with features on her during coverage of Polands Fashion Week in Lodz. All this attention steadies a starward trajectory for Perea that includes brand work with H&amp;M, Wrangler and Adidas among others. All before shes even left school. Fashion for me is like art, Perea say. Thats what I want in my clothing. And thats what I want in my life.
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: 15.6000003814697px;">
              And these days? She just won first prize in a
             </span>
             <em>
              Glamour
             </em>
             <span style="line-height: 15.6000003814697px;">
              magazine contest, besting a large field of wanna-bes and giving her her own column. As a result, shes relocating to Warsaw. Where were quite sure shell remain as fashionably electric as ever.
             </span>
            </p>
