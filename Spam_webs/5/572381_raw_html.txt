         <h2 class="title">
          Step 1: Join the Navy
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="notify">
        </div>
        <div class="body" data-button-class="all">
         <p>
          Before you can become a Navy SEAL, you must first join the U.S. Navy.  To do this, you must visit a Navy recruiter.  You must also:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Take and pass the Armed Service Vocational Aptitude Battery (ASVAB) test
          </li>
          <li>
           Undergo a physical examination
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Once you have enlisted, you will undergo eight weeks of recruit training, including classroom instruction and physical training.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="section movable text" data-dj-class="TextSection" data-js-class="TextSection" data-layout-position="5" data-type="text" id="b234ddb9ba754f42a1a076719fe35359">
        <div class="head">
         <div class="grip">
         </div>
         <h2 class="title">
          Step 2: Do You Qualify for the SEAL Program?
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="notify">
        </div>
        <div class="body" data-button-class="all">
         <ul>
          <li>
           To qualify for the Navy SEAL program, you must:
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Be male
          </li>
          <li>
           Be 28 years old or younger
          </li>
          <li>
           Be a U.S. citizen
          </li>
          <li>
           Meet specific eyesight requirements
          </li>
          <li>
           Meet minimum Armed Services Vocational Aptitude Battery (ASVAB) score
          </li>
          <li>
           Complete a variety of physical screening tests that involve:
           <ol>
            <li>
             Swimming
            </li>
            <li>
             Push-ups
            </li>
            <li>
             Sit-ups
            </li>
            <li>
             Running
            </li>
           </ol>
          </li>
          <li>
           Pass the Basic Underwater Demolition/SEALS (BUD/S) during boot camp
          </li>
         </ol>
         <ul>
          <li>
           If you are already an officer in the Navy, you can apply to be a SEAL.  Your training will be the same as others who enlist.
          </li>
         </ul>
        </div>
       </div>

        <div class="head">
         <div class="grip">
         </div>
         <h2 class="title">
          Step 3: SEAL Training
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="notify">
        </div>
        <div class="body" data-button-class="all">
         <ul>
          <li>
           If you meet all qualifications and are accepted into the Navy SEALs training program, you will spend more than two years learning the necessary skills you will need.  Prospective Navy SEALs undergo 12 months of initial training, including:
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Physical conditioning
          </li>
          <li>
           Diving
          </li>
          <li>
           Parachute jump school
          </li>
         </ol>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Once you complete the initial training, you will begin 18 months of intensive advanced pre-deployment training.  After your advanced training is completed, you will be a Navy SEAL and will be deployed on your first mission.
          </li>
         </ul>
