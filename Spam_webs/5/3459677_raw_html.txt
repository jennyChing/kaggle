        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Bug Salve: How to Make a DEET-Free Bug Repellent Balm
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2013-05-04T17:54:34+00:00">
          May 4, 2013
         </span>
         By
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://frugallysustainable.com/author/andrea/" rel="author">
            Andrea
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://frugallysustainable.com/2013/05/bug-salve-how-to-make-a-deet-free-bug-repellent-balm/#comments">
           18 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <script async="" data-pin-color="red" data-pin-hover="true" defer="" src="http://frugallysustainable.com/wp-content/plugins/pinterest-pin-it-button-on-image-hover-and-post/js/pinit.js" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://frugallysustainable.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/036.jpg">
           <img alt="Frugally Sustainable's Bug Repellent Balm" class="aligncenter wp-image-3502" height="409" src="http://frugallysustainable.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/036-660x492.jpg" width="549"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Biting insects.
          <br/>
          Mosquitoes.
          <br/>
          Stinging bees.
         </p>
         <p>
          Anti-bite solution.
         </p>
         <p>
          Spring is here and summer is on its way.
         </p>
         <p>
          Myfamily isloving the warmer weather andweare all thrilled to beenjoying more time outdoors.
         </p>
         <p>
          Gardening.
          <br/>
          Hiking.
          <br/>
          Camping.
         </p>
         <p>
          We all know that with outside play and warmer weathercomes the signs of biting insects.
         </p>
         <p>
          And insect repellent isa nice thing to have around.
         </p>
         <p>
          Not the toxic, chemical laden ones.
         </p>
         <p>
          The
          <a href="https://www.etsy.com/listing/95421710/natural-bug-be-gone-spray-2oz">
           handmade, all-natural,eco-friendly ones
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive
          <a href="http://frugallysustainable.com/2012/03/handmade-natural-bug-repellent-spray-a-recipe/">
           shared my recipe with you for an all-natural bug repellent spray
          </a>
          . But this year I wanted to try making
          <a href="https://www.etsy.com/listing/130976979/new-bug-salve">
           a bug repellent salve
          </a>
          . I like the spray for coveragebut the salveI thought would besuper handy for the face, neck, and hard-to-reachareas.
         </p>
         <p>
          results=amazing
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           <strong>
            Bug Repellent Salve Recipe
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ingredients
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          -8 ounces olive,
          <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=123853">
           coconut
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=123853">
           sweet almond
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=123853">
           jojoba
          </a>
          , or
          <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=123853">
           sesame oil
          </a>
          <em>
           (or a blend of any of these oils)
          </em>
          <br/>
          -1-2 ounces
          <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/90401500/100-raw-natural-and-locally-harvested">
           beeswax
          </a>
          <br/>
          -0.4 ounces
          <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=123853">
           citronella essential oils
          </a>
          <br/>
          -0.2 ounces
          <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=123853">
           eucalyptus essential oils
          </a>
          <br/>
          -0.2 ounces
          <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=123853">
           lemongrass essential oils
          </a>
          <br/>
          -0.2 ounces
          <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=123853">
           cedarwood essential oils
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Method
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Measure the oils and beeswax using a
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003E7AZQA/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B003E7AZQA&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=frugalsustai-20">
            kitchen scale
           </a>
           .
           <em>
            ***Use1-2 ounces of
            <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/90401500/100-raw-natural-and-locally-harvested">
             beeswax
            </a>
            (depending on how thick youd like your salve).I suggest starting with the lesser amount of wax and adding more if needed. To check if the mixture is the right consistency, because the salve hardens as it cools,
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1603420789/ref=as_li_tf_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=frugalsustai-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1603420789">
             Rosemary Gladstar
            </a>
            <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=frugalsustai-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1603420789" width="1"/>
            again suggests placing a tablespoon of the mixture in the freezer for just a minute or two. If its soft, add more beeswax; if too hard, add more oil.***
           </em>
          </li>
          <li>
           In a double boiler, or small pot, over very low heatslowly melt the oils and beeswax
           <em>
            (
            <strong>
             Note:
            </strong>
            Do NOT include the essential oils at this time).
           </em>
          </li>
          <li>
           Once melted remove from heat and allow to cool slightly before adding the essential oils.
          </li>
          <li>
           Pour mixture intoseveral
           <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/supply/misc.php?AID=123853">
            roll-up tubes, glass containers, tins, orlip balmtubes
           </a>
           (smaller mason jars work well too)and allow it to cool on the counter overnight.
          </li>
          <li>
           Label with the name, ingredients, and date of creation.
          </li>
          <li>
           Salve will keep for 2-5 years depending on quality of oils used.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          <strong>
           To use:
          </strong>
          Roll over skin prior to gardening, working in the yard, hiking, biking, enjoying a sunset or anything that requires being in the great outdoors. Apply generously and as often as needed for protection from biting insects.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           <strong>
            Note
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          -Searching for an After-Bite BalmI have also
          <a href="http://frugallysustainable.com/2012/04/a-recipe-for-homemade-itch-relief-stick-and-a-giveaway-for-you/">
           shared my recipe for an Anti-Itch Stick
          </a>
          one that gives the over-the-counter antihistamine a serious run for its money!
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           <strong>
            Where Can I Get This Stuff?

         </p>
