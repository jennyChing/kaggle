               Oh my yes! this is one of those revelations that many people, I suppose, never experience. I have seen this play out several ways in my own life, and I am sure there are many other falsity of my own thoughts paths that others should PLEASE describe so that we can benefit!
               <br/>
               * Politics / social: I have been on many occasions, but particularly on the occasion of OJ Simpsons conviction, quite horrified at how my personal cultural identification, and other peoples, colors (pun intended) their views of how events regarding crimes have happened. Hurricane Katrina, The Innocence Project, all kinds of other occasions spring to mind. You may want to think that you are neutral and open, but if you watch yourself very carefully, you most likely are notyou identify and you justify, for good or ill, with people who seem kin to you. Distance and plenty of excellent information are your only helps here. You are going to automatically attribute the best motives to those who are in your house, and the worst to those who are in the other house, however you define house. Be aware of this! Be open to information! ALWAYS verify, ESPECIALLY if bad news about the other side appeals to you greatly.
               <br/>
               * Psychological: Depression takes an accurate view of the world and its negative possibilities. After all, in the long run we are all dead. But a 2 year course of anti-depressant, anti-anxiety meds somewhere about 20 years ago revealed to me that my ever ready tears and my tragic view of the world were generated by brain chemistry, which could be changed. The tragic view was not the ONE TRUE VIEW. It was just the view my brain had taken for me since I was about 13. Suddenly, at 50-something, I found out that tears and sadness were not the only realistic reaction to everything.
               <br/>
               Further, I learned that I was a person who just automatically catastrophized in all kinds of situations. I imagined the worst possible outcome. It sprang into my mind fully costumed and plotted and story-boarded at the drop of a hat. The more outre and bizarre the idea, the more fully clothed it appeared. My antidote? Repeating the mantra over and over and over again you dont know.
              </p>
              <p>
               Honestly, you dont know is one of the most powerful things you can say to yourself. Say it frequently. Believe it. It is true. You DONT.
