           <h3>
            <strong>
             Heres where we want to go in 2015 and why:
            </strong>
           </h3>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/countries/philippines-guide/" target="_blank" title="Nat Geo Travel site - Philippines Guide">
              Philippines
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            I cant get the Philippines out of my mind. This archipelago is the land of my ancestors, so I am a little biased. Nevertheless, there is a lot to lure even those who dont have family ties there. Islands like
            <a href="http://www.bohol.ph" target="_blank" title="Bohol tourism site">
             Bohol
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/philippines/palawan" target="_blank" title="Lonely Planet site - Palawan">
             Palawan
            </a>
            beckon with strange and lovely creatures like the tarsier (looks like Yoda) and the monitor lizard (looks like a dragon). Manila is one of Asias most dynamic capitals. Hotels range from the historic
            <a href="http://www.manila-hotel.com.ph" target="_blank" title="Manila Hotel site">
             Manila Hotel
            </a>
            (Douglas McArthur lived there shortly before World War II) to the luxury castaway
            <a href="http://www.elnidoresorts.com/elnido/" target="_blank" title="El Nido Resorts site">
             El Nido Resorts
            </a>
            in Palawan (on stilts overlooking transparent waters). And the fooddont even get me started. Lets just say Im dreaming of
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halo-halo" target="_blank" title="Wikipedia site - Halo-halo">
             <em>
              halo-halo
             </em>
            </a>
            , an icy dessert of tropical fruits thats simply heaven on the tongue. Norie Quintos (on
            <a href="https://twitter.com/noriecicerone" target="_blank" title="Twitter site - Norie Cicerone (Quintos) profile">
             Twitter
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://instagram.com/noriecicerone" target="_blank" title="Instagram site - Norie Cicerone (Quintos) profile">
             Instagram
            </a>
            @NorieCicerone), acting editor in chief,
            <em>
             National Geographic Traveler
            </em>
           </p>
           <figure aria-labelledby="figcaption_attachment_58779" class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_58779" style="width: 342px">
            <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2015/01/borneo-orangutan.jpg">
             <img alt="The imperiled Bornean orangutan (Pongo pygmaeus) is native to the island of Borneo. (Photograph by romeral, Flickr)" class=" wp-image-58779" height="249" src="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2015/01/borneo-orangutan.jpg" width="332"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="figcaption_attachment_58779">
             The imperiled Bornean orangutan (Pongo pygmaeus) is native to the island of Borneo. (Photograph by romeral, Flickr)
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/countries/cuba-guide/" target="_blank" title="Nat Geo Travel site - Cuba Guide">
              Cuba
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Like many Americans, Im so curious about Cuba. Now that travel restrictions have been eased, I plan to watchclassic cars cruise past crumbling colonial buildings in Old Havana, awkwardly dance along to live
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timba" target="_blank" title="Wikipedia site - Timba">
             timba music
            </a>
            , and cycle through the tobacco fields of the
            <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/840" target="_blank" title="World Heritage Convention site - Vinales Valley">
             Viales Valley
            </a>
            the very first chance I get. Christine Blau (on Twitter
            <a href="https://twitter.com/Chris_Blau" target="_blank" title="Christine Blau - Twitter Profile">
             @Chris_Blau
            </a>
            and on Instagram
            <a href="http://instagram.com/christineblau" target="_blank" title="Instagram site - Christine Blau profile">
             @christineblau
            </a>
            ), researcher,
            <em>
             National Geographic Traveler
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://www.borneotourism.com" target="_blank" title="Borneo Tourism site">
              Borneo
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            I am longing to go to see wild orangutans before they disappear. Two places you can see them:
            <a href="http://orangutan.org/our-projects/research/camp-leakey/" target="_blank" title="Orangutan Foundation International site - Camp Leakey">
             Camp Leakey
            </a>
            and the
            <a href="http://orangutan.org/our-projects/rehabilitation/orangutan-care-center/" target="_blank" title="Orangutan Foundation International site - Orangutan Care Center">
             Orangutan Care Center
            </a>
            , both established in central Borneo by primatologist
            <a href="http://orangutan.org/about/dr-birute-mary-galdikas/" target="_blank" title="Orangutan Foundation International site - Dr. Birute Mary Galdikas biography">
             Dr. Birut Mary Galdikas
            </a>
            , the first person to study the primates in their native habitat (and the author of a June 1980 cover story on the subjectfor
            <em>
             National Geographic
            </em>
            magazine). Dr. Galdikas has worked to protect the orangutans and their rain forest habitat for the past 40 years, defending these charismatic primates from illegal logging and mining. Marilyn Terrell (on
            <a href="https://twitter.com/marilyn_res" target="_blank" title="Twitter site - Marilyn Terrell profile">
             Twitter
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://instagram.com/marilyn_res" target="_blank" title="Instagram site - Marilyn Terrell profile">
             Instagram
            </a>
            @marilyn_res), chief researcher,
            <em>
             National Geographic Traveler
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://www.visitisleofman.com" target="_blank" title="Visit Isle of Man site">
              Isle of Man
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            This year is my year to finally visit the Isle of Man, a self-governing British Crown dependency sitting lonely in the middle of the Irish Sea. Ive always been fascinated by the island, which boasts its own currency, breed of cat (the
            <a href="http://www.cfa.org/Breeds/BreedsKthruR/Manx.aspx" target="_blank" title="Cat Fanciers' Association site - Manx">
             Manx
            </a>
            ), team sport (
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cammag" target="_blank" title="Wikipedia site - Cammag">
             <em>
              cammag
             </em>
            </a>
            , akin to Irish hurling), and Celtic tongue. Dramatic cliffside views, Viking and medieval castle ruins, kaleidoscopic sunsets, and uber-fresh seafood (is it overkill to have kippers, salmon, and scallops every single day?) should fill a fantastic week. Larry Porges, Nat Geo Travel Books
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://chile.travel/en/where-to-go/chiloe/" target="_blank" title="Chile Travel site - Chiloe">
              Chilo
             </a>
             (Chile)
            </strong>
           </p>
           <figure aria-labelledby="figcaption_attachment_58780" class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_58780" style="width: 342px">
            <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2015/01/chiloe-chile.jpg">
             <img alt="Chilo is known for colorful houses on stilts known as palafitos. (Photograph by silviavk, Flickr)" class=" wp-image-58780" height="249" src="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2015/01/chiloe-chile.jpg" width="332"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="figcaption_attachment_58780">
             Chilo is known for colorful houses on stilts known as palafitos. (Photograph by silviavk, Flickr)
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            Im hearing a lot about this land mass off the coast of Chile, considered a rising destination for Chileans. The fifth largest island in South America, Chiloe has been compared to Scotland for its moors, green sheep-grazed meadows, and rugged fishing communities. Of particular interest: the many wooden churches built by fishermen (who had few other materials to work with), severalof which are protected as
            <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/971" target="_blank" title="World Heritage Convention site - Churches of Chiloe">
             World Heritage sites
            </a>
            . Also creating buzz: new, locally inspired lodgings, including the
            <a href="http://www.hpq.cl/en/" target="_blank" title="Quilquico Park Hotel site">
             Quilquico Park Hotel
            </a>
            , modeled on the indigenous
            <em>
             palafitos
            </em>
            fishermen houses built on stiltsand the architecturally striking
            <a href="http://www.tierrachiloe.com" target="_blank" title="Tierra Chiloe site">
             Tierra Chilolodge
            </a>
            . The word is, get here sooner than later if you want to have the place more or less to yourself.Jayne Wise, senior editor,
            <em>
             National Geographic Traveler
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://www.skiportillo.com/?lang=en" target="_blank" title="Ski Portillo site">
              Portillo
             </a>
             (Chile)
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            By the time August rolls around here at Nat Geos D.C. headquarters, Im completely over the horrible, humid summer that engulfs the nations capital and want to escape to the cold. Thats precisely what this resort in the Chilean Andesoffers: open-piste skiing on spring corn with refreshingly brisk temperatures, lots of wine, and even more beef. Then, after a week of skiing, I hope to spend a few days at the edges of
            <a href="http://chile.travel/en/where-to-go/patagonia-2/" target="_blank" title="Chile Travel site - Patagonia">
             Patagonia
            </a>
            , spotting animals as they start to emerge from the shadows and warm themselves in the Southern Hemisphere sun. Nathan Borchelt, senior product manager, National Geographic Travel and Adventure
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/city-guides/rome-italy/" target="_blank" title="Nat Geo Travel site - Rome Guide">
              Rome
             </a>
             (Italy)
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            I spent around seven years of my educational experience studying Latin. Unless I quickly need to translate the motto on a college seal, my knowledge of the extinct language doesnt get much use. This year I hope to visit Rome for the first time in order to satisfy my inner Latin nerd, surrounded by ancient history and plenty of pasta. Hannah Sheinberg (on Twitter
            <a href="https://twitter.com/h_sheinberg" target="_blank" title="Twitter site - Hannah Sheinberg profile">
             @h_sheinberg
            </a>
            ), assistant editor,
            <em>
             National Geographic Traveler
            </em>
           </p>
           <figure aria-labelledby="figcaption_attachment_58782" class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_58782" style="width: 347px">
            <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2015/01/trevi-fountain.jpg">
             <img alt="Trevi Fountain, a Baroque masterpiece in the center of Rome (Photograph by evanblaser, Flickr)" class=" wp-image-58782" height="225" src="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2015/01/trevi-fountain.jpg" width="337"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="figcaption_attachment_58782">
             Trevi Fountain, a Baroque masterpiece in the center of Rome (Photograph by evanblaser, Flickr)
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/countries/botswana-guide/" target="_blank" title="Nat Geo Travel site - Botswana Guide">
              Botswana
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Two literary prompts put Botswana at the top of my wish list this year. One is the recent opportunity to work with
            <a href="http://www.alexandermccallsmith.co.uk" target="_blank" title="Alexander McCall Smith site">
             Alexander McCall Smith
            </a>
            ,author of the
            <a href="http://www.goodreads.com/series/43418-no-1-ladies-detective-agency" target="_blank" title='Goodreads site - "No. 1 Ladies Detective Agency" series'>
             <em>
              No. 1 Ladies Detective Agency
             </em>
             series
            </a>
            , which is set in Botswana, on a piece for
            <em>
             Traveler
            </em>
            s My City section. The other isJodi Picoults
            <a href="http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/18816603-leaving-time" target="_blank" title='Goodreads site - "Leaving Time"'>
             <em>
              Leaving Time
             </em>
            </a>
            , a recent
            <em>
             Traveler
            </em>
            Book Club selection centered around a researcher studying elephant cognition in Botswana. The novel imparted a ton of surprising information about elephant behavior that I found fascinating, and Id love the opportunity to see these amazingand endangeredanimals in their natural habitat. Amy Alipio (on Twitter
            <a href="https://twitter.com/amytravels" target="_blank" title="Twitter site - Amy Alipio profile">
             @amytravels
            </a>
            and on Instagram
            <a href="http://instagram.com/amyalipio" target="_blank" title="Instagram site - Amy Alipio profile">
             @amyalipio
            </a>
            ), features editor,
            <em>
             National Geographic Traveler
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/united-states/hawaii-guide/" target="_blank" title="Nat Geo Travel site - Hawaii Guide">
              Hawaii
             </a>
             (United States)
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            As someone who loves the beach and the woods with equal fervor, Hawaii has always been at the top of my travel list. And if Im being completely honest, Im a huge fan of
            <em>
             Jurassic Park
            </em>
            , most of which was filmed on Kauai. With
            <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0369610/" target="_blank" title='IMDB site - "Jurassic World"'>
             <em>
              Jurassic World
             </em>
            </a>
            premiering this year, 2015 seems like the perfect time to experiencethe beautiful scenery of the original film firsthand. Plus, who doesnt love a good meal made withSPAM? Megan Heltzel (on
            <a href="https://twitter.com/MeganHeltzel" target="_blank" title="Twitter site - Megan Heltzel profile">
             Twitter
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://instagram.com/meganheltzel" target="_blank" title="Instagram site - Megan Heltzel profile">
             Instagram
            </a>
            @meganheltzel), associate producer, National Geographic Travel
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/countries/palau-guide/" target="_blank" title="Nat Geo Travel site - Palau Guide">
              Palau
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            This year, Im determined to tradethe city streets of D.C. for the idyllic island ofPalau. Tropical wildlife abounds on this archipelago in the western Pacific Ocean and travelers can get up close and personal with many species. I plan to snorkel amonghordes of 
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jellyfish_Lake" target="_blank" title="Wikipedia site - Jellyfish Lake (Palau)">
             friendly jellyfish
            </a>
             without fear of being stung. Other must-dosinclude kayaking across clear, shallow water to view colorful coral and, of course, lying on the beach and drinking in thebeautifulsetting. Rebecca Davis, associate producer, National Geographic Travel
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/countries/vietnam-guide/" target="_blank" title="Nat Geo Travel site - Vietnam Guide">
              Vietnam
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <figure aria-labelledby="figcaption_attachment_58783" class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_58783" style="width: 346px">
            <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2015/01/palau-jellyfish.jpg">
             <img alt="Palau's genetically distinctive jellyfish inhabit a saltwater lake on Eil Malk island. (Photograph by picturecorrect, Flickr)" class=" wp-image-58783" height="252" src="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2015/01/palau-jellyfish.jpg" width="336"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="figcaption_attachment_58783">
             Palaus genetically distinctive jellyfish inhabit a saltwater lake on Eil Malk island. (Photograph by picturecorrect, Flickr)
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            My father passed away at the end of December after a three-year battle with cancer. He completed a tour of duty in the Vietnam War, and though his feelings about the experience were complex, his service gained significance for him as he aged. Id like to travel to Vietnam to honor his memory and better understand his experience there. Id also like to see another side of the Southeast Asian nation that he never got the chanceto appreciate. Leslie Trew Magraw, editor/producer (on
            <a href="https://twitter.com/leslietrew" target="_blank" title="Twitter site - Leslie Trew Magraw profile">
             Twitter
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://instagram.com/leslietrew" target="_blank" title="Instagram site - Leslie Trew Magraw profile">
             Instagram
            </a>
            @leslietrew), Intelligent Travel
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/ireland/county-kerry" target="_blank" title="Lonely Planet site - County Kerry">
              County Kerry
             </a>
             (Ireland)
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            After visiting
            <a href="http://visitcorkcounty.com" target="_blank" title="Visit Cork County site">
             County Cork
            </a>
            and receiving such a warm welcomethat it felt like home (even though I dont have a stitch of Irish in me), I now want to visit County Kerry, where my husbands paternal lineoriginated. Id add in a trip to view the puffins on
            <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/757" target="_blank" title="World Heritage Convention site - Skellig Michael">
             Skellig Michael
            </a>
            , a remote island where a community of monks once lived in the early Middle Ages. Irish playwright
            <a href="http://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/literature/laureates/1925/shaw-bio.html" target="_blank" title="Nobel Prize site - George Bernard Shaw biography">
             George Bernard Shaw
            </a>
            described the Skellig Islands in a letter as being part of our dream world. Susan OKeefe, associate editor,
            <em>
             National Geographic Traveler
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             &gt;
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/city-guides/san-francisco-california/" target="_blank" title="Nat Geo Travel site - San Francisco Guide">
              San Francisco
             </a>
             (United States)
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Ive been building a playlist of songs that remind me of, or make reference to, the Bay Area. (The most recent addition? Mayer Hawthornes cover of Tony Bennetts I Left My Heart in San Francisco.) I plan to listen to it as Im touching down atSFO. I cant wait to eat good Chinese food. Kevin Kunitake, editorial assistant,
            <em>
             National Geographic Traveler
            </em>
           </p>
