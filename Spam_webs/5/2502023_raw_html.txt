     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        TEENick
       </b>
      </i>
      was a teen-oriented television
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Programming_block" title="Programming block">
       programming block
      </a>
      that aired on
      <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">
       Nickelodeon
      </a>
      . The block launched on March 6, 2001
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and lasted until February 1, 2009. TEENick aired on Sunday nights from 6-9 p.m. ET/PT and, beginning in 2004, began to also air on Saturday nights from 8-10 p.m. ET/PT (replacing the popular
      <a href="/wiki/SNICK" title="SNICK">
       SNICK
      </a>
      block that started in 1992). It was originally hosted by
      <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon" title="Nick Cannon">
       Nick Cannon
      </a>
      , and then by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jason_Everhart" title="Jason Everhart">
       Jason Everhart
      </a>
      (aka "J. Boogie").
     </p>
     <p>
      "TEENick" and "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_N" title="The N">
       The N
      </a>
      " had a programming block called
      <i>
       <b>
        TEENick on The N
       </b>
      </i>
      . It aired on January 16, 2007 and then lasted until May 12, 2007, but then returned on March 1, 2008. The block included shows such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Romeo!" title="Romeo!">
        Romeo!
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Just_Jordan" title="Just Jordan">
        Just Jordan
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/ICarly" title="ICarly">
        iCarly
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Are_You_Afraid_of_the_Dark%3F" title="Are You Afraid of the Dark?">
        Are You Afraid of the Dark?
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Drake_%26_Josh" title="Drake &amp; Josh">
        Drake &amp; Josh
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/All_That" title="All That">
        All That
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Amanda_Show" title="The Amanda Show">
        The Amanda Show
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Zoey_101" title="Zoey 101">
        Zoey 101
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Mr._Meaty" title="Mr. Meaty">
        Mr. Meaty
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/All_Grown_Up!" title="All Grown Up!">
        All Grown Up!
       </a>
      </i>
      . On August 3, 2009, TEENick was dropped from The N again.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       TEENick
      </i>
      name was discontinued on February 1, 2009 to give a separation of time between the block's end and the September 28, 2009 launch of
      <a href="/wiki/TeenNick" title="TeenNick">
       TeenNick
      </a>
      , the replacement name for "The N", a 24-hour network which had progressed from taking half of the broadcasting day on Noggin from 2002-2009 to eventually becoming its own network at the end of 2007 under the former 1999-2007 (2009 on
      <a href="/wiki/Dish_Network" title="Dish Network">
       Dish Network
      </a>
      ) channel space of
      <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_Games_and_Sports_for_Kids" title="Nickelodeon Games and Sports for Kids">
       Nickelodeon Games and Sports for Kids
      </a>
      . Nickelodeon continued to air new episodes of live-action series on Saturday nights like
      <a href="/wiki/ICarly" title="ICarly">
       iCarly
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Victorious" title="Victorious">
       Victorious
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Big_Time_Rush" title="Big Time Rush">
       Big Time Rush
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/How_to_Rock" title="How to Rock">
       How to Rock
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/You_Gotta_See_This" title="You Gotta See This">
       You Gotta See This
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Supah_Ninjas" title="Supah Ninjas">
       Supah Ninjas
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bucket_and_Skinner%27s_Epic_Adventures" title="Bucket and Skinner's Epic Adventures">
       Bucket and Skinner's Epic Adventures
      </a>
      without any theme branding. In Fall 2012, the final episodes of iCarly and Victorious, new episodes of Big Time Rush, How to Rock and You Gotta See This was branded as part of Nick's newest Saturday block
      <a href="/wiki/Gotta_See_Saturdays" title="Gotta See Saturdays">
       Gotta See Saturdays
      </a>
      , which airs Saturday mornings and nights.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
