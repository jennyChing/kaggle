                <h2 id="release-happy-neurotransmitters">
                 1.Release Happy Neurotransmitters
                </h2>
                <div>
                 <div class="paszone_container paszone-container-125 " id="paszonecont_125" style="overflow:hidden;  text-align:center;  ">
                  <div class="pasinfotxt above">
                   <small style="font-size:11px; color:#C0C0C0; text-decoration:none;">
                   </small>
                  </div>
                  <div class="wppaszone proadszone-125 " id="125" style="overflow:hidden;  width:100%; height:auto;  text-align:center; ">

                <p>
                 There are four neurotransmitters primarily responsible for your feeling of happiness: endorphins (numbs pain), dopamine (increases pleasure), oxytocin (feeling of love/inclusion) and serotonin (feeling of satisfaction). By learning how to trigger the production of these chemicals, you can use your own bodys natural defences to fight pain, anxiety and stress.
                </p>
                <h3>
                 Get Some Sunlight
                </h3>
                <p>
                 Humans need a certain amount of sunlight in order to be happy and healthy. Too much or too little and we start to suffer both physically and mentally. For example,
                 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seasonal_affective_disorder" target="_blank" title="Seasonal Affective Disorder Wikipedia Article">
                  Seasonal Affective Disorder
                 </a>
                 (SAD)is a type of depression that some people experienceas a result of the change of seasons, often linked to the under exposure of sunlight.
                </p>
                <p>
                 If its difficult to get the necessary dose of daily sunlight exposure where you are, look into vitamin D supplements or light therapy. Please consult a doctor to ensure that you can safely proceed!
                </p>
                <h3>
                 Exercise
                </h3>
                <p>
                 One of the most effective ways to produce endorphins, dopamine and serotonin, is to exercise.
                </p>
                <p>
                 When marathon runners refer to the runners high or bodybuilders mention how they love the burn, theyre referring, in part, to the endorphin rush they get from exercising. In fact, its been speculated that many professional athletes are actually addicted to exercise for this reason.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The most beneficial type of exercise is short and intense, but even a brisk walk or a few minutes of casual exercise canhave a positive effect.
                </p>
                <h3>
                 Eat Something Spicy
                </h3>
                <p>
                 Your body produces endorphins to combat pain and stress. Therefore, one way to force your body to release endorphins is to endure more pain and stress! The burning sensation you get from eating spicy food is one such example. In response to the pain your mouth endures from spicy food, your body automatically releases endorphins.
                </p>
                <h3>
                 Laugh &amp; Smile
                </h3>
                <p>
                 Its no secret that laughter and smiling can combat stress and anxiety. Luckily, its easier than ever to access things that make uslaugh and smile, such as:
                </p>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  Calling a friend or loved one that knows how to make you laugh or smile for a quick chat
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Watching motivational or inspirational videos on YouTube
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Looking at funny animal pictures
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <p>
                 Just a fewminutes of these positive mood changing distractions can have a drastic positive impact on your productivity.However, you dont want to get stuck watching dog pictures for 20 minutes, so make sure to keep these sessions short and brief!
                </p>
                <h3>
                 Eat Dark Chocolate
                </h3>
                <p>
                 Certain foods are known to stimulate the release of oxytocin. One of the most famous such foods is dark chocolate. However, I highly advise against relying on food as a stress combatant. This is because it can lead to you developing a habit ofeating when stressed, which is not a goodhabit to develop. That being said, it is goodas an occasional mood booster.
                </p>
                <h3>
                 Give
                </h3>
                <p>
                 Study after study has shown that one of the greatest ways to obtain happiness and fulfillment is through helping others. This includes, but is not limited to:
                </p>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  Give study advice
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Give acompliment
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Give a present
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Help with a task
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <p>
                 Example: If you had only $1 and you had two choices, buy yourself a chocolate bar or share the chocolate bar with your friend, which one do you think would increase your happiness level and reduce your stress more?
                </p>
                <h2 id="deep-breathing-exercise">
                 2. Deep Breathing Exercise
                </h2>
                <p>
                 One of the most effective ways to alleviatestress and anxiety is to slow your heart rate. Aslow heart willreduce sweating, helps regulate your temperature and alleviates other symptoms of stress.
                </p>
                <p>
                 There are many ways to slow your heart rate, such as meditation and yoga, but Ill introduce one very simple and effective routine.
                </p>
                <h3>
                 7-7-7 Relaxation Routine
                </h3>
                <p>
                 From start to finish, this exercise will take less than 2 minutes. Its preferable to do this in a quiet, distraction free environment, but it can done anywhere.
                </p>
                <ol>
                 <li>
                  Close your eyes
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Relax the muscles in your body
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Breath in through your nosewhile counting to7 seconds
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Breath out through your mouth while counting to 7 seconds
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Repeat 7 times
                 </li>
                </ol>
                <p>
                 After this routine, you will be in a hyper relaxed state andmost of the physical symptoms of stress should be completely gone!
                </p>

                 Another symptom of stress and anxiety is muscle tension. Similar to your heart rate, these problem areas can betargeted with a simple routine.

                <h3>
                 2-Minute Finger Massage
                </h3>
                <p>
                 Finger massages dont require any special skill and can be performed anywhere. All you have to do is lightly apply pressure and make slow circular motions on the spots listed below. Thats it!
                </p>
                <ol>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Your Temples
                  </strong>
                  :Gently massage the soft spots between your eyes and ears.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Your Eyelids
                  </strong>
                  :Close your eyelids and gently massage the upper eyelid and lower eyelid.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Behind Your Ears
                  </strong>
                  :Gently massage the bony area right behind your ears.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Rear Neck
                  </strong>
                  :Feel around the area under your hairline on the back of your head and down to your shoulders for muscle tension.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   The Palms &amp; Soles Of Your Feet
                  </strong>
                  :Use your thumbs to feel around for muscle tension in the palms of your hand and soles of your feet. Note: Theres an entire industry associated with this type of massage called
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflexology" target="_blank" title="Reflexology WIkipedia Article">
                   Reflexology
                  </a>
                  .
                 </li>

                <h2 id="wash-it-away">
                 5.Wash It Away
                </h2>
                <p>
                 Another obvious symptom of stress is sweat. If left untreated,it can leave you feeling uncomfortable exacerbating the problem.
                </p>
                <h3>
                 Wash Your Hands And Face
                </h3>
                <p>
                 Rinse your hands under warm water (if your hands are cold) or cold water (if your hands are hot) and splash some on your face tofeel instantly revitalized.
                </p>
                <h3>
                 Take A Shower
                </h3>
                <p>
                 Showering is a great stress reliever. If youre at home, dont hesitate to take a quick 5-minute rinse.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The act of washing with water signals to your body that youre resetting your mood.
                </p>
                <h2 id="prevent-it">
                 6. Prevent It
                </h2>
                <div>
                 <div class="paszone_container paszone-container-137 " id="paszonecont_137" style="overflow:hidden;  text-align:center;  ">
                  <div class="pasinfotxt above">
                   <small style="font-size:11px; color:#C0C0C0; text-decoration:none;">
                   </small>
                  </div>
                  <div class="wppaszone proadszone-137 " id="137" style="overflow:hidden;  width:100%; height:auto;  text-align:center; ">
                   <div class="wppasrotate proadszoneholder-137" style="width:100%; height:100%;">
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <p>
                 Rather than deal with stress when it occurs, its much more effective to prevent it. There are many factors that you may not be consciously aware of that can negatively impact your mood. Some of these things are minor, but cumulatively, they can have a significant impact.
                </p>
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Location
                  </strong>
                  If youre twice as productive at a cafe than at home, but it takes you 15 minutes to walk to the cafe, then go to the cafe.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Energy Level
                  </strong>
                   Dont start studying if you feel unusually tired or lazy. Youre better off doing something else (exercising, going for a walk,cooking, etc.) and coming back to it.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Sleep
                  </strong>
                   Inadequate sleephas a wide range of negative effects on your health and well-being. Make sure to get a minimum of 7 hours of sleep a day.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Temperature
                  </strong>
                   Most people are comfortable between 21 to 24 degrees celsius (70 to 75 fahrenheit) If you cant control the temperature, dress appropriately to compensate. Its better to be slightly cold than too hot.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Hunger
                  </strong>
                   Dont start a study session too full (youll feel lethargic) or too hungry (youll be unable to focus).
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Thirst
                  </strong>
                   Always stay hydrated. Keep a cup of water or a sugar-free beverage in front of you when possible.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Study Area
                  </strong>
                  Clear your study area of any distractions (no games, toys, electronics, etc.).
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  <strong>
                   Study Partner
                  </strong>
                   Working witha good study partner (not a socializing partner) can have an amazing effect on your productivity and mood, especially if you find it difficult to stay productive on your own.
                 </li>
                </ul>
