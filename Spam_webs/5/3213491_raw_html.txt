      <h1 class="headline old-post-headline">
       Everybodys Gone Couchsurfing!
      </h1>
      <div class="post_content">
       <p>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         <strong>
          Everybody but us.
         </strong>
         At least, it sure felt that way.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         And that had apparently left us with a large blank space on our traveling resume. Weve read/heard several times that
        </span>
        <em>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          you arent a real traveler if you havent
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.couchsurfing.org/">
          couchsurfed
         </a>
        </em>
        .
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         I cant even say why we hadnt done it before. We signed up on the website at the beginning of our travels in South America, but our account sat stagnant. I largely blame it on the fact that it requires actually planning
         <em>
          where were going to be
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          when were going to be there
         </em>
         , which we are not particularly good at.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         So when we knew our schedule for Ireland well in advance, we had plenty of time to do our research and apply for a couchsurf. We happily spent four nights in Dublin with a lovely couple living near the popular Ranelagh district.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <strong>
         Now we get it.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         The avoidance of cash outlay on a hostel room is of the smallest importance in the whole couchsurfing concept.
         <em>
          Its about meeting locals, getting to know a neighborhood outside of the main tourist areas, and engaging in absorbing and informative conversations that bridge oceans.
         </em>
         <strong>
          Its about the beauty of connecting with strangers with trust, openness and respect.
         </strong>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         Its also about getting spoiled with a delicious home cooked Guinness stew.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/guinnesstew.jpg">
         <img alt="Guinness Stew" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3292" height="400" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/guinnesstew.jpg" title="Guinness Stew" width="600"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         <br/>
         And reconnecting with purring, four legged creatures that
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/freedom" title="Freedom">
         we miss the comfort of
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/kitty.jpg">
         <img alt="Kitty friend in Dublin while couchsurfing" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3293" height="400" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/kitty.jpg" title="Kitty friend in Dublin while couchsurfing" width="600"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         We are very grateful for the new friends weve made and the wonderfully comfortable bed to recover from our jet lag.
         <em>
          (Thanks C &amp; F!)
         </em>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         While I am not someone who will ever tell you what you 
         <em>
          have to do
         </em>
         
         <strong>
          you really should try couchsurfing
         </strong>
         . And this goes for all those with a spare bed or particularly comfy couch that can be
         <em>
          surfed
         </em>
         as well.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>

             <p>
              As fellow travelers and CouchSurfers who also move through the world with no plans, I can say this about CS: It is ABSOLUTELY possible to surf even if you dont know where youre going to be or when youre going to be there. In fact, weve gained some of greatest CS friends out of these last-minute scenarios. A couple of tips:
             </p>
             <p>
              -Last minute couch groups: These are usually a subgroup within a city group. It consists of people who are totally comfortable with last minute requests and will jump at the chance to help you out. Weve found couches (and awesome hosts) in as little as 4 minutes this way. If a city doesnt have a last minute group, you can post a last minute request in the city group Just be sure to read the group rules before posting. If the group doesnt like last minute requests posted there, theyll specify.
             </p>
             <p>
              -CS Meetups: Many cities hold these on a weekly basis. First of all, its just a cool way to meet locals and socialize. Second, if you mention youre in need of a couch, people will almost always step up and offer although I certainly wouldnt go JUST for a couch or absolutely expecting to find one, just a bonus if you do
              <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>

             <p>
              We have couchsurfed a bunch over our 2 months of traveling already  12 times in japan, 3 times in South Korea and 4 times in China. While the sleeping situations were not always ideal (sleeping in the same room 4 people to a room, or sleeping on bean bag chairs in a living room) the experiences have always been great. Weve had some of our best meals with couchsurfers taking us to local food restaurants and have some seriously amazing home cooked meals prepared for us as well which is definitely well appreciated when we are backpacking. Its been great meeting locals and getting to really know the people and the culture from a non-touristy perspective. Even as a couple we havent had trouble finding hosts and are just now switching to hostels to switch it up a little bit. Definitely recommend that everyone try couchsurfing! Hosting people is a lot of fun too  before we left for our trip we hosted a bunch of people in DC and always had good experiences!
