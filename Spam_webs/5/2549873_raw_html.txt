           <div class="block-intro ">
            <div class="title-line">
             <h1>
              Black Forest Lines scenic train
             </h1>
            </div>
            <div class="image ">
             <span class="image-frame">
             </span>
             <span data-cin="asset-5496">
              <img alt="Regional train crossing Black Forest, Germany" height="126" src="http://www.eurail.com/sites/eurail.com/files/styles/asset_image_universal_size_182x126/public/train_crossing_black_forest_germany.jpg?itok=zpbfwrux" width="182"/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="text">
             <p>
              Climbing 650 meters vertically and covering a distance of 150 kilometers, the Black Forest Railway (or Badische Schwarzwaldbahn in German) passes through some of the most spectacular scenery in Europe. Think pine forests, rock faces and dark overhead branches.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Please note: this is a regular train doing a scenic route. Your Eurail pass is valid on this train.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="nav-link" href="#routes">
               Black Forest Linesroutes
              </a>
              <br/>
              <a class="nav-link" href="#facilities">
               Facilities and Services
              </a>
              <br/>
              <a class="nav-link" href="#tips">
               Tips and tricks
              </a>
              <br/>
              <a class="nav-link" href="#additional-information">
               Additional information
              </a>
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="enddiv">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="routes anchor-link">
            routes
           </div>
           <div class="blocks ">
            <div class="block-bg">
             <div class="block-bg">
              <div class="blocks-wrapper">
               <div class="block">
                <div class="block-title">
                 <div class="title">
                  <h2>
                   Black Forest Lines scenic train route
                  </h2>
                 </div>
                 <span class="arrow">
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="content">
                 <div class="text">
                  <p>
                   From the small workday town of Offenburg, the train quickly leaves the overhead trappings of urban life behind and glides into pristine outdoor scenery. Grassy fields stretch away from the track as church spires pierce the skyline and small clusters of villages flash past the windows.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   From Hornberg the scenery changes as deep green pines cover the landscape and the train passes through tunnel after tunnel and runs between dense, dark foliage.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   From Donaueschingen the route runs right down to Singen where it joins the Upper Rhine Railway to continue to the shores of Lake Constance (Konstanz).
                  </p>
                 </div>
                 <div class="block-map">
                  <span data-cin="asset-7331">
                   <img alt="Map with route of Black Forest Line" height="504" src="http://www.eurail.com/sites/eurail.com/files/styles/asset_image_train_full_route_map/public/assets/images/2014/07/black-forest.jpg?itok=DgtrT8VS" width="643"/>
                  </span>
                 </div>
                 <div class="text block-list-lineheight">
                  The Black Forest line operates on the following route:
                  <ul>
                   <li>
                    <span class="emphasis">
                     Offenburg - Konstanz
                    </span>
                   </li>
                  </ul>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="enddiv">

                 <div class="title">
                  <h2>
                   Travel tips
                  </h2>
                 </div>
                 <span class="arrow">
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="content">
                 <div class="text">
                  <p>
                   Donaueschingen makes a good place for a pit stop, about an hour and a half into the route. Youll find plenty of places to tuck into hearty Black Forest dishes such as the Schwarzwalder Shinken (ham) or Kalbsrouladen (veal roulade).
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>

              <div class="blocks-wrapper">
               <div class="block">
                <div class="block-title">
                 <div class="title">
                  <h2>
                   Additional information
                  </h2>
                 </div>
                 <span class="arrow">
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="content">
                 <div class="text">
                  <h3>
                   Conditions for Eurail pass holders
                  </h3>
                  <p>
                   <a href="/eurail-passes">
                    Eurail passes
                   </a>
                   valid for Germany are valid on all Black Forest lines.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                  </p>
                  <h3>
                   Special features
                  </h3>
                  <p>
                   From today's modern trains you view stunning scenery of the genuine Black Forest, shortly interrupted by the many tunnels on this spectacular rail route. Get off at Donaueschingen and visit the birthplace of the Danube, one of Europes longest and mightiest rivers.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   From the vivid and charming city of Freiburg it is only a one-hour train ride through the impressive Hllental (Hell Valley) to the Lakes of Titisee or Schluchsee surrounded by dense and calm forests.
                  </p>
