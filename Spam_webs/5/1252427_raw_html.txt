        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Cherry beef carnitas sandwiches
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-04-23T06:00:15+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          April 23, 2014
         </time>
         
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/cherry-beef-carnitas-sandwiches/#comments">
           12 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14695787" data-link="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/cherry-beef-carnitas-sandwiches/" data-summary="" data-title="Cherry beef carnitas sandwiches">
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          These cherry beef carnitas sandwiches are cooked in cherry juice resulting in a sweet pulled beef sandwich.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Do you guys remember the game pick up sticks? Omgthey were my fave.
        </p>
        <p>
         But lets rewind a sec, I mean, someone put a bunch of colored sticks in a canister, made up directions that basically said drop them onto a surface and then try and pick one up at a time without disturbing any of the others around itand that was considered (good) entertainment. Can you imagine giving that game to a kid today? Theyd be like yo, where my iPad at? Because kids speak gangsta. Obvi.
        </p>
        <p>
         But lets not even discuss that because after the realization that Easter is now like Christmas for 95% of the kids of the people in my facebook feed last weekend, Im seriously disturbed by todays give me more, more, more society and what these spoiled brats are going to turn out like in 20 years when theyre running the world.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Beef-carnitas-sandwiches-made-with-sweet-cherry-juice1.jpg">
          <img alt="Beef carnitas sandwiches made with sweet cherry juice" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10430" height="800" src="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Beef-carnitas-sandwiches-made-with-sweet-cherry-juice1.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Back to pick up sticks
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-8886">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Sweet-cherry-pulled-beef-sandwiches.jpg">
          <img alt="Sweet cherry pulled beef sandwiches" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10432" height="800" src="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Sweet-cherry-pulled-beef-sandwiches.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Back to pick up sticks
        </p>
        <p>
         I havent thought about that game in ages until I picked up this pretty little bunch of chives the other day. As I scattered them around these insanely delicious cherry beef carnitas sandwiches and they fell haphazardly, overlapping each other, I couldnt help but think back to 1988 or so and playing this on the hideous coffee table in our living room. It had wood trim with 4 bevelled glass inlays which I used to my advantage. Any sticks laying on top of the part where the glass and wood met were immediately deemed out of the question, the uneven surface making them more susceptible to movement. But that wasnt something my 2.5 year younger brother could quite grasp yet so while Id pick around the edges for easy ones, hed go for those and Id win, never letting onto my secret.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Cherry-beef-carnitas-sandwiches.jpg">
          <img alt="Cherry beef carnitas sandwiches" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10433" height="848" src="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Cherry-beef-carnitas-sandwiches.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im not gonna lie, cleaning this shoot up, I played the pick up game myself and only got 5 off before disturbing one of the other chives. Apparently, Ive lost my touch over the past 26 years.
        </p>
        <p>
         Might have to eat some more cherry beef carnitas to brush up.
         <br/>
        </p>

         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Gina Matsoukas
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSCategory">
           Recipe type:
           <span itemprop="recipeCategory">
            sandwich
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 - 3 pound beef roast
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            salt &amp; pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 large yellow onion, sliced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 ounces chopped mushrooms (any kind will do, I used shiitake and button)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            12 ounces cherry juice
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup beef broth
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup frozen tart cherries
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            6 pretzel rolls
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            fresh chopped chives for garnish
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 300 degrees.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Heat olive oil in a dutch oven over medium heat.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Season the beef roast generously with salt and pepper on all sides.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Once hot, add the roast to the dutch oven and brown on all sides.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the onions and mushrooms and let cook for a couple of minutes until the onions start to brown.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the cherry juice, beef broth and frozen cherries to the pot and stir.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cover and transfer to the oven to cook for 4 hours, turning the beef halfway through.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Remove from the oven, transfer the beef to a cutting board and pull apart using two forks.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Using an immersion blender, pulse the remaining cherry juice/sauce in the pot a few times to blend. This can also be done by transferring to a blender or food processor.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the shredded beef back to the pot and toss until the juices are fully coating the meat.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Spoon onto pretzel rolls, garnish with cilantro, chives or green onions and serve.
           </li>
          </ol>
