     <span id="print_title">
      <h2>
       Argentine Midfielder Conca to Return to China
      </h2>
     </span>
     <p class="title">
      <span id="print_date">
       2015-01-27
      </span>
      <span style="padding-right:10px;">
       <a href="javascript:printArticle();">
        <img alt="print" src="http://www.visitbeijing.com.cn/upload/cn_visitbj/new/print_btn.gif"/>
       </a>
      </span>
     </p>
     <div class="cont" id="cont">
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        Fluminense's Dario Conca will play 
again in the Chinese Super League following Brazilian club approved a proposal 
from Shanghai SIPG for the midfielder.
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        The Argentine player will receive 
56 million reals for a two-year contract with Shanghai SIPG, who just signed 
former England team mentor Sven-Goran Eriksson.
       </font>
      </p>
      <table align="center" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
       <tbody>
        <tr>
         <td align="center">
          <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
           <tr>
            <td>
             <img alt="Argentine Midfielder Conca to Return to China" src="http://images.visitbeijing.com.cn/20150127/Img215124013.jpg"/>
            </td>
           </tr>
          </table>
          <a href="http://images.visitbeijing.com.cn/20150127/Img215124013.jpg">
          </a>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </tbody>
      </table>
      <br/>
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        Although the final contract hasn't 
been signed by the two sides, Brazilian media believed that there's no hurdle 
within Conca's transfer after Fluminense accepted the actual proposal. Unimed, 
owns the actual economic rights regarding Conca, has already authorized the 
transfer.
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        Conca is well-known in China from a 
successful two-and-a-half years spell with Guangzhou Evergrande that ended in 
December 2013.
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        He came back to his former club 
Fluminense in January 2014 and scored 16 goals in 59 matches.
       </font>
      </p>
