          <h3 class="entry-header">
           Ann McGee Kurz
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             <a href="http://curiouscook.typepad.com/.a/6a0133f5708090970b014e5ff5b3aa970c-pi">
              <img alt="Ann_pic" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a0133f5708090970b014e5ff5b3aa970c" src="http://curiouscook.typepad.com/.a/6a0133f5708090970b014e5ff5b3aa970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Ann_pic"/>

            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 10pt;">
              A year ago today, on November 7, 2004, my sister Ann died from the complications of multiple sclerosis.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 10pt;">
              Ann created all of the hundred-odd line drawings that illustrated the first edition of
              <em>
               On Food &amp; Cooking
              </em>
              . She was a talented self-taught artist, a patient and exacting collaborator, and much more: a scholar of botany and forestry, an enthusiast of the natural world and Indian food and folk music, a loving sister and daughter and friend and wife. She endured the eighteen years of her illness with inspiring good humor and courage. I and all who knew her miss Ann very much, and remember her with joy.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             <span style="font-size: 10pt;">
              - Harold McGee
             </span>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             <span style="font-size: 10pt;">
              November 7, 2005
             </span>
