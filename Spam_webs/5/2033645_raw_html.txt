I want to share what Isaw and thought from aninterviewer&#8217;s point of view, to help you. (&lt;em&gt;note: The examples I give are composites and not real.)&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;These tips are from new grad RN interviews, but can be used for all interviews.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;&lt;span style="color: #808080;"&gt;What they brought&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;h4&gt;Resumes&lt;/h4&gt;&lt;p&gt;Some applicants brought one page resumes (good), most on heavy weight paper (nice). Nice, creative touch!&lt;/p&gt; &lt;figure id="attachment_2420" style="width: 348px;" class="wp-caption alignleft"&gt;&lt;a href="http://i0.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/dreamstime_m_41597043.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="wp-image-2420 " src="http://i0.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/dreamstime_m_41597043.jpg?resize=348%2C232" alt="nursing interview, interviewer, nervous job interview" data-recalc-dims="1" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;figcaption class="wp-caption-text"&gt;Try to relax. Remember you were selected over others for this interview!&lt;/figcaption&gt;&lt;/figure&gt;&lt;p&gt;More than oneapplicant did not bring enough resumes for everyone. Bringing your resume to an interview is not required, as we already have a copy in yourmaster application packet, but if you choose to bring your resume, make sure you have plenty ofcopies.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Most used a &#8220;&lt;span style="text-decoration: underline;"&gt;Personal Summary&lt;/span&gt;&#8221; (better) instead of an &#8220;&lt;span style="text-decoration: underline;"&gt;Objectives&lt;/span&gt;&#8221; statement.Here are two examples:&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;&nbsp;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Best Personal Summary:&lt;/p&gt;&lt;blockquote&gt;&lt;p&gt;Seeking challenging new graduateposition in acute health care.&#8221;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/blockquote&gt;&lt;p&gt;Worst Personal Summary:&lt;/p&gt;&lt;blockquote&gt;&lt;p&gt;To obtain a position as a Registered Nurse with opportunities for professional development while providing excellent healthcare and customer service utilizing clinical skills, critical thinking, and evidence-based practice in conjunction with management, leadership, and organizational skills from previous professional experience.&#8221;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/blockquote&gt;&lt;p&gt;Did your eyes glaze over a bit on the second one? Mine did.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;figure id="attachment_2455" style="width: 200px;" class="wp-caption alignleft"&gt;&lt;a href="http://i0.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/iStock_000015042598Medium.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="wp-image-2455 size-medium" src="http://i0.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/iStock_000015042598Medium.jpg?resize=200%2C300" alt="iStock_000015042598Medium" data-recalc-dims="1" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;figcaption class="wp-caption-text"&gt;Oh, dear. He misspelled the name of our hospital in his Resume&lt;/figcaption&gt;&lt;/figure&gt;&lt;p&gt;Few of my fellow interviewers actually read any of resume handouts during the interview. At most theygot a five or six second glance.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;But even though a resume handed outduring an interview may not count &lt;em&gt;for&lt;/em&gt; you, it can still count &lt;em&gt;against&lt;/em&gt; you. I actually saw one with a glaring typo, and another with lengthy gaps, and conflicting dates in their employment history. See my poston &lt;a title="Why your Resume Should NOT Be All About You" href="http://nursecode.com/2014/09/resume/"&gt;Resumes&lt;/a&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h4&gt;Reference Letters&lt;/h4&gt;&lt;p&gt;Personally, I read referenceletters carefully, for what is said, and for what is not said. Igive extra weight to those written by Clinical Instructors. I find Clinical Instructors to becredible. They&#8217;ve had ample opportunity to see you under stress, and they&#8217;ve watchedyou interact with classmates and patients.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;&lt;span style="color: #808080;"&gt;First Impression&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h3&gt; &lt;figure id="attachment_2423" style="width: 420px;" class="wp-caption alignright"&gt;&lt;a href="http://i2.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/dreamstimemedium_13211012.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="wp-image-2423" src="http://i2.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/dreamstimemedium_13211012.jpg?resize=420%2C279" alt="???????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????" data-recalc-dims="1" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;figcaption class="wp-caption-text"&gt;First impressions can seal the deal&lt;/figcaption&gt;&lt;/figure&gt;&lt;p&gt;From the moment youenter the room, youareunder constant scrutiny.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;The first 90seconds are critical. Quick judgements are made about you in that time, including &#8220;Will you fit in here?&#8221;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Intwo cases, my inner voicesaid &#8220;Hire this person&#8221; &lt;em&gt;before they even sat down.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;a title="The Secret that Nurse Managers Look For In An Interview" href="http://nursecode.com/2014/10/nail-job-interview/"&gt;Interview presence&lt;/a&gt;can be learned. It&#8217;s how others readyour personal energy, vibe, aura, whatever you want to call it.First impressions are a function of facial expression, body language, bearing, appearance, and confidence.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;blockquote&gt;&lt;p&gt;Your body is telling us allabout you. Before your voicespeaks.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/blockquote&gt;&lt;p&gt;A couple of people whoprojected warmth the instantthey walked in the room scored bonuspoints. After all, you are going to be working with patients, and we want to know you are kind. Project openness and warmth.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Be humble. Humble butconfident. Visualize yourself getting the job as a mental warm-up activity, but don&#8217;t be cocky. It&#8217;s more than what you say, it&#8217;s how you say it. One candidate seemed to communicate that we would be lucky to hire her and of course we were going to because of her lofty academic goals and achievements. Guess what? No one likes cocky.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Be open. Onecandidate held herself so tight and closed that I have no idea who she really is. It would be a big risk to hire her and put her out on floor, right? She could be wonderful, or she could end up being a &lt;a title="Is there a Queen Bee on Your Nursing Unit?" href="http://nursecode.com/2014/09/the-queen-bee-dont-get-stung/"&gt;Queen Bee&lt;/a&gt;, or worse.We needto know who we&#8217;re getting. You need to tell us who that is.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Be calm. Pre-game nerves? Nerves are natural, but remember, we already like you. We picked you. You&#8217;re here because you have potential, we&#8217;ve seen it, and now all you have to do is bring it home. (&lt;em&gt;Being nervous is not held against you. We know it&#8217;s natural, friend!&lt;/em&gt;) Advice? Practice relaxing techniques beforehand.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;&lt;span style="color: #808080;"&gt;Be Savvy but don&#8217;t gush&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;Show that you are savvy enough to have done your research about ourorganization and mission statement, yes, but be brief. Don&#8217;t belaboreach and every service line, new building, etc. You will lookdesperate, and it will seemcontrived.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;We don&#8217;t need you to tell us that we are Joint Commission Accredited and that you are amazed bythat accomplishment.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;But- if you are familiar with our culture, and you can speak to it, illustrating howyou are a good fit, that&#8217;s great. Really great. As long as you&#8217;re genuine.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;&lt;span style="color: #808080;"&gt;What do we really want?&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;Competent RNs with good attitude who are loyal.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;figure id="attachment_2554" style="width: 255px;" class="wp-caption alignright"&gt;&lt;a href="http://i0.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_1349.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="wp-image-2554 " src="http://i0.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_1349.jpg?resize=255%2C243" alt="nervous, interview" data-recalc-dims="1" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;figcaption class="wp-caption-text"&gt;Try not to be nervous! We already like you, we asked you here!&lt;/figcaption&gt;&lt;/figure&gt;&lt;p&gt;We want to know you. Welove hearing a bit about outside interests, such as the candidate who is also a musician, and another who interprets for the deaf at his church.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Show us some personality. Don&#8217;t be timid. Remember that &lt;em&gt;stories will be remembered&lt;/em&gt;, so talk in stories.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Instead of saying &#8220;I excel at customer service&#8221;, say&lt;/p&gt;&lt;blockquote&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;One day in clinical I had a Spanish-speaking patient. He was nervous and waiting for his daughter. It was the end of our shift, and time for post-clinical conference, but I just knewit wasn&#8217;t a good time to leave him. I asked my instructor if it would be OK if I stayed over long enough to continue translating for him, until his daughter arrived. I&#8217;mfluent in Spanish.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;She said it would be alright, and I stayed at hisbedside until his daughter came. It turned out he was embarrassed to ask for a urinal and I was able to get one for him, and provide privacy. I really like when I have a chance to help someone like that. I&#8217;m the one who feels blessed.&#8221;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/blockquote&gt; &lt;figure id="attachment_2424" style="width: 380px;" class="wp-caption alignleft"&gt;&lt;a href="http://i2.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/dreamstimemedium_19345625.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="wp-image-2424" src="http://i2.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/dreamstimemedium_19345625.jpg?resize=380%2C253" alt="????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????" data-recalc-dims="1" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;figcaption class="wp-caption-text"&gt;Here&#8217;s a secret: We want you to succeed!&lt;/figcaption&gt;&lt;/figure&gt;&lt;p&gt;If you liveout of area, you have an extra challenge. You have to reasonably convince us that you are not going to leave in two years and go back to Location More Desirable. If you say that you have family or a love interest in our area(and we believe you), that helps.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;&lt;span style="color: #808080;"&gt;We don&#8217;t want to bedistracted by your appearance&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;What I recall most about one applicant is her mega thick upper lid eyeliner, but not much of what she said. While she talked, I found myself wondering why she found it attractive, how did she put it on, what kind of brush did she use to get it that thick, did it take two strokes or three, why was it an ashy light brown color and not black?&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Anothervery attractive applicant with lovelyhair and makeup wore a white, shirtwaist, button-down blouse. The blouse looked casual, as if it had been worn and washed many times, it was neither sparkling whitenor crisp, and she had blouse gaposis at right about the V1electrodeplacement level. Ooops. Test your outfit for wardrobe malfunctions ahead of time by standing, walking, and sitting in it.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;figure id="attachment_2559" style="width: 185px;" class="wp-caption alignright"&gt;&lt;a href="http://i1.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_2958.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="wp-image-2559 " src="http://i1.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_2958.jpg?resize=185%2C205" alt="IMG_2958" data-recalc-dims="1" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;figcaption class="wp-caption-text"&gt;Don&#8217;t let make-up, hair or clothing be a distraction&lt;/figcaption&gt;&lt;/figure&gt;&lt;p&gt;Nicely styled hair and light makeup help women look their best. Overdress just alittle bit. Being overlyoverdressed shows you do not know the culture.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;One manwore a suit that lookedtoo small, with a tie that waschoking him. Itwas distracting because he looked uncomfortable. At the end of the day, it was regrettable that the tight suit was my strongest memory of him.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;We haven&#8217;t even gotten to the interview itself! Keep reading the following posts to find out what we asked, and how they answered.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;&lt;span style="color: #808080;"&gt;How to Answer Common Interview Questions and Behavioral Interview Questions&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;ul&gt;&lt;li&gt;There areclassic interview questions you mustprepare for. Interview Question" href="http://nursecode.com/2014/11/answer-hire-interview-question/"&gt;Why should we hire you?&lt;/a&gt;&#8221; question.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Mostly we focused on behavioral questions, and listened carefully to those responses. Read how at &#8220;&lt;a title="How to Answer Behavioral Interview Questions" href="http://nursecode.com/2014/11/answer-behavioral-interview-questions/"&gt;Behavioral Interview Questions.&lt;/a&gt;&#8220;&lt;/li&gt;&lt;/ul&gt;&lt;h3&gt;Who stood out&lt;/h3&gt; &lt;figure id="attachment_2557" style="width: 300px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter"&gt;&lt;a href="http://i0.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_0926.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="wp-image-2557 size-medium" src="http://i0.wp.com/nursecode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_0926.jpg?resize=300%2C257" alt="IMG_0926" data-recalc-dims="1" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;figcaption class="wp-caption-text"&gt;Be genuine&lt;/figcaption&gt;&lt;/figure&gt;&lt;p&gt;What makes you stand out- a firm handshake. Shaking each interviewer&#8217;s hand. Genuineness. Eye contact, projecting a humble confidence. Personality. Stories and examples! Making us laugh.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;It was exciting because we found some &lt;a title="I0 no 11 Reasons I Love New Nurses" href="http://nursecode.com/2014/09/i0-no-11-reasons-i-love-new-nurses/"&gt;wonderful new grad RNs&lt;/a&gt;to join our work family.
