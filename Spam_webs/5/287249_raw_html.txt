     <div class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Daffy's No. 1 fan" src="/content/dam/images/1/1/2/i/o/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.18rpx.png/1292042600477.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Daffy's No. 1 fan
        <cite>
         <i>
          Photo: Reuters
         </i>
        </cite>
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       IT WAS exciting to learn this week that the Dear Leader of Australias thespian community, Geoffrey Rush, shares a passion with the Dear Leader of the Democratic Peoples Republic of Korea, Kim Jong-Il. Both are obsessed with Daffy Duck.
      </p>
      <p>
       Just when the world is being inherited by a generation who think
       <em>
        The Simpsons
       </em>
       are the pinnacle of animated psychoanalysis, we get a useful reminder of the contribution of Looney Tunes to the making of the modern mind.
      </p>
      <p>
       Rush (who fondled a Daffy symbol for luck on the night he won the Oscar) said his recent performance onstage in
       <em>
        The Diary of a Madman
       </em>
       had been inspired by Daffys arrogance and assertiveness and ability to have his beak smashed round his head  but still feel wronged by the world and come back with renewed energy. That sounds scarily like a description of Kim Jong-Il.
      </p>
      <div class="cT-imagePortrait">
       <img alt="Daffy's No. 2 fan" itemprop="image" src="/content/dam/images/e/5/9/n/image.related.articleLeadNarrow.300x0.18rpx.png/1292042600477.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Daffy's No. 2 fan
        <cite>
         <i>
          Photo: David Crosling
         </i>
        </cite>
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       Kims recent behaviour can only be explained by Daffyphilia. When he became leader of North Korea in 1994, we learned that Kim had a collection of 20,000 videos, including several hundred adventures of the mallard imaginaire. It struck me at the time that Pyongyang sounded more like a Warner Bros sound effect than the name of a national capital, and that if East Asia were plunged into a nuclear war, we might blame it on years of persecution by Elmer Fudd and Bugs Bunny, representing in Kims mind South Korea and the United States. (In this analogy, China would be Foghorn Leghorn, stirring trouble from the sidelines: You not gonna let them talk to you lahk that, are ya, boy?)
      </p>
      <div class="ad adAlignRight" id="adspot-300x250-pos3" style="display:none;">
       <small>
        Advertisement
       </small>
       <script>
        titan.requestAd.push("adspot-300x250-pos3");
       </script>
      </div>
      <p>
       When the President of South Korea makes peace overtures, you can imagine Kim wanting to reply Thankth for the thour perthimmonth, buthter (the quintessence of sarcasm, for Daffy). When North Korean troops fire missiles at the South, Kim is no doubt chortling Thatll fixth hith little red wagon. And when Hillary Clinton issues a reprimand, Kim can just glare and spit Youre dethpicable.
      </p>
      <p>
       Looney Tunes left a powerful legacy because the writers at Warner Bros created characters we could identify with, while Disney just did slapstick animals.
      </p>
      <p>
       Daffy was born in 1937 in a short called
       <em>
        Porkys Duck Hunt
       </em>
       . On his 50th birthday,
       <em>
        The New York Times
       </em>
       asked a psychoanalyst to describe his personality, and got this diagnosis: He started as a simple manic depressive, but developed over the years into a fully-fledged paranoid schizophrenic with delusions of grandeur.
      </p>
      <p>
       The writer who contributed most to that development was Chuck Jones, who gave this analysis in
       <em>
        Chuck Amuck: The Life and Times of an Animated Cartoonist
       </em>
       : Elmer never knows whats going on; Bugs always knows whats going on and is in control of events; Daffy is bright enough to understand how to be in control, but never quite makes it. Bugs stands back from a situation, analyses it and makes his move. Daffy becomes emotionally involved, loses his distance, and blows it.
      </p>
      <p>
       Its pleasant to ponder how various Looney Tunes anticipated the personalities of such dear leaders as Rudd, Turnbull, Abbott, Gillard, Katter, OFarrell and Keneally. Go to Comments to tell us who they match in the Warner catalogue.
      </p>
      <p>
