         DIY Valentines Day/Date Night Shirts for your Little One!
        </h1>

         <p>
          Hi!
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Perhaps Im going to the wrong places, but it seems that I always see more, or cuter, clothes and accessories for girls than for boys. Last time we were looking for shoes for Beni, I went to a store that only had ONE pair of boy shoes and 10 different styles of shoes for girls.When I went to Hobby Lobby with the idea of making Beni some Valentines Day shirts, I was surprised by the lack of selection when it came to onesies/toddler shirts and decorating items. There were all kinds of leggings and tutus for girls (and many other things), butthere were only about 5shirts to choose from for boys. However, I love being creative and being limited only challenges me to think outside of the box! I have to thank Kyle for being extra patient with me while we were at Hobby Lobby, thoughhaha.
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          I am so excited to be sharing the onesie and shirt I made for Beni!
          <del>
           Sadly, my little model fell asleep before I finished the project. Tomorrow I will update the post and add pictures of him wearing his new Valentines Day looks.
           <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
          </del>
          <a href="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DIY-valentines-shirts-together.jpg">
           <br/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_641" style="width: 760px">
          <a href="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/gentleman-shirt.jpg">
           <img alt="gentleman shirt" class="wp-image-641 size-large" height="750" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/gentleman-shirt-1024x1024.jpg" width="750"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Look at that pose! What a sweet little model. :)
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_642" style="width: 760px">
          <a href="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/lovebug-shirt.jpg">
           <img alt="lovebug shirt" class="wp-image-642 size-large" height="632" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/lovebug-shirt-1024x863.jpg" width="750"/>
          </a>

           And those eyelashes! I promise I didnt put mascara on him, theyre just that gorgeous! (Hes probably going to hate me for saying things like this, haha.)

         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          The reason I say these are Date Night shirts, as well, is because I made sure they werent Valentines Day specific. They can be worn any day and the black one doubles as a casual or formal shirt. Plus, these styles work with babies, toddlers, and young children!
         </p>
         <p>
          So, I bought a black shirt and used a white onesie we already had. One of the lovely things about DIY projects is that you can totally make them your own! This will work just fine with two red shirts, if that is what you want to use. For the black shirt, I bought a burlap-y bow, a set of wooden buttons that came in circles and flower shapes, and an iron-on quote that says, I love you to the moon and back. For the white onesie, I got black iron-on letters and a cute ladybug iron-on design. Everything, except for the white onesie that we already had, was from Hobby Lobby and totaled a bit over $15. It was all in the same aisle, too!
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="DSC_0086" class="alignleft wp-image-617 size-full" height="2850" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0086-e1423032334880.jpg" width="5115"/>
          <a href="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0090.jpg">
           <img alt="DSC_0090" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-618" height="500" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0090-1024x683.jpg" width="750"/>
          </a>
          The black shirt requires some sewing, but if that is not your thing, dont worry! I dont sew much myself and this was really easy! All you have to do is learn how to sew a button. Ill walk you through the steps, but I would advise you watch a YouTube tutorial (
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MjmUaWkzF-I">
           such as this one
          </a>
          ) if you are a visual learner, as I am.
         </p>
         <p>
           Lets start!
         </p>
         <p>
          Ok, so to begin you will have to thread the needle. Then, pull the thread down to the desired length. As you can see in the picture below, it doesnt not have to be very long. I do advise, though, that you make it longer rather than shorter so you dont have to worry about not having enough thread to secure what you are sewing. I couldve used an extra inch here! Now, cut the thread so that you have the same length on each side of the needle. Tie a knot to unite the ends. I do this by wrapping the thread around my finger, which creates a loop, and then I sneak the tail of the thread into the loop. I tie a knot about 3 times in the same place to make sure it isnt small enough that it will go through the fabric. It may take a little practice to get the knot in the same spot each time, but take your timeit will make it easier.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_620" style="width: 760px">
          <a href="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0092.jpg">
           <img alt="DSC_0092" class="wp-image-620 size-large" height="500" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0092-1024x683.jpg" width="750"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Sorry, I know its a bit blurry!
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Ok, now you are ready to sew! Grab the shirt and see where you want to place your bow. Once you have your spot, insert your needle from the inside of the shirt. That means that the little dangly tail from the thread will be on the inside of the shirt, not the outside. To make sure that your thread isnt visible, poke the needle through the back part of the middle loop on the bow. Since I find this kind of hard to explain, I took a picture! Haha.
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
          Im not sure why you cant see the thread, but I went through the bow where the dotted line is.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="DSC_0106a" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-631" height="455" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0106a-1024x621.jpg" width="750"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Now, push the needle back through the shirt so that it is on the insideand pull it through all the way. Do this about 5 times or until your bow feels secure. If youre worried about where to put the needle, use the middle loop of the bow as a guide. The distance between the first prick and the second should be about the sameor smaller. I make the space smaller since I feel that that secures what I am sewing a bit better. Now that you can see where your bow is on the shirt, you may notice it is a bit to the right or maybe a little crooked. Dont worry!
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
          Since you have to repeat the process, you can use the needle to manipulate the position of the bow.
         </p>
         <p>
          Once you feel that your bow is secure, turn the shirt inside out. Push the needle through the wad of thread that you see on the inside of the shirt. Dont push the needle through the shirt, just through the thread. As soon as you push the needle through, you will notice that the thread has created a loop. Insert the needle through the loop and pull. Do this about 3-5 times. Then, cut the thread, and you are done!
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_623" style="width: 760px">
          <a href="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0109a.jpg">
           <img alt="DSC_0109a" class="wp-image-623 size-large" height="500" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0109a-1024x682.jpg" width="750"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Insert the needle through the thread on the inside of the shirt. If yours look loopier than this, dont worryits fine!
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_622" style="width: 760px">
          <a href="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0110.jpg">
           <img alt="DSC_0110" class="wp-image-622 size-large" height="500" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0110-1024x683.jpg" width="750"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Put the needle through the loop and pull. Uh, oh, Beni approaches!
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The process is the same with the buttons, but easier! Grab a new piece of thread, knot it, sew from the inside to the outside, and let the holes guide you! You can even be creative with the way you sew the buttons. You can make an X, =, ll, a squarewhatever you can think of! Of course, with the buttons that only have two holes you will only be able to make a line, though. I placed four of the circular buttons in a column below the bow to give the shirt a formal look. Then, I put three flower buttons on the top right (imitating a pocket) to give it a romantic feel!The most important part of projects like this one is that you need to have fun! Experiment with the positioning of your items and make it your own.
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          So, once I was done sewing the buttons, I decided to put the quote on the back of this shirt. I wanted to make it a bit cutesy-er. For this part, all you need is an iron!
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
          All of these iron-ons are a bit different, so its important to read the instructions. However, they all needed to be ironed firmly for about 15-25 seconds. Once they cooled off, I had to turn the shirts inside out and iron again. I repeated this process 2-3 times just to make sure they wouldnt peel off. And they came out great! The letters were a bit fragile and I ripped two of thembut that gives the shirt character, right?
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_625" style="width: 760px">
          <a href="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0111.jpg">
           <img alt="DSC_0111" class="wp-image-625 size-large" height="500" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0111-1024x683.jpg" width="750"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           For the quote, I had to peel the sheet off once I had ironed it and let it cool off.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_624" style="width: 760px">
          <a href="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0112a.jpg">
           <img alt="DSC_0112a" class="wp-image-624 size-large" height="500" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DSC_0112a-1024x683.jpg" width="750"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The ladybug required fabric over it for ironing.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Making these two shirts took me about an hour and a half. I took my time, though. It was definitely worth it and it will make our Valentines Day extra special. Kyle thought they looked so nice that he told me to open an Etsy store, haha. I couldnt have done it without his inspiration.
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://bohemian-mama.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Id love to know if you try making these or something similar!
         </p>
         <p>
          See you soon!
         </p>
         <p>
           Raquel
         </p>
