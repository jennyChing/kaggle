              <p align="center">
               <b>
                <font size="3">
                 <strong>
                  The  Five Healthiest Beverages You Can Drink
                  <br/>
                  (Including Two You May Not Have Heard  Of)
                 </strong>
                 <br/>
                 <font style="font-size: 11px;">
                  by
                  <a href="http://www.sixwise.com/">
                   www.SixWise.com
                  </a>
                 </font>
                </font>
               </b>
              </p>
              <p align="center">
              </p>
              <p>
               When  youre feeling parched and need to quench your thirst, the beverage you choose  can make a big difference to your health. And while most of us know that soda  and other sweet drinks are not the healthiest choices around, which beverages  are truly health-promoting remains more of a mystery.
              </p>
              <table align="right" border="0" cellpadding="6" cellspacing="6" width="1">
               <tbody>
                <tr>
                 <td bgcolor="pink" style="border-style: solid; border-color: rgb(153, 153, 153) rgb(51, 51, 51) rgb(51, 51, 51) rgb(153, 153, 153); border-width: 1px;">

                   <img alt="" height="250" src="/images/articles/2009/03/18/5324137.thb.jpg" width="166"/>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Pure water is  essential for your survival, and truly one of the healthiest beverages on the  planet.
                  </p>
                 </td>
                </tr>
               </tbody>
              </table>
              <p>
               Ironically,  while we need fluids for our very survival, continually choosing the wrong ones  may push your health out of balance. So here weve detailed some of the best of  the best when it comes to good-for-you drink choices. With these healthy  beverages you really can do no wrong.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                1.  Water
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Water  is crucial for survival -- it's the base of all your body fluids, like blood  and digestive juices, it helps nutrients from your food get absorbed and be  transported, and it helps eliminate waste. Even
               <a href="/newsletters/06/08/02/are-you-dehydrated-many-people-are-and-dont-even-know-it.htm">
                becoming mildly dehydrated
               </a>
               (when you lose as little as 1 percent to 2 percent of your body weight) can  seriously impact your body's ability to function.
              </p>
              <p>
               How  much water do you need? It's commonly said that you should drink eight eight-ounce  glasses of water a day to stay healthy, but this is really just a rule of  thumb, as so many factors (weather, age, activity level, health) affect how  much water your body needs.
              </p>
              <p>
               In  general, you can prevent dehydration by focusing on staying hydrated throughout  the day. Keep water with you and drink it regularly -- before you get thirsty.  If it's hot outside or you're exercising (or pregnant or breastfeeding), you'll  need even more fluids so drink more water.
              </p>
              <p>
               A  word of caution: not all water is good for you. Tap water can be potentially contaminated with
               <a href="/Newsletters/2008/November/26/Five-Chemicals-That-May-be-Lurking-in-Your-Water.htm">
                chemicals
               </a>
               ,  pesticides   or even
               <a href="/newsletters/06/02/16/pharmaceutical_pollution_what_it_is_and_how_pharmaceutical_pollution_threatens_your_health.htm">
                pharmaceutical  drugs
               </a>
               .  And bottled water, which is regulated by the Food and Drug Administration  (FDA), has weaker regulations than the Environmental Protection Agency requires  for tap water.
              </p>

              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                2. Kefir
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Kefir  is a tart, yogurt-like beverage made from fermented milk that is popular in Russia at  breakfast as well as an after-dinner snack. It has been a staple beverage in Russia since ancient times, and is just  beginning to become popular among health-conscious U.S. consumers.
              </p>
              <p>
               What  makes kefir so healthy is that its full of beneficial bacteria (probiotics),  which produce beneficial enzymes, aid digestion and promote healthy flora in  your digestive tract. This nourishing drink is considered by many to boost the  immune system, help fight disease and improve overall health (it is even  customary for patients in Russian hospitals to receive kefir).
              </p>
              <p>
               It's  simple to make kefir at home using milk and kefir starter granules you can find  online or in health food stores. You simply heat the milk slightly, add the  granules, then let it sit, covered, for about a day.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                3. Kvass
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Kvass  is another fermented beverage from Russia, this one made from stale  dark, sourdough rye bread. Kvass has been enjoyed in Russia  for at least 1,000 years, yet even today if you visit Russia during  the summer months you will see people lining up to get a cool glass of this  tangy beverage from a street vendor.
              </p>
              <p>
               Kvass  in traditional form, if you can find it, is an extremely healthy beverage. It  is rich in B vitamins, thought to relieve intestinal problems and hangovers.  But more importantly, like kefir kvass is fermented, which means it contains  beneficial bacteria, or probiotics, that are regarded as a digestive aid, and  that may destroy disease-causing microbes in your intestine.
              </p>

                  </p>

               </a>

Many people sip it religiously everyday  in the hopes that it will make them healthier. Here is just a short list of  some of the conditions green tea is supposed to help:
              </p>

                <p>

                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <p>

                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <p>
                 High       blood sugar levels
                </p>
               </li>

              <p>
               Many  of the health benefits are attributed to the fact that green tea is a rich  source of catechin polyphenols, namely epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), which  is a potent antioxidant.
               <a href="/Newsletters/2009/February/11/Chai-Tea-A-Quick-History.htm">
                Other  types of tea
               </a>
               ,  such as black tea, may also have healthy antioxidant benefits, so feel free to  expand your tea repertoire beyond green tea if youre so inclined.
              </p>

              <p>
               <strong>
                5.  High-Antioxidant 100% Fruit Juice
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Fruit  juice is high in sugar and because of this
               <a href="/newsletters/08/04/09/are-fruit-juices-good-for-you-or-not-the-opposing-views.htm">
                whether or not its truly healthy is debatable
               </a>
               .  And though most experts will unanimously agree that eating the whole fruit is  always better than drinking fruit juice, certain types of juice do have their  benefits.
              </p>
              <p>
               A  University of California, Los Angeles, study ranked 10 beverages  according to their levels of disease-fighting antioxidants, and concluded that  the 10 best were mostly fruit juices. The most antioxidant-rich beverages they  found were:
              </p>
              <ol>
               <li>
                Pomegranate juice
               </li>
               <li>
                Red wine
               </li>
               <li>
                Concord grape juice
               </li>
               <li>
                Blueberry juice
               </li>
               <li>
                Black cherry juice
               </li>
               <li>
                Aa juice
               </li>
               <li>
                Cranberry juice
               </li>
               <li>
                Orange juice
               </li>
               <li>
                Tea
               </li>
               <li>
                Apple juice
               </li>
              </ol>
              <p>
               If  you do choose to drink juice, doing so in moderation is recommended. Further,  there are major differences in juice quality out there. Here's what to look  for, and avoid, when choosing a fruit juice for yourself or your child:
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Choose 100-percent fruit juice only.
               </li>
              </ul>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Avoid "fruit drinks," "fruit-juice  cocktails," "fruit beverages," and any juices that have added  sugars, flavors or colors.
               </li>
              </ul>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Choose dark-colored fruit juices (such as grape, cranberry,  pomegranate, and blueberry), as they're rich in antioxidants.
               </li>
              </ul>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Look for juice that has sediment on the bottom of the jar.  This means the fruit skins, which are very nutritious, were used in the  processing (and be sure to shake the bottle up before your pour a glass).
               </li>
              </ul>
              <ul>
               <li>
                If you love fruit juice but want to limit your calories, mix  half a serving with sparkling water.
               </li>
