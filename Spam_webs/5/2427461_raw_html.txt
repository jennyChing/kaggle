          <p>
           When we start our
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freelancer" rel="dofollow" target="_blank">
            freelancing
           </a>
           career we accept every client who comes our way. We start working with them and enjoy it for first few days. But slowly we start feeling the real pain of
           <a href="http://www.inspireyourway.com/earn-by-selling-books-online/">
            freelancing
           </a>
           when our clients startsetting weird &amp; impossible to achieve goals.
          </p>
          <p>
           Not all clients are a pain in the a**, in fact, most clients I have worked with are really great and helpful. But sometimes, due to our misfortune, we tend to come across
           <a href="http://www.hongkiat.com/blog/clients-from-hell/" rel="dofollow" target="_blank">
            evil clients
           </a>
           . Working with these clients can render heavy mental agony, emotional as well as physical stress. This situation may stop you from enjoying your freelancing career. So these clients MUST go. You need to fire them.
          </p>
