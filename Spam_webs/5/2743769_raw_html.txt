       <div class="body">
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/1GA7jHhNmiw" width="640">
        </iframe>
        <p>
         Cartridge razors are impressive, but they can be tough on sensitive skin and arent all that wallet-friendly. Straight razors look damn cool, but are difficult (not to mention terrifying) to use, and require a fair bit of upkeep. The perfect middle ground?
         <strong>
          Safety razors
         </strong>
         . Heres how, and why, to use one.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <h4>
         Advantages
        </h4>
        <ul>
         <li>
          The single razor blade reduces your beard more gradually than a five blade cartridge would. Youll still get a smooth face, but without irritation or ingrown hairs from cutting too closely.
         </li>
         <li>
          Itll glide smoothly across your skin, without that tugging on your whiskers that can happen with a cartridge razor.
         </li>
         <li>
          The replacement blades are dirt cheap, so you save cash.
         </li>
         <li>
          Lets face it: a safety razor is just plain cool.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <br/>
        <h4>
         How to Load
        </h4>
        <p>
         There are a three different types, but theyre all easy to load.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Three piece:
          </strong>
          If you got
          <a href="/box/the-barber" target="_blank">
           The Barber
          </a>
          , this is the type of razor youll be using. Unscrew the handle and take the safety bar and top piece apart. Load a blade into the top piece, layer on the safety bar, and screw the handle back on.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Two piece:
          </strong>
          Loosen the knob at the bottom of the handle to detach the top of the razor. Load a blade, reinsert the top, and tighten the knob on the handle.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           One piece:
          </strong>
          Twist the handle to open the flaps at the top of the razor. Load a blade and then twist the handle the opposite way to close up the flaps.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <br/>
        <h4>
         Prep
        </h4>
        <p>
         Before you shave, you want to
         <strong>
          steam your face
         </strong>
         to loosen the whiskers and open up your pores. The best way to do that is by shaving while youre taking a hot shower. If you shave outside of the shower, you can soak a towel in hot water and hold it against your face for a minute for the same effect.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <h4>
         Lather
        </h4>
        <p>
         If youve got one,
         <a href="/the-post/how-to-use-a-shave-brush" target="_blank">
          use a
          <strong>
           shave brush
          </strong>
         </a>
         . Itll generate a richer, thicker lather with whatever shave cream you prefer, while exfoliating the skin.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <h4>
         Shaving
        </h4>
        <p>
         Ok, heres the important part. Finding the right angle and pressure for your skin may take a bit of trial and error, since everyones different. But its dead simple once youve gotten the hang of things, so dont stress.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Aim for a 30-35 degree angle between the razor and your skin.
         </li>
         <li>
          Use a light touch, letting the weight of the safety razor do the work. You dont need to apply much, if any, pressure.
         </li>
         <li>
          Use lots of small strokes, rather than dragging the razor across a large section.
         </li>
         <li>
          Always shave with the grain on your first pass.
         </li>
         <li>
          If your skin isnt too sensitive and you want an extra close shave, you can reapply lather and go for round 2. Shave across or against the grain this time.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <br/>
        <h4>
         Post shave
        </h4>
        <p>
         Soothe your skin by wiping down your face with a warm towel. Then get it cold and hold it against your skin to
         <strong>
          snap the pores closed
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         For an old-school finishing touch, you can use an
         <strong>
          alum block
         </strong>
         . Run it under warm water, then rub it onto your face to soothe, tone, and clear your pores. Itll also stop any nicks from bleeding  but its gonna sting a little bit.
        </p>
