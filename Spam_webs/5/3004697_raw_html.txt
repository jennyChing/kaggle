            <p data-textannotation-id="b1649fd44c687409f02d54834ad34328">

            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="556ece85fb8cee590ba3b3bf29d9e24d">

            <h2 data-textannotation-id="b7c7a517b78445202cba2a6d0ad094f6">
             What does this have to do with a removing mugshots from Google?
            </h2>
            <p data-textannotation-id="76cf39336cfec9e8095dccbfee3d7349">
             If a modern business man were to find his mugshot online, it would be cause for alarm. For Gates, however, the issue doesn't seem important. In fact, he seems so comfortable with his mugshot that he included it in a
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_Gates" target="_blank">
              commercial
             </a>
             made with the comedian Jerry Seinfeld in 2008. If Gates were uncomfortable with the image, he certainly wouldn't hold the photo up in front of billions of viewers.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="fc880bfa21dee62e5aca48fba895d9d2">
             So why isn't the mugshot damaging? The answer lies in the power of keywords. Most people who type in the words "Bill Gates" into their search engines will be rewarded with hundreds of thousands of articles about his business prowess and his good deeds. The mugshot is visible, but its effect is blunted. There's so much information out there about the good that Gates has done that the wee article about his mugshot doesn't seem important. In fact, it's relatively hard to find a midst all of the glowing articles about the wonders of Gates.
            </p>
            <h2 data-textannotation-id="a800fb3220df4357b05f060753fc7636">
             Getting Rid of a Mugshot on Search Engine Results...Bill Gates Style
            </h2>
            <p data-textannotation-id="d5d6bef119a346b1ebd131ad1057947d">
             If you've been arrested, following in the footsteps of Gates might be an excellent idea. If you can quickly amass dozens of press releases, blog entries, photos and videos about the other things you've done, you might bump your mugshot to the bottom of search results, making it harder to find. Unfortunately, you'll have programmers working against you, copying your mugshot and putting it on other mugshot websites.
