                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://raerae0889.blogspot.com/2014/02/ive-realized-in-certain-moments-i-can.html">
                      Captured Moments
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8835301750561903788" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-J7gzi5si3x0/Uw_6u8DXD0I/AAAAAAAAAGc/yJ2EqIxQDkw/s1600/IMG_20140227_1.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-J7gzi5si3x0/Uw_6u8DXD0I/AAAAAAAAAGc/yJ2EqIxQDkw/s1600/IMG_20140227_1.png" width="240"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I've realized in certain moments I can be sublimely, and uncharacteristically happy. It's not often, if ever, that these moments are captured. I was going through my photos from yesterday and I came across one. A moment captured in time when everything was right, everything was colorful, and everything was calm. Nothing was dark or grey, and everything bad, every little horrible thing disappeared for a while, and it was beautiful.
                     <br/>
                     I've come to terms with the fact that happiness is not a destination, but a journey. I have struggled with the idea that in life, to wish to be happy all the time is impossible. That's not what it's about. It's about these moments. The ones that make life worth living. The ones you spend with the people you love. The ones where you forget your need to be happy and you just are.

                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      Walking up to the time clock I saw
her waiting for me. She didnt have her usual Its my best friend! smile on
her face, she looked worried, she looked sad. I couldnt help but think I was
in trouble for something, though, I couldnt for the life of me think what it
could be and why they would make her talk to me about it.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      Hey, can
you come to the office with me for a second? She asked me quietly.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      Am I in
trouble? I asked thinking full well I knew the answer to that question. What
else could it be?
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      No. She
nervously chuckled
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      But I hesitated. I couldnt fathom
any other reason and per my horrific personality, I was already defensive.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      I promise youre not in trouble, just come with me. She added as she led me down
the hall towards the office.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      So I followed her going over and
over in my head what I could have done. As we rounded the corner to the office
I looked up and saw my mother standing against the desk. Confusion swept over
me. What was she doing here? Was this a surprise? Was she visiting me for my
lunch? Then as her face crinkled into the all too familiar expression when she
cried, it finally became clear. Time slowed down then, as she reached for me,
her eyes tired with woe. But I couldnt believe it, I wouldnt believe it.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      Its
grandpa. The words dripped out of her mouth slowly, and as I stood there in
disbelief tears forming andthreatening to spill, all I could think to do was run.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      No...No
were the only words that came out as I backed away from the awfulness that was
surrounding theroom, but before I could turn my body to flee, I felt an arm
on my back pushing me away from the door and towards the outstretched arms of
my mother. She was strong but I would be stronger, but before I could back up and
escape the walls that seemed to be closing in around me my mother grabbed hold
of me and shook her head Hes not dead. He had a heart attack and we dont
know how long hes going to last. We have to go to the hospital now ok? She
was weeping now and as she held me my sobs began. I could feel a slow familiar
pat on my back as I wept in my mothers arms. I couldnt control it, this
couldnt be happening.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      Finally I
pulled my face off of her sweater and realized my makeup had left a stain on
her shoulder.  Ive gotten makeup all over you, Im so sorry. I said as I tried
to calm myself down.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      She looked
down at the stain and hugged me Thats alright, thats alright She cooed. 
Come on weve already talked to the manager were leaving right now to go to the
hospital.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      I turned and looked at my best friend whose
eyes were red and puffy full of yearning to do something, anything to help. I knew how badly she wanted to fix this, to make me smile, to take away this moment, but she couldn't and it was killing her. I nodded my head attempting to reassure her that we would be ok and took my mothers hand as we made the slow walk
out of the store. In that endless journey to the door I clung to moms side. I
became a little girl again who was toldher favorite person in the world might die tonight.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      As we made
the drive to the hospital I looked out towards the sky and begged god, to ask
my grandpa to wait. I begged him to make sure my mother and I got the chance to
tell him goodbye. I knew that I didnt deserve that favor, after all I had
abandoned him out of weakness, but he deserved it, he deserved to hear
everything I wanted to say to him before he left us. Before he left me.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>

                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: .5in; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      She slowly turned her chair to face me and I could see her
tear stained cheeks but her eyes were vacant, a haze over them, like she wasnt
really there.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: .5in; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      Im just having a really hard time right now. I dont want
to burden you she finally told me.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: .5in; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      Talk to me mom, whats going on? I responded, thinking I
knew where this was going, her and Q were in a fight and she was just upset
about it but they would be talking tomorrow.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: .5in; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      Im so she began to sob
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: .5in; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      Im so sad, I have no friends, I have no one to talk to, and
I know I dont ever say it but you know that I am in love with that man and he
treats me like shit. She spat out through sobs.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: .5in; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      I know youre in love with him, mom but you guys fight all
the time itll be ok I tried to resolve for her
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: .5in; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      No, no this is different, he told me I am unable to behappy.
Who says that to someone? she put her face in her hands and sobbed quietly
while I tried to think of
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      something,
anything to say to comfort her.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      I found
nothing.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: .5in; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      I just feel so alone, like if I wasnt there it wouldnt
make one bit of difference, I dont even want to live anymore. Some days I just
want to end it and be done with it and stop feeling so fucking sad all the
time She lifted her head and finished.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: .5in; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      It felt like being in an open field with no one around for
miles no houses in the distance or cars driving by on the scenic route, no one
but the wind and grass that was caressing your legs, to hear you scream.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      I watched the words come out of her mouth and
come straight for me like daggers aimed right where I was most vulnerable. They
came for the place they could break and there was nowhere to run, and nothing
to do but sit there and watch them tear into me.
                      <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
                      </span>
                      With this not being the first time I had ever
heard her say something like this I knew to well what it felt like, but was
mentally unprepared to feel it again 8 years later. A fifteen year old girl
does not need to hear her mother tell her she wants to kill herself, and
despite the years and experience it does not get any easier when a 23 year old
hears her mother tell her the same thing. I suddenly
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      became
a little girl again crying because someone told her that some day her mother
was going to leave her. All my life it had been nothing but her and me against
the world, how could the thought ever cross her mind to leave me alone. Iwas
on such a treacherous path stuck at a fork. I needed her more than I ever had
and here she was telling me that her life was not worth sticking around for. So
through tears and the remains of hope I had left in me I said the only thing
that would come out
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      Mom,
please you cant leave me.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      That was all I had, despite every
single thought floating around in my head that perhaps I shouldve said. That
she was strong, that she was beautiful, that I would cease to exist without
her. Those six words were the only ones that would come out and even though
there were a million other things to say and even though they were things that
she needed to hear I kept my mouth shut and just stared at her, this broken
woman who couldnt find a reason to stay alive. After a few minutes of just the
two of us staring, my last statement floating around looking for a place to
rest, finally came to land in her eyes and they began to clear from the fog
that had taken residence there. She then looked at the floor shook her head and
replied,
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      I know
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-justify: inter-ideograph;">
                      <span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">
                      </span>
                      It was like the wind was kicked out
of her then, like every single drop of energy she had was gone,I wont leave you, thank god for
you. She said as she rose from her chair and went to her bedroom, leaving me
with the faint scent of her cigarette and reassurance that she wouldnt go
through with it at least not tonight.
                     </div>

                      <o:p>

                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      The room was dark, a vague light coming in from the lamppost
in the courtyard. Not enough to see anything, which I was glad for. I welcomed
the darkness. It meant I couldnt see the water spots on the ceiling, or the
tacky 70s wallpaper peeling of on the corners. The bedside desk was no longer
covered in scratches and graffiti and I couldnt see the reflection of the girl
I was about to become in the foggy dirtied mirror. It didnt really matter
though, the air inside the cheap motel room reminded me of where I was, it was
heavy and smelled old, the covers on the bed scratchy and stiff. But as he
lowered himself on top of me all of those things began to disappear and it was
just him softly kissing my neck, telling me how beautiful I am, how much he
wanted me, and making his way to my lips he kissed me deeply. He smelled
faintly of cologne from early in the day and cigarettes, and tasted of jack Daniels.
But he wanted me, and that in spite of everything made up my mind. For all the
wrong reasons when he finally asked do you want to go all the way?

                      I whispered yes

                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      He positioned himself on top of me and slowly eased his way
inside, destroying every last bit of innocence I had been saving. The feeling
was so unbearable it took everything inside me not to cry. He kept going slowly
in and out and all I wanted was to scream Stop! It hurts, Stop! but I
didnt, I lied there taking every bit of it praying for the end. When it was
finally over and he was lying, all his weight, on top of me panting like an
overly stimulated dog I knew that this would never be a happy memory. I pushed
him off and stumbled in the dark to find my clothes and the bathroom. I turned
on the light and put on my pants and shirt avoiding at all costs my reflection.
I turned on the faucet and washed the ruined makeup of my face and accidentally
glanced up and locked eyes with the girl in the mirror. She disgusted me. I
looked at myself for what I was, a used up piece of trash and I hated myself. I
had given my virginity to a boy I hardly knew because in his drunken state he
had said he wanted to. I looked into the eyes of a girl who took it because she
didnt think anyone else would ever want to. As the tears welled up I snapped
off the light and headed back to bed unwilling to face my demons any longer.

                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      He
was lying there waiting for me as I crawled back into bed. He had turned the
radio on while I was gone and put his arm around me and pulled me close as he
began singing along to hey there Delilah and kissing the top of my head. A
song that never stopped turning my stomach sour when it came on the radio after
that. But as we were lying there I was able to convince myself that this was
not that bad, here he was holding me, singing to me. Maybe I would fall in love
with him and look back on this shitty hotel room and smile. I allowed myself
that one night of peace with the thoughts of a happier life before the sun came
up, but when it did the darkness around everything had lifted and we could see
it all the way that it was. He was not my fairytale. After that night I never
spoke to him again. He became a stranger with another notch on his belt and I
became a non-virgin with a bad taste for jack Daniels and a hatred for the Plain White T-shirts and herself.
