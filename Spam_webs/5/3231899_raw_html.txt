      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       Beck Gives Us A Lesson In The Arc Of Content Marketing
      </h1>
      <!-- Make individual group author appear -->
      <div class="mninst">
      </div>
      <div class="comment_links">
       <a class="scroll_to post_your_comment" href="#comment_reply">
        Comment Now
       </a>
       <div class="comments_follow followable_block">
        <div class="followable_block initializing" data-object-json='{"title":"Beck Gives Us A Lesson In The Arc Of Content Marketing","avatar_src":"","follow_count":""}' data-target-id="blogAndPostId/blog/post/1905-2603" data-target-name="" data-target-type="article" data-tracking-link-name="articleLink_ArticleCommentsFollow" data-type-advoice="false">
         <div class="follow_unireg">
          <a class="follow_unireg_link" href="javascript://follow">

     <div class="article_body article" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         With his announcement of a new recorded album covering his sheet-music album, 
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Song-Reader-Beck/dp/193807338X/ref=as_sl_pd_wdgt_ex?&amp;linkCode=wey&amp;tag=wwwideasiclec-20" title="Song Reader on Amazon">
          Song Reader
         </a>
         , Beck has shown us how content marketing can have an arc, a storyline  with a beginning, middle, and end  if we are bold enough to let the content go.
        </p>
        <p>
         I remember how excited I was when Beck released this sheet-music album. Proof comes in the form of my, 
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/willburns/2012/08/09/beck-produces-a-genius-innovation-that-appeals-to-the-user-generated-generation/" title="Beck Produces A Genius Innovation That Appeals To The User-Generated Generation">
          Beck Produces A Genius Innovation That Appeals To The User-Generated Generation
         </a>
         . Because it wasnt a typical recorded album, but sheet music, it was an open invitation to the world to interpret and record his songs.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fast forward two years to the announcement of this new album and what can we learn from Becks foray into content marketing? Lots.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_2618" style="width: 1510px">
         <img alt="Photo credit  Peter Hapak" class="size-full wp-image-2618" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/willburns/files/2014/07/Beck1_PeterHapak_HI.jpg"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo credit  Peter Hapak
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          You dont have to know where its going to go.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         After I wrote the original Forbes piece mentioned above, I was lucky enough to interview Beck about Song Reader. I issued the interview, like his album, in written form and on sheet music (you can read the whole interview
         <a href="http://www.ideasicle.com/Blog_%26_Podcast/Entries/2014/7/23_The_Beck_Hansen_Interview__His_Original_Vision_For_Song_Reader.html" target="_blank" title="The Beck Interview">
          here
         </a>
         ). In it, and before there was any arc to speak of, I asked Beck flat out if he had any intentions of recording these songs someday. Heres what he said:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          I dont know. PossiblyIm kind of waiting to see what happens with them. It may come out and nobody cares, you know? So whatever the outcome, I hope that other artists take some of the songs and do something with them.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Beck took a chance with the content. He didnt focus group it. He just released it and waited to see what happened. His research was the worlds actual reaction. We could all stand to be this bold with our content marketing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          If the idea is great, it will be discovered.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Because the Song Reader idea was so fresh and interesting  an album on sheet music?  it had to get talked about. Sure, the famous Beck the rockstar was behind it, which helped, but this idea was just begging to be discussed.
         <em>
          Thats
         </em>
         great content. It doesnt need a media plan, or much of one. It just needs to be a great idea that gets published. Then watch it light up. Or not.
        </p>
        <p>
         As Beck put it in my same interview with him,
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          I hope that people will find these songs and that something happens with them. Thats the unknown quality, which is very interesting for me.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         I love that he said find these songs. To Beck, this sheet-music content was all about the discovery. Song Reader was like performance art, in a way, where those who discover it become part of the act.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the interview, Beck referenced the old days when sheet music was the only way songs could be actively shared between people:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          I had no idea how prevalent sheet music was and home-played music was. There was a time where that was the way most people heard music. You heard your neighbor, or your aunt, or whoever had some musical talent, playing the songs of the day. And that interaction, that kind of relationship to music and songs is so fundamentally different from our relationship now, where songs are these MP3s that pop up on a blog, or posted somewhere and you hear it once, and then the next day theres another 200 songs.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         By removing his own production from the songs, Beck added anticipation, motivation, and desire in people to play these songs themselves. Like our neighbors and aunts used to do.
        </p>
        <p>
         Talk about engaging content. And engage they did.Just go on YouTube and search Song Reader to see the thousands of uploads.
        </p>
        <p>
         But the Song Reader storyline didnt end there.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A satisfying crescendo.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Once launched, Beck wisely sat back for over a year and watched interest build organically. That year gave people time to interact with the songs, post their own version, listen to others versions. And all along the way, as great as some of the interpretations were, people wondered how Beck would have wanted these songs played. His songs. His sheet music.
        </p>
        <p>
         So he staged a concert to satisfy the latent curiosity. But even the concert was on strategy from a Song Reader perspective. He played some songs himself, but also invited other famous artists to join him and play their own interpretations of the songs, the sheet music, the throwback to Aunt Nellys piano.Jack White, Jack Black, Jeff Tweedy, Fun., Norah Jones, David Johansen and many others joined Beck on stage to play their interpretations.
        </p>
        <p>
         Is the Song Reader storyline over? It may be. But who knows with Beck? Regardless, I applaud this artist for boldly letting
         <a href="/companies/the-arc/">
          the arc
         </a>
         <span class="quotecard_hook" data-exchange="null" data-naturalid="fred/company/91257" data-quotes-closing="0.0" data-quotes-now="0.0" data-ticker="null" data-type="organization">
          <span class="wrapper">
           <a href="/companies/the-arc/">
            the arc
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         of the content take him, and not the other way around.
        </p>
        <p class="sigfile">
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal">
         <i>

           . Follow him on Twitter @WillOBurns.
          </span>
         </i>
        </p>
