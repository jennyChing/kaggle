           <h2 class="p1">
            My Reboot Diet. Why I Started Juicing
           </h2>
           <p class="p1">
            Life is what happenswhileyouare busy making other plans.
            <br/>
             John Lennon
           </p>
           <p class="p2">
            <b>
             How Low Can You Go?
            </b>
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            No. I wont be discussing the limbo.
            <br/>
            Its a bit more personal.
            <br/>
            How Low Can You Go describes my state of mind at the beginning of December 2014.
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            I consider myself to be an optimist, and as far as time management and goal settingareconcerned I am confident in planning and managing stuff.
            <br/>
            And I think it is safe to say that although I was working on some projects life interfered.
           </p>
           <p>
            When I look back on the last past years of my life just like many of us occasionally do, I noticed my share of appointments and disappointments.
            <br/>
            I had to deal with some challenges, but I know I am not unique in saying so.
            <br/>
            The fact I can share this with you is evidence that even my obstacles and setbacks were manageable.
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            Nevertheless.
            <br/>
            At the beginning of December, I took stock of my life and covered the many areas and roles I have.
            <br/>
            And askedmyselfthree simple questions.
            <br/>
            1. Was I a success in that role or that area?
            <br/>
            2. Could I have done it any other way?
            <br/>
            3. Is it possible to make amends or start all over againand do it differently next time?
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            I noticed that I had to readjust my expectations and even say good-bye to some pipe dreams.
            <br/>
            Well. Thats the way it is.
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            <strong>
             <em>
              Its not the misfortune that holds you back; its the way you deal with it.
             </em>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            By chance, I happened to watch a documentary by Joe Cross on Netflix called
            <a href="http://www.fatsickandnearlydead.com/" target="_blank">
             <span class="s1">
              Fat, Sick and Nearly Dead
             </span>
            </a>
            .
            <br/>
            I noticed many similarities between Joe Cross and me Lifestyle wise.
            <br/>
            I ate too much, worked too much and had a sedentary way of life.
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            This had an adverse effect on my health both physically andmentally.
            <br/>
            I was in a dreary mood, and my risk to develop type 2 diabetes very high and the risk to develop other medical disorders that would increase my health risks over time was too high too.
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            Myprimary goal for 2015 became clear.
            <br/>
            In 2015, I would highlight my health.
            <br/>
            No more excuses.
           </p>
           <p class="p1">
            In the documentary, the tool was handed to me on a plate.
            <br/>
            The instrument would be Juicing.
            <br/>
