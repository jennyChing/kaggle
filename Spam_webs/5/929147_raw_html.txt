          <p>
           Reading time: 70 seconds 
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            <strong>
             If you want instant cooperation always use the word because
            </strong>
            (have you thought about talking to/incorporating this because)Automatic response is based on reason, or at least the appearance of reason.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Adjust your attitude to fit the situation before you approach someone
            </strong>
            , or they approach you because your attitude not only drives your behavior  it drives theirs. Welcoming, curious, enthusiastic and resourceful are really useful attitudes.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Speak in positives because when you say what you mean it makes cooperation easy
            </strong>
            . (Rest assured instead of dont worry, you are welcome, instead of no problem, call me with any questions about delivery, instead of dont hesitate to call if you are unsure about delivery)
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Make eye contact because you cant create trust without it.
            </strong>
            The same goes for open
            <a href="https://nicholasboothman.com/changing-minds-with-body-language/" target="_blank" title="body language">
             body language
            </a>
             open hands, uncrossed arms, etc.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Give feedback, physical and spoken
            </strong>
            (nod, say I see etc.), it makes the person feel understood and gets them to open up.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Make sure your voice tone, your body language and your words are all saying the same thing
            </strong>
            . Mixed messages (like smirking when youre angry) cause confusion and destroy trust.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Be on the lookout for opportunities to say Me, too
            </strong>
            (or What a coincidence, or Funny you should say that). There is no faster way to engender trust and build bonds than by finding common ground.
           </li>
          </ol>
