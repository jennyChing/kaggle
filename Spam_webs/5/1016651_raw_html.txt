         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           This timelapse footage released by the European Space Agency shows spectacular views of the Northern Lights from
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/science/space/">
            <strong>
             space.
            </strong>
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           Bright green light surrounds the space station as it passes over the northern hemisphere.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           The images were captured from the International Space Station (ISS) on July 23, a week after a large solar mass ejection.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           The phenomenon, also known as Aurora Borealis, is caused by collisions between electrically charged particles from the sun as they enter the
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/earth/">
            <strong>
             Earth's
            </strong>
           </a>
           atmosphere.
          </p>
