        <p>
         I dont really remember ever loving myself. I always found something wrong with the way I looked, thought, felt, spoke and behaved. The reasons are many, from the way sexism teaches girls and women to think that there is always something about our bodies or minds we need to fix, to my specific upbringing, social and economic class, etc. I could go on and analyze it, and I have many times, but the point is this: I do not love myself, but I really, really want to. For the first time in my awkward little life, I want to love everything about me  my thoughts, my feelings, my body, my choices, my everything, just as it is right now, right at this moment. Not when I lose those extra pounds, or when I can speak confidently in front of a crowd, or when I stop feeling nervous about a social outing. No. Not then. Right the fuck now.
        </p>
        <p>
         There isnt a specific moment I can point to. I have a time frame and some key moments. I finished graduate school in the spring of 2013, and I was adrift. I had figured out that school had been the one thing in my life that I could control. I did the work, I got the good grades and everyone was happy. It was the only real gift I could offer my mother, who busted her hump to raise us by herself. But I did not know it would hit me so hard once I was done with it for real. With my security blanket gone, my unemployment, my lifetime of issues and a world just generally losing its damned collective mind, I felt utterly hopeless. I have struggled with depression and anxiety since I was a teenager, but this was different. I had never felt that depressed in my goddamned life. I thought about dying every single day, several times a day. And that scared the shit out of me. I had never considered it so seriously before, so I got my ass out of bed one day and walked into a clinic and asked for help. This surprised me. It was a glimpse of that raw survival instinct I often wondered if I possessed. This was key moment one.
        </p>
        <p>
         I sat in an exam room with a young, white resident, and as I cried in front of her and told her about all the ugly things that were living inside me. She squeezed my shoulder, looked me in the eye, and said, Im so sorry you feel so bad. I am going to help you, and youre going to be okay. Basic human kindness from a person who probably had a VERY different, probably better life than I did was key moment two. A big part of the darkness I was so full of at that moment was a profound disappointment with human beings. That moment of contradiction between what I was thinking and feeling and the decency and kindness she, a complete stranger, showed me, gave me a little light, a beam full of golden dust. I found that there were openings and cracks in that horrible place I was in. That isnt to say that I dont have other people in my life who are kind to me, but it is beautiful and affects me in a different way when it is a stranger who doesnt really have to behave that way but chooses to do so.
        </p>
        <p>
         After I took meds that I swore I never would (but that honestly saved my life), I was stable enough to work on my mental sludge. I started seeing a counselor, a lovely social worker who actually listened to everything I had to say, even when I thought what I was saying didnt matter. She took my terrible thoughts, ripped them apart, and put them back together as tools I could use to cope. She helped me see that my choices werent lazy or immature, but that I was trying my best to function despite all the pain I was going through. During one of these sessions, as I listened to her explain to me why she thought I was intelligent, brave and generally awesome, I started to believe her. That was key moment number three.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the weeks after, I found that I could be happy with myself. I could cut myself some slack. I could think something nice about my body. I could be pleased with what I accomplished at work without dissecting everything I thought I did wrong. And today I am getting ready to submit my writing for publication for the first time in my life because I can finally think that what I have to say matters, that I matter. And just like, Ive accomplished this. One day I might fulfill my desire to love myself.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

She enjoys watching Buffy the Vampire Slayer and The Office on a loop, eating in bed, and writing down interesting sounding words. She will not eat any sea creature unless it is the apocalypse and there is nothing else left to eat.
        </p>
