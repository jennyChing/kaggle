     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Etlingera elatior
       </b>
      </i>
      (also known as torch ginger, ginger flower, red ginger lily, torch lily, wild ginger,
      <i>
       combrang, bunga kantan
      </i>
      , Philippine wax flower,
      <i>
       xiang bao jiaing
      </i>
      , Indonesian tall ginger,
      <i>
       boca de dragn, rose de porcelaine
      </i>
      , and porcelain rose) is a species of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herbaceous" title="Herbaceous">
       herbaceous
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Perennial_plant" title="Perennial plant">
       perennial plant
      </a>
      .
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Botanical_synonym" title="Botanical synonym">
       Botanical synonyms
      </a>
      include
      <i>
       Nicolaia elatior
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Riffle_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Riffle-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       Phaeomeria magnifica
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Riffle_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Riffle-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       Nicolaia speciosa
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Phaeomeria speciosa
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Alpinia elatior
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       Alpinia magnifica.
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      The showy pink flowers are used in decorative arrangements, while the flower buds are an important ingredient in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nonya_Food" title="Nonya Food">
       Nonya
      </a>
      dish
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Laksa" title="Laksa">
        laksa
       </a>
      </i>
      . In North Sumatra, the flower buds are used for a dish called
      <i>
       arsik ikan mas
      </i>
      (Andaliman/Szechuan pepper-spiced carp).
     </p>
     <p>
      It is known in
      <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">
       Indonesian
      </a>
      as
      <i>
       bunga
      </i>
      <i>
       kecombrang
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       honje
      </i>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">
       Malay
      </a>
      as
      <i>
       bunga kantan
      </i>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">
       Thai
      </a>
      as  ,
      <i>
       daalaa
      </i>
      . In Thailand, it is eaten in a kind of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_salad" title="Thai salad">
       Thai salad
      </a>
      preparation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karo_people" title="Karo people">
       Karo
      </a>
      , it is known as
      <i>
       asam cekala
      </i>
      (
      <i>
       asam
      </i>
      meaning 'sour'), and the flower buds, but more importantly the ripe seed pods, which are packed with small black seeds, are an essential ingredient of the Karo version of
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sayur_Asem" title="Sayur Asem">
        sayur asam
       </a>
      </i>
      , and are particularly suited to cooking fresh fish.
     </p>

     <p>
      From the leaves of
      <i>
       E. elatior
      </i>
      , three
      <a href="/wiki/Caffeoylquinic_acid" title="Caffeoylquinic acid">
       caffeoylquinic acids
      </a>
      , including
      <a href="/wiki/Chlorogenic_acid" title="Chlorogenic acid">
       chlorogenic acid
      </a>
      (CGA), and three
      <a href="/wiki/Flavonoid" title="Flavonoid">
       flavonoids
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Quercitrin" title="Quercitrin">
       quercitrin
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isoquercitrin" title="Isoquercitrin">
       isoquercitrin
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Catechin" title="Catechin">
       catechin
      </a>
      , have been isolated.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chan2009_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-chan2009-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Content of CGA was significantly higher than flowers of
      <i>
       Lonicera japonica
      </i>
      (Japanese honeysuckle), the commercial source.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chan2009a_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-chan2009a-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A protocol for producing a standardized herbal extract of CGA from leaves of
      <i>
       E. elatior
      </i>
      (40%) has been developed, compared to commercial CGA extracts from honeysuckle flowers (25%).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chan2009_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-chan2009-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Leaves of
      <i>
       E. elatior
      </i>
      have the highest antioxidant, antibacterial, and tyrosinase inhibition activities among five
      <i>
       Etlingera
      </i>
      species studied.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chan2007_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-chan2007-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Antioxidant properties (AOP) of leaves were significantly stronger than flowers and rhizomes. Leaves of highland populations had higher AOP values than lowland counterparts. Thermal drying of leaves led to drastic declines in AOP, while freeze-dried leaves showed significantly higher AOP values.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chan2009_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-chan2009-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Ethanolic extracts of inflorescences have antimicrobial activity and are cytotoxic to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HeLa_cell" title="HeLa cell">
       HeLa cells
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mackeen97_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-mackeen97-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Antioxidant activity of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diarylheptanoids" title="Diarylheptanoids">
       diarylheptanoids
      </a>
      isolated from rhizomes is greater than
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%CE%91-tocopherol" title="-tocopherol">
       -tocopherol
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-habsah2005_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-habsah2005-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       E. elatior
      </i>
      has an antioxidant effect against lead-induced
      <a href="/wiki/Hepatotoxicity" title="Hepatotoxicity">
       hepatotoxicity
      </a>
      in rats.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>

        <span>
