       <div class="headline">
        <h1>
         Peter King: We'll investigate Secret Service over White House breach
        </h1>
        <div class="subhead">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="byline">
        By
        <span class="author">
         <a class="event-tracking" data-action="article" data-label="author" href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/author/susan-ferrechio">
          Susan Ferrechio

       <div class="body">
        <p>
         A top House Republican Sunday said it is inexcusable that a man was able to gain access to the
         <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/politics/white-house" target="_blank">
          White House
         </a>
         on Friday without the
         <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/section/secret-service" target="_blank">
          Secret Service
         </a>
         stopping him, and he believes
         <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/politics/congress" target="_blank">
          Congress
         </a>
         will launch a investigation.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have great respect for the Secret Service, Rep. Peter King, R-N.Y., told "Fox News Sunday." But this is absolutely inexcusable.
        </p>
        <p>
         King, who recently chaired the House
         <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/section/homeland-security" target="_blank">
          Homeland Security
         </a>
         Committee, said he is sure the current chairman, Rep.
         <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/section/michael-mccaul" target="_blank">
          Mike McCaul
         </a>
         , R-Texas, will be holding a hearing as to what happened.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Secret Service reported that Omar J. Gonzalez, 42, of Copperas Cove, Texas, scaled a fence on the north lawn of the White House, sprinted across the lawn and entered the mansion.
        </p>
        <div class="ad dynamic-load sharethrough" data-desktop-placement="WEX_Inline_Lists" data-desktop-sizes="[2,1]" data-mobile-placement="WEX_Inline_Lists" data-mobile-sizes="[2,1]" data-position="" data-tablet-placement="WEX_Inline_Lists" data-tablet-sizes="[2,1]" data-targeting="strnativekey:a9eeb01a,keywords:politics|white house|susan ferrechio|secret service|pennave|peter king" id="ad_2553742_">
        </div>
        <p>
         Gonzalez did not have a gun, but was carrying a knife.
        </p>
        <p>
         He could have had a bomb, King said on Fox. He could have had [an explosive-laden] vest on.
        </p>
        <p>
         King is among several lawmakers who have criticized the Secret Service over the incident. Rep.
         <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/section/jason-chaffetz" target="_blank">
          Jason Chaffetz
         </a>
         , R-Utah, said hell look into the matter in his role as chair of a Government Oversight subcommittee on
         <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/policy/national-security" target="_blank">
          national security
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         On Fox, King suggested the Secret Service had plenty of time to respond to Gonzalez, but appeared to do nothing.
        </p>
        <p>
         I believe it takes 35 to 40 seconds to run across the lawn, a dog can be released in 4 seconds, the sensors are working there, and then that he made it all the way to the White House and actually entered the front door, King said.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/politics/barack-obama" target="_blank">
          President Obama
         </a>
         and his family were not home at the time.
        </p>
        <p>
         Secret Service officials said on Saturday they are investigating the incident and have increased security in and around the White House.
        </p>
