       <h1 class="postTitle">
        Benefits of Hiring a Licensed and Insured Contractor
       </h1>
       <div class="blogMeta">
        November 16th, 2014 | By
        <a href="http://www.heatingandairsanmateo.com/author/150932/" rel="author" title="Posts by 150932">
         150932
        </a>
        | Posted in
        <a href="http://www.heatingandairsanmateo.com/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Uncategorized">
         Uncategorized
        </a>
        |
       </div>
       <div class="theContent">
        <p>
         Your furnace has stopped working andits 30 degrees out, or maybe your air conditioning unit is barely keeping your house below 80 degrees in the middle of summer. Whatever the case, its time to call a heating and air conditioning specialist. You may be tempted to save a few dollars by hiringyour neighbor whoclaims to be ado-it-yourself guru, but this could end up costing you in the long run. By hiring a licensed and insuredHVAC professional, you can ensure that the job will be done correctly and thatyou will have proper recourse if problemsdo arise.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Job Done Right
        </h2>
        <p>
         In order to become licensed, an HVAC technician must havespecialized training, whichbetter ensures that your heating or air conditioningproblemwill be fixedefficiently and professionally.This willprevent your neighbor from misdiagnosing the problem and will save you time and energy in rounding up tools and equipment for the do-it-yourself job.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Insurance = Security
        </h2>
        <p>
         By choosing a licensed HVAC contractor, not only do you have the security that he has gone through the process of being trained for your specific issue but he is also insuredagainst any problems that may arise from his work. This gives you peace of mind that you are entitled to recourse if anyassociated problems should arise.Keep in mind that these are just two of the reasons it is important to hire a licensed and insured heating and air conditioning specialist for your HVAC needs.
        </p>
