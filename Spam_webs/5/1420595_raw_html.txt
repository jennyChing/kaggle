        <h1 class="post-title" itemprop="name">
         Three Anti-Aging Effects of the Paleo Diet Lifestyle
        </h1>
        <a class="comments" href="https://paleomagonline.com/three-anti-aging-effects-of-the-paleo-diet-lifestyle/#comments">
         <i class="fa fa-comments-o">
         </i>
         4
        </a>
       </header>
       <!-- .post-header -->
       <div class="post-meta">
        <span class="posted-by">
         By
         <span class="reviewer" itemprop="author">
          <a href="https://paleomagonline.com/author/drbragg/" rel="author" title="Posts by Drew Bragg">
           Drew Bragg
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="posted-on">
         on
         <span class="dtreviewed">
          <time class="value-datetime" datetime="2014-05-12T20:32:13+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           May 12, 2014
          </time>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="cats">
         <a href="https://paleomagonline.com/lifestyle/" rel="category tag">
          Paleo Lifestyle
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="post-container cf">
        <div class="post-content-right">
         <div class="post-content description " itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           Many people are attracted to the Paleo diet and lifestyle because of its reputation as both a means to lose weight and to improve overall health. But what if I told you that going Paleo might actually make you younger as well? To be clear, were not suggesting that you can literally go back in time (
           <i>
            to meet Grok perhaps
           </i>
           ) but it is possible to turn back the hands of another clock.
          </p>
          <p>
           The concept of age encompasses two distinct components, chronological age and biological age. Chronological age operates in a linear fashion. Always going forward and at distinct intervals; seconds, minutes, hours, days, and years. Biological age, on the other hand, is flexible. Depending on how well, or how poorly, you take care of yourself, you can actually be much older or much younger than your chronological age belies.
          </p>
          <p>
           If youre still stuck on the Standard American Diet (SAD) and living the Standard American Lifestyle (SAL) you can almost guarantee that your biological age is not where youd want it to be. The smooth skin, sharp mind, and sexual prowess that you took for granted in your youth are at risk if you continue on this path of poor eating and physical inactivity.
          </p>
          <p>
           The good news is, the Paleo diet and lifestyle offers the perfect antidote to being a SAD SAL (
           <i>
            or Sally
           </i>
           ).
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Smoother Skin
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           As the years roll by, most of us expect to find the face looking back at us from the mirror to gradually sag, wrinkle, and roughen. We accept that, in general, we will watch helplessly as the vibrancy and vitality of youth slips away.
          </p>
          <p>
           The financial opportunity presented by this seemingly inevitable decline has not gone unnoticed and, as a result, there are entire stores stocked with the numerous pills, potions, and creams created to fight the effects of aging on our skin.
          </p>
          <p>
           If superficial solutions arent enough, we can consult our friendly neighborhood plastic surgeon who can simply nip and tuck our offending epidermis back in line. Somehow missed in this conversation, however, is the impact of diet on the aging process.
          </p>
          <p>
           According to recent estimates
           <sup>
            1
           </sup>
           the average American consumes 22 teaspoons of sugar each and every day, the vast majority coming by way of high-fructose corn-syrup sweetened soft drinks and snacks. Most of us are aware that this can lead to diabetes and heart disease, but few consider the effect this can have on our face.
          </p>
          <p>
           Inside the body, the effect of sugar is anything but sweet. Sugar molecules are highly reactive and when they come in contact with the protein collagen, they quickly attach themselves in a process called glycation. If collagen didnt do anything useful, this might not be that big of a deal, but the truth is that collagen is the major structural component of skin and other connective tissues.
          </p>
          <p>
           Once bonded with sugar, collagen becomes weak and fragile, forming compounds known as Advanced Glycation End Products (appropriately abbreviated as AGEs). Without its collagen scaffolding, skin has no choice but to sag.
          </p>
          <p>
           Scientists in the Netherlands actually quantified this effect, estimating that with every 1mm increase in sugar per liter of blood, a persons perceived age increased by five months.
           <sup>
            2
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           Fortunately, a diet low in processed sugars and refined carbohydrates (
           <i>
            such as that recommended by the Paleo diet
           </i>
           ) keeps blood sugar levels in check, slowing down the formation of excess AGEs reversing process of accelerated skin aging.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Sharper Mind
            <img alt="three-anti-aging-effects-of-the-paleo-diet-lifestyle-2" class="lazy lazy-hidden alignright size-full wp-image-1538" data-lazy-type="image" data-src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/three-anti-aging-effects-of-the-paleo-diet-lifestyle-2.jpg" height="244" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7" style="border: 1px solid black; margin: 10px;" width="375"/>
            <noscript>
             <img alt="three-anti-aging-effects-of-the-paleo-diet-lifestyle-2" class="alignright size-full wp-image-1538" height="244" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/three-anti-aging-effects-of-the-paleo-diet-lifestyle-2.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin: 10px;" width="375"/>
            </noscript>
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           Have you ever forgotten your keys, misplaced your phone, or struggled to remember the name of that guy in that show? Have you then explained away this mental lapse as a simple senior moment?
          </p>
          <p>
           As with our skin, we often anticipate that our mental faculties will only get worse with time, and to a certain extent this is true. We cant completely ensure that our mind will never leave us. We can, however, change our diet.
          </p>
          <p>
           In a study conducted at UCLAs David Geffen School of Medicine, researchers found that rats whose water was spiked with fructose did significantly worse navigating a maze than their counterparts whose sugar water also included the omega-3 fatty acid docosahexaenoic acid (DHA).
           <sup>
            3
           </sup>
           But its not just rats whose brains suffer from a lack of DHA. Human studies have correlated low DHA levels in the brain with an increased incidence of dementia and Alzheimers disease.
           <sup>
            4
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           As you might imagine, low DHA levels are standard for the SAD diet. We need dietary DHA because our bodies cannot synthesized adequate quantities on their own. This is the reason why DHA is known as an essential fatty acid.
          </p>
          <p>
           The richest dietary sources of brain food derive from fatty, cold-water fish like salmon and mackerel. Unfortunately, the average American only consumes about 3 oz of fish per week and the increased prevalence of farm-raised seafood means even less DHA per ounce than what would be found in wild-caught seafood.
           <sup>
            5
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           Unfortunately, eating more wild-caught seafood might not be the solution either as the increasing amount of mercury and other heavy metals found in large predatory fish makes regular fish consumption less desirable. The good news is, small fish, like anchovies, are both rich in DHA and low in toxic metals.
           <sup>
            6
           </sup>
           Alternate sources of DHA also include Paleo-friendly options like grass-fed beef and pastured eggs.
           <sup>
            7
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           Going beyond diet, another way to buffer your brain from the effects of aging is to exercise. In a paper published in the scientific journal
           <i>
            Dialogues in Clinical Neuroscience
           </i>
           , researchers concluded that physical activity is a promising intervention that can influence the endogenous pharmacology of the brain to enhance cognitive and emotional function in late adulthood. In other words, staying physically fit keeps your brain young!
           <sup>
            8
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Better Sex
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           The scene opens with a handsome older couple walking along a lake, building a birdhouse, or preparing food in the kitchen. At some point, the husband and wife catch eyes, a sign that things will soon heat up, and Im not talking about dinner. The narrators voice cuts in and announces that If you suffer from erectile dysfunction, ask your doctor about Viagra.
          </p>
          <p>
           In the months following the introduction of Viagra, the first pill marketed to treat erectile dysfunction (ED), the pharmaceutical company Pfizers profits soared by 38%.
           <sup>
            9
           </sup>
           In the years hence, Viagra has continued to be a top seller and several other companies have gotten in on the action with their own ED pills. The question no one seems to ask however is why so many men need the little blue pill in the first place.
          </p>
          <p>
           Just as a heart attack results from the blockage of arteries supplying blood to the heart, ED can be the result of blockages in the arteries that supply blood to the penis.
           <sup>
            10
           </sup>
           The problem then isnt the lack of an erection, its the lack of overall health!
          </p>
          <p>
           To be sure, there is a percentage of ED patients that suffer due to injuries to the spine or pelvis, complications related to prostate disease, or from any number of physical, psychological, or emotional issues, but the fact that heart disease is the number one cause of death in the United States, claiming over 500,000 lives each year, speaks to the prevalence of this problem in our society.
           <sup>
            11
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           Sexual problems related to poor health are not exclusive to men either. Nearly 43% of women report having at least one sexual difficulty (
           <i>
            a number higher than that of men
           </i>
           ) which can include painful intercourse, lack of desire or arousal, failure to reach orgasm, or inadequate vaginal lubrication. In a study of 100 women that controlled for body mass index (BMI), age, and premenopausal status, it was found that women with metabolic syndrome have an increased prevalence of sexual dysfunctions.
           <sup>
            12
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           In addition to increased physical activity and a high quality diet, several other aspects of the Paleo lifestyle have been connected with either preventing or reversing the incidence of cardiovascular disease. Excess stress and poor quality sleep are known to detrimentally impact cardiovascular health and by addressing these lifestyle components it is likely that ones sexual health will be improved as well.
           <sup>
            13 14
           </sup>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            The Anti-Aging Protocol in 75 Words
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           Slowing down the incessant forward march of time doesnt have to be complicated. In fact, if you are already following the Paleo diet and lifestyle you are already following the Anti-Aging Protocol.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             To sum it up
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           Everyday, eat from a variety of fresh and cooked fruits and vegetables, meats, seafood, eggs, nuts and seeds. Drink water and limit your consumption of alcohol. Avoid processed foods, industrial seed oils, gluten containing grains, and legumes. Go outside and play. Lift weights and run fast. Get off your computer, turn off the TV, and go to sleep at a reasonable time. Cultivate hobbies that help you to relax. Connect with your family and friends.
          </p>
