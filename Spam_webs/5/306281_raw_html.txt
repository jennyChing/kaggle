     <p>
      <b>
       Philip James "Phil" Selway
      </b>
      (born 23 May 1967) is an English musician, singer, and songwriter best known as the drummer of English rock group
      <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">
       Radiohead
      </a>
      . In addition to drums, he provides backing vocals, along with occasional guitar and lead vocals, for
      <a href="/wiki/7_Worlds_Collide" title="7 Worlds Collide">
       7 Worlds Collide
      </a>
      . Selway is well known for his precision and proficiency in various styles and unusual time signatures,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      being named the 26th greatest drummer of all time by
      <a href="/wiki/Gigwise" title="Gigwise">
       Gigwise
      </a>
      in 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He has worked with
      <a href="/wiki/Samaritans_(charity)" title="Samaritans (charity)">
       Samaritans
      </a>
      since 1991.
     </p>
     <p>
      Selway has released two solo albums:
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Familial_(album)" title="Familial (album)">
        Familial
       </a>
      </i>
      (2010)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-independent_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-independent-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Weatherhouse_(album)" title="Weatherhouse (album)">
        Weatherhouse
       </a>
      </i>
      (2014).
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Radiohead">
       Radiohead
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philip_Selway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Radiohead">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">
       Radiohead
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Before Radiohead became successful, Selway studied Literature, Life, and Thought (English and History) at Liverpool Polytechnic's CF Mott Campus near Prescot (prior to it being renamed
      <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_John_Moores_University" title="Liverpool John Moores University">
       Liverpool John Moores University
      </a>
      ). Before that he had studied maths and worked for various touring musicians and worked as an
      <a href="/wiki/English_studies" title="English studies">
       English
      </a>
      teacher as well. In the very early '90s, Selway left Radiohead's early line-up (then called Shindig or On a Friday) to move to Ireland, and only returned to the band when a relationship fell apart.
     </p>
     <p>
      His timing and varied drumming helps to give the band its sound. Whereas his technique on earlier albums such as
      <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Honey" title="Pablo Honey">
       Pablo Honey
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/The_Bends" title="The Bends">
       The Bends
      </a>
      corresponded to the classic rock feel of the band's original style, the more experimental and atmospheric feel of their more recent work has been complemented by Selway's transition to a more solid, repetitive technique, often featuring a
      <a href="/wiki/Motorik" title="Motorik">
       motorik
      </a>
      sound.
     </p>
     <p>
      Apart from his regular band duties, Selway also occasionally sings backing vocals during live performances, especially notable on "
      <a href="/wiki/There_There" title="There There">
       There There
      </a>
      ", "
      <a href="/wiki/2_%2B_2_%3D_5_(song)" title="2 + 2 = 5 (song)">
       2+2=5
      </a>
      ", "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Will_(Radiohead_song)" title="I Will (Radiohead song)">
       I Will
      </a>
      ", and "
      <a href="/wiki/Go_to_Sleep" title="Go to Sleep">
       Go to Sleep
      </a>
      ".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Since the album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Kid_A" title="Kid A">
        Kid A
       </a>
      </i>
      , he has also contributed
      <a href="/wiki/Drum_machine" title="Drum machine">
       drum machine
      </a>
      programming.
     </p>
     <p>
      Selway has studied drums in London at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drumtech" title="Drumtech">
       Drumtech
      </a>
      under the tutelage of Francis Seriau along with fellow Radiohead member
      <a href="/wiki/Ed_O%27Brien" title="Ed O'Brien">
       Ed O'Brien
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Work_outside_of_Radiohead">
       Work outside of Radiohead
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philip_Selway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work outside of Radiohead">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:7_Worlds_Collide2.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2736" data-file-width="3648" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/7_Worlds_Collide2.jpg/225px-7_Worlds_Collide2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/7_Worlds_Collide2.jpg/338px-7_Worlds_Collide2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/7_Worlds_Collide2.jpg/450px-7_Worlds_Collide2.jpg 2x" width="225"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:7_Worlds_Collide2.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Selway performing with 7 Worlds Collide, 2009
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Selway has been associated with emotional support group
      <a href="/wiki/Samaritans_(charity)" title="Samaritans (charity)">
       Samaritans
      </a>
      as a listening volunteer since 1991. He performed with the band
      <a href="/wiki/Dive_Dive" title="Dive Dive">
       Dive Dive
      </a>
      in March 2005 and appeared in the movie
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(movie)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (movie)">
        Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire
       </a>
      </i>
      as a member of the band "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Weird_Sisters_(Harry_Potter)" title="The Weird Sisters (Harry Potter)">
       The Weird Sisters
      </a>
      " along with Radiohead bandmate
      <a href="/wiki/Jonny_Greenwood" title="Jonny Greenwood">
       Jonny Greenwood
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Pulp_(band)" title="Pulp (band)">
       Pulp
      </a>
      frontman
      <a href="/wiki/Jarvis_Cocker" title="Jarvis Cocker">
       Jarvis Cocker
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Selway has also toured and recorded with
      <a href="/wiki/Neil_Finn" title="Neil Finn">
       Neil Finn
      </a>
      as part of the 7 Worlds Collide project. He drummed on their eponymous
      <a href="/wiki/7_Worlds_Collide" title="7 Worlds Collide">
       2001 live album
      </a>
      , and provides drums, guitar and occasional lead vocals on their 2009 studio album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Sun_Came_Out" title="The Sun Came Out">
        The Sun Came Out
       </a>
      </i>
      , where he also penned two tracks.
     </p>
     <p>
      He appears on two tracks  "Rest on the Rock" and "Out of Light"  on the album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Before_the_Ruin" title="Before the Ruin">
        Before the Ruin
       </a>
      </i>
      , by
      <a href="/wiki/Roddy_Woomble" title="Roddy Woomble">
       Roddy Woomble
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Kris_Drever" title="Kris Drever">
       Kris Drever
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/John_McCusker" title="John McCusker">
       John McCusker
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2009 Selway was featured as a guitarist on Martin Simpson's studio album
      <i>
       True Stories
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Selway released his debut solo album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Familial_(album)" title="Familial (album)">
        Familial
       </a>
      </i>
      , on 30 August 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-independent_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-independent-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The album features Selway on guitar and vocals, along with Wilco members
      <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Kotche" title="Glenn Kotche">
       Glenn Kotche
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Pat_Sansone" title="Pat Sansone">
       Pat Sansone
      </a>
      as well as fellow
      <a href="/wiki/7_Worlds_Collide" title="7 Worlds Collide">
       7 Worlds Collide
      </a>
      artists
      <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Germano" title="Lisa Germano">
       Lisa Germano
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Steinberg" title="Sebastian Steinberg">
       Sebastian Steinberg
      </a>
      . He announced a solo tour on 4 February 2011.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>

        <span>

        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

     <p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">
       Personal life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philip_Selway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Selway was born in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abingdon,_Oxfordshire" title="Abingdon, Oxfordshire">
       Abingdon
      </a>
      , Berkshire (now
      <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">
       Oxfordshire
      </a>
      , England). He and his wife Cait have three children: Leo, Jamie, and Patrick (to whom
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Kid_A" title="Kid A">
        Kid A
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Amnesiac_(album)" title="Amnesiac (album)">
        Amnesiac
       </a>
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Hail_to_the_Thief" title="Hail to the Thief">
        Hail to the Thief
       </a>
      </i>
      were dedicated, respectively).
     </p>
     <p>
      His mother Thea died during a Radiohead tour in May 2006. The band cancelled one of their dates in
      <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">
       Amsterdam
      </a>
      , so that Selway could carry out his family duties:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       Just wanted to say sorry to the people who were due to come to our show in Amsterdam last night, particularly those who made wasted journeys. My mum died suddenly in the early hours of yesterday morning and so I just wanted to be at home with my family. Mum was a big Radiohead fan, and was very proud of all we've done as a band. I love and miss her very much.
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      The band later returned to Amsterdam in August to play the missed show. The discbox, LP, and CD release of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/In_Rainbows" title="In Rainbows">
        In Rainbows
       </a>
      </i>
      mentions a dedication to Thea Selway.
     </p>
