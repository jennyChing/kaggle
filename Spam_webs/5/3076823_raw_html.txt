        <h1>
         Welcome
        </h1>
        <p class="bodytext">
         <b>
          Welcome to the Department of Arts, Heritage and the Gaeltachts World Heritage Ireland website. We aim to provide a valuable information resource on Irelands World Heritage sites and World Heritage Programme.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p class="bodytext">
         Hopefully you will enjoy navigating our website whilst enhancing your appreciation of World Heritage matters from an Irish perspective.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="two-boxes">

        <div class="middle">
         <div class="green">
          <h2>
           <span>
            World Heritage Site:
           </span>
           Br na Binne
          </h2>
          <p class="bodytext">
           The Bend of the Boyne, or Br na Binne, has been an important ritual, social and economic centre for thousands of years. Internationally renowned for its elaborate Neolithic passage tombs, and containing the largest assemblage of megalithic art in Europe, its universal value was recognised in 1993 when it was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a class="internal-link" href="bru-na-boinne/" title="Opens internal link in current window">
             Read more about Br na Binne
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="blue">
          <h2>
           <span>
            World Heritage Site:
           </span>
           Skellig Michael
          </h2>
          <p class="bodytext">
           Skellig Michael is an outstanding and in many ways unique example of early religious settlement, spectacularly sited on a pyramidal rock in the Atlantic Ocean. This well-preserved early monastery provides a rare insight into the organisation, architectural achievements and way of life of an early Irish monastic community.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a class="internal-link" href="skellig-michael/" title="Opens internal link in current window">
             Read more information about Skellig Michael
             <br/>
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>

            <span>
             Tentative List
            </span>
           </h2>
