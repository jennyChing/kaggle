         My Running Mojo Has Turned Into A Running Mojito
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://speedskatingmom.com/my-running-mojo-has-turned-into-a-running-mojito/" rel="bookmark" title="3:51 pm">
         <span class="entry-date">
          April 28, 2015
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://speedskatingmom.com/author/speeds/" title="View all posts by speeds">
          speeds
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         For the past month whenever I run, in simple laymans terms, I crap out. I hit the 3k mark and mentally I just stop which means physically I just stop. Except for that day last week when I was attacked by a bird. Then I kept running because I didnt want to turn into a Hitchcock horror movie ending. Basically my running mojo has turned into a running mojito. And people, mojitos are good. Much better than mojo which I would imagine tastes slightly like tootsie rolls.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres no real reason for it although both my very-smart-physically-fit-husband-who-has-no-problem-running-15k-before-he-even-eats-breakfast and I believe its mostly due to the heat because I am not good with heat. The physically fit husband above dubbed me seal skin when we first met 17 years ago after watching me turn bright red while working out and yet only managed to get a slight glowy, sheen on my face.
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont sweat a lot which means I overheat a lot. I wont even get into the whole pre-menopause hot flash thing.
        </p>
        <p>
         So last night I forced myself on the treadmill and made myself a promise not to stop until I had done at least 5k and I wasnt going to slow things down. This time it was all about mind over matter, and apparently my body didnt mind because dudes, I did it.
        </p>
        <p>
         I think I may have found my running mojo again but it will never replace my love affair with the mojito.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you liked this post then please follow me on
         <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Two-Writes-Dont-Make-A-Wrong-by-SharonDV/194432953981078">
          Facebook

         and

        </p>

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         The SAT exam can ruin the confidence of students. It has done this many times before when students find that they do not know the answers to the questions of the exam. Confidence in your exam abilities can plummet too even further lows in this sort of situation.         </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">

        </span>

       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Can LSAT perfection be achieved? Well that is a good question, since many people believe that achieving perfection (getting every single answer correct in the exam) is out of our reach as mere mortals. And actually, the people who think this are often right. It is only on very rare occasions that you will find someone has been able to score 100%. On any exam that is worth taking (ie is an exam that leads to something great happening, career wise) there will be barely any students who are able to pass so well. But maybe you can?
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, I think you would be aiming a little bit high. Even if you
         <a href="http://crushthelsatexam.com/lsatmax-review/">
          used the LSAT max review
         </a>
         , you would be very hard pushed to get the result you want, since the exam is so hard. You could try I guess but to be honest I do not like your odds of success. It would be much better to aim for 80 or 90%, thus allowing for a little bit of human error. Aim a little bit lower and then you will not have to feel disappointed when it comes to the exam result. But do not aim too low, because that too could cause an issue, which would be the issue of you possibly not quite getting enough marks for a pass.
        </p>

My point here is that if you are aiming to only just scrape through with a pass grade, then you have got something wrong there, because you should be aiming a little bit higher than that. I really think you should be aiming to get like 90% of the marks. Aiming for 100% is a little unrealistic because nobody is perfect, but 90% is a good target and if you dont manage to reach it, thats no problem because at least having aimed so high, you should at least get a pass, as you would have done more studying, hopefully all the while
