          <h1 class="entry-title">
           Painted Swiss Cross Curtains
          </h1>
         </div>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <div class="meta-info-wrap post_hide_author post_hide_cats post_hide_single_cat post_hide_tags post_hide_permalink">
           <span class="sep posted-on">
            Posted on
           </span>
           <a href="http://houseologie.com/painted-swiss-cross-curtains/" rel="bookmark" title="7:43 am">

             July 18, 2014

           </a>
           <span class="by-author">
            <span class="sep">

            </span>
            <span class="author vcard">

           </span>
          </div>

         <!-- .entry-meta -->

        <div class="entry-content cf">

         <p>
          Hey yall!
         </p>
         <p>
          Today Im sharing my DIY painted dropcloth curtains with you lovely people. I love the Swiss cross design trend (some people call it plus sign trend). I decided to make a DIY stencil and paint some imperfect Swiss crosses/plus signs on my boring dropcloth curtains. I love how they turned out!
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Swiss Cross Painted Curtains 4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-788" height="513" src="http://houseologie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Swiss-Cross-Painted-Curtains-4.jpg" width="684"/>
         </p>
         <div class="insert-post-ads" style="clear:both;float:left;width:100%;margin:0 0 20px 0;">
          <center>
           <!-- Javascript tag: -->
           <!-- begin ZEDO for channel:  houseologie.com , publisher: houseologie.com , Ad Dimension: Medium Rectangle - 300 x 250 -->
           <script language="JavaScript">
            var zflag_nid="1303"; var zflag_cid="1905"; var zflag_sid="1700"; var zflag_width="300"; var zflag_height="250"; var zflag_sz="9";
           </script>
           <script language="JavaScript" src="http://d3.zedo.com/jsc/d3/fo.js">
           </script>
          </center>
          <!-- end ZEDO for channel:  houseologie.com , publisher: houseologie.com , Ad Dimension: Medium Rectangle - 300 x 250 -->
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          (Sorry for the weird picture. These huge windows are hard for me to photograph)
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://houseologie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/swiss-Cross-Painted-Curtains-3.jpg">
           <img alt="swiss Cross Painted Curtains 3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-787" height="933" src="http://houseologie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/swiss-Cross-Painted-Curtains-3.jpg" width="700"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I still had plenty of black paint (Valspar Paint + Primer color matched to Sherwin Williams Tricorn Black) left over from my gallon that Ive used in the
          <a href="/dining-room-light/" target="_blank" title="Dining Room Light">
           dining room
          </a>
          , in the
          <a href="/hallway-makeover-final-reveal/" target="_blank" title="Hallway Makeover  Final Reveal!">
           hallway makeover
          </a>
          and on this
          <a href="/moroccan-side-table-makeover/" target="_blank" title="Moroccan Side Table Makeover">
           Moroccan table
          </a>
          . It just never runs out!!
         </p>
         <p>
          Instead of buying a stencil I made my own. I used a file box top,a box cutter, a ruler and a pencil. Super professional here at the Houseologie headquarters  It took less than 10 minutes to make, its so easy!
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://houseologie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Swiss-Cross-Stencil.jpg">
           <img alt="Swiss Cross Stencil" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-781" height="525" src="http://houseologie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Swiss-Cross-Stencil.jpg" width="700"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          For the curtains I used two drop cloth curtains I had hanging on either side of this window in the first place. They were so boring but cheap. I used a 2 inch angled paint brush to paint the crosses. I laid the drop cloth across my dining room table with newspaper underneath, just in case the paint saturated through. For the most part it did not. I just made sure I didnt have much paint on the brush and swiped it both ways a few times. I didnt want the crosses to be saturated and full, or to go through the back of the curtain and they didnt. Using less paint and not getting too perfect with it helped this happen.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://houseologie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Swiss-Cross-Painted-Curtains-graphic.jpg">
           <img alt="Swiss Cross Painted Curtains graphic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-791" height="700" src="http://houseologie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Swiss-Cross-Painted-Curtains-graphic.jpg" width="700"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I used ring clips from IKEA to hang them on the rod. They were a bit too long so I just folded the top over, towards the back until they were the right length. I think these curtains add a nice little pop of fun to this otherwise boring room. Ive got a long way to go in my living room but Im looking forward to making it a space I love!
         </p>
         <p>
          Hope youre having a good week so far. Have you painted curtains before? You can see my painting dining room curtains too. Its a cheap and easy way to update curtains and a room.
         </p>
