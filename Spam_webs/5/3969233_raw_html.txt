   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    VALIS trilogy
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">

    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       VALIS trilogy
      </b>
      is a set of
      <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">
       science fiction
      </a>
      /
      <a href="/wiki/Philosophical_fiction" title="Philosophical fiction">
       philosophical
      </a>
      novels by author
      <a href="/wiki/Philip_K._Dick" title="Philip K. Dick">
       Philip K. Dick
      </a>
      which include
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/VALIS" title="VALIS">
        VALIS
       </a>
      </i>
      (1978),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Divine_Invasion" title="The Divine Invasion">
        The Divine Invasion
       </a>
      </i>
      (1980), and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Owl_in_Daylight" title="The Owl in Daylight">
        The Owl in Daylight
       </a>
      </i>
      (unfinished/unpublished). The "trilogy" may also include
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Radio_Free_Albemuth" title="Radio Free Albemuth">
        Radio Free Albemuth
       </a>
      </i>
      (1985) and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Transmigration_of_Timothy_Archer" title="The Transmigration of Timothy Archer">
        The Transmigration of Timothy Archer
       </a>
      </i>
      (1982). Dick's first novel about the VALIS concept originally titled "
      <i>
       VALISystem A
      </i>
      " (written 1976), was published as
      <i>
       Radio Free Albemuth
      </i>
      after Dick's death in 1982.
      <i>
       The Transmigration of Timothy Archer
      </i>
      was posthumously substituted for the unfinished
      <i>
       The Owl in Daylight
      </i>
      as the third novel in the "trilogy."
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
       Background
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=VALIS_trilogy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In February and March 1974, Dick experienced a series of visions and other inexplicable perceptual and cognitive phenomena. For the rest of his life, Dick explored the philosophical implications and hypothesized about the origins of the experience, in a journal which eventually ran to hundreds of thousands of words. This work became known as the
      <i>
       Exegesis
      </i>
      , selections of which were published as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Exegesis_of_Philip_K._Dick" title="The Exegesis of Philip K. Dick">
        The Exegesis of Philip K. Dick
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Dick's leading hypothesis was that he had been contacted by a transcendental, mystical mind he called VALIS (vast active living intelligence system).
     </p>
     <p>
      In the summer of 1976, Dick completed a novel based on these so-called "2-3-74 experiences," which he titled
      <i>
       VALISystem A
      </i>
      . The novel was sold to
      <a href="/wiki/Bantam_Books" title="Bantam Books">
       Bantam Books
      </a>
      , but after editor Mark Hurst suggested some possible revisions, Dick began contemplating a revision so radical as to constitute a new novel. The original
      <i>
       VALISystem A
      </i>
      was published posthumously as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Radio_Free_Albemuth" title="Radio Free Albemuth">
        Radio Free Albemuth
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The new version, titled simply
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/VALIS" title="VALIS">
        VALIS
       </a>
      </i>
      , was completed late in 1978 and published in 1981 (the plot of the earlier version appears as the plot of a science fiction movie, also called "VALIS," that the characters see). By that time, Dick had completed a second novel, one also filled with his thoughts about religion and philosophy and very indirectly linked to
      <i>
       VALIS
      </i>
      (the VALIS entity gets but two mentions), which he called
      <i>
       VALIS Regained
      </i>
      and which was published as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Divine_Invasion" title="The Divine Invasion">
        The Divine Invasion
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Dick soon began talking (in letters and interviews) about a third novel to complete a "VALIS Trilogy." The novel was first to be called
      <i>
       Fawn, Look Back
      </i>
      , then
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Owl_in_Daylight" title="The Owl in Daylight">
        The Owl in Daylight
       </a>
      </i>
      . At least two outlines for the unwritten novel were published posthumously, and descriptions have appeared in several interviews and letters. These schema are to some extent contradictory, and it is likely that the novel would have been significantly different from any of them.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2007)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After Dick's death, several omnibus editions of the "VALIS Trilogy" were published, with his final mainstream novel
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Transmigration_of_Timothy_Archer" title="The Transmigration of Timothy Archer">
        The Transmigration of Timothy Archer
       </a>
      </i>
      standing in for the unwritten final volume.
      <i>
       Timothy Archer
      </i>
      does not cite VALIS and was not intended by Dick to be part of the trilogy; however, the book fits comfortably with the two finished volumes and Dick himself called the three novels a trilogy, saying "the three do form a trilogy constellating around a basic theme."
      <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.philipkdick.com/media_twilightzone.html" rel="nofollow">
       [1]
      </a>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       1978 -
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/VALIS" title="VALIS">
         VALIS
        </a>
       </i>
      </li>
      <li>
       1980 -
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Divine_Invasion" title="The Divine Invasion">
         The Divine Invasion
        </a>
       </i>
      </li>
      <li>
       (Unfinished) -
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Owl_in_Daylight" title="The Owl in Daylight">
         The Owl in Daylight
        </a>
       </i>
       <ul>
        <li>
         1981 -
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/The_Transmigration_of_Timothy_Archer" title="The Transmigration of Timothy Archer">
           The Transmigration of Timothy Archer
          </a>
         </i>
         (
         <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/posthumous" title="wikt:posthumous">
          posthumously
         </a>
         substituted for unfinished third volume)
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
     </ul>
