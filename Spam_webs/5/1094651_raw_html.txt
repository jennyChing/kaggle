             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               If you've tried downloading
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/09/17/ios-8-review/">
                iOS 8
               </a>
               , or know anyone who has already done so, you're probably familiar with the biggest complaint: The download is
               <em>
                huge
               </em>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               It's not that iOS 8 occupies a ton of storage on your
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/iphone/">
                iPhone
               </a>
               or iPad (iTunes says the file is about 1.2GB), but rather that the download itself needs up to 5.7GB of free storage to be completed.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/09/17/ios-8-arrives-how-to-download-it-now/">
                 iOS 8 Arrives: How to Download It Now
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               As a result, people are scrambling to delete pictures, apps and music in order to make room for iOS. The previous version,
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/ios-7/">
                iOS 7
               </a>
               , only required 2.9GB of space to install, so the added storage requirement has hit some users pretty hard.
               <strong>
                Note
               </strong>
               : Depending on which model you have  and the last time you updated the iOS  how much space you'll need in order to download the software will vary.
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                I no longer have anything on my phone, BUT I HAVE IOS 8!
               </p>
               <p>
                 Brian Koerber (@bkurbs)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/bkurbs/status/512302727201050624" target="_blank">
                 September 17, 2014
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                deleted my sex life, life insurance policy and three cats to get iOS 8
               </p>
               <p>
                 taylor hatmaker (@tayhatmaker)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/tayhatmaker/status/512302084369027072" target="_blank">
                 September 17, 2014
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               But before you start deleting everything you've ever saved, there's a very easy solution: Sync your iPhone or iPad to the computer and download iOS 8 directly from there. This won't suck up any space on your device, and you can manually transfer the new software back to your device. Updating via your PC or Mac is more reliable way to upgrade, versus OTA (over-the-air), which is performed directly on the device.
              </p>
              <p>
               If you're taking this alternate route, be sure to select "check for update" when the prompt pops up on iTunes  not "restore iPhone." The latter option will indeed wipe all content from your device.
              </p>

               <p>
                <img alt="iTunes" class="" data-caption="" data-credit-label="" data-credit-link="" data-credit-name="Mashable" data-credit-provider="Custom type" data-fragment="m!7d4f" data-image="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzA5LzE3L2VkL2lUdW5lczEuYmNmZTkuanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/345ab947/238/iTunes1.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="1280" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzA5LzE3L2VkL2lUdW5lczEuYmNmZTkuanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/345ab947/238/iTunes1.jpg" title="iTunes"/>
               </p>
               <div class="image-credit">
                <p>
                 Image:  Mashable
                </p>
               </div>
              </figure>
              <p>
               For those who haven't downloaded the software yet, we recommend
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/09/17/what-to-do-before-downloading-ios-8/">
                backing up your data via iCloud
               </a>
               , among other tips.
              </p>
              <p>
               If anything, the 5.7GB requirement to update to iOS 8 is a subtle nudge from Apple that you may need to splurge for the 64GB device next time.
              </p>

                    now has a Safari extension so iOS users no longer have to rely on the cumbersome bookmarklet to send articles to their accounts. And when youre using Pocket itself, you can share what youre reading to other apps like Twitter or Evernote with one touch. The app also takes advantage of iOS handoff features so you can easily switch between iOS devices (and eventually OS X) without losing your place.
                   </p>

                     finance app
                    </a>

                     OmniFocus 2

This allows you to pin anything while browsing, whether it be a photo, article, recipe or video.
