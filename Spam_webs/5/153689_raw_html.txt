          <p>
           Residents raised their coal seam gas-related health concerns with Premier Mike Baird during and after the Narellan Chamber of Commerce breakfast meeting at Camden Civic Centre on Friday morning.
          </p>
          <p>
           During a question and answer opportunity at the breakfast one resident challenged Mr Baird about his governments reaction to a recent gas leak at a Spring Farm coal seam gas well.
          </p>
          <p>
           The resident asked Mr Baird if the well in question, which was about 200metres from homes, would be decommissioned.
          </p>
          <p>
           No you can't do that, Mr Baird told the chamber of commerce meeting. That's one of the challenges.
          </p>
          <p>
           In a legal system where you are legally given entry, legally make investments ...to have that retrospective change, that is a massive challenge around the world.
          </p>
          <p>
           Mr Baird told the meeting the government would act if there are any health concerns even with those existing licences.
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p>
           After the meeting, Spring Farm local Danielle Hodges delivered a letter signed by 20 Spring Farm residents concerned about the coal seam gas leak near their homes.
          </p>
          <p>
           More than a week has passed since AGLs coal seam gas well leaked uncontrollably for hours near our homes but the NSW Government hasbeen silent, Ms Hodges said.
          </p>
          <p>
           We have written to Premier Baird, Resources Minister Anthony Roberts and our local MP Chris Patterson to ask that they investigate and decommission the well that was leaking gas at the end of our street, she said.
