       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Agriculture Pest Detection Dogs  Meet Linus
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2011-07-12T15:19:09+00:00">
         July 12, 2011
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://twolittlecavaliers.com/author/admin" rel="author">
           Felissa Elfenbein
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://twolittlecavaliers.com/2011/07/agriculture-pest-detection-dogs-meet-linus.html#comments">
          1 Comment
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="8141761" data-link="http://twolittlecavaliers.com/2011/07/agriculture-pest-detection-dogs-meet-linus.html" data-summary="" data-title="Agriculture Pest Detection Dogs - Meet Linus">
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3686" style="width: 510px">
         <a href="http://twolittlecavaliers.com/2011/07/agriculture-pest-detection-dogs-meet-linus.html/dont-pack-a-pest" rel="attachment wp-att-3686">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-3686" height="413" src="http://twolittlecavaliers.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/Dont-Pack-a-Pest.jpg" title="Don't Pack a Pest" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Linus the Beagle for the Don't Pack a Pest Campaign
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services, in  partnership with the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA), U.S.  Department of Homeland Security (DHS) Customs and Border Protection  (CBP) and Miami-Dade County, launched the Dont Pack a Pest public  awareness campaign today, with a public service announcement, billboards  and dioramas on display at the Miami International Airport.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont Pack a Pest will help us inform travelers about the potential  consequences foreign pests or diseases can have on Floridas agriculture  industry and our economy, said Commissioner Putnam. It is important  for travelers to declare their agricultural products for inspection so  that we can protect our nations food supply.
        </p>
        <p>
         Because of its climate, geography and crop diversity, combined with  the multiple international air and marine ports throughout the state,  Florida faces a high risk for the introduction of pests and diseases. At  least one pest or disease is introduced into Florida every month,  including pests that are new to Florida, new to the continent or new to  the hemisphere.
        </p>
        <p>
         The agriculture detector dog program started in  1984 at Los Angeles International Airport with one team consisting of a  beagle and a handler. Beagles were selected because of their strong  sense of smell and their gentle nature with people.
        </p>
        <p>
         A Beagles natural love of food makes them effective detectives and happy to work for treats. These dogs are  bright, inquisitive, active hounds whose sense of smell makes them  curious wanderers by nature. They also serve as ambassadors for the  importance of agricultural quarantine work, making hundreds of  appearances yearly. The Beagle Brigade works in airports inspecting passenger luggage looking for prohibited fruit, meat and plant material.
        </p>
        <p>
         Larger  breeds are used to work airport cargo areas and the main distribution  centers for international mail. These larger dogs also work at northern  and southern land border stations inspecting luggage and cargo from  trucks, buses and vehicles. There are  currently 115 agriculture detector dog teams around the United States  primarily located at international airports, seaports, land border ports  of entry and international mail facilities.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3687" style="width: 510px">
         <a href="http://twolittlecavaliers.com/2011/07/agriculture-pest-detection-dogs-meet-linus.html/dontpackapest_luggage_tag" rel="attachment wp-att-3687">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-3687" height="310" src="http://twolittlecavaliers.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/DontPackaPest_luggage_tag.jpg" title="DontPackaPest_luggage_tag" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Campaign Luggage Tag
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Request your luggage tag today by emailing dpihelpline{@}freshfromflorida.com. Its really a cute tag with Linus on it and all the information you will need to remember when traveling overseas.
        </p>
