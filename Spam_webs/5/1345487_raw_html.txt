       <p>
        <em>
         Fried Rice a simple meal perfect for using up leftovers in your fridge!
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="fried rice" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-908" height="400" src="http://www.tablefortwoblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/fried-rice-11.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Fried rice, the staple Chinese take-out entree or the staple filler in Hibachi restaurants. Ive been eating homemade fried rice since I was a little tyke. Its a super easy dish to make and I always make it whenever I have leftover rice and veggies. I literally throw everything thats about to go bad into the dish and call it a meal. Or, like last night, when I was lazy, I decided to make fried rice for dinner since it only takes 10 minutes. Well, another 15 or so if you have rice took cook but thats just popping it in the rice cooker and hanging out until its done.
       </p>
       <p>
        The way I make fried rice might be different than what everyone else thinks of fried rice. Ive always had it numerous ways. Whenever my mom made it, she would throw in whatever veggies she had on hand and whatever leftover white rice we had sitting in the fridge. Thats where I got the influence of making fried rice. To me, fried rice is always different. In the sense of ingredients. Sometimes it can have carrots in it, sometimes it will be meatless (like mine was last night), sometimes itll have deli meat thats about to expire. However, the
        <em>
         process
        </em>
        of making fried rice is always the same and its how my mom and I have always cooked it.
       </p>
       <p>
        I hope you make some yourself so you can enjoy the deliciousness of Chinese take-out in your own home. Its loads healthier too  no MSG and you can tailor the sodium &amp; oil levels yourself!
       </p>
       <p>
        Also: if youre wondering, metal chopsticks?  yeah, I know its weird &amp; you dont see that often, but we dont like wooden chopsticks because they warp after theyve been washed and used for a handful of times and theyre more unsanitary. And we arent going to go buy disposable, individually wrapped wooden chopsticks since thatd be a waste :)
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.tablefortwoblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/fried-rice-21.jpg">
         <img alt="fried rice" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-909" height="400" src="http://www.tablefortwoblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/fried-rice-21.jpg" title="fried-rice-2" width="600"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Note: mine was meatless but you can add whatever meats you have on hand. Ive made this with deli ham diced in cubes, Ive made this with shredded chicken, Ive made mine without eggs before. Its a really versatile dish so be creative! :)
        <br/>
       </p>
       <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">

         <div class="ERSAuthor">
          Author:

           Julie Wampler
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSHead">
          Recipe type:
          <span itemprop="recipeCategory">
           Main Entree

         </div>
         <div class="ERSHead">
          Cuisine:
          <span itemprop="recipeCuisine">
           Chinese
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTimes">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSHead">
          Serves:
          <span itemprop="recipeYield">
           4
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="ERSIngredients">
         <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
          Ingredients:
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           2 cups uncooked white rice
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           3 large eggs
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 cup frozen corn
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 cup frozen peas
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 medium onion, diced
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            cup low sodium soy sauce
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            tsp. pepper
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           2 tbsp. vegetable oil
          </li>
         </ul>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="ERSInstructions">
         <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
          Instructions:
         </div>
         <ol>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Cook your rice however you usually prep it. Or, if you have leftover rice, take that out of the fridge and warm it up a bit to get it soft.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           In a large skillet, cook your eggs (break them apart like scrambled eggs), corn, peas and onions in vegetable oil until the onions are translucent. Add in a couple pinches of salt and pepper. If you have meats in your fried rice, you can cook them with this step. Just make sure they're small cube/bite-size pieces to keep the size of the ingredients relatively the same.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Add in the rice. Keep folding it with the veggies until well-combined. If your rice is hard (from the fridge), try to let it sit in the warm skillet for a little bit and then break it up. You want to make sure there are no big lumps of rice.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Pour the soy sauce over the rice. You might want to do this slowly in case it gets too salty for you or in case the rice doesn't soak up all the soy sauce (at this point, it would mean you need more rice). I'd pour it in 1 circle around the skillet at a time and then mix it altogether and see if there is any rice that's still white.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Continue mixing it together until all incorporated and you see no white rice kernels. Sprinkle pepper on top and mix together one last time. Taste it to make sure it's to your liking.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Serve hot and enjoy!
          </li>
         </ol>

           <p>
            Since Ive been making fried rice for years (and its BETTER than any chinese restaurant) heres a tips from an old pro.
           </p>
           <p>
            1.) Use a rice cooker for the rice, then let it chill in the refrigerator for 1-2 days. This gets rid of extra moisture AND for the best flavor, THE RICE MUST BE COLD when you fry it. It absorbs the flavors MUCH better!
           </p>
           <p>
            2.) Use sesame oil to fry it with. This is the major KEY ingredient for a perfect flavorful, sumptuous fried rice!
           </p>
           <p>
            Try it this way and tell me you cant tell the difference! And those two key things will make a difference between good fried rice and OMG! fried rice!
           </p>
           <p>
            Enjoy!
