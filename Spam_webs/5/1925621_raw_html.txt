       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Blood Orange Polenta Cake
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-date">
          <a href="http://elsbro.com/blog/2015/02/16/blood-orange-polenta-cake/" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-02-16T23:45:02+00:00">
            16 February, 2015
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://elsbro.com/blog/author/admin/" rel="author">
            Elsa Brobbey
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="467" src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7379/16480184315_25d533887d_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="700"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im crazy about blood oranges right now, absolutely in love with them!
         <br/>
         I started preparing for their arrival by collecting recipes weeks before they appeared at my local grocery store.
        </p>
        <p>
         I believe nature truly gives us citrus in winter to help colour and brighten our long dark days, and vibrant
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blood_orange" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blood_orange', 'blood oranges']);" target="_blank">
          blood oranges
         </a>
         are our greatest reward.
        </p>
        <p>
         While theyre in season, I grab a couple every time Im at the store; I cant get enough of their deeply red tones, robust flavour and fragrance. Theres just something dramatic and special about them
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="467" src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7435/15857658494_1f5e6f97b3_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="700"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="521" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8646/16478432671_3c5ec37362_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="348"/>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="521" src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7403/16454181226_df8309b771_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="348"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive eaten a lot of blood oranges this year!
        </p>
        <p>
         I snack on them, juice them, cook with them and throw them in salads; a few weeks ago, I put a few splashes into a vinaigrette for this
         <a href="http://instagram.com/p/ytctU1AuT7/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://instagram.com/p/ytctU1AuT7/', 'this cauliflower and chickpea salad']);" target="_blank">
          this cauliflower and chickpea salad
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         With its relatively short season, Ive been trying to find more recipes that showcase their beauty and bounty, so on weekends, I bake and cook with them when Im not busy doing other things because I want to eat all the blood oranges I can before its season is over!
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span id="more-17772">
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="1050" src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7450/16454142516_9fb6eed2d9_h.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="700"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="467" src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7446/15860043823_052d579403_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="700"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Since
         <a href="http://elsbro.com/blog/2013/08/08/sweet-corn-polenta-with-toasted-coconut/" target="_blank">
          I love polenta,
         </a>
         and Im having this moment with blood oranges, it was inevitable that Id find a way to put these two together.
        </p>
        <p>
         I made this blood orange polenta cake a few weeks ago when a family member was feeling poorly, I hoped itll brighten their day and bring them much needed cheer.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is the kind of cake you bake on a quiet Saturday afternoon just before your evening starts; its a moment for peaceful contemplation as you measure, slice, mix and finally bake.
        </p>
        <p>
         Or make it on a Sunday morning when everyone is sleeping in, youll get to savour the silence and gratitude as delicious aromas of baking wakes up the house.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="467" src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7282/16478404021_2c969b494b_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="700"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="521" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8592/16294255907_a089575349_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="348"/>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="521" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8591/15860046103_78752bec68_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="348"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="467" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8609/16454169876_f74d2b44f2_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="700"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is a beautiful cake, not just because its good-looking but figuratively because its infused with loving ingredients citrus, coconut milk, agave, almond milk, stone-ground polenta and white whole-wheat flour.
        </p>
        <p>
         It takes little effort to put together and its wonderfully crumbly with a moist interior and a sweet crispiness from the cornmeal, its also fruity, the blood orange has a tangy, rich marmalade-like flavour that makes me wish Id loaded the bottom with more slices.
        </p>
        <p>
         This cake does bring the cheer; it goes perfectly with coffee or tea, its good on its own too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="467" src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7284/16454172386_32e0a9d5a5_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="700"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blood Orange Polenta Cake" height="467" src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7324/15857656704_2e216141ff_c.jpg" title="Blood Orange Polenta Cake | Elsa Brobbey" width="700"/>
        </p>

          <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="0">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            INGREDIENTS
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             1 -2 blood oranges, thinly sliced crosswise with pith and seeds removed
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             1 cup whole-wheat flour (I used white whole-wheat flour)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             1 cup polenta/cornmeal (fine or medium ground)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
             2 tablespoons blood orange zest
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
             1 teaspoon baking powder
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
             1/2 teaspoon baking soda
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="6">
             1/2 teaspoons salt
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="7">
             1 cup almond milk
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="8">
             1/2 cup agave or maple syrup
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="9">
             1/4 cup blood orange juice
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="10">
             1/3 cup melted coconut oil
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="11">
             1 teaspoon pure orange extract, optional
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="12">
             1 teaspoon vanilla extract
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="1">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            DIRECTIONS
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
             Preheat your oven to 350 degrees F.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">
             Grease 9-inch round cake pan and line bottom with parchment paper
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">
             Arrange orange slices at the bottom of the parchment-lined pan in a single layer and set aside
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">
             In a large bowl, combine flour, polenta, orange zest, baking powder, baking soda and salt, and mix
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="4">
             In a separate bowl, whisk together milk, agave, orange juice, and coconut oil, add orange and vanilla extracts
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="5">
             Pour milk mixture into dry ingredients and use a spatula to mix until well combined but do not over mix
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="6">
             Pour batter into prepared pan and bake for 30  35 minutes or until toothpick inserted into the center of cake comes out clean
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="7">
             Remove from oven and let it cool for 2 minutes in the pan
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="8">
             Invert cake onto cooling rack and allow to cool slightly before serving
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="9">
             Enjoy!
            </li>
           </ol>
