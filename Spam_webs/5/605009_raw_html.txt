       <h1 id="post-headline">
        <a href="http://www.gcministry.com/the-feasts-of-the-lord-feast-of-tabernacles/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to The Feasts of The Lord-Feast of Tabernacles">
         The Feasts of The Lord-Feast of Tabernacles
        </a>
       </h1>
       <p class="postmeta">
        January 17th, 2011 |
        <a href="http://www.gcministry.com/the-feasts-of-the-lord-feast-of-tabernacles/#respond" title="Comment on The Feasts of The Lord-Feast of Tabernacles">

       </p>

        </a>

        <p>
         The last feast of the Lord is the Feast of Tabernacles. This feast was originally established by God as a reminder to the Israelites as a reminder of His provisions during their forty years in the barren desert. All were commanded to live in booths or huts during this week long festival. It was one of the three pilgrimage holidays were all Jewish men were required to appear at the temple. The festival began just five days after the solemn day of Yom Kippur.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-40">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         It was during this time the Israelites celebrated the in-gathering of all their crops. The Harvest was finally complete. Gods provision for the Israelites was celebrated. They would share in the bounty of the harvest.
        </p>
        <p>
         Prophetically the holiday speaks of a time when God will once again tabernacle with mankind. It will be during this future time that God will establish His messianic Kingdom. The earth will be returned to the amazing paradise found before the fall of mankind in the garden.
        </p>
        <p>
         All of Gods feasts are a time of celebration of the Goodness of God. They are not times of Man to God but celebrate Gods gifts and blessings on man. They are to be cherished and celebrated by all believers.
        </p>
