            <h1 class="title dark" itemprop="title headline">
             Almost Genius: A Cheap, Flat-Pack, Recyclable Bike Helmet
            </h1>
            <div class="deck fc-deck" itemprop="deck description">
             <p>
              A new bike-helmet concept is a one-way ticket to a vegetative state.
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="byline">
             By
             <a class="navigate" data-resource-type="user" href="/user/cliff-kuang" itemprop="byline" rel="author">
              Cliff Kuang
             </a>
            </div>
           </header>
           <div class="row collapse">
            <div class="body prose article-prose twelve columns" itemprop="body">
             <p>
              <img alt="tatoo" border="0" class="float-center" src="http://images.fastcompany.com/upload/tatoo02.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              How cool is this? The
              <a href="http://www.abitare.it/selected-by-abitare/tatoo-helmet/">
               Tatoo Helmet
              </a>
              , designed by
              <a href="http://www.julienbergignat.com/tatoo.php?current=19" target="_blank">
               Julien Bergignat
              </a>
              and
              <a href="http://patricemouille.com/" target="_blank">
               Patrice Mouille
              </a>
              , is meant to be an easily stored, totally recyclable bike helmet.
             </p>
             <p>
              "Tatoo" is derived from tatou, the French word for "armadillo." And that's what the design evokes. Rather than a single carapace, it comprises several polypropylene strips, which come into shape after the joints are clicked together:
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="tatoo" border="0" class="float-center" src="http://images.fastcompany.com/upload/tatoo05.jpg"/>
              And actually, the entire helmet is made of polypropylene, so that it's readily recyclable. When you're not wearing it, it can be packed flat for storage:
             </p>
             <p class="center">
              <!--object-->
              <object height="450" width="600">
               <param name="allowfullscreen" value="true">
               </param>
               <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
               </param>
               <param name="movie" value="http://vimeo.com/moogaloop.swf?clip_id=8653179&amp;server=vimeo.com&amp;show_title=1&amp;show_byline=1&amp;show_portrait=0&amp;color=&amp;fullscreen=1">
               </param>
               <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="450" src="http://vimeo.com/moogaloop.swf?clip_id=8653179&amp;server=vimeo.com&amp;show_title=1&amp;show_byline=1&amp;show_portrait=0&amp;color=&amp;fullscreen=1" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="600">
               </embed>
              </object>
              <!--object-->
             </p>
             <p>
              Now, folding helmets can be ingeniousfor example, the
              <a href="http://www.tatamet.com/" target="_blank">
               Tatamat
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              This one, however, is totally ridiculous, and neglects the way bike helmets actually work. It's not simply that they're paddedthe padding inside a bike helmet is mostly for wearer comfort, rather than protection. Rather, helmets protect you because they're monolithic and rigidthat allows the force of an impact to transmitted along their length, rather than directly into your head. They're made of foam both to be lightweight and so that they can easily crackjust like a racecar, they're meant to break-up upon impact, to further disperse kinetic energy.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              When wearing the Tatoo, you might feel the warm glow of future-forward design. You also won't feel your legs, after you're paralyzed.
             </p>
             <p>
              For more examples of Almost Genius, click
              <a href="http://www.fastcompany.com/tag/almost-genius" target="_blank">
               here
              </a>
              .
             </p>
