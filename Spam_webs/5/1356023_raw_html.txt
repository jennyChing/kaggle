      <p>
       Ive been using Digg Digg plugin for quite some time and found it very useful. It allows one click submission to various social media.
      </p>
      <p>
       Today, out of nowehere, I start seeing the following error on one of my blogs.
      </p>
      <p>
       Warning: file_get_contents() [function.file-get-contents]: URL file-access is disabled in the server configuration in /wp-content/plugins/digg-digg/dd.class.php on line 693
      </p>
      <p>
       Warning: file_get_contents(http://feeds.delicious.com/v2/json/urlinfo/data?url=.. [function.file-get-contents]: failed to open stream: no suitable wrapper could be found in ../wp-content/plugins/digg-digg/dd.class.php on line 693
      </p>
      <p>
       This blog is hosted on
       <a href="http://1and1.co.uk/xml/init/?k_id=14165023" target="_blank" title="1&amp;1 Web Hosting">
        <strong>
         1&amp;1 web hosting
        </strong>
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Digg Digg plugin works fine on rest of my hosts which are hosted on dedicated servers,
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.bluehost.com/track/olena/text1" target="_blank" title="Bluehost">
         Bluehost
        </a>
       </strong>
       ,
       <strong>
        <a href="http://secure.hostgator.com/cgi-bin/affiliates/clickthru.cgi?id=akslltd" target="_blank" title="Hostgator">
         Hostgator
        </a>
       </strong>
       etc.
      </p>
      <p>
       It was quite strange.
      </p>
      <p>
       The error clearly says that Host doesnt allow remote connection? Weired.
      </p>
      <p>
       Which means fopen is not turned on? That is certainly not the case as Ive been using 1&amp;1 since ages and in many other plugins and applications fopen works.
      </p>
      <p>
       Next solution was to turn on allow_url_fopen in php.ini
      </p>
      <p>
       ;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;
      </p>
      <div id="_mcePaste">
       ; Fopen wrappers ;
      </div>
      <div id="_mcePaste">
       ;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;
      </div>
      <div id="_mcePaste">
       ; Whether to allow the treatment of URLs (like http:// or ftp://) as files.
      </div>
      <div id="_mcePaste">
       allow_url_fopen = On
      </div>
      <p>
       Which is already On.
      </p>
      <p>
       So what could be the problem now?
      </p>
