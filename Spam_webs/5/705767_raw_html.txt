        <hr/>
        <p>
         Score a point. Level up. Win a badge. Millennials and many members of Generation X were raised on video games and have been trained to seek gratification from immediate, minor rewards that acknowledge their achievements. Because of this, some businesses have integrated gamification into their day-to-day operationsin an effort to drive productivity and improve company morale.
        </p>
        <p>
         PCWorld recently reported on an IDC study on gamification that highlighted the method's success at
         <a href="http://www.pcworld.com/article/2047564/gamification-using-play-to-motivate-employees-and-engage-customers.html" target="_blank">
          engaging executives in professional development programs
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         The study found that many users were sometimes unlocking as many as 30 achievements, and that participation increasedby greater than 35 percent on a weekly basis.
        </p>
        <p>
         "Savvy executives should explore ways to experiment with this strategy, using pilot projects to gain information about how their enterprise might best use gamification," said the report's author, Michael Fauscette, group vice president at IDC Software Business Solutions, according to PCWorld.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          It's about more than just the badge
         </strong>
         <br/>
         However, PCWorld highlighted the fact that badges and achievements alone will not drive user participation in gamifiedprograms. Daniel Debow, senior vice president of Salesforce.com's Work.com group, mentioned that it works best at encouraging greater participation in an activity. People still need to be invested in the action or initiative in order for gamification to have an effect.
        </p>
        <p>
         That said, businesses should not implement gamified programs, apps or activities without some planning. VentureBeat recently highlighted that
         <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2013/08/28/8-in-10-gamification-projects-may-suck-but-gamification-leader-badgeville-plans-to-change-that/?utm_source=feedburner&amp;utm_medium=feed&amp;utm_campaign=Feed%3A+Venturebeat+(VentureBeat)" target="_blank">
          eight in 10 gamification apps fail
         </a>
         . They are often poorly designed or included with a bad product. When well-crafted gamified products are introduced, though, they have the potential to drive product engagement, as occurred with EMC's community network, according to VentureBeat.
        </p>
        <p>
         For organizations contemplating gamification, employing
         <a href="http://teksystems.com/services/it-staffing-solutions/applications-staffing-services">
          applications consultants
         </a>
         canhelp them find someone already familiar with the process.
        </p>
