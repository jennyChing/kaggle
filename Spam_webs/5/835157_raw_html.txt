          <h1>
           Soft Tissue Injuries
          </h1>
          <hr/>
          A soft tissue injury often occurs from a strain, sprain, tear, or direct blow, causing damage to a body muscle, ligament, or tendon. When this type of injury occurs, bruising, swelling, inflammation, and/or bleeding may occur. To expedite the healing process, minimize tissue damage, and reduce inflammation, the treatment of choice recommended by medical providers is
          <strong>
           RICE
          </strong>
          , meaning: Rest, Ice, Compression, and Elevation
          <strong>
           .
          </strong>
          <br/>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="stacks_out" id="stacks_out_424_page0">
         <div class="stacks_in com_yourhead_stack_4Columns_stack" id="stacks_in_424_page0">
          <div class="stacks_div stacks_column_left stacks_column_4">
           <div class="stacks_out" id="stacks_out_429_page0">
            <div class="stacks_in text_stack" id="stacks_in_429_page0">
             <h3>
              Rest
             </h3>
             <hr/>
             <i>
              Rest.
             </i>
             or stopping the activity during which your injury or accident occurred. Rest is absolutely vital to protect soft tissue (such as skeletal muscle and ligaments). It is important to avoid any activity which could exacerbate the injury in order for healing to take place-  such as bearing weight on the leg or arm which may have been injured.
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="stacks_div stacks_column_left stacks_column_4">
           <div class="stacks_out" id="stacks_out_418_page0">
            <div class="stacks_in text_stack" id="stacks_in_418_page0">
             <h3>
              Ice
             </h3>
             <hr/>
             <i>
              Ice.
             </i>
             Apply ice or a cold pack immediately following the injury. Doing so minimizes swelling that occurs when the blood vessels dilate to supply extra blood to the injured area. Ice or a cold pack should only be applied for 20 minutes, 3 or 4 times per day. Please note, ice should not be applied directly to the skin. Doing so may damage the skin.
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="stacks_div stacks_column_right stacks_column_4">
           <div class="stacks_out" id="stacks_out_422_page0">
            <div class="stacks_in text_stack" id="stacks_in_422_page0">
             <h3>
              Elevation
             </h3>
             <hr/>
             <i>
              Elevation.
             </i>
             Raise the affected limb at or above the level of your heart is important to for proper body circulation and blood flow, as well as to keep swelling under control. A common way to elevate an injured arm or leg would be to use one or more pillows, high enough to ensure that the injured body part is equal to or above the level to the heart.
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="stacks_div stacks_column_right stacks_column_4">
           <div class="stacks_out" id="stacks_out_420_page0">
            <div class="stacks_in text_stack" id="stacks_in_420_page0">
             <h3>
              Compression
             </h3>
             <hr/>
             <i>
              Compression.
             </i>
             or wrapping the injured area tightly with an elastic bandage aims to reduce swelling to the injured area. Refrain from wrapping the bandage too tightly, as doing so may prevent adequate circulation to the injury. Signs of poor circulation include numbness, tingling, increased pain, or swelling in areas below or near the bandage.
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>

           <br/>
           *SEEK EMERGENCY MEDICAL ATTENTION If swelling and pain persist past more than a day of utilizing the RICE method, notify your healthcare provider, as a more serious problem may be present.
          </strong>
