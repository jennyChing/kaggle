      <header>
       <h1>
        The Beginners Guide to Crochet  Part 3
       </h1>
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </header>
      <section class="entry fix">
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #ec119a;">
         The thirdpart of The Beginners Guide to Crochetis here! Today youll be learning to work in rows &amp; turning your project!
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="the beginners guide to crochet part 3" class="aligncenter wp-image-1674 size-full" height="1102" src="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Beginners-3.png" title="the beginners guide to crochet part 3" width="735"/>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Today youll need to grab your hook &amp; yarn again!
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        As last time the type of hook or yarn doesnt matter, but a big hook and thick yarn will be the best. Iwill be going through hook sizes &amp; yarn weights in another blog post, but for now dont worry about that too much.
       </p>
       <p>
        What youll learn today is working in rows &amp; turning your project. This is a key element to understanding how to crochet. After you have learned the
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/blog/beginners-guide-crochet-part-1/" title="The Beginners Guide to Crochet  Part 1">
         Chain
        </a>
        &amp;
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/blog/beginners-guide-crochet-part-2/" title="The Beginners Guide to Crochet  Part 2">
         Single Crochet
        </a>
        you can make a bigger project. Crocheting back &amp; forth in rows is one of the ways a crochet project is made.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         I have split this in 4steps:
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        1.Continuing from last time 
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/blog/beginners-guide-crochet-part-2/" title="The Beginners Guide to Crochet  Part 2">
         The Beginners Guide to Crochet  Part 2
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        2.Turning your project
       </p>
       <p>
        4.Starting a row.
       </p>
       <p>
        5.Continuing with Single Crochet (Sc) along the row
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lets start!
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #ec119a;">
         1.Continuing from last time 
         <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/blog/beginners-guide-crochet-part-2/" title="The Beginners Guide to Crochet  Part 2">
          The Beginners Guide to Crochet  Part 2.
         </a>
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        By now you have made a *chain row* with 10 chains. You have crocheted your *Single Crochet* (Sc) in the next stitches.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows1-400.png">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-1677 size-full" height="266" src="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows1-400.png" title="the beginners guide to crochet part 2" width="400"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows2-400.png">
         <img alt="the beginners guide to crochet part 3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1678" height="266" src="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows2-400.png" width="400"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        You are ready to turn your project.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #ec119a;">
         2. Turning you project
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        This is quite easy  but this is also a place you might get confused in the beginning.
        <br/>
        The thing that happens when you are turning the project is that you will go back and crochet a new stitch in every stitch you just have crocheted (previous row).
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows3-400.png">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-1680 size-full" height="266" src="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows3-400.png" title="the beginners guide to crochet part 2" width="400"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows4-400.png">
         <img alt="the beginners guide to crochet part 3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1681" height="266" src="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows4-400.png" width="400"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        You will hold your crochet hook &amp; yarn in the same place. The project will be the thing that is turning (or flipping) forward to the left.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #ec119a;">
         3. Starting a row
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        When you are starting a row you will in most crochet pattern do one or more chains as the first stitch (in place of). The number of chains is often given in the crochet pattern. To make the turn look the best you willuse the number of chains that fits the height of the crochet stitch you are using. For *Single Crochet* one *Chain* is most common.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows5-400.png">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-1682 size-full" height="266" src="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows5-400.png" title="the beginners guide to crochet part 2" width="400"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        Start your row by *chain* one.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #ec119a;">
         4.Continuing with Single Crochet (Sc) along the row
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows6-400.png">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-1683 size-full" height="266" src="http://joyofmotion.no/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/workinrows6-400.png" title="the beginners guide to crochet part 2" width="400"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        Now you will continue with
        <a href="http://joyofmotion.no/blog/beginners-guide-crochet-part-2/" title="The Beginners Guide to Crochet  Part 2">
         *Single Crochet*
        </a>
        along the row.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        When you come to the end you will repeat from point number 2, &amp; continue like this.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
       </p>
       <div class="woo-sc-hr">
       </div>
       With this knowledge you can easily make a headband with *Single Crochet* or even a blanket with just colorful squares.
       <h2>
       </h2>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <strong>
         This is all youll learn today! Now it is time to practice!
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        Watch out for Part-4 of The Beginners Guide to Crochet that is coming soon!
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        This part will be about how to increase &amp; decrease when crocheting.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        This blog post will be the last of  The Beginners Guide to Crochet &amp; in the end you will be able to make a crocheted sweater. I will be sharing this sweater with you really soon to.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
