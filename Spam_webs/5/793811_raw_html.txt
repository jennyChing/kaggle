         Stained Glass Turkeys + Free Printable {Kid Craft}

         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

        <p>
         <a href="http://gluedtomycraftsblog.com/2014/11/stained-glass-turkeys-free-printable-kid-craft.html/stained_glass_turkey_cover_1" rel="attachment wp-att-2677">

        </p>
        <p>
         Happy Monday readers! Boy do I have a TON of kid friendly Thanksgiving crafts to share with you all! The next few days Ill be sharing several different ways to craft up a turkey with your child, in honor of the upcoming holiday. {Gobble Gobble!} Im kick starting the fun with these Stained Glass Turkeys and they are just as easy as my past tutorials. I even made a FREE printable to go along with this craft, so all you need to do is download, print and craft away. No need to think out designs or wonder what the heck did I just draw? Now lets grab our supplies and make up these beauties  shall we?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://gluedtomycraftsblog.com/2014/11/stained-glass-turkeys-free-printable-kid-craft.html/stained_glass_turkey_2" rel="attachment wp-att-2670">
          <img alt="stained_glass_turkey_2" class="aligncenter wp-image-2670" height="400" src="http://gluedtomycraftsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/stained_glass_turkey_2-1024x682.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h1>
         <em>
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #00ccff;">
            Supplies:
           </span>
          </strong>
         </em>
        </h1>
        <p>
         * Free Printable {
         <a href="https://app.box.com/s/d7n9fffs266kbvsllzv0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Turkey Body Printable
         </a>
         
         <a href="https://app.box.com/s/z26rac8h7ttg7w3iajxw" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Turkey Feather Printable
         </a>
         }
         <br/>
         * White Cardstock/Printer
         <br/>
         * Contact Paper* Tissue Paper {yellow, red, orange, brown}
         <br/>
         * Scissors
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://gluedtomycraftsblog.com/2014/11/stained-glass-turkeys-free-printable-kid-craft.html/stained_glass_turkey_1" rel="attachment wp-att-2669">
          <img alt="stained_glass_turkey_1" class="aligncenter wp-image-2669" height="400" src="http://gluedtomycraftsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/stained_glass_turkey_1-1024x682.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>

          <img alt="stained_glass_turkey_3" class="aligncenter wp-image-2671" height="400" src="http://gluedtomycraftsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/stained_glass_turkey_3-1024x682.jpg" width="600"/>

          <img alt="stained_glass_turkey_5" class="aligncenter wp-image-2673" height="400" src="http://gluedtomycraftsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/stained_glass_turkey_5-1024x682.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>

         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <h1>
         <span style="color: #00ccff;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            Directions:
           </strong>
          </em>
         </span>
        </h1>
        <p>
         * First download and print the FREE printables {
         <a href="https://app.box.com/s/d7n9fffs266kbvsllzv0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          1
         </a>
         &amp;
         <a href="https://app.box.com/s/z26rac8h7ttg7w3iajxw" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          2
         </a>
         }
         <br/>
         * Using your scissors, trim out the turkey pieces. For the feather portion {where the stained glass effort will be} you can cut out different sections {or all} to get various looks. No two turkeys will look alike!
         <br/>
         * Now grab your contact paper and cut two pieces the same length. Take off the protective paper off one and lay it on a flat surface.
         <br/>
         * Lay your turkey cutouts on the contact paper.
         <br/>
         * Grab your tissue paper and cut it into small squares. Now have your child place it on the contact paper, to make a beautiful design.
         <br/>
         * Once done, grab the other piece of contact paper and remove its protective sheet. Then lay it on top of the other piece and press firmly. Make sure every corner is stuck together.
         <br/>
         * To finish off the craft, use your scissors to trim out the turkeys. Then display proudly on a window.
        </p>
        <p>
