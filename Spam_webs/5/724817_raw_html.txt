        <p style="text-align: center;">
         baked, not fried, but still delicious
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          <em>
           Chocolate Old Fashioned
          </em>
         </strong>
         <b>
          <i>
           Doughnuts
          </i>
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Glazed Chocolate Old Fashioned Doughnuts" class="aligncenter" height="727" src="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5232/14239056184_17e9440c58_b.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive never tried to hide the fact that I LOVE doughnuts.
        </p>
        <p>
         I love them SO much.
        </p>
        <p>
         Especially a glazed old fashioned.
        </p>
        <p>
         I try not to think about them, and then when my dad was visiting last week, he surprised the kids with doughnuts for breakfast.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-3440">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Glazed Chocolate Old Fashioned Doughnuts" class="aligncenter" height="900" src="https://farm3.staticflickr.com/2899/14239340385_77f3b35e80_b.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now I know some of you are strong, and can sip happily on your
         <a href="http://www.heathersfrenchpress.com/2014/02/copy-cat-whole-foods-tropical-green-smoothie.html">
          green smoothie
         </a>
         while watching others enjoy delicious, sticky, sweet, fried dough. Hooray for you. I am
         <em>
          not
         </em>
         that person. If I see them, I must eat them. Just staring at that pink bakery box is enough to get my stomach growling.
        </p>
        <p>
         I enjoyed my glazed old fashioned with just a hint of guilt, but unfortunately have been thinking about it ever since.
        </p>
        <p>
         I could have made these glazed copycat
         <a href="http://www.heathersfrenchpress.com/2013/02/the-best-ever-glazed-doughnuts-a-k-a-copycat-krispy-kreme.html">
          krispy kreme doughnuts,
         </a>
         but I really was not in the mood to fry {de-greasing the kitchen was not how I wanted to spend my night}
        </p>
        <p>
         So I decided to try and make old fashioned {my favorite} in a baked version, and then I made them chocolate just cause, and of course they had to get a glaze bath.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Baked Chocolate Old Fashioned Dooughnuts" class="aligncenter" height="900" src="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5199/14239056344_00411c81d7_b.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         You dont need to tell me they dont taste exactly the the real deal, Im well aware of that, but that doesnt mean they arent delicious.
        </p>
        <p>
         They are, and because theyre baked and not fried,
         <a href="https://www.fitbit.com/flex" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          my fitbit
         </a>
         doesnt hate me.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

             Baked Chocolate Old Fashioned Doughnuts

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 1/4 cups flour {I used King Arthur AP flour}
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tsp baking soda
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 tsp nutmeg
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 tsp salt
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/3 cup sugar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/3 cup buttermilk
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 egg
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 Tbsp butter {melted}
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
             For the Glaze -
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup of powdered sugar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12" itemprop="ingredients">
             3 - 4 Tbsp buttermilk
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-13" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 tsp vanilla
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-14" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-15" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-16" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <ul class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             combine all of the dry ingredients
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             whisk together buttermilk, egg, and butter
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             add to the dry ingredients, and combine
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             knead a few times on a well floured surface
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             roll to about 1/4 inch
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             cut using a doughnut cutter
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             place on a lined baking sheet {use one for the doughnuts and another for the doughnut holes}
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             bake in a pre-heated 350 degree oven - 10 minutes for the doughnuts {4 -5 minutes for the doughnut holes}
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             make the glaze while the doughnuts are baking
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-9" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             whisk together the powdered sugar, 3 Tbsp buttermilk, and the vanilla - adding more milk 1 tsp at a time until you have the desired consistency
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-10" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             cool slightly before glazing
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-11" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             allow the glaze  to dry completely before storing in an airtight container
            </li>
           </ul>
           <div class="ziplist-recipe-plugin" style="display: none;">
            3.1
           </div>
           <a href="http://www.heathersfrenchpress.com/2014/05/baked-chocolate-old-fashioned-doughnuts.html" id="zl-printed-permalink" title="Permalink to Recipe">
            http://www.heathersfrenchpress.com/2014/05/baked-chocolate-old-fashioned-doughnuts.html
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         For the doughnuts you can substitute yogurt or sour cream for the buttermilk, or
         <a href="http://frugalliving.about.com/od/condimentsandspices/r/Buttermilk_Sub.htm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          make your own buttermilk
         </a>
         and you can also use milk or cream for the glaze, whichever you prefer and have on hand.
        </p>
        <p>
         I made 12 doughnuts with 12 doughnut holes, but you could simply roll the dough into balls to make doughnut holes if you dont have doughnut cutter {but I do love my doughnut cutter and it was super cheap}
        </p>
        <p>
         You could even thin the batter just a bit by using more milk/sour cream/ or yogurt and pour into muffin tins to make doughnut muffins {bake for 20 minutes}
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Glazed Chocolate Old Fashioned Doughnuts {Baked}" class="aligncenter" height="900" src="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5040/14236045241_d236da0b8c_b.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy!
        </p>
