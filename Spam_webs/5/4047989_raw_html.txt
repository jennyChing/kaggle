       <div class="post-content-content">
        <p>
         Gday! Just back frommy first trip down to Tasmania to cover the second round of the V8 Supercars championship at Symmons Plains Raceway just outside of Launceston last weekend. Another one of the great opportunities to explore a new circuit, and photograph something fresh.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-2193">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now before I get into a few of my favourite photos from the round, Id like you to check out the articles and photos I put together for MyDriveover the course of the weekend. Theyve got plenty more photos for you to enjoy and updates on how the V8 Supercars championship has progressed.
        </p>

             It was a wet start to the weekend, with Friday morning sessions extremely wet. Whichmade for some unique photo opportunities. I used the water spray and lights to maximum effect to get some interesting photos.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/rvandersyde/16383458874" title="V8 Supercars - Symmons Plains - 2015 by Rhys Vandersyde, on Flickr">

            <p>
             As the weekend progressed the weather improved and I took the opportunity to capture the racing actionwith the picturesque background of the Tasmanian hills and farmland that the track was nestled in. Heading into this round Iwanted to create contrast to the photos Id captured during the previous two rounds, from the two confinedtemporary street circuit layouts.
            </p>

             Lastly, I wanted to share a photo of Matt Hall. He aerial display was sensation as always, but what really got my attentionwas his proximity to the ground on many occasions. I saw him fly under the TV Camera guy on a couple of occasions, not that I managed to get a good photo of it. So heres a shot of him playing with a row a trees.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/rvandersyde/16818122818" title="V8 Supercars - Symmons Plains - 2015 by Rhys Vandersyde, on Flickr">

            <p>
             Sticking with the fitness tracker theme Ive started this season,I didnt cover as many ks at Symmons Plains, being a small track. Butover the course of the 3days of the V8 SupercarsI ended up walking over 38,000steps for a total of 22kilometres.However the benefit of the short Symmons Plain layout, is I still would have clocked up10 laps of the track in that time.
