       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Overtime, Night Shifts Bring Pay Premiums for Some
       </h1>
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
       <div class="beforepostmeta">
        February 11, 2014
        <span>
         |
        </span>
        Categories:
        <a href="http://mthink.mercer.com/category/infographics/" rel="category tag">
         Infographics
        </a>
        <span>
         |
        </span>
        Tags:
        <a href="http://mthink.mercer.com/tag/benefits/" rel="tag">
         Benefits
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://mthink.mercer.com/tag/night-shifts/" rel="tag">
         Night shifts
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://mthink.mercer.com/tag/overtime/" rel="tag">
         Overtime
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Overtime and night-shift pay practices give employers the flexibility to extend work hours for business needs and certain emergencies, while often compensating employees handsomely for their extra efforts. Mercers
         <a href="http://www.imercer.com/wbeg">
          <i>
           2013 Worldwide Benefit &amp; Employment Guidelines
          </i>
         </a>
         sheds light on overtime and night-shift work in 64 countries worldwide, in addition to a broad range of the most pressing issues in mandatory and private benefit practices, statutory regulations, and employment conditions.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whether a company already has a presence in other markets or is investigating new, prime locations, it must know how much leeway it has and what additional costs it might incur in both regular and non-standard business situations, says Samantha Polovina, the Mercer Principal responsible for the report. Overtime and night-shift work are often necessary for businesses to meet shifting demands, schedules, and opportunities, but often companies have not planned for the statutory limitations and business costs.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          <i>
           Take a look at overtime and night-shift pay practices worldwide.
          </i>
         </b>
        </p>
