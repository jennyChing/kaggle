        <p>
         So I had this favorite pair of socks that I loved. They were fabulous. I liked them so much I took them on vacation with us several years ago. Unfortunately, only one came home. Im Lisa Mabey from
         <a href="http://www.mabeyshemadeit.com" target="_blank">
          Mabey She Made It
         </a>
         , and apparently I have a sock saga to share with you.
        </p>
        <p>
         But can I get rid of this lone sock? No. Im not even a sentimental person, really. My practical side usually wins out, and I move on. But even though it was lost literally on the other side of the world (in South Africa), I couldnt bring myself to throw it away. Side note: I dont know why, but Ive lost some of my favorite things in foreign countriesa mitten in Ireland, the other mitten in England, this sock, etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         So if I cant get rid of this sock, I decided to at least DO something with it. So here it isa little Sock Snowman with a striped sweater thats pretty darn cute. And now I dont have to feel guilty about a lone sock in my drawer. I followed this tutorial for a
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/pin/131871095314586029/" target="_blank">
          Sock Snowmen from Then She Made
         </a>
         .
        </p>

        </p>

         And within a few minutes and just a few things I already had around the house, I created two little snowmen that look super cute and would even make a fun kids crafttheyd just need a little help with the hot glue.
