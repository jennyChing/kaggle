       <h1>
        Hyper Realism in a bowl by Keng Lye
       </h1>
       <p class="hentry-tags">
        Posted by
        <a class="url fn n" href="http://designcollector.net/author/RSNY/" title="View all posts by Arseny Vesnin">
         Arseny Vesnin
        </a>
        on 11 04 2013 12:15  in
        <a href="http://designcollector.net/c/art/" rel="category tag">

        <div class="hentry-content clear">
         <p>
          Singapore-based artist
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kenglye/">
           Keng Lye
          </a>
          meticulously produces three-dimensional works of art with acrylics and epoxy resin that lie somewhere between painting and sculpture. Using a technique originated by
          <a href="http://designcollector.net/riusuke-fukahori-paints-three-dimentional-gold-fishes/">
           Riusuke Fukahori
          </a>
          , Lye manages to produce the illusion of different animals swimming in water. The time-consuming process involves pouring resin into a bowl and then painting on top of it with acrylics, layer by layer. (via
          <a href="http://www.mymodernmet.com/profiles/blogs/keng-lye-alive-without-breath/">
           MMN
          </a>
          )
          <br/>

         </p>
