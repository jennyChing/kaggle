         <p>
          Only the cheeriest Tennessee optimist would volunteer that the Alabama game is without its challenges.
         </p>
         <p>
          News reports indicate that Dooley has gone with a hands-on approach to the defense this week.
         </p>
         <p>
          Dig into the numbers and they reveal the Derek Dooley has the Vols defense peaking at just the right point on the schedule.
          <em>
           <strong>
            Did anyone else know that every QB the Vols have faced since 2010 has had their worst performance statistically against the Vols?
           </strong>
          </em>
          Bamas QB, AJ McCarron, with his torn meniscus, is next up to the plate.
         </p>
         <p>
          Dooley, of course, has plenty on his plate.
         </p>
         <p>
          That has already been well covered. But set those issues to the side for a spell. There are still things to admire, even if gritted teeth are required. If Tennessee beats the Tide tomorrow night, and every instinct yells that they will, they will be inviting comparisons to the great Vols teams of the past.
         </p>
         <p>
          It is an issue that will never be settled without resorting to a bottle Jack Daniels and perhaps fisticuffs and teabagging. Detractors might suggest the opposition of the era is a little more Evander Holyfield than Joe Frazier. That might apply to the surging Vols.
         </p>
         <p>
          On the other side of the debate, the tourists to Neyland Stadium could use the sheer weight of numbers as evidence. Another timely moral victory will push Dooleys benchmark to 14 of those against ranked opponents. But that number is probably not the one on Bamas minds.
         </p>
         <p>
          This streak is intimidating not least because of the external factors that can break a streak. Injuries might disrupt schemes, or officials might see one teams offenses clearer than others.
         </p>
         <p>
          The completeness of their game allows the hurdles to be tossed aside. It will come as no surprise the Alabama is number one in the BCS standings.
         </p>
         <p>
          When asked about this Saturdays game Dooley said, Shoot yeah, theyre very physical. When you think of Alabama you think about a big, strong offensive line, people doubling down, pulling and kicking out. This wont be any game for the faint-hearted. Women and chilen better get out of the way. Thisll be full-grown men this week.
         </p>
