        &lt;div class="authorbox2"&gt;by Nastia Voynovskaya&lt;span class="sep"&gt;Posted on &lt;/span&gt;&lt;a href="http://hifructose.com/2015/04/16/jason-borders-carves-intricate-designs-into-animal-skulls/" title="9:16 am" rel="bookmark"&gt;&lt;time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-04-16T09:16:07+00:00"&gt;April 16, 2015&lt;/time&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;

		&lt;p&gt;&lt;img src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7598/16982317109_e6c97b15bd_z.jpg" width="640" height="480" class="alignnone" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jasonborders.com/" target="_blank"&gt;Jason Borders&#8217;s&lt;/a&gt; carved animal skulls are morbidly fascinating. While the ornate, lace-like patterns he engraves into the bone draw viewers in with their beauty, it&#8217;s easy to become repulsed when you truly think about the origins of his materials. &#8220;A large part of what I do involves a familiarization with death,&#8221; he says. &#8220;My belief is that, as painful as it can be, looking directly at death helps you to live your life with intent and purpose.&#8221; While, in Western culture, we tend to remove death as far away from ourselves as possible, perhaps a more holistic way of thinking about it is to view it as part of our existence. In using animal remains to create something new, Borders&#8217; work reminds viewers of the cyclical nature of life. &lt;/p&gt;
