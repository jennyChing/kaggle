        <header class="entry-header">
         <h1 class="entry-title">
          10 Tips for Making your SharePoint project a Success
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="posted-on">
           Posted on
           <a href="http://www.evokeit.com/project-success-infographic/" rel="bookmark">
            <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2013-07-06T15:23:11+00:00">
             July 6, 2013
            </time>
            <time class="updated" datetime="2015-02-27T16:58:37+00:00">
             February 27, 2015
            </time>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="byline">
           by
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.evokeit.com/author/lee-harrison/">
             Lee Harrison
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <img alt="Ten-Tips" class="aligncenter wp-image-205 size-full" height="3846" src="http://www.evokeit.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Ten-Tips.png" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          1. Engage Stubborn Customers
         </p>
         <p>
          Engage the most stubborn customers and work with them to appreciate their ideas and requirements. Your most stubborn and vocal customers when won over will be your best and most vocal champions.?
         </p>
         <p>
          2. Assign a Project Owner
          <br/>
          Assign a project owner from the business who is senior enough to make decisions and pull rank if required.
         </p>
         <p>
          3. Provide Proactive Training
          <br/>
          Dont expect users to get on with it and dont just hand over a user guide. Lets face it, theyre not going to read it.
         </p>
         <p>
          4. Dont let IT run SharePoint
          <br/>
          They are not the people in the business who will be using SharePoint and, no matter what they try to tell you, they cant give you valid requirements on behalf of another user!
         </p>
         <p>
          5. Project Name
          <br/>
          Give your project a name and dont refer to it as SharePoint. SharePoint is just a framework so dont let anyone get hung up on what they think it can and cannot do. Think about the end benefits.
         </p>
         <p>
          6. No End in Sight
          <br/>
          Your SharePoint project, unlike most projects, should not have an end and requirements should grow with the business. Take an interactive approach to development and product updates. Remember the business users and project steering group need to understand and proactively contribute to development iterations.
         </p>
         <p>
          7. Go with the Flow
          <br/>
          Your SharePoint strategy and requirements will change over time so gather detailed requirements every time you need them for developers. Thats life. Things change!
         </p>
         <p>
          8. Manage Expectation
          <br/>
          Dont ignore the figures. Senior management will want two magic figures at the start of the project  cost and delivery date. You need to give them high level costs and timescales but you must manage their expectations so they understand how the project will be delivered.
         </p>
         <p>
          9. Project Steering Group
          <br/>
          Get business users and other key people to be part of a project steering group. Your project steering group cant be made up of senior management alone.
         </p>
         <p>
          ?10. Dont Overdose on Rules
          <br/>
          Get the right balance with governance. Dont overdose on rules  keep them simple and easy to follow. Start small and allow your policies to grow as your project grows.
         </p>
        </div>
