           Posted on
           <a href="http://www.payonperformance.com.au/seo-mistakes-2/" rel="bookmark" title="10:00 am">
            <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-04-17T10:00:43+00:00">
             April 17, 2015
            </time>
           </a>
          </div>
          <!-- .entry-meta -->
         </header>
         <!-- .entry-header -->
         <div class="entry-image">
         </div>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p class="lead">
           <a href="http://www.payonperformance.com.au/seo-mistakes-1">
            Part 1 of this article
           </a>
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           In part 1 of this piece we covered 5 of the most common SEO mistakes. Were finishing our top 10 with another 5 common SEO problems.
          </p>
          <ol start="6">
           <li>
            <h2>
             Title spam/branding
            </h2>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p class="lead">
           This is controversial in the SEO world but internally we have tested this in every conceivable way. Repeating your brand name in your title will hurt you if your brand contains commercial keyphrases. Having every title on your site end in | Melbourne Plumbing Company is doing you much more harm than good.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           This works similar to the above. You are now telling Google that every page on your site is about Melbourne plumbing. So which one should they rank? Whenever possible, avoid confusion and limit your pages to one topic, title and block of content per page.
          </p>
          <ol start="7">
           <li>
            <h2>
             Zero link building
            </h2>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p class="lead">
           Google has cracked down fairly well on link spam. SEOs no longer generate thousands of crummy backlinks that have only negative effects on your site.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           Instead, some have gone the other way. No links are good! So they dont build them. Or they double-talk about building them. We only do link earning. Link earning is link building that takes more time. You arent earning a link by creating a network of web owners who know &amp; like your brand. Youre doing link building externally, just not internally.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           <a href="http://www.payonperformance.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/30674229.jpg">
            <img alt="30674229" class=" wp-image-783 aligncenter" height="223" src="http://www.payonperformance.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/30674229.jpg" width="343"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           Keep in mind that whatever you call it, you need to be building the authority to your site. Think of any popular website. Use a tool like Ahrefs, Majestic, Moz or OpenLinkProfiler to find the backlinks to the site. They will, without a doubt, have millions of backlinks. Earn, build, do whatever you need to  but you need authority pointing at your site to rank.
          </p>
          <ol start="8">
           <li>
            <h2>
             No recent work
            </h2>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p class="lead">
           You started a blog with good intentions. 8 posts in the first month. 4 the month after. 2 for about 3 months after that and now 1 every so often. Your viral links of the week became of the month which also became of the whenever I can.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           You built a solid readership quickly but lost interest, didnt have time or simply ignored your blog. This is a mistake. Blog regularly or turn your blog posts into pages and forget about it. Setting up a date-based blog with no new info tells Google that you abandoned the ship.
          </p>
          <ol start="9">
           <li>
            <h2>
             But my competitor does it
            </h2>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p class="lead">
           So?
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           100s of your competitors have had massive ranking drops over the last few years with Panda or Penguin destroying their entire business. Do you really want to fear updates? Do you want to wonder if this is the one that gets you?
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           SEO should be done as right as you can. Sure, sometimes you have to play the game. If you dont get rankings now, Google cant penalise you or drop your non-existent page 1s. But its useless to get them only to lose them weeks or a couple months later, too.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           <a href="http://www.payonperformance.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/cliff-jumpers.jpg-550x0.jpg">
            <img alt="cliff jumpers.jpg-550x0" class=" wp-image-784 aligncenter" height="240" src="http://www.payonperformance.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/cliff-jumpers.jpg-550x0.jpg" width="425"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <ol start="10">
           <li>
            <h2>
             Being Impatient
            </h2>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p class="lead">
           SEO is often compared to a marathon. Its a good metaphor but its imperfect. A marathon does eventually end. You can sprint the first kilometre of a marathon if you choose to do so. SEO is very similar. However, if you sprint in the first 5 minutes of a marathon you will burn out and lose. You likely wont finish the race. If you sprint in SEO you may get a penalty or you may burn out quickly as well.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           Patience is not always easy but if you are doing SEO properly and you have learned from your mistakes, you are headed the right way toward achieving your goals in SEO.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           So there you have it. 10 of the most common SEO mistakes. Dont forget to check out the lyrics version of this post!
          </p>
