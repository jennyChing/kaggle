        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         Caramel Filled Pretzel Cookies
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="caramel filled pretzel cookies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1670" height="846" src="http://adayincandiland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Caramel-filled-Pretzel-Cookies.jpg" width="645"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://adayincandiland.com/caramel-filled-pretzel-cookies/&amp;media=http://adayincandiland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Caramel-filled-Pretzel-Cookies.jpg&amp;description=A Day In Candiland | Caramel Filled Pretzel Cookies | http://adayincandiland.com')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         This weekend I made these amazing cookies after I was asked to bake for our new families at our school. We are having back to school night tomorrow and they welcome all the new families with basket filled with cookies and treats. I think it is really a nice touch.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Caramel filled pretzel cookie ingredients" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1671" height="850" src="http://adayincandiland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Caramel-Stuffed-Pretzel-Cookies-Ingredients.jpg" width="640"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://adayincandiland.com/caramel-filled-pretzel-cookies/&amp;media=http://adayincandiland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Caramel-Stuffed-Pretzel-Cookies-Ingredients.jpg&amp;description=A Day In Candiland | Caramel Filled Pretzel Cookies | http://adayincandiland.com')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I made these cookies in a few hours. The longest time it takes is the refrigeration time. You form the cookies, place them in the fridge and a few hours or days later you can bake them off in 10 minutes. They were really simple and I was able to make a few extra for the family. I really liked that the recipe made 4 dozen cookies, that is a great amount to give away and keep some yourself. Now for the recipe.
        </p>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 sticks of butter, softened
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup of granulated sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup packed brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 large eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 TB
            <a href="http://adayincandiland.com/2014/05/diy-vanilla-extract.html" target="_blank">
             vanilla extract
            </a>
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 cups of all purpose flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 TB cornstarch
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tsp baking soda
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 ts salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 cups crushed pretzels
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 dozen Rolo candies
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 350 and grease or use parchment paper on cookie sheet.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cream butter and sugars together in a large bowl. Beat in eggs and vanilla.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a separate bowl combine flour, cornstarch, baking soda and salt. Add to butter mixture.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Stir until combined. Stir in crushed pretzels. Use about 1 TB of dough, roll each ball around a Rolo, completely sealing the candy inside the dough.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Chill for at least 2 hours or up to a couple days if you don't want to eat them right away.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake for 8-11 minutes until edges are lightly brown.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Allow to cool on the cookie sheet for 5 minutes and then cool completely on wire rack.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">

          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           Place 3 cups of pretzels in a large baggie and using a rolling pin crush until small pieces remain.
