        <h1 class="headline">
         Headed to Mavs?
        </h1>
        <div id="post-info-wrapper">
         <ul class="post-info">
          <li>
           <div class="post-author">

           </div>
          </li>
          <li>
           <ul class="post-social">
            <li>

        <div class="post-15956 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-nba tag-headed tag-mavs" id="post-area">
         <div id="content-area">
          <p>
           <img alt="Amar'e Stoudemire could be a Maverick soon. (USATSI)" height="375" src="http://sports.cbsimg.net/images/visual/whatshot/021515_amare.jpg" width="640"/>
           Amare Stoudemire could be a Maverick soon.
           <span>
            (USATSI)
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Free-agent big man
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/307861/amare-stoudemire">
            Amare Stoudemire
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://fantasynews.cbssports.com/fantasybasketball/update/25070691/knicks-waive-amare-stoudemire" target="_blank">
            bought out
           </a>
           by the
           <a href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/teams/page/NY/new-york-knicks">
            New York Knicks
           </a>
           on Monday, is leaning toward joining the
           <a href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/teams/page/DAL/dallas-mavericks">
            Dallas Mavericks
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://twitter.com/IanBegley/status/567359691026890752" target="_blank">
            according to ESPNs Ian Begley
           </a>
           . This is great news, as there might not be a better fit for Stoudemire.
          </p>
          <p>
           Dallas desperately needs depth. It misses
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/1231906/brandan-wright">
            Brandan Wright
           </a>
           , who was sacrificed in the
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/1113178/rajon-rondo">
            Rajon Rondo
           </a>
           deal in December. Wright was absurdly effective as a pick-and-roll finisher and also a dangerous shot blocker at the rim.
          </p>
          <p>
           While Stoudemire isnt the same kind of defensive deterrant, he has better range and is more than capable of scoring in similar ways. Hes obviously no longer the monster dunker he was in his prime, but running thousands of pick-and-rolls has made him an expert at moving into open space.
          </p>
          <p>
           Since Wrights departure, the Mavs have tried
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/555982/charlie-villanueva">
            Charlie Villanueva
           </a>
           ,
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/2135559/dwight-powell">
            Dwight Powell
           </a>
           ,
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/240295/richard-jefferson">
            Richard Jefferson
           </a>
           and
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/1755172/al-farouq-aminu">
            Al-Farouq Aminu
           </a>
           at power forward. Center
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/1646241/greg-smith">
            Greg Smith
           </a>
           has been in and out of the rotation. Presumably, Stoudemire would give Rick Carlisle some stability  he can be the be big off the bench every night, and he can play power forward and center.
          </p>
          <p>
           For some contending teams, Stoudemire wouldnt be a clear upgrade over the reserves who are sopping up minutes. Thats not the case here, even considering his issues on defense. The only argument against this is Dallas could instead wait on the maybe-available Jermaine ONeal or try to swing another trade.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/eye-on-basketball/25065997/if-amare-stoudemire-is-bought-out-where-should-he-go" target="_blank">
            CBSSports.coms Matt Moore examined Stoudemires options
           </a>
           last week. While the
           <a href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/teams/page/PHO/phoenix-suns">
            Phoenix Suns
           </a>
           and
           <a href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/teams/page/LAC/los-angeles-clippers">
            Los Angeles Clippers
           </a>
           could also make sense, its worth the Mavs track record with big men.
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/240273/tyson-chandler">
            Tyson Chandler
           </a>
           looks like a completely different (read: way, way better) player than he was in New York last season, and its notable that Wright and even
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/240289/brendan-haywood">
            Brendan Haywood
           </a>
           and Erick Dampier got better when Dallas acquired them and put them next to
           <a class="playerLink" href="http://www.cbssports.com/nba/players/playerpage/20622/dirk-nowitzki">
            Dirk Nowitzki
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Theres also an excellent training staff, even if it doesnt get as much attention as Phoenixs. With all that and the chance to play a meaningful role for an elite coach alongside a bunch of gifted offensive players, this seems like a smart choice. Itd certainly be a lot more fun than sticking it out with the Knicks, anyway.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
