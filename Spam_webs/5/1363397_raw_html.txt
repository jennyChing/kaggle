           <article class="article " xmlns:fb="http://www.facebook.com/2008/fbml" xmlns:msxsl="urn:schemas-microsoft-com:xslt" xmlns:regexp="http://exslt.org/regular-expressions">
            <div class="articlehead">
             <header>
              <span>
              </span>
              <h1>
               11 Holiday Party Tricks for Crohn's Disease
              </h1>
             </header>
             <aside class="disclaimer">
             </aside>
             <h2>
              Here's how to party right and have a healthy holiday when you have Crohn's disease.
             </h2>
             <div class="bottom">
              <div class="byline">
               By
               <a href="/contributing-writers-and-editors.aspx">
                Madeline Vann, MPH
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="reviewedby">
               Medically reviewed by
               <a href="/medical-reviewers.aspx">
                Farrokh Sohrabi, MD
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="clear">
             </div>
            </div>
            <div id="video-player-top">
            </div>
            <aside class="aside">
            </aside>
            <p>
             Although your holiday party invites might read and guest, no doubt youd prefer not to take Crohns symptoms along with you. With a little planning, it's possible to have a
             <a href="/health-report/crohns-holiday-guide.aspx">
              healthy holiday
             </a>
             and enjoy socializing with family and friends.
            </p>
            <p>
             The first step to take when party season arrives is to ask yourself how well youre managing your Crohns disease. People with Crohns need to know if theyre in remission or if their disease is more active," says gastroenterologist David Rubin, MD, a professor of medicine and co-director of the Inflammatory Bowel Disease Center at the University of Chicago. "Remission means that you have no symptoms related to active inflammation and youre feeling well. Remission isnt a fleeting stage  it should last months or even years. If your disease is active, one gift you can give yourself this holiday season is to talk with your doctor about what you need to do to get to that point, or at least better
             <a href="/crohns-pictures/crohns-management-plan-for-healthy-holidays.aspx">
              manage your Crohns symptoms
             </a>
             . Then, when you receive party invitations, let how well you feel determine which ones youll attend.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              A Guide to Navigating Holiday Parties With Crohns Disease
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             To keep Crohns symptoms at bay as you go from party to party, try these tips for healthy holiday socializing:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Avoid trigger foods.
              </strong>
              Your diet is not the cause of your Crohns disease, but certain foods can trigger Crohns symptoms (and you probably already know what they are). If youre lactose intolerant and youre going to eat rich, dairy-containing foods for the holidays, be aware of the probable symptoms, Dr. Rubin says. Not sure what ingredients are in something? Ask your host 
              <a href="/crohns-pictures/quiz-crohns-friendly-holiday-foods.aspx">
               holiday foods
              </a>
              often contain food triggers and most people with Crohns are best off avoiding the troubling foods entirely. I never eat  or eat very little of  any of the cream-based or cheese-based dips. They're fatty and really upsetting to my stomach, says Mary, a 30-year-old Philadelphia native who was diagnosed with Crohns at age 22.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Pick and choose Crohns-friendly foods.
              </strong>
              Holiday parties are often buffet style, which is good news for people with Crohns disease in that it means there will be lots of foods to choose from and at least a few Crohns-friendly options. Mary says she finds that veggies without too much dip, shrimp cocktail, crackers, and breads are all easy on her stomach. Of course, this might be different for you  the key is to choose foods that are best for you.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Talk to your host.
              </strong>
              Although you might not want to ask the host to change the menu for you, asking whats being served ahead of time can help you plan your course of action. If few Crohns-friendly foods will be offered, bring a dish that would be appropriate for you and plentiful enough for others to enjoy as well.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Everything in moderation.
              </strong>
              Another general rule is to not overdo it, Rubin says. People have a tendency to overeat during the holidays and do things they wouldnt normally do. Eating and drinking too much is likely to make you uncomfortable, so sample but dont gorge.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               You
               <em>
                can
               </em>
               drink alcohol.
              </strong>
              Most people with Crohns can drink alcohol in moderation, and some selections are even healthy. Red wine contains antioxidants, points out gastroenterologist Maria T. Abreu, MD, a professor of medicine and chief of the division of gastroenterology at the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine. On the other hand, she adds, Binging is a bad idea, and beer probably isnt a good choice  its too sugary. You may need to skip the booze if youre on medications that can have unpleasant or severe interactions with alcohol.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Avoid cigarette smoke.
              </strong>
              Although alcohol consumption hasnt been correlated with worsening Crohns symptoms in most people, cigarette smoke is a known trigger. So dont smoke, and try to steer clear of secondhand smoke at holiday parties.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Plan ahead.
              </strong>
              Mary says her eating habits change a bit when she's
              <a href="/health-report/crohns-holiday-guide/managing-holidays-with-crohns.aspx">
               planning to go to a holiday party
              </a>
              . I eat very clean the day of any party, so I'm feeling my best going into the social engagement," she says. Do your best to be careful about the foods you eat beforehand, especially if you know you might splurge a little.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Have your hors doeuvres at home.
              </strong>
              Eat in advance so youre not hungry, Rubin advises. That way you can focus more on the socializing and sample certain foods casually rather than gorging on unhelpful foods because youre starving.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Be choosy about holiday parties.
              </strong>
              Rather than exhausting yourself on a social whirlwind, say yes to the events you really want to go to and politely decline those you dont. Attending every party out of a sense of obligation could tire you out for the ones you really want to enjoy.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Skip the after-party.
              </strong>
              You need good sleep if you want to stay on top of Crohns disease. Resist staying out late and get refreshing rest instead.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Prepare for the possibility of Crohns symptoms.
              </strong>
              As Mary points out, its a reality that you might have symptoms during or after holiday parties. Be prepared and know where the restrooms are. No one notices if I slip away, and most of the anxiety about this is just from my own worry," she says. You should also take a just in case kit with medications and a change of clothes  you probably wont need it, but youll feel less anxious knowing its there.
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             Above all, remember the meaning of the holiday-party season  to enjoy being with family and friends. If you make this  rather than food or booze  your focus, youll be more likely to have a great time.
            </p>
