       <h2 style="text-transform: none; font-size: 16px; letter-spacing: 1px; color: #ff6600;">
        <strong>
         Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary in India
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        In the catchment area of River Subarnarekha
        <strong>
         Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary
        </strong>
        is near to Jamshedpur, Jharkhand. Spread in an area of 193 Sq.km the sanctuary comprises of undulating terrain with hillocks, plateau and deep valley providing a diverse habitat for a wide range of different wildlife species of flora and fauna. Dalma is the name derived from the Goddess Dalma- Mai (mother) worshipped by local inhabitants. Endowed with flora and fauna, India finds its pride in the Sates hinterlands and natural beauty. Inaugurated by late Sanjay Gandhi in 1975, Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary is located 40.2kms of driving distance from Jamshedpur. The forests are so dense that visibility reduces to just about 15 feet. Some of the tribes in this area still follow Stone Age rituals. Besides the wildlife there are temples of Dalma Mai, Lord Shiva and Bajarangbali that are worth visiting.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          Flora and Fauna
         </strong>
         <b>
         </b>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          Flora:
         </strong>
        </span>
        Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary is rich in flora and comprises of various species of plants and trees. 90% of the Sanctuary is covered with Timber. The chief species of trees available are Jamun, Terminalias, Karam, Dhaura, Kendu etc. The forests in Sanctuary are usually Dry Mixed Deciduous with few Dry Peninsular Sals.The forest area is so densely covered by tall trees and lush green wild shrubs and bushes that give a look like a green carpet spread on the surface.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          Fauna:
         </strong>
        </span>
        The primary animals found in Dalma are: Elephants, Sloth Deers, Barking Deers, Peacocks, Giant Squirrels, Wild Bears, Mouse Deers, Indian Foxes, Rabbits, Mongoose, Monkeys and Langurs.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Park attractions :A view from the hill top at the city beneath especially during nights is a visual pleasure as it appears like millions of stars twinkling. If one has the impetus to look forward to the sights and sounds of entering into a sanctuary that greets you at every turn and with every glance, then one is sure to get the feeling of being one with the nature and is at peace with oneself. The distance does not matter neither do the staid formalities or the endless regulations and checks. It is the sight of the jungle that is enchanting. The seductive smell of the damp earth, the nameless wild flowers that shyly nod their heads in greeting with every passing breeze, the deafening cacophony of birds in sync with the chirping of the crickets and the pungent odour of the elephant scat makes you wonder at the city life that is shorn of such appendices.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          Best time to visit :
         </strong>
        </span>
        Except for the monsoons the park is open during all other seasons.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          Accommodation :
         </strong>
        </span>
        Up the hill there is a government rest house along with a hotel. Tourists can avail accommodation in here.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          How to reach Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary:
         </strong>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        The Sanctuary is near to Jamshedpur city which is availed with all means of transport. Hence reaching the
        <strong>
         Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary
        </strong>
        is not a thing to worry about.
       </p>

        <strong>
