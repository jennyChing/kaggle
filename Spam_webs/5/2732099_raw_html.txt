           <p>
            It takes a special kind of woman to choose and marry a
            <a data-component="link" data-inlink="9XdM0aHQfScRx5dPVuu7_w==" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/abusiverelationships/f/passiveaggressivebehavior.htm">
             passive aggressive
            </a>
            man. The woman who marries the
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="2" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/abusiverelationships/a/Pass_Agg.htm">
             passive aggressive
            </a>
            man was taught in her family of origin to accept a high level of frustration for a minimal level of love and caring. When a woman marries the passive aggressive man she gets little return for all her effort.
           </p>
           <p>
            What does that mean? How about we use me as an example. When I was a child my father was constantly withdrawing from my mother.
           </p>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="3" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense1">
           </div>
           <div class="radlinks ads-half" data-character-limit="null" data-display-inline="true" data-display-label="false" data-num-links="5" id="radlinks3">
           </div>
           <div data-defer="onPageLoad" id="videoPlaceholderLarge">
           </div>
           <p class="cb-split">
            He was an alcoholic who found it easier to deal with problems by drinking. His withdrawal frustrated my mother who became resentful at his withdrawal. As an adult it makes sense that I would choose a man who mirrored what I had grown up witnessing.
           </p>
           <p>
            My ex husband was charming, needy and couldnt be around me enough. He was every womans idea of the perfect manin the beginning. Never once did I question his lack of relationship with his family. Or the fact that he didnt have any long-standing friendships.
           </p>
           <p>
            I was in love with love and this man, from all appearances had a lot of love to give. What I didnt understand at the time that, due to my childhood, I was conditioned to rescue the needy, to help them, help themselves.
           </p>
           <p>
            The passive aggressive man or womans behavior cycles between hostility and withdrawal. If you become involved with a
            <a data-component="link" data-inlink="vEOSlsI95DOGlMMzNrpctg==" data-ordinal="3" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/abusiverelationships/qt/Divorcing-A-Passive-Aggressive-Spouse.htm">
             passive aggressive
            </a>
            man within a few months you will come face to face with a man who is either very hostile or shuts down and withdraws. In some cases the passive aggressive man will do both.
           </p>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="2" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense2">
           </div>
           <p>
            I remember the first time my ex withdrew from me. It was while we were still
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="4" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/romanceafterdivorce/p/reboundrelation.htm">
             dating
            </a>
            . He was unable to form an
            <a data-component="link" data-inlink="YqHuQMCdXmcf4Gg20j-ETg==" data-ordinal="5" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/canorshoulditbesaved/fl/5-Ways-To-Maintain-a-Healthy-Emotional-Connection-With-Your-Spouse.htm">
             emotional connection
            </a>
            with me but instead of take responsibility for his own inability he behaved as if I was the one with an issue.
           </p>
           <p>
            I willing took on the responsibility. I made his faults my fault. I convinced myself I was not doing enough to keep him happy. The funny thing is, the harder I worked on the relationship the more he withdrew.
           </p>
           <p>
            My entire marriage consisted of me trying to find solutions to our problems and him withdrawing further and further away both emotionally and intimately.
           </p>
           <p>
            A woman married to the passive aggressive man lives daily attempting to connect with her husband. Her attempts to connect threaten him and bring to the surface his fear of attachment, which means more withdrawal. The cycle goes on and on and on!
           </p>
           <p>
            What happens when someone you love dismisses your efforts and withdraws? You become angry and frustrated. Your attempts to communicate calmly turns into deeper resentment and anger. In response he withdraws a bit more and you both end up not having your needs met.
           </p>
           <p>
            The
            <a data-component="link" data-inlink="FVDL3ijoZPvqyulf1yop0Q==" data-ordinal="6" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/abusiverelationships/f/Is-Your-Spouse-Passive-Aggressive.htm">
             passive aggressive
            </a>
            husband wont return his wifes anger. He will ,
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="7" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/abusiverelationships/a/pa_anger.htm">
             get back at her in covert ways
            </a>
            though. He will
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="8" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/abusiverelationships/a/pa_sex.htm">
             withhold affection
            </a>
            , forget important datesif it is something she needs, he will make sure she doesnt get it.
           </p>
           <p>
            His covert anger drives the wife of the passive aggressive man crazy. The crazier she feels they angrier she becomes and the yelling and screaming becomes a desperate attempt to be heard by a husband who refuses to listen.
           </p>
           <p>
            The passive aggressive man fears becoming emotionally attached to a woman. Lessons he learned in his childhood taught him that doing so isnt safe. What happens when the passive aggressive mans wife becomes angry? His fears are confirmedshe is not safe and he is not safe in the relationship.
           </p>
           <p>
            The woman who marries the passive aggressive man spends a lot of time hoping for more than her husband is willing to give her. She wants closeness, cooperation, love and attention. She wants actions and behaviors from him that show her he loves her.
           </p>
           <p>
            By the time my marriage to my passive aggressive husband came to an end I had no self-esteem. I dont think Ive ever felt as lonely and the sense of abandonment by my own husband was overwhelming. The loneliness I experienced in my marriage was worse than any I had ever felt as a single woman.
           </p>
           <p>
            The passive aggressive man sabotages his marriage but it takes that one special woman to enable him to do so. That woman who, in dealing with her own issues is attracted to the walking wounded. That woman who goes above and beyond when it comes to making a relationship work.
           </p>
           <p>
            And, she will continue to attract
            <a data-component="link" data-inlink="gM4tQWy9m_ivmrn8-iVtlA==" data-ordinal="9" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/passiveaggressive/qt/What-Causes-Passive-Aggressive-Behavior.htm">
             passive aggressive
            </a>
            men until she realizes that, as an adult woman she has the ability to limit how much damage another person can do to her life. Being loved should never mean turning yourself inside out for anyone. Being loved means knowing when to set boundaries, knowing your own worth and if need be, walking away from a man who does nothing but withdraw and withhold what you desire.
           </p>
