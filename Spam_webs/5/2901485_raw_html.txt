       <h2 class="post-title">
        Gestational diabetes recipes
       </h2>
       <p>
        Garlic Sauteed Spinach and the Skinless chicken breast Gumbo are the most healthy gestational diabetes meal plans. They are low calorie
        <a href="http://gestationaldiabetesblog.com/gestational-diabetes-recipes/">
         Gestational diabetes recipes
        </a>
        pregnant women should consider to manage their pregnancy related diabetes better. These two recipes contain low saturated fats, low carbs m High protein and fiber.
       </p>
       <h2 class="post-title">
        Gestational diabetes meal plan
       </h2>
       <h3 class="post-title">
        Gumbo recipe for Gestational diabetes management
       </h3>
       <p>
        Making Gumbo will take about 2  hours {10 minutes for preparation, and 2 hours 20 minutes for cooking}. This recipe will provide about 10 servings.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="gestational diabetes meal plan" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-291" height="300" src="http://gestationaldiabetesblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/gestational-diabetes-meal-plan.jpg" width="533"/>
       </p>
       <h3 class="post-title">
        Gestational diabetes meal plan: Ingredients
       </h3>
       <ul>
        <li>
         3 medium large boneless, skinless chicken breasts cut into halves.
        </li>
        <li>
          cup of vegetable oil.
        </li>
        <li>
          cup of salt and pepper.
        </li>
        <li>
          cup of an all-purpose flour.
        </li>
        <li>
         1/4 cup of vegetable oil.
        </li>
        <li>
         4 teaspoon of Margarine.
        </li>
        <li>
         2 cups of sliced frozen Okra.
        </li>
        <li>
         1 large chopped Onion.
        </li>
        <li>
         8 cloves of Minced Garlic.
        </li>
        <li>
         1 green bell, chopped and seeded.
        </li>
        <li>
         3 stalks of chopped celery.
        </li>
        <li>
          cup of Worcestershire sauce.
        </li>
        <li>
          flat bunch Parsley leaf with the stems and leaves coarsely chopped.
        </li>
        <li>
         3-4 cups of hot water.
        </li>
        <li>
          pound of small peeled cooked small shrimps.
        </li>
        <li>
         4 sliced green and white Onion.
        </li>
        <li>
         4 to 5 beef bouillon cubes.
        </li>
        <li>
         14 ounce can of stewed tomato juice.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h3 class="post-title">
        Gestational diabetes recipe:  Preparation
       </h3>
       <p>
        Season the medium large boneless chickens with Salt and pepper. Prepare the oil be heating it over a heavy bottom oven over medium-high heat. Cook the chicken in the oven until it turns light brown at the sides. Remove and place in a pan. Add the sausage to the chicken and cook again until they turn brown and remove.
       </p>
       <p>
        Sprinkle the multi-purpose flour over the oil. Add 2 teaspoon of margarine, cook over medium heat while stirring continuously. This should take about 10 minutes. Remove and let the roux cool down.
       </p>
       <p>
        Take the Dutch over back to low heat and melt the 3 teaspoon of margarine. Add the green pepper, onion, celery and Garlic and cook the mixture for about 10 minutes. Add other ingredients such as the Worcestershire sauce, pepper, salt, and  bunch parsley to enhance the taste- cook and stir this mixture for about 10 minutes.
       </p>
       <p>
        Add the 3 cups of water, the bouillon cubes. Whisk continuously before adding the chicken and sausage. Heat the whole content until boiling and reduce the heat afterwards. Cover the content and let it simmer for about 50 minutes. Add the tomatoes and Okra, cover and let it simmer for about 1 hour.
       </p>
       <p>
        Add the green onions, shrimps, and chopped parsley just before serving. Gumbo recipe provides you with a total of 330 calories, 27 grams of Protein, 3 grams of saturated fat, and 18 grams of carbs.
       </p>
