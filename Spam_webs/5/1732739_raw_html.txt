       <h1 class="postTitle">
        3 Tips for Rebuilding an Engine on a Budget
       </h1>
       <div class="blogMeta">
        December 16th, 2014 | By
        <a href="http://www.salvageyardlosangeles.com/author/140913/" rel="author" title="Posts by 140913">
         140913
        </a>
        | Posted in
        <a href="http://www.salvageyardlosangeles.com/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Uncategorized">
         Uncategorized
        </a>
        |
       </div>
       <div class="theContent">
        <p>
         <img align="right" alt="" src="http://www.salvageyardlosangeles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/mechanic-11.jpg" width="300px"/>
         <br/>
         Rebuilding an engine is a great way to save money if you need to buy a new motor. If you like rebuilding engines for fun, thats great. But if you are like most people, you want to rebuild an engine to save money (as well as have some fun). So what can you do to make the process as inexpensive as possible? Here are a few suggestions.
        </p>
        <ol start="1">
         <li>
          If you want to put a new engine in your vehicle but cant afford to buy a newly built one, visit a junk yard and salvage an old one yourself. You can find an entire engine for only a few hundred dollars. Even with the extra parts and labor involved, you can still rebuild an old engine for under $2,200.
         </li>
         <li>
          Check spark plugs and the engine block for signs of damage. A gently used engine will require less work to rebuild, whereas a completely ruined engine will cost a small fortune in parts. But how do you know if the engine is in good condition? Check the block and spark plugs. If both are free of extra oil and cracks, the engine is probably in good condition.
         </li>
         <li>
          Only do what you know how to. Whether its your first time rebuilding an engine or you have done a few in the past, dont be afraid to admit defeat and get help. Making a mistake will cost you a lot more than paying a professional.
         </li>
        </ol>
