         <h1 class="title single-title entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          LEGOLAND Hotel California for Kids of All Ages
         </h1>
         <div class="post-info">
          <span class="theauthor">
           By
           <span itemprop="author">
            <a href="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/author/dianarowe/" rel="nofollow" title="Posts by Diana Rowe">
             Diana Rowe
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
          <span class="thetime updated">
           On
           <span itemprop="datePublished">
            July 3, 2014
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>
        </header>
        <!--.headline_area-->
        <div class="post-single-content box mark-links entry-content">
         <div class="thecontent" itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           After spending 3 days at Disneyland with my multigenerational family, we continued the theme park adventures and drove to
           <a href="http://california.legoland.com/en/LEGOLAND-Hotel1/" target="_blank">
            LEGOLAND Hotel in Carlsbad, California.
           </a>
           From the moment you step into the LEGOLAND world, the 3-story, 250-room LEGOLAND Hotel California is all about the kids  and the LEGOLAND theme park is literally mere steps from the hotel.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.tkqlhce.com/click-5722046-10537500?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.hotels.com%2Fho424796%2Flegoland-hotel-carlsbad-united-states-of-america%2F%3Fwapb1%3Dhotelcontentfeed&amp;cjsku=424796" target="_blank">
            <img alt="LegolandHotelPool" class="aligncenter wp-image-15430 size-large" height="393" src="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/LegolandHotelPool-590x393.jpg" width="590"/>
           </a>
           <span id="more-15422">
           </span>
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            Arrival
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           Enter the main entrance of the LEGOLAND Hotel in
           <a href="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/index.php?s=california" target="_blank">
            California
           </a>
           , and youll see how much it is about kids (ages 2-12). The entire building is LEGOs, and my grandkids are running around excited to see the life-size characters crafted from Lego bricks. Enter the hotel by walking beneath a giant Lego dragon and voila, the lobby is another Lego adventure.
          </p>
          <p>
           Check-in was a breeze as the entire lobby is a Lego themed playground, the Castle Play area. Kids play at various stations, all within eyesight of the front desk. We arrived late morning, so our room wasnt available. However, the receptionist checked us in and gave us our key. The new technology allows them to continue check in remotely once room is available, and then text us when its ready! We were able to check in a couple hours later, by 1 pm.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/LegolandLobby11.jpg">
            <img alt="LegolandLobby1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15435" height="333" src="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/LegolandLobby11.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Accommodations
          </h2>
          <p>
           Heres the cool part. We gathered our luggage which was stored in valet, and headed to the elevators. Guests can choose
           <a href="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/5-themed-hotels-for-families/" target="_blank">
            themed hotel rooms
           </a>
           : Pirate, Adventure or Kingdom Our room was on the Adventure Floor, a Raiders of the Lost Ark adventure-esque setting. We stepped into the elevators to be greeted by the ho-hum boring music, but the moment the doors closed, the disco ball lit up and Saturday Night Live jammed on the speakers. It set the stage for a fun adventure.
          </p>
          <p>
           Every room has a separate sleeping area for up to 3 children, with bunk beds and trundle-bed (and their own TV), right at the door, while parents sleep on the other side of the bathroom. The room is adequate, but it was tight for 3 adults and 2 children  I slept on the trundle bed, while my daughter and her hubby slept in the adult room.
           <a href="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/LegolandAdventureHotelRoom.jpg">
            <img alt="LegolandAdventureHotelRoom" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15429" height="250" src="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/LegolandAdventureHotelRoom.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           The kids were all about the in-room treasure chest. Upon check in, they were given a scavenger hunt to keep themselves active, and the answers became a combination number which opened the chest, rewarding them some Lego bling.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Dining
          </h2>
          <p>
           A buffet breakfast at Bricks Family Restaurant is included in your hotel reservation. The buffet is typical breakfast fare, but it does include a kid-size buffet station with healthy food options. I have to say this was one of the tastiest buffets Ive ever had! The bonus is that LEGO characters roam the dining area and chat with the kids.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/LegolandBuffet1.jpg">
            <img alt="LegolandBuffet1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15434" height="333" src="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/LegolandBuffet1.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Adjacent to the buffet restaurant is Skyline Caf, serving a lunch and dinner menu, as well as full bar. It also services outdoor seating and the pool area. Kids have fun here too as the caf includes a LEGO skyline with 35 different scenes.
          </p>
          <p>
           A casual dining option is also available adjacent to the Castle Play area. Minis Lounge offers quick snacks and beverages.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Amenities
          </h2>
          <p>
           The LEGOLAND Hotel is brimming with amenities, including a fitness center, LEGO Event Room, family pool, lounge and so many LEGO models and interactive features throughout the hotel. The hotel can keep active kids busy for hours. There is also loads of built-in entertainment for kids, from Model Building contests to Hero Training and Kids Improv Hour. We took full advantage of the pool, and the daily Swim-in Movie.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Legoland_entertainment1.jpg">
            <img alt="Legoland_entertainment1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15431" height="333" src="http://www.hotel-scoop.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Legoland_entertainment1.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Summary
          </h2>
          <p>
           We fell in love with the
           <a href="http://california.legoland.com/en/LEGOLAND-Hotel1/" target="_blank">
            LEGOLAND Hotel California.
           </a>
           It was a perfect ending to an all-about-the-kids California theme park vacation. Check rates at
           <a href="http://www.tkqlhce.com/click-5722046-10537500?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.hotels.com%2Fho424796%2Flegoland-hotel-carlsbad-united-states-of-america%2F%3Fwapb1%3Dhotelcontentfeed&amp;cjsku=424796" target="_blank">
            Hotels.com
           </a>
           .
          </p>
