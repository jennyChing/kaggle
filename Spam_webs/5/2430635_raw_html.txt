            <h3 class="post-name">
             Automating Finance for the Undisciplined and Disorganized
            </h3>
            <div class="post-author">
             <span class="avatar">
              <a href="/members/NoraDunn/default.aspx">
               <img alt="" src="/resized-image.ashx/__size/32x32/__key/CommunityServer-Components-Avatars/00-01-53-26-31/4TFSIH3GVEMA.jpg" style="border-width:0px;"/>
              </a>
             </span>
             <span class="user-name">
              <a class="internal-link view-user-profile" href="/members/NoraDunn/default.aspx">
               <span>
               </span>
               NoraDunn
              </a>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="post-date">
             <span class="value">
              Thu, Apr 5 2012 9:01 AM
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="post-attributes">
             <div class="attribute-list-header">
             </div>
             <ul class="attribute-list">
              <li class="attribute-item post-reply-count">
               <span class="attribute-name">
                Comments
               </span>
               <span class="attribute-value">
                <a class="internal-link view-replies" href="#comments">
                 <span>
                 </span>
                 3
                </a>
               </span>
              </li>
             </ul>
             <div class="attribute-list-footer">
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="post-content user-defined-markup">
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                <img alt="Automating Finance for the Undisciplined and Disorganized" src="http://community.careonecredit.com/resized-image.ashx/__size/550x0/__key/CommunityServer-Blogs-Components-WeblogFiles/00-00-00-00-34/4212.Nora_5F00_04.05.jpg" style="margin: 10px; float: right; border-width: 0px;" title="Automating Finance for the Undisciplined and Disorganized"/>
                Is managing finance not a passion for you? An onerous task you'd prefer to ignore than attend to? No problem. Here are nine ways to automate your finances so you can focus on other things in life.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 1. Manage it in One Place
                </span>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                Many of us have bank accounts, credit cards, and investments spread across various institutions. Manage it in one place with an online financial planning tool such as
               </span>
              </span>
              <a href="http://community.careonecredit.com/controlpanel/blogs/posteditor.aspx/rel=" target="_blank">
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 Mint
                </span>
               </span>
              </a>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                or
               </span>
              </span>
              <a href="http://community.careonecredit.com/controlpanel/blogs/posteditor.aspx/rel=" target="_blank">
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 Thrive
                </span>
               </span>
              </a>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                . This allows you to track your spending and get a snapshot of your financial picture easily.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 2. Lose the Bank Accounts
                </span>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                A friend of mine had four different bank accounts and was paying over $20/month to maintain them, without knowing why she had all these accounts! One checking account will suffice for deposits and debits.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 3. Use Online Savings Accounts
                </span>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                For multiple savings vehicles, use high-interest online savings accounts (such as
                <a href="http://home.ingdirect.com/orange-savings-account?s=OrangeSavingsAccount" target="_blank">
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                <a href="http://home.ingdirect.com/orange-savings-account?s=OrangeSavingsAccount" target="_blank">
                 ING
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                ) which carry no fees and allow you to transfer money in and out easily. This also staves off
                <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                 <a href="http://community.careonecredit.com/b/life_balance/archive/2010/10/28/how-to-conquer-impulse-shopping-syndrome.aspx" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  impulse buying
                 </a>
                </span>
                , since you need at least 24 hours before money from an online savings account is transferred to your bank/debit account.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                You can create as many online savings accounts as you wish; each one dedicated to a separate savings goal (i.e.: one for a house down payment, one for a new computer,
               </span>
              </span>
              <a href="http://community.careonecredit.com/b/life_balance/archive/2010/11/04/educating-your-children-about-money-part-1-the-timeline.aspx" target="_blank">
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 one for the kids
                </span>
               </span>
              </a>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                , etc.).
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
               </span>
              </span>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 4. Go Paperless
                </span>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                It's easy to manage your financial affairs online. Register for online banking, and arrange for all bills to be sent to you electronically.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 5. Pay Bills Automatically
                </span>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                Never miss a billing deadline by paying bills automatically. Non-fluctuating bills can be debited directly from your bank account, since they're easy to budget for. Rent can be paid with post-dated checks. Fluctuating bills can be charged to your credit card for review and payment - as long as you pay it off in full each month!
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                <i>
                 Money-saving bonus
                </i>
                : some vendors will give you a discount for setting up direct payments.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 6. Adjust Billing Dates
                </span>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                Shortly after your pay is deposited to your account, you'll want your bill payments and savings to come out. This way, the money left in your bank account is yours to spend discretionarily.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                To do this you might need to adjust the billing cycles for some bills. This is as easy as calling your provider and asking them to bill you on day "x" of each month going forward.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
               </span>
              </span>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 7. Save Automatically
                </span>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                Remember those online savings accounts you opened? Just like paying your bills automatically, set up automatic savings too! Think of your bank account as an inbox of sorts; your paychecks are deposited first, then everything gets allocated (automatically) to different places: savings, debts, and expenses.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 8. Join the Company Retirement Plan
                </span>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                If your workplace offers a retirement plan, get involved. Money is often deducted before taxation (which gives you more bang for your buck), and if there is a matching component, it's like receiving an immediate 100% return on investment.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
                <span style="font-size: small;">
                 9. Use a Rewards Credit Card
                </span>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                As long as you're paying some bills automatically with a credit card, maximize value by using a credit card with rewards benefits like
               </span>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                <a href="http://www.wisebread.com/the-travel-hacking-cartel-fly-around-the-world-for-almost-free" target="_blank">
                 frequent flyer miles
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                <a href="http://www.wisebread.com/the-travel-hacking-cartel-fly-around-the-world-for-almost-free" target="_blank">
                 .
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-family: verdana,geneva;">
               <span style="font-size: small;">
                Saving and managing money doesn't have to be about sheer willpower! By automating your financial affairs, you remove the discipline; pay yourself first, and rest assured nothing has been overlooked.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
