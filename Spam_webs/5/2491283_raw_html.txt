        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Orange Cream Cake with Cool Whip Pudding Frosting light and fluffy.
         <br/>
         Tastes like a big Push Up Ice Cream Pop, remember those?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Orange Cake with Cool Whip Pudding Frosting " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41973" height="1500" src="http://cf.callmepmc.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Orange-Cake-with-Cool-Whip-Pudding-Frosting-collage.jpg" width="550"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Orange Cream Cake | Cool Whip Pudding Frosting
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The first time I ever had an Orange Cake, I was like what? Orange? Cake? It just seemed strange. One bite was all it took for me to be convinced that, yes indeed, orange can be a cake and can befabulous!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         My Orange cake is very moist with an extremelylight frosting on it. This frosting recipe is the easiest ever to make. Its pudding and whipped cream. The flavors of this cake just scream summerto me!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         If youveeverenjoyed a Push Up Frozen ice cream pop, that is what I modeled this cake after. Frozen orange sorbet with cream.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Orange Cake with Cool Whip Pudding Frosting " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41974" height="825" src="http://cf.callmepmc.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Orange-Cake-Instand-Pudding-Cool-Whip-Frosting.jpg" width="550"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I started this cake with an orange cake mix, then kicked it up. It has a fine crumb and rich texture. One item I used to make this cake super moist is instant pudding. I dont recommend leaving it out. Sour cream also helps keeps this cake moist and rich.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Not only did I use instant pudding in the cake, I also used it in the icing. Not only is it in the icing, its the primary component of the icing. If you live in an area where instant pudding is unavailable, there is really no substitution. You may want to order it from my
         <a href="http://astore.amazon.com/camepm-20?_encoding=UTF8&amp;node=8" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Amazon Store
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          Pudding mixed with cool whip
         </strong>
         makes a really light frosting that I think youll love. You can also use different pudding flavors and match it with different cake flavors. I think a lemon cake with lemon instant pudding frosting would be out of this world.
         <em>
          I may have to make that next!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Orange Cake with Cool Whip Pudding Frosting " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-41972" height="825" src="http://cf.callmepmc.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Orange-Cake-with-Pudding-Cool-Whip-Frosting-wm.jpg" width="550"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Do you make layer cakes? I used to make layered cakes fora local restaurant. It was big fun, I got to create whatever crazy flavors each week that I wanted. But, Ill be honest, I dont make them very often now. I usually reserve a tall, impressive layered cake for holidays.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://wp.me/p3iHoj-aUL" target="_blank">
          <img alt="How to Frost a 3 layer Cake" class="aligncenter wp-image-42220 size-full" height="500" src="http://cf.callmepmc.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/How-to-Frost-a-3-layer-Cake.jpg" title="How to Frost a 3 layer Cake" width="350"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h4 style="text-align: left;">
         How to frost a 3-layer cake.
        </h4>
        <ol>
         <li>
          It very important to work with cool cake. Otherwise, chances are the icing will slide off or youll get it completely frosted and itll slowly slide to one side. I know this for a fact! Its even good to refrigerate the layers. I find cold layers the easiest to work with.
         </li>
         <li>
          Level the layers. Using a long serrated knife, as best as you can, shavethe dome hump off the cake to make the cake flat.
          <em>
           Snack on that cake or give it to your children!
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          Place one cake layer on a pretty serving tray.
          <em>
           Check that it will fit in your cake cover so you can keep it fresh
          </em>
          ! If you have a layer that thicker than the others, place it on the bottom, the weight of the other layers will compress it and theyll end up being about the same thickness. Scoop a big spoonful of icing on the layer and spread it evenly with an
          <a href="http://astore.amazon.com/camepm-20?_encoding=UTF8&amp;node=5" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           off-set spatula
          </a>
          . Place the second layer on top, add icing and spread it evenly. Place the last layer on the cake. Using the of-set spatula frost the sides of the cake then the top. Make swirly designs in frosting with the tip of the spatula.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Thats all there is to frosting a layer cake!
         </strong>
         <br/>
        </p>

         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Orange Cream Cake with Cool Whip Pudding Frosting
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSDetails">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Paula Jones
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
          <div class="ERSTimes">
           <div class="ERSHead">
            Prep time:
            <time datetime="PT10M" itemprop="prepTime">
             10 mins
            </time>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSHead">
            Cook time:
            <time datetime="PT25M" itemprop="cookTime">
             25 mins
            </time>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSHead">
            Total time:
            <time datetime="PT35M" itemprop="totalTime">
             35 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
          <div class="ERSHead">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            12 servings
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          Orange Cream Cake with Cool Whip Pudding Frosting is light, fuffy and full of citrus flavor! The zesty tanginess of this moist cake tastes like orange sorbet and makes a mind blowing dessert for any occasion!
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Cake
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 box orange cake mix
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 box (3.4oz) Vanilla Instant pudding mix, (don't make the pudding recipe, we'll use it dry.)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup canola oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup whole milk (I use 2%)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup sour cream
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 large eggs
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Frosting
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup cold milk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 (3.5 ounce) package instant pudding (dry) - I used French Vanilla flavor
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 ounce cool whip
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon orange zest (usually it's the zest of one large naval orange)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahreinheit. Spray three 9 inch round cake pans with nonstick spray.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            For the Cake: Mix the cake mix, vanilla instant pudding mix, vegetable oil, milk, sour cream and eggs with an electric or stand mixer until fluffy (about 2 minutes on medium speed).
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pour the cake batter evenly into your 3 prepared cake pans.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake for 25-27 minutes or until a wooden pick inserted in the center of the cake comes out clean.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cool on a wire rack 5 to 10 minutes before inverting. Allow to cool completely before frosting.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            For the frosting: Whisk milk and pudding until pudding begins to get thick. Fold in Cool Whip and orange zest until well combined. Fold carefully to keep it light and fluffy.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Frost cake immediately and refrigerate until ready to serve.
           </li>
          </ol>
