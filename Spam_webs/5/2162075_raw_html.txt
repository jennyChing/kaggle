     <h2>
      <a href="http://www.designjuices.co.uk/2012/06/interview-matt-pealing/" rel="bookmark" title="Interview Web &amp; Graphic Design Freelancer Matt Pealing">
       Interview Web &amp; Graphic Design Freelancer Matt Pealing
      </a>
     </h2>
     <div class="clear">
     </div>
     <div class="entry-content">
      <p>
       <strong>
        <em>
         <a href="http://www.designjuices.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/portrait.jpg">
          <img alt="" class=" wp-image-8142 alignright" height="190" src="http://www.designjuices.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/portrait.jpg" title="portrait" width="190"/>
         </a>
         Its great to interview and introduce to our Design Juices readers today another creative mind from the world of design. Today we introduce
         <a href="http://www.mattpealing.co.uk/">
          Matt Pealing
         </a>
         , now matt is from jolly old england like myself. Firstly matt could you introduce yourself to our readers who may not know your background.
        </em>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Hi Jared, thanks for featuring me!
      </p>
      <p>
       I am a freelance
       <a href="http://www.mattpealing.co.uk/">
        web &amp; graphic designer based in Liverpool
       </a>
       (originally from Ellesmere Port) and I work mostly with design and development agencies. I specialise in designing and building custom WordPress themes as well as branding. This is the kind of work that is outsourced to me by the type of clients I mentioned above.
      </p>
      <p>
       I spend a few days a week working from
       <a href="http://www.twitter.com/Basecamp_3">
        @Basecamp_3
       </a>
       as well as working from Jelly co-working events. Every now and then Ill work from my apartment, I like to mix up my working environments!
      </p>
      <p>
       As well as agencies, I occasionally work with my own, direct clients. I like working with direct clients as it generally means I have more creative control over a project, and there are no restrictions on what I can or cant add to my portfolio.
      </p>
      <p>
       Having said that, I do not class myself as a salesman (far from it!). So for that reason, I focus mainly on attracting work from agencies. This gives me more time to focus on what Im good at and what I enjoy; design. I then work with direct clients whenever they crop up, normally through word of mouth or occasionally search engines. For this reason I class myself as more of a freelancer than an actual business-owner.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://www.mattpealing.co.uk/">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-8144" height="563" src="http://www.designjuices.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/01.jpg" title="01" width="564"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <em>
         Is Design Juices a blog you have come across much yourself? If so what do you like about the site and what other blogs and sites do you check out for inspiration and news from the design world in your RSS reader?
        </em>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       I think I stumbled across Design Juices last year (2011) through Twitter. I do have a read of it every now and then, normally at interesting features of other creatives which I find really inspiring. I think its a great little community you have going and definately something to be proud of!
      </p>
      <p>
       I also like to look at design work on the following websites:
      </p>
      <p>
       
       <a href="http://siteinspire.com">
        Siteinspire
       </a>
       : great showcase of contemporary web designs which are well categorised
      </p>
      <p>
       
       <a href="http://www.behance.net">
        Behance
       </a>
       : not the best of search facilities in my opinion, but theres plenty of design work on there of a really high standard. Lots of different creative fields, and you can even showcase your own portfolio on there too!
      </p>
      <p>
       
       <a href="http://dribbble.com">
        Dribble
       </a>
       : great website when you need ideas some something really specific, e.g. navigation, buttons, website footers etc
      </p>
      <p>
       
       <a href="http://logopond.com">
        Logopond
       </a>
       : well categorised directory of logo designs. All designs aretagged so its easy to get ideas for logos containing virtually anything
      </p>
      <p>
       If something really catches my eye Ill add it to my @littlesnapper (nifty little Mac app) library for future reference. I keep all my inspiration organised with tags so I can quite easily get inspiration and put together moodboards for future projects.
      </p>
      <p>
       I also grab inspiration in everyday life, such as leaflets, flyers, posters etc. There is a really vibrant creative community in Liverpool and I sometimes receive flyers for various creative get togethers or even club nights that have really nice typography. So again, anything that looks super tasty gets scanned in and added to my
       <a href="http://www.twitter.com/littlesnapper">
        @littlesnapper
       </a>
       library!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://www.mattpealing.co.uk/">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-8143" height="650" src="http://www.designjuices.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/02.jpg" title="02" width="538"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <em>
         What kinds of plans and work do you have coming up for the rest of 2012?
        </em>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       For the rest of 2012, I plan to continue my mission to find more design work. I spent the first couple of years of my freelancing career spending a significant amount of time working on things that I didnt feel passionate about, such as building websites based on other peoples designs.
      </p>
      <p>
       For the last few months Ive been focusing on taking on projects where all of the design work is taken care of myself, such as designing
       <em>
        and
       </em>
       building WordPress themes. I am much happier with my career this way as it feels much more creative than it use to. Even if I cant add the work to my portfolio and an agency takes all the credit for it, its still my passion to produce unique and creative designs that clearly communicate a brands message.
      </p>
      <p>
       Not only do I want to continue focusing on more design-based work, I would like to find more work related to the arts such as musicians, art galleries and contemporary museums. I would also like to find work that involves designing to engage young people, such as working on projects for colleges and universities.
      </p>
      <p>
       I imagine these kind of clients will be hard to win over due to the amount of competition from design agencies and in-house design teams but Im determined to give it a shot!
      </p>
      <p>
       Also I like the idea of contracting in other design agencies studios, especially those in other cities. I imagine this would lead to some interesting experiences which would hopefully result in working on cool new projects and meeting cool new people!
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.mattpealing.co.uk/">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8145" height="550" src="http://www.designjuices.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/03.jpg" title="03" width="350"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <em>
         When trying to switch off at evenings or weekends what activities do you find are great to switch off as a designer?
        </em>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Good question. I spend a lot of time immersing myself in design so I often feel the need to get away from my screen even if its just for a stroll at lunchtime with my headphones on.
      </p>
      <p>
       I listen to a wide range of music from a variety of genres (generally nothing mainstream of chart-related) and find I am listening to music whatever I do.
      </p>
      <p>
       Like a lot of ex-students, I enjoy alcohol and partying and all that kind of thing. Im quite proud of myself these days as Ive managed to limit myself to about one night out a week. It took a while to adapt after my university days but I got there in the end after a few years.
      </p>
      <p>
       Also, I like running and going the gym. Theyre a great way to de-stress as well as making up for the effects of the aforementioned partying antics. Over the last few months Ive got back on my wheels and started skating again, normally at
       <a href="http://www.twitter.com/Rampworx">
        @Rampworx
       </a>
       skatepark in Liverpool. Although Ive gotta say I struggle to find the time to do it these days and I really dont have the moves like I used to years ago! (
       <a href="http://tinyurl.com/d5ycz5x">
        see here from 2006
       </a>
       , in my baggy clothes days nice Prodigy soundtrack). I forgot just how injured you get after a session, even on a good day. Im sure Ill eventually get my moves back if I stick at it though
      </p>
      <p>
       I like meeting new people by visiting creative meetups such as
       <a href="http://tinyurl.com/3cesvvs">
        SWIG
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.twitter.com/NorthernSoho">
        @NorthernSoho
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.twitter.com/_creativelpool">
        @_creativelpool
       </a>
       in order to chat to other creatives.
      </p>
      <p>
       As I mentioned earlier, I also like working from co-working events such as
       <a href="http://www.twitter.com/JellyLiverpool">
        @JellyLiverpool
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.twitter.com/SalfordJelly">
        @SalfordJelly
       </a>
       . Jelly events are a great idea for freelancers or home-workers as it helps them to avoid feelings of isolation. Not only that, some of them provide free food!
       <a href="http://www.uk-jelly.org.uk">
        http://www.uk-jelly.org.uk
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Although the two above paragraphs are design-related, it doesnt really feel like
       <em>
        work
       </em>
       as such as I am out there meeting new and interesting people.
      </p>
      <p>
       I also spend a lot of time speaking to other creatives on Twitter, which helps me feel a lot more connected to the creative community. In fact I wouldnt even know about the events mentioned above if it wasnt for Twitter!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://www.mattpealing.co.uk/">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-8147" height="458" src="http://www.designjuices.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/04.png" title="04" width="578"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <em>
         What advice would you give to graduate web / graphic designers coming out of university hoping to take a similar route into design to yourself?
        </em>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Start off by getting in with a half decent agency, even if its only part time. This will give you some valuable insight into how the industry works and how other designers go about doing things. I thought I could just teach myself everything there is to know about the industry but seeing how a design agency operated when I started in one was certainly an eye opener.
      </p>
      <p>
       Also think about some pro-bono work to help build your portfolio and to help make a name for yourself, like some free or discounted work for a charity or none-profit. Make sure theyre not going to lose interest when youre halfway through working on a project for them though! So it could be a better idea to get at least *some* money for it, just to prove the client wants to see it through to completion.
      </p>
      <p>
       And last but not least, make sure your spread your name about online through social networks like Twitter, and showcase your work on websites like Behance and Dribbble.
      </p>
      <p>
       Also you can drive a bit of extra traffic to your website by leaving comments on other peoples blogs. These are all great ways to help make a name for yourself and get your work recognised.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <em>
         Who are your main inspirations in the creative fields? Do you take inspiration from any other creative industries?
        </em>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Funnily enough theres not really
       <em>
        that
       </em>
       many designers in particular who I regularly draw inspiration from. I do, however, find the work of
       <a href="http://www.thedesignersrepublic.com">
        The Designers Republic
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.behance.net/Sub88">
        David Vineis
       </a>
       really fascinating.
      </p>
      <p>
       I love how most of their work is ultra modern and abstract, always a massive inspiration when it comes to working on my own things (such as when Im working on my own branding). I love the way some of tDRs work has a little touch of humour too.
      </p>
      <p>
       Apart from those two, most of the inspirational work I look at just tends to be whatever I stumble across as I mentioned earlier. If I really like someones work on Behance for example, then Ill be sure to follow them.
      </p>
      <p>
       I dont so much take inspiration from other creative fields that I use in my work exactly, however I do find other fields really interesting nonetheless. Im really fond of modern architecture and often have trouble looking where Im going when there is any around, as Im generally to0 busy looking upwards. Im always keen to talk to creatives from other fields about what they do are the various creative get togethers I go to also.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Are you involved in any local or national creative events? Are these important to visit do you feel as a freelancer?
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Apart from Jelly, there are also the other shindigs I mentioned earlier.
       <a href="http://interconnectit.com/category/wordpress/interest-group-events/">
        SWIG
       </a>
       is the Scouse WordPress Interest Group (I think about 2% of the members are actual Scousers, but its still a cool name) which is an informal gathering for WordPress related banter, followed by the pub. Its just
       <em>
        just
       </em>
       about WordPress however as we cover the web in general, so dont feel put off if youre not interested in WordPress!
      </p>
      <p>
       Then theres
       <a href="http://www.twitter.com/NorthernSoho">
        @NorthernSoho
       </a>
       and Creative Liverpool (
       <a href="http://www.twitter.com/_creativelpool">
        @_creativelpool
       </a>
       ). Both are quite similar to each other, and are basically informal gatherings of like-minded creative folk. Northern Soho is based in Manchester and Creative Liverpool is based in, you guessed it, Liverpool! Both consist of people just having a few drinks and chatting about what they do for a living, as well as just generally getting to know each other whats not to like about that!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://www.mattpealing.co.uk/">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-8146" height="721" src="http://www.designjuices.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/05.png" title="05" width="539"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Thanks to
        <a href="http://www.mattpealing.co.uk/">
         Matt Pealing
        </a>
        for taking his time out for this Design Juices interview today, follow Matt
        <a href="https://twitter.com/#!/pealo86">
         @pealo86
        </a>
        on Twitter and on
        <a href="http://www.mattpealing.co.uk/">
         his Website
        </a>
        .
       </strong>
      </p>
