     <h2 class="title">
      <a href="http://www.andrewsgibson.com/blog/2011/06/finding-your-sweet-spot-an-ebook-by-ben-kadel/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Finding Your Sweet Spot: An eBook by Ben Kadel">
       Finding Your Sweet Spot: An eBook by Ben Kadel
      </a>
     </h2>
     <p class="postdate">
      June 27th 2011 by Andrew S Gibson
      <br/>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p align="center">
      
      <a href="http://www.andrewsgibson.com/blog/2011/06/microstock-from-passion-to-paycheck-%e2%80%93-an-ebook-by-nicole-s-young/">
       (Micro)stock: From Passion to Paycheck  An eBook by Nicole S. Young
      </a>
      |
      <a href="http://www.andrewsgibson.com/blog/2011/06/a-deeper-frame-an-ebook-by-david-duchemin/">
       A Deeper Frame: An eBook by David duChemin
      </a>
      
     </p>
     <div class="entry">
      <div class="mailmunch-forms-before-post" style="display: none !important;">
      </div>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <img alt="Finding Your Sweet Spot by Ben Kadel cover" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2312" height="528" src="http://www.andrewsgibson.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/findingyoursweetspot-1.jpg" style="margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 20px;" title="Finding Your Sweet Spot by Ben Kadel cover" width="410"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       As a writer and photographer creativity is an important part of my life. I feel happiest when Im creating things and its one of the reasons that I decided to become a writer. In my previous career, while thinking about what I wanted to do when I quit that job, I realised that it was about time to become involved in something creative. Ive been a creative person all my life, yet for some reason had never held a truly creative job. Its just one of those things  sometimes you get sidetracked.
       <span id="more-2311">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       I decided that I wanted to work as a writer, for a photography magazine, and one of the driving forces behind that decision was the motivation to be part of something creative. I wanted to see the results of my creativity at the end of each month and hold something in my hands that I could be proud of and helped to create.
      </p>
      <p>
       Now Im freelancing but the basic motivation is still the same. Every time that a new issue of EOS magazine or a new eBook comes out I have a deep sense of pride in what I created.
      </p>
      <p>
       As a creative freelance productivity is important to me. Its very easy to procrastinate or concentrate on things that seem interesting but dont bring money in at the end of the month (blog posts like these are a good example!) Part of productivity is time and project management  when something gets done I need to issue myself some kind of deadline and concentrate on getting it done.
      </p>
      <p>
       Things get done best when Im in the flow  that kind of magical state of mind that most creatives will be familiar with where everything is going well, theres no such thing as writers block and projects get completed, on time and to the best of my ability. It goes without saying, that the more I can get into a state of flow the more productive I am. Taking a long term view:
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        more flow = greater productivity = greater earning potential
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       So how do you get in the flow? For me, the trick is to get started. Once I get stuck into whatever needs doing I get caught up in it and work on it until the days work is completed. It helps that I work from home. I have a lunch break and tea breaks but I dont get any of the countless interruptions that you do in the workplace. I can ignore the phone, close my email application and just get on with things.
      </p>
      <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
      </div>
      <p>
       This fits in with an idea I came across about the difference in approach to time management that creatives have to business types. You may be familiar with the concept of breaking the day into 30 minute blocks of time, and allocating tasks to be done in each. Ouch! That way of working may suit a hard-working middle-manager or businessman but its not for me.
      </p>
      <p>
       Creatives work differently. We need long periods of time to concentrate on one thing. Our productivity accelerates as we get into a project. I often lose track of time when Im working on something and the day just whizzes by.
      </p>
      <h5>
       Finding Your Sweet Spot
      </h5>
      <p>
       In a roundabout way that brings me to an eBook that I purchased today:
       <a href="http://www.clamorate.com/2011/finding-your-sweetspot/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.clamorate.com/2011/finding-your-sweetspot/', 'Finding Your Sweet Spot');">
        Finding Your Sweet Spot
       </a>
       by
       <a href="http://www.emotusoperandi.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.emotusoperandi.com/', 'Ben Kadel');">
        Ben Kadel
       </a>
       . The sweet spot is Bens term for the flow and hes made something of a career out of studying the way that creativity works.
       <em>
        Finding Your Sweet Spot
       </em>
       is about the flow, and how to enter a state of flow more often. I read it straight through and need to go back to it and study some of the ideas in it, for the simple reason they are really good.
      </p>
      <p>
       One of the concepts that I liked is the idea that you start each day with a certain amount of energy. This energy can be sapped by things like negative emotions, office politics and procrastination. And any energy that disappears cant be directed into whatever work you need to do for the day.
      </p>
      <p>
       Another idea that will resonate with a lot of people is that we have a preconception that work is meant to be horrible. Enjoy your job? What a thought! What if work were fun? Bens point is that its much easier to find your sweet spot if youre having fun.
      </p>
      <p>
       Anyway, I just thought Id share this with you. If youre looking for ideas on boosting your creativity and productivity, then Finding Your Sweet Spot is well worth the $US5 price. Youll find the eBook
       <a href="http://www.clamorate.com/2011/finding-your-sweetspot/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.clamorate.com/2011/finding-your-sweetspot/', 'here');">
        here
       </a>
       at
       <a href="http://www.clamorate.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.clamorate.com/', 'Clamorate');">
        Clamorate
       </a>
       , a new website for creative entrepreneurs created by
       <a href="http://corwinhiebert.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://corwinhiebert.com/', 'Corwin Hiebert');">
        Corwin Hiebert
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.photographybb.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.photographybb.com/', 'Dave Seeram');">
        Dave Seeram
       </a>
       . The sites in its early days but looks promising and there are already some interesting articles on there. Well worth bookmarking if you make your living from any type of creative endeavour.
      </p>
