      Seven Important DIY Tips To Maintaining Your Oil Paintings
     </h1>
     <ul class="meta">

     <h2>
      Owning an Oil Painting and Maintaining It
     </h2>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Being the owner of a genuine work of art is always something the owner can take pride in. And with the ever increasing popularity of cyber art galleries and e shopping, purchasing an original
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/oil-paintings" rel="nofollow">
       oil painting
      </a>
      isnt a common gripe among art lovers anymore. However, just like choosing the painting you want to own is a task involving time, its maintenance also demands the owners time and patience. For without proper maintenance and care, the painting can and will lose its beauty and become less valuable for the owner and the viewer alike.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Though there are services out there which you can employ to maintain your paintings for you, were here to let you know a few dos and donts of maintaining
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/blog/decorate-your-home-walls-with-inexpensive-3d-wall-art/" rel="nofollow">
       paintings
      </a>
      ; some very effective
      <em>
       do it yourself (DIY) tips
      </em>
      which will enable you to easily take on the task yourself.
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Oil_Paintings_Thomas_Kinkade-e1405486123949.jpg">
       <img alt="Oil_Paintings_Thomas_Kinkade" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1171" height="490" src="http://artgaga.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Oil_Paintings_Thomas_Kinkade-e1405486123949.jpg" width="600"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <h2>
      Seven Tips To Caring For Your Oil Paintings
     </h2>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Here are seven diy tips you can apply to care for your oil paintings
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
      1. Dont Expose to an Immoderate Amount of Light
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Intense levels of light can potentially fade and/or darken a painting. In order to avoid this, make sure the paintings arent exposed to direct sunlight. Brighter the light, the greater the risk of exposure to heat, so try to use moderate light levels. Dont ever place the bulbs near the surface of the painting. Dont mount lights upon the frame of the painting. And lastly, instead of using direct light in close proximity to the painting, use scattered light from a safe distance of ten feet to fully avoid any damage from heat.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
      2. Dont Expose to Dark
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Avoid storing
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/oil-paintings/architecture" rel="nofollow">
       oil paintings
      </a>
      in the dark. If a painting is kept in a dark place for a long duration of time, it will start to become increasingly darker.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
      3. Dont Expose to High Temperatures &amp; Humidity
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Huge variations in temperature and humidity can cause the wooden frame and the canvas to expand or contract depending upon the change. Needless to say, this can cause damage to the painting. Both the canvas and the frame absorb moisture and absorbing moisture can cause them to expand when humidity is high and shrink when its low. As a result of this, the
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/oil-paintings/food" rel="nofollow">
       oil paint
      </a>
      can break and might even peel off as it wont expand with the changes.
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Abstract_Oil_painting-e1405489517104.jpg">
       <img alt="Abstract_Oil_painting" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1169" height="501" src="http://artgaga.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Abstract_Oil_painting-e1405489517104.jpg" width="600"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
      4. Maintain a set temperature range and humidity level
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Maintain this temperature range and humidity level in order to display your oil paintings as they are:
     </p>

      <tbody>
       <tr>
        <td valign="top" width="280">
         <p align="center">

         </p>
        </td>

          40%-45%
         </p>

          45%-55%

       </tr>

     </table>
     <p>
      <strong>
       If you are interested in buying abstract oil paintings, have a look at these
      </strong>
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/oil-paintings/abstract" rel="nofollow">
       <strong>
        huge range
       </strong>
      </a>
      <strong>
       of oil paintings.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <h3>
      5. Handle With Care
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      An old oil painting is nothing short of a relic. Its best that you take extra care about its handling. Make sure you hold the painting on both ends while moving or transporting it.
      <em>
       Dont hold it only from the top of the frame, and definitely, never ever by the hanging wire!
      </em>
      Remember, even the lightest of bumps on the
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/index.php?route=product/product&amp;product_id=4211" rel="nofollow">
       canvas
      </a>
      of the painting can cause a break in the surface of the paint, so avoid that at all cost.
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
      6. Dont Let Them get Dirty
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Once accumulated there, dirt will attract pollutants, moisture and mould upon the paintings surface. And of course these elements can damage the
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/index.php?route=product/product&amp;product_id=5774" rel="nofollow">
       artwork
      </a>
      so, its best you keep the painting in a smoke free environment and away from windows if you open them regularly. Try to avoid hanging them near candles and fireplaces as well.
      <a href="http://artgaga.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Street_Oil_Painting-e1405486013622.jpg">
       <img alt="Street_Oil_Painting" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1172" height="499" src="http://artgaga.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Street_Oil_Painting-e1405486013622.jpg" width="600"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
      7. Clean Your Paintings Regularly &amp; Carefully
     </h3>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      A great way to clean paintings is to lightly wipe the surface of the paint with a clean and dry soft cloth or an anti-static cloth (which is used to clean photographic negatives). The advantage is that these cloths behave like magnets and thus lift the dust from off the surface of the paintings while avoiding scratching and scraping. Balls of cotton, first made slightly wet by adding distilled water, can be pressed slightly on the surface to remove the dirt which you are unable to remove by wiping.
     </p>
