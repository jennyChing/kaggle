          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="meta">

         <p>
          Pji, pji! When you think China and you think beer quite frankly the first thing that comes to mind is the watered down Snow, Harbin and Tsingtao that they have the cheek to call beer. Now Ill fully admit Im not a beer drinker (I can hear all of my home country Australia sighing in disgust!) that being said I can appreciate a good bottle of brew! And if youre a Shanghai resident or just passing through the beer fairy has a grand treat for you, delicious, quality international beers thanks to four clever French guys and their new store
          <a href="http://cheers-in.com/">
           <em>
            Cheers In
           </em>
           .
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Cheers In
          </strong>
          is a newly opened boutique beer store on the quaint, done up stretch on Yongkang Lu in the French Concession. From floor to ceiling dark amber, deep chocolate and emerald bottles glitter like a beacon to all
          <strong>
           beer lovers.
          </strong>
          Beautifully designed labels tempt you to discover just whats inside the bottle, luring you to travel to over 30 different countries, tasting over 300 different brews, one bottle at a time all from the comfort of your couch! This is certainly not a dull Chinese beer experience!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://shanghainovice.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/cheers-in-5-e1320915085704.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-972" height="825" src="http://shanghainovice.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/cheers-in-5-e1320915085704.jpg" title="cheers-in 5" width="550"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          But it gets even better! If youre too lazy to visit the store (which you must do at some stage, its a beer shrine!) you can jump onto the online store place an order and in no time have cold beers delivered
          <strong>
           right to your door step!
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Browsing the
          <em>
           Cheers In
          </em>
          website is such a treat with easy to navigate categories to narrow down your search like country of origin, strong or not, dark or light and budget. The only category that was missing, girly beers or flavoured beers for gals like me who have no clue what to look for in a beer but know they love a tinge of sugar or fruity flavours.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://shanghainovice.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Cheers-In-Website.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-954" height="367" src="http://shanghainovice.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/Cheers-In-Website.jpg" title="Cheers In Website" width="500"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Perusing the website made my
          <strong>
           mouth water
          </strong>
          and for a seldom beer drinker thats a big deal! Each beer has a lovingly, poetically crafted description that will have you almost tasting it on your tongue like:
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          <em>
           It pours a milky pale orange. The aroma distinctly citrus and not much else but some sweetness.
           <strong>
            Schoefferhofer Grapefruit Mix Beer,
           </strong>
          </em>
          <strong>
           20RMB
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          <em>
           You know you shouldnt This is a beer that orange dress her hair with a fine mousse reliable persistence in the glass.
           <strong>
            BrewDog Trashy Blonde,
           </strong>
          </em>
          <strong>
           27RMB
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          <em>
           Burgundy color, lightly spicy taste, this beer is fragrant with coriander and curacao.
           <strong>
            Hoegarrden Forbidden Fruit,
           </strong>
          </em>
          <strong>
           28RMB
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Now once youve been seduced by the decadent descriptions and lively labels its time to add them to your shopping cart and hit order. And thats exactly what I did. Less then 2 hours later the very friendly Cedric arrived at my door with 4 chilled beers. What more could you ask for! Now if only my supermarket would deliver and that swiftly!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://shanghainovice.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/P1190096e.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-969" height="733" src="http://shanghainovice.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/P1190096e.jpg" title="P1190096e" width="550"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          So the store is delightful, the staff are fantastic, the website is packed full of info, easy to navigate and place your order and the delivery was swift with my beers arriving nice and cold, but what about my 4 beer presents from the beer fairy?
         </p>
         <p>
          Well I cant tell you about how it pours or what the head was like because its all the same for me but what I can tell you is that
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.cheers-in.com/beer/schoefferhofer-grapefruit-mix-beer-330-ml">
           <strong>
            Schofferhofer Grapefruit
           </strong>
          </a>
          despite looking like an alcho pop is most certainly beer, light and summery with just enough of a hint of grapefruit that you know its there but not enough to overpower the beer.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.cheers-in.com/beer/young-s-double-chocolate-stout-330-ml">
           <strong>
            Youngs Double Chocolate Stout
           </strong>
          </a>
          had me at chocolate, I was expecting this to be sugary but like any good quality piece of dark chocolate it was dark, smooth and bitter.
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.cheers-in.com/beer/delirium-tremens-330-ml">
            Delirium Tremens
           </a>
          </strong>
          I chose it entirely for the pretty label, what can I say appearance does matter and Im 100% shallow! This beer at a strong 8.5% was surprisingly not at all over powering, it was easy to drink and had a subtle flavour of something I couldnt place that like that mysterious guy or gal you can never get out your mind,
          <em>
           Delirium Tremens
          </em>
          is that mysterious beer that will stick in your mind!
         </p>
         <p>
          But out of all those fantastic beers that turned me from a sometimes beer drinker because it was just at the table to a beer drinker by choice the standout for me (call me girly) was
          <a href="http://www.cheers-in.com/beer/lindemans-framboise-raspberry-250-ml">
           <strong>
            Lindemans Framboise
           </strong>
           .
          </a>
          What can I say I fell in love with this raspberry beer with its fruity undertone, deep, sweet aroma and subtly bitter aftertaste. This is my perfect beer!
         </p>
         <p>
          So if you have a party coming up or just dont fancy moving from your couch to get a delicious international beer then jump online and put in your order, youll be sitting back with a cold one in no time! And if your ever strolling the
          <a href="http://shanghainovice.com/this-is-china-backstreets-of-the-french-concession/">
           French Concession
          </a>
          pop in and say hi to Cdric, Greg, Alex and Sam and most of all thanks for bringing international beers at an affordable price along with the convenience of buying to Shanghai, we needed it!
         </p>
