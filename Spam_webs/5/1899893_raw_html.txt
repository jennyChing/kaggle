I had an interest in business from the tender age of about 5 when i was selling my (less than Picasso) paintings to my parents from the shop' I&nbsp;had constructed in my bedroom. Growing up in a house where neither of my parents had conventional jobs - my mother was a freelance celebrity make up artist and my dad was a entrepreneur - I&nbsp;think business was instilled in me from a very young age. I have always loved the idea of taking an idea and making it a reality; being the author of my own destiny.            What were your first entrepreneurial ventureS?      Age 14  - I started a dance school called Stance where friends and I taught street dance in local after school and summer clubs.   Age 17  - I started an under 18s events company called Phoenix Productions with a friend, we only managed to do two profitable events before my parents forced me to stop and concentrate on my A-Levels.   Age 23  - was when i started my first serious business The Be Group which I&nbsp;still run now, which is a personal development services business offering; personal branding, employability and image consultancy. The idea came from me working for Accenture as HR advisor to their graduate group and assisting them on their personal development and promotion cycle. Realising even graduates from the best universities needed help on marketing themselves for the future.            What led you to applying for The Apprentice?   I had always been a fan of the show but hadnt thought of being on the show since I was under 18, then one day my fianc came home and suggested i apply. I&nbsp;said no quite definitively, I felt there was a significant level of risk by being in a competition like that. We have all been there shouting at the TV at the idiotic decisions they make or situations they are in and for me that reality was scary as I had high profile clients i wanted to be able to go back to. But after a few texts, emails and whats app messages with the link, I applied and was asked to the auditions - when I was offered the chance to be a candidate I decided for the experience it was a risk worth taking.            What are you working on at the moment?   I am developing my business The Be Group further by expanding our client base and hopefully gaining an international reach. I want to launch Bianca Miller London with an initial range of 8 shades of tights and lace top stockings and once we have some success&nbsp;with this initial range we&nbsp;will then start offering other innovative collections which align&nbsp;with the needs of the modern and fashion conscious woman.

         <div class="row sqs-row">
          <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-caa3aae95dddf1fbb5ac">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <h1 class="text-align-center">
              <strong>
               Bianca Miller interview
              </strong>
             </h1>
             <h3 class="text-align-center">
              <strong>
               <span>
                An interview with Apprentice finalist, Bianca Miller
               </span>
              </strong>
             </h3>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block horizontalrule-block sqs-block-horizontalrule" data-block-type="47" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1432819846039_23208">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <hr/>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1432819846039_23289">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <h3>
              <strong>
               When did you discover your love of business?
              </strong>
             </h3>
             <p>
              I had an interest in business from the tender age of about 5 when i was selling my (less than Picasso) paintings to my parents from the shop' Ihad constructed in my bedroom. Growing up in a house where neither of my parents had conventional jobs - my mother was a freelance celebrity make up artist and my dad was a entrepreneur - Ithink business was instilled in me from a very young age. I have always loved the idea of taking an idea and making it a reality; being the author of my own destiny.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block horizontalrule-block sqs-block-horizontalrule" data-block-type="47" id="block-yui_3_17_2_4_1432820324386_7191">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <hr/>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_4_1432820324386_7271">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <h3>
              <strong>
               What were your first entrepreneurial ventureS?
              </strong>
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </h3>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Age 14
              </strong>
              - I started a dance school called Stance where friends and I taught street dance in local after school and summer clubs.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Age 17
              </strong>
              - I started an under 18s events company called Phoenix Productions with a friend, we only managed to do two profitable events before my parents forced me to stop and concentrate on my A-Levels.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Age 23
              </strong>
              - was when i started my first serious business The Be Group which Istill run now, which is a personal development services business offering; personal branding, employability and image consultancy. The idea came from me working for Accenture as HR advisor to their graduate group and assisting them on their personal development and promotion cycle. Realising even graduates from the best universities needed help on marketing themselves for the future.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block horizontalrule-block sqs-block-horizontalrule" data-block-type="47" id="block-yui_3_17_2_4_1432820324386_8236">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <hr/>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_4_1432820324386_8315">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <h3>
              <strong>
               What led you to applying for The Apprentice?
              </strong>
             </h3>
             <p>
              I had always been a fan of the show but hadnt thought of being on the show since I was under 18, then one day my fianc came home and suggested i apply. Isaid no quite definitively, I felt there was a significant level of risk by being in a competition like that. We have all been there shouting at the TV at the idiotic decisions they make or situations they are in and for me that reality was scary as I had high profile clients i wanted to be able to go back to. But after a few texts, emails and whats app messages with the link, I applied and was asked to the auditions - when I was offered the chance to be a candidate I decided for the experience it was a risk worth taking.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block horizontalrule-block sqs-block-horizontalrule" data-block-type="47" id="block-yui_3_17_2_4_1432820324386_9083">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <hr/>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_4_1432820324386_9163">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <h3>
              <strong>
               What are you working on at the moment?
              </strong>
             </h3>
             <p>
              I am developing my business The Be Group further by expanding our client base and hopefully gaining an international reach. I am also working on the hosiery brand that I pitched in the final of the The Apprentice. The brand is called Bianca Miller London and we work to increase the number of skin tones represented in the nude hosiery arena. Our campaign to Redefine Nude has lead me to a crowd fund that allows customers to preorder their tights at a discount in advance of our AW15 launch and is really a opportunity to prove to retailers that consumers want a more diverse offering.
             </p>
             <p>
              You can preorder yours here:
              <a href="https://www.indiegogo.com/projects/bianca-miller-london-redefining-nude-hosiery">
               https://www.indiegogo.com/projects/bianca-miller-london-redefining-nude-hosiery
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              The crowd fund ends
              <a href="x-apple-data-detectors://10">
               on 21st June
              </a>
              so please spread the word using #ReDefineNude
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block horizontalrule-block sqs-block-horizontalrule" data-block-type="47" id="block-yui_3_17_2_4_1432820324386_9820">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <hr/>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_4_1432820324386_9899">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <h3>
              <strong>
               Future ambitions?
              </strong>
             </h3>
             <p>
              I want to launch Bianca Miller London with an initial range of 8 shades of tights and lace top stockings and once we have some successwith this initial range wewill then start offering other innovative collections which alignwith the needs of the modern and fashion conscious woman.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
