              My 8 year old is enjoying cub scouts. They do take an oath "to do my duty to my God" - I'm not sure what happens if you're an atheist! But that's about the only mention of God!
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I have found if you go to all the activities it can get a bit pricey. e.g. our group had a snow trip this year, no way we could afford DS to go. But most families seem to be a bit selective as to what they attend. Our group also has an annual family camp which is lots of fun!
              <br/>
              <br/>
              My son has ODD and we have found it good because they are achieving badges, but there's no completion as such as to who has the most badges. No competition = less opportunity to get into fights.

              I initially held off on joining my son up because of the God thing, and in hindsight I wish I hadn't.I honestly can't think of a downside - I consider my decision to take my son along to Cubs to be one of my best parenting decisions ever. It has been fabulous for his confidence, and he has had a huge amount of fun,
              <br/>

              <br/>
              The other potential downside is that it is a volunteer organisation, so they like parents to do their bit.Be willing to volunteer for something (fundraising, parent helper on a bushwalk, packing a trailer for camps etc) a couple of times a year.
