       <p>
        Up to 40,000 people have joined a so-called Anti-Maidan demo to mark the first anniversary of the bloody events during the Ukrainian coup on Kievs Independence Square, Russian police reported on Saturday.
       </p>
       <p>
        The 40,000-strong march against violence and revolutions,
  organized by the Anti-Maidan movement in the center of the
  Russian capital has been approved by local authorities, Moscow
  police said in a statement.
       </p>

        </em>

       </p>

       <p>
        The march had a slogan:
        <em>
         A year since Maidan. We wont
  forget, we wont forgive!
        </em>
        The banners people were carrying
  read:
        <em>
         No to Maidan  no to war
        </em>
        ,
        <em>
         Russia against
  Maidan
        </em>
        ,
        <em>
         Maidan is deceit
        </em>
        . According to its
  organizers, the rally numbered 50,000 people.
       </p>
       <p>
        Aleksandr Zaldostanov, the leader of Russian motorcycle club
        <em>
         The Night Wolves
        </em>
        , said:
        <em>
         When the mobilization of
  Russias enemies takes place, I call to rally round Russias
  president,
        </em>
        TASS reported.
       </p>
       <p>
        The Anti-Maidan movement was founded this January in Moscow by
  a group of social activists to counter what they believe could
  pose a threat to Russia. Its main goal is to prevent so-called
  color revolutions in the country.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt='People attend an "Anti-Maidan" rally to protest against the 2014 Kiev uprising, which ousted President Viktor Yanukovich, in Moscow February 21, 2015 (Reuters / Sergey Karpukhin)' src="/files/news/39/38/70/00/8.jpg" style="float: none; margin: 0px;"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Last February, central Kiev
        <a href="http://rt.com/news/233163-ukraine-maidan-february-timeline/">
         saw
        </a>
        several days of bloody clashes between rioters and the police at
  the
        <em>
         EuroMaidan
        </em>
        rallies, where approximately 100 people
  were killed on a single day
        <em>
         
        </em>
        February 20, 2014.
       </p>
       <p>
        Events led to then-President Viktor Yanukovich fleeing the
  country and the opposition forming a new government. In April,
  the new Kiev authorities started an anti-terrorist operation
  against breakaway regions in eastern Ukraine, who didnt
  recognize the coup-imposed authorities in Ukraine. According to
  the latest UN estimates, the resulting civil war has claimed the
  lives of at least 5,600 people.
       </p>
