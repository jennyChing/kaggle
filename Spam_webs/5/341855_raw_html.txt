         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Enlightenment + The Full Bust Adjustment
         </h1>
         <div class="meta-below-title entry-meta clearfix clearfix-title">
          <div class="left">
           Shared on
           <span class="entry-date published">
            January 19, 2009
           </span>
           by
           <a class="author-link fn nickname url" href="http://www.pinkchalkstudio.com/blog/author/administrator-2/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Kathy">
            Kathy
           </a>
           in
           <a href="http://www.pinkchalkstudio.com/blog/category/sewing/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Sewing">
            Sewing
           </a>
          </div>
          <!-- .left -->
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="333" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3430/3209467257_e5d2795105.jpg?v=0" title="my first attempt at altering a pattern" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I spent 8 hours on Saturday at the Pacific Fabrics in Bremerton learning the
           <a href="http://www.palmerpletsch.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.palmerpletsch.com']);">
            Palmer/Pletsch
           </a>
           method of fitting, taught by the very talented Nancy Seifert. 2 other lovely ladies and I spent the day walking around in our bras learning how to fit commercial patterns to our unique shapes. Part 2 is another 8 hours next Saturday.
          </p>
          <p>
           To say I had a light bulb moment would be an understatement. All the pattern failures Ive experienced over the last 10 years! OMG. I never even had a chance without the Full Bust Adjustment.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="371" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3450/3210314136_504e08b43a.jpg?v=0" title="the book we used in class" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The class uses the book
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/0935278656/?tag=wwwpinkchalks-20" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.amazon.com']);">
            Fit for Real People
           </a>
           and McCalls
           <a href="http://www.mccallpattern.com/item/M2718.htm?search=2718&amp;page=1" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.mccallpattern.com']);">
            Learn to Fit Palmer/Pletsch pattern 2718
           </a>
           . The instructor has bodices made up in each size and within the size theres one for each bust adjustment provided with the pattern. The student tries these on to determine their base pattern size.
          </p>
          <p>
           All commercial patterns are a B cup. The class takes the student through fitting a basic bodice adjusting from the B cup to their cup size plus all the other funky things a 46 year old body has like high round back, forward shoulder, high shoulder (from carrying those babies around!), sagging breasts (they refer to this as lowering the bust darts), and, my favorite, more waist width.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="500" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3075/3209468801_9e94bfa2bd.jpg?v=0" title="altered bodice back" width="333"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           A pattern adjustment technique exists for each type of alteration. Ive made several attempts over the years to use these books and teach myself at home how to do the alteration. Looking back that was just plain crazy. A trained instructor can identify exactly the alterations you need and move straight to Go by teaching you how to do each one. Ive always gotten bogged down in the past, feeling overwhelmed by all the different alterations possible and not being able to identify what I needed.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="500" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3433/3210314316_a647731149.jpg?v=0" title="altered bodice front" width="333"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           But lets go back to that full bust adjustment. You can see on the tissue here that I added a full 1 3/4 to the width of the bodice and deepened the bust darts accordingly. It fits perfectly!
          </p>
          <p>
           What was I doing before:
          </p>
          <p>
           1. I was buying size 14 or 16 patterns to match my bust measurement. My base pattern size should be a 12. The larger sized patterns created all kinds of baggy fitting issues and still didnt fit through the chest (because the patterns were still a B cup!)
          </p>
          <p>
           2. I was trying to give my chest space by extending the side seam allowances. Again, All I ended up with was a baggy shirt and it still didnt fit my bust.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="500" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3307/3210314672_ccfcbda7ee.jpg?v=0" title="altered sleeve" width="333"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           For next Saturdays class we bring in a commercial pattern that well tissue fit in class. I started practicing last night. Repeating the technique while its fresh in my mind is the way I learn best. Im so darned excited I can hardly stand it!
          </p>
          <p>
           1/23/09: I was asked about finding a class in your area. Ive updated the reference to
           <a href="http://www.palmerpletsch.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.palmerpletsch.com']);">
            Palmer/Pletsch
           </a>
           above with a link to their website. They provide a list of
           <a href="http://www.palmerpletsch.com/teacherlist-CSI.htm" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.palmerpletsch.com']);">
            certified instructors here
           </a>
           . Many of these instructors will teach a class at local sewing shops or provide custom classes for groups. I first heard about them from other members in my American Sewing Guild group. Definitely contact the person in your area for more info.
          </p>

           <div class="comment-body">
            <p>
             Both Simplicity and McCall patterns have a few patterns which have different patterns for B, C, and D-cups. They wont have all the adjustments needed, but I find that it can help to have some of the work already done!
            </p>
            <p>
             Its easier to locate the Simplicity ones on their website, under the Pattern Shop heading at the left navigating column, look under Special Collections for B, C, D cup Adjustment Collection and click to get a list/thumbnail of the patterns available. Some of the patterns are already in their out-of-print patterns, and those you have to look for by going through each top or dress listing  the adjustment is usually noted in the pattern description.
            </p>
            <p>
             McCalls are the Made-For-You patterns  but there isnt any easy way to find them on the website. Ive tried using the search function but as I get what seem to be random listings including everything from crafts to baby clothes, Im either entering the wrong words or the search engine isnt given access to the field that has those words.
            </p>
            <p>
             The one thing I do differently from the Palmer Pletsch method is that I trace the pattern pieces onto Swedish Tracing paper. Its sturdier than tissue paper, and I still have the original pattern pieces if I mess up the alterations. Id love to take the class, Ive just worked from the book (and from Fitting Finesse by Nancy Zieman), and some patterns I havent been able to adjust to work for me (ex. one of the Simplicity blouse patterns with the cup adjustments just wont work for me) and Id like to know why!
            </p>
