           <h1 class="blog-title">
            <a href="http://www.styledbytara.com/what-to-start-and-stop-doing/">
             What to Start and Stop Doing!
            </a>
           </h1>
           <div class="blog-content">
            <p>
             Hello my lovely readers! As you know I like to express my opinion and suggestions to anyone and anything that reads or listens,So check out my list of suggestions of what to Do and what Not to Do when it comes toyour beauty regiment.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.styledbytara.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/ausntm-6-kathryn-beauty-shot.jpg">
              <img alt="ausntm-6-kathryn-beauty-shot" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-112" height="819" src="http://www.styledbytara.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/ausntm-6-kathryn-beauty-shot-1024x819.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span class="caps">
               START
              </span>
             </strong>
             <br/>
             1. Experimenting with trends and color. Makeup isnt a tattoo! If you dont like it, wash it off and try another day! My favorite time to experiment with a new look isnt right before a big event. Would you believe its right before bed? Youve got to wash your face anyway so why not experiment.
            </p>
            <p>
             2. Defining your brows. It will make a noticeable difference and make you look younger in the process. You can define with powder, pencil, brow gels, or a combination of products.
            </p>
            <p>
             3. Using a retinol or retinoid product. I am in love with
             <a href="http://www.sephora.com/" target="_blank" title="Sephora">
              Philosophy Help Me
             </a>
             Retinol Night Treatment.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.styledbytara.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/images.jpeg">
              <img alt="images" class="aligncenter wp-image-114" height="337" src="http://www.styledbytara.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/images.jpeg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             4. Using an eye cream. The delicate skin around our eyes needs special attention. If you already have existing wrinkles, try
             <a href="http://tarasfashionblog.com/start-stop/www.sephora.com" target="_blank" title="Sephora">
              Philosophy Miracle Worker Eye Reform
             </a>
             or for more preventative care,
             <a href="http://thebay.com/" target="_blank" title="Hudson's Bay">
              Shiseido Ibuki Eye Correcting Cream.
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             5. Incorporating
             <span class="caps">
              SPF
             </span>
             into your moisturizer or primer. Skip a step and sneak your
             <span class="caps">
              SPF
             </span>
             into a step youre already doing. My favorite
             <span class="caps">
              SPF
             </span>
             moisturizer is
             <a href="http://www.nordstrom.com/" target="_blank" title="Nordstrom">
              Peter Thomas Roth Max All Day MoistureDefense
             </a>
             with
             <span class="caps">
              SPF
             </span>
             30 or
             <a href="http://www.divinecaroline.com/shop/go.html?p=50d2e010e4b04ce919d1e2e7&amp;pid=115" target="_blank">
              Smashbox Photo Finish Foundation Primer with
              <span class="caps">
               SPF
              </span>
              20
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             6. Using waterproof mascara on your bottom lashes. My favorite is
             <a href="http://www1.shoppersdrugmart.ca/" target="_blank" title="Shoppers Drugmart ">
              Maybelline Lash Discovery
             </a>
             , because the tiny brush is
             <em>
              perfect
             </em>
             for the lower lashes. Accentuating lower lashes only makes your look more complete. Waterproof formulas will ensure you dont get raccoon eyes.
            </p>
            <p>
             7. Washing your makeup brushes. Regularly. If you need details on how, stay tuned for a video.
            </p>
            <p>
             8. Enjoying your morning beauty routine! I like to put on my coziest robe and slippers, sip my coffee, play music and sometimes catch up on Good Morning America while I do my makeup. Its my favorite part of the day.
            </p>
            <p>
             9. Exfoliating. Either via a
             <a href="http://www.tradesecrets.ca/" target="_blank" title="Trade Secrets ">
              Clarisonic brush
             </a>
             or a gentle at-home peel. One of my favorite peels to do at home is
             <a href="http://www.tradesecrets.ca/" target="_blank" title="Trade Secrets ">
              Algenist Triple-Action Micropolish and Peel.
             </a>
             Or if you decide you want to be monitored with a professional then visit the fabulous
             <a href="http://http//www.claritelaser.ca/" target="_blank" title="Dr. Rice">
              Dr. Rice
             </a>
             . Start asking what you want to know. Always wanted to learn how to do a smoky eye? Ask a makeup artist to show you. Wondering how a friend always looks so put together? Ask her what product shes using. Curious about a new skincare innovation but afraid to try it on your skin? Ask for a sample at Sephora. You wont know until you try!
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.styledbytara.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/up-to-80-off-skin-treatments-964342-regular.jpg">
              <img alt="up-to-80-off-skin-treatments-964342-regular" class="aligncenter wp-image-115" height="372" src="http://www.styledbytara.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/up-to-80-off-skin-treatments-964342-regular.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span class="caps">
               STOP
              </span>
             </strong>
             <br/>
             1. Thickly lining your bottom lash line. Placing too much emphasis on your lower lash line drags your eye down, making your eyes look smaller and making you look older. Outlining your entire eye in black liner should be reserved for smoky eye looks at night.
            </p>
            <p>
             2. Wearing your blush too low. Wearing blush below your nose visually drags your cheeks down, making you look older and your cheeks saggier. Imagine a line below the end of your nose that connects to the end of your ears and try to keep blush above it.
            </p>
            <p>
             3. Overlining your lips to make them appear larger. This never looks natural. If you want the appearance of fuller lips, use light-reflecting glosses, nude shades with lip plumpers.
            </p>
            <p>
             4. Using old school wedge sponges to apply your foundation. These soak up your foundation and hold bacteria. If you must sponge, a beautyblender is the way to go.
            </p>
            <p>
             5. Lining only the outside of your lips with lip liner and doing so after lipstick. A fresher look: Outline your lip shape and fill the lips in entirely before applying lipstick. This way, when your lipstick wears away, you still have color underneath and not a ring around the mouth. Filling in also gives the lipstick something to which it can anchor.
            </p>
            <p>
             6. Using bronzer all over the face to appear more tan. Malibu Barbie, theres a better way! If you must use a bronzer, use a fluffy brush to lightly dust on the high points of your face. Otherwise, I use a matte bronzer to add shadow to contour areas only-under the cheekbones, at temples and hairline.
            </p>
            <p>
             7. Smoking. This doesnothing for you in the beauty department. Many employers help cover the costof smoking cessation programs. Find support and kick this habit. Your face will thank you.
            </p>
            <p>
             8. Tanning. You can still be a bronzed beauty without the harsh effects of natural sunlight. Trysomesunless products- its 2015 they have gotten better, you wont look like a carrot this time.
            </p>
            <p>
             9. Using one eye shadow brush for everything. No single brush can do it all. If you find your shadow never looks as blended and professional as youd like, I bet you are relying on one brush to do it all. At the very least, you need a fluffy crease brush and a flat shader brush.
            </p>
            <p>
             10. Using a nude lip color. There are nude lip colors and then there are what I call mouth-erasers. Your skin tone plays a big role here but nearly every one would benefit from a little pink or peach in their nude lip color. Colors like
             <span class="caps">
              MAC
             </span>
             s Creme de Nude are extremely difficult to wear. One of my favourites is
             <a href="http://www.sephora.com/" target="_blank" title="Sephora">
              <span class="caps">
               NARS
              </span>
              Lip Lacquer in Chelsea Girls.
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Let me know whatyou think of my suggestion and what your favourite products are!
            </p>
            <p>
             Till next time xo
            </p>
