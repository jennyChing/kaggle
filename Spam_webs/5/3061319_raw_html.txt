     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Battle at Kruger
       </b>
      </i>
      is an eight-minute amateur wildlife video that depicts an unfolding confrontation between a herd of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_buffalo" title="Cape buffalo">
       Cape buffalo
      </a>
      , a small pride of
      <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">
       lions
      </a>
      , and one
      <a href="/wiki/Crocodile" title="Crocodile">
       crocodile
      </a>
      . The video was shot in September 2004 at the Transport Dam
      <a href="/wiki/Depression_(geology)" title="Depression (geology)">
       watering hole
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Kruger_National_Park" title="Kruger National Park">
       Kruger National Park
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">
       South Africa
      </a>
      , during a
      <a href="/wiki/Safari" title="Safari">
       safari
      </a>
      guided by Frank Watts. It was filmed by videographer David Budzinski and photographer Jason Schlosberg.
     </p>
     <p>
      Since being posted on
      <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">
       YouTube
      </a>
      on 3 May 2007,
      <i>
       Battle at Kruger
      </i>
      has received 75 million views and has become a
      <a href="/wiki/Viral_video" title="Viral video">
       viral video
      </a>
      sensation. It was widely praised for its dramatic depiction of wildlife on the African
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Savannah" title="Savannah">
       savannah
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It has since become one of YouTube's most popular nature videos, and has won the Best Eyewitness Video in the 2nd Annual YouTube Video Awards.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The video was also the subject of an article in the 25 June 2007 issue of
      <a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">
       <i>
        Time
       </i>
      </a>
      magazine,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and was featured in the first episode of
      <a href="/wiki/ABC_News" title="ABC News">
       ABC News
      </a>
      '
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/I-Caught" title="I-Caught">
        i-Caught
       </a>
      </i>
      , which aired on 7 August 2007. A
      <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Society" title="National Geographic Society">
       National Geographic
      </a>
      documentary on the video debuted on the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Channel" title="National Geographic Channel">
       National Geographic Channel
      </a>
      on 11 May 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
       Background
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_at_Kruger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Taken from a small
      <a href="/wiki/Game_viewer_vehicle" title="Game viewer vehicle">
       vehicle
      </a>
      on the opposite side of the watering hole with a digital
      <a href="/wiki/Camcorder" title="Camcorder">
       camcorder
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the video begins with the herd of African buffalos approaching the water, unaware that lions are crouched nearby lying in wait for them. Upon seeing the lions, the buffalos flee and the lions charge and disperse the herd, picking off a buffalo calf and unintentionally knocking it into the water while attempting to make a kill. As the lions try to drag the buffalo out of the water, it is grabbed by a crocodile, who fights for it in a brief
      <a href="/wiki/Tug_of_war" title="Tug of war">
       tug of war
      </a>
      before giving up and leaving it to the lions. The lions lie down and prepare to feast, but are quickly surrounded by the massive reorganized buffalo herd, which moves in and surrounds the lions. One of the lions is tossed into the air by the alpha male buffalo and the buffalo chases it away. The remaining lions are subsequently scattered and chased away soon after the initial engagement, and the baby buffalo escapes into the herd while a few lions remain surrounded by the buffalos. The buffalos then proceed to aggressively chase the remaining lions away.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Expert_commentary">
       Expert commentary
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_at_Kruger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Expert commentary">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Two veterinarians and animal behaviorists interviewed by
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">
        Time
       </a>
      </i>
      assert that the behavior exhibited by the buffalo is not unusual. Dr. Sue McDonnell of the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">
       University of Pennsylvania
      </a>
      (School of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veterinary_Medicine" title="Veterinary Medicine">
       Veterinary Medicine
      </a>
      )
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      said of the video:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       "The larger herd is broken down into smaller
       <a href="/wiki/Harem" title="Harem">
        harems
       </a>
       , with a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alpha_male" title="Alpha male">
        dominant male
       </a>
       and several females and their babies. If a youngster is threatened, both the harem males and bachelor males  which usually fight with one another  will get together to try to rescue it."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         8
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      It is, however, rare for such events to be captured on film even by professional wildlife photographers. Indeed Dereck Joubert, a photographer and writer
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      for
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Geographic_magazine" title="National Geographic magazine">
        National Geographic
       </a>
      </i>
      said of the video:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       "There is no doubt at all that the tourist who shot that scene [...] was unbelievably lucky. I mean, we would've considered ourselves lucky to have had that whole scene happen in front of us."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         10
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
