        <div class="body parsys">

         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           OTTAWAWhat do Canadian veterans, advocates for the disabled and the countrys largest union have in common? Their activities were monitored and reported on by police and government agencies over the last year.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Documents show the central Government Operations Centre received reports on more than 160 protests, community events, and demonstrations between May 2014 and February 2015. The RCMP, Public Safety Canada, and the Privy Council Office prepared reports for the GOC  which co-ordinates the federal governments response to national emergencies and natural disasters.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           While much of the monitoring focused on First Nations causes and environmental activism, the GOC showed a diverse set of interests, including:
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <li>
           A rally on Parliament Hill pushing for better benefits for Canadian veterans.
          </li>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <li>
           A die-in protesting police brutality against black Americans, including
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/news/gta/2014/11/25/thousands_protest_against_ferguson_grandjury_decision_in_toronto.html">
            vigils for Ferguson, Mo. shooting victim Michael Brown
           </a>
           organized by the Black Lives Matter movement.
          </li>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <li>
           An event called Paddle for Peace in Fort St. Jean, B.C., where the report noted public order issues are not expected.
          </li>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <li>
           Canadian Doctors for Refugee Cares national day of action.
          </li>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <li>
           An interfaith peace demonstration in Mississauga.
          </li>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The RCMP also reported on the Occupy movement in B.C.s efforts to reach out to like-minded groups to face the Industrial Hydra. The intelligence looks to have come from a Facebook posting.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The documents show police and government agencies have been active on Facebook, reporting on protest plans organized through social media. One report to the centre dealt with a potential cyber protest aimed at the oil and gas industry.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The centre was established by the former Liberal government in 2004. It was meant to provide 24/7 monitoring and situational awareness to government departments about potential or actual hazards on a national scale.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The centre co-ordinates with federal departments, provincial and territorial governments, and other countries, including the United States.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           But Wayne Easter, a former solicitor general and the Liberal MP who obtained the documents, said the agency seems to have strayed from its original mandate.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Theyre supposed to be there in terms of co-ordinating operations for the safety and security of Canadians and the need for an initial response to, whether its a natural disaster or a man-made disaster, in the most effective and efficient way possible, Easter said Tuesday. Thats what theyre supposed to be doing.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           A June 2014 letter leaked to the Ottawa Citizen asked all government departments and agencies to help the operations centre build a comprehensive list of demonstrations across the country.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           An operations centre analysis obtained by The Canadian Press earlier this month found that few demonstrations actually
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/news/canada/2015/03/18/federal-government-eyes-protests-in-national-risk-forecast. html">
            meet the criteria
           </a>
           for events in the national interest, leading to questions about why seemingly innocuous gatherings are being recorded by the centre.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Its a really ridiculous way to go about doing something, said Michael Vonn, policy director at the B.C. Civil Liberties Association. The notion that there might be something that could happen, an eruption if you will ... in the context of any of these demonstrations, constitutionally-protected exercises of peoples rights, are so rare that the sense that were going to survey the landscape for the purpose is instantly suspect.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The Star reported in September that the centre has received reports on around
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/news/canada/2014/09/18/ottawa_admits_to_tracking_hundreds_of_protests.html">
            800 demonstrations
           </a>
           , community events, and protests since 2006. The reports vary from open source intelligence (such as newspaper reports), to information provided by the RCMP and the Canadian Security Intelligence Service.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Activists have raised concerns the Conservative governments new anti-terror law, Bill C-51, would allow CSIS even greater powers to
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/opinion/commentary/2015/02/17/bill-c-51-moves-us-one-step-closer-to-the-end-of-privacy. html">
            spy on their activities
           </a>
           .
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Conservative MPs, including Public Safety Minister Steven Blaney, have repeatedly said that lawful protests do not fall under C-51s purview. But numerous internal documents reported by the Star and other outlets show law enforcement agencies already have a keen interest in lawful protests.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           We asked about this some time ago, and the government assured us that they were concerned these groups were about to turn violent, and they were going to keep monitoring them, said Randall Garrison, the NDPs public safety critic. I just cant understand how they could continue to waste public resources on this.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The Star requested an interview with Blaneys office, as well as the RCMP, for this article. Detailed questions were sent to both.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           In a brief prepared statement, Blaneys spokesman, Jean-Christophe de la Rue said the government respects Canadians right to protest peacefully, and that the operations centre does not conduct its own surveillance.
          </p>
         </div>
