      <div class="eza-body clearfix p402_premium" id="story-body">
       <p>
        For 35 years, students and professors at
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Sonoma+State+University" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Sonoma State University
        </a>
        in
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/California" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         California
        </a>
        have been taking a hard look at American media, concluding that some of the most important stories have been censored.
       </p>
       <p>
        Censorship is a provocative word, usually implying government control of news or other information that deserves to be publicly known. Think
        <a href="http://www.csmonitor.com/Topics/wikileaks" target="_blank">
         WikiLeaks
        </a>
        and its controversial editor-in-chief
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Julian+Assange" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Julian Assange
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        But in the context of
        <a href="http://www.projectcensored.org/" target="_blank">
         Project Censored
        </a>
        at Sonoma State, its much more than that:
       </p>
       <div class="promo_link ezc-csm_list_article">
        <span class="recommended_designation">

        </span>

       <p>
        Its the intentional non-inclusion of a news story  or piece of a news story  based on anything other than a desire to tell the truth, according to the projects mission statement. Such manipulation can take the form of political pressure (from government officials and powerful individuals), economic pressure (from advertisers and funders), and legal pressure (the threat of lawsuits from deep-pocket individuals, corporations, and institutions).
       </p>

       <p>
        Every year, Project Censored students and faculty consider hundreds of issues and news stories, then a panel of judges whittles it down to 25.
       </p>
       <p>
        Here are the headlines for the top 10 for this past year:
       </p>
       <p>
        1. Global Plans to Replace the Dollar
        <br/>
        2.
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/U.S.+Department+of+Defense" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         US Department of Defense
        </a>
        is the Worst Polluter on the Planet
        <br/>
        3. Internet Privacy and Personal Access at Risk
        <br/>
        4. ICE Operates Secret Detention and Courts
        <br/>
        5. Blackwater (Xe): The Secret
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/United+States" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         US
        </a>
        War in Pakistan
        <br/>
        6. Health Care Restrictions Cost Thousands of Lives in US
        <br/>
        7. External Capitalist Forces Wreak Havoc in Africa
        <br/>
        8. Massacre in
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Amazon+Rain+Forest" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Peruvian Amazon
        </a>
        over US Free Trade Agreement
        <br/>
        9. Human Rights Abuses Continue in Palestine
        <br/>
        10. US Funds and Supports the Taliban
       </p>
       <p>
        Among the other top 25: 
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Barack+Obama" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Obama
        </a>
        s Charter School Policies Spread Segregation and Undermine Unions, War Crimes of
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Stanley+McChrystal" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         General Stanley McChrystal
        </a>
        , The H1N1 Swine Flu Pandemic: Manipulating Data to Enrich Drug Companies, and Increased Tensions with Unresolved 9/11 Issues.
       </p>
       <p>
        As the headlines suggest, the general inclination is progressive (i.e., leftist). And although Project Censored has been praised by some well-known media figures (the late
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Walter+Cronkite" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Walter Cronkite
        </a>
        among them), it has been criticized for some of its choices  including conspiracy theories about the September 2001 terrorist attacks in
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/New+York" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         New York
        </a>
        and
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Washington%2c+DC" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Washington
        </a>
        (over which two Project Censored judges resigned in protest).
       </p>
       <p>
        Since its founding in 1976, Project Censoreds work has entered the world of the Internet, cable television, blogging, and the crowd sourcing of news where anybody with a smart phone can publish or broadcast via social media like
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Twitter+Inc." rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Twitter
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        But Mickey Huff, Project Censoreds current director, says the project remains relevant  perhaps more so these days  as a goad to the media.
       </p>
       <p>
        Increasingly, corporate media is proving itself irrelevant in terms of news and reporting as more and more Junk Food News (
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Hostess+Twinkies" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Twinkies
        </a>
        for the brain) becomes standard programming (with stories like
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Falcon+Heene" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Balloon Boy
        </a>
        , various celebrity deaths and anniversaries of them, the latest escapades of
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Britney+Spears" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Britney Spears
        </a>
        or
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Lindsay+Lohan" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         Lindsay Lohan
        </a>
        , "reality" television, and the like), he
        <a href="http://www.truth-out.org/project-censoreds-mickey-huff-finds-news-that-didnt-make-news66231" target="_blank">
         said in a recent interview
        </a>
        with the progressive news and commentary web site Truthout. In the US, corporate media help create an excited delirium of knowinglessness on a hyperreal landscape where the end result is a confused and alienated public  from FOX to
        <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Cable+News+Network" rel="nofollow" target="_self">
         CNN
        </a>
        , and across the AM radio dial, we increasingly have vitriol not virtue, gossip not fact, surface not substance.
       </p>
       <p>
        Thats an assertion that shouldnt be censored.
        <span class="eos_marker" id="end-of-story" title="-30-">
        </span>
       </p>
