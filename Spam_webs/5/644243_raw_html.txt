        <p>
         <img alt="Guide to Visiting Plitvice Lakes" height="433" src="//www.rearviewmirror.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/plitvice-lakes-croatia-7.jpg" title="Guide to Visiting Plitvice Lakes" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As stunning as Plitvice Lakes is, I must admit its not somewhere I want spend all day strolling around. We barrelled past slow paced tourists, only stopping at the most scenic points, and finished a 5 hour walking trail in 3 hours. I dont need to linger to appreciate the beauty of a place and Plitvice is certainly beautiful but a couple of hours of lakes and waterfalls is enough for me.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Emerald Green Lakes at Plitvice" height="433" src="//www.rearviewmirror.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/plitvice-lakes-croatia-9.jpg" title="Emerald Green Lakes at Plitvice" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         My Quick Guide
        </h2>
        <p>
         Just because I dont like to be trapped on a set circuit for 5 hours doesnt mean I dont recommend a visit to Croatias most important National Park. Experiencing the emerald green lakes and waterfalls of all sizes was one of the highlights of my time in Croatia and I would love to go back in winter to see it under snow.
        </p>
        <p>
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
         <!-- RVM Plitvice -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-0938906102171120" data-ad-slot="7067646755" style="display: inline-block; width: 300px; height: 250px;">
         </ins>
         <script type="text/javascript">

         </script>
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
         <!-- RVM Plitvice -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-0938906102171120" data-ad-slot="7067646755" style="display: inline-block; width: 300px; height: 250px;">
         </ins>
         <script type="text/javascript">
          // &lt;![CDATA[
(adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
// ]]&gt;
         </script>
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres my guide and tips for getting the best out of a one day visit to Plitvice Lakes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Waterfalls at Plitvice in Croatia" height="433" src="//www.rearviewmirror.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/plitvice-lakes-croatia-6.jpg" title="Waterfalls at Plitvice in Croatia" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Getting There
        </h3>
        <p>
         Many people visit Plitvice Lakes on a day trip from either
         <a href="http://www.rearviewmirror.tv/picturing-zagreb/">
          Zagreb
         </a>
         or Zadar. I decided against this for two reasons. Firstly, by staying in Plitvice you can get to lakes before the day trippers arrive ie. before the masses of tourists arrive. Secondly, I thought I might want to get a two-day pass to visit Plitvice and staying nearby is the best way to do that.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you prefer you can do a day trip from Zagreb or Split. Book your tour here:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.partner.viator.com/en/16005/tours/Zagreb/Small-Group-Plitvice-Lakes-National-Park-Day-Trip-from-Zagreb/d5391-5360LAKES">
           Plitvice Lakes Day Trip from Zagreb
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         In the end though, I only got the single day pass and that was definitely the best decision for me. One day at Plitvice Lakes is more than enough. I know its not always possible but if you want to see more Id recommend visiting again at a different time of the year to see the lakes during a different season, in a different light.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Visiting Plitvice in Summer" height="433" src="//www.rearviewmirror.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/plitvice-lakes-croatia-13.jpg" title="Visiting Plitvice in Summer" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         When is the Best Time to Visit?
        </h3>
        <p>
         If you dont want to share the walkways with hundreds of other tourists, have people in your photos or be walking in the heat of the day, Id suggest arriving at the lakes before 9am. When we finished our visit at 12pm the walkways were very crowded and tour groups were still arriving. I was glad to get out of there at that time!
        </p>
        <p>
         The lakes are expected to be even busier in 2015 so get there early or visit in spring or autumn.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Tourists at Plitvice Lakes" height="433" src="//www.rearviewmirror.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/plitvice-lakes-croatia-10.jpg" title="Tourists at Plitvice Lakes" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As far as the best time of the year to visit goes, Id recommend visiting in spring when the waterfalls are at their peak or in autumn when the trees start to change colour. I visited in June and it was a nice time to visit, not too hot and not too crowded. When to avoid visiting Plitvice? I would definitely stay away in July and August, the two busiest months of the year for tourism in Croatia.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Green Lakes in Croatia" class="aligncenter" height="650" src="//www.rearviewmirror.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/plitvice-lakes-22.jpg" title="Green Lakes in Croatia" width="488"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Which Trail to Take?
        </h3>
        <p>
         The staff at our hotel recommended taking Trail C from Entrance No 1. This typically takes 4 to 6 hours. You can read about the
         <a href="http://en.np-plitvicka-jezera.hr/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=71&amp;Itemid=85">
          other trails here
         </a>
         or ask at the tourist office at the entrance.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Trail C at Plitvice Lakes National Park" height="433" src="//www.rearviewmirror.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/plitvice-lakes-croatia-8.jpg" title="Trail C at Plitvice Lakes National Park" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Should You Take a Tour?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Honestly, I cant imagine why you would want to take a tour or have a guided visit. The trails are very clearly marked and on an independent visit you can walk at your own pace. Tours are offered from Zagreb and Zadar but I see no benefit in taking one and being guided along the marked paths. The tourist office at the entrance can advise you on which trail best suits your needs and you dont need much more than that. You can even get by without a map.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Mini Waterfall" height="433" src="//www.rearviewmirror.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/plitvice-lakes-croatia-4.jpg" title="Mini Waterfall" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Can You Swim in the Lakes?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Swimming in Plitvice Lakes is forbidden as its a protected UNESCO World Heritage Site and National Park.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="No Swimming at Plitvice Lakes" height="433" src="//www.rearviewmirror.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/plitvice-lakes-croatia-3.jpg" title="No Swimming at Plitvice Lakes" width="650"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Food and Drinks
        </h3>
        <p>
         Another reason to arrive early is so you can be finished by lunch time and avoid eating the overpriced food within the park. Drinks are a little pricey too so Id recommend taking your own if youre on a budget. Youll need lots of water if youre hiking through the park on a hot day and the cafes/kiosks are quite a distance apart which is another reason to take your own drinks.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Entrance Tickets and Parking
        </h3>
        <p>
         A daily ticket is 110 kuna and the two day ticket is 180 kuna (its cheaper in the off season). The entrance ticket includes a boat ride on the lake and the tourist train to take you back to the entrance. The park is open from 7am to 8pm in summer (
         <a href="http://en.np-plitvicka-jezera.hr/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=40&amp;Itemid=55">
          see here for opening times
         </a>
         for the rest of the year). Entrance No 2 is closed from October to March.
        </p>
        <p>
         Parking at Entrance No 1 costs 7 kuna per hour or 70 kuna for the day.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Where to Stay
        </h3>
        <p>
         I stayed at
         <a href="http://www.rearviewmirror.tv/rvm/plitvice-hotel.php">
          Hotel Degenija
         </a>
         , a 4 star hotel located 5 kms from Entrance No 1. Its one of the nicest hotels in the area and the location is perfect. We also had a nice meal of local trout at their restaurant.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you are day tripping from Zagreb I recommend the
         <a href="http://www.rearviewmirror.tv/rvm/zagreb-hotel.php">
          Arcotel
         </a>
         where I have stayed a number of times.
        </p>
