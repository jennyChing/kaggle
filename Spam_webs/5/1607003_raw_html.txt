        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         US drought set to intensify in 2013
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="bottomcontainerBox" style="border:1px solid #808080;background-color:#F0F4F9;">

        <div style="clear:both">

        <div style="padding-bottom:4px;">
        </div>
        <p>
         The US starts 2013 with drought more severe and widespread than this time last year
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/materialsrisk/8346548014/" title="US drought conditions 2013 vs 2012 by Materials Risk, on Flickr">
          <img alt="US drought conditions 2013 vs 2012" height="330" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8076/8346548014_44f3f980ed_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="code-block code-block-1" style="text-align:left;margin:8px 0px;">
         <strong>
          Unlock commodity market insight now and
          <a href="http://materials-risk.us4.list-manage.com/subscribe?u=1c5ef2efbc2ebf9523c59f9b7&amp;id=7456528d2c">
           subscribe
          </a>
          to our email updates or
          <a class="twitter-follow-button" data-show-count="false" href="https://twitter.com/materialsrisk">
           Follow @materialsrisk
          </a>
          <script>
           !function(d,s,id){var js,fjs=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];if(!d.getElementById(id)){js=d.createElement(s);js.id=id;js.src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js";fjs.parentNode.insertBefore(js,fjs);}}(document,"script","twitter-wjs");
          </script>
         </strong>
        </div>
        <p>
         and according to the latest forecast from the National Weather Service, drought will persist or intensify over most of the US into 2013.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/materialsrisk/8345493813/" title="US drought forecast 2013 by Materials Risk, on Flickr">
          <img alt="US drought forecast 2013" height="425" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8501/8345493813_6dea410066_z.jpg" width="546"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Related article:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://materials-risk.com/business-counts-cost-extreme-weather-reduce-risk/" target="_blank">
          Weather risk: Mother Nature is a hot commodity
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Historical data suggests there will be little improvement in crop growing conditions. The Palmer drought index fell to -4 in July 2012 and as of early January parts of the Mid-West corn and soyabean growing regions remain at -4.
        </p>
        <p>
         History suggests that soil conditions take between 18 to 51 months to recover once drought levels reach -4.
        </p>
        <p>
         All of this points to higher corn and soyabean prices in 2013. A recent note from Goldman Sachs called for corn prices to rise to $8.25 per bushel within six months, just shy of 2012 record levels.
        </p>
