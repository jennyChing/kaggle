        <p>
         Not really sure I can call these carmelitas, but if not I am hoping the carmelita police is busy pursuing other more serious offenders.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Peanut Butter Brownie Caramelita's!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47193" data-pin-description="peanut butter brownie caramelitas ~ http://iambaker.net" height="600" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/caramel-brownies-1.jpg" width="400"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I kinda love the name brownie carmelita and want to keep it so please dont tell me if it is completely being used out of context.
        </p>
        <p>
         But you can see why right? I mean, yeah. That carmel is pretty much all over the place with its awesome buttery smooth richness.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Peanut Butter Brownie Caramelita's!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47194" data-pin-description="peanut butter brownie caramelitas ~ http://iambaker.net" height="600" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/caramel-brownies.jpg" width="493"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         They looked harmless enough at first. But then I started cutting into them and the crispy flakey chocolately crust cracked under the pressure of my knife.
        </p>
        <p>
         Just under the surface was this river of golden carmel.
        </p>
        <p>
         I may have shed a tear of joy, or maybe it was drool but either way it was hard to contain my excitement.
        </p>

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup butter melted, then cooled
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             4 eggs
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 teaspoons McCormick vanilla
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 cups white sugar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             2/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup all-purpose flour
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 teaspoon salt
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 teaspoon baking powder
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup peanut butter morsels
            </li>
            <div class="ingredient-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9">
             Caramel Sauce
            </div>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 eleven ounce bag of Kraft Caramel Bits
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoon McCormick vanilla extract
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 cup milk
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-13" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-14" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Melt butter in microwave and set aside to cool slightly.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Sift together all dry ingredients in a large bowl.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             In a medium bowl, beat the eggs with the vanilla. Add into the dry ingredients  and gently stir.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Pour the melted butter into brownie mixture and mix until just incorporated.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Spread 1/2 of the brownie batter (about 2 cups)  into a prepared 9-inch square pan and bake for 10-12 minutes at 350 degrees.
            </li>
            <div class="instruction-label" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5">
             Caramel Sauce
            </div>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Place caramel bits in a medium saucepan and turn heat to medium-low.  Add in milk and stir until all caramel bits are melted (about 5 minutes).  Remove from heat and add in vanilla, stir to full incorporate.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             When time is up, remove brownie from oven and cover with 1 cup of peanut butter morsels.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Pour caramel sauce over over peanut butter morsels.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-9" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Cover caramel sauce with remaining two cups of brownie batter.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-10" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until edges start to get a little dark.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-11" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Remove from oven and allow to cool completely, at least 4 hours.
            </li>
           </ol>

        <p>
         <img alt="Carmel Brownies with Peanut Butter!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47195" data-pin-description="peanut butter brownie caramelitas ~ http://iambaker.net" height="428" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/caramel-brownies-2.jpg" width="600"/>
         Tips for Success:
        </p>
        <p>
         I mention in the recipe to use a prepared pan. For this recipe specifically, I recommend lining the pan with parchment paper that extends up over the sides. Carmel can be very sticky and unforgiving when washing dishes! Not only does the parchment help you to easily remove the brownies from the pan, but also means there is one less dish to wash.
        </p>
        <p>
         You are baking one layer of brownie then immediately adding some peanut butter morsels over top, then covering the morsels with hot caramel. (and THEN the remaining uncooked brownie batter) Even though there is a lot of heat there, not all the morsels will melt. I preferred the texture of unmelted morsels, but if you prefer you can melt the morsels first and spread on with an offset spatula.
        </p>
        <p>
         Make sure that you allow the brownies to cool before cutting. The caramel does need the time to cool so that it may slightly solidify.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Peanut Butter Brownie Carmelita's!" class="aligncenter wp-image-47196 size-full" data-pin-description="peanut butter brownie caramelitas ~ http://iambaker.net" height="600" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/caramel-brownies-3.jpg" width="379"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you happen to love brownies and peanut butter and carmel I cannot recommend these brownie carmelitas more. They seriously are one of the BEST brownies I have ever made and eaten and I will be making them for everyone that comes over. Be warned! 
        </p>

           <p>
            I made these last night and it was a disaster so Im hoping you might be able to help me figure out where I went wrong. The recipe didnt state what temp to bake at so I just did it at 350 degrees.
           </p>
           <p>
            I couldnt find Kraft caramel bits in the grocery so I used an 11oz bag of caramel squares. They were all individually wrapped so I unwrapped and melted. I used Hersheys unsweetened cocoa, King Arthur organic unbleached all purpose flour, whole organic milk, KerryGold butter, unbleached organic sugar, organic vanilla extract.
           </p>
           <p>
            Everything seemed fine in mixing and prep except the brownie mixture was so very thick. Like so thick I couldnt even hardly get it out of the bowl into the 99 glass brownie dish. I put parchment paper in dish and it just stuck so much to the parchment paper that is was hard to spread that 1st layer down. Needless to say when I was putting the 2nd layer of brownie mixture over the peanut butter and caramel it was hard to spread it over it all.
           </p>
           <p>
            I ended up cooking it for almost 25-30 minutes because it just seemed so liquid still when I checked it at 18-20 minutes.  I then let it cool overnight instead of 4 hours. This morning when I tried to take it out of the pan and cut into squares it was a big gooey mess. The parchment paper was stuck to the glass and stuck to the brownies. I ended up getting it out of the pan but it was just a mess. Peeling the paper off the bottom of brownies resulted in tearing the paper and just having to cut it off the brownies. Needless to say they arent pretty squares but a gooey mess of crumbles and most of it in the trash because I couldnt get paper off.
           </p>
           <p>
            Where did I go wrong?
           </p>

          <div class="comment-content" itemprop="commentText">
           <p>
            I actually made these and thought I would share my experience  for anyone else considering hazarding a try at this sinful treat. I followed the recipe pretty much exactly, with a 99 inch pan,  no substitutions, and baked at 350,, but I  baked them longer than called for. The first layer I left in about 15-17 minutes. It was still reeeaally liquidy after the 10-12 called for, so I gave them a few more minutes until it had firmed up a bit.
           </p>
           <p>
            The second layer on top of the caramel is trickier to spread than the first, but I find its not bad if you just put dollops all around and then the warm caramel underneath will make it easier to smooth out. Plus, once its in the oven, itll even out anyway.
           </p>
           <p>
            The second round in the oven I found again was still very jiggly/not at all firm or crackly after 20 minutes. Usually my oven runs a bit hot and I pull stuff out early, but these seemed to need more time. I probably could have pulled them after 28-30 minutes, or whenever I noticed the top seemed firm to touch, but I left them in another few minutes since I couldnt really tell if the edges had started to darken. Mine didnt turn out looking quite as pretty with the caramel flowing out (I think some of it may have gotten more mixed in with the brownie layers, or perhaps its just because I baked them much longer) but they were still really fudgy and had caramel and peanut butter flavor. Perhaps if I had baked them somewhere between the recommended time and when I pulled them would have looked more like the picture.
           </p>
           <p>
            By the way, just a warning to prep the pan underneath the parchment paper too. Whenever I make stuff with caramel, I always seem to get some that seeps around the parchment paper into the corner of the pan, and this time was no exception.
           </p>
           <p>
            Definitely a tasty treat if youre willing to splurge on some major calories! Thanks for sharing!
           </p>
