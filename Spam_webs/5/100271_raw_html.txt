     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn" itemprop="name" style="font-size: 27px;">
      Amazing Brussels Sprout Latkes
     </h1>
     <img height="526" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Brussels-Sprout-Latke-790x526.jpg" width="790"/>
     <div class="yui3-g">
      <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-well">
       <div class="grub-byline" style="margin-top: 1em;">
        Recipe by
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/113274240511284571445" rel="author">
         Jerry James Stone
        </a>
         December 12, 2012
       </div>
       <div class="grub-mainPostContent" itemprop="recipeInstructions" not-style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 23px;">
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/amazing-brussels-sprout-latkes/brussels-sprout-latke/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/brussels-sprout-latke" rel="attachment wp-att-660">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         My affinity for Brussels sprouts is no secret. I mean, have you seen my
         <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/balsamic-glazed-brussels-sprouts-with-crushed-pine-nuts-and-parmesan/" title="Balsamic-Glazed Brussels Sprouts with Crushed Pine Nuts and Parmesan">
          Balsamic-Roasted Brussels Sprouts
         </a>
         ? Or what about my
         <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/garlic-and-herb-stuffed-brussels-sprouts/" title="Garlic and Herb Stuffed Brussels Sprouts">
          Stuffed Brussels Sprouts
         </a>
         ?! So when came time to experiment with latkes, you just know I had to add Brussels sprouts, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="338" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/w1inA87DKKU" width="600">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <div class="yui3-g" id="grub-recipeSummary">
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-left">
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul id="grub-ingredients">
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Pound Yukon Gold Potatoes (2 medium-sized potatoes)
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Pound Brussels Sprouts (about 12 sprouts)
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 large
            <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/ingredient/egg/">
             Eggs
            </a>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup Matzo Meal
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Teaspoon Salt
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup Olive Oil
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Green Onion, chopped
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            Freshly Ground Black Pepper
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-right">
          <h2>
           <a class="grub-button" href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/amazing-brussels-sprout-latkes/printable/" target="_blank">
            <span class="grub-printIcon">
            </span>
            Printable View
           </a>
          </h2>
          <h2>
           Yield:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6 latkes
           </span>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT30M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          <h2>
           Total Time
           <div style="float: right;">
            30 minutes
           </div>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT20M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Prep time
           <div style="float: right">
            20 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <meta content="PT10M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Cook time
           <div style="float: right">
            10 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Recipe Type:
           </h2>
           Breakfast
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Main Ingredients:
           </h2>
           Brussels Sprouts, Potato
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         1. Remove any dark spots from 1/2 pound of potatoes and trim the ends of 1/2 pound of Brussels sprouts.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/amazing-brussels-sprout-latkes/brussels-sprouts-and-potatoes/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/brussels-sprouts-and-potatoes" rel="attachment wp-att-661">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-661" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Brussels-Sprouts-and-Potatoes-600x399.jpg" title="Brussels Sprouts and Potatoes" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Run the potatoes, length-wise, through a food processor or use a cheese grater. This is very important when making latkes. You want to remove any excess water from the base. I roll the potatoes in a towel, then place them in a colander to dry. You can also use cheesecloth. You just want them to be very dry.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/amazing-brussels-sprout-latkes/shredded-yukon-gold-potatoes/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/shredded-yukon-gold-potatoes" rel="attachment wp-att-664">
          <img alt="Shredded Yukon Gold Potatoes" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-664" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Shredded-Yukon-Gold-Potatoes-600x399.jpg" title="Shredded Yukon Gold Potatoes" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Shred the Brussels sprouts.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Whisk 2 eggs and then whisk in 1/4 cup of Matzo Meal flour and 1 teaspoon of salt.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="577" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/apple-latke-with-bourbon-caramel-sour-cream/latke-mix-eggs-flour/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/latke-mix-eggs-flour" rel="attachment wp-att-645">
          <img alt="Latke Mix Eggs Flour" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-645" height="346" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Latke-Mix-Eggs-Flour-600x346.jpg" title="Latke Mix Eggs Flour" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Work in the potatoes until they are completely covered.
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Then fold in the shredded Brussels sprouts. I do this because I want that green to pop!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/amazing-brussels-sprout-latkes/brussels-sprout-latke-mix/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/brussels-sprout-latke-mix" rel="attachment wp-att-663">
          <img alt="Brussels Sprout Latke Mix" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-663" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Brussels-Sprout-Latke-Mix-600x399.jpg" title="Brussels Sprout Latke Mix" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Add enough oil to an iron skillet so that its about 1/4 deep, and then warm it over a medium high heat until it is shimmering.
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Drop a heap of the latke mixture into the skillet and flatten it out, letting it sizzle. Cook it for about 2 minutes, until the edges are golden and crispy. Then flip.
        </p>
        <p>
         9. When cooked on both sides, remove from the skillet and place on a towel to remove the excess oil.
        </p>
        <p>
         10. Serve with a spoonful of sour cream on top, garnish with green onions and fresh ground pepper.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="1025" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/amazing-brussels-sprout-latkes/brussels-sprout-latke-1/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/brussels-sprout-latke-1" rel="attachment wp-att-662">
          <img alt="Brussels Sprout Latke" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-662" height="615" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Brussels-Sprout-Latke-1-600x615.jpg" title="Brussels Sprout Latke" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>

          This recipe originally appeared on KQEDs Bay Area Bites.
         </a>
        </p>
