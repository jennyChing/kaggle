                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 Zanzibar, also called the Spice Isle, is a exotic heaven of palm-studded shorelines and crystal clear seas and blue skies. It is situated Twenty five miles off the Tanzania mainland.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 The area is among the globes best-known exotic islands having a lengthy, packed background and a distinctively passionate environment. Its a part of a coralline reef which extended down from the Indian Ocean as far Mafia Isle, southern part of Dar es Salaam. As a coral reefs island, Zanzibar offers outstanding white-colour sandy shorelines. Its 85 km long and 40 km wide and it has an elevation of 390 ft.
                 <br/>
                 <a href="http://www.travelycia.com/charming-zanzibar-islands/alla-fonda/" rel="attachment wp-att-225">
                  <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-225" height="600" src="http://www.travelycia.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Zanzibar_Island_.jpg" title="Zanzibar_Island_" width="900"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 Zanzibars vibrant background is really a saga which involves numerous actors in history. The cast consists of traders and travellers, colonizers and riders. To its coast came Egyptians Sumerians, Phoenicians, Assyrians, Chinese, Indians, Arabs, Persians, Malays, Portuguese, British and the Dutch. Each one of the visitors arrived at a distinct time, and every one of them left out a legacy of their stay.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 <a href="http://www.travelycia.com/charming-zanzibar-islands/zanzibar_island_1/" rel="attachment wp-att-226">
                  <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-226" height="596" src="http://www.travelycia.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Zanzibar_Island_1.jpg" title="Zanzibar_Island_1" width="900"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 The Bantu tribes in the mainland had been the very first occupants of the island, but by 700 AD the Indian Ocean winds had carried Arabs and Persians to the shoreline. It has been the intermarriage with the local occupants that delivered the new population  the Waswahili using their brand new language, Kiswahili thats talked not just in Zanzibar, but across the entire East African coast.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 <a href="http://www.travelycia.com/charming-zanzibar-islands/zanzibar_island_2/" rel="attachment wp-att-227">
                  <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-227" height="595" src="http://www.travelycia.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Zanzibar_Island_2.jpg" title="Zanzibar_Island_2" width="900"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 The very first noted reference to the island is within the mariners guide created around 50AD. In 1499, Vasco Da Gama came and constructed a church and a fortification. In 1832, the Sultan of Oman moved his chair of government to Zanzibar. Zanzibar was in those days the globe greatest maker of cloves, amounting to between Twelve hundred to Fifteen hundred tons annually. It had been even the centre of the infamous slave trade.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 <a href="http://www.travelycia.com/charming-zanzibar-islands/zanzibar_island_3/" rel="attachment wp-att-228">
                  <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-228" height="588" src="http://www.travelycia.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Zanzibar_Island_3.jpg" title="Zanzibar_Island_3" width="900"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 Going to this tropical island today, all of this wealthy and vibrant history is seen. You notice it and sense in the folks, their own life style and the structures. A visitor to Zanzibar is generally thrilled by the slim lanes and inclined fine old buildings. It might not have a very romantic title, but Stone Town is the aged place and the cultural centre of Zanzibar Island. Its from this point that the Sultan reigned over. Very little has evolved within the last Two centuries. Its a place of twisting alleys, bustling bazaars mosques and great Arab house whose luxury is mirrored in their brass-studded, wooden doorways.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 <a href="http://www.travelycia.com/charming-zanzibar-islands/zanzibar_island_4/" rel="attachment wp-att-229">
                  <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-229" height="600" src="http://www.travelycia.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Zanzibar_Island_4.jpg" title="Zanzibar_Island_4" width="900"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 For present day visitors lodgings and holiday, Zanzibar provides over Twenty five stunning white-colour exotic beaches. Zanzibar resorts offer world-class accommodations. You may relish all kinds of water and seaside activities such as diving, swimming and fishing. That old city itself features a significant choice of hotels and guesthouses for just about all budgets.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 <a href="http://www.travelycia.com/charming-zanzibar-islands/zanzibar_island_5/" rel="attachment wp-att-230">
                  <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-230" height="598" src="http://www.travelycia.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Zanzibar_Island_5.jpg" title="Zanzibar_Island_5" width="900"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: justify;">
                 Zanzibar is surely a consumers heaven. Carvings in copper and Curios, silver and brass are of excellent selection. Models of outriggers canoes, coffee pots, dhows, and Arabic doorways make fascinating souvenirs. Additional great buys are bracelets, silver jewellery, cocktail sticks, woven baskets, coffee spoons, and clove pomanders.
                </p>
