       <h1 class="title">
        From Birth to Death to Dinner Table: Photographer Documents the Lives of Horses in Japan
       </h1>
       <div class="postmeta">
        March 25, 2014 by
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.featureshoot.com/author/alyssa-coppelman/" rel="author" title="Posts by Alyssa Coppelman">
          Alyssa Coppelman
         </a>
        </strong>
        |
        <a href="http://www.featureshoot.com/2014/03/hajime-kamura/#comments">

          1 comment
         </span>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.featureshoot.com/2014/03/hajime-kamura/#!BllrR">
          <img alt="Hajime Kimura" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-67160" height="413" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Hajime_Kimura-03.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.featureshoot.com/2014/03/hajime-kamura/#!BllrR">
          <img alt="Hajime Kimura" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-67165" height="413" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Hajime_Kimura-08.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Tokyo-based photographer
         <a href="http://hajimekimura.net/tracks/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://hajimekimura.net/tracks/', 'Hajime Kimura']);" target="_blank">
          Hajime Kimura
         </a>
         didnt have an easy time finding a slaughterhouse that would allow him to photograph on the premises. With his project
         <em>
          Tracks
         </em>
         , Kimuras aim was to show all aspects of the uses of horses in Japan. For this, he needed to photograph in a slaughterhouse because horse is eaten in Japan.
         <span id="more-67440">
         </span>
         Its neither eaten often, nor is it a delicacy or controversial. Because horses cost far more than other animals to raise and maintain, its expensive. Kimura guesses that farmers consume it more than anyone else for the simple reason that they have access.
        </p>
        <p>
         After being refused by all ten slaughterhouses that still deal with horse meat, Kimura was ultimately granted permission to take pictures at a private slaughterhouse in Hokkaido. Kimuras photographs make up the story of the horses life cycle in Japan, from insemination to death.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kimuras work is on view at
         <a href="http://www.artelagunaprize.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.artelagunaprize.com/', 'Arte Laguna Prize']);" target="_blank">
          Arte Laguna Prize
         </a>
         exhibition through April 6, 2014 in Venice, Italy.
        </p>
