        <p>
         <em>
          Dont let their size fool you, these Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies pack a lot of flavor! Spiced apple pie filling in an easy cream cheese crust with a brown sugar toasted pecan crumble topping!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042707.jpg">
          <img alt="Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4568" height="680" src="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042707.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         These little pies will be the star of any party or holiday feast.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-2-1410042713.jpg">
          <img alt="Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies" class="aligncenter wp-image-4561 size-full" height="680" src="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-2-1410042713.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         But thats not why I like them. I like them because they can be stored in the freezer for that random moment when an apple pie craving hits. Or, in my case, a crumble cravingI get those too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042724.jpg">
          <img alt="Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4566" height="713" src="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042724.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its old news that I am a huge fan of the humble crumble topping. I use it on
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/2014/10/pumpkin-pecan-pie-cupcakes-bourbon-brown-sugar-cream-cheese-frosting/" target="_blank" title="Pumpkin Pecan Pie Cupcakes {Bourbon Brown Sugar Cream Cheese Frosting}">
          cupcakes
         </a>
         , on
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/2014/09/cardamom-pear-crisp/" target="_blank" title="Cardamom Pear Crisp">
          crisps
         </a>
         , on
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/2014/07/soft-chewy-smores-crumble-bars/" target="_blank" title="Soft Chewy Smores Crumble Bars">
          smores bars
         </a>
         , on
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/2014/08/peanut-butter-banana-muffins-peanut-butter-streusel-topping/" target="_blank" title="Peanut Butter Banana Muffins with Peanut Butter Streusel Topping">
          muffins
         </a>
         , on more
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/2014/08/peach-crisp-cupcakes-vanilla-buttercream/" target="_blank" title="Peach Crisp Cupcakes with Vanilla Swiss Meringue Buttercream">
          cupcakes
         </a>
         , and even on
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/2014/07/toasted-marshmallow-smores-ice-cream/" target="_blank" title="Toasted Marshmallow Smores Ice Cream">
          ice cream
         </a>
         . I have been known to eat it straight out of the bowlbut dont tell anyone because that is embarrassing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Last year I was taste testing and practicing making my
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/2013/10/pecan-tassies/" target="_blank" title="My Great-Grandmother Rosas Pecan Tassies">
          Great Grandmothers Pecan Tassies
         </a>
         for my wedding, so I had a constant supply of mini pecan pies in my freezer, which, in case you are wondering, was not good for the pre-wedding diet and a certain
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/2013/06/double-chocolate-banana-muffins/" target="_blank" title="Double Chocolate Banana Muffins">
          Wedding Fairy
         </a>
         , if youve been reading long enough to remember her {
         <em>
          thank you if you have!
         </em>
         }, was not impressed with my late night tassie taste testing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042739.jpg">
          <img alt="Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4567" height="833" src="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042739.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-4558">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I only made a half batch of these Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies and of the four mini pies that made it to the freezer, only 1 stayed in there longer than 24 hours! My husband LOVED these little treats.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042672.jpg">
          <img alt="Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4563" height="940" src="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042672.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The cream cheese pastry is flaky and rich, and contrasts nicely with the slightly tart, spiced apple filling! I managed to sneak some of my new favorite spice, cardamom, into the filling and it was delightful! A simple twist on an old favorite.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-CollageTEXT.jpg">
          <img alt="Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4570" height="1583" src="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-CollageTEXT.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Can we talk about the toasted pecan crumble already? Geez, Lindsey. Its (arguably) the best part! It is a vanilla scented brown sugar crumble packed with toasted pecans. The pecans? My favorite last minute addition. Why sprinkle them on top when you can mix them up inside?
        </p>
        <p>
         The pecan crumble is one of those innocent crumbles that I mentioned above that I almost taste tested to death. I may or may not have had to make more.
        </p>
        <p>
         My bad.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whatevs. The
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/2013/08/baked-halibut-champagne-vinaigrette/" target="_blank" title="Baked Halibut with Champagne Vinaigrette">
          Wedding Fairy
         </a>
         is long gone.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042651.jpg">
          <img alt="Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4562" height="680" src="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-1410042651.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         So after I get back to Atlanta, it is back to the kitchen for me to whip up more of these Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies to stock my freezer, because, obviously, one batch just wasnt enough!
        </p>

             <img alt="Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies" class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://americanheritagecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Mini-Apple-Pecan-Crumble-Pies-2-1410042713.jpg" style="width: 100px;" title="Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies"/>

            <div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description">
             <p class="summary italic">
              Dont let their size fool you, these Mini Apple Pecan Crumble Pies pack a lot of flavor! Spiced apple pie filling in an easy cream cheese crust with a brown sugar toasted pecan crumble topping!
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <div class="ingredient-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0">
             For the Crust:
            </div>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup unsalted butter
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             3 oz cream cheese
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup flour
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
            <div class="ingredient-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5">
             For the Apple Pie Filling:
            </div>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 apples, pealed and diced into small pieces (Granny Smith works well here)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup light brown sugar, not packed
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
              teaspoon cinnamon
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
             large pinch kosher salt
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
             large pinch of ground cardamom (or more to taste)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12" itemprop="ingredients">
             Pinch freshly ground nutmeg (fresh is better but you could use a ground as well)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-13" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoon cornstarch
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-14" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
            <div class="ingredient-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-15">
             For the Crumble:
            </div>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-16" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup unsalted butter, melted
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-17" itemprop="ingredients">
              teaspoon vanilla
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-18" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup light brown sugar, packed
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-19" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup all-purpose flour
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-20" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup toasted pecans, finely chopped
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-21" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Preheat the oven to 350
            </li>
            <div class="instruction-label" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1">
             To make the crust:
            </div>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Sift flour into a medium sized bowl. Cut butter into small cubes and add to flour. Add the cream cheese and cut the butter and cheese into the flour with two knives or a pastry blender. Continue until the dough holds together. Since we arent adding any additional liquid, youll want to continue to blend way past the stage at which you would normally stop with a pie crust.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Gather the crust into a ball and divide into 24 little pieces. I make a little log out of the dough, divide the log in half with a bench scraper or knife, divide each half roughly into thirds, then divide each third into 4 pieces. The dough is pliable enough that if you come up with several pieces that are way smaller and way larger than the others, which you probably will, you can pinch some off the larger and add it to the smaller pieces. Works every time.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Press each piece into one cup of a miniature muffin tin with your forefinger to create a little crust.
            </li>
            <div class="instruction-label" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5">
             To make the Apple Pie Filling:
            </div>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Combine apples, sugar, lemon juice and spices in a medium bowl. Toss to coat evenly. Taste and adjust sugar, lemon juice or spices as necessary. Add cornstarch and toss to coat evenly.
            </li>
            <div class="instruction-label" id="zlrecipe-instruction-7">
             Make the Crumble:
            </div>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Stir the vanilla into the melted butter. Add all the remaining ingredients and stir with a fork until all the ingredients are equally moistened. They should hold together and then break into crumbles when squeezed in your hand. If it is still too dry you can add melted butter 1 teaspoon at a time, but be careful not to make it too moist or it will be greasy and not crunchy/soft like it should be.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-9" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Also note that if you chop your pecans too big, they will impede the crumbles from forming.
            </li>
            <div class="instruction-label" id="zlrecipe-instruction-10">
             Assemble the Pies:
            </div>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-11" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Spoon a teaspoon of filling into each (unbaked) pastry shell. The filling should come to the top because it will cook down a lot.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-12" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Press some crumble onto each mini pie. Make sure to mound it on top so that the finished pies have a generous helping of crumble.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-13" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Bake in preheated oven 25-30 minutes or until the filling is bubbling around the edges and the crumble and pie crusts have begun to brown. Let cool completely in the tins before removing. Ive found that spinning the pie in the tin to release the edges and then gently pressing down on one side is a quick and easy way to remove them without breaking the pie shells.
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-notes">
            Notes:
           </p>
           <div id="zlrecipe-notes-list">
            <p class="notes">
             The recipe can easily be doubled. If you want to make more than 48, then I would make the crust in separate double batches. Also, if you have a kitchen scale or your cream cheese comes marked with ounces, you can easy make a half batch.
            </p>
            <p class="notes">
             You can serve them warm or cold. My husband said they were even good frozen! A little scoop of ice cream is never remiss.
            </p>
