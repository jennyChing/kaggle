      <h1>
       Lena Dunhams New Yorker Dog or Jewish Boyfriend? quiz isnt anti-Semitic, its just not funny
      </h1>
      <div class="posts post_spacer" id="post-80000">
       <div class="main_img">
        <div class="zoom">
         <a href="http://www.laughspin.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/lena-dunham.jpg" rel="lightbox" title="Lena Dunhams New Yorker Dog or Jewish Boyfriend? quiz isnt anti-Semitic, its just not funny">
          <img alt="Lena Dunhams New Yorker Dog or Jewish Boyfriend? quiz isnt anti-Semitic, its just not funny" class="fade_hover" src="http://www.laughspin.com/wordpress/wp-content/themes/widezine/thumb.php?src=http://www.laughspin.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/lena-dunham.jpg&amp;w=700&amp;h=290&amp;zc=1&amp;q=80&amp;bid=1"/>

       <p>
        <a href="http://www.laughspin.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/Lena-Dunham.jpg">
         <img alt="Lena Dunham" class="alignright size-full wp-image-76847" height="250" src="http://www.laughspin.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/Lena-Dunham.jpg" width="250"/>
        </a>
        In an effort to appeal to readers under the age of 106 and to make Passover cool,
        <em>
         The New Yorker
        </em>
        enlisted the help of Lena Dunham, the 28-year-old writer, actress,
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Not-That-Kind-Girl-Learned/dp/081299499X" target="_blank">
         author
        </a>
        , director, producer, Jewish human and hipster goddess who has found massive success since the debut of
        <a href="http://www.hbo.com/girls#/" target="_blank">
         <em>
          Girls
         </em>
        </a>
        on HBO in 2012. To push
        <em>
         The New Yorker
        </em>
        to palatable status, Dunham penned a humor column titled
        <a href="http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2015/03/30/dog-or-jewish-boyfriend-a-quiz" target="_blank">
         Dog or Jewish Boyfriend? A Quiz.
        </a>
        If youre thinking, Wow, this sounds like something
        <a href="http://www.buzzfeed.com/geico/jokes-that-will-definitely-make-you-groan#.oya2r64BX" target="_blank">
         BuzzFeed
        </a>
        would do just to get 8 trillion clicks to drive up the cost of their native advertising, youd be correct. But
        <a href="http://www.newyorker.com/" target="_blank">
         <em>
          The New Yorker
         </em>
        </a>
        uses those old-school serif fontsand there are 35 questions instead of a more manageable 10 or 12 so its pretty much totally different.
       </p>
       <p>
        Anyway, the point is that Dunham wrote this column pegged to the April 3 start of Passover (the commemoration of Jews liberation from Egyptian slavery) and not everyone was tickled with the whole Jews-Are-like-Dogs conceit. Jewish parenting site
        <em>
         <a href="http://www.kveller.com/lena-dunham-equated-jews-to-dogs-thats-not-ok/" target="_blank">
          Kveller
         </a>
        </em>
        was not a fan; neither was
        <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/lev-raphael/why-lena-dunhams-dog-quiz_b_6962192.html" target="_blank">
         Lev Raphael at
         <em>
          Huffington Post
         </em>
        </a>
        . At the top of the pack of the non-tickled was the Anti-Defamation League.
        <a href="http://www.adl.org/press-center/press-releases/anti-semitism-usa/adl-lena-dunhams-jewish-boyfriend-or-dog-quiz-tasteless.html" target="_blank">
         Abraham H. Foxman, the ADLs national director
        </a>
        , released a rather measured statement:
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Humor is a matter of taste, and people can disagree if it is funny or not. Some will certainly find Lena Dunhams stereotypes about cheap Jews offensive. Others will take issue with the very idea of comparing a dog and a Jewish boyfriend. The piece is particularly troubling because it evokes memories of the No Jews or Dogs Allowed signs from our own early history in this country, and also because, in a much more sinister way, many in the Muslim world today hatefully refer to Jews as dogs.
        </p>
        <p>
         We doubt that Ms. Dunham had any intention of evoking such comparisons. While we understand that humor is its own special brand of expression and always try to give leeway to comedians, we wish that she had chosen another, less insensitive way to publicly reflect on her boyfriends virtues and vices. We are surprised that the New Yorker chose to print it.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        Im not surprised
        <em>
         The New Yorker
        </em>
        chose to print it (see above business reasons for printing). Im more surprised that the list penned by Dunham, an excellent comedy writer, barely made any sense. The whole point of this faux-quiz format is that most of the answers could go either way like when Comedy Centrals
        <em>
         @Midnight
        </em>
        recently
        <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/766390" target="_blank">
         played Ska or Improv
        </a>
        to great effect. Host Chris Hardwick flashed press photos of ska bands and improv troupes and the shows guests had to determine which was which. But Dunhams piece is largely populated by characteristics that could be assigned to any or all of these following categories:
        <strong>
         1.
        </strong>
        Any boyfriend.
        <strong>
         2.
        </strong>
        Any human.
        <strong>
         3.
        </strong>
        Any animal
        <strong>
         4.
        </strong>
        No one
       </p>
       <p>
        Take #3, for example: Hes crazy for cream cheese. Who the fuck isnt crazy for cream cheese? I get it. Jews eat bagels that are often slathered with cream cheese. You know who else eats bagels with cream cheese? Everyone. And if you dont, youre a monster who should be immediately eliminated from society. And do dogs enjoy cream cheese more than other human food? My family has had many dogs over the years and not once have I seen any of them eat cream cheese. I have no doubt they wouldve; Ive seen them eat their own vomit, after all. But the connection between dogs and Jews love of cream cheese is flimsy at best. In order for a joke to work, even in a hyperbolic setting, there needs to be some flicker of truth. If the list was Cat or Jewish Boyfriend, she couldve written, Hes crazy for Lox because lox is a more Jewish-specific food and is also fish, which cats love. Its still not funny but at least theres some logic to it.
       </p>
       <p>
        I wish he were more excited about spending time with my friends, reads #11. Most dudes  Jewish, Christian, Satanist, lapsed Zoroastrian  dont want to spend time with their own friends, let alone their girlfriends friends. And what kind of dog-for-a-pet doesnt like the company of any human (girlfriends friends included)? Are there many 20 or 30-something year old gals keeping Rottweilers, Doberman Pinschers or Akitas as pets?
       </p>
       <p>
        I dont believe Dunhams attempt at humor is anti-Semitic. I simply think a proven writer and performer who usually executes well wrote a really awful column for a publication in desperate need for youthful clicks and eyeballs.
       </p>

Im not offended as a Jew, but as a humorist it pisses me off because that editorial space could have been used to publish the work of someone who needs the exposure and the cash. The New Yorker needs to forget about trying to appeal to people who will never subscribe and remember its tradition of literacy and enterprise journalism. Shitcan these vanity essays (like the Woody Allen and Steve Martin detritus the magazine runs) and publish worthy articles that appeal to those of us who actually subscribe.
