               <h1 class="entry-title">
                Wedding Guest Dos and Donts
               </h1>
              </header>
              <!-- .entry-header -->
              <div class="entry-content">
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Do:
                </strong>
                Check the invitation for a wedding website or registry information. Brides and Grooms spend a lot of time and effort to put all pertinent information into one place for their guests. Most include a link to the registry since etiquette says not to put it directly on the invitation. So, if you are looking for directions to the venue, a suggestion on what to wear (black tie or beach casual?) then look to the invitation before calling the bride and groom for these types of questions.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Dont:
                </strong>
                Buy a random gift. If the gift registry is depleted by the time you get around to looking at a gift to buy the newlyweds, opt for money instead of buying something you just see at a store. Registries are made for a reason so unless you just talked to the bride about a specific item they want but didnt include in the registry, writing a check is the best bet.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Do:
                </strong>
                Figure out the wedding colors before buying your outfit. Most wedding colors will be obvious from the Save the Date, Invitation or wedding website. If still in doubt, feel free to ask. It is much better than showing up looking like a member of the wedding party when you are not.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Dont:
                </strong>
                This should go without saying but
                <span style="text-decoration: underline">
                 do not
                </span>
                wear white, cream, ivory or any other variation on a solid white dress.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Do:
                </strong>
                Tell the bride and groom early if you have a food allergy or specific diet. Most caterers are more than happy to make specific dishes to accommodate the vegetarian, gluten-free, or peanut-allergy crowds. Just make sure you give them enough heads up so they can craft a version of the meal you will be able to eat. And dont feel guilty, most of these changes to the meal will not result in a change in price.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Dont:
                </strong>
                Feel bad about going up and saying hi to the newlyweds. There is only so much time at a wedding and they really do want to see you and talk to you. They will probably appreciate it more than you think and youll get a little face time (and hopefully a picture) that you might not gettherwise.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Do:
                </strong>
                Bring cash (10 dollars in ones should be sufficient) for tipping at the bar. If it doesnt state cash bar on the invitation, most weddings will be open bar but you should still have some small bills to tip the bartenders.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Dont:
                </strong>
                Go to the wedding with any expectations of how the ceremony or reception should go. The bride and groom decided on what they did and did not want at their wedding so just be there with an open mind. The groom doesnt need to be asked for the 10
                <sup>
                 th
                </sup>
                time why they dont have a cake and the bride doesnt want to hear how she has to do a bouquet toss. Just be there to celebrate with them however they choose.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Do:
                </strong>
                Dance. Period. If there is music get up and get groovy.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Dont Forget:
                </strong>
               </p>
