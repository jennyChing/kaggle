     <h1>
      5 Towns to Visit Along the Great Ocean Road in Australia
     </h1>
     <div class="meta">
      <span>
       By
       <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/author/craig/" rel="author" title="Posts by Craig">
        Craig
       </a>
      </span>
      <span class="date">
       Wednesday, July 9th, 2014
      </span>
      <span class="comments">
       30 Comments
      </span>
     </div>

     <p style="text-align: center;">
      =====
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/towns-on-the-great-ocean-road.jpg">
       <img alt="5 Towns to Visit along the Great Ocean Road in Australia" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-43568" height="710" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/towns-on-the-great-ocean-road-473x710.jpg" width="473"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      If youre going toexplore
      <strong>
       The Great Ocean Road
      </strong>
      let me make a suggestion.
     </p>
     <p>
      Dont rush it.Dont do it in one day. And dont just visit the Twelve Apostles for your 
      <em>
       I was here pic
      </em>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The Great Ocean Roaddeserves more. You deserve more. Its Australias most famous drive for a reason!
     </p>
     <p>
      Everyone has different time constraints, but if you have the time give yourself at least three days. We took two weeks.The tour buses thatroutinely drive the Great Ocean Road in one day and get to the 12 Apostles at lunchtime (the best time is sunrise or sunset) will bypassmany of the highlights.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="color: #000000;">
       Hire a car if you dont have your own. This 243km road needs to be experienced from behind the wheel and having your own vehicle allows you the flexibility to stop when and where you like.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="color: #000000;">
       Besides the stunning beaches, waterfalls, walks, and forests there are some great towns dotted along the Great Ocean Road worthy of your time.
       <br/>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      You can check out our drive as mapped out
      <a href="http://lovedriv.es/d/36qx" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       here
      </a>
      on the LoVe Drives site.
     </p>
     <p>
      Hereare five towns we recommend you not only visit, but stay at and enjoy what they have to offer.
     </p>
     <h2>
      1. Torquay
     </h2>
     <p>
      <span style="color: #ff0000;">
       <span style="color: #000000;">
        The Great Ocean Road officially starts in the town of Torquay. We recommend starting the Great Ocean Road from this direction so youll be on thelane closest to the ocean which makes for easier stops at the view points.
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="color: #000000;">
       The township of Torquay is famous for its surfing culture, which you can learn more about at thesurfing museum
       <em>
        Surf World
       </em>
       . Even surf brands
       <em>
        Rip Curl
       </em>
       and
       <em>
        Quicksilver
       </em>
       were founded in Torquay. Nearby is one of Australias best surfing beaches,
       <strong>
        Bells Beach
       </strong>
       , home to the annual
      </span>
      Rip Curl Pro Surfing Competition (made infamous by the movie Point Break).
     </p>
     <p>
      Bells has an impressive cliff-faceand the views are spectacular.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43303" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/7725040034_cf0e6f512b_b.jpg">
       <img alt="Bells Beach, Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-43303" height="473" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/7725040034_cf0e6f512b_b-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Bells Beach  credit
       <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/clogwog/7725040034/in/photolist-bFEfHn-6eqAY3-cynYSs-6emrpx-6eqy3L-bFEcUF-5T9Afo-e3b6AU-7HFmkV-fKfo4B-b9Pijv-cLCRgq-jBLQB-7QbHAJ-6emnU2-6emt7z-5n7LaR-6emwmi-6eqyDU-7HFm1X-93xCe6-4bFRaA-bwumzc-4ReM98-9jaa8L-ddZFzG-2AFAb-8w9EaT-bThDnc-dXQSx6-8NFEq5-e5cGoL-6BvSqe-4Rj3tb-2AFAc-9j8zXc-5obm5p-4RiZpQ-4Bkd24-hZPdBc-4z43d5-5Y5BqN-57ddHo-6zy3bw-9j8auR-eRhJwf-YRgc-9jbndE-e8EEtG-4Rj3Y3" target="_blank">
        Clogwog
       </a>
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      Torquay also offers
      <span style="color: #000000;">
       popular activities such as nature walks, fishing, sailboarding, surfing and mountain bike riding.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Just down the road is
      <strong>
       Jan Juc
      </strong>
      a neighbouring suburb known for its hills and native tea tree. Theres a
      <span style="color: #ff0000;">
       <span style="color: #000000;">
        trail running along the coast between Torquay and Jan Juc for walkers and bike riders that offers excellent views.
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Leaving Torquay and continuing west, check out the townships of
      <strong>
       Anglesea
      </strong>
      and
      <strong>
       Aireys Inlet
      </strong>
      which are beautiful. Youll definitely notice the change in the scenery.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43302" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/shutterstock_153529325.jpg">
       <img alt="Jan Juc Beach, Victoria, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-43302" height="473" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/shutterstock_153529325-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Bird Rock Lookout over Jan Juc Beach near Torquay  Credit
       <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/cat.mhtml?lang=en&amp;language=en&amp;ref_site=photo&amp;search_source=search_form&amp;version=llv1&amp;anyorall=all&amp;safesearch=1&amp;search_tracking_id=VdxHHrjY2aO_RZc4zTOqnQ&amp;searchterm=torquay+victoria+australia&amp;show_color_wheel=1&amp;orient=&amp;commercial_ok=&amp;media_type=images&amp;search_cat=&amp;searchtermx=&amp;photographer_name=&amp;people_gender=&amp;people_age=&amp;people_ethnicity=&amp;people_number=&amp;color=" target="_blank">
        Shutterstock.com
       </a>
      </p>
     </div>
     <h2>
      2. Lorne
     </h2>
     <p>
      Lorne was our favourite town along the Great Ocean Road driveand we spent three relaxing days here. Its picturesque and situated right on the ocean with a charm thats hard to resist.
     </p>
     <p>
      Lornehas some great cafes to keep your coffee and cake addiction well satisfied. There are several unique shops, boutiques, and galleries tokeep the shoppers happy.
     </p>
     <p>
      The beach is lovely for a walk either early morning or late afternoon and stop at the
      <strong>
       Lorne Beach Pavilion
      </strong>
      for a coffee, or beer, with uninterrupted views of the water.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43309" style="width: 810px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-044.jpg">
       <img alt="Lorne - Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="wp-image-43309 size-full" height="533" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-044.jpg" width="800"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Lorne Beach
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_41303" style="width: 810px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-112.jpg">
       <img alt="Coffee at the Lorne Beach Pavillion - Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="wp-image-41303 size-full" height="533" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-112.jpg" width="800"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Coffee at the Lorne Beach Pavillion
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      For lunch, eat at
      <strong>
       <i>
        The Bottle of Milk,
       </i>
      </strong>
      famous for their burgers, or grab some takeaway and sit on the grass at the excellent playground by the beach and let the kids run around.
      <strong>
       <i>
        <br/>
       </i>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43311" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-055.jpg">
       <img alt="The Playground" class="size-large wp-image-43311" height="465" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-055-710x465.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       The Playground
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      Just behind Lorne, take a short drive to
      <strong>
       Teddys Lookout for
      </strong>
      spectacular views of the Great Ocean Road. Theres also a 45 minute walk you can do here.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_41301" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-074.jpg">
       <img alt="Teddys Lookout - Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-41301" height="449" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-074-710x449.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       View from Teddys Lookout
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      Lorne also plays host to
      <a href="http://lorne.fallsfestival.com.au/" target="_blank">
       The Falls Music &amp; Arts Festival
      </a>
      at the end of December which is very popular.
     </p>
     <p>
      For a comfortable place to stay, we stayed at the
      <a href="http://www.mantra.com.au/victoria/great-ocean-road/lorne/accommodation/resorts/mantra-lorne/?cvosrc=ppc.google.+lorne%20+mantra&amp;matchtype=b&amp;gclid=CPfhup3Wi74CFVcRvQodMpsAPw" target="_blank">
       Mantra Apartments
      </a>
      right on the beach and an easy walk into town.
     </p>
     <h2>
      3. Kennett River
     </h2>
     <p>
      With a population of only 50, Kennett River is a quiet and laid-back townbetween Lorne and Apollo Bay.
     </p>
     <p>
      Flanked by
      <strong>
       Otway National Park
      </strong>
      this town is probably best known for the place to see koalas in the wild along the
      <a href="http://otwaycoast.com.au/short-walks/" target="_blank">
       Grey River Road
      </a>
      in the middle of town, just look for the crowds of people and follow their gazes up for quick koala spotting.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-141.jpg">
       <img alt="Koalas in the wild - Kennet River, Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-41290" height="473" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-141-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Theres also the
      <a href="http://otwaycoast.com.au/short-walks/" target="_blank">
       glowworms at night
      </a>
      (which arent that great) and you can buy bird feed from the store here and feed wild king parrots and Rosellas.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43304" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/4703503750_44efefbd9d_b.jpg">
       <img alt="Wild King Parrot - Kennett River, Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-43304" height="473" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/4703503750_44efefbd9d_b-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Wild King Parrot  credit
       <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/jhecking/4703503750/in/photolist-8aCFNf-7ffopt-epxaqa-fKfS6X-8azrED-9uvt4c-epxahZ-fKxojs-fKxsau-fKfRDV-eqtomf-fKxrrY-eJFg59-fKxrFm-epxbcR-fKfMaB-fKfH1Z-aFUPBc-fKfMHM-fKxjbQ-dX8oJa-fKfSiZ-fKfJfx-fKfKeH-fKfAu6-dxG8HQ-fKxiCQ-fKxujJ-fKfTuD-fKfSHP-aFUSoa-8aCFe3-buh9fC-eqtp89-fKxjod-fKxcK9-aFURek-fKfB76-buh9nY-bHbWnv-buh8YJ-fKfJs2-fKxphb-fKfNmv-fKxiTw-fKfNCr-fKfJGK-fKfTED-fKfJVv-dX8p1n" target="_blank">
        Jan
       </a>
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      It can be very popular here with many tourist buses lined up. But its a lot safer, and easier, to view the koalas here compared to driving Otway Lighthouse Road.
     </p>
     <p>
      If seeing koalas and glow worms isnt enough, theres excellent fishing and surfing beaches for beginners.Theres a caravan park at Kennet River should you wish to stay longer with Australias favourite furry friends.
     </p>
     <h2>
      4. Apollo Bay
     </h2>
     <p>
      Half-way along the Great Ocean Road is our second favourite town, Apollo Bay, often described as the place where the hill meets the sea.
     </p>
     <p>
      Panoramic views over the harbour and the coastline from the
      <strong>
       Mariners Lookout
      </strong>
      will give you the best perspective of this. The lookout is located at the northern end of town off Mariners Lookout Road.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43305" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-287.jpg">
       <img alt="Apollo Bay, Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-43305" height="473" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-287-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       View of Apollo Bay from Mariners Lookout
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      Apollo Bay was once a sleepy fishing village, but is now a modest seaside town of 2,000 people with plenty of cafes, restaurants and a community market each Saturday. In April it hosts one of the best three day music festivals in Victoria, the
      <a href="http://www.apollobaymusicfestival.com/" target="_blank">
       Apollo Bay Music Festival.
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Just down the road at Cape Otway are some of the best walks in the country and the town makes for a great base to explore the Otway region of lighthouses, rainforests, waterfalls and farmland.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://parkweb.vic.gov.au/explore/parks/great-otway-national-park/things-to-do/maits-rest" target="_blank">
       Maits Rest
      </a>
      is an easy 800m self-guidedcircuit walk through ancient, cool temperate rainforest and
      <span style="color: #ff0000;">
       <span style="color: #000000;">
        the walk to
        <strong>
         Triplet Falls
        </strong>
        , a one hour return loop, is a highlight.
       </span>
       <br/>
      </span>
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_41286" style="width: 469px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-274.jpg">
       <img alt="Triplet Falls, Otways National Park - Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-41286" height="710" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-274-459x710.jpg" width="459"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Triplet Falls
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="http://www.lightstation.com/" target="_blank">
       Cape Otway Lighthouse
      </a>
      is Australias oldest lighthouse still in operation today, you can
      <a href="http://www.lightstation.com/index.php?page=tours" target="_blank">
       take a tour
      </a>
      and climb to the top 90 metres high with breathtaking views of Bass Straight.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43306" style="width: 483px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/shutterstock_193277000.jpg">
       <img alt="Cape Otway Lighthouse, Great Ocean Road, Australia " class="size-large wp-image-43306" height="710" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/shutterstock_193277000-473x710.jpg" width="473"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Cape Otway Lighthouse  credit
       <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic-193277000/stock-photo-cape-otway-lighthouse-great-ocean-road-australia.html?src=FPiP5baU-vyvXMsg46cYVg-1-16" target="_blank">
        Shutterstock.com
       </a>
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      Just up the road from Apollo Bay is one of those best kept secrets of the Great Ocean Road, the
      <strong>
       Marengo Reefs Marine Sanctuary
      </strong>
      , a rocky beach with a seal colony.
      <a href="http://apollobaysurfkayak.com.au/seal-kayaking/" target="_blank">
       Apollo Bay Surf &amp; Kayak
      </a>
      run 2 hour tours to view the seals.
     </p>
     <p>
      For an affordable and homely place to stay we recommend the
      <a href="http://www.yha.com.au/Hostels/VIC/great-ocean-rd-grampians/Apollo-Bay/" target="_blank">
       Apollo Bay YHA Eco Hostel
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      5. Port Campbell
     </h2>
     <p>
      Our least favourite town out of the five mentioned, but what makes itappealing for travellers is its close proximity to
      <strong>
       Port Campbell National Park
      </strong>
      , home to the Twelve Apostles and many other famous Great Ocean Road landmarks.
     </p>
     <p>
      Its still a nice enough town with a few cafes, shops and a bay area in a natural setting, but we mainly used it as a base to explore the surrounding area.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43307" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/shutterstock_151104110.jpg">
       <img alt="Port Campbell, Victoria, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-43307" height="473" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/shutterstock_151104110-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Port Campbell  credit
       <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic-151104110/stock-photo-view-towards-port-campbell-along-its-inlet-from-the-road-to-the-west.html?src=GSyDOSyIlEiDLoZvCCL1Ug-3-9" target="_blank">
        Shutterstock.com
       </a>
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      We spent the whole day in the National Park, starting with sunrise over the 12 Apostles and finishing with a sunset picnic.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43312" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-318.jpg">
       <img alt="The Twelve Apostles - Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="wp-image-43312 size-large" height="473" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-318-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       The 12 Apostles at sunrise
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      During the day we walked down
      <strong>
       Gibsons Steps
      </strong>
      and then visited
      <strong>
       Loch Ard Gorge
      </strong>
      ,
      <strong>
       London Bridge
      </strong>
      ,
      <strong>
       The Grotto
      </strong>
      ,
      <strong>
       The Arch
      </strong>
      , and the
      <strong>
       Bay of Islands
      </strong>
      with a handy lunch break back in Port Campbell.
     </p>
     <p>
      We stayed in a cabin in the
      <a href="http://www.pchp.com.au/" target="_blank">
       Port Campbell Holiday Park
      </a>
      which was a one minute walk into town.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_41276" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-484.jpg">
       <img alt="12 Apostles - Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-41276" height="446" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-484-710x446.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Gibsons Steps leads to here!
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_41379" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-562.jpg">
       <img alt="Loch Ard Gorge - Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-41379" height="473" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-562-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Loch Ard Gorge
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43313" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-677.jpg">
       <img alt="London Bridge - Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-43313" height="463" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Ocean-Road-677-710x463.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       London Bridge
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_41294" style="width: 720px">
      <a href="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-590.jpg">
       <img alt="Port Campbell National Park - Great Ocean Road, Australia" class="size-large wp-image-41294" height="473" src="http://www.ytravelblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Great-Ocean-Road-590-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       walking in Port Campbell National Park
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      The Great Ocean Road continues on and finishes at Port Fairy. We didnt really explore the last section too much, but did stop at Port Fairy for lunch. It was pouring with rain so could not explore more. Through the drizzle it looked like alovely historicalfishing village.
     </p>
     <p>
      Find the mapped out drive
      <a href="http://lovedriv.es/d/36qx" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       here.
      </a>
     </p>
