           <p>
            <span id="bodycontent_0_ArticleDescription">
             <em>
              Here at MDC, we're always on the lookout for top-of-the-line beauty gurus to share advice on hair, makeup and skin care. Today, we are thrilled to have
              <strong>
               nail expert
               <a class="black-link-text" href="http://www.kaitmosh.com" target="_blank">
                Kait Mosh
               </a>
              </strong>
              share her unique ideas on how to get simple, yet edgy nails. Take it away Kait!
             </em>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="negative space" height="760" src="~/media/2E1ABF27DAF148739BBF485356E35575.ashx?la=en" width="600"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 18px;">
               Supplies:
              </span>
             </strong>
             <br/>
               Rubbing alcohol
             <br/>
               Acetone
             <br/>
               Base coat like
             <a class="black-link-text" href="http://www.essie.com/Nail-Care/base-coat/first-base-base-coat.aspx" target="_blank">
              essie First Base
             </a>
             <br/>
               Nude/beige nail polish (I love
             <a class="black-link-text" href="http://www.essie.com/search.aspx?q=sand%20tropez" target="_blank">
              essie Sand Tropez
             </a>
             )
             <br/>
               Black nail art-specific polish like Orly Instant Artist Paint in Black
             <br/>
               Top coat (I used Seche Vite)
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Step 1.
             </strong>
             Start with clean, manicured nails. Wipe them with alcohol to remove any excess oil or dirt.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Step 2.
             </strong>
             Paint on your base coat.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Step 3.
             </strong>
             Apply two coats of your nude beige shade.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Step 4.
             </strong>
             Dip a small cleanup brush (or Q-Tips Precision Tips) into acetone and carefully remove the polish only on the half-moons at the base of the nail.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Step 5.
             </strong>
             Take your black polish and line the half-moon. Allow five minutes to dry -- we don't want to smudge the lines!
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Step 6.
             </strong>
             Finish with your top coat and don't forget to moisturize your nails with cuticle oil!
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              How do you feel about this spaced-out mani?
             </strong>
             Sound off in the comments below!
