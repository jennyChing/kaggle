               <div class="body-teaser">
                The Smithsonian has George Washingtons Revolutionary War uniform, Abraham Lincolns top hat and the lap desk Thomas Jefferson used to write the Declaration of Independence. But it hasnt had anything from our mutant superheroesuntil now.
               </div>
               <!--teaser-->
               <div class="body-remainder">
                <br/>
                The Smithsonian Institutions National Museum of American History has acquired costumes from the
                <em>
                 X-Men
                </em>
                . Patrick Stewartone of the stars of 20th Century Foxs movie series about a team of mutant humans with an X-gene that gives them unusual powersdonated his Professor Xavier costume to the Smithsonian in a ceremony May 17.
                <br/>
                Stewart also presented to the museum Ian McKellens Magneto costume, Hugh Jackmans Wolverine costume and Halle Berrys Storm costume. Additionally, the museum received a set of resin claws and a Zippo lighter from the Wolverine costume and the final shooting script from the latest film in the series,
                <em>
                 X-Men: Days of Future Past
                </em>
                , which opens May 23.
                <br/>
                From comic books to the multiplex cinema screens,
                <em>
                 X-Men
                </em>
                has captured the imaginations of audiences for several generations, said John Gray, director of the National Museum of American History. The costumes and props will expand the story of the American superhero genre that the Smithsonian can tell.
                <br/>
                The
                <em>
                </em>
                first
                <em>
                 X-Men
                </em>
                comic book, written Stan Lee and illustrated by Jack Kirby, was published by Marvel Comics in September 1963. The comic was brought to the cinema by 20th Century Fox in 2000. There have been six films in the series so far:
                <em>
                 X-Men
                </em>
                (2000),
                <em>
                 X2: X-Men United
                </em>
                (2003),
                <em>
                 X-Men: The Last Stand
                </em>
                (2006),
                <em>
                 X-Men Origins: Wolverine
                </em>
                (2009),
                <em>
                 X-Men: First Class
                </em>
                (2011) and
                <em>
                 The Wolverine
                </em>
                (2013).
                <br/>
                Besides portraying the leader of the
                <em>
                 X-Men,
                </em>
                Stewart is well-known for his role as Capt.Jean-Luc Picard of
                <em>
                 Star Trek: The Next Generation
                </em>
                .
                <br/>
                Preceding the X-Men items in the pantheon of superhero artifacts in the history museums collection were Christopher Reeves outfit from
                <em>
                 Superman III
                </em>
                , both Julie Newmars and Halle Berrys Catwoman costumes, the Batman cowl and mask created for
                <em>
                 Batman &amp; Robin,
                </em>
                and a selection of Batman comic books.
