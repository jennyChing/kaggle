       <!-- CONTENT OF THE POST -->
       After reading
       <a href="http://www.boingboing.net/2009/04/14/secret-bible-of-the.html">
        yesterday's post
       </a>
       about the role that Henry Mayhew's
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0140432418/downandoutint-20">
        London Labour and the London Poor
       </a>
       played in the birth of steampunk, William Gibson wrote in to add,
       <blockquote>
        <img align="left" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/craphound.com/images/2614605381_3385ee29ef.jpg"/>
        I've never actually read Mayhew, but feel I've long had him, through
brilliant osmosis, with Kellow Chesney's
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0851170021/downandoutint-20">
         Victorian Underworld
        </a>
        , which is easily one of my favorite books
ever.  People assume, when I tell them that, that Chesney would mainly
have influenced
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/055329461X/downandoutint-20">
         The Difference Engine
        </a>
        , but actually this was very
consciously the basis of the criminal society of
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0441569595/downandoutint-20">
         Neuromancer
        </a>
        , et al. It
was a Victorian model, as I saw what's since come to be called
neoconservatism producing a neo-Victorian world. Not a bad call, either!
        <p>
         I  literally had The Victorian Underworld on my desk constantly,
throughout the writing of Neuromancer, and for years after.
         <br clear="all"/>
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0851170021/downandoutint-20">
        Victorian Underworld
       </a>
       (
       <i>
        Thanks,
        <a href="http://www.williamgibsonbooks.com/blog/blog.asp">
         Bill
        </a>
        !
       </i>
       )
       <p>
        (
        <i>
         Image:
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/27556454@N07/2614605381">
          The Victorian Underworld
         </a>
         from rauter25's photostream
        </i>
        )
       </p>
