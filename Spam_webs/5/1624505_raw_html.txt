        <p>
         <b>
          <span class="srTitle">
           Renaissance
          </span>
         </b>
         <b>
          ,
         </b>
         <a class="bps-assembly-container media-overlay-link" data-id="191850" data-type="video" data-ytrk="77" href="/event/Renaissance/images-videos/Overview-of-Florence-widely-regarded-as-the-birthplace-of-the/191850">
          <span class="md-assembly-content">
           <img alt="Florence: Renaissance [Credit: Contunico  ZDF Enterprises GmbH, Mainz]" height="240" src="http://media-2.web.britannica.com/eb-media/18/179718-049-FBA832EF.jpg" width="426"/>
           <i class="fa fa-play-circle-o fa-3x">
           </i>
           <span class="md-assembly-thumb-caption">
            <span class="md-assembly-thumb-title">
             Florence: Renaissance
            </span>
            <span class="md-assembly-thumb-credit">
             Contunico  ZDF Enterprises GmbH, Mainz
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         literally rebirth, the period in European civilization immediately following the
         <a data-ytrk="/548" href="/event/Middle-Ages">
          <span href="http://semantic.britannica.com/accepted_headword/core/380873/Middle-Ages" property="about">
           Middle Ages
          </span>
         </a>
         and conventionally held to have been characterized by a surge of interest in
         <a data-ytrk="/548" href="/topic/classical-scholarship">
          <span href="http://semantic.britannica.com/accepted_headword/core/527967/classical-scholarship" property="about">
           Classical scholarship
          </span>
         </a>
         and values. The Renaissance also witnessed the discovery and exploration of new continents, the substitution of the
         <a data-ytrk="/548" href="/topic/Copernican-system">
          <span href="http://semantic.britannica.com/accepted_headword/core/136567/Copernican-system" property="about">
           Copernican
          </span>
         </a>
         for the
         <a data-ytrk="/548" href="/topic/Ptolemaic-system">
          <span href="http://semantic.britannica.com/accepted_headword/core/482079/Ptolemaic-system" property="about">
           Ptolemaic
          </span>
         </a>
         system of
         <a data-ytrk="/548" href="/science/astronomy">
          <span href="http://semantic.britannica.com/accepted_headword/core/40047/astronomy" property="about">
           astronomy
          </span>
         </a>
         , the decline of the feudal system and the growth of commerce, and the invention or application of such potentially powerful innovations as paper,
         <a data-ytrk="/548" href="/topic/printing-publishing">
          <span href="http://semantic.britannica.com/accepted_headword/core/477017/printing" property="about">
           printing
          </span>
         </a>
         , the mariners compass, and gunpowder. To the scholars and thinkers of the day, however, it was primarily a time of the revival of Classical learning
         <span class="md-gist-nb-words">
          ... (100 of 3,189 words)
         </span>
        </p>
