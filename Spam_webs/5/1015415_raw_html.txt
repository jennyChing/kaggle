        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Party Popcorn
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-02-03T17:35:22+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          02.3.12
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://shewearsmanyhats.com/author/ajohnson/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Amy Johnson
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://shewearsmanyhats.com/party-popcorn/#comments">
           89 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://shewearsmanyhats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/party-popcorn-11.jpg">
          <img alt="Party Popcorn" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17503" height="883" src="http://cf.shewearsmanyhats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/party-popcorn-11.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Okay, Im going to cut right to the chase, this candy popcorn, what Im calling
         <strong>
          Party Popcorn
         </strong>
         for a lack of a better term, is the quickest tastiest thing Ive made in a while. And there are reasons for that. With just a few ingredients (as few as three, depending on what route you take), and not really any cooking, just some melting, this is a fast favorite for me now. Perfect for a party of any kind. And to think of all of my childrens birthday parties in years gone by that I couldve used this one for.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-12390">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Like I said, its simple. You only need 3 things:
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002HQI2EG?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B002HQI2EG&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=amyjoh-20" target="_blank">
           popcorn
          </a>
         </strong>
         +
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0000VONAM?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B0000VONAM&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=amyjoh-20" target="_blank">
           melted white candy melts
          </a>
         </strong>
         +
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001K7Q5PQ?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B001K7Q5PQ&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=amyjoh-20" target="_blank">
           sprinkles
          </a>
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         For the popcorn, you can pop your own (
         <a href="http://simplyrecipes.com/recipes/perfect_popcorn/" target="_blank">
          this is a good method from Simply Recipes)
         </a>
         , or take a short cut like I did, and use microwave popcorn. I like
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002HQI2EG?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B002HQI2EG&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=amyjoh-20" target="_blank">
           Orville Redenbachers Natural, Simply Salted
          </a>
          .
         </strong>
         It had the least amount of crud I could find in the way of microwave popcorn, and just the right amount of salt to balance the sweetness.
        </p>
        <p>
         The
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B006299MBG?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B006299MBG&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=amyjoh-20" target="_blank">
           Vanilla CandiQuik
          </a>
         </strong>
         or
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0000VONAM?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B0000VONAM&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=amyjoh-20" target="_blank">
           wafer style candy melts
          </a>
         </strong>
         worked great for me. I tend to like using them better than the white chocolate chips. Sometimes white chocolate chips act wonky when melting.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://shewearsmanyhats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/party-popcorn-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Party Popcorn" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12392" height="485" src="http://cf.shewearsmanyhats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/party-popcorn-2.jpg" title="party-popcorn-2" width="730"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Basically pop the popcorn, melt the white candy/chocolate, gently mix them together. I used my hands, clean hands, of course. If youre using your hands to mix, be aware that melted candy coating is hot-ish. Not scalding mind you, but youll want to work swiftly. A large baking sheet worked great for a surface to combine the popcorn and melted candy. Once mixed, sprinkle with your favorite sprinkle colors before candy hardens. I had multi-color nonpareils on hand, but wouldnt your favorite teams colors be perfect for a Super Bowl party, or tailgating? Or customize to match the birthday girl or boys cake. The fun never ends when
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001K7Q5PQ?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B001K7Q5PQ&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=amyjoh-20" target="_blank">
           sprinkles
          </a>
         </strong>
         are involved.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://shewearsmanyhats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/party-popcorn-1.jpg">
          <img alt="Party Popcorn" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12393" height="485" src="http://cf.shewearsmanyhats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/party-popcorn-1.jpg" title="party-popcorn-1" width="730"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #99cc00;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            I hope you enjoy this easy one. And party on with your popcorn!
           </strong>
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
        </p>

           <p>
            I love salty-sweet popcorn!  I love how it looks with the rainbow non-pareils!
           </p>
           <p>
            When I want to put in the effort, I make salted caramel corn, but if I just want a quick snack, I simply add a little sugar to the pot over the kernals when I pop them.  It melts slightly and distributes over the popcorn in the pot.  I havent found salting in the pot all that effective, so I add salt and butter after popping.  You can use coloured sugar, too.  Havent tried it myself, but Ive heard jello powder can be used similarly for coloured popcorn, and I guess would lend a fruity taste (maybe dont salt in this case.)
           </p>
           <p>
            I make popcorn all the time, batches large and small, using *almost* the same method described in the simply recipes link.  I do not believe that removing the pot for 30 seconds after adding the kernals makes any difference as I get the exact same results without doing so.  Nothing burns, nearly every kernal pops.
           </p>
