                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     Fruit Leather and Fruit Chips
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     Mmmmm.  Fruit leather.
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2vBjLWdyI/AAAAAAAAQOA/GNXKXIsTtSI/s1600-h/15.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345120773916227362" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2vBjLWdyI/AAAAAAAAQOA/GNXKXIsTtSI/s400/15.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 300px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     Let's try to make some
                     <span style="font-style: italic;">
                      au naturale
                     </span>
                     , shall we?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The ingredients and tools:  peaches, honey (agave will also work), lemon juice, plastic wrap, a flat sheet of some sort, and an oven or several hours of hot sunshine.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Here's how we did it:
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I peeled four peaches.  Two of them were ripe, and two were still a bit under-ripe.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Maddie cut them up, and placed them into the blender.
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2oeCa7kRI/AAAAAAAAQNA/vrvubi_DH44/s1600-h/8.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345113566758015250" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2oeCa7kRI/AAAAAAAAQNA/vrvubi_DH44/s400/8.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 329px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     We added a teaspoon or two of squeezed lemon juice (one wedge)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     and a couple of tablespoons of honey.
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2odyqhjiI/AAAAAAAAQM4/fZMVpf74b-M/s1600-h/7.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345113562528452130" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2odyqhjiI/AAAAAAAAQM4/fZMVpf74b-M/s400/7.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 283px; height: 400px;"/>
                     </a>
                     vvvrrrrrrrrr.....
                     <br/>
                     tada!
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2od21xYpI/AAAAAAAAQMw/MoZPAGcpCbo/s1600-h/6.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345113563649368722" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2od21xYpI/AAAAAAAAQMw/MoZPAGcpCbo/s400/6.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 300px; height: 400px;"/>
                     </a>
                     tis done.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     We poured our mixture (taste it to make sure it's right, ours was perfect the first time) onto plastic wrap and onto your sheets or trays.
                     <br/>
                     Instead of stirring it around, we just rotated our trays until the peach yumminess was even over our entire trays.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The lowest temp on our oven is 170f, so that's what we set it to (most suggest a temp of 140*), with our oven door open to it's "catch" place.
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2vZ3Uq3eI/AAAAAAAAQOI/BAt8UiVG7lI/s1600-h/1.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345121191640882658" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2vZ3Uq3eI/AAAAAAAAQOI/BAt8UiVG7lI/s400/1.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 300px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     If your oven goes down to 140f, then set it there, and leave your door open about two inches.
                     <br/>
                     If you're drying in the sunshine, set it out for twenty-four hours, and make sure to cover it up with screens so little critters don't get stuck in your treats.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     We expected 4 hours or so in our oven, but watched it carefully.
                     <br/>
                     The small batch of peach was done after about three hours (we like our leather thin).
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     You can tell it's done pretty easily - if it's wet, it's certainly not done, if it's too sticky give it a few more minutes.
                     <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uh0oHPxI/AAAAAAAAQN4/H6ai3pyYOsE/s1600-h/14.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345120228844453650" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uh0oHPxI/AAAAAAAAQN4/H6ai3pyYOsE/s400/14.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 272px;"/>
                     </a>
                     It will look consistent when it's ready.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     For our peach leather, we left it on the saran wrap, and cut the whole thing with our pizza cutter.
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uhfEK__I/AAAAAAAAQNo/bbEYu4Hw6Zw/s1600-h/12.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345120223056560114" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uhfEK__I/AAAAAAAAQNo/bbEYu4Hw6Zw/s400/12.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 300px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     We rolled 'em up, and stuck them into a sealed container to keep them fresh.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     'Tis done!
                     <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uhOVmp2I/AAAAAAAAQNY/9GcqVK8SgGg/s1600-h/10.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345120218566272866" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uhOVmp2I/AAAAAAAAQNY/9GcqVK8SgGg/s400/10.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 334px; height: 400px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     Should you have extra (or make a lot at a time) then you can store these in the freezer for six months to a year!
                     <br/>
                     Though they'd never last that long around here.....
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uhnkThbI/AAAAAAAAQNw/DcJJYvA0Qew/s1600-h/13.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345120225338820018" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uhnkThbI/AAAAAAAAQNw/DcJJYvA0Qew/s400/13.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 317px; height: 400px;"/>
                     </a>
                     Each of these little strips is the equivalent of about 1/2 a large peach.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     * * *
                     <br/>
                     We tried the same with green apples. (Trevelyn's favorite.)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     This time we used apples, saran wrap, the oven, sheet trays, lemon juice, the blender, and just a dash of water and apple sauce (you don't really need apple sauce, you can add extra water).
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2oeBIvx3I/AAAAAAAAQNI/YSvDwpotOzM/s1600-h/5.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345113566413309810" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2oeBIvx3I/AAAAAAAAQNI/YSvDwpotOzM/s400/5.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 300px;"/>
                     </a>
                     We cut up four apples, after coring them.  We left the peel on-- if you don't have organic apples, then I would recommend peeling them. (The peel is often waxed with paraffin, to preserve the fruit.  That's what gives them that funny feel.)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     We tossed our apples and the juice of half a lemon into our blender.
                     <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2oeUXRH7I/AAAAAAAAQNQ/98hCPKkknm0/s1600-h/3.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345113571574489010" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2oeUXRH7I/AAAAAAAAQNQ/98hCPKkknm0/s400/3.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 294px; height: 400px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     We didn't add honey to this one, as Trev likes sour flavors.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Our blender was having a bit of trouble grinding these up, so we added a dash of water and a maybe a 1/4 cup of applesauce.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Soon enough we had it whirring, grinding our apples into liquid.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     We had this one on the the same temp -170f- and figured four hours.
                     <br/>
                     We let it go a bit too long,
                     <br/>
                     and upon taking the tray out of the oven our leather was crispy instead of a soft leather.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Well!  No matter about that....
                     <br/>
                     after tasting them, they are absolutely delicious.
                     <br/>
                     A flavorful apple chip, friends!
                     <br/>
                     Crispy and tart and so, so fine.
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uhSjjMqI/AAAAAAAAQNg/a_GGrC6CN4Y/s1600-h/11.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5345120219698508450" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_G41xpW1S5xk/Si2uhSjjMqI/AAAAAAAAQNg/a_GGrC6CN4Y/s400/11.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 300px; height: 400px;"/>
                     </a>
                     Two fabulous treats!
                     <br/>
                     Next we're making some using strawberries, bananas, kiwi... and in a few weeks we'll use our raspberries and blackberries!
                     <br/>
                     Mmmmmm....
