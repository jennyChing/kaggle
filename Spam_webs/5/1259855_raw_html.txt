        <h1 class="title">
         Delicious and refreshing watermelon and cucumber ice-cream
        </h1>
        <p>
         I am not a dessert person. I will happily have 5 starters, but desserts, meh. I can take or leave them, perhaps because by the time I get there, the excitement has waned; where a delicious sounding pudding read from the menu sounded initially like a delicious temptress, something to look forward to for the end of the meal, by the time I actually get there, she seems like a slightly vacuous cheap tart (ba-boom). I occasionally in winter have a weakness for public-school style desserts (having never gone to one, I havent been put off by the reality!), and can occasionally be taken for a ride by a sexy sticky toffee pudding, or spotted dick (cue dirty chuckle).
        </p>
        <p>
         This evening we had friends to dinner and given that it was the hottest day of the year, I felt compelled for some weird reason to make ice-cream. Me, the anti-dessert person, I made dessert, possibly for the first time, so thought I would share my recipe for a rather delicious watermelon and cucumber ice-cream.
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://mama-andmore.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/watermeloncuc1.jpg.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img border="0" height="640" src="http://mama-andmore.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/watermeloncuc1.jpg.jpg" width="480"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a name="more">
         </a>
        </p>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         Ingredients:
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         Half a small watermelon (you can if you wish substitute the watermelon for a whole gala melon)
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         1 cucumber  de-seeded and peeled
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         4 tbsps raw honey
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         400ml Almond milk (easily available from large supermarkets)
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         A pinch of rock salt  I used Maldon
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         De-seed and chop the watermelon into chunks, and then do the same to the cucumber and place in a blender with 4tbsps raw honey.
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         Put into a sealable freezer container and freeze for 8 hours or overnight.
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         Remove the following day and put back into the blender, adding the almond milk and pinch of salt to make it creamier. Put back into the freezer and when ready, serve with a bit of mint (optional, but so delicious, and perfect for summer!).
         <p>
          You could also make this with a splash of vodka for a naughty summer dessert/cocktail too!
         </p>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://mama-andmore.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/watermeloncuc2.jpg.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img border="0" height="640" src="http://mama-andmore.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/watermeloncuc2.jpg.jpg" width="480"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <i>
          PS  I served this dessert after a starter of chilled cucumber soup (recipe to follow next week!) and a main dish of Courtbouillon de poisson, a Martiniquan fish stew (recipe
          <a href="http://mama-andmore.com/2014/01/mama-loves-food-courtbouillon-de-poisson-a-creole-fish-stew-from-martinique.html" target="_blank">
           <span style="color: blue;">
            here
           </span>
          </a>
          ), light, and perfect for summer eating!
         </i>
