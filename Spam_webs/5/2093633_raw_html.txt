        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         5 Time Management Mistakes of Bloggers
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-10-15T08:46:03+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 15, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.keepingyourcents.com/author/desiab/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Mardesia
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.keepingyourcents.com/time-management-mistakes-of-bloggers/#comments">
           2 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-color="red" data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonPin" data-pin-zero="true" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.keepingyourcents.com%2Ftime-management-mistakes-of-bloggers%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.keepingyourcents.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2014%2F10%2FTime_Management_Mistakes_Bloggers1.png&amp;description=5%20Time%20Management%20Mistakes%20of%20Bloggers" rel="nobox">
          <img data-pib-button="true" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <img alt="Time Management Mistakes Bloggers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-72207" height="944" src="http://www.keepingyourcents.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Time_Management_Mistakes_Bloggers1.png" width="944"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As a full-time working mom whos trying to grow my blog, time management is crucial. Work, the kids schedules, spiritual activities and other commitments dont stop because Id like to work on my blog. I read an article recently that made me look at time management as a blogger in a different way. The conclusion I came to is this:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Being a good time manager is often not about learning how to manage your time well; instead its about making sure you dont make mistakes that cause stress and inefficiency in your life.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          Five Common Time Management Mistakes of Bloggers
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         These are five of the most common time management mistakes that I and Im sure other bloggersmake, along with how to avoid them.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          ==&gt; Mistake #1: Not Setting Time Limits on Tasks
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Some tasks can go over time by quite a lot. For example, lets say you are creating and formatting an e-book to the Kindle format. At first, you might think its a piece of cake, but looking into it further, you realize there are all kinds of intricacies to the formatting process. By not setting atime limit, you could easily spend hours and hours on that one task, which detracts from your ability to get other things done.
        </p>
        <p>
         For tasks that could go over time,
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          set a strict time limit
         </span>
         . At the end of the time, schedule another time to work on the task. Scheduling the task means that it wont take over the rest of your day.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          ==&gt; Mistake #2: Ignoring Not Urgent but Important Tasks
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Its easy to focus your attention on things that are urgent. For example, if your website hosts server goes down, thats extremely urgent and calls for everyones attention.
        </p>
        <p>
         But people tend to ignore things that are important, but not urgent. For example, planning your ten-year goals. Or researching your next marketing tactic. Or networking with potential mentors. These tasks are important, but have no deadline.
        </p>
        <p>
         Make sure to schedule some time for these tasks.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          ==&gt; Mistake #3: Blaming Yourself When Its Your Schedule
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         If you always feel like youre not getting enough done, or if youre regularly late on tasks, theres a good chance its not your fault. Its not you lacking in competence, nor your commitment. Instead, you may just have an unrealistically packed schedule.
        </p>
        <p>
         Learn to say no to requests. Dont stuff more into your schedule than you can realistically achieve.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          ==&gt; Mistake #4: Thinking Busy Means Efficient
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Theres a big difference between doing a lot of things and getting a lot done. People often force themselves to constantly be busy, even if they arent achieving a whole lot with all the commotion.
        </p>
        <p>
         Focus instead on high leverage activities. Learn to delegate. Get as much done as possible by looking at your actual end results, not on how much time you spend working on a problem.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          ==&gt; Mistake #5: Too Much Timeon Social Media
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Do you ever feel like you spend far too much time on Social Media andnot much is getting accomplished? Chances are, youre right.
        </p>
        <p>
         In any given Social Media session, try to get clear on the purpose of being there. When you arrive on Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest or the like, work towards that purpose. Avoid chatting about topics that arent relevant or getting sidetracked. If wasting time on social mediabecome a regular issue, set a time limit.
        </p>
        <p>
         These are the mistakes I am personally working to avoid as I blog to be more efficient.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          What mistakes would you add to this list?
         </span>
        </h3>
