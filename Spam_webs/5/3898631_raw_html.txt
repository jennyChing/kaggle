                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Women In Dubai: Their Status And Involvement
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5605287993010915571" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        The position of women in the
                        <b>
                         UAE
                        </b>
                        has highly developed in  latest years, making the UAE one of the leader in women's rights and Protection  in the Arabic world. However there were some chances for women outside the home  before 1960, the discovery of oil led to advancement in women's position. The  UAE constitution guarantees equality between men and women in areas including  legal status, claiming of titles, and access to education. The wide changes to  Emirati women's conventional rights and position have been one of the most  noticeable alteration taking place in the UAE during its about forty years of  modern history. The share of females in upper education has increased outstandingly  at a rate that has not been accomplished by any other country in the world.  During the years 1990 to 2004 the number of female university students has  grown to double that of male students. This is the result of the endorsement  and support of women's education by state and family. According to
                        <b>
                         Dubai  Women's
                        </b>
                        College, 50-60% of its 2,300 students proceed to seek employment upon  graduation.
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                         <a href="http://www.dubaivisas.in/visa-guide/challenges-and-opportunities-for-women-workforce-in-uaedubai/">
                          <b>
                           UAE Women
                          </b>
                         </a>
                        </span>
                        have the highest rate of participation in employment of  the GCC countries, according to the Booz &amp; Company on Women's Employment.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                       </span>
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-eGL_UaNFWxA/U9Cj4qq4_yI/AAAAAAAAA4o/Dh9PixRqqG8/s1600/Women+In+Dubai.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="424" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-eGL_UaNFWxA/U9Cj4qq4_yI/AAAAAAAAA4o/Dh9PixRqqG8/s1600/Women+In+Dubai.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        UAE women  account for 59 percent of the workforce sharing, while Kuwait women share 42  percent, Qatar women 36 percent, Bahrain is 34 percent, and in
                        <b>
                         Saudi Arabia
                        </b>
                        it  is 20 percent. Female graduates in the UAE are employed within historically  male-dominated professions of government, engineering, science, computer  technology, law, commerce, and the oil industry. Sheikha Lubna holds the  distinction of being the first woman to hold a ministerial post in the country.  Promoting trade relations throughout the world on behalf of the UAE, her  efforts have led her to be rated within the Forbes Magazine's 100 Most Powerful  Women.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                        The UAE regularly participates and hosts international  conferences on women's issues. UAE is fairly strict on sexual assault weather  you are a Local or on Dubai Visit.
                        <span style="color: blue; font-size: large;">
                         <b>
                          <a href="http://www.dubai-visa.co.uk/blog/how-to-enjoy-your-holidays-in-dubai">
                           <span class="bold2" title="Dubai Tourism">
                            Dubai Tourism
                           </span>
                          </a>
                         </b>
                        </span>
                        Department has taken extensive  measure for protecting the rights of Foreign Visitors who are visiting Dubai.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                     </div>
