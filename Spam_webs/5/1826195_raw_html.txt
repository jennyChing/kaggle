       The Italian supercar brand Lamborghini has unveiled its all-new
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.lamborghini.com/en/models/huracan-lp-610-4/explore/#cfg14,A3A3,RAD,C3E,BAV,PC1,MAD,PD1,0UJ,0UJ_NA,0UJ_FCU_VF,LRA,2PF,EMI,NR1" target="_blank" title="Huracan Super Trofeo">
         Huracan Super Trofeo
        </a>
       </strong>
       race car at this years Quail Motorsport Gathering.
      </p>
      <p>
       Envisioned only for the race tracks, the Huracan Super Trofeo has been made in collaboration with Dallara Engineering and is a non-street-legal edition of the new Huracan sports car, which is already arrived at dealerships. The vehicle has an improved aerodynamic wide body which features front and rear diffusers and adjustable front air intakes.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span id="more-2289">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://cdn.dubaicars.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Lamborghini-Huracan-Super-Trofeo.jpg" target="_blank" title="Lamborghini Huracan Super Trofeo">
        <img alt="Lamborghini Huracan Super Trofeo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2290" height="353" src="http://cdn.dubaicars.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Lamborghini-Huracan-Super-Trofeo.jpg" width="625"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Super Trofeo racing car features an advanced version of the road-legal models 5.2-litre V-10 engine with a seven-speed dual-clutch gearbox, delivering an added 9 horsepower and taking the output to 611hp. However, it is more than 300 lbs lighter than the original model  thanks to the use of carbon fibre and aluminium. This is likely to produce a breakneck speed on the tracks.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://cdn.dubaicars.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Lamborghini-Huracan-Super-Trofeo1.jpg" target="_blank" title="Lamborghini Huracan Super Trofeo">
        <img alt="Lamborghini Huracan Super Trofeo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2291" height="353" src="http://cdn.dubaicars.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Lamborghini-Huracan-Super-Trofeo1.jpg" width="625"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       A major difference between the production and tracks-only models is that the latter boasts a rear-wheel drive system whereas the standard Huracan has an all-wheel drive system.
      </p>
      <p>
       CEO Lamborghini, Stephan Winkelmann, stated that the original Huracan is already a worldwide hit, with sales figures crossing 2,500 units mark. According to him, these figures were much more than the company had been expecting.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://cdn.dubaicars.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Lamborghini-Huracan-Super-Trofeo2.jpg" target="_blank" title="Lamborghini Huracan Super Trofeo">
        <img alt="Lamborghini Huracan Super Trofeo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2292" height="353" src="http://cdn.dubaicars.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Lamborghini-Huracan-Super-Trofeo2.jpg" width="625"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Winkelmann also pointed out the use of V-10 engines in its supercars to be a reason of increased sales.
      </p>
      <p>
       Lamborghinis research and development head, Maurizio Reggiani, commented that the Super Trofeo had been designed from scratch to cater strictly to racing track needs. The car would not only be fun to drive but will be an equal pleasure for our fans to watch it being driven.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://cdn.dubaicars.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Lamborghini-Huracan-Super-Trofeo3.jpg" target="_blank" title="Lamborghini Huracan Super Trofeo">
        <img alt="Lamborghini Huracan Super Trofeo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2293" height="353" src="http://cdn.dubaicars.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Lamborghini-Huracan-Super-Trofeo3.jpg" width="625"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Lamborghini Huracan Super Trofeo was just one of the many cars on display at the event. Other brands vying for buyers attention included McLaren, Porsche, Audi, Bentley, Bugatti, Tesla, Ferrari, Jaguar, Lincoln and Aston Martin.
      </p>
