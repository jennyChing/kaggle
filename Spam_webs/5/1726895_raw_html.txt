          <h2>
           1. Cookies and Cream Mug Cake
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="Cookies And Cream Mug Cake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3718" height="291" src="http://fit.top.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Cookies-And-Cream-Mug-Cake.png" width="375"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: 13px;">
            Serves: 1
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Ingredients
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           2 tbsp Coconut Flour (find on fitandfiesty.com)
           <br/>
           1/2 scoopBalance Sports Nutrition AustraliaWhey Protein (Cookies and Cream or Vanilla)
           <br/>
           2 tbspChobaniPlain Greek Yoghurt
           <br/>
           2 tbsp Milk of Choice (I usedBlue Diamond Almond Breeze)
           <br/>
           1 egg white
           <br/>
           Pinch of Baking Powder
           <br/>
           Sweetener if desired
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Instructions
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Combine all ingredients in a mug.
           </li>
           <li>
            Microwave for 2 minutes,until cooked through checking every 30 seconds. The end result should spring back when pressed.
           </li>
           <li>
            Top with more Greek Yoghurt, a Cookies and Cream Quest Bar or an Oreo- if it fits your macros
           </li>
          </ol>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            2. Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough Icecream
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough Icecream" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3719" height="397" src="http://strong.top.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Chocolate-Chip-Cookie-Dough-Icecream.png" width="530"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Serves: 1
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Ingredients
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           1 large Banana, frozen
           <br/>
           1 scoop Chocolate Protein Powder (I used Balance Sports Nutrition WPI)
           <br/>
           2 tbsp Almond Milk (or any milk of choice)
           <br/>
           1/2 Double Choc Chunk Quest Bar OR 1/4 cup Cacao Nibs, both of which you can findhere
           <br/>
           Sprinkle of Coconut Shreds
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Peanut Butter Sauce (Optional)
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           1 tbsp Chocolate Protein Powder
           <br/>
           1 tbsp Almond Milk
           <br/>
           1 tbsp Peanut Butter
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Instructions
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            In a strong blender, blend chopped banana pieces, protein powder and almond milk. It should end up in an icecream/smoothie like texture but if its too thick, add more almond milk.
           </li>
           <li>
            Pour mixture into a bowl and freeze while you prepare the other ingredients.
           </li>
           <li>
            Chop up Quest bar or Cacao Nibs.
            <strong>
             <br/>
            </strong>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           <strong>
            For PB Sauce
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol start="3">
           <li>
            Microwave the Almond Milk for 10 secs. While it is still warm, stir in PB and protein powder.
           </li>
           <li>
            Take Icecream Mixture out of the freezer, top with Cacao Nibs/Questbars and then drizzle with the Peanut Butter Sauce.
           </li>
           <li>
            Sprinkle Coconut Shreds on top, just to be a little fancy.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            3. Dark Chocolate Peanut Butter Cups
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="Dark Chocolate Peanut Butter Cups" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3721" height="371" src="http://strong.top.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Dark-Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cups.png" width="494"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Serves: 6 Cups
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Ingredients
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           1 cup Coconut Oil
           <br/>
           6 tbsp Agave Syrup or other liquid sweetener 120g Cacao Powder
           <br/>
           1 tsp of Peanut Butter PER cup (or any other nut butter) Toppings of choice
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Instructions
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            In a saucepan, over a low heat, melt the Coconut Oil.
           </li>
           <li>
            Take the pan off the stove and stir in the Agave and Cacao Powder.
           </li>
           <li>
            Pour 3-4 tbsp each into a lined muffin case then leave to set in the fridge for 10mins or until hardened. You should only have used half the batter.
           </li>
           <li>
            Remove from the fridge and place the tsp Peanut Butter in the middle of the cups. I used closer to a tablespoon (#sorrynotsorry).
           </li>
           <li>
            Top the cups with the remaining chocolate batter and place back into the fridge for 15mins.
           </li>
           <li>
            Just before the cups are fully hardened, top with desired toppings. I used Goji Berries, Desiccated Coconut and Seed Mix.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            4. Protein Yoghurt Wake-Me-Up
           </strong>
           <strong>
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="Wake Me Up Protein Yogurt" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3722" height="446" src="http://sexy.top.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Wake-Me-Up-Protein-Yogurt.png" width="428"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Ingredients
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Greek Yoghurt- No Fat, No Sugar Added
           <br/>
           Chopped up Questbar of Choice (I used Strawberry Cheesecake)
           <br/>
           Any of your favourite fruits (I used Grapes and Berries)
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Istructions
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Combine all ingredients and devour!
           </li>
          </ol>
          <h2>
           5. Sweet Potato Fries
           <strong>
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="Sweet Potato Fries" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3725" height="353" src="http://slim.top.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Sweet-Potato-Fries.jpg" width="530"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Serves: 4
           <strong>
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Ingredients
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           1 Large Sweet Potato
           <br/>
           Olive Oil
           <br/>
           Sweet Paprika (or ground paprika)
           <br/>
           Cayenne Pepper (can omit if youre not a fan of hot stuff)
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Instructions
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Preheat oven to 450F/220C
           </li>
           <li>
            Cut Sweet Potato into strips
           </li>
           <li>
            Toss with Olive Oil and spices, to taste.
           </li>
           <li>
            Lay out on a tray lined with baking paper and put into oven for 15mins.
           </li>
           <li>
            Take tray out and flip pieces onto the other side and place back in for 10-15mins.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Tips:
           </strong>
           When baking the fries, spread them out to ensure that they become crunchy.
          </p>
          <h2>
           6. Protein Chocolate Pavlova
           <strong>
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="Protein Chocolate Pavlova" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3720" height="530" src="http://fit.top.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Protein-Chocolate-Pavlova.png" width="502"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Serves: 6-8
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <strong>
             Ingredients
            </strong>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           8 egg whites
           <br/>
           4 tsp. Sweetener (e.g stevia or agave)
           <br/>
           4 tsp. Cacao Powder
           <br/>
           1/2 cCacao Nibsor Dark Chocolate
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            For topping
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           1 cancoconut cream, chilled
           <br/>
           1 scoop @balancesportsnutrition WPC/WPI Chocolate or Vanilla
           <br/>
           Fruit of your choice
           <br/>
           Dark Chocolate
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Instructions
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Preheat oven to 150C.
           </li>
           <li>
            Beat egg whites until peaks form and continue beating while gradually adding in sweetener,
           </li>
           <li>
            Fold cacao powder and nibs into egg mixture then spoon onto a lined baking tray.
           </li>
           <li>
            Place the baking tray in oven and reduce heat to 140C.
           </li>
           <li>
            Bake for 30 mins.
           </li>
           <li>
            Reduce heat again to 120C and bake for a further 25 mins.
           </li>
           <li>
            Turn heat off and leave oven doorclosed until completely cooled.
           </li>
           <li>
            TOPPING: whip coconut cream and add protein powder until soft peaks form.
           </li>
           <li>
            Spread onto meringue just before serving and top with berries and crumbed chocolate. If you dont have protein powder, thats totally cool- just add a little sweetener into the cream and Greek yoghurt.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <h2>

          <p>
           <strong>
            Instructions
           </strong>
          </p>

           <li>
            Combine all ingredients in a rice paper roll.
           </li>
           <li>
            Wet the edges with water and fold together.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <h2>
           8. Healthy Chicken Salad Wrap
           <strong>
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
