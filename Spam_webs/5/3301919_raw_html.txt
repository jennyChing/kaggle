			&lt;span&gt;
								Don&#8217;t Postpone Joy			&lt;/span&gt;
		&lt;/h1&gt;
	&lt;div class="entry-meta"&gt;&lt;span class="date"&gt;&lt;i class="fa fa-clock-o"&gt;&lt;/i&gt;&lt;time class="entry-date" itemprop="datePublished" datetime="2013-04-11T04:00:24+00:00"&gt;April 11, 2013&lt;/time&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="categories-links"&gt;&lt;i class="fa fa-folder-open"&gt;&lt;/i&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.boomercafe.com/category/books-baby-boomer/" rel="category tag"&gt;Books&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="comments-link"&gt;&lt;i class="fa fa-comments-o"&gt;&lt;/i&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.boomercafe.com/2013/04/11/dont-postpone-joy/#comments"&gt;2 Comments&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;!-- .entry-meta --&gt;									&lt;/header&gt;&lt;!-- .entry-header --&gt;

									
									&lt;div class="entry-content" itemprop="text"&gt;
										&lt;div class='shareaholic-canvas' data-app-id='14890128' data-app='share_buttons' data-title='Don&#039;t Postpone Joy' data-link='http://www.boomercafe.com/2013/04/11/dont-postpone-joy/' data-summary='We are the generation that doesn&#039;t live by the calendar. Gary Carter, author of ELIOT&#039;S TALE, has a philosophy that matches that of many baby boomers: Make the most out of life.'&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;We are the generation that doesn&#8217;t live by the calendar. Which is why we like what Gary Carter writes. The author of &lt;a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1601458754/ref=as_li_tf_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1601458754&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=boomercafe"&gt;ELIOT&#8217;S TALE&lt;/a&gt;&lt;img style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" alt="" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=boomercafe&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1601458754" width="1" height="1" border="0" /&gt;, Gary has a philosophy that matches ours: make the most out of life.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Theres a bumper sticker Id probably seen many times before I actually paused and considered the sentiment. It says simply: Dont Postpone Joy.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div id="attachment_12695" style="width: 329px" class="wp-caption alignright"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.boomercafe.com/dont-postpone-joy/mugshot-gary-carter/" rel="attachment wp-att-12695"&gt;&lt;img class="size-medium wp-image-12695" alt="Writer Gary Carter" src="http://www.boomercafe.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Mugshot-Gary-Carter-319x350.jpg" width="319" height="350" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;p class="wp-caption-text"&gt;Writer Gary Carter&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I assume for the twenty-somethings who see life stretched in front of them, perhaps propelled by a live fast, die young, leave a good-looking corpse attitude, this is a working philosophy. In fact, they probably cant imagine why you would postpone joy, or maybe theyre too busy living to consider such a possibility may haunt them someday.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;But for those of us well into our boomer years, that simple three-word phrase &#8212; Don&#8217;t Postpone Joy &#8212; may prompt some unsettling questions: did I postpone joy? If so, why?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;For that matter, we may be driven to consider what, or who, constituted joy in our lives. Or what, or who, could have? Was there something we wanted to do or should have done, but postponed it because of other commitments or responsibilities? Or maybe the right opportunity didnt present itself, or we simply failed to act and create that opportunity.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;This is the kind of reflection on the past that many people shun, perhaps with goodreason. Maybe you believe the past is the past, it cannot be changed, and its better not to dwell on such matters. But even with that attitude, its hard to argue that the past hasnt shaped your life in many ways and, even though technically its in the rear-view mirror, it may still have a profound impact on your daily existence.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The writer William Faulkner proclaimed, The past is never dead. Its not even past. Or you can fall back on T.S. Eliot who wrote in his Four Quartets, Time past and time future/ What might have been and what has been/ Point to one end, which is always present.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.boomercafe.com/dont-postpone-joy/dontpostponejoy-4-13/" rel="attachment wp-att-12709"&gt;&lt;img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-12709" alt="DontPostponeJoy, 4-13" src="http://www.boomercafe.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/DontPostponeJoy-4-13-350x71.jpg" width="350" height="71" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;Putting aside the past, maybe we can now consider the wisdom of no longer postponing joy. Its something quite consistent with the Buddhist admonition to live in the moment, since we cannot know when the end will come. That is an unalterable truth. Its an approach that focuses you on the small things and makes you appreciate the unexpected, and find your own ways to create joy. Maybe it encourages you to undertake something youve always wanted to do or try or experience, but lacked the time or courage or means.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Heres the cold, hard truth as offered by Bronnie Ware, a palliative care nurse in Australia who spent a number of years caring for patients and listening to their reflections. She collected what she heard into a book and gave it a title that pulls no punches: The Top Five Regrets of the Dying. Number one on the list is, I wish I&#8217;d had the courage to live a life true to myself, not the life others expected of me.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;See if that doesnt slow you down and cause you to reflect on your existence.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;For those of us still kicking, consider it a warning to consider your life, and your future a great unknown. And the answer, so simple that it fits on a bumper sticker, is, Dont Postpone Joy.&lt;/p&gt;
