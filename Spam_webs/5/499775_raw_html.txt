    Faggot (food)
   </h1>

     <p>
      <b>
       Faggots
      </b>
      are a traditional dish in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
       UK
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      especially South and Mid Wales and the
      <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(county)" title="West Midlands (county)">
       Midlands
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">
       England
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GBK_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-GBK-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is made from meat off-cuts and
      <a href="/wiki/Offal" title="Offal">
       offal
      </a>
      , especially pork.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GBK_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GBK-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A faggot is traditionally made from pig's heart, liver and fatty belly meat or bacon minced together, with herbs added for flavouring and sometimes
      <a href="/wiki/Bread_crumbs" title="Bread crumbs">
       bread crumbs
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Faggots originated as a traditional cheap food of ordinary country people in Western England, particularly west Wiltshire. Their popularity spread from there, especially to South Wales in the mid-nineteenth century, when many agricultural workers left the land to work in the rapidly expanding industry and mines of that area. Faggots are also known as "ducks" in the
      <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(county)" title="West Midlands (county)">
       Midlands
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">
       Yorkshire
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">
       Lincolnshire
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">
       Lancashire
      </a>
      , often as "savoury ducks."
     </p>
     <p>
      The first use in print cited in the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Oxford_English_Dictionary" title="Oxford English Dictionary">
        Oxford English Dictionary
       </a>
      </i>
      is in 1851, from
      <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Mayhew" title="Thomas Mayhew">
       Thomas Mayhew
      </a>
      , where he describes a dish identical to the modern product with chopped liver and lights in an outer wrapper of
      <a href="/wiki/Caul_fat" title="Caul fat">
       caul
      </a>
      . This was in London.
     </p>
     <p>
      In classic sauce making, a faggot is also a herb bundle, or
      <a href="/wiki/Bouquet_garni" title="Bouquet garni">
       bouquet garni
      </a>
      of bay leaf, thyme and parsley tied together.
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation_and_serving">
       Preparation and serving
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Commonly, the faggot consists of minced pork liver and heart, wrapped in bacon, with onion and breadcrumbs. Often, the faggot is cooked in a
      <a href="/wiki/Crock_(dishware)" title="Crock (dishware)">
       crock
      </a>
      with gravy and served with peas and mashed potato. The mixture is shaped by hand into small balls, wrapped with
      <a href="/wiki/Caul_fat" title="Caul fat">
       caul fat
      </a>
      (the
      <a href="/wiki/Greater_omentum" title="Greater omentum">
       omentum
      </a>
      membrane from the pig's abdomen), and baked.
     </p>
     <p>
      Another variation of faggot is Pig's
      <a href="/wiki/Testicle_(food)" title="Testicle (food)">
       fry
      </a>
      wrapped in pig's caul: the pig's fry and boiled onions are minced (ground) together, then mixed with breadcrumbs or cold boiled potatoes, seasoned with sage, mixed herbs and pepper, all beaten together and then wrapped in small pieces of caul to form a ball. They are then baked in the oven and are usually served cold.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The dish gained in popularity during the
      <a href="/wiki/Rationing_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Rationing in the United Kingdom">
       rationing in World War II
      </a>
      , though its popularity has declined in recent years.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Faggots are often homemade and are to be found in traditional
      <a href="/wiki/Butcher" title="Butcher">
       butchers
      </a>
      ' shops and market stalls, though larger supermarkets generally stock the Mr Brain's brand of mass-produced faggot. This is a
      <a href="/wiki/Frozen_food" title="Frozen food">
       frozen food
      </a>
      product available in
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
       Britain
      </a>
      , made of
      <a href="/wiki/Liver_(food)" title="Liver (food)">
       liver
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Onion" title="Onion">
       onions
      </a>
      rolled into
      <a href="/wiki/Meatball" title="Meatball">
       meatballs
      </a>
      and served in a sauce. These differ significantly from traditional faggots, which have a coarser texture and contain far less water.
     </p>
     <p>
      A popular dish is faggots and peas. This combination is common in the
      <a href="/wiki/Black_Country" title="Black Country">
       Black Country
      </a>
      area of the
      <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(region)" title="West Midlands (region)">
       West Midlands
      </a>
      . It is still common to see small butchers' shops in the area selling faggots cheaply, made to their own recipe.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Double_meaning">
       Double meaning
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The use of the word "faggot" has caused controversy due to its additional meaning as a
      <a href="/wiki/Faggot_(slang)" title="Faggot (slang)">
       pejorative term for a homosexual man
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Comparison_of_American_and_British_English" title="Comparison of American and British English">
       American English
      </a>
      . In 2004, a radio commercial for the UK supermarket chain
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Somerfield_(UK_Retailer)" title="Somerfield (UK Retailer)">
       Somerfield
      </a>
      , in which an American man rejects his wife's suggested dinner saying "I've got nothing against faggots, I just don't fancy them" was found to have breached the Advertising and Sponsorship Code and was banned by the industry regulator
      <a href="/wiki/Ofcom" title="Ofcom">
       Ofcom
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In November 2013, it was reported that British
      <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">
       Facebook
      </a>
      users had been blocked temporarily for using the word, in its culinary sense, on the website. Facebook said that the word had been "misinterpreted."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
