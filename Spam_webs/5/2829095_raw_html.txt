     <p>
      <b>
       Computer literacy
      </b>
      is defined as the knowledge and ability to utilize computers and related technology efficiently, with a range of skills covering levels from elementary use to programming and advanced problem solving.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OTA84_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-OTA84-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Computer literacy can also refer to the comfort level someone has with using
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_programs" title="Computer programs">
       computer programs
      </a>
      and other applications that are associated with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computers" title="Computers">
       computers
      </a>
      . Another valuable component is understanding how computers work and operate. Computer literacy may be distinguished from
      <a href="/wiki/Computer_programming" title="Computer programming">
       computer programming
      </a>
      which is design and coding of computer programs rather than familiarity and skill in their use.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_developed_countries">
       In developed countries
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_literacy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In developed countries">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Computer literacy is considered to be a very important skill to possess in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developed_countries" title="Developed countries">
       developed countries
      </a>
      . Employers want their workers to have basic computer skills because their company becomes ever more dependent on computers. Many companies try to use computers and other technology to improve business efficiency.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Computers are just as common as pen and paper are for writing, especially among
      <a href="/wiki/Youth" title="Youth">
       youth
      </a>
      . There seems to be an inversely proportional relationship between computer literacy and compositional literacy among first world computer users.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      For many applications - especially communicating - computers are preferred over pen, paper, and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Typewriters" title="Typewriters">
       typewriters
      </a>
      because of their ability to duplicate and retain information and ease of editing.
     </p>
     <p>
      As personal computers become commonplace and they become more powerful, the concept of computer literacy is moving beyond basic functionality to more powerful applications under the heading of multimedia literacy or
      <a href="/wiki/New_literacies" title="New literacies">
       new literacies
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      It is frequently assumed that as computer and Internet access is common-place in the first world, everyone in those countries must have equal and ready access to this technology, and to skills in how to effectively use it. There is, however, a significant
      <a href="/wiki/Digital_divide" title="Digital divide">
       digital divide
      </a>
      in even the most technologically advanced and enabled countries, with digital haves and have-nots.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Older workers who do not use the internet at home and are computer illiterate may be frozen out of the job market even for relatively unskilled jobs such as clerking in an auto parts store.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT81813_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYT81813-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Digital Inclusion Forum,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      a consortium set up through joint participation from the Wireless Internet Institute,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">
       IBM
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">
       Intel
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">
       Microsoft
      </a>
      and Ohio's One Community,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is just one organization developed to address this. Their organizational mission in this is to provide a "comprehensive resource center to inform, educate and share best practices among state and local government leaders, industry and institutional stakeholders on identifying and implementing sustainable market solutions to bridge the digital divide in North America."
     </p>
     <p>
      A variety of private sector nonprofits and foundations also contribute to this, in addressing the needs of underserved communities.
      <a href="/wiki/Per_Scholas" title="Per Scholas">
       <i>
        Per
       </i>
       Scholas
      </a>
      , for example runs programs offering free and low cost computers to children and their families in underserved communities in the
      <a href="/wiki/South_Bronx" title="South Bronx">
       South Bronx
      </a>
      , New York,
      <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">
       Miami
      </a>
      , Florida and in
      <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">
       Columbus, Ohio
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
