        <h1 class="posttitle">
         DailyDirt: Getting Some Shuteye...
        </h1>
        <h3>
         from the
         <i>
          urls-we-dig-up
         </i>
         dept
        </h3>
        <div class="postbody">
         Sleep is a vital bodily function -- even if no one knows exactly why we do it. There's a lot of sleep research, but not a lot of solid answers for the origins of sleep. But that doesn't mean we're not learning cool stuff about sleeping all the time. Here are just a few tidbits of research on the topic of sleeping.
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://slate.me/1jgDXw0" title="http://www.slate.com/articles/video/video/2014/05/how_to_lucid_dream_researchers_use_brain_zaps_to_induce_lucid_dreaming.html">
            Lucid dreaming can be induced with some brain stimulation.
           </a>
           It's not an exact science yet, but someday we won't watch TV or play video games -- we'll wear lucid dream head gear and entertain ourselves while we sleep. [
           <a href="http://www.slate.com/articles/video/video/2014/05/how_to_lucid_dream_researchers_use_brain_zaps_to_induce_lucid_dreaming.html">
            url
           </a>
           ]
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://bit.ly/1jgDXvZ" title="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mnDfPpUC_jg">
            Blue light can signal your brain that it's morning.
           </a>
           Unfortunately, some artificial light sources (eg. iPads, smartphones) emit a lot of blue light, so when you use them just before bedtime... it can mess with your body's sleep schedule. [
           <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mnDfPpUC_jg">
            url
           </a>
           ]
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://bit.ly/1oFsWKd" title="http://gawker.com/daytime-nappers-to-die-young-1562556903?utm_campaign=socialflow_gawker_facebook&amp;utm_source=gawker_facebook&amp;utm_medium=socialflow">
            According to a British study, there's a correlation between taking a nap during the day -- and dying before the age of 65.
           </a>
           The causation isn't clear, but if you take a lot of daytime naps.... [
           <a href="http://gawker.com/daytime-nappers-to-die-young-1562556903?utm_campaign=socialflow_gawker_facebook&amp;utm_source=gawker_facebook&amp;utm_medium=socialflow">
            url
           </a>
           ]
          </li>
         </ul>
         If you'd like to read more awesome and interesting stuff, check out this unrelated (but not entirely random!)

          Techdirt post
