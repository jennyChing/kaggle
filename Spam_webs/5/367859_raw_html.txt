              <a href="http://www.littlebigburger.com/">
               <span class="Apple-style-span" style="color:#FF0000;">
                Little Big Burger
               </span>
              </a>
              ...  I heard a lot about LBB so I had to give it a try.  The burger was good, cooked to Medium and well seasoned, the bun was great, toasty and tasty, the condiments on the burger were there, but they didn't shine.  Now the fries said that they were tossed with truffle oil, I didn't taste any truffle oil, I assumed they would be hand cut fries, pretty sure they weren't, if they were, they resembled frozen fries, which is a drag. I'll go back to LBB for the burger, I'll get two next time and I'll give the fries a second chance, maybe I was there on a bad day.  I like what LBB is doing and look forward to getting back to give them another go...

             <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
              <a href="http://foodandink.blogspot.com/2009/10/fletch-halyburton-park-city-utah.html">
               Fletch Halyburton, Park City, Utah
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8723336151965203968" itemprop="description articleBody">
              <div>
               Fletch Halyburton is the Executive Chef and Partner for
               <a href="http://savouryparkcity.com/">
                <span class="Apple-style-span" style="color:#FF0000;">
                 Savoury Kitchen
                </span>
               </a>
               in Park City, Utah.  They're a catering company that is doing it right, brining their own pickles, breaking down whole hogs, canning, composting and buying locally.  Those sweet tongs were laid on at
               <a href="http://eleventhstreettattoo.com/home.html">
                <span class="Apple-style-span" style="color:#FF0000;">
                 11th Street Electric Tattoo
                </span>
               </a>
               in Salt Lake City.
              </div>
              <div>
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/Ss0iVIklnhI/AAAAAAAAAx0/ol90kAW7vrI/s1600-h/jfwh_8-31-09_lomo.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5390002075505892882" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/Ss0iVIklnhI/AAAAAAAAAx0/ol90kAW7vrI/s320/jfwh_8-31-09_lomo.jpg" style="display:block; margin:0px auto 10px; text-align:center;cursor:pointer; cursor:hand;width: 320px; height: 270px;"/>
              </a>
              <div>
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div>
               It's great to see people banging out good food in different parts of the country, the "farm to table" movement is huge in the Northwest and its nice to see it popping up elsewhere.  There is something about Chefs breaking down whole hogs that makes me happy, using those bits that are so often frowned upon, braising the cheeks and eating them on the line, not even sharing with the customer, making head cheese and using the trotters, damn, I'm getting hungry!  I truly miss working in kitchens.  Hats off to the guys at Savoury Kitchen, if you are in need of some catering in Park City, Utah, give them a call.
              </div>
              <div>
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/Ss0iNI4AyUI/AAAAAAAAAxs/NE-BRyMz69g/s1600-h/image.png" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5390001938148411714" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/Ss0iNI4AyUI/AAAAAAAAAxs/NE-BRyMz69g/s320/image.png" style="display:block; margin:0px auto 10px; text-align:center;cursor:pointer; cursor:hand;width: 300px; height: 150px;"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <div>
               Savoury Kitchen
              </div>
              <div>
               Park City, Utah

               www.savouryparkcity.com

              <a href="http://foodandink.blogspot.com/2009/10/mary-angelas-pizzeria-richmond-va.html">
               Mary Angela's Pizzeria- Richmond, VA
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-678868848715140364" itemprop="description articleBody">
              <div>
               I roll in to town after driving about 3000 miles in 2.5 days and all that I can think about is making my down to Carytown for a Cheesesteak from
               <a href="www.maryangelaspizzeria.com">
                <span class="Apple-style-span" style="color:#FF0000;">
                 Mary A's
                </span>
               </a>
               !  It wasn't until the next day that I finally made it downtown for that delicious sandwich.  I also had to get a pie for my son, who has turned into quite the pizza connoisseur over the past few years.
              </div>
              <div>
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/SsfyzwV7LTI/AAAAAAAAAxk/OcUdnCmY6OY/s1600-h/Mary+A%27s+Pie.JPG" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5388542450136198450" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/SsfyzwV7LTI/AAAAAAAAAxk/OcUdnCmY6OY/s320/Mary+A%27s+Pie.JPG" style="display:block; margin:0px auto 10px; text-align:center;cursor:pointer; cursor:hand;width: 320px; height: 237px;"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <div>
               When I lived in the "Fan" 15 years ago, I made it to Mary A's at least once a week, probably twice, it was cheap and awesome, huge slices, good folks and in my opinion, still the best Cheesesteak in Richmond.  Didn't see any tattoos behind the counter, kind of a bummer, but that's becoming a trend, not a lot of ink in this town!
              </div>
              <div>
               <br/>
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/SsfytKUdP-I/AAAAAAAAAxc/oOfwhVdXvrI/s1600-h/Mary+A%27s+Philly.JPG" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5388542336850280418" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/SsfytKUdP-I/AAAAAAAAAxc/oOfwhVdXvrI/s320/Mary+A%27s+Philly.JPG" style="display:block; margin:0px auto 10px; text-align:center;cursor:pointer; cursor:hand;width: 320px; height: 198px;"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div>
               Everyone in town knows that Mary Angela's kicks ass, so this isn't news to any Richmonders, but for those of you who have never been to RVA, if you find yourself here, looking for some tasty food, Mary A's is a must!!!!!!
              </div>
              <div>
               <br/>

                Mary Angela's Pizzeria

                3345 W Cary Street

              <a href="http://foodandink.blogspot.com/2009/09/apizza-scholls-portland-oregon.html">
               Apizza Scholls - Portland, Oregon
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6147997476263666103" itemprop="description articleBody">
              <div>
               <a href="www.apizzascholls.com">
                <span class="Apple-style-span" style="color:#FF0000;">
                 Apizza Scholls
                </span>
               </a>
               has had so much press and such a buzz around it that I avoided it until now, actually, my son and I tried going about a year ago and the staff was so rude, that we decided not to wait the hour and went to Kens instead.  Apizza Scholls didn't do take out for a long time and as a single Dad, it's much easier to grab a pie and take it home, so that's another reason I avoided this place for so long.  So, with all of that said, it was my last night in Portland, my son was already on the East Coast, I was sleeping on a air mattress and I was hungry!
              </div>
              <div>
               <br/>
              </div>
              <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/Sr7MOQGZowI/AAAAAAAAAxU/W6mewnICm8g/s1600-h/Apizza+Scholls+1.JPG" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5385966749593740034" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_nDJhWr43qQI/Sr7MOQGZowI/AAAAAAAAAxU/W6mewnICm8g/s320/Apizza+Scholls+1.JPG" style="display:block; margin:0px auto 10px; text-align:center;cursor:pointer; cursor:hand;width: 320px; height: 214px;"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <div>
               I rolled in to Apizza Scholls and the place was packed, since they now do "to go" pies a few night a week, I decided to order it to go, grab a drink and take it to my empty house.  I went with a Sausage and Onion pie, it was not only what I expected, but it was a bit more.  I was really impressed with Apizza Scholls, still kind of pissed about the first time I tried to go, but will certainly go back next time I'm in Portland.  My favorite pie in town is still Ken's, but Apizza Scholls is a solid second and with a few more visits, I might just have a new favorite!!!
              </div>
              <div>
               <br/>
              </div>

               Apizza Scholls
