      Top 5 Cave Hotels
     </h2>
     <div class="post-331 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-enviroment tag-5 tag-adventure-photos tag-cave tag-earth tag-earth-traveling tag-globe-traveling tag-hotels tag-nature-2 tag-photography tag-top tag-travel tag-travel-photos postBody">

      <p>
       Hide out in a cool cave hotel, no a/c required
       <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
       <span id="more-331">
       </span>
      </p>
      <h4>
       The Mine Suite, Sala Silvermine, Sweden
      </h4>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Billed as the worlds deepest hotel room, this subterranean suite is found 155 metres underground. It is maintained at a comfortable 18C (and extra blankets are also provided), but its a chilly 50-metre dash to the loo  temperatures elsewhere in the mine hover at just above freezing. Sustenance is provided in the form of cheese, biscuits, fruit and sparkling wine.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <a href="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-1.jpg">
        <img alt="Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-333" height="500" src="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-1.jpg" width="801"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
       Le Grotte della Civita, Matera, Italy
      </h4>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The floors are uneven, the rock walls unadorned. There is no restaurant. With a few exceptions, there are no windows. Rather, this is the luxury of the unique experience, the luxury of the one-off, the luxury of a setting and backstory with which no other hotel can compete or compare.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <a href="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-2.jpg">
        <img alt="Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-334" height="500" src="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-2.jpg" width="801"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
       The Laleh Kandovan International Rocky Hotel, Iran
      </h4>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The hotel, which opened in 2007, is on the edge of the mountain village of Kandovan, 27 miles south of the city of Tabriz. All the rooms have under-floor heating and some have whirlpool baths. There are Persian rugs on the floor and the walls have recessed lighting.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <a href="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-3.jpg">
        <img alt="Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-335" height="499" src="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-3.jpg" width="799"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
       Kelebek Hotel, Turkey
      </h4>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Located in the Cappadocian village of Goreme  where cave lovers are spoilt for choice when it comes to subterranean accommodation  rooms here are housed within the cone-shaped fairy chimneys for which the region is renowned. They come complete with hand-carved furniture, locally-made fabrics and marble-tiled bathrooms. Theres also a pretty garden at the rear.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <a href="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-4.jpg">
        <img alt="Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-336" height="500" src="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-4.jpg" width="801"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
       Kokopellis Cave, USA
      </h4>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Built into sandstone cliffs, Kokopellis Cave has fabulous views of the La Plata river and the Four Corners (where the states of Arizona, New Mexico, Utah and Colorado meet). Caves consist of a master bedroom, separate living area, dining area, kitchen and bathroom.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <a href="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-5.jpg">
        <img alt="Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-337" height="500" src="http://globe-travelling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Top-Five-Cave-Hotels-5.jpg" width="801"/>
       </a>
      </p>
