     <h2>
      How to Make a Rockman Sling - Bruno Tosso
     </h2>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0336.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <b>
       You will need:
      </b>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Scissors
      </li>
      <li>
       20 meters of string
      </li>
      <li>
       Measuring tape
      </li>
     </ul>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0337.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Start by taking three strings, each 5 meters long and mark the middle. In this case, 2.5 meters. I use 5 meters to be safe, a common estimate is 3.5 the length of your finished sling.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0340.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Make a simple 3-strand braid.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0341.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Fold the braid, and forming pairs with all strings, divide them into three strands.Now you have your finger loop.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0342.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Continue the braid until you reach a length that suits you.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0345.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      When you are ready to make your pouch, divide the strings, three on each side.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0346.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Take a fourth string and find the middle.Tie a simple knot over one side of the bundle.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0349.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Use the string to weave the pouch. The weave is a simple one.Up, down, up, down, up, down.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0350.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Continue the weave.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0354.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      When you have finished one side of the pouch, untie the knot and proceed to the second side.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0355.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Keep weaving; its important that both pouches be the same size.Keep the weave tight, as open spaces in the pouch will lead to problems later.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0356.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      This is what it will look like when you are done with both sides.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0358.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Divide the strings into pairs one more time.Overlap the pouches before you continue the braid.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0359.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Continue the braid.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0360.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Drop a strand and continue the simple braid, this time it will be asymmetrical, but keep braiding as a 3-strand.(This technique is known as tapering and is optional. If you want to skip this, continue to braid normally with all 6 strands..)
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0362.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Keep braiding while dropping one strand at a time as you go.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0363.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      You will end up with something like this. Three strands at the end of the release cord.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0369.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Make a knot at the end of the release cord. Leave some strands for a snapping sound.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0364.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Untangle the string from the remainders of the pouch.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0365.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Make a loop and pull tight.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0367.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Make a second loop and pull tight.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0368.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Do the same on the other pouch and cut off the excess.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0370.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Congratulations. You are now the owner of a Rockman sling.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <hr/>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <b>
       If you want to go the extra mile, proceed as below.
      </b>
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      Waste not. The remainders of the strings can be used for a whip wrap.
     </div>
     <div>
      A whip wrap is a piece of string secured around the sling to protect the main cords from abrasion.
     </div>
     <div>
      A whip wrap should always be tied next to the pouch, on the release cord. Any other whip wrap is optional.
     </div>
     <div>
     </div>
     <div>
      <img alt="" border="1" src="/articleimages/bruno/CAM_0371.jpg"/>
     </div>
