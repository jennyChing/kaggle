       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Tips To Make Apartment Hunting Easier
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-08-13T04:09:06+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          08.13.2013
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://newlywedsurvival.com/author/swerner/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Stacey Werner
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://newlywedsurvival.com/tips-to-make-apartment-hunting-easier/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <img alt="Follow these tips to help make your apartment search as easy as possible!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4472" height="300" src="http://newlywedsurvival.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/apartment-hunting-tips.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Searching for a new apartment and finding the right one can be challenging, but armed with the the following knowledge and planning tips it doesnt have to be.
        </p>
        <p>
         The following is a guide to help on your apartment hunt
        </p>
        <h2>
         Apartment hunting
        </h2>
        <p>
         First you must find apartments that are for rent. There are lots of tools you can use to help you in your search:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Check
          <a href="http://www.craigslist.org/about/sites" target="_blank">
           Craigslist
          </a>
          for apartments in the area you are looking
         </li>
         <li>
          Drive around the areas you are interested in, most apartment buildings will have signs when they have units available
         </li>
         <li>
          Make use of online tools such as
          <a href="https://www.padmapper.com/" target="_blank">
           Padmapper
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://livelovely.com/" target="_blank">
           Lovely
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.apartments.com/" target="_blank">
           Apartments.com
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         To stay organized once you begin searching, plan on keeping a list of each place you have visited. Take a picture if you can and then make any notes on what you thought about the apartment. This will help you remember details of individual apartments when looking at several different places.
         <a href="http://clicktotweet.com/30v4f" target="_blank">
          Tweet this tip!
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Apartment location
        </h2>
        <p>
         Location is everything  always consider the following when looking at places to live:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Safety  is it a safe neighborhood? Sometimes you can get a feel of how safe an area is simply by walking around but it probably pays to do a quick search online and check out crime statistics in the area.
         </li>
         <li>
          Will you have to commute to work, if so how far will it be? Is there public transportation nearby?
         </li>
         <li>
          Are there businesses nearby or is it strictly residential? Do you want to be within walking distance of things like restaurants, bars and grocery stores?
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Add all of these details to the list you have created.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Apartment amenities
        </h2>
        <p>
         Most people have certain amenities they cant live without  make a list of what your need to have items are. For example,
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Washer and dryer
         </li>
         <li>
          Dishwasher
         </li>
         <li>
          Parking
         </li>
         <li>
          Fitness Center
         </li>
         <li>
          Pool
         </li>
         <li>
          Patio
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Make a list of your top 3 priorities to help narrow down your search. Include cost / budget as one of the three and then go through your list of must haves and decide which two are the most important to you.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Apartment features
        </h2>
        <p>
         Use a critical eye when walking through an apartment to check for any signs of neglect or problems.
        </p>
        <p>
         Check:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Faucets  check water pressure, how hot or cold the water gets and for leaky faucets.
         </li>
         <li>
          Make sure all the windows open, close and lock easily.
         </li>
         <li>
          Find out what utilities are included and if there is A/C Heating
         </li>
         <li>
          Privacy
         </li>
         <li>
          Storage space
         </li>
         <li>
          Appliances
         </li>
         <li>
          Natural light, views and, of course, space.
         </li>
         <li>
          Dont forget to ask about the pet policy if you own a pet.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          Related article:
          <a href="http://newlywedsurvival.com/2012/04/tips-for-negotiating-your-apartment-rent/" title="Tips for Negotiating Your Apartment Rent">
           Tips for Negotiating Your Apartment Rent
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Apartment lease
        </h2>
        <p>
         Before you sign a lease  always, always review the lease and make sure its agreeable.
        </p>
        <p>
         Pay attention to:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Rent
         </li>
         <li>
          Security deposit, possible holding deposits
         </li>
         <li>
          Other additional fees
         </li>
         <li>
          Short term vs long term lease options
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Make sure you have all of the appropriate information you will need in order to sign the lease, fill out the rental agreement and put down a deposit. Having all of that information ready and available in a folder is a great idea.
        </p>
        <p>
         Follow these tips and you will be well on your way to finding the apartment that is just right for you!
         <strong>
          Do you have any tips to add?
         </strong>
        </p>
