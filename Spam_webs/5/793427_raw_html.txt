       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 2: What is OBD-II?
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SUSTLRFI84B8NN6" data-entry-url="/id/OBD-Pi/step2/What-is-OBD-II/" id="photoset-SUSTLRFI84B8NN6">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SUSTLRFI84B8NN6" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FKT/FUQC/I84B8NQR/FKTFUQCI84B8NQR.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FKTFUQCI84B8NQR" data-notes="" href="/file/FKTFUQCI84B8NQR" id="photoset-link-FM28CT5I85QRQ25-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:600px;">
           <img alt="Picture of What is OBD-II?" class="photoset-photo id_FKTFUQCI84B8NQR " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FKT/FUQC/I84B8NQR/FKTFUQCI84B8NQR.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        <p>
         OBD stands for On-Board Diagnostics, and this standard connector has been mandated in the US since 1996. Now you can think of OBD-II as an on-board computer system that is responsible for monitoring your vehicles engine, transmission, and emissions control components.
        </p>
        <p>
         Vehicles that comply with the OBD-II standards will have a data connector within about 2 feet of the steering wheel. The OBD connector is officially called a SAE J1962 Diagnostic Connector, but is also known by DLC, OBD Port, or OBD connector. It has positions for 16 pins, and looks like this:
        </p>
       </div>

       <a id="stepanchor-step3">

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 3: PyOBD?
       </h2>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.obdtester.com/pyobd" rel="nofollow">
          pyOBD
         </a>
         (aka pyOBD-II or pyOBD2) is an open source OBD-II (SAE-J1979) compliant scantool software written entirely in Python. It is designed to interface with low-cost ELM 32x OBD-II diagnostic interfaces such as ELM-USB. It will basically allow you to talk to your car's ECU, display fault codes, display measured values, read status tests, etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         I took a fork of
         <a href="http://www.obdtester.com/pyobd" rel="nofollow">
          pyOBDs
         </a>
         software from their GitHub repository,
         <a href="https://github.com/peterh/pyobd" rel="nofollow">
          https://github.com/peterh/pyobd
         </a>
         , and used this as the basis for my program.
        </p>
        <p>
         The program will connect through the OBD-II interface, display the gauges available dependent on the particular vehicle and display realtime engine data to the cars aftermarket head unit in an interactive GUI.
        </p>
