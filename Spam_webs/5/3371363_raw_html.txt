        <img alt="Post image for My Top 10 Historic Travel Destinations" class="post_image aligncenter frame" height="386" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Romemain-520-x-386.jpg" width="520"/>
        <p>
         <strong>
          History has a weight, a specific gravity. As travelers, we seek out destinations that feel heavy, that impress us with their endurance and their power.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The following destinations are my picks for the heaviest history destinations in the world.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1) Angkor,
          <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/Cambodia-Vacations-c.html" target="_blank" title="Cambodia ">
           Cambodia
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Your first approach to the massive temple of Angkor Wat will be an
         <strong>
          awe-inspiring experience
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         The sheer scale and striking symmetry of the towers is staggering. The best thing about Angkor, though, is that the temple complex is huge, so you will have no trouble finding moments of solitude amongst the ruins.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont miss the rest of Cambodia, though  its far and away my favorite country in Southeast Asia.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Angkor.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4733 aligncenter" height="346" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Angkor.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Angkor" width="616"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tylerdurden/361274809/" target="_blank">
           tylerdurden1
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2)
          <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/Varanasi-Vacations-India-l.html" target="_blank" title="Varanasi">
           Varanasi
          </a>
          , India
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Varanasi is an
         <strong>
          ancient and sacred city on the banks of the Ganges
         </strong>
         in northern India.
        </p>
        <p>
         Home to hundreds of temples and shrines, Varanasi attracts religious pilgrims from across India and around the world, some of whom come to the city at the end of their life so that they can be cremated in one of the ghats by the river.
        </p>
        <p>
         Varanasi is a destination for seasoned travelers with strong stomachs. I got awfully sick there, as have many travelers before me, but I dont regret a moment of my journey.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Varanasi.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4734 aligncenter" height="428" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Varanasi.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Varanasi" width="616"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/jorgesflickr/2668659414/" target="_blank">
           Jorge Bassy
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3)
          <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/Aleppo-Vacations-Syria-l.html" target="_blank" title="Aleppo">
           Aleppo
          </a>
          , Syria
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Aleppo, a medium sized city in northern Syria, is one of the
         <strong>
          oldest continually inhabited cities in the world.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         My favorite places in Aleppo are the hilltop Citadel, an imposing fortress in the center of the city, and the twisting alleyways of the Christian quarter.
        </p>
        <p>
         One of the best souks, or marketplaces, in the Middle East is in Aleppo, located just a short walk from the Citadel.
        </p>
        <p>
         Pick up some locally made soap in the souk  its made with olive oil and laurel  ingredients that havent changed since the Crusades.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Aleppo.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4735 aligncenter" height="412" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Aleppo.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Aleppo" width="616"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/guido_camici/4019210129/" target="_blank">
           guido camici
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4)
          <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/Rome-Vacations-Italy-l.html" target="_blank" title="Rome">
           Rome
          </a>
          , Italy
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If I wasnt so biased towards Asian destinations, Rome would probably be #1 on this list.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres plenty of modern vibrancy in modern Rome, but the
         <strong>
          weight of its history as the imperial capital of the Roman Empire
         </strong>
         is felt on every street corner.
        </p>
        <p>
         The ancient ruins are impressive  dont miss the Colosseum  but the most beautiful structures in Rome are from the Renaissance.
        </p>
        <p>
         One of the most striking examples of Renaissance architecture in Rome is San Pietro in Montorio, a church with a lovely tomb in its courtyard.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Rome-540-x-3641.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4746 aligncenter" height="364" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Rome-540-x-3641.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Rome (540 x 364)" width="540"/>
         </a>
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/artistica2004/3546144675/" target="_blank">
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/artistica2004/3546144675/" target="_blank">
           fancesco sgroi
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5)
          <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/Kyoto-Vacations-Japan-l.html" target="_blank" title="Kyoto">
           Kyoto
          </a>
          , Japan
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Like Rome, Kyoto is a modern city, but the
         <strong>
          past is never far from view.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         There are striking old buildings in Kyoto, like the Golden Pavillion, and there are immense temples, like Kiyomizu-dera, but history in Kyoto is more about an intangible sense of grace than any particular building.
        </p>
        <p>
         My favorite spot in Kyoto is Fushimi Inari, a network of shrines on a mountainside near the old city, where you can walk through thousands of red
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torii" target="_blank" title="torii gates">
          torii
         </a>
         gates that were an inspiration for Christo and Jean-Claudes notorious public art display The Gates in Central Park.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Kyoto.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4737 aligncenter" height="452" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Kyoto.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Kyoto" width="616"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tiseb/44024053/" target="_blank">
           tiseb
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          6) Tigers Nest, Bhutan
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Traveling in Bhutan can feel like stepping back in time.
        </p>
        <p>
         The tiny Himalayan nation is geographically isolated, and the government has prioritized the
         <strong>
          preservation of traditional culture.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Some say the governments policies are fascist and repressive, while others applaud innovative measures like Gross National Happiness, but regardless, Bhutans historical sites are incredibly well preserved.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tigers Nest, a
         <strong>
          temple hanging onto the edge of a cliff
         </strong>
         not far from the town of Paro, is justifiably the most famous historical destinations in Bhutan.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Bhutan.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4738 aligncenter" height="405" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Bhutan.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Bhutan" width="616"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/babasteve/2758223825/" target="_blank">
           babasteve
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          7)
          <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/Machu_Piccho-Vacations-Peru-l.html" target="_blank" title="peru">
           Machu Picchu
          </a>
          , Peru
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I havent seen Machu Picchu myself, but a good friend once assured me that Tigers Nest and Machu Picchu are the two places in the world where
         <strong>
          human endeavor and natural beauty combine
         </strong>
         to produce something utterly sublime.
        </p>
        <p>
         Machu Picchu has been thoroughly discovered at this point, so youll have to travel smart in order to avoid hordes of your fellow tourists, but it truly is one place all travelers should make a point of visiting at least once in their lives.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Machu-Picchu.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4739 aligncenter" height="411" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Machu-Picchu.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Machu Picchu" width="616"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="Photo by http://www.flickr.com/photos/proimos/4015248297/" target="_blank">
           Alex. E Proimos
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          8)
          <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/Petra-Vacations-Jordan-l.html" target="_blank" title="petra">
           Petra
          </a>
          , Jordan
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The landscapes of southern Jordan are stunning in and of themselves, but the ancient city of Petra deep in the desert canyons is like
         <strong>
          something out of a dreamor a movie.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Movie fans will recognize parts of the ruins from the film Indiana Jones and the Last Crusades.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Petra.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4740 aligncenter" height="411" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Petra.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Petra" width="616"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="Photo by http://www.flickr.com/photos/kudumomo/3943159533/" target="_blank" title="petra">
           kudumomo
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          9)
          <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/Luang_Prabang-Vacations-Laos-l.html" target="_blank" title="laos">
           Luang Prabang
          </a>
          , Laos
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Luang Prabang is a small city on the Mekong river in northern Laos.
        </p>
        <p>
         Over
         <strong>
          35 Buddhist temples are located in the old town
         </strong>
         , but unlike the preserved ruins of other destinations, the temples of Luang Prabang are very much a functional element of Lao religious and community life.
        </p>
        <p>
         Try to visit Luang Prabang during a festival, although youll need to book lodging in advance.
        </p>
        <p>
         A trip to Kuang Si waterfall will be awesome, but pass up on the boat ride to the relatively unimpressive Buddha Caves near the Pak Ou confluence.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Luang-Prabang.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4741 aligncenter" height="462" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Luang-Prabang.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Luang Prabang" width="616"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="Photo by http://www.flickr.com/photos/blackwing_de/3413602483/" target="_blank">
           blackwing_de
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          10)
          <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/Athens-Vacations-Greece-l.html" target="_blank" title="athens">
           Athens
          </a>
          , Greece
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I included Rome, so I have to include Greece. Athens has recently been beset by riots and political crisis, but the ruins of the ancient Greeks have survived a lot worse over the centuries.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Athens acropolis remains
         <strong>
          one of the most iconic historical sites in the world.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Athens.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4742 aligncenter" height="410" src="http://www.tripbase.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Athens.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black; margin-top: 1px; margin-bottom: 1px;" title="Athens" width="616"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          <a href="Photo by http://www.flickr.com/photos/chrissy575/4062281039/" target="_blank">
           Chrissy575
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
