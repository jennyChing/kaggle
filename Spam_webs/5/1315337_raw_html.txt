     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Shimizu TRY 2004 Mega-City Pyramid
      </b>
      is a proposed
      <a href="/wiki/Shimizu_Corporation" title="Shimizu Corporation">
       Shimizu Corporation
      </a>
      project for the construction of a massive pyramid over
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Bay" title="Tokyo Bay">
       Tokyo Bay
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japan
      </a>
      . The structure would be more than 14 times as high as the
      <a href="/wiki/Great_Pyramid_of_Giza" title="Great Pyramid of Giza">
       Great Pyramid of Giza
      </a>
      (139 metres, 456.04 feet), and would house 1,000,000 people. The structure would be 2000 meters (6,561 feet) tall measured from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AMSL" title="AMSL">
       mean sea level
      </a>
      , including 5 stacked
      <a href="/wiki/Truss" title="Truss">
       trusses
      </a>
      , each with similar dimensions to that of the Great Pyramid of Giza. This pyramid would help answer Tokyo's increasing lack of space, although the project would only handle a small fraction of the population of the
      <a href="/wiki/Greater_Tokyo_Area" title="Greater Tokyo Area">
       Greater Tokyo Area
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The proposed structure is so large that it could not be built with current conventional materials, due to their weight. The design relies on the future availability of super-strong lightweight materials based on
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_nanotube" title="Carbon nanotube">
       carbon nanotubes
      </a>
      presently being researched.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Materials_and_construction_process">
       Materials and construction process
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimizu_Mega-City_Pyramid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Materials and construction process">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      First, the pyramid's
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foundation_(architecture)" title="Foundation (architecture)">
       foundation
      </a>
      would be formed by 36 piers made of special concrete.
     </p>
     <p>
      Because the seismically active
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Ring_of_Fire" title="Pacific Ring of Fire">
       Pacific Ring of Fire
      </a>
      cuts right through
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japan
      </a>
      , the external structure of the pyramid would be an open network of megatrusses, supporting struts made from
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_nanotube" title="Carbon nanotube">
       carbon nanotubes
      </a>
      to allow the pyramid to stand against and let through high winds, and survive
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earthquakes" title="Earthquakes">
       earthquakes
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">
       tsunamis
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The trusses would be coated with
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_cell" title="Solar cell">
       photovoltaic
      </a>
      film to convert sunlight into electricity and help power the city. The city will also be powered by pond scum or algae.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Interior_traffic_and_buildings">
       Interior traffic and buildings
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shimizu_Mega-City_Pyramid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Interior traffic and buildings">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Transportation within the city would be provided by
      <a href="/wiki/Moving_walkway" title="Moving walkway">
       accelerating walkways
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Elevator" title="Elevator">
       inclined elevators
      </a>
      , and a
      <a href="/wiki/Personal_rapid_transit" title="Personal rapid transit">
       personal rapid transit
      </a>
      system where automated pods would travel within the trusses.
     </p>
     <p>
      Housing and office space would be provided by twenty-four or more 30-story high
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Skyscraper" title="Skyscraper">
       skyscrapers
      </a>
      suspended from above and below, and attached to the pyramid's supporting structure with nanotube cables.
     </p>
