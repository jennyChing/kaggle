       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         If you cant beat them
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="date">
          <a href="http://www.whydontyou.org.uk/blog/2009/01/04/if-you-cant-beat-them/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to If you cant beat them">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2009-01-04T01:22:58+00:00">
            Sun, 4 January, 2009
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="categories-links">
          <a href="http://www.whydontyou.org.uk/blog/category/general/rants/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Rants">
           Rants
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.whydontyou.org.uk/blog/category/society/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Society">
           Society
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="tags-links">
          <a href="http://www.whydontyou.org.uk/blog/tag/newsbiscuit/" rel="tag">
           newsbiscuit
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.whydontyou.org.uk/blog/author/pollyunsaturate/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Polly Unsaturate">
           Polly Unsaturate
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         vjack reported an
         <a href="http://www.atheistrev.com/2009/01/atheist-revolution-post-subject-of.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.atheistrev.com']);">
          Alliance Defence Fund alert about one of his posts
         </a>
         . Its cheering that the ADF actually read and worry about atheists arguments, although I reckon few bloggers would welcome that kind of attention.
        </p>
        <p>
         This led me to look at their site, follow a few links, and wander around the reaches of the Christian right.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Quietly giggle at the argument that the self-styled pro-life movement is the true inheritor of
          <a href="http://www.firstthings.com/article.php3?id_article=6453" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.firstthings.com']);">
           the 1960s new left
          </a>
          .  Which in this context is somehow a good thing, flying directly in the face of the values expressed in this stuff.  (I dont understand it either, unless the objective is to trick people who dont assume the New Left represented the lead horseman of the apocalypse)  This, erm, unusual argument was put by Richard John Neuhaus, who turns out to be a Lutheran-pastor-turned-Catholic-priest, described
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_John_Neuhaus" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://en.wikipedia.org']);">
           in Wikipedia
          </a>
          as:
          <br/>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            A close, yet unofficial, collaborator of President George W. Bush, Neuhaus advises Bush, who simply calls him Father Richard, on a range of religious and ethical matters, including abortion, stem-cell research, cloning, and the defense of marriage amendment
           </p>
          </blockquote>
         </li>
         <li>
          Laugh out loud at the Reagan Ranch. This seems to be Graceland for people who would find the living quarters of a 1950s singer to be too interesting. Its run by the Young Americas
          <em>
           (sic)
          </em>
          Foundation (motto: 
          <em>
           The Conservative Movement Starts Here.
          </em>
          ) I initially assume its a parody site, on the basis of
          <a href="http://yaf.org/reaganranch/reagan/presidential_releases.cfm" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://yaf.org']);">
           some seriously mind-numbing content.
          </a>
          . This is a typical press release, ffs:
          <br/>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            The President and Mrs. Reagan had breakfast together this morning at their residence at Rancho del Cielo, where the weather is sunny and warm. After breakfast, the President attended to routine paperwork and is now getting ready to go horseback riding. After riding, the President will have lunch with Mrs. Reagan and this afternoon, will clear brush on the ranch property. The Reagans will have dinner together this evening.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
         </li>
         <li>
          Stop laughing rather swiftly when I bump into The Young Americas Foundation
          <a href="http://www.yaf.org/njc/forms/njc_internapplication.php" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.yaf.org']);">
           National Journalism Center Internships in Washington DC
          </a>
          This Journalism Center seems to specialise in getting its conservative-by-definition students placed in major news outlets. For instance, heres the 2008 tally of placements
          <br/>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            .. media outlets around the Washington, D.C. area, including Fox News, CNN, BBC, Comcast Sports Net, Roll Call, The Washington Times, Washington Examiner, Human Events, Evans &amp; Novak Political Report, The Michael Reagan Show, The Sean Hannity Show, The G. Gordon Liddy Show, National Review Online, News Max, London Daily Telegraph, and Doublethink Magazine.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           Arent rightwingers continually bleating about the liberal media and its bias against them? Well, this is part of their fightback strategy. From the FAQs.:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            Q:  What are you seeking in a candidate?
            <br/>
            A:  The National Journalism Center seeks candidates that are dedicated to becoming a better journalist, becoming more responsible reporter, and combating media bias.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           (How many grammatical errors are there in that answer? Yeah, ok, pot/kettle, but they must have had enough time to check the FAQ pages surely. And Im not  a journalist, let alone a journalism training centre.)
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Being from the UK, I have only the haziest idea what interns do and I suspect this idea is not 100% accuarate, based, as it is, on the Clinton/Lewinsky saga.  But I guess its work experience, for a $1000 a month stipend, apparently.  I look at the list of articles published by the Fall 2008 alumni (Let the Americanisms run riot
         <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.whydontyou.org.uk/blog/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
         tr: people who did the course this Autumn)
        </p>
        <p>
         I naturally assume its
         <a href="http://newsbiscuit.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://newsbiscuit.com']);">
          News Biscuit link
         </a>
         when I see:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Bridge to Nowhere leads nowhere
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         But its a real article in the American Spectator. By an alumnus.
        </p>
        <p>
         (Heres a gratuitous link to Apathy Sketchpads fine
         <a href="http://www.apathysketchpad.com/blog/2008/12/30/my-newsbiscuit-annual/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.apathysketchpad.com']);">
          News Biscuit Annual post
         </a>
         anyway)
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Young Catholics share similar political and social views
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         (Yawn.)
        </p>
        <p>
         A handful more in the same vein. Straight reports of not very interesting topics but professionally done. The site shows the optimistic fresh faces of the student alumni.   Imagine getting the opportunity to work for the Washington Post and to get paid for doing it. They must feel pretty grateful to the Young Americas Foundation.  I start to feel churlish about mocking the project. Then I turn to the page showing
         <a href="http://yaf.org/njc/graduates/books.cfm" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://yaf.org']);">
          Books and Articles by NJC graduates.
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive heard of some of these graduates, such as Ann Coulter. (Hmmm.) Here are a few titles from the alumnis publications. I have only picked out about one in four and I stopped at the letter K in the alphabetical list., in order to retain the will to live.  So you can take it that there are plenty more where these came from.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          The Writing Christian
         </li>
         <li>
          Treason: Liberal Treachery from the Cold War to the War on Terror
         </li>
         <li>
          Slander: Liberal Lies about the American Right
         </li>
         <li>
          Triumph: The Power and Glory of the Catholic Church
         </li>
         <li>
          Robert E. Lee on Leadership
         </li>
         <li>
          Polluted Science: The EPAs Campaign to Expand Clean Air Regulations
         </li>
         <li>
          The Myth of Heterosexual AIDS
         </li>
         <li>
          Enemies of Eros: How the Sexual Revolution is Killing Family
         </li>
         <li>
          Economic Casualties: How U.S. Foreign Policy Undermines Trade, Growth, and Liberty
         </li>
         <li>
          The Age of Reagan: The Rebirth and Triumph of the American Spirit
         </li>
         <li>
          The Rise of the Nanny State: How Consumer Advocates Try to Run Our Lives
         </li>
         <li>
          Fostering Environmental Prosperity: Multinationals in Developing Countries
         </li>
         <li>
          The Heroic Enterprise: Business and the Common Good
         </li>
         <li>
          Investor Politics: The New Force That Will Transform American Business, Government and Politics in the 21st Century
         </li>
         <li>
          Global Taxes for World Government
         </li>
         <li>
          Global Bondage: The U.N. Plan to Rule the World
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Not only are these books apparently so uniformly US-style-rightwing that you wouldnt be surprised to find this was the Unabomberss reading list, they sound so far beyond dull that the concept would have to be reinvented.  Liberal, left-leaning media, indeed.
         <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.whydontyou.org.uk/blog/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
        </p>
