      <div class="bodycontent">
       <p>
        This baby girl just couldn't wait to make her dramatic entrance!
       </p>
       <p>
        Mom-to-be, Kelsey Marcus, 21, was scheduled to be induced after her 8-day overdue baby wasn't ready to leave mommy's motel. The morning of, Marcus woke up in a lot of pain...and then her water broke.
       </p>
       <p>
        Her 14-year-old sister, Kennedy Smith, and her mother, Kellie Ann Smith, piled into the car and barreled up I-95 near Newark towards the hospital.
       </p>
       <p>
        "We were able to get her in the car and got her down 896 when Kelsey said she felt the baby's head coming out," Kellie told
        <a href="http://www.delawareonline.com/story/news/local/2014/09/29/aunt-delivers-baby-born-delaware-rest-area/16440513/" rel="nofollow">
         The Delaware News Journal
        </a>
        . "We weren't sure if it was the baby, or if she was just scared."
       </p>
       <p>
        As Kellie drove, Kennedy was in the back seat coaching her sister as they drove.
       </p>
       <p>
        "Everyone was going crazy, so someone had to stay calm and keep everything together," Kennedy said. "Ten minutes after my sister's water broke, the baby came."
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         FOR YOU:
        </strong>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.whattoexpect.com/pregnancy/emergency-labor">
          Emergency Labor: What to Do If Your Give Birth Alone
         </a>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        By the time Kellie pulled over to call 911, the baby girl already made her debut.
       </p>
       <p>
