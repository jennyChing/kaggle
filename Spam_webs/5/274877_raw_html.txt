     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System
      </b>
      , commonly known as the Federal Reserve Board, is the main governing body of the
      <a href="/wiki/Federal_Reserve_System" title="Federal Reserve System">
       Federal Reserve System
      </a>
      . It is charged with overseeing the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Reserve_Banks" title="Federal Reserve Banks">
       Federal Reserve Banks
      </a>
      and with helping implement
      <a href="/wiki/Monetary_policy_of_the_United_States" title="Monetary policy of the United States">
       monetary policy of the United States
      </a>
      . Governors are appointed by the
      <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">
       President of the United States
      </a>
      and confirmed by the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">
       Senate
      </a>
      for staggered 14-year terms.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usc12-241_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-usc12-241-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1

      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-federalreserve1_2-0">

        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
       Description
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Federal_Reserve_Board_of_Governors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      By law, the appointments must yield a "fair representation of the financial, agricultural, industrial, and commercial interests and geographical divisions of the country".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usc12-241_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-usc12-241-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-federalreserve1_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-federalreserve1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As stipulated in the Banking Act of 1935, the Chairman and Vice Chairman of the Board are two of seven members of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Board_of_Governors" title="Board of Governors">
       Board of Governors
      </a>
      who are appointed by the
      <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">
       President
      </a>
      from among the sitting Governors.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usc12-241_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-usc12-241-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-federalreserve1_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-federalreserve1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Board of Governors does not receive funding from Congress, and the terms of the seven members of the Board span multiple presidential and congressional terms. Once a member of the Board of Governors is appointed by the president, he or she functions mostly independently. The Board is required to make an annual report of operations to the Speaker of the U.S. House of Representatives.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It also supervises and regulates the operations of the Federal Reserve Banks, and the U.S. banking system in general.
     </p>
     <p>
      Membership is by statute limited in term, and a member that has served for a full 14 year term is not eligible for reappointment.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usc12-242_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-usc12-242-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      There are numerous occasions where an individual was appointed to serve the remainder of another member's uncompleted term, and has been reappointed to serve a full 14-year term.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usc12-242_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-usc12-242-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Since "upon the expiration of their terms of office, members of the Board shall continue to serve until their successors are appointed and have qualified",
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usc12-242_4-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-usc12-242-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      it is possible for a member to serve for significantly longer than a full term of 14 years. The law provides for the removal of a member of the Board by the President "for cause".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usc12-242_4-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-usc12-242-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      </sup>
     </p>
