     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air
       </b>
      </i>
      is an American television
      <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">
       sitcom
      </a>
      that originally aired on
      <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">
       NBC
      </a>
      from September 10, 1990, to May 20, 1996. The show stars
      <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">
       Will Smith
      </a>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith_(character)" title="Will Smith (character)">
       a fictionalized version of himself
      </a>
      , a street-smart teenager from
      <a href="/wiki/West_Philadelphia" title="West Philadelphia">
       West Philadelphia
      </a>
      who is sent to move in with his wealthy aunt and uncle in their
      <a href="/wiki/Bel_Air,_Los_Angeles" title="Bel Air, Los Angeles">
       Bel Air
      </a>
      mansion after getting into a fight on a local basketball court. In the series, his lifestyle often clashes with the lifestyle of his relatives in Bel Air. The series lasted for six seasons and aired 148 episodes.

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The pilot episode began taping on May 1, 1990.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Season 1 production began in July 1990 with #446802 and ended in March 1991 with #446824. The series finale was filmed on Thursday, March 21, 1996, specifically production codes #60122 and #60123.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Season_1_Production_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Season_1_Production-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finale_Film_Date_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Finale_Film_Date-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The theme song was written and performed by Smith, as "
      <a href="/wiki/DJ_Jazzy_Jeff_%26_The_Fresh_Prince" title="DJ Jazzy Jeff &amp; The Fresh Prince">
       The Fresh Prince
      </a>
      ". The music was composed by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/QDIII" title="QDIII">
       QDIII
      </a>
      (Quincy Jones III), who is credited with Smith at the end of each episode. The music often used to bridge scenes together during the show is based on a similar chord structure. The full version of the theme song was used unedited in the first three episodes. The full-length version, which is 2:52, was included on Will Smith's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Will_Smith_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Will Smith album)">
        Greatest Hits
       </a>
      </i>
      album and attributed to him only. A 3:23 version was released in the Netherlands in 1992, reaching #3 on the charts.
     </p>
     <p>
      The mansion used for the exterior shots of "the Banks mansion" is a house located at 251 North Bristol Avenue, Los Angeles, CA 90049, which was built in 1937.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-test_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-test-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Interiors were shot on a sound stage on pre-made sets. After Season 1 the mansion sets were completely rebuilt. In the second season, the kitchen and living room sets were rebuilt much larger with a more contemporary style (as opposed to the much more formal style of the first season), and were connected directly by an archway, allowing scenes to be shot continuously between the sets.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Fresh_Prince_of_Bel-Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The theme song and opening sequence set the premise of the show. Will Smith is a street-smart teenager, born and raised in
      <a href="/wiki/West_Philadelphia" title="West Philadelphia">
       West Philadelphia
      </a>
      , while playing
      <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">
       basketball
      </a>
      , Smith misses a shot, and the ball hits a group of people, which causes a confrontation that frightens the mother of Will, who sends Will to live with his aunt and uncle in the "town" of
      <a href="/wiki/Bel_Air,_Los_Angeles" title="Bel Air, Los Angeles">
       Bel Air, Los Angeles
      </a>
      . He flew from Philadelphia to Los Angeles on a one-way ticket in first class.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He then whistles for a taxi that is described in the opening sequence as having a
      <a href="/wiki/Vanity_plate" title="Vanity plate">
       vanity plate
      </a>
      with the word "FRESH" on it.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Crossovers_and_other_appearances">
       Crossovers and other appearances
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Fresh_Prince_of_Bel-Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Crossovers and other appearances">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      During the fall 199192 season, NBC gained two hit television shows to anchor their Monday night line-up (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Blossom_(TV_series)" title="Blossom (TV series)">
        Blossom
       </a>
      </i>
      aired immediately after
      <i>
       The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air
      </i>
      ). To gain popularity between the two shows, Will Smith appeared in an episode as himself under the rap persona of The Fresh Prince. Several episodes later, Karyn Parsons showed up in an episode of
      <i>
       Blossom
      </i>
      as Hilary Banks. Parsons also appeared in the Patti LaBelle sitcom,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Out_All_Night" title="Out All Night">
        Out All Night
       </a>
      </i>
      as her
      <i>
       Fresh Prince
      </i>
      character.
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/In_the_House_(TV_series)" title="In the House (TV series)">
        In the House
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Fresh Prince
      </i>
      were both executive produced by Winifred Hervey, David Salzman and Quincy Jones. During the second season's first episode, Alfonso Ribeiro and Tatyana Ali appeared as their
      <i>
       Fresh Prince
      </i>
      characters (Carlton and Ashley Banks) in the crossover episode "Dog Catchers". Later that season, James Avery (Phillip Banks) appeared as a mediator in the episode "Love on a One-Way Street". In the Season 4 episode "My Pest Friend's Wedding", James Avery and Daphne Maxwell Reid (Vivian Banks) guest starred as Dr. Maxwell Stanton's parents. Both Avery and Reid portrayed the parents of Ribeiro's
      <i>
       Fresh Prince
      </i>
      character. Joseph Marcell, who played the wisecracking Geoffrey Butler on
      <i>
       Fresh Prince
      </i>
      , also appeared as an officiating minister in the same episode.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Syndication">
       Syndication
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Fresh_Prince_of_Bel-Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Syndication">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The series was originally an NBC production in association with The Stuffed Dog Company and Quincy Jones Entertainment. After the show was released to syndication in 1994, the series has been distributed by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Television_Distribution" title="Warner Bros. Television Distribution">
       Warner Bros. Television Distribution
      </a>
      , which continues to distribute the show worldwide (although NBCUniversal does own the series' copyright).
      <a href="/wiki/WGN_America" title="WGN America">
       WGN America
      </a>
      was the first cable channel to acquire the series in 1997, TBS acquired the series a year later in 1998; both channels carried the series until the fall of 2003, though TBS reacquired the series in 2007. The theme song was shown in the original TBS run, but after TBS re-acquired
      <i>
       Fresh Prince
      </i>
      in 2007, the opening credits were truncated and the theme song removed and replaced with the instrumental version used as the show's closing theme; these versions also re-added portions of scenes cut from the original syndicated prints for some episodes, particularly those from seasons 3-6. TBS continues to air the series today, early in the morning. Reruns also aired on WPIX-TV on weeknights from 1994 until 1996, and as a weekday basis, sometimes on weekends until 2004.
     </p>
     <p>
      The series aired on
      <a href="/wiki/Nick_at_Nite" title="Nick at Nite">
       Nick at Nite
      </a>
      from 2004 to 2009, as well as sister network (through
      <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">
       Nickelodeon
      </a>
      )
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_N" title="The N">
       The N
      </a>
      (now
      <a href="/wiki/TeenNick" title="TeenNick">
       TeenNick
      </a>
      ) with portions of scenes that were removed from other syndicated airings, with parts of scenes kept in other syndicated airings removed due to time constraints; the series was dropped from its schedule in September 2009 after Disney/ABC purchased the rights to the show. The show returned to Nick at Nite on September 29, 2014.
     </p>
     <p>
      In July 2009,
      <a href="/wiki/Disney_XD" title="Disney XD">
       Disney XD
      </a>
      acquired the rights to the series, though it was quickly moved from prime-time to late night airings, and only episodes from seasons 1-3 are aired, mainly because those episodes are more appropriate for young viewers and does not contain as much mature themes, sexual content and strong language as later episodes. But in August 2010, Disney XD stopped airing the show.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/ABC_Family" title="ABC Family">
       ABC Family
      </a>
      acquired the series in September 2008, though airing all 148 episodes; originally airing exclusively on Saturday nights, the series was added to ABC Family's weekday line-up in late 2009. On September 29, 2014, Disney/ABC rights to the show expired.
      <a href="/wiki/Viacom_Media_Networks" title="Viacom Media Networks">
       Viacom Media Networks
      </a>
      got the series back.
     </p>
     <p>
      In October 2014,
      <a href="/wiki/Viacom_Media_Networks" title="Viacom Media Networks">
       Viacom Media Networks
      </a>
      also gave the series to
      <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">
       BET
      </a>
      , it also will air on
      <a href="/wiki/Centric" title="Centric">
       Centric
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      It also currently airs on
      <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">
       MTV
      </a>
      's retro block.
     </p>
