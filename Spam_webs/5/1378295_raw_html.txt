        <p class="content__segment">
         Major NASCAR and IndyCar events would be nice, but theyre not foundational to the business plan a buyer has developed for the idled Nashville Superspeedway.
        </p>
        <p class="content__segment">
         Rather than focus on a handful of high-profile events,
         <a href="nashville/search/results?q=Robert Robb Sexton">
          Robert Robb Sexton
         </a>
         says he wants to turn the track into a 52-weeks-a-year operation,
         <a href="http://www.tennessean.com/story/money/2014/06/15/nashville-superspeedway-buyer-says-plan/10500823/">
          The Tennessean reports
         </a>
         . Before anything, though, Sexton still has to secure the financing necessary to close on the speedway. The deal calls for him to pay $27 million in cash and give Wilson County a $20 million letter of credit in order to purchase it from
         <a class="saveLink" href="http://www.bizjournals.com/profiles/company/us/de/dover/dover_motorsports_inc/1186288">
          Dover Motorsports
         </a>
