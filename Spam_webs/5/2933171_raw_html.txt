         grilled shrimp with garlic &amp; beer
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2011-07-01T10:00:21+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          July 1, 2011
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.thewickednoodle.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Kristy Bernardo
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>

         </span>

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <img alt="grilled shrimp" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4545" height="282" src="http://cdn0.thewickednoodle.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/grilled-shrimp.jpg" title="grilled shrimp" width="425"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Happy Fourth of July weekend!
         <span id="more-4544">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ill be doing my laundry.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know  very boring. Although I will be headed to my sisters on Monday for the actual holiday  you cant miss food &amp; fireworks on the fourth  but Sat &amp; Sun will be dedicated to cleaning my house. But the reason Im doing that is a bit more exciting, so please dont write me off yet.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres a film crew arriving in DC on Tuesday night. After a nights rest,
         <em>
          theyre coming to my house
         </em>
         . To film. ME. Well, me along with four other bloggers for a cooking show. And so, my house must be clean.
        </p>
        <p>
         I wish I could share more than that but Ive been sworn to secrecy. So Ill just say that its going to be a lot of fun and I really cant wait for it all to go down. Theyll be here three days, so I do plan on whipping us all up a few margaritas while theyre here. Were going to need something to go with all the yummy food well be creating! ;-)
        </p>
        <p>
         But before all that fun, and after all that cleaning, Ill be bringing these yummy grilled shrimp to my sisters to toss on her grill. Theyre my contribution to her Fourth of July feast, although her feasts dont really need any contributions (shes pretty party savvy, that one). And she makes the BEST margaritas on planet earth (I promise to share her secret weapon with you one of these days, if she lets me!). Anyway, about these grilled shrimp.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theyre crazy easy, make a fabulous compliment to grilled steak, and taste amazing! Be sure to let them marinate the full time to get the best flavor out of them. I like to skewer them but you can do it any way youd like.
        </p>
        <p>
         Happy Fourth!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.mylivesignature.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="http://signatures.mylivesignature.com/54488/99/D3A72B13FC0F28A9794429E17EF90C47.png" style="border: 0 !important; background: transparent;"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="print-this-button-shell">
         <button class="print-this-button" onclick="parent.location='http://www.thewickednoodle.com/grilled-shrimp-garlic-beer/?printthis=1&amp;printsect=1'" type="button">
          PRINT
         </button>
        </div>
        <div class="print-this-content">
         <strong>
          Grilled Shrimp with Garlic &amp; Beer
         </strong>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Tip:
          </strong>
          Unless youre buying shrimp thats fresh and never frozen (which is rare), buy it frozen. Its typically cheaper, plus you can control when and how it gets thawed. Just place your shrimp in a colander and rinse it under cold water until its thawed.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           2 pounds large shrimp
          </li>
          <li>
           a bottle of good beer
          </li>
          <li>
           1 T minced garlic, plus 2 cloves smashed
          </li>
          <li>
           a couple dashes of your favorite hot sauce
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Put everything in a resealable plastic bag, making sure its mixed well and the shrimp is well coated. Marinate for
          <em>
           at least
          </em>
          two hours and up to four.
         </p>
         <p>
          Place shrimp on skewers and discard marinade. Grill until done (this goes quickly!) and enjoy!
         </p>
