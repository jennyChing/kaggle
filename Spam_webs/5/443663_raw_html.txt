                        <div class="story__content__body story__content__body ">
                         <p>
                          "Dreams Never Die," a digital art and acrylic painting exhibit by Kate Browning Word, is being shown at the City Gallery at City Church.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Kate Browning Word is known for unique photography manipulations presented in mixed media collage in which she uses items such as used coffee filters and dyed fabrics.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Word, a native Texan, spent a year in Taos, New Mexico, where she operated a weaving studio. Collectors from throughout North America and as far away as Kyoto, Japan, recognize her unique appreciation for traditional and fine art craftsmanship.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          This exhibit presents a new focus in Word's photo manipulation and is the first public showing of her abstract acrylic paintings on canvas. She signs her works, "Kate's Branch," because, as she says, "If you feel like the last leaf on your branch,branch out from where you are.'"
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          This became her focus after she experienced reading Zechariah 6:12, "Behold, a man whose name is Branch, for He will branch out from where He is." She realized she could branch out from where she was, and she began building again.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          "Kate's Branch" was formed this year when her husband, Bill, inspired her to embrace her creativity and to encourage other artists, also. Word says all children dream about who they want to be when they grow up and she was no different, but "dream killers" crushed her dreams.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          "One day when I was asked about my personal life dreams, I drew an absolute blank. My inner little girl dreams were so dead, that I had no reply," Word said. "My husband remembered overhearing me describe my love for New Mexico and how I had wanted to live there and make my living with my art. Dreams Never Die' is a personal affirmation to encourage others whose trampled dreams are waiting to be reborn."
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          For 43 years, she made her living using a sculptural approach to hair design. Her participation in the art community was limited to the walls of her beauty salon, so it became an art gallery. Once she retired from cosmetology she was able to rekindle the dream to pursue art.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          "More than a hobby or merely something that I do, now I realize that my art is more about who I am," she said.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Her passion has always been for revealing beauty in any form. She feels that she really does not create anything new, but she unveils what is already hiding there. She believes that creative people use their imaginations to unveil impressions that they see, hear and sense and then express those impulses in various art forms.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          The exhibit is the result of daily studio practice. Some of the practice is on the computer; other practices are mixed-media collage, and still others are abstract paintings.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          IF YOU GO
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          What: "Dreams Never Die"
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          When: 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday - Thursday through June 30
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Where: City Gallery at City Church, 4425 S. Staples
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Cost: Free
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Information: 361-852-1810 or office@citychurchcc.com or katebrowning-word.artistwebsites.com
