                   Sunday, 1 March 2015
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Pc7YZYcgfiw/VPOZ5rSFlyI/AAAAAAAABDw/Yif93MQ3R5c/s1600/banner.jpg" itemprop="image"/>
                    <a name="1297535551774068948">
                    </a>
                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Top 5 Kitchen Gadgets
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1297535551774068948" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Pc7YZYcgfiw/VPOZ5rSFlyI/AAAAAAAABDw/Yif93MQ3R5c/s1600/banner.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Pc7YZYcgfiw/VPOZ5rSFlyI/AAAAAAAABDw/Yif93MQ3R5c/s1600/banner.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I am a total sucker when it comes to kitchen gadgets, anything that claims to make preparing food or cooking quicker, easier or healthier then I probably have tried it. From pineapple cutters to slow cookers I have it and after a few weeks it will either in up in my utility room - AKA 'The graveyard' or it has managed to gain a place in the kitchen. So here are my top 5 kitchen gadgets I own, use and recommend...
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     5. Sistema Microwave Rice Steamer/Cooker
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-w9CJYLuCbIM/VPOGt8UgIjI/AAAAAAAABCk/mIogHn3UPKE/s1600/rice%23.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Sistema Microwave Rice Steamer/Cooker" border="0" height="526" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-w9CJYLuCbIM/VPOGt8UgIjI/AAAAAAAABCk/mIogHn3UPKE/s1600/rice%23.jpg" title="Sistema Microwave Rice Steamer/Cooker" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      As a busy Mum of two boys with big appetites I love a gadget which will help me prepare a freshly cooked meal quickly, which is why this Rice steamer by Sistema Plastic comes in at No 5. This
                      <a href="http://www.lakeland.co.uk/brands/sistema?intcmp=INTSRCH:sistema" target="_blank">
                       microwave rice steamer
                      </a>
                      has fresh rice ready in as little as 10 minutes, simply add the rice, water and a pinch of salt and popped into the microwave. It comes with a inner lid to prevent the rice boiling over and the top lid clips on in 4 places keeping it tight so the rice can steam. It also has a rice paddle so you can fluff up the cooked rice before serving. All parts are dishwasher safe although it is super easy to rinse as it is non-stick. You can also cook over grains in here such as couscous or polenta. I purchased this from Lakeland and it was under 10.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      4. The NutriBullet
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-giXPDrTfKUw/VPOJv3JWCPI/AAAAAAAABCw/O3sjWa1PsKE/s1600/bullet.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="The NutriBullet" border="0" height="480" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-giXPDrTfKUw/VPOJv3JWCPI/AAAAAAAABCw/O3sjWa1PsKE/s1600/bullet.jpg" title="The NutriBullet" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      I have been through so many blenders, smoothie makers and juicers in the past and nothing compares to this little gadget. The
                      <a href="http://www.buynutribullet.co.uk/nutri-bullet-microsite.html" target="_blank">
                       NutriBullet is small and so easy to use
                      </a>
                      , the main unit is smaller than an average kitchen kettle so takes up very little space. It comes with 2 attachments which you screw directly onto the cup so cleaning up is super easy and quick. The first attachment is for smoothie making. This breaks down fruit and veg in no time to make a quick and easy smoothie. I tend to use frozen fruit and it has no problem blasting through this or even ice! The second attachment is for chopping. I use this to grind coffee beans and to cut up vegetables for cooking. One of my favourite things to chop in this is onion, no stinging eyes around here. Okay, so this isn't the cheapest of gadgets but I have had this for over a year now and use it almost daily and it has soon become a gadget I wouldn't be without.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      3.
                      <a href="http://www.lakeland.co.uk/18401/Lekue-Microwave-Omelette-Maker" target="_blank">
                       LKU Microwave Omelette maker
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-L7RpQPDyTvU/VPONXZAeHQI/AAAAAAAABC8/MAyE79MhzOs/s1600/egg.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="LKU Microwave Omelette maker" border="0" height="532" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-L7RpQPDyTvU/VPONXZAeHQI/AAAAAAAABC8/MAyE79MhzOs/s1600/egg.jpg" title="LKU Microwave Omelette maker" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <a href="http://www.lakeland.co.uk/in-the-kitchen/cookware/microwave-cookware" target="_blank">
                       Breakfast in a rush
                      </a>
                      ? No problem with this fantastic invention. Okay, so cooking eggs in the microwave has been a 'thing' forever but with this omelette maker you can make a really good omelette that isn't rubbery or flavourless. You can through in whatever ingredients you have along with a couple of eggs and seasoning and within 2 minutes you have an omelette that tastes just as good one made in a pan. The omelette maker is dishwasher safe but is non stick so really takes no time to clean. The is currently on offer at Lakeland.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      2.
                      <a href="http://www.morphyrichards.co.uk/products/cooking-and-baking/soup-makers/48822-Soup-Maker.html" target="_blank">
                       Morphy Richards Soup Maker
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ly_6GXIvxYI/VPOPlezS0xI/AAAAAAAABDI/iRyS6lKOI0A/s1600/soup%2Bmaker.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="Morphy Richards Soup Maker" border="0" height="588" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ly_6GXIvxYI/VPOPlezS0xI/AAAAAAAABDI/iRyS6lKOI0A/s1600/soup%2Bmaker.jpg" title="Morphy Richards Soup Maker" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      Nothing beats home-made soup and Morphy Richards have taken all the hard work out of it giving you fresh soup in as little as 21 minutes. You can make a serving for up to 6 people and with a smooth and chunky setting the options are endless. Check out a full
                      <a href="http://www.homelifewithheather.co.uk/2014/12/morphy-richards-soup-maker-review-soup.html" target="_blank">
                       review and recipe ideas for the Morhy Richards Soup maker here.
                      </a>
                      I have had this soup maker for 18 months now and use it 2-3 times a week. I am looking to upgrade soon to the
                      <a href="http://www.morphyrichards.co.uk/products/cooking-and-baking/soup-makers/501011-saute-and-soup-maker.html" target="_blank">
                       Saut and soup maker
                      </a>
                      as I do find that some vegetables need precooking before otherwise you end up with raw chunks of veg in your soup.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      1.
                      <a href="http://www.tefal.co.uk/Cookware-%26-Kitchenware/Pots-%26-Pans/Tefal-Cookware/Tefal-Ingenio-Enamel/p/R-tefal-ingenio-enamel" target="_blank">
                       Tefal Ingenio Enamil Pan Set
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Ao5o0RelEck/VPOSP0gqf5I/AAAAAAAABDU/XU_9hN44BH4/s1600/pan1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="Tefal Ingenio Enamil Pan Set" border="0" height="502" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Ao5o0RelEck/VPOSP0gqf5I/AAAAAAAABDU/XU_9hN44BH4/s1600/pan1.jpg" title="Tefal Ingenio Enamil Pan Set" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      And in at Number 1 is this
                      <a href="http://www.johnlewis.com/home-garden/kitchen/pots-pans/saucepan-sets-ranges/c80000898" target="_blank">
                       space saving pan set
                      </a>
                      by Tefal. Ingenio is a nesting pan set with detachable handles so not only do you save valuable kitchen space but you can also transfer these pans from hob to oven or grill. The Pans have the Tefal non stick coating that Tefal are famous for and also the wok and frying pans all have the red heat spot which indicates when the pan has reached the perfect cooking temperature. This set also comes with 3 pan lids withcollapsible handles for easy storage and 2 bakekite handles which clip on and off the pans in one click.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-sXfukBr2tHQ/VPOUtsIwYzI/AAAAAAAABDg/6WRO1JPRN70/s1600/pan2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="Tefal Ingenio Enamil Pan Set bakelite handle" border="0" height="484" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-sXfukBr2tHQ/VPOUtsIwYzI/AAAAAAAABDg/6WRO1JPRN70/s1600/pan2.jpg" title="Tefal Ingenio Enamil Pan Set bakelite handle" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      The pans are also fridge, freezer and dishwasher safe and you can buy extras such as plastic tight fit lids, a grill, pasta insert, steamer and drainer. I just love how versatile these are and although they do come with a big price tag I would say worth every single penny.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      So that concludes my top 5 kitchen gadgets. I have really enjoyed writing this post and hope you have enjoyed reading. Please do leave me a comment below letting me know what gadget you most love in your kitchen.
