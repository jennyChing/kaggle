       </p>
       <p>
        You know how much I love the
        <a href="http://www.shutterbean.com/2011/knead-pizza-dough/" target="_blank">
         no-knead pizza dough recipe
        </a>
        , right? I've been wanting to try it grilled because WHO WANTS TO PUT THEIR OVEN AT 500F in the summer??! Also, I've relied too heavily on the
        <em>
         make it on a cookie sheet and get rectangular pizza
        </em>
        method. This lady wants a round pizza. Once and for all. But then again, this lady loves the no knead recipe because it's such a good set it and forget it situation. This lady thrives on a set it &amp; forget it situation.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" height="400" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-121.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        About an hour into the dough making process (it was just sitting covered in the window) I decided to invite new friends over for dinner. WHAT?! Try a new recipe on new friends?! Blasphemous. My point to all of this is to tell you that if I can take pictures while grilling pizza AND entertaining people ON A WEEKNIGHT, then there's no doubt in my mind that you can pull this off in a snap.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" height="600" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-115-1.jpg" style="line-height: 1.5em;" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        BBBQ sauce as a base, chicken, red onions, red pepper, cilantro, PEPPER JACK CHEESE and corn combine for an amazing combination. It makes an amazing summer meal. It also solidifies new friendships....just sayin'!
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-27421">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        And we're off!
       </p>
       <p>
        The first item of business is DOUGH. It takes about 2 hours to make the no knead pizza dough.
       </p>
       <p>
        There's a
        <a href="http://www.shutterbean.com/2011/knead-pizza-dough/" target="_blank">
         step-by-step photo instruction post here.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27432" height="400" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-116.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        We divide the dough into 4 rounds. The toppings for this recipe make TWO BBQ Chicken pizzas.
       </p>
       <p>
        I made four pizzas total. One plain cheese (for the kids) A corn, tomato basil number and two bbq chicken pizzas. Four adults, three kids and about 2-3 slices leftover
        <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27431" height="400" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-117.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        I got all my ingredients together, covered them and put the toppings in the fridge and let the dough sit out covered.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27426" height="400" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-118.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        I had leftover rotisserie chicken from the night before. I shredded it up and mixed bbq sauce up in.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27433" height="400" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-115.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Are you ready to grill some pizza??!
       </p>
       <p>
        One side of your grill will be the
        <strong>
         HOT ZONE
        </strong>
        . The other side will be the
        <strong>
         WARM ZONE.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Shape a pizza into a circle. If I didn't have an audience and if I wasn't trying to snap a million pictures without being rude, I would have a better shot. But you know...
       </p>
       <p>
        The pizza will go onto the grill. Grill for about 3-4 minutes. Flip over and top you pizza with the toppings.
       </p>
       <p>
        Yes I did throw some meatballs on the grill!
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27437" height="600" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-114-1.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Move pizza to the Warm Zone and cook until cheese has melted and the edges are nice and golden.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27430" height="400" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-119.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        It takes about 5-7 minutes. I actually like mine a little charred, so I went to about 9.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27429" height="400" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-120.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Top the pizza with cilantro and cut it up.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" height="400" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-122.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Enjoy a meal OUTSIDE!!
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza // shutterbean" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27435" height="600" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bbqchickenpizza-116-1.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        And feel proud of your work. You didn't even have to turn the oven on!
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <blockquote class="recipe-block">
        <div class="title">
         <h2>
          Grilled BBQ Chicken Pizza
         </h2>
         <a class="printlink" href="http://www.shutterbean.com/2014/grilled-bbq-chicken-pizza/print" rel="nofollow" title="Print This Post">
          <img class="printbtn" src="http://www.shutterbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/printrecipe_sm.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         makes 2--10 inch pizzas
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          no knead pizza dough (use half to make 2 pizzas)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 1/2 cups barbecue sauce
         </li>
         <li>
          2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 medium red onion, thinly sliced
         </li>
         <li>
          1 1/2 cups pepper jack cheese, shredded
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
         </li>
         <li>
          2 ears corn
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 red bell pepper, diced
         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 cup feta cheese
         </li>
         <li>
          handful fresh cilantro, for garnish
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Preheat grill, making sure one section is on high and the other is at medium heat. In a medium bowl, mix chicken with 1/2 cup barbecue sauce until well combined. Set aside.
        </p>
        <p>
         Shape each pizza into a 10 inch round and place on the hot section of the grill. Cook pizzas for 3-4 minutes (dough should not stick) and flip pizza over. Move pizzas over to cooler side of the grill and lay down about 1/2 cup barbecue sauce onto each pizza, making sure to leave about a 1 inch edge for the crust. Top each pizza with half the chicken, red onion, pepper jack, mozzarella, corn, red pepper and feta. Cover grill and cook pizzas for 5-7 minutes, or until crust is browned and cheese has melted. Take pizza off of the grill, top with cilantro and serve.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
