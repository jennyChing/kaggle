          <p>
           <strong>
            WHETHER YOU
           </strong>
           call it a toast, a bruschetta or a tartine, as chef Renee Erickson does, you are essentially talking about a sandwich without a top. And isn't that about as complicated as cooking should get at this time of year?
          </p>

          <p>
           For her final Slow Food Fast contribution, Ms. Erickson tops grilled crusty bread with ricotta, luscious apricots and prosciutto fried to a crisp, and then finishes it with a drizzle of olive oil and a scattering of mint. Everything is minimally fussed-with, so there is nothing to mask the flavor of, say, a subpar apricot or an insipid ricotta.
          </p>
          <p>
           "If you can, get Jersey milk ricotta from a farm," the chef said. "Something really rich and decadent." Before spreading the cheese over the toast, let it come to room temperature. "If it's really cold, it won't taste as good," Ms. Erickson explained.
          </p>
          <p>
           When it comes to searing the apricots, do nothing more than brush them with olive oil, sprinkle them with salt and lay them on a hot grill pan. A quick fry transforms the prosciutto into savory chips. Then, pretty much all that's left is to assemble the tartine while these ingredients are warm. "Here I'm just a delivery system between farm and customer," Ms. Erickson said. "It feels a little like cheating."
          </p>

          <p>

          <h6>
           Ricotta, Apricot and Crisp Prosciutto Tartine
          </h6>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Total Time:
           </strong>
           20 minutes
           <strong>
            Serves:
           </strong>
           4
          </p>
          <h6>
           Ingredients
          </h6>
          <p>
           2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus extra for brushing and garnish
          </p>
          <p>
           4 fresh apricots, halved and pitted
          </p>
          <p>
           Sea salt
          </p>
          <p>
           Four -inch thick slices country bread
          </p>
          <p>
           4 thin slices prosciutto
          </p>
          <p>
            cup ricotta, at room temperature
          </p>
          <p>
           Freshly ground black pepper
          </p>
          <p>
           6-8 mint leaves, roughly torn
          </p>

          <h6>
           What To Do
          </h6>
          <p>
           <strong>
            1.
           </strong>
           Set a grill pan over medium heat and brush lightly with oil. Brush apricots lightly with more oil and season with salt. Once grill pan is hot, lay in apricots, cut-side down, and cook until charred on both sides and just tender, 3-5 minutes total. Remove apricots from pan and set aside. Lay bread into grill pan and cook both sides until golden and charred in spots, 3-4 minutes total.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            2.
           </strong>
           Meanwhile, swirl 2 tablespoons oil into a medium saut pan set over medium-high heat. Once hot, add prosciutto and cook until darkened in spots, less than 1 minute per side. Transfer to a paper-towel-lined plate.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            3.
           </strong>
           While toasts are warm, spread generously with ricotta. Top with apricots and prosciutto. Garnish with oil, salt, pepper and mint.
