              </b>

             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Quickly pack your mixture into molds, and quickly unmold them and place on a flat surface, such as a cookie sheet.
              </b>
              Let dry overnight, and when you take your next shower place one on the floor in front of you, with you in between it and the spray. Let the steam do most of the work, if you don't like a very hot shower you can let the spray hit it every now and then to release a burst of oil into the air. As it melts, it will run over your feet and leave them nice and soft, too.

          <h2>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Tips">
            Tips
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="section_text" id="tips">
           <ul>
            <li>
             Some people will add dry colorants, but you may prefer to stay all natural. Do you really care what color the shower floor is?
            </li>
            <li>
             For molds, try Easter eggs and then smoosh them into cookies when you unmold them. Do not let them sit in the mold, as they will either refuse to leave the mold or crack apart upon exiting. You can form them however you like, as long as the bottom is flat (unless you like chasing things around your shower!).
            </li>
            <li>
             Some people may not like to use cornstarch for fear of mold, but that may not be a problem for you. The cornstarch, epsom salts, and honey melt and run around your feet leaving them nice and soft, an added benefit!
            </li>
