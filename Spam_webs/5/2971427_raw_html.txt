        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.burgesspetcare.com/2014/06/how-to-play-with-your-cat-or-kitten/">
          How to Play with Your Cat or Kitten
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p class="date">
         Mon 23rd June, 2014
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="content">
        <p>
         Kittens begin playing with their littermates around 4 weeks of age. By 8 weeks, a lot of their play transfers to pouncing inanimate objects. Playing for kittens is essential for their physical and mental development, and its almost necessary to play with adult cats if you intend to keep your furniture intact.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          The need for felines to play
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         Kittens will play with almost anything  each other, your shoelaces, a small ball or bits of paper that didnt quite make it into the bin. However, the most important part of playing with kittens is that they are discouraged from scratching, clawing and biting at you and your familys hands and arms. These activities may not hurt you while kittens are still young, but this kind of behaviour can make them anti-social as they age.
        </p>
        <p>
         It is important to remember that scratching is a natural behaviour and shouldnt be completely discouraged. Providing your kittens a scratching post will hopefully allow them to exhibit this behaviour in more appropriate way. A catnip spray can help your kittens to learn this is the place to scratch.
        </p>
        <p>
         Adult cats may not
         <i>
          need
         </i>
         as
         <i>
          much
         </i>
         play as kittens do, but it is still important for them to have plenty of play in their lives. It is especially important for cats that live alone during the day or that do not have access to the outside world. Cats need physical and mental stimulation to remain happy and healthy.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Playing with your kittens and cats
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         Cats and kittens that live in groups with each other will not need as much of your attention as single cats will. However, you should still spend some time playing with your cats every day. Keep in mind that activities will need to be adapted for elderly cats that no longer have the mobility they once did.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Cats love toys that appeal to more than one sense. Look for toys that make noise as well as reflect light, or any other combination you can think of.
         </li>
         <li>
          Felines are naturally active in the early morning and the evening, so try to schedule your playtime together during these times for maximum benefit.
         </li>
         <li>
          All cats are attracted to new items, so its essential to rotate toys often.
         </li>
         <li>
          Think about hiding and pouncing activities when organising new toys for your pets. Theyll appreciate toys that appear to move on their own, and boxes to jump in and out of (sometimes quite senselessly).
         </li>
         <li>
          Never underestimate the power of a laser pointer to amuse both you and your cats for hours.
         </li>
         <li>
          Cats should never play with string. This can be swallowed leading to potentially serious gut blockages.
          <b>
           <br/>
          </b>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <b>
          Catnip
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         Kittens, cats and senior cats will all generally love catnip. Catnip is non-addictive, and you can rest assured that even if your cat is nibbling on the dried leaves, it will do no harm at all. In fact, there are many catnip toys available to buy. These range from all sorts o
         <a href="http://www.burgesspetcare.co.uk/pet-care/supacat.html">
          <img alt="Burgess Pet Care" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2946" height="300" src="http://www.burgesspetcare.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/15226978_s-230x300.jpg" width="230"/>
         </a>
         f toys stuffed with catnip, to balls, treats, and blankets.
        </p>
        <p>
         Catnip can also be a brilliant exercise and training tool to keep your cat healthy without them even knowing theyre having a workout. You can buy catnip in a spray form as well. By spraying the liquid onto a scratching post, youll encourage your cat to scratch and nibble the post and not your arms or furniture. Not only does this help discourage your cat from bad habits, but it also acts as an immediate reward for good behaviour
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Top tips to take away:
         </b>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Kittens need to play in order to develop physically and mentally; however,
          <i>
           how
          </i>
          they play will change as they age.
         </li>
         <li>
          Adult cats still need exercise to remain healthy, not to mention that its their favourite way to bond with humans.
         </li>
         <li>
          Rotate your cats toys often to keep things fresh as cats love new things.
         </li>
         <li>
          You can buy toys, or simple wad up bits of paper; your cats will be happy as long as toys appeal to their senses.
         </li>
         <li>
          Catnip can be used as a useful training tool.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
