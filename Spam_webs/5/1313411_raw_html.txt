        <h2 class="date-header">
         02/03/2014
        </h2>
        <div class="entry-category-diy entry-author-the_homesteady entry-type-post entry" id="entry-6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cbc39970d">
         <div class="entry-inner">
          <h3 class="entry-header">
           DIY wall lamp (Under $40!)
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cb4fb970d-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cb4fb970d image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cb4fb970d-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             This was one of the most fun parts of
             <a href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/my-blog/2014/01/the-big-reveal-our-new-bedroom.html" target="_self">
              redecorating our bedroom
             </a>
             -- mostly because it's deceptively easy. (I may or may not have started referring to myself as an electrician after wiring these lamps.)
            </p>
            <p>
             Kristie and I both love the look of industrial vintage lamps like
             <a href="http://shop.onefortythree.com/collections/lamps/products/brass-swing-lamp" target="_blank">
              this
             </a>
             and
             <a href="http://www.westelm.com/products/cord-sets-w769/?pkey=clighting-hardware-bulbs&amp;cm_src=lighting-hardware-bulbs||NoFacet-_-NoFacet-_--_-" target="_blank">
              this
             </a>
             . But the price tag made it tough, especially since we wanted two (one for each side of the bed). So after shopping around quite a bit, we realized it would probably be cheaper to just make them ourselves. We were inspired by tutorials from
             <a href="http://www.storefrontlife.com/diy-west-elm-copper-light/" target="_blank">
              Storefront Life
             </a>
             for the wall bracket and
             <a href="http://thevow.tumblr.com/post/50272511159/in-lieu-of-mothers-day-and-because-my-mom-is-the" target="_blank">
              the Vow
             </a>
             for the cord-wrapping, and I'm crazy about how they came together.
            </p>
            <p>
             So here's what we did. There are a ton of pictures because I want to make sure everything is explained as well as it possibly can be. I never would've been able to tackle the lamp-wiring part without the help of Home Depot employee Matt who broke it all down for me very patiently. I'm no Matt, but hopefully this tutorial will be helpful if you're working on a similar project.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              Kristie made the brackets from scratch, but you can order them online from
              <a href="http://www.ikea.com/us/en/catalog/products/56696109/" target="_blank">
               Ikea
              </a>
              pretty cheaply -- it was the cost of shipping, however, that sent us searching for a DIY option. I've included brief instructions for assembling our homemade bracket at the bottom of this post.
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1a879970b-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1a879970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1a879970b-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              These instructions are to make
              <strong>
               one lamp
              </strong>
              . If you want to make two, you will need to buy two lamp kits and two bulbs.
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             You will need:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Make-A-Lamp Kit, $11,
              <a href="http://www.homedepot.com/p/Westinghouse-Make-A-Lamp-Kit-7002500/100192701" target="_blank">
               Home Depot
              </a>
             </li>
             <li>
              Craft jute, $2,
              <a href="http://www.joann.com/1-2-lb-natural-jute-cord--3-ply--28lb-strength/6156616.html#prefn1=isProject&amp;q=jute&amp;pmin=0&amp;prefv1=false&amp;pmax=5&amp;start=1" target="_blank">
               JoAnn Fabrics
              </a>
             </li>
             <li>
              Multi-purpose glue
             </li>
             <li>
              Pliers/wire strippers
             </li>
             <li>
              Bag clip
             </li>
             <li>
              Vintage-style lightbulb,
              <a href="http://www.homedepot.com/p/Feit-Electric-Original-Vintage-Style-60-Watt-Incandescent-ST19-Light-Bulb-BP60ST19-RP/202245668" target="_blank">
               Home Depot
              </a>
              , $10 (not pictured)
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cb740970d-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cb740970d image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cb740970d-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             This is what comes in the Make-A-Lamp Kit. I crossed off all the things I didn't use for this project.
            </p>
            <p>
             First, make sure your cord is
             <strong>
              not plugged in
             </strong>
             . Seriously. It's worth checking.
            </p>
            <p>
             Thread the cord through the socket cap (pictured below) so that the narrow end of the cap faces the plug, and your wires are emerging from the wide end.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a51161354e970c-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a51161354e970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a51161354e970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             The wires correspond to two different screws (one silver, one gold, called "terminal screws") on the socket interior, and you can remember which goes with which by their plastic casing. The casing with the ridges will attach to the silver screw, and the casing that's smoother will attach to the gold.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116136c7970c-pi">
              <img alt="How to wire a lamp" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116136c7970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116136c7970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="How to wire a lamp"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Clip off the copper tips from the ends of both wires. I pulled them apart gently to help them separate a little more. That way I had more space to work with.
            </p>
            <p>
             Then I
             <em>
              carefully
             </em>
             removed some more of the plastic casing from each wire to expose more of the wire. This will make it easier to wrap around the screw later. (Don't despair if you accidentally snip the wire off along with the casing. Just separate the wires a little more, and start again. Make sure both wires end up the same length and exposed the same amount.)
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116136ed970c-pi">
              <img alt="How to wire a lamp" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116136ed970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116136ed970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="How to wire a lamp"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Twist the wires tighter in the direction they were already going.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cbb07970d-pi">
              <img alt="How to wire a lamp" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cbb07970d image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cbb07970d-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="How to wire a lamp"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Now is the time to make an
             <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KHRaRRWW34w" target="_blank">
              underwriter's knot
             </a>
             if you're into that kind of thing (I didn't do this, mainly because I hadn't heard of it yet, but I think skipping it is okay for this project).
            </p>
            <p>
             Next, loosen both terminal screws from the socket interior using your screwdriver.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b2b3970b-pi">
              <img alt="How to wire a lamp" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b2b3970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b2b3970b-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="How to wire a lamp"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Hook the wires around each screw (make sure they're corresponding correctly, ridges to the silver screw, smooth to gold) in a clockwise direction, and tighten with the screwdriver.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b2eb970b-pi">
              <img alt="How to wire a lamp" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b2eb970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b2eb970b-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="How to wire a lamp"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Now is a good opportunity to test and make sure you've wired everything correctly. Screw in a lightbulb, plug in the cord, and flip the switch! If everything works, turn it off, remove the lightbulb, unplug the cord, and proceed to the next step.
            </p>
            <p>
             If the light doesn't turn on, remove the lightbulb,
             <strong>
              unplug the cord
             </strong>
             , and check to make sure your wires are corresponding to the screws correctly (see above). Correct if necessary and test again.
            </p>
            <p>
             If everything works, slide the socket shell over the interior.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a511613740970c-pi">
              <img alt="How to wire a lamp" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a511613740970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a511613740970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="How to wire a lamp"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Slide the socket cap down over the interior and shell and click it into place.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a51161374e970c-pi">
              <img alt="How to wire a lamp" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a51161374e970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a51161374e970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="How to wire a lamp"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Now for the jute wrapping! This took about two episodes of "Big Love" when done all at once, but it's an easy project to pause and pick up again as needed.
            </p>
            <p>
             Tie a knot in the jute close to the socket.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b32f970b-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b32f970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b32f970b-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             I kept the rest of the cord wrapped up with its twist-tie during this process, letting it out as needed; this made the wrapping much easier and faster.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cbb7f970d-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cbb7f970d image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a73d6cbb7f970d-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Dab a tiny bit of glue and start wrapping.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b34f970b-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b34f970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b34f970b-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             I pushed the jute down every few rounds to make sure it was tight and that no cord was visible.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b366970b-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b366970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b366970b-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Every few inches, add another dab of glue and keep wrapping. If you need to take a break, use the bag clip to hold the jute in place.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b380970b-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b380970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a3fcb1b380970b-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             When you're finished, cut the jute, leaving a tail of a few inches. Tie another knot, add a dab of glue, and wrap the tail, making sure all of it is secured with the glue.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116137da970c-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116137da970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116137da970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Clip it for a little while until the glue has completely dried.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116137eb970c-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116137eb970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116137eb970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Kristie fashioned our brackets out of wood from Home Depot, which cost about $10 for enough to make two brackets. It was two inches thick, and she pieced together three pieces (one 11" and two 10") to form the bracket. She carved a notch into the end to hold the cord in place, and painted the whole thing white. We then screwed them directly into the wall.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116137ff970c-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116137ff970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a5116137ff970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Decide the height you'd like for your lamp, and fit it into the notch. Then coil the cord around, and plug it in.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a511613829970c-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a511613829970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a511613829970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             You have full permission to now yell (as we did), "Let there be light!"
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a51161383e970c-pi">
              <img alt="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a017ee3c48414970d01a51161383e970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.thehomesteady.com/.a/6a017ee3c48414970d01a51161383e970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="DIY wall lamp - Make it yourself for under $40!"/>
             </a>
            </p>
