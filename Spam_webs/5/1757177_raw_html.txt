   <p>
    <b>
     In 1923, pioneer filmmaker Cecil. B. DeMille built the largest set in movie
history for his silent (and early Technicolor) epic, The Ten Commandments. It
was called "The City of the Pharaoh."
    </b>
   </p>
   <p>
    When filming was completed, DeMille ordered that the entire edifice be
dismantled... and secretly buried.  And there it lay, forgotten, for the next
60 years.... the "lost city of Cecil B. DeMille."
   </p>
   <p>
    In 1983, a group of determined film buffs -- inspired by a cryptic clue in
DeMille's posthumously published Autobiography --  located the remains of the
set.
   </p>
