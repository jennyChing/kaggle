        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <b>
           Profile:
          </b>
          Chemmy Alcott
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Sport:
          </b>
          Alpine skier
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Fun fact:
          </b>
          Chemmy Alcott currently holds the world record for the number of beer mats flipped and caught in 30 seconds.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-596">
          </span>
          Angela Lee Duckworth, a teacher from the U.S. said in a
          <a href="http://www.ted.com/talks/angela_lee_duckworth_the_key_to_success_grit.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ted.com/talks/angela_lee_duckworth_the_key_to_success_grit.html', 'Ted Talk ']);">
           Ted Talk
          </a>
          in April 2013 that she believes the key to success is grit, and if Alpine skier Chemmy Alcott is anything to go by then it seems this is true. Alcott has broken both her legs several times, as well as her neck and ankles. In 2010 Alcott shattered her leg during a race, and had to be airlifted off the mountain. At the time many believed her career was over, but not so. She did physio everyday and competed in
          <i>
           Dancing on Ice
          </i>
          . She came back to the sport during the 2012/2013 season and secured a place on Team GB for Sochi 2014  and broke her leg again!
         </p>
         <p>
          The re-break has not stopped her Olympic dreams, it has only made her more determined as she fully plans to make it to the World Cup circuit and to Sochi.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ill be skiing by December, racing by January and then peaking for February, said Alcott. Im a bit of a veteran at the Olympics and I know how to fast-track my performance so that I will be at my best then.
         </p>
         <p>
          Rehab is going fantastically. Ive been jumping in the altitude trainer and everythings on track. Obviously the leg break was a little blip but I know how to deal with it and I know how to come back stronger.
         </p>
         <p>
          Alcott began skiing at the age of 18 months on a family ski holiday in France and began competing at the age of eight. Since then Alcott has been the number one British women skier for seven years and is the highest ranked British skier ever.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.seatoskylifestyle.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/CH-headshot.jpg" rel="lightbox[596]" title="Profile: Chemmy Alcott - Olympic Alpine Skier ">
           <img alt="CH headshot" class="size-full wp-image-601 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.seatoskylifestyle.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/CH-headshot.jpg" width="200"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          She competes in all the five disciplines of skiing: downhill, super G, giant slalom, slalom and combined. At 19 years old, Alcott competed in her first Winter Olympics  Salt Lake City 2002. She has also made the Torino 2006 and Vancouver 2010 Winter Olympic Games. Sochi will be her fourth and final Winter Olympics  where she hopes to get her hand on her first medal  though the re-breaking of her leg has cast doubts on whether she will be able to achieve a medal.
         </p>
         <p>
          It is not just through injury that Alcott has had to show determination and grit she has had to fund herself through the sport. UK Sport has stopped funding alpine skiers, which means Alcott and her colleagues have to self-fund. She found out her funding had been cut via a journalist a week after she crashed out in 2010.
         </p>
         <p>
          I felt like I was being stabbed in the back. British Ski chose to fund technical skiers even though I am their highest-ranked skier, Alcott said, in an interview with the
          <i>
           Telegraph
          </i>
          back in October 2013. She has since held a fundraiser which has so far raised 40,000 but she is still short on her target.
         </p>
         <p>
          With her leg recovering Alcotts future looks bright and even though this will be her last Olympics. As long as she is skiing fast enough then she will continue  if not she plans on going into adventuring.
         </p>
