       <h2 itemprop="name">
        'Jews Are Evil' Teacher Gets Leave
       </h2>
       <div class="Desc" itemprop="description">
        High school English teacher on leave assigning students a writing composition that sought to persuade reader that Jews are evil.
       </div>
       <div class="Author">
        <!--&lt;script&gt;View.FontSize.Write()&lt;/script&gt;-->
        <script>
         AddthisRegularBox(0,167090,1).write();
        </script>
        By Arutz Sheva staff
        <div class="clf">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="Date">
        First Publish: 4/14/2013, 7:19 PM
       </div>
      </div>
      <br/>
      <div class="Content">
       <div class="ImageOut">
        <div class="Image">
         <img alt="High school students (illustrative)" src="/static/Resizer.ashx/news/468/282/243912.jpg"/>
         <div>
          <div class="ImageDesc">
           High school students (illustrative)
          </div>
          <div class="ImageCredit">

         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <!--INFOLINKS_ON-->
       <div id="ctl00_ctl00_ctl00_Body_Body_Body_ArticleBody">
        <p>
         New York school district officials have placed a high school English teacher on leave for having assigned students a writing composition that sought to persuade the reader that Jews are evil.
        </p>
        <p>
         The composition assigned by a tenth grade teacher involved research into Nazi propaganda, and included making the assumption that the teacher was a Nazi government official. The students were then required to write a paper persuading the official that Jews are evil in order to convince the official of their loyalty to the Nazi cause.
        </p>
        <p>
         District Superintendent Marguerite Vanden Wyngaard held a news conference Friday apologizing for the assignment, which was brought to administrators' attention by a parent of one of the students.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          The Times Union
         </em>
         in Albany reported that the teacher was not in class Friday and has been placed on leave. According to the paper, one-third of the students refused to do the assignment. The school district has not named the teacher, who was described as a veteran teacher.
        </p>
