         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Options Trading Education
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          <br/>
          Options trading is a potential lucrative sideline for those willing to put in the effort. Epsilon Options is here to help you learn the skills youll need to become a better trader, whatever your current level of knowledge or experience.
         </p>
         <p>
          The options trading education is in five main sections:
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <div class="content-box-gray">
          <h2>
           How Options Work
          </h2>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/how-options-work/">
            <img alt="Businessman" class="homepageimg1" src="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/wp-content/uploads/Dollarphotoclub_62354031-e1414727291495.jpg"/>
           </a>
           Understanding
           <a href="http://epsilonoptions.com/how-options-work/">
            how options work
           </a>
           is key for anyone wanting to trade options seriously. We look at the key things you need to know including the background and history of options trading, the so-called options greeks, how to calculate an options trades profitability and some key options terms.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br style="clear: both; padding-top: 10px;"/>
           In particular we look at:
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What Are Options?
           </strong>
           : Where did they come from? How do they work? What are the common types?
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Using an Options Broker
           </strong>
           : How do you buy or sell options? How do you read an options chain?
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Options Pricing Models
           </strong>
           : How do you work out how much to pay for an option?
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Uses of an Option
           </strong>
           : What are options used for now? Focus on insurance, leverage and speculation.
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Common Options Trading Terms
           </strong>
           : Glossary of key terms you need to know to be an options trader.
          </p>
         </div>
         <hr style="clear: both;"/>
         <div class="content-box-gray">
          <h2>
           Option Greeks
          </h2>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/option-greeks/">
            <img alt="6953637770_35cde4ac6f-e1385633824583" class="homepageimg2" height="300" src="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/wp-content/uploads/6953637770_35cde4ac6f-e13856338245831.jpg" width="400"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/option-greeks/">
            Option Greeks
           </a>
           are such an important part of understanding how to trade options that they have a section all on their own.The Greeks are a collection of metrics used to measure how an options price changes given a change in factor. For example delta measures how much an options price moves should there be a change in the stock price.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br style="clear: both; padding-top: 10px;"/>
           For each greekbelow we look at exactly what its measure and, more importantly, how it can be used by a trade:
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Delta
           </strong>
           : How is options pricing affected by stock price?
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Gamma
           </strong>
           : How is delta itself affected by stock price?
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Vega
           </strong>
           How does volatility affect an options price?
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Theta
           </strong>
           How does an options price change over time?
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Rho
           </strong>
           Whats the effect of interest rates on options pricing?
          </p>
         </div>
         <hr style="clear: both;"/>
         <div class="content-box-gray">
          <h2>
           Basic Option Spreads
          </h2>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/basic-option-spreads">
            <img alt="option spreads" class="homepageimg3" height="300" src="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/wp-content/uploads/6646812063_f44dc50a98-e13872384348391.jpg" width="400"/>
           </a>
           Option Spreads are the way most options traders trade. Theyre a combination of bought and sold options which are put on in such a way that they can be used to profit whenever an underlying security behaves in a forecast way.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br style="clear: both; padding-top: 10px;"/>
           Here we look at some
           <a href="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/basic-option-spreads" title="Basic Option Spreads: Call Spreads And Put Spreads">
            basic option spreads
           </a>
           including covered calls and vertical spreads:
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Bear Spreads
           </strong>
           : Vertical spread used when you think a stock will fall
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Bull Spreads
           </strong>
           : Used when expecting a stock to rise
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Horizontal Spreads
           </strong>
           : For example the calendar spread
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Diagonal Options Spreads
           </strong>
           : A combination of a vertical and horizontal spread
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Covered Calls
           </strong>
           : A common beginner trade involving a stock and a sold call
          </p>
         </div>
         <hr style="clear: both;"/>
         <div class="content-box-gray">
          <h2>
           Advanced Option Spreads
          </h2>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/advanced-options-spreads">
            <img alt="Advanced Options Strategies" class="homepageimg4" height="300" src="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/wp-content/uploads/CoveredCall1-1.png" width="400"/>
           </a>
           Here we look at some
           <a href="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/advanced-options-spreads" title="Advanced Options Spreads: Complex Put and Call Trades">
            advanced option spreads
           </a>
           , used by sophisticated traders to profit whatever the market is doing.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br style="clear: both; padding-top: 10px;"/>
           Well look at:
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Calendar spreads
           </strong>
           : An example of a horizontal spread.
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Butterflies
           </strong>
           : Used when expecting a stock not to move.
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Iron condors
           </strong>
           : Another spread to trade when not expecting a stock to move.
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Straddles
           </strong>
           : Used when expecting a strong stock move, but not sure in which direction.
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Strangles
           </strong>
           : Similar to a straddle.
          </p>
         </div>
         <hr style="clear: both;"/>
         <div class="content-box-gray">
          <h2>
           Options Brokers
          </h2>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/best-options-broker">
            <img alt="Stockbroker for Options" class="homepageimg5" height="300" src="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/wp-content/uploads/2927031931_5e20116826-e13856326116803.jpg" width="400"/>
           </a>
           Getting the right
           <a href="http://www.epsilonoptions.com/best-options-broker" title="Whos The Best Options Broker? Reviews Of The Top Firms">
            options broker
           </a>
           isvital for any options trader. We consider what to look for  including cost, tools, education, customer service and user interface  so that you can match your knowledge and experience with the best broker for you.
          </p>
          <p>
           And then wereview some of the main brokers: which is the best?
          </p>
          <p>
           <br style="clear: both; padding-top: 10px;"/>
           We look at the following popular brokers, used by amateur traders with different levels of skill and experience:
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Interactive Brokers
           </strong>
           : Full service broker particularly good for international trading products
           <br/>
           <strong>
            OptionsXpress
           </strong>
           : Specialised broker now owned by Charles Schwabb
           <br/>
           <strong>
            OptionsHouse
           </strong>
           : Value broker offering a good no frills service
          </p>
         </div>
