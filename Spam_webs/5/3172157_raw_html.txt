         <header>
          <h1 id="page-title">
           14 special wild places with Valentine's flavor
          </h1>
         </header>
         <div id="content">
          <div class="region region-content">
           <div class="block block-system block-odd block-count-7 block-region-content" id="block-system-main">
            <div class="block-inner clearfix">
             <div class="block-content content">
              <article about="/14-special-wild-places-valentines-flavor" class="article article-type-page odd article-full clearfix" id="article-107789" role="article" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">
               <div class="article-content">
                <span class="print-link">
                </span>
                <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                 <div class="field-items">
                  <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                   <p>
                    Wilderness lovers know that protected public wildlands offer amazing opportunities for special connections with loved ones and with the majestic beauty of our world.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Many of our public lands from Americas national parks and forests and wildlife refuges and even BLM lands are wildly romantic - like Heart Rock in the San Bernardino National Forest and Cupids Cave in Yellowstone National Park.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Like lasting love,
                    <a href="http://wilderness.org/wild-places" target="_self">
                     keeping these places wild
                    </a>
                    is hard work. If you share our affection for beautiful landscapes, adventures of a lifetime, and exquisite wildlife,
                    <a href="/node/201" target="_self">
                     join us
                    </a>
                    in protecting them!
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Heres our tribute to 14 wild places that capture that special Valentines spirit:
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     1. Heart Lake at Olympic National Park, Washington
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    This heart-shaped lake is a lovely destination in the
                    <a href="http://www.nps.gov/olym/planyourvisit/high-divide-loop.htm%20" target="_blank">
                     seven lakes basin
                    </a>
                    of Olympic National Park, where visitors can take long backcountry hikes while enjoying excellent views of Mount Olympus. Hikers are more likely to see fish, however, on the
                    <a href="http://www.olympicnationalparks.com/media/13358/nps%20sol%20duc%20hiking.pdf" target="_blank">
                     Lover's Lane trail
                    </a>
                    - one of the only places where every year one can see salmon pairs laying their eggs. Visitors can find respite at the
                    <a href="http://www.nps.gov/olym/planyourvisit/campgrounds.htm%20" target="_blank">
                     Heart O' Hills
                     <span style="display: none;">
                     </span>
                     Campground
                     <span style="display: none;">
                     </span>
                    </a>
                    .
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="685" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/heartlake.OlympicNP.mnemophobe.jpg" style="width: 940px; height: 629px;" typeof="foaf:Image" width="1024"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: flickr,mnemophobe
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     2. Heart Rock, San Bernardino National Forest, California
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    An
                    <a href="http://articles.latimes.com/1999/nov/28/travel/tr-38238" target="_blank">
                     easy climb
                    </a>
                    in
                    <a href="http://www.fs.usda.gov/recarea/sbnf/recreation/hiking/recarea/?recid=26321&amp;actid=50%20" target="_blank">
                     San Bernardino National Forest
                    </a>
                    leads hikers to an overlook called Heart Rock. The name indicates that a 20-foot waterfall doesnt overshadow the better treat to be found there: a unique heart-shaped hole carved into a boulder by natures loving touch. A romantic sight for a Wilderness lover indeed!
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="480" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/HeartRcok.SanBernardinoNF.USFS_.jpg" typeof="foaf:Image" width="640"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: USFS
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     3. Great Heart of Timpanogos, Timpanogos Cave National Monument, Utah
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Timpanogos is one of three caves at this monument, named after the Ute tribe who once roamed the valleys nearby. Their
                    <a href="http://www.nps.gov/tica/historyculture/the-legend-of-timpanogos.htm" target="_blank">
                     Legend of Timpanogos
                    </a>
                    describes the tale of a warrior and his princess, whose tragic death is marked by an outline of a woman seen on Mount Timpanogos. Their broken hearts, it is told, are joined in the "Great Heart," a two-ton stalactite inside the cave.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="768" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/Great%20Heart%20of%20Timpanogos.TimpanogosCaveNM.randwill.jpg" style="width: 940px; height: 705px;" typeof="foaf:Image" width="1024"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: flickr, randwill
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     4. Cupids Cave, Yellowstone National Park, Wyoming
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    <a href="/node/706">
                     Yellowstone
                    </a>
                    s hydrothermal area known as
                    <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mammoth_Hot_Springs%20" target="_blank">
                     Mammoth Hot Springs
                    </a>
                    contains features known as Cupid's Cave and Cupid Springs. A couple miles away from Heart Lake Geyser Basin is
                    <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heart_Lake_(Wyoming)%20" target="_blank">
                     Heart Lake
                    </a>
                    , named for both its shape and a historical person. The lake is drained by the
                    <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heart_River_(Wyoming)" target="_blank">
                     Heart River
                    </a>
                    , forming a circulatory system filled with trout at the base of
                    <a href="http://www.backpacker.com/2007/printngopdfs/october2010/1010LocalHikes_PDF_MtnWest1.pdf%20" target="_blank">
                     Mount Sheridan
                    </a>
                    .
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="637" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/HeartLake.YellowstoneNP.NPS_.jpg" style="width: 940px; height: 585px;" typeof="foaf:Image" width="1024"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: wikimedia commons, NPS
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     5. Chocolate Mountains, Indian Pass Wilderness, California
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Chocolate Mountain looks like a chocolate chip ice cream sundae plopped into the Californian desert. This area is rich with Valentines treats of other varieties too: not only is Quartz Peak the highest point in this wilderness, but there have been gold mines in this mountain range since the westward rush. Summers bring beautiful desert wildflowers to the region as well. You can take in the delicious view of Inyo National Forests Chocolate Mountains in Piper Mountain, Little Picacho and
                    <a href="/node/107037">
                     John Muir Wilderness
                    </a>
                    Areas as well.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="700" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/Long%20LakeChocolate%20Mountain.InyoNF.JohmMuirW.justinjohnsen_cropped.jpg" typeof="foaf:Image" width="940"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: flickr,justinjohnsen
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     6. Beauty Mountain Wilderness, California
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    <a href="/node/551">
                     Beauty Mountain Wilderness
                    </a>
                     plateaus and deep canyons are an apt metaphor for the experiences of the heart. Like a wellspring of love, its Million Dollar Spring is named for the vitality it brings to the region. The area also hosts the lovely Quino checkerspot butterfly, one of several endangered species happy to call this place home.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="368" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/beautyMountain-California-DougSteakley_0.jpg" style="width: 700px; height: 368px;" typeof="foaf:Image" width="700"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: Doug Steakley
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     7. Hearts Content National Scenic and Recreation Areas, Allegheny National Forest, Pennsylvania
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearts_Content_Scenic_Area%20" target="_blank">
                     This National Scenic Area
                    </a>
                    has one of the few remaining northeastern old-growth forests with
                    <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_white_pine" target="_blank">
                     white pine
                    </a>
                    trees. A
                    <a href="http://www.fs.usda.gov/recarea/allegheny/recreation/recarea/?recid=6108&amp;actid=29%20" target="_blank">
                     recreation area with the same lovely name
                    </a>
                    is located just west, with trails leading to flowery
                    <a href="http://www.wilderness.net/NWPS/wildView?WID=243" target="_blank">
                     Hickory Creek Wilderness
                    </a>
                    .
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="683" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/Hearts%20Content%20Scenic%20Area.AlleghenyNF.Nicholas_T.jpg" typeof="foaf:Image" width="1024"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: flickr,Nicholas_T
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     8. Theodore Roosevelt Wilderness, North Dakota
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Did you know stuffed Teddy bears are named after the conservation president? Ironically, he was a bear hunter, but his consequent love for the outdoors led him to permanently protect many of Americas beloved wildlands. Because of his leadership, the places he loved most have since been enjoyed by wildlife and recreationists alike. Although you might not find bears in the
                    <a href="/node/107275">
                     wild area named for him
                    </a>
                    , you will find plenty of other cuddly creatures, like desert cottontails, prairie dogs and least chipmunks.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="685" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/desertcottontail.TRooseveltNP.NPS_.jpg" style="width: 940px; height: 629px;" typeof="foaf:Image" width="1024"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: NPS
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     9. Virgin Islands National Park
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    <a href="http://www.nps.gov/viis/index.htm%20" target="_blank">
                     This national park
                    </a>
                    is one of the most popular as a wedding destination despite its remote location. Historically, there once was a large sugar plantation here, and the sugar factory and mills remain to tell the story of how sweet tooths once fueled a cross-continental industry.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="683" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/VirginIslandsNP.markbyzewski.jpg" style="width: 940px; height: 627px;" typeof="foaf:Image" width="1024"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: flickr,markbyzewski
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     10. Death Valley National Park, California and Nevada
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Evidence that you can find romance in the unlikeliest of places can be found in
                    <a href="/node/537">
                     Death Valley
                    </a>
                    . The hottest place on Earth offers unexpected true beauty. Its windswept rocky peaks look like chocolate and vanilla swirled ice cream, and in winter they are frosted with elegant snow. Rainfall is very rare here, but when it does come gorgeous flowers suddenly appear.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="683" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/chocbutterscotch%20hills.DeathValleyNP.string_bass_dave.jpg" style="width: 940px; height: 627px;" typeof="foaf:Image" width="1024"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: flickr,string_bass_dave
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     11. Kissing Couple rock formation, Colorado National Monument, Colorado
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    A romantic
                    <a href="http://www.nps.gov/colm/planyourvisit/historic-rim-rock-drive.htm" target="_blank">
                     scenic drive
                    </a>
                    transports visitors into Monument Canyon. The most popular rock formations here include the Kissing Couple, which looks like two figures with touching faces.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="683" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/kissingcouple.ColoradoNM.daveynin.jpg" style="width: 940px; height: 627px;" typeof="foaf:Image" width="1024"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: flickr, daveynin
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     12. Heart of Rocks trail, Chiricahua National Monument, Arizona
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    The trail known as
                    <a href="http://www.nps.gov/chir/planyourvisit/upload/Trail%20Guide%2008.pdf">
                     <u>
                      Heart of Rocks Loop
                     </u>
                    </a>
                    has many of Chiricahuas most unusual rock formations, which form a seemingly endless wonderland in this one-of-a-kind landscape. Dreamers can also find lovely scenic drives, perfect picnic destinations and wide
                    <a href="/node/2625">
                     stargazing skies
                    </a>
                    . There may just be a stone here for
                    <a href="/node/1965">
                     every reason we adore Wilderness
                    </a>
                    .
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="680" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/ChiricahuaNM.dennyforreal.jpg" style="width: 1416px; height: 940px;" typeof="foaf:Image" width="1024"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: flickr, dennyforreal
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     13. Mr. Misty Kiss Rock Climb, Joshua Tree National Park, California
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Rock climbers consider this park to be a regional mecca. The steep surface known as Dairy Queen Wall is where a climb known as Mr. Misty Kiss leads to a panoramic view of
                    <a href="/node/537">
                     this beautiful desert region
                    </a>
                    . Some of the
                    <a href="http://peakclimbing.org/articles/JoshuaTree.asp%20" target="_blank">
                     other climbs in the park
                    </a>
                    sound like they could be messages on Valentines heart candies, such as Walk On The Wild Side, I'm so Embarrassed for You, Loose Lady and, the most scandalous, I Can't Believe It's A Girdle. This romantic park is also where
                    <a href="http://www.mydesert.com/article/20140210/NEWS0301/302100008/Rep-Raul-Ruiz-engaged-to-longtime-girlfriend-Monica-Rivers?nclick_check=1" target="_blank">
                     U.S. Rep. Raul Ruiz proposed to his longtime girlfriend last December
                    </a>
                    .
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <img alt="" class="media-image" height="700" src="http://wilderness.org/sites/default/files/rockclimb.JoshuaTreeNP.nick_.mealey.cropped.jpg" typeof="foaf:Image" width="940"/>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    <em>
                     photo credit: flickr, nick.mealey
                    </em>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <h3>
                    <strong>
                     14. Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore, Michigan
                    </strong>
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    After a day full of love for wild places, you might want to curl up with your "Teddy" bear and dream of even more heavenly places. Sleeping Bear Dunes would be an ideal inspiration. Voted in 2011 as Americas most beautiful place on Good Morning America, Sleeping Bear Dunes is known for gorgeous sunsets over towering bluffs that rise over 400 feet from the coastline. Some hearty visitors climb the whole way to reach truly romantic heights. But a real dream come true would be
                    <a href="/node/107763">
                     wilderness designation
                    </a>
                    so this lovely place remains indefinitely elegant.
                   </p>
