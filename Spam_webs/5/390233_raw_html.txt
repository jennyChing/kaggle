        Add A Little Punch To Your Portrait Photography
       </span>
      </div>
      <div id="left-area">
       <article class="entry-content clearfix">
        <p class="post-meta">
         Posted By
         <a href="http://resourcemagonline.com/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by Resource Magazine">
          Resource Magazine
         </a>
         on Jun 4, 2014 |
         <span class="comments-number">
          <a href="http://resourcemagonline.com/2014/06/add-punch-portrait-photography/39477/#respond">
           <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="39477 http://resourcemagonline.com/?p=39477">
            0 comments
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its amazing what you can accomplish with an idea, a few talented people and some incredible tools.I wanted to create a vintage-inspired image of a boxer and was able to enlist the phenomenal talents of make-up artist
         <a href="http://www.jenbrownmakeup.com">
          Jen Brown
         </a>
         and my mustachioed friend Stephe. In this quick tutorial Ill show you add a little punch to your portrait photography.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_39483" style="width: 630px">
         <a href="http://rossfloyd.com/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="phase-one, ross-floyd, jen-brown, tips, tricks, techniques, portrait-photography" class="size-full wp-image-39483" height="413" src="http://resourcemagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/RossFloyd-final-620.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Ross Floyd
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         For this shoot, I was working with a Phase One
         <a href="http://www.phaseone.com/en/Camera-Systems/P-Series.aspx?utm_source=Rmag&amp;utm_medium=Blog&amp;utm_campaign=SW-June14">
          P30+
         </a>
         digital back on the Phase One
         <a href="http://www.phaseone.com/en/Camera-Systems/645DFplus.aspx?utm_source=Rmag&amp;utm_medium=Blog&amp;utm_campaign=SW-June14">
          645DF+
         </a>
         , and we built a small corner of a boxing ring in my studio and lit it with three Profoto acute heads. The final image was adjusted and processed with
         <a href="http://www.phaseone.com/en/Imaging-Software/Capture-One-Pro-7.aspx?utm_source=Rmag&amp;utm_medium=Blog&amp;utm_campaign=SW-June14">
          Capture One Pro 7
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h2>
         <b>
         </b>
         <b>
          Creative use of adjustment layers
         </b>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <b>
         </b>
         Immediately, I could tell that there were things about the image that I wanted to emphasize and some distracting elements I wanted to tone down. Most of those adjustments were made with Capture Ones Local Adjustment tool.
        </p>
        <p>
         First, I brought down the exposure in the bottom of the image with a separate layer to tone down the bright red color of the shorts and then, I used the Brush Mask tool to draw a mask over the ropes of the ring as they were a bit too bright and distracting.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_39478" style="width: 630px">
         <a href="http://rossfloyd.com/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="phase-one, ross-floyd, jen-brown, tips, tricks, techniques, portrait-photography" class="size-full wp-image-39478" height="397" src="http://resourcemagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/3-ropes-mask_620.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Ross Floyd
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         I prefer making these kinds of adjustments directly in Capture One Pro 7, as it allows me to take full advantage of the wide dynamic range offered by the RAW files.
        </p>
        <p>
         I also wanted to emphasis the fighters face as his head were darker than his torso. To do this, I drew another Gradient Mask in the top of the image and brought the exposure up. However, this made a big part of the image too bright, so I used the Erase Mask Tool to remove parts of the mask and maintain the dramatic effect while drawing more attention to the face.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_39480" style="width: 630px">
         <a href="http://rossfloyd.com/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="phase-one, ross-floyd, jen-brown, tips, tricks, techniques, portrait-photography" class="size-full wp-image-39480" height="342" src="http://resourcemagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/mik_6-face-head-mask_620.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Ross Floyd
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         I also wanted to bring up the Clarity to emphasize the tremendous amount of detail in the image, so I made another layer and painted a mask over the top two thirds of the image, leaving the ropes and his torso unaffected by the added Clarity.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_39479" style="width: 630px">
         <a href="http://rossfloyd.com/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="phase-one, ross-floyd, jen-brown, tips, tricks, techniques, portrait-photography" class="size-full wp-image-39479" height="342" src="http://resourcemagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/9-Clarity_620.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Ross Floyd
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         By this point, I was pretty satisfied with the image but I wanted to add a final touch to the coloring in order to manipulate the mood and emphasize the fantastic make-up. Therefore, I went to the Styles and Presets tool and applied the Style Color  Cross Blue to the entire image. Lastly, I reduced the saturation a bit to keep the skin tones looking natural while the reds of the cuts really stood out and the bruises looked darker and deeper.
        </p>
        <p>
         With a little wood, a few lengths of rope and some fake blood, excellent colleagues and Capture One, I was able to realize my vision for this image.
        </p>
        <p>
         I hope you have found the techniques I have shared useful to you in your own work. Wed love for you to share samples of your work using these techniques.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_39481" style="width: 630px">
         <a href="http://rossfloyd.com/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="phase-one, ross-floyd, jen-brown, tips, tricks, techniques, portrait-photography" class="size-full wp-image-39481" height="232" src="http://resourcemagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/RossFloyd-before-after-620.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Ross Floyd
         </p>
        </div>
