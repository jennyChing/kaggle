       </strong>
       <br/>
       This modern take on the French twist uses a French braid along the bottom half of the hair to help anchor the rest of the style. Start by dividing hair in half horizontally in the back, put up the top half of hair, then make your braid along the bottom, starting from either ear and working your way toward the center. Let the top half down, part it on the side, and start rolling the sides up, pinning them as you go. Add as many pins as you need to make it feel secure, then spray a fine mist of hairspray to hold the style in place.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="wp-image-41201 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://cl.wantable.com/image/upload/c_scale,w_300/v1412910955/blog/celine/refinery29-side-view.jpg" width="300"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       <strong>
        Style:
        <a href="http://www.refinery29.com/41217#slide-12" onclick="ga('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.refinery29.com/41217#slide-12', 'Curly Side Chignon via Refinery 29');">
         Curly Side Chignon via Refinery 29
        </a>
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        Main Skill: Curling Hair with an Iron
       </strong>
       <br/>
       Its easier than it looks, which is the first reason we love this look. The first step is making a deep part on the right side, then start curling hair all over the head. Use hairspray to hold the curls, especially if your hair is fine or naturally straight. Once all the hair is curled, sweep hair from right to left, pinning it as necessary. If possible, make a ponytail below the left ear, then twist curls into a loose chignon and pin until they feel secure.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="wp-image-41221 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://cl.wantable.com/image/upload/c_scale,w_300/v1412911696/blog/celine/french-knot.jpg" width="300"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       <strong>
        Style:
        <a href="http://www.brit.co/fancy-hairdos-for-short-hair/" onclick="ga('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.brit.co/fancy-hairdos-for-short-hair/', 'Half-Up French Knot via Brit + Co.');" target="_blank">
         Half-Up French Knot via Brit + Co.
        </a>
       </strong>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        Main Skill: Bobby Pinning
       </strong>
       <br/>
       Prep hair by straightening hair if theyre naturally curly, then part on one side. Start by picking up two strands like you would if you were going to make a braid. Then tie them into a loose knot, and pin the middle, leaving the two ends loose. Keep tying the two strands and pinning the area where they intersect, picking up another piece of hair when the ends get too short to tie. Once you reach the back, pin the ends to the crown of your head, and repeat on the other side. Youll be ready to take the look out on the town  or at least to the office  in no time.
      </p>
