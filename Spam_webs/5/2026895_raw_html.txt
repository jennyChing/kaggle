          <p>
           Whether you run a Fortune 500 company or own a small business, maintaining your bottom line and increasing your revenue are essential to your success? and every penny counts. Here are five simple tips to help you find the places where your company can save the most. From learning to buy in bulk to renting office equipment, just a few steps in the right direction will help you cut costs and save cash.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Tip #1: Rent Office Equipment When Possible
          </h2>
          <p>
           Office equipment is expensive... if it breaks, it is even more expensive to fix or replace. Renting office equipment  particularly high-ticket items such as copiers, printers, and scanners  is an all-inclusive solution because it allows your company the luxury of using high-quality equipment without the stress of high upfront cost and upkeep. Plus, most rental equipment comes with free (or inexpensive) maintenance thats usually just a phone call away.
           <br/>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Tip #2: Go Paperless
          </h2>
          <p>
           Decreasing paper usage is an obvious choice for environmentally conscious businesses and can also free up some of your employees time by eliminating the need for sorting, filing, and organizing documents. After all, time is money, right? Use a scanner to input, sort, and save your files online  this allows for quick and easy access for all employees, and saves space that would normally be used by large filing cabinets. If youre worried about losing files, simply save a backup on another device.
          </p>
          <p>
           Did you know that large corporations typically spend anywhere from 70 cents to one dollar to print, organize, and send each bill and statement to their customers (Rapid Onboarding of Brands, 2014)? It doesnt take an accountant to discover how quickly that can add up to a big expense. If your company currently mails client statements the old-fashioned way, consider converting to an electronic system. While it might cost a little to set up the system initially, you will soon see the savings in each piece of paper you
           <em>
            dont
           </em>
           print!
          </p>
          <h2>
           Tip #3: Buy in Bulk
          </h2>
          <p>
           Think strategically about your office and the necessary supplies your employees use most often. If each employee uses the same supplies  notebooks, pens, staplers, etc.  buy them in bulk. Having extra supplies on hand can also save someone a trip to the store every time the printer runs out of paper.
          </p>
          <p>
           All offices arent created equal  different businesses need different everyday tools. Florists, wedding planners, event organizers, office managers, and more can find great deals on products in bulk, which will help lower costs. Here are a few suggested items that nearly any business could benefit from buying in bulk:
          </p>
          <ul style="list-style-type:disc; padding-left:14px; margin-bottom:10px;">
           <li>
            Writing Supplies
           </li>
           <li>
            Stationery/Card Stock
           </li>
           <li>
            Glassware
           </li>
           <li>
            Notebooks
           </li>
           <li>
            Kitchen/Breakroom Supplies
           </li>
           <li>
            Staplers
           </li>
           <li>
            Baskets
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           Tip #4: Buy House Brands
          </h2>
          <p>
           Purchasing house or off-brand supplies can save a lot of money in comparison to buying only brand-name products. Unless you have picky employees or clients, using off-brand notebooks and pens will probably go unnoticed. Luckily, many off-brand supplies are also available in bulk, so you can save year-round. When it comes to buying generic or store brand products, there may be some hesitation to the quality of the product. However, some manufacturers will develop the brand name and the off-brand-name item within the same factory, which means the level of quality is either the same or very similar.
          </p>
