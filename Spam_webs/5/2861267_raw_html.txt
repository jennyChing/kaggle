        <div class="story">
         <b class="dateline">
          LAKELAND --
         </b>
         <span itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           A Polk County girl formerly accused of cyberbullying another teen who committed suicide spoke out on national television on Wednesday.
          </p>
          <p>
           After all charges were dropped against Kaitlyn Roman, she decided to come out and tell her story.
          </p>
          <p>
           Roman and a 14-year-old girl were accused of cyberbulling and 'tormenting' 12-year-old Rebecca Sedwick. Polk County deputies said those harsh words pushed Sedwick to her death in September.
          </p>
          <p>
           But Roman and her attorney, Jose Baez, said the posts werent criminal.
          </p>
          <p>
           I mean yes, theres some things I couldve changed but I didnt really do anything wrong, Roman said.
          </p>
          <p>
           This child here did nothing wrong, did no criminal acts and I certainly believe theres a place in the justice system for bullying but this was not the case, said Baez.
          </p>
          <p>
           Baez said the state agreed with his defense and dropped all the charges against his client and the other suspect. Roman said shes attempting to make something positive out of the tragedy.
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p>
           All this that has happened, I want to make all of this negative stuff a positive," said Roman. "I want to move on from this and become a better person.
          </p>
          <p>
           The attorneys for the young girls said theyre getting the help and counseling they need.
          </p>
          <p>
           Rebecca Sedwicks mother is set to hold a press conference on Monday with her response to the charges being dropped. Her attorney said they are planning to go after the parents of the two former suspects in this case and several others.
          </p>
