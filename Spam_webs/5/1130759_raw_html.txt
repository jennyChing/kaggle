        </h1>

       <section>
        <p>
         If you know a little bit about international cinema, you're aware that the biggest producer of films in the world isn't Hollywood -- it's Bollywood (Hollywood
         <a href="http://www.un.org/apps/news/story.asp?NewsID=30707#.U6jC_PldXD8" target="c">
          isn't even number two
         </a>
         ). And if you know
         <i>
          more
         </i>
         than a little bit about international cinema, then you're already writing a comment about how the previous statement is inaccurate, because the name "Bollywood" doesn't refer to all of the Indian film industry (just like "Hollywood" doesn't cover Christian films, or pornos, or
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2520338/" target="c">
          Christian pornos
         </a>
         ). To that I say: You're right, and also eat a dick.
        </p>
        <p>
         However, Bollywood's impressive output of about
         <a href="http://www.ibtimes.com/bollywood-100-how-big-indias-mammoth-film-industry-1236299" target="c">
          1,000 movies per year
         </a>
         is a little less impressive when you consider that like 999 of those are apparently thinly disguised ripoffs of U.S. movies, from classics like
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manorama_Six_Feet_Under" target="c">
          <i>
           Chinatown
          </i>
         </a>
         to not-classics like
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God_Tussi_Great_Ho" target="c">
          <i>
           Bruce Almighty
          </i>
         </a>
         . Are Indian moviegoers, as we say in my country, getting "
         <a href="https://translate.google.com/translate?sl=es&amp;tl=en&amp;js=y&amp;prev=_t&amp;hl=en&amp;ie=UTF-8&amp;u=http%3A%2F%2Fdiccionariochileno.cl%2Fterm%2Fmeter%2Bel%2Bpico%2Ben%2Bel%2Bojo&amp;edit-text=" target="c">
          dicked in the eye
         </a>
         "? No. No, they are not, because what Indian movies lack in originality they more than make up for in
         <i>
          complete and utter insanity
         </i>
         . For example, check out the baffling ways they decided to retell famous movies like ...
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #5.
         <i>
          The Silence of the Lambs
         </i>
         Is a Bizarre Romantic Movie
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/8/0/289580_v1.jpg" height="355" width="500"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 500px; text-align: right;">
          Vishesh Films, Orion Pictures
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          What They Stole From Hollywood:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <i>
          Sangharsh
         </i>
         (1999) is about a young female FBI -- I'm sorry,
         <i>
          CBI
         </i>
         agent who has to track down a crazy dress-wearing serial killer with the help of an incarcerated genius. Yep, it's
         <i>
          The Silence of the Lambs
         </i>
         (1991) with Indian actors.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/7/8/289578_v1.jpg" height="371" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Vishesh Films, Orion Pictures
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          So he's more into dark meat, I guess?
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          What They Made Up:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Actually, unlike Hannibal Lecter, the genius behind bars in the Indian version isn't a cannibal, probably because the Hindi language doesn't have any names that rhyme with the word for "someone who eats people." Also, he was unjustly incarcerated. Oh, and one more little detail: While the agent is creeped out by him
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2TGLvBtViUQ" target="c">
          when they meet
         </a>
         and he's
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z4jaVedL2-A" target="c">
          kind of a jerk to her
         </a>
         at first, slowly they start falling in love. At one point "Hannibal Lecter"
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-NQV4vIdkrc" target="c">
          fantasizes about running in the snow
         </a>
         to meet "Agent Starling" and smelling her arm.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/8/4/289584_v1.gif" height="177" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Vishesh Films
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Nope, this looks nothing like a cannibal.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         The agent also dreams about meeting "Lecter" in a dark, romantic parking lot and making out with him -- all of this while her boyfriend
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=txEPjAe8Vh0" target="c">
          sings to her through the phone
         </a>
         (or at least I think it's her boyfriend; maybe it's the Dr. Chilton character?).
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          The Craziest Part:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         The following clip is NSFW due to undiluted batshit insanity (and loud screaming):
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/bgt9n33p824" width="420">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         That's right, this goes from thriller to romantic movie and then from romantic movie to some sort of Jodorowsky-esque nightmare. It turns out that the serial killer they were looking for planned to sacrifice a kid on a
         <i>
          Temple of Doom
         </i>
         -style altar in order to be granted immortal powers from an eclipse. The good guys stop him (by impaling him through the gut), but "Lecter" gets hurt and dies in the agent's arms after a tender kiss.
        </p>
        <p>
         The movie ends with the agent getting a medal for her bravery and thinking about "Lecter." How do I know she's thinking of him, you ask?
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/8/2/289582_v1.gif" height="154" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Vishesh Films
         </font>
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/8/6/289586_v1.gif" height="154" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Vishesh Films
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         What the fuck, you fucking movie.
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #4.
         <i>
          Leon: The Professional
         </i>
         Is a Musical With Explosions
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/8/8/289588_v1.jpg" height="328" width="500"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 500px; text-align: right;">
          Video Sound Inc., Columbia Pictures
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          What They Stole From Hollywood:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <i>
          Bichhoo
         </i>
         (2000) follows a cold and ruthless hit man who ends up taking care of his neighbors' daughter after her entire family gets whacked by corrupt officers. Like 70 percent of the movie is lifted straight from
         <i>
          Leon: The Professional
         </i>
         (1994) ... but without the pedo vibes, because the "little girl" is actually 22 in this version.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/9/0/289590_v1.jpg" height="312" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Video Sound Inc., Columbia Pictures
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Also, it looks like the part of Jean-Pierre the Plant has been recast as a can of coffee.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          What They Made Up:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         The other 30 percent? All songs and dances. I know I shouldn't be surprised that this is a musical, because every Indian movie is a musical, but it's still jarring to see this badass Terminator of human flesh move like this:
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/9/8/289598_v1.gif" height="193" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Video Sound Inc.
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          "I got chiiiiiiiiiills ..."
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         To be honest, my 70/30 estimate is probably way off, because the first half hour of the movie is about how the hit man met his wife, and of course it was during
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OXznKwhI7_c" target="c">
          an elaborate dance number in the street
         </a>
         . Then she kills herself and he kills her dad for making her do it, and that's how come he's so cold now, you see.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          The Craziest Part:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         The best part of the original movie is
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1YXCUodkSmM" target="c">
          the shootout at the end
         </a>
         when the corrupt cops storm the building and Leon fights them off in classic action movie style. He even gets to jump from an explosion! India looked at that and said, "How do we make this crazier? I know: Let's turn him into Superman, with no explanation."
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/9/6/289596_v1.gif" height="178" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Video Sound Inc.
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Does this mean the girl is
          <a href="http://www.cracked.com/article_16704_9-foreign-rip-offs-cooler-than-hollywood-originals.html" target="c">
           Spiderwoman
          </a>
          ?
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         I mean, that guy is flying. He just started flying two hours into this movie. Then he actually catches fire, but he just sorta wiggles his arms on the floor until it turns itself off. Also, the real Leon kills, what, like three guys during the five-minute shootout? In the Indian version,
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Wp0-1Yp_ypA" target="c">
          the scene lasts twice as long
         </a>
         and is exactly six times more brutal -- Indian Leon and Indian Natalie Portman have a kill count of 18. I'm pretty sure more cops died than came into the building.
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #3.
         <i>
          Talladega Nights
         </i>
         Has Drama, Dancing Cartoon Bears
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/9/2/289592_v1.jpg" height="335" width="500"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 500px; text-align: right;">
          Yash Raj Films, Columbia Pictures
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          What They Stole From Hollywood:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <i>
          Ta Ra Rum Pum
         </i>
         (2007), like the Will Ferrell "vehicle"
         <i>
          Talladega Nights: The Ballad of Ricky Bobby
         </i>
         (2006), is about a famous race car driver with a hot wife, two ridiculously named kids, and a big mansion who loses everything when he's involved in a big crash that traumatizes him. Then he gets better, the end.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/9/4/289594_v1.jpg" height="175" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Yash Raj Films
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          This is both a still from the movie and a visual representation of my "vehicle" pun above.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         The movie was even shot in the U.S., because there are no cars in India (they have jet packs).
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          What They Made Up:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Despite having the same fame-disgrace-fame structure as every Will Ferrell movie, this is not a wacky, largely improvised comedy. In fact, some pretty dramatic shit happens: The protagonist's kids, Champ and Princess, decide to help out with the family's finances by not eating, but Champ (ironically) is weak and starts
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dx_d24_r_yc" target="c">
          secretly eating from the garbage
         </a>
         , ending up with a piece of glass lodged in his stomach, which obviously requires an expensive operation they can't afford.
        </p>
        <p>
         But before all that:
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgFsAX2qulY" target="c">
          dancing CGI bears
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/1/0/289610_v1.gif" height="193" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Yash Raj Films
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          They are all voiced by John C. Reilly.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         At one point, the family breaks into a Disney store at night, and apparently the only security measure in there was a potent nerve gas, because soon they're all dancing with bears. Wait, how is this not the craziest part of the movie?
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          The Craziest Part:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Because "Ricky Bobby" straight up kills another driver. Like in
         <i>
          Talladega Nights
         </i>
         , in the last race the protagonist has to conquer his fears by going up against the same asshole who caused him to crash in the first place, but in this version, "conquering his fears" involves
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jaz2pgpbtsE#t=5m" target="c">
          intentionally ramming the guy against a wall
         </a>
         until his car flips over. Several times. The vehicle peacefully lands in the middle of the track ... and then another car comes out of nowhere and fucking disintegrates it in a huge, unsurvivable explosion.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/0/0/289600_v1.jpg" height="156" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          Yash Raj Films
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=onE8utryxE8" target="c">
           This
          </a>
          seems appropriate.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         So, to recap, this is a dramatic remake of a Will Ferrell movie with dancing bears ... and murder. Holy shit, India.
        </p>

        </p>
