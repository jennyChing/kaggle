        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Find an Ontario Maple Syrup Festival near Toronto in 2015
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-date">
          <a href="http://dehaanservices.ca/?p=4367" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-02-26T22:23:24+00:00">
            February 26, 2015
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://dehaanservices.ca/?author=2" rel="author">
            Mike DeHaan
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Ontario Maple Syrup festivals are an annual tradition, celebrating a natural sweetener and a great Canadian flavour. Where can you find a maple syrup festival near Toronto in the early spring of 2015?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         (
         <em>
          <strong>
           Updated
          </strong>
         </em>
         March 9, 2015). Toronto weather has begun to cooperate with maple syrup production! Daytime high temperatures are above freezing, while the nights plunge back below freezing. Weather forecasts predict this trend will continue for a while, so the sap should be running at these southern Ontario maple syrup festivals. (And now back to the original article).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         First, just when are they held? And just how do we get maple syrup?
        </p>
        <div class="mceTemp" style="text-align: justify;">
         <dl class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_4369" style="width: 490px;">
          <dt class="wp-caption-dt">
           <a href="http://dehaanservices.ca/?attachment_id=4369" rel="attachment wp-att-4369">
            <img alt='"Collecting Maple Sap for Maple Syrup" image by Bobolink (Robert Taylo) under CC license' class="size-full wp-image-4369" height="640" src="http://dehaanservices.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/C04_Maple_Sap.jpg" title='"Collecting Maple Sap for Maple Syrup" image by Bobolink (Robert Taylo) under CC license' width="480"/>
           </a>
          </dt>
          <dd class="wp-caption-dd">
           Collecting Maple Sap for Maple Syrup image by Bobolink (Robert Taylo) under CC license
          </dd>
         </dl>
        </div>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         Early Spring Temperatures Make the Sap Run for Maple Syrup
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Deciduous trees store sap, a watery sugar solution, in their roots for the winter. The sap had been drained away from the leaves in the autumn. The trees then prepare to bud their spring leaves by pumping the sap back up in early spring.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         A specific type of tree, the Sugar Maple, has the sweetest sap, with up to 2% sugar. (Some other varieties can be tapped, but you have to boil off more water to get similar syrup). By hammering a small tap, or metal tube, into the tree as winter begins changing to spring, some sap can be drained from the sugar maple tree. Collect the sap, reduce it by boiling off most of the water, and youve got maple syrup. Evaporate even more water to create maple sugar.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Reducing the water content concentrates both the sugar and the unique maple flavour.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         See 
         <a href="http://biology.clc.uc.edu/fankhauser/Buds_and_Bark/tapping_sugar_maple_index.html" target="_blank" title='Link to "Making Maple Syrup"'>
          Making Maple Syrup
         </a>
          for more details.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         Timing the Traditional Maple Syrup Festival
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Farmers depend on weather conditions for crops to ripen; the they harvest the bounty when the plants are ready. In the same way, the person working a sugar bush only taps the trees when the sap is running. The ideal conditions require several days with the high temperatures above freezing, probably 5C (41F) or more, but below-freezing temperatures at night. The sap might run for a few weeks, but only if the temperatures fluctuate properly.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Modern Ontario maple syrup festivals require forward planning to coordinate vendors with civic infrastructure. This can challenge the people harvesting the sap.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         The Elmira Maple Syrup Festival
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Lets start with perhaps the best-known Ontario maple syrup festival, the Elmira syrup festival. Elmira is a small town in southern Ontario, west of Toronto, Mississauga, and Guelph; and north of Kitchener-Waterloo. Frugal shoppers should find bargains for locally prepared maple syrup in Elmira (even if it does not cut your grocery bill in half overall). They also sell a variety of other foods to keep you happy for the day.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The 2015
         <a href="http://www.elmiramaplesyrup.com/" target="_blank" title="Link to Elmira maple syrup festival home page">
          Elmira maple syrup festival
         </a>
         will be held on March 28, from 7am-4pm. Check the map in that site for detailed directions once youre in Elmira.
        </p>
        <div class="mceTemp" style="text-align: justify;">
         <dl class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_4370" style="width: 1034px;">
          <dt class="wp-caption-dt">
           <a href="http://dehaanservices.ca/?attachment_id=4370" rel="attachment wp-att-4370">

           </a>
          </dt>
          <dd class="wp-caption-dd">

          </dd>

        </div>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         Three Maple Syrup Festivals near Toronto
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         One
         <a href="http://www.maplesyrupfest.com/" target="_blank" title="Link to Sugarbush Maple Syrup Festival web site">
          web site
         </a>
         covers three festivals near Toronto: at Bruces Mills; the Kortwright Centre; and the Terra Cotta Centre. Admission is under $10/person, plus HST; they recommend buying tickets online. Each site offers different programs on their own schedules; but they all serve pancakes and syrup for breakfast (at an extra cost).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The
         <strong>
          Bruces Mills Conservation Centre
         </strong>
         s maple sugar festival runs from Feb. 28 until April 6, 2015 but only Wed.-Sundays. The maple syrup demonstrations are held on weekends and during March Break, from 10am  4:30pm; but Id phone ahead (416-661-6600) to make reservations and be sure of just whats on tap that day.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Find Bruces Mills at 3291 Stouffville Road in Stouffville, just north of Toronto. Id take Hwy 404 north, then east on road 14 (which is Stouffville Road), past Warden Ave.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The
         <strong>
          Kortwright Centre
         </strong>
         also hosts its festival from Feb. 28 through April 6, and should be open every day. Again, phone ahead (905-832-2289) to be sure of what they offer on any given day. Find 9550 Pine Valley Drive between Major Mackenzie Drive and Rutherford Road, and well west of highway 27 and Islington Ave. but east of Weston Road.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The
         <strong>
          Terra Cotta Conservation Area
         </strong>
         s festival is only open for March weekends, plus the week of March Break (March 16-20), plus April 3-5. Theyre pretty quiet about any special programs or activities, so if the location suits you, be sure to call 1 (800) 367-0890 for their details. The Terra Cotta Conservation Area is at 14452 Winston Churchill Blvd. in Halton Hills. I might start from Acton, take Main St. (Hwy 25) NW to the Halton-Erin Road (45), turn NE and then go SE on route Winston Churchill Blvd (19).
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         Venture Further Afield to Bronte Creek for Maple Syrup Month
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.ontarioparks.com/park/brontecreek" target="_blank" title="Link to Bronte Creek Provincial Park">
          Bronte Creek Provincial Parks web page
         </a>
         has several sections, including the map. They dont say much about why March is Maple Syrup Month, but it is open every day except Christmas.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Bronte Creek Park is south-west of Oakville; Id take Hwy 25 north from the QEW/Hwy 403.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #993300;">
          Buying Maple Syrup and Maple Syrup Recipes Online
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         If you missed out on buying Elmira maple syrup, order
         <a href="http://www.amazon.ca/Pure-Maple-Syrup-Certified-Organic/dp/B00BMCW71U/" target="_blank" title="Link to certified organic maple syrup from Quebec via Amazon">
          certified organic maple syrup
         </a>
         from Quebec via Amazon Canada. This maple syrup product is
         <em>
          <strong>
           amber
          </strong>
         </em>
         grade, between the light and dark shades of maple syrup.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Who wants maple syrup recipes so you can go beyond flapjacks and syrup? Amazon Canada offers 
         <a href="http://www.amazon.ca/Maple-Syrup-Cookbook-Delicious-SuperFoods-ebook/dp/B00NDEXJK8/" target="_blank" title="Link to The Maple Syrup Cookbook 2: 40 More Easy, Delicious &amp; Healthy Maple Syrup Recipes for Breakfast Lunch &amp; Dinner (Maple SuperFoods) [Kindle Edition]">
          The Maple Syrup Cookbook 2: 40 More Easy, Delicious &amp; Healthy Maple Syrup Recipes for Breakfast Lunch &amp; Dinner (Maple SuperFoods) [Kindle Edition]
         </a>
         . The one very happy reviewer said, Anyone who loves this syrup needs to have a copy of this book.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #993300;">
          Looking for More Annual Events or a Holiday in Toronto Canada?
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         My blog tracks many of the annual Toronto events that I find interesting, free or frugal.
