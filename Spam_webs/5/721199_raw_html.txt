         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">

         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p annotation="note">
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p annotation="note">
           Ronald Stans life was starting to burn down around him, so when the big century-old barn full of pigs caught fire, he saw a chance to escape.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Hed been cheating on his wife  regularly  and in his mind his marriage was about to fall apart.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Amid the roar of the flames and squeal of the pigs, the 32-year-old father of two had a thought: Better to just leave. Sure, I have some debt but shell get the life insurance money.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           It was 1977 when Stan bolted into the forest to begin a new life. Now, 37 years, three more wives and another child later, Stans world would turn to ash again.
           <a href="http://on.thestar.com/1qK0RD8">
            The Ontario Provincial Police discovered he was living in Oklahoma
           </a>
           as Jeff Walton, a 69-year-old plumber with a wife and 35-year-old son. His family there had no knowledge of the barn fire, his disappearance, and the Canadian wife, toddler and newborn child left behind.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           For those 37 years he lived a clumsily constructed lie, stumbling and crawling through the boozy corners of New Orleans and leveraging his charm with women to forge and temper his new identity. His first American wife, Rose Ann Walton, says he could charm a snake.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           He is a man consumed by remorse but aware of the self-wrought nature of his plight. His most recent wife, Debra Proctor, unceremoniously divorced him, taking everything after she uncovered the lie beneath Jeff Walton.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Gail Stan, Rose Ann Walton, Holly Kelley and Debra Proctor. These are the women who Walton married. He cheated on all of them, with the exception of Proctor, hes careful to note.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           At 69 and after suffering a stroke, his roguish charms remain apparent. His smile engulfs his face, folding down his brow sitting at the kitchen table of his American son, Jeff Walton Jr. in a small parish outside New Orleans, recounting the women whose lives hes trod upon.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Legally, he died in that barn fire. Ronald Stan would be no more. In New Orleans he would be reborn as Jeff Walton, baptized in the rivers of booze and debauchery that flow in the French Quarter.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
          </p>
          <hr class="stars"/>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="parbase section genericimage">
          <div class="generic-image force-original" id="cq-image-jsp-/content/thestar/news/gta/2014/09/14/inside_the_secret_life_of_ronald_stan_lust_lies_and_a_new_identity/jcr:content/body/genericimage">
           <img alt="" src="http://www.thestar.com/content/dam/thestar/uploads/2014/9/14/1410683462724.jpg"/>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           His first wife in his new life was Rose Ann Cardona  a New Orleans woman in her mid-thirties from a reasonably well-to-do family.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Before he fled Canada he sold one of his cars and pocketed the cash  about $3,000 that he could use to start his life in the United States.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           I stayed in the French Quarter a little while then I realized in all actuality I had to do something to get me legal, said Walton. So I thought well the first thing I do is I gotta find me a local girl with a little bit of money. Didnt have to have a lot, just a little. And I did.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           By 1978, he married Rose Ann, who took his last name and kept it to this day.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="parbase section genericimage">
          <div class="generic-image force-original" id="cq-image-jsp-/content/thestar/news/gta/2014/09/14/inside_the_secret_life_of_ronald_stan_lust_lies_and_a_new_identity/jcr:content/body/genericimage_0">
           <img alt="No. 2 wife Rose Ann Walton with Jeff around 1979. He'd have been about 34. How long can someone carry on a double life? The couple remained married about 15 years. Jeff's assumed surname, Walton, remains her real surname." src="http://www.thestar.com/content/dam/thestar/uploads/2014/9/15/1410796012619.jpg"/>
           <div class="caption">
            <p class="credit">
             ROSE ANN WALTON PHOTO
            </p>
            <p class="description">
             No. 2 wife Rose Ann Walton with Jeff around 1979. He'd have been about 34. How long can someone carry on a double life? The couple remained married about 15 years. Jeff's assumed surname, Walton, remains her real surname.
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Now she lives in a quiet corner of Baton Rouge, La., tucked into the bayous. She keeps the photos of her and Jeff stuffed in a box in the closet, their wedding book sealed away where it wont remind her of their troubled life together.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           He introduced himself to her as Jeff Walton, a freshly concocted identity. Jeff, because his infant son in Canada is named Jeff, and Walton, because of the TV show
           <i>
            The Waltons
           </i>
           .
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           He said he was from Alaska and told her his mother and siblings died in a plane crash and that his father was wealthy but estranged. Throughout their marriage of about 15 years, he was sparse with details about his past. Occasionally he would talk about fighting in the Vietnam War  a blatant lie that would surface repeatedly.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Rose Ann tried to get his birth certificate so the two could get married six months after they met. Alaska, of course, had no record of his birth. She used her connections with the clerk at Jefferson Parish in New Orleans to get a marriage license without a birth certificate.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Hes a pathological liar, said Rose Ann. Hes very ill. This man could carry on a double-life and keep going. How could you live with this pain?
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Walton took jobs in the energy industry, using his background in plumbing to help open the door. He says he found a name in the obituaries and obtained the associated social security number.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Rob Douglas, a private investigator and information security consultant specializing in identity theft, said the social security number could have been found through public records at the library. Walton could have used it as his own since there was no centralized death index for social security numbers until 1980, says Douglas, who started working in the field in 1983.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           He would have caught it right in the time frame when nobody was paying attention to that stuff, said Douglas. If he didnt do anything to ping law enforcement or civil infractions, he was probably free and clear and it looks like he was.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Rose Ann had a steady income from a job in waste management. Almost immediately he began cheating on her, she says, with people she knew.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Their son was born in 1979  premature and with a slim chance of survival. He too would be named Jeff Walton  a second chance for the senior Walton to be a father. Rose Ann said he was a good dad, doting and attentive to his son.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           In the 1980s, Rose Ann was involved in an accident with a truck that left her debilitated and with a large court settlement in the area of $500,000  half of which Jeff senior had control over.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The booze started flowing again. Walton, who liked fast cars, bought a Pontiac Fiero and used a body-kit to make it look like a Ferrari.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The pair separated, though they would not divorce until the mid-90s. In the meantime, Jeff Walton Sr. began reaching back into the world of Ronald Stan. Over the years he would keep tabs on his family through a few select friends in Canada who knew the full story. Once in awhile hed even reach out.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           I wanted to (keep in touch) so bad, and I did call a couple times just to hear their voices, he said, laughing. Theyd say hello, and hed hang up without speaking. Thats silly. But no, I didnt think it was right for me to talk to them, so I didnt.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
          </p>
          <hr class="stars"/>
         </div>
         <div class="HTML5 section">
          <div class="html5-box star-box float-clear">
           <div class="star-box-heading float-clear">

             Where did Ronald Stan go?
            </strong>
           </div>

             </iframe>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Walton met his match with his third wife, Holly Kelley, a New Orleans woman who appeared in Playboys October 1981 issue and claims to have psychic abilities.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           He met her through a friend and kicked off a romance that would flay his secret life open.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Holly was a new-age person, she was into new-age things at the time, and thats what brought Jeff Walton Sr. to his knees, said Rose Ann. She got it out of him about Ronald Stan. Shes the one that got it out of him.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="parbase section genericimage">
          <div class="generic-image" id="cq-image-jsp-/content/thestar/news/gta/2014/09/14/inside_the_secret_life_of_ronald_stan_lust_lies_and_a_new_identity/jcr:content/body/genericimage_1">
           <img alt="No. 3 wife Holly Kelley as she posed for an October, 1981 feature in Playboy magazine called the Girls of the Southeastern Conference." src="http://www.thestar.com/content/dam/thestar/uploads/2014/9/15/1410795797976.jpg.size.xxlarge.promo.jpg"/>
           <div class="caption">
            <p class="description">
             No. 3 wife Holly Kelley as she posed for an October, 1981 feature in Playboy magazine called the Girls of the Southeastern Conference.
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Kelley is now working in Hawaii,
           <a href="http://bit.ly/1wmHMdC">
            running a business with her identical twin sister, Bobbi
           </a>
           , and providing 30-minute psychic readings over Skype or the telephone for $75 U.S. When reached by the Star, she inquired whether or not she would be paid for an interview. The Star does not pay interview subjects.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Kelley did not return subsequent phone calls but emailed to say she was apprehensive about speaking publicly.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           I am very apprehensive about talking to you about Jeff Walton because he is such a habitual liar . . . He has always needed therapy . . . He is an ego manic and will do anything for attention, she wrote.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Throughout the 1990s, Walton began disclosing his secret to his family and to Kelley. First was with his Canadian wife, then he began reaching out to his biological family.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Much remains unclear about the Canadian aspects of Waltons story. An uncle, who admittedly had little contact with Ronald Stan and his side of the family, said the family never believed he was dead. Stans Canadian wife, Gail, refused to speak with the Star. His Canadian son Jamie also refused, saying it was too painful to relive the memories from that time.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Ronald Stan was formally declared dead in 1986. At that time, you couldnt simply declare a person dead, according to lawyer Joshua Eisen at Hull and Hull LLP.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Before the Declarations of Death Act came in 2002, there was no process to apply to a court just to have somebody declared dead. You couldnt just bring an application, said Eisen. There would have to have been some other kind of proceeding for which the fact of his death or non-death was an important issue.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           There are several possibilities that could set the gears in motion for the declaration of death. A life insurance payout would require it, as would issues with his will or estate. If Gail Stan were to have remarried and required a formal divorce, that may have also led to the declaration of his death.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           In 1990, Walton began to step outside his faulty persona and reach into his past. He phoned his Canadian wife.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           I did call her and talk to her. After that, she didnt believe I was who I said I was. Thought someone was playing a joke on her. But I knew things that other people had no idea, and she gave all the (insurance) money back. Its the truth, said Walton.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           He went to stay with his brother, John, in London one weekend in 1995 and they invited his father over. At first, the elder Stan didnt believe it was his son.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           He comes over and touches me all over I go, Hey Dad, how you doin? says Walton, laughing at the memory. He says, Ah, it is you.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           He returned again to Canada as his father was on his deathbed. He and his two brothers and sisters began to talk about dividing the patriarchs assets among the children.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Theyre sayin well were going to divide his assets up into four. I said no, divide it three ways. I dont want nothin  I aint been a son to him, said Walton.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           His father passed away in 1997 and Walton stayed in touch with his brother and sister, John and Wendy, over the years. Despite the revelation of his survival, Ronald Stan remained dead in the eyes of the law.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Walton also told Kelley about his mysterious past. He said he told her things that he had told none of his other wives. Rose Ann said Kelley took it upon herself to break the news to her, though wont say when she found out.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Shes the one that got it out of him, said Rose Ann. She used to love rubbing my nose in everything.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Rose Ann hid the truth from her son, Jeff Walton Jr. He didnt find out about his fathers past as Ronald Stan until last month, when the rest of the world found out.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Throughout the 1990s Waltons relationship with Kelley fell apart. He took a job with a company in the Virgin Islands where they gave him a computer  still a novelty in 1998  and set to work right away.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           I thought wow, what kind of trouble can I get in with this? And I did. I would look up those dating sites and thats how I found Debra, said Walton.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
          </p>
          <hr class="stars"/>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Jeff Walton married Debra Proctor, his fourth wife, in 2000. The pair set up a life of football, golf games and grandkids in Tahlequah, Okla.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="parbase section genericimage">
          <div class="generic-image force-original" id="cq-image-jsp-/content/thestar/news/gta/2014/09/14/inside_the_secret_life_of_ronald_stan_lust_lies_and_a_new_identity/jcr:content/body/genericimage_2">
           <img alt="Fourth wife Debra Proctor and Jeff Walton in 2013, attending a fundraising gala at Northeastern State University in Tahlequah, Okla. They had met in the early days of online dating." src="http://www.thestar.com/content/dam/thestar/uploads/2014/9/15/1410795921159.jpg"/>
           <div class="caption">
            <p class="credit">
             Mesha Leggett Sanchez / TRA Photography
            </p>
            <p class="description">
             Fourth wife Debra Proctor and Jeff Walton in 2013, attending a fundraising gala at Northeastern State University in Tahlequah, Okla. They had met in the early days of online dating.
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Nestled in the foothills of the Ozark Mountains, the town of roughly 16,000 is home to a major hospital, where Proctor works, and the main campus of Northeastern State University. Its a town of classic storefronts and coffee shops that buzz with the days gossip. In August, news rippled through the town about the man with a secret identity who had married Proctor, a prominent member of the community.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p annotation="note">
           He met Debra online in the nascent days of Internet dating.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           It appears the pair lived a happy life.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Photos from that era and the years that follow show a smiling Jeff Walton, seen cuddling his grandchildren  his son Jeffs three daughters  golfing, and on his wifes arm at various charity galas she attended as part of her position with the local hospital.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           In 2003, Walton took a trip to Edmonton to attend his mothers 80th birthday party, continuing his process of re-uniting with his Canadian family, which his latest wife knew nothing of.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p annotation="note">
           Id see somebody and go, Oh my God, they remember me as Ron, so Id see em come in and Id hook em in the elbow and take em outside. I said, Now look, this is my name now, so dont be calling me no Ron,  said Walton.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Evidently his family listened. His mothers obituary, published after her death in 2010, reads She will be lovingly remembered and missed by her children: Jeff, Wendy, Alan and John.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Walton continued to live his lie.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The enduring mystery at the heart of this story is how and why the Ontario Provincial Police reopened the investigation into the disappearance of Ronald Stan. The police force said in a statement it was part of a routine audit of its case files.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           We want to be able to account for missing parties whether theyre deceased or not, said Const. Laurie Houghton.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Walton thinks Proctor was the one who sparked the renewed investigation. Proctor refused to speak to the Star for this story and instead issued a statement saying the OPP had phoned her at work on Aug. 1, 2014 and told her the story of Ronald Stan.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           We are truly saddened from this unimaginable deceit and betrayal, reads the statement.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Ever on the cutting edge of technology, Walton is an avid Facebook user. He became sloppy with his secrets, adding and chatting with his brother from his past life, John Stan. According to Facebook, Proctor and John Stan became friends in 2013.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           In March 2014, Walton suffered a stroke that has left him with vascular dementia, a condition that caused Proctor to enroll him first in a daytime care facility for seniors, then have him placed at Go Ye Village, a Christian long-term care facility in Tahlequah. Walton thinks Proctor was looking into his past while he was in the facility.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           She started digging into all these other things. She found a way to save her, make me look like the bad guy  which I am  and make her a little princess, he said. She comes out smelling like a rose because Im an ahole and thats okay.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           In the divorce paperwork she filed August 14, and that he agreed to, she got the two cars, the house, all of their shared assets, and he would take their shared debts.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Asked why he didnt fight it, no point, he said.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           On the top of Waltons list of concerns is the future of his social security pension. The FBI typically investigates cases of identity theft, but would not disclose whether or not it is conducting an investigation into Walton, who said he has been paying taxes for his entire time in the United States.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Its been almost 40 years that Ive lived this lie. And actually Ive been a real good boy, except for the philandering. I paid my taxes, I did everything right, said Walton.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
          </p>
          <hr class="stars"/>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Thirty-seven years after running away from his first wife Gail, Ronald Stan says he still loves her. He is revealing his story to the Toronto Star because he hopes she reads it.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Walton, whom the Star found in New Orleans, has made a list of what he insists are truths, plucked from a life populated by lies.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Its written in careful pencil strokes on an unlined piece of paper. His hands fold over it as the afternoon light shines in his sons living room. His smile consumes his face, drawing down the corners of his eyes. Missing teeth give him a childlike grin.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           So, what do you want to know? he says in a Louisiana drawl acquired after he fled Canada. The words flow forth from the penitent man.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           He starts from the beginning, when he took a job at Fanshawe College teaching plumbing.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Wow  Ive never seen so many women in my life. Thats where the trouble started. So, I cheated on my wife. With more than one woman. A lot. Everyone I could, said Stan.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           On the day of the fire, his mother received a call from the mother of a girl from St. Marys, Ont., who Stan had been sleeping with. The girl told her mother that Stan was going to marry her. His mother, Rhoda, phoned him.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           She said, This lady is lookin for you. Youre in deep trouble. Shes from a very wealthy family,  Stan recalled.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           That night he came home to the farm, knowing that his world would soon unravel. His wife and two kids were inside the house when he went to the pig barn just after midnight.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           His explanation is that the fire started from a bale of wet hay.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           God, it went up so fast I couldnt believe it  like a bomb, says Walton. I went outside and I was standing there and went, oh my God what the hell am I going to do.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Walton has admittedly lied his whole life, but now, sitting destitute in the kitchen of his son, he says he is telling the truth. Wet hay produces a particular kind of bacteria that can cause a rapid rise in temperatures and explosive combustion, Washington State University agriculture professor agriculture professor Steven Fransen confirms. Walton says he kicked a bale of hay, a move that Fransen says would be the equivalent of pouring gas on the fire.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The truth appears lost to history. The Archives of Ontario houses records from the Ontario Fire Marshal, but has no record from that particular night. In its 2014 statement, the OPP says the force and the fire marshals office investigated  no charges have been laid in this case.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Neighbour Bert Toonen, who is skeptical of the wet hay explanation, says Stan never fed the pigs  they were Toonens. Toonen, who now owns the property, remembers the 70 pigs lost that night, and he remembers sifting through their charred remains in search of a human body.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           As he watched the flames lick into the September night sky, Ronald Stan could hear his wife, Gail, screaming in the distance. On Sept. 29, 1977, Ronald Stan slipped into the acreages of North Middlesex under the cover of darkness.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           She would get his life insurance payout, he thought. She would also be saddled with all the debt that came from running a farm. She was going to leave him anyway when she found out about the young girl from St. Marys, who got it in her head that Ron was going to marry her.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Stan picked his way through the fields and forests of north Middlesex County, travelling at night until he made it to Port Huron. With the $3,000 from the sale of a car he hopped a bus to New Orleans.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p annotation="note">
           Stan now relies on the kindness of his son, who only recently learned about his fathers secret identity. Jeff Walton Jr. appears to be taking the news in stride  the 35-year-old with a young family says it doesnt change the man hes known for his whole life.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           For Walton, he has reached the end of his story. Sitting at his sons kitchen table in a small parish outside New Orleans, he draws pictures. Theyre pointillism  little dots that, when viewed together, make up the whole.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           At least you got my part of the story, which is the truth. I have not lied at all. Its not going to save me or anything. Theres still going to be people that think Im a d---head and thats fine, I dont care, said Walton.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Hes at once remorseful and nostalgic for his life. He was a boy on a long adventure, where the horses and princesses are replaced by fast cars and women, and the wounds he inflicted dont fade with time but echo through generations. Two sons without a father in Canada, another who is just getting to know who his dad really is, and a trail of women left broken-hearted. All felt the sting of betrayal; of loving a man who was a living lie.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Im not going to lie to you and say I didnt really want to mess with all those girls  I did, and I did. How do you get forgiveness for that? I can apologize and say Im sorry . . . thats all I can do. If I apologize, thats good, but how do you apologize for all 40 years? Say Im sorry? Am I going to get forgiven? I doubt it, says Walton.
          </p>
         </div>
