        <h1 class="title">
         Top 10 Survival Items You Would Never Think You Would Need
        </h1>
        <div class="content-panel post-3841 post type-post status-publish format-standard category-blogs category-guest-articles tag-survival-tips" id="post-3841">
         <div class="article-image">
         </div>
         <!-- responsive purposes - hidden for standard desktop view-->
         <div class="article-image responsive">
          <br class="clearer hide-responsive-small"/>
         </div>
         <!-- end responsive purposes -->
         <div class="the-content">
          <p>
           Its easy to focus on the basics of food and water, but once you have a good supply built up, its time to stockpile items that you may not think of right away, but are extremely necessary to your health and well-being during a crisis.
          </p>
          <p>
           When you are building up your food storage and emergency preparedness kits, the best way to approach it is to start acquiring the basic must have items, then adding other things to that in order of usefulness. After the basics are covered, analyze what else you might need to gather to help you and your family in the event of an emergency.
          </p>
          <p>
           Here are the top 10 important survival items you probably havent thought of or dont have enough of:
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            1. First aid manual.
           </strong>
           You may have the best first aid kit, but unless you are medically trained, its a good idea to have a detailed reference manual to consult for those inevitable injuries that are bigger than slivers and small cuts.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            2. Maps.
           </strong>
           From city maps to topographical maps, these can show you everything from alternate evacuation routes to the elevation and terrain around you. A forest service map can also be hand to show fire and logging roads that dont appear on standard maps.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            3. Minor medicines.
           </strong>
           Once you have the more important medicines, like pain relievers and anti-diarrheals, gather up medicines that can make life a little better if you cant reach a doctor right away. Examples include cold medicine, sore throat lozenges and antacids.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            4. Spare glasses and contact lenses.
           </strong>
           If your vision is impaired, its critical that you have backup corrective lenses, in case your working pair of glasses gets broken. You can also buy up to a years supply of disposable contact lenses so youll be able to see clearly in an emergency situation.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            5. Feminine products
           </strong>
           . Menstrual cycles dont stop just because there is an emergency, so stock sanitary napkins and tampons to get through several cycles of your period. These products can also double as bandages.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            6. Sunscreen and aloe
           </strong>
           . In a survival situation, severe sunburn can lead to reduced physical activity, discomfort, pain and even infection. Since you may be outside in all kinds of weather, protect your skin.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            7. Non-electric hand tools.
           </strong>
           From bolt cutters and axes to hammers and saws, hand tools will be an important part of your life in an emergency situation. Whether its cutting wood for a fire or driving stakes deep into the ground, hand tools can make your whole ordeal much easier. Consider doubling up on hand tools that may break easily so youll always have a spare.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            8. Supplies for growing babies.
           </strong>
           Because babies change and grow so rapidly, stock up on a range of baby clothes that are several sizes larger than your baby. Dont forget different diaper sizes.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            9. Pet food.
           </strong>
           If your dog or cat is like another member of the family, youll want to ensure that you have enough food and supplies for their needs too.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            10. Extra socks and underwear.
           </strong>
           In an emergency that can last for several days to several weeks, you can reduce the risk of developing health issues that result from wet, dirty socks and underwear. Spare pairs of socks and underwear will help keep you healthy and comfortable.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <em>
             Chett Wright enjoys writing about camping, outdoor living and emergency preparedness. His most recent articles include how to grow a survival garden, beginning survival tips and family food storage.
            </em>
           </strong>
          </p>
