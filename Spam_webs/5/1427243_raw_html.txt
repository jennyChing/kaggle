       <aside>
        <h1 id="news-title">
         Soil Resistivity Helpful Tips
        </h1>
        <p id="link-category">
         <a href="http://greymattersglobal.com/category/soil-resistivity/wenner-array/" rel="category tag">
          Wenner Array
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://greymattersglobal.com/category/soil-resistivity/" rel="category tag">
          Soil Resistivity
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="clearfix" id="blog-time">
         <time datetime="2014-03-10">
          March 10, 2014
         </time>
         <span class="link-comment-header">
          Comments Off
          <span class="screen-reader-text">
           on Soil Resistivity Helpful Tips
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
       </aside>
      </header>
      <h2>
       Soil Resistivity ... Helpful Tips Video Series - Script
      </h2>
      <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
       <span style="color: #b81354;">
        Introduction [00:00]
       </span>
       <strong style="color: #b81354; font-size: 1.5em; line-height: 1.5em;">
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       We've got our kit, consisting of two DET 2's, a set of leads, 2 leads which span 300 metres total, so we're getting probe spacings upto 100 metres.
       <span id="more-1156">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="400" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/GUVLaJz94bQ" width="700">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       We've got multiple copper rods, in this case, because were sitting on granite [cough]. We want to be able to get as maximised contact with the surface layer as possible, so that we can get [cough] good return signal.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Often, you won't get a chance to take soundings directly on construction sites cos its' too busy - too much plant, too many risks, and the ground itself is probably made up, so its of little real value because its all kind of imported.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        [1:00]
       </strong>
       So hunt out ...
       <em>
        [in this case we've gone a little bit Ray Mears, if you're not familiar with Ray Mears - he's a survival expert]
       </em>
       but anyway we've gone a little bit Ray Mears outside of the construction site, to find a place that has been undisturbed - and here we are in the forest.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        [1:16]
       </strong>
       We've just laid out the pins - first pin and point leads, first pin is about, [I dunno] 10 inches apart spaced - this sets the trend for analysis later on.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        [1:29]
       </strong>
       It's really important you get a close spacing first of all to see where the tipping point is for the layer'isation.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        [1:36]
       </strong>
       So, next phase is to really get the spacings out, to about 54 - 100 metre intervals, so we're on about a 300 metre run into the forest.
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
       <span style="color: #b81354;">
        TIP 1: Multiple Probes [01:58]
       </span>
       <span style="color: #b81354; font-size: 1.5em; line-height: 1.5em;">
        <br/>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Right, we're using multiple probes to increase the surface contact with the signal on the sounding, so we get a good return and more reliable results, basically.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        [2:15]
       </strong>
       This is what they look like, the potential probe on one side, then we've got three probes on the other. The mole grips are just there because its practicably what we had available, but just to give you an idea.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        [2:26]
       </strong>
       We're using copper probes, driven to about 3 inches - 4 inches deep, with multiple stack connectors on, which then go into the leads
       <em>
        [I can show you ...these particular leads have seen a bit of action, but anyway]
       </em>
       , it just gives you an idea of what we're doing, and cos the [
       <em>
        multiple
       </em>
       ] on the current signals especially, multiple probes they allow better connection, so you've effectively got a much bigger probe area in contact ... it sends s decent signal through, for the instrument to pick up ... that's the theory ...
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        [3:21]
       </strong>
       <em>
        [try and get you in focus ... there you go ... all good]
       </em>
       ... 51.8 - mark that off against the probe spacing and then what happens then is that, that is the rule reading - it then gets processed, software processed - to actually give the correct, apparent reading ... and this we repeat numerous soundings, up to 300 metre spacings sometimes, so it can be quite a time consuming activity.
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
       <span style="color: #b81354;">
        Tip 2: Surface Test [4:03]
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       A really useful tip for the modelling side of soil resistivity is to get the immediate surface layer tests and sounded - which means probe spacings typically 4 - 6 inches apart
       <em>
        [don't know if you can see that]
       </em>
       but this is just to set the trend when it comes to the analysis side of things.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        [4:34]
       </strong>
       So useful tip - get that in - it may not form part of the formal report, but it does set the scene for the model analysis.
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
       <span style="color: #b81354;">
        Tip 3: Angle [4:50]
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       To improve the connection on the signal - its slightly at an angle as well so you have more surface contact.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        [5:08]
       </strong>
       credits
