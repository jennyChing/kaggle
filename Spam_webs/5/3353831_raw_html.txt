        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Music from the Worst Album Covers  Merry Christmas &amp; Happy New Year with the Mom and Dads
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-date">
          <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/music-from-the-worst-album-covers-merry-christmas-happy-new-year-with-the-mom-and-dads" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2012-12-10T09:31:52+00:00">
            December 10, 2012
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/author/admin" rel="author">
            Chris Holmes
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <em>
          Weve all seen at least one article showing the worst album
          <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/covers">
           covers
          </a>
          of all-time. But what about the
          <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/music">
           music
          </a>
          inside? Should you judge an album by its horrible cover? Well in this
          <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/series">
           series
          </a>
          , were going to find out. Today we examine
         </em>
         Merry
         <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/christmas">
          Christmas
         </a>
         &amp; Happy New Year
         <em>
          by The Mom and Dads.
         </em>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p>
         Ill say this much for the 1972 holiday album
         <em>
          Merry Christmas &amp; Happy New Year
         </em>
         by the Mom and Dads  if ever there was a Christmas album that sounded
         <strong>
          exactly
         </strong>
         like you would expect it to, this is it. Im not going to sit here and judge whether or not thats a positive thing; Im just throwing it out there as a heads up of sorts.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Merry Christmas &amp; Happy New Year with the Mom and Dads" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17313" height="638" src="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/merry-christmas-with-the-mom-and-dads_album-cover.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black;" title="Merry Christmas &amp; Happy New Year with the Mom and Dads" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yes, thats the actual group on the cover. Weve got (l to r) Quentin Ratliff (saxophone), Harold Hendren (drums), Doris A. Crow (
         <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/piano">
          piano
         </a>
         ), and Leslie Welch (accordion) looking resplendent in their early 70s finery. But in case this photo wasnt enough to entice you into buying the album, check out the back cover!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Merry Christmas &amp; Happy New Year with the Mom and Dads" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17314" height="621" src="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/merry-christmas-with-the-mom-and-dads_album-cover-rear.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black;" title="Merry Christmas &amp; Happy New Year with the Mom and Dads" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Boom! Matching purple vests for the win!
        </p>
        <p>
         So just who are the Mom and Dads? Well, they got their start in the early 50s and were based out of Spokane, Washington. They played on a part-time basis early on, and specialized in waltzes, polkas,
         <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/country">
          country
         </a>
         &amp; western you know, music for moms and dads. They did actually achieve some commercial success, believe it or not.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-17312">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Turns out that two of the groups LPs charted in
         <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/canada">
          Canada
         </a>
         in the early
         <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/1970s">
          1970s
         </a>
         , and they claim several gold and platinum
         <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/albums">
          albums
         </a>
         north of the border. Their biggest taste of Stateside success came in 1971, when their album
         <em>
          The Rangers Waltz
         </em>
         reached #85 on the Billboard Top 200 and #4 on Billboards Country chart. A single of the same name bubbled under at #101.
        </p>
        <p>
         With that in mind, a Christmas album makes sense. Its what most acts do when they get a taste of success, right? And I guess as holiday cash-ins go its not horrible. At least its in keeping with the bands typical sound, which is to be commended. Its pleasant enough alright, and sounds like the type of music youd hear played live at an American Legion or Elks Club Christmas party.
        </p>
        <p>
         With all that out of the way, Ive got four songs from
         <em>
          Merry Christmas &amp; Happy New Year
         </em>
         (GNP Crescendo
         <a href="http://www.grayflannelsuit.net/blog/tag/records">
          Records
         </a>
         , GNPS 2067) for your listening enjoyment. Because really thats all you need to hear to get the idea. Check out Here Comes Santa Claus, Let It Snow! Let It Snow! Let It Snow!, May You Have a Happy New Year, and a
         <strong>
          five-minute
         </strong>
         version of Auld Lang Syne.
        </p>

         <iframe frameborder="no" height="166" scrolling="no" src="http://w.soundcloud.com/player/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fapi.soundcloud.com%2Ftracks%2F69768600&amp;show_artwork=true" width="100%">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>

         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe frameborder="no" height="166" scrolling="no" src="http://w.soundcloud.com/player/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fapi.soundcloud.com%2Ftracks%2F69768797&amp;show_artwork=true" width="100%">

        </p>
