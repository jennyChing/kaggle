     <h1>
      Hamas Combat Manual Explains Benefits of Using Human Shields
     </h1>
     <div class="author">
      By
      <a href="/neil_snyder">
       Neil Snyder
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="article_body top">
      <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
       <span style="font-size: 14px;">
        This is one of the many positive things to come out of Operation Protective Edge. The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) has found a
        <a>
         Hamas combat manual
        </a>
        explaining the importance of using human shields. Below is an excerpt from the IDF report:
       </span>
      </span>
      <blockquote>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         IDF forces in the Gaza Strip found a Hamas manual on Urban Warfare, which belonged to the ShujaiyaBrigade of Hamas military wing, the Al-Qassam Brigades. The manual explains how the civilian population can be used against IDF forces and reveals that Hamas knows the IDF is committed to minimizing harm to civilians.
        </span>
       </span>
      </blockquote>
     </div>
     <div class="article_box_ad">
      <script type="text/javascript">
       &lt;!--

     </div>
     <div class="article_body bottom">
      <blockquote>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         ThroughoutOperation Protective Edge, Hamas has continuously used the civilian population of Gaza ashuman shields. The discovery of a Hamas urban warfare manual by IDF forces reveals that Hamas callous use of the Gazan population was intentional and preplanned.
        </span>
       </span>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          This Hamas urban warfare manual exposes two truths: (1) The terror group knows full well that the IDF will do what it can tolimit civilian casualties. (2) The terror group exploits these efforts by using civilians as human shields against advancing IDF forces.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          In a portion entitled Limiting the Use of Weapons, the manual explains that:
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          <em>
           The soldiers and commanders (of the IDF) must limit their use of weapons and tactics that lead to the harm and unnecessary loss of people and [destruction of] civilian facilities. It is difficult for them to get the most use out of their firearms, especially of supporting fire [e.g. artillery].
          </em>
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          Clearly Hamas knows the IDF will limit its use of weapons in order toavoid harming civilians, including refraining from using larger firepower to support for infantry.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          The manual goes on to explain that the presence of civilians are pockets of resistance that cause three major problems for advancing troops:
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          <em>
           (1) Problems with opening fire(2) Problems in controlling the civilian population during operations and afterward(3) Assurance of supplying medical care to civilians who need it
          </em>
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          Lastly, the manual discusses the benefits for Hamas when civilian homes are destroyed:
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          <em>
           The destruction of civilian homes: This increases the hatred of the citizens towards the attackers [the IDF] and increases their gathering [support] around the city defenders (resistance forces[i.e. Hamas]).
          </em>
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          It is clear that Hamas actuallydesires the destruction of homesand civilian infrastructure, knowing it will increase hatred for the IDF and support their fighters.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Im ashamed that the IDF felt the need to report it because it should be patently obvious to everyone by now. An imbecile should know it, especially since we have video footage of Hamas firing rockets from hospitals, mosques, and schools, and we know that they stored weapons in U.N. schools.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Physical evidence is not enough for a large number of people who are either brain-dead morons or anti-Semites to the core. Im afraid that masses of people live by this principle: Ive made up my mind and facts dont matter. I just wish they understood that they are complicit in Hamas actions since their support is an important reason why Hamas uses human shields. If the public reacted negatively, they would stop it.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         So rest easy tonight Hamas supporters, if you can. If I were complicit in murder it would trouble me greatly.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         <span style="font-size:12px;">
          <strong>
           <em>
            Neil Snyder is the Ralph A. Beeton Professor Emeritus at the University of Virginia. His blog, SnyderTalk.com, is posted daily.
           </em>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="article_body">
      <p>

      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Im ashamed that the IDF felt the need to report it because it should be patently obvious to everyone by now. An imbecile should know it, especially since we have video footage of Hamas firing rockets from hospitals, mosques, and schools, and we know that they stored weapons in U.N. schools.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Physical evidence is not enough for a large number of people who are either brain-dead morons or anti-Semites to the core. Im afraid that masses of people live by this principle: Ive made up my mind and facts dont matter. I just wish they understood that they are complicit in Hamas actions since their support is an important reason why Hamas uses human shields. If the public reacted negatively, they would stop it.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         So rest easy tonight Hamas supporters, if you can. If I were complicit in murder it would trouble me greatly.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
