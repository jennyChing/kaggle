         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Low-Carb Swaps for Family Favorites
         </h2>
         <p>
          If you have type 2 diabetes, you have to watch what you eat very closely. Sugars and carbs in particular should be eaten in moderation so that your blood glucose levels don't hike. But having to eat
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/diabetes/monitoring-carbohydrates.aspx">
           diets that are low in sugar and carbohydrates
          </a>
          doesn't mean you have to limit yourself to tasteless meals while your loved ones indulge in gourmet feasts. You too can enjoy a meatloaf without fearing a blood sugar spike. All it takes is a touch of creativity to match heavy, popular dishes with a lighter, diabetes-friendly fare.
         </p>
         <p>
          Preparing healthier adaptations of heavy dishes will help level your blood sugars without having the constant impression of restricting yourself. It also provides a balance between low-carb and great taste that will teach your family what constitutes a healthy diet.
         </p>
         <p>
          Here's our healthier, diabetes-friendly take on a few popular dishes:
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="2" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-02-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Lasagna" id="slide-img-2" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-02-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-02-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Lasagna
         </h2>
         <p>
          Lasagna is, in some ways, a perfect meal  a mix of
fat, protein, and carbohydrates. But it is loaded with carbs from refined pasta
and fatty cheeses, neither of which, if you are diabetic, should be in your diet.
Choosing whole-grain pasta over the regular kind is always the better
alternative, said Bonnie Carminati, RN, CDE, a diabetes educator and nurse
practitioner at the University of Mississippi Medical Center in Jackson. But
most importantly, instead of opting for the traditional layering, replace one
or two noodle sheets with thinly sliced eggplants and add a copious amount of
vegetables to the sauce. Low-fat cheeses and lean meats are essential and smart
substitutes for the kind of meat and cheese that lasagna traditionally includes.
Eating
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/health-recipe/healthified-roastedvegetable-lasagna-.aspx">
           vegetable lasagna
          </a>
          in excess would make the dish heavier than you wish,
so if you're really hungry, accompany it with a salad on the side.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="3" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-03-RM-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Meatloaf+and+Mash" id="slide-img-3" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-03-RM-pg-full.jpg"/>

          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Meatloaf and Mash
         </h2>
         <p>
          Your mom's meatloaf is probably the stuff of family
legends. Two slices and a generous portion of mashed
potatoes and peas make for a comfort-food classic. But it's also easy to convert
into a low-carb, diabetes-friendly dish. You can use oatmeal instead of regular
bread crumbs and substitute potatoes with steamed or mashed cauliflower. Fill the
rest of your plate with a
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/diabetes-pictures/8-low-carb-veggies-for-diabetic-diets.aspx#/slide-1">
           variety of vegetables
          </a>
          .
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="4" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-04-RM-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Pizza" id="slide-img-4" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-04-RM-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-04-RM-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Pizza
         </h2>
         <p>
          Many people look forward to a casual pizza night, but even
a thin crust can give you too big of a carbohydrate load. So roll up your
sleeves and go with the DIY pizza. Use small whole-grain pitas or tortillas and
carpet them with a layer of sliced tomatoes, which are rich in lycopene and
antioxidants and among the best foods for diabetes. Sprinkle a modest amount of
cheese and balance it with a profuse portion of vegetables. If you'd like, you
can also add a source of
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/health-report/type-2-diabetes/meat-on-diabetes-diet.aspx">
           lean protein
          </a>
          , like chicken.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="5" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-05-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Spaghetti" id="slide-img-5" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-05-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">

          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">

          <span class="slide-pinterest">

          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Spaghetti
         </h2>
         <p>
          Spaghetti squash provides a healthy and delicious
alternative to a big bowl of regular pasta without crushing the satisfaction
and comfort that you get from eating it. To prepare it, put the
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/health-recipe/sweet-spaghetti-squash.aspx">
           spaghetti
squash
          </a>
          in a dish that you then cover with plastic wrap. Pierce the plastic wrap
to allow some of the steam to vent and cook for seven to 10 minutes. Mix the
squash with your favorite, low-carb spaghetti sauce and don't hesitate to serve
yourself a generous portion. The caloric and carb values of this dish are low
enough to allow it.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="6" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-06-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Tacos" id="slide-img-6" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-06-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-06-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Tacos
         </h2>
         <p>
          Like pizza night, taco night offers everyone the chance to choose their toppings and create their own culinary masterpiece  a concept that makes for a great diabetes option. On your taco night, build your own low-carb taco mix: lean meat, a serving of beans, salsa, and other vegetables of your choice. Avocado and tomatoes are always great fruits to include. Leave out the sour cream, but allow yourself a little bit of cheese. Put everything on a bed of greens and crush whole-grain tortilla chips to add a little bit of crunch.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="7" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-07-RM-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Tuna-Rice+Casserole" id="slide-img-7" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-07-RM-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-07-RM-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Tuna-Rice Casserole
         </h2>
         <p>
          You have two options for improving the carbohydrate
profile of traditional tuna-rice casserole. The first is to use quinoa instead
of rice. "It still has carbs but is much higher in protein and fiber than
rice," said diabetes educator Andrea Haugen, RD, CDE, of Sanford Health in
Fargo, N.D. If you go for quinoa, however, you're going to have to limit your
portion sizes to stick to your
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/health-report/type-2-diabetes-lifestyle/carb-counting-for-type-2-diabetes.aspx">
           carbohydrate goals
          </a>
          . But you can add a large
salad to your meal, which will fill you up with few carbs. Another option is to
use cooked cauliflower in place of part or all of the rice.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="8" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-08-RM-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Beef+Stew" id="slide-img-8" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-08-RM-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-08-RM-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Beef Stew
         </h2>
         <p>
          Beef stew is a staple for cold winter nights, which you can
tweak to make a standard among your diabetes recipes. To do so, picking a lean
cut of grass-fed beef is your first step. Slow cook it with onion, garlic, mushrooms,
and other vegetables. For the low-carb version, leave out the potatoes and the
carrots, and if your family likes the stew on rice, try mashed  or steamed cauliflower, or a small
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/diabetes-pictures/whole-grains-in-the-diabetes-diet.aspx#/slide-1">
           whole-grain serving
          </a>
          , such as quinoa.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="9" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-09-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Bun-less+Burgers" id="slide-img-9" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-09-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-09-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Bun-less Burgers
         </h2>
         <p>
          Does a juicy burger sound appealing? No need to give up
on this American standard. Cook a beef patty. Ideally, you want to skip the bun
and compensate with lots of grilled onions, mushrooms, green peppers, tomatoes,
lettuce, and any other veggies of your choosing. If you aren't a keen red-meat
eater, turkey or
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/health-recipe/californiastyle-veggie-burger.aspx">
           veggie burgers
          </a>
          are actually great low-carb options.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="10" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-10-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Chicken+Pot+Pie" id="slide-img-10" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-10-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Diabetes-Low-Carb-Dishes-10-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Chicken Pot Pie
         </h2>
         <p>
          Like beef stew, chicken pot pie leaves plenty of room to come up with a creative
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/type-2-diabetes/healthy-meals-for-type-2-diabetes.aspx">
           diabetes diet plan
          </a>
          . Cook up onions, garlic, lean chicken meat, chicken stock, and seasoning. Once heated through, bake it with a biscuit topping. Pot pies typically have crust under, around, and on top. But if you're trying to control the carbs, use nut flours instead and opt for a partial crust, said Haugen. Almond flour can be used to make a low-carb, dumpling-style topping for this pot pie.
         </p>
