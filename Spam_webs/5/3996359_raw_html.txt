       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="Autostraddle 5th B'day_Cats plus changes_Rory Midhani_640px (1)" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-226651" height="350" src="//www.autostraddle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Autostraddle-5th-Bday_Cats-plus-changes_Rory-Midhani_640px-1.jpg" width="640"/>
         <br/>
         <em>
          Were celebrating
          <a href="http://www.autostraddle.com/autostraddle-is-5-years-old-this-march-and-celebrating-all-month-long-226257/">
           Autostraddles Fifth Birthday
          </a>
          all month long by publishing a bunch of
          <a href="http://www.autostraddle.com/tag/top-5/">
           Top Fives
          </a>
          . This is one of them!
         </em>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p>
         Ive lived in the North Oakland/South Berkeley area now for about three and a half years, but because there are no seasons, I havent noticed time passing in the usual way. Prior to taking up residence in this strange bay, Id spent six years living in New York and before that a few places, but mostly Northern and Southeastern Michigan. I was vaguely aware that this part of the state had unique meteorological features unlike the climates of New York and Michigan, statesthat boast awful frozen winters and awful humid summers yet still manage to delight immense numbers of residents. But I was unprepared for exactly how much sunshine I was about to get in my life.Of course, like every state in this country, California has its fair fucking share of problems (and this area in particular is awful w/r/t police brutality and corruption) but the California coast area also has these other things going on that those other states dont. Like eternal sunshine! And oranges! All these things come together to help dull the potential pain of all my best friends living in other cities and working 16 hours a day (from home). Lets discuss.
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <h2>
         Top Five Reasons Living In California Feels Like Cheating
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="actual-thing" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-227780" height="429" src="//www.autostraddle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/actual-thing-640x429.png" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         5. Ordering Weed Is Easier Than Ordering Anything Else, Ever
        </h2>
        <p>
         In New York, getting weed was generally a time-consuming affair, involving a series of cryptic phone calls, usually teaming up with a friend to coordinate a bulk buy worth your dealers while,waiting around for an unspecific interval of time and then selecting a seemingly arbitrarily priced product from a smelly backpack offered up by a complete stranger. I mean, sometimes youd have a guy, but then sometimes your guy would go away from a while (and sometimes your guy would go away for a while) and youd be between guys, or using a delivery service that sent a different guy every time. Now, here, in this strange place called The San Francisco Bay Area, you can access an online menu of flowers, pre-rolls, baked goods, candies, tinctures  anything the heart desires  select your desired items, place them in your cart, place your order online, pick your desired delivery time and then sit and wait for the nice boy to come to your door with your product. You can even
         <em>
          pay for it on a credit card machine at the door
         </em>
         ! Its so weird.
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_208814" style="width: 650px">
         <img alt="Look at these queers" class="size-full wp-image-208814" height="427" src="//www.autostraddle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/bay_area_5.jpg" width="640"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Look at these queers
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         4. Everyone is Gay
        </h2>
        <p>
         I dont even get the but you dont look gay double-take I got on the reg when I lived in New York and was forced by some turn in conversation or administrative process to reveal that I had a girlfriend or ran a lesbian website. Ive never felt more comfortable being gay or talking about it than I do here, even with my dentist! Oakland has the highest number of lesbian couples per capita in the country, which seems true. I was just at the grocery store and I saw maybefive lesbian couples per aisle. San Francisco might be a gay mans paradise, but Oakland has the largest and most diverse queer female population Ive ever witnessed and has a rich history of lesbian activism, which made it a great choice for last years
         <a href="http://www.autostraddle.com/butch-voices-2013-butch-gets-a-press-pass-191341/" title="BUTCH Voices 2013: Butch Gets a Press Pass">
          Butch Voices
         </a>
         conference.
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <h2>
         <img alt="bb-produce-section" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-228886" height="426" src="//www.autostraddle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/bb-produce-section-640x426.jpg" width="640"/>
         3. Grocery Shopping Is Otherworldly
        </h2>
        <p>
         Alameda County residents are encouraged to bring their own reusable shopping bags with them everywhere or else pay ten cents for a paper bag / feel like an asshole. (Exceptions are made for those on food stamps). Heres the thing: WE ALL DO IT. WE ALL BRING THE REUSABLE SHOPPING BAGS. I mean,this is the kind of policy that incites many Americans to declare their freedom impinged upon by those crazy environmentals, but not here!
        </p>
        <p>
         Its cute, its like every time I go to Trader Joes Im playing Show and Tell Your Special Bag with the entire parking lot, so I feel like were all very responsible and in tune with the earth.Also adding to the Earth Goddess aura is
         <strong>
          the bounty of fresh affordable produce
         </strong>
         available at Berkeley Bowl. However, Berkeley Bowl is also stressful because the shopping carts are too wide and its too crowded, but whatever. There is so much fresh produce here!! Cutting processed foods out of my diet has been about 100 times easier here than it was in the other places I lived.
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_230110" style="width: 650px">
         <img alt="My cute girlfriend on a day trip we took to Half Moon Bay" class="size-medium wp-image-230110 " height="640" src="//www.autostraddle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/half-moon-640x640.jpg" width="640"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          My cute girlfriend on a day trip we took to Half Moon Bay
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         2. So Much Beauty So Nearby
        </h2>
        <p>
         We are within biking distance of awesome natural parks and biking trails and also of the ocean and gorgeous marina. This is a feature shared by most major cities in the Pacific Northwest  similar delightfulness can be obtained in Portland, for example, and Los Angeles. This is great for humans from all income brackets because theres plenty of free things to do, likehiking and beaching all along the coast, and theres also plenty of not-free things to do, like spa/resorts and wine-tasting. (That being said, being amongst the 16% of Berkeley residents that dont own a car really sucks in this regard, but there is a non-profit
         <a href="http://citycarshare.org">
          carshare service
         </a>
         with a pod a block from our place, which is also not-real-lifey.)Furthermore, allegedly one can ski on a slope within three hours of here?
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_227734" style="width: 650px">
         <img alt="Typical" class="size-full wp-image-227734" height="302" src="//www.autostraddle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/seriously-what.png" width="640"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Typical
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         1. The weather
        </h2>
        <p>
         The weather here is STUPID. Its just stupid! What have we done to deserve such plentitude? Its always about 68 degrees. It almost never rains (but it always rains when
         <a href="http://www.autostraddle.com/author/alex/">
          Alex
         </a>
         comes to visit, because the world refuses to let Alex believe me that the weather here is awesome). Granted, the lack of rain is
         <a href="http://www.theatlanticcities.com/jobs-and-economy/2014/03/no-relief-sight-drought-ravished-california/8696/">
          actually a bad thing when it comes to farming and the overall climate
         </a>
         , but it is a good thing if you ride your bike everywhere and I think we get
         <em>
          so little
         </em>
         rain here that I have room inside my heart for at least another 30 days of rain per year, should the goddesses wish to bestow that upon our land. I might even buy an umbrella.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the winter, we generally remain in the neighborhood of 50 degrees, and in the summer its rare to meet or exceed 80. Ive never needed an air conditioner or a good winter coat.Whats even weirder, though, is how this perfect weather is limited to very specific pockets of land (I think Oakland/Berkeley, San Jose, Palo Alto, San Francisco, Monterey, etc.).I was astounded a while back when, driving a carshare twenty minutes to a doctors appointment in Walnut Creek from Oakland, I watched the temperature rise from the mid-60s in Oakland to the mid-90s in Walnut Creek! Id gotten into the car in a hoodie, and emerged wishing Id worn a tank top. Sometimes I wish it would rain or be dark though, because sometimes it rains or feels dark inside of me and the weather feels like a bully. You know what I mean? Sometimes I still feel like New York on the inside.
         <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
         </span>
        </p>
