     <h2 class="article-subhead" itemprop="description">
      The Seth Rogen-starring comedy is available north of the border via the studio's dedicated Kernel website
     </h2>
     <div class="article-text" itemprop="articleBody">
      <p>
       Sony's
       <em>
        The Interview
       </em>
       is now streaming in Canada.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <strong>
        Seth Rogen
       </strong>
       and
       <strong>
        James
       </strong>
       <strong>
        Franco
       </strong>
       -starrer is available on Sony's own dedicated Canadian website,
       <a href="https://www.seetheinterview.com/" target="_blank">
        SeeTheInterview.com
       </a>
       , which is powered by Kernel, for a $6.99 rental fee. That leaves
       <em>
        The Interview
       </em>
       only streaming legitimately in North America via a U.S. or Canadian IP address, and available elsewhere
       <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/interview-leaks-online-file-sharing-760361" target="_blank">
        internationally via illegal
       </a>
       downloads.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Read more
       </strong>
       <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/watch-seth-rogen-cheer-first-760359">
        Watch Seth Rogen Cheer On First 'Interview' Audience
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Canadian Internet release follows a limited theatrical release in U.S. indie cinemas
       <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/interview-released-vod-twitter-explodes-760352" target="_blank">
        and on VOD
       </a>
       from Christmas Day. Sony has yet to release
       <em>
        The Interview
       </em>
       in Canadian theaters.
      </p>
      <p>
       Cineplex Entertainment, the Canadian exhibition giant, earlier
       <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/sony-hack-canadas-cineplex-postpone-758881" target="_blank">
        said it will delay
       </a>
       the theatrical release of
       <em>
        The Interview
       </em>
       after hackers with apparent links to North Korea threatened 9/11-style attacks if cinema chains released the Sony picture.
      </p>
      <p>
       Rogen used his
       <a href="https://twitter.com/Sethrogen/status/547960203316965376" target="_blank">
        Twitter account
       </a>
       to welcome the streaming of
       <em>
        The Interview
       </em>
       in his native country: "Canada!!! You can now watch
       <em>
        The Interview
       </em>
       ! Please enjoy. It was made in your country."
      </p>
