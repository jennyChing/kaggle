         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://www.healthforefront.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cookies-448362_1920.jpg">
           <img alt="cookies-448362_1920" class="size-medium wp-image-224 aligncenter" height="200" src="http://www.healthforefront.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cookies-448362_1920-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          You must have heard that excess of everything is bad. Similar is the case of eating, especially at night. For you it may be just a case of overeating, but in medical terms, it is well known as
          <strong>
           Binge Eating.
          </strong>
          Binge eating at night is not normal. If you have just had a big dinner with your family and your stomach still craves for more, it is a time for reality check.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          It is high time for you to get in touch with your doctor who may tell you that sneaking a late-night snack is a devastating problem, and usually arises from
          <strong>
           obesity, stress, depression
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           diabetes
          </strong>
          . Ironically, he may also call you a sumo wrestler who prefers to have giant meal right before going to bedguaranteed to pack on pounds.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Now, dont you think that it really needs a serious discussion with him? After all, whatever happens always happens for a cause. Even having a sumo wrestler diet just after a big meal is also backed by a sensible science, and includes following information.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          First of all, you need to understand that longing for food, more sugar, and more fried items is not a flaw of your character.
          <strong>
           Unhealthy
          </strong>
          <strong>
           binging
          </strong>
          is a biological disorder and the changes it leads to is because of imbalanced hormones that regulate your appetite. Lets throw some light on five of them in detail
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Insulin-
          </strong>
          It is a very important hormone produced by your body to process the sugar in your diet. It is believed that if it is spiked, then it gets crashed after consuming sugar meal or junk food. This leads to a stage where you begin to feel hunger.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Leptin-
          </strong>
          It is an appetite suppressant that has the direct connectivity with your brain and sends the message that you shouldnt eat more. However, when you eat a lot of processed food or take sugar content in your meal, it fails to respond and hence your brain becomes leptin resistant.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Ghrelin-
          </strong>
          It acts as a supervisor to your untimely eating habits. Basically, it is a hunger hormone that regulates your appetite.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Peptide YY-
          </strong>
          It is another hormone produced within your intestines. Its keeps your stomach full throughout the day.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Cortisol:
          </strong>
          It is a stress hormone that leads to weight gain.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          For a quick recovery you must meet a doctor soon. But meanwhile eating right and sleeping right are the best that can be done to overcome your problem.
         </p>
