             backed REY as a long term investment and since we unveiled the companys cunning plan in the Canning their share prices has risen as high as 75%!

             At
             <i>

             we have identified REY as a long term, high potential investment and a steady stream of recent developments have reinforced our star October portfolio addition.
            </p>
            <p>
             The Canning Basin is where youll find the Ungani oilfield  which is soon to be producing 1,000 barrels of oil per day (BOPD) and is predicted to produce 5,000 (BOPD) as early as 2014.
            </p>
            <p align="center" style="text-align: center;">
             <a href="http://www.buruenergy.com/download/recent-presentations/53.2013%20AGM%20Presentation.pdf" target="_blank">
              <img alt="REY update 012" class="wp-image-3462 aligncenter" height="296" src="http://www.nextoilrush.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/10/2013/12/REY-update-012.png" width="122"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p align="center" style="text-align: center;">
             Source:
             <a href="http://www.buruenergy.com/download/recent-presentations/53.2013%20AGM%20Presentation.pdf" target="_blank">
              http://www.buruenergy.com/download/recent-presentations/53.2013%20AGM%20Presentation.pdf
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             The blocks where the Ungani discovery was made is owned jointly by two big players  Buru (ASX: BRU) and Mitsubishi (TSE:8058). BRU is a $445 million oil and gas explorer and producer, meanwhile Mitsubishi is quite simply THE largest trading company in Japan.
            </p>
            <p>
             But two neighbouring areas  known as the Fitzroy Blocks 
             <b>
              that cover the majority of the Ungani trend
             </b>
              are split between Buru, Mitsubishi and our $70 million market cap junior explorer
             <b>
              REY
             </b>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Somehow, REY managed to sneak into the Ungani trend and gain 25% of the Fitzroy Blocks from the big boys. REYs 25% interest in two of the Fitzroy Blocks was something we dubbed the jam in the doughnut because any discovery in the surrounding areas should impact positively on REYs share price.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a href="http://www.nextoilrush.com/who-is-the-mystery-third-man-we-reveal-the-next-junior-explorer-in-was-canning-super-basin/" target="_blank">
              <img alt="REY update 013" class="wp-image-3463 aligncenter" height="476" src="http://www.nextoilrush.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/10/2013/12/REY-update-013.png" width="811"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Source:
             <a href="http://www.buruenergy.com/download/recent-presentations/40.Corporate%20Presentation.pdf" target="_blank">
              http://www.buruenergy.com/download/recent-presentations/40.Corporate%20Presentation.pdf
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             REYs business plan is simple. Get access to energy production  if they dont get taken over first  by sitting back, riding its interest in the Fitzroy Blocks and let the others do all the heavy lifting.
            </p>
            <p>
             And its working.
            </p>
            <p>
             A big US player has entered the game, further sweetening the lucrative donut shaped blocks that surround REY, and Buru is moving fast, exploring and developing this patch of potential riches in Western Australias North-West.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             <span style="font-size: large;">
              <b>
               Why the excitement about REY?
              </b>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.asx.com.au/asxpdf/20131205/pdf/42lf7hzktj7gxz.pdf" target="_blank">
              Buru has just completed 3D seismic surveys
             </a>
             on its Ungani trend oil discovery confirming the potential of the field. Well workovers on Ungani 1 and Ungani 2 are complete and production start-up preparations are underway. Buru says everything is on time and on budget at the Port of Wyndham for the export of oil and we expect to see the first Ungani oil pumped into on-site tanks very soon.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a href="http://www.buruenergy.com/download/recent-presentations/53.2013%20AGM%20Presentation.pdf" target="_blank">
              <img alt="REY update 014" class="wp-image-3464 aligncenter" height="248" src="http://www.nextoilrush.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/10/2013/12/REY-update-014.png" width="428"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Source:
             <a href="http://www.buruenergy.com/download/recent-presentations/53.2013%20AGM%20Presentation.pdf" target="_blank">
              http://www.buruenergy.com/download/recent-presentations/53.2013%20AGM%20Presentation.pdf
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             All this activity from BRU is great news for REY, as it all directly relates to the Ungani oil field, where REY have a 25% interest in the key Fitzroy Blocks.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             <span style="font-size: large;">
              <b>
               REY and BRU moving forward in the Fitzroy Blocks
              </b>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             Of even more interest to REY is the work BRU have completed on the Fitzroy Blocks  known officially as EP 457 &amp; EP 458  of which REY have a 25% interest.
             <a href="http://www.asx.com.au/asxpdf/20131125/pdf/42l32kqy89cygb.pdf" target="_blank">
              BRU just completed a 2D geophysical survey
             </a>
             of 234 line kms over EP 457.
            </p>
            <p>
             Data quality is reported to be good and a regional airborne gravity survey of the area including the Fitzroy Blocks is set to be completed by the end of the year. When all that data comes in, the JV will select targets areas for drilling, slated for Q2 in 2014.
            </p>
            <p>
             EP 458 will go under the microscope in 2014, with a 2D seismic survey scheduled.
            </p>
            <p>
             With all this exploration activity, BRU are clearly very interested in the Ungani trend, and are now well funded to do so.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              <span style="font-size: large;">
               How does BRU find the time and money to concentrate on the Ungani trend?
              </span>
             </b>
            </p>
            <p>
             Enter Apache, the US energy giant renowned for its ability to find and exploit hard to reach deposits.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a href="http://www.asx.com.au/asxpdf/20131104/pdf/42km1v74z95t6y.pdf" target="_blank">

             The Fitzroy Blocks (split between BRU, Mitsubishi and REY) also overlap the Goldwyer and Laurel trends. These areas contain unconventional oil and natural gas  the very things driving Americas energy independence revolution. Hence Apaches interest.
            </p>
            <p>
             This is good news for REY as it relieves BRU of much of its financing commitment in the shale areas of its holdings  so it can concentrate on the Ungani oil field and the Laurel gas province  two areas REY has a nice share of and will want to see quick results in.
            </p>
            <p>
             These developments in the Canning Basin are attracting lots of attention, and not just from investors.
            </p>

               BHP as a natural gas producer?
              </b>
             </span>
            </p>

             BHP Billitons boss Andrew Mackenzie told the crowd at the companys November AGM in Perth that natural gas has the potential to overtake iron ore as the companys big earner  a prediction that has the energy industry buzzing, particularly after
             <a href="http://au.news.yahoo.com/a/19786938/" target="_blank">
              BHP recently won approval
             </a>
             for the remote offshore Scarborough gas project in WAs North West.
            </p>

             In September, the head of BHPs conventional gas business
             <a href="http://news.smh.com.au/breaking-news-business/bhp-keen-to-talk-wa-shale-gas-20130925-2udg8.html" target="_blank">
              Steve Pastor said the company was eyeing the Canning Basin
             </a>
             and wants to chat with WAs Premier Colin Barnett about how to get involved. That throwaway line is now looking a bit more serious.
