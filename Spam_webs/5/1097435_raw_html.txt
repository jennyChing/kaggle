  <meta content="Some nights I wake up in a complete panic; burning skin and dripping with 
sweat. Some days I get the fuzziest of heads and I cant find the skill to 
string together a sentence. Some days I simply cannot raise my head from 
the pillow to face the day.

I suffer from an illness that is often misconstrued, that is rarely fully 
understood, but that accompanies me everywhere, like a small child clinging 
onto my leg. Its not a disease that is fashionable, but I suffer, and so 
do the people around me.

I am type one diabetic. Not to be confused with type two, the illness that 
is commonly acquired by people who have made poor health and lifestyle 
choices, and who tend to be older or overweight, my illness picks on people 
utterly randomly. It is an autoimmune disease, and the treatment of it 
requires me to take at least five injections a day, not to mention pricking 
my finger for blood before each and every one of those shots.

Some days, some weeks, some months, its fine, dont get me wrong. Some 
times, as long as I take my shots right and live as healthily as I can, we 
work together in a comfortable ceasefire. But right now it absolutely 
consumes me, and it sucks.

My body produces no insulin by itself, so I take it all manually. As yours 
drips you insulin to combat your food intake and your liver production 
through the day, mine sleeps. Its been sleeping for the last twelve years; 
I actually think it ran away somewhere warm with lots of cocktails and a 
box of Celebrations, but its definitely not working in there. So I am at 
risk of highs (when I have eaten more sugar than I have covered with 
insulin) or lows (when I have taken too much insulin and not eaten enough).

In my handbag you will find jelly babies and needles. And if I forget them 
for whatever reason, I am literally left high and dry. Ive learnt to have 
one handbag and not care if it matches my outfit  I just roll with it. at 
least everything that goes out of there goes back in, and we roll on.

I feel for the people around me when im not at my best. I am lucky to have 
an amazing boss who gets it, and my fianc never bats an eyelid at the 
smell of hospitals (insulin smells nasty FYI), the 3am alarm when I have to 
check my blood in the night, or the emergency rustling when I need Jelly 
Babies, and STAT. and very rarely, I allow myself to wallow. Its not often, 
Im a glass full human, but every now and then I lament on the fact that I 
have been saddled with this for twelve long years (including three hospital 
stays and countless A&amp;E and outpatients visits) and have at least three 
times that left.

We pee on him, FYI

There is a silver lining to every cloud. I know the workings of my body so 
well that if I feel ill I can identify it and quickly work out how to solve 
the problem. I can detect the start of most infections and deal with my 
insulins to make sure that whatever the weather; Im covering myself from 
as much lasting damage as I can. So Im kind of bionic, dont you think?

Id love to know about other peoples illnesses, since I have been 
diagnosed I find the knowledge really interesting. Please share.

        <h1 class="post-title" data-content-field="title">
         Yes I CAN Eat That Cookie!
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" data-updated-on="1420635905043" id="item-54ad20a9e4b041e831338c09">
          <div class="row sqs-row">
           <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-c7f90a626465a5928568">

              </p>
              <p>
               Some nights I wake up in a complete panic; burning skin and dripping with sweat. Some days I get the fuzziest of heads and I cant find the skill to string together a sentence. Some days I simply cannot raise my head from the pillow to face the day.
              </p>
              <p>
               I suffer from an illness that is often misconstrued, that is rarely fully understood, but that accompanies me everywhere, like a small child clinging onto my leg. Its not a disease that is fashionable, but I suffer, and so do the people around me.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>

             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p>
               I am type one diabetic. Not to be confused with type two, the illness that is commonly acquired by people who have made poor health and lifestyle choices, and who tend to be older or overweight, my illness picks on people utterly randomly. It is an autoimmune disease, and the treatment of it requires me to take at least five injections a day, not to mention pricking my finger for blood before each and every one of those shots.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1420632118536_31427">

            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1420632118536_31645">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1420632118536_31215">
               Some days, some weeks, some months, its fine, dont get me wrong. Some times, as long as I take my shots right and live as healthily as I can, we work together in a comfortable ceasefire. But right now it absolutely consumes me, and it sucks.
              </p>
              <p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1420632118536_31216">
               My body produces no insulin by itself, so I take it all manually. As yours drips you insulin to combat your food intake and your liver production through the day, mine sleeps. Its been sleeping for the last twelve years; I actually think it ran away somewhere warm with lots of cocktails and a box of Celebrations, but its definitely not working in there. So I am at risk of highs (when I have eaten more sugar than I have covered with insulin) or lows (when I have taken too much insulin and not eaten enough).
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1420632118536_21320">

            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1420632118536_21552">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p>
               In my handbag you will find jelly babies and needles. And if I forget them for whatever reason, I am literally left high and dry. Ive learnt to have one handbag and not care if it matches my outfit  I just roll with it. at least everything that goes out of there goes back in, and we roll on.
              </p>
              <p>
               I feel for the people around me when im not at my best. I am lucky to have an amazing boss who gets it, and my fianc never bats an eyelid at the smell of hospitals (insulin smells nasty FYI), the 3am alarm when I have to check my blood in the night, or the emergency rustling when I need Jelly Babies, and STAT. and very rarely, I allow myself to wallow. Its not often, Im a glass full human, but every now and then I lament on the fact that I have been saddled with this for twelve long years (including three hospital stays and countless A&amp;E and outpatients visits) and have at least three times that left.
              </p>
             </div>

             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p>
               There is a silver lining to every cloud. I know the workings of my body so well that if I feel ill I can identify it and quickly work out how to solve the problem. I can detect the start of most infections and deal with my insulins to make sure that whatever the weather; Im covering myself from as much lasting damage as I can. So Im kind of bionic, dont you think?
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Id love to know about other peoples illnesses, since I have been diagnosed I find the knowledge really interesting. Please share.
               </strong>
              </p>
