    <h1>
     10 Reasons to Visit Queens Right Now
    </h1>
    <div class="ssw-content-dek">
     <p>
      Queens, New York is getting plenty of love these days:
      <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/north-america/travel-tips-and-articles/best-in-the-us-2015">
       Lonely Planet
      </a>
      recently named it as the top destination for 2015, and the
      <em>
       New York Times
      </em>
      bestowed it with the ever-popular designation of being "the next Brooklyn" (sigh). And while savvy travelers have long known about Queens's awesomeness, we're sure there are some people who know it only as a place to go when you're flying to or from New York City. If you fall into the latter category, here are ten reasons why Queens should be on your travel radar right now.
     </p>
    </div>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The History
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Youll find plenty of old,
       <em>
        old
       </em>
       New York in Queens: the neighborhoods Flushing and Jamaica have structures dating back to the 17th century. Queens was the site of the citys two most prominent Worlds Fairs, in 1939 and 1964, and remnants of those massive events remain in Flushing MeadowsCorona Park (including the iconic Unisphere, pictured). Head deeper into Queenss neighborhoods, and youll find a treasure trove of fascinating historical tidbits: Louis Armstrongs house is located in Corona; and President Theodore Roosevelt once gave a speech near Forest Hills Gardens, a beautiful, Tudor-style planned community. The
       <a href="http://www.queenshistoricalsociety.org/">
        Queens Historical Society
       </a>
       in Flushing is a great place to get acquainted with the boroughs past.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/12/11/5489fd42b6e03e8c2d0cba0c_5-unisphere-stacy-walsh-rosenstock-alamy.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The Outdoor Activities
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Some of New York Citys best parks can be found in Queens. The biggest is Flushing MeadowsCorona Park, home to the
       <a href="http://www.queensmuseum.org/">
        Queens Museum
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="http://nysci.org/">
        New York Hall of Science
       </a>
       . On the boroughs western edge, youll find Gantry Plaza State Park, which hugs the East River and has Instagrammable views of the Manhattan skyline. And Astoria Park has plenty of space to hang out, plus an Olympic-size swimming pool thats one of the citys most popular come summertime. (There are also plans afoot to build a High Line-style park along a disused railway that goes from Rego Park to Ozone Park; well see if that comes to pass.)
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/12/11/5489fd4cb6e03e8c2d0cba2a_4-gantry-plaza-state-park-david-giral-alamy.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The Food
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Queens is known for its ethnic enclaves that have produced many delicious dining optionsNew Yorkers regularly make the trek to Flushing for Chinese food, or Astoria for Greek dishes. But go beyond those pockets and youll find even more culinary goodies:
       <a href="http://www.cntraveler.com/stories/2014-12-05/casa-enrique-michelin-star-mexican-long-island-city-queens">
        Michelin-starred Mexican in Long Island City
       </a>
       , authentic Thai in Woodside, and a panoply of cuisines (Himalayan, Ecuadorian, even French bistros) in Jackson Heights, one of the boroughs most diverse neighborhoods. You could eat at a new spot in Queens every day for a year and never get bored.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/12/05/5481e186860c74c1162c6d8d_casa-enrique-restaurant-new-york.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The Beer
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Queens was once a hub for New York Citys brewing industry, and, in recent years, the borough has experienced a craft-beer renaissance. Homegrown breweries like
       <a href="http://www.singlecutbeer.com/">
        SingleCut
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.finbackbrewery.com/home.html">
        Finback
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="http://www.transmitterbrewing.com/">
        Transmitter
       </a>
       have put Queenss beer-makers on the map, while bars like Alewife and Sunswick 35/35 offer large craft selections to please hops heads. And of course, theres Astorias
       <a href="http://bohemianhall.com/">
        Bohemian Hall and Beer Garden
       </a>
       (pictured), a Czech-style
       <em>
        bierhaus
       </em>
       thats been a neighborhood staple for decades. Drink up!
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/08/07/53e2ff21c2d3f39d3610e4d6_bohemian-5.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The Nightlife
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Queens has quietly become one of New Yorks best destinations for comedy:
       <a href="http://creeklic.com/">
        The Creek and the Cave
       </a>
       in Long Island City has welcomed an impressive roster of funny folks, including Colin Quinn, Louis C.K., and Donald Glover. Meanwhile, up in Astoria, new venue
       <a href="http://qedastoria.com/">
        Q.E.D
       </a>
       . hosts a diverse array of events, including trivia nights, open-mic shows, and more. And comedy isnt the only thing Queens has going for it: In the summer, MoMA PS1s Warm Up is one of the citys hottest dance parties, hosting big names with an engaging, site-specific sculpture as the backdrop.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/12/11/5489fd37b6e03e8c2d0cb9ee_6-moma-ps1-warm-up-courtesy-moma-ps1.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The Art Scene
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       For decades, artists have repurposed once-abandoned spaces in Queens to create stunning creative hubs.
       <a href="http://socratessculpturepark.org/">
        Socrates Sculpture Park
       </a>
       , located on the water in Astoria, was once a landfill;
       <a href="http://momaps1.org/">
        MoMA PS1
       </a>
       , the contemporary-art hub, is housed in a former public school; and
       <a href="http://www.noguchi.org/">
        a museum dedicated to Isami Noguchi
       </a>
       is located in his former home. Long Island City (LIC) has also long attracted artists looking for more studio space and cheaper rents, though rising costs in that neighborhood may soon change that. (We've already had to say goodbye to one major Queens art hub: 5 Pointz Aerosol Art Center,
       <a href="http://www.cntraveler.com/galleries/2014-11-07/the-world-s-best-cities-for-street-art/9">
        a beloved graffiti hub
       </a>
       , thanks to incoming luxury housing.)
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/12/11/5489fd4c860c74c1162c8973_3-noguchi-museum-cr-philip-scalia-alamy.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The Museums
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Art isnt the only thing Queens has going for it. One of the boroughsnay, the citysbest institutions, the Queens Museum, is located in Flushing and houses art and historical artifacts. Dont miss
       <em>
        The Panorama of the City of New York
       </em>
       , a scale model of the city built for the 1964 Worlds Fair dotted with miniscule versions of the Empire State Building, the Statue of Liberty, and other landmarks. In Astoria, the
       <a href="http://www.movingimage.us/">
        Museum of the Moving Image
       </a>
       is a haven for pop-culture fiends, with exhibits and events devoted to film, TV, the Internet, and other media. (Catch the exhibit about
       <em>
        Looney Tunes
       </em>
       creator Chuck Jones before it closes in January.)
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/07/31/53daad896dec627b149faebb_museum-of-moving-image-behind-the-screen.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The Sports
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       The New York Mets have called Queens home since 1964, first playing at Shea Stadium, and then moving to
       <a href="http://newyork.mets.mlb.com/nym/ballpark/">
        Citi Field
       </a>
       in 2009. (And heres a secret: Citi is a nicer ballpark than Yankee Stadiumcertainly worth the trip if youre looking to catch a ball game in the city.) Just across the way in Flushing MeadowsCorona Park, the USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center hosts the US Open tennis tournament each year, drawing celebrities out to the borough. (Alas, we doubt youll run into Rafael Nadal on the 7 train.)
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/12/11/5489fd45860c74c1162c8955_7-citi-field-christopher-penler-alamy.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The Beach
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       Rockaway Beach has grabbed all the attention in recent yearsand to be fair, it deserves the hype. The water is clear, the beach (what parts of it are currently openpost-Superstorm Sandy repairs are still underway) is clean, and, thanks to the renewed interest in the area, theres plenty to eat and drink there. (
       <a href="http://rockawaytaco.com/">
        Rockaway Taco
       </a>
       alone is worth the trip.) Head west on the same inlet, though, and youll find two lesser-known patches of sand: Jacob Riis Park, named for the famous photographer, and Fort Tilden, which is good for sunbathers only. (There are no lifeguards, so swim at your own risk.)
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/08/06/53e2f113dddaa35c30f65ace_rockaway-couple-beach.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
    <section>
     <h2>
      The Convenience
     </h2>
     <div class="ssw-content-slide-content">
      <p>
       If youve only ever come to Queens to fly out of LaGuardia or JFK Airports, then okay, we can see how you might assume its not the easiest place to get to. But neighborhoods like Long Island City, Sunnyside, and Jackson Heights are a quick trip from Manhattanjust take the 7 train and youre there in minutes. And as much as New Yorkers like to bash the G train, it does serve as a connector between central Brooklyn and Long Island City, with plenty of transfers along the way. You have no excuse to not go there.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="image-wrapper ">
      <img alt="" class="" src="http://photos.cntraveler.com/2014/12/11/5489fd38860c74c1162c8937_10-7-train-philip-scalia-alamy.jpg" title=""/>
     </div>
    </section>
