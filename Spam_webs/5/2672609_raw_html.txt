      <h1>
       <a href="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/2010/06/rain-collector-skyscraper-edition/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Rain Collector Skyscraper Edition">
        Rain Collector Skyscraper Edition
       </a>
      </h1>
      <div class="entry">
       <div id="dd_ajax_float">
        <div class="dd_button_v">

       <div class="dd_content_wrap">
        <p>
         <a class="thickbox no_icon" href="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_cartoon.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_cartoon.jpg');" rel="gallery-6704" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_cartoon">
          <img alt="rain_collector_skyscraper_cartoon" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6706" height="706" src="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_cartoon-510x706.jpg" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_cartoon" width="510"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         via
         <a href="http://www.designboom.com/weblog/cat/9/view/9436/rain-collector-skyscraper.html" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.designboom.com/weblog/cat/9/view/9436/rain-collector-skyscraper.html');" target="_blank">
          designboom
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Everyday we live in a world where we mustadapt to atmospheric elements that have an effect on our daily routine (like this past weekend in Toronto). We take out our shades when its sunny, throw on a sweater when its a little windy and throw out the ever reliable umbrella when it rains. From solar panels used to harness those rays of sunshine to massive wind turbines to convert the wind into a renewable energy source, our civilization has learned to adapt to the elements and harness their natural power.
         <span id="more-6704">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Using thisphilosophy, Ryszard Rychlicki and Agnieszka Nowak, Polish Architectual Students of
         <a href="http://www.h3ar.pl/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.h3ar.pl/');" target="_blank">
          H3AR
         </a>
         (a
         <a href="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/2010/04/water-harvesting-skyscraper-in-sudan/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.vidafine.com/blog/2010/04/water-harvesting-skyscraper-in-sudan/');" target="_blank">
          previously talked about group
         </a>
         here at Vidafine), developed the capture the rain skyscraper. This building has a roof and external shell made up of a gutter system that aims at collecting as much rainwater as possible (The average daily consumption of water per person reaches 150 litres of a day  which includes water for showering, washing hands, toilet water, drinking water, and cooking water). Of the 150 litres, 85 litres (just over 50%) can be replaced with rainwater  astaggeringfact that we as socially aware individuals should look more closely at. When
         <a href="http://www.who.int/water_sanitation_health/mdg1/en/index.html" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.who.int/water_sanitation_health/mdg1/en/index.html');" target="_blank">
          over a billion people worldwide lack clean drinking water
         </a>
         , how can we sit on our hands and simply flush our toilets with water that is cleaner than the drinking water that a significant portion of our world consumes? Coupled with the fact that since 1900, the consumption of water in the Western world has increased by 1000% and individuals in North America consume five times more water than people in developing countries, we should celebrate the efforts of architects who formulate brave new designs focusing on combining sustainability efforts and modern design.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="thickbox no_icon" href="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_city.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_city.jpg');" rel="gallery-6704" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_city">
          <img alt="rain_collector_skyscraper_city" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6707" height="294" src="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_city-510x294.jpg" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_city" width="510"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Designers are often focusing on compact objects that provide a wide range of functionality. The Swiss army knife is a knife but also a spoon. The iPhone is an iPod but also a phone. The capture the rain skyscraper is a beautiful architectural building but also a rainwater collector. It only makes sense and a judging committee thought so too, awarding the architects a special mention for their proposal into the
         <a href="http://www.evolo.us/category/2010/page/2/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.evolo.us/category/2010/page/2/');" target="_blank">
          2010 Skyscraper Competition
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="thickbox no_icon" href="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_diagram.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_diagram.jpg');" rel="gallery-6704" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_diagram">
          <img alt="rain_collector_skyscraper_diagram" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6708" height="439" src="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_diagram-510x439.jpg" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_diagram" width="510"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thedifficultyin seeing such proposals come to realization, much like many things in life, comes to a budgetary concern. Investment in such a building, I would imagine, would be hefty and the trade off of spending dollars on a rain collectorskyscraperversus a typical cookie cutterskyscraperdesign must be weighty enough to be even considered. However, understanding that tenants would save tremendously in water bills (Breaking News: Rainwater is freefor now) in the long run might have construction companies take a closer look at such a design. While investment might come at a heavy price, we are investing not only in our environment, but in our future.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="thickbox no_icon" href="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_funnel.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_funnel.jpg');" rel="gallery-6704" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_funnel">
          <img alt="rain_collector_skyscraper_funnel" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6709" height="561" src="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_funnel-510x561.jpg" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_funnel" width="510"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="thickbox no_icon" href="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_stats.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_stats.jpg');" rel="gallery-6704" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_stats">
          <img alt="rain_collector_skyscraper_stats" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6710" height="298" src="http://www.vidafine.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/rain_collector_skyscraper_stats-510x298.jpg" title="rain_collector_skyscraper_stats" width="510"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         If we had more buildings designed in such a fashion, perhaps wed stop singing 
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=80wH1jkgw2o&amp;feature=related" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.youtube.com/watch?v=80wH1jkgw2o&amp;feature=related');" target="_blank">
          rain, rain go away, dont come back til another day
         </a>
         and start doing more
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rain_dancing" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rain_dancing');" target="_blank">
          rain dances
         </a>
         instead. In the meantime well have to stick with the singing, but its great to see architects really take a look at incorporated sustainable efforts in their designs. What do YOU think?
        </p>
