          <img alt="shaving set" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14192" height="1097" src="http://sharpologist.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Shaving-Set.jpg" width="800"/>
          So you received a fancy shaving set for a holiday gift? Thats terrific! Theres just one thing: you dont know how to use it, right? Lets take care of that. Here is how to use that nice shaving set.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-14181">
          </span>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Shaving Set Contents
         </h2>
         <p>
          Your new shaving set no doubt has a brush, a razor, perhaps a stand, and hopefully some kind of shaving soap or cream. Some may also have a pre-shave oil included as well. Lets take a look at each piece and show how it works.
         </p>
         <h3>
          The Brush And Soap/Cream
         </h3>
         <p>
          The first item in your shaving set is a brush. A shaving brush may be the most effective tool for shaving, perhaps more important than the razor! A shaving brush can gently clean out tiny bits of debris from the skin and apply shaving lather much more thoroughly and effectively compared to just rubbing it in with your fingers. Heres a video that explains how to load and apply lather with a shaving brush:
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="300" src="http://blip.tv/play/sEOtmWUC.x?p=1" width="400">
          </iframe>
          <embed src="http://a.blip.tv/api.swf#sEOtmWUC" style="display:none" type="application/x-shockwave-flash">
          </embed>
         </p>
         <p>
          You will want to take care of your brush. Here is where the decorative stand can actually come in handy. There is not much to it but a little preventative maintenance will go a long way:
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="300" src="http://blip.tv/play/sEPbm0AC.x?p=1" width="400">
          </iframe>
          <embed src="http://a.blip.tv/api.swf#sEPbm0AC" style="display:none" type="application/x-shockwave-flash">
          </embed>
         </p>
         <p>
          You may have received some pre-shave oil. You would use this before applying lather. However, pre-shave oils may not work very well for some people, so maybe just a good, thorough beard preparation is the way to go:
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="300" src="http://blip.tv/play/sEPxxhAC.x?p=1" width="400">
          </iframe>
          <embed src="http://a.blip.tv/api.swf#sEPxxhAC" style="display:none" type="application/x-shockwave-flash">
          </embed>
         </p>
         <h3>
          The Razor
         </h3>
         <p>
          The next part of your shaving set is a razor. You received a manual razor of some kind. If it is cartridge razor like a Mach3 or Fusion you probably already know how to use it (though perhaps not properly!). Here is a video to review:
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="300" src="http://blip.tv/play/sEOul3YC.x?p=1" width="400">
          </iframe>
          <embed src="http://a.blip.tv/api.swf#sEOul3YC" style="display:none" type="application/x-shockwave-flash">
          </embed>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you have been using a cartridge razor and you received a double edge (DE) razor, you may be entering new territory! Dont get discouraged if your first few shaves are not as good as you are used to. You may have to unlearn some bad habits (particularly putting pressure on the razor) and learn (or re-learn) some simple skills to be successful at DE shaving. Here is another video you might find useful:
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="300" src="http://blip.tv/play/sEOuyBoC.x?p=1" width="400">
          </iframe>
          <embed src="http://a.blip.tv/api.swf#sEOuyBoC" style="display:none" type="application/x-shockwave-flash">
          </embed>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you received a straight razor you may be in for a challenge! If you dont know anything about straight razors, heres an introduction:
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="300" src="http://blip.tv/play/sEODiqcoAg.x?p=1" width="400">
          </iframe>
          <embed src="http://a.blip.tv/api.swf#sEODiqcoAg" style="display:none" type="application/x-shockwave-flash">
          </embed>
         </p>
         <p>
          So there are the basics. Be sure to keep reading Sharpologist and join the legions of people who have changed shaving from an annoyingeven painful!chore into a pleasant diversion.
         </p>
