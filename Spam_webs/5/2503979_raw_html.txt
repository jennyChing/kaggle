          <p>
           The third wealthiest nation in the world. Number one for ease of doing business. Number one in Asia for quality of life. Singapore certainly has a lot going for it.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_20834" style="width: 860px">
           <img alt="The Merlion in Singapore" class="size-full wp-image-20834" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.noobpreneur.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/merlion-singapore.jpg?resize=850%2C428"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            photo credit:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/adforce1/14440190572/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             William Cho
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           A towering tax haven for high-rollers and millionaires-in-waiting, this city state is a boon for large businesses, with zero capital gains or dividend tax luring in foreign companies like a profiteering pied piper.
          </p>
          <p>
           And following these huge multinationals are a host of entrepreneurs hoping for a slice of that Singapore success-cake. Armed with nothing but a startup idea, some capital and a willful optimism, these young bucks are looking to reap the rewards of this hub of profit.
          </p>
          <p>
           So if youre aiming to be one of Singapores finest, here are a few facts you need to know.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Make your office virtual
          </h3>
          <p>
           When youre a budding startup, the biggest headache is having to shell out for a premises that can impress people while still remaining in your price range.
          </p>
          <p>
           The Central Business District (CBD) is the happening place to be for any business in Singapore, but an office in these skyscrapers can cost towering sums of cash.
          </p>
          <p>
           But the solution is at hand  invest in a virtual office to give you an impressive address without having to shell out for an actual location.
           <a href="http://www.servcorp.com.sg">
            The cream of these facilities
           </a>
           will even give you temporary business suites where you can hold meetings and wow your clients.
          </p>
          <p>
           Its an ideal way to balance the books and still look like a swish multinational.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Know your visa needs
          </h3>
          <p>
           Having a work visa (known as an Entrepass) is a must if you want to start a business in Singapore  and youll have to meet a few special criteria if you want to gain one.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            First off, your business structure will have to be registered as a private limited company.
           </li>
           <li>
            You arent allowed to own more than 30 per cent of its shares.
           </li>
           <li>
            Youll need at least SGD50,000 paid-up capital.
           </li>
           <li>
            You cannot have been registered as a business for more than six months before you apply.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           For more details on gaining your Entrepass, contact
           <a href="http://www.mom.gov.sg/Pages/default.aspx">
            Singapores Ministry of Manpower
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_20835" style="width: 860px">
           <img alt="Business as Usual in Orchard Road, Singapore" class="size-full wp-image-20835" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.noobpreneur.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/business-as-usual.jpg?resize=850%2C564"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            photo credit:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/marioopal/6276798689" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             mario_opal
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
          <h3>
           Stay friendly with the locals
          </h3>
          <p>
           The reception to expats has been frosty in recent years, with numerous protests against foreigners taking place in the central park.
          </p>
          <p>
           Thats why, especially if you want your startup to be a hit, its necessary to keep the locals on your side. Give Singaporeans the chance for employment instead of vying for outside help, and consider helping out in your local community.
          </p>
          <p>
           Do this and youll be in Singapores good books in no time.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Network to success
          </h3>
          <p>
           The population of Singapore consists of 38 per cent foreigners, and many will be in the same field as you.
          </p>
          <p>
           Because of this, a number of
           <a href="http://singapore.angloinfo.com/af/851/singapore-expat-business-associations-and-networking.html">
            networking events for expats
           </a>
           have sprung up across the city-state, allowing people to meet, get to know each other and maybe even spark up a business partnership. Take advantage of these connections and youll strike success in no time.
          </p>
