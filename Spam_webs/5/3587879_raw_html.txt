              <h1 class="title" itemprop="title headline">
               Infographic: What Kind of Worker Are You?
              </h1>
             </div>
             <span class="deck pull-three" itemprop="deck description">
              Are you a hustler? A charlatan? A martyr?
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="tools row collapse">
             <div class="twelve columns hide-on-print">
              <div class="share-box">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </header>
           <div class="row collapse">
            <div class="body prose twelve columns" itemprop="body articleBody">
             <p>
              We're all familiar with the myriad types of personalities you experience in the workplace, from suck-ups to slave drivers. But designer
              <a href="http://www.fastcompany.com/blog/cliff-kuang/design-innovation/portfolio-day-joey-roth-material-witness">
               Joey
              </a>
              <a href="http://www.fastcodesign.com/node/1658182">
               Roth
              </a>
              has managed to boil all these down in an infographic, using two simple metrics: How hard do you work? And how much do you talk?
             </p>
             <p>
              Martyrs work but don't talk; charlatans talk but don't work; and hustlers work and talk:
             </p>
             <p>
              <figure class="inline-large">
               <img src="http://images.fastcompany.com/upload/JoeyRothPoster.jpg"/>
              </figure>
             </p>
             <p>
              Originally created for
              <a href="http://www.fastcompany.com/1640042/theme-is-announced-for-48-hour-magazine">
               <em>
                48 Hour Magazine
               </em>
              </a>
              , the infographic is actually a beautiful letterpress, 11"x17" poster that Roth is now selling for $25
              <a href="http://joeyroth.com/poster/">
               on his website
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              Our only wish is that Roth had kept going with the conceit: Imagine new posters with just one more metric, such as efficiency! Then you'd have all kinds of funky shapes to illustrate many more types. And on this poster, we can think of at least one archetype who's missing: The lazy-ass, who'd be represented here simply as a straight line, neither talking nor working.
             </p>
             <p>
              <figure class="inline-large">
               <img src="http://images.fastcompany.com/upload/offthepress.jpg"/>
              </figure>
             </p>
             <p>
              <figure class="inline-large">
               <img src="http://images.fastcompany.com/upload/Hustler.jpg"/>
              </figure>
             </p>
