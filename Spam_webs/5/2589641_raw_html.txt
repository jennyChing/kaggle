         <p>
          There are more than just cherry blossoms in Japan. The peach blossoms are also lovely!
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-2168">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          This is an interesting kind of peach tree.
          <span class="numbers">
           3
          </span>
          different colors grow on the same tree: Red, Pink, and white. Lovely tree. Too bad the blossoms seem to last only a few days, which is less than even the cherry blossoms.
         </p>
         <h3 id="dailyphoto:redwhiteandpinkpeachblossoms">
          Daily Photo: Red, White, and Pink Peach Blossoms
         </h3>
         <p>
          In America, I suppose one might look odd if standing in the middle of a neighborhood, pointing a camera towards a house. Explanations of shooting the flowers in the tree rather than the house might be taken as excuses. Not so in Japan. Not only would everyone easily understand and accept such a reason, but the streets are full of people taking their own photos of the blossoms in other peoples yards. Different culture, huh?
         </p>
