       <a href="http://shufflingdead.com/">

        Entertainment
       </a>
        Currently Reading:
      </h2>
      <div class="block">
       <div class="article first_main_article">
        <h1>
         <a href="http://shufflingdead.com/articles/popculturepain/movie-maundering-1-hackers.php" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Movie Maundering #1  Hackers">
          Movie Maundering #1  Hackers
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="meta">
         <span class="author">
          <a href="http://shufflingdead.com/author/weskimo" rel="author" title="Posts by Weskimo">
           Weskimo
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="time">
          May 15, 2010
         </span>
         <span class="categories">
          <a href="http://shufflingdead.com/category/articles/popculturepain" rel="category tag">
           Entertainment
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="discuss">
          1 Comment
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10653" style="width: 560px">
         <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-10653" height="305" src="http://shufflingdead.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/05/Hackers-cover.jpg" title="Hackers-cover" width="550"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Image: Hackers DVD box cover. Copyright Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <b>
          Plot
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Hackers
         </em>
         follows the story of Dade Murphy, a young hacker who, at the age of 11, manages to crash 1507 systems in a single day.  Impressive, right?  Right.  But he gets caught and is court ordered to not operate a touch-tone phone or a computer until his 18th birthday. So we fast forward to his 18th birthday, and Dade has moved to New York City with his mother for her new job.  Cue typical teenage angst, some rebellious attitude, and new-kid-in-school highschool activities.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-7570">
         </span>
         <br/>
         At his new school, Dade quickly falls in with other 1337s, taking on a new alias Crash Override for his hacking hijinks. Most notable among his new friends is the one with the nice rack, none other than Kate Libby, alias Acid Burn.  Nerd chicks are hot, even short-haired ones.
        </p>
        <p>
         All seems to be going well for young Dade when two of his friends get arrested for hacking a high-profile system, the Ellingson Mineral Companys, and framed for creating a virus set to cause a major environmental disaster. The framing is done by the security officer for the company, a rather Snape-looking hacker named The Plague who is using the virus he created and pinned on two highschool kids as a decoy while a worm he planted siphons off $25 million dollars.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dade and his remaining 1337 friends must band together and strike back against this slimy dickrag and his FBI dupes by trying a risky concentrated attack on the Ellingson supercomputer to try to download evidence of Dickrags treachery.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Cast
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         Great cast. Smartass protagonist, some truly zany friends, stick-up-their-ass grownups, and a smokin hot love interest with a passion for tight clothing. Not to mention the rather androgynous pair of asian boys running a regular webisode called Hack the Planet, an informative program for curious hackers Umm, take that however you want.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Weskimos Take
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         Let me just send out a quick message: Fuck you, every game console I ever owned in highschool.  If I hadnt spent my time playing YOU, I could have been invited to some really cool rollerblading/hacker parties at the blackmarket arcade!  Unless this was just a contrived theatrical ploy to make the audience think that nerds were cool in highschool oh, wait a second. I see what you did there. Nevermind.
        </p>
        <p>
         But, excellent movie. Fun from start to finish, if a little predictable and/or unlikely. From two hackers fighting over a TV program to sending out arrest warrants on FBI agents and shutting down New Yorks traffic control system, entertaining hacker antics abound. A few stereotypical teenager themes rear their ugly heads (mission: find friends to accept me, why wont the hot girl date me!?, I hate my life!, etc.), but that really doesnt take much away from the enjoyment of the movie. Plus, Angelina Jolie is in it, usually without a bra, and occasionally with a see-through shirt.  And was it just me being lonely, or was Jolies ass particularly spectacular in this movie?
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyway, Hackers is a great viewing, from when it came out to present day.  On top of the sheer entertainment value, it dishes out pretty decent social commentary on the coming of the computer age and the relative computer illiteracy of the 90s.  It makes mention of the warrior concept referring to computer savvy individuals, and the importance that they will all play in the shaping of the world as we become more and more dependent upon new technologies.
        </p>
        <p>
         In closing, if anyone ever wants to watch Hackers, do so. Again and again. Also, as a public service announcement, this film has given rise to the greatest drinking game ever devised. Group A, drink every time Angelina Jolie is hot. Group B, drink every time Angelina Jolie is goth-hot. Enjoy alcohol poisoning. I know I did.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Scoring
         </b>
