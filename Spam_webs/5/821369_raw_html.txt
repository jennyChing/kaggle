        <article class="item item-answer clearfix" id="answer-36">
         <section class="content sl">
          <p>
           I never wanted having kids to keep me from traveling and since most of my family lives all over the country, taking the kids alone (without my husband) on flights has been something that has been both a necessity and a goal of mine.  I completely respect how expensive and special travel is for people and the last thing I want to do is potentially dampen a vacation by having unruly kids. No one wants to see the woman with four kids traveling alone on theirflight.
          </p>
          <p>
           First pack sippy cups for each child - empty initially due to security etc. but easily filled (only 1/2 way - you dont want to hit the potty every 10 minutes) by the flight attendants when the come around with the cart.  I have learned the hard way that kids cant be expected to balance a glass of liquid on a folding tray easily bumped. We dont have apple juice at home so its a huge treat on the flights and I dilute it a bit with water so they arent guzzling it down super fast. Next, have each kid pack a small backpack filled with a coloring book, crayons, perhaps a new crayola wonder book set (grabbed from the grocery store or pharmacy) and a few books that are either new (for my older kids) or old standbys (my younger kids are comforted by monotony). I also shove in their blanket and favorite stuffed animal and bust those out the minute we strap in our seats. I have been known to throw in an extra tshirt as well in their bags.  I pack an ipad (when possible), and toss out my iphone into the mix as well. I also bring Leapster Explorers with a few games and a pair of headsets for each kid (the little ones the airplane has dont work for them and occasionally I have been lucky enough to score DirectTV on a flight (Nick Jr., Disney, hooray!).  My daughter brings an
           <span class="caps">
            MP3
           </span>
           in her bag as she will sing the Sound of Music and Annie soundtracks til the cows come home.  Finally we bring tons and tons of food - its ridiculous really.  Each kid has a bag of goldfish, raisins, fruit chews, nuts, etc. I have found that trying to police the snacks just causes stress for me and there are plenty of other opportunities to lay down rules - this is all about getting through the air as quietly and politely as possible. In my bag, I have gum (for takeoff/landing), hand sanitizer, diaper wipes and advil. :)  Oh and one more thing I have never let the kids walk or crawl in the aisles - not that I havent been tempted but it always seemed like if I did it once than it would be confusing or frustrating when I couldnt do it for all the kids or when it was time to sit back down  - just seems like it would bite me in theend.
          </p>

        <article class="item item-answer clearfix" id="answer-35">
         <section class="content sl">
          <p>
           As adults we have to keep in mind that children are not adults. They are not built to be quiet and sit still for long periods of time. That said, I think there are things we can do to make the situation more manageable.  First, whenever possible, schedule flights around sleep pattens.  In your scenario, you may be able to schedule a coast to coast flight could at a time that would maximize the time children would be sleeping.  Bringing their pajamas and putting them in their pajamas might signal to the kids that its bed time! If that is not possible your best line of defense is distraction.  Bring lots of activities and snacks to keep them interested.  New books, toys, movies, etc. that they have never seen before will peak their interest.  I often visit the local library and check out
           <span class="caps">
            DVD
           </span>
           s for the children before a trip.  It keeps them interested and quiet.  As a passive activity it may even cause them to doze off for a while.  Lastly, dont forget that the whole world does not think that you children are as cute as you do!  As a parent, your job is to keep your children in control as much as possible.  Hope thathelps!
          </p>
