       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Cooking With Beer
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Sources:
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1412781817?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=jeffreyholtco-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=1412781817">
          Cooking with Beer
         </a>
         ,
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=jeffreyholtco-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1412781817" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         ,
         <a href="http://thebrewingnetwork.com/shows/The-Sunday-Session/The-Sunday-Session-11-12-06-Cooking-with-Beer" target="new">
          Sean Z. Paxton Interview on The Brewing Network
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Using beer when you cook isnt difficult.  Start by tasting the beer you drink, and thinking about its flavor and what would go well with it.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Ales
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          IPA
         </strong>
          use in place of wine or vinegar
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Amber Ale (Also, American Dark Lager)
         </strong>
          use with or in place of stock or broth
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Brown Ale
         </strong>
          Use in sauces
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Stout
         </strong>
          Use in gravies and barbecue sauces
        </p>
        <h2>
         Lagers
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Pilsner/American Light Lager
         </strong>
          use in biscuits, fritters and batters for seafood
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Bock
         </strong>
          Use with Beef or Pork, either braise or marinate
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Dark Lager
         </strong>
          Use with meats, or in place of stock or broth
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Malt Liquor
         </strong>
          Use is sauces and glazes or onion ring batter
        </p>
        <h2>
         Fruit Beer
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          P$egrave;che Lambic
         </strong>
          Use with chicken or poultry
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Kriek Lambic
         </strong>
          Use is sauces or desserts
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Framboise Lambic
         </strong>
          use in glazes for seafood
        </p>
        <h2>
         Belgian Ale
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Strong Ale
         </strong>
          Use with cheese and in dips
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Witbier
         </strong>
          Poach seafood or fritters
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Dubbel or Tripel
         </strong>
          Use in Carbonnade, soups and stews
        </p>
