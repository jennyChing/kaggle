              <h1 class="blog-title">
               <a href="http://twinsopinion.com/epa-to-monitor-hotel-showers/">
                EPA To Monitor Hotel Showers
               </a>
              </h1>
              <div class="blog-info-wrapper gdl-item-border">
               <div class="blog-date">
                <span class="head">
                 Posted On
                </span>
                <a href="http://twinsopinion.com/2015/03/17/">
                 17 Mar 2015
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="blog-author">
                <span class="head">
                 By :
                </span>
                <a href="http://twinsopinion.com/author/alecceravolo/" rel="author" title="Posts by Alec Ceravolo">
                 Alec Ceravolo
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="blog-comment">
                <a href="http://twinsopinion.com/epa-to-monitor-hotel-showers/#respond">
                 Comment: 0
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="blog-tag">
                <span class="head">
                 Tag:
                </span>
                <a href="http://twinsopinion.com/tag/obama-hotels-water-epa-tax-monitor-usage-anti-oil/" rel="tag">
                 Obama Hotels Water EPA Tax Monitor Usage Anti-oil
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="clear">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="blog-content">
               <p>
                <strong>
                 To Track How Long Guests Spend Showering At Hotels
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                According to FoxNews (EPA wants to monitor how long hotel guests spend in the shower), the EPA issued a grant to the University of Tulsa to develop a wireless system that will track hotel guests water usage in an attempt to modify their behavior. We dont know about you but our shower time at hotels is virtually no different than at home. In fact, with the poor shower heads in most hotels and low water pressure, shower times may actually be less.
               </p>
               <p>
                It is not the job of the EPA to attempt to modify our behavior, especially when it comes to taking a shower. Claiming millions of gallons a year are wasted as a result of hotel guest water usage, the Presidents ant-oil, anti-coal, and now anti-water agency is attempting to control our daily lives.
               </p>
               <p>
                The device will attempt to provide hotel guests with the ability to monitor their daily water usage in an attempt to help guests conserve water. We dont know about you, but the last thing we are going to do on business or vacation is to check our daily water usage. What will eventually come like everything else, is a tax to either hotel guests or hotels themselves for some arbitrary daily water use number.
               </p>
               <p>
                What the EPA should be concerned about us the zillions of regulations imposed on businesses that are costing our economy billions and billions of dollars each year. Instead, we get the destruction of the coal industry, an attempt to tax greenhouse gas emissions, and now potentially a tax on hotel guest water usage.
               </p>
               <p>
                The EPA, without any surprise, is delivering the Presidents leftist ideology upon the people through regulation after regulation that appeases the green community and the donors that support it. Instead of understanding the importance of limiting regulations on business so our economy can flourish, the President and his environmental agency do the exact opposite. Time for the Presudent to go and the EPA to be eliminated altogether.
               </p>
