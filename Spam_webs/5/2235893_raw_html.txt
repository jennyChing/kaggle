    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      <b>
       Catalina Lercaro
      </b>
      (Catherine Lercaro), 16th century, was an
      <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">
       Italian
      </a>
      -
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canarian" title="Canarian">
       Canarian
      </a>
      woman of the Lercaro family, renowned in the city of
      <a href="/wiki/San_Crist%C3%B3bal_de_La_Laguna" title="San Cristbal de La Laguna">
       San Cristbal de La Laguna
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">
       Tenerife
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">
       Canary Islands
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
       Spain
      </a>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      The Lercaro was an important family of
      <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">
       Genoese
      </a>
      merchants, based in Tenerife after the conquest. Catalina, daughter of Antonio Lercaro, was forced to marry an older man, who enjoyed a good position and great wealth. This marriage of convenience did not please Catalina, who on her wedding day decided to kill herself by leaping into the pit which is located in the courtyard of the family mansion (since 1993, the
      <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_the_History_of_Tenerife" title="Museum of the History of Tenerife">
       Museum of the History of Tenerife
      </a>
      ). The legend suggests that Catalina's body is buried in one of the rooms in the house, because, having committed suicide, the
      <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">
       Catholic Church
      </a>
      opposed a
      <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">
       Christian
      </a>
      burial in a consecrated cemetery. These events led the Lercaro family to move to
      <a href="/wiki/La_Orotava" title="La Orotava">
       La Orotava
      </a>
      . Since then many people claim to have seen the specter of Catalina Lercaro walking through the halls of the museum.
     </p>
     <p>
      Currently Catalina Lercaro is the most famous
      <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">
       spectre
      </a>
      of the Canary Islands. Also the mansion where he lived, is the
      <i>
       "haunted mansion"
      </i>
      most famous of the islands.
     </p>
