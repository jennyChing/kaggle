        Art That Passes the Meemaw Test
       </h1>
       <span class="sub-title">

      <div itemprop="articleBody">
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Over the past couple of years Ive been quick to denounce modern art that is
        <a href="http://www.firstthings.com/blogs/firstthoughts/2011/11/10/the-world%E2%80%99s-most-expensive-photograph-is-very-straight-and-slightly-overpriced/">
         overpriced
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.firstthings.com/blogs/firstthoughts/2011/03/15/a-shared-hatred-for-readymade-art/">
         ready-made
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.firstthings.com/blogs/firstthoughts/2011/07/22/woman-pays-10000-for-non-visible-work-of-art/">
         non-visible
        </a>
        , or simply by
        <a href="http://www.firstthings.com/web-exclusives/2010/06/thomas-kinkadersquos-cottage-fantasy">
         Thomas Kinkade
        </a>
        . What I havent done (at all, now that I think about it) is highlight contemporary art that is worthy of praise. In a modest attempt at balance, Id like to introduce you to the insanely talented and creative Shea Hembrey.
       </p>
       <p>
        I wont ruin the surprises in his charming presentation (which both skewers and humanizes modern art) by explaining what hes up to. Instead, Ill just encourage you to watch this video:
       </p>
