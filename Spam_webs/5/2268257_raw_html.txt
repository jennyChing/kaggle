       <div class=" withRelated right" id="WNStoryBody">
        <span id="WNStoryDateline">
         DALLESPORT, WA (KPTV) -
        </span>
        <p>
         A burglary suspect at the Columbia Gorge Airport was held face-down on the ground at gunpoint until law enforcement arrived at the scene.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Klickitat County Sheriff's Office was called out to the airport near Dallesport, WA at 2:42 a.m. Friday.
        </p>
        <p>
         Investigators said a burglar had smashed out the windows of the airport's main office and went inside the building.
        </p>
        <p>
         The caller to 911 reported hearing the glass breaking downstairs, so he had called for a neighbor to come by, as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         That neighbor, Darren Lacock, responded to the scene and confronted the suspect with a gun, according to deputies.
        </p>
        <p>
         Lacock held the suspect, identified as 25-year-old Christopher Wirshup, at gunpoint until law enforcement officers arrived.
        </p>
        <p>
         Deputies from Klickitat County and Wasco County, as well as police officers from The Dalles, responded to the call. Wirshup was taken into custody and booked in the Klickitat County Jail on charges of burglary, malicious mischief and theft.
        </p>
        <p>
         Investigators said Wirshup smashed out the windows of the office by using landscape bricks that were outside the building.
        </p>
        <p>
         According to
         <a href="http://www.thedalleschronicle.com/" target="_blank">
          The Dalles Chronicle
         </a>
         , Lacock is the line service technician at the airport. He told the newspaper that the suspect was stuffing candy in his pockets when he noticed Lacock with his gun drawn.
        </p>
        <p>
         At that point, the newspaper reports Wirshup was so startled that he flung the box of peppermint patties he was holding into the air and then dropped to the floor.
        </p>
        <p>
         I just started praying to God that he wouldn't do anything stupid that would force me into having to make that decision to fire his weapon, Lacock told
         <a href="http://www.thedalleschronicle.com/" target="_blank">
          the Chronicle
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Lacock said he is licensed to carry a concealed weapon in Oregon and Washington.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Klickitat County Sheriff's Office described him as a "vigilant citizen."
        </p>
